Value-Based Pricing

Setting prices based on the perceived or estimated value of a product or service to the customer rather than on the cost of the product or historical prices.

Return on Health (ROH)

The measurable financial and quality-of-life benefits a client receives from improving their metabolic markers (e.g., lower insurance premiums, fewer sick days).

Signature Program

A proprietary, multi-week coaching container that follows a specific methodology (like STABLE) to achieve a predictable outcome.

The Value-Based Pricing Paradigm

Most health coaches fail because they price themselves like a commodity. If you charge $75 per hour, the client views you as an expense to be cut. If you charge $2,500 for a 12-week Diabetes Reversal Transformation, the client views you as an investment in their future.

In the metabolic health space, the stakes are exceptionally high. A 2023 analysis found that individuals with diagnosed diabetes incur average medical expenditures of approximately $19,745 per year, of which about $12,022 is attributed directly to diabetes. When you help a client achieve "STABLE" blood sugar, you are effectively saving them tens of thousands of dollars over the next decade.

Tiered Service Models

To maximize your reach, use a "Good, Better, Best" tiered pricing structure. This allows you to capture clients at different stages of their journey.

Research suggests that when presented with three options, 60-70% of clients will choose the middle option. Price your "Better" option as your primary target, and use the "Best" option to anchor the value high.

The High-Ticket Transformation

High-ticket coaching ($3,000+) is often easier to sell than low-ticket coaching because it attracts highly committed individuals. When a client invests $3,000, they are psychologically primed to follow the protocols. This leads to better results, which leads to better testimonials, which leads to more high-ticket sales.

A 2021 study on health coaching engagement showed that financial commitment was positively correlated with adherence to lifestyle modifications (p < 0.05). By charging more, you are actually helping your client achieve a better outcome.
