Glycemic Variability (GV)

The frequency and magnitude of blood glucose fluctuations. High GV is a primary driver of oxidative stress and vascular damage.

HOMA-IR

Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance. Calculated via fasting insulin and glucose to determine receptor sensitivity.

Time in Range (TIR)

The percentage of time a client spends within a target glucose window (typically 70-140 mg/dL or 3.9-7.8 mmol/L).

Postprandial Peak

The highest glucose value reached after a meal, usually occurring 45-90 minutes following ingestion.

The Assessment Framework: Why A1c Isn't Enough

In traditional clinical settings, the Glycated Hemoglobin (HbA1c) test is the gold standard. Tactical Tracking (T) Set-up: The 14-Day Baseline

The "T" in STABLE stands for Tactical Tracking. We initiate every coaching relationship with a 14-day "Discovery Phase." During this period, the client wears a CGM but makes no significant changes to their existing diet or exercise. This provides the "Raw Metabolic Signature."

Coaching Protocol

During the Discovery Phase, instruct clients to log "The Big Three": Food (type and order), Stress (1-10 scale), and Sleep (duration and quality). Without these logs, CGM data is just a line on a graph; with them, it is a diagnostic tool.

Circadian & Lifestyle Audit (L)

Blood sugar is not just a function of what we eat. The Lifestyle & Circadian Modulation (L) pillar recognizes that insulin sensitivity follows a rhythmic pattern. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that even a single night of 4-hour sleep deprivation reduced insulin sensitivity by an average of 18-25% in healthy adults.

Your assessment must include:

  • Sleep Hygiene: Consistency of wake/sleep times and exposure to morning sunlight.
  • Chrononutrition: The timing of the largest meal (morning vs. evening).
  • Stress Load: Identifying HPA-axis triggers that cause "cortisol spikes" (non-food related glucose elevations).

The STABLE Intake Questionnaire

A comprehensive intake must bridge the gap between biochemistry and behavior. Use the following high-impact questions during your initial consultation:

1. The Energy Arc Question

"On a scale of 1-10, where is your energy 60 minutes after lunch? Does it crash, or stay steady?" (Assesses postprandial reactive hypoglycemia).

2. The Dawn Phenomenon Check

"Do you wake up feeling refreshed, or do you feel 'wired but tired'?" (Assesses cortisol/glucose synergy and HPA-axis status).

3. The Muscle-Glucose Link

"How often do you engage in resistance training, and what is your immediate energy level afterward?" (Assesses GLUT4 translocation potential).

Clinical Interpretation: Building the Roadmap

Once you have gathered the lab work, the 14-day CGM data, and the intake questionnaire, you create the Metabolic Snapshot. This snapshot categorizes the client into one of three STABLE phenotypes:

  • The Spiker: Low fasting glucose but high postprandial excursions (>160 mg/dL). Needs focus on Evolutionary Nutrition (E) and Food Order.
  • The Flatliner: High baseline glucose (110-130 mg/dL) with little movement. Indicates chronic insulin resistance. Needs focus on Active Sensitization (A) and HPA-axis regulation.
  • The Rollercoaster: Extreme highs followed by extreme lows. Needs focus on Stabilization (S) and Circadian Modulation (L).
