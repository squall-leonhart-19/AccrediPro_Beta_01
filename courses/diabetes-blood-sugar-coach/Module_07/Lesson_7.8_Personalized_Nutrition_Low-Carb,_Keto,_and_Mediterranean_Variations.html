Nutritional Ketosis

A metabolic state where the body utilizes ketone bodies (beta-hydroxybutyrate) as the primary fuel source instead of glucose, typically achieved at < 50g of carbs/day.

MUFAs

Monounsaturated Fatty Acids, found in olive oil and avocados, which are central to the Mediterranean variation and improve insulin sensitivity.

Metabolic Flexibility

The body's ability to efficiently switch between oxidizing carbohydrates and fats based on availability and demand.

Glycemic Load (GL)

A measure that accounts for both the Glycemic Index and the quantity of carbohydrates in a serving, providing a more accurate picture of blood sugar impact.

The Spectrum of Carbohydrate Restriction

The core of the STABLE Methodâ„¢ Phase 6 is aligning modern nutrition with evolutionary biology. While our ancestors consumed a wide variety of diets based on geography, they all shared one trait: a lack of refined, acellular carbohydrates. In clinical settings, we categorize carbohydrate intake along a spectrum to determine the degree of insulin suppression required for stabilization.

A 2021 systematic review published in The BMJ analyzed 23 randomized controlled trials (n=1,357) and found that patients on low-carbohydrate diets achieved higher rates of type 2 diabetes remission (up to 32% higher) compared to those on control diets at 6 months. However, the "intensity" of restriction must be balanced against the client's glycemic variability and lifestyle sustainability.

Low-Carb Nutrition (LCD): The Entry Point

A "Low-Carb" diet is typically defined as consuming less than 26% of total daily calories from carbohydrates, or roughly 50g to 130g per day. This variation is the most versatile and serves as an excellent starting point for clients with mild insulin resistance or those transitioning from a Standard American Diet (SAD).

Physiologically, the LCD variation works by reducing the postprandial glucose spike, thereby lowering the "area under the curve" (AUC) for insulin. This allows the beta cells of the pancreas to "rest" and helps reverse the downregulation of insulin receptors. For many clients, this level of restriction is sufficient to achieve a Time in Range (TIR) of >70% without the metabolic rigor of full ketosis.

Clinical Insight

The LCD variation is often the "sweet spot" for active individuals. By providing 100-130g of carbohydrates, we support the GLUT4 translocation mechanism during exercise (Phase 3 of STABLE) while preventing the chronic hyperinsulinemia seen in higher-carb patterns.

The Ketogenic Variation (VLCKD): Metabolic Switching

The Very Low-Carbohydrate Ketogenic Diet (VLCKD) restricts carbohydrates to < 50g per day (often < 20g-30g net carbs). This is not merely a "low carb" diet; it is a metabolic intervention designed to induce nutritional ketosis.

When glucose availability is low, the liver converts fatty acids into ketone bodies. From a blood sugar perspective, this is the ultimate "stabilizer." In a landmark 2-year study by Virta Health (Hallberg et al., 2018), 53% of participants achieved T2D reversal, and 67% of all diabetes-specific prescriptions were discontinued. The mechanism is a profound reduction in the insulin-to-glucagon ratio, which shifts the body from a storage state to a mobilization state.

The Mediterranean Variation: The Fiber & Polyphenol Powerhouse

While Low-Carb and Keto focus on *quantity* of carbohydrates, the Mediterranean variation focuses on *quality* and *synergy*. It is characterized by high intakes of monounsaturated fats (olive oil), fiber-rich legumes, vegetables, and moderate amounts of whole grains.

The Mediterranean diet excels in improving cardiovascular outcomes, which is critical since T2D patients are at a 2-4x higher risk of heart disease. The PREDIMED study (2013) showed a 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events. The mechanism here is the synergy between polyphenols (which protect the endothelium) and high fiber (which slows glucose absorption and promotes natural GLP-1 production).

Personalization Strategies

The STABLE Methodâ„¢ does not view these diets as static. We use Tactical Tracking (Phase 2) to pivot between them. A client might start on Keto to achieve rapid stabilization and weight loss, then transition to a Mediterranean-Low-Carb hybrid for long-term maintenance.

Personalization Factors:

  • Glycemic Variability (GV): If CGM data shows high "spikes" even with whole grains, move toward lower carb restriction.
  • Lipid Profile: If LDL-P increases significantly on high-saturated fat Keto, pivot to the Mediterranean variation (MUFA-focus).
  • Gut Health: If a client suffers from constipation on Keto, increase fiber via the Mediterranean approach while keeping net carbs low.

Knowledge Check

Test your mastery of personalized nutrition variations.

1

A client with Type 2 Diabetes is currently taking a Sulfonylurea medication. Which variation requires the most immediate medical coordination and why?

The Ketogenic Variation (VLCKD). Because it drastically lowers blood sugar rapidly, the risk of hypoglycemia is high if medication dosages are not adjusted downward immediately by their physician.
2

What is the primary physiological marker that distinguishes a "Ketogenic" diet from a standard "Low-Carb" diet?

The presence of nutritional ketosis, typically measured via blood beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) levels between 0.5 and 3.0 mmol/L. Standard low-carb diets may not consistently produce ketones.

Key Takeaways

  • No "Best" Diet: The most effective variation is the one the client can sustain that keeps their Time in Range (TIR) above 70%.
  • Keto for Reversal: VLCKD is the most powerful tool for T2D reversal and rapid insulin reduction but requires the most oversight.
  • Mediterranean for Longevity: This variation provides superior cardiovascular protection and is often the best "maintenance" phase.
  • The STABLE Pivot: Use CGM data to decide when to move a client along the carbohydrate spectrum.
