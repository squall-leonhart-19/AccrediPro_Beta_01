    5. Collaborating with the Multi-Disciplinary Team

    A coach is a vital piece of the puzzle, but not the whole puzzle. To sustain long-term success, you must facilitate a Circle of Care. This prevents the client from receiving conflicting advice which leads to "Decision Paralysis."

    Your role includes communicating with:

    • Primary Care Physicians (PCPs) & Endocrinologists: Sharing CGM trends and weight loss data to assist the doctor in medication de-prescription (deprescribing).
    • Registered Dietitians (RDs): Collaborating on specific medical nutrition therapy requirements.
    • Certified Personal Trainers: Ensuring the "Active Movement" pillar is executed safely, especially regarding exercise-induced hypoglycemia.

    6. Sustaining the S.T.A.B.L.E. Vision

    The final stage of the E: Empower & Sustain module is moving from "active coaching" to "community stewardship." In this phase, the coach acts as a facilitator of the environment. You are no longer the source of all answers; you are the curator of a culture that values metabolic stability.

    Check Your Understanding

    1. Why is the "Teacher Effect" considered a relapse prevention strategy?

    It shifts the client's identity from a "passive patient" to an "active expert." By teaching the S.T.A.B.L.E. principles, they reinforce their own cognitive pathways and create a social reputation that they are motivated to maintain.

    2. What is the primary risk of a digital community, and how can a coach mitigate it?

    The primary risk is "data fatigue" or "comparison anxiety." A coach mitigates this by shifting the focus from "perfect numbers" to "metabolic experiments" and "shared learning," ensuring the community remains a supportive laboratory rather than a leaderboard.
