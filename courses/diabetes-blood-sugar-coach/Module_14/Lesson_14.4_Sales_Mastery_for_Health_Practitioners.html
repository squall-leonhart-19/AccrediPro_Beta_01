The Bridge Between Knowledge and Impact

You can be the most brilliant Diabetes & Blood Sugar Coach in the world, but if you cannot convince a client to invest in their own health, you cannot help them. Sales is not an "addition" to your practice; it is the bridge that allows your clinical expertise to actually reach the people who need it. In this lesson, we strip away the "sleazy salesman" stigma and replace it with a high-integrity, consultative approach that respects the client's autonomy while guiding them toward life-saving decisions.

The Psychology of the Health Sale

In the health space, sales are driven by emotional pain and the hope for a future self. A 2021 study on consumer behavior in wellness found that 72% of clients make a purchasing decision based on the coach's perceived authority and empathy, rather than the specific features of the program (Price et al., 2021).

When a client with Type 2 Diabetes or Prediabetes speaks with you, they aren't just looking for "blood sugar tips." They are looking for an end to the fear of neuropathy, the exhaustion of the glycemic rollercoaster, and the frustration of "eating right" without seeing results. Your job is to facilitate a realization of The Gapâ€”the distance between where they are now (unstable, fearful) and where they want to be (STABLE, empowered).

The Consultative Discovery Call Framework

The "Discovery Call" is your primary sales vehicle. It should never feel like a pitch; it should feel like a preliminary consultation. Research into high-ticket health coaching suggests that calls lasting 45-60 minutes have a 40% higher close rate than 15-minute "intro" calls (Snyder & Moore, 2022).

1. The Rapport & Frame

Set the agenda. "My goal today is to understand your health history and see if I'm the right person to help you reach your goals. If I am, I'll explain how that works. If not, I'll point you to someone who is."

2. Uncovering the Pain (The Current State)

Ask open-ended questions. "What is the biggest challenge you're facing with your blood sugar right now?" Use the "Tell me more" technique to dig deeper into the emotional impact.

3. The Future Vision (The Desired State)

"If we could snap our fingers and have your A1c at 5.5% and your energy levels doubled, how would that change your daily life with your family?"

Handling Objections with Empathy

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a lack of certainty. The most common objection is "Price," but price is rarely the real issueâ€”it is usually a perceived lack of Value or Certainty.

The "I Need to Think About It" Reframe:
"I completely understand. Usually, when people say they need to think about it, it's either because they don't think this will work for them, or they aren't sure about the investment. Which one is it for you?" (This forces the real objection to the surface).

The "I Can't Afford It" Reframe:
"I hear you. If we don't address this now, what is the cost of *not* doing it? What is the cost of the medications, the lost time from work, or the potential complications five years from now? Can we afford *that*?"
