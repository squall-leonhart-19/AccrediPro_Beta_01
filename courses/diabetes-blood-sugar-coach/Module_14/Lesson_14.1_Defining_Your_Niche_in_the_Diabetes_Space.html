The Riches in the Niches

In the world of professional coaching, there is a dangerous temptation to be "everything to everyone." Many new practitioners fear that by narrowing their focus, they are turning away potential revenue. However, the data suggests the exact opposite. According to a 2023 industry analysis of 1,850 health practitioners, those who identified as "Specialists" reported an average annual income of $118,000, while "Generalists" averaged only $64,000.

In the diabetes space, niching is not just a marketing strategy; it is a clinical necessity. The needs of a Type 1 Athlete are radically different from a post-menopausal woman with Type 2 Diabetes and PCOS. By choosing a niche, you move from being a "commodity" to being an "expert."

The Generalist Trap

A generalist health coach markets themselves as helping people "eat better and lose weight." A specialist Diabetes & Blood Sugar Coach markets themselves as helping "Executives with Type 2 Diabetes stabilize their A1c without sacrificing their performance at work."

When you are a generalist, you compete on price. When you are a specialist, you compete on outcomes. Patients with chronic metabolic conditions are often desperate for someone who "speaks their language"‚Äîsomeone who understands the Dawn Phenomenon, CGM data, and the nuances of insulin sensitivity.

Identifying Your Blue Ocean

A "Blue Ocean" is a market space where there is little to no competition. Instead of fighting for Type 2 Diabetes clients in general, look for specific intersections of health. Consider these high-value niches:

  • The Performance Niche: Athletes with Type 1 or Type 2 Diabetes using the Active Sensitization principle of the STABLE Method‚Ñ¢.
  • The Hormonal Niche: Women with PCOS and Insulin Resistance focusing on fertility and weight loss.
  • The Executive Niche: High-stress professionals dealing with cortisol-induced hyperglycemia and the "Dawn Phenomenon."
  • The "Reversal" Niche: Clients with Pre-diabetes or early-stage Type 2 looking for clinical remission through Evolutionary-Based Nutrition.
