Professional Outreach Strategy

When approaching a medical professional, avoid "marketing-speak." Physicians respond to clinical data and professional reliability. The goal is to move from "someone I met" to a "trusted colleague."

The Peer-to-Peer Mindset

Never ask a doctor for a referral as a favor. Instead, offer to take their most "difficult" patientsâ€”those who are struggling to lose weight or lower their A1c despite medicationâ€”and provide the intensive tracking they need.

Your outreach should follow a three-step process:

  • The Introductory Letter: A brief, professional letter (on letterhead) introducing your specialization and your use of Tactical Tracking (CGM data).
  • The Value-Add Meeting: A 10-minute "meet and greet" where you demonstrate a sample client progress report.
  • The SBAR Communication: Using the medical standard: Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation.

The fastest way to lose a medical referral partnerâ€”and potentially your businessâ€”is to overstep your scope of practice. In the STABLE Methodâ„¢, we emphasize Biological & Endocrine Synergy, but we never adjust medication.

The Golden Rule of Referral Networking:

  • NEVER: Tell a client to stop, start, or change a medication dose.

Building Long-Term Trust

Trust is built in the "follow-up." Successful coaches maintain a database of their referral partners and send occasional relevant research papers or updates on new CGM technology. This positions you as a Thought Leader in the blood sugar space.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Tactical Tracking" (CGM data) the most important tool for building physician trust?

It provides objective, clinical data that the physician recognizes and values. It moves the conversation from subjective "feelings" to objective "trends," allowing the physician to make better medical decisions based on your coaching outcomes.

2. What is the SBAR protocol and why should a coach use it?

SBAR stands for Situation, Background, Assessment, and Recommendation. It is the standardized communication framework used by medical professionals. Using it demonstrates your professionalism and respect for their clinical environment.
