3. The Ethics of Discharge & Client Mastery

Ethical coaching requires us to avoid creating learned helplessness. A Master Practitioner knows that the goal of every engagement is to eventually become unnecessary. However, discharging a client too early can lead to regression, while holding on too long creates dependency.

We define Client Mastery through three pillars:

  1. Physiological Stability: HbA1c, HOMA-IR, and Triglyceride/HDL ratios remain within optimal ranges for at least 6 consecutive months without intensive intervention.
  2. Behavioral Resilience: The client can navigate high-risk environments (holidays, travel, stress) without a total metabolic collapse.
  3. Cognitive Competence: The client can explain the *why* behind their choices (e.g., "I'm choosing vinegar and fiber first to blunt the glucose spike of this meal").
