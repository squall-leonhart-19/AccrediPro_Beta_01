3. Interpreting Kraft Insulin Patterns

Dr. Joseph Kraft, the pioneer of the "Insulin Assay," performed over 14,000 oral glucose tolerance tests with simultaneous insulin measurements. He discovered that the shape of the insulin curve tells a more accurate story than the glucose curve alone. In our Screen & Assess (S) phase, we look for these patterns:

Pattern 1: Normal

Pattern 2: Hyperinsulinemia (Early)

4. Advanced Biomarker Ratios

Since most clients cannot easily access a 5-hour Kraft test, we utilize two powerful proxies in the S.T.A.B.L.E. framework: HOMA-IR and the TG:HDL Ratio.

HOMA-IR (Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance)

Formula: (Fasting Glucose x Fasting Insulin) / 405.
A score > 1.9 indicates early insulin resistance; > 2.9 indicates significant insulin resistance. Even if glucose is 85, an insulin of 15 yields a HOMA-IR of 3.1â€”a clear red flag.

Triglyceride-to-HDL Ratio

This is perhaps the most accessible marker of insulin sensitivity. In a 2023 meta-analysis, a ratio > 2.0 (mg/dL) was found to be 80% predictive of insulin resistance. For a client with "normal" A1c but a TG:HDL ratio of 3.5, you are looking at a metabolic crisis in the making.

5. The S.T.A.B.L.E. Intervention Strategy

When working with a Hidden Diabetic, the goal is not to lower glucose (which is already "normal") but to lower the insulin requirement of the body. We do this through the Transform Nutrition (T) and Activate Movement (A) pillars.

Transform Nutrition (T) for Hyperinsulinemia

  • Protein Leverage: Increase protein to 1.2â€“1.5g/kg of body weight. Protein stimulates glucagon, the "anti-insulin" hormone, while providing high satiety.
  • Carbohydrate Threshold: Implement a "Net Carb" limit (typically < 50-75g/day) to reduce the post-prandial insulin spike.
  • Fiber Matrix: Ensure 30g+ of fiber to slow gastric emptying and dampen the cephalic phase insulin response.

Activate Movement (A): The Glucose Sink

For MONW clients, aerobic exercise (like Sarah's running) often isn't enough because it doesn't significantly increase GLUT4 translocation in the muscle tissue. We prioritize Resistance Training to build the "Glucose Sink." More muscle mass means more surface area for insulin to work, eventually allowing the pancreas to dial back production.

Why is HbA1c a poor screening tool for detecting early-stage metabolic dysfunction in "thin" clients?

HbA1c only measures average glucose over 3 months. In early insulin resistance, the pancreas compensates by overproducing insulin to keep glucose normal. Therefore, a client can have a perfect A1c while suffering from severe hyperinsulinemia, which drives inflammation and visceral fat storage.
2

A client has a Fasting Glucose of 95 and Fasting Insulin of 22. What is their HOMA-IR, and what does it indicate?

HOMA-IR = (95 x 22) / 405 = 5.16. This indicates severe insulin resistance, despite the "normal" (under 100) fasting glucose. This client is at high risk for cardiovascular disease and Type 2 Diabetes.
