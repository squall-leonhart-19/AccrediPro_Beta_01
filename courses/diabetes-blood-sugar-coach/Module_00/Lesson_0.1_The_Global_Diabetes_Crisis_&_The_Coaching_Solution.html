4. The Economic and Personal Impact

Metabolic dysfunction is expensive. A 2023 meta-analysis found that individuals with diagnosed diabetes incur average medical expenditures of $16,752 per year, of which about $9,601 is attributed directly to diabetes. For the individual, the "personal tax" of poor blood sugar control includes chronic fatigue, cognitive decline ("brain fog"), and a 2-4x increased risk of cardiovascular events.

When you help a client reverse prediabetes, you aren't just helping them lose weight; you are potentially saving them over $100,000 in lifetime medical costs and adding high-quality years to their life expectancy.

5. Target Demographics & At-Risk Populations

Who is your ideal client? While the epidemic is global, specific populations are in desperate need of specialized coaching:

The "Hidden" Prediabetic

Professionals aged 35-55 with high stress, sedentary jobs, and rising fasting glucose levels. They are often "TOFI" (Thin Outside, Fat Inside).

Women with PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome is essentially "Diabetes of the Ovaries." 70% of these women have underlying insulin resistance that is poorly managed by standard care.

Post-Gestational Support

Women who had Gestational Diabetes have a 50% chance of developing Type 2 Diabetes within 10 years. They are a prime demographic for preventative coaching.
