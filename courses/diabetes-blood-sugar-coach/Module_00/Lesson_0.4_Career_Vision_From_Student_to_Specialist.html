3. KPIs for Client Success Beyond Simple Glucose

While Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) and HbA1c are critical, they are lagging indicators. To provide premium value, you must track leading indicators that demonstrate the efficacy of your intervention before the next blood draw.

15-20% Reduction in Glycemic Variability
40% Improvement in Sleep Quality
2x Increase in Post-Meal Energy

Your KPI dashboard for clients should include:

  • Glycemic Variability (GV): The "swings" in blood sugar, often more damaging to vasculature than a steady high.
  • Post-Prandial Recovery: How quickly glucose returns to baseline after a carbohydrate load.
  • Triglyceride/HDL Ratio: A powerful surrogate marker for insulin sensitivity.
  • Subjective Vitality Score: Tracking brain fog, energy crashes, and "hanger" episodes.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is tracking "Glycemic Variability" considered a more advanced KPI than just Fasting Glucose?

Fasting glucose is a snapshot in time, whereas Glycemic Variability measures the fluctuations and "spikes" throughout the day. High variability is strongly linked to oxidative stress and cardiovascular complications, even if the average glucose (HbA1c) appears normal.

2. Which career pathway typically involves "split-billing" or referral fees?

Clinical Integration. In this model, the coach works alongside medical professionals (MDs/NDs) where the revenue is often shared or based on a referral partnership within the clinic's billing structure.
