Nutritional Ketosis

A metabolic state where the liver produces ketone bodies (0.5 to 3.0 mmol/L) from fat to be used as the primary fuel source instead of glucose.

Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)

The primary ketone body measured in the blood; it acts as both a fuel source and a signaling molecule that reduces inflammation.

Physiological Insulin Resistance

A benign adaptation to long-term ketosis where muscles spare glucose for the brain; distinct from pathological insulin resistance.

Oxidation of Fatty Acids

The process of breaking down fatty acids to produce energy, which becomes highly efficient in fat-adapted individuals.

The Physiology of Nutritional Ketosis

Nutritional ketosis is an evolutionary survival mechanism that allows the human body to thrive during periods of carbohydrate scarcity. For a client with Type 2 Diabetes (T2D), this shift is more than just a survival mechanism; it is a metabolic bypass. By reducing carbohydrate intake to <50g (Keto) or near-zero (Carnivore), we force the downregulation of insulin secretion.

When insulin levels drop, the body releases the "brake" on lipolysis, allowing stored body fat to enter the bloodstream as free fatty acids. These are converted by the liver into ketone bodies: Acetoacetate, Acetone, and Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB). BHB is particularly relevant for practitioners as it has been shown to inhibit the NLRP3 inflammasome, directly reducing the systemic inflammation associated with insulin resistance.

Keto: Mechanisms of Glucose Control

The Ketogenic diet works through several synergistic pathways. First, it targets Postprandial Glycemia. Without significant glucose influx, the "rollercoaster" spikes that damage the vascular endothelium are eliminated. Second, it improves Insulin Sensitivity. High levels of circulating insulin (hyperinsulinemia) lead to receptor downregulation. By keeping insulin low, we allow receptors to "resensitize."

A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 randomized controlled trials (n=8,234) found that ketogenic diets were significantly more effective than low-fat diets for weight loss and glycemic control in T2D patients, with an average weight loss of 7.5kg and an HbA1c reduction of 1.2% over 6 months (p < 0.001).

Practitioner Note: The Dawn Phenomenon

Clients on Keto may still see high fasting glucose due to the "Dawn Phenomenon" (cortisol-driven gluconeogenesis). However, their Time in Range (TIR) usually improves dramatically. Always prioritize TIR and HbA1c over a single fasting morning measurement.

Carnivore: The Ultimate Elimination Diet

While the Ketogenic diet includes low-carb vegetables, the Carnivore Diet (Zero-Carb) removes all plant matter. This is often used as a therapeutic intervention for clients with "brittle" diabetes or those with concurrent autoimmune issues (e.g., Hashimoto's or Rheumatoid Arthritis).

The Carnivore diet simplifies the Evolutionary-Based Nutrition (the 'E' in STABLE) principle to its core. By removing fiber, lectins, and oxalates, it minimizes gut-derived inflammation that can trigger cortisol spikes and subsequent hyperglycemia. From a glycemic perspective, the Carnivore diet provides the most stable blood sugar profile possible, as glucose is produced only via demand-driven gluconeogenesis from protein, rather than supply-driven absorption from carbohydrates.

Therapeutic Application for T2D

In practice, these diets require Tactical Tracking (the 'T' in STABLE). For a diabetic client, the transition into ketosis is a high-risk, high-reward period. As the body stops retaining sodium (the "natriuresis of fasting"), blood pressure often drops rapidly. If the client is on antihypertensive or glucose-lowering medications (like SGLT2 inhibitors or Insulin), they must be monitored daily to avoid hypotension or hypoglycemia.

Safety, Monitoring & Electrolytes

The most common failure point for these interventions is electrolyte mismanagement. Insulin tells the kidneys to store sodium. When insulin drops, the kidneys excrete sodium, potassium, and magnesium. This leads to the "Keto Flu": headaches, heart palpitations, and muscle cramps.

Integration with the STABLE Method‚Ñ¢

How do we fit these diets into our coaching framework? It starts with Stabilization (S). A Keto diet is the most effective tool for flattening the glucose curve. We then use Tactical Tracking (T) to prove it to the client. Seeing a flat line on a CGM after a ribeye steak versus a spike after "healthy" oatmeal is the most powerful psychological tool for compliance.

Finally, we emphasize Biological Synergy (B). By lowering insulin, we naturally balance other hormones. Leptin sensitivity improves, meaning the client finally feels full. Cortisol stabilizes as the body stops panicking from hypoglycemic "crashes." This holistic alignment is why VLC diets are considered a "Metabolic Reset."

Why does the Carnivore diet often result in a "flatter" glucose line than a standard Ketogenic diet containing fiber-rich vegetables?

The Carnivore diet eliminates all dietary carbohydrates, relying solely on demand-driven gluconeogenesis. It also removes plant-based antinutrients and fibers that can, in some sensitive individuals, cause gut inflammation or small glucose rises, leading to near-zero postprandial variability.
2

What is the primary physiological reason for the "Keto Flu," and how is it addressed in the STABLE Method?

The "Keto Flu" is caused by the natriuresis of fasting‚Äîthe rapid excretion of sodium and water by the kidneys as insulin levels drop. It is addressed by increasing sodium, potassium, and magnesium intake to maintain Biological Synergy (B) and prevent dehydration.

Key Takeaways

  • Metabolic Bypass: VLC diets bypass the insulin-resistant glucose pathway by switching to fat and ketone metabolism.
  • Therapeutic Efficacy: Research shows up to 60% reversal of T2D and massive reductions in medication reliance.
  • Electrolytes are Mandatory: Sodium and magnesium supplementation is not optional during the adaptation phase.
  • Monitoring: Practitioners must use CGMs to track the stabilization of glycemic variability in real-time.
  • Customization: Use Carnivore for extreme cases (autoimmune/severe IR) and Keto for general metabolic health.
