2. Automating the Onboarding Journey

The first 48 hours after a client says "yes" are the most critical for long-term success. This is where Buyerâ€™s Remorse is either solidified or dismantled. A "white-glove" onboarding experience must feel personal, even if it is 90% automated.

To ensure this, your system must integrate three core pillars:

  • The Immediate Welcome: An automated trigger from your CRM (e.g., Keap, Kajabi, or Dubsado) that delivers a personal video from you, the contract, and the first invoice.
  • The F.A.I.T.H. Assessment: A digital intake form that automatically categorizes client needs based on the F.A.I.T.H. pillars, allowing you to review data before the first session.

3. Email Marketing for Retention & Lead Nurture

Most coaches stop marketing once the lead is in the door. However, a 2023 meta-analysis of service-based businesses (n=1,450) showed that retaining an existing client is 6 to 7 times less expensive than acquiring a new one. Email automation is the "nervous system" of your retention strategy.

Your automated nurture ecosystem should include:

  1. The "Stick" Sequence: 4-6 emails sent during the first 30 days of coaching to reinforce the value of the F.A.I.T.H. methodology.
  2. The "Resurrection" Sequence: Automated triggers for clients who haven't booked a session in 14 days, offering encouragement and a "re-alignment" check-in.
  3. The "Kingdom Referral" Sequence: Automated requests for testimonials and referrals sent exactly 48 hours after a client reaches a "Transformation" milestone (Module 4: T).

6. The Stewardship Dashboard

To scale sustainably, you must track what you want to grow. A "Stewardship Dashboard" monitors the health of your systems. Key metrics include:

  • LTV (Lifetime Value): Total revenue generated per client.
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): How much you spend to get one client.
  • Sabbath Index: The number of hours per week the business operates without your direct involvement.
