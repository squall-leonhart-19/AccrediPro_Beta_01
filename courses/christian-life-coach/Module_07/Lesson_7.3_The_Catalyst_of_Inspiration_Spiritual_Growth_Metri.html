4. Measuring the 'Intangibles': Spiritual Maturity Metrics

How do we measure "inspiration" or "spiritual growth"? While secular metrics focus on ROI (Return on Investment), we focus on ROSG (Return on Spiritual Growth). We must track the "intangibles" to ensure the coaching is actually facilitating a deeper relationship with Christ, not just better time management.

Key metrics for spiritual maturity include:

  • The Anxiety-to-Trust Ratio: Measuring the frequency of "peace in the storm" vs. "panic in the storm" over a 30-day period.
  • Discipline Consistency: Tracking the "Spiritual Disciplines" (prayer, scripture, fasting) as catalysts for the Inspire phase.
  • Relational Reconciliation: The ability to forgive and seek peace, which is a primary indicator of spiritual health (Colossians 3:13).
  • Obedience Latency: The time elapsed between a client sensing Godâ€™s leading and taking the first step of action.

5. Inspirational Questioning: The Socratic Method Redefined

Inspirational questioning is the art of asking questions that the Holy Spirit can use to "pierce the heart" (Acts 2:37). Unlike standard coaching questions that seek information, inspirational questions seek revelation. They are designed to expose the gap between a client's current reality and God's promised future.

Examples of Inspirational Questions in the F.A.I.T.H. Framework:

  • "If you knew God could not fail in this situation, what would be your very next move?"
  • "What is the 'giant' in your life that is currently making God look small?"
  • "In this struggle, what is the Holy Spirit trying to produce in you, rather than just remove from you?"
  • "Which attribute of God's character is the most difficult for you to believe right now?"

The ultimate metric of the "Inspire" phase is the manifestation of the Fruit of the Spirit.
