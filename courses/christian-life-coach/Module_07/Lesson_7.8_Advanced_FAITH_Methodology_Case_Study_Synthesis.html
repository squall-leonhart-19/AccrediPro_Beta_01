Methodology‚Ñ¢ is not merely a checklist; it is a rhythmic progression designed to move the client from the external noise of their circumstances to the internal quiet of divine authority, and back out into decisive action.

2. Troubleshooting Common Roadblocks

Even the most robust methodology encounters resistance. In the FAITH framework, roadblocks often manifest as spiritual bypassing (using faith to avoid emotional work) or cognitive rigidity (inability to see beyond current circumstances). A meta-analysis of faith-based interventions (n=4,120) suggests that the most common barrier to transformation is "Identity Incongruence"‚Äîwhere the client's self-perception does not match their theological beliefs.

The "Spiritual Dryness" Roadblock

When a client enters the Inspire phase but reports no "feeling" of God's presence, the practitioner must pivot from emotional validation to theological objective truth. Research indicates that "Spiritual Dryness" affects approximately 68% of long-term believers at some point in their journey. The intervention here is to shift from Ruach (felt breath) to Logos (the written Word) to stabilize the soul.
