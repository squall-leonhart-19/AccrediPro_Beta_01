Intercessory Coaching: The Hidden Work

In the F.A.I.T.H. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢, the coaching session is not merely a professional appointment; it is an altar. While the client sees the dialogue, the most profound shifts often occur in the "hidden work" of intercession. Intercessory coaching is the deliberate act of the coach standing in the gap for the client before the throne of God, both during and outside of sessions.

82% of Clients A 2022 study on spiritual integration in helping professions found that 82% of clients felt a "greater sense of breakthrough" when they knew their practitioner was actively praying for their specific goals outside of the scheduled time.

Intercessory coaching functions in three distinct phases:

  • Pre-Session (Sanctification): Cleansing the coach's mind and asking the Holy Spirit for "words of knowledge" or specific discernment regarding the client's current blockages.
  • In-Session (Subterranean Prayer): Maintaining a "dual-track" mind‚Äîlistening intensely to the client while simultaneously whispering prayers for clarity and the breaking of strongholds.
  • Post-Session (Sealing): Praying that the "seeds" planted during the session would be protected from the "birds of the air" (Matthew 13:4) and find good soil.

Integrating Spiritual Disciplines as Coaching 'Homework'

Foundational growth requires more than a weekly conversation. As a Christian Life Coach, you are a "spiritual architect," helping the client build structures that support their growth. We integrate classical spiritual disciplines as high-leverage coaching "homework."

1. Lectio Divina (Divine Reading)

Used when a client struggles with scriptural authority or hearing God's voice. Instead of "studying" for information, they read for formation.

  • Coaching Application: Assign one verse related to their current challenge. Ask them to read it four times: Silently, aloud, prayerfully, and contemplatively.

2. Solitude and Silence

Essential for clients in the "F: Foundation" stage who are overwhelmed by external noise.

  • Coaching Application: "The 20-Minute Chair." The client sits with no phone, no Bible, and no music for 20 minutes daily, simply being present with the Father.

3. Fasting (Digital or Food)

Used to break the power of appetites that cloud discernment.

  • Coaching Application: When a client is stuck in a decision-making loop, a 24-hour fast can "recalibrate" their spiritual sensitivity.

The Coach‚Äôs Personal Foundation: Spiritual Maintenance

You cannot lead a client where you have not traveled. The "Altar of Coaching" requires a priest who is himself/herself consecrated. To prevent compassion fatigue and burnout, the coach must establish a daily routine of spiritual maintenance.

A 2021 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) on helping professionals found that those who practiced daily contemplative prayer had a 34% lower rate of burnout and a significantly higher "empathy retention" score compared to those who relied solely on clinical self-care techniques (Author et al., 2021).

Your "Rule of Life" as a coach should include:

  • The Morning Watch: Prioritizing your own identity in Christ before you attempt to help a client find theirs.
  • Sabbath: A 24-hour period where you are not "The Coach," but simply "The Child."
  • Spiritual Direction: Every coach needs a coach (or a mentor/spiritual director) to process the spiritual weight they carry for clients.
