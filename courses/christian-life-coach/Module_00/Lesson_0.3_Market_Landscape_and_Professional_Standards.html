5. Risk Management: The Legal Landscape

As a Christian Life Coach, you are a professional practitioner, not just a "friend who gives advice." This distinction carries legal weight. Failure to manage risk can lead to professional liability, even in a ministry context.

Key Risk Areas for the Faith-Based Coach:

  • The "Coaching vs. Counseling" Boundary: Explicitly stating in your contract that coaching is not a substitute for clinical therapy or psychiatric care.
  • Mandatory Reporting: Understanding that while coaching is confidential, laws regarding child abuse, elder abuse, or threats of self-harm still apply.
  • Professional Liability Insurance: A critical requirement for any practitioner to protect against claims of negligence or "malpractice" in advice.
  • Data Privacy (GDPR/HIPAA): Ensuring client notes and session recordings are stored on secure, encrypted platforms.
