The "I" in our framework stands for Inspiration, specifically referring to the Inspiration of the Holy Spirit. Case Study: The Pivot Point

Client: Michael (Age 48)

Presenting Issue: Mid-career burnout and lack of direction.

Intervention: Instead of standard "career mapping," the coach used the Inspiration phase to lead Michael through a "listening prayer" exercise during the session, focusing on identifying where he felt the Spirit's "peace" (Colossians 3:15).

Outcome: Michael identified that his burnout wasn't from overwork, but from misaligned work. He transitioned to a non-profit leadership role, reporting a significant reduction in cortisol-related stress symptoms within 90 days.

Check Your Understanding

1. How does the F.A.I.T.H. Method distinguish between "Transformation" and simple habit change?

Transformation (T) in this framework involves both the spiritual "renewal of the mind" (Romans 12:2) and the neurobiological process of neuroplasticity, aiming for a change in heart and identity, not just outward behavior.

The primary anchor is the objective authority of Scripture, ensuring coaching goals are biblically sound rather than just personally desirable.
