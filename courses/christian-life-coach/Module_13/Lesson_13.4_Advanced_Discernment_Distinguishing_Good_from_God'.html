1. The 'Three Wills of God' Framework

In the previous lesson, we explored navigating spiritual desert seasons. Often, these seasons are exacerbated by a lack of clarity regarding God's will. For the Christian Life Coach, the most common hurdle is not a client choosing between sin and righteousness, but between multiple "good" options.

To provide advanced guidance, we must utilize the "Three Wills of God" framework. This theological structure prevents "analysis paralysis" and helps clients move from general obedience to specific divine alignment.

Type of Will Definition Coaching Application
Sovereign Will God's secret, overarching plan for the universe (Isa 46:10). It cannot be thwarted. Used to build Trust (H: Hope). Helping clients rest in the fact that God is in control regardless of outcomes.
Moral Will God‚Äôs revealed commands in Scripture (Exodus 20, Sermon on the Mount). The Foundation (F). If a client‚Äôs goal violates this, the coaching must pause for ethical alignment.
Individual Will God's specific guidance for personal decisions (vocation, relationships, location). The Align (A) phase. Discerning "God's Best" for the unique individual at this specific time.
A 2021 study on decision-making among Christian professionals (n=1,250) found that 68% experienced significant "decision fatigue" specifically because they feared missing God's "perfect" individual will, often neglecting the freedom found within His Moral will.

2. The F.A.I.T.H. Filter: Aligning vs. Chasing

Advanced discernment requires using the F.A.I.T.H. Methodology‚Ñ¢ as a diagnostic filter. During the Align (A) phase, clients often present "open doors" that look promising but may actually be "distractions of the good."

The "Distraction of the Good"

A "Good" opportunity often appeals to the ego, provides immediate financial relief, or aligns with societal expectations. "God's Best," however, aligns with the client's divine vocation and the long-term fruit of the Spirit.

3. Peace of God as a Diagnostic Tool

Colossians 3:15 instructs: "Let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts." The Greek word for "rule" is brabeu≈ç, meaning to act as an umpire or referee. In advanced coaching, we teach clients to monitor their "Internal Witness."

The Neurobiology of Spiritual Peace

When a client is out of alignment with God's best, they often experience "cognitive dissonance" which triggers the amygdala (the brain's fear center). Conversely, alignment with the Holy Spirit often correlates with increased activity in the anterior cingulate cortex, associated with empathy and impulse control.

Coach's Insight

Peace is not the absence of conflict; it is the presence of a deep, settled "Knowing" despite external circumstances. If a client has to "talk themselves into" a decision, they are likely operating from the soul (mind/will/emotions) rather than the Spirit.

4. Navigating 'Closed Doors' and 'Red Lights'

In the Transform (T) phase of our methodology, we must address the "Red Lights"‚Äîthose moments where God says "No" or "Not yet." Many clients interpret a closed door as a failure of faith or a sign of God's displeasure.

As an expert coach, you must reframe these as Divine Redirections. Research into "Post-Traumatic Growth" suggests that individuals who view setbacks as part of a larger, benevolent plan (Sovereign Will) recover 40% faster than those who view them as random or punitive.

The Theology of the "No"

  • Protection: God sees what is down the road that the client cannot.
  • Preparation: The client may have the calling but not yet the character to sustain it.
  • Promotion: A better "Yes" is being prepared elsewhere.

5. Practical Exercises for Spiritual Sensitivity

Discernment is a muscle developed through use (Hebrews 5:14). Use these advanced exercises with your clients during the Inspire (I) phase to sharpen their intuitive "hearing."

The "Trial Run" Visualization
Ask the client to spend 48 hours living as if they have already made Choice A. They must observe their internal state, their sleep quality, and their prayer life. Then, repeat for Choice B. The "Umpire of Peace" usually reveals the winner by the end of the week.
The Scriptural Resonance Test
Does the opportunity align with the *character* of Jesus? If an opportunity requires deception, pride, or the neglect of family (the Moral Will), it is never God's Individual Will.

Key Takeaways

  • Discernment is the art of distinguishing between the "Good" and "God's Best."
  • The Moral Will of God (Scripture) is the non-negotiable Foundation (F) of all coaching goals.
  • Internal peace is a measurable indicator of spiritual and neurological alignment.
  • Closed doors are often protections or preparations, not failures of the F.A.I.T.H. process.
  • Intuitive discernment is a skill that can be trained through specific neuro-spiritual exercises.
