1. The Anatomy of Biblical Values

In previous lessons, we identified the client's spiritual gifts and god-given passions. However, gifts without values are dangerous. While gifts define the "what" of a person's calling, values define the "how." In the F.A.I.T.H. Coaching Methodâ„¢, the Align phase requires that a client's daily conduct matches their eternal identity.

Biblical values are not merely moral suggestions; they are the character of Christ manifested in the believer. We primarily derive these from two foundational New Testament passages:

The Fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23)

These represent the internal disposition of the believer. When a client identifies "Patience" or "Self-Control" as a core value, they are aligning with the active work of the Holy Spirit rather than just a personality trait.

The Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3-12)

These represent the external ethics of the Kingdom. Values such as "Mercy," "Purity of Heart," and "Peacemaking" provide a counter-cultural framework for how a Christian interacts with a competitive, often hostile world.

A 2022 study on workplace ethics (n=3,400) found that employees who perceived a "values-match" with their work reported 67% higher job satisfaction and 42% lower rates of burnout compared to those in "values-conflict."

2. Values as Guardrails of Alignment

Values act as "Guardrails." Just as guardrails on a mountain road do not tell the driver where to go, they do prevent the driver from plummeting off the cliff during a turn. In coaching, when a client faces a major decision, their values should narrow the field of acceptable choices.

Secular Success Metric Biblical Value Counterpart The "Guardrail" Effect
Efficiency at all costs Sabbath / Rest (Exodus 20:8) Prevents burnout and idolatry of work.
Market Dominance Servant Leadership (Mark 10:43-45) Prevents the exploitation of others for gain.
Personal Branding Humility / Christ-Centricity (Phil 2:3) Prevents pride and maintains focus on God's glory.
Strategic Ambiguity Integrity / Truth (Matthew 5:37) Prevents deception and builds long-term trust.

3. The 'Values Conflict' Assessment

As a Christian Life Coach, you will often encounter clients who are "successful" by worldly standards but feel a deep, internal unrest. This is frequently due to Cognitive Dissonanceâ€”the mental discomfort experienced by a person who holds two or more contradictory beliefs, ideas, or values.

The Assessment Process:

  1. Identification: Have the client list their top 5 biblical values (e.g., Integrity, Family, Generosity, Excellence, Peace).
  2. Audit: Review the client's calendar and bank statement from the last 30 days.
  3. Gap Analysis: Where does the allocation of time and money contradict the stated values?
  4. The "Cost" Question: What is the spiritual and emotional cost of maintaining this misalignment?

4. Scriptural Mandates vs. Personal Preferences

A critical skill for the coach is helping the client distinguish between Non-Negotiables (Biblical Mandates) and Preferences (Personal Liberty). Misidentifying a preference as a mandate can lead to legalism; misidentifying a mandate as a preference leads to compromise.

According to a 2021 meta-analysis on religious coping, individuals who can clearly articulate their "moral absolutes" show 31% higher resilience in high-stress environments because their "Internal Locus of Control" is rooted in unchanging scripture rather than shifting circumstances.

The Mandate Test

Does the Bible explicitly command or forbid this? (e.g., "Do not bear false witness"). If yes, this is a non-negotiable guardrail for alignment.

The Wisdom/Preference Test

Is this a matter of stewardship or season? (e.g., "Should I take this high-paying job or stay in ministry?"). This requires discernment of Passions and Gifts (covered in Lessons 2 & 3).

5. Ethics in Modern Professional Living

In the modern marketplace, Christian ethics are often tested in the "Gray Areas." As a coach, you guide the client to move from "What is legal?" to "What is honoring to Christ?"

Consider the Ethical Triad of the Christian Professional:

  • Transparency: Is there anything I am hiding that would change the decision of the other party?
  • Justice: Am I profiting from someone else's unfair disadvantage?
  • Stewardship: Am I using these resources (time, talent, money) in a way that I can joyfully report to the Master? (Matthew 25:14-30).

Key Takeaways
