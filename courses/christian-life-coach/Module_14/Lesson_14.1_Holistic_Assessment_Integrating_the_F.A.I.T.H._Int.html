4. Establishing the Biblical Baseline

You cannot coach a client toward a "Renewed Mind" (Romans 12:2) if you do not know the current state of their "Software" (Scripture). Assessing the Biblical Baseline is not a test of intelligence, but an assessment of Scriptural Literacy and Spiritual Rhythms.

A 2019 study by the Center for Bible Engagement (n=40,000) found that individuals who engage with the Bible 4 or more times a week are 228% more likely to share their faith and 59% less likely to struggle with destructive habits. Your intake should quantify this:

  • Frequency: "How many days per week do you engage in deep scriptural study (not just reading a verse)?"
  • Application: "Can you name one scripture that has governed a major decision in the last 90 days?"
  • Prayer Depth: "Is your prayer life primarily transactional (asking) or relational (listening)?"

5. Christ-Centered SMART Goals

Standard SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) are effective for worldly success, but they often lack eternal significance. In this deep dive, we introduce the Kingdom-Aligned SMART Goal.

A Kingdom-Aligned goal must answer: "How does the achievement of this goal further the Great Commission or demonstrate the Fruit of the Spirit?"

Standard SMART Goal Kingdom-Aligned SMART Goal The "Faith" Shift
"I want to lose 20 lbs in 3 months to look better." "I will steward my body (God's temple) to increase my energy for ministry by losing 20 lbs." Shifts from Vanity to Stewardship.
"I want to earn a $10k promotion this year." "I will excel in my vocation to provide more for my family and increase my Kingdom giving by $5k." Shifts from Accumulation to Generosity.

Key Takeaways

  • The Foundation (F) assessment determines if the client is building their life on scriptural authority or personal preference.
  • Intake is a diagnostic for Strongholds; look for the "Root of Belief" behind the presenting problem.
  • A Biblical Baseline is essential for measuring the "Renewing of the Mind" (Transformation) later in the process.
  • Goals must be Kingdom-Aligned, moving the focus from self-actualization to God-glorification.
  • Double-Listening allows the Holy Spirit to guide the diagnostic process, uncovering hidden "Heart Issues" quickly.
