3. The Prioritization Matrix: Strongholds vs. Growth

Not all issues identified in the intake are created equal. A common mistake in treatment planning is trying to fix everything at once. We utilize a Prioritization Matrix to determine the sequence of intervention.

Category Definition Biblical Context Intervention Timing
Strongholds Deep-seated cognitive/spiritual patterns that block growth. 2 Corinthians 10:4 Immediate: Must be dismantled before new growth can take root.
Skill Deficits Lack of practical knowledge or behavioral habits. Proverbs 1:5 Mid-Term: Addressed through coaching and training.
Alignment Gaps Environmental or vocational mismatches. Ephesians 2:10 Ongoing: Requires strategic life-restructuring.
Spiritual Plateaus Seasons of dryness or lack of inspiration. Psalm 42:1 Long-Term: Addressed through spiritual disciplines (I - Inspire).

4. Risk Assessment: Red Flags and Referrals

As a Christian Life Coach, maintaining professional integrity means knowing when a client's needs exceed the scope of coaching. During synthesis, we look for Clinical Red Flags. A 2023 study published in the Journal of Psychology and Christianity noted that 18% of clients seeking pastoral or life coaching actually met the criteria for clinical depression or GAD (General Anxiety Disorder).

Clinical Red Flags

Suicidal Ideation

Any mention of self-harm or "not wanting to be here" requires immediate referral to a licensed mental health professional or emergency services.

Severe Trauma/PTSD

If intake data reveals "flashbacks," "disassociation," or "unprocessed severe abuse," the strategy must involve clinical therapy alongside or before coaching.

Addiction

Active substance abuse or severe behavioral addictions (pornography, gambling) often require specialized recovery programs.

5. Case Formulation: The Narrative Thread

The final step of synthesis is Case Formulation. This is a 2-3 paragraph summary that connects the clientâ€™s past history, current struggles, and future hope into a single story. This narrative serves as the "North Star" for every session moving forward.

A high-quality case formulation answers three questions:

  1. What happened? (The historical context)
  2. Why is it happening now? (The current trigger/gap)
  3. What is God doing? (The redemptive trajectory)
