2. The 72-Hour Stabilization Protocol

When a client enters a state of crisis, the coaching agenda (Align, Inspire, Transform) must be suspended. We move immediately into Stabilization Mode. The goal of the first 72 hours is not "growth"; it is the restoration of the Foundation (F).

Research indicates that immediate emotional stabilization within the first 72 hours of a traumatic event significantly reduces the long-term incidence of post-traumatic stress symptoms (Roberts, 2005).

The 72-Hour Protocol follows four specific steps:

1. Physiological Regulation

The coach guides the client in breathing and grounding exercises to move the brain from the sympathetic (fight/flight) to the parasympathetic (rest/digest) nervous system. Without this, scriptural truth cannot be cognitively processed.

2. Environmental Safety

Assess the client's physical surroundings. Are they alone? Do they have food? Are they in a safe location? We establish a "Sanctuary Space."

3. Emotional Ventilation

Allowing the client to "pour out" their heart without judgment. This is the Ruach (breath/spirit) work of the F.A.I.T.H. method.

4. Spiritual Anchor

Providing a single, "low-complexity" scriptural anchor (e.g., Psalm 46:1). In crisis, the brain cannot handle complex theology.

6. Immediate Intervention Steps (The Triage Flow)

When the phone rings and a client is in the midst of an acute event, follow this flow:

  1. Listen for Safety: Are they safe? Are they thinking of self-harm?
  2. Validate the Reality: "What you are feeling makes sense given what has happened." (Validation reduces cortisol).
  3. Restrict the Timeline: In crisis, the future is terrifying. Bring them back to the next 15 minutes. "We aren't going to solve the next year today. We are just going to focus on the next hour."
  4. The Breath of Life: Use a "Box Breathing" technique while reciting a short scripture (e.g., "Be still... and know...").
  5. Actionable Anchor: Give them one small, physical task (e.g., "Drink a glass of water and call your sister").

Check Your Understanding

1. A client discovers their business partner has embezzled all their funds. They are shaking, crying, and saying "God has abandoned me." What is your first priority according to the 72-Hour Protocol?

Physiological Regulation. Before addressing the theology of "God abandoning them," you must help the client regulate their nervous system (breathing/grounding) so their prefrontal cortex can engage with truth.

2. What is the primary difference between a "Life Crisis" and a "Clinical Emergency"?

The primary difference is safety and reality orientation. A clinical emergency involves a threat to life (suicide/homicide) or a break from reality (psychosis), requiring immediate psychiatric referral.
