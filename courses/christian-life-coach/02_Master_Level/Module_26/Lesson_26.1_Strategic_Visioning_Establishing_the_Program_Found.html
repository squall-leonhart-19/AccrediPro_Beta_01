2. Exegetical Mission Crafting

A Christian coaching program must be built on more than just "good ideas"; it must be anchored in Biblical Exegesis. At the Level 3 level, your program‚Äôs "Foundation" (the F in F.A.I.T.H.) is its Mission Statement. This is not a marketing slogan, but a theological declaration of the program‚Äôs purpose.

When developing your foundation, you must move beyond topical verses and engage in a deep study of the scriptural mandates relevant to your niche. For example, if your program focuses on leadership burnout, your foundation might be rooted in the Jethro Principle (Exodus 18) or the Sabbath Rest (Hebrews 4). Your mission statement should answer: What is the specific biblical restoration this program facilitates?

The "Habakkuk 2:2" Protocol

"Write the vision and make it plain on tablets, that he may run who reads it." Your program foundation must be:

  • Theologically Sound: Rooted in original context and sound doctrine.
  • Outcome-Oriented: Clearly defining the "metanoia" (transformation) expected.
  • Vocationally Aligned: Reflecting your unique calling as a Master Coach.

5. Niche Specialization: From Generalist to Expert

In the L3 Program Development phase, Niche Specialization is not a limitation; it is an amplification. A generalist is viewed as a commodity; a specialist with a programmatic solution is viewed as an authority. In the Christian context, this is the difference between being "a guy who knows the Bible" and "the expert on Biblical Grief Recovery for Widowed Mothers."

The "Expertise Equation" for your foundation is:
(Specific Pain Point) + (Biblical Exegesis) + (F.A.I.T.H. Framework) = Authority.

6. Resource & IP Auditing

Before launching a program, you must audit your "storehouse." This involves identifying your Intellectual Property (IP)‚Äîthe unique ways you explain concepts, the worksheets you've designed, and the metaphors you use. In the L3 context, your IP is a stewardship of the wisdom God has given you through experience.

Your audit should cover three domains:

  1. Spiritual Assets: What spiritual gifts (Romans 12:6-8) fuel this program? (e.g., Teaching, Encouragement, Discernment).
  2. Intellectual Assets: What frameworks or methods have you developed that consistently yield results?
  3. Technical Assets: What tools (LMS, assessments, CRM) are required to deliver a premium, professional experience?

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of Strategic Visioning concepts.

1. What is the primary difference between a "session" and a "program" in Level 3 Coaching?

A session is a reactive, one-off interaction addressing immediate needs. A program is a proactive, structured, time-bound journey designed to move a specific demographic from a defined struggle to a biblical breakthrough using a systematic framework.

2. Why is "Biblical Exegesis" required for a program's mission statement?

It ensures the program is anchored in the original intent and sound doctrine of Scripture rather than just topical "proof-texting," providing the theological integrity necessary for deep spiritual transformation.
