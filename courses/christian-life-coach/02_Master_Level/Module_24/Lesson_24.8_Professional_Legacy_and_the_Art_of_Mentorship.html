4. Building a Sustainable Legacy Practice

Legacy is the architecture of impact that remains when the practitioner steps away. A "Legacy Practice" shifts from a solopreneur model to an institutional model.

The Pillars of Legacy Architecture:

  1. Intellectual Property (IP): Codifying your proprietary niche into workbooks, digital courses, or certification tracks.
  2. Succession Planning: Identifying "Timothy-style" successors who can carry the mantle of your specific methodology.
  3. Kingdom Diversification: Creating sustainable revenue models (memberships, corporate faith-based wellness, etc.) that fund pro-bono work for underserved demographics.
