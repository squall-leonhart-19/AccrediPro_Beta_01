4. Advanced Auditory Masking: Pink and Brown Noise

White noise is the industry standard, but for N3 (Slow Wave Sleep) enhancement, it is often suboptimal. White noise contains equal intensity at all frequencies, which can be perceived as "harsh" by the auditory cortex. Master coaches utilize Pink Noise (which mimics the 1/f power spectrum found in nature) or Brown Noise (deeper, bass-heavy frequencies).

The mechanism here is "Acoustic Stimulation." Studies have shown that playing pink noise in sync with the rising phase of slow-wave oscillations can actually increase the amplitude of those waves, effectively "deepening" the sleep. This is particularly useful for clients over age 50, who naturally experience a decline in slow-wave amplitude.
