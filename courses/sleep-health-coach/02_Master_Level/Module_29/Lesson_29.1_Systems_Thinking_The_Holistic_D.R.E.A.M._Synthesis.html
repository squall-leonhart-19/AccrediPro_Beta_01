Case Study 1: The Corporate Executive Paradox

Client: Marcus, 48 | Symptom: Maintenance Insomnia (3 AM Awakenings)

Initial Assessment: Marcus had an "optimized" environment (Oura ring, chiliPAD, blackout curtains). He followed strict caffeine limits. However, he remained wired at night.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a "perfect" sleep environment (E) fail to improve sleep for a client with high allostatic load (A)?

High allostatic load (chronic stress) keeps the HPA axis hyper-active, maintaining a high core body temperature and sympathetic dominance. This "internal environment" overrides the external cooling and darkness, preventing the physiological transition into sleep.

2. What is the "Prioritization Logic" for a client with both Circadian Dysregulation (R) and Sleep Apnea symptoms (D)?

Safety and Diagnosis (D) always come first. You must refer for clinical screening of Sleep Apnea before attempting to "Reset" the rhythm, as the apnea is a physiological barrier that can make circadian interventions ineffective or even dangerous if fatigue is masked.
