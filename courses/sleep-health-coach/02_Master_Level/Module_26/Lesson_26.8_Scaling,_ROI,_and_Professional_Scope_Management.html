2. Quantifying Success: The Program Efficacy Score (PES)

To scale a coaching practice or sell into corporate environments, "feeling better" is insufficient data. You must calculate a Program Efficacy Score (PES). This proprietary metric combines objective wearable data with validated subjective assessments.

The formula used by elite AccrediPro practitioners is a weighted average of three key variables:

  1. Sleep Efficiency (SE) Improvement: (Post-Program SE - Pre-Program SE).
  2. Insomnia Severity Index (ISI) Reduction: Percentage decrease in clinical scores.
  3. Subjective Vitality Scale (SVS): Daily energy ratings (1-10).

3. Building the Multi-Disciplinary Referral Network

A Level 3 Sleep Health Coach is the "Quarterback" of the sleep care team, not the sole player. Scaling your impact requires a trusted Multi-Disciplinary Team (MDT). This network ensures that when a client presents with issues beyond your scope (e.g., severe Sleep Apnea or clinical depression), they are transitioned smoothly rather than abandoned.

Your referral network should include:

  • Board-Certified Sleep Physicians: For PSG (Polysomnography) and OSA diagnosis.
  • CBT-I Therapists: For complex psychophysiological insomnia requiring clinical psychology.
  • Functional Nutritionists/RDs: For deep-dive metabolic testing (DUTCH, GI-MAP) that informs the "Address Root Causes" (A) pillar.
  • Biological Dentists: For mandibular advancement devices or airway assessments.

4. Legal Scope & Ethical Boundaries

As you advance to program development, the temptation to "prescribe" or "diagnose" increases. This is a significant legal risk. Level 3 coaching operates in the realm of Functional Education and Behavioral Design.

The "Identify, Don't Diagnose" Rule

A coach may identify that a client's data suggests a high probability of Sleep Apnea (e.g., oxygen desaturations on a wearable + snoring) and refer them to a physician. A coach may not state, "You have Sleep Apnea."

When addressing "Root Causes" (Module 4), your role is to educate on the mechanisms of the HPA axis or glucose dysregulation and suggest lifestyle adaptations. If the client requires pharmaceutical intervention or clinical hormone replacement, your program must defer to their medical provider.

2. How does a "Train the Trainer" model differ from standard group coaching?

Standard group coaching involves the lead coach working with many clients. "Train the Trainer" involves the lead coach teaching the methodology to other facilitators, who then coach the end-users, allowing for exponential scaling.
