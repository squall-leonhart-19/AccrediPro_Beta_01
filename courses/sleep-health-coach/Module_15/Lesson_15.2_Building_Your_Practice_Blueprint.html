Legal Scope & Ethical Boundaries

As an expert Sleep Health Coach, your role is educational and behavioral, not medical. It is critical to differentiate your practice from that of a Sleep Physician or Polysomnographic Technologist.

The "Red Flag" Protocol

If a client demonstrates any of the following, you must refer them to a medical professional before proceeding with coaching:

  • Observed apneas (gasping/choking in sleep).
  • Sudden onset of narcoleptic symptoms.
  • Severe, chronic insomnia that does not respond to behavioral hygiene (potential underlying pathology).
  • Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS) causing physical pain.

Your "Practice Blueprint" must include a signed Client Agreement (available in your DFY Accelerator) that explicitly states you are not a doctor and are not providing medical treatment. This protects both you and the client, establishing a professional boundary that actually increases your credibility.
