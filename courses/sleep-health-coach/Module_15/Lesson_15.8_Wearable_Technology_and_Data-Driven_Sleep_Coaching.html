1. The Hierarchy of Evidence: PSG vs. Actigraphy vs. Wearables

In the world of sleep science, not all data is created equal. As a Sleep Health Coach, you must navigate the "Hierarchy of Evidence" to understand the weight you should give to specific data points. While Polysomnography (PSG) remains the gold standard, it is a "snapshot" in time, often plagued by the "first-night effect."

A 2023 systematic review published in Sleep Medicine Reviews found that while consumer wearables have improved, they still show a mean absolute percentage error (MAPE) of 12% to 18% for total sleep time compared to PSG.

2. Interpreting Sleep Stages: The Accuracy Gap

Most clients will come to you obsessed with their "Deep" or "REM" sleep percentages. It is critical to manage expectations here. Consumer devices use Photoplethysmography (PPG) to estimate heart rate and heart rate variability, which are then used as proxies for sleep stages. However, autonomic nervous system activity does not always perfectly correlate with cortical brain waves (EEG).

Research suggests that while wearables are increasingly accurate at detecting sleep vs. wake (often >90% sensitivity), their ability to differentiate between Light, Deep, and REM stages is significantly lower, often ranging between 60% and 75% accuracy. As a coach, you should focus on longitudinal trends rather than nightly specifics.

4. Using Biofeedback to Validate the 'A' (Address Root Causes)

The true power of wearables lies in validating the Address Root Causes pillar of our framework. By tracking Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and Resting Heart Rate (RHR), we can see real-time evidence of how lifestyle changes impact the nervous system.

Specifically, look for:

  • HRV Recovery: Does a 5-minute evening meditation increase the following night's HRV?
  • Metabolic Impact: How does eating a heavy meal 1 hour before bed (violating the 'M' - Master Sleep Hygiene) impact the RHR "hammock" curve?
  • Thermal Regulation: Does a 1-degree drop in room temperature (the 'E' - Environment) correlate with higher Deep Sleep scores?

5. The D.R.E.A.M. Data Dashboard

To provide premium coaching, you should synthesize data into a "D.R.E.A.M. Data Dashboard." This avoids over-reliance on technology by balancing objective metrics with subjective experience.

The Hybrid Dashboard Components:
  • Objective (Wearable): Total Sleep Time (TST), HRV, RHR, Respiratory Rate.
  • Subjective (Log): Sleep Quality (1-10), Mood upon waking, Stress levels during the day.
  • Behavioral (The DREAM Method): Compliance with light exposure (R), room temperature (E), and caffeine cutoff (M).

6. Ethics of Data: When to Refer

As a coach, your scope of practice is strictly behavioral and educational. However, wearable data can often flag clinical issues. You must refer to a Sleep Physician if you observe:

  • High Respiratory Rate/SpO2 Drops: Potential Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA).
  • Consistently High RHR/Irregular Heart Rate: Potential cardiovascular issues or AFib (flagged by Apple Watch/Fitbit).
  • Extreme Sleep Fragmentation: If a client shows 20+ "wake events" per night consistently despite perfect hygiene.
