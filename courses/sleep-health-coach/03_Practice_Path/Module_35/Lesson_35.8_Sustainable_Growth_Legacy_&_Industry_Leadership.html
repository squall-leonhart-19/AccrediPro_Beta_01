Philanthropy & The "Sleep Gap"

Legacy is inextricably linked to impact. In sleep science, the Sleep Gap refers to the statistical reality that lower socio-economic communities suffer from significantly higher rates of sleep deprivation due to environmental noise, light pollution, and "shift work" demands. A 2021 study in Sleep Health found that individuals in underserved areas averaged 58 minutes less sleep per night than those in affluent areas.

Sustainable growth involves using your success to fund advocacy. This might include:

  • Pro-Bono Clinics: Offering the D.R.E.A.M. framework to community centers.
  • Policy Advocacy: Lobbying for later school start times or "Right to Disconnect" laws for employees.
  • Environmental Grants: Funding blackout curtains or white noise machines for low-income housing.

Scaling for profit focuses on increasing revenue and client volume. Scaling for legacy focuses on increasing "Epistemic Authority," reducing "Key Person Dependency," and creating long-term social impact through IP and advocacy.
