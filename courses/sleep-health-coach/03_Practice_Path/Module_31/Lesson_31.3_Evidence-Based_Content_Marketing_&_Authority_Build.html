In high-ticket coaching, a testimonial that says "I feel so much better" is weak. Premium clients want to see objective data shifts. Your case studies should mirror the clinical rigor of the D.R.E.A.M. method. When marketing, use "The Gap Analysis" format:

5. The Multi-Channel D.R.E.A.M. Content Calendar

A consistent authority brand requires a content calendar that touches on every pillar of your method. This ensures you don't become the "light coach" or the "supplement coach," but the Sleep Health Expert.

The 5-Day Authority Rotation
  • Monday (D: Diagnose): Educate on how to identify different insomnia phenotypes. Goal: Awareness of the problem.
  • Tuesday (R: Reset): The science of light, temperature, and the SCN. Goal: Establishing biological expertise.
  • Wednesday (E/A: Environment/Address): Practical walkthrough of a "Cool Sanctuary" or the HPA axis. Goal: Demonstrating the solution.
  • Thursday (M: Master): Cognitive tools, stimulus control, and habit formation. Goal: Proving behavioral mastery.
  • Friday (Results): A data-driven case study or "Client Spotlight." Goal: Conversion through social proof.

6. Scientific Integrity in Marketing

As a Sleep Health Coach, your greatest asset is your integrity. Avoid "clickbait" that promises "One weird trick to fall asleep in 30 seconds." A 2023 study published in Sleep Health noted that misinformation regarding sleep hygiene is rampant on social media, leading to "orthosomnia"â€”a perfectionist anxiety about sleep data.

Your marketing should be "The Antidote to Orthosomnia." Position yourself as the expert who helps clients interpret their data without the anxiety. Use your content to set realistic expectations: sleep optimization is a 12-week biological reset, not a 7-day hack. This honesty builds long-term authority and filters out "quick-fix" clients who are likely to fail and damage your reputation.
