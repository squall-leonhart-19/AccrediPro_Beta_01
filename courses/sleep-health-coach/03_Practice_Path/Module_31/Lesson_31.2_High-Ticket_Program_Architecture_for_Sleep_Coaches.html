4. Calculating CLV and CAC for L4 Practitioners

To run a sustainable premium practice, you must understand the underlying economics of your business. Two metrics reign supreme: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Client Lifetime Value (CLV).

  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): The total cost of marketing and sales divided by the number of new clients acquired. In high-ticket coaching, a CAC of $500â€“$1,000 is acceptable if the program price is $5,000.
  • CLV (Client Lifetime Value): The total revenue a client generates over the entire duration of their relationship with you.

5. Scalable Group Architectures

While 1:1 coaching is the "Gold Standard," scaling your impact requires group coaching. However, the risk in group models is the dilution of the D.R.E.A.M. methodology. To maintain integrity, use a "Hybrid High-Ticket" model:

The Hybrid Group Blueprint

1. Core Curriculum: 12 Pre-recorded modules following the D.R.E.A.M. Method.
2. Weekly Hot Seats: 90-minute group calls focusing on specific biometric trends.
3. Community Peer Support: A private channel where clients share "Environment" (E) hacks.
4. Limited 1:1 Access: One 30-minute private "Diagnostic" (D) call at the start and one "Mastery" (M) call at the end.

This model allows you to charge $1,500â€“$2,500 per person while serving 10â€“20 clients simultaneously, significantly increasing your hourly effective rate without sacrificing the clinical outcomes of the DREAM method.

6. Maintenance Tiers and Continuity

The biggest mistake sleep coaches make is "graduating" a client and never speaking to them again. Sleep is a dynamic biological process that changes with age, stress, and lifestyle shifts. Your architecture must include a "Continuity Program."

Foreshadowing Module 32, we will discuss how to position the "Maintenance" tier not as a luxury, but as "Sleep Insurance." This ensures that the progress made during the 12-week Intensive is not lost to the next major life stressor. From a business perspective, this creates the recurring revenue that stabilizes your practice.
