Case Study: Executive Burnout

JD

Jonathan, 46

Tech CEO | 60-70 hours/week | Chronic Insomnia

Presenting Symptoms: Jonathan reported "crashing" at 9:00 PM on the sofa, only to wake at 11:30 PM with a racing heart and "brain on fire." He relied on 2-3 espressos before noon and used 5mg of Zolpidem 3 nights a week. Wearable data showed a Resting Heart Rate (RHR) of 72 bpm (high for his fitness level) and a Sleep Efficiency of 64%.

Intervention (The DREAM Method):

  • Diagnose: Identified "Conditioned Arousal"‚Äîthe bedroom had become a place of work and worry.
  • Reset: Implemented a "Sunset Protocol." 100% blue-light blocking glasses after 8:00 PM to protect the Melatonin-Cortisol Axis.
  • Address: Introduced "Worry Time" at 5:00 PM and a 15-minute physiological sigh breathing practice before bed to lower sympathetic tone.

Outcome: After 6 weeks, Jonathan‚Äôs Sleep Efficiency rose to 88%. His RHR dropped to 64 bpm, and he successfully tapered off pharmacological sleep aids under medical supervision.

Case Study: The Night Shift Nurse

MA

Maria, 32

ER Nurse | Rotating Shifts (3 nights on, 4 off)

Presenting Symptoms: Extreme "brain fog," digestive issues (IBS-like symptoms), and inability to sleep more than 4 hours after a night shift. Maria was consuming caffeine until 4:00 AM to stay alert.

Intervention:

  • Environment (E): Total blackout (0.0 lux) using magnetic window covers. Use of a cooling mattress pad set to 18¬∞C (65¬∞F) to facilitate the core temperature drop needed for daytime sleep.
  • Reset (R): "Dark Cocooning" on the drive home. Wearing Grade-4 sunglasses from the moment she exited the hospital to prevent morning sunlight from suppressing melatonin.
  • Master (M): Caffeine cessation 6 hours before her "intended" sleep time (approx. 2:00 AM).

Outcome: Daytime sleep duration increased from 4.2 hours to 6.5 hours. Maria reported a 40% reduction in subjective GI distress, illustrating the gut-clock connection discussed in Module 9.

Troubleshooting 'Non-Responders'

What happens when a client follows the DREAM method but sees no improvement? As a professional Sleep Health Coach, you must look for Hidden Barriers. Statistics show that up to 30% of chronic insomnia cases have an underlying, undiagnosed physiological component.

Barrier Type The "Hidden" Culprit Coach's Action (Scope-Defined)
Environmental (E) High CO2 levels (>1000ppm) Recommend improved ventilation/open window.
Physiological (A) Occult Sleep Apnea (n=24% men) Refer for a Level 1 Polysomnography (PSG).
Nutritional (M) Nocturnal Hypoglycemia Adjust macronutrient timing (Complex carb snack).
Pharmacological Beta-Blockers / SSRIs Collaborate with prescribing MD on timing.

Synthesizing the D.R.E.A.M. Sleep Method‚Ñ¢

Transformation is a process, not an event. A 12-week roadmap ensures that habits are hardwired into the client's biology. A 2022 study on habit formation found that complex health behaviors take an average of 66 days to become automatic.

Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
Focus on Diagnose and Environment.
  • Establish baseline data via wearables/sleep diaries.
  • The "Bedroom Audit": Light, Sound, Temperature, and Air Quality.
  • Objective: Eliminate external friction.
Phase 2: Alignment (Weeks 5-8)
Focus on Reset and Address.
  • Circadian anchoring: Consistent wake times and light exposure.
  • Cognitive tools: Addressing sleep-interfering thoughts and stress.
  • Objective: Re-align the biological clock with the social clock.
Phase 3: Optimization (Weeks 9-12)
Focus on Master and Sustainability.
  • Refining supplement protocols and nutritional timing.
  • High-performance strategies: Sleep extension and tactical napping.
  • Objective: Move from "fixing a problem" to "maximizing potential."

Check Your Understanding

Test your clinical reasoning skills.

1

A client reports they are doing "everything right" but still wake up feeling unrefreshed. Their wearable shows high movement during the night. What is the most likely "Hidden Barrier" to investigate?

Investigate physiological barriers (Pillar A: Address). High nocturnal movement often indicates Sleep-Disordered Breathing (Apnea) or Periodic Limb Movement Disorder (PLMD). A referral for a clinical sleep study is warranted.
2

For a shift worker transitioning from nights back to days, what is the most effective Zeitgeber to use upon waking in the morning?

High-intensity blue-enriched light (10,000 lux) and a high-protein breakfast. This signals the SCN (Master Clock) that the "active phase" has begun, helping to phase-advance the rhythm.

Key Takeaways

  • Individualization: High-performance sleep coaching requires pivoting the D.R.E.A.M. pillars based on the client's unique stressors (Executive vs. Athlete).
  • Harm Reduction: For shift workers, focus on light management and temperature control to maximize the quality of "anchor sleep."
  • The 30% Rule: Always maintain a high index of suspicion for physiological sleep disorders in non-responsive clients.
  • Consistency: The 12-week roadmap respects the biological timeline of habit formation and circadian adaptation.
