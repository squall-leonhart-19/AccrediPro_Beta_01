2. Relapse Prevention Synthesis: The Maintenance Map

Sleep is not a static achievement; it is a dynamic biological process sensitive to life‚Äôs stressors. A common pitfall for new coaches is concluding the intervention once the client reaches "good sleep." The master coach, however, prepares the client for the inevitable "Sleep Slump."

Your "Relapse Prevention Synthesis" should involve a three-tier strategy:

  1. The Baseline Anchor: Identifying the non-negotiable D.R.E.A.M. habits (e.g., Pillar R: Morning Sunlight) that keep the circadian rhythm tethered during high-stress periods.
  2. The Rescue Protocol: A pre-defined set of actions for when a client experiences three consecutive nights of poor sleep (e.g., immediate return to Pillar D: Stimulus Control).
  3. The Quarterly Audit: A self-assessment tool where clients review their Pillar E (Environment) and Pillar A (Root Causes) every 90 days.
