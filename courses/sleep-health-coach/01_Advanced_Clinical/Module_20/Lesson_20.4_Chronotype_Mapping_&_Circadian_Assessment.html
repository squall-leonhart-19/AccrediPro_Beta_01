2. MCTQ & Social Jetlag Math

While the MEQ measures "preference," the Munich Chronotype Questionnaire (MCTQ) measures "reality." It distinguishes between sleep behavior on work days versus free days. This distinction allows the coach to calculate Social Jetlag (SJL)â€”the discrepancy between biological time and social time.

The formula for Social Jetlag is:
SJL = |Mid-sleep on free days (MSF) - Mid-sleep on work days (MSW)|

A meta-analysis of over 300,000 individuals found that every 1 hour of Social Jetlag is associated with a 33% increase in the risk of obesity and a 11% increase in the likelihood of clinical depression. For the Sleep Health Coach, correcting SJL is often more impactful than increasing total sleep duration.

Case Study: The "Weekend Recovery" Fallacy
JS

James, 34, Software Architect

Symptoms: Monday morning "brain fog," chronic low-grade anxiety, caffeine dependency.

Assessment: James's MCTQ revealed a Mid-sleep on work days (MSW) of 2:30 AM (Sleep 11 PM - 6 AM). His Mid-sleep on free days (MSF) was 6:00 AM (Sleep 2 AM - 10 AM).

Calculation: SJL = 6.0 - 2.5 = 3.5 hours. James was effectively flying from New York to California and back every single weekend.

Intervention: Using the DREAM Method, we stabilized his wake time to 7:15 AM across all 7 days and introduced 10,000 lux light at 7:30 AM.

Outcome: 65% reduction in anxiety scores within 3 weeks; eliminated afternoon caffeine.

3. Mapping the Biological Clock

Subjective questionnaires are powerful, but objective mapping provides the "biological blueprint" needed for difficult cases. There are two primary markers the Sleep Health Coach can track with the client:

  • Core Body Temperature (CBT) Tracking: The Temperature Minimum (Tmin) usually occurs 2 hours before habitual wake time. Tracking this via wearable technology (as discussed in Lesson 3) allows us to find the "pivot point" for light therapy.
  • Light Exposure Logs: Clients often underestimate their evening light exposure. A 7-day log tracking "Lux Hours" can reveal why a clientâ€™s melatonin is suppressed. A 2022 study showed that exposure to even 30 lux (dim room light) can delay melatonin onset by up to 90 minutes in sensitive individuals.

4. DSPD vs. Lifestyle Night Owls

A critical skill in the "D" (Diagnose) phase of DREAM is differentiating between Delayed Sleep Phase Disorder (DSPD) and a "Lifestyle Night Owl."

DSPD is a clinical circadian rhythm sleep-wake disorder (CRSWD) where the internal clock is genetically or biologically wired significantly later than social norms. Lifestyle Owls, conversely, are individuals whose delay is driven by Revenge Bedtime Procrastination or poor light hygiene.

5. Light-Therapy & Phase Response Curves (PRC)

The Phase Response Curve (PRC) is the roadmap for the "Reset" phase. It describes how the timing of a stimulus (usually light) affects the direction and magnitude of a circadian phase shift.

Key PRC Principles for Coaches:

  1. Phase Advance (Shifting earlier): Light exposure after the Tmin (early morning) causes the clock to shift earlier. For a client with a Tmin of 5:00 AM, light at 7:00 AM is highly effective.
  2. Phase Delay (Shifting later): Light exposure before the Tmin (evening/night) causes the clock to shift later. This is why late-night screen use is so disruptive.
  3. The Dead Zone: Light in the middle of the biological day has little to no effect on circadian phase shifting.
Case Study: The "Phase Advance" Protocol
ML

Maria, 42, Night Shift Transition

Goal: Transition from a 3:00 AM bedtime to an 11:00 PM bedtime after changing jobs.

Mapping: MEQ score of 32 (Evening). Estimated Tmin at 7:00 AM.

Protocol: To achieve a 4-hour phase advance, we implemented 30 minutes of 10,000 lux light therapy starting at 7:15 AM (immediately after Tmin). We coupled this with 0.5mg of melatonin at 7:00 PM (the "Phase Advance" side of the Melatonin PRC).

Result: Maria successfully shifted her sleep onset to 11:30 PM over 14 days with minimal daytime sleepiness.

Check Your Understanding

A client has an MEQ score of 28 and a Tmin of 6:30 AM. To shift their sleep earlier, when should they prioritize bright light exposure?

Immediately after 6:30 AM (e.g., 6:45 AM - 7:30 AM). Light exposure after the temperature minimum (Tmin) triggers a phase advance, shifting the circadian rhythm to an earlier time.

If a client sleeps from 12 AM to 8 AM on workdays and 3 AM to 11 AM on weekends, what is their Social Jetlag score?

3 hours. (Workday mid-sleep = 4 AM; Weekend mid-sleep = 7 AM. The difference is 3 hours.)

Key Takeaways

  • The MEQ measures biological preference, while the MCTQ measures behavioral reality and social jetlag.
  • Social Jetlag is a stronger predictor of metabolic health issues than total sleep duration alone.
  • The Temperature Minimum (Tmin) is the most critical marker for timing light therapy interventions.
  • Clinical DSPD requires a different coaching approach than behavioral "Night Owl" tendencies.
  • Successful "Resets" require precise timing relative to the client's unique Phase Response Curve.
