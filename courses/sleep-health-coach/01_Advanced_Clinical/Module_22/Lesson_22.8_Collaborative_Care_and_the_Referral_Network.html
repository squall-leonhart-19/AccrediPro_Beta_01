2. Ethics of the Clinical 'Hand-off'

3. Communicating with Medical Doctors (MDs)

To be taken seriously by the medical community, coaches must use professional, clinical language. Avoid vague terms like "toxins" or "bad energy." Instead, focus on objective behavioral data. When sending a progress report, use the SBAR Communication Model (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation).

The DREAM Progress Report Structure:

  • Situation: "Client is currently in Week 4 of the D.R.E.A.M. Sleep Methodâ„¢ focusing on Stimulus Control."
  • Background: "Initial Sleep Efficiency (SE) was 62%. History of chronic sleep onset latency (>60 mins)."
  • Assessment: "Current SE has improved to 84%. However, client continues to report early morning awakenings (EMA) at 4:00 AM."
  • Recommendation/Query: "Requesting a review of thyroid markers (TSH, fT3, fT4) to rule out metabolic drivers of EMA."

4. Handling Clinical Disagreements Ethically

A common ethical dilemma occurs when a client's physician provides advice that contradicts evidence-based sleep coaching. For example, a physician might prescribe a benzodiazepine for long-term use, while the coach is attempting to implement Sleep Restriction Therapy (SRT).

The Ethical Hierarchy of Resolution:

  1. Never Countermand Medical Advice: You must never tell a client to stop taking a medication or ignore a doctor's orders. This is a violation of scope and a legal liability.
  2. Educate the Client on Options: Present the evidence. "Your doctor has prescribed X for immediate relief. The DREAM Methodâ„¢ focuses on long-term behavioral changes. These can often work in tandem."
  3. The "Inquiry" Approach with the MD: Instead of challenging the MD, send a professional note: "I am working with [Client] on behavioral sleep optimization. I noted they were prescribed [Medication]. Are there specific contraindications for us proceeding with Sleep Compression at this time?"

5. The 'Lead Coach' Role: Orchestrating the Team

In Level 2 coaching, you often transition into the Lead Coach role. This is common in functional medicine clinics or concierge coaching packages. As the Lead Coach, you are the primary point of contact for the client, synthesizing inputs from various specialists into a cohesive weekly action plan.

Lead Coach Responsibilities:

  • Information Synthesis: Translating complex medical jargon from the Somnologist into actionable "Environment" or "Hygiene" steps for the client.
  • Compliance Monitoring: Ensuring the client is actually using their CPAP or taking their prescribed supplements as directed by the clinical team.
  • Gap Identification: Noticing if the psychologist's work on anxiety is being undermined by the client's excessive caffeine intake (Module 5).

A client's doctor tells them that "Sleep Hygiene doesn't work" and they should just take a sedative. How should an L2 coach respond ethically?

The coach should not contradict the doctor. Instead, they should explain that while medication provides immediate relief, the DREAM Method focuses on the underlying biological and behavioral drivers. The coach should offer to send the doctor a summary of the client's progress with stimulus control to see if a collaborative approach is possible.
2

What is the primary difference between a 'Cold Referral' and a 'Warm Hand-off'?

A cold referral simply provides a name/number. A warm hand-off involves a professional clinical summary sent to the provider (with consent) and a structured transition plan that ensures the client remains supported by the coach during the clinical intervention.

6. Building Your Network Protocol

To finalize your L2 status, you must move from "knowing about" specialists to "partnering with" them. This requires a proactive outreach strategy. Consider the following "Vetting Criteria" when selecting partners for your network:

  • Alignment on Root Causes: Does the practitioner look for why sleep is disturbed, or do they only treat symptoms?
  • Communication Style: Are they open to receiving brief, data-driven reports from a Sleep Health Coach?
  • Evidence-Based Practice: Do they follow AASM (American Academy of Sleep Medicine) guidelines or equivalent functional medicine standards?

Key Takeaways

  • The referral network is a mandatory component of L2 practice to manage clinical "red flags" identified in the 'D' phase.
  • Professionalism is defined by using clinical language (SBAR) and respecting medical hierarchies while advocating for behavioral health.
  • The coach's role is to ensure continuity of care, preventing the client from becoming overwhelmed by fragmented medical advice.
  • Never countermand medical advice; instead, seek a collaborative middle ground through professional inquiry.
