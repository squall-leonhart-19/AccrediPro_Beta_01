The Pre-Call Framework

High-ticket sales do not start on the call; they start with pre-qualification. You should never get on a discovery call with someone who isn't a potential fit. This preserves your authority and ensures your "closing rate" remains high.

The "Application-First" Strategy

Require every lead to fill out a sleep assessment form before booking. This form should ask:

  • How long have you struggled with sleep?
  • What have you already tried (CPAP, medication, supplements)?
  • On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to fixing this now?
  • Are you in a position to invest in a premium solution if we are a fit?

The Discovery Call Structure

The high-ticket sales call is a structured 45-minute conversation designed to move the client from denial to desire. It follows a specific psychological arc:

Step 3: The Gap (10 Mins)

Handling Objections

In high-ticket sales, an objection is rarely about the money; it's about certainty. The client is asking: "Can I actually do this, and will this actually work for me?"

The "Price" Objection

Client: "That's a lot of money."
Coach: "I understand. If this program did nothing but give you back the energy you had 10 years ago, would it be worth it?" (Isolate the value).

The "Time" Objection

Client: "I'm too busy to start right now."
Coach: "Actually, being busy is exactly why we should start. How much time are you losing every day because you're operating at 50% capacity?"
