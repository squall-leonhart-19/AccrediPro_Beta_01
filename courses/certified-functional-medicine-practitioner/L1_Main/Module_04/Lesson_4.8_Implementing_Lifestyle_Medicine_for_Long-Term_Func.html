Moving from "Removal" to "Restoration"

In my practice, I often see practitioners get stuck in the "Remove" phase. We take away gluten, we take away stress, we take away toxins. But if we don't move into Restoration, the client remains in a fragile state. Restoration is about rebuilding the metabolic reserve.

A 2022 study in Frontiers in Physiology demonstrated that mitochondrial dysfunction is a primary driver of the "fatigue-weight gain" loop in women aged 45-55, with ATP production efficiency dropping by an average of 22% during the menopausal transition without targeted intervention.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a client feel "flat" or "low energy" even after successfully removing inflammatory triggers?

Because removing triggers stops the damage, but it doesn't automatically repair the machinery. The mitochondria may be in a "cell danger response" or simply lack the nutrient cofactors (like B vitamins, CoQ10, or Magnesium) needed to ramp up ATP production after a period of chronic stress.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Restore" phase compared to the "Remove" phase?

The goal of "Remove" is to stop the insult to the system. The goal of "Restore" is to build metabolic reserve and upregulate biological function so the body can handle future stressors without crashing.

Success Markers: The Restore Phase

  • The client reports "resilience"‚Äîthey can handle a busy day without a 3-day crash.
  • Morning energy improves before the first cup of coffee.
  • Mental clarity returns (the "brain fog" lifts as mitochondrial function in the brain improves).
  • The client expresses excitement about what they are *adding* to their life.

Key Takeaways

  • Biology of Restoration: Restoration requires signaling "safety" to the mitochondria through light, nutrients, and nervous system regulation.
  • Empowerment over Fear: Transitioning from "don't eat this" to "let's fuel this" shifts the client's psychology from fear to power.
  • The 22% Gap: Remember that perimenopausal women face a significant biological drop in energy efficiency that requires active restoration.
  • Metabolic Reserve: The ultimate goal is not just "no symptoms," but a robust reserve that allows for a high-quality, active life.
