Check Your Understanding

1. What percentage of the immune system is estimated to reside in the gut?

Approximately 70-80%. This is why the gut is often considered the "primary" organ of the immune system in Functional Medicine.

2. What is the primary role of Secretory IgA (SIgA) in the gut-immune axis?

SIgA acts as the "first line of defense" in the mucosal lining, binding to pathogens and toxins to prevent them from attaching to the gut wall and entering the bloodstream.

3. How does butyrate (a Short-Chain Fatty Acid) influence the immune system?

Butyrate promotes the differentiation of T-regulatory cells (Tregs), which help suppress excessive inflammation and prevent the immune system from attacking the body's own tissues.
