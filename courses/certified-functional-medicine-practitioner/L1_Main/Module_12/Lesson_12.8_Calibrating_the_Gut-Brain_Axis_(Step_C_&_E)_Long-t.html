Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Secretory IgA (sIgA) considered the "first line of defense" in the microbiome-immune axis?

sIgA is an antibody that coats the mucosal lining of the gut. It binds to pathogens and toxins, preventing them from attaching to the gut wall and entering the bloodstream. Low sIgA means the "gate" is poorly guarded.

2. What is Lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and why should a coach care about it?

LPS is a toxin found in the cell walls of certain "bad" bacteria. When the gut is leaky, LPS enters the blood (endotoxemia), triggering systemic inflammation, which shows up as brain fog, fatigue, and joint pain.
