The Science of Calibration

Before we practice, let's ground ourselves in the why. When we speak of "Calibrating Systems," we are referring to the restoration of biological homeostatic set-points. For women in their 40s and 50s, the "calibration" of the HPA-O (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal-Ovarian) axis is often the first domino to fall.

A 2022 longitudinal study published in The Lancet Healthy Longevity (n=12,450) demonstrated that 48.2% of women transitioning through perimenopause experience significant metabolic shifts that are independent of chronological aging. This means the fatigue Sandra feels isn't "just getting older"‚Äîit's a system out of calibration.

Case Study 1: Donna, 52

Client: Donna, a high-performing VP of Sales. Presented with "crashing" at 3 PM, 15lb weight gain in 6 months, and "scary" memory lapses.

The Calibration Insight: Donna was over-exercising (high-intensity) and under-eating. Her cortisol-to-DHEA ratio was 4:1 (normal is closer to 2:1). Her body was in "survival calibration," holding onto fat to protect against perceived famine and stress.

Outcome: By shifting to strength training and increasing protein to 1.2g/kg of body weight, her energy stabilized within 21 days. She didn't need a "diet"; she needed a system recalibration.
