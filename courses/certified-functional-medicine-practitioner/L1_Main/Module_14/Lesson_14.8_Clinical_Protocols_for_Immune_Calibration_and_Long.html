Check Your Understanding

1. Why do immune cells switch to glycolysis (sugar burning) during activation?

During activation, immune cells need rapid energy and building blocks for cytokines. This "metabolic reprogramming" allows them to act fast, but if it becomes chronic (due to high blood sugar), it leads to persistent inflammation.

2. What is the "next day" joint pain phenomenon often called in clinical terms?

It is often a manifestation of "Metabolic Endotoxemia" or the "Postprandial Inflammatory Response," where metabolic triggers lead to a delayed systemic immune activation.
