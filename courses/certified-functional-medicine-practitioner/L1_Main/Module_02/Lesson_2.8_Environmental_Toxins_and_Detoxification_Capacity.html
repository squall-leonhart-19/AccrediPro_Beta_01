Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a client with a "Normal" TSH of 3.5 still feel fatigued?

In functional medicine, the optimal TSH range is typically 1.0‚Äì2.0 mIU/L. A level of 3.5 indicates the pituitary is working harder than ideal to stimulate the thyroid, often resulting in subclinical symptoms like fatigue and weight gain.

2. What is the primary difference between a pathological range and a functional range?

Pathological ranges are based on the average population (including sick people) to identify disease. Functional ranges are based on healthy populations to identify optimal performance and prevent future disease.
