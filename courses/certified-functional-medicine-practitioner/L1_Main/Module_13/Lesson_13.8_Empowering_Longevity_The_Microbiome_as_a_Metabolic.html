Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Estrobolome" particularly important for women aged 45-55?

The estrobolome is a collection of gut bacteria that produce beta-glucuronidase, an enzyme that deconjugates estrogen. In midlife, if the microbiome is imbalanced (dysbiosis), estrogen can be reabsorbed into the bloodstream instead of excreted, worsening symptoms of estrogen dominance or hormonal fluctuations.

2. What is the primary mechanism by which a "leaky gut" causes systemic symptoms like brain fog?

Intestinal permeability allows Lipopolysaccharides (LPS) and other endotoxins to enter systemic circulation. This triggers a pro-inflammatory immune response. These inflammatory cytokines can cross the blood-brain barrier, leading to neuroinflammation, which manifests as brain fog and cognitive fatigue.
