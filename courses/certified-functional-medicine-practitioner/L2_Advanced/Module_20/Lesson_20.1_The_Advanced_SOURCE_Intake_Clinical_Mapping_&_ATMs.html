2. Advanced Timeline Mapping: ATMs

The timeline is the chronological story of the client's health. However, the Advanced SOURCE Intake requires you to categorize every event into one of three categories: Antecedents, Triggers, or Mediators (ATMs).

  • Antecedents: These are the "pre-conditions." They can be genetic (MTHFR SNPs), developmental (C-section birth), or environmental (childhood mold exposure). They set the stage but don't cause the disease.
  • Triggers: These are the "match that lit the fire." Common triggers include a severe viral infection, a period of intense grief, a course of broad-spectrum antibiotics, or a physical injury.
  • Mediators: These are the "logs on the fire." They keep the process going. Mediators are often biochemical (pro-inflammatory cytokines like TNF-alpha), but they can also be lifestyle-based (poor sleep, ongoing toxic relationships, or a high-sugar diet).

3. MSQ 2.0: Quantifying the Subjective

The Medical Symptoms Questionnaire (MSQ) is more than just a checklist; it is a biomarker of systemic burden. In the SOURCE Framework‚Ñ¢, we use the MSQ 2.0 to establish a numerical baseline.

A 2022 meta-analysis found that an MSQ score of >60 correlates strongly with elevated high-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein (hs-CRP), a marker of systemic inflammation. As a practitioner, you can use this to demonstrate the "invisible" progress before labs even come back.

MSQ Scoring Interpretation

  • <10: Optimal Health
  • 10-40: Mild Toxicity/Imbalance
  • 40-100: Moderate Systemic Burden
  • >100: Severe Chronic Dysfunction

Clinical Priority

Focus on the section with the highest score (e.g., if "Digestive" is 25 but "Joints" is 5, start with Assimilation).

4. Advanced Interviewing: Finding the "Hidden" Trigger

For the career changer, this is where your "soft skills" become "clinical skills." Clients often omit the most important data because they don't think it's "medical."

Use the "Three-Why" Technique. When a client mentions a symptom, ask "Why do you think that started?" then "What was happening in your life at that time?" and finally "How did that change your daily habits?"

Statistics show that 78% of chronic illness triggers are lifestyle or environment-based, yet only 12% are captured in standard medical intakes. You are looking for:

  • Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs): A high ACE score changes the set-point of the HPA axis for life.
  • Environmental Toxins: "Did you move into a new home or renovate just before these symptoms started?" (Mold/VOCs).
  • Hidden Infections: "Have you had any root canals or dental work in the last 5 years?" (Oral microbiome/systemic inflammation).
