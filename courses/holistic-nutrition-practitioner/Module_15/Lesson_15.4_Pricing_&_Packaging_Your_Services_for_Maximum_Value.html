Value-Based Pricing

Setting prices based on the perceived or estimated value of a product or service to the customer rather than on the cost of the product or historical prices.

Signature Program

A proprietary, multi-week framework (like a 12-week gut reset) that guides a client through a specific transformation journey.

Price Anchoring

A cognitive bias where the first price mentioned sets a mental benchmark for all subsequent price evaluations.

LTV (Lifetime Value)

The total revenue a practitioner can expect from a single client over the duration of their professional relationship.

The Hourly Rate Trap

Most new practitioners begin by charging an hourly rate (e.g., $100/hour). While this seems logical, it creates a fundamental conflict of interest. In an hourly model, the more efficient and effective you become at solving a client's problem, the less you get paid. Furthermore, hourly sessions encourage "transactional thinking," where clients view each session as an optional expense rather than a committed journey toward healing.

Pro Tip: The Anchoring Effect

Always present your most comprehensive, highest-priced "Platinum" package first. Even if the client chooses your "Gold" package, the higher anchor makes the second option feel like a significant value, reducing price resistance.

Tiered Membership Models

To maximize your reach and revenue, implement a "Good, Better, Best" tiered strategy. This allows you to serve clients at different price points while maintaining your premium positioning.

Tier 1: The Self-Paced Entry (The "Good")

A digital course or group program with minimal 1-on-1 time. Focuses on general Nutrient Density (N). Price Point: $197 - $497.

Tier 2: The Signature Hybrid (The "Better")

Your core 12-week NOURISH Methodâ„¢ program. Includes bi-weekly sessions and group support. Price Point: $1,200 - $2,500.

Tier 3: The Concierge Intensive (The "Best")

High-touch, 1-on-1 support with weekly calls, lab analysis, and 24/7 priority access. Price Point: $5,000+.
