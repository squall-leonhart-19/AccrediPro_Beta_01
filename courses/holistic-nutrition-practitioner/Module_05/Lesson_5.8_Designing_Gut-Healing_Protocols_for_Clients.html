The process of gradually increasing or decreasing a supplement dose to find the minimum effective dose while monitoring for adverse reactions.

The 5R Framework Applied

The 5R Framework provides a logical sequence to ensure that we aren't "planting seeds in toxic soil." Below is the clinical application of each phase.

1. REMOVE: Clearing the Path

Removing triggers that damage the gut or fuel dysbiosis. This includes:

  • Pathogenic overgrowths (SIBO, parasites, Candida) using botanicals.
  • Food sensitivities (Gluten, dairy, or specific IgG triggers).
  • Lifestyle stressors (NSAIDs, excessive alcohol, chronic stress).

2. REPLACE: Supporting Digestion

Restoring digestive secretions that may be deficient.

  • Betaine HCl: If hypochlorhydria is suspected (low B12, iron, or burping).
  • Digestive Enzymes: To assist in macronutrient breakdown and reduce colonic fermentation.
  • Bile Salts: If fat malabsorption is present (steatorrhea).

Supplementation Hierarchy

To avoid overwhelming the client, prioritize supplements that address the most significant barriers to healing first. A meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that targeted mucosal support significantly improved outcomes in patients with increased intestinal permeability.

Tier 1: Digestive Support (Weeks 1-12)

Betaine HCl, Pancreatic Enzymes, and Ginger/Artichoke for motility. These ensure that food is properly broken down so it doesn't fuel dysbiosis.

Tier 2: Mucosal Repair (Weeks 4-16)

L-Glutamine (5-10g/day), Zinc Carnosine (75mg/day), and Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice (DGL). These provide the raw materials for enterocyte regeneration.

Monitoring and Titration

Gut healing is rarely linear. Clients often experience "Die-off" (Herxheimer reactions) when removing pathogens. A 2021 clinical guideline suggests that slow titration of antimicrobials and prebiotics reduces adverse event drop-out rates by 45%.

üí° Clinical Tip

Use a "Symptom Tracking Journal." Have the client rate Bloating, Energy, and Stool Consistency on a scale of 1-10 weekly. This objective data helps you decide when to move from the "Remove" phase to the "Repair" phase.

Check Your Understanding

Test your clinical reasoning skills.

1

A client presents with severe bloating immediately after meals and a history of low stomach acid. Which "R" should you prioritize first in their supplement protocol?

Priority should be Replace. By providing Betaine HCl and/or Digestive Enzymes, you ensure food is chemically broken down, preventing the fermentation that causes immediate post-prandial bloating.

2

Why is it clinically risky to keep a client on a Low FODMAP diet for 6 months or longer?

Prolonged Low FODMAP diets starve beneficial commensal bacteria (like Bifidobacteria) that rely on prebiotic fibers, potentially leading to long-term dysbiosis and reduced production of Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs).

Key Takeaways

  • Assessment First: Use functional testing and symptom tracking to drive the protocol, not guesswork.
  • Sequence Matters: Follow the 5R sequence; don't try to repair the gut while active overgrowths or triggers are still present.
  • Diet as a Tool: Therapeutic diets are temporary interventions meant to calm inflammation while the underlying work is done.
  • The 12-Week Rule: Significant mucosal repair typically requires 3-4 months of consistent support; manage client expectations accordingly.
  • Holistic Integration: Long-term success requires rebalancing the nervous system and lifestyle through the NOURISH Method‚Ñ¢.
