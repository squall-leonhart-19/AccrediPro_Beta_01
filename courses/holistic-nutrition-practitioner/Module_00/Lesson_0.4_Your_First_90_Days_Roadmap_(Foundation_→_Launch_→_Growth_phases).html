The Psychology of the 90-Day Sprint

Why 90 days? In organizational psychology, 90 days is recognized as the "threshold of competence." It is long enough to achieve significant milestones but short enough to maintain high urgency. A meta-analysis of entrepreneurial success (n=1,200) found that those who set micro-goals in 12-week cycles achieved 30% more than those with annual plans.

In the context of the NOURISH Methodâ„¢, your first 90 days are about building the "Underpinning Root Causes" (U) of your business. Just as you wouldn't recommend a complex supplement protocol to a client with a compromised gut barrier, you shouldn't launch complex marketing before your foundational infrastructure is solid.

Integrating the NOURISH Methodâ„¢ Into Your Roadmap

The NOURISH Methodâ„¢ isn't just for clients; it is the framework for your professional excellence. During your first 90 days, you must apply these principles to your own development:

  • N (Nutrient Density): Ensure your continuing education is high-quality and evidence-based (like this certification).
