LTV (Lifetime Value)

CAC (Client Acquisition Cost)

The total amount spent on marketing and sales to acquire one new paying client.

MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)

Predictable revenue generated each month from memberships or long-term retainer programs.

Package-Based Pricing

Charging for a specific outcome or duration (e.g., 3 months) rather than an hourly rate.

The Part-Time Practice Model (The Transition)

Most practitioners begin here. This model is ideal for those currently in full-time jobs or with primary caregiving responsibilities. The focus is on Individualized Biochemistry (I) with a small, high-touch client load.

The Part-Time Math

Target: 5 active clients per month.
Program: 3-month "Gut Reset" at $1,200 ($400/mo).
Gross Revenue: $2,000 / month.
Estimated Time: 5-8 hours per week (Consultations + Admin).

The Scaled Practice (The Visionary)

The Scaled Practice moves away from "dollars for hours." By leveraging group coaching, digital courses, and Sustainable Lifestyle Integration (S) at scale, the income ceiling is effectively removed.

A scaled practitioner might run 2-3 group cohorts per year with 50 students each, priced at $597. This generates $29,850 per cohort with minimal additional time investment once the curriculum is built.

Net Profit vs. Gross Revenue

It is a common mistake to focus solely on the "Top Line." A high-earning practice is only successful if it is also a high-profit practice. Fortunately, holistic nutrition is a low-overhead business.

Typical Monthly Expenses (Digital Practice)

  • Practice Management Software (HIPAA compliant): $50-$100
  • Supplement Dispensary (Fullscript/Wellevate): Free (Generates 25-35% commission)
  • Marketing/Ads: $200-$1,000 (Scalable)
  • Professional Insurance: $20-$40
  • Continuing Education: $100

Average Profit Margin: 70% - 85%
