Quantifying the ROI of Resilience

When discussing pricing with high-level clients or organizations, you must speak the language of Return on Investment (ROI). Burnout is not just a feeling; it is a financial drain. A 2022 meta-analysis (n=12,400) found that employees with high burnout scores had 2.6 times the rate of absenteeism and a 63% higher likelihood of visiting an emergency room.

Use the following data points to justify your pricing:

  • Healthcare Savings: Reducing stress-related chronic illness (hypertension, GI issues).
  • Opportunity Cost: The value of the clientâ€™s time if they were operating at 100% capacity vs. 40% capacity.
  • Replacement Cost: For corporate clients, replacing a burned-out executive costs 1.5x to 2x their annual salary.
  • Relational Capital: The intangible value of saving a marriage or being a present parent.

Pro Tip: The Anchoring Effect

Always present your most comprehensive, high-touch package first. Even if the client chooses a mid-tier option, the high-touch package "anchors" the value of your expertise, making the mid-tier option seem like an incredible bargain.
