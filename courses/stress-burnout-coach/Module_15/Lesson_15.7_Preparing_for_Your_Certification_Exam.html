HPA-Axis

The Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal axis; the body's central stress response system that regulates cortisol and homeostasis.

Vagal Tone

An internal biological process that represents the activity of the vagus nerve, indicating the capacity for parasympathetic recovery.

Allostatic Load

The cumulative "wear and tear" on the body which grows when an individual is exposed to repeated or chronic stress.

Stress Shield

A framework for internal and external boundaries designed to protect cognitive bandwidth and prevent energy leaks.

Physiological High-Yield Topics

Expect approximately 40% of the exam to focus on the biological mechanisms of stress. You must understand the transition from Sympathetic Dominance to Parasympathetic Recovery.

Key areas for review include:

  • The Cortisol Curve: Recognizing "tired but wired" vs. "adrenal exhaustion" patterns.
  • Heart Rate Variability (HRV): Understanding HRV as a proxy for Vagal Tone and readiness for load.
  • Circadian Biology: The role of light exposure and the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) in cortisol regulation.
  • The Gut-Brain-Stress Axis: How chronic stress increases intestinal permeability and systemic inflammation.

Exam Tip

When a question asks for the "first step" in physiological regulation, always look for options that prioritize safety and stabilization of the autonomic nervous system before moving into high-intensity recovery protocols.

Psychological & Boundary Mastery

The remaining portion of the exam focuses on the "Stress Shield" and cognitive interventions. You will be tested on your ability to distinguish between passive rest (which often fails to resolve burnout) and active recovery.

Review these concepts deeply:

  • Cognitive Bandwidth: The finite nature of executive function and how burnout depletes it.
  • Boundary Architecture: Differentiating between physical (environment), temporal (time), and cognitive (mental) boundaries.
  • Somatic Experiencing: The importance of "bottom-up" processing (body to brain) in releasing stored trauma/stress.

Strategic Study Protocols

To maximize your cognitive performance during the exam, we recommend utilizing evidence-based study techniques. Research in the Journal of Educational Psychology suggests that Spaced Repetition and Active Recall are significantly more effective than re-reading notes.

The 3-Step Review Protocol

1. Blank Sheet Recall: Write "R.E.V.I.V.E." on a blank paper and fill in everything you remember about each pillar without looking at your notes.
2. Targeted Gap Filling: Only review the modules where your recall was weak.
3. Scenario Testing: Create a fictional client and walk them through all six stages of the method.
