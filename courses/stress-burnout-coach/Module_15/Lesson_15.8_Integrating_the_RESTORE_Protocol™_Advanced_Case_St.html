2. Case Study 1: The High-Performance Executive (Type A Burnout)

Case Study: "Marcus" (48, Tech COO)

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic insomnia (3-4 hours/night), severe irritability, "brain fog," and a resting heart rate that had climbed from 58 to 74 bpm over six months. Marcus felt he was "losing his edge" and feared a cardiac event.

RESTORE Intervention:

  • Evaluate: Oura ring data showed nearly zero "Deep Sleep" and a suppressed HRV of 18ms.
  • Stabilize: Marcus resisted "meditation," so we implemented Physiological Sighs (cyclic sighing) for 5 minutes before board meetings.
  • Transform: Identified a core belief: "My worth equals my output." Reframed to: "My recovery dictates my output quality."
  • Optimize: Removed blue light exposure after 8 PM and introduced magnesium glycinate (400mg) pre-sleep.

Outcome: After 12 weeks, Marcus's HRV increased to 42ms (a 133% improvement). He reported a 70% reduction in perceived stress and successfully negotiated a 4-day work week for his executive team, demonstrating the Reclaim pillar.

4. Adapting the Protocol to Different Populations

While the biological foundations of stress are universal, the contextual application must be bespoke. As an expert coach, you must adapt your language and priorities based on your niche.

Corporate/Executive

Focus on Optimization and Energy Management. Use data (HRV, sleep trackers) to appeal to their logic. Frame rest as "strategic recovery" for peak performance.

Healthcare/First Responders

Focus on Stabilization and Compassion Fatigue. Interventions must be "micro-habit" based because they lack time. Focus on the "Transition" between work and home.

Entrepreneurs

Focus on Boundaries (Reclaim) and Limiting Beliefs (Transform). They often struggle with "hustle culture" indoctrination. Focus on decoupling self-worth from revenue.

Parents/Caregivers

Focus on Self-Efficacy (Empower) and Nervous System Regulation. Focus on co-regulation (how their state affects their children) to increase buy-in.

5. Measuring Efficacy: Data-Driven Coaching

To command premium fees ($997 - $5,000+ per program), you must provide tangible proof of transformation. We recommend a "Double-Gate" assessment strategy:

1. The Subjective Gate: Use the Burnout Assessment Tool (BAT) or the Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) at Week 1, Week 6, and Week 12. A 2023 meta-analysis showed that clients who see their progress reflected in scores are 3x more likely to maintain lifestyle changes.

2. The Objective Gate: Whenever possible, use wearable data. A client might *feel* stressed, but if their HRV is trending upward, you can prove their nervous system is becoming more resilient. This objective data is the cornerstone of the Evaluate pillar.

The Stabilize pillar. In Stage 4, the prefrontal cortex is often "offline" due to chronic amygdala hijacking. You must regulate the nervous system (breathwork, grounding, safety) before the client has the cognitive capacity for the "Transform" (cognitive restructuring) phase.
