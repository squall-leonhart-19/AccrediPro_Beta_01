    The Outreach Framework: The "Low-Friction" Ask

    The biggest mistake coaches make is asking for referrals in the first meeting. Medical professionals protect their patients fiercely. You must first demonstrate Clinical Competence. Your outreach should focus on how you can make their job easier, not how they can help you grow your business.

    The 3-Step Outreach Script Strategy

    • The Introduction: "I‚Äôm a Certified Stress & Burnout Coach using the REVIVE Method‚Ñ¢. I‚Äôve noticed many patients in our area struggling with the behavioral side of chronic stress recovery."
    • The Value Proposition: "I specialize in the lifestyle implementation that physicians often don't have time for‚Äîspecifically HPA-axis stabilization and boundary architecture."
    • The Low-Friction Ask: "May I send you a one-page summary of my protocols and a few business cards for patients you feel might benefit from intensive lifestyle support?"

    Collaborative Reporting & The Feedback Loop

    To maintain a referral relationship, you must provide feedback (with client consent). Physicians love data. When a client is referred to you, your "Evaluative Resilience Architecture" (the final 'E' in REVIVE) should include a brief progress report sent to the referring MD every 90 days.

    This report should include biometric improvements (e.g., improved HRV, better sleep markers) and psychological indicators (e.g., reduced Burnout Inventory scores). When a doctor sees their patient getting better under your care, the referral "faucet" stays open indefinitely.

    Ethical & Legal Boundaries

    When working with medical professionals, clarity on your Scope of Practice is vital. You are not diagnosing or treating medical conditions; you are coaching lifestyle and behavioral change to support the physician's care plan.

    Ensure you have a Release of Information (ROI) form signed by the client before discussing any details with their doctor. This maintains HIPAA compliance (or local equivalent) and builds trust with the medical professional that you are a "safe" and professional partner.

    ROI of Referral Networks

    A single medical referral source can be worth $25,000 to $60,000 per year in revenue. Unlike social media marketing, which requires constant content creation and ad spend, a referral network is a "set and maintain" asset.

    Medical Referral: 60-80% conversion rate, $0 acquisition cost, high trust baseline (transferred from MD).
