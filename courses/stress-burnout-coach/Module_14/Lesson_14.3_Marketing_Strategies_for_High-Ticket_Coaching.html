The Transition to Premium Practitioner

Marketing a high-ticket stress and burnout program is fundamentally different from selling general wellness coaching. When you are asking a client to invest $5,000 or $10,000 in their recovery, you are not selling a "service"; you are selling a guaranteed return on their most valuable asset: their health and cognitive capacity. This lesson provides the tactical roadmap to position yourself as an elite specialist who solves an expensive problem.

The High-Ticket Paradigm

In the coaching world, "High-Ticket" generally refers to packages priced between $3,000 and $15,000 for a 3-to-6 month engagement. For a Stress & Burnout Coach, this pricing is justified by the massive cost of the alternative. According to a 2022 study by the American Psychological Association, burnout-related turnover and lost productivity cost global businesses an estimated $322 billion annually.

When you sell high-ticket, you move away from "dollars for hours." Instead, you focus on the Value-Based Pricing model. If an executive earns $300,000 a year but is at risk of losing their job due to burnout-induced cognitive decline, a $10,000 intervention that restores their performance has a 30x ROI in the first year alone.

Authority-Based Content Strategy

High-ticket clients (Executives, Entrepreneurs, Physicians) do not "scroll and buy." They "research and invest." Your marketing must demonstrate Epistemic Authority. This is achieved through "Deep-Dive" content rather than "snackable" social media posts.

  • White Papers & Case Studies: Publish detailed reports on how you helped a specific client demographic (e.g., "The 90-Day Recovery Roadmap for Tech Founders").
  • The "Mechanism" Video: A 15-20 minute masterclass explaining the physiology of burnout and how your ASCEND framework addresses it.
  • Podcasting/Guesting: Appearing on industry-specific podcasts where your target high-ticket clients already spend their time.

Pro Tip: The 7-11-4 Rule

Marketing research suggests that for high-ticket sales, a lead needs roughly 7 hours of interaction, across 11 touchpoints, in 4 different locations (e.g., email, YouTube, LinkedIn, and a Discovery Call) before they feel comfortable investing $5k+.

Conversion Funnels for Premium Offers

A high-ticket funnel is designed to filter out the wrong people and indoctrinate the right ones. Unlike low-ticket funnels that push for an immediate "Buy Now," your goal is to push for a "Discovery Call."

The "Application" Funnel Structure

1. Traffic: LinkedIn, Referrals, or Targeted Ads.
2. Education: A "Value-First" video or long-form article explaining the ASCEND Methodologyâ„¢.
3. Application: A short form asking about their current stress levels and financial readiness.
4. The Call: A 45-minute consultative enrollment call.

The High-Ticket Sales Conversation

In high-ticket coaching, you are not "selling"; you are "prescribing." If you have positioned yourself correctly, the client should feel that you are the only one who understands the biological complexity of their burnout. The call follows a specific psychological architecture:

  • The Gap: Clearly define the distance between where they are (Burnout) and where they want to be (Resilience).
  • The Cost of Inaction: Ask, "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?" (Health collapse, divorce, job loss).
  • The Prescription: Present the ASCEND Methodologyâ„¢ as the specific bridge across that gap.
  • The Investment: State the price with absolute certainty. High-ticket clients equate high price with high quality and high commitment.
