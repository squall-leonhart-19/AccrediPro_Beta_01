Coach's Warning: Contempt

Contempt is the single greatest predictor of relationship failure and is highly correlated with suppressed immune function in the person receiving it. If your client is experiencing contempt from a manager or partner, boundary setting (Lesson 6.2) must take priority over "resolution" skills.

The DEAR MAN Protocol

Originating from Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), the DEAR MAN acronym provides a structured script for clients who need to express a need or set a boundary without escalating conflict. This is particularly effective for burnt-out professionals who feel "guilty" for asking for help.

"I" Statements & De-escalation

The linguistic shift from "You" to "I" is the difference between triggering a clientâ€™s defense mechanism and inviting collaboration. When we start a sentence with "You" (e.g., "You always ignore my emails"), the recipientâ€™s brain perceives a social threat, activating the SNS.

An effective "I" statement follows a specific tripartite structure:

  1. "I feel..." (Naming the emotion: frustrated, overwhelmed, worried).
  2. "...when [behavior]..." (Describing the objective fact, not the person's intent).
  3. "...because [impact]." (Explaining the consequence to your energy or workload).

Coaching Implementation: Step-by-Step

As a Stress & Burnout Coach, you are not a therapist, but you are a "Communication Architect." Use these steps to integrate this into your coaching sessions:

1. The Conflict Audit

Ask the client to list the three most common recurring conflicts in their life. Have them identify which of the "Four Horsemen" are usually present.

2. Physiological Monitoring

Teach the client to recognize "Flooding." If their heart rate is elevated or they feel heat in their chest, they must implement a "Strategic Pause" before continuing the conversation.

3. Scripting and Roleplay

Use the DEAR MAN or "I" Statement framework to script a real-world upcoming conversation. Roleplay the conversation with you acting as the "difficult" party.

Key Takeaways

  • Conflict triggers an "Amygdala Hijack," which physically impairs the brain's ability to communicate logically and increases allostatic load.
  • The "Four Horsemen" (Criticism, Contempt, Defensiveness, Stonewalling) are toxic patterns that must be replaced with evidence-based antidotes.
  • DEAR MAN is a structured script for assertive communication that minimizes the risk of triggering defensiveness in others.
  • "I" statements reduce social threat perception and allow for "Affect Labeling," which downregulates amygdala activity.
  • Repair attempts are essential for preventing "emotional flooding" and maintaining the social network's integrity during burnout recovery.
