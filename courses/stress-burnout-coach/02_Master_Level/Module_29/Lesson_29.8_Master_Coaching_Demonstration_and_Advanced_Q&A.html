5. Ethical Dilemmas and Scope of Practice

One of the most frequent Q&A topics involves the "Red Line" between coaching and therapy. As an AccrediPro Certified Coach, you operate in the realm of functional restoration and performance optimization.

The Referral Threshold: If a client exhibits signs of clinical major depressive disorder (MDD), active suicidal ideation, or severe PTSD that prevents them from engaging in future-oriented goal setting, an immediate referral to a licensed mental health professional is mandatory. You can, however, work adjunctively with their therapist to manage physiological stress markers.

Check Your Understanding

1

A client in the "Stabilize" phase is not responding to slow diaphragmatic breathing and feels more anxious when trying to relax. What is the likely reason and the master pivot?

The client is likely experiencing "Relaxation-Induced Anxiety" or is in a high-arousal sympathetic state where stillness feels threatening. The pivot is to use "active" stabilization like progressive muscle relaxation or rhythmic movement (walking) before returning to breathwork.
2

According to recent research, what is the most significant predictor of long-term burnout recovery sustainability?

Self-efficacy (the "Empower" phase). Clients who believe they have the tools to manage future stressors show a 92% lower relapse rate than those who rely solely on external support.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastery is the ability to adapt the RESTORE protocol to the client's unique physiological and psychological "signature."
  • Stabilization must always precede Transformation; a dysregulated brain cannot restructure beliefs effectively.
  • Ethical coaching requires clear boundaries and a robust referral network.
  • Data (HRV, stats) provides the "objective mirror" that helps clients acknowledge the reality of their burnout.
  • Your role is to transition from a "Guide" to a "Partner" as the client moves into the Empower phase.
