2. Precision Personalization: Moving Beyond General Advice

Personalization in the RESTORE Protocolâ„¢ is driven by the Bio-Psychosocial Model. We don't just look at the stress; we look at the individual's capacity to process that stress. This involves synthesizing data from three primary streams:

  1. Physiological Markers: Heart Rate Variability (HRV), sleep architecture, and cortisol awakening response (CAR).
  2. Psychometric Data: Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) scores and Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) results.
  3. Lifestyle Constraints: The reality of the client's 24-hour cycle and resource availability.

For example, in the Stabilize phase, a client with a high-sympathetic "wired but tired" profile requires down-regulation techniques like 4-7-8 breathing. Conversely, a client in the "exhaustion" phase (low cortisol) may require gentle up-regulation through light movement and cold thermotherapy to restore circadian rhythmicity.

3. Measuring Outcomes and Tracking Progress

Mastery requires evidence. To justify the $997+ investment in your coaching, you must demonstrate tangible shifts in the client's biology and psychology. We utilize a dual-tracking system to ensure a comprehensive view of recovery.

Subjective vs. Objective Metrics

A 2022 meta-analysis of coaching interventions (n=1,450) found that clients who tracked objective data (like HRV) along with subjective feelings showed a 22% higher adherence rate to protocol changes.

  • Subjective: Burnout Assessment Tool (BAT), Energy Audits (1-10 scale), and Mood Logs.
  • Objective: Resting Heart Rate (RHR) trends, Sleep Latency (time to fall asleep), and Blood Glucose Variability (stress-induced spikes).
