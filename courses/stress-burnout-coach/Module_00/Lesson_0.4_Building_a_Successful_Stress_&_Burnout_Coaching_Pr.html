1. Business Foundations: The Legal & Financial Infrastructure

Before accepting your first client, you must move from "hobbyist" to "professional." This transition protects your personal assets and builds immediate trust with high-level clients who are often skeptical of unregulated wellness services.

Legal Structure: Most coaches begin as a Sole Proprietorship or an LLC (Limited Liability Company). An LLC is generally recommended as it creates a "corporate veil," separating your personal finances from your business liabilities. This is particularly important when dealing with clients in high-stress states who may experience health complications during your tenure.

Professional Liability Insurance: General liability is not enough. You require Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions) insurance. This covers you against claims of negligence or "failure to deliver results." Given that burnout coaching often touches on mental health boundaries, ensure your policy specifically covers "Life/Wellness Coaching."

4. Client Onboarding: Setting the Stage for Success

The "Onboarding Phase" is the period between the client paying and the first official session. For a burnout client, this period is critical for lowering their allostatic load (the "wear and tear" on the body).

A professional onboarding process should include:

  • The Welcome Kit: A PDF outlining what to expect, how to communicate, and a "Quick Win" exercise (e.g., a 2-minute physiological sigh technique).
  • Legal Agreement: Clear terms of service, cancellation policies, and the "Not-Medical-Advice" disclaimer.
  • Intake Assessment: Using tools like the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) or the Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) to establish a baseline.
  • Payment Automation: Using platforms like Stripe or HoneyBook to ensure you aren't "chasing checks," which adds to your own stress.

Check Your Understanding

Why is "Package Pricing" superior to "Hourly Pricing" in the context of burnout coaching?

Package pricing shifts the focus to the transformation (The R.E.S.T.O.R.E.d state) rather than the time spent. It ensures client commitment over the 3-6 months required for physiological nervous system regulation and provides the coach with predictable income, reducing their own burnout risk.

What is the primary purpose of Professional Liability Insurance for a coach?

It protects the coach against claims of negligence, "errors or omissions," or failure to deliver promised results. It is a critical layer of protection when working with clients whose high stress levels may impact their physical or mental health.
