The Global Burnout Crisis: Data & Demand

In 2019, the World Health Organization (WHO) officially recognized burnout in the ICD-11 as an "occupational phenomenon" resulting from chronic workplace stress that has not been successfully managed. This was a watershed moment for the industry. Since then, the numbers have only grown more staggering.

According to a 2022-2023 Gallup report, approximately 76% of employees experience burnout at least sometimes, with 28% reporting they feel burned out "very often" or "always." This is not just a human tragedy; it is an economic one. Burnout costs the global economy an estimated $322 billion annually in turnover and lost productivity.
