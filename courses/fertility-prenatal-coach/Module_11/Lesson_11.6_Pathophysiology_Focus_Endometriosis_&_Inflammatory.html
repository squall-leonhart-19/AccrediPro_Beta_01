1. Beyond the "Period Problem": A Systemic Disease

For decades, endometriosis was erroneously simplified as "retrograde menstruation." However, current clinical data suggests a far more complex immune-mediated systemic inflammatory disease. While ectopic endometrial-like tissue is the hallmark, the driving force is a failure of the immune system to clear these cells, coupled with an overactive inflammatory response in the peritoneal fluid.

3. Nutritional Biochemistry: Modulating NF-kB & PGE2

The NF-kB (Nuclear Factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells) is a protein complex that controls cytokine production and cell survival. In endometriosis, NF-kB is chronically "switched on," leading to the proliferation of lesions and the production of Prostaglandin E2 (PGE2).

PGE2 is a double-edged sword: it causes the intense uterine contractions (pain) and also stimulates the enzyme aromatase within the lesions themselves. This creates a local "estrogen factory," where the lesion produces its own estrogen, fueling further growth. Our nutritional strategy must focus on:

  • Inhibiting NF-kB: Utilizing high-dose Curcumin (with piperine) and Resveratrol to inhibit the signaling pathway.
  • PGE2 vs. PGE3 Balance: Shifting the Omega-6 to Omega-3 ratio. EPA and DHA compete for the same enzymes as arachidonic acid, leading to the production of the anti-inflammatory PGE3 instead of the pro-inflammatory PGE2.
  • Aromatase Inhibition: Utilizing cruciferous vegetables (DIM/I3C) to favor the 2-OH estrone pathway over the more inflammatory 16-OH pathway.
