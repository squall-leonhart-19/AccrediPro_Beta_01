4. Developing the 90-Day Roadmap

The 90-day window is non-negotiable because it reflects the timeline of oogenesis‚Äîthe maturation process of the egg. The first 30 days of any Level 2 plan must prioritize the 'C' (Cleanse & Prepare) pillar.

Phase 1 (Days 1-30): The Metabolic Reset
Focus on blood sugar stabilization and liver detoxification pathways (Phase I and II). If the liver is congested, it cannot clear excess estrogens, leading to estrogen dominance which hinders the 'O' (Optimize) phase.

Phase 2 (Days 31-60): Micronutrient Loading
Once the "drain" is open (detoxification), we fill the "tank" (Nourish). This is where CoQ10, Methylfolate, and Omega-3s have the highest impact on mitochondrial health.

Phase 3 (Days 61-90): Uterine Receptivity & Cycle Refinement
Focusing on the 'C' (Cycle Awareness) and 'V' (Visualize) pillars to ensure the pelvic environment is prepared for implantation.

Case Study: The "Unexplained" 37-Year-Old

Client: Elena, 37, trying for 18 months. Diagnosis: Unexplained Infertility. AMH: 0.9 ng/mL. TSH: 3.2 mIU/L.

Functional Intake Findings: Elena had chronic bloating (Estrobolome dysfunction), cold hands/feet (Subclinical hypothyroidism), and high phthalate exposure from her skincare routine.

Intervention: Instead of immediate IVF, we mapped her to Cleanse (removed EDCs), Optimize (Thyroid support), and Nourish (Mitochondrial support).

Outcome: After 90 days, Elena's TSH dropped to 1.8 mIU/L. She conceived naturally in month 4. The "unexplained" factor was simply a metabolic environment that wasn't conducive to sustaining a pregnancy.

5. Clinical Red Flags: Knowing When to Refer

Professional ethics (Module 0) dictate that a coach must never "play doctor." Identifying red flags during intake is critical for client safety and your professional liability.

IMMEDIATE MEDICAL REFERRAL REQUIRED IF:

  • Severe Dysmenorrhea: Pain that prevents daily activities (Rule out Stage III/IV Endometriosis).
  • Recurrent Pregnancy Loss (RPL): Two or more consecutive losses (Refer for thrombophilia and karyotype testing).
  • Amenorrhea: Absence of menses for 3+ months (Rule out POI or pituitary tumors).
  • Unilateral Pelvic Pain: Sharp, localized pain (Rule out ectopic pregnancy or adnexal torsion).
  • Male Factor: Sperm count < 5 million/mL (Refer to Urologist for varicocele screening).

While 3.8 is within the "general" range, fertility research (ASRM guidelines) suggests that TSH should ideally be below 2.5 mIU/L to reduce the risk of first-trimester miscarriage and support optimal ovulatory function.
