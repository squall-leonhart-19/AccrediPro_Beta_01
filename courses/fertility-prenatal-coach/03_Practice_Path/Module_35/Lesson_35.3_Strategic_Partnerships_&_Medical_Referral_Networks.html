4. Ethical Affiliate Marketing & Tech Stacks

As you scale, you will be approached by supplement companies and fertility tracking tech brands. While this offers "passive" revenue, your integrity is your currency. In the C.O.N.C.E.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢, we prioritize biochemical individuality. Therefore, "blind" affiliate promotion is unethical and clinically unsound.

The Vetting Protocol for Brands:

  1. Third-Party Testing: Does the supplement brand use NSF or USP certification?
  2. Data Privacy: For fertility trackers (e.g., Oura, Mira), how is patient data stored and shared?
  3. Clinical Evidence: Is there peer-reviewed data supporting the specific claims of the device or nutrient?
  4. Transparency: Always use a clear disclosure: "I may receive a commission, but I only recommend products I use in my clinical practice."

5. Co-Branded Educational Webinars

Co-branded webinars are the ultimate lead generation engine. By partnering with a complementary professional (e.g., a Prenatal Yoga Instructor or an Embryologist), you tap into their audience while providing massive value.

The "Expert Interview" Model: Instead of a sales pitch, host a "Deep Dive" into a specific topic like "Mitochondrial Health & Egg Quality." You provide the lifestyle/nutritional framework (Nourish/Optimize), and the partner provides the clinical or movement perspective. At the end, both partners offer a low-friction "Next Step" (e.g., a discovery call or a discounted workshop).

6. Closing the Referral Loop

The biggest mistake coaches make is failing to "close the loop" with referring physicians. When a doctor refers a patient to you, they want to know what happened. This builds the Referral Loop that ensures future leads.

The Professional Feedback Loop:

  • Intake Notification: A brief email to the MD: "Just wanted to let you know [Patient Name] started the C.O.N.C.E.I.V.E. program today. We are focusing on inflammatory markers."
  • Progress Update: A 60-day update: "[Patient Name] has improved their sleep hygiene and reduced fasting insulin by 10%."
  • Success Celebration: "Thrilled to share that [Patient Name] is now in her second trimester. Thank you for the referral!"

Key Takeaways

  • Allies, Not Rivals: Position yourself as the "compliance layer" that helps medical clinics improve their success rates.
