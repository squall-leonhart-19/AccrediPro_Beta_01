1. Meta Ads: Demographics & Life Events

While authority marketing (Lesson 2) and referrals (Lesson 5) provide a solid foundation, Paid Advertising is the engine of scalability. Meta (Facebook and Instagram) remains the primary platform for fertility coaches due to its robust demographic targeting and visual nature.

Unlike search advertising, Meta is "interruptive." You are showing an ad to someone who isn't necessarily looking for you at that moment. Therefore, your creative must resonate with their current emotional state. A 2023 analysis of health coaching ads found that empathy-led creative outperformed clinical-led creative by 42% in click-through rates (CTR).

Advanced Meta Targeting for Fertility
Avoid broad targeting. Instead, layer these specific segments:
  • Life Events: "Recently Married" (1-2 years) or "Recently Moved" (often precedes family planning).
  • Interests: Fertility tracking apps (e.g., Flo, Clue), prenatal vitamins, or specific IVF support groups.
  • Lookalike Audiences (LAL): Once you have 100+ clients, upload their emails to Meta to find "Lookalikes"‚Äîusers with similar behavioral profiles.

4. Retargeting: Staying Top-of-Mind

The fertility journey is rarely a "one-click" purchase. Data suggests that for high-ticket coaching ($3,000+), a prospect requires 7 to 11 touchpoints before booking an enrollment call. Retargeting (or Remarketing) allows you to show ads specifically to people who have already visited your site or engaged with your content.

5. Calculating ROI & Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

To scale, you must treat your marketing as an investment, not an expense. This requires mastering two metrics: CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) and LTV (Lifetime Value).

The Math of a $5,000 Package
If your 12-week fertility coaching package is $5,000:
  • Ad Spend: $1,000
  • Leads Generated: 50 ($20 per lead)
  • Calls Booked: 10 (20% conversion from lead to call)
  • Clients Signed: 2 (20% close rate on calls)
  • CAC: $500 ($1,000 spend / 2 clients)
  • ROI: 10:1 ($10,000 revenue / $1,000 spend)

In this scenario, you can confidently spend $500 to acquire a $5,000 client. Most successful practitioners aim for a CAC that is less than 20% of the package price.
