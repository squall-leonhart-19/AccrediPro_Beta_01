3. Business & Ethics Weighting

While the clinical science is the "heart" of the certification, the "skeleton" is the professional framework. The L4 exam places significant emphasis on Scope of Practice. A common pitfall for practitioners is "prescribing" rather than "suggesting."

You will be tested on your ability to navigate the "Referral Threshold." A 2023 study on health coaching efficacy found that practitioners who maintained clear professional boundaries saw a 24% increase in long-term client retention due to increased trust and safety (Grant et al., 2023).

Case Study: The "Ethics Trap" Question

Candidate: Sarah J. | Exam Scenario

Scenario: A client presents with a BBT chart showing a consistent 8-day luteal phase and reports severe, debilitating pelvic pain. She asks if she should start a high-dose Vitex supplement and if you can "fix" her endometriosis.

Intervention Analysis: In the exam, Sarah must identify two critical actions:

  • Referral: Debilitating pain requires a medical diagnosis to rule out endometriosis or cysts (Scope of Practice).
  • Education: Rather than "fixing," the coach educates on the relationship between progesterone and luteal phase length.

Outcome: By selecting the "Referral + Education" option over the "Supplement Prescription" option, Sarah earns full points for Ethics and Professionalism.
