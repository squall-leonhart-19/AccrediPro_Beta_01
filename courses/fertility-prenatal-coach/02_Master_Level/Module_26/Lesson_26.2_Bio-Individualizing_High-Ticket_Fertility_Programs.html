2. Layering Advanced Functional Testing

High-ticket programs should almost always include functional testing. This moves the coach from "guessing" to "assessing." Within our framework, testing falls under the Cleanse (C) and Optimize (O) pillars.

78% of "unexplained infertility" cases show significant gut dysbiosis or hormonal metabolite imbalances on functional tests.

The DUTCH Test (Dried Urine Test for Comprehensive Hormones): Essential for understanding estrogen metabolism. You aren't just looking at total estrogen; you are looking at the 2-OH, 4-OH, and 16-alpha-OH pathways. For an Endometriosis client, seeing a preference for the 4-OH pathway (pro-carcinogenic/pro-inflammatory) allows for a targeted intervention with Sulforaphane or I3C.

The GI-MAP (Gut Microbiome Health): Crucial for the Estrobolome. If Beta-glucuronidase is elevated, your client is de-conjugating estrogen in the gut and reabsorbing it, driving estrogen dominance regardless of how well their liver is functioning. This is a "High-Ticket Insight" that justifies the premium price point.

3. Customizing the 'N' Pillar: Metabolic Typing

Generic "Fertility Diets" (like the Mediterranean diet) are excellent baselines, but high-level coaching requires Metabolic Typing. This is the art of matching macronutrient ratios to a client's specific oxidative rate and autonomic nervous system dominance.

In the Nourish (N) pillar, we categorize clients into three primary metabolic types:

  • Protein Types: Fast oxidizers who require higher fats and proteins to stabilize blood sugar. Often these are "adrenal-driven" clients with high stress.
  • Carbo Types: Slow oxidizers who thrive on higher complex carbohydrates and lower fats. These are often the "sluggish metabolism" or hypothyroid-leaning clients.
  • Mixed Types: Require a balanced 40/30/30 ratio.

A study in the Journal of Personalized Medicine (2023) showed that matching macronutrient intake to metabolic phenotype improved ovulatory regularity in 62% of participants compared to 31% in the control group. When your program includes a Metabolic Typing Assessment, you are providing a level of service that justifies a premium fee.

4. Creating 'Flex-Modules' for Clinical Pivots

The fertility journey is rarely linear. A high-ticket program must be antifragileâ€”it should get better or adapt when things change. This is where Flex-Modules come in.

A Flex-Module is a pre-designed "sub-protocol" that you can swap into the roadmap if a client's clinical status changes. Examples include:

  1. The IVF Pivot: If a client decides to move to an egg retrieval, you swap the "Natural Conception Timing" module for the "Mitochondrial Priming & Stim Support" module.
  2. The Loss Recovery: If a client experiences a chemical pregnancy or miscarriage, you pause the 'O' pillar and move into the "Inflammation Clearing & Emotional Integration" module (Pillars 'I' and 'E').
  3. The Male Factor Surprise: If a repeat SA (Semen Analysis) shows a decline, you activate the "Spermatogenesis Intensive" module for the partner.
