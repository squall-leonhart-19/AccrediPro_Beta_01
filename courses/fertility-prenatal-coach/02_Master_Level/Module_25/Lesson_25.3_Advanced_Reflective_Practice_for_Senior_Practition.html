The Gibbs Reflective Cycle

This six-stage model forces a move from superficial description to deep action planning:

  • Description: What happened? (e.g., Client became defensive during the 'Nourish' audit).
  • Feelings: What were you thinking/feeling?
  • Evaluation: What was good/bad about the experience?
  • Analysis: What sense can you make of the situation? (Integration of science/theory).
  • Conclusion: What else could you have done?
  • Action Plan: If it arose again, what would you do?

3. Managing Countertransference in Fertility Coaching

In the fertility space, countertransference is exceptionally common. Most practitioners enter this field due to their own reproductive journeys. Countertransference occurs when the coach projects their own unresolved emotions, fears, or "success stories" onto the client.

If you successfully conceived using a specific ketogenic protocol (Nourish), you may unconsciously push that protocol onto a client for whom it is metabolically contraindicated. This is a failure of reflective practice. Advanced supervision helps you separate "My Journey" from "The Client's Physiology."

4. The 'Parallel Process' in Supervision

The Parallel Process is a phenomenon where the dynamics between the client and the coach are unconsciously recreated in the relationship between the coach and the supervisor. It is a powerful diagnostic tool for senior practitioners.

For example, if a coach feels "stuck" and helpless with a client who refuses to track their BBT (Cycle Awareness), and the coach then shows up to supervision feeling "stuck" and helpless, unable to present the case clearlyâ€”the parallel process is in play. 6. Developing Your Personal Philosophy of Supervision

As you transition into a senior role, you must define your Supervision Philosophy. This is your "North Star" that guides how you mentor others or how you engage with your own supervisor. An AccrediPro-standard philosophy should encompass:

  • The Developmental Approach: Tailoring supervision to the coach's current stage of growth (Novice to Expert).
  • The Integrative Lens: Ensuring that the biological (Cleanse/Optimize) and the psychological (Visualize/Embrace) are given equal weight.
  • Ethical Vigilance: A commitment to scope of practice and the prevention of "medicalizing" the coaching relationship.
  • Relational Safety: Creating a "brave space" where mistakes can be analyzed without shame.
