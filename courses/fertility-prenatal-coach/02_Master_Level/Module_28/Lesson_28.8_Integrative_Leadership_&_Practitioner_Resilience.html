2. Case Supervision: The Peer-Review Model

In Level 3 practice, you will inevitably encounter cases that feel "stuck." The complexity of co-morbidities (as seen in Lesson 4) combined with psychological crises (Lesson 5) can lead to clinical myopiaâ€”where the coach becomes too close to the case to see the solution. Professional supervision is the antidote.

The Peer-Review Model utilized by AccrediPro practitioners follows a structured 4-step process:

  1. The Presentation: De-identified case history, including the current "block."
  2. The Inquiry: Peers ask clarifying questions regarding the CONCEIVE pillars (e.g., "Have we looked at the husband's DNA fragmentation?").
  3. The Synthesis: The group identifies patterns the primary coach may have missed (e.g., an unaddressed autoimmune flare during the 'Nourish' phase).
  4. The Action Plan: Three specific, high-leverage interventions to pivot the case.

6. Sustaining the L3 Practice: The Resilience Blueprint

To remain in this field for decades rather than years, you must treat your own resilience as a clinical necessity. This involves Somatic Recovery. Because trauma is stored in the body, talking about a hard case is often insufficient. Research suggests that "bottom-up" interventions (movement, temperature change, rhythm) are more effective at clearing the physiological residue of a high-crisis session.

Consider the "Rule of Three" for L3 Practitioners:

  1. Three Minutes of Somatic Discharge: Immediately after a session (shaking, dancing, or splashing cold water on the face).
  2. Three Hours of "Non-Fertility" Focus: Each day, engaging in activities completely unrelated to reproduction or health.
  3. Three Days of Quarterly Sabbatical: A total disconnect from the practice to reset the mirror neuron system.
