A 2022 industry analysis found that specialized health coaches command rates 44% higher than general wellness practitioners. How you deliver your expertise determines your "Business Burnout" risk. High-touch coaching is rewarding but lacks scalability, while digital products offer scale but may lack the clinical nuance required for complex fertility cases.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a "niche" focus considered more profitable and clinically effective than general health coaching?

Specialization reduces the client's cognitive load, establishes you as an authority for a specific problem (e.g., PCOS), and allows for higher pricing due to the "expert" status. Clinically, it allows you to master a specific set of physiological protocols rather than being a "jack of all trades."

2. How does the cost of fertility coaching compare to the ROI of traditional ART (Assisted Reproductive Technology)?

While coaching may cost $1,500-$3,000, it addresses the underlying physiological "soil." Given that a single IVF cycle costs $12k-$17k, coaching that improves the success rate or prevents a failed cycle offers a massive financial and emotional ROI.
