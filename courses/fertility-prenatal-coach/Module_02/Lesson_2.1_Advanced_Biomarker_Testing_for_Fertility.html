Ovarian Reserve & Egg Quality Markers

While we cannot "test" egg quality directly without an embryologist, we can use hormonal proxies to assess the ovarian environment. The primary marker is AMH (Anti-MÃ¼llerian Hormone). Unlike FSH, which fluctuates significantly throughout the cycle, AMH remains relatively stable. However, AMH must be interpreted alongside age and lifestyle factors.

Practitioner Insight

Low AMH does not necessarily mean "infertile." It indicates a lower quantity of eggs (reserve). Egg quality is largely determined by mitochondrial health and inflammatory status (the "I" in P.R.I.M.E.), which we can influence through coaching.

Metabolic & Thyroid Synergy

The thyroid gland and the ovaries are deeply interconnected via the HPO (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Ovarian) axis. Thyroid receptors are present on human oocytes. If the thyroid is sluggish (even within "normal" ranges), the ovaries may not receive the signal to mature a follicle correctly.

Advanced Male Factor Profiling

In 40-50% of infertility cases, the male factor is a contributing or primary cause. A standard semen analysis only looks at "moving parts" (count, motility, morphology). It does not look at the integrity of the genetic cargo.

Advanced profiling should include a Sperm DNA Fragmentation Index (DFI). High fragmentation (>25-30%) is associated with poor embryo development and recurrent early loss. This is often driven by oxidative stress, which can be mitigated through the "I" (Inflammatory Inhibition) and "M" (Metabolic Mapping) pillars of our protocol.

Interpreting the HPO Axis: FSH and LH

Day 3 testing of FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) and LH (Luteinizing Hormone) provides a snapshot of the communication between the brain and the ovaries. An FSH >10 IU/L on Day 3 may suggest the brain is "shouting" at the ovaries to respond, indicating diminished reserve. Conversely, an LH:FSH ratio of >2:1 is a hallmark biomarker for PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a TSH of 4.2 mIU/L considered problematic for a client trying to conceive, even if the lab report says it is "Normal"?

While 4.2 is within the general population range, fertility research shows that TSH levels above 2.5 mIU/L are associated with increased risk of miscarriage and decreased implantation success. For pregnancy, we aim for an optimal range of 0.5 - 2.0 mIU/L.

2. Which marker is the most accurate proxy for the quantity of remaining follicles (ovarian reserve)?

AMH (Anti-MÃ¼llerian Hormone) is the gold standard for assessing ovarian reserve, as it is produced by the follicles themselves and remains relatively stable throughout the menstrual cycle.
