Designing the P.R.I.M.E. Curriculum

A successful group program isn't just a series of random talks; it is a structured journey. By using The P.R.I.M.E. Protocolâ„¢, you ensure that every participant receives a comprehensive biological and emotional reset. This methodology provides the "skeleton" for your curriculum, allowing you to deliver standardized high-value content while leaving room for personalized group Q&A.

P: Physiological Profiling (Week 1-2)

Participants complete self-assessment labs or symptom trackers. In a group setting, you teach them how to read their own markers (e.g., basal body temperature, cervical mucus, or bloodwork summaries).

R & I: Rhythmic Regulation & Inflammatory Inhibition (Week 3-6)

The core of the program. You deliver masterclasses on cycle syncing and anti-inflammatory nutrition. This is where the group "challenges" (e.g., a 7-day sugar detox) create the most community engagement.

M & E: Metabolic/Microbiome & Environmental Equilibrium (Week 7-10)

Deep dives into gut health and home toxin removal. The final weeks focus on emotional resilience, preparing the group for the next steps in their fertility journey.

The Launch Runway Strategy

Most failed programs suffer from a "silent launch"â€”the practitioner simply posts a link and hopes for the best. Professional launches require a Launch Runway of at least 30 days. According to a 2023 meta-analysis of digital product sales, programs with a structured pre-launch phase converted at a 312% higher rate than those without.

The 3-Phase Launch Framework

1. Awareness (Days 1-14): Free content, webinars, and lead magnets. Goal: Grow the interest list.
2. Evaluation (Days 15-24): Case studies, "sneak peeks" of the curriculum, and addressing objections.
3. Conversion (Days 25-30): Open Cart. Use scarcity (limited spots) and urgency (early bird pricing).

Pricing & Tiered Packages

When launching group programs, offering a "Good-Better-Best" pricing structure can maximize revenue. This captures both the price-sensitive client and the high-end client who wants more access to you. Research indicates that 15-20% of group participants will opt for the most expensive tier if it offers significant additional value.

Tier 1: Essential ($497 - $697)

Includes access to the curriculum, the community forum, and the weekly group Q&A calls. Best for those who are self-motivated.

Tier 2: Enhanced ($897 - $1,197)

Includes everything in Tier 1, plus a personalized lab review or one 30-minute private strategy session. This is usually your "Best Seller."

Tier 3: VIP ($2,497+)

Includes everything in Tier 1, plus full 1:1 support throughout the duration of the program. Limited to 3-5 people.

Technology & Delivery Stack

The "tech hurdle" is often the biggest barrier for new practitioners. However, modern tools have made delivery seamless. You need three core components: a Content Hub (where videos/PDFs live), a Communication Tool (for live calls), and a Community Platform (for interaction).

For online workshops, simplicity is key. A 90-minute masterclass can serve as a "mini-launch" for your larger group program. A 2023 survey of health coaches found that 74% use Zoom for delivery, while 52% use dedicated LMS (Learning Management Systems) like Kajabi or Teachable for content hosting.

The "Lean Tech" Recommendation

If you are just starting, do not over-invest. Use Zoom for calls, Google Drive for handouts, and a private Facebook Group or Slack for community. You can upgrade to all-in-one platforms like Kajabi once you have your first $10k launch under your belt.

Managing Group Dynamics

Group coaching requires a different skill set than 1:1 work. You are no longer just a coach; you are a facilitator. You must manage diverse personalities, ensure everyone feels heard, and maintain the "vibe" of the group, especially when dealing with the sensitive topic of infertility.

Key strategies for successful facilitation include:

  • The 70/30 Rule: Spend 30% of the call delivering new content and 70% facilitating group coaching and Q&A.
  • Boundary Setting: Clearly state that group calls are not for personal medical crises. Establish a "Community Agreement" at the start.
  • Micro-Wins: Celebrate "small victories" (e.g., a client seeing improved BBT charts) to maintain momentum and hope within the group.
