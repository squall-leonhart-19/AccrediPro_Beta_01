Key Longevity Terms

Epigenome

The chemical compounds and proteins that can attach to DNA and direct such actions as turning genes on or off.

Sirtuins

A family of NAD+-dependent protein deacetylases that regulate cellular health and aging.

Hormesis

A biological phenomenon where a beneficial effect results from exposure to low doses of an agent that is otherwise toxic or even lethal in high doses.

Senescence

The condition or process of cellular aging where cells stop dividing but remain metabolically active, often secreting inflammatory factors.

1. The Information Theory of Aging

For decades, the prevailing view of aging was the "Accumulation of Damage" theory—the idea that our bodies simply wear out like an old car. However, modern research, led by figures like Dr. David Sinclair, suggests a more profound mechanism: The Information Theory of Aging.

This theory posits that aging is caused by the loss of epigenetic information. Think of your DNA as the "digital" hardware of a computer (the genome) and the epigenome as the "analog" software. While the hardware remains largely intact as we age, the software becomes corrupted. Cells lose their identity; a skin cell forgets it is a skin cell and begins to express genes it shouldn't, leading to cellular dysfunction and eventual senescence.

2. Sirtuins: Guardians of the Genome

At the center of this epigenetic regulation are Sirtuins. In humans, there are seven sirtuins (SIRT1-SIRT7). These enzymes serve as the "guardians" of our cells, performing two primary tasks: regulating gene expression and repairing DNA.

The conflict of aging arises when DNA damage occurs. Sirtuins must leave their post of "gene silencing" (keeping the epigenome tidy) to assist in DNA repair. If the damage is chronic—due to environmental toxins, radiation, or poor lifestyle—the sirtuins are spread too thin. They fail to return to their original posts, leaving genes "open" that should be "closed," accelerating the aging process.

3. NAD+ Metabolism & Cellular Fuel

Sirtuins have a unique requirement: they are NAD+-dependent. Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide (NAD+) is a coenzyme found in all living cells and is essential for energy metabolism. Without NAD+, sirtuins cannot function. They are essentially "fuel-starved" as we age.

A 2021 study published in Nature Aging confirmed that NAD+ levels decline precipitously with age, largely due to the overactivation of an enzyme called CD38 (which destroys NAD+) and increased demand from PARPs (DNA repair enzymes). When sirtuins compete with PARPs for a dwindling supply of NAD+, the sirtuins often lose, leading to the epigenetic drift described in the Information Theory of Aging.

4. Caloric Restriction Mimetics

For decades, caloric restriction (CR) was the only proven way to extend lifespan in mammals. However, CR is difficult for most clients to sustain. This led to the discovery of Caloric Restriction Mimetics (CRMs)—compounds that trigger the same longevity pathways (like sirtuins and AMPK) without the need for extreme hunger.

  • Resveratrol: Found in grape skins, this polyphenol is a potent SIRT1 activator. It acts like the "accelerator pedal" for sirtuin activity.
  • NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide): A direct precursor to NAD+. If Resveratrol is the accelerator, NMN is the "fuel" in the tank.
  • Quercetin & Fisetin: Known as senolytics, these compounds help the body clear out "zombie" (senescent) cells that secrete inflammatory cytokines (the SASP phenotype).
  • Spermidine: Found in wheat germ and aged cheese, it triggers autophagy—the cellular recycling process—independent of sirtuins but synergistic with them.

5. Telomere Maintenance

While sirtuins manage the "software," telomeres protect the "hardware." Telomeres are the protective caps at the ends of chromosomes. Every time a cell divides, the telomeres shorten. When they become too short, the cell enters senescence or apoptosis (the Hayflick Limit).

Research has shown that telomere length is not just a clock, but a highly dynamic feature influenced by the environment. A landmark study (Epel et al., 2004) found that women under high perceived stress had telomeres equivalent to 10 years of additional aging compared to low-stress peers. Conversely, SIRT6 is essential for stabilizing telomeres, bridging the gap between sirtuin science and telomere biology.

Key Takeaways

  • Aging as Information Loss: Longevity is about maintaining the "analog" epigenetic instructions of the cell.
  • The NAD+ Dependency: Sirtuins cannot function without sufficient NAD+ levels, which naturally decline by 50% every 20 years of life.
  • SIRT1 & SIRT3: These are the most clinically relevant sirtuins for metabolic health and mitochondrial protection.
  • Hormesis is Key: Comfort is the enemy of longevity; low-level stress (fasting, exercise, temperature) is the primary trigger for sirtuin activation.
  • Supplement Synergy: Precursors (NMN/NR) and activators (Resveratrol/Quercetin) work best when integrated with a WHOLE lifestyle approach.
