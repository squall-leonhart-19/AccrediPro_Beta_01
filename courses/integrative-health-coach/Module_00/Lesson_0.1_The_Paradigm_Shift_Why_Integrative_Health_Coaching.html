1. The Collapse of the 'Sick-Care' Model

For the last century, the Western medical model has operated under a pathogenic paradigm‚Äîfocusing on the origins of disease. This "Sick-Care" model was brilliantly successful at addressing acute trauma, bacterial infections, and emergency surgical needs. If you have a broken leg or acute appendicitis, the conventional system is where you belong.

However, we are currently facing a systemic failure. The "find it and fix it" approach is fundamentally incompatible with chronic, lifestyle-driven dysfunction. While acute care requires a passive patient and an expert doctor, chronic health requires an active client and a collaborative partner.
