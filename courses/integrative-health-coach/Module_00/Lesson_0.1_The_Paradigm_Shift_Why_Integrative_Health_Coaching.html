1. The Failure of "Sick-Care": A Historical Analysis

For the past century, the modern medical system has operated on an Acute Care Model. This paradigm was revolutionary for its time, designed to combat infectious diseases, trauma, and surgical emergencies. If you have a broken leg or a bacterial infection, the current system is unparalleled.

However, the landscape of human suffering has shifted. We are no longer primarily dying from "fast" diseases (pathogens); we are dying from "slow" diseases (lifestyle). The medical system, often referred to as "Sick-Care," is designed to manage symptoms through pharmacology rather than resolving root causes. A 2022 study published in The Lancet highlighted that nearly 70% of all global deaths are now attributable to chronic, non-communicable diseases.
