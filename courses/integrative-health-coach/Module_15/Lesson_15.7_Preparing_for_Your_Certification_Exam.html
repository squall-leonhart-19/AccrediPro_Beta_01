Applying Knowledge to Client Scenarios

The most challenging questions on the exam are "Scenario Applications." These questions present a client profile and ask you to choose the best next step. Remember: always look for the root cause (Leverage Functional Systems) before suggesting a behavioral change.

Check Your Understanding

Test your readiness with these sample exam concepts.

Q1: A client presents with chronic bloating, brain fog, and skin breakouts. According to the ALIGN Methodâ„¢, which pillar should be addressed first?

Answer: Assess Bio-Individuality. Before suggesting a protocol, you must assess the client's unique markers (dietary triggers, stress levels, environmental toxins) to determine if the root is dysbiosis, food sensitivity, or hormonal imbalance.

Q2: You are designing a protocol for a high-stress executive. Why is "Guiding Behavioral Architecture" more effective than "Willpower"?

Answer: Behavioral Architecture focuses on redesigning the environment and habit loops (e.g., meal prepping, removing trigger foods), which reduces the cognitive load on a client who is already suffering from decision fatigue.
