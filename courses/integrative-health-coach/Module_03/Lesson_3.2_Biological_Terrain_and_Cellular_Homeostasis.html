3. Oxidative Stress and Redox Homeostasis

Oxidative stress is the biological equivalent of rust. It occurs when there is an imbalance between the production of Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) and the body's ability to neutralize them with antioxidants. A 2022 meta-analysis involving over 12,000 participants confirmed that oxidative damage to lipids and DNA is a primary driver in 85% of age-related metabolic disorders.

In the "Optimize Function" phase of our framework, we look at Redox Signaling. This is the process where ROS actually serve as signaling molecules to trigger repair. However, when the terrain is flooded with ROS‚Äîdue to ultra-processed oils, EMF exposure, or chronic hyperglycemia‚Äîthe repair signals are drowned out by "noise," leading to mitochondrial decay.
