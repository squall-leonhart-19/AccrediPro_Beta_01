5. Risk Management & Liability

As a supervisor, you face a unique legal concept: Vicarious Liability (Respondeat Superior). This means that under certain conditions, a supervisor can be held liable for the negligence of their supervisee. Protecting your business requires specific structural safeguards.

Legal & Contractual Essentials

What is the critical difference between "Professional Liability Insurance" and "Supervisor's Liability Insurance"?

Standard professional liability covers your direct actions with clients. Supervisor's liability (or an endorsement on your policy) specifically covers the advice you give to other practitioners and the potential liability arising from their actions under your guidance. Always ensure your policy explicitly mentions "Supervision" or "Mentoring of Professionals."

The Supervision Contract

Never enter a supervisory relationship without a formal contract. This document must define:

  • Nature of Oversight: Is it clinical supervision or business mentoring?
  • Emergency Procedures: How the coach reaches you during a client crisis.
  • Termination Clause: How either party can exit the relationship if ethical alignment fails.
  • Indemnification: Clauses that protect the supervisor from the coach's independent malpractice.
