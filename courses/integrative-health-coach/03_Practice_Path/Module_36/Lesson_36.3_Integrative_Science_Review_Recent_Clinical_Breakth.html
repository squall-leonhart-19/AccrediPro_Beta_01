4. Chronobiology: Circadian Rhythms & Hormonal Balance

Chronobiology is no longer just about sleep; it is about Circadian Medicine. Every organ has a peripheral clock. When we eat, move, and view light, we are setting these clocks. Disruption, known as Circadian Misalignment, is now linked to a 40% increased risk of metabolic syndrome (Arble et al., 2024).

Practitioners must now coach clients on "Light Hygiene" and "Time-Restricted Eating" not just for weight loss, but for Hormonal Synergy. For example, cortisol and melatonin have an inverse relationship that is governed by the Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN). If a client views blue light at 10 PM, they suppress melatonin and artificially extend the cortisol curve, leading to systemic inflammation.
