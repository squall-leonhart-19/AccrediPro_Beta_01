2. Prioritization: When to Address the Gut vs. the Nervous System

One of the most common mistakes in integrative health is attempting to "heal the gut" while the client remains in a state of chronic sympathetic dominance (fight-or-flight). If the nervous system perceives a threat, it downregulates blood flow to the digestive tract and inhibits the production of Secretory IgA (SIgA), making gut repair nearly impossible.

2. Why is "Metabolic Endotoxemia" a central concept in chronic disease management?

It explains the mechanism by which gut dysfunction becomes systemic disease. When LPS (endotoxins) leak into the blood, they trigger TLR4 receptors throughout the body, causing low-grade, chronic inflammation that drives insulin resistance, obesity, and neurodegeneration.
