A: Assessing Bio-Individuality

Assessment in the ALIGN Methodâ„¢ goes beyond standard blood panels. We must look at the metabolites of hormones to understand how a client is processing them. For example, two women may have identical serum estrogen levels, but one may be metabolizing estrogen through the protective 2-OH pathway, while the other uses the proliferative 4-OH or 16-OH pathways, significantly altering their risk profile and symptom presentation.

Key assessment tools in this phase include:

  • Functional Blood Chemistry: Looking for "optimal" markers (e.g., Ferritin > 50 ng/mL for thyroid health, even if the "normal" range starts at 15).
  • Symptom Mapping: Using the Hormonal Symptom Inventory to correlate data with the client's lived experience.
  • Genetic Markers: Evaluating SNPs like COMT (estrogen/catecholamine clearance) or MTHFR (methylation support for hormone detox).

Assessment: Sarah's labs showed "normal" TSH (3.4) and "normal" fasting glucose (98). However, functional assessment revealed a reverse T3 (rT3) elevation and a flat diurnal cortisol curve (low morning, high night).

Intervention (ALIGN):

  • L: Addressed blood sugar stability to reduce adrenal demand.

Outcome: After 12 weeks, Sarah's rT3 normalized, her morning energy returned, and she lost 8 lbs of inflammatory visceral fat without increasing exercise intensity.

G: Guide Behavioral Architecture

Behavioral architecture is where most hormonal protocols fail. We cannot out-supplement a lifestyle that the endocrine system perceives as a constant threat. We must help clients design environments that support circadian biology. This includes light exposure therapy (morning sunlight to set the master clock) and habit-stacking protein intake to stabilize the insulin-glucagon ratio.

A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that circadian-aligned eating windows (Time-Restricted Feeding) improved insulin sensitivity by 22% and lowered systemic inflammation markers (hs-CRP) by 18%, independent of caloric restriction.
