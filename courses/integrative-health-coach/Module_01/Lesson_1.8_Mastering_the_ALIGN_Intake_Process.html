Bio-Individuality

The unique nutritional, environmental, and biological needs of an individual based on genetics and lifestyle history.

Antecedents

Factors that predispose an individual to a condition (e.g., genetics, birth history, early childhood trauma).

Triggers

Discrete events that initiate a health decline or symptom flare (e.g., infection, surgery, acute stress).

Mediators

Ongoing factors that contribute to the persistence of symptoms (e.g., poor sleep, chronic inflammatory diet).

We are not looking for a name for the symptoms; we are looking for the underlying systemic dysfunction that allows those symptoms to exist.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Personalized Medicine found that practitioners who utilized a comprehensive, systems-based intake process had a 42% higher rate of client adherence compared to those using standard health histories. Why? Because the client feels truly "seen" and understands the logic behind the suggested lifestyle changes.

The Art of the Clinical Interview

The clinical interview is where "Assess" meets "Guide" (the G in ALIGN). It is not just about what the client says, but how they say it. Research suggests that 70% of communication is non-verbal. An expert practitioner listens for "The Story Behind the Symptom."

The "OARS" Technique in Intake

Derived from Motivational Interviewing, OARS is essential during the ALIGN intake:

  • Open-Ended Questions: "Tell me more about when your energy first started to dip."

Mapping the Health Timeline

The Health Timeline is a visual representation of a client's life from preconception to the present. We look for Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators (ATMs). This allows the practitioner to see the "domino effect" of health issues.

For example, a client presenting with adult-onset eczema may have an Antecedent of C-section birth (altered microbiome), a Trigger of high-dose antibiotics for a UTI at age 22, and a Mediator of chronic stress in their current job. Without the timeline, you might only suggest a topical cream; with the ALIGN Method, you address the gut-skin axis.

Red Flags & Referral Protocols

As covered in Lesson 7, the intake process is your primary defense against operating outside your scope. If a client reveals any of the following during the ALIGN intake, an immediate referral to a medical professional is required:

  • Unexplained Weight Loss: More than 10% of body weight in 6 months without trying.
  • Neurological Changes: Sudden numbness, slurred speech, or loss of balance.
  • Severe Abdominal Pain: Localized, sharp pain (potential appendicitis or gallbladder crisis).
  • Mental Health Crisis: Suicidal ideation or signs of clinical psychosis.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between an Antecedent and a Trigger in the ALIGN Health Timeline?

An Antecedent is a pre-existing factor that "sets the stage" (like genetics or birth history), whereas a Trigger is a specific event that "flips the switch" and initiates symptoms (like an infection or a car accident).

2. A client reports that her bloating is worse when she is stressed at work. In the ALIGN Method, what would "Stress" be categorized as?

Stress in this context is a "Mediator"‚Äîan ongoing factor that contributes to the persistence or exacerbation of symptoms.
