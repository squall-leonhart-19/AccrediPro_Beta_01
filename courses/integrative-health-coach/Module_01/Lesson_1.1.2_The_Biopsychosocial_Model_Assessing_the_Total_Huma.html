Key Concepts to Master

Allostatic Load

The cumulative "wear and tear" on the body which grows when an individual is exposed to repeated or chronic stress.

Exposome

The measure of all the exposures of an individual in a lifetime and how those exposures relate to health.

Psychological Flexibility

The ability to stay in the present moment and change or persist in behaviors in the service of chosen values.

The Evolution: From Machine to Ecosystem

For over a century, the Biomedical Model dominated Western health. This model views the body as a complex machine where disease is merely a "malfunction" of biological parts. While this paradigm led to extraordinary breakthroughs in acute care and surgery, it fundamentally fails to address the chronic, multi-factorial conditions that define modern health challenges.

In 1977, psychiatrist George Engel proposed the Biopsychosocial Model, arguing that to understand health, we must consider the interplay between biological, psychological, and social factors. Mental Resilience & Psychological Flexibility

Assessments must go beyond "Are you stressed?" We must evaluate Psychological Flexibility. This is not the absence of stress, but the ability to remain present and take value-based action even when difficult emotions arise. Chronic psychological stress manifests as Allostatic Load, which physically degrades the hippocampus and increases systemic inflammation (IL-6 and CRP).

The Spiritual Mediator: Purpose as Biology

In the W.H.O.L.E. Health Methodâ„¢, "Spiritual" does not necessarily refer to religion. It refers to a sense of meaning, purpose, and transcendence. Research in psychoneuroimmunology has shown that having a strong "sense of purpose" (Ikigai) is a biological mediator of health.

A 2019 study published in JAMA Network Open (n=6,985) demonstrated that individuals with the lowest sense of purpose had a 2.4x higher risk of death during the study period compared to those with the highest sense of purpose. Purpose appears to regulate the Conserved Transcriptional Response to Adversity (CTRA), a genomic pattern characterized by increased expression of pro-inflammatory genes and decreased expression of antiviral genes.

Environmental Health: The Exposome & Light Hygiene

The assessment of the "Total Human" is incomplete without evaluating the Exposome. Our modern environment is an evolutionary mismatch for our biology. Key areas for assessment include:

  • Light Hygiene: Blue light exposure after sunset suppresses melatonin, disrupting the glymphatic system's ability to clear metabolic waste from the brain.
  • Endocrine Disruptors: Phthalates and BPA in household products that mimic hormones and disrupt metabolic signaling.
  • Air Quality: Particulate matter (PM2.5) exposure is directly linked to systemic oxidative stress and cardiovascular risk.

The W.H.O.L.E. Integration: Assessing the Intersections

The mastery of the Integrative Health Coach lies in seeing the intersections. A biological symptom (e.g., bloating) may be triggered by a psychological state (anxiety), which is exacerbated by a social condition (isolation), and maintained by an environmental factor (poor light hygiene).

Key Lesson Takeaways
  • The Biomedical model is insufficient for chronic care; the Biopsychosocial-Spiritual model is the gold standard for integrative assessment.
  • Social connection is a biological necessity, with an impact on longevity equal to major clinical risk factors.

  • Assessments should identify the intersections between domains rather than viewing them in silos.
