Exam Blueprint & Format

The AccrediPro Autoimmune Specialist Exam is a criterion-referenced assessment. This means your performance is measured against a fixed set of standards‚Äîspecifically, the core competencies of the A.R.M.O.R. Method‚Ñ¢. The exam consists of 20 multiple-choice and scenario-based questions. You must answer at least 16 correctly (80%) to earn your certification.

Clinical Decision-Making: Scenario Strategies

Scenario-based questions present a client profile and ask for an intervention. These test your ability to distinguish between "good" and "best" answers. Use the Elimination Strategy: remove options that fall outside your scope of practice (e.g., diagnosing or prescribing medication) or options that address symptoms rather than root causes.

Final Readiness Checklist

Before you click "Start Exam," ensure you have completed the following steps to maximize your performance:

  • Review the "Red Flags": Be certain you know when to refer a client to an MD or ER (e.g., sudden neurological changes, unexplained weight loss).
  • Master Lab Ranges: Distinguish between "Standard Reference Ranges" and "Functional Ranges" for markers like Ferritin, Vitamin D, and hs-CRP.
  • The "ARMOR" Sequence: Be able to define each letter of the A.R.M.O.R. Method‚Ñ¢ and provide one specific intervention for each.
  • Scope of Practice: Remember, as a specialist, you educate and support; you do not treat, cure, or diagnose.
