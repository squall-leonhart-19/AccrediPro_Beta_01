Value-Based Pricing

Setting prices based on the perceived or estimated value of a product or service to the customer rather than according to the cost of the product or historical prices.

High-Ticket Package

A comprehensive bundle of services (usually $2,500 - $10,000+) designed to take a client from a state of disease to a state of remission.

Price Elasticity of Health

The economic principle that health-related services often have low elasticity; clients are willing to pay significant premiums for life-altering outcomes.

The Sunk Cost Commitment

A psychological phenomenon where clients who invest more financially are statistically more likely to adhere to strict dietary and lifestyle protocols.

The Value-Based Paradigm

Most health practitioners begin by charging "by the hour." This is a fundamental mistake. When you charge hourly, you are incentivized to take longer, and your client is incentivized to want shorter sessions to save money. This creates a misalignment of goals. In autoimmune recovery, the "value" isn't the 60 minutes you spend on Zoom; it is the reduction in antibodies, the return of energy, and the avoidance of $50,000/year biologic medications.

Tiered Architecture Models

While your Signature Program is your flagship, offering tiers allows you to capture different segments of the market. However, even your "lowest" tier should be a package, never an hour of time.

The 3-Tier Model

The Essential (Silver)

90-day A.R.M.O.R. foundational protocol. Self-paced modules + 3 group Q&A calls. Price point: $997 - $1,497.

The Signature (Gold)

6-month full A.R.M.O.R. implementation. Bi-weekly 1:1 calls + Lab reviews + Voxer support. Price point: $3,500 - $5,500.

The VIP (Platinum)

12-month concierge support. Weekly calls + Home visits/Kitchen pantry cleanouts + Unlimited support. Price point: $10,000+.

Communicating ROI & Value

To successfully sell high-ticket packages, you must move the conversation from "cost" to "investment." You do this by quantifying the Cost of Inaction (COI). For an autoimmune client, the COI includes:

  • Lost wages due to "brain fog" or fatigue (often 10-20% of annual income).
  • Cost of specialized biologics ($30k - $80k per year if insurance fails).
  • Out-of-pocket costs for emergency room visits during flares.
  • The intangible cost of missed family events and reduced quality of life.
