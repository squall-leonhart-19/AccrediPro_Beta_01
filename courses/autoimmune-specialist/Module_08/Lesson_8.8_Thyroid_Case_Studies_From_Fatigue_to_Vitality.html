The Gut-Thyroid Axis Connection

We cannot discuss thyroid autoimmunity without returning to Mucosal Integrity. The gut-thyroid axis is a bidirectional highway. Low thyroid hormone slows gut motility (leading to SIBO), while gut dysbiosis prevents the conversion of T4 to T3. A 2021 study involving 1,200 participants found that those with thyroid autoimmunity had significantly lower microbial diversity, particularly in Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus species.

When working with thyroid clients, always assess for:

  • Hypochlorhydria: Low stomach acid, common in Hashimoto's, leads to poor protein digestion and mineral malabsorption (Zinc/Selenium).
  • Intestinal Permeability: LPS translocation triggers systemic cytokines (IL-6, TNF-alpha) that downregulate thyroid receptor sensitivity.
  • Bile Stasis: Thyroid hormones are metabolized in the liver and excreted via bile; poor bile flow leads to hormone recirculation and toxicity.
