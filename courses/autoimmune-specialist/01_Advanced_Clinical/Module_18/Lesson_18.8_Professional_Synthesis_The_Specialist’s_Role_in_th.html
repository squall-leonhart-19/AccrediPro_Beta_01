2. Ethical Boundaries & Scope of Practice

Your role is to optimize the host (the client) while the physician manages the disease. Understanding where your scope ends is critical for client safety and professional longevity. Never adjust, recommend, or comment on prescription dosages. Your focus is on the physiological terrain that determines how the body responds to both the disease and the medication.

1. Why is a 6-12 month program structure considered the gold standard for autoimmune recovery?

Autoimmune recovery requires immune retraining and mucosal healing (e.g., intestinal lining turnover and T-regulatory cell stabilization), which are biological processes that take months, not weeks. Shorter programs often lead to "rebound flares" because the underlying immune memory has not been addressed.
