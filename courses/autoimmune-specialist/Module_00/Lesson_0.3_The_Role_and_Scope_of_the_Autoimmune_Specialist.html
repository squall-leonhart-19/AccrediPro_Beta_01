3. Legal and Ethical Considerations

Operating as an Autoimmune Specialist requires a "Safety First" approach. You must be aware of the laws in your specific jurisdiction regarding the Practice of Nutrition and Health Coaching.

82%
of practitioners who face legal challenges do so because of "unlicensed practice of medicine" or "making curative claims."

The Three Pillars of Practitioner Protection:

1. Informed Consent & Disclosure: Every client must sign a document stating they understand you are not a doctor and that your services do not replace medical advice.

2. Product Neutrality: While you may recommend supplements (as we will cover in Module 3), you must never claim a supplement "treats" a disease. You are "supporting biological function."

3. Referral Thresholds: You must have a list of "Red Flags" that trigger an immediate referral to urgent care or a specialist (e.g., sudden unexplained weight loss, severe neurological changes, or suicidal ideation).

6. Measuring Clinical Impact

How do we know we are successful if we aren't "treating" the disease? We use Functional Outcome Markers. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that systematic tracking of "Subjective Vitality" and "Symptom Burden" was a more accurate predictor of long-term remission in autoimmune cases than isolated inflammatory markers.

As a specialist, you will track:

  • Flare Frequency: Are the episodes getting further apart?
  • Flare Duration: When they do happen, are they shorter?
  • Recovery Time: How quickly can the client bounce back after a stressor?
  • Resilience: Can the client now tolerate foods or environments that previously triggered them?

  • Success is measured through improved systemic resilience and reduced symptom burden.
  • The Specialist acts as a "Clinical Detective," using the C.A.L.M. framework to find unique triggers.
