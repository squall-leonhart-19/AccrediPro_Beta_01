Retention Science: The Value Loop

Retention is not about "pleasing" the client; it is about demonstrating progress. Autoimmune recovery is non-linear. Clients will experience flares even while doing everything "right." If they don't have a system to track the "invisible wins," they will quit during a flare.

To maintain a high retention rate (aim for >85% for 6-month programs), you must implement a "Value Loop":

  • Bi-Weekly Progress Tracking: Use a standardized symptom scale (0-10) across 5 categories: Energy, Digestion, Pain, Cognition, and Mood.
  • Celebration of Non-Scale Victories (NSVs): Documenting when a client can finally walk 2 miles or sleep through the night.
  • The "R" Phase (Remission Maintenance): As the client feels better, the focus shifts to lifestyle sustainability to prevent future flares.

Handling Client Flares: The Retention Critical Point

A "Flare" is the number one reason for client churn in an autoimmune practice. The client feels "defeated" and assumes the protocol isn't working. Your system must have a Flare Protocol ready to deploy.

A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) on chronic disease management found that "self-efficacy" (the belief that one can manage their condition) was the strongest predictor of long-term retention. By providing a Flare Protocol (e.g., specific anti-inflammatory diet adjustments, vagus nerve stimulation exercises, and oxidative stress mitigation), you turn a "failure" into a "learning event."

Measuring Success: KPIs for Practice Growth

To build a $100k+ practice, you must track your numbers. Vague feelings about "being busy" are not a business strategy.

LTV (Lifetime Value)

The total revenue a single client generates over their entire relationship with you. High LTV = Scalable Business.

Churn Rate

The percentage of clients who stop working with you before completing their program. Aim for < 15%.

NPS (Net Promoter Score)

A measure of how likely clients are to refer you. Collected via a simple 1-10 survey at the end of Module 1 and Module 3.
