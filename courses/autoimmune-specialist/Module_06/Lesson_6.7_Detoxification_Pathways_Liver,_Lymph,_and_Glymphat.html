2. The Glutathione Nexus: The Master Antioxidant

Glutathione (GSH) is the most abundant endogenous antioxidant and a critical cofactor for Phase II conjugation. In autoimmune conditions, the demand for glutathione often exceeds its production. A 2021 study involving patients with Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) found that intracellular glutathione levels were 30-40% lower compared to healthy controls, directly correlating with increased disease activity scores (n=112, p<0.01).

85% of Autoimmune Patients

A meta-analysis of clinical data suggests that up to 85% of individuals with chronic inflammatory conditions exhibit SNPs (Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms) in the GST (Glutathione S-Transferase) genes, significantly reducing their ability to neutralize xenobiotics.

Why Depletion Happens in Autoimmunity:

  • Oxidative Stress: Chronic inflammation consumes GSH to neutralize Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS).
  • Nutrient Deficiencies: Lack of precursor amino acids (Cysteine, Glycine, Glutamate) or cofactors (Selenium, B2).
  • Toxic Overload: Heavy metals like Mercury have a high affinity for sulfur, "stealing" glutathione and rendering it inactive.
