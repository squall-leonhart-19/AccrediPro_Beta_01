Mitigating Medication Toxicity

Oxidative Stress Mitigation (the 'O' in A.R.M.O.R.) is essential for protecting healthy organs from the "friendly fire" of long-term medication use. For example, long-term use of Methotrexate can deplete folate and increase homocysteine levels, raising cardiovascular risk. Similarly, many medications are metabolized in the liver, increasing the demand for Glutathione.

Targeted Antioxidant Support

When a client is on a heavy pharmaceutical load, the Specialist should focus on N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC) and Alpha-Lipoic Acid. These do not interfere with most autoimmune medications (always check contraindications) but provide the liver with the necessary precursors to process and clear drug metabolites efficiently.

Key Takeaways

  • Synergy, Not Competition: A.R.M.O.R. is designed to work alongside conventional medicine to improve outcomes and reduce side effects.
  • Diagnostic Coordination: Use medical labs to track progress while using Antigen Mapping to identify the "hidden" drivers of flares.
  • Protective Support: Use Oxidative Stress Mitigation to protect the liver and mitochondria from the metabolic burden of pharmaceuticals.
  • Scope of Practice: Always defer medication changes to the prescribing physician while providing the nutritional foundation for successful tapering.
  • The Gut-Medication Connection: Prioritize mucosal repair for any client on long-term anti-inflammatory or steroid therapy.
