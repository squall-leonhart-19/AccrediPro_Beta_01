We breathe approximately 11,000 liters of air and should consume 2-3 liters of water daily. If these two inputs are contaminated, the liver and kidneys are under constant "maintenance" stress, leaving little reserve for immune regulation.

Indoor Air: The 5x Pollution Rule

The EPA estimates that indoor air is 2 to 5 times more polluted than outdoor air. For autoimmune practitioners, air stewardship involves three steps:

  1. Source Removal: Eliminating "plug-in" air fresheners, paraffin candles, and high-VOC cleaning products.
  2. Filtration: Utilizing HEPA 13 or 14 (Medical Grade) filters with activated carbon for VOC absorption.
  3. Ventilation: Strategic "flushing" of the home to reduce CO2 and stagnant particulate matter.

    4. Safe Detoxification Support

    Detoxification is not a "cleanse" but a physiological process requiring specific nutrients and metabolic energy. In the Maintenance phase, we utilize gentle tools to support the Nrf2 pathway and lymphatic drainage.

    Infrared Sauna Therapy

    Saunas facilitate the excretion of heavy metals (cadmium, lead, mercury) through sweat, often at higher concentrations than found in urine.

    • Mechanism: Heat shock proteins (HSPs) act as molecular chaperones, assisting in protein folding and preventing the aggregates often seen in neuro-autoimmunity.
    • Protocol: 15-20 minutes, 3x weekly, followed by an immediate shower to prevent reabsorption.

    The liver's Phase II conjugation requires methyl donors (B12, Folate, TMG). Many autoimmune clients carry MTHFR polymorphisms that hinder this. Maintenance should include "food-first" methylation support: beets, dark leafy greens, and cruciferous vegetables (to support sulforaphane-mediated glutathione production).
