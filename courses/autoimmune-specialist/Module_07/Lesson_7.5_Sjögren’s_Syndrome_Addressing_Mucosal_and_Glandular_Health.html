A critical aspect of SS is the Interferon Signature. Approximately 60-80% of patients exhibit an upregulation of interferon-stimulated genes, which drives the maturation of B-cells into plasma cells, leading to the production of anti-SSA (Ro) and anti-SSB (La) antibodies. This chronic B-cell hyperactivation is what places SS patients at a significantly higher risk for B-cell non-Hodgkin lymphoma compared to the general population.

A 2022 study published in Frontiers in Immunology found that EBV-encoded small RNAs (EBERs) were present in the salivary glands of 85% of pSS patients. When mapping antigens, always screen for chronic latent viral loads and consider protocols that support antiviral immunity.

This creates a feed-forward loop of inflammation that sustains the autoimmune attack even after the initial trigger is gone.
