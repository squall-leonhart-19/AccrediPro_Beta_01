Modern Biological Reality: The Liver-Gut Axis

While the ancients focused on "purgatives," modern science focuses on Biotransformation. The liver does not "store" toxins; it converts them.

Phase I (Modification): Uses the CYP450 enzymes to make a toxin more reactive (often making it more dangerous temporarily).
Phase II (Conjugation): Attaches a molecule (like glutathione or a methyl group) to the toxin to make it water-soluble.
Phase III (Excretion): The final transport out of the cell into bile or urine.
