Ethical Considerations & Scope of Practice

To be a successful practitioner, you must operate within your legal scope of practice. Unless you are a licensed medical doctor, you do not "diagnose," "treat," or "cure" diseases. Instead, you "assess metabolic function" and "support the body's innate detoxification systems." This distinction is vital for your legal protection and professional reputation.

Professional Boundaries Checklist
  • Always use a signed Client Agreement (template provided in L6).
  • Refer clients to a medical professional if you suspect underlying pathology.
  • Focus on education and lifestyle modification rather than medical intervention.
  • Maintain strict confidentiality of client health records (HIPAA/GDPR compliance).
