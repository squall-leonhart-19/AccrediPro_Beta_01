Estrobolome

A collection of bacteria in the gut which are capable of metabolizing and modulating the body's circulating estrogen.

Beta-Glucuronidase

An enzyme produced by certain gut bacteria that "un-conjugates" toxins and estrogens, making them re-absorbable into the bloodstream.

Enterohepatic Circulation

The process where substances are metabolized in the liver, excreted into the bile, and then reabsorbed from the small intestine back to the liver.

Cholagogues

Substances that stimulate the contraction of the gallbladder to promote the discharge of bile into the duodenum.

The Estrobolome Mechanism: The "Unzipping" Problem

In Phase 3 (Restore Bio-transformation), we taught the liver to "conjugate" toxinsâ€”essentially wrapping them in a chemical "envelope" (like glucuronic acid) so they are water-soluble and safe. However, certain dysbiotic bacteria in the gut produce beta-glucuronidase. This enzyme acts like a pair of scissors, cutting the "envelope" open.

Once "unzipped," the toxin or estrogen becomes fat-soluble again and immediately passes through the gut lining back into the portal vein. This is the root cause of many "detox reactions" or "healing crises"â€”it is not the detox working; it is autointoxication.

Why is Calcium D-Glucarate considered essential for the estrobolome during a detox?

It inhibits the enzyme beta-glucuronidase, which prevents the "unzipping" and reabsorption of conjugated toxins and estrogens in the gut, ensuring they are excreted in the stool.

What is the difference between a cholagogue and a choluretic?

A choluretic increases the total volume of bile produced by the liver, while a cholagogue stimulates the gallbladder to contract and release the stored bile into the small intestine.
