Case Study: The "Rebound" Prevention

Client: Marcus, 52 | Goal: Post-Protocol Sustainability

Initial Presentation: Marcus completed the 12-week P.U.R.I.F.Y. Protocol with a 15lb weight loss but feared the "rebound" effect. His baseline GGT was 48 U/L and hs-CRP was 3.2 mg/L.

Intervention: During Phase 6 (Yield), we implemented quarterly testing and focused on the TG:HDL ratio and fasting insulin. We identified a creeping TG:HDL ratio (rising from 1.1 to 1.8) at month four, despite his weight remaining stable.

Outcome: By catching this KPI early, we identified "hidden" sugar intake in his "healthy" maintenance snacks. Adjusting this prevented a return to systemic inflammation. At month 12, his hs-CRP remained at 0.4 mg/L and GGT at 18 U/L.

Inflammatory & Oxidative Stress

Chronic toxicity inevitably leads to oxidative stress. In the maintenance phase, we monitor 8-hydroxy-2'-deoxyguanosine (8-OHdG), a biomarker of oxidative DNA damage. A 2021 meta-analysis found that high urinary 8-OHdG levels correlated significantly with environmental heavy metal burden, even when blood levels appeared "normal."

Additionally, High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein (hs-CRP) serves as our global "fire alarm." While conventional medicine ignores values under 3.0 mg/L, the P.U.R.I.F.Y. Protocol views anything above 1.0 mg/L as an indication of low-grade systemic friction, often stemming from gut permeability or environmental triggers in the client's "Yield" environment.

The Maintenance Scorecard

Objective markers are powerful, but they must be paired with subjective KPIs to create a holistic picture of success. We use the P.U.R.I.F.Y. Maintenance Scorecard, which clients complete monthly. This scorecard tracks five domains:

  • Elimination Consistency: Bristol Stool Scale Type 3-4 daily (reflecting Phase III clearance).
  • Cognitive Clarity: Absence of "brain fog" (reflecting low neuro-inflammation).
  • Recovery Capacity: Heart Rate Variability (HRV) trends and sleep quality.
  • Chemical Sensitivity: Reaction to perfumes, exhaust, or cleaning agents (reflecting Phase II load).
  • Metabolic Stability: Ability to go 5-6 hours between meals without "hanger" symptoms.

Practitioner Monitoring & Frequency

How often should you test? In the Yield phase, the goal is to empower the client, not create medical dependency. However, "what gets measured gets managed." The recommended frequency for a P.U.R.I.F.Y. graduate is:

Months 1-6 Post-Protocol: Monthly subjective scorecard, quarterly (3-month) basic blood chemistry (GGT, hs-CRP, Fasting Insulin, Lipids).

Months 6-24 Post-Protocol: Bi-annual (6-month) comprehensive screening, including oxidative stress markers (8-OHdG) or toxic element hair/urine analysis if environmental exposure is high.

Key Takeaways

  • Long-term success is measured by the stability of biomarkers (GGT, hs-CRP, Insulin) in the "Yield" phase.
  • GGT should ideally be maintained between 10-20 U/L for optimal detoxification capacity.
  • Metabolic flexibility (TG:HDL ratio < 1.0) is essential for preventing toxin sequestration in fat tissue.
  • Subjective tracking via the Maintenance Scorecard provides a monthly "early warning" for practitioners.
  • Quarterly testing for the first six months post-protocol significantly improves long-term habit adherence.
