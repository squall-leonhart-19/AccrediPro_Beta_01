The Cephalic Phase Digestive Response (CPDR)

The Cephalic Phase Digestive Response (CPDR) is the physiological process where the brain prepares the body for digestion before food even enters the stomach. Research indicates that as much as 30% to 40% of the total digestive response to a meal is initiated in this phase through the senses of sight, smell, and thought.

When a client eats while distractedâ€”scrolling on a phone, driving, or workingâ€”the CPDR is significantly blunted. This leads to inadequate enzyme production, which results in undigested food particles fermenting in the gut, contributing to dysbiosis and the very "toxic burden" the PURIFY protocol seeks to eliminate.

Hormonal Signaling: Leptin vs. Ghrelin

The "Yield" phase relies on restoring the body's natural feedback loops. Leptin (the satiety hormone) and Ghrelin (the hunger hormone) are often dysregulated in clients with high toxic burdens or chronic inflammation. Chronic inflammation can lead to "Leptin Resistance," where the brain no longer "hears" the signal that the body has enough energy stored.

Intuitive nutrition is not just "eating whatever you want"; it is the practice of clearing the metabolic noise so these hormones can function. By following the PURIFY protocol, we have already reduced the inflammation that causes leptin resistance. Now, mindful eating ensures the client remains sensitive to these signals.

Intuitive Nutrition Principles

Intuitive nutrition is the ultimate goal of the Yield phase. It moves the client away from external rules (counting calories, macros, or restricted lists) and toward internal wisdom. However, this is only possible once the "biological terrain" has been cleaned. You cannot trust your gut if your gut is full of pathogenic bacteria screaming for sugar.

The 10 Principles of Intuitive Eating (adapted for the PURIFY practitioner) include:

  • Reject the Diet Mentality: View the PURIFY protocol as a reset, not a temporary restriction.
  • Honor Your Hunger: Keep the body biologically fed with nutrient-dense, whole foods to prevent "primal hunger."
  • Make Peace with Food: Eliminate the "good vs. bad" labels that trigger the stress response.
  • Feel Your Fullness: Pause in the middle of a meal to check the hunger-satiety scale.
  • Respect Your Body: Acknowledge your bio-individual needs (e.g., some need more protein, others more complex carbs).

The Hunger-Satiety Scale

A critical tool for the sustainable maintenance phase is the Hunger-Satiety Scale (1-10). Clients should aim to start eating at a "3" and stop at a "6" or "7."
