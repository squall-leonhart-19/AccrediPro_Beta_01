4. The Glycemic Load Factor

Insulin is more than a blood sugar hormone; it is a storage hormone. High levels of insulin signal the body to hold onto fat. Since many modern toxins (like BPA, Phthalates, and Pesticides) are lipophilic (fat-soluble), they are stored in adipose tissue. If a client is eating a high-glycemic diet, their body is in "storage mode," making it biologically impossible to release these toxins into the bloodstream for elimination.

While we remove triggers, we use specific "priming agents" to upregulate the machinery. These are not just "healthy foods"; they are biological modifiers. Research shows that certain compounds can increase the "patency" (openness) of the liver's exit routes.

  • Sulforaphane (Broccoli Sprouts): The most potent known natural activator of the Nrf2 pathway. A study showed it increases the excretion of airborne pollutants by 61% over 12 weeks.
