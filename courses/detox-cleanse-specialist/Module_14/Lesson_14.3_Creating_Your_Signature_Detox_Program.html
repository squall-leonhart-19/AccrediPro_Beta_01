Program Deliverables & Structure

A premium program isn't just "more talking." It is a curated experience. To justify a $2,500+ investment, your program should typically include:

  • Bi-Weekly Coaching Calls: 60-minute sessions to review progress and adjust the protocol.
  • Customized P.U.R.I.F.Y. Manual: A 50-page guide detailing food lists, toxin-free living, and supplement timing.
  • Functional Lab Review: (Optional but recommended) Reviewing GI-MAP or Organic Acids Tests to personalize the "Intensify Gut Integrity" phase.
  • On-Demand Support: Messaging via a HIPAA-compliant platform for quick questions.
  • Supplement Starter Kit: High-quality binders, liver supports, and probiotics included in the program price.

Value-Based Pricing Strategies

As an AccrediPro Certified Specialist, you are a high-level consultant. Your pricing should reflect the Total Cost of Inaction (COI) for the client. If a client remains toxic, they may face medical bills, lost wages, and decreased quality of life costing tens of thousands of dollars.

This "low-volume, high-value" model prevents practitioner burnout and ensures every client receives elite-level care.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of signature program architecture.

1

Why is the "Prepare the Pathways" phase critical for the business success of a signature program?

It minimizes "healing crises" (Herxheimer reactions). If clients feel terrible in the first week due to autointoxication, they are likely to quit and request a refund. Proper preparation ensures a positive client experience and better testimonials.

2

What is the primary difference between hourly pricing and outcome-based pricing?

Hourly pricing commoditizes your time, making you a "cost" to be minimized. Outcome-based pricing focuses on the value of the health transformation (e.g., reversing fatigue), making you an "investment" in the client's future.
