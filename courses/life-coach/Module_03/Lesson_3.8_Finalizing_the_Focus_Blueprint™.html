4. The Verbal Contract Protocol

The "Verbal Contract" is a high-level coaching tool designed to trigger the Consistency Principle. Robert Cialdini, a renowned psychologist, posits that once we make a stand or take a commitment, we encounter personal and interpersonal pressures to behave consistently with that commitment.

To execute the Verbal Contract, the coach asks: "We have finalized this Blueprint. On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to the specific actions we've laid out for the next 14 days?" If the answer is less than a 9, the blueprint is not yet final; it is too ambitious or lacks sufficient resources. We must adjust until the client gives a "Hell Yes" commitment.

5. The 5-Point Focus Review Checklist

Before moving to "Module 4: Execute," every coach must run the Focus Blueprintâ„¢ through this rigorous audit. This ensures the strategy is not just a "paper plan" but a functional reality.

1. The Simplicity Test

Can the client explain the top 3 priorities of this blueprint in under 30 seconds? Complexity is the enemy of execution.

2. The Friction Audit

Have we identified the primary environmental friction points? (e.g., if the goal is gym at 6 AM, are the clothes laid out?)

3. The Resource Alignment

Does the client have the 3 T's (Time, Talent, Treasure) required to sustain this specific focus for 90 days?

4. The "If-Then" Contingency

Are there 2-3 "Implementation Intentions" for when things go wrong? (e.g., "If I miss my morning run, then I will do 20 burpees at lunch.")

5. The Emotional Resonance

Does the finalized plan feel like a "burden" or a "relief"? A successful Focus Blueprintâ„¢ should feel like a weight being lifted.

6. Transitioning to 'E: Execute'

As we conclude Module 3, we stand at the threshold of action. We have Listened to the client's deep desires, Identified the blocks and goals, and Focused the strategy into a sharp, actionable blueprint. Check Your Understanding

Why is a "Verbal Contract" more effective than a simple written agreement in a coaching session?

The Verbal Contract activates the "Consistency Principle." Human beings have a deep-seated psychological need to be (and appear to be) consistent with what they have publicly declared, especially to an authority figure or coach.

What is the primary difference between a Lag Measure and a Lead Measure in the Focus Blueprintâ„¢?

A Lag Measure is the end result (e.g., revenue), which you cannot directly control. A Lead Measure is the predictive action (e.g., 10 sales calls) that you can directly control and track daily.
