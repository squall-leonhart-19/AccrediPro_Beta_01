1. Multidimensional Identification: The 8 Domains

In previous lessons, we focused on Precision Goal Clarification and Core Values. However, a goal does not exist in a vacuum. To truly "Identify" the current state, we must look at the human experience as a complex, adaptive system. The Wheel of Life 2.0 is not merely a "happiness check"; it is a diagnostic tool for human flourishing.

We assess eight key domains, each validated by positive psychology research as essential components of Subjective Well-Being (SWB):

  • Career & Professional Contribution: Alignment with purpose and skill utilization.
  • Financial Health: Security, literacy, and the "freedom ratio."
  • Physical & Mental Health: Vitality, sleep quality, and physiological resilience.
  • Relationships & Social Capital: Depth of intimacy and community support.
  • Personal Growth: Intellectual stimulation and self-actualization.
  • Recreation & Play: The "Recovery" component of the L.I.F.E. Blueprint.
  • Physical Environment: The impact of the home and workspace on the nervous system.
  • Spirituality/Inner Peace: Connection to something greater and internal homeostasis.

2. The 'Satisfaction vs. Importance' Matrix

A fatal flaw in traditional coaching is treating all low-scoring domains as equal priorities. If a client scores a 3/10 in "Spirituality" but ranks its importance as a 2/10, focusing coaching energy there is a strategic error. The Wheel of Life 2.0 introduces the Importance Weighted Score.

Domain Zone Satisfaction Importance Coaching Action
The Red Zone Low (1-4) High (8-10) Immediate Intervention: This is the primary source of psychological distress.
The Maintenance Zone High (8-10) High (8-10) Protection: Ensure these areas aren't sacrificed for other goals.
The Acceptance Zone Low (1-4) Low (1-4) Monitoring: Accept the current state; no active intervention required.
The Surplus Zone High (8-10) Low (1-4) Leverage: Use the energy/resources from here to fuel "Red Zone" shifts.

4. Objective vs. Subjective Reality

A core skill in the "Identify" phase is helping clients reconcile Emotional Perception with Factual Data. Humans are prone to "Affective Forecasting" and "Negativity Bias," which can skew their Wheel of Life scores.

As an AccrediPro Coach, you must facilitate a "Reality Audit":

  • Subjective: "I feel like I'm failing financially."
  • Objective: Reviewing net worth, cash flow, and debt-to-income ratio.
  • Subjective: "My relationship is dead."
  • Objective: Tracking "Positive-to-Negative" interaction ratios (Gottman's 5:1 ratio).

When there is a gap between the two, you have identified Cognitive Dissonance or a Limiting Belief (as discussed in Lesson 2.3). The Wheel 2.0 requires clients to provide "Evidence" for their scores (e.g., "Why is this a 5? What data supports that?").

5. Creating the 'Identification Baseline' Report

The transition from Module 2 (Identify) to Module 3 (Focus) is marked by the delivery of the Identification Baseline Report. This document provides the client with a "Mirror" of their current life system.

The report should include:

  1. The Visual Wheel: A snapshot of the 8 domains.
  2. The Priority Matrix: Highlighting the "Red Zone" domains.
  3. The Contamination Map: Explaining how Domain A is suppressing Domain B.
  4. The Evidence Log: Comparing subjective feelings with objective data points.
  5. The L.I.F.E. Readiness Score: A metric (1-100) based on the client's current resources and psychological capital.

Check Your Understanding

A client scores "Relationships" as a 4/10 in satisfaction but ranks it as a 3/10 in importance. According to the Matrix, what is the correct coaching action?

The correct action is Monitoring/Acceptance. Since the importance is low, this is not a "Red Zone" priority. Forcing a client to work on a low-importance domain often leads to resistance and poor coaching ROI.

What is "Cross-Domain Contamination"?

It is the systemic phenomenon where a deficit or dysfunction in one life domain (e.g., health or environment) creates a functional ceiling or negative impact on another domain (e.g., career or relationships).

Key Lesson Takeaways

  • The Wheel of Life 2.0 is a diagnostic tool, not just a subjective feeling tracker.
  • Weighting satisfaction against importance is critical for strategic prioritization.
  • Coaches must look for "Contamination Nodes" to find the root cause of client blocks.
  • Subjective feelings must be balanced with objective data to overcome cognitive biases.
  • The Identification Baseline Report establishes the professional "Starting Line" for the coaching engagement.
