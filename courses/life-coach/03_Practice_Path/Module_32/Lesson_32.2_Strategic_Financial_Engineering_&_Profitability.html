2. Pricing Psychology: Beyond the Hourly Trap

The "Hourly Rate" is a psychological ceiling that limits your perceived value to a unit of time rather than a unit of transformation. A 2023 industry analysis of 1,200 executive coaches found that those using Value-Based Pricing earned an average of 42% more per client than those billing by the hour, despite working fewer hours (Sherpa Coaching, 2023).

3. Tax Optimization & Structural Engineering

As a high-earning coach (typically above $75k-$100k in net profit), the transition from a Sole Proprietorship to an S-Corp election becomes a critical financial maneuver. This allows the practitioner to split income between a "reasonable salary" (subject to self-employment tax) and "shareholder distributions" (not subject to self-employment tax).

Furthermore, strategic retirement vehicles like the Solo 401(k) allow for contributions of up to $66,000 (as of 2023) or 25% of compensation, significantly reducing taxable income while building long-term wealth. This is the "Execute" phase of your personal financial blueprint.

4. Financial Forecasting: The 12-36 Month Roadmap

Predictability is the antidote to the "feast or famine" cycle. Professional financial modeling for coaches relies on three primary variables:

  • LTV (Lifetime Value): The total revenue a single client generates over the duration of your relationship.
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): The total marketing and sales spend required to acquire one new client.
  • Churn Rate: The percentage of clients who discontinue services month-over-month.

A healthy coaching practice aims for an LTV:CAC ratio of at least 3:1. If you spend $1,000 to get a client (CAC), they should generate at least $3,000 in revenue (LTV) to justify the marketing investment.

5. Managing Overhead: The Efficiency Audit

Overhead in a coaching business often creeps up through "SaaS bloat" and unoptimized staffing. Every dollar spent must be viewed through the lens of ROI. If a $200/month CRM doesn't save you at least 2 hours of administrative work (valued at your effective hourly rate), it is a liability, not an asset.
