2. Technical Requirements: GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA

Privacy is no longer a courtesy; it is a regulated mandate. Depending on where you and your clients are located, you may fall under several jurisdictions simultaneously.

Regulation Scope Key Requirement for Coaches
GDPR (EU/UK) Any client residing in the EU or UK. "Privacy by Design," explicit consent, and the right to data portability.
CCPA/CPRA (California) Clients in California. Right to know what data is collected and the right to opt-out of "sale" (sharing).
HIPAA (USA) Health information (if integrated with medical providers). Strict encryption standards and Business Associate Agreements (BAAs).

Even if you are not a "covered entity" under HIPAA, adopting HIPAA-level security (AES-256 encryption) is considered the "Gold Standard" for professional coaches. It signals to high-net-worth clients that their secrets are safe with you.

3. Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)

Most coaches do not host their own servers. We use CRMs (like Dubsado or Honeybook), scheduling tools (Calendly), and video platforms (Zoom). Under the law, you are the Data Controller, and these tools are Data Processors.

4. The 'Right to be Forgotten' & Record Retention

Clients have the right to request that you delete all data you hold on them. However, this conflicts with your legal need to keep records for liability and tax purposes. A compliant Retention Policy must balance these two needs.

The Deletion Workflow:

  1. Verification: Confirm the identity of the person making the request.
  2. Classification: Identify what must be deleted (session notes, recordings) vs. what must be kept (invoices for tax, signed contracts for legal defense).
  3. Third-Party Purge: Instruct your CRM and email providers to delete the relevant records.
  4. Confirmation: Provide the client with a written "Certificate of Erasure."

5. Cyber-Incident Response Plans

It is not a matter of *if*, but *when* a security friction occurs. A professional coach must have a pre-written Incident Response Plan (IRP). Waiting until a breach happens to decide what to do is a recipe for legal disaster.

The 72-Hour Rule

Under GDPR, you generally have 72 hours from the moment you become aware of a breach to notify the relevant supervisory authority if the breach poses a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals.

6. Technical Implementation Checklist

To move from theory to practice, implement the following "Hardening" steps in your coaching business today:

  • Encryption: Ensure all hard drives use FileVault (Mac) or BitLocker (Windows).
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Mandatory on all accounts (Email, CRM, Zoom).
  • Zero-Knowledge Storage: Use services like ProtonDrive or Tresorit for client files, where even the provider cannot see your data.
  • VPN: Never access client data or your CRM on public Wi-Fi without a high-quality VPN.

Check Your Understanding

1. You are a US-based coach with a client in Germany. Which regulation takes precedence regarding their data privacy?

GDPR. Because the data subject (the client) is in the EU, you must comply with GDPR regardless of where your business is physically located.

2. What is the primary purpose of a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)?

A DPA is a legally binding document between a data controller (you) and a data processor (your software provider) that regulates the processing of personal data to ensure compliance with privacy laws.
Key Takeaways
  • The "Listen" phase generates PII that requires high-level encryption and strategic handling.
  • Compliance is location-independent; you must follow the laws of the client's jurisdiction.
  • Always use professional-tier software that provides signed DPAs or BAAs.
  • Establish a clear data retention policy that balances "The Right to be Forgotten" with legal record-keeping requirements.
  • Prepare a cyber-incident response plan before you actually need it.
