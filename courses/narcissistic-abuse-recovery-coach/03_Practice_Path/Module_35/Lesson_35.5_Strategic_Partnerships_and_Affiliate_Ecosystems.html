5. Contractual & Ethical Revenue Sharing

When you enter into partnerships involving revenue sharing (referral fees or commissions), you must navigate the legalities of "fee-splitting." In many jurisdictions, licensed therapists are prohibited from paying or receiving referral fees. As a coach, you have more flexibility, but ethical rigor is mandatory.

Legal Warning

Always consult with local counsel regarding "Anti-Kickback" statutes. In some regions, receiving a commission for referring a client to a legal or medical professional can be categorized as a conflict of interest or even a criminal offense.

Best Practices for Partnership Agreements

  1. Fixed-Fee vs. Percentage: Percentage-based sharing can sometimes incentivize "over-selling." A flat "Introduction Fee" is often cleaner.
  2. Clawback Provisions: If a client requests a refund from the partner, your commission should be returned to maintain fairness.
  3. The "Client-First" Clause: Every contract should state that the partnership can be terminated immediately if the partnerâ€™s service quality drops or if they violate the survivor's safety.
