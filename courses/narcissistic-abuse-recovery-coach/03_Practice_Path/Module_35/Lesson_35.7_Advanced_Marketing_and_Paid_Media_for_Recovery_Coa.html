A 2023 analysis of trauma-recovery search intent (n=12,400 queries) revealed that survivors do not search for "coaching"; they search for their symptoms. To scale, your paid media must mirror the survivor's internal dialogue.

Targeting the "Hoovering" Phase

Survivors in the "Hoovering" phase (Module 8.4) are high-intent but high-risk. Targeting keywords or interests related to "reconciliation after abuse" or "why is my ex being nice again" allows you to intercept the cycle before the survivor is sucked back in.

Targeting the "Smear Campaign" Phase

When a survivor is facing a smear campaign, their primary psychological need is validation and legal/social protection. Your ads should offer resources on "protecting your reputation" or "gray rocking during legal battles."

2. Compliance and Ethics in Trauma Advertising

Major advertising platforms (Meta, Google, TikTok) have strict policies regarding Personal Health and Sensitive Topics. For recovery coaches, this is the "Compliance Minefield." If your ad copy says, "Are you being abused by a narcissist?" your account will likely be flagged or banned for "Personal Attributes" violations.

Platform Restriction The "Non-Compliant" Approach The "Master Practitioner" Compliant Approach
Personal Attributes "Are YOU suffering from gaslighting?" "Understanding the mechanics of gaslighting and how to reclaim reality."
Negative Self-Perception "Stop feeling like a victim today." "Resources for building emotional fortitude and post-traumatic growth."
Guaranteed Outcomes "Heal your trauma in 30 days." "A structured methodology for navigating the recovery journey."

Ethical Warning

Never use "Fear-Based Scarcity" in trauma marketing. While common in general business coaching, survivors of narcissistic abuse are hyper-sensitized to coercion. Using countdown timers or "last chance" language can trigger fawn responses or panic, which violates the "Do No Harm" principle of the N.A.R.C. Method.

3. Data-Driven Scaling: CAC vs. LTV

Scaling is not about spending more; it is about knowing what your spend produces. In a high-ticket coaching environment, your unit economics must be crystal clear. A 2022 industry benchmark study found that the average CAC for professional services in the wellness space ranges from $150 to $450 depending on the funnel depth.

$325 Avg. Acquisition Cost (CAC)
8.4x Target ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
$2,800 Avg. Lifetime Value (LTV)
12% Funnel Conversion Rate

To calculate your Scaling Ratio, use the formula: LTV / CAC. If your ratio is above 3.0, you have a "green light" to increase your daily budget. If it is below 2.0, your marketing is inefficient, and you likely have a "leaky bucket" in your nurturing sequence.

4. The Empathy-First Retargeting Funnel

Most marketers use retargeting to "remind" people to buy. In recovery coaching, retargeting is used to re-establish safety. A survivor may visit your site but leave because they were interrupted by their partner or felt a surge of cognitive dissonance (Module 2.3).

Your retargeting sequence should follow the R.E.S.T. Framework:

  • R - Reassure: Content that validates their experience without asking for a sale.
  • E - Educate: Short videos explaining a concept like "Trauma Bonding" (Module 2.1).
  • S - Social Proof: Anonymized case studies showing that recovery is possible.
  • T - Transition: A gentle invitation to a low-pressure discovery call or workshop.

5. SEO and Long-Form Authority

While paid media provides immediate "fuel," SEO (Search Engine Optimization) provides the "engine" for long-term sustainability. Survivors often search in "Incognito Mode" or late at night. Dominating the search landscape for terms like "Narcissistic Collapse Symptoms" or "How to explain gaslighting to a lawyer" builds massive authority.

A 2023 Meta-Analysis of SEO in the mental health space showed that Long-Form Content (2,500+ words) ranks 3x higher for "complex trauma" keywords than shorter, listicle-style posts. This is because Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) guidelines prioritize depth in "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) topics.

The "Search-to-Support" Pipeline

Structure your blog posts to move from Education (What is happening?) to Empathy (I understand how this feels) to Empowerment (Here is the first step of the N.A.R.C. Method).

6. Advanced Scaling: The Multi-Channel Approach

True growth occurs when you move from a single channel to an ecosystem. This is known as the "Omnipresence Strategy." By the time a client books a high-ticket package, they should have encountered your brand on at least three different platforms.

Test your mastery of advanced marketing concepts.

1

Why is "Psychographic Targeting" more effective than "Demographic Targeting" for narcissistic abuse recovery coaches?

Because demographics (age/gender) don't account for the survivor's stage in the abuse cycle. Psychographics allow you to target the specific psychological state (e.g., cognitive dissonance or hoovering), which is the primary driver for seeking help.
2

If your CAC is $400 and your LTV is $800, what is your Scaling Ratio, and should you increase your ad spend?

The Scaling Ratio is 2.0 ($800 / $400). According to industry benchmarks, this is a "yellow light." You should optimize your funnel efficiency or increase your LTV before aggressively scaling, as a 2.0 ratio leaves little room for platform fluctuations or overhead.

Key Takeaways

  • Psychographics over Demographics: Meet the client where their mind is, not just where their zip code is.
  • Compliance is Safety: Use "Third-Person" language in ads to protect your account and respect survivor boundaries.
  • The 3:1 Rule: Aim for a Lifetime Value that is at least 3x your Acquisition Cost for healthy scaling.
  • Retargeting = Relationship: Use follow-up ads to provide value and validation, not just a "buy" button.
  • Depth Wins SEO: Produce authoritative, long-form content to satisfy both Google's algorithms and a survivor's need for deep understanding.
