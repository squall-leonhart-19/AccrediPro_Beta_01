2. Triangulation Mechanics: Divide and Conquer

Triangulation occurs when the narcissist brings a third person into the dynamic of a relationship to create friction, jealousy, and insecurity. It is a calculated move to ensure the narcissist remains the "hub" of all communication and emotional energy.

4. The Smear Campaign: Systematic Eradication of Identity

A Smear Campaign is a pre-emptive or retaliatory strike where the narcissist spreads false or distorted information about the survivor. The goal is two-fold: to destroy the survivor's credibility and to isolate them so they have no support system to turn to during the discard.

Narcissists often use DARVO during smear campaigns:

  • Deny the behavior.
  • Attack the survivor.
  • Reverse Victim and Offender.

As a coach, you will witness the "social death" your clients experience. A 2021 study on social ostracization in abusive systems found that survivors often rank the loss of their social circle as more painful than the end of the romantic relationship itself, as it triggers primal fears of abandonment and tribal exclusion.

5. Defensive Maneuvers: Grey Rock and Beyond

When a client is being social-engineered, their instinct is to explain, defend, and justify (JADE). This is exactly what the narcissist wants. In the Navigate the Reality phase of the N.A.R.C. Method, we teach the following:

The Grey Rock Method

Becoming as uninteresting as a grey rock. You provide short, non-committal answers ("Okay," "I see," "Thatâ€™s interesting"). This starves the narcissist of "Narcissistic Supply"â€”the emotional reaction they crave.

The Yellow Rock Method

Used primarily in co-parenting situations. It is Grey Rock but with a "polite veneer." You use formal, professional language to avoid looking "cold" or "uncooperative" to Flying Monkeys or legal professionals.

No Contact / Low Contact Perimeter

Identifying "Leaky Boundaries." If a friend shares information with the narcissist, that friend must be moved to the outer circle of the client's life. This is the "Protective Shield" in action.

6. Coaching a Client Through Social Loss

One of the hardest parts of your job as a coach is helping a client accept that they may lose friends, family members, or colleagues who choose to believe the narcissist. This is not a failure of the client; it is a feature of the narcissist's social engineering.

The Coaching Roadmap for Social Loss:

  2. Grieving the "Living Dead": Treating Flying Monkeys as if they have passed away. They are no longer the people the client once knew; they are now extensions of the abuser.
  3. Vetting the New Circle: In Module 4, we focus on building secure attachments. The "empty space" left by Flying Monkeys is the fertile ground where healthy, vetted relationships will eventually grow.
