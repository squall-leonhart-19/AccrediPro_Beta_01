3. Identifying the 'Gap in Care'

Why do survivors often spend years in traditional talk therapy only to feel "stuck" or even re-traumatized? This is what we call the Gap in Care. Traditional therapy often relies on the "Neutrality Principle," which assumes that in any conflict, both parties share equal responsibility for the dysfunction.

In narcissistic abuse, this "it takes two to tango" approach is devastating. It validates the narcissist‚Äôs gaslighting and further erodes the survivor's sense of reality. Narcissistic Abuse Recovery Coaching fills this gap by:

  • Expert Identification: Recognizing specific tactics like Hoovering, Love Bombing, and DARVO that generalist therapists may miss.
  • Strategic Safety: Providing practical "Exit Strategies" and "Grey Rock" communication protocols.
  • Cognitive Dissonance Resolution: Actively dismantling the "trauma bond" rather than just talking about it.
