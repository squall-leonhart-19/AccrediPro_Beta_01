1. The 12-to-24 Week Roadmap

A 24-week roadmap is the gold standard for complex cases involving long-term marriages, shared children, or high-intensity trauma bonds.

2. Determining Phase-Readiness

One of the most common mistakes in recovery coaching is pushing a client into the "Rebuild" phase while they are still experiencing acute cognitive dissonance. As a coach, you must look for specific "Phase-Readiness Markers" before advancing the treatment plan.

From Acknowledge to Rebuild

To move from processing the wounds (Acknowledge) to reclaiming identity (Rebuild), the client must demonstrate:

  • Reduced Amygdala Hijacking: The client can discuss the abuser for 10 minutes without entering a full "fight-flight-freeze" response.
  • Linguistic Shift: A move from "We" or "He/She" centered narratives to "I" centered narratives.
  • Cognitive Clarity: The ability to name at least three specific manipulation tactics (e.g., DARVO, gaslighting) used in the relationship without making excuses for the abuser.

Because her nervous system was still in a state of high alarm, the values work felt "hollow" and increased her toxic shame.

Intervention: The coach moved back to the "Acknowledge" phase for 4 additional weeks, focusing on somatic tracking and biochemical education regarding the dopamine-cortisol loop.

3. Managing the Recovery Pendulum

Recovery is rarely linear. The Recovery Pendulum describes the natural swing between empowerment (The "I'm Done" state) and regression (The "Maybe it wasn't that bad" state). As a coach, your strategic map must account for these swings.

When a client experiences a setbackâ€”such as responding to a hoover or checking the abuser's social mediaâ€”the treatment plan should not be scrapped. Instead, it should pivot to "Stabilization Mode."

Adjusting for Hoovering Attempts

If a hoover occurs, the strategic map shifts to:

  1. Immediate Safety Audit: Re-evaluating physical and digital boundaries.
  2. Reality Anchoring: Reviewing the "Evidence Log" (created in the Navigate phase) to counteract the fresh gaslighting.
  3. Biochemical Reset: Implementing 72 hours of high-intensity self-care to manage the cortisol spike.

5. Milestones of the 'Navigate' Phase

The "Navigate" phase is the foundation of the entire map. If the client does not achieve Objective Reality Acceptance, the rest of the coaching will be built on sand. You must set firm milestones for this phase before moving forward.

Milestone 1: The Timeline of Truth

The client creates a chronological list of the relationship's "red flags" and abusive incidents. Success Marker: The client can read the list without using "minimizing language" (e.g., "He was just stressed").

Milestone 2: The Digital Fortress

The client completes a full digital audit, including blocking, changing passwords, and setting up "Flying Monkey" filters. Success Marker: Zero unintentional contact for 14 consecutive days.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client is in Week 6 of a 24-week plan. They are still ruminating heavily on "why" the abuser acted that way. Which N.A.R.C. phase should you be focusing on?

You should still be in the Acknowledge phase. Rumination and the search for "closure" are markers that the client has not yet processed the cognitive dissonance or the grief associated with the "A" phase. Moving to "Rebuild" now would be premature.

2. What is the primary indicator that a client is ready to move from 'Acknowledge' to 'Rebuild'?

A shift in language from "victim-centric" (focusing on the abuser's actions) to "agency-centric" (focusing on their own choices and identity), combined with a regulated nervous system that can discuss the abuse without immediate dissociation or panic.

Key Takeaways

  • The 12-to-24-week timeline is a framework, not a cage; adjust based on trauma bond scores.
  • Never advance to the "Rebuild" phase until the client has achieved significant nervous system regulation.
  • The "Recovery Pendulum" is a normal part of the process; pivot to stabilization when setbacks occur.
  • Denial must be broken objectively through tools like the "Timeline of Truth" before emotional processing begins.
  • Long-term success is dependent on a robust Relapse Prevention Plan established in the final phase.
