The 'Decompression Protocol'

Master Practitioners do not leave their sessions "carrying" the client's energy. In the N.A.R.C. Methodâ„¢, we implement the 15-Minute Transitional Shield. This protocol is designed to down-regulate the nervous system and signal to the brain that the "threat" (the client's trauma story) is over.

The Transitional Shield Protocol
  • Phase 1: Somatic Discharge (5 mins): Utilize "shaking" or "tremoring" (TRE-inspired) to release muscular tension held during the session. This interrupts the freeze response.
  • Phase 2: Sensory Grounding (5 mins): Temperature shift (cold water on wrists) or olfactory stimulation (citrus/peppermint) to re-engage the ventral vagal state.
  • Phase 3: Linguistic Closure (5 mins): A brief, written "container" exercise. Write down 3 key insights, then physically close the notebook to signal cognitive completion.
