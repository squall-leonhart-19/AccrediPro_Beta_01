The Scapegoat is often the most likely member of a narcissistic system to seek coaching. Because they have been the "truth-teller" and the target of systemic projection, they possess a unique, albeit painful, clarity. The Scapegoat-to-Sovereign Pipeline is a specific integration strategy within the N.A.R.C. Method‚Ñ¢ designed to turn systemic rejection into individual power.

4. Navigating 'Flying Monkeys' & Systemic Resistance

As the client claims their power, the system typically deploys Flying Monkeys‚Äîthird parties (often siblings, aunts, or family friends) who act on behalf of the narcissist to shame the survivor back into the fold. This is a critical test of the "Claim Your Power" pillar.

Systemic resistance is often proportional to the client's level of healing. When the client is no longer available to hold the narcissist's projections, the narcissist experiences a "narcissistic injury," which the rest of the system feels as increased tension. The Flying Monkeys are sent to "fix" the client so the system can stop feeling the narcissist's wrath.

Master Practitioners must coach clients to recognize the Triangulation Trap. Instead of defending their choices to Flying Monkeys, the client must learn to use "Grey Rock" techniques at a systemic level or, in many cases, expand their boundary circle to include the enablers as well as the primary abuser.
