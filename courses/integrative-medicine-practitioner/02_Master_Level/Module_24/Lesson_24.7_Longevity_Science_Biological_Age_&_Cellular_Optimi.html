3. Quantifying the Inevitable: Biological Age Testing

Chronological age is a fixed variable; biological age is a plastic one. As a Master Practitioner, you must be able to interpret and recommend the appropriate "clock" for your client's specific goals. A meta-analysis of 13,000+ individuals confirmed that epigenetic age acceleration is a robust predictor of all-cause mortality, independent of traditional risk factors.

Test Type Mechanism Best Used For... Clinical Limitation
Horvath Clock (DNAm) Measures cytosine methylation at specific CpG sites. Gold standard for overall cellular "age" and mortality risk. Slow to change; may not reflect short-term lifestyle shifts.
GlycanAge Analyzes IgG glycosylation patterns (pro vs. anti-inflammatory). Tracking systemic inflammation and lifestyle/hormone interventions. Highly sensitive to acute illness or injury.
PhenoAge / Levine Clock Calculated from 9 standard blood markers (CRP, Albumin, etc.). Cost-effective screening using standard lab work. Less precise for "bio-hacking" optimization.
Telomere Length Measures the protective caps on chromosomes (via qPCR or FISH). Assessing long-term stress and replicative senescence. High variability; length doesn't always correlate with function.

4. Senolytics & NAD+ Metabolism: Pruning the Garden

Two of the most potent levers in cellular optimization are the clearance of senescent cells ("zombie cells") and the restoration of NAD+ levels. Senescent cells secrete the SASP (Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype), a cocktail of pro-inflammatory cytokines that "infect" neighboring healthy cells.

The Senolytic Protocol

Unlike standard supplements, senolytics are often used in a "pulsed" fashion. A 2020 study showed that a combination of Dasatinib and Quercetin (D+Q) significantly reduced the senescent cell burden in human adipose tissue within 11 days of treatment. In integrative practice, we often use Fisetin, a flavonoid found in strawberries, which has shown a 25-50% reduction in senescence markers in animal models with high safety profiles.

NAD+ Bioavailability

NAD+ levels decline by roughly 50% every 20 years of life. This decline cripples the Sirtuins (longevity genes) and PARPs (DNA repair enzymes). Practitioners must choose between:

  • NR (Nicotinamide Riboside): Highly effective for increasing blood NAD+ levels.
  • NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide): Preferred for cellular uptake and muscle/brain health.
  • IV NAD+: Rapid saturation, though bypasses the natural salvage pathway.

Why is "pulsed" dosing preferred for senolytics like Fisetin or Quercetin over daily low-dose administration?

Senescence is a process of accumulation. Pulsed dosing (the "hit and run" approach) allows for the selective apoptosis of senescent cells while giving healthy cells time to recover and avoiding potential interference with beneficial acute senescence (which is needed for wound healing).
