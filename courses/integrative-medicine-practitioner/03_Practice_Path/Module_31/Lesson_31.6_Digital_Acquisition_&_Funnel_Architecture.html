2. High-Converting Landing Pages

A landing page for an integrative practitioner must solve a specific problem. General "wellness" pages fail because they lack the urgency required for a lead to exchange their contact information. High-converting pages in this niche follow a specific anatomical structure:

1. The Specificity Hook
The headline must address a specific ICD-10 level concern or a systemic functional dysfunction (e.g., "Why Your 'Normal' Thyroid Labs Are Leaving You Exhausted").
2. The Mechanism of Action (MOA)
Explain why they haven't found relief yet. This is where you introduce the "Root Cause" concept, shifting blame from the patient to the incomplete diagnostic model they‚Äôve been using.
3. The Authority Bridge

3. Lead Magnet Strategy: Root Cause & Biomarkers

In the integrative space, the lead magnet (the "ethical bribe") must demonstrate immediate clinical value. We recommend two primary models that align with the Data-Driven Decisions (D) and Root Cause Analysis (R) pillars of our framework.

The Root Cause Checklist

This is a self-assessment tool. It allows the lead to identify "Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators" (ATM) in their own history. By the time they finish the checklist, they should realize that their condition is complex and requires a practitioner who understands systemic biology.

The Optimal Biomarker Guide

This is a high-authority PDF that compares "Conventional Lab Ranges" vs. "Functional/Optimal Ranges." It educates the lead on why they still feel unwell despite being told their labs are "normal." This creates immediate cognitive dissonance with their current care provider and positions you as the expert who sees the hidden data.

4. Paid Traffic Frameworks

To feed the funnel, we use two primary engines: Meta (Facebook/Instagram) for interruption-based marketing and Google Ads for search-intent marketing. Each requires a different strategic approach.

Meta Ads: The "Aha!" Strategy

Meta is best for reaching people who aren't searching for a solution yet but are suffering.

  • Creative: Use video ads that debunk a common health myth (e.g., "Why calories aren't the root of your weight gain").
  • Targeting: Interest-based (Functional Medicine, Dr. Hyman, specific health conditions) and Lookalike Audiences (LAL) based on your current client list.

Google Ads: The "Precision" Strategy

Google is for high-intent leads searching for specific terms like "SIBO practitioner near me" or "Natural Hashimoto's treatment."

  • Strategy: Bid on "long-tail" keywords that indicate a high level of research and readiness to invest.
  • Landing: Send these directly to a specific landing page for that condition, not your homepage.

6. Practice Economics: CAC vs. LTV

To run a professional integrative practice, you must move from "hoping for referrals" to "buying customers." This requires understanding two critical metrics:

1. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): The total marketing spend divided by the number of new clients.
Example: $1,000 spend / 5 clients = $200 CAC.

2. Lifetime Value (LTV): The total revenue generated from a single client over the duration of your relationship.
Example: Initial Program ($3,000) + Maintenance ($500/yr for 3 years) = $4,500 LTV.

The Golden Ratio

A healthy integrative practice should aim for an LTV:CAC ratio of at least 5:1. If your CAC is $500 and your LTV is $5,000, you have a 10:1 ratio, indicating a highly profitable and scalable acquisition model.
