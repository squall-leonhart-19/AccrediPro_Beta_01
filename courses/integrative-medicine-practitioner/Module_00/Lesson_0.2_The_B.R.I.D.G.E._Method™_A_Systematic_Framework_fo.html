2. R: The Science of Root Cause Analysis

The "R" in B.R.I.D.G.E. represents Root Cause Analysis. In conventional medicine, the diagnosis is the destination. In Integrative Medicine, the diagnosis is merely the starting point. We utilize the ATM Framework to deconstruct disease:

  • Antecedents: Congenital or developmental factors (genetics, birth trauma, early life nutrition).
  • Triggers: Acute events that initiate the symptoms (infections, physical trauma, severe psychological stress).
  • Mediators: Factors that keep the inflammatory process or dysfunction "alive" (poor sleep, ongoing toxic exposure, nutrient deficiencies).

3. I: Individualization vs. Standardization

The current medical model relies heavily on "Standard of Care" protocols based on the average of a population. However, as the 2021 Journal of Personalized Medicine meta-analysis highlighted, individual variance in response to the same intervention can range from 0% to 100% depending on genetic polymorphisms.

Individualization (I) moves us past the "one-size-fits-all" approach. We consider:

  1. Epigenetics: How the client's environment (diet, stress, toxins) is currently talking to their genes.
  2. Bio-individuality: The unique metabolic needs of the client. For example, while a ketogenic diet may resolve insulin resistance in Client A, it may cause thyroid downregulation in Client B.
  3. The Exposome: The sum total of non-genetic exposures over a lifetime.

1. What is the primary difference between an "Antecedent" and a "Trigger" in Root Cause Analysis?

An Antecedent is a pre-existing factor (like genetics or birth history) that sets the stage, whereas a Trigger is an acute event (like an infection or high-stress period) that initiates the onset of symptoms.

2. Why is "Data-Driven Decisions" (D) critical for client compliance?

Objective data (labs/biomarkers) provides "visual proof" of dysfunction, which validates the client's experience and creates a measurable baseline to track progress, significantly increasing motivation and adherence.
