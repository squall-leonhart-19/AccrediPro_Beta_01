3. Modified Align Chakras (A) for Dissociation

When a client is prone to "drifting" or dissociation, the standard Align Chakras (A) technique must be modified. In a typical session, we might move from Root to Crown. In trauma-informed energy work, we use the Centripetal Alignment method.

Instead of moving linearly, we focus on the Root (Safety) and the Heart (Integration) simultaneously. This creates an energetic "tether." If you focus too heavily on the Upper Triad (Throat, Third Eye, Crown) with a traumatized client, you risk "pulling" their consciousness further out of the body, potentially triggering a dissociative episode.

4. Safe Neutralization (N): The Principle of Titration

In the Neutralize Blocks (N) phase, our goal is to clear energetic debris. However, in trauma work, the "block" is often a "dam" holding back a flood of repressed emotion. If you neutralize the block too quickly, the client may experience floodingâ€”an overwhelming surge of traumatic memory or affect.

We use Energetic Titration:

  1. Identify the block's edge, not its center.
  2. Neutralize in small, "sip-sized" increments.
  3. Frequently check in with the client's somatic state (breath, eye movement, muscle tension).
  4. If the client's breath becomes shallow, stop neutralizing and return to Align (A) at the Root.

5. The Root-Heart Safety Axis

The neuro-energetic foundation of trauma recovery lies in the relationship between the 1st (Root) and 4th (Heart) chakras. The Root provides the biological safety (linked to the adrenal glands and the sympathetic nervous system), while the Heart provides the emotional coherence (linked to the Vagus nerve and parasympathetic tone).

When these two centers are out of sync, the client exists in a state of "functional freeze." By consciously linking these two centers during the Activate Healing (A) phase, we stimulate the Vagal Brake. This physiological shift allows the body to move from "Survival Mode" to "Repair Mode."

Clinical data suggests that heart-focused energetic interventions can increase Vagal Tone by up to 15% within a single 30-minute session, significantly reducing the "startle response" in trauma survivors.

6. Empowering the Client (E): Somatic Grounding Anchors

The final stage of the protocol, Empower Client (E), is critical for trauma. The client must leave the session with the ability to "re-collect" their fragmented energy if they become triggered in daily life. We teach Somatic Grounding Anchors.

An anchor is a physical-energetic trigger. For example:

  • The 5-5-5 Anchor: Pressing the heels into the floor (Root), placing a hand on the chest (Heart), and taking 5 coherent breaths.
  • The Peripheral Expansion: Teaching the client to soften their gaze and notice the space around them, which energetically "expands" the biofield and reduces the "tunnel vision" of a trauma response.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it potentially dangerous to focus heavily on the Crown chakra when working with a client in a dissociative state?

Focusing on the upper chakras can draw the client's energy and consciousness further "up and out" of the body, exacerbating the dissociation and potentially triggering a crisis. Trauma-informed work prioritizes grounding (Root) to ensure the client remains present.

2. What is "Energetic Titration" in the context of the Neutralize (N) phase?

It is the process of clearing energetic blocks in small, manageable increments to prevent the "flooding" of traumatic emotions that could re-traumatize the client's nervous system.
