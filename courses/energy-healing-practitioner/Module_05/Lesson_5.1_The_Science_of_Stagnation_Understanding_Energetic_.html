4. The 'Freeze' Response: Trapping Survival Energy

To understand the most stubborn blocks, we must look at the Polyvagal Theory developed by Dr. Stephen Porges. When a human faces a threat they cannot fight or flee from, the nervous system enters the Dorsal Vagal Shutdown (the Freeze response).

During "Freeze," the body generates a massive amount of sympathetic survival energy, but immediately "brakes" it. This unspent energy doesn't just disappear; it becomes a standing wave within the biofield. This is why many clients feel "numb" or "disconnected" in areas where they have experienced significant trauma. The block is actually a protective shell of unspent survival energy that has been neutralized by the body's own inhibitory systems.
