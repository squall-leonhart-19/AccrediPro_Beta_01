For the L4 Master Practitioner, the most significant barrier to professional scaling is the commodity trap. When you price by the hour, you inadvertently signal to the client that your value is tied to your physical presence rather than their energetic transformation. This creates a ceiling on your income and, more importantly, a ceiling on the client's results.

3. The Psychology of Premium Pricing

The "Veblen Effect" in economics suggests that for certain high-status or high-impact services, demand actually increases as the price increases. In energy medicine, this is tied to the Cognitive Dissonance Theory. If a client has a "life-destroying" problem (e.g., chronic autoimmune fatigue or deep-seated trauma), a $100 solution feels insufficient. It creates dissonance. A $5,000 solution, however, matches the magnitude of their pain.

As a Master Practitioner, your energetic frequency regarding money is a part of the healing field. If you feel "guilty" charging $5,000, that dissonance will be felt by the client as a lack of confidence in the protocol itself. This is often rooted in the "Healer's Poverty Vow" archetype.
