1. The Professional Biofield Interview

The biofield interview differs from a standard medical intake. While we respect physical diagnoses, our goal is to uncover the Hidden Energetic Stressors that precede or exacerbate physical pathology. Research indicates that subjective client reports of "vitality" often correlate with objective measures of Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and biophoton emission.

A professional interview should follow a structured narrative flow:

  • The Chronological Origin: "When did you last feel truly vital?" This identifies the point of energetic depletion.
  • Somatic Resonance: "When you experience this [symptom], where else in your life do you feel a similar 'heaviness'?" This bridges the gap between emotion and physiology.
  • Environmental Triggers: Identifying geopathic stress or interpersonal "energy drains" that compromise the biofield boundary.

3. Utilizing the Standardized Biofield Map

The Biofield Map is a visual and narrative document used to record findings from Lesson 1.3 (Scanning) and 1.4 (Coherence). Using a standardized chart ensures that your work is reproducible and professional.

When mapping, use the Three-Layer Documentation System:

  1. The Etheric Plot: Documenting the distance of the field from the body (e.g., "Field extends 4 inches at the heart, but only 1 inch at the knees").
  2. The Texture Overlay: Using shorthand for haptic sensations (e.g., T for Tingle, D for Density, V for Void).
  3. The Vector Flow: Marking the direction of energy movement. Is it spiraling clockwise (coherent) or erratic (incoherent)?

5. Translating Findings into Professional Language

One of the greatest challenges for practitioners is describing intuitive findings without sounding "unscientific." To maintain a $997+ certification standard, we use Clinical Energetic Nomenclature.

Avoid Shamanic/Vague Terms

"Your aura looks really dark and muddy today, like there's a bad spirit there."

Use Professional Nomenclature

"The assessment indicates significant density in the emotional layer of the biofield, suggesting a lack of energetic clearance in the thoracic region."

Professional language focuses on mechanics (flow, density, coherence, expansion) rather than judgments (good, bad, dark, light).
