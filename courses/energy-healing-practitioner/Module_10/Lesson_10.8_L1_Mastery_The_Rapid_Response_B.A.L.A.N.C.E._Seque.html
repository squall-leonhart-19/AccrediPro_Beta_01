Final L1 Review: Troubleshooting 'N' and 'A'

In Level 1, students often struggle with Neutralizing Blocks (N) and Activating Healing (A). These phases require the highest degree of practitioner-client resonance. If the "Rapid Response" fails, it is usually due to a bottleneck in one of these two areas.

1. The "N" Bottleneck: Secondary Gain

You may find a block that refuses to budge despite perfect frequency matching. This is often due to Secondary Gain‚Äîthe subconscious benefit the client receives from the blockage (e.g., the block provides an excuse to avoid a stressful job). In a Rapid Response, do not fight the block. Instead, move to Phase C (Clearing) the surrounding field to reduce the pressure on the block itself.

2. The "A" Bottleneck: Parasympathetic Lag

Activation of healing requires the body to be in a parasympathetic state. If the client is still in "High Beta" brainwave activity, the Activate Healing phase will simply "bounce off" the biofield. Master practitioners use the Vagal Brake (a specific pressure point application or breath cue) to force a parasympathetic shift before attempting Activation.
