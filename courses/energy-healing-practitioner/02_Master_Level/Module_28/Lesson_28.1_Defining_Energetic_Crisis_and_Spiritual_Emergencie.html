2. Differential Diagnosis: Clinical vs. Spiritual

The work of Stanislav and Christina Grof (1989) revolutionized our understanding of the "Spiritual Emergency." They defined it as a critical and transitionary stage of a deep psychological transformation. However, the practitioner's primary ethical duty is to distinguish this from clinical psychiatric crises requiring immediate medical intervention.

5. Immediate Safety Protocols

As a Master Practitioner, your first priority is the safety of the client and yourself. You must be able to recognize when a case has moved out of the energetic realm and into the medical or psychiatric realm.

The "Red Flag" Referral Criteria
Refer to emergency clinical services immediately if the client exhibits:
  • Active Suicidal/Homicidal Ideation: Specific plans or intent to harm self or others.
  • Severe Disorientation: Inability to state their name, the date, or their current location.
  • Catatonia: Complete lack of movement or response to external stimuli.
  • Command Hallucinations: Voices telling them to perform specific, dangerous actions.

If these "Red Flags" are absent, your protocol shifts to Stabilization. This involves:

  1. Ceasing all activating techniques (Breathwork, High-frequency sound).
  2. Physical grounding (Weighted blankets, heavy food, barefoot contact with earth).
  3. E (Empowering) the client through simple, repetitive somatic tasks to bring them back into their body.
