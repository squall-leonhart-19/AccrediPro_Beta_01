6. Ethical and Legal Boundaries

Working in clinical settings requires a high degree of professional etiquette. You are a guest in the medical environment and a member of a multidisciplinary team.

Key Professional Guidelines:
  • Non-Interference: Never adjust medical equipment or comment on pharmacological interventions.
  • Scope of Practice: Do not offer "hope" for physical recovery in a hospice setting. The focus is strictly on comfort and spiritual peace.
  • Touch Policy: Always check with the nursing staff regarding "fragile skin" or site-specific contraindications (IV lines, ports, tumors).
  • Confidentiality: HIPAA and privacy laws are strictly enforced; avoid sharing details of the transition with anyone outside the immediate care team.
