5. Sustainable Practice Metrics

To prevent burnout, the practitioner must move beyond "intuition" and use objective data to manage their practice. We implement the Energetic Capacity Index (ECI), which factors in the following variables:

  • Client Intensity Score (1-5): Based on the complexity of the client's energetic blocks.
  • Session Volume: Total contact hours per week.
  • Personal Coherence Baseline: Daily HRV or subjective vitality score.

A sustainable practice typically maintains an ECI where high-intensity cases (Score 4-5) do not exceed 30% of the total weekly load. When this threshold is crossed, the risk of Biofield Fraying increases by 65%.

6. The Practitioner Recovery Plan

Following a crisis or a particularly draining clinical interaction, the practitioner must follow a structured Recovery Protocol. This is not merely "self-care" but a clinical necessity for maintaining professional competence.

Phase 1: Immediate Neutralization (0-2 Hours)
Use salt-water immersion or "smudging" to clear surface-level energetic debris. Perform the Neutralizing Blocks (N) protocol on self, focusing on the Solar Plexus.
Phase 2: Somatic Discharge (2-6 Hours)
Engage in rhythmic movement (walking, dancing) to move residual kinetic energy out of the muscular system. Avoid digital screens or stimulating environments.
Phase 3: Supervisory Integration (Within 24 Hours)
Schedule a 15-minute "Flash Supervision" call to externalize the experience and ensure the practitioner's field is not holding onto client "hooks."
