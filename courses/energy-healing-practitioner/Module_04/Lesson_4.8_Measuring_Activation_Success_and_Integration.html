2. Subjective vs. Objective Markers

To operate at a premium level, you must track both what the client feels and what their body does. Objective markers provide the scientific "anchor" for the work, while subjective markers provide the "narrative" of the healing journey.

Category Subjective (Client-Reported) Objective (Practitioner-Observed)
Sensory Feeling of warmth, tingling, or "lightness." Visible skin flushing (vasodilation), micro-tremors.
Autonomic "I feel very relaxed/sleepy." Lowered respiratory rate, increased HRV (Heart Rate Variability).
Energetic "I feel a buzzing in my hands/feet." Increased haptic resistance in the biofield (The "Pushback" effect).
Psychological Sudden clarity or emotional release. Pupillary dilation changes, shift in vocal prosody (tone).

3. The Somatic Feedback Loop: Heat, Tingling, and Flow

When you successfully Activate Healing, the body undergoes immediate physiological shifts. The most common reports are heat and tingling. These are not merely "metaphorical" energies; they are the result of specific biological processes triggered by the biofield shift.

Heat (Vasodilation): As the parasympathetic nervous system takes over (Vagal Activation), blood vessels dilate. This increases local skin temperature. In energy medicine, we often see a "Thermal Surge" where the practitioner's hands and the client's target area rise by 1-3 degrees Celsius. This indicates increased micro-circulation and nutrient delivery to the tissues.

Tingling (Neural Firing): Tingling is often the sensation of nerves "coming back online" or the movement of interstitial fluid. From a biophotonic perspective, this represents a sudden increase in coherent light emission from the cells, which the nervous system interprets as a "buzzing" or "electric" sensation.
