4. Measuring HRV: The Practitionerâ€™s Metric

How do we know if our energetic activation is working? The gold standard is Heart Rate Variability (HRV). HRV is the variation in time between each heartbeat (the R-R interval). Contrary to popular belief, a healthy heart does not beat like a metronome; it is slightly irregular.

This irregularity is controlled by the Vagus nerve. When the Vagus nerve is active, it acts as a "brake" on the heart, increasing the time between beats during exhalation. A high HRV indicates a nervous system that is flexible and ready to heal. A low HRV indicates a system locked in "Survival Mode."
