Measuring Spin and Velocity

When holding a pendulum over a chakra center (approximately 2-4 inches above the body), we look for three primary metrics:

  • Direction: Clockwise indicates an "open" or receptive center. Counter-clockwise often indicates a defensive or "releasing" state. Linear movement suggests a partial blockage.
  • Amplitude: The diameter of the circle. A large swing suggests high energy (potentially hyperactive), while a small swing suggests low vitality.
  • Velocity: The speed of the spin. A healthy chakra maintains a rhythmic, consistent speed.
