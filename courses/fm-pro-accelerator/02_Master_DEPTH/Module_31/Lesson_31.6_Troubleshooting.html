When Cases Get Stuck

Every practitioner encounters cases that don't respond as expected. These "stuck" cases are frustrating‚Äîbut they're also your greatest teachers. This lesson provides a systematic framework for troubleshooting difficult cases and identifying what you might be missing.

The 7 Reasons Cases Get Stuck

Reason 1: Wrong Primary Pattern

  • You identified the downstream pattern as primary
  • The real driver is still unaddressed
  • Action: Revisit timeline. What came first? What would fixing this pattern NOT fix?

Reason 2: Missed Trigger/Exposure

  • An ongoing trigger is perpetuating the dysfunction
  • Common: mold, food sensitivity, relationship stress, environmental toxin
  • Action: Deep dive into environment, diet, relationships. What's still present?

Reason 3: Non-Compliance

  • Client isn't following the protocol (often won't tell you)
  • Protocol is too complex, too expensive, or lifestyle doesn't support it
  • Action: Have an honest conversation. Simplify. Address barriers.

Reason 4: Insufficient Dose or Duration

  • The right intervention, but not enough of it or not long enough
  • Common with SIBO, mold, deep gut issues
  • Action: Review literature dosing. Extend treatment. Consider more aggressive protocol.

Reason 5: Structural Issue

  • Functional approach won't fix an anatomical problem
  • Examples: thyroid nodule, hiatal hernia, adhesions, cervical stenosis
  • Action: Consider imaging or specialist referral if indicated

Reason 6: Psychological Component

  • Trauma, secondary gain, or mental health component limiting recovery
  • Body is expressing what mind won't acknowledge
  • Action: Explore gently. Consider integrating trauma-informed approach or referring to therapist.

Reason 7: Need More Time

  • Healing takes longer than expected‚Äîespecially for long-standing issues
  • Impatience from client or practitioner leads to premature pivots
  • Action: Review expected timelines. Is progress happening, just slowly? Stay the course if so.

Troubleshooting Case: Marcus

Situation: Marcus, 48M. Treated for SIBO (hydrogen) for 8 weeks. Bloating improved 50% then plateaued. Fatigue and brain fog unchanged.

Troubleshooting Checklist:

  • Wrong pattern? Unlikely‚ÄîSIBO confirmed, bloating responded initially
  • Missed trigger? Asked about home environment‚Äîdiscovered recent water damage in bathroom. Possible mold.
  • Compliance? Yes‚Äîhe's diligent with protocol
  • Dose/duration? Standard protocol‚Äîmay need extension
  • Structural? Nothing suggests this
  • Psychological? No obvious component
  • Time? 8 weeks is reasonable‚Äîbut plateau suggests something else needed

Action Plan: Mold/mycotoxin testing. If positive, address mold exposure before continuing SIBO treatment. The fatigue/brain fog that didn't respond may be mold-related neuroinflammation, not gut-related.

Your Assignment

Think of a case (yours or from this course) that isn't responding as expected. Work through the 7 reasons:

  1. Could it be wrong primary pattern? Evidence?
  2. Could there be a missed trigger? What haven't you explored?
  3. Could compliance be an issue? How do you know?
  4. Is dose/duration sufficient? What does literature say?
  5. Could there be a structural component? Indicators?
  6. Could there be a psychological component? Any clues?
  7. Does this just need more time? What's reasonable expectation?
