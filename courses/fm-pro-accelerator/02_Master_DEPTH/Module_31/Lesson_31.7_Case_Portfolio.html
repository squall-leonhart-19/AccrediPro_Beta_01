Case Portfolio Structure

Essential Elements of Each Case

  • De-identified summary: Demographics (age/sex only), chief complaint, patterns
  • Clinical presentation: Key symptoms, timeline highlights, relevant labs
  • Your analysis: Pattern identification, your clinical reasoning
  • Treatment approach: What you did and why (phased approach)
  • Outcome data: Objective improvements with numbers/percentages
  • Key learning: What this case taught you

Privacy & Consent

Consent Requirements

  • Always obtain written consent before using any case publicly
  • De-identify thoroughly: no names, specific locations, unique identifiers
  • Change non-essential details if needed (occupation, exact age)
  • Distinguish internal learning cases (no consent needed) from public cases (consent required)

Using Your Portfolio

Portfolio Applications

  • Marketing: "I've helped 40+ clients with gut issues" + anonymized success stories
  • Teaching: Use cases in presentations, workshops, mentoring
  • Credentialing: Some certifications require case submissions
  • Self-assessment: Review yearly to see your growth and patterns
  • Referral building: Show potential referral sources your capabilities

Your Assignment

Choose one case (actual or from this course) and create a portfolio entry:

  1. Write a 1-paragraph de-identified summary
  2. List the patterns identified and your reasoning
  3. Outline the phased treatment approach
  4. Document 3-5 outcome metrics with before/after data
  5. Write one sentence on your key learning from this case
