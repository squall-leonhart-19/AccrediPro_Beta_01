When Patterns Collide

Real clients rarely present with a single clean pattern. Most complex cases involve multiple overlapping patterns affecting multiple systems. This lesson teaches you to cross-reference cases‚Äîfinding the connections between patterns‚Äîand determine which to address first when multiple systems are involved.

Common Pattern Combinations

Pattern Combinations You'll See Often

  • HPA + Gut: Stress disrupts digestion; gut dysfunction worsens HPA. Bidirectional.
  • Gut + Autoimmune: Leaky gut drives immune activation. Gut almost always upstream.
  • Metabolic + Hormonal: Insulin resistance disrupts sex hormones. Usually metabolic first.
  • Inflammation + Everything: Inflammation is present in almost all patterns as amplifier.
  • Toxicity + HPA + Gut: Mold often presents with all three simultaneously.

The Upstream Question

When multiple patterns are present, ask: "Which pattern, if resolved, would most improve the others?"

Finding the Upstream Driver

  • Which pattern appeared FIRST on the timeline?
  • Which pattern, if fixed, would create conditions for other patterns to resolve?
  • Is there a trigger that initiated multiple cascades?
  • Which pattern is perpetuating the others?

Multi-System Case: Andrea

Presentation: Fatigue, weight gain, brain fog, bloating, irregular periods, anxiety

Patterns Identified:

  • Pattern 1 (HPA): Wired-tired, 3am waking, anxiety
  • Pattern 3 (Gut): Bloating, SIBO positive
  • Pattern 5 (Hormonal): Irregular cycles, low progesterone

Timeline: High-stress job started 3 years ago ‚Üí GI symptoms 18 months ago ‚Üí hormonal symptoms 6 months ago

Analysis: Timeline suggests HPA dysfunction was the initiating trigger. Stress disrupted gut motility ‚Üí SIBO developed. Ongoing HPA dysfunction + gut inflammation ‚Üí hormonal disruption (progesterone steal + inflammation). HPA is upstream of both gut and hormones.

Treatment Sequence: Foundations (sleep, stress) ‚Üí HPA support + Gut protocol simultaneously ‚Üí Reassess hormones after 3 months (may normalize without direct intervention).

Check Your Understanding

Q1: In Andrea's case, why NOT address hormones directly first?

Because her hormonal imbalance is downstream of HPA and gut dysfunction. High cortisol "steals" pregnenolone from progesterone production (pregnenolone steal). Gut inflammation disrupts estrogen metabolism. Treating hormones directly without fixing HPA and gut would be treating symptoms without addressing cause‚Äîresults would be temporary at best.

Q2: What's the key question to ask when determining upstream vs. downstream patterns?

"Which pattern, if resolved, would most improve the others?" Also: "Which pattern appeared first on the timeline?" Timeline sequence often reveals causal sequence. The pattern that preceded others and that, if fixed, would create conditions for other patterns to improve is your upstream priority.
