Module 35 Quiz: Master Practitioner Capstone

1. A 52-year-old female presents with persistent fatigue, weight gain, and cold intolerance. Her TSH is elevated, and free T4 is low. She is already on levothyroxine 100mcg daily, but her symptoms persist. What is the MOST appropriate next step in managing her hypothyroidism, considering advanced functional medicine principles?

Correct Answer: C. While increasing levothyroxine or adding T3 might be considered, the MOST appropriate next step is a comprehensive thyroid panel to investigate potential underlying causes of her persistent symptoms despite medication. This includes autoimmune thyroiditis (TPO/TgAb), impaired T4 to T3 conversion (reverse T3), and nutrient deficiencies crucial for thyroid hormone synthesis and function. Referral might be necessary later if the underlying cause remains unclear.

2. A 40-year-old male reports experiencing anxiety, insomnia, and palpitations. He also mentions frequent headaches and muscle tension. He works a high-stress job and consumes several cups of coffee daily. Which of the following would be the MOST comprehensive functional medicine approach to address his symptoms?

Correct Answer: C. A comprehensive approach addresses the root causes of his symptoms. While magnesium and lifestyle changes are helpful, a holistic program encompassing stress management, adaptogens, dietary changes, and gut health assessment provides the most comprehensive and sustainable solution. His caffeine intake is likely exacerbating his symptoms, and gut health plays a significant role in neurotransmitter production and anxiety.

3. A 60-year-old male with a history of type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease is looking to improve his metabolic health. He is currently on metformin and a statin. Beyond medication, what is the MOST important functional medicine intervention to recommend?

Correct Answer: B. While other options have benefits, a personalized dietary plan combined with exercise and stress management provides the most comprehensive and sustainable approach to improve metabolic health. A ketogenic diet might be too restrictive and difficult to maintain long-term. HIIT is beneficial but should be part of a broader exercise plan. Omega-3s are helpful but not the most crucial intervention.

4. A 35-year-old female presents with chronic fatigue, brain fog, and digestive issues (bloating, gas). She has been diagnosed with Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and has tried various medications without significant relief. What is the MOST appropriate initial functional medicine investigation?

Correct Answer: A. While the other options might be considered later, a comprehensive stool analysis is the MOST appropriate initial investigation. It can identify dysbiosis, inflammation, and digestive enzyme deficiencies, providing valuable insights into the root causes of her IBS and guiding targeted interventions. A low-FODMAP diet is often helpful but doesn't address underlying imbalances.

5. A 48-year-old male presents with declining cognitive function, including memory loss and difficulty concentrating. He has a family history of Alzheimer's disease. Which of the following is the MOST comprehensive functional medicine approach to assess and manage his cognitive decline?

Correct Answer: C. A comprehensive assessment is crucial to identify underlying causes and modifiable risk factors. While medication and supplements might have a role, a personalized plan addressing diet, exercise, sleep, stress, and other modifiable factors offers the most comprehensive approach to managing cognitive decline and potentially slowing disease progression. APOE genotype gives insight into genetic predisposition.

6. A 28-year-old female is struggling with infertility. She has regular menstrual cycles but has been unable to conceive after 18 months of trying. What is the MOST comprehensive initial functional medicine workup?

Correct Answer: B. A comprehensive hormonal assessment, thyroid evaluation, vitamin D level, and screening for underlying conditions are crucial to identify potential causes of infertility. Partner sperm analysis is equally important. Referral for IVF might be necessary later, but a thorough initial workup is essential.

7. A patient undergoing chemotherapy for cancer is experiencing significant side effects, including nausea, fatigue, and mucositis. What is the MOST appropriate functional medicine approach to support them?

Correct Answer: B. A personalized nutrition plan, specific supplements (with oncologist approval), and gentle exercise can significantly improve quality of life during chemotherapy. Glutamine helps repair the gut lining, reducing mucositis. Ginger is effective for nausea. It's crucial to ensure no supplement interactions with the chemotherapy protocol.

8. A 70-year-old male presents with chronic joint pain, stiffness, and limited range of motion in his knees. He has been diagnosed with osteoarthritis. Which of the following is the MOST comprehensive functional medicine approach to manage his condition?

Correct Answer: C. A comprehensive plan addressing inflammation, supporting joint health, and promoting mobility is the most effective approach. NSAIDs provide temporary pain relief but don't address the underlying causes. Exercise is crucial for maintaining joint function and reducing pain. Gut health and food sensitivities can contribute to inflammation.

9. You have a patient with a known MTHFR gene mutation. They are experiencing symptoms of anxiety, depression, and fatigue. How would you BEST manage this patient using functional medicine principles?

Correct Answer: C. While MTHFR mutations don't always cause problems, in symptomatic individuals, supporting methylation is key. Methylfolate is the active form of folate and bypasses the need for the MTHFR enzyme. Addressing gut health is crucial as it impacts nutrient absorption. High doses of folic acid are generally avoided, as they can interfere with methylfolate metabolism.

10. A patient presents with symptoms suggestive of adrenal dysfunction (fatigue, brain fog, sleep disturbances, salt cravings). An initial salivary cortisol test reveals a flattened diurnal cortisol curve. What is the MOST appropriate next step, considering advanced functional medicine principles?

Correct Answer: C. A flattened cortisol curve often reflects chronic stress. Identifying and addressing the underlying stressors is crucial for restoring adrenal function. Adaptogens and lifestyle changes can be helpful, but they are most effective when combined with addressing the root causes. Hydrocortisone should only be considered in cases of confirmed adrenal insufficiency and under the guidance of an endocrinologist.
