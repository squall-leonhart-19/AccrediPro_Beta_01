The Autoimmune Triad

Three Factors Required for Autoimmunity

  • Genetic susceptibility: HLA genes, family history
  • Intestinal permeability: Leaky gut allows triggers to cross barrier
  • Environmental trigger: Infection, toxin, food, stress‚Äîthe match that lights the fire

Common Autoimmune Triggers

  • Gluten (celiac, Hashimoto's, rheumatoid)
  • EBV reactivation (MS, lupus, Hashimoto's)
  • Mold/mycotoxins
  • Chronic stress (HPA-immune axis)
  • Gut dysbiosis (molecular mimicry)

The Functional Approach

Autoimmune Protocol Hierarchy

  • 1. Remove triggers: Gluten, dairy, identified food sensitivities, toxins
  • 2. Heal the gut: Address permeability, dysbiosis, infections
  • 3. Reduce inflammation: Anti-inflammatory diet, omega-3s, curcumin
  • 4. Modulate immunity: Vitamin D optimization, low-dose naltrexone in some cases
  • 5. Manage stress: HPA dysfunction drives immune flares

Autoimmune Case: Michelle

Presentation: Michelle, 35F. Hashimoto's diagnosed 3 years ago. TPO antibodies 580 (high). Fatigue, weight gain, brain fog despite levothyroxine.

Investigation: Timeline revealed symptoms started after mono (EBV) in college, worsened after pregnancy. Gut: SIBO positive. Food: reacting to gluten and dairy.

Protocol:

  • 100% gluten and dairy elimination
  • Treat SIBO (8 weeks antimicrobials + prokinetic)
  • Gut healing (L-glutamine, zinc carnosine, bone broth)
  • Vitamin D optimization (target 60-80 ng/mL)
  • Selenium 200mcg for thyroid/antibody support

Outcome (6 months): TPO antibodies dropped to 220, energy improved 70%, brain fog resolved, lost 12 lbs without trying.
