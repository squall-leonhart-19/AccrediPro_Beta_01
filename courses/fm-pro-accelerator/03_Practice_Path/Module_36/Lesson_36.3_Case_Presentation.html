Present Cases Effectively

How you present a case demonstrates your clinical thinking and invites useful feedback.

Case Presentation Format

Standard Presentation (5-7 min)

  • Opening (30 sec): Demographics, reason for consultation
  • History (1-2 min): Relevant health and symptom history
  • Data (1-2 min): Key lab findings, assessments
  • Analysis (1-2 min): Your clinical reasoning
  • Proposal (1 min): Recommended approach
  • Question (30 sec): What you need help with

Presentation Tips

Do's

  • Be concise: Focus on relevant information
  • Show reasoning: Explain why you think what you think
  • Identify gaps: What you don't know
  • Ask specific questions: Not "what should I do?"

Don'ts

  • Don't recite everything: Curate information
  • Don't hide uncertainty: It's okay not to know
  • Don't defend prematurely: Listen to feedback first

Common Mistakes

Avoid These

  • Too much detail: Overwhelming, loses focus
  • Too little context: Reviewers can't follow
  • Anchoring: Fixating on first hypothesis
  • Vague questions: "Any thoughts?" isn't specific
