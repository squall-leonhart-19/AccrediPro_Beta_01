Make Feedback Work for You

Feedback is a gift‚Äîbut only if you know how to receive and use it.

Receiving Feedback Well

In the Moment

  • Listen fully: Don't interrupt or defend
  • Take notes: Write down what's said
  • Ask questions: Clarify what you don't understand
  • Thank: Appreciate the time and input

Managing Emotions

  • Normalize discomfort: Feedback often triggers defensiveness
  • Separate identity: Your work isn't you
  • Growth mindset: Mistakes are learning opportunities
  • Process later: Don't react immediately

Integrating Feedback

After the Session

  • Review notes: What were the key points?
  • Identify patterns: Same feedback repeatedly?
  • Create action items: What will you do differently?
  • Update your approach: Implement changes

Feedback Log

  • Date: When received
  • Source: Who gave it
  • Feedback: What was said
  • Pattern: Have you heard this before?
  • Action: What you'll do

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Listen without defending: Take notes, ask questions
  • Separate identity from work: Feedback on work isn't personal
  • Create action items from feedback
  • Track patterns in what you hear
