The Controversial Test

Food sensitivity testing is one of functional medicine's most debated topics. IgG panels are commonly ordered, but their clinical utility is questioned. This lesson cuts through the confusion to help you use food sensitivity testing appropriately‚Äîand know when elimination diets are superior.

The IgG Testing Debate

Why IgG Tests Are Used

  • Practitioners observe clinical improvement when reactive foods removed
  • Provides a "roadmap" for elimination that clients find easier to follow
  • May identify foods clients didn't suspect
  • Some research shows correlation with symptoms in specific conditions

The Scientific Concerns

  • IgG may indicate exposure, not pathology: You make IgG to foods you eat regularly
  • Poor reproducibility: Same sample sent to different labs ‚Üí different results
  • No gold standard validation: Results don't consistently correlate with blind food challenges
  • Risk of over-restriction: Removing 20 foods creates nutritional deficit and disordered eating risk

‚ö†Ô∏è Clinical Reality

Major medical organizations (AAAAI, EAACI) do not recommend IgG testing for diagnosing food sensitivity. However, many functional practitioners use them as a guide alongside clinical judgment. Know the limitations; don't treat results as gospel.

When to Use Food Sensitivity Testing

Potentially Useful Scenarios

  • Client struggles to identify triggers through elimination diet
  • Multiple vague symptoms (brain fog, fatigue, joint pain) without clear pattern
  • Client motivation increases with "test results" to follow
  • Concurrent with gut healing protocol (indicates permeability)

Better to Use Elimination Diet

  • Clear symptom patterns (e.g., bloating after dairy)
  • Limited budget (elimination diets are free)
  • History of disordered eating (test results can trigger restriction)
  • Already suspect specific foods

Interpreting Results Wisely

Best Practices

  • Prioritize high reactives: Remove only the highest-scoring foods initially (top 3-5)
  • 30-60 day trial: Remove, assess symptoms, then rechallenge one at a time
  • Don't remove everything: Removing 20 foods is unsustainable and unnecessary
  • Always rechallenge: Many foods can be reintroduced after gut healing
  • Address root cause: High IgG across many foods often indicates gut permeability ‚Üí heal the gut

Gut Healing Resolves Sensitivity

Many food sensitivities are secondary to gut dysfunction. When intestinal permeability is addressed, sensitivities often resolve:

The Healing Timeline

  • Remove high reactive foods while healing gut
  • Complete 3-6 month gut repair protocol
  • Systematically rechallenge previously reactive foods
  • Many will now be tolerated
  • Retest only if necessary (usually not needed)
