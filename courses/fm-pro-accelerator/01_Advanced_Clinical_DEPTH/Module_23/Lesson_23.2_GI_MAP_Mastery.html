The Microbiome Intelligence Report

The GI-MAP (Gastrointestinal Microbial Assay Plus) uses DNA analysis to identify pathogens, opportunists, beneficial bacteria, and gut health markers. It answers questions blood tests can't: Is there an infection? Is the microbiome balanced? Is there inflammation or permeability?

üéØ What You'll Master

  • Reading opportunistic overgrowth patterns
  • Understanding beneficial bacteria levels and diversity
  • Gut health markers: Zonulin, SIgA, Calprotectin, Beta-glucuronidase
  • Creating actionable protocols from GI-MAP results

Pathogen Panel: What Must Be Addressed

True pathogens require treatment. These aren't "maybe" findings:

üî¥ H. pylori (with virulence factors)
  • CagA and VacA positive = more virulent strains
  • Associated with ulcers, GERD, low stomach acid
  • Protocol: Antimicrobial herbs or refer for triple therapy
üî¥ Parasites
  • Giardia, Cryptosporidium, Entamoeba histolytica
  • Can cause chronic GI symptoms, fatigue, malabsorption
  • Protocol: Targeted antiparasitic protocols
üü° Bacterial Pathogens
  • C. difficile, pathogenic E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella
  • May require medical referral if severe
  • Protocol: Depends on severity and presentation
üü° Viral Markers
  • Cytomegalovirus, EBV if present
  • Consider immune support
  • May indicate chronic viral reactivation

Opportunistic Overgrowth

These organisms are normal in small amounts but problematic when overgrown:

Candida Species

  • Mild elevation: Diet modification, probiotics often sufficient
  • Significant elevation: Antifungal protocol (herbs: oregano, caprylic acid, berberine)
  • Context matters: Recent antibiotics? High-sugar diet? Immunocompromised?

Bacterial Opportunists

  • Klebsiella, Citrobacter, Pseudomonas: Often respond to antimicrobial protocols
  • Streptococcus, Staphylococcus: Consider biofilm disruption
  • Approach: Target with broad-spectrum botanicals, support immunity

Beneficial Bacteria Assessment

Key Beneficial Species

  • Lactobacillus spp: Low = consider probiotic supplementation
  • Bifidobacterium spp: Essential for gut barrier, immune function
  • Akkermansia muciniphila: Mucin-degrader, metabolic health marker
  • Faecalibacterium prausnitzii: Anti-inflammatory, butyrate producer

üí° Pro Tip

Low beneficial bacteria often improves naturally when you remove pathogens and opportunists. Don't just throw probiotics at it‚Äîaddress what's crowding out the good guys first.

Gut Health Markers Deep Dive

Marker What It Measures High Means Low Means

Clinical Patterns

Pattern: High Zonulin + Low SIgA

  • Interpretation: Leaky gut with exhausted immune response
  • Client profile: Chronic stress, autoimmune, food sensitivities
  • Protocol focus: Gut repair (L-glutamine, zinc carnosine), stress reduction

Pattern: High Calprotectin + Pathogens Present

  • Interpretation: Active infection causing inflammation
  • Client profile: GI symptoms, may have bloody stool if severe
  • Protocol focus: Eradicate pathogen first, then repair

Pattern: High Beta-glucuronidase

  • Interpretation: Estrogen recirculation ‚Üí estrogen dominance contribution
  • Client profile: Heavy periods, PMS, fibroids, hormone-related symptoms
  • Protocol focus: Calcium-D-glucarate, fiber, probiotics (especially glucuronidase-low strains)

Creating the Protocol

The 5R Framework Applied to GI-MAP

  • Remove: Based on pathogens/opportunists identified
  • Replace: If steatocrit high ‚Üí digestive enzymes, bile support
  • Reinoculate: If beneficials low ‚Üí targeted probiotics
  • Repair: If zonulin high ‚Üí gut healing nutrients
  • Rebalance: Address lifestyle factors perpetuating dysbiosis

Key Terms

GI-MAP

DNA-based stool test measuring pathogens, opportunists, beneficials, and gut health markers

Zonulin

Protein that regulates intestinal permeability; elevated = "leaky gut"

Beta-glucuronidase

Enzyme that deconjugates estrogen, allowing reabsorption; high = estrogen recirculation

SIgA

Secretory Immunoglobulin A - first-line mucosal immune defense; low indicates immune exhaustion
