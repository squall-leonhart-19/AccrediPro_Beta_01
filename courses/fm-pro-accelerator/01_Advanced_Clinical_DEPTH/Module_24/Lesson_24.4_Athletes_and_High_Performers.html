Fueling Peak Performance

Athletes and high performers have elevated nutrient demands, recovery challenges, and performance optimization goals. Whether weekend warriors or elite competitors, understanding their unique physiology allows you to provide targeted support.

Macronutrient Considerations

Protein for Athletes

  • Endurance athletes: 1.2-1.4g/kg body weight
  • Strength/power athletes: 1.6-2.2g/kg body weight
  • Distribution: 20-40g per meal, spaced every 3-4 hours
  • Post-exercise window: 20-30g within 2 hours of training
  • Leucine threshold: 2.5-3g triggers muscle protein synthesis

Carbohydrates for Performance

  • Light activity: 3-5g/kg/day
  • Moderate training: 5-7g/kg/day
  • High-volume training: 6-10g/kg/day
  • Extreme training/competition: 8-12g/kg/day
  • Timing: Complex carbs for sustained energy; simple during long events

Fat for Athletes

  • Minimum: 20% of calories (hormone production)
  • Optimal: 25-35% for most athletes
  • Low-fat diets risk: Hormone disruption, especially in females
  • Fat adaptation: Some endurance athletes benefit from higher fat

Overtraining Syndrome

Recognizing Overtraining

  • Performance plateau or decline despite continued training
  • Persistent fatigue that rest doesn't resolve
  • Mood disturbances (irritability, depression, anxiety)
  • Frequent illness (immune suppression)
  • Elevated resting heart rate
  • Sleep disturbances despite fatigue
  • Loss of motivation to train

Functional Approach to Overtraining

  • Rest is essential‚Äînot something to push through
  • Assess HPA axis: DUTCH cortisol pattern often blunted
  • Nutritional support: Adaptogens, B vitamins, magnesium
  • Sleep optimization: Critical for recovery
  • Gradual return: Rebuild training volume slowly

Female Athlete Considerations

‚ö†Ô∏è RED-S: Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport

When energy intake doesn't match expenditure, females risk: menstrual dysfunction (missing periods), bone stress injuries, hormonal imbalance, and long-term health consequences. Any female athlete with irregular or absent periods needs investigation‚Äîthis is not "normal" from training.

Supporting Female Athletes

  • Track menstrual cycles: Missing periods is a red flag, not a training badge
  • Adequate calories: Fear of weight gain leads to under-fueling
  • Bone density support: Calcium, vitamin D, vitamin K2
  • Iron monitoring: Menstruation + training = high loss
  • Cycle considerations: Performance varies with menstrual phase

Evidence-Based Supplements

Well-Supported for Performance

  • Creatine: 3-5g daily; strength, power, cognitive benefits
  • Caffeine: 3-6mg/kg body weight pre-exercise; proven ergogenic
  • Beta-alanine: 3-6g daily; buffers acid in high-intensity exercise
  • Sodium bicarbonate: Acid buffering for repeated sprints

Functional Additions

  • Tart cherry: Reduces muscle soreness, improves sleep
  • Curcumin: Anti-inflammatory recovery support
  • Ashwagandha: Stress adaptation, may improve VO2 max
  • Collagen + vitamin C: Tendon and ligament support
