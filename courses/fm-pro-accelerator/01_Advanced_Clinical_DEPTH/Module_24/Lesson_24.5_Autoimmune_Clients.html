Autoimmune conditions occur when the immune system attacks the body's own tissue. Functional medicine offers powerful support through identifying triggers, reducing inflammation, and supporting immune regulation‚Äîoften achieving what medications alone cannot.

The Autoimmune Triad

Three factors must be present for autoimmunity to develop:

1. Genetic Susceptibility

  • Family history increases risk but doesn't guarantee disease
  • Genes load the gun; environment pulls the trigger
  • HLA genes, MTHFR, and others create vulnerability

2. Environmental Triggers

  • Infections: EBV, H. pylori, Lyme, and others
  • Toxins: Heavy metals, mold, pesticides
  • Food antigens: Gluten, dairy (molecular mimicry)
  • Stress: Chronic psychological stress dysregulates immune
  • Hormonal changes: Postpartum, perimenopause are high-risk windows

3. Intestinal Permeability

  • 70-80% of immune system is in the gut
  • "Leaky gut" allows antigens to enter bloodstream
  • Cross-reactivity triggers autoimmune response
  • Healing the gut is often the linchpin

The Autoimmune Protocol (AIP)

AIP Elimination Phase

  • Remove: Grains, dairy, eggs, legumes, nightshades, nuts, seeds, refined sugars, alcohol, NSAIDs
  • Duration: Minimum 30-90 days
  • Focus on: Quality meats, fish, vegetables, fruits, healthy fats
  • Organ meats: Encouraged for nutrient density

Reintroduction Phase

  • Methodically reintroduce one food at a time
  • Wait 3-5 days between reintroductions
  • Track symptoms carefully (fatigue, pain, digestion, skin)
  • Many foods can return; some may never be tolerated

üí° Pro Tip

Full AIP is aggressive and difficult. For many clients, start with gluten, dairy, and sugar removal for 30 days. If significant improvement, may not need full AIP. Reserve full protocol for stubborn cases.

Key Supplements for Autoimmunity

Foundational Support

  • Vitamin D: Most AI patients are deficient; target 50-70 ng/mL
  • Omega-3: 3-4g EPA+DHA for anti-inflammatory effect
  • Glutathione: Master antioxidant; liposomal or precursors (NAC)
  • Probiotics: Specific strains support immune regulation

Gut Healing Protocol

  • L-glutamine: 5-15g daily for intestinal repair
  • Zinc carnosine: Mucosal healing
  • Collagen: Supports gut lining integrity
  • Bone broth: Contains glycine, proline, glutamine

Immune Modulators (with caution)

  • Curcumin: Modulates NF-kB pathway
  • Resveratrol: Anti-inflammatory, immune regulating
  • Low-dose naltrexone (LDN): Prescription, but often used functionally
  • Avoid "immune boosters": Echinacea, elderberry can worsen AI

Managing Flares

During Active Flare

  • Return to strict elimination: Remove all suspect foods
  • Rest: Sleep and stress reduction critical
  • Increase omega-3: Temporarily higher doses
  • Avoid new supplements: Not the time to experiment
  • Work with their medical team: Medications may be needed
