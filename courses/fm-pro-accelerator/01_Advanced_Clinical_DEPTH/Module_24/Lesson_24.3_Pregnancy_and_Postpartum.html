Nourishing Two Lives

Pregnancy and postpartum are the most nutritionally demanding periods of a woman's life. Optimal nutrition influences fertility, pregnancy outcomes, fetal development, lactation, and maternal recovery. This lesson covers what every health coach needs to know.

üéØ What You'll Master

  • Trimester-specific nutritional needs
  • Critical nutrients: folate, choline, iron, DHA, vitamin D
  • Managing common pregnancy discomforts nutritionally
  • Postpartum depletion and recovery
  • Supplements that are safe vs. contraindicated

Nutritional Needs by Trimester

First Trimester

  • Folate crucial (neural tube)
  • Calories: Normal (not eating for two)
  • Manage nausea
  • Avoid listeria-risk foods
  • Iron needs increase slightly

Second Trimester

  • +340 calories/day
  • Protein needs increase
  • DHA critical (brain development)
  • Calcium for bone development
  • Iron demand increases

Third Trimester

  • +450 calories/day
  • Highest iron needs
  • DHA continues
  • Prepare for labor (dates, tea)
  • GBS considerations

Critical Pregnancy Nutrients

Folate (not just folic acid)

  • Why: Prevents neural tube defects; critical in first 28 days
  • Amount: 600-800mcg DFE daily
  • Form: Methylfolate (5-MTHF) preferred‚Äîmany women have MTHFR variants
  • Food sources: Leafy greens, legumes, liver

Choline

  • Why: Brain development, prevents neural tube defects (works with folate)
  • Amount: 450mg/day pregnancy, 550mg lactation
  • Most women deficient: Prenatal vitamins often don't include enough
  • Food sources: Eggs (highest), liver, beef, fish

DHA (Omega-3)

  • Why: Brain and eye development; reduces preterm birth risk
  • Amount: Minimum 200-300mg DHA daily; ideally 500-1000mg
  • Sources: Low-mercury fish (salmon, sardines) 2-3x/week or algae-based DHA

Iron

  • Why: Blood volume increases 50%; prevents anemia
  • Amount: 27mg daily (nearly double non-pregnant)
  • Note: Check ferritin; many women start pregnancy depleted
  • Gentle forms: Iron bisglycinate better tolerated than sulfate

Vitamin D

  • Why: Bone development, immune function, reduces preeclampsia risk
  • Amount: 4000 IU often needed to achieve optimal levels (40-60 ng/mL)
  • Most deficient: Especially women with darker skin, limited sun

Managing Pregnancy Discomforts

Nausea/Morning Sickness

  • Small, frequent meals: Never let stomach get empty
  • Ginger: 1g/day in divided doses (tea, capsules, candy)
  • B6: 25-50mg twice daily
  • Protein at bedtime: Stabilizes blood sugar overnight

Constipation

  • Magnesium: Citrate or glycinate form
  • Fiber + water: Increase together
  • Movement: Walking, prenatal yoga
  • Probiotic: Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium species

Heartburn

  • Small meals: Reduces stomach distension
  • Elevate after eating: Don't lie flat
  • Avoid triggers: Spicy, acidic, fatty foods
  • Papaya enzymes: Can help (chewable)

Postpartum Depletion

The Reality of Maternal Depletion

  • Baby "strips" mother of nutrients in utero
  • Blood loss at birth further depletes iron
  • Lactation continues to drain: calories, protein, DHA, iodine
  • Sleep deprivation compounds nutritional stress
  • Postpartum depression risk increases with nutrient depletion

Postpartum Recovery Protocol

  • Iron: Rebuild stores lost in birth
  • Omega-3: Continue high-dose DHA for brain recovery and milk
  • Protein: 1.1-1.3g/kg for tissue repair and lactation
  • B vitamins: Energy production, mood support
  • Vitamin D: Continue testing and supplementing

Supplement Safety

üö® Contraindicated in Pregnancy

  • Vitamin A (retinol): >10,000 IU risk of birth defects (beta-carotene OK)
  • High-dose vitamin E: Bleeding risk
  • Certain herbs: Black cohosh, blue cohosh, pennyroyal, dong quai
  • Cleansing/detox protocols: Mobilizes toxins that can cross placenta

Generally Safe in Pregnancy

  • Prenatal multivitamin (quality brand)
  • Fish oil/DHA (tested for heavy metals)
  • Probiotics (well-studied in pregnancy)
  • Ginger, B6 for nausea
  • Magnesium for constipation, sleep, leg cramps
  • Vitamin D (check levels, dose appropriately)
