The Gut-Brain Connection

Why Gut Health = Mental Health

  • 90% of serotonin is produced in the gut
  • Vagus nerve provides bidirectional communication
  • Gut inflammation ‚Üí brain inflammation (cytokines cross blood-brain barrier)
  • Microbiome diversity correlates with mental health outcomes
  • Gut dysbiosis associated with depression, anxiety, and cognitive decline

Priority: Heal the Gut First

  • Address dysbiosis, SIBO, permeability
  • Remove inflammatory foods
  • Support microbiome diversity
  • Many clients see mood improve from gut work alone

Key Nutrients for Mental Health

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA specifically)

  • Most researched nutrient for depression
  • Dose: 1-2g EPA daily for therapeutic effect
  • Works synergistically with antidepressants
  • Anti-inflammatory mechanism in brain

Vitamin D

  • Vitamin D receptors throughout brain
  • Deficiency strongly associated with depression
  • Target: 50-70 ng/mL
  • Seasonal affective disorder (SAD) responds to D

B Vitamins

  • B12: Deficiency mimics depression; check methylmalonic acid
  • Folate: Required for neurotransmitter synthesis; use methylfolate
  • B6: Cofactor for serotonin and GABA production
  • B vitamin deficiency common in depression

Magnesium

  • Calms nervous system via GABA modulation
  • Deficiency associated with anxiety, insomnia
  • Glycinate or threonate for brain penetration
  • Safe and well-tolerated; start 200-400mg

Zinc

  • Modulates glutamate and GABA
  • Low levels associated with depression
  • Works synergistically with antidepressants
  • 15-30mg daily; balance with copper if long-term

Blood Sugar and Mood

The Blood Sugar-Mood Connection

  • Hypoglycemia: Causes anxiety, irritability, panic-like symptoms
  • Blood sugar spikes: Followed by crashes that affect mood
  • Insulin resistance: Brain impacts‚Äîassociated with depression, cognitive decline
  • Many "anxiety" symptoms are actually blood sugar dysregulation

Blood Sugar Stabilization Protocol

  • Protein at every meal: Minimum 20g
  • Pair carbs with protein/fat: Never eat carbs alone
  • Regular meal timing: Every 3-4 hours initially
  • Avoid refined sugars: They worsen the cycle
  • Morning protein: Critical for cortisol and energy
