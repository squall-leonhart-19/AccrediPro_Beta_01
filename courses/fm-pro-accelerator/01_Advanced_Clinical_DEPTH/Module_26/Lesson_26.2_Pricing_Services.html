Hourly vs. Package Pricing

Problems with Hourly Pricing

  • Caps your income: Limited hours = limited revenue
  • Punishes efficiency: Getting faster means earning less
  • Focuses on time, not results: Clients buy transformation, not hours
  • Doesn't account for prep: Research, protocols, emails not captured
  • Creates transaction-by-transaction mindset: No commitment

Benefits of Package Pricing

  • Predictable revenue: Know what you'll earn in advance
  • Client commitment: Investment creates buy-in and compliance
  • Focuses on outcomes: Sell the transformation, not the time
  • Allows padding: Built-in buffer for emails, extra support
  • Better results: Continuity of care = better outcomes

Calculate Your Minimum Viable Rate

The Formula

  • Step 1: Calculate annual expenses (personal + business)
  • Step 2: Add desired profit margin (20-30%)
  • Step 3: Determine available billable hours (realistically 60-70% of work hours)
  • Step 4: Divide total by billable hours = minimum hourly rate

Value-Based Pricing

Price Based on Transformation

  • What is the outcome worth? Resolving chronic fatigue, reversing prediabetes, losing 50 lbs‚Äîwhat's that worth?
  • Consider cost of NOT solving: Medical bills, missed work, quality of life
  • Position as investment, not expense: "This program is $2,500 vs. years of fatigue"
  • Your expertise has value: Years of training, continuing education, clinical experience

Positioning for Premium Pricing

  • Specialize: Specialists always command higher fees
  • Document results: Case studies and testimonials justify rates
  • Professional presentation: Website, branding, intake process
  • Confidence: If you don't believe your value, neither will clients

Raising Your Rates

When to Raise Rates

  • Fully booked: If you're at capacity, increase rates
  • After additional training: New certifications add value
  • Annually: At minimum, raise to match inflation
  • When you resent your prices: Undercharging leads to burnout

How to Raise Rates

  • Give notice: "As of [date], my rates will be..."
  • Grandfather existing clients: Optional grace period
  • No justification needed: "My investment in continuing education..." if you must
  • Raise for new clients first: Test new pricing without disrupting current clients
