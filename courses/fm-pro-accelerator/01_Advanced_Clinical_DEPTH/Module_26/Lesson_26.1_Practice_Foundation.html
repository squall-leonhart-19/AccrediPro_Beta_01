Legal Structure Options

Sole Proprietorship

  • Simplest to set up: No formal registration in most states
  • No liability protection: Personal assets at risk
  • Tax simplicity: Income reported on personal return (Schedule C)
  • Best for: Testing the waters, very low risk situations
  • Not recommended for clinical practice due to liability exposure

LLC (Limited Liability Company)

  • Liability protection: Personal assets protected from business lawsuits
  • Flexible taxation: Can elect to be taxed as sole prop, partnership, or S-Corp
  • Moderate complexity: Required filings vary by state
  • Best for: Most wellness practitioners‚Äîgood balance of protection and simplicity
  • Recommended starting point for clinical practice

S-Corporation

  • Tax advantages: Potentially lower self-employment taxes at higher income levels
  • Requires reasonable salary: Must pay yourself a salary + distributions
  • More compliance: Payroll, annual filings, more accounting needs
  • Best for: Net income consistently above $50-75K; consult an accountant
  • Often elected as LLC ‚Üí S-Corp tax treatment

Essential Business Setup

Checklist: Getting Started

  • Choose your business name: Check availability, domain, social handles
  • Register your business: LLC registration with your state
  • Get an EIN: Free from IRS; used for banking, taxes
  • Open business bank account: Separate personal and business finances
  • Set up business accounting: QuickBooks, Wave, or hire a bookkeeper
  • Get professional liability insurance: Non-negotiable for clinical work
  • Create client intake forms: Informed consent, health history
  • Set up scheduling system: Calendly, Acuity, Practice Better

Insurance Considerations

Professional Liability Insurance

  • Also called: Malpractice insurance, errors & omissions (E&O)
  • Covers: Claims of negligence, mistakes, failure to provide adequate service
  • Typical cost: $200-$600/year for nutritionists (varies by state, scope)
  • Providers: HPSO, Mercer, Nutritional Business Insurance
  • Required: Essential for any clinical practice; some states mandate

Other Insurance to Consider

  • General liability: Covers slip-and-fall, property damage (if in-person office)
  • Business owner's policy (BOP): Bundles general liability + property
  • Cyber liability: Covers data breaches (important for telehealth)
  • Health insurance: As self-employed, you'll need your own coverage
