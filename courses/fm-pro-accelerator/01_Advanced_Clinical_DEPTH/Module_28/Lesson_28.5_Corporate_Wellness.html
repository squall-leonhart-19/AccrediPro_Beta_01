Beyond 1:1 to B2B

Corporate wellness offers larger contracts, recurring revenue, and impact at scale. This lesson covers breaking into the corporate market.

The Corporate Opportunity

Why Companies Invest in Wellness

  • Healthcare costs: Wellness reduces insurance claims
  • Productivity: Healthy employees perform better
  • Retention: Benefits attract and keep talent
  • Culture: Wellness programs boost morale
  • Trending: Post-COVID focus on employee wellbeing

What Companies Want

Popular Corporate Offerings

  • Lunch and learns: Educational sessions during lunch
  • Wellness workshops: Stress, nutrition, sleep, movement
  • Challenges: Step challenges, hydration, healthy eating
  • 1:1 coaching: Employee perk, individual sessions
  • Health screenings: Biometrics, health risk assessments
  • Wellness portals: Online resources and programs

What Decision-Makers Care About

  • ROI: How does this save money or improve metrics?
  • Ease: Will this be easy to implement?
  • Engagement: Will employees actually participate?
  • Measurable results: Can we track outcomes?

Packaging for Corporate

Productized Offerings

  • Single workshop: $500-2,000 for a 60-90 min session
  • Workshop series: 4-6 sessions over a quarter ($3,000-10,000)
  • Ongoing program: Monthly workshops + resources ($1,000-3,000/month)
  • 1:1 coaching for employees: Per-session or packages
  • Virtual or in-person: Virtual scales better

Finding Companies

Where to Find Opportunities

  • Local businesses: Start with small-medium companies
  • HR networks: SHRM chapters, HR conferences
  • LinkedIn: Connect with HR directors, benefits managers
  • Insurance brokers: They recommend wellness providers
  • Your existing network: Who do you know that works somewhere?
  • Other practitioners: Partner on larger contracts

The Pitch

  • Focus on their problems: Stress, burnout, healthcare costs
  • Present solutions: Your specific offering
  • Show results: Case studies, testimonials, data
  • Make it easy: "Here's a ready-to-go program"

Proposals and Pricing

Corporate Proposal Elements

  • Executive summary: What you're offering, why it matters
  • Problem/opportunity: What they're facing
  • Your solution: Specific program details
  • Outcomes: Expected results, how you'll measure
  • Investment: Clear pricing, what's included
  • About you: Credentials, experience, testimonials

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Corporate wellness = larger contracts, more impact
  • Companies want: ROI, ease, engagement, measurable results
  • Productize offerings: Workshops, series, ongoing programs
  • Find opportunities: LinkedIn, HR networks, local businesses
  • Focus on their problems in your pitch, not your credentials
