Leverage Others' Audiences

Building strategic relationships is one of the fastest ways to grow. Partnerships multiply your reach without the grind of building from zero.

Strategic Networking

Quality Over Quantity

  • Target: People who serve your ideal clients
  • Build genuine relationships: Not just collecting business cards
  • Give first: How can you help them before asking?
  • Follow up consistently: One coffee doesn't build a relationship

Where to Network

  • Professional associations: NTA, NANP, IFM, etc.
  • Local business groups: Chamber of commerce, BNI
  • Health practitioner meetups: In person and online
  • Conferences: Speak, attend, sponsor
  • Online communities: Facebook groups, Slack communities

Partnership Opportunities

Types of Partnerships

  • Guest expert: Appear on their podcast, webinar, in their group
  • Cross-promotion: Promote each other to your lists
  • Joint workshops: Co-host an event
  • Bundle offers: Package your services with complementary practitioners
  • Affiliate: Earn commission for referring their programs

Ideal Partners

  • Same audience, different service: Fitness trainers, therapists, health coaches
  • Upstream: Who has your clients before they need you?
  • Downstream: Who helps them after you've finished?
  • Complementary modalities: Acupuncturists, chiropractors, yoga instructors

Professional Referral Networks

Building Referral Relationships

  • Identify 10-15 key partners: Quality over quantity
  • Reach out genuinely: Coffee chat, video call
  • Be clear about who you serve: So they know when to refer
  • Refer to them first: Give before you ask
  • Thank and update: When you receive referrals, follow up

Medical Provider Relationships

  • Local MDs, DOs: Especially integrative-minded
  • Naturopaths: Often looking for nutrition specialists
  • Physical therapists: Inflammation, recovery nutrition
  • Mental health providers: Nutrition-mood connection
  • Approach professionally: One-page "How I can help your patients"

Community Building

Creating Your Own Community

  • Facebook group: Free community around your niche
  • Local meetups: Host monthly health gatherings
  • Alumni network: Past clients stay connected
  • Benefits: Nurture leads, referrals, stay top of mind

  • Give first: Build relationships before asking
  • Partnerships: Guest appearances, cross-promotion, joint events
  • Referral network: 10-15 key partners you nurture
  • Community: Build your own for long-term engagement
