Run Your Practice Like a Business

Many practitioners avoid looking at their numbers. Financial clarity is essential for sustainability and growth. This lesson covers the fundamentals of practice finances.

Key Financial Metrics

Numbers to Track Monthly

  • Gross revenue: Total money collected
  • Net profit: Revenue minus all expenses
  • Profit margin: Net profit √∑ revenue (aim for 30-50%)
  • Average client value: Total revenue √∑ number of clients
  • Client acquisition cost: Marketing spend √∑ new clients
  • Monthly recurring revenue (MRR): Predictable monthly income

Profit vs. Revenue

  • Revenue: Money coming in (vanity metric alone)
  • Profit: What you actually keep after expenses
  • $10K/month with $8K expenses = $2K profit
  • Focus on profit margin, not just top-line revenue

Business Banking

Separate Business Finances

  • Dedicated business account: Never mix personal and business
  • Business credit card: For all business expenses
  • Pay yourself: Regular owner's draw or salary
  • Profit First method: Set aside profit before expenses

Profit First Basics

  • Revenue comes in ‚Üí immediately allocate percentages
  • Profit: 5-15% (set aside first)
  • Owner's pay: 35-50%
  • Taxes: 15-30%
  • Operating expenses: What's left

Tracking and Reporting

Financial Systems

  • Bookkeeping software: QuickBooks, Wave, FreshBooks
  • Categorize expenses: Software, marketing, supplies, professional development
  • Monthly review: Review P&L statement each month
  • Hire a bookkeeper: Worth the $200-500/month

Key Reports

  • Profit & Loss (P&L): Revenue minus expenses = profit
  • Cash flow: Money in vs. money out timing
  • Balance sheet: Assets vs. liabilities
  • Accounts receivable: Money owed to you

Tax Planning

Tax Basics for Practitioners

  • Quarterly estimated taxes: Required if self-employed
  • Set aside 25-30%: For taxes (varies by state)
  • Track deductions: Software, training, supplies, home office
  • Business entity: Sole prop, LLC, S-Corp (consult accountant)
  • Hire a tax professional: Worth it for optimization

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Know your numbers: Revenue, profit, margins, CAC
  • Separate business finances from personal
  • Profit First: Set aside profit before expenses
  • Monthly review: Check P&L statement regularly
  • Tax planning: Set aside 25-30%, hire a professional
