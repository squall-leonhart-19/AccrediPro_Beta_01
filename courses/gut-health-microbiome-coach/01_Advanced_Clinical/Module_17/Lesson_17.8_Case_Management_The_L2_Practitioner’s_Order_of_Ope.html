3. Titration and Micro-dosing for Extreme Sensitivity

For the "Universal Reactor," the standard dose on a supplement bottle is often a recipe for disaster. L2 practitioners use Micro-titration. This involves starting at 1/8th or 1/10th of a standard dose.

Mechanistically, this prevents the sudden release of Lipopolysaccharides (LPS) into the bloodstream, which in sensitive individuals can trigger a massive cytokine storm. 4. Exit Strategies: Transitioning to Maintenance

Many practitioners fail by keeping clients on "kill protocols" or restricted diets for too long, leading to microbial extinction and orthorexia. An exit strategy must be planned from Day 1.

The transition from "Repair" to "Maintenance" is triggered by specific clinical markers:

  • Consistently formed stools (Bristol 3-4) for 30 consecutive days.
  • Expansion of diet to include at least 25 different plant types per week.
  • Resolution of systemic "off-target" symptoms (e.g., brain fog, joint pain).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Drainage" phase prioritized before the "Eradication" phase in complex cases?

To ensure the body's elimination pathways (liver, kidneys, bowels, lymph) are open and capable of handling the toxic byproduct (LPS, metabolites) released during the killing of pathogens, thereby preventing a Herxheimer/Die-off reaction.

2. What is the "Red Flag" symptom that mandates immediate medical referral regardless of gut health goals?

Unexplained weight loss, blood in the stool, nocturnal symptoms (waking up to have a bowel movement), or persistent high fever.
