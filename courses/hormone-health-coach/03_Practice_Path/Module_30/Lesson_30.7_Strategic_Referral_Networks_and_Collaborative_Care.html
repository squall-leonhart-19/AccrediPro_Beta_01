2. The Shared Care Model: Communicating with Medical Teams

The biggest barrier between health coaches and medical doctors is the "Language Gap." Doctors speak in terms of ICD-10 codes, clinical markers, and risk mitigation. Coaches often speak in terms of "wellness," "balance," and "vibrancy." To bridge this, you must adopt the Shared Care Model.

The SBAR Communication Protocol

When communicating with a client‚Äôs physician regarding their Optimize Hormones progress, use the clinical SBAR format:

S (Situation): Brief statement of why you are contacting them. "I am the Hormone Health Coach for [Client Name], who is currently under your care for PCOS."
B (Background): Relevant data from your Baseline Assessment. "We have been implementing the B.L.O.O.M. Method‚Ñ¢ for 12 weeks focusing on insulin sensitivity."
A (Assessment): What you are seeing. "Client reports a 50% reduction in cystic acne and a regular 29-day cycle for two consecutive months."
R (Recommendation/Request): "The client is scheduled for follow-up labs with you; would you consider adding fasting insulin to her panel to monitor our progress?"

4. The Complementary Circle: Expanding Your Network

Hormone health is structural, emotional, and physiological. To truly support the Overcome Symptoms phase of the B.L.O.O.M. Method‚Ñ¢, you should have a vetted list of the following practitioners:

  • Pelvic Floor Physical Therapists: Essential for clients with endometriosis, painful intercourse (dyspareunia), or postpartum recovery.

  • Mental Health Professionals: Specifically those trained in PMDD (Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder) or trauma-informed care, as hormonal shifts often trigger underlying psychological distress.

5. Developing a Professional Pitch Deck

When approaching a medical clinic for a partnership, you are not asking for a favor; you are offering a Service Extension. Your pitch deck should be a 5-8 slide PDF that demonstrates your clinical literacy and the value you add to their patient outcomes.

Core Slides for Your Clinical Pitch Deck:

  1. The Problem: The "Time Gap" in modern medicine (Physicians have 15 mins; patients need 60 mins of education).
  2. The Solution: The B.L.O.O.M. Method‚Ñ¢ as a complementary support system.
  3. Scope of Practice: Clearly state that you do not diagnose or treat, but provide lifestyle implementation of their medical advice.
  4. Sample Reporting: Show a redacted client progress chart (BBT, symptom tracking).
  5. Evidence: Include 2-3 brief case studies with data-driven results.

6. Ethics, Legalities, and Scope of Collaboration

As an L4 practitioner, you must navigate the legalities of referrals carefully. In many jurisdictions, "kickbacks" (receiving a fee for referring a patient to a doctor) are illegal and unethical.

Instead, focus on Reciprocal Value. A doctor refers to you because you make their job easier and their patients healthier. You refer to them because you trust their diagnostic skill. This "clean" referral loop protects your professional integrity and ensures the client's best interests remain the priority.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge on strategic networking and collaborative care.

1. What is the primary purpose of using the SBAR protocol when communicating with a client's physician?

The SBAR protocol provides a concise, clinical framework (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) that bridges the communication gap between coaches and medical professionals, ensuring your insights are taken seriously and integrated into the client's medical care.

2. Why is a Pelvic Floor Physical Therapist considered a key partner in a Hormone Health referral network?

Pelvic Floor PTs address the structural and neuromuscular consequences of hormonal issues (like pelvic pain in endometriosis or vaginal atrophy in menopause), which lifestyle and nutrition alone cannot fully resolve.
