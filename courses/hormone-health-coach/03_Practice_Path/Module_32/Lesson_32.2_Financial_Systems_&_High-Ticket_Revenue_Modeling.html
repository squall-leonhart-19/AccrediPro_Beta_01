2. The Economics of Hormone Coaching: CAC & LTV

To scale your practice, you must understand two critical metrics: Client Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV). In the hormone niche, your LTV is exceptionally high because hormonal needs evolve from perimenopause through post-menopause.

Calculating CAC

CAC is the total cost of sales and marketing divided by the number of new clients acquired.
Example: If you spend $1,000 on social media ads and acquire 5 clients, your CAC is $200.

Calculating LTV

LTV is the total revenue a client generates throughout their relationship with you.
Example: A client pays $500 for a Baseline Assessment, $5,000 for a 6-month Transformation, and stays in a $150/month maintenance program for 2 years. Their LTV is $9,100.

The LTV:CAC Ratio

A healthy coaching business should aim for an LTV:CAC ratio of at least 5:1. If your ratio is lower, you are either spending too much to get clients or failing to transition them into the "Maintain & Thrive" (M) phase of the B.L.O.O.M. Methodâ„¢.

3. Lab & Supplement Revenue Models

Hormone health coaching is often "inventory light" but "data heavy." Managing the financial flow of lab testing and supplements is vital for cash flow health.

Wholesale vs. Retail Margins

Practitioners typically have two choices for supplements:

  • Inventory Model: You buy at 50% wholesale and sell at 100% retail. This offers the highest margin but carries the risk of expired stock and capital tie-up.
  • Affiliate/Drop-ship Model: You use platforms like Fullscript or Wellevate. You earn a 25â€“35% commission without touching inventory.

5. Tax Strategies for the Health Entrepreneur

As a certified Hormone Health Coach, your business has unique deductible opportunities. Optimizing these can effectively increase your net income by 15â€“25% annually.

  • Professional Development: Your AccrediPro certification, continuing education (CEUs), and clinical mentorship programs are fully deductible.
  • Diagnostic Tools: Subscriptions to lab portals (e.g., Rupa Health), BBT thermometers used for client demos, and specialized software.
  • Home Office Deduction: If you coach virtually, a percentage of your mortgage/rent, utilities, and internet is deductible based on square footage.
  • Section 179 Deductions: Allows you to deduct the full purchase price of qualifying equipment (computers, high-end cameras for courses) in the year they are purchased.

Compliance Alert

Never "mark up" lab tests in a way that violates state-specific anti-kickback laws or fee-splitting statutes. In many jurisdictions, it is safer to charge a "Lab Interpretation Fee" as part of your professional service rather than profiting directly from the lab kit itself.
