2. Overcoming the "I've Tried Everything" Barrier

Most clients seeking a Hormone Health Coach have already visited multiple doctors, tried "Keto for PCOS," or bought a dozen supplements from Instagram ads. When they say "I've tried everything," they are actually saying, "I've tried many tactics, but I lack a strategy."

This is where you introduce the Optimize (O) phase of your method. You must explain that their previous failures were likely due to poor sequencing. For example, if they tried to "fix" their thyroid without first addressing the Phase I and II Liver Detoxification (which we covered in Module 3), they were pouring water into a leaky bucket. By showing them the B.L.O.O.M. Framework map, you demonstrate why your process is different: it is sequential, biological, and evidence-based.

4. Price Anchoring & ROI in Health

Price anchoring is a cognitive bias where the first piece of information offered (the "anchor") is used to make subsequent judgments. In hormone coaching, your anchor should not be other coaches; it should be the cost of chronic hormonal illness.

Consider these anchors during your presentation:

  • The Medical Anchor: The cost of lifelong HRT, frequent specialist co-pays, and diagnostic imaging.
  • The Productivity Anchor: A 2023 report indicated that women in perimenopause lose an average of $6,500 in productivity annually due to brain fog and fatigue.
  • The Lifestyle Anchor: The cost of "quick fixes," gym memberships they are too tired to use, and expensive supplements that don't work.

When you present a $3,500 package against a $10,000+ annual "cost of dysfunction," your coaching becomes the logical financial choice.

5. Handling Financial Objections

When a client says, "I can't afford it," they are often saying "I don't see the value yet" or "I am afraid to fail again." As an AccrediPro certified coach, you handle this with empathy and logic. Use the "Feel, Felt, Found" technique combined with the B.L.O.O.M. methodology.

"I understand how you feel about the investment. Many of my most successful clients felt the same way, especially after spending so much on previous doctors. However, what they found was that by following the Baseline and Optimize phases, they saved thousands in the long run by avoiding unnecessary testing and ineffective treatments."

Pro Tip: The Spouse Objection

If a client says "I need to talk to my husband/partner," offer to send a "Spouse Transformation Brief." This is a 1-page PDF that outlines the clinical outcomes and ROI of the package, shifting the conversation from "spending money on a coach" to "investing in the family's health foundation."
