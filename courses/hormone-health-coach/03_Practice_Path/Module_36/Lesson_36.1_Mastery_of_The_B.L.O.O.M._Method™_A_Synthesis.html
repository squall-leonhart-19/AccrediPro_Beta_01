The hierarchy of intervention typically follows this sequence:

  • The Adrenals (The Safety Switch): If the HPA axis is in a state of perceived threat, the body will downregulate the thyroid and ovaries to conserve energy. We address cortisol rhythm first.
  • The Thyroid (The Metabolic Engine): Once the body feels "safe," we optimize cellular metabolic rate. Without thyroid efficiency, estrogen detoxification (Phase I and II) cannot occur effectively.
  • The Ovaries (The Reproductive Output): Estrogen and progesterone balance is often the *result* of adrenal and thyroid stability, not just the cause of symptoms.

4. Case Conceptualization: Collaborative Care Protocols

As a Level 4 practitioner, you serve as the Integrative Bridge between the client and their medical team. To be taken seriously by endocrinologists or OBGYNs, your case conceptualization must be data-driven and concise. This is the "B" (Baseline) and "L" (Listen) pillars translated into clinical language.

When presenting a B.L.O.O.M.-aligned protocol to a physician, use the S.B.A.R. Framework (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation):

  • Situation: "Client is experiencing secondary amenorrhea for 4 months."
  • Background: "History of intense caloric restriction and high-intensity exercise; BBT charts show a lack of thermal shift."
  • Assessment: "Symptom mapping suggests Functional Hypothalamic Amenorrhea (FHA) secondary to energy deficiency."
  • Recommendation: "I am supporting the client with nutritional repletion and stress management; requesting a serum panel for LH, FSH, and Estradiol to confirm baseline."
