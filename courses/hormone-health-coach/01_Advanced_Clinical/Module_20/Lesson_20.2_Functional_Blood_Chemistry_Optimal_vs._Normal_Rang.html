A 2022 study found that up to 15% of patients with "normal" TSH levels actually had significantly low Free T3, the active hormone responsible for cellular energy.

3. Metabolic Proxy Markers: Insulin & HOMA-IR

In the Optimize (O) phase, we cannot balance sex hormones without first balancing insulin. Fasting insulin is perhaps the most underrated marker in hormone health. High insulin levels directly stimulate the theca cells in the ovaries to produce excess testosterone, a primary driver of PCOS.

A critical tool for the coach is the HOMA-IR (Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance).
Formula: (Fasting Insulin x Fasting Glucose) / 405.

A HOMA-IR score > 1.9 indicates early insulin resistance, even if HbA1c and Glucose appear "normal." By the time HbA1c rises above 5.7%, significant metabolic damage has already occurred. Research shows that insulin resistance can precede a Type 2 Diabetes diagnosis by up to 10-15 years.

4. The Lipid Paradox: Cholesterol as Hormone Substrate

Modern medicine often views cholesterol solely as a cardiovascular villain. However, for a Hormone Health Coach, cholesterol is the raw material. All steroid hormonesâ€”Pregnenolone, Progesterone, Estrogen, Testosterone, and Cortisolâ€”are synthesized from cholesterol.

When we see Total Cholesterol drop below 150 mg/dL, we must investigate if the client has enough substrate to manufacture hormones. This is common in cases of extreme "clean eating," over-exercising, or over-prescription of statins. Conversely, very high cholesterol can sometimes be a sign of unaddressed hypothyroidism, as thyroid hormone is required to clear LDL from the blood.
