2. Advanced Cycle Mapping Integration

Objective labs tell us the quantity of a hormone at a specific moment. Subjective biofeedback tells us the quality of the hormone's action at the cellular level. To bridge this gap, we utilize Advanced Cycle Mapping.

By merging Basal Body Temperature (BBT), cervical mucus (CM), and Luteinizing Hormone (LH) strips with objective salivary cortisol curves, we can identify "hidden" hormonal gaps. For example, a client may have normal serum progesterone on Day 21, but their BBT chart shows a slow "stair-step" rise after ovulation rather than a sharp shift. This suggests a potential Corpus Luteum insufficiency or receptor-level resistance that a single blood draw would never capture.

Data synthesis also tells us when our current plan isn't working.
