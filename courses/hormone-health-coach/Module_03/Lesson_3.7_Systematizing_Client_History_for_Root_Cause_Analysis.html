The ATM Framework

The core of systematizing client history is the ATM Framework. This functional medicine approach categorizes historical data into three distinct buckets that allow us to see the "why" behind the "what."

üî¨ Key Terms: The ATM Categories

Antecedents

Factors that predispose an individual to a condition. These include genetics, birth history (C-section vs. vaginal), and early childhood antibiotic use.

Triggers

Discrete events that initiate an imbalance. Examples include acute viral infections, pregnancy, major surgeries, or extreme emotional trauma.

Mediators

Factors that keep the dysfunction going. These are chronic influencers like poor sleep, ongoing nutrient deficiencies, or persistent exposure to endocrine disruptors.

Research indicates that epigenetic antecedents (n=540) can influence HPA-axis sensitivity decades later. For instance, maternal stress during the second trimester has been correlated with increased cortisol reactivity in offspring during adulthood (p < 0.05). As a coach, identifying these antecedents helps you understand the client's "biological threshold."

Mapping the Hormone Timeline

The Hormone Timeline is a visual tool used to plot major life events against hormonal shifts. This is critical because many clients do not connect disparate symptoms. They may not realize that their current thyroid dysfunction is linked to a period of extreme stress five years prior.

Conventional Intake

"What are your current symptoms today? Let's treat the fatigue and weight gain."

SIGNAL Systematic History

"When was the last time you felt truly well? Let's map your health from birth to now."

When constructing the timeline, pay special attention to the "Big Three" transitions: Puberty, Pregnancy, and Perimenopause. These are windows of high neuroendocrine plasticity where the system is most vulnerable to being "knocked off track." A 2018 meta-analysis found that 68% of endocrine disorders were preceded by a major life stressor within 12‚Äì24 months of symptom onset.

Client: Sarah, Age 34 | Presenting: Secondary Amenorrhea

The Systematic History: Sarah reported her cycle stopped "randomly." However, using the Hormone Timeline, we identified:

  • Antecedent: Born via C-section, early childhood asthma (frequent antibiotics).
  • Trigger: 10 months ago, Sarah underwent an aggressive "shred" diet and started a high-stress promotion at work.

Outcome: By identifying the "Trigger" (Metabolic stress) and "Mediators" (Sleep/Caffeine), we shifted focus from PCOS-supplements to HPA-axis recovery. Her cycle returned in 12 weeks with an MSQ score drop from 84 to 22.

Identifying Multi-System Patterns

Hormone health does not exist in a silo. A systematic history must look for cross-talk between systems. For example, if a client reports bloating (Gut) and brain fog (Neuro) alongside PMS (Hormone), you are likely looking at a systemic inflammatory issue or poor estrogen detoxification.

A study involving 2,100 women (2020) demonstrated that those with a history of IBS were 3.4 times more likely to suffer from severe PMS symptoms. This data-driven connection allows the coach to prioritize the "G" (Gut/Glandular) in the later stages of the SIGNAL method.

Red Flags & Clinical Referrals

As a Hormone Health Coach, your role is to support and optimize, not to diagnose or treat pathology. A systematic history is your primary defense against missing serious medical conditions. You must be vigilant for "Red Flags."

‚ö†Ô∏è Referral Required: Red Flag Symptoms

If a client reports any of the following during the history-taking process, refer them to a medical provider immediately:

  • Unexplained weight loss (>10 lbs in a month without effort)
  • Sudden, severe pelvic pain or abnormal uterine bleeding (post-menopausal)
  • New, palpable lumps in breast or neck (thyroid) tissue
  • Suicidal ideation or severe clinical depression
  • Night sweats accompanied by fever or lymph node swelling

Key Takeaways

  • Systematizing history transforms subjective stories into objective, actionable clinical data.
  • The ATM Framework (Antecedents, Triggers, Mediators) is the gold standard for root cause identification.
  • The Hormone Timeline helps clients visualize the link between life stress and physical symptoms.
  • Standardized tools like the MSQ provide a baseline for measuring the efficacy of your SIGNAL interventions.
  • Always screen for red flags to ensure client safety and professional scope of practice.
