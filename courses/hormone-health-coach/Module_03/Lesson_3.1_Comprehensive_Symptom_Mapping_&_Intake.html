Clinical Baseline

Symptom Clustering

The time elapsed between the onset of symptoms and the formal diagnosis of a condition (e.g., 7-10 years for endometriosis).

A 2021 study published in the Journal of Endocrine Society noted that nearly 60% of thyroid conditions remain undiagnosed because clinicians rely solely on TSH levels without considering clinical presentation.

Symptom Clustering & Differentiation

Hormonal systems are interconnected. A client presenting with "fatigue" could have low cortisol, low T3, or iron deficiency. To be a master practitioner, you must look at symptom clusters. For example, fatigue paired with "feeling wired but tired" and salt cravings points to the adrenals, while fatigue paired with cold intolerance and thinning eyebrows points to the thyroid.

HPT Axis (Thyroid) Cluster

- Constipation
- Brain fog/Slowed cognition

- Cold hands and feet

HPA Axis (Adrenal) Cluster

- Dizziness upon standing
- Afternoon energy crashes
- Salt/Sugar cravings
- Difficulty falling/staying asleep
- Irritability under stress

The Menstrual Cycle as a Vital Sign

The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) has recommended that the menstrual cycle be treated as the fifth vital sign. For women of reproductive age, the cycle provides a monthly report card on the HPO (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Ovarian) axis. If ovulation is skipped (anovulation), progesterone is not produced, leading to "estrogen dominance" symptoms like heavy periods and breast tenderness.

Your intake must go beyond "When was your last period?" You must ask about cycle length, flow quality (color, clots), and the presence of cervical mucus. A 2019 meta-analysis (n=12,430) found that cycle irregularities in early adulthood are a significant predictor of cardiovascular disease and Type 2 diabetes later in life, highlighting the diagnostic power of this data.

Environmental & Lifestyle Exposure Data

Hormones do not exist in a vacuum. We must survey the "Exposome"â€”the sum of environmental exposures over a lifetime. Endocrine Disrupting Chemicals (EDCs) like phthalates, bisphenols (BPA), and parabens can mimic or block natural hormones. A 2023 review in The Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology estimated that EDC exposure costs the US healthcare system over $340 billion annually due to hormone-related diseases.

Your intake must include an audit of:

  • Personal Care: Fragrances, plastic containers, and "forever chemicals" in makeup.
  • Circadian Hygiene: Blue light exposure after sunset and morning sunlight exposure.
  • Stress Load: Both "macro" stressors (job, divorce) and "micro" stressors (constant notifications, caffeine on an empty stomach).

The SIGNAL Intake Protocol

To "Systematize," you need a consistent workflow. The SIGNAL Intake Protocol uses a tiered approach to data collection:

Tier 1: The Bio-Individual History

Birth history (C-section vs. Vaginal), antibiotic use, and trauma history. These "legacy events" shape the microbiome and HPA-axis set-point.

Tier 2: The Multi-System Symptom Inventory

A 100-point questionnaire covering Digestion, Energy, Mood, Skin, and Reproductive health. Symptoms are rated 0-3 for severity.

Tier 3: Objective Tracking (Wearables/BBT)

Using Oura rings, Whoop, or simple thermometers to track Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and Basal Body Temperature (BBT) as proxies for metabolic health.

Interpreting Subjective Data with Clinical Nuance

As a coach, your job is to translate "I feel tired" into physiological pathways. If a client says they are "tired," you must probe deeper. Is it physical exhaustion (mitochondrial/thyroid) or mental apathy (neurotransmitter/dopamine)?

The SIGNAL Methodâ„¢ teaches you to look for the "Lead Domino." Often, addressing a digestive signal (Survey) reveals that the hormonal signal was secondary to nutrient malabsorption. Systematizing this data prevents the "supplement-chasing" trap where coaches try to fix every symptom with a different pill.
