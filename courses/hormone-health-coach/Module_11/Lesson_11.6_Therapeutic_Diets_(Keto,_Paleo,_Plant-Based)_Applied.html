Ketogenic Diets & Insulin Sensitivity

The Ketogenic Diet (KD) is characterized by very low carbohydrate intake (<50g/day), moderate protein, and high fat. Its primary endocrine benefit is the profound reduction in postprandial insulin secretion. This makes it a powerful tool for conditions driven by hyperinsulinemia, such as Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS).

A 2021 study involving 14 women with PCOS found that a 12-week ketogenic intervention resulted in a 54% reduction in fasting insulin and a significant decrease in the LH/FSH ratio. However, the KD is a "stressor" diet. For clients with low adrenal reserve (HPA-axis dysregulation), the lack of dietary glucose can force the body into chronic gluconeogenesis, further elevating cortisol and potentially suppressing thyroid function (T3 conversion).

Paleo & Ancestral Eating

The Paleo diet focuses on whole foods that would have been available to our hunter-gatherer ancestors: lean meats, fish, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds, while excluding grains, legumes, and dairy. From a hormonal perspective, Paleo is an anti-inflammatory protocol.

By removing common immunogenic triggers (gluten, A1 casein, lectins), Paleo reduces systemic inflammation, which is a primary driver of leptin resistance and thyroid autoimmunity (Hashimoto's). Research published in the Journal of Clinical Medicine (2019) indicated that ancestral diets improved glucose tolerance more effectively than standard "diabetic" diets, even when calorie intake was matched. This diet is often the "Goldilocks" protocol for those who need more carbohydrates than Keto provides but cannot tolerate the inflammatory load of a standard Western diet.

Plant-Based & The Estrobolome

Plant-based protocols (Vegan/Vegetarian) are exceptionally high in fiber and phytonutrients. In the context of hormone health, fiber is the "janitor" of the endocrine system. It binds to conjugated estrogens in the digestive tract, preventing their reabsorption via the enterohepatic circulation.

A high-fiber plant-based diet increases levels of Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin (SHBG). SHBG acts as a "sponge" for excess sex hormones; when SHBG is high, "free" (active) estrogen and testosterone levels are lower. This makes plant-based diets particularly effective for Estrogen Dominance and high-androgen states. However, practitioners must watch for deficiencies in B12, Zinc, and Omega-3 (DHA/EPA), which are critical for hormone synthesis and neurological health.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a strict ketogenic diet be contraindicated for a client with advanced HPA-axis dysregulation (adrenal fatigue)?

Ketogenic diets require the body to produce its own glucose via gluconeogenesis, a process driven by cortisol. In a client with low adrenal reserve, this creates an unsustainable demand on the HPA axis, potentially worsening fatigue and suppressing thyroid function.

2. What is the primary mechanism by which a high-fiber plant-based diet helps resolve estrogen dominance?

Fiber binds to conjugated estrogens in the gut, ensuring they are excreted in the stool rather than being reabsorbed into the bloodstream via the enterohepatic circulation. It also supports a healthy estrobolome.
