Scope of Practice

The specific procedures, actions, and processes that a healthcare practitioner is permitted to undertake in keeping with the terms of their professional certification.

Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)

A clinical approach to treating medical conditions via diet, strictly reserved for Registered Dietitians (RDs) and licensed physicians.

Informed Consent

The process of providing a client with all necessary information about an intervention so they can make an autonomous decision to proceed.

Diagnostic Mimicry

The act of using non-clinical language to describe what is effectively a medical diagnosis (e.g., "You have Adrenal Fatigue" instead of "Your HPA-axis data suggests dysregulation").

Coaching vs. Clinical Practice

The SIGNAL Method‚Ñ¢ is designed to be a bridge between subjective symptoms and physiological systems. However, we must be careful how we present these findings. Below is a critical comparison of how a Coach operates versus a Licensed Clinician.

Activity Hormone Health Coach (SIGNAL) Licensed Medical Provider (MD/ND)
Lab Interpretation Reviews labs to identify "functional ranges" and metabolic trends for education. Orders and interprets labs to diagnose disease and pathology.
Nutrition Provides bio-individual protocols based on nutrient density and blood sugar stability. Prescribes Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT) for disease treatment.
Supplements Suggests targeted botanicals and micronutrients to support glandular function. Prescribes pharmaceutical drugs or high-dose therapeutic supplements.
Outcome Goal Optimization of physiological systems and lifestyle longevity. Resolution of pathology, symptom management, and acute care.
Critical Warning

Never tell a client to stop taking a prescribed medication. If you believe a medication is interfering with their hormonal goals (e.g., hormonal birth control or thyroid meds), your only ethical path is to provide the client with research to discuss with their prescribing physician.

Informed Consent & Data Security

In the digital age, "Survey & Systematize" (the S in SIGNAL) involves collecting sensitive health data. Ethically, you are responsible for the protection of this data. A 2023 study published in the Journal of Medical Internet Research highlighted that health coaches are often the "weakest link" in the data privacy chain (n=450).

Essential Ethics Checklist:

  • HIPAA Compliance: Even if not legally "covered," following HIPAA standards (encrypted email, secure portals) builds professional trust.
  • The "Why": Always explain why you are asking for specific data (e.g., "We track your basal body temperature to identify the metabolic 'signal' of your luteal phase").
  • Right to Withdraw: Clients must know they can stop the protocol or delete their data at any time.

Professional Boundaries

Hormone health is deeply personal. Clients may share stories of infertility, miscarriage, or sexual dysfunction. While empathy is vital, emotional enmeshment is a risk. You are a coach, not a therapist or a best friend. Maintaining a "professional distance" allows you to remain objective in your analysis of their SIGNAL data.

A client asks you to interpret their blood work because their doctor said it was "normal," but they still feel terrible. What is the most ethical response?

"I can review these results with you from a functional perspective to identify where your levels fall within 'optimal' ranges for hormone health, rather than just the 'absence of disease' ranges. However, this is for educational purposes and not a medical diagnosis."

Adjusting a client's dose of a prescribed thyroid medication because their symptoms have improved. This is a direct violation of scope and highly dangerous.

Key Takeaways

  • Language Matters: We "support" and "optimize" systems; we do not "treat" or "cure" diseases.
  • S.I.G.N.A.L. is Educational: Every assessment tool used must be framed as a way to gather metabolic data, not diagnostic proof.
  • Referral is Power: Knowing when to refer out protects the client and elevates your professional standing.
  • Data is Sacred: Maintain high standards of privacy and security for all client hormonal data.
  • Boundaries Prevent Burnout: Keep coaching sessions focused on the SIGNAL framework and actionable lifestyle changes.
