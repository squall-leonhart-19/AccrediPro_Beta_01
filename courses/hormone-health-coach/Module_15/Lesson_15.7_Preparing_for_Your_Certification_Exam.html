The SIGNAL Method‚Ñ¢ Synthesis

The backbone of your exam‚Äîand your future practice‚Äîis the SIGNAL Method‚Ñ¢. You must be able to identify which phase of the method is most appropriate for a client's current status. Review these core pillars:

  • S - Survey & Systematize: Establishing the baseline. Expect questions on intake forms and identifying "red flags."
  • I - Identify Root Dysfunctions: Analyzing the "why." Is it HPA-axis dysregulation, gut dysbiosis, or nutrient depletion?
  • G - Glandular Support: Targeted nourishment. Know which nutrients support specific glands (e.g., Selenium for Thyroid, Magnesium for Adrenals).
  • N - Nutritional Precision: Blood sugar and building blocks. Focus on the role of lipids in steroid hormone synthesis.
  • A - Alignment of Rhythms: Circadian and infradian health. Understanding the cortisol-melatonin relationship.
  • L - Lifestyle Longevity: Stress management and detoxification. Addressing environmental endocrine disruptors.

Final Strategy & Mindset

Preparation is as much about mindset as it is about mechanics. When you open the exam portal, remember these three strategies:

  1. Read the Full Prompt: Some questions include "distractor" symptoms. Focus on the primary complaint and the biomarker data provided.
  2. The "Root Cause" Rule: If two answers seem plausible, choose the one that addresses the most foundational issue (e.g., addressing blood sugar before addressing specific sex hormone levels).
  3. Trust the SIGNAL: Use the acronym to mentally check off if you've considered all aspects of the client's presentation.
