HRV: The Window into HPA-Axis Resilience

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the gold standard for assessing the Autonomic Nervous System (ANS). In the Overcome (O) phase, we use HRV to detect burnout before the client "feels" it. A high HRV generally indicates a resilient, parasympathetic-dominant state, while a consistently low HRV suggests sympathetic overdrive and potential HPA-axis dysregulation.

The Ethics of Bio-Data: Preventing Data-Obsession

While data is powerful, it can be a double-edged sword. For some clients, seeing a "poor" sleep score or a glucose spike can trigger orthorexia nervosa or "Data-Induced Stress" (DIS). This paradoxically raises cortisol, further dysregulating the hormones we are trying to balance.

Master Coach Protocol:
1. The 80/20 Rule: Data should inform, not dictate. If the data says "rest" but the client feels "great," teach them to prioritize interoception.
2. Data Sabbaticals: Periodically have clients remove wearables for 3-7 days to reconnect with their internal cues (the Listen phase).
3. Contextualization: Always ask, "What was happening in your life when this data point occurred?" A glucose spike during a stressful meeting is a cortisol response, not a carbohydrate failure.
