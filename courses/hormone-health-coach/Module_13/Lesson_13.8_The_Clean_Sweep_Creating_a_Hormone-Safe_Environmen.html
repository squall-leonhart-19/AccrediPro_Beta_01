2. Personal Care Clean Sweep: Prioritizing Swaps

The skin is the body's largest organ and a primary route of entry for EDCs. Unlike the digestive tract, which has the "first-pass metabolism" of the liver, substances absorbed through the skin enter the bloodstream directly. The average woman applies 12 products containing 168 unique ingredients daily.

The "Big Three" to Evict:

  • Phthalates: Often hidden under the term "Fragrance" or "Parfum." These are known anti-androgens and are linked to insulin resistance and obesity.
  • Parabens: Synthetic preservatives (methyl-, ethyl-, propyl-, butylparaben) that mimic estrogen by binding to estrogen receptors (ERÎ± and ERÎ²).
  • Oxybenzone: A common chemical UV filter in sunscreens that significantly disrupts thyroid function and mimics estrogen.

4. Water Filtration Science: Comparing Systems

Tap water is a significant source of EDCs, including atrazine (a potent herbicide), chlorine, fluoride, and "forever chemicals" (PFAS). When coaching clients on the Baseline Assessment phase of the B.L.O.O.M. Methodâ„¢, evaluating their water source is non-negotiable.

Filtration Technology Breakdown:

1. Carbon Filtration (Pitchers/Fridge): Effective for chlorine and some VOCs, but largely ineffective for fluoride, heavy metals, or PFAS. Efficiency: 40-60% EDC removal.

2. Reverse Osmosis (RO): The gold standard for purity. Uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove up to 99% of contaminants. Note: Must be remineralized to prevent electrolyte depletion.

3. Gravity-Fed Systems (e.g., Berkey): Utilize specialized elements to remove viruses, bacteria, and EDCs without electricity. Highly effective if maintained correctly.
