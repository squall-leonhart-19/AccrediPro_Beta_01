The Rationale for Thyroid Glandulars

The thyroid gland does not merely produce Thyroxine (T4). In a healthy state, it produces a spectrum of iodinated amino acids and proteins. Synthetic Levothyroxine (T4) assumes that the body will perfectly convert T4 into T3 in peripheral tissues. However, data suggests that up to 15-20% of patients do not experience symptomatic relief on T4-only therapy due to genetic polymorphisms (like the DIO2 gene) or systemic inflammation.

Thyroid glandulars, specifically Desiccated Thyroid Extract (DTE), provide a biological match to human thyroid output. Beyond T4 and T3, glandulars contain 3,5-diiodothyronine (T2), which has been shown in recent studies to stimulate hepatic fatty acid oxidation and increase resting metabolic rate without the cardiac side effects often associated with T3 excess.

The Iodine Paradox: Support vs. Suppression

Iodine is the central atom of thyroid hormone. Without it, the gland cannot function. However, iodine follows a U-shaped dose-response curve. Both deficiency and excess can trigger thyroid dysfunction. The Wolff-Chaikoff Effect is a physiological phenomenon where a large bolus of iodine (usually >500-1000mcg) causes the thyroid to temporarily shut down hormone production to prevent thyrotoxicosis.

In clients with autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto's), high-dose iodine can be like "pouring gasoline on a fire." Iodine stimulates the enzyme Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO). If there is insufficient selenium to neutralize the hydrogen peroxide produced during this process, oxidative damage occurs, increasing TPO antibodies and tissue destruction.

The Selenium-Iodine Synergy

You cannot discuss iodine without Selenium. Selenium is the cofactor for the glutathione peroxidase enzymes that protect the thyroid from oxidative stress. It is also a critical component of the deiodinase enzymes that convert T4 to T3.

Research indicates that supplementing with selenium (typically 200mcg/day) can reduce TPO antibodies by an average of 30-50% over six months. When implementing the "G" (Glandular Support) in the SIGNAL Methodâ„¢, selenium should almost always precede or accompany iodine introduction to ensure "oxidative fireproofing" of the gland.

Practitioner Safety & Monitoring

When a client is using thyroid glandulars or therapeutic iodine, monitoring is mandatory. Because DTE contains active T3, the TSH will often "suppress" (drop below the lab range). This is not necessarily "hyperthyroidism" if the Free T3 remains within the upper third of the optimal range and the client is asymptomatic.

Red Flags for Glandular Over-Support

If a client experiences resting tachycardia (pulse >90 bpm), unexplained anxiety, night sweats, or fine tremors, the glandular dose may be too high or the iodine is over-stimulating the gland. Immediate reduction and re-evaluation of the "SIGNAL" are required.
