Biological Triage

The body's innate prioritization of survival-based functions (like blood sugar and electrolyte balance) over reproductive or metabolic speed functions.

Upstream Driver

Metabolic Reserve

The capacity of an organism to respond effectively to stressors; low reserve requires slower, more foundational intervention sequencing.

The SIGNAL Decision Matrix

How do you decide if a client is ready to move from "Identify Root Dysfunctions" to "Glandular Support"? Use this 3-point checklist:

1. The "Safety" Check

Is the client sleeping 7+ hours and having daily bowel movements? If not, the body is in "survival mode" and will resist glandular stimulation.

2. The "Glycemic" Check

Are energy levels stable between meals? If the client gets "hangry" or needs caffeine to function, you must stay in the Nutritional Precision phase.

3. The "Inflammatory" Check

Is there active "fire" (High Histamine, Mast Cell flares, or acute Gut pain)? You must extinguish the fire before trying to rebuild the hormone structure.

Building the 90-Day Roadmap

When presenting the roadmap to a client, clarity is key for compliance. Use the following structure to set expectations:

  • Days 1-30: Extinguish & Stabilize. Focus on Gut (Elimination/Support) and Blood Sugar. Goal: Reduce systemic inflammation and stabilize cortisol.
  • Days 31-60: Nourish & Support. Begin Glandular Support (Adrenals/Thyroid). Goal: Increase metabolic rate and resilience.
  • Days 61-90: Refine & Optimize. Address Sex Hormones and Environmental Detoxification. Goal: Cycle regularity, libido, and long-term protection.
