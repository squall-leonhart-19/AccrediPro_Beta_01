2. Designing 'Neurodiversity at Work' Programs

An enterprise-level program is more than a one-off workshop. It is a multi-phase implementation that aligns with the companyâ€™s ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) goals. A premium program typically follows the AccrediPro "Systemic Inclusion" Framework:

Phase 1: Discovery & Cultural Baseline

Utilizing anonymous surveys and focus groups to identify the "Invisible Workforce"â€”neurodivergent employees already present but masking. Statistics show that up to 15-20% of a workforce is neurodivergent, yet only 3% typically disclose to HR.

Phase 2: Management Training & "The Double Empathy" Workshop

Training middle management is the highest leverage point. You will teach them to bridge the "Double Empathy Problem" in performance reviews and daily communication, moving from "vague expectations" to "explicit directives."

Phase 3: Talent Pipeline Optimization

Redesigning recruitment. This includes replacing open-ended "behavioral" interviews with work-sample tests and providing interview questions 48 hours in advance to mitigate executive functioning barriers.

3. Conducting Neuro-Inclusive Audits

A core high-ticket deliverable is the Inclusion Audit. Corporations will pay a premium for a Specialist to walk through their physical and digital environments to identify "friction points" that cause sensory overload or cognitive fatigue.

Physical Environment Audit

Using a Decibel Meter and Lux Meter, you assess:

  • Acoustics: Identification of high-frequency hums (HVAC systems) that trigger sensory processing distress.
  • Lighting: Measuring flicker rates of LED/Fluorescent bulbs and recommending "warm zones."
  • Wayfinding: Assessing if office navigation relies on intuitive social cues or clear, high-contrast signage.

Digital Workflow Audit

5. Developing Executive-Level Reporting

To maintain high-ticket retainers, you must speak the language of the C-Suite. Your reports should avoid clinical jargon and focus on Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).

A 2022 study published in the Journal of Business and Psychology (n=1,200) found that teams with neurodivergent members were 20% faster at problem-solving, but only when "Psychological Safety" scores were high. Your report should measure:

  • Retention Delta: The difference in turnover rates before and after program implementation.
  • Engagement Scores: Utilizing Likert-scale data from neurodivergent vs. neurotypical employee cohorts.
  • Accommodation Costs: Demonstrating that 58% of neuro-inclusive accommodations cost $0, while the average one-time cost is only $500 (Job Accommodation Network data).

6. Navigating the Corporate Sales Cycle

Selling to a corporation is different from selling to a parent or individual. It involves multiple "Buyers":

  • The Champion: Often an HR Manager or an ERG (Employee Resource Group) lead who loves your mission but lacks budget authority.
  • The Economic Buyer: The VP of HR or COO who cares about the bottom line.
  • Procurement: The department that will vet your insurance, certifications (like your AccrediPro credential), and tax status.

Pro-Tip: The Procurement Bridge

Always have a "Professional Services Capability Statement" ready. This is a 1-2 page PDF outlining your insurance (Errors & Omissions), your methodology, and your past performance data. Enterprise clients will not hire you without proof of professional liability coverage.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of B2B scaling strategies.

1. Why is middle management training considered the "highest leverage point" in corporate consulting?

Middle managers are the primary interface for neurodivergent employees. They control daily communication, performance reviews, and task assignments. If they do not understand the Double Empathy Problem or sensory triggers, systemic inclusion fails regardless of C-Suite support.

2. What is the primary advantage of a value-based pricing model over hourly billing for enterprise clients?

Value-based pricing aligns your incentives with the client's results (e.g., reducing turnover or increasing productivity). It allows for much higher profit margins and positions you as a strategic partner rather than a commodity service provider.
