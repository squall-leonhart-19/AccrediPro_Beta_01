1. The Longevity Supplement Hierarchy

In the previous lesson, we discussed how nutritional programming sets the metabolic stage via mTOR and AMPK cycling. However, even the most optimized diet often requires targeted exogenous support to reach peak longevity biomarkers. As an AccrediPro Longevity Coach, your role is to guide clients through the Longevity Supplement Hierarchy, ensuring they don't spend thousands on advanced NAD+ boosters while remaining deficient in foundational micronutrients.

The hierarchy is structured to ensure physiological stability before metabolic enhancement:

2. NAD+ Restoration & Sirtuin Activation

Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide (NAD+) is the "currency" of cellular repair. Levels decline by roughly 50% every 20 years of life. Advanced stacking often centers on the "Sinclair Stack"‚Äîcombining an NAD+ precursor with a sirtuin activator.

NMN vs. NR: The Precursor Debate

While both Nicotinamide Mononucleotide (NMN) and Nicotinamide Riboside (NR) effectively raise systemic NAD+, NMN has shown superior results in improving skeletal muscle insulin sensitivity and aerobic capacity in clinical trials. A 2023 study (n=80) demonstrated that 900mg of NMN daily significantly improved walking distance and biological age scores compared to placebo.

Sirtuin Activators: Resveratrol and Pterostilbene

Sirtuins (SIRT1-7) are longevity genes that repair DNA and protect the epigenome. They require NAD+ to function. Stacking NMN with Trans-Resveratrol (mornings) or Pterostilbene provides the "gas and the spark" for cellular longevity. Note: Resveratrol must be taken with a fat source (e.g., full-fat yogurt or olive oil) to increase its notoriously low bioavailability.

3. Senolytic Sequencing: The "Hit and Run" Protocol

Senescent cells, or "zombie cells," stop dividing but refuse to die, secreting the SASP (Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype)‚Äîa cocktail of pro-inflammatory cytokines that accelerates aging in neighboring cells. Unlike daily supplements, senolytics are used in a "pulse" or "intermittent" fashion.

The Fisetin "Mayo Clinic" Protocol

The most popular evidence-based protocol involves high-dose Fisetin. Fisetin is a flavonoid that selectively induces apoptosis (programmed cell death) in senescent cells. A common protocol currently under clinical investigation involves:

  • Dosage: 20mg per kg of body weight (approx. 1,400mg - 2,000mg for most adults).
  • Frequency: 2 consecutive days, once per month, or for 3 months per year.

4. Biohacking Integration: Layering Tech with Chemistry

Supplements work biochemically, but biophysical interventions can amplify their effects. When developing a program, schedule these modalities to coincide with the supplement's peak action.

Red Light Therapy (Photobiomodulation)

Red and Near-Infrared light (660nm/850nm) stimulate Cytochrome c Oxidase in the mitochondria, increasing ATP production. Stacking Tip: Use Red Light Therapy 30-60 minutes after taking CoQ10 or PQQ to maximize mitochondrial output.

Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy (HBOT)

A landmark 2020 study by Hacham et al. showed that HBOT could increase telomere length by up to 20% and reduce senescent cells by 10-37%. Stacking Tip: Do not use high-dose antioxidants (like Vitamin C or E) immediately before HBOT, as the therapy relies on a controlled "oxidative burst" to trigger repair mechanisms.

6. Safety, Polypharmacy, and Contraindications

As a coach, you must respect the boundary between wellness and medicine. Many longevity supplements interact with common "longevity-adjacent" pharmaceuticals.

Critical Safety Warning

Berberine & Metformin: Both activate AMPK. Combining high doses can lead to hypoglycemia or gastrointestinal distress. Always require medical clearance before a client stacks these.

Omega-3s & Blood Thinners: High-dose fish oil (>3g/day) has mild anticoagulant effects. Clients on Warfarin or Eliquis must be monitored for bruising or bleeding risks.

Furthermore, the Antioxidant Paradox must be managed. High doses of Vitamin C and E can blunt the beneficial hormetic stress of exercise (mitohormesis). Advise clients to avoid these antioxidants within a 4-hour window of resistance training.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of advanced longevity stacking.

1. Why is Fisetin typically taken in a "pulse" protocol rather than daily?

Senolytics target the clearance of senescent cells. Continuous use may interfere with normal cellular signaling and the beneficial roles of temporary senescence (e.g., wound healing). The "hit and run" approach allows for clearance followed by a period of tissue regeneration.

2. A client wants to take NMN, Vitamin D, and Magnesium. What is the optimal timing for each?

NMN in the morning (empty stomach) for circadian alignment; Vitamin D in the morning (with fat) to avoid melatonin suppression; Magnesium in the evening to support sleep architecture.

Key Takeaways

  • Follow the Hierarchy: Never build a Tier 4 (Senolytic) protocol on a Tier 1 (Foundational) deficiency.
  • NMN/NR Efficacy: NMN is often preferred for metabolic and muscle health, while NR is well-studied for systemic NAD+ elevation.
  • The Pulse Method: Senolytics like Fisetin should be cycled (e.g., 2 days on, 28 days off) to avoid interfering with acute physiological repair.
  • Bio-Synergy: Combine biochemical interventions (CoQ10) with biophysical ones (Red Light) for amplified results.
  • Safety First: Always screen for drug-supplement interactions, particularly with AMPK activators and anticoagulants.
