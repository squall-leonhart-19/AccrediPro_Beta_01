3. The Quarterly Longevity Audit (QLA) Framework

Static protocols are the enemy of longevity. Biological systems are dynamic, and adaptation occurs rapidly. The Quarterly Longevity Audit is a systematic 90-day review process designed to pivot the client‚Äôs strategy based on objective data.

Component 1: Biomarker Delta Analysis

Compare current bloodwork (HbA1c, hs-CRP, IGF-1, Cystatin C) against the previous quarter. Identify "drifts" before they become pathologies.

Component 2: Functional Capacity Testing

Assessment of grip strength, VO2 Max estimates, and balance. As discussed in Module 3, these are the strongest predictors of all-cause mortality.

Component 3: Lifestyle Friction Audit

Identifying "protocol fatigue." If a HNW client is traveling 50% of the time, a complex 12-supplement morning routine will fail. We simplify to ensure 100% adherence to the "Critical Few" interventions.

Check Your Understanding

Mastery Level Questions

1. According to the Longevity Intervention Hierarchy, which level does "Metformin for non-diabetics" fall into, and what is the Practitioner's role in this recommendation?

It falls into Level 3 (Pharmacological/Off-label). The Practitioner's role is NOT to prescribe or recommend, but to provide the client with peer-reviewed data to share with their medical doctor for clinical oversight.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Lifestyle Friction Audit" within the Quarterly Longevity Audit (QLA)?

The primary purpose is to identify barriers to adherence. Even the most scientifically perfect protocol is useless if the client's lifestyle (travel, stress, schedule) prevents them from following it. It ensures the protocol remains sustainable.
