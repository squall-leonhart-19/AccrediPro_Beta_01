1. The Peptide Revolution: Beyond Traditional Supplementation

In previous lessons, we explored senolytics and mitochondrial bioenergetics. While those interventions focus on clearing damage or fueling the cell, Peptide Therapy represents the next frontier: Regenerative Signaling. Peptides are short chains of amino acids (typically 2-50) that act as precise signaling molecules, instructing cells to perform specific functions like protein synthesis, immune modulation, or tissue repair.

Unlike traditional drugs that often force a biochemical pathway (antagonists or agonists), peptides often act as biomodulators. They provide the "software update" the body needs to return to homeostasis. As we age, the production of these endogenous signaling molecules declines, leading to the systemic "noise" and inefficiency we recognize as aging.

BPC-157 (Body Protection Compound)

Derived from human gastric juice, BPC-157 is a pentadecapeptide known for its "stable" nature. Its primary mechanism involves the upregulation of growth factor receptors (specifically VEGFR2) and the promotion of angiogenesis. A 2021 review highlighted its ability to accelerate the healing of transected rat Achilles tendons, showing significant biomechanical recovery compared to controls.

GHK-Cu (Copper Peptide)

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper complex first identified in human plasma. It is a powerful gene modulator, capable of up-regulating and down-regulating over 4,000 human genes. In longevity, it is prized for its ability to reset genes to a "younger" state, specifically increasing collagen, elastin, and glycosaminoglycan synthesis while boosting antioxidant enzyme levels (SOD).

5. Mitochondrial Signaling: MOTS-c (The Exercise Mimetic)

In Module 3, we discussed the importance of mitochondrial biogenesis. MOTS-c (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA Type-c) is a 16-amino acid peptide encoded within the mitochondrial genome itselfâ€”not the nucleus.

MOTS-c acts as a metabolic hormone. When the cell experiences metabolic stress (like exercise), MOTS-c translocates to the nucleus to regulate gene expression. It has been shown to:

  • Increase GLUT4 translocation, improving insulin sensitivity.

A 2021 study published in Nature Communications found that MOTS-c treatment in mice significantly improved physical performance and "stayed" the decline of muscle function during aging, even when the treatment was started late in life.
