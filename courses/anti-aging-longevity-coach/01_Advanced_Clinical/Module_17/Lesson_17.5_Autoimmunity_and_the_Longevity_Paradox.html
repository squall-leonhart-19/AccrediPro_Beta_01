Sauna and the Heat Shock Response

While Heat Shock Proteins (HSPs) are vital for protein folding and longevity, excessive heat can trigger a flare in Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) or Multiple Sclerosis (MS). Protocol Modification: Start with "Infrared" sauna (lower ambient temp) for 10-15 minutes, ensuring electrolyte repletion. Monitor the heart rate variability (HRV) the following morning; a drop of >10% suggests the stressor was too aggressive.

5. Anti-Inflammatory 'E' (Eat) Protocols: Beyond the Basics

In Module 5, we focused on mTOR and AMPK. For complex autoimmune clients, we must add a layer of "Immunonutrition." This involves identifying Antinutrients that may be benign for the general population but catastrophic for the autoimmune-compromised.

The Three Primary Triggers:

  • Lectins: Proteins found in legumes and nightshades that can bind to the gut wall and increase permeability. A 2020 meta-analysis found that lectin-restricted diets significantly improved symptoms in 62% of RA patients.
  • Nightshades: (Tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, eggplant). These contain alkaloids like solanine which can aggravate joint pain in sensitive individuals.
  • Oxalates: Found in "superfoods" like spinach and almonds. In clients with poor gut health, oxalates can form crystals in tissues, mimicking the pain of fibromyalgia or RA.

Practitioner Note

Always prioritize Nutrient Density over Restriction. When removing a trigger (like spinach), ensure you replace it with a safe alternative (like arugula or bok choy) to maintain the phytonutrient diversity required for the longevity microbiome.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a 72-hour fast be contraindicated for a client in an active Hashimoto's flare?

Extended fasting significantly raises cortisol and can suppress T3 (active thyroid hormone) production. In an active flare, this additional metabolic stress can exacerbate the "wired but tired" state and further dysregulate the HPA axis.

A high NLR indicates systemic inflammation and a shift toward innate immune dominance over adaptive immunity. This is a hallmark of "inflammaging" and suggests the client is at higher risk for flares and accelerated biological aging.
