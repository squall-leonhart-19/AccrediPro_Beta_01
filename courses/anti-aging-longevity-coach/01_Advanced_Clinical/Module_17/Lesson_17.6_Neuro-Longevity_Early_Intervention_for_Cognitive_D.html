2. Glymphatic Health: The Brain's Nightly Car Wash

The glymphatic system is a macroscopic waste clearance system that utilizes perivascular channels to eliminate metabolic byproducts from the central nervous system. This "car wash" is almost exclusively active during deep, slow-wave sleep (NREM Stage 3).

4. BDNF: Leveraging Exercise for Hippocampal Health

In the 'E' (Exercise) pillar, our primary target for neuro-longevity is Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF). BDNF is often called "Miracle-Gro for the brain" because it supports the survival of existing neurons and encourages the growth of new ones (neurogenesis) in the hippocampus.

A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=2,145) found that 60-90 minutes of moderate-to-vigorous physical activity per week increased serum BDNF by an average of 15.4%. However, the intensity matters. Brief bouts of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) appear to produce the greatest transient spikes in BDNF, while Zone 2 aerobic exercise supports the vascular health necessary to deliver that BDNF to the brain.
