1. The Cortisol-DHEA Drain: Biological Erosion

In the high-stakes world of executive leadership, stress is often viewed as a badge of honor. However, from a longevity perspective, chronic HPA-axis (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal) overactivation is a primary driver of accelerated biological aging. While acute stress can be hormetic, the executive environment typically presents as "chronic-unrelenting" stress.

A 2021 study published in Psychoneuroendocrinology demonstrated that individuals with a chronically low DHEA-S to Cortisol ratio exhibited significantly shorter telomeres and higher epigenetic age. This phenomenon, often termed the "Cortisol-DHEA drain," occurs when the body prioritizes the production of cortisol (the survival hormone) over DHEA (the repair and longevity hormone).

5. Biomarker Tracking for the 'A' Pillar: HRV & Core Temp
