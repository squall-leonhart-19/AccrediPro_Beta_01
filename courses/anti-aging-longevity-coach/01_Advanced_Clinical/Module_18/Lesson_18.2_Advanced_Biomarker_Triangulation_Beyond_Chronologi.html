CGM Data: Refining the 'Eat for Longevity' Pillar

While HbA1c provides a 90-day average, it masks Glycemic Variability (GV)â€”the "spikes and crashes" that drive oxidative stress and Advanced Glycation End-products (AGEs). In advanced triangulation, we use Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM) data to refine the nutritional protocol.

A meta-analysis of 12 studies (n=4,500) found that individuals with a Standard Deviation (SD) in glucose > 20 mg/dL had a significantly higher risk of cardiovascular events, even if their HbA1c was "normal" (under 5.7%).

Key CGM Triangulation Points:

  • Time in Range (70-110 mg/dL): Aim for >90% for longevity optimization.
  • Post-Prandial Peak: Should stay below 140 mg/dL, returning to baseline within 2 hours.
  • The Sleep-Glucose Link: High nocturnal glucose often triangulates with low Deep Sleep scores on wearables, indicating late-night eating or high cortisol.
