2. Customizing for Phenotypes

A "one-size-fits-all" approach is the antithesis of longevity science. We primarily categorize clients into two dominant phenotypes that require divergent roadmap priorities: the Stressed Executive and the Aging Athlete.

The Stressed Executive

This phenotype is characterized by high cortisol, disrupted sleep architecture (low REM/Deep sleep), and "inflammaging" driven by chronic HPA-axis activation. Their roadmap prioritizes the Lifestyle (L) and Gut (G) pillars early on.

  • Priority 1: Circadian Entrainment (Blue light blocking, morning sunlight).
  • Priority 2: Vagal Tone (Breathwork, HRV monitoring).
  • Priority 3: Glycemic Stability (Preventing stress-induced hyperglycemia).

The Aging Athlete

This phenotype prioritizes the Exercise (E) and Eat (E) pillars. Their primary risks are sarcopenia (muscle loss) and oxidative stress from overtraining. Their roadmap focuses on mTOR/AMPK cycling‚Äîensuring enough protein for muscle synthesis while utilizing periodized fasting for cellular cleanup.

4. The Annual Periodization Model

Longevity is not a linear progression; it is a series of cyclical phases. We utilize Annual Periodization to prevent cellular adaptation and ensure the body is exposed to both anabolic (growth) and catabolic (cleanup) signals.

A meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) suggests that intermittent metabolic switching‚Äîmoving between glucose and ketone utilization‚Äîis superior for metabolic flexibility and brain health compared to chronic caloric restriction.

The Quarterly Roadmap:

  • Q1: Foundation & Gut Reset. Focus on microbiome diversity and eliminating processed "longevity disruptors."
  • Q2: Strength & Anabolic Drive. Focus on hypertrophy (Resistance Training) and high protein intake (1.6g/kg+).
  • Q3: Metabolic Flexibility & Sprints. Introduction of FMD (Fasting Mimicking Diet) and Zone 2 cardiovascular peaks.
  • Q4: Cellular Cleanup & Recovery. Senolytic protocols, focused parasympathetic work, and deep tissue repair.

5. Longevity Sprints: FMD & Senolytics

Within the 12-month roadmap, we insert "Longevity Sprints." These are high-intensity, short-duration interventions designed to trigger significant epigenetic shifts.

The Fasting Mimicking Diet (FMD) Sprint: Based on the work of Dr. Valter Longo, a 5-day FMD performed 3-4 times per year has been shown to reduce IGF-1, lower systemic inflammation (CRP), and stimulate stem cell-based regeneration. In our roadmap, these are typically scheduled at the change of seasons.

The Senolytic Sprint: For clients over 40, clearing "zombie cells" is crucial. A "hit and run" protocol (e.g., Fisetin for 2 days) can be integrated into the Q4 roadmap. Research indicates that 90% of senescent cells in specific tissues can be cleared with targeted protocols, but these must be spaced at least 3-4 months apart to avoid interfering with normal wound healing.

6. Risk Stratification & Red Flags

As an expert coach, you must know when to de-escalate. Not every client is ready for advanced biohacking. We monitor specific "Red Flags" that indicate the roadmap needs immediate adjustment.

Biomarker/Symptom The "Red Flag" Required Action
HRV (Heart Rate Variability) >20% drop from baseline for 7+ days. Reduce exercise intensity; increase sleep; pause fasting.
HbA1c / Glucose Rising HbA1c despite low-carb diet. Assess stress/cortisol; check for over-training.
Liver Enzymes (ALT/AST) Elevated after starting new supplements. Immediate pause on the Supplement (S) pillar; re-test in 2 weeks.
Subjective Energy "Wired but tired" sensation. HPA-axis burnout; pause NAD+ boosters and caffeine.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of bespoke program design.

Why is the "Stressed Executive" phenotype generally advised against aggressive prolonged fasting in the first 3 months of a roadmap?

Aggressive fasting is a hormetic stressor that can further elevate cortisol levels in a client already suffering from chronic HPA-axis activation. This can lead to muscle wasting, worsened sleep, and "stress-induced hyperglycemia," counteracting the longevity benefits.

According to the A.G.E.L.E.S.S. Protocol‚Ñ¢, when should the 'Supplements & Biohacks' (S) pillar be fully integrated?

Ideally in the "Optimization Phase" (Months 9‚Äì12), after the Foundational (G, L, E) and Targeted (A, E, S) pillars have stabilized the client's biology. This ensures that the supplements are "fine-tuning" a healthy system rather than trying to fix a broken foundation.

Lesson Takeaways

  • Phase Before You Optimize: Always prioritize Gut, Lifestyle, and Exercise (G, L, E) for the first 90 days.
  • Phenotype Matters: Executives need down-regulation; Athletes often need anabolic support and periodized cleanup.
  • Scale the 'S': You can deliver 80% of longevity results on a budget by focusing on foundational nutrients and free lifestyle habits.
  • Use Sprints: Integrate FMD and senolytics as cyclical "shocks" to the system rather than daily habits.
  • Watch the Red Flags: A drop in HRV or a rise in liver enzymes is a signal to pull back, regardless of the roadmap schedule.
