This isn't just about sleep quality; melatonin is the primary antioxidant for the mitochondria. Without it, the "nightly repair" of the G and E pillars is compromised.

5. Terroir: Soil Quality and the "Eat" (E) Pillar

In the culinary world, Terroir refers to the environmental factors that give a food its unique character. In longevity science, we use it to describe the link between Soil Health and Nutrient Density. A vegetable is only as healthy as the soil it grows in.

Since the 1950s, the nutrient density of common crops has declined significantly (some estimates suggest a 20-40% drop in minerals like magnesium and iron). This is due to industrial farming practices that destroy the Mycorrhizal Fungi network. These fungi are the "microbiome of the soil" that help plants absorb minerals and produce polyphenols.

Longevity Strategy: Prioritize "Regenerative Organic" produce. These plants typically have higher levels of xenohormetic compoundsâ€”stress-induced molecules like resveratrol or quercetin that activate our own longevity genes (Sirtuins).
