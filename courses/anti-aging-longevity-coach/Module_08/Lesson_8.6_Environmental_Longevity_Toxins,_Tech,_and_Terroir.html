1. The Exposome: Identifying 'Gerontogens'

While we are familiar with "carcinogens," longevity science has introduced the term Gerontogens: environmental factors that accelerate the biological aging process by mimicking or exacerbating the hallmarks of aging.

Unlike acute toxins, gerontogens often operate at sub-clinical levels, creating a state of chronic inflammaging. A 2021 study published in Environmental Health Perspectives estimated that environmental exposures may account for up to 70% to 90% of chronic disease risk, overshadowing genetic predispositions.

Primary Classes of Gerontogens:

  • Endocrine Disrupting Chemicals (EDCs): Phthalates and BPA found in plastics that interfere with hormonal signaling, particularly affecting the HPA axis and metabolic rate.
  • Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs): Legacy chemicals like PCBs that bioaccumulate in fatty tissues, impairing mitochondrial function.
  • Heavy Metals: Lead, mercury, and cadmium which catalyze the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and deplete cellular glutathione.
  • Airborne Particulates (PM2.5): Fine particulate matter that crosses the blood-brain barrier, triggering neuroinflammation and telomere shortening.

3. Designing the 'Longevity Home'

The home should be a sanctuary for cellular recovery. We categorize the Longevity Home into three pillars: Air, Water, and Light.

I. Air Quality (The Respiratory Exposome)

Indoor air is often 2 to 5 times more polluted than outdoor air. To optimize the "L" (Lifestyle) pillar, clients should:

  • Utilize HEPA filters with a minimum CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate) of 250 for bedrooms.
  • Incorporate "Phytoremediation" (plants like Sansevieria) to reduce benzene and formaldehyde.
  • Maintain humidity between 40-60% to prevent mold sporulation (a major gerontogen).

II. Water Purity (The Aqueous Pillar)

Standard municipal water often contains chlorine, fluoride, and "forever chemicals" (PFAS). PFAS are linked to metabolic dysfunction and immune senescence. Coaches should recommend Multi-stage Reverse Osmosis (RO) with a remineralization step to ensure the "E" (Eat/Drink) pillar isn't introducing toxins.

III. Light Hygiene (The Circadian Pillar)

The goal is to mimic the solar cycle.

  • Morning: 10-20 minutes of direct sunlight (unfiltered by glass) to set the circadian clock.
  • Day: High-intensity full-spectrum light to support dopamine and cortisol.
  • Evening: Switch to amber/red lighting (low Kelvin, <2000K) and utilize blue-blocking software or eyewear.
