5. The 12 Hallmarks of Aging: Our Biological Map

To coach longevity, you must understand what "aging" actually is. In 2013, a landmark paper defined the 9 Hallmarks of Aging. In 2023, this was updated to 12 Hallmarks. These are the biochemical processes that drive decay. Our protocol targets these specifically:

  • Genomic Instability: Damage to DNA over time.
  • Telomere Attrition: The shortening of the "caps" on our chromosomes.
  • Epigenetic Alterations: Changes in how our genes are expressed (on/off switches).
  • Cellular Senescence: "Zombie cells" that refuse to die and cause inflammation.
  • Mitochondrial Dysfunction: The decline of our cellular power plants.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between Lifespan and Healthspan?

Lifespan is the total number of years an individual lives, while Healthspan is the period of life spent in good health, free from chronic disease and functional disability. The "Longevity Gap" is the period of ill health between the two.

2. Why is "Morbidity Compression" considered the "Holy Grail" of longevity medicine?

Because it aims to shorten the duration of age-related decline to the very end of life, allowing individuals to maintain a high quality of life for as long as possible before a rapid, terminal decline.
