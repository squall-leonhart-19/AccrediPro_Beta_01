The Highway of Longevity: Anatomy of the Vagus Nerve

The Vagus Nerve (Cranial Nerve X) is the longest nerve in the body, wandering from the brainstem through the neck, thorax, and abdomen. It is 80% sensory (afferent), meaning it constantly reports the state of your organs back to the brain. This bidirectional communication makes it the primary regulator of the "Rest and Digest" and "Tend and Befriend" systems.

From a longevity perspective, the Vagus Nerve acts as a master brake on the sympathetic "Fight or Flight" response. Chronic activation of the sympathetic system leads to elevated cortisol, which accelerates telomere shortening and mitochondrial dysfunction. By stimulating the Vagus Nerve, we effectively "downregulate" the aging signals sent to every major organ system.
