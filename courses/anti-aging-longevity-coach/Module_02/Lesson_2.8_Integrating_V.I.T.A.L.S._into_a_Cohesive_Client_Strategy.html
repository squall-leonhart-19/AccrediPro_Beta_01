Phasing the Intervention

We typically implement the V.I.T.A.L.S. strategy in three 12-week phases. This allows the body to adapt to hormetic stressors without triggering a chronic stress response (maladaptation).

Phase 1: The Priming Phase (Weeks 1-12)
Focus: V and I. We optimize sleep, basic nutrition, and light zone 2 cardio. The goal is to lower the "background noise" of inflammaging and ensure mitochondria have the nutrients (B-vitamins, CoQ10, Magnesium) needed for the upcoming work.

Phase 2: The Resilience Phase (Weeks 13-24)
Focus: T and L. We introduce strategic hormetic stressors (sauna, cold plunge, HIIT) and focus on DNA-protective phytonutrients. This phase is about lengthening the "biological fuse" by improving the client's Stress Resilience Score.

Phase 3: The Deep Cleanse (Weeks 25-36)
Focus: A and S. We implement longer fasting windows (autophagy) and targeted senolytic support (e.g., Fisetin or Quercetin protocols). Because the client's vascular system (V) is now robust and their inflammation (I) is low, they can handle the metabolic demand of cellular pruning.

  • Increased resting heart rate.
  • Poor recovery from exercise.

Advanced Strategy Integration

For high-performing clients, we can utilize Cross-Pillar Stacked Interventions. This involves pairing two pillars to achieve a synergistic effect. A prime example is the "A-L Stack": performing light exercise (L) during the final hours of a 16-hour fast (A). The energy deficit from fasting upregulates AMPK, which is further stimulated by the exercise, leading to a 40% increase in autophagic flux compared to fasting alone.

Another advanced strategy is the "V-T Stack": utilizing Pulsed Electromagnetic Field (PEMF) therapy or hyperbaric oxygen to improve microcirculation (V) while simultaneously taking DNA-repair supplements like NR or NMN (T). The improved blood flow ensures the precursors reach the cells more effectively, maximizing NAD+ synthesis and PARP activity (DNA repair).
