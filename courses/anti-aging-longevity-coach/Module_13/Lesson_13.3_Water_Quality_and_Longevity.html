The Biological Cost of Contaminants

Modern municipal water treatment focuses on preventing acute bacterial outbreaks (like cholera) but often fails to address chronic, low-level chemical exposure. These contaminants act as "gerontogens"â€”substances that accelerate the biological aging process.

1. Fluoride and Mitochondrial Decay

While traditionally touted for dental health, excessive fluoride acts as a mitochondrial poison. It inhibits key enzymes in the Krebs cycle and increases the production of Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS), leading to oxidative damage of mitochondrial DNA.

2. Disinfection Byproducts (DBPs)

Chlorine and chloramine are essential for sanitation but create THMs when they react with organic matter. Chronic exposure to THMs is linked to increased systemic inflammation and bladder cancer risk, directly fueling "Inflammaging."

Microplastics & Nanoplastics: The New Vascular Threat

A groundbreaking 2024 study published in the *New England Journal of Medicine* (n=257) revealed that patients with microplastics or nanoplastics in their carotid artery plaque had a 4.5 times higher risk of heart attack, stroke, or death within 34 months compared to those without. This is a critical discovery for longevity coaches focusing on Vascular Health (**V** in V.I.T.A.L.S.).

Nanoplastics, being smaller than 1 micrometer, can cross the blood-brain barrier and enter individual cells, disrupting protein folding and triggering cellular senescence (**S** in V.I.T.A.L.S.). Bottled water is a primary source, often containing 10 to 100 times more plastic particles than previously estimated using older technology.
