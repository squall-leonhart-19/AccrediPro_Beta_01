2. Mechanisms of PBM: Cytochrome C Oxidase

The primary chromophore (light-absorbing molecule) in our cells is Cytochrome C Oxidase (CCO), located within the mitochondrial respiratory chain. During the aging process, or under oxidative stress, Nitric Oxide (NO) binds to CCO, displacing oxygen and effectively "clogging" the production of ATP.

When photons of red (660nm) or near-infrared (850nm) light hit the mitochondria, they cause the dissociation of Nitric Oxide from CCO. This produces three critical downstream effects:

  • Increased ATP: With NO removed, oxygen consumption increases, accelerating the electron transport chain.
  • Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) Signaling: A brief, controlled burst of ROS triggers antioxidant gene expression (Hormesis).
  • Retrograde Signaling: Signals are sent from the mitochondria to the nucleus, activating transcription factors like NF-kB for tissue repair and anti-inflammation.

6. Safety, Eye Protection & Contraindications

While PBM is exceptionally safe, coaches must adhere to professional boundaries and safety standards:

  • Eye Protection: NIR light is invisible to the human eye but can still heat the retina. Clients should use wavelength-specific safety goggles, especially with high-powered panels.
  • Thyroid Sensitivity: The thyroid is highly vascular and sensitive. Avoid direct, high-intensity NIR over the thyroid unless under medical supervision.
  • Photosensitizing Meds: Certain medications (e.g., Tetracycline, some NSAIDs) increase light sensitivity. Always screen clients for these before starting a PBM protocol.
  • Active Malignancy: Because PBM stimulates cellular proliferation and ATP, it is strictly contraindicated directly over known cancerous tumors.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Near-Infrared (850nm) preferred over Red Light (660nm) for treating deep muscle soreness?

NIR light has a longer wavelength, allowing it to bypass the skin and reach depths of 3-5cm, whereas Red light is largely absorbed within the first few millimeters of the dermis.

2. How does the Inverse Square Law affect a client's PBM session?

It means that as the distance from the device increases, the power density (irradiance) drops significantly. To maintain a therapeutic dose, the client must remain at the manufacturer's recommended distance.
