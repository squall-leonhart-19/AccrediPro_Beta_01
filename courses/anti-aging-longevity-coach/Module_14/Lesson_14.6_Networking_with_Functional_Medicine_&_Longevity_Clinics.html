Financial & Legal Models of Partnership

When structuring a deal with a clinic, you have three primary options. Note that referral kickbacks (paying a doctor for a patient) are illegal in many jurisdictions (e.g., Stark Law in the US). Always consult a local attorney.

  1. The "Rent-a-Coach" (In-House) Model: You work inside the clinic 1-2 days a week. The clinic bills the client, and you receive a percentage (typically 60-70%).
  2. The "White Label" Model: The clinic sells "The [Clinic Name] Longevity Program," which is actually your coaching. You are a 1099 contractor behind the scenes.
  3. The Strategic Referral Loop: You and the doctor cross-refer. You provide free educational workshops for their patients, and they recommend your private practice for implementation.

The Collaborative Care Loop

Once you have secured a partnership, the key to longevity (of the business relationship!) is the Feedback Loop. Every 30 days, provide the referring clinician with a "Progress Snapshot" for their patient. This should include:

  • Biometric improvements (Weight, Body Fat %, HRV).
  • Protocol adherence scores (e.g., "Client has maintained 100% adherence to the prescribed supplement stack").
  • Subjective improvements (Energy levels, cognitive clarity).

This data makes the doctor look like a hero, reinforces the value of their clinical work, and ensures a steady stream of high-ticket referrals for your practice.
