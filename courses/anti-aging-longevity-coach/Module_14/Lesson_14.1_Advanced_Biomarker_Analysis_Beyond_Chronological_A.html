To assess a client's inflammatory burden, we must look beyond the standard CBC. hs-CRP (high-sensitivity C-reactive protein) is a general marker of systemic inflammation. However, Interleukin-6 (IL-6) and Tumor Necrosis Factor-alpha (TNF-alpha) offer deeper insight into the "cytokine storm" associated with visceral adiposity and cellular senescence. Elevated IL-6 is particularly nefarious, as it is a primary driver of muscle wasting (sarcopenia) and cognitive decline.

3. Metabolic Precision: Beyond HbA1c

Standard medicine often waits for HbA1c to reach 5.7% (prediabetes) before intervening. In longevity coaching, this is "too little, too late." We utilize the HOMA-IR (Homeostatic Model Assessment for Insulin Resistance) to identify metabolic dysfunction up to a decade before blood sugar rises.

This is critical because hyperinsulinemia is a potent activator of the mTOR pathway, which, when chronically elevated, accelerates cellular aging and inhibits autophagy.

4. Functional Vitality: Grip Strength & VO2 Max

Biomarkers aren't just found in tubes of blood; they are found in the weight room and the treadmill. Two of the most powerful predictors of all-cause mortality (ACM) are functional.

  • Grip Strength: A 2018 meta-analysis of 2 million participants found that for every 5kg decrease in grip strength, there was a 16% increase in ACM. It serves as a proxy for total body muscle mass and neurological integrity.
  • VO2 Max: Often called the "ultimate longevity marker." Moving from the bottom 25% to the top 2.5% of cardiorespiratory fitness for one's age group is associated with a 5-fold reduction in mortality risk. This is a larger effect size than smoking status or end-stage renal disease.

5. GlycanAge & Telomere Length: The New Frontier

While DNA methylation is the "software" of aging, GlycanAge measures the "hardware" of the immune system. It looks at the glycosylation (sugar molecules) attached to Immunoglobulin G (IgG). Pro-inflammatory glycans increase with age, while anti-inflammatory glycans decrease. GlycanAge is often the first marker to respond to lifestyle changes, making it an excellent tool for client motivation.

Telomere testing, once the darling of the longevity world, has become more nuanced. While short telomeres are associated with cellular senescence, current research suggests that telomere attrition rate is more important than absolute length. As a coach, use telomere data as a secondary "stress marker" rather than a primary diagnostic tool.
