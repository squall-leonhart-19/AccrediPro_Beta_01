2. Prioritization Logic: The Triage Theory

In 2006, Dr. Bruce Ames proposed the Triage Theory, suggesting that when the body is modest in micronutrients, it prioritizes short-term survival over long-term health. We apply a similar logic to longevity coaching. If a client has a VO2 max in the bottom 25th percentile and a fasting glucose of 115 mg/dL, adding an NMN supplement (Supplements pillar) is premature. The "weakest links"â€”metabolic dysfunction and low cardiorespiratory fitnessâ€”will undermine any cellular-level intervention.

Prioritization follows this hierarchy:

  1. Acute Pathophysiology: Referral to MD for any values outside clinical ranges (e.g., Blood pressure >140/90).
  2. Metabolic Stability: Addressing insulin resistance and glycemic variability (Eat & Lifestyle pillars).
  3. Physical Foundation: Building muscle mass and Zone 2 aerobic capacity (Exercise pillar).
  4. Cellular Optimization: Introducing senolytics, NAD+ precursors, and advanced hormesis (Supplements & Biohacks).

3. Iterative Monitoring: When to Re-Test

Data without iteration is just noise. A Master Longevity Plan must include a testing cadence that respects biological lag times. For example, while continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) provides real-time feedback, HbA1c requires 90 days to show meaningful change. Similarly, epigenetic methylation patterns (Bio-Age) generally require 6â€“12 months of consistent intervention to reflect a "younger" biological state.

The Recommended Testing Cadence:

  • Monthly: Subjective wellness scores (sleep, mood, energy), body composition (DEXA or bio-impedance).
  • Quarterly (Every 90 Days): Metabolic panel (A1c, Fasting Insulin, Lipids), hs-CRP, and Vitamin D.
  • Bi-Annually: VO2 Max testing, advanced hormone panels (if applicable).
  • Annually: Epigenetic clock (e.g., DunedinPACE or Horvath), Gut microbiome sequencing.

4. The Coach-Physician Relationship

Longevity medicine often occupies a "grey zone" between traditional healthcare and wellness optimization. As a coach, you are the Integrative Architect. You do not diagnose or treat disease; you optimize physiological function. However, the most successful longevity protocols are those where the coach and physician act as a "Longevity Care Team."

Ethical Boundaries & Scope

When reviewing biomarkers, use the phrase: "Based on longevity literature, these values are outside the 'optimal longevity range,' though they may be within the 'standard clinical range.' I recommend discussing these specific markers with your physician to rule out underlying pathology."

  • Coach Role: Behavior change, protocol adherence, lifestyle education, data tracking.
  • Physician Role: Diagnostic oversight, prescription management (e.g., Metformin, Rapamycin), medical clearance for high-intensity hormesis.

6. Troubleshooting Plateaus & Advanced Iteration

Clients often experience a "Longevity Plateau" around the 8-month mark. This usually occurs because the body has adapted to the initial hormetic stressors. To continue the deceleration of biological aging, we must introduce Stochastic Signalingâ€”varying the intensity and timing of interventions.

Strategies for Overcoming Plateaus:

  • Dietary Cycling: Moving from constant TRF to periodic "Protein Cycling" (restricting protein for 2 days a week to trigger autophagy via mTOR inhibition).
  • Hormetic Progression: Increasing sauna temperature or duration, or moving from cold showers to ice baths (3-5 minutes at 50Â°F).
  • Exercise Variance: Shifting focus from aerobic base (Zone 2) to anaerobic peak (HIIT/VO2 Max intervals) for 4 weeks.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of integrated protocol design.

According to the Triage Theory, why should a coach address metabolic health (glucose/insulin) before introducing expensive longevity supplements like senolytics?

Because the body prioritizes short-term survival and repair. If metabolic dysfunction is present, cellular resources are diverted to managing acute oxidative stress and glycation, rendering "optimization" supplements like senolytics significantly less effective and potentially wasteful.

What is the recommended frequency for testing an Epigenetic Biological Age clock?

Annually (every 12 months). Epigenetic methylation patterns change slowly in response to lifestyle interventions; testing more frequently often yields "noise" rather than actionable data and increases unnecessary costs for the client.

Key Lesson Takeaways

  • Sequence Matters: Always stabilize the Foundation (Sleep, Nutrition, Basic Movement) before moving to advanced biohacks.
  • The Weakest Link: Use the Triage Theory to identify the one biomarker that, if improved, would have the largest systemic impact.
  • Collaborative Care: Position yourself as the architect who helps the client implement the medical insights provided by their physician.
  • Iterate with Data: A protocol is a living document; it must be adjusted every 90 days based on objective biomarker feedback.
