4. Developing a 'Practitioner Referral Kit'

To secure partnerships with medical professionals, you must speak their language. A physician will not refer to a "health coach" who seems unscientific. You must present a Professional Referral Kit that demonstrates your clinical literacy and respect for scope of practice.

Your kit should include:

  • The A.G.E.L.E.S.S. Protocolâ„¢ Summary: A one-page scientific abstract of your methodology.

5. The Economics of Strategic Partnerships

A successful longevity coaching business should aim for a "Triad Revenue Model":

  1. Service Revenue: High-ticket coaching fees (The core).
  2. Affiliate Revenue: One-time commissions from labs and testing (The entry point).
  3. Recurring Revenue: Monthly commissions from supplement protocols (The tail).

A 2023 industry analysis showed that coaches who integrated lab and supplement partnerships increased their average revenue per user (ARPU) by 34% without increasing their coaching hours.

6. Ethics, Disclosure, and Professional Boundaries

Transparency is the bedrock of a premium brand. In the longevity space, "kickbacks" can look like conflicts of interest if not handled correctly. Always follow these three rules:

1. Full Disclosure

Include a clear disclosure in your client agreement: "I may receive a commission from the products or labs recommended. You are under no obligation to use these specific providers."

2. Patient-Centric Selection

Only partner with companies whose products are third-party tested (NSF, Informed Choice) and backed by peer-reviewed evidence.

3. Respect the Medical Hierarchy

Never advise a client to change a medication prescribed by their physician. Your role is to provide the lifestyle data that the physician can use to make informed medical decisions.
