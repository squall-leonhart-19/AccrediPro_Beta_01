4. The Retargeting Bridge: Moving from L to A

Rarely does a premium client sign up for a $10,000 longevity program after seeing one ad. Your strategy must involve Sequential Retargeting. We use the A.G.E.L.E.S.S. framework to move them through the funnel:

  1. Top of Funnel (Lifestyle - L): Ads focusing on sleep architecture or Zone 2 training. These are "low threat" and high value.
  2. Middle of Funnel (Education): Retargeting those who clicked with content on "The 9 Hallmarks of Aging" or "Why your blood work is 'normal' but not 'optimal'."
  3. Bottom of Funnel (Assess - A): The direct call to action for a Biomarker Audit or Discovery Call.

Data from a 2023 meta-analysis of high-ticket coaching funnels suggests that prospects require 7 to 11 touchpoints before committing to a diagnostic-led program. Retargeting ensures those touchpoints are scientific and authoritative.

6. Compliance and Regulations: Navigating Health Policies

Meta and Google have strict policies regarding "Personal Health." To avoid ad account bans, you must follow these rules:

  • No "You" Phrasing: Avoid "Are YOU tired?" or "Is YOUR biological age high?" Instead, use third-person: "Many executives struggle with..." or "Research shows that biological age..."
  • No Before/After Photos: Meta specifically bans images that show a "miraculous" physical transformation or zoomed-in body parts (e.g., skin wrinkles).
  • Avoid "Cure" Language: Never claim to "cure" aging or "reverse" disease. Use terms like "optimize," "support," "mitigate," or "influence healthspan."

Key Takeaways

  • Value-First Economics: Focus on LTV over lead cost; premium longevity coaching justifies a higher acquisition spend.
  • Search vs. Social: Use Google for high-intent diagnostics (A: Assess) and Meta for interest-based lifestyle disruption (L: Lifestyle).

  • Strict Compliance: Avoid "You" language and "Cure" claims to maintain ad account health.
