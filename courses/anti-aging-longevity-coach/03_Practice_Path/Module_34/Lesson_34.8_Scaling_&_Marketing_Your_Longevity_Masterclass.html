3. B2B Sales: Pitching to Corporations & Executive Teams

Corporate longevity is the next frontier of human resources. High-performance organizations are realizing that executive burnout is essentially accelerated biological aging. When pitching to B2B clients, your language must shift from "wellness" to "Biological ROI" and "Human Capital Preservation."

The Executive Longevity Pitch Deck:

  1. The Cost of Attrition: Highlight that losing a C-suite executive due to health issues costs the company 213% of their annual salary.
  2. Cognitive Longevity: Use data showing how Zone 2 training and glucose stability improve executive function and decision-making clarity.
  3. The "Team Biological Age": Offer to track the aggregate biological age of the leadership team, creating a competitive yet supportive environment for health optimization.

4. Automation and Systems: Managing 100+ Students

Scaling requires moving away from manual emails and spreadsheets. To manage a large-scale longevity masterclass, you need an "Automation Stack":

  • CRM (Customer Relationship Management): Tools like GoHighLevel or HubSpot to track lead behavior and automate follow-ups.
  • LMS (Learning Management System): Kajabi, Mighty Networks, or Circle to host the A.G.E.L.E.S.S. modules and facilitate community interaction.
  • Data Aggregators: Platforms like Heads Up Health or Wild.AI that allow you to view group wearable data (HRV, Sleep, Glucose) in a single dashboard.

A 2022 survey of digital health coaches (n=1,200) found that those who implemented automated onboarding reduced churn by 28% and increased client satisfaction scores by 15% due to the immediate "win" of getting started without waiting for a manual response.

5. Ethical Scaling: Maintaining Intimacy vs. Enrollment

The "Longevity Coach's Dilemma" is how to scale without losing the personal touch required for biological interventions. You must maintain professional boundaries while fostering a sense of "belonging."

The "Squad" Model for Scaling

Instead of one large group of 100, break the cohort into "Squads" of 10-12 people based on their primary goal (e.g., Cognitive Performance vs. Physical Longevity). Assign a "Lead Peer" or a junior coach to each squad to ensure no one feels like just a number.
