3. Adjusting Exercise for Mobility & Sarcopenia

The "Exercise & Movement" pillar often hits a plateau when Anabolic Resistance sets in. This is the phenomenon where older muscle becomes less responsive to protein intake and resistance training. In these cases, simply "lifting more" isn't the answer and may lead to injury.

4. Refining the Gut & Microbiome Approach

When the "Gut & Microbiome" pillar (Module 2) fails to respond to standard prebiotics and probiotics, the coach must look for Biofilm Interference or SIBO (Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth). Standard "longevity foods" like broccoli and legumes can actually exacerbate inflammation in a client with SIBO.

The "Plan B" Gut Protocol:

  1. Remove Fermentables: Temporarily switch to a Low-FODMAP approach to starve overgrowth.
  2. Enzyme Support: Use HCl and digestive enzymes to ensure protein isn't putrefying in the colon.
  3. Microbial Diversity over Quantity: Instead of high-dose probiotics, focus on Polyphenol-Rich Foods (pomegranate, green tea) to encourage the growth of Akkermansia muciniphila, the "longevity keystone" species.

5. Conflict Resolution: Demands for Non-Evidence Interventions

As a Level 4 Master Practitioner, you will encounter clients who demand experimental therapies (e.g., off-label pharmaceutical use, unverified stem cell clinics). Your role is to act as the Evidence-Based Gatekeeper.

The "Bridge" Technique for Conflict Resolution
When a client demands an intervention outside your scope or evidence base:
  • Acknowledge: "I understand you've read about the potential of [Intervention X] for longevity."
  • Pivot to Evidence: "Current peer-reviewed data shows a high risk-to-reward ratio in human subjects for that specific approach."
  • Re-Anchor to Protocol: "Our priority is the A.G.E.L.E.S.S. Protocol‚Ñ¢ because it has the highest statistical probability of success without the side effects. Let's optimize your NAD+ precursors first."

6. The Plateau Synthesis: A Master Checklist

Before concluding that a protocol isn't working, use this troubleshooting hierarchy:

  • Biological Check: Is there a hidden infection or environmental toxin (mold, heavy metals)?
  • Behavioral Check: Is "lifestyle creep" occurring? (e.g., alcohol consumption increasing, sleep consistency decreasing).
  • Dosage Check: Is the stimulus too low (under-training) or too high (over-training/over-supplementing)?
  • Psychological Check: Is the client‚Äôs mindset driving a stress response that nullifies the biological interventions?
