Scientific Application: The mTOR/AMPK Seesaw

Critical Concept for Exam Part 1

The Scenario: A client is over-consuming protein (3g/kg) and performing heavy resistance training 6 days a week but shows elevated IGF-1 and high fasting insulin.

The Mechanism: Chronic mTOR activation without periods of nutrient-sensing suppression (AMPK activation) accelerates cellular aging. The exam will require you to balance anabolism (for sarcopenia prevention) with autophagy (for cellular cleanup).

The Intervention: Implementing a 16:8 time-restricted feeding window or a 5-day Fasting Mimicking Diet (FMD) twice yearly to trigger autophagy while maintaining muscle mass through targeted protein timing.

4. Case Study Submission: Documenting Transformation

For Phase III of your exam, you must present a case study. This is not merely a list of supplements given to a client. It is a narrative of biological and lifestyle evolution. Successful submissions must include:

  • Intake Data: Baseline biological age (GrimAge or PhenoAge), subjective vitality scores, and objective biomarkers.
  • The "Why": Why did you prioritize Pillar G (Gut) over Pillar E (Exercise) in the first 90 days? (e.g., resolving systemic inflammation before adding high-intensity stress).
  • The Pivot: Describe a challenge the client faced (e.g., sleep disruption from Zone 5 training) and how you adjusted the protocol.
  • Outcome Data: Re-testing results showing a shift in biological age or specific metabolic markers.

Final Self-Assessment Questions

1. Can you explain the difference between Senolytics and Senomorphics, and provide an example of each?

Senolytics (e.g., Quercetin + Dasatinib) selectively induce apoptosis in senescent cells. Senomorphics (e.g., Rapamycin, Metformin) suppress the proinflammatory SASP (Senescence-Associated Secretory Phenotype) without killing the cell.

2. What are the four "pillars" of cardiovascular longevity as defined in Module 1 and 18?

1. ApoB/Lipid Management, 2. Blood Pressure Control, 3. Glycemic/Metabolic Health, 4. Smoking/Toxin Cessation. (VO2 Max is the functional overlay).

3. How does the Glymphatic System relate to sleep architecture and Alzheimer's prevention?

During Deep Sleep (N3), the brain's waste clearance system (glymphatic) becomes 10x more active, clearing beta-amyloid and tau proteins. Disrupted sleep architecture prevents this "neurological rinse."
