5. Final Graduation Responsibilities

Graduation is a commitment to the Ethical Code of the Longevity Practitioner. This includes a strict adherence to your scope of practice, ensuring you never "prescribe" or "diagnose," but rather "educate" and "optimize."

Your responsibilities include:

  1. Transparency: Clearly stating the limitations of current longevity science (e.g., the difference between animal studies and human longevity).
  2. Safety First: Prioritizing the "Primum non nocere" (First, do no harm) principle, especially regarding advanced supplements and biohacks.
  3. Empowerment: Moving the client from dependence on the coach to mastery of their own longevity variables.
