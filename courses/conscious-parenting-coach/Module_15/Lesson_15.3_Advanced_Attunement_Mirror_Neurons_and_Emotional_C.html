4. Developing Emotional Granularity

To enhance the "Attune" phase, a parent must move beyond "good" or "bad" feelings. Emotional Granularity, a term coined by Dr. Lisa Feldman Barrett, refers to the ability to identify and label emotions with precision. Research indicates that individuals with higher emotional granularity are better at regulating their emotions and are less likely to be overwhelmed by emotional contagion.

Instead of saying "My child is being difficult," an attuned parent with high granularity might say, "My child is feeling overstimulated, misunderstood, and slightly anxious because of the transition." This precision allows the parent to respond (the "R" in RAISE) to the specific need rather than reacting to the behavior.
