5. Narrative Legacy Rewriting: Advanced Techniques

Narrative Therapy suggests that we live our lives according to the stories we tell about ourselves. Parents often carry a "Problem-Saturated Narrative" (e.g., "We are a family of short-tempered people"). A Master Practitioner uses Externalization and Re-authoring to rewrite this legacy.

The "Three-Generation Storyboard" Technique

In this exercise, you guide the client through three stages of their family narrative:

  1. The Victim Narrative: "My parents were cold, so I am cold."
  2. The Survivor Narrative: "I survived my parents' coldness, and I am trying to be different."
  3. The Author Narrative (The Goal): "I acknowledge the coldness of the past, and I am intentionally building a culture of warmth for my children."
