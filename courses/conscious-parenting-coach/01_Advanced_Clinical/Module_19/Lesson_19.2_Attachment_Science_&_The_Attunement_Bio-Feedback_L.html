Attachment is not merely an emotional preference; it is a survival strategy. John Bowlby (1969) posited that the human infant is born with a biological drive to seek proximity to a protective figure. 4. Oxytocin and Vasopressin: The Hormonal Architecture

The bond is fueled by a delicate balance of neuropeptides. While Oxytocin is often called the "love hormone," its role in the RAISE framework is specifically to down-regulate the Amygdala. When a child feels attuned to, oxytocin is released, inhibiting the production of CRF (Corticotropin-releasing factor) and mitigating anxiety.

Vasopressin, conversely, is associated with mobilization and protection. In fathers and non-gestational parents, vasopressin levels often rise alongside oxytocin, facilitating a "protective-bond" that supports the childâ€™s "S" (Support) and "E" (Empower) phases by providing a secure perimeter for exploration.

5. Secure Base Scripting & Longitudinal Outcomes

Why does this matter for the long term? The concept of Secure Base Scripting refers to the internal mental models a child builds about relationships. Research shows these scripts are highly predictive of future success.

A 30-year longitudinal study (Sroufe et al., 2005) found that children with secure attachment at age 12 months were significantly more likely to:

  • Demonstrate higher Emotional Intelligence (EQ) in their 20s.
  • Maintain stable, long-term romantic relationships.
  • Reach higher tiers of management and leadership in their careers.
  • Exhibit lower rates of inflammatory diseases (linked to chronic childhood cortisol).
