2. Curriculum Architecture: The 8-12 Week Roadmap

A successful group program requires an Intentional (I) architecture. Unlike 1:1 coaching, which can be fluid and client-led, a group program must follow a structured scaffolding that moves the entire cohort through the R.A.I.S.E. methodology simultaneously. We recommend a 10-week "Conscious Foundation" model:

  • Weeks 1-2: Respond (R). Focusing on the neurobiology of triggers and the "Pause."
  • Weeks 3-4: Attune (A). Somatic exercises for emotional resonance.
  • Weeks 5-6: Intentional (I). Values auditing and legacy work.
  • Weeks 7-8: Support (S). Practical scaffolding and neuro-individualism.
  • Weeks 9-10: Empower (E). Transitioning from control to collaboration.

The key to curriculum design is the 70/30 Rule: 70% of the content should be universal principles, and 30% should be reserved for "Hot Seat" coaching where individual nuances are addressed in front of the group, providing a vicarious learning experience for all members.

4. The Hybrid Scaling Model

The most efficient way to scale without burnout is the Hybrid Delivery Model. This separates the "Cognitive Loading" from the "Emotional Processing."

Pre-recorded Intentional (I) Lessons: These are the "lectures." By recording the science of the brain, the history of parenting paradigms, and the methodology, you free up your live time. This ensures that when you are live with the group, you aren't just talking at them; you are working with them.

Live Support (S) Sessions: These are the heart of the program. Use these for somatic check-ins, Rupture and Repair roleplays, and Q&A. This model allows you to serve 50 people in the same 90 minutes you used to serve one, while actually providing more educational content through the recordings.

5. Empowerment-Based Marketing (Launch Psychology)

Traditional "Bro-Marketing" uses scarcity and fear (e.g., "Your child's future is at risk if you don't buy now!"). As a Conscious Parenting Coach, your marketing must be an extension of the Empowerment (E) phase. We call this Invitational Marketing.

Invitational Marketing focuses on:

  • Radical Honesty: Being clear about who the program is for (and who it isn't).
  • Value First: Using workshops or "masterclasses" to provide genuine relief before the sale.
  • Sovereignty: Encouraging parents to "check in with their gut" rather than pressuring them into a quick decision.

A 2022 study on consumer psychology in the wellness space found that "High-Autonomy" marketing (where the consumer feels in control) led to a 22% higher long-term retention rate compared to high-pressure tactics.

Check Your Understanding

1. How does the "70/30 Rule" apply to curriculum design in a group coaching program?

The 70/30 Rule suggests that 70% of the curriculum should cover universal principles (educational/cognitive) that apply to all parents, while 30% is reserved for "Hot Seat" coaching or individualized support to address specific nuances within the group setting.

2. Why is the Hybrid Model considered more "Sustainable" for the coach?

It separates the delivery of information (pre-recorded) from the delivery of transformation (live support). This prevents the coach from repeating the same lectures every week and allows them to focus their energy on deep attunement and co-regulation during live sessions.
