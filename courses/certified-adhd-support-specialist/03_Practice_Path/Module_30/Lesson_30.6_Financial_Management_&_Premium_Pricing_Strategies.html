For ADHD clients, the value is often found in the reduction of "ADHD Taxes" (late fees, lost opportunities, medical costs) and the restoration of familial harmony.

3. Financial Forecasting & Practice Sustainability

Financial forecasting for an ADHD practice requires a nuanced understanding of capacity management. Unlike general consulting, ADHD support involves high emotional labor and "cognitive switch costs."

When forecasting, you must account for:

  • The 70% Capacity Rule: Never book more than 70% of your working hours with client-facing time. The remaining 30% is required for "Administrative Executive Function" and recovery.
  • The ADHD Tax Reinvestment: Budgeting for systems (CRMs, VAs) that handle the tasks you struggle with, ensuring your "Zone of Genius" remains focused on the client.
  • Tax Liability: A 2022 survey found that 64% of self-employed neurodivergent practitioners struggle with quarterly tax payments. Automated "tax-holding" accounts are mandatory.

5. Equitable Access: Scholarships & Sliding Scales

As an ADHD specialist, you will often encounter clients whose ADHD has caused significant financial trauma. Maintaining a premium practice while offering equitable access is a balancing act.

The "Tiered Impact" Model:

  • Full Premium Tier: Funds the practice and allows for high-touch support.
  • Scholarship Tier: 10% of your client capacity offered at a 50-75% discount. These should be "application-based" to ensure they go to those most in need.
  • Community Tier: Low-cost group sessions or digital resources that provide "The Method" at scale without requiring your 1-on-1 time.

6. Revenue Protection & Automated Systems

The "No-Show" is a common symptom of ADHD. However, if your practice doesn't protect against it, you are effectively "paying" for your client's symptoms. This leads to practitioner resentment and practice failure.

Practices without automated, pre-paid billing systems report a 15-22% revenue loss due to late cancellations. Implementing "Card-on-File" and "Pre-Payment" policies reduces this to less than 3% (Industry Data, 2023).

The Revenue Protection Framework:

  • Automated Pre-Payment: Clients pay for the month or the package upfront. This removes the "executive hurdle" of paying for each session.
  • The "Compassionate Boundary" Policy: A 48-hour cancellation policy that is clearly communicated during onboarding. If a client misses a session, the fee is forfeited, but the practitioner can offer a "15-minute catch-up call" later in the week to maintain momentum without losing a full hour of revenue.
  • Systematized Reminders: Use a 3-stage reminder system (72 hours, 24 hours, and 1 hour via SMS). This supports the client's executive function while protecting your time.

Check Your Understanding

Why is hourly billing often considered detrimental to both the ADHD practitioner and the client in an L4-tier practice?

Hourly billing focuses on "time spent" rather than "transformation achieved." It creates a ceiling on practitioner income and fails to account for the high emotional labor and asynchronous support (like check-ins) that are vital for ADHD success. Value-based packaging aligns the practitioner's income with the client's actual life improvements.

How does the 'Profit First' methodology specifically support a neurodivergent practitioner's executive functioning?

It uses "Bank Account Partitioning" to provide instant visual feedback on financial health. This bypasses the need for complex spreadsheet analysis (which taxes working memory and planning) and uses the practitioner's natural tendency to check balances as a regulatory tool for spending and tax preparation.
