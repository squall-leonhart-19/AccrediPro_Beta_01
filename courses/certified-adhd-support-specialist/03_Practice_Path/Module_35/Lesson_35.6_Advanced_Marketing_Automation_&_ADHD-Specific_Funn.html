1. Psychographic Targeting: Reaching the 'Late-Diagnosed Professional'

In the transition from a solo practitioner to a scaled practice director, your marketing must shift from broad awareness to surgical precision. Standard demographic targeting (age, location, gender) is insufficient for high-ticket ADHD support. We must target the Psychographic Profile‚Äîthe internal motivations, fears, and behaviors of our ideal client.

The "Late-Diagnosed Professional" (LDP) is a high-value segment characterized by "The Masking Paradox." A 2021 study by Hupfeld et al. indicated that adults with ADHD in high-stress professions experience significantly higher rates of burnout and "imposter syndrome" compared to the general population, despite often having superior cognitive abilities in specific domains.

Data Points for LDP Targeting:

  • Search Intent: High-intent keywords like "Executive Function burnout," "ADHD productivity systems for executives," or "Late diagnosis ADHD grief."
  • Behavioral Signals: Frequent interaction with productivity tools (Notion, Obsidian, Slack) combined with "night owl" browsing patterns (peaking between 10:00 PM and 2:00 AM).
  • Pain Points: The "Wall of Shame"‚Äîhaving 50+ open tabs, a history of started-but-unfinished projects, and the fear of being "found out" by colleagues.

2. Building 'Dopamine-Friendly' Marketing Funnels

Traditional marketing funnels are often "friction-heavy," requiring long-form webinars, 5-day challenges, or complex email sequences. For a neurodivergent audience, these are Executive Function traps. If your funnel requires too much sustained attention before providing value, your leads will drop out due to Low Dopamine Engagement.

A "Dopamine-Friendly" funnel utilizes Micro-Wins and Immediate Gratification. According to the Reward Deficiency Syndrome (RDS) model, ADHD brains require a higher threshold of stimulation to maintain task persistence. Your marketing must provide a "Dopamine Hit" within the first 60 seconds of interaction.

Case Study 1: The Executive Audit Funnel

Client: Marcus (42, Tech Executive, Late-Diagnosed)

Presenting Issue: Marcus was overwhelmed by "standard" 60-minute webinars. He would register but never attend, leading to a 0% conversion rate for the practitioner.

Intervention: The practitioner replaced the webinar with a "3-Minute Executive Function Audit." This was a high-speed, interactive quiz that provided Marcus with an immediate "Neuro-Signature" score and one actionable tip for his specific struggle (inbox management).

Outcome: Marcus completed the audit (Immediate Dopamine). The results page included a 2-minute video (Low Friction). He booked a discovery call immediately. The practice saw a 240% increase in lead-to-call conversion compared to the webinar model.

3. Using AI & CRM Automation for Lead Pipelines

Scaling requires you to be "omnipresent" without being "always on." This is achieved through Intelligent Automation. By integrating AI with your CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system, you can nurture leads based on their specific ADHD subtype or challenge area.

A 2023 industry analysis of specialized coaching practices (n=450) found that practices using Behavioral Trigger Automation (emails sent based on what a user clicked) had a 42% higher retention rate than those using static weekly newsletters. For ADHD clients, "generic" content is perceived as noise and leads to "Unsubscribe" impulses.

The "Method" Automation Stack:

  • Lead Scoring: Assign points when a lead watches a video or opens an email. High scores trigger a "Personalized Loom Video" notification for your team.
  • AI-Chatbots: Not for support, but for navigation. "Are you looking for workplace strategies or relationship support?" This segments the lead immediately.
  • SMS Reminders: Essential for the ADHD demographic. A 2022 study in the Journal of Attention Disorders showed that SMS prompts improved appointment adherence by 31% in ADHD populations.

4. Analyzing CAC vs. LTV in a Specialized Niche

Growth is a math problem. To scale safely, you must understand two critical metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV).

In a specialized ADHD practice, your CAC will likely be higher than a general life coach because the targeting is more specific. However, your LTV should be significantly higher due to the Ecosystem of Support (Group coaching, digital products, and long-term maintenance) discussed in previous lessons.

Scaling Rule of Thumb: LTV should be at least 3x CAC.

5. Retargeting Strategies: Respecting the Neurodivergent Brain

Retargeting‚Äîthe practice of showing ads to people who have already visited your site‚Äîis often done aggressively. For those with ADHD, aggressive retargeting can trigger Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) or "Demand Avoidance." If a lead feels pressured, they will block your brand.

Effective "Neuro-Retargeting" focuses on Helpfulness and Reminders, not "FOMO" (Fear of Missing Out). Remember, your lead likely wanted to sign up but got distracted by a notification or a wandering thought. Your ad should be a "gentle nudge" back to the path.

The 3-Step Retargeting Sequence:

  1. The "Life Got in the Way" Reminder (Day 1-3): "Hey, we noticed you didn't finish the EF Audit. No stress‚Äîhere's the link to pick up where you left off."
  2. The Value Add (Day 4-7): Instead of a sales pitch, show a 60-second tip relevant to the page they visited. "Since you were looking at workplace burnout, here's one trick to manage Slack fatigue."
  3. The Social Proof (Day 8-14): Share a case study of a professional who was in their exact shoes. This builds trust rather than pressure.

Case Study 2: Sarah's Gentle Re-Engagement

Sarah visited the practice's "Group Mastermind" page three times but never applied. Standard retargeting would have shown her "Only 2 spots left!" countdown timers, which Sarah found anxiety-inducing.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of ADHD marketing systems.

1. Why are traditional "5-day challenges" often ineffective for a neurodivergent audience?

They require high levels of sustained Executive Function (attention, organization, and persistence) over multiple days without immediate rewards. This leads to "dropout" when the initial dopamine of the new project fades.

2. What is the primary goal of "Neuro-Retargeting" compared to standard retargeting?

Standard retargeting uses pressure and FOMO. Neuro-Retargeting uses "gentle nudges" and value-adds to overcome distraction and inertia without triggering Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) or Demand Avoidance.

Lesson Takeaways

  • Target the Mask: Focus marketing on the internal psychographic experience of the professional ADHDer (e.g., imposter syndrome and burnout).
  • Optimize for Dopamine: Create funnels with low friction and immediate "Micro-Wins" to keep the ADHD brain engaged.
  • Automate the Nurture: Use CRM behavioral triggers to send the right message at the right time, especially SMS reminders.
  • Master the Math: Scale by ensuring your LTV remains at least 3x your CAC through diverse product ecosystems.
  • Lead with Empathy: Use retargeting as a tool for helpful reminders rather than high-pressure sales tactics.
