1. Google Ads: Capturing High-Intent Search

In previous lessons, we discussed building authority through organic content. However, organic growth takes time. Paid Search (PPC) allows you to bypass the wait by appearing exactly when a potential client is experiencing a "pain point." For ADHD services, this is often a moment of crisis or a realization of executive dysfunction.

A 2023 analysis of healthcare search behavior found that 77% of patients begin their journey on a search engine. For ADHD, users aren't just searching for "ADHD"; they are searching for solutions to specific symptoms. Effective Google Ads strategies for ADHD specialists focus on "long-tail" keywords that indicate a high readiness to engage.

Keyword Type Example Term Intent Level Strategy
Informational "What is executive dysfunction?" Low Drive to blog post/lead magnet
Problem-Solving "How to stop procrastinating with ADHD" Medium Drive to webinar or workshop
High-Intent "ADHD coach for professionals near me" High Drive to booking/consultation page
Crisis-Driven "ADHD workplace accommodation help" Very High Direct call-to-action (CTA) for discovery call

2. Meta Ads: Interest-Based & Lookalike Audiences

While Google captures those *looking* for help, Meta (Facebook/Instagram) allows you to find people who *need* help but haven't started searching yet. This is "Interruption Marketing." For the ADHD demographic, this requires a deep understanding of neurodivergent psychographics.

Targeting should go beyond the "ADHD" interest tag. Specialists should utilize Lookalike Audiences (LLAs). ADHD users often struggle with "infinite scroll." To convert on Meta, your creative must use a Pattern Interruptâ€”a visual or headline that stops the dopamine-seeking scroll. High-contrast colors, bold typography, and addressing a specific internal monologue (e.g., "Why is my brain like this?") perform 40% better than generic clinical imagery.

3. The Economics of Growth: CAC vs. LTV

Performance marketing is only successful if the math works. You must understand two critical metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV). A 2022 benchmark study of private practice marketing found that the average CAC for specialized coaching ranges from $150 to $450 depending on the niche.

If your ADHD support program costs $1,500 and the average client stays for 6 months, your LTV might be $9,000. In this scenario, spending $300 to acquire that client is highly sustainable. However, if you offer one-off $100 sessions, a $150 CAC will bankrupt your practice. We recommend an LTV:CAC ratio of at least 3:1 for long-term health.

4. A/B Testing for the ADHD Brain

A/B testing (or split testing) is the process of comparing two versions of an ad to see which performs better. When marketing to neurodivergent audiences, your variables should focus on Cognitive Load.

A 2024 eye-tracking study (n=450) indicated that users with ADHD traits were significantly more likely to abandon landing pages with "walls of text" or auto-playing videos. Testing "Minimalist Copy" vs. "Detailed Copy" is essential. Often, a bulleted list of "What you get" outperforms a long emotional narrative for this demographic because it allows for quick scanning.

5. Retargeting: Supporting the Executive Function Gap

One of the most common reasons an ADHD lead doesn't sign up isn't a lack of interestâ€”it's distraction. They may have been halfway through your contact form when a notification or an internal thought diverted their attention. This is where Retargeting (Remarketing) becomes an act of service.

Instead of aggressive sales tactics, use "Gentle Reminders."

  • Day 1-3: "Life gets busy. We noticed you were looking at our Executive Function program. Need a hand getting started?"
  • Day 4-7: Social proof. "Here is how Mark managed his ADHD at work after our 12-week program."
  • Day 10+: Low-friction entry. "Not ready for the full program? Download our free 'ADHD Morning Routine' guide instead."

This sequence respects the client's neurobiology while keeping your services "top of mind" (addressing the "out of sight, out of mind" object permanence challenge).

6. Implementation: The Ethical Performance Framework

As an AccrediPro Certified Specialist, your marketing must remain neuro-inclusive. Avoid "scare tactics" or "deficit-based" marketing. Performance marketing in the ADHD space should be about Alignment. You are not "selling" a cure; you are offering a partnership in management.

Check Your Understanding

Why is a "Gentle Reminder" retargeting strategy specifically effective for ADHD audiences compared to a standard "Hard Sell"?

It accounts for executive function challenges like distractibility and task-switching. A lead may have intended to sign up but got sidetracked; the reminder acts as an external 'working memory' support rather than a high-pressure sales tactic, which can trigger Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) or avoidance.

If your CAC is $200 and your LTV is $500, is your marketing strategy sustainable for a growing practice?

While technically profitable (2.5:1 ratio), it is below the ideal 3:1 benchmark. After accounting for software, taxes, and your time (labor costs), a 2.5:1 ratio may result in very thin margins. You should look to either increase LTV (retention/upsell) or optimize the ad creative to lower the CAC.

Key Takeaways

  • Search vs. Social: Use Google for high-intent "crisis" searches and Meta for "awareness" through interest-based targeting.
  • The 3:1 Rule: Aim for a Lifetime Value that is at least three times your Acquisition Cost.
  • Creative Clarity: ADHD audiences respond better to scannable, high-contrast, and low-cognitive-load ad designs.
  • Remarketing as Support: Use retargeting ads to bridge the gap caused by executive function distractions.
  • Ethical Alignment: Focus on neuro-affirming solutions rather than fear-based marketing.
