1. The Multidimensional Lens

In our previous lessons, we explored the developmental path of the ADHD specialist and the ethics of supervision. However, when working with complex ADHD casesâ€”where comorbidities, rejection sensitivity, and chronic executive dysfunction create a "tangled web"â€”a simple linear review of interventions is insufficient. We need a framework that looks at the case from multiple angles simultaneously.

The 7-Eyed Model, originally developed by Peter Hawkins and Robin Shohet, is the gold standard for advanced clinical and coaching supervision. It moves beyond "What did you do with the client?" and asks "What is happening between us, within you, and within the system?" For the ADHD Support Specialist, this model prevents the common trap of "strategy-hopping" when a client struggles to implement change.

The Client System Eye 1, 2, 3 Client experience, specialist interventions, and the working alliance.
The Supervisory System Eye 4, 5, 6 Specialist's internal state, the supervisor-specialist dynamic, and supervisor's intuition.
The Contextual System Eye 7 Organizational, cultural, and systemic influences on the case.

2. Eyes 1-3: The Client Focus

The first three eyes represent the "traditional" focus of consultation. In ADHD support, these are often where we start, but they are rarely where we finish.

Eye 1: The Clientâ€™s Experience

This eye focuses on the phenomenology of the client. What is the client actually saying? How are they presenting their ADHD? We look for the narrative behind the symptoms. For example, if a client is "lazy," Eye 1 asks: What is the internal experience of their task paralysis? A 2021 study found that 74% of ADHD adults feel their internal experience is misunderstood by generalist practitioners who focus only on outward behavior.

Eye 2: The Specialistâ€™s Interventions

Here, we evaluate the tools. Are we using the AccrediPro "Method" correctly? Are we over-relying on planners when the client needs emotional regulation? We deconstruct the "why" behind the chosen strategy. If a specialist suggests "body doubling" but the client resists, Eye 2 examines the technical delivery of that intervention.

Eye 3: The Relationship (The "Between" Space)

This eye looks at the relational dynamic. Is there a "parent-child" dynamic forming? In ADHD work, specialists often fall into a "rescuer" role, while the client falls into "helplessness." Eye 3 identifies these unconscious patterns before they lead to specialist burnout or client dependency.

3. Eyes 4-5: The Relationship Focus

This is where supervision becomes "advanced." We move from the client to the practitioner.

Eye 4: The Specialistâ€™s Internal Process

What is the specialist feeling *during* the session? In ADHD support, specialists often experience vicarious dysregulation. If the client is scattered, does the specialist feel scattered? If the specialist has ADHD themselves, are they over-identifying with the clientâ€™s struggle? Eye 4 is about self-congruence and identifying countertransference.

Eye 5: The Supervisory Relationship (Parallel Process)

This is arguably the most powerful tool in the model. Parallel process occurs when the specialist unknowingly recreates the clientâ€™s behavior within the supervision session. If a specialist is chronically late to supervision or "forgets" their case notes while discussing a client with executive dysfunction, they are "enacting" the case. A meta-analysis (n=1,240) suggested that identifying parallel processes accounts for a 22% increase in practitioner self-efficacy.

4. Eyes 6-7: The Systemic Focus

These eyes zoom out to the widest possible perspective.

Eye 6: The Supervisorâ€™s Internal Process

The supervisor uses their own reactions as data. If the supervisor feels bored while the specialist is talking, it may reflect the "stuckness" or lack of engagement in the specialist-client relationship. It is a "gut-level" check that informs the consultation.

Eye 7: The Wider Context

No ADHD case exists in a vacuum. Eye 7 considers:

  • The Medical Model: Is the client being pressured to "medicate away" their personality?
  • Workplace Culture: Does the clientâ€™s employer view ADHD as a disability or a lack of discipline?
  • Family Systems: Is a spouse or parent undermining the specialist's work?
  • Socio-Economics: Does the client have the financial resources to sustain the recommended supports?

5. Deconstructing Stalled Progress

When progress stalls, the specialist often feels a sense of shame. The 7-Eyed Model removes this shame by framing the "stall" as a data point across seven possible dimensions. Use the following table to troubleshoot cases where the client "knows what to do but isn't doing it."

If the case is... Look through Eye... Actionable Question
"Stuck" or boring Eye 6 (Supervisor) Is the lack of energy in this room reflecting a lack of hope in theirs?
High conflict Eye 3 (Relationship) Is the specialist triggering the client's RSD or demand avoidance?
Technically perfect but failing Eye 7 (Context) What systemic barrier (job, spouse, debt) is making this strategy impossible?
Specialist feels "drained" Eye 4 (Specialist) Is the specialist taking on too much responsibility for the client's "success"?

6. The Parallel Process in ADHD

Because ADHD is a disorder of regulation and consistency, these themes almost always leak into supervision. As a specialist (or future supervisor), you must be hyper-aware of these "leaks." If you find yourself wanting to "fix" the specialist's case for them, you are likely experiencing the same pressure the specialist feels to "fix" the client. This is Eye 5 in action.

Define "Parallel Process" in the context of ADHD supervision.

Parallel process occurs when the specialist unconsciously mimics the client's ADHD symptoms (e.g., disorganization, emotional dysregulation) within the supervisory relationship (Eye 5).
Key Lesson Takeaways
  • The 7-Eyed Model shifts supervision from a "report" to a "multidimensional exploration."
  • Eyes 1-3 cover the client, the tools, and the working alliance.
  • Eyes 4-6 focus on the internal and relational dynamics of the practitioner and supervisor.
  • Eye 7 addresses the systemic and cultural factors (e.g., workplace stigma) that hinder ADHD progress.
  • Identifying Parallel Process is the fastest way to resolve "stuck" cases in neurodivergent support.
