4. Neuroplasticity in L2: Managing the "Healing Crisis"

As clients undergo rapid synthesis, they may experience a Healing Crisis (also known as a Herxheimer-like reaction in the nervous system). This is a period where symptoms may temporarily intensify as the brain "prunes" old synaptic connections to make way for new ones.

Biological markers of the Healing Crisis:

  • Neuro-inflammation: Temporary "brain fog" as the microglia clear out old cellular debris.
  • Emotional Lability: The "thawing" of suppressed somatic emotions (I) as the nervous system moves from dorsal shutdown to sympathetic mobilization.
  • Metabolic Fatigue: The high caloric cost of building new neural networks.
