Why Hourly Billing Fails

Hourly billing is the fastest route to burnout. It creates a "transactional" relationship where the client is constantly watching the clock, and the practitioner is incentivized to work slowly. More importantly, it fails to account for the "behind-the-scenes" expertise, the ASCENDâ„¢ methodology, and the 24/7 support structures you provide.

The "Hourly" Trap

"I charge $100 per hour. If I want to make $10,000, I need to work 100 hours a month. This excludes marketing, admin, and prep time. I am capped and exhausted."

The "ASCEND" Specialist

"I offer a 12-week transformation for $3,600. I only need 3 new clients a month to hit $10,000+. I spend more time on each client, ensuring deep results and referrals."

The Three-Tier Strategy

Psychologically, giving a client one price leads to a "Yes/No" decision. Giving them three options leads to a "Which one?" decision. This is known as choice architecture.

Tier 1: The Essential Journey ($1,500 - $2,500)

The core ASCENDâ„¢ framework. Self-paced modules with bi-weekly group calls or limited 1:1 support. Ideal for those starting their wellness journey.

Tier 2: The Signature Transformation ($3,000 - $5,500)

The "Gold Standard." Full 1:1 support, personalized ASCENDâ„¢ protocols, and daily messaging access. This should be your most popular option (The "Decoy" effect).

Tier 3: The VIP Concierge ($7,500 - $15,000+)

Ultra-premium. Includes in-person intensives, 24/7 priority access, and coordination with the clientâ€™s other medical professionals. Only for high-net-worth or high-need individuals.

Pro Tip: The Power of the Pause

After you state your package price (e.g., "The investment for the ASCEND transformation is $3,800"), stop talking. The first person to speak usually feels the need to justify. Allow the client to process the value. If they object, it is usually not about the price, but about their belief in the outcome.

What is "Price Anchoring" in a coaching consultation?
It is the practice of presenting a higher-tier option (like a $10k VIP package) first. This makes the "Signature" package (e.g., $3,500) appear much more affordable and accessible by comparison, even though it is still a premium investment.
