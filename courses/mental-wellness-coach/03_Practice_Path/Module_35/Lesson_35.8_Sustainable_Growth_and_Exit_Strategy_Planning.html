5. The Exit Strategy Framework

An exit strategy is not an admission of defeat; it is a plan for the evolution of the mission. There are four primary exit paths for a high-level wellness practitioner:

  1. The Lifestyle Legacy: You remain the owner but hire a CEO to run the business. You receive a monthly dividend while pursuing other passions.
  2. Internal Buy-Out (MBO): Your key employees or junior partners buy the business from you over 5-7 years using the business's own profits.
  3. Third-Party Acquisition: A larger entity (like a corporate wellness firm) buys your brand, methodology, and client list for a lump sum.
  4. Licensing Transition: You shut down the coaching practice but continue to earn royalties by licensing your M.I.N.D.F.U.L.â„¢ curriculum to other institutions.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of sustainable growth and exit planning.

1. What is the primary difference between SDE and EBITDA in the context of business valuation?

SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) includes the owner's salary and benefits, typically used for small "owner-operator" businesses. EBITDA is used for larger organizations where the owner is not the primary service provider, providing a clearer picture of the company's standalone profitability.

2. How does the 'Resilience Reserve' (Module 4) apply to a team-based scaling model?

In a team model, the Resilience Reserve becomes a collective metric. The leader must ensure that the "systemic load" (workload, emotional labor) does not exceed the team's capacity, preventing organizational burnout and maintaining the quality of client care.
