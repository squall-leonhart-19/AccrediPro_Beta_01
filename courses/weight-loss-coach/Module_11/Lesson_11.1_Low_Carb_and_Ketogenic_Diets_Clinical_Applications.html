Nutritional Ketosis

A metabolic state where blood ketone levels (BHB) are between 0.5 and 3.0 mmol/L, indicating the body is primarily using fat for fuel.

Beta-Hydroxybutyrate (BHB)

The primary ketone body produced by the liver; it acts as both an efficient fuel source and a powerful signaling molecule for gene expression.

Gluconeogenesis

The metabolic pathway by which the liver produces glucose from non-carbohydrate sources (amino acids, glycerol, lactate).

Insulin-Glucagon Ratio

The primary hormonal driver of fat storage vs. fat burning. A low ratio (low insulin, high glucagon) promotes lipolysis.

The Physiology of Carbohydrate Restriction

To understand low-carb diets, we must first look at the Insulin-Carbohydrate Model of obesity. When dietary carbohydrates are restricted, serum glucose levels drop, leading to a significant reduction in insulin secretion. This shift is the "master switch" for fat oxidation. In the absence of high insulin, the enzyme Hormone-Sensitive Lipase (HSL) is activated, allowing stored triglycerides to be broken down into free fatty acids.

A 2021 meta-analysis of 61 randomized controlled trials (n=6,925) found that ketogenic diets resulted in significantly greater weight loss and reduction in cardiovascular risk factors compared to low-fat diets over a 6-month period, with an average weight loss difference of 3.8kg (95% CI: 2.5â€“5.1). This isn't just about calories; it's about the Thermic Optimization and hormonal environment created by the macronutrient shift.

Therapeutic Applications & Clinical Markers

Beyond weight loss, ketogenic protocols are powerful for specific clinical conditions. In Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS), the primary driver is hyperinsulinemia. By suppressing insulin, we can reduce androgen production and restore ovulation. A 2020 study showed that women with PCOS on a ketogenic diet for 12 weeks saw a 12% reduction in free testosterone and significant improvements in LH/FSH ratios.

When monitoring clients, look beyond the scale. Key clinical markers for success include:

  • Triglyceride/HDL Ratio: A decrease indicates improved cardiovascular health.
  • HOMA-IR: A calculation of insulin resistance that should decrease.
  • Highly Sensitive C-Reactive Protein (hs-CRP): Often drops as ketones exert an anti-inflammatory effect.

Managing the Transition: The "Keto Flu"

The transition from burning sugar to burning fat is a physiological stressor. As insulin levels drop, the kidneys excrete sodium and water (the "whoosh" effect). This can lead to the "Keto Flu"â€”headaches, fatigue, and cramps. This is where Intake Precision (I) is critical.

Coaches must ensure clients proactively manage electrolytes:

  • Sodium: 3,000â€“5,000mg/day (unless hypertensive).
  • Potassium: 1,000â€“3,000mg/day via food (avocado, spinach).
  • Magnesium: 300â€“400mg/day (Glycinate or Malate).

Clinical Safety & Contraindications

While powerful, these diets are not for everyone. Resistance & Recovery (R) is vital hereâ€”if a client is not sleeping or is under extreme chronic stress, a strict ketogenic diet can sometimes elevate cortisol further.

Absolute Contraindications:

  • Pancreatitis
  • Gallbladder disease (active)
  • Impaired fat metabolism (Carnitine deficiency, CPT I/II deficiency)
  • Type 1 Diabetes (requires intensive medical co-management to avoid euglycemic ketoacidosis)

Key Takeaways

  • Hormonal over Caloric: Weight loss on low-carb diets is driven primarily by insulin suppression and the activation of lipolytic pathways.
  • Spectrum of Use: Match the "dose" of carbohydrate restriction to the client's metabolic damage; more insulin resistance requires stricter restriction.
  • Electrolytes are Non-Negotiable: Most "side effects" are actually mineral deficiencies caused by the natriuretic effect of low insulin.
  • Metabolic Flexibility: Use ketosis to "reset" the metabolism, then introduce strategic carbohydrates to maintain mitochondrial health.
  • Clinical Monitoring: Watch markers like HbA1c, Triglycerides, and hs-CRP to validate the internal health improvements beyond the scale.
