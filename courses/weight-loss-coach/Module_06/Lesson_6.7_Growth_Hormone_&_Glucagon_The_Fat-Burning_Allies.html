2. Growth Hormone: The Preservationist

Human Growth Hormone (hGH), or Somatotropin, is a peptide hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland. While famous for its role in childhood growth, its primary role in adults is metabolic regulation and tissue repair.

The Lipolytic Mechanism

GH is a potent stimulator of lipolysis. It acts directly on adipocytes (fat cells) by increasing the activity of Hormone-Sensitive Lipase (HSL) and decreasing the activity of Lipoprotein Lipase (LPL). In simpler terms: it encourages fat to leave the cell and prevents it from entering.

Crucially, GH is "protein-sparing." A 2022 study published in Nature Metabolism highlighted that GH inhibits the oxidation of amino acids. This means that during a caloric deficit, high GH levels ensure that the body burns fat for fuel while protecting the skeletal muscle you worked so hard to build in Module 5: Strengthen.

Key Terms

Lipolysis

The biochemical pathway responsible for the catabolism of triacylglycerols into free fatty acids and glycerol.

Somatopause

The age-related decline in GH production, often contributing to increased visceral adiposity and sarcopenia.

3. Glucagon: Unlocking the Vault

If Insulin is the "Key to the Storage Unit," Glucagon is the "Key to the Vault." Secreted by the alpha cells of the pancreas, glucagon's primary job is to prevent blood glucose from dropping too low (hypoglycemia).

When you haven't eaten for several hours, your insulin levels drop, and the pancreas releases glucagon. This triggers two vital processes:

  1. Glycogenolysis: Breaking down stored liver glycogen into glucose.
  2. Gluconeogenesis: Creating new glucose from non-carbohydrate sources (like glycerol from fat).

5. HIIT and the Lactate Surge

While steady-state cardio (Zone 2) is excellent for general health, High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) is the gold standard for GH optimization. The mechanism is driven by the Lactate Threshold.

When exercise intensity crosses the anaerobic threshold, lactate accumulates in the blood. Lactate acts as a signaling molecule that stimulates the secretion of GH. Research indicates that exercise-induced GH release is intensity-dependent; "sprint" intervals (30 seconds of maximal effort) can increase GH levels by up to 450-500% for several hours post-exercise.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Growth Hormone referred to as a "protein-sparing" hormone during weight loss?

GH promotes the mobilization and oxidation of fats for energy while simultaneously inhibiting the breakdown of amino acids (muscle protein). This ensures the body uses fat stores rather than muscle tissue for fuel during a caloric deficit.

2. How does late-night eating specifically hinder fat loss from a hormonal perspective?

Late-night eating (especially carbs) raises insulin levels. High insulin suppresses the pituitary gland's secretion of Growth Hormone during deep sleep, missing the most significant natural fat-burning window of the 24-hour cycle.
