4. Systematizing Anti-Inflammatory Protocols

Delivering the A: Anti-Inflammatory Diet phase can be administratively heavy due to meal planning and trigger identification. Systematization involves creating "Protocol Modules" that can be deployed based on the client's metabolic profile (from the T: Test phase).

The "Modular Protocol" System

Instead of custom meal plans, create 3-4 standardized "Anti-Inflammatory Paths" based on common inflammatory profiles (e.g., Insulin Resistant, Gut Dysbiosis, or High-Stress/Cortisol). Use automation to assign the path based on the intake form data.

By using a digital library of anti-inflammatory recipes and automated shopping list generators, you reduce the time spent on nutrition education while increasing the client's self-efficacy.

6. Workflow Optimization Analytics: Measuring Efficiency

As a professional coach, you must track your Operational KPIs. If you are spending more than 20% of your time on "non-coaching" tasks, your systems are failing.

Key metrics to track monthly:

  • Client Support Response Time: Aim for <4 hours during business windows.
  • Onboarding Completion Rate: Percentage of clients who complete all intake steps within 48 hours.
  • Retention-to-System Correlation: Identifying which automated touchpoints correlate with the highest client satisfaction scores.
