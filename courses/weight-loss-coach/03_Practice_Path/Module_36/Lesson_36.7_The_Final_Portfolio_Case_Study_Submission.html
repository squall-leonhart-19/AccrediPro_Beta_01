3. Evidence-Based Reporting: Quantitative & Qualitative

AccrediPro requires a dual-track reporting system. You must present Quantitative Data (the numbers) and Qualitative Data (the client's subjective experience). This provides a 360-degree view of the transformation.

Quantitative Requirements:

  • Biometric Shifts: Changes in body composition (BF%, LBM), waist-to-hip ratio, and weight.
  • Metabolic Markers: If available, fasting glucose, HbA1c, or lipid panels provided by the client's physician.
  • Performance KPIs: Increases in strength (1RM or volume) and NEAT levels (step counts).

Qualitative Requirements:

  • Hunger/Satiety Scales: Using the 1-10 visual analog scale (VAS) discussed in Module 4.
  • Psychological Resilience: Observations on the client's shift from "all-or-nothing" thinking to a growth mindset.
  • Energy & Sleep: Subjective reporting on sleep quality and mid-day energy dips.

5. Submission Protocols & Board Review Standards

To maintain the $997+ premium value of this certification, the AccrediPro Board of Examiners adheres to strict submission protocols. Portfolios that do not meet these standards will be returned for revision.

Standard 1: Formatting & Clarity

Submissions must be in a single PDF document. Use clear headings for each phase of the T.R.A.N.S.F.O.R.M. method. All charts and graphs must be legible and properly labeled.

Standard 2: Data Integrity

All data must be authentic. Falsifying client data is a violation of the Ethical Boundaries (Module 0, Lesson 3) and results in immediate disqualification.

Standard 3: Client Anonymity

Remove all Personal Health Information (PHI). Use initials or fictional names. Ensure photos (if included) do not reveal the client's identity unless a signed waiver is provided.
