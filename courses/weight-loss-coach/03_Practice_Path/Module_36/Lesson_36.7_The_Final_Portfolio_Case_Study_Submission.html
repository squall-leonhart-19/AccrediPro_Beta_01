5. Formatting & Board Standards

To reflect the premium nature of this certification, your portfolio must be presented with clinical precision. Submissions that are unorganized, contain typos, or lack data visualization will be returned for revision.

Portfolio Structure Checklist:
  • Title Page: Professional branding, client pseudonym (for privacy), and date.
  • Executive Summary: A 250-word overview of the case.
  • Biometric Appendix: Scans, blood work (redacted), and habit logs.
  • Visual Progress: Graphs showing the relationship between variables (e.g., Sleep vs. Hunger).
  • The Maintain Forever Plan: The specific 12-month maintenance strategy developed for the client.

6. Ethical Submission Protocols

As a Master Level Coach, you must uphold the highest ethical standards. This is particularly vital when handling client data. A 2023 review of health coaching ethics highlighted that 92% of practitioners overlook basic data privacy in their case reporting.

Critical Privacy Requirement

DO NOT include the client's real name, date of birth, or address in your submission. Use pseudonyms and redact all identifying information from medical documents. Failure to do so results in an immediate ethics violation and automatic failure of the portfolio review.

Why is it mandatory to include RMR (Resting Metabolic Rate) data in the AccrediPro portfolio?

To demonstrate metabolic preservation or recovery. Standard weight loss often leads to metabolic adaptation (slowing down). By showing stable or increasing RMR, you prove your intervention (Nourish & Satisfy + Strengthen) protected the client's metabolic health.

What is the primary difference between a "Coach Summary" and a "Reflective Practitioner Essay"?

A summary describes what the client did. A reflective essay analyzes what the coach did, including clinical reasoning, mistakes made, and how the coach's own mindset evolved through the process.
