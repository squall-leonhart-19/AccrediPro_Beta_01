2. Integrating Individual Testing (T & F) in Group Workflows

The hallmark of the TRANSFORMâ„¢ framework is the T: Test Metabolism and F: Fix Hormones stages. Critics of group coaching argue that these require too much individual attention. The Hybrid Model solves this through asynchronous data collection and synchronous synthesis.

The "Testing Onramp" Strategy

Instead of testing everyone at once, the Hybrid Model uses an onramp phase. Before the group curriculum begins, clients complete their individual metabolic testing (Indirect Calorimetry or RMR estimation) and hormonal labs. As a coach, you review these individually and provide a "Precision Roadmap" that the client then brings into the group setting.

The "Node" Integration Method

Identify specific weeks in your group program where "Individual Data Breaks" occur. For example:

  • Week 2 (T): Group lesson on metabolism, followed by individual 15-minute "T-Check" calls to set personalized caloric floors.
  • Week 6 (F): Group lesson on insulin/cortisol, followed by a private portal review of their individual lab markers.

4. The Office Hours Protocol: Handling Clinical Nuance

One of the greatest challenges in group coaching is the "Special Case" client who derails a group session with highly specific clinical questions. The Hybrid Model handles this through Strategic Office Hours.

The 10/40/10 Rule for Office Hours

To maximize efficiency, structure your 60-minute Office Hours as follows:

  • First 10 mins: Rapid-fire common questions (The "FAQ Blitz").
  • Middle 40 mins: Deep-dive case reviews (Pre-submitted). You teach the group through the individual's data.
  • Final 10 mins: Individual "Private Room" breakouts (using Zoom breakout rooms) for 2-minute sensitive questions.
