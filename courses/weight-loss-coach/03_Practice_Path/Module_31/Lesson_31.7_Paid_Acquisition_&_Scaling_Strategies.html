1. Navigating Ad Compliance: The Weight Loss Minefield

In the world of paid acquisition, the weight loss niche is considered "high-risk" by major platforms. To protect users from predatory marketing and unrealistic body standards, Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google have strict Personal Health policies.

A common mistake for new coaches is using "Before and After" photos. While highly effective in organic content (as discussed in Lesson 3), these are strictly prohibited in Meta Ads. A 2023 analysis of ad account bans found that 64% of health-related account shutdowns were due to "unrealistic claims" or "negative self-perception" triggers.

Critical Compliance Restrictions
  • No "Before & After": Side-by-side comparisons will lead to immediate ad rejection.
  • No Zoomed-In Body Parts: Focusing on "belly fat" or "love handles" violates "Personal Health" policies.
  • No Negative Self-Image: You cannot imply the user is "out of shape" or "overweight" (e.g., "Tired of being the heavy friend?").
  • No Specific Timeframes: Avoid claims like "Lose 20 lbs in 30 days." Instead, focus on the process (e.g., "Reset your metabolism using our 9-step framework").

3. Retargeting Funnels: Staying Top-of-Mind

Research in consumer psychology suggests that for high-ticket services (like a $2,000+ TRANSFORM coaching package), a prospect needs between 7 and 15 touchpoints before making a purchase. Paid retargeting is the most efficient way to deliver these touchpoints.

A "Retargeting Funnel" works by showing specific ads only to people who have already interacted with your brand. For example, if a prospect watches a video on Module 6: Fix Hormones, your retargeting ad should specifically address Cortisol and Visceral Adiposity, reinforcing your authority in that specific area.

Case Study: The "Lifestyle Hook" Retargeting Strategy

Coach: Sarah M., Metabolic Specialist
Initial Problem: Sarah was running "Direct-to-Call" ads for her $3,000 program, but her Lead Cost was $120 and conversion was low.

The Intervention: We pivoted to a 3-stage funnel:

  1. Awareness: A $5/day video ad explaining the "Circadian Biology" from Module 7.
  2. Retargeting (Engagement): Anyone who watched 50% of the video saw an ad for a "Metabolic Health Quiz" (Lead Magnet).
  3. Retargeting (Conversion): Anyone who took the quiz saw a testimonial video and an invite to an "Enrollment Call."

Outcomes: Sarah's cost per booked call dropped to $42, and her close rate increased by 30% because prospects were already "pre-sold" on her methodology.

4. The Economics of Scaling: CAC vs. LTV

Scaling is not just about spending more money; it is about maintaining a profitable ratio between what you spend to get a client (Customer Acquisition Cost - CAC) and what that client pays you over time (Lifetime Value - LTV).

A healthy coaching business should aim for an LTV:CAC ratio of at least 3:1. If it costs you $500 in ads to acquire one client who pays you $2,500 for a 6-month TRANSFORM program, your ratio is 5:1â€”this is highly scalable.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to Track

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend): Total Revenue from Ads / Total Ad Spend. Aim for 3x+.
CPL (Cost Per Lead): Total Spend / Number of Emails/Phone numbers collected.
Set Rate: Percentage of leads that actually book a call. (Benchmark: 10-20%).
Show Rate: Percentage of booked calls that actually show up. (Benchmark: 70-80%).

6. Budgeting for Growth: Hiring Your First Team Members

You cannot scale while also being the person who manages every ad comment and tech integration. Once your revenue hits a consistent $8,000 - $10,000 per month, it is time to reinvest in human capital.

The First Hire: Virtual Assistant (VA). Their role is to manage "Lead Triage"â€”responding to ad comments, confirming appointments, and ensuring the CRM is updated. This frees up 10-15 hours per week of your time.

The Second Hire: Media Buyer/Ads Manager. Scaling an ad budget from $1,000/mo to $10,000/mo requires technical expertise in "CBO" (Campaign Budget Optimization) and "Creative Testing" that most coaches do not possess.
