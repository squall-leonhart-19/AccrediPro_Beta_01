1. The Jurisdictional Maze: Where Does the Law Live?

When coaching remotely, a fundamental question arises: Which law applies? Is it the law where the coach is located, or the law where the client sits? In the eyes of many regulatory bodies, the service is deemed to occur where the client receives it. This is a critical distinction that many practitioners overlook.

A 2022 survey of digital health practitioners found that 64% were unaware that they might be subject to the professional licensing laws of their client's home state or country, even if they never physically entered that territory.

Jurisdiction is generally divided into two categories:

  • Personal Jurisdiction: The court's authority over the parties involved.
  • Subject Matter Jurisdiction: The court's authority to hear the specific type of case (e.g., a contract dispute vs. a medical malpractice claim).

2. Determining 'Governing Law' and 'Jurisdiction' in Contracts

Your Service Level Agreement (SLA), which we covered in Lesson 5, must explicitly define these terms. Without these clauses, a dispute could force you to defend yourself in a foreign court under laws you do not understand.

Clause Type Purpose Example Language
Governing Law Determines which state/country's laws will be used to interpret the contract. "This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware."
Forum Selection Determines where the legal proceeding will physically take place. "Any legal action shall be brought exclusively in the courts of New Castle County, Delaware."
Choice of Language Ensures a specific version of the contract is the authoritative one in bilingual cases. "The English language version of this agreement shall prevail."
Case Study 1: The "Long Arm" of the Law

Coach: Sarah (Florida-based Weight Loss Coach)
Client: Jean (Paris, France)
The Issue: Jean suffered a metabolic setback and claimed Sarah’s "Anti-Inflammatory Protocol" (Module 3) was medical advice provided without a license. Jean sued in a French court.

Outcome: Because Sarah’s contract did not have a clear "Governing Law" or "Forum Selection" clause, the French court asserted jurisdiction under EU consumer protection laws. Sarah had to hire French counsel, costing her $15,000 in retainers alone, regardless of the case's merit.

3. The 'Corporate Practice of Medicine' (CPOM) Doctrine

In the United States, the Corporate Practice of Medicine (CPOM) doctrine is a legal principle that prohibits corporations (non-physicians) from practicing medicine or employing physicians to provide professional medical services. This is designed to ensure that medical decisions are made by licensed professionals, not business interests.

For a Weight Loss Coach, the risk is "Scope Creep." If your coaching practice involves ordering lab tests (Module 1: Test Metabolism) or providing specific macronutrient "prescriptions" to treat a disease (like Type 2 Diabetes), you may be inadvertently violating CPOM or state-specific Medical Practice Acts.

  • Strict States: California, New York, and Texas have robust CPOM enforcement.
  • Coaching Implications: You must ensure your marketing and contracts clearly state you are providing educational and wellness support, not medical diagnosis or treatment.

4. International Taxation: VAT and GST Considerations

If you sell digital coaching services or courses to clients in the European Union (EU), United Kingdom (UK), or Australia, you may be liable for Value Added Tax (VAT) or Goods and Services Tax (GST).

Unlike US sales tax, which often depends on "Nexus" (physical or economic presence), VAT/GST for digital services is usually based on the location of the consumer. Many jurisdictions have a "de minimis" threshold, but some (like the EU) require registration from the very first Euro earned.

As of 2023, over 90 countries have implemented specific VAT/GST rules for "Electronically Supplied Services" (ESS), which includes remote coaching and online courses.

5. International Dietary Supplement Regulations

Recommending supplements is a core part of optimizing the "F" (Fix Hormones) and "A" (Anti-Inflammatory) pillars of the T.R.A.N.S.F.O.R.M.™ framework. However, a supplement that is legal in the US may be a "controlled drug" or "unauthorized novel food" elsewhere.

Region Regulatory Body Key Constraint for Coaches
USA FDA (DSHEA 1994) Supplements are regulated as food; no "disease claims" allowed.
European Union EFSA Strict "Positive List" of vitamins/minerals; many herbal extracts are restricted.
Australia TGA Many supplements are classified as "Complementary Medicines" with high scrutiny.
Canada Health Canada Requires a Natural Product Number (NPN) for legal sale/recommendation.
Case Study 2: The Customs Catastrophe

Coach: Mark (California)
Client: Elena (Germany)
The Issue: Mark recommended a high-dose Melatonin and DHEA stack for Elena’s cortisol management (Module 6). He shipped the products from his office to her.

Outcome: German customs seized the package. In Germany, DHEA is a prescription-only hormone, and high-dose Melatonin is regulated strictly. Mark was flagged by German authorities for "illegal importation of medicinal products," resulting in a permanent ban on shipping to the EU and a significant legal headache.

6. Strategic Cross-Border Compliance

To operate a world-class coaching practice, you must adopt a "Compliance First" mindset. This involves three layers of protection:

  1. Contractual Shield: Use "Governing Law" and "Forum Selection" clauses to keep legal battles on your home turf.
  2. Scope Definition: Use a "Notice of No Medical Advice" and ensure your T.R.A.N.S.F.O.R.M.™ interventions remain within the realm of lifestyle and wellness.
  3. Tax Automation: Use platforms like Stripe Tax or Quaderno to automatically calculate and collect VAT/GST based on the client's IP address or billing address.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a coach in New York provides remote services to a client in London, where is the "service" generally considered to have taken place for regulatory purposes?

In the client's location (London). Most international and state-level regulators view the service as occurring where the consumer receives the benefit.

2. What is the primary difference between "Governing Law" and "Forum Selection"?

Governing Law dictates *which* laws apply to the contract's interpretation, while Forum Selection dictates *where* a court case or arbitration will physically be held.

Key Takeaways

  • Client Location Rules: Always assume the laws of the client's jurisdiction may apply to your coaching relationship.
  • Contractual Clarity: Your agreements must specify your home state as the exclusive jurisdiction and governing law.
  • Tax Compliance: Digital services are increasingly taxed at the point of consumption; monitor your international revenue thresholds for VAT/GST.
  • Supplement Caution: Never ship supplements internationally yourself; provide links to local reputable retailers in the client's country to ensure local compliance.
  • CPOM Vigilance: Stay within the "Wellness" scope to avoid violating the Corporate Practice of Medicine doctrines in strict US states.
