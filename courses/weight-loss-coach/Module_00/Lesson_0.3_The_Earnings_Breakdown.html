Pricing Models & Revenue Streams

Expert coaches rarely rely on a single source of income. To build a robust $100k+ practice, you must diversify how you capture value. The most successful practitioners utilize a "Value Ladder" approach:

  • 1-on-1 Premium Coaching: The highest price point, providing personalized application of the METRIC Methodâ„¢. Typically ranges from $500â€“$1,500 per month.
  • Group Coaching Programs: Leveraging "one-to-many" dynamics. 10 clients paying $300/month generates $3,000/month for only 2-4 hours of live group work.
  • Digital Products: Low-touch revenue such as "The 21-Day Metabolic Reset" or "Circadian Alignment Guide." These sell while you sleep.
  • Corporate Wellness: High-contract value coaching for companies looking to reduce insurance premiums and absenteeism.

Package vs. Hourly Analysis

The single biggest mistake new coaches make is charging by the hour. Hourly billing incentivizes the client to want fewer sessions to save money, even if they need more support to achieve the result. Value-based packaging flips this script.

Feature Hourly Billing Model Value-Based Package (AccrediPro)
Pricing Focus Cost of 60 minutes of time. Value of the total transformation.
Client Commitment Low (week-to-week). High (12-16 week commitment).
Average Revenue $100 - $150 per session. $1,800 - $3,500 per program.
Income Stability Volatile (cancellations = $0). Predictable (upfront or installments).
Perceived Expertise Service Provider / Vendor. Specialist / Transformation Expert.

Pro Tip: The Retainer Model

Once a client completes their initial transformation (Resistance & Recovery phase), transition them to a "Maintenance Membership" at a lower price point ($150-$250/mo). This builds predictable Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR).
