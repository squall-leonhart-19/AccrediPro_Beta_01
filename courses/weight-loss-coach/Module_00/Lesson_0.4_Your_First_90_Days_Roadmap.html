The 90-Day Sprint Philosophy

Why 90 days? In behavioral psychology, 90 days is recognized as the "sweet spot" for meaningful neurological and physiological change. A 2010 study by Lally et al. demonstrated that while the average habit takes 66 days to form, complex professional transitions require an extended "calibration period" of approximately 12-14 weeks to reach automaticity.

As an AccrediPro coach, you are not just learning to give diet advice; you are learning to manage a client's metabolic flexibility and circadian alignment. This requires a structured approach to your own business habits. We treat your first 90 days as a "Metabolic Reset" for your career, applying the same METRIC Methodâ„¢ principles to your business that you will apply to your clients' bodies.

During this month, your focus is on the "M" and "E" of the METRIC Method: Metabolic Flexibility (your ability to pivot) and Emotional Regulation (managing the stress of a new venture).

  • The Tech Stack: Select and set up your client management software, payment processors, and data tracking tools.

Pro Tip: The Beta Protocol

Do not offer coaching for free. Free clients rarely demonstrate the "skin in the game" required for the metabolic data collection you need. Charge a nominal "commitment fee" to ensure they follow the Resistance & Recovery (R) protocols.

The "Business METRIC" Framework

To succeed, you must apply the METRIC Methodâ„¢ to your business operations. This ensures your growth is as healthy as your clients' metabolism.

M - Metabolic Flexibility

Be ready to pivot your marketing strategy based on what the data (lead generation numbers) tells you.

Success Metrics & KPIs

A professional coach tracks their business with the same precision they track a client's macronutrients. You should monitor these three Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) during your first 90 days:

  1. Lead Velocity: How many new potential clients are entering your "Intake Precision" funnel each week?
  2. Compliance Rate: What percentage of your Beta clients are actually hitting their "Resistance & Recovery" targets? (Aim for >80%).
  3. Metabolic ROI: The ratio of energy spent on marketing vs. revenue generated. High-ticket coaching should have a high Metabolic ROI.
