1. The Three-Factor Eating Questionnaire (TFEQ)

The TFEQ is the gold standard for understanding the "why" behind eating behaviors. Developed by Stunkard and Messick, it measures three distinct domains that correlate heavily with long-term weight maintenance success or failure.

A 2021 study (n=1,240) found that individuals scoring in the top quartile for Disinhibition were 3.2 times more likely to regain weight within 12 months compared to those in the bottom quartile.

The Three Factors Explained

  • Cognitive Restraint: The conscious restriction of food intake to control body weight. While some restraint is necessary for weight loss, rigid restraint often leads to the "all-or-nothing" cycle.
  • Disinhibition: The tendency to overeat in response to external cues (smell, sight) or emotional states. This is a primary target for the Reset Habits (R) pillar.
  • Hunger: The perceived level of hunger and its impact on food intake. High scores here often indicate a need for Nourish & Satisfy (N) interventions like increased protein and fiber.

2. Yale Food Addiction Scale (YFAS 2.0)

While controversial in some clinical circles, the concept of "food addiction" is a powerful tool in the Reset Habits (R) phase. The YFAS 2.0 applies the DSM-5 criteria for substance use disorders to the consumption of highly palatable, ultra-processed foods.

Emotional Overeating

Eating to soothe stress or sadness. Responds well to standard Cognitive Behavioral Coaching (CBC).

Food Addiction (YFAS+)

Dopaminergic dysregulation requiring environmental "Choice Architecture" and total trigger food management.

Practitioners use the YFAS to identify if a client is struggling with a Dopamine Loop. If a client meets 2+ criteria with significant clinical impairment, the coaching strategy shifts from "moderation" to "protective boundaries" during the Reset phase.

3. Acceptance and Action Questionnaire (AAQ-W)

The AAQ-W measures Psychological Flexibilityâ€”the ability to experience uncomfortable thoughts or cravings without acting on them. This is the cornerstone of the Rewire Mindset (R) pillar.

A low AAQ-W score suggests the client is highly reactive to weight-related distress. They may "give up" the moment the scale fluctuates. By identifying this early, the coach can introduce Cognitive Reframing and Defusion techniques before the first plateau occurs.

4. Visual Analog Scales (VAS) for Real-Time Data

While the TFEQ and YFAS are retrospective, Visual Analog Scales (VAS) provide real-time biofeedback. These are typically 100mm lines where a client marks their current state.

Scale Type Question Asked TRANSFORM Pillar
Hunger VAS "How hungry do you feel right now?" Nourish & Satisfy (N)
Satiety VAS "How satisfied do you feel?" Nourish & Satisfy (N)
Prospective Consumption "How much food could you eat right now?" Reset Habits (R)
Palatability "How pleasant was that meal?" Anti-Inflammatory (A)

5. Screening for BED and Orthorexia

As weight loss coaches, we must identify when a client's behavior crosses from "motivated" to "clinically disordered." This is vital for Scope of Practice.

Binge Eating Disorder (BED) Red Flags

  • Eating much more rapidly than normal.
  • Eating until uncomfortably full.
    • Eating large amounts when not physically hungry.
  • Feeling disgusted with oneself, depressed, or very guilty afterward.

Orthorexia Nervosa

Often overlooked in the fitness industry, Orthorexia is an obsession with "healthy" or "clean" eating. If a clientâ€™s score on the ORTO-15 scale indicates that their diet is causing social isolation or extreme anxiety over food quality (rather than quantity), a referral to a specialized dietitian or therapist is mandatory.

Check Your Understanding

A client scores high on the TFEQ "Cognitive Restraint" scale but also has a history of "yo-yo" dieting. What is the most likely risk?

The risk is Rigid Restraint. High cognitive restraint without psychological flexibility often leads to a "break-down" of control when a small slip occurs, leading to significant disinhibition (the "what the hell" effect).

Which scale is best suited for identifying if a client's overeating is driven by dopaminergic triggers from ultra-processed foods?

The Yale Food Addiction Scale (YFAS 2.0). It specifically screens for addiction-like symptoms related to hyper-palatable foods.

6. Clinical Integration: Building the Behavioral Map

Assessment is not a one-time event. In the Maintain Forever (M) module, we revisit these scales to ensure the client hasn't traded one maladaptive behavior for another (e.g., trading binge eating for orthorexic tendencies).

By combining the metabolic data from Lesson 1 with the psychological data from this lesson, you create a "Dual-Track" intervention. Key Takeaways

  • TFEQ identifies the behavioral drivers of intake: Restraint, Disinhibition, and Hunger.
  • YFAS 2.0 provides a framework for managing "food addiction" through environmental design.
  • AAQ-W measures the mindset resilience needed for the Rewire (R) phase.
  • VAS scales offer real-time data to adjust meal timing and composition.
  • Scope of Practice requires screening for BED and Orthorexia to ensure client safety.
