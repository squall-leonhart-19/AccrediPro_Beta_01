If a client presents with significant metabolic adaptation (an RMR >15% below predicted levels), starting with "S: Strengthen" (high-intensity) may be contraindicated due to elevated cortisol levels.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is starting with "Strengthen" (S) potentially counterproductive for a client with a severely depressed RMR?

High-intensity exercise increases cortisol and physical stress. In a metabolically adapted state, the body may respond by further slowing the RMR to conserve energy, rather than oxidizing fat. The "Fix Hormones" and "Optimize Lifestyle" pillars should stabilize the system first.

2. What does the "C" in SMART-C represent, and why is it necessary for metabolic change?

"C" stands for Challenging. Because the human metabolism is a highly adaptive homeostatic system, it requires a significant enough stimulus (or challenge) to force an adaptation, such as increasing mitochondrial density or improving insulin sensitivity.
