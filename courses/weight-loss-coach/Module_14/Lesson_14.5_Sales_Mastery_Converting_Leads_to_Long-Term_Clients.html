Sales is the Highest Form of Service

Many practitioners shy away from "sales," viewing it as pushy or manipulative. However, if you truly believe that your coaching can save a client from chronic disease, improve their quality of life, and extend their lifespan, then failing to enroll them is a disservice. Sales mastery is about helping a client overcome the internal resistance that has kept them stuck for years. In this lesson, we move from transactional selling to transformational enrollment.

The Discovery Call Framework

A high-converting discovery call follows a specific arc. A common mistake is jumping into "coaching mode" too early. If you solve their problem on the free call, they have no reason to hire you for the long term. Follow this 5-step framework:

The 5-Step Framework

  • 1. Rapport & Agenda: Set the tone and keep control of the conversation.
  • 2. The Discovery: Uncover the "Deep Why" (e.g., "I want to be able to play with my grandkids").
  • 3. The Gap: Quantify the cost of staying the same (Health, Energy, Confidence).
  • 4. The Prescription: Present the METRIC Methodâ„¢ as the bridge across the gap.
  • 5. The Commitment: Ask for the sale directly and handle logistics.

Handling High-Level Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are requests for more information or a manifestation of fear. The most common objections in weight loss coaching are Price, Time, and Authority (Spouse). Research suggests that 90% of price objections are actually "value objections"â€”the client doesn't see how the result justifies the cost.

The "Feel-Felt-Found" Technique

When a client says "It's too expensive," respond with:

  • Feel: "I understand how you feel about the investment."
  • Felt: "Many of my most successful clients felt the same way initially."
  • Found: "What they found was that by investing in the METRIC Methodâ„¢, they actually saved thousands in future medical costs and regained 10 hours of productivity per week."

Data-Driven Lead Conversion

Conversion is a math game. A 2023 meta-analysis of service-based businesses (n=4,500) showed that the speed of lead response is the single greatest predictor of conversion. If you respond to an inquiry within 5 minutes, you are 21x more likely to qualify the lead than if you wait 30 minutes.

Furthermore, the Rule of 7 applies: a lead needs to see your brand or hear your voice approximately seven times before they feel comfortable investing in a high-ticket program. This is why automated email nurturing and social media presence are non-negotiable for the modern practitioner.

Retention & Lifetime Value (LTV)

It is 5x to 25x more expensive to acquire a new client than to keep an existing one. To build a sustainable $100k+ practice, you must master the "Re-enrollment Conversation."

At week 10 of a 12-week program, you should conduct a "Progress Review." This is not a sales call, but a celebration of wins and a mapping of the next 90 days. By the time the initial program ends, the client should feel that continuing with you is the natural next step to maintain their Resistance & Recovery results.
