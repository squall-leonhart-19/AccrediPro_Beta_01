Pricing Strategy Architecture

This creates a "conflict of interest" where the coach is incentivized to work slowly, and the client perceives the value based on time spent rather than the magnitude of the transformation. To build a premium practice, you must shift to value-based package pricing.

Legal & Tax Foundations

To protect your personal assets and maximize your tax benefits, you must establish a formal business structure. While you should always consult with a local CPA or attorney, the following are the industry standards for weight loss coaches:

1. Business Entity: Most practitioners start as a Sole Proprietorship but quickly move to a Limited Liability Company (LLC). An LLC provides a "corporate veil" that separates your personal assets (house, car) from business liabilities.

2. Professional Liability Insurance: Often called "Errors and Omissions" (E&O) insurance. This is mandatory. It protects you in the event a client claims your advice caused them harm. Typical coverage should be at least $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate.

3. Client Agreements: Every client must sign a contract that includes:

  • Scope of Practice (Clarifying you are a coach, not a doctor).
  • Refund Policy (Strictly defined to protect your time).
  • Payment Terms (Late fees, failed payment protocols).
  • Disclaimers (Results not guaranteed; medical clearance required).

KPIs for Long-Term Growth

You cannot manage what you do not measure. In the METRIC Methodâ„¢, we track biomarkers; in business, we track Key Performance Indicators (KPIs). A healthy practice should aim for the following benchmarks:

LTV to CAC Ratio (Target 3:1 or higher)

If it costs you $500 in ads to get a client (CAC), but that client pays you $3,000 over their lifetime (LTV), your ratio is 6:1. This is a highly profitable and scalable business.

Retention Rate (Target 85%+)

The percentage of clients who complete their initial package and move into a maintenance or "alumni" program. High retention is the key to "passive" growth via referrals.
