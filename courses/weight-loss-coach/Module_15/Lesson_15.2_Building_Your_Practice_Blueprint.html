Pricing for Value vs. Time

The greatest mistake a new practitioner makes is charging by the hour. Hourly billing incentivizes you to take longer to solve a problem. Instead, use Result-Based Packaging. A client isn't paying for 60 minutes of your time; they are paying for the 15lb weight loss, the reversal of pre-diabetes, and the return of their confidence.

A 2021 study on consumer psychology in healthcare found that clients who pay a "commitment-level" fee (over $1,000) have a 22% higher adherence rate to lifestyle interventions than those paying session-by-session. High pricing is actually a clinical tool for better results.
