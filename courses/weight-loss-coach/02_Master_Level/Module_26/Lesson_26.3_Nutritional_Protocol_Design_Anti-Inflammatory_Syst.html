3. Phase N: Micronutrient Density & Cellular Malnutrition

The Nourish & Satisfy (Phase N) protocol addresses the "Hidden Hunger" phenomenon. When a client consumes a diet high in calories but low in essential micronutrients (magnesium, zinc, B-vitamins, chromium), the brain continues to signal for food to meet physiological needs, regardless of energy status.

A meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that micronutrient supplementation significantly reduced BMI and waist circumference by improving insulin sensitivity and reducing oxidative stress. In Phase N, we program for a "Nutrient-to-Calorie" ratio, prioritizing foods that score high on the ANDI (Aggregate Nutrient Density Index).

4. The Satiety Per Calorie (SPC) Model

Successful program development requires mastering the Satiety Per Calorie (SPC) Model. This model prioritizes three physiological "brakes" on hunger:

  • The Gastric Brake: Mechanoreceptors in the stomach wall that respond to volume (Phase N: High-fiber/High-water).
  • The Ileal Brake: Chemoreceptors in the distal small intestine that respond to fats and proteins (Phase N: Protein Leverage).
  • The Hormonal Brake: The release of Peptide YY (PYY) and Cholecystokinin (CCK).

In practice, a Phase N meal plan should target an average fiber intake of 15g per 1,000 kcal. This specific ratio has been shown to increase post-prandial satiety by 31% compared to low-fiber matched controls.

How does the Satiety Per Calorie (SPC) model help prevent the 'rebound effect' seen in traditional calorie-counting diets?

Key Takeaways

  • Phase A is Diagnostic: It identifies food-driven inflammation that stalls fat oxidation, regardless of calories.
  • The 21-Day Rule: It takes approximately 3 weeks for the gut mucosa to begin healing and for systemic inflammatory markers to stabilize.
  • Micronutrients Drive Satiety: "Hidden hunger" is a primary cause of non-compliance; Phase N solves this through nutrient-dense programming.
  • Integrate Phase T: Never design a macro split in a vacuum; use the client's metabolic RER to determine their optimal fuel source.
