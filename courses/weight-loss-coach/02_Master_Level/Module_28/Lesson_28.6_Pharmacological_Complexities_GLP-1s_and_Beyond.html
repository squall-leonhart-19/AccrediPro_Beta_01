3. Pillar S: Preventing Medication-Induced Sarcopenia

Rapid weight loss via GLP-1s often results in Sarcopenic Obesityâ€”a condition where the client loses significant weight but retains a high body fat percentage because a disproportionate amount of that loss came from skeletal muscle. This is a "Metabolic Crisis" because muscle is the primary driver of Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR).

  • The 1.6g Threshold: Medicated clients should aim for a minimum of 1.6g of protein per kg of body weight to signal muscle protein synthesis despite a caloric deficit.

  • Leucine Triggering: Ensuring at least 2.5g of Leucine per meal (found in whey or high-quality animal proteins) to overcome the "anabolic resistance" often seen in rapid weight loss.

4. Pillar N: Managing Nutritional Deficiencies

When a client is "medicated into anorexia," the volume of food they consume drops significantly. This creates a risk for micronutrient bankruptcy. We must pivot from "Caloric Density" to "Nutrient Density" with surgical precision. Common deficiencies observed in GLP-1 users include Vitamin B12, Zinc, Magnesium, and Electrolytes.

The "Nourish" (N) Pivot: In complex cases, the coach must insist on a "Protein-First, Fiber-Second" hierarchy. If a client can only eat 500 calories in a sitting due to delayed gastric emptying, those calories must be high-bioavailability protein and essential fats. We utilize liquid nutrition (collagen, high-quality shakes) when solid food volume is too difficult for the client to manage.

5. The 'Rebound Crisis': Transitioning Off Medication

A significant crisis occurs when a client stops medicationâ€”whether due to reaching their goal, side effects, or insurance changes. Research shows that without the T.R.A.N.S.F.O.R.M. habits, most clients regain two-thirds of the lost weight within one year. This is often accompanied by "Hyperphagia" (extreme hunger) as the body attempts to return to its previous set-point.

Critical Coaching Note

The "Rebound Crisis" is not a failure of will; it is a biological compensation. As a coach, you must prepare the client 3 months before they titrate off. This involves "Metabolic Testing" (Pillar T) to ensure their RMR has not crashed and "Habit Reset" (Pillar R) to ensure the food environment is bulletproof.

6. Polypharmacy & Metabolic Conflict

Many weight loss clients are on other medications that directly conflict with the TRANSFORM protocols. Understanding these interactions is vital for managing "Stalled Progress" crises.

  • Glucocorticoids (Steroids): Induce insulin resistance and visceral fat storage, potentially "canceling out" the benefits of GLP-1s.
  • Beta-Blockers: Can mask the symptoms of hypoglycemia and slightly lower the metabolic rate.
  • SSRIs/Antipsychotics: Often associated with weight gain via increased appetite and altered glucose metabolism. Coaches must work with the client to optimize Circadian Biology (Module 7) to mitigate these side effects.

Why is the 'S' (Strengthen) pillar more critical for a client on Wegovy than a client using traditional calorie restriction?

GLP-1 medications cause much more rapid weight loss, which significantly increases the risk of sarcopenia (muscle loss). Since muscle drives the metabolic rate, losing it makes weight maintenance nearly impossible once medication is stopped.
2

A client on a GLP-1 reports they "can't even look at a piece of chicken" due to nausea. What is the coaching priority?

The priority is preventing protein malnutrition. The coach should suggest highly bioavailable liquid protein sources (whey isolate, collagen) and smaller, more frequent "fueling windows" to bypass the nausea caused by delayed gastric emptying.

Key Takeaways

  • Coaching is Mandatory: Medication fixes the "plumbing" (hormones), but coaching fixes the "wiring" (habits and mindset).
  • Muscle is Currency: The primary goal for medicated clients is preserving Lean Body Mass (LBM) at all costs.
  • Prepare for the Void: When food noise stops, clients need new "Dopamine Architecture" to prevent depression or cross-addiction.
  • The Rebound is Biological: Prepare for titration off meds by focusing on metabolic health (Pillar T) and protein leverage (Pillar N).
