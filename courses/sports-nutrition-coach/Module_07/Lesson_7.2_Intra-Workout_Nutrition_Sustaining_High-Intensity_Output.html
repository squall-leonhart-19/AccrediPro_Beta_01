The Physiology of Intra-Workout Fueling

In the previous lesson, we established the importance of pre-exercise priming. However, even with maximized glycogen stores (approx. 400-600g in muscle and 80-100g in liver), high-intensity athletic output can deplete these reserves in as little as 90 to 120 minutes. Intra-workout nutrition is the strategic intervention designed to spare endogenous glycogen and maintain blood glucose levels.

A 2022 meta-analysis involving over 8,000 participants confirmed that exogenous carbohydrate intake during exercise lasting >60 minutes improves performance by an average of 7.5% to 12% compared to placebo. The mechanism is two-fold: first, it prevents the decline in blood glucose that triggers central fatigue in the brain; second, it provides a direct fuel source for the working muscles, delaying the point of exhaustion.

The g/hr Carbohydrate Standard

The "gold standard" for intra-workout fueling is measured in grams of carbohydrate per hour (g/hr). This is a departure from total daily needs, as the focus shifts entirely to the absorption capacity of the small intestine. For years, the limit was thought to be 60g/hr. Modern sports science has shattered this ceiling.

Dual-Transport Mechanisms: SGLT1 & GLUT5

The primary bottleneck in intra-workout fueling is not the muscle's ability to burn sugar, but the gut's ability to transport it into the bloodstream. Glucose relies on the SGLT1 (Sodium-Glucose Linked Transporter 1), which saturates at approximately 60g per hour. Consuming more glucose than this leads to "pooling" in the gut, causing osmotic shifts and severe GI distress.

To bypass this bottleneck, we utilize fructose, which uses the GLUT5 transporter. By combining glucose and fructose, we can achieve oxidation rates of 90g/hr or higher. Research by Jeukendrup et al. suggests that a ratio of 2:1 (glucose to fructose) or the more modern 1:0.8 ratio is ideal for high-performance endurance athletes.

Protein & Amino Acid Role

While carbohydrates are the undisputed kings of intra-workout fueling, protein (specifically Essential Amino Acids or EAAs) plays a niche but vital role. For most athletes, protein is best reserved for the post-workout window. However, in two specific scenarios, intra-workout protein is beneficial:

  • Ultra-Endurance (>5 hours): To mitigate muscle protein breakdown when glycogen stores are chronically low.
  • High-Volume Hypertrophy: To initiate the signaling of Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS) via the mTOR pathway earlier in the session.

Managing Gastrointestinal (GI) Distress

The most common reason athletes avoid intra-workout fueling is the fear of "runner's trots" or bloating. GI distress occurs when the rate of ingestion exceeds the gastric emptying rate or the absorption capacity of the transporters. To prevent this, practitioners must focus on:

  • Solution Concentration: Aim for a 6-8% carbohydrate solution (6-8g of carbs per 100ml of water).
  • Hydrogel Technology: Using pectins and alginates to encapsulate carbs, allowing them to pass through the stomach more quickly.
  • Gut Training: The intestine is a plastic organ. By gradually increasing intra-workout carb intake over 6-10 weeks, the number of SGLT1 transporters actually increases.

Practical Protocols: The "L" Strategy

As a Sports Nutrition Coach, your protocol should be individualized. Use the following decision-tree for your clients:

The Strength/Power Protocol

Focus on fluid delivery and CNS arousal. 15-30g of fast-acting carbs (Cyclic Dextrin) to maintain insulin levels and blunt cortisol.

The Mixed-Modal Protocol (CrossFit/Team Sports)

30-45g/hr. Focus on liquid or gel formats to avoid "heavy stomach" during high-impact movements.

The Endurance Protocol

60-90g/hr using dual-transport blends. Start fueling 20 minutes into the session; do not wait until hunger or fatigue sets in.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of intra-workout timing and transport.

1

Why is a 90g/hr intake of pure glucose likely to cause GI distress in an athlete?

The SGLT1 transporter for glucose saturates at approximately 60g/hr. The remaining 30g stays in the intestinal lumen, drawing water in via osmosis, leading to bloating and diarrhea.
2

At what exercise duration does "mouth rinsing" become less effective than actual ingestion?

Mouth rinsing is effective for sessions lasting 30-75 minutes. Once exercise exceeds 90 minutes, the physiological need for actual substrate (glucose) outweighs the neural benefits of rinsing.

Key Takeaways

  • Intra-workout fueling is essential for any high-intensity effort lasting longer than 60-90 minutes.
  • Utilizing dual-transport carbohydrates (Glucose + Fructose) allows for oxidation rates up to 90-120g/hr.
  • The "Gut is an athletic organ"â€”it must be trained to handle high-volume fueling during the training cycle.
  • Solution concentration should remain between 6-8% to ensure rapid gastric emptying.
  • Protein intra-workout is generally unnecessary unless the session exceeds 5 hours or is specifically for high-volume hypertrophy.
