3. Quality Assurance (QA) & Bias Auditing

Master Practitioners must audit their own systems. Cognitive biases, such as Confirmation Bias (seeking data that supports your favorite diet) or Survivorship Bias (only looking at the athletes who didn't get injured), can lead to systemic failure. A formal QA process involves a quarterly review of all nutritional interventions against performance outcomes.

Audit Area Common Bias/Error Master Practitioner Solution
Periodization Rigidity (sticking to the plan despite fatigue) Biometric-led "Auto-Regulation" protocols.
Supplements Brand Loyalty over Efficacy Third-party batch testing (NSF/Informed Sport) audits.
Mental Edge Placebo Over-reliance Double-blind internal trials for ergogenic aids.
Results Tracking "Vanity Metrics" (Body fat % only) Integration of Power-to-Weight and Recovery kinetics.
