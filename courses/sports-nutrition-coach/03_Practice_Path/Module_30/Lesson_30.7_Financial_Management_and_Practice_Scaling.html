2. Diversifying Revenue: Beyond the "Time-for-Money" Trap

Scaling a practice requires decoupling income from hours worked. While high-ticket 1-on-1 coaching (covered in Lesson 4) is the flagship, it is inherently capped by your metabolic and mental bandwidth. To scale, you must introduce low-friction, high-margin revenue streams.

The Revenue Pyramid

  • Digital Performance Blueprints: Low-touch, automated delivery of niche-specific protocols (e.g., "The 4-Week Weight Cut Blueprint").
  • Group Coaching & Masterminds: Leveraging the PERFORMâ„¢ framework for 10-20 athletes simultaneously. A 2023 analysis showed that group coaching models in sports nutrition carry a 72% profit margin compared to 28% for solo 1-on-1 work.
  • Corporate Performance Wellness: High-ticket contracts with executive teams seeking "Executive Athlete" protocols. These contracts typically yield 3-5x the hourly rate of individual athlete coaching.

4. Profit Margin Analysis: Identifying the "Winners"

Not all revenue is created equal. A "successful" practice can be broke if its most popular service has the lowest margin. Practitioners must perform a Contribution Margin Analysis on every service offered.

A 2021 study of private performance practices (n=112) indicated that many coaches lose money on "one-off" consultations when accounting for the time spent on pre-consultation screening and post-consultation charting. 6. Advanced Financial Metrics for Scaling

To scale to a 7-figure practice, you must track more than just "money in vs. money out." You must track Leading Indicators of financial health:

  • LTV (Lifetime Value): The total revenue expected from an athlete over their entire relationship with you. If your LTV is $5,000, you can afford to spend $500 to acquire them (CAC).
  • Churn Rate: The percentage of clients who leave each month. In sports nutrition, a churn rate above 12% usually indicates a failure in the "Results Tracking" (R) or "Mental Edge" (M) delivery.
  • Revenue per Employee (RPE): Essential when using the Associate Model. Aim for an RPE of at least 2.5x the associate's salary/commission.
