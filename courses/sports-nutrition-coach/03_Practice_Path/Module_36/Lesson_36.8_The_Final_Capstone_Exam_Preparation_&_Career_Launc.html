2. The Capstone Project: Your Professional Portfolio

The Capstone Project is the bridge between being a student and becoming a practitioner. You are required to submit a Comprehensive Athlete Performance Plan (CAPP). This isn't just a meal plan; it is a strategic document that justifies every nutritional intervention based on the athlete's specific physiological demands.

A successful Capstone must include:

  • Biometric Baseline: Detailed analysis of body composition, metabolic rate, and current nutritional status.
  • Periodized Macro-Plan: A 12-week nutritional roadmap aligned with the athlete's training phases.
  • Supplement Efficacy Review: Evidence-based justification for any ergogenic aids, including dosage and timing.
  • The "Pivot" Strategy: A contingency plan for common setbacks (injury, travel, or stalled progress).

3. Advanced Exam-Taking Strategies

High-stakes exams often induce "cognitive tunneling," where the candidate focuses on one detail while ignoring the broader context. In sports nutrition, the context is everything. A question might ask for the protein requirement of an athlete, but the correct answer changes based on whether they are in a caloric deficit, a hypertrophy phase, or recovering from surgery.

When approaching scenario questions, use the S.I.F.T. Method:

  • S - Status: What is the athlete's current state (e.g., injured, peaking, off-season)?
  • I - Intent: What is the primary goal of this specific intervention?
  • F - Factor: What are the limiting factors (e.g., budget, time, GI distress)?
  • T - Timing: When does this intervention occur relative to the performance window?

4. The Day 1 Launch Protocol

Receiving your digital badge is the beginning, not the end. To operate as a professional, you must transition from a "learning mindset" to a "business mindset" immediately. This involves mitigating risk and establishing credibility.

Professional Liability Insurance: This is non-negotiable. Even within your scope of practice, providing nutritional advice carries inherent risks. Ensure your policy covers "Professional Indemnity" and "Public Liability."

The Legal Framework: Ensure your client contracts include clear Informed Consent and Scope of Practice Disclosures. You are a Sports Nutrition Coach, not a Medical Doctor or Registered Dietitian (unless otherwise licensed). Your documentation must reflect that you do not diagnose or treat medical conditions.
