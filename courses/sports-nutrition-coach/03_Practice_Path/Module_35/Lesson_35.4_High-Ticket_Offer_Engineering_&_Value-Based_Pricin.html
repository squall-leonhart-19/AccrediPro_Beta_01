5. Contract Negotiation: Deliverables & Performance Bonuses

When dealing with high-ticket contracts, clarity is your best defense against "scope creep." Your contract should clearly define what is included and, more importantly, what is not.

Performance-Based Bonuses: In the professional world, "incentive-laden" contracts are common. You can negotiate a base retainer plus a bonus for specific outcomes. For example: "$1,000/mo base + $5,000 bonus if the athlete makes the Pro-Bowl or All-Star team." This aligns your incentives with theirs and demonstrates your confidence in the P.E.R.F.O.R.M.â„¢ Method.

Practitioner Note: Scope Boundaries

High-ticket clients often expect 24/7 access. Your contract must define "Response Windows" (e.g., 2-hour response during game days, 12-hour response during off-days). Failure to set these boundaries leads to burnout, even at a $10k price point.

6. Transitioning from Hourly Consulting to Subscriptions

The "Hourly Trap" is the greatest ceiling on your growth. When you charge by the hour, you are incentivized to work slower, and the client is incentivized to meet less. Value-based subscription revenue flips this dynamic.

To transition, stop quoting "sessions." Start quoting "Programs." Instead of saying "My rate is $150/hour," say, "To achieve the body composition goals required for your combine, we need a 12-week Performance Phase. The investment for this entire phase is $2,400."
