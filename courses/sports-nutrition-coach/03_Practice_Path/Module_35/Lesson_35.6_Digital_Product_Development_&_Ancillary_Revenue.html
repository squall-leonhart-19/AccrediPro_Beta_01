5. Marketing Funnels for Sports Nutrition

Scaling requires a systematic way to attract "leads" (potential clients) and convert them into customers. A "Marketing Funnel" is the journey an athlete takes from first hearing your name to purchasing your $997 certification-level course or supplement line.

6. Ethics of Ancillary Revenue

As a Sports Nutrition Coach, your primary duty is the health and performance of the athlete. Ancillary revenue should never compromise objective advice. If a functional food (F) is better than a supplement (O) for a specific client, your integrity demands you recommend the food, even if you sell the supplement.

Transparency is key. Always disclose financial interests in any product you recommend. Research suggests that practitioners who are transparent about their product lines actually build higher levels of trust (p < 0.05) with their client base than those who are perceived to have "hidden" agendas.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Results Tracking (R)" pillar particularly well-suited for a membership (recurring revenue) model?

Because results tracking is an ongoing process. Athletes require continuous data interpretation (wearables, body comp, performance metrics) to adjust their periodization, creating a long-term need for the coach's expertise rather than a one-time fix.

2. What is the most critical safety certification to look for when white-labeling supplements for competitive athletes?

NSF Certified for Sport (or Informed Sport). These certifications ensure the product is free from banned substances and that the label accurately reflects the contents, protecting the athlete from accidental doping violations.
