Bioenergetics

The study of how energy flows through living systems, specifically the conversion of macronutrients into ATP for muscular work.

Ergogenic Aid

Any substance, mechanical aid, or training method that improves athletic performance beyond what is possible through training alone.

Metabolic Efficiency

The body's ability to utilize different fuel sources (fat vs. carbohydrates) at varying intensities of exercise.

Periodization

The strategic planning of nutrition to match the specific phases of an athlete's training cycle (e.g., hypertrophy, tapering, competition).

We are currently in the midst of a "Precision Revolution," where the coach's role is to bridge the gap between clinical research and real-world application.

The modern Sports Nutrition Coach is part scientist, part strategist, and part behavior change specialist.
