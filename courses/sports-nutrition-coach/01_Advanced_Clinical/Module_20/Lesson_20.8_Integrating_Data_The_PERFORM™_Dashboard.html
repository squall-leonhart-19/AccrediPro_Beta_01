3. Red Flag Thresholds & Intervention

The PERFORMâ„¢ Dashboard is not just a tracking tool; it is a Decision Support System. A 2023 study published in the Journal of Sports Sciences demonstrated that athletes whose coaches used automated red-flag alerts for sleep and HRV had 28% fewer non-functional overreaching (NFO) episodes.

Critical Red Flag Thresholds

Rapid Weight Loss: >3% body mass reduction within 24 hours (Hydration Crisis).

HRV Suppression: A drop of >2 Standard Deviations (SD) from the 7-day rolling average (Sympathetic Overdrive).

Biomarker Shift: Ferritin dropping below 30 ng/mL in female athletes (Iron Deficiency risk).

GI Distress: >4 consecutive days of "Type 6 or 7" on the Bristol Stool Scale during training (Malabsorption risk).

Integration of HRV, GI, and Energy Intake

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Smallest Worthwhile Change" (SWC) critical when analyzing an athlete's body composition data on a dashboard?

The SWC helps the practitioner distinguish between meaningful physiological changes and the "typical error" or "noise" inherent in the measurement tool (e.g., BIA or skinfolds). Without it, a practitioner might make unnecessary changes based on a 0.5% fluctuation that is statistically insignificant.

2. An athlete presents with a "Red Flag" HRV drop of 3 Standard Deviations, but their dietary compliance is 100%. What is the most likely next step in the PERFORMâ„¢ method?

The practitioner should pivot the 'P' (Periodization) plan to a recovery-focused microcycle. This may include increasing anti-inflammatory functional foods (F), increasing total caloric intake to support the stress response, and communicating with the sport coach to reduce training load.
