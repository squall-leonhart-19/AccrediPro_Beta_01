2. 'Ghosts in the Bedroom': Ancestral Disruptions

The term "Ghosts in the Nursery," coined by Selma Fraiberg, refers to the way parents' past traumas haunt their parenting. In sex coaching, we adapt this to "Ghosts in the Bedroom." These ghosts are the unvoiced criticisms of a grandmother, the repressed desires of a father, or the religious rigidity of an entire community that manifests as a physical "block" during intimacy.

When a client experiences sudden arousal loss or "spectatoring" (dissociating to watch oneself during sex), they are often responding to a ghost. The investigation phase must identify whose voice is actually speaking in those moments of shut-down.

3. Advanced Socratic Questioning: Beyond the Surface

Standard intake questions (e.g., "How often do you have sex?") fail to reach the subconscious. Advanced Investigation requires Socratic Inquiryâ€”a form of cooperative argumentative dialogue to stimulate critical thinking and to draw out underlying presuppositions.

As an AccrediPro certified coach, your goal is to ask questions that force the client to look at the "why" behind the "what." Consider these high-level prompts:

  • "If your pleasure had a voice, what would it be afraid to tell your mother?"
  • "In your family's story, who was allowed to be 'erotic,' and what happened to them?"
  • "What is the 'price' you believe you have to pay for experiencing intense desire?"
  • "When you close your eyes during intimacy, whose judgment are you trying to hide from?"

6. Clinical Application: The Genogram Interview

When conducting the genogram interview, use the following symbols and focus areas to maintain professional rigor:

  • The "Double Line": Represents an overly enmeshed relationship where sexual boundaries were blurred.
  • The "Jagged Line": Represents conflict or trauma regarding sexual identity or expression.
  • The "Dashed Line": Represents emotional distance or "the silent treatment" regarding sexual topics.

Focus on the "Transmission of Taboo." Ask: "What was the unspoken rule about nudity in your childhood home?" or "How was a woman's desire discussed by the men in your family?" This unearths the Sexual Script that the client is currently performing.
