Vague desires like "I want to want sex more" are difficult to coach. To ignite passion, we must translate abstract longing into SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Quantitative Assessment: FSFI & GRISS

To operate as a premium practitioner, you must use validated instruments to track progress. This moves your coaching from "subjective feeling" to "data-driven results." Two primary tools are the Female Sexual Function Index (FSFI) and the Golombok Rust Inventory of Sexual Satisfaction (GRISS).

1. The FSFI (Female Sexual Function Index)

The FSFI is a 19-item questionnaire assessing six domains: Desire, Arousal, Lubrication, Orgasm, Satisfaction, and Pain.

  • Clinical Cutoff: A total score of 26.55 or lower is generally considered the threshold for sexual dysfunction.
  • Coaching Application: Use this at the start of Module 1 and the end of Module 4 to demonstrate the efficacy of the Ignite Protocol.

2. The GRISS Scale

The GRISS is used for both individuals and couples. It measures 12 subscales including frequency, communication, and sensuality. It is particularly effective for identifying where the "Brakes" are located in a relationship dynamic (e.g., high scores in "Avoidance" or "Non-sensuality").

Synthesis: Bridging Trust and Ignite

A critical failure in many coaching programs is attempting to "Ignite" (Module 4) before establishing "Trust" (Module 3). In the Neurobiology of Desire, the amygdala (fear center) must be quieted before the ventral striatum (reward center) can be activated.

If a client has high "Brakes" due to a lack of emotional safety, novelty will feel threatening rather than exciting. This is why the INTIMATE Method is sequential. Trust is the container; Ignite is the contents. Without the container, the fire either goes out or burns the house down. As you conclude this module, ensure your client feels the "Secure Base" of Module 3 before they venture into the "Erotic Risk" of Module 4.

Check Your Understanding

1. According to the FSFI clinical guidelines, what is the total score cutoff below which a female client is generally considered to have sexual dysfunction?

The clinical cutoff score for the FSFI is 26.55. Scores below this indicate a higher risk of sexual dysfunction and may require closer clinical attention or medical referral.

Neurobiologically, the amygdala (threat detection) must feel safe before the reward systems (dopamine/arousal) can be fully activated. Novelty without trust can trigger a "threat" response rather than an "erotic" response.
