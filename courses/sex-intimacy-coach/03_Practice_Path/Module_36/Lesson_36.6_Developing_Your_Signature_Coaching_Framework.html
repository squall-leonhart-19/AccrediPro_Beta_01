3. Developing Your Practitioner Manifesto

A Manifesto is more than a mission statement; it is a declaration of your Clinical Philosophy. It tells the client not just what you do, but what you believe about human sexuality. In a field often clouded by shame, a strong manifesto acts as a beacon of safety.

Your manifesto should address three core areas:

1. The Nature of Pleasure

Is pleasure a luxury or a biological necessity? Your stance here dictates your coaching intensity and client selection.

2. The Role of the Body

Do you believe the mind leads the body, or the body leads the mind? This defines whether you use more Cognitive Behavioral (CBT) or Somatic tools.

3. The Responsibility of the Client

What is the "coaching contract"? Defining the client's role in their own transformation prevents burnout and establishes professional boundaries.
