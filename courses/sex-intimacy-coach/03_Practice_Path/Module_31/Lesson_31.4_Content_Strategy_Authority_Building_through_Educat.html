3. Utilizing the 'Trust' Pillar in Copywriting

Writing about taboo subjectsâ€”kink, desire discrepancy, or erectile dysfunctionâ€”requires a delicate balance. If the copy is too clinical, it lacks connection. If it is too "spicy," it feels like pornography or exploitation. The Trust Pillar in copywriting focuses on Safe Language.

To build immediate rapport, use the "Reflective Mirror" technique: Describe the internal experience of shame or frustration (The "Investigate" phase) before offering the solution. This signals to the reader that you have been in the "room" with clients who share their struggle. Avoid superlative claims (e.g., "The secret to 10-minute orgasms") and opt for process-oriented authority (e.g., "The neurobiology of the refractory period and how to navigate it").

5. The Role of Vulnerability: Relatable Authority

A common mistake in intimacy coaching is over-sharing personal trauma in an attempt to be "relatable." However, true authority is built when vulnerability is shared from a Scars, Not Wounds perspective. A "wound" is an unintegrated trauma; a "scar" is a healed experience that provides a lesson.

Use the 80/20 Authority Rule:

  • 80% Expertise: Research, client patterns (anonymized), framework application, and clinical insights.
  • 20% Relatability: Personal anecdotes that illustrate a specific point of the I.N.T.I.M.A.T.E. Method in action.
