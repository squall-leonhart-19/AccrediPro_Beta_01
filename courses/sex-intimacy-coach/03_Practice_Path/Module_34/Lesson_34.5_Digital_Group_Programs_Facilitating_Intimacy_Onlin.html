The "Proxy Touch" Exercise

In breakout rooms (ideally pairs), facilitate a 5-minute exercise where one person describes a physical sensation in their body (e.g., "I feel a tightness in my solar plexus"). The partner is instructed to place their own hand on their solar plexus, practicing "Empathic Resonance." This bridges the digital gap by using the partner's own body as a proxy for the speaker's experience.

Privacy Protocols & Recording Ethics

Facilitating intimacy online requires a radical approach to privacy. Standard coaching contracts often fall short of the nuances required for sexual wellness. You must establish a "Sanctity of the Screen" agreement.

Key Ethical Pillars:

  • The "No-Record" Zone: Sensitive shares (the "Investigate" and "Transform" portions) should never be recorded. Only the educational "lecture" portions should be archived.
  • Visual Privacy: Clients must be in a private room. No "Zooming from a coffee shop" allowed. This protects the group's collective safety.
  • The "Right to be Off-Camera": While presence is preferred, allowing a "Somatic Mute" (camera off, listening only) during high-trigger topics prevents trauma-response exits from the program.

Combatting Cohort Fatigue in 8-12 Week Programs

Digital programs suffer from "The Week 5 Dip." This is where the initial dopamine of the "Ignite" phase wears off, and the hard work of "Transform" begins. To maintain intimacy, you must implement Micro-Attunements.

The 3-2-1 Engagement Loop

To prevent drop-off, every week should include:

  • 3 Micro-Checkins: Automated or manual prompts in the community forum (e.g., "Where in your body are you feeling 'Normal' today?").
  • 2 Peer Interactions: Required responses to a "pod" member's share.
  • 1 Somatic Homework: A non-digital exercise (e.g., Sensory Focus touch) to be reported back on.
