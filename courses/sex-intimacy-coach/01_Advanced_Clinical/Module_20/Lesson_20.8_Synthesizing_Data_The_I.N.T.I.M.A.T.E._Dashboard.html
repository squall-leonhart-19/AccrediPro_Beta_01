3. Integrating Quantitative vs. Qualitative Data

Effective synthesis requires the marriage of Hard Metrics (scores from the CSI-16 or FSFI) and Soft Somatics (the client's body language during session). If a client scores a 9/10 on "Trust" in a written survey but crosses their arms and avoids eye contact when discussing their partner, the Dashboard must reflect this Incongruence Score.

We use a dual-layer scoring system:

  • Layer 1: Objective Score (What the client reports).
  • Layer 2: Practitioner Observation (The somatic and narrative subtext).

When these layers diverge, we have found the "Growth Edge"â€”the specific area where the client's conscious narrative hasn't yet caught up to their nervous system's reality.

4. Relational Connection Mapping

For couples, the Dashboard becomes a Connection Map. By overlaying two individual dashboards, we can visualize "Relational Friction Points." If Partner A is high in Ignite (Dopamine-driven novelty) but Partner B is low in Trust (Oxytocin-driven safety), they will constantly trigger each otherâ€™s "brakes."

A 2023 meta-analysis (n=1,450 couples) demonstrated that couples who received a visual representation of their "intimacy gaps" showed a 22% higher retention rate in coaching than those receiving verbal feedback alone. 5. Designing the 90-Day "Transform & Expand" Roadmap

Once the Dashboard is complete, you move to the Architectural Phase. You are no longer just exploring; you are building. A standard 90-day roadmap is divided into three 30-day "Sprints":

Month 1: The Foundation (Normalize & Trust)

Focus on de-shaming and building the secure base. If the Dashboard shows low "Normalize" scores, Month 1 is heavy on psychoeducation and deconstructing cultural scripts.

Month 2: The Shift (Mindful Presence & Attune)

Moving from head to body. This month focuses on somatic exercises, interoceptive awareness, and "Sensate Focus" protocols. We are rewiring the nervous system for pleasure.

Month 3: The Expansion (Ignite & Expand)

Integrating novelty, advanced pleasure mapping, and erotic intelligence. This is where we move from "healing" to "thriving."

6. Final Case Study Synthesis: The Full Cycle

Consider Marcus (42), who presented with "Performance Anxiety."

  • Initial Dashboard: High "Investigate" (he knew why it happened), but 2/10 on "Mindful Presence" (he was 'spectatoring' constantly).
  • Intervention: Instead of focusing on "Ignite" (which would increase pressure), the coach synthesized that his "Mindful Presence" was the bottleneck.
  • Outcome: After 60 days of sensory focus and interoceptive training, his "Mindful Presence" score rose to 8/10.
  • Re-evaluation: Performance anxiety decreased by 85% without ever "treating" the anxiety directly. The Dashboard revealed that the lack of presence was the root cause.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of data synthesis and dashboarding.

1

What is the primary purpose of an "Incongruence Score" on the I.N.T.I.M.A.T.E. Dashboard?

It identifies discrepancies between a client's cognitive report (what they say) and their somatic/behavioral reality (what their body shows). This often highlights the "growth edge" or a hidden trauma response that needs addressing before moving to arousal-based work.
2

In a 90-day roadmap, why is "Ignite" usually placed in the final month rather than the first?

"Ignite" focuses on arousal and novelty. Without a foundation of "Trust," "Normalization," and "Mindful Presence," increasing arousal can often trigger the nervous system's "brakes" (anxiety, shame, or shutdown). We build the container before turning up the heat.

Key Takeaways

  • Synthesis is Strategy: Raw data must be converted into a visual Dashboard to provide a clear roadmap for the client.
  • The 8 Pillars: Every client should be assessed across Investigate, Normalize, Trust, Ignite, Mindful Presence, Attune, Transform, and Expand.
  • Visual Feedback: Using Connection Maps for couples increases engagement and reduces defensiveness by externalizing the "intimacy gaps."
  • The 90-Day Sprint: Structure interventions in 30-day blocks, moving from foundational safety to somatic presence, and finally to erotic expansion.
