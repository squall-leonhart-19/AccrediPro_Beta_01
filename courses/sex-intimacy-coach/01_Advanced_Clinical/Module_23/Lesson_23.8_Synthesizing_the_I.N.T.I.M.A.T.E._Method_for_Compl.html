3. Advanced Ethics: Transference & Somatic Boundaries

As an L2 coach, you are working deeper in the body and the psyche. This proximity often triggers transferenceâ€”where the client projects feelings for a significant figure onto the coachâ€”or counter-transference, where the coachâ€™s own history influences their perception of the client. In intimacy coaching, erotic transference is a common risk due to the vulnerable nature of the topics discussed.

The Three-Layer Boundary Protocol

  • Professional Boundaries: Clear contracts, no contact outside sessions, and strict adherence to the scope of practice (coaching vs. therapy).
  • Emotional Boundaries: Recognizing when a client is becoming emotionally dependent and redirecting them to their own internal resources (Self-Attunement).
  • Somatic Boundaries: In somatic-heavy coaching, ensuring all "touch" exercises are client-led or performed on themselves, maintaining the coach as a witness/facilitator rather than a participant.
