2. Case Formulation vs. Treatment Planning

One of the most common errors in sexual wellness coaching is jumping straight to the "fix" (Treatment Planning) before understanding the "structure" (Case Formulation). In the I.N.T.I.M.A.T.E. Methodâ„¢, Case Formulation occurs primarily during the Investigate and Normalize phases, while Treatment Planning is the roadmap for the remaining six phases.

Feature Case Formulation (The "Why") Treatment Planning (The "How")
Primary Focus Identifying the underlying mechanisms (e.g., attachment style, sexual scripts). Selecting specific exercises and behavioral shifts to address symptoms.
Methodology Phase Investigate, Normalize. Trust, Ignite, Mindful Presence, Attune, Transform, Expand.
Outcome A hypothesis of why the client is stuck. A chronological list of interventions and milestones.
Flexibility Static (refines as more history is revealed). Dynamic (adjusts based on client response to exercises).

3. Mapping Symptoms to Methodology Phases

Not every client needs equal time in every phase. A high-performing coach maps the client's specific presentation to the phase most likely to yield a "therapeutic breakthrough." For example, a client with Hypoactive Sexual Desire Disorder (HSDD) may require an extended Ignite phase, whereas a couple recovering from infidelity may spend 60% of their time in the Trust and Attune phases.

Strategic Mapping Examples:

  • Erectile Dissatisfaction (Psychogenic): Focus on Normalize (reducing performance anxiety) and Mindful Presence (moving from "spectatoring" to somatic awareness).
  • Anorgasmia: Focus on Investigate (physical/medication checks) and Expand (neurological pleasure capacity).
  • Vaginismus/Genito-Pelvic Pain: Focus on Trust (nervous system safety) and Transform (reframing pain-fear cycles).

4. Establishing Baseline Metrics: The Science of Tracking

To be an expert practitioner, you must move beyond subjective reports of "feeling better." We utilize validated clinical scales to establish a quantitative baseline. This allows you to track progress across the I.N.T.I.M.A.T.E. spectrum and provides the client with tangible evidence of growth.

Key Assessment Tools:

  1. Female Sexual Function Index (FSFI): A 19-item scale measuring desire, arousal, lubrication, orgasm, satisfaction, and pain. A score of 26.55 or less is generally the cutoff for sexual dysfunction.
  2. International Index of Erectile Function (IIEF-5): A gold-standard tool for assessing erectile quality. Scores range from 5-25; scores below 21 indicate varying degrees of ED.
  3. The Sexual Relationship Scale (SRS): Measures the relational component of sexual wellness, correlating strongly with the Attune and Trust phases.

A 2022 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that clients who reviewed their baseline metrics monthly showed a 19% increase in treatment compliance. Data acts as a mirror, reflecting the efficacy of the interventions in the Ignite and Transform phases.

5. The 'Investigate' Phase: Identifying Contraindications

Operationalizing the method requires a "Safety First" filter. During the Investigate phase, the coach must be vigilant for contraindications that require a referral to a medical or mental health professional. Intimacy coaching is not therapy, and knowing the boundary is a hallmark of the AccrediPro expert.

Red Flags for Immediate Referral:

  • Sudden Onset Dysfunction: If erectile or arousal issues appear overnight without psychological triggers, refer to a urologist/gynecologist (potential cardiovascular or endocrine issues).
  • Active Trauma Processing: If the Investigate phase unearths un-integrated sexual trauma (PTSD), the client must see a trauma-informed therapist before proceeding to the Ignite or Expand phases.
  • Severe Relationship Violence: The Attune and Trust phases cannot be operationalized in an environment of physical or emotional abuse.

6. Building the Chronological Roadmap

A treatment plan is essentially a calendar of the I.N.T.I.M.A.T.E. Method. While the phases are conceptual, their application is chronological. Below is the standard AccrediPro template for an 8-week intensive.

History taking, baseline scales (FSFI/IIEF), deconstructing shame, and setting the "Safe Container."
Weeks 3-4: The Relational Bridge (Trust & Mindful Presence)
Somatic grounding exercises, non-demand touch (Sensate Focus), and attachment-based safety building.
Weeks 5-6: The Erotic Engine (Ignite & Attune)
Identifying accelerators/brakes, responsive desire education, and co-regulating during intimacy.
Weeks 7-8: Integration & Growth (Transform & Expand)
Cognitive reframing of limiting beliefs and advanced pleasure mapping for long-term sustainability.
