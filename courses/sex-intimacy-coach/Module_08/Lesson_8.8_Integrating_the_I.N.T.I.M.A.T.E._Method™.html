To provide premium value, coaches must move beyond "How do you feel?" and into evidence-based reporting. We utilize the Intimacy Progress Index (IPI), which tracks progress across four key domains:

4. The Practitionerâ€™s Ethics of Expansion

As clients reach the "Expand" phase, they often enter states of high vulnerability and "erotic opening." This requires the practitioner to maintain Impeccable Boundaries. Expansion coaching is not an excuse for the coach to become a "guru" or to overstep into the client's personal erotic choices.

Key Ethical Guardrails:

1. Avoidance of Transference: Recognizing when a client begins to project their newfound erotic vitality onto the coach. Practitioners must use Somatic Counter-transference tracking to stay grounded.

2. The "Deep End" Rule: Never encourage a client to expand into a practice (e.g., Kink, non-monogamy) that they do not have the Trust or Attunement foundation to support. Expansion without safety leads to retraumatization.

Neuroplasticity research suggests that approximately 90 days are required to weaken old neural pathways (the "brakes") and strengthen new behavioral and somatic responses (the "accelerators") into a permanent "new normal."
