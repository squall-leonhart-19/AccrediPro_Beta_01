5. Overcoming 'Upper Limit Problems'

A significant barrier to expansion is what psychologist Gay Hendricks calls the Upper Limit Problem. This is an internal thermostat that determines how much love, joy, and pleasure we allow ourselves to experience. When we exceed this limit, we often unconsciously "self-sabotage" to bring ourselves back to a familiar (though lower) level of happiness.

In intimacy, this manifests as:

  • Picking a fight right after a profound moment of connection.
  • Feeling "weird" or "guilty" after a particularly intense orgasm.
  • Sudden dissociation or "numbing out" when pleasure reaches a certain threshold.
