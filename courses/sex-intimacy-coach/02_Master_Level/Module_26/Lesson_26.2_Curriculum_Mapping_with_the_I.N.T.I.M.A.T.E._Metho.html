Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of the curriculum mapping process.

1. Why is it scientifically risky to map "Ignite" (arousal) strategies before "Normalize" (safety) strategies?

Introducing arousal before safety can trigger a sympathetic nervous system "threat" response. Without normalization, the client often feels "broken," leading to high cortisol levels that biologically inhibit the parasympathetic response required for sexual arousal and pleasure.

2. What is the primary difference between the "Mindful Presence" phase and the "Expand" phase in the curriculum arc?

"Mindful Presence" focuses on grounding, interoception, and overcoming distraction in the present moment (foundational somatic work). "Expand" focuses on increasing pleasure capacity, exploring novelty, and erotic intelligence (advanced thriving work).
