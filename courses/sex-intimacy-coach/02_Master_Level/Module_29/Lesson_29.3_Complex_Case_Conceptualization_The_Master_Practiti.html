2. Differential Coaching vs. Pathology

One of the hallmarks of a Master Practitioner is knowing when not to coach. Differential coaching requires a deep understanding of the boundaries between psychological distress and clinical pathology. While we work within the Normalize (N) and Transform (T) phases, certain symptoms mandate a medical or psychotherapeutic referral.

Presentation Coaching Appropriate Pathology / Clinical Referral
Low Desire Stress-related, boredom, or communication blocks. Sudden onset with suicidal ideation or hormonal collapse.
Sexual Pain Psychosomatic tension or lack of arousal education. Endometriosis, active infections, or severe vaginismus.
Arousal Issues Performance anxiety or "spectatoring." Consistent erectile dysfunction despite zero anxiety.
Trauma Integration of past events into current intimacy. Active PTSD, dissociation, or unmanaged flashbacks.

Ethical Boundary Note

Master Practitioners maintain a "Referral Circle" of at least 3-5 clinical specialists (Urologists, Gynecologists, Trauma Therapists). Attempting to coach through a clinical pathology is not only ineffective but potentially harmful to the client's long-term sexual health.
