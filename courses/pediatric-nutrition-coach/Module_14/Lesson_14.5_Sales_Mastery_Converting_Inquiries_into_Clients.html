The High-Ticket Discovery Call Framework

A successful enrollment call is structured to move the parent from a state of anxiety to a state of empowerment. Following the NURTURE Methodâ„¢, your discovery call should follow this 6-step architecture:

1. The Frame & Rapport (3 Minutes)

Set the agenda. "My goal today is to understand what's happening with [Child's Name], see if my NURTURE Method is a fit, and if so, show you what that looks like. Does that sound good?" This establishes professional authority immediately.

2. Discovery: The Root-Cause Assessment (10 Minutes)

Ask deep, "Why" questions. Don't just ask what they eat. Ask: "How does [Symptom] affect your family's stress levels at dinner?" This uncovers the emotional cost of the problem.

3. The Gap Analysis (5 Minutes)

Help them visualize the "After." "If we could resolve this digestive issue and get [Child] sleeping through the night, how would your life change three months from now?"

4. Prescription & The NURTURE Method (5 Minutes)

Comparison: Transactional vs. Transformational Sales

Many coaches fail because they sell "hours" or "sessions." High-ticket practitioners sell outcomes. Use the following table to audit your current sales language.

Transactional (Low-Value)

Objection Handling with Empathy

In pediatric coaching, objections are rarely about the money itself; they are about certainty. The parent is asking: "Will this actually work for my child?"

Pro Tip: The "Feel, Felt, Found" Technique

"I understand how you feel about the investment. Many of the parents I work with felt the same way initially, especially after trying so many other things. What they found was that by addressing the root cause through the NURTURE Method, they actually saved thousands in the long run on supplements and doctor visits that weren't working."

Common Objections in Pediatric Coaching:

  • "I need to talk to my spouse": This usually means they aren't confident enough to "sell" it to their partner. Offer a 3-way call or a specific "Spouse PDF" that outlines the ROI of the program.
  • "My child is too picky, they won't do this": Address this through Transitional Habit Formation (the T in NURTURE). Explain that your method is designed specifically for resistant children.
  • "It's too expensive": Shift the focus from cost to investment. "What is the cost of not fixing this for the next two years?"
