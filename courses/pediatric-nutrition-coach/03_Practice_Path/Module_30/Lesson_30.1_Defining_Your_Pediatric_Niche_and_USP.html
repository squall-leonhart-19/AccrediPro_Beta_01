4. Conducting Market Gap Analysis

A Market Gap Analysis identifies where the current healthcare system is failing parents. In pediatric nutrition, these gaps usually occur at the intersection of "clinical diagnosis" and "daily implementation."

To find your gap, follow the R.I.D.E. Framework:

  1. Review: Look at local pediatricians. Do they provide nutrition support, or just a "wait and see" approach?
  2. Interview: Speak to 5 parents in your target niche. What is the one thing their doctor *didn't* tell them?
  3. Detect: Identify the "Google Search Void." Are parents searching for "help with toddler picky eating" but finding only generic listicles?
  4. Evaluate: Can the G.R.O.W. Method bridge this specific gap?

6. Revenue Models: Specialist vs. Generalist Scalability

The financial math of a specialized pediatric practice is fundamentally different from a generalist one. Specialists can command higher "Value-Based Pricing" because the perceived risk of *not* hiring them is higher.

The Generalist Trap

  • High competition on price
  • Exhausting "constant hunting" for new leads
  • Low referral authority
  • Average Hourly: $60-$90

The Specialist Advantage

  • Premium "Expert" pricing
  • Referrals from doctors/OTs/SLPs

  • Average Hourly: $150-$350

Check Your Understanding

1. Why does a "specialist" model typically yield higher retention rates in pediatric nutrition?

Specialists address the "Parental Urgency Factor" by solving a specific, high-stress pain point (like ASD feeding or allergies) that generalists often overlook. This builds higher trust and perceived value, leading to better compliance and longer engagement.

2. How does the "G" in the G.R.O.W. Method serve as a USP for a sports nutrition niche?

In sports nutrition, the "G" (Growth Velocity) ensures that performance goals do not compromise biological growth. Highlighting this prevents the common mistake of under-fueling young athletes, positioning you as a "safety-first" expert compared to general sports coaches.
