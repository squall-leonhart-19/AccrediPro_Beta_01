The Professional Code of Conduct

As an AccrediPro Certified Pediatric Nutrition Coach, your primary ethical obligation is the safety and thriving of the child, followed closely by the support of the family unit. Unlike general health coaching, pediatric ethics require a heightened sense of responsibility because the "client" (the child) often cannot provide informed consent and relies on the coach and parents to act in their best interest.

The AccrediPro Standard is built upon four pillars of ethical practice:

  • Beneficence: Acting in the best interest of the child‚Äôs physiological and psychological development.
  • Non-maleficence: "First, do no harm"‚Äîavoiding restrictive diets that could trigger growth stunting or eating disorders.
  • Autonomy: Respecting the child's internal cues (sDOR) while supporting parental leadership.
  • Justice: Providing equitable, evidence-based care regardless of socioeconomic status or neurodiversity.

Professional Liability & Risk Management

Operating at Level 4 (L4) means you are handling complex clinical reasoning. This increases your professional risk profile. Navigating Professional Liability Insurance is a mandatory step before accepting your first paying client. You must distinguish between General Liability (trips and falls) and Professional Liability (errors and omissions in your coaching advice).

Key risk management strategies for the Pediatric Coach:

  1. The "Not a Doctor" Disclaimer: Every document must clearly state that you do not diagnose or treat medical conditions.
  2. Mandatory Referral Triggers: If a child‚Äôs growth falls below the 2nd percentile or crosses two major centile lines, you must refer to a pediatrician immediately (Refer to Module 1, L4).
  3. Insurance Tail Coverage: Ensure your policy covers you even after you stop practicing for a specific period, as pediatric claims can sometimes surface years later.

Check Your Understanding

1. A parent asks you to interpret their child's blood panel and suggest specific high-dose supplements to "cure" their child's ADHD. What is the ethical L4 response?

The coach should state that interpreting labs for the purpose of diagnosis or "curing" a condition is outside the scope of practice. The coach can, however, use the labs to identify potential micronutrient gaps (Remediate Deficiencies) and provide a summary for the parent to discuss with their pediatrician, focusing on nutritional support for brain health rather than a "cure."

2. Which digital communication method is considered HIPAA-compliant for sending a growth chart?

A secure, encrypted client portal (e.g., Practice Better) with a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA) in place. Standard email and SMS are not compliant.
