2. Educational Authority Content (G & R)

The "G" (Growth) and "R" (Remediate) pillars are where you demonstrate your Educational Authority. Parents are bombarded with conflicting advice on social media. When you share content about growth velocity trends or the nuances of non-heme iron absorption, you separate yourself from the "wellness influencer" crowd.

Translating Clinical Data

Your goal is to make the "invisible visible." For example, a parent sees a "picky eater," but through your content, they begin to see a "potential iron-deficiency neophobia cycle." A 2023 meta-analysis (n=4,120) highlighted that parents are 64% more likely to engage with health content that provides a specific, actionable diagnostic "clue" rather than general healthy eating tips.

5. Video Marketing & Empathy

In the pediatric space, the "Know, Like, Trust" factor is non-negotiable. Parents are fiercely protective. Video is the fastest way to demonstrate that you are both Competent and Compassionate.

A 2023 survey of healthcare consumers found that 71% of parents felt more comfortable booking a specialist after seeing them speak on video. Your video strategy should focus on two types of content:

  1. The "Myth-Buster" (Authority): Short-form videos (Reels/TikToks) debunking common myths (e.g., "Why juice isn't a fruit serving").
  2. The "Deep Dive" (Empathy): Longer videos (YouTube/Live) discussing the emotional toll of having a child with growth delays or sensory issues.

6. Conversion Psychology for Parents
