3. Sales Psychology: The 'Time vs. Money' Paradigm

When selling to parents, you are rarely competing against other coaches; you are competing against the Opportunity Cost of Inaction. The objection "it's too expensive" is often a mask for "I don't believe this will work faster than what I'm doing for free."

4. Social Media Ethics & Compliance

Marketing pediatric health services carries a higher ethical burden than marketing fitness or general wellness. You must navigate the tension between "persuasive marketing" and "medical misinformation."

Key compliance factors include:

  • The "No Cure" Rule: Never promise to "cure" ADHD, Autism, or clinical deficiencies. Use terms like "support," "optimize," and "remediate gaps."
  • Privacy & Consent: Even with fictionalized names, ensure case studies do not contain enough PII (Personally Identifiable Information) to identify a child.
  • Scope of Practice: Your marketing must clearly state that you are a Nutrition Coach, not a medical doctor or licensed dietitian (unless you hold those credentials).

5. SEO Strategies: Local vs. Global Intent

Your Search Engine Optimization (SEO) strategy must align with your business model. A local practitioner needs different keywords than a global consultant.

Local Search Intent (The "Near Me" Economy)

If you see clients in person, your SEO must be hyper-local. A 2023 analysis showed that 46% of all Google searches have local intent. Keywords should look like: "Pediatric Nutritionist in [City]" or "Picky eating help [Neighborhood]."

Global/Niche Search Intent (The "Problem" Economy)

If you operate virtually, you compete on Topic Authority. Keywords should focus on specific pathologies or frameworks: "G.R.O.W. Method for pediatric iron deficiency" or "Nutrition for sensory-sensitive toddlers."

6. The Authority Flywheel

Authority is not static; it is a flywheel that gains momentum. As you apply the G.R.O.W. Method, your results lead to better case studies, which lead to higher-tier content, which leads to media opportunities and referrals.

To scale, you must move from Active Marketing (posting daily) to Passive Authority (published assets). This includes guesting on pediatric podcasts, contributing to health journals, and developing white papers that other practitioners (pediatricians, OTs) can use as resources.
