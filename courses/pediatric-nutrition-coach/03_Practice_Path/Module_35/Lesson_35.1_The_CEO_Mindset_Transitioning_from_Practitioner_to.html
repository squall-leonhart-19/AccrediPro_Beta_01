5. The Economics of Pediatric Scaling

To scale confidently, you must understand two critical metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV). In pediatric nutrition, LTV is often higher than in adult weight loss because families stay with a trusted advisor through multiple developmental stages (infancy to school age).

The Scaling Formula:

LTV : CAC Ratio â€” A healthy scaling practice should aim for a ratio of 3:1 or higher. If it costs you $200 to acquire a client (CAC) and they generate $1,200 in revenue (LTV), your ratio is 6:1, indicating high scalability.
