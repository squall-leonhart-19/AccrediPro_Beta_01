3. Optimal vs. Normal: The Flare/Remission Spectrum

The "Normal" range provided by labs is a statistical average of the population, which includes many undiagnosed hypothyroid individuals. The "Optimal" range is where the thyroid-brain feedback loop functions with minimal friction. However, during a Hashimoto's flare, these ranges must be interpreted with nuance.

The "Leaking" Phenomenon

During an acute autoimmune attack, thyroid cells (thyrocytes) are destroyed, releasing pre-formed T4 and T3 into the bloodstream. This can cause a temporary drop in TSH (sometimes below 0.5 mIU/L) and a spike in fT4. A client might feel anxious or suffer from heart palpitations. This is not "hyperthyroidism" in the traditional sense, but rather "Hashitoxicosis."
