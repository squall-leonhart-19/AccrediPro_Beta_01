Hourly vs. Value-Based Models

The "Hourly Trap" is the leading cause of practitioner burnout. When you charge $100 per hour, you must find 20 clients a week to make a decent living, leading to a "treadmill" effect where you have no time for deep study or practice growth. More importantly, hourly coaching encourages clients to "pay as they go," making them more likely to quit when they hit the common 3-week "healing crisis" or plateau.

ROI & Outcome Projection

When a client says "it's too expensive," they are usually saying "I don't see the value yet." You must shift the conversation to the Cost of Inaction. A person with unmanaged Hashimoto's or hypothyroidism often spends thousands of dollars annually on:

  • Ineffective supplements ($100-$300/mo)
  • Copays for specialists who offer no solutions ($500+/yr)
  • Lost productivity/Sick days ($2,000-$5,000+/yr)
  • Weight loss programs that fail due to low T3 ($1,000+/yr)

A $2,500 investment that resolves these issues actually saves the client money over a 24-month period. This is the "Health ROI" you must communicate during your discovery calls.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is hourly coaching considered detrimental to client outcomes in thyroid restoration?

It encourages a "transactional" relationship where clients are more likely to quit during the early, difficult stages of healing. Packages provide the "container" necessary for the long-term physiological changes (like antibody reduction) required in the THRIVE method.

2. What is the primary benefit of the "Tiered Pricing" model for the practitioner?

It utilizes the "Center-Stage Effect," where most clients will select the middle (Gold) option, while still providing an entry point for those with lower budgets and a premium option for those wanting high-touch support.
