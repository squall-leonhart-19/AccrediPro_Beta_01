2. The Critical Medical Disclaimer & Assumption of Risk

Thyroid coaching involves discussing physiological processes‚ÄîHPT axis function, nutrient synergy, and autoimmune triggers. This creates a "gray zone" where clients may confuse coaching for medical treatment. Your contract must include a layered disclaimer system.

The Assumption of Risk Clause

Because you are guiding clients through dietary changes (such as the AIP framework) and supplement optimization (like Selenium or Zinc), the client must acknowledge that they are voluntarily assuming the risk of these changes. This clause should explicitly state that the client has been advised to consult their primary care physician (PCP) or endocrinologist before implementing any suggestions.

Clause Type Legal Purpose Thyroid-Specific Example
Medical Disclaimer Prevents "Unlicensed Practice of Medicine" claims. "Coach is not an endocrinologist; services do not replace medical TSH monitoring."
Assumption of Risk Shifts liability for physiological reactions to the client. "Client assumes all risk associated with dietary changes or nutritional supplementation."
Indemnification Protects coach from third-party lawsuits. "Client agrees to hold Coach harmless from any damages resulting from services."

4. Structuring 'Termination for Cause' Clauses

Not every client is a good fit. In thyroid coaching, a "high-liability client" is one who ignores medical advice, stops their Levothyroxine without doctor supervision, or becomes verbally abusive. You need a "trap door" to exit the relationship safely.

A Termination for Cause clause allows you to fire a client immediately if they breach specific terms. For Thyroid Coaches, these "causes" should include:

  • Failure to maintain a relationship with a licensed medical provider.
  • Self-adjusting prescription medication against the Coach's explicit written warnings.
  • Dishonesty regarding health history or current symptoms.
  • Non-payment or persistent "no-shows" for scheduled sessions.

5. Payment Terms, Refunds, and Chargeback Protection

High-ticket thyroid transformation programs (often ranging from $2,500 to $7,500) are susceptible to "Buyer‚Äôs Remorse." To protect your cash flow, your contract must be explicit about financial obligations.

Chargeback Defense: Credit card chargebacks are a significant threat to online coaches. Your agreement should include a clause where the client waives the right to dispute a charge if the services were delivered as described. Including a "Refund Policy" that requires a specific "Notice of Dissatisfaction" period can help win 90% of merchant bank disputes.

PRO TIP: THE 72-HOUR RULE

Always include a "Cooling Off" period (usually 72 hours) where a client can cancel for a full refund. After this window, transition to a "No Refund" or "Prorated Refund" policy. This demonstrates "Good Faith" and is highly favored by courts and payment processors like Stripe or PayPal.

6. Digital Execution: E-Signatures and Legal Validity

In a global coaching practice, you will rarely meet a client in person to sign a physical paper. You must use ESIGN Act and UETA compliant platforms (e.g., DocuSign, HelloSign, or PandaDoc).

Click-Wrap vs. Browse-Wrap: A "Click-Wrap" agreement (where the client must click "I Agree" before paying) is significantly more enforceable than a "Browse-Wrap" agreement (where the terms are just a link at the bottom of the page). For high-ticket thyroid coaching, a full signature on a standalone document is the gold standard for protection.

2. What is the difference between a "Limited License" and "Ownership" in an IP clause?

A "Limited License" means the client can use the B.A.L.A.N.C.E.‚Ñ¢ materials for their own health, but you (the coach) retain "Ownership." Without this distinction, the client could argue they "bought" the rights to the methodology and could resell it.
