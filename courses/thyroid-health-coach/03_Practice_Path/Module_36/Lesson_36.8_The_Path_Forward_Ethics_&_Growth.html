The AccrediPro Practitioner Code of Ethics

As you transition from student to certified practitioner, your commitment to ethical conduct becomes the bedrock of your reputation. In the specialized field of thyroid health, where clients often arrive after years of medical gaslighting and complex physiological distress, the power dynamic between coach and client is significant.

The AccrediPro Practitioner Code of Ethics is designed to protect both the client and the coach. A 2022 survey of health coaching ethics found that 64% of legal challenges in the wellness industry arise from a lack of clear professional boundaries or "scope creep." By adhering to these standards, you safeguard your practice against liability while providing the highest level of care.

Core Ethical Pillars:

  • Primum Non Nocere (First, Do No Harm): Always prioritize client safety, especially when recommending supplements that may interact with thyroid medications (e.g., Iodine or high-dose Selenium).
  • Informed Consent: Clients must understand the B.A.L.A.N.C.E. Thyroid Method‚Ñ¢ is a supportive framework, not a replacement for medical diagnosis or treatment.
  • Autonomy: Respect the client's right to make their own health decisions, even if they differ from your recommendations.
  • Confidentiality (HIPAA Compliance): Maintaining the privacy of lab results and personal health history is mandatory.

1. A client asks for your opinion on increasing their Levothyroxine dosage because they still feel fatigued. What is the ethically correct response according to the AccrediPro Code?

The correct response is to refer the client back to their prescribing physician. As a coach, you can help the client prepare a list of symptoms and lab results (like Free T3/rT3 ratios) to discuss with their doctor, but you must never recommend dosage changes, as this falls outside the coaching scope of practice.

Because practitioner burnout (HPA-axis dysfunction) not only affects the coach's health but also their clinical decision-making and ability to hold space for complex thyroid cases. Modeling the B.A.L.A.N.C.E. method increases practitioner longevity and authenticity.
