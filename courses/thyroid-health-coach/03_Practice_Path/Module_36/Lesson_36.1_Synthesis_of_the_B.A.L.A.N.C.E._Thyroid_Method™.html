2. Interconnectivity of Systemic Pillars

Expert coaching requires understanding the biochemical domino effect. For example, a failure in the 'L' (Lifestyle/Stress) pillar increases cortisol. High cortisol inhibits 5'-deiodinase activity, which directly impacts the 'B' (Baseline) results by increasing Reverse T3 (rT3) and lowering Free T3.

84.2% of patients with subclinical hypothyroidism show improvement in T3 levels when Magnesium and Selenium (N) are optimized alongside stress management (L).

Consider the synergy between 'C' (Clear Toxins) and 'N' (Nutrient Optimization). Heavy metals like mercury have a high affinity for selenium. If a client is toxic (C), their selenium (N) is "sequestered" to neutralize the metal, leaving none for the deiodinase enzymes. You cannot fix the nutrient deficiency without addressing the toxic load.
