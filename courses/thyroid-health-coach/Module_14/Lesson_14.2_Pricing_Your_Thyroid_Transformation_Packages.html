Value-Based Pricing

Setting prices based on the perceived or estimated value of a product or service to the customer rather than according to the cost of the product or historical prices.

The Hourly Trap

A pricing model where practitioners trade time for money, often leading to burnout and client non-compliance due to lack of perceived commitment.

ROI (Return on Investment)

In health coaching, this refers to the financial and quality-of-life gains a client receives (e.g., reduced medication costs, increased productivity) relative to the coaching fee.

High-Ticket Package

A comprehensive program typically priced between $1,500 and $5,000 that includes multiple touchpoints, assessments, and support over a set duration.

The Psychology of Pricing in Thyroid Health

Thyroid patients are often the "underserved" of the medical community. A 2021 survey of 1,200 thyroid patients found that 68% felt their concerns were dismissed by primary care providers. When a client comes to you, they aren't looking for an hour of your time; they are looking for the end of their fatigue, brain fog, and weight gain.

Psychologically, a higher financial investment correlates with higher therapeutic compliance. When a client pays $2,500 for a 3-month program, they are "all in." They are more likely to follow the dietary protocols, take the recommended supplements, and prioritize sleep. Conversely, a $100 "pay-as-you-go" session makes it easy for the client to cancel when life gets busy, stalling their progress and damaging your reputation as a practitioner who gets results.

Handling Pricing Objections

Objections are rarely about the money; they are about certainty. If a client says "I can't afford it," they are often saying "I'm not sure if this will work for me." Use the following strategies:

The "Cost of Inaction" Reframe

"I understand this is an investment. However, what is the cost of staying exactly where you are for another year? What does it cost your family, your career, and your joy if your energy doesn't return?"

The Payment Plan Strategy

Offering a 3-month or 6-month payment plan (e.g., $850/month for 3 months) can lower the barrier to entry while maintaining the total package value. Never discount your base price; instead, offer flexible terms.
