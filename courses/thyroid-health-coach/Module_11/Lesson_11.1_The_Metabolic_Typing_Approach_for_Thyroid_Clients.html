Oxidation Rate

The speed at which your cells convert nutrients (carbohydrates, fats, proteins) into energy (ATP) through the Krebs cycle.

Autonomic Dominance

The tendency for an individual's physiology to be governed more by either the Sympathetic or Parasympathetic nervous system.

A specific pattern of blood glucose response to various foods, which varies significantly between individuals regardless of thyroid status.

Specific Dynamic Action (SDA)

The energy expenditure above the resting metabolic rate due to the cost of processing food for use and storage.

Autonomic vs. Oxidative Systems

Metabolic typing is primarily determined by two fundamental control systems that regulate energy production and internal balance. As a coach, identifying which system is "dominant" or "imbalanced" is the first step in the H (Hormonal Synergy) phase of the THRIVE method.

1. The Autonomic Nervous System (ANS): This system regulates involuntary functions.
â€¢ Sympathetic Dominance: Often associated with "Fast" metabolism but can lead to thyroid receptor resistance due to chronic cortisol elevation.
â€¢ Parasympathetic Dominance: Often associated with "Slow" metabolism and sluggish thyroid output.

2. The Oxidative System: This relates to the rate at which cells convert fuel into energy.
â€¢ Fast Oxidizers: Burn through carbohydrates too quickly, leading to blood sugar crashes that stress the adrenals and thyroid.
â€¢ Slow Oxidizers: Have a "clogged" metabolic engine where carbohydrates are not efficiently converted to ATP, often leading to T3 pooling or low cellular T3 uptake.

The Endocrine Triangle Synergy

In the H (Hormonal Synergy & Axis Balance) phase of the THRIVE Method, we look at the relationship between the Thyroid, Adrenals, and Blood Sugar (The Endocrine Triangle). Metabolic typing is the glue that holds this triangle together.

When a client eats for their type, they minimize glycemic variability. High glycemic variability triggers cortisol release from the adrenals. Excessive cortisol inhibits 5'-deiodinase, the enzyme responsible for converting T4 into active T3, and increases the production of Reverse T3 (rT3). Therefore, metabolic typing is not just a "diet"â€”it is a clinical strategy to downregulate Reverse T3 and upregulate cellular metabolism.

Key Takeaways

  • Bio-Individuality: There is no single "Thyroid Diet"; the best diet is the one that matches the client's oxidation rate.
  • The Two Systems: Metabolic typing balances the Autonomic Nervous System and the Cellular Oxidative System.
  • Macro Ratios: Slow oxidizers need higher protein/complex carbs and lower fat; Fast oxidizers need higher fat/heavy protein and lower carbs.
  • The Goal: Metabolic typing aims to improve T4 to T3 conversion by stabilizing the Endocrine Triangle.
