4. Circadian Rhythm Disruption: The Timing of the Attack

Our immune system follows a strict circadian clock. Pro-inflammatory cytokines like IL-12 and IFN-gamma naturally peak during the early morning hours, which is why many Hashimotoâ€™s patients feel their worst upon waking. When a clientâ€™s circadian rhythm is disrupted (due to blue light at night, irregular sleep, or late-night eating), the "clock genes" in their T-cells become desynchronized.

This desynchrony leads to a failure in T-regulatory (Treg) cell function. Tregs are the "peacekeepers" of the immune system. Without proper circadian signaling, Treg cells cannot effectively suppress the self-reactive T-cells that attack the thyroid gland, leading to frequent and unpredictable autoimmune flares.
