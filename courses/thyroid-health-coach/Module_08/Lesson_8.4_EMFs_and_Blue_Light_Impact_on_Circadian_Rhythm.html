    Oxidative Stress & TPO Antibodies

    The thyroid is a "pro-oxidant" environment by nature; it requires hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) to oxidize iodide into iodine. This process is tightly controlled by the enzyme Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO). However, when external EMFs increase systemic oxidative stress, the thyroid's internal antioxidant defenses (like Glutathione Peroxidase) become overwhelmed.

    When TPO is damaged by excessive free radicals (ROS), its structure changes. The immune system then recognizes this altered protein as a foreign invader, leading to the production of TPO Antibodies. This is why we often see "unexplained" antibody spikes in clients who live near high-voltage power lines or cell towers, even when their diet is "perfect."
