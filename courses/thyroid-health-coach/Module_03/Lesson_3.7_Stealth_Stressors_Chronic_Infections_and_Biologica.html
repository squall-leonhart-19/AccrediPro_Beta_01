2. Latent Viral Loads: EBV and HHV-6

The Epstein-Barr Virus (EBV) is a primary suspect in the trigger of autoimmune thyroiditis. While most adults carry EBV, the virus remains latent in B-lymphocytes. When the immune system is compromised, EBV can reactivate, leading to a process called Molecular Mimicry.

A meta-analysis published in Pathogens (2021) involving over 1,200 subjects demonstrated that EBV seroprevalence was significantly higher in Hashimoto's patients compared to healthy controls (OR 4.5; 95% CI). The viral proteins share structural similarities with thyroid peroxidase (TPO). When the immune system attacks the virus, it inadvertently attacks the thyroid tissue.
