4. Preventing Immunosenescence

As we conclude the "I" module, we must address Immunosenescence‚Äîthe gradual deterioration of the immune system associated with aging. This process is accelerated by "Inflammaging," a term describing the age-related increase in systemic inflammatory markers.

Key drivers of inflammaging include:

  • Mitochondrial Dysfunction: Leaking mtDNA into the cytosol, triggering the NLRP3 inflammasome.
  • Gut Dysbiosis: Increased intestinal permeability leading to endotoxemia (LPS leakage).
  • Cellular Senescence: The "zombie cell" phenomenon where cells stop dividing but secrete inflammatory cytokines (SASP).

Case Study: The Exhausted Executive

Chronic Inflammation & Immune Dysregulation

Client: Marcus, 48 | Occupation: Tech CEO | Symptoms: Brain fog, joint pain, recurrent sinus infections, high stress.

Outcomes (12 Weeks): hs-CRP dropped to 1.1 mg/L. Marcus reported zero sinus infections during the winter quarter and a significant reduction in subjective "brain fog" scores (from 8/10 to 2/10).
