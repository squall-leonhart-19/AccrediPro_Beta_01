Why is Fasting Insulin considered a superior marker to Fasting Glucose for early detection of metabolic dysfunction?

Fasting glucose is often the last marker to break. The body will hyper-secrete insulin for years (or decades) to keep blood glucose "normal." By tracking fasting insulin, we can identify insulin resistance long before it manifests as high blood sugar or Type 2 Diabetes.
2

What is the relationship between Gut Inflammation (Module 2) and Mitochondrial Function (Module 10)?

Gut inflammation leads to increased intestinal permeability, allowing Lipopolysaccharides (LPS) into systemic circulation. LPS binds to TLR4 receptors on cells, triggering a shift from mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation (efficient) to glycolysis (inefficient), leading to oxidative stress and cellular fatigue.
