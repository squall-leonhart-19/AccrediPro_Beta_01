2. The Targeted Hierarchy of Needs

When a client presents with multiple imbalances, the novice coach tries to fix everything at once. The expert coach uses the Targeted Hierarchy of Needs. This hierarchy ensures that we aren't "painting the walls of a house while the foundation is sinking."

Level 1 Blood Sugar / Metabolism Glucose spikes drive inflammation and hormonal chaos. Chromium, Myo-inositol, Fiber

JD

Executive with Metabolic Syndrome and persistent Brain Fog.

Outcome: Within 60 days, James reported a "lifting of the veil" (Cognitive) and his HbA1c dropped to 5.7% (Metabolic) without changing his caloric intake.

4. Protocol vs. Personalization

As you conclude this module, you must understand the difference between a Protocol (the map) and Personalization (the actual journey). A protocol says, "PCOS requires Myo-inositol." Personalization says, "This specific client has PCOS *and* a history of IBS-D, so we must use a powder form to avoid gastric distress."

The T: Targeted Nourishment phase is dynamic. We use the Vitality Assessment (Module 1) to set the baseline, but we use Weekly Biofeedback to adjust the target. If a targeted protocol for sleep (Magnesium Bisglycinate + Tart Cherry) causes daytime grogginess, we pivot to a morning-focused adrenal support protocol instead.

According to the Targeted Hierarchy of Needs, why must Blood Sugar/Metabolism be addressed before complex Hormonal interventions?

Glucose instability (spikes and crashes) acts as a primary stressor on the endocrine system. High insulin can interfere with hormone receptor sensitivity and drive inflammation, making it nearly impossible to balance thyroid or sex hormones until the metabolic foundation is stable.
2

What is "Nutrient Synergy" and why is it critical in the "T" phase?

Nutrient synergy is the interaction where the combined effect of nutrients is greater than the sum of their individual effects (e.g., Vitamin D + K2, or Vitamin C + Iron). It is critical because it maximizes bioavailability and ensures that targeted nutrients reach their cellular destination without causing secondary deficiencies.
