Case Study 1: The "Burned Out" Executive

Complex HPA-Metabolic Dysregulation

M

Mark, 48

CEO, high-stress, 40lbs overweight, "wired but tired," poor sleep.

Presenting Symptoms: Mark was consuming 1,800 calories (low for his size) but unable to lose weight. He relied on 5 cups of coffee daily and felt a "crash" at 3 PM.

The Deep Dive Intervention:

  • V (Vitality): Identified a flat diurnal cortisol curve (low morning, high night).
  • I (Inflammation): High hs-CRP (3.2 mg/L) indicating systemic inflammation.
  • T (Targeted Nourishment): Increased protein to 1.6g/kg to support gluconeogenesis without cortisol spikes; introduced phosphatidylserine at night to blunt evening cortisol.

Outcomes: Within 12 weeks, Mark lost 22lbs. More importantly, his fasting glucose dropped from 104 mg/dL to 88 mg/dL without changing his total caloric intake, simply by shifting nutrient timing and supporting his HPA axis.

Why might a client with high cortisol levels show signs of hypothyroidism (fatigue, cold skin) even if their TSH is "normal"?

High cortisol inhibits the enzyme 5'-deiodinase, which converts inactive T4 to active T3. It also increases the production of Reverse T3 (rT3), which blocks T3 receptors. This results in "cellular hypothyroidism" that doesn't always show up on a standard TSH-only screening.
