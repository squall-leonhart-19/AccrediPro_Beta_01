2. The Autophagy-Longevity Axis

Autophagy, derived from the Greek for "self-eating," is the body's mechanism of cleaning out damaged cells to regenerate newer, healthier cells. In the L: Longevity & Legacy module, we introduced this concept. In this deep dive, we focus on the mTOR/AMPK balance.

The mammalian target of rapamycin (mTOR) is an anabolic signaling pathway. While necessary for muscle growth and repair, chronic mTOR activation (often caused by constant over-nutrition and high insulin) inhibits autophagy. Conversely, AMPK is the "fuel sensor" that, when activated by fasting or specific nutrients like Berberine, triggers cellular cleanup.

Key Regulators of Autophagy

  • Sirtuins (SIRT1-7): NAD+-dependent deacetylases that link metabolism to genome stability.
  • FoxO Transcription Factors: The "longevity genes" that regulate stress resistance.
  • Mitophagy: The specific autophagic destruction of dysfunctional mitochondria.
