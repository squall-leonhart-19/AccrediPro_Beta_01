4. Personalized 'Inflammation Control': Genetic Markers

Systemic inflammation ("Inflammaging") is the driver of most menopausal symptoms. However, the *type* of anti-inflammatory support a client needs is genetically determined.

The SOD2 Gene (Superoxide Dismutase 2): This gene produces the primary antioxidant enzyme in the mitochondria. Women with the "Ala/Ala" variant have lower mitochondrial protection. For these clients, the Vitality Boost protocol must include higher levels of exogenous antioxidants (CoQ10, Vitamin E) because their internal "defense system" is genetically hampered.

FADS1/2 (Fatty Acid Desaturase): These genes control the conversion of plant-based Omega-3 (ALA) into active EPA/DHA. A 2023 meta-analysis found that approx. 45% of women are "poor converters." For these individuals, a vegan anti-inflammatory diet may actually increase systemic inflammation because they cannot produce enough EPA to counteract the arachidonic acid cascade. Coaching must pivot to direct marine-based EPA/DHA supplementation.

6. Ethical Boundaries in Genetic Coaching

As an AccrediPro Certified Coach, you must navigate the "Genetic Frontier" with extreme professional integrity. You are a data interpreter, not a medical diagnostician.

The Three Pillars of Ethical Genomic Coaching
  • No Diagnostic Claims: Never tell a client "You have MTHFR disease." Instead, say "Your genetic markers suggest you may benefit from more bioavailable forms of folate."
  • The "Probability" Clause: Always remind clients that genes represent *potential*, not *certainty*. The THRIVE Methodâ„¢ focuses on the *expression* of health.
  • Referral Protocol: If genetic data suggests a high risk for hereditary conditions (e.g., BRCA1/2 or APOE4), the coach MUST refer the client to a licensed Genetic Counselor or Medical Doctor.
