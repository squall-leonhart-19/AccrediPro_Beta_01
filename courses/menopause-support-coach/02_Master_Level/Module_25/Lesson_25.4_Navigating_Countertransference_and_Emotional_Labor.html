5. Setting Emotional Boundaries: De-roling Techniques

Detaching from heavy client outcomes does not mean losing empathy; it means maintaining Clinical Symmetry. One of the most effective ways to navigate emotional labor is through the practice of "De-roling." This is a conscious transition from the "Coach Persona" back to the "Private Self."

Advanced De-roling Strategies:

1. The Physical Threshold: Use a physical action (washing hands, changing clothes, or stepping outside) to symbolize the end of the emotional labor period.

2. The "Locker" Visualization: At the end of a session, mentally place the client's "burden" in a secure locker that you can only open during their next scheduled session.

3. Somatic Shaking: Based on the work of Dr. Peter Levine, 60 seconds of gentle "shaking" of the limbs can help discharge the sympathetic nervous system arousal accumulated during a difficult session.
