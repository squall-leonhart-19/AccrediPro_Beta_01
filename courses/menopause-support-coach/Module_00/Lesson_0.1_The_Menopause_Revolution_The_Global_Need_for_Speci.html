Defining the Menopause Support Coach

It is vital to distinguish your role from other practitioners. You are not a doctor (unless you hold that license), and you are not a generalist life coach. You are a Specialized Menopause Practitioner.

Your role includes:

  • Symptom Mapping: Helping clients identify perimenopausal patterns that doctors often miss.
  • Evidence-Based Advocacy: Empowering clients with data (like the NAMS position statements) so they can have productive conversations with their medical team.
  • Lifestyle Architecture: Designing nutrition, movement, and stress protocols specifically for the shifting hormonal landscape.
  • Emotional Processing: Navigating the identity shift that often accompanies the end of the reproductive years.
