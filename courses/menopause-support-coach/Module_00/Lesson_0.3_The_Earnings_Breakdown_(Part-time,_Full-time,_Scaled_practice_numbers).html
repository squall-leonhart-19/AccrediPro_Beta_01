The Part-Time Revenue Model

Many practitioners begin their journey as a "side-hustle," dedicating 5 to 10 hours per week to their practice. The beauty of the menopause niche is that even at low volume, the specialized nature of the knowledge allows for premium pricing. Unlike general health coaching, you are addressing specific, high-pain symptoms like insomnia, "menopause middle," and vasomotor symptoms.

In a part-time model, the focus is on 1-on-1 coaching packages. A standard entry-level package for an AccrediPro certified coach is typically $1,200 for a 3-month engagement. With just 4 active clients, you generate $4,800 every three months, or $1,600/month, for approximately 4-5 hours of direct coaching work per week.

The Full-Time 6-Figure Path

Transitioning to full-time coaching (20-30 hours per week) requires a shift from "selling sessions" to "selling outcomes." A full-time practitioner typically manages 15-20 active 1-on-1 clients or a mix of 1-on-1 and small group cohorts. By utilizing the VITAL Methodâ„¢, you provide a comprehensive framework that justifies a higher price point because you are addressing vascular, metabolic, thermal, adrenal, and lifestyle longevity factors simultaneously.

The Scaled Practice Architecture

Practice Profitability & Overhead

A major advantage of the Menopause Support Coach career path is the low overhead. Unlike a brick-and-mortar medical clinic, a coaching practice can be run almost entirely digitally. Typical overhead for a full-time practice includes:

  • Practice Management Software: $50 - $150/month
  • Professional Insurance: $20 - $50/month
  • Marketing/Website: $100 - $300/month
  • Continuing Education: $100/month (allocated)

This results in profit margins often exceeding 85-90%. In contrast, traditional medical practices often operate on 20-30% margins after staff, rent, and insurance costs. This lean business model allows you to reinvest in your growth or simply enjoy a higher take-home pay.
