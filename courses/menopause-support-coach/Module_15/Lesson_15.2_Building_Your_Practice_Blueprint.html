Legal & Tech Essentials

Professionalism is non-negotiable. Before taking your first paying client, you must ensure your "back office" is secure and legally compliant. This protects both you and your clients.

  • Professional Liability Insurance: Essential for protecting against claims of negligence or "malpractice" (even if you aren't a doctor).
  • Client Service Agreement: A signed contract that clearly defines your Scope of Practice, refund policies, and the fact that you are not providing medical advice.
  • HIPAA/GDPR Compliant Platform: Use tools like Practice Better, NutriAdmin, or SimplePractice to store sensitive health data.
  • The "Done-For-You" Accelerator: Use the templates provided in your AccrediPro portal (Intake forms, assessment spreadsheets, and email scripts) to save 100+ hours of setup.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of practice building fundamentals.

1

Why is "Value-Based Pricing" superior to "Hourly Pricing" for a Menopause Support Coach?

Value-based pricing focuses on the outcome (the transformation) rather than the time spent. It increases client commitment, provides predictable income for the practitioner, and allows for a more comprehensive support system (like between-session messaging) that isn't possible in an hourly model.
2

What is the primary function of a Client Service Agreement?

It establishes legal boundaries, defines the scope of practice (clarifying that you are not a medical doctor), outlines refund/cancellation policies, and protects the practitioner from liability while ensuring the client understands the nature of the coaching relationship.
