3. Pitching to HR & Leadership: The Data-Driven Presentation

When presenting to Human Resources or Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) directors, your language must shift from clinical empathy to organizational ROI (Return on Investment). HR professionals are primarily concerned with three metrics: Retention, Recruitment, and Risk.

The "Three Pillars" Pitch Strategy:

  • Retention: Highlight the cost of replacing a senior female executive (often 1.5x to 2x her annual salary).
  • The Gender Pay Gap: Explain how menopause-driven exits contribute to the lack of women in senior roles, directly impacting the companyâ€™s public gender pay gap reporting.
  • Psychological Safety: Position menopause support as a key component of an inclusive culture where employees feel safe to discuss health challenges without fear of bias.

4. Policy & Consultancy Models: Beyond the Webinar

A workshop is a one-time event; a policy is a permanent structural change. Scaling your business into consultancy means helping companies write and implement Menopause Policies. This positions you as a strategic partner rather than a guest speaker.

A robust corporate menopause policy should include:

  1. Environmental Adjustments: Desk fans, proximity to toilets, breathable uniform fabrics, and temperature-controlled meeting rooms.
  2. Flexible Working: Permission to start later after a night of vasomotor-induced insomnia or to work from home during heavy menstrual flooding.
  3. Absence Management: Ensuring menopause-related sickness is recorded separately so it doesn't trigger "disciplinary" absence thresholds.
  4. The Menopause Champion: Training internal employees to act as first-point-of-contact peers.

5. Scaling through 'Train the Trainer' Models

To reach the "Scaling" phase of your business, you must decouple your income from your time. For a company with 50,000 employees, you cannot personally coach everyone. Instead, you sell a Train the Trainer (TTT) license.

In this model, you train the company's internal wellness leads or HR staff to deliver your proprietary T.H.R.I.V.E. Methodologyâ„¢ basics. You charge a significant upfront licensing fee plus an annual renewal fee for updated content and quality assurance. This creates passive, recurring B2B revenue.

Key B2B Terms

Presenteeism

When an employee is physically at work but mentally or physically underperforming due to symptoms (e.g., brain fog).

ESG Criteria

Environmental, Social, and Governance. Menopause support falls under the "Social" pillar of corporate responsibility.

MSA

Master Service Agreement. A contract that outlines the foundational terms for all future work with a corporate client.

6. Navigating Corporate Logistics: Procurement & Indemnity

Entering the corporate world requires a professional "back office." HR departments will rarely hire a coach who doesn't meet their procurement standards.

Professional Indemnity Insurance

Standard coaching insurance may not cover corporate consultancy. You must ensure your policy includes Professional Indemnity (PI) for business advice and "Errors and Omissions" coverage. Corporate contracts often require a minimum of $1Mâ€“$5M in coverage.

The Procurement Process

Be prepared for "Vendor Onboarding." This involves providing your tax ID, insurance certificates, data protection policy (GDPR/HIPAA compliance), and potentially a diversity and inclusion statement for your own business. Pro-Tip: Have a "Corporate Media Kit" ready as a single PDF that includes all these documents to speed up the process.

Check Your Understanding

Test your corporate scaling knowledge before moving to the final module integration.

1. Why is menopause support considered a "retention strategy" rather than just a "wellness perk"?

Because the cost of replacing senior female leaders (who are most likely to be in perimenopause) is significantly higher than the cost of implementing support. Retention keeps institutional knowledge and reduces recruitment costs (1.5x-2x salary).

2. What is the primary advantage of a 'Train the Trainer' model for a scaling coach?

It allows the coach to scale their impact to thousands of employees without increasing their own working hours, shifting the revenue model from "time-for-money" to "intellectual property licensing."
