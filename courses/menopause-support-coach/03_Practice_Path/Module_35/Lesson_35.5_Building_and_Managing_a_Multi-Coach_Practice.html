As a Menopause Support Coach, you eventually reach a "Capacity Ceiling." Whether you are running high-ticket masterminds or a referral-heavy 1:1 practice, there are only so many hours in your day. To expand your impact and reach the thousands of women currently underserved by the medical system, you must move from being the sole practitioner to the Clinical Director of a multi-coach practice.

1. The Solopreneur to CEO Evolution

The transition from solopreneur to CEO is less about "hiring help" and more about decoupling your time from your revenue. In a solo practice, if you don't show up, the business stops. In a multi-coach practice, you are building an asset that functions independently of your physical presence.

4. Financial Compensation Models

One of the most critical decisions in managing a multi-coach practice is how to pay your team. You must balance the coach's desire for a high income with the business's need for a 30-40% profit margin after all expenses.

Model Structure Pros Cons
Commission (Split) Coach gets 40-60% of the session fee. Low risk for owner; highly motivating for the coach. Income can be inconsistent; harder to control culture.
Flat Fee per Session Coach gets $X per hour/client. Predictable margins; easy to calculate. Coaches may feel undervalued as you raise your prices.
Salary + Bonus Base salary plus performance incentives. Highest loyalty; coach acts as a true "team member." Highest financial risk for the owner; requires steady lead flow.

5. Quality Control & Supervision

In a multi-coach practice, your brand is no longer defined by your work, but by the worst session delivered by your newest coach. Maintaining the integrity of the The T.H.R.I.V.E. Menopause Methodâ„¢ is paramount.

Implement a Supervision Loop consisting of:

  1. Session Recording Audits: Review one session per month for each coach to ensure they are adhering to the scope of practice and the "Red Flag" protocols learned in Module 0.
  2. Bi-Weekly Case Rounds: Meet as a team to discuss the most difficult "Hormone Harmony" cases. This fosters collective intelligence.
  3. Net Promoter Score (NPS): Survey clients specifically about their experience with the associate coach vs. the brand as a whole.

As you scale, your role shifts toward Lead Generation and Strategic Partnerships (as discussed in Lesson 4). You become the "Face" of the brand, while your team becomes the "Engine."
