2. The AccrediPro Code of Conduct

Maintaining the integrity of the Menopause Support Coach certification requires adherence to a strict ethical framework. This ensures that the coaching industry remains a respected partner to the medical community.

The Four Pillars of the Code
  • Evidence-Based Advocacy: Never making claims that contradict current peer-reviewed consensus (e.g., NAMS guidelines).
  • Scope Sovereignty: Explicitly stating that coaching is not a replacement for medical diagnosis or prescription of HRT.
  • Confidentiality & Data Integrity: Adhering to HIPAA (or local equivalent) standards for client symptom tracking and personal data.
  • Continuous Competence: Committing to 20 hours of Continuing Education (CE) every two years to maintain certification status.

4. Building a Referral Network

A Master Coach does not work in a vacuum. Your professional identity is strengthened by the quality of your interdisciplinary network. You should aim to be the "Hub" of your client's wellness team.

When approaching medical professionals, lead with the burden reduction you provide. Physicians often have only 15 minutes per patient; they cannot explain the nuances of the anti-inflammatory kitchen or sleep architecture. By positioning yourself as the "Implementation Specialist," you become an asset rather than a competitor.

Key Referral Partners
  • OB/GYNs & Endocrinologists: For hormone management and screening.
  • Pelvic Floor Physical Therapists: For GSM and urinary health.
  • Registered Dietitians: For complex metabolic disorders (e.g., T2 Diabetes).
  • Psychotherapists: For clinical depression or trauma-informed care.

5. The Path to Mastery: AccrediPro Master Coach

Graduation is not the end; it is the "Second Spring" of your career. To achieve the AccrediPro Master Coach designation, practitioners must demonstrate advanced application of the THRIVE framework over time.
