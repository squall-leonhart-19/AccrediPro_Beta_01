1. The Economic Imperative: Why Corporations Care

While individual coaching focuses on personal transformation, Corporate Menopause Wellness (B2B) focuses on organizational stability. For a Menopause Support Coach, the corporate sector represents the single highest-leverage opportunity for impact and income. The "business case" for menopause support is no longer theoretical; it is backed by staggering data regarding talent retention.

When you pitch to a corporation, you are not selling "hormone balance"; you are selling the mitigation of turnover risk for their most experienced female leaders.

2. Positioning as CSR and DEI Initiatives

To successfully enter a medium-to-large firm, your services must be mapped to existing corporate buckets: Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) or Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR). Menopause is the "missing link" in gender equity strategies. Most firms have robust support for maternity leave (recruitment/early career) but zero support for the "menopausal cliff" (retention/senior leadership).

The DEI Alignment Strategy

  • Gender Pay Gap: Menopause often hits at the peak of a woman's career. If she leaves or takes a step back, the gender pay gap widens at the executive level.
  • Age Diversity: Supporting menopausal employees demonstrates a commitment to retaining age-diverse talent and institutional knowledge.
  • Inclusive Culture: Normalizing menopause conversations reduces the "stigma of aging" and fosters psychological safety.

4. Structuring B2B Packages: Workshops & Coaching

Corporate clients rarely buy "sessions." They buy Programs. Your offer should be structured to provide broad awareness (workshops) and deep intervention (executive coaching).

The "Awareness-to-Action" Model
  • Tier 1: Educational Seminars (The "T" & "H" focus): 60-minute "Lunch and Learns" for all staff (including managers) to normalize the conversation and explain the science of symptoms.
  • Tier 2: Group Coaching Cohorts (The "R", "I", "V" focus): 8-week intensive programs for menopausal employees focusing on metabolic health, inflammation, and vitality.
  • Tier 3: Executive 1:1 Coaching (The "E" focus): High-touch coaching for C-Suite and VP-level women to manage the psychological transition and maintain high-performance leadership.
  • Tier 4: Manager Training: Equipping HR and line managers with the "Red Flag Protocol" and legal compliance knowledge.
Case Study: TechForward Solutions (n=450 employees)

Scenario: A mid-sized tech firm noticed a 12% increase in turnover among female employees aged 45-55 over two years.

Intervention: A Menopause Support Coach pitched a 6-month "Retention Through Wellness" program. This included 3 company-wide webinars, a private Slack channel for peer support, and 1:1 coaching for 10 senior leaders.

Outcomes:

  • Reported absenteeism in the target demographic dropped by 22%.
  • 94% of participants reported improved "Brain Fog" management (The 'V' in THRIVE).
  • The firm saved an estimated $420,000 in recruitment/onboarding costs by retaining three key VPs who were considering early retirement.

5. Navigating the B2B Sales Cycle: Stakeholders & Gatekeepers

B2B sales are rarely "one-and-done." They involve a cycle of Discovery, Proposal, Procurement, and Implementation. Understanding who holds the power is vital.

Key Stakeholders

  • The Champion: Often a female leader in her 40s/50s who personally understands the struggle. She is your internal advocate.
  • The Gatekeeper: HR Generalists or Wellness Coordinators. They filter proposals. You must give them the data to justify your price to their boss.
  • The Decision Maker: CHRO (Chief Human Resources Officer) or CEO. They care about EBITDA, retention rates, and employer branding.
  • Procurement/Legal: They handle the contracts. Ensure your insurance and certifications (AccrediPro) are readily available.

6. Pricing Strategies: Retainers vs. Per-Head Fees

Pricing for B2B requires a shift from "hourly thinking" to "value-based thinking." There are three primary models:

Per-Head / Project Fee

Best for one-off workshops or seminars.
Typical Range: $1,500 - $5,000 per 90-minute session depending on company size and customization.

Retainer Model

Monthly fee for "on-call" support, monthly workshops, and a set number of 1:1 hours.
Typical Range: $3,000 - $10,000/month (6-12 month contracts).

When calculating your fee, use the 10x Value Rule. If your program saves the company one senior leader (replacement cost: $150k), a $15,000 package is an easy "yes" for the CFO.

Check Your Understanding

1

Why is it more effective to pitch menopause support as a "Retention Strategy" rather than a "Health Benefit"?

Health benefits are often viewed as costs/expenses to be minimized. Retention strategies are viewed as investments that protect the company's bottom line (EBITDA) by avoiding the massive costs associated with replacing senior talent and losing institutional knowledge.
2

Which stakeholder is most likely to care about the "Gender Pay Gap" implications of your pitch?

The DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) Director or the CHRO. They are often measured on their ability to move the needle on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) metrics and gender parity at the executive level.

Key Takeaways

  • B2B coaching focuses on organizational ROI (retention and productivity) rather than just individual wellness.
  • Position your services within DEI and CSR frameworks to access dedicated corporate budgets.
  • A successful pitch deck transitions from the economic problem to your proprietary THRIVE solution.
  • The B2B sales cycle requires identifying an internal champion to navigate HR and Procurement.
  • Value-based pricing (retainers) is superior to hourly rates for long-term corporate sustainability.
