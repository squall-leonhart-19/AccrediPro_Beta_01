2. Ethical Boundaries & HRT Integration

A critical component of your professional scope is the Legal-Ethical Barrier. As a coach, you occupy a unique space: you are often the most frequent point of contact for a woman in transition, yet you are not a medical prescriber. Your role regarding Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) is Educational and Supportive, not diagnostic.

A 2023 survey of 1,200 midlife women found that 74% felt their primary care physician lacked the time to explain the nuances of HRT options. This is where you bridge the gap. You provide the pre-consultation education (helping them understand the difference between transdermal estradiol and oral synthetics) and the post-consultation implementation (tracking symptoms once they start a protocol).

CASE STUDY: The Boundary Protocol

Client: Sarah, 51, experiencing severe vasomotor symptoms and mood swings. She wants the coach to recommend a specific dosage of progesterone based on her Dutch Test results.

The Intervention: Instead of recommending a dose, the coach used the T.H.R.I.V.E. framework to help Sarah organize her symptom data into a "Physician Communication Brief." The coach explained the mechanism of progesterone on GABA receptors but directed Sarah to her gynecologist for the prescription.

Outcome: Sarah felt empowered during her 15-minute medical appointment, obtained the correct prescription, and the coach focused on the "Reset Metabolism" pillar to manage the water retention Sarah experienced as a side effect.
