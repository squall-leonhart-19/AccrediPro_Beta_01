Core Neuropsychological Terms

Subjective Cognitive Decline (SCD)

A self-reported experience of worsening or more frequent confusion or memory loss, often preceding objective clinical impairment.

Executive Function

A set of mental skills including working memory, flexible thinking, and self-control, primarily governed by the prefrontal cortex.

Cognitive Reserve

The brain's ability to improvise and find alternate ways of getting a job done, acting as a buffer against age-related changes.

2. PHQ-9 and GAD-7: Differential Diagnosis

As a Menopause Support Coach, you must distinguish between clinical psychiatric disorders and hormone-withdrawal-induced mood shifts. While we do not diagnose, we screen to ensure client safety and appropriate referral (the Red Flag Protocol).

Primary Driver Neurotransmitter imbalance / Genetics / Trauma Estrogen withdrawal / HPA Axis dysregulation
PHQ-9 Profile Persistent high scores (>15) across all domains Variable scores; often high in "Sleep" and "Energy"

The PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) and GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7) are gold-standard tools. However, in menopause, we look for the "Somatic Overlay." If a client scores high on GAD-7 but focuses exclusively on heart palpitations and hot flash-induced panic, this points toward the "H" (Hormone Harmony) pillar rather than a primary anxiety disorder.

3. Assessing 'Executive Function' Fatigue

The "Executive Function" (EF) refers to the brain's "Air Traffic Control" system. Estrogen receptors are densely packed in the prefrontal cortex, the seat of EF. When estrogen levels fluctuate, the air traffic control tower becomes foggy.

Assessment of EF fatigue should focus on three domains:

  • Working Memory: "Do you lose your train of thought mid-sentence?"
  • Inhibitory Control: "Are you more impulsive or irritable with colleagues?"
  • Cognitive Flexibility: "How much effort does it take to switch between tasks?"

4. The Menopause Anxiety Scale (MAS)

Standard anxiety tools often fail to capture the unique somatic anxiety of menopause. The Menopause Anxiety Scale (MAS) or the use of the Greene Climacteric Scale specifically targets the physical manifestations of hormonal shifts.

Key indicators on the MAS include:

  • Heart racing or pounding for no reason.
  • Feeling of "inner tension" or vibrating.
  • Panic sensations specifically triggered by a hot flash (Vasomotor-Panic Coupling).
  • Exaggerated startle response (Hyperarousal).

5. Cognitive Load Management: Assessing 'Bandwidth'

Midlife is often the period of maximum Cognitive Load. Women are frequently in the "sandwich generation," caring for aging parents and adolescent children while navigating peak career demands. When you add the neurobiological tax of perimenopause, the "bandwidth" is exceeded.

The Bandwidth Assessment Protocol
As a coach, you must assess the client's current mental "overhead." Use the following 1-10 scale for three categories:
  • Domestic Load: Decision fatigue related to home/family.
  • Professional Load: Complexity and volume of work tasks.
  • Biological Load: The energy drained by symptoms (sleep loss, flashes).
If the total exceeds 22/30, cognitive interventions will fail until the "Biological Load" is reduced via the THRIVE method.

This likely represents Hormone-Mediated Mood Fluctuation or PMDD exacerbation rather than MDD. While you must follow the Red Flag Protocol if safety is a concern, the primary intervention should focus on Hormone Harmony (H) and Sleep (V) before assuming a primary psychiatric disorder.
2

Why might a perimenopausal woman pass an objective memory test (like the MoCA) but still meet the criteria for Subjective Cognitive Decline (SCD)?

High-functioning individuals often have a high "Cognitive Reserve." They can use compensatory strategies to pass basic tests, but the *effort* required to do so is significantly higher than their baseline, leading to subjective distress and "Brain Fog."

Key Takeaways

  • Validate the Subjective: SCD is a valid clinical marker in menopause, even in the absence of objective test failure.
  • Screen, Don't Diagnose: Use PHQ-9 and GAD-7 to stratify risk and identify when a client needs medical or psychiatric referral.
  • Executive Function is Estrogen-Dependent: Professional "incompetence" is often a transient EF fatigue caused by neuro-steroid fluctuation.
  • Assess Bandwidth: Cognitive interventions require available mental energy; biology must be stabilized first.
