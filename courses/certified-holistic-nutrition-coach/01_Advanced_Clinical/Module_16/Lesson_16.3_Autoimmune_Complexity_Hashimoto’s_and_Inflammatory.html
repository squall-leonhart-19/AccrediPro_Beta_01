Restore Balance (R): The Micronutrient Thyroid Triad

The 'R' phase (Restore Balance) focuses on the physiological requirements of the thyroid gland. Without specific cofactors, the gland cannot produce hormone, even if the diet is "perfect."

  • Selenium (200mcg/day): Essential for glutathione peroxidase, which protects the thyroid from oxidative damage during hormone synthesis. A 2023 Cochrane Review found selenium supplementation reduced TPO antibodies by an average of 32% over 6 months.
  • Zinc (20-30mg/day): Necessary for the T3 receptor to function. Zinc deficiency often manifests as "thinning outer eyebrows" in Hashimoto's clients.
  • Vitamin D3 (Optimized to 60-80 ng/mL): Acts as an immune modulator, increasing T-regulatory (Treg) cells that prevent the immune system from overreacting.

Check Your Understanding

Test your clinical reasoning for Hashimoto's management.

1. Why might a client's TPO antibodies spike even after removing gluten?

This often occurs due to "cross-reactivity" with other proteins like dairy (casein) or coffee, or due to a latent "U" (Root Cause) trigger like a viral flare (EBV) or mold exposure, which keeps the immune system in a hyper-vigilant state.

2. What is the physiological purpose of Selenium in the thyroid gland?

Selenium is a cofactor for the enzyme glutathione peroxidase. This enzyme neutralizes the hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) produced during thyroid hormone synthesis, preventing oxidative damage to the thyroid tissue.
