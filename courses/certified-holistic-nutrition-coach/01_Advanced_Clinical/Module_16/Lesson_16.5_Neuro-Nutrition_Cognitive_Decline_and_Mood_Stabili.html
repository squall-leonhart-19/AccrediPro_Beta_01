BDNF: The Brain's "Fertilizer"

Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) is a protein that acts as "Miracle-Gro" for the brain. It supports the survival of existing neurons and encourages the growth of new ones (neurogenesis). Low levels of BDNF are hallmarks of both cognitive decline and clinical depression.

As Holistic Nutrition Coaches, we can influence BDNF levels through two primary nutritional levers:

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA): A 2021 meta-analysis found that high-dose EPA/DHA (2g+) significantly increased serum BDNF levels. DHA, specifically, makes up 90% of the omega-3 fat in the brain.
  • Polyphenols (Flavonoids): Compounds found in blueberries (anthocyanins), cocoa (flavanols), and green tea (EGCG) cross the blood-brain barrier and stimulate the signaling pathways that produce BDNF.

Check Your Understanding

Test your clinical reasoning on neuro-nutrition.

1. Why is insulin resistance (HbA1c > 5.6%) considered a primary driver of cognitive decline in the Bredesen Protocol?

Insulin resistance in the brain (sometimes called "Type 3 Diabetes") prevents neurons from effectively utilizing glucose for energy. Furthermore, the enzyme that breaks down insulin (Insulin-Degrading Enzyme) also breaks down amyloid-beta plaques. If insulin is chronically high, the enzyme is "busy" with insulin and cannot clear the plaques from the brain.

2. How does systemic inflammation (Inflammaging) specifically contribute to the symptoms of depression?

Systemic cytokines cross the blood-brain barrier and activate microglia. These cells then trigger the "Kynurenine Pathway," which diverts Tryptophan away from Serotonin production and toward the production of neurotoxic metabolites like Quinolinic Acid, leading to mood disturbances and neurodegeneration.
