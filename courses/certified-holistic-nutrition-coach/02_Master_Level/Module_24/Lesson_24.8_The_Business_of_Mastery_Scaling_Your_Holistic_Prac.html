4. Legal & Ethical Guardrails for Master Practitioners

As you move into advanced protocols (e.g., nutritional genomics or high-dose therapeutic supplementation), your liability increases. It is imperative to maintain clear Scope of Practice boundaries.

Key legal pillars for the Master Practitioner include:

  • Informed Consent: Every client must sign a document stating they understand you are a Nutrition Coach, not a licensed medical doctor, and that your protocols are for educational/nutritional support.
  • Professional Liability (E&O) Insurance: Ensure your policy covers "Nutritional Consulting" and "Digital Programs" specifically.
  • The "Refer Out" Threshold: Establish a clinical "red flag" list (e.g., unexplained rapid weight loss, suicidal ideation, acute cardiac symptoms) that triggers an immediate referral to a physician.
