2. Meta vs. Google: Targeting by Interest and Intent

Choosing the right platform depends on where your ideal client is in their "problem-awareness" journey. We categorize digital traffic into two primary buckets: Interruptive Traffic and Intent-Based Traffic.

Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram)

Meta is the king of interruptive marketing. Users are not searching for a "Holistic Nutritionist for Hashimotos"; they are scrolling through social feeds. Your ad must "interrupt" their pattern with a compelling hook. Meta‚Äôs power lies in its Interest-Based Targeting and Lookalike Audiences (LLAs).

  • Ideal For: Building brand awareness, lead magnet downloads, and visual storytelling.
  • Targeting: Interests in "Functional Medicine," "Autoimmune Protocol," or followers of specific health influencers.

Google Ads (Search)

Google is intent-based. When someone types "how to reverse insulin resistance naturally" into Google, they have a high level of problem awareness and are actively seeking a solution. This traffic is often more expensive but converts at a significantly higher rate for direct discovery calls.

A "funnel" is simply the automated path a stranger takes to become a client. For high-ticket holistic nutrition ($2,000 - $5,000+ packages), a direct-to-purchase ad rarely works. Instead, we use a Value-First Funnel architecture.

4. Retargeting & The Rule of 7

Marketing psychology dictates the "Rule of 7"‚Äîa prospect needs to interact with your brand at least seven times before making a high-stakes decision. In the health space, where trust is the primary currency, this number is often higher.

Retargeting (or Remarketing) allows you to show specific ads only to people who have already visited your website or engaged with your content. This is the most cost-effective portion of your ad spend.

The Three Layers of Retargeting:

  • Layer 1: Social Proof. Show testimonials and case studies to people who visited your "Work With Me" page but didn't book.
  • Layer 2: Logic/Education. Show a video breaking down the science of a specific root cause (e.g., Intestinal Permeability) to those who downloaded your lead magnet.
  • Layer 3: Overcoming Objections. Address common concerns (time, cost, "will this work for me?") via short, punchy video ads.

5. The Economics of Growth: CAC vs. LTV

To run a sustainable practice, you must understand your "Unit Economics." You are essentially "buying" clients. If you know how much a client is worth, you know exactly how much you can afford to spend to acquire them.

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
The total amount spent on marketing divided by the number of new clients.
Example: $1,000 spend / 5 clients = $200 CAC.
Lifetime Value (LTV)
The total revenue a client generates over their entire relationship with you.
Example: $2,500 initial program + $500 supplement commission + $1,000 maintenance program = $4,000 LTV.

The Golden Ratio: In the coaching industry, an LTV:CAC ratio of 3:1 is considered healthy. However, premium holistic practitioners often achieve 7:1 or 10:1 by utilizing the high-ticket "Integrative Protocol" model taught in Module 31.1.

6. A/B Testing & Optimization

Optimization is the process of making your funnel more efficient. We use A/B Testing (Split Testing) to compare two versions of a single variable to see which performs better. In health marketing, small changes can lead to 50-100% increases in conversion.

What to Test First:

  • The Headline: "How to Heal Your Gut" vs. "The 3 Hidden Triggers of Your Bloating." (The latter usually wins due to curiosity and specificity).
  • The Creative: A professional headshot vs. a candid video of you in your kitchen. (Authenticity often outperforms "polished" in holistic health).
  • The CTA (Call to Action): "Book a Call" vs. "Apply for the Protocol." ("Apply" implies scarcity and exclusivity, often increasing lead quality).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Google Ads considered "Intent-Based" while Meta Ads are "Interruptive"?

Google Ads target users who are actively searching for a solution via keywords (high intent), whereas Meta Ads target users based on their interests and demographics while they are browsing social media (requiring a pattern interrupt).

2. If your CAC is $400 and your LTV is $1,200, what is your LTV:CAC ratio, and is it sustainable?

The ratio is 3:1. This is the baseline for sustainability in digital marketing, meaning for every $1 spent, you generate $3 in revenue. For premium services, practitioners should aim to increase this through better conversion or higher-ticket pricing.

Key Takeaways

  • Paid acquisition is a distribution engine for your authority, not a replacement for it.
  • Meta is for discovery; Google is for solutions. Most successful practices eventually use a hybrid of both.
  • The 'Integrative Protocol' Funnel works by providing value (Lead Magnet) before asking for a commitment (Discovery Call).
  • Retargeting is mandatory. Most conversions happen between the 4th and 10th touchpoint.
  • Know your numbers. If you don't know your CAC and LTV, you aren't running a business; you're running a hobby.
