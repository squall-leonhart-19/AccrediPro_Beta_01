4. The Hierarchy of Intervention

When a client is "stuck," the practitioner must decide which fire to put out first. We use a Triage Logic based on the NOURISH hierarchy. Generally, we cannot fix the "R" (Restore Balance/Hormones) if the "O" (Optimize Digestion) is broken, as malabsorption prevents the raw materials from reaching the endocrine system.

System Priority Condition Examples Why Address First?
1. Digestion (O) SIBO, Dysbiosis, Low HCl The gut is the gateway. Malabsorption and endotoxemia (LPS) drive all other systemic issues.
2. Blood Sugar (R) Insulin Resistance, Hypoglycemia Glycemic variability is a primary stressor to the HPA axis. You can't fix "adrenals" with unstable sugar.
3. HPA Axis (R) Burnout, High Cortisol Stress shuts down digestion and thyroid function. Essential for long-term "S" (Sustainability).
4. Environment (I) Mold, Heavy Metals, Plastics If the "bucket is full," metabolic pathways remain sluggish despite good food.

5. Iterative Protocol Adjustment

Clinical success is not a straight line. It is a series of Biofeedback Loops. As a Holistic Nutrition Coach, you must teach your clients to track their "Big Five" biofeedback markers daily:

  1. Sleep Quality: Are they waking rested? (Circadian health)
  2. Hunger: Is it stable or "hangry"? (Blood sugar)
  3. Mood: Anxiety or irritability? (Neurotransmitter balance)
  4. Energy: Is there a mid-afternoon slump? (Mitochondrial health)
  5. Digestion: Daily BM? Bloating? (Microbiome status)

If markers 1 and 2 are improving but 5 is stagnant, we pivot. This prevents the client from feeling like they are "failing" the protocol and instead makes them a co-investigator in their own health.
