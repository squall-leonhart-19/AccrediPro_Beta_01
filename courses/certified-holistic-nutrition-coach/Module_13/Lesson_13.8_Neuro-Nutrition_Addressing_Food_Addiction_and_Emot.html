Wanting vs. Liking: The Berridge Model

Neuroscientist Kent Berridge identified two distinct systems:

  • Incentive Salience (Wanting): Driven by dopamine. It creates the "urge" or "craving."
  • Hedonic Impact (Liking): Driven by endogenous opioids and endocannabinoids. It provides the "pleasure" of the actual bite.
In food addiction, the "wanting" system becomes hyper-sensitized while the "liking" system becomes desensitized (tolerance). This leaves the client in a state of constant craving for a reward that no longer provides satisfaction.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do clients often feel "less pleasure" from food even as their cravings increase?

This is due to the decoupling of the "Wanting" (Dopamine) and "Liking" (Opioid) systems. Chronic overstimulation leads to dopamine receptor downregulation (tolerance), requiring more of the substance to feel "normal," while the actual hedonic pleasure derived from the food decreases.

2. How does reactive hypoglycemia contribute to impulsive eating behaviors?

Rapid drops in blood sugar trigger a survival response in the Amygdala. This shifts the brain into a "threat state," which deactivates the Prefrontal Cortex (the area responsible for logic and impulse control) and prioritizes high-calorie, quick-energy foods.
