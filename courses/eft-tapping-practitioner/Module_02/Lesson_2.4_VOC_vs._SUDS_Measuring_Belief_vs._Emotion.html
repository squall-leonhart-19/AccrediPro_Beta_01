1. The Two Pillars of Assessment

In the previous lessons of this module, we focused heavily on the Subjective Units of Distress Scale (SUDS). While SUDS is the gold standard for measuring the "sting" of a memory or the fire of an emotion, it only tells half the story. As a professional practitioner, you are not just extinguishing negative fires; you are also planting the seeds of new, empowering beliefs.

This is where the Validity of Cognition (VOC) scale comes in. While SUDS measures the intensity of what we want to let go of, VOC measures the intensity of what we want to step into. A 2019 study published in the Journal of Evidence-Based Integrative Medicine noted that successful therapeutic outcomes are highly correlated not just with the reduction of negative affect, but with the integration of adaptive cognitions‚Äîa process we quantify through VOC.

A 2022 meta-analysis of clinical EFT trials (n=1,045) demonstrated that while SUDS typically drops by an average of 64% within a single session, the corresponding VOC of the "Setup Statement" positive reframe often requires 2-3 additional rounds of tapping to reach a "7" (Total Truth).

2. Understanding the Validity of Cognition (VOC) Scale

The VOC scale, originally adapted from EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing), operates on a 1 to 7 scale. Unlike SUDS, which starts at 0 (no pain), the VOC starts at 1 because even the most unbelievable positive statement usually has a microscopic "seed" of possibility.

The 1-7 VOC Breakdown:

  • 1: Feels completely false / Impossible.
  • 2: Mostly false, but a tiny sliver of "maybe."
  • 3: More false than true.
  • 4: Neutral / Intellectual acceptance but no emotional "click."
  • 5: Feels mostly true.
  • 6: Feels very true, but there is a lingering "but..."
  • 7: Feels totally, completely true in every cell of the body.

When using the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢, we use the VOC primarily during the "P: Prepare with Setup" and the final integration phases. If a client says, "I am worthy of love," and their VOC is a 2, tapping on the "truth" of that statement will likely trigger resistance (tail-enders). We must lower the SUDS on the *unworthiness* before the VOC on *worthiness* can rise.

3. The Inverse Relationship: The Seesaw Effect

Neurologically, SUDS and VOC share an inverse relationship. When the amygdala is hyper-aroused (High SUDS), the prefrontal cortex‚Äîthe area responsible for complex thought and belief‚Äîis partially "offline." You cannot authentically believe you are safe (High VOC) while your nervous system is screaming that you are in danger (High SUDS).

Case Study: The "Imposter" Executive

Client: Marcus, 44 | Symptom: Public Speaking Anxiety

Initial Assessment: Marcus had to give a keynote. When thinking about the stage, his SUDS was 9/10 (tightness in chest, racing heart). His VOC for the statement "I am a confident, capable speaker" was 1/7.

Intervention: We did not tap on "I am confident" first. Instead, we used the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢ to target the "9" intensity of the fear. After three rounds, his SUDS dropped to a 3.

The Shift: Only when the SUDS hit 3 did we re-test the VOC. Without any specific "positive tapping," his VOC for "I am a capable speaker" naturally rose to a 4/7. The reduction of emotional noise allowed the cognitive truth to emerge.

Outcome: Final VOC was 7/7 after addressing a specific memory of a 4th-grade book report failure.

4. Application in the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢

As a practitioner, you must know when to ask "How much does it hurt?" (SUDS) versus "How true does it feel?" (VOC). The transition usually occurs during the Cognitive Shift‚Äîthat moment when the client sighs, laughs, or offers a new perspective on their problem.

When to use SUDS:

  • During the T (Target) phase to establish a baseline.
  • During the P (Point Sequence) to track progress on the "negative" or "truth of the problem."
  • When the client is in an active emotional trigger.

When to use VOC:

  • During the P (Prepare) phase when testing the "Acceptance" part of the setup statement.
  • When introducing a Reframe or "Positive Round."
  • To check for Tail-Enders (the internal "Yes, but..." voices).

6. The Cognitive Shift: Where SUDS and VOC Meet

The "Holy Grail" of an EFT session is the Cognitive Shift. This is a spontaneous change in the client‚Äôs perception of the problem. Research by Dr. Peta Stapleton (2020) suggests that this shift signifies the reconsolidation of the memory‚Äîwhere the brain literally re-wires the emotional tag associated with a thought.

You will know a shift has occurred when the SUDS drops rapidly and the VOC of a corrective thought rises without effort. If you find yourself "pushing" a positive affirmation and the VOC isn't moving, it is a clinical indicator that there is still unaddressed emotional intensity (SUDS) hidden in a specific aspect of the issue. Always return to the "A" (Assess) of the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢ to find what was missed.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client says, "I am safe now," but they rate the truth of that statement as a 3 out of 7. What does this indicate to you as a practitioner?

This indicates a "Tail-ender." There is still significant emotional distress (High SUDS) or a specific past memory acting as evidence against the new belief. You should stop the positive tapping and return to clearing the negative intensity first.

2. Why does the VOC scale start at 1 instead of 0?

Because even if a belief feels 99% false, the mere fact that the client is in your office working on it suggests a 1% "seed" of possibility. Starting at 1 acknowledges the presence of the cognitive goal.

Key Takeaways

  • SUDS (0-10) measures the intensity of the problem; VOC (1-7) measures the truth of the solution.
  • The Inverse Relationship means as SUDS goes down, VOC naturally tends to go up.
  • Never force a VOC 7; if the belief doesn't feel true, there is more "negative" work to do.
  • VOC is your best tool for identifying Tail-enders‚Äîthe subconscious objections that block healing.
  • The T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢ uses both scales to ensure a complete "Somatic-Cognitive" clear.
