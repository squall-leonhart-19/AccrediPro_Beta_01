1. The Historical Bridge: From TFT to Clinical EFT

Emotional Freedom Techniques (EFT) did not emerge in a vacuum. It is the result of decades of clinical observation and refinement. The journey began in the 1980s with Dr. Roger Callahan, a clinical psychologist who developed Thought Field Therapy (TFT). Callahan discovered that by tapping on specific meridian points while a patient focused on a phobia, the emotional distress could vanish in minutes.

However, TFT was complex, requiring specific "algorithms" for different issues. In the 1990s, Gary Craig, a Stanford-trained engineer and student of Callahan, simplified the process into a single, universal sequence. This became Emotional Freedom Techniques. While Craigâ€™s work popularized tapping, it was the subsequent "Clinical EFT" movement, led by researchers like Dr. Dawson Church and Dr. Peta Stapleton, that brought the method into the halls of science through rigorous peer-reviewed research.
