4. The 9-Gamut Technique: Brain Balancing

The 9-Gamut is a specialized Level 1 tool used when the SUDS are "stuck" or when the client feels "disconnected" from the tapping process. It involves stimulating the Gamut Point (on the back of the hand between the 4th and 5th metacarpals) while performing a series of eye movements, humming, and counting.

The science behind this involves Dual Attention Stimulation (similar to EMDR) and hemispheric synchronization. The eye movements engage different parts of the brain, while the humming (Right Brain/Creative) and counting (Left Brain/Logical) force the two hemispheres to communicate, effectively "unsticking" the neurological loop of the trauma.

The 9-Gamut Steps

While continuously tapping the Gamut point, perform these 9 actions: 1. Eyes closed. 2. Eyes open. 3. Eyes hard down right. 4. Eyes hard down left. 5. Roll eyes in a full circle. 6. Roll eyes in the opposite direction. 7. Hum 2 seconds of a song (e.g., Happy Birthday). 8. Count rapidly from 1 to 5. 9. Hum again for 2 seconds.

5. When Aspects are 'Hiding'

In Module 6, we explored N: Navigate Aspects. Troubleshooting often requires us to realize that we aren't tapping on the right aspect. If the SUDS for "the car accident" aren't moving, it may be because the client is actually reacting to a "Hiding Aspect," such as the smell of the upholstery or the specific look on the other driver's face.

To uncover these, we use the Sensory VAKOG Model (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic, Olfactory, Gustatory). Ask the client: "If there were a hidden part of this that we haven't touched yet, what would it be?" or "What part of this is the worst part right now?" Often, the target shifts from a global event to a micro-detail that holds the emotional charge.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client's SUDS level remains at an 8 after three rounds of tapping on a specific memory, what is the first "physiological" factor you should check?

Hydration. Check if the client has had enough water, as dehydration can impede the electrical conductivity of the meridian system.

2. Why does the 9-Gamut technique involve both humming and counting?

To balance the brain hemispheres. Humming activates the right (creative/emotional) hemisphere, while counting activates the left (logical/linear) hemisphere, facilitating neurological integration.
