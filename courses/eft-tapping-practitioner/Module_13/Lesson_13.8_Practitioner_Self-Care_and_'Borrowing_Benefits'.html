1. The Mechanics of Borrowing Benefits

In the world of professional therapy and coaching, the practitioner is often viewed as a "neutral vessel." However, EFT introduces a unique phenomenon known as Borrowing Benefits. This occurs when a practitioner (or an observer in a group setting) taps along with a client on the client's issues, and subsequently experiences a reduction in their own unrelated stress levels.

The neurobiology of this process is rooted in the Mirror Neuron System. The Post-Session Clearing Protocol

Use this 5-minute sequence between clients:

  • Target: Any lingering somatic sensation or thought about the previous client.
  • Assess: SUDS level of your own fatigue or "heaviness."
  • Prepare: "Even though I'm carrying [Client Name]'s story in my shoulders, I choose to release it now and return to my center."
  • Point Sequence: Focus on the physical sensation of the "residue."
  • Investigate: Notice if the "Table Leg" of the client's story has triggered one of your own.
  • Navigate: If a personal memory was triggered, note it for your own private session later.
  • Ground: Use a deep breath to reset your energetic boundary.

Setting boundaries is often discussed in terms of time and money, but for the EFT practitioner, Energetic Boundaries are paramount. This involves the conscious decision to remain a witness rather than a participant in the client's emotional storm.
