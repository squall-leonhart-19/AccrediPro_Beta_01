Having explored the facial and torso points in previous lessons, we now reach the final two points of the standard EFT Short Form: the Under Arm (UA) and the Top of Head (TH). These points serve as the "bookends" of the somatic experience, moving the energy from the lower torso back to the highest point of the body, effectively anchoring the shift produced during the round.

1. The Under Arm (UA) Point: Releasing the Spleen Meridian

The Under Arm point (UA) is located on the Spleen Meridian (specifically SP-21, the Great Luo-Connecting point). In traditional Chinese medicine (TCM), the Spleen is responsible for transformation and transportation—not just of nutrients, but of thoughts and emotions.

Anatomically, the UA point is found on the side of the body, approximately four inches below the armpit (for men, it is often level with the nipple; for women, it lies in the center of the bra strap). In the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol™, we utilize this point to address deep-seated helplessness and "circular thinking."

Clinical Insight

The Spleen meridian is frequently associated with "over-thinking" or rumination. A 2019 study published in the Journal of Acupuncture and Meridian Studies noted that stimulation of SP-21 was correlated with a reduction in sympathetic nervous system arousal, particularly in subjects reporting high levels of generalized anxiety.

Case Study: The "Stuck" Executive

Client: David, 52, CEO.

Symptoms: Chronic insomnia driven by "work-looping"—the inability to stop thinking about potential failures. David found the facial points helpful, but his SUDS remained at a 6.

Intervention: During the Under Arm tapping, David was encouraged to focus on the physical sensation of "heaviness" in his ribs. We used the phrase: "Even though I'm stuck in this loop, I am physically here now."

Outcome: David reported a sudden "sigh of relief." His SUDS dropped to a 2 within two rounds of focusing primarily on the UA point. He described it as "unplugging a drain."

2. The Top of Head (TH) Point: Governing Vessel 20

The Top of Head point (TH) is perhaps the most spiritually and neurologically significant point in the sequence. Known in TCM as GV-20 (Bai Hui) or "The Meeting of a Hundred," it is the point where all the Yang meridians of the body meet.

In modern EFT, we use the crown of the head as the anchor. It serves to integrate the work done on the individual "table legs" of the problem (as discussed in Module 1). While the other points focus on specific emotional aspects, the TH point often brings a sense of spiritual perspective and cognitive clarity.

3. The Evolution of Gary Craig: Why TH was Added

It is a common point of confusion for new practitioners that the original Thought Field Therapy (TFT) sequences did not always include the crown of the head. When Gary Craig developed EFT, his goal was to create a "Universal Basic Recipe" that worked regardless of the specific diagnosis.

The addition of the Top of Head point was a strategic evolution for two reasons:

  • The "Meeting" Point: Because GV-20 connects so many meridians, it acts as a "fail-safe" point. If a practitioner missed a specific meridian earlier in the round, the TH point likely provides a secondary stimulation to that system.
  • Psychological Closure: Tapping on the crown feels like a "full stop" at the end of a sentence. It signals to the brain that the somatic processing of that specific aspect is complete for this round.

4. Sequence Directionality: Top-to-Bottom Logic

You may notice that the EFT sequence generally moves from the top of the face (Eyebrow) downward to the Under Arm, before "jumping" back to the Top of Head. This is not accidental. In the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol™, we follow the natural descent of energy.

Stimulating the points in a descending order (EB → SE → UE → UN → Ch → CB → UA) mimics the grounding process. We are moving the "charge" from the overactive mind (facial points) down through the body (torso points) to be released. Returning to the Top of Head at the end creates a "closed loop" of energy, ensuring the client feels integrated rather than "opened up" and left raw.

5. Safety, Comfort, and Accessibility

As a professional practitioner, you must be sensitive to the physical limitations of your clients. The Under Arm point, in particular, can be challenging for certain populations.

Practitioner Alert: Physical Comfort

Clients with shoulder injuries, frozen shoulder, or obesity may find reaching for the UA point painful or embarrassing. Adjustment: You may instruct the client to tap on the side of their hand (Karate Chop point) as a substitute, or use the "monkey" style (reaching across the body with the opposite hand) to reach the UA point more comfortably.

Furthermore, the Top of Head point should be tapped gently. A 2022 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that while tapping pressure does not significantly alter the physiological outcome, the psychological safety of the client is paramount. Aggressive tapping on the crown can trigger a "startle response" in trauma survivors.

6. Integration: The Final Anchor

When you reach the Top of Head, you are completing the P: Point Sequence phase of our framework. This is the moment to observe the client's micro-expressions. Often, the shift happens exactly at this point—a deep breath, a softening of the eyes, or a slight smile.

Case Study: Cognitive Shift at the Crown

Client: Maria, 29, struggling with "imposter syndrome" before a presentation.

Symptoms: SUDS 9. Rapid heartbeat, shaking hands.

Intervention: We completed two full rounds. On the third round, at the Top of Head point, Maria stopped tapping and laughed.

Outcome: She said, "I just realized, even if I mess up, I'm still the most qualified person in that room." This spontaneous cognitive reframe occurred while anchoring at the TH point. Her SUDS dropped to 1.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the Under Arm (UA) point specifically useful for clients who are "stuck" in a worry loop?

The UA point is on the Spleen meridian (SP-21). In TCM, the Spleen is associated with the transformation of thoughts; stimulation here helps break the cycle of rumination and "circular thinking."

2. What is the anatomical location of the GV-20 point used for the Top of Head tapping?

It is located at the crown of the head. A simple way to find it is to follow the line up from the tips of the ears to the very center-top of the skull.

Key Takeaways

  • The Under Arm (UA) point (Spleen 21) is a powerhouse for releasing helplessness and obsessive worry.
  • The Top of Head (TH) point (GV-20) acts as a "Universal" point that integrates all other meridians.
  • Gary Craig added the TH point to ensure a "Basic Recipe" that provided comprehensive coverage of the meridian system.
  • Sequence directionality moves energy from the mind (face) to the body (torso) for grounding.
  • Always prioritize client comfort; use cross-body reaching or substitutes for the UA point if needed.
