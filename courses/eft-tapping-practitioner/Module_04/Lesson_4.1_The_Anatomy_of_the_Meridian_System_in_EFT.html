1. The Infrastructure: The 12 Primary Meridians

In the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢, the "P" stands for Point Sequence. To master this phase, a practitioner must first understand the "highway system" they are navigating. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) posits that the human body contains a network of energy channels known as meridians, through which Qi (vital energy) flows.

While there are hundreds of acupuncture points, EFT focuses on a specific subset that intersects with the 12 primary meridians. These meridians are bilateral (appearing on both sides of the body) and are typically named after the internal organs they are associated with. In Clinical EFT, we don't necessarily need to know the complex TCM organ associations to achieve results, but understanding the infrastructure provides the practitioner with the necessary biological authority.

A 2021 systematic review of meridian studies noted that meridian points exhibit significantly higher electrical conductivity and lower electrical resistance compared to non-meridian skin areas, with a mean difference in impedance of approximately 40-60%.

2. The Piezoelectric Effect: The Science of the Tap

Why do we tap? The answer lies in mechanosensation and the piezoelectric effect. Our connective tissue (fascia) is primarily composed of collagen, a structural protein that possesses semi-conductive properties. When we apply mechanical pressure‚Äîsuch as the rhythmic tapping used in EFT‚Äîwe generate a tiny electrical charge.

This process, known as the piezoelectric effect, converts mechanical stress into electrical energy. In the context of the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢, this electrical signal travels through the fascial network at speeds far exceeding nerve impulses. This signal is hypothesized to reach the amygdala, signaling a "safety" response that counteracts the stress response targeted during the "T" (Target) and "A" (Assess) phases of our framework.

4. The 'Short Form' Sequence: 8 Primary Points

In the early days of Thought Field Therapy (TFT), complex "algorithms" were used for different emotions. Clinical EFT simplified this into the Short Form Sequence, which stimulates the start or end points of major meridians, ensuring a "full system flush" regardless of the specific emotion. These 8 points are:

  • Eyebrow (EB): Bladder Meridian (releases trauma/frustration).
  • Side of Eye (SE): Gallbladder Meridian (releases rage/resentment).
  • Under Eye (UE): Stomach Meridian (releases anxiety/fear).
  • Under Nose (UN): Governing Vessel (releases shame/powerlessness).
  • Chin (CH): Central Vessel (releases confusion/uncertainty).
    • Collarbone (CB): Kidney Meridian (the "K27" point; vital for overall energy).
  • Under Arm (UA): Spleen Meridian (releases guilt/worry).
  • Top of Head (TH): "Hundred Meetings" point (connects all meridians).
