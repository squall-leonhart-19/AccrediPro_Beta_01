1. The Under Nose (UN) Point: Governing Vessel 26

As we descend from the upper facial points‚Äîthe Eyebrow, Side of Eye, and Under Eye‚Äîwe reach the transition from the ocular orbit to the midline of the face. The Under Nose (UN) point, located in the philtrum (the vertical groove between the base of the nose and the upper lip), corresponds to Governing Vessel 26 (GV 26).

In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), GV 26 is known as "Water Center" or "Human Center." It is a powerful revival point used in clinical acupuncture to restore consciousness after fainting. In the context of EFT, stimulating this point targets feelings of embarrassment, self-consciousness, and social anxiety. It acts as a bridge between the brain's motor cortex and the limbic system, helping to "ground" a client who feels overwhelmed by the judgment of others.

A 2021 study utilizing infrared thermography found that stimulating GV 26 significantly altered facial temperature patterns associated with the autonomic nervous system, suggesting a direct modulation of the sympathetic "fight-or-flight" response (n=45, p < 0.05).

2. The Chin (CH) Point: Conception Vessel 24

Directly below the UN point lies the Chin (CH) point, specifically located in the indentation between the lower lip and the prominence of the chin. This point is Conception Vessel 24 (CV 24), often referred to as "Container of Fluids."

While the Governing Vessel (UN) governs the "Yang" or active energy of the back and spine, the Conception Vessel (CH) governs the "Yin" or receptive energy of the front of the body. Psychologically, the CH point is the primary release valve for shame, guilt, and repressed emotions. When a client feels "choked up" or unable to speak their truth, the CH point often holds the somatic tension of those unsaid words.

3. The Collarbone (CB) Point: Kidney 27

The Collarbone (CB) point is arguably the most significant point in the entire EFT sequence. Located approximately one inch down and one inch out from the "U-shaped" notch at the top of the breastbone (sternum), it corresponds to Kidney 27 (K27), the terminal point of the Kidney meridian.

In clinical practice, K27 is considered a "master point." It is the junction where all the meridians of the body meet or are influenced. If the meridian system were a house, K27 would be the main circuit breaker. Stimulating this point helps to "un-scramble" the body's energy, making it particularly effective for clients who feel "switched" or psychologically reversed (where their energy is working against their stated goals).

4. Techniques for Effective CB Stimulation

Because the CB point is so critical, practitioners often use different methods to stimulate it depending on the client's intensity levels. In the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢, we teach two primary variations:

The "U-Shape" Two-Finger Tap

Using the index and middle fingers of one hand to tap precisely on the K27 points on one or both sides. This is ideal for precision work when targeting specific, nuanced aspects of a trauma.

The "Collarbone Thump"

Using a loose fist or the flat of the hand to gently "thump" the entire upper chest area. This stimulates both K27 points simultaneously and often engages the Thymus gland. This is the "emergency" technique for high-intensity SUDS (8-10) or panic attacks.

5. Clinical Significance in the 'P' Phase

The transition from the facial points to the torso marks a shift in the Investigate Changes (Module 5) phase. While facial points often address the cognitive and immediate emotional "spikes," the torso points (CB, Under Arm) tend to reach deeper, more systemic physiological states.

Research by Dr. Peta Stapleton has shown that tapping on these specific points reduces cortisol levels by an average of 24% to 37% in just 60 minutes. The CB point is often where the first "sigh of relief" or spontaneous deep breath occurs in a session, signaling a shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic dominance.

6. Integration & Protocol Flow

As a practitioner, your goal is to maintain a rhythmic, steady flow. The transition from Under Eye (UE) to Under Nose (UN) should feel like a natural descent. Note that the UN and CH points are midline points, meaning they are singular, whereas the CB points are bilateral. You may tap the CB points with both hands or just one; the neurological effect remains consistent due to the "meridian crossover" effect in the brain.
