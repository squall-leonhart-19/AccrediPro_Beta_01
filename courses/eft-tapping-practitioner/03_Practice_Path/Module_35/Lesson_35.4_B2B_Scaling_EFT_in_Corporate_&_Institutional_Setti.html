In a clinical setting, we talk about "trauma," "meridians," and "emotional freedom." In a boardroom, these terms can often trigger skepticism or be dismissed as "too soft." To scale into corporate environments, you must adopt the language of performance, resilience, and neuro-regulation.

    3. Designing 'Corporate Tapping Workshops'

    In B2B settings, time is the most expensive commodity. You cannot expect a corporate group to sit for a 90-minute deep dive. You must adapt the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢ into modular, high-impact segments.

    The "15-Minute Executive Reset" Framework:

    • T (Target): Identify the immediate "Next 2 Hours" stressor (e.g., the upcoming presentation).
    • A (Assess): Quick 0-10 SUDS check.
    • P (Prepare): Simplified Setup: "Even though I'm feeling this pressure, I'm focused and ready."
    • P (Point Sequence): 2 Rounds of rapid tapping on the face and collarbone points.
    • I (Investigate): Notice the shift in breath or shoulder tension.
    • N (Navigate): Address any "residual" tightness.
    • G (Ground): One deep "Box Breath" to integrate.

    4. Navigating Institutional Contracts & Procurement

    Working with institutions requires a higher level of administrative rigor. You are no longer just a practitioner; you are a vendor. To scale successfully, you must have the following in place:

    1. Professional Indemnity & Liability Insurance
    Most corporations require a minimum of $1M‚Äì$2M in professional liability coverage. Ensure your policy specifically covers "Group Facilitation" and "Corporate Consulting."
    2. Data Privacy & GDPR/HIPAA Compliance
    When collecting SUDS data or feedback from employees, you must guarantee anonymity. Use encrypted platforms for any digital surveys or intake forms.
    3. The Master Service Agreement (MSA)
    Institutional contracts usually involve an MSA which outlines intellectual property (you own the protocol, they own the employee data) and "Scope of Work" (SOW) which defines exactly what you will deliver.

    5. Long-Term Integration: Wellness Champions

    The most effective way to scale B2B is to move away from being the "sole provider" to being a "strategic partner." This is achieved by training internal Wellness Champions.

    By teaching a select group of HR staff or team leads the basics of the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢ (specifically for non-clinical stress), you create a sustainable ecosystem. This allows you to charge for:

    • Train-the-Trainer Certification: High-ticket licensing of your specific corporate curriculum.
    • Ongoing Supervision: Monthly calls with Champions to discuss implementation challenges.
    • Digital Access: Subscriptions to your practitioner-led library of tapping videos for the company intranet.
