2. Scaling 'A' and 'I' for Mass Feedback

The greatest challenge in group facilitation is maintaining the precision of the Assess Intensity (A) and Investigate Changes (I) phases. In a 1:1 session, you can wait for a clientâ€™s verbal SUDS. In a group of 200, you need a data-driven feedback loop.

The "Mass SUDS" Collection Method

To scale Assess Intensity, use the following hierarchy of feedback:

  • Digital Polling: For online sessions (Zoom/Webinar), use integrated polls to see a bell curve of SUDS (0-10) before and after rounds.
  • Somatic Hand Signals: In-person, ask participants to hold up fingers representing their SUDS level. This allows you to scan the room and identify "outliers" (those still at an 8 or 9).
  • The "Chat Avalanche": Ask everyone to type their current intensity into the chat but wait for your signal to hit "Enter." The visual rush of numbers provides immediate data on the group's progress.

4. Safety & Abreaction Protocols

Scaling introduces the risk of Emotional Contagion. If one participant has a major abreaction (uncontrolled emotional release), it can trigger a sympathetic nervous system response in the rest of the group. As a Master Practitioner, your primary role is "Guardian of the Container."

The "Stop-and-Stabilize" Protocol

If you detect a participant in distress during a large group session:

  1. Do not stop tapping: Keep the group tapping on the "Side of Hand" or "Collarbone" point. Rhythmic tapping is inherently grounding.
  2. Ground (G) the Individual: Use direct eye contact (if in person) or direct verbal cues (if online) to bring them back to the room. "Look at me, keep tapping, feel your feet on the floor."
  3. The "Co-Facilitator" Safety Net: For groups over 50, always have a "Safety Monitor" (another trained practitioner) who can move to a breakout room (online) or a side area (in person) with the distressed individual without interrupting the group flow.

5. The Surrogate Tapping Model

In advanced group dynamics, we move beyond "Borrowing Benefits" into Surrogate Tapping. This involves the group tapping on behalf of a specific individual or a global issue. Scientifically, this aligns with the Maharishi Effect research, suggesting that a small percentage of a population practicing coherence can measurably reduce stress markers in the surrounding environment.

In the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocolâ„¢, surrogate work is used in the Navigate Aspects (N) phase. We ask the group to imagine they are "stepping into the shoes" of the target. 6. Hybrid Facilitation Strategies

To truly scale, you must integrate live facilitation with asynchronous (pre-recorded) content. This is the "Hybrid T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Model."

  • The Core Sequence: Record high-quality, generic rounds for the Point Sequence (P) that address common themes (e.g., "The Inner Critic").
  • The Live Bridge: Use live sessions to handle the Target (T) and Investigate (I) phases, which require the most practitioner intuition.
  • Data Integration: Use your pre-recorded content to gather "baseline" data via surveys, which you then address during your live "Growth Intensives."
