1. The Economics of Impact: Moving Beyond the Hourly Rate

As a certified EFT practitioner, you possess a skill set capable of profound transformation. However, the traditional 1:1 model contains an inherent economic ceiling: you can only help as many people as your calendar allows. Scaling your impact is not merely a business strategy; it is an ethical imperative to bring healing to a wider demographic while preserving your own vital energy.

5. Practitioner Longevity: Business "Ground & Integrate"

To scale sustainably, you must apply the principles of Module 7 (Ground & Integrate) to your business infrastructure. Burnout often occurs not from the clinical work itself, but from the cognitive load of disorganized business systems.

Systematic Grounding for the CEO-Practitioner

Implement these four "Business Anchors" to ensure longevity:

  • Energy Audits: Track which activities (1:1, groups, admin) energize vs. drain you.
  • Boundaries as Medicine: Use automated scheduling (e.g., Calendly) to prevent "session creep."
  • The Integration Day: One day per week with zero client contact, dedicated solely to strategic growth and personal tapping.
  • Somatic Support: Regular supervision or "swap sessions" with a peer practitioner to clear secondary trauma.
