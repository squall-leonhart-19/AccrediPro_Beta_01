The 12-Point CIQ Architecture

In Level 1, we learned that specificity is the primary driver of clinical results in EFT. However, practitioners often encounter "The Global Wall"‚Äîa state where the client feels overwhelmed by a general sense of unease but cannot pinpoint a specific event. The Core Issues Questionnaire (CIQ) is our diagnostic scalpel, designed to bypass the conscious mind's defenses and access the limbic system's "trauma files."

The CIQ is not merely a list of questions; it is a neurological provocation tool. A 2022 study by Stapleton et al. indicated that structured clinical assessments in energy psychology sessions increased the likelihood of identifying "Apex" events by 64% compared to unstructured dialogue. The CIQ targets 12 specific dimensions of the human experience that are most likely to store emotional "charge."

"A 2023 meta-analysis of trauma-focused interventions (n=1,420) found that practitioners who utilized structured discovery tools like the CIQ achieved a 22% higher rate of symptom remission in complex PTSD cases."

Assessing the 'Big 4' Core Fears

At the root of almost every "Table Top" (global issue) lies one of four foundational fears. These are the biological drivers of the stress response. When we tap, we aren't just tapping on a memory; we are tapping on the underlying threat perception associated with one of these Big 4:

Core Fear Biological Driver Common "Table Top" Presentation
Abandonment Survival/Isolation Co-dependency, fear of being alone, panic attacks.
Rejection Social Exclusion Social anxiety, perfectionism, people-pleasing.
Failure Resource/Status Loss Procrastination, imposter syndrome, workaholism.
Loss of Control Safety/Autonomy OCD tendencies, chronic anger, health anxiety.

By identifying which of the "Big 4" is active, the practitioner can refine the Setup Statement in the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢. For example, if a client is afraid of public speaking, is it a fear of Failure (looking incompetent) or Rejection (being disliked)? The tapping points remain the same, but the cognitive target changes entirely.

Case Study: The Perfectionist's Paralysis

Focus: Identifying Core Fears

S

Sarah, 42

Presenting Symptom: Severe procrastination on a new business venture.

Initial Assessment: Sarah presented with "vague anxiety" about her business. Her SUDS was 8/10 whenever she sat down to work. Initial tapping on "this anxiety" yielded no results (The Apex Effect).

CIQ Intervention: Using the CIQ, the practitioner asked: "If you succeed and then lose it all, what is the worst part?" Sarah replied, "My father would say he was right about me." This shifted the target from "Business Anxiety" to the core fear of Failure and Rejection.

Outcome: We identified a "Table Leg": 3rd-grade spelling bee where she forgot the word 'Success' and her father laughed. After 3 rounds of tapping on that specific event, her procrastination SUDS dropped to 0/10.

The CIQ Matrix: Linking Physical to Emotional

The "CIQ Matrix" is a Level 2 technique that bridges Somatic Mapping (covered in Lesson 4) with Cognitive Beliefs. The body often stores the "snapshot" of a trauma before the mind can label it. When a client reports a physical sensation (e.g., "a knot in my stomach"), the CIQ Matrix asks specific prompts to translate that sensation into a memory.

Prompts include:

  • "If this knot had a voice, what would it be screaming?"
  • "When was the first time you felt this exact pressure in your chest?"
  • "If this physical pain was a scene from a movie, who else is in the room?"

Strategies for 'Targeting the Issue' (T) in Vague Anxiety

Vague anxiety is often a "protective globalizing" mechanism. The brain keeps the issue vague so the practitioner doesn't "poke" the trauma. To implement the T (Target) phase of our protocol effectively, we use Narrowing Funnels.

The Funnel Technique:

  1. The Global Statement: "I'm just stressed."
  2. The Somatic Inquiry: "Where is that stress in your body right now?"
  3. The CIQ Provocation: "If that stress were a person from your past, who would it be?"
  4. The Specific Event: "What is the specific time that person made you feel this way?"

"In a 2024 clinical observation study, practitioners who spent 10+ minutes on the 'Targeting' phase using CIQ reduced the total number of rounds required for SUDS neutralization by 40%."

Prioritizing 'Table Legs' vs. 'Table Tops'

We must remember the Table Metaphor: The "Table Top" is the problem (e.g., "I have low self-esteem"). The "Table Legs" are the specific events that support that belief. If you tap on the table top, you are trying to break a thick piece of wood with a hammer. If you tap on the legs, the table collapses on its own.

How to Prioritize:

  • Intensity: Which memory has the highest current SUDS?
  • Chronology: Which memory happened first (The Foundation Leg)?
  • Thematic Weight: Which memory most clearly represents the "Big 4" fear?

Case Study: The "Loss of Control" Matrix

Focus: Somatic CIQ Linking

M

Marcus, 55

Presenting Symptom: Chronic digestive issues and "control freak" tendencies at work.

The CIQ Matrix: Marcus identified a "tightening in the solar plexus" (Somatic Marker). When asked the CIQ prompt, "When did you first have to be the one in charge to stay safe?" he recalled being 12 years old, hiding his younger sister in a closet while his parents had a violent argument.

The Target: We didn't tap on "digestive issues." We tapped on the 12-year-old's hands shaking while holding the closet door shut (Specific Event/Table Leg).

Results: SUDS on the memory went from 10 to 0. Within 48 hours, Marcus reported a 70% reduction in digestive discomfort, demonstrating the somatic-emotional link.

Clinical Implementation: When to Deploy the CIQ

The CIQ should not be used as an interrogation. It is an investigative partnership. As an advanced practitioner, you deploy the CIQ when:

  1. The SUDS level is not moving after two rounds of tapping.
  2. The client is "looping"‚Äîrepeating the same global complaints.
  3. The client reports "feeling nothing" (numbness is a somatic indicator of a deep core issue).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Abandonment" considered a core fear in the CIQ framework?

Biologically, for a social species, isolation (abandonment) was equivalent to death. It triggers the highest level of survival threat in the amygdala, often manifesting as global anxiety or panic in modern contexts.

2. What is the primary difference between a "Table Top" and a "Table Leg"?

The Table Top is the global issue (e.g., "I'm not good enough"), while the Table Leg is a specific, sensory-rich event that supports that belief (e.g., "My teacher yelled at me in 2nd grade for failing math"). We tap on legs to collapse the top.

Key Takeaways

  • The CIQ is a 12-point diagnostic tool used to break through globalized, vague anxiety.
  • Most trauma is rooted in the "Big 4" fears: Abandonment, Rejection, Failure, and Loss of Control.
  • The CIQ Matrix uses somatic (body) sensations as a bridge to find hidden emotional "Table Legs."
  • Specific events (Table Legs) must be prioritized based on intensity, chronology, and thematic relevance.
  • Effective targeting (T) using Narrowing Funnels reduces the total time needed for emotional neutralisation.
