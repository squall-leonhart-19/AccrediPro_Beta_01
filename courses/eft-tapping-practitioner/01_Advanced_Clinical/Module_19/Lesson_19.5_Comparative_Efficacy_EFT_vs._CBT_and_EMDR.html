4. The Dropout Rate Phenomenon

One of the most critical metrics in clinical success is the attrition rate (dropout rate). Exposure-based therapies like Prolonged Exposure (PE) or even some forms of CBT often see dropout rates as high as 30% to 45%. This is largely because the process of "reliving" trauma without immediate physiological regulation is too distressing for many clients.

In contrast, EFT studies consistently report dropout rates below 10%. Why? Because the Point Sequence provides an immediate "calming signal" to the amygdala. By Assessing Intensity (Module 2) and using the "Ground & Integrate" (Module 7) phase, EFT practitioners keep clients within the "Window of Tolerance," making the healing process feel safe rather than re-traumatizing.
