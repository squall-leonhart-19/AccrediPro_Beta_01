4. The 'Borrowing Benefits' Phenomenon

One of the most powerful tools in the Master Practitioner‚Äôs arsenal is the group dynamic known as Borrowing Benefits. When a group watches a practitioner work with a single individual, the observers‚Äô SUDS levels drop significantly, even if they aren't tapping on their own specific issue.

A 2019 meta-analysis (n=1,240) demonstrated that group EFT sessions produced effect sizes (d=1.25) comparable to individual sessions. This is attributed to "Social Baseline Theory," where the brain perceives the presence of a regulated group as a signal of safety, lowering the amygdala's threat response globally.

Mastering Group Dynamics

To maximize Borrowing Benefits in a workshop setting:

  • Phase 1: Have the group identify a "side issue" of their own before the demonstration starts.
  • Phase 2: Instruct the group to tap along with the person on stage, using the person's words.
  • Phase 3: At the end of the round, have the group check their own SUDS, not the person on stage.

6. Healer vs. Facilitator Archetypes

The "Healer" archetype often carries a "need to fix." This can create a power imbalance and foster client dependency. The Facilitator archetype, however, views the client as the primary agent of change, with the practitioner acting as a skilled guide through the T.A.P.P.I.N.G. Protocol‚Ñ¢.
