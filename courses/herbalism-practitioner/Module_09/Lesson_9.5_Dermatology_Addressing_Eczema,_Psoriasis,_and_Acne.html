Eczema: Barrier Dysfunction and Immune Hyper-reactivity

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is characterized by a "leaky" skin barrier and a Th2-dominant immune response. Research published in The Lancet (2022) confirms that filaggrin mutations contribute to barrier loss, but systemic inflammation fuels the fire. Our herbal approach focuses on Individualized Formulation (I) that addresses both the barrier and the immune system.

Key Botanicals:

  • Chickweed (Stellaria media): Topically cooling and anti-pruritic (itch-relieving). It is specific for "hot" eczema.
  • Nettle Leaf (Urtica dioica): A nutritive alterative that stabilizes mast cells, reducing the histamine response common in atopic individuals.
  • Burdock Root (Arctium lappa): Rich in inulin and arctigenin, it supports the liver and colon, ensuring toxins are processed internally rather than through the skin.
