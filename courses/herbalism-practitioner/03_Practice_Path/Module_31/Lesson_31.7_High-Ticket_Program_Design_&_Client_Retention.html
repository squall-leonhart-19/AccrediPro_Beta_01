  1. Onboarding (Week 1): Welcome kit, expectation setting, and the first "ROOTS" deep dive.

4. Maintenance Memberships: Long-Term Constitutional Support

What happens when a client completes a 6-month program? In a transactional model, they leave. In a sustainable practice, they transition into a Maintenance Membership. This reflects the Traditional Wisdom (T) that health is a rhythmic, seasonal process, not a destination.

Effective maintenance memberships often include:

  • Seasonal Formula Updates: Transitioning from warming, circulatory stimulants in winter to cooling, lymphatic alteratives in spring.
  • Constitutional "Tune-ups": A quarterly 30-minute session to re-evaluate the Six Tissue States.
  • Preferential Access: Guaranteed stock of bespoke tinctures or priority scheduling.

6. Implementation Framework: The ROOTS Sustainability Audit

To implement high-ticket programs, you must audit your current practice. Ask yourself:

  • Research (R): Do I have the evidence-based data to justify a 6-month timeline for this specific condition (e.g., thyroid antibodies or gut microbiome shifts)?
  • Observe (O): What automated systems can I use to collect client feedback between sessions?
  • Sustainable (S): Does my pricing reflect the true "Cost of Goods Sold" (COGS) including my time, the herbs, and the software?
