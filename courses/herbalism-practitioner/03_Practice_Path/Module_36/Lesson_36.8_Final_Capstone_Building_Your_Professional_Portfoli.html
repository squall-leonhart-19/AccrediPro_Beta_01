1. Architecture of the Clinical Portfolio

Your professional portfolio is not merely a collection of assignments; it is the tangible evidence of your clinical reasoning. As you transition from student to AccrediPro Certified Herbalism Practitioner, this document serves as your "proof of concept" for insurance providers, professional associations, and potential clinical partners.

A high-level portfolio must demonstrate your ability to navigate the complexity of human physiology while maintaining the vitalist roots of herbal tradition. It requires a balance of quantitative data (dosage math, lab markers) and qualitative observations (energetic shifts, tissue state changes).

3. The 12-Month Sustainable Practice (S) Roadmap

The "S" in ROOTS stands for Sustainable Practice. This encompasses both the environmental sustainability of your herbs and the economic sustainability of your business. For final certification, you are required to submit a 12-month growth strategy.

This roadmap must address three critical pillars:

  • Financial Viability: Projected overhead (insurance, software, apothecary stock) vs. revenue targets based on your "Specialization Path" (Module 36, L6).
  • Supply Chain Integrity: A list of 5-8 verified "Gold Standard" suppliers who meet the ethical criteria established in Module 5.
  • Clinical Evolution: A plan for continuing education (CEUs), focusing on emerging research in the gut-brain axis or botanical immunomodulation.
