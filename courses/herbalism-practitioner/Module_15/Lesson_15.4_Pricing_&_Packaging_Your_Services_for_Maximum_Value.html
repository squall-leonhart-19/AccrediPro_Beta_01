Hourly vs. Outcome-Based Models

The "Hourly Trap" creates a conflict of interest: the faster you help a client, the less you get paid. Furthermore, a single hour is rarely enough to conduct a thorough Vitality & Vigor Assessment (V) and perform a Toxicological & Safety Screening (T). Outcome-based packaging focuses on the transformation (e.g., "The 90-Day Hormone Harmony Journey") rather than the clock.

The Hourly Model (Avoid)

"It's $85 for a 60-minute consultation."

  • Client views you as an expense.
  • Inconsistent income for you.
  • Client drops off after 1 session.

The V.I.T.A.L. Package (Goal)

"It's $1,800 for the 12-week Digestive Restoration Program."

  • Client views you as an investment.
  • Predictable, upfront cash flow.
  • Guaranteed time for integration.

The Founding Member Strategy

If you are just starting, the "Founding Member" strategy is your fastest path to revenue. This involves offering a beta-discount to your first 5-10 clients in exchange for their commitment to the process and a detailed testimonial upon completion.

Pro Tip: The Beta Bridge

Tell your prospects: "I am launching my new V.I.T.A.L. Digestive Protocol. The regular investment will be $1,800, but I'm looking for 5 'Founding Members' to go through it at a 40% discount ($1,080) so I can document your success stories. Would you like one of those spots?"

Handling Pricing Objections

When a client says, "That's too expensive," they are usually saying, "I don't yet see the value commensurate with the price." Your job is to bridge that gap by focusing on the cost of inaction.

Ask the client: "What is it costing you to not solve this? How much have you already spent on supplements that didn't work because they weren't Individualized (I)? What is the value of having your energy back for your children?" By shifting the focus from the price of the herbs to the value of the outcome, the objection often dissolves.
