2. The Emunctory Theory of Chronic Skin Disease

The Emunctory Theory, rooted in Physiomedicalism and Vitalism, posits that health is maintained through the free flow of fluids and the efficient removal of waste. In dermatology, we assess the "load" on the emunctories. If a client presents with chronic eczema, we do not merely apply a topical steroid; we ask: "Why is the skin being forced to do the liver's job?"

The ROOTS Assessment of Skin Tissue States:

  • Heat/Excitation: Redness, burning, rapid onset (e.g., acute urticaria). Requires refrigerants and sedatives.
  • Cold/Depression: Pale, slow-healing, chronic (e.g., cold sores, chronic stasis dermatitis). Requires stimulants and rubefacients.
  • Damp/Stagnation: Oozing, weeping, cystic (e.g., cystic acne, HS). Requires alteratives and lymphatics.

3. Advanced Protocols for Psoriasis & HS

Psoriasis is a Th17-mediated autoimmune condition characterized by keratinocyte hyperproliferation. Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS) is a chronic inflammatory condition of the hair follicle. Both require a dual-action approach: systemic immunomodulation and targeted topical antimicrobial/vulnerary support.

4. Lymphatics: Clearing Deep-Seated Tissue Congestion

In dermatology, the "swamp" must be drained. If the lymphatic system is stagnant, cellular debris accumulates in the interstitial fluid, leading to "boggy" skin conditions. Two primary herbs dominate this clinical application:

  • Galium aparine (Cleavers): The premier "lymphatic alterative" for acute, hot, or weeping conditions. It moves fluid specifically toward the kidneys for excretion.
  • Ceanothus americanus (Red Root): Indicated for chronic, hard, or "stuck" lymphatic congestion. It is essential in HS cases where tunneling and deep nodules are present.

5. Wound Healing & Scar Tissue Remodeling

The goal of clinical dermatology isn't just to stop the disease, but to restore the integumentary architecture. This involves collagen synthesis and the prevention of hypertrophic scarring (keloids).

Centella asiatica (Gotu Kola): The Architect

Centella stimulates Type I collagen synthesis while modulating the inflammatory phase of wound healing. It contains asiaticoside, which increases antioxidant levels at the wound site. In clinical practice, it is used internally and externally to "knit" tissues together without excessive scar formation.

Helichrysum italicum: The "Everlasting" Healer

High in neryl acetate, Helichrysum essential oil (diluted) or infused oil is the gold standard for scar remodeling. It acts as a mild anticoagulant, preventing the "pooling" of blood that leads to bruising and fibrous adhesions. It is particularly effective for post-surgical scars and old striae (stretch marks).
