Pulse Analysis: Depth & Quality

Pulse analysis in herbalism is less about counting beats and more about feeling the architectural quality of the blood flow. We use the radial artery, applying three levels of pressure: Superficial, Middle, and Deep.

1. The Floating Pulse (Superficial)

Felt with a light touch. Indicates the body's energy is moving outward, often seen in acute infections or high-stress "fight or flight" states.

2. The Deep Pulse (Hidden)

Only felt by pressing near the bone. Suggests internal "Cold," deficiency, or deep-seated chronic issues where the "V" (Vitality) is struggling to reach the surface.

3. The Wiry/Tense Pulse

Feels like a guitar string under the finger. This is the classic pulse of Tension or Liver Qi Stagnation. It signals a need for antispasmodic herbs (e.g., Viburnum opulus).
