The "V" in our methodology stands for Vitality & Vigor Assessment. This is the diagnostic cornerstone. We are looking for the "spark" in the client. Energetic Obstructions to the Vis

The VMN often fails to resolve chronic disease because of obstructions. In energetics, these are categorized as Heat, Cold, Dampness, or Dryness. Think of these as "physiological noise" that prevents the body's internal "healing signal" from being heard.

For example, Dampness (clinically correlated with metabolic endotoxemia and lymphatic congestion) acts like a thick fog. The immune system (part of the VMN) cannot effectively navigate the interstitial fluid to reach the site of infection or repair. In such cases, the V.I.T.A.L. approach requires "clearing the path" with alteratives (lymphatic cleansers) before tonifying the system.

In chronic states, the body often reaches a "maladaptive equilibrium." The VMN has effectively "given up" on total resolution and is now merely managing the damage. Clinical Parameters for Assessment

How do we quantify the Vis? Use these four pillars during your V.I.T.A.L. intake:

  • The Thermic Response: Does the client mount a fever when ill? Lack of fever in the presence of infection is a sign of a "depressed" Vis.
  • The Tongue Topography: A tongue with "root" (the coating seems to grow out of the tongue) indicates strong digestive VMN. A tongue that looks "peeled" or "shiny" indicates exhausted Yin/Fluids, a state of low VMN reserve.
  • The Tissue State: Is there "Atrophy" (Dryness/Wasting) or "Torpor" (Damp/Stagnation)? Both are states where the VMN is hindered.
  • The Recovery Rate: Ask: "How long does it take you to recover from a minor cut or a common cold?" This is the most direct question to assess the Vis.

The Role of Trophorestoratives: Feeding the Vis

When the VMN is exhausted (Low V.I.T.A.L. 'V' score), "stimulating" herbs like Echinacea are often ineffective or even draining. Instead, we use Trophorestoratives. These are herbs that "feed" the organ or system, restoring its functional integrity. Examples include Avena sativa for the nervous system or Crataegus spp. for the cardiovascular system.

By providing the building blocks (Active Constituent Synergy - the 'A' in V.I.T.A.L.), we allow the Vis to restart its repair work. This is the difference between "whipping a tired horse" (stimulation) and "giving the horse oats and rest" (trophorestoration).
