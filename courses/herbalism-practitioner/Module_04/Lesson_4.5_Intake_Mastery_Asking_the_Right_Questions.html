Questions for Vitality (V): Assessing Constitutional Strength

The first letter of our method, V (Vitality & Vigor), requires us to determine how much "fuel" is in the client's tank. We are looking for the Vis Medicatrix Naturae. If vitality is low, we must use nourishing tonics; if vitality is high but blocked, we use moving or clearing herbs.

To assess vitality, avoid asking "Do you have energy?" Instead, use comparative and situational questions:

  • "If you have a busy day, how long does it take you to recover the next morning?" (Assesses Adrenal/Kidney Essence)
  • "Between 2:00 PM and 4:00 PM, what is your state of mind and body?" (Checks for Post-Prandial metabolic efficiency)
  • "When you get a common cold, does it manifest as a quick fever that resolves, or does it linger for weeks in the chest?" (Assesses Immune Vigor)

Presenting: Chronic fatigue, bloating, and "feeling stuck."

Initial Assessment: Sarah complained of fatigue. A conventional intake might suggest "stimulant" herbs like Ginseng. However, through V.I.T.A.L. Inquiry, the practitioner asked: "How do you feel after a warm bowl of soup versus a salad?"

Outcome: Energy levels improved by 40% within 14 days simply by addressing the energetic mismatch, without any caffeine or stimulants.

Safety & Toxicology (T) Screening: The Critical "T"

As an AccrediPro Certified Practitioner, your highest priority is Primum non nocere (First, do no harm). The T in V.I.T.A.L. stands for Toxicological & Safety Screening. You must ask about every pharmaceutical, over-the-counter medication, and supplement.

Specific questions for the "T" phase:

  • "Are you taking any blood thinners (e.g., Warfarin, Aspirin)?" (Crucial for herbs like Ginkgo or Dan Shen).
  • "Have you ever had an allergic reaction to the Asteraceae family (daisies, ragweed)?" (Crucial for Chamomile, Calendula, Echinacea).
  • "Are there any planned surgeries in the next 3 weeks?" (Many herbs must be discontinued 7-14 days prior to surgery).
  • "What is your current alcohol consumption?" (Impacts liver metabolism of herbal constituents).

Environmental & Lifestyle Context: The "L" Foundation

The L (Long-term Integration) begins during the intake. We must understand the client's environment to ensure our protocol is sustainable. A complex 5-herb decoction that requires 20 minutes of simmering will fail for a single mother of three working two jobs.

The "Sustainability" Inquiry:
1. "On a scale of 1-10, how much capacity do you have to change your morning routine?"
2. "Do you prefer tea, tinctures, or capsules? Why?"
3. "Who else lives in your home, and do they support your health goals?" (Social determinants of health).

Structuring the Intake Flow

A professional intake should follow a logical sequence to ensure no data is missed while maintaining rapport. A 2019 meta-analysis of clinical communication found that a "funnel" approachâ€”starting broad and getting specificâ€”yields the highest diagnostic accuracy.

  1. The Opening (5-10 mins): "What brings you here today, and what is your primary goal?" (Uninterrupted listening).
  2. The Timeline (10 mins): "When was the last time you felt truly well?" (Mapping the progression of the imbalance).
  3. The V.I.T.A.L. Review (20 mins): Systematic inquiry into Vitality, Energetics, and Safety.
  4. The Physical Assessment (10 mins): Tongue, pulse, and nail analysis (as covered in Lesson 4.4).
  5. The Synthesis (5 mins): Mirroring back what you heard to ensure the client feels understood.

Key Takeaways

  • The intake is a clinical intervention; the first 10 minutes should be focused on deep, uninterrupted listening.
  • Vitality (V) is assessed through recovery capacity and circadian energy patterns, not just a "yes/no" to fatigue.
  • Translate sensory experiences (heaviness, cold feet, thirst) into energetic patterns (Damp, Cold, Dry).
  • The "T" (Toxicology) screening is mandatory for every client to prevent Herb-Drug Interactions.
  • Long-term (L) success depends on matching the herbal delivery method (tea vs. tincture) to the client's lifestyle capacity.
