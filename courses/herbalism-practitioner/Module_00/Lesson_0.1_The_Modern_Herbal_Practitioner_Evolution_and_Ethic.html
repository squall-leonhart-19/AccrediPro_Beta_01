5. The Practitioner's Code of Ethics

Professionalism is defined by boundaries. As an AccrediPro certified practitioner, you adhere to a strict ethical code that protects both you and your client.

The Principle of "Do No Harm" (Primum non nocere)

In herbalism, this means more than just avoiding toxic plants. It means:

  • Screening every client for Herb-Drug Interactions (HDI).
  • Referring clients to medical doctors when symptoms are beyond your scope (e.g., "Red Flags").
  • Avoiding "prescribing" or "diagnosing" (terms legally reserved for MDs).

Confidentiality and Integrity

You are a custodian of sensitive health data. Professionalism requires HIPAA-compliant (or equivalent) data storage and a "Client-First" mentality where financial gain never outweighs the client's actual physiological needs.
