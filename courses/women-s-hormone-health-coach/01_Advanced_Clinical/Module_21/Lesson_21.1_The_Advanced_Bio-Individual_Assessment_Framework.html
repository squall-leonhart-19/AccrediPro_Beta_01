    4. Utilizing the 'Hormonal Load' Score

    To provide a professional, quantifiable assessment, we utilize the AccrediPro Hormonal Load Score (HLS). This is a weighted calculation that helps you prioritize which system to address first.

    The HLS Calculation (Scale 1-100):

    • Inflammatory Load (30%): CRP levels, gut permeability markers, and food sensitivities.
    • Circadian Load (20%): Cortisol awakening response (CAR) and blue light exposure.
    • Glycemic Load (30%): Post-prandial glucose spikes and insulin levels.
    • Toxic Load (20%): EDC exposure (plastics, fragrances) and Phase II liver clearance.

    A score over 70 indicates that targeted hormone supplementation (like progesterone cream) will likely fail because the "cellular noise" is too high. In these cases, the first 60 days of your treatment plan must focus exclusively on Load Reduction.

    5. Identifying 'Red Flags' & Clinical Boundaries

    Expertise is defined as much by what you *don't* work with as what you do. L2 practitioners must be vigilant for symptoms that mimic hormonal imbalance but indicate underlying pathology.

    Immediate Medical Referral (Red Flags):
    • Post-menopausal bleeding: Any spotting after 12 months of amenorrhea requires an ultrasound to rule out endometrial hyperplasia/cancer.
    • Unexplained weight loss (>10lbs in 3 months): Potential sign of hyperthyroidism or malignancy.
    • Palpable breast or thyroid nodules: Even if labs are "normal."
    • Severe, localized pelvic pain: Rule out ectopic pregnancy or ovarian torsion.
    • Suicidal ideation in PMDD: Requires immediate psychiatric integration.

    A client presents with high androgens and PCOS symptoms. Her fasting insulin is 3.2 ÂµIU/mL (Optimal), but her HS-CRP is 4.2 mg/L (High). Where should your primary intervention focus?

    Focus on the Inflammatory Load. Since her insulin is optimal, her androgen excess is likely "Inflammatory PCOS" rather than "Insulin-Resistant PCOS." Addressing gut health or hidden infections is the priority over a low-carb diet.

    Why is post-menopausal bleeding considered a "Red Flag" even if the client feels fine?

    Post-menopausal bleeding is never "normal" and is a primary clinical indicator for endometrial cancer. It requires immediate transvaginal ultrasound and/or biopsy by a gynecologist.
