2. Designing the 'Lunch & Learn' Series

In the corporate world, time is the most valuable currency. Unlike your 12-week intensive group programs, corporate workshops must be condensed, actionable, and high-impact. The "Lunch & Learn" (45-60 minutes) is the entry point for most B2B relationships.

The High-Impact Format

To maximize engagement, follow the 10-30-20 rule: 10 minutes of "The Problem" (why this matters to their work), 30 minutes of "The Solution" (actionable hormone-balancing strategies), and 20 minutes of "The Q&A" (where the real connection happens).

Topic Idea 1: The Menopause Advantage

Focusing on how to manage brain fog and energy dips during the workday using The Method's blood sugar stabilization techniques.

Topic Idea 2: Cycle Syncing for Peak Productivity

Teaching employees how to align their high-focus tasks with their follicular phase and restorative tasks with their luteal phase.

3. Legal and Liability Considerations

Working with corporations introduces a layer of complexity regarding data privacy and scope of practice. When you transition from individual coaching to a B2B environment, you must align with HR compliance standards.

  • HIPAA & GDPR: Even if you aren't a "covered entity" in your private practice, corporate contracts often require HIPAA-compliant data handling for any employee health metrics you track.
  • The "Coaching vs. Medical Advice" Barrier: HR departments are terrified of liability. Your contracts must explicitly state that you are providing educational wellness services, not medical diagnoses or treatment for conditions like clinical depression or infertility.
  • Professional Liability Insurance: Ensure your policy includes "Corporate/Group Workshop" coverage. Some standard individual policies exclude B2B settings.

4. B2B Sales Strategy: Pitching HR

Selling to a corporation is different from selling to an individual. In B2C, you appeal to emotions; in B2B, you appeal to efficiency and economics. You must identify the "Economic Buyer" (the person who signs the check) and the "Champion" (the person who wants the program).

Key Decision-Makers

  • HR Director: Concerned with compliance, turnover, and employee satisfaction.
  • Benefits Manager: Looking to optimize the ROI of the company's health insurance spend.
  • DEI (Diversity, Equity, & Inclusion) Officer: Focused on gender equity and supporting marginalized groups in the workplace.
The "Pain Point" Pitch

Instead of saying "I help women balance hormones," say: "I provide a framework that reduces the $4,500 per-employee annual loss associated with untreated hormonal fatigue and brain fog."

5. Scaling to Enterprise: Train-the-Trainer

Once you have a successful workshop model, the next step is Enterprise Scaling. You cannot be in five cities at once. To scale, you must move from a "Done-for-You" model to a "Licensed Content" or "Train-the-Trainer" model.

In this model, you train the companyâ€™s internal "Wellness Champions" or HR staff to deliver your AccrediPro-based curriculum. You provide the slides, the handouts, and the tracking metrics, while charging a licensing fee based on the number of employees (PEPM - Per Employee Per Month).

6. Measuring Corporate ROI & Data Analytics

Corporations run on data. To renew your contract, you must prove the program worked. Use the following metrics:

  • Utilization Rate: What percentage of the target population attended?
  • Symptom Reduction: Pre- and post-workshop surveys on energy levels, focus, and sleep.
  • Presenteeism: A measure of how much "distracted work" was reduced.

Check Your Understanding

Why is the "Per Employee Per Month" (PEPM) model often preferred over a flat-fee workshop for large enterprises?

PEPM allows for recurring revenue and scales with the company's growth. It also integrates more easily into corporate benefit budgets, which are often calculated on a per-head basis rather than a one-time marketing expense.

What is the most critical legal distinction a coach must maintain during a corporate workshop?

The distinction between "Educational Wellness Information" and "Medical Advice/Diagnosis." To mitigate liability, coaches must avoid prescribing specific medical treatments or diagnosing conditions like PMDD in a group setting.
