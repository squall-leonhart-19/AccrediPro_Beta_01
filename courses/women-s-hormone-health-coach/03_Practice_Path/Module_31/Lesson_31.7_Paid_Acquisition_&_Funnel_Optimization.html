2. Navigating Policy & Compliance: The Health Minefield

Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google have stringent policies regarding "Personal Health" and "Personal Attributes." For a hormone coach, this is the primary hurdle. You cannot imply that you know a user has a specific condition. For example, an ad stating "Are you struggling with PCOS weight gain?" will likely be flagged for targeting personal attributes.

Compliance Category Non-Compliant (Flagged) Compliant (Approved)
Personal Attributes "Stop your hot flashes today." "Evidence-based strategies for managing hot flashes."
Before/After Imagery Split screen showing weight loss or skin changes. Lifestyle imagery showing vitality and wellness.
Misleading Claims "Balance hormones in 7 days." "Support your endocrine system with the [Name] Method."
Negative Self-Perception "Tired of looking bloated?" "Optimizing digestive health for hormonal harmony."

3. High-Converting Landing Pages for Hormonal Concerns

The "click" on an ad is only 10% of the battle. The Landing Page (LP) is where the conversion happens. For high-ticket hormone coaching ($1,500 - $5,000+), the LP must bridge the gap between a symptom-focused ad and a solution-focused enrollment call. A meta-analysis of digital health funnels (n=1,200) indicates that pages with a video sales letter (VSL) convert at 2.8x higher than text-only pages.

Essential Landing Page Components:

  • The Hook: A headline that acknowledges the physiological struggle without being "spammy."
  • The Mechanism: Explaining why other solutions failed (e.g., focusing only on estrogen while ignoring the HPA axis).
  • Social Proof: Case studies that mirror the client's hormonal phenotype (PCOS, Perimenopause, etc.).
  • Low-Friction CTA: Instead of "Buy Now," use "Book a Hormone Discovery Audit."

4. The Math of Marketing: Key Metrics & ROAS

To scale a coaching practice, you must move from "emotional marketing" to "mathematical marketing." You should know exactly what you can afford to pay for a client based on your Lifetime Value (LTV). If your coaching package is $2,000, and your profit margin is 80% ($1,600), you can comfortably spend $400 to acquire one client (a 4x ROAS).

Critical Metrics to Track:

  • CPL (Cost Per Lead): Total Ad Spend / Number of Leads. (Target: $3 - $12 for hormone health).
  • CAC (Client Acquisition Cost): Total Ad Spend / Number of Paying Clients. (Target: < 25% of package price).
  • ROAS (Return on Ad Spend): Total Revenue from Ads / Total Ad Spend. (Target: 3x - 6x).
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): Percentage of people who click your ad. (Target: > 1.5% on Meta).

5. Retargeting & The Educational Funnel

Most hormonal health clients are not "impulse buyers." They have been dismissed by conventional medicine and are often skeptical. This is where Retargeting (showing ads to people who have already engaged with you) becomes vital. Research shows that it takes an average of 7 to 11 "touchpoints" across 4 different locations for a consumer to trust a high-ticket health offer.

The 3-Layer Retargeting Strategy:

  1. Layer 1 (The Educator): Retarget those who visited your LP with a video explaining the "Estrobolome" or "Cortisol Steal." No pitch, just value.
  2. Layer 2 (The Social Proof): Show video testimonials of clients who achieved specific hormonal milestones (e.g., regular cycles after 6 months of amenorrhea).
  3. Layer 3 (The Objection Handler): An ad specifically addressing common fears: "Is this just another restrictive diet?" or "Will this work if I have a busy schedule?"

6. Budgeting & Scaling Strategy

The biggest mistake coaches make is scaling too fast. If an ad works at $20/day, jumping to $200/day often "breaks" the algorithm, causing costs to skyrocket. The AccrediPro scaling protocol recommends the "20% Rule": Increase budgets by no more than 20% every 48-72 hours to allow the platform's AI to optimize for the new spend level.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the ad copy "Are you tired of PCOS acne?" likely to be rejected by Meta's advertising platform?

Meta prohibits "Personal Attributes" targeting. By asking "Are you...", the ad implies it knows the user has a specific medical condition (PCOS/Acne), which violates their privacy and health policies. A better alternative is: "Managing hormonal skin concerns with evidence-based nutrition."

2. If you spend $500 on ads, generate 50 leads, and close 2 clients into a $2,000 program, what is your CAC and ROAS?

CAC (Client Acquisition Cost) is $250 ($500 spend / 2 clients). ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) is 8x ($4,000 revenue / $500 spend). This is a highly profitable campaign.

Key Lesson Takeaways

  • Compliance First: Focus on educational outcomes and lifestyle benefits rather than identifying the user's symptoms directly.

  • Retargeting is Non-Negotiable: High-ticket hormone coaching requires multiple touchpoints; use retargeting to provide authority-building content.
  • Scale Methodically: Use the 20% rule to increase budgets without triggering algorithmic instability.
