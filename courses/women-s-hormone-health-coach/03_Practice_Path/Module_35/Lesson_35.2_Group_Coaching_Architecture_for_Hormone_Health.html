2. Cohort vs. Evergreen Models

Choosing your delivery model is the most critical architectural decision you will make. This choice dictates your marketing cycle, your energy management, and the community culture.

Feature Cohort-Based (Live) Evergreen (On-Demand)
Enrollment Fixed start/end dates. Rolling enrollment (join anytime).
Community High intimacy; "we're in this together." Dynamic; mix of veterans and newbies.
Support Synchronous (Live Q&As). Asynchronous (Recorded/Chat).
Revenue Large cash injections; "Launch" cycles. Consistent, predictable monthly flow.
Best For Intensive resets (e.g., 12-week Perimenopause). Foundational habits (e.g., The Method Basics).

For high-ticket hormone health programs ($1,500+), Cohort-Based Models typically yield better clinical outcomes because the collective energy of the group mirrors the cyclical nature of female physiology. However, for scaling to 1,000+ members, an evergreen "membership" model is the ultimate goal.

3. Curriculum Layering: Adapting The Method

One of the biggest mistakes practitioners make when scaling is trying to teach "everything" in a group. This leads to clinical dilution. Instead, we use Curriculum Layering.

Curriculum Layering involves separating your methodology into three distinct tiers:

  • Tier 1: The Universal Foundation (Asynchronous). Pre-recorded videos on circadian rhythm, seed cycling, and blood sugar stabilization.
  • Tier 2: Phenotype-Specific Modules (Selective). Specific tracks for PCOS (Insulin-driven vs. Inflammatory) or Perimenopause.
  • Tier 3: The Interactive Synthesis (Live). Group calls focused on *application* rather than *information*.

As a Womenâ€™s Hormone Health Coach, you must navigate the fine line between education and medical advice. In a group setting, the risk of "prescriptive contagion"â€”where one participant tries another's supplement protocolâ€”is high.

The Safety Framework:

  1. The "Education Only" Disclaimer: Every call must begin with a verbal and visual slide stating that the information is for educational purposes.
  2. The "Hot Seat" Protocol: When coaching an individual in front of the group, frame the response as: "If I were working with a client presenting with these markers, I would look at [X, Y, Z]..."
  3. Moderation: Use a community manager (or a trusted veteran student) to flag posts where participants are giving medical advice to each other.

5. The Scaling Tech Stack

To operate at a professional level (the $997+ standard), your technology must be seamless. A "duct-taped" stack of Facebook Groups and Google Drive links will erode your authority and increase churn.

The "Pro-Level" Stack Recommendation:

  • LMS (Learning Management System): Kajabi or Mighty Networks. This houses your Tier 1 and Tier 2 curriculum.
  • Community Hub: Circle.so or Slack. Avoid Facebook Groups to minimize distractions and data privacy concerns.
  • Automated Check-ins: Typeform or VideoAsk. Weekly "Hormone Scorecards" that participants fill out to track their cycle symptoms.
  • CRM & Email: ConvertKit or ActiveCampaign. To automate the "nurture" sequence during the program.

The primary benefit is social contagion and synchronized peer support, which significantly increases adherence to lifestyle protocols (up to 42%). This is especially effective for cycle-synced programs where participants are moving through phases together.

Key Takeaways

  • Group coaching is a distinct clinical modality leveraging social contagion for higher adherence rates.
  • Cohort-based models are superior for high-ticket transformations, while Evergreen models provide business stability.

  • Safety frameworks must be strictly enforced to prevent participants from exchanging medical advice.
