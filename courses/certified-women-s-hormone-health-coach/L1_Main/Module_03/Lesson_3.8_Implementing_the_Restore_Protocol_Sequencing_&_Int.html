Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we prioritize the nervous system in the "Restore Balance" phase?

Because the HPA (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal) axis sits at the top of the hormonal hierarchy. If the body perceives a state of "survival" (high cortisol/stress), it will prioritize survival hormones over reproductive hormones (progesterone/estrogen), making balance impossible to achieve through diet alone.

2. A client is overwhelmed by a 4-page protocol. What is the best coaching move?

Apply the "Rule of Two." Identify the two most high-impact actions (e.g., protein at breakfast and a 10 PM screen ban) and tell the client to ignore the rest for the first 7 days. This builds self-efficacy and reduces the cortisol spike associated with "perfectionism."

Common Mistakes in the Restoration Phase

Even experienced coaches fall into these traps. Being aware of them will set you apart as a Premium Practitioner.

  • The "Information Dump": Explaining the entire Krebs cycle when the client just wants to know why she's tired. Fix: Keep science explanations to 2 sentences max.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Telling a client to stop HIIT without explaining that it's currently driving her midsection weight gain via cortisol. Fix: Always link the action to her specific goal.
  • Over-Supplementing: Suggesting 12 bottles of pills in the first session. Fix: Start with 1-2 "hero" supplements to prove the concept.
  • Failing to Validate: Jumping straight to the "fix" before acknowledging how hard she's been working. Fix: Use the phrase, "It makes sense that you feel this way."

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Validation is an Intervention: Telling a woman "it's not your fault" can lower her cortisol in real-time.
  • The Tier System: Always restore the nervous system and blood sugar before moving to specific hormone replacement or advanced detox.
  • Simplicity is Luxury: Premium coaching isn't about how much you give; it's about how effectively you curate the path.
  • Language Matters: Use "restore" and "recalibrate" instead of "fix" or "correct" to maintain a partnership dynamic.
