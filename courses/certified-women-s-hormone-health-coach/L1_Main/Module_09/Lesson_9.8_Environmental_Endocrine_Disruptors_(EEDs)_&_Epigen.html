Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my early days, I made these mistakes often. Learning to avoid them will fast-track your legitimacy as a coach.

  • Mistake #1: The "Information Dump." Don't explain the entire Krebs cycle. Give her enough science to feel validated, but not so much that she feels overwhelmed.
  • Mistake #2: Over-Promising Quick Fixes. Diane didn't become insulin resistant overnight. Be honest: "We are retraining your metabolism. This takes 12 to 16 weeks of consistency."
  • Mistake #3: Ignoring the Wine. In Diane‚Äôs case, the wine is a major "Insulin Disruptor." Don't be afraid to address it, but do so through the lens of hormone health, not "bad habits."
  • Mistake #4: Not Charging Your Worth. Complex cases like Diane require your specialized knowledge. Coaches who undercharge often burn out because they provide $1,000 worth of value for $50 a session.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Diane's 4x/week Spin class potentially counterproductive for her midsection weight gain?

High-intensity cardio (like Spin) can spike cortisol. In a perimenopausal woman with insulin resistance, elevated cortisol signals the body to store visceral fat (around the organs/midsection) and can further disrupt blood sugar regulation.

2. What is the "Success Marker" in the dialogue provided above?

The success marker is when Diane says, "That... actually makes sense." This indicates she has moved from a state of "self-blame" to "physiological understanding," which is the foundation for behavioral change.
