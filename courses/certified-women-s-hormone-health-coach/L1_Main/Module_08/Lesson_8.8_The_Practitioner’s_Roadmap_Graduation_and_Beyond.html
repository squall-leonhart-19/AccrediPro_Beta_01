Client: Elena, 51, High-level Attorney.

Presenting Symptoms: "Wired but tired" at night, waking at 3 AM with racing thoughts, and a 12lb weight gain in the midsection over 6 months despite no dietary changes.

Outcome: After 8 weeks, Elena reported sleeping through the night 5 days a week and a 4lb reduction in visceral fat. Her "brain fog" lifted, allowing her to bill more hours with less effort.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's "brain fog" before suggesting dietary changes?

Validation builds trust and lowers the client's cortisol response. If a client feels judged or "crazy," her nervous system remains in a sympathetic (fight/flight) state, making her less receptive to making long-term lifestyle changes.

2. Sandra is hesitant to give up her nightly wine. What is the most effective coaching response?

Use a "curiosity-based" approach rather than a "restriction-based" one. Suggest a short-term experiment (1 week) and ask her to track her morning energy levels. This puts the power and the data back in her hands.
