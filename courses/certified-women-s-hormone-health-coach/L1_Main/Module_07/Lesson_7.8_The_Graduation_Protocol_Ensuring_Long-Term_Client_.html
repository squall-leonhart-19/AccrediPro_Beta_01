Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Minimum Viable Plan" (MVP) crucial for long-term sustainability?

It prevents the "All or Nothing" collapse. By maintaining a small baseline of habits (like hydration or a specific supplement) during high-stress times, the client maintains their identity as a "healthy person" and makes it much easier to return to full protocols later.

2. What is "Health Equity" in the context of a 40-55 year old woman?

It refers to the cumulative physiological benefits of consistent healthy habits (improved mitochondrial function, hormonal balance, muscle mass). This "buffer" means the body is more resilient to occasional stressors or dietary slips than it was at the start of the journey.

Success Markers: You'll Know it Worked When...

  • The client stops using the word "cheating" or "failing."
  • She proactively suggests her own MVP when she knows a busy week is coming.
  • She reports "bouncing back" in 2 days rather than 2 weeks.
  • She expresses interest in a long-term "Maintenance Membership" because she values the partnership over just the information.
