Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Biological Safety" more important than "Calorie Counting" for a client like Diane?

In perimenopause, the body is already in a state of high perceived stress due to fluctuating hormones. Calorie restriction further triggers the HPA-axis, leading to increased cortisol and fat storage. Establishing biological safety (stable blood sugar, better sleep) lowers cortisol, allowing the body to release stored fat.

2. What is the most effective way to handle a client's "imposter syndrome" about their own body?

Validation through science. By explaining the neurological shifts (like the remodeling of the hypothalamus), you take the "blame" off the client and place the focus on biological transition, which reduces shame and increases compliance.
