1. The Art of Hormone Assessment

Assessment is not just a checklist; it is a detective story where the client provides the clues and you provide the magnifying glass. A 2022 cross-sectional study of 1,500 women (aged 40-60) found that 78.4% reported significant vasomotor symptoms, yet only 24% felt their healthcare provider fully addressed their hormonal concerns (Maki et al., 2022).

As a coach, your role is to fill this "validation gap." When a woman in her 40s or 50s walks into your office (or onto your Zoom screen), she often brings a history of being told her symptoms are "just part of aging" or "just stress." Your assessment is the first time she will feel truly seen.

Assessment Area "Normal" Transition Coaching Priority (Imbalance)
Sleep Patterns Occasional light sleeping. 3 AM wake-ups with "wired but tired" feeling (Cortisol/Progesterone).
Menstrual Cycle Gradual shortening of cycle length. Heavy flooding, clots, or cycles < 21 days (Estrogen Dominance).
Mood/Cognition Mild forgetfulness. Rage, debilitating brain fog, or sudden onset anxiety (GABA/Progesterone).
Body Composition Slow metabolic shift. Rapid "spare tire" weight gain despite no diet changes (Insulin/Cortisol).
