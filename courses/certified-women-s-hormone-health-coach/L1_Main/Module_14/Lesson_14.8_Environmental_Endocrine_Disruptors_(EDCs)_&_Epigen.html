Check Your Understanding

1. Why is 1,200-calorie dieting often counterproductive for a woman in her 50s?

It triggers a "famine response," elevating cortisol levels. High cortisol in a low-estrogen environment promotes visceral adiposity (belly fat) and further disrupts the HPA axis.

2. What is the "backup source" of estrogen mentioned in the script?

Adipose tissue (fat cells) contains the enzyme aromatase, which converts androgens into estrone (a weaker form of estrogen). The body may hold onto fat as an adaptive mechanism to mitigate the sharp drop in ovarian estrogen.

Key Takeaways

  • Validate First: Always acknowledge that her symptoms are real and not a "lack of willpower."
  • Mechanism Over Rules: When a client understands the why (e.g., aromatase in fat cells), she is more likely to comply with the how.
  • Safety Signals: In midlife coaching, the goal is to move the body from a "threat state" to a "safety state."
  • Strength is Vital: Muscle mass is the primary driver of metabolic flexibility in the post-menopausal years.
