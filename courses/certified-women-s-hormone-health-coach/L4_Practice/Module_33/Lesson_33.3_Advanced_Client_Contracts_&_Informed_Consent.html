2. Informed Consent for Hormone Optimization

In the context of the H.A.R.M.O.N.Y. Method‚Ñ¢, informed consent is not about surgical risks; it is about documenting the client's understanding of the non-medical nature of your work. This is especially vital when discussing lab results (Module 1) or specific nutrient protocols (Module 6).

A meta-analysis of health coaching litigation (2021) found that 85% of legal challenges arose from a lack of clarity regarding the practitioner's credentials and the expected outcomes of the intervention. Your informed consent should explicitly state:

  • You do not diagnose, treat, or cure specific diseases (e.g., PCOS, Endometriosis).
  • The client is advised to remain under the care of a licensed physician.
  • Information provided is for educational purposes to support "Metabolic Optimization" (Module 4).

High-ticket hormone coaching (often priced between $2,500 and $5,500 for a 4-month container) represents a significant commitment of your time and resources.

5. Force Majeure & Liability Limitation

In a post-2020 world, Force Majeure (Acts of God) clauses are essential. This protects you if you cannot fulfill coaching sessions due to illness, natural disasters, or major technological failures.

Furthermore, your Limitation of Liability clause should cap your financial exposure. For example: "The practitioner's total liability for any claim arising out of this agreement shall not exceed the total amount paid by the client for services rendered." This is a standard professional protection that prevents a single disgruntled client from jeopardizing your entire business assets.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a "Client Responsibility" clause particularly important in a hormone health coaching contract?

Hormonal restoration (the H.A.R.M.O.N.Y. Method‚Ñ¢) is heavily dependent on daily lifestyle choices (sleep, diet, stress management). Without this clause, a client might blame the practitioner if their "numbers" don't improve despite the client failing to implement the agreed-upon lifestyle changes.

2. What is the primary purpose of a "Limitation of Liability" clause?

It caps the maximum amount a practitioner would have to pay in a legal dispute, usually limiting it to the total fees paid by the client, thereby protecting the practitioner's personal and business assets from catastrophic loss.
