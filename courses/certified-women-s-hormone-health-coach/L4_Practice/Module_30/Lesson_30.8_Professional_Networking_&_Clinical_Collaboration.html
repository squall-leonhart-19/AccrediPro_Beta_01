3. Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are often just a request for more information or a manifestation of the client's fear of failing again. A 2022 study in the Journal of Professional Coaching noted that coaches who addressed financial concerns directly rather than avoiding them had a 40% higher long-term retention rate.

"It's a lot of money right now."

"I completely respect that. When we look at the $1,500 investment over 3 months, it's about $16 a day. Considering the supplements you're currently buying that aren't working, and the missed productivity at work, does it feel like a priority to solve this once and for all?"

"I need to talk to my husband/partner."

"I think that's a great idea. I want you to have full support at home. What information can I provide you with so you can explain the metabolic approach we'll be taking together?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Phase 2" (Deep Dive) the longest part of the discovery call?

Because you must establish the "Cost of Inaction" and deeply understand the client's pain points. Without this, the price in Phase 4 will always seem "too high" because the value hasn't been established.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I've tried everything and nothing works"?

Validate their frustration, then pivot to the "Root Cause" approach. Explain that they haven't tried a program that addresses the underlying hormonal signaling and metabolic health specifically tailored to their life stage.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Structure = Confidence: Following a script isn't "fake"; it provides a professional container for the client to feel safe.
  • Price for Outcomes: You are selling a "restored life," not a 60-minute Zoom call.
  • Objections are Opportunities: View them as the client asking for permission to invest in themselves.
  • Consistency is Key: Aim for 5-10 discovery calls per month to maintain a thriving $5k-$10k/month practice.
