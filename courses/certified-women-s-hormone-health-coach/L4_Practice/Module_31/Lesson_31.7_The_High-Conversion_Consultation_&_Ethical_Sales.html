Ethical Psychological Triggers

We use three primary psychological triggers in the Hormone Health Audit. These are not coercive; they are foundational to the human decision-making process.

Overcoming Common Objections

Objections are not "nos"; they are requests for more information or a manifestation of fear. In a 2023 survey of health coaches, 64% of lost sales were attributed to the practitioner's inability to handle the "Price" objection confidently.

The "Price" Objection

The Reframe: Shift from "Cost" to "Investment." Compare the program price to the cost of continued illness (lost work days, medication, low quality of life).

The "Time" Objection

The Reframe: "You don't have time to be this tired." Explain how the Sustainable Plan (Y) actually creates time by restoring cognitive function and energy.

The "Failed Previous Attempts" Objection: This is common in hormone health. The client says, "I've tried Keto, I've tried HRT, I've tried every supplement." Your response: "I'm glad you tried those, because they gave us data. They showed us that a 'one-size-fits-all' approach doesn't work for your unique endocrine blueprint."

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for Follow-Up

The "fortune is in the follow-up." Most practitioners stop after the first "I'll think about it." An ethical follow-up SOP looks like this:

  1. 24 Hours Post-Call: Send a "Recap & Resources" email. Include a specific scientific article related to their main symptom (e.g., a study on Magnesium and sleep).
  2. Day 4: The "Case Study" email. "I was thinking about our call and wanted to share how a client with similar symptoms found relief using the HARMONY method."
  3. Day 7: The "Final Invitation." Ask if they have any lingering questions before you close their audit file.
