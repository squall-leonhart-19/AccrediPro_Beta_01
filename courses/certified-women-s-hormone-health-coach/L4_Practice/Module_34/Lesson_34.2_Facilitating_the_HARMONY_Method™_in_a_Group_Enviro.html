1. Adapting 'H' (Hormone Assessment) for Group Intake

In a group setting, you are no longer just a clinician; you are a data synthesizer. Instead of spending 90 minutes on one intake, you will use aggregate data to identify the "Hormonal Signature" of your cohort.

A 2022 study published in the Journal of Personalized Medicine found that group-based interventions using shared biometric tracking led to a 28% higher adherence rate compared to individual counseling. This is because women feel "normalized" when they see their symptoms reflected in a group trend.

The Aggregate Intake Strategy

Before your first group call, require all participants to submit a standardized HARMONY Symptom Inventory. Your job is to look for the "Common Thread":

  • The 70% Rule: If 70% of the group reports "Mid-afternoon energy crashes," your first teaching module should prioritize blood sugar (Metabolic Optimization).
  • Trend Mapping: Use a simple spreadsheet to tally symptoms. Are you looking at a "Stress-Heavy" cohort or an "Estrogen Dominant" cohort?

3. Implementing 'R' (Restore Balance) Protocols

Providing supplement and lifestyle guidance in a group requires a "Tiered Approach" to ensure safety and efficacy. You must distinguish between Foundational Support and Targeted Intervention.

The Tiered Protocol Framework

  1. Tier 1: Foundational (Universal): Magnesium, Vitamin D3/K2, Omega-3s. These are generally safe for the entire cohort and can be recommended as a "Group Baseline."
  2. Tier 2: Targeted (Conditional): Vitex for low progesterone or DIM for estrogen dominance. These are presented as "Options for those whose 'H' assessment showed [X] pattern."
  3. Tier 3: Clinical (Individual): Bio-identical hormones or high-dose interventions. These are flagged for private consultation or medical referral.

Scope of Practice Note
Always use "Suggested Protocol" language rather than "Prescription." In a group setting, include a disclaimer on every handout: "These suggestions are for educational purposes. Consult your primary physician before starting new supplements."
