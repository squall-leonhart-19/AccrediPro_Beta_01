4. Financial Modeling: CPA and ROAS

When you move from organic growth (word of mouth) to paid scaling (ads), you must understand your Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) and Return on Ad Spend (ROAS).

CPA (Cost Per Acquisition): How much does it cost you in advertising to get one paying client? If you spend $1,000 on Facebook ads and get 5 clients, your CPA is $200. If your program is $2,000, this is a healthy margin.

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend): This is the ratio of revenue to ad spend. A 4:1 ROAS means for every $1 you spend, you make $4. In the hormone coaching space, a 3:1 to 5:1 ROAS is considered excellent for high-ticket offers.

6. Scaling Infrastructure: When to Upgrade

One of the biggest mistakes in scaling is "over-tooling" too early or "under-tooling" too late. Use these data-driven triggers to know when to invest:

  • The CRM Trigger: When you have >50 active leads or >20 active clients, manual spreadsheets will lead to "lead leakage." Upgrade to a health-pro CRM (like Practice Better or Healthie).
  • The Custom App Trigger: When your group program exceeds 100 participants, a custom-branded app increases retention by an average of 22% by providing easier access to "Nourish & Replenish" (N) meal plans and "Optimize Lifestyle" (O) trackers.
  • The Automation Trigger: When you find yourself repeating the same educational "Address Root Causes" (A) speech more than 3 times a week, it‚Äôs time to record it as an automated module.

It allows you to identify "niche-market fit." If one demographic has significantly higher success and retention, you can focus your marketing and scaling efforts there to increase efficiency and outcomes.
