The Psychology of the Discovery Call

Before we look at the script, we must understand the mechanism. A Discovery Call is a structured conversation designed to bridge the gap between where a client is (frustrated, symptomatic) and where they want to be (vibrant, balanced).

A 2023 industry analysis of health coaching outcomes (n=1,240) found that practitioners who utilized a standardized discovery protocol achieved a 68% higher conversion rate than those who used an unstructured "chat" format. This is because structure provides safety for the client, especially women in midlife who feel dismissed by the medical system.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Use this exact structure. Do not rush. Silence is your best friend during the "Discovery" phase.

Phase 1: Setting the Container 3 min
YOU:
"Hi Michelle! I'm so glad we're connecting. My goal for the next 30 minutes is to dive deep into what's going on with your hormones, see what your goals are, and if I'm the right person to help you get there. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Deep Discovery 12 min
YOU:
"You mentioned on your form that brain fog is your biggest challenge. Tell me, how is that actually impacting your day-to-day as a VP?"

Handling Objections with Confidence

An objection is not a "No." It is a request for more information or a manifestation of fear. As a coach, you coach them through the decision.

Objection Type The "Fear" Behind It Your Professional Response
Price: "It's too expensive." Fear of wasting money again. "I understand. Compared to continuing to buy supplements that don't work and the cost of lost productivity at work, what is the value of feeling like yourself again?"
Time: "I'm too busy." Overwhelmed by current symptoms. "I hear you. But how much time are you losing right now to fatigue and brain fog? My program is designed to give you time back by increasing your energy."
Spouse: "I need to ask my husband." Lack of personal agency/guilt. "Of course! Does he know how much you've been struggling with your sleep? Usually, partners are the most supportive when they realize this is about your health, not just a 'luxury'."

Presenting Your Pricing: The "Sandwich" Method

Never just state a number. Wrap the price in the transformation.

Step 3: The Bread (Outcome)
"This covers everything we need to get you sleeping through the night and feeling sharp for those VP presentations again. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Discovery" phase (Phase 2) the most important part of the call?

It allows the client to articulate the "cost of inaction." By asking about the impact on their career or marriage, you are helping them realize that the cost of the program is lower than the cost of staying sick.

2. What should you do immediately after stating your price?

Stop talking. This is called the "Golden Silence." It allows the client to process the investment. If you keep talking, you signal a lack of confidence in your value.

3. How does a structured script help with "Imposter Syndrome"?

Structure provides a "professional container." It moves the focus away from you (the person) and onto the process (the protocol). It establishes you as the expert leader of the conversation.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • The Discovery Call is a 30-minute bridge from pain to possibility.
  • Focus 80% of the call on the client's problems and 20% on your solution.
  • Price is only an issue in the absence of value; always link cost to the outcome.
  • Consistency in your script leads to consistency in your conversion rate (aim for 30-50%).
  • You are not "selling"; you are offering a lifeline to a woman who is struggling.
