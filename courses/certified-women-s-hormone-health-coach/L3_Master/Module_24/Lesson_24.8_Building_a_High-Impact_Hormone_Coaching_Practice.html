The Do's and Don'ts of Mentoring

Check Your Understanding

1. A mentee presents a case where the client isn't losing weight. The mentee wants to further restrict calories. What is the Master Practitioner's first step?

Ask the mentee to evaluate the client's current stress markers (sleep, mood, cycle regularity) to determine if the metabolism is "stalled" due to caloric excess or HPA-axis protection.

2. What is the primary difference between "Directing" and "Mentoring" in a supervision session?

Directing provides the specific answer/instruction, whereas Mentoring uses inquiry to help the mentee arrive at the clinical reasoning themselves, fostering long-term independence.

Master Practitioner Takeaways

  • Mentoring is an Income Stream: As a Master Practitioner, you can charge $150-$300/hour for professional supervision, creating a "B2B" revenue model alongside your client work.
  • The Socratic Method: Use questions to lead your mentee to the answer. This builds their "clinical muscle."
  • Validation First: New practitioners are often terrified of making mistakes. Your first job is to provide psychological safety.
  • Mechanism over Protocol: Ensure your mentees understand the *hormonal mechanism* so they can adapt when a standard protocol fails.
  • Model Boundaries: You cannot teach boundary-setting if you are answering your mentees' emails at midnight. Lead by example.
