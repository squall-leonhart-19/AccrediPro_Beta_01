In supervision, we use the Reflective Feedback Model. Instead of telling Lisa what she did wrong, we ask questions that lead her to the realization herself. This builds the "Clinical Muscle" she needs to work independently.

Section 5: Supervision Best Practices

Effective supervision is a balance of Support, Education, and Evaluation. Use the following "Do's and Don'ts" to maintain a professional yet empowering relationship with your mentees.

Do Don't
Ask: "What do you think is happening here?" Say: "You should have done X instead."
Normalize clinical uncertainty. Act like you have all the answers instantly.
Focus on the process of thinking. Focus only on the supplement list.
Set clear boundaries for the session. Let the session turn into a personal therapy session for the coach.

Check Your Understanding

1. A mentee presents a case where the client's lab results are worsening despite a "perfect" protocol. What is the most effective first question for a supervisor to ask?

"What is your hypothesis about why the body is responding this way, and how does the client feel about these results?" This encourages clinical reasoning rather than just looking for a "fix."

2. What is the "Parallel Process" in clinical supervision?

The phenomenon where the dynamics between the coach and the client are recreated in the relationship between the supervisor and the coach (e.g., the coach feeling "stuck" just as the client feels "stuck").
