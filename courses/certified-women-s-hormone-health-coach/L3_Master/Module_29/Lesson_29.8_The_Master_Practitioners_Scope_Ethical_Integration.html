Phase 3: The Supervisory Analysis

As a Master Practitioner, your job is not just to "fix" Susan, but to grow Lisa. We use the Bernard Discrimination Model, which requires you to switch between three roles:

Teacher Specific skills & knowledge gaps. Explain why Vitex might be contraindicated for a post-menopausal woman (it can sometimes spike LH, which Susan doesn't need).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Parallel Process" in supervision?

Parallel Process occurs when the mentee (Lisa) interacts with the supervisor (You) in the same way the client (Susan) interacts with the mentee. For example, if Susan is anxious and demanding answers, Lisa may become anxious and demand answers from you. Recognizing this helps you remain calm and break the cycle.

2. In the case of Susan, why was Vitex a potentially poor choice for a post-menopausal woman?

Vitex is primarily used to support progesterone production by modulating the pituitary gland and LH/FSH levels. In menopause, the ovaries are no longer responding to these signals. Stimulating a "tired" pituitary can sometimes lead to increased autonomic arousal (racing heart) without providing the desired hormonal benefit.

Key Takeaways for the Master Mentor

  • Supervision is about the Practitioner: While client safety is paramount, your primary focus is developing the mentee's clinical reasoning.
  • Normalize the "Paradoxical": Teach mentees that negative reactions are "data," not "failure."
  • The Three Hats: Learn to switch between Teacher (knowledge), Counselor (emotion), and Consultant (collaboration).
  • Financial Leverage: Mentoring allows you to scale your impact and income beyond the "dollars-for-hours" client model.
