Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a "clean" paleo diet containing fermented foods and bone broth actually worsen a perimenopausal client's anxiety and hot flashes?

These foods are extremely high in histamines. In perimenopause, fluctuating estrogen can impair the DAO enzyme's ability to clear histamine. Excess histamine acts as a central nervous system stimulant, mimicking anxiety and causing vasodilation (flushing/hot flashes).

2. What is the clinical significance of a Ferritin level of 18 in a client taking thyroid medication?

Ferritin levels below 30-50 ng/mL are often insufficient for optimal thyroid function. Iron is a required cofactor for Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO) and for the conversion of T4 to T3. A client may remain symptomatic (fatigue, hair loss) despite "normal" TSH if ferritin is low.
