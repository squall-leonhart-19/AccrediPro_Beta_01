CGM Data: Non-Diabetic Hormonal Fluctuations

Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGMs) are no longer just for diabetics. For the hormone coach, they provide a real-time window into how a woman's cycle dictates her glucose metabolism. Research indicates that insulin sensitivity varies by up to 25% across the menstrual cycle.

  • Follicular Phase: High estrogen levels typically enhance insulin sensitivity. Women can often tolerate higher carbohydrate loads during this time.
  • Luteal Phase: Rising progesterone levels induce a state of mild insulin resistance. This is why non-diabetic women may see higher fasting glucose and higher "spikes" after identical meals compared to their follicular phase.

A 2022 study utilizing CGM data on 1,200 non-diabetic women showed that the "dawn phenomenon" (a natural rise in blood sugar in the morning) is more pronounced in the late luteal phase. For your clients, this means that "low carb" strategies may need to be cycled to prevent adrenal strain during the week before menstruation.

Resistance Training & Androgen Optimization

Many women in their 40s and 50s shy away from heavy lifting, fearing "bulk" or injury. However, the data on metabolic optimization is clear: Sarcopenia (muscle loss) is the primary driver of hormonal weight gain in perimenopause.

A systematic review of resistance training in women found that lifting at 70-85% of 1-Rep Max (1RM) significantly increases GLUT4 translocation. GLUT4 is the "glucose gatekeeper" that pulls sugar out of the blood and into the muscle without needing massive amounts of insulin. Furthermore, for women with PCOS or high androgens, resistance training helps "burn off" excess testosterone by increasing androgen receptor sensitivity in the muscle tissue, rather than allowing those androgens to cause acne or hair loss.

The Role of the 'Metabolic Set Point'

Why do some clients plateau despite "doing everything right"? The Metabolic Set Point is the weight range your body defensively maintains through hormonal regulation. This is controlled primarily by the hypothalamus via leptin and ghrelin signaling.

Research on weight loss resistance shows that chronic calorie restriction (the "Eat Less, Move More" trap) lowers the basal metabolic rate (BMR) by up to 15% as a survival mechanism. To shift the set point, we must address the "Adrenal-Insulin" see-saw. High cortisol from over-exercising signals the body to "store for the winter," effectively locking the set point at a higher weight.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a non-diabetic woman see higher post-meal glucose spikes during her luteal phase?

Progesterone, which rises in the luteal phase, naturally induces a state of mild insulin resistance. This is a physiological adaptation, but it can be exacerbated by poor diet or high stress, leading to more significant glucose fluctuations compared to the follicular phase.

2. What is the primary benefit of Myo-inositol over Metformin regarding PCOS and fertility?

While both improve insulin resistance, Myo-inositol acts as a second messenger for FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone), directly supporting better egg quality and higher ovulation rates, whereas Metformin primarily focuses on systemic glucose management.
