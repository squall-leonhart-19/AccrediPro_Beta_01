2. PCOS and Hormonal Balance: The Fiber Mechanism

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is the leading cause of female infertility, characterized by insulin resistance and androgen excess. The P.L.A.N.T. Methodâ„¢ utilizes fiber-mediated estrogen clearance and insulin sensitization to restore ovulatory function.

Fiber acts as a metabolic "sink" for excess hormones. In the liver, estrogens are conjugated and excreted into the bile. If the diet is low in fiber, these estrogens can be deconjugated by gut bacteria and reabsorbed (enterohepatic circulation). High fiber intake ensures these hormones are bound and eliminated.

5. Lactation: The Metabolic Marathon

Lactation is more nutritionally demanding than pregnancy. A lactating mother requires approximately 500 additional calories per day. In the P.L.A.N.T. Methodâ„¢, we focus on "Caloric Anchors" like avocados, walnuts, and hemp seeds to meet these needs without causing volume fatigue.

The DHA and B12 Transfer

The levels of DHA and Vitamin B12 in breast milk are directly reflective of the mother's current intake. Unlike calcium or iron, which the body can pull from maternal stores, B12 and DHA must be consistently consumed to ensure infant adequacy.

  • DHA: Essential for infant visual and cognitive development. Plant-based mothers should supplement with 300mg of algal oil daily.
  • B12: Even if a mother shows no symptoms of deficiency, low B12 in breast milk can lead to severe neurological delay in the infant.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is fiber specifically beneficial for clients with PCOS attempting to conceive?

Fiber increases the production of Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin (SHBG) and facilitates the clearance of excess estrogens through the stool, preventing reabsorption and helping to restore hormonal balance.

2. Which two nutrients in breast milk are most sensitive to a mother's immediate dietary intake?

Vitamin B12 and DHA. Unlike minerals like calcium, these do not have large maternal stores that can be mobilized for the infant; they must be supplied via the mother's current diet or supplements.
