The Science of Belief & Confidence

Self-efficacy (the belief in one's ability to succeed) is a significant predictor of practitioner success. According to Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory, your self-efficacy has been built through vicarious experience (watching case studies) and mastery experiences (completing the modules and quizzes). A study of 2,400 health coaches found that those with high self-efficacy scores had clients with 22% better clinical outcomes (HbA1c reduction and weight loss) than those with low self-efficacy.

Your confidence is now rooted in science. You aren't guessing; you are applying principles that have been validated by thousands of peer-reviewed studies. When a client asks "Why?" you have the biochemical and physiological rationale to provide a professional answer.
