Cognitive Load

The total amount of mental effort being used in the working memory. Strategic study reduces this to improve exam performance.

Distractor

An incorrect option in a multiple-choice question that is designed to look plausible to those who haven't fully mastered the material.

Strategic Answering Techniques

Multiple-choice exams are as much about logic as they are about knowledge. Use the Elimination Protocol for every question:

1. Read the Stem Twice: Identify the "Negative" (e.g., "Which of these is NOT a principle of...").

2. Identify the Distractors: Often, one answer is "mostly right" but contains one incorrect word. Look for absolute terms like "Always," "Never," or "Only"â€”these are rarely correct in nutritional science.

3. The "Coach" Filter: If you are stuck, ask yourself: "What would the most evidence-based, responsible coach tell a client in this situation?"
