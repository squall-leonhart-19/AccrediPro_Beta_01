1. Writing Professional SOAP Notes

Documentation is not merely a record of what happened; it is a legal safeguard and a clinical tool for tracking progress within the P.L.A.N.T. Nutrition Methodâ„¢. Professional documentation distinguishes an "influencer" from a "practitioner."

The SOAP note (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan) is the gold standard for healthcare professionals. For a Plant-Based Nutrition Coach, these notes should focus on nutrient density, inflammatory markers (subjective), and behavioral adherence.

Objective (O) Weight, waist circumference, lab results (if provided), and 3-day food log data (e.g., fiber intake in grams).

Essential components of the Roadmap include:

3. Legal and Ethical Boundaries: The 'Red Flags'

As a Plant-Based Nutrition Coach, you must operate strictly within your Scope of Practice. Documentation must reflect that you are not diagnosing or treating medical conditions, but rather providing nutritional education and support.

CRITICAL: RED FLAGS FOR REFERRAL

If a client presents with any of the following, you must document a formal referral to their Primary Care Physician (PCP) and pause nutrition coaching until medical clearance is granted:

  • Unexplained weight loss (>5% of body weight in 1 month).
  • Persistent severe abdominal pain or blood in stool.
  • Symptoms of clinical eating disorders (orthorexia, binge eating).
  • Laboratory results indicating severe deficiency (e.g., Hemoglobin < 10 g/dL).
  • Sudden, severe changes in mood or cognitive function.

5. The Discharge Protocol: Transitioning to Self-Sufficiency

The goal of the P.L.A.N.T. Nutrition Methodâ„¢ is to make the coach obsolete. A formal discharge protocol ensures the client doesn't regress once the weekly accountability ends.

The Discharge Session should cover:

  • Relapse Prevention: Identifying "High-Risk Situations" (e.g., holiday travel) and creating a P.L.A.N.T. backup plan.
  • The 80/20 Rule: Defining how the client will handle non-WFPB meals in a social context without losing progress.
  • Bi-Annual Check-ins: Scheduling a 6-month and 12-month follow-up to review labs and adherence.
  • Final Documentation: Providing the client with a "Graduation Certificate" and their finalized Roadmap.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which section of the SOAP note should contain the client's self-reported bloating and energy levels?

The Subjective (S) section. This is for anything the client tells you that cannot be independently measured in the moment.

2. True or False: If a client shows signs of orthorexia, you should adjust their plant-based plan to be more flexible before referring them to a therapist.

False. Signs of an eating disorder are a "Red Flag" that requires immediate medical/psychological referral. It is outside the scope of a nutrition coach to "treat" orthorexia through plan adjustments.
