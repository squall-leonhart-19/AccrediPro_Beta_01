3. Visual Branding Standards for Premium Coaches

Before a potential client reads a single word of your content, they have already made a subconscious judgment about your authority based on your visual aesthetic. In the premium market, "DIY" visuals often signal a lack of professionalism.

Visual Element Low-Authority (Generalist) High-Authority (Premium)
Color Palette Random, overly bright, or neon colors. Sophisticated earth tones, muted greens, or deep blues.
Typography Standard fonts (Arial, Comic Sans). Serif fonts for headings (authority) + clean Sans-serif for body.
Imagery Generic, low-res stock photos of salads. Professional headshots, custom lifestyle brand photography.
Consistency Every post looks different. Strict adherence to a brand style guide across all platforms.

4. The L.A.S.T. Framework for Thought Leadership

To be an authority, you must produce content that doesn't just inform, but leads. The L.A.S.T. Framework is a systematic way to ensure every piece of content you produce reinforces your professional standing.

L - Listen to the Market

Identify a specific pain point or "myth" currently circulating in the plant-based community (e.g., "Plant protein is incomplete").

A - Analyze the Science

Reference a recent study (e.g., a 2022 meta-analysis on leucine triggers) to provide objective weight to your perspective.

S - Solve with Methodology

Show how a specific pillar of the P.L.A.N.T. Method‚Ñ¢ (e.g., Pillar 2: Learn Nutrients) addresses the problem.

T - Teach the Implementation

Give one actionable "micro-win" the reader can implement immediately to build trust in your expertise.

5. Building a Professional Media Kit

Authority branding extends beyond your own social media. To scale your reach, you must leverage Other People's Audiences (OPA) through podcasts, guest blogging, and speaking. A media kit is your "professional resume" for the media world.

Essential Components of a Premium Media Kit:

  • The "Expert" Bio: A 150-word bio written in the third person, highlighting your certification and the P.L.A.N.T. Method‚Ñ¢.
  • Signature Talk Topics: 3-4 specific titles of presentations you can deliver (e.g., "The Gut-Skin Axis: A Plant-Based Approach to Inflammatory Dermatitis").
  • Sample Interview Questions: A list of 10 questions that allow you to showcase your proprietary knowledge.
  • Social Proof: Logos of publications where you've been featured or testimonials from high-profile clients.
