3. The 90-Day Psychological Arc

Managing a client over 90 days requires an understanding of the Transtheoretical Model (TTM) and the predictable emotional ebbs and flows of dietary transition. Research by Lally et al. (2010) suggests that while the "21 days to a habit" myth persists, the actual average for automaticity is 66 days, with a range of 18 to 254 days.

4. Value-Based Pricing vs. Hourly Rates

One of the most significant barriers to scaling a coaching practice is the "Time-for-Money" trap. At Level 4, your pricing should reflect the Economic Value of the Outcome, not the minutes spent on Zoom. A client curing chronic inflammation and avoiding future statin use represents a value of tens of thousands of dollars in healthcare savings and quality of life.

A meta-analysis of coaching businesses found that practitioners using package-based pricing earned an average of 312% more per hour of actual work than those charging by the session. When designing your 90-day program, consider the following tiers:

  • Essential Tier: Group sessions + Digital IP ($997 - $1,497)
  • Professional Tier: Bi-weekly 1:1 + Voxer support ($2,500 - $3,500)
  • Concierge Tier: Weekly 1:1 + Bloodwork analysis + Pantry overhaul ($5,000+)

6. Designing for Group Cohesion

When moving the P.L.A.N.T. Method‚Ñ¢ to a group format, the Social Facilitation Effect becomes a powerful tool. Research in the Journal of Behavioral Medicine indicates that individuals in group-based lifestyle interventions achieve 20% better adherence than those working solo, due to peer accountability and shared experience.

To maintain L4 quality in a group setting:

  • Cohort-Based Launches: Start everyone on Day 1 together to build community.
  • Tiered Support: Use a community platform (e.g., Circle, Slack) for peer support, moderated by a "Success Coach" (or yourself).
  • Outcome Benchmarking: Use group data to show collective progress (e.g., "As a group, we have reduced our systemic inflammation markers by 15%").

1. Why is the 90-day window considered the "Gold Standard" for physiological transition in the P.L.A.N.T. Method‚Ñ¢?

It aligns with the 66-day average for habit automaticity and the ~120-day turnover of red blood cells, allowing for measurable changes in chronic biomarkers and permanent lifestyle integration.

The risk is resistance and social friction as the initial "honeymoon" motivation wanes. Coaches should address this by focusing on the "Anti-Inflammatory" benefits and reinforcing the client's internal "Why" through bio-feedback and small wins.

  • Structure over Sessions: Sell the transformation (the destination), not the time (the journey).
