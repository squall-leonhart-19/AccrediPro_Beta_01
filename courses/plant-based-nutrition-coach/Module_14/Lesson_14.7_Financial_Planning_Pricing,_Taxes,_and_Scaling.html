Value-Based Pricing

Setting prices based on the perceived or estimated value of a product or service to the customer rather than according to the cost of the product or historical prices.

LTV (Lifetime Value)

The total revenue a business can expect from a single client account throughout the business relationship.

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)

The total cost of sales and marketing efforts needed to acquire a new customer.

Scalability

The ability of a business to handle growing work or sales in a capable manner and its potential to be enlarged in order to accommodate that growth.

Tax Strategy & Deductions

As a business owner, you are taxed on profit, not revenue. Understanding legal deductions is critical for maintaining cash flow. For a Plant-Based Nutrition Coach, your "laboratory" is often your kitchen, and your "research" is your education.

Specific deductions often overlooked by nutrition practitioners include:

  • Education & Certifications: Your AccrediPro certification and any continuing education credits.
  • Software Subscriptions: ANDI database access, EHR/CRM software, and meal planning tools.
  • Content Creation: Ingredients purchased specifically for recipe development or cooking demonstrations.
  • Home Office: A percentage of your rent/mortgage and utilities if you have a dedicated workspace.
  • Professional Fees: Legal fees for client contracts and accounting software.

Financial Tracking Metrics

A 2023 meta-analysis of small business success factors (n=5,000+) identified that practitioners who tracked financial metrics weekly had a 310% higher survival rate after 5 years. You must track more than just your bank balance.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for Coaches:

  • Profit Margin: Aim for 70-80% in a solo practice. If it drops below 50%, you are over-spending on software or marketing.
  • Churn Rate: The percentage of clients who stop coaching before their goal is met. High churn usually indicates a breakdown in the "Sustainable Lifestyle Integration" (S) phase of the P.L.A.N.T.S. Methodâ„¢.
  • Conversion Rate: The percentage of discovery calls that turn into paying clients. Elite practitioners aim for 25-40%.

Scaling to 1:Many Models

Scaling is the process of decoupling your income from your time. Once your 1:1 practice is 80% full, it is time to scale. This usually follows a three-tier hierarchy:

  1. Tier 1: Group Coaching. You deliver the same P.L.A.N.T.S. Methodâ„¢ curriculum to 10-20 people simultaneously via Zoom. This increases your hourly rate from ~$250 to ~$2,500+.
  2. Tier 2: Digital Products. Self-paced courses or meal plan subscriptions. These have 95%+ profit margins and provide "passive" income.
  3. Tier 3: Licensing/Certification. Training other coaches in your specific niche or method (Advanced Stage).
