2. Pre-Call Qualification Strategies

Your time is your most valuable asset. Taking calls with "tire-kickers" or individuals who are not ready for a Sustainable Lifestyle Integration leads to burnout. High-performing practitioners use a multi-step qualification process before the Zoom link is even sent.

The Three Pillars of Qualification

  • Financial Readiness: Do they understand that this is a premium investment in their longevity?
  • Pain Level: Is their health struggle significant enough that they are motivated to change (e.g., chronic inflammation or metabolic dysfunction)?
  • Decisional Authority: Are they the ones making the choice, or do they need a spouse's "permission"? (Address this early).

3. The 6-Step Discovery Call Script

A structured framework ensures you lead the conversation rather than being interviewed by the prospect. The goal is to move from surface-level symptoms to deep-seated motivations.

Phase Objective Key Question
1. Rapport & Frame Establish authority and set the agenda. "The goal today is to see if we're a fit. Is it okay if I lead the way?"
2. The Current State Uncover symptoms (Inflammation, low energy). "What is the #1 health challenge holding you back right now?"
3. The Desired State Define their "Longevity Vision." "If we solved this, what would your life look like in 12 months?"
4. The Obstacles Identify why they haven't succeeded alone. "What have you tried in the past that didn't stick?"
5. The Gap Analysis Connect their struggles to the P.L.A.N.T.S. Method. "It sounds like you're missing the 'Phytonutrient Synergy' piece..."
6. The Invitation Offer the solution and the price. "Based on what you've said, here is how we'll bridge that gap."

4. Handling Objections with Integrity

Objections are rarely about money; they are about uncertainty. When a client says "It's too expensive," they are actually saying "I don't yet believe the outcome is worth the cost."

Case Study: The "Price" Objection

Practitioner: Elena R. | Client: Robert, 54

Scenario: Robert has Type 2 Diabetes and high systemic inflammation. Elena proposes a $4,500 6-month P.L.A.N.T.S. protocol. Robert says, "That's more than I expected."

Outcome: Robert realized the "cost of inaction" ($20k+ in medical bills) far outweighed the "cost of investment." He signed the agreement that afternoon.

6. Conversion Metrics & Optimization

A professional practice is managed by data, not feelings. Track your metrics monthly to identify where your "leaks" are.

A meta-analysis of coaching business models (n=1,200) suggests that the most successful practitioners maintain a Discovery-to-Close ratio of 1:4. If your ratio is lower, you likely have a "qualification" problem. If it is higher (e.g., 80%), your prices are likely too low.
