Phase 1: The Foundation (Days 1-30)

The first 30 days are about internalizing excellence. This is where you master the P.L.A.N.T.S. Methodâ„¢ and establish the "back office" of your practice. You cannot build a skyscraper on a swamp; your foundation must be rooted in scientific literacy and professional ethics.

Phase 1 Priority

Mastering the Phytonutrient Synergy (P) and Therapeutic Whole Foods (T) principles. You must be able to explain the biochemical "why" behind your recommendations to establish authority.

During this phase, you will focus on:

  • Certification Completion: Finishing all modules and the final exam.
  • The MVP Setup: Securing professional liability insurance, setting up a HIPAA-compliant intake form, and establishing a basic payment processor.
  • Niche Identification: Deciding whether you will focus on Longevity Optimization (L) for seniors, Anti-Inflammatory Alignment (A) for autoimmune clients, or another specific demographic.

Phase 2: The Launch (Days 31-60)

In Phase 2, you move from the classroom to the clinic. This is the Beta-Testing Phase. The goal here is not perfection; it is data collection and feedback. By working with a small group of founding clients, you refine your "bedside manner" and your ability to apply the P.L.A.N.T.S. Method in real-world scenarios.

Phase 3: The Growth (Days 61-90)

By day 60, you should have case studies and testimonials from your beta clients. Phase 3 is about Authority Positioning and Sustainable Lifestyle Integration (S). This is where you shift from "getting clients" to "building a practice."

Growth is fueled by the Nutrient-Density Focus (N). As your clients see measurable results in their energy, biomarkers, and weight, your practice will grow through organic referrals. In this phase, you begin to automate your onboarding and explore group coaching or digital products to scale your impact beyond 1-on-1 hours.
