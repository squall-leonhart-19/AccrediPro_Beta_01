2. Scope of Practice: Information vs. Advice

The Inform & Educate pillar requires a delicate balance. A certified B.I.R.T.H. practitioner provides *evidence*, not *directives*. The moment you tell a client "You should do X," you have stepped out of the doula scope and into the medical or clinical realm.

3. Sustainability in the On-Call Life

Sustainability is the art of being a long-term practitioner. To avoid the "on-call anxiety" that plagues many birth workers, you must develop a Robust Backup System. This is part of the 'Honor' pillarâ€”honoring your own need for rest and family time.

Effective sustainability includes:

  • The 24-Hour Rule: Taking a full 24 hours of "off-grid" time following a birth.
  • Backup Reciprocity: Partnering with 1-2 other B.I.R.T.H. certified doulas to cover births if you are ill or at another birth.
  • The "Doula's Breath" Ritual: A specific centering practice performed when the "labor call" comes in, transitioning you from "home mode" to "professional mode."

4. The 'Honor' Phase: Closing the Business Relationship

The final pillar, Honor & Heal, applies to your business lifecycle. How you end a client relationship determines your referral rate and professional reputation. A structured "exit" includes:

  1. The Final Postpartum Visit: Reviewing the birth story (Transforming the Experience).
  2. The Exit Interview: Asking for feedback on your support.
  3. The Referral Loop: Connecting the client to long-term resources (Lactation consultants, pelvic floor PTs, PMAD specialists).
