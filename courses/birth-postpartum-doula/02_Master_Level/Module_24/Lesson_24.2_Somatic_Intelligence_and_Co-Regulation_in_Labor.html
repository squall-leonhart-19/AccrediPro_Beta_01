The Hierarchy of Response in Labor

4. Identifying Somatic Freeze Cues

While a screaming client is clearly in a sympathetic state, the Dorsal Vagal Freeze response is more subtle and often praised by medical staff as the client being "quiet" or "well-behaved." However, for the Master Doula, this is a red flag for dissociation, which can lead to postpartum trauma and stalled labor.

Case Study: The "Quiet" Transition
Somatic Intervention for Dorsal Shutdown

Client: Elena, 32, G1P0, unmedicated labor.

Presentation: Elena had been active and vocal. Suddenly, at 8cm, she became completely still. Her eyes were open but fixed on a spot on the wall. She stopped responding to her partner's touch and her breathing became shallow and rapid (upper chest only).

Intervention: The doula recognized a "Freeze" state. Instead of asking Elena questions, the doula placed a cold washcloth on Elenaâ€™s face (diver's reflex) and used a firm, grounding voice: "Elena, feel my hand on your shoulder. Feel your feet on the bed." The doula then used "squeezing" touch on Elena's arms to bring awareness back to the periphery.

Outcome: Elena blinked, took a deep "cleansing breath," and began to moan again. Labor resumed its previous intensity, and she birthed 45 minutes later.

Somatic Markers of Freeze (Dorsal Vagal)

  • Fixed Gaze: Eyes looking "through" people or staring at a single point without blinking.
  • Muscle Flaccidity or Extreme Rigidity: A sudden loss of tone or a "frozen" posture.
  • Cold Extremities: Blood shunting to the core, beyond what is normal for labor.
  • Absence of Vocalization: A sudden, eerie silence during intense contractions.
