The cost of replacing a high-level employee is estimated to be 150% to 200% of their annual salary. If a birthing parent does not feel supported during their transition back to work, they are significantly more likely to resign.

3. Insurance Reimbursement Mastery

To scale an agency to the multi-million dollar level, you must master the "In-Network" landscape. While many doulas operate on a cash-pay basis, institutional scaling requires the ability to bill insurance. This requires a transition from "service provider" to "healthcare entity."

Key technical steps for your agency include:

  • NPI (National Provider Identifier): Every doula in your agency must have an individual Type 1 NPI, and the agency must have a Type 2 NPI.
  • Taxonomy Codes: Use code 374J00000X (Doula) for proper classification.
  • CPT Codes: While a universal "doula code" is still emerging, many agencies successfully use 99499 (Unlisted Evaluation and Management Service) or S9445 (Patient Education, Non-Physician) for prenatal/postpartum visits.

Pro-Tip: The "Superbill" Strategy

Before becoming fully in-network, provide clients with automated, ICD-10 coded superbills. This allows your agency to collect its premium fee upfront while providing the client the documentation needed to seek 60-80% reimbursement from their PPO plans.

5. ROI and Impact Reporting

Institutional stakeholders do not buy "feelings"; they buy "outcomes." To maintain and grow contracts, your agency must produce quarterly Impact Reports. This is where the B.I.R.T.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ moves from a philosophy to a data set.

Your reports should track:

  1. Cesarean Rate: Compare your agency's rate against the hospital's baseline (Target: 25-40% lower).
  2. Epidural Timing: Data shows doula support often leads to later (more physiological) epidural requests.
  3. Breastfeeding Initiation: (Target: >90% for B.I.R.T.H. Method clients).
  4. Patient Satisfaction (HCAHPS) Scores: Hospitals are literally paid by the government based on these scores. Show how your doulas raise them.
