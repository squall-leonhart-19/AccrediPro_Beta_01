The 'Inform' to Enrollment Funnel

Marketing a workshop is not about "selling"; it is about demonstrating the "I" (Inform & Educate) pillar of our methodology before the client even pays. A successful funnel moves a prospect through four distinct stages:

  1. Awareness (The Lead Magnet): Providing a free resource, such as a "Hospital Bag Evidence Checklist," that establishes you as an expert.
  2. Engagement (The Nurture): Using email sequences to explain the *why* behind comfort measures or postpartum planning.
  3. The Invitation (The Webinar/Intro): A low-stakes virtual session (see Lesson 34.6) that gives a "taste" of your facilitation style.
  4. Enrollment: The clear call-to-action for the full-scale workshop.

Case Study: Scaling Through Education

From Solo Doula to Program Director

Sarah T., CD(BIRTH)

Location: Urban Metro Area | Experience: 6 Years

The Challenge: Sarah was capped at 3 births per month, earning $4,500 gross. She was experiencing burnout and unable to meet the demand for her services.

The Intervention: Sarah developed a 4-week "Advanced Comfort Measures & Partner Support" series based on the B.I.R.T.H. Method. She priced it at $249 per couple, with a capacity of 10 couples per month.

Outcome:

  • Monthly Revenue: Increased from $4,500 to $6,990 (3 births + 1 workshop).
  • Time Investment: The workshop required 12 hours total (facilitation + admin), compared to the 40-60 hours required for an additional birth.
  • Scaling: Within 12 months, Sarah hired two junior doulas to facilitate the curriculum, taking a 30% management fee.

Contractual Considerations & IP Protection

As you scale, your Intellectual Property (IP)â€”your slides, handouts, and unique methodologyâ€”becomes a primary business asset. Protecting this asset is critical when hiring other facilitators or partnering with venues.

1. Intellectual Property Protection

Ensure all workshop materials carry a copyright notice (Â© [Year] [Your Business Name]). If hiring contractors to teach your curriculum, your contract must state that the curriculum remains your exclusive property and cannot be used by the contractor outside of your agency agreement.

2. Liability Waivers

Workshops involving physical movement (e.g., Reassure & Comfort techniques, pelvic mechanics) carry inherent risks. Participants must sign a Release of Liability that specifically covers physical injury, advice-giving (clarifying you are not providing medical care), and the use of props like birth balls or TENS units.

3. Venue Agreements

When renting space, scrutinize the "Force Majeure" and cancellation clauses. If a workshop is cancelled due to a venue issue, ensure you are not liable for the full rental fee and have a path for rescheduling.

Scaling to a Multi-Doula Workshop Agency

Standardizing the B.I.R.T.H. brand is the key to scaling. Clients should receive the same high-quality "Inform & Educate" experience regardless of which doula is facilitating. This requires:

  • The Facilitator's Playbook: A minute-by-minute guide of the workshop, including scripts for difficult questions and supply checklists.
  • Quality Assurance (QA) Audits: Periodic observation of facilitators to ensure adherence to the evidence-based standards established in Module 2.
  • Centralized Admin: One system for registration, waivers, and follow-up emails, allowing the facilitators to focus purely on the "R" (Reassure) and "T" (Transform) pillars.

Sponsorships and Strategic Partnerships

Not all revenue must come from the pockets of expectant parents. Scaling impact often involves third-party funding. Local businesses, such as pediatricians, pelvic floor physical therapists, and organic baby boutiques, are often willing to sponsor workshops to gain access to their target demographic.

Sponsorship Tiers Example:

  • Bronze ($150): Logo on handouts and one social media shout-out.
  • Silver ($350): Bronze + a 5-minute presentation slot at the workshop.
  • Gold ($750): Silver + "Presented By" naming rights and exclusive referral status for that workshop series.

Furthermore, healthcare grantsâ€”specifically those aimed at reducing maternal mortality and improving "H" (Honor & Heal) outcomes in marginalized communitiesâ€”can fund entire workshop series, allowing you to offer them for free to the public while still being paid your professional rate.

How does the "VIP Tier" in a workshop pricing model support the "H" (Honor & Heal) pillar of the B.I.R.T.H. Method?

The VIP tier provides high-margin revenue that can be used to subsidize "Scholarship Seats," ensuring that evidence-based education is accessible to under-resourced families, thereby honoring the community's need for equitable healing and support.

What is the most critical contractual element when hiring a second doula to teach your proprietary workshop curriculum?

An Intellectual Property (IP) clause. This ensures that the curriculum remains the property of your agency and prevents the contractor from taking your materials to start a competing program.

Key Takeaways

  • Scaling requires moving from a 1-to-1 service model to a 1-to-many educational model.
  • Tiered pricing (Early-bird, VIP, Scholarship) balances business sustainability with community equity.
  • Marketing funnels should lead with value (the "I" pillar) to build trust before the ask.
  • Protect your business through robust IP clauses, liability waivers, and standardized facilitator playbooks.
  • Sponsorships and grants can decouple your income from participant fees, allowing for broader community impact.
