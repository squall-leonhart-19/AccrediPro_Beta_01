1. Advanced Bookkeeping for On-Call Professionals

In Lesson 1, we established the legal entity of your doula practice. Now, we must build the financial architecture that supports that entity. Doula work is unique; it is a blend of professional services and high-readiness emergency response. Standard bookkeeping often fails to capture the true cost of being "on-call."

Advanced bookkeeping requires moving beyond simple income/expense tracking to activity-based costing. For a birth doula, every client represents a "project" with variable costs that can fluctuate based on labor length, travel distance, and support requirements.

Key Financial Terms

Accrual Accounting

Recording income when earned and expenses when incurred, rather than when cash actually moves.

Variable Expenses

Costs that change in proportion to the number of clients served (e.g., birth pool liners, mileage).

2. Tax Optimization: Beyond the Basics

For the independent doula, taxes are often the single largest expense. However, because your home is your headquarters and your car is your mobile office, the opportunities for optimization are significant. A 2022 survey of independent healthcare consultants found that those who utilized strategic tax planning saved an average of 18% more annually than those who only used standard deductions.

The Birth Kit & Equipment Depreciation

Your birth kit is not just a bag of tools; it is a capital asset. Under Section 179 of the tax code, many doulas can deduct the full cost of expensive equipment (like high-end TENS units, birth pools, or specialized massage tables) in the year they are purchased, rather than depreciating them over several years.

The "feast or famine" cycle is the primary reason doulas leave the profession within the first three years. One month may bring three births (high income), while the next brings none (zero income), yet fixed costs like insurance and software remain constant.

4. Retirement Planning for the High-Earning Doula

As an independent professional, you do not have an employer-sponsored 401(k). You must become your own pension manager. For the doula earning above $60,000, the SEP-IRA (Simplified Employee Pension) is often the gold standard.

  • High Contribution Limits: You can contribute up to 25% of your net earnings (up to a cap of $66,000 in 2023).
  • Tax-Deferred Growth: Contributions are tax-deductible, reducing your current tax bill while growing tax-free until retirement.
  • Flexibility: Unlike some plans, you aren't required to contribute every year. In a "low-birth" year, you can skip contributions without penalty.

A SEP-IRA allows for significantly higher contribution limits (up to 25% of net income) compared to a traditional IRA, which is crucial for high-earning independent practitioners looking to reduce their taxable income while building substantial long-term wealth.
