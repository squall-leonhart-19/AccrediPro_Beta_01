Ethical Considerations and Scope of Practice

As a Birth Doula Coachâ„¢, it's crucial to adhere to a strict code of ethics and understand the boundaries of your scope of practice. This includes:

  • Maintaining confidentiality and respecting the privacy of your clients.
  • Avoiding conflicts of interest and disclosing any potential biases.
  • Providing honest and accurate information, and refraining from making false or misleading claims.
  • Respecting the autonomy of your clients and empowering them to make their own decisions.
  • Understanding the legal and regulatory requirements that govern the doula profession in your area.

It's important to remember that you are not a therapist or counselor. If your clients are struggling with emotional or mental health issues, it's essential to refer them to a qualified professional. Similarly, you should not provide medical advice or interfere with the client-doula relationship. Your role is to support and guide doulas in their professional development, not to dictate their actions or beliefs. The DONA International Standards of Practice and Code of Ethics can serve as a foundational guide.
