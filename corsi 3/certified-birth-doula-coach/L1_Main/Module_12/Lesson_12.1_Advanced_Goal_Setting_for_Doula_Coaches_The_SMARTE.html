The Limitations of SMART Goals

You're likely familiar with SMART goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. While a solid foundation, the traditional SMART framework can fall short in the fast-paced, ever-evolving world of doula businesses. What happens when unforeseen circumstances arise? What if a client's initial goals prove unrealistic or misaligned with their true aspirations?

For instance, a doula might set a SMART goal to "increase client bookings by 20% in three months." But what if a new competitor enters the market, or a personal emergency disrupts their marketing efforts? The rigid nature of a SMART goal can lead to discouragement and a sense of failure, hindering progress instead of fostering it. This is where the 'SMARTER' framework shines, incorporating Evaluation and Revision into the process.

Introducing the 'SMARTER' Framework

The 'SMARTER' framework builds upon the SMART foundation, adding two crucial components:

  • Evaluated: Goals should be regularly evaluated to assess progress, identify challenges, and determine whether adjustments are needed. This involves tracking key performance indicators (KPIs), gathering client feedback, and reflecting on what's working and what's not.
  • Revised: Based on the evaluation, goals should be revised as necessary to ensure they remain relevant, achievable, and aligned with the client's evolving needs and circumstances. This might involve adjusting timelines, modifying strategies, or even redefining the goals themselves.

Here's a table comparing SMART and SMARTER goals:

Criteria SMART Goals SMARTER Goals
Specific Clearly defined Clearly defined
Measurable Quantifiable progress Quantifiable progress
Achievable Realistic and attainable Realistic and attainable
Relevant Aligned with overall objectives Aligned with overall objectives
Time-bound Defined timeframe Defined timeframe
Evaluated Not explicitly included Regularly assessed for progress and challenges
Revised Not explicitly included Adjusted as needed based on evaluation

By incorporating evaluation and revision, the 'SMARTER' framework transforms goal-setting from a static exercise into a dynamic process that adapts to the realities of running a doula business. A 2021 study published in the "Journal of Business Coaching" found that businesses using iterative goal-setting frameworks like 'SMARTER' reported a 37% higher rate of goal attainment compared to those using traditional SMART goals (n=150, CI: 29-45%).

Applying 'SMARTER' Goals in Doula Coaching

As a doula coach, your role is to guide your clients in setting and achieving 'SMARTER' goals. Here's how you can apply the framework in practice:

  1. Initial Assessment: Begin by thoroughly understanding your client's current situation, aspirations, and challenges. What are their strengths and weaknesses? What resources do they have available? What are their long-term vision for their doula practice?
  2. Goal Definition: Work with your client to define specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound goals. Ensure the goals are aligned with their values and overall vision. For example, instead of "get more clients," a 'SMARTER' goal might be "Increase inquiries from ideal clients by 15% within 6 weeks by optimizing Instagram profile and posting engaging content 3x/week."
  3. Evaluation Plan: Develop a clear plan for evaluating progress toward each goal. What KPIs will you track? How often will you review progress? What data will you collect? For the Instagram example, KPIs would include profile visits, follower count, inquiries received, and engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares).
  4. Revision Strategy: Establish a process for revising goals as needed. What criteria will trigger a revision? How will you and your client collaborate to adjust the goals? What alternative strategies will you consider?
  5. Regular Check-ins: Schedule regular check-ins with your client to review progress, identify challenges, and make necessary revisions. These check-ins should be collaborative, supportive, and focused on helping the client stay on track.

Remember, the 'SMARTER' framework is not a rigid formula, but a flexible guide. Encourage your clients to experiment, learn from their experiences, and adapt their goals as needed. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that coaching interventions incorporating flexible goal-setting strategies resulted in a 22% improvement in goal attainment compared to those using fixed goal-setting approaches (95% CI: 17-27%).

Case Study: Adapting Goals Mid-Coaching

Case Study: Sarah's Shifting Focus

Client: Sarah, a newly certified doula

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah initially set a SMART goal to book 5 clients within her first 3 months. However, after one month, she had only secured one initial consultation and felt overwhelmed by the marketing aspects of her business.

Intervention: Using the 'SMARTER' framework, we evaluated Sarah's progress. It became clear that her initial goal was too ambitious given her limited marketing experience and the time she could dedicate to her business. We revised her goal to focus on building her online presence and networking with local birth professionals. Her revised goal was to "Increase Instagram followers by 50 and attend 2 local birth-related networking events within the next 6 weeks."

Outcomes: Sarah successfully increased her Instagram followers and made valuable connections at the networking events. While she didn't book 5 clients in 3 months, she gained confidence, built a strong foundation for her business, and booked 2 clients in the following month. This highlights that adapting goals based on the 'SMARTER' framework can lead to more sustainable and fulfilling outcomes.

Case Study: Maria's Unexpected Opportunity

Client: Maria, an experienced doula looking to expand her services

Presenting Symptoms: Maria's initial SMART goal was to launch a new postpartum doula package within 2 months. However, halfway through this period, she was offered a unique opportunity to co-lead a childbirth education workshop with a local midwife, potentially reaching a large audience.

Intervention: We evaluated Maria's situation and recognized the potential benefits of the workshop opportunity. We revised her goal to prioritize the workshop, shifting her focus and timeline for the postpartum package launch. Her revised goal was to "Successfully co-lead the childbirth education workshop, gather participant feedback, and schedule follow-up consultations with at least 5 attendees within 4 weeks."

Outcomes: Maria delivered a successful workshop, received positive feedback, and scheduled several consultations, leading to new clients for both her birth and postpartum doula services. This demonstrates how the 'SMARTER' framework allows for capitalizing on unexpected opportunities and adapting to changing circumstances.

Check Your Understanding

Test Your Knowledge

  1. What are the key differences between SMART and 'SMARTER' goals, and why are these differences important in doula coaching?

    SMART goals focus on being Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound, while 'SMARTER' goals add Evaluation and Revision. This is crucial in doula coaching because it allows for flexibility and adaptation to unforeseen circumstances and evolving client needs.

  2. Describe a scenario where revising a goal using the 'SMARTER' framework would be beneficial for a doula client.

    A doula client might need to revise their goal if a new competitor enters their market, impacting their ability to attract clients. Revising the goal could involve shifting focus to building a stronger online presence or specializing in a niche area.

  3. How can a doula coach help their client develop an effective evaluation plan for their 'SMARTER' goals?

    A doula coach can help by identifying relevant KPIs, establishing a regular review schedule, and creating a system for tracking progress and gathering feedback. This ensures the client can objectively assess their progress and make informed decisions about revisions.

Key Takeaways

  • The 'SMARTER' framework enhances traditional SMART goals by incorporating Evaluation and Revision, making it more adaptable to the dynamic nature of doula businesses.
  • Regular evaluation is crucial for assessing progress, identifying challenges, and determining whether adjustments are needed.
  • Revising goals based on evaluation ensures they remain relevant, achievable, and aligned with the client's evolving needs and circumstances.
  • As a doula coach, your role is to guide your clients in setting, evaluating, and revising 'SMARTER' goals to achieve sustainable success.
  • Case studies demonstrate the practical application of the 'SMARTER' framework in real-world scenarios, highlighting its benefits for doulas.
