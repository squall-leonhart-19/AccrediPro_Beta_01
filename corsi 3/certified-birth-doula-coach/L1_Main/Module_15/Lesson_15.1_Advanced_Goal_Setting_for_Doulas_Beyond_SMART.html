Refining SMART Goals with CLEAR

You're familiar with SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). But let's introduce CLEAR, an evolution that enhances client engagement and motivation. CLEAR stands for:

  • Collaborative: Goals should involve input and buy-in from all relevant parties.
  • Limited: Goals should be limited in scope and duration for focused effort.
  • Emotional: Goals should connect with the client's emotions and values.
  • Appreciable: Break down large goals into smaller, appreciable steps.
  • Refinable: Goals should be flexible and adaptable to changing circumstances.

Incorporating CLEAR principles helps doulas feel more ownership and excitement about their goals, increasing the likelihood of achievement. For example, instead of a SMART goal like "Increase client bookings by 10% in 3 months," a CLEAR goal might be "Collaboratively develop a marketing plan to attract ideal clients, focusingon connecting emotionally through shared values, breaking the plan into weekly, appreciable tasks, and refining the approach based on client feedback."

Exploring the WOOP Method

The WOOP method, developed by Gabriele Oettingen, is a powerful cognitive strategy that helps individuals identify and overcome obstacles to goal achievement. WOOP stands for:

  • Wish: Identify a specific and achievable wish or goal.
  • Outcome: Visualize the best possible outcome of achieving that wish.
  • Obstacle: Identify the most significant obstacle preventing you from achieving the wish.
  • Plan: Create an "if-then" plan to overcome the obstacle.

For doulas, WOOP can be incredibly effective in addressing common challenges such as marketing, networking, or time management. For example:

  • Wish: Increase attendance at my childbirth education classes.
  • Outcome: More expectant parents are well-prepared and confident for birth, leading to positive birth experiences and increased referrals.
  • Obstacle: I struggle to consistently promote my classes on social media.
  • Plan: If I feel overwhelmed by social media, then I will schedule 30 minutes each week to create and schedule posts using a social media management tool.

Visualization Techniques and Affirmations

Visualization and affirmations are powerful tools for reinforcing positive beliefs and fostering a growth mindset. For visualization, encourage your clients to create a vivid mental image of themselves successfully achieving their goals. This could involve visualizing a packed childbirth education class, receiving glowing testimonials from satisfied clients, or confidently navigating a challenging birth. A 2018 study in the Journal of Applied Sport Psychology found that visualization can improve performance by up to 23%.

Affirmations are positive statements that reinforce desired beliefs and behaviors. Some examples of affirmations for doulas include:

  • "I am a confident and skilled doula who provides exceptional support to birthing families."
  • "My childbirth education classes empower expectant parents to have positive and informed birth experiences."
  • "I attract ideal clients who value my expertise and dedication."

Encourage clients to repeat their affirmations daily, ideally in front of a mirror, to maximize their impact.

Integrating Values-Based Goal Setting

Values-based goal setting ensures that your client's professional goals align with their personal ethics and values. This alignment increases motivation and fosters a sense of purpose. To identify core values, ask clients questions like:

  • What is most important to you in your work as a doula?
  • What principles guide your decisions and actions?
  • What kind of impact do you want to have on the world?

Common values for doulas might include compassion, empowerment, integrity, education, and community. Once values are identified, help clients set goals that directly reflect and support those values. For instance, if a client values education, they might set a goal to attend a specific number of continuing education workshops each year or to develop a new childbirth education curriculum.

Case Study: Sarah's Thriving Doula Practice

Client: Sarah, 32, a certified birth doula with 2 years of experience.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt stuck in her business, struggling to attract enough clients and feeling overwhelmed by marketing tasks.

Intervention: Sarah worked with her coach to refine her SMART goals using the CLEAR framework. She also used the WOOP method to address her social media marketing challenges. Finally, she identified her core values (compassion, education, and empowerment) and set goals that aligned with those values.

Outcomes: Within 6 months, Sarah's client bookings increased by 30%. She felt more confident and motivated in her marketing efforts and experienced a renewed sense of purpose in her work. She stated that aligning her goals with her values made her work feel more authentic and rewarding.
