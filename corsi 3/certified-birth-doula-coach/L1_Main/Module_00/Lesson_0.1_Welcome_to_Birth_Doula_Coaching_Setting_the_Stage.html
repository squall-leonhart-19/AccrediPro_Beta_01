You Belong Here

Are you in your 40s, 50s, or beyond? Perfect! You're exactly who we need. Forget any whispers of "Am I too old?" or "Do I have enough experience?" Your life experience is your superpower. The wisdom, empathy, and resilience you've cultivated over the years are invaluable assets in this field.

Don't underestimate the power of lived experience. You've navigated relationships, careers, and perhaps even raised your own children. You understand the complexities of life and the challenges families face. This empathy is something that cannot be taught, and it will allow you to connect with your clients on a much deeper level.

Remember, doula work and parent coaching aren't about being perfect; they're about being present, supportive, and non-judgmental. Your age and experience make you uniquely qualified to offer this kind of support. Embrace your journey, trust your instincts, and know that you have something incredibly valuable to offer.

Case Study: Sarah, the "Older" Doula

Client: Sarah, 52

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah was hesitant to enroll in the doula certification program, believing she was "too old" compared to other aspiring doulas.

Intervention: Sarah was encouraged to focus on her life experience as a strength. She was paired with a mentor who was also an "older" doula. She participated in workshops focused on leveraging her unique skills and experiences.

Outcomes: Sarah excelled in the program. Her clients specifically sought her out because of her maturity and life experience. She quickly built a thriving doula practice and reported feeling more fulfilled than ever before.

First Steps

Ready to dive in? Here are your first steps to maximize your success in this program:

  1. **Familiarize yourself with the course platform.** Explore the modules, resources, and community forums.
  2. **Introduce yourself to your cohort.** Share your background, your goals, and what you hope to gain from this program.
  3. **Set realistic goals for your learning.** Break down the curriculum into manageable chunks and create a study schedule that works for you.
  4. **Participate actively in the community.** Ask questions, share your insights, and offer support to others.
  5. **Celebrate your progress!** Acknowledge your accomplishments, no matter how small, and reward yourself for your hard work.
