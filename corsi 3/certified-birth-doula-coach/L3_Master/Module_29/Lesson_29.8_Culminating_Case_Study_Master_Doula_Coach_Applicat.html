Her Question
"How do I handle a client who wants me to do things outside my scope of practice?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case:

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 35, who is planning a home birth. She wants me to administer herbal remedies during labor and provide postpartum massage. I know that's not really my role, but I don't want to disappoint her. What should I do?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Is Sarah clear on her scope of practice as a doula?
  • Does she understand the potential legal and ethical implications?
  • How can she communicate boundaries effectively and empathetically?
  • Does she know how to refer the client to appropriate professionals?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Clearly define the doula's role: emotional, informational, and physical support. Highlight what falls outside this scope (e.g., medical interventions, administering herbs, massage therapy).

2

Discuss Legal and Ethical Considerations

Explain the potential risks of practicing outside the scope of certification. Emphasize the importance of protecting both the client and herself.

3

Develop Communication Strategies

Help Sarah craft a clear, empathetic response to the client. Focus on what she CAN do, while gently explaining her limitations.

4

Provide Referral Resources

Equip Sarah with a list of trusted professionals (e.g., midwives, massage therapists, herbalists) she can refer the client to. This demonstrates care while staying within her boundaries.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's great that you're thinking so carefully about your client's needs. Your desire to support her is admirable. That said, let's explore how to do that ethically and safely."
Ask for Her Perspective
"Before I share my thoughts, what are your initial concerns about providing herbal remedies or massage? What feels uncomfortable to you?"
Educate on Scope and Risks
"As doulas, we provide invaluable support, but we're not medical professionals. Administering herbs or performing massage could have unintended consequences, and it falls outside our training and certification. This could open you up to legal liability, and more importantly, could potentially harm your client."
Collaborate on a Response
"Let's work together on a script you can use with Emily. How about something like, 'I'm so happy to support you during your home birth. While I can't administer herbs or provide massage, I can connect you with some amazing midwives and massage therapists who specialize in prenatal care. How does that sound?'"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to clearly define the scope of practice for a new doula?

Defining the scope of practice protects both the doula and the client by ensuring that the doula provides services within their training and expertise, avoiding potential harm or legal issues.

2. What are some strategies for helping a new doula communicate boundaries effectively with clients?

Role-playing difficult conversations, providing scripts, and emphasizing the importance of clear and empathetic communication can help new doulas set boundaries while maintaining a positive client relationship.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new doulas is a powerful way to give back and elevate the profession.
  • Clearly defining scope of practice is crucial for ethical and safe care.
  • Communication skills are essential for setting boundaries and managing client expectations.
  • Providing resources and referrals demonstrates care while staying within your role.
  • Your experience and wisdom are invaluable assets to share with the next generation of doulas.
