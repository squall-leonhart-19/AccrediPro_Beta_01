Scope of Practice and Boundaries

One of the most common ethical pitfalls involves blurring the lines of your coaching role. Remember, you are a coach, not a therapist, legal advisor, or medical professional. Experienced doulas may present complex personal or professional challenges that require expertise outside your scope.

Key Considerations:

  • Defining Your Expertise: Clearly articulate what you can and cannot address within your coaching services.
  • Recognizing Red Flags: Be alert to client issues that require referral to qualified professionals.
  • Maintaining Boundaries: Establish clear professional boundaries to prevent conflicts of interest and protect client well-being.

Managing Conflicts of Interest

Conflicts of interest can arise in various forms. Perhaps you're coaching a doula who is also a close friend, or you're being asked to endorse a product that you haven't fully vetted. Navigating these situations requires transparency and careful consideration.

Strategies for Managing Conflicts:

  • Disclosure: Be upfront with clients about any potential conflicts.
  • Recusal: Step aside from coaching if a conflict compromises your objectivity.
  • Objective Decision-Making: Base your recommendations on evidence and ethical principles, not personal relationships.

Proactive Strategies for Ethical Dilemmas

Prevention is always better than cure. By proactively addressing potential ethical dilemmas, you can minimize the risk of harm and maintain a high standard of practice. This aligns with the Uncover phase of The G.U.I.D.E. Frameworkâ„¢, where we identify potential challenges before they escalate.

Examples of proactive strategies:

  • Informed Consent: Ensure clients understand the nature of coaching, confidentiality policies, and their rights.
  • Ethical Frameworks: Familiarize yourself with established ethical guidelines for coaching and healthcare professionals.
  • Documentation: Maintain detailed records of coaching sessions, including discussions of ethical considerations.

Ethical Case Studies in Doula Coaching

Let's examine a couple of realistic case studies to illustrate the application of ethical principles in doula coaching.

Case Study 1: The Overextended Doula

Client: Sarah, a 38-year-old doula with 8 years of experience.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reports feeling overwhelmed and burned out, taking on too many clients, and struggling to provide adequate support.

Intervention: As her coach, you help Sarah analyze her client load, time management skills, and personal boundaries. You encourage her to explore strategies for delegating tasks, setting realistic expectations, and prioritizing self-care.

Ethical Dilemma: Sarah reveals she has been subtly pressuring clients to choose specific birth locations based on her own preferences and ease of access. This raises concerns about her objectivity and client autonomy.

Resolution: You address Sarah's behavior by highlighting the ethical implications of influencing client choices for personal gain. You guide her in developing a more client-centered approach, emphasizing informed consent and respecting diverse preferences. You suggest she disclose any personal biases regarding birth locations to future clients.

Outcomes: Sarah acknowledges her unethical behavior and commits to prioritizing client autonomy. She implements strategies to manage her workload and avoid future burnout, ultimately improving the quality of her doula practice.

Case Study 2: The Social Media Influencer Doula

Client: Emily, a 29-year-old doula aiming to grow her business.

Presenting Symptoms: Emily seeks coaching to improve her social media presence and attract more clients.

Intervention: You advise Emily on effective social media strategies, including content creation, engagement tactics, and ethical marketing practices.

Ethical Dilemma: Emily starts sharing client birth stories and photos on her social media without obtaining explicit, informed consent. She believes it's "good for business" and assumes implied consent.

Resolution: You immediately address the ethical violation of breaching client confidentiality. You educate Emily on the importance of obtaining written, informed consent before sharing any client information, even if anonymized. You emphasize the potential harm to client trust and the legal repercussions of unauthorized disclosure.

Outcomes: Emily understands the severity of her actions and immediately removes the unauthorized content. She develops a comprehensive consent form for future clients and implements strict protocols for protecting client privacy on social media.
