"I have my first potential client consult tomorrow and I'm terrified! Can you help me prepare?"

The Scenario

Sarah, your mentee, is feeling overwhelmed as she prepares for her first client consultation. She's seeking your guidance to navigate this critical step with confidence.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Consultation Structure

Walk her through a structured consultation process: introductions, understanding client needs, explaining your services, setting expectations, and discussing next steps.

2

Active Listening Skills

Emphasize the importance of active listening. Teach her how to ask open-ended questions and truly hear the client's concerns and desires.

3

Value Proposition

Help her articulate the value she brings as a birth doula coach. What unique benefits does she offer? How does she stand out from the competition?

4

Pricing Confidence

Discuss pricing strategies and help her determine a fair and confident fee structure. Role-play addressing potential client objections related to cost.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Encouragement
"Sarah, it's completely normal to feel nervous before your first consultation! Remember all the knowledge you've gained. You're ready for this."
Role-Play Practice
"Let's role-play a mock consultation. I'll be the client. Show me how you would handle the session. Don't worry about perfection, just give it your best shot."
Positive Reinforcement
"I loved how you [insert specific positive action, e.g., actively listened to my concerns]. That's a valuable skill that will make clients feel heard and understood."
Constructive Suggestion
"One small tweak: try to articulate your value proposition more clearly. Instead of saying '[generic statement],' try saying '[specific benefit to the client].' How does that feel?"
Empowerment
"You've got this, Sarah! Remember to breathe, trust your instincts, and focus on serving the client. I'm here if you need me, but I believe in you!"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to normalize challenges and setbacks when mentoring a new practitioner?

Normalizing challenges helps the mentee build resilience, avoid discouragement, and view setbacks as learning opportunities rather than failures.

2. What are some effective ways to help a mentee develop pricing confidence?

Discuss pricing strategies, help them articulate their value proposition, role-play addressing client objections, and share examples of successful pricing models.

You're Becoming a Leader!

Key Takeaways

  • Structured Guidance: Provide a framework for new practitioners to follow, especially during initial client interactions.
  • Active Listening: Emphasize the importance of truly hearing and understanding client needs.
  • Value Articulation: Help mentees clearly communicate the unique benefits they offer.
  • Pricing Confidence: Address pricing anxieties and empower mentees to value their services appropriately.
  • Positive Reinforcement: Focus on strengths and celebrate small victories to build confidence.
