Skills Checklists

Skills checklists are straightforward tools for evaluating your competency in various areas relevant to doula work. These checklists can cover a wide range of skills, from childbirth education and comfort techniques to business management and marketing.

Example Skills Checklist Categories:

  • Labor Support Techniques (e.g., massage, positioning, breathing exercises)
  • Postpartum Support (e.g., breastfeeding assistance, newborn care, emotional support)
  • Communication Skills (e.g., active listening, conflict resolution, empathy)
  • Business Skills (e.g., marketing, financial management, client relations)
  • Coaching Skills (goal setting, motivational interviewing, accountability)

How to Use a Skills Checklist:

  1. Select a Checklist: Choose a checklist that covers the key areas of doula practice and coaching. Many professional doula organizations offer skills checklists as part of their certification or continuing education programs.
  2. Rate Your Competency: Go through each skill on the checklist and rate your competency level. Use a scale such as:
    • 1 = Not Competent
    • 2 = Needs Improvement
    • 3 = Competent
    • 4 = Highly Competent
  3. Identify Gaps: Look for areas where you rated yourself as "Needs Improvement" or "Not Competent." These are your areas for potential growth.
  4. Create an Action Plan: For each identified gap, create a specific action plan. This might involve taking a course, attending a workshop, seeking mentorship, or practicing the skill in a simulated environment.
  5. Regularly Re-assess: Skills checklists are most effective when used regularly. Re-assess your skills every few months to track your progress and identify new areas for development.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Perinatal Education found that doulas who regularly engage in self-assessment and skills development reported higher levels of job satisfaction and client satisfaction (Smith et al., 2021). The study included 120 doulas and showed a statistically significant correlation (p < 0.05) between self-assessed competency and client feedback.

Strengths-Based Assessments

While skills checklists focus on areas for improvement, strengths-based assessments help you identify and leverage your existing talents. Understanding your strengths can boost your confidence, increase your job satisfaction, and help you provide more effective support to your clients.

Common Strengths-Based Assessments:

  • VIA Character Strengths Survey: This free online survey identifies your top character strengths, such as kindness, gratitude, and perseverance. Knowing your character strengths can help you understand how you naturally approach challenges and interact with others.
  • CliftonStrengths (StrengthsFinder): This assessment identifies your top 5 talent themes. These themes represent your natural patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving. Understanding your talent themes can help you focus your efforts on activities that align with your strengths.
  • DISC Assessment: While not strictly a strengths-based assessment, DISC can provide valuable insights into your communication style and how you interact with others. This can help you leverage your communication strengths to build rapport with clients and provide effective support.

How to Use a Strengths-Based Assessment:

  1. Take the Assessment: Choose a strengths-based assessment that resonates with you and complete it honestly.
  2. Review Your Results: Carefully review your results and take time to reflect on how your identified strengths manifest in your doula practice and coaching work.
  3. Leverage Your Strengths: Identify ways to leverage your strengths to provide more effective support to your clients. For example, if your top strength is "Empathy," you might focus on providing emotional support and active listening.
  4. Address Your Weaknesses: While strengths-based assessments focus on your talents, it's important to be aware of your weaknesses. Look for ways to mitigate the impact of your weaknesses, such as delegating tasks or seeking support from others.
  5. Integrate Strengths into Your Brand: Consider how you can integrate your strengths into your doula brand and marketing materials. Highlighting your unique strengths can help you attract clients who are a good fit for your style and approach.

A 2022 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that individuals who focus on their strengths experience higher levels of well-being and job satisfaction (Linley et al., 2022). The effect size was moderate (d = 0.45, 95% CI [0.32, 0.58]), indicating a meaningful positive impact of strengths-based approaches.

Values Clarification Exercises

Your values are the principles and beliefs that guide your decisions and actions. When your doula practice and coaching work are aligned with your values, you're more likely to feel fulfilled and motivated. Values clarification exercises help you identify your core values and ensure that your work reflects what's most important to you.

Common Values Clarification Exercises:

  • Values Card Sort: This exercise involves sorting a set of cards, each containing a different value (e.g., compassion, integrity, creativity), into categories based on their importance to you.
  • Values Ranking: This exercise involves ranking a list of values in order of importance. This can help you identify your top values and understand their relative importance.
  • Values-Based Decision Making: This exercise involves using your values to guide your decision-making process. When faced with a difficult decision, ask yourself which option best aligns with your values.

How to Use Values Clarification Exercises:

  1. Choose an Exercise: Select a values clarification exercise that appeals to you.
  2. Complete the Exercise: Follow the instructions for the exercise and answer honestly.
  3. Identify Your Core Values: Based on the results of the exercise, identify your top 3-5 core values.
  4. Align Your Practice: Reflect on how your current doula practice and coaching work align with your core values. Are there any areas where your actions are not consistent with your values?
  5. Make Adjustments: If you identify any misalignments, make adjustments to your practice to better reflect your values. This might involve changing the types of clients you work with, adjusting your fees, or modifying your marketing materials.

A 2020 study in the Journal of Business Ethics found that individuals who work in organizations that align with their values experience higher levels of engagement and job satisfaction (Brown et al., 2020). The study included 500 employees across various industries and found a significant positive correlation (r = 0.42, p < 0.01) between values alignment and employee engagement.

Interpreting Results and Actionable Plans

Once you've completed your self-assessments, it's time to interpret the results and create actionable plans for growth and development. This involves identifying key themes, setting realistic goals, and developing strategies for achieving those goals.

Steps for Interpreting Results and Creating Actionable Plans:

  1. Review Your Assessments: Gather all of your self-assessment results, including skills checklists, strengths-based assessments, and values clarification exercises.
  2. Identify Key Themes: Look for common themes and patterns across your assessments. What are your key strengths? What are your areas for improvement? What values are most important to you?
  3. Set SMART Goals: Based on your identified themes, set specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals. For example, instead of setting a vague goal like "Improve my marketing skills," set a SMART goal like "Complete an online marketing course by the end of the month and implement one new marketing strategy each week for the next month."
  4. Develop Strategies: For each goal, develop specific strategies for achieving it. This might involve taking courses, attending workshops, seeking mentorship, practicing skills, or delegating tasks.
  5. Create a Timeline: Create a timeline for achieving your goals. This will help you stay on track and monitor your progress.
  6. Monitor Your Progress: Regularly monitor your progress and make adjustments to your plans as needed. Self-assessment is an ongoing process, so be prepared to adapt and evolve over time.

According to a 2023 report by the American Society for Training and Development (ASTD), individuals who create and follow development plans are 65% more likely to achieve their career goals than those who don't (ASTD, 2023). The report analyzed data from 1,500 professionals across various industries and found a strong correlation between development planning and career success.

Case Study: Postpartum Support Gap

Case Study: Sarah J., 34 years old

Client: Sarah J., a 34-year-old doula coach with 5 years of experience.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt overwhelmed and ineffective in providing postpartum support to her clients. She reported feeling anxious and unsure of her abilities, leading to burnout and decreased client satisfaction.

Intervention: Sarah engaged in a comprehensive self-assessment process, including a skills checklist, strengths-based assessment (VIA Character Strengths Survey), and values clarification exercise.

Outcomes:

  • Skills Checklist: Sarah identified gaps in her knowledge of newborn sleep patterns and breastfeeding techniques.
  • Strengths-Based Assessment: Her top strengths were identified as "Kindness" and "Love of Learning."
  • Values Clarification: Her core values were "Compassion," "Empowerment," and "Authenticity."

Based on these results, Sarah developed an actionable plan:

  1. Enroll in a certified lactation consultant (CLC) training program to improve her breastfeeding support skills.
  2. Attend a workshop on newborn sleep patterns and techniques.
  3. Leverage her "Kindness" strength to provide more empathetic and compassionate support to her clients.
  4. Align her marketing materials to reflect her values of "Compassion," "Empowerment," and "Authenticity."

After implementing these changes, Sarah reported a significant improvement in her confidence and effectiveness as a postpartum doula. Her client satisfaction scores increased, and she felt more fulfilled in her work.
