Simulated Coaching Sessions

This lesson centers around simulated coaching sessions. You will have the opportunity to act as a coach in a realistic scenario, working with a participant acting as a doula client. These sessions are designed to mimic real-world coaching engagements, providing a safe and supportive environment to practice and refine your skills. Remember the principles we've covered throughout the program, from active listening and powerful questioning to goal setting and accountability.

Consider this your dress rehearsal! Use this opportunity to experiment with different coaching techniques, apply the G.U.I.D.E. Frameworkâ„¢, and discover what works best for you. Focus on creating a safe and empowering space for your client to explore their challenges and opportunities. Remember the importance of building rapport and establishing trust right from the start. This will help your client feel comfortable opening up and engaging in the coaching process.

Peer Review

Following each simulated coaching session, you will participate in a peer review process. This is a valuable opportunity to learn from your fellow coaches and gain different perspectives on your coaching style and effectiveness. You will provide constructive feedback to your peers, focusing on their strengths and areas for improvement. You will also receive feedback on your own coaching performance, which can help you identify blind spots and refine your approach. Remember to approach the peer review process with an open mind and a willingness to learn.

When providing feedback, be specific and actionable. Instead of saying "You need to be more confident," try something like "I noticed that you hesitated before asking about the client's financial goals. Perhaps practicing those questions beforehand could help you feel more confident." Remember to focus on the behavior, not the person. Frame your feedback in a way that is supportive and encouraging. Your goal is to help your peers grow and develop as coaches.

Personalized Improvement Plans

The final stepin this lesson is to develop a personalized improvement plan based on the feedback you receive from your peers and the instructor. This plan should outline specific steps you will take to address your areas for improvement and further develop your coaching skills. Your improvement plan should be realistic, achievable, and time-bound.

Consider using the SMART goals framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) to guide your improvement plan. For example, instead of saying "I want to improve my active listening skills," you could say "I will practice active listening techniques for 15 minutes each day for the next week, focusing on summarizing and reflecting back what the client says. I will then ask a friend to give me feedback on my active listening skills in a practice conversation." This is a much more specific and actionable goal.
