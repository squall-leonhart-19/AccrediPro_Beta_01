Susan, 42

Saw your ad on a local parenting Facebook group. Interested in postpartum doula services.

Her Situation
Expecting her second child. Struggled with postpartum depression after her first. Anxious about juggling two kids.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if we can afford a doula, but I know I need help."
Decision Style
Emotional, values connection and empathy. Needs reassurance and practical solutions.
Her Goal
"I want to enjoy this baby and not feel overwhelmed and alone."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out! I'm so excited to chat with you about your upcoming arrival. First, congratulations! How are you feeling?"
YOU:
"I understand this is your second. How was your postpartum experience with your first child?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about what you're most concerned about this time around. What are you hoping to avoid or do differently?"
YOU:
"It sounds like you're juggling a lot. How do you envision a doula fitting into your postpartum plan?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and have the perfect postpartum experience, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for someone who can provide practical support, emotional reassurance, and help you navigate the challenges of having two young children. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach as a postpartum doula is to provide personalized support that meets your specific needs. This includes newborn care, sibling care, light household tasks, meal preparation, and emotional support. I also offer evidence-based information on breastfeeding, sleep, and postpartum recovery. With my support, you can focus on bonding with your baby and enjoying this special time."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you're looking for? Great! Let's talk about my packages and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if we can afford it."
"I understand that budget is a consideration. I offer different packages to fit various needs and budgets. We can explore those options together. Also, consider the long-term benefits of investing in your postpartum well-being. Reduced stress and improved mental health can have a ripple effect on your entire family."
"I need to talk to my partner."
"That's completely understandable. Would you like me to send you a detailed proposal that you can share with them? Or, I'd be happy to schedule a quick call with both of you to answer any questions."
"I'm worried about having a stranger in my home."
"That's a valid concern. I prioritize building a trusting relationship with my clients. I'm happy to provide references from other families I've worked with. We can also schedule a meet-and-greet before the birth so you can get to know me better and feel comfortable with my presence."
"I'm not sure if I really need a doula."
"Many families find that having a doula provides invaluable support during the postpartum period. It's an investment in your well-being and can help you navigate the challenges of new parenthood with greater confidence and ease. However, it's a personal decision. Let's talk more about your specific needs and see if my services are a good fit for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and closing the deal with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, but gently inquire about what specifically they need to think about. Offer to provide additional information or answer any remaining questions.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value of your services.
  • Close the call with a clear call to action and make it easy for the client to move forward.
