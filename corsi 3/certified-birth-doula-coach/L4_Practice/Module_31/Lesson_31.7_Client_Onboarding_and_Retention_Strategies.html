Developing a Clear and Concise Contract

A well-drafted contract is the foundation of a professional and ethical doula coaching practice. It protects both you and your client by clearly outlining the services, fees, and policies. This is a crucial part of the 'D' (Develop Systems) phase of The G.U.I.D.E. Frameworkâ„¢, ensuring smooth operations.

  • Scope of Services: Clearly define what services are included in your coaching package.
  • Fees and Payment Schedule: Specify your fees, payment due dates, and accepted payment methods.
  • Cancellation Policy: Outline your policy for cancellations, refunds, and rescheduling.
  • Confidentiality: Assure clients that their information will be kept confidential.

Case Study: Sarah J., First-Time Mother

Client: Sarah J., 32, first-time mother

Presenting Symptoms: Anxiety about labor, lack of confidence in breastfeeding, and feeling overwhelmed by the amount of information available.

Intervention: Six-session doula coaching package including childbirth education, breastfeeding support, and postpartum planning. Regular check-ins via video call and email. Access to a private online community for support.

Outcomes: Sarah reported feeling much more confident and prepared for labor. She successfully breastfed her baby for six months and felt supported throughout her postpartum period. She also provided a glowing testimonial, which was featured on the doula's website.

Case Study: Maria R., VBAC Journey

Client: Maria R., 35, preparing for a VBAC (Vaginal Birth After Cesarean)

Presenting Symptoms: Fear and uncertainty surrounding VBAC, navigating hospital policies, and seeking emotional support.

Intervention: Eight-session doula coaching package focused on VBAC education, advocacy skills, and emotional support. Included birth plan development and relaxation techniques. Regular communication with Maria's healthcare provider.

Outcomes: Maria felt empowered and informed throughout her VBAC journey. She successfully achieved a vaginal birth and reported a positive and healing birth experience. She also became a strong advocate for VBAC and referred several other women to the doula's services.
