Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds like you're carrying a lot right now. Many women in their 50s experience similar challenges, especially those in demanding roles like teaching. Know that you're not alone, and we can definitely work together to find some relief."
MARIA:
"That's reassuring to hear. I feel like I'm failing at everything lately."
YOU:
"Absolutely not. You're showing up and seeking support, which is a sign of strength, not failure. Based on what you've said, I think focusing on regulating your nervous system and finding small pockets of calm throughout your day will be key. How does that sound to you?"
MARIA:
"That sounds wonderful. I just don't know where to start."
YOU:
"We'll take it step by step. I'd like to ask you some questions about your daily routine, focusing on moments of stress and potential opportunities for small shifts. Is that okay with you?"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Minimizing her feelings ("Everyone gets stressed!")
‚úì Validate: "It sounds like you're carrying a heavy load."
‚ùå Immediately suggesting complex solutions (e.g., a rigid meditation schedule)
‚úì Start with small, manageable shifts: "Let's find 5-minute pockets of calm."
‚ùå Focusing solely on symptoms (insomnia) without addressing the root cause (nervous system dysregulation)
‚úì Connect the dots: "Your sleep issues are likely connected to your stress levels."
‚ùå Giving unsolicited advice ("You should quit your job!")
‚úì Empower her to make her own choices: "What feels like the most manageable first step for you?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria says, "Yes, that's exactly what I'm feeling!"
  • She visibly relaxes (shoulders dropping, sighing)
  • She expresses a sense of hope: "I didn't realize it could be this simple."
  • She actively participates in brainstorming solutions.
  • She commits to trying a small action step.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport. It shows you understand their experience and makes them more receptive to your guidance.

2. What's a better approach than giving unsolicited advice?

Empower the client to make their own choices by asking questions like, "What feels like the most manageable first step for you?"

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate the client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Focus on small, manageable action steps.
  • Address the root cause of the issue, not just the symptoms.
  • Empower the client to make their own choices.
  • Practice empathy and active listening.
