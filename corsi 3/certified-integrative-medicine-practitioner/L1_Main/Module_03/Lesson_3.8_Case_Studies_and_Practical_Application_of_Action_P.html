Your Coaching Script

Here's a script to guide you through creating a realistic action plan with Maria. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, based on what you've shared, it sounds like consistency is key. Let's create an action plan that's realistic and sustainable for your busy life. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds great! I'm tired of starting and stopping."
YOU:
"Perfect. We'll focus on small, manageable steps. First, let's identify one or two specific, achievable goals for this week. What feels like a win for you?"
MARIA:
"Okay... maybe prepping my lunch for three days this week instead of buying fast food every day."
YOU:
"Excellent. Prepping lunch three days a week. And what about movement? Is there something small you can commit to?"
MARIA:
"I could probably walk for 20 minutes after school twice this week. I enjoy walking, but I haven't made it a priority."
YOU:
"Wonderful. So, our action plan is: prep lunch three days and walk for 20 minutes twice this week. Let‚Äôs talk about potential obstacles and how you'll overcome them."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to focus on small, achievable goals when creating an action plan?

Small goals increase the likelihood of success, build momentum, and boost client confidence, leading to long-term behavior change.

2. What are some effective strategies for helping a client overcome potential obstacles to their action plan?

Encourage clients to identify potential obstacles in advance, brainstorm solutions, and develop contingency plans. Help them reframe challenges as opportunities for growth.

Key Takeaways

  • Action plans should be realistic, sustainable, and tailored to the client's individual needs and lifestyle.
  • Focus on small, achievable goals to build momentum and boost client confidence.
  • Address potential obstacles proactively and help clients develop strategies to overcome them.
  • Empower clients to take ownership of their health choices and celebrate their successes along the way.
