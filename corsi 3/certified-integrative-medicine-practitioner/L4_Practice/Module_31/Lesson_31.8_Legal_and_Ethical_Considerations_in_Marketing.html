The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Your Proven Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Sarah Mitchell, and I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. To start, what resonated with you on my Instagram page?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! Many women find relief through the approaches I use. So, Susan, what's been going on that made you decide to reach out now?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Problem (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the menopause symptoms you're experiencing. Which ones are the most disruptive to your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage these symptoms? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in six months? And if you could wave a magic wand and eliminate these symptoms, what would your life look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I'm hearing [summarize her key pain points]. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on addressing the root causes of your hormonal imbalances, not just masking the symptoms. I work with women over 12 weeks to create personalized plans that integrate nutrition, lifestyle adjustments, and targeted supplementation. Most clients experience significant relief within the first month."
YOU:
"Unlike conventional medicine, we look at the whole picture - stress, gut health, sleep, and more."
Phase 4: Confident Close (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you feel like this approach aligns with what you're looking for? ... Fantastic! Let me share the program details and how we can get started."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of an effective discovery call, and what is the primary goal of each phase?

The four phases are: 1) Connection & Rapport (build trust), 2) Uncover the Problem (understand the client's challenges), 3) Present Your Solution (show how you can help), and 4) Confident Close (invite them to become a client).

2. What is the MOST important thing to remember when handling client objections?

Empathy is key. Acknowledge their concerns, validate their feelings, and then address the objection with confidence and clarity. Focus on the value you provide.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Understand your ideal client and tailor your script to their needs.
  • Listen more than you talk: Ask open-ended questions and truly hear your prospect's concerns.
  • Focus on value, not price: Highlight the transformation your program offers and the long-term benefits.
  • Practice your closing lines: Confidence is contagious! The more you practice, the more natural you'll sound.
  • Follow up: If they don't sign up immediately, stay in touch and provide additional value.
