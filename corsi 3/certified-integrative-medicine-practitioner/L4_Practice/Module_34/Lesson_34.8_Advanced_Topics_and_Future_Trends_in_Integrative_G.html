Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Intention 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call! I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. To start, what resonated with you about the 'Renew Your Energy' program?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful. Many women find themselves at a similar crossroads. What are you hoping to achieve by joining a group program like this?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about this burnout. What does it feel like in your daily life? What are the biggest energy drains?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried in the past to address these challenges? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If you could design your ideal life, filled with energy and purpose, what would it look like? What activities would you be doing?"
Phase 3: Linking Her Needs to the Program 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking a structured approach to regain your energy, find renewed purpose, and connect with supportive women. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"The 'Renew Your Energy' program is specifically designed to address those needs. Over 8 weeks, we'll explore [mention 2-3 key modules/benefits], all within a supportive group environment. Many participants report feeling a significant shift in their energy levels and overall well-being by week 4."
YOU:
"Does this sound like it could be a good fit for what you're looking for?"
Phase 4: Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me share the details about the program and how you can secure your spot."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have time for a group program."
"I understand. Time is valuable. The program is designed to be manageable, with [mention specific time commitment, e.g., 90-minute weekly calls and 30 minutes of daily exercises]. Many participants find they actually *gain* time and energy by implementing the strategies we teach. Plus, the recordings are available if you miss a live session. How does that sound?"
"I'm not sure if I'm comfortable sharing in a group setting."
"That's perfectly normal. The group environment is carefully cultivated to be safe and supportive. You're welcome to participate at your own comfort level. Many women find that hearing other's experiences is incredibly validating and empowering. You're not alone in this. Would knowing that take some of the pressure off?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically are you considering? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to be sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."
"Is this really going to be different from other things I've tried?"
"That's a valid question, Susan. What makes this program different is our focus on [mention your unique approach, e.g., holistic energy restoration, root cause analysis, personalized action plans]. We don't just address symptoms; we empower you to create lasting change. Plus, the group support adds another layer of accountability and encouragement. Does that provide some clarity?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call for a group program?

Building rapport, understanding client needs, linking program benefits to those needs, confidently presenting the offer, and handling objections effectively.

2. How can you address the common objection of "I don't have time" when selling a group program?

Highlight the program's flexibility, time commitment, and potential time-saving benefits. Emphasize that the program is designed to integrate into their existing lifestyle.

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on understanding your prospect's needs and desires.
  • Clearly articulate the value and benefits of your group program.
  • Practice handling common objections with confidence and empathy.
  • Believe in the transformative power of your program.
