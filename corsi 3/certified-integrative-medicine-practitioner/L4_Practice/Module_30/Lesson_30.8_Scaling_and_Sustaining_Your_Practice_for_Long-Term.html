Handling Objections with Grace

Addressing Common Concerns

"I need to think about it."
"I understand completely. It's a big decision. Is there anything specific you'd like to think about? Perhaps the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I'm happy to provide more information or address any lingering concerns."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I appreciate you bringing that up. While it is an investment, consider this: how much have you already spent on treatments that haven't provided lasting relief? My program is designed to address the root causes of your issues, which can lead to long-term results and potentially reduce your reliance on other interventions. We can also explore payment plan options."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand that time is valuable. My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. The weekly sessions are [duration], and the lifestyle recommendations are gradual and sustainable. Think of it as investing in your long-term well-being, which will ultimately free up more time and energy for the things you love."
"Will this really work for me? I've tried so many things."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. What sets my approach apart is the personalized nature of the program. We'll use functional lab testing to pinpoint your specific imbalances and create a plan tailored to your unique needs. While I can't guarantee specific results, I am confident that my approach can provide significant improvements, and I am committed to supporting you every step of the way. I also offer a free, no-obligation consultation to see if we're a good fit. Would you like to schedule that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Rapport & Connection, Uncover Challenges & Aspirations, Present Your Solution, and Transition to Program Details & Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the objection, "It's more expensive than I expected?"

Acknowledge the concern, reframe the investment in terms of long-term value and potential savings on ineffective treatments, and explore payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting potential clients into paying clients.
  • Building rapport and establishing trust are essential for creating a strong connection with prospects.
  • Identifying the prospect's core challenges and aspirations allows you to tailor your solution to their specific needs.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can overcome resistance and increase conversion rates.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and value proposition ensures that prospects understand the benefits of investing in your services.
