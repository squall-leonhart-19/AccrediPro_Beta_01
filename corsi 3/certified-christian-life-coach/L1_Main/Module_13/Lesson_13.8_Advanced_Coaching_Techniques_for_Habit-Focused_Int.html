Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to start exploring limiting beliefs with Maria. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your vision with me. It sounds like you have a real passion for helping children. I also hear a lot of fear and uncertainty. Can you tell me more about what's holding you back?"
MARIA:
"Well, I keep thinking, 'Who am I to start a business? I'm just a retired teacher. The world has changed so much.'"
YOU:
"That's a powerful statement, Maria. Let's unpack that a bit. If you had to give that thought a name, what would it be?"
MARIA:
"Hmm, maybe 'The Imposter' or 'The Worrier'."
YOU:
"Okay, Maria. So, 'The Worrier' is telling you that you're not qualified. On a scale of 1 to 10, how much do you believe that statement is true?"
MARIA:
"Probably an 8. It feels very real."
YOU:
"I understand. Now, let's gently challenge 'The Worrier'. What evidence do you have that contradicts that belief? What skills and experience do you bring to the table?"

Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

Modify the script to sound like YOU. Keep the structure, but use your own natural language. For example, instead of "Let's unpack that," you might say, "Tell me more about that thought."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria starts to identify her limiting beliefs more easily.
  • She uses phrases like "I realize that's just 'The Worrier' talking."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal when initially exploring a client's limiting beliefs?

The primary goal is to help the client become aware of the limiting belief and its impact, not to immediately eliminate it.

2. Why is it important to avoid dismissing a client's fears during this process?

Dismissing their fears can damage trust and make them feel unheard. Validation is key to creating a safe space for exploration.

Key Takeaways

  • Identifying limiting beliefs is a crucial step in helping clients overcome obstacles.
  • Active listening and empathy are essential for building trust and rapport.
  • Challenge limiting beliefs by exploring the evidence for and against them.
  • Focus on planting seeds of doubt in the limiting belief, rather than trying to eliminate it immediately.
  • Celebrate small victories and progress along the way.
