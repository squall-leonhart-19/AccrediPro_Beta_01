You Belong Here

Let's address something that might be whispering in the back of your mind: "Am I too old for this? Is it too late to start a new career?"

If you're in your 40s, 50s, or even 60s, let me assure you: your life experience is your superpower. You've navigated relationships, career challenges, maybe even raised a family. You've learned, grown, and developed a unique perspective that no textbook can teach. That's invaluable in the coaching world.

A 2021 study published in the International Journal of Aging and Human Development found that individuals over 50 often possess higher levels of emotional intelligence and empathy, crucial qualities for effective coaching (Grossman, et al., 2021). Your wisdom and maturity are not liabilities; they are assets that will resonate deeply with your clients.

And what if you're thinking, "I'm not 'qualified' enough. I haven't overcome enough challenges. Who am I to guide others?"

The truth is, you don't need to be perfect to be a powerful coach. In fact, your imperfections, your vulnerabilities, and your own journey of healing are what will allow you to connect with your clients on a deeper level. People don't want a guru on a pedestal; they want someone who understands their struggles because they've been there too.

Remember my story? I didn't become a coach because I had it all figured out. I became a coach because I knew what it felt like to be broken, lost, and desperate for guidance. And that experience allows me to offer a unique blend of compassion, wisdom, and practical support to my clients.

So, silence the inner critic. You are here for a reason. You have something valuable to offer. And this program will equip you with the skills and knowledge to confidently step into your calling.

Your Cohort & Community

One of the most valuable aspects of this program is the community you'll be joining. You're not alone on this journey. You'll be surrounded by a cohort of like-minded individuals who share your passion for coaching and your commitment to faith.

Research consistently shows that social support plays a crucial role in professional development and success. A 2018 study in the Academy of Management Journal found that individuals with strong social networks experienced greater career advancement and job satisfaction (Burt, 2018).

Here's how you can connect with your cohort and utilize the community:

  • Online Forum: Engage in discussions, ask questions, share insights, and offer support to your fellow students.
  • Group Coaching Calls: Participate in live coaching sessions where you can practice your skills and receive feedback from instructors and peers.
  • Study Groups: Form study groups to review course materials, discuss challenging concepts, and hold each other accountable.
  • Networking Events: Attend virtual networking events to connect with other coaches, build relationships, and explore potential collaborations.

Don't underestimate the power of this community. These are the people who will celebrate your successes, offer encouragement during challenges, and provide invaluable support as you build your coaching career. Lean on them, learn from them, and build lasting connections.
