Advanced Values Clarification Techniques

While basic values assessments can be helpful, truly understanding a client's values requires deeper exploration. Here are some advanced techniques to incorporate into your coaching sessions:

  • The Values Card Sort: Present clients with a deck of cards, each listing a different value (e.g., faith, family, financial security, creativity). Have them sort the cards into categories such as "most important," "important," and "not important." Then, delve into the why behind their choices.
  • The "Ideal Day" Visualization: Ask clients to vividly imagine their ideal day, from morning to night. What activities are they engaged in? Who are they spending time with? What feelings are they experiencing? Their responses will reveal what they truly value.
  • The "Eulogy Exercise": Have clients write their own eulogy, focusing on how they want to be remembered. This can provide profound insights into their deepest values and aspirations.
  • Values-Based Questionnaire: Administer a comprehensive questionnaire designed to uncover deeply held beliefs and values.

These techniques can help your clients move beyond surface-level answers and identify the values that truly drive their behavior.

Differentiating Worldly and Kingdom Values

It's essential to help clients distinguish between worldly values, which are often focused on material possessions, status, and personal gain, and Kingdom values, which are rooted in biblical principles and focused on serving God and others. Consider this comparison:

Worldly Values Kingdom Values
Success defined by wealth and achievement Success defined by faithfulness and obedience to God
Seeking personal recognition and praise Seeking to glorify God in all things
Focus on individual desires and ambitions Focus on serving others and meeting their needs
Pursuit of pleasure and self-gratification Pursuit of righteousness and spiritual growth
Material possessions as a measure of worth Character and integrity as a measure of worth

Helping clients understand this distinction is crucial for guiding them toward a life that is aligned with God's will.

Identifying Values Conflicts

Values conflicts arise when a person holds two or more values that are in opposition to each other. For example, a client may value both financial security and risk-taking, which can create inner turmoil when making career decisions. A 2018 study in the Journal of Applied Psychology found that values conflicts are associated with increased stress, decreased job satisfaction, and higher rates of burnout. (Johnson et al., 2018). It's essential to help clients identify these conflicts and develop strategies for resolving them.

Here are some questions to help clients uncover values conflicts:

  • "What are some areas in your life where you feel torn or conflicted?"
  • "What are some decisions you've made that you later regretted? What values were at odds in those situations?"
  • "Are there any areas where your actions don't align with your stated values?"

By exploring these questions, clients can gain a deeper understanding of their values conflicts and their impact on their lives.

Aligning Values with Biblical Principles

Once clients have identified their core values, the next step is to help them align those values with biblical principles. This involves examining each value through the lens of Scripture and asking questions such as:

  • "How does this value reflect God's character and His purposes for humanity?"
  • "What does the Bible say about this value?"
  • "How can I live out this value in a way that honors God and blesses others?"

For example, if a client values success, you can explore biblical passages about hard work, diligence, and stewardship. If a client values relationships, you can examine passages about love, forgiveness, and reconciliation. By grounding their values in biblical truth, clients can develop a stronger sense of purpose and direction.

Practical Exercise: Values-Based Decision Making

To solidify the concepts discussed in this lesson, guide your clients through a practical exercise in values-based decision making. Here's one approach:

  1. Identify a current decision: Ask the client to identify a decision they are currently facing.
  2. List relevant values: Have them list the values that are most relevant to this decision.
  3. Evaluate options: For each possible course of action, have them evaluate how well it aligns with each of their values.
  4. Consider biblical principles: Ask them to consider how each option aligns with biblical principles.
  5. Make a decision: Based on this evaluation, help them make a decision that is consistent with their values and biblical teachings.

This exercise will help clients develop the habit of making decisions that are aligned with their values and God's will.

Case Study: Sarah's Career Crossroads

Client: Sarah, 35, a marketing professional.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt unfulfilled in her current job and was considering a career change. She was torn between pursuing a higher-paying position in a corporate setting and starting her own business that aligned with her passion for sustainable living.

Intervention: Through values clarification exercises, Sarah identified her core values as financial security, creativity, and making a positive impact on the environment. We explored the tension between her desire for financial stability and her passion for sustainability. We then examined biblical principles related to stewardship and using our gifts to serve others.

Outcomes: Sarah realized that while financial security was important, it wasn't her ultimate priority. She decided to start her own business, creating eco-friendly products. While it initially involved taking a financial risk, she found deep satisfaction in aligning her work with her values and making a positive impact. Within two years, her business became profitable, exceeding her previous corporate salary.
