Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation to inspire Karen. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Karen, it sounds like you're at a crossroads, and that can feel overwhelming. But I want you to know that feeling 'stuck' is often a sign that something new is trying to emerge. What if this feeling is actually an invitation to rediscover your passions?"
KAREN:
"I don't know... I've been a teacher for so long. It's all I know."
YOU:
"I understand. And 28 years is a significant commitment. But your skills as a teacher ‚Äì your patience, your creativity, your ability to connect with others ‚Äì those are incredibly valuable and transferable to many other fields. We can explore how to leverage those strengths."
YOU:
"Imagine, for a moment, that anything is possible. If you could wave a magic wand and design your ideal work life, what would it look like? Don't worry about the 'how' right now, just focus on the 'what'."
KAREN:
"Well... I've always loved gardening. And I've thought about starting a little flower shop."
YOU:
"A flower shop! That's wonderful. What is it about that idea that excites you?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Karen starts to smile and talk more enthusiastically about her dreams.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's fears before inspiring them to take action?

Validating fears builds trust and shows empathy. It acknowledges the client's current reality before inviting them to envision a new one.

2. What is the key difference between telling a client what to do and inspiring them to discover their own path?

Telling a client what to do disempowers them and creates dependence. Inspiring them empowers them to tap into their own inner wisdom and resourcefulness.

Key Takeaways

  • Inspiring action starts with validating the client's current feelings and fears.
  • Focus on highlighting the client's strengths and transferable skills.
  • Ask powerful questions to help the client connect with their passions and vision.
  • Encourage the client to envision a positive future and explore possibilities.
  • Remember that inspiration is about empowering the client to discover their own path, not telling them what to do.
