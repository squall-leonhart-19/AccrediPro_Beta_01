The Limitations of SMART Goals

While SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) are widely used, they often fall short in the realm of spiritual transformation. They can be too focused on earthly achievements and may not adequately address the deeper, faith-based aspirations of our clients. A 2019 study in the Journal of Psychology and Theology found that individuals who incorporated spiritual values into their goal-setting experienced greater life satisfaction (Smith et al., 2019). We need a framework that integrates faith and purpose.

Introducing 'GOD' Goals

Let's introduce 'GOD' Goals, a framework designed specifically for Christian Life Coaching. 'GOD' Goals are:

  • God-Honoring: Aligned with biblical principles and God's will.
  • Outcome-Focused: Clearly defined desired results that reflect spiritual growth.
  • Divinely-Inspired: Goals that come from prayerful reflection and seeking God's guidance.
  • Time-Bound: Set within a reasonable timeframe to encourage accountability and progress.

This framework ensures that goals are not just about personal achievement but about aligning with God's purpose and growing in faith. This is a significant departure from secular coaching methods. The 'GOD' Goals align perfectly with the "Inspire" and "Transformation" components of The F.A.I.T.H. Frameworkâ„¢.

Helping Clients Discern God's Will

A key aspect of setting 'GOD' Goals is helping clients discern God's will for their lives. Here are some techniques:

  • Prayerful Reflection: Encourage clients to spend time in prayer, seeking God's guidance and clarity.
  • Scripture Study: Explore relevant Bible verses that speak to their aspirations and challenges.
  • Journaling: Writing down thoughts, feelings, and insights can reveal patterns and divine nudges.
  • Seeking Wise Counsel: Talking with trusted mentors or spiritual advisors can provide valuable perspective.

Remember, this process is about surrendering to God's plan, not dictating our own. It aligns with the "Assessment" and "Inspire" phases of the F.A.I.T.H. Frameworkâ„¢.

Crafting Transformation Goals Rooted in Biblical Values

Transformation goals should reflect Christian virtues like love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control (Galatians 5:22-23). Ensure that goals are not just about personal gain but about embodying these values in daily life. For example, a goal could be to "demonstrate patience in challenging situations by responding with grace and understanding," rather than simply "avoiding conflict."

Consider the following table, which provides examples of how to transform common goals into 'GOD' Goals rooted in biblical values:

Common Goal Biblical Value 'GOD' Goal
Increase income Stewardship Increase income by 15% in the next year to support family needs and tithe generously to the church.
Improve health Taking care of your body as a temple Exercise for 30 minutes, 5 times a week, to honor God by taking care of the body He has given me.
Strengthen relationships Love and forgiveness Practice active listening and offer forgiveness in disagreements with family members, reflecting Christ's love.
Advance in career Service to others Seek opportunities to use my professional skills to serve others in my workplace and community, glorifying God through my work.

Creating a Christian Vision Board

A vision board is a powerful tool for visualizing goals. From a Christian perspective, it becomes a way to visualize God's plan for your life. Encourage clients to include:

  • Images that represent their faith-based aspirations.
  • Bible verses that inspire and guide them.
  • Words and affirmations that reflect their identity in Christ.
  • Symbols that represent their values and purpose.

Regularly reviewing the vision board can reinforce their commitment to 'GOD' Goals and keep them focused on God's vision for their lives. This aligns with the "Honor" phase of The F.A.I.T.H. Frameworkâ„¢.

Case Study: Sarah's Transformation

Client: Sarah, 35, struggling with career dissatisfaction and a sense of spiritual emptiness.

Presenting Symptoms: Anxiety, lack of motivation, questioning her purpose.

Intervention: Guided Sarah through the 'GOD' Goals framework, focusing on discerning God's will for her career. She spent time in prayer, scripture study, and journaling. We identified her passion for helping others and her desire to use her skills for a greater purpose.

Outcomes: Sarah discovered a calling to work in a non-profit organization serving underprivileged communities. She set a 'GOD' Goal to "secure a position in a non-profit organization within six months, using my skills to serve others and glorify God." She experienced renewed purpose, reduced anxiety, and a deeper connection with her faith. She successfully obtained a job at a local charity within four months.

Recognizing the Income Potential for Christian Life Coaches

While the primary focus is on serving God and helping others, it's important to acknowledge the income potential of Christian Life Coaching. Coaches can generate income through individual coaching sessions, group coaching programs, workshops, and online courses. The average hourly rate for life coaches ranges from $75 to $200+, depending on experience and specialization (ICF Global Coaching Study, 2020). By building a thriving practice, coaches can support themselves and their families while fulfilling their calling.
