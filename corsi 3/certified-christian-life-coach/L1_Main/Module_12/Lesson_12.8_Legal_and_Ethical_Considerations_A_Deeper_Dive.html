Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to establish before suggesting spiritual disciplines to a client?

Establish trust and empathy. The client needs to feel safe and understood before being open to suggestions.

2. Give an example of how to phrase a question that connects a spiritual discipline to a client's specific goal.

Instead of "Will you pray more?", ask "How will spending 10 minutes in prayer each day help you feel more connected to God's presence?"

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial for building trust with clients who feel spiritually disconnected.
  • Offer a variety of spiritual disciplines and allow the client to choose what resonates with them.
  • Help clients set realistic goals and create actionable plans for incorporating disciplines into their daily lives.
  • Focus on God's grace and new beginnings rather than dwelling on past failures or feelings of guilt.
  • Regularly check in with clients to track their progress and provide ongoing support and encouragement.
