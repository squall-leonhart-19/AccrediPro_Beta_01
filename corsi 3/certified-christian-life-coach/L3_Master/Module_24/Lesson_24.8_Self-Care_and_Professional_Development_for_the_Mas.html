Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reinforce the Importance of Client-Centered Approach

Emphasize that faith-based interventions should always be client-led and aligned with their beliefs. Client autonomy is paramount.

2

Review Session Structuring Techniques

Introduce frameworks like the GROW model (Goal, Reality, Options, Way forward) to provide structure and focus. This helps avoid rambling and ensures each session has a clear objective.

3

Clarify Scope of Practice

Discuss the boundaries between coaching, counseling, and spiritual direction. Highlight the importance of referring clients to appropriate professionals when necessary.

4

Address Pricing Confidence

Share examples of successful Christian Life Coaches, like Maria Rodriguez, a 48-year-old former nurse who now earns $75,000/year coaching Christian women. Help Sarah identify her unique value proposition and set appropriate rates.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to clarify the scope of practice with a new coach?

To ensure they understand the boundaries between coaching, counseling, and other helping professions, and to protect both the coach and the client.

2. What is the GROW model, and how can it help a new coach?

The GROW model (Goal, Reality, Options, Way forward) is a structured coaching framework that helps coaches guide clients towards achieving their goals in a focused and effective manner. It provides structure and clarity to the coaching process.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new coaches, not direct client work.
  • Client-centered approach is paramount, especially in faith-based coaching.
  • Structured frameworks like the GROW model enhance session effectiveness.
  • Clarifying scope of practice protects both coach and client.
  • Constructive feedback fosters growth and confidence in new practitioners.
