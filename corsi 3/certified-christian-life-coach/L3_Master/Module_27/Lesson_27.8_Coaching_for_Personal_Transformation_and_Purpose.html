The Challenging Case

Sarah's Client: Emily, 48

Background Sarah Shares:

"Emily is a 48-year-old woman struggling with burnout and a crisis of faith. She feels overwhelmed by her responsibilities as a mother and wife. She wants to find a deeper connection with God but doesn't know where to start. We've had three sessions, but she keeps repeating the same issues."

Areas to Explore with Sarah:

  • Has Sarah explored Emily's values and how they align with her current life?
  • Is Sarah using specific tools to help Emily identify actionable steps?
  • Are there boundary issues preventing progress (e.g., excessive communication outside sessions)?
  • Is Sarah appropriately addressing the faith-based aspects without imposing her own beliefs?

Guiding Principles for Sarah

Key Coaching Points to Emphasize

1

Focus on Values Alignment

Encourage Sarah to use values-based exercises. Values clarification helps clients make decisions that align with their core beliefs.

2

Teach the Power of Small Wins

Help Sarah guide Emily to identify small, achievable goals that build momentum and confidence. Emphasize that even small steps can lead to significant transformation.

3

Address Boundary Setting

Guide Sarah to establish clear boundaries with Emily regarding communication and availability. This protects both the coach and the client.

4

Reinforce Ethical Considerations

Remind Sarah to remain client-centered and avoid imposing her own religious beliefs. The goal is to help Emily discover her own path to spiritual fulfillment.

Your Feedback Strategy

Constructive Feedback Framework

Acknowledge Effort
"Sarah, I commend you for seeking supervision. It shows your commitment to providing excellent coaching. Let's explore this together."
Inquire About Her Approach
"Before I offer suggestions, can you walk me through the specific tools and techniques you've used with Emily so far? What results did you observe?"
Introduce New Perspectives
"Consider incorporating a values assessment to help Emily identify her core values. This can provide clarity and direction as she navigates her burnout and faith crisis."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on our discussion, what adjustments would you like to make in your next session with Emily? I'm here to support you as you implement these changes."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of addressing boundary issues in supervision?

Addressing boundary issues is crucial for protecting both the coach and the client. Clear boundaries ensure the coach's well-being and maintain a professional coaching relationship.

2. How can a supervisor help a new coach integrate faith-based principles ethically?

Supervisors can emphasize the importance of client-centered coaching, where the client's beliefs and values are respected. The coach facilitates the client's spiritual journey without imposing their own religious views.
