The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 52, who is feeling lost after her children left home. She keeps asking me what the Bible says about finding purpose in midlife. I want to help, but I feel like I'm giving her sermons instead of coaching."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Sarah clear on the difference between coaching and counseling?
  • Has she established clear boundaries with Emily regarding theological advice?
  • Is she equipped to redirect the conversation back to Emily's goals?
  • Does Sarah understand how to use scripture as a supportive, not prescriptive, tool?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Clarify Coaching vs. Counseling

Emphasize that coaching focuses on empowering clients to find their own answers, while counseling often provides direct advice and guidance. Christian Life Coaching integrates faith, but remains client-led.

2

Establish Clear Boundaries

Teach Sarah how to respectfully redirect Emily back to her goals. For example, "Emily, that's a great question, and it's important to explore your beliefs. How does this relate to your goal of finding purpose in this new chapter?"

3

Use Scripture as Support, Not Prescription

Help Sarah understand how to use scripture to inspire reflection rather than provide direct answers. Ask: "What scriptures resonate with you as you consider your future?"

4

Role-Play Difficult Conversations

Practice scenarios where Emily asks for theological advice, and Sarah gently redirects the conversation back to Emily's goals and action steps.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about Emily and her spiritual journey. It's wonderful that you want to integrate your faith into your coaching practice."
Ask Before Telling
"What do you think is the underlying need Emily is expressing when she asks for theological advice? What's she really searching for?"
Teach the Principle
"Remember, our role as coaches is to empower clients to discover their own wisdom, guided by the Holy Spirit. We're facilitators, not preachers. We hold space for them to connect with God's leading."
Empower Next Steps
"How would you like to approach your next session with Emily? What specific strategies will you use to keep the focus on her goals while honoring her spiritual needs?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between Christian Life Coaching and Christian Counseling?

Christian Life Coaching empowers clients to discover their own wisdom and action steps, while Christian Counseling often provides direct advice and guidance.

Teach them to respectfully redirect the conversation back to the client's goals and action steps, and to use scripture as a tool for reflection rather than prescription.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision and mentoring are essential for developing confident and competent Christian Life Coaches.
  • Clarify the distinction between coaching and counseling to ensure mentees understand their role.
  • Teach mentees how to establish clear boundaries and redirect conversations effectively.
  • Use scripture as a supportive tool for reflection, not a prescriptive guide.
  • Provide constructive feedback that affirms, encourages, and empowers mentees to grow.
