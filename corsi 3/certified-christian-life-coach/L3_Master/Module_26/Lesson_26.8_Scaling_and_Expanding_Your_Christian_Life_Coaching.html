Her Question
"I'm creating my first coaching program, but I don't know where to start. It feels like too much!"

The Challenge She Presents

Sarah's Program Design Dilemma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I want to create a comprehensive program on 'Finding Your Purpose.' I've outlined 12 modules, each with multiple sub-sections, covering everything from values clarification to career planning to spiritual gifts. I feel like I *have* to include it all. But it's overwhelming me, and I'm sure it will overwhelm my clients!"

What You Need to Explore:

  • What are her core program objectives? Are they clearly defined?
  • Is she trying to solve too many problems at once?
  • How is she incorporating the FAITH methodology?
  • What's the ideal client transformation, and how can she streamline the process?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

The Power of "Less"

Explain that a focused, streamlined program delivers better results than a sprawling, unfocused one. Less is often more.

2

Clarify Core Objectives

Help her define 2-3 *core* objectives for the program. What specific transformation does she want to create for her clients?

3

Apply the FAITH Framework

Guide her to structure the program using the FAITH methodology. How does each module align with a specific phase of the framework?

4

Identify "Must-Have" vs. "Nice-to-Have" Content

Help her differentiate between essential content and supplementary material. What can be removed without compromising the core transformation?

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Enthusiasm
"Sarah, I can see your passion for this topic! It's wonderful that you want to offer so much value. Let's channel that enthusiasm into a program that's both impactful and manageable."
Question Assumptions
"What makes you feel like you *have* to include all 12 modules? What's the underlying belief there? Is it possible that you're putting too much pressure on yourself?"
Teach Program Design Principles
"In program design, it's crucial to prioritize depth over breadth. A focused program with clear objectives will always be more effective than a scattered one. Think of it like a laser beam vs. a floodlight."
Offer Practical Steps
"Let's revisit your program objectives. If you could only achieve *one* key transformation for your clients, what would it be? Once we clarify that, we can build the program around it."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important principle to emphasize when a new coach is overwhelmed by program design?

Focus on simplification and prioritizing core objectives. Less is often more effective than trying to include everything.

2. How can you help a new coach apply the FAITH methodology to their program design?

Guide them to structure each module around a specific phase of the FAITH framework (Foundation, Assessment, Inspire, Transformation, Habits).

Key Takeaways

  • A streamlined, focused program delivers better results than a sprawling, unfocused one.
  • Help new coaches clarify their core program objectives and align content accordingly.
  • Emphasize the importance of prioritizing depth over breadth in program design.
  • Guide them to apply the FAITH methodology to structure their programs effectively.
  • Your mentorship is invaluable ‚Äì you're not just sharing knowledge, you're developing future leaders.
