Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Scope of Practice

Remind Sarah that her role is not to provide trauma therapy. Coaching focuses on present and future goals, not resolving past trauma.

2

Teach Safe Referral Practices

Discuss how to gently suggest a referral to a licensed therapist or counselor who specializes in trauma. Provide Sarah with local resources.

3

Explain the Importance of Grounding Techniques

Teach Sarah simple grounding techniques (e.g., 5-4-3-2-1 method) to help Emily manage any potential emotional distress during sessions.

4

Discuss Self-Care for the Coach

Acknowledge that hearing about trauma can be emotionally taxing. Encourage Sarah to practice self-care and seek support from her own supervisor or mentor.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary ethical consideration when a coaching client discloses past trauma?

Maintaining appropriate scope of practice and avoiding interventions that could be harmful.

2. What are some key elements of a safe and supportive referral to a trauma therapist?

Expressing concern for the client's well-being, explaining the limits of coaching in addressing trauma, and providing specific referrals to qualified professionals.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is crucial for new coaches, especially when dealing with complex client presentations.
  • Maintaining scope of practice is paramount; coaches are not trauma therapists.
  • Referral to a qualified mental health professional is often the most ethical course of action.
  • Providing education and support helps new coaches build confidence and competence.
