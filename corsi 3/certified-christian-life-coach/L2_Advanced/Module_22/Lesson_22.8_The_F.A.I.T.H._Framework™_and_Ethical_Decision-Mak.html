Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Considerations in Eleanor's Case

Step 1: Identify Ethical Dilemmas
Several ethical dilemmas emerge:
  • Autonomy: Balancing Eleanor's autonomy with her husband's skepticism.
  • Beneficence and Non-Maleficence: Ensuring interventions benefit Eleanor without causing harm, considering her medical history and medications.
  • Justice: Providing fair and equitable access to care, considering her financial situation.
  • Scope of Practice: Staying within your coaching boundaries while addressing interconnected health concerns.
Step 2: Apply Ethical Frameworks
Consider applying the following ethical frameworks:
  • Virtue Ethics: Act with integrity, compassion, and prudence.
  • Deontology: Adhere to professional codes of conduct and standards.
  • Utilitarianism: Aim for the greatest good for Eleanor while considering the impact on her family.
Step 3: Navigate Informed Consent
Ensure Eleanor fully understands the proposed interventions, potential risks and benefits, and alternatives. Obtain her explicit consent, documenting the process thoroughly. Acknowledge her husband's skepticism while respecting Eleanor's autonomy.
Step 4: Manage Confidentiality
Establish clear boundaries regarding confidentiality. Eleanor's health information should not be shared with her husband or children without her explicit consent. Discuss potential implications of family dynamics on confidentiality.

Case Study: Sarah, 52

Sarah, a 52-year-old former teacher, sought coaching for weight management and increased energy. During the initial assessment, she disclosed a history of childhood trauma and ongoing struggles with anxiety. Sarah also mentioned that her husband was recently diagnosed with early-stage Alzheimer's disease, placing significant stress on their relationship.

The ethical dilemma arose when Sarah began to rely heavily on coaching sessions for emotional support, blurring the boundaries between coaching and therapy. Her husband's declining health created additional challenges, as Sarah often expressed feelings of guilt and resentment.

To address these ethical concerns, the coach took the following steps: First, she clarified the scope of coaching and gently redirected Sarah towards appropriate mental health resources. Second, she emphasized the importance of self-care and encouraged Sarah to prioritize her own well-being. Third, she facilitated a conversation with Sarah about her husband's condition and offered resources for caregiver support. Finally, she documented all ethical considerations and actions taken, ensuring transparency and accountability.

By navigating these ethical complexities with compassion and professionalism, the coach helped Sarah regain her focus on weight management and energy levels while also addressing her underlying emotional needs. This case highlights the importance of ethical awareness and responsible decision-making in complex coaching scenarios.

Check Your Understanding

1. What steps should you take when a client's needs appear to exceed your scope of practice?

First, acknowledge the limitations of your role as a coach. Then, discuss the client's needs openly and recommend appropriate referrals to licensed professionals, such as therapists or medical doctors. Ensure the client understands the distinction between coaching and therapy, and document all recommendations and referrals made.

2. How can you address conflicts of interest in coaching relationships?

Identify potential conflicts of interest upfront, such as dual relationships or personal biases. Disclose these conflicts to the client and discuss potential implications. If the conflict cannot be resolved, consider terminating the coaching relationship or seeking supervision from a qualified professional. Always prioritize the client's best interests and maintain transparency in all interactions.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical decision-making is essential for responsible coaching practice.
  • Complex cases require careful consideration of autonomy, beneficence, and justice.
  • Informed consent and confidentiality are paramount in coaching relationships.
  • Referrals to other professionals are necessary when client needs exceed your scope of practice.
  • Documenting ethical considerations and actions taken ensures transparency and accountability.
