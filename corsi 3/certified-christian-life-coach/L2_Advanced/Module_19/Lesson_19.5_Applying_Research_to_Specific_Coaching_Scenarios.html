Case Study: Anxiety Management

Case: Sarah, a 32-year-old experiencing anxiety

Client: Sarah, 32 years old, Marketing Manager

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reports feeling overwhelmed, experiencing frequent panic attacks, and struggling with insomnia. She feels disconnected from her faith and questions her ability to manage her responsibilities.

Intervention:

  2. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Techniques: Implement CBT techniques, focusing on identifying and challenging negative thought patterns.
  3. Mindfulness and Prayer: Introduce mindfulness exercises combined with Christian prayer to help Sarah ground herself in moments of anxiety.
  4. Biblical Reframing: Help Sarah reframe her challenges through a biblical lens, focusing on themes of trust and reliance on God.
  5. The F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢: Apply the F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢ to identify and strengthen Sarah's foundational beliefs, address inner transformation, integrate actionable steps, tailor holistic strategies, and foster hope.

She has developed coping strategies to manage panic attacks and feels more connected to her faith. Sarah credits the biblical reframing and mindfulness exercises for her improved mental and spiritual well-being.

Ethical Considerations in Applying Research

When applying research in coaching, it is crucial to consider ethical implications. Ensure that interventions are evidence-based, culturally sensitive, and aligned with the client‚Äôs values. Obtain informed consent, maintain confidentiality, and continuously evaluate the effectiveness of your coaching approach. Be transparent about the limitations of research and avoid making unsubstantiated claims.
