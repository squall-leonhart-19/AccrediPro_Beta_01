Sarah, 42

Struggling with work-life balance and feeling overwhelmed. Found you on LinkedIn.

Her Situation
Working mom, feels guilty about not spending enough time with her kids, career plateau.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure I can afford this right now, but I'm desperate for a change."
Decision Style
Analytical, needs to see a clear process and ROI. Values practical solutions and accountability.
Her Goal
"I want to feel more in control of my life and enjoy my family without sacrificing my career."

Your Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Grace, and I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. To start, I'd love to hear how you found me on LinkedIn."
YOU:
"That's great! I'm glad you connected with my content on work-life balance. What resonated with you the most? What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
Phase 2: Understanding the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about feeling overwhelmed. What does a typical day look like for you? Where do you feel the most pressure?"
YOU:
"And how long have you been feeling this way? What have you already tried to improve the situation?"
YOU:
"If you continue on this path, what do you see happening in the next 6-12 months? What's at stake for you?"
YOU:
"Now, imagine you've achieved the perfect work-life balance. What would that look like? How would you feel each day?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're struggling with [summarize her pain points: overwhelm, guilt, lack of control]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My coaching program is designed to help you create a personalized plan to achieve that work-life balance you described. Over 12 weeks, we'll identify your core values, set realistic goals, implement time-management strategies, and build habits that support your vision. My clients typically see a significant improvement in their stress levels and overall happiness within the first month."
YOU:
"We'll use a combination of weekly one-on-one sessions, personalized action plans, and ongoing support to keep you accountable and on track. We will also explore how your Christian faith can be a source of strength and guidance."
Phase 4: Closing the Call 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what we've discussed, does this sound like the support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections with Grace and Confidence

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients feel the same way initially. However, consider this: What if investing in this program actually saves you time by helping you prioritize effectively and eliminate time-wasting activities? We can tailor the program to fit your schedule."
"I need to think about it and discuss it with my spouse."
"Of course. I can send you a program overview to share with your spouse. Would it be helpful to schedule a brief call together where I can answer any questions they might have? I find that often leads to a more informed decision."
"It's more expensive than I anticipated."
"I appreciate you being upfront about that. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. My clients report increased productivity at work (leading to potential promotions), reduced stress (improving health and reducing medical costs), and deeper connections with their families. What are those benefits worth to you in the long run? We also have flexible payment options available."
"I've tried coaching before, and it didn't work."
"I'm sorry to hear that. What was your experience like? What didn't work for you? My approach is different because [explain your unique methodology and focus on faith-based principles]. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you're not seeing results after the first month, we can discuss a refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a 30-minute discovery call?

The four phases are: (1) Rapport & Connection, (2) Understanding the Problem, (3) Presenting Your Solution, and (4) Closing the Call.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "It's more expensive than I anticipated"?

Acknowledge their concern, then focus on the value they'll receive, the potential ROI (increased productivity, reduced stress), and any available payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is Key: Thoroughly understand your prospect's needs and challenges before the call.
  • Listen Actively: Pay close attention to what your prospect is saying (and not saying) to identify their core issues.
  • Offer a Clear Solution: Clearly articulate how your coaching program can address your prospect's specific needs.
  • Handle Objections with Confidence: Be prepared to address common objections with empathy and compelling responses.
  • Practice Your Close: Confidently ask for the sale and make it easy for your prospect to say "yes."
