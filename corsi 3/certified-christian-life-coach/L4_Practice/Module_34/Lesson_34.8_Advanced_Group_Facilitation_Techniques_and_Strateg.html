Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Reconnect & Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! It's so great to connect with you again. How did you enjoy the 'Renew Your Purpose After 50' workshop?"
YOU:
"I loved your insightful comments during the workshop! What resonated most with you?"
Phase 2: Deepen Understanding of Her Needs 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling a bit lost as an empty nester. Can you tell me more about what that feels like for you?"
YOU:
"What are some of the passions you used to enjoy before life got so busy?"
YOU:
"Imagine yourself six months from now, having gone through this group program. What's different? How do you feel?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program & Benefits 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe our 'Renew Your Purpose' group program can be transformative for you. It's a 10-week journey where we dive deep into rediscovering your passions, building confidence, and creating a roadmap for your next chapter."
YOU:
"What sets this program apart is the incredible community of women who support each other every step of the way. You'll gain valuable tools, personalized guidance, and lasting friendships."
YOU:
"We meet weekly via Zoom, and you'll also have access to our private online forum for ongoing support and connection. Plus, I include one 30-minute 1:1 session to personalize your path."
Phase 4: Close & Invite to Enroll 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support and guidance you've been looking for? ... Fantastic! Let me share the investment details and how you can secure your spot in the group."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Time is precious. Let's look at it this way: what's the cost of *not* investing this time in yourself? Where will you be in six months if you continue feeling lost and unfulfilled? This program is designed to help you reclaim your time and energy by focusing on what truly matters to you."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial commitment, the program content, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make a confident decision. I also have a few testimonials I can share from past participants."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate you being upfront. Can I ask, what value do you place on rediscovering your passions, feeling energized, and connecting with a supportive community? The program is an investment in *you* and your future happiness. I also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure if group coaching is right for me."
"That's a valid concern. Group coaching offers a unique dynamic where you learn from others' experiences, gain diverse perspectives, and build lasting connections. It's not just about me teaching; it's about the collective wisdom and support within the group. Plus, I include a 1:1 session with me to personalize your path."

Check Your Understanding

What are the four phases of a 30-minute discovery call, and how much time should be allocated to each?
The four phases are: 1) Reconnect & Build Rapport (5 min), 2) Deepen Understanding of Her Needs (10 min), 3) Present the Group Program & Benefits (10 min), and 4) Close & Invite to Enroll (5 min).
Describe three common objections you might encounter during a discovery call and how you would respond to each.
Common objections include: "I'm not sure I have the time," "I need to think about it," and "It's too expensive." Responses should address the underlying concern, highlight the value of the program, and offer solutions like payment plans or testimonials.
Why is it important to practice presenting your pricing with confidence?
Presenting your pricing with confidence demonstrates your belief in the value of your program and helps potential clients feel more comfortable investing in themselves. It also prevents you from underselling your services.

Key Takeaways

  • Prepare a detailed script for your discovery calls to ensure you cover all key points and stay on track.
  • Practice active listening and ask open-ended questions to understand your potential client's needs and motivations.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses to address them effectively.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and clearly articulate the value of your program.
  • Follow up with potential clients after the call to answer any remaining questions and encourage enrollment.
