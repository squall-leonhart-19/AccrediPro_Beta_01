Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy women. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find times that work for you, and the results ‚Äì that renewed sense of purpose ‚Äì will actually give you MORE energy and time in the long run."
"I need to talk to my husband about it."
"That's perfectly fine. What are some of the questions or concerns you think he might have? Maybe I can address them now so you have the information you need when you talk to him. Or, I'm happy to hop on a quick call with both of you to answer any questions."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Let's talk about the value you're getting. Imagine a year from now, still feeling lost and unfulfilled. What's the cost of staying where you are? Now, imagine a year from now, feeling energized, confident, and living a life you love. What's THAT worth to you?"

"I understand your hesitation. Can you tell me what specifically makes you feel unsure? I have several testimonials from women in similar situations who have had great success. Perhaps hearing their stories would help you feel more confident."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and a confident close.

2. How can you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Reframe the cost by focusing on the value the client will receive and the cost of *not* solving their problem.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for client acquisition.
  • Confidence in your pricing and program is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Handling objections effectively can overcome potential barriers to enrollment.
  • Your income potential as a coach is directly linked to your client acquisition efforts.
  • Remember to listen more than you speak, and always tailor your approach to the individual client.
