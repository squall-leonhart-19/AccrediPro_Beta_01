Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call, and what is the purpose of each?

The four phases are: 1) Rapport Building (creating connection), 2) Needs Assessment (identifying client's pain points and desires), 3) Solution Presentation (showcasing your program as the answer), and 4) Closing & Next Steps (inviting them to join your program).

2. Describe three effective ways to handle common objections during a discovery call.

1) Acknowledge the objection and empathize with the client's concerns. 2) Reframe the objection by highlighting the value and benefits of your program. 3) Ask clarifying questions to understand the root cause of the objection and address it directly.

3. How can you present your pricing with confidence and address potential concerns about the investment?

State your price clearly and confidently, highlighting the value and benefits of your program. Break down the investment into smaller components and emphasize the potential return on investment (ROI). Share success stories and testimonials to demonstrate the transformative results clients have achieved.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls into four distinct phases: Rapport, Needs, Solution, and Close.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, value-driven responses.
  • Present your pricing with assurance, emphasizing the return on investment.
  • Believe in your calling and the transformative power of your coaching program.
  • Practice, refine, and personalize these scripts to align with your unique style and target audience.
