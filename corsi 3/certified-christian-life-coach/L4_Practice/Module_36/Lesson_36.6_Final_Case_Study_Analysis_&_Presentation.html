Case Study Presentations

Each of you will present a detailed case study, illustrating how you utilized the F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢ to guide a client toward transformation. Your presentation should cover the following key areas:

  • Foundation: How you established trust and explored the client's values and beliefs.
  • Assessment: The methods you used to assess the client's spiritual, emotional, and practical needs.
  • Inspire: How you instilled hope and motivation, drawing upon Scripture and the client's personal story.
  • Transformation: The specific strategies you employed to facilitate spiritual growth and positive change.
  • Habits: How you helped the client establish new, faith-based habits to sustain their transformation.

Aim for a presentation that is both informative and engaging. Use visuals, storytelling, and personal anecdotes to bring your case study to life. Remember, this is not just about demonstrating your knowledge; it's about showcasing your passion for Christian Life Coaching and your ability to make a real difference in people's lives.

Many Christian Life Coaches find that specializing in a particular niche helps them attract clients and build a successful practice. For example, a coach who specializes in helping women over 40 transition into new careers can easily earn $75,000 - $150,000 per year by charging $150 - $300 per session and seeing just 10-15 clients per week. This level of income can be achieved with flexible hours and the satisfaction of doing meaningful work.
