Aisha, New L1 Graduate

Former marketing professional, passionate about helping women overcome challenges.

Excellent communication skills, empathetic, and eager to learn.
Growth Areas
Needs help with setting boundaries, managing client expectations, and dealing with difficult emotions.
Her Question
"I had a client who started crying during our session, and I didn't know what to do. How do I handle emotional clients?"

The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Encounter

What Aisha Tells You:

"I was working with Sarah, 52, who is going through a divorce. During our session, she started crying and sharing very personal details about her marriage. I felt overwhelmed and didn't know how to help her. I just listened and offered tissues, but I feel like I should have done more."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Aisha have a clear framework for handling emotional responses?
  • Did she establish boundaries and scope of practice with Sarah?
  • How did Aisha manage her own emotions during the session?
  • What self-care strategies does Aisha have in place?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Validate Her Feelings

"It's normal to feel overwhelmed when clients express strong emotions. Acknowledge her feelings and reassure her that she's not alone."

2

Teach Emotional Regulation Techniques

Share strategies for staying grounded and centered during emotional sessions, such as deep breathing or visualization.

3

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Remind Aisha of the importance of staying within the boundaries of her practice. If Sarah needs therapy, refer her to a licensed therapist.

4

Emphasize Self-Care

Encourage Aisha to prioritize self-care activities to prevent burnout and maintain her well-being. Supervision is part of that self-care!

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Aisha, thank you for sharing this with me. It takes courage to admit when you feel unsure. It sounds like you handled a difficult situation with empathy."
Explore Her Perspective
"What did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time? I'm curious to hear your thoughts."
Offer Guidance and Support
"One strategy I find helpful is to acknowledge the client's feelings, set a boundary, and then guide them back to the session's objective. For example, 'I hear you're going through a tough time, Sarah. While I'm here to support you, my role is to help you with [specific goal]. Let's gently shift our focus back to that.'"
Empower Next Steps
"What steps will you take to prepare yourself for handling emotional clients in the future? How can I support you in this process?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to remember when a mentee presents a challenging client case?

Focus on creating a safe space where the mentee feels comfortable sharing their experiences without judgment.

2. How can you help a mentee understand and respect their scope of practice?

Regularly review the boundaries of their certification level and discuss appropriate referral strategies when client needs exceed their scope.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding and supporting new practitioners, not dictating their actions.
  • Validate your mentee's feelings and experiences to build trust and rapport.
  • Use open-ended questions to encourage self-reflection and critical thinking.
  • Share your own experiences and insights to provide practical guidance.
  • Emphasize the importance of self-care and boundary-setting for sustainable practice.
