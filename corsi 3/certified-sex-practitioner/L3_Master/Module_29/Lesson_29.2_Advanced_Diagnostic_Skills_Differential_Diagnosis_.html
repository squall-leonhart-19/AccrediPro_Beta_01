Impact of Trauma, Attachment, and Personality

Sexual functioning is deeply intertwined with our emotional history, particularly experiences of trauma, attachment styles, and personality traits. Trauma can profoundly impact sexual desire, arousal, and the ability to experience pleasure. Attachment styles, formed in early childhood, influence how we approach intimacy and connection in adult relationships. Personality disorders can also contribute to sexual dysfunction through difficulties with emotional regulation, interpersonal relationships, and self-image.

A groundbreaking study by Briere & Spinazzola (1998) demonstrated a strong correlation between childhood trauma and adult sexual dysfunction, with 80% of women with a history of sexual abuse reporting difficulties with orgasm. Furthermore, research by Bartholomew & Horowitz (1991) highlighted how insecure attachment styles (anxious-preoccupied, dismissive-avoidant, and fearful-avoidant) can lead to challenges in sexual communication, intimacy, and satisfaction.

Consider these key questions during assessment:

  • "Have you experienced any traumatic events in your life, particularly related to sex or intimacy?"
  • "How would you describe your relationship with your primary caregivers growing up?"
  • "Do you find it difficult to trust others or feel comfortable being vulnerable in relationships?"

Case Study: Complex Diagnostic Picture

Client: Emily, 34 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Emily reports low sexual desire, difficulty achieving orgasm, and pain during intercourse. She has been married for 8 years and reports a loving but sexually unfulfilling relationship.

Intervention:

  1. Medical Referral: Emily was referred to her gynecologist to rule out medical causes of dyspareunia and low libido.
  2. Psychometric Assessment: The FSFI revealed low scores in desire, arousal, and orgasm. The DAS indicated moderate relationship satisfaction but low sexual satisfaction.
  3. Trauma Assessment: During the clinical interview, Emily disclosed a history of childhood emotional neglect and a recent experience of workplace harassment.
  4. Therapeutic Approach: A combination of CBT for sexual dysfunction, EMDR for trauma processing, and couples therapy to improve communication and intimacy.

Outcomes: After 6 months of therapy, Emily reported increased sexual desire, improved ability to achieve orgasm, and reduced pain during intercourse. Her relationship with her husband also improved, with enhanced communication and intimacy.
