The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a new client, Emily, 52, who is experiencing low desire after menopause. I have a standard program, but Emily also mentions feeling lonely and disconnected from her partner. I'm not sure how to adjust the program to address these emotional factors."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah assess the client's relationship dynamics?
  • Is she equipped to address emotional intimacy issues?
  • How can she integrate relationship-focused exercises into the program?
  • Does she understand the interplay between hormonal changes and emotional well-being?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Holistic Assessment

Explain the importance of assessing not just the physical symptoms but also the emotional and relational context. A 2021 study in the Journal of Sexual Medicine highlights the impact of relationship satisfaction on sexual desire in postmenopausal women.

2

Teach Program Customization

Demonstrate how to modify the standard program to include exercises focused on communication, emotional intimacy, and connection. For example, incorporate a 'shared gratitude' exercise or a 'vulnerability sharing' activity.

3

Address Scope of Practice

Discuss the boundaries of her practice and when to refer to a couples therapist or mental health professional. It's crucial to stay within your scope and provide appropriate support.

4

Reinforce the Mind-Body Connection

Explain how hormonal changes can impact emotional well-being and vice versa. Help her understand the interplay and how to address both aspects in her program. A meta-analysis by Davis et al. (2018) in Menopause showed a significant correlation between depression and sexual dysfunction in postmenopausal women.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for recognizing the emotional component in Emily's case. It shows you're thinking beyond the surface. Let's explore how we can integrate that into her program."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What are some exercises or activities from our training that you think might address feelings of loneliness and disconnection? Let's brainstorm together."
Provide Targeted Education
"Remember our discussion on the impact of oxytocin on bonding and desire? Perhaps we can incorporate activities that stimulate oxytocin release, like partnered massage or shared laughter."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"How would you like to present these options to Emily? What feels authentic to your style and approach?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address scope of practice in supervision?

It ensures the mentee provides ethical and appropriate care, referring clients when necessary. This protects both the client and the practitioner.

2. Give an example of how to customize a standard program to address emotional needs.

Incorporate exercises focused on communication, emotional intimacy, and connection, like shared gratitude or vulnerability sharing activities.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about empowering new practitioners to develop their clinical reasoning and intuition.
  • Always start with affirmation and ask guiding questions before offering solutions.
  • Address scope of practice to ensure ethical and appropriate care.
  • Tailor programs to individual client needs by considering emotional and relational factors.
  • Celebrate progress and create a supportive learning environment.
