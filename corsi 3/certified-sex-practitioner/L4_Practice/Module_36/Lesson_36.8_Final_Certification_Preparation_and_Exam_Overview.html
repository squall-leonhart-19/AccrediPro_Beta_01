Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Expectations 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm thrilled to connect with you. Let's use this time to explore your needs and see if my approach is a good fit. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Before we dive in, can you tell me a bit about what prompted you to reach out?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned low libido and feeling disconnected. Can you tell me more about that? How long has this been going on, and how is it impacting your relationship?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to address this issue? Any medications, therapies, or lifestyle changes?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in six months? A year? And what would your ideal outcome be? What would it feel like to have that confidence and connection back?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds like you're feeling [summarize her pain points and validate her feelings]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because we focus on holistic sexual wellness. We'll explore the physical, emotional, and relational factors contributing to your challenges. My program includes personalized coaching sessions, practical exercises, and resources to help you reclaim your sexuality and strengthen your connection with your partner. Many of my clients experience significant improvements within 8-12 weeks."
YOU:
"I understand you're concerned about affordability. It's important to think of this as an investment in your well-being and your relationship. What questions do you have about how my approach can help you achieve your goals?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you think my program could be a good fit for you? ... Great! Let me tell you about the program options and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to feel confident."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Let's break down the value. My program not only addresses your immediate concerns but also provides you with tools and knowledge for long-term sexual wellness. Think of the cost of neglecting this issue ‚Äì the strain on your relationship, the impact on your self-esteem. Can we explore some payment options that might work for you?"
"I'm not sure if this will really work for me."
"That's a valid concern. I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not satisfied with the program after the first month, I'll refund your investment. I'm confident in my approach, and I want you to feel secure in your decision."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan where you can split the investment into smaller, more manageable installments over a period of time. Let me explain the options available and see what works best for your budget."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges, presenting your approach, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, break down the value of your program, discuss payment options, and highlight the long-term benefits.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps guide the conversation and ensures you cover all essential points.
  • Empathy and active listening are crucial for building trust and understanding your client's needs.
  • Confident pricing and objection handling demonstrate your value and build client confidence.
  • Consistent client acquisition efforts lead to a thriving and financially rewarding practice.
