Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Maria, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Sarah. I'm excited to chat with you about the Renew Your Vitality program. First, tell me, what caught your eye about the program?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! I created this program because I saw so many women just like you needing a supportive space. What are you hoping to get out of a group program?"
Phase 2: Understand Her Needs & Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling isolated. Can you tell me more about that? How long has that been going on, and how is it impacting your life?"
YOU:
"And what about your energy levels? What does a typical day look like for you, and what are the biggest challenges you face?"
YOU:
"Have you participated in group programs before? What did you like or dislike about them?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program as the Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Maria, it sounds like you're really looking for connection, support, and a structured way to boost your energy and feel good again. Does that resonate?"
YOU:
"The Renew Your Vitality program is designed to address exactly what you're looking for. It's a 10-week program with weekly group calls, personalized action steps, and a private online community. We focus on nutrition, movement, and mindset ‚Äì all tailored for women in midlife. Many of our members form lasting friendships and find a renewed sense of purpose."
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I think you'd really thrive in this environment. What are your initial thoughts?"
Phase 4: Close with Confidence 5 min
YOU:
"So Maria, based on everything we've discussed, do you see yourself being a good fit for the Renew Your Vitality program? I have a few spots left, and I'd love to welcome you."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'll have time."
"I understand. Life gets busy! The weekly calls are 90 minutes, and the action steps are designed to fit into your existing routine. Plus, you'll have access to recordings if you miss a call. Think of it as an investment in yourself ‚Äì a way to carve out time for your well-being. How much time are you currently spending feeling tired and disconnected? This program is designed to give you back that time, and more."
"I'm worried I won't connect with the other women."
"That's a valid concern. We create a very welcoming and supportive environment. Before the program starts, we have a 'meet and greet' call to help everyone get acquainted. And throughout the program, we encourage interaction and sharing. Many of our members have become close friends. I‚Äôm confident you will find your tribe!"
"It's a bit more than I was planning to spend."
"I understand the investment is important. Let's break down what you're getting: 10 weeks of group coaching, personalized support, access to a valuable community, and tools to transform your vitality. What is that worth to you? What would it be worth to you to feel energized, connected, and confident again? Plus, we offer a payment plan to make it more manageable."
"Can I talk to someone who's already in the program?"
"Absolutely! I can connect you with one of our current members who would be happy to share her experience. Would you prefer to speak with someone who is a similar age, or has similar interests?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to highlight when presenting the price of your group program?

Highlight the value, benefits, and transformation they'll receive. Also, clearly explain payment options.

2. How do you address a prospect's concern about not having enough time for the group program?

Emphasize that the program is designed to fit into their existing routine, offer flexible options like recordings, and highlight the potential time-saving benefits of increased energy and well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a fantastic way to scale your impact and income.
  • Building rapport and understanding your prospect's needs is crucial.
  • Address objections with empathy and confidence.
  • Clearly present the value and benefits of your program.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently enroll new members.
