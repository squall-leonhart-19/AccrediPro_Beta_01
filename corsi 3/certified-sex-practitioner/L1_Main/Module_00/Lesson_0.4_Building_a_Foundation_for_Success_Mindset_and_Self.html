What You Can and Cannot Do

As a Certified Sex Practitionerâ„¢, itâ€™s vital to understand your scope of practice. This program equips you with the knowledge to educate and coach clients on various aspects of sexual health and wellness. Remember, we educate, we don't diagnose. This distinction is crucial.

You can:

  • Provide information on sexual health topics.
  • Offer coaching on communication and intimacy skills.
  • Guide clients in exploring their sexual desires and boundaries.
  • Help clients create personalized wellness plans.

You cannot:

  • Diagnose or treat medical conditions.
  • Prescribe medications or supplements.
  • Provide therapy for mental health disorders.

Itâ€™s about working alongside medical professionals, not replacing them. The power of coaching lies in empowering clients to take control of their sexual wellbeing, but within clearly defined boundaries.

Legal Considerations

The legality of health coaching varies by state. Generally, as long as you are not practicing medicine without a license, you are within legal bounds. However, it's always a good idea to consult with a legal professional in your area.

Consider these points:

  • Insurance: Look into professional liability insurance to protect yourself.
  • Liability: Ensure your client agreements clearly state the scope of your services.
  • "Staying in your lane": Refer clients to medical professionals for diagnosis and treatment.

Ethics & Professionalism

Maintaining high ethical standards is paramount to building trust and credibility.

  • Client Confidentiality: Always protect your client's privacy.
  • Professional Conduct: Maintain clear boundaries and a respectful demeanor.
  • When to Refer Out: Recognize when a client needs medical or psychological help beyond your scope.

Building trust is a cornerstone of your practice. Be authentic, empathetic, and committed to your clients' wellbeing.
