Chief Complaints
Decreased libido, painful intercourse, anxiety, difficulty reaching orgasm, feelings of shame related to sexual experiences in early adulthood.
Medical History
Endometriosis (diagnosed at 32, managed with hormonal birth control), migraines, history of depression (treated with SSRIs for 5 years, discontinued 2 years ago).
Medications
Hormonal birth control (for endometriosis), occasional sumatriptan (for migraines), over-the-counter pain relievers.
Relationship Status
Married for 20 years, reports a loving but sexually infrequent relationship. Husband is supportive but struggles to understand her challenges.
Trauma History
Discloses a history of unwanted sexual advances from a family member during adolescence, which she has never fully processed.
Presenting Dilemma
Eleanor is seeking help to improve her sexual satisfaction and intimacy with her husband. She is hesitant to fully disclose her trauma history, fearing judgment and potential disruption of her marriage.

Ethical Considerations

This case presents several ethical considerations that require careful navigation:

  • Confidentiality: Balancing Eleanor's right to privacy with the potential need to address her trauma history for effective treatment.
  • Informed Consent: Ensuring Eleanor fully understands the potential benefits and risks of different therapeutic approaches, including trauma-informed care.
  • Scope of Practice: Recognizing the limits of your expertise and knowing when to refer Eleanor to a specialized trauma therapist or mental health professional.
  • Dual Relationships: Avoiding any situation that could compromise your objectivity or create a conflict of interest.
  • Values Conflicts: Being aware of your own personal values and beliefs and ensuring they do not unduly influence your therapeutic approach with Eleanor.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Ethical Landscape

Step 1: Assess for Immediate Safety Concerns
Determine if Eleanor is currently experiencing any abuse, suicidal ideation, or other immediate safety risks that require mandatory reporting. Even if the trauma is historical, assess for current impact on mental health.
Step 2: Establish Clear Boundaries and Expectations
Clearly communicate your role, responsibilities, and limitations as a sex practitioner. Explain the importance of honesty and transparency for effective treatment, while respecting Eleanor's right to control the pace and depth of disclosure.
Step 3: Prioritize Symptom Management and Relationship Enhancement
Begin by addressing Eleanor's presenting complaints of decreased libido and painful intercourse. Implement strategies to improve sexual comfort, communication, and intimacy with her husband. This can build trust and create a safer space for deeper exploration.
Step 4: Facilitate Trauma Awareness and Processing (If Appropriate)
If Eleanor expresses a willingness to explore her trauma history, provide psychoeducation about trauma and its impact on sexual function. Offer resources for trauma-informed therapy and support groups. Emphasize that she is in control of the process and can stop at any time.
Important: Do not attempt to provide trauma therapy unless you are specifically trained and licensed to do so.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Hormonal Imbalances

Endometriosis management with hormonal birth control can significantly impact libido and sexual function. Rule out estrogen deficiency or other hormonal imbalances that may be contributing to Eleanor's symptoms.

2

Pelvic Floor Dysfunction

Painful intercourse could be related to pelvic floor muscle tension or dysfunction, especially given Eleanor's history of endometriosis. Consider referral to a pelvic floor physical therapist.

3

Relationship Dynamics

Explore the dynamics of Eleanor's relationship with her husband. Are there unresolved conflicts, communication barriers, or mismatched sexual desires that are contributing to her dissatisfaction?

4

Underlying Mental Health Conditions

Even though Eleanor discontinued SSRIs two years ago, she may still be experiencing residual symptoms of depression or anxiety that are impacting her sexual function. Consider referral to a psychiatrist or therapist for further evaluation.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important ethical consideration when working with a client who has a history of trauma?

Prioritizing client safety and autonomy. Ensuring the client feels in control of the pace and depth of disclosure, and providing appropriate referrals for trauma-informed care if needed.

2. In this case, what are some potential boundary violations to be aware of?

Attempting to provide trauma therapy without proper training and licensure, imposing personal values on the client's sexual choices, or engaging in any form of sexual contact with the client.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases often involve overlapping medical, psychological, and relationship factors.
  • Ethical decision-making requires careful consideration of client autonomy, confidentiality, and scope of practice.
  • Trauma-informed care is essential when working with clients who have a history of sexual abuse or assault.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare professionals (MDs, therapists, physical therapists) can enhance treatment outcomes.
  • Always prioritize client safety and well-being above all else.
