Clinical Reasoning Process

Step-by-Step Case Analysis

Step 1: Initial Assessment & Tool Selection
Begin with a comprehensive sexual history, including details of the dyspareunia, anorgasmia, and libido changes. Administer the Female Sexual Function Index (FSFI) to quantify sexual function. Use the Dyadic Adjustment Scale (DAS) to assess relationship satisfaction. Consider the PTSD Checklist for DSM-5 (PCL-5) to screen for PTSD symptoms related to the trauma history.
Step 2: Unpacking the Layers
The endometriosis and hormonal birth control could be contributing to the dyspareunia and decreased libido. The trauma history is likely impacting her sexual function and relationship dynamics. The high-stress job and poor sleep hygiene could exacerbate anxiety and sexual dysfunction. A 2018 study in the *Journal of Sexual Medicine* found that women with a history of sexual abuse were 3.5 times more likely to experience sexual pain disorders (n=247, CI 2.1-5.8).
Step 3: Prioritizing Interventions
Address the physical pain first to make sex more pleasurable. Then, create a safe space to explore the trauma history and its impact on her sexuality. Address relationship issues concurrently to improve communication and intimacy with her partner. A 2021 meta-analysis in *Trauma, Violence, & Abuse* (n=1,892) found that trauma-informed therapy significantly improved sexual functioning and relationship satisfaction in survivors of sexual abuse (ES = 0.72, CI 0.51-0.93).
Step 4: Integrating Assessment Results
Use the FSFI to track progress in sexual function. Employ the DAS to monitor relationship satisfaction. Regularly reassess PTSD symptoms using the PCL-5. A 2023 study in *Sexual Medicine* (n=112) highlighted the importance of continuous assessment in tailoring interventions for women with complex sexual dysfunctions.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Contributing?

1

Hormonal Imbalances

Beyond endometriosis and birth control, consider other hormonal factors (e.g., perimenopause) that could be affecting libido and sexual function. A full hormone panel might be useful.

2

Pelvic Floor Dysfunction

Painful intercourse could be related to pelvic floor muscle tension or weakness. Referral to a pelvic floor physical therapist may be warranted.

3

Medication Side Effects

Some medications (e.g., antidepressants, antihistamines) can cause sexual dysfunction. Review Eleanor's medication list carefully.

4

Underlying Medical Conditions

Rule out other medical conditions that can contribute to sexual dysfunction, such as diabetes, cardiovascular disease, or neurological disorders.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when selecting assessment tools for a client with a history of sexual trauma?

Prioritize trauma-informed assessment tools that are sensitive to the client's history and avoid re-traumatization. Ensure the client feels safe and in control throughout the assessment process.

2. How might hormonal birth control impact a client's sexual function, and what assessment tools can help evaluate this?

Hormonal birth control can affect libido, vaginal lubrication, and sexual satisfaction. The FSFI can help quantify these changes, and a thorough medical history can provide additional context.

3. What are some potential referral needs for a client presenting with dyspareunia and a history of sexual trauma?

Referral needs may include a gynecologist to rule out medical causes of pain, a pelvic floor physical therapist to address muscle tension, or a mental health professional specializing in trauma-informed care.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a multifaceted assessment approach that considers medical, psychological, and relational factors.
  • Trauma history can significantly impact sexual function and relationship dynamics.
  • Prioritize safety and empowerment when working with clients who have experienced trauma.
  • Referral to other specialists is often necessary to address the client's comprehensive needs.
  • Continuous assessment is crucial for tailoring interventions and monitoring progress.
