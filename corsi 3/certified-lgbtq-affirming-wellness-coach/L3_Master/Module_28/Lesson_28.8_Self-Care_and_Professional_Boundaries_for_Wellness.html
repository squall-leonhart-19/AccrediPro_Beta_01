Her Question
"I feel out of my depth with a client who just disclosed suicidal ideation. What do I do?"

The Case She Presents

Alex's Client Case

What Alex Tells You:

"My client, Jamie, 28, non-binary, disclosed during our session that they've been having thoughts of ending their life. They said they feel hopeless about their transition and the discrimination they face. I froze. I didn't know what to say, and I'm worried I made things worse."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Alex assess the immediacy and severity of the suicidal ideation?
  • Does Alex know the appropriate crisis intervention protocols and resources?
  • Did Alex breach confidentiality appropriately, considering the risk?
  • Is Alex aware of their own emotional response to the disclosure and the potential for countertransference?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Alex

1

Prioritize Safety and Immediate Action

Emphasize the importance of assessing the client's immediate safety. Discuss questions to gauge the severity and plan for suicide (e.g., "Do you have a plan? Do you have access to the means?").

2

Review Crisis Intervention Protocols

Walk through the steps of crisis intervention: ensuring client safety, contacting emergency services or a crisis hotline, and notifying relevant parties (if appropriate and with the client's consent, where possible).

3

Address Scope of Practice and Ethical Considerations

Reinforce the boundaries of a wellness coach's role. Clarify when a referral to a mental health professional is necessary and ethical obligations related to confidentiality and duty to protect.

4

Process Personal Reactions and Countertransference

Acknowledge the emotional impact of such disclosures on the coach. Discuss strategies for managing countertransference and seeking supervision for support.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge the Difficulty
"Alex, it's completely understandable that you felt overwhelmed. Disclosures like these are challenging for even experienced practitioners. The fact that you're seeking guidance shows your commitment to your clients."
Empathetic Inquiry
"Before we dive into the protocols, can you share what went through your mind in that moment? What were you feeling?"
Clarify Protocols and Resources
"In these situations, our priority is always safety. Let's review the steps for assessing immediate risk and connecting the client with appropriate resources. Remember the Suicide Prevention Lifeline and the Crisis Text Line are invaluable."
Reinforce Boundaries and Self-Care
"It's crucial to remember that you're a wellness coach, not a therapist. While we offer support, these cases require specialized mental health care. Also, remember to prioritize your own well-being after such intense sessions. Debriefing with a supervisor or trusted colleague can be incredibly helpful."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the first steps a coach should take when a client discloses suicidal ideation?

Assess the immediacy and severity of the risk. Ensure the client's safety. Connect the client with immediate support resources such as a crisis hotline or mental health professional.

2. Why is it important to discuss scope of practice in supervision related to crisis situations?

To clarify the boundaries of the coach's role and to ensure the client receives appropriate care from qualified professionals. It also protects the coach from liability.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize immediate safety and well-being when clients disclose crisis situations.
  • Know and follow established crisis intervention protocols and resources.
  • Understand and respect the boundaries of your scope of practice as a wellness coach.
  • Address your emotional reactions and seek support to prevent countertransference.
  • Supervision plays a vital role in preparing coaches for complex and sensitive client cases.
