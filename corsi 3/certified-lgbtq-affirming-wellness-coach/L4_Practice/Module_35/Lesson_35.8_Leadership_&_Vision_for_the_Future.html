Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I'm Rachel. I appreciate you taking the time to connect. To make the best use of our time, I'd like to understand what brought you here today, share how I can help, and if it feels like a good fit, discuss next steps. How does that sound?"
YOU:
"I noticed you found me on Instagram. Was there anything specific in my content that resonated with you?"
Phase 2: Explore Challenges & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the stress you're experiencing. What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"And how is this stress impacting your sleep? What have you tried so far to manage it?"
YOU:
"Imagine you've successfully managed your stress and are sleeping soundly. What would that feel like? How would it impact your work and personal life?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're dealing with significant stress that's impacting your sleep and overall well-being. I understand how frustrating that can be. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is based on proven techniques, including mindfulness, cognitive behavioral strategies, and personalized wellness practices. I work with clients over 12 weeks to create a customized plan that addresses their specific needs and goals. Many clients report feeling calmer and sleeping better within the first few weeks. This isn't just about quick fixes; it's about building sustainable habits for long-term well-being."
YOU:
"I also provide ongoing support and resources to help you stay on track, including weekly check-ins, personalized meditations, and access to a private online community."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I'm confident I can help you manage your stress and improve your sleep. Does this approach sound like something you'd like to explore further?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about my program and how we can get started..."

"I hear you. It's frustrating when things don't work out. My program is different because it's highly personalized and addresses the root causes of your stress. Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to establish in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Building rapport and setting the agenda. This helps create a comfortable environment and ensures the call stays focused.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then gently probe to understand their specific concerns (budget, time commitment, etc.). Offer to provide more information or answer any questions.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key. Understand your prospect's needs and pain points before the call.
  • Practice your script and pricing so you can speak confidently and naturally.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide clear, concise responses.
  • Focus on the value you provide and the transformation your clients can achieve.
  • Follow up promptly after the call to reinforce your message and answer any remaining questions.
