Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Time is valuable. The program is designed to be flexible, with weekly sessions and exercises that you can fit into your schedule. Plus, the connections you'll make with the other women in the group will provide ongoing support and accountability. How about we look at your schedule together and see where we can realistically fit it in?"
"I'm not good at group settings."
"I hear you. Some people feel nervous about groups. This program is designed to be a safe and supportive space where everyone feels comfortable sharing and connecting. We have ground rules for respectful communication, and I facilitate the group to ensure everyone feels heard and valued. Many participants find that the group dynamic is actually one of the most rewarding aspects of the program."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you. Perhaps I can send you some testimonials from past participants?"
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that it's an investment. How much would it be worth to you to finally feel fulfilled and excited about your future? Can you imagine the benefits of the program, not just for you but for your whole family?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call for group programs?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Program, and Close.

2. What are some common objections you might hear when enrolling clients into a group program?

Common objections include: "I'm not sure I have the time," "I'm not good at group settings," "I need to think about it," and "It's too expensive."

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is essential for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the client's problems and goals allows you to tailor your program presentation to their specific needs.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and providing clear solutions can increase enrollment rates.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence and highlighting the value of your program is crucial for closing the sale.
  • Consistent effort and a genuine passion for helping others can lead to significant income potential with group coaching programs.
