Handling Objections: Turn "No" into "Maybe"

Common Objections & Your Empathetic Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand. Time is precious. However, think of this as an investment in reclaiming your time. Many of my clients find they actually GAIN time by implementing the strategies we develop. We'll work together to create a schedule that fits your lifestyle. What adjustments would make this feel manageable for you?"
"I need to research other options first."
"That's a smart approach. What specific information are you hoping to find in your research? Perhaps I can address some of those questions now to save you time. I'm confident that my personalized approach and proven results will stand out."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't ethically guarantee specific outcomes (as results depend on your commitment and effort), I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the tools, support, and accountability you need to succeed. My clients typically report significant improvements in their work-life balance and overall well-being within the first few weeks. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied after the first two weeks, we can discuss a prorated refund."
"I'm worried I won't be able to keep up with the program."
"I hear you. We'll work together to create a pace that feels comfortable for you. The program is designed to be flexible and adaptable to your individual needs. We'll break down your goals into manageable steps and celebrate your progress along the way. Plus, you'll have my full support throughout the entire process."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a 30-minute discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set the Stage, Deep Dive into Challenges & Desires, Introduce Your Solution & Approach, and Confident Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to research other options first?"

Acknowledge their approach, inquire about what specific information they're seeking, and offer to address some of those questions to save them time. Highlight the unique value of your personalized approach and proven results.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is paramount: Thoroughly understand your ideal client and tailor your approach accordingly.
  • Rapport is essential: Build genuine connections with prospects by actively listening and demonstrating empathy.
  • Value-based pricing: Confidently present your pricing by highlighting the tangible benefits and long-term ROI of your program.
  • Objection handling is an opportunity: View objections as opportunities to address concerns and build trust.
  • Consistent action leads to results: Regularly practice your sales skills and track your progress to achieve your income goals.
