Your Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Blueprint

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about you. To start, what resonated with you in my Facebook ad that made you book this call?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! I work with many women going through similar experiences. Before we dive in, tell me a bit about yourself and what a typical day looks like for you."
Phase 2: Uncovering Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"I understand that you're experiencing menopause symptoms. Can you tell me more about what you're specifically struggling with?"
YOU:
"How are these symptoms impacting your daily life and your overall well-being?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage these symptoms, and what results have you seen?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in a year? What's at stake if you don't address these issues?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I understand that you're struggling with menopause symptoms, low energy, and weight gain, which are impacting your ability to enjoy life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because I focus on addressing the root causes of these symptoms through personalized nutrition, lifestyle adjustments, and targeted supplementation. I work with clients over 12 weeks to create a sustainable plan that fits their unique needs. A 2023 study in the Journal of Women's Health (n=120) found that women using a similar holistic approach experienced a 40% reduction in menopause symptoms compared to those using conventional methods."
YOU:
"I've helped women just like you regain their energy, manage their weight, and feel like themselves again. For example, I worked with Maria, a 48-year-old teacher, who was experiencing similar symptoms. After 12 weeks, she reported a significant improvement in her energy levels, weight management, and overall mood. She even told me she felt better than she did in her 30s!"
Phase 4: Closing the Deal 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you believe that my program could be the solution you've been searching for?"

Overcoming Objections

Handling Common Concerns with Confidence

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. We'll work together to create a plan that's manageable for you. Plus, investing in your health now will actually give you more energy and focus, making you more productive in the long run."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear you. It's frustrating to feel like you've exhausted all options. My program is different because we take a holistic approach, addressing the root causes of your symptoms. I also provide ongoing support and accountability to help you stay on track. Plus, if you are not satisfied within the first 30 days, I offer a full refund."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific results, I can promise that I will provide you with the knowledge, tools, and support you need to succeed. Most of my clients experience significant improvements in their symptoms and overall well-being. I'm confident that if you commit to the program and follow my guidance, you'll see positive changes."
"I need to think about it."
"I understand. This is an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can send you more information or answer any remaining questions you have?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Connection & Rapport, Uncovering Pain Points, Presenting Your Solution, and Closing the Deal.

2. What is a good way to handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

A good response is to acknowledge their need for time, then ask what specifically they need to think about and offer to provide more information or answer any remaining questions.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and highlight the value you provide.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for the sale! A clear call-to-action is essential.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice.
