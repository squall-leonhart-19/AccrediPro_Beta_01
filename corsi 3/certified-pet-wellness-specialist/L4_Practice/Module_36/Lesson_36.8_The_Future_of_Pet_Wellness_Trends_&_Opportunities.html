Susan, 52

Saw your ad on Facebook. Has a senior dog with mobility issues and anxiety. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Frustrated with her dog's declining health. Feels helpless and guilty. Wants to improve her dog's quality of life.
Budget Concern
"I'm willing to invest, but I need to see results. I've tried so many things that haven't worked."
Decision Style
Emotional decision-maker. Responds to empathy and genuine care. Needs reassurance and a clear plan.
Her Goal
"I want my dog to be happy and comfortable in his senior years. I want to enjoy our time together."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you're concerned about your senior dog. To start, could you tell me a bit about [dog's name] and what you're experiencing?"
YOU:
"I appreciate you sharing that. It sounds like you have a very special bond with [dog's name]. I also work with other senior dogs and their owners, and I know how challenging it can be."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the mobility issues and anxiety. When did you first notice them? What specific behaviors are you seeing?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to help [dog's name]? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would a perfect day look like for you and [dog's name]?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're looking for a holistic approach that addresses both [dog's name]'s physical and emotional well-being. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My program focuses on creating a personalized wellness plan that combines gentle exercises, targeted nutrition, stress-reduction techniques, and environmental enrichment. We'll work together over 12 weeks to help [dog's name] regain mobility, reduce anxiety, and enjoy a happier, more comfortable life. Many of my clients see noticeable improvements within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared and the approach I use, do you feel like this could be a good fit for you and [dog's name]?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started. I have a couple of openings next month, and I'd love to help you and [dog's name]."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my dog is too old to benefit from this."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. However, age isn't necessarily a barrier. Many senior dogs experience significant improvements in mobility and comfort with the right approach. We'll assess [dog's name]'s individual needs and tailor the plan accordingly. My goal is to enhance his quality of life, regardless of his age."
"I'm worried about the time commitment."
"I understand. The program requires a commitment from both of us, but I've designed it to be manageable for busy pet owners. We'll work together to create a schedule that fits your lifestyle. Plus, the time you invest now can lead to more quality time with [dog's name] in the long run."
"I need to discuss this with my family."
"Of course. I can send you a detailed program overview that you can share with them. Or, if it would be helpful, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with you and your family to answer any questions they may have. What works best for you?"
"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that budget is a consideration. Let's talk about the value you'll receive from the program. Think about the potential savings on vet bills, medications, and other treatments. More importantly, consider the priceless value of seeing [dog's name] happy, comfortable, and enjoying life again. I also offer flexible payment plans to make the program more accessible."

Believe in Yourself and Your Services!

Remember, Susan, you're providing a valuable service that can transform the lives of pets and their owners. Approach each discovery call with confidence, empathy, and a genuine desire to help. Your passion and expertise will shine through, attracting the right clients to your practice.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Connect, Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome, Present Your Approach & Value, and Close & Next Steps.

2. What is the key to handling client objections effectively?

The key is to listen empathetically, acknowledge their concerns, and provide reassuring and valuable responses that address their specific needs and hesitations.

3. What is the importance of practicing your pricing and call-to-action?

Practicing your pricing and call-to-action builds confidence, ensures clarity, and increases the likelihood of converting potential clients into paying clients. It allows you to articulate the value of your services and confidently guide clients toward enrollment.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting leads into clients.
  • Building rapport, understanding the client's needs, and presenting your value are crucial components of a successful call.
  • Anticipate and prepare for common objections to address client concerns effectively.
  • Practice your pricing and call-to-action to convey confidence and guide clients toward enrollment.
  • Believe in the value of your services and approach each call with empathy and a genuine desire to help.
