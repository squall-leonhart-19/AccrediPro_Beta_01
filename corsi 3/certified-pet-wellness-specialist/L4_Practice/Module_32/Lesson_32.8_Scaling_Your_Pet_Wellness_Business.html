Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the initial minutes of a discovery call?

Building rapport involves expressing gratitude for their time, referencing something specific from their initial contact (e.g., workshop questions), and asking about their motivations for reaching out.

2. How can you effectively address the objection that the program is too expensive?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the cost as an investment in their long-term health and well-being. Highlight the value they'll receive, including personalized support, comprehensive assessment, and potential savings on medical expenses.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is Key: Thoroughly understand your prospect's needs and goals before the call.
  • Structure Your Call: Follow a clear structure to build rapport, understand the problem, present your approach, and close the sale.
  • Handle Objections with Confidence: Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses that address the prospect's concerns.
  • Practice Your Pricing: Confidently state your price and highlight the value of your program.
  • Close with a Clear Call-to-Action: End the call with a clear call-to-action that invites the prospect to take the next step.
