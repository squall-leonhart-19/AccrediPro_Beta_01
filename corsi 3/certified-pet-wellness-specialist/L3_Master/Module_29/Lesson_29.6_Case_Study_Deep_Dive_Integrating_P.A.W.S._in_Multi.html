Case Study:Whiskers, the Multi-Morbid Cat

Whiskers: A Complex Case of Feline Wellness

Client: Sarah Miller

Pet: Whiskers, a 12-year-old Domestic Shorthair cat

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah contacted us out of concern for Whiskers, who has been diagnosed with diabetes mellitus two years prior, osteoarthritis in her hips, and recently, has displayed signs of anxiety (hiding, excessive vocalization, and decreased appetite). Sarah reports difficulty managing Whiskers' blood glucose levels, decreased mobility affecting her quality of life, and increasing stress for both Whiskers and herself.

Initial Assessment: Our initial assessment revealed that Whiskers was overweight (7.2 kg, body condition score 7/9), had limited range of motion in her hips, and displayed anxious behavior during the consultation. P.A.W.S. Application: A Tailored Wellness Plan

Based on the initial assessment, we developed a P.A.W.S.-aligned wellness plan for Whiskers:

  1. Purpose (Environment and Enrichment):
    • Created safe spaces for Whiskers, including elevated perches and quiet hiding spots.
    • Introduced interactive food puzzles to stimulate mental activity and slow down eating.
    • Recommended pheromone diffusers (Feliway) to reduce anxiety. A 2015 study in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery found that feline pheromone analogs significantly reduced stress-related behaviors in cats (DePorter et al., 2015).
  2. Activity (Exercise Adaptation):
    • Implemented gentle, low-impact exercises, such as short play sessions with a laser pointer or feather wand, to improve mobility without stressing her joints.
    • Recommended physical therapy exercises, including passive range of motion exercises, to be performed by Sarah under guidance.
    • Monitored Whiskersâ€™ response to exercise closely, adjusting intensity and duration as needed.
  3. Wellness (Nutrition and Supplementation):
    • Worked with Sarah and Whiskersâ€™ veterinarian to adjust her diet to a high-protein, low-carbohydrate formula specifically designed for diabetic cats. This diet aims to stabilize blood glucose levels and promote weight loss.
    • Introduced joint supplements containing glucosamine and chondroitin to support cartilage health and reduce inflammation. A meta-analysis published in Veterinary Surgery in 2017 showed that glucosamine and chondroitin sulfate had a moderate positive effect on pain reduction in cats with osteoarthritis (Comblain et al., 2017).
    • Ensured fresh water was always available, encouraging hydration to support kidney health and overall wellness.
  4. Support (Preventative Care and Monitoring):
    • Maintained regular veterinary check-ups to monitor blood glucose levels, kidney function, and overall health.
    • Educated Sarah on how to monitor Whiskers' blood glucose at home using a glucometer, empowering her to actively participate in her care.
    • Collaborated closely with Whiskersâ€™ veterinarian to adjust insulin dosage as needed based on blood glucose monitoring.

Navigating Polypharmacy and Drug Interactions

Given Whiskersâ€™ multiple conditions, managing her medications required careful consideration. Polypharmacy, the use of multiple medications simultaneously, increases the risk of drug interactions and adverse effects. A 2019 study in the Journal of the American Veterinary Medical Association found that adverse drug events were significantly more common in pets receiving multiple medications (Boothe, 2019).

To mitigate these risks, we:

  1. Conducted a thorough review of all medications Whiskers was taking, including insulin, pain medication for osteoarthritis, and any supplements.
  2. Consulted with Whiskersâ€™ veterinarian and a veterinary pharmacist to identify potential drug interactions and adjust dosages as needed.
  3. Educated Sarah on the importance of administering medications as prescribed and monitoring for any signs of adverse effects, such as changes in appetite, vomiting, diarrhea, or lethargy.
