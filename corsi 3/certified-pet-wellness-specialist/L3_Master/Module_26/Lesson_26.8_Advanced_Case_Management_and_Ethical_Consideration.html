The Program Development Challenge

Sarah's Program Dilemma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I created a 12-week wellness program for dogs with anxiety, but only one person signed up. I included diet changes, training exercises, and supplements. What am I doing wrong? Should I lower my prices?"

Areas for Exploration:

  • Was there sufficient market research to validate the program?
  • Is the pricing aligned with the perceived value?
  • Are the program components realistic and achievable for clients?
  • Is Sarah clearly communicating the program benefits?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Key Mentoring Points for Sarah

1

Emphasize Market Validation

Guide Sarah to conduct surveys or interviews to understand client needs and validate the program's appeal. "Did you ask potential clients what THEY are struggling with?"

2

Refine Pricing Strategy

Help Sarah evaluate her pricing in relation to program value, competitor pricing, and target audience affordability. "What value are you offering that justifies your price point?"

3

Simplify Program Components

Encourage Sarah to break down the program into smaller, more manageable steps. "Can you offer a 'quick start' option to build momentum?"

4

Enhance Communication of Benefits

Assist Sarah in articulating the program's tangible benefits in a clear and compelling way. "How can you showcase the positive transformations clients can expect?"

Crafting Constructive Feedback

Delivering Supportive Mentoring

Acknowledge Effort
"Sarah, I admire your initiative in creating this program. It shows your dedication to helping pets. Let's work together to make it irresistible to clients."
Encourage Reflection
"What's your initial thought on why the program isn't attracting clients? What assumptions did you make?"
Share Your Experience
"Early on, I launched a program with too many steps. Clients felt overwhelmed. I learned to start small and build from there."
Empower Ownership
"What adjustments feel right to you? How can I support you in refining your approach?"

You're Shaping the Future!

By mentoring new practitioners, you're expanding the reach of pet wellness and building a stronger community. Your guidance makes a real difference.

Check Your Understanding

1. How can you best support a mentee struggling with pricing their services?

Encourage them to research competitor pricing, calculate their expenses and time investment, and consider the unique value they offer. Help them articulate this value to clients.

2. What is the benefit of sharing your own failures with a mentee?

Sharing failures normalizes the learning process, builds trust, and provides valuable insights into overcoming challenges. It helps the mentee feel less alone in their struggles.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through real-world challenges.
  • Effective mentors emphasize market validation and clear communication.
  • Sharing personal experiences helps build trust and normalize learning.
  • Constructive feedback empowers mentees to take ownership of their growth.
  • Your guidance shapes the future of pet wellness by building a stronger community.
