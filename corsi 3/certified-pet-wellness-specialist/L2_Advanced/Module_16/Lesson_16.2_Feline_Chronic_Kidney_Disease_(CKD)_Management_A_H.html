Case Study

Mittens: A Holistic Approach to CKD Management

üêà

Mittens

14-year-old Domestic Shorthair Cat

Mittens, a 14-year-old domestic shorthair cat, presents with a history of progressive weight loss (0.5 kg over 6 months), increased thirst (polydipsia), and lethargy. Her owner, Sarah, a 48-year-old teacher looking for natural ways to support Mittens, reports that Mittens has also been experiencing occasional vomiting and decreased appetite. Sarah is dedicated to providing the best possible care for Mittens and is open to exploring various treatment options.

Applying the 'P': Profile & Partnership

The 'P' in The P.A.W.S. Framework‚Ñ¢ stands for Profile & Partnership. This step involves gathering comprehensive information about Mittens and building a strong rapport with Sarah. As we learned in Module 1, building trust and understanding the owner's capabilities are crucial for successful wellness plan implementation.

  1. Dietary History: Obtain a detailed account of Mittens' diet, including the type of food, feeding schedule, and any recent changes. It's important to understand if Mittens is on a kidney-friendly diet.
  2. Water Intake Habits: Assess Mittens' water consumption, including the number of water bowls available, their location, and whether Mittens prefers running water (e.g., a water fountain).
  3. Environmental Factors: Evaluate Mittens' living environment, including access to litter boxes, stress levels, and opportunities for enrichment.
  4. Owner's Capabilities: Determine Sarah's ability to administer medications, make dietary changes, and provide supportive care.

In Mittens' case, Sarah reveals that Mittens is currently on a dry food diet and has only one water bowl. Sarah works long hours and sometimes forgets to refill the water bowl. Mittens also seems stressed by the presence of a new kitten in the household.

Applying the 'A': Assess & Analyze

The 'A' represents Assess & Analyze. This step entails reviewing diagnostic results and assessing the impact of CKD on Mittens' overall well-being. Remember, early detection and proper staging of CKD are essential for effective management.

  1. Reviewing Veterinary Diagnostics: Analyze Mittens' bloodwork, including creatinine, BUN (blood urea nitrogen), phosphorus, and SDMA (symmetric dimethylarginine) levels. Also, review her urine specific gravity (USG) and proteinuria.
  2. Staging CKD: Based on the International Renal Interest Society (IRIS) guidelines, stage Mittens' CKD. This will help determine the severity of the disease and guide treatment decisions. A 2019 study in the Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine found that accurate staging of CKD significantly improves patient outcomes.
  3. Assessing Clinical Signs: Evaluate Mittens' clinical signs, such as weight loss, dehydration, vomiting, and oral ulcers.
  4. Quality of Life Assessment: Use a validated quality of life questionnaire to assess the impact of CKD on Mittens' daily life.

In Mittens' case, her bloodwork reveals elevated creatinine (2.8 mg/dL) and SDMA (22 ¬µg/dL), indicating Stage II CKD. Her USG is low (1.018), suggesting impaired kidney function. She exhibits mild dehydration and has lost 10% of her body weight over the past six months.

Applying the 'W': Wellness Plan & Implementation

The 'W' stands for Wellness Plan & Implementation. This stage involves creating a tailored wellness plan to address Mittens' specific needs and implementing it in collaboration with Sarah. Remember, a holistic approach considers nutrition, hydration, environmental enrichment, and supportive therapies.

  1. Nutritional Management: Recommend a kidney-friendly diet that is low in phosphorus and protein. Consider a wet food diet to increase water intake. A 2021 study in the Journal of Feline Medicine and Surgery demonstrated that cats with CKD fed a renal diet had significantly longer survival times.
  2. Hydration Strategies: Encourage increased water intake by providing multiple water bowls, a water fountain, and adding water to her food. Subcutaneous fluid administration may be necessary in severe cases.
  3. Environmental Enrichment: Reduce stress by providing Mittens with a safe and comfortable environment, away from the new kitten. Offer scratching posts, toys, and vertical spaces to climb.
  4. Supportive Therapies: Consider supplements such as omega-3 fatty acids, probiotics, and antioxidants to support kidney function and overall health. Consult with a veterinarian regarding the use of phosphate binders if phosphorus levels are high.

For Mittens, the wellness plan includes transitioning her to a renal wet food diet, adding a water fountain, providing her with a quiet space away from the kitten, and supplementing her diet with omega-3 fatty acids. Sarah is committed to implementing these changes.

Applying the 'S': Sustain & Support

The 'S' represents Sustain & Support. This final step focuses on long-term monitoring, adjustments to the wellness plan, and ongoing support for Sarah. Regular communication and follow-up are crucial for ensuring the plan's success.

  1. Monitoring Kidney Function: Schedule regular veterinary check-ups to monitor Mittens' kidney function (creatinine, BUN, phosphorus, SDMA, USG) and adjust the wellness plan as needed.
  2. Assessing Clinical Signs: Continuously monitor Mittens' clinical signs, such as weight, appetite, and hydration status.
  3. Providing Emotional Support: Offer ongoing support and encouragement to Sarah, addressing any concerns or challenges she may face.
  4. Adjusting the Wellness Plan: Be prepared to make adjustments to the wellness plan based on Mittens' response and Sarah's feedback.

In Mittens' case, Sarah will monitor her weight, appetite, and water intake daily. She will also schedule regular veterinary check-ups every three months to monitor her kidney function. We will provide ongoing support and guidance to Sarah, addressing any challenges she may encounter.

Check Your Understanding

Question 1: What are the key components of the 'A' (Assess & Analyze) step in managing feline CKD?

The key components include reviewing veterinary diagnostics (bloodwork, USG), staging CKD according to IRIS guidelines, assessing clinical signs, and evaluating the cat's quality of life.

Question 2: Why is nutritional management so crucial in feline CKD, and what dietary recommendations are typically made?

Nutritional management is crucial because it can significantly impact the progression of CKD and the cat's survival time. Recommendations typically include a kidney-friendly diet low in phosphorus and protein, and a wet food diet to increase water intake.

Question 3: Explain the importance of the 'S' (Sustain & Support) phase in the P.A.W.S. Framework‚Ñ¢ for long-term CKD management.

The 'S' phase is crucial for ensuring the long-term success of the wellness plan. It involves regular monitoring of kidney function and clinical signs, providing ongoing emotional support to the owner, and being prepared to adjust the wellness plan as needed based on the cat's response and the owner's feedback.
