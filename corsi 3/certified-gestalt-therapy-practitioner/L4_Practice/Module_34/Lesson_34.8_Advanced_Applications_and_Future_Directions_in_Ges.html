Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah. It's lovely to connect with you. I saw you were interested in the Empowered Aging program. What resonated with you about it?"
YOU:
"I understand you're recently retired. How are you finding that transition?"
Phase 2: Exploring Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about what you're looking for in this next chapter. What does 'empowered aging' mean to you?"
YOU:
"What activities or roles have given you the most fulfillment in the past?"
YOU:
"What's missing in your life right now?"
Phase 3: Program Overview & Benefits 10 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Susan, I think the Empowered Aging program could be a great fit. It's designed to help women like you rediscover their passions, build meaningful connections, and create a fulfilling life after retirement."
YOU:
"The program includes weekly group sessions, guest speakers, and a supportive online community. We focus on things like volunteering, creative expression, lifelong learning, and sharing wisdom with younger generations. Many of our participants have started new businesses, mentored young people, or become active in local charities."
YOU:
"How does that sound to you so far?"
Phase 4: Addressing Concerns & Enrollment 5 min
YOU:
"Do you have any questions about the program or how it works?"
YOU:
"The investment for the 10-week program is $997, and we also have a payment plan option available. We've found that the connections, purpose, and renewed sense of self are invaluable for our participants. Would you like to explore the payment options and secure your spot in the program?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I understand. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. Think about the cost of feeling isolated, the lost opportunities, and the lack of purpose. This program provides a structured path to overcome those challenges. We also have a payment plan to make it more accessible. Would you like me to share those details?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"That's a valid concern. Each session is 2 hours per week, and there's some optional homework. But think about how you're currently spending your time. Is it truly fulfilling? This program is an investment in yourself and your future happiness. Can you carve out 2 hours a week for that?"
"I'm worried I won't fit in."
"We create a very welcoming and supportive environment. All the women in the group are in a similar stage of life, looking for connection and purpose. I'm confident you'll find kindred spirits and build lasting friendships. We also have a pre-program onboarding call to ease any anxieties. How does that sound?"
"Can I get a discount?"
"While I don't offer discounts, I can highlight our payment plan option, which breaks the investment into smaller, more manageable installments. This allows you to access the full program benefits without a large upfront cost. Would you like me to share the details of the payment plan?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to cover in an enrollment call for a group program?

Building rapport, exploring needs, presenting the program's benefits, addressing concerns, and confidently stating the price.

2. How should you handle budget objections during an enrollment call?

Acknowledge the concern, highlight the program's value, offer payment plan options, and reframe the investment as a solution to a problem.
