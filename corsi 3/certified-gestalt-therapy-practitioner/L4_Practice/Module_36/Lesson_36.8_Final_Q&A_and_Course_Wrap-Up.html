Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand, Maria. It's a big decision. What aspects are you wanting to consider further? Perhaps I can provide some clarity."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate your honesty. Thinking long term, what is the cost of staying where you are? My program is an investment in your well-being and future. What kind of return would you like to see on that investment?"
"I don't have time."
"Time is valuable. My program is designed to be efficient and effective. We can work together to create a schedule that fits your needs. What if dedicating just a few hours a week could dramatically improve your quality of life?"
"I'm not sure if it will work for me."
"That's a valid concern. My approach is personalized and based on proven techniques. I offer a free consultation to ensure we're a good fit. What would need to happen for you to feel confident in my ability to help you?"

Case Study: Building a Thriving Practice

Client: Sarah M., 45, Career Changer

Presenting Situation: Sarah, a former teacher, felt overwhelmed with the prospect of starting her own Gestalt therapy practice. She had the clinical skills but lacked confidence in marketing and sales.

Intervention: Sarah focused on building her online presence through social media and networking. She practiced her discovery call script and objection handling techniques. She also invested in professional coaching to boost her confidence.

Outcomes: Within six months, Sarah had a thriving practice with a waitlist. She consistently booked 5-7 clients per month and generated a reliable income of $7,000 - $10,000 per month. Sarah attributes her success to consistent effort, a clear understanding of her value, and a commitment to providing exceptional client experiences.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge the concern, explore the long-term costs of not addressing the problem, and emphasize the value and potential ROI of your program.

Key Takeaways

  • Client acquisition is a skill that can be learned and mastered with practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Confidence in your value and pricing is crucial for successful sales.
  • Consistent effort and a commitment to client success will lead to a thriving practice.
