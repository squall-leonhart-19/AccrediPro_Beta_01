Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Blueprint

Phase 1: Rapport & Intro 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm passionate about helping women like you find balance. To start, what sparked your interest in Gestalt therapy?"
YOU:
"I appreciate you sharing that. It sounds like you're ready to prioritize yourself. I am curious, what are you hoping to achieve from our conversation today?"
Phase 2: Problem Exploration 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe what a typical stressful day looks like for you? What are the main triggers?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried in the past to manage stress, and what were the results?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like without this stress? What's truly important to you that this stress is blocking?"
Phase 3: Solution & Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, from what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with [summarize her key challenges]. Is that an accurate reflection?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because we focus on Gestalt therapy, which addresses the root causes of stress by exploring your present experience and past patterns. We'll work together over 12 weeks to develop personalized strategies. Many of my clients report feeling more grounded and resilient within the first month."
Phase 4: Program & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with you, Susan? Would you like to hear more about the program details and how we can get started?"

Mastering Objections

Handling Common Objections with Grace

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program requires [X] hours per week, and we can tailor sessions to fit your schedule. Think of it as investing in your long-term well-being. A 2023 study in the 'Journal of Occupational Health Psychology' found that individuals who dedicate time to stress management experience a 30% increase in productivity."
"I need to compare other options first."
"That's a smart approach. What specific factors are you comparing? I can provide clarity on how my program addresses those needs. For instance, 90% of my clients report significant improvements in stress levels within 3 months, based on pre- and post-program assessments."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"I get your concern. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, my program is based on proven Gestalt therapy techniques and personalized to your unique situation. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you're not seeing value within the first [X] weeks, we can discuss adjustments or a partial refund."
"I've tried therapy before and it didn't help."
"I hear you and appreciate you sharing that vulnerability. Gestalt therapy is different in that it focuses on the present moment and your immediate experience, rather than solely analyzing the past. We actively work on your awareness and how you interact with your current challenges. Would you be open to experiencing a brief exercise to see how it feels different?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the recommended discovery call structure?

The four phases are: Rapport & Intro, Problem Exploration, Solution & Approach, and Program & Close.

2. How can you address the objection "I'm not sure if I have the time?"

Emphasize the flexibility of the program, the potential for increased productivity, and the importance of investing in long-term well-being. You can also quantify the time commitment.

3. What is the key difference between Gestalt therapy and other therapy approaches you can highlight to a potential client?

Gestalt therapy focuses on the present moment and immediate experience, rather than solely analyzing the past.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls into clear phases: rapport, problem exploration, solution, and close.
  • Address client objections with empathy, data, and a focus on their desired outcomes.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and highlight the value and return on investment.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently guide prospects toward enrollment.
  • Visualize your income potential to stay motivated and focused on your business goals.
