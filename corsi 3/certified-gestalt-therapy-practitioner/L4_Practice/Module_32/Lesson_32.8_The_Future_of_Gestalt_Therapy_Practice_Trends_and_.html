YOU:

YOU:
"The Gestalt approach I use focuses on the 'here and now' and helps you become more aware of your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. We'll work together to identify the root causes of your anxiety and develop practical strategies for managing it. Many of my clients report feeling significantly calmer and more empowered within just a few weeks."
Phase 4: Closing the Call 5 min
YOU:
"Maria, based on what you've shared, I truly believe I can help you. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Wonderful! Let's discuss the program details and how you can get started."

Handling Common Objections

Addressing Concerns with Confidence

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. The program requires [number] hours per week. However, think of this as an investment in your well-being. By reducing your anxiety, you'll likely become more productive and efficient in other areas of your life. Plus, we can tailor the sessions to fit your schedule."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can tell you that I'm fully committed to your success. I provide personalized support and guidance every step of the way. And, I have many client testimonials you can review. Ultimately, your success depends on your willingness to engage in the process."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a flexible payment plan to make the program more accessible. Instead of paying the full amount upfront, you can spread the cost over [number] months. Let me explain the details..."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a 30-minute discovery call?

The four phases are: Rapport & Connection, Exploring the Challenge, Presenting Your Solution, and Closing the Call.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I'm not sure if I can afford it"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment as a solution to their problem. You can also discuss payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process is essential for effective client acquisition.
  • Building rapport and understanding the client's challenges are crucial for establishing trust.
  • Confidently presenting your solution and handling objections can increase your conversion rate.
  • Practicing your pricing and call-to-action will make you more comfortable and persuasive.
  • Realistic income goals are achievable with consistent effort and effective sales strategies.
