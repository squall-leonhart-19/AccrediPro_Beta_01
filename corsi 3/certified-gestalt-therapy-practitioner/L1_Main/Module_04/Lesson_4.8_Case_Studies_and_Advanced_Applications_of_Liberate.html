Maria, 52

Former Teacher, Denver, CO ‚Ä¢ Divorced, one grown child

Chief Complaint
Feeling "stuck" and unfulfilled since retiring from teaching. Wants to learn something new but feels overwhelmed.
Background
Taught elementary school for 28 years. Enjoys reading, gardening, and spending time with her granddaughter.

Signed up for an online photography course but didn't complete it. Bought several books on coding but found them too confusing.

"I want to feel excited about learning again. I want to find something that challenges me and gives me a sense of purpose."

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your experience with me. It sounds like you have a strong desire to learn and grow, but you're feeling blocked. Is that accurate?"
MARIA:
"Yes, exactly! I feel like I *should* be learning something, but I just don't know where to start. It's frustrating."
YOU:
"I understand. Many people experience that feeling of overwhelm, especially when trying something new. Let's explore what's getting in the way of your learning. What comes to mind when you think about past learning experiences?"
MARIA:
"Well, with the photography course, I felt like I didn't have enough time. And with the coding books, I just felt stupid. Like everyone else understood it but me."
YOU:
"Okay, so we have time constraints and feelings of inadequacy. Those are powerful obstacles. Let's reframe this. Instead of focusing on the *what* (what you should learn), let's focus on the *how* (how you learn best). How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That actually sounds...relieving. I haven't thought about it that way."
YOU:
"Great! Now, let's explore your learning preferences. Do you prefer learning alone or in a group? Do you learn best by reading, watching, or doing?"

Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

1

Read the script silently first

Understand the flow and the intention behind each statement, especially the shift from "what" to "how."

2

Pretend Maria is sitting across from you. Use a warm, encouraging tone. Make eye contact and nod to show you're listening.

3

Use your phone to record, then listen back. Notice if you sound genuinely curious and supportive.

4

Adapt it to your voice

Once you've practiced the script, modify it to sound like YOU. Keep the structure, but use your own words and examples.

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief: "I feel like you understand what I'm going through."
  • She starts brainstorming ideas about her learning style.

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary shift in focus encouraged in this scenario?

The primary shift is from focusing on *what* the client should learn to focusing on *how* the client learns best.

2. Why is it important to validate the client's feelings of frustration or inadequacy?

Validating their feelings builds trust and creates a safe space for exploration. It acknowledges their experience and reduces resistance.

You're Becoming a Real Coach!

By practicing this dialogue, you're developing the skills to help clients unlock their learning potential. You're not just teaching; you're empowering them to learn in a way that works for them!

Key Takeaways

  • Shift the focus from *what* to learn to *how* to learn.
  • Validate the client's feelings of frustration and inadequacy.
  • Explore the client's learning preferences and obstacles.
  • Empower the client to take ownership of their learning journey.
