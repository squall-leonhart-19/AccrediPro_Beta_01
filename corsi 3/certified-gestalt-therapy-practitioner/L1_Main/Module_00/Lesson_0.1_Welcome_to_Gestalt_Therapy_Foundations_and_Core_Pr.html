If you're in your 40s, 50s, or beyond â€“ you're EXACTLY who we need! Your life experience is your superpower. Don't believe me? Just look at these amazing women who have successfully transitioned into Gestalt therapy later in life:

Case Study: Maria Rodriguez

Maria, a 52-year-old former marketing executive, felt burned out and unfulfilled. After raising her children, she decided to pursue her lifelong dream of becoming a therapist. Despite initial doubts about her age and lack of formal training, Maria enrolled in a Gestalt therapy program. Her life experience and strong interpersonal skills proved invaluable. She quickly built a thriving practice specializing in helping women navigate mid-life transitions. Maria's clients consistently praise her empathy and ability to connect on a deep level.

Case Study: Susan Davies

Susan, a 48-year-old retired teacher, felt a calling to help others heal from trauma. She had personally experienced loss and hardship, giving her a unique understanding of human suffering. Susan's age and experience were assets. Her clients appreciated her calm demeanor, wisdom, and ability to create a safe space for vulnerability. Susan now runs a successful practice focused on trauma-informed Gestalt therapy, empowering survivors to reclaim their lives.

These are just two examples of countless women who have successfully reinvented themselves as Gestalt therapists later in life. You have what it takes. Your age is an asset, not a liability. Embrace your unique experiences and trust that you're exactly where you're meant to be.

What You'll Learn & Become

This program isn't just about learning theory; it's about undergoing a profound personal transformation. You'll not only gain the knowledge and skills to become a Certified Gestalt Therapy Practitionerâ„¢, but you'll also experience significant growth in your own self-awareness, emotional intelligence, and interpersonal effectiveness.

Here's a glimpse of what you'll learn and become:

  • Master the Core Principles of Gestalt Therapy: You'll learn the fundamental concepts of awareness, contact, experiment, and the here-and-now, and how to apply them effectively in your clinical work.
  • Develop Advanced Clinical Skills: You'll acquire practical techniques for working with a wide range of client issues, including anxiety, depression, trauma, and relationship problems.
  • Cultivate Deep Empathy and Compassion: You'll learn to truly understand your clients' experiences and create a safe and supportive therapeutic environment.
  • Enhance Your Self-Awareness: You'll explore your own beliefs, values, and patterns of behavior, allowing you to become a more authentic and effective therapist.
  • Build a Thriving Practice: You'll gain the business and marketing skills you need to attract clients and create a sustainable practice.

By the end of this program, you'll not only be a skilled Gestalt therapist, but you'll also be a more confident, compassionate, and fulfilled individual. You'll have the tools to make a real difference in the lives of others, while also creating a life that you truly love.

Your Cohort & Community

One of the most valuable aspects of this program is the sense of community you'll find here. You're not alone on this journey. You'll be surrounded by a supportive cohort of fellow students who are just as passionate about Gestalt therapy as you are. Together, you'll learn, grow, and support each other every step of the way.

Here's how you can connect with your community:

  • Live Q&A Sessions: Attend our weekly live Q&A sessions with Sarah and other experienced Gestalt therapists. This is a great opportunity to ask questions, share your experiences, and learn from others.
  • Online Forum: Join our exclusive online forum, where you can connect with fellow students, share resources, and discuss challenging cases.
  • Peer Practice Groups: Participate in peer practice groups, where you can practice your skills and receive feedback from your peers.
  • Mentorship Program: Connect with a mentor who can provide guidance and support throughout your journey.

Don't be afraid to reach out and connect with your fellow students. Building strong relationships with your cohort will not only enhance your learning experience but also provide you with a valuable network of support that will last long after you complete the program.

First Steps

Okay, are you ready to dive in? Here are some concrete first steps you can take to maximize your learning experience:

  1. Complete Your Profile: Make sure your profile is complete with a picture and a brief bio. This will help you connect with your fellow students.
  2. Introduce Yourself in the Forum: Head over to the online forum and introduce yourself to the community. Share your background, your goals, and what you're hoping to get out of the program.
  3. Review the Course Syllabus: Familiarize yourself with the course syllabus so you know what to expect in the coming weeks.
  4. Join the Next Live Q&A Session: Mark your calendar for the next live Q&A session and come prepared with questions.

These simple steps will help you get off to a strong start and set you up for success in the program. Remember, you're not alone on this journey. We're here to support you every step of the way. Welcome to the family!
