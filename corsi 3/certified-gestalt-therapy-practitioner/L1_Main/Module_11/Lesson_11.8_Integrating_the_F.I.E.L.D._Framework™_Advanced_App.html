Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on present awareness. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing this with me. I hear your desire for purpose and excitement. Before we talk about the future, let's bring our attention to the present moment. What are you experiencing right now as you share this?"
MARIA:
"I feel‚Ä¶ a little nervous, actually. And also a sense of relief that I'm finally saying this out loud to someone."
YOU:
"Notice those feelings. Where do you feel the nervousness in your body? And what does the relief feel like? Let's stay with those sensations for a moment."
MARIA:
"The nervousness is in my stomach, kind of tight. The relief‚Ä¶ it's like a weight lifting from my shoulders."
YOU:
"Good. Now, as you stay with those feelings, what thoughts or images come to mind?"
MARIA:
"I see myself standing at the front of my classroom, but the faces of the children are blurry."
YOU:
"What is your awareness now?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria describes her feelings in detail (e.g., "My chest feels tight, like a balloon about to burst.")

Check Your Understanding

1. What Gestalt principle is most emphasized in the coaching script?

Present awareness. The script focuses on Maria's immediate feelings, sensations, and thoughts.

2. Why is it important to avoid jumping to solutions in this scenario?

Jumping to solutions bypasses the crucial step of helping Maria understand her underlying feelings and needs. Gestalt therapy prioritizes awareness and exploration before intervention.

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on present awareness before problem-solving.
  • Use open-ended questions to encourage exploration.
  • Validate the client's experience and feelings.
  • Allow space for silence and reflection.
  • Trust the process of awareness to guide the client toward insight.
