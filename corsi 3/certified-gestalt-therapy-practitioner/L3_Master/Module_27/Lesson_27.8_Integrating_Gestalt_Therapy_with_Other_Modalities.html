Her Question
"I had a client who got really angry during our session. I froze. How can I handle that better?"

The Case She Presents

Lisa's Client Case

What Lisa Tells You:

"I was working with Sarah, 50, who is dealing with grief after losing her husband. During our session, I encouraged her to express her feelings, and she suddenly became very angry, yelling about how unfair life is. I didn't know what to do, so I just sat there quietly until she calmed down. I feel like I failed her."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What specific techniques did Lisa use to encourage expression?
  • How did Lisa manage her own emotional response to Sarah's anger?
  • Did Lisa explore the underlying needs or unmet expectations behind the anger?
  • What is Lisa's understanding of the Gestalt approach to anger?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Lisa

1

Normalize the Experience

Reassure Lisa that feeling overwhelmed by client emotions is common, especially for new practitioners. It's a valuable learning opportunity.

2

Teach Grounding Techniques

Explain the importance of staying grounded in the present moment when clients express intense emotions. Share techniques like deep breathing, body scans, or focusing on sensory details.

3

Explore the Function of Anger

Help Lisa understand that anger is often a secondary emotion, masking underlying feelings like fear, sadness, or vulnerability. Encourage her to explore what Sarah's anger might be protecting.

4

Review Gestalt Techniques for Working with Anger

Discuss techniques like the empty chair, exaggeration, and staying with the feeling. Explain how these techniques can help clients integrate their anger and access deeper emotions.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Lisa, thank you for sharing this experience. It takes courage to admit when we feel stuck. It sounds like you were really present with Sarah's pain."
Ask Before Telling
"What was going through your mind when Sarah started yelling? What did you notice in your body?"
Teach the Principle
"In Gestalt therapy, we see emotions as energy that needs to be expressed. Sometimes, that expression can be intense. Our job is to create a safe space for that expression and help clients understand what their emotions are telling them."
Empower Next Steps
"What feels like the most important thing for you to focus on in your next session with Sarah? What skills do you want to practice?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to validate when a mentee brings a challenging case?

Validate their willingness to seek guidance and their commitment to their clients.

2. What Gestalt principle is most relevant when a client expresses intense emotions?

The principle of present moment awareness, staying with the feeling, and exploring the emotion's function.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not directing.
  • Focus on the mentee's experience and promote self-reflection.
  • Use challenging cases as opportunities to reinforce Gestalt principles.
  • Validation and encouragement are essential for building confidence.
