The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Challenge

Aisha's Description:

"I'm working with Sarah, 48, who reminds me so much of my mother. I find myself being overly cautious and trying to 'fix' her problems. Sarah often comments on how 'wise' I am, even though I'm just starting out. I feel uncomfortable and unsure how to proceed."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Aisha's awareness of her own transference.
  • Strategies for maintaining professional boundaries.
  • Techniques for addressing the client's projections.
  • How to use the therapeutic relationship as a mirror.

Your Teaching Approach

Essential Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Acknowledge and Normalize

"It's common for new practitioners to experience these dynamics. The key is awareness and intentionality."

2

Explore Aisha's Transference

Guide her to reflect on her relationship with her mother and how it might be influencing her interactions with Sarah.

3

Teach Boundary Setting

Discuss techniques for gently redirecting the client's projections and maintaining a professional distance.

4

Emphasize Self-Reflection

Encourage Aisha to use supervision as a space to process her feelings and gain clarity on her role in the therapeutic relationship.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive and Supportive Feedback

Begin with Affirmation
"Aisha, I commend you for recognizing this dynamic. It takes courage and self-awareness to bring this to supervision. Let's explore it together."
Inquire About Her Experience
"Before I offer suggestions, what feelings or thoughts come up for you when Sarah makes those comments? What's your immediate reaction?"
Introduce the Concept of Transference
"It sounds like there might be some transference happening on both sides. Sarah might be seeing you as a maternal figure, and you, in turn, might be responding as you would with your mother. This is valuable information for the therapeutic process."
Collaborate on Next Steps
"How would you like to address this in your next session with Sarah? What feels authentic and ethical to you? I'm here to support you in finding the best approach."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of supervision in Gestalt therapy?

To enhance the practitioner's self-awareness, ethical practice, and clinical skills, ultimately benefiting the client.

2. How can a supervisor help a mentee navigate transference and countertransference?

By providing a safe space for the mentee to explore their feelings, identify patterns, and develop strategies for maintaining professional boundaries.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a collaborative process focused on the mentee's professional development.
  • Addressing transference and countertransference is crucial for ethical practice.

  • Your role as a supervisor is to empower new practitioners to thrive.
