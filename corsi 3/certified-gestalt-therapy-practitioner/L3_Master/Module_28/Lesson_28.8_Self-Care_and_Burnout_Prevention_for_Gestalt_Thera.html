Maria, New L1 Graduate

Excited to apply Gestalt principles but struggling with a complex client.

Background
Former teacher, strong foundation in communication, limited experience with crisis situations.
Strengths
Excellent at building rapport, creating a safe space for clients.
Growth Areas
Anxious about handling intense emotions, needs guidance on setting boundaries.
Her Question
"I feel overwhelmed by my client's trauma history. How can I stay present and helpful?"

The Case She Presents

Maria's Client: Sarah, Trauma Survivor

What Maria Tells You:

"Sarah, 52, is struggling with complex PTSD after years of abuse. She becomes easily triggered and dissociates during sessions. I'm afraid of making things worse. I feel like I'm not equipped to help her."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Maria practicing within her scope of competence?
  • Does Maria have a vicarious trauma support system in place?
  • Is Maria using appropriate grounding techniques with Sarah?
  • Is Maria aware of her own countertransference in this case?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Maria

1

Acknowledge Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when working with trauma. It's a sign you care deeply. Let's focus on practical steps."

2

Emphasize Scope of Competence

Discuss the importance of recognizing limitations and referring when necessary. A 2018 study in the "Journal of Traumatic Stress" found that therapists who exceeded their competence level reported higher rates of burnout (p < 0.05).

3

Review Grounding Techniques

Share specific, practical grounding exercises (e.g., 5-4-3-2-1 technique) that Maria can use with Sarah to manage dissociation.

4

Discuss Self-Care & Vicarious Trauma

Highlight the importance of Maria's own self-care to mitigate vicarious trauma. Encourage her to seek supervision and peer support.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Maria, thank you for sharing this case. Your willingness to seek supervision is a strength. Many new practitioners feel this way. You're not alone."
Explore Her Perspective
"What aspects of Sarah's case are most challenging for you? What are your biggest fears in working with her?"
Offer Education & Guidance
"Complex trauma requires specialized training. While you can certainly offer support, it's crucial to ensure Sarah receives appropriate care. Have you considered referring her to a trauma specialist?"
Empower Her Decision
"Ultimately, the decision is yours. But I want you to feel confident that you're providing ethical and effective care. Let's explore resources and referral options together."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important ethical consideration when supervising a new therapist working with trauma?

Ensuring the therapist is practicing within their scope of competence and referring clients when necessary.

2. What are two strategies you can teach your mentee to manage vicarious trauma?

1) Regular self-care practices (mindfulness, exercise, hobbies). 2) Seeking supervision or peer support to process their experiences.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a crucial component of ethical and effective practice, especially with complex cases.
  • New therapists often feel overwhelmed; validation and support are essential.
  • Prioritize scope of competence and referral when necessary.
  • Teach practical tools and techniques for managing challenging client presentations.
  • Promote self-care and awareness of vicarious trauma.
