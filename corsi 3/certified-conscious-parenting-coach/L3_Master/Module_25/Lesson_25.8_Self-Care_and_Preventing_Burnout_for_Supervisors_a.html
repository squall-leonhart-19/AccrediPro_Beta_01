The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client: Emily

The Situation:

Sarah is working with Emily, a 38-year-old mother of two. Emily reports feeling overwhelmed and constantly yelling at her children. She feels guilty but can't seem to change her behavior. Sarah has tried reflective listening and positive reinforcement techniques, but Emily hasn't made much progress.

Sarah's Concerns:

  • "I'm not sure if I'm being effective. Emily seems resistant to my suggestions."
  • "I'm starting to feel frustrated myself. Is this client a good fit for me?"
  • "Am I missing something? Should I be using different techniques?"

Key Areas for Exploration:

  • Explore Emily's underlying beliefs about parenting and her own childhood experiences.
  • Assess Sarah's boundary-setting skills and ability to maintain objectivity.
  • Review Sarah's use of advanced reframing techniques.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reframe "Resistance" as Information

Help Sarah understand that client resistance is valuable data. It often indicates a deeper, unmet need or limiting belief.

2

Deepen Exploration of Limiting Beliefs

Guide Sarah to use advanced questioning techniques to uncover Emily's beliefs about "good" parenting, discipline, and her own worthiness.

3

Reinforce Boundary-Setting Skills

Discuss the importance of Sarah maintaining her own emotional well-being and setting clear boundaries with clients to avoid burnout.

4

Model Advanced Reframing Techniques

Demonstrate how to reframe Emily's negative statements into opportunities for growth and self-compassion. For example, "I'm a terrible mother" could be reframed as "I'm a mother who is striving to do her best and is willing to seek help."

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Sarah's Efforts
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about your clients, and I commend you for seeking support. It takes courage to ask for help, and it shows you're committed to being an effective coach."
Ask About Her Perspective
"Before I offer my suggestions, what are your thoughts on why Emily might be struggling to make progress? What's your intuition telling you?"
Offer Reframing Strategies
"Sometimes, when a client is resistant, it's a sign that we need to dig deeper into their underlying beliefs. Have you explored Emily's own experiences as a child and how they might be influencing her parenting?"
Empower Her Next Steps
"What small step could you take in your next session with Emily to explore those underlying beliefs? Remember, progress doesn't always have to be linear. Sometimes, it's about uncovering hidden obstacles."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to remember when a mentee presents a challenging client case?

Focus on the coach's growth and development, not just the client's outcome.

2. How can you help a mentee reframe client resistance?

Help them see resistance as valuable information about unmet needs or limiting beliefs.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not directing.
  • Client resistance is a valuable source of information.
  • Boundary-setting is crucial for coach well-being.
  • Reframing can empower both coach and client.
