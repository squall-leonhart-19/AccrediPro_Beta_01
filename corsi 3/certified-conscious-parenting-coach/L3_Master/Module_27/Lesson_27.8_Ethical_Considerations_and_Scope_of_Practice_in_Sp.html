The Scenario

Sarah's Dilemma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a mom, Emily, who is really struggling with her 7-year-old's tantrums. I gave her some strategies, but she texts me almost daily with updates or questions. I want to be supportive, but it's becoming too much. I'm not sure how to set boundaries without seeming uncaring."

Key Considerations:

  • Is Sarah confusing support with being available 24/7?
  • How can she set boundaries while maintaining a strong client relationship?
  • What are the ethical considerations of responding to texts outside of sessions?
  • How can Sarah empower Emily to become more self-reliant?

Crafting Your Feedback

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, this is a common challenge for new coaches. Your desire to support Emily is admirable. Let's find a way to do it sustainably."
Explore the Underlying Issue
"What's driving Emily's need for constant contact? Is she feeling insecure? Overwhelmed? Understanding the root cause will help you guide her."
Offer a Practical Solution

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary risk of failing to set boundaries as a mentor?

Burnout for the mentor and dependency from the mentee. Sustainable growth requires healthy boundaries.

2. How can you balance support with empowering a new coach to develop their own style?

By asking questions, encouraging self-reflection, and celebrating their instincts, rather than simply giving answers.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not rescuing.
  • Effective supervision requires clear boundaries.
  • Empower new coaches to find their own voice.
  • Celebrate small wins to build confidence.
  • Your leadership shapes the future of Conscious Parenting.
