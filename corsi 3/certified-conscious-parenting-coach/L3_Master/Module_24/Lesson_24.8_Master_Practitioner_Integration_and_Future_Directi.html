The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: The Overwhelmed Mom

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, a 38-year-old mom of two. She's completely overwhelmed, constantly yelling, and feels like a failure. I've given her some strategies, but she keeps saying they're too hard and she doesn't have time. I feel like I'm not helping at all."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah explored Emily's support system and self-care practices?
  • Are the strategies realistic for Emily's current capacity?
  • Has Sarah addressed Emily's underlying beliefs about motherhood?
  • Is Sarah getting pulled into Emily's emotional state?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Self-Care for the Coach

Remind Sarah that she can't pour from an empty cup. Discuss strategies for managing her own emotional responses to challenging clients.

2

Reframe Resistance as Information

Explain that resistance isn't necessarily a sign of failure. It's valuable feedback that the approach needs to be adjusted. Perhaps the strategies are too complex or not aligned with Emily's values.

3

Explore Underlying Beliefs

Guide Sarah to explore Emily's beliefs about what it means to be a "good mom." Are these beliefs realistic and supportive, or are they contributing to her overwhelm?

4

Teach Session Structuring

Help Sarah structure her sessions for better time management. Suggest time limits for sharing/venting, strategy discussion, and action planning.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Sarah, thank you so much for bringing this to me. Your willingness to seek support is a sign of a truly dedicated coach. Let's unpack this together."
Acknowledge the Challenge
"Working with overwhelmed parents can be incredibly draining. It's important to acknowledge that this is a tough case, and it's okay to feel challenged."
Offer a New Perspective
"Sometimes, when clients are resistant, it's a sign that we need to shift our approach. Instead of pushing harder, we can try exploring their underlying beliefs and values. What does Emily believe about what it means to be a 'good mom'?"
Suggest a Small Action
"What's one small thing you could try differently in your next session with Emily? Maybe just dedicate the first 10 minutes to exploring her beliefs about motherhood."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to remember when mentoring a new practitioner?

To create a safe space for learning and growth, focusing on their development rather than immediate perfection.

2. How can you help a mentee who is feeling overwhelmed by a challenging client?

By validating their feelings, helping them reframe the situation, and suggesting small, manageable actions they can take.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves creating a supportive environment for learning and growth.
  • Resistance from clients can be valuable feedback, indicating a need to adjust strategies.
  • Exploring underlying beliefs is crucial for addressing resistance and promoting lasting change.
  • Effective mentors prioritize self-care and model healthy boundaries.
  • Celebrating small wins and focusing on growth fosters confidence and competence.
