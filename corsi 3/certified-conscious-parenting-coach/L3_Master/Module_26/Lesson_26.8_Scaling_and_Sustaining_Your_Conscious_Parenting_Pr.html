Her Question
"I'm struggling with a client who constantly interrupts me and dismisses my suggestions. How can I handle this?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: The Interrupting Parent

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, a 38-year-old mother of two. During our sessions, Emily frequently interrupts me, questions my expertise, and dismisses my suggestions for implementing conscious parenting techniques. I feel like I'm not getting through to her, and it's affecting my confidence."

Key Considerations:

  • Explore the client's underlying beliefs and resistance.
  • Assess Sarah's boundary-setting skills.
  • Identify strategies for redirecting the client's behavior.
  • Ensure Sarah feels empowered and supported.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Sarah's Experience

"It's completely normal to feel challenged by clients who exhibit resistance. Your feelings are valid."

2

Explore the Client's Perspective

Encourage Sarah to consider why Emily might be resisting. Is she feeling overwhelmed? Scared of change? Does she have past experiences that are influencing her behavior?

3

Teach Boundary-Setting Techniques

Help Sarah develop strategies for gently but firmly redirecting Emily's interruptions and disruptive behavior. Role-playing can be helpful here.

4

Emphasize the Importance of Self-Care

Remind Sarah that it's crucial to protect her own energy and well-being when working with challenging clients. Encourage her to practice self-compassion and seek support when needed.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for seeking guidance on this. It shows your commitment to providing the best possible support to your clients."
Ask About Her Strategies
"Before I offer suggestions, what strategies have you already tried to address Emily's interruptions? What felt effective, and what didn't?"
Teach a Principle
"Remember, resistance is often a sign that a client is feeling vulnerable or overwhelmed. Instead of taking it personally, try to approach it with curiosity and compassion."
Empower Her to Take Action
"Based on our discussion, what's one small step you can take in your next session with Emily to address her interruptions and create a more productive environment?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of providing supervision to a new practitioner?

The primary goal is to support their professional development, build their confidence, and ensure they provide ethical and effective services to their clients.

2. What are some effective strategies for addressing resistance or defensiveness in a mentee?

Strategies include validating their feelings, exploring their underlying concerns, asking open-ended questions, and reframing the situation in a more positive light.
