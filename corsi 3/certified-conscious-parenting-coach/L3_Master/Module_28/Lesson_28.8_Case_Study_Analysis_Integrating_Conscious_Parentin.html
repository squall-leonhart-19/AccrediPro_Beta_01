The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I was working with Emily, a mother of an 8-year-old with ADHD. During the session, Emily revealed she's been feeling overwhelmed, like she's failing as a parent. She started crying, and I just felt so bad for her. I spent almost the entire session comforting her, and I'm not sure if I actually helped her."

What You Need to Explore:

  • How did Sarah balance empathy with setting boundaries for the session?
  • Did Sarah help Emily shift from emotional overwhelm to problem-solving?
  • Did Sarah explore what resources Emily has available?
  • How did Sarah take care of herself after this emotionally charged session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Empathy vs. Over-Involvement

Discuss the importance of empathy while maintaining professional boundaries. Over-involvement can be draining for the coach and disempowering for the client.

2

The Art of Reframing

Teach Sarah how to reframe negative emotions into actionable steps. Help Emily move from "I'm failing" to "What strategies can I try?".

3

Resource Identification

Guide Sarah to explore what resources are available to Emily. This could include support groups, online resources, or respite care.

4

Self-Care Strategies

Emphasize the importance of self-care for coaches. Discuss techniques like mindfulness, exercise, or debriefing with a colleague.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Empathy
"Sarah, it's clear you have a huge heart. Your empathy is one of your greatest strengths. It's wonderful that you can connect with your clients on such a deep level."
Introduce the Concept of "Holding Space"
"Sometimes, the most powerful thing we can do is simply 'hold space' for our clients. This means being present, listening without judgment, and allowing them to feel their emotions without trying to fix them."
Offer Alternative Strategies
"Next time, you might try saying something like, 'I hear how overwhelmed you're feeling, Emily. Let's take a few deep breaths together. When you're ready, we can explore some strategies that might help.' This acknowledges her feelings while gently guiding her towards problem-solving."
Empower Her to Set Boundaries
"Remember, you can't pour from an empty cup. It's okay to say, 'I'm here for you, and we have X minutes left in our session. Let's focus on what we can accomplish in that time.' Setting boundaries is not selfish; it's essential for your well-being and your client's progress."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key differences between empathy and over-involvement when coaching?

Empathy is understanding and sharing the feelings of another, while maintaining professional boundaries. Over-involvement is when the coach becomes too emotionally invested, blurring those boundaries and potentially hindering the client's progress.

2. Why is self-care important for Conscious Parenting Coaches, especially when dealing with complex cases?

Self-care prevents burnout, maintains objectivity, and ensures the coach can provide the best possible support to their clients. Complex cases can be emotionally draining, making self-care even more crucial.
