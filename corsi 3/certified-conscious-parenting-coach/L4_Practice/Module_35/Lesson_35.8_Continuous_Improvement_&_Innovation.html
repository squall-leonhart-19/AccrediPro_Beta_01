Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for coaching."
"I understand. Let's look at your week together. My clients find that dedicating just 1-2 hours a week to coaching actually *creates* more time, because they're spending less time dealing with conflicts and feeling stressed. What would it be worth to reclaim those hours?"
"I'm worried it won't work for my child."
"That's a valid concern. Every child is different, and that‚Äôs why my program is highly personalized. We'll tailor the strategies to your son's specific needs and personality. I also have a satisfaction guarantee - if you are not fully satisfied after the first month, I will refund your investment."
"It's more than I was expecting to invest."
"I appreciate that. Let's talk about the value you're getting. This isn't just about quick fixes; it's about creating lasting change in your family dynamic and equipping you with tools for a lifetime. Many of my clients consider it an investment in their family's well-being. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible. Would you like to discuss that?"
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (because every family and child are unique), I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the highest quality coaching and support. My clients who fully engage in the process and implement the strategies consistently see positive changes in their relationships with their children. I encourage you to view coaching as a collaborative partnership, where we work together to achieve your desired outcomes."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, uncovering challenges, presenting your approach, and transitioning to enrollment.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge the concern, then focus on the value and long-term benefits of your coaching program. Offer flexible payment options if available.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Address objections with empathy and confidence, highlighting the value of your coaching.
  • Practice stating your prices clearly and confidently.
  • Focus on the transformative impact your coaching can have on families.
