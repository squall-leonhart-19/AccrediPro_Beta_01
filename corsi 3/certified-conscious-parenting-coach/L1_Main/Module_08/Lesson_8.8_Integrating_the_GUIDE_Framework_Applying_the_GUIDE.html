Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on uncovering patterns. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Karen, thank you for sharing. Bedtime struggles can be so draining. Let's explore some patterns. I'm curious ‚Äì what's the *typical* evening routine look like on nights when your son resists bedtime?"
KAREN:
"Well, I get home around 6:30 pm. We have dinner, try to do homework, and then it's supposed to be bath and bedtime by 8:00. But he always fights me."
YOU:
"Okay, and what happens *right before* the bedtime routine starts? Is there anything specific that tends to trigger the resistance?"
KAREN:
"Usually, it's when I tell him to turn off the TV or stop playing video games. He gets really upset."
YOU:
"That's helpful. Now, let's look at *who* is involved. Is it always you putting him to bed, or does your husband sometimes handle it? And how does your daughter react to all of this?"
YOU:
"By understanding these patterns ‚Äì the *when*, the *what*, and the *who* ‚Äì we can start to identify what's truly driving this behavior. Does that make sense?"

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on uncovering patterns (the *when*, *what*, and *who*) before offering solutions.
  • Ask open-ended questions to encourage the client to share their experiences and insights.
  • Explore the entire family dynamic, not just the child's behavior.
  • Listen empathetically and validate the client's feelings.
  • Remember that pattern identification is the foundation for effective coaching.
