Meet Your Client

Maria, 45

Elementary School Teacher, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Single mom with one child (age 8)

Parenting Challenge
Difficulty managing her son's morning routine. He often procrastinates, leading to stress and lateness for school.
Expressed Feelings
Overwhelmed, frustrated, guilty for yelling. "I feel like I'm always nagging him."
Current Strategies
Setting multiple alarms, offering rewards (screen time), occasional threats (taking away privileges).
Desired Outcome
A calmer, more cooperative morning routine with less conflict and more connection.

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on gathering insights. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing. It sounds like mornings are a real challenge right now. To help me understand better, can you walk me through a typical morning, step by step, from the moment your alarm goes off until you leave for school?"
MARIA:
"Ugh, where do I even begin? It's chaos! I hit snooze a few times, then I rush to wake him up. He says he's tired, then he dawdles getting dressed. I'm yelling to hurry up, and then we're scrambling to find his backpack..."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. Now, let's zoom in a bit. When you say he dawdles getting dressed, what does that look like specifically? Is he distracted by something? Is he struggling with buttons? The more details, the better."
MARIA:
"Well, he often starts playing with his toys, or he'll get distracted by the TV. Sometimes he says his clothes are itchy, or he can't find his socks."
YOU:
"Got it. And when you offer rewards like screen time, how effective is that, on a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being not effective at all and 10 being completely effective?"
MARIA:
"Maybe a 4 or 5? It works sometimes, but then he expects it every day, and it feels like a bribe."
YOU:
"I understand. And finally, Maria, what do you think your son would say is the hardest part about getting ready in the morning?"
MARIA:
"Oh, that's a good question. I never thought to ask him! Probably that he just wants to sleep more."

Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

1

Read the script silently first

Focus on the intention behind each question. You're seeking specific details.

2

Now speak it out loud

Imagine Maria is on a video call with you. Use a gentle, curious tone. Nod and acknowledge her feelings.

3

Record yourself (optional but powerful)

Listen back. Do you sound genuinely curious? Are you pausing to let her answer fully?

4

Adapt it to your voice

Use your own words to ask similar questions. The goal is to gather detailed insights, not just follow a script.
