Clinical Reasoning Process

Deconstructing the Case

Step 1: Map the Family System
Visualize the family as a system with interconnected parts. Identify subsystems (Carol & her kids, David & his child, the couple). Note the boundaries and power dynamics. Key Insight: The stepchild's behavior may be a symptom of systemic dysfunction.
Step 2: Identify Intergenerational Patterns
Carol's emotional neglect history likely impacts her parenting style and relationship patterns. David's challenges with his ex-wife create additional stress and conflict. Consider: How past trauma influences present-day interactions.
Step 3: Assess Attachment Styles
Explore Carol's and David's attachment styles and how they influence their parenting and relationship. Are they secure, anxious, avoidant, or disorganized? Attachment Theory: Understanding attachment can illuminate communication challenges and emotional reactivity.
Step 4: Prioritize Interventions
Start with couple's work to improve communication and co-parenting. Then address individual trauma and attachment issues. Finally, involve the children in family sessions. Important: Address the foundation (couple relationship) before tackling child-specific issues.

Differential Considerations

Considering Other Factors

1

Adolescent Development

The 15-year-old's defiance could be typical adolescent behavior, exacerbated by the blended family situation. Rule out substance use or mental health issues.

2

Parental Alienation

David's ex-wife's undermining behavior could be a form of parental alienation, impacting the teenager's relationship with David.

3

Unresolved Grief

Children may be experiencing unresolved grief related to the divorce of their biological parents. This can manifest as behavioral issues.

4

ADHD/Learning Differences

Consider whether any of the children have undiagnosed ADHD or learning differences contributing to behavioral challenges.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are some potential ethical considerations when coaching blended families?

Maintaining confidentiality for each family member, managing conflicting interests, and ensuring that the coaching process benefits all parties involved are key ethical considerations.

2. How can you help Carol address her feelings of resentment toward her stepchild?

Explore the root of her resentment (e.g., unmet needs, perceived unfairness), validate her feelings, help her develop empathy for the stepchild's perspective, and teach her communication skills to express her needs assertively.

Key Takeaways

  • Blended families present unique challenges related to communication, power dynamics, and intergenerational patterns.
  • A systems-thinking approach is essential for understanding the complexities of these families.
  • Addressing individual trauma and attachment issues can significantly improve family functioning.
  • Knowing when to refer to specialized therapy is crucial for ethical and effective coaching.
