Eleanor, 48

Small business owner, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Married with two children

Presenting Issue
Eleanor seeks coaching for parenting her 16-year-old son, who is struggling with anxiety and potential substance use. She also mentions marital discord related to parenting styles.
Complicating Factor #1
Eleanor is the president of the local PTA, and you are a member of the same organization. You interact with her regularly at school events.
Complicating Factor #2
During a session, Eleanor discloses that her son has confided in her about using marijuana and alcohol. She asks you not to disclose this information to her husband, as he would react harshly.
Complicating Factor #3
Eleanor expresses interest in couples coaching to address marital issues, assuming you can seamlessly transition into that role.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making

Step 1: Identify Ethical Dilemmas
  • Dual Relationship: PTA membership creates a potential conflict of interest and blurs professional boundaries.
  • Confidentiality: Balancing the client's request for confidentiality regarding her son's substance use with ethical obligations to protect the child's well-being.
  • Scope of Practice: Determining whether couples coaching falls within your expertise and training as a parenting coach.
Step 2: Consider Ethical Codes
Refer to the AccrediPro Code of Ethics, focusing on sections related to confidentiality, dual relationships, scope of competence, and informed consent. Principle A: Beneficence and Nonmaleficence dictates that you must act in the best interest of your client and "do no harm".
Step 3: Consult with a Supervisor/Colleague
Discuss the case with a qualified supervisor or experienced colleague to gain an objective perspective and guidance on navigating the ethical complexities. Document the consultation.
Step 4: Develop a Course of Action
Prioritize the client's well-being while upholding ethical standards. This may involve:
  • Addressing the dual relationship by clarifying boundaries and potential conflicts.
  • Determining the appropriate course of action regarding the son's substance use, considering legal and ethical obligations.
  • Referring Eleanor to a qualified couples therapist if couples coaching is outside your scope of practice.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äî Requires Referral

  • Suspected Child Abuse or Neglect: If the son's substance use is linked to abuse or neglect, mandatory reporting laws apply.
  • Suicidal Ideation or Self-Harm: Immediate referral to a mental health professional is required.
  • Severe Substance Use Disorder: Referral to a substance abuse treatment specialist is necessary.
  • Marital Issues Requiring Therapy: If the marital conflict is beyond the scope of parenting coaching, refer to a qualified couples therapist.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key ethical considerations when a client discloses information about their child's risky behavior that they want kept confidential from the other parent?

Balancing client confidentiality with the child's well-being and safety is paramount. Assess the severity of the risk, review confidentiality limits with the client, consult with a supervisor, and consider mandatory reporting laws if applicable. Document all decisions and actions.

2. How should you address a dual relationship with a client, such as being members of the same community organization?

Acknowledge the dual relationship, clearly define the boundaries of the coaching relationship, and discuss potential conflicts of interest. Ensure the client understands the limitations of the relationship and that your professional judgment will not be compromised. If the dual relationship poses a significant risk, consider referring the client to another coach.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are inherent in coaching practice, requiring careful consideration and decision-making.
  • Prioritize the client's well-being while upholding ethical standards and legal obligations.
  • Consult with supervisors or colleagues to gain objective perspectives and guidance.
  • Clearly define boundaries and limitations of the coaching relationship.
  • Refer clients to qualified professionals when issues fall outside your scope of practice.
