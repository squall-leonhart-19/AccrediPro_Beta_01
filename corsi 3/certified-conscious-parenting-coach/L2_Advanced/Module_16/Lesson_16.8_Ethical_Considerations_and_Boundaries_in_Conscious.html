Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Perimenopause

While still menstruating, Eleanor could be experiencing early perimenopausal symptoms, which can significantly impact mood, sleep, and libido. A 2021 study in *Menopause* found that up to 40% of women experience significant mood changes during the perimenopausal transition.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Given her reliance on processed foods, consider deficiencies in key nutrients like magnesium, B vitamins, and omega-3 fatty acids, all of which play critical roles in mood regulation. A 2023 meta-analysis in the *Journal of Affective Disorders* showed a strong correlation between low magnesium levels and increased anxiety symptoms.

3

Adrenal Dysfunction

Chronic stress can lead to imbalances in the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, contributing to fatigue, anxiety, and sleep disturbances. Cortisol Awakening Response (CAR) testing can be useful in assessing HPA axis function.

4

Underlying Autoimmunity

Given her history of endometriosis and migraines, explore the possibility of an underlying autoimmune component contributing to inflammation and systemic symptoms.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases often require a systems-thinking approach to identify interconnected factors.
  • Hormonal imbalances can significantly impact mood, sleep, and cognitive function in women.
  • Lifestyle factors play a crucial role in managing stress, anxiety, and overall well-being.
  • Prioritizing foundational strategies (sleep, stress, diet) is essential before implementing targeted interventions.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare professionals (MDs, therapists) may be necessary to provide comprehensive care.
