Case Study: Maria, 42

Client: Maria, a 42-year-old woman who lost her husband unexpectedly in a car accident.

Presenting Symptoms: Maria presented with symptoms of prolonged grief disorder, including intense yearning for her husband, difficulty accepting his death, and avoidance of places and people associated with him. She also reported feelings of guilt, hopelessness, and social isolation.

Intervention: The intervention involved a combination of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and grief-focused therapy. CBT techniques were used to challenge Maria's negative thoughts and beliefs about her husband's death and her ability to cope. Grief-focused therapy involved exploring Maria's relationship with her husband, processing her emotions, and developing coping strategies to manage her grief.

Outcomes: After 12 weeks of therapy, Maria reported a significant reduction in her symptoms of prolonged grief disorder. She was able to accept her husband's death, engage in meaningful activities, and reconnect with her social support network. She also reported feeling more hopeful about the future.
