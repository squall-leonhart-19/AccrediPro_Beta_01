Your Coaching Scenario

In this Practice Lab, you'll work through a realistic client scenario focused on grief and loss. Read the client profile, review the coaching script, then practice speaking the dialogue out loud.

Meet Your Client

üë©

Eleanor, 52

Librarian, Portland, OR ‚Ä¢ Divorced, one adult child

Chief Complaint
Persistent feelings of sadness and loneliness one year after her mother's death. "I feel stuck."
Background
Eleanor was the primary caregiver for her mother during her final years. She describes their relationship as "complicated but loving."
Current Symptoms
Difficulty sleeping, loss of interest in hobbies, social withdrawal, feelings of guilt about not doing enough for her mother.
Her Goal
"I want to find joy in my life again and feel connected to others. I want to honor my mother without being consumed by grief."

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to reach out, especially when you're feeling so vulnerable. I want you to know that grief is a unique journey, and there's no 'right' or 'wrong' way to feel. What you're experiencing is valid, and I'm here to support you."
ELEANOR:
"Thank you. It's just...everyone else seems to have moved on. I feel like I should be doing better by now."
YOU:
"That's a common feeling, Eleanor. Grief doesn't follow a timeline, and comparing yourself to others can be incredibly painful. Remember, your relationship with your mother was unique, and your grieving process will be too. Let's focus on honoring your mother's memory in a way that feels authentic to you, while also creating space for healing and growth."
YOU:
"I understand you were her caregiver. That adds another layer of complexity to your grief. We can explore those feelings of guilt or regret, if you're open to it. Would that be helpful for you?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, I think so. I keep replaying certain moments in my head, wondering if I could have done things differently."
YOU:
"We can gently unpack those moments and explore the emotions attached to them. My goal is to help you find peace and self-compassion, knowing that you did the best you could with the resources you had at the time. Sound good?"

Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

1

Read the script silently first

Understand the flow and the intention behind each statement. Pay attention to the empathy and validation.

2

Now speak it out loud

Pretend Eleanor is sitting across from you. Use a warm, compassionate tone. Imagine the weight of her grief.

3

Record yourself (optional but powerful)

Use your phone to record, then listen back. Notice if your tone conveys genuine empathy and understanding.

4

Adapt it to your voice

Once you've practiced the script, modify it to sound like YOU. Keep the structure, but use your own words while maintaining the core message of support and validation.

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Eleanor expresses relief that someone understands her feelings.
  • She shares more details about her relationship with her mother.
  • Her body language softens (relaxed shoulders, making eye contact).
  • She acknowledges her guilt and expresses a desire to work through it.
  • She feels a sense of hope that she can find joy in her life again.
