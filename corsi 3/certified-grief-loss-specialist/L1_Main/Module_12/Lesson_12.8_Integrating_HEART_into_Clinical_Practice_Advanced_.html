In this Practice Lab, you'll work through a realistic client scenario focused on grief and loss. Read the client profile, review the coaching script, then practice speaking the dialogue out loud.

Meet Your Client

Eleanor, 52

Librarian, Asheville, NC ‚Ä¢ Widowed, one adult child

Chief Complaint
Persistent feelings of sadness and loneliness, difficulty concentrating, and a sense of being "stuck" since her husband's death two years ago.
Background
Husband passed away unexpectedly from a heart attack. Eleanor was the primary caregiver for her mother before her mother's passing five years ago.
Symptoms
Insomnia, loss of appetite, avoids social gatherings, often replays memories of her husband.
Her Goal
"I want to find joy in life again and feel connected to others. I don't want to just exist."

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to reach out, and I want you to know that your feelings are valid. Two years after losing your husband is still a very real and impactful time in your grief journey. Many people feel pressure to 'move on,' but grief doesn't work on a set timeline."
ELEANOR:
"That's exactly how I feel. Everyone tells me I should be over it by now. But some days, it feels like it just happened yesterday."
YOU:
"I understand. And it's important to honor those feelings. We're not here to erase your grief, but to help you integrate it into your life in a way that allows you to experience joy and connection again. It's about finding a way to carry your love for your husband with you, while also creating space for new experiences."
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I'd like to explore how we can honor your grief, find ways to reconnect with yourself, and identify small steps you can take towards building a more fulfilling life. How does that sound?"
ELEANOR:
"That sounds wonderful. I'm willing to try anything."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by exploring some of the specific memories and emotions that come up for you. This will help us understand what needs to be honored and processed as we move forward. Are you comfortable with that?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Eleanor expresses relief at being heard and understood.
  • She acknowledges that her grief is valid and doesn't have a set timeline.
  • She shows willingness to explore her emotions and memories.
  • She feels a sense of hope, even if it's small.
  • She expresses a desire to take small steps toward reconnecting with herself and others.
