Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand, Susan. It's a significant decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the program structure, the investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to feel confident."
"I'm not sure I can afford this right now."
"I hear you, and I know finances can be a concern. Think about how much you've already spent on things that haven't brought you lasting peace. What if this program could actually transform your grief and allow you to live a more fulfilling life? How much would that be worth to you in terms of emotional well-being and quality of life?"
"I don't have the time."
"I understand that time is precious. However, consider this: How much time are you currently spending feeling lost, overwhelmed, and unable to focus? This program is designed to help you reclaim your time and energy by providing you with effective strategies to manage your grief and move forward."
"Will this really work for me?"
"That's a valid question, Susan. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can tell you that my program is based on proven techniques and the HEART Framework‚Ñ¢, and I provide personalized support every step of the way. I've helped many women in similar situations find peace and rebuild their lives. Are you willing to give yourself the opportunity to experience that transformation?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the recommended 30-minute discovery call?

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, and then ask clarifying questions: "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the program structure, the investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to feel confident."
