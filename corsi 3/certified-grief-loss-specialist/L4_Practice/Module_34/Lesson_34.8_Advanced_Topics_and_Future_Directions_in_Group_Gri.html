Discovery Call Script

"It takes courage to seek support during such a difficult time. How are you doing today, and what prompted you to consider this program?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Grief can bring unexpected expenses. The program is an investment of $497, which includes all materials and weekly sessions. Think of it as an investment in your well-being and future. We also offer a payment plan of two installments of $250. Would that be more manageable for you?"
"I'm worried about sharing my feelings with strangers."
"That's a valid concern. It takes time to build trust. The program is designed to create a safe and supportive environment. I facilitate the group to ensure everyone feels respected and heard. Many participants find that sharing with others who understand their pain is incredibly healing. Plus, studies show that group therapy reduces feelings of isolation. Would you feel more comfortable if I shared some testimonials from past participants?"
"I'm not sure I have the time to commit to weekly sessions."
"I understand that time is precious. The sessions are 90 minutes per week, and the program lasts for 8 weeks. Think of it as carving out time for your healing. The tools and support you'll gain can actually help you manage your time and energy more effectively in the long run. What does your schedule typically look like?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call for a group program?

Expressing empathy, acknowledging their loss, and creating a safe space for them to share their experiences.

2. How can you address concerns about the cost of a group program without discounting your value?

Offer a payment plan, emphasize the long-term benefits of the program, and frame it as an investment in their well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and acknowledging the client's loss are crucial for establishing trust.
  • Understanding the client's needs and challenges is essential for tailoring your program presentation.
  • Sharing testimonials and social proof can help alleviate concerns about the program's effectiveness.
  • Addressing concerns about cost and time commitment with empathy and practical solutions is key to overcoming objections.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and call-to-action can lead to successful program enrollment.
