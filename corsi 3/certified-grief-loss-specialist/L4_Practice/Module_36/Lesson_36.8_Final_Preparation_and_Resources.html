Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Empathy 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. I'm Olivia, and I understand you're going through a difficult time. I'm so sorry for your loss. How are you holding up today?"
YOU:
"It takes courage to reach out, Susan. I want you to know you're in a safe space. Can you tell me a little bit about what's been happening since your mother passed?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Needs 10 min
YOU:
"What are the biggest challenges you're facing right now? How is this grief impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"Have you experienced grief like this before? What kind of support have you tried in the past?"
YOU:
"What would it look like for you to feel supported and at peace through this process, Susan?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 8 min
YOU:
"Susan, I hear you're feeling overwhelmed, struggling with daily tasks, and having trouble sleeping. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on creating a personalized grief journey for you, Susan. We'll explore your feelings, honor your memories, and develop coping strategies to help you navigate this difficult time. Many of my clients report feeling a sense of peace and renewed hope within a few weeks. This isn't about 'getting over' the grief, but about learning to live *with* it, in a healthy way."
YOU:
"I tailor my sessions to meet your unique needs, drawing from various therapeutic techniques. I also create a safe space for you to share your feelings without judgment, and I offer practical tools to manage stress and anxiety."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me tell you about my program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand, Susan. Investing in your healing is an investment in your future. Let's explore some options. I offer payment plans to make it more manageable. What feels like a comfortable investment range for you?"
"I'm not sure if therapy is right for me."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. My approach is about providing support and guidance, not forcing anything. It's about empowering you to find your own path. Would you like to schedule a trial session so you can experience it firsthand?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course, Susan. Take your time. What specifically do you need to consider? I'm happy to provide more information or answer any questions you have."
"I'm worried about opening up to a stranger."
"I understand that vulnerability can be scary, Susan. I want to reassure you that our sessions are confidential and judgment-free. My goal is to create a safe and trusting environment where you feel comfortable sharing your thoughts and feelings. Many clients find that talking to someone objective can be incredibly helpful."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Build Rapport & Empathy" phase of the discovery call?

To create a safe and trusting environment where the prospect feels comfortable sharing their feelings and needs.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I'm not sure if therapy is right for me?"

Reassure the prospect that your approach is about providing support and guidance, not forcing anything. Offer a trial session so they can experience it firsthand.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and understanding are crucial for building trust with potential clients.
  • Be prepared to address common objections with confident and reassuring responses.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your services and how they can help clients achieve their goals.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to confidently state your fees.
  • Remember that client acquisition is an ongoing process that requires persistence and dedication.
