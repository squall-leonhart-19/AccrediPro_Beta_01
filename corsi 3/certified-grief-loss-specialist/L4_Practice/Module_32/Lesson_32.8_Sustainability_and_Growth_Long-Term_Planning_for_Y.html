Handling Objections

Common Concerns & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that budget is a consideration, Susan. I have a few options that might make it more accessible for you. We can explore a payment plan, or I can offer a discounted rate for a shorter program. Would you like to discuss those options?"
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial commitment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"I'm not sure if therapy/coaching really works."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. I can share testimonials from past clients who have found the program incredibly helpful. Also, remember that this is a collaborative process. Your active participation is key. I'm confident that if you're willing to put in the work, you'll see positive results."
"I'm worried about opening up to a stranger."
"That's perfectly normal, Susan. Building trust is essential. I want you to know that I create a safe, non-judgmental space for my clients. Everything you share with me is confidential. My goal is to support you without any pressure. We can start slowly, and you can share what feels comfortable for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the initial minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing empathy, actively listening, finding common ground, and creating a safe space for the prospect to share their story are essential for building rapport.

2. How can you effectively address a prospect's concern about the cost of your services while still conveying the value of your program?

Acknowledge their concern, explore payment options, highlight the long-term value and benefits they'll receive, and share testimonials from satisfied clients. Focus on the transformation you provide, not just the price tag.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial in the initial phase of a discovery call to establish trust and connection.
  • Active listening and empathetic responses are key to understanding the prospect's needs and challenges.
  • Addressing objections with confidence and offering solutions can increase the likelihood of closing the sale.
  • Clearly communicating the value of your services and presenting pricing options transparently builds trust and confidence.
  • Consistent practice of your discovery call script and objection handling techniques will enhance your sales skills and boost your client acquisition rate.
