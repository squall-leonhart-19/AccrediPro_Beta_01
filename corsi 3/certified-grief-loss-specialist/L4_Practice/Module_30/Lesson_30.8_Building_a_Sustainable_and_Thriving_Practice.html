Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Empathetic Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. It's important to be mindful of your finances. I offer a few options to make it more accessible, including payment plans. Would you like me to share those with you? "Of course. Take your time. What specific aspects are you unsure about? Perhaps I can provide some additional information or resources to help you make a more informed decision."

"I understand that time is precious. This program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. The sessions are [length of session] and can be done remotely. Think of it as an investment in your well-being, allowing you to be more present and engaged in all areas of your life."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of an effective discovery call?

The key phases are: Building rapport and empathy, understanding the problem and its impact, presenting your approach and value, and closing with confidence.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I'm not sure I can afford it"?

Acknowledge their concern, offer payment options, and highlight the long-term costs of not addressing their grief.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and genuine connection are crucial for building trust with potential clients.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your services and how they address the client's specific needs.
  • Be prepared to handle common objections with confidence and compassion.
  • Practice stating your prices clearly and confidently.
  • Remember that each client you acquire is an opportunity to make a positive impact on their life.
