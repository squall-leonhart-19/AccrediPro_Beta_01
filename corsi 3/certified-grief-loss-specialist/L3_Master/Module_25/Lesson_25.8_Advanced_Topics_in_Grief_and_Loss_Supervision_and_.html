Her Question
"I just had a session with a young client who lost his grandfather, and I'm not sure if I handled his anger appropriately. Can we talk it through?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: 8-year-old Ben

What Sarah Tells You:

"Ben was lashing out, throwing toys, and yelling about how unfair it was that his grandfather died. I tried to validate his feelings, but I felt like I was just making it worse. I ended up cutting the session short because I didn't know what else to do."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What specific validation techniques did Sarah use? Were they developmentally appropriate?
  • Did she explore the underlying emotions driving Ben's anger (e.g., sadness, fear, guilt)?
  • Did she offer any coping strategies or grounding techniques?
  • How did she manage her own emotional response to Ben's outburst?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Normalize Anger in Grief

Explain that anger is a common and healthy response to loss, especially for children. It's often a way to express feelings they don't have the words for.

2

Developmentally Appropriate Techniques

Discuss using play therapy, art therapy, or storytelling to help children express their grief in non-verbal ways. "Sometimes, actions speak louder than words, especially with children."

3

Coping Strategies for Anger

Teach Sarah about techniques like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or creating a "calm down corner" in her office.

4

Self-Care for Vicarious Trauma

Emphasize the importance of setting boundaries, processing her own emotions, and seeking support from colleagues or a supervisor.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for reaching out. It takes courage to acknowledge when a session doesn't go as planned. Your willingness to learn is a huge strength."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before I offer suggestions, what did you notice about your own emotional state during the session? Did you feel triggered or overwhelmed by Ben's anger?"
Offer Alternative Approaches
"With children, direct questioning can sometimes backfire. Have you considered using play therapy techniques, like having him draw a picture of his anger or act it out with puppets?"
Reinforce Self-Care
"Remember, you can't pour from an empty cup. What are some self-care strategies you can use after a challenging session like this to replenish your emotional reserves?"

Check Your Understanding

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable asking questions and exploring their challenges.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with vicarious trauma?

Encourage them to practice self-care, set boundaries, and seek support from colleagues or a therapist.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves creating a supportive and non-judgmental environment.
  • Normalize challenges and mistakes as part of the learning process.
  • Encourage self-reflection and critical thinking in your mentees.
  • Provide specific, actionable feedback to help them improve their skills.
  • Prioritize self-care and boundary setting to prevent vicarious trauma.
