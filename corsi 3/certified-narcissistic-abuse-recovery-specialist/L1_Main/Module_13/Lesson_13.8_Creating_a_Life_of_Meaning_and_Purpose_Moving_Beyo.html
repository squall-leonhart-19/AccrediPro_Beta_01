Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing your experience with me. I understand how challenging it can be to navigate the aftermath of a relationship with someone who exhibits narcissistic traits. It's important to acknowledge that the manipulation and emotional abuse you endured were not your fault."
DIANE:
"It's just...I keep wondering if I imagined it, or if I'm overreacting. He was so good at making me feel like *I* was the problem."
YOU:
"That's a very common tactic, Diane, and it's called gaslighting. It's designed to make you doubt your own sanity and perception. We're going to work on validating your experience and rebuilding your sense of self."
YOU:
"Today, I'd like to focus on three key areas: Identifying the specific patterns of abuse you experienced, understanding how those patterns affected your self-esteem, and developing strategies for setting healthy boundaries in the future. Does that sound like a good plan?"
DIANE:
"Yes, absolutely. I need to understand what happened and how to prevent it from happening again."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by exploring some of the specific behaviors you observed in your partner. This will help us identify the narcissistic tactics and understand their impact on you. Are you ready to begin?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of validating the client's experience when dealing with narcissistic abuse?

Validating the client's experience is crucial because it helps them overcome self-doubt and recognize that the abuse they endured was real and not their fault.

2. Why is it important to avoid clinical jargon when coaching a client who has experienced narcissistic abuse?

Using clear, simple language helps ensure that the client understands the concepts being discussed and feels more comfortable sharing their experiences. Clinical jargon can be confusing and alienating.

Key Takeaways

  • Validate the client's experience and acknowledge the impact of narcissistic abuse.
  • Use clear, simple language and avoid clinical jargon.
  • Focus on identifying patterns of abuse and their impact on the client's self-esteem.
  • Empower the client to set healthy boundaries and protect themselves from future abuse.
  • Highlight the client's strengths and resilience to foster hope and healing.
