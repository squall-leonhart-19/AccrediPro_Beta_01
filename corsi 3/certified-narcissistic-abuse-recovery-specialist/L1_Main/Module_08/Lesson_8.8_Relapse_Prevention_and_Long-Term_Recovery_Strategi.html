Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for being so open and vulnerable with me. I understand this is a difficult topic, and I want you to know you're not alone. Many women experience similar challenges after relationships with narcissistic individuals."
DIANE:
"It's been so long since the divorce, I feel like I should be over it by now. But I keep replaying the past, and I don't know how to stop."
YOU:
"It's completely understandable that you're still processing these experiences, Diane. Narcissistic abuse leaves deep wounds, and healing takes time and intentional effort. It's not about 'getting over it,' but about rebuilding your sense of self and reclaiming your power."
YOU:
"I want to assure you that the feelings of self-doubt and worthlessness are a direct result of the manipulation you endured, and they are not a reflection of your true value. We're going to work on challenging those beliefs and replacing them with empowering truths."
DIANE:
"That sounds amazing. I just don't know where to start."
YOU:
"We'll start by focusing on three key areas: understanding the dynamics of narcissistic abuse, building strong boundaries, and cultivating self-compassion. How does that sound as a starting point?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it crucial to validate a client's feelings when they've experienced narcissistic abuse?

Validation helps the client feel heard, understood, and less alone in their experience. It also counteracts the gaslighting and invalidation they likely experienced in the abusive relationship.

2. What are some key areas to focus on when coaching a client recovering from narcissistic abuse?

Key areas include understanding the dynamics of narcissistic abuse, building strong boundaries, cultivating self-compassion, challenging negative self-beliefs, and developing healthy coping mechanisms.

Key Takeaways

  • Validating the client's experience is paramount in establishing trust and rapport.
  • Focus on empowering the client to rebuild their sense of self and reclaim their power.
  • Avoid minimizing their experience or offering unsolicited advice.
  • Speak in plain language and avoid clinical jargon.
  • Emphasize the healing process and celebrate small victories along the way.
