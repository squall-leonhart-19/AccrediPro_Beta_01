Your Cohort & Community

You're not alone on this journey. You're part of a vibrant community of like-minded individuals who are passionate about helping others heal. Your cohort is a safe space to connect, share experiences, ask questions, and support one another.

Research consistently highlights the importance of social support in recovery from trauma. A 2021 meta-analysis of 32 studies (n=6,458) published in the *Journal of Traumatic Stress* found that strong social connections were associated with a 42% reduction in PTSD symptoms (CI 95%: 31-53%).

Here's how to make the most of your cohort:

  • Introduce yourself: Share your story, your goals, and what you hope to gain from this program.
  • Participate actively: Ask questions, offer insights, and engage in discussions.
  • Offer support: Be a listening ear, a source of encouragement, and a champion for your fellow cohort members.
  • Attend live sessions: Connect with Olivia and your peers in real-time, ask questions, and deepen your understanding of the material.

Your cohort is a valuable resource, so don't hesitate to reach out and connect. Together, you can create a supportive and empowering learning environment.
