What You Can and Cannot Do

As a Certified Narcissistic Abuse Recovery Specialistâ„¢, you'll be empowering clients to heal and rebuild their lives after narcissistic abuse. But it's crucial to understand what falls within your expertise and where the line is drawn.

We educate, we don't diagnose. Your role is to provide education, guidance, and support. You're not a therapist or medical professional, so you cannot diagnose mental health conditions or prescribe treatment. Instead, you'll help clients understand the dynamics of narcissistic abuse, develop coping strategies, and set healthy boundaries.

Working alongside, not replacing, medical professionals. Encourage clients to seek professional help from therapists, counselors, or psychiatrists when needed. You can work in tandem with these professionals, providing a holistic approach to recovery.

The power and boundaries of coaching. Coaching is about empowering clients to find their own solutions. You'll use powerful questioning, active listening, and proven strategies to help them achieve their goals. However, you must respect your boundaries and avoid giving advice that falls outside your scope.

Legal Considerations

Navigating the legal landscape is essential for protecting yourself and your clients.

Brief overview of health coaching legality. Health coaching is a relatively new field, and regulations vary by state. Familiarize yourself with the laws in your area to ensure you're operating legally.

Insurance and liability basics. Consider obtaining professional liability insurance to protect yourself in case of legal claims. Consult with an insurance professional to determine the best coverage for your needs.

Staying in your lane professionally. Always operate within your scope of practice. If a client needs medical or psychological treatment, refer them to a qualified professional. Document all referrals and maintain clear communication with your clients.

Ethics & Professionalism

Upholding the highest ethical standards is paramount to building trust and credibility.Confidentiality is key. Protect your clients' privacy by maintaining strict confidentiality. Obtain their informed consent before sharing any information with third parties.

Avoiding dual relationships. Avoid entering into dual relationships with clients, such as becoming friends or business partners. These relationships can compromise your objectivity and create conflicts of interest.

Maintaining boundaries. Set clear boundaries with your clients from the outset. This includes defining the length and frequency of sessions, payment terms, and communication protocols. Be firm but compassionate in enforcing these boundaries.

Check Your Understanding

  1. What is the difference between coaching and clinical therapy in the context of narcissistic abuse recovery?
    Coaching focuses on empowering clients to find their own solutions, while therapy involves diagnosing and treating mental health conditions. As a coach, you provide education, guidance, and support, but you cannot diagnose or prescribe treatment.
  2. What are some ethical considerations to keep in mind when working with clients who have experienced narcissistic abuse?
    Confidentiality, avoiding dual relationships, and maintaining clear boundaries are essential ethical considerations. Always prioritize your clients' well-being and avoid any actions that could compromise your objectivity or create conflicts of interest.
