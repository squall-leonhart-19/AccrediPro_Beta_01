The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 48, who is experiencing intense flashbacks after leaving an abusive relationship. She called me last night saying she was suicidal and doesn't know where to turn. I talked to her for two hours, but I'm worried I made things worse. I don't know what to do now."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah assess for immediate safety risks (access to means, plan, intent)?
  • Did she provide resources for crisis support (hotlines, emergency services)?
  • Did she explore the client's support system?
  • Does Sarah understand the limits of her scope of practice in crisis situations?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Safety Assessment

Emphasize the importance of assessing for suicidality as the first step in a crisis. Review the key questions to ask and how to interpret the responses.

2

Establish Clear Boundaries

Discuss the importance of setting boundaries to protect both the client and the practitioner. Explain that it's okay to say, "I'm not equipped to handle this level of crisis."

3

Provide Crisis Resources

Ensure Sarah is familiar with local and national crisis hotlines, emergency services, and mental health resources. Provide a list she can share with clients.

4

Emphasize Self-Care

Acknowledge the emotional toll of working with clients in crisis. Encourage Sarah to prioritize self-care and seek support from colleagues or a therapist.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Sarah, I commend you for being there for Emily in a moment of crisis. It takes courage to step up like that. Let's talk about how we can ensure both her safety and your well-being moving forward."
Address the Gap
"It sounds like you were feeling overwhelmed by the intensity of Emily's suicidal feelings. What protocols or resources do you think would have been helpful to have in that moment?"
Teach the Principle
"In crisis situations, our primary role is to ensure safety and connect the client with the appropriate level of care. This may involve activating emergency services or referring to a specialist."
Empower Next Steps
"What steps can you take now to support Emily while also protecting your own boundaries? How can I support you in this process?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the first step you should emphasize when a mentee presents a crisis case?

The first step is to prioritize the client's safety assessment, including assessing for suicidality and harm to others.

2. How can you help your mentee establish healthy boundaries when working with complex clients?

Discuss the importance of setting boundaries to protect both the client and the practitioner. Explain that it's okay to say, "I'm not equipped to handle this level of crisis."

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision in crisis cases requires a focus on safety assessment and risk management.
  • Mentors should help new practitioners establish clear boundaries and understand their scope of practice.
  • Providing resources for crisis support and self-care is essential for both the client and the practitioner.
  • Constructive feedback should validate the mentee's efforts while addressing gaps in knowledge or skills.
  • Encouraging consultation and collaboration can prevent new practitioners from feeling overwhelmed by complex cases.
