The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Concern

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a client, Emily, 48, who is recovering from narcissistic abuse. She's making progress, but she texts me almost every day with updates or questions. I want to be supportive, but I'm starting to feel drained. What should I do?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah establish clear boundaries with Emily from the beginning?
  • Is Emily's constant contact a sign of anxiety or dependency?
  • How can Sarah address this without damaging the therapeutic relationship?
  • What strategies can Sarah teach Emily to manage her anxiety independently?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel drained when a client is excessively contacting you. Your feelings are valid."

2

Review Boundary Setting

Discuss the importance of setting clear boundaries from the outset and how to communicate them effectively.

3

Role-Play Boundary Communication

Practice different ways Sarah can respond to Emily's texts while maintaining empathy and firmness.

4

Teach Self-Soothing Techniques

Help Sarah identify and teach Emily strategies for managing anxiety and emotional distress independently (e.g., grounding exercises, mindfulness).

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for recognizing this pattern and seeking guidance. It shows your commitment to both your client and your own well-being."
Explore Her Perspective
"What do you think is driving Emily's need for constant contact? What needs might she be trying to meet?"
Provide Education
"Clients with a history of narcissistic abuse often struggle with anxiety and attachment issues. Excessive contact can be a way of seeking reassurance or testing boundaries."
Offer Specific Strategies
"Here's a script you could adapt: 'Emily, I'm so glad you're finding our sessions helpful. To ensure I can give you my full attention during our time together, I'll be checking messages only during business hours. If you need immediate support, please contact [crisis hotline or emergency contact].' How does that feel to you?"
Reinforce Self-Care
"Remember, setting boundaries is not selfish; it's essential for ethical practice and preventing burnout. Prioritizing your well-being allows you to be a more effective practitioner."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of setting boundaries with clients?

Clarity and consistency. Clients need to understand the boundaries and see them upheld consistently.

2. Why is it important to explore the mentee's perspective before offering advice?

It helps you understand their thought process, identify any gaps in their knowledge, and tailor your feedback to their specific needs.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Boundary setting is crucial for both the practitioner and the client.
  • Validating the mentee's feelings builds trust and rapport.
  • Teaching self-soothing techniques empowers clients.
  • Self-care is essential for sustainable practice.
