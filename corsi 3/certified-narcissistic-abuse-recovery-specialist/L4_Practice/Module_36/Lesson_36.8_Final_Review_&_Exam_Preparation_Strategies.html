Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you share some of the behaviors that made you suspect your ex-husband was a narcissist? What patterns did you notice?"

"Imagine a year from now, Susan, if you continue on this path without support. What does that look like? Now, imagine you've healed and rebuilt your life. What's different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing [summarize her key challenges: confusion, low self-esteem, difficulty trusting]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on understanding the dynamics of narcissistic abuse, validating your experiences, and building your resilience. Over 12 weeks, we'll use tools like inner child work, value alignment, and empowerment techniques to help you heal and reclaim your life. Many clients report feeling a significant shift in their confidence and clarity within the first month."
YOU:
"Specifically, using the THRIVE methodology we will address Trauma, Healing, Resilience, Inner Child work, Value Alignment, and Empowerment. Does this holistic approach resonate with you and your goals?"
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, considering what we've discussed, do you feel like this program is a good fit for your needs? ... Excellent! Let's discuss the program details and how you can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Investing in yourself is a big decision. Let's look at it this way: how much have you already spent on therapy, books, or other resources that haven't fully addressed the issue? What if this program could finally give you the tools and support you need to heal, saving you time and money in the long run?"
"I need to think about it and do some research."
"Of course. What specific information are you looking for? I'm happy to provide testimonials, case studies, or answer any questions you have to help you make an informed decision. Perhaps we can schedule a brief follow-up call to address any remaining concerns?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. What specifically makes you feel that way? I want to reassure you that my program is tailored to each individual's needs. We'll work together to create a personalized plan, and I'll be there to support you every step of the way. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee ‚Äì if you're not seeing progress after the first month, we can discuss adjustments or a refund."
"I'm overwhelmed and don't know where to start."
"I completely understand. It's common to feel overwhelmed after experiencing narcissistic abuse. That's why my program provides a structured, step-by-step approach to healing. We'll break down the process into manageable pieces, and I'll guide you through each stage. You don't have to do this alone."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "I can't afford it"?

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the investment as a solution to a costly problem, and offer payment options if available.

3. What are some effective closing lines to use in a discovery call?

"Are you ready to take the next step and commit to your recovery?" and "Based on everything you've shared, I am confident that my program can help you heal and reclaim your life."

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Confidently present your pricing and the value of your program.
  • Focus on building rapport and understanding the client's needs.
  • Remember, your goal is to help clients transform their lives and achieve lasting healing.
