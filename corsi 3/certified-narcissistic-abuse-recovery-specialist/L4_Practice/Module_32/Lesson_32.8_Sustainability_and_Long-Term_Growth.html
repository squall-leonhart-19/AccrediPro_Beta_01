Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that investing in yourself is a big decision. Many of my clients have felt the same way initially. However, I encourage you to consider the long-term value of investing in your well-being and future happiness. What is it costing you to stay where you are? What is it worth to you to feel confident and empowered again? I also offer payment plans to make the investment more manageable."
"I need to think about it."
"That's perfectly fine. I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is there anything I can clarify for you right now to help you make a more informed decision?"
"I'm not sure if I have the time commitment."
"I understand that you're busy. However, my program is designed to be flexible and adaptable to your schedule. We can work together to create a plan that fits into your life. Think about it this way: investing a few hours a week in yourself can ultimately save you time and energy in the long run by increasing your confidence, clarity, and overall well-being."
"I've tried coaching before, and it didn't work for me."
"I'm sorry to hear that. It's important to find the right fit. My approach is different because [explain what makes your approach unique and effective]. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee ‚Äì if you're not completely satisfied with the program after the first month, I'll refund your investment."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's core issue, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions to uncover their specific concerns and address them directly.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key. Know your prospect and tailor your approach to their specific needs.
  • Listen more than you talk. Focus on understanding the client's challenges and goals.
  • Present your solution with confidence and clarity, highlighting the value and benefits.
  • Don't be afraid to address objections head-on and offer solutions.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines until they feel natural and authentic.
