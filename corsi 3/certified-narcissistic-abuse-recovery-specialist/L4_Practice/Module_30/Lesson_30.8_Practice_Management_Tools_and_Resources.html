Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Investing in yourself is a big decision. What specifically about the investment feels challenging? We have payment plans available, and I can also share some client success stories where they saw a return on investment in their career and overall well-being."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What aspects do you need more time to consider? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else?"
"I'm not sure if it's the right time for me."
"I hear you. Sometimes it's hard to know when the timing is perfect. What would need to be different for you to feel like it's the right time? Often, taking action is what creates the momentum for positive change. Remember, Maria (51, empty nester) felt the same way, but she decided to prioritize herself, and it completely transformed her life."
"I've tried other programs before, and they didn't work."
"I appreciate you sharing that. It's understandable to feel hesitant. What specifically didn't work for you in those programs? My approach is different because [explain your unique methodology and focus on personalized support]. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so you can feel confident in your investment."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, uncovering the prospect's challenges and desires, presenting your solution, and confidently inviting them to take the next step.

2. How should you handle the "I need to think about it" objection?

Acknowledge their need for time, ask what specific aspects they need to consider, and offer to provide additional information or schedule a follow-up call.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script out loud to build confidence.
  • Listen actively to understand your prospect's needs and desires.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide valuable solutions.
  • Confidently state your pricing and highlight the value of your program.
  • Remember that every "no" brings you closer to a "yes."
