  • Articulate the ethical considerations when working with abuse survivors.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Case

Step 1: Identify Potential Narcissistic Abuse
Eleanor's description of her husband aligns with characteristics of covert narcissism: gaslighting, emotional unavailability, financial control, and a disconnect between public persona and private behavior. The gradual increase in anxiety and worthlessness over time is also a red flag.
Step 2: Choose Appropriate Assessment Tools
  1. Narcissistic Personality Inventory (NPI): Administer to Robert (if possible) to assess narcissistic traits.
  2. Experiences of Gaslighting Scale (EGS): Assess Eleanor's experience of gaslighting within the relationship.
  3. Impact of Event Scale-Revised (IES-R): Measure Eleanor's trauma symptoms related to the abusive relationship.
  4. Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale (RSES): Quantify Eleanor's self-esteem levels.
Step 3: Rule Out Other Conditions
Consider differential diagnoses such as generalized anxiety disorder, major depressive disorder, and complex PTSD. The IES-R and a thorough trauma history are crucial for differentiating between these conditions.
Step 4: Assess Co-occurring Conditions
Explore potential co-occurring conditions such as codependency and adult attachment issues. Tools like the Codependency Assessment Tool (COAT) can be helpful.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Major Depressive Disorder

Rule out depression as the primary diagnosis. Consider the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI) to assess the severity of depressive symptoms.

2

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Differentiate between anxiety stemming from the abusive relationship and pre-existing anxiety. The GAD-7 scale can be used to quantify anxiety levels.

3

Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)

Assess for symptoms of C-PTSD, such as difficulty with emotional regulation, distorted self-perception, and relationship difficulties. The International Trauma Questionnaire (ITQ) can be utilized.

4

Relationship Distress (Non-Abusive)

Consider couples therapy if both partners are willing to engage and there is no evidence of abuse. The Dyadic Adjustment Scale (DAS) can measure relationship satisfaction and adjustment.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key assessment tools you would use to evaluate Eleanor's situation, and why?

The Experiences of Gaslighting Scale (EGS) to assess the level of manipulation, the Impact of Event Scale-Revised (IES-R) to quantify trauma symptoms, and the Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale (RSES) to understand her current self-perception.

2. What are the ethical considerations when working with a client who may be experiencing narcissistic abuse?

Maintaining client safety, ensuring informed consent, avoiding dual relationships, and recognizing the power dynamics inherent in abusive relationships are crucial ethical considerations. It's also important to avoid pathologizing the client and to validate their experiences.

Key Takeaways

  • Narcissistic abuse is a subtle and insidious form of emotional abuse that can have devastating effects on survivors.
  • Comprehensive assessment is crucial for identifying narcissistic abuse and differentiating it from other conditions.
  • A phased intervention plan focusing on safety, trauma processing, and empowerment is essential for helping survivors heal and rebuild their lives.
  • Collaboration with other professionals, such as attorneys and psychiatrists, may be necessary to provide comprehensive support.
  • Self-care is essential for practitioners working with abuse survivors to prevent burnout and maintain ethical practice.
