Karen, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic about functional medicine, but lacks confidence in complex cases.

Background
Former teacher, certified health coach, recently completed L1.
Strengths
Excellent communication skills, strong understanding of foundational principles.
Growth Areas
Struggles with complex cases, needs help with differential diagnosis.
Her Question
"I'm overwhelmed by this client's history. Can you help me prioritize?"

The Case She Presents

Karen's Client: Sarah, 52

What Karen Tells You:

"Sarah has a history of chronic fatigue, migraines, and IBS. She's tried several diets and supplements with minimal relief. Her labs show elevated inflammatory markers and possible gut dysbiosis. I don't know where to start!"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Karen considered the interconnectedness of Sarah's symptoms?
  • Is she focusing on symptom management rather than root cause resolution?
  • Does she need help interpreting the lab results and formulating a targeted plan?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Karen

1

Emphasize the HEALS Framework

Remind Karen to use the HEALS framework (History, Evaluate, Actionable Plan, Lifestyle, Sustain) as a guide to organize the client's health journey.

2

Prioritize Root Causes

Help Karen identify the most likely root causes contributing to Sarah's symptoms, such as gut dysbiosis, chronic inflammation, and mitochondrial dysfunction.

3

Teach Interpretation Skills

Walk Karen through the process of interpreting Sarah's lab results, focusing on key markers and their clinical significance.

4

Guide Plan Development

Help Karen develop a personalized plan for Sarah that addresses the identified root causes, incorporating dietary modifications, targeted supplements, and lifestyle interventions.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Karen's Efforts
"Karen, I can see you've put a lot of thought into Sarah's case. It's great that you're seeking guidance to ensure you're providing the best care possible."
Ask About Her Thought Process
"Before we dive in, can you walk me through your thought process? What were your initial hypotheses, and how did you arrive at your current recommendations?"
Provide Targeted Guidance
"Let's take a closer look at Sarah's lab results. Notice how her elevated inflammatory markers align with her symptoms of chronic fatigue and migraines. This suggests a possible link between inflammation and her overall health."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on what we've discussed, what adjustments would you make to Sarah's plan? Remember, there's no one-size-fits-all approach. It's about finding what works best for her individual needs."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing feedback to a new practitioner?

Creating a supportive environment where they feel comfortable asking questions and learning from their experiences.

2. How can you help a mentee prioritize when faced with a complex client case?

By guiding them to identify the most likely root causes contributing to the client's symptoms and focusing on addressing those first.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new practitioners is a valuable way to contribute to the field of functional medicine.
  • Providing constructive feedback and guidance can help mentees develop their clinical skills and build confidence.
  • Emphasizing the HEALS framework and root cause resolution can lead to better client outcomes.
  • Ongoing support and encouragement are essential for mentees' success.
