Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. May I ask what you need to think about specifically? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? Knowing this will help me provide you with relevant information."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Investing in your health is a big decision. Let's reframe this: What if this program could help you avoid future doctor visits, reduce your reliance on medications, and restore your energy? What would that be worth to you?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand that time is precious. This program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. We have weekly calls, but you can access the materials and implement the plan at your own pace. Plus, think about the time you'll save when you're not struggling with symptoms!"
"Will this really work for me? I've tried so many things."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. What sets my approach apart is that we focus on personalized solutions based on your unique needs and root causes. It‚Äôs not a one-size-fits-all approach. We use functional lab testing to get a clear picture of what's happening in your body, and tailor your plan accordingly."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the "It's too expensive" objection?

Reframe the investment by focusing on the long-term benefits, such as reduced healthcare costs, increased energy, and improved quality of life.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls into four key phases: Rapport, Problem, Approach, and Close.
  • Prepare for common objections and have confident, value-driven responses ready.
  • Practice presenting your pricing with assurance, emphasizing the value and ROI of your program.
  • Focus on the transformative outcomes your clients will achieve, not just the features of your program.
  • Believe in the value you offer and the impact you can make on your clients' lives.
