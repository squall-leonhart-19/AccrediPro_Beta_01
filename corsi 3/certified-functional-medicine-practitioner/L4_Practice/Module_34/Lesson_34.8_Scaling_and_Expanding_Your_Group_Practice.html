Discovery Call Script: Group Program Follow-Up

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Reconnect & Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, it's [Your Name]! Thanks for taking my call. How did you enjoy the workshop?"
YOU:
"I'm so glad you found it valuable. What was your biggest takeaway from the workshop?"
Phase 2: Explore Her Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"During the workshop, you mentioned [specific challenge she shared]. Is that still a concern for you?"
YOU:
"What would it mean to you to overcome that challenge and achieve your goal of feeling more balanced and connected?"
YOU:
"Imagine yourself 8 weeks from now, having completed the program. How would your life be different?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program as the Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, the 8-week group program is specifically designed to help women like you achieve exactly what you're looking for. It provides a structured framework, personalized support, and a supportive community."
YOU:
"In the program, we'll cover [mention 2-3 key modules]. You'll also receive [mention bonuses, like a private coaching session or resource guide]."
YOU:
"Many of our participants experience significant reductions in stress, improved sleep, and a stronger sense of self-connection within the first few weeks. For example, 85% of participants report feeling less overwhelmed after Module 2."
Phase 4: Close & Enroll 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Sarah, I truly believe this program can be transformative for you. Are you ready to take the next step and join us?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to highlight when presenting the value of a group program?

Highlight the structured framework, personalized support, supportive community, and transformative potential. Use specific examples and testimonials.

2. How can you address the "time commitment" objection effectively?

Emphasize that the program is designed to be manageable, the live sessions are recorded, and participants often save time and energy by becoming more efficient and focused.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a scalable way to impact more lives and increase your income.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide concrete solutions or alternative perspectives.
  • Confidence in your pricing is crucial. Practice stating your price clearly and confidently.
  • Focus on the transformative potential of your program and the long-term benefits for participants.
  • Remember to highlight the community aspect of your group program.
