Your Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about you and see how I can help. First, can you tell me what prompted you to reach out to me today?"
YOU:
"Great! Just to give you an idea of how this call will go, I'd like to hear about your health concerns, what you've tried so far, and then I'll share how my program can help. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Uncover Challenges & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the hormonal imbalance. What symptoms are you experiencing, and how are they impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address these issues? What worked, and what didn't? What are your biggest frustrations?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and feel your best, what would that look like? What specific outcomes are you hoping to achieve?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with [summarize her pain points and goals]. Is that an accurate summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is to identify the root causes of your hormonal imbalance using functional lab testing and personalized nutrition and lifestyle interventions. I work with you to create a customized plan to restore balance and optimize your health. Many of my clients see significant improvements in their energy, mood, and overall well-being within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"This involves a comprehensive assessment, including a deep dive into your health history, targeted lab testing, and ongoing support and guidance."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you feel like this approach resonates with you and aligns with your goals? ... Great! Let me share the details of my program and how we can get started."

Mastering Objection Handling

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. It's a big decision. To help me understand your hesitation, is there anything specific you need to think about? Perhaps the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the right decision for you."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Many of my clients initially feel that way. However, when they consider the cost of ongoing symptoms, ineffective treatments, and the impact on their quality of life, they realize the value of investing in a long-term solution. We can also discuss payment plan options to make it more manageable. What if we could actually solve this in 12 weeks? What would that be worth to you?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand that time is valuable. My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy schedule. We can work together to create a plan that works for you, with realistic goals and manageable steps. And remember, investing in your health now will give you more energy and focus in the long run, making you more productive in all areas of your life."
"I need to talk to my partner."
"That's perfectly fine. Would it be helpful if I sent you some detailed information about the program that you can share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions they may have. Which option would work best for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, uncovering challenges and goals, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the call.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the long-term value of your program, and offer payment plan options.

3. What is the importance of practicing your closing lines?

Practicing your closing lines helps you to be confident and assertive when asking for the sale, increasing your chances of converting prospects into clients.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Effective objection handling can turn potential roadblocks into opportunities.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing demonstrates the value of your services.
  • Consistent client acquisition can lead to a significant and sustainable income.
