Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Discovery Call

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so great to connect with you today! Thank you for scheduling this call. To start, I'd love to hear what prompted you to reach out to me specifically?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that you found my website helpful. So, tell me a bit about what's been going on with your perimenopause symptoms. What's been the most challenging for you?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Susan's Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the specific symptoms you're experiencing. When did they start, and how are they impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage these symptoms? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If these symptoms continue unchecked, where do you see yourself in a year? And on the flip side, if you could magically wave a wand and resolve these issues, what would your life look like?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Functional Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're struggling with [summarize her key pain points related to perimenopause]. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different. Instead of just masking symptoms with hormone replacement therapy, we dig deep to identify the root causes of your hormonal imbalance. My 12-week program is designed to uncover those root causes and create a personalized plan with diet, lifestyle adjustments, and targeted supplements. Many of my clients experience noticeable improvements within the first month."
YOU:
"I've helped women just like you, in their 40s and 50s, reclaim their energy, balance their hormones naturally, and feel amazing again. For example, I worked with Client Jane (age 48) who was experiencing similar symptoms and felt completely exhausted. Through our program, we identified gut imbalances and nutrient deficiencies that were contributing to her issues. Within 8 weeks, she reported a 70% reduction in her fatigue and hot flashes."
Phase 4: Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, does this approach sound like what you've been searching for? ... Fantastic! Let me share the details of my program and how we can get you started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it and do some research."
"I understand completely, Susan. This is an important decision. May I ask what specifically you'd like to research further? Perhaps I can provide some additional information to help you. Many of my clients have found the testimonials on my website helpful."
"It's more expensive than I anticipated."
"I hear you. Many people initially feel that way. However, let's consider the long-term costs of continuing to struggle with these symptoms. Think about the money you've already spent on temporary solutions or ineffective treatments. My program offers a comprehensive, personalized approach that addresses the root causes, potentially saving you money and frustration in the long run. We also have payment plan options available."
"I'm not sure I have the time commitment right now."
"That's a valid concern. The program requires a commitment of [number] hours per week for [duration]. However, think about how much time you're already spending feeling unwell, managing symptoms, and searching for solutions. This program is designed to help you reclaim that time by restoring your health and energy. Plus, we can work together to create a schedule that fits your lifestyle."
"I'm not sure if this will really work for me."
"I understand your hesitation, especially if you've tried other approaches that haven't worked. My program is based on the principles of Functional Medicine, which focuses on identifying and addressing the root causes of illness. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied with the program, I offer a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Warm welcome & connection, deep dive into needs, presenting your approach, and a confident close.

2. What is a good way to respond to the objection "It's more expensive than I anticipated"?

A good response involves acknowledging their concern, then reframing the cost by discussing the long-term costs of not addressing the problem and the potential savings from a personalized, effective approach. Mention payment options if available.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Focus on understanding the prospect's needs and pain points before presenting your solution.
  • Be prepared to handle common objections with confident and empathetic responses.
  • Practice stating your prices clearly and confidently, highlighting the value you provide.
  • Remember that every "no" brings you closer to a "yes."
