Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of this with me. It sounds like you've been through a lot lately, and it's completely understandable that you're feeling this way. Many of my clients in similar situations feel the same."
MARIA:
"It's just... I feel like I'm constantly on edge. I can't relax, and I'm exhausted."
YOU:
"I hear you, and it's important to know that your body is reacting to stress. We're going to work together to find gentle, sustainable ways to support your nervous system and help you find that sense of calm again."
YOU:
"Based on what you've described, I'd like to explore some strategies around sleep hygiene, gentle movement, and mindfulness techniques. How does that sound to you?"
MARIA:
"That sounds great. I'm willing to try anything at this point."
YOU:
"Perfect. Let's start by talking about your sleep environment and your evening routine. Tell me about what a typical night looks like for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important first step when working with a client experiencing high stress and anxiety?

Validating their feelings and creating a safe space for them to share their experience.

2. Why is it important to avoid jumping to solutions immediately when coaching a client?

Jumping to solutions before fully understanding the client's context and building rapport can make them feel unheard and misunderstood.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial for establishing trust with clients.
  • Address the root causes of symptoms, not just the symptoms themselves.
  • Start with gentle, sustainable changes that feel manageable for the client.
  • Collaborate with the client to create a personalized plan.
