    The 30-Minute Discovery Call

    Structure for Success

    The 30-minute discovery call is your opportunity to connect with potential clients, understand their challenges, and present your program as the solution. Here's a breakdown of the key phases:

    Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda (5 minutes)

    Start by building rapport ‚Äì ask about their day, express genuine interest. Then, clearly state the agenda for the call: "Today, I'd like to understand your biggest challenges with stress, share how my program can help, and see if we're a good fit to work together."

    Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem (10 minutes)

    Ask open-ended questions to uncover their pain points: "What are your biggest stressors right now?" "How is stress impacting your daily life?" "What have you tried in the past to manage stress?" Listen actively and take notes.

    Phase 3: Present Your Solution (10 minutes)

    Now, connect your program to their specific needs. "Based on what you've shared, my program can help you by [specific benefit 1], [specific benefit 2], and [specific benefit 3]." Briefly explain the key components of your program and how they address the client's challenges.

    Phase 4: Close & Next Steps (5 minutes)

    Ask for the sale: "Based on everything we've discussed, I'm confident I can help you. Are you ready to get started?" If they say yes, explain the enrollment process. If they have questions or objections, address them directly (see below).
