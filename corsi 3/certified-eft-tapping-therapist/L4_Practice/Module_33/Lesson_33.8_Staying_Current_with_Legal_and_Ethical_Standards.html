Actionable Steps: Legal Checklist

Compliance Tasks for Sarah

Step 1: Business Structure 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, since you're starting part-time, operating as a sole proprietor is fine for now. However, as your business grows, consider forming an LLC to protect your personal assets. This separates your personal and business liabilities."
YOU:
"To form an LLC, you'll need to register your business name with your state and obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS, even if you don't plan to hire employees right away."
Step 2: Professional Liability Insurance 10 min
YOU:
"Professional liability insurance, also known as malpractice insurance, is crucial. It protects you if a client claims your services caused them harm. Even with EFT, clients could have adverse reactions, so it's a safeguard."
YOU:
"Shop around for quotes. Premiums can vary, but typically range from $500 to $1,500 per year depending on coverage limits and your location. Look for policies with at least $1 million per occurrence and $3 million in aggregate."
YOU:
"Consider policies tailored to wellness professionals. Some insurers specialize in covering EFT practitioners and understand the nuances of our work."
Step 3: Client Agreements and Informed Consent 10 min
YOU:
"A clear client agreement is essential. It should outline the scope of your services, your fees, cancellation policies, confidentiality terms, and a disclaimer stating that EFT is not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment."
YOU:
"Informed consent is also vital. Ensure clients understand the nature of EFT, its potential benefits and risks, and that they are voluntarily participating. Document this consent in writing."
Step 4: Privacy Compliance (HIPAA/GDPR) 5 min
YOU:
"Even if you're not directly billing insurance, you still need to comply with privacy laws. In the US, HIPAA applies if you transmit health information electronically. If you have clients in Europe, GDPR applies."
YOU:
"Use secure, encrypted platforms for client communication and data storage. Obtain explicit consent for collecting and processing client data. Have a privacy policy on your website."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is professional liability insurance important for an EFT practitioner?

Professional liability insurance protects you if a client claims your services caused them harm, even if the claim is unfounded. It covers legal fees and potential settlements.

2. What are some key elements to include in a client agreement?

A client agreement should outline the scope of services, fees, cancellation policies, confidentiality terms, and a disclaimer stating that EFT is not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment.

3. How can an EFT practitioner comply with privacy laws like HIPAA and GDPR?

Use secure, encrypted platforms for client communication and data storage. Obtain explicit consent for collecting and processing client data. Have a privacy policy on your website.

Key Takeaways

  • Operating as an LLC can protect your personal assets from business liabilities.
  • Professional liability insurance is a crucial safeguard against potential client claims.
  • Clear client agreements and informed consent build trust and protect you legally.
  • Complying with privacy laws like HIPAA and GDPR is essential for ethical practice.
  • Offering compliance guidance can be a valuable and profitable service.
