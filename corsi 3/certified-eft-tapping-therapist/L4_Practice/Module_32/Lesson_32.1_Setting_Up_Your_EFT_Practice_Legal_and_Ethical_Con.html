Choosing a Business Structure

Selecting the right business structure is a fundamental step. The structure you choose impacts your personal liability, taxes, and administrative requirements. Here's a breakdown of common options for EFT practitioners:

Business Structure Pros Cons Legal Implications
Sole Proprietorship Easy to set up, minimal paperwork, direct access to profits. Personal liability for business debts, harder to obtain funding. Business income taxed as personal income.
Limited Liability Company (LLC) Protects personal assets from business debts, flexible tax options. More complex setup than sole proprietorship, ongoing compliance requirements. Offers liability protection; can elect to be taxed as a sole proprietorship, partnership, or corporation.
S-Corporation (S-Corp) Potential tax savings (salary vs. dividends), credibility with clients. Most complex setup, stricter compliance requirements, higher administrative costs. Shareholders receive a salary and dividends; profits and losses are passed through to the owners' personal income without being subject to corporate tax rates.

Income Example: Sarah, asole proprietor, earns $80,000 in revenue, but after expenses, her net profit is $50,000. She pays self-employment taxes on the entire $50,000. If Sarah forms an S-Corp and pays herself a $40,000 salary, she only pays self-employment taxes on that salary, potentially saving thousands.

Considerations: Consult with a qualified accountant and attorney to determine the best structure for your specific circumstances. State laws vary, so ensure you're compliant with local regulations.

Informed Consent & Client Agreements

Informed consent is the cornerstone of ethical practice. It ensures clients understand the nature of EFT, potential risks and benefits, and their rights as clients. A comprehensive informed consent form should include:

  • Description of EFT and the R.E.S.E.T. Frameworkâ„¢
  • Qualifications and experience of the practitioner
  • Explanation of the therapeutic process, including tapping points and techniques
  • Potential benefits and risks of EFT
  • Confidentiality policies and exceptions
  • Fees and payment arrangements
  • Cancellation policies
  • Client rights and responsibilities
  • Telehealth considerations (if applicable), including technology requirements and security measures

EFT-Specific Considerations: Clearly explain the emotional intensity that may arise during EFT sessions and how you will support clients through these experiences. Address the potential for abreactions and dissociation, and outline your strategies for managing these situations.

Telehealth Addendum: If offering EFT via telehealth, include a separate addendum addressing:

  • Data security and privacy protocols
  • Emergency contact procedures
  • Technology requirements and troubleshooting
  • Limitations of telehealth (e.g., difficulty assessing nonverbal cues)
  • Jurisdictional issues (licensure requirements in the client's location)

Client Agreement: In addition to informed consent, a client agreement outlines the terms of the therapeutic relationship, including session frequency, duration, and communication protocols. This helps to establish clear expectations and prevent misunderstandings.

HIPAA Compliance & Data Security

If you are considered a covered entity under HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act), you must comply with its regulations to protect client Protected Health Information (PHI). This includes:

  • Privacy Rule: Protecting the privacy of PHI, including obtaining client authorization for disclosure of information.
  • Security Rule: Implementing administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect electronic PHI.
  • Breach Notification Rule: Notifying clients and the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) in the event of a data breach.

Practical Steps for HIPAA Compliance:

  • Use a HIPAA-compliant Electronic Health Record (EHR) system.
  • Implement strong password protection and encryption for all devices.
  • Secure your office space to prevent unauthorized access to client files.
  • Obtain Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with any third-party vendors who have access to PHI (e.g., cloud storage providers, billing services).
  • Conduct regular security risk assessments and implement corrective action plans.
  • Provide HIPAA training to all staff members.

Data Security Best Practices: Beyond HIPAA, prioritize data security by:

  • Regularly backing up client data to a secure, offsite location.
  • Using secure communication channels (e.g., encrypted email, secure messaging apps).
  • Implementing a data retention policy that complies with legal and ethical requirements.
  • Shredding paper documents containing PHI.

Liability Insurance

Professional liability insurance (also known as malpractice insurance) protects you from financial losses in the event of a lawsuit alleging negligence or malpractice. It covers legal defense costs and potential settlements or judgments.

Factors to Consider When Choosing Coverage:

  • Coverage Amount: Select a coverage amount that adequately protects your assets. Common coverage levels are $1 million/$3 million (per occurrence/aggregate).
  • Policy Type: Choose between occurrence and claims-made policies. Occurrence policies cover incidents that occur during the policy period, regardless of when the claim is filed. Claims-made policies cover claims that are filed during the policy period, regardless of when the incident occurred.
  • EFT-Specific Coverage: Ensure the policy covers EFT-related services and techniques.
  • Telehealth Coverage: Verify that the policy covers telehealth services in all jurisdictions where you practice.
  • Cost: Compare premiums from different providers and consider factors like deductible and policy limits.

Her liability insurance covered her legal defense, ultimately leading to a settlement that avoided significant financial strain and protected her reputation. Without insurance, she would have had to pay legal fees and the settlement out of pocket.

Ethical Guidelines

Adhering to ethical guidelines is paramount for maintaining client trust and upholding professional standards. Key ethical considerations for EFT practitioners include:

  • Confidentiality: Protecting client information and respecting their privacy. Exceptions include mandated reporting of child abuse, elder abuse, or threats of harm to self or others.
  • Dual Relationships: Avoiding relationships with clients that could impair objectivity or exploit the client's vulnerability (e.g., business partnerships, romantic relationships).
  • Scope of Practice: Providing services within your area of competence and referring clients to other professionals when necessary.
  • Informed Consent: Ensuring clients fully understand the nature of EFT, potential risks and benefits, and their rights as clients (as discussed earlier).
  • Advertising and Marketing: Representing your qualifications and services accurately and avoiding deceptive or misleading claims.
  • Professional Development: Engaging in ongoing training and education to maintain competence in EFT and related areas.

Addressing Ethical Dilemmas: When faced with an ethical dilemma, consult with colleagues, supervisors, or ethics experts to obtain guidance. Document your decision-making process and rationale.

Example: A client discloses they are experiencing suicidal ideation. Your ethical duty is to assess the risk of harm, implement safety measures (e.g., contacting emergency services, involving family members), and provide appropriate referrals for mental health treatment. This supersedes confidentiality.

Check Your Understanding

Question 1: What are the key differences between a sole proprietorship, LLC, and S-Corp, and how do these differences impact your liability and taxes?

A sole proprietorship is the simplest structure, offering easy setup but no liability protection. An LLC provides liability protection and flexible tax options. An S-Corp offers potential tax savings but has more complex requirements. The best choice depends on your individual circumstances and risk tolerance.

Question 2: What are the essential components of a comprehensive informed consent form for EFT practice, and why is it important to obtain informed consent from clients?

An informed consent form should include a description of EFT, potential risks and benefits, confidentiality policies, fees, and client rights. It's crucial for ensuring clients understand and agree to the terms of therapy.

Question 3: Describe three practical steps you can take to ensure HIPAA compliance in your EFT practice.

Using a HIPAA-compliant EHR, implementing strong password protection and encryption, and obtaining Business Associate Agreements with third-party vendors are three key steps to ensure HIPAA compliance.
