Handling Objections with Grace

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Investing in yourself is a big decision. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. Many of my clients find that the increased energy, clarity, and sense of purpose they gain actually *saves* them money in the long run ‚Äì by reducing stress-related health issues, for example. Would you like me to share some client testimonials about the ROI they experienced?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course! What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."
"I'm skeptical about EFT/Tapping. I'm not sure if it really works."
"I understand your skepticism. It's natural to be cautious. While EFT/Tapping may seem unconventional, it's backed by a growing body of research. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that EFT/Tapping significantly reduced anxiety symptoms compared to control groups (Anderson et al., 2023). I'm happy to share some of that research with you. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee ‚Äì if you're not completely satisfied with the program after the first few weeks, I'll offer a full refund."
"I'm too old to start something new."
"Susan, that's simply not true! Age is just a number. Many of my most successful clients are women in their 50s, 60s, and beyond. They've proven that it's never too late to pursue your passions and create a fulfilling life. In fact, your life experience is a huge asset! My program is designed to help you tap into that wisdom and use it to create a truly meaningful new chapter. Remember Maria? She was 48 when she started volunteering and tapping the animals at the shelter! What's your gut feeling about this?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in your discovery call structure?

Build rapport, explore client needs, present your solution, and close the call.

2. How should you respond to a potential client who says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask what specifically they need to consider (time, money, etc.) to address their concerns.

3. What's a good strategy for handling a client's skepticism about EFT/Tapping?

Acknowledge their skepticism, share research findings, and offer a satisfaction guarantee.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are crucial for converting leads into clients. Structure them effectively.
  • Master the art of handling objections with empathy and confidence.
  • Present your pricing with conviction, highlighting the value you offer.
  • Craft a compelling call-to-action that encourages clients to take the next step.
  • Remember the income potential of your practice and strive for achievable goals.
