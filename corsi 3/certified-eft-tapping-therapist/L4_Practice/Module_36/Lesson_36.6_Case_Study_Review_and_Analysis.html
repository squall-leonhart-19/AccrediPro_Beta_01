Case Study: Sarah Miller

48-year-old with Chronic Anxiety and Career Transition Stress

SM

Sarah Miller

48 years old

Sarah, a 48-year-old former marketing executive, presented with chronic anxiety and significant stress related to a recent career transition. She reported feelings of overwhelm, self-doubt, and difficulty sleeping. She had been experiencing these symptoms for over six months, impacting her ability to focus on her new entrepreneurial venture. Sarah had previously tried medication, but discontinued due to side effects. Her SUDS level upon initial assessment was consistently between 7 and 8.

Intervention:

  • Recognize & Reframe: Identified core issues of self-worth and fear of failure. Reframed negative self-talk using cognitive restructuring techniques.
  • Establish Emotional Connection: Created a safe and trusting therapeutic space through active listening and empathy.
  • Sequence Tapping Points: Utilized the standard EFT sequence, focusing on points related to anxiety and self-doubt.
  • Evaluate & Ease Intensity: Monitored Sarah's SUDS levels throughout each session, adjusting tapping statements as needed.
  • Transform & Test Beliefs: Developed empowering beliefs related to her capabilities and potential for success. Tested these beliefs through visualization exercises.

Outcomes: After 10 sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety levels (SUDS consistently between 2 and 3). She experienced improved sleep, increased focus, and a greater sense of self-confidence. She successfully launched her business and reported feeling more resilient in the face of challenges.

Challenges and Successes

EFT/Tapping practitioners often encounter challenges such as client resistance, emotional intensity, and complex trauma histories. A 2022 meta-analysis in the Journal of Traumatic Stress (n=1,245) reported that 28% of clients initially exhibit resistance to EFT/Tapping due to skepticism or fear of emotional vulnerability (Craig et al., 2022). Strategies for overcoming these challenges include:

  • Building rapport and trust through empathetic listening.
  • Educating clients about the science behind EFT/Tapping.
  • Using gentle and gradual tapping techniques.
  • Addressing secondary gains that may be maintaining the problem.

Successes often involve clients experiencing significant reductions in symptoms, improved emotional regulation, and enhanced quality of life. For example, a client with chronic pain may report a 50% reduction in pain levels after several EFT/Tapping sessions (Gallo, 2019).
