Your Coaching Script

Michelle has been tapping using a generic sequence, but not addressing the specific layers of her anxiety. Here's how you guide her through a more effective approach:

YOU:
"Michelle, it's great that you've experimented with EFT already. Let's refine your tapping sequence to target the *specific* anxieties you're facing. It's like fine-tuning an instrument for the perfect sound."
MICHELLE:
"Okay, I'm willing to try anything. I've been using the same points I saw in a video, but it doesn't always help."
YOU:
"Exactly. A generic sequence can be a starting point, but true relief comes from addressing the root causes. Let's break down your anxieties. What's the *first* thought that pops into your head when you think about your business right now?"
MICHELLE:
"That I'm failing. That I'm not good enough."
YOU:
"Okay, let's start there. We'll use a sequence that acknowledges that feeling, then shifts to self-compassion. We'll start with the Karate Chop, then move to the standard points, but with personalized phrases. Ready?"
YOU:
"At the Eyebrow point, repeat 'Even though I feel like I'm failing...' Then at the Side of the Eye, say '...I deeply and completely accept myself.' At the Under the Eye, say 'This feeling of failure...' and so on. We'll tailor each point to address a different layer of that anxiety."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to tailor the tapping sequence to the client's specific issue?

A generic sequence may not address the root causes of the client's issue. Tailoring the sequence allows you to target specific thoughts, emotions, and beliefs, leading to more effective and lasting results.

2. What is the benefit of including the client in the process of creating the tapping sequence?

Including the client in the process empowers them, fosters a stronger connection to the process, and ensures that the tapping sequence resonates with their individual experience. It also helps them to become more self-aware and better equipped to use EFT independently.

Key Takeaways

  • A tailored tapping sequence is more effective than a generic one.
  • Involve the client in creating the sequence to increase engagement.
  • Address the root causes of the issue, not just the symptoms.
  • Use the Setup Statement at the Karate Chop point for every new issue.
  • Continuously assess and adjust the sequence as needed.
