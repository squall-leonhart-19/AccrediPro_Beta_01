Introduction to the EFT Tapping Points

The standard EFT tapping sequence involves stimulating specific energy points on the body by tapping on them with your fingertips. These points are located on meridians, which are pathways in traditional Chinese medicine believed to carry energy throughout the body. By tapping on these points in a specific sequence, we aim to clear emotional and energetic blockages.

The core tapping points are:

  • Karate Chop (KC): Side of the hand
  • Top of Head (TH): Center of the top of the head
  • Eyebrow (EB): Beginning of the eyebrow, closest to the nose
  • Side of Eye (SE): On the bony area at the side of the eye
  • Under Eye (UE): On the bony area under the eye
  • Under Nose (UN): Space between the nose and upper lip
  • Chin (CH): Crease of the chin
  • Collarbone (CB): Junction of the collarbone and sternum
  • Under Arm (UA): Side of the body, about four inches below the armpit

Rationale Behind the Sequence

The specific order of the tapping points isn't arbitrary. It's designedto systematically address various aspects of the emotional or energetic blockage. While the exact mechanisms are still being researched, the sequence is believed to work by:

  • Activating multiple meridians: Covering a range of meridian points ensures a broad energetic influence.
  • Addressing cognitive and emotional components: The sequence encourages the client to focus on the issue while stimulating the points, potentially facilitating cognitive restructuring and emotional processing.
  • Promoting relaxation: The rhythmic tapping and focused attention can induce a state of calm, counteracting the stress response associated with negative emotions. A 2012 study in the Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease found that EFT significantly reduced cortisol levels, a key stress hormone (n=83, p < 0.001).

Demonstrating the Standard Tapping Sequence

Here's how to perform the standard EFT tapping sequence:

  1. The Setup: While continuously tapping the Karate Chop point, repeat the setup statement three times: "Even though I have this [problem/feeling], I deeply and completely accept myself."
  2. The Sequence: Tap each of the following points approximately 5-7 times, while repeating a reminder phrase related to the problem:
    • Top of Head (TH)
    • Eyebrow (EB)
    • Side of Eye (SE)
    • Under Eye (UE)
    • Under Nose (UN)
    • Chin (CH)
    • Collarbone (CB)
    • Under Arm (UA)
  3. The Repeat: Repeat the sequence 2-3 times, or as needed, until the intensity of the emotion decreases.
  4. The 9 Gamut Procedure (Optional): This involves performing nine specific actions (e.g., eye movements, humming) while continuously tapping the gamut point (on the back of the hand, between the knuckles of the ring and pinky fingers). This is often used for more complex or stubborn issues.

Common Variations in the Sequence

While the standard sequence is highly effective, there are some variations that you might find useful in specific situations:

  • Shortened Sequence: Omitting certain points (e.g., UN, CH) for a quicker process. This can be helpful for maintenance or less intense issues.
  • Point Holding: Instead of tapping, holding the points for a longer duration. This may be preferred by some clients or helpful for very sensitive individuals.
  • Incorporating Somatic Awareness: Paying attention to physical sensations in the body while tapping. This can help to uncover deeper emotional layers.

Case Study: Sarah, 34, Anxiety

Client: Sarah, a 34-year-old marketing manager, presented with generalized anxiety and panic attacks. She reported feeling overwhelmed by work and personal stressors.

Intervention: Sarah was guided through the standard EFT tapping sequence, focusing on her feelings of anxiety and the physical sensations associated with her panic attacks (e.g., rapid heart rate, shortness of breath). The setup statement was: "Even though I feel this anxiety in my chest, I deeply and completely accept myself." The reminder phrase was: "This anxiety."

Outcomes: After several sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in her anxiety levels and the frequency of her panic attacks. She also developed a greater sense of self-compassion and resilience. Her subjective units of distress (SUDS) score decreased from 8/10 to 2/10 after the first session.
