Top of Head (TH)

The Top of Head (TH) point is located at the very top of your head, in line with the center of your ears. Energetically, this point is often associated with a sense of connection and overall well-being. It's believed to help integrate the tapping process and bring a sense of closure to the emotional issue being addressed.

Tapping Technique: Use all your fingertips to gently tap the top of your head. You can tap with all fingers simultaneously or use a circular motion. Ensure the tapping is gentle yet firm enough to feel a slight vibration. Many people find this point particularly soothing.

Collarbone (CB)

The Collarbone (CB) point is found at the junction where the collarbone meets the sternum (breastbone). To locate it, place your index finger on the U-shaped notch at the base of your throat and then move your finger about an inch down and to either side. You'll find a slightly tender spot â€“ that's the Collarbone point.

Energetically, the Collarbone point is connected to the respiratory system and is believed to help release anxiety and fear. Tapping this point can improve breathing and promote a sense of calm.

Tapping Technique: Use your fingertips to tap firmly on both Collarbone points simultaneously. You can use one hand or both hands, whichever feels more comfortable. Tapping this point can also help to release physical tension in the chest and shoulders.

Under Arm (UA)

The Under Arm (UA) point is located on the side of your body, about four inches below your armpit. It corresponds roughly to where the side seam of a bra would be for women.

Energetically, the Under Arm point is associated with the spleen meridian and is believed to help release held-in emotions and self-sabotaging thoughts. Itâ€™s a powerful point for promoting self-acceptance and releasing negative energy.

Tapping Technique: Use your fingertips to tap firmly on the Under Arm point. You can tap with one hand or both hands, whichever feels more comfortable. This point is particularly effective for releasing long-held emotional baggage.

Case Study: Releasing Physical Tension

Client: Anna, 42 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Anna presented with chronic neck and shoulder tension, which she attributed to stress at work and difficulty setting boundaries. She reported feeling overwhelmed and constantly on edge.

Intervention: We began with a standard EFT setup phrase: "Even though I have this tension in my neck and shoulders, I deeply and completely accept myself." We then proceeded with the tapping sequence, focusing on the Top of Head, Collarbone, and Under Arm points to release the physical tension and associated emotions. Reminder phrases included: "This tension in my neck," "Feeling overwhelmed," and "Difficulty setting boundaries."

Outcomes: After just one session, Anna reported a significant reduction in her neck and shoulder tension. She also felt more grounded and empowered to set boundaries at work. By the third session, her physical tension was minimal, and she had developed effective coping strategies for managing stress.

Anna's SUDS score decreased from an 8 to a 2 after the first session, indicating a significant reduction in her perceived distress.
