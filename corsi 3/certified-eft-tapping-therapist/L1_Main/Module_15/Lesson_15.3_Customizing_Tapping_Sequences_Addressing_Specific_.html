Customizing EFT Sequences

While the standard EFT tapping sequence provides a solid foundation, the beauty of EFT lies in its adaptability. Customizing sequences allows you to target specific issues and cater to client preferences, enhancing the therapeutic experience and potentially accelerating healing. As a Certified EFT/Tapping Therapistâ„¢, you have the knowledge and skills to modify the sequence strategically.

Here are several ways to customize EFT sequences:

  • Altering the Tapping Points: Consider adding or removing points based on the client's specific needs. For example, if a client is highly sensitive, you might omit the Karate Chop point and focus on gentler facial points.
  • Adapting the Setup Statement: Craft setup statements that directly address the client's core issue, using their own language. This ensures the tapping is highly relevant and impactful.
  • Modifying the Reminder Phrase: The reminder phrase should encapsulate the core issue or emotion being addressed. Experiment with different phrases to find the one that resonates most strongly with the client.
  • Incorporating Affirmations: Interweave positive affirmations into the sequence to promote self-compassion and resilience.
  • Adjusting the Pace: Some clients prefer a faster tapping pace, while others benefit from a slower, more deliberate approach. Observe your client's reactions and adjust accordingly.

Remember, the goal is to create a sequence that feels safe, comfortable, and effective for the client. Trust your intuition and be willing to experiment.

Surrogate Tapping

Surrogate tapping is a powerful technique used when a client is unable to physically tap on themselves. This may be due to physical limitations, emotional distress, or being in a location where tapping is not feasible. In surrogate tapping, the therapist taps on themselves on behalf of the client, while maintaining a strong emotional connection and focusing on the client's issue.

Here's how to effectively use surrogate tapping:

  • Establish a Strong Connection: Before beginning, ensure you have a clear understanding of the client's issue and a strong sense of empathy.
  • Use Clear Intention: State your intention to tap on behalf of the client, clearly identifying them by name. For example, "I am tapping for [Client's Name] on their anxiety."
  • Maintain Focus: Throughout the tapping sequence, stay focused on the client's issue and their emotional state.
  • Monitor Your Own Reactions: Pay attention to any physical or emotional sensations that arise in your own body. These may provide valuable insights into the client's experience.
  • Check in Regularly: After each round of tapping, check in with the client to gauge their response and adjust the sequence as needed.

Surrogate tapping can be particularly useful in situations where the client is experiencing intense emotional distress or dissociation. It allows you to provide support and facilitate healing even when the client is unable to actively participate.

Integrating Somatic Experiencing

Somatic Experiencing (SE) is a body-awareness approach to healing trauma developed by Peter Levine. Integrating SE principles with EFT can enhance the processing of trauma and body-based memories, allowing clients to release deeply held tension and emotional blockages. The integration leverages the cognitive and emotional restructuring of EFT with the somatic awareness and release techniques of SE.

Here's how to integrate Somatic Experiencing with EFT:

  • Pendulation: Encourage the client to alternate between focusing on the traumatic memory and a safe, neutral sensation in their body. This helps to regulate the nervous system and prevent overwhelm.
  • Titration: Break down the traumatic memory into smaller, more manageable pieces. Focus on one aspect at a time, allowing the client to gradually process the experience without being flooded.
  • Tracking Sensations: Guide the client to notice and describe the physical sensations associated with the traumatic memory. This helps to bring awareness to the body's response and facilitate release.
  • Resource Installation: Before processing the trauma, help the client identify and connect with internal and external resources that provide a sense of safety and support.
  • Completion of Defensive Responses: Encourage the client to gently express any incomplete defensive responses, such as trembling, shaking, or crying. This can help to release pent-up energy and restore a sense of wholeness.

When integrating SE with EFT, it's crucial to proceed slowly and gently, always prioritizing the client's safety and comfort. Monitor their reactions closely and be prepared to adjust the approach as needed.

A 2017 study in the *Journal of Traumatic Stress* found that integrating somatic awareness techniques with cognitive therapies significantly improved outcomes for individuals with PTSD (Cohen et al., 2017).

Case Study: Anxiety and Panic Attacks

Client: Sarah, 32 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reports experiencing frequent anxiety and panic attacks, often triggered by social situations or work-related stress. She describes feeling overwhelmed, having difficulty breathing, and experiencing a racing heart. She also reports a history of childhood emotional neglect.

Intervention: A customized EFT tapping sequence was designed, incorporating elements of Somatic Experiencing. The sequence focused on addressing Sarah's physical sensations during panic attacks, as well as the underlying emotional wounds related to her childhood experiences.

Example Tapping Sequence:

  • Setup: "Even though I feel this anxiety in my chest and I'm afraid I might have a panic attack, I deeply and completely accept myself."
  • Eyebrow: "This anxiety in my chest"
  • Side of Eye: "This feeling of being overwhelmed"
  • Under Eye: "The fear of having a panic attack"
  • Under Nose: "This racing heart"
  • Chin: "This difficulty breathing"
  • Collarbone: "This feeling of being unsafe"
  • Under Arm: "I choose to release this anxiety"
  • Head: "I am safe in this moment"

During the tapping, Sarah was guided to notice and describe the physical sensations in her body, using pendulation to alternate between the anxiety and a sense of safety. Affirmations of self-compassion and resilience were also incorporated.

Outcomes: After several sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in the frequency and intensity of her panic attacks. She also reported feeling more grounded and connected to her body. She developed a greater sense of self-compassion and resilience, and was able to navigate social situations and work-related stress with greater ease.

Check Your Understanding

Question 1: Describe how you would customize a tapping sequence for a client who is highly sensitive and finds the Karate Chop point overwhelming.

For a highly sensitive client, you could omit the Karate Chop point and focus on gentler facial points, such as the Eyebrow, Side of Eye, and Under Eye points. You could also use a softer tapping pressure and a slower pace.

Question 2: Explain the purpose of pendulation in Somatic Experiencing and how it can be integrated with EFT.

Pendulation involves alternating between focusing on the traumatic memory and a safe, neutral sensation in the body. This helps to regulate the nervous system and prevent overwhelm. In EFT, you can guide the client to pendulate between the physical sensations of the trauma and a sense of safety during the tapping sequence.

Question 3: In what situations might surrogate tapping be most useful?

Surrogate tapping is most useful when a client is unable to physically tap on themselves due to physical limitations, emotional distress, or being in a location where tapping is not feasible. It can also be helpful for clients who are experiencing intense emotional distress or dissociation.
