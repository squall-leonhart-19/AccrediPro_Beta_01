Understanding Psychological Reversal

Psychological reversal, often abbreviated as PR, is a phenomenon where a person unconsciously resists healing or positive change. It's like an internal block that prevents them from fully benefiting from EFT or other therapeutic interventions. Think of it as a part of them that, paradoxically, doesn't *want* to get better.

Common signs and symptoms of PR include:

  • Resistance to change: Clients may express a desire for change but consistently sabotage their efforts.
  • Self-sabotage: Engaging in behaviors that undermine their progress, such as procrastination, negative self-talk, or unhealthy habits.
  • Negative thinking: A persistent focus on negative aspects and a difficulty in seeing positive possibilities.
  • Feeling "stuck": A sense of being trapped in their current situation, despite wanting to move forward.
  • Minimizing progress: Discounting or downplaying any positive changes they experience.

Causes of Psychological Reversal

PR can stem from various underlying beliefs and emotional blocks. Some common causes include:

  • Limiting beliefs: Deep-seated beliefs about oneself, the world, or what is possible. Examples include "I don't deserve to be happy," or "I'm not good enough."
  • Fear of change: Even positive change can be scary because it disrupts the familiar, even if the familiar is painful.
  • Secondary gain: Unconscious benefits derived from staying in the current state, such as attention, sympathy, or avoiding responsibility.
  • Guilt or shame: Feeling undeserving of happiness or success due to past actions or perceived flaws.
  • Protection mechanism: Believing that staying "sick" or "stuck" protects them from future pain or disappointment.

Understanding the root cause of PR is crucial for tailoring your EFT interventions effectively. By uncovering these underlying beliefs, you can help your clients release the blocks that are holding them back.

Techniques for Correcting Psychological Reversal

EFT provides several powerful techniques for addressing and correcting PR. These techniques often involve specific tapping points and carefully crafted statements.

  1. The Setup Statement: This is the cornerstone of addressing PR in EFT. It acknowledges the problem while simultaneously accepting oneself. A typical setup statement includes: "Even though I have this problem, I deeply and completely accept myself." You can customize the statement to address the specific issue and suspected cause of the PR. For example, "Even though I'm afraid of succeeding, I deeply and completely acceptmyself."
  2. The Karate Chop Point: While repeating the setup statement, tap firmly on the karate chop point (the fleshy part on the side of your hand). This helps to break down the resistance and prepare the energy system for change.
  3. Reminder Phrase: After the setup, use a reminder phrase that acknowledges the problem while tapping through the remaining EFT points. For example, "This fear of success" or "This resistance to change."
  4. Addressing Specific Beliefs: Once you've addressed the general PR, target the underlying beliefs that are fueling it. Use statements like, "I'm releasing the belief that I don't deserve to be happy" or "I'm letting go of the fear of change."

Here's an example of how to use EFT to address PR related to a fear of success:

  • Setup: "Even though I'm terrified of succeeding and what that might mean, I deeply and completely accept myself." (Tap the karate chop point while repeating this three times.)
  • Reminder Phrase: "This fear of success."
  • Tapping Sequence: Tap each of the EFT points (eyebrow, side of the eye, under the eye, under the nose, chin, collarbone, under the arm) while repeating the reminder phrase.
  • Follow-up: Continue tapping, addressing specific beliefs and fears related to success, such as "What if I can't handle the pressure?" or "What if I disappoint people?"

Case Study 1: Sarah's Procrastination

Client: Sarah, 35 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah consistently procrastinates on important work projects, leading to increased stress and anxiety. She expresses a strong desire to advance in her career but struggles to complete tasks.

Intervention: After initial EFT sessions focusing on anxiety reduction, it becomes clear that Sarah has underlying psychological reversal. She seems to unconsciously resist completing her work. The therapist suspects a fear of failure is contributing to this resistance.

EFT Script:

  • Setup: "Even though I keep procrastinating and I'm afraid of failing, I deeply and completely accept myself." (Karate Chop)
  • Reminder Phrase: "This procrastination"
  • Tapping Sequence: Eyebrow, Side of the Eye, Under the Eye, Under the Nose, Chin, Collarbone, Under the Arm â€“ repeating "This procrastination" at each point.
  • Follow-up: The therapist then guides Sarah to address her fear of failure with statements like, "I'm releasing the fear of failing" and "I'm open to the possibility of succeeding."

Outcomes: After several sessions addressing the PR, Sarah reports a significant reduction in procrastination. She is able to complete her work projects more efficiently and experiences less anxiety. She also reports feeling more confident in her abilities.

Case Study 2: John's Relationship Patterns

Client: John, 42 years old

Presenting Symptoms: John has a history of entering into short-term, unsatisfying relationships. He desires a long-term, committed partnership but consistently pushes potential partners away.

Intervention: Through exploration, it's revealed that John fears vulnerability and intimacy. He unconsciously believes he is not worthy of love and sabotages relationships before they become too serious. Psychological reversal is suspected due to this self-sabotaging behavior.

EFT Script:

  • Setup: "Even though I'm afraid of getting close to someone and I don't believe I deserve love, I deeply and completely accept myself." (Karate Chop)
  • Reminder Phrase: "This fear of intimacy"
  • Tapping Sequence: Eyebrow, Side of the Eye, Under the Eye, Under the Nose, Chin, Collarbone, Under the Arm â€“ repeating "This fear of intimacy" at each point.
  • Follow-up: The therapist assists John in addressing his feelings of unworthiness with statements such as, "I'm releasing the belief that I'm not worthy of love" and "I'm open to receiving love and connection."

Outcomes: Over time, John begins to challenge his negative beliefs about himself and his ability to form meaningful relationships. He reports feeling more comfortable with vulnerability and starts to build healthier, more fulfilling connections with others.

The Importance of Persistence

Addressing psychological reversal can sometimes be challenging and require persistence. It's important to remember that deeply ingrained beliefs and emotional blocks may take time to unravel. Don't get discouraged if you don't see immediate results. Keep exploring different angles, refining your statements, and tapping consistently.

It's also crucial to be flexible and adapt your interventions as needed. What works for one client may not work for another. Pay close attention to your client's responses and adjust your approach accordingly. Remember to continuously reassess for PR throughout the EFT process, as it can surface at different stages of healing.
