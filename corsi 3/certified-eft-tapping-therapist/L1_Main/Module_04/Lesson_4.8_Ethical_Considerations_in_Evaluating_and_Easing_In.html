Your Coaching Script

Here's how to begin a session focused on evaluating and easing intensity. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open about what you're experiencing. I understand the immense pressure nurses face, especially in a demanding environment like a hospital. It's completely valid to feel overwhelmed."
MARIA:
"It's just... constant. I feel like I'm always on edge. Even when I'm home, I can't relax."
YOU:
"I hear you. Let's use EFT tapping to help you evaluate the intensity of those feelings and then gently ease them. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 10 being the most intense anxiety you've ever felt, what number would you give your anxiety right now?"
MARIA:
"Probably an 8. My chest feels tight, and I'm starting to sweat."
YOU:
"Okay, an 8. Let's start by acknowledging that feeling. Repeat after me: 'Even though I feel this anxiety at an 8, and my chest feels tight, I deeply and completely accept myself.'"
YOU:
"Now, let's move through the tapping points while focusing on that feeling of anxiety. We'll check in again afterward to see if the intensity has shifted."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before beginning EFT tapping?

Validating the client's feelings builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to the EFT process. It also acknowledges the legitimacy of their experience.

2. What is the purpose of using a 0-10 scale to assess the intensity of a client's emotions?

The 0-10 scale provides a quantifiable measure of the client's emotional state, allowing you to track progress and adjust the EFT approach as needed. It gives both you and the client a clear benchmark.

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate your client's feelings before starting EFT.
  • Use a 0-10 scale to quantify the intensity of their emotions.
  • Ensure the client understands and actively participates in the tapping process.
  • Reassess the intensity of their emotions after each round of tapping.
  • Empower your client to use EFT on their own between sessions.
