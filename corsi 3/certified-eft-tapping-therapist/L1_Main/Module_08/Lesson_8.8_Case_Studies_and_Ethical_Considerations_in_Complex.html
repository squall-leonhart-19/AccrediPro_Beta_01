Your Coaching Script

Here's a script to guide your initial interaction with Diane. Remember to adapt it to your own style and the specific needs of the client.

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you're carrying a heavy load right now. Before we get started, I want to assure you that it's absolutely possible to regain a sense of calm and control, even amidst these challenges."
DIANE:
"I hope so. I feel like I'm constantly on edge."
YOU:
"I understand. Many of my clients come to me feeling the same way. We're going to explore how EFT can help you shift those feelings. Have you heard of EFT or tapping before?"

"I've read a little bit about it online. Something about tapping on acupressure points?"
YOU:
"That's right. EFT is a simple but powerful technique that combines elements of cognitive therapy and acupressure. We use our fingertips to tap on specific points on the body while focusing on the issue at hand. This helps to calm the nervous system and release emotional blockages."
YOU:
"Would you be open to trying a short tapping sequence to address your anxiety right now? It will give you a feel for how it works, and we can see how your body responds."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before introducing EFT?

Validating the client's feelings builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to trying a new technique like EFT. It also ensures they feel heard and understood.

2. What are some simple ways to explain EFT to a client who has never heard of it?

You can explain EFT as a combination of cognitive therapy and acupressure, where tapping on specific points helps calm the nervous system and release emotional blockages. Avoid technical jargon and focus on the potential benefits.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize building rapport and trust before introducing EFT.
  • Explain EFT in simple, accessible language, avoiding technical terms.
  • Validate the client's feelings and acknowledge their struggles.
  • Respect the client's boundaries and don't force EFT if they're hesitant.
  • Practice your introductory dialogue to build confidence and fluency.
