Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to approach this conversation using advanced reframing. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing your vulnerability with me. It takes courage to admit feeling 'stuck' after dedicating so much time to your career. I want to acknowledge that your feelings are valid, and it's completely understandable to feel this way."
DIANE:
"It's just... everyone tells me I should be happy. I have a stable job, a pension... but I feel like something's missing."
YOU:
"And it's okay to want more, Diane. Let's reframe this 'stuck' feeling. What if we saw it as a chrysalis stage? A period of transformation before something beautiful emerges? Your 28 years of teaching haven't just been about lesson plans; they've equipped you with invaluable skills."
DIANE:
"Skills? Like what? Wrangling 8-year-olds?"
YOU:
"Exactly! Think about it: You're a master communicator, a patient leader, a creative problem-solver, and an expert at motivating others. These are highly sought-after skills in countless fields. What if we explored how those strengths could translate into a new chapter? A chapter where you're not just teaching children, but perhaps mentoring adults, leading workshops, or even writing curriculum?"

Key Takeaways

  • Validate the client's feelings of being "stuck" as understandable and valid.
  • Use reframing techniques to shift the client's perspective on their current situation.
  • Challenge limiting core beliefs by questioning their absolute truth.
  • Connect the client's existing skills and experience to potential new opportunities.
  • Encourage small steps and exploration without pressure to make drastic changes.
