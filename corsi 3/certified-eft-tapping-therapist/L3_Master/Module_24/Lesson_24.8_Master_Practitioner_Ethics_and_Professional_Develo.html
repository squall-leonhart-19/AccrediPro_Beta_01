The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 35, Anxiety

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily has generalized anxiety. We did some tapping, and she felt a bit better, but then she started crying and talking about childhood trauma. I didn't know what to do, so I just kept tapping on 'Even though I feel anxious...' but I felt like I was making it worse."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Did Sarah have a clear treatment plan?
  • How did Sarah handle the emotional intensity?
  • Was Sarah prepared for potential trauma responses?
  • Scope of practice considerations.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

The Importance of a Treatment Plan

Explain that even with EFT's flexibility, a basic plan prevents feeling lost. Discuss how to set session goals.

2

Emotional Regulation Techniques

Review grounding techniques and the importance of pacing. Discuss how to help a client regulate intense emotions during a session.

3

Recognizing Trauma Responses

Help Sarah identify signs of trauma activation. Emphasize the need for specialized training and referral when appropriate.

4

Self-Care and Boundaries

Discuss the importance of Sarah's own self-care and setting boundaries to avoid burnout and vicarious trauma.

Crafting Your Feedback

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's wonderful that you're reaching out. It shows you care deeply about your clients. What did you learn from this session?"
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What did you notice about Emily's response as you continued tapping? What was your internal experience?"
Offer Guidance
"When a client brings up trauma, it's often best to gently acknowledge it and then shift the focus back to the present moment and their current feelings. For example: 'That sounds incredibly painful. Right now, in this moment, what are you feeling?'"
Empower Next Steps
"What adjustments would you make for your next session with Emily? How can you ensure you're both safe and effective?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to establish with a new mentee early on?

Establishing clear expectations and boundaries for the mentoring relationship is crucial. This includes frequency of meetings, communication methods, and the scope of your support.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with imposter syndrome?

Share your own experiences with self-doubt and highlight their strengths and accomplishments. Focus on their growth and progress, rather than perfection.

You're Shaping the Future!

By guiding new practitioners, you're expanding the reach of EFT and ensuring high-quality care for more people. Your mentorship is invaluable.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding and supporting new EFT practitioners.
  • A treatment plan, even flexible, is essential for effective sessions.
  • Emotional regulation and recognizing trauma responses are critical skills.
  • Constructive feedback should be affirming and empowering.
  • Self-care and boundaries are crucial for both you and your mentee.
