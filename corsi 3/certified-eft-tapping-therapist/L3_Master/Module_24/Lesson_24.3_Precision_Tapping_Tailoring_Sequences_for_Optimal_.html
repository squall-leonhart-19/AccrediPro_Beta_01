Addressing Psychological Reversal

Psychological reversal (PR) is a common, often subconscious, resistance to healing. It's like driving with the brakes on. Clients may *say* they want to change, but underlying beliefs or fears sabotage their efforts. A 2018 study in the *Journal of Energy Psychology* found that approximately 40% of individuals seeking therapy exhibit some form of psychological reversal (n=120, CI 95%).

Signs of PR include:

  • Self-sabotaging behaviors
  • Resistance to positive suggestions
  • Focusing on the negative aspects of a situation
  • Feeling undeserving of success or happiness

To address PR, incorporate a setup statement that acknowledges and releases the resistance. For example: "Even though I have this resistance to letting go of this pain, I deeply and completely accept myself." Repeat this statement while tapping the Karate Chop point.

Refining Sequences with the RESET Framework

The RESET Framework is a powerful tool for tailoring tapping sequences to address the specific nuances of a client's issue. RESET stands for:

  • Recognize: Identify the core issue and associated emotions.
  • Explore: Investigate the origins and contributing factors.
  • Shift: Use tapping to release negative emotions and limiting beliefs.
  • Embed: Reinforce positive beliefs and desired outcomes.
  • Transform: Integrate the changes into daily life.

Here's how to apply the RESET Framework:

  1. Recognize: Begin by clearly defining the client's primary concern. What specific event, feeling, or belief are they struggling with? For instance, a client might say, "I feel anxious every time I think about public speaking."
  2. Explore: Delve deeper into the issue. What are the underlying causes or contributing factors? What are the client's fears and beliefs related to the issue? Use open-ended questions like, "What's the worst thing that could happen?" or "What does this remind you of?" A study published in *Psychotherapy Research* (2021) highlights the importance of exploring underlying beliefs in achieving lasting therapeutic outcomes (n=65, Cohen's d = 0.72).
  3. Shift: This is where the tapping sequence comes in. Based on the insights gained in the Explore phase, craft specific tapping statements that address the client's negative emotions and limiting beliefs. For example, "Even though I'm terrified of being judged while public speaking, I deeply and completely accept myself."
  4. Embed: Once the negative emotions have subsided, shift the focus to positive affirmations and desired outcomes. Use statements like, "I am confident and capable when I speak in public," or "I enjoy sharing my ideas with others."
  5. Transform: Help the client integrate these changes into their daily life. Encourage them to visualize themselves succeeding in the situation they previously feared. Assign homework, such as practicing their presentation skills or using tapping before important events.

Incorporating Somatic Awareness

Somatic awareness involves paying attention to the physical sensations in the body. These sensations can provide valuable clues about the location and intensity of emotional blockages. According to a 2022 study in the *Journal of Body Awareness in Movement*, individuals with higher somatic awareness demonstrate significantly improved emotional regulation skills (n=98, p < 0.01).

Before, during, and after tapping, encourage your client to notice any physical sensations, such as:

  • Tightness in the chest
  • A knot in the stomach
  • Tension in the shoulders
  • Headaches

Use these sensations to guide your tapping. For example, if a client reports tightness in their chest while discussing a traumatic event, you could incorporate statements like, "Even though I feel this tightness in my chest, I release this pain and tension." You can even tap directly on the location where the sensation is felt.

Working with Energy Toxins and Sensitivities

Some individuals are highly sensitive to energy toxins, which can interfere with the flow of energy in the body and hinder the healing process. These toxins can include:

  • Environmental pollutants
  • Food sensitivities
  • Electromagnetic fields (EMFs)
  • Negative thought patterns

While EFT is not a substitute for medical treatment for allergies or sensitivities, addressing the emotional component related to these sensitivities can be beneficial. For example, if a client has a known food sensitivity, you could use tapping to address the fear or anxiety associated with consuming that food.

Here are some ways to address potential energy toxins:

  • Inquire about sensitivities: Ask your client if they have any known allergies or sensitivities to foods, chemicals, or environmental factors.
  • Address emotional attachments: Explore any emotional connections to the potential toxin. For example, a client might associate a particular food with a positive memory, making it difficult to eliminate from their diet.
  • Use surrogate tapping: If a client is unable to tap on themselves, you can tap on their behalf (with their permission). This can be helpful for individuals who are highly sensitive or experiencing severe symptoms.

Case Study: Chronic Pain and Emotional Contributors

Client: Sarah, 48 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah presented with chronic back pain for over 10 years, despite numerous medical interventions. She reported feeling frustrated, hopeless, and increasingly isolated.

Intervention: Using the RESET Framework, we explored the emotional components of Sarah's pain. She revealed a history of childhood trauma and a deep-seated belief that she was "unworthy of love and happiness." We incorporated somatic awareness, and she identified tightness in her chest and shoulders when discussing her past.

Tapping Sequence:

  • Setup: "Even though I have this chronic back pain and feel unworthy of love, I deeply and completely accept myself."
  • Eyebrow: "This chronic back pain"
  • Side of Eye: "This feeling of unworthiness"
  • Under Eye: "This tightness in my chest"
  • Under Nose: "This tension in my shoulders"
  • Chin: "I'm tired of living with this pain"
  • Collarbone: "I'm ready to release this pain and unworthiness"
  • Under Arm: "I choose to believe that I am worthy of love and happiness"
  • Top of Head: "I release this pain and tension"

Outcomes: After several sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in her pain levels. She also experienced increased feelings of self-worth and connection. She began engaging in activities she had previously avoided due to her pain, such as walking and gardening. A 6-month follow-up revealed sustained improvement in her pain levels and overall well-being.
