The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 48

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily, 48, has childhood trauma. During tapping, she gets triggered and dissociates. I'm not sure how to proceed safely. I'm also feeling really drained after our sessions."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Sarah using titration effectively?
  • Is she aware of her own countertransference?
  • Are appropriate grounding techniques being used?
  • Does Emily need a referral for more specialized trauma therapy?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Safety First

Emphasize the importance of client safety. Dissociation is a red flag. Review titration techniques: slowing down, focusing on the body, using gentle language.

2

Self-Care for the Therapist

Address Sarah's feelings of being drained. Teach her about countertransference. "It's normal to feel things. The key is to recognize it and not let it influence your work." Recommend grounding exercises for herself after sessions.

3

Scope of Practice

Discuss the ethical considerations. Is this case beyond Sarah's current skill level? Is a referral needed? "It's okay to say, 'I'm not the best fit for this,' and help the client find someone who is."

4

Empowerment Through Knowledge

Suggest resources: books, articles, workshops on trauma-informed EFT. Remind her that continuous learning is part of being a professional.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Effort
"Sarah, I commend you for seeking supervision. It shows you're committed to doing what's best for Emily."
Validate Feelings
"It's completely understandable to feel drained when working with trauma. It's intense work. Let's explore what's happening for you."
Offer Guidance
"With dissociation, we want to be extra cautious. What grounding techniques are you using with Emily? Let's brainstorm some others."
Empower Decision-Making
"Given what you know now, what do you think is the most ethical course of action? I trust your judgment."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important consideration when a client dissociates during a tapping session?

Client safety is paramount. The practitioner should use titration techniques and grounding exercises to bring the client back to the present moment.

2. How should a supervisor address a mentee's feelings of being drained after a session?

The supervisor should validate the mentee's feelings, discuss countertransference, and recommend self-care strategies.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about guiding, not directing.
  • Prioritize client safety above all else.
  • Address the mentee's well-being and emotional responses.
  • Encourage ethical awareness and continuous learning.
