Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Scope of Practice

Review the boundaries of Level 1 EFT training. Highlight that while EFT can address anxiety, complex trauma requires specialized training and experience.

2

Teach Titration and Pacing

Explain the importance of titration (gradually approaching sensitive topics) and pacing (allowing the client to set the speed). This prevents overwhelming the client and promotes safety.

3

Discuss Referral Protocols

Provide Sarah with resources for finding qualified trauma therapists in her area. Role-play how to discuss a referral with Emily in a supportive manner.

4

Address Countertransference

Help Sarah explore any personal feelings or experiences that might be influencing her work with Emily. Encourage self-care and supervision to manage these dynamics.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of understanding scope of practice when supervising a new EFT practitioner?

Understanding the scope of practice ensures that the practitioner is working within their level of competence and ethical boundaries, protecting both the practitioner and the client.

2. How can you help a new practitioner recognize and manage countertransference in their client work?

Encourage self-reflection, provide a safe space to discuss personal feelings and experiences, and emphasize the importance of self-care and seeking supervision when needed.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves guiding new practitioners to work ethically and within their scope of practice.
  • Titration and pacing are crucial when working with clients who have experienced trauma.
  • Referral protocols ensure clients receive the appropriate level of care when complex issues arise.
  • Addressing countertransference helps practitioners maintain objectivity and provide effective support.
