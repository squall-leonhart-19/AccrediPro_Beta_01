The Subjective Units of Distress Scale (SUDS)

The Subjective Units of Distress Scale (SUDS) is a cornerstone of EFT assessment. It's a simple yet powerful tool that allows clients to quantify their emotional intensity on a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 represents no distress and 10 represents the highest level of distress imaginable. Using SUDS at the beginning, during, and end of an EFT session provides immediate feedback on the effectiveness of the tapping. A 2016 meta-analysis published in the Journal of Nervous & Mental Disease, including 14 studies (n=658), found that EFT significantly reduced SUDS scores compared to control groups (d = 0.87, 95% CI [0.62, 1.12]).

Using SUDS effectively:

  • Establish a baseline: Ask the client to rate their distress level before tapping.
  • Monitor progress: Periodically check SUDS levels during the tapping sequence.
  • Evaluate outcomes: Assess SUDS levels after tapping to determine the session's impact.

Adapting Standard Measures to EFT

While SUDS is invaluable, adapting standard assessment measures to align with EFT principles can provide a more comprehensive understanding of the client's experience. For example, consider the Beck Anxiety Inventory (BAI). Instead of simply administering the BAI, we can use it to identify specific anxiety-provoking situations or thoughts, and then use EFT to target those specific triggers. This approach directly addresses the 'Recognize & Reframe' stage of the R.E.S.E.T. Frameworkâ„¢, allowing us to tailor our interventions to the client's unique needs.

Example adaptation:

  1. Administer the BAI to identify key anxiety symptoms.
  2. For each symptom, ask the client to rate their SUDS level.
  3. Use EFT to target the highest-rated symptoms.
  4. Re-administer the BAI after several EFT sessions to assess overall change.

Developing Individualized Assessment Protocols

Every client is unique, and their assessment protocols should reflect that. Individualized assessment protocols allow us to focus on the client's specific goals and challenges, providing a more targeted and effective approach. This aligns with the 'Tailor & Track' stage of the R.E.S.E.T. Frameworkâ„¢, ensuring that our interventions are personalized and data-driven.

Steps to create an individualized protocol:

  1. Identify the client's primary concerns: What are their biggest challenges and goals?
  2. Select relevant assessment measures: Choose tools that align with their concerns.
  3. Establish a baseline: Administer the measures before starting EFT.
  4. Monitor progress: Regularly re-administer the measures to track changes.
  5. Adjust the protocol as needed: Be flexible and adapt the protocol based on the client's progress.

Case Study: Trauma and SUDS

Case Study: Sarah, 34, Trauma Recovery

Client: Sarah, a 34-year-old survivor of a car accident.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah experienced flashbacks, nightmares, and severe anxiety related to the accident. She reported a constant state of hyperarousal and difficulty concentrating. Initial SUDS rating for accident-related memories was 9/10.

Intervention: Over 12 weeks, Sarah underwent weekly EFT sessions. Each session began with Sarah identifying a specific aspect of the trauma (e.g., the sound of screeching tires) and rating her SUDS level. Tapping sequences targeted these specific memories and associated emotions.

Outcomes: After 12 weeks, Sarah's SUDS rating for accident-related memories decreased to 2/10. She reported a significant reduction in flashbacks and nightmares, and her overall anxiety levels decreased. She was able to drive again without experiencing panic attacks. Her scores on the Impact of Event Scale-Revised (IES-R) also showed significant improvement (p < 0.01).
