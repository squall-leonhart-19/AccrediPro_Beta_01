Clinical Reasoning Process

Applying Assessment Tools

Step 1: Initial Assessment & Screening
Begin with a comprehensive intake, including the Subjective Units of Distress Scale (SUDS), Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item (GAD-7) scale, and Patient Health Questionnaire-9 (PHQ-9) to quantify anxiety and depression symptoms. A childhood trauma questionnaire (CTQ) can provide insight into past experiences.
Step 2: Identify Key Emotional Themes
Use open-ended questions and active listening to explore the clientâ€™s emotional landscape. Identify recurring themes such as fear of failure, perfectionism, difficulty setting boundaries, and unresolved grief related to childhood experiences.
Step 3: Physiological & Somatic Assessment
Given the fibromyalgia diagnosis and IBS/GERD symptoms, consider a body scan meditation to assess body awareness and identify areas of tension or pain. Explore the connection between emotional states and physical symptoms. For example, ask: "Where do you feel the anxiety in your body?"
Step 4: Evaluate Belief Systems & Cognitive Patterns
Employ the Cognitive Distortions Checklist to identify negative thought patterns contributing to anxiety and overwhelm. Explore core beliefs related to self-worth, competence, and safety. For instance, "I'm not good enough," "I have to do everything perfectly," or "It's not safe to express my needs."
Step 5: Assess for Secondary Gain
Gently explore potential secondary gains associated with the client's symptoms. Is she receiving attention or avoiding responsibilities? This is a sensitive area, and should be approached with empathy and curiosity.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Hormonal Imbalance

At age 48, perimenopause could be exacerbating migraines, fatigue, and anxiety. Consider hormonal testing.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Chronic stress, GERD medication, and poor sleep can lead to deficiencies in key nutrients like magnesium, B vitamins, and vitamin D.

3

Sleep Apnea

Difficulty sleeping and fatigue could indicate sleep apnea. Refer for a sleep study if suspected.

4

Underlying Autoimmune Condition

Fibromyalgia is often associated with other autoimmune disorders. Further investigation may be warranted.

  • Elevated liver enzymes or other abnormal lab results require medical evaluation.
  • Suicidal ideation or self-harm requires immediate mental health intervention.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to assess for potential secondary gains in complex cases like Eleanor's?

Assessing for secondary gains helps to understand unconscious motivations that may be perpetuating the client's symptoms. It's essential to approach this topic with sensitivity and empathy, as it can uncover deeply rooted emotional needs.

2. How can you adapt EFT/Tapping techniques to address Eleanor's skepticism and prior negative experience with EFT?

Begin by educating Eleanor about the neuroscientific basis of EFT and its effectiveness in regulating the nervous system. Start with simple, non-threatening tapping sequences focused on stress reduction and relaxation. Emphasize that EFT is a collaborative process and that her feedback is valued. Validate her skepticism and acknowledge that it's okay if she doesn't feel immediate results.

3. What are some key questions you might ask Eleanor to explore the connection between her childhood trauma and current physical symptoms?

You might ask: "When you experience a migraine, what emotions are you feeling?" "Are there any memories or situations from your childhood that come to mind when you're in pain?" "How do you cope with stress and emotional pain?" "Do you feel safe expressing your needs and emotions?"

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a multi-faceted assessment approach, integrating psychological, physiological, and lifestyle factors.
  • EFT/Tapping can be a powerful tool for addressing emotional and physical symptoms, but it's essential to tailor the approach to the individual client's needs and preferences.
  • Building a strong therapeutic alliance and creating a safe space for emotional exploration are crucial for successful outcomes.
  • Referral to other healthcare professionals (MD, nutritionist, etc.) may be necessary to address underlying medical conditions and nutrient deficiencies.
