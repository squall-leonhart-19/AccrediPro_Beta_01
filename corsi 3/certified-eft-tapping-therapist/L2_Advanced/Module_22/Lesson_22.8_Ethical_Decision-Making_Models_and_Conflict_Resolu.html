Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Steps

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
Eleanor presents with multiple vulnerabilities, including potential substance abuse and unresolved grief. The ethical dilemma is how to provide effective EFT therapy while ensuring her safety, respecting her autonomy, and maintaining professional boundaries.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Refer to the EFT International Code of Conduct, which emphasizes competence, confidentiality, informed consent, and avoiding harm. Also, consider relevant state licensing board regulations regarding substance abuse and mental health referrals.
Step 3: Consider Client Autonomy and Well-being
Eleanor has the right to make her own decisions, but her judgment may be impaired by alcohol use and emotional distress. Balance respecting her autonomy with the duty to protect her from harm.
Step 4: Evaluate Practitioner Competence
Assess your own competence in addressing substance abuse and complex grief. If you lack specialized training, consider co-therapy or referral to a qualified addiction specialist or grief counselor.
Step 5: Document the Decision-Making Process
Maintain detailed records of your assessment, consultations, and treatment plan. Document the rationale for your ethical decisions and any deviations from standard practice.

Assess for potential codependency or unhealthy attachment patterns in Eleanor's marriage. Marital therapy may be a necessary adjunct to individual EFT.

4

Medical Conditions

Encourage Eleanor to consult with her physician to rule out any underlying medical conditions contributing to her anxiety and insomnia (e.g., thyroid dysfunction, hormonal imbalances).

When to Refer

Red Flags and Scope of Practice

  • Suicidal ideation or intent: Immediate referral to mental health crisis services.
  • Active alcohol dependence: Referral to addiction specialist or treatment program.
  • Severe marital conflict with risk of violence: Referral to couples therapist with expertise in domestic violence.
  • Uncontrolled anxiety despite EFT interventions: Referral to psychiatrist for medication evaluation.
  • Client requests services outside your scope of practice: Be honest about your limitations and provide appropriate referrals.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key ethical considerations when working with a client who has a history of childhood emotional neglect?

Key ethical considerations include: (1) Recognizing the potential for re-traumatization and using a gentle, paced approach. (2) Being aware of transference and countertransference issues related to attachment. (3) Ensuring clear boundaries and informed consent.

2. How would you address a situation where a client discloses information about illegal activities during an EFT session?

The appropriate response depends on the nature of the illegal activity and relevant legal requirements. Generally, you should: (1) Clarify the limits of confidentiality at the outset of therapy. (2) Consult with a legal professional or ethics board for guidance. (3) Document the disclosure and your response. (4) Encourage the client to seek legal counsel.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical decision-making in EFT practice requires careful consideration of client autonomy, practitioner competence, and relevant ethical codes.
  • Complex client presentations with co-occurring disorders demand a phased intervention plan that addresses multiple vulnerabilities.
  • Referral to other professionals is essential when client needs exceed practitioner competence or scope of practice.
  • Documenting the clinical reasoning process and ethical decisions is crucial for accountability and risk management.
