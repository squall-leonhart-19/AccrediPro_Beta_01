Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Introduce the Triage Concept

Explain that not all information is equally important. Teach her to identify the most pressing issues (e.g., insomnia significantly impacting daily function) and address those first.

2

Emphasize the Importance of the 'C' in COSMOS

Remind her to consider the Celestial Context. Are there any immediate transits or astrological events that might be contributing to the client's current state? For instance, a challenging Saturn transit could amplify feelings of anxiety and restriction. Understanding this can provide a focused lens for intervention.

3

Teach the Art of Asking Targeted Questions

Instead of broad, open-ended questions, guide her to ask specific questions that help narrow down the root cause. For example, "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much does lack of sleep affect your ability to perform your job?"

4

Share a Prioritization Framework

Introduce a simple framework like the "Urgent-Important Matrix". Help her categorize client data based on urgency and importance to create a clear action plan.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of effective supervision?

The primary goal is to empower the mentee to develop their own critical thinking and problem-solving skills, rather than creating dependency.

2. How can you help a mentee prioritize client information?

Introduce frameworks like the Urgent-Important Matrix, emphasize the Celestial Context (C in COSMOS), and guide them to ask targeted questions to narrow down the most critical issues.
