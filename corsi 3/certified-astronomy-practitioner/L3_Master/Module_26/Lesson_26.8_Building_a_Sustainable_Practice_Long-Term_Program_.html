The Case She Presents

Priya's Client Case: Sarah, 52

What Priya Tells You:

"Sarah came to me feeling lost and overwhelmed. I did a Stellar Strengths reading, and it showed a strong alignment with creativity but a blockage in career. I suggested she take an art class, but she said she doesn't have time. I'm not sure what else to recommend."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Priya explore Sarah's values and interests beyond the reading?
  • Was the art class suggestion too prescriptive without understanding Sarah's lifestyle?
  • Did she consider other approaches to unlock creativity?
  • Does Priya understand how to tailor recommendations to the client's reality?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Priya

1

Active Listening is Key

Emphasize the importance of truly hearing the client's concerns and limitations before offering solutions. The Orbital Observation phase is critical.

2

Explore Alternatives Together

Instead of giving direct answers, brainstorm with Priya. "What other ways could Sarah express her creativity if an art class isn't feasible?"

3

Connect to Daily Life

Help Priya see how creativity can be integrated into Sarah's existing routine. Even small changes can make a big difference.

4

Empower Client Autonomy

Remind Priya that the goal is to guide, not dictate. Sarah needs to feel ownership of her journey. Mapping Milestones should be collaborative.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Priya, I'm impressed with your enthusiasm and your dedication to helping Sarah. It's clear you care deeply about your clients."
Inquire About Her Process
"Tell me more about how you arrived at the art class recommendation. What were you hoping to achieve, and what did Sarah say that made you pause?"
Offer a Different Perspective
"Sometimes, the most powerful interventions are the simplest. What if Sarah started by spending just 15 minutes a day doodling in a notebook, or listening to uplifting music during her commute? Baby steps can lead to big transformations."
Encourage Experimentation
"What if you and Sarah brainstormed a list of 10 creative activities, big and small, and she committed to trying one new thing each week? How would that feel to you, and to her?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to remember when mentoring a new practitioner?

Creating a safe space for them to learn and grow without fear of judgment.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with a client case?

By asking open-ended questions, encouraging them to explore different perspectives, and offering support without giving direct answers.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Active listening is crucial for understanding the mentee's perspective.
  • Constructive feedback should be encouraging and empowering.
  • Share your own experiences to normalize the learning process.
  • Celebrate small victories and progress.
