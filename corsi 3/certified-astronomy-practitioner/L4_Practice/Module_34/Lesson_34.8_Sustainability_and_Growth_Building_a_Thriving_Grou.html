Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand, Susan. We have a couple of payment options available to make it more accessible. Many participants find the value they receive ‚Äì the sense of community, the clarity of purpose ‚Äì far outweighs the investment. What feels like a comfortable investment for you?"
"I'm worried I won't have enough time."
"That's a valid concern. The weekly sessions are 90 minutes, and we encourage about an hour of personal reflection each week. However, it's flexible. The recordings are available if you miss a session, and you can engage with the community at your own pace. Think of it as investing in yourself and your well-being."
"I'm not sure if I'm 'astronomy material'."
"Absolutely no prior knowledge is needed! 'Celestial Pathways' is designed for beginners. It's more about self-discovery through the lens of astronomy. If you're curious and open to exploring, you're definitely 'astronomy material'!"
"Can I talk to someone who's already done the program?"
"Of course! I'd be happy to connect you with one of our alumni who can share their experience. Would you prefer to speak with someone in a similar age range or with similar interests?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are two key elements to highlight when presenting the value of a group program?

Community support and personalized insights are key elements to emphasize.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the time commitment required for a group program?

Acknowledge the concern, highlight the flexibility of the program (recordings, self-paced engagement), and reframe it as an investment in their well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on building genuine connections with potential group program participants.
  • Understand their needs and desires to effectively present your program as a solution.
  • Confidently address objections with empathy and offer flexible solutions.
  • Clearly communicate the value and benefits of your group program.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to feel comfortable and confident stating your fees.
