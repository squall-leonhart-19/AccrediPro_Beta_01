Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the technical skills for this."
"That's a common concern, Susan. However, this program is designed for people of all backgrounds, even those with limited technical experience. We provide step-by-step guidance and support, and many of our most successful graduates started with little to no prior knowledge. Plus, our community is incredibly supportive and always willing to help each other out."
"I'm worried I won't be able to make any money with this."
"I understand your concern about the financial aspect, especially on a fixed income. While I can't guarantee income, I can tell you that many of my graduates are earning a substantial income using the skills they learned in this program. For example, Maria, a former teacher in her late 40s, now earns over $3,000 per month offering astrology readings online. We provide comprehensive training on marketing and client acquisition to help you build a successful practice."
"It sounds interesting, but I need to do more research."
"Absolutely, Susan. I want you to feel completely confident in your decision. What specific information are you looking for? I can provide case studies, testimonials, and a detailed program syllabus to help you make an informed choice. Would that be helpful?"
"I need to discuss it with my family."
"That's completely understandable. Would it be helpful if I provided you with a summary of the program and its benefits that you can share with them? I'm also happy to answer any questions they might have. Perhaps we could schedule a brief follow-up call together?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you handle the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the potential ROI, both financially and in terms of improved quality of life.

3. What is a good closing line to use on a discovery call?

"Based on everything you've shared, I genuinely believe this program can help you [achieve their goals]. Are you ready to enroll?"
