Client Case Selection

Choosing the right client case is crucial for the success of your capstone project. Ideally, you should select a case that allows you to demonstrate a strong understanding of the C.O.S.M.O.S. Frameworkâ„¢ and its practical application. Consider the following criteria when selecting your client case:

  • Relevance: Choose a case that aligns with your areas of interest and expertise. This will make the project more engaging and allow you to draw on your existing knowledge.
  • Complexity: Select a case that presents a sufficient level of complexity to allow you to demonstrate your analytical skills. Avoid cases that are too straightforward or too overwhelming.

Ethical Considerations

As a Certified Astronomy Practitionerâ„¢, you are expected to adhere to the highest ethical standards. This includes maintaining confidentiality, obtaining informed consent, and respecting the client's autonomy. In the context of the capstone project, it is essential to demonstrate your awareness of these ethical considerations and your commitment to upholding them.

Specifically, you should address the following ethical considerations in your capstone project:

  • Informed Consent: Ensure that you have obtained informed consent from the client before using their information in your project. Explain the purpose of the project, how their information will be used, and their right to withdraw at any time.
  • Confidentiality: Protect the client's privacy by anonymizing their data and avoiding the disclosure of any sensitive information.
  • Boundaries: Maintain professional boundaries with the client and avoid engaging in any activities that could compromise your objectivity or impartiality.
  • Competence: Only provide services that are within your scope of practice and expertise. Refer clients to other professionals when necessary.
