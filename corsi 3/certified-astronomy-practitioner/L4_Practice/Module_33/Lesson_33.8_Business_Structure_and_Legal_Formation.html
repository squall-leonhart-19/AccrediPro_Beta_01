Handling Objections with Grace

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I believe in astrology."
"I understand your skepticism. My approach is less about 'belief' and more about using symbolic language to gain self-awareness and clarity. Many clients find that even without prior belief, they resonate deeply with the insights they receive."
"I don't have the time for a three-month program."
"I appreciate your concern about time. The program is designed to be flexible and integrate into your lifestyle. We meet for one hour per week, and the exercises can be done at your own pace. Think of it as investing in your own well-being and future fulfillment."
"I need to think about it."
"That's perfectly fine. What specifically would you like to think about? Perhaps I can provide more information or clarify any concerns you have to help you make a confident decision."
"This feels like a luxury expense."
"I understand. Consider this an investment in your happiness and fulfillment during retirement. What is that worth to you? Many clients find that the renewed sense of purpose and excitement they gain from the program is priceless."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the recommended discovery call structure?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need for time, then ask clarifying questions to understand their specific concerns and offer additional information or support.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural and conversational.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections to address concerns effectively.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and its potential impact on your clients' lives.
  • Believe in your ability to transform lives and create a thriving, fulfilling practice.
