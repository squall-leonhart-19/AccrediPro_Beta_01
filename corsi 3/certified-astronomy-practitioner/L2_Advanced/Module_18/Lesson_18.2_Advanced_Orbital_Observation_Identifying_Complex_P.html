Synastry and Composite Charts

Synastry is the art of comparing two natal charts to assess relationship dynamics. It reveals areas of harmony, challenge, and potential growth between individuals. Composite charts, on the other hand, create a new chart based on the midpoints between planets in two natal charts, representing the "relationship" as a separate entity.

Key considerations in synastry include:

  • Sun-Moon Aspects: These aspects indicate fundamental compatibility and emotional connection. Harmonious aspects (conjunction, trine, sextile) suggest ease and understanding, while challenging aspects (square, opposition) can create tension and conflict.
  • Venus-Mars Aspects: These aspects reveal the level of romantic and sexual attraction. Positive aspects enhance passion and desire, while difficult aspects may lead to misunderstandings or power struggles.
  • Saturn Aspects: Saturn aspects can create stability and commitment, but also restriction and control. Itâ€™s crucial to assess whether these aspects are supportive or stifling.

Composite charts offer a broader perspective on the relationship's purpose and potential. Pay attention to the sign and house placement of the composite Sun, Moon, and Ascendant, as well as any prominent planetary configurations.

Transits to Natal Chart

Transits are the current positions of the planets as they move through the sky, and their aspects to planets in the natal chart can trigger significant life events and psychological shifts. Understanding transits is crucial for predictive work.

Hereâ€™s how to interpret transits:

  • Outer Planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto): These planets have longer cycles and their transits bring about profound, long-lasting changes.
  • Inner Planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars): These planets move more quickly and their transits tend to trigger short-term events and emotional fluctuations.
  • House Placement: The house in the natal chart that is being transited indicates the area of life that will be most affected.

For example, a transit of Saturn through the 7th house of relationships could indicate a period of increased commitment or challenges in partnerships. A transit of Uranus conjunct the natal Sun might bring unexpected opportunities or disruptions in career and identity.

Important Note: Always consider the natal chart's overall themes and potentials when interpreting transits. A challenging transit may not necessarily be negative if the individual has the inner resources to navigate it successfully.

Predictive Timing Techniques

Beyond transits, several other predictive timing techniques can help you identify key periods in a client's life:

  • Solar Returns: A solar return chart is cast for the moment the Sun returns to its natal position each year. It provides insights into the themes and challenges of the coming year.
  • Lunar Returns: Similar to solar returns, lunar returns chart the Moon's return to its natal position each month, highlighting emotional trends.
  • Progressions: Progressions involve symbolically advancing the planets in the natal chart to represent the unfolding of time. They reveal deeper, more gradual shifts in personality and life direction.

By combining these techniques with your knowledge of transits and cycles, you can provide clients with a comprehensive roadmap for navigating their life journey.
