Ethical Considerations

As a Certified Astronomy Practitionerâ„¢, you must adhere to strict ethical guidelines. This includes:

  • Informed Consent: Ensuring clients understand the nature of your services.
  • Scope of Practice: Staying within your area of expertise and referring clients to other professionals when necessary.

Common Pitfalls in Treatment Planning

Even with a strong understanding of the C.O.S.M.O.S. Frameworkâ„¢, certain pitfalls can hinder effective treatment planning. These include:

  • Over-reliance on predictions: Focusing too much on future events rather than present choices.
  • Imposing personal beliefs: Projecting your own values onto the client's chart.
  • Ignoring client feedback: Failing to adjust the treatment plan based on the client's experience.
  • Lack of cultural sensitivity: Not considering the client's cultural background and beliefs.

By being aware of these potential pitfalls, you can ensure that your treatment planning is client-centered, ethical, and effective.
