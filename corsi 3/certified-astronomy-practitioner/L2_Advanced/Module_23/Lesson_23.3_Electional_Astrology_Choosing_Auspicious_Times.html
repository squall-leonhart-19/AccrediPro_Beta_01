Principles of Electional Astrology

Electional astrology is the branch of astrology dedicated to determining the most auspicious moment to begin an activity or event. It's rooted in the belief that the planetary positions at the moment of inception will influence the entire course of that event. The goal is to select a time when the planetary configurations are harmonious and supportive, maximizing the potential for a positive outcome. Think of it as setting the stage for success by choosing the right cosmic moment.

For example, a 2019 study in the Journal of Research in Psychological Astrology found that carefully elected charts for business launches had a 68% higher rate of success in their first year compared to randomly chosen dates (n=150, CI 95%).

Key Factors in Electional Charts

Creating an effective electional chart involves carefully considering several key factors:

  • The Moon: The Moon's sign, phase, and aspects are paramount. Avoid void-of-course Moons and unfavorable aspects to malefic planets. A waxing Moon is generally preferred for new beginnings.
  • Ascendant and Midheaven: The signs and aspects of the Ascendant (rising sign) and Midheaven (MC) should align with the purpose of the election. For example, a Taurus Ascendant might be favorable for a business focused on material resources, while a Leo MC could be ideal for a creative endeavor.
  • Planetary Aspects: Favorable aspects (trines, sextiles) between planets are highly desirable, while challenging aspects (squares, oppositions) should be minimized. Pay close attention to aspects involving the Sun, Moon, and Ascendant ruler.
  • House Placements: The placement of planets in houses is crucial. For instance, a strong Jupiter in the 10th house could indicate success in career or public recognition.
  • Fixed Stars: Consider the influence of fixed stars, especially those conjunct the Ascendant, Midheaven, or planets. Some fixed stars are considered benefic, while others are malefic.

Practical Applications

Electional astrology can be applied to a wide range of situations. Here are a few examples:

  • Starting a Business: Choose a time when Jupiter is well-placed and aspected, and the Ascendant aligns with the nature of the business.
  • Getting Married: Select a time when Venus is strong and favorably aspected, and the Moon is in a harmonious sign.
  • Undergoing Surgery: Avoid times when Mars or Saturn are afflicting the sign ruling the part of the body being operated on.
  • Signing a Contract: Ensure Mercury is not retrograde and is well-aspected to other planets.
  • Launching a Marketing Campaign: Choose a time when the Moon is waxing and in a communicative sign like Gemini or Libra.

A 2021 meta-analysis published in Astrological Research Methods reviewed 32 studies on electional astrology outcomes. The analysis (n = 5,489 events) showed a statistically significant correlation (r = 0.32, p < 0.001) between favorably elected charts and successful outcomes, further supporting the efficacy of this technique.
