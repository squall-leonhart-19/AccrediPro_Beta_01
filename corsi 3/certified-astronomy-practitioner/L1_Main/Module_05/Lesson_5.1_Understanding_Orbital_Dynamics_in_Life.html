Mapping Your Current Orbital Patterns

A key step in optimizing your orbit is to identify and map your current patterns. This involves taking a close look at your daily, weekly, and monthly routines, as well as the "gravitational forces" that influence them. Consider using a journal, a digitalcalendar, or even a mind-mapping tool to visualize your life orbit.

Here's a simple exercise to get you started:

  1. List your daily routines: From the moment you wake up to the time you go to bed, write down everything you do on a typical day.
  2. Identify your "gravitational forces": Who or what exerts the most influence on your time and decisions?
  3. Assess alignment: Are your routines and influences aligned with your goals and values? Where are the disconnects?

By gaining a clearer understanding of your current orbit, you can begin to make conscious choices about where to stay the course and where to shift your trajectory.

Case Study: Sarah's Orbit Shift

Client: Sarah, 42, was feeling stuck in her career as a project manager. She felt a strong pull toward exploring her passion for astronomy, but her established routines and family responsibilities made it seem impossible.

Intervention: Sarah began by mapping her current orbit, identifying her daily routines and the "gravitational forces" influencing her decisions. Outcomes: Sarah started making small, incremental changes to her orbit. Over time, these small shifts created a significant change in her trajectory, leading to a fulfilling career as a astronomy consultant.
