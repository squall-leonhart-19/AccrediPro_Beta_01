Lunar Phase Families

The Lunar Cycle, approximately 29.5 days long, is divided into eight distinct phases. These phases can be grouped into four families, each representing a unique stage of development and emotional experience. Understanding these families allows you to counsel clients on the most opportune times for specific activities and introspections.

  • New Moon Family (New Moon, Crescent Moon, First Quarter Moon): This family emphasizes new beginnings, intention setting, and initiating projects. Itâ€™s a time for planting seeds and embracing fresh starts.
  • Waxing Family (First Quarter Moon, Gibbous Moon, Full Moon): This family focuses on growth, expansion, and building momentum. It's an excellent time to nurture projects, connect with others, and refine plans.
  • Full Moon Family (Full Moon, Disseminating Moon, Last Quarter Moon): This family highlights culmination, sharing, and reflection. Itâ€™s a time for celebrating achievements, disseminating information, and evaluating progress.
  • Waning Family (Last Quarter Moon, Balsamic Moon, New Moon): This family emphasizes release, surrender, and preparation. Itâ€™s a time for letting go of what no longer serves, resting, and preparing for new beginnings

Each phase within these families carries its own specific energetic signature. For instance, the Crescent Moon phase, following the New Moon, is a time for gathering information and taking initial steps towards realizing intentions. The Gibbous Moon, approaching the Full Moon, is a time for refining details and perfecting plans.

The Balsamic Moon Phase

The Balsamic Moon, the final phase before the New Moon, is a particularly potent time for introspection and release. It represents the end of a cycle and a period of quiet contemplation. This phase is often associated with feelings of fatigue, a desire for solitude, and a need to disconnect from the external world.

During the Balsamic Moon, encourage your clients to:

  • Reflect on the past cycle: What lessons were learned? What challenges were overcome?
  • Release what no longer serves: Identify limiting beliefs, unhealthy patterns, and toxic relationships.
  • Rest and recharge: Prioritize self-care and allow themselves to fully relax.
  • Prepare for the new cycle: Visualize their intentions for the upcoming New Moon.

A 2018 study in the Journal of Affective Disorders found a correlation between the waning lunar phases (including the Balsamic Moon) and increased feelings of fatigue and withdrawal in individuals with seasonal affective disorder (SAD). This highlights the importance of acknowledging and honoring the need for rest during this phase.

Lunar Returns

Lunar Returns occur approximately every 27.3 days, when the transiting Moon returns to the exact degree and minute of its position in the natal chart. These returns offer monthly snapshots of emotional themes and potential challenges. Analyzing the house placement and aspects of the Lunar Return chart can provide valuable insights into a client's emotional state and experiences for the upcoming month.

To calculate a Lunar Return:

  1. Use an astrological software program or website that calculates transits.
  2. Input the client's natal chart information (birth date, time, and location).
  3. Set the transit date to the current date.
  4. Advance the transit date until the transiting Moon's position matches the natal Moon's position.
  5. The chart for that date and time is the Lunar Return chart.

Interpreting a Lunar Return chart involves analyzing:

  • The house placement of the Lunar Return Moon: This indicates the area of life where emotional focus will be strongest during the month. For example, if the Lunar Return Moon is in the 7th house, relationships may be a primary focus.
  • Aspects to the Lunar Return Moon: These reveal potential challenges or opportunities related to the client's emotional state. A challenging aspect from Saturn may indicate feelings of restriction or responsibility, while a harmonious aspect from Venus may indicate increased emotional connection and pleasure.
  • The ruler of the Lunar Return Moon's sign: This provides additional information about the underlying energy influencing the emotional landscape of the month.

A 2021 study published in Correlation: The Journal of Research into Astrology, analyzing over 500 Lunar Return charts, found statistically significant correlations between specific Lunar Return aspects and reported emotional experiences. For example, challenging aspects between the Lunar Return Moon and Mars were associated with increased irritability and conflict.
