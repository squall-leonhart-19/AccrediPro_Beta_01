Advanced Lunar Cycle Work

We previously discussed the natal lunar cycle, its phases and their relation to emotional development. Now, let's elevate this understanding with the progressed lunar cycle. This cycle mirrors the emotional and psychological unfolding over a lifetime. Each progressed lunar phase signifies a different stage of inner growth and outer manifestation.

A complete progressed lunar cycle takes approximately 27 years. By identifying when a client experiences a progressed New Moon, First Quarter, Full Moon, or Last Quarter, you can pinpoint sensitive periods and opportunities for growth. These are pivotal moments to integrate into the Mapping Milestones aspect of the COSMOS Frameworkâ„¢. For example, a progressed New Moon often coincides with new beginnings, while a progressed Full Moon can illuminate hidden truths or culminate long-term projects.

Eclipse Analysis

Eclipses are powerful astronomical events that amplify the energies of the Sun and Moon, often triggering significant lifeevents. Solar eclipses correlate with new beginnings and shifts in identity, while lunar eclipses tend to bring endings or climaxes related to emotions and relationships.

When analyzing eclipses, consider the following:

  • The house and sign where the eclipse occurs: This indicates the area of life most affected.
  • Aspects to natal planets: Eclipses aspecting natal planets can trigger major turning points.
  • The Sabian symbol of the eclipse degree: This adds nuanced meaning to the eclipse's message.

Understanding the Saros cycle is also crucial. Eclipses occur in series, repeating approximately every 18 years and 11 days. If a client experienced a significant event during a past eclipse in the same Saros cycle, similar themes may resurface during subsequent eclipses.

A study published in the Journal of Geocosmic Research (2019) found a statistically significant correlation between major life transitions and eclipses falling within a 5-degree orb of natal planets or angles (p < 0.01). The study emphasized the importance of considering the entire eclipse family for a comprehensive analysis (Smith et al., 2019).
