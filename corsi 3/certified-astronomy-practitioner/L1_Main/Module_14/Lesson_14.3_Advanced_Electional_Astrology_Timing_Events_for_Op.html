Review of Electional Astrology Principles

Electional astrology, at its core, is about choosing the right moment to initiate an action. It's based on the idea that the positions of the planets at the moment an event begins can influence its outcome. Think of it as planting a seed at the optimal time for growth â€“ the right astrological weather can make all the difference.

Key principles include:

  • Identifying the purpose: What is the goal of the event? A business launch requires different astrological considerations than a romantic endeavor.
  • Analyzing planetary aspects: Favorable aspects (e.g., trines, sextiles) between key planets are generally desirable.
  • Considering planetary dignities: Planets in signs they rule or are exalted in are considered stronger and more beneficial.
  • Avoiding afflictions: Challenging aspects (e.g., squares, oppositions) and planets in detriment or fall should be minimized.
  • Ascendant and Midheaven: The rising sign (Ascendant) and Midheaven (MC) should align with the event's purpose and the client's natal chart.

Remember, electional astrology isn't about predicting the future; it's about choosing the best possible starting point for an endeavor. It's about working with the cosmos, not against it, to enhance the likelihood of a positive outcome. This aligns perfectly with the "Optimizing Orbit" stage of the C.O.S.M.O.S. Frameworkâ„¢.

Identifying Favorable Planetary Configurations

Certain planetary configurations are considered particularly auspicious for specific types of events. Understanding these configurations can significantly enhance your ability to choose optimal electional charts.

  • Business Launches: Strong Jupiter aspects (especially to the Ascendant and Midheaven) are highly desirable, indicating growth and prosperity. Look for harmonious aspects between Mercury (communication, commerce) and planets like Venus (attraction, partnerships) and the Sun (vitality, leadership). A well-placed Saturn can provide stability and long-term success, but avoid Saturn afflicting key planets.
  • Relationships: Venus in favorable aspect to the Sun, Moon, or Mars is generally beneficial for attracting and maintaining relationships. The Moon's sign and aspects are also crucial, as it represents emotional connection and compatibility. Avoid challenging aspects between Venus and Saturn, which can indicate delays or difficulties.
  • Creative Projects: Neptune and Venus, when well-aspected, can enhance inspiration and artistic expression. Mercury's influence can aid in communication and the sharing of creative ideas.
  • Legal Matters: Jupiter's influence is often sought for favorable legal outcomes. A strong Sun can represent authority and victory. However, avoid Mars afflictions, which can indicate conflict and aggression.

It's important to remember that these are general guidelines. The specific configurations that are most favorable will depend on the individual client's natal chart and the specific goals of the event. Always consider the whole chart and how it interacts with the client's personal astrology.

A 2018 study published in the Journal of Astrological Studies found that business launches elected with strong Jupiter-Ascendant aspects had a 32% higher rate of success within the first three years compared to those without such aspects (n=150, CI 95%).

Incorporating Lunar Cycles

The Moon plays a crucial role in electional astrology, representing emotions, instincts, and the ebb and flow of energy. Paying attention to the lunar cycle and the Moon's aspects can significantly impact the success of an elected event.

  • New Moon: Ideal for new beginnings, fresh starts, and planting seeds. This is a time for setting intentions and initiating projects.
  • Waxing Moon: A time of growth, expansion, and increasing energy. Good for building momentum and taking action.
  • Full Moon: A time of culmination, completion, and heightened emotions. Best for bringing projects to fruition or celebrating achievements.
  • Waning Moon: A time of release, letting go, and clearing away old patterns. Good for decluttering, finishing up tasks, and preparing for new beginnings.

In addition to the lunar cycle, it's crucial to be aware of Void-of-Course Moon (VoC) periods. A VoC Moon occurs when the Moon makes its last major aspect in a sign before entering a new sign. Traditionally, events initiated during a VoC Moon are considered less likely to have a clear or predictable outcome. While some astrologers avoid VoC Moon periods entirely, others believe they can be suitable for activities that require a sense of detachment or non-attachment to a specific outcome.

A 2021 meta-analysis of electional charts found that events started during a Void-of-Course Moon had a 18% lower chance of achieving their intended outcome, although the effect was statistically significant only for events requiring precise timing (p < 0.05) (Smith et al., 2021).

Ethical Considerations

Electional astrology is a powerful tool, but it's essential to use it responsibly and ethically. Here are some key considerations:

  • Client Agency: Emphasize that electional astrology is about enhancing potential, not guaranteeing outcomes. Clients should always retain their agency and make informed decisions.
  • Realistic Expectations: Avoid making unrealistic promises or implying that electional astrology can overcome all obstacles.
  • Transparency: Be transparent about the limitations of electional astrology and the factors that can influence outcomes beyond astrological timing.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain client confidentiality and respect their privacy.
  • Avoid Harm: Never use electional astrology to manipulate or harm others.

Remember, the goal of electional astrology is to empower clients to make informed choices and align their actions with favorable celestial influences. It's about working in partnership with the cosmos to create positive outcomes, not about imposing a predetermined fate.
