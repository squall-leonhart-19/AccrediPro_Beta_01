Defining Milestones

Milestones are specific, measurable steps that mark progress toward a larger goal. To be effective, milestones should adhere to the SMART criteria:

  • Specific: Clearly defined, leaving no room for ambiguity.
  • Measurable: Quantifiable, allowing progress to be tracked.
  • Achievable: Realistic and attainable, given the client's resources and circumstances.
  • Relevant: Aligned with the client's overall goals and values.
  • Time-bound: With a defined start and end date, creating a sense of urgency and accountability.

For example, instead of setting a vague goal like "improve my career," a SMART milestone might be "Network with three professionals in my desired field by the end of next month." This is specific, measurable (three professionals), achievable, relevant to career improvement, and time-bound (by the end of next month).

When mapping milestones, consider the following:

  • Client's Natal Chart: Identify areas of strength and potential challenges.
  • Current Transits: Note any significant transits that could support or hinder progress.
  • Progressed Chart: Look for longer-term trends and opportunities.
  • Client's Goals: Understand what the client wants to achieve and why.

Ethical Considerations

As with any practice that involves providing guidance and support, ethical considerations are paramount. When mapping milestones, it's crucial to:

  • Maintain Client Autonomy: Empower clients to make their own decisions, rather than imposing your own beliefs or values.
  • Avoid Making Predictions: Focus on providing insights and guidance, rather than claiming to predict the future.
  • Be Transparent About Limitations: Acknowledge the limitations of astronomical practice and avoid making unrealistic promises.
  • Respect Confidentiality: Protect the privacy of your clients and their personal information.
  • Obtain Informed Consent: Ensure that clients understand the nature of your services and agree to participate.

Remember, you are a facilitator, not a fortune teller. Your role is to empower clients to make informed decisions and take ownership of their lives, guided by astronomical insights.

Case Study: Sarah's Career Transition

Client: Sarah, 35 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Feeling unfulfilled in her current job as a marketing manager, desiring a career change but unsure where to start.

Intervention: Using the COSMOS framework, we analyzed Sarah's natal chart, focusing on her career indicators (Midheaven, 10th house ruler). We identified a strong aptitude for creative fields, particularly writing and communication. We then mapped milestones aligned with favorable transits of Jupiter and Mercury to her Midheaven.

Milestones:

  1. Research different career options in creative writing (by week 2).
  2. Take an online course in copywriting (by month 2).
  3. Update her LinkedIn profile to highlight her writing skills (by month 3).
  4. Network with three copywriters (by month 4).
  5. Apply for freelance copywriting jobs (by month 5).

Outcomes: Within six months, Sarah landed a part-time freelance copywriting role, which she found much more fulfilling than her previous job. She is now working towards transitioning to full-time freelance work.
