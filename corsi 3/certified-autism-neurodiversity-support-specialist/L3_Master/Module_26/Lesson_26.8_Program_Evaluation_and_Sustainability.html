Supervision & Mentoring Practice

Stepping into a leadership role? This lab provides practical mentoring scenarios to hone your skills.

Objectives

In this lesson, you will:

  • Identify common challenges faced by new practitioners.
  • Develop strategies for providing constructive feedback and support.
  • Practice active listening and inquiry skills in a mentoring context.
  • Learn how to guide a mentee in ethical decision-making.
  • Understand the importance of setting boundaries and expectations in a mentoring relationship.

The Challenge She Presents

Sarah's Time Management Struggles

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm spending hours on each client intake and follow-up. I want to provide personalized support, but I'm burning out. I feel like I'm drowning in emails and paperwork. How do you balance everything?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Sarah setting clear boundaries for communication with clients?
  • Is she using templates or systems to streamline her paperwork?
  • Is she spending too much time on non-billable tasks?
  • Is she delegating tasks where appropriate?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Self-Care

Share templates for intake forms, follow-up emails, and progress reports. Show her how to automate tasks.

3

Set Boundaries

Help Sarah define her working hours and communication policies. Teach her to politely decline requests outside of those boundaries.

4

Delegate When Possible

If appropriate, discuss options for hiring administrative support or outsourcing tasks like billing and scheduling.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about your clients, and it's admirable that you want to provide the best possible support. It's common for new practitioners to feel overwhelmed at first."
Ask About Her Current Strategies
"Tell me about your current system for managing paperwork and client communication. What's working, and what's not?"
Share Practical Tips
"One thing that helped me was creating email templates for common questions and follow-ups. It saved me hours each week. Would you like me to share some examples?"
Encourage Experimentation
"Try implementing one or two of these strategies this week and see how it goes. We can discuss your progress at our next meeting."

You're Becoming a Leader!

By mentoring new practitioners, you're not only helping them succeed but also contributing to the growth and development of our field. Your guidance and support are invaluable.

Key Takeaways

  • New practitioners often feel overwhelmed and need support in managing their workload.
  • Providing practical tips and strategies can help mentees streamline their processes and improve their efficiency.
  • Setting boundaries and prioritizing self-care are essential for preventing burnout.
  • Mentoring is an opportunity to share your knowledge and experience and contribute to the growth of the field.
