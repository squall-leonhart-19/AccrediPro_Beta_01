The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client: Emily, 35

Sarah's Description:

"Emily is a 35-year-old client with diagnosed ADHD and suspected autism, experiencing frequent anxiety attacks. During our last session, Emily had a panic attack triggered by a change in her routine. I tried deep breathing exercises, but it didn't seem to help. I felt helpless and unsure of what to do."

Key Information to Explore:

  • What strategies has Emily used in the past to manage anxiety?
  • Was the change in routine anticipated or unexpected?
  • What resources (e.g., sensory tools, grounding techniques) were available during the session?
  • What are Emily's specific sensory sensitivities and triggers?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Sarah's Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client experiences a crisis. Acknowledge that it's okay not to have all the answers."

2

Emphasize the Importance of a Crisis Plan

Discuss the need for a pre-established crisis plan with clients, including coping strategies, emergency contacts, and safe spaces.

3

Review Sensory Considerations

Explore Emily's sensory profile and how it impacts her anxiety. Discuss the use of sensory tools and modifications to create a more calming environment.

4

Teach Grounding Techniques

Share alternative grounding techniques beyond deep breathing, such as the 5-4-3-2-1 method or visualization exercises. Encourage Sarah to explore these options with Emily.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, thank you for bringing this case to me. It takes courage to seek support, and I commend you for prioritizing Emily's well-being."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time? What resources do you think would be helpful for Emily?"
Share Knowledge and Resources
"One strategy that can be helpful is creating a sensory toolkit with items like fidget toys, noise-canceling headphones, or weighted blankets. It's also crucial to develop a crisis plan collaboratively with Emily."
Empower Future Action
"How can I support you in implementing these strategies with Emily? Would you like me to provide additional resources or role-play crisis scenarios with you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the first step you should take when a mentee presents a challenging client case?

The first step is to validate their feelings and acknowledge that it's normal to feel overwhelmed in such situations.

2. Why is it important to encourage self-reflection in your mentees?

Encouraging self-reflection helps mentees develop critical thinking skills, learn from their experiences, and become more independent practitioners.

You're Nurturing the Next Generation!

By sharing your expertise and providing guidance, you're empowering new practitioners to make a positive impact on the lives of neurodiverse individuals. Your mentorship is invaluable!

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves providing guidance, support, and feedback to new practitioners.
  • Validate your mentee's feelings and normalize the challenges they face.
  • Encourage self-reflection and critical thinking.
  • Share your knowledge and resources to empower their growth.
  • Celebrate their strengths and progress to build their confidence.
