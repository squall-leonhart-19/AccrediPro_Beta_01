Let's briefly revisit each component:

  • Assess Strengths & Needs: This involves gathering information through formal and informal assessments to understand the individual's unique profile.
  • Cultivate Connection & Trust: Building a strong, trusting relationship is crucial for effective support.
  • Create Collaborative Goals: Working together with the individual to set meaningful and achievable goals.

Case Study: Maria, 45

Maria, a 45-year-old woman recently diagnosed with ADHD, seeks support in managing her daily life. She reports feeling overwhelmed, struggling with organization, and having difficulty maintaining focus at her new job as a remote customer service representative. Sheâ€™s also expressed feelings of isolation and low self-esteem.

Integrated Intervention Plan:

  • Assess: Conduct a thorough assessment to identify Maria's strengths (e.g., strong communication skills) and specific needs (e.g., executive function deficits).
  • Cultivate: Build rapport by actively listening to Maria's concerns and validating her experiences. Emphasize her strengths and potential.
  • Create: Collaboratively set SMART goals, such as implementing a daily planner, breaking down tasks into smaller steps, and joining a support group for women with ADHD.
  • Empower: Teach Maria strategies for improving focus, such as the Pomodoro Technique, and techniques for organization, such as using color-coded folders.
  • Promote: Provide positive reinforcement for Maria's efforts and achievements, celebrating small wins along the way.
  • Track: Monitor Maria's progress through regular check-ins and self-report measures, adjusting the intervention plan as needed.

Outcome: Within three months, Maria reported improved focus, better organization, and increased self-esteem.
