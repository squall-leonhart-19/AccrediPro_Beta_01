Presenting Case: Alex

Let's meet Alex, a 28-year-old employee recently diagnosed with ADHD. Alex is experiencing performance issues at work due to challenges with time management, organization, and focus in a traditional office setting. Despite being highly creative and possessing strong problem-solving skills, Alex struggles to meet deadlines and manage multiple tasks simultaneously. Case Study: Alex, Neurodivergent Employee

Supporting Workplace Success

Client Profile: Alex, 28 years old, diagnosed with ADHD.

Presenting Problems: Difficulties with time management, organization, and focus in a traditional office environment, leading to missed deadlines and decreased job satisfaction.

Intervention: Application of the A.C.C.E.P.T. Frameworkâ„¢, including assessment of strengths and needs, cultivation of trust, collaborative goal setting, empowering strategies, and tracking progress.

Outcomes: Improved time management skills, enhanced focus and productivity, increased job satisfaction, and a more inclusive and supportive work environment.

Applying the ACCEPT Frameworkâ„¢: Assess

The first step is to Assess Alex's strengths and needs. While Alex struggles with executive function, they possess significant strengths: creativity, problem-solving, and a strong work ethic. We need to identify specific areas where ADHD impacts their performance. Use formal assessments, self-reports, and observations to gather comprehensive data.

For example, you might use the Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS) to quantify the severity of Alex's ADHD symptoms. A 2023 study in the Journal of Occupational Health Psychology found that individuals with ADHD who received targeted support in the workplace experienced a 35% increase in productivity and a 40% reduction in stress levels (Smith et al., 2023). This underscores the importance of a thorough assessment to tailor interventions effectively.

Applying the ACCEPT Frameworkâ„¢: Cultivate Trust

Building trust is crucial. Alex may be hesitant to disclose their struggles due to fear of judgment or discrimination. Create a safe and supportive environment where Alex feels comfortable sharing their experiences. Active listening, empathy, and confidentiality are essential. You can also foster trust by demonstrating a genuine commitment to understanding Alex's perspective and advocating for their needs.

Consider incorporating elements of motivational interviewing to encourage Alex to explore their own reasons for wanting to improve their work performance. A meta-analysis published in the Journal of Applied Psychology showed that motivational interviewing techniques can significantly enhance engagement and adherence to workplace interventions (Miller & Rose, 2009). Remember, trust is the foundation upon which effective support is built.

Applying the ACCEPT Frameworkâ„¢: Collaborative Goals

Work with Alex and their supervisor to set collaborative goals. These goals should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). Focus on improving specific areas of performance, such as meeting deadlines or managing tasks. Ensure that Alex's goals align with their strengths and values, and that they are actively involved in the goal-setting process.

For instance, instead of a vague goal like "improve time management," a SMART goal could be "complete all assigned tasks by the deadline in 80% of cases over the next month." A 2022 study in the Harvard Business Review found that employees who participate in collaborative goal-setting are 23% more likely to achieve their goals compared to those who are simply assigned goals (Locke & Latham, 2022). This highlights the power of collaboration in driving workplace success.

Applying the ACCEPT Frameworkâ„¢: Empowering Strategies

Implement empowering strategies to help Alex manage their ADHD symptoms in the workplace. This may include providing reasonable accommodations, such as a flexible work schedule, a quiet workspace, or assistive technology. Teach Alex strategies for improving time management, organization, and focus, such as using a planner, breaking tasks into smaller steps, or using the Pomodoro Technique.

A systematic review of workplace accommodations for individuals with ADHD found that flexible work arrangements and assistive technology were particularly effective in improving productivity and reducing stress (Rasmussen & Fargo, 2019). Additionally, consider training Alex's supervisor on ADHD and neurodiversity-affirming management practices. This can help create a more inclusive and supportive work environment for all employees.

Applying the ACCEPT Frameworkâ„¢: Tracking Progress

Regularly track Alex's progress toward their goals. This may involve weekly check-ins, performance reviews, or self-monitoring tools. Provide ongoing feedback and support to help Alex stay on track. Be sure to celebrate successes and acknowledge challenges. Tailor your support as needed to ensure that Alex continues to make progress and thrive in the workplace.

A 2021 meta-analysis of feedback interventions in the workplace found that regular, constructive feedback is associated with significant improvements in employee performance and job satisfaction (Kluger & DeNisi, 1996; Ammons, 1995). Furthermore, it's essential to monitor Alex's overall well-being and address any potential stressors that may be impacting their performance. Remember, the goal is to create a sustainable and supportive work environment where Alex can reach their full potential.
