Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Analysis

Step 1: Identify Ethical Issues
Several overlapping issues: Autonomy (Eleanor's right to self-determination), beneficence (acting in Eleanor's best interest), non-maleficence (avoiding harm), fidelity (maintaining trust and confidentiality), and justice (equitable access to services).
Step 2: Consider Stakeholders
Eleanor, her husband, her children, and you as the support specialist. Each stakeholder has potentially conflicting needs and perspectives. A 2021 study in the Journal of Family Psychology (n=152) found that differing beliefs about neurodiversity within families can significantly impact family dynamics (p < .01).
Step 3: Apply Ethical Principles
Prioritize Eleanor's autonomy while respecting her family's beliefs. Ensure informed consent regarding assessment and intervention options, including potential risks and benefits. Address financial constraints transparently.
Step 4: Develop Action Plan
Provide Eleanor with comprehensive information about ADHD assessments and support services. Facilitate open communication with her husband, if Eleanor consents. Explore affordable resources and strategies. Document all ethical considerations and decisions.

Differential Considerations

Alternative Scenarios

1

Husband Refuses Support

If Eleanor's husband actively undermines her pursuit of diagnosis or support, explore strategies to address his concerns while upholding Eleanor's autonomy. This could involve psychoeducation about neurodiversity or couples counseling.

2

Financial Strain Worsens

If financial pressures intensify, explore pro bono or sliding-scale services. Help Eleanor prioritize essential support and develop a cost-effective plan. A 2023 study by the Autism Society found that 78% of autistic adults report financial difficulties due to lack of affordable support services.

3

Co-occurring Mental Health Conditions

If Eleanor presents with severe depression or anxiety, prioritize mental health support. Coordinate with a mental health professional to address these issues concurrently with neurodiversity support.

4

Boundary Violations

Be vigilant for potential boundary violations (e.g., Eleanor seeking emotional support beyond the professional relationship). Maintain clear professional boundaries at all times.

Check Your Understanding

1. How would you address the potential conflict of interest if Eleanor seeks an ADHD diagnosis primarily to access disability benefits?

I would openly discuss the potential motivations and implications of seeking a diagnosis. I would emphasize the importance of accurate self-understanding and ethical use of disability benefits, ensuring Eleanor is fully informed and making autonomous decisions.

2. Eleanor's husband is skeptical of neurodiversity. How can you ethically navigate this situation?

With Eleanor's consent, offer to provide her husband with psychoeducation about neurodiversity. Acknowledge his concerns while upholding Eleanor's autonomy and right to seek support. Avoid imposing my own beliefs or invalidating his perspective.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are common in neurodiversity support and require careful consideration.
  • Prioritize client autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, fidelity, and justice.
  • Address potential conflicts of interest transparently and ethically.
  • Maintain clear professional boundaries and seek supervision when needed.
  • Document all ethical considerations and decisions thoroughly.
