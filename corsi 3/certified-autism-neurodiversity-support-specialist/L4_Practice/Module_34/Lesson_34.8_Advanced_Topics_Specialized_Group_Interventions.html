Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Olivia, and I'm thrilled to connect. I saw you were interested in the 'Thriving with Neurodiversity' program. What caught your eye about it?"
YOU:
"It's so great you found us on Facebook. What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing right now with your ADHD diagnosis?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the overwhelm. What does that look like in your daily life?"
YOU:
"What kind of support have you tried so far? What has been helpful, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your current situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for practical strategies and a supportive community to help you manage your ADHD. Is that right?"
YOU:
"The 'Thriving with Neurodiversity' program is designed to do just that. It's an 8-week group program where you'll learn evidence-based strategies for managing your symptoms, connect with other neurodivergent individuals, and receive personalized support. Many of our participants report feeling more confident, organized, and connected after completing the program."
YOU:
"This program is different because it combines education, community, and personalized support. You're not just getting information, you're getting a safe space to practice and implement what you learn."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you think this program could be a good fit for you? ... Great! Let's talk about the investment and how you can enroll."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Life can get busy. The program is designed to be flexible, with weekly sessions that are recorded so you can catch up if you miss one. Plus, the strategies you'll learn can actually help you become MORE efficient with your time."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I appreciate you bringing that up. Let's look at the value you're getting. For the price of [relate to something tangible, e.g., a few therapy sessions], you're getting 8 weeks of group support, evidence-based strategies, and a community that understands what you're going through. What‚Äôs the value of finally feeling understood and equipped to manage your ADHD?"
"I'm worried I won't fit in with the group."
"That's a valid concern. We create a very welcoming and inclusive environment in the program. Everyone is there to support each other, and we have guidelines in place to ensure everyone feels safe and respected. You'll be surprised how quickly you connect with the other participants."
"Can I get a discount?"
"I understand you're looking to make the best investment. While I don't offer discounts on the program itself, I do have a payment plan available to make it more accessible. Would you like to hear more about that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to include in your pricing presentation?

Clearly state the price, list inclusions, and highlight the value and benefits the client will receive. Offer payment options if available.

2. How can you handle the objection "I'm not sure if I have the time"?

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the program's flexibility (recorded sessions), and highlight how the strategies learned can actually SAVE them time.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial in the initial phase of the discovery call.
  • Understanding the prospect's specific challenges allows you to tailor your presentation effectively.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and handling objections are essential for successful enrollment.
  • Group programs offer a scalable way to increase your income while providing valuable support to clients.
