Your 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Olivia, and I'm passionate about helping families like yours. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"I understand you found me on LinkedIn. What specifically caught your eye?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the challenges you're facing with your child and the school system. What are your biggest frustrations?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you already tried, and what were the outcomes?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest fears or concerns about your child's future if things don't improve?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her key pain points]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on [explain your core methodology]. I work with parents over 12 weeks to develop tailored strategies that address the root causes of these challenges. Many of my clients see significant improvements within the first month. For example, I recently worked with a mom, Sarah, whose son was struggling with meltdowns at school. After just six weeks of implementing our strategies, the meltdowns decreased by 70%."
Phase 4: Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? ... Wonderful! Let's discuss the program details and how we can get started."

Mastering Objections

Common Objections & Your Expert Responses

"I'm not sure if my child is 'bad enough' for this."
"I understand your concern. It's not about being 'bad enough.' It's about proactively creating a supportive environment where your child can thrive. My program is designed for families who want to unlock their child's full potential, regardless of their current challenges. In fact, starting early can prevent more significant issues down the road."
"I'm worried I won't have time to implement the strategies."
"That's a valid concern. The strategies we develop are designed to be integrated into your daily routine, not to create extra work. We'll focus on small, consistent changes that make a big impact. Plus, I'll provide you with ongoing support and accountability to ensure you stay on track. Many of my clients, even those with demanding schedules, find that the program actually saves them time and reduces stress in the long run."
"What if it doesn't work for my child?"
"I understand your hesitation. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I'm confident in my ability to help your family. My program is based on proven strategies and tailored to your child's unique needs. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee: if you're not completely satisfied with the program after the first month, I'll refund your investment. I'm committed to your success."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can provide more information to help you make a decision. Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or the specific strategies involved?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in the "Presenting Your Solution" phase of a discovery call?

Summarizing the client's pain points, presenting your methodology, and sharing a success story or case study.

2. How should you address the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions to uncover the specific concerns they have. Offer to provide additional information or schedule a follow-up call.

3. What is the importance of practicing your pricing presentation?

Practicing allows you to state your price confidently and clearly, emphasizing the value you provide and addressing potential hesitations.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps guide the conversation and ensures you address all critical points.
  • Anticipating and preparing for common objections is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence conveys the value of your services.
  • Consistent practice builds confidence and improves your client acquisition skills.
  • Remember to tailor your approach to each individual client's needs and concerns.
