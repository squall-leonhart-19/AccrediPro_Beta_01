1. Mission, Vision, and Values

Your mission statement defines your purpose: what you do and who you serve. Your vision statement paints a picture of your ideal future impact. Your values are the guiding principles that shape your decisions and actions.

Consider these questions to clarify your core identity:

  • What unique contribution do you want to make to the neurodiversity community?
  • What kind of impact do you want to see in the lives of your clients?
  • What principles will guide your practice (e.g., empathy, collaboration, empowerment)?

Example:
Mission: To empower neurodivergent individuals to thrive by providing personalized support and fostering a strengths-based approach.
Vision: A world where neurodiversity is celebrated, understood, and supported.
Values: Empathy, Respect, Collaboration, Empowerment, Authenticity

2. Service Offerings and Pricing

Clearly define the services you'll offer. This could include:

  • Individual coaching
  • Group workshops
  • Family support sessions
  • Educational consultations
  • Advocacy support

Your pricing structure should reflect the value you provide, your expertise, and the market rate in your area. Research what other practitioners are charging and consider offering packages or sliding-scale fees to increase accessibility.

Income Example: Sarah, a former teacher, offers individual coaching at $75/hour and group workshops at $45/participant. Working 20 hours/week with 10 clients and running one workshop with 8 participants brings in approximately $3390/month.

3. Competitive Analysis

Understanding your competitive landscape is crucial. Identify other professionals or organizations offering similar services in your area. Analyze their strengths and weaknesses to find opportunities for differentiation.

Consider what makes you unique: your specific expertise, your personal approach, your commitment to the A.C.C.E.P.T. Frameworkâ„¢. This differentiation will attract your ideal clients.

4. Marketing Strategy

A well-defined marketing strategy is essential for reaching your target audience. Utilize a mix of online and offline channels:

  • Online: Website, social media (Facebook groups, LinkedIn), online directories, email marketing, webinars.
  • Offline: Networking events, community workshops, partnerships with local organizations, brochures.

Focus on building relationships and providing valuable content. Share your expertise, offer free resources, and engage withyour audience authentically.

5. Financial Plan

A solid financial plan is the backbone of your business. It should include:

  • Startup costs: Website development, marketing materials, office supplies, legal fees.
  • Operating expenses: Rent, utilities, insurance, software subscriptions, marketing costs.
  • Revenue projections: Estimate your income based on your service offerings and pricing.
  • Funding sources: Personal savings, loans, grants.

Create a budget and track your expenses carefully. Consider using accounting software to manage your finances effectively. Regularly review your financial performance and make adjustments as needed.

According to the Small Business Administration (SBA), businesses with a well-developed financial plan are 30% more likely to secure funding and achieve long-term success.

6. Legal Structure

Choosing the right legal structure is essential for protecting your personal assets and minimizing your tax liability. Common options include:

  • Sole proprietorship: Simple to set up, but offers no liability protection.
  • Limited Liability Company (LLC): Provides liability protection and flexible taxation options.
  • S Corporation: Can offer tax advantages for established businesses.

Consult with an attorney or accountant to determine the best legal structure for your specific needs.

Case Study: Maria's Coaching Practice

Client: Maria Rodriguez, 35 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Maria, a certified ADHD coach, struggled to attract clients and manage her finances effectively. She felt overwhelmed by the business aspects of running her practice.

Intervention: Maria developed a comprehensive business plan using the principles outlined in this lesson. She clarified her mission, defined her service offerings, analyzed her competition, and created a marketing strategy focused on social media and community partnerships. She also implemented a budget and started tracking her expenses using accounting software.

Outcomes: Within six months, Maria's client base increased by 40%, and her monthly revenue doubled. She felt more confident and in control of her business.
