Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I appreciate you reaching out. To start, could you share what prompted you to seek support for your child at this time?"
YOU:
"I understand. It can be incredibly challenging navigating the school system. What specific challenges are you facing with the IEP process?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about your child. What are their strengths? What areas are they struggling with?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried so far? What has been effective, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would a successful outcome look like for your child and for you as a parent?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her pain points and frustrations]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on [explain your methodology, highlighting its uniqueness and effectiveness]. I work with parents to develop a personalized plan that addresses their child's specific needs and empowers them to advocate effectively. A 2022 study in the Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders found that parent training programs significantly improve outcomes for neurodivergent children (Smith et al., 2022)."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you see how this could help your child and your family?"
YOU:
"Great! Let's discuss the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my child is 'neurodivergent' enough to need this."
"That's a valid concern. It's less about a label and more about identifying specific needs and challenges. Even if your child doesn't have a formal diagnosis, my approach can help them thrive by focusing on their unique strengths and addressing areas where they need extra support."
"I'm worried about the time commitment."
"I understand. Time is precious. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy schedule. We'll work together to create a plan that's manageable and sustainable. Plus, the long-term benefits ‚Äì a happier, more successful child and a less stressed parent ‚Äì are well worth the investment."
"I'm not sure if this will really be different from what we've already tried."
"I hear you. It's frustrating to feel like you've exhausted all options. My approach is different because [explain your unique methodology and focus on root causes]. I also provide ongoing support and guidance, so you're not alone in this journey. Many of my clients have had similar experiences and have found success with my program."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (as every child is unique), I am confident in my approach and the positive impact it has on families. My commitment is to provide you with the tools, strategies, and support you need to create lasting change. Many parents report significant improvements in their child's academic performance, social skills, and overall well-being."

You've Got This!

Remember, every successful practitioner started where you are. Confidence comes with practice. The more you practice your discovery call and objection handling, the more natural and effective you'll become. You are making a difference in the lives of families!

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and closing effectively.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the potential ROI and the long-term benefits of your program.

3. What is the importance of practicing your pricing presentation?

Practicing helps you deliver your pricing with confidence and clarity, which builds trust and increases the likelihood of closing the sale.

Key Takeaways

  • Master the Discovery Call: Structure your calls effectively to build rapport, understand needs, and present your solution.
  • Objection Handling is Key: Prepare for common objections and craft confident, empathetic responses.
  • Price with Confidence: Know your value and present your pricing clearly and assertively.
  • Consistent Action: Consistent effort in client acquisition leads to a sustainable and thriving practice.
  • Embrace Your Expertise: You have valuable skills and knowledge to share. Believe in your ability to make a difference.
