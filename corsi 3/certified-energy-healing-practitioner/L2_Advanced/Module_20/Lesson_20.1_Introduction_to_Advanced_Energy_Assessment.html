Ethical Considerations

As you progress to advanced energy assessment, ethical considerations become even more critical. It's essential to operate within your scope of practice, obtain informed consent from clients, and maintain clear client boundaries. Remember, you are not a medical doctor or licensed therapist unless you hold those credentials. Energy healing is a complementary modality and should not replace conventional medical treatment.

Informed consent should include a clear explanation of the assessment techniques you will be using, the potential benefits and risks, and the client's right to refuse or withdraw from the assessment at any time. Boundaries are crucial to maintain a professional and ethical relationship with your clients. Avoid dual relationships (e.g., becoming friends or business partners with clients) and always prioritize the client's well-being.

Intuition vs. Objective Measurement

In Level 2, you'll learn to integrate your intuition with objective measurement techniques. While intuition can provide valuable insights, it's crucial to ground your assessments in objective data whenever possible. Reliance solely on intuition can lead to bias and inaccurate conclusions. Objective measurements, such as biofeedback or meridian assessments, provide tangible data that can validate or challenge your intuitive hunches.

Strive for a balance between subjective and objective data. Use your intuition to guide you toward areas that require deeper exploration, and then use objective measurements to confirm or refine your understanding. This approach ensures a more accurate and reliable assessment.
