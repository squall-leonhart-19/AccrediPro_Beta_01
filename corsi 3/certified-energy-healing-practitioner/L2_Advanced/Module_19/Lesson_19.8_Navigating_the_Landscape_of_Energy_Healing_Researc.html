Differential Considerations

Ruling Out Other Possibilities

1

Autoimmune Disorders

Although ANA is negative, consider other autoimmune conditions that can mimic fibromyalgia (e.g., early-stage lupus, Sjogren's syndrome). Further testing may be warranted if symptoms worsen.

2

Thyroid Dysfunction

A "normal" TSH doesn't always rule out thyroid issues. Consider testing Free T3, Free T4, and thyroid antibodies (TPO, TgAb) to assess for subclinical hypothyroidism or Hashimoto's thyroiditis.

3

Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO)

Given her IBS and GERD, SIBO is a strong possibility. A breath test can help confirm this diagnosis.

4

Nutrient Deficiencies

Beyond Vitamin D, assess for other common deficiencies (e.g., B12, iron, magnesium) that can contribute to fatigue and pain.

When to Refer

Red Flags and Scope of Practice

  • Sudden onset of severe pain, especially if accompanied by fever or neurological symptoms (rule out infection or serious underlying condition).
  • Unexplained weight loss (possible malignancy or malabsorption).
  • Severe depression or suicidal ideation (immediate mental health referral).
  • New or worsening neurological symptoms (e.g., weakness, numbness, vision changes) â€“ neurological evaluation needed.
