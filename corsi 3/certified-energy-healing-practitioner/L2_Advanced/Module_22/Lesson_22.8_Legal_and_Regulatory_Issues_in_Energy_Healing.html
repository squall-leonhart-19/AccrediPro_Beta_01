Relevant History
No significant medical history. Reports feeling disconnected from her husband for several years. Affair has been ongoing for 6 months.
Client Goal
To alleviate her anxiety and regain a sense of balance and joy in her life.
Ethical Concern
The client's actions are causing potential harm to her husband and family. You are aware that disclosing the affair would be unethical and a breach of confidentiality, but you also feel a sense of responsibility to the well-being of all parties involved.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Framework

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is balancing the client's right to confidentiality with your responsibility to prevent harm to others. This involves conflicting ethical principles.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Refer to the AccrediPro Academy's code of ethics, emphasizing confidentiality, non-maleficence (do no harm), and beneficence (promoting well-being). Consider legal and regulatory requirements regarding confidentiality in your jurisdiction.
Step 3: Explore Potential Courses of Action
  • Continue energy healing without addressing the affair directly.
  • Encourage the client to seek marriage counseling or individual therapy.
  • Gently explore the client's values and the potential consequences of her actions.
  • Disclose the affair (unethical and likely illegal).
Step 4: Evaluate the Consequences
Each option has potential consequences. Continuing without addressing the issue may prolong the client's distress and the potential harm to her family. Encouraging therapy or exploring values could lead to positive change. Disclosure is unethical and would likely damage the client's trust and your professional reputation.

Differential Considerations

Alternative Perspectives

1

Client Autonomy

Respecting the client's right to make her own decisions, even if they are not aligned with your personal values.

2

Systemic Impact

Recognizing that the client's actions have a ripple effect on her family and community.

3

Your Own Values

Acknowledging your personal beliefs and how they might influence your judgment. Strive for objectivity and avoid imposing your values on the client.

When to Refer

When to Seek External Support

  • Client expresses suicidal ideation or intent to harm others: Immediate referral to mental health services.
  • Client's anxiety significantly impairs her daily functioning: Referral to a licensed therapist or psychiatrist.
  • You feel overwhelmed or unable to maintain objectivity: Seek consultation with a seasoned energy healing practitioner or ethics expert.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are inherent in energy healing practice, particularly when dealing with sensitive client information.
  • Confidentiality is paramount, but it is not absolute. Practitioners must balance it with the responsibility to prevent harm.
  • A structured ethical decision-making framework can guide practitioners through complex situations.
  • Referral to other professionals is essential when client needs exceed the practitioner's scope of practice.
  • Self-reflection and ongoing ethical education are crucial for maintaining ethical competence.
