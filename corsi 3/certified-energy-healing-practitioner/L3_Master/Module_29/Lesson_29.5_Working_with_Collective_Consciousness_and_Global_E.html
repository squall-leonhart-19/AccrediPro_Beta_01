Ethical Considerations in Collective Healing

Collective healing raises unique ethical considerations. It's crucial to respect individual boundaries, maintain confidentiality, and avoid imposing personal beliefs on others. Informed consent is essential, especially when working with vulnerable populations. Always emphasize that energy healing is a complementary therapy and should not replace conventional medical treatment.

Ensure you're trained and competent to handle the energies involved in group healing. Seek supervision or mentorship if needed. Be mindful of power dynamics and avoid exploiting your position for personal gain. A 2022 report by the National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health (NCCIH) emphasized the importance of ethical guidelines in energy healing practices, citing a 15% increase in complaints related to boundary violations in the past five years.
