The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 52

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily has chronic fatigue, fibromyalgia, and anxiety. I feel overwhelmed trying to address all her issues. I started with a basic energy balancing protocol, but I'm not sure if it's enough or if I should be doing more. I'm also worried about triggering a healing crisis."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Is Sarah clear on contraindications for certain techniques with complex conditions?
  • Did she establish clear, achievable goals with Emily?
  • Is she trying to do too much, too soon?
  • Is she aware of her scope of practice and when to refer?

Your Teaching Approach

Guiding Sarah Through Complexity

1

Emphasize Foundational Principles

Remind Sarah that even with complex cases, the A.U.R.A.‚Ñ¢ methodology still applies: Assess, Understand, Release, Activate. Focus on the foundational steps first.

2

Teach Prioritization

Guide Sarah to identify the client's primary concern. Is it fatigue, pain, or anxiety? Addressing the root cause of one can often positively impact others.

3

Review Scope of Practice

Ensure Sarah understands the importance of referrals. Energy healing is a powerful complement to medical care, not a replacement. Encourage collaboration with other healthcare providers.

4

Discuss Healing Crisis Management

Explain that a mild healing response is normal, but overwhelming symptoms require a re-evaluation. Teach her how to adjust the intensity and frequency of sessions.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Sarah, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed with a client like Emily. Her case is complex, and it shows you're taking your responsibility seriously."
Guide Her Thinking

"What feels like the most aligned next step for Emily? Trust your intuition, and remember you can always adjust your approach based on her response."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing feedback to a new practitioner?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable asking questions and exploring their concerns.

2. How can you help a mentee understand the importance of scope of practice?

By emphasizing that energy healing complements medical care and encouraging collaboration with other healthcare professionals.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through complex cases and building their confidence.
  • Prioritize teaching foundational principles and encouraging self-reflection.
  • Address scope of practice and ethical considerations to ensure client safety.
  • Constructive feedback should be supportive, encouraging, and focused on growth.
