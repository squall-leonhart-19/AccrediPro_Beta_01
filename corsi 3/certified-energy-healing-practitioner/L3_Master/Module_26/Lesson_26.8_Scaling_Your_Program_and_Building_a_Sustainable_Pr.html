The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client: Emily, 52, Anxious and Overwhelmed

Sarah's Summary:

"Emily has chronic anxiety and feels constantly overwhelmed. I did a session focusing on calming her energy, but she called me saying she felt even MORE anxious afterwards. I'm not sure what I did wrong."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • What specific techniques did Sarah use?
  • Did Sarah address potential root causes of the anxiety?
  • Was Emily prepared for a potential emotional release?
  • Did Sarah establish clear boundaries and expectations?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Acknowledge Her Effort

Start by validating Sarah's effort and willingness to learn. "It's great that you're reaching out. This is part of the learning process."

2

Discuss Potential Emotional Release

Explain that sometimes, energy work can bring suppressed emotions to the surface. This isn't necessarily negative, but clients need to be prepared.

3

Review Techniques for Grounding

Ensure Sarah is familiar with grounding techniques to help clients manage overwhelming emotions after a session. Suggest specific techniques they can use.

4

Emphasize Root Cause Assessment

Guide Sarah to explore potential root causes of Emily's anxiety (e.g., trauma, limiting beliefs) for a more holistic approach.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback with Compassion

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for seeking guidance. Your dedication to your clients is evident. Let's explore this case together."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What are your initial thoughts about why Emily felt more anxious? What did you observe during the session?"
Introduce Key Concepts

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to remember when mentoring a new practitioner?

The most important thing is to create a safe and supportive environment where the mentee feels comfortable asking questions and sharing their concerns.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with setting boundaries?

You can help by sharing your own experiences with setting boundaries, providing scripts or templates for communicating boundaries to clients, and role-playing difficult conversations.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new practitioners is a crucial part of growing the field of energy healing.
  • Effective mentoring requires active listening, empathy, and constructive feedback.
  • Normalize the learning process and help mentees understand that mistakes are opportunities for growth.
  • Encourage mentees to develop their own solutions and build their confidence.
  • By sharing your knowledge and experience, you can empower the next generation of energy healing practitioners.
