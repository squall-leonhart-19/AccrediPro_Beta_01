The Assess (A) Phase in Group Settings

The first step in the A.U.R.A. Frameworkâ„¢ is Assess, which involves scanning the energy field to identify areas of imbalance or blockage. In a group setting, this can be achieved through various techniques that tap into collective energy. Remember, the power of a group can amplify individual sensitivities and insights.

Here are some effective techniques:

  • Guided Meditation for Collective Energy Scanning: Lead the group through a guided meditation, focusing on sensing the energy field of the room and individual participants. Encourage them to notice any areas of tension, heat, cold, or tingling sensations.
  • Group Dowsing with a Pendulum: Use a large pendulum or have each participant use their own. Ask specific questions about the group's collective energy, such as "Is there a common energy blockage present in this group?" Observe the pendulum's movements to gain insights.
  • Visual Observation of Auras: While more challenging in a group, you can guide participants to subtly observe the auras of others. Focus on general colors and any perceived distortions or breaks in the energy field.

It's important to emphasize that this phase is about gathering information and fostering awareness. Encourage participants to share their observationsand insights without judgment. The goal is to create a shared understanding of the group's energetic landscape.

The Understand (U) Phase in Group Settings

Once the energy field has been assessed, the next step is Understand, which involves exploring the root causes of any identified imbalances. In a group setting, this can be a powerful process of collective inquiry and shared learning. Facilitate discussions that encourage participants to connect their individual experiences to the group's overall energetic state.

Here are some techniques to facilitate understanding:

  • Open Dialogue and Sharing: Create a safe space for participants to share their personal experiences, emotions, and beliefs that may be contributing to the identified energetic imbalances. Encourage active listening and empathy.
  • Journaling Prompts for Self-Reflection: Provide journaling prompts that encourage participants to explore their inner landscape and identify potential sources of energetic blockage. Examples include: "What limiting beliefs might be holding me back?" or "What past experiences might be affecting my energy flow?"
  • Small Group Discussions: Divide the group into smaller groups of 3-4 people to discuss specific themes or topics related to the identified energetic imbalances. This allows for more intimate sharing and deeper exploration.

It's crucial to guide the discussion in a way that is both supportive and empowering. Avoid blaming or shaming, and instead focus on fostering a sense of collective responsibility and shared healing. Remind participants that understanding is the first step towards transformation.

The Release & Rebalance (R) Phase in Group Settings

With a deeper understanding of the energetic imbalances, the next step is Release & Rebalance, which involves using various techniques to clear away blocked energy and restore balance to the energy field. In a group setting, this can be a particularly potent process, as the collective energy can amplify the healing effects.

Here are some effective techniques for group release and rebalancing:

  • Guided Visualization for Energy Clearing: Lead the group through a guided visualization, inviting them to imagine releasing any negative emotions, limiting beliefs, or energetic blockages that they have identified. Use imagery that is both powerful and comforting, such as visualizing these energies being released into a cleansing light.
  • Group Sound Healing: Use sound healing instruments, such as singing bowls, gongs, or tuning forks, to create resonant frequencies that can help to clear away stagnant energy and restore balance to the energy field. The vibrations can penetrate deeply into the body and energy field, promoting relaxation and healing.
  • Movement and Breathwork: Incorporate gentle movement exercises and breathwork techniques to help release tension and promote energy flow. Examples include yoga poses, tai chi movements, or simple breathing exercises.

When guiding these techniques, be mindful of the group's energy and adjust the intensity as needed. It's important to create a safe and supportive environment where participants feel comfortable releasing and rebalancing their energy.

The Activate & Anchor (A) Phase in Group Settings

Once the energy field has been cleared and rebalanced, the final step is Activate & Anchor, which involves activating positive energy and anchoring it into the energy field. In a group setting, this can be a powerful process of collective intention and shared creation.

Here are some techniques for group activation and anchoring:

  • Affirmation and Intention Setting: Guide the group in creating and repeating positive affirmations and intentions that align with their desired outcomes. This helps to reprogram the subconscious mind and attract positive energy into their lives.
  • Gratitude Circle: Invite each participant to share something that they are grateful for. This helps to shift the focus to positive energy and create a sense of appreciation and abundance.
  • Creative Expression: Engage the group in creative activities, such as drawing, painting, or writing, to express their positive energy and intentions. This helps to anchor the positive energy into their physical reality.

Encourage participants to continue practicing these techniques on their own after the workshop, to maintain their energetic balance and continue attracting positive energy into their lives. Remind them that they have the power to create their own reality.
