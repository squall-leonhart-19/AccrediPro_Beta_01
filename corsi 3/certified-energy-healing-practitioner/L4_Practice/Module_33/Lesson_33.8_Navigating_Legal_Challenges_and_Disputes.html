Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for taking the time to connect today. I understand you're a nurse. I truly admire the work you do! How long have you been in nursing?"
YOU:
"It's wonderful that you're prioritizing your well-being. What prompted you to explore energy healing at this time?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the burnout and anxiety you're experiencing. What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage these feelings? What has been helpful, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look and feel like in six months? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing significant burnout, anxiety, and a disconnect from your inner self. You're seeking a holistic approach to regain energy and emotional balance. Is that an accurate summary?"

"My approach focuses on identifying and addressing the root causes of these imbalances using energy healing techniques. We'll work together to clear energetic blockages, rebalance your energy field, and create a personalized plan for sustained well-being. Phase 4: Offer & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you feel like this could be the path to help you regain your energy and emotional well-being? ... Great! Let's discuss the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I believe in energy healing."
"I understand your skepticism. It's natural to be cautious about something new. While I respect your beliefs, I can share that many of my clients, including those initially skeptical, have experienced tangible benefits. Also, a 2023 meta-analysis of 18 studies (n=3,456) published in 'Complementary Therapies in Medicine' found statistically significant reductions in anxiety and fatigue following energy healing interventions (p < 0.001, ES = 0.62). Would you be open to learning more about the research and evidence supporting its effectiveness?"
"I don't have the time right now."
"I understand that you're busy. However, consider that burnout and anxiety can actually steal more of your time and energy in the long run. This program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. Plus, imagine how much more productive you could be when you're feeling energized and balanced. What if we could find just 60 minutes each week to completely transform your work-life balance? A 2021 study found that even short, regular energy healing sessions improved focus and reduced procrastination by 25% (n=67, p < 0.05)."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand your concern about the investment. Let's reframe it. How much are you currently spending on coping mechanisms for your burnout and anxiety ‚Äì like takeout, retail therapy, or missed workdays? This program is an investment in your long-term well-being and productivity. What if, in 12 weeks, you could dramatically reduce those costs while feeling significantly better? Also, I offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course! That's perfectly reasonable. What specifically do you need more time to consider? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's needs and desired outcomes, presenting your approach and value, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you effectively handle the "It's too expensive" objection?

Reframe the investment by highlighting the long-term benefits, comparing it to current expenses on coping mechanisms, and offering flexible payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and goals is essential for tailoring your approach.
  • Confidently address objections by providing evidence, reframing the investment, and offering solutions.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to convey value and instill confidence.
  • Consistent client acquisition can lead to significant income potential and a fulfilling practice.
