Working with Subtle Energy Systems

Beyond the chakras, the body has other subtle energy systems, such as meridians and nadis, that play a crucial role in overall health and well-being. These systems are like invisible pathways that carry energy throughout the body, nourishing organs and tissues.

  • Meridians: Channels used in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM), each associated with specific organs and functions. Acupuncture and acupressure work by stimulating these meridians to restore balance.
  • Nadis: Thousands of energy channels in the body, as described in yogic tradition. The three main nadis are Ida, Pingala, and Sushumna, which correspond to the left, right, and central energy channels, respectively.

Understanding these systems can help you fine-tune your energy healing practice. For example, if a client has digestive issues, you might focus on the stomach and spleen meridians. If they are feeling ungrounded, you might work with the root chakra and the Ida nadi.

Safety Considerations and Contraindications

As with any healing modality, it's important to be aware of safety considerations and contraindications when working with advanced energy healing techniques. Some clients may not be suitable for certain modalities, and it's crucial to assess their individual needs and limitations.

Here are some general guidelines:

  • Pregnancy: Some energy healing techniques may be contraindicated during pregnancy. Always consult with a healthcare professional before working with pregnant clients.
  • Severe Mental Illness: Clients with severe mental illness, such as psychosis or schizophrenia, may not be suitable for certain modalities, such as shamanic journeying.
  • Medical Conditions: Clients with certain medical conditions, such as heart problems or epilepsy, may need to take precautions when receiving energy healing.

Always obtain informed consent from your clients before beginning any energy healing session. Explain the potential risks and benefits, and answer any questions they may have. Be prepared to refer clients to other healthcare professionals if needed. Remember, your role is to support their healing journey, not to replace conventional medical care. The A.U.R.A.
