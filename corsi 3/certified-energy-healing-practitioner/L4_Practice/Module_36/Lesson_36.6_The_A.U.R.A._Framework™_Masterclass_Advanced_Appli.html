Troubleshooting Common Challenges

Even with a well-structured framework, challenges may arise during the healing process. This section addresses common obstacles and provides strategies for overcoming them.

  • Resistance: Clients may resist change due to fear, limiting beliefs, or secondary gains. Address resistance with empathy, education, and gentle encouragement.
  • Stagnation: Progress may plateau despite consistent effort. Explore potential underlying issues, adjust techniques, or refer to other specialists if needed.
  • Setbacks: Clients may experience temporary regressions due to stress, emotional triggers, or external events. Provide support, reinforce coping mechanisms, and adjust the treatment plan accordingly.

A 2021 meta-analysis in Frontiers in Psychology (n=1247) found that addressing client resistance through motivational interviewing techniques improved treatment adherence by 35% and overall outcomes by 28% (p < 0.01). This highlights the importance of addressing resistance directly.
