2. Certification Submission Process

Submitting your certification materials is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to ensure your application is complete and processed efficiently:

  1. Access the Online Portal: Log in to your AccrediPro Academy account and navigate to the "Certification Submission" portal. You'll find this in the final module.
  2. Complete Required Documentation: The portal will guide you through each required document. This includes:
    • A summary of your 50 practical application hours.
    • Your 3 detailed case studies.
    • A signed copy of the ethics agreement.
  3. Review and Submit: Before submitting, carefully review all documents to ensure accuracy and completeness. Once you are satisfied, click the "Submit" button.
  4. Deadlines: Be mindful of the submission deadline, which is 30 days from the completion of the final module. Late submissions may result in delays in processing your certification.

4. Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to some frequently asked questions about the certification process:

Q: What if I need an extension on the submission deadline?

A: Extensions may be granted on a case-by-case basis. Contact the AccrediPro Academy support team with a detailed explanation of your circumstances and the length of extension you require. Supporting documentation may be requested.

Q: What if my case studies are not approved?

A: If your case studies are not approved, you will receive detailed feedback outlining the areas that need improvement. You will have the opportunity to revise and resubmit your case studies within a specified timeframe.

Q: Can I appeal the certification decision?

A: Yes, you have the right to appeal the certification decision. To initiate an appeal, submit a written request to the AccrediPro Academy certification committee, outlining the reasons for your appeal and providing any supporting documentation. The committee will review your appeal and make a final determination.

5. Maintaining Certification

Maintaining your Certified Energy Healing Practitionerâ„¢ designation requires ongoing commitment to professional development and ethical practice. Here are the requirements for maintaining your certification:

  1. Continuing Education: Complete a minimum of 15 hours of continuing education every two years. These hours should be related to energy healing, holistic wellness, or professional development. Approved continuing education activities include workshops, conferences, online courses, and mentorship programs.
  2. Annual Ethics Review: Review the ethics agreement annually and reaffirm your commitment to ethical conduct. This ensures you remain aware of your responsibilities to clients and the profession.
  3. Certification Renewal: Renew your certification every two years by submitting documentation of your continuing education hours and reaffirming your ethics agreement. A renewal fee applies.
