Reiki for Chronic Pain Management

Chronic pain conditions such as fibromyalgia, arthritis, and neuropathic pain can significantly impact a person's quality of life. While traditional medicine offers pain management strategies, Reiki provides a complementary approach that addresses the energetic components of pain. This involves identifying energy blockages, reducing inflammation, and promoting relaxation. Remember The A.U.R.A. Adapting Reiki Techniques:

  1. Scanning the Energy Field: Use your hands to scan the affected areas and identify areas of heat, cold, tingling, or numbness. These sensations indicate energy imbalances.
  2. Targeted Hand Positions: Focus your Reiki energy on specific areas of pain, as well as related chakras. For example, for lower back pain, focus on the root chakra and the sacral chakra.
  3. Infusing Intentions: Clearly state your intention to reduce pain, inflammation, and promote healing.
  4. Gentle Touch: Use a light, gentle touch or hover your hands slightly above the body if the client is sensitive to touch.

A 2015 meta-analysis published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found that Reiki significantly reduced pain intensity compared to placebo in various chronic pain conditions (n = 12 studies, p <0.001). The effect size was moderate, suggesting a clinically meaningful benefit.

Distance Reiki and Group Healing

Distance Reiki allows you to send Reiki energy to individuals or groups who are not physically present. This technique is based on the principle that energy is not limited by time or space. With the A.U.R.A. Frameworkâ„¢, we are assessing the energy field remotely, understanding the energetic needs of the client, releasing blockages across distance, and activating healing from afar.
