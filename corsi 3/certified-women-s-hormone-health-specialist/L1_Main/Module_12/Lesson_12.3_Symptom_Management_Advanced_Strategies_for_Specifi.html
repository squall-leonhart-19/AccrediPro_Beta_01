  • Targeted Nutrients: Consider incorporating nutrients like calcium (1000-1200mg/day), magnesium (200-400mg/day), and vitamin B6 (50-100mg/day). A 2016 meta-analysis showed that magnesium supplementation significantly reduced PMS symptoms (p < 0.05).1
  • Herbal Remedies: Chasteberry (Vitex agnus-castus) has been shown to reduce PMS symptoms by modulating prolactin levels.   • Lifestyle Modifications: Emphasize the importance of regular exercise, stress management techniques (like mindfulness and meditation), and adequate sleep. A 2023 study found that women who practiced yoga for 30 minutes, three times a week, experienced a 40% reduction in PMS symptoms.3
  • Seed Cycling: This involves consuming specific seeds during different phases of the menstrual cycle to support hormone balance. During the follicular phase (days 1-14), consume 1 tablespoon each of flax and pumpkin seeds. During the luteal phase (days 15-28), consume 1 tablespoon each of sunflower and sesame seeds.

3. Strategies for Alleviating Fatigue and Improving Energy Levels

Fatigue is a common complaint among women with hormonal imbalances. Addressing fatigue requires a comprehensive approach that considers mitochondrial function, nutrient deficiencies, and sleep optimization. Again, the F.L.O.U.R.I.S.H. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a valuable lens.

  • Mitochondrial Support: Supplement with CoQ10 (100-200mg/day), alpha-lipoic acid (ALA) (200-600mg/day), and L-carnitine (500-1000mg/day) to support mitochondrial energy production. A 2015 study showed that CoQ10 supplementation improved fatigue symptoms in individuals with chronic fatigue syndrome.6
  • Nutrient Deficiencies: Assess for deficiencies in iron, vitamin D, and B vitamins. Correcting these deficiencies can significantly improve energy levels.
  • Sleep Optimization: Prioritize sleep hygiene by establishing a regular sleep schedule, creating a relaxing bedtime routine, and optimizing the sleep environment. Consider using melatonin (0.5-5mg) or magnesium glycinate (200-400mg) to improve sleep quality.

4. Case Study: PCOS Management

Case Study: Sarah, 28 years old

Client: Sarah, a 28-year-old woman, presented with irregular menstrual cycles, acne, hirsutism, and difficulty losing weight. She was diagnosed with PCOS two years prior. Her symptoms were impacting her self-esteem and quality of life.

Intervention:

  • Dietary Modifications: Sarah was placed on a low-glycemic diet with an emphasis on whole foods, lean protein, and healthy fats.
  • Supplementation: She was prescribed Inositol (4g/day), Berberine (500mg twice daily), and Spearmint tea (2 cups daily).
  • Lifestyle Changes: Sarah started a regular exercise routine that included both cardio and strength training. She also incorporated stress management techniques such as yoga and meditation.

Outcomes: After three months, Sarah experienced more regular menstrual cycles, a reduction in acne and hirsutism, and a weight loss of 10 pounds. Her energy levels improved, and she reported a significant improvement in her mood and self-esteem.
