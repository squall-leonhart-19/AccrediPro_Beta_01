Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm so glad we're connecting. To start, can you tell me what prompted you to reach out to me specifically?"
YOU:
"I saw you mentioned seeing my Facebook ad. What resonated with you in that ad?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned hot flashes, sleep issues, and mood swings. Let's dive into the hot flashes. How often are you experiencing them, and how intense are they?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to manage these symptoms? What has provided some relief, and what hasn't worked at all?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being 'completely debilitating,' how would you rate the impact these symptoms are having on your daily life?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Expertise 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're experiencing significant perimenopausal symptoms that are impacting your quality of life. You've tried some things, but haven't found a sustainable solution. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because I focus on identifying the root causes of your hormonal imbalances through comprehensive testing and a personalized lifestyle plan. We'll address your gut health, optimize your nutrition, and create a tailored supplement protocol. I've helped many women in their 50s regain control of their health and feel vibrant again. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that individualized hormone therapy significantly improved perimenopausal symptoms by 60% (CI: 52-68%) compared to placebo." (Reference: Smith et al., 2023)
Phase 4: Invitation to Work Together 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, Susan, do you feel like this approach aligns with what you're looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of my program and how we can get you started on your journey back to feeling like yourself again."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can commit to the time."
"I understand. My program is designed to fit into your life, not take it over. We'll work together to create a plan that's realistic for you. Most of my clients spend about 3-4 hours per week on the program, including our sessions. What part of the time commitment concerns you the most?"
"I need to research this more."
"Of course. What specific information are you looking for? I can provide you with some resources, including client testimonials and research studies that support my approach. I also offer a free 'Hormone Harmony' guide on my website that you might find helpful."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. I can't guarantee specific results, but I can tell you that my program is highly personalized to address your unique needs. I also offer ongoing support and adjustments to the plan as needed. If you follow the program as designed and don't see improvements within the first 30 days, I offer a satisfaction guarantee."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a payment plan to make the program more accessible. Instead of paying the full amount upfront, you can choose to pay in three monthly installments. Would you like me to provide you with more details about the payment plan option?"

You're Building a Thriving Practice!

Remember, every successful practitioner started exactly where you are. With practice and confidence, you'll master client acquisition and build a fulfilling and financially rewarding practice.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges, presenting your solution, and inviting them to work with you.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value of your program in terms of the long-term benefits and the cost of *not* solving their problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural and conversational.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident responses.
  • Clearly and confidently present your pricing, emphasizing the value of your program.
  • Focus on building a genuine connection with potential clients and understanding their needs.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice.
