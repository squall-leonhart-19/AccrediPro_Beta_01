Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that investing in your health is a big decision. Let's look at it this way: How much have you already spent on products or treatments that haven't worked? My program is designed to provide lasting results, saving you money and frustration in the long run. We also have payment plan options available."
"I'm worried I won't have time to commit to the program."
"I hear you. Time is valuable. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy lifestyle. The weekly sessions are [duration] and the lifestyle changes are gradual and sustainable. We can work together to create a plan that works for you. Plus, think about the time you'll save when you're not struggling with these symptoms anymore!"
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I understand your skepticism. Many of my clients have felt the same way before starting the program. My approach is different because it's highly personalized and addresses the root causes of your hormonal imbalances, not just masking the symptoms. We use comprehensive testing and tailor the program specifically to your needs. I'm confident we can find a solution that works for you."
"Can I get back to you?"
"Of course! What additional information would be helpful for you to make a decision? I'm happy to answer any further questions you have now, or I can send you some resources to review. When do you think you'll be ready to decide?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge the concern, reframe the value, and offer payment options if available. Emphasize the long-term benefits and potential cost savings.
