Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the initial phase of a discovery call?

Building rapport involves welcoming the prospect, expressing gratitude for their time, and asking open-ended questions to understand their motivations and interests.

2. How can you address the objection that your program is "too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, but reframe the cost as an investment in their long-term health and well-being. Highlight the potential savings from reduced medical expenses and improved quality of life.

3. What are some effective closing lines to use at the end of a discovery call?

Use confident and direct closing lines such as: "Are you ready to take the first step?" or "I believe I can help you feel like yourself again. Are you ready to get started?"

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Building rapport and understanding the prospect's challenges are essential for creating a connection.
  • Confidently presenting your program's value and addressing objections are key to closing the sale.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines to increase your confidence and success rate.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice and consistent effort.
