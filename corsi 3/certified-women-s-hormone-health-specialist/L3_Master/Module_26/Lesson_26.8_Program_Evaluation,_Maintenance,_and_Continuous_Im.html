Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize a Holistic Approach

Remind Sarah that supplements are just one piece of the puzzle. Discuss the importance of diet, exercise, stress management, and sleep hygiene.

2

Teach Dosage Strategies

Explain the importance of starting with low doses and gradually increasing until the desired effect is achieved. Discuss potential side effects and how to manage them.

3

Review Supplement Interactions

Ensure Sarah is aware of potential interactions between supplements and medications. Encourage her to always ask clients about their current medications and to consult with a pharmacist or physician if needed.

4

Clarify Scope of Practice

Reinforce the importance of staying within the scope of practice. Remind Sarah that she is not a medical doctor and cannot diagnose or treat medical conditions. Explain when to refer clients to a medical doctor.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to start with a low dosage of supplements when working with a new client?

Starting with a low dosage helps to assess the client's tolerance and minimize the risk of side effects. It also allows you to gradually titrate up to the optimal dose.

2. What are some key lifestyle factors to consider when addressing PMS symptoms?

Key lifestyle factors include diet, exercise, stress management, and sleep hygiene. Addressing these factors can significantly reduce PMS symptoms.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not just providing answers.
  • A holistic approach is essential when addressing women's hormone health.
  • Dosage strategies should be individualized and carefully monitored.
  • Understanding scope of practice is crucial for ethical and safe practice.
  • Providing encouragement and support builds confidence in new practitioners.
