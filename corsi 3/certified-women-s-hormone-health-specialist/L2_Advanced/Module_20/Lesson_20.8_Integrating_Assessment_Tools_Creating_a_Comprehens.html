Step 1: Identify the Interconnected Systems
Eleanor's case highlights the interplay between the endocrine, immune, and nervous systems. Hashimoto's (autoimmune thyroid) impacts hormone production, contributing to perimenopausal symptoms. Stress and poor sleep further dysregulate these systems. Eczema flare-ups suggest immune activation.

The elevated FSH and low estradiol confirm perimenopause. However, her symptoms are likely exacerbated by Hashimoto's. Stress, poor sleep, and potential nutrient deficiencies (despite multivitamin) are also key drivers. The coffee/wine habit impacts blood sugar and liver detoxification.

Considering Other Possibilities

1

Adrenal Dysfunction

Consider a comprehensive stool analysis to evaluate gut bacteria, yeast, and parasites. Gut dysbiosis can trigger immune responses and worsen eczema and thyroid autoimmunity.

Even with low estradiol, she may have relative estrogen dominance if progesterone is even lower. Consider testing progesterone levels to evaluate this.
