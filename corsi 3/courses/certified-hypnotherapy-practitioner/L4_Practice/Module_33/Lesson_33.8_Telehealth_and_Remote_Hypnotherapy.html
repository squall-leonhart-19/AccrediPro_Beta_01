Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if hypnotherapy really works."
"I understand your skepticism. It's natural to be cautious. Hypnotherapy has been shown in studies to be effective for smoking cessation. A 2007 meta-analysis showed a 20% quit rate with hypnotherapy. I can share more research with you if you'd like. But the best way to know is to experience it. Are you open to trying a brief hypnosis exercise right now to see how it feels?"
"I'm worried I don't have enough willpower."
"That's a common concern, and the beauty of hypnotherapy is that it doesn't rely on willpower. We're working with your subconscious mind, which is much more powerful. It's about changing your desires and cravings, not just resisting them."
"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"That's perfectly reasonable. How about I send you a summary of the program and some testimonials that you can share? And if your partner has any questions, I'm happy to hop on a quick call with both of you to address them."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan of two installments to make it more accessible for you. Would you like to learn more about that option?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection, "I'm not sure if hypnotherapy really works"?

Acknowledge their skepticism, share research or testimonials, and offer a brief hypnosis exercise to experience it.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide evidence or solutions.
  • Present your pricing confidently and highlight the value of your program.
  • Practice your closing lines to increase your conversion rate.
  • Consistent client acquisition can lead to a significant income stream.
