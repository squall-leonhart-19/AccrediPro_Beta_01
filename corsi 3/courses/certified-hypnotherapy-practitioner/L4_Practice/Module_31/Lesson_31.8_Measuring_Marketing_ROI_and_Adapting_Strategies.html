Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the business skills to succeed."
"That's a common concern, Susan. The truth is, many successful hypnotherapists don't come from a business background. That's why my program includes comprehensive business training and ongoing support. We'll walk you through every step, from creating a marketing plan to managing your finances. You don't need to be a natural entrepreneur ‚Äì you just need to be willing to learn and take action."
"I'm worried about the investment. It's a lot of money."
"I understand. It's important to consider the return on investment. Think about what it would be worth to you to create a fulfilling career that provides financial freedom and allows you to make a positive impact. My program is designed to help you generate income quickly, often within the first few months. Plus, consider this an investment in yourself and your future. We also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it and do more research."
"Of course. I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision. What specific areas do you need to research further? Perhaps I can provide some additional resources or connect you with some of my successful graduates who have made a similar transition. What would be most helpful for you?"
"I'm not sure if I'm good enough to be a hypnotherapist."
"Imposter syndrome is very common, especially when starting something new. Remember, everyone starts somewhere. My program provides a supportive and encouraging environment where you'll receive personalized feedback and guidance to develop your skills and build your confidence. I believe in your potential, and I'm committed to helping you succeed."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key questions to ask during the "Understand Her Needs" phase of a discovery call?

Potential answers: "What are you hoping to achieve?", "What are your biggest concerns?", "What does your ideal business look like?"

2. How would you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it and do more research"?

A good response acknowledges their need, asks what specifically they need to research, and offers helpful resources.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and connecting with your prospect is crucial for a successful discovery call.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and desires is essential for presenting a relevant solution.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy can turn potential "no's" into "yes's."
  • Confidently stating your prices and highlighting the value of your program is key to closing the sale.
  • Consistent practice and a belief in your abilities are essential for client acquisition success.
