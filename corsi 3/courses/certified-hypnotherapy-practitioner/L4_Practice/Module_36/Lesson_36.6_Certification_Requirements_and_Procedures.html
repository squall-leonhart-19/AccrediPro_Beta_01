    3. Code of Ethics

    As a Certified Hypnotherapy Practitionerâ„¢, you are expected to adhere to a strict code of ethics that governs your professional conduct. This code is designed to protect the well-being of your clients and maintain the integrity of the profession. Ethical considerations are woven into the T.R.A.N.C.E. Frameworkâ„¢, emphasizing client-centered care and responsible practice.

    Key principles of the code of ethics include:

    • Client Welfare: Prioritize the well-being and best interests of your clients at all times.
    • Confidentiality: Maintain strict confidentiality regarding client information, except where disclosure is required by law.
    • Informed Consent: Obtain informed consent from clients before commencing any hypnotherapy session, ensuring they understand the nature of the treatment, potential risks, and benefits.
    • Professional Boundaries: Maintain clear professional boundaries with clients, avoiding dual relationships or any behavior that could be construed as exploitative.
    • Competence: Practice within the scope of your competence, and seek supervision or referral when necessary.
    • Integrity: Conduct yourself with honesty, integrity, and professionalism in all interactions with clients, colleagues, and the public.
