Clinical Reasoning Process

Step-by-Step Analysis

Step 1: Identify Primary Systems Involved
This case involves the nervous system (chronic pain, migraines, PTSD, anxiety, insomnia), the musculoskeletal system (lower back pain, fibromyalgia), and the digestive system (GERD, potential medication side effects). The biopsychosocial model is essential here.
Step 2: Uncover Potential Root Causes
The car accident appears to be a significant trigger, leading to PTSD and chronic pain. The central sensitization associated with fibromyalgia may be amplifying her pain experience. Consider the impact of long-term medication use (Amitriptyline, Omeprazole) on nutrient absorption and overall health.

Differential Considerations

Considering Other Possibilities

1

Underlying Autoimmune Condition

Fibromyalgia can sometimes coexist with autoimmune disorders. Consider screening for conditions like rheumatoid arthritis or lupus, especially if inflammatory markers are elevated.

2

Medication-Induced Migraines

Frequent use of Sumatriptan can lead to medication-overuse headaches (rebound headaches). Evaluate her usage patterns and discuss alternative migraine management strategies with her physician.

3

Hormonal Imbalances

At age 48, perimenopause could be contributing to her migraines, insomnia, and anxiety. Consider a hormonal evaluation.

4

Substance Use Disorder

Regular alcohol consumption (2-3 glasses of wine nightly) could be a coping mechanism for underlying distress. Assess for signs of alcohol dependence or abuse using validated screening tools.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when using hypnotherapy with a client who has both chronic pain and a history of trauma?

Prioritize safety and stabilization. Use trauma-informed techniques. Start with resource activation before directly addressing the trauma. Monitor for signs of distress or re-traumatization.

2. How can you differentiate between medication-induced migraines and migraines caused by other factors in this case?

Assess the frequency and timing of Sumatriptan use. If migraines occur more frequently after the medication wears off, consider medication-overuse headaches. Consult with her physician about alternative migraine management strategies.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases like Sarah's require a holistic, biopsychosocial approach.
  • Trauma is a significant factor in chronic pain and must be addressed carefully.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare professionals is essential for optimal outcomes.
  • Hypnotherapy can be a powerful tool for pain management, anxiety reduction, and trauma processing.
  • Always prioritize client safety and well-being.
