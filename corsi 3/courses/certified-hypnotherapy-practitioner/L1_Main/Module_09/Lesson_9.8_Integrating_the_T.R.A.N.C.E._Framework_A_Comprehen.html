Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your experiences with me. It sounds like you're at a significant crossroads, and it takes courage to explore new directions. Many women find themselves re-evaluating their lives around this time, and it's completely normal to feel a bit lost."
MARIA:
"Lost is exactly the right word. I feel like I've been on autopilot for years, and now I don't know where to steer."
YOU:
"I understand. So, let's start by acknowledging all that you *have* accomplished. You've dedicated years to teaching, raised a child, and navigated a divorce. Those are all huge achievements. Now, it‚Äôs about redirecting that energy toward something new. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"It sounds good, but I'm afraid of making the wrong choice. What if I invest time and money into something and it doesn't work out?"
YOU:
"That's a valid concern, Maria. We'll address that fear directly. This process isn't about making a perfect, irreversible decision. It's about taking small, manageable steps, gathering information, and adjusting as we go. We'll focus on exploring your values, passions, and skills to find a path that truly resonates with you."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief that her feelings are normal.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to acknowledge Maria's past achievements before discussing her future goals?

Acknowledging her past achievements validates her experiences and builds her confidence, making her more receptive to exploring new possibilities.

2. What is the primary goal of the coaching conversation outlined in the script?

The primary goal is to create a safe and supportive space for Maria to explore her fears, values, and passions, and to take small, manageable steps toward finding a new sense of purpose.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial for building trust with clients.
  • Addressing fears and concerns upfront can help clients feel more secure and open to change.
  • Focusing on values and passions can guide clients toward fulfilling new directions.
  • Small, manageable steps can make overwhelming goals feel more attainable.
