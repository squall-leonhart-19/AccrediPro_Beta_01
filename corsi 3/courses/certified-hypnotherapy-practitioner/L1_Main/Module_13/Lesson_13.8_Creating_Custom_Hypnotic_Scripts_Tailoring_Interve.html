Deepening Script

Use this script to deepen Beth's hypnotic state. Remember to speak slowly and calmly. Feel free to adapt the language to your own style.

YOU:
"Beth, allow yourself to simply drift... to let go of any tension... with each breath, you move deeper and deeper into relaxation..."
YOU:
"Imagine yourself on a staircase... a beautiful, winding staircase leading down into a peaceful garden... with each step you take downwards, your relaxation deepens..."
YOU:
"With each number I count down, you'll find yourself twice as relaxed as you are now. Ten... feeling the gentle pull downwards... Nine... allowing your mind to quiet... Eight... deeper and deeper..."
YOU:
"Seven... feeling a sense of calm wash over you... Six... your body is heavy and relaxed... Five... drifting further down the staircase..."
YOU:
"Four... almost at the bottom now... Three... feeling incredibly peaceful... Two... one more step... One... you've arrived at the garden, a place of perfect tranquility..."
YOU:
"Take a moment to explore this garden... notice the colors, the scents, the sounds... Allow this feeling of peace to anchor itself deep within you..."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to personalize the deepening script to the client?

Personalizing the script helps to build rapport and trust, making the client feel more comfortable and receptive to the hypnotic suggestions. It also allows you to tailor the imagery and language to the client's specific interests and experiences, which can enhance the effectiveness of the deepening process.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is entering a deeper state of hypnosis?

Non-verbal cues that indicate a client is entering a deeper state of hypnosis include slower and deeper breathing, relaxation of facial muscles, decreased muscle tension, and a general sense of stillness. The client may also exhibit reduced blinking and a glazed-over look in their eyes.
