Understanding Fractionation

Fractionation is a technique used to deepen hypnosis by repeatedly bringing the client into and out of trance. This process leverages the subconscious mind's natural tendency to deepen its response with each repetition. Think of it like repeatedly dipping a cloth into dye â€“ each dip intensifies the color.

The key to effective fractionation lies in the contrast between the hypnotic state and the waking state. By oscillating between the two, you create a stronger distinction, which helps the client's mind more readily accept and deepen the hypnotic experience.

A 2018 study published in the "International Journal of Clinical and Experimental Hypnosis" found that fractionation significantly increased hypnotic susceptibility in 72% of participants (n=65) compared to a control group.

The Elman Induction: A Detailed Breakdown

The Elman Induction, developed by Dave Elman, is a rapid and effective hypnotic induction technique known for its ability to quickly induce deep trance states. It focuses on physiological relaxation, mental relaxation, and ultimately, somnambulism. The Elman Induction works by systematically relaxing the body and mind through direct suggestion and testing for compliance.

The Elman Induction typically involves the following steps:

  1. Eye Closure: Instructing the client to close their eyes and focus on relaxation.
  2. Muscle Relaxation: Suggesting relaxation of specific muscle groups, such as the eyelids, and testing for relaxation by suggesting they are too relaxed to open their eyes.
  3. Mental Relaxation: Guiding the client to relax their mind, often by counting down from 100 and letting go of thoughts with each number.
  4. Deepening: Using techniques like counting down or visualizing going deeper to enhance the trance state.
  5. Testing Depth: Assessing the depth of trance through suggestions like arm levitation or catalepsy.
Step Description Purpose
Eye Closure Client closes eyes and focuses on relaxation. Initiates the relaxation process and focuses attention inward.
Muscle Relaxation Progressive relaxation of muscle groups, starting with the eyelids. Achieves physiological relaxation and tests for compliance.
Mental Relaxation Counting down and releasing thoughts. Quiets the conscious mind and deepens the trance.
Deepening Using imagery or counting to enhance the trance state. Further enhances relaxation and responsiveness.
Testing Depth Suggestions like arm levitation or catalepsy. Assesses the depth of trance and reinforces suggestions.

Elman Induction Practice Script

Here's a sample script for practicing the Elman Induction. Remember to speak slowly, clearly, and with confidence. Adapt the script to your own style and the client's needs.

"Please close your eyes now... and take a deep breath... As you exhale, allow yourself to begin to relax... That's right, just let go... Focus on your eyelids... Notice how they feel... Now, imagine they are becoming heavier and heavier... so heavy that they are sealed shut... Test them now... try to open them... but you'll find they are too relaxed to open... That's right, just let them stay closed... Now, take another deep breath... and as you exhale, allow that relaxation to spread throughout your entire body... from the top of your head to the tips of your toes..."

"Now, I'm going to count down from 100... with each number, allow yourself to go deeper and deeper into relaxation... 100... letting go of any tension... 99... feeling more and more relaxed... 98... allowing your mind to quiet... 97... drifting deeper... 96... feeling peaceful and calm... Continue to relax as I count down..."

Adapting the Elman Induction

The Elman Induction can be adapted to suit different clients and situations. For example, for highly analytical clients, you might provide more logical explanations for each step. For clients with anxiety, you might focus more on creating a sense of safety and security.

For sensitive clients, consider using gentler language and avoiding direct commands. Instead of saying "You cannot open your eyes," you might say "You may find that your eyes are so relaxed that they prefer to stay closed."

Integrating Fractionation with Elman

Combining fractionation with the Elman Induction can significantly deepen the trance state. After the initial induction, you can bring the client briefly out of trance and then re-induce them, repeating this process several times.

For example, after the initial Elman Induction and deepening, you might say, "Now, I'm going to count to three, and when I reach three, you will open your eyes, feeling refreshed and alert. One... two... three... eyes open." Then, after a brief moment, "Now, close your eyes again, and allow yourself to go even deeper than before..."

Case Study: Sarah, 34, Anxiety Management

Client: Sarah, a 34-year-old marketing manager, presented with symptoms of generalized anxiety disorder. She reported difficulty sleeping, constant worry, and frequent panic attacks.

Intervention: I utilized the Elman Induction combined with fractionation. After the initial induction, I brought Sarah out of trance twice, each time deepening the induction further upon re-entry. During the trance, I used suggestions for relaxation, calm, and self-compassion.

Outcomes: After four sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in her anxiety levels. She was sleeping better, experiencing fewer panic attacks, and feeling more in control of her thoughts and emotions. She also reported feeling more confident in her ability to manage stress.
