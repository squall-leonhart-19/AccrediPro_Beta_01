Your Coaching Script

Here's a sample script to help Maria reframe her situation and activate her inner resources. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, it sounds like you're at a crossroads, and it's completely understandable to feel stuck given everything you've been through. Many talented writers experience similar feelings, especially after significant life changes. Let's reframe this situation: instead of seeing your past challenges as roadblocks, let's explore how they might have equipped you with unique insights and resilience."
MARIA:
"I don't know... it just feels like I'm starting over, and everyone else is already ahead of me."
YOU:
"I hear you. And what if 'starting over' is actually a chance to design a career that's even more aligned with who you are today? Think about it: you have years of writing experience, you've navigated a divorce, you've learned new skills in those online courses. All of that is valuable. What's one strength you've developed through these experiences that could benefit your writing career?"

"Well, I guess I'm more empathetic now. I understand people's struggles better."
YOU:
"Exactly! That empathy is a superpower. How could you leverage that in your writing? Perhaps by focusing on topics that help others through similar challenges? Or by crafting more relatable characters? Let's brainstorm some specific niches where your empathy would shine, and then we'll identify resources to help you get there."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the key difference between simply giving advice and effectively reframing a client's situation?

Reframing involves helping the client see their situation from a new perspective, often highlighting potential strengths or opportunities they hadn't recognized. Advice, on the other hand, is simply telling them what to do, without necessarily changing their underlying mindset.

2. How can you activate a client's inner resources during a coaching session?

By asking open-ended questions that prompt them to identify their strengths, skills, and past successes. Encourage them to reflect on how they've overcome challenges in the past and how those experiences can inform their current situation.

Key Takeaways

  • Reframing helps clients see challenges from new, empowering perspectives.
  • Activating resources reminds clients of their inner strengths and past successes.
  • Empathy is crucial for building trust and understanding the client's experience.
  • Open-ended questions are powerful tools for both reframing and resource activation.
  • Practice is essential for developing confidence and skill in these techniques.
