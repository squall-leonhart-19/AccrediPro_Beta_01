Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on establishing trust and rapport. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you so much for being here today and sharing what's been going on. I want you to know that I truly admire your courage in facing these challenges head-on, especially after such a long and dedicated career."
MARIA:
"Thank you. It means a lot to hear that. I feel like I should have it all figured out by now."
YOU:
"It's completely understandable to feel that way, Maria. But honestly, life transitions like these ‚Äì career shifts, retirement planning, navigating life after divorce ‚Äì they can bring up a lot of uncertainty for anyone, no matter their experience. What you're feeling is valid, and you're not alone."
YOU:
"Before we dive into specific strategies, I'd love to hear more about what anxiety and feeling 'stuck' actually *feel* like for you. Can you paint a picture for me?"

"Well, it's like this constant knot in my stomach, especially at night. And during the day, I just feel...unmotivated. Like I'm going through the motions."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Maria. It sounds incredibly draining. Knowing this, let's focus on creating a safe space for you to explore these feelings without judgment. Does that sound good?"

  • Maria visibly relaxes and becomes more open.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is building trust and rapport especially important with clients experiencing life transitions?

Life transitions often bring uncertainty and vulnerability. Building trust creates a safe space for clients to explore their feelings and challenges without judgment.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is building trust with you?

Non-verbal cues include relaxed body language, eye contact, nodding, and a more open posture.

Key Takeaways

  • Building trust and rapport is foundational to effective coaching.
  • Validating a client's feelings is crucial for creating a safe space.
  • Active listening and empathy are essential skills for building trust.
  • Personalizing your approach and being genuine fosters stronger connections.
  • Pay attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues to assess the level of trust.
