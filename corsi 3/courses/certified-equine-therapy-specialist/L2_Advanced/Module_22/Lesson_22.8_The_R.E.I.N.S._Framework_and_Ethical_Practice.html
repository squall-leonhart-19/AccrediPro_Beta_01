Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Considerations in This Case

Step 1: Identify Potential Ethical Conflicts
Multiple conflicts exist: dual relationships (friend of client, spouse of veterinarian), impaired consent (mild cognitive impairment), boundary violations (client self-disclosure, bartering), and potential family dynamics issues (treating mother and daughter).
Step 2: Apply Relevant Ethical Principles
Prioritize beneficence (doing good), non-maleficence (avoiding harm), autonomy (respecting client's rights), and justice (fairness). Consider the APA Ethical Principles and Code of Conduct.
Step 3: Evaluate Risks and Benefits
Assess the potential harm versus benefit of each decision. For example, accepting Eleanor's offer of bartering could compromise objectivity and create a power imbalance.
Step 4: Develop a Course of Action
Address each ethical concern systematically. This might involve consulting with a supervisor, seeking legal counsel, or referring Eleanor to another therapist.

Check Your Understanding

1. What steps should you take regarding Eleanor's mild cognitive impairment and informed consent?

Obtain informed consent in a manner that is easily understandable to Eleanor. Consider involving a healthcare proxy or legal guardian if her cognitive impairment significantly affects her decision-making capacity. Document all steps taken to ensure informed consent.

2. How would you address the potential dual relationship with Eleanor's best friend, Jane, who is also a client?

Acknowledge the dual relationship and discuss potential conflicts of interest with both Eleanor and Jane. Obtain informed consent from both clients, ensuring they understand the risks and benefits of continuing therapy. Maintain clear boundaries and objectivity in all interactions. If the dual relationship compromises your ability to provide effective therapy, consider referring one or both clients to another therapist.

3. Discuss the ethical implications of accepting Eleanor's offer to trade services from her business for therapy sessions.

Accepting Eleanor's offer to trade services creates a conflict of interest and compromises objectivity. It also introduces a power imbalance into the therapeutic relationship. Decline the offer and explore alternative payment options. If Eleanor is unable to afford therapy, consider offering a sliding scale fee or referring her to a low-cost provider.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex ethical dilemmas require a systematic approach and careful consideration of ethical principles.
  • Dual relationships, impaired consent, and boundary violations are common ethical challenges in equine therapy practice.
  • Consultation with a supervisor or ethics expert is crucial for navigating complex ethical situations.
  • Documentation of all ethical considerations and interventions is essential for protecting both the client and the therapist.
  • Prioritizing client well-being and maintaining ethical boundaries are paramount in equine therapy practice.
