Susan, 52

Found you on Instagram. Booked a discovery call after seeing your post about equine-assisted therapy for stress relief.

Her Situation
High-stress job as a lawyer, feeling burnt out, struggles to relax.
Budget Concern
"I'm willing to invest in my well-being, but I need to see the value."

Analytical, needs evidence-based information, appreciates a structured approach.

"I want to find a way to manage my stress and feel more balanced in my life."

Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. I noticed you mentioned my stress-relief post ‚Äì what resonated with you?"
YOU:
"That's interesting. Many of my clients in high-pressure roles find equine therapy uniquely helpful. Tell me, what's been contributing to your stress lately?"
Phase 2: Problem Exploration 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe a typical day for me? What are the biggest stressors you face?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you don't address this stress, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you *could* wave a magic wand and eliminate it, what would that look like for you?"
Phase 3: Solution Presentation 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing [summarize Susan's pain points]. Is that a fair assessment?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different because it's experiential. Equine-assisted therapy allows you to connect with horses in a way that bypasses the thinking mind and accesses your emotions directly. Over 8 weeks, we'll work together to identify your stress triggers, develop coping mechanisms, and build resilience. A 2018 study in the Journal of Equine Assisted Activities and Therapy found that participants experienced a 34% reduction in perceived stress after an 8-week program."
Phase 4: Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you see how working with horses could help you manage your stress? ... Fantastic! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get you started."

Handling Common Objections

Navigating Objections with Confidence

"I'm not sure if I'm comfortable working with horses."
"That's perfectly understandable. Many people feel that way initially. We always prioritize your safety and comfort. You don't need any prior experience with horses. We'll start slowly, and you'll be guided by experienced professionals. Would it help if I shared some testimonials from other clients who were also initially hesitant?"
"I need to research equine therapy more."
"I understand. I can send you some credible research articles and resources about the benefits of equine-assisted therapy, including a meta-analysis published in 2022 in Frontiers in Psychology that showed significant improvements in mental well-being measures. Would that be helpful?"
"I'm worried about the time commitment."
"I appreciate that you're busy. The program is designed to be efficient and effective. It's one 90-minute session per week for 8 weeks. Many of my clients find that the benefits they experience ‚Äì reduced stress, increased focus ‚Äì actually *save* them time in the long run. How does that sound?"
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a payment plan. You can split the investment into two monthly installments. This makes it more accessible while still allowing you to commit to your well-being. Would you like me to explain the payment plan in more detail?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the investment in terms of value and outcomes, and offer a payment plan if possible.
