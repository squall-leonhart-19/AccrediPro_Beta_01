Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Karen, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm excited to chat with you. I saw you signed up for my interest list through Facebook. What caught your eye about the program?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! So, tell me a bit about what's been going on in your life that led you to explore something like this?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling isolated. Can you tell me more about that? What does a typical week look like for you right now?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried to combat this? Have you joined any other groups or activities?"
YOU:
"If things stay the same, where do you see yourself in six months? A year? And if you *could* create your ideal social life, what would it look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Karen, based on what you've said, it sounds like you're really craving connection and a sense of belonging. Am I understanding that correctly?"
YOU:
"That's exactly what this group program is designed for. It's a safe space for women to connect, share their stories, and support each other. We meet weekly for 8 weeks, and each session focuses on a different theme related to [program topic]. It's not just about talking, though. We also do fun activities together to foster deeper connections."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Karen, does this sound like the kind of community you're looking for? Fantastic! Let me tell you more about the program details and how you can join us."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'm ready to commit to something like this."
"I understand. It can be scary to try something new. What feels scary about it? Is it the time commitment, the group setting, or something else? Maybe I can address your concerns."
"I don't have a lot of extra money right now."
"I hear you. Many of my clients are on a budget. Think of this as an investment in your well-being. What if, by the end of this program, you felt more confident, more connected, and more empowered to create the life you want? What would that be worth to you?"
"I'm worried I won't fit in."
"That's a common fear, Karen. But I carefully curate the group to ensure everyone feels welcome and supported. I also facilitate activities designed to help people connect quickly. I'm confident you'll find your place."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan of two installments. The first is due upon registration, and the second is due halfway through the program. Does that help make it more manageable?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are some key elements to include in your discovery call to effectively attract participants to your group program?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problems, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you handle the objection that the program is "too expensive" when speaking with a potential participant?

Reframe the cost as an investment in their well-being and highlight the long-term benefits they will receive. You can also offer payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a powerful way to scale your impact and income.
  • Effective discovery calls are crucial for converting prospects into paying participants.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence is key to overcoming sales hurdles.
  • Clearly communicate the value and benefits of your program to justify the price.
