Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Linda, thanks for joining me today! I enjoyed seeing you at the workshop. What resonated most with you?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear. So, what made you want to explore this further with a call? What's been going on for you lately?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about your stress levels. What does a typical day look like, and where do you feel the most pressure?"
YOU:
"And what have you already tried to manage your stress? What has worked, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in six months? And if you could change one thing about your current situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Linda, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing significant burnout and are seeking a sustainable solution. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on addressing the root causes of stress through equine therapy. We'll work together over 10 weeks to identify triggers, develop coping mechanisms, and build resilience. Most clients report feeling calmer and more in control within the first few weeks, with improved sleep and overall well-being."
YOU:
"A 2022 study in the *Journal of Equine Assisted Therapies* found that participants in a 10-week equine therapy program experienced a 35% reduction in cortisol levels, a key indicator of stress."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Linda, based on everything we've discussed, do you feel like this could be a good fit for you? ... Fantastic! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if equine therapy is right for me."
"That's a valid concern. Many people are new to this. I can share a case study about a 48-year-old teacher who felt the same way initially. After the 10-week program, she reported a 60% decrease in anxiety symptoms. Would you be open to hearing more about her experience?"
"I'm too busy to commit to 10 weeks."
"I understand. Time is precious. However, think of this as an investment in your long-term well-being. We can tailor the sessions to fit your schedule, and many clients find that the benefits they gain in stress reduction and productivity actually *create* more time in their lives. A 2023 study in *Frontiers in Psychology* showed that individuals who prioritize self-care activities report a 25% increase in overall productivity."
"I need to discuss it with my family."
"Of course. I can provide you with a detailed program overview to share with them. Would it be helpful to schedule a brief call where I can answer any questions they might have? Or, I can email you a detailed brochure."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (as everyone's journey is unique), I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the tools and support you need to succeed. My program is based on proven techniques and tailored to your individual needs. Most of my clients experience significant improvements in their stress levels and overall well-being. I am confident you will too, if you are committed to the process."

    Introduction to Discovery Calls

    The discovery call is a crucial step in converting potential clients into paying clients. It's your opportunity to understand their needs, showcase the value of your program, and build a strong relationship. A well-executed discovery call can significantly increase your conversion rates and boost your practice's income.

    This lesson will provide you with a proven framework for conducting effective discovery calls, addressing common objections, and closing the sale with confidence.

    The Four Phases of an Effective Discovery Call

    1. Build Rapport: Create a warm and welcoming environment.
    2. Understand the Problem: Uncover their challenges and goals.
    3. Present Your Approach: Showcase how your program can help.
    4. Close: Guide them toward the next step.

    Building Rapport

    The first few minutes of the call are critical for establishing trust and creating a connection. Here are some tips:

    • Start with a warm greeting: "Hi [Client Name], thank you for taking the time to speak with me today."
    • Express gratitude: "I appreciate you reaching out to learn more about our equine therapy program."
    • Find common ground: "I noticed on your intake form that you're also passionate about animal welfare. That's wonderful!"
    • Active Listening: Pay close attention to what the client is saying, both verbally and nonverbally.

    Understanding the Problem

    Once you've established rapport, it's time to delve into the client's challenges and goals. Ask open-ended questions like:

    • "What are some of the biggest stressors you're currently facing?"
    • "What have you tried in the past to manage your stress levels?"
    • "What are your goals for participating in equine therapy?"
    • "How would you know if this program was successful for you?"

    Listen carefully to their answers and take notes. This information will help you tailor your presentation to their specific needs.

    Case Study: Sarah, 35-Year-Old Accountant

    Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reported feeling overwhelmed, anxious, and disconnected from herself. She had difficulty sleeping and experienced frequent panic attacks.

    Intervention: After understanding Sarah's challenges, the therapist highlighted specific aspects of the equine therapy program that addressed her needs, such as mindfulness exercises with the horses and activities designed to promote emotional regulation.

    Outcomes: After 10 weeks, Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety and panic attacks. She also developed a stronger sense of self-awareness and improved her ability to manage stress.

    Presenting Your Approach

    Now that you understand the client's needs, it's time to showcase how your program can help. Highlight the unique benefits of equine therapy and how it addresses their specific challenges. For example:

    • "Our program combines the healing power of horses with evidence-based therapeutic techniques to help you reduce stress, improve emotional regulation, and build resilience."
    • "The horses provide a unique opportunity for non-judgmental connection and emotional exploration."
    • "We tailor each session to your individual needs and goals, ensuring that you get the most out of your experience."

    Use specific examples and success stories to illustrate the transformative potential of your program.
