Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to create a plan that works for you. Think of it this way: investing in your health now will actually *create* more time and energy in the long run."
"I need to think about it and compare other options."
"Of course. What other options are you considering? Perhaps I can help clarify how my program is different. I am confident that my unique approach with equine therapy and personalized support can provide the results you‚Äôre looking for. What specific information would help you make a confident decision?"
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not seeing progress within the first month, we can discuss adjustments or a partial refund. I'm committed to your success."
"It seems like a lot of money."
"I understand that it's an investment. However, consider the cost of *not* addressing these issues. What is it costing you in terms of your energy, your relationships, and your overall quality of life? My program is designed to provide lasting results, which will save you money in the long run on things like doctor visits and unnecessary supplements."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: 1) Build Rapport & Set Agenda, 2) Deep Dive into the Problem, 3) Present Your Solution, and 4) Close & Next Steps.

2. Why is it important to address a client's objections during a discovery call?

Addressing objections shows that you understand the client's concerns and are confident in your ability to help them. It also builds trust and increases the likelihood of them moving forward with your program.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Building rapport and understanding the client's needs are crucial for a successful call.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can overcome barriers to enrollment.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and payment options empowers clients to make informed decisions.
  • Consistent practice and a positive mindset are key to mastering client acquisition.
