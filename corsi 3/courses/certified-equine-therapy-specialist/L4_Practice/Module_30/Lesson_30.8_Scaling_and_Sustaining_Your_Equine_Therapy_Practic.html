Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Intro 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Sarah, and I'm passionate about helping people like you find relief from stress. To start, tell me a little about what led you to reach out."
YOU:
"I see you mentioned seeing my ad. What specifically caught your eye about equine-assisted therapy?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling burned out. Can you describe what that feels like for you on a daily basis?"
YOU:
"How long have you been feeling this way, and what have you tried to manage it?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the most stressed you've ever been, where would you rate yourself right now? And what number would you *like* to be at?"
YOU:
"If you continue down this path, what do you see happening in the next 6 months? What's at stake for you if you *don't* address this?"
Phase 3: Present Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're dealing with significant stress and burnout that are impacting your daily life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"Equine-assisted therapy is a powerful way to address this. The horses act as biofeedback, helping you become more aware of your emotions and energy. We'll work together over 8 weeks, combining sessions with the horses with mindfulness techniques and practical strategies for managing stress in your daily life. I've seen clients reduce their stress levels by 40-50% within the first month. A 2021 study in the *Journal of Equine Assisted Activities* showed a significant reduction in cortisol levels after just four sessions (Smith et al., 2021)."
YOU:
"My approach is unique because it's not just about riding horses. It's about building a connection, learning to regulate your nervous system, and developing sustainable coping mechanisms."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the program and how we can get started."

Mastering the Discovery Call

Optimizing Your Online Presence

In today's digital age, your online presence is crucial for attracting clients. Here's how to optimize it:

  • Professional Website: Ensure your website is user-friendly, mobile-responsive, and clearly communicates your services and expertise.
  • Social Media: Use platforms like LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram to share valuable content, engage with potential clients, and build your brand.
  • Online Directories: List your practice on relevant online directories to increase visibility.
  • Search Engine Optimization (SEO): Optimize your website and content for search engines to improve your ranking in search results.

A 2022 study by HubSpot found that companies with active blogs generate 67% more leads per month than those without (HubSpot, 2022).

Ethical Considerations in Marketing

It's essential to maintain ethical standards in all your marketing efforts. This includes:

  • Transparency: Be honest and upfront about your qualifications, services, and pricing.
  • Confidentiality: Protect client confidentiality in all your marketing materials.
  • Avoiding Misleading Claims: Do not make exaggerated or unsubstantiated claims about the effectiveness of your services.
  • Respecting Boundaries: Avoid spamming or using aggressive marketing tactics.

The American Counseling Association (ACA) Code of Ethics provides detailed guidelines on ethical marketing practices for counselors (ACA, 2014).

Building Referral Networks

Referrals are a powerful source of new clients. Here's how to build strong referral networks:

  • Network with Other Professionals: Connect with therapists, doctors, and other healthcare providers in your community.
  • Provide Excellent Service: Word-of-mouth referrals are more likely when clients are satisfied with your services.
  • Ask for Referrals: Don't be afraid to ask satisfied clients and colleagues for referrals.
  • Offer Incentives: Consider offering a small discount or bonus to clients who refer new clients to your practice.

A 2023 survey by Software Advice found that 71% of consumers are more likely to use a business if it was recommended by a friend or family member (Software Advice, 2023).

Tracking Your Marketing Efforts

It's essential to track your marketing efforts to determine what's working and what's not. This includes:

  • Website Analytics: Use tools like Google Analytics to track website traffic, bounce rate, and conversion rates.
  • Social Media Analytics: Monitor your social media engagement and reach.
  • Client Surveys: Ask new clients how they heard about your practice.
  • Return on Investment (ROI): Calculate the ROI of your marketing campaigns to determine their effectiveness.

By tracking your marketing efforts, you can optimize your strategies and maximize your return on investment.
