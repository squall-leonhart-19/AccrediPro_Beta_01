Her Question

The Pricing Dilemma

Linda's Client Pricing Concern

What Linda Tells You:

"I offered a discounted rate to a client, Sarah, 52, because she mentioned financial constraints. Now, after three sessions, I realize the work is more complex than I anticipated, and I feel undervalued. I'm dreading our next session."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Linda clearly define the scope of the discounted rate?
  • Did she assess Sarah's actual financial situation or just take her word?
  • Is Linda experiencing 'compassion fatigue' due to the discounted rate?
  • How can Linda re-negotiate or set future boundaries?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Key Points to Cover with Linda

1

Validate Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel resentful when you feel undervalued. Many practitioners experience this, especially early on."

2

Discuss Value Proposition

Help Linda articulate the value she brings to clients. What transformations does she facilitate? How does her work improve lives?

3

Teach Boundary Setting

Explain the importance of clear boundaries in maintaining a sustainable practice. This includes pricing, session length, and communication expectations.

4

Role-Play a Conversation

Practice how Linda can communicate with Sarah about adjusting the rate or setting clearer expectations for future sessions.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Courage
"Linda, it takes courage to admit you're feeling resentful. That honesty is the first step to resolving this."
Explore Underlying Beliefs
"What beliefs do you have about money and worthiness? Do you believe you deserve to be paid fairly for your skills?"
Offer Practical Strategies
"Here's a script you can adapt: 'Sarah, I've realized the scope of our work requires a higher investment. Moving forward, my rate will be X. How does that feel for you?'"
Empower Self-Advocacy
"Remember, setting boundaries isn't selfish ‚Äì it's self-respectful. It allows you to serve your clients with energy and enthusiasm."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the first thing you should do when a mentee expresses self-doubt?

Validate their feelings and normalize the experience. Let them know that self-doubt is common, especially when starting out.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with setting boundaries?

Provide specific scripts and role-play scenarios. Help them practice how to assert their boundaries in a professional and respectful manner.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves validating feelings, teaching principles, and empowering action.
  • Pricing issues often stem from underlying beliefs about worthiness and value.
  • Boundary setting is essential for a sustainable and fulfilling practice.
  • Role-playing difficult conversations can build confidence and competence.
  • Your experience and wisdom are invaluable to emerging practitioners.
