Identifying Common Ethical Dilemmas

Supervisors in equine therapy often encounter situations that require careful ethical consideration. Recognizing these dilemmas is the first step towards responsible conduct. Some common ethical challenges include:

  • Dual Relationships: Occur when a supervisor has more than one relationship (e.g., personal, business) with a supervisee, potentially compromising objectivity and professional boundaries.
  • Confidentiality Breaches: Involve the unauthorized disclosure of client information shared during therapy or supervision sessions.
  • Scope of Practice Issues: Arise when a supervisee practices beyond their level of competence or training, potentially harming clients.
  • Conflicts of Interest: Situations where a supervisor's personal or professional interests could compromise their judgment or objectivity in supervising a supervisee.
  • Informed Consent: Ensuring clients fully understand the nature of the therapy, potential risks, and their rights before participating.

Applying Ethical Decision-Making Models

When faced with an ethical dilemma, a structured approach can help you make sound decisions. Several ethicaldecision-making models exist, such as the "Ethical Decision-Making Model for Helping Professionals" by Corey, Corey, and Callanan (2019). This model involves:

  1. Identifying the problem or dilemma.
  2. Identifying potential issues involved.
  3. Reviewing relevant ethical codes.
  4. Knowing applicable laws and regulations.
  5. Obtaining consultation.
  6. Considering possible and probable courses of action.
  7. Enumerating the consequences of various decisions.
  8. Deciding on the best course of action.

Another useful model is the "Social Constructivist Model", emphasizing the importance of cultural and contextual factors in ethical decision-making. This approach encourages collaboration and dialogue with stakeholders to find mutually agreeable solutions.

Consulting with Colleagues and Experts

Seeking consultation from experienced colleagues, ethics experts, or legal professionals is crucial when navigating complex ethical dilemmas. Consultation provides:

  • Different perspectives on the situation.
  • Validation of your reasoning and decision-making process.
  • Information about relevant ethical codes, laws, and regulations.
  • Support and guidance during a challenging time.

Remember, seeking consultation is a sign of ethical strength, not weakness. Document all consultations, including the date, individuals involved, and key points discussed.

Promoting Ethical Awareness

As a supervisor, you play a vital role in fostering ethical awareness among your supervisees. Strategies for promoting ethical conduct include:

  • Regular Discussions: Dedicate time during supervision sessions to discuss ethical issues, dilemmas, and best practices.
  • Role-Playing: Use role-playing exercises to simulate ethical scenarios and help supervisees practice their decision-making skills.
  • Case Studies: Analyze real-world case studies to identify ethical considerations and potential courses of action.
  • Ethical Audits: Conduct periodic ethical audits of your supervisees' practices to identify areas for improvement.
  • Modeling Ethical Behavior: Demonstrate ethical conduct in your own actions and interactions with supervisees, clients, and colleagues.

A 2021 study in the "Journal of Interprofessional Education & Practice" found that regular ethics training and supervision significantly improved ethical decision-making skills among healthcare professionals (p < 0.01).

Reviewing Ethical Codes and Guidelines

Familiarize yourself and your supervisees with relevant ethical codes and guidelines, such as those provided by the American Hippotherapy Association (AHA), the Professional Association of Therapeutic Horsemanship International (PATH Intl.), and other relevant professional organizations. These codes outline ethical principles, standards of conduct, and best practices for equine therapy practitioners. Regularly review these codes to ensure your practices align with current ethical standards.

Key areas covered in ethical codes often include:

  • Confidentiality
  • Informed consent
  • Competence
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Dual relationships
  • Advertising and solicitation

Case Study: Navigating a Complex Situation

Case Study: Sarah's Dilemma

Client: Sarah, a 16-year-old with cerebral palsy, has been participating in equine therapy for six months. Her therapist, Emily, notices that Sarah's mother, Jane, is becoming increasingly reliant on Emily for emotional support, often confiding in her about personal issues unrelated to Sarah's therapy. Emily is also Sarah's soccer coach outside of therapy sessions.

Dilemma: Emily recognizes she is entering into a dual relationship with both Sarah and Jane, potentially compromising her objectivity and professional boundaries. Jane's reliance on Emily for emotional support could also blur the lines between therapist and friend.

Intervention: Emily consults with her supervisor, David, to discuss the situation. David advises Emily to:

  1. Acknowledge the dual relationship with Jane and its potential impact on Sarah's therapy.
  2. Gently but firmly set boundaries with Jane, explaining the importance of maintaining a professional therapeutic relationship.
  3. Suggest alternative sources of emotional support for Jane, such as a support group or individual counseling.
  4. Refer Sarah to another therapist if the dual relationship continues to compromise Emily's objectivity.
  5. Discuss the ethical concerns with Sarah, ensuring she feels comfortable and safe in the therapeutic environment.

Outcome: Emily successfully navigated the dual relationship by setting clear boundaries with Jane and prioritizing Sarah's well-being. Jane initially expressed disappointment but ultimately respected Emily's professional boundaries. Sarah continued to benefit from equine therapy, and Emily learned valuable lessons about maintaining ethical boundaries in her practice. Emily also stopped coaching Sarah's soccer team to avoid further complications. The R.E.I.N.S. Frameworkâ„¢ helped Emily and David re-establish a strong therapeutic Relationship focused on Sarah's needs, allowing for Emotional Exploration within appropriate boundaries, and reinforcing Intentional Interactions that supported Sarah's goals.
