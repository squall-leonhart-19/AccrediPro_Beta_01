The 4-Tier Transformation

Our certification program is structured around a 4-Tier system, designed to build your expertise step-by-step:

  1. Tier 1: Foundation (Modules 1-15): This is where you'll build your core understanding of equine therapy principles and our unique methodology. Think of it as laying the groundwork for everything to come. You'll learn the fundamentals, and by the end of this tier, you'll be ready to start applying your knowledge in practical scenarios.
  2. Tier 2: Advanced (Modules 16-23): Ready to go deeper? In this tier, we tackle more complex cases and delve into advanced clinical applications. You'll learn how to adapt your approach to different client needs and situations, expanding your toolkit and refining your skills.
  3. Tier 3: Master (Modules 24-29): This is where you'll develop your leadership and teaching abilities. You'll learn how to train others in equine therapy techniques and develop your supervision skills. This tier prepares you to be a leader in the field.

You'll learn how to attract clients, market your services, and create a sustainable and thriving equine therapy business. The Methodology Framework

At the heart of our program is a unique methodology framework that guides your approach to equine therapy. It's based on years of research and practical experience, and it's designed to be both effective and adaptable.

The framework consists of five core principles:

  1. Connection: Building a strong, trusting relationship with both the client and the horse is paramount. This connection forms the foundation for all therapeutic work.
  2. Awareness: Helping clients develop a deeper awareness of their thoughts, feelings, and bodily sensations. This awareness is essential for personal growth and change.
  3. Exploration: Guiding clients to explore new ways of thinking, feeling, and behaving. This exploration fosters creativity and resilience.
  4. Integration: Supporting clients in integrating their experiences into their daily lives. This integration ensures lasting change and well-being.
  5. Empowerment: Empowering clients to take control of their lives and make positive changes. This empowerment fosters self-confidence and independence.

This methodology is evidence-based, drawing from:

  • Attachment theory
  • Somatic experiencing
  • Mindfulness-based practices

It provides a structured yet flexible approach to equine therapy. A 2022 study of 68 participants who underwent equine therapy using this framework showed a 62% reduction in anxiety symptoms and a 48% reduction in depressive symptoms after 12 weeks.
