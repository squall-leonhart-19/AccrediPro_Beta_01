What You Can and Cannot Do

As an Equine Therapy Specialistâ„¢, it's crucial to understand your scope of practice. Think of it as your professional lane. We empower clients through education and guidance but always remember: we educate, we don't diagnose or treat medical conditions.

You'll be providing valuable coaching in areas like stress reduction, emotional regulation, and building confidence using equine-assisted activities. Your expertise lies in facilitating experiences with horses that promote well-being.

What you CAN do:

  • Provide education on equine-assisted activities and their potential benefits.
  • Guide clients through exercises and activities designed to improve emotional and mental well-being.
  • Offer support and encouragement to clients as they work towards their goals.
  • Collaborate with other healthcare professionals to provide holistic care.

What you CANNOT do:

  • Diagnose or treat any medical or mental health condition.
  • Prescribe medication or other medical treatments.
  • Provide therapy or counseling services unless you are also a licensed therapist.
  • Make claims that equine-assisted activities can cure or treat specific diseases.

Remember, you are working alongside medical professionals, not replacing them. This collaboration is key to providing comprehensive support to your clients.

Legal Considerations

Health coaching is generally legal, but it's your responsibility to understand the regulations in your specific state or country.

Key Legal Considerations:

  • Insurance: Look into professional liability insurance. It protects you in case of unforeseen circumstances.
  • Liability: Ensure you have proper waivers and consent forms for clients participating in equine activities.
  • Staying in Your Lane: Always operate within the boundaries of your certification and avoid practicing medicine without a license.

Consult with a legal professional in your area to ensure you are compliant with all applicable laws and regulations. This is an investment in the longevity and integrity of your practice.

Ethics & Professionalism

Your reputation is built on trust and ethical behavior. Here are some key principles to guide you:

  • Confidentiality: Protect your clients' privacy. Never share their information without their explicit consent.
  • Boundaries: Maintain professional boundaries. Avoid dual relationships that could compromise your objectivity.
  • Honesty: Be truthful in your marketing and communication. Don't make exaggerated claims about the benefits of equine-assisted activities.
  • Respect: Treat all clients with respect and dignity, regardless of their background or beliefs.

Upholding these ethical standards will not only protect you but will also enhance your credibility and build strong relationships with your clients.
