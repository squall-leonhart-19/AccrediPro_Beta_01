Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to admit you're feeling stuck, especially when you have a stable career. I want to acknowledge that first."
MARIA:
"Thank you. It feels silly to complain when I have a good job, but I can't shake this feeling that I'm meant to do something more... something with horses."
YOU:
"It's not silly at all, Maria. That feeling is your intuition guiding you. Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s and 50s, experience this same pull towards a more fulfilling path. And your lifelong connection with horses? That's a powerful asset."
YOU:
"Let's explore what's holding you back. You mentioned fear of failure and financial concerns. Are those the biggest roadblocks right now?"
MARIA:
"Yes, definitely. I'm afraid of leaving a secure job and not being able to make a living. And I don't even know where to begin with equine therapy... it seems so overwhelming."
YOU:
"I understand. It's normal to feel overwhelmed when facing a big change. Let's break it down into smaller, manageable steps. We can start by clarifying your vision for your ideal equine therapy practice, and then identify the skills and resources you'll need to get there. Does that sound like a good approach?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Maria's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating her feelings builds trust and rapport, creating a safe space for her to share her fears and vulnerabilities. This makes her more receptive to coaching.

2. What are some specific questions you could ask to help Maria clarify her vision for her ideal equine therapy practice?

You could ask: "What type of clients do you envision working with?" "What kind of setting would you prefer (e.g., private farm, therapeutic riding center)?" "What are your core values and how do you want to incorporate them into your practice?"

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize building rapport and validating the client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Help the client identify and address their underlying fears and limiting beliefs.
  • Break down overwhelming goals into smaller, manageable steps.
  • Encourage the client to envision their ideal future and connect with their passion.
  • Be a supportive and empathetic guide, instilling hope and confidence in the client's ability to achieve their dreams.
