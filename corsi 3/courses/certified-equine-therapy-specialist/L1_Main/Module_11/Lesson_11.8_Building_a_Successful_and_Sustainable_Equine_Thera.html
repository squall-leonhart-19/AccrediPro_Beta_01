Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on equine behavior. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing your experience with Comet. It's very common for riders to feel disconnected, especially when re-entering the equestrian world. It's not about being a 'bad' rider; it's often about understanding equine communication and how horses perceive the world."
DIANE:
"That's a relief to hear. I thought I'd lost my touch. He just doesn't seem to like me sometimes."
YOU:
"Horses communicate very differently than we do. They rely heavily on body language, energy, and consistency. Let's explore Comet's behavior from his perspective. For example, when he pins his ears, what's happening in the environment, and what were you doing right before that?"
DIANE:
"Usually, it's when I'm asking him to move over in the stall. I might be a little impatient."
YOU:
"That's valuable information. Comet might be interpreting your impatience as pressure or even a threat. Remember, horses are prey animals; their instinct is to react to perceived danger. Let's explore some techniques to communicate your request in a way that feels safer and clearer to him."
YOU:
"We can also consider Comet's herd dynamics. Does he have a dominant personality or a more submissive one? Understanding his place within a group can help us understand his reactions to you."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to consider a horse's perspective as a prey animal in equine therapy?

Understanding that horses are prey animals helps therapists recognize that their behaviors are often rooted in self-preservation and fear responses. This allows for more compassionate and effective communication.

2. What's a good first step when a client reports feeling disconnected from their horse?

A good first step is to validate their feelings and assure them that this is a common experience. Then, shift the focus to understanding equine communication and behavior patterns.

  • Equine communication is fundamentally different from human communication.
