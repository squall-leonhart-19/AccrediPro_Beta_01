YOU:

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating feelings builds trust and rapport. It shows the client that you understand and care about their experience, making them more receptive to your guidance.

2. How can you assess a client's experience level without making assumptions?

Ask open-ended questions such as, "Tell me about your previous experience with horses" or "What kind of training methods have you used in the past?" This allows the client to share their knowledge and skills without feeling judged.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize building rapport and trust during the initial assessment.
  • Validate the client's feelings and acknowledge the horse's potential trauma.
  • Use open-ended questions to gather information and assess the client's experience level.
  • Avoid technical jargon and use simple, easy-to-understand language.
  • Emphasize the importance of understanding the horse's perspective and needs.
