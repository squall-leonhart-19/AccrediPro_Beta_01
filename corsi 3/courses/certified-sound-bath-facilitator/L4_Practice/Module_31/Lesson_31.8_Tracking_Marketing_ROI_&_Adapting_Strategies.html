Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"That's perfectly fine! No prior experience is necessary. I guide you every step of the way, and the group setting provides a supportive environment. It's a gentle and accessible way to explore the power of sound."
"I need to talk to my family about it."

"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can share that many of my clients experience significant positive changes. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee: if you're not happy after the first two sessions, I'll gladly refund your money. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing gratitude for their time, finding common ground, and genuinely inquiring about their journey are crucial for establishing a strong connection.

2. How can you confidently address the "It's too expensive" objection?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment in terms of the long-term value and benefits they'll receive, such as improved wellbeing and reduced stress.

Key Takeaways

  • Master the Discovery Call: Structure your calls to build rapport, uncover needs, present solutions, and close confidently.
  • Handle Objections with Grace: Prepare for common objections and craft thoughtful, reassuring responses.
  • Price with Confidence: Know your worth and communicate the value of your services clearly.
  • Close the Deal: Practice your closing lines and make it easy for clients to say "Yes!"
