Client Agreement Review

Explaining Your Agreement (10 Minutes)

Phase 1: Introduction & Reassurance 2 min
YOU:
"Hi Brenda, thanks again for reaching out. I understand you have some concerns about confidentiality, and I want to assure you that your privacy is my top priority. Let's walk through my client agreement together."
YOU:
"This agreement outlines our professional relationship and ensures we're both on the same page regarding confidentiality, payment, cancellations, and scope of practice."
Phase 2: Confidentiality Clause 4 min
YOU:
"Section 3 specifically addresses confidentiality. It states that all information you share with me during our sessions will be kept strictly confidential. I will not disclose any information to third parties without your explicit consent, except as required by law."
YOU:
"This means that unless I am legally obligated to report something (like harm to yourself or others), everything you tell me stays between us. I take this very seriously."
Phase 3: Scope of Practice & Disclaimer 4 min
YOU:
"It's also important to understand the scope of my practice. As a sound bath facilitator, I am not a licensed therapist or medical professional. Sound baths are a complementary wellness practice and are not intended to diagnose or treat any medical conditions. Section 5 outlines this clearly."
YOU:
"This agreement also includes a disclaimer stating that you are responsible for consulting with your healthcare provider for any medical concerns. Sound baths are a wonderful tool for relaxation and stress reduction, but they are not a substitute for medical treatment."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a client agreement for a sound bath practitioner?

A client agreement should include clauses on confidentiality, scope of practice, cancellation policy, payment terms, and a disclaimer stating that sound baths are not a substitute for medical treatment.

2. How should you respond to a client who is hesitant to sign your client agreement?

Acknowledge their concerns, patiently explain each section of the agreement, and offer reassurance about your commitment to their well-being and privacy. Provide references if necessary.

3. Why is it important to carry professional liability insurance as a sound bath practitioner?

Professional liability insurance protects you and your clients in case of claims of negligence or malpractice. It demonstrates your commitment to professionalism and client safety.

Key Takeaways

  • Client Agreements: A well-drafted client agreement is the foundation of a legally sound practice.
  • Confidentiality: Uphold strict confidentiality to build trust with your clients.
  • Scope of Practice: Clearly define the boundaries of your practice to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Insurance: Carry professional liability insurance for added protection.
  • Ethical Conduct: Adhere to a code of ethics to ensure responsible and professional practice.
