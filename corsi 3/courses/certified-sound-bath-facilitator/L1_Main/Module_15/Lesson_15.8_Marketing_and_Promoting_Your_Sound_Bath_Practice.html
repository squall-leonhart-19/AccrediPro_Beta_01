Your Coaching Script

Practice using these exact words to start the conversation. Remember to project warmth and understanding:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of this with me. It sounds like you're experiencing a lot right now. Many people find themselves in similar situations, feeling the weight of new responsibilities and life changes."
MARIA:
"Yes, exactly! It's like I can't turn my mind off, especially at night."
YOU:
"I understand. Before we dive deeper, I want to assure you that what you're feeling is valid and manageable. We can work together to find techniques that will help you relax and improve your sleep."
YOU:
"Based on what you've told me, I suggest we explore how sound baths can help. They're known for promoting relaxation, reducing anxiety, and improving sleep quality. Does that sound like a good direction for you?"
MARIA:
"Yes, I've heard good things about sound baths. I'm willing to try anything at this point."
YOU:
"Great! Let's start by discussing what a typical sound bath session involves and how it can address your specific concerns. We can also talk about setting up a series of sessions to help you achieve your goals. How does that sound?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief: "I feel like I'm finally talking to someone who understands."
  • She shows interest: "Tell me more about how sound baths work."
  • Her body language softens: relaxed posture, open expression.
  • She asks about scheduling: "How soon can I book a session?"
  • She expresses hope: "I'm excited to see if this can help me."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to do before suggesting a sound bath session?

The most important thing is to validate the client's feelings and show empathy. Make sure they feel heard and understood before suggesting any solutions.

2. What should you do if the client is skeptical about sound baths?

Acknowledge their skepticism and provide a brief, simple explanation of how sound baths can help, focusing on relaxation and stress reduction. Offer to answer any questions they may have.
