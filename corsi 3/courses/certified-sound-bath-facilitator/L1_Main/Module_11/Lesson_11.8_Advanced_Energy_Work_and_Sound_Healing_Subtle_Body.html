Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you've been going through a lot, and it's completely understandable that you're feeling overwhelmed and anxious. Many women find themselves in similar situations, especially during significant life transitions."
MARIA:
"Yes, it feels like my whole world has been turned upside down. I just can't seem to relax."
YOU:
"I understand. And I want you to know that you're not alone, and there are ways to find that sense of calm again. We can explore some sound-based practices that can help you relax, ease your anxiety, and improve your sleep."
YOU:
"Before we get started, I want to ask you: on a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being 'not at all open' and 10 being 'completely open,' how open are you to trying new approaches to manage your stress and anxiety?"
MARIA:
"I'd say I'm about an 8. I'm willing to try anything that might help."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. With that openness, I think we can make some real progress. Let's start with a gentle sound bath experience designed to calm your nervous system. How does that sound?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief: "It's good to know I'm not alone."
  • She seems more relaxed and open to the process.
  • Her body language softens (e.g., shoulders relax, facial tension eases).
  • She asks questions about the sound bath experience.
  • She expresses hope: "I'm really hoping this will help me sleep better."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to do before offering a sound bath to a client experiencing anxiety?

The most important thing is to validate their feelings and acknowledge their experience. This builds trust and makes them more receptive to the intervention.

2. Why is it important to ask a client about their openness to trying new approaches?

Asking about their openness helps gauge their willingness to engage with the process and allows you to tailor your approach accordingly. It also avoids pushing them into something they're not comfortable with.

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate a client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Keep your language simple and avoid technical jargon.
  • Gauge the client's openness to new experiences.
  • Get the client's buy-in before starting a sound bath.
  • Pay attention to the client's body language for cues on how they're responding.
