Husband, David, has admitted to a past affair (5 years prior) and current pornography use. Eleanor feels betrayed and is questioning the marriage. They have two teenage children.
Faith Background
Both are active members of a conservative Christian church. Divorce is strongly discouraged within their community.
Individual History
Eleanor: History of anxiety, perfectionistic tendencies. David: Difficulty expressing emotions, workaholic.
Coaching Goal
Initially, to "save the marriage." Eleanor now unsure. David wants forgiveness and restoration.
Complicating Factors
Eleanor also volunteers at your church and is on the leadership team. You have known the couple for 10 years.
Legal Concerns
David discloses he had a brief affair with a minor 15 years ago, before they were married. No charges were ever filed.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making

Step 1: Identify Ethical Dilemmas
Multiple dilemmas: Dual relationship (church connection), conflicting values (Eleanor's uncertainty vs. church stance), mandatory reporting (past affair with a minor), confidentiality (protecting David while ensuring Eleanor's safety), and competence (managing trauma related to betrayal).
Step 2: Consult Ethical Guidelines
Review your coaching code of ethics, focusing on confidentiality, boundaries, competence, and legal obligations. Seek guidance from a supervisor or ethics board.
Step 3: Consider Stakeholders
Identify all stakeholders: Eleanor, David, their children, the church community, and yourself. Weigh the potential impact of each decision on each stakeholder.
Step 4: Generate Potential Courses of Action
Options include: Referring the couple to another coach, focusing on individual counseling, reporting the past affair, setting strict boundaries, or terminating the coaching relationship.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Unresolved Trauma

Eleanor's anxiety and David's emotional avoidance may stem from deeper, unresolved trauma. Consider referring for trauma-informed therapy.

2

Co-dependency

Assess for co-dependent patterns in their relationship. Eleanor's perfectionism may contribute to a dynamic where David seeks external validation.

3

Spiritual Abuse

The pressure from the church to stay together may be experienced as spiritual abuse by Eleanor. Ensure she feels empowered to make her own decision.

4

Mental Health Concerns

Pornography addiction can be linked to underlying mental health issues. Assess David for depression, anxiety, or other disorders.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äî Immediate Referral Needed

  • Current child abuse or neglect: Mandatory reporting to child protective services.
  • Suicidal or homicidal ideation: Immediate mental health crisis intervention.
  • Severe mental health symptoms: Psychosis, severe depression, or mania.
  • Domestic violence: Ensure Eleanor's safety and refer to a domestic violence shelter.
  • Legal charges filed related to past affair: The coach is not a legal expert and must refer to legal counsel.

Case Study: Sarah, 52

Sarah, a 52-year-old woman, sought coaching after discovering her husband's secret gambling addiction. As a faith-based coach, you are aware that her church strongly discourages divorce, even in cases of financial infidelity. Sarah feels conflicted between her religious beliefs and her desire for financial security and peace of mind. After 6 sessions, it's clear Sarah is experiencing significant anxiety and symptoms of depression. You gently suggest Sarah seek guidance from a therapist or counselor specializing in addiction and trauma. You also emphasize Sarah's agency in making her own decision, regardless of the church's stance, and provide a list of resources for financial planning and legal advice, ensuring she has access to support outside of the faith community. This approach honors Sarah's autonomy while respecting her faith background.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key ethical considerations when a client discloses a past affair with a minor?

The coach must determine if there is a legal obligation to report the past abuse, even if no charges were filed. Consult with a legal professional and ethics board. Prioritize the safety and well-being of any potential victims.

2. How should a coach navigate a dual relationship with a client who is also a member of their church?

Disclose the dual relationship to the client and obtain informed consent. Set clear boundaries to avoid conflicts of interest. If the dual relationship compromises objectivity or client well-being, refer the client to another coach.

3. What steps can a coach take to ensure a client feels empowered to make their own decision regarding their marriage, despite pressure from their faith community?

Validate the client's feelings and experiences. Explore their values and goals without imposing your own beliefs. Provide resources for support outside of the faith community. Emphasize the client's autonomy and right to choose their own path.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex ethical dilemmas require careful consideration of all stakeholders and potential consequences.
  • Dual relationships can compromise objectivity and client well-being. Strict boundaries are essential.
  • Mandatory reporting laws must be followed, even if they conflict with client confidentiality.
  • Clients should be empowered to make informed decisions based on their own values, free from coercion.
  • Consultation with supervisors and ethics boards is crucial when navigating complex ethical situations.
