Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify Core Relational Patterns
Eleanor feels unsupported and resentful, while her husband withdraws and avoids conflict. This creates a negative feedback loop. Research suggests that demand-withdraw patterns are highly predictive of marital dissatisfaction (Schrodt et al., 2014).
Step 2: Assess Individual Factors
Eleanor's anxiety and perfectionism may contribute to her critical communication style. Her husband's difficulty with vulnerability hinders emotional intimacy. Assess for possible attachment styles. Secure attachment is positively correlated with marital satisfaction.
Step 3: Integrate Faith-Based Principles
Explore their shared faith values and how they can be used to foster forgiveness, empathy, and commitment. Consider biblical principles on communication, conflict resolution, and sacrificial love. A spiritual integration approach may enhance outcomes (Worthington et al., 2011).
Step 4: Prioritize Interventions Based on Evidence
Begin with communication skills training, focusing on active listening, empathy, and non-violent communication. Address underlying emotional needs and attachment insecurities. Incorporate faith-based practices such as prayer, scripture reading, and service to others.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Underlying Mental Health Issues

Assess for depression, anxiety disorders, or trauma history in either partner. These conditions can significantly impact relationship dynamics.

2

Addiction or Infidelity

Explore the possibility of substance abuse, pornography use, or extramarital affairs. These issues require specialized intervention.

3

Unresolved Grief or Loss

Determine if either partner is struggling with unresolved grief related to past losses, which may be affecting their ability to connect emotionally.

4

Communication Disorders

Consider undiagnosed ADHD or Autism Spectrum Disorder which can impact communication skills and cause misunderstanding and frustration.

Phased Intervention Plan

üìÖ Phase 1: Assessment & Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
  • Comprehensive relationship assessment: Administer the ENRICH Marital Satisfaction Scale and Prepare/Enrich Assessment.
  • Individual sessions: Explore individual histories, attachment styles, and emotional needs.
  • Establish clear coaching goals and expectations.
  • Introduce basic communication skills: Active listening, empathy, "I" statements.
üìÖ Phase 2: Skill Building & Spiritual Integration (Weeks 5-8)
  • Conflict resolution training: Teach constructive conflict management techniques.
  • Emotional regulation skills: Help partners identify and manage their emotions effectively.
  • Spiritual integration: Explore biblical principles on forgiveness, grace, and unconditional love.
  • Implement shared faith practices: Prayer, scripture reading, attending church together.
üìÖ Phase 3: Deepening Intimacy & Maintaining Gains (Weeks 9-12)
  • Enhance emotional and physical intimacy: Encourage vulnerability, affection, and quality time.
  • Address specific relationship challenges: Finances, parenting, in-laws.
  • Develop a relapse prevention plan: Identify potential triggers and strategies for maintaining positive changes.
  • Follow-up sessions: Provide ongoing support and encouragement.

Case Study: Sarah, 45

Sarah, a 45-year-old stay-at-home mom, sought marriage coaching after 20 years of marriage. Her husband, a busy executive, worked long hours and was often emotionally unavailable. Sarah felt neglected and resentful. After implementing a 12-week coaching protocol focusing on communication skills, emotional regulation, and spiritual integration, Sarah and her husband reported significant improvements in their relationship. They began spending more quality time together, communicating more effectively, and expressing their love and appreciation for each other. Sarah even started a small online business, giving her a sense of purpose and fulfillment outside of her marriage. This highlights the potential for positive change even in long-term marriages.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a faith-based approach to marriage coaching, and how do they differ from secular approaches?

Faith-based approaches integrate spiritual principles, biblical teachings, and prayer into the coaching process. They emphasize forgiveness, grace, and unconditional love as essential elements of a healthy marriage, whereas secular approaches focus primarily on communication skills and behavior modification.

2. Based on the case study presented, what strategies can be used to address feelings of resentment and neglect in a long-term marriage?

Strategies include improving communication skills, increasing quality time together, expressing love and appreciation, and encouraging individual pursuits and personal growth. Addressing underlying emotional needs and attachment insecurities is also crucial.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex marriage cases require a comprehensive assessment of individual, relational, and spiritual factors.
  • Evidence-based interventions, such as communication skills training and emotional regulation techniques, should be integrated with faith-based principles.
  • Identifying potential referral triggers outside the scope of coaching is essential for client safety and ethical practice.
  • A phased protocol plan allows for gradual progress and addresses different aspects of the relationship over time.
  • The integration of faith and evidence can lead to meaningful and lasting change in marriages.
