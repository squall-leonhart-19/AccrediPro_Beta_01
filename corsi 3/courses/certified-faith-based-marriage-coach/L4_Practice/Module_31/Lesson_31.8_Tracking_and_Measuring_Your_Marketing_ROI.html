Handling Objections Like a Pro

Navigating Concerns with Grace

"I'm not sure if my husband will be open to this."
"I understand. It's not always easy to get our partners on board. How about this: I can provide you with a detailed program overview that you can share with him. It explains the key benefits and outlines the faith-based principles we'll be exploring. Would you like me to send that over?"
"I'm worried about the cost."
"I get it. Investing in your marriage is a big decision. Let me ask you this: what is the cost of *not* investing in your marriage? What would it be worth to you to feel truly connected, supported, and loved again? My program isn't just about the money; it's about investing in your future happiness and well-being. I also offer a payment plan option that makes it more accessible."
"I don't have the time."
"Time is precious, and I respect that. These coaching sessions are designed to be efficient and impactful, and we can schedule them at times that work best for both of you. Think of this as an investment in reclaiming your time and energy. When your marriage is thriving, you'll have more energy for all the other things that matter to you."
"I'm not sure if coaching is right for us."
"That's perfectly okay. Coaching isn't for everyone. But based on what you've shared, I truly believe it could be a powerful tool for you and your husband. It provides a safe, supportive space to explore your challenges, gain clarity, and develop practical strategies for growth. I also offer a complimentary introductory session so you can both experience the value of coaching firsthand. Would you be interested in scheduling that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in your discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's needs, and presenting your coaching approach.

2. How can you confidently address concerns about the cost of your program?

By emphasizing the value and potential ROI of investing in their marriage, and by offering payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering your sales pitch is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide valuable solutions.
  • Present your pricing with assurance and highlight the value of your program.
  • Believe in your ability to transform marriages and create a thriving practice.
