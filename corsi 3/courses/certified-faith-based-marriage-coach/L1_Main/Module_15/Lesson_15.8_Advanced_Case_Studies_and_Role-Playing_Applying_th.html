Meet Your Client

EM

Emily, 52

Elementary School Teacher, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Divorced, one adult child

Chief Complaint
Feeling disconnected from God, struggling with forgiveness after a painful divorce. "I don't know where to start."
Background
Active in church for 20+ years, now feels alienated and resentful.
Her Goal
"To find peace and reconnect with my faith. I want to forgive, but I don't know how."
Spiritual Gifts
Service, teaching, encouragement.

Your Coaching Script

Here's how to start this delicate conversation. Remember to use a compassionate and empathetic tone:

YOU:
"Emily, thank you for your vulnerability in sharing this with me. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings, especially after being so committed to your faith. I want you to know that God's grace is always available, even when we struggle."
EMILY:
"I want to believe that, but it's hard. I feel so much anger and resentment. How can I forgive when I'm still hurting?"
YOU:
"That's a valid question, and many people in your situation feel the same way. Forgiveness isn't about condoning what happened; it's about releasing the hold that anger has on your heart. It's a process, not an event. Are you willing to explore that process with me?"
EMILY:
"I think so. I don't want to live like this anymore. I just need some guidance."
YOU:
"I understand. Let's start by identifying the specific wounds that are preventing you from forgiving. We'll then explore biblical principles of forgiveness and find practical ways to apply them to your situation. Does that sound like a good approach?"
EMILY:
"Yes, that sounds helpful. I appreciate you being so understanding."
YOU:
"Of course, Emily. You're not alone in this. Let's begin with prayer and ask for God's guidance as we move forward."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Emily expresses relief and hope.
  • She acknowledges her willingness to work on forgiveness.
  • She identifies specific wounds to address.
  • She feels safe and understood.
  • She agrees to a follow-up session.

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to establish at the beginning of a coaching session like this?

Trust and safety. Emily needs to feel comfortable sharing her deepest feelings without judgment.

2. How can you help Emily use her spiritual gifts in the healing process?

Encourage her to use her gifts of service, teaching, or encouragement to help others, which can bring healing and purpose.

Key Takeaways

  • Compassion and empathy are essential when coaching clients through painful experiences.
  • Forgiveness is a process, not an event, and requires patience and guidance.
  • Prayer and biblical principles can provide comfort and direction.
  • Helping clients identify and use their spiritual gifts can aid in their healing journey.
