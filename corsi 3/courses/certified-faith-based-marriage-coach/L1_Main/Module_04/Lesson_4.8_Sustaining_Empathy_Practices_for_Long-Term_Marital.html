Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on empathy. Practice saying these words out loud, injecting your most compassionate tone:

YOU:
"Carol, thank you for being so open with me. I can hear the pain and disappointment in your voice. It takes incredible courage to share what you're going through, especially after such a long marriage. I want you to know that I'm here to listen without judgment, and we'll walk through this together."
CAROL:
"It's just... I thought I was doing the right thing by leaving. But now I feel so lost and alone. I don't even know who I am anymore."
YOU:
"That feeling of being 'lost' is completely understandable, Carol. After 25 years, your marriage was a huge part of your identity. It's natural to feel adrift as you redefine yourself. Many women I work with experience similar feelings, and you're not alone in this."
YOU:
"And it sounds like your faith is also being challenged right now. Is that fair to say?"
CAROL:
"Yes, absolutely. I don't understand how God could let my marriage fall apart. I prayed for so long. I feel abandoned."
YOU:
"Carol, it‚Äôs okay to feel angry and confused with God right now. Many people of faith struggle with similar questions when life throws them curveballs. Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

1

2

Use a warm, gentle tone. Slow down your pacing. Allow pauses for Carol to respond. Focus on your tone and body language.

4

Use your own words, but maintain the same level of validation and support. Practice until it feels natural and authentic.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is empathy especially important when coaching someone through a divorce?

Divorce is a deeply emotional experience involving grief, loss, and identity shifts. Empathy creates a safe space for the client to process these feelings without judgment.

2. What are some specific phrases you can use to demonstrate empathy in this scenario?

Examples include: "I can hear the pain in your voice," "It's understandable that you feel lost," "It takes courage to share what you're going through," and "I'm here to listen without judgment."
