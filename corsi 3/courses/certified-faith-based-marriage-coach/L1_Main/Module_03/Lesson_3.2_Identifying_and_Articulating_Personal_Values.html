Values Assessment Tools

Identifying values can be challenging. Many people haven't consciously considered what truly matters to them. Values assessment tools can be incredibly helpful in this process. These tools provide a structured framework for exploring and clarifying values.

Here are a few examples suitable for faith-based coaching:

  • Values Clarification Worksheet: A simple worksheet that prompts individuals to list and rank their most important values.
  • Values Card Sort: A set of cards, each with a different value, that individuals sort into categories based on importance.
  • Online Values Assessments: Several free or low-cost online assessments are available, such as the VIA Character Strengths Survey or the Personal Values Assessment. Be sure to review any assessment for alignment with your faith-based approach.

Encourage your clients to approach these tools with an open mind and a willingness to explore. Emphasize that there are no right or wrong answers. The goalis simply to gain clarity about what truly matters to them.

Self-Reflection Exercises

While values assessment tools provide a starting point, self-reflection exercises help individuals connect with their values on a deeper level. These exercises encourage introspection and exploration of personal experiences to uncover deeply held beliefs and principles.

Here are a few effective self-reflection exercises:

  • Life Story Reflection: Ask clients to reflect on significant events in their lives â€“ both positive and challenging. What values were being honored or violated in those moments?
  • Ideal Day Visualization: Guide clients through a visualization of their ideal day. What activities are they engaged in? What qualities are they embodying? The answers often reveal core values.
  • Values-Based Journaling: Encourage clients to journal about their values, exploring what each value means to them and how it manifests in their daily lives.

Remind your clients that self-reflection is an ongoing process. It's not about finding all the answers immediately, but rather about cultivating a deeper awareness of their inner world.

Prioritizing Values

Often, individuals discover that they hold many different values. The challenge then becomes prioritizing those values, especially when faced with conflicting choices. This is crucial for making decisions that align with one's authentic self.

Here are some techniques to help clients prioritize their values:

  • Values Ranking: Have clients rank their identified values in order of importance. This can be done individually or as a couple.
  • Values Trade-Offs: Present clients with hypothetical scenarios that require them to choose between two or more values. This helps them understand which values are most important to them in practice.
  • Values-Based Decision-Making Framework: Introduce a structured framework for making decisions based on values. This involves identifying the values at stake, weighing the potential consequences of each option, and choosing the option that best aligns with their top values.

Emphasize that prioritizing values is not about discarding less important values, but rather about understanding which values should take precedence in different situations.

Articulating Values Clearly

Once values have been identified and prioritized, it's essential to articulate them clearly. This involves putting values into words in a way that is both meaningful and easily understood by others. This is especially important for couples, as clear communication of values is essential for building a strong and aligned relationship.

Here are some tips for articulating values effectively:

  • Use Specific Language: Avoid vague or abstract terms. Instead, use language that is concrete and descriptive. For example, instead of saying "I value honesty," say "I value being truthful and transparent in all my interactions."
  • Provide Examples: Illustrate each value with examples of how it manifests in your life. This helps others understand what the value means to you in practice.
  • Share Your "Why": Explain why each value is important to you. This adds depth and meaning to your articulation.

Encourage couples to share their values with each other regularly. This can be done through intentional conversations, journaling prompts, or even creating a shared values statement for their marriage.

Practical Application: Role-Playing

To solidify your understanding of these concepts, let's engage in a practical exercise. The best way to learn is by doing! Consider the following role-playing exercise:

Scenario: A couple, Sarah and David, come to you for coaching. They are constantly arguing about finances. Sarah is a saver, while David is a spender. They both feel like their values are being violated.

Your Role: The coach.

Exercise:

  1. Guide Sarah and David through a values assessment exercise.
  2. Help them identify their top 3-5 values individually.
  3. Facilitate a discussion where they share their values with each other.
  4. Help them identify any conflicting values that may be contributing to their financial disagreements.
  5. Guide them in developing a plan for honoring each other's values in their financial decision-making.

Remember to use open-ended questions, active listening, and empathy throughout the exercise. The goal is to help Sarah and David gain a deeper understanding of each other's values and find a way to align their financial goals with their shared principles.

Case Study: The Conflicted Couple

Client: Emily and Mark, ages 45 and 48

Intervention:
