Identifying Shared Values

One of the most effective ways to strengthen a marriage is to identify and nurture shared values. These common principles act as anchors, providing stability and direction, especially during challenging times. As a coach, you can guide couples in uncovering these shared foundations.

Here are some practical steps to help couples identify shared values:

  1. Facilitate Open Dialogue: Encourage couples to openly discuss what matters most to them. Use questions like, "What principles guide your decisions?" or "What do you want your marriage to stand for?"
  2. Identify Overlapping Areas: Look for common themes and areas of agreement in their individual values lists. These shared values become the bedrock of their marital bond.
  3. Create a Shared Values Statement: Help the couple articulate their shared values into a concise statement. This statement can serve as a reminder of their common ground and a guide for future decisions.

  3. Reframe the Conflict: Encourage them to see the conflict not as a battle to be won, but as an opportunity for growth and understanding.

Compromise and Negotiation

Compromise and negotiation are essential skills for navigating value differences. Within a faith-based context, these skills should be rooted in love, respect, and a desire to honor God in the marriage.

Here are some strategies for fostering compromise and negotiation:

  1. Focus on Mutual Benefit: Encourage couples to seek solutions that benefit both partners. This requires empathy and a willingness to see things from the other personâ€™s perspective.
  2. Identify Non-Negotiables: Help them identify the values that are absolutely non-negotiable for each of them. This clarifies the boundaries and helps avoid unnecessary conflict.
  3. Explore Creative Solutions: Encourage them to think outside the box and find creative solutions that honor both their values. This may involve trade-offs or finding alternative approaches.

  3. Celebrate Small Victories: Acknowledge and celebrate even small instances of compromise or agreement. This reinforces positive communication and builds momentum.

The COVENANT Framework

Remember our COVENANT framework? The 'V' stands for Values, and it's deeply intertwined with the other elements. When couples have aligned values, it strengthens their Commitment (C). Understanding each other's values fosters Empathy (E). Openly discussing values improves Communication (C). Shared values promote Openness (O) and Vulnerability (V). Addressing conflicting values with grace demonstrates Nurturing (N). And finally, a shared value system reinforces Trust (T) within the marriage.

Case Study: The Millers

Client: John and Sarah Miller, ages 32 and 30, respectively.

Presenting Symptoms: Frequent arguments about finances and parenting styles. John values financial security and a structured approach to parenting, while Sarah prioritizes experiences and a more flexible parenting style.

Intervention: We facilitated discussions to identify their individual values. John realized his financial conservatism stemmed from a childhood of poverty, while Sarah's emphasis on experiences came from a desire to create lasting memories. We then helped them identify shared values, such as providing a loving home for their children and serving their community.

Outcomes: By understanding the roots of their differing values and focusing on their shared goals, John and Sarah were able to compromise. They created a budget that allowed for both savings and experiences, and they developed a parenting style that incorporated both structure and flexibility. Their communication improved, and they reported feeling more connected and understood.
