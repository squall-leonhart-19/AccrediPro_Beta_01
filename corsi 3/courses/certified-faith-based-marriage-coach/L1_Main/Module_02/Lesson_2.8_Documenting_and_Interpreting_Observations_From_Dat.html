Observing the Client

Here's a script to help you practice observing Emily's cues and responding appropriately:

YOU:
"Emily, I notice you mentioned feeling stressed several times. I also see that you're fidgeting with your hands. Is that an accurate observation?"
EMILY:
"Yes, definitely. I didn't even realize I was doing that."
YOU:
"It's completely understandable. When you talk about your bakery, I hear a lot of passion, but also a sense of being overwhelmed. Can you tell me more about what that feels like?"
EMILY:
"It's like I'm constantly putting out fires. I love what I do, but it's taking a toll on my marriage. I feel guilty that I don‚Äôt have the energy for my husband at the end of the day."

"Thank you for sharing that, Emily. I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like the rapid growth of your business has created a significant imbalance in your life. Check Your Understanding

1. Why is observing non-verbal cues important in marriage coaching?

Non-verbal cues often reveal underlying emotions and feelings that clients may not be able to articulate verbally.

2. What's a good way to validate your observations with a client?

Ask open-ended questions like, "I notice you seem a bit hesitant when discussing this topic. Is that accurate?"
