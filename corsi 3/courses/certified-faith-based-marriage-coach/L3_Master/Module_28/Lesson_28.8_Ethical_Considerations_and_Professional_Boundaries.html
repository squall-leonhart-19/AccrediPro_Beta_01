The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 38, who just disclosed her husband is having an affair and wants a divorce. She's devastated and questioning her faith. She called me last night, threatening self-harm. I talked her down, but I don't know what to do next. Should I have contacted emergency services? I'm so worried about her."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah assess the immediacy and severity of the self-harm risk?
  • Does Sarah have a safety protocol in place for clients in crisis?
  • Is Sarah aware of the legal and ethical considerations in such cases?
  • How is Sarah managing her own emotional response to the client's distress?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Safety Assessment

Emphasize the importance of a thorough risk assessment. Use specific, direct questions to gauge the client's intent and plan for self-harm. Document everything.

2

Establish Clear Boundaries

Discuss the limits of the coaching relationship. Coaches are not therapists and must know when to refer to mental health professionals. Help Sarah define her role clearly.

3

Develop a Crisis Protocol

Guide Sarah in creating a step-by-step protocol for handling crisis situations, including emergency contact information, referral resources, and documentation procedures. This builds confidence.

4

Self-Care is Non-Negotiable

Remind Sarah that vicarious trauma is real. Encourage her to seek supervision, practice mindfulness, and set healthy boundaries to protect her well-being. A drained coach helps no one.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Courage
"Sarah, it took courage to reach out. Many new coaches avoid these tough cases. You're showing integrity by seeking guidance. That's commendable."
Validate Feelings
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed in a situation like this. Crisis intervention is stressful, even for seasoned professionals. Your feelings are valid."
Focus on Learning
"Let's view this as a valuable learning opportunity. What did you learn about your own limits and capabilities in this situation? What will you do differently next time?"
Empower Action
"Based on what we've discussed, what specific steps will you take to ensure Emily's safety and your own well-being moving forward? Let's create a concrete plan."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important initial step when a mentee presents a client case involving potential self-harm?

The most important initial step is to assess the immediacy and severity of the risk of self-harm through direct questioning.

2. As a supervisor, how can you best support a new coach who is struggling with setting boundaries with clients in crisis?

Help the coach define their role clearly, emphasizing the limits of the coaching relationship, and guide them in creating a crisis protocol that includes referral resources.

You're Shaping Future Leaders!

By guiding new practitioners through challenging cases, you're not only supporting their growth but also ensuring that more clients receive competent and ethical care. Your mentorship is invaluable.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize safety assessment and risk management in crisis situations.
  • Establish clear boundaries and understand the limits of the coaching role.
  • Develop a crisis protocol with referral resources and emergency contacts.
  • Emphasize self-care and vicarious trauma prevention for new practitioners.
  • Provide specific, actionable feedback and celebrate growth moments.
