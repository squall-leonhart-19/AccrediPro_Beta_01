Her Question
"I'm working with a couple where one partner is considering separation. I'm not sure how to guide them while honoring their faith and individual needs."

The Case Sarah Presents

Client Scenario: Considering Separation

Sarah's Description:

"I'm working with a couple, John and Mary, married for 15 years. John feels emotionally neglected, while Mary feels overwhelmed by his expectations. They both express a desire to honor God, but John is considering separation. I'm not sure how to proceed without pushing them in one direction or another."

Points for Discussion:

  • Explore Sarah's understanding of healthy boundaries in marriage.
  • Discuss the importance of empowering the couple to make their own decisions, guided by their faith.
  • Review coaching techniques for facilitating open and honest communication.
  • Address any personal biases Sarah may have regarding separation.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Guidance for Sarah

1

Emphasize Client Autonomy

Remind Sarah that her role is to facilitate, not dictate. The couple must ultimately decide their path.

2

Review Boundaries

Discuss how to help John and Mary establish healthier boundaries, respecting each other's needs and limitations.

3

Role-Play Communication Techniques

Practice active listening, empathetic responses, and reframing negative statements into positive requests.

4

Address Countertransference

Help Sarah identify any personal beliefs or experiences that might influence her coaching, ensuring she remains objective and supportive.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Constructive and Encouraging Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I appreciate you bringing this case to me. It shows your commitment to ethical and effective coaching. This is a common challenge, and you're already on the right track by seeking guidance."
Inquire About Her Approach
"Before I offer suggestions, what strategies have you considered so far? What feels most aligned with your values and the couple's faith?"
Offer Guidance, Not Solutions
"One approach could be to explore each partner's expectations and needs, helping them communicate more effectively. Remember, our role is to empower them to find their own answers within their faith framework."
Empower Her Confidence
"You have a natural gift for empathy and a strong understanding of scripture. Trust your instincts and continue to learn from each case. You're making a real difference!"

Check Your Understanding

1. As a mentor, what is the most important thing to remember when a new coach presents a challenging case?

The most important thing is to empower the new coach to think critically and trust their instincts, while providing guidance and support.

2. What are some strategies for addressing countertransference in a mentee?

Encourage self-reflection, explore personal beliefs and experiences, and emphasize the importance of objectivity and ethical boundaries.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not directing.
  • Empower your mentee to trust their instincts and develop their own coaching style.
  • Provide constructive feedback that is both encouraging and challenging.
  • Address ethical considerations and personal biases to ensure responsible coaching practices.
  • Celebrate successes and acknowledge growth to build confidence and foster a positive learning environment.
