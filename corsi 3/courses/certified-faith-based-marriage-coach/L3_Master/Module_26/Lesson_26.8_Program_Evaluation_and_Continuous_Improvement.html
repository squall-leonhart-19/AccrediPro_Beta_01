The Session Structure Challenge

Sarah's Coaching Session Dilemma

Sarah's Description:

"I had my first client session yesterday, and it went okay, but I feel like I didn't really guide the conversation well. We jumped from topic to topic, and I'm not sure if she got any real value. How do I structure a session better?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Does Sarah understand the core components of a coaching session (intake, goal setting, action planning)?
  • Is she clear on the difference between coaching and counseling?
  • How can she gently redirect the conversation when it goes off-topic?
  • What tools and techniques can she use to create structure?

Constructive Feedback

Delivering Encouraging Guidance

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's completely normal to feel a bit lost after your first session. The fact that you're reflecting on it and seeking guidance shows you're on the right track. Let's break it down together."
Inquire About Her Process
"Before I offer suggestions, can you walk me through how you planned the session? What structure did you have in mind?"
Teach the Principle of Structure
"Think of a coaching session like a roadmap. You need a clear starting point (intake), a destination (goal), and a route to get there (action plan). Without that structure, it's easy to get lost."
Collaborate on Solutions
"What if we create a session template together? We can adapt it to your style and the needs of your clients. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important first step when a mentee presents a challenging client case?

Actively listen to understand the full context of the situation before offering advice.

2. How can you help a mentee differentiate between coaching and counseling?

Emphasize the forward-focused nature of coaching and gently redirect clients who delve too deeply into the past.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves active listening, specific feedback, and collaborative problem-solving.
  • Clarify the boundaries of coaching, especially in relation to counseling or therapy.
  • Empower mentees to develop their own solutions and build confidence in their abilities.
  • Session structure is crucial for providing value and guiding clients towards their goals.
