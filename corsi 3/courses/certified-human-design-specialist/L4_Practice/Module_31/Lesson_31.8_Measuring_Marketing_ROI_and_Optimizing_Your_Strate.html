Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, tell me a bit about your experience with Human Design and what sparked your interest?"
YOU:
"That's great! So, what are the biggest challenges you're facing right now in terms of your career path?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you elaborate on what feels unfulfilling about your current role? What aspects are draining your energy?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to find more fulfilling work?"
YOU:
"If you could design your ideal career, what would it look like? What kind of impact would you be making?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking a career that aligns with your values, utilizes your strengths, and provides a sense of fulfillment. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach uses Human Design to provide a personalized roadmap for your career. We'll uncover your unique gifts, decision-making strategy, and potential pitfalls. This isn't just about finding a job; it's about designing a career that's authentically you. Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s and 50s, find this process incredibly empowering. For example, I worked with Maria, a 48-year-old nurse, who felt burned out. Through Human Design, she discovered her natural talent for coaching and now runs a successful wellness business. She increased her income by 30% in the first year!"
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me tell you about my program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if Human Design is 'real' or just another personality test."
"That's a valid question, Sarah. While it's not a scientifically 'proven' system in the traditional sense, Human Design synthesizes ancient wisdom like astrology and the I Ching with modern science like genetics. What I've found, and what my clients report, is that it offers incredibly accurate and actionable insights. Think of it as a framework for self-discovery. Would you be open to exploring how it resonates with your experience?"
"I'm worried about the time commitment."
"I understand. My program is designed to be manageable, even for busy professionals. We meet for 90 minutes per week for 12 weeks, and you'll have access to resources and support in between sessions. Think of it as an investment in your long-term career happiness. What's one thing you could shift in your schedule to prioritize this?"
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can address any remaining questions or concerns you have right now."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I hear you. Let's break down the value. My program includes personalized Human Design readings, one-on-one coaching, and ongoing support. Many of my clients see a return on their investment through increased job satisfaction, higher earning potential, and a greater sense of purpose. Another client, Lisa, a 52-year-old teacher, invested in my program and landed a new role with a 20% salary increase within six months. How would finding a truly fulfilling career impact your life?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach as the solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you effectively handle the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions to uncover the specific concerns they're pondering. Offer to provide more information or schedule a follow-up call to address those concerns.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Present your pricing with confidence, highlighting the value and potential ROI for your clients.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice and consistency.
