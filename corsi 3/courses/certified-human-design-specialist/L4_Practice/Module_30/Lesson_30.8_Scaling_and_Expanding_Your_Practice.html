Handling Objections

"I'm not sure I understand Human Design well enough."
"That's perfectly fine, Susan. It's a complex system. The program includes a thorough introduction to the core concepts, tailored to *your* specific chart. Think of it as a guided journey, not a crash course. Are there any specific aspects you're unsure about?"
"I'm concerned about the time commitment."
"I understand. The weekly calls are 60 minutes, and the homework is designed to be integrated into your daily life ‚Äì small, actionable steps. "I respect that. What specific factors are you comparing ‚Äì is it the price, the length of the program, or the specific deliverables? I'm happy to highlight what makes my approach unique, such as the personalized career alignment focus and the ongoing support."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Explore the Problem & Desired Outcome, Connect Human Design to Her Needs, and Present Offer & Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions: "I completely understand. Can I ask ‚Äì what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and motivations is essential for tailoring your offer.
  • Confident objection handling can address concerns and build trust.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and value builds confidence.
  • Consistent practice will make you more comfortable and effective in client acquisition.
