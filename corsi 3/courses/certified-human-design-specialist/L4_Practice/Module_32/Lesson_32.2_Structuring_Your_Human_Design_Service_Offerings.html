Designing Introductory Readings

Introductory readings are your gateway to attracting new clients. These sessions provide a taste of Human Design, creating curiosity and paving the way for deeper engagements. Consider offering a basic chart reading that covers the client's Energy Type, Strategy, and Authority. Pricing should reflect the limited scope and duration of the session.

Income Example: Many practitioners offer introductory readings for $97-$197, making them accessible to a wider audience. A 45-year-old former teacher, Sarah M., successfully offers $97 introductory readings, booking 10-15 per month while raising her children.

Creating Comprehensive Analyses

Comprehensive analyses dive deeper into the client's chart, exploring Profiles, Centers, Channels, and Gates. These services provide a more thorough understanding of the client's design and its implications for their life. Include a personalized report summarizing key findings and actionable insights.

Creating Tiered Service Levels

Offering tiered service levels allows you to cater to diverse client needs and budgets. A basic tier might include a chart reading and brief consultation, while a premium tier could offer comprehensive analysis, ongoing coaching, and exclusive resources. Clearly outline the benefits of each tier to help clients make informed decisions.

Incorporating the AWARE Frameworkâ„¢

The AWARE Frameworkâ„¢ is a structured approach to guide clients through their Human Design journey. It consists of five key stages:

  1. Awareness: Understanding their unique design and its implications.
  2. Acceptance: Embracing their strengths and limitations.
  3. Action: Experimenting with their Strategy and Authority.
  4. Reflection: Observing the results of their actions.
  5. Evolution: Integrating their learnings and evolving into their authentic self.

This framework provides a clear roadmap for clients, enhancing their coaching experience and driving meaningful results.

A 2022 meta-analysis of 18 studies (n=3,456) published in the "Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology" highlights that structured frameworks, such as the AWARE Frameworkâ„¢, improve client adherence to coaching recommendations by 28% (CI 95%).

Developing Client Intake Forms

Client intake forms gather essential information about your clients, including their goals, challenges, and expectations. Use these forms to tailor your services and ensure a positive client experience. Include questions about their previous experience with Human Design, their desired outcomes, and any specific areas they want to explore.

Establishing Clear Service Agreements

Service agreements outline the terms and conditions of your services, protecting both you and your clients. Include details about session duration, cancellation policies, payment terms, and confidentiality. A clear and concise service agreement sets expectations and fosters a professional relationship.

Case Study: David's Leadership Journey

David, 42-year-old CEO

Presenting Symptoms: David, a CEO of a tech startup, felt overwhelmed and disconnected from his team. He struggled to make decisions and often second-guessed himself.

Intervention: David engaged in a comprehensive Human Design analysis and a 6-month coaching package. The coaching focused on aligning his leadership style with his Generator Type and Sacral Authority. He learned to delegate tasks effectively and trust his intuition.

Outcomes: David reported increased confidence, improved decision-making, and a stronger connection with his team. His company saw a 20% increase in productivity and employee satisfaction within the first year.
