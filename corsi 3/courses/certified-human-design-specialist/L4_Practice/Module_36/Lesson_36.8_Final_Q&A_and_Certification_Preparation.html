Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. The program requires a commitment of a few hours per week, but it's designed to fit into your existing schedule. Think of it as investing in your future happiness and fulfillment. What if, in 12 weeks, you had a clear path forward and felt energized about your work?"
"I've tried other things that didn't work."
"I hear you. Many of my clients have had similar experiences. Human Design is different because it's personalized to your unique energetic blueprint. It's not a one-size-fits-all approach. It‚Äôs about understanding *your* specific design and how to work *with* it, not against it."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you actively participate in the program and don't see any value, I'll provide a partial refund. My goal is your success."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan to make the program more accessible. We can break it down into smaller monthly installments to fit your budget."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Explore the Problem & Desired Outcome, Introduce Human Design & Your Program, and Close with Confidence.

2. How should you handle the objection, "I'm not sure if I have the time?"

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the long-term benefits, and highlight the flexibility of your program. Offer solutions like breaking down the program into manageable chunks or adjusting the schedule to fit their needs.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and pain points is essential for crafting a compelling offer.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence builds trust and increases your chances of closing the sale.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and value proposition is key to attracting clients who are willing to invest in themselves.
  • Consistent practice and refinement of your sales skills will lead to greater success in your Human Design practice.
