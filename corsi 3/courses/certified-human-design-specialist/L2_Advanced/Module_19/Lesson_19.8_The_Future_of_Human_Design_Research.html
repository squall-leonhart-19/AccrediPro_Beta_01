Clinical Reasoning Process

Analyzing Eleanor's Case

Step 1: Connect Symptoms to Human Design
Eleanor's Emotional Manifestor type suggests the need to honor her emotional wave before initiating. The defined Sacral-Throat channel can lead to overwork and burnout, especially without proper emotional processing. The 5/1 profile creates unconscious pressure to be the expert, leading to anxiety if she feels she's not performing.
Step 2: Identify Key Imbalances
Hormonal imbalance (irregular periods, endometriosis), adrenal dysregulation (high-normal cortisol), nutrient deficiencies (Vitamin D, iron), and lifestyle stressors (job, travel, sleep). All exacerbated by ignoring her emotional authority and sacral burnout.
Step 3: Prioritize Interventions
First, address the emotional processing and Sacral burnout. Then focus on adrenal support, nutrient repletion, and gentle hormonal balancing. Lifestyle adjustments are crucial for long-term success.
Step 4: Integrate Evidence-Based Practices
Combine Human Design awareness with evidence-based nutrition, stress management techniques (mindfulness, breathwork), and potentially targeted supplementation to address specific deficiencies and imbalances.

Differential Considerations

Ruling Out Other Possibilities

1

Thyroid Dysfunction

TSH is within normal limits, but consider testing free T3 and thyroid antibodies (TPO, TgAb) to rule out subclinical hypothyroidism or Hashimoto's thyroiditis. Up to 10% of women have thyroid antibodies.

2

Adrenal Fatigue/Exhaustion

While AM cortisol is high-normal, assess diurnal cortisol curve (four-point saliva test) to evaluate adrenal function throughout the day. Adrenal insufficiency affects up to 5% of the population.

3

Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI)

Given irregular periods, rule out POI (especially if family history). Check FSH and estradiol levels. POI affects approximately 1% of women under 40.

4

Mood Disorder

Anxiety and feeling "stuck" could indicate underlying depression. Screen for mood disorders using validated questionnaires (e.g., GAD-7, PHQ-9). 1 in 5 US adults experience mental illness each year.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a holistic approach that integrates Human Design with evidence-based practices.
  • Prioritize interventions based on the client's Human Design type, imbalances, and lifestyle factors.
  • Always consider differential diagnoses and be prepared to refer to medical professionals when necessary.
  • Empower clients to take an active role in their healing journey by educating them about their Human Design and providing them with the tools and support they need to make sustainable lifestyle changes.
