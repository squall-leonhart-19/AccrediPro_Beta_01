Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Ethical Maze

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is balancing Eleanor's autonomy (right to self-determination) with the potential harm to her marriage and family finances. There's also a conflict between her values (authenticity, passion) and her husband's values (stability, practicality). Furthermore, her husband's request puts you in a precarious position.
Step 2: Consider Relevant Ethical Principles
Key principles include:
  • Autonomy: Respecting Eleanor's right to make her own decisions.
  • Non-maleficence: Avoiding actions that could harm Eleanor or her family.
  • Beneficence: Acting in Eleanor's best interest.
  • Fidelity: Maintaining trust and confidentiality.
  • Justice: Ensuring fairness and equitable treatment.
Step 3: Assess Scope of Practice
As a Human Design Specialist, your role is to provide guidance based on Eleanor's chart. You are NOT a financial advisor, marriage counselor, or mental health professional. Avoid giving advice outside your area of expertise.
Step 4: Evaluate Options and Consequences
  • Option A: Encourage Eleanor to pursue her passion without considering her husband's concerns. Consequence: Risk of marital conflict and financial instability.
  • Option B: Advise Eleanor to prioritize her marriage and abandon her career aspirations. Consequence: Potential for resentment and unfulfillment.
  • Option C: Facilitate a dialogue between Eleanor and her husband, focusing on compromise and shared values. Consequence: Requires skillful communication and willingness to compromise from both parties.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the primary ethical considerations when a client's career aspirations conflict with their family's values and financial stability?

Balancing client autonomy with non-maleficence (avoiding harm to the family) is crucial. Informed consent, clear scope of practice, and referral to appropriate professionals (financial advisor, therapist) are essential.

2. How should a Human Design Specialist respond when a client's spouse expresses skepticism about Human Design and asks the client to discontinue consultations?

Acknowledge the spouse's concerns, emphasize the client's autonomy, and offer to facilitate a dialogue to bridge the gap in understanding. Avoid disparaging the spouse or undermining their beliefs. Focus on the client's well-being and responsible decision-making.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are inherent in Human Design practice, especially when dealing with complex client situations.
  • A strong ethical framework, including principles of autonomy, non-maleficence, and beneficence, is essential for responsible decision-making.
  • Recognizing and respecting scope of practice limitations is crucial to avoid harm and ensure client well-being.
  • Collaboration with other professionals, such as therapists, financial advisors, and medical doctors, can provide comprehensive support for clients.
  • Maintaining client confidentiality, obtaining informed consent, and engaging in ongoing self-reflection are vital for ethical practice.
