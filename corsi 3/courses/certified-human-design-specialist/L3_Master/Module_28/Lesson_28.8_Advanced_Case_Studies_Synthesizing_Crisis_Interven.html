Former project manager, strong organizational skills, new to holistic wellness.

Detail-oriented, enthusiastic, committed to client success.

The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 51, with a defined Spleen and a triple split definition. Sarah is constantly anxious and feels like she needs to be doing everything all the time. I can see the open centers being conditioned, but I'm not sure how to explain it to her in a way that makes sense. Also, her profile is 5/1, and I don't understand how that influences her decision-making."

What You Need to Explore:

  • How well does Olivia understand the client's authority?
  • Is she getting caught up in the complexity instead of focusing on strategy and authority?
  • Does she understand the 5/1 profile lines and their potential challenges?
  • Is she able to translate the chart into practical advice?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Start with the Basics

Remind Olivia to always ground the client in their strategy and authority. Even with a complex chart, these are the foundations.

2

Prioritize Information

Teach her to focus on the most impactful elements first. For example, with a defined Spleen, addressing fear and intuition is crucial.

3

Translate into Practical Steps

Help her translate chart elements into actionable advice. For instance, suggest Sarah experiment with saying "no" to things that don't feel correct in the moment.

4

Address the Profile

Explain the 5/1 profile dynamics. The 5th line projects expectations, and the 1st line needs to do the research. This can create pressure for Sarah if she feels she has to be an expert.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge the Challenge
"Olivia, I understand why you're feeling overwhelmed. Sarah's chart has a lot going on. It's great that you're seeking guidance."
Focus on Strengths
"What did you notice in Sarah's chart that felt particularly relevant to her experience? What insights did you have?"
Guide Back to Fundamentals
"Let's go back to the basics: what is Sarah's strategy and authority? How can she use those in her daily life?"
Offer a Practical Tip
"With a defined Spleen, help Sarah understand the power of her intuition in the now. Suggest she start noticing what feels 'off' in her body and honor those signals."
Encourage Intuition
"Trust your intuition, Olivia! You have valuable insights. Don't get lost in the details; connect with Sarah on a human level."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important foundation to revisit when a new practitioner feels overwhelmed by a complex chart?

Revisit the client's strategy and authority. These are the core elements that guide decision-making, regardless of the chart's complexity.

2. How can you encourage a mentee to trust their own insights and intuition?

Ask them what they noticed in the chart and what insights they had. Validate their feelings and remind them that they have valuable intuition to offer clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new practitioners involves guiding them back to the foundational principles of Human Design.
  • Help mentees prioritize information and translate chart elements into practical, actionable advice.
  • Encourage them to trust their intuition and connect with clients on a human level.
  • Provide constructive feedback that focuses on strengths and empowers them to simplify complex information.
  • By mentoring others, you amplify your impact and contribute to the growth of the Human Design community.
