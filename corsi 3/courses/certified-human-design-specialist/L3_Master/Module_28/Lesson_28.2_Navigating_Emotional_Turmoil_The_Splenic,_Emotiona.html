The Splenic Authority

The Splenic Authority relies on immediate, gut-level intuition. This authority is all about the "now." During a crisis, this can be incredibly valuable, but also easily clouded by fear and anxiety. A person with Splenic Authority needs to learn to recognize and trust that initial intuitive hit, which is often quiet and easily dismissed.

Key Strategies for Splenic Authority in Crisis:

  • Trust the First Thought: Encourage your client to pay attention to the very first thought or feeling that arises in response to the crisis. This is often the clearest expression of their intuition.
  • Minimize External Noise: In a crisis, everyone has an opinion. Remind your client to limit their exposure to external advice and opinions, focusing instead on their inner guidance.
  • Address Fears Directly: Splenic Authority is linked to the spleen center, which governs fears. Help your client identify and acknowledge their fears, rather than allowing them to unconsciously drive decisions.
  • Practice Mindfulness: Regular mindfulness practices can help your client become more attuned to their intuition and less reactive to stress.

The Emotional Authority

The Emotional Authority experiences the world through a wave of emotions. Clarity comes over time, not in the heat of the moment. For someone with this authority, making decisions during emotional peaks (highs or lows) is a recipe for regret. They need to ride the wave to gain perspective.

Challenges for Emotional Authority in Crisis:

  • Impulsivity: The intensity of emotions can lead to impulsive reactions that are not aligned with their true values.
  • Emotional Contagion: They are highly sensitive to the emotions of others, which can amplify their own stress and anxiety during a crisis.
  • Difficulty Discerning Truth: Emotional clarity takes time. They may struggle to see the situation objectively in the midst of emotional turmoil.

The Solar Plexus Authority

The Solar Plexus Authority (also referred to as the Emotional Authority) operates on an emotional wave. Clarity emerges over time, necessitating patience and awareness. During a crisis, itâ€™s crucial to avoid immediate reactions and allow the emotional wave to crest and subside. This waiting period enables a more objective and aligned decision-making process.

Strategies for Solar Plexus Authority in Crisis:

  • Delayed Decisions: Emphasize the importance of postponing decisions until they have ridden the emotional wave. This could mean waiting a day, a week, or even longer, depending on the situation.
  • Emotional Processing: Encourage them to actively process their emotions through journaling, talking to a trusted friend or therapist, or engaging in creative expression.
  • Self-Care Practices: Remind them to prioritize self-care activities that help regulate their emotions, such as meditation, yoga, spending time in nature, or engaging in hobbies.
  • Seeking Counsel: Advise them to seek counsel from trusted individuals who can offer objective perspectives without adding to the emotional intensity.

Undefined Emotional Centers

Individuals with undefined Emotional Centers are particularly susceptible to emotional amplification during crises. They tend to absorb and mirror the emotions of those around them, which can lead to feeling overwhelmed and disconnected from their own inner guidance. Itâ€™s crucial for them to recognize this tendency and take steps to protect their emotional boundaries.

Strategies for Undefined Emotional Centers in Crisis:

  • Emotional Boundaries: Teach them to create clear emotional boundaries by limiting exposure to emotionally charged environments and individuals.
  • Self-Awareness: Encourage them to develop self-awareness practices, such as journaling or meditation, to distinguish between their own emotions and those they are absorbing from others.
  • Detachment Techniques: Introduce detachment techniques, such as visualization or deep breathing exercises, to help them release absorbed emotions and regain a sense of inner calm.
  • Seeking Solitude: Remind them to prioritize solitude and alone time to decompress and reconnect with their own energy.

The A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ is a powerful tool to help clients navigate crises. It encompasses:

  • Acknowledge: Validating their feelings and experiences.
  • Wait: Encouraging them to avoid impulsive decisions.
  • Ask: Guiding them to seek wise counsel.
  • Reflect: Helping them to examine their options objectively.
  • Empower: Reinforcing their ability to make aligned choices.

Case Study

Case Study: Sarah - Emotional Authority

Client: Sarah, a 38-year-old Projector with Emotional Authority

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah experienced a sudden job loss and felt overwhelmed by anxiety and fear. She wanted to immediately apply for any available position, fearing financial instability.

Intervention: Using Human Design principles, we identified Sarah's Emotional Authority and the importance of waiting for emotional clarity. We applied the A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢:

  • Acknowledge: Validated Sarah's feelings of anxiety and fear.
  • Wait: Encouraged her to postpone job applications for at least a week.
  • Ask: Suggested seeking counsel from a career coach and trusted friends.
  • Reflect: Guided her to reflect on her career values and ideal work environment.
  • Empower: Reinforced her ability to make aligned career choices.

Outcomes: After a week of emotional processing and reflection, Sarah identified a clear vision for her career. She secured a new position that aligned with her values and offered greater opportunities for growth. She reported feeling more confident and empowered in her decision-making process.

Case Study: Emily - Splenic Authority

Client: Emily, a 45-year-old Manifestor with Splenic Authority

Presenting Symptoms: Emily's business partner suddenly decided to leave the company, creating a significant crisis. Emily felt pressured to make immediate decisions about the future of the business, but was experiencing conflicting intuitive signals.

Intervention: We focused on helping Emily connect with her splenic intuition. We used the following strategies:

  • Mindfulness Exercises: Guided Emily through mindfulness exercises to quiet her mind and tune into her gut feelings.
  • Decision-Making Journaling: Encouraged her to journal about each potential decision, noting the immediate intuitive response she experienced.
  • Limiting External Input: Advised her to temporarily limit conversations with others about the business to avoid clouding her intuition.

Outcomes: By trusting her initial intuitive hits, Emily was able to make clear and decisive choices about the future of her business. She streamlined operations, refocused her energy on core strengths, and ultimately increased profitability. She reported feeling more aligned and confident in her leadership.
