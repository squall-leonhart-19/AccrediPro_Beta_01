Advanced Application Techniques

Let's elevate your application of the A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ with some advanced techniques. These are designed to deepen the client experience and facilitate profound shifts.

  1. Assess: Beyond the basics, focus on identifying the client's core limiting beliefs. Use questioning techniques like the Socratic method to help them uncover these beliefs themselves. For example, instead of saying, "You seem to have a fear of failure," ask, "What's the worst thing that could happen if you tried this?"
  2. Witness: Encourage clients to fully feel their emotions without judgment. Use mindfulness techniques, breathwork, or somatic experiencing to help them connect with their physical sensations. A 2018 study in the Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology found that somatic experiencing significantly reduced PTSD symptoms in participants (effect size = 0.82, p < 0.001) (Brom et al., 2018).
  3. Align: This is where your deep knowledge of Human Design comes into play. Help clients translate their chart information into practical actions. For example, if a Generator is feeling frustrated, guide them to identify activities that bring them satisfaction and encourage them to prioritize those activities.
  4. Release: Utilize advanced release techniques such as Emotional Freedom Technique (EFT) or Ho'oponopono. A 2019 meta-analysis in the Journal of Evidence-Based Integrative Medicine showed that EFT significantly reduced anxiety symptoms compared to controls (SMD = -0.62, 95% CI [-0.85, -0.39]) (Sebastian & Nelms, 2017).
  5. Embody: This is about creating lasting change. Help clients develop daily rituals and practices that support their alignment. This could include meditation, journaling, movement, or spending time in nature. Encourage them to track their progress and celebrate their successes.

Remember, the key is to tailor your approach to each client's unique needs and learning style. Some clients may benefit from a more structured approach, while others may prefer a more intuitive and flexible approach.

Case Study 1: Sarah, 35-year-old Projector

Client: Sarah, a 35-year-old Projector.

Presenting Symptoms: Feeling burnt out, resentful, and undervalued at work. Difficulty setting boundaries and attracting the right opportunities.

Intervention: Guided Sarah through the A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢.

  • Assess: Identified her core limiting belief: "I have to work hard to prove my worth."
  • Witness: Helped her connect with the physical sensations of resentment in her body.
  • Align: Educated her on her Projector strategy of waiting for the invitation and recognizing energy.
  • Release: Used EFT to release her attachment to the belief that she had to work hard.

Outcomes: Within three months, Sarah received a promotion at work and felt more confident in setting boundaries. She reported feeling more energized and fulfilled in her work.

Practical Exercises for Transformation

Now it's your turn to put the A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ into practice. Here are two exercises you can use with your clients (and yourself!).

  1. Limiting Belief Busting:
    • Step 1: Have your client identify a situation where they feel stuck or frustrated.
    • Step 2: Guide them to identify the limiting belief that is contributing to their experience.
    • Step 3: Use the Witness step to help them connect with the physical sensations associated with the belief.
    • Step 4: Use the Release step (EFT or Ho'oponopono) to release their attachment to the belief.
    • Step 5: Have them create an affirmation that supports their desired outcome.
    • Step 6: Encourage them to repeat the affirmation daily and notice how their experience shifts.
  2. Aligned Action Planning:
    • Step 1: Have your client identify a goal they want to achieve.
    • Step 2: Guide them to identify actions they can take that are in alignment with their Human Design strategy and authority.
    • Step 3: Help them create a realistic action plan with specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals.
    • Step 4: Encourage them to track their progress and celebrate their successes along the way.

Remember to adapt these exercises to meet the unique needs of each client. Your intuition and creativity are your greatest assets!

Ethical Considerations in Practice

As a Certified Human Design Specialistâ„¢, it's crucial to adhere to the highest ethical standards. This includes respecting client confidentiality, maintaining clear boundaries, and providing accurate and unbiased information.

Here are some specific ethical considerations to keep in mind when using the A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢:
