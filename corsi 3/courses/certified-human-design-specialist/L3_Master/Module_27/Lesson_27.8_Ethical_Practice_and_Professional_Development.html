Supervision & Mentoring Practice

Step into your role as a mentor. This lab offers a scenario to refine your supervisory skills.

Your Mentee

Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic graduate, eager to start but lacks practical confidence.

Background
Former teacher, strong theoretical knowledge, limited practical experience.
Strengths
Excellent communication, empathetic, quick learner.
Growth Areas
Anxious about "doing it wrong," struggles with client boundaries.
Her Question
"I'm overwhelmed by all the information. How do I simplify this for clients?"

The Challenge She Faces

Sarah's Overwhelm

Sarah's Perspective:

"I feel like I need to explain every single gate, channel, and definition to my clients. I'm afraid they won't understand the value if I don't give them ALL the information. But then their eyes glaze over, and I lose them."

Key Areas to Address:

  • Prioritizing essential information for each client.
  • Tailoring explanations to the client's level of understanding.
  • Setting realistic expectations about the depth of the reading.
  • Building client confidence in their own authority.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Focus on Strategy & Authority

Remind her that the core of Human Design is Strategy and Authority. These are the most impactful elements for immediate application.

2

Teach the "90-Second Summary"

Help her craft a concise summary of each client's chart, focusing on their Type, Strategy, and Authority. This is their starting point.

3

Emphasize Experiential Learning

Encourage her to guide clients to experiment with their Strategy and Authority, rather than overloading them with theoretical details.

4

Model Confidence, Not Perfection

Share your own experiences of learning and growing. Show her that it's okay not to know everything, and that authenticity is key.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Enthusiasm
"Sarah, your passion for Human Design is contagious! It's wonderful that you want to share so much with your clients. Now, let's channel that energy effectively."
Reframe "Overwhelm" as "Abundance"
"Instead of feeling overwhelmed, think of it as having an abundance of riches to share. You get to choose the most valuable jewels for each person."
Offer a Practical Tool
"Let's create a template for a '90-Second Summary' ‚Äì a quick overview of Type, Strategy, and Authority that you can use as a starting point for every reading. This will help you focus on what's most important."
Encourage Client-Centered Approach
"Remember, it's about empowering the client to experiment with their design, not about you proving how much you know. Ask them: 'What resonates most with you? What feels true?'"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of Human Design to focus on with new clients?

The most important aspect is their Strategy and Authority. These are the practical tools for making aligned decisions.

2. What's a good way to reframe a mentee's feeling of overwhelm?

Reframe it as having an abundance of riches to share, empowering them to choose the most valuable information for each client.

3. Why is it important to share your own struggles with a mentee?

Sharing your struggles normalizes the learning process, builds trust, and shows that it's okay not to be perfect.

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on Strategy and Authority as the core elements for new clients.
  • Help mentees develop a concise summary of each client's chart.
  • Encourage experiential learning and client-centered exploration.
  • Share your own experiences and vulnerabilities to build confidence.
  • Celebrate their growth and progress along the way.
