Creating a Supportive Environment

Living in alignment isn't always easy, especially when surrounded by people who may not understand or support your journey. Creating a supportive environment is crucial for sustaining long-term alignment. Here are some strategies to consider:

  • Communicate your needs: Clearly express your needs and boundaries to those around you.
  • Seek out like-minded individuals: Connect with people who resonate with your values and support your growth.
  • Set healthy boundaries: Protect your time and energy by saying "no" to commitments that don't align with your priorities.
  • Practice self-care: Prioritize activities that nourish your mind, body, and spirit.

Remember, you are the architect of your life. Surround yourself with people and environments that uplift and empower you.

Practical Exercise: Aligning Your Life

Now, let's put these concepts into practice with a simple exercise you can also use with your clients. This exercise will help you identify areas where you may be out of alignment and create a plan for making positive changes.

  1. Identify: Think about different areas of your life (career, relationships, health, finances, etc.).
  2. Assess: For each area, ask yourself: "Am I making decisions that honor my strategy and authority?"
  3. Evaluate: What emotions are you experiencing in each area? Are you feeling ease, joy, and clarity, or frustration, anger, bitterness, or disappointment?
  4. Plan: Based on your assessment, identify one small change you can make in each area to move towards greater alignment.

Remember, even small steps can make a big difference. Be patient with yourself, and celebrate your progress along the way.

Case Study: Sarah, a 35-Year-Old Projector

Client: Sarah, 35, a Projector

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt deeply unfulfilled in her corporate job, despite being successful. She experienced chronic fatigue, bitterness, and a sense of being overlooked for promotions. She often initiated projects, leading to frustration when her ideas were not valued.

Intervention: We focused on Sarah's Projector strategy: waiting for invitations. She began to consciously refrain from initiating and instead focused on mastering her skills and sharing her insights only when asked. We also explored her emotional authority, helping her to navigate her emotional waves before making decisions.

Outcomes: Within three months, Sarah received two unexpected invitations to lead key projects. She felt more valued, her energy levels improved, and her bitterness subsided. She realized that her attempts to force her ideas upon others were draining her energy and leading to resentment. By honoring her strategy, she attracted opportunities that aligned with her unique talents.
