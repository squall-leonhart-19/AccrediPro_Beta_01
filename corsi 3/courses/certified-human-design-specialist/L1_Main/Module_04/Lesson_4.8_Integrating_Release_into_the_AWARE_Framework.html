Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on releasing resistance. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing that with me. It sounds like you're at a real crossroads. I want to acknowledge the courage it takes to even consider a change after 22 years. Many people stay stuck in situations that don't serve them, but you're actively seeking something more."
MARIA:
"Thank you. I appreciate that. It's just... the fear is overwhelming sometimes. What if I fail? What if I can't find anything better?"
YOU:
"Those 'what ifs' are very common, and they're often a sign of resistance. Resistance is like an emergency brake ‚Äì it's there to protect us, but sometimes it holds us back even when it's no longer needed. Let's explore what that resistance might be trying to tell you."
YOU:
"I'd like to ask you a question: If you knew, without a shadow of a doubt, that you couldn't fail, what would you do differently starting tomorrow?"
MARIA:
"(Pauses) I think... I would finally start that online course in graphic design I've been putting off. I've always loved art, but I never thought I could make a career out of it."
YOU:
"That's a fantastic start. So, what's the smallest, most manageable step you could take today to move towards that graphic design course? Even if it's just spending 15 minutes researching courses online."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal when coaching someone experiencing resistance?

The primary goal is to acknowledge and explore the resistance, not to eliminate it. Understanding the underlying fear or belief driving the resistance is key to helping the client move forward.

2. Why is it important to focus on small, manageable steps when a client feels overwhelmed?

Small steps reduce the perceived risk and make the goal feel less daunting. This helps to overcome the paralysis caused by fear and build momentum.

3. What's a good question to help a client identify their resistance?

"What is that fear trying to protect you from?" or "If you knew you couldn't fail, what would you do differently?" These questions help uncover the underlying beliefs and fears driving the resistance.

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge and validate the client's fears and resistance.
  • Explore the underlying beliefs driving the resistance.
  • Focus on small, manageable steps to build momentum.
  • Ask questions to help the client discover their own path.
  • Celebrate small wins and progress along the way.
