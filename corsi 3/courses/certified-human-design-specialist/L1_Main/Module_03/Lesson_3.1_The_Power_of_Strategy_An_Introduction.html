The Purpose of Strategy

Why is Strategy so important? Because it helps you:

  • Reduce Resistance: By aligning with your natural energy flow, you minimize friction and struggle in your life. Think of it as navigating with the current instead of paddling against it.
  • Minimize Frustration: When you operate outside of your Strategy, you're more likely to encounter frustration, anger, bitterness, or disappointment â€“ the "Not-Self" Themes. Strategy helps you avoid these pitfalls.
  • Invite Opportunities: Strategy positions you to be in the right place at the right time, attracting opportunities that are aligned with your authentic self.
  • Align with Natural Energy Flow: Ultimately, Strategy is about living in harmony with your unique energetic design, allowing you to experience greater ease, joy, and fulfillment.

    Common Misconceptions About Strategy

    Many people initially resist the idea of Strategy, viewing it as restrictive or inauthentic. Let's debunk some common misconceptions:

    • Misconception #1: Strategy is Restrictive: Some believe that following a Strategy limits their freedom and spontaneity. In reality, Strategy provides a framework for making aligned choices, ultimately leading to greater freedom and authenticity.
    • Misconception #2: Strategy is About Control: Strategy is not about controlling outcomes or manipulating situations. It's about aligning with your natural energy and allowing things to unfold in their own time.
    • Misconception #3: Strategy is Inauthentic: Some worry that following a Strategy will make them feel artificial or disconnected from their true selves. In fact, Strategy is a pathway to greater self-discovery and authenticity.

    It's important to remember that Strategy is not a rigid set of rules but a flexible guideline that can be adapted to your individual needs and preferences. The goal is to experiment and find what works best for you.

    Initial Experimentation

    Embarking on your Strategy experiment requires patience, curiosity, and a willingness to learn. Here are some tips for getting started:

    • Start Small: Don't try to overhaul your entire life overnight. Begin by focusing on one or two specific areas where you want to see a change.
    • Be Patient: It takes time to unlearn old patterns and integrate new ones. Be kind to yourself and celebrate small victories along the way.
    • Observe and Reflect: Pay attention to how you feel when you are aligned with your Strategy versus when you are not. Journaling can be a helpful tool for tracking your experiences.
    • Seek Support: Connect with other Human Design enthusiasts or work with a qualified practitioner to gain insights and guidance.
    • Trust the Process: Remember that Strategy is a continuous journey of self-discovery, not a destination. Trust that you are exactly where you need to be, and enjoy the ride.
