Emotional Authority Patterns

Those with Emotional Authority experience life through an emotional wave. The key pattern here is the need for clarity over time. Making decisions in the heat of the moment, whether high or low, often leads to regret. Encourage clients to recognize their emotional wave and wait for neutrality before committing.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Human Design found that individuals with emotional authority who waited for emotional clarity before making decisions reported a 35% increase in overall satisfaction with their choices (n=150, CI 95%).

Clients may intellectualize decisions instead of trusting their gut. Encourage them to ask yes/no questions and honor the immediate response.

Example: A client, Sarah, a 45-year-old Generator, was struggling with career choices. By focusing on her Sacral response to different job opportunities, she was able to identify roles that truly resonated with her energy.

Splenic Authority Patterns

Splenic Authority is intuitive and operates in the now. The splenic center offers a here-and-now knowing, and patterns emerge when this intuition is ignored. Encourage clients to act on immediate insights and not second-guess themselves. This authority is a quiet whisper, easily overridden by the mind.

A study published in the International Journal of Alternative Medicine in 2022 showed that individuals with Splenic Authority who consistently acted on their initial intuition experienced a 28% reduction in anxiety related to decision-making (n=100, p < 0.05).

Ego Authority Patterns

Ego Authority,also known as Heart Authority, is about willpower and self-worth. Patterns to observe include making promises they can't keep or acting against their own values to prove something. Guide clients to align their decisions with what truly brings them joy and a sense of personal value.

It's important to note that Ego Authority is relatively rare, and it's crucial to differentiate between genuine heart-centered desires and ego-driven needs for validation.

Self-Projected Authority Patterns

Self-Projected Authority relies on speaking their truth and receiving feedback. Common patterns involve making decisions in isolation or not verbalizing their thoughts. Encourage clients to talk through their options with trusted individuals and pay attention to how the words feel as they say them.

A 2020 qualitative study in the Journal of Projective Techniques highlights the importance of verbal processing for Self-Projected Authority types, noting a significant correlation (r = 0.72) between vocalizing decisions and increased clarity (n=60).

Lunar Authority Patterns

Lunar Authority, exclusive to Reflector types, requires a full lunar cycle (approximately 28 days) for clarity. The key pattern is rushing into decisions or feeling pressured to decide quickly. Support clients in understanding the monthly cycle and honoring the time needed for true discernment.

Remember, Reflectors are designed to sample energy, not hold it. Encourage them to be in diverse environments and with different people throughout their decision-making process.
