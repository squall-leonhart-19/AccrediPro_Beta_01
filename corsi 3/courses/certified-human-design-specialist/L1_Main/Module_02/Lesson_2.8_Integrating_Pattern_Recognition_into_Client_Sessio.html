Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your experiences with me. It takes courage to acknowledge these patterns. I want to assure you that you're not alone, and these repetitive cycles often stem from deeply ingrained beliefs and behaviors. We'll work together to witness these patterns without judgment and identify the root causes."
MARIA:
"It's just so frustrating. I feel like I keep making the same mistakes over and over again."
YOU:
"I understand your frustration, Maria. Let's explore these patterns with curiosity and compassion. Instead of blaming yourself, let's see if we can understand *why* these patterns are showing up in your life. We'll focus on witnessing these patterns, understanding their origin, and then creating new, empowering choices."

"Now, let's dive a little deeper. Can you recall a specific instance where you felt like you were repeating a pattern? What were the circumstances, and how did you feel at that moment?"
MARIA:
"Well, just last week, I met someone new online, and he seemed so charming at first. But then he started canceling plans last minute, and I found myself making excuses for him, just like I have in the past."

"Thank you for sharing that, Maria. This is a perfect example of a pattern we can explore. Let‚Äôs unpack this. What thoughts and feelings were present when you were making excuses for him? What needs were you trying to meet by doing so?"

‚ùå Rushing the process
‚úì Allow space for silence and reflection. The client may need time to process their emotions.
‚ùå Making assumptions about the root cause

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses a sense of relief: "It feels good to finally talk about this."
  • She gains a new perspective on her patterns: "I never thought about it that way before."
  • She identifies the underlying needs driving her behavior: "I guess I was seeking validation."
  • She feels empowered to make different choices in the future: "Next time, I'll set a boundary."
  • She expresses gratitude for your support: "Thank you for helping me see this more clearly."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Witness Patterns" coaching technique?

The primary goal is to help the client observe and understand their repetitive behaviors without judgment, allowing them to identify the root causes and make more conscious choices.

2. Why is it important to avoid offering solutions early in the coaching process?

Offering solutions prematurely can prevent the client from fully exploring and understanding the underlying patterns and needs driving their behavior. It's crucial to first create a safe space for exploration and self-discovery.

Key Takeaways

  • Witnessing patterns involves observing repetitive behaviors without judgment.
  • Focus on understanding the root causes rather than simply addressing the symptoms.
  • Empathy and non-judgment are crucial for creating a safe space for the client.
  • Empower the client to discover their own solutions and make aligned choices.
