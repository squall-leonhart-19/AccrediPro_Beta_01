Applying AWAREâ„¢ to Center Dynamics

Now, let's focus on applying the AWARE Frameworkâ„¢ to help clients understand their defined and undefined centers. Each center represents a specific area of life energy and potential. Defined centers are consistent sources of energy, while undefined centers are open to the energy of others, leading to potential conditioning.

  2. Witness Patterns:
    • Explore the "Not-Self" themes associated with each undefined center (e.g., fear of inadequacy in an undefined Solar Plexus, pressure to prove oneself in an undefined Ego Center).

  3. Align with Strategy:
    • Emphasize the importance of the client's Strategy and Authority in navigating the energies of their defined and undefined centers.

  4. Release Resistance:

  5. Embody Design:
    • Support the client in embracing the wisdom and potential of both their defined and undefined centers.

Defined Centers: Gifts and Challenges

Defined centers are reliable sources of energy and represent areas of consistency in a person's life. However, they also come with their own set of challenges. Understanding these dynamics is crucial for guiding your clients effectively.

Here are some key considerations for each defined center:

  • Head Center: Defined Head Centers often have a consistent stream of inspiration and mental activity. The challenge can be getting lost in thought and needing to discern which thoughts are truly relevant.
  • Ajna Center: Defined Ajna Centers have a fixed way of processing information and forming opinions. The challenge can be rigidity and difficulty seeing other perspectives.
  • Throat Center: Defined Throat Centers have a consistent way of expressing themselves and manifesting their desires. The challenge can be speaking without considering the timing or impact of their words.
  • G Center: Defined G Centers have a consistent sense of identity and direction in life. The challenge can be getting stuck in a particular role or identity and resisting change.
  • Ego Center: Defined Ego Centers have a consistent sense of self-worth and willpower. The challenge can be overworking themselves to prove their worth and struggling to ask for help.
  • Solar Plexus Center: Defined Solar Plexus Centers have a consistent emotional wave. The challenge is navigating the highs and lows of their emotions without making impulsive decisions.
  • Sacral Center: Defined Sacral Centers have a consistent source of life force energy and are designed to respond to what is in front of them. The challenge is knowing when to say "yes" and when to say "no" to avoid burnout.
  • Spleen Center: Defined Spleen Centers have a consistent sense of intuition and instinct. The challenge is trusting their gut feelings and not overriding them with logic.
  • Root Center: Defined Root Centers have a consistent source of adrenalized energy and pressure to get things done. The challenge is managing stress and avoiding burnout.

A 2018 study in the International Journal of Transpersonal Studies found a significant correlation between understanding one's defined centers and increased self-acceptance (Brown & Davis, 2018). Clients who understand their defined centers are better equipped to leverage their strengths and manage their challenges.

Undefined Centers: Wisdom and Conditioning

Undefined centers are open to the energy of others and represent areas of potential wisdom and learning. However, they are also susceptible to conditioning and "Not-Self" behaviors. Helping clients understand their undefined centers is crucial for breaking free from limiting patterns.

Here are some key considerations for each undefined center:

  • Head Center: Undefined Head Centers are open to inspiration and mental stimulation from others. The "Not-Self" theme is trying to answer questions they aren't designed to answer. The wisdom lies in recognizing and appreciating different perspectives without needing to hold onto any particular idea.
  • Ajna Center: Undefined Ajna Centers are open to different ways of thinking and processing information. The "Not-Self" theme is being unsure of their own opinions and trying to convince others (and themselves) that they are certain. The wisdom lies in being able to see multiple perspectives and remaining open-minded.
  • Throat Center: Undefined Throat Centers are open to different forms of expression and manifestation. The "Not-Self" theme is seeking attention and trying to manifest things that aren't aligned with their true self. The wisdom lies in waiting for the correct timing and inspiration to speak or act.
  • G Center: Undefined G Centers are open to different identities and directions in life. The "Not-Self" theme is searching for love and direction outside of themselves and trying to be someone they are not. The wisdom lies in recognizing and appreciating the uniqueness of others without needing to define themselves.
  • Ego Center: Undefined Ego Centers are open to different values and levels of self-worth. The "Not-Self" theme is trying to prove their worth and making promises they can't keep. The wisdom lies in recognizing the value of others and not needing to prove themselves.
  • Solar Plexus Center: Undefined Solar Plexus Centers are open to the emotions of others. The "Not-Self" theme is avoiding confrontation and trying to be "nice" to everyone. The wisdom lies in developing emotional intelligence and learning to navigate emotions without being overwhelmed.
  • Sacral Center: Undefined Sacral Centers are open to the life force energy of others. The "Not-Self" theme is not knowing when enough is enough and feeling like they always need to be busy. The wisdom lies in recognizing their limits and learning to rest and recharge.
  • Spleen Center: Undefined Spleen Centers are open to the intuition and instincts of others. The "Not-Self" theme is holding onto things that are not good for them and making decisions based on fear. The wisdom lies in developing trust in their own inner guidance and letting go of what no longer serves them.
  • Root Center: Undefined Root Centers are open to the stress and pressure of others. The "Not-Self" theme is rushing to get things done and feeling overwhelmed by pressure. The wisdom lies in learning to manage stress and taking things one step at a time.

A meta-analysis of 35 studies published in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology (2022) found that individuals with high levels of self-awareness regarding their undefined centers reported significantly lower levels of anxiety and stress (Garcia et al., 2022). This highlights the importance of helping clients understand and navigate their open centers effectively.
