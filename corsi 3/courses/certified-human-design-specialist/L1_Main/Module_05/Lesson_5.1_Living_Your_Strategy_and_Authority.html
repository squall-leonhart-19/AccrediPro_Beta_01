Deep Dive into Strategy

Your Strategy is your unique roadmap for navigating the world with ease and flow. Let's revisit each Type and explore practical applications:

Recognizing and Correcting 'Not-Self'

The "not-self" is the conditioned aspect of your being, driven by societal expectations and mental programming. Each Type has a specific not-self theme:

  • Generators: Frustration when initiating.
  • Manifestors: Anger when resisted.
  • Projectors: Bitterness when not recognized.
  • Reflectors: Disappointment when trying to be someone they're not.

When you feel these emotions, it's a signal that you're out of alignment. To correct, pause, reconnect with your Strategy and Authority, and ask yourself: "Am I operating from my true self, or am I being driven by external pressures?"

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Sarah, a Generator

Client: Sarah, 35, Generator with Sacral Authority

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic fatigue, feelings of being overwhelmed, dissatisfaction with her career.

Intervention: Sarah was guided to consciously apply her Strategy of "waiting to respond." She started paying attention to what truly lit her up and said "no" to tasks that drained her energy. She also began asking herself "yes/no" questions related to her career and listened to her gut response.

Outcomes: Within three months, Sarah reported a significant increase in energy levels, reduced feelings of overwhelm, and a newfound sense of purpose in her work. She also made the decision to transition to a new role that was more aligned with her Sacral Authority.

Case Study 2: Mark, a Projector

Client: Mark, 42, Projector with Emotional Authority

Presenting Symptoms: Bitterness, feelings of being overlooked, difficulty making decisions.

Intervention: Mark was coached to focus on mastering his skills and sharing his wisdom with others. He was also taught to recognize his Emotional Authority and to avoid making decisions in the heat of the moment. He learned to ride his emotional wave and wait for clarity before acting.

Outcomes: Over time, Mark began to attract the right invitations and opportunities. He also developed more confidence in his decision-making abilities and experienced a reduction in bitterness. He became recognized for his expertise and found fulfillment in guiding others.
