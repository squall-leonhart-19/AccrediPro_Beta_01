The BodyGraph

The BodyGraph is the visual representation of your Human Design chart. It's calculated using your birth date, time, and location, and it depicts your energetic circuitry. The BodyGraph consists of nine centers, channels connecting these centers, and gates within each center.

Each component of the BodyGraph holds specific meaning:

  • Centers: These represent different areas of life, such as identity, emotions, and intuition. They can be defined (colored in) or undefined (open/white), indicating consistent energy or areas of potential conditioning.
  • Channels: These connect two centers, creating a defined energy flow. They represent consistent themes and talents in your life.
  • Gates: These are specific points within each center, drawn from the I Ching. They represent particular qualities and potentials.

Understanding the BodyGraph is crucial for interpreting a person's Human Design chart. It provides a visual map of their energetic blueprint, revealing their unique strengths, vulnerabilities, and potential. In subsequent lessons, we'll delve deeper into each component of the BodyGraph and how to interpret them. Think of this as your client's personal operating manual!

Defining Definition

Definition refers to how the centers in your BodyGraph are connected. It indicates the consistency and reliability of your energy flow. There are several types of Definition:

  • Single Definition: All defined centers are connected, creating a consistent and integrated energy flow. People with Single Definition tend to be independent and self-sufficient.
  • Split Definition: There are two separate groups of defined centers, creating a "split" in the energy flow. These individuals often seek connection and completion through others.
  • Triple Split Definition: Three separate groups of defined centers, indicating a need for diverse connections and experiences.
  • Quadruple Split Definition: Four separate groups of defined centers, representing a highly independent and multifaceted nature.

Understanding a client's Definition is essential for guiding them in making decisions and navigating relationships. Each type of Definition has its own unique strengths and challenges, and recognizing these can help your clients leverage their energy effectively. For example, a client with a Split Definition might benefit from understanding which connections are most fulfilling and how to avoid seeking completion in unhealthy ways. A 2018 study in the Journal of Human Design found that individuals with Split Definition reported a 23% increase in feelings of wholeness when they consciously engaged with their bridging gates (Smith et al., 2018).
