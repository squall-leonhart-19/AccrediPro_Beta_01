Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on Maria's Human Design Authority. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're at a real turning point. Based on your Human Design chart, you have Emotional Authority. This means big decisions are best made when you've allowed yourself to process your feelings over time. Does that resonate with you?"
MARIA:
"That's interesting. I tend to make decisions quickly, and then regret them later. So, I guess it does."
YOU:
"Many with Emotional Authority feel that initial rush, but true clarity comes after riding the emotional wave. So, let's explore this 'stuck' feeling. When you imagine yourself in a truly fulfilling role, what emotions come up for you?"
MARIA:
"Excitement...but also fear. Fear that I'm too old to change, or that I'll fail."
YOU:
"Those are valid emotions, Maria. The key is to not make any decisions *right now*. Let's just observe those feelings. Over the next few days, notice what brings you joy, and what drains you. We'll use that emotional data to guide your next steps. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That feels...safe. Less pressure. Okay, I can do that."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to understand a client's Human Design Authority before offering advice?

Understanding a client's Authority helps you tailor your coaching approach to align with their natural decision-making process, leading to more effective and sustainable results.

2. How would you adapt this coaching script for a client with Splenic Authority?

For Splenic Authority, you'd focus on helping them access their immediate intuition and trust their gut feelings. The script would emphasize making decisions in the moment, rather than over time.

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding a client's Human Design Authority is crucial for effective coaching.
  • Clients with Emotional Authority need time to process their feelings before making decisions.
  • Validate your client's emotions, both positive and negative.
  • Adapt your coaching style to align with the client's unique decision-making process.
  • Practice active listening and empathy to build trust with your clients.
