The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 34, whose baby is tongue-tied. She's seen a lactation consultant who recommended a frenotomy. Sarah is hesitant about the procedure but is struggling with latch and pain. I suggested some latching techniques and nipple shields, but I'm not sure if I'm doing enough or if I should push her to get the frenotomy."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Aisha overstepping her scope by giving medical advice?
  • Did she explore Sarah's fears and concerns about the procedure?
  • What other non-invasive techniques has Sarah tried?
  • Is Aisha projecting her own beliefs onto the client?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize Client-Centered Care

Remind Aisha that her role is to support Sarah's decisions, not to make them for her. The client is the expert on her own experience.

2

Review Scope of Practice

Reiterate that suggesting or discouraging medical procedures is outside the scope of a breastfeeding support coach. Focus on providing information and emotional support.

3

Explore Alternative Techniques

Encourage Aisha to explore other latching techniques, positions, and comfort measures that Sarah can try before or instead of a frenotomy.

4

Teach Reflective Listening

Help Aisha practice reflective listening to understand Sarah's fears and concerns. Ask open-ended questions like, "What are your biggest concerns about the frenotomy?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Aisha, thank you for bringing this case to me. I can see you're deeply invested in helping Sarah and her baby."
Ask for Self-Reflection
"What are your thoughts on how you handled the situation? What do you think went well, and what could you have done differently?"
Provide Guidance on Scope of Practice
"Remember, as a breastfeeding support coach, our role is to empower the client to make informed decisions, not to give medical advice. Suggesting or discouraging a medical procedure is outside our scope."
Offer Alternative Strategies
"Instead of focusing on the frenotomy, what other techniques could you explore with Sarah to improve latch and reduce pain? Let's brainstorm some options."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary role of a breastfeeding support coach regarding medical decisions?

The primary role is to empower the client to make informed decisions, not to give medical advice.

2. Why is it important to explore alternative techniques with a client before recommending a medical procedure?

Exploring alternative techniques allows the client to make an informed decision and feel empowered in their choices, aligning with the client-centered approach.

Key Takeaways

  • Emphasize client-centered care by supporting the client's decisions, not making them.
  • Clearly define and adhere to the scope of practice for a breastfeeding support coach.
  • Explore alternative techniques and comfort measures to provide comprehensive support.
  • Use reflective listening to understand the client's fears and concerns.
  • Provide constructive feedback that focuses on learning and growth.
