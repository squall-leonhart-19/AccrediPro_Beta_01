Your Teaching Approach: Guiding Principles

Key Points to Cover with Emily

1

Acknowledge the Complexity

Start by validating the difficulty of the case: "Breast surgery can indeed complicate breastfeeding, and it's normal to feel challenged."

2

Assess for Nerve Damage

Explain that breast augmentation can sometimes damage nerves, leading to altered sensation and pain. "Ask Sarah about any changes in sensation since her surgery."

3

Explore Alternative Feeding Methods

Suggest exploring pumping, cup feeding, or supplemental nursing system to reduce nipple trauma. "These methods can provide relief while maintaining milk supply."

4

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Emphasize the importance of medical referral. "This case may require a lactation consultant or physician to rule out medical causes of pain."

Your Feedback Dialogue: A Supportive Approach

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Emily, I appreciate you bringing this case. It shows your commitment to your clients. Breast surgery cases can be tricky."
Ask Before Telling
"What are your initial thoughts about the possible causes of Sarah's pain? What other questions did you ask her?"
Teach the Principle
"With breast surgery, nerve damage is always a consideration. Even subtle changes in sensation can impact breastfeeding."
Empower Next Steps
"What would you like to do next with Sarah? How can I support you in finding the best resources for her?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of mentoring a new practitioner?

Empowering them to develop their own clinical reasoning and problem-solving skills.

2. How can you help a mentee who is unsure about their scope of practice?

Provide clear guidelines and examples of what falls within and outside their scope, and encourage them to seek medical referrals when necessary.

You're Becoming a Leader!

By guiding new practitioners, you're shaping the future of breastfeeding support. Your experience and wisdom are invaluable assets. Embrace your role as a mentor and watch your impact multiply!

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners to develop their own clinical reasoning.
  • Acknowledge the complexity of cases and validate the mentee's feelings.
  • Emphasize the importance of assessing for underlying issues, like nerve damage.
  • Reinforce the scope of practice and the need for medical referrals when necessary.
  • Provide constructive feedback that empowers the mentee to take the next steps.
