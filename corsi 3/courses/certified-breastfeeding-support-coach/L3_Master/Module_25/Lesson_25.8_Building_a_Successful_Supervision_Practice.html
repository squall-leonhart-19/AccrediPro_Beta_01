Sarah's Client Case: The Anxious Mom

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a new mom, Emily, who is struggling with low milk supply and is super anxious about it. She's tried everything ‚Äì teas, pumping, latch support. I suggested she try power pumping and lactation cookies, but she's still worried. She keeps texting me at all hours, and I feel like I'm not helping."

Points to Consider:

  • Is Sarah setting appropriate boundaries with the client?
  • Is Emily's anxiety being addressed or just her milk supply?
  • Are there underlying medical issues contributing to low supply?
  • Is Sarah clear on her scope of practice versus an IBCLC?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Boundary Setting

Discuss the importance of setting clear boundaries with clients regarding communication hours and response times. Explain that this protects her time and prevents burnout.

2

Addressing Anxiety

Explore how to acknowledge and validate Emily's anxiety without getting drawn into it. Teach techniques for calming the client and referring her to mental health professionals if needed.

3

Medical Referrals

Emphasize the importance of assessing for underlying medical issues that could be affecting milk supply (thyroid issues, retained placental fragments). Explain when and how to refer to an IBCLC or physician.

4

Scope of Practice

Reinforce the boundaries of a Breastfeeding Support Coach versus an IBCLC. Ensure she understands what she can and cannot do, and how to communicate this to clients.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's wonderful that you're so invested in your client's success. Your empathy is a real strength. This is a common situation, and it's great you're seeking guidance."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What boundaries have you set with Emily regarding communication? What resources have you given her for managing her anxiety? How would you describe the difference between your role and an IBCLC‚Äôs?"
Offer Specific Suggestions
"Consider setting specific hours for client communication. You might say, 'I check messages between 9am-5pm.' "What feels like the most important next step for Emily? How can you communicate that clearly and kindly? I trust your judgment, and I'm here to support you."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is setting boundaries important for a new Breastfeeding Support Coach?

Setting boundaries helps prevent burnout, protects the coach's time, and ensures sustainable practice.

2. What is the difference between a Breastfeeding Support Coach and an IBCLC?

A Breastfeeding Support Coach offers education and support for common breastfeeding challenges, while an IBCLC is a medical professional who can address complex medical issues related to breastfeeding.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners with empathy and support.
  • Boundary setting is crucial for both the coach and the client.
  • Addressing client anxiety is as important as addressing physical challenges.
  • Understanding and communicating scope of practice is essential.
  • Effective supervision empowers mentees to develop their own clinical judgment.
