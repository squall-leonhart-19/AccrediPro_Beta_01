The Challenge She Faces

Aisha's Session Structure Concerns

"I had a consultation with a mom, Sarah, who was having trouble with latch. I spent two hours with her, going over everything from positioning to milk supply, but I felt like I was just throwing information at her. She left feeling overwhelmed, and I felt like I didn't really help. I'm not sure how to make my sessions more focused and effective."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • How does Aisha currently structure her sessions?
  • What tools or frameworks does she use (if any)?
  • How does she assess the client's needs and prioritize information?
  • Does she understand the importance of client-centered goals?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Introduce the "3-Goal Framework"

Teach her to identify the client's top 3 goals for the session. This creates focus and prevents information overload. For example: improved latch, reduced pain, and increased confidence.

2

Emphasize Active Listening and Questioning

Remind her that the client already has valuable knowledge. Teach her to ask open-ended questions to uncover the client's needs and preferences. For example: "What's been working for you so far? What are your biggest concerns?"

3

Teach the "Chunking" Technique

Explain how to break down complex information into smaller, manageable chunks. Instead of covering everything at once, focus on one or two key points per session. For example, during the first visit address latch and positioning, and schedule a follow-up for milk supply concerns.

4

Reinforce the Importance of Client Empowerment

Highlight the need to empower clients to make their own informed choices. Encourage her to offer options and support the client's decisions, even if they differ from her own preferences.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Aisha, I can see how much you care about your clients. It's clear you want to give them the best possible support, and that's a wonderful quality."
Ask for Self-Reflection
"Before I share my thoughts, what parts of the session with Sarah do you feel went well? Where do you think you could have improved?"
Introduce a New Strategy
"One technique that I've found helpful is to start each session by asking the client to identify their top three goals for that visit. This can help you stay focused and ensure that you're addressing their most pressing concerns."
Encourage Experimentation
"Why don't you try using the '3-Goal Framework' in your next few sessions and see how it goes? I'm here to support you as you experiment and refine your approach."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the benefits of using a "3-Goal Framework" in client sessions?

It helps create focus, prevents information overload, and ensures that the practitioner addresses the client's most pressing concerns.

2. Why is it important to encourage self-reflection in your mentees?

Self-reflection helps mentees identify their strengths and weaknesses, learn from their experiences, and develop their own unique approach to practice.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners towards skill development and self-sufficiency.
  • Effective feedback should be specific, actionable, and focused on growth.
  • Tools like the "3-Goal Framework" can help new practitioners structure their sessions more effectively.
  • Encouraging self-reflection helps mentees learn from their experiences and develop their own unique approach.
  • Your role as a mentor is crucial in shaping the future of breastfeeding support.
