Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Tongue-tie or Lip-tie

A restricted tongue or lip can significantly impact latch and milk transfer. Assess Amelia for tongue-tie and lip-tie and refer to a qualified professional for evaluation and possible release.

2

Retained Placental Fragments

Although Eleanor experienced postpartum hemorrhage, retained placental fragments can still interfere with milk supply. Monitor for signs of infection or prolonged lochia and consult with her OB/GYN if suspected.

3

Thyroid Dysfunction

Postpartum thyroiditis can mimic symptoms of fatigue and anxiety, and impact milk supply. Check thyroid function (TSH, Free T4, Free T3) to rule out thyroid issues.

4

Postpartum Depression/Anxiety

Eleanor's history of anxiety and current feelings of inadequacy may indicate postpartum mood disorders. Screen for postpartum depression/anxiety using validated tools (e.g., Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale).

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when supporting a breastfeeding mother with a history of gestational diabetes and PCOS?

Consider potential impacts on milk supply due to insulin resistance and hormonal imbalances. Monitor blood sugar levels and collaborate with the healthcare provider for optimal management.

2. When is referral for tongue-tie/lip-tie assessment indicated in a breastfeeding infant?

Referral is indicated when there are difficulties with latch, nipple pain, poor weight gain, or clicking sounds during feeding, despite optimized positioning and latch techniques.

3. What are the potential benefits and risks of using galactagogues to increase milk supply?

Galactagogues like fenugreek can increase milk supply, but potential risks include allergic reactions, gastrointestinal upset, and drug interactions. Domperidone carries risks of cardiac side effects and should only be used under medical supervision. Always discuss options with the mother and her healthcare provider.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex breastfeeding cases require a comprehensive assessment of maternal and infant factors.
  • Underlying maternal health conditions can significantly impact lactation and breastfeeding outcomes.
  • Evidence-based interventions should be prioritized to optimize latch, milk transfer, and maternal comfort.
  • Referral to medical professionals is crucial when red flags are present or when interventions are beyond your scope of practice.
  • A phased approach allows for gradual implementation of interventions and ongoing monitoring of progress.
