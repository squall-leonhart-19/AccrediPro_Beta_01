Introduction to Ethical Frameworks

In previous lessons, we've covered key ethical considerations such as scope of practice, informed consent, confidentiality, cultural sensitivity, and conflicts of interest. Now, we'll delve into practical tools that will help you navigate complex situations: ethical decision-making frameworks. These frameworks provide a structured approach to analyzing dilemmas and arriving at well-reasoned, justifiable decisions. Think of them as your ethical compass, guiding you toward responsible and empowering support for breastfeeding families.

Many coaches find that developing their ethical acumen not only helps them avoid pitfalls but also builds confidence and enhances their professional reputation. A breastfeeding support coach with a strong ethical foundation can earn a comfortable $60,000 - $90,000+ per year providing valued services to families.

The Ethical Decision-Making Model

One widely used framework is the general Ethical Decision-Making Model. This model typically involves the following steps:

  1. Identify the ethical problem: Clearly define the issue and the conflicting values or principles involved.
  2. Gather information: Collect relevant facts, including client perspectives, professional guidelines, and legal considerations.
  3. Identify options: Brainstorm potential courses of action.
  4. Evaluate options: Assess each option based on ethical principles, potential consequences, and relevant values.
  5. Choose an option: Select the option that best aligns with ethical principles and values, while minimizing negative consequences.
  6. Implement the decision: Take action based on the chosen option
  7. Evaluate the outcome: Reflect on the results of the decision and identify lessons learned for future situations.

Let's consider an example:

Case Study: The Conflicted Recommendation

Client: Sarah, a 32-year-old first-time mother.

Presenting Problem: Sarah is struggling with low milk supply and is considering supplementing with formula. She has received conflicting advice from various sources, including her pediatrician and online forums. Her pediatrician recommended supplementing with formula right away. Sarah is feeling overwhelmed and unsure of what to do.

Ethical Dilemma: You are aware of strategies to increase milk supply but also respect Sarah's autonomy and right to make informed choices. Recommending strategies to increase milk supply may conflict with the pediatrician's advice. You are also aware that exclusive breastfeeding is recommended for the first six months by organizations such as the AAP and WHO.

Application of Ethical Decision-Making Model:

  1. Identify the problem: Balancing the support for Sarah's breastfeeding goals with respect for her autonomy and the pediatrician's recommendation.
  2. Gather information: Sarah's perspective, pediatrician's advice, evidence-based strategies to increase milk supply, and ethical guidelines on informed consent and scope of practice.
  3. Identify options:
    • A) Strongly encourage exclusive breastfeeding and provide intensive support to increase milk supply.
    • B) Present all options (including formula supplementation) neutrally and support Sarah's choice.
    • C) Defer to the pediatrician's recommendation and encourage formula supplementation.
  4. Evaluate options:
    • A) Aligns with breastfeeding promotion but may undermine Sarah's autonomy.
    • B) Respects autonomy but may not adequately address Sarah's breastfeeding goals.
    • C) May not align with Sarah's goals or the evidence-based benefits of breastfeeding.
  5. Choose an option: Present all options, including the pediatrician's recommendation, alongside evidence-based strategies to increase milk supply. Emphasize the importance of informed consent and Sarah's right to choose.
  6. Implement the decision: Provide Sarah with comprehensive information, resources, and support to make an informed decision.
  7. Evaluate the outcome: Sarah feels empowered to make a decision that aligns with her values and goals. She chooses to try strategies to increase milk supply while having formula as a backup.

The PLUS Ethical Reasoning Model

The PLUS Ethical Reasoning Model is another valuable framework. PLUS stands for:

  • Policies: Is it consistent with my organizationâ€™s policies and procedures?
  • Legal: Is it acceptable under the applicable laws and regulations?
  • Universal: Does it conform to universal principles and values?
  • Self: Does it satisfy my personal definition of right, good, and fair?

This model provides a quick checklist to ensure that your decision aligns with organizational standards, legal requirements, ethical principles, and personal values. If the answer to any of these questions is "no," further consideration is needed.

For example, imagine you are offered a financial incentive by a formula company to promote their product to your clients. Applying the PLUS model:

  • Policies: This likely violates the policies of most professional breastfeeding organizations, which discourage conflicts of interest.
  • Legal: There may be legal restrictions on accepting incentives that could influence professional advice.
  • Universal: It conflicts with the ethical principle of providing unbiased, evidence-based information to clients.
  • Self: It likely goes against your personal values of integrity and client-centered care.

Given the negative responses to multiple PLUS criteria, it's clear that accepting the incentive would be unethical.

Considering Consequences

A critical step in any ethical framework is evaluating the potential consequences of each course of action. Consider both the short-term and long-term effects on the client, their family, other professionals, and yourself. Ask yourself:

  • Who will be affected by this decision?
  • What are the potential benefits and harms of each option?
  • What are the likely outcomes, and how certain are they?

Sometimes, the consequences may be difficult to predict. In such cases, it's helpful to consult with colleagues or mentors to gain different perspectives.

Documenting the Process

It's essential to document your ethical decision-making process, including the steps you took, the information you considered, the options you evaluated, and the rationale for your final decision. This documentation serves several purposes:

  • It provides a record of your ethical reasoning, which can be helpful if your decision is questioned later.
  • It demonstrates your commitment to ethical practice.
  • It allows you to reflect on your decision-making process and identify areas for improvement.

Your documentation should be clear, concise, and objective. Avoid emotional language or personal opinions. Focus on the facts and the ethical principles that guided your decision.

Ethical Practice and the L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢

The L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a systematic approach to breastfeeding support, integrating ethical considerations into each step. Specifically, the 'Track & Troubleshoot' and 'Encourage & Empower' steps are critical for ethical practice:

  • Track & Troubleshoot: This step involves continually assessing the client's progress, identifying any challenges, and adjusting the plan of care as needed. Ethically, this ensures that you are providing the most effective and appropriate support, and that you are not causing unintended harm.
  • Encourage & Empower: This step focuses on building the client's confidence, promoting their autonomy, and supporting their decision-making. Ethically, this ensures that you are respecting the client's right to choose and that you are empowering them to make informed decisions about their breastfeeding journey.

By integrating these steps, you can ensure that your practice is not only effective but also ethically sound. Remember, ethical decision-making is an ongoing process, not a one-time event. Continually evaluate and refine your practice to ensure that you are providing the best possible support to breastfeeding families.
