Defining Informed Consent

Informed consent is more than just a signature on a form. It's a process that ensures your clients understand the proposed breastfeeding support plan, its potential benefits, risks, and alternatives. As a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coachâ„¢, you are ethically obligated to provide this information in a clear and understandable manner. Informed consent rests on three pillars: information, comprehension, and voluntariness.

  • Information: Providing all relevant facts about the proposed support, including alternative options and their potential outcomes.
  • Comprehension: Ensuring the client understands the information provided, which may require using plain language and addressing any questions or concerns.
  • Voluntariness: Confirming that the client's decision is made freely, without coercion or undue influence from you or others.

Implementing Informed Consent in Breastfeeding Support

Applying informed consent in breastfeeding support requires a proactive approach. It's not enough to simply present information; you must actively engage with the client to ensure they understand and can make an informed decision. This aligns directly with the 'Collaborate & Customize' step of the L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢, where you work together to create a personalized breastfeeding plan.

For example, when discussing different latch techniques, explain the potential benefits (e.g., improved milk transfer, reduced nipple pain) and potential risks (e.g., temporary discomfort, frustration if the technique doesn't work immediately). Always present alternatives and respect the mother's choice, even if it differs from your recommendation.

Promoting Shared Decision-Making

Shared decision-making is a collaborative process where you and the client work together to make choices about their breastfeeding journey. This approach recognizes the mother as the expert on her own body and values, while you provide evidence-based information and support. It's a cornerstone of ethical practice and aligns perfectly with the 'Acknowledge' and 'Collaborate' stages of the L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢.

Actively solicit the mother's preferences, values, and cultural beliefs. For instance, some cultures may have specific traditions or beliefs about breastfeeding duration or techniques. Respecting these beliefs is crucial for building trust and ensuring the mother feels empowered in her decisions.

Addressing Capacity to Provide Informed Consent

In some situations, a client may lack the capacity to provide informed consent. This could be due to cognitive impairment, severe emotional distress, or the influence of medications. If you suspect a client lacks capacity, it's essential to proceed with caution and seek guidance from a healthcare professional. Document your concerns and the steps you take to address them.

Remember, your role is to support the mother's well-being. If she cannot make informed decisions, involving a trusted family member or healthcare provider can help ensure her best interests are considered. This underscores the importance of the 'Advocate' stage of the L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢.

Case Study: Maria's Informed Choice

Case Study: Maria
Empowering a mother through informed decision-making.

Client: Maria, 42 years old, first-time mother.

Presenting Symptoms: Nipple pain and difficulty with latch.

Intervention: After listening to Maria's concerns (Listen & Learn), the coach assessed her latch (Assess & Acknowledge). The coach then provided Maria with several latching options, explaining the benefits and potential drawbacks of each. Maria expressed concerns about one option due to cultural beliefs. The coach respected her beliefs and focused on alternative strategies that aligned with her values (Collaborate & Customize).

Outcomes: Maria felt empowered to choose a latching technique that felt comfortable and respectful of her cultural background. Her nipple pain decreased, and her confidence in breastfeeding increased (Teach & Train, Encourage & Empower).

Integrating Informed Consent into the L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢

The L.A.C.T.A.T.E. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a structured approach to breastfeeding support, and informed consent should be integrated into each stage. Here's how:

  • Listen & Learn: Actively listen to the mother's concerns, values, and preferences.
  • Assess & Acknowledge: Acknowledge the mother's expertise on her own body and breastfeeding experience.
  • Collaborate & Customize: Work together to create a breastfeeding plan that aligns with the mother's informed choices. This is where the bulk of the informed consent process occurs.
  • Teach & Train: Provide education and training on the chosen techniques, ensuring the mother understands how to implement them safely and effectively.
  • Advocate & Affirm: Advocate for the mother's choices and affirm her ability to make informed decisions.
  • Track & Troubleshoot: Monitor progress and address any challenges, always involving the mother in the decision-making process.
  • Encourage & Empower: Encourage the mother's confidence and empower her to continue making informed choices throughout her breastfeeding journey.
