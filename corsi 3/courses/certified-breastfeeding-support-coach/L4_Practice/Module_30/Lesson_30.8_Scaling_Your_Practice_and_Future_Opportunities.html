Handling Common Objections

Confidently Address Concerns

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. I offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible. Also, consider the cost of formula, potential health issues, and the emotional toll of struggling alone. Investing in my support can actually save you money and stress in the long run."
"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"That's perfectly reasonable. How about I send you a detailed overview of the program that you can share with your partner? We can also schedule a brief call where all of you can ask questions. Would that be helpful?"
"I'm worried I won't have enough time."
"My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy schedule. Each session is tailored to your specific needs, and I provide resources you can access anytime. Plus, successful breastfeeding can actually save you time compared to the constant preparation and cleaning of bottles."
"I'm not sure if it will work for me at my age."
"That's a valid concern, Susan, and one I hear often. I specialize in supporting mothers over 40, and I've seen incredible success stories. My program is tailored to address the unique physiological and emotional aspects of breastfeeding later in life. I can connect you with some of my other clients who have had similar experiences if you'd like."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in the "Welcome & Connection" phase of a discovery call?

Acknowledge the client's feelings, thank them for their time, and briefly reiterate your expertise.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your program?

Offer flexible payment plans, highlight the long-term value and potential cost savings, and emphasize the emotional benefits.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script out loud to build confidence and improve your delivery.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and how it addresses the client's specific needs.
  • Believe in the transformative impact you can have on your clients' lives.
