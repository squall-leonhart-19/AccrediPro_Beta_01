Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Maria, thank you so much for reaching out. I know these early weeks can be challenging. First, congratulations on your little one! How are you doing today, all things considered?"
YOU:
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed. Many new moms do. I'm here to help. Tell me, what prompted you to seek breastfeeding support right now?"
Phase 2: Understand the Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"I understand. Tell me more about the latch. What does it feel like for you? For the baby?"
YOU:
"And how is the weight gain progressing? What did the pediatrician say exactly?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to improve the latch or increase milk supply?"
YOU:
"If things don't improve, what are you most worried about?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Expertise 10 min
YOU:
"Maria, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing [summarize her pain points and validate her feelings]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is holistic and personalized. We'll work together to assess your baby's latch, positioning, and suck. We'll also explore your milk supply, nutrition, and any underlying factors. I'll provide you with practical strategies and ongoing support to help you achieve your breastfeeding goals. Many of my clients see significant improvements within the first week."
YOU:
"I'm a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach and I've helped dozens of moms just like you overcome these challenges."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Maria, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me tell you about my packages and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Let's talk about the value of successful breastfeeding. Formula can cost hundreds of dollars a month. My support can help you avoid those costs and provide your baby with the optimal nutrition. I also offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I think I should just try harder on my own first."
"I admire your determination! However, sometimes we need expert guidance to overcome obstacles. Continuing without proper support could lead to more frustration and potentially impact your milk supply. Let's work together to create a plan that sets you up for success from the start."
"My doctor recommended supplementing, so maybe breastfeeding isn't for me."
"Supplementing can be a temporary solution, but it doesn't have to be the end of your breastfeeding journey. We can work together to address the underlying issues causing the weight loss and potentially reduce or eliminate the need for supplementation over time. Many doctors aren't specialized in lactation support, so I can provide a different perspective."
"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"Of course! I can send you a detailed outline of my services that you can share. Would you also like to schedule a brief call with both of you so I can answer any questions your partner might have?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you address a client's budget concerns without lowering your prices?

Highlight the long-term value of your services, offer payment plans, and focus on the cost savings compared to formula or other interventions.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call can significantly increase your client conversion rate.
  • Practice your pricing presentation until you feel comfortable and confident.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Highlight the value and long-term benefits of your breastfeeding support services.
  • Remember, you are providing a valuable service that empowers mothers and benefits babies.
