1. Developing Your Content Strategy

A content strategy is your roadmap for creating and distributing valuable, relevant, and consistent content to attract and retain a clearly defined audience. It's about understanding your ideal client's needs and providing solutions through your content.

Start by revisiting your niche and target audience from Lesson 1. What are their biggest breastfeeding challenges? What questions do they have? What kind of information are they actively seeking?

Next, align your content with your brand identity, which we established in Lesson 2. Your content should reflect your brand's voice, values, and unique selling proposition. This consistency builds trust and recognition.

Consider these questions:

  • What are the key topics you want to cover?
  • What keywords will you target to attract your ideal client?
  • What is your content calendar? (How often will you publish new content?)
  • How will you measure the success of your content?

2. Exploring Content Formats

Variety is key to keeping your audience engaged. Experiment with different content formats to see what resonates best with your target audience. Here are some popular options:

  • Blog Posts & Articles: Share informative articles on breastfeeding techniques, troubleshooting common issues, and maternal wellness.
  • Videos: Create how-to videos demonstrating latch techniques, pumping tips, or interviews with other experts. Videos generate 1200% more shares than text and images combined (BuzzSumo, 2022).
  • Infographics: Visually appealing graphics that present complex information in an easy-to-understand format.
  • Podcasts: Discuss breastfeeding topics, interview experts, and share personal stories.
  • Social Media Posts: Share tips, quotes, images, and videos on platforms like Instagram and Facebook.

Remember, the best format depends on your audience and your strengths. If you enjoy writing, focus on blog posts and articles. If you're comfortable on camera, videos might be a better choice. You can even repurpose content from one format to another, which we'll discuss later.

3. Optimizing for Readability and Engagement

Creating great content is only half the battle. You also need to ensure it's easy to read and engaging. Here are some tips:

  • Use Clear Headings and Subheadings: Break up your content into logical sections to make it easier to scan.
  • Use Bullet Points and Lists: Present information in a concise and organized way.
  • Incorporate Visuals: Images, videos, and infographics can break up text and make your content more appealing.
  • Write in a Conversational Tone: Imagine you're talking to a friend. Avoid jargon and technical terms.
  • Ask Questions: Encourage interaction and engagement by asking questions throughout your content.

A 2022 study by Nielsen Norman Group found that users only read about 20% of the text on an average web page. Optimizing for readability ensures that your key message gets across, even if readers don't read every word.

4. Promoting Your Content

Once you've created compelling content, it's time to promote it! Here are some effective channels:

  • Social Media: Share your content on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Pinterest. Use relevant hashtags to reach a wider audience. We covered this in detail in Lesson 4.
  • Email Marketing: Send your content to your email list. Email marketing remains highly effective, with an average ROI of $42 for every $1 spent (DMA, 2019).
  • Guest Blogging: Write articles for other websites in your niche to reach a new audience.
  • Networking: Share your content with your referral partners, as discussed in Lesson 5.

Consistency is key. Regularly promoting your content increases its visibility and reach. Consider creating a social media schedule to stay organized.

5. Repurposing Content for Maximum Impact

Don't let your content sit idle! Repurposing existing content into different formats is a great way to maximize its reach and impact. For example:

  • Turn a blog post into a video script.
  • Create an infographic from key statistics in a research article.
  • Record a podcast episode based on a popular social media post.
  • Combine several blog posts into an e-book.

Repurposing saves time and effort while allowing you to reach different audiences and reinforce your message.

Case Study: Maria's Success Story
Turning Content into Clients

Client: Maria Rodriguez, 45, Career Changer

Presenting Situation: Maria, a former teacher, was passionate about breastfeeding support but struggled to attract clients to her new coaching practice.

Intervention: Maria developed a content strategy focused on addressing common breastfeeding challenges. She created blog posts, videos, and infographics on topics like latch difficulties, milk supply issues, and managing breastfeeding while working. She shared her content on social media and through email marketing.

Outcomes: Within six months, Maria's website traffic increased by 300%, and she consistently booked new clients. Her income as a breastfeeding support coach now exceeds her previous teaching salary. By focusing on providing value through her content, Maria established herself as a trusted authority in her niche.
