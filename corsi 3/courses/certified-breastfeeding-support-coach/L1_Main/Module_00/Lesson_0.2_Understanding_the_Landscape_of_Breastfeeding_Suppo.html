The "Can I Actually Make Money?" Conversation

I know what you're thinking: "This sounds amazing, but can I actually make a real income doing this?" It's a valid question, and honestly, it's the first one most people have. You're investing your time, energy, and resources, and you deserve to know what kind of return you can expect.

The answer? Absolutely. But like any rewarding career, it takes dedication, effort, and a strategic approach. This isn't a "get rich quick" scheme, but a pathway to building a sustainable and meaningful business that aligns with your values.

Let's break down the possibilities and see how you can turn your passion for breastfeeding support into a thriving career.

8 Lucrative Career Paths

One of the best things about becoming a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach is the flexibility it offers. You're not limited to one specific role or income stream. Here are eight distinct career paths you can explore:

  1. Private Practice (1-on- 1 Consultations): Offer personalized, in-home or virtual consultations to families. This allows you to build deep relationships and provide tailored support.
  2. Group Classes & Workshops: Host prenatal breastfeeding classes, postpartum support groups, or specialized workshops on topics like pumping, weaning, or returning to work.
  3. Online Programs & Courses: Create and sell online courses or membership programs to reach a wider audience and generate passive income.
  4. Hospitals & Clinics: Partner with hospitals, birthing centers, or pediatric clinics to offer breastfeeding support services to their patients.
  5. Corporate Wellness Programs: Provide breastfeeding education and support to employees of companies that prioritize family-friendly benefits.
  6. Retail & Product Sales: Sell breastfeeding-related products, such as nursing pillows, pumps, or lactation teas, either online or in a physical store.
  7. Writing & Content Creation: Share your expertise through blog posts, articles, ebooks, or social media content, and monetize your platform through advertising or affiliate marketing.
  8. Speaking & Training: Become a sought-after speaker or trainer at conferences, workshops, or professional development events.

Each of these paths offers unique opportunities and income potential. The key is to identify the ones that align with your skills, interests, and lifestyle goals.

Realistic Timeline to Income

Rome wasn't built in a day, and neither is a successful coaching practice. It's important to set realistic expectations and understand the typical timeline for building your business. Here's a general framework:

  1. Month 1-3: Complete your certification, define your niche, create your website and social media presence, and start networking.
  2. Month 3-6: Offer free or discounted services to build your portfolio and gather testimonials, refine your marketing strategy, and start booking paying clients.
  3. Month 6-12: Increase your rates as your experience and reputation grow, diversify your income streams, and focus on building a sustainable business model.
  4. Month 12+: Continue to learn and grow, expand your reach, and explore new opportunities to serve your clients and increase your income.

Remember, this is just a guideline. Your individual timeline may vary depending on your background, resources, and level of commitment. The key is to stay consistent, persistent, and adaptable.

Inspiring Success Stories

Case Study: Maria Rodriguez

Client: Maria Rodriguez, 32 years old

Presenting Situation: Maria, a new mother, was experiencing significant challenges with breastfeeding, including latch difficulties, pain, and low milk supply. She felt overwhelmed and discouraged.

Intervention: Maria engaged in weekly private consultations, totaling 6 sessions. The intervention included hands-on support with latch techniques, guidance on milk supply boosting strategies (including galactagogue herbs and power pumping), and emotional support to address her feelings of overwhelm.

Outcomes: Over the course of the six weeks, Maria's latch improved dramatically, her pain subsided, and her milk supply increased significantly. She was able to breastfeed her baby exclusively for six months and continued breastfeeding with supplemental foods until her baby was one year old. Maria reported feeling empowered, confident, and deeply grateful for the support she received.

Case Study: Sarah Chen

Client: Sarah Chen, 28 years old

Presenting Situation: Sarah, a first-time mother, was returning to work after maternity leave and struggling to balance breastfeeding with her job responsibilities. She was concerned about maintaining her milk supply and finding time to pump during the day.

Intervention: Sarah participated in a "Back to Work Breastfeeding" workshop and received a personalized consultation to develop a pumping schedule and strategies for communicating with her employer. She also joined an online support group for working mothers.

Outcomes: Sarah successfully transitioned back to work while continuing to breastfeed her baby. She felt confident in her ability to manage pumping at work and maintain her milk supply. She also found the online support group to be a valuable source of encouragement and practical advice.

These are just a few examples of the many ways you can make a positive impact on the lives of families while building a successful career as a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach. There are countless women already making a substantial income doing this work, and you can too!

The Math That Makes Sense

Let's get down to the numbers. How much can you realistically earn as a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach? The answer depends on several factors, including your location, experience, services offered, and marketing efforts.

Here's a simplified example:

Let's say you charge $75 per hour for private consultations and see an average of 10 clients per week. That's $750 per week or $3,000 per month. Now, imagine you also host two workshops per month, each generating $500 in revenue. That's an additional $1,000 per month, bringing your total monthly income to $4,000.

Of course, you'll have expenses to consider, such as marketing, insurance, and continuing education. But even after deducting these expenses, you can still earn a comfortable living while making a difference in the lives of families.

According to a 2022 survey of lactation consultants and breastfeeding support professionals, the average hourly rate for private consultations ranged from $60 to $120, with some experienced practitioners charging even more.

The key is to set your rates strategically, based on your value, experience, and market demand. Don't be afraid to charge what you're worth!

Your 90-Day Vision Exercise

Now that you have a better understanding of the possibilities, it's time to start envisioning your own success. Take a moment to complete this 90-day vision exercise:

  1. What are your top three goals for your first 90 days as a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach? (e.g., complete certification, launch website, book five paying clients)
  2. What specific actions will you take each week to achieve these goals? (e.g., dedicate 5 hours to studying, post on social media three times per week, attend one networking event)
  3. What resources do you need to support your success? (e.g., mentor, website template, marketing budget)
  4. How will you measure your progress and celebrate your wins? (e.g., track client bookings, monitor website traffic, reward yourself for reaching milestones)

Write down your answers and keep them visible as a constant reminder of your goals. Refer back to them regularly to stay motivated and on track.
