Your Coaching Scenario

In this Practice Lab, you'll work through a realistic client scenario focused on supporting a new mother through breastfeeding challenges. Read the client profile, review the coaching script, then practice speaking the dialogue out loud.

Maria, 42

Chief Complaint
Painful nipples, unsure if baby is latching correctly, feeling overwhelmed and exhausted.

Since birth, but worsening over the past week.

Lanolin cream, different breastfeeding positions (football hold, cradle hold), online videos.

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for reaching out. I can hear how much you want to make breastfeeding work, and it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed at this stage. Many new moms experience similar challenges, and you're not alone."
MARIA:
"It's just so hard! Everyone makes it sound so natural, but it's been anything but."
YOU:
"I understand. It's a skill that both you and your baby are learning together. Let's focus on making it more comfortable and effective for both of you. Can you tell me more about the pain you're experiencing?"
MARIA:
"It's mostly when he latches on. It's a sharp, stinging pain, and my nipples are really sore afterward."
YOU:
"Okay, that gives us some valuable information. It sounds like we need to assess the latch and positioning. Would you be open to going through a few key steps together to see if we can identify any adjustments that might help?"
MARIA:
"Yes, please! I'm willing to try anything."

‚ùå Jumping to solutions without understanding the root cause.
‚úì Ask clarifying questions: "Can you describe the pain? When does it occur? What have you already tried?"
‚ùå Using technical jargon or overwhelming her with information.
‚úì Use simple, clear language: "Let's check the way your baby is latching on."
‚ùå Making her feel guilty or inadequate if breastfeeding is challenging.

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief and feels heard: "Thank you, I feel so much better just talking to you."
  • She is receptive to your suggestions and guidance.

  • Her body language softens (relaxed shoulders, making eye contact).
  • She says: "I feel more hopeful about breastfeeding now."

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize empathy and validation when working with breastfeeding mothers.
  • Avoid jumping to solutions without fully understanding the client's specific challenges.
  • Use simple, clear language and avoid technical jargon.
  • Reassure clients that they are not alone and that seeking help is a sign of strength.
