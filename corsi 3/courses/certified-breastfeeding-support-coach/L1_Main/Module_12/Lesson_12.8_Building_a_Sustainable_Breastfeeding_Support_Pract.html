Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for reaching out. I understand you're experiencing some challenges with breastfeeding, specifically with latching, nipple pain, and concerns about your milk supply. It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed at this stage. Many new mothers experience similar difficulties."
MARIA:
"Yes, it's been so stressful. I feel like I'm failing."
YOU:
"I want to assure you that you're not failing. Breastfeeding is a learned skill for both you and your baby, and it takes time and patience. It's important to acknowledge that you've already taken steps to address these challenges by trying different positions, using nipple cream, and consulting with a lactation consultant. That shows your commitment."
YOU:
"Before we dive deeper, can you describe the pain you're experiencing? Is it during the latch, throughout the feeding, or both? Also, what makes you concerned about your milk supply?"
MARIA:
"The pain is mostly during the latch, a sharp, stinging pain. And I'm worried because my baby seems fussy after feedings and doesn't always seem satisfied."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. Let's explore some strategies together to improve the latch and address your concerns about milk supply. We'll focus on making breastfeeding a more comfortable and enjoyable experience for both you and your baby. Does that sound good?"

‚ùå Immediately jumping to solutions without understanding her specific situation.

‚ùå Making her feel guilty or inadequate if breastfeeding is challenging.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating a client's feelings builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to your guidance. It shows that you understand their experience and are there to support them.

2. What are some open-ended questions you can ask to understand a client's breastfeeding challenges?

Examples include: "Can you describe the pain you're experiencing?", "What makes you concerned about your milk supply?", "What have you already tried to address these challenges?"

Key Takeaways

  • Validate the client's feelings and acknowledge their pain or concerns.
  • Ask open-ended questions to gather information and understand their specific situation.
  • Emphasize that breastfeeding is a learned skill and it's okay to seek support.
  • Provide clear, concise explanations and focus on practical strategies.
  • Recognize the client's previous efforts and acknowledge their commitment.
