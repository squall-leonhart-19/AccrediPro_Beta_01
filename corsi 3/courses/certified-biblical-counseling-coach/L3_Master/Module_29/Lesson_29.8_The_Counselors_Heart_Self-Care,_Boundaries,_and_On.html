The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 48, who is struggling with grief after losing her mother. I've been praying with her and encouraging her to read scripture, but she still seems really down. She says she feels guilty for not being a better daughter. I'm not sure how to help her process this."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah explored the specifics of Emily's relationship with her mother?
  • Is Sarah aware of the stages of grief and how they manifest differently?
  • Is Sarah integrating practical counseling techniques with her spiritual guidance?
  • Is there a risk of spiritual bypassing?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Her Efforts

Acknowledge Sarah's compassion and willingness to integrate faith into her practice. Remind her that building trust is crucial.

2

Teach the Importance of Exploration

Explain that grief is complex and requires a thorough understanding of the client's experience. Encourage her to ask specific questions about Emily's relationship with her mother.

3

Introduce Grief Counseling Techniques

Share practical techniques like active listening, validation of feelings, and cognitive reframing to help Emily process her grief. Refer to Module 27 on Trauma and Grief.

4

Address Spiritual Bypassing

Help Sarah recognize the potential for spiritual bypassing (using spirituality to avoid painful emotions). Emphasize the importance of allowing Emily to fully experience her grief while offering scriptural support.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about Emily. Your desire to help her find peace is admirable. Let's explore some ways to deepen your approach."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What have you noticed about Emily's emotions? What specific details about her relationship with her mother have stood out to you?"
Introduce New Concepts
"Sometimes, grief can be intertwined with guilt and unresolved issues. Exploring these areas can be incredibly helpful. Have you considered using techniques like 'empty chair' or 'memory journaling'?"
Reinforce Biblical Integration
"How can you integrate scripture in a way that comforts Emily without minimizing her pain? Perhaps focusing on God's grace and forgiveness could be beneficial."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is spiritual bypassing, and how can you help a mentee recognize it in their counseling practice?

Spiritual bypassing is using spiritual practices to avoid dealing with painful emotions or unresolved issues. Help your mentee recognize it by encouraging them to explore clients' emotions thoroughly and avoid prematurely offering spiritual solutions.

2. How can you encourage a new practitioner to integrate faith and evidence-based techniques effectively?

Emphasize the importance of understanding both the client's spiritual beliefs and psychological needs. Encourage them to use techniques that validate emotions, promote healing, and align with biblical principles.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves validating the mentee's efforts and building their confidence.
  • Encourage mentees to explore client cases thoroughly and ask specific questions.
  • Introduce practical counseling techniques that can be integrated with spiritual guidance.
  • Help mentees recognize and address spiritual bypassing in their practice.
  • Provide ongoing support and resources to help mentees grow and develop their skills.
