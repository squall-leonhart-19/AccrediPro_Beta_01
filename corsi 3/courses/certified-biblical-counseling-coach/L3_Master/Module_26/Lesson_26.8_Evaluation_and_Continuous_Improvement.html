The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, a 48-year-old woman, who is struggling with anxiety. She constantly emails and texts me between sessions, seeking reassurance that she's on the right track. I'm not sure how to handle it without enabling her anxiety."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah established clear boundaries with Emily?
  • Is Sarah reinforcing Emily's anxiety by responding immediately?
  • Is Sarah teaching Emily coping mechanisms to manage her anxiety?
  • Is there a spiritual component to Emily's anxiety that needs to be addressed?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Establish Clear Boundaries

Discuss the importance of setting boundaries to protect both Sarah's time and Emily's progress. Explain that constant reassurance can hinder Emily's ability to develop self-reliance.

2

Review the TRUST Model

Walk Sarah through the TRUST model, emphasizing the "Transformative Action" phase. Encourage her to equip Emily with practical tools and strategies to manage her anxiety independently.

3

Biblical Application

Guide Sarah to explore relevant Scripture passages that address anxiety, such as Philippians 4:6-7. Discuss how to integrate these verses into her coaching sessions with Emily.

4

Self-Care for the Coach

Remind Sarah of the importance of self-care to prevent burnout. Encourage her to set aside time for personal reflection, prayer, and rest.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Effort
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about Emily, and it's wonderful that you're so attentive. Your empathy is a real strength!"
Address the Core Issue
"Let's talk about boundaries. I know it can feel unkind to not respond immediately, but sometimes the most loving thing we can do is empower our clients to find their own answers."
Offer Practical Solutions
"How about suggesting to Emily that she journal her anxious thoughts between sessions, and then bring them up during your meeting? This can help her process her feelings and reduce her reliance on immediate reassurance."
Affirm Her Calling
"You have a gift for biblical counseling, Sarah. Trust your instincts, rely on Scripture, and remember that you're equipping Emily to draw closer to God, not just to you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of supervision in biblical coaching?

The primary goal is to equip new coaches with the skills, knowledge, and confidence to effectively guide their clients toward spiritual and emotional well-being, while adhering to biblical principles.

2. Why are boundaries important in the coach-client relationship?

Boundaries protect both the coach and the client. They prevent burnout for the coach and encourage the client to develop self-reliance and independence.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a critical component of training new biblical counseling coaches.
  • Setting clear boundaries is essential for both the coach and the client.
  • The TRUST model provides a framework for transformative action in coaching.
  • Biblical principles should be integrated into all aspects of coaching.
  • Self-care is vital for preventing burnout and maintaining effectiveness.
