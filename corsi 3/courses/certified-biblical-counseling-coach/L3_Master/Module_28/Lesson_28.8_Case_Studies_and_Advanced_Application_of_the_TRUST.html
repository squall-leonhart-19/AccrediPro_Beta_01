Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Safety and Assessment

Emphasize the importance of assessing for domestic violence, suicidal ideation, and other immediate risks. Safety is paramount.

2

Trauma-Informed Approach

Explain the need for a trauma-informed approach, even with hints of past trauma. Discuss the potential for re-traumatization if not handled sensitively.

3

Balancing Scripture and Emotional Support

Guide Sarah to understand when direct application of Scripture may not be the most helpful approach. Sometimes, clients need emotional validation and support first.

4

Case Conceptualization

Help Sarah develop a comprehensive case conceptualization that integrates Emily's anxiety, marital issues, and potential trauma history. Encourage her to consider underlying factors.

5

Referral Resources

Discuss when and how to make appropriate referrals to therapists, psychiatrists, or other professionals who can provide specialized care.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, thank you for bringing this case to supervision. It shows your commitment to providing the best possible care for your clients. I appreciate your honesty and willingness to learn."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I share my thoughts, what are your initial reflections on this case? What feels most challenging to you?"
Introduce Key Principles
"In cases like this, it's crucial to prioritize safety and assessment. Have you explored any potential risks, such as domestic violence or suicidal ideation?"
Offer Guidance on Trauma
"When a client hints at past trauma, it's important to proceed with caution. Consider saying something like, 'Thank you for sharing that. We can explore this further when you feel ready, but for now, let‚Äôs focus on your immediate needs.'"
Suggest Alternative Approaches
"Instead of directly applying Scripture, try validating Emily's emotions first. You could say, 'It sounds like you're going through a really difficult time. I'm here to listen and support you.'"
Empower Next Steps
"Based on our discussion, what adjustments would you like to make in your approach to Emily? I'm here to support you every step of the way."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the first priority when a mentee presents a complex case involving potential trauma?

Assessing for immediate safety and risk factors, such as domestic violence or suicidal ideation.

2. How can you best support a mentee who is struggling to balance scriptural guidance with a client's emotional needs?

Encourage them to validate the client's emotions and provide a safe space for expression before directly applying Scripture.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize safety and assessment in complex cases.
  • Adopt a trauma-informed approach when appropriate.
  • Balance scriptural guidance with emotional support.
  • Encourage self-reflection and critical thinking in your mentees.
  • Maintain ethical boundaries and provide ongoing support.
