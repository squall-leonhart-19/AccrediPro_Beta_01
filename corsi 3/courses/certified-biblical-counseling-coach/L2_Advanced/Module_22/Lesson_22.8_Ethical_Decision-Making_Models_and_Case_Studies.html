Eleanor, 48

Presenting Issue
Eleanor seeks biblical counseling for marital discord and feelings of resentment towards her husband, who recently experienced a significant career setback. She also reports struggling with anxiety and insomnia.
Relevant History
Eleanor's husband, David, is a respected elder in their church. Eleanor volunteers in the children's ministry. You also know David casually through church events.
Additional Information
During the third session, Eleanor reveals she has been having an emotional affair with a coworker and feels immense guilt. She asks you to keep this information confidential from her husband.
Spiritual Background
Both Eleanor and David are active members of a conservative evangelical church and hold traditional views on marriage and gender roles.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Considerations

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is the conflict between maintaining confidentiality and the potential harm to the marital relationship. Eleanor's request to keep the affair secret creates a direct conflict with the biblical principle of honesty and transparency in marriage. Additionally, your existing relationship with David introduces a dual relationship concern.
Step 2: Consult Ethical Codes
Review the ethical guidelines of your certifying body (e.g., American Association of Christian Counselors). These codes typically address confidentiality, dual relationships, and the counselor's responsibility to promote the well-being of the client and, potentially, the family unit. AACC Code of Ethics, Section IV, addresses marital and family relationships, emphasizing the counselor's responsibility to consider the welfare of all parties involved.
Step 3: Consider Legal Implications
While biblical counseling is not typically subject to the same legal regulations as licensed therapy, be aware of any relevant state laws regarding mandated reporting (e.g., child abuse, threats of violence). In Texas, where Eleanor resides, there is no legal mandate to report infidelity. However, failing to address potential harm could expose you to liability.
Step 4: Explore Potential Courses of Action
Several options exist: (a) encourage Eleanor to disclose the affair to David, (b) inform Eleanor that you cannot continue counseling if she maintains the secrecy, (c) seek consultation with a seasoned biblical counselor for guidance, or (d) refer Eleanor to a licensed therapist specializing in marital issues.

Case Study: Sarah, 52

Sarah, a 52-year-old woman, sought counseling for feelings of loneliness and isolation after her children left home. During sessions, she developed a strong attachment to you, viewing you as a confidante and friend. She began offering you gifts and inviting you to social events. Recognizing the potential for a boundary violation, you gently but firmly declined the gifts and invitations, explaining the importance of maintaining professional boundaries. You reframed the therapeutic relationship, emphasizing your role as a counselor rather than a friend. Sarah initially felt hurt but eventually understood and appreciated your ethical stance. This case highlights the importance of proactively addressing potential dual relationships, particularly with clients who may be vulnerable or seeking connection.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the primary ethical considerations when a client requests confidentiality regarding an extramarital affair?

The primary ethical considerations include the duty to maintain confidentiality, the potential harm to the marital relationship, and the counselor's responsibility to promote honesty and transparency. Balancing these competing interests requires careful consideration and consultation.

2. How should a biblical counselor navigate a dual relationship when the client's spouse is also known to them within a church setting?

The counselor should acknowledge the dual relationship and its potential impact on objectivity and impartiality. Transparency with both parties is crucial. Depending on the circumstances, referral to another counselor may be necessary to avoid conflicts of interest.

3. What steps should a counselor take if a client discloses information that suggests potential abuse or illegal activity?

The counselor must be aware of any mandated reporting laws in their jurisdiction. If there is reasonable suspicion of abuse or illegal activity, the counselor has a legal and ethical obligation to report it to the appropriate authorities, while also informing the client of this action.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are inherent in biblical counseling, particularly when dealing with complex marital issues.
  • Maintaining confidentiality is crucial, but it must be balanced with the responsibility to promote the well-being of all parties involved.
  • Dual relationships can compromise objectivity and should be carefully managed or avoided.
  • Consultation with seasoned counselors and adherence to ethical codes are essential for navigating challenging situations.
  • Knowing when to refer a client to a licensed therapist or other professional is a sign of ethical competence.
