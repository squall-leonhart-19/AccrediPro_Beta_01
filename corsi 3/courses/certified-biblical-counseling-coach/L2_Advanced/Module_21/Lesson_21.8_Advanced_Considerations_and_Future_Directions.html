Clinical Reasoning Process

Applying the TRUST Model

Step 1: Truthful Assessment
Eleanor presents with a complex interplay of anxiety, trauma history, lifestyle factors, and spiritual distress. The PPI use is likely contributing to nutrient deficiencies, exacerbating her symptoms. We need to assess the severity of her childhood trauma and its ongoing impact on her emotional and spiritual well-being. A thorough assessment of her marriage is also critical.
Step 2: Relational Understanding
Building rapport and creating a safe space for Eleanor to share her struggles is paramount. Her feelings of emptiness and disconnection suggest a deep longing for connection and meaning. Exploring her relationship with her husband and children will provide valuable insights into her relational dynamics. Her history of emotional neglect may make it difficult for her to trust others.
Step 3: Unveiling Beliefs
Eleanor's questioning faith and struggles with guilt suggest underlying beliefs that need to be explored. Is she holding onto unforgiveness towards herself or others? Does she believe she is unworthy of God's love? Identifying and challenging these limiting beliefs is crucial for her healing.
Step 4: Scriptural Guidance
Integrating relevant scriptures that speak to God's love, forgiveness, and healing can provide comfort and guidance. Exploring biblical principles related to healthy relationships, self-care, and finding purpose can also be beneficial. However, itâ€™s important to approach this with sensitivity and avoid imposing beliefs.
Step 5: Transformative Action
The treatment plan should include practical steps Eleanor can take to address her anxiety, improve her relationships, and reconnect with her faith. This may involve lifestyle changes, trauma-informed therapy, marriage counseling, and spiritual practices. The goal is to empower her to take ownership of her healing journey.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Unresolved Trauma

Childhood emotional neglect can have long-lasting effects on emotional regulation and attachment. Complex PTSD should be considered.

2

Hormonal Imbalance

At age 48, perimenopause could be contributing to her anxiety and insomnia. Consider hormone testing.

3

Relationship Issues

The strained marriage could be a significant source of stress and emotional pain. Assess for communication problems, infidelity, or other underlying issues.

4

Spiritual Crisis

Her questioning faith could be a sign of a deeper spiritual crisis or disillusionment. Explore her beliefs and values without judgment.

When to Refer

Red Flags â€” Refer to MD/Mental Health Professional

  • Suicidal ideation or self-harm: Immediate mental health intervention is required.
  • Severe depression or psychosis: Requires psychiatric evaluation and management.
  • Substance abuse: Refer to addiction specialist or treatment program.
  • Medical conditions requiring specialized care: Refer to appropriate medical specialist (e.g., endocrinologist for hormone imbalances).
  • Worsening anxiety despite interventions: May require medication adjustment or alternative therapies.

Case Study: Eleanor's Journey

Eleanor, a 48-year-old marketing executive, initially presented with chronic anxiety and spiritual disconnection. After 12 weeks of integrated biblical counseling, including trauma-informed therapy and lifestyle modifications, Eleanor reported a 60% reduction in anxiety symptoms (measured by GAD-7). Her marital satisfaction improved (assessed by ENRICH Marital Satisfaction Scale) by 45%. She also reported a renewed sense of purpose and connection with her faith community. This case highlights the effectiveness of a holistic approach that addresses the interconnectedness of mind, body, and spirit.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when addressing Eleanor's PPI use?

PPIs can impair nutrient absorption (B12, magnesium), potentially exacerbating anxiety and other symptoms. Work with her physician to explore weaning off, while addressing underlying GERD causes with dietary changes and lifestyle modifications.

2. How can you integrate Eleanor's faith into the treatment plan without imposing your own beliefs?

Create a safe space for her to explore her own beliefs and values. Use scripture and spiritual practices as resources, but avoid judgment or pressure. Focus on helping her find meaning and connection in her own way.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a holistic approach that addresses the interconnectedness of mind, body, and spirit.
  • The TRUST model provides a framework for integrating spiritual principles into counseling.
  • Trauma-informed care is essential when working with clients who have a history of childhood trauma.
  • Lifestyle modifications and nutrient support can play a significant role in improving mental and emotional well-being.
  • Knowing when to refer to other professionals is crucial for ethical and effective practice.
