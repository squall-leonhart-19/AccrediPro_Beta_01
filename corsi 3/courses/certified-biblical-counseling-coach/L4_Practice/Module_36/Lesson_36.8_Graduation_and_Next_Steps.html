Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you saw my ad on Facebook. What specifically caught your eye?"
YOU:
"That's interesting. Many of my clients are in a similar stage of life. What are you hoping to achieve in our time together?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about feeling 'lost.' What does that look like in your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to find your purpose? What resonated, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you continue on this path, where do you see yourself in a year? If you could design your ideal life, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, you're seeking clarity and purpose after retirement, and you want to make a difference. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is designed to help you uncover your God-given talents and passions and align them with a meaningful purpose. Over 12 weeks, we'll identify your core values, explore potential avenues for impact, and create a step-by-step action plan. Many of my clients find a renewed sense of excitement and direction within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me share more about the program details and how we can begin."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Time is valuable. This program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. It requires a commitment of a few hours per week, but the return on investment in terms of clarity and direction is significant. Can we explore how to carve out that time?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specific aspects are you considering? Is it the program structure, the investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have complete clarity before making a decision."
"I'm not sure it's worth the money."
"I hear you. Investing in yourself is a big decision. Think about the cost of staying where you are ‚Äì feeling unfulfilled and without purpose. What is it worth to you to find that excitement and make a meaningful impact? This program provides a structured path to achieve that."
"I'm not tech-savvy."
"No worries at all! Our sessions are conducted via Zoom, which is very user-friendly, and I provide step-by-step instructions. Plus, I offer tech support if you need it. You don't need to be a computer expert to benefit from this program."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask what specific aspects they are considering to address their concerns and provide clarity.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and motivations is key to a successful call.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and handling objections are essential skills.
  • Practice your script and closing lines to build confidence and improve your conversion rate.
