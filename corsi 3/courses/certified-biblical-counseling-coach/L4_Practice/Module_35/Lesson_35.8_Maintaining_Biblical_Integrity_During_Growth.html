Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Grace, and I'm excited to chat with you. To start, what caught your eye about my ad?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! So, Susan, tell me a bit about what's been going on for you since retirement and what brought you to seek out a Biblical Counseling Coach?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"I hear you're feeling a bit lost. Can you describe that feeling more? What does a typical day look like, and what aspects are causing the most anxiety?"
YOU:
"Have you tried anything to address these feelings of anxiety and lack of purpose? What was helpful, and what wasn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like six months from now? What would you be doing, and how would you be feeling?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking clarity, purpose, and a reduction in anxiety as you navigate this new chapter. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is rooted in Biblical principles and practical coaching techniques. We'll work together over 12 weeks to identify your God-given strengths, uncover limiting beliefs, and develop a personalized action plan for a fulfilling life. Many of my clients experience significant shifts in their mindset and a renewed sense of purpose within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"Specifically, we'll use the TRUST Blueprint to ensure that our coaching process is targeted and effective."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you and what you're looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. It's an investment of your time. However, think about the time you're currently spending feeling lost and anxious. This program is designed to help you reclaim that time and energy, creating more space for the things you truly value. Plus, the weekly sessions are designed to fit into your schedule, and you'll have access to resources and support in between sessions."
"I need to pray about it."
"That's a wise approach, Susan. I encourage you to seek God's guidance. May I ask, as you pray, is there anything specific you're seeking clarity on? Understanding your concerns will help me provide you with any additional information you need to make an informed decision."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. I can assure you that my approach is highly personalized and tailored to your unique needs and goals. We'll work collaboratively, and I'll provide you with the tools, support, and accountability you need to succeed. Plus, I have several client testimonials I can share with you that are from women in similar life stages."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the discovery call structure outlined in this lesson?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How can you address the objection, "I'm not sure if I have the time for this"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the program as a way to reclaim wasted time and energy. Emphasize the flexibility of the program and the support available between sessions.

3. What is one potential closing line you can use to encourage a prospectto commit to your coaching program?

"Based on our conversation, I truly believe I can help you rediscover your passion and purpose. Are you ready to take the next step and commit to creating a fulfilling life?"

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls into four key phases: Rapport, Problem, Approach, and Close.
  • Prepare effective responses to common objections, such as time constraints or uncertainty about results.
  • Practice presenting your pricing with confidence, clearly articulating the value of your program.
  • Use a compelling call-to-action to encourage prospects to commit to your coaching program.
  • Remember that consistent practice and a genuine desire to help others are essential for building a thriving coaching practice.
