Your Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's Grace here. Thank you for scheduling this call! I appreciate you taking the time. I'm so glad you enjoyed the podcast. What resonated with you most?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! So, Susan, tell me a bit more about what prompted you to reach out for Biblical Counseling at this stage in your life?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about this feeling of being 'lost.' What does that look like on a daily basis? How is it impacting your relationships and your sense of self?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to address these feelings? Have you explored other counseling methods, self-help resources, or spiritual practices?"
YOU:
"If you could paint a picture of your ideal life, say six months from now, what would that look like? What would be different? How would you *feel*?"
Phase 3: Introduce Your Biblical Counseling Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing so openly. It sounds like you're experiencing a significant transition and a desire to reconnect with your purpose, rooted in your faith. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My approach to Biblical Counseling is different because we integrate evidence-based counseling techniques with timeless Scriptural principles. We'll work together to uncover limiting beliefs, identify your God-given strengths, and develop a personalized plan for moving forward with clarity and confidence. Many of my clients report feeling a renewed sense of hope and direction within just a few weeks."
Phase 4: Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I genuinely believe Biblical Counseling could be a powerful tool for you. Does this approach resonate with what you're looking for? ... Excellent! Let's discuss the program details and how we can get started."

Navigating Objections

Common Concerns & Empowered Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time commitment right now."
"I understand completely, Susan. Time is precious. To clarify, the program requires approximately 2 hours per week, including our session and personal reflection. If you could carve out those two hours, imagine how much more productive and fulfilling the rest of your week could be with renewed clarity and purpose. Is there anything specific about the time commitment that concerns you?"
"I've tried counseling before, and it didn't really work for me."
"Thank you for sharing that, Susan. It's important to acknowledge past experiences. Biblical Counseling is unique in that it integrates your faith and values into the process. We're not just addressing surface-level issues; we're seeking God's wisdom and guidance for transformational change. What aspects of your previous counseling experiences didn't resonate with you? Perhaps we can address those directly."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"That's a fair question, Susan. While I can't *guarantee* specific outcomes, I can promise to provide you with the tools, support, and guidance you need to create lasting change. My clients who fully engage with the program and actively apply the principles we discuss consistently report significant improvements in their well-being, relationships, and sense of purpose. Ultimately, the results depend on your willingness to invest in yourself and embrace the process."
"I need to talk to my family about it."
"Absolutely, Susan. It's important to involve your loved ones in your decision. Would it be helpful if I provided you with some information you can share with them? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call where they can ask any questions they may have? I want to ensure everyone feels comfortable and supported."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's needs, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the long-term benefits and potential ROI, such as improved well-being and relationships.

Key Takeaways

  • Confidence is key: Practice your script and pricing to present yourself as a valuable guide.
  • Listen actively: Understand your prospect's needs and tailor your approach accordingly.
  • Address objections: Prepare thoughtful responses to common concerns.
  • Visualize success: Believe in your ability to create a thriving practice.
