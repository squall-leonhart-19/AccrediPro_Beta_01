Case Study: Sarah, a 32-Year-Old Woman Experiencing Grief

Client: Sarah, 32 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah presented with symptoms of prolonged grief following the death of her mother six months prior. She reported feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and difficulty engaging in daily activities. Sarah also expressed feelings of isolation and a lack of support.

Intervention: The practitioner applied the T.R.U.S.T. Frameworkâ„¢ to guide the intervention:

  • Trust: The practitioner established a trusting therapeutic relationship by demonstrating empathy, active listening, and unconditional positive regard. This involved creating a safe space for Sarah to share her feelings and experiences without judgment.
  • Resilience: The practitioner helped Sarah identify her strengths and coping mechanisms. They explored past experiences of resilience and developed strategies for managing her grief, such as journaling, mindfulness exercises, and engaging in self-care activities.
  • Understanding: The practitioner explored Sarah's beliefs about grief and loss, as well as her cultural and spiritual background. They helped Sarah understand the normal grieving process and identify any maladaptive thought patterns or behaviors.
  • Support: The practitioner connected Sarah with a grief support group and encouraged her to seek support from family and friends. They also provided ongoing emotional support and guidance during therapy sessions.
  • Transformation: The practitioner helped Sarah reframe her experience of loss and find meaning in her grief. They explored ways for Sarah to honor her mother's memory and integrate her loss into her life narrative.

Outcomes: After several months of therapy, Sarah reported a significant reduction in her symptoms of grief. She was able to engage in daily activities, connect with others, and find meaning in her loss. Sarah also reported feeling more resilient and hopeful about the future.
