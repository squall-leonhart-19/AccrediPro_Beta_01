Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing such vulnerability. It takes courage to admit when we feel 'stuck', especially when we're outwardly successful. I want you to know that it's absolutely possible to rediscover that spark and create a life that feels deeply fulfilling again."
SUSAN:
"I really hope so. I feel guilty even saying this, because I know I have so much to be grateful for."
YOU:
"That's a very common feeling, Susan. Gratitude and a desire for more aren't mutually exclusive. In fact, acknowledging both can be a powerful catalyst for change. Let's start by exploring what 'fulfillment' truly means to you, beyond your roles as a teacher and caregiver."
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared about your values - faith, family, creativity, learning, and community - I'm curious, if you could wave a magic wand, what would your ideal week look like?"
SUSAN:
"Oh, wow. I haven't even let myself dream like that in years..."
YOU:
"That's perfectly okay. We'll take it one step at a time. No pressure, just pure brainstorming. What activities or experiences would nourish your soul?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Susan says, "That's a great question. I need to think about that." (Engagement!)
  • She starts brainstorming possibilities, even small ones.
  • Her voice softens and she sounds more hopeful.
  • She expresses relief: "It's nice to finally talk about this."
  • She's willing to schedule a follow-up session.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Susan's feelings of being "stuck" before offering solutions?

Validating her feelings builds trust and rapport. It shows you understand her experience and creates a safe space for her to explore her emotions without judgment.

2. How does connecting Susan's desires to her core values help in the coaching process?

Connecting her desires to her core values provides a deeper sense of purpose and meaning. It helps her make decisions that are aligned with what truly matters to her, leading to more sustainable and fulfilling outcomes.

Key Takeaways

  • Validate the client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Connect their desires to their core values.
  • Allow space for reflection and brainstorming.
  • Encourage them to dream big, even if it feels scary.
  • Celebrate small wins and progress along the way.
