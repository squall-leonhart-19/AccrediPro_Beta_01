Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to admit you're feeling lost, especially after dedicating so much of your life to others. Many women experience this transition, and it's a powerful opportunity for rediscovery."
DIANE:
"It's just... I feel like I'm supposed to have it all figured out by now. Everyone else seems to know what they're doing."
YOU:
"That's a common misconception, Diane. In my practice, I've seen that most people are also figuring it out as they go. What's important is that you're actively seeking something more, and that's a fantastic starting point."
YOU:
"Let's explore what truly resonates with you, beyond the expectations of others. We'll tap into your core values and passions to create a vision for your next chapter. How does that sound?"
DIANE:
"That sounds... hopeful. I'm just afraid I won't find anything that clicks."
YOU:
"I understand that fear, Diane. But I believe that with focused exploration and a willingness to try new things, you'll uncover passions you never knew existed. Are you ready to embark on that journey?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's feelings at the start of the coaching session?

Validating her feelings builds trust and creates a safe space for her to share vulnerabilities. It shows that you understand and acknowledge her experience, which is crucial for establishing a strong coaching relationship.

2. What's the key difference between imposing your own values and helping Diane discover her own?

Imposing your own values means telling Diane what you think she *should* want or do. Helping her discover her own values involves asking questions and guiding her to identify what's truly important to *her*, regardless of external pressures or expectations.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation is crucial for building trust and rapport.
  • Focus on the client's values, not your own.
  • Ask open-ended questions to encourage self-discovery.
  • Create a safe space for vulnerability and exploration.
  • Emphasize hope and possibility for the future.
