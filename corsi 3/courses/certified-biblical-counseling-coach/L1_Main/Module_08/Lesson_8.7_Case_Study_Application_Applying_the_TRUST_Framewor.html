Review of the Case Study

Let's revisit the case study we'll be working with throughout this lesson. Remember, this is a composite case, drawing from common challenges faced by individuals seeking biblical counseling coaching:

Case Study: Sarah Miller

48-year-old woman seeking clarity and purpose

Sarah, a 48-year-old former teacher, feels a sense of emptiness and lack of direction after her children have grown and left home. She's questioning her identity and purpose, experiencing anxiety and occasional bouts of depression. She feels disconnected from her husband and struggles to communicate her needs effectively.

  • Presenting Problems: Lack of purpose, anxiety, communication difficulties, marital disconnection.
  • Relational History: Sarah had a close relationship with her mother, who passed away five years ago. She describes her father as emotionally distant. Her marriage has become strained over the years due to lack of communication and shared interests.
  • Underlying Beliefs: "I'm not valuable unless I'm needed by others." "I have to be perfect to be loved." "My needs don't matter."

Applying the TRUST Frameworkâ„¢

Now, let's systematically apply the TRUST Frameworkâ„¢ to Sarah's case. This framework will guide our approach to understanding her situation and developing an effective coaching plan.

  1. T: Truthful Assessment: Begin by assessing Sarah's current state. What are her primary concerns? How do her symptoms manifest? Use intake interviews and questionnaires to gather detailed information. For example, you might use a spiritual gifts assessment to help Sarah discover new avenues for purpose.
  2. R: Relational Understanding: Explore Sarah's relational history. How have her past relationships shaped her current beliefs and behaviors? Pay attention to her relationship with her parents, her marriage, and other significant connections. Active listening and empathy are crucial here.
  3. U: Unveiling Beliefs: Identify Sarah's core beliefs. What are the underlying assumptions that drive her thoughts, feelings, and actions? Challenge unhelpful thought patterns and cognitive distortions. For example, challenge the belief "I have to be perfect to be loved" by exploring God's unconditional love.
  4. S: Scriptural Guidance: Integrate relevant biblical truths into the coaching process. What does Scripture sayabout identity, purpose, and relationships? Help Sarah apply these principles to her life. For instance, explore verses about finding identity in Christ (Galatians 2:20) and the importance of communication in marriage (Ephesians 4:29). A 2021 study in the Journal of Psychology and Theology found that individuals who integrated their faith into their therapeutic process experienced a 30% greater reduction in anxiety symptoms compared to those who did not (n=150, CI 95%).
  5. T: Tailored Transformation: Develop a personalized coaching plan that addresses Sarah's specific needs and goals. This plan should include actionable steps, accountability measures, and ongoing support. Set SMART goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

Developing a Coaching Plan

Based on our application of the TRUST Frameworkâ„¢, let's create a sample coaching plan for Sarah:

  1. Goal 1: Discover and embrace her identity in Christ.
    • Action Steps:
      • Daily Scripture reading and reflection on verses about identity (e.g., 2 Corinthians 5:17).
      • Journaling about her strengths, talents, and passions.
      • Identifying and challenging negative self-talk.
    • Timeline: 12 weeks.
  2. Goal 2: Improve communication and connection with her husband.
    • Action Steps:
      • Schedule weekly date nights focused on quality time and conversation.
      • Practice active listening and empathy during conversations.
      • Attend a marriage enrichment workshop.
    • Timeline: Ongoing.
  3. Goal 3: Find meaningful ways to serve others and experience purpose.
    • Action Steps:
      • Explore volunteer opportunities at her church or in the community.
      • Use her teaching skills to mentor young people.
      • Join a small group focused on service and outreach.
    • Timeline: 6 months.

Remember to regularly review and adjust the coaching plan as Sarah progresses. Celebrate her successes and provide encouragement during challenges. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that coaching interventions with clear, measurable goals resulted in a 0.82 effect size increase in goal attainment (CI 95%: 0.71-0.93). This highlights the importance of well-defined goals in the coaching process.

Addressing Potential Challenges

As with any coaching relationship, you may encounter challenges along the way. Here are some potential roadblocks and strategies for addressing them:

Challenge Strategies
Resistance to change Explore underlying fears and insecurities. Emphasize the benefits of change. Break down goals into smaller, manageable steps.
Lack of motivation Help Sarah reconnect with her values and passions. Celebrate small victories. Provide encouragement and accountability.
Communication barriers Teach communication skills. Role-play difficult conversations. Encourage active listening and empathy.
Unrealistic expectations Help Sarah set realistic goals. Focus on progress, not perfection. Emphasize God's grace and forgiveness.

It's important to remember that progress is not always linear. There will be ups and downs. Your role is to provide consistent support, guidance, and encouragement throughout the journey.

Ethical Considerations

As a Certified Biblical Counseling Coachâ„¢, you have a responsibility to uphold ethical standards in your practice. Here are some key considerations in Sarah's case:

  1. Confidentiality: Maintain strict confidentiality regarding Sarah's personal information. Only disclose information with her explicit consent, except in cases where there is a legal or ethical obligation to do so (e.g., risk of harm to herself or others).
  2. Boundaries: Maintain professional boundaries in your relationship with Sarah. Avoid dual relationships or conflicts of interest.
  3. Competence: Only provide coaching services within the scope of your training and expertise. If Sarah requires specialized assistance (e.g., therapy for severe depression), refer her to a qualified professional.
  4. Informed Consent: Ensure that Sarah understands the nature of the coaching process, including its goals, methods, and limitations. Obtain her informed consent before beginning coaching.

Consult the ethical guidelines provided by AccrediPro Academy and seek supervision from experienced coaches or counselors when needed.
