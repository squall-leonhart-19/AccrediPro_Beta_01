Case Studies

Case Study: Sarah, 34, Seeking Guidance in Career Transition

Client: Sarah, a 34-year-old woman, presented with feelings of anxiety and uncertainty regarding a potential career transition. She felt called to leave her corporate job to pursue a ministry-focused role but struggled with fear and self-doubt.

Presenting Symptoms: Anxiety, indecisiveness, lack of confidence, and spiritual questioning.

Intervention: The assessment process involved exploring Sarah's past experiences, her spiritual gifts, her values, and her fears. Using open-ended questions and active listening, the counselor helped Sarah identify her core beliefs and how they were influencing her decision-making. The counselor also used biblical principles to address Sarah's fears and encourage her to trust in God's guidance.

Outcomes: Sarah gained clarity about her calling and developed a plan for transitioning into her new role. She reported a significant decrease in anxiety and an increase in confidence. She also experienced a deeper sense of connection with God and a renewed sense of purpose.

Case Study: Emily, 45, Struggling with Marital Conflict

Client: Emily, a 45-year-old woman, sought counseling due to ongoing conflict with her husband. She reported feeling unheard, unloved, and resentful.

Presenting Symptoms: Frequent arguments, emotional distance, communication breakdown, and feelings of resentment.

Intervention: The initial assessment focused on understanding the dynamics of Emily's marriage, including communication patterns, conflict resolution styles, and underlying needs. The counselor used assessment tools to identify areas of strength and weakness in the relationship. The counselor also explored Emily's past experiences and how they were influencing her present relationship.

Outcomes: Emily gained a better understanding of her own role in the conflict and developed new communication skills. She also learned to identify and express her needs in a healthy way. As a result, she and her husband were able to communicate more effectively, resolve conflicts more constructively, and experience greater intimacy.
