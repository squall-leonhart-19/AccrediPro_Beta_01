Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. Digestive issues like bloating and gas are incredibly common, especially with the demands of your job. It sounds like you've been putting up with this for a while."
MARIA:
"Yes, it's been ongoing for a few months now. I thought it would just go away on its own, but it's not getting better."
YOU:
"I understand. Let's explore some potential root causes. It's important to understand what's happening in your body so we can create a plan that works for you. How does that sound?"
YOU:
"I'd like to focus on three areas: your eating habits, your gut health, and your stress levels related to your job. These are often interconnected. Does that resonate with you?"
MARIA:
"Yes, definitely. My eating habits are terrible, I'm sure my gut is a mess, and my job is incredibly stressful."
YOU:
"Okay, great. Let's start with your typical meals during the day. Walk me through what you usually eat from morning until night."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria nods and says, "Yes, that makes sense."
  • She starts sharing more details about her daily routine.
  • She expresses relief that there are potential solutions beyond medication.
  • She says, "I didn't realize how much my eating habits could be affecting me."
  • She agrees to start tracking her meals and symptoms.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Maria's experience before jumping to solutions?

Validating her experience builds trust and rapport. It shows that you understand her concerns and are genuinely interested in helping her.

2. What are the three key areas you should focus on with Maria, and why are they important?

The three key areas are her eating habits, gut health, and stress levels. These are important because they are often interconnected and can significantly impact digestive health.

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate the client's experience before offering solutions.
  • Focus on identifying the root causes of digestive issues, not just treating the symptoms.
  • Emphasize the interconnectedness of eating habits, gut health, and stress levels.
  • Use plain language and avoid technical jargon.
  • Encourage the client to actively participate in the process by tracking meals and symptoms.
