"I'm on a fixed income, so the cost is a concern."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Deep Dive into the Problem, Present Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask probing questions to uncover the specific concerns they need to address before making a decision. Offer to provide more information or schedule a follow-up call.

3. What is the purpose of a call-to-action in a discovery call?

The purpose of a call-to-action is to gently guide the prospect toward making a decision and taking the next step, such as enrolling in your program or scheduling a follow-up consultation.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Building rapport and understanding the prospect's needs are essential.
  • Confidently presenting your solution and handling objections are key to closing the sale.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to convey value and build trust.
