The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, with chronic migraines and anxiety. I did a sound assessment and started with a crystal bowl session, focusing on the crown chakra. Sarah said she felt more anxious during and after the session, and her migraine worsened. I‚Äôm not sure what I did wrong."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Was the crown chakra the most appropriate starting point given Sarah's anxiety?
  • Were there any grounding techniques used before or after the session?
  • Was the intensity of the sound appropriate for someone with migraines?
  • Did Olivia thoroughly assess Sarah's emotional state before beginning?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Validate Her Concerns

"It's completely normal to feel this way, Olivia. Complex cases require careful consideration, and it's great that you're seeking guidance."

2

Revisit the Assessment

Guide her to review the initial assessment. Did she identify any potential sensitivities to sound? Were there any indications of trauma or emotional blockages that might be triggered by crown chakra work?

3

Explore Alternative Approaches

Discuss starting with more grounding and balancing frequencies, such as the root or sacral chakra, before addressing the crown. Explain the importance of meeting the client where they are.

4

Emphasize the Importance of Client Feedback

Highlight the need to continuously check in with the client during the session and adjust the approach based on their feedback. Teach her how to recognize signs of distress and how to respond appropriately.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Positive Reinforcement
"Olivia, I admire your willingness to learn and your commitment to your clients. It's clear you care deeply about providing the best possible care."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What did you observe during the session when Sarah's anxiety increased? What were some of the cues she was giving you?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Sometimes, starting with the crown chakra can be overwhelming for clients with anxiety. It might be more beneficial to begin with frequencies that promote grounding and stability, like the root chakra. What are your thoughts on that?"
Empower Her to Take Action
"How would you approach the next session with Sarah differently, based on what we've discussed? What specific techniques would you use to create a safer and more comfortable experience for her?"

You're Becoming a Leader!

By mentoring new practitioners, you're shaping the future of sound healing. Your guidance and support are invaluable in helping them develop into confident and competent professionals.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to establish when beginning a supervision relationship with a new practitioner?

Establishing clear boundaries and expectations for the supervision relationship is crucial. This ensures both the mentor and mentee understand their roles, responsibilities, and the scope of the supervision.

2. A mentee is consistently late for supervision meetings and doesn't complete assigned tasks. How should you address this?

Address the issue directly and professionally. Start by acknowledging the pattern of behavior and its impact on the supervision process. Explore the reasons behind the lateness and incomplete tasks, and collaboratively develop strategies to improve time management and task completion. If the behavior persists, reiterate the expectations and consider setting consequences.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves validating the mentee's concerns and normalizing the learning experience.
  • Guiding mentees to revisit their assessments and explore alternative approaches fosters critical thinking.
  • Constructive feedback should start with positive reinforcement and empower the mentee to take action.
  • Setting clear boundaries and expectations is essential for a healthy supervision relationship.
  • Mentoring new practitioners is an opportunity to shape the future of sound healing and contribute to the growth of the profession.
