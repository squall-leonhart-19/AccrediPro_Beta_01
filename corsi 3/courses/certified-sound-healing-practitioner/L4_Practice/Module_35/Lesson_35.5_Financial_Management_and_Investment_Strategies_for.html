Understanding Tax Implications

As a self-employed sound healing practitioner, you'll need to understand the tax implications of your business. Key considerations include:

  • Self-Employment Tax: Paying both the employer and employee portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes.
  • Estimated Taxes: Making quarterly tax payments to avoid penalties.
  • Deductible Expenses: Claiming business-related expenses to reduce your taxable income.

Consult with a tax professional to ensure you are complying with all tax laws and maximizing your deductions. The IRS offers resources for self-employed individuals, including Publication 334, "Tax Guide for Small Business" (IRS, 2023). A study by the National Association for the Self-Employed found that 40% of self-employed individuals overpay their taxes due to a lack of understanding of deductible expenses (NASE, 2023). Therefore, seeking professional advice is highly recommended.
