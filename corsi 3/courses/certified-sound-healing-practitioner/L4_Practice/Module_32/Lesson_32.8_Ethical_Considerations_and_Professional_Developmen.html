Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks so much for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm a Certified Sound Healing Practitioner. How's your day going so far?"
YOU:
"Before we dive into sound healing, I'd love to hear a little bit about what brought you here today. What are you hoping to achieve?"
Phase 2: Exploring Her Needs 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling stressed and overwhelmed. Can you tell me more about that? What does that look like in your daily life?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried in the past to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about how you're feeling right now, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Introducing Sound Healing 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for a gentle yet effective way to find inner peace and manage stress. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"Sound healing works by using specific frequencies to bring your body and mind back into balance. It's non-invasive, deeply relaxing, and can help you release tension, improve sleep, and reconnect with yourself. Many of my clients, like Maria, a 48-year-old teacher, have experienced significant relief from stress and anxiety after just a few sessions."
YOU:
"My approach is tailored to each individual. We'll start with a sound assessment to identify any imbalances and then create a personalized treatment plan using various instruments like singing bowls, gongs, and tuning forks."
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like something you'd be interested in exploring further? ... Great! Let me tell you about my 1:1 program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I believe in sound healing."
"I understand your skepticism. It's natural to be curious about something new. Sound healing has ancient roots and is increasingly being recognized for its therapeutic benefits. I'm happy to share some research and testimonials with you, and I encourage you to experience it for yourself to see how it resonates with you."
"I don't have time for this right now."
"I hear you. Life can get busy. But think about it this way: investing in your well-being is an investment in everything else you do. My program is designed to fit into your schedule, and even small, consistent sessions can make a big difference. What if we started with just one session per week?"
"It's too expensive."
"I understand your concern about the investment. Let's break it down. My 1:1 program is $X, which includes [list inclusions]. Think of it as an investment in your long-term well-being. Many of my clients find that they actually save money on things like stress-related doctor visits or sleep aids. We also have payment plan options available."
"Can I try just one session first?"
"I appreciate your interest in trying a single session. While a single session can provide a taste of the benefits, the real transformation happens through consistent, personalized work. That's why I focus on my 1:1 program to ensure you receive the support and guidance you need to achieve lasting results."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of an effective discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's needs, introducing your approach, and inviting them to take the next step.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, break down the value of your program, and offer payment plan options if available. Focus on the long-term benefits and potential ROI.

3. What are some effective closing lines to use at the end of a discovery call?

"Based on everything you've shared, I truly believe I can help you. Are you ready to take the first step?" or "Are you ready to get started?"

Key Takeaways

  • Master the Discovery Call: A structured call is your key to converting prospects into clients.
  • Handle Objections with Confidence: Prepare responses to common objections to navigate concerns effectively.
  • Present Pricing Clearly: Be confident and transparent about your program's value and investment.
  • Practice Your Closing: A strong call-to-action can make all the difference in securing a new client.
