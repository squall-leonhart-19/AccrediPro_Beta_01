Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's only a 2-hour commitment on [Date]. Think of it as an investment in your well-being. Many participants find that the stress relief they experience during the workshop actually *creates* more time in their lives by improving their focus and energy levels."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, the techniques we use are based on proven sound healing principles. Plus, the group setting often enhances the experience. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee: if you attend the entire workshop and don't feel any benefit, I'll refund your money."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"I appreciate you asking. While I don't typically offer payment plans for the workshop, I can offer a 10% discount if you register and pay in full today. How does that sound?"
"I need to check my schedule."
"Of course! How about this, I can hold a spot for you for 24 hours. That gives you time to confirm your availability. If I don't hear back, I'll release the spot to someone else. Does that work?"

1. What are three key questions you should ask during the "Uncover Needs & Challenges" phase of the consultation call?

Three key questions could include: "Can you tell me more about what a typical day looks like for you?", "What are you hoping to achieve by participating in the workshop?", and "Have you tried other methods for managing stress, and what were your experiences with those?"

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and connecting with your prospect is crucial for a successful consultation.
  • Understanding the prospect's needs and challenges allows you to tailor your presentation of the workshop benefits.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and providing clear solutions builds trust and increases enrollment.
  • Confidently stating your price and offering payment options makes the workshop more accessible.
  • Practicing your closing lines ensures you end the call with a clear call to action.
