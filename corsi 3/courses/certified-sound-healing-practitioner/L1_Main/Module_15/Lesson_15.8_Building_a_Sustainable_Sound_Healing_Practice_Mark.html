Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before suggesting solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to your suggestions. It shows you understand their experience and aren't just offering generic advice.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is feeling heard and understood?

Relaxed body language, eye contact, nodding, and verbal affirmations like "yes" or "I understand."
