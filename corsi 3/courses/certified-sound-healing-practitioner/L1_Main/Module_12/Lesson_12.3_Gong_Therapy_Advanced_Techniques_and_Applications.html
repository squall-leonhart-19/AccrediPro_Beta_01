  • Striking Points: Striking the gong in different areas alters the sound. The center produces the fullest tone, while the edges create higher frequencies.

Case Study: Gong Therapy for Anxiety, Insomnia, and PTSD

Case Study: Sarah, 38

Client: Sarah, a 38-year-old woman, presented with symptoms of anxiety, insomnia, and mild PTSD following a car accident six months prior.

Intervention: Sarah received eight weekly gong therapy sessions. Each session included 45 minutes of gong playing, focusing on slow, resonant tones to promote relaxation and emotional release. Singing bowls were integrated to enhance the calming effect, and gentle chimes were used at the end of each session to facilitate grounding.

Outcomes: After eight sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety and improved sleep quality. Her PTSD symptoms also decreased, and she felt more grounded and present in her daily life. The Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item (GAD-7) scale score decreased from 18 (severe anxiety) to 7 (mild anxiety). Her Insomnia Severity Index (ISI) score decreased from 22 (severe insomnia) to 10 (subthreshold insomnia). This demonstrates the power of sound to navigate blockages and activate healing.
