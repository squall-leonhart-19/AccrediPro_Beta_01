Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open with me. It sounds like you're going through a lot right now. Many women in your situation feel similarly overwhelmed. Let's explore some ways we can use sound healing to bring you back to center. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds wonderful. I'm willing to try anything."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by talking about your sleep. Can you describe what a typical night looks like for you?"
MARIA:
"Well, I toss and turn for hours. My mind just races. I might fall asleep around 2 AM, and then I'm up at 6 AM for work. It's exhausting."
YOU:
"Okay, thank you for sharing that. Given your difficulty sleeping and increased anxiety, I would like to address this first. Would you be open to exploring some gentle sound frequencies to help regulate your nervous system?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the first thing you should do when meeting a new client?

Build rapport by actively listening and validating their experiences.

2. How should you frame sound healing to a client experiencing anxiety?

As a tool for managing anxiety and promoting relaxation, not as a "cure."

Key Takeaways

  • Active listening and empathy are crucial for building trust with clients.
  • Frame sound healing as a tool for managing symptoms and promoting overall well-being.
  • Always consider the client's emotional state and health history.
  • Use clear and understandable language.
  • Practice the coaching script out loud to build confidence.
