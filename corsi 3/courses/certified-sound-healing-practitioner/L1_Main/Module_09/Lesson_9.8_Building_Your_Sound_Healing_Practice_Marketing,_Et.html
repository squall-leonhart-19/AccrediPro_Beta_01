Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on sound healing as a potential path. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing so openly. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings. Many women in your stage of life experience similar transitions. Before we move forward, I want to reassure you that what you're feeling is valid, and you're not alone. We're going to explore ways to reconnect you with your inner joy and purpose."
DIANE:
"Thank you. I feel like I've lost touch with myself. Everything I used to define myself by is gone."
YOU:
"That's understandable, Diane. Now, let's explore some avenues to help you rediscover that connection. Have you ever considered how sound and vibration might play a role in your well-being?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before suggesting sound healing?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to new approaches.

2. How can you adjust your approach if a client is hesitant about sound healing?

Offer it as a suggestion, not a demand, and provide alternative options if they're not comfortable.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy is crucial: Connect with your client's emotions before offering solutions.
  • Simplify explanations: Avoid jargon when introducing sound healing concepts.
  • Offer, don't impose: Respect your client's boundaries and preferences.
  • Validation matters: Acknowledge their feelings to build trust and rapport.
