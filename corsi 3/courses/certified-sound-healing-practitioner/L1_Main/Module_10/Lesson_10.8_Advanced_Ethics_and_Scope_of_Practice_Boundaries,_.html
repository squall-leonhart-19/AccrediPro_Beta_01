Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you're carrying a heavy load right now, and it takes courage to seek support. I want to assure you that feeling overwhelmed and anxious is a very common response to what you're experiencing, and we can definitely find some relief."
MARIA:
"I just feel like I'm constantly running on empty. I'm exhausted, but I can't sleep. I'm worried about everything."
YOU:
"I understand. That feeling of being 'wired and tired' is a sign that your nervous system is stuck in a state of high alert. We can use sound healing to gently guide your nervous system back into a state of balance and calm. How does that sound to you?"
MARIA:
"Sound healing? I've heard of it, but I don't really know much about it."
YOU:
"That's perfectly fine! Essentially, we'll be using specific sounds and vibrations to help your body relax and release tension. Think of it as a gentle massage for your nervous system. We can start with a simple sound bath to see how you respond. Would you be open to trying that?"
MARIA:
"I suppose so. I'm willing to try anything at this point. I just want to feel better."
YOU:
"Great. Before we begin, let's talk a little more about what you're hoping to achieve with sound healing. What does feeling 'better' look like for you?"

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize building trust and rapport with new clients.
  • Validate their experiences and acknowledge their struggles.
  • Explain sound healing in simple, relatable terms, avoiding jargon.
  • Set realistic expectations and avoid promising quick fixes.
  • Address any concerns or hesitations they may have about trying sound healing.
