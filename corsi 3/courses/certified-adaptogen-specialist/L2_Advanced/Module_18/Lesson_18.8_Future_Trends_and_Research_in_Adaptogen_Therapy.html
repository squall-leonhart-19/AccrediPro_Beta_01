Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Case

Step 1: Identify Key Imbalances
Hormonal dysregulation (irregular cycles, PCOS, endometriosis), HPA axis dysfunction (elevated morning cortisol, low evening cortisol), nutrient deficiencies (Vitamin D, ferritin), chronic inflammation (headaches, endometriosis), and lifestyle factors (poor diet, high stress).
Step 2: Prioritize Systems to Support
Address HPA axis dysfunction and chronic stress first to positively impact hormonal balance. Then, focus on nutrient repletion and anti-inflammatory strategies.
Step 3: Select Appropriate Adaptogens
Consider adaptogens that support HPA axis function, reduce stress, and possess anti-inflammatory properties. Rhodiola rosea, Ashwagandha, and Holy Basil are potential options.
Step 4: Consider Potential Interactions
Be mindful of potential interactions between adaptogens and the birth control pill. Some adaptogens may affect liver enzymes that metabolize medications. Start with low doses and monitor closely.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Underlying Autoimmunity

Given her history of endometriosis and migraines, explore potential autoimmune involvement. Consider testing for common autoimmune markers.

2

Estrogen Dominance

PCOS and endometriosis often coexist with estrogen dominance. Consider assessing estrogen and progesterone levels to further tailor the protocol.

3

Thyroid Dysfunction

Although her TSH is within the normal range, suboptimal thyroid function can mimic many of her symptoms. A full thyroid panel (including T3, T4, and antibodies) is warranted.

4

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Migraines and endometriosis have been linked to MCAS. Consider this if she experiences widespread symptoms like hives, itching, or digestive issues.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when recommending adaptogens to a client on hormonal birth control?

Some adaptogens can affect liver enzymes (e.g., CYP450) involved in the metabolism of hormonal birth control, potentially altering its effectiveness. Start with low doses, monitor for changes in menstrual cycle, and advise the client to consult with their physician.

2. How would you differentiate between adrenal fatigue and HPA axis dysfunction in this case?

"Adrenal fatigue" is an outdated term. HPA axis dysfunction is the more accurate description. In Eleanor's case, her elevated morning cortisol and low evening cortisol suggest a dysregulated HPA axis, where the normal diurnal rhythm is disrupted due to chronic stress. This needs to be supported with adaptogens and lifestyle modifications.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-thinking approach to identify interconnected imbalances.
  • Prioritize addressing HPA axis dysfunction and chronic stress as a foundation for hormonal balance.
  • Adaptogens can be powerful tools, but consider potential interactions with medications and individual sensitivities.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare providers is essential for optimal client outcomes.
  • Always prioritize safety and refer to an MD when red flags arise.
