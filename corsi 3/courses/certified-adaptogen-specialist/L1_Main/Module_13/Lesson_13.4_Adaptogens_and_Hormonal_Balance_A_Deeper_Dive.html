Adaptogens for Thyroid Health

Adaptogens can support thyroid function through several mechanisms. First, they reduce inflammation, a common culprit in thyroid disorders. Second, adaptogens improve nutrient absorption, ensuring the thyroid receives the necessary building blocks for hormone production. Finally, they modulate thyroid hormone levels, promoting a healthy balance. It's important to note that adaptogens should be used cautiously in individuals with hyperthyroidism and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.

A 2013 study published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found that Ashwagandha improved thyroid hormone levels in subclinical hypothyroid patients. Specifically, T4 levels increased by 19.6%, and T3 levels increased by 41.5%. This demonstrates the potential of adaptogens to positively impact thyroid function.

Adaptogens and Blood Sugar Regulation

Insulin resistance and fluctuating blood sugar levels can wreak havoc on hormonal balance. Adaptogens like Holy Basil and Ginseng have demonstrated the ability to improve insulin sensitivity and stabilize blood sugar. This is particularly beneficial for women with polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), a common hormonal disorder characterized by insulin resistance.

A 2012 study in the journal Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine showed that Holy Basil significantly reduced blood glucose levels in individuals with type 2 diabetes. Furthermore, a 2014 meta-analysis in the journal PLoS One indicated that Ginseng improved insulin sensitivity in both healthy individuals and those with diabetes. These findings underscore the potential of adaptogens to support healthy blood sugar regulation.
