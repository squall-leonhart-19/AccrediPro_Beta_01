Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm too busy to commit to a group program."
"I understand. Many participants initially feel that way. However, the program is designed to be flexible, with recordings available if you miss a live session. Plus, the strategies we teach will actually free up your time by increasing your efficiency and reducing stress-related time-wasters."
"I'm not sure if it's the right fit for me."
"That's a valid concern. To ensure it's a good fit, I offer a 100% satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied after the first week, you'll receive a full refund. This allows you to experience the program risk-free."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a payment plan. You can choose to pay in three monthly installments of [price]. This makes the program more accessible and manageable for your budget."
"I'm worried I won't have time to implement everything."
"The program is structured to provide small, achievable steps each week. We focus on sustainable changes that fit into your existing lifestyle. You'll also have access to the online community for ongoing support and accountability."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key benefits of offering a group program versus one-on-one coaching?

Group programs offer community support, shared learning, and can be more accessible in terms of pricing.

2. How would you respond to a prospect who says they are too busy to commit to a group program?

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the program's flexibility (recordings available), and highlight how the strategies taught will ultimately free up their time by increasing efficiency and reducing stress.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls to build rapport, understand challenges, present your program, and invite enrollment.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, value-driven responses.
  • Practice stating your program price with confidence and highlight the transformative investment.
  • Group programs offer a powerful way to impact more lives while building a sustainable income.
