The Challenging Case

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 48

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily, 48, has chronic fatigue and anxiety. I suggested Rhodiola for energy and Ashwagandha for calming. But she reports feeling *more* anxious and wired, especially in the evenings. I'm so confused! Did I choose the wrong adaptogens?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Dosage: Was the Rhodiola dose too high, exacerbating anxiety?
  • Timing: Was Ashwagandha taken at night, potentially causing daytime sedation and rebound anxiety?
  • Individual Sensitivity: Some individuals are sensitive to specific adaptogens.
  • Underlying Factors: Are there other lifestyle factors contributing to anxiety (caffeine, stress)?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Guiding Sarah to Clarity

1

Reframe the "Failure" Narrative

Emphasize that unexpected reactions are valuable data. "This isn't a failure, Sarah; it's an opportunity to refine your approach."

2

Deconstruct the Adaptogen Protocol

Break down each adaptogen's potential effects. "Rhodiola can be stimulating for some. Let's consider the dosage and timing."

3

Teach Dosage Nuances

Explain that adaptogen dosing is highly individual. "Start low and go slow" is a guiding principle. A 2018 study in the *Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine* highlights the importance of individualized Rhodiola dosing for anxiety.

4

Emphasize the Importance of Lifestyle Factors

Remind Sarah to consider caffeine intake, sleep habits, and stress levels. "Adaptogens are part of a holistic approach, not a magic bullet."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to normalize unexpected reactions when mentoring a new practitioner?

Normalizing unexpected reactions helps to reduce anxiety and encourages the mentee to view them as learning opportunities rather than failures, fostering a growth mindset.

2. What is the "start low and go slow" principle, and why is it important in adaptogen protocols?

"Start low and go slow" means beginning with a minimal dose of an adaptogen and gradually increasing it while monitoring for effects. This is crucial because individual sensitivity to adaptogens varies significantly, and starting with a low dose minimizes the risk of adverse reactions.
