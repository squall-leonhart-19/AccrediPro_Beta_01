The Case Olivia Presents

Olivia's First Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I saw my first client, Sarah, 52, complaining of stress and insomnia. I recommended ashwagandha and lemon balm. After a week, Sarah says she's even MORE anxious. Did I choose the wrong adaptogens?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Dosage: Was the dosage appropriate for Sarah's sensitivity?
  • Individual Variation: How did Olivia assess Sarah's individual needs and sensitivities?
  • Timing: When was Sarah taking the adaptogens?
  • Underlying Factors: Were there other lifestyle factors contributing to Sarah's anxiety?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Individualized Assessment is Key

Emphasize the importance of tailoring recommendations to each client's unique needs and sensitivities. Not all adaptogens are suitable for everyone. A 2021 study in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine highlighted the importance of personalized approaches.

2

Dosage and Timing Matter

Discuss how incorrect dosage or timing can exacerbate symptoms. Ashwagandha, for example, can be stimulating for some individuals if taken in high doses or at the wrong time of day. A 2022 meta-analysis in Nutrients explored optimal adaptogen dosages for different populations.

3

Consider Underlying Factors

Help Olivia understand that adaptogens are most effective when combined with lifestyle modifications. Stress management techniques, sleep hygiene, and a balanced diet can significantly enhance the effects of adaptogens. The American Institute of Stress offers resources on integrative stress management.

4

Empower Her Clinical Reasoning

Instead of providing direct answers, ask guiding questions like: "What other factors might be contributing to Sarah's anxiety? What alternative adaptogens could you consider?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Effort
"Olivia, I commend you for taking the leap and seeing your first client! It's natural to feel uncertain. Let's explore this case together."
Explore Her Thought Process
"Before I offer my perspective, what was your rationale for choosing ashwagandha and lemon balm for Sarah? What were you hoping to achieve?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Sometimes, seemingly calming adaptogens can have paradoxical effects. For instance, ashwagandha can be stimulating in sensitive individuals. We might consider gentler options like rhodiola or holy basil."
Collaborate on a Plan
"What adjustments would you like to make to Sarah's protocol? I'm here to support you, and together, we can create a plan that better suits her needs."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask the mentee about their reasoning BEFORE offering your own perspective?

Understanding their thought process helps you tailor your feedback and address any misconceptions they may have. It also fosters their critical thinking skills.

2. What are some strategies for creating a safe and supportive environment for your mentee?

Active listening, empathy, non-judgmental feedback, and celebrating successes are all essential for creating a safe and supportive environment.
