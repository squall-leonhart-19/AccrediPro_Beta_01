Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! Thank you for joining me today. I really appreciate you taking the time. I enjoyed your participation in the workshop! To make the best use of our time, let's spend a few minutes getting to know each other, then dive into what's been going on for you, and finally, if it feels like a good fit, we can discuss how I can help. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Great. I saw you're a lawyer. That sounds demanding. What's a typical day look like for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Explore Impact 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the stress you're experiencing. What does it feel like in your body? How is it impacting your sleep, your relationships, and your overall well-being?"
YOU:
"Have you tried any stress-reduction techniques in the past? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you continue down this path, what do you see happening in the next 6 months, the next year? And conversely, if you *could* magically resolve this stress and sleep issue, what would your life look like? How would you feel? What would you be able to do?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Highlight Benefits 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing significant stress related to your demanding career, it's impacting your sleep and overall well-being, and you're looking for a sustainable solution. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because it's not just about quick fixes. We work together over 12 weeks to identify the *root causes* of your stress, create personalized mindfulness practices, and develop coping mechanisms that you can use in real-time, even during high-pressure situations. My clients often report feeling calmer, more focused, and sleeping better within the first month. Plus, the techniques become a natural part of their daily routine."
YOU:
"I also provide resources and support that are tailored specifically to professional women like yourself. We integrate mindfulness into your existing schedule, so it doesn't feel like another thing you *have* to do."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what I've described, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share with you the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals just like you. The beauty of mindfulness is that even small, consistent practices can make a big difference. We'll work together to integrate these techniques into your existing schedule, even if it's just for 5-10 minutes a day. Think of it as an investment in your long-term well-being and productivity. Isn't it worth a few minutes a day to reclaim your calm and focus?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? Perhaps I can provide some additional information to help you make a more informed decision."
"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. Think about the cost of *not* addressing your stress ‚Äì the potential health issues, the impact on your relationships, the lost productivity. My program is designed to provide lasting results, and many of my clients find that the benefits far outweigh the financial investment. We also have payment plan options available to make it more accessible. Would you like to discuss those?"
"I'm skeptical that this will actually work for me."
"I appreciate your honesty. It's natural to be skeptical, especially if you've tried other things that haven't worked. However, mindfulness is backed by extensive research and has been shown to be effective in reducing stress, improving sleep, and enhancing overall well-being. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that mindfulness interventions significantly reduced perceived stress levels (p < 0.001). I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so if you're not completely satisfied with the program, I'll work with you to make it right. Are you open to trying it and seeing for yourself?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in your discovery call introduction?

Acknowledge the prospect's time, build rapport, and set the agenda for the call.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, ask clarifying questions about what they need to consider, and offer additional information or support.

3. What's a confident way to state your pricing?

Clearly state the price, highlight the inclusions and value, and then ask a closing question.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's pain points and desired outcomes is essential for tailoring your approach.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can increase your chances of closing the sale.
  • Clearly communicating the value of your program and the potential return on investment is vital for justifying your pricing.
  • Consistent practice and a clear strategy can lead to a thriving practice and a fulfilling career.

Case Study: Sarah, 42, Executive

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah, a 42-year-old executive, contacted me feeling overwhelmed, stressed, and unable to sleep. She reported difficulty concentrating at work, irritability with her family, and a general sense of unease. She had tried meditation apps but found it hard to stay consistent and felt she wasn't getting the personalized guidance she needed.

Intervention: Sarah enrolled in my 12-week mindfulness program. We started with basic breath awareness techniques and gradually introduced more advanced practices like body scan meditations and mindful movement. We also explored techniques for managing difficult emotions and communicating more effectively in stressful situations. I provided personalized feedback and support during our weekly sessions, tailoring the practices to her specific needs and challenges.

Outcomes: After 12 weeks, Sarah reported a significant reduction in her stress levels and a noticeable improvement in her sleep quality. She was able to focus more easily at work, felt more patient and present with her family, and had developed a greater sense of self-awareness and emotional regulation. She reported a 60% reduction in her perceived stress scale (PSS) score. She continues to use the mindfulness techniques she learned in the program and credits them with helping her navigate the demands of her high-pressure job with greater ease and resilience.
