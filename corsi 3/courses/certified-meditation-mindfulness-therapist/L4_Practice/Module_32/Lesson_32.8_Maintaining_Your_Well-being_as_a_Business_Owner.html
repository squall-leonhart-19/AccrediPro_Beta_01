Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Introduction 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thanks for scheduling this call. I'm Maya, and I help busy professionals like you find more balance and reduce stress through mindfulness and meditation. How's your day going so far?"
YOU:
"Before we dive in, I'd love to hear a bit about what prompted you to reach out. What's been going on?"
Phase 2: Uncover Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the stress you're experiencing. Where do you feel it most in your body?"
YOU:
"How is this stress impacting your work and personal life? What's at stake if you don't address it?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, it sounds like you're dealing with a significant amount of stress that is impacting multiple areas of your life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is to help you develop practical mindfulness and meditation techniques that you can integrate into your daily routine, even with a busy schedule. We'll work together to identify your specific stress triggers, create personalized strategies, and track your progress. Many of my clients report feeling a noticeable difference within just a few weeks."
YOU:
"This isn't about adding another thing to your to-do list; it's about shifting your relationship with stress so you can be more present and effective in all areas of your life."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Sarah, do you think this approach could be a good fit for you? ... Great! I have a few options for working together."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I don't have time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. However, what they discover is that by investing a small amount of time each day in mindfulness, they actually become MORE efficient and productive. It's about working smarter, not harder. Plus, I offer flexible scheduling options to fit your busy life."
"I'm not sure I can meditate."
"That's perfectly normal! Most people think meditation is about clearing your mind completely, but it's really about learning to observe your thoughts and emotions without judgment. I'll guide you through simple, beginner-friendly techniques that you can easily adapt to your own needs and preferences. You don't need any prior experience."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Let's look at the value you're getting: personalized guidance, proven techniques, and ongoing support. Think of it as an investment in your long-term well-being and career success. What is it worth to you to reduce stress, improve your sleep, and regain control of your life? I also offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically would you like to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I'm happy to answer any questions you have to help you make an informed decision. And I want to be upfront that I only have a few slots left this month, so I can't guarantee availability if you wait too long."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call, and what is the purpose of each?

The four phases are: 1) Rapport & Introduction (build connection), 2) Uncover Pain Points (understand the client's challenges), 3) Present Your Solution (explain your approach), and 4) Close & Next Steps (invite them to work with you).

2. How can you address the objection, "I don't have time for this"?

Acknowledge their concern, then explain how mindfulness can actually increase efficiency and productivity. Offer flexible scheduling options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for building a thriving practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you qualify prospects and present your solution effectively.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can turn potential clients into paying clients.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and the value you offer is crucial for closing the sale.
  • Consistent client acquisition efforts can lead to a significant and sustainable income stream.
