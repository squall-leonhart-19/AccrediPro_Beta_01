Handling Objections with Grace

Turn Concerns into Opportunities

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible, with short, impactful practices that fit into your schedule. Plus, the time you invest now will free up more time later by increasing your focus and productivity. How does that sound?"
"I've tried meditation before, and it didn't work for me."
"I hear you. It's common for people to have mixed experiences with meditation. My program is different because it's highly personalized, evidence-based, and provides ongoing support. We'll tailor the practices to your specific needs and challenges. Are you open to trying a different approach?"
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can promise that I will give you my full support, expertise, and guidance. My clients who actively participate and follow the program consistently report significant improvements. I can share some of their testimonials if you'd like."
"I need to discuss this with my family first."
"That's completely understandable. What questions do you anticipate they might have? Perhaps I can provide some information or even schedule a brief call to address their concerns directly?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Warm Welcome & Connection, Uncover Challenges & Aspirations, Present Your Solution, and Invite to Enroll.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I'm not sure if I have the time for this?"

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the program's flexibility, and emphasize that the time investment will lead to increased focus and productivity, ultimately saving them time.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Empathy and active listening build trust and rapport.
  • Confidently present your value and address objections with grace.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to convey value and inspire confidence.
  • Consistent action leads to a thriving practice and financial freedom.
