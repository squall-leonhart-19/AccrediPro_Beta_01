Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so nice to finally connect with you! I remember you were particularly engaged during the mindfulness workshop. What resonated with you the most?"
YOU:
"That's fantastic! And what prompted you to schedule this call today? What are you hoping to achieve?"
Phase 2: Understanding the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the overwhelm you're experiencing. What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"And how does the anxiety manifest itself? Does it impact your sleep or other areas of your life?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to manage these challenges?"
YOU:
"Imagine six months from now, you've successfully integrated mindfulness into your life. What would that look like? How would you feel?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, you're looking for a practical and structured way to integrate mindfulness into your busy life to manage stress and improve sleep. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My program is designed to do just that. Over 8 weeks, we'll explore mindfulness techniques tailored to your specific needs, develop personalized strategies, and track your progress. It's not just about theory; it's about creating lasting change."
YOU:
"Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s and 50s, find that even 10-15 minutes of daily mindfulness can make a significant difference. A 2023 study in the Journal of Applied Psychology found that mindfulness interventions reduced stress by 20% and improved sleep quality by 15% in working women."
Phase 4: Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you feel like this could be the solution you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started. I have two spots available this month and I think you'd be a perfect fit."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients feel the same way initially. But we'll work together to find small pockets of time in your day, even just 5-10 minutes, to practice mindfulness. And the benefits ‚Äì reduced stress, better sleep ‚Äì will actually *give* you more time and energy in the long run."
"I've tried mindfulness before, and it didn't work for me."
"I'm sorry to hear that. What did you try, and what didn't resonate with you? My program is highly personalized, so we'll tailor the techniques to your specific needs and preferences. Plus, you'll have my ongoing support and guidance throughout the process."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that it's an investment. But consider the cost of *not* addressing your stress and sleep issues ‚Äì the impact on your health, your relationships, your work. "Can I think about it and get back to you?"
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make a confident decision. Also, the two slots I have open are likely to fill quickly, so I wouldn't want you to miss out."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key questions to ask during the "Understanding the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

Possible answers include: "What does a typical day look like for you?", "How does the issue manifest itself?", "What have you tried in the past to manage these challenges?" and "Imagine six months from now, you've successfully integrated mindfulness into your life. What would that look like? How would you feel?"

2. How would you respond to the objection, "I'm not sure I can afford this right now?"

Possible answers include: Acknowledge their concern, highlight the long-term costs of *not* solving the problem, emphasize the value of your program, and offer flexible payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • Master the Discovery Call: Structure your calls to build rapport, understand prospects' needs, present your solution, and confidently close.
  • Handle Objections with Grace: Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Know Your Value: Confidently state your price and highlight the transformative benefits of your program.
  • Practice, Practice, Practice: Rehearse your script and closing lines until they feel natural and authentic.
  • Believe in Your Potential: With dedication and skill, you can build a thriving practice and make a real difference in people's lives.
