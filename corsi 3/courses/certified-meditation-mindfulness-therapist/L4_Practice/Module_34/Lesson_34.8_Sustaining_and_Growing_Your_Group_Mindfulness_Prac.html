Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Reconnect & Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! It's so great to connect with you again after the workshop. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"I'm so glad you enjoyed the workshop. What was your biggest takeaway from it?"
YOU:
"Great! So, you mentioned you were interested in learning more about the 8-week group program. What sparked your interest?"
Phase 2: Understand Her Needs & Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about what's been feeling overwhelming at work. What are the specific challenges you're facing?"
YOU:
"And how is the difficulty sleeping impacting your daily life? What have you tried so far to improve your sleep?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being 'not at all' and 10 being 'completely', how important is it for you to reduce your stress and improve your sleep?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and instantly feel more calm and centered, what would that look like and how would it impact your life?"
Phase 3: Present the Program & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like the 8-week group program could be a really good fit for you. It provides practical mindfulness techniques, social support, and a structured approach to reduce stress and improve sleep. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The program includes [list key benefits and modules, e.g., guided meditations, mindful movement, stress management techniques, sleep hygiene strategies]. We also have a supportive online community where you can connect with other participants."
YOU:
"Many of my clients have reported significant improvements in their stress levels, sleep quality, and overall well-being after completing the program. They also appreciate the sense of community and connection."
Phase 4: Address Concerns & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Do you have any questions about the program so far? ... Great! I understand your concern about the cost. Let's talk about some options to make it more accessible for you."
YOU:
"So Susan, knowing what you know now, do you see yourself benefiting from this program and ready to join us?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm too busy to commit to an 8-week program."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible. The sessions are recorded, so you can catch up if you miss one. And even 15-20 minutes of mindfulness practice a day can make a big difference. Where can we find 15 minutes?"
"I'm not sure if mindfulness is really for me."
"That's perfectly okay! It's normal to feel a bit skeptical. The best way to find out is to try it. Remember how you felt at the workshop? The group program builds on that foundation with even more practices. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee."
"I need to talk to my family about it first."
"Of course! Would it be helpful if I sent you a program overview and some testimonials you can share with them? Or, I'm happy to answer any questions they might have directly."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand. Let's explore some payment options. We have a payment plan available, or perhaps you can prioritize this investment in your well-being by cutting back on smaller, less impactful expenses. Think of this as an investment in your long-term health and happiness."

    Case Study: Susan's Transformation

    Client: Susan, 45 years old

    Presenting Symptoms: Susan was experiencing chronic stress, anxiety, and difficulty sleeping. She felt overwhelmed by her demanding job and struggled to find time for self-care.

    Intervention: Susan enrolled in an 8-week mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) group program. The program included weekly sessions, guided meditations, and group discussions.

    Outcomes: After completing the program, Susan reported a significant reduction in stress and anxiety levels. She was sleeping better, feeling more centered, and had developed effective coping mechanisms for managing stress. She also appreciated the sense of community and support from the group.

    The Power of Group Programs

    Group programs offer a powerful way to leverage your time and expertise while providing clients with a supportive and transformative experience. By enrolling multiple participants at once, you can significantly increase your income potential and impact more lives.

    Building Rapport and Trust

    The foundation of a successful discovery call is building rapport and trust with the potential client. This involves active listening, empathy, and genuine interest in their well-being. Remember, people buy from those they trust.

    Understanding Client Needs

    Before presenting your program, take the time to truly understand the client's needs, challenges, and goals. Ask open-ended questions and listen carefully to their responses. This will allow you to tailor your presentation to their specific situation and demonstrate the value of your program.

    Presenting the Value Proposition

    Clearly articulate the benefits of your group program and how it can help the client achieve their desired outcomes. Focus on the transformational aspects of the program and the positive impact it can have on their lives. Use testimonials and success stories to illustrate the value of your program.

    Closing the Sale with Confidence

    Once you have addressed the client's concerns and presented the value of your program, it's time to confidently close the sale. Ask for the enrollment and make it easy for them to take the next step. Offer payment options and provide clear instructions on how to register.

    Mindfulness in Sales

    Approach the sales process with mindfulness and compassion. Focus on serving the client's best interests and creating a win-win situation. Remember that not everyone is a good fit for your program, and that's okay. Trust that the right clients will be drawn to your offering.

    Case Study: John's Increased Productivity

    Client: John, 38 years old

    Presenting Symptoms: John was struggling with focus and productivity at work. He felt easily distracted and overwhelmed by his workload.

    Intervention: John participated in a 6-week mindfulness for productivity group program. The program included mindfulness exercises, time management techniques, and strategies for reducing distractions.

    Outcomes: After completing the program, John reported a significant improvement in his focus and productivity. He was able to manage his time more effectively, reduce distractions, and accomplish more in less time. He also felt less stressed and more in control of his work.
