Your Coaching Script: Guiding a Mindful Breathing Exercise

Use these words to guide Maria through a simple mindful breathing exercise. Remember to speak slowly and calmly:

YOU:
"Maria, let's try a short exercise to bring you into the present moment. Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down. There's no right or wrong way to do this."
YOU:
"Now, gently close your eyes if that feels comfortable. If not, simply soften your gaze."
YOU:
"Bring your awareness to your breath. Notice the sensation of the air entering your nostrils, filling your lungs, and then leaving your body."
YOU:
"You don't need to change your breathing in any way. Just observe it, like a curious scientist."
YOU:
"Your mind will likely wander, and that's perfectly normal. When you notice your thoughts drifting, gently guide your attention back to your breath, without judgment."
YOU:
"Let's continue this for the next few minutes, simply focusing on the breath. I'll let you know when our time is up."
YOU (after 3 minutes):
"Okay, Maria. Gently bring your awareness back to the room. Wiggle your fingers and toes. When you're ready, open your eyes."
YOU:
"Take a moment to notice how you feel. What sensations are present in your body? What thoughts are arising? Just observe without getting carried away."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary goal of this mindful breathing exercise?

2. What should you do if the client reports their mind wandering during the exercise?

Key Takeaways

  • Mindfulness exercises can be powerful tools for reducing anxiety and promoting presence.
  • Gentle guidance and validation are essential for creating a safe and supportive environment.
  • Simplicity is key. Avoid jargon and focus on the client's direct experience.
  • Encourage clients to explore mindfulness as an ongoing practice, not a one-time fix.
