Ethical Concerns
Dual relationship, transference, potential exploitation of power imbalance, compromised objectivity.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Ethical Dilemma

Step 1: Acknowledge and Validate Feelings
Recognize that Eleanor's feelings are a manifestation of transference, a common occurrence in therapy. Validate her feelings without reciprocating or encouraging them.
Step 2: Reinforce Boundaries
Clearly and firmly reiterate the boundaries of the therapeutic relationship. Explain that a romantic or sexual relationship is unethical and harmful to the therapeutic process. Use phrases like, "While I appreciate you sharing your feelings, our relationship must remain professional to ensure your well-being."
Step 3: Explore the Underlying Issues
Gently explore what might be driving Eleanor's feelings. Is she seeking validation, connection, or escape from other stressors in her life? Understanding the root causes can inform the therapeutic approach.
Step 4: Discuss Termination and Referral
If Eleanor is unable to respect the boundaries or if the transference is significantly impeding progress, discuss the possibility of terminating therapy and providing a referral to another qualified therapist.

Differential Considerations

3

Explore Eleanor's history for any past trauma or insecure attachment patterns that might be influencing her current feelings and behaviors.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are common in therapy and require careful consideration.
  • Maintaining professional boundaries is crucial for protecting client well-being.
  • Transference is a normal phenomenon, but it must be managed ethically.
  • Seeking supervision or consultation is essential when facing complex ethical challenges.
