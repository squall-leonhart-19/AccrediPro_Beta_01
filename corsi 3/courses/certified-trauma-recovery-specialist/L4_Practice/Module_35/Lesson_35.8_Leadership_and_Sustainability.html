Handling Objections with Confidence

Common Objections & Your Powerful Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand completely. It's a big decision. May I ask what specifically you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to feel comfortable."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I hear you. Think about all the money you've already spent on solutions that haven't worked. What if, in 12 weeks, you could finally resolve these issues and feel like yourself again? What would that be worth to you in terms of your energy, relationships, and overall quality of life?"
"I need to discuss it with my family."
"Of course. Would it be helpful if I sent you a detailed program outline and some client testimonials you could share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with you and your family to answer any questions they might have. What would be most helpful?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. Plus, think about how much time you're currently losing to anxiety and lack of sleep. What if we could reclaim that time and energy for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Rapport & Connection, Problem Exploration, Solution Presentation, and Closing & Next Steps.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Focus on the value of the program and the potential ROI in terms of improved health, productivity, and overall quality of life. Ask them to consider the cost of continuing to struggle with their current issues.

3. What is the primary goal of the "Closing & Next Steps" phase?

To confidently ask the prospect if they are ready to commit to the program and take the next steps towards transformation.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Handling objections effectively can turn potential "no's" into "yes's."
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence demonstrates your value.
  • Consistent practice is key to feeling comfortable and natural during sales conversations.
  • Realistic income goals are achievable with dedication and a proven system.
