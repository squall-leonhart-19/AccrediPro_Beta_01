Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's great to finally connect! Thanks for reaching out. I saw you found me on LinkedIn - what specifically caught your eye about my profile?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. I'm passionate about helping women like you navigate career transitions. So, in a nutshell, what's prompting you to explore this now?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about feeling 'stuck.' What does that look and feel like on a day-to-day basis?"
YOU:
"You mentioned past trauma impacting your confidence. Can you elaborate on how that shows up in your career-related decisions?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and be in your dream career situation one year from now, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, if I'm hearing you correctly, you're feeling stuck and unconfident due to past trauma, and you desire a fulfilling career. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My trauma-informed coaching program is designed to address those exact challenges. We'll work together to reframe limiting beliefs, regulate your nervous system, and build resilience. Many of my clients experience significant shifts in confidence and clarity within the first few weeks. I had a client, Maria, a 48-year-old teacher, who similarly felt stuck after years in the same role. Within 8 weeks, she landed a new position she loves, earning 20% more."
Phase 4: The Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, do you feel like this approach resonates with you and what you're looking for? ... Fantastic! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get you started on this transformative journey."

Objection Handling

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Time is valuable. The program requires [X hours] per week, but it's an investment in your future. Think about how much time you're currently spending feeling stuck and unhappy. This program is designed to help you reclaim that time and energy."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, my program is based on proven methodologies and tailored to your individual needs. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you fully participate and don't see value, we can discuss a partial refund."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a flexible payment plan. You can choose to pay in [X] installments of [Amount]. This makes the program more accessible while still allowing you to achieve your goals."
"I need to research other options."
"I respect that. What specific information are you hoping to find? Perhaps I can address those questions now to help you make an informed decision. I want you to feel completely confident in your choice."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem deeply, and confidently presenting your solution.

2. How can you address the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, but gently inquire about the specific concerns they need to consider, offering additional information or support.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script regularly to build confidence.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • State your pricing clearly and confidently, highlighting the value you provide.
  • Remember that client acquisition is about building relationships and serving your ideal clients.
