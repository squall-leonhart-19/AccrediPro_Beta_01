Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I saw you found me through the 'Moms Supporting Moms' group. What resonated with you about my post?"
YOU:
"I understand. Being a mom is incredibly rewarding, but also incredibly demanding. What's a typical day like for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned trouble sleeping. How long has that been going on, and how is it impacting your energy levels during the day?"
YOU:
"And feeling disconnected from your kids... can you tell me more about that? What does that look like in your daily interactions?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what will your life look like in six months? And if you could magically transform this situation, what would be different?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy moms. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find times that work for you, and the tools and techniques you'll learn will actually *save* you time in the long run by reducing stress and increasing your focus."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you, Susan. Let's look at it this way: What's the cost of *not* addressing this? What's the impact on your health, your relationships, and your overall happiness? This program is an investment in yourself and your family's well-being. Also, I do offer a payment plan to make it more manageable."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is there anything holding you back from saying 'yes' today? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."
"Will this really work for me?"
"That's a valid question, Susan. I can't guarantee specific results, but I can tell you that my program is based on proven techniques and tailored to each individual's needs. I also offer ongoing support and guidance throughout the process. And if you're not satisfied within the first two weeks, I offer a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the cost as an investment in their well-being, and offer payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you stay focused and confident.
  • Handling objections effectively is crucial for closing sales.
  • Confidently stating your price demonstrates your value.
  • Your success directly impacts your clients' lives and your own financial well-being.
