Business Practice Lab

Let's practice enrolling clients into your group program. This lab provides a realistic scenario and walks you through the key steps.

Your Prospect

Susan, 52

Attended your free workshop on "Overcoming Trauma Triggers." Interested in the 8-week group program.

Her Situation
Divorced, empty-nester, feels stuck in the past, wants to move forward but struggles with anxiety.
Budget Concern
"I'm on a fixed income, so I need to be sure this is the right investment."
Decision Style
Cautious, needs social proof, values a supportive community.
Her Goal
"I want to feel confident and happy again. I want to enjoy my life without constantly worrying."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Review Workshop 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! It's so great to connect with you after the workshop. I really enjoyed your participation. What was your biggest takeaway from the workshop?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! Many participants found that helpful. I'm curious, what motivated you to attend the workshop in the first place? What are you hoping to achieve?"
Phase 2: Connect to Group Program Benefits 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, the 8-week group program could be a fantastic fit. It builds on the workshop concepts, providing a deeper dive into trauma recovery strategies. We focus on creating a supportive community where everyone feels safe sharing their experiences."
YOU:
"In the program, we'll cover [mention 2-3 key modules, e.g., regulating emotions, releasing limiting beliefs, and building resilience]. How do those topics resonate with you?"
YOU:
"Imagine yourself 8 weeks from now, feeling more confident, less anxious, and surrounded by a supportive group of women. How would that impact your life?"
Phase 3: Share Success Stories & Social Proof 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, I want to share a quick story about another client, Maria, who was in a similar situation. [Share a brief, relevant success story about a 50+ woman]. Her transformation was truly inspiring, and she found the group support invaluable."
YOU:
"The group dynamic is a huge part of the program's success. Clients often form lasting friendships and continue supporting each other long after the 8 weeks are over. Does that aspect appeal to you?"
YOU:
"I have several testimonials I can share with you as well, from women who have said this program changed their lives."
Phase 4: Present Offer & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on our conversation, I truly believe this program can help you achieve your goals. Are you ready to learn more about the program details and how to enroll?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. The program requires a commitment of [number] hours per week, including the group sessions and individual work. However, think about the time you're currently spending feeling anxious or stuck. What if this program could help you reclaim that time and energy?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can tell you that the program is based on proven trauma recovery principles and tailored to meet individual needs. Plus, you'll have the support of the group and my guidance every step of the way. We also offer a satisfaction guarantee."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! We offer a flexible payment plan to make the program more accessible. You can choose between [options, e.g., 3 monthly installments or a longer payment plan]. Which option works best for you?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specific questions do you have that I can answer now to help you make a decision? Or, what part of the program are you still unsure about?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to highlight when presenting the group program to a potential client?

Highlight the program's benefits, share relevant success stories, and emphasize the supportive community aspect.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the time commitment required for the group program?

Acknowledge their concern and then reframe the time commitment by highlighting how the program can help them reclaim time and energy currently spent feeling anxious or stuck.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and reviewing the workshop experience is crucial for connecting with potential group program clients.
  • Sharing relevant success stories and testimonials can provide social proof and increase trust in the program.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and offering flexible payment options can overcome enrollment barriers.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and clearly outlining the program's value is essential for successful enrollment.
