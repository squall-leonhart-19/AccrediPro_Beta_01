Former elementary school teacher, strong foundation in communication and empathy.

Excellent at building rapport, highly organized, dedicated to client well-being.

Struggles with setting boundaries, needs help with crisis intervention protocols.
Her Question
"I'm not sure how to handle a client who is threatening self-harm. Can you help me?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a client, Emily, 38, who is dealing with severe anxiety and panic attacks after a recent job loss. During our last session, Emily mentioned she felt like 'ending it all.' I froze. I didn't know what to do or say. I'm so worried about her."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah assess for immediate risk? (Suicidal ideation, plan, means, intent)
  • Does Sarah have a safety protocol in place? (Emergency contacts, crisis hotline numbers)
  • Did Sarah consult with a supervisor or mental health professional?
  • Does Sarah understand the legal and ethical obligations in cases of potential self-harm?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Immediate Safety

Emphasize the importance of assessing for immediate risk and taking appropriate steps to ensure the client's safety. This includes contacting emergency services if necessary.

2

Review Crisis Intervention Protocols

Walk Sarah through the steps of a crisis intervention protocol, including active listening, validation, and connecting the client with resources.

3

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Clarify the boundaries of Sarah's role and when it's necessary to refer to a licensed mental health professional.

4

Address Secondary Trauma

Acknowledge the emotional impact of working with clients in crisis and provide strategies for self-care and support.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concern
"Sarah, I can hear how much you care about Emily. It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed in these situations. You reached out for guidance, and that shows your commitment to doing what's best for your client."
Explore Her Response
"Tell me more about what went through your mind when Emily said she felt like 'ending it all.' What were you thinking and feeling in that moment?"
Teach the Protocol
"In situations like this, our priority is always safety. The first step is to assess for immediate risk: Does she have a plan? Does she have the means? What's her intent? Based on that assessment, we take appropriate action, which might include contacting a crisis hotline or emergency services."
Empower Her Moving Forward
"Let's role-play this scenario. I'll be Emily, and you practice asking the questions to assess her risk. This will help you feel more confident and prepared in the future."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important first step when a mentee presents a case involving potential self-harm?

The most important first step is to assess for immediate risk, including suicidal ideation, plan, means, and intent.

2. How can you best support a mentee who is feeling overwhelmed by a complex case?

Validate their feelings, provide clear and actionable steps, and reinforce the importance of self-care and boundaries.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize immediate safety in crisis situations.
  • Provide clear, actionable guidance and protocols.
  • Reinforce scope of practice and ethical obligations.
  • Foster a supportive environment for learning and growth.
  • Address secondary trauma and promote self-care.
