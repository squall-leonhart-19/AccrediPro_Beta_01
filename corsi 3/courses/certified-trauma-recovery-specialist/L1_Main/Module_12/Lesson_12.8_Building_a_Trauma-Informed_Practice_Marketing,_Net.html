Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open with me. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings, especially after everything you've been through. I want to assure you that feeling 'stuck' after a significant life transition, especially with a history of trauma, is something we can absolutely work through together."
MARIA:
"I just feel like I'm going through the motions. I don't know what I want anymore."
YOU:
"That's a very common feeling, Maria. It's like your internal compass needs recalibration. Before we start charting a new course, let's acknowledge the terrain you've already crossed. You mentioned a car accident and caring for your mother. How are those experiences impacting you today?"
MARIA:
"The accident... I don't think about it much. But Mom... she needs so much help. I feel guilty when I take time for myself."
YOU:
"That guilt is a powerful emotion, and it often masks deeper needs. Let's explore that guilt, Maria. What would it mean to prioritize your own well-being, even in the face of your mother's needs?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses a sense of being heard and understood.
  • She begins to explore the connection between past experiences and present feelings.
  • She acknowledges the impact of caregiving on her well-being.
  • She expresses a willingness to explore new possibilities.
  • She asks about strategies for managing guilt and prioritizing self-care.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to acknowledge Maria's past trauma in this scenario?

Acknowledging past trauma helps establish trust and demonstrates an understanding of how past experiences can influence present feelings and behaviors. It also opens the door for addressing potential underlying issues that may be contributing to her current state of being.

2. What open-ended question could you ask to explore Maria's feelings about her caregiving responsibilities?

"Maria, can you tell me more about what it's like caring for your mother? What are some of the challenges and rewards, and how does it impact your own time and energy?"

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge the impact of past trauma and life transitions.
  • Validate the client's feelings and experiences.
  • Ask open-ended questions to explore their challenges and goals.
  • Address potential guilt and burnout associated with caregiving responsibilities.
  • Empower clients to prioritize self-care and explore new possibilities.
