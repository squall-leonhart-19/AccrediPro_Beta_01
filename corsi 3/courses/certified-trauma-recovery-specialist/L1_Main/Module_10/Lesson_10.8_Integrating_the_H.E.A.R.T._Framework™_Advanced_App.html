You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Diane expresses a sense of being heard and understood.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to avoid dismissing a client's trauma narrative as "just a story"?

Dismissing the narrative invalidates the client's experience and minimizes the impact of trauma on their life. The narrative holds significant meaning and shapes their beliefs and behaviors.

2. What are some ways to help a client identify strengths and resilience within their trauma narrative?

Ask questions like, "What helped you get through that difficult time?" or "What did you learn about yourself as a result of that experience?" Focus on moments where the client demonstrated agency or resourcefulness.

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge the power and impact of a client's trauma narrative on their life.
  • Avoid pushing clients to rewrite their narrative too quickly; allow them to explore their feelings at their own pace.
  • Help clients identify strengths, resilience, and moments of agency within their story.
  • Tailor your responses to each client's specific experiences and needs.
  • Validate the client's emotions and provide a safe space for them to share their story.
