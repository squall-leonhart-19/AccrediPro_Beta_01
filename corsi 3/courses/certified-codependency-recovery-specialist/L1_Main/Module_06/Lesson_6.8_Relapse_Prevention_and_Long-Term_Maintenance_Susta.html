Diane, 52

Elementary School Teacher, Omaha, NE ‚Ä¢ Divorced, one adult child

Chief Complaint
Feeling "stuck" and unable to leave a toxic relationship with her adult son.
Background
Single mother since son was 5. Always put his needs first. Now feels resentful and guilty.
Symptoms
Anxiety, difficulty sleeping, constant worry about her son's choices. Neglecting her own needs.
Her Goal
"I want to be able to set boundaries with my son without feeling like a terrible mother. I want my life back."

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing this with me. It takes courage to admit that a relationship, especially with a child, is causing you pain. Many mothers in your situation struggle with similar feelings of guilt and resentment. You're not alone."
DIANE:
"I feel so ashamed. Everyone tells me I should be grateful he's still in my life."
YOU:
"It's okay to feel both love and frustration, Diane. Those feelings can co-exist. Let's explore what's making you feel stuck and how we can gently create some healthy distance. Is that something you're open to?"
DIANE:
"Yes, I desperately want to feel less responsible for his happiness."
YOU:
"Okay. Let's start by identifying the specific behaviors that trigger your anxiety and guilt. We'll then work on strategies to respond differently, focusing on your own needs and well-being. How does that sound?"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚úì Acknowledge her specific struggles: "This constant worry is exhausting."
‚ùå Blaming her: "You enabled him by always giving in."

‚ùå Promising a quick fix: "You'll feel better in a week!"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Diane says "I've never told anyone this before." (sign of trust)
  • She starts to cry (releasing pent-up emotions)
  • Her body language softens (shoulders relax, eye contact)
  • She expresses relief: "It helps just to say it out loud."
  • She agrees to explore options: "What kind of boundaries can I set?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating her feelings builds trust and rapport. She needs to feel heard and understood before she's open to change.

2. What are some signs that Diane is beginning to feel safe and comfortable in the coaching session?

Signs include: sharing vulnerable information, emotional release (crying), relaxed body language, and expressing a willingness to explore solutions.

You're Becoming a Real Coach!

Each time you practice these scripts, you're honing your ability to connect with clients on a deeper level. Diane, and women like her, need your empathy and guidance. Keep practicing, and you'll see your confidence soar!

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate a client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Focus on empathy and understanding.
  • Avoid judgment and blame.
  • Encourage small steps toward change.
  • Remember that progress, not perfection, is the goal.
