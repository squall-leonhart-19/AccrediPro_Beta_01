Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing this with me. It sounds like you're carrying a heavy load. Many people experience these feelings, especially those with a history of caretaking. What you're describing could be related to codependent tendencies, which simply means that your sense of self-worth has become overly reliant on the needs and actions of others. Does that resonate with you?"
DIANE:
"Yes, it does. I've heard the term 'codependent' before, but I never really understood it. I just thought I was a helpful person."
YOU:
"It's wonderful to be helpful, and that's a great quality. However, in codependency, helping becomes a compulsion, often at the expense of your own well-being. We can explore how that might be showing up in your relationships. Would you be open to that?"
DIANE:
"Yes, definitely. I'm ready to understand this better."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by looking at some common codependent behaviors. As I describe them, see if any feel familiar to you. Remember, there's no judgment here‚Äîjust awareness."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Diane says, "That makes so much sense!"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the key element to emphasize when introducing the concept of codependency to a new client?

Empathy and validation. It's important to create a safe space where the client doesn't feel judged or blamed.

2. Why is it important to acknowledge the positive intention behind codependent behaviors?

Acknowledging the positive intention (e.g., wanting to be helpful) helps the client feel understood and less resistant to change. It shows that you recognize the underlying motivation behind their actions.

Key Takeaways

  • Codependency involves an excessive reliance on others for self-worth.
  • Empathy and validation are crucial when introducing this concept.
  • Acknowledge the positive intentions behind seemingly negative behaviors.
  • Focus on creating awareness, not judgment.
  • Practice is key to becoming a confident coach.
