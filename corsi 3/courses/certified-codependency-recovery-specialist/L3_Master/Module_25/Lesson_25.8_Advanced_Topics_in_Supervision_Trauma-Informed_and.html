Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Strengths
"Aisha, your compassion is palpable. Sarah is lucky to have found someone so caring."
Gently Introduce Areas for Growth
"Sometimes, our empathy can lead us to lose track of the session's goals. Have you considered using a session structure template?"
Provide Specific Tools
"I use a simple agenda: 'What do you want to discuss today? What would you like to achieve by the end of this session? Let's recap the key takeaways.' It helps me, and maybe it can help you too."
Encourage Experimentation
"Try using this structure in your next session and see how it feels. I'm here to discuss how it went afterward."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is normalizing the learning experience important when supervising new practitioners?

Normalizing the learning experience helps reduce anxiety and encourages open communication, fostering a supportive environment for growth.

2. What are some strategies for helping a mentee set effective boundaries with clients?

Strategies include role-playing difficult scenarios, creating session structure templates, and discussing the importance of self-care to prevent burnout.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy is a strength, but structure is essential for effective sessions.
  • Providing practical tools and techniques empowers new practitioners.
  • Constructive feedback should always start with acknowledging strengths.
  • Mentoring strengthens the field of codependency recovery.
