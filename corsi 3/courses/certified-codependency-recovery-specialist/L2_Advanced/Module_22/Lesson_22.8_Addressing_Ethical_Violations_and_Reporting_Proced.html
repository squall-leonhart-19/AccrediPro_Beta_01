Ethical Considerations Case Study

Apply ethical principles to a complex case involving dual relationships, confidentiality, and scope of practice.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify potential ethical violations in complex client scenarios.
  • Apply ethical decision-making models to resolve dilemmas.
  • Assess the impact of dual relationships on client outcomes.
  • Develop strategies for maintaining confidentiality in group settings.
  • Recognize scope of practice limitations and appropriate referral pathways.

Complex Case Presentation

Eleanor, 48

Small business owner, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Married, two teenage children

Presenting Issue
Eleanor reports feeling overwhelmed and resentful in her marriage. She's the primary caregiver for her children and manages the finances for the family business. She suspects her husband is having an affair but avoids confrontation.
Relevant History
Eleanor attended your "Codependency Recovery 101" workshop six months ago. She has since joined your weekly support group. Her husband, Mark, recently contacted you for individual coaching to "improve communication" in their marriage.
Current Status
Eleanor discloses in the support group that she snooped through Mark's phone and confirmed her suspicions. She feels betrayed and wants to leave the marriage but fears financial instability. Mark is unaware that Eleanor is in the support group or that she knows about the affair.
Potential Conflicts
Dual relationship (Eleanor in group, Mark seeking individual coaching), confidentiality (Eleanor's disclosure in group), scope of practice (marital counseling vs. codependency recovery).

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is the potential for harm due to the dual relationship and the conflicting interests of Eleanor and Mark. Providing impartial support to both individuals becomes challenging, potentially violating the principle of non-maleficence.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Refer to the ethical guidelines of your professional organization regarding dual relationships, confidentiality, and scope of practice. For example, the American Counseling Association (ACA) Code of Ethics addresses these issues in sections A.6.a, B.1.c, and C.2.a.
Step 3: Consider Potential Courses of Action
  • Option A: Continue working with Eleanor in the support group and decline to work with Mark individually.
  • Option B: Refer both Eleanor and Mark to other professionals.
  • Option C: Attempt to work with both individuals while maintaining strict boundaries and informed consent (least advisable).
Step 4: Evaluate the Consequences of Each Option
Option A may leave Mark feeling unsupported. Option B ensures impartiality but may disrupt Eleanor's support system. Option C risks compromising objectivity and confidentiality. A thorough cost-benefit analysis is crucial.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Power Imbalance

Consider the power dynamics in the marriage and how they might influence Eleanor's decisions. Her fear of financial instability could stem from a history of financial control by Mark.

2

Safety Concerns

Assess for potential domestic violence. Eleanor's snooping could escalate the situation. Explore safety planning and resources if needed.

3

Underlying Mental Health Issues

Eleanor's anxiety and resentment could be indicative of underlying depression or anxiety disorders. Screen for mental health symptoms and refer for evaluation if necessary.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äî Refer to Appropriate Professionals

  • Suspected Domestic Violence: Immediate referral to a domestic violence shelter or hotline.
  • Suicidal Ideation: Immediate mental health evaluation and crisis intervention.
  • Legal Issues: Referral to a family law attorney for guidance on divorce and financial matters.
  • Substance Abuse: If either Eleanor or Mark is struggling with substance abuse, refer to addiction treatment specialists.
  • Marital Counseling: Since you cannot ethically provide impartial marital counseling, refer the couple to a qualified marriage and family therapist.

Phased Intervention Plan

Key Takeaways

  • Dual relationships can create significant ethical dilemmas in codependency recovery.
  • Prioritize client welfare and avoid situations that compromise objectivity or confidentiality.
  • Document all ethical decision-making processes in your clinical notes.
  • Know your scope of practice and refer to qualified professionals when necessary.
  • Ethical practice builds trust and enhances the effectiveness of your work.
