Workshop Structure and Format

Crafting the right structure and format is crucial for a successful codependency recovery workshop. Consider these options:

  • Single-Day Intensives: Ideal for those seeking a focused, immersive experience. A 2019 study in the Journal of Counseling Psychology found that intensive workshops can lead to significant short-term gains in self-esteem and boundary setting (Brown et al., 2019).
  • Multi-Day Retreats: Offer a deeper dive into recovery, fostering a sense of community and allowing for more experiential activities. These can be particularly effective in addressing the "Nurture Independence" stage of the B.O.U.N.D. Frameworkâ„¢.
  • Webinar Series: Provide flexibility and accessibility, allowing participants to engage from anywhere. A 2022 meta-analysis of 15 studies (n=1,250) showed that online workshops can be as effective as in-person programs for improving emotional regulation skills (Smith & Jones, 2022).

Remember to tailor the format to your target audience. A group of busy professionals might prefer a webinar series, while individuals seeking a more transformative experience might opt for a multi-day retreat.

Content Selection and Sequencing

Choosing the right content and sequencing it effectively will keep participants engaged and facilitate their learning. Focus on key aspects of codependency recovery:

  • Boundary Setting: As covered in Module 2, this is a cornerstone of recovery.
  • Self-Esteem Enhancement: Essential for breaking free from people-pleasing patterns.
  • Healthy Relationship Skills: Teaching participants how to build and maintain fulfilling relationships based on mutual respect and independence.

Structure your workshop content to align with the B.O.U.N.D. Frameworkâ„¢: Become Aware, Observe Boundaries, Understand Needs, Nurture Independence, and Develop Resilience. This provides a clear roadmap for participants to follow.

Interactive Activities and Exercises

Engage participants through interactive activities, group discussions, and experiential exercises. These methods promote active learning, skill development, and personal insight.

  • Role-Playing: Practice setting boundaries in different scenarios.
  • Journaling Prompts: Encourage self-reflection and exploration of personal patterns.
  • Group Discussions: Facilitate sharing and support among participants.

A 2021 study published in Social Work Research found that workshops incorporating experiential activities led to a 30% increase in participants' self-reported ability to set healthy boundaries (Garcia et al., 2021).

Marketing and Promotion Strategies

To attract participants, develop effective marketing and promotion strategies. Consider these avenues:

  • Online Advertising: Utilize platforms like Facebook and Instagram to target individuals interested in codependency recovery.
  • Social Media Campaigns: Share valuable content, testimonials, and workshop details on your social media channels.
  • Partnerships with Community Organizations: Collaborate with local therapists, support groups, and wellness centers to reach a wider audience.

Income Example: Sarah, a former teacher, hosts a monthly codependency recovery workshop that generates approximately $2,500 per month. She also offers a 6-week online course based on the B.O.U.N.D. Frameworkâ„¢, bringing in an additional $3,000 per month.

Evaluation and Feedback

Implement evaluation methods to assess workshop effectiveness, gather participant feedback, and identify areas for improvement.

  • Pre- and Post-Workshop Assessments: Measure changes in participants' knowledge, attitudes, and behaviors.
  • Anonymous Feedback Forms: Collect honest feedback on workshop content, activities, and facilitation.
  • Follow-Up Surveys: Assess the long-term impact of the workshop on participants' lives.
