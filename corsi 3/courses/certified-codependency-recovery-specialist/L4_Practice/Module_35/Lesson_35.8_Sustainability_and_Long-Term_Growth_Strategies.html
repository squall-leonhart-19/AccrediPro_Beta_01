Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Let's look at your schedule. The program requires [X hours] per week, and we can tailor it to fit your lifestyle. Think of it as investing in your future well-being and relationships. Plus, many clients find they actually GAIN time as they become more efficient and less enmeshed in other people's problems."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. I have a proven track record with clients just like you. My program is designed with personalized support and accountability. Plus, I offer a [X-day] satisfaction guarantee. If you do the work and don't see progress, I'll [offer a partial refund/additional support sessions]."
"I need to talk to my best friend/sister about it."
"Of course. I encourage you to get support. Would it be helpful if I provided you with some materials to share with them, or even scheduled a quick call with all of us to answer any questions? I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the call.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment by focusing on the value and ROI of solving their problem. Ask what it would be worth to them to finally achieve their goals.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process increases your chances of converting prospects into clients.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence builds trust and demonstrates your expertise.
  • Practicing your pricing presentation helps you communicate the value of your program effectively.
  • Consistent client acquisition efforts lead to a sustainable and thriving practice.
