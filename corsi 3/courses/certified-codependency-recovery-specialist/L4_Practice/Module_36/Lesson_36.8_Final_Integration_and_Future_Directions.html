Susan, 52

Saw your ad on Facebook. Struggling with emotional eating and weight gain after a recent divorce.

Feels lost and overwhelmed. Uses food for comfort. Wants to regain control of her health and life.
Budget Concern
"I'm on a fixed income now, so I need to be smart about where I spend my money."
Decision Style
Cautious but motivated. Needs to feel understood and supported. Responds well to empathy and practical solutions.
Her Goal
"I want to feel good about myself again. I want to stop using food as a crutch."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time. I saw you found me on Facebook. What caught your eye about my ad?"
YOU:
"That's great you resonated with it. So, Susan, what's been going on that made you want to reach out to a codependency recovery specialist?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the emotional eating. When did it start, and what triggers it?"
YOU:
"And how has the divorce impacted your eating habits and overall well-being?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what do you imagine your life will look like in six months? And if you could change one thing immediately, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, from what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed and using food to cope with the emotional fallout of your divorce. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on helping you understand the underlying codependency patterns that are driving your emotional eating. We'll work together to develop healthier coping mechanisms, set boundaries, and build your self-esteem. I have a 12-week program where we meet weekly to help you achieve your goals."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you think this approach could be the support you need?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share more details about the program and how we can get you started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Many of my clients have similar concerns. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. Think of it this way: what's the cost of not addressing this? What's the impact on your health, your happiness, your relationships? I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. It sounds like you've been through a lot. My approach is different because we focus on the root causes of your emotional eating, not just the symptoms. We'll create a personalized plan tailored to your specific needs and challenges. Plus, you'll have ongoing support and accountability to help you stay on track."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand that time is a precious commodity. However, think about how much time you're currently spending feeling bad, dealing with the consequences of emotional eating. This program is designed to be efficient and effective, helping you reclaim your time and energy in the long run. Plus, the sessions are flexible and can fit around your schedule."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What's a good response to the objection "I need to think about it?"

A good response is to acknowledge their need to think, then ask what specifically they need to think about (time, investment, etc.) to address their concerns.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Know your pricing and present it with confidence and clarity.
  • Focus on the value you provide to help clients overcome their codependency patterns.
  • Remember that consistent effort in client acquisition leads to a thriving practice.
