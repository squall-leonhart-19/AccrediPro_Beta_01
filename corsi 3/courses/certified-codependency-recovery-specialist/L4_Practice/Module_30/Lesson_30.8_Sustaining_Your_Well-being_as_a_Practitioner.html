Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that investing in yourself is a big decision. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. What if, in 12 weeks, you had a clear vision for your future, renewed energy, and a concrete plan to achieve your goals? What would that be worth to you?"

"I respect that. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? Knowing this will help me provide you with the information you need to make the right decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your solution, and a confident close.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Focus on the value and ROI of your program. Ask what their problem is costing them now, and what it would be worth to solve it.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your program and its impact on clients' lives.
  • Believe in your ability to help clients achieve their goals.
