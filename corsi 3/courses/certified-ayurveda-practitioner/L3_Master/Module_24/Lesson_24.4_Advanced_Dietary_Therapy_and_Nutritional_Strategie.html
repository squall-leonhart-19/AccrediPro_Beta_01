Food Combining Principles

Ayurveda emphasizes the importance of proper food combining for optimal digestion. When incompatible foods are consumed together, they can create ama (undigested food toxins) in the body.

Here are some key food combining guidelines:

  • Avoid combining milk with: Sour fruits, salty foods, meat, fish, yogurt, or bread.
  • Avoid combining fruits with: Vegetables, grains, or proteins (except for certain sweet, ripe fruits like mangoes with dairy).
  • Avoid combining beans with: Dairy, eggs, or fruits.
  • Honey should never beheated. Heating honey changes its molecular structure, making it difficult to digest and potentially toxic.

These guidelines are based on the principle that different foods require different digestive enzymes and transit times. When incompatible foods are combined, the digestive process becomes inefficient, leading to fermentation, gas, bloating, and the accumulation of ama.
