Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for a 12-week program."
"I understand your concern. However, the program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your daily life. We'll break it down into manageable steps. Plus, investing in your health now will actually give you *more* time and energy in the long run. Think of it as an investment, not an expense."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. Ayurveda is different because it's holistic and personalized. We're not just treating symptoms; we're addressing the root cause of your imbalance. Also, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not seeing results within the first month, we'll re-evaluate and adjust the plan or offer a partial refund."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific results (because everyone's body is different), I can guarantee my commitment to your success. I‚Äôll provide you with personalized guidance, ongoing support, and the tools you need to achieve your health goals. I have a proven track record of helping women just like you feel better, and I'm confident we can achieve similar results for you."
"I need to discuss this with my family first."
"That's perfectly reasonable. What specific questions do you anticipate they might have? Perhaps I can provide you with some information to share with them, or we could even schedule a brief call together to address their concerns directly."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful Ayurveda discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's challenges, presenting your Ayurvedic approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the value of your program, compare it to other expenses, and offer a payment plan.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition and sales is crucial for scaling your Ayurveda practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you build rapport, understand client needs, and present your solution effectively.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident responses to address client concerns.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and confidently state your pricing.
  • Remember, you're providing a valuable service that transforms lives.
