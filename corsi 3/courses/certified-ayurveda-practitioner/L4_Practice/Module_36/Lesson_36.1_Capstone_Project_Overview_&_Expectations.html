Capstone Project Overview

Project Requirements & Client Selection

Your capstone project must adhere to the following key requirements:

  1. Client Selection: Choose a client who is willing to participate and provide informed consent. Ideal clients are those with whom you can establish a clear therapeutic relationship and who are motivated to make lifestyle changes. Consider friends, family or acquaintances seeking wellness guidance.
  2. Comprehensive Assessment: Conduct a detailed Ayurvedic assessment, including pulse diagnosis (Nadi Pariksha), tongue diagnosis (Jihva Pariksha), and observation of physical characteristics. Document your findings meticulously, clearly differentiating between Prakriti and Vikriti.
  3. Intervention Plan: Develop a personalized intervention plan based on your assessment, incorporating dietary recommendations, lifestyle adjustments (Dinacharya), and herbal remedies (if applicable). Ensure your recommendations align with the client's doshic imbalances and health goals.
  4. Documentation: Maintain detailed records of your client interactions, assessment findings, intervention plan, and progress. Your documentation should demonstrate a clear understanding of Ayurvedic principles and the DOSHA Frameworkâ„¢.

Client Selection Criteria: While you can't diagnose or treat medical conditions, you *can* work with clients looking for wellness support. Focus on areas like stress management, improved sleep, better digestion, or increased energy. Be sure to clearly communicate your role as an Ayurvedic Practitioner, not a medical doctor.

Ethical Considerations & Boundaries

As an Ayurvedic Practitioner, it's crucial to adhere to ethical guidelines and maintain professional boundaries. Key considerations include:

  • Informed Consent: Obtain informed consent from your client before starting the assessment and intervention process. Explain the nature of your services, potential risks and benefits, and their right to withdraw at any time.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain the confidentiality of your client's personal information and health history. Do not disclose any information without their explicit consent.
  • Scope of Practice: Practice within the scope of your training and expertise. Do not diagnose or treat medical conditions, and refer clients to qualified healthcare professionals when necessary.
  • Dual Relationships: Avoid dual relationships with clients that could compromise your objectivity or create a conflict of interest.
  • Professionalism: Maintain a professional demeanor at all times, respecting your client's autonomy and cultural background.

Ethical Dilemma: If faced with an ethical dilemma, consult with a mentor, supervisor, or ethics board for guidance.

Potential Challenges & Time Management

Completing the capstone project can be challenging, but with proper planning and time management, you can overcome these obstacles. Common challenges include:

  • Client Availability: Coordinating schedules with your client can be difficult. Establish clear communication channels and be flexible with your availability.
  • Time Constraints: Balancing the project with your other commitments can be overwhelming. Break down the project into smaller, manageable tasks and set realistic deadlines.
  • Data Collection: Gathering accurate and comprehensive data can be time-consuming. Use standardized assessment forms and practice your diagnostic skills.
  • Intervention Adherence: Clients may struggle to adhere to the intervention plan. Provide ongoing support and encouragement, and adjust the plan as needed.

Time Management Tips: Create a project timeline, prioritize tasks, and avoid procrastination. Allocate specific time slots for client interactions, data analysis, and report writing.
