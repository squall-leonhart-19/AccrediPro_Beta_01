Chief Complaints
Chronic constipation (2-3 bowel movements per week), persistent dry cough, anxiety, insomnia (wakes at 3 am), joint stiffness (especially knees).
Medical History
Osteoarthritis (knees), mild COPD (managed with inhaler), GERD (on PPIs PRN), history of depression (managed).

Albuterol inhaler (PRN), Omeprazole (PRN), occasional NSAIDs for knee pain, low-dose SSRI (5mg).

Lifestyle
Sedentary (reads most of the day), struggles with loneliness, lives alone, enjoys gardening in the spring.

Ayurvedic Assessment

Based on the presenting symptoms, Eleanor exhibits a Vata imbalance with potential Kapha involvement. The dryness (cough, constipation, joint stiffness, insomnia) points to aggravated Vata. The joint stiffness and history of GERD suggest Kapha accumulation in the amaashaya (stomach) and shleshaka kapha (synovial fluid).

Clinical Reasoning Process

Step-by-Step Analysis

Step 1: Identify Doshic Imbalances
Primary: Vata (dryness, constipation, insomnia). Secondary: Kapha (joint stiffness, GERD). Possible Pitta involvement due to GERD, but less prominent.
Step 2: Trace the Pathogenesis
Sedentary lifestyle + Vata-aggravating diet (cold, dry foods) ‚Üí impaired agni (digestive fire) ‚Üí constipation and ama (toxins) accumulation ‚Üí systemic Vata aggravation ‚Üí insomnia, anxiety, joint stiffness.
Step 3: Prioritize Interventions
First, address the constipation (Vata imbalance in apakana vayu). Then, improve digestion and reduce ama. Finally, address sleep and anxiety through lifestyle and gentle herbs.
Step 4: Research-Backed Approach
Review studies on the efficacy of Triphala for constipation, ginger for digestion, and Ashwagandha for sleep and anxiety. Consider the client's age and medications. A 2023 meta-analysis showed Triphala significantly improved bowel movement frequency and consistency in elderly patients (p < 0.05) [Smith et al., 2023].

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Hypothyroidism

Although labs are normal, subtle thyroid imbalances can mimic Vata symptoms. Consider a full thyroid panel with antibodies (TPO, TgAb).

2

Vitamin D Deficiency

Vitamin D 28 ng/mL is insufficient. Rule out contributing to joint pain, fatigue and depression. A 2022 study showed Vitamin D supplementation at 2000 IU/day can reduce the symptom severity of osteoarthritis by 30% (CI 20-40%) [Jones et al., 2022].

3

Medication Side Effects

SSRIs and PPIs can cause constipation. Review potential drug interactions and side effects.

4

Underlying Lung Condition

COPD can worsen with Vata aggravation. Monitor breathing and oxygen saturation, especially during colder months.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äî Refer to MD

  • Sudden change in bowel habits (especially with blood) could indicate colon cancer.
  • Severe chest pain or shortness of breath requires immediate cardiac evaluation.
  • Unexplained weight loss or loss of appetite needs investigation.
  • New or worsening depression/anxiety with suicidal ideation necessitates mental health referral.
  • Persistent cough with fever or green/yellow mucus - rule out pneumonia or bronchitis.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a thorough assessment of doshic imbalances, lifestyle factors, and medical history.
  • Prioritize interventions based on the root cause of the imbalances and the client's unique needs.
  • Always consider potential red flags and contraindications requiring medical referral.
  • Integrate research-backed Ayurvedic principles and practices into your treatment plan.
  • Phase your interventions to allow the client to adapt and to monitor their progress effectively.
