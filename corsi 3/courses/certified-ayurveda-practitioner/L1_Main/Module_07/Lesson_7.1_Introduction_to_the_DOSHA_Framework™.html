Origins and Principles

The D.O.S.H.A. Frameworkâ„¢ is rooted in the ancient wisdom of Ayurveda, which views health as a state of balance among the body, mind, and spirit. It's designed to be practical and accessible, particularly for practitioners entering the field from other backgrounds. The framework's principles include:

  • Individualization: Recognizing that each person is unique and requires a customized approach.
  • Holism: Addressing the interconnectedness of physical, mental, andemotional well-being.
  • Prevention: Emphasizing proactive strategies to maintain health and prevent disease.
  • Empowerment: Encouraging clients to take an active role in their healing process.

D.O.S.H.A. Frameworkâ„¢ Steps

The D.O.S.H.A. Frameworkâ„¢ consists of several key steps, each designed to build upon the previous one, creating a comprehensive and effective treatment plan:

  1. Discovery: Gathering comprehensive information about the client's health history, lifestyle, and current symptoms.
  2. Observation: Assessing the client's physical and mental characteristics to determine their Prakriti (constitution) and Vikriti (imbalances).
  3. Solution: Developing a personalized treatment plan that addresses the client's specific needs, including dietary recommendations, lifestyle modifications, and herbal remedies.
  4. Harmony: Implementing the treatment plan and monitoring the client's progress, making adjustments as needed to restore balance.
  5. Action: Empowering the client to take an active role in their health, providing ongoing support and education to maintain long-term well-being.

Ethical Considerations

When applying the D.O.S.H.A. Frameworkâ„¢, practitioners must adhere to ethical guidelines to ensure client safety and well-being. Key considerations include:

  • Informed Consent: Obtaining clear consent from clients before initiating any treatment.
  • Confidentiality: Protecting client privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of their personal information.
  • Scope of Practice: Practicing within the boundaries of their training and expertise, referring clients to other healthcare professionals when necessary.
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respecting clients' cultural beliefs and values, tailoring treatment plans to their individual needs.
