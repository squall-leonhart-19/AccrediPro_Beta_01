Addressing Physical and Emotional Changes

Ageing brings about a multitude of physical and emotional changes that can impact sexuality. These may include hormonal shifts, decreased libido, erectile dysfunction, vaginal dryness, changes in body image, and societal pressures. It's crucial to acknowledge and validate these experiences.

A 2021 study published in the Journal of Sexual Medicine found that over 50% of men aged 40-70 experience some degree of erectile dysfunction, while a 2023 meta-analysis in Menopause revealed that up to 60% of postmenopausal women report experiencing vaginal dryness. These statistics highlight the prevalence of these issues and underscore the importance of addressing them with compassion and evidence-based strategies.

Challenging Societal Expectations

Societal expectations often portray ageing as a period of sexual decline. It is important to challenge these narratives and promote body positivity and self-acceptance. Help clients embrace the wisdom and beauty of their bodies at every stage of life. Encourage them to redefine what sexuality means to them, focusing on joy, connection, and self-expression.

Integrating Mindfulness and Self-Compassion

Mindfulness practices and self-compassion are invaluable tools for cultivating a positive relationship with the ageing body. Encourage clients to practice mindful breathing, body scan meditations, and loving-kindness meditations. These practices can help them to cultivate acceptance, reduce self-criticism, and embrace the present moment.
