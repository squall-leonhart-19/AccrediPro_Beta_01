Clinical Reasoning Process: A Step-by-Step Approach

Breaking Down the Case

Step 1: Identifying Core Imbalances
Eleanor presents with imbalances across multiple domains: physical (chronic pain, IBS), emotional (anxiety, disconnection), and energetic (low libido, difficulty experiencing pleasure). The history of endometriosis is a significant factor, potentially leading to pelvic floor dysfunction and altered nerve pathways.
Step 2: Uncovering Potential Root Causes
The OCP may be suppressing natural hormone fluctuations, contributing to low libido. SSRIs can also impact sexual function. The chronic pain and emotional stress are likely perpetuating a cycle of tension and disconnection. Gut-brain axis dysfunction could be contributing to both IBS and anxiety.
Step 3: Connecting the Dots: The Energetic Impact
From a tantric perspective, Eleanor's symptoms suggest a blockage in the Svadhisthana chakra (sacral chakra), the center of creativity, sexuality, and pleasure. The chronic pain and emotional numbness may be hindering the flow of energy in this region.
Step 4: Prioritizing Integrative Interventions
Focus on a holistic approach: addressing the physical pain, supporting emotional well-being, and opening energetic pathways. Consider incorporating pelvic floor therapy, mindfulness practices, and targeted tantric techniques.

Differential Considerations: Beyond the Obvious

Considering Other Possibilities

1

Pudendal Neuralgia

Chronic pelvic pain, especially with sitting, could indicate pudendal nerve entrapment. Requires specialized evaluation.

2

Hormonal Imbalances

OCP use can mask underlying hormonal issues. Consider comprehensive hormone testing (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, DHEA-S).

3

Trauma History

Explore any potential history of sexual trauma, as it can significantly impact pelvic pain and sexual function.

4

Interstitial Cystitis

Bladder pain and urinary urgency could indicate interstitial cystitis, a chronic bladder condition.
