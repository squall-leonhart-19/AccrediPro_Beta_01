Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's feelings at the start of the session?

Validating her feelings helps build trust and rapport, making her feel safe and understood. It also normalizes her experience, reducing any shame or guilt she may be experiencing.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate Diane feels comfortable and safe?

Non-verbal cues include relaxed body language, making eye contact, nodding in agreement, and a softer tone of voice.

Key Takeaways

  • Always begin by validating the client's feelings and normalizing their experience.
  • Create a safe, non-judgmental space for the client to share openly.
  • Focus on the emotional and energetic components of sensuality, not just the physical.
  • Adapt your language to the client's level of understanding.
  • Emphasize consent and honoring boundaries at every step.
