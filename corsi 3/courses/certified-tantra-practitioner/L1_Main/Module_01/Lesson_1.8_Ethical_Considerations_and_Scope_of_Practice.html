Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing so openly. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings. Many women experience a similar disconnection, especially after periods of stress or emotional upheaval. I want you to know that reawakening your senses and reconnecting with your body is absolutely possible."
MARIA:
"I hope so. It feels like I'm living in my head all the time. I barely notice things anymore."
YOU:
"That's a very common experience, and it's often a learned response to protect ourselves. We can gently and safely begin to explore ways to bring your awareness back into your body. How does that sound to you?"
MARIA:
"It sounds good, but also a little scary. I'm not sure where to start."
YOU:
"I understand. We'll take it one small step at a time, and I'll be here to guide you. We'll focus on simple sensory exercises that you can easily incorporate into your daily life. Would you be open to trying a very brief exercise right now, just to get a sense of what's possible?"
MARIA:
"Okay, I'm willing to try."

‚ùå Dismissing her fears or anxieties
‚úì Acknowledge and validate her concerns. "It's understandable to feel apprehensive."
‚ùå Overwhelming her with too much information
‚úì Focus on one or two key areas at a time. "Let's start with your sense of touch."
‚ùå Pushing her beyond her comfort zone
‚úì Emphasize that she's in control and can stop at any time.
‚ùå Using overly technical or spiritual language

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief that you understand her experience.
  • She seems more relaxed and open to trying new things.
  • She asks clarifying questions about the exercises.
  • Her body language softens (e.g., shoulders relax, deeper breaths).
  • She expresses a sense of hope or curiosity.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to establish with a client who feels disconnected from their body?

Safety and trust. Make sure the client feels heard and understood.

2. What is an example of a simple sensory exercise you might suggest to Maria?

Mindfully touching a soft object (like a blanket or a pet) and noticing the sensations.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy is crucial when working with clients who feel disconnected from their bodies.
  • Start with simple, accessible exercises to build trust and confidence.
  • Acknowledge and validate the client's fears and anxieties.
  • Focus on the client's present experience, not past traumas.
  • Emphasize that the client is in control and can stop at any time.
