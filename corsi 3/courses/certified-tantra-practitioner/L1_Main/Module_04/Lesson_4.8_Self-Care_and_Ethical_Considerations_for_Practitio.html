Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on reframing her limiting beliefs. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open with me. I hear your desire to feel hopeful again. The beliefs 'I'm too old to start over' and 'I'll never find love again' ‚Äì these are powerful statements. Let's explore where they come from, and whether they're truly serving you now."
MARIA:
"Well, it just feels true. I see all these younger people succeeding, and I feel like I missed my chance."
YOU:
"I understand that feeling. Let's reframe this. Instead of 'too old,' what if we saw this as 'experienced' and 'wise'? You have decades of life experience that these younger people don't. How can we leverage that?"
YOU:
"And about finding love again, many women find fulfilling relationships after 50. What if we reframe 'never' to 'not yet'? What small steps can you take to be open to new connections?"
MARIA:
"I don't know... It's hard to imagine."
YOU:
"I get it. It's a process. But let's start by challenging these beliefs. I'm going to ask you some questions to help you see things from a different angle. Are you open to that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the first thing you should do when a client shares a deeply ingrained limiting belief?

First, validate their feelings and acknowledge the difficulty of changing deeply held beliefs. Build trust before attempting to reframe.

2. What's a better reframe for "I'm too old to learn new skills" (common in 40+ women)?

Try: "I bring decades of experience to learning, and I can leverage that to learn efficiently" or "I'm choosing to invest my time in skills that truly align with my goals now."

Key Takeaways

  • Reframing is about shifting perspectives, not denying reality.
  • Always validate the client's feelings before attempting to reframe.
  • Encourage clients to challenge their limiting beliefs by asking questions.
  • Focus on empowering clients to create a more hopeful and fulfilling future.
