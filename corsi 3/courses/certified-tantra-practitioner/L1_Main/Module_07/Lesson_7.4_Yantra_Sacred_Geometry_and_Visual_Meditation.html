Detailed Examination of the Sri Yantra

The Sri Yantra is one of the most revered Yantras in Tantric tradition. It represents the totality of the cosmos and the union of Shiva and Shakti. It consists of nine interlocking triangles, four pointing upwards (Shiva) and five pointing downwards (Shakti), surrounded by lotus petals and a square frame. Meditating on the Sri Yantra is said to bring abundance, prosperity, and spiritual enlightenment. It's a powerful tool for Embodied Integration, aligning with the final stage of The S.A.C.R.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢.

Techniques for Meditating with Yantras

Here are several techniques to deepen your meditation with Yantras:

  1. Focusing the gaze: Gaze softly at the center of the Yantra, allowing your eyes to relax. Let the image fill your awareness without strain.
  2. Visualization: Close your eyes and visualize the Yantra in your mind's eye. Hold the image as clearly as possible.
  3. Mantra association: Combine the visual focus with a relevant mantra, repeating it silently or aloud.
  4. Progressive absorption: Start by focusing on the outer elements of the Yantra and gradually move your attention inwards, towards the central point (bindu).

Regular practice can lead to profound insights and a deeper connection with your inner self, enhancing the Release & Reframe aspect of The S.A.C.R.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢ by facilitating a shift in perspective.
