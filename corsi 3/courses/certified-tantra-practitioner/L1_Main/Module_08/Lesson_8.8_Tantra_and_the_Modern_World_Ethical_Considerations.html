Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on creating a safe and supportive space. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for being so open and vulnerable with me. I understand it can be challenging to talk about these intimate aspects of your life. I want you to know that everything we discuss here is confidential and held with the utmost respect. My intention is to create a space where you feel safe to explore your feelings and desires without judgment."

"Thank you. I appreciate that. It's been hard to even admit this to myself."
YOU:
"That's completely understandable. Many women experience similar feelings, especially after significant life changes. Let's start by exploring what 'feeling alive and vibrant' means to you specifically. What does that look and feel like in your body and in your life?"
DIANE:

YOU:
"Okay, dancing and confidence. Let's dive deeper into that. What kind of dancing did you enjoy? And what specifically made you feel confident and attractive back then?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to establish a safe space at the beginning of a coaching session focused on sensuality?

Establishing a safe space encourages the client to feel comfortable sharing vulnerable feelings and experiences without judgement, which is essential for effective exploration and growth.

2. What are some non-verbal cues you might look for to determine if a client feels safe and comfortable during a coaching session?

Relaxed body language, eye contact, and a willingness to share personal information are all indicators that a client feels safe and comfortable. Conversely, crossed arms, fidgeting, or avoidance of eye contact might suggest discomfort.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize creating a safe and supportive coaching environment.
  • Listen deeply and validate your client's feelings and experiences.
  • Empower your client to discover their own solutions and embrace their authentic desires.
  • Practice active listening and ask open-ended questions.
  • Remember that the coaching process is a journey of self-discovery, not a destination.
