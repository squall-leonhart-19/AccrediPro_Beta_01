Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for being so open. Many women experience a disconnect from their bodies, especially after major life changes. What you're feeling is valid and incredibly common. I want you to know that rediscovering that connection is absolutely possible."
DIANE:
"It's just... I don't even know where to start. It feels like a part of me is missing."
YOU:
"That feeling of being 'missing' is something I've heard from many clients. We're going to approach this gently, using sensory awareness as our guide. We'll start with simple, safe practices to help you reconnect with your physical sensations, without any pressure or expectation."
YOU:
"Before we begin, I want to emphasize that this is YOUR journey. We'll move at your pace, and you're in control every step of the way. Does that feel comfortable for you?"
DIANE:
"Yes, that sounds much better than what I imagined. I was worried it would be all about... you know... *that*."
YOU:
"Absolutely not, Diane. While sexuality can be a part of this journey for some, our focus will be on cultivating presence and self-awareness. We're building a foundation of connection, and that looks different for everyone."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to establish safety and trust before suggesting any sensory awareness exercises?

Many clients, especially those with trauma or disconnection from their bodies, may feel vulnerable. Establishing safety and trust helps them feel comfortable exploring their sensations without fear or judgment.

2. What are some ways to adapt the coaching script to make it sound more like "you"?

Use your own natural language and phrases. Share a brief, relevant personal anecdote if appropriate. Infuse your unique personality and warmth into the conversation.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize establishing safety and trust with your client.
  • Use simple, accessible language to explain complex concepts.
  • Emphasize the client's control and autonomy throughout the process.
  • Focus on cultivating presence and self-awareness, not just sexual pleasure.
  • Adapt your coaching style to match your unique personality and voice.
