Her Question
"I feel overwhelmed. How do I handle clients with trauma?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Concern

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 50, who mentioned past trauma in our session. I felt out of my depth. I wanted to help, but I'm worried about retraumatizing her. Should I refer her out? How do I even talk about this?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Sarah's understanding of trauma-informed practices.
  • Her comfort level in holding space for sensitive topics.
  • Her awareness of her own boundaries and limitations.
  • Appropriate referral protocols and resources.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Affirm Her Concerns

Validate Sarah's awareness of the potential for retraumatization. It shows she's being responsible.

2

Define Scope of Practice

Reiterate that as L1 practitioners, we don't provide trauma therapy. Our role is to create safety and embodiment.

3

Teach Grounding Techniques

Equip Sarah with simple grounding exercises she can use with clients who become dysregulated.

4

Role-Play Referral Conversations

Practice how to sensitively suggest a referral to a therapist or trauma specialist.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Provide Constructive Guidance

Start with Appreciation
"Sarah, thank you for bringing this to supervision. It takes courage to admit when you feel unsure. That's a sign of integrity."
Assess Her Understanding
"What are your thoughts on how trauma impacts the body and our work?"
Teach the Principle
"Remember, our role is NOT to 'fix' trauma. It's to create a safe container for clients to connect with their bodies and their pleasure. That can be deeply healing in itself."
Empower Next Steps
"How would you like to proceed with Emily? What feels like the most ethical and supportive approach?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary role of an L1 practitioner when working with clients who have a history of trauma?

To create a safe and supportive environment for embodiment and pleasure, not to provide trauma therapy.

2. What are some grounding techniques you could suggest to your mentee?

Deep breathing exercises, body scans, visualization, or connecting with the senses.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a crucial component of professional development.
  • New practitioners need support in navigating complex client scenarios.
  • Trauma-informed practices are essential for ethical client care.
  • Mentoring involves teaching principles, not just giving answers.
