The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a potential client, Emily, 50, who is seeking help with low libido after menopause. Emily has read about the benefits of Tantra for intimacy but is also dealing with past trauma. I'm worried about my ability to handle such a complex case."

Your Considerations:

  • Is Sarah aware of the contraindications for certain practices with trauma survivors?
  • Does she understand the importance of establishing clear boundaries and informed consent?
  • Is she equipped to co-regulate with a client who may become triggered during a session?
  • Does Sarah need to refer Emily to a therapist specializing in trauma?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Affirm Her Concerns

Start by acknowledging Sarah's awareness of the complexities involved. "It's excellent that you're recognizing the potential challenges here. That shows responsibility."

2

Review Scope of Practice

Discuss what falls within her L1 scope and what requires advanced training or referral. Emphasize that client safety is paramount.

3

Trauma-Informed Principles

Explain the importance of titration (gradual exposure), grounding techniques, and empowerment when working with trauma. Review relevant ethical considerations.

4

Encourage Collaboration

Suggest the possibility of working in conjunction with a licensed therapist. This ensures Emily receives comprehensive care.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Sarah, I'm so impressed that you're seeking supervision for this case. It shows a deep commitment to ethical practice and client well-being. That's a fantastic quality in a practitioner."
Guiding Questions
"What are your initial thoughts about Emily's case? What aspects feel within your comfort zone, and what feels like a stretch?"
Educate & Empower
"Remember, as an L1 practitioner, your focus is on basic awareness and gentle exploration. With Emily, that might mean focusing on self-compassion practices and mindful movement, while deferring any deep emotional work to a trained therapist."
Next Steps
"How would you like to proceed with Emily? Would you like to discuss how to explain the scope of your practice to her? Or perhaps practice some phrases for setting healthy boundaries?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important aspect of providing supervision to a new Tantra practitioner?

Ensuring client safety and ethical practice, even if it means referring out.

2. How can you empower a new practitioner while still providing guidance?

By asking guiding questions, validating their concerns, and helping them identify their strengths.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is essential for maintaining ethical standards in Tantra practice.
  • New practitioners often need guidance on scope of practice and client suitability.
  • Trauma-informed principles are crucial when working with complex client cases.
  • Collaboration with other professionals can enhance client care.
  • Your mentorship empowers the next generation of Tantra practitioners.
