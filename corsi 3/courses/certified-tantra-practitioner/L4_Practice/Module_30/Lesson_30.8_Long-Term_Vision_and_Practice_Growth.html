Handling Objections

Common Concerns & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if this is right for me."
"I understand. It's a big step. What specifically makes you hesitant? Perhaps I can address your concerns directly."
"I don't have time for this right now."
"I hear you. Life gets busy. What if we could find a way to integrate this into your life in a manageable way? My clients often find that prioritizing self-care actually INCREASES their energy and productivity in other areas."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that budget is a concern. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. This isn't just about spending money; it's about investing in yourself and your well-being. What is feeling alive and connected worth to you?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. It's an important decision. What aspects are you still considering? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Connection & Rapport, Exploring the Challenge, Presenting the Solution, and Invitation & Next Steps.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive"?

Explore the value the client will receive, emphasizing the investment in themselves and their well-being. Ask what feeling alive and connected is worth to them.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for a successful discovery call.
  • Understanding the client's challenges deeply allows you to tailor your solution effectively.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and emphasize the value you provide.
  • Be prepared to handle objections with empathy and address concerns directly.
  • Consistent effort in client acquisition can lead to a thriving and fulfilling practice.
