Susan, 49

Saw your ad on Facebook. Booked a free consultation.

Her Situation
Stressed, overwhelmed, feeling disconnected from her body and her partner.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if I can afford this right now, but I know I need something."
Decision Style
Emotional decision-maker. Needs to feel safe and understood. Responds well to testimonials.
Her Goal
"I want to feel alive again. I want to reconnect with my sensuality and feel more confident."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Luna, and I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. To start, tell me a little bit about yourself and what you're hoping to achieve."
YOU:
"It's wonderful that you're taking this step for yourself. What specifically resonated with you about my Facebook ad?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling disconnected. Can you describe what that feels like in your body?"
YOU:
"How is this affecting your relationship with your partner, and with yourself?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to address these feelings? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and instantly transform your experience, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're craving a deeper connection with yourself and your partner, and you're looking for a way to reignite your sensuality. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on embodied awareness and conscious connection. We'll work together over 12 weeks to release limiting beliefs, explore your authentic expression, and cultivate a deeper sense of self-love. Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s and 50s, find this transformative. For example, I worked with Sarah, a 45-year-old teacher who felt the same way you do. After working together, she reported feeling more confident, connected, and alive than she had in years."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe I can help you. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Wonderful! Let's talk about the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals and mothers. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. We can work together to create a schedule that works for you. Plus, think about the time you'll save by feeling more energized and focused!"
"It's a big investment."
"I hear you. Think about what you're currently spending on things that aren't truly fulfilling you ‚Äì dinners out, shopping, etc. This is an investment in yourself and your well-being. And I offer payment plans to make it more accessible. What feels like a manageable monthly investment for you?"
"I'm not sure if this is right for me."
"That's perfectly okay. What specific concerns do you have? I want to make sure you feel completely comfortable and confident before moving forward. Let's address any hesitations you might have."
"Can I get back to you?"
"Of course. When would be a good time for us to reconnect? I'd love to answer any further questions you might have. Also, just so you know, I have a couple of other women interested in starting soon, and I only take on a limited number of clients each month to ensure personalized attention."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment as an investment in their well-being and quality of life. Offer payment plans if available.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection.
  • Understanding the client's problem deeply allows you to tailor your approach effectively.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can turn hesitations into commitments.
  • Presenting your pricing with conviction communicates the value of your services.
  • Consistent practice and a belief in yourself are essential for success in client acquisition.
