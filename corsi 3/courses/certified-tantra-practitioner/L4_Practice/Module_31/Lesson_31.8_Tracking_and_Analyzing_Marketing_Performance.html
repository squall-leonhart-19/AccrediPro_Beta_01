Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. It's important to be mindful of your budget. Let's explore some options. I offer payment plans to make it more accessible. Also, consider this an investment in your happiness and well-being. What is finding your purpose worth to you?"
"I need to think about it and do some research."
"Of course! I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision. What specific information are you looking for? I can provide testimonials from past clients or answer any questions you have right now."
"I'm not sure if I have the time to commit to a 12-week program."
"That's a valid concern. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We have weekly sessions, but you can access the materials and complete the exercises at your own pace. Think of it as dedicating a few hours each week to invest in yourself and your future."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"I understand your hesitation. I can assure you that my program is based on proven principles and tailored to each individual's needs. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first few weeks, I'll provide a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the recommended discovery call structure?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Connection, Understand the Problem & Desires, Present Your Approach & Solution, and Close with Confidence.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the objection, "I'm not sure if I can afford it right now"?

A good strategy is to explore payment plan options, highlight the value of the program as an investment in their well-being, and ask what finding their purpose is worth to them.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and connecting with your prospect is crucial for a successful discovery call.
  • Understanding your prospect's problems and desires allows you to tailor your approach and offer a relevant solution.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy can turn potential "no's" into "yes's".
  • Presenting your pricing clearly and confidently communicates the value of your program.
  • Consistent client acquisition leads to a thriving and impactful practice.
