Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'm ready to commit to 12 weeks."
"I understand that committing to a 12-week program can feel like a big step. However, this is a journey of self-discovery and healing, and it takes time to create lasting change. We can explore a shorter introductory package to get you started, or break down the program into smaller, manageable phases. How does that sound?"
"I'm worried about the financial investment."
"I understand that investing in yourself is a significant decision. I offer flexible payment plans to make the program more accessible. Consider the long-term benefits of emotional healing, increased confidence, and renewed joy in your life. Many of my clients find that the investment pays off in terms of improved relationships, career opportunities, and overall well-being."
"I'm nervous about exploring Tantra because of the misconceptions."
"I completely understand your concern. There are many misconceptions about Tantra, and it's important to me that you feel safe and informed. My practice is strictly non-sexual and focuses on emotional, spiritual, and energetic healing. I encourage you to ask any questions you have, and I'm happy to provide resources and information to clarify any doubts."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary focus of your Tantra practice, and how do you communicate this to potential clients?

The primary focus of my Tantra practice is emotional, spiritual, and energetic healing. I clearly communicate this to potential clients by emphasizing that my practice is strictly non-sexual and focuses on creating a safe and supportive environment for self-discovery and empowerment.

2. How do you address potential client concerns about the misconceptions surrounding Tantra?

I address potential client concerns by acknowledging the misconceptions and providing clear information about my practice. I encourage clients to ask questions, provide resources, and emphasize the non-sexual nature of my approach.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical client acquisition involves clear communication, transparency, and respect for client boundaries.
  • Address potential client concerns and misconceptions with empathy and accurate information.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and highlight the value and benefits of your services.
  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and ensure you address key points.
  • Focus on creating a safe and supportive environment for clients to explore their emotions and heal.
