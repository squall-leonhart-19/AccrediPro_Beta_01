Handling Objections Like a Pro

Common Concerns & Your Empowered Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand that investing in your health is a big decision. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. Can I ask, what's the cost of *not* addressing these issues? Consider the impact on your energy, your career, and your relationships. My program is designed to provide lasting results, potentially saving you money on ineffective treatments in the long run. We can also discuss payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. It's completely understandable to feel skeptical. The difference with my approach is that we focus on identifying and addressing the *root causes* of your symptoms, rather than just masking them with temporary fixes. We'll work together to create a personalized plan that's tailored to your unique needs and biochemistry. Plus, you'll have my ongoing support and guidance throughout the entire process."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course! It's important to feel confident in your decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I'm happy to answer any questions you have to help you make an informed choice."
"I'm too busy right now."
"I appreciate your honesty. Many of my clients are busy professionals and mothers. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. We can work together to create a schedule that works for you, and the results you'll achieve ‚Äì increased energy, improved focus, and better overall health ‚Äì will actually *increase* your productivity and free up more time in the long run. Think of it as an investment in your future self."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Warm Welcome & Connection, Deep Dive into Her Needs, Present Your Solution, and Confident Close.

2. What's a good response to "I'm not sure if I can afford it?"

A good response involves acknowledging their concern, exploring the cost of *not* addressing the issue, highlighting the program's long-term value, and offering payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and fears is paramount.
  • Confidently presenting your program's value is essential for closing the sale.
  • Handling objections effectively can turn potential clients into paying clients.
  • Consistent practice and a belief in your program's effectiveness will boost your confidence and success.
