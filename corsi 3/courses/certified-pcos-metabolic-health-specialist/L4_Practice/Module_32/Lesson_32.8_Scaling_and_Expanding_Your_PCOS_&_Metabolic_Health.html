Handling Objections with Grace

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I need to do more research."
"I understand. What specific information are you looking for? Perhaps I can point you to some resources or answer any remaining questions you have right now. I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"That's a valid concern. Can you tell me more about your schedule? We can explore ways to integrate the program into your existing routine. The beauty of this program is that it's designed to be flexible and adaptable to your lifestyle. We can also break down the work into smaller, manageable steps."
"My husband isn't supportive."
"I'm sorry to hear that. It's important to have support. Would he be open to joining a quick call with us so I can address any concerns he may have? Sometimes, having a clear understanding of the process and the potential benefits can ease those concerns."
"I'm worried I won't be able to stick with it."
"I hear you. Many of my clients have felt that way before starting. That's why I provide ongoing support and accountability throughout the program. We'll work together to create a plan that's realistic and sustainable for you. Plus, you'll have the support of our online community to keep you motivated."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Express gratitude for their time, state the call's purpose, and ask an engaging question to understand their initial interest.

2. How can you effectively address the objection that "I need to do more research?"
