The Client Case

Sarah's Client: Emily, 32

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily, 32, has PCOS, insulin resistance, and anxiety. I recommended inositol, NAC, and magnesium. She's been on it for two weeks and says her anxiety is worse! I'm worried I made the wrong call."

Key Areas for Discussion:

  • Did Sarah inquire about potential interactions between the supplements and Emily's existing anxiety medication?
  • Was the dosage of magnesium appropriate, considering potential side effects like diarrhea, which can exacerbate anxiety?
  • Did Sarah adequately explain the potential for temporary side effects and set realistic expectations?
  • Did she consider lifestyle factors (sleep, stress) that could be contributing to the increased anxiety?

Your Mentoring Approach

Teaching Points for Sarah

1

Acknowledge Her Concerns

Start by validating Sarah's concern and acknowledging the importance of addressing adverse reactions promptly. "It's great that you're paying close attention to your client's feedback, Sarah. That's a sign of a responsible practitioner."

2

Explore Potential Interactions

Guide Sarah to consider potential interactions between the supplements and Emily's existing medications. "Did you ask Emily about other medications or supplements she's taking? Sometimes, there can be unexpected interactions."

3

Review Dosage and Timing

Discuss the importance of appropriate dosages and timing of supplements. "Let's review the dosages you recommended. Could the magnesium be contributing to digestive upset, which in turn might be affecting her anxiety?"

4

Emphasize Holistic Assessment

Encourage Sarah to consider lifestyle factors that could be contributing to Emily's anxiety. "Beyond supplements, what's Emily's sleep like? How's her stress level? Remember, we need to address the whole picture."

Constructive Feedback

Guiding Sarah Towards Solutions

Start with Positive Reinforcement
"Sarah, it's commendable that you're proactively seeking guidance. Your willingness to learn is a huge asset!"
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before I offer my suggestions, what are your initial thoughts on why Emily's anxiety might be worsening? What's your gut telling you?"
Offer Targeted Guidance
"One thing to consider is the potential interaction between magnesium and anxiety medications. Also, high doses of magnesium can sometimes cause digestive upset, which can indirectly worsen anxiety. Perhaps we could explore a lower dose or a different form of magnesium."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on this, what adjustments would you like to make to Emily's protocol? I'm here to support you, but I want you to feel confident in your decisions."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important first step when a mentee brings you a challenging case?

Actively listen and validate their concerns. This builds trust and creates a safe space for learning.

2. Why is it crucial to ask open-ended questions instead of giving direct answers?

Open-ended questions encourage critical thinking, problem-solving, and independent decision-making in the mentee.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves active listening, validation, and constructive feedback.
  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating. Empower your mentees to develop their clinical reasoning skills.
  • Sharing your own experiences, including mistakes, can help normalize the learning process and build trust.
  • Focus on teaching principles, not just providing answers, to foster independent practice.
  • Remember, you are helping to shape the next generation of PCOS and metabolic health specialists!
