Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Prioritize Foundational Elements

Explain the importance of addressing sleep, stress, and gut health before implementing advanced interventions. A 2021 study in the *Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism* showed that even modest improvements in sleep can significantly improve insulin sensitivity.

2

Teach the 'Triage' Method

Demonstrate how to break down complex cases into manageable steps. Focus on the most pressing issue first (e.g., insulin resistance) before addressing other concerns. Explain that a 2023 meta-analysis in *Nutrients* found that targeting insulin resistance can improve outcomes in women with PCOS by 30-40%.

3

Review Relevant Supplementation

Discuss evidence-based supplementation strategies for insulin resistance and Hashimoto's, such as inositol and selenium. Emphasize the importance of proper dosing and potential contraindications. For example, a 2018 study in *Gynecological Endocrinology* showed that inositol supplementation improved insulin sensitivity in 60% of women with PCOS.

4

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Remind Aisha of the importance of collaborating with Sarah‚Äôs endocrinologist regarding thyroid medication adjustments. Stress the need for clear communication and appropriate referrals.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important initial step when mentoring a new practitioner who feels overwhelmed by a complex case?

The most important initial step is to validate their feelings and normalize the experience of feeling overwhelmed. This helps build trust and creates a safe space for learning.

2. When reviewing a complex case with a mentee, what is the benefit of asking questions before providing answers?

Asking questions encourages critical thinking and helps the mentee develop their own clinical reasoning skills. It also allows you to assess their current understanding and identify areas where they need the most support.

Key Takeaways
