Differential Considerations

Considering Alternative Explanations

1

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Migraines, bloating, allergies, and skin issues can be indicative of MCAS. Consider testing for histamine levels and ruling out triggers.

2

Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO)

Bloating, GERD, and PPI use increase the risk of SIBO. A breath test can confirm diagnosis.

3

Autoimmune Condition

While less likely given normal CBC, rule out early stages of autoimmune disease with appropriate antibody testing if symptoms persist.

4

Mold Toxicity

Consider if Eleanor lives or works in a water-damaged building. Mold exposure can trigger inflammation and neurological symptoms.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address Eleanor's liver function in this case?

The elevated ALT indicates liver stress, which can impair detoxification processes and exacerbate hormonal imbalances. Supporting liver function is crucial for overall health and hormone regulation.

2. What are the key considerations when addressing Eleanor's endometriosis while she is on birth control?

Birth control masks underlying hormonal imbalances. Focus on supporting natural hormone balance through diet, lifestyle, and targeted supplements. Encourage communication with her MD regarding long-term endometriosis management.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-based approach that considers the interconnectedness of various physiological processes.
  • Addressing gut health and liver function is often foundational for resolving hormonal and neurological issues.
  • It's crucial to differentiate between masking symptoms and addressing root causes when clients are on medications.
  • Prioritize interventions and educate clients on the importance of gradual, sustainable changes.
