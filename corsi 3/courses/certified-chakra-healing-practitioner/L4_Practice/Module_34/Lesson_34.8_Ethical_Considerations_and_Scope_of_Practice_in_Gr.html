Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Time is precious. The workshop is designed to be a focused, efficient way to learn these techniques. It's just [duration of workshop], and you'll gain tools you can use in just a few minutes each day. Think of it as investing in your long-term well-being."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. I can share testimonials from past participants who have found it incredibly helpful. Also, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you attend the entire workshop and don't feel you've gained valuable tools, I'll gladly refund your investment."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely. I offer a payment plan of two installments to make it more accessible. Would you like me to send you the details?"
"I don't know anything about chakras."
"That's perfectly fine! The workshop is designed for beginners. I'll explain everything in a clear, easy-to-understand way, and you'll have plenty of opportunities to ask questions."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to include in your workshop promotion to attract Karen?

Highlight the workshop's ability to provide simple, effective stress management techniques and practical tools for daily use. Emphasize the supportive group environment and the satisfaction guarantee.

2. How can you address Karen's budget concern without lowering your workshop price?

Offer a payment plan, highlight the value and long-term benefits of the workshop, and share testimonials from satisfied participants. Also, emphasizing the early bird pricing can incentivize her to register quickly.

Key Takeaways

  • Group workshops offer a fantastic way to scale your impact and income.
  • Discovery calls are crucial for understanding your potential participants' needs and addressing their concerns.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and handling objections are essential for successful enrollment.
  • Focus on the value and benefits participants will receive, rather than just the cost.
  • Practice your script and closing lines to feel more natural and confident during your calls.
