Case Study: Anxiety and Pain Relief

Case Study: Emily, 72, Hospice Patient

Client: Emily, a 72-year-old female diagnosed with terminal cancer, was admitted to hospice care. She presented with severe anxiety, chronic pain (rated 7/10), and difficulty sleeping. Emily expressed a deep fear of death and a sense of unfinished business.

Intervention: Over four weeks, Emily received weekly chakra healing sessions focusing on the root, heart, and crown chakras. Each session included gentle energy work, guided meditations, and affirmations tailored to her fears and concerns. We incorporated emotional release techniques from the 'Integrate Shifts' step of the A.L.I.G.N. Frameworkâ„¢.

Outcomes: After the first session, Emily reported a significant reduction in anxiety and a decrease in pain (rated 4/10). By the fourth session, she expressed a sense of peace and acceptance regarding her impending death. Her sleep improved, and she was able to communicate more openly with her family. Emily passed away peacefully two weeks later, surrounded by loved ones.

Ethical Considerations

When providing chakra healing for end-of-life care, it is essential to adhere to ethical guidelines and respect the patient's autonomy and dignity. Obtain informed consent, ensure confidentiality, and collaborate with the healthcare team. Be mindful of cultural and religious beliefs, and avoid imposing your own spiritual views. Remember, your role is to provide comfort and support, not to proselytize or make promises you cannot keep. According to the National Hospice and Palliative Care Organization, holistic therapies like chakra healing should be integrated into a comprehensive care plan, with clear communication among all providers.
