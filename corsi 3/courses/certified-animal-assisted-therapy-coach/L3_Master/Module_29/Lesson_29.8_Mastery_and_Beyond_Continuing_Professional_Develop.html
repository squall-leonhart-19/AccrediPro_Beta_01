The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Intake Process

What Sarah Tells You:

"I've created this long intake form, but I'm not sure if it covers everything. I'm also spending so much time on each intake that I am exhausted."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is the intake form too extensive?
  • Is she clear on what information is essential?
  • Is she setting appropriate time limits for intake?
  • Is she addressing potential red flags effectively?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Streamlining Intake

Help Sarah identify the essential questions for a thorough yet time-efficient intake. What's "need to know" vs. "nice to know"?

2

Red Flag Assessment

Review how to assess for contraindications and potential risks during the intake process. Discuss specific questions and scenarios.

3

Boundary Setting

Discuss strategies for setting and maintaining healthy boundaries with clients from the initial intake. This includes time limits and communication protocols.

4

Building Confidence

Emphasize Sarah's strengths and provide positive reinforcement to combat imposter syndrome. Encourage her to trust her instincts and training.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Effort
"Sarah, I can see how much thought and effort you've put into creating this intake process. It's clear you're dedicated to providing the best service to your clients."
Ask for Self-Reflection
"What parts of the intake process do you find most challenging or time-consuming? Where do you feel you might be getting 'stuck'?"
Offer Specific Suggestions
"Consider prioritizing questions related to the client's history with animals and their current mental health status. You can always gather additional details later as needed."
Empower Decision-Making
"Based on our discussion, what adjustments do you feel would be most beneficial for you and your clients? How can I support you in implementing these changes?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of supervision in AAT coaching?

The primary goal is to support the professional development and competence of new AAT coaches, ensuring they provide ethical and effective services.

2. How can you best address a mentee's imposter syndrome?

By providing positive reinforcement, highlighting their strengths, and sharing your own experiences with self-doubt.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is essential for maintaining ethical standards and ensuring quality in AAT coaching.
  • Effective supervision involves providing constructive feedback, guidance, and support to new practitioners.
  • Building confidence and addressing imposter syndrome are crucial aspects of mentoring.
  • Encouraging self-reflection and critical thinking empowers mentees to develop their skills and expertise.
