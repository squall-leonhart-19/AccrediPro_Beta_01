Her Question

The Case She Presents

Lisa's Client Case

What Lisa Tells You:

"I was working with Sarah, 10, who is grieving the loss of her dog. During our session with my therapy dog, Bella, Sarah started crying uncontrollably and said she missed her dog so much. I froze. I didn't know if I should hug her, distract her, or just let her cry. I felt so unprepared."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Lisa have a plan for emotional dysregulation?
  • Did she understand the stages of grief in children?
  • Was she projecting her own feelings onto the situation?
  • Did she have a debriefing plan for herself and her animal partner?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Lisa

1

Validate Lisa's Feelings

"It's normal to feel overwhelmed when a client expresses intense emotions. It shows you care."

2

Teach Emotional Regulation Techniques

Share techniques like grounding exercises, deep breathing, and the "safe place" visualization. Explain how to teach these to clients.

3

Discuss Transference and Countertransference

Help Lisa understand how her own feelings about loss might be influencing her reaction to Sarah.

4

Emphasize the Importance of Self-Care

Discuss the emotional toll of AAT and the need for regular self-care practices, including debriefing with Bella.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Empathy
"Lisa, I remember feeling the same way when I started. It's tough when a client is in distress. You're not alone."
Ask Before Telling
"What did you notice in your body when Sarah started crying? What was your initial reaction? Let's explore that."
Teach a Specific Skill
"One technique I find helpful is the '5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise'. It helps both you and the client regulate emotions in the moment. Have you heard of it?"
Reinforce Boundaries
"Remember, you don't have to 'fix' their sadness. Your role is to create a safe space for them to experience it. Sometimes, just being present is enough."
Animal Well-being
"How do you think Bella was affected by Sarah's distress? What's your plan for debriefing after a session like that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three strategies for helping a mentee deal with a client experiencing intense emotions?

Three strategies include: 1) validating their feelings, 2) teaching emotional regulation techniques, and 3) discussing transference and countertransference.

2. Why is it important to discuss self-care with new AAT practitioners?

AAT can be emotionally taxing. Discussing self-care helps prevent burnout and vicarious trauma, ensuring the practitioner can sustainably provide quality care.

Key Takeaways

  • Validate feelings: Normalize the challenges new practitioners face.
  • Teach skills: Provide concrete techniques for emotional regulation and boundary setting.
  • Encourage reflection: Help mentees understand their own reactions and biases.
  • Prioritize self-care: Emphasize the importance of sustainable practice.
  • Animal welfare: Always consider the well-being of the animal partner.
