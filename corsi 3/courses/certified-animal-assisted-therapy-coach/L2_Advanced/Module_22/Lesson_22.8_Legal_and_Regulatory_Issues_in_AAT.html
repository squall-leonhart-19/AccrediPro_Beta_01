Advanced Clinical Ethics Case Study

This case explores the ethical complexities of AAT coaching. Consider scope of practice, client autonomy, and animal welfare.

Complex Case Presentation

Eleanor, 48

Marketing Executive, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Married, two children

Presenting Issue
Eleanor seeks AAT coaching to address work-related stress and anxiety. She believes her Bernese Mountain Dog, Gus, would benefit from being a therapy animal and wants guidance on training and certification.
Client Goals
Reduce stress, improve work-life balance, certify Gus as a therapy dog to bring to her office to improve morale.
Animal Information
Gus, 3-year-old Bernese Mountain Dog. Generally well-behaved, but can be reactive to loud noises and unfamiliar people approaching quickly.
Relevant History
Eleanor has a history of pushing boundaries and perfectionistic tendencies. She tends to over-schedule herself and Gus. Gus previously showed signs of stress during basic obedience training (excessive panting, lip licking).
Ethical Concern
Eleanor's desire to certify Gus as a therapy dog may be driven by her own needs rather than Gus's well-being. Potential conflict of interest and risk of animal distress.
Initial Assessment
Eleanor demonstrates limited awareness of Gus's stress signals and may not fully appreciate the demands placed on therapy animals.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Steps

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is balancing Eleanor's desire to certify Gus as a therapy dog with the potential for compromising Gus's welfare. Is it ethical to proceed when the client's goals may not align with the animal's best interests?
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Refer to the AAT coaching ethical guidelines, focusing on animal welfare, client autonomy, and scope of practice. Principle A: Prioritize the animal's physical and emotional well-being. Principle B: Respect client's autonomy while ensuring informed consent. Principle C: Operate within the boundaries of your training and expertise.
Step 3: Consider Potential Consequences
If Gus is forced into a role he's not suited for, he may experience chronic stress, anxiety, and behavioral problems. Eleanor may become frustrated and resentful if Gus doesn't meet her expectations. Failure to address these concerns could damage the client-coach relationship and the reputation of AAT coaching.
Step 4: Develop a Plan of Action
Engage in open and honest communication with Eleanor about the potential risks and benefits of therapy animal work. Conduct a thorough assessment of Gus's temperament and stress threshold. Explore alternative ways for Eleanor to manage stress and improve work-life balance that don't involve Gus.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key ethical considerations in this case?

Balancing client autonomy with animal welfare, ensuring informed consent, and operating within your scope of practice.

2. What are some potential red flags that would warrant a referral in this case?

Suspected animal abuse, client resistance to considering the animal's welfare, severe anxiety or aggression in the animal, and deterioration of the client's mental health.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are common in AAT coaching, requiring careful consideration of all stakeholders.
  • Animal welfare should always be the top priority, even when it conflicts with client desires.
  • Open and honest communication is essential for building trust and navigating ethical challenges.
  • Knowing your limitations and seeking expert consultation when needed is a sign of ethical competence.
