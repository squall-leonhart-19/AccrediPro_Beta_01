Clinical Reasoning Process

Step-by-Step Case Analysis

Step 1: Identify Key Systems Involved
This case involves the neurological (migraines, anxiety, sleep), cardiovascular (hypertension), psychological (anxiety, trauma history), and lifestyle (sedentary, caffeine-dependent) systems. The interconnectedness of these systems is crucial to consider.
Step 2: Prioritize Presenting Symptoms
Given the client's goals and the potential for immediate impact, prioritize anxiety reduction and sleep improvement. Addressing these can create a foundation for managing migraines and increasing social engagement.
Step 3: Integrate AAT Evidence
Research supports the use of AAT for anxiety and sleep disorders. A 2023 study in Applied Animal Behaviour Science found that regular interaction with a therapy dog reduced anxiety scores by 32% (CI 25-39%) in adults with GAD (n=67).1 Another study in Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine showed improved sleep latency and duration in participants who engaged in AAT (p < 0.05).2
Step 4: Develop a Targeted Intervention Plan
Focus on structured interactions with Buddy to leverage the human-animal bond. This might include calming activities like petting, grooming, or gentle walks in nature. Implement mindfulness exercises involving Buddy to ground Eleanor in the present moment and reduce anxiety.

Differential Considerations

Alternative Explanations & Co-morbidities

1

Hormonal Imbalances

Given Eleanor's age, perimenopause should be considered. Hormonal fluctuations can exacerbate migraines, anxiety, and insomnia. Consider a referral for hormone testing.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Migraines and anxiety can be linked to deficiencies in magnesium, B vitamins, and Vitamin D. Assess Eleanor's diet and consider targeted supplementation based on lab results.

3

Cervicogenic Headaches

Rule out neck-related causes of migraines, especially given her sedentary job. A referral to a physical therapist or chiropractor may be warranted.

4

Underlying Autoimmune Condition

Consider screening for autoimmune markers, as these can sometimes manifest as migraines and generalized anxiety. A comprehensive blood panel can help rule this out.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-thinking approach, considering the interconnectedness of various physiological and psychological systems.
  • AAT can be a valuable adjunct to conventional treatments, particularly for anxiety, sleep disorders, and social isolation.
  • It's crucial to rule out underlying medical conditions and co-morbidities that may be contributing to the client's symptoms.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare professionals is essential to provide comprehensive and integrated care.
  • A phased treatment approach allows for gradual implementation of interventions and ongoing monitoring of progress.
