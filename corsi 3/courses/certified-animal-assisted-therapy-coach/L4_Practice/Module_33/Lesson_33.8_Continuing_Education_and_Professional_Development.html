Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Hi Susan, it's so lovely to connect with you! Thank you for scheduling this call. I noticed you attended my workshop on animal-assisted therapy - what resonated most with you?"
YOU (Sarah):
"That's wonderful! Many women find that connection with animals is deeply healing. Tell me a bit more about what's going on in your life right now and what brought you to explore this path."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"It sounds like you're at a pivotal point, Susan. What does 'finding purpose' look like for you? What activities make you feel most alive and fulfilled?"
YOU (Sarah):
"And what are the biggest obstacles preventing you from creating that life right now? Is it lack of clarity, confidence, or something else?"
YOU (Sarah):
"Imagine it's a year from now, and you've successfully integrated animals into your career. What does your day-to-day look like? How do you *feel*?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Approach 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're craving a career that aligns with your love for animals and provides a sense of purpose. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU (Sarah):
"My Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching program is designed to help women just like you. We'll work together over 12 weeks to clarify your vision, identify your unique skills, and create a concrete plan to launch your AAT practice. I provide mentorship, resources, and a supportive community to help you every step of the way. Many of my clients are earning a comfortable income within 6-12 months of completing the program."
YOU (Sarah):
"The program includes weekly coaching calls, access to my AAT resource library, and personalized feedback on your business plan. Does this sound like something that could help you achieve your goals?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, I truly believe this program can help you create a fulfilling and profitable career with animals. Are you ready to take the next step and explore how we can work together?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Time is precious. The program requires approximately 5-7 hours per week, including our coaching calls. However, many of my clients find that it actually saves them time in the long run by providing clarity and direction. How about we explore how you could integrate the program into your current schedule? We can identify areas where you can delegate or eliminate tasks to make room for your dreams."
"I'm worried about the investment."
"I hear you. Investing in yourself is a big decision. Let's break down the value you'll receive: 12 weeks of personalized coaching, access to my AAT resource library, a supportive community, and a concrete business plan. Many of my clients recoup their investment within 6-12 months through their AAT practices. What if this investment allowed you to create a sustainable income doing what you love? How would that change your life?"
"I need to think about it."
"Absolutely. This is an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the program content, the time commitment, or the investment? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make an informed choice. Perhaps we can schedule a follow-up call to address any remaining questions."
"I'm not sure if I'm qualified."
"That's a common concern, Susan. The truth is, many successful AAT coaches come from diverse backgrounds. Your love for animals and your desire to help others are the most important qualifications. I'll provide you with the training, resources, and support you need to develop your skills and expertise. Remember, everyone starts somewhere. I believe in your potential, and I'm here to guide you every step of the way."

Case Study: From Corporate to Canine Career

Meet Emily: From Burnout to Bliss

Client: Emily, 42

Presenting Symptoms: Emily was experiencing burnout in her corporate job and felt a deep longing to work with animals. She lacked clarity on how to transition her skills and passions into a viable career.

Intervention: Emily enrolled in the Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching Program. Through the program, she identified her unique strengths (project management and communication) and discovered a niche in providing AAT services to corporate wellness programs.

Outcomes: Within 6 months of completing the program, Emily launched her AAT business, offering stress-reduction workshops with therapy dogs to local companies. She is now earning a comfortable income, working fewer hours, and experiencing a profound sense of purpose.

Case Study: Finding Fulfillment After Retirement

Meet Carol: Retirement Reinvented

Client: Carol, 68

Presenting Symptoms: Carol was recently retired and felt a lack of purpose and connection. She had always loved animals and wanted to find a way to give back to her community.

Intervention: Carol joined the Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching Program. She explored various AAT modalities and discovered a passion for working with seniors in assisted living facilities, offering pet therapy visits with her trained cat.

Outcomes: Carol now volunteers several hours a week at a local assisted living facility, bringing joy and companionship to residents. She has found a renewed sense of purpose and connection, and her cat has become a beloved member of the community.

Check Your Understanding

What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?
The four phases are: Rapport Building, Deep Discovery, Present Your Solution & Approach, and Close & Next Steps.
What are some common objections and how can you confidently respond to them?
Common objections include concerns about time commitment, investment, qualifications, and needing to think about it. Confident responses address these concerns by highlighting the value of the program, offering solutions, and providing reassurance.
Why is it important to practice your pricing and call-to-action?
Practicing your pricing and call-to-action allows you to present them with confidence and clarity, increasing the likelihood of converting potential clients into paying clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call script is essential for converting leads into clients.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence builds trust and overcomes barriers.
  • Presenting your pricing clearly and confidently demonstrates the value of your services.
  • A strong call-to-action encourages potential clients to take the next step.
  • Sharing success stories and case studies builds credibility and inspires confidence.
