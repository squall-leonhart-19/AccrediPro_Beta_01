Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Intent 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah. It's great to connect with you. I understand you're looking for support with managing stress and finding more balance. Is that right?"
YOU (Sarah):
"My intention for this call is to understand your current challenges and see if my program is the right fit for you. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Challenges 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Tell me more about what's causing you to feel overwhelmed. What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU (Sarah):
"What have you tried in the past to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU (Sarah):
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the most stressed you've ever been, where would you rate your stress level right now? And what impact is this having on your relationships, your health, and your overall well-being?"
Phase 3: Present Solution & Value 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're juggling a lot and feeling the pressure. I understand. Many of my clients come to me feeling exactly the same way. Is that accurate?"
YOU (Sarah):
"My program is designed to help you create sustainable strategies for managing stress, improving your work-life balance, and reclaiming your sense of control. We'll work together over 12 weeks to identify your specific stressors, develop personalized coping mechanisms, and implement practical tools for a more balanced life. Most clients report feeling significantly less stressed within the first few weeks."
YOU (Sarah):
"Imagine yourself in 3 months feeling calm, centered, and in control of your time. How would that impact your life?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Then let me tell you about the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. This program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to prioritize and find pockets of time that work for you. How much time per week do you think you could realistically dedicate to this?"
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. This is a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"I'm not sure if it will work for me."
"I understand your hesitation. I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first two weeks, you'll receive a full refund. I'm confident that if you commit to the process, you'll see results."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Let's break down the investment. My 12-week program is designed to provide lasting results. What would it be worth to you to finally feel less stressed, more in control, and more balanced?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: Connection & Intent, Deep Dive into Challenges, Present Solution & Value, and Close & Next Steps.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive?"

A good response is to acknowledge their concern, then break down the investment and highlight the value they will receive in return. Ask them what it would be worth to finally solve their problem.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you understand your prospect's needs and present your program effectively.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence is crucial for closing the sale.
  • Practice saying your price with conviction and highlighting the value you provide.
  • Consistent client acquisition can lead to a significant income and a fulfilling career.
