Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my dog is suitable for AAT."
"That's a valid concern. We can schedule an initial assessment session to evaluate his temperament and determine if AAT is the right fit. There's no obligation to continue if it's not a good match."
"I'm worried it won't work."
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee results, I can assure you that I'm committed to providing the best possible care and support. Many clients see positive changes within weeks. Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions: "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?"

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script regularly to build confidence.
  • Address client objections with empathy and provide clear solutions.
  • Confidently state your pricing and highlight the value of your services.
  • Focus on the client's needs and desired outcomes to build trust.
  • Remember that consistent effort leads to sustainable income growth.
