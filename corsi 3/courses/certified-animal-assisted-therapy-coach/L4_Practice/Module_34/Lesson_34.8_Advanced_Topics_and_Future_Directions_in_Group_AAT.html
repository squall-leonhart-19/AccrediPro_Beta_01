Your Ideal Client

Susan, 52

Saw your flyer at the local community center. Interested in the "Calm Canines" workshop.

Her Situation
Empty-nester, feeling lonely, looking for connection and purpose. Loves dogs.
Budget Concern
"I'm on a fixed income, so I need to be careful with my spending."
Decision Style
Values genuine connection. Needs to feel safe and supported. Appreciates a structured program.
Her Goal
"I want to feel less alone and find a new hobby that brings me joy."

Discovery Call Script for Group Enrollment

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for calling about the Calm Canines workshop! I'm Sarah, and I'm so glad you're interested. What caught your eye about the program?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! Many people find that working with animals brings a unique sense of peace and joy. Have you worked with animals before?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"I understand you're looking for connection and a new hobby. Can you tell me more about what you're hoping to gain from this workshop?"
YOU:
"What does 'feeling less alone' look like for you? What kind of activities do you enjoy?"
YOU:
"What's most important to you in a group setting? Safety? Support? Structure?"
Phase 3: Present the Workshop 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, Calm Canines seems like a great fit. It's a 6-week workshop designed to help people like you find connection and purpose through gentle interactions with therapy dogs. We focus on stress reduction techniques, mindfulness, and building a supportive community."
YOU:
"The workshop includes [list key features: dog handling skills, guided meditation, group discussions, etc.]. It's a safe and supportive environment where you can learn new skills, connect with others, and experience the unconditional love of animals."
YOU:
"We've had participants in their 40s, 50s, and 60s all say this program has helped them find joy and make new friends. One participant, Maria, age 48, said 'I was feeling lost after my kids left home. This workshop gave me a new purpose and a wonderful group of friends.'"
Phase 4: Enroll & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like something you'd be interested in?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the investment and how to register."

Handling Enrollment Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'm good enough with dogs."
"That's perfectly understandable! No prior experience is required. We'll teach you everything you need to know in a safe and supportive way. Our therapy dogs are specially trained and gentle. It's all about learning and enjoying the experience. Plus, our trainers are there to guide you every step of the way."
"It's a bit more expensive than I was expecting."
"I understand. Let's talk about the value you're getting. The workshop includes [reiterate key features and benefits]. Many participants find that the connection, stress relief, and new skills are well worth the investment. Think of it as an investment in your well-being and happiness. We also offer a payment plan if that makes it more manageable."
"The dates don't quite work for me."
"I'm sorry to hear that. We are planning another session in [Month]. Would you like me to add you to the waiting list so you'll be notified as soon as the dates are confirmed? Alternatively, I can offer you a discount on a one-on-one session with a therapy dog if that's of interest."
"I'm worried about being in a group."
"That's a valid concern. We prioritize creating a safe and inclusive environment. Our groups are small (max 10 participants) and we focus on building connection and support. You're welcome to observe the first session and see if it's a good fit."

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on building rapport and understanding the client's needs and desires.
  • Present the workshop as a solution to their specific problems.
  • Address objections with empathy and offer solutions.
  • State your price with confidence and highlight the value.
  • Always end with a clear call to action.
