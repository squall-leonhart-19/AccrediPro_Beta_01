Discovery Call Script: Group Workshop

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out! I'm excited to explore how AAT coaching can benefit your residents. I appreciate you taking the time to connect."
YOU:
"To start, could you tell me more about your residents and what you're hoping to achieve with this workshop?"
Phase 2: Understand the Needs 10 min
YOU:
"What are some of the biggest challenges your residents face in terms of well-being and social engagement?"
YOU:
"Have you tried any similar activities in the past? What were the outcomes, and what could have been better?"
YOU:
"What are your key metrics for success? How will you measure the impact of the workshop on your residents?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like your residents could greatly benefit from a structured AAT coaching workshop that focuses on reducing social isolation, boosting mood, and enhancing overall well-being. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My AAT coaching workshops are designed to create a safe and engaging environment where residents can connect with animals, learn valuable coping skills, and experience the therapeutic benefits of human-animal interaction. We can tailor the activities to meet the specific needs and abilities of your residents."
YOU:
"I have designed a 4-week program that includes [mention specific activities like animal-assisted mindfulness, gentle movement with animals, and creative expression]. We can also incorporate pre- and post-workshop assessments to measure the impact on residents' mood and social engagement."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, does this sound like the kind of workshop you're looking for? ... Great! Let me share the pricing options and how we can move forward."

Handling Objections: Group Workshops

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"Your price is higher than other activity providers."
"I understand your concern about cost. However, my AAT coaching workshops are unique because they offer a structured, therapeutic approach with measurable outcomes. I invest time in customizing the program to meet the specific needs of your residents, and I provide pre- and post-workshop assessments to demonstrate the impact. Can you tell me more about what the other activity providers offer? Perhaps we can compare specific deliverables and value."
"I'm not sure if our residents will be receptive to animals."
"That's a valid concern. My AAT coaching approach prioritizes safety and comfort. We carefully screen the animals to ensure they are well-suited for interacting with vulnerable populations, and we provide a supportive environment where residents can choose their level of engagement. We can also start with a small introductory session to gauge interest and address any concerns."
"We have a very limited budget."
"I appreciate you sharing that. I'm happy to explore options to make the workshop more accessible within your budget. We could start with a shorter program, offer a smaller group size, or explore grant opportunities to offset the cost. My goal is to find a solution that works for both of us."
"Can you provide references from other facilities?"
"Absolutely! I'd be happy to provide you with contact information for several assisted living facilities that have successfully implemented my AAT coaching workshops. They can share their experiences and the positive impact the program has had on their residents. I can send those over to you immediately."

Case Study: Group Workshop Success

Case: Harmony Haven Assisted Living

Client: Harmony Haven Assisted Living Facility

Presenting Problem: Residents experiencing increased social isolation and decreased engagement in activities, leading to lower mood and overall well-being.

Intervention: A 6-week customized AAT coaching workshop was implemented, focusing on animal-assisted mindfulness, gentle movement with animals, and creative expression activities tailored to the residents' abilities and interests.

Outcomes:

  • 85% of residents showed improved mood scores based on pre- and post-workshop assessments (Geriatric Depression Scale).
  • 70% of residents reported increased social interaction and engagement in group activities.
  • Staff observed a noticeable decrease in agitation and restlessness among residents during and after the workshops.
  • The facility reported increased resident satisfaction and positive feedback from family members.

Conclusion: The AAT coaching workshop effectively addressed social isolation and improved the overall well-being of residents at Harmony Haven Assisted Living Facility. The customized approach and focus on individual needs contributed to the positive outcomes.

Check Your Understanding

What are some key questions to ask a potential client when offering group AAT coaching workshops?
Key questions include: "What are your residents' specific needs and interests?", "What are your goals for the workshop?", "Have you tried similar activities in the past?", and "What are your key metrics for success?"
How can you address the objection that your price is higher than other activity providers?
Emphasize the unique value of your AAT coaching workshops, including the structured, therapeutic approach, measurable outcomes, customization, and pre- and post-workshop assessments. Offer to compare specific deliverables and value.

Key Takeaways

  • Group AAT coaching workshops provide a valuable service for assisted living facilities and other organizations serving vulnerable populations.
  • Effective sales strategies include building rapport, understanding client needs, presenting a tailored solution, and confidently handling objections.
  • Customization, measurable outcomes, and a focus on safety and comfort are key differentiators for your AAT coaching workshops.
  • Scaling your income with group workshops can significantly increase your practice's revenue.
  • Always follow up with potential clients after the initial consultation to reinforce your value proposition and address any remaining concerns.
