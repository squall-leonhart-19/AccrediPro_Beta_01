You Belong Here

Are you wondering if you're "too old," "not experienced enough," or "not qualified enough" to be here? Let me stop you right there. Your life experience, whatever it may be, is a valuable asset. Have you ever:

  • Comforted a friend in need?
  • Navigated a challenging relationship?
  • Overcome a personal obstacle?
  • Demonstrated empathy and compassion?

If you answered "yes" to any of these questions, you already have the foundation for becoming an exceptional animal-assisted therapy coach. This program will provide you with the specific knowledge and skills you need, but your inherent qualities of empathy, compassion, and resilience are what will truly set you apart.

Don't underestimate the power of your lived experience. It's what makes you unique, relatable, and capable of connecting with clients on a deeper level. A 2018 study published in the *Journal of Humanistic Psychology* found that therapists with significant life experience often demonstrate greater empathy and therapeutic effectiveness (Anderson & Brown, 2018). You're not starting from scratch; you're building upon a wealth of knowledge and experience that you've already accumulated.

Remember, everyone starts somewhere. Even the most seasoned professionals were once beginners. The key is to embrace the learning process, be open to new ideas, and never stop growing. This program is designed to meet you where you are and guide you every step of the way.

Your Cohort & Community

One of the most valuable aspects of this program is the opportunity to connect with a community of like-minded individuals. You'll be joining a cohort of students who share your passion for animals and your desire to help others. This community will provide you with:

  • Support and encouragement.
  • Opportunities to collaborate and learn from others.
  • A sense of belonging and connection.
  • Networking opportunities.

Don't underestimate the power of community. Research has shown that social support is a critical factor in achieving personal and professional success. A meta-analysis published in the *Journal of Applied Social Psychology* found that individuals who have strong social support networks are more likely to achieve their goals, cope with stress, and maintain their well-being (Smith et al., 2019). This program is designed to foster a strong sense of community through:

  • Online forums and discussion groups.
  • Live Q&A sessions with instructors.
  • Peer-to-peer coaching opportunities.
  • Optional in-person workshops and events.

Take advantage of these opportunities to connect with your fellow students. Share your experiences, ask questions, offer support, and celebrate each other's successes. The relationships you build in this program will not only enrich your learning experience but will also provide you with a valuable network of colleagues and friends for years to come.
