8 Career Paths (With Income Ranges)

There's no one-size-fits-all approach to this career. That's the beauty of it! Here are eight potential paths, each with its own income potential:

  1. Private Practice (1-on-1 Coaching): Offering personalized coaching sessions to individuals. Potential: $5,000 - $20,000/month. Example: Offering a 3-month package at $1500/client and working with 10 clients a month gets you to $15,000.
  2. Group Programs: Facilitating group coaching programs or workshops. Potential: $3,000 - $50,000/launch. Example: A 6-week online program priced at $750 with 30 participants generates $22,500 per launch.
  3. Corporate Wellness: Providing animal-assisted therapy coaching services to companies for employee well-being. Potential: $2,000 - $10,000/month retainer. Example:Contracting with 3 companies at a $3,000/month retainer brings in $9,000 monthly.
  4. Schools & Educational Institutions: Implementing programs in schools to support students' emotional and social development. Potential: $1,500 - $5,000/school per program. Example: Running a 10-week program in 4 schools at $2,500/school earns $10,000 per program cycle.
  5. Retreats & Workshops: Organizing immersive retreats or workshops focused on specific themes. Potential: $2,500 - $10,000/retreat. Example: Hosting a weekend retreat priced at $1,250 with 8 attendees generates $10,000.
  6. Online Courses & Memberships: Creating and selling online courses or membership programs. Potential: $500 - $10,000+/month (recurring). Example: A membership site with 100 members paying $50/month provides $5,000 in recurring monthly income.
  7. Speaking & Training: Offering presentations and training sessions at conferences and events. Potential: $500 - $5,000+/speaking engagement. Example: Securing 5 speaking engagements at $2,000 each brings in $10,000.
  8. Author & Content Creator: Writing books, articles, or creating content related to animal-assisted therapy coaching. Potential: Varies widely based on sales, royalties, and sponsorships. Example: Earning $2,000/month from book royalties and $1,000/month from sponsored content creates a $3,000 monthly income stream.

These are just a few examples, and the potential is truly limitless. The key is to find the path that resonates most with your passions and skills.

Realistic Timeline to Income

Let's be real: building a successful practice takes time and effort. Here's a realistic timeline to consider:

  • Months 1-3: Focus on completing the certification program, building your website and social media presence, and networking with potential clients and partners. Expect to invest time and resources without immediate financial returns.
  • Months 3-6: Start offering introductory sessions or workshops at a discounted rate to gain experience and testimonials. Aim to secure your first few paying clients.
  • Months 6-12: Refine your services based on client feedback, develop marketing strategies to attract more clients, and explore different income streams. Begin to see a steady increase in income.
  • Months 12+: Continue to grow your practice, expand your offerings, and establish yourself as an expert in the field. Explore opportunities for passive income, such as online courses or membership programs.

Remember, this is just a guideline. Your timeline may vary depending on your individual circumstances, such as your prior experience, network, and marketing efforts.

The Math That Makes Sense

Let's break down the numbers to see how you can achieve your income goals. Here's a simple formula:

Number of Clients x Price per Service = Income

For example, if you want to earn $5,000 per month, you could:

  • Work with 10 clients at $500 per month.
  • Offer a group program to 20 participants at $250 each.
  • Secure two corporate wellness contracts at $2,500 each.

The key is to determine your desired income, then break it down into achievable targets based on your chosen service model.

Your 90-Day Vision Exercise

Now it's time to get specific! Let's create a 90-day vision for your practice. Answer the following questions:

  1. What is your ideal client profile? (e.g., age, gender, interests, challenges)
