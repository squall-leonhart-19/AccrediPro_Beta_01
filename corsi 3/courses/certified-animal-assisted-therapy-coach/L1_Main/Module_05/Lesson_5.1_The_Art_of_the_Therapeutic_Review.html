The Purpose of Therapeutic Review

Welcome to the "Summarize & Succeed" module! This module focuses on the crucial final stages of the P.A.W.S.S. Frameworkâ„¢, where we consolidate learning and celebrate achievements. This lesson introduces the art of the therapeutic review, a formal process of reflecting on the AAT coaching journey with your client. Think of it as a guided tour through the landscape you've both traversed, pointing out landmarks of progress and areas for future exploration.

The review serves several vital purposes:

  • Consolidation of Learning: It allows clients to integrate insights gained throughout the coaching process.
  • Progress Assessment: It offers a structured way to measure progress against initial goals.
  • Identification of Remaining Needs: It helps uncover any unaddressed issues or areas requiring further attention.
  • Empowerment: It reinforces the client's self-efficacy by highlighting their accomplishments and resilience.

For you, as an AAT coach, the therapeutic review provides invaluable feedback on your coaching effectiveness. It allows you to refine your techniques and better serve future clients. Remember, we're building not just better client outcomes, but also a thriving, sustainable practice for you. Many AAT coaches are earning $75,000 - $120,000 annually helping clients achieve breakthroughs. The therapeutic review is a key to reaching that level of success.

Creating a Safe and Collaborative Environment

Before diving into the review itself, it's critical to cultivate a safe and collaborative environment. This is where your skills in building rapport and trust, honed in the "Support & Sustain" module, truly shine. Clients need to feel comfortable sharing their honest reflections, even if those reflections include challenges or perceived failures.

Consider these techniques:

  • Emphasize Confidentiality: Reiterate the confidentiality of the coaching relationship.
  • Express Unconditional Positive Regard: Create a non-judgmental space where all feelings are validated.
  • Use Empathetic Listening: Actively listen to the client's perspective, reflecting back their feelings and experiences.
  • Involve the Animal Partner: If appropriate, include the animal in the review session. The animal's presence can be calming and provide non-verbal feedback.

Remember, the goal is to create a space where clients feel empowered to honestly assess their journey, knowing they are supported regardless of the outcome.

Structuring the Review Session

A well-structured review session provides clarity and focus. Consider these key elements:

  1. Timeline: Schedule the review session in advance, allowing ample time for reflection. A 90-minute session is often ideal.
  2. Key Questions: Prepare a list of open-ended questions to guide the discussion. Examples include:
    • "What were your initial goals for AAT coaching, and how do you feel you've progressed toward them?"
    • "What were the most significant insights you gained during our sessions?"
    • "What challenges did you encounter, and how did you overcome them?"
    • "How has your relationship with the animal partner evolved throughout this process?"
    • "What are your next steps for maintaining your progress and continued well-being?"
  3. Documentation: Use a standardized form to document the review session. This form should include:
    • Client's initial goals
    • Progress made towards those goals
    • Key insights and learnings
    • Challenges encountered and how they were addressed
    • Client's plan for continued well-being
    • Your observations and recommendations

Remember to adapt the structure to the individual client's needs and preferences. Some clients may prefer a more informal, conversational approach, while others may benefit from a more structured, goal-oriented review.

Identifying and Addressing Roadblocks

It's crucial to acknowledge that not every coaching journey is smooth. Clients may encounter roadblocks or setbacks along the way. The therapeutic review provides an opportunity to identify and address these challenges in a supportive and constructive manner.

When discussing roadblocks, consider these strategies:

  • Normalize the Experience: Remind clients that setbacks are a normal part of the growth process.
  • Focus on Learning: Frame setbacks as opportunities for learning and growth.
  • Collaboratively Problem-Solve: Work with the client to identify strategies for overcoming future challenges.
  • Reinforce Resilience: Highlight the client's strengths and coping mechanisms.

By addressing roadblocks openly and honestly, you can help clients build resilience and develop a more positive outlook on their journey. This aligns with the "Empowering Self-Efficacy" principles we discussed in the "Support & Sustain" module.

Case Study: Integrating Review into Coaching

Case Study: Sarah's Journey with AAT

Client: Sarah, a 48-year-old former teacher, sought AAT coaching to address feelings of isolation and low self-esteem following a career change.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reported feeling disconnected from her community and struggling to find purpose in her new life. She also expressed anxiety about her financial future.

Intervention: Sarah participated in 10 AAT coaching sessions with a certified AAT coach and a therapy dog named Buddy. The sessions focused on building self-confidence, exploring new interests, and developing coping mechanisms for anxiety.

Therapeutic Review: During the review session, Sarah reflected on her initial goals and acknowledged significant progress in building her self-confidence. She identified her relationship with Buddy as a key factor in her improved mood and social interactions. Sarah also discussed lingering anxiety about finances. The coach and Sarah developed a plan for Sarah to connect with a financial advisor and explore volunteer opportunities to build community.

Outcomes: Sarah reported feeling more connected, confident, and hopeful about her future. She continues to volunteer at a local animal shelter and is actively pursuing new career opportunities. Sarah mentioned that she is considering starting her own dog walking business, which could generate an income of $30-$40 per walk, and she could easily do 5-10 walks a week!
