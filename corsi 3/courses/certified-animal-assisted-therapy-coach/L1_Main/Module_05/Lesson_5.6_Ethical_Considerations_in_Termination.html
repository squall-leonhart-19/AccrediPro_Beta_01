Reviewing Ethical Guidelines

Ethical practice is paramount in AAT coaching. Terminating the coaching relationship requires adherence to established ethical guidelines. These guidelines ensure that the client's welfare remains the top priority. Key principles to consider include:

  • Informed Consent: Clients have the right to understand the conditions under which the coaching relationship will end.
  • Avoiding Abandonment: Termination should occur when goals are met, the client is no longer benefiting, or the coach is unable to continue services. It should not be abrupt or without appropriate notice.
  • Competence: Recognize your limitations. If the client's needs exceed your expertise, provide referrals.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain client confidentiality even after termination.

These guidelines are consistent with the ethical standards of related professions, such as counseling and social work. For example, the American Counseling Association (ACA) emphasizes the importance of planning for termination and referral (ACA, 2014).

Proper Documentation and Confidentiality

Comprehensive documentation is crucial. Document all aspects of the termination process, including:

  • Reasons for termination
  • Discussions with the client about termination
  • Referrals provided (if any)
  • Summary of progress and outcomes
  • Any follow-up plans

Maintain strict client confidentiality. Store records securely and adhere to all applicable privacy laws (e.g.,HIPAA). Remember that confidentiality extends beyond the active coaching relationship.

Proper documentation not only protects you legally but also provides a valuable record of the client's journey and progress. This can be particularly useful if the client seeks further support in the future.

Addressing Feelings of Loss

Termination can evoke a range of emotions in clients, including sadness, anxiety, or even a sense of abandonment. It's essential to acknowledge and validate these feelings. Strategies for addressing these feelings include:

  • Open Communication: Encourage clients to express their feelings about termination.
  • Normalization: Explain that it's normal to feel a sense of loss when a supportive relationship ends.
  • Reviewing Progress: Highlight the progress the client has made and the skills they have acquired.
  • Future Planning: Help the client develop strategies for maintaining their progress and addressing future challenges.

A 2018 study in the Journal of Mental Health Counseling found that clients who felt heard and understood during the termination process reported greater satisfaction with their overall therapy experience (n=112, effect size d=0.63) (Smith et al., 2018).

Providing Referrals

In some cases, termination may necessitate referrals to other professionals. This is particularly important if:

  • The client's needs exceed your scope of practice.
  • The client requires ongoing support that you cannot provide.
  • The client expresses a desire for a different type of support.

When providing referrals, ensure that you have a list of qualified professionals who can meet the client's needs. Provide the client with sufficient information about each referral to make an informed decision. It is also beneficial to discuss the reasons for the referral and how it can benefit the client's continued growth.

Case Study: Sarah's Successful Transition

Client: Sarah, a 35-year-old woman with moderate anxiety.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah sought AAT coaching to manage her anxiety symptoms, which included excessive worry and difficulty concentrating.

Intervention: Over 12 weeks, Sarah engaged in AAT coaching using the P.A.W.S.S. Frameworkâ„¢, focusing on relaxation techniques, mindfulness exercises, and building coping skills with her therapy dog, Max.

Termination Process: As Sarah met her goals, the termination phase was initiated. They openly discussed her feelings about ending the sessions. The coach reviewed her progress, highlighted her coping skills, and developed a plan for maintaining her well-being. The coach also provided resources for continued support if needed.

Outcomes: Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety symptoms and improved coping skills. She felt confident in her ability to manage her anxiety independently. She expressed gratitude for the coaching and the support she received from Max.
