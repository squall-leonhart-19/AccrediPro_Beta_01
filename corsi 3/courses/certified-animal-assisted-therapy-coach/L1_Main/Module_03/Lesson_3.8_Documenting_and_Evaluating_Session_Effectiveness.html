Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on the "Wonder" aspect. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open about feeling stuck. It takes courage to admit that. What I hear is a deep desire to reconnect with a sense of wonder and passion in your life. Does that resonate?"
MARIA:
"Yes, absolutely. I used to love teaching, but now it feels like a chore. And my personal life... well, it's just...routine."
YOU:
"I understand. Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s, experience a similar feeling of being disconnected from their passions. We're going to explore how to reignite that spark. Let's start by tapping into your sense of curiosity and wonder. Think back to when you were a child ‚Äì what activities made you lose track of time?"
MARIA:
"Hmm... I loved to read. I would get lost in books for hours. And I loved being outside, exploring nature."
YOU:
"That's fantastic. So, reading and nature. Let's explore how we can weave those elements back into your current life. Even small doses of wonder can make a big difference. Are you open to brainstorming some ideas, no matter how silly they might seem?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria's eyes light up when she remembers a childhood passion.
  • She starts brainstorming ideas, even if they seem small or silly.
  • She says something like, "I haven't thought about that in years!"
  • Her body language shifts from closed-off to more open and engaged.
  • She expresses a sense of hope and excitement about the future.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary emotion you want to evoke in the client during this initial conversation?

The primary emotion is hope. You want to instill a sense that rediscovering passion and wonder is possible, even in the midst of feeling stuck.

2. Why is it important to connect with the client's childhood passions?

Childhood passions often represent a pure, unadulterated source of joy and wonder. Tapping into these memories can help the client reconnect with a part of themselves they may have forgotten.

You're Becoming a Real Coach!

By guiding your clients back to their sense of wonder, you're helping them unlock a more fulfilling and meaningful life. Keep practicing, and you'll see incredible transformations!

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on reigniting the client's sense of wonder and passion.
  • Connect with their childhood passions to uncover forgotten sources of joy.
  • Validate their feelings of being stuck; it's a common experience.
  • Emphasize the power of small, daily doses of wonder.
  • Avoid jumping to solutions; explore their interests and values first.
