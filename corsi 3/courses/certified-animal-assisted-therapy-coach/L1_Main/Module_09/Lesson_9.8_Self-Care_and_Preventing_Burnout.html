Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on building rapport and addressing Maria's concerns. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your vision with me. It's wonderful that you want to combine your love for dogs and your teaching experience. Many people in your position feel overwhelmed at first, and that's perfectly normal."
MARIA:
"I just feel like there are so many options, and I don't know which one is right for me. Plus, I'm worried about the dogs' well-being."
YOU:
"Those are excellent and valid concerns. We'll address both. First, let's acknowledge that your dogs' well-being is paramount. We'll only explore options that prioritize their comfort and safety. Second, we'll break down the possibilities into manageable steps."
YOU:
"Before we delve into specific programs, let's clarify your core values and what success looks like to you. This will help us narrow down the choices. Does that sound good?"
MARIA:
"Yes, that makes a lot of sense. I hadn't really thought about my values in this context."
YOU:
"Perfect. Let's start with a simple exercise: Imagine it's one year from today, and you're absolutely thrilled with how things have progressed. What does your day look like? Be as specific as possible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to establish with a new client before discussing program options?

Building rapport and addressing their underlying fears and uncertainties.

2. Why is it important to emphasize animal welfare when working with clients who want to incorporate their animals into therapy work?

Because the animals' well-being is paramount, and it's essential to ensure that any activities are safe, comfortable, and enjoyable for them.

3. What is the purpose of the "one year from today" exercise?

To help the client clarify their core values and envision what success looks like to them, which can then be used to guide the coaching process.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize building rapport and trust with new clients.
  • Always emphasize animal welfare in animal-assisted therapy coaching.
  • Address clients' fears and uncertainties before diving into program options.
  • Help clients clarify their values and envision their ideal future.
  • Tailor your guidance to each client's unique situation and goals.
