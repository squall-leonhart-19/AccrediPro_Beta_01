Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on gentle application of aromatherapy. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of this. It sounds like you're navigating a really challenging time. I want to assure you that aromatherapy can be a supportive tool, used gently, to help you find moments of calm and improve your sleep."
MARIA:
"I'm a little skeptical, to be honest. I've tried lavender candles before, and they didn't really do much."

"I understand your skepticism. The quality of the essential oils and how they're used makes a big difference. We'll be working with high-quality, therapeutic-grade oils and exploring methods beyond just candles. It's about finding what resonates with you."

"Let's start with a simple breathing exercise using a calming blend. This can help ease your anxiety and prepare you for sleep. Would you be open to trying that?"

"I was thinking of a blend of Lavender, Roman Chamomile, and Sweet Orange. These are known for their calming and comforting properties. We can adjust it based on your preferences, of course. Does that sound appealing?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to do when a client is skeptical about aromatherapy?

Acknowledge their skepticism and previous experiences without dismissing them. Explain how your approach is different and emphasize the importance of quality and personalized application.

2. When recommending an essential oil blend, what's a good phrase to use?

Instead of pushing a specific product, say something like, "I was thinking of a blend of [oils], but we can adjust it based on your preferences." This emphasizes personalization and client choice.

Key Takeaways

  • Aromatherapy coaching involves gently guiding clients towards natural solutions.
  • Addressing skepticism with empathy and education is crucial.
  • Personalization and client choice are paramount in aromatherapy application.
  • Always prioritize safety and proper usage guidelines.
  • Focus on building trust and empowering clients to take control of their well-being.
