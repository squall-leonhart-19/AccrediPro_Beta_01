The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, experiencing anxiety and insomnia related to menopause. I created a blend of Lavender, Roman Chamomile, and Clary Sage in a carrier oil for topical application before bed. Sarah is also taking medication for high blood pressure. I'm a little nervous about potential interactions or side effects. Is this blend safe and effective?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Aisha consider the client's medication and potential interactions with Clary Sage?
  • Was the dilution rate appropriate, especially considering the client's age and health condition?
  • Did Aisha educate the client about potential photosensitivity with certain essential oils?
  • Did Aisha discuss alternative application methods if topical application wasn't preferred?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Acknowledge Her Initiative

Start by praising Aisha for seeking guidance and demonstrating a commitment to client safety. Reinforce that asking questions is a sign of a responsible practitioner.

2

Review Contraindications & Drug Interactions

Discuss the importance of researching potential contraindications and drug interactions for each essential oil, especially when clients are taking medication. Explain that Clary Sage can have estrogen-like effects and may interact with hormone-related medications or conditions.

3

Refine Blending Skills

Explore alternative essential oils for anxiety and insomnia that are generally considered safer for clients with high blood pressure or hormone-related concerns. Consider oils like Frankincense, Bergamot (bergaptene-free), or Cedarwood.

4

Emphasize Client Education

Highlight the importance of educating clients about potential side effects, proper application methods, and the need to consult with their healthcare provider before using aromatherapy, especially if they have existing health conditions or are taking medication.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Aisha, I'm so glad you brought this case to me! It shows you're really thinking critically about your client's well-being, and that's fantastic."
Ask Before Telling
"What were your primary concerns when you created this blend for Sarah?"
Teach the Principle
"When a client is taking medication, it's crucial to double-check for potential interactions. For example, Clary Sage has some estrogen-like properties, so we need to be cautious with hormone-sensitive conditions or medications."
Empower Next Steps
"What adjustments would you consider making to this blend, knowing what you know now? What oils might be a safer alternative for Sarah?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to do when a mentee presents a case they're unsure about?

First, validate their concerns and praise them for seeking guidance. This creates a safe space for learning.

2. Why is it crucial to review contraindications and drug interactions with a new practitioner?

New practitioners may not be fully aware of all potential risks. Reviewing this ensures client safety and helps them develop strong clinical reasoning skills.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves guiding new practitioners to think critically and make informed decisions.
  • Always validate their concerns and create a supportive learning environment.
  • Review contraindications, drug interactions, and safe blending practices to ensure client safety.
  • Encourage them to research and question established practices to foster their growth.
