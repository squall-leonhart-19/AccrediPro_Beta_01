The Case Lisa Presents

Lisa's Client Case: Anxiety & Insomnia

What Lisa Tells You:

"I saw Sarah, 52, who has been struggling with anxiety and insomnia. I recommended a blend of lavender, chamomile, and frankincense for diffusion at night and a rollerball with the same oils in a carrier oil to apply to her wrists during the day. Sarah called me and said the blend smells great, but she feels more restless. I‚Äôm not sure what to do now."

Points to Consider:

  • Did Lisa consider Sarah's individual sensitivities and preferences?
  • Was the dosage of essential oils appropriate?
  • Were there any potential contraindications or interactions with other medications Sarah is taking?
  • Did Lisa explore other contributing factors to Sarah's anxiety and insomnia (e.g., diet, lifestyle)?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Key Teaching Points for Lisa

1

Emphasize Individualized Assessment

Remind Lisa that aromatherapy is not a one-size-fits-all approach. Encourage her to explore Sarah's unique needs and preferences.

2

Discuss Dosage and Sensitivity

Help Lisa understand the importance of starting with low dilutions and gradually increasing as needed. Discuss potential sensitivities to specific essential oils.

3

Explore Contributing Factors

Guide Lisa to consider other factors that may be contributing to Sarah's anxiety and insomnia, such as diet, lifestyle, and underlying health conditions.

4

Encourage Client Feedback

Stress the importance of ongoing communication and feedback from clients to adjust treatment plans as needed.

Crafting Your Feedback

Providing Constructive and Supportive Feedback

Acknowledge Lisa's Efforts
"Lisa, it's great that you're reaching out and seeking guidance. It shows you're committed to providing the best care for your clients. Let's work through this together."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What do you think might have contributed to Sarah's restlessness? Were there any clues in her intake form that you might have missed?"
Offer Alternative Solutions
"Perhaps Sarah is sensitive to one of the oils in the blend, or maybe the dosage is too high. We could explore other calming oils or adjust the dilution."
Empower Future Decisions
"What's your plan for your next session with Sarah? How will you incorporate her feedback and adjust her aromatherapy plan?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important element in providing effective supervision and mentoring to a new aromatherapy practitioner?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable asking questions, sharing concerns, and learning from their experiences.

2. How can you help a mentee develop confidence in their aromatherapy practice?

By providing positive reinforcement, acknowledging their strengths, and encouraging them to take on challenges that stretch their abilities.

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through real-world challenges.
