The Case She Presents

Lisa's Client Case

What Lisa Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, with anxiety, insomnia, and mild hypertension. I was thinking of a blend with lavender, chamomile, and ylang-ylang in a carrier oil for a massage. Is this safe and appropriate?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Lisa considered potential contraindications with Sarah's hypertension?
  • Is the blend addressing the root cause of Sarah's issues or just masking symptoms?
  • Is Lisa aware of the potential sedative effects and advising Sarah accordingly?
  • Is the client on any medications that could interact with the oils?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Lisa

1

Emphasize Safety First

Review the importance of checking for contraindications and potential drug interactions, especially with clients presenting with hypertension. Lavender, while generally safe, can lower blood pressure in some individuals.

2

Encourage a Holistic Approach

Discuss the importance of addressing the root causes of anxiety and insomnia, rather than just treating the symptoms. Suggest exploring lifestyle factors, stress management techniques, and other holistic modalities.

3

Refine Formulation Skills

Help Lisa understand how to select oils based on their specific properties and potential synergistic effects. Discuss the importance of considering individual client needs and preferences when formulating blends.

4

Build Confidence Through Knowledge

Recommend resources for expanding her knowledge of essential oil safety and therapeutic applications. Encourage her to participate in continuing education and professional development opportunities.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Begin with Affirmation
"Lisa, it's great that you're proactively seeking guidance on this case. It shows your commitment to client safety and well-being. Let's explore this together."
Inquire About Her Reasoning
"Can you walk me through your thought process in selecting these oils for Sarah? What specific benefits were you hoping to achieve with each one?"
Offer Guidance on Safety
"With Sarah's hypertension, it's wise to be cautious with oils that can lower blood pressure. Lavender, for example, may need to be used in lower concentrations or replaced with a safer alternative like sweet marjoram."
Empower Decision-Making
"What alternative oils might you consider that would address Sarah's anxiety and insomnia without posing a risk to her blood pressure? What resources can you consult to make an informed decision?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important initial step when a mentee presents a complex case?

The first step is to validate the mentee's concerns and anxieties. This builds trust and creates a safe space for learning.

2. How can you encourage critical thinking in a mentee?

Ask open-ended questions that prompt them to analyze the case, consider different perspectives, and justify their reasoning. Avoid simply giving them the answers.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize safety by reviewing contraindications and potential drug interactions.
  • Encourage a holistic approach to address the root causes of client issues.
  • Refine formulation skills by understanding essential oil properties and synergistic effects.
  • Build confidence through knowledge and continuing education.
  • Validate concerns, provide resources, and foster critical thinking in your mentee.
