Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if aromatherapy really works."
"That's a valid concern. Many people are skeptical at first. However, research shows that specific essential oils can effectively reduce stress and improve sleep. My approach is based on evidence-based practices, and I track your progress to ensure you're seeing results. For example, a 2023 study in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found that lavender oil significantly improved sleep quality in participants with mild insomnia."
"It's more expensive than I expected."

My program provides a personalized solution and ongoing support, which can lead to significant improvements in your well-being. Plus, many clients find they reduce their spending on other remedies and medications once they address the root cause of their issues. We can also discuss payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I don't have time for another commitment."

"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (as everyone responds differently), I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with a personalized plan, ongoing support, and evidence-based recommendations. My goal is to help you achieve your desired results, and I'll work closely with you every step of the way.     Mastering Discovery Calls and Closing the Sale

    Learning Objectives

    • Understand the key elements of a successful discovery call.
    • Learn how to build rapport and establish trust with potential clients.
    • Identify common objections and prepare effective responses.
    • Practice presenting your pricing with confidence.
    • Develop a compelling call-to-action to close the sale.

    The Art of the Discovery Call

    The discovery call is your opportunity to connect with potential clients, understand their needs, and demonstrate how your services can help them achieve their goals. It's not just about selling; it's about building relationships and providing value.

    A well-structured discovery call will:

    • Build rapport and establish trust.
    • Uncover the client's pain points and desired outcomes.
    • Present your approach and demonstrate your expertise.
    • Address any objections or concerns.
    • Guide the client towards a decision.

    Handling Objections with Grace

    Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They indicate that the client is engaged and considering your offer. The key is to address objections with empathy, understanding, and confidence.

    Here are some common objections and effective responses:

    "I need to think about it."
    "I understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Is there anything I can clarify for you to help you make a decision?"

    Case Study: Sarah's Successful Discovery Call

    Client: Sarah, 45, experiencing chronic stress and insomnia.

    Presenting Symptoms: Difficulty falling asleep, waking up frequently, feeling overwhelmed and anxious.

    Intervention: During the discovery call, the practitioner actively listened to Sarah's concerns, asked probing questions to understand the root causes of her stress and sleep issues, and explained how a personalized aromatherapy plan could help her relax, improve her sleep quality, and reduce her anxiety. The practitioner also addressed Sarah's concerns about the cost of the program by highlighting the long-term benefits and offering a payment plan.

    Outcomes: Sarah felt understood and supported during the discovery call. She appreciated the practitioner's expertise and personalized approach. She enrolled in the 12-week program and experienced significant improvements in her sleep quality, stress levels, and overall well-being.

    Case Study: David's Pricing Strategy

    Client: David, a newly certified aromatherapist.

    Challenge: David was hesitant to charge premium prices for his services, fearing he wouldn't attract clients.

    Intervention: David worked with a business coach to identify his unique value proposition, develop a clear pricing strategy, and practice presenting his prices with confidence. He learned to emphasize the personalized nature of his services, the quality of his aromatherapy blends, and the transformative results his clients could achieve.

    Outcomes: David gained the confidence to charge premium prices for his services. He attracted clients who valued his expertise and were willing to invest in their well-being. His income increased significantly, and he felt more fulfilled in his work.
