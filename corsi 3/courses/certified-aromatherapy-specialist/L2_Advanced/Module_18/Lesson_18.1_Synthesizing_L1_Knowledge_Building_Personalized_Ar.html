Maria Rodriguez, 52

Teacher, experiencing menopause and stress

Maria, a 52-year-old teacher, presents with symptoms of insomnia, anxiety, and hot flashes related to menopause. Her client history reveals a sensitivity to strong scents and a preference for calming, floral aromas.

Intervention: A personalized aromatherapy protocol was developed, incorporating:

  • Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia): For calming and sleep support. A 2015 meta-analysis of 15 studies (Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine) showed lavender significantly improved sleep quality.
  • Clary Sage (Salvia sclarea): Known for its hormone-balancing properties. A randomized controlled trial in 2014 (Journal of Phytotherapy Research) demonstrated Clary Sage's ability to reduce cortisol levels and anxiety in menopausal women.
  • Roman Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile): To soothe anxiety and promote relaxation.

The essential oils were blended in a carrier oil (Jojoba) at a 1% dilution for topical application (pulse points) and a 2% dilution for a diffuser blend.

Outcomes: After three weeks, Maria reported a significant improvement in sleep quality, reduced anxiety levels, and a decrease in the frequency and intensity of hot flashes.

This case study demonstrates the power of integrating client history with essential oil knowledge. Maria's sensitivity to strong scents guided the selection of oils and their dilutions, ensuring a positive outcome.

Protocol Customization

While standard aromatherapy protocols can serve as a starting point, true expertise lies in adapting these protocols based on individual client responses and preferences.

For example, if a client finds a particular oil overpowering, consider reducing the dilution or substituting it with a similar oil. The S.C.E.N.T.
