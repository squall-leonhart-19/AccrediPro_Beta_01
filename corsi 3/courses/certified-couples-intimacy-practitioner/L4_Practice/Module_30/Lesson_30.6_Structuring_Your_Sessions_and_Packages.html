A well-defined session structure provides clarity for both you and your clients. It ensures you cover all necessary areas while offering a predictable and comfortable experience. Hereâ€™s a recommended structure:

  1. Intake (15-20 minutes): Review paperwork, discuss client goals, and establish rapport.
  2. Assessment (20-30 minutes): Gather detailed information about the couple's history, communication patterns, and intimacy challenges. This is where you'll utilize tools and techniques learned throughout this certification, including identifying attachment styles and exploring family-of-origin dynamics.
  3. Intervention (20-30 minutes): Introduce relevant concepts from the TRUST Frameworkâ„¢, facilitate communication exercises, and provide actionable strategies.
  4. Follow-up (5-10 minutes): Summarize key takeaways, assign homework, and schedule the next session.

Creating Session Packages

Offering session packages can be a win-win. Clients benefit from a discounted rate, encouraging them to commit to the process. You benefit from increased revenue and improved client outcomes due to consistent engagement.

Here are a few package ideas:

  • The "Jumpstart" Package: 3 sessions focused on immediate conflict resolution and communication skills. Price: $450 ($150/session)
  • The "Deep Dive" Package: 6 sessions exploring underlying relational patterns and attachment styles. Price: $840 ($140/session)
  • The "Transformation" Package: 10 sessions for comprehensive intimacy building and long-term relationship health. Price: $1300 ($130/session)

Income Example: Let's say you sell 5 "Deep Dive" packages per month. That's $4,200 in revenue from just one package offering! With strategic marketing and client referrals, this is very achievable.

Offering Online Sessions

Offering online sessions expands your reach and provides flexibility for clients who may not be able to attend in-person appointments. A 2021 study in the *Journal of Marital and Family Therapy* found that online couples therapy can be just as effective as in-person therapy (effectiveness rate of 70-80%) for improving relationship satisfaction and communication (Whitton et al., 2021).

To offer effective online sessions:

  • Use a secure and reliable video conferencing platform (e.g., Zoom, Google Meet).
  • Ensure a private and quiet environment for both you and your clients.
  • Adapt your session structure and activities for the online format.
  • Address any technical challenges proactively.

Consider offering a discounted rate for online sessions to attract new clients. Market the convenience and accessibility of online therapy to reach a wider audience.

The TRUST Frameworkâ„¢ should be seamlessly integrated into your session structure and package offerings. Hereâ€™s how:

  1. T - Trust Building: Begin each session by creating a safe and supportive environment. Use active listening and empathy to build rapport.
  2. R - Respectful Communication: Facilitate communication exercises that promote respectful and understanding dialogue.
  3. U - Understanding Attachment: Help couples identify their attachment styles and how they impact their relationship patterns.
  4. S - Shared Values: Explore the couple's values and how they align (or misalign). Facilitate discussions on how to bridge any gaps.
  5. T - Tangible Actions: Provide actionable strategies and homework assignments that couples can implement in their daily lives.
