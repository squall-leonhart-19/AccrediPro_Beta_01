The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I'm working with Sarah, age 38, who has a history of childhood trauma. Lately, Sarah's anxiety has been through the roof. She calls me multiple times a day, even on weekends, saying she feels like she's 'going to explode.' I'm not sure how to help her manage these intense feelings, and I'm starting to feel burned out myself."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Aisha helped Sarah develop coping mechanisms for managing anxiety?
  • What boundaries has Aisha set regarding communication outside of sessions?
  • Does Aisha understand the potential for vicarious trauma and how to protect herself?
  • Is Sarah's anxiety triggered by a specific event or pattern?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize Self-Care

Remind Aisha that she cannot effectively help others if she is running on empty. Discuss the importance of setting boundaries and prioritizing her own well-being.

2

Teach Crisis Intervention Techniques

Share strategies for helping Sarah manage her anxiety in the moment, such as grounding exercises, deep breathing techniques, and creating a crisis plan.

3

Explore Trauma-Informed Approaches

Discuss how Sarah's past trauma may be contributing to her current anxiety and brainstorm trauma-informed interventions that could be helpful.

4

Guide Boundary Setting

Help Aisha develop a clear communication policy for her clients, including when and how they can contact her outside of sessions. Role-play difficult conversations about boundaries.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Aisha's Efforts
"Aisha, I can see how much you care about your clients, and it's clear you're putting a lot of effort into helping Sarah. It's admirable that you're seeking guidance on this."
Validate Her Feelings
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed and burned out when working with clients who are in crisis. Your feelings are valid."
Offer Practical Strategies
"Let's explore some specific techniques you can use to help Sarah manage her anxiety, and also some strategies for setting boundaries and protecting your own well-being."
Empower Her to Take Action
"I'm confident that with a few adjustments to your approach, you can continue to support Sarah while also taking care of yourself. What feels like the most important step for you to take right now?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to emphasize with a new practitioner struggling with a crisis case?

Prioritizing self-care and setting healthy boundaries is crucial for preventing burnout and ensuring the practitioner can continue to provide effective support.

2. How can you best help a mentee develop their own clinical judgment?

By asking open-ended questions, encouraging critical thinking, and providing a supportive environment for them to explore different approaches.
