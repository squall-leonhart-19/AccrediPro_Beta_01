The Program Development Challenge

Lisa's Client Scenario

What Lisa Tells You:

"I spoke with Sarah, 52, who's been married for 25 years. She says the spark is gone, and they're more like roommates. She wants to rekindle intimacy, but I'm not sure where to start or how many sessions to recommend."

Key Questions to Consider:

  • What are Sarah's and her partner's communication styles?
  • What specific areas of intimacy are lacking (emotional, physical, sexual)?
  • What is their willingness to invest time and effort into the program?
  • How can Lisa structure a program that addresses Sarah's needs while setting realistic expectations?

Guiding Lisa Through Program Development

Key Points to Cover with Lisa

1

Start with a Thorough Assessment

Emphasize the importance of a detailed intake session to understand the client's specific needs and goals. A needs assessment is crucial.

2

Structure a Flexible Program

Suggest creating a modular program with different phases, allowing for customization based on the client's progress. Offer options like a 3-month foundational program and a 6-month deeper dive.

3

Set Clear Expectations and Boundaries

Advise Lisa to clearly outline the scope of the program, the expected outcomes, and the commitment required from the client. This includes payment policies and communication guidelines.

4

Incorporate Progress Tracking and Feedback

Encourage Lisa to include regular check-ins and assessments to monitor the client's progress and adjust the program as needed. Client feedback is invaluable.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge and Validate
"Lisa, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when creating a program for a new client. You're doing great by seeking guidance."
Encourage Exploration
"What are some initial ideas you have for Sarah's program? What areas of intimacy do you think need the most attention?"
Provide a Framework
"Consider structuring the program into phases: Assessment, Education, Implementation, and Integration. This provides a clear roadmap for both you and the client."
Empower Decision-Making
"Ultimately, the program should reflect your unique style and the client's individual needs. Trust your instincts and create something that feels authentic to you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the importance of a thorough needs assessment in program development?

A thorough needs assessment ensures the program is tailored to the client's specific needs, leading to better outcomes and client satisfaction.

2. How can a mentor help a new practitioner set realistic expectations with clients?

A mentor can share their experiences, provide sample contracts and communication scripts, and role-play difficult conversations to help the new practitioner set clear boundaries and expectations.
