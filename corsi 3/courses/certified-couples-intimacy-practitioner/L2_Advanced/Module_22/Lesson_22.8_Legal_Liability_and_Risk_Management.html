Presenting Issue
Eleanor seeks support to reignite passion in her marriage after 18 years. She reports feeling disconnected from her husband, particularly in the bedroom. She also admits to developing strong feelings for a colleague, Mark.
Background
Eleanor has been married to David since 2006. They have two children, ages 12 and 15. David is a successful lawyer, often working long hours. Eleanor feels that David prioritizes work over their relationship.
Session 1 Disclosure
During the first individual session, Eleanor discloses that she and Mark have shared intimate moments, including kissing. She insists that it hasn't gone further but admits she's tempted.
Ethical Concern
Eleanor is seeking couples therapy but has not disclosed her emotional and physical intimacy with Mark to her husband. You are concerned about the impact of this secret on the therapeutic process and potential harm to David.
Client Goal
Eleanor wants to improve her marriage and rediscover intimacy with David. She also expresses a desire to explore her feelings for Mark in a safe and confidential space.
Legal Considerations
Texas is a no-fault divorce state. However, infidelity can impact asset division in some cases.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Steps

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary dilemma is the conflict between Eleanor's desire for confidentiality and the potential harm to David due to the undisclosed affair. A secondary dilemma involves balancing Eleanor's individual needs with the goals of couples therapy.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Consider principles related to confidentiality, informed consent, non-maleficence (do no harm), and fidelity (honesty and commitment in relationships). The APA's Ethical Principles of Psychologists and Code of Conduct, specifically standards 4.01 (Maintaining Confidentiality) and 10.02 (Therapy Involving Couples or Families) are relevant here.
Step 3: Consider Legal and Regulatory Requirements
While Texas is a no-fault divorce state, there is still a legal obligation to act responsibly and avoid actions that could be construed as aiding or abetting harmful behavior.
Step 4: Explore Options and Consequences
  • Option A: Insist on full disclosure to David before commencing couples therapy. Consequence: Eleanor may terminate therapy.
  • Option B: Proceed with individual therapy for Eleanor, focusing on her feelings and decision-making process. Consequence: May not address the marital issues directly.
  • Option C: Attempt to facilitate a disclosure process with Eleanor and David. Consequence: Could lead to conflict and emotional distress.
Step 5: Consult with Colleagues or Supervisors
Seeking guidance from experienced therapists or ethicists can provide valuable perspectives and support in making a sound decision.
Step 6: Make a Decision and Document the Process
Choose the option that aligns best with ethical principles and document the reasoning behind the decision, including consultations and considerations.

When to Refer

Red Flags and Scope of Practice

  • Suspected Domestic Violence: If there is any indication of physical, emotional, or psychological abuse, immediate referral to appropriate resources is mandatory.
  • Suicidal Ideation: If Eleanor or David express thoughts of harming themselves, immediate mental health intervention is required.
  • Legal Consultation Needed: If either party requires legal advice regarding divorce, custody, or other legal matters, refer them to a qualified attorney.
  • Substance Abuse: If substance abuse is identified as a contributing factor, refer to addiction specialists or treatment programs.
  • Underlying Psychiatric Disorders: If symptoms suggest a more complex psychiatric condition, refer for psychiatric evaluation and treatment.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the primary ethical considerations in this case?

The primary ethical considerations include confidentiality, informed consent, non-maleficence (avoiding harm), and fidelity (honesty and commitment). The therapist must balance Eleanor's right to privacy with the potential harm to David if the affair remains undisclosed.

2. What steps should you take before proceeding with couples therapy?

Before commencing couples therapy, you should conduct individual assessments with both Eleanor and David, obtain informed consent, and address the issue of disclosure. It's crucial to ensure that both parties understand the limits of confidentiality and the potential impact of undisclosed information.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas in intimacy practice often involve conflicts between individual needs and relational well-being.
  • Open and honest communication about the limits of confidentiality is essential.
  • A phased approach, starting with individual assessment, can help to clarify the ethical landscape.
  • Consultation with colleagues or supervisors is invaluable when navigating complex ethical challenges.
  • Prioritizing the safety and well-being of all parties involved is paramount.
