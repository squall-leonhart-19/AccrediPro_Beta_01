Understanding the Aura

The aura is an electromagnetic energy field that surrounds all living beings. It's composed of several layers, each corresponding to different aspects of our being: physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual. These layers interact and influence each other, creating a complex and dynamic energy field. Some believe that the aura extends several feet from the body, while others perceive it as more closely aligned. Understanding the aura's structure and function is key to effective energetic assessment.

Visualizing the Aura

Developing the ability to see or sense the aura takes practice, but it's an achievable skill. Here's a simple technique to start:

  1. Find a dimly lit room with a plain, light-colored background.
  2. Have your client stand a few feet away from the background.
  3. Relax your eyes and soften your gaze. Don't try to focus too hard.
  4. Look slightly past your client, allowing your peripheral vision to expand.
  5. Look for a faint light or colored haze surrounding their body.

Don't be discouraged if you don't see anything at first.It may take several attempts to develop your visual perception. With consistent practice, you'll begin to perceive the aura more clearly. Some people find it easier to sense the aura initially, rather than see it. You can use your hands to feel the energy field around a person or object. Remember, everyone's experience is unique, and your perception will develop over time.

Hands-On Aura Assessment

This method involves using your hands to sense the energy of the aura. It's a tactile way to gather information about the client's energetic state.

  1. Stand or sit comfortably in front of your client.
  2. Hold your hands a few inches away from their body, palms facing them.
  3. Slowly move your hands up and down, paying attention to any sensations you feel.
  4. Notice any tingling, heat, pressure, or other sensations.

Different sensations can indicate different energetic qualities. For example, heat might suggest inflammation or excess energy, while coolness might indicate stagnation or depletion. The strength and density of the aura can also be assessed through this method. A thick, strong aura generally indicates vitality, while a thin, weak aura may suggest fatigue or illness. Intuitive Aura Assessment

Intuition plays a significant role in aura assessment. As you practice, you'll develop the ability to "read" the aura intuitively, gaining insights beyond what you can see or feel physically.

  1. Prepare yourself with a short meditation to clear your mind.
  2. Focus your attention on your client's aura.
  3. Ask yourself questions like: "What colors do I perceive?" "What emotions or thoughts come to mind?" "What areas feel strong or weak?"
  4. Trust the information that comes to you, even if it doesn't make immediate sense.

Intuitive insights can provide valuable information about a client's emotional state, thought patterns, and underlying energetic imbalances. Don't dismiss your initial impressions; they often hold important clues. It's also helpful to combine intuitive readings with other assessment methods to gain a more comprehensive understanding. Remember, intuition is like a muscle; the more you use it, the stronger it becomes.

Aura colors and patterns can provide insights into a person's emotional, mental, and spiritual state. While interpretations can vary, here are some common associations:

It's important to note that aura colors can change depending on a person's mood, thoughts, and experiences. A muddy or dark aura may indicate negative emotions or energetic blockages, while a bright and clear aura suggests positive energy and well-being. Consider the context of the reading and the client's current situation when interpreting the aura. For example, a flash of red in the aura might signify a momentary surge of anger, while a consistent red hue could indicate a more deeply rooted issue.

Using Aura Assessment to Identify Energetic Influences

The aura can be influenced by various external factors, including:

  • Environmental stressors (e.g., pollution, electromagnetic fields)
  • Other people's energy fields
  • Geopathic stress (e.g., underground water lines, ley lines)
  • Emotional or mental stress

By assessing the aura, you can identify areas where the client is being negatively affected by these influences. For example, a weakened area in the aura might correspond to a location where the client spends a lot of time in a stressful environment. Once you've identified these influences, you can take steps to mitigate their impact, such as recommending lifestyle changes, suggesting energy clearing techniques, or modifying the client's environment.

Case Study: Sarah, 35, Chronic Fatigue

Client: Sarah, a 35-year-old marketing executive, presented with chronic fatigue and persistent feelings of overwhelm.

Assessment: Using hands-on aura assessment, a noticeable depletion of energy was detected in the solar plexus and root chakra areas. Intuitive assessment revealed a dominant greyish hue in these regions, indicating exhaustion and a sense of being ungrounded. Further inquiry revealed that Sarah worked long hours in a high-pressure environment and lived near a busy intersection.

Intervention: The Reiki sessions focused on clearing and energizing the solar plexus and root chakras. Sarah was also advised to incorporate grounding exercises into her daily routine, such as spending time in nature and practicing mindful breathing. She was also encouraged to create a more peaceful workspace and limit her exposure to electromagnetic fields.

Outcomes: After several sessions, Sarah reported a significant increase in energy levels and a reduction in feelings of overwhelm. The aura assessment showed a noticeable improvement in the clarity and vibrancy of her aura, particularly in the solar plexus and root chakra areas. The greyish hue had diminished, replaced by brighter, more vibrant colors.
