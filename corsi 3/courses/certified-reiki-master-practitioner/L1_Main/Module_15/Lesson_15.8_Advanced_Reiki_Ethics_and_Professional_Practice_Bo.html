Your Coaching Script

Use this script to guide your initial conversation. Remember to personalize it to your own style:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds like you're carrying a lot right now. Many people find Reiki helpful for managing anxiety and improving sleep. Have you experienced Reiki before?"
MARIA:
"No, I haven't. I'm a little skeptical, to be honest."
YOU:
"That's perfectly okay. Skepticism is healthy! Let me explain a little about how Reiki works, and then you can decide if it feels right for you. Essentially, Reiki is a gentle energy healing technique that promotes relaxation and reduces stress. It works by balancing your body's energy flow."
YOU:
"For you, Maria, I believe Reiki could help calm your nervous system, reduce anxiety, and promote restful sleep. It's a gentle, non-invasive approach. Would you be open to experiencing a short Reiki session to see how it feels?"
MARIA:
"I suppose I could try it. What does it involve?"
YOU:
"You'll simply lie down comfortably, fully clothed, and I'll gently place my hands on or near your body. Most people feel warmth, tingling, or deep relaxation. It usually lasts about an hour. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to do when a client expresses skepticism about Reiki?

Acknowledge and validate their skepticism. Don't dismiss it or try to force them to believe.

2. How should you describe Reiki to a new client who's unfamiliar with energy healing?

Use simple, non-technical language. Focus on the benefits they can expect (relaxation, stress reduction).

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and trust are crucial for building rapport with new clients.
  • Address skepticism with patience and clear explanations.
  • Focus on the tangible benefits of Reiki, not just abstract concepts.
  • Offer a short introductory session to allow clients to experience Reiki firsthand.
