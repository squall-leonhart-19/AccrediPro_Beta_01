Refining Intention Setting

Moving beyond basic intentions involves creating SMART intentions: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. This approach ensures that intentions are clear, actionable, and aligned with the client's goals. The A.W.A.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a structured way to refine intentions, focusing on long-term outcomes and integration.

Consider the difference between a general intention like "I want to feel better" and a SMART intention like "I intend to reduce my anxiety levels by 30% within the next month by practicing daily mindfulness and receiving weekly Reiki sessions." The latter is much more likely to yield tangible results.

The Power of Visualization

Co-Creative Healing

Co-creative healing involves collaborating with the client's higher self and Reiki energy to facilitate healing. This approach recognizes that the client is an active participant in their healing journey, not just a passive recipient of energy.

To foster co-creative healing:

  1. Encourage clients to express their intentions and desires openly.
  2. Facilitate a dialogue with their higher self during the Reiki session.
  3. Use intention statements that incorporate the client's own words and energy.

For example, instead of imposing an intention, ask the client, "What does your higher self want you to know right now?" and incorporate their response into the session.

Ethical Considerations

Ethical considerations are paramount in intention setting. Itâ€™s crucial to ensure that intentions align with the client's highest good and do not infringe upon the free will of others. Always obtain informed consent and respect the client's autonomy.

Key ethical guidelines include:

  1. Informed Consent: Ensure the client understands and agrees with the intentions being set.
  2. Non-interference: Refrain from setting intentions that could negatively impact others.
  3. Confidentiality: Maintain client confidentiality regarding their intentions and healing process.
  4. Client Autonomy: Empower clients to actively participate in setting their intentions.
