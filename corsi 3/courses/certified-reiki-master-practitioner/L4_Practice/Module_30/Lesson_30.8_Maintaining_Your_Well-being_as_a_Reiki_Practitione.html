Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if Reiki is really effective."
"I understand your skepticism. Many people are unfamiliar with Reiki. While it's true that Reiki is subtle, numerous studies have shown its positive effects on stress reduction and relaxation. For example, a 2015 meta-analysis published in the Journal of Evidence-Based Complementary & Alternative Medicine found that Reiki had significant benefits for pain and anxiety reduction. Ultimately, the best way to know if it works for you is to experience it firsthand."
"I need to think about the cost."
"I understand. Investing in your health is a big decision. Let's break down the value you're receiving. My program isn't just about Reiki sessions; it's a comprehensive approach that includes personalized guidance, self-care techniques, and ongoing support. Think about what it's costing you to continue struggling with stress and insomnia ‚Äì in terms of lost productivity, healthcare costs, and overall quality of life. How does that compare to the investment in my program?"
"I don't have time for this right now."
"I hear you. Life is busy, especially as a nurse. But consider this: how much more productive and present could you be if you were sleeping better and feeling less stressed? My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find session times that work for you, and the self-care techniques I teach can be easily incorporated into your daily routine. Investing a small amount of time now can yield significant benefits in the long run."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can promise that I will dedicate my expertise and energy to supporting your healing journey. Many of my clients experience significant improvements in their stress levels, sleep quality, and overall well-being. I am confident that if you commit to the process and actively participate in the sessions and self-care practices, you will see positive changes. I encourage you to read testimonials from other clients who have experienced the benefits of my program."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the initial minutes of a discovery call?

Building rapport involves expressing gratitude for their time, actively listening, finding common ground, and showing genuine interest in their situation and goals.

2. How can you effectively address the "I need to think about it" objection while remaining respectful of the prospect's decision-making process?

Acknowledge their need for time, then gently inquire about the specific aspects they need to consider (investment, time commitment, etc.). Offer additional information or resources to help them make an informed decision.
