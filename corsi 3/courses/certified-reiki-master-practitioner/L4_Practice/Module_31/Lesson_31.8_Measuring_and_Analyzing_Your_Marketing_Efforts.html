Handling Common Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand, Susan. It's a big decision. What specific aspects are you considering? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask, what value do you place on feeling calm, grounded, and enjoying your retirement without constant anxiety? This program is designed to provide lasting results, and many of my clients find that it pays for itself in terms of reduced stress and improved quality of life. I also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure if Reiki is right for me."
"That's perfectly okay! Many people are initially skeptical. Reiki is a gentle and non-invasive technique that complements traditional medicine. I offer a money-back guarantee if you don't experience any benefits after the first month. How does that sound?"
"Can I get back to you next week?"
"Of course! I want you to feel 100% comfortable with your decision. What information can I provide you with now, that would help you decide by next week? Also, I do have limited spots available, and I'd hate for you to miss out if this is truly what you're looking for."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Welcome & Rapport, Understanding the Prospect's Challenges, Presenting Your Approach, and Invitation & Next Steps.

2. How can you address the objection: "It's more expensive than I expected?"

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the value and lasting results of your program, and offer payment plan options.

3. What is the importance of practicing your closing lines out loud?

Practicing your closing lines builds confidence and helps you deliver them naturally and persuasively during the call.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is Key: Thoroughly understand your ideal prospect's needs and challenges.
  • Structure Your Calls: Follow a clear, concise script to guide the conversation.
  • Address Objections Confidently: Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Practice Your Pricing: State your prices clearly and confidently, highlighting the value you provide.
  • Embrace the Process: Client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice. Don't be afraid to put yourself out there!
