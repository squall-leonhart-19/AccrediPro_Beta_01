Scenario 1: Informed Consent

üë©

Sarah, 52

New client seeking Reiki for anxiety and insomnia.

Health History
Takes medication for high blood pressure.
Concerns
Wants to ensure Reiki won't interfere with medication.

Sample Informed Consent Script

Your Approach to Informed Consent

Phase 1: Introduction 2 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for choosing me as your Reiki practitioner. Before we begin, I want to ensure you fully understand the process and its potential benefits and limitations."
Phase 2: Explanation of Reiki 5 min
YOU:
"Reiki is a gentle, hands-on healing technique that promotes relaxation, reduces stress, and supports the body's natural healing abilities. It is not a substitute for medical treatment, but it can be a complementary therapy."
YOU:
"As a Certified Reiki Master Practitioner, I am trained to facilitate this energy flow. However, I am not a medical professional, and I do not diagnose or treat medical conditions."
Phase 3: Addressing Concerns 5 min
YOU:
"You mentioned taking medication for high blood pressure. Reiki is generally safe and should not interfere with your medication. However, it's always best to consult with your doctor if you have any concerns. Also, please keep me informed of any changes to your medication or health condition."
YOU:
"Are there any other health conditions or treatments I should be aware of?"
Phase 4: Consent & Documentation 3 min
YOU:
"Do you understand the nature of Reiki and agree to proceed with the session? I have a consent form here that outlines these points. Please read it carefully, and let me know if you have any questions before signing."

Scenario 2: Client Confidentiality

üë©

Maria, 48

Regular client dealing with a difficult divorce. Shares personal details during sessions.

Situation
Feels comfortable sharing sensitive information with you.
Ethical Dilemma
You run into a mutual friend who asks about Maria.

Handling Confidentiality

Maintaining Client Privacy

"How are you, Maria's been seeing you for Reiki, right? How is she doing?"
"It's lovely to see you! I'm doing well, thank you. I'm afraid I can't discuss any of my client's personal information, but I appreciate you asking about Maria. I hope she's doing well too!"
"I'm really struggling with my marriage. Can you give me some advice based on what Maria has told you about her divorce?"
"I understand you're going through a tough time. While I can offer general support and resources, I can't discuss Maria's personal situation or provide advice based on her experiences. It would be unethical and a breach of her confidentiality. However, I can recommend a qualified therapist or counselor who can help you navigate your own marriage challenges."
"I'm worried about Maria. She seems really down lately. Is there anything you can tell me?"
"I appreciate your concern for Maria. As a Reiki practitioner, I am bound by strict confidentiality guidelines. If I have serious concerns about a client's safety or well-being, I am ethically obligated to take appropriate action, such as encouraging them to seek professional help or contacting emergency services. However, I can't share any details about her sessions with you."

Scenario 3: Scope of Practice

üë©

Linda, 60

Client with diagnosed fibromyalgia seeking pain relief and emotional support.

Expectations
Hopes Reiki can "cure" her fibromyalgia.
Your Role
Need to manage expectations and stay within your scope.

Setting Boundaries

Defining Your Expertise

"Linda, I understand you're hoping Reiki can cure your fibromyalgia. While Reiki can be a wonderful complementary therapy for managing pain and promoting relaxation, it's important to understand that it's not a cure for fibromyalgia. My role as a Certified Reiki Master Practitioner is to support your overall well-being by reducing stress, easing muscle tension, and promoting emotional balance. I encourage you to continue working with your healthcare team for medical treatment and management of your condition. Reiki can work alongside those treatments to enhance your overall quality of life."

Your Marketing Messaging

Check Your Understanding

1. What key information should be included in an informed consent form for a Reiki session?

Explanation of Reiki, potential benefits and risks, your qualifications, client's right to withdraw consent, and a statement that Reiki is not a substitute for medical treatment.

2. How should you respond if a client asks you to diagnose their medical condition?

Politely explain that you are not a medical professional and cannot provide a diagnosis. Encourage them to consult with a qualified healthcare provider.

3. What are some examples of unethical advertising claims for Reiki services?

Promising guaranteed cures for medical conditions, making claims that are not supported by scientific evidence, or misrepresenting your qualifications or experience.

Key Takeaways

  • Informed Consent: Always obtain informed consent from clients before providing Reiki sessions.
  • Client Confidentiality: Maintain strict client confidentiality to build trust and protect their privacy.
  • Scope of Practice: Stay within your scope of practice as a Certified Reiki Master Practitioner and avoid making medical claims.
  • Ethical Advertising: Advertise your services honestly and avoid misleading or exaggerated claims.
  • Professional Boundaries: Establish and maintain clear professional boundaries with clients.
