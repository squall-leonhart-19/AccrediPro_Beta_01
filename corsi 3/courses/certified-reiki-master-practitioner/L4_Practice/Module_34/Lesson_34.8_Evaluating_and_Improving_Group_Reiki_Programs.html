Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, what caught your eye about the Stress Relief Reiki Group?"
YOU:
"That's great you saw the ad on Facebook. What prompted you to book the call today?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the stress you're experiencing. What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"And how is this stress affecting your sleep? What happens when you try to wind down at night?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being completely overwhelmed, where would you rate your stress level right now? What would it take to get you down to a 3 or 4?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're dealing with significant stress from your job, which is impacting your sleep and overall well-being. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My Stress Relief Reiki Group is designed to help you do just that. Over six weeks, we'll meet weekly for 90 minutes to learn simple Reiki techniques you can use anytime, anywhere, to calm your mind and body. You'll also receive personalized support and guidance within a supportive community."
YOU:
"Many of my clients report feeling a significant reduction in stress and improved sleep within the first few weeks. For example, I had a client, Maria, a 48-year-old marketing manager, who was struggling with similar issues. After completing the group program, she told me she was finally able to sleep through the night and felt more in control of her stress levels."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what you've shared, do you think this group program could be a good fit for you?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if Reiki is really going to work for me."
"I understand your hesitation. Reiki is a gentle yet powerful energy healing modality that has helped many people manage stress and improve their well-being. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can share that a 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found that Reiki significantly reduced anxiety and pain levels compared to control groups."
"I'm worried I won't have time to attend the sessions."
"I understand that time is precious. The group meets for 90 minutes once a week, and the sessions will be recorded if you have to miss one. Plus, the Reiki techniques you'll learn can be practiced in just a few minutes each day, so you can easily integrate them into your busy schedule."
"It's a little more expensive than I was expecting."
"I appreciate you bringing that up. When considering the investment, think about the value you'll receive: six weeks of personalized Reiki instruction, a supportive community, and tools to manage your stress long-term. Many of my clients find that the benefits far outweigh the cost. Plus, I offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"Can I have a discount?"
"I understand the desire to save money. While I don't offer discounts on the group program itself, I do have a few options that might help. I can offer a payment plan to split the cost into more manageable installments. Also, if you sign up today, I'll include a bonus 30-minute one-on-one Reiki session with me at no extra charge. This will allow us to personalize your experience even further and address any specific concerns you might have. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are some key elements of a successful discovery call for a group Reiki program?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's pain points, presenting a clear solution, and confidently stating your price are crucial.

2. How can you handle the objection that Reiki might not work for a potential client?

Share statistics, testimonials, and case studies to demonstrate the effectiveness of Reiki. Acknowledge their concerns and provide evidence-based information.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a scalable way to increase your income and reach more clients.
  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into paying participants.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy can overcome potential barriers to enrollment.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your group program and its benefits to potential clients.
