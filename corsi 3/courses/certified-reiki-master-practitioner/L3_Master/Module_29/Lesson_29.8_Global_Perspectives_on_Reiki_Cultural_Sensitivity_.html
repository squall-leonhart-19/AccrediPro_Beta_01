The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a new client, Emily, 52, with fibromyalgia and anxiety. During the session, Emily started crying and said she felt overwhelmed. I didn't know whether to continue the Reiki or stop. I ended up just holding space, but I'm not sure if that was the right thing to do."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah explain the possibility of emotional release during the initial consultation?
  • What techniques did Sarah use during the session, and were they appropriate for Emily's condition?
  • How did Sarah manage her own emotional response to Emily's distress?
  • Did Sarah have resources to refer Emily to for additional support?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prepare Clients for Emotional Release

Explain that emotional release is a normal part of Reiki. Normalize the experience by mentioning it in the initial consultation, so clients feel safe and prepared.

2

Teach Grounding Techniques

Share grounding techniques Sarah can use with clients experiencing emotional release, such as deep breathing or visualizing roots connecting to the earth.

3

Review Session Adaptation

Discuss how to adapt a Reiki session based on the client's needs. For example, using gentle techniques for chronic pain or focusing on specific energy centers for anxiety.

4

Highlight Self-Care

Emphasize the importance of self-care for practitioners, especially after emotionally intense sessions. This includes grounding, meditation, or seeking support from a mentor or colleague.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, I commend you for holding space for Emily. It takes courage to support someone during an emotional release. Let's explore how we can make these situations even more empowering for both you and your clients."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I share my thoughts, what did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time?"
Offer Practical Strategies
"One strategy I find helpful is to explain the possibility of emotional release before the session. This helps clients feel safe and understand that it's a part of the healing process. You can also use grounding techniques to help them feel more centered."
Empower Her Confidence
"Remember, you have the knowledge and skills to support your clients. Trust your intuition and continue to learn and grow. I'm here to support you every step of the way."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of providing supervision to a new Reiki practitioner?

The primary goal is to support the new practitioner's growth, confidence, and competence in providing Reiki treatments while ensuring client safety and well-being.

2. How can you best support a mentee who is feeling overwhelmed by a complex client case?

Offer a safe space for them to share their concerns, validate their feelings, and provide practical strategies and resources to help them navigate the situation. Encourage them to trust their intuition and seek additional support when needed.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a crucial aspect of Reiki mastership, fostering growth in new practitioners.
  • Preparing clients for emotional release is essential for creating a safe and empowering environment.
  • Grounding techniques and session adaptation are valuable tools for addressing diverse client needs.
  • Constructive feedback involves acknowledging efforts, exploring perspectives, and offering practical strategies.
  • Effective mentoring requires creating a safe space, encouraging intuition, and celebrating successes.
