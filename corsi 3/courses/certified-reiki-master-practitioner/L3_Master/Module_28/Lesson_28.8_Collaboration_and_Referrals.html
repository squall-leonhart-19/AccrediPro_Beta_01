Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Excited but anxious about handling complex cases.

Background
Former teacher, strong interest in holistic wellness, limited practical experience.
Strengths
Excellent listener, compassionate, dedicated to learning.
Growth Areas
Needs help with treatment planning, setting boundaries, and managing expectations.
Her Question
"I have a client with chronic pain and trauma. I'm not sure how to best support her."

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 52

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily has fibromyalgia and a history of childhood trauma. She's very sensitive and gets easily overwhelmed during sessions. I've been doing Reiki, but I'm worried I'm not equipped to handle her emotional reactions. She cries a lot, and I feel like I should be doing more."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Sarah practicing within her scope of practice?
  • Is she equipped to deal with a client that has a history of trauma?
  • Is Sarah setting appropriate boundaries with Emily?
  • Has Sarah considered if Emily needs more specialized trauma support?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Clarify Scope of Practice

Reiterate that Reiki is complementary, not a replacement for medical or psychological treatment. Encourage collaboration with other professionals.

2

Emphasize Trauma-Informed Approach

Discuss the importance of creating a safe and predictable environment. Teach techniques for grounding and centering the client.

3

Guide Treatment Planning

Help Sarah create a structured treatment plan with clear goals and realistic expectations. Break down the plan into manageable steps.

4

Address Emotional Boundaries

Discuss the importance of setting healthy boundaries and practicing self-care. Teach Sarah how to manage her own emotional reactions during sessions.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Sarah, I appreciate you bringing this case to me. It's normal to feel uncertain when working with complex clients. Your willingness to seek guidance is a strength."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I offer suggestions, what are your thoughts on how the sessions are progressing? What do you feel is working well, and what feels challenging?"
Offer Guidance on Scope of Practice
"Remember, Reiki is wonderful for stress reduction and relaxation, but it's not a substitute for therapy. Have you considered suggesting that Emily seek support from a therapist specializing in trauma?"
Empower Her Decision-Making
"What steps do you feel comfortable taking with Emily? How can you create a safer and more supportive environment for her? I'm here to help you explore your options."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important thing to clarify when supervising a new Reiki practitioner working with a client with a history of trauma?

The practitioner's scope of practice and the importance of referring the client to a qualified therapist if needed.

2. How can you best support a mentee who is feeling overwhelmed by a client's emotional reactions during a Reiki session?

Help them develop strategies for setting healthy boundaries, practicing self-care, and creating a safe and predictable environment for the client.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves guiding and supporting new practitioners in their Reiki journey.
  • Clarifying scope of practice is crucial, especially when dealing with complex cases.
  • A trauma-informed approach is essential when working with clients with a history of trauma.
  • Setting healthy boundaries and practicing self-care are vital for both the practitioner and the client.
  • Empowering mentees to make informed decisions fosters their growth and confidence.
