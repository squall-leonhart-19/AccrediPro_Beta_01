Clinical Reasoning Process

Navigating the Complexity

Step 1: Acknowledge the Interconnectedness
Recognize that Eleanor's symptoms are likely interconnected, with trauma, autoimmunity, and lifestyle factors all playing a role. The polyvagal theory can be helpful in understanding the link between trauma and physical symptoms.
Step 2: Prioritize Safety and Stabilization
Given the trauma history, prioritize creating a safe and supportive environment. Begin with gentle Reiki techniques to promote relaxation and grounding. Avoid pushing too hard or triggering emotional overwhelm.
Step 3: Address the Physical Manifestations
Use Reiki to address the physical symptoms of fibromyalgia, Hashimoto's, and IBS. Focus on areas of pain and inflammation, while also supporting the body's natural healing processes.
Step 4: Support Emotional Processing
As Eleanor becomes more comfortable, gently support her emotional processing. Use Reiki to help her release trapped emotions and connect with her inner resources. Be mindful of potential abreactions and have strategies in place to manage them.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Contributing?

1

Adrenal Dysfunction

Chronic stress and trauma can impact adrenal function. Consider testing cortisol levels and providing adrenal support if indicated. A 2020 study in the Journal of Endocrinology found that chronic stress significantly alters HPA axis function, impacting cortisol release patterns.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Fibromyalgia and IBS can be associated with nutrient deficiencies. Assess for deficiencies in Vitamin D, magnesium, B vitamins, and iron. A meta-analysis in Nutrients (2022) showed a significant association between low Vitamin D levels and increased pain in fibromyalgia patients.

3

Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO)

IBS symptoms can be caused by SIBO. Consider testing and treating SIBO if indicated. Research indicates that up to 84% of IBS patients test positive for SIBO (source: American Journal of Gastroenterology).

4

Mold Toxicity

Exposure to mold can trigger inflammation and autoimmune responses. Explore potential mold exposure history and consider testing if indicated. Studies suggest that mycotoxins produced by molds can disrupt immune function and exacerbate autoimmune conditions.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when working with a client with a history of trauma?

Prioritize safety and stabilization, use gentle techniques, be mindful of potential triggers, and be prepared to refer to a therapist if needed.

2. How can Reiki help address the physical symptoms of fibromyalgia and Hashimoto's?

Reiki can help reduce pain and inflammation, support the body's natural healing processes, and promote relaxation.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a holistic approach that addresses the interconnectedness of physical, emotional, and lifestyle factors.
  • Prioritize safety and stabilization when working with clients with trauma histories.
  • Reiki can be a valuable tool for addressing both the physical and emotional symptoms of complex conditions.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare providers is essential in complex cases.
