Clinical Reasoning Process: Ethical Considerations

Navigating the Ethical Landscape

Step 1: Identify Ethical Dilemmas
Several ethical dilemmas arise: Dual relationship (potential romantic interest), scope of practice (past life regression), managing client expectations, and maintaining professional boundaries.
Step 2: Apply Ethical Principles
Consider the principles of beneficence (doing good), non-maleficence (doing no harm), autonomy (client's right to choose), and justice (fairness). Prioritize client well-being and avoid actions that could exploit or harm her.
Step 3: Boundary Setting
Address Carolyn's suggestive comments directly and professionally. Reiterate the professional nature of the relationship and set clear boundaries. Acknowledge her feelings but emphasize that a romantic relationship is not possible due to ethical constraints.
Step 4: Scope of Practice
Past life regression falls outside the scope of a Reiki Master Practitioner. Clearly explain that you are not qualified to offer this service and suggest referrals to qualified professionals (e.g., licensed therapists or hypnotherapists). Offering services beyond your expertise can be unethical and potentially harmful.

Differential Considerations: Underlying Issues

What Else Could Be Contributing?

1

Transference

Carolyn may be experiencing transference, projecting feelings onto you based on past relationships. This is common in therapeutic settings but requires careful management.

2

Unmet Emotional Needs

Her attraction may stem from unmet emotional needs or a desire for connection. Reiki can create a sense of intimacy, which may be misinterpreted.

3

Underlying Trauma

Migraines, anxiety, and IBS can sometimes be linked to unresolved trauma. While Reiki can be supportive, it's crucial to avoid re-traumatization.

4

Medication Side Effects

Amitriptyline and birth control pills can have side effects that contribute to her symptoms. Consider potential drug-nutrient interactions.

Check Your Understanding

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are common in advanced practice and require careful consideration.
  • Maintaining professional boundaries is essential for client safety and well-being.
  • Knowing your scope of practice and referring appropriately protects both you and your client.
  • Integrating Reiki with other modalities can enhance treatment outcomes.
  • Self-awareness and seeking supervision are crucial for navigating complex ethical challenges.
