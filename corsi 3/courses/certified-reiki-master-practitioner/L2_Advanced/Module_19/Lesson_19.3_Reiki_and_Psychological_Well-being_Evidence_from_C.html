Reiki and Anxiety Reduction

Anxiety disorders are among the most prevalent mental health conditions worldwide. Fortunately, research suggests that Reiki may be a valuable complementary therapy for reducing anxiety symptoms. Several RCTs have investigated this connection. For example, a 2015 study published in the *Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine* found that Reiki significantly reduced anxiety in participants compared to a placebo group (p < 0.05). Reiki's Impact on Depression

Depression is another significant mental health challenge, affecting millions globally. While Reiki is not a replacement for conventional medical treatment, research indicates that it can be avaluable adjunct therapy for alleviating depressive symptoms. A meta-analysis of 7 studies, published in *Complementary Therapies in Medicine* in 2018, concluded that Reiki had a moderate effect on reducing depression symptoms compared to control groups (SMD = -0.42, 95% CI [-0.68, -0.15]). The results showed that nurses who received regular Reiki sessions experienced a significant reduction in perceived stress and burnout levels compared to a control group. Enhancing Sleep Quality

Sleep disturbances are often intertwined with psychological distress. Research suggests that Reiki may promote better sleep. A randomized controlled trial published in *Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine* found that Reiki significantly improved sleep quality in elderly participants with insomnia. Participants receiving Reiki reported falling asleep faster, sleeping longer, and experiencing fewer nighttime awakenings compared to the control group. The Role of Expectation

It's crucial to acknowledge the potential role of client expectation and belief in shaping Reiki's psychological outcomes. The placebo effect is a well-documented phenomenon in healthcare, and it may contribute to the benefits observed in Reiki studies. However, even when accounting for the placebo effect, many studies still demonstrate statistically significant improvements in psychological well-being with Reiki. Furthermore, some research suggests that Reiki may have physiological effects that go beyond expectation alone, such as modulating the autonomic nervous system and reducing inflammation.
