Differential Considerations

Bloating, abdominal pain, and IBS-D are hallmark symptoms. A breath test is warranted to rule this out.

2

Eczema, migraines, and anxiety can be triggered by histamine. Assess dietary histamine load and consider DAO enzyme support.

If symptoms are widespread and seemingly unrelated, MCAS should be considered. Requires specialized testing and management.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the potential risks of long-term PPI use, and how might you address them in a client's treatment plan?

Long-term PPI use can lead to nutrient malabsorption (B12, magnesium, calcium), increased risk of SIBO, and rebound acid hypersecretion upon discontinuation. Address this by gradually weaning off PPIs (under medical supervision), supporting stomach acid production with betaine HCl and digestive enzymes, and addressing underlying gut dysbiosis.

2. A client presents with bloating, gas, and abdominal pain that worsens after eating high-FODMAP foods. What are the potential causes, and how would you investigate and manage this?

Potential causes include SIBO, fructose malabsorption, and general gut dysbiosis. Investigate with a SIBO breath test and a trial of a low-FODMAP diet. Manage with a tailored dietary approach, gut-healing supplements, and probiotics. Consider addressing underlying stress or anxiety, as these can exacerbate digestive symptoms.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex gut health cases require a holistic approach that considers the interconnectedness of various body systems.
  • Identifying and addressing root causes (e.g., medications, stress, diet) is crucial for long-term success.
  • A phased protocol allows for gradual implementation, monitoring of individual responses, and adjustment of interventions.
  • Knowing your scope of practice and when to refer to a medical professional is essential for client safety.
  • Empowering clients with education and lifestyle strategies is key to fostering sustainable gut health.
