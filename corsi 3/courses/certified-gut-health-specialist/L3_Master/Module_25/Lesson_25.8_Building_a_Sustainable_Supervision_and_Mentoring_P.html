The Challenging Case

Susan's Client: Emily, 52

What Susan Tells You:

"Emily came in with bloating, constipation, and fatigue. I put her on a standard gut healing protocol: bone broth, probiotics, L-glutamine, and a gluten-free diet. After two weeks, she reports feeling *worse* ‚Äì more bloated, gassier, and even more tired. I'm stumped! I feel like I failed her."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Probiotic Strain: Was it appropriate for her specific dysbiosis?
  • Underlying Conditions: Were there potential SIBO, histamine intolerance, or other issues not initially assessed?
  • Dietary Changes: Was the gluten-free diet implemented correctly? Were there hidden sources?
  • Pacing: Was the protocol introduced too quickly or aggressively?

Crafting Your Teaching Strategy

Guiding Susan Towards Solutions

1

Reframe "Failure" as Data

Emphasize that non-response is valuable information. "It tells us the initial approach wasn't quite right, and now we have clues to investigate."

2

Deepen Assessment Skills

Guide Susan to revisit Emily's intake forms. "Let's look for clues we might have missed. Any history of food sensitivities? Medication use? Stress levels?"

3

Introduce Advanced Concepts

Explain the potential role of SIBO or histamine intolerance in Emily's case. "Sometimes, probiotics can exacerbate these conditions. We need to rule them out."

4

Empower Her Decision-Making

Instead of dictating the next steps, ask Susan: "Based on this new information, what adjustments would you consider making to Emily's protocol?"

The Mentoring Conversation

Providing Constructive Guidance

Start with Affirmation
"Susan, I commend you for seeking guidance. It shows you're committed to providing the best care for your clients. Many practitioners would have just given up!"
Inquire About Her Process
"Walk me through your thought process with Emily. What were your initial assumptions? What specific symptoms were you targeting?"
Offer Targeted Insights
"In cases like this, I always consider the possibility of SIBO or histamine intolerance. Probiotics can sometimes worsen these conditions. Perhaps we need to investigate further."
Collaborate on Solutions
"What do you think about ordering a SIBO breath test for Emily? Or perhaps trying a low-histamine diet for a short period? What feels like the most logical next step to you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important first step when a mentee presents a challenging case?

The most important first step is to create a safe space for the mentee to share their concerns and challenges without judgment. Validate their feelings and acknowledge the complexity of the case.

2. How can you help a mentee move beyond a "failure" mindset when a protocol doesn't work?

Reframe the situation as a learning opportunity and emphasize that non-response provides valuable data. Encourage them to analyze the case, identify potential factors that were overlooked, and adjust their approach accordingly.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not doing the work for them.
  • Effective supervision requires creating a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Challenging cases are opportunities for growth and deeper understanding.
  • Empowerment and confidence-building are crucial aspects of successful mentoring.
