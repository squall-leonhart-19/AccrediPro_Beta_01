Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about your health goals. First, I'd like to understand what you're experiencing, then share how my program can help, and finally, answer any questions you have. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"I see you found me through Google. What specifically were you searching for that led you to my website?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Susan's Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned bloating, digestive discomfort, and low energy. Can you describe what a typical day looks like for you? When do these symptoms usually appear?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address these issues? What were the results?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being 'completely debilitating,' how would you rate the impact of these symptoms on your daily life?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing a range of gut-related symptoms that are significantly impacting your quality of life. Is that a fair assessment?"
YOU:
"My 12-week gut health program is designed to address the root causes of these issues. We use a personalized approach that includes functional testing, dietary modifications, lifestyle adjustments, and targeted supplementation. A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that personalized nutrition plans led to a 32% improvement in digestive symptoms compared to standard dietary advice (Smith et al., 2023)."
YOU:
"Many of my clients, like Sarah (age 48), who came to me with similar symptoms, experienced significant relief within the first few weeks of the program. She reported a 70% reduction in bloating and a 50% increase in energy levels."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, do you have any initial questions about what I've shared?"
YOU:
"Based on our conversation, I believe my program could be a great fit for you. Are you interested in learning more about how we can work together?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Health is an investment, and it's important to be mindful of your budget. Can I ask, what have you spent on managing these symptoms so far? We find that many clients save money in the long run by addressing the root cause rather than just managing symptoms. We also have payment plan options available to make it more accessible."
"I'm worried it won't work for me. I've tried so many things already."
"I hear your frustration. It's tough when you've tried things that haven't worked. My program is different because it's highly personalized and based on functional testing to identify your specific imbalances. We have a 90% success rate with clients who complete the full 12-week program. I'm confident we can find solutions that work for you."
"I need to think about it and do some more research."
"Absolutely, Susan. I want you to feel completely comfortable with your decision. What specific information are you looking for? I'm happy to provide you with additional resources or answer any further questions you have. Perhaps we can schedule a brief follow-up call next week to discuss your findings?"
"I'm not sure I have the time to commit to a 12-week program."
"I understand that time is valuable. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your lifestyle. The weekly time commitment is approximately 3-4 hours, including consultations, meal prep, and implementing lifestyle changes. Many of my clients find that they actually gain time and energy as they start feeling better. Plus, the skills and knowledge you gain during the program will benefit you for years to come."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of an effective discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's challenges, presenting your solution, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions to uncover the underlying concern (e.g., budget, time commitment). Offer additional resources and a follow-up call.

3. What's a confident way to present your pricing?

Clearly state the price, highlight the value and benefits of your program, and relate it to the client's desired outcomes. Offer payment planoptions if appropriate.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is Key: Thoroughly research your prospect and prepare targeted questions.
  • Listen Actively: Pay close attention to the prospect's needs and pain points.
  • Present Solutions, Not Just Features: Focus on how your program will solve their problems and achieve their goals.
  • Handle Objections with Empathy and Confidence: Address concerns directly and offer solutions.
  • Practice Your Pricing and Closing: Be confident and clear about the value you provide.
