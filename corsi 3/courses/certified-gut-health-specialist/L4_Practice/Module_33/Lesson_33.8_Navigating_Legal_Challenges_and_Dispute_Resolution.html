Navigating Objections

Common Concerns & Effective Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this right now."
"I understand. It's about prioritizing. Think about how much time you're currently spending feeling unwell. This program is designed to give you more energy and better health, which ultimately saves you time in the long run. Plus, the sessions are flexible and can be scheduled around your life."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing seems to work."
"I hear your frustration. What sets my approach apart is that we focus on personalized solutions based on your unique needs. We'll dig deep to uncover the root causes of your symptoms and create a plan tailored just for you. It's not a one-size-fits-all approach."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan to make it more accessible. We can break the investment into three monthly installments. Would that work better for you?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can provide some more information to help you make a confident decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: Warm Welcome & Rapport, Deep Dive into Challenges, Present Personalized Solution, Confident Close.

2. How should you handle the objection, "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions: "What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can provide more information to help you make a confident decision."

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you understand the client's needs and present your solution effectively.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident responses.
  • Practice stating your price with conviction.
  • Focus on the value you provide and the transformation your clients will experience.
