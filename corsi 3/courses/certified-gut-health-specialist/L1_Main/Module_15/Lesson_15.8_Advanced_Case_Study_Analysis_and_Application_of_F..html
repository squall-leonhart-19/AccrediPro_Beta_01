Your Coaching Script

Here's a sample script to guide your conversation. Remember to adapt it to your own style and your client's specific needs.

YOU:
"Emily, thank you for sharing your story with me. It sounds like you've been dealing with these symptoms for a while now. I want to assure you that you're not alone, and there are effective strategies we can use to improve your gut health and overall well-being."
EMILY:
"I appreciate that. I've tried a few things, but nothing seems to make a lasting difference."
YOU:
"That's understandable. Gut health can be complex, and it often requires a personalized approach. Based on what you've told me, I suspect factors like your antibiotic history, stress levels, and dietary habits are contributing to your symptoms. Does that sound accurate?"
EMILY:
"Yes, definitely. I've suspected that, but I wasn't sure where to start."
YOU:
"Great. To get a clearer picture, I'd like to ask you some specific questions about your diet, lifestyle, and bowel habits. This will help us identify potential imbalances in your gut microbiome and develop a targeted plan to restore balance. How does that sound?"
EMILY:
"That sounds perfect. I'm ready to get started."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to do before suggesting any dietary changes to a client?

Building trust and rapport. Make sure your client feels heard and understood before suggesting any changes.

2. How can you make sure a client feels comfortable sharing sensitive information about their bowel habits?

Use a non-judgmental and empathetic tone. Normalize the topic and explain why this information is important for understanding their gut health.
