Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on somatic awareness and safety. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open with me. It takes courage to share what you're experiencing. Before we begin, I want you to know that our work together will prioritize your safety and comfort above all else. We'll move at your pace, and you're always in control."
MARIA:
"That's a relief to hear. I've tried therapy before, and it felt overwhelming at times."
YOU:
"I understand. Somatic work is different. We'll be focusing on the wisdom of your body, not just your thoughts. Let's start by noticing what's happening in your body right now. Can you describe any sensations you're aware of?"
MARIA:
"Well, my chest feels a little tight, and my hands are clammy."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. Now, without trying to change anything, can you simply acknowledge those sensations? Tightness in the chest, clammy hands... just noticing."
YOU:
"We'll use this gentle, body-centered approach throughout our sessions. How does this feel to you so far?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is establishing safety paramount in somatic trauma work?

Because trauma often disrupts the nervous system's ability to regulate, creating a state of hyperarousal or hypoarousal. Establishing safety helps the client's nervous system begin to regulate and allows for deeper exploration.

2. Give an example of a somatic-based question you could ask a client to increase body awareness.

"What sensations are you noticing in your body right now?" or "Where in your body do you feel that emotion most strongly?"

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize safety and client autonomy in all somatic trauma sessions.
  • Focus on building body awareness and regulation skills.
  • Move at the client's pace, respecting their boundaries.
  • Avoid pushing clients to relive traumatic memories before they are ready.
