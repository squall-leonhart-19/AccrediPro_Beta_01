Identifying Key Somatic Targets

Identifying key somatic targets is essential for effective trauma resolution. These targets are specific sensations, movements, and postures that are associated with the trauma. By addressing these targets directly, you can help the client to release the trauma that is stored in their body.

Here are some examples of somatic targets:

  • Sensations: Tightness in the chest, knots in the stomach, tingling in the limbs
  • Movements: Trembling, shaking, fidgeting
  • Postures: Hunched shoulders, clenched fists, frozen gaze

When identifying somatic targets, pay attention to the clientâ€™s nonverbal cues, such as facial expressions, body language, and tone of voice. Ask the client to describe their sensations in detail, using open-ended questions such as:

  • "What does that sensation feel like?"
  • "Where do you feel it in your body?"
  • "Does the sensation have a shape, size, or color?"

By carefully observing and listening to the client, you can identify the somatic targets that are most relevant to their trauma.

  1. Resourcing: Begin by building a strong foundation of resources. This might include identifying internal resources (e.g., strengths, skills, positive memories) and external resources (e.g., supportive relationships, safe places).
  2. Sensation Exploration: Gradually introduce sensation exploration, starting with gentle and neutral sensations. As the client becomes more comfortable, you can begin to explore sensations that are associated with the trauma.
  3. Body Awareness & Movement: Incorporate body awareness and movement practices to help the client to reconnect with their body and regulate their nervous system. This might include mindful movement, yoga, or dance.
