Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your story. It sounds like you've been through a lot, and I commend you for seeking support. I understand you're experiencing chronic pain, anxiety, and sleep difficulties. I want to assure you that these symptoms are often connected to past trauma, and somatic trauma release can be a powerful approach to help you find relief and restore a sense of safety in your body."
MARIA:
"I've heard of somatic therapy, but I'm not sure I fully understand it. How is it different from regular talk therapy?"
YOU:
"That's a great question. While talk therapy focuses on processing thoughts and emotions, somatic trauma release works directly with the body's stored trauma. Think of it this way: traumatic experiences can get 'stuck' in our nervous system, leading to physical tension, pain, and emotional dysregulation. Somatic work helps release those trapped energies and restore a sense of balance."
YOU:
"Before we begin, I want to emphasize that this is a collaborative process. We'll work together, at your pace, to gently explore any sensations or memories that arise. Your safety and comfort are my top priorities. Does this approach resonate with you?"
MARIA:
"It does. I'm a little nervous, but I'm also hopeful that this could finally help me move forward."
YOU:
"That's perfectly understandable. Let's start with a simple grounding exercise to help you connect with your body in the present moment. This will help create a foundation of safety and stability as we move forward."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between talk therapy and Somatic Trauma Release?

Talk therapy focuses on processing thoughts and emotions, while Somatic Trauma Release works directly with the body's stored trauma.

2. Why is it important to emphasize a gentle, gradual approach when working with trauma?

Forcing a client to relive the trauma can be retraumatizing. A gentle approach allows the client to feel safe and in control.

Key Takeaways

  • Somatic Trauma Release addresses trauma stored in the body.
  • Prioritize creating a safe and supportive environment for your clients.
  • Emphasize a gentle, gradual approach to avoid retraumatization.
  • Validate the client's experience and acknowledge their pain.
  • Explain somatic concepts in simple, accessible language.
