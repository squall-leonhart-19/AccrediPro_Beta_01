Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on somatic awareness and safety. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing that with me. I understand you're feeling disconnected and stuck. Before we begin, I want to assure you that our work together will always prioritize your safety and comfort. We'll move at your pace, and you're in control."
DIANE:
"That's reassuring. I've felt overwhelmed in the past trying to process everything."
YOU:
"I understand. Somatic work is different from talk therapy. We'll be focusing on your body's wisdom and how it holds memories. We'll gently explore sensations, not force any specific emotions or stories to come up."
YOU:
"Let's start by simply noticing your breath. Can you feel your chest rising and falling, or your belly expanding and contracting? There's no right or wrong way; just observe. What do you notice?"
DIANE:
"I guess I feel a little tightness in my chest."
YOU:
"Thank you for noticing that. There's no need to change it. Just acknowledge it. We'll use this as a starting point to build awareness and regulation. How does it feel to simply acknowledge the tightness without needing to fix it?"

‚ùå Using jargon or technical terms without explanation.
‚úì Use simple, accessible language. For example, say "body awareness" instead of "interoception."
‚ùå Ignoring or dismissing the client's reported sensations.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is establishing safety paramount in somatic trauma work?

Because trauma can dysregulate the nervous system, creating a sense of unsafety. Establishing safety allows the client to gently explore their sensations without being overwhelmed.

2. What's a good way to help a client connect with their body in the initial session?

Guide them through a simple breath awareness exercise, focusing on the physical sensations of breathing without trying to change anything.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize establishing safety and trust with the client.
  • Use simple, accessible language to explain somatic concepts.
  • Help the client connect with their body through breath awareness.
  • Validate the client's sensations and experiences.
  • Move at the client's pace, always checking in on their comfort level.
