    The Art of the Discovery Call

    Building Connections and Uncovering Needs

    Phase 1: Building Rapport

    Begin by creating a warm and welcoming atmosphere. Ask open-ended questions to learn about the prospect's background, interests, and aspirations. Show genuine interest in their story and establish a personal connection.

    Phase 2: Uncovering Needs

    Dive deeper into the prospect's challenges and pain points. Ask probing questions to understand the root causes of their issues and the impact on their lives. Listen actively and empathetically to their responses.

    Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution

    Clearly articulate how your program can address the prospect's specific needs and help them achieve their desired outcomes. Highlight the unique benefits of your approach and the transformative results they can expect.

    Phase 4: Closing

    Confidently invite the prospect to take the next step and enroll in your program. Address any remaining concerns or objections and provide clear instructions on how to get started. Create a sense of urgency and excitement about the possibilities ahead.

    Case Study: Sarah, 58, Seeking Purpose in Retirement

    Client: Sarah, a 58-year-old recently retired teacher, felt lost and without purpose. She missed the structure and social interaction of her job. During the discovery call, she expressed feeling anxious and unsure of how to fill her time.

    Intervention: The Somatic Trauma Release Specialist actively listened to Sarah's concerns, validated her feelings, and highlighted how the program could help her rediscover her passions and create a fulfilling new chapter. They emphasized the importance of self-care and connecting with her body to reduce anxiety.

    Outcomes: Sarah enrolled in the program and, within a few weeks, reported feeling more energized, connected to her body, and excited about exploring new hobbies. She joined a local hiking group and started volunteering at a children's literacy program.

    Case Study: John, 62, Struggling with Physical Pain

    Client: John, a 62-year-old former construction worker, suffered from chronic back pain and limited mobility. He felt frustrated and discouraged by his physical limitations, which prevented him from enjoying his favorite activities.

    Intervention: The Somatic Trauma Release Specialist focused on how the program could help John release tension in his body, improve his flexibility, and reduce his pain levels. They explained how somatic exercises could help him reconnect with his body and regain a sense of control.

    Outcomes: John experienced a significant reduction in pain and improved mobility after several weeks of the program. He was able to resume gardening and playing golf, activities he had previously given up.
