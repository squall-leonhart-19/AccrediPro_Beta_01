Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you so much for scheduling this call. I'm Olivia, and I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, can you tell me a bit about what brought you here today?"
YOU:
"I saw you mentioned anxiety and sleep issues. Is there anything specific you'd like to focus on during our call?"
Phase 2: Uncovering the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about your anxiety. When did it start, and what does it feel like in your body?"
YOU:
"And what about your sleep? How many hours are you getting, and how does it affect your day?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage your anxiety and sleep? What has worked, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what do you see your life looking like in 6 months? How would you *like* your life to look?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're experiencing [summarize her pain points and goals]. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The Somatic Trauma Release approach I use is different because we address the root causes of your anxiety and sleep issues by connecting with your body's wisdom. We'll work together over 12 weeks to gently release stored tension and re-establish a sense of safety within your nervous system. Many of my clients report feeling calmer, more grounded, and sleeping better within just a few weeks."
YOU:
"This program includes [list 2-3 key features, e.g., weekly 1:1 sessions, personalized movement practices, access to a private online community]."
Phase 4: Invitation & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe this program can help you find the relief you're seeking. Does this sound like a good fit for you?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can move forward."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Welcome & Connection, Uncovering the Problem, Presenting Your Solution, and Invitation & Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Ask clarifying questions such as, "What part do you need to think about? Is it the program itself, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to feel comfortable."

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting potential clients into paying clients.
  • Structure your calls into four distinct phases: Welcome & Connection, Uncovering the Problem, Presenting Your Solution, and Invitation & Close.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections such as, "I need to think about it" or "It's more than I was expecting to spend."
  • Practice stating your prices clearly and confidently, highlighting the value and benefits of your program.
  • Consistently invite potential clients to take the next step and commit to their well-being.
