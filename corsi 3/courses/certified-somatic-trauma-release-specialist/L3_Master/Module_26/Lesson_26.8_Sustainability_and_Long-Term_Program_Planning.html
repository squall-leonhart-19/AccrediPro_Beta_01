The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 52, who is dealing with chronic pain and anxiety. During our last session, I guided her through a body scan, and she started crying uncontrollably. I felt panicked and didn't know what to do. I ended up just stopping the session. Did I make things worse?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Was Sarah prepared for a strong emotional response?
  • Did she have a plan for containment and resourcing if the client became overwhelmed?
  • Did she debrief with the client after the session to ensure they felt safe?
  • What were Sarah's own feelings and reactions during the session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Acknowledge Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client has a strong reaction. It shows you care."

2

Review the Importance of Resourcing

Emphasize that resourcing is not a one-time event, but an ongoing process. Explore whether Emily had sufficient resources in place *before* the body scan.

3

Teach Titration and Containment

Explain the concept of titration ‚Äì gently introducing potentially triggering material in small, manageable doses. Discuss containment strategies to help clients manage intense emotions.

4

Encourage Self-Reflection

Ask Sarah: "What did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time? How did *you* feel during the session, and how might that have impacted Emily?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I'm so glad you reached out. It takes courage to ask for help, and it demonstrates your commitment to your clients. Let's unpack this together."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I offer my thoughts, can you tell me more about what was going through your mind during the session? What were you feeling?"
Introduce Key Concepts
"In Somatic Trauma Release, we often talk about the importance of titration ‚Äì gently pacing the work to avoid overwhelming the client. It sounds like Emily might have accessed some intense emotions very quickly. What do you think?"
Empower Her Next Steps
"What feels like the most important thing to address with Emily in your next session? How can you create a sense of safety and support for her?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of validating a mentee's feelings when they present a challenging case?

Validating their feelings helps build trust, creates a safe space for learning, and normalizes the challenges inherent in clinical work. It also helps them feel heard and understood, which reduces defensiveness.

2. Describe the concept of "titration" and why it is important in Somatic Trauma Release work.

Titration involves introducing potentially triggering material in small, manageable doses. This allows the client to gradually process the trauma without becoming overwhelmed. It's important because it respects the client's nervous system and promotes safety.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new practitioners involves creating a safe and supportive learning environment.
  • Validating their feelings and experiences is crucial for building trust and rapport.
  • Teaching key concepts like resourcing, titration, and containment can empower them to handle challenging situations.
  • Encouraging self-reflection helps them develop their clinical reasoning and intuition.
  • Focus on their strengths and provide ongoing support for their professional growth.
