Check Your Understanding

1. What is the difference between empathy and emotional contagion, and why is this distinction important in trauma work?

Empathy is understanding the client's feelings without necessarily experiencing them yourself, while emotional contagion is when you start to feel the client's emotions as your own. Maintaining this distinction is crucial for practitioners to stay grounded, avoid burnout, and provide effective support without becoming overwhelmed by the client's trauma.

2. Describe three self-resourcing techniques that a new practitioner can use to manage countertransference during sessions.

Three self-resourcing techniques include: (1) Breathwork: Practicing deep, calming breaths to regulate the nervous system. (2) Grounding exercises: Connecting with the present moment through sensory awareness (e.g., feeling the chair, noticing sounds). (3) Mental imagery: Visualizing a safe and calming place to create a sense of inner peace.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is crucial for new practitioners to navigate the complexities of trauma work.
  • Countertransference is a normal experience and an opportunity for self-awareness.
  • Self-resourcing techniques and boundary setting are essential for practitioner well-being.
  • Constructive feedback should be validating, empowering, and focused on principles, not just answers.
