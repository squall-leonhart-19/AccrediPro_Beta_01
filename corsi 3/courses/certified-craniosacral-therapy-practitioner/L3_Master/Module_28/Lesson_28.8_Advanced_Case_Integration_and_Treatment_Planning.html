The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Emily, 48 years old

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily, 48, presents with chronic lower back pain, headaches, and fatigue. She reports a history of emotional neglect during childhood. During our first session, Emily became very tearful when I palpated her sacrum. I felt unsure how to proceed and ended the session early."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Sarah establish clear boundaries and informed consent regarding potential emotional release?
  • How did Sarah manage her own emotional response to Emily's tears?
  • What specific CST techniques did Sarah use, and were they appropriate for a trauma-sensitive approach?
  • Did Sarah have a plan for grounding and resourcing Emily after the emotional release?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Trauma-Informed Principles

Emphasize the importance of safety, choice, collaboration, and empowerment in working with clients with trauma histories. Review the key principles of trauma-informed care.

2

Somatic Awareness & Titration

Discuss the concept of titration ‚Äì introducing small, manageable doses of sensation to allow the client to build tolerance and resilience. Explain how to help Emily connect with her body in a safe and controlled way.

3

Resourcing Techniques

Explore resourcing techniques that can help Emily feel more grounded and safe in her body. These may include guided imagery, breathwork, or gentle movement exercises.

4

Scope of Practice & Referrals

Clarify the boundaries of CST practice and the importance of referring clients to mental health professionals when appropriate. Discuss the ethical considerations of working with trauma.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Sarah, it's completely understandable that you felt overwhelmed. Working with trauma can be challenging, and it's commendable that you're seeking guidance."
Validate Her Intuition
"It sounds like you were very attuned to Emily's emotional state when you palpated her sacrum. That's a valuable skill."
Offer Specific Suggestions
"Next time, consider starting with techniques that are less likely to evoke strong emotional responses. For example, you could begin by working with the client‚Äôs feet or hands. You could also try a resourcing exercise before beginning any bodywork."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on what you know about Emily, what feels like the most appropriate next step? Remember, you have the power to modify the session as needed."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key principles of trauma-informed care?

The four key principles of trauma-informed care are safety, choice, collaboration, and empowerment.

2. What is "titration" in the context of trauma-sensitive Craniosacral Therapy?

Titration involves introducing small, manageable doses of sensation to allow the client to build tolerance and resilience.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is a powerful way to contribute to the field of CST.
  • Trauma-informed care is essential when working with complex cases.
  • Focus on empowering your mentee to develop their clinical reasoning skills.
  • Remember the importance of self-care as a mentor.
