Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you've been carrying a lot, both physically and emotionally. Many of my clients in their 50s experience similar feelings of being 'stuck,' and it often manifests as tension in the neck and shoulders."
MARIA:
"Yes, exactly! I feel like I'm just going through the motions. And my neck is always tight."
YOU:
"That makes perfect sense. Our bodies hold onto stress and unresolved emotions. Craniosacral therapy can be a powerful tool to release that tension and create space for new possibilities. Are you open to exploring how the physical and emotional aspects might be connected?"
MARIA:
"I am. I've never really thought about it that way, but it resonates with me."
YOU:
"Great. Let's begin with a gentle assessment of your craniosacral system. This will give us a sense of where the restrictions are and how we can best support your body's natural healing process. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of acknowledging the emotional component of a client's physical pain?

Acknowledging the emotional component helps build trust and allows for a more holistic approach to healing, recognizing that the body and mind are interconnected.

2. Why is it important to avoid using technical jargon when speaking with a client?

Using simple, relatable language ensures that the client understands the process and feels comfortable and empowered to participate in their own healing journey.

Key Takeaways

  • Recognize the interconnectedness of physical and emotional well-being.
  • Communicate in clear, relatable language.
  • Build rapport and trust with your clients.
  • Set realistic expectations for therapy outcomes.
