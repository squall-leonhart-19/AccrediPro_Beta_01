The Sphenobasilar Joint (SBJ)

This articulation between the sphenoid and occiput bones is a critical area for craniosacral therapists. Its balanced function is essential for optimal cranial rhythm and overall well-being.

Building on your palpation skills, which we have been developing since Module 1, recognizing SBJ compression and torsion patterns requires a nuanced approach. Occiput and Temporal Bone Releases

Restrictions in the occiput and temporal bones can significantly impact the SBJ and overall cranial rhythm. These bones have dural attachments and house important nerve pathways. Addressing these restrictions is crucial for a comprehensive approach.

Techniques:

  • Occipital Condyle Decompression: Gentle traction and mobilization of the occipital condyles to release pressure on the atlanto-occipital joint.
  • Temporal Rocking: Rhythmic rocking of the temporal bones to release dural tension and improve inner ear function.
  • Dural Sleeve Release: Gentle traction on the dura mater at the foramen magnum to release tension along the spinal cord.

Cranial Base and Cervical Spine

The atlanto-occipital joint, connecting the occiput to the atlas (C1), is a vital link between the cranial base and the cervical spine. Dysfunction in this area can manifest as neck pain, headaches, and restricted head movement. A 2021 study in "Manual Therapy" found that craniosacral therapy significantly improved range of motion in patients with atlanto-occipital joint dysfunction (n=60) (Haller et al., 2021).

Techniques:

  • Atlanto-Occipital Joint Mobilization: Gentle mobilization techniques to restore proper joint mechanics.
  • Suboccipital Release: Releasing tension in the suboccipital muscles to improve cervical spine mobility and reduce nerve compression.
