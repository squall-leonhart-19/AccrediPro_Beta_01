Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify the Key Imbalances
Eleanor presents with a complex interplay of musculoskeletal issues (TMJ, neck stiffness, scoliosis, whiplash history), neurological symptoms (migraines, anxiety), digestive complaints (GERD), and systemic issues (fibromyalgia, fatigue). This points to a potential central sensitization issue along with structural imbalances.
Step 2: Uncover the Contributing Factors
Her whiplash history likely created lasting restrictions in the craniosacral system. High stress and poor sleep exacerbate her pain and anxiety. Low ferritin and Vitamin D contribute to fatigue and pain sensitivity. The use of Famotidine can impair nutrient absorption, potentially worsening her ferritin levels.
Step 3: Prioritize Your Treatment Strategy
Address the most immediate structural restrictions (SBS, sacral base) to improve CSF flow and overall nervous system regulation. Then, focus on releasing dura tension and temporal bone restrictions to alleviate migraine triggers. Consider gentle visceral work to support digestive function. Prioritize the craniosacral system first, then move distally as the body integrates.
Step 4: Integrate Lifestyle Recommendations
Suggest stress-reduction techniques (mindfulness, meditation), gentle exercise (yoga, walking), and dietary changes (reducing caffeine, increasing iron-rich foods). Encourage her to work with her physician to optimize Vitamin D and ferritin levels.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Cervicogenic Headaches

Given her whiplash history and neck stiffness, consider that her migraines may be cervicogenic in origin. Thoroughly assess cervical spine mobility and muscle tension.

2

Hormonal Imbalances

At age 48, perimenopause could be contributing to her migraines, anxiety, and sleep disturbances. Consider recommending hormone testing with her physician.

3

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Chronic migraines, GERD, and fibromyalgia can sometimes be associated with MCAS. Look for patterns of symptom flares and consider dietary triggers.

4

Occult Tethered Cord

Scoliosis and sacral base restriction could indicate a tethered cord. Assess for neurological signs like bowel/bladder dysfunction or lower extremity weakness.
