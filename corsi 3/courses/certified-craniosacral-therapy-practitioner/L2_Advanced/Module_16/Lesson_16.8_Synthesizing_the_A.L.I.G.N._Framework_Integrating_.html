  • Identify red flags and contraindications for Craniosacral Therapy.
  • Create a phased treatment plan that addresses the client's needs safely and effectively.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify Key Systems Involved
This case involves the neurological (migraines, anxiety, insomnia), musculoskeletal (TMJ, neck stiffness, fibromyalgia), cardiovascular (mitral valve prolapse), and autonomic nervous systems (wired but tired). The history of whiplash is also a critical factor.
Step 2: Explore the Interconnections
The whiplash likely created restrictions in the cervical spine and cranial base, contributing to the migraines and TMJ pain. The chronic pain and stress have likely sensitized her nervous system, leading to anxiety and insomnia. The mitral valve prolapse, while mild, may contribute to her feeling of being "wired."
Step 3: Prioritize Assessment Areas
Focus on the cranial base (occiput, temporal bones), cervical spine (especially C0-C2), TMJ, and dural system. Assess for restrictions, asymmetries, and areas of congestion. Also, evaluate her autonomic nervous system tone.
Step 4: Formulate Initial Treatment Goals
The primary goals are to reduce pain, improve sleep, decrease anxiety, and restore balance to the autonomic nervous system. Start with gentle techniques to release restrictions in the cranial base and cervical spine. Address TMJ dysfunction and dural tension as tolerated.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Cervicogenic Headaches

Headaches originating from the neck. Rule out through careful assessment of cervical spine mobility and palpation.

2

Temporomandibular Joint Disorder (TMD)

Assess for clicking, popping, or limited range of motion in the TMJ. Consider referral to a dentist specializing in TMD.

3

Autonomic Nervous System Dysregulation

Evaluate heart rate variability (HRV) and other indicators of autonomic imbalance. Consider referral to a healthcare provider specializing in autonomic disorders.

4

Medication Side Effects

Amitriptyline and Xanax can have side effects that mimic or exacerbate her symptoms. Discuss with her physician.

After 12 weeks of Craniosacral Therapy, Eleanor reported a 50% reduction in migraine frequency and intensity. Her sleep improved, and she was able to reduce her Xanax use. She also noticed a significant decrease in neck stiffness and TMJ pain. Eleanor felt more relaxed, less anxious, and more in control of her health.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-thinking approach that considers the interconnectedness of various body systems.
  • Prioritize assessment and treatment based on the client's presentation and history.
  • Always rule out red flags and contraindications before initiating Craniosacral Therapy.
  • Develop a phased treatment plan that addresses the client's needs safely and effectively.
  • Consider referral to other healthcare providers for comprehensive care.
