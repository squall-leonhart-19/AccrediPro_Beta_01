Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this consultation. I'm Maya, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can help you. To make the best use of our time, I'd like to understand your current challenges, discuss how Craniosacral Therapy can help, and then explore if my program is a good fit for you. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Great! To start, tell me a bit more about what you're experiencing with your back pain."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU:
"When did the back pain start? How does it impact your ability to garden and play with your grandchildren?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to alleviate the pain? What were the results?"
YOU:
"Imagine you wake up three months from now, and your back pain is significantly reduced. What does your daily life look like? What activities are you able to enjoy?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with chronic back pain that's impacting your quality of life and preventing you from doing the things you love. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"Craniosacral Therapy can help by gently releasing restrictions in the connective tissues surrounding your brain and spinal cord, which can reduce pain, improve mobility, and promote relaxation. My 12-week program is designed to address the root causes of your pain and help you achieve lasting relief. We'll work together to create a personalized treatment plan that fits your specific needs."
YOU:
"Many of my clients experience significant improvements in their pain levels and overall well-being within the first few weeks of the program. For example, I recently worked with a client, Maria, who is 48 and was experiencing similar back pain. After 8 weeks, she was able to garden for an hour without pain and sleep through the night."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you think my Craniosacral Therapy program could be a good fit for you?"
YOU:
"Great! Let's talk about the details of the program and how we can get you started on the path to pain relief."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for a 12-week program."
"I understand that time is valuable. The sessions are designed to be efficient and effective, and we can work together to create a schedule that fits your lifestyle. Remember, investing in your health now can save you time and energy in the long run by reducing pain and improving your overall well-being."
"I'm concerned about the cost."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. However, think about the long-term benefits of reducing your pain and improving your quality of life. Many of my clients find that they save money on pain medication and other treatments after completing the program. We also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to do more research on Craniosacral Therapy."
"That's a great idea! I can send you some articles and research studies that demonstrate the effectiveness of Craniosacral Therapy for back pain. I'm also happy to answer any specific questions you have after you've had a chance to review the information."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can promise that I will provide you with the highest quality care and support throughout the program. Many of my clients experience significant improvements in their pain levels, mobility, and overall well-being. I am confident that if you commit to the program and follow my recommendations, you will see positive results."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem and desired outcome, presenting your approach and value, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, then gently inquire about the specific concerns they need to consider (investment, time, etc.). Offer to provide more information or schedule a follow-up call to address their concerns.

Key Takeaways
