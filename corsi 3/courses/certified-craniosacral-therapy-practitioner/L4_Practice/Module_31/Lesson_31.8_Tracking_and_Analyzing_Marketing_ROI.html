Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Qualify 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time. To start, can you tell me a bit more about what prompted you to reach out regarding Craniosacral Therapy?"
YOU:
"I understand you're dealing with chronic back pain. How long has this been affecting you, and what have you tried so far?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe the pain? Is it constant, or does it come and go? What activities make it worse?"
YOU:
"How is this back pain impacting your daily life? Are you able to work, enjoy hobbies, or spend time with loved ones comfortably?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate this pain, what would your life look like? What would you be able to do that you can't do now?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like this back pain is significantly impacting your quality of life. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"Craniosacral Therapy can help by gently releasing tension patterns in your body, which can alleviate pain and improve mobility. My approach is to work with you over 8-12 sessions to address the root causes of your pain, not just the symptoms. Many clients report noticeable improvements within the first few sessions."
YOU:
"I've worked with many clients in their 40s and 50s who have experienced similar challenges, and they've found significant relief through Craniosacral Therapy. For example, I worked with Maria, a 48-year-old teacher, who had chronic neck pain. After 10 sessions, she reported a 70% reduction in pain and was able to return to her active lifestyle."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, do you feel like this approach resonates with you and what you're looking for? ... Great! Let's discuss the program details and how we can get you started on the path to pain relief."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand that cost is a concern, Susan. Let's look at it this way: how much have you already spent on treatments that haven't worked? What if, instead of just managing the pain, we could actually address the root cause and help you regain your quality of life? What is that worth to you?"
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration, Susan. Craniosacral Therapy is different because it works with the body's natural healing mechanisms to release tension and restore balance. It's a gentle, holistic approach that addresses the underlying causes of your pain. Many of my clients come to me after trying other treatments without success, and they're often surprised by the results."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course, Susan. It's an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed choice."
"Will this really work for me?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can tell you that many clients with similar conditions have experienced significant improvements with Craniosacral Therapy. We'll work together to create a personalized treatment plan tailored to your specific needs and goals. And, I offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you don't see any improvement after the first few sessions, we'll reassess and adjust the plan or provide a refund for the unused sessions."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting a tailored solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you handle the objection "I'm not sure if I can afford it"?

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the investment by highlighting the potential return on investment (e.g., improved quality of life, reduced reliance on medications), and offer flexible payment options if possible.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Thoroughly understand your ideal client's needs, concerns, and goals.
  • Active listening is crucial: Pay close attention to what your prospect is saying and tailor your responses accordingly.
  • Confidence is contagious: Believe in the value of your services and communicate that belief with conviction.
  • Objections are opportunities: Use objections as a chance to address concerns and reiterate the benefits of your services.
  • Practice makes perfect: The more you practice your sales skills, the more natural and effective you'll become.
