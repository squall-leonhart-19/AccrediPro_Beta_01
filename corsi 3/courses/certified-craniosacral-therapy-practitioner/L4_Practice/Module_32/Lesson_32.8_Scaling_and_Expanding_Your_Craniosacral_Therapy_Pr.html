Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call! I'm Maya, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can help you. To start, what sparked your interest in craniosacral therapy?"
YOU:
"That's interesting that you found me on Instagram! What were you searching for when you came across my profile?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Problem & Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the stress and anxiety you're experiencing. How long has this been going on, and what does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"You mentioned trouble sleeping. Can you describe what that's like? Are you having difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or both?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage your stress and anxiety? And what results did you see?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in six months or a year? What's at stake for you if you don't address this?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with significant stress and anxiety that's impacting your sleep and overall well-being. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"Craniosacral therapy can help by gently releasing tension in your central nervous system, promoting deep relaxation, and restoring balance to your body. It's a very gentle, hands-on approach that works with your body's natural healing abilities."
YOU:
"My approach is tailored to your specific needs. We'll work together over [Number] sessions to identify the root causes of your stress and anxiety and develop a personalized treatment plan. Many clients report feeling a sense of calm and ease after just a few sessions."
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with you, Susan?"
Phase 4: Close & Invite to Next Step 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, I believe craniosacral therapy could be a great fit for you. Are you interested in learning more about my program and how we can get started?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. What aspects do you need more time to consider? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make a confident decision."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate you sharing that. Let's explore that a bit. What value do you place on feeling less stressed, sleeping better, and regaining control of your life? Consider the cost of *not* addressing this ‚Äì the continued stress, potential health issues, and impact on your relationships. My program is an investment in your long-term well-being."
"I'm not sure if it will work for me."
"That's a valid concern. I encourage you to read the testimonials on my website from clients who have experienced similar challenges. Every body responds differently, but craniosacral therapy has a high success rate in addressing stress and anxiety. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you don't feel a positive shift after the first session, I'll gladly refund your money."
"I don't have the time."
"I understand that time is precious. However, think of this as an investment in your health and productivity. By reducing your stress and improving your sleep, you'll likely find you have more energy and focus throughout the day. We can also work together to find appointment times that fit your schedule."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: building rapport, uncovering the problem, presenting your solution, and closing/inviting to the next step.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the cost as an investment in their well-being and quality of life. Discuss the potential costs of *not* addressing the problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key. Understand your ideal client and their needs.
  • A structured discovery call builds trust and uncovers pain points.
  • Confidently present your value and pricing.
  • Address objections with empathy and understanding.
  • Consistent client acquisition is essential for a sustainable practice.
