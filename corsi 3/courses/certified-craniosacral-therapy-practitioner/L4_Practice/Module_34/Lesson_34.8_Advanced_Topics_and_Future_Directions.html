Crafting Your Group Program Pitch

Your 20-Minute Discovery Call Outline

Phase 1: Connection & Validation 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so great to connect with you! I know how challenging this stage of life can be. Many women experience similar feelings of overwhelm and isolation."
YOU:
"What specifically led you to explore group programs?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Needs 7 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about what a 'successful' program would look like for you. What are your biggest hopes?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest fears or concerns about joining a group program?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to making a change in your life right now?"
Phase 3: Presenting the Group Program 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe my 'Renew You' group program is the perfect fit. It's designed to help women in your exact situation regain their sense of self, build meaningful connections, and learn practical self-care strategies."
YOU:
"The program includes [list key benefits and features]. Imagine feeling [paint a picture of the positive outcome]."
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 3 min
YOU:
"Does 'Renew You' sound like the kind of support you've been searching for? If so, I'd love to invite you to join us."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful group program pitch?

Connection, needs assessment, presenting the program as a solution, and a clear call to action.

2. How can you address the objection "I don't have enough time"?

Reframe the program as an investment in overall well-being that can ultimately create more time and energy.

Key Takeaways

  • Clearly define your ideal group program client to tailor your messaging effectively.
  • Craft a compelling pitch that addresses their specific needs and desires.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections.
  • State your pricing with assurance and highlight the value of your program.
  • Include a clear call to action to guide potential participants to enroll.
