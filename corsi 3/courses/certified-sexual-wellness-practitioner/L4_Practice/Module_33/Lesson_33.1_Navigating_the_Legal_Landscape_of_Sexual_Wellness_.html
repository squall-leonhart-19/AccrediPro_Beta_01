Activities that fall outside your scope of practice include:

Recognize the boundaries of your expertise and refer clients to qualified professionals when necessary. Collaboration with doctors, therapists, and other healthcare providers can enhance your services and ensure your clients receive comprehensive care.

Informed Consent: The Cornerstone

Informed consent is the ethical and legal bedrock of your practice. It ensures that clients understand the nature of your services, potential risks and benefits, and their right to withdraw at any time.

Key elements of informed consent include:

  • Clear Explanation of Services: Describe your services in plain language, avoiding jargon. Emphasize that you are not a medical professional.
  • Potential Risks and Benefits: Outline potential benefits, such as improved communication, increased confidence, or enhanced sexual satisfaction. Also, discuss potential risks, such as emotional discomfort or frustration.
  • Confidentiality: Explain the limits of confidentiality. While you will maintain client privacy, there are exceptions, such as legal requirements to report abuse or threats of harm.
  • Right to Withdraw: Clearly state that clients can withdraw from your services at any time without penalty.
  • Documentation: Obtain written consent from your clients, documenting that they understand and agree to the terms of service.

Example of consent form language: "I understand that is a Certified Sexual Wellness Practitionerâ„¢ and not a medical doctor or licensed therapist. I understand that the services provided are for educational and coaching purposes only and are not intended to diagnose or treat any medical or psychological condition."

Legal Considerations for Online Services

Offering online sexual wellness services presents unique legal challenges. Be aware of the following:

  • Telehealth Regulations: Understand the telehealth laws in your jurisdiction and the jurisdictions where your clients reside. Some states have specific requirements for online counseling and coaching.
  • Cross-Jurisdictional Practice: If you have clients in different states or countries, you may need to comply with the laws of those jurisdictions. This can be complex and may require legal consultation.
  • Data Security: Implement robust data security measures to protect client information transmitted and stored online. Use secure platforms, encrypt data, and comply with data privacy regulations. According to Verizon's 2023 Data Breach Investigations Report, 82% of breaches involved a human element, emphasizing the importance of employee training and awareness.

Client: Maria S., 38

Presenting Symptoms: Maria, a Certified Sexual Wellness Practitionerâ„¢, launched an online program promising "guaranteed orgasms in 30 days." Her marketing materials included testimonials from satisfied clients but lacked disclaimers about individual results varying.

Intervention: A potential client flagged Maria's advertisement with the FTC for making unsubstantiated claims. AccrediPro Legal Counsel advised Maria to immediately remove the "guaranteed" language and include a prominent disclaimer stating that results may vary and are not guaranteed. Outcomes: Maria avoided legal action from the FTC. Case Study: David and the Scope of Practice Violation

Client: David L., 45
