Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's about prioritizing what's important. How much time are you currently spending feeling unhappy or disconnected? This program requires [time commitment], but the long-term benefits of feeling confident and connected can save you time and energy in other areas of your life."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. However, my program is tailored to your specific needs and challenges. Plus, I offer ongoing support and guidance throughout the process. I also have testimonials from women just like you who have seen amazing results. Would you like to see some of those?"
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a payment plan to make it more accessible. We can break it down into [number] monthly installments of [amount]. Does that work better for you?"
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? Let's address those concerns now so you can make an informed decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it"?

Reveal Answer
Ask clarifying questions to understand the specific concerns driving the hesitation (e.g., investment, time commitment) and address them directly.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process increases your chances of converting leads into paying clients.
  • Building rapport and actively listening are crucial for understanding your potential client's needs.
  • Clearly articulate your unique approach and the specific benefits clients can expect.
  • Prepare for common objections and have confident responses ready.
  • Practice presenting your pricing with conviction and focus on the value you provide.
