Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Considerations: A Step-by-Step Approach

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
The primary ethical dilemma revolves around conflicting values: Eleanor‚Äôs autonomy to explore her sexuality versus her commitment to her marriage and her husband's discomfort. There's also the issue of potential impact of cannabis use on her sexual health and decision-making.
Step 2: Apply Ethical Principles
Consider the principles of autonomy (respecting Eleanor's right to self-determination), beneficence (acting in her best interest), non-maleficence (avoiding harm), and justice (ensuring equitable access to care). How do these principles intersect and potentially conflict in Eleanor's case?
Step 3: Consider Legal and Professional Guidelines
Review your professional code of ethics regarding confidentiality, scope of practice, and dual relationships. Are there any legal implications related to her cannabis use in your state? Document everything thoroughly.
Step 4: Explore Potential Courses of Action
Possible actions include: individual therapy focusing on Eleanor's sexual desires and communication skills; couples therapy (if husband is willing); referral to a BDSM-aware therapist; providing education on responsible cannabis use; and open, honest communication about the limitations of your role as a sexual wellness practitioner.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Influencing Eleanor‚Äôs Experience?

1

Hormonal Changes

At 48, perimenopause could be significantly impacting her libido and vaginal dryness. Assess hormone levels and consider hormone therapy options in collaboration with her physician.

2

Medication Side Effects

SSRIs (like escitalopram) and oral contraceptives are known to decrease libido and cause sexual dysfunction. Discuss potential alternatives with her prescribing physician.

3

Underlying Trauma

Explore Eleanor's history for any potential past trauma that may be influencing her current sexual experiences and feelings of shame. If trauma is suspected, refer to a qualified trauma therapist.

4

Relationship Dynamics

Assess the overall health and communication patterns in her marriage. The BDSM interests may be a symptom of deeper relationship issues. Encourage couples counseling.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äì When to Seek External Expertise

  • Suspected substance abuse or dependency: Refer to a substance abuse specialist for assessment and treatment.
  • Severe marital distress or risk of domestic violence: Immediate referral to a qualified couples therapist or domestic violence hotline.
  • Signs of a mental health crisis (suicidal ideation, psychosis): Immediate referral to a psychiatrist or mental health crisis center.
  • Medical conditions requiring diagnosis or treatment: Refer back to her primary care physician or gynecologist for hormone testing, pain management, or other medical interventions.
  • Ethical concerns beyond your scope of competence: Consult with a seasoned colleague or ethics expert.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas in sexual wellness practice often involve balancing client autonomy with relationship dynamics and potential risks.
  • A thorough assessment of medical, psychological, and relational factors is crucial for ethical decision-making.
  • Referral to other professionals (therapists, physicians, specialists) is essential when issues fall outside your scope of practice.
  • Documentation is key to demonstrating ethical and responsible care.
  • Continuous education and consultation with peers are vital for navigating complex ethical situations.
