Introduction to Mindfulness-Based Sex Therapy (MBST)

Welcome back! In the previous lesson, we explored the power of hypnosis. Now, we shift our focus to another transformative approach: Mindfulness-Based Sex Therapy (MBST). As a Certified Sexual Wellness Practitionerâ„¢, you're building a diverse toolkit to help your clients achieve fulfilling and joyful sexual experiences. MBST offers a unique pathway by focusing on present moment awareness and acceptance.

MBST builds upon the foundational mindfulness practices you learned earlier in the course, expanding them to specifically address sexual concerns. It's about cultivating a non-judgmental awareness of sensations, thoughts, and emotions that arise during sexual activity. This approach can be particularly powerful for women in their 40s and 50s, who may be navigating hormonal changes, relationship shifts, or evolving desires. Many women find this approach empowering.

MBST Protocols

MBST protocols are designed to address a range of sexual issues, including:

  • Sexual desire and arousal difficulties
  • Pain during intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Difficulty reaching orgasm
  • Anxiety related to sexual performance

These protocols typically involve a combination of mindfulness exercises, psychoeducation about sexuality, and cognitive restructuring techniques. For example, a client struggling with low desire might be guided through mindfulness exercises to reconnect with bodily sensations and explore their desires without judgment. This aligns perfectly with the Unleash Desires component of the PLEASURE Frameworkâ„¢.

Advanced Mindfulness Exercises

While basic mindfulness practices involve focusing on the breath or bodily sensations, advanced exercises for MBST delve deeper. These may include:

  • Sensate focus exercises: Focusing on physical sensations without the pressure of performance.
  • Mindful masturbation: Exploring one's own body with curiosity and acceptance.
  • Body scan meditations: Cultivating awareness of different parts of the body.
  • Mindful communication exercises: Practicing open and honest communication with a partner about sexual needs and desires.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Sexual Medicine found that women who practiced mindful masturbation reported significant increases in sexual desire and satisfaction. The study involved 68 women aged 40-55, demonstrating the effectiveness of these techniques for this demographic.

Mindfulness for Pain and Sensory Enhancement

Mindfulness can be a powerful tool for managing pain during sex. By focusing on the present moment and accepting sensations without judgment, clientscan learn to reduce their anxiety and tension, which can exacerbate pain. Furthermore, mindfulness can enhance sensory experiences by increasing awareness of pleasurable sensations.

A randomized controlled trial published in Pain in 2019 showed that mindfulness-based interventions significantly reduced pain intensity and improved sexual function in women with vulvodynia. The study reported a 30% reduction in pain scores among participants who completed an 8-week MBST program.

Integrating MBST and Cognitive Restructuring

MBST is most effective when combined with cognitive restructuring techniques. Many individuals hold negative beliefs about sex, their bodies, or their sexual performance. These beliefs can create anxiety and interfere with sexual pleasure. Cognitive restructuring involves identifying and challenging these negative thoughts, replacing them with more positive and realistic ones.

For instance, a client who believes "I'm too old to enjoy sex" can be guided to examine the evidence for and against this belief. They might be encouraged to consider examples of older adults who enjoy fulfilling sex lives or to explore new ways of experiencing pleasure that are appropriate for their age and physical condition. This is directly relevant to the Reframe Beliefs component of the PLEASURE Frameworkâ„¢.

Case Study: Sarah, 48

Client: Sarah, a 48-year-old woman, presented with decreased sexual desire and pain during intercourse following menopause.

Intervention: An 8-week MBST program was implemented, incorporating mindfulness exercises (body scans, mindful masturbation), cognitive restructuring to address negative beliefs about aging and sexuality, and sensate focus exercises with her partner.

Outcomes: Sarah reported a significant increase in sexual desire, a reduction in pain during intercourse, and improved communication with her partner about her sexual needs. She also reported feeling more comfortable and accepting of her body.

Case Study: Emily, 52

Client: Emily, a 52-year-old woman, reported anxiety and difficulty reaching orgasm.

Intervention: Emily engaged in a 6-week MBST program with a focus on mindful breathing exercises, sensate focus, and cognitive restructuring to challenge negative thoughts about her ability to experience pleasure. Homework included daily mindfulness practice and journaling.

Outcomes: Emily reported reduced anxiety, increased ability to focus on sensations during sex, and improved ability to reach orgasm. She also expressed feeling more connected to her body and her partner.
