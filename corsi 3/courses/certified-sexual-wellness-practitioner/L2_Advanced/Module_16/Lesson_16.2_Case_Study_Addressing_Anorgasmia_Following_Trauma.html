Presenting Case: Emily

Emily, a 29-year-old woman, presents with secondary anorgasmia. She reports experiencing satisfying orgasms prior to a sexual trauma two years ago. Since the trauma, she has been unable to reach orgasm, despite wanting to. This has significantly impacted her self-esteem and relationship satisfaction. She feels disconnected from her body and experiences anxiety surrounding sexual activity.

Case Study: Emily's Journey

Reclaiming Pleasure After Trauma

Emilyâ€™s case highlights the complex interplay between trauma and sexual functioning. Her experience is not uncommon; studies show that up to 68% of women who experience sexual trauma report subsequent sexual dysfunction (Briere & Spinazzola, 2000). This dysfunction can manifest as decreased desire, arousal difficulties, pain, or, as in Emily's case, anorgasmia.

During the initial assessment, Emily reported feeling shame and guilt surrounding her inability to orgasm. She feared disappointing her partner and expressed concerns about her desirability. Her trauma history included details of unwanted sexual contact, feelings of powerlessness, and subsequent avoidance of intimacy. She also reported symptoms of anxiety and depression, for which she was receiving therapy.

Applying the 'L' (Lived Experience)

The 'Lived Experience' component of the P.L.E.A.S.U.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ emphasizes understanding the client's unique history, beliefs, and values related to sexuality. In Emily's case, this involved a trauma-informed approach to gathering information about the sexual assault and its aftermath. It was crucial to create a safe and supportive environment where Emily felt comfortable sharing her story without judgment.

Key questions explored during this stage included:

  • Can you describe your sexual experiences before the trauma? What did you enjoy? What felt good?
  • How has the trauma impacted your relationship with your body and your sexuality?
  • What are your current beliefs and feelings about sex and intimacy?
  • What are your goals for your sexual well-being?

Understanding Emily's pre-trauma sexual experiences helped identify potential pathways to pleasure that could be revisited. Exploring her beliefs and feelings revealed internalized shame and negative self-perceptions that were contributing to her anorgasmia. Identifying her goals provided a roadmap for the intervention process.

Incorporating 'S' and 'A'

The 'Sensory Exploration' (S) and 'Attunement & Alignment' (A) components are essential for addressing trauma-related anorgasmia. 'Sensory Exploration' involves guiding the client to reconnect with their body through mindful awareness of sensations. 'Attunement & Alignment' focuses on fostering a sense of safety, trust, and connection with oneself and, if applicable, with a partner.

In Emily's case, 'Sensory Exploration' began with simple exercises, such as:

  • Mindful breathing exercises to reduce anxiety and increase body awareness.
  • Gentle touch exercises using different textures to explore sensations without pressure for arousal.
  • Guided imagery to visualize safe and pleasurable experiences.

'Attunement & Alignment' involved:

  • Establishing clear boundaries and consent protocols during sessions.
  • Validating Emily's feelings and experiences without minimizing her trauma.
  • Helping Emily identify and challenge negative self-talk related to her sexuality.

These exercises helped Emily gradually reconnect with her body and reclaim a sense of agency over her sexual experiences. A 2018 study in the Journal of Sex & Marital Therapy found that mindfulness-based interventions significantly improved sexual function and reduced distress in women with a history of sexual trauma (Goldman et al., 2018).

Intervention Strategies

Based on Emily's assessment and her responses to 'Sensory Exploration' and 'Attunement & Alignment' exercises, several intervention strategies were implemented:

  1. Trauma-Informed Sex Therapy: Addressing the underlying trauma through therapy was crucial. Working in collaboration with Emily's therapist ensured a coordinated approach to her healing.
  2. Sensate Focus Exercises: Modified sensate focus exercises were introduced, focusing on non-genital touch and gradual exploration of erogenous zones. The emphasis was on pleasure and connection, not necessarily orgasm.
  3. Self-Pleasuring Techniques: Emily was encouraged to explore self-pleasuring in a safe and comfortable environment. This allowed her to regain control over her body and discover what felt good without the pressure of performance.
  4. Communication Skills Training: If applicable, working with Emily and her partner to improve communication about sexual needs and boundaries. This helped foster a more supportive and understanding relationship.
  5. Pelvic Floor Therapy: Addressing any pelvic floor dysfunction that may be contributing to anorgasmia. A pelvic floor therapist can assess and treat muscle tension or weakness that can interfere with sexual function.

Potential Challenges

Several challenges may arise during the intervention process:

  • Triggers: Unexpected reminders of the trauma can lead to flashbacks, anxiety, and avoidance. It's essential to develop strategies for managing triggers, such as grounding techniques and safe words.
  • Co-Occurring Mental Health Conditions: Anxiety, depression, and PTSD can complicate the healing process. Close collaboration with a mental health professional is crucial.
  • Relationship Dynamics: Partner-related issues, such as lack of understanding or support, can hinder progress. Couple's therapy may be beneficial.
  • Patience and Persistence: Reclaiming sexual well-being after trauma is a process that takes time and effort. It's important to manage expectations and celebrate small victories.
