Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on sensory awareness. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're craving a deeper connection to yourself, and that's a beautiful goal. Many women experience a similar disconnect, especially after significant life changes. We're going to explore ways to gently awaken your senses and bring you back into your body."
MARIA:
"I'm a little nervous. It feels silly to focus on things like 'senses' when I have so many real-world problems."
YOU:
"I understand. And I want to assure you, this isn't about escaping your problems. It's about building a stronger foundation of self-awareness and pleasure, so you can navigate those problems with more resilience and joy. Think of it as self-care that actually works."
YOU:
"I'd like us to start with a simple exercise: I'm going to guide you through a sensory grounding activity. It's designed to bring you into the present moment. Are you comfortable with that?"
MARIA:
"Okay, I'm willing to try."
YOU:
"Great. To start, could you close your eyes, and I will guide you through a breathing exercise. As you breath, notice any scents in the room. What do you smell?"

Key Takeaways

  • Sensory exploration can be a powerful tool for women experiencing emotional numbness or disconnection.
  • It's crucial to validate the client's feelings and create a safe, supportive environment.
  • Start with gentle, accessible exercises and avoid pushing the client beyond her comfort zone.
  • Connect sensory practices to the client's overall goals and values.
  • Emphasize that sensory exploration is a form of self-care that can enhance resilience and well-being.
