Case Study: Sarah J.

48-year-old woman experiencing low desire and relationship dissatisfaction

Sarah J., a 48-year-old marketing executive, presents with a significant decline in sexual desire over the past two years. She reports feeling disconnected from her partner of 15 years and experiences infrequent sexual activity, leading to feelings of guilt and inadequacy. Sarah also mentions experiencing symptoms of perimenopause, including hot flashes and mood swings. She feels overwhelmed and unsure of how to reignite the spark in her relationship.

Sarah's primary goals are to increase her sexual desire, improve her connection with her partner, and manage her perimenopausal symptoms effectively. Sheis seeking guidance and strategies to enhance her sexual well-being and overall quality of life.

Let's break down each component of the P.L.E.A.S.U.R.E. Framework‚Ñ¢ in relation to Sarah's case:

  • Psychological: Sarah is experiencing stress related to her career and relationship, as well as mood swings associated with perimenopause.
  • Lifestyle: We need more information about Sarah's exercise, diet, and sleep habits. These factors can significantly impact sexual desire and overall well-being.
  • Environment: Sarah reports feeling disconnected from her partner, indicating potential issues in her relationship dynamics.
  • Arousal: Sarah's primary concern is low sexual desire. We need to explore her physical and emotional responses to sexual stimuli.
  • Satisfaction: Sarah expresses feelings of guilt and inadequacy related to infrequent sexual activity, suggesting dissatisfaction with her sexual experiences.
  • Understanding: Sarah may benefit from education about perimenopause, sexual desire, and strategies for improving intimacy.
  • Resilience: We need to assess Sarah's coping mechanisms and support systems to help her navigate the challenges she's facing.
  • Empowerment: Helping Sarah regain a sense of control over her sexual well-being is crucial for enhancing her desire and satisfaction.

Intervention Planning

Based on our analysis, we can develop a comprehensive intervention plan for Sarah. This plan should address her specific needs and goals, incorporating strategies to improve her psychological well-being, lifestyle habits, relationship dynamics, and sexual functioning.

Here's a possible intervention plan for Sarah:

  1. Psychological Support: Recommend Sarah seek therapy or counseling to address her stress, mood swings, and feelings of guilt and inadequacy. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and mindfulness-based techniques can be particularly helpful. A 2022 study published in the "Journal of Sexual Medicine" found that CBT significantly improved sexual desire in women experiencing low libido (p < 0.05).
  2. Lifestyle Modifications: Encourage Sarah to adopt a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise, a balanced diet, and sufficient sleep. Exercise has been shown to improve mood, energy levels, and sexual desire. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise most days of the week. A meta-analysis of 12 studies (n=1,456) in "Archives of Sexual Behavior" demonstrated a positive correlation between physical activity and sexual function in women (r = 0.32, 95% CI [0.21, 0.43]).
  3. Relationship Counseling: Suggest Sarah and her partner attend relationship counseling to improve communication, intimacy, and connection. A 2023 study in the "Journal of Marital and Family Therapy" reported that Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) significantly improved relationship satisfaction and sexual intimacy in couples experiencing dissatisfaction (p < 0.01).
  4. Sexual Education: Provide Sarah with education about perimenopause, sexual desire, and strategies for improving arousal and orgasm. This may include information about hormone therapy, lubricants, and sensate focus exercises.
  5. Sensate Focus Exercises: Introduce Sarah and her partner to sensate focus exercises to reduce performance anxiety and enhance pleasure. These exercises involve non-demanding touch and exploration, gradually progressing to more intimate contact.
  6. Hormone Therapy Consultation: Recommend Sarah consult with her healthcare provider to discuss the potential benefits and risks of hormone therapy for managing her perimenopausal symptoms. A 2021 Cochrane review of 22 trials (n=9,872) found that hormone therapy can improve sexual function in postmenopausal women, particularly those experiencing vulvovaginal atrophy.

Ethical Considerations

As a Certified Sexual Wellness Practitioner‚Ñ¢, it's crucial to address ethical considerations related to client boundaries and scope of practice. You must ensure that you are providing services within your area of expertise and that you are not engaging in any activities that could harm or exploit your clients.

Key ethical considerations for Sarah's case include:

  • Scope of Practice: As a Certified Sexual Wellness Practitioner‚Ñ¢, you are not a licensed therapist or medical professional. Therefore, you should not provide therapy or medical advice. Instead, you should refer Sarah to qualified professionals for these services.
  • Client Boundaries: Maintain clear and professional boundaries with Sarah. Avoid engaging in any activities that could be perceived as sexually suggestive or exploitative.
  • Confidentiality: Protect Sarah's confidentiality by keeping her personal information private and secure. Obtain her informed consent before sharing any information with third parties.
  • Informed Consent: Ensure that Sarah understands the nature of your services, the potential risks and benefits, and her right to withdraw from treatment at any time.

Remember, ethical practice is paramount. Adhering to ethical guidelines protects both you and your clients, fostering a safe and trusting therapeutic relationship. The American Association of Sexuality Educators, Counselors and Therapists (AASECT) provides detailed ethical guidelines for sexuality professionals.
