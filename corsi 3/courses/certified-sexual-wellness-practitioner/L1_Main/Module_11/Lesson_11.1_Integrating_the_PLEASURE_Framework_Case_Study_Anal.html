Case Study: Sarah, 48

Client: Sarah, 48 years old

Sarah is a 48-year-old woman who has been married for 20 years. She presents with a significant decrease in sexual desire over the past several years. She reports feeling disconnected from her body and experiences pain during intercourse. Sarah also shares that she has a history of childhood emotional neglect and struggles with feelings of shame and inadequacy.

Presenting Symptoms:

  • Low sexual desire
  • Pain during intercourse (dyspareunia)
  • Feelings of disconnection from her body
  • History of childhood emotional neglect
  • Feelings of shame and inadequacy

Relationship Status: Married for 20 years; reports some communication difficulties with her husband regarding sexual needs and desires.

Medical History: No significant medical conditions reported, but she is currently perimenopausal.

Lifestyle: Works full-time in a stressful job; reports limited time for self-care and relaxation.

Applying the PLEASURE Frameworkâ„¢ to Sarah's Case

Let's apply the PLEASURE Frameworkâ„¢ to Sarahâ€™s case to identify key areas of focus:

  • P (Personal Profile): Sarah values her marriage but feels disconnected. Her beliefs about sex may be influenced by her upbringing and societal expectations.
  • L (Lived Experience): Childhood emotional neglect likely contributes to her feelings of shame and disconnection from her body. This could manifest as difficulty experiencing pleasure or setting boundaries.
  • E (Education & Empowerment): Sarah may benefit from education about female sexual anatomy, physiology, and the impact of perimenopause on sexual function. Empowerment comes from helping her understand her rights to pleasure and set boundaries.
  • A (Attunement & Alignment): Sarah struggles to connect with her body. Mindfulness practices and somatic experiencing techniques could help her increase body awareness and emotional regulation.
  • S (Sensory Exploration): Given the pain during intercourse, non-genital touch and exploration of other erogenous zones could be beneficial.
  • U (Unleash Desires): Sarah needs a safe space to explore her desires without judgment. This involves identifying what brings her pleasure and learning to communicate those desires.
  • R (Relationship Enhancement): Improving communication with her husband is crucial. Couples therapy or communication exercises could help them address their sexual needs and desires.
  • E (Embodiment & Evolution): Integrating sexual well-being into Sarah's life involves prioritizing self-care, setting boundaries, and developing a positive body image.

Tailored Interventions for Sarah

Based on the PLEASURE Frameworkâ„¢ analysis, here are some potential interventions for Sarah:

  1. Individual Therapy: To address the impact of childhood emotional neglect and feelings of shame. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) or Trauma-Informed Therapy may be helpful.   5. Communication Skills Training: To improve communication with her husband regarding sexual needs and desires.
  6. Education about Perimenopause: Providing information about the hormonal changes that can affect sexual function and strategies for managing symptoms.
  7. Self-Care Strategies: Encouraging Sarah to prioritize self-care activities such as exercise, healthy eating, and relaxation techniques.

Challenges and Ethical Considerations

When working with clients like Sarah, it's important to be aware of potential challenges and ethical considerations:

  • Trauma: Sarah's history of childhood emotional neglect requires a trauma-informed approach. Avoid pushing her to disclose information before she is ready and be mindful of potential triggers.
  • Desire Discrepancy: Differences in sexual desire between Sarah and her husband can create tension in the relationship. It's important to help them communicate their needs and expectations openly and honestly.
  • Cultural and Religious Beliefs: Sarah's cultural and religious beliefs may influence her views on sexuality. Respect her values and work within her belief system.
  • Informed Consent: Ensure that Sarah understands the nature of the interventions and provides informed consent before proceeding.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain confidentiality and respect Sarah's privacy.
  • Scope of Practice: Be aware of your scope of practice and refer Sarah to other professionals (e.g., therapist, medical doctor) when necessary.

Ongoing Assessment and Adaptation

The intervention plan for Sarah should be flexible and adapted based on her progress and feedback. Regular assessment is crucial to ensure that the interventions are effective and meeting her needs.

Here are some ways to assess Sarah's progress:

  • Regular Check-ins: Schedule regular check-ins with Sarah to discuss her progress and any challenges she is experiencing.
  • Questionnaires: Use standardized questionnaires to assess her sexual function, mood, and quality of life.
  • Feedback: Encourage Sarah to provide feedback on the interventions and make adjustments as needed.

Remember, the goal is to empower Sarah to take control of her sexual well-being and create a fulfilling and satisfying life.
