Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It's incredibly common to experience a shift in desire, especially after major life changes. I want to assure you that feeling disconnected doesn't mean anything is 'wrong' with you. Let's explore what might be blocking that spark and how we can reignite it."
MARIA:
"I'm glad to hear that. I was starting to think I was just getting old and sex wasn't for me anymore."
YOU:
"Absolutely not. Desire evolves, and it's often influenced by factors we don't even realize. Let's start by exploring your erotic blueprint. Have you ever heard of that concept?"
MARIA:
"No, I haven't."
YOU:
"Think of it as your unique map to pleasure and arousal. It's understanding what truly turns you on, beyond societal expectations or what you *think* you should like. It includes your senses, your fantasies, your emotional needs... Does that sound interesting to explore?"
MARIA:
"Definitely. I'm willing to try anything at this point."
YOU:
"Great. We'll start with a few questions and exercises to uncover your personal erotic blueprint. This isn't about performance; it's about rediscovering your pleasure. Ready?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings about decreased desire before suggesting solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport. It assures the client that their experience is normal and that you understand their concerns, making them more receptive to exploring solutions.

2. What are some non-physical factors that can influence a person's desire?

Non-physical factors include emotional connection with a partner, stress levels, past trauma, body image, cultural or religious beliefs, and overall mental health.
