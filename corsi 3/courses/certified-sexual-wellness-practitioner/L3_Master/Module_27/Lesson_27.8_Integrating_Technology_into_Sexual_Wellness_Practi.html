The Case Sarah Presents

Complex Client Case

Sarah's Summary:

"I have a client, Emily, 52, experiencing painful intercourse after menopause. She's tried over-the-counter lubricants with no relief and is now hesitant to engage sexually. She also mentioned a history of childhood trauma, but I'm unsure how much to explore that."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Has Sarah considered differential diagnoses for painful intercourse beyond lubrication issues?
  • Is Sarah equipped to handle the trauma history, or is a referral necessary?
  • Did Sarah discuss the client's expectations and goals for therapy?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Differential Diagnosis

Emphasize the importance of considering various causes of dyspareunia, such as hormonal imbalances, pelvic floor dysfunction, or vulvodynia. Encourage Sarah to refer Emily to a physician for a thorough medical evaluation.

2

Address Trauma Sensitivity

Discuss the ethical considerations of exploring trauma history. Advise Sarah to assess her own competence and comfort level. If Sarah is not trained in trauma-informed care, a referral to a specialized therapist is crucial.

3

Set Realistic Expectations

Guide Sarah on discussing realistic expectations with Emily. Remind her that progress can be gradual and that consistent communication is key. Help Sarah articulate a clear treatment plan with achievable goals.

4

Empower Clinical Judgment

Instead of providing direct answers, encourage Sarah to develop her clinical reasoning skills. Ask probing questions like, "What are the potential risks and benefits of exploring the trauma history at this stage?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Sarah's Concerns
"Sarah, I appreciate you bringing this case to me. It's great that you're recognizing the complexities involved. This shows a commitment to your clients' well-being."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before we dive in, what are your initial thoughts about this case? What aspects are making you feel unsure?"
Provide Guidance and Resources
"It's wise to consider a possible medical cause for Emily's pain. I can provide you with resources for pelvic pain specialists in your area. Additionally, since trauma is involved, here are some therapists specializing in trauma-informed care."
Instill Confidence
"Remember, Sarah, you have the skills and knowledge to support Emily. Focus on providing a safe space, validating her experiences, and collaborating with other professionals when needed."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address the mentee's feelings of uncertainty before addressing the client case?

Addressing the mentee's feelings first creates a safe and trusting environment, allowing them to be more receptive to feedback and guidance. It also validates their experience and reduces feelings of inadequacy.

2. What are some strategies for addressing potential scope of practice concerns with a mentee?

Encourage self-assessment, provide clear guidelines on scope of practice, discuss the importance of referrals, and share resources for further training and education.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize creating a safe and supportive supervision environment.
  • Encourage mentees to develop their clinical reasoning skills through probing questions.
  • Address scope of practice concerns proactively and ethically.
  • Celebrate mentees' successes and instill confidence in their abilities.
  • Remember that effective supervision contributes to the growth and development of the entire field.
