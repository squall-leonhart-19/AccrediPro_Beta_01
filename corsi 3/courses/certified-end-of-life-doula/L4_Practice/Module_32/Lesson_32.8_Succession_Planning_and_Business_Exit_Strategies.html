Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Compassionate Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Empathy 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. I understand this must be a difficult time. I'm so sorry for your loss. How are you holding up today?"
YOU:
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed. Many women in similar situations feel the same way. What's been the hardest part for you so far?"
Phase 2: Understanding Her Needs 10 min
YOU:
"What kind of support are you looking for right now? What would make things easier for you in the coming weeks?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest fears or concerns about the future? What keeps you up at night?"
YOU:
"If you could design the ideal support system for yourself, what would it look like? What would it include?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Services 8 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're craving connection, guidance, and a sense of control. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"As an End-of-Life Doula, I offer personalized support to help you navigate this transition. I can provide emotional support, practical assistance with planning, and guidance as you redefine your life's purpose. I work with clients over [duration] to provide stability and create a plan that feels right for *you*."
Phase 4: Gentle Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been hoping for? I'm here to help you find your way forward."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Compassionate Responses

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Let's talk about your budget and see if we can find a payment plan that works for you. I also offer a sliding scale for clients in need. My priority is ensuring you get the support you deserve."
"I don't want to be a burden."
"Susan, seeking support is a sign of strength, not weakness. You deserve to have someone in your corner during this difficult time. I'm here to lighten your load, not add to it."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. This is a big decision. What are the key things you need to consider? Perhaps I can provide some information to help you feel more confident in your choice."
"I'm not sure I'm ready."
"That's perfectly okay. There's no rush. I'm here when you are ready. Would you like me to check in with you in a week or two to see how you're feeling?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a compassionate discovery call with a grieving client?

Empathy, active listening, understanding their needs, presenting your services as a solution, and a gentle close. Focus on building trust and rapport.

2. How can you address a client's budget concerns without compromising your value?

Offer flexible payment plans, sliding scale options, or package discounts. Focus on the long-term value and benefits of your services, such as improved quality of life and reduced stress.

Key Takeaways

  • Client acquisition is about building genuine connections and understanding your client's needs.
  • Empathy and compassion are crucial when working with vulnerable clients.
  • Address objections with understanding and offer flexible solutions.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and highlight the value you provide.
  • Believe in your ability to transform lives and build a thriving practice.
