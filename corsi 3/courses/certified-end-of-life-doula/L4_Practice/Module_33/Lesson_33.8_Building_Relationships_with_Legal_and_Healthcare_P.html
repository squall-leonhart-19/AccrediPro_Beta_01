Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Introduction 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Eleanor, and [daughter's name], thank you for reaching out. It's lovely to speak with you both. I understand you saw my presentation at the senior center, [daughter's name]. What resonated with you enough to schedule this call?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, I want you to know that this is a safe space to share whatever is on your mind. How are you feeling about everything right now?"
Phase 2: Understand Needs & Concerns 10 min
YOU:
"What are some of your biggest concerns or fears about this stage of your life?"
YOU:
"What does a 'good' end-of-life experience look like to you, Eleanor? What is most important for you and your family?"
YOU:
"What kind of support are you hoping to receive during this time? Are there any specific needs you have?"
Phase 3: Present Your Services 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor and [daughter's name], based on what you've shared, I believe my end-of-life doula services can provide significant comfort and support. I can help you navigate the emotional, spiritual, and practical aspects of this journey."
YOU:
"Specifically, I can assist with creating a personalized end-of-life plan, facilitating meaningful conversations with loved ones, providing emotional and spiritual support, and connecting you with valuable resources in the community."
YOU:
"My approach focuses on empowering you, Eleanor, to make informed decisions and live each day with purpose and dignity. I work alongside your medical team to ensure your wishes are honored."
Phase 4: Address Concerns & Close 5 min
YOU:
"What questions do you have for me about my services or how I can support you?"
YOU:
"If you feel this is the right fit, we can discuss the details of my packages and how we can move forward. Are you ready to explore those options?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"We're not sure if we can afford it."
"I understand that budget is a concern. I offer flexible payment options and can tailor a package to fit your specific needs and financial situation. We can focus on the most essential services to start."
"We need to think about it and discuss it as a family."
"That's perfectly understandable. I can provide you with a detailed proposal outlining my services and fees. Would it be helpful to schedule a follow-up call with the entire family to answer any questions?"
"We're already working with hospice. Is this really necessary?"
"That's great that you have hospice support. My services complement hospice care by providing additional emotional, spiritual, and practical support. I can focus on the holistic needs of Eleanor and her family, ensuring a more personalized and meaningful experience."
"We're not sure if Eleanor is ready to talk about this."
"I understand that this is a sensitive topic. I approach these conversations with gentleness, compassion, and respect for Eleanor's wishes. I can adapt my approach to meet her where she is and create a safe space for her to explore her feelings and preferences."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key areas of support an end-of-life doula can provide to clients and their families?

Emotional support, spiritual guidance, and practical assistance with end-of-life planning.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your services?

Offer flexible payment options, tailor a package to fit their budget, and highlight the value and benefits of your services.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and understanding with potential clients.
  • Actively listen to the client's needs and concerns to tailor your services effectively.
  • Confidently present your pricing and address objections with empathy and understanding.
  • Highlight the unique value and benefits of your end-of-life doula services.
  • Practice your sales skills regularly to improve your client acquisition rate.
