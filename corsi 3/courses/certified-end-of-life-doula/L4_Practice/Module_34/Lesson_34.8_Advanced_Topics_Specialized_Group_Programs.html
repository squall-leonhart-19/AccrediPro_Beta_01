Workshop Promotion Email Script

Crafting Your Invitation

Phase 1: Subject Line 2 min
YOU:
"Finding Peace After Loss: A Grief Workshop for Healing" or "Navigating Grief Together: A Supportive Workshop"
Phase 2: Opening & Empathy 3 min
YOU:
"Dear Susan, I'm reaching out because I know this can be a particularly difficult time. Losing a loved one is never easy, and the journey through grief can feel overwhelming and isolating."
Phase 3: Introducing the Workshop 5 min
YOU:
"I'm hosting a special workshop, 'Navigating Grief,' designed to provide a safe and supportive space for you to explore your feelings, learn practical coping strategies, and connect with others who understand what you're going through. In this workshop, we'll cover [list 3 key benefits, e.g., understanding the stages of grief, practicing self-compassion, building resilience]."
Phase 4: Call to Action & Details 2 min
YOU:
"If you're ready to take a step toward healing and connect with a compassionate community, I invite you to join us. The workshop will be held on [Date] at [Time] at [Location/Online Platform]. The investment is [Price]. To learn more and register, click here: [Link]."

Addressing Concerns

Anticipating Questions & Providing Reassurance

"I'm not sure I'm ready to talk about it."
"That's perfectly okay. The workshop is designed to be a gentle and supportive space. You're welcome to participate at your own pace, and there's no pressure to share anything you're not comfortable with. Many attendees find comfort just being in the presence of others who understand."
"I'm worried it will be too expensive."
"I understand. I've priced the workshop to be accessible while still providing high-quality support and resources. Consider it an investment in your well-being and healing process. Think about the cost of staying stuck in grief ‚Äì emotionally, physically, and even financially. Plus, I offer a payment plan option if that helps."
"I'm afraid it will be too emotional."
"It's natural to feel apprehensive about that. The workshop is facilitated with sensitivity and care. We'll create a safe container for exploring emotions, and I'll provide grounding techniques and self-care strategies to help you manage any difficult feelings that may arise. My goal is to empower you, not overwhelm you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements of a compelling workshop promotion email?

Empathy, clear value proposition, and a strong call to action.

2. How can you address the concern that a workshop might be too emotional for a potential attendee?

Reassure them that the workshop will be facilitated with sensitivity, offer grounding techniques, and emphasize that participation is at their own pace.

Key Takeaways

  • Workshops provide a scalable way to serve more clients and increase your income.
  • Empathy and understanding are crucial in promoting workshops, especially those dealing with sensitive topics.
  • Addressing potential concerns proactively builds trust and encourages registration.
  • Highlighting the value and benefits of the workshop is essential in justifying the investment.
  • Consistent promotion and a strong call to action are key to filling your workshops.
