Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi [Daughter's Name], thank you for reaching out. It's lovely to connect after my presentation at the senior center. How are you doing today, given everything you're going through?"
YOU:
"I understand you're calling about your mother, Eleanor. Could you tell me a little more about her situation and what you're hoping for?"
Phase 2: Understand Needs & Concerns 10 min
YOU:
"What are your biggest concerns about your mother's comfort and well-being during this time? What are her wishes, as far as you know?"
YOU:
"Since you live out of state, what kind of support system does Eleanor currently have in place? What are her biggest fears or anxieties about the end of life?"
YOU:
"What's most important to you in terms of how Eleanor spends her final days? What kind of legacy do you both want to create?"
Phase 3: Present Your Services & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking peace of mind knowing Eleanor is comfortable, supported, and her wishes are honored. Is that right?"
YOU:
"As an End-of-Life Doula, I provide personalized support to Eleanor and your family. This includes creating a comfort plan, facilitating meaningful conversations, offering emotional and spiritual support, and ensuring her environment is peaceful and dignified. I can also coordinate with her medical team and other caregivers to ensure seamless care."
YOU:
"I offer different packages tailored to individual needs, from basic companionship to comprehensive end-of-life planning and bedside support. Would you like me to share some options?"
Phase 4: Answer Questions & Close 5 min
YOU:
"What questions do you have for me about my services or how I can support Eleanor and your family?"
YOU:
"Knowing what you know now, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Excellent. Let's discuss the next steps and how we can get started."

Handling Objections with Grace

Navigating Concerns & Hesitations

"I'm not sure if my mother would be open to this."
"That's a valid concern. I often find that gentle introduction and clear communication can ease any anxieties. Perhaps we could schedule a brief introductory call with Eleanor, where I can explain my role and answer her questions directly? This allows her to feel comfortable and in control."
"I'm worried about the cost. We're already facing so many medical expenses."
"I understand. Let's explore some options that fit your budget. I offer different packages with varying levels of support, and we can tailor a plan to meet your specific needs. Think of this as an investment in Eleanor's comfort and peace of mind, and in your own peace of mind knowing she's well cared for. Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Connect, Understand Needs & Concerns, Present Your Services & Value, Answer Questions & Close.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your services?

Acknowledge their concerns, explore different package options, emphasize the value and peace of mind your services provide, and frame it as an investment in their loved one's comfort and well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and trust is essential for a successful client relationship.
  • Thoroughly understanding the client's needs and concerns allows you to tailor your services effectively.
  • Confidently presenting your value and addressing objections is crucial for securing clients.
  • Tailor your services to fit the client's budget and needs.
  • Remember that you are providing a valuable service that can bring comfort and peace to families during a difficult time.
