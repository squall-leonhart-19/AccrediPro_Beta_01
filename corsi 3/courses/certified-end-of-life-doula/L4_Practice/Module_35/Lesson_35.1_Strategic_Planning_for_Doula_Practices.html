Defining Your Vision and Mission

Let's start by solidifying your vision and mission. Your vision is your aspirational view of the future, while your mission is how you plan to achieve that vision. For example:

  • Vision: To be the leading provider of compassionate end-of-life doula services in your community, ensuring every individual experiences a peaceful and dignified transition.
  • Mission: To provide personalized, holistic care to individuals and families facing end-of-life, grounded in the principles of Presence, Exploration, Action, Comfort, and Embrace (P.E.A.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢).

Revisit your initial vision from Module 0, Lesson 2. Has it evolved? Refine it to reflect your current aspirations and understanding of your market.

Conducting a SWOT Analysis

A SWOT analysis helps you understand your current business environment. It involves identifying your:

  • Strengths: What advantages do you have? (e.g., specialized training, strong community connections)
  • Weaknesses: What areas need improvement? (e.g., limited marketing experience, lack of administrative support)
  • Opportunities: What external factors can you leverage? (e.g., growing demand for end-of-life services, partnerships with hospices)
  • Threats: What external factors could harm your practice? (e.g., increased competition, changes in healthcare regulations)

Here's an example of a SWOT analysis for a doula practice:

Strengths Weaknesses Opportunities Threats
Specialized training in palliative care Limited marketing budget Partnerships with local hospices Increased competition from new doulas
Strong network of healthcare professionals Lack of administrative support Growing awareness of doula services Changes in healthcare regulations
Excellent client testimonials and referrals Dependence on word-of-mouth marketing Expansion into new service areas (e.g., grief support) Economic downturn affecting client affordability

Setting SMART Goals

SMART goals provide a clear roadmap for your practice's growth. Each goal should be:

  • Specific: Clearly defined (e.g., Increase client bookings)
  • Measurable: Quantifiable (e.g., Increase client bookings by 20%)
  • Achievable: Realistic and attainable
  • Relevant: Aligned with your vision and mission
  • Time-bound: With a specific deadline (e.g., Increase client bookings by 20% within the next 6 months)

Examples of SMART goals for an end-of-life doula practice:

  • Increase client bookings by 20% within the next 6 months through targeted online advertising.
  • Establish partnerships with at least three local hospices within the next year.
  • Increase website traffic by 30% within 3 months by optimizing SEO and content marketing.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) are metrics that help you track progress toward your SMART goals. They provide valuable insights into what's working and what needs adjustment. Common KPIs for a doula practice include:

  • Number of client bookings per month
  • Client acquisition cost
  • Client satisfaction scores
  • Website traffic and engagement
  • Referral rate

Regularly monitor your KPIs (e.g., monthly or quarterly) and compare them against your targets. Use this data to refine your strategies and make informed decisions.

Creating a Strategic Roadmap

A strategic roadmap is a visual representation of your growth plan, outlining key activities, timelines, and milestones. It provides a clear and actionable path toward achieving your vision. Your roadmap should include:

  • Specific actions (e.g., launching a new marketing campaign, attending industry events)
  • Timelines for each action (e.g., Q1 2024, Q2 2024)
  • Responsible parties (i.e., who is accountable for each action)
  • Key milestones (e.g., securing first hospice partnership, reaching target revenue)

Case Study: "Serene Passages Doula Services"

Client: Serene Passages Doula Services, a solo practitioner specializing in end-of-life care for cancer patients.

Presenting Challenge: Stagnant client bookings and revenue, despite positive client feedback.

Intervention: Conducted a SWOT analysis, revealing strengths in specialized training and weaknesses in marketing. Implemented SMART goals to increase client bookings by 15% in 3 months through targeted Facebook ads and partnerships with local oncology clinics.

Outcomes: Client bookings increased by 18% within 3 months. Established partnerships with two oncology clinics, leading to a 25% increase in referral rates. Improved overall business profitability.
