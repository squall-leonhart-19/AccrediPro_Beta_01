Her Situation
Mother recently diagnosed with terminal illness. Susan feels overwhelmed with caregiving responsibilities.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure what a doula costs, but I want the best care for my mom."
Decision Style
Emotional, values compassion, needs reassurance, and seeks practical solutions.

"To ensure my mother is comfortable, respected, and not alone during this difficult time."

Your Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Compassion 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. I understand you're going through a very difficult time. I'm so sorry to hear about your mother's diagnosis. How are you holding up?"
YOU:
"It takes courage to reach out for support. I'm here to listen. Tell me a little bit about what's been happening."
Phase 2: Understanding Needs & Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"What are your biggest concerns right now regarding your mother's care? What kind of support are you hoping to find?"
YOU:
"What does a 'good day' look like for your mom? What are her priorities and wishes at this stage?"
YOU:
"What are some of the challenges you're facing as her caregiver? What resources do you currently have in place?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Services & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for someone to provide compassionate support, practical assistance, and peace of mind during this challenging time. Is that right?"
YOU:
"As an end-of-life doula, I can offer a range of services to support both your mother and you. This includes creating a comfortable and peaceful environment, facilitating meaningful conversations, providing emotional support, and coordinating with other healthcare providers. My goal is to ensure your mother's wishes are honored and that you feel supported throughout this journey. Many families find that having a doula allows them to focus on spending quality time with their loved one, rather than being overwhelmed by logistical and emotional burdens."
Phase 4: Next Steps & Closing 5 min
YOU:
"Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for, Susan? ...Great! Let's discuss how we can move forward."

Addressing Objections

Common Concerns & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if we can afford a doula."
"I understand that cost is a concern. Let's talk about the value I bring. My services can actually *save* you money by preventing unnecessary hospital visits and ensuring your mother receives the right care in the right setting. Plus, the emotional support I provide can reduce stress and burnout for you, allowing you to be more present and effective as a caregiver. We can also explore flexible payment options to make this more manageable. What's most important to you in terms of your mother's comfort and care?"
"I'm worried my family won't understand what a doula does."
"That's a valid concern. I'm happy to explain my role to your family members and address any questions they may have. I often say that I'm there to support the *entire* family, not just the person nearing end-of-life. My goal is to work collaboratively with everyone to create a peaceful and supportive environment. Perhaps we could schedule a brief call with your family together?"
"I'm already working with hospice. Do I really need a doula too?"
"That's a great question. Hospice provides excellent medical care and symptom management. However, a doula offers a different kind of support ‚Äì focusing on emotional, spiritual, and practical needs that may not be fully addressed by hospice. I can complement hospice care by providing companionship, facilitating conversations, creating meaningful rituals, and offering respite for family caregivers. Think of me as an extra layer of support to ensure your mother's final days are as comfortable and fulfilling as possible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. This is a big decision. To help you in your decision-making, what are the key things you need to consider? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call?

Active listening, empathy, and genuine interest in the prospect's situation are crucial for building rapport.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your services?

Focus on the value you provide, potential cost savings, and offer flexible payment options. Highlight the long-term benefits and peace of mind your services offer.

Key Takeaways

  • Compassion and empathy are essential for connecting with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's needs and challenges is crucial for tailoring your services.
  • Confidently present your pricing and address objections with clear, value-driven responses.
  • Practice your call-to-action to confidently close the sale.
  • Realistic income goals are achievable with dedication and a strong client acquisition strategy.
