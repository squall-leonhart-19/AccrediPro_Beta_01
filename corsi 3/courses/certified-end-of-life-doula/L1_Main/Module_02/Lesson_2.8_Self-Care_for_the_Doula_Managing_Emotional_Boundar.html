Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open about what you're going through. It takes courage to admit when you feel lost, especially when you're facing something as daunting as this. Let's take a deep breath together..."
MARIA:
"Thank you. I just feel so clueless. Everyone keeps saying 'be strong,' but I don't even know what that *means* right now."
YOU:
"I understand. 'Being strong' doesn't mean having all the answers. It means being willing to learn and to ask for help. And that's exactly what you're doing. In our time together, we'll explore what resources are available, and I'll provide some education around the typical stages and decisions you might encounter. How does that sound as a starting point?"
MARIA:
"That sounds... manageable. Less overwhelming, at least. What kind of resources are you talking about?"
YOU:
"We can explore everything from local hospice organizations and palliative care options to grief counseling and legal support. I can also provide information about advance care planning, which includes things like living wills and durable power of attorney. Does any of that sound immediately relevant to you?"
MARIA:
"The hospice and palliative care... I've heard those terms, but I'm not really sure what they mean or when they're appropriate."
YOU:
"That's a great place to start. Let's begin with a brief overview of hospice and palliative care, and then we can discuss how they might apply to your mother's situation. Does that work for you?"

‚úì Speak in plain language that Maria can easily understand. Avoid overwhelming her with information.
‚ùå Minimizing her feelings or telling her to "stay positive"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Exploration & Education" phase in end-of-life doula work?

The primary goal is to help clients and their families understand their options, resources, and potential challenges, empowering them to make informed decisions.

2. Why is it important to avoid using technical jargon when educating a client about end-of-life care?

Technical jargon can be confusing and overwhelming, creating a barrier to understanding. Using plain language fosters trust and ensures the client can actively participate in the decision-making process.

Key Takeaways

  • The "Exploration & Education" phase is crucial for empowering clients facing end-of-life decisions.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for building trust and understanding the client's needs.
  • Providing clear, concise information in plain language reduces anxiety and promotes informed decision-making.
  • Remember to address both the practical and emotional aspects of end-of-life care.
  • Practice your coaching skills regularly to build confidence and competence.
