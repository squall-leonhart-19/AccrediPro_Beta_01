Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating feelings builds trust and rapport, making the client more receptive to guidance. It also ensures that solutions address the root of the issue, not just the symptoms.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client feels heard and understood?

Relaxed posture, eye contact, nodding, and a softening of facial expressions are all positive non-verbal cues.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize validation and empathy in your initial interactions.
  • Address the client's emotional well-being, not just practical concerns.
  • Help the client reframe negative thoughts and beliefs.
  • Encourage self-compassion and self-care.
  • Create a safe space for the client to explore their fears and uncertainties.
