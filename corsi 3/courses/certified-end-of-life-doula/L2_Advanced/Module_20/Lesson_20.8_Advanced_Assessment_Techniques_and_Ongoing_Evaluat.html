Clinical Reasoning Process

Working Through the Case

Step 1: Identify Primary Concerns
Eleanor presents with symptoms suggestive of depression, cognitive decline, and existential distress related to aging and loss of independence. The Mini-Cog score warrants further investigation.
Step 2: Assess Contributing Factors
Consider the impact of her medical history (stroke, macular degeneration, osteoarthritis), social isolation, and lack of formal advance care planning. Explore her fears and values regarding end-of-life care.
Step 3: Choose Assessment Tools
Administer the Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) to quantify depressive symptoms. Use the Edmonton Functional Assessment Tool to evaluate functional decline. Initiate Values Clarification exercises to understand her end-of-life wishes.
Step 4: Develop a Holistic Plan
Integrate findings from assessment tools to create a care plan addressing her emotional, cognitive, and spiritual needs. Collaborate with the assisted living staff, family (if available), and healthcare providers.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Dementia

Cognitive decline could indicate early-stage dementia (Alzheimer's or vascular). The Mini-Cog requires follow-up with a more comprehensive neuropsychological evaluation.

2

Medication Side Effects

Lorazepam, even at low doses, can contribute to cognitive impairment and depression in older adults. Review medication list with physician.

3

Sensory Impairment

Macular degeneration can exacerbate social isolation and depression. Ensure she has access to appropriate visual aids and support.

4

Unresolved Grief

Consider the impact of her husband's death and other losses. Explore opportunities for grief counseling or support groups.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key assessment tools to consider when working with a geriatric client presenting with depression and cognitive decline?

Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS), Mini-Cog (or other cognitive screening tool), Edmonton Functional Assessment Tool, and Values Clarification exercises are crucial. Consider also the Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA) for a more detailed cognitive evaluation.

2. What are the ethical considerations when facilitating advance care planning with a client who has mild cognitive impairment?

Ensure the client has decision-making capacity, provide clear and understandable information, and protect the client from undue influence. Document the process thoroughly and involve family members or other trusted individuals as appropriate.

Key Takeaways

  • Geriatric clients often present with complex, overlapping physical, emotional, and cognitive challenges.
  • Holistic assessment tools (e.g., GDS, Edmonton Functional Assessment Tool) are essential for understanding the client's needs and developing a comprehensive care plan.
  • Advance care planning is a critical component of end-of-life doula work with older adults, but it requires careful attention to ethical considerations and decision-making capacity.
  • Collaboration with healthcare providers, family members, and assisted living staff is crucial for providing optimal support to geriatric clients.
