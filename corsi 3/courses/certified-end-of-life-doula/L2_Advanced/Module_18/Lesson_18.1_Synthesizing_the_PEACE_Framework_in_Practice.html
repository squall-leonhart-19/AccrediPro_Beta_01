Review of the PEACE Framework

Let's revisit the core tenets of the PEACE Frameworkâ„¢. Remember, each element is interconnected, informing and influencing the others. This holistic approach ensures comprehensive and compassionate support for your clients and their families.

  1. Presence & Perspective: Being fully present with the client, actively listening, and understanding their unique perspective on mortality.
  2. Exploration & Education: Providing information about end-of-life options, addressing fears, and encouraging open conversations.
  3. Action & Advocacy: Facilitating advance care planning, connecting clients with resources, and advocating for their wishes.
  4. Comfort & Connection: Focusing on physical, emotional, and spiritual comfort, fostering meaningful connections, and reducing suffering.
  5. Embrace & Evolve: Embracing the dying process, adapting to evolving needs, and supporting emotional and spiritual closure.

Case Study: Eleanor, 72

Eleanor, a 72-year-old retired teacher, has been diagnosed with advanced pancreatic cancer. She lives alone and has limited family support. Eleanor is experiencing significant pain, anxiety, and spiritual distress. She expresses a desire to remain at home but is worried about managing her symptoms and feeling isolated.

PEACE Frameworkâ„¢ Application:

  1. Presence & Perspective: The doula spends time actively listening to Eleanor's fears and concerns, validating her feelings of isolation and anxiety. They explore her beliefs about death and dying, honoring her unique perspective.
  2. Exploration & Education: The doula provides information about pain management options, home healthcare services, and hospice care. They discuss the benefits of advance care planning and help Eleanor document her wishes.
  3. Action & Advocacy: The doula connects Eleanor with a palliative care team and a volunteer organization that provides companionship and support. They advocate for Eleanor's right to receive adequate pain relief and emotional support at home.
  4. Comfort & Connection: The doula provides gentle massage, guided imagery, and relaxation techniques to help Eleanor manage her pain and anxiety. They facilitate meaningful conversations with friends and family, fostering a sense of connection and belonging.
  5. Embrace & Evolve: The doula helps Eleanor find meaning and purpose in her remaining days, encouraging her to reflect on her life and legacy. They support her emotional and spiritual closure, creating a peaceful and dignified end-of-life experience.

Outcome: Eleanor was able to remain comfortably at home, surrounded by a supportive team of healthcare professionals, volunteers, and friends. She experienced a reduction in pain and anxiety, and felt a sense of peace and acceptance as she approached the end of her life.

Identifying Key Decision Points

The end-of-life journey is marked by numerous decision points, each requiring careful consideration and compassionate guidance. The PEACE Frameworkâ„¢ provides a roadmap for navigating these critical junctures, ensuring that the client's values and wishes are honored.

Here are some key decision points where the PEACE Frameworkâ„¢ can be particularly helpful:

  1. Transition to Hospice Care: Deciding when to transition to hospice care can be emotionally challenging. The doula can provide information about the benefits of hospice and help the client and family understand the services that are available.
  2. Pain Management: Managing pain effectively is essential for ensuring comfort and dignity. The doula can advocate for the client's right to receive adequate pain relief and work with the healthcare team to develop a personalized pain management plan.
  3. Advance Care Planning: Completing advance care planning documents, such as a living will and durable power of attorney for healthcare, allows the client to express their wishes regarding medical treatment. The doula can facilitate these conversations and ensure that the client's wishes are documented and respected.
  4. Spiritual and Emotional Support: Addressing spiritual and emotional needs is crucial for promoting peace and acceptance. The doula can provide a safe and supportive space for the client to explore their feelings and connect with their spiritual beliefs.
  5. Family Support: Supporting the family is an integral part of end-of-life care. The doula can provide emotional support, practical assistance, and respite care to help the family cope with the challenges of caregiving.

Ethical Considerations in PEACE

Implementing the PEACE Frameworkâ„¢ requires careful attention to ethical considerations. Doulas must adhere to professional standards of conduct and respect the client's autonomy, confidentiality, and cultural beliefs.

Here are some ethical dilemmas that may arise when implementing the PEACE Frameworkâ„¢:

  1. Confidentiality: Maintaining confidentiality is essential for building trust with the client and family. Doulas must protect the client's privacy and avoid disclosing sensitive information without their consent.
  2. Autonomy: Respecting the client's autonomy means honoring their right to make decisions about their own care, even if those decisions differ from the doula's personal beliefs.
  3. Cultural Sensitivity: Providing culturally sensitive care requires an understanding of the client's cultural background and beliefs about death and dying. Doulas must avoid imposing their own cultural values and respect the client's traditions and customs.
  4. Scope of Practice: Doulas must be aware of their scope of practice and avoid providing medical advice or performing tasks that are outside of their training and expertise.
  5. Conflicts of Interest: Doulas must avoid conflicts of interest that could compromise their objectivity or impartiality.

Strategies for Resolving Ethical Dilemmas:

  1. Consult with a Supervisor or Mentor: Seeking guidance from a more experienced doula or healthcare professional can help you navigate complex ethical dilemmas.
  2. Review Professional Codes of Ethics: Familiarize yourself with the ethical guidelines established by professional doula organizations.
  3. Engage in Reflective Practice: Take time to reflect on your own values and biases and how they may influence your decision-making.
  4. Document Your Actions: Keep accurate and detailed records of your interactions with the client and family, including any ethical concerns that arise.
