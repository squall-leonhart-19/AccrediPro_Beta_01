The Challenging Case

Sarah's Case: The Johnson Family

The Situation:

Sarah is supporting Mrs. Johnson, whose adult children have very different ideas about her end-of-life care. One wants aggressive medical intervention, while the other supports comfort care only. Mrs. Johnson is increasingly frail and unable to clearly express her wishes.

Sarah's Dilemma:

"I feel caught in the middle. The children are constantly calling me, asking me to advocate for their preferred approach. I want to honor everyone, but I'm not sure how."

Key Questions for You:

  • What resources can Sarah use to help the family reach a consensus?
  • How can she facilitate a conversation that honors Mrs. Johnson's values?
  • What are the ethical considerations?

Your Teaching Strategy

Guiding Sarah Through the Challenge

1

Emphasize Neutrality

Remind Sarah that her role is to support the client's (Mrs. Johnson's) wishes, not to take sides with family members.

2

Review Advance Directives

Explore whether Mrs. Johnson has a living will or healthcare proxy. If so, that document should guide decisions.

3

Teach Facilitation Skills

Help Sarah develop strategies for mediating family discussions, focusing on shared values and goals.

4

Set Boundaries

Advise Sarah to limit phone calls and schedule structured family meetings to manage expectations and reduce stress.

Your Feedback Conversation

Providing Constructive Guidance

Acknowledge Sarah's Feelings
"Sarah, it's completely understandable that you feel overwhelmed. These situations are emotionally charged."
Explore Her Understanding
"What do you think Mrs. Johnson would want if she could clearly tell you? What values has she expressed in the past?"
Offer Practical Strategies
"Let's brainstorm ways to facilitate a family meeting. We can create an agenda together and practice some communication techniques."
Reinforce Boundaries
"Remember, it's okay to set limits. You can say, 'I'm available for scheduled meetings, but I can't be on call 24/7.'"

Your Mentorship Matters!

By guiding new doulas, you're shaping the future of end-of-life care. Your experience and wisdom are invaluable.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important principle to emphasize when a mentee is caught between conflicting family wishes?

The doula's primary responsibility is to advocate for the client's (Mrs. Johnson's) wishes, not to take sides with family members.

2. What are some practical strategies a mentee can use to manage demanding family members?

Strategies include setting boundaries (limiting phone calls), scheduling structured family meetings, and referring to advance directives.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentorship involves guiding new doulas through complex ethical dilemmas.
  • Emphasize the importance of client-centered care and respecting advance directives.
  • Teach facilitation skills to help mentees navigate family conflicts.
  • Encourage mentees to set boundaries and prioritize self-care.
