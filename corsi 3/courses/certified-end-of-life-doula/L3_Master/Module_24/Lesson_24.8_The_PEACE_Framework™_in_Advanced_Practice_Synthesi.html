Mentoring a New Practitioner

Step into a mentor role and guide a new doula with their first client experiences.

Meet Your Mentee

üåü

Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic about end-of-life care but lacks practical experience.

Background
Former teacher, strong communication skills, compassionate nature.
Strengths
Excellent listener, empathetic, eager to learn.
Growth Areas
Needs confidence in handling difficult conversations, setting boundaries.
Her Question
"How do I navigate a family disagreement about end-of-life care?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: The Miller Family

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with the Miller family. Mr. Miller, 88, is declining rapidly. The daughter wants hospice care immediately, but the son insists on aggressive medical intervention. They are constantly arguing, and I don't know how to help them find common ground. I feel like I'm making it worse."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah facilitated a family meeting to discuss Mr. Miller's wishes?
  • Is she aware of resources for conflict resolution and mediation?
  • Has she explored the underlying fears and concerns of each family member?
  • Is she equipped to handle the emotional intensity of family disagreements?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Conflict as a Natural Part of Grief

Explain that disagreements are common during times of stress and loss. Validate the family's emotions without taking sides.

2

The Importance of Facilitation Skills

Teach Sarah how to facilitate a constructive conversation, focusing on active listening, empathy, and identifying shared goals.

3

Exploring Underlying Fears

Guide Sarah to ask questions that uncover the fears driving each family member's position. Is the son afraid of losing his father? Is the daughter worried about his suffering?

4

Referral Resources

Inform Sarah about resources such as mediators, counselors, and elder care specialists who can provide additional support.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge the Challenge
"Sarah, family disagreements are one of the toughest parts of this work. It takes courage to step into that situation."
Assess Her Perspective
"What do you think is contributing to the conflict between the daughter and son? What have you observed about their relationship?"
Offer Guidance and Resources
"Have you considered facilitating a family meeting where each person can share their concerns and wishes? I can share some resources on conflict resolution techniques."
Empower Her Next Steps
"What feels like the next best step to you? How can I support you in navigating this situation with the Miller family?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing constructive feedback to a new practitioner?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable sharing their challenges and learning from their experiences.

2. How can you help a mentee develop confidence in handling difficult conversations?

By providing them with practical tools, role-playing scenarios, and encouraging them to reflect on their own communication style.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through real-world challenges.
  • Effective supervision requires empathy, active listening, and constructive feedback.
  • Facilitating family meetings and conflict resolution are crucial skills for end-of-life doulas.
  • Providing resources and support empowers mentees to grow and develop their practice.
