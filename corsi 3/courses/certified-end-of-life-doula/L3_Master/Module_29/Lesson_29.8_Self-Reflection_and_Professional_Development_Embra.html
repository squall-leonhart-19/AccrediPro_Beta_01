Supervision & Mentoring Practice

As a Master Practitioner, you'll guide and mentor new doulas. This lab helps develop your supervisory skills and leadership presence.

Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Eager to start, but lacking confidence in handling complex family dynamics.

Background
Former social worker, strong on empathy, new to end-of-life care specifically.
Strengths
Excellent active listening skills, compassionate, and dedicated.
Growth Areas
Needs help setting boundaries, navigating family conflicts, and managing emotional intensity.
Her Question
"I'm unsure how to handle a family disagreement about the client's care plan. Can you help me navigate this?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a client, Robert, 78, with advanced COPD. His daughter wants aggressive interventions, but his son honors Robert's wish for comfort-focused care. They're constantly arguing, and it's creating a lot of stress for Robert. I don't know how to help them reach a consensus."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah explored Robert's advance directives and wishes in detail?
  • What communication techniques has Sarah used to facilitate a discussion between the siblings?
  • Is Sarah aware of conflict resolution strategies for families in end-of-life care?
  • Is she clear on her role in advocating for the client's wishes?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Client-Centered Advocacy

Remind Sarah that her primary role is to advocate for Robert's expressed wishes and ensure his comfort, not to resolve the family conflict directly. Client autonomy is paramount.

2

Teach Communication and Conflict Resolution

Share specific communication techniques, such as active listening, reframing, and conflict resolution strategies. Offer scripts she can adapt. For example, "I understand you both want what's best for Robert. Let's focus on what he wants and how we can honor that."

3

Discuss Boundary Setting

Help Sarah set clear boundaries with the family. She is there to support Robert, not to be a mediator or therapist. Encourage her to refer the family to a professional counselor if needed.

4

Role-Play and Practice

Engage in role-playing scenarios to help Sarah practice navigating difficult conversations with the family. This will build her confidence and ability to handle emotional intensity.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, I appreciate you bringing this complex situation to me. It shows your commitment to providing the best care for Robert and his family."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I offer suggestions, what strategies have you already tried, and what were the results? What feels most challenging for you in this situation?"
Offer Guidance and Resources
"In cases like this, it can be helpful to facilitate a family meeting where Robert's wishes are clearly communicated and honored. I can share some resources on family conflict resolution that you might find useful."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"What actions do you feel most comfortable taking next? Remember, you have the authority to advocate for Robert's wishes. How can I support you in doing that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of your role as a mentor in this scenario?

The most important aspect is to empower Sarah to advocate for Robert's wishes while navigating a complex family dynamic.

2. How can you help Sarah develop her skills in conflict resolution?

By providing her with specific communication techniques, conflict resolution strategies, and role-playing scenarios to practice difficult conversations.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners to develop their skills and confidence.
  • Client-centered advocacy is paramount in end-of-life care.
  • Effective communication and conflict resolution skills are essential for navigating family dynamics.
  • Boundary setting and self-care are critical for maintaining well-being as a doula.
  • Empowering new doulas builds a stronger and more compassionate end-of-life care community.
