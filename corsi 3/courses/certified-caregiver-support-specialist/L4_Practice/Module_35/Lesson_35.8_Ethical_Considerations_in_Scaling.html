Client Acquisition Practice Lab

Your Prospect

Susan, 52

Saw your Facebook ad about caregiver support. Booked a call.

Her Situation
Caring for her elderly mother with Alzheimer's. Overwhelmed, exhausted, and feels isolated.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if I can afford this, but I don't know what else to do."
Decision Style
Emotional, needs reassurance, looking for practical solutions and a supportive relationship.
Her Goal
"I need to find a way to manage my stress and feel like I'm doing the best for my mom."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you saw my ad on Facebook. To start, can you tell me a little more about what prompted you to reach out?"
YOU:
"I appreciate you sharing that, Susan. It takes courage to admit you need support. How long have you been caring for your mother?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What are the biggest challenges you're facing on a day-to-day basis? What's a typical day like for you?"
YOU:
"And how are *you* doing, Susan? What kind of support system do you have in place for yourself? Are you getting enough sleep, eating well, and taking time for yourself?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what do you see happening in the next few months? What are you most afraid of?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're carrying a heavy load. I want you to know that what you're experiencing is incredibly common among caregivers. My approach is to provide personalized support that addresses both your mother's needs and *your* well-being. I help caregivers like you create sustainable routines, find respite care, and develop coping strategies to reduce stress and prevent burnout."
YOU:
"My program includes weekly one-on-one coaching sessions, access to a private online community of other caregivers, and a library of resources and tools. We'll work together to create a plan that's tailored to your specific situation."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe I can help you find more balance and peace of mind. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. It might seem counterintuitive to add something else to your plate when you're already overwhelmed. However, investing in your own well-being will actually give you *more* time and energy in the long run. We can tailor the program to fit your schedule and focus on strategies that will save you time and reduce stress."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"I'm confident that my program can help you, Susan. I have a proven track record of success with caregivers facing similar challenges. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you don't feel like you're getting value from the program, we can discuss a refund."
"I need to think about it and talk to my family."
"That's perfectly reasonable. I can send you a detailed program overview that you can share with your family. Would it also be helpful to schedule a brief follow-up call with them so they can ask any questions they might have?"
"I'm worried about the cost."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. Let's look at it this way: How much is your health and well-being worth? Caregiver burnout can lead to serious health problems and impact your ability to care for your mother. My program is an investment in your long-term health and your ability to provide the best possible care. I also offer payment plans to make it more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call for a caregiver support specialist?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the deal.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your services?

Frame it as an investment in their long-term health and well-being, highlight the value they'll receive, and offer payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for building trust with potential clients.
  • Confidently present your pricing and address objections with clear, concise responses.
  • Consistent effort in client acquisition can lead to a sustainable and rewarding income.
