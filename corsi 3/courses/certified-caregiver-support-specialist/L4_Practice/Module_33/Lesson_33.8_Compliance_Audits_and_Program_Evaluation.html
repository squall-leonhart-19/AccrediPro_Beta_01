Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so nice to finally connect with you! I'm Sarah. Thank you for attending my workshop. What resonated most with you?"
YOU:
"I understand you're caring for your mother. That's a huge responsibility. What's a typical day look like for you?"
Phase 2: Identify Pain Points & Needs 10 min
YOU:
"What are the biggest challenges you're facing right now in caring for your mom?"
YOU:
"How is this affecting your own health and well-being? What are you sacrificing?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Solution & Benefits 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling incredibly overwhelmed and isolated. Many caregivers I work with feel the same way. Let me share how I can help."
YOU:
"My program is designed to provide you with practical strategies, emotional support, and access to resources to make caregiving more manageable. We focus on [list 2-3 key benefits, e.g., reducing stress, improving communication, finding respite care]. Imagine feeling more in control, less guilty, and having time for yourself again."
YOU:
"Clients like you, often report feeling 70% less stressed and find at least 5 hours per week for self-care after just 6 weeks."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the type of support you've been looking for? ... Great! I have a few options to share with you."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to talk to my siblings first."
"I understand. It's important to have everyone on board. What specific information do you think they'll need to make a decision? Perhaps I can provide some resources or we can schedule a brief call together to answer their questions."
"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"That's a valid concern. However, think of it this way: what's the cost of NOT making time? Burnout, exhaustion, and potentially neglecting your own health? My program is designed to be flexible and integrate seamlessly into your existing schedule. We can tailor it to fit your needs."
"I'm worried about the cost."
"I understand that budget is a factor. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. My program is an investment in your well-being and your mother's care. Think about the potential savings from reduced doctor visits, fewer hospitalizations, and increased efficiency in managing your mother's needs. We also have flexible payment options available."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing genuine interest in the prospect, actively listening to their story, and finding common ground. Showing empathy is key.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your services?

Focus on the value and ROI they'll receive, highlight the long-term benefits, and offer flexible payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection.
  • Actively listen to identify the prospect's pain points and needs.
  • Present your solution in a way that addresses their specific challenges.
  • Confidently state your pricing and highlight the value you provide.
  • Practice handling common objections with empathy and understanding.
