Saw your workshop on "Stress-Free Caregiving" at the local community center.

Her Situation
Caring for her elderly mother with Alzheimer's. Overwhelmed, exhausted, and feels guilty.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure we can afford extra help, but I can't keep going like this."
Decision Style
Needs reassurance and practical solutions. Values empathy and a personal connection.
Her Goal
"I need to find a way to balance caring for my mom with taking care of myself."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. It was lovely to meet you at the workshop. How did you find the workshop?"
YOU:
"I understand you're caring for your mother. That's a big responsibility. What's been the biggest challenge for you lately?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about your daily routine. What does a typical day look like?"
YOU:
"What kind of support system do you have in place? Are other family members involved?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest fears or concerns about the future?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're carrying a heavy load. What I do is help caregivers like you create sustainable routines, find resources, and prioritize their own well-being. Does that sound like something that would be valuable for you?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on practical strategies and emotional support. We'll work together to create a personalized plan that addresses your specific needs and challenges. Many of my clients find they have more energy and feel less overwhelmed within a few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, I believe I can help you find more balance and peace of mind. Are you interested in learning more about my program?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for another commitment."
"I understand. The program is designed to save you time and energy in the long run by creating efficient routines and accessing helpful resources. We can tailor the sessions to fit your schedule. What does your schedule look like?"
"I feel guilty spending money on myself when my mom needs so much."
"That's a common feeling among caregivers. However, taking care of yourself is not selfish; it's essential for providing the best care for your mom. If you burn out, you won't be able to help her. Think of this as an investment in both of your well-being."
"I need to talk to my siblings first."
"Of course. What information would be most helpful for them to understand the value of this program? Perhaps I can provide a summary of the benefits and how it can ease the burden on the whole family."
"I've tried other things before, and they didn't work."
"I hear your frustration. My program is different because it's highly personalized and focuses on addressing the root causes of caregiver stress. We'll work together to identify what hasn't worked in the past and create a plan that's tailored to your specific situation. What did you try, and what did you learn from it?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call?

Building rapport involves active listening, empathy, and finding common ground with the prospect. It's about creating a genuine connection and making them feel heard and understood.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your services?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment in terms of the value they'll receive, such as reduced stress, improved well-being, and increased efficiency. Highlight the long-term benefits and potential return on investment.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to feel confident and natural.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Present your pricing with conviction and highlight the value you offer.
  • Remember that client acquisition is about building relationships and helping people.
