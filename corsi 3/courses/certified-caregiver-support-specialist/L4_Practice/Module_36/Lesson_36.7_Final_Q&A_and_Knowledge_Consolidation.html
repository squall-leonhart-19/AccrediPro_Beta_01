  2. Effective coping mechanisms

Case Study: Maria, 58

Maria, a 58-year-old woman, is the primary caregiver for her 82-year-old mother who has advanced Parkinson's disease. Maria reports feeling overwhelmed, exhausted, and resentful. She struggles to balance her caregiving responsibilities with her full-time job and her own personal needs.

Intervention: Using the H.E.A.R.T. Frameworkâ„¢, the support specialist actively listened to Maria's concerns (Hear & Honor), explored her feelings of resentment and isolation (Explore & Empathize), assessed her support system and coping mechanisms (Assess & Affirm), provided information about local resources such as respite care and support groups (Resource & Restore), and helped her develop strategies for setting boundaries and prioritizing her own well-being (Transform & Thrive).

Outcomes: After several weeks of support, Maria reported feeling less overwhelmed and more in control of her situation. She began attending a weekly caregiver support group, which provided her with a sense of community and validation. She also started using respite care services, which allowed her to take breaks and recharge. As a result, Maria's overall well-being improved, and she was better able to provide quality care for her mother.
