Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I know your time is valuable. To start, can you tell me a bit about what led you to reach out?"
YOU:
"That's understandable. Caring for a parent is incredibly demanding. How long have you been providing care for your mother?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What are the biggest challenges you're facing right now? What's a typical day look like?"
YOU:
"And what resources have you already explored or tried?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what impact will this have on your own well-being and your family?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed and isolated, and you're looking for a way to regain some control and balance. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is to provide you with personalized support and resources to navigate the challenges of caregiving. I work with clients over 8 weeks to create a tailored plan that addresses their specific needs, including emotional support, practical strategies, and connection to helpful resources. Many of my clients report feeling significantly less stressed and more confident within just a few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with you, Susan? Do you see how this could help you in your situation? ... Fantastic! Let me share the details of the program and how you can get started."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the "I need to think about it" objection?

Ask what specific aspects they need to think about and offer to provide more information or address any remaining concerns.

3. How can you confidently present your pricing?

Clearly state the price, highlight the value and benefits of the program, and emphasize the positive outcomes clients have experienced.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Building rapport and understanding the client's needs are essential for a successful call.
  • Confidently address objections by empathizing and providing clear solutions.
  • Present your pricing with confidence, highlighting the value and benefits of your program.
  • Consistent practice and a client-focused approach will lead to increased income potential.
