Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for connecting! I'm Sarah, and I'm so glad you reached out. I know how challenging it can be to balance everything. To start, can you tell me a bit about what led you to seek caregiver support?"
YOU:
"That makes perfect sense. So many women find themselves in similar situations. What's a typical day like for you right now?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"What are the biggest challenges you're facing in caring for your mom?"
YOU:
"And how is this impacting your own well-being? Are you finding time for yourself?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your current situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're feeling overwhelmed, stretched thin, and worried about both your mom's well-being and your own. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on creating a personalized support system that addresses both your mom's needs and your own. Over 12 weeks, we'll develop strategies for managing her care, accessing resources, and prioritizing your self-care. Many of my clients report feeling less stressed and more in control within the first month."
YOU:
"For example, I worked with a client, Maria, a 48-year-old who was in a very similar situation. Within a few weeks, she was able to delegate some of the caregiving tasks and start taking time for herself again. She even joined a local support group that I recommended."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me share some details about the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand. Many of my clients have similar concerns initially. Let's look at the value you'll receive. Think about the cost of not addressing this ‚Äì the potential impact on your health, your job, and your relationship with your mom. My program is an investment in your well-being and her care. We can also explore payment options to make it more manageable."
"I need to talk to my family about it."
"That's perfectly reasonable. What specific information do you think they'll need to make a decision? I'm happy to provide resources or even join a brief call with them to answer any questions."
"I'm just not sure if it will work for me."
"I appreciate your honesty. What are your biggest concerns about the program's effectiveness? Perhaps I can share some success stories from other clients who had similar doubts initially. Also, remember that I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not seeing results within the first few weeks, we can adjust the program or offer a refund."
"I don't have time."
"I understand completely. Time is precious, especially when you're caregiving. This program is designed to actually *save* you time by streamlining your caregiving responsibilities and helping you prioritize. Many of my clients find that they actually gain more free time as a result of the strategies we implement. We'll work together to create a schedule that fits your life."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the "It's too expensive" objection?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the cost as an investment in their well-being and the well-being of their loved one. Discuss the potential costs of *not* addressing the problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Understand your prospect's needs and pain points before the call.
  • Empathy builds trust: Show genuine care and understanding for their situation.
  • Value-based pricing: Focus on the transformation and results you provide, not just the price tag.
  • Confident closing: Practice your closing lines and be ready to guide them to the next step.
  • You've got this!: With practice and preparation, you can confidently acquire new clients and build a thriving practice.
