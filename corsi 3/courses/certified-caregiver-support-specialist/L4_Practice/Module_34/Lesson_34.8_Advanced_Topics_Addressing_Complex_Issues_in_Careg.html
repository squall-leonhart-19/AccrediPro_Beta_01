Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm too busy."
"I understand. Life gets hectic. This workshop is designed to give you focused time to prioritize YOURSELF. It's an investment in your well-being that can actually create more time and energy in the long run. Plus, the recordings are available if you miss a session!"
"I'm not sure if it's for me."
"That's perfectly okay. What specifically makes you unsure? I can share some testimonials from past participants who felt the same way initially. I'm confident that the workshop will provide value and clarity, and I offer a satisfaction guarantee."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand budget is a consideration. However, think about what it's costing you to stay stuck and unfulfilled. This workshop provides a structured, supportive environment to rediscover your passions and create a roadmap for your future. Many participants have told me it was the best investment they've made in themselves. I also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"I'm not a 'group' person."
"I get it. Group settings aren't for everyone. But this workshop is designed to create a safe and supportive community. It's a chance to connect with other women who are going through similar experiences. You'll be surprised at the power of shared experiences and the support you'll receive. You are welcome to participate at your own comfort level."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a compelling 30-second workshop sales pitch?

The pitch should include a hook, identification of the problem, presentation of the solution (your workshop), and a clear call to action.

2. How can you confidently address the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the cost as an investment in their well-being and future. Highlight the value they'll receive and offer payment options if available.

3. What are some effective closing lines to encourage someone to sign up for your workshop?

Use closing lines that connect with their emotions and goals, such as, "Are you ready to say 'yes' to yourself?" or "Are you ready to invest in your next chapter?" Always end with a clear call to action.

Key Takeaways

  • Craft a compelling 30-second pitch highlighting the problem you solve and the value of your workshop.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, empathetic responses.
  • Present your pricing with confidence, emphasizing the value and ROI of the workshop.
  • Practice your closing lines to encourage immediate action and secure registrations.
  • Remember, every successful workshop launch starts with a belief in your ability to help others.
