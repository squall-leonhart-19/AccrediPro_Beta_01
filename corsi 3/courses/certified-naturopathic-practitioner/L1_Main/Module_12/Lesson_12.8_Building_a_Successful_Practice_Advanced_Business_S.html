Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds incredibly frustrating to be dealing with these gut issues, especially when you've already tried a few things. I want to assure you that we can explore this together and find a solution that works for you."
MARIA:
"I really hope so. I'm starting to feel like this is just my new normal."
YOU:
"I understand that feeling, but I want you to know that gut health is complex, and there are many factors that could be contributing to your symptoms. We're going to look beyond just probiotics and gluten, and consider everything from your stress levels to the diversity of your gut microbiome."
YOU:
"I'd like to start by understanding your typical day, especially around mealtimes and stress triggers. Then, we can discuss some targeted strategies to support your gut health. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds great. I'm ready to try anything."
YOU:
"Perfect. Let's start with a few questions about your diet and lifestyle. I'll explain why each question is important as we go. Sound good?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses relief that you're considering more than just diet.
  • She actively participates in describing her daily routine.
  • She seems more hopeful about finding a long-term solution.
  • She asks about specific tests or assessments you might recommend.
  • She schedules a follow-up appointment to continue the conversation.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to establish with a client before recommending any gut health interventions?

Building trust and rapport, and assuring them that you understand their frustration.

2. Why is it important to address stress levels when coaching a client with gut health issues?

Stress can significantly impact gut motility, inflammation, and the balance of gut bacteria.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial when discussing sensitive health issues.
  • Gut health coaching involves more than just diet; consider stress, lifestyle, and the gut-brain connection.
  • Avoid jumping to conclusions or quick-fix solutions.
  • Focus on building a comprehensive and personalized plan.
  • Set realistic expectations and emphasize the importance of long-term commitment.
