Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your health journey with me. It sounds like you've been dealing with a lot. I want you to know that it's absolutely possible to feel better and manage your Hashimoto's effectively. Many of my clients with similar conditions have experienced significant improvements through targeted dietary strategies."
MARIA:
"I've tried so many things already. Gluten-free and dairy-free haven't been enough, and I'm overwhelmed by all the conflicting information out there."
YOU:
"I understand your frustration. It's common to feel overwhelmed, especially with autoimmune conditions. The key is to personalize your approach based on your specific needs and triggers. While gluten and dairy are a good start, we can explore more advanced strategies to calm your immune system and reduce inflammation."
YOU:
"I'd like to discuss the Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) diet as a potential tool. It's a more restrictive approach initially, but it can help us identify hidden food sensitivities that are contributing to your symptoms. How does that sound to you?"
MARIA:
"AIP? I've heard of it, but it seems so complicated. Is it really necessary?"
YOU:
"It's a valid concern, Maria. AIP isn't a forever diet, but rather a therapeutic tool to help us understand your body better. We'll work together to implement it gradually, and I'll provide you with resources, meal plans, and ongoing support. The goal is to reduce inflammation and reintroduce foods strategically to see how your body responds. Are you open to learning more about it?"

  • Maria expresses relief: "It's good to know there are other options."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Maria's previous dietary efforts before suggesting AIP?

Validating her efforts builds trust and shows that you appreciate the work she has already put in. It avoids making her feel like her previous attempts were a failure.

2. What is the key message to convey when introducing the Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) diet to a client with Hashimoto's?

The key message is that AIP is a therapeutic tool for identifying food sensitivities and reducing inflammation, not a restrictive, long-term diet. Emphasize its temporary nature and your support throughout the process.

Key Takeaways

  • Approach clients with autoimmune conditions with empathy and understanding.
  • Validate their previous efforts and acknowledge their frustrations.
  • Present advanced dietary strategies, like AIP, as manageable tools for healing.
  • Offer practical support, resources, and ongoing guidance.
  • Emphasize the importance of personalized approaches and strategic food reintroductions.
