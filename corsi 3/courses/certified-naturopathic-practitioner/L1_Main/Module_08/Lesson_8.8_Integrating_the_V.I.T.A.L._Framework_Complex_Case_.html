Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you've been going through a challenging time. Before we dive deeper, I want to assure you that feeling anxious and having trouble sleeping, especially after a period of intense stress, is very common. Many of my clients experience something similar."
MARIA:
"It's just so frustrating. I thought once the project was over, I'd feel better, but it's like my body is still stuck in high gear."
YOU:
"I understand. What you're describing is often a sign that your nervous system needs some support to reset. We can work together to identify specific strategies to help calm your mind and body in the evenings, so you can naturally transition into a restful sleep."
YOU:
"I'd like to explore a few key areas with you: your evening routine, your stress management techniques, and your dietary habits. Does that sound good to you?"
MARIA:
"Yes, that makes sense. I'm willing to try anything at this point."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by talking about what a typical evening looks like for you. And I'll explain along the way how each element might be impacting your anxiety and sleep. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate the client's experience before offering solutions?

Validating the client's experience helps build trust and rapport. It shows that you understand their struggles and are genuinely interested in helping them, which makes them more receptive to your recommendations.

2. What are some key areas to explore when coaching a client struggling with anxiety and sleep issues?

Key areas to explore include the client's evening routine, stress management techniques, and dietary habits. These areas often have a significant impact on anxiety levels and sleep quality.

Key Takeaways

  • Always start by validating the client's experience and building rapport.
  • Focus on a few key areas that are most likely to make a difference.
  • Use simple and relatable language, avoiding technical jargon.
  • Ask open-ended questions to gather information and avoid making assumptions.
  • Empower the client to take an active role in their healing process.
