Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Intro 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can help you. To start, what prompted you to reach out to me specifically?"
YOU:
"That's interesting. What are your initial impressions of my approach based on what you've seen?"
Phase 2: Problem Deep Dive 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe your digestive issues in more detail? When did they start, and what does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"What specific foods or situations trigger your symptoms? How have these issues impacted your daily life and overall well-being?"
YOU:
"What treatments or approaches have you tried so far? What were the outcomes, and what did you learn from those experiences?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like [summarize Susan's pain points and goals]. Is that an accurate summary?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on identifying the root causes of your digestive issues, rather than just managing symptoms. I use a combination of dietary adjustments, lifestyle modifications, and targeted supplementation to restore balance to your gut. My 12-week program is designed to provide you with personalized support and guidance every step of the way."
YOU:
"Typically, clients start experiencing noticeable improvements within the first few weeks, such as reduced bloating and increased energy. We'll work together to create a sustainable plan that fits your lifestyle and helps you achieve long-term relief."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you see how this could be different from what you've tried before? ... Wonderful! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand budget is a consideration. Let's explore the value of resolving these issues. How much are you currently spending on over-the-counter remedies, doctor visits, and missed social opportunities? My program is an investment in your long-term health and well-being. We can also discuss payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I'm worried I won't be able to stick to the program."
"That's a valid concern. My program is designed to be flexible and adaptable to your lifestyle. I provide ongoing support, accountability, and guidance to help you stay on track. We'll work together to create realistic goals and strategies that fit your needs. Many of my clients have successfully transformed their health, even with busy schedules."
"I need to do more research first."
"I respect that. What specific information are you looking for? I'm happy to provide you with additional resources, client testimonials, or answer any further questions you may have. My goal is to ensure you feel confident and informed about your decision."
"I've tried so many things before that haven't worked."
"I hear your frustration. It sounds like you've been through a lot. My approach is different because we focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of your issues, rather than just masking symptoms. I also provide a personalized plan tailored to your unique needs and circumstances. Let's explore what hasn't worked in the past and how we can create a more effective strategy for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: 1) Rapport & Intro, 2) Problem Deep Dive, 3) Present Your Solution, and 4) Close & Next Steps.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the "It's too expensive" objection?

A good strategy is to reframe the investment by emphasizing the long-term value and potential savings on other expenses, and by offering payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is crucial for a thriving practice.
  • The discovery call is your opportunity to connect with potential clients and showcase your value.
  • Confidently handle objections by addressing concerns and highlighting the benefits of your program.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to convey the worth of your services.
  • Consistent effort and effective strategies can lead to a sustainable and rewarding income.
