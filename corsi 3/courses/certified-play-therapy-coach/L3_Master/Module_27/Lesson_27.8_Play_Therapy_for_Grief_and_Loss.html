Former elementary school teacher, strong foundation in child development.

"I'm stuck on this case. How do I manage the parent's expectations without compromising the child's process?"

The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I'm working with a 6-year-old, Leo, whose parents are constantly asking for updates and progress reports. Leo is benefiting from the sessions, but I feel pressured to deliver quick results to satisfy the parents. They want me to 'fix' his behavior immediately, but I know play therapy is a process."

What You Need to Explore:

  • How has Aisha explained the play therapy process to the parents?
  • What strategies has she used to manage parental expectations?
  • Is there a potential for a parent consultation session to address their concerns?
  • How is Aisha managing her own anxiety around parental expectations?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize the Importance of Psychoeducation

Discuss how educating parents about the principles of play therapy can align their expectations with the therapeutic process.

2

Role-Play Difficult Conversations

Practice scenarios where Aisha can confidently explain the value of child-centered play therapy and manage parental demands.

3

Review Ethical Considerations

Highlight the ethical obligation to prioritize the child's needs and the importance of maintaining confidentiality.

4

Promote Self-Care Strategies

Encourage Aisha to establish boundaries and practice self-care to prevent burnout from managing demanding parents.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Aisha's Concerns
"Aisha, it's completely normal to feel pressured by parents, especially when you're starting out. It shows you care deeply about your clients."
Explore Her Current Strategies
"Tell me about how you've been communicating with Leo's parents so far. What have you already tried?"
Offer Alternative Approaches
"One approach could be to schedule a parent consultation where you explain the play therapy process, emphasizing that it's not a quick fix but a journey of self-discovery for Leo."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Ultimately, the decision is yours. Which approach feels most authentic and ethically sound for you and Leo?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a new practitioner's feelings when they present a challenging case?

Validating their feelings helps build trust and rapport, making them more receptive to feedback and guidance. It also acknowledges the emotional toll of the work.

2. What are some strategies for managing parental expectations in play therapy?

Strategies include psychoeducation about play therapy, setting clear boundaries, regular parent consultations, and emphasizing the child's needs as the priority.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through challenging cases and ethical dilemmas.
  • Psychoeducation is crucial for aligning parental expectations with the play therapy process.
  • Validating a mentee's feelings and experiences fosters a supportive learning environment.
  • Ethical decision-making should be modeled and discussed openly during supervision.
  • Supporting new practitioners contributes to the growth and integrity of the Play Therapy Coaching field.
