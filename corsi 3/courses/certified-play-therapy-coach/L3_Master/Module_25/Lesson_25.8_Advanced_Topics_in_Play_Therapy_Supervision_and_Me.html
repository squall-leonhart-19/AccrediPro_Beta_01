Your Mentee

Maria, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic, just completed L1, seeking guidance on a client case.

Background
Former elementary school teacher, great with kids, new to coaching
Strengths
Excellent rapport, creative, passionate about helping children
Growth Areas
Needs help with session structure, setting boundaries, and handling parent interactions.
Her Question
"I feel lost in the session. How do I keep it on track?"

The Case She Presents

Maria's Client Case

What Maria Tells You:

"I had a session with a 6-year-old, Leo, who has anxiety. I started with a feelings check-in, but then he just wanted to build a tower of blocks. I let him, but I felt like I wasn't really doing anything. The mom expects progress."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What were Maria's goals for the session?
  • Did she communicate those goals to Leo's mom?
  • Did she observe any themes or patterns in Leo's play?
  • How can she gently guide the session while honoring Leo's process?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Maria

1

The Power of Observation

Explain that simply observing Leo's play is valuable. What kind of tower is he building? Is it strong? Wobbly? What happens when it falls?

2

Translate Play into Meaning

Help Maria see that the tower is a metaphor. Ask: "What might the tower represent for Leo? His anxiety? His sense of security?"

3

Gentle Guidance Techniques

Discuss ways to gently guide the session. Suggest phrases like: "That's a strong tower! What happens if we add another block?"

4

Parent Communication

Emphasize the importance of setting expectations with parents. Explain that play is Leo's language and that you're observing and learning.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Maria, I love your enthusiasm! It's clear you care deeply about Leo. This is a common feeling for new practitioners. Let's unpack it."
Ask Before Telling
"What did you notice about Leo's play? What did your intuition tell you about what he needed in that moment?"
Teach a Key Principle
"Remember, play is communication for children. The tower *is* the session. Your job is to observe and help him express himself through it."
Offer Specific Strategies
"Next time, try narrating his play: 'You're building a tall tower. It looks very strong.' This helps him feel seen and understood."
Empower Her Next Steps
"How will you communicate this to Leo's mom? What's one thing you'll do differently in the next session?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to validate when mentoring a new practitioner?

Their feelings of uncertainty and self-doubt are normal and expected.

2. How can you help a mentee translate a client's play into meaning?

Ask open-ended questions about what the play might represent for the client, connecting it to their presenting issues.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not directing.
  • Focus on the practitioner's experience and feelings.
  • Help them connect theory to real-world practice.
  • Encourage self-reflection and critical thinking.
  • Celebrate their strengths and build their confidence.
