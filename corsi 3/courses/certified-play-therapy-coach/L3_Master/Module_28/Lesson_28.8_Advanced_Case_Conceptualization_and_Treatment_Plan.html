Her Question
"I feel overwhelmed by a client's trauma disclosure. Am I doing enough?"

The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I'm working with a 7-year-old, Ben, who disclosed abuse in a play session. I followed reporting protocols, but I feel so responsible. I'm worried I'm not equipped to help him through this."

What You Need to Explore:

  • How did Aisha manage her own emotional response during the disclosure?
  • Did she provide appropriate validation and support to Ben?
  • Does she understand the importance of self-care and boundaries in trauma work?
  • Is she aware of the resources available to support Ben and his family?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Acknowledge Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed after a disclosure like that. It shows you care, and it's also a sign you're human."

2

Review Reporting Procedures

Ensure she followed protocol correctly and understands her legal and ethical obligations. This can reduce anxiety.

3

Emphasize the Power of Play

Remind her that play is Ben's language. Even if she feels inadequate, she's providing a safe space for him to process.

4

Focus on Self-Care

Discuss strategies for managing vicarious trauma: supervision, mindfulness, setting boundaries, and personal therapy.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Aisha, it takes courage to work with such sensitive cases. I commend you for being there for Ben and for reaching out for supervision."
Ask About Her Experience
"Before we dive in, tell me about how you're feeling. What's been most challenging for you since Ben's disclosure?"
Reframe Her Role
"Remember, you're not solely responsible for fixing Ben's trauma. You're a compassionate guide. His healing journey is his own, and you're supporting him along the way."
Empower Her Going Forward
"What small, manageable steps can you take this week to recharge and set better boundaries for yourself? Let's create a self-care plan together."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate the mentee's emotional response in complex cases?

Validating their emotions helps build trust and creates a safe space for them to discuss their challenges openly. It also normalizes the impact of vicarious trauma.

2. What are some practical self-care strategies you can suggest to a mentee working with trauma?

Examples include: setting boundaries, scheduling regular supervision, practicing mindfulness or meditation, engaging in hobbies, spending time with loved ones, and seeking personal therapy.

You're Shaping the Future!

By mentoring new practitioners, you're not only supporting them but also raising the standard of care in play therapy. Your guidance is invaluable!

Key Takeaways

  • Validating the mentee's emotions is crucial for building trust and rapport.
  • Reviewing ethical guidelines and reporting procedures provides clarity and reduces anxiety.
  • Emphasizing the power of play reminds the mentee of their unique contribution.
  • Focusing on self-care helps prevent burnout and vicarious trauma.
  • Your guidance shapes the future of play therapy by supporting the next generation of practitioners.
