Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Validate Her Feelings

Acknowledge that it's normal to feel overwhelmed when working with emotionally intense clients. Emphasize the importance of self-care and seeking support.

2

Review Limit-Setting Techniques

Discuss specific strategies for setting limits in the playroom while still allowing for emotional expression. Share examples of effective language and techniques.

3

Explore Countertransference

Help Olivia understand and manage her countertransference reactions to the parents. Discuss strategies for maintaining professional boundaries and seeking consultation when needed.

4

Emphasize Documentation

Highlight the importance of thorough documentation for tracking progress, identifying patterns, and informing treatment decisions. Review best practices for note-taking and record-keeping.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Olivia, thank you for bringing this case to supervision. It takes courage to admit when you're feeling stuck, and I appreciate your vulnerability."
Acknowledge Strengths
"I know you have a gift for connecting with children. You have a natural ability to build rapport and create a safe space for them."
Offer Specific Suggestions
"When Ben starts breaking toys, you could try saying something like, 'I see you're feeling really angry right now. It's okay to feel angry, but it's not okay to break the toys. Can you show me how angry you are in a way that keeps the toys safe?'"
Empower Her to Find Solutions
"What are some ways you can take care of yourself after a particularly challenging session? Maybe taking a walk, listening to music, or talking to a friend?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important element to establish at the beginning of a supervision relationship?

A safe and trusting environment where the mentee feels comfortable sharing their challenges and vulnerabilities.

2. How can you best help a mentee who is struggling with countertransference?

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a collaborative process that requires trust, respect, and open communication.
  • Effective supervision involves validating the mentee's feelings, offering specific feedback, and empowering them to find solutions.
  • Managing countertransference is crucial for both the mentee and the supervisor.
  • Thorough documentation is essential for tracking progress, identifying patterns, and informing treatment decisions.
  • By investing in the growth of new practitioners, you're contributing to the long-term success of the Play Therapy Coaching field.
