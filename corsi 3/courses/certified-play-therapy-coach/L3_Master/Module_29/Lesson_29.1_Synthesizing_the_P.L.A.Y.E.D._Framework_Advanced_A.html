Interconnectedness of P.L.A.Y.E.D.

In previous modules, we explored each element of the P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢ individually. Case Study: Trauma and Attachment

Applying P.L.A.Y.E.D. to Complex Trauma

Client: Sarah, age 7, presenting with symptoms of trauma and attachment disorder following a history of neglect.

Intervention: The play therapy coach used P.L.A.Y.E.D. to create a safe and attuned environment where Sarah could express her emotions and process her experiences. The coach focused on Presence and Listening to build trust and rapport. Assessment involved observing Sarah's play patterns and identifying underlying emotional needs. Yielding to Sarah's lead allowed her to explore her trauma at her own pace. Empowerment techniques helped Sarah develop a sense of control and mastery. Developmental considerations informed the choice of play materials and interventions.

Outcomes: Over several months, Sarah showed significant improvement in her emotional regulation, attachment security, and overall well-being. She was able to form healthier relationships and engage in more adaptive coping strategies.

Tailoring P.L.A.Y.E.D. for Diverse Needs

Cultural sensitivity and individualization are essential when applying the P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢. Each child is unique, and their cultural background, experiences, and needs must be considered. Tailoring the framework involves adapting the interventions and techniques to align with the child's specific circumstances.

For example, when working with children from diverse cultural backgrounds, it's important to be aware of cultural norms and values related to play, communication, and emotional expression. Using culturally relevant play materials and incorporating cultural traditions into the therapy process can enhance engagement and build trust. Additionally, adapting the language and communication style to match the child's preferences can facilitate a stronger therapeutic connection. Remember, the goal is to create a safe and inclusive environment where every child feels valued and understood.
