Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out! I appreciate you taking the time to speak with me today. To start, could you tell me a little bit about what prompted you to schedule this call?"
YOU:
"I understand. It takes courage to reach out for help. How has your child's anxiety been impacting your family life?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about your child's separation anxiety. When did it start? What triggers it?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried so far to help your child manage their anxiety? What has been effective, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"If your child continues to struggle with anxiety, what impact do you see this having on their future? If you could wave a magic wand and resolve this, what would that look like for your child and your family?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my child will participate."
"That's a valid concern. In my experience, children are often hesitant at first, but once they experience the fun and engaging nature of play therapy, they become more willing to participate. I can also provide strategies to help you encourage your child's involvement."
"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"Of course! I'm happy to provide you with a summary of our conversation and additional information to share with your partner. Would it be helpful to schedule a brief call together to answer any questions they may have?"
"What if it doesn't work?"
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I'm confident in the effectiveness of my approach. I provide ongoing support and guidance throughout the program, and I'm committed to working with you to achieve the best possible results. A 2021 meta-analysis of 32 studies (n=1,542) found play therapy to be effective in reducing anxiety symptoms in children."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. Let's explore the value of addressing your child‚Äôs anxiety now. What is the long-term cost of not addressing this issue? We can also discuss flexible payment options to make the program more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How can you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, explore the long-term value of addressing the issue, and discuss flexible payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is crucial for building a thriving practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you understand your prospect's needs and present your services effectively.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy can turn potential "no's" into "yes's."
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and demonstrating the value of your services is essential for closing the deal.
  • Realistic income goals are achievable with consistent effort and effective sales strategies.
