Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out! I saw you found me on Instagram. What caught your eye about my profile?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! I love helping families navigate these challenges. What's been happening with your child lately?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the separation anxiety. How long has it been going on, and what does it look like?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to help your child?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what impact will this have on your child's future? And what would it mean to you to see your child thriving?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her concerns]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My play therapy coaching program is designed to address the root causes of anxiety. Over 10 weeks, we'll work together to [explain key components of your program, e.g., parent-child interaction, emotional regulation techniques]. Many parents see positive changes within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with what you're looking for? ... Great! Let me share the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if my child will participate."
"That's a valid concern. My program is designed to be engaging and fun for children. We start with building a strong connection and using their natural play instincts. I also provide strategies for encouraging participation. Would you like to hear some success stories from other parents who had similar concerns?"
"I'm already seeing a therapist."
"That's great that you're already seeking support. My coaching program complements therapy by providing practical tools and strategies you can use at home, in between therapy sessions. It's like having an extra layer of support to accelerate your child's progress."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can promise that I'll provide you with the tools, knowledge, and support to make meaningful progress. My program is based on evidence-based practices, and I have a track record of helping families like yours. I'm confident that if you commit to the process, you'll see positive changes."
"It's a big investment."
"I understand. Let's break down what you're getting. This is a 10-week program that includes [list inclusions - weekly coaching calls, personalized play plans, resources]. Think about the long-term benefits ‚Äì reduced anxiety, improved school performance, and a happier child. What's that worth to you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: building rapport, understanding the problem, presenting your approach, and closing.

2. How should you handle the objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions: "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?"
