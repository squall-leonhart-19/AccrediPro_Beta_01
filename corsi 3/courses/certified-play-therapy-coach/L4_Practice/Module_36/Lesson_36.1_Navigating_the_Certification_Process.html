Understanding Ethical Guidelines and Professional Conduct

As a Certified Play Therapy Coachâ„¢, you are expected to adhere to the highest ethical standards and professional conduct. This is critical for building trust with clients and maintaining the integrity of the profession.

  • Confidentiality: Maintain strict confidentiality regarding client information and sessions, adhering to HIPAA guidelines.
  • Boundaries: Establish and maintainstrict professional boundaries with clients, avoiding dual relationships or conflicts of interest.
  • Informed Consent: Obtain informed consent from clients (or their guardians) before commencing coaching sessions, ensuring they understand the process, potential risks, and their rights.
  • Competence: Practice within the scope of your competence, seeking supervision or further training when working with clients who present with complex or specialized needs.
  • Integrity: Act with honesty, transparency, and integrity in all your professional interactions, avoiding misrepresentation or unethical practices.

Case Study: Ethical Dilemma

Client: Sarah, a 7-year-old girl.

Presenting Issue: Sarah's parents are going through a difficult divorce, and she is exhibiting signs of anxiety and withdrawal.

Ethical Dilemma: During a play therapy session, Sarah discloses that she witnessed her father engaging in abusive behavior towards her mother. You are unsure whether to report this information, considering the potential impact on Sarah and her family.

Intervention: You consult with a qualified supervisor to discuss the ethical implications of this situation. Following their guidance and legal requirements, you decide to report the suspected abuse to the appropriate authorities, while also providing ongoing support and therapy to Sarah.

Outcome: The situation is addressed by child protective services, and Sarah receives the necessary support and protection. You continue to work with Sarah to process her emotions and develop coping mechanisms.

Navigating the Certification Renewal Process

Your certification as a Certified Play Therapy Coachâ„¢ is valid for a period of two years. To maintain your certification, you must complete the renewal process before your certification expires.

  • Renewal Application: Submit a renewal application, including documentation of your continuing education hours and any updates to your professional information.
