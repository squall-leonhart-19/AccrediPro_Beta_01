Enrollment Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Reconnect 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Maria, it's so great to connect with you again! Thank you for joining me today. How did you enjoy the 'Raising Resilient Children' workshop?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear! I remember you shared [mention something specific Maria shared during the workshop]. What resonated with you the most?"
Phase 2: Explore Needs & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing as a parent right now?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and create the perfect family life, what would that look like?"
YOU:
"Why is it important for you to achieve these goals?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program 10 min
YOU:
"Maria, based on what you've shared, our 6-week group program, 'Confident Parenting,' is designed to help you achieve exactly what you're looking for. We'll cover strategies for setting boundaries, managing challenging behaviors, and building stronger connections with your children. Plus, you'll be part of a supportive community of parents who understand what you're going through."
YOU:
"Each week, we'll have a 90-minute live session where we'll discuss the week's topic, practice new skills, and share our experiences. You'll also receive a workbook with exercises and resources to support your learning. And, you'll have access to our private online community where you can connect with other parents between sessions."
Phase 4: Close & Invite Enrollment 5 min
YOU:
"So Maria, does this program sound like it could be a good fit for you and your family? ... Excellent! Let me share the enrollment details."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm too busy right now."
"I understand. Life can be hectic. However, investing in yourself and your family can actually create more time and energy in the long run. The program is designed to be flexible, and you can access the recordings if you miss a live session. What would need to shift in order for you to prioritize this for yourself?"
"I'm not sure if it will work for me."
"That's a valid concern. I've seen many parents transform their parenting skills and family dynamics through this program. We provide a supportive environment, practical tools, and personalized guidance to help you succeed. We also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you attend all the sessions and complete the exercises, and you're not satisfied with the results, we'll refund your investment. How does that sound?"
"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"Of course. I want you both to feel comfortable with this decision. Would you like me to send you some information to share with your partner? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call together to answer any questions they may have?"
"I'm not a 'group' person."
"I understand. Group programs aren't for everyone. However, many participants find the community aspect to be one of the most valuable parts of the program. It's a safe space to share your experiences, learn from others, and receive support. You're welcome to participate at your own comfort level. You can observe and listen, or actively engage ‚Äì whatever feels right for you. Plus, I incorporate individual coaching elements within the group setting to ensure everyone's needs are met."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful enrollment conversation for a group program?

Building rapport, exploring needs and goals, presenting the program as a solution, handling objections, and inviting enrollment.

2. How can you address the objection of "I'm too busy" when enrolling someone in a group program?

Acknowledge their busyness, then reframe the program as an investment that creates more time and energy in the long run. Highlight the flexibility of the program and the availability of recordings.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a powerful way to scale your impact and increase your income.
  • Enrollment conversations should focus on understanding the client's needs and demonstrating how the program provides a solution.
  • Anticipate and prepare for common objections, such as "I'm too busy" or "I can't afford it."
  • Practice presenting your pricing with confidence and offering clear call-to-action statements.
  • Remember, you're not just selling a program; you're offering transformation and community support.
