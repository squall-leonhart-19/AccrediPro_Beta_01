Susan, 45

Saw your ad on Facebook. Looking for guidance for her anxious child.

Her Situation
Child (age 7) struggling with separation anxiety, school refusal. Susan feels overwhelmed and helpless.
Budget Concern
"Play therapy is expensive. I'm not sure we can afford it long-term."
Decision Style
Wants practical strategies she can implement at home. Values expertise and compassion.
Her Goal
"I want my child to feel happy and confident again. And I want to feel like I'm helping, not making things worse."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for reaching out! I appreciate you taking the time to chat. First, can you tell me a little more about what's been going on with your child?"
YOU:
"I understand. It sounds like you're going through a tough time. I work with many parents who are experiencing similar challenges."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"When did you first notice the separation anxiety? What does it look like on a typical school day?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried so far? And what has your child's teacher suggested?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being 'completely resolved', where would you like to be in three months?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like your child's anxiety is significantly impacting both of your lives. Is that a fair assessment?"
YOU:
"My approach is to work with both you and your child. We'll use play therapy techniques to help your child express their feelings and develop coping mechanisms. I'll also provide you with practical strategies you can use at home to support your child's progress. A 2022 study in the *Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry* found that parent-child interventions reduced childhood anxiety symptoms by 40% (n=124)."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like a good fit for your needs? ... Great! Let me explain my program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my child will participate."
"That's a valid concern. Play therapy is designed to be engaging and fun for children. I create a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable expressing themselves. Plus, we can start with shorter sessions and gradually increase the length as your child becomes more comfortable."
"We've tried therapy before, and it didn't work."
"I understand your hesitation. Every therapist has a different approach. My focus is on creating a strong connection with your child and tailoring the therapy to their specific needs. I also involve parents actively in the process, which can significantly improve outcomes."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that cost is a factor. Think of this as an investment in your child's long-term well-being. Untreated anxiety can lead to academic difficulties, social isolation, and other problems down the road. My program includes [list inclusions], and many families find it to be a worthwhile investment. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can provide more information to help you make a decision. I am available to answer any questions you have."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key pieces of information you should gather during the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

You should gather information about the onset of the issue, the strategies the client has already tried, and what their desired outcome is.

2. Give an example of how you might reframe the objection "It's too expensive" to focus on value.

You could say, "I understand that cost is a factor. Think of this as an investment in your child's long-term well-being. Untreated anxiety can lead to academic difficulties, social isolation, and other problems down the road."

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust with potential clients.
  • Understanding the client's problem deeply allows you to tailor your approach effectively.
  • Address objections confidently by reframing them and focusing on the value you provide.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to convey confidence and professionalism.
  • Consistent client acquisition efforts can lead to a sustainable and fulfilling practice.
