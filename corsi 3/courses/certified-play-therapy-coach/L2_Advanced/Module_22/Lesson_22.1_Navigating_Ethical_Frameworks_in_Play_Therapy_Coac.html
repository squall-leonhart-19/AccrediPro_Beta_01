Core Ethical Principles

At the heart of ethical practice lie several core principles. Understanding these is fundamental to making sound ethical decisions:

  • Autonomy: Respecting the client's right to make their own decisions (as appropriate for their developmental level).
  • Beneficence: Acting in the best interest of the client.
  • Non-maleficence: "First, do no harm." Avoiding actions that could cause harm.
  • Justice: Ensuring fairness and equity in the provision of services.
  • Fidelity: Maintaining trust and loyalty in the therapeutic relationship.

Limitations of Ethical Codes

While ethical codes provide valuable guidance, they are not exhaustive. They cannot cover every possible situation. This is where ethical reasoning comes in. Ethical reasoning involves critical thinking, consultation, and applying ethical principles to specific situations.

Remember, ethical codes are a starting point, not a substitute for thoughtful decision-making. Don't hesitate to consult with supervisors, mentors, or ethics experts when facing a challenging situation. This proactive approach reflects the "Listening & Limits" aspect of the P.L.A.Y.E.D. framework, ensuring you're attuned to potential ethical pitfalls and setting appropriate boundaries.

P.L.A.Y.E.D. Framework & Ethics

Let's explore how the P.L.A.Y.E.D. framework can guide ethical decision-making:

  • Presence & Perspective: Being fully present with the child and understanding their perspective is crucial for ethical assessment.
  • Listening & Limits: Actively listening to the child's needs while setting appropriate limits ensures their safety and well-being.
  • Assessment & Attunement: Ethically assessing the child's needs and attuning to their emotional state informs appropriate interventions.
  • Yielding & Validation: Yielding to the child's lead within ethical boundaries and validating their experiences fosters trust.
  • Empowerment & Expression: Empowering the child to express themselves ethically promotes autonomy and self-determination.
  • Development & Discussion: Considering the child's developmental stage and engaging in ethical discussions with caregivers ensures comprehensive care.

By integrating the P.L.A.Y.E.D. framework into your ethical reasoning, you create a holistic and child-centered approach to play therapy coaching.
