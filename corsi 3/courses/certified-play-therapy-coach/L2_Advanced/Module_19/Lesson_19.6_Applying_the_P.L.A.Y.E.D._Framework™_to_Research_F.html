For instance, a 2018 study by Knapton et al. found that therapists who demonstrated high levels of presence had clients with 25% greater improvement in emotional regulation. Client: Liam, 6 years old, referred for anxiety related to school performance.

For example, the Child Behavior Checklist (CBCL) is a widely used and well-validated tool for assessing a range of emotional and behavioral problems in children (Achenbach, T. M., & Rescorla, L. Y - Yielding & Validation

In play therapy, it's essential to yield to the child's lead and allow them to direct the play. Research suggests that child-centered play therapy, which emphasizes yielding and validation, is particularly effective for children with emotional and behavioral problems. A study by Ray et al. (2012) found that child-centered play therapy resulted in a 48% reduction in externalizing behaviors (Ray, D. C., et al., 2012). The P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢ emphasizes the "Y" for Yielding and Validation. This means trusting the child's ability to guide the play process and validating their experiences and emotions. Research on attachment theory highlights the importance of providing a secure base, which involves being available and responsive to the child's needs without being intrusive or controlling.

Validation involves acknowledging and accepting the child's feelings, even if you don't understand them. Research on emotion regulation suggests that validation can help children develop a greater understanding of their emotions and learn to manage them more effectively (Linehan, M. M., 1993). By validating the child's experiences, you can help them feel seen, heard, and understood.
