Case Study Analysis

Let's dive into a case study that showcases the power of integrating the P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢:

Case Study: Lily's Journey

Integrating P.L.A.Y.E.D. for Emotional Growth

L

Lily, Age 7

Presenting symptoms: Anxiety, difficulty expressing emotions, social withdrawal.

Lily, a 7-year-old girl, presented with anxiety, difficulty expressing her emotions, and social withdrawal. Her mother, Sarah, a 45-year-old single mom and teacher, sought support after noticing Lily's increasing distress at school.

Intervention: The play therapy coach utilized the P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢ in the following ways:

  • Presence: The coach created a safe and accepting environment, demonstrating full attention and empathy towards Lily's feelings.
  • Listening: The coach actively listened to Lily's verbal and non-verbal cues during play, paying close attention to the themes and emotions that emerged.
  • Assessment: Through play-based assessment techniques, the coach identified Lily's underlying anxieties and her difficulties in expressing anger and sadness.
  • Yielding: The coach followed Lily's lead in play, allowing her to explore her emotions at her own pace and in her own way.
  • Empowerment: The coach helped Lily develop coping strategies for managing her anxiety and encouraged her to express her emotions in a healthy and assertive manner.
  • Development: The coach worked with Lily to build her self-esteem, improve her social skills, and foster her emotional resilience.

Outcomes: Over the course of 12 play therapy sessions, Lily showed significant improvement in her anxiety levels, emotional expression, and social interactions. She became more confident, assertive, and able to manage her emotions effectively. Sarah also reported a significant decrease in Lily's anxiety at school and an improvement in their overall relationship. A follow-up assessment 3 months later indicated that Lily maintained her progress and continued to thrive.

Ethical Considerations

When integrating the different aspects of the P.L.A.Y.E.D. Frameworkâ„¢, it is crucial to be mindful of ethical considerations. These include maintaining confidentiality, respecting the client's autonomy, and avoiding dual relationships.

For example, if a child reveals information about abuse or neglect during play therapy coaching, you have a legal and ethical obligation to report it to the appropriate authorities. Similarly, if you are working with a child who has a diagnosed mental health condition, it is important to collaborate with their therapist or psychiatrist to ensure that your interventions are aligned with their overall treatment plan.
