Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Prioritization Frameworks

Introduce frameworks like the Pareto Principle (80/20 rule) to identify the most impactful areas to address first. What 20% of interventions will yield 80% of the results?

2

Client-Centered Goal Setting

Emphasize the importance of collaborative goal setting. What are Sarah's primary concerns and what does she hope to achieve in the short term?

3

The "Low-Hanging Fruit" Strategy

Suggest starting with simple, easily achievable interventions to build momentum and client confidence. This could be hydration, gentle movement, or sleep hygiene improvements.

4

Managing Client Expectations

Discuss the importance of setting realistic expectations and explaining the iterative nature of the wellness journey. Wellness is a marathon, not a sprint.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the Pareto Principle, and how can it be applied to client prioritization?

The Pareto Principle (80/20 rule) suggests that 80% of results come from 20% of efforts. In client prioritization, it means identifying the 20% of interventions that will likely yield 80% of the positive outcomes.

2. Why is it important to focus on building a new practitioner's confidence?

Confidence is crucial for new practitioners. It enables them to apply their knowledge effectively, build rapport with clients, and navigate challenges with resilience. A confident practitioner is more likely to take initiative and provide optimal care.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not just giving answers.
  • Use frameworks like the Pareto Principle to help mentees prioritize client issues.
  • Focus on building confidence and empowering mentees to make their own decisions.
  • Share your own experiences and mistakes to normalize the learning process.
  • Provide specific, actionable feedback to help mentees improve their skills.
