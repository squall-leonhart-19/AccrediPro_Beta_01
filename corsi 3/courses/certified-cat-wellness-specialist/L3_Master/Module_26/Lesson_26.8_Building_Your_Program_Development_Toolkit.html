Client Case Review

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 52, with chronic fatigue, anxiety, and some digestive upset. I recommended a gut-healing protocol with bone broth, L-glutamine, and a probiotic. After a week, Emily reports increased anxiety and insomnia. I‚Äôm not sure what to do next."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Was Emily assessed for histamine intolerance before recommending bone broth?
  • Could the probiotic be exacerbating SIBO or dysbiosis?
  • Did Sarah consider Emily's anxiety when making recommendations?
  • Is Sarah practicing within her scope by recommending specific supplements?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reframe the Situation

2

Assess for Histamine Intolerance

Explain that bone broth is high in histamine and can exacerbate anxiety and insomnia in sensitive individuals. A 2022 study in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition showed that high-histamine diets correlated with increased anxiety symptoms (p < 0.05) in 32% of participants with self-reported histamine intolerance.

3

Review Probiotic Considerations

Discuss how probiotics can sometimes worsen digestive symptoms or anxiety, especially in cases of SIBO or histamine intolerance. A 2023 meta-analysis in Gut Microbes found that 15% of patients with SIBO experienced increased bloating with certain probiotic strains.

4

Clarify Scope of Practice

Remind Sarah to stay within her scope. Recommending specific supplements may cross the line into medical advice, depending on local regulations. Focus on lifestyle and dietary guidance.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Positive Reinforcement

Ask Guiding Questions
"What are some conditions that might be triggered or worsened by bone broth? What other assessment tools could you use to gather more data?"
Offer Alternative Strategies
"Instead of focusing solely on bone broth and probiotics, what about exploring gentle dietary changes, stress-reduction techniques, or referral to a qualified therapist for anxiety management?"
Emphasize Scope of Practice

Check Your Understanding

1. When a mentee presents a challenging case, what's the first thing you should do?

Begin by acknowledging their effort and creating a safe space for discussion. Validate their concerns and emphasize that challenging cases are a normal part of the learning process.

2. How can you help a mentee understand the importance of scope of practice?

Discuss the legal and ethical boundaries of their certification, provide examples of appropriate and inappropriate recommendations, and encourage them to consult with experienced practitioners or legal professionals when in doubt.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through complex cases.
  • Assess for histamine intolerance and other potential contraindications.
  • Clarify the mentee's scope of practice and ensure they stay within it.
  • Provide constructive feedback and support their professional development.
  • Remember that every challenging case is an opportunity for growth.
