Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize the Importance of a Vet Check

Reinforce that ruling out medical causes (e.g., hypothyroidism, arthritis) is always the first step in addressing weight gain and lethargy in cats.

2

Discuss Gradual Dietary Changes

Explain that sudden dietary changes can cause digestive upset and decreased appetite. A gradual transition over 7-10 days is crucial.

3

Brainstorm Specific Playtime Activities

Help Sarah identify engaging activities tailored to Whiskers' age and physical limitations (e.g., laser pointer, puzzle feeders, short bursts of feather wand play).

4

Set Realistic Expectations

Guide Sarah on communicating that weight loss is a gradual process and requires consistent effort. Emphasize the importance of monitoring progress and adjusting the plan as needed.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Effort
"Sarah, I appreciate you bringing this case to me. It shows you're committed to providing the best care for your clients. Let's work through this together."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before I share my thoughts, what do you think might have contributed to Whiskers' lethargy? What would you do differently next time?"
Share Your Expertise
"In my experience, it's always best practice to recommend a vet visit first. This helps rule out any underlying medical issues that could be contributing to the symptoms. It‚Äôs also crucial to transition food gradually to avoid digestive upset."
Offer Support and Guidance
"How can I best support you in communicating these recommendations to Emily? Perhaps we can work on a script together or brainstorm some engaging playtime activities."
