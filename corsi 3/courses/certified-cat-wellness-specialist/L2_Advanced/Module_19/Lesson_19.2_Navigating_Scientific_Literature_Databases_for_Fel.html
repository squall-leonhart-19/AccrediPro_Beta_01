Introduction to Scientific Databases

Scientific databases are essential tools for accessing peer-reviewed research. Here are some key databases for feline health and behavior:

  • PubMed: A comprehensive database of biomedical literature, including veterinary science.
  • Web of Science: A multidisciplinary database covering a wide range of scientific journals.
  • CAB Abstracts: Focuses on agriculture, veterinary science, and related fields.
  • Google Scholar: A broad search engine for scholarly literature across various disciplines.

Each database offers unique features and search capabilities. Mastering these resources will significantly enhance your ability to find relevant and reliable information.

Effective Search Strategies

Effective searching involves using the right keywords and search operators. Hereâ€™s how to refine your search:

  • Keywords: Use specific terms related to your topic. For example, instead of "cat behavior," try "feline anxiety treatment."
  • Boolean Operators:
    • AND: Narrows your search (e.g., "feline" AND "obesity").
    • OR: Broadens your search (e.g., "cat" OR "feline").
    • NOT: Excludes terms (e.g.,"feline" NOT "kitten").
  • Filters: Use filters to refine your search by publication date, study type (e.g., randomized controlled trials, meta-analyses), and species.

For example, a search in PubMed for "feline hyperthyroidism treatment" limited to the last 5 years and randomized controlled trials will yield highly relevant and up-to-date results.

A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies (n=8,234) found that using Boolean operators and filters increased the precision of search results by 35%.

Accessing Full-Text Articles

Accessing full-text articles can sometimes be challenging. Here are several strategies:

  • Institutional Access: If you are affiliated with a university or research institution, you may have access through their library.
  • Open Access Journals: Many journals offer open access articles that are freely available. Examples include PLOS One and BMC Veterinary Research.
  • PubMed Central: A free archive of biomedical and life sciences literature.
  • Contacting Authors: If you cannot access an article, consider contacting the authors directly. Many researchers are happy to share their work.

Remember to check for open access options first, as this can save time and resources. Utilizing resources like PubMed Central can provide immediate access to a wealth of research.

Identifying Reputable Sources

Not all scientific publications are created equal. It's crucial to distinguish between reputable and predatory journals:

  • Peer Review: Reputable journals have a rigorous peer-review process.
  • Impact Factor: The impact factor is a measure of how often articles in a journal are cited. Higher impact factors generally indicate more reputable journals.
  • Journal Reputation: Check the journal's editorial board and publisher. Well-established journals are typically more reliable.
  • Predatory Journals: Be wary of journals that promise rapid publication with little or no peer review. These journals often charge high fees and may not provide quality research.
Feature Reputable Journals Predatory Journals
Peer Review Rigorous and thorough Minimal or non-existent
Impact Factor High or well-established Low or non-existent
Editorial Board Experts in the field Often lacking or with questionable credentials
Publication Fees Reasonable and transparent High and often hidden
Indexing Indexed in major databases (e.g., PubMed, Web of Science) Often not indexed

Citation Management Software

Citation management software can greatly simplify the process of organizing and citing research articles. Popular options include:

  • Zotero: A free, open-source option that integrates with web browsers and word processors.
  • Mendeley: A popular tool owned by Elsevier, offering both free and paid versions.
  • EndNote: A comprehensive citation management software, often used in academic settings.

These tools allow you to import citations, organize your library, and generate bibliographies in various citation styles (e.g., APA, MLA, Chicago). Using citation management software can save you significant time and effort when writing research papers or preparing presentations.

Case Study: Implementing Research in Practice

Case Study: Mitigating Stress in Shelter Cats

Client: Shelter environment for newly arrived cats.

Age: Varies. Presenting Symptoms: Increased stress, anxiety, and reduced appetite in newly arrived shelter cats.

Intervention:

  1. Literature Review: Conducted a search in PubMed and CAB Abstracts using keywords like "feline stress," "shelter environment," and "environmental enrichment."
  2. Implementation: Based on research findings, implemented environmental enrichment strategies, including providing hiding places, scratching posts, and interactive toys.
  3. Nutritional Support: Ensured access to palatable and nutritionally balanced food to encourage appetite.

Outcomes: After one month, observed a 40% reduction in stress-related behaviors (e.g., hiding, hissing) and a 25% increase in appetite among newly arrived cats. This demonstrates the practical application of research in improving feline welfare in a shelter setting.
