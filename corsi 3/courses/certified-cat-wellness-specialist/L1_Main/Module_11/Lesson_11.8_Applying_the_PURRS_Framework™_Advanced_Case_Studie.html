Chief Complaint

Background

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for reaching out. It's very common for cats, especially indoor cats, to exhibit these behaviors when they're not getting enough stimulation. It's wonderful that you're so attuned to Whiskers' needs. Many owners wouldn't even notice."
MARIA:
"I just feel bad. I love her so much, but I work all day. I'm afraid she's bored and lonely."
YOU:
"That's completely understandable. The good news is that even small changes to Whiskers' environment can make a big difference. We can work together to create a plan that fits your lifestyle and provides her with the enrichment she needs."
YOU:
"To start, I'd like to ask some questions about Whiskers' daily routine and her current environment. This will help us identify areas where we can introduce more enrichment. Does that sound good to you?"
MARIA:
"Yes, that sounds great. I'm ready to do whatever it takes."
YOU:
"Perfect. Let's begin with her feeding schedule and the types of toys she currently has. Then we can explore some simple and affordable ways to make her life more stimulating and enjoyable."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's a good first question to ask Maria about Whiskers' enrichment?

A great starting point is to ask about the types of toys Whiskers currently has and how often Maria plays with her. This provides a baseline for assessing her current level of enrichment.

2. What's a simple, low-cost enrichment option you could suggest?

Suggest creating a window perch where Whiskers can observe the outside world. This provides mental stimulation and satisfies her natural curiosity.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial when discussing a client's concerns about their cat's behavior.
  • Start with simple, low-cost enrichment options before suggesting expensive solutions.
  • Consider the client's lifestyle and time constraints when developing an enrichment plan.
  • Focus on addressing the cat's natural instincts and providing mental and physical stimulation.
