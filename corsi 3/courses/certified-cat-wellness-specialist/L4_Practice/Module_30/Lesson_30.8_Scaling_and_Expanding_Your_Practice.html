Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand your concern. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into busy schedules. The weekly coaching calls are only 30 minutes, and the daily activities can be integrated into your existing routine. Many of my clients find that the increased energy and reduced stress actually *save* them time in the long run. Plus, you'll have lifetime access to the materials."
"I need to compare other programs first."
"That's a smart approach, Susan. While you're comparing, consider what makes my program unique: the personalized approach, the focus on root causes, and the ongoing support community. I'm confident you'll see the value in what I offer. What specific aspects are you comparing, and what information would help you make a decision?"
"What if it doesn't work?"
"I understand your hesitation. I'm so confident in my program that I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you actively participate in the program and don't see results within the first 30 days, I'll provide additional support and resources. My goal is to ensure you and Mittens achieve the desired outcomes. Plus, I am always available to answer any questions or concerns you may have."
"It's a bit more than I was expecting to spend."
"I understand. Let's look at the value you're receiving. You're not just getting a program; you're getting personalized coaching, a customized plan, and a supportive community. Many of my clients find they save money on unnecessary vet visits and ineffective products. Think about the long-term benefits for Mittens' health and your peace of mind. We can also discuss a payment plan to make it more manageable."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Uncover Underlying Needs & Challenges, Position Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.

2. What is one effective way to handle the objection, "It's more than I was expecting to spend?"

Focus on the value they are receiving, discuss potential savings on vet visits and ineffective products, and/or offer a payment plan.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Understanding your prospect's underlying needs is essential for positioning your solution effectively.
  • Confidently handling objections builds trust and increases your conversion rate.
  • Presenting your pricing with clarity and conviction demonstrates your value.
  • Consistent client acquisition leads to a thriving and financially rewarding practice.
