Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you so much for reaching out. I understand you're looking for some guidance and support. To start, can you tell me a bit about what prompted you to schedule this call?"
YOU:
"It takes courage to acknowledge when we need help. I admire you for taking this step. How are you feeling about the process so far?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling anxious and disconnected. Can you describe what that feels like on a daily basis? What are some of the triggers?"
YOU:
"Have you tried any strategies or therapies in the past? What were the results?"
YOU:
"Imagine you wake up three months from now, and these issues are resolved. What does your life look like? How do you feel?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're seeking peace, purpose, and a deeper connection with your faith. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My faith-based coaching program helps women like you reconnect with their spiritual core and find clarity. We integrate biblical principles with practical strategies to manage anxiety and cultivate inner peace. It's a 12-week journey of self-discovery and transformation."
YOU:
"Clients often tell me they experience a renewed sense of hope and purpose within the first few weeks. They feel more grounded in their faith and better equipped to handle life's challenges."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you believe this program could be a good fit for you? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details and how we can move forward."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's about prioritizing what's most important. This program requires a few hours per week, but the long-term benefits ‚Äì increased peace, clarity, and purpose ‚Äì can transform your life. Where could you find that time if you knew it would lead to lasting change?"
"It's a big investment."
"It is an investment in yourself and your well-being. Think of it as an investment in your future happiness and fulfillment. What is that worth to you? Also, I do offer payment plans to make it more manageable."
"I need to pray about it."
"That's wonderful! I encourage you to seek guidance. In the meantime, would it be helpful if I sent you some additional information about the program that you can reflect on during your prayer time? I'm confident that if this is meant to be, the path will become clear."
"I've tried coaching before, and it didn't work."
"I'm sorry to hear that. Every coaching experience is different. My approach is unique because it integrates faith-based principles. We're not just addressing symptoms; we're seeking spiritual healing and transformation. I'm confident you'll find this experience to be different."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

Phase 1: Build Rapport, Phase 2: Understand the Problem, Phase 3: Present Your Approach, Phase 4: Close

2. How should you handle the objection, "I need to think about it?"

Ask clarifying questions to understand what specifically they need to think about (e.g., investment, time commitment). Offer additional information or resources.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for building a thriving practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you connect with potential clients and assess their needs.
  • Confidently address objections with empathy and provide valuable solutions.
  • Present your pricing with conviction and highlight the value you offer.
  • Consistent effort and effective sales techniques can lead to a rewarding income.
