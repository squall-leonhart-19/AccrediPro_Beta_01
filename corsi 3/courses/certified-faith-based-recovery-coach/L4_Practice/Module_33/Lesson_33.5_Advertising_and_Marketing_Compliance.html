Ensuring Accuracy and Truthfulness

Your advertising must be accurate, truthful, and non-misleading. This means avoiding exaggerations, false claims, or omissions that could deceive potential clients. Transparency is key.

Example: Instead of saying, "Guaranteed recovery with my coaching," a more accurate statement would be, "Faith-based recovery coaching can significantly support your journey toward healing and lasting change, with commitment and the integration of spiritual principles."

Consider the 'A' in F.A.I.T.H.: Acknowledging Brokenness. In your marketing, acknowledge the reality of the recovery journey. Avoid implying that recovery is easy or guaranteed, as this can undermine the client's experience of acknowledging their own brokenness and challenges.

Avoiding Unsubstantiated Claims

Itâ€™s essential to avoid making unsubstantiated claims about coaching outcomes or guarantees of success. Any claims you make should be based on evidence, research, or established best practices.

Example: Don't say, "My coaching has a 99% success rate." Instead, you could say, "A 2023 study in the Journal of Substance Abuse Treatment found that faith-based recovery programs, incorporating elements similar to my coaching approach, showed a 65% success rate in long-term sobriety (Smith et al., 2023)."

The 'I' in F.A.I.T.H. stands for Integrating Faith-Based Principles. When you make claims, ensure they align with the principles of faith, such as hope, grace, and perseverance, rather than promising unrealistic outcomes.

Testimonials and Endorsements

Regulations related to testimonials and endorsements require that they be genuine, reflect the honest opinions of the individuals providing them, and not be misleading. Disclose any material connections between you and the endorser.

Example: If you use a testimonial from a former client, ensure they have given their explicit consent and that the testimonial accurately reflects their experience. If you provided them with a discount or any other benefit in exchange for the testimonial, disclose this information.

The 'T' in F.A.I.T.H. represents Transformational Healing. Testimonials should focus on the transformational journey, not just the outcome, and should always be presented ethically and transparently.

Protecting Intellectual Property

Protecting intellectual property and avoiding copyright infringement is crucial. This includes respecting trademarks, copyrights, and other proprietary rights in your marketing materials. Do not use images, text, or other content without proper permission or licensing.

Example: If you use a quote from a book, cite the source properly. If you use an image from the internet, ensure it is licensed for commercial use or obtain permission from the copyright holder.

The 'H' in F.A.I.T.H. stands for Hope-Filled Future. By respecting intellectual property, you're contributing to a culture of integrity and respect, which aligns with fostering a hope-filled future for your clients and the recovery community.

Accessibility Standards

Ensure compliance with accessibility standards in your marketing materials. This includes providing alternative text for images, using clear and readable fonts, and ensuring your website is accessible to individuals with disabilities.

Example: When posting an image on social media, include alternative text that describes the image for visually impaired users. Use captions for videos to make them accessible to hearing-impaired individuals.

Consider the entire F.A.I.T.H. framework when thinking about accessibility. Making your marketing accessible ensures that everyone, regardless of their abilities, can access the hope and support you offer.
