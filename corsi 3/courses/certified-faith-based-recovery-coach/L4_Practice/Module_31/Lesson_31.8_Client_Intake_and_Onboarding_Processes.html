Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Heartfelt Connection 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so good to connect with you. I understand you found me through the church directory. What resonated with you that made you reach out?"
YOU:
"I'm so glad you did. Tell me, what's been weighing on your heart lately?"
YOU:
"It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings. I commend you for taking this step."
Phase 2: Uncovering the Need 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling lost. Can you describe what that feels like for you day-to-day?"
YOU:
"And what scriptures or spiritual practices have you found comfort in during challenging times?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and feel completely aligned with God's purpose, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 8 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is a deep desire to reconnect with your purpose and find joy in serving God again. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My faith-based coaching program is designed to help women like you rediscover their God-given gifts and create a life of meaning and fulfillment. Over 12 weeks, we'll explore scripture, prayer, and practical strategies to overcome anxiety and embrace your unique calling. Clients often report feeling a renewed sense of peace and direction within the first month."
YOU:
"It's a journey of faith and action, guided by biblical principles and personalized support."
Phase 4: Gentle Enrollment 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this resonate with you and what you're seeking? ... Wonderful. Let me share the details of the program and how we can begin."

Handling Objections

"That's a beautiful and wise approach, Susan. I encourage you to do so. In your prayers, consider what God is calling you to do with this season of your life. If it feels right, I'll be here to support you."

Check Your Understanding

1. How can you integrate faith into the discovery call without being pushy?

Focus on shared values, ask about their spiritual practices, and frame your coaching as a journey of faith and action.

2. What's a good way to address the "I need to pray about it" objection?

Encourage them to pray and consider what God is calling them to do. Offer support if they feel it's the right path.

Key Takeaways

  • Lead with heartfelt connection and empathy.
  • Uncover the client's deepest needs and desires.
  • Present your faith-based coaching as a personalized solution.
  • Handle objections with grace and understanding.
  • Confidently state your price and invite them to begin.
