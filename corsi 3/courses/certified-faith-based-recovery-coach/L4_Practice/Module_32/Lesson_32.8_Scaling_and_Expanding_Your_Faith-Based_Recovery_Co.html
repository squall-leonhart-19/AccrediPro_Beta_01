Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you're looking for guidance in finding a new purpose after retirement. I'm Grace, and I'm here to help you explore that."
YOU:
"Before we dive in, tell me a little about what retirement has been like for you so far. What are you enjoying, and what are you missing?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What does 'purpose' mean to you at this stage in your life? What would it look like if you were living a truly purposeful life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to find that purpose? Any volunteer work, hobbies, or new skills you've explored?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being completely fulfilled and 1 being utterly lost, where would you rate yourself right now? And what would it take to move up just one or two points?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're at a crossroads, seeking meaningful engagement but unsure where to start. Many of my clients in similar situations have found clarity and direction through our faith-based coaching program."
YOU:
"My approach is rooted in biblical principles and practical strategies. Over 12 weeks, we'll explore your God-given gifts, identify opportunities for service, and create a roadmap to a more purposeful life. We will integrate faith-based principles to help with your transformation, and address the brokenness that keeps you from your higher purpose."
YOU:
"I've seen women like you, who felt directionless, discover passions they never knew they had and start making a real impact in their communities. For example, Martha, a 60-year-old former teacher, started a literacy program at her church after our coaching, and now feels more alive than ever."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you see how this could help you find the purpose you're seeking? ... Wonderful! Let's discuss the program details and how we can begin."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand that time is precious, especially in retirement. However, think of this as an investment in your own well-being and fulfillment. The program requires just a few hours per week, and the payoff in terms of purpose and joy can be tremendous. Plus, we can tailor the schedule to fit your lifestyle."
"I don't know if I can afford it."
"I appreciate your honesty, Susan. Let's look at the value you'll receive. This isn't just about coaching; it's about rediscovering your passions, making a difference, and living a life aligned with your faith. Many of my clients find that the increased sense of purpose actually leads to new opportunities and even financial blessings. We also have payment plan options available to make it more manageable."
"I'm not sure this is right for me."
"That's perfectly okay to feel unsure. What specifically makes you hesitant? Is it the faith-based aspect, the time commitment, or something else? I want to address any concerns you have so you can make an informed decision. I'm happy to answer any questions or provide more information."
"Can I get back to you next week?"
"Of course! I want you to feel completely comfortable. What specific information would help you make your decision by next week? Perhaps I can send you some client testimonials or a detailed program outline? Also, I am offering a fast action bonus this week only, so signing up today would give you access to a free one-on-one session with me. Would you like to take advantage of that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle a prospect who says "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, ask what specifically they need to think about, and offer to provide additional information or schedule a follow-up call.

You're On Your Way!

Remember, every "no" brings you closer to a "yes." Keep practicing these skills, and you'll be confidently acquiring clients in no time. Your unique gifts and faith-based approach are exactly what many people are seeking.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you guide the conversation and identify if you are the right fit for a client.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence builds trust and increases conversion rates.
  • Presenting your pricing clearly and showcasing the value of your program is essential for closing the sale.
  • Consistent practice will make you feel more comfortable and confident during sales conversations.
  • Remember to show the client the value you bring to their life.
