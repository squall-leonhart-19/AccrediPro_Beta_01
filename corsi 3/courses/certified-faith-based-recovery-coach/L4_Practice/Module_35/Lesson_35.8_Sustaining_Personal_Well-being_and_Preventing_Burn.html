Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm just not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Life is busy! Many of my clients feel the same way initially. But think about it ‚Äì if you *don't* invest the time, where will you be in a year? This program is designed to help you create more time and energy by aligning your life with your values and priorities. It's an investment in yourself."
"I need to pray about it."
"That's wonderful, Susan. I encourage you to do that. I truly believe God guides us in making these decisions. Perhaps you could ask for clarity about whether this is the right step for you at this time. How about we schedule a brief follow-up call in a few days so you can share what you've discerned?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. Can you tell me what specifically makes you feel that way? I want to assure you that my program is tailored to each individual's unique needs and goals. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied after the first month, I'll refund your investment. I'm confident that if you commit to the process, you'll see results."
"Can I pay in installments?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are some key questions to ask during the "Explore Challenges & Desires" phase of the discovery call?

Examples include: "Can you tell me more about that?" "What does that look like in your daily life?" "How has your relationship with God been feeling lately?" "If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like in six months?"

2. How can you address the objection, "I'm just not sure if I have the time?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment of time as an opportunity to create more time and energy by aligning their life with their values and priorities.

3. What are some components to include when presenting your pricing?

Clearly state the price, list inclusions, and offer a payment plan if possible. Highlight the value and potential ROI of the program.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and connecting on a personal level is crucial for establishing trust with potential clients.
  • Asking open-ended questions helps uncover the prospect's true challenges and desires.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can turn hesitation into commitment.
  • Clearly and confidently presenting your pricing is essential for conveying the value of your services.
  • Practice closing lines to confidently invite prospects to take the next step.
