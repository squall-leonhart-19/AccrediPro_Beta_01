Former teacher, strong understanding of faith-based principles, limited coaching experience.

Excellent listener, compassionate, dedicated to her faith.

Her Question
"I'm working with a client who is struggling with resentment towards God. How can I best support her faith journey?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"My client, Emily, 52, is a devout Christian who feels abandoned by God after experiencing a relapse in her addiction. She questions her faith and feels deep resentment. I've been encouraging her to pray and read scripture, but it doesn't seem to be helping."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah explored the root of Emily's resentment beyond the relapse?
  • Is she validating Emily's feelings or just offering solutions?
  • Is Sarah equipped to handle the theological complexities of Emily's struggle?
  • Is there a potential need for referral to a pastoral counselor or therapist?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Empathy and Validation First

Remind Sarah that before offering solutions, she needs to validate Emily's feelings of anger and disappointment. Unacknowledged emotions can block spiritual growth.

2

Explore the 'Why' Behind the Resentment

Guide Sarah to explore what Emily believes about God's role in her life and recovery. What are her expectations? Are they realistic and biblically sound?

3

Introduce the Concept of Lament

Explain that lament is a biblically valid expression of grief and disappointment with God. Encourage Sarah to explore lament with Emily, using Psalms as a guide.

4

Know Your Scope of Practice

Help Sarah recognize when Emily's struggles require more in-depth theological or psychological intervention. Discuss the importance of referral to a qualified professional.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's wonderful that you're seeking guidance on this case. It shows your commitment to your clients and your desire to grow as a coach. You're on the right track."
Ask Before Advising
"Before I offer my suggestions, what have you already considered in terms of addressing Emily's resentment? What scriptures or principles have you thought about using?"
Introduce a New Perspective
"One concept that can be helpful in these situations is the idea of 'holy discontent' ‚Äì the idea that it's okay to wrestle with God and express our pain. The Psalms are full of examples of this. It's not about doubting God, but about being honest with Him."
Guide Towards Action
"Based on what we've discussed, what feels like the next best step with Emily? How can you create a safe space for her to express her feelings without judgment?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of validating a client's emotions before offering solutions?

Validating emotions creates a safe space for clients to express themselves and feel understood, which is essential for building trust and facilitating deeper exploration.

2. How can you help a new coach understand their scope of practice in complex cases?

Encourage them to reflect on their training, identify their limitations, and seek supervision or consultation when needed. Provide resources for professional development and ethical guidelines.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Empathy and validation are crucial in mentoring.
  • Encourage critical thinking and self-reflection.
  • Know your scope of practice and ethical boundaries.
  • Celebrate growth and learning in your mentees.
