The Scenario: A Faith-Based Dilemma

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 52, who is struggling with alcohol addiction and feels disconnected from her faith. I want to help her reconnect, but I don't want to push my beliefs on her. She told me she was raised Catholic but hasn't been to church in years. How do I gently integrate faith without making her feel judged or uncomfortable?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • What are Emily's specific spiritual needs and preferences?
  • How can Sarah create a safe space for Emily to explore her faith on her own terms?
  • What faith-based resources can Sarah offer without being prescriptive?
  • How can Sarah maintain ethical boundaries and respect Emily's autonomy?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Client-Centered Faith Integration

Remind Sarah that the goal is to support Emily's spiritual journey, not to impose her own beliefs. Faith integration should always be driven by the client's needs and preferences.

2

Teach the Art of Gentle Inquiry

Help Sarah develop open-ended questions to explore Emily's spiritual background, beliefs, and desires. For example: "What role has faith played in your life in the past?" or "What does spirituality mean to you now?"

3

Offer Resources, Not Prescriptions

Suggest that Sarah provide Emily with a variety of faith-based resources (e.g., books, podcasts, guided meditations) without telling her what to do. Empower Emily to choose what resonates with her.

4

Reinforce Ethical Boundaries

Stress the importance of respecting Emily's autonomy and avoiding any form of spiritual coercion. Sarah should never pressure Emily to adopt her beliefs or practices.

Crafting Your Feedback

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Sarah's Enthusiasm
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about Emily and want to help her reconnect with her faith. Your passion is a real asset."
Address the Risk of Imposition
"It's easy to want to share what's meaningful to us, but we have to be careful not to impose our beliefs. Remember, it's about guiding, not directing."
Offer a Reframing Strategy
"Instead of thinking, 'How can I get Emily back to church?', try asking, 'What does Emily need to feel spiritually supported?' Let her answer guide you."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on what we've discussed, what's one small step you could take in your next session with Emily to gently explore her faith without pressure?"

Key Takeaways

  • Client-centered faith integration is paramount: always prioritize the client's needs and preferences.
  • Gentle inquiry is more effective than prescriptive advice. Ask open-ended questions to explore the client's spiritual background.
  • Offer a variety of faith-based resources without telling the client what to do.
  • Reinforce ethical boundaries: avoid any form of spiritual coercion.
  • Provide supportive feedback: acknowledge their enthusiasm and reframe challenges as learning opportunities.
