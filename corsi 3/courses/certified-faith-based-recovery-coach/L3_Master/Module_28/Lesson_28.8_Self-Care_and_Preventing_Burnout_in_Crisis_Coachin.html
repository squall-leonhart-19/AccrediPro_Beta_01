The Case Presented

Sarah's Client: Emily, 48

What Sarah Tells You:

"Emily, 48, is struggling with alcohol use and has a history of childhood trauma. She's experiencing panic attacks and flashbacks. I want to incorporate her faith, but I'm afraid of triggering her trauma or doing more harm than good. I don't know where to start."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Has Sarah assessed Emily's current mental state and safety?
  • Does Sarah understand the potential impact of spiritual practices on trauma survivors?
  • Is Sarah aware of the ethical considerations regarding dual relationships and scope of practice?
  • Does Sarah have a clear plan for managing potential crises?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritize Safety and Stabilization

Emphasize the importance of assessing Emily's immediate safety and mental state. Discuss strategies for managing panic attacks and flashbacks, such as grounding techniques and safe space exercises. Reinforce that safety is the foundation for all other interventions.

2

Trauma-Informed Faith Integration

Explain that integrating faith with trauma requires sensitivity and caution. Discuss how certain spiritual practices (e.g., prayer, scripture reading) could be triggering for some individuals. Teach Sarah how to adapt faith-based interventions to be safe and empowering for trauma survivors.

3

Ethical Boundaries and Scope of Practice

Review the ethical guidelines related to dual relationships, confidentiality, and scope of practice. Clarify that Sarah should not provide therapy or counseling outside her certification. Discuss the importance of referring Emily to qualified mental health professionals for trauma-specific treatment.

4

Crisis Management Planning

Help Sarah develop a crisis management plan that includes identifying warning signs, establishing clear communication protocols, and knowing when and how to involve emergency services. Emphasize the importance of documenting all interventions and consultations.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Sarah's Concerns
"Sarah, I commend you for recognizing the complexities of this case and seeking guidance. It shows you're committed to ethical and responsible practice. It's wise to be cautious when trauma is involved."
Explore Her Understanding
"Before we dive in, can you tell me what you understand about the potential impact of trauma on spiritual practices? What are some of your concerns in this area?"
Provide Guidance and Resources
"One helpful approach is to focus on strength-based interventions that empower Emily and promote resilience. We can explore scripture that emphasizes hope and healing, but always with her consent and comfort level. Also, remember resources like the SAMHSA's National Helpline can provide additional support."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Given what we've discussed, what specific steps will you take to ensure Emily's safety and well-being? How will you balance faith integration with trauma sensitivity? I'm here to support you in developing a plan that feels both ethical and effective."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the first priority when supervising a new coach working with a client who has a history of trauma?

Ensuring the client's immediate safety and mental stabilization. This includes assessing for suicidal ideation, self-harm behaviors, and current mental state.

2. How should a faith-based recovery coach approach incorporating spiritual practices with a client who has experienced trauma?

With sensitivity and caution. It's important to assess the client's comfort level and potential triggers associated with specific spiritual practices. Adapt interventions to be trauma-informed and empowering.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize client safety and stabilization above all else.
  • Integrate faith with trauma in a sensitive and informed manner.
  • Adhere to ethical guidelines and scope of practice.
  • Provide ongoing support and guidance to new coaches.
  • Celebrate their growth and progress as practitioners.
