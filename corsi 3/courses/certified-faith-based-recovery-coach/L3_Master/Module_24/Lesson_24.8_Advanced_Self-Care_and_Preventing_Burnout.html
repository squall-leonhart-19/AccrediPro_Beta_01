Guiding Principles for Mentoring

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Self-Care as Ethical Practice

Explain that burnout helps no one. A depleted practitioner is less effective and more prone to poor judgment. Self-care isn't selfish; it‚Äôs essential.

2

Review Boundary Setting Techniques

Discuss specific strategies: setting clear communication hours, using an auto-responder, referring to crisis lines, and role-playing difficult conversations.

3

Promote Empowerment, Not Enabling

Help Sarah see that immediate responses can inadvertently reinforce dependence. The goal is to equip clients with tools to manage crises independently.

4

Offer Practical Self-Care Suggestions

Encourage Sarah to establish a non-negotiable self-care routine: prayer, meditation, exercise, hobbies, or spending time with loved ones. Lead by example and share your own practices.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is self-care an ethical imperative for faith-based recovery coaches?

Burnout diminishes effectiveness and can lead to impaired judgment, ultimately harming clients. Self-care ensures practitioners are equipped to provide the best possible support.

2. What is the difference between empathy and responsibility in the context of client care?

Empathy is understanding and sharing a client's feelings. Responsibility is taking ownership of their problems. Coaches should be empathetic but avoid assuming responsibility for clients' outcomes.
