Sample Consultation Script

Here's a suggested script to guide your initial conversation. Practice saying these words aloud to build confidence:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to reach out, and I want to assure you that you're not alone in feeling this way. Many people experience similar challenges at this stage of life."
MARIA:
"It's just hard. I feel lost and don't know where to turn."
YOU:
"I understand. It sounds like you're experiencing a significant transition. Before we move forward, I want to clarify what faith-based recovery coaching is and what it isn't. Are you open to that?"
MARIA:
"Yes, please. I'm not really sure what to expect."
YOU:
"Okay. Faith-based recovery coaching is about integrating your spiritual beliefs and values into the recovery process. We'll explore how your faith can be a source of strength and guidance as you navigate these challenges. It's important to note that I won't impose my beliefs on you, but rather help you explore your own spiritual path."
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, it seems like focusing on reconnecting with your faith, finding new sources of purpose, and building meaningful relationships could be beneficial. Does that resonate with you?"
MARIA:
"Yes, definitely. Those are the things I'm longing for."

Use your phone to record your practice session. Listen back, paying attention to your pacing, tone, and overall confidence.

4

Adapt the script to reflect your unique voice and style. Maintain the structure, but use your own words and expressions.

Signs of a Successful Consultation

  • Maria expresses feeling understood and validated.
  • She asks clarifying questions about the coaching process.
  • Her body language indicates a sense of trust and openness.
  • She articulates a clear desire to move forward with coaching.
  • She expresses hope for positive change and renewed purpose.

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary goal of an initial consultation?

To establish rapport, understand the client's needs, and determine if coaching is a good fit.

2. Why is it important to clarify what faith-based recovery coaching is and isn't?

To manage expectations and ensure the client understands the scope and limitations of the coaching relationship.
