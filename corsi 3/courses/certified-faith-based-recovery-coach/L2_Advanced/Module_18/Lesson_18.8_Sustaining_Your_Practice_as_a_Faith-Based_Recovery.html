Clinical Reasoning

Step-by-Step Analysis

1. Identify Core Issues
The core issues are relapse of AUD, spiritual distress, and compromised mental health. The relapse is not an isolated event but intertwined with her emotional, relational, and spiritual well-being.
2. Uncover Triggers
The triggers for relapse are multifactorial: increased job stress, family dynamics, and emotional burden of her motherâ€™s illness. There is a correlation between life stressors and the return to maladaptive coping mechanisms.
3. Assess Spiritual State
Eleanor experiences significant spiritual distress, feeling alienated from her faith due to guilt and questioning God's forgiveness. This spiritual disconnection exacerbates her emotional and psychological distress.
4. Formulate Integrated Approach
An integrated approach must address her AUD, mental health, relational issues, and spiritual needs. This requires a collaborative effort involving medical, psychological, and spiritual support.

Differential Considerations

Considering Other Factors

1

Co-occurring Mental Health Disorders

Evaluate for other underlying mental health conditions like generalized anxiety disorder, major depressive disorder, or PTSD, which may exacerbate AUD and spiritual distress.

2

Hormonal Imbalances

Consider perimenopause or other hormonal imbalances, which can affect mood, anxiety, and alcohol cravings in women in their 40s.

3

Relational Trauma

Explore past relational trauma or unresolved grief, which may contribute to current emotional distress and relapse triggers.

4

Nutritional Deficiencies

Assess for nutritional deficiencies commonly associated with chronic alcohol use, such as B vitamins, magnesium, and zinc, which can impact mood and cognitive function.

Referral Triggers

When to Seek Medical or Psychiatric Support

  • Severe withdrawal symptoms: Confusion, hallucinations, seizures require immediate medical attention.
  • Suicidal ideation or intent: Immediate psychiatric evaluation and intervention.
  • Unstable medical conditions: Liver disease, cardiovascular issues necessitate medical management.
  • Lack of response to initial interventions: If no progress is observed within a reasonable timeframe, consider referral for intensive treatment or psychiatric evaluation.
