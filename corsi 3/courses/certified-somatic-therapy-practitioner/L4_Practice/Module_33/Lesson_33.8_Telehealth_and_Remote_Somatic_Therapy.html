Discovery Call Script: Focusing on Legal & Compliance

30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Introduction & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Maya Chen, and I'm a Certified Somatic Therapy Practitioner. I appreciate you taking the time to explore how somatic therapy can help you manage stress. To start, tell me what prompted you to reach out today?"
YOU:
"I'm glad you attended the workshop! It's great to hear you're interested in somatic therapy. Before we dive deeper, do you have any initial questions about my qualifications or the therapy process?"
Phase 2: Understanding Needs & Addressing Concerns (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"I understand you're dealing with high stress at work. Can you describe how this stress manifests in your body? What sensations or patterns do you notice?"
YOU:
"Many of my clients are concerned about confidentiality. I want to assure you that I adhere to strict ethical guidelines and legal requirements regarding client privacy. Everything we discuss is confidential, and I follow HIPAA regulations to protect your personal information. I also have a detailed privacy policy available for your review. Does this address your concern?"
YOU:
"Have you experienced any form of therapy before? What are your expectations from our sessions?"
Phase 3: Presenting Somatic Therapy & Legal Compliance (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Somatic therapy focuses on the connection between your mind and body to release stored stress and trauma. We'll work together to identify and address the root causes of your stress through gentle movement, breathwork, and mindful awareness. My approach is personalized, and I prioritize creating a safe and supportive environment for you."
YOU:
"To ensure ethical and legal compliance, I always obtain informed consent before starting therapy. This means I will provide you with a clear explanation of the therapy process, potential risks and benefits, your rights as a client, and my professional responsibilities. You'll have the opportunity to ask questions and make an informed decision. I'm also fully insured and adhere to the ethical guidelines of the Somatic Therapy Association."
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe somatic therapy could be a great fit for you. Would you like to learn more about my program and how we can work together?"
Phase 4: Call to Action & Next Steps (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"If you're ready to move forward, I offer a 12-week program designed to help you reduce stress, improve work-life balance, and regain a sense of calm and control. The program includes weekly sessions, personalized exercises, and ongoing support. Are you interested in learning more?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Ethical Responses

"I'm concerned about the cost."
"I understand. Investing in your well-being is a significant decision. Let's discuss the value you'll receive from the program. Many of my clients find that the long-term benefits, such as reduced stress and improved health, outweigh the financial investment. I also offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure if somatic therapy is right for me."
"That's perfectly understandable. Somatic therapy is a unique approach, and it's not for everyone. I encourage you to do some research and explore testimonials from other clients. I'm also happy to provide you with additional information and answer any questions you have. Ultimately, the decision is yours, and I want you to feel confident in your choice."
"I need to talk to my spouse before making a decision."
"Absolutely. It's important to involve your partner in this decision. I can provide you with a summary of our conversation and answer any questions they may have. We can also schedule a brief call together to address their concerns directly."
"What happens if I don't see results?"
"That's a valid question. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I am committed to providing you with the best possible care. We'll regularly assess your progress and adjust the therapy plan as needed. If you're not satisfied with your progress, we can discuss alternative approaches or refer you to other qualified professionals. My priority is your well-being and satisfaction."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key legal and ethical considerations to address during a discovery call with a potential somatic therapy client?

Key considerations include confidentiality, informed consent, data privacy, adherence to ethical guidelines, and professional insurance. It's crucial to address these concerns transparently and provide clear information to the client.

2. How can you confidently present your pricing while addressing potential client objections related to cost?

Present your pricing clearly and confidently, emphasizing the value and benefits of your program. Address cost objections by highlighting the long-term benefits, offering payment plans, and showcasing testimonials from satisfied clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize ethical and legal compliance in all client interactions.
  • Address client concerns about confidentiality and data privacy proactively.
  • Present your pricing confidently and communicate the value of your services.
  • Handle objections with empathy and provide informative responses.
  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and professionalism.
