Discovery Call Script for Group Program

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, so glad we could connect! I understand you're interested in my upcoming group program. To start, can you tell me a bit about what's bringing you here today?"
YOU:
"Thanks for sharing that, Susan. I know how challenging it can be juggling work, family, and personal well-being. What's been the hardest part lately?"
Phase 2: Explore Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"When you feel overwhelmed, what does that look like for you? Are you experiencing physical symptoms, emotional changes, or shifts in your relationships?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you could change one thing about your current situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Introduce Group Program 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for practical strategies and a supportive community to help you manage stress and reclaim your well-being. Is that right?"
YOU:
"That's exactly what my 'Calm & Connected' group program offers. Over 8 weeks, we'll explore somatic techniques for stress reduction, mindfulness practices, and strategies for building resilience. Plus, you'll connect with a group of like-minded women who understand what you're going through."
YOU:
"My clients often tell me that the group support is just as valuable as the techniques themselves. It's a safe space to share, learn, and grow."
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on our conversation, I think the 'Calm & Connected' group program could be a great fit for you. Would you like to learn more about the program structure, investment, and start date?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for a group program."
"I understand. The sessions are [duration] per week, and they're designed to fit into busy schedules. Many of my clients find that dedicating this time to themselves actually increases their overall productivity and well-being. Plus, all sessions are recorded, so you can catch up if you miss one."
"I'm not comfortable sharing personal information in a group setting."
"That's perfectly normal. I create a safe and supportive environment where everyone feels comfortable sharing at their own pace. You're never pressured to share anything you don't want to. Many clients find that hearing other people's stories is incredibly validating and helpful."
"I've tried other programs before, and they didn't work."
"I hear you. What makes my program different is the focus on somatic techniques, which help you connect with your body and release stress at a deeper level. We're not just talking about stress; we're actively working with your nervous system to create lasting change. Plus, the group dynamic provides ongoing support and accountability."
"It's more than I wanted to spend."
"I understand budget is important. Let's look at what you're investing in. You're getting 8 weeks of training, personalized feedback, access to a supportive community, and lifetime access to the recordings. Many of my clients tell me they spend more than this on coffee or takeout each month. This is an investment in your long-term well-being, not just a quick fix."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key benefits of offering a group program versus individual sessions?

Increased income potential, expanded reach, and creation of a supportive community.

2. How can you address a potential client's concern about sharing personal information in a group setting?

Emphasize the safe and supportive environment you create, and assure them they are never pressured to share more than they are comfortable with. Highlight the value of hearing other people's stories.
