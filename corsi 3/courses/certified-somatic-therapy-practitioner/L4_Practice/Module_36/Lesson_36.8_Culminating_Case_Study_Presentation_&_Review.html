Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Intention 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about you. To start, what inspired you to reach out today?"
YOU:
"I appreciate you sharing that. What are you hoping to get out of this call?"
Phase 2: Uncovering Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about feeling stuck. What does that look and feel like in your day-to-day life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address this feeling of burnout, and what were the results?"
YOU:
"If you could design your ideal career, what would it look like? What values are most important to you in your work?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're seeking a career that's both fulfilling and aligned with your values, and you're looking for a clear path to get there. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My Somatic Therapy Practitioner certification program is designed to help people just like you. It provides the training, tools, and support to transition into a meaningful and flexible career. It will allow you to use the EMBODY method to connect with your clients. Over 12 weeks, we'll work together to explore your values, identify your strengths, and develop a plan to create a career you love. Many of my clients start seeing results within the first few weeks, gaining clarity and confidence."
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I genuinely believe this program can help you achieve your goals. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been searching for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the program details and how we can get started. We can schedule an onboarding call to get you started as soon as possible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, uncovering challenges, presenting your solution, and inviting the prospect to take the next step.

2. How should you handle a prospect who says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, and ask what specifically they need to think about, offering additional information or support.
