Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand. Many of my clients have invested in multiple treatments before finding me. Let's look at it this way: what is it costing you to stay in pain? Consider the lost productivity, medical bills, and impact on your quality of life. My program is an investment in your long-term well-being. We also have flexible payment options available."
"I've tried so many things already. I'm not sure if this will be any different."
"I hear you. It's frustrating to feel like you've exhausted all options. Somatic therapy is different because it addresses the root cause of the pain, not just the symptoms. We work with your nervous system to release stored tension and trauma, allowing your body to heal. I encourage you to read some of the client testimonials on my website. They share how somatic therapy has helped them when nothing else did."
"I need to think about it."
"That's perfectly fine. It's an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision."
"Do you have any scientific evidence to support this approach?"
"Absolutely! Somatic therapy is based on principles of neuroscience and mind-body connection. There are studies showing its effectiveness for chronic pain, trauma, and other conditions. I can send you some research articles if you'd like. For example, a 2021 study in the Journal of Bodywork and Movement Therapies found that somatic therapy significantly reduced pain and improved quality of life in participants with chronic pain."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Uncover Pain Points, Present Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I'm not sure if I can afford it?"

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the investment as a cost of staying in pain, and offer flexible payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Understanding your prospect's pain points is essential for presenting a relevant solution.
  • Practice handling common objections with confidence and empathy.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value of your program.
  • Remember, you are providing a valuable service that can transform lives.
