The Case Sarah Presents

Client Case Review

Sarah's Description:

"I'm working with Emily, 52, who has a history of childhood trauma. She freezes up during sessions, and I'm not sure how to help her regulate. I'm afraid of retraumatizing her."

Points for Exploration:

  • Is Sarah using appropriate titration techniques?
  • Is she co-regulating effectively with Emily?
  • What are Emily's specific triggers and coping mechanisms?
  • Is Sarah aware of her own countertransference?

Teaching Approach

Key Teaching Points

1

Emphasize Titration

Explain the importance of titration: introducing small, manageable pieces of the trauma narrative to avoid overwhelming the client. Focus on sensation and present moment awareness.

2

Reinforce Co-Regulation

Explain how the therapist's calm, grounded presence can help the client regulate. Discuss techniques like mirroring and attunement. A 2019 study in the Journal of Trauma & Dissociation highlights the role of co-regulation in trauma recovery (n=68, p < 0.01) [Reference: Porges, S. W. (2019). "The Polyvagal Theory: A neurophysiological perspective on psychological trauma." Journal of Trauma & Dissociation.]

3

Address Countertransference

Help Sarah identify any personal reactions or feelings that arise during sessions with Emily. Discuss the importance of self-care and setting boundaries to avoid burnout. A meta-analysis of 32 studies found that therapist self-care significantly reduces burnout rates (OR = 0.62, 95% CI [0.48, 0.81]) [Reference: Stamm, B. H. (2010). "The Concise ProQOL Manual." ProQOL.org.]

4

Explore Resources

Recommend resources for Sarah to deepen her understanding of trauma-informed care, such as books, workshops, and consultations with experienced trauma therapists.

Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Sarah, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when working with complex trauma. The fact that you're seeking supervision shows your commitment to ethical and effective practice."
Inquire About Her Process
"Tell me more about what happens when Emily freezes up. What are you doing in those moments? What sensations do YOU notice in your body?"
Offer a Somatic Technique
"Have you tried using a grounding exercise with Emily when she freezes? Something simple like noticing her feet on the floor or her hands in her lap. This can help her reconnect with the present moment."
Empower Next Steps
"What feels like the next best step with Emily? What small experiment could you try in your next session? I'm here to support you in whatever you choose."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address countertransference in supervision?

Addressing countertransference helps the mentee become aware of their own emotional responses to clients, preventing potential harm and enhancing therapeutic effectiveness.

2. What are some effective ways to validate a mentee's feelings of overwhelm?

Acknowledge the difficulty of the work, normalize feelings of overwhelm, and express confidence in their ability to learn and grow.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is crucial for developing competent and ethical somatic therapists.
  • Focus on titration and co-regulation when working with trauma.
  • Address countertransference to promote self-awareness and prevent burnout.
  • Validation, encouragement, and empowerment are key elements of effective supervision.
