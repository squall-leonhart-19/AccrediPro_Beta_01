Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on observing impulses. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, it sounds like you're caught in a really frustrating cycle. Thank you for being so open about this. Let's start by exploring these shopping impulses themselves. What does it feel like *right before* you make a purchase?"
MARIA:
"It's like... this pull. I see something, and I just *have* to have it. My heart races a little, and I get this little thrill."
YOU:
"That's a very clear description. Now, let's try something. The next time you feel that 'pull,' I want you to simply observe it, without judgment. Can you describe what the physical sensations are in your body? Don‚Äôt try to stop it, just notice."
MARIA:
"Just... notice? That sounds too simple. Will that even work?"
YOU:
"It might surprise you. The goal isn't to *stop* the impulse immediately, but to create space between the impulse and your reaction. By observing, you gain awareness, and awareness gives you choice. We're building your 'pause button.' How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"Okay, I'm willing to try. So, next time, I just... watch the feeling?"
YOU:
"Exactly. Become a scientist observing your own experiment. Notice the sensations, the thoughts, the emotions that arise. Then, we'll debrief. Ready to experiment?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to avoid judgment when guiding a client to observe their impulses?

Judgment can create shame and resistance, making it harder for the client to honestly explore their experience and build awareness.

2. What is the primary goal of having a client observe their impulses, rather than immediately trying to suppress them?

The goal is to create space between the impulse and the reaction, fostering awareness and choice, and preventing automatic, habitual behaviors.

Key Takeaways

  • Observing impulses without judgment is a powerful tool for building awareness.
  • Focus on the physical sensations associated with the impulse.
  • Help clients create space between the impulse and their reaction.
  • Connect the impulse to underlying emotions and needs.
  • Emphasize that this is a process, not an immediate fix.
