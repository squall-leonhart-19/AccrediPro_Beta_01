Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on befriending the body. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're wanting to reconnect with your body in a more positive way. Many women experience this feeling of disconnect, especially during life transitions. Let's explore how we can help you befriend your body again."

"It's hard. I look in the mirror and just see wrinkles and extra weight. I feel like my body is betraying me."
YOU:
"I understand. Those feelings are valid. But what if we could shift your perspective? What if we could focus on what your body *can* do, instead of what it looks like? What if we could cultivate gratitude for its resilience?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's negative feelings about their body before suggesting solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport. It shows that you understand their experience and aren't just dismissing their concerns.

2. Give an example of a sensory-based exercise you could suggest to help a client connect with their body.

A simple exercise is mindful breathing, focusing on the sensation of the breath entering and leaving the body. Another is a body scan meditation, bringing awareness to different parts of the body without judgment.

Key Takeaways

  • Focus on befriending the body *before* pursuing physical changes.
  • Validate your client's feelings without judgment.
  • Introduce the concept of body attunement to increase awareness.
  • Suggest small, sensory-based exercises to foster connection.
  • Tailor your approach to each client's unique history and preferences.
