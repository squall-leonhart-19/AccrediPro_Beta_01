Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you've been through a lot lately. I want to acknowledge your courage in seeking support and taking steps towards feeling better."
MARIA:
"Thank you. It's been hard, and I feel like I'm just going through the motions."
YOU:
"I understand. Many of my clients experience something similar, especially during major life transitions. We can work together to help you reconnect with your body and find a sense of calm amidst the chaos. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds wonderful. I'm willing to try anything."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start by exploring how stress is showing up in your body. Can you describe any physical sensations you've been experiencing?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses a sense of relief: "It's good to know I'm not alone."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's experience before offering solutions?

Validating their experience builds trust and rapport, making them feel heard and understood. This creates a safe space for deeper exploration and collaboration.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is feeling safe and engaged in the session?

Relaxed posture, eye contact, nodding, softer tone of voice, and open body language (uncrossed arms) are all positive indicators.

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge and validate the client's experience before offering solutions.
  • Create a safe and supportive space for them to share their feelings and sensations.
  • Use clear, accessible language and avoid jargon.
  • Check in with the client regularly to ensure they feel heard and understood.
  • Remember that somatic therapy focuses on the connection between mind and body.
