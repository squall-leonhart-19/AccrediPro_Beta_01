Clinical Reasoning: A Somatic Approach

Applying Somatic Resonance to Autoimmunity

Step 1: Acknowledge the Complexity
Eleanor's case presents a complex interplay of autoimmune conditions, chronic pain, medication side effects, and significant emotional distress. A reductionist approach focusing solely on lab values will likely be insufficient. Somatic Resonance helps us understand how these factors interact and manifest in her lived experience.
Step 2: Identify Key Somatic Patterns
Notice the patterns: restricted breathing (fight-or-flight activation), guarded posture (protection against pain), numbness (dissociation from the body), and heightened startle response (hypervigilance). These patterns reflect a nervous system stuck in a chronic state of defense.
Step 3: Uncover the Emotional-Somatic Connection
Explore the emotional themes associated with these patterns. The "stuck" feeling may relate to suppressed emotions or unresolved trauma. The numbness could be a protective mechanism against overwhelming pain or emotional intensity. Gentle inquiry, using techniques like Focusing or Somatic Experiencing, can help Eleanor connect with these underlying emotions.
Step 4: Facilitate Somatic Resonance
Use techniques like pendulation (alternating between safe and challenging sensations) and titration (gradually increasing exposure to difficult sensations) to help Eleanor gently explore and release these patterns. Encourage her to notice subtle shifts in sensation, breath, and emotional state. The goal is to help her reclaim a sense of agency and connection to her body.

Differential Considerations

Ruling Out Other Possibilities

1

Medication Side Effects

Methotrexate and Omeprazole can contribute to fatigue, digestive issues, and nutrient deficiencies. It's crucial to work collaboratively with her physician to assess the risk-benefit ratio of these medications.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Autoimmune diseases and medications can impair nutrient absorption. Low Vitamin D is common in autoimmune conditions. Consider testing for other deficiencies (B12, magnesium, iron) and addressing them through diet and supplementation.

3

Adrenal Dysfunction

Chronic stress can lead to adrenal fatigue, exacerbating fatigue and anxiety. Evaluate her stress response through salivary cortisol testing and support adrenal function with adaptogenic herbs and lifestyle modifications.

4

Underlying Infections

Rule out chronic infections (Lyme disease, EBV, mold toxicity) that can trigger or worsen autoimmune symptoms. Consider comprehensive testing if clinically indicated.

Referral Triggers

When to Collaborate with an MD

  • Sudden worsening of joint pain or swelling, especially with fever or signs of infection.
  • Unexplained neurological symptoms (e.g., vision changes, weakness, numbness) that could indicate a flare-up of autoimmune disease or other neurological condition.
  • Severe shortness of breath or chest pain, which could indicate cardiac or pulmonary complications.
  • Signs of depression or suicidal ideation. Mental health support is crucial for individuals with chronic illness.
  • Failure to respond to interventions. If Eleanor's symptoms do not improve after several months of somatic therapy and lifestyle modifications, further medical evaluation is warranted.
