Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time. I'd love to hear a little bit about what brought you to me today."
YOU:
"That's interesting you found me through [wellness group]. What specifically resonated with you from what you saw online?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the perimenopausal symptoms. When did they start? How are the hot flashes and insomnia impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage these symptoms? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If these symptoms continue, how do you see your life being affected in the next year? And if you could wake up tomorrow feeling completely balanced, what would that look like?"

YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing significant discomfort and disruption due to perimenopause, and you're seeking a natural, personalized solution. Is that a fair summary?"

Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Does this approach sound like it aligns with what you're looking for, Susan? ... Excellent! Let me share the details of my program and how we can move forward."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's about prioritizing. My program requires approximately 2-3 hours per week, including our calls. Think about how much time you're currently spending feeling unwell and researching solutions. This is an investment in reclaiming that time and energy."
"I need to do more research on medicinal mushrooms."
"That's a great idea. I can send you some research articles and resources that support their effectiveness in managing hormonal symptoms. I'm also happy to answer any specific questions you have about the science behind them."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, my program is highly personalized. I offer ongoing support and adapt the protocol based on your individual response. Plus, I have many satisfied clients who have successfully managed their perimenopausal symptoms using this approach. I can share testimonials if you'd like."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I do offer a payment plan option to make the program more accessible. Let me explain the details..."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Focus on genuine connection, active listening, and showing empathy. Ask open-ended questions to understand their story and motivations.

2. How can you address the objection "I need to think about it" without being pushy?

Acknowledge their need for time, then gently explore what specifically they need to consider. Offer to provide more information or schedule a follow-up call.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key. Understand your prospect's needs and concerns before the call.
  • Active listening builds trust and allows you to tailor your approach effectively.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value you provide.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide solutions.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently guide prospects toward the next step.
