The Challenge: Program Structuring

Emily's Program Structuring Dilemma

What Emily Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, experiencing severe burnout. I want to help her create a sustainable recovery plan. I'm just overwhelmed by where to start and what to include in each week of our 8-week program."

Key Areas to Address:

  • Prioritizing foundational elements in the early weeks
  • Balancing reflection, strategy, and empowerment activities
  • Ensuring realistic, achievable weekly goals
  • Integrating RESET methodology components effectively

Your Mentoring Approach

Essential Points to Guide Emily

1

Focus on Foundational Weeks First

Emphasize the importance of Recognize & Reflect (R) and Evaluate & Explore (E) in the first 2-3 weeks. These lay the groundwork for effective strategy.

2

Balance the RESET Components

Ensure the program includes activities from each RESET phase: Reflection, Evaluation, Strategy, Empowerment, and Transformation. Don't front-load any single area.

3

Set Achievable Weekly Goals

Help Emily understand the importance of setting realistic weekly goals. Overwhelming the client can hinder progress. Focus on small, consistent wins.

4

Incorporate Regular Check-ins

Suggest Emily schedule regular check-ins with Sarah to adjust the program as needed. Client feedback is crucial for program success.

Constructive Feedback Dialogue

Guiding Emily with Encouragement

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Emily, it's great that you're thinking so deeply about structuring this program. It shows your commitment to Sarah's recovery. Program development can feel overwhelming at first, but you've got this!"
Ask About Her Initial Ideas
"Before I share my thoughts, what are some of the activities you were considering for the first few weeks? What feels most important to address early on?"
Offer Structuring Principles
"One principle I use is to start with reflection and evaluation. Help Sarah understand where she is and what's contributing to her burnout before jumping into solutions. This builds buy-in and ensures the strategies are truly aligned with her needs."
Empower Her to Make Decisions
"Based on this, how would you adjust your initial plan? What small changes can you make to better align with Sarah's needs and the RESET framework?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of the "Recognize & Reflect" phase that should be incorporated into the program's initial weeks?

Facilitating self-awareness of the client's burnout triggers and symptoms.

2. How can you empower a new practitioner to trust their intuition when structuring a burnout recovery program?

By validating their initial ideas and offering guidance to refine them, rather than imposing a fixed solution.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves validating the mentee's experience and normalizing challenges.
  • Focus on teaching principles rather than providing prescriptive solutions.
  • Empowerment comes from encouraging the mentee to trust their intuition and make informed decisions.
  • The RESET methodology provides a structured framework for burnout recovery programs.
  • Regular check-ins and client feedback are essential for program adaptation and success.
