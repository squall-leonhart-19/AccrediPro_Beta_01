Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm too busy to commit to a group program right now."
"I understand. Many of my clients feel that way initially. However, this program is designed to help you *create* more time and energy by prioritizing self-care and setting boundaries. Think of it as an investment in your well-being that will ultimately free up more time in the long run. Plus, the group support can actually make it easier to stay committed."
"I'm not sure if a group setting is right for me. I'm usually more comfortable working one-on-one."
"That's a valid concern. While one-on-one coaching can be beneficial, the group dynamic offers unique advantages. You'll learn from other women's experiences, gain new perspectives, and build lasting connections. Many of my clients find the group support to be invaluable. And, I keep the groups small to ensure everyone gets personalized attention."
"I'm concerned about the cost."
"I hear you. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. The program includes six weekly group sessions, access to a private online forum, and valuable resources and tools. Think about what it's costing you to stay in your current state of burnout ‚Äì the lost productivity, the missed opportunities, the strain on your relationships. This program is an investment in your long-term well-being and happiness. We also offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful group program sales call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's challenges and desires, presenting the group program as a solution, handling objections effectively, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you address the objection that someone is "too busy" to join a group program?

Reframe the program as an investment in creating more time and energy by prioritizing self-care and setting boundaries. Emphasize the support and accountability of the group dynamic.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a scalable way to impact more lives and increase your income.
  • Effective sales calls for group programs require building connection and addressing concerns about time, cost, and group dynamics.
  • Confidently present your pricing and the value of the program.
  • Practice handling common objections with empathy and solutions.
  • Remember to close with a clear call to action and make it easy for prospects to join.
