Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I don't have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. However, what they discovered is that by prioritizing their well-being, they actually became more efficient and productive. We can tailor the program to fit your schedule and focus on small, manageable steps."
"I'm not sure I can afford it."
"That's a valid concern. Let's look at the long-term cost of burnout ‚Äì the potential health problems, lost productivity, and strained relationships. My program is an investment in your well-being and your future. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I've tried other things before, and they didn't work."
"I hear your frustration. My program is different because it's personalized to your specific needs and challenges. We focus on identifying the root causes of your burnout and creating sustainable solutions. I also provide ongoing support and accountability to ensure you stay on track."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (because results depend on your commitment), I can guarantee that I will provide you with the tools, support, and guidance you need to make significant progress. Most of my clients experience noticeable improvements in their energy levels, stress management, and overall well-being within the first few weeks. I encourage you to read the testimonials on my website."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Connect, Uncover Pain Points & Goals, Present Your Solution & Program, and Close & Next Steps.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the objection "I don't have the time"?

Acknowledge their concern and explain that prioritizing well-being can actually increase efficiency. Offer to tailor the program to their schedule and focus on manageable steps.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural and confident.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and the potential ROI for your clients.
  • Believe in your ability to help others and confidently present your pricing.
  • Remember, every "no" gets you closer to a "yes"!
