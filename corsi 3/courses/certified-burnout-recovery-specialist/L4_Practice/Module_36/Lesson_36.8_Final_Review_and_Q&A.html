Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Sarah, and I'm a Certified Burnout Recovery Specialist. I saw you responded to my post in the women's group. What resonated with you?"
YOU:
"That's interesting. Many women in that group are feeling the same way. Tell me a bit about what's been going on for you lately."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed. Can you describe a typical day for me?"
YOU:
"And how long have you been feeling this way? Has anything changed recently?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being completely burned out and 10 being thriving, where would you rate yourself right now? What would it take to get you to an 8 or 9?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing a classic case of burnout. You're juggling a lot, neglecting your own needs, and feeling overwhelmed. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because it's not just about quick fixes. We'll identify the root causes of your burnout, create a personalized strategy, and implement practical tools to help you regain control. My 12-week program includes one-on-one coaching, personalized action plans, and ongoing support. Many of my clients, like you, are women in their 40s and 50s who are ready to reclaim their lives. They often report feeling significantly better within the first month."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients feel the same way initially. But think about it this way: how much time are you *already* spending feeling stressed, exhausted, and unproductive? This program is designed to help you reclaim your time and energy by setting boundaries and prioritizing self-care. We can work together to find realistic ways to integrate the program into your busy schedule."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. It's an investment, no doubt. But let's consider the cost of *not* addressing your burnout. What's the impact on your health, your relationships, your career? What if we could help you increase your productivity by 20%, reduce your sick days by 50%, and regain your joy in life? Would that be worth it? I also offer payment plans to make it more manageable."
"I need to talk to my husband."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Would it be helpful if I sent you a detailed program overview that you can share with him? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions he might have. What would be most helpful?"
"I've tried other things before, and they didn't work."
"I understand your hesitation. It's frustrating to invest time and money in something that doesn't deliver results. Can you tell me what you've tried in the past and what didn't work? That will help me understand your situation better and explain how my approach is different. I focus on personalized strategies and ongoing support to ensure you get the results you deserve."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment by focusing on the cost of *not* solving the problem and the potential ROI of your program. Offer payment plans if possible.

3. What is the importance of practicing your pricing presentation?

Practicing your pricing presentation builds confidence and helps you communicate the value of your program effectively. This makes clients more likely to invest.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Understanding your prospect's situation, goals, and concerns is crucial for tailoring your approach.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence can overcome potential roadblocks.
  • Presenting your pricing with clarity and value builds trust and encourages commitment.
  • Consistent practice is key to mastering client acquisition and building a successful practice.
