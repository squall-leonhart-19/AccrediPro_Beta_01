Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call! I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, what resonated with you in my Facebook ad?"
YOU:
"That's great! I love helping people find clarity. So, tell me a bit about what's been going on for you lately."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling stuck. Can you elaborate on that? What aspects of your current situation are causing that feeling?"
YOU:
"And what have you already tried to find a more fulfilling career path? Any courses, assessments, or coaching?"
YOU:
"Imagine a year from now, if nothing changes. How would that impact your overall happiness and well-being? Now, if you *did* find that aligned career, what would that look and feel like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Okay Susan, so if I'm hearing you correctly, you're feeling stuck in your current job, you've tried some things without lasting success, and you're really seeking a career that aligns with your soul's purpose. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because we tap into the Akashic Records to uncover your soul's blueprint for your career. We identify your natural talents, passions, and the unique contribution you're meant to make. It's a 12-week program where we create a personalized roadmap, and most clients find significant clarity within the first month."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, Susan, do you feel like this approach resonates with you and what you're looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I believe in Akashic Records."
"That's perfectly okay! Many of my clients are initially skeptical. Think of the Records as a vast database of your soul's journey. We don't need to 'believe' in it for it to provide valuable insights. Would you be open to seeing how it works in practice?"
"I need to think about it."
"I understand. What aspects do you need more time to consider? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the right decision."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate you being upfront. I understand that it's an investment. Let's reframe that. How much is it costing you to stay in a job you dislike? What's the value of finding a career that brings you joy and fulfillment? My clients often see a return on their investment through increased income and overall happiness."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the tools, guidance, and support you need to create meaningful change. My program is designed to empower you to take control of your career path. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you're not happy with the first two weeks, you'll receive a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive"?

Reframe the investment by highlighting the cost of staying in the current situation and the potential return on investment from the program. Offer payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition and scaling your practice.
  • Focus on understanding the prospect's pain points and presenting your program as the solution.
  • Practice handling common objections with confidence and empathy.
  • Confidently stating your price is essential for attracting serious clients.
  • Remember, you're not just selling a service; you're offering transformation and lasting change.
