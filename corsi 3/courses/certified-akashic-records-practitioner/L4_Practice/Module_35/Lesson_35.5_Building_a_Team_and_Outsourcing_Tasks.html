Building a team and outsourcing tasks are essential steps to free up your time and energy, allowing you to focus on what you do best: connecting with the Akashic Records and guiding your clients. Identifying Tasks to Delegate or Outsource

The first step in building a team is to identify the tasks that can be delegated or outsourced. This often includes tasks that are time-consuming, repetitive, or outside of your core expertise. Here are some examples:

  • Administrative Tasks: Scheduling appointments, managing emails, handling client inquiries, and bookkeeping.
  • Marketing: Social media management, content creation, email marketing, and running advertisements.
  • Website Maintenance: Updating your website, managing blog posts, and ensuring website security.
  • Accounting: Tracking income and expenses, preparing financial statements, and filing taxes.

Consider using time-tracking tools to understand where your time is being spent. This will help you identify the tasks that are taking up the most time and could be delegated. Remember, your time is valuable, and delegating tasks allows you to focus on high-value activities such as conducting readings and developing new services.

Hiring Virtual Assistants

A Virtual Assistant (VA) can be a valuable asset to your Akashic Records practice. VAs can handle a wide range of tasks, freeing up your time to focus on your core work. Here's how to find and hire qualified candidates:

  1. Finding Qualified Candidates: Use online platforms such as Upwork, Fiverr, or specialized VA agencies.
  2. Setting Clear Expectations: Clearly define the tasks, responsibilities, and deadlines for the VA.
  3. Providing Ongoing Training: Provide training and resources to ensure the VA understands your business and can perform their tasks effectively.

When interviewing potential VAs, ask about their experience, skills, and availability. Be sure to check references and conduct a trial period to assess their performance. Remember, communication is key to a successful VA relationship.

Many practitioners find it beneficial to start with a VA for administrative tasks and then gradually delegate marketing or technical responsibilities as they become more comfortable.

Building a Team of Associate Practitioners

As your practice grows, you may consider building a team of associate practitioners. This can allow you to expand your services and reach a wider audience. Here's how to establish a team:

  1. Establishing Clear Roles: Define the roles and responsibilities of each associate practitioner.
  2. Compensation Structures: Determine a fair and sustainable compensation structure, such as a percentage of revenue or a fixed fee per reading.

When building a team, it's essential to find practitioners who share your values and have a similar approach to working with the Akashic Records. Provide ongoing training and support to ensure consistency and quality in your services. Consider offering mentorship and opportunities for professional development to retain your team members.

Income Example: Consider a practitioner who charges $150 per reading and has three associate practitioners each conducting 10 readings per week. If the practitioner takes a 30% commission, they could earn an additional $1,350 per week, or $70,200 per year, from their team's efforts.

Creating Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) are documented instructions that outline how to perform specific tasks. Creating SOPs ensures consistency and quality in your services, even when tasks are delegated or outsourced. Here are some key areas to document:

  • Client intake process
  • Scheduling and appointment management
  • Preparing for an Akashic Records reading
  • Conducting the reading
  • Post-reading follow-up and integration support

SOPs should be clear, concise, and easy to follow. Regularly review and update your SOPs to reflect any changes in your processes or services. Consider using video tutorials or screenshots to illustrate key steps. Share your SOPs with your team members and provide training on how to use them effectively.

Effective Communication and Collaboration

Effective communication and collaboration are essential for building a successful team. Here are some strategies to promote teamwork and ensure everyone is on the same page:

  • Project Management Tools: Use tools such as Asana, Trello, or Monday.com to track tasks, set deadlines, and communicate with team members.
  • Regular Team Meetings: Schedule regular team meetings to discuss progress, address challenges, and share insights.

Encourage open communication and feedback among team members. Create a culture of trust and support, where everyone feels comfortable sharing their ideas and concerns. Consider using communication channels such as Slack or Microsoft Teams to facilitate ongoing communication and collaboration.

Case Study: Eleanor's Empowerment
Scaling with a VA and Associate

Client: Eleanor, 48, started her Akashic Records practice after a career as a teacher.

Presenting Symptoms: Eleanor felt overwhelmed by administrative tasks, marketing, and client communication, leaving her with little time for readings.

Intervention: Eleanor hired a VA to manage scheduling and social media, and an associate practitioner to handle overflow readings.

Outcomes: Eleanor reduced her administrative workload by 50%, increased her income by 30%, and felt more energized and focused on her clients. She now earns approximately $6,000 per month and is expanding her team.
