Addressing Objections

Handling Common Concerns with Confidence

"I'm not sure if I can commit to 12 weeks."
"I understand the time commitment can seem daunting. However, most clients find that the structured approach and weekly support actually helps them stay on track and achieve their goals faster than trying to figure it out on their own. Plus, the sessions are designed to fit into your schedule. What part of the 12 weeks feels most challenging for you?"
"I've tried career coaching before, and it didn't work."
"I'm sorry to hear that. What was your experience like, and what didn't work for you? My approach is unique because we combine traditional career coaching with the insights from the Akashic Records. This allows us to tap into a deeper level of understanding about your soul's purpose and design a career that is truly aligned with your authentic self."
"I need to think about the investment."
"I completely understand. Investing in yourself is a big decision. To help you make an informed choice, let's consider the potential ROI. What would it be worth to you to wake up every day excited about your work, knowing you're making a difference and living your purpose? How much is the current state of unfulfillment costing you in terms of energy, health, and overall happiness? I also offer payment plans to make the investment more manageable."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the call.

2. How can you handle the objection, "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment by focusing on the potential ROI and the cost of staying in their current situation. Offer payment plans if possible.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural and confident.
  • Prepare for common objections and have responses ready.
  • Confidently state your pricing and emphasize the value you provide.
  • Focus on the client's needs and how your program can help them achieve their goals.
  • Remember your income potential and stay motivated to build your practice.
