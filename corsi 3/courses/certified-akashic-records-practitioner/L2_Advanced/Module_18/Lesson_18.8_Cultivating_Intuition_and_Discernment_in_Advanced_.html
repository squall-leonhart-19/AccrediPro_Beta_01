Differential Considerations

Considering Other Possibilities

1

Estrogen Dominance

Weight gain, low libido, anxiety could be related to estrogen dominance, especially given birth control pill use. Consider testing estrogen/progesterone ratios.

2

Gut Dysbiosis/SIBO/Leaky Gut

Frequent antibiotic use and GERD strongly suggest gut dysbiosis. SIBO (Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth) and intestinal permeability ("leaky gut") should be considered. Studies show that up to 60% of Hashimoto's patients may have SIBO (Lauritano et al., 2007).

3

Adrenal Fatigue/HPA Axis Dysfunction

Chronic stress, fatigue, and anxiety point to potential adrenal fatigue. Consider adrenal saliva testing to assess cortisol levels throughout the day.

4

Nutrient Deficiencies

Hashimoto's and gut issues can lead to nutrient deficiencies (selenium, zinc, iron, B vitamins). Assess nutrient status through comprehensive testing.

When to Refer

Know Your Scope of Practice

  • Sudden onset of severe abdominal pain could indicate a serious gastrointestinal issue requiring immediate medical attention.
  • Unexplained bleeding (vaginal or rectal) warrants a prompt referral to a physician for evaluation.
  • Severe depression or suicidal ideation requires immediate mental health intervention.
  • Any symptoms suggestive of a thyroid storm (fever, rapid heart rate, agitation) require emergency medical care.
  • If the client is resistant to conventional medical care and insists on solely relying on Akashic Record work for a serious medical condition.
