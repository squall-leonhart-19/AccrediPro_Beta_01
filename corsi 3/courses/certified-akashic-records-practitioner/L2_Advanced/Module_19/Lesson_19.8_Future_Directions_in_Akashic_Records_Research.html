Differential Considerations

Ruling Out Other Possibilities

1

Premature Ovarian Insufficiency (POI)

Irregular periods and fatigue could indicate early menopause. Further hormone testing (FSH, LH, estradiol) is warranted.

2

Adrenal Dysfunction

Elevated morning cortisol and chronic stress suggest potential adrenal fatigue or dysregulation. Consider a 24-hour saliva cortisol test.

3

Underlying Infection

Recurrent UTIs could indicate a chronic or resistant infection. Rule out other potential infections (e.g., Lyme disease, EBV).

4

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Seasonal allergies, migraines, and fatigue can be symptoms of MCAS. Consider testing histamine levels and tryptase.

When to Refer

Red Flags Requiring Medical Attention

  • Severe abdominal pain that is unresponsive to medication may indicate a ruptured ovarian cyst or other acute condition.
  • Heavy or prolonged vaginal bleeding could be a sign of uterine fibroids or other gynecological issues.
  • Sudden onset of severe headaches with neurological symptoms (e.g., vision changes, weakness) requires immediate medical evaluation.
  • Persistent or worsening anxiety or depression should be addressed by a mental health professional.
