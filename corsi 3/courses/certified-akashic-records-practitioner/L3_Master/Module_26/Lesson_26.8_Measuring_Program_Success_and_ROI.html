The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I did a reading for Sarah, 52, who wanted clarity about her career. I felt like I was forcing the connection. The Records showed images of a sailboat, but Sarah doesn't like boats. I told her she needed to embrace adventure, but she seemed confused. I felt like a fraud."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Aisha properly ground and center before the reading?
  • Did she clearly state her intention for the reading?
  • Did she explore the symbolism of the sailboat with Sarah?
  • How did Aisha manage her own anxiety during the reading?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Validate Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to have readings that feel 'off' sometimes. It happens to all of us, even experienced practitioners."

2

Revisit Grounding & Intention

Emphasize the importance of a strong foundation. Did she ground herself? Did she clearly state her intention for the reading?

3

Explore Symbolism, Don't Dictate

Teach her to ask open-ended questions about the symbolism. "What does a sailboat represent to you? What does adventure mean in your life?"

4

Address Self-Doubt

Help her identify and challenge her negative self-talk. Remind her of her strengths and accomplishments.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Aisha, thank you for sharing this with me. It takes courage to admit when a reading doesn't go as planned. That shows you're committed to growth."
Ask About Her Process
"Walk me through your preparation for the reading. How did you ground yourself? What was your intention?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Instead of telling Sarah she needed to embrace adventure, you could have asked, 'What does adventure mean to you right now?' or 'Where in your life do you feel a need for more freedom?'"
Encourage Self-Compassion
"Remember, every reading is a learning experience. Be kind to yourself. You're doing great, and you're getting better every day."

Check Your Understanding

1. As a mentor, what's the most important thing to prioritize when a mentee shares a challenging case?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable sharing their experiences and vulnerabilities.

2. What's a better approach than directly criticizing a mentee's actions during a reading?

Ask open-ended questions about their process and preparation, guiding them to reflect on their own decisions and identify areas for improvement.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about empowering others, not showcasing your own expertise.
  • Validation and encouragement are crucial for building confidence in new practitioners.
  • Teach principles rather than just providing answers to foster independent thinking.
  • Creating a safe space for vulnerability allows mentees to learn from their mistakes.
  • Your leadership and guidance can help shape the future of Akashic Records practice.
