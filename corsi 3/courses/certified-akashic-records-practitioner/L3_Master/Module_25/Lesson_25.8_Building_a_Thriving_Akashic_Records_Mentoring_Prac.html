Supervision & Mentoring Practice

Let's practice guiding new Akashic Records Practitioners as you step into your leadership role, Sarah.

Meet Your Mentee

As a Master Practitioner, you'll often find yourself guiding newer practitioners. This practice lab focuses on honing your mentoring skills. Let's meet your mentee for this session:

Mentee Profile: Emily, Newly Certified L1

Emily is a vibrant 45-year-old, a former teacher who recently completed her Level 1 Akashic Records Practitioner certification. She's enthusiastic but also admits to feeling overwhelmed by the prospect of working with clients independently.

Strengths: Strong intuition, excellent listening skills, compassionate nature.

Areas for Growth: Needs help structuring sessions, struggles with setting boundaries, and lacks confidence in interpreting complex record information.

Emily's Concern: "I had a practice client who asked about their career path, and I felt totally lost in the Records! I wasn't sure what to focus on or how to translate what I was seeing into practical advice. I’m worried I’m not ready."

The Case She Presents

Emily is seeking guidance on a practice session she conducted. Here's what you know:

  • Client: Practice client, Sarah, 38, feeling stuck in her current job.
  • Inquiry: "What is my ideal career path, and what steps can I take to get there?"
  • Emily's Experience: Emily felt overwhelmed by the volume of information in the Records. She saw images of Sarah in various roles (teacher, artist, entrepreneur) but couldn't discern a clear direction. She ended the session feeling like she hadn't provided concrete guidance.

Your Teaching Approach

The goal is to help Emily develop her skills without overwhelming her. Here are key teaching points to consider:

  1. Validate Her Feelings: Acknowledge that feeling lost in the Records is common, especially when starting. Normalize the experience.
  2. Focus on Inquiry Refinement: Help Emily understand how to refine the client's inquiry for more specific answers. For example, "What are Sarah's core values in a career?" or "What skills does Sarah most enjoy using?"
  3. Teach Grounding Techniques: Share techniques for staying grounded and focused during a reading, such as breathwork or visualization.
  4. Emphasize Practical Application: Guide Emily on translating the record information into actionable steps. Instead of just describing images, help her connect them to Sarah's real-world opportunities.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Here's a sample dialogue to guide your feedback:

  1. Start with Encouragement: "Emily, it's fantastic that you're seeking supervision. That shows real dedication to your practice and your clients."
  2. Ask About Her Perspective: "Before I share my thoughts, what parts of the session felt strong to you? What did you feel confident about?"
  3. Offer Constructive Criticism: "I noticed the inquiry was quite broad. Sometimes, narrowing the focus can lead to clearer answers. What if you had asked, 'What are Sarah's top three skills she wants to utilize in her next career move?' How might that have shifted the reading?"
  4. Provide Specific Techniques: "When feeling overwhelmed, try this grounding exercise: Visualize roots extending from your feet into the earth, anchoring you to the present moment. This can help you filter the information and stay focused."
  5. Empower Her Next Steps: "What’s one small change you’d make in your next practice session based on our conversation?"

Supervision Best Practices

Keep these do's and don'ts in mind as you mentor Emily:

Do's Don'ts
Ask open-ended questions to encourage critical thinking. Give direct answers without allowing the mentee to explore solutions.
Share your own experiences and challenges to normalize the learning process. Present yourself as perfect or infallible.
Focus on the mentee's strengths and build upon them. Dwell on weaknesses or mistakes.
Provide specific, actionable feedback. Offer vague or generic advice.
Encourage self-care and boundary setting. Promote overworking or neglecting personal needs.

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary benefit of asking a mentee about their perspective before offering feedback?

It encourages self-reflection, identifies areas of confidence, and fosters a collaborative learning environment.

2. Why is it important to normalize challenges and share your own learning experiences as a mentor?

It helps the mentee feel less isolated, reduces imposter syndrome, and creates a safe space for vulnerability and growth.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not just giving answers.
  • Inquiry refinement is crucial for clearer Akashic Records readings.
  • Grounding techniques help maintain focus during sessions.
  • Constructive feedback should be specific, actionable, and encouraging.
  • Sharing your own experiences builds trust and normalizes the learning process.
