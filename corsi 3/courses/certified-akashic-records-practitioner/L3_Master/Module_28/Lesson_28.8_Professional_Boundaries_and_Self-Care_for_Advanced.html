  • Did Sarah screen for a history of trauma or PTSD before the session?

  • Did Sarah debrief and provide adequate support after the session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Trauma-Informed Practices

Explain the importance of screening for trauma history and adapting the approach accordingly. A trauma-informed approach prioritizes safety, predictability, and client control.

2

Review Grounding Techniques

Teach Sarah specific grounding techniques to use during sessions when clients become overwhelmed. Examples include deep breathing, visualization, and focusing on sensory input.

3

Clarify Scope of Practice and Referral Options

Remind Sarah about the importance of staying within her scope of practice and knowing when to refer clients to licensed mental health professionals. Provide a list of trusted referral sources.

4

Discuss Debriefing and Aftercare

Explain the importance of debriefing with clients after intense sessions and providing resources for ongoing support. Encourage Sarah to check in with Emily regularly.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a trauma-informed approach in Akashic Records readings?

Prioritizing client safety, establishing clear boundaries, providing predictability, and empowering client autonomy are essential.

2. Why is it important to clarify the scope of practice with a new practitioner?

To ensure they understand their limitations, avoid practicing beyond their competence, and know when to refer clients to appropriate professionals.

  • Trauma-informed practices are crucial when working with sensitive client issues.
