The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"My client, Sarah, 52, wanted to know about a recurring pattern of relationship failures. In the Records, we saw a past life where she betrayed a lover. I told her she needed to forgive herself and do some cord-cutting. But now Sarah says she feels even more guilt and shame. What did I do wrong?"

Points to Consider:

  • Was the past life information presented in a supportive way?
  • Was the client prepared for potentially difficult revelations?
  • Were integration techniques tailored to the client's emotional state?
  • Could this trigger deeper trauma that requires specialized therapy?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize Client Readiness

Discuss the importance of assessing a client's emotional capacity before diving into potentially challenging past life scenarios. Explain that some clients may need more grounding and support.

2

Reframe 'Mistakes' as Learning

Help Aisha understand that every session is a learning opportunity. Encourage her to view this experience as a chance to refine her approach and build resilience.

3

Teach Nuanced Interpretation

Explain that past life information isn't always literal. It can be symbolic, representing underlying emotional patterns. Discourage simplistic cause-and-effect interpretations.

4

Promote Empowerment, Not Guilt

Guide Aisha on how to frame past life revelations in a way that empowers clients to make positive changes in the present, rather than fostering guilt or shame.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Acknowledgment
"Aisha, thank you for bringing this to me. It takes courage to seek guidance, and it shows you're committed to your clients' well-being. It's admirable that you're reflecting on this session."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"What did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently if you could go back? What were you hoping to achieve with the cord-cutting exercise?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Sometimes, past life information is less about specific events and more about the emotional themes they represent. Maybe Sarah's 'betrayal' is about a pattern of self-sabotage. How could you reframe this for her?"
Reinforce Boundaries & Referrals
"It's also essential to recognize when a client needs more than we can offer. If Sarah's guilt is overwhelming, she might benefit from seeing a therapist who specializes in trauma. It's okay to refer out!"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to consider when a mentee brings you a challenging case?

Focus on creating a safe space for learning and growth, rather than immediately offering solutions.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with setting boundaries?

Share practical strategies for setting boundaries, such as limiting session times, clearly defining scope of practice, and practicing assertive communication. Role-playing can be helpful.

Key Takeaways
