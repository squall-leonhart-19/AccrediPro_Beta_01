Your Coaching Script

Here's a script to help Diane clarify the information she received. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, the Records often communicate in symbols and metaphors, which can feel vague at first. Let's break down what you saw and felt. The pen and flowing river suggest creativity and communication. The group listening indicates you have a message that resonates with others. How does that feel to you?"
DIANE:
"It sounds nice, but I've been writing articles for years. It's just a job. I don't feel like I'm really 'guiding' anyone."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. So, writing *itself* isn't necessarily the core of your purpose, but it could be a vehicle for it. Let's explore the 'Guidance' aspect. When you imagine guiding others, what comes to mind?"
DIANE:
"I don't know... maybe helping women going through divorce? I went through a really tough one, and I wish I'd had someone who really understood."
YOU:
"That's powerful, Diane. Let's try this: if your writing could specifically support women navigating divorce, would that feel more aligned with 'Guidance'?"
DIANE:
"Yes! Actually, that feels... right. It gives my writing more meaning."
YOU:
"Excellent. So, it's not just writing, it's writing with the specific intention of guiding women through divorce. The Records gave you the broad strokes; now we're adding the details that make it personal and meaningful for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the danger of telling a client "This is exactly what the Records mean?"

It can shut down their own intuition and interpretation, making them dependent on you. The Records are a collaborative process.

2. What's a good clarifying question to ask if a client says, "I don't understand what this means?"

"What's the first thing that comes to mind when you see/hear that?" or "How does that make you feel?" Start with their initial reaction.

Key Takeaways

  • Clarifying information is about helping clients find their *own* meaning in the Records.
  • Use open-ended questions to guide them toward deeper understanding.
  • Avoid imposing your own interpretations or beliefs.
  • Look for concrete connections to their personal experiences and goals.
  • The goal is clarity and actionable insights.
