Using Divination Tools

Divination tools can serve as valuable adjuncts in clarifying information received from the Akashic Records. Pendulums, for instance, can provide simple yes/no answers to confirm or deny specific details. Tarot cards, with their rich symbolism, can offer deeper insights into the underlying energies and themes present in a client's situation. It's important to remember that these tools are not replacements for your intuitive abilities but rather aids to focus your intention and validate information.

A 2018 study published in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine found that individuals using intuitive tools like tarot experienced a 27% increase in feelings of empowerment and self-awareness (n=120, CI 95%).

Employing Journaling Prompts

Journaling is a valuable tool for processing and clarifying the information you receive during an Akashic reading. By writing down your thoughts, feelings, and insights, you can deepen your understanding and uncover hidden meanings. Specific journaling prompts can help you focus your inquiry and explore different aspects of the reading. This aligns with the "Explore" and "Solve" stages of the A.C.C.E.S.S. Frameworkâ„¢, facilitating a deeper dive into the Records' insights.

Example Journaling Prompts:

  • What is the core message I received for this client?
  • What are the potential blocks or challenges related to this situation?
  • What actions can the client take to move forward?
  • What are the key lessons or growth opportunities present in this situation?

A 2021 meta-analysis in Perspectives on Psychological Science, involving 54 studies and over 4,700 participants, found that expressive writing interventions significantly improved mental well-being and reduced stress levels (ES = 0.32, 95% CI [0.21, 0.43]).
