Determining a Competitive Pricing Structure

Pricing your program is an art and a science. It's about striking the right balance between the value you offer and what your target market is willing to pay. Consider these factors:

  • Value Proposition: What unique benefits does your program provide? How does it address parents' pain points and deliver transformative results using the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢?
  • Program Length and Content: A longer, more comprehensive program justifies a higher price point.
  • Target Market: Research what similar programs charge in your area and adjust accordingly. Consider your ideal client's income level and willingness to invest in parenting support.
  • Your Expertise and Credentials: As a Certified Positive Parenting Coachâ„¢, your expertise adds significant value.

A common strategy is to start by calculating the cost of delivering your program and then adding a profit margin. Don't be afraid to price your program competitively, reflecting the life-changing impact you can have on families. Many coaches successfully charge between $500 to $2500 for a comprehensive program, depending on the factors above.

Developing Varied Pricing Options

Offering different pricing options can attract a wider range of clients. Here are a few ideas:

  • Tiered Pricing: Offer basic, standard, and premium packages with varying levels of support and resources.
  • Payment Plans: Allow clients to pay in installments, making your program more accessible.
  • Early Bird Discounts: Incentivize early registration with a special discount.
  • Group Discounts: Offer reduced rates for groups of friends or family members who sign up together.

Consider offering a "Taste of T.H.R.I.V.E." introductory workshop at a lower price point, allowing potential clients to experience your coaching style and the framework's effectiveness before committing to the full program. This aligns with the "Tune-In" phase of the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢, building rapport and trust.

Creating a Detailed Budget

A well-defined budget is essential for financial stability. Include all program-related expenses, such as:

  • Marketing and Advertising: Website, social media ads, flyers, etc.
  • Materials and Resources: Workbooks, handouts, online platform subscriptions.
  • Administrative Costs: Software, office supplies, insurance.
  • Your Time: Factor in your hourly rate for program development, delivery, and client support.

Use a spreadsheet or accounting software to track your income and expenses. This will help you identify areas where you can reduce costs and increase profitability.

Establishing a Tracking System

Implement a system to accurately track your income and expenses. This could be as simple as a spreadsheet or using accounting software like QuickBooks or Xero. Consistent tracking allows you to:

  • Monitor your program's financial performance.
  • Identify trends and patterns.
  • Make informed decisions about pricing and marketing.
  • Prepare for tax season.

Consider using a customer relationship management (CRM) system to manage client information, track payments, and automate invoicing. This will save you time and improve your overall efficiency.

Developing a Sustainable Financial Plan

Your financial plan should outline your goals, strategies, and timelines for achieving financial success. Consider these elements:

  • Revenue Projections: Estimate your program's income based on enrollment targets and pricing.
  • Expense Forecasts: Project your program-related expenses over a specific period.
  • Profitability Analysis: Determine your program's potential profit margin.
  • Savings Goals: Set aside a portion of your profits for future investments or unexpected expenses.

Regularly review and adjust your financial plan as needed. As your program grows, you may need to revise your pricing, marketing strategies, or operational processes.
