"I have so many ideas for helping parents, but I don't know how to organize them into a cohesive program. I feel like I'm just throwing things at the wall and hoping something sticks! I want to offer a 6-week course, but I am not sure where to start."

Guiding Principles for Program Structure

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Start with the End in Mind

Help Sarah define the core transformation her program offers. What specific results will parents achieve?

2

Chunk Down the Content

Teach Sarah to break the overall program into smaller, thematic modules (e.g., "Understanding Your Child's Brain," "Effective Communication," "Discipline Strategies").

3

Balance Theory and Practice

Emphasize the importance of blending theoretical concepts with practical exercises and real-world examples. Actionable steps are key.

4

Incorporate Validation and Feedback

Advise Sarah to build in opportunities for parents to share their experiences, ask questions, and receive personalized guidance.

Your Mentoring Dialogue

How to Provide Constructive Guidance

Acknowledge Her Strengths
"Sarah, your teaching background is a HUGE asset! You already know how to create engaging learning experiences. Let's translate that to the coaching space."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What are the 3 biggest challenges parents tell you they're facing? If you could wave a magic wand, what would they be able to do differently after your program?"
Offer a Framework
"Think of each module as building on the previous one. Start with mindset, then move to skills, then focus on implementation. That's a proven formula."
Encourage Experimentation
"The first version won't be perfect, and that's okay! Get it out there, see what works, and refine it. That's how you create a truly impactful program."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important first step in helping a new coach develop their program?

Help them clarify the core transformation and desired outcomes for their clients.

2. What is a good way to encourage a new coach who is feeling overwhelmed by the program development process?

Remind them of their existing strengths and skills, and break the process down into smaller, more manageable steps.

You're Nurturing the Next Generation!

By sharing your expertise, you're helping new Positive Parenting Coaches make a real difference in families' lives. You're not just building programs, you're building a community of empowered practitioners.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective mentoring involves guiding, not dictating.
  • Help new coaches identify their unique strengths and style.
  • Program development should focus on clear outcomes and actionable steps.
  • Encourage experimentation and learning from experience.
  • Your guidance can empower others to create impactful programs.
