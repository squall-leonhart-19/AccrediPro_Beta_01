Priya, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic but anxious about handling complex client situations.

Background
Former teacher, strong understanding of child development, limited experience with crisis intervention.
Strengths
Excellent communication skills, patient, good at building rapport with families.
Growth Areas
Needs guidance on setting boundaries, managing transference, and handling high-conflict situations.
Her Question
"I'm feeling overwhelmed by a client case. Can you help me figure out the best way forward?"

The Case She Presents

Priya's Client Case

What Priya Tells You:

"I'm working with the Miller family. The parents are constantly arguing, and their 8-year-old son, Ethan, is exhibiting severe anxiety. During our last session, the mother broke down crying, accusing the father of emotional abuse. I feel completely out of my depth. I'm not sure how to manage the conflict or support Ethan effectively."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Priya assessed for safety concerns, such as domestic violence or child endangerment?
  • Is Priya aware of her scope of practice and the need for referrals to specialized services?
  • Has Priya explored the family's history of conflict and trauma?
  • Is Priya taking care of her own emotional well-being in the face of such a challenging case?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Priya

1

Prioritize Safety and Ethical Considerations

Emphasize the importance of assessing for safety concerns and adhering to ethical guidelines. Discuss mandatory reporting requirements and the need to prioritize the child's well-being.

2

Review Scope of Practice and Referral Options

Help Priya understand her limitations and the importance of referring to specialists when necessary. Provide resources and guidance on identifying qualified professionals.

3

Teach Strategies for Managing Conflict and Emotional Intensity

Share techniques for de-escalating conflict, setting boundaries, and managing transference. Role-play challenging scenarios to build Priya's confidence and skills.

4

Emphasize Self-Care and Emotional Boundaries

Encourage Priya to prioritize self-care and set healthy emotional boundaries. Help her develop strategies for managing stress and avoiding burnout.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Priya's Feelings
"Priya, it's completely understandable that you're feeling overwhelmed. This is a complex case, and it's natural to feel uncertain. Let's break it down together."
Ask About Her Assessment Process
"Tell me about your assessment process. What information have you gathered about the family's history, dynamics, and current stressors?"
Offer Guidance on Prioritizing Interventions
"In situations like this, it's essential to prioritize safety and ethical considerations. Have you assessed for any immediate risks to Ethan's well-being? Are you aware of your mandatory reporting obligations?"
Empower Her to Seek Support and Referrals
"Remember, you don't have to handle this alone. It's okay to seek support from colleagues, supervisors, or specialized professionals. Have you considered referring the family to a therapist who specializes in family conflict or trauma?"

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves guiding new practitioners through complex cases, prioritizing safety and ethical considerations.
  • It's essential to assess for safety concerns, such as domestic violence or child endangerment, and adhere to ethical guidelines.
  • Help mentees understand their limitations and the importance of referring to specialists when necessary.
  • Share techniques for managing conflict, setting boundaries, and promoting self-care.
  • Encourage mentees to seek supervision and consultation regularly to enhance their skills and confidence.
