Informed Consent Script

Essential Elements of Informed Consent

Phase 1: Introduction & Purpose 3 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for choosing me as your parenting coach. Before we begin, I want to go over the informed consent process. This ensures you understand the nature of our coaching relationship, my role, and your rights."
YOU:
"The purpose of our coaching is to help you navigate the challenges of your blended family and create a more harmonious home environment. It's important to remember that I am not a therapist or legal professional, and our sessions are not a substitute for therapy or legal advice."
Phase 2: Confidentiality & Limits 5 min
YOU:
"Everything we discuss in our sessions will be kept confidential. However, there are some limits to confidentiality. I am legally obligated to report any suspected child abuse or neglect, or if you disclose any intent to harm yourself or others."
YOU:
"Additionally, because you have a joint custody agreement, it's important that our coaching aligns with the best interests of your children. I will not engage in any strategies that could be detrimental to their well-being or violate the terms of your custody agreement."
Phase 3: Your Rights & Responsibilities 4 min
YOU:
"You have the right to ask questions, express concerns, and terminate our coaching relationship at any time. You also have the responsibility to be honest and open with me about your situation and goals."
YOU:
"I will provide you with my professional expertise and support, but ultimately, the success of our coaching depends on your willingness to implement the strategies we discuss and make positive changes in your family."
Phase 4: Agreement & Signature 3 min
YOU:
"Do you have any questions about what I've shared? ...Great. If you agree to these terms, please sign and date this informed consent form. This acknowledges that you understand and agree to the terms of our coaching relationship."

Handling Ethical Dilemmas

Navigating Tricky Situations

Susan asks you to document her ex-husband's "parenting failures" to use in court.
"I understand your frustration, Susan. However, as your parenting coach, my role is to support you in creating a positive and healthy family environment. I cannot provide documentation for legal purposes, as that would be outside the scope of our coaching relationship. I recommend consulting with a legal professional for advice on how to address your concerns in court."
Susan discloses that she is not following the custody agreement regarding visitation.
"Susan, I appreciate your honesty. However, because this involves a legal agreement and the well-being of your children, I must advise you to seek legal counsel. I cannot continue coaching you if you are knowingly violating a court order. My priority is always the best interests of the children involved."
Susan asks you to communicate directly with her children without her ex-husband's knowledge.
"Susan, I believe it's important to maintain transparency and open communication with both parents, especially when it comes to the children's well-being. Therefore, I cannot communicate directly with your children without your ex-husband's knowledge and consent. It's best to approach this collaboratively, perhaps through a family meeting or with the assistance of a mediator."

Scope of Practice Clarification

Defining Your Boundaries

It's crucial to define what you can and cannot do as a parenting coach. Here's a simple table:

What You CAN Do What You CANNOT Do
Offer guidance and support on parenting strategies. Provide therapy or mental health counseling.
Help clients set goals and develop action plans. Give legal advice or represent clients in court.
Share evidence-based information on child development. Diagnose or treat medical conditions.
Facilitate communication and conflict resolution. Make decisions for clients or their families.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is informed consent crucial in parenting coaching?

Informed consent ensures clients understand the nature of the coaching relationship, their rights, and the limits of confidentiality, fostering trust and ethical practice.

2. What steps can you take to ensure you stay within your scope of practice as a parenting coach?

Clearly define your services, avoid providing therapy or legal advice, and refer clients to appropriate professionals when necessary. Continuous professional development helps maintain ethical boundaries.

Key Takeaways

  • Informed consent is paramount for ethical coaching.
  • Clearly define your scope of practice and boundaries.
  • Prioritize the best interests of the children involved.
  • Seek legal counsel when facing complex legal situations.
  • Professional liability insurance provides essential protection.
