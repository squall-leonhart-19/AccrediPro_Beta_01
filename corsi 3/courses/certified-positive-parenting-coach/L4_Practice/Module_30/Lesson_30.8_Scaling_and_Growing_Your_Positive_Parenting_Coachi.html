Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, this is Emma. Thanks so much for scheduling this call. I know your time is valuable. To start, tell me a little about what prompted you to reach out about parenting coaching."
YOU:
"That's helpful context. I work with many parents facing similar challenges. Before we dive deeper, is there anything specific you'd like to get out of this call?"
Phase 2: Explore Challenges & Desired Outcomes 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe some of the specific parenting challenges you're facing with your teenager right now? What does a typical week look like?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address these challenges? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If we were working together and things were going exceptionally well, what would be different in your relationship with your teenager? What would a 'win' look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her challenges and desired outcomes]. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"The THRIVE method I use is designed to help parents like you regain control and build stronger relationships with their teens. Over 12 weeks, we'll focus on [mention 2-3 key aspects of your program, e.g., communication skills, setting boundaries, understanding teen development]. My clients typically report seeing noticeable improvements within the first month."
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with what you're looking for?"
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Great! Let me briefly outline the program details and investment, and then you can decide if it feels like the right fit."

Handling Objections

Mastering Common Objections

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that investment is a consideration. Many of my clients initially felt the same way. Can I ask, what's the cost of *not* addressing this challenge? What's the impact on your family, your stress levels, your overall well-being? We can also explore payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I need to discuss it with my partner first."
"That's perfectly reasonable. What information would be most helpful for you to share with your partner? I'm happy to provide a program overview, testimonials, or even schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions."
"I'm not sure if coaching really works."
"I appreciate your skepticism. Coaching is different from therapy or advice-giving. It's about empowering you to find your own solutions and create lasting change. I have several testimonials from parents who were initially unsure but experienced significant positive results. Would you like me to share a few of those with you?"
"I'm too busy right now."
"I hear you. Life is hectic! The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find times that work best for you. Think of this as an investment in creating more peace and harmony in your home, which ultimately saves you time and energy in the long run."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges and goals, and presenting your solution with confidence.

2. How should you respond to the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, then gently probe to understand their specific concerns (investment, time commitment, etc.) and offer additional information or support.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Understand your ideal client and anticipate their needs and objections.
  • Value-based pricing: Frame your pricing in terms of the value and transformation you provide.
  • Practice your script: The more you practice, the more confident and natural you'll sound.
  • Follow-up is crucial: Don't be afraid to follow up with prospects who expressed interest but didn't immediately enroll.
  • Believe in yourself: Your passion and belief in your ability to help others will shine through and attract clients.
