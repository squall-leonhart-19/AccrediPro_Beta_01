Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To make the best use of our time, I'd like to spend a few minutes understanding your situation, then share how I can help, and finally, discuss next steps if it feels like a good fit. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"I noticed you found me through Facebook. What specifically caught your eye?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the challenges you're facing with your son. What's been the most difficult aspect?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to improve the situation?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your relationship with your son look like in 6 months?"
YOU:
"What's at stake if you *don't* address this?"

YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her pain points]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on [briefly explain the THRIVE method]. I help parents like you rebuild connection with their teens by teaching practical communication skills and evidence-based strategies. Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's a commitment. However, consider this: what's the cost of not addressing this? How much time is being wasted on arguments and stress right now? My program is designed to be efficient, with just a few hours per week. And the long-term benefits of a stronger relationship with your son are invaluable."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (as every family is different), I can guarantee that I will provide you with the tools, strategies, and support you need to make positive changes. My program is based on evidence-based techniques, and most of my clients experience significant improvements. If you actively participate and implement what you learn, you'll see results."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. Perhaps I can address any remaining concerns right now?"
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I understand. Let's break down the value. My program includes [list inclusions: individual coaching, group sessions, resources, etc.]. This isn't just about quick fixes; it's an investment in your family's long-term well-being. Many of my clients find that the improved communication and reduced stress lead to increased productivity and happiness in other areas of their lives, too. We also have payment plans available to make it more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome, Present Your Approach & Credentials, and Close & Next Steps.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's more expensive than I expected?"

Break down the value of the program, highlight the inclusions, and emphasize that it's an investment in their family's long-term well-being. Offer payment plans if available.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident responses.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and its potential impact on clients' lives.
  • Believe in your ability to help families create positive change.
  • Remember that every "no" gets you closer to a "yes"!
