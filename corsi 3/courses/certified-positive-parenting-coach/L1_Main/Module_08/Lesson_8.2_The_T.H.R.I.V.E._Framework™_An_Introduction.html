The T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢ consists of six interconnected steps:

  1. Tune-In: Cultivating empathy and active listening to understand the child's and parent's perspectives.
  2. Harmonize: Identifying and addressing conflicting beliefs and triggers that hinder positive interactions.

  4. Implement: Collaboratively designing and implementing positive parenting strategies.

Applying T.H.R.I.V.E. in Different Parenting Scenarios

Case Study: The Bedtime Battle

Client: Sarah, a 38-year-old mother, and her 6-year-old son, Ethan.

Presenting Symptoms: Ethan consistently resists bedtime, leading to late nights, increased stress for Sarah, and morning fatigue for both.

Intervention:

  • Tune-In: Sarah spent time actively listening to Ethan's concerns about bedtime, discovering his fear of being alone in the dark.
  • Harmonize: They addressed Sarah's need for Ethan to be in bed by a certain time and Ethan's fear.
  • Reframe: Sarah reframed Ethan's resistance not as defiance but as an expression of his fear.
  • Implement: They created a bedtime routine that included a nightlight, a short story read together, and a "check-in" system where Sarah would quietly check on Ethan after he fell asleep.
  • Validate: Sarah acknowledged Ethan's feelings, saying, "It's okay to feel scared of the dark. I understand."
  • Empower: Ethan was given choices within the routine, such as which story to read, fostering a sense of control.

Outcomes: Within two weeks, Ethan's bedtime resistance significantly decreased, leading to improved sleep and a more positive parent-child relationship.

Case Study: Sibling Rivalry

Client: Maria, a 42-year-old mother, and her two children, 8-year-old Leo and 6-year-old Sofia.

Presenting Symptoms: Constant bickering and fighting between Leo and Sofia, causing stress for Maria and a tense home environment.

Intervention:

  • Tune-In: Maria listened to each child's perspective on the conflicts, identifying feelings of jealousy and competition.
  • Harmonize: She addressed the underlying needs for attention and fairness.
  • Reframe: Maria reframed the rivalry not as intentional malice but as attempts to gain attention and validation.
  • Implement: She implemented structured "special time" with each child individually and taught them conflict-resolution skills.
  • Validate: Maria validated each child's feelings, saying, "It's understandable to feel jealous when you think your sibling is getting more attention."
  • Empower: The children were empowered to come up with solutions to their conflicts, with Maria acting as a facilitator.

Outcomes: Over a month, the frequency and intensity of sibling conflicts decreased, and the children began to demonstrate improved communication and empathy towards each other.

Ethical Considerations

As a positive parenting coach, it's essential to adhere to ethical guidelines when using the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢.
