The Challenge: A Case Review

Lisa's Client Case

Client Profile:

Sarah, 52, recently divorced, feeling lost and uncertain about her future. She sought guidance on career direction.

Lisa's Approach:

Lisa conducted a reading focusing on Sarah's passions and potential. She identified several promising avenues but felt she lacked concrete advice.

Lisa's Concern:

"I pulled cards indicating creativity and leadership, but I didn't know how to translate that into actionable steps for Sarah. I worry I left her feeling more confused."

Your Teaching Strategy

Key Discussion Points

1

Validating Intuition

Acknowledge Lisa's intuitive strengths and emphasize the importance of trusting her initial impressions.

2

Translating Insights into Action

Guide Lisa in developing techniques for converting card meanings into tangible, practical suggestions. For example, "Creativity" + "Leadership" could suggest leading creative workshops.

3

Building Client Confidence

Discuss strategies for empowering clients to explore their potential, such as suggesting networking events or skill-building courses.

4

Business Acumen

Briefly discuss the importance of pricing, marketing, and client management. Share resources she might find helpful.

Constructive Feedback: A Sample Dialogue

A Coaching Framework

Start with Affirmation
"Lisa, I'm impressed by your ability to connect with your clients. I can see Sarah felt heard and understood."
Explore the Challenge
"You mentioned feeling unsure about translating the cards into actionable steps. What specific obstacles did you encounter?"
Offer Guidance
"One technique I use is to brainstorm at least three concrete actions for each card. For example, if a card suggests 'communication,' what actions might Sarah take to improve her communication skills or find a career where communication is key?"
Empower Growth
"What steps can you take to develop your confidence? Perhaps practicing with friends or family, or seeking feedback from other practitioners?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important element of providing constructive feedback to a new practitioner?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable sharing their challenges and asking questions.

2. How can you help a new practitioner translate card interpretations into actionable steps for their clients?

By brainstorming concrete actions associated with each card's meaning and encouraging the practitioner to think creatively about how those actions could benefit the client.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Focus on building confidence and empowering the mentee's own intuition.
  • Share your experiences, both successes and challenges.
  • Promote ethical practices and continued learning.
