Adapting to Client Personalities

Not every client is the same. Some clients are open and receptive, while others are skeptical and guarded. Some clients are detail-oriented, while others are more interested in the big picture. It's crucial to adapt the C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢ to each client's individual personality and needs. Here's a breakdown of how to adapt the framework for different client types:

The Skeptic Emphasize evidence-based insights. Frame interpretations as possibilities rather than certainties. Logical, rational, and objective. Avoid overly spiritual language. Concrete outcomes and practical applications.

Troubleshooting Challenges

Even with a structured framework, challenges can arise. Here are some common challenges and how to address them:

  1. Blanking Out: If you suddenly draw a blank, take a deep breath and reconnect with your intuition. Ask the client to rephrase their question or to provide more information. You can also refer back to the cards for inspiration.
  2. Difficult Cards: If you draw a card that seems negative or confusing, don't panic. Remember that every card has a positive aspect. Focus on the potential for growth and transformation.
  3. Resistant Clients: If a client is resistant to your interpretations, don't take it personally. Respect their perspective and try to find common ground. You can also offer alternative interpretations or suggest that they revisit the reading at a later time.
