Exploring Potential Consequences

Tarot can be a powerful tool for exploring the potential consequences of different choices. This allows clients to visualize possible outcomes and make decisions with greater awareness. Instead of asking "What will happen if I do X?", try framing the question as "What energies are likely to be activated if I choose X?".

Techniques:

  • Decision Spread: Design a spread with each card representing a different potential outcome based on a specific choice.
  • "Worst-Case/Best-Case" Scenario: Use cards to explore both the most challenging and most favorable potential outcomes.
  • Clarifying Questions: Ask follow-up questions based on the cards to delve deeper into the potential challenges and opportunities.

Aligning Decisions with Values

True empowerment comes from making decisions that resonate with a client's core values. Tarot can help bring these values to the surface, providing a compass for navigating complex choices. This builds on the "Assess Archetypes" stage of the C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢, as values are often tied to specific archetypal energies.

Techniques:

  • Values Identification Spread: Create a spread to identify the client's top 3-5 core values.
  • Values AlignmentSpread: Use cards to assess how well a potential decision aligns with each identified value.
  • Ethical Considerations: Discuss potential ethical implications of different choices, ensuring they align with the client's moral compass.

For example, if a client values security but is considering a risky career change, the cards can help explore how to mitigate risk while still pursuing their aspirations. A 2018 study in the *Journal of Vocational Behavior* (Brown et al., 2018) found that aligning career decisions with personal values led to increased job satisfaction and reduced turnover intention (Effect Size = 0.62, 95% CI [0.48, 0.76]).

Overcoming Fear and Self-Doubt

Fear and self-doubt can be significant barriers to making informed decisions. Tarot can help clients identify the root causes of these emotions and develop strategies for overcoming them. This connects to the "Release Limiting Beliefs" stage of the C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢, as fear is often rooted in negative beliefs about oneself or the world.

Techniques:

  • Fear Identification Spread: Use cards to pinpoint the specific fears and anxieties associated with a decision.
  • Challenging Negative Beliefs: Explore the validity of these fears, questioning the evidence that supports them.
  • Empowerment Cards: Draw cards that represent strength, courage, and resilience, focusing on the client's inner resources.

Setting Realistic Expectations

Unrealistic expectations can lead to disappointment and hinder effective decision-making. Tarot can help clients develop a balanced perspective, acknowledging both potential challenges and opportunities. This ties into the "Transform Perspective" stage of the C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢, encouraging clients to see situations from multiple angles.

Techniques:

  • Outcome Spread: Explore the most likely outcome, as well as potential variations and unexpected twists.
  • Risk Assessment: Identify potential risks and develop contingency plans.
  • Acceptance and Adaptability: Emphasize the importance of accepting uncertainty and being adaptable to changing circumstances.

Case Study: Career Decision

Case Study: Sarah, 32, Career Change

Client: Sarah, 32 years old, feeling unfulfilled in her current accounting job.

Presenting Symptoms: Anxiety about career stagnation, fear of financial instability, uncertainty about her true passions.

Intervention:

  1. Values Identification Spread: Identified creativity, independence, and financial security as core values.
  2. Decision Spread: Explored the potential outcomes of staying in her current job vs. pursuing freelance graphic design.
  3. Fear Identification Spread: Uncovered fears of failure, judgment from family, and lack of consistent income.
  4. Realistic Expectations Discussion: Acknowledged the challenges of freelancing, including inconsistent income and the need for self-promotion.

Outcomes: Sarah decided to take a part-time freelance graphic design role while maintaining her accounting job. This allowed her to explore her passion while maintaining financial security. After six months, she felt confident enough to transition to full-time freelancing, having built a solid client base and savings.
