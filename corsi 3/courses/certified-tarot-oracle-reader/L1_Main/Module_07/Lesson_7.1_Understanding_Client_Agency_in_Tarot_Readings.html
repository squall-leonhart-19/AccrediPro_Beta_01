Defining Client Agency

Client agency, in the context of tarot readings, refers to the client's ability to make independent choices and take responsibility for their actions and outcomes. It acknowledges that individuals are not merely passive recipients of fate but active participants in shaping their lives. As tarot readers, we must respect and nurture this agency. A reading should provide insight and guidance, but it should never dictate or remove the client's power to choose their own path. This is particularly important for women in career transition, as they seek to reclaim their power and create fulfilling new chapters.

Embracing client agency aligns with ethical tarot practice, fostering trust and promoting self-empowerment. By emphasizing free will, you encourage clients to view challenges as opportunities for growth and transformation. This approach is especially valuable for clients navigating significant life changes, such as career transitions, relationships, or personal development.

Pitfalls of Deterministic Readings

Deterministic readings, which present the future as fixed and inevitable, can be detrimental to client agency. Such readings often leave clients feeling disempowered, as if they have no control over their lives. This can lead to anxiety, resignation, and a reluctance to take action. For example, telling a client that they are "destined" to fail in a new venture can discourage them from even trying, regardless of their potential.

A 2018 study published in the *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* found that individuals who believe in free will are more likely to take responsibility for their actions and exhibit greater self-control (Rigoni et al., 2018). Conversely, those who believe in determinism may feel less accountable for their choices, leading to negative outcomes. As tarot readers, we have a responsibility to avoid deterministic language and instead frame our readings in a way that highlights the client's capacity for choice and change.

Framing Readings: Free Will & Responsibility

Framing your readings to emphasize free will and personal responsibility is crucial for empowering clients. Instead of presenting outcomes as predetermined, focus on the potential consequences of different choices and actions. Use language that encourages clients to reflect on their options and make informed decisions. For example, instead of saying "You will lose your job," you could say "The cards suggest that if you continue on your current path, you may face challenges in your job. However, you have the power to change this outcome by taking proactive steps to improve your performance or seek new opportunities."

When discussing potential challenges, frame them as opportunities for growth and learning. Encourage clients to view obstacles as temporary setbacks rather than insurmountable barriers. By emphasizing their ability to overcome challenges, you instill a sense of hope and resilience. A meta-analysis of 32 studies (n=5,487) published in *Health Psychology Review* in 2022 demonstrated that individuals who perceive challenges as opportunities for growth experience lower levels of stress and greater psychological well-being (Liu et al., 2022).

Power Dynamics in the Reader-Client Relationship

The tarot reading relationship inherently involves a power dynamic. Clients often come to readers seeking guidance and insight, which can create a sense of vulnerability. As readers, we must be mindful of this dynamic and strive to create a safe and equitable space for clients to explore their concerns. Avoid making authoritative pronouncements or using language that suggests you have absolute knowledge of their future. Instead, position yourself as a facilitator, guiding them toward their own inner wisdom.

Transparency is key to mitigating power imbalances. Explain your approach to tarot reading, emphasizing that the cards are tools for self-reflection and exploration, not instruments of divination. Encourage clients to ask questions and challenge your interpretations. By fostering open communication and mutual respect, you can create a more balanced and empowering reading experience. A 2021 study in *Counseling Psychology Quarterly* highlighted the importance of collaborative goal-setting in therapeutic relationships, finding that it leads to increased client engagement and positive outcomes (De Jong et al., 2021). This principle applies equally to tarot readings.

Case Study: Empowering vs. Undermining Agency

Case Study: Sarah, 38, Career Crossroads

Client: Sarah, a 38-year-old marketing professional, felt stuck in her current job and sought guidance on whether to pursue a career change.

Scenario 1: Undermining Agency

The reader, using a deterministic approach, told Sarah that the cards indicated she was "destined" to remain in her current job, as any attempt to change careers would lead to failure and financial hardship. Sarah left the reading feeling discouraged and hopeless, abandoning her aspirations for a more fulfilling career.

Scenario 2: Empowering Agency

The reader, using an empowering approach, acknowledged Sarah's desire for change and explored the potential challenges and opportunities associated with a career transition. The reader highlighted Sarah's strengths and skills, encouraging her to consider different options and develop a plan for achieving her goals. Sarah left the reading feeling motivated and empowered, taking proactive steps to explore new career paths and invest in her professional development. She successfully transitioned into a new role that aligned with her values and interests.

Analysis: This case study illustrates the profound impact that a tarot reader's approach can have on a client's sense of agency. By avoiding deterministic language and emphasizing Sarah's capacity for choice and change, the second reader empowered her to take control of her career and create a more fulfilling life.
