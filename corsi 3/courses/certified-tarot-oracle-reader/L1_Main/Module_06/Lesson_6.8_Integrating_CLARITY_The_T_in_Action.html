Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It takes courage to admit you're feeling stuck, especially after dedicating so much time to your career. I want you to know that feeling unfulfilled is a common experience, and it doesn't mean you've failed."
MARIA:
"It's just... I always thought I'd retire from teaching. Now I'm not so sure. I feel guilty for even thinking about leaving."
YOU:
"That guilt is understandable. You've invested so much in this profession. But let's reframe it: Exploring other options doesn't diminish your past accomplishments. It's about honoring your current needs and desires."
YOU:
"Before we explore solutions, I want to understand what truly matters to you in your work. What aspects of teaching brought you the most joy and fulfillment?"
MARIA:
"Definitely the kids. Seeing them learn and grow, making a real impact on their lives. That's what I loved."
YOU:
"Perfect. So, we know that making a positive impact on others is a core value for you. Let's explore how you can continue to honor that value, whether it's within or outside of the traditional classroom setting. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and rapport. It shows that you understand and acknowledge their experience, which makes them more receptive to your guidance.

2. What are some open-ended questions you could ask to help a client identify their core values?

Examples include: "What aspects of your work bring you the most joy?", "What do you value most in your career?", "What kind of impact do you want to make on the world?"
