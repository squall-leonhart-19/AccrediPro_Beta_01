The 'Pathways' Spread

The 'Pathways' Spread is designed to illuminate potential paths and choices available to the querent, aiding in decision-making. This spread is particularly useful when a client feels stuck or uncertain about their next steps. It helps to clarify options and reveal the potential outcomes of different choices, aligning with the 'Reveal Relationships' and 'Interpret Insights' stages of The C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢.

Spread Layout:

  1. Card 1: Current Situation
  2. Card 2: Pathway A - Potential outcome if this path is chosen.
  3. Card 3: Pathway B - Potential outcome if this path is chosen.
  4. Card 4: Pathway C - Potential outcome if this path is chosen.
  5. Card 5: Guidance - What to consider when making a choice.

Case Study: Maria, 48

Career Crossroads

Maria, a 48-year-old teacher, felt burned out and unsure whether to stay in her current job, pursue a promotion, or switch careers entirely. Using the 'Pathways' Spread, we explored each option. The spread revealed that staying put would lead to continued dissatisfaction, the promotion would bring temporary relief but long-term stress, and a career change aligned with her passions would require initial sacrifice but lead to greater fulfillment. Maria chose the career change, enrolling in a coding bootcamp. Six months later, she secured a remote developer role, earning 20% more than her teaching salary and enjoying greater work-life balance.

The 'Inner Child Healing' Spread

The 'Inner Child Healing' Spread is designed for exploring past experiences and emotional wounds that may be influencing present-day behaviors and relationships. It is particularly useful for clients with a history of trauma, neglect, or adverse childhood experiences. This spread aligns with the 'Assess Archetypes' and 'Liberate Limiting Beliefs' stages of The C.L.A.R.I.T.Y. Frameworkâ„¢, facilitating the integration of past experiences and the release of limiting beliefs.

Spread Layout:

  1. Card 1: The Inner Child's Current State
  2. Card 2: The Original Wound
  3. Card 3: The Inner Child's Needs
  4. Card 4: Healing Action to Take
  5. Card 5: Integration and Growth

Case Study: David, 35

Relationship Patterns

David, a 35-year-old marketing manager, struggled with forming lasting romantic relationships. He consistently chose partners who were emotionally unavailable, mirroring his distant father. The 'Inner Child Healing' Spread revealed the deep-seated wound of feeling unloved and unseen as a child. The spread highlighted the inner child's need for validation and acceptance. David began practicing self-compassion exercises and engaging in activities that nurtured his inner child, such as revisiting childhood hobbies. Over time, he developed a healthier sense of self-worth and attracted more emotionally available partners. After a year, David entered a fulfilling relationship, reporting a 70% increase in relationship satisfaction compared to his previous relationships.
