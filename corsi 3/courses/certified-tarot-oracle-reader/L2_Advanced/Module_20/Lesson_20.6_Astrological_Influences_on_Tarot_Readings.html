Reviewing Astrological Basics

Astrology, at its core, is the study of the movements and relative positions of celestial objects as a means for understanding and predicting earthly events. Understanding the basic components of astrology will provide a solid foundation for integrating it into your Tarot practice.

  • Zodiac Signs: Twelve divisions of the ecliptic, each associated with specific personality traits and energies.
  • Planets: Represent different aspects of the personality and life experiences. Each planet carries specific archetypal energy.
  • Houses: Twelve divisions of the birth chart, representing different areas of life, such as career, relationships, and home.

For example, Aries is associated with initiative and courage, while Venus representslove and beauty. The 1st House signifies self-identity and beginnings, whereas the 7th House represents partnerships.

Identifying Astrological Correspondences in Tarot

The Tarot and astrology have a long and intertwined history. Many Tarot scholars and practitioners believe that the Tarot was designed to reflect astrological principles. Recognizing these correspondences can add depth and nuance to your readings.

Hereâ€™s how to identify astrological correspondences in Tarot:

  • Major Arcana: Many Major Arcana cards are associated with specific planets or zodiac signs. For example, The Sun card corresponds to the Sun in astrology, representing vitality and joy.
  • Minor Arcana: Each suit of the Minor Arcana is associated with an element (Wands with Fire, Cups with Water, Swords with Air, and Pentacles with Earth), which in turn corresponds to astrological signs.
  • Court Cards: Often represent the qualities of the signs. For example, the Knight of Wands can represent the active, fiery energy of Sagittarius.

  • Relate to Tarot Cards: Connect the transit to relevant Tarot cards. For instance, if Saturn is transiting the 7th House (relationships), cards like the Two of Cups or the Ten of Swords could provide additional insight into the relationship dynamics.

  • Planetary Placements: The position of each planet in the chart indicates how that planetâ€™s energy is expressed in the personâ€™s life.
  • Aspects: The angles between planets reveal how different aspects of the personality interact.

Case Study: Sarah's Transformative Transit

Client: Sarah, 35 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt stuck in her career and was experiencing a general sense of dissatisfaction. She sought guidance on whether to make a significant career change.

Intervention: I combined a Tarot reading with an analysis of Sarah's astrological transits. The Tarot reading revealed the Three of Swords (heartbreak), the Tower (sudden upheaval), and the Eight of Pentacles reversed (stagnation in work). Astrologically, Sarah was experiencing a transit of Uranus conjunct her Midheaven (career point), indicating a period of sudden and unexpected changes in her professional life.

Outcomes: I advised Sarah that the transits and Tarot cards both suggested that she was on the cusp of a significant transformation. I encouraged her to embrace the changes, even if they felt unsettling at first. Within six months, Sarah was laid off from her job, but she used the opportunity to pursue her lifelong dream of becoming a freelance artist. She reported feeling more fulfilled and aligned with her true purpose.
