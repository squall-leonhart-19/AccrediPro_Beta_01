Advanced Centering & Connecting

Building a strong connection with your querent is paramount, especially when facing skepticism or resistance. Remember, the Centering & Connecting stage is all about creating a safe and trusting space. Consider these advanced techniques:

  • Mirroring: Subtly mirroring the querent's body language and tone can create a sense of rapport. A 2019 study in the Journal of Nonverbal Behavior found that mirroring enhances trust and connection.
  • Empathy Statements: Use statements that demonstrate your understanding of their feelings, such as, "I can sense that you're feeling uncertain about this situation."
  • Setting Intentions: Clearly state your intention to provide guidance and support, emphasizing that the reading is a collaborative process.

Refining Listening & Learning Skills

Effective Listening & Learning goes beyond simply hearing the querent's question. It involves actively seeking to understand their underlying motivations, fears, and desires. Here are some advanced strategies:

  • The "5 Whys" Technique: Ask "why" repeatedly to drill down to the root cause of their concern. For example:
    • Querent: "I'm unhappy in my job."
    • You: "Why are you unhappy?"
    • Querent: "Because I feel unfulfilled."
    • You: "Why do you feel unfulfilled?" (and so on)
  • Identifying Language Patterns: Pay attention to the words and phrases the querent uses, as they can reveal hidden beliefs and assumptions.
  • Intuitive Listening: Trust your intuition and allow yourself to pick up on subtle cues and energies.
