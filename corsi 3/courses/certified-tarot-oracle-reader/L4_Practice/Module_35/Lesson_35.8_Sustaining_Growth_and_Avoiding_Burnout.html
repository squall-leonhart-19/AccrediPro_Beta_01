Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if tarot is right for me."
"I understand your hesitation. Many people have misconceptions about tarot. It's not about predicting the future; it's about gaining insights into your current situation and empowering you to make conscious choices. Would you be open to learning more about how I use tarot as a tool for self-discovery?"
"I don't have the time."
"I hear you. Time is precious. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your life. We can work together to create a schedule that works for you. Think of it this way: what if investing this time now could save you years of feeling lost and unfulfilled? What's that worth to you?"
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can promise that I will provide you with the tools, support, and guidance you need to create positive change in your life. Your results depend on your willingness to engage in the process and take action on the insights you receive. Most of my clients report feeling significantly more clarity and purpose within the first few weeks."
"I'm worried I won't be able to do it."
"That's a completely normal feeling! I'm here to support you every step of the way. We'll break down the process into manageable steps, and I'll provide you with personalized guidance and encouragement. You are capable of far more than you think. Let's unlock that potential together."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call?

Key elements include actively listening, asking open-ended questions, finding common ground, and expressing empathy.

2. How can you handle the objection "I need to think about it" effectively?

Acknowledge their need for time, then gently inquire about the specific concerns they need to consider (e.g., investment, time commitment). Offer additional information or support to address those concerns.

3. What is the importance of practicing your pricing presentation?

Practicing your pricing presentation helps you deliver it with confidence and clarity, conveying the value of your services and increasing the likelihood of closing the sale.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's problem deeply allows you to tailor your approach and demonstrate the value of your services.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence can turn potential roadblocks into opportunities to close the sale.
  • Practicing your pricing presentation and call-to-action will empower you to confidently guide prospects toward becoming clients.
  • Realistic income goals are achievable with consistent effort and a genuine desire to help others.
