Welcome to Pricing Strategies & Packages!

Welcome back! In the previous lessons, we covered building your website, leveraging social media, crafting compelling content, and building your email list. Now, it's time to put all that hard work into practice and determine how much to charge for your valuable services. This lesson will guide you in setting prices that reflect your expertise, attract clients, and ensure your financial sustainability. Remember, you are offering a unique and transformative experience, and your pricing should reflect that.

1. Researching Industry Standards

Before setting your prices, it's crucial to understand the current market landscape. Start by researching the pricing of similar services offered by other tarot and oracle readers. This will give you a benchmark and help you position your offerings effectively.

Consider these factors when conducting your research:

  • Experience Level: Are you comparing your prices to established readers with years of experience or to newer readers just starting out?
  • Specialization: Do you specialize in a particular area, such as career readings or relationship readings? Readers with niche expertise may be able to charge more.
  • Reading Format: Are you offering in-person readings, phone readings, video call readings, or written readings? Different formats may command different prices.
  • Location: Prices can vary depending on your geographic location. Readers in major metropolitan areas may be able to charge more than those in smaller towns.

Income Example: Sarah, a tarot reader in Austin, Texas, researched local pricing and found that most readers with 2-5 years of experience charged between $75-$125 for a 60-minute reading. This gave her a solid starting point for determining her own pricing.

2. Calculating Your Costs

Understanding your business costs is essential for setting prices that ensure profitability. Don't just pull a number out of thin air! You need to know how much it costs you to operate your business.

Here's a breakdown of the costs you should consider:

  • Time: How much time do you spend preparing for, conducting, and following up on each reading? Factor in time for marketing, administrative tasks, and continuing education.
  • Materials: Do you use any physical materials, such as tarot decks, crystals, or candles? What about software costs or subscription fees for online platforms?
  • Marketing: How much are you spending on marketing and advertising? This includes website hosting, social media ads, and email marketing software.
  • Overhead: Do you have any overhead costs, such as rent for a physical office space or utilities?
  • Taxes: Don't forget to factor in taxes! Depending on your location and business structure, you may need to pay income tax, self-employment tax, and sales tax.

Example Calculation: Let's say you estimate that you spend a total of 2 hours on each reading (including prep time, the reading itself, and follow-up). Your hourly operating costs (including marketing, software, and other expenses) are $20. To simply break even, you'd need to charge at least $40 per reading. However, this doesn't account for profit. To generate income, you'll need to charge significantly more.

3. Developing Different Pricing Tiers

Offering different pricing tiers can help you attract a wider range of clients with varying budgets and needs. Consider creating packages that offer different levels of service and value.

Here are some ideas for pricing tiers:

  • Basic: A shorter reading focusing on a specific question or area of concern. This could be a good option for clients who are new to tarot or oracle readings.
  • Standard: A more in-depth reading covering multiple areas of life. This could include a general overview of the client's current situation, as well as guidance on specific issues.
  • Premium: A longer, more comprehensive reading with added benefits, such as a personalized follow-up report or a recorded audio file. This could be a good option for clients who are seeking deep insights and transformative guidance.

4. Creating Attractive Packages

Packages can be a great way to offer added value to your clients and increase your revenue. When designing your packages, think about what additional services or products you can include to make them more appealing.

Here are some ideas for creating attractive packages:

  • Bundled Readings: Offer a discount for clients who purchase multiple readings at once. For example, you could offer a package of three 60-minute readings for the price of two.
  • Reading + Coaching: Combine a tarot or oracle reading with a coaching session to help clients implement the insights they receive.
  • Reading + Product: Include a physical product, such as a crystal, essential oil, or handmade talisman, with your reading.
  • Subscription Model: Offer weekly or monthly readings at a discounted rate for subscribers.

Package Example: "The Transformation Package" includes a 90-minute tarot reading, a personalized action plan, and a 30-minute follow-up coaching call to help clients integrate the reading's insights into their daily lives. This package is priced at $250.

5. Clearly Communicating Your Pricing

Transparency is key when it comes to pricing. Make sure your pricing information is clearly displayed on your website and marketing materials. Avoid hidden fees or surprises.

Here are some tips for communicating your pricing effectively:

  • Create a Pricing Page: Dedicate a page on your website to outlining your services and pricing.
  • Use Clear Language: Avoid jargon or ambiguous terms. Use simple, straightforward language that your clients can easily understand.
  • Highlight Value: Focus on the benefits that clients will receive from your services, rather than just the price.
  • Offer Payment Options: Make it easy for clients to pay you by offering multiple payment options, such as credit card, PayPal, or bank transfer.

Case Study: Emily's Pricing Success

Client: Emily, a tarot reader with 1 year of experience

Presenting Problem: Emily was struggling to attract clients and felt her prices were too low.

Intervention: Emily researched industry standards, calculated her costs, and developed three pricing tiers: Basic (30 minutes, $45), Standard (60 minutes, $85), and Premium (90 minutes with follow-up report, $150). She also created a "Clarity Package" that included a 60-minute reading and a 30-minute coaching call for $125.

Outcomes: After implementing her new pricing strategy, Emily saw a 40% increase in bookings within the first month. Clients appreciated the transparency and the value offered by her packages. She also felt more confident in her pricing, which translated into a more positive and professional experience for her clients.
