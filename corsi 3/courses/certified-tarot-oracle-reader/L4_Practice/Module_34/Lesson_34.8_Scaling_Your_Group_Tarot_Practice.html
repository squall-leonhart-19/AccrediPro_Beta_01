Handling Objections with Grace

Common Concerns & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time commitment."
"I understand. The program requires about 3 hours per week, including the weekly session. However, many participants find that the energy and clarity they gain actually *saves* them time in the long run. How do you currently spend your time each week, and where might you be able to carve out a few hours for yourself?"
"I'm worried I won't connect with the other women in the group."
"That's a valid concern. I carefully curate the groups to ensure a supportive and inclusive environment. I also facilitate activities designed to foster connection and vulnerability. Many lasting friendships have formed within these groups. Plus, if you truly don't feel it's the right fit after the first two sessions, I offer a full refund."
"It's a bit more than I was planning to spend."
"I appreciate you being upfront. Can I ask, what value do you place on finding your purpose and connecting with a supportive community? Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key questions to ask during the "Exploring Needs" phase of a discovery call for a group program?

Possible answers include: What are your biggest challenges right now? What does your ideal future look like? What are you hoping to gain from a group program?

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the time commitment required for a group program?

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the potential time-saving benefits of the program, and explore how they might be able to carve out time in their schedule.

3. What are two effective closing lines to use when inviting a prospect to join your group program?

Examples include: "Are you ready to say 'yes' to yourself and join our community?" or "Based on everything you've shared, I truly believe this program is the perfect next step for you. Are you ready to get started?"

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your discovery calls to build rapport, understand needs, present your program, and invite enrollment.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide confident responses that highlight the value of your program.
  • Present your pricing clearly and offer flexible payment options to make your program accessible.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently invite prospects to join your transformative group program.
  • Group programs offer a powerful way to scale your income and impact.
