Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Estrogen Dominance/Hormone Imbalance

Endometriosis, migraines, and acne suggest a possible estrogen dominance pattern. The OCP could be contributing. A full hormone panel is essential.

2

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Migraines, allergies, IBS-D, and acne can overlap with MCAS. Consider triggers and potential antihistamine support.

3

Mitochondrial Dysfunction

Extreme fatigue and brain fog could point to impaired mitochondrial function. Consider targeted support with CoQ10, PQQ, and B vitamins.

4

Gluten Sensitivity/Celiac Disease

Migraines, fatigue, and IBS-D can be linked to gluten sensitivity or celiac disease. Consider testing or a trial elimination diet.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address liver function in Eleanor's case, even though her ALT elevation is only mildly elevated?

Even a mild elevation in ALT can indicate impaired detoxification capacity, which can exacerbate other symptoms like fatigue, hormonal imbalances, and skin issues. Supporting liver function can improve overall well-being.

2. What are some key considerations when addressing hormonal imbalances in a client taking the oral contraceptive pill?

The OCP can mask underlying hormonal issues and impact nutrient absorption. It's important to understand the client's goals regarding the OCP (e.g., managing endometriosis symptoms) and work with their physician to explore alternative management options if appropriate.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-based approach that considers the interconnectedness of various physiological processes.
  • Prioritizing interventions based on the most likely root causes can lead to more effective and sustainable outcomes.
  • It's crucial to identify and address potential referral triggers to ensure client safety and appropriate medical management.
  • A phased intervention plan allows for gradual and personalized adjustments based on the client's response and progress.
  • Consider the impact of medications and work collaboratively with other healthcare providers to optimize the client's care.
