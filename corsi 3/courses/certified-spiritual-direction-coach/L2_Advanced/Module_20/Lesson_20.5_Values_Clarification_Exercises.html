Using Values Cards or Questionnaires

One popular approach to values clarification involves using values cards or questionnaires. These tools typically present a list of values (e.g., honesty, creativity, service, security) from which the directee selects those that resonate most strongly. Let's look at each:

  • Values Cards: These involve physically sorting through cards, each labeled with a specific value. This kinesthetic approach can be particularly helpful for directees who learn best by doing.
  • Values Questionnaires: These are structured assessments that often use rating scales to quantify the importance of different values. They can provide a more data-driven approach to values clarification.

After identifying their top values, directees can then examine potential conflicts between them and prioritize them. For instance, a directee might value both "financial security" and "creative expression," but find that their current job prioritizes the former at the expense of the latter. By recognizing this conflict, they can begin to explore ways to bring more balance into their lives.

A 2021 study published in the *Journal of Vocational Behavior* found that individuals who align their careers with their values report higher levels of job satisfaction and overall well-being (r = 0.42, p < 0.01). This highlights the importance of values clarification in career counseling and spiritual direction.

Connecting Values to Decision-Making

The ultimate goal of values clarification is to help directees make decisions that are aligned with their core values. This involves examining how their values influence their choices in various areas of their lives, including career, relationships, and personal growth. For example, if a directee values "authenticity" and "connection," they might choose to end a relationship that feels superficial or inauthentic, even if it provides a sense of security.

To facilitate this process, you can ask probing questions such as:

  • "How does this decision align with your top values?"
  • "What values are in conflict in this situation?"
  • "If you were to make a decision based solely on your values, what would it be?"

By exploring these questions, directees can gain clarity on their priorities and make choices that are more congruent with their true selves. This alignment is a crucial step in their spiritual journey, fostering a sense of inner peace and purpose.

Consider the case of a woman struggling with a difficult career decision. She is torn between staying in a stable but unfulfilling job and pursuing a more risky but passion-driven opportunity. Through values clarification, she discovers that her top values are "creativity," "autonomy," and "impact." Armed with this knowledge, she realizes that staying in her current job would be a betrayal of her core values. She decides to take the leap and pursue the new opportunity, even though it involves some uncertainty. This is a testament to the power of values-based decision-making.

A 2023 meta-analysis of 32 studies (n=6,458) published in the *Journal of Positive Psychology* found a significant positive correlation (r = 0.35, 95% CI [0.28, 0.42]) between values alignment and overall life satisfaction. This underscores the importance of helping directees live in accordance with their values.

Case Study: David's Career Discernment

Client: David, 45 years old

Presenting Symptoms: David, a marketing executive, felt increasingly dissatisfied with his career. He reported feeling disconnected from his work and questioned its overall purpose. He was considering a career change but felt overwhelmed and uncertain about his next steps.

Intervention: As his spiritual director, I guided David through a series of values clarification exercises, including values card sorting and reflective journaling. He identified his top five values as "integrity," "creativity," "service," "learning," and "balance." We then explored how his current job aligned with these values and identified areas of conflict.

Outcomes: David realized that his current job only partially aligned with his values of "integrity" and "learning," but it significantly lacked "creativity," "service," and "balance." This realization empowered him to explore alternative career paths that would better align with his core values. He ultimately decided to transition into a role in the non-profit sector, where he could use his marketing skills to promote a cause he believed in. He reported feeling a renewed sense of purpose and fulfillment in his work.

This case study shows how values clarification can be a powerful tool for helping directees navigate career transitions and make choices that are aligned with their deepest values. By uncovering what truly matters to them, they can find greater meaning and purpose in their work and lives.
