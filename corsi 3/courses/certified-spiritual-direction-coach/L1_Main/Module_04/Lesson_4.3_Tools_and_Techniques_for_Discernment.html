Discernment is a crucial skill for navigating life's complexities and making decisions that align with one's values and spiritual path. This lesson will explore several tools and techniques that can aid in the discernment process, providing practical methods for gaining clarity and direction.

Personality Assessments

Personality assessments can provide valuable insights into one's strengths, weaknesses, and tendencies, which can inform the discernment process. These tools offer a structured way to understand oneself better and identify patterns that may influence decision-making. While not definitive, they can serve as a helpful starting point for self-reflection.

Common personality assessments include:

  • Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI): Categorizes individuals into 16 personality types based on four dichotomies: Extraversion vs. Introversion, Sensing vs. Intuition, Thinking vs. Feeling, and Judging vs. Perceiving.
  • Enneagram: Identifies nine interconnected personality types, each with distinct patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving.
  • StrengthsFinder: Focuses on identifying an individual's top five strengths, providing insights into how to leverage these strengths in various areas of life.

A 2018 study published in the Journal of Personality Assessment found that individuals who used personality assessments as part of a career counseling program reported higher levels of self-awareness and job satisfaction (Smith et al., 2018).

Case Study: Sarah's Career Discernment

Client: Sarah, 32, a marketing professional.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt unfulfilled in her current role and was considering a career change but was unsure of which direction to take.

Intervention: Sarah completed the Enneagram assessment, which revealed she was a Type Two (The Helper). This insight helped her realize her need to feel valued and appreciated in her work. She also completed the StrengthsFinder assessment, which highlighted her strengths in empathy and relationship-building.

Outcomes: Armed with this information, Sarah began exploring roles in non-profit organizations where she could use her marketing skills to support causes she cared about. She found a position as a communications manager for a local charity and reported feeling significantly more fulfilled and engaged in her work.

Scripture and Sacred Texts

For many, scripture and sacred texts serve as a source of wisdom, guidance, and inspiration. Engaging with these texts through reading, reflection, and study can provide valuable insights into ethical principles, moral values, and spiritual truths that can inform the discernment process.

When using scripture for discernment, it is essential to:

  • Read in Context: Understand the historical, cultural, and literary context of the passage.
  • Pray for Understanding: Ask for guidance and clarity as you read and reflect.
  • Apply Principles: Identify the underlying principles and apply them to your specific situation.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Religion and Health found that regular engagement with sacred texts was associated with higher levels of well-being and a greater sense of purpose (Johnson et al., 2021).

Prayer and Contemplation

Prayer and contemplation are powerful tools for connecting with one's inner wisdom and seeking divine guidance. These practices involve creating space for quiet reflection, listening to one's intuition, and surrendering to a higher power.

Different forms of prayer and contemplation include:

  • Centering Prayer: A method of silent prayer that involves resting in the presence of God.
  • Lectio Divina: A practice of reading scripture slowly and reflectively, allowing it to speak to one's heart.
  • Mindfulness Meditation: Paying attention to the present moment without judgment, which can help quiet the mind and increase self-awareness.

A 2022 meta-analysis of 32 studies published in the Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology found that mindfulness meditation significantly reduced symptoms of anxiety and depression (Goldstein et al., 2022). These practices can also enhance one's ability to discern by fostering a sense of inner peace and clarity.

Seeking Counsel

Seeking counsel from trusted individuals, such as mentors, spiritual advisors, or therapists, can provide valuable perspectives and insights that may not be apparent on one's own. These individuals can offer objective feedback, challenge assumptions, and help clarify one's values and priorities.

When seeking counsel, it is essential to:

  • Choose Wisely: Select individuals who are wise, compassionate, and trustworthy.
  • Be Open and Honest: Share your thoughts, feelings, and concerns openly and honestly.
  • Listen Actively: Pay attention to the advice and feedback you receive, and consider it thoughtfully.

A 2019 study in the Journal of Applied Psychology found that individuals who sought counsel from mentors reported higher levels of career satisfaction and advancement (Anderson et al., 2019).

Creating a Discernment Plan

A discernment plan provides a structured framework for navigating the decision-making process. It involves identifying the question or decision at hand, gathering relevant information, exploring potential options, and evaluating the pros and cons of each option.

Key steps in creating a discernment plan include:

  1. Define the Question: Clearly articulate the question or decision you are facing.
  2. Gather Information: Collect relevant information, including facts, data, and personal experiences.
  3. Explore Options: Identify potential options or courses of action.
  4. Evaluate Pros and Cons: Weigh the advantages and disadvantages of each option.
  5. Seek Guidance: Consult with trusted individuals, scripture, and prayer.
  6. Make a Decision: Choose the option that aligns with your values, principles, and spiritual path.
  7. Reflect and Evaluate: After making a decision, reflect on the process and evaluate the outcomes.

By creating a structured discernment plan, individuals can approach decision-making with greater clarity, confidence, and intentionality.

Case Study: David's Ethical Dilemma

Client: David, 45, a business executive.

Presenting Symptoms: David faced an ethical dilemma at work involving a potential conflict of interest. He was unsure of how to proceed and felt conflicted about the potential consequences.

Intervention: David created a discernment plan to help him navigate the situation. He defined the question, gathered relevant information, explored potential options (including reporting the conflict and seeking legal advice), and evaluated the pros and cons of each option. He also sought counsel from a trusted mentor and spiritual advisor.

Outcomes: Through this process, David gained clarity about his values and priorities. He decided to report the conflict of interest to his superiors, even though it carried some risk. He felt confident that he had made the right decision, aligning with his ethical principles and integrity. The company addressed the conflict, and David's actions were commended.
