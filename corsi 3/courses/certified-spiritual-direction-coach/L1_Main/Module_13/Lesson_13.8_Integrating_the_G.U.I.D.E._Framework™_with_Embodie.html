Setting the Stage

This lab focuses on a "deep dive" scenario, where you're helping a client uncover a hidden block or limiting belief. The key is to create a safe space and ask powerful, open-ended questions.

"I keep starting projects but never finishing them. It's like I self-sabotage."
Background

Stated Goal

Hidden Block
Fear of success. Subconscious belief that she doesn't deserve it after "failing" to maintain her career earlier.

Your Coaching Script

Use these questions to gently guide Maria toward her hidden block. Pause often and allow space for silence.

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing this. When you say you 'self-sabotage,' what does that look like in practice?"
MARIA:
"I don't know... I just lose momentum. I find other things to do, or I get overwhelmed and give up."
YOU:
"And what's the feeling *behind* that overwhelm? If that feeling had a name, what would it be?"
MARIA:
"(Pause) I guess... fear. Fear that I can't actually do it."
YOU:
"Fear that you can't do it... Tell me more about that fear. Where does it come from?"
MARIA:
"Well, I guess it's because I haven't really succeeded in a long time. I'm afraid of putting myself out there and failing again."
YOU:
"So, there's a fear of failing. What else is there? What would it mean to actually *succeed*?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria has an "aha" moment and connects to a deeper truth.
  • She expresses vulnerability and shares something personal.
  • Her body language shifts (e.g., relaxes her shoulders, makes eye contact).
  • She gains clarity about her limiting beliefs.
  • She feels empowered to take action, even if it's a small step.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is silence so important in a deep dive coaching session?

Silence allows the client to process their thoughts and feelings, and it creates space for deeper insights to emerge.

2. What's the difference between a leading question and an open-ended question?

A leading question suggests the answer, while an open-ended question invites the client to explore their own thoughts and feelings.

Key Takeaways

  • Deep listening is about being fully present with the client, without judgment or agenda.
  • Powerful questions can help clients uncover hidden blocks and limiting beliefs.
  • Silence is a valuable tool for creating space and allowing insights to emerge.
  • Empathy and vulnerability are essential for building trust and rapport.
  • The goal is to empower the client to take action and create lasting change.
