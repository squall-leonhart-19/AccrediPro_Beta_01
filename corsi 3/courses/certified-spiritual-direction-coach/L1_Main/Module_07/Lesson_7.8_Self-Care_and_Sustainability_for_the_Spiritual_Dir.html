Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your story with me. It takes courage to explore these deeper questions. Many find themselves at a crossroads like this, especially after years of dedicating themselves to others. Know that you're not alone."
MARIA:
"Thank you. It's just... I've spent so much time taking care of others, I've lost sight of what *I* believe in."
YOU:
"That's a very common experience, Maria. It's like the well has been emptied, and now it's time to refill it. As we work together, we'll explore what truly nourishes your spirit and helps you reconnect with your inner wisdom."
YOU:
"Let's start by exploring your past experiences with spirituality. What practices or beliefs resonated with you at different points in your life?"
MARIA:
"Well, as I mentioned, I was raised Catholic... but I also remember feeling peaceful in nature as a child. I loved hiking and being near the water."
YOU:
"That's wonderful. Both formal religion and experiences in nature can be powerful pathways to the divine. We can explore these further to see how they might inform your spiritual direction now."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses a sense of relief and validation.
  • She shares more details about her past experiences and beliefs.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Maria's feelings of disconnection?

Validating her feelings creates a safe space for her to explore her spirituality without judgment. It also builds trust and rapport.

2. What's the risk of imposing your own spiritual beliefs on Maria?

Imposing your beliefs can alienate Maria and prevent her from discovering her own authentic spiritual path. It's crucial to remain neutral and supportive.

Key Takeaways

  • Spiritual direction coaching is about facilitating exploration, not providing answers.
  • It's crucial to create a safe and non-judgmental space for clients to share their experiences.
  • Validating a client's feelings and experiences is essential for building trust and rapport.
  • Avoid imposing your own beliefs or offering quick fixes; focus on empowering clients to discover their own path.
