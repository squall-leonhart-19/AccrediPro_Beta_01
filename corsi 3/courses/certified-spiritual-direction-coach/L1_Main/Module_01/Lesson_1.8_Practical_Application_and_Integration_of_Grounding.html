Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on Grounding & Gathering. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're feeling a deep yearning for connection and a sense of peace. Many people experience this, especially after significant life changes. Let's explore what 'grounding' and 'gathering' mean to you right now."
MARIA:
"Grounding? I guess it means feeling stable, not so scattered. Gathering‚Ä¶ maybe collecting the good things, the things that nourish me?"

"Those are beautiful interpretations. Let's use those as our starting points. What used to make you feel grounded, even before your divorce?"
MARIA:
"Going to church, definitely. And spending time in nature. I used to hike a lot."
YOU:
"Okay. So, church and nature. What about gathering? What activities or people filled you up, made you feel connected and joyful?"

YOU:
"Maria, it sounds like you already have a clear sense of what nourishes your soul. Our work together will be about finding ways to reintroduce those elements into your life, adapted to your present circumstances. Does that resonate with you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask the client to define "grounding" and "gathering" in their own words?

It avoids assumptions and ensures the coaching is tailored to their individual understanding and experience.

2. What's the risk of focusing too much on the client's past experiences?

While the past is important, dwelling on it can prevent the client from focusing on present-day possibilities and taking action.

Key Takeaways

  • Grounding & Gathering involves helping clients reconnect with their spiritual core and find sources of nourishment.
  • Always ask the client to define key terms like "grounding" and "gathering" personally.
  • Validate the client's feelings and experiences without immediately jumping to solutions.
  • Gently guide the client toward identifying small, actionable steps they can take in the present.
  • Empathy and active listening are crucial for building trust and rapport.
