Your Mentoring Strategy

Essential Points to Guide Alicia

1

Scope of Practice

Reiterate the importance of staying within the boundaries of spiritual direction coaching. Emphasize that while empathy is important, coaching is not a substitute for therapy.

2

Assessing Client Suitability

Teach Alicia how to evaluate whether a potential client is a good fit for her services. Discuss questions she can ask to determine if the client's needs align with her expertise.

3

Referral Resources

Help Alicia compile a list of trusted therapists, counselors, and other professionals she can refer clients to when necessary. This demonstrates ethical practice and client care.

4

Building Confidence

Remind Alicia of her strengths and the value she brings to her clients. Encourage her to focus on her skills and celebrate her successes, while acknowledging that it‚Äôs okay to refer out when necessary.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between spiritual direction coaching and therapy?

Spiritual direction coaching focuses on exploring values and purpose, while therapy addresses mental health conditions.

2. What is the importance of establishing a referral network as a spiritual direction coach?

A referral network ensures that clients receive appropriate care if their needs fall outside the scope of spiritual direction coaching.
