Excited but apprehensive about working with clients.

Background
Former teacher, strong interpersonal skills, limited experience in spiritual direction.
Strengths
Empathetic, intuitive, and a natural listener.
Growth Areas
Needs help setting boundaries and managing client expectations.
Her Question
"I'm not sure how to handle a client who keeps asking for advice."

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with a client, Emily, 50, who is going through a career transition. Every session, she asks me what I think she should do. I keep redirecting her back to her own intuition, but she gets frustrated. I don't want to give her advice, but I also don't want to lose her as a client."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What specific techniques has Sarah used to redirect the client?
  • Has she explicitly discussed the difference between spiritual direction and therapy/coaching?
  • What are Emily's underlying needs and expectations?
  • Is Sarah experiencing countertransference (e.g., a need to be seen as an expert)?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reiterate the Role of Spiritual Direction

Emphasize that spiritual direction is about facilitating inner exploration, not providing solutions. It's about being with the client, not doing for the client.

2

Teach Boundary-Setting Techniques

Provide specific phrases and strategies for gently redirecting the client's requests for advice. For example: "It sounds like you're looking for guidance. Let's explore what your own inner wisdom has to say about this."

3

Explore Client Expectations

Suggest that Sarah have an open conversation with Emily about her expectations for spiritual direction. "I'm noticing you often ask for my advice. Let's talk about what you're hoping to get from our sessions."

4

Address Countertransference

Help Sarah explore her own feelings and motivations. Is she trying to prove herself? Does she fear losing the client? Understanding her own reactions can help her respond more effectively.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's completely normal to feel challenged by clients who want advice. It shows you're committed to staying within the scope of spiritual direction."
Inquire About Her Approach
"Tell me more about how you've been redirecting Emily. What specific techniques have you tried?"
Offer Alternative Strategies
"One thing I've found helpful is to say something like, 'What possibilities feel most aligned with your values?' Or, 'What does your heart tell you about this?'"
Empower Her to Experiment
"Next session, try one of these techniques and see how Emily responds. We can discuss it afterward. Remember, it's okay if it doesn't work perfectly right away."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of supervision in spiritual direction coaching?

The primary goal is to support the coach's development and ensure ethical practice, not to dictate client outcomes.

2. How can a supervisor help a new coach address countertransference?

By providing a safe space for the coach to explore their own feelings and motivations related to the client.

You're Becoming a Leader!

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is crucial for maintaining ethical standards in spiritual direction.
  • New coaches often need guidance in setting boundaries and managing client expectations.
  • Addressing countertransference is essential for effective coaching.
  • Providing concrete tools and techniques empowers new coaches to develop their skills.
