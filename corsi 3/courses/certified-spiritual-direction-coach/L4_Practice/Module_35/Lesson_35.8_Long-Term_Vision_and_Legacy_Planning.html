Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's so lovely to connect with you today. Thank you for reaching out. I always start by asking what resonated with you on my website that made you schedule this call?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! I'm glad something resonated with you. So, Susan, tell me, what's been going on in your life that led you to seek out spiritual direction coaching?"
Phase 2: Explore Her Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about this feeling of being lost. What does that look and feel like in your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to find more meaning and purpose?"
YOU:
"If you could design your ideal life, Susan, what would that look like? What would you be doing, and how would you be feeling?"
Phase 3: Present Your Coaching Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I hear that you're feeling lost, longing for purpose, and ready to create a more meaningful life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My spiritual direction coaching program is designed to help you connect with your inner wisdom, identify your core values, and create a clear path forward. We'll work together over 12 weeks to explore your beliefs, uncover your passions, and design a life that aligns with your authentic self. Many of my clients experience a profound sense of clarity and purpose within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Invite & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been seeking? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this right now."
"I understand that time is precious. However, think about the long-term benefits. Investing in your spiritual well-being can actually create more time and energy by reducing stress and increasing your focus. My clients find that even dedicating just a few hours a week to this work makes a significant difference. Where could you find an extra 2 hours a week to dedicate to yourself?"
"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask, what value do you place on feeling fulfilled and living a life of purpose? Many of my clients see this as an investment in their overall well-being, which ultimately saves them money on things like stress-related health issues or impulsive purchases. We also have a payment plan option to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it and pray on it."
"That's completely understandable. I honor your process. What specific questions or concerns are you hoping to gain clarity on through prayer and reflection? Perhaps I can provide some additional information or resources to support your discernment."
"I've tried other things before, and they haven't worked."
"I hear your hesitation. What was missing in those past experiences? My approach is different because it focuses on connecting you with your own inner wisdom and creating a personalized path that aligns with your unique spiritual journey. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution; it's a collaborative process tailored to your specific needs and desires."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing gratitude for their time, asking about what resonated with them on your website, and genuinely listening to their initial story.

2. How do you effectively address the objection, "I'm not sure if I can afford it?"

Acknowledge their concern, explore the value they place on feeling fulfilled, highlight the long-term benefits, and offer payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Building genuine rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding your prospect's needs and desires is essential for tailoring your coaching approach.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and addressing objections are key to closing the sale.
  • Focus on the value and long-term benefits your spiritual direction coaching provides.
  • Believe in your ability to help others and create a thriving practice.
