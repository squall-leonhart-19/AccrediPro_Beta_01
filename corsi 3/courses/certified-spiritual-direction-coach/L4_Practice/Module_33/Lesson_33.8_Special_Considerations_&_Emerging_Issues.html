Discovery Call Script: Focusing on Compliance

30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Introduction and Establishing Boundaries 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm a Certified Spiritual Direction Coach. Before we start, I want to clarify that while I offer guidance and support, I'm not a therapist or legal advisor. Is that clear?"
YOU:
"Great. Also, while our conversations are confidential, I am legally obligated to report any imminent threats of harm to yourself or others. Do you understand and agree to these terms?"

YOU:

YOU:
"I understand you mentioned some challenging experiences in your teaching career. While I'm here to support you, it's important to remember that I cannot provide legal advice. If you are considering legal action, I recommend consulting with an attorney."
YOU:
"Can you describe what a successful outcome of our coaching relationship would look like for you?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Approach (With Disclaimers) 10 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe my spiritual direction coaching can help you find clarity and purpose. My approach involves [briefly describe your methodology], always within ethical and legal boundaries."
YOU:
"It's important to note that coaching is not a substitute for therapy or legal counsel. If, during our sessions, I believe you would benefit from professional help in those areas, I will recommend appropriate resources."

YOU:

YOU:
"If you're comfortable moving forward, I can explain my coaching packages and payment options. I also have a client agreement that outlines our responsibilities and protects both of us. Would you like to proceed?"

    Setting the Stage: The Discovery Call

    The discovery call is your first opportunity to connect with a potential client. It's a crucial step in determining if your coaching services align with their needs. However, it's also a stage where ethical and legal considerations must be carefully managed.

    Establishing Boundaries: What You Are and Aren't

    One of the most important aspects of a discovery call is setting clear boundaries. Clients need to understand your role as a coach and what services you can and cannot provide.

    • Clarify Your Role: "As a coach, I help you identify goals, develop strategies, and achieve desired outcomes. I am not a therapist or counselor."
    • Define Scope: "My coaching focuses on forward-moving action. It is not designed to address deep-seated psychological issues."
    • No Legal Advice: "I am not a legal professional and cannot provide legal advice. If you have legal questions, please consult with an attorney."

    Case Study: Sarah's Misunderstanding

    Client: Sarah, 42, seeking career coaching.

    Presenting Symptoms: Sarah discussed feeling overwhelmed and anxious about her career transition, delving into past traumas and relationship issues.

    Intervention: The coach gently redirected Sarah, explaining the difference between coaching and therapy. The coach emphasized the forward-focused nature of coaching and recommended exploring therapy for past traumas.

    Outcomes: Sarah appreciated the clarity and decided to seek therapy first, with the intention of returning to coaching later. She felt validated and understood.

    Navigating Confidentiality: What Clients Need to Know

    Confidentiality is a cornerstone of trust. However, it's essential to clarify the limits of confidentiality in a coaching setting.

    • Explain Ethical Guidelines: "I adhere to ethical guidelines regarding confidentiality. What you share with me remains private, with some exceptions."
    • Mandatory Reporting: "Please be aware that if I believe you are at risk of harming yourself or others, or if I am legally required to disclose information, I must do so."
    • Document Everything: Keep detailed records of client agreements and disclosures to protect yourself and your clients.

    Handling Sensitive Disclosures: Staying Within Your Scope

    Clients may disclose sensitive information during discovery calls. It's your responsibility to handle these disclosures with care and professionalism.

    • Active Listening: Listen empathetically without judgment.
    • Assess Appropriateness: Determine if the disclosure falls within the scope of coaching.
    • Redirection: If the issue is beyond your scope, gently redirect the client.
    • Referrals: Provide referrals to qualified therapists or legal professionals.
