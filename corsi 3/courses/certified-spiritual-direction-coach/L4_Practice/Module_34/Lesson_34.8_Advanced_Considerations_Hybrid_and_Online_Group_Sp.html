Business Practice Lab

Let's practice enrolling clients into your group program. This lab provides a real-world scenario to hone your sales skills.

Your Prospect

Susan, 52

Interested in your "Finding Your Purpose After 50" group program. Saw your ad on Facebook.

Her Situation
Empty-nester, feeling lost, questioning her identity, wants more meaning in her life.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if I can justify spending the money on myself right now."
Decision Style
Needs to feel a connection with you. Values community and shared experiences. Wants practical steps.
Her Goal
"I want to wake up excited about my life again. I want to feel like I'm contributing something."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call! I'm Sarah. I'm so glad you're interested in the 'Finding Your Purpose After 50' program. To start, what resonated with you about the program description?"
YOU:
"I understand that feeling of being an empty nester. Many women in our community share that sentiment. What are some of the things you've been trying to fill that void?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about what 'purpose' means to you at this stage of your life. What does a purposeful day look like?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to find your purpose? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If you continue down this path, what do you imagine your life will be like in a year? And if you *did* discover your purpose, what would that change?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're longing for a sense of direction and contribution. You're ready to redefine yourself after this major life change. Am I understanding correctly?"
YOU:
"This group program is designed specifically for women like you. Over six weeks, we'll explore your values, passions, and skills. We'll create a practical plan to help you find meaningful activities and connections. It's not just about discovering your purpose, but about building a supportive community along the way."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe this program can help you rediscover your passion. Does this sound like the support you've been seeking? ... Wonderful! Let's talk about the program details and how to join."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if it's the right time."
"I understand. What would need to happen for it to feel like the *right* time? Sometimes, we have to make the time for ourselves to prioritize our well-being. Delaying can lead to further feelings of being lost. What feels most important to you right now?"
"I'm worried I won't have time with my schedule."
"That‚Äôs a valid concern. The sessions are [duration] and held on [day/time]. We also provide recordings. Many participants find that dedicating this time actually *frees* up time in other areas, because they're more energized and focused. How much time per week are you willing to invest in yourself and your future?"
"I'm not sure if I can justify spending the money on myself right now."
"I hear you, Susan. Many women feel that way, especially when they're used to putting others first. But investing in yourself is not selfish, it's necessary. Think of it as an investment in your future happiness and well-being. What's the cost of *not* investing in yourself? What is your well-being worth to you?"
"Can I get a discount?"
"The price reflects the value of the program, the expertise I bring, and the community support you'll receive. However, I do offer a payment plan that makes it more accessible. Would you like to hear more about that option?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key questions to ask during the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

Possible answers include: "Tell me more about what 'purpose' means to you," "What have you already tried," and "If you *did* discover your purpose, what would that change?"

2. How can you respond to the objection, "I'm not sure if I can justify spending the money on myself right now"?

Emphasize that investing in oneself is not selfish, but necessary for well-being. Ask about the cost of *not* investing in herself.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's problem deeply allows you to tailor your program to their needs.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and handling objections are essential for successful enrollment.
  • Group programs offer a scalable way to increase your income and impact.
  • Remember to emphasize the value and community aspects of your group program.
