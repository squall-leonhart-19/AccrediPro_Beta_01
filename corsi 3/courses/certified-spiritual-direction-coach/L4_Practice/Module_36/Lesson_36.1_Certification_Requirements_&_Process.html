Certification Requirements

To achieve your certification, you need to fulfill these key requirements:

  1. Coursework Completion: All modules and lessons within the Certified Spiritual Direction Coachâ„¢ program must be marked as complete. This ensures you have a solid theoretical foundation.
  2. Supervised Coaching Hours: A minimum of 20 hours of spiritual direction coaching with real clients, under the supervision of an approved mentor. This provides practical experience and guidance.
  3. Case Study Submissions: Submission of two detailed case studies, demonstrating your ability to apply The G.U.I.D.E. Frameworkâ„¢ effectively.
  4. Final Assessment: A comprehensive final assessment that tests your knowledge of spiritual direction principles, ethical considerations, and coaching techniques.

Remember, each component is designed to ensure you are competent and confident in your abilities as a Spiritual Direction Coachâ„¢.

Application Process

Once you've met all the requirements, you can begin the application process:

  1. Required Documentation: Gather all necessary documents, including proof of coursework completion, supervised coaching hour logs, and case study submissions.
  2. Fees: Submit the certification application fee of $297. This covers the administrative costs of reviewing your application.
  3. Submission Deadlines: Be mindful of the submission deadlines. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, with deadlines at the end of each quarter (March 31, June 30, September 30,December 31).
  4. Review Process: Your application will be reviewed by the certification board. They will assess your qualifications and may request additional information if needed.

Make sure to double-check all documents before submitting to avoid delays. A well-prepared application demonstrates your professionalism and commitment.

Ethical Guidelines

As a Certified Spiritual Direction Coachâ„¢, you are expected to adhere to the highest ethical standards. Key principles include:

  1. Confidentiality: Maintaining the confidentiality of client information is paramount. Ethical Principle: Non-Disclosure
  2. Boundaries: Establishing and maintaining clear boundaries with clients to avoid conflicts of interest. Ethical Principle: Professional Distance
  3. Competence: Providing services only within the scope of your competence and seeking supervision when necessary. Ethical Principle: Scope of Practice
  4. Respect: Treating all clients with respect and dignity, regardless of their beliefs or backgrounds. Ethical Principle: Respect for Autonomy

Upholding these ethical guidelines is essential for building trust and maintaining the integrity of the profession. Violation of these principles can result in revocation of certification.

Maintaining Certification

Certification is not a one-time achievement. To maintain your certification, you must:

  1. Continuing Education: Complete 20 hours of continuing education every two years. This ensures you stay current with the latest research and best practices in spiritual direction.
  2. Renewal Fees: Pay the certification renewal fee of $147 every two years.
  3. Adherence to Ethical Guidelines: Continue to adhere to the ethical guidelines outlined in the certification agreement.

Maintaining your certification demonstrates your commitment to ongoing professional development and ethical practice.

Case Study: Sarah M.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reported feeling lost and disconnected from her spiritual practices following a job loss and subsequent relocation. She expressed feelings of anxiety, uncertainty, and a lack of purpose. She rated her spiritual well-being a 3 out of 10.

Intervention: Using The G.U.I.D.E. Frameworkâ„¢, the coach guided Sarah through a process of:

  • Illumination: Identifying new opportunities for spiritual growth and connection.
  • Direction: Developing a plan for integrating spiritual practices into her daily life.
