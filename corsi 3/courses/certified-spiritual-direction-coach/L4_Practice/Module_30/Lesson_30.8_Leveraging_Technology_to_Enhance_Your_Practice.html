Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, it's Sarah! Thank you for scheduling this call with me. I appreciate you taking the time. To start, I'd love to hear a little more about what sparked your interest in spiritual direction coaching."
YOU:
"That's wonderful. And what are you hoping to achieve by working with a coach?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about this feeling of being lost. What does that look like in your day-to-day life?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to find more purpose? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If you continue on this path, what do you imagine your life will be like in a year? And if you could design your ideal life, what would it look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking a sense of direction and meaning after a major life transition. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My spiritual direction coaching program is designed to help you uncover your inner wisdom and connect with your values. Over 12 weeks, we'll work together to create a personalized plan for your next chapter. Many of my clients experience a renewed sense of purpose and excitement within the first few sessions."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you're looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me tell you more about the program and how you can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand, Susan. Investing in yourself is a big decision. I have a couple of payment options available, including a payment plan. Would you like to hear more about those?"
"I need to think about it and talk to my family."
"Of course. It's important to make sure this is the right fit for you. What specific questions do you have for your family? Perhaps I can provide some information that would be helpful for your conversation."
"I'm not sure if coaching is really for me."
"That's a fair concern. Many people are unsure about coaching at first. Based on what you've shared today, I truly believe that spiritual direction coaching could help you find the purpose and fulfillment you're seeking. Are you open to trying a sample session to experience the benefits firsthand?"
"I'm worried I won't have time."
"I understand that time is precious. Our sessions are designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find a time that works best for you. Remember, investing in your own well-being can actually *create* more time and energy in the long run by reducing stress and increasing focus."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call, and what is the purpose of each?

The four phases are: 1. Build Rapport (establish connection), 2. Understand the Problem (identify client's pain points), 3. Present Your Approach (explain how you can help), and 4. Close (invite the client to enroll).

2. What are some effective strategies for handling the objection "It's too expensive?"

Strategies include: Highlighting the value and ROI of the program, offering payment plans, and comparing the investment to the cost of *not* solving the problem.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process helps you effectively connect with potential clients and assess their needs.
  • Anticipating and preparing for common objections builds confidence and increases your chances of closing the sale.
  • Clearly articulating the value of your program and presenting your pricing with confidence is essential for successful client acquisition.
  • Consistent practice and refinement of your sales skills will lead to increased client enrollment and a thriving practice.
