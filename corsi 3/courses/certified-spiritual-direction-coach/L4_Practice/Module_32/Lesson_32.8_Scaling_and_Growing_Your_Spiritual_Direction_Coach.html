Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'm ready to commit to 12 weeks."
"I understand. Committing to a coaching program is a big decision. Let's break it down. What part of the 12-week commitment feels most challenging? Knowing that will help me tailor the program to your needs."
"I need to talk to my family about it."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Family support is important. What information do you think they'll need to feel comfortable with your investment in coaching? I'm happy to provide resources or even answer their questions directly."
"I'm worried I won't have enough time."
"Time is a precious resource, Susan. Let's explore that. Where do you see your time currently going? Could we identify areas where you could reclaim even an hour a week? Remember, this is an investment in YOU and your future. What if, by investing this time, you actually GAINED more energy and focus?"
"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that investing in yourself requires careful consideration. Many of my clients have found that the clarity and direction they gain from coaching actually saves them money in the long run by preventing them from pursuing dead-end paths or making impulsive decisions. Would you like me to share some financing options or payment plans that could make the program more accessible?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is building rapport essential in the initial phase of a discovery call?

Building rapport helps establish trust and connection with the prospect, making them more receptive to your coaching services. It creates a safe space for open communication.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your coaching program?

You can reframe the cost as an investment in their future well-being and potential for growth, highlighting the long-term benefits and value they will receive. Offer payment plans or financing options to make it more accessible.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Effective objection handling demonstrates empathy and provides solutions to address concerns.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence and clarity reinforces the value of your services.
  • Consistent practice of your sales script will build confidence and improve your conversion rates.
  • Remember your unique background and how it adds value to your coaching practice.
