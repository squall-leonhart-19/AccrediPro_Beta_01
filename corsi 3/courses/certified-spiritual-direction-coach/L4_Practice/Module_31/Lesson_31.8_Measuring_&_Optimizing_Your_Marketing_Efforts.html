Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Investing in yourself can feel daunting, especially during a transition. Let's look at the potential ROI. What would it be worth to you to find a fulfilling career that provides financial stability? Many of my clients see a 20-30% increase in income within the first year after completing the program. We also have flexible payment plans available."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. I am confident that you can turn this around and find the next opportunity. I will follow up next week to ensure you are on the right path."
"I'm not sure if this is the right time for me."
"I hear you. Sometimes the timing never feels "perfect." But consider this: the longer you wait, the more time you spend feeling lost and unfulfilled. This program is designed to provide clarity and direction quickly. What if, in just 12 weeks, you could be on a completely different path?"
"I've tried other programs before, and they didn't work."
"I understand your skepticism. What was missing from those previous experiences? My program is different because it focuses on [unique aspects of your program]. We also provide personalized support and guidance every step of the way. Let me tell you how we are different from other programs, and how we can help you succeed."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of an effective discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your solution, and confidently closing.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, ask what specifically they need to think about, and offer to provide additional information or answer any questions.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script regularly to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and highlight the value of your program.
  • Focus on the transformation you provide and the ROI for your clients.
