Your Coaching Script

Use this script to reinforce Janice's progress and solidify her new habits:

YOU:
"Janice, it's fantastic to see the progress you've made! Implementing time-blocking, delegating tasks, and starting meditation are significant achievements. How do you feel about these changes?"

"I feel much better, but I'm worried about slipping back into my old habits now that things are getting busier."
YOU:
"That's a common concern. Let's acknowledge the strength and discipline you've shown in establishing these new routines. What specific benefits have you noticed from each of these changes?"
JANICE:
"Well, with time-blocking, I'm more focused and less overwhelmed. Delegating has freed up my time, and meditation helps me stay calm even when things get hectic."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to acknowledge progress before addressing challenges in a reinforcement session?

Acknowledging progress builds rapport, boosts confidence, and sets a positive tone for the session. It reminds the client of their capabilities and the value of their efforts.

2. What are some effective ways to help a client reinforce new habits during a busy season?

Effective strategies include identifying small, manageable tweaks to existing habits, creating a system of rewards for sticking to routines, and practicing self-compassion during challenging times.
