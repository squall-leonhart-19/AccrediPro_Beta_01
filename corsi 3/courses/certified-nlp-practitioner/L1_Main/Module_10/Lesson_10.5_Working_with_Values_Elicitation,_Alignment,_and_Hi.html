Deep Dive into Values

Welcome back! In this lesson, we're taking a deep dive into the world of values. Values are the deeply held beliefs that guide our actions and decisions. They represent what is most important to us and serve as the foundation for our motivation and sense of purpose. Understanding and working with values is a powerful tool in NLP, enabling you to help clients achieve lasting change and fulfillment. As a certified NLP practitioner, you'll be equipped to guide your clients in uncovering, understanding, and aligning their values for profound personal and professional growth. This is a skill that can lead to significant income opportunities; many practitioners like yourself charge $150-$300 per hour for values-based coaching, with some specializing in career or executive coaching and earning upwards of $100,000 annually.

Eliciting and Identifying Core Values

Eliciting values is the process of uncovering a client's most important values. There are several techniques you can use:

  • Values Questionnaire: Use a structured questionnaire to prompt reflection on important qualities and principles.
  • Guided Visualization: Lead the client through a visualization exercise to identify what truly matters to them.
  • Values Interview: Ask open-ended questions to explore the client's beliefs and priorities.
  • "Ideal Day" Exercise: Have the client describe their ideal day, then identify the underlying values being expressed.

Remember the importance of sensory acuity and calibration from Module 2? These skills are crucial for observing non-verbal cues and subtle shifts in the client's state as they discuss their values. A genuine, heartfelt connection to a value will often be accompanied by specific physiological responses.

Aligning Values with Goals and Behaviors

Once you've identified a client's core values, the next step is to ensure that their goals and behaviors are congruent with those values. When there is alignment, motivation and satisfaction increase. When there is misalignment, conflict and dissatisfaction arise.

Here's a structured approach to aligning values:

  1. Identify the Goal: Clearly define the client's goal.
  2. List Core Values: Review the client's identified core values.
  3. Assess Alignment: Ask, "How does achieving this goal support your core values?"
  4. Address Misalignment: If there is misalignment, explore alternative goals or behaviors that are more congruent with the client's values.

Understanding Value Hierarchies

Values don't exist in isolation; they exist in a hierarchy. Some values are more important than others, and this hierarchy influences decision-making. Identifying the relative importance of different values can provide valuable insights into a client's choices and behaviors.

Consider this example:

Value Description Example
Family The importance of relationships and connection with family members. Prioritizing family time over work commitments.
Career The desire for professional success and achievement. Working long hours to advance in a company.
Personal Growth The commitment to continuous learning and self-improvement. Attending workshops or taking courses to develop new skills.

If a client consistently chooses career advancement over family time, it suggests that "Career" may be a higher value than "Family" in their personal hierarchy. Understanding this hierarchy helps in tailoring interventions that address potential conflicts and promote greater fulfillment.

Case Study: Aligning Values to Overcome Career Dissatisfaction

Case Study: Sarah, a 35-Year-Old Marketing Manager

Client: Sarah, 35, Marketing Manager

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reported feeling increasingly dissatisfied with her career despite achieving professional success. She experienced a lack of motivation, frequent stress, and a sense of disconnect from her work.

Intervention: Using values elicitation techniques, Sarah identified her core values as Creativity, Authenticity, and Connection. It became clear that her current role, while financially rewarding, did not allow her to express these values. She felt constrained by corporate bureaucracy and lacked genuine connection with her colleagues.

Outcomes: Sarah made a strategic career shift to a smaller, more creative agency where she could express her creativity and build authentic relationships. Within six months, her job satisfaction significantly increased, her stress levels decreased, and she reported feeling more aligned with her work.
