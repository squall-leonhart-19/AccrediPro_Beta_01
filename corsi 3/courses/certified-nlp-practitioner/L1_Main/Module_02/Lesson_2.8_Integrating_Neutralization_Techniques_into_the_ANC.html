Your Coaching Script: Neutralizing the Belief

Use these questions to challenge and reframe Diane's limiting belief. Practice saying them out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing that. Let's explore this belief: 'I'm too old to start a new career.' On a scale of 1 to 10, how strongly do you believe this right now?"
DIANE:
"Probably an 8 or 9. It's something I've told myself for years."
YOU:
"Okay. Now, can you think of anyone, even someone you don't know personally, who started a new career later in life and was successful?"
DIANE:
"Well, I guess Colonel Sanders started Kentucky Fried Chicken in his 60s. But that's an exception, right?"
YOU:
"Perhaps. But what does that example tell you about the absolute truth of your belief? Does Colonel Sanders' story create even a tiny crack in that belief?"
DIANE:
"I suppose it shows it's *possible*. Just not probable for me."
YOU:
"Alright. Let's look at the word 'too'. What exactly does 'too old' mean? Is there a specific age cutoff that makes someone incapable of learning and growing?"
DIANE:
"No, I guess not. People learn new things at all ages. I just worry about competing with younger people."
YOU:
"And what advantages might you have, Diane, because of your age and experience? What can you offer that a younger person might not?"

  • Diane starts to question her belief ("Maybe you're right...")

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to acknowledge a client's limiting belief before trying to neutralize it?

Acknowledging the belief builds trust and rapport. It shows the client you understand their perspective before challenging it.

2. What's the purpose of asking, "On a scale of 1 to 10, how strongly do you believe this?"

It establishes a baseline. As you challenge the belief, you can revisit the scale and track any shifts in her conviction.

Key Takeaways

  • Neutralizing beliefs is about gently challenging, not forceful arguing.
  • Use questions that guide the client to discover limitations in their own thinking.
  • Focus on finding exceptions and reframing negative statements.
  • Patience is key. Deep-seated beliefs take time to shift.
  • Remember that even small cracks in a belief can create significant change over time.
