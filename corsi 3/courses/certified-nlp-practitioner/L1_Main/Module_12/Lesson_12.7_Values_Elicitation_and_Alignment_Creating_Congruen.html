Practical Exercise: Values Elicitation

Time to put theory into practice. This exercise is designed to help you refine your values elicitation skills. Remember, the goal is to uncover the hierarchy and intensity of values, not just list them.

  1. Prepare: Choose a partner or client (with their consent, of course!). Explain the purpose of the exercise and ensure they feel comfortable sharing.
  2. Elicitation Questions: Ask open-ended questions like:
    • "What's most important to you in your life right now?"
    • "What do you value most in your relationships?"
    • "What makes you feel truly fulfilled?"
  3. Clarification: For each value identified, ask clarifying questions:
    • "What does [value] mean to you specifically?"
    • "How do you know when you're experiencing [value]?"
    • "What's the opposite of [value]?"
  4. Hierarchy: Use comparison questions to determine the hierarchy:
    • "Is [value A] more important than [value B], or vice versa?"
    • "If you had to choose between [value A] and [value B], which would you prioritize?"
  5. Debrief: Discuss the experience with your partner/client. What did you learn? What was surprising? What challenges did you encounter?
