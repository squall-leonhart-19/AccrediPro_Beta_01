Scenario: Expanding Your Practice Online

You're ready to take your NLP practice online, offering coaching sessions via video conferencing and selling digital products. This opens up exciting possibilities, but also introduces new legal and compliance considerations.

Prospect Profile

Imagine you're approached by Sarah, a 45-year-old marketing consultant who is interested in your online NLP coaching program. She found you through a Facebook ad and is intrigued by the promise of overcoming limiting beliefs and enhancing her career. Sarah lives in California, a state with strict consumer protection laws.

Discovery Call Script

Here's a structured 30-minute discovery call script to guide your conversation with Sarah, focusing on legal and ethical considerations:

Phase Time Your Dialogue Notes
Introduction & Consent 5 min "Hi Sarah, thanks for scheduling this call. Before we begin, I want to ensure you understand this is a discovery call to see if we're a good fit. I'm not providing therapy or medical advice. Do you consent to proceed with that understanding?" Emphasize the nature of the call. Document consent.
Understanding Sarah's Needs 10 min "Can you tell me more about what you're hoping to achieve with NLP coaching? What challenges are you facing in your career?" Listen actively and take notes. Identify potential red flags (e.g., mental health issues requiring a licensed professional).
Presenting Your Program (Disclaimer) 10 min "Based on what you've shared, my NLP coaching program focuses on [describe program benefits]. It's important to note that NLP is a complementary approach and not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment. Results vary, and I can't guarantee specific outcomes." Clearly state the scope and limitations of your services. Avoid making unsubstantiated claims.
Discussing Payment & Refund Policy 3 min "The program fee is [state price], and I have a clear refund policy outlined in my client agreement. Would you like me to send you a copy to review?" Be transparent about pricing and payment terms. Ensure your refund policy complies with local laws.
Closing & Next Steps 2 min "Sarah, do you have any questions about the program, my qualifications, or the legal aspects we've discussed? If you're interested in moving forward, I'll send you the client agreement and payment information." Offer opportunity for questions. Reiterate your commitment to ethical practices.

Handling Objections

Here are some common objections and how to address them while staying compliant:

  • Objection: "I'm not sure if NLP is legitimate."
    Your Response: "I understand your skepticism. While NLP is not universally recognized as a science, it draws from established psychological principles. I'm happy to provide you with resources and testimonials to help you make an informed decision. I also encourage you to consult with other professionals if you have concerns."
  • Objection: "Your prices seem high."
    Your Response: "I appreciate your feedback. My pricing reflects the personalized attention, expertise, and time I dedicate to each client. I also offer payment plans to make the program more accessible. Remember, investing in yourself is an investment in your future."
  • Objection: "What if I don't see results?"
    Your Response: "While I strive to help all my clients achieve their goals, I can't guarantee specific outcomes. Results vary depending on individual effort and circumstances. However, I am committed to providing you with the tools, support, and guidance you need to succeed. I also have a client agreement that outlines the terms of service and refund policy."
  • Objection: "I need to think about it."
    Your Response: "I completely understand. Take your time to review the information I've provided. I'm available to answer any further questions you may have. When you're ready, let me know, and we can schedule a follow-up call."

Presenting Your Pricing

Confidence is key when presenting your pricing. Here's how to do it ethically and effectively:

  1. State your price clearly and confidently. Avoid apologizing or hesitating.
  2. Highlight the value you provide. Focus on the benefits clients will receive, not just the features of your program.
  3. Offer payment options. Consider offering payment plans or discounts for upfront payment to make your services more accessible.
  4. Be transparent about your refund policy. This builds trust and demonstrates your commitment to client satisfaction.

Example Script:

"My comprehensive 12-week NLP coaching program is priced at $2,497. This includes [list specific deliverables, e.g., weekly coaching sessions, personalized exercises, email support]. I also offer a payment plan of three installments of $850. I'm confident that this program will provide you with the tools and support you need to achieve your career goals. Do you have any questions about the pricing or payment options?"

Practice these closing lines out loud to build confidence:

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of obtaining consent during a discovery call?

Obtaining consent ensures the client understands the nature of the call and agrees to proceed with that understanding. It helps protect you from potential legal issues.

2. How can you ethically present your pricing and payment options?

Present your pricing clearly and confidently, highlight the value you provide, offer payment options, and be transparent about your refund policy.

3. Why is it important to avoid making unsubstantiated claims about the results of NLP coaching?

Making unsubstantiated claims can be misleading and unethical. It can also expose you to legal liability. It's important to be honest and realistic about the potential benefits of NLP coaching.

Key Takeaways

  • Always obtain informed consent before starting a discovery call.
  • Be transparent about your qualifications, services, and pricing.
  • Avoid making unsubstantiated claims or guarantees.
  • Familiarize yourself with relevant laws and regulations in your area.
  • Prioritize ethical practices and client well-being.
