The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 48, who wants to overcome procrastination. During our session, she revealed a history of childhood emotional neglect. I feel out of my depth. Should I refer her out?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Aisha explored Sarah's resources and coping mechanisms?
  • Is Aisha equipped to handle potential abreactions or emotional flooding?
  • Does Aisha understand the importance of pacing and titration in trauma work?
  • Is Sarah's procrastination directly linked to the trauma, or is it a separate issue?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Validating Her Concerns

"It's wise to recognize when a case is beyond your current skillset. That awareness is crucial for ethical practice."

2

Assessing the Trauma Link

Help Aisha explore whether Sarah's procrastination is a direct result of the trauma or a separate learned behavior. Use timeline techniques to identify key events and patterns.

3

Resource Installation

Guide Aisha on installing resourceful states (e.g., confidence, self-compassion) in Sarah before addressing the trauma directly. This builds resilience.

4

Scope of Practice

Reinforce the importance of staying within her scope. If Sarah needs intensive trauma therapy, referral is necessary. NLP can still be a valuable adjunct.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Initiative
"Aisha, I commend you for seeking supervision on this case. It shows your commitment to client well-being."
Explore Her Assessment
"Before we discuss options, tell me more about how you assessed Sarah's resources and coping mechanisms. What did you notice?"
Teach Pacing and Titration
"With trauma, we always pace the work and titrate the intensity. Small, manageable steps are key. Overwhelming the client can be re-traumatizing."
Empower Her Decision
"Based on what we've discussed, what feels like the most ethical and effective path forward for Sarah? Do you feel equipped to continue, or is referral the best option?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of supervision in this scenario?

To guide the new practitioner in making ethical decisions and developing their skills, not to solve the client's problem directly.

2. What key principle should guide your feedback on Aisha's handling of the case?

Empowerment. Help Aisha develop her own clinical reasoning and decision-making skills, rather than creating dependency on you.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about training, guidance, and ethical practice.
  • New practitioners need validation and support, not just answers.
  • Always emphasize scope of practice and client safety.
  • Help your mentees develop their own clinical reasoning.
  • Your experience is invaluable - share it wisely!
