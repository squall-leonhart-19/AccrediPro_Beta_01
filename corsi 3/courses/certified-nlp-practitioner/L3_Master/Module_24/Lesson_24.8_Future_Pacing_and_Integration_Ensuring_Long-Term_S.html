The Boundary Case

Olivia's Client Boundary Issue

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, who is working through anxiety. Our sessions are great, but she texts me almost every night with panic attacks. I feel bad ignoring her, but it's exhausting. What should I do?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Olivia set clear boundaries during the initial consultation?
  • Does Olivia understand the importance of self-care and preventing burnout?
  • How can Olivia communicate boundaries without harming the client relationship?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Emphasize the Importance of Boundaries

Explain that boundaries protect both the practitioner and the client, ensuring sustainable and ethical practice.

2

Review Initial Consultation Procedures

Discuss how to clearly communicate boundaries regarding communication, session times, and emergency support during the first meeting.

3

Provide Scripting Examples

Offer specific phrases Olivia can use to address the boundary issue with her client in a compassionate yet firm manner.

4

Discuss Self-Care Strategies

Encourage Olivia to prioritize her well-being to avoid burnout and maintain her effectiveness as a practitioner.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why are boundaries so important for NLP practitioners?

Boundaries protect the practitioner from burnout and ensure the client receives appropriate, ethical support. They also help maintain a professional relationship.

2. What's a good way to start a conversation about boundaries with a new practitioner?

Begin by validating their dedication and acknowledging that boundary-setting is a common challenge for new practitioners.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating. Ask questions first.
  • Share your own experiences to normalize the learning process.
  • Emphasize sustainable practices and self-care.
  • Empower new practitioners to develop their own solutions.
