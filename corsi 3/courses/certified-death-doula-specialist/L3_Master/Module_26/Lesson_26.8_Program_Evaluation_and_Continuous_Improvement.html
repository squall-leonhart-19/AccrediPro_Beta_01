Sarah, Recent L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic about doula work but lacks practical experience and confidence.

Background
Former social worker, excellent interpersonal skills.
Strengths
Strong empathy, active listening, and a genuine desire to help.
Growth Areas
Needs guidance on setting boundaries, managing client expectations, and handling difficult conversations.
Her Question
"I had a client whose family is very resistant to her end-of-life wishes. How do I navigate this delicate situation?"

The Challenging Case

Sarah's Client: Elizabeth's Story

Sarah's Account:

"Elizabeth, 78, has terminal cancer and wants a peaceful, natural death at home. Her adult children, however, are pushing for aggressive treatments and are dismissive of Elizabeth's wishes. They see me as enabling her 'giving up.' I'm feeling stuck and unsure how to proceed."

Key Exploration Points:

  • What specific end-of-life wishes has Elizabeth expressed?
  • Have Elizabeth's wishes been formally documented (e.g., advance directive)?
  • What is the family's understanding of Elizabeth's prognosis?
  • What strategies has Sarah already tried to facilitate communication?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Essential Points to Address with Sarah

1

Emphasize Client Autonomy

Remind Sarah that the client's wishes are paramount. Her role is to support Elizabeth in achieving her desired end-of-life experience, within legal and ethical boundaries.

2

Explore Communication Techniques

Discuss various methods for facilitating difficult conversations, such as active listening, empathetic communication, and reframing perspectives.

3

Review Ethical Guidelines

Reinforce the importance of adhering to ethical guidelines, including respecting client autonomy, maintaining confidentiality, and avoiding conflicts of interest.

4

Develop Boundary-Setting Skills

Help Sarah establish clear boundaries with the family, ensuring she doesn't get drawn into conflicts or compromise her role as Elizabeth's advocate.

Crafting Your Feedback

Delivering Constructive and Supportive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concern
"Sarah, it's completely understandable that you're feeling challenged by this situation. It's a common scenario, and your concern for Elizabeth is commendable."
Inquire About Her Approach
"Before I offer suggestions, can you share what strategies you've already tried to communicate with the family?"
Offer Guiding Principles
"Remember, our primary responsibility is to honor Elizabeth's wishes. While it's important to acknowledge the family's concerns, we must gently guide them toward respecting her autonomy."
Suggest Practical Steps
"Perhaps you could facilitate a family meeting with a neutral mediator to discuss Elizabeth's wishes and address the family's fears and concerns. Or, refer them to resources on grief and acceptance."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Ultimately, the decision on how to proceed rests with you and Elizabeth. I'm here to support you in any way I can. What feels like the most aligned next step?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important principle to emphasize when mentoring a new death doula?

Client autonomy and honoring their end-of-life wishes.

2. What is a helpful way to approach a mentee who is struggling with a difficult client case?

Ask open-ended questions to encourage reflection and critical thinking, rather than immediately providing solutions.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not directing.
  • Focus on empowering the mentee to develop their own skills and confidence.
  • Ethical considerations and client autonomy should always be at the forefront.
  • Constructive feedback and a supportive environment are essential for growth.
