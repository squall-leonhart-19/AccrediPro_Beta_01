Sarah, Recent Graduate (L1)

Enthusiastic, but feeling overwhelmed by her first complex case.

Background
Former social worker, strong interpersonal skills, new to end-of-life care specifically.
Strengths
Excellent at active listening, building trust quickly, and advocating for clients.
Growth Areas
Struggles with setting boundaries, feels pressure to "fix" everything, needs help with self-care.
Her Dilemma
"I'm so drained after each visit with my client. I'm not sure I'm helping, and I'm starting to dread going."

The Case Sarah Presents

Complex Client Case: Eleanor, 78

What Sarah Tells You:

"Eleanor is 78 and has advanced COPD. She's very anxious and angry about her diagnosis. Her family is distant and unsupportive. Eleanor constantly calls me with complaints and asks me to do things outside my scope, like contact her doctors or manage her finances. I want to help, but I'm burning out."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What boundaries has Sarah set with Eleanor?
  • Is Sarah confusing "support" with "taking over"?
  • How is Sarah managing her own emotional responses to Eleanor's distress?
  • Does Sarah understand the resources available to Eleanor beyond her doula services?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Her Feelings

"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed in cases like this. Eleanor is carrying a lot of pain, and it's easy to get caught up in it."

2

Teach the Importance of Boundaries

Explain that clear boundaries are essential for both Sarah's well-being and Eleanor's empowerment. Review scope of practice.

3

Introduce Self-Care Strategies

Help Sarah identify practical self-care activities she can implement immediately, such as mindfulness, setting time limits for client calls, and debriefing with a colleague.

4

Explore Resource Navigation

Guide Sarah to identify resources beyond her doula services that can support Eleanor, such as social workers, elder care services, and support groups.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Empathy
"Sarah, thank you for being so open about this. It takes courage to admit when you're struggling. I can hear how much you care about Eleanor."
Ask About Her Perspective
"Before I offer suggestions, what have you already tried in terms of setting boundaries with Eleanor? What felt effective, and what didn't?"
Provide Gentle Guidance
"It sounds like Eleanor's anxiety is leading her to lean heavily on you. While empathy is crucial, it's also important to remember that you're not her only support system. Let's brainstorm ways to connect her with other resources."
Reinforce Self-Care
"Remember, you can't pour from an empty cup. What's one small thing you can do TODAY to replenish your energy after seeing Eleanor?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary reason for establishing clear boundaries with clients?

To protect the doula's well-being and ensure the client receives appropriate support within the doula's scope of practice.

2. Name three self-care strategies a death doula can use to prevent burnout when working with complex cases.

Mindfulness practices, setting time limits for client calls, debriefing with a colleague or supervisor, engaging in hobbies, spending time in nature.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is vital for new death doulas navigating complex cases.
  • Effective mentoring involves empathy, guidance, and resource support.
  • Boundaries are essential for both the doula's well-being and the client's empowerment.
  • Self-care is a non-negotiable aspect of sustainable doula practice.
