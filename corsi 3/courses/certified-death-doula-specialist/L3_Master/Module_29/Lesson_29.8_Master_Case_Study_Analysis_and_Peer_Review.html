Sarah, L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic new doula seeking guidance on a challenging client interaction.

Background
Former social worker, strong advocacy skills, limited experience with grief.
Strengths
Empathetic listener, passionate about client autonomy, excellent organizational skills.
Growth Areas
Struggles with setting boundaries, can become emotionally overwhelmed, needs help with self-care.
Her Question
"I'm feeling drained after a visit with a client's grieving family. How can I better support them without taking on their pain?"

The Challenging Case

Sarah's Concern: Vicarious Trauma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I visited the family of Mr. Johnson, a terminally ill client. They were all so distraught and kept sharing their fears and regrets. I listened and offered comfort, but I left feeling completely depleted. I'm worried I'm not cut out for this if I can't handle their grief."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Did Sarah establish clear boundaries for the visit?
  • What self-care practices does Sarah have in place?
  • Did Sarah validate her own feelings after the visit?
  • Is she confusing empathy with taking on others' emotions?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Essential Points to Discuss with Sarah

1

Validate Her Experience

"It's completely normal to feel emotionally impacted by witnessing intense grief. It shows you care."

2

Teach Boundary Setting

Explain the importance of setting intentions before each visit: "I am here to support, not absorb."

3

Introduce Grounding Techniques

Share simple exercises like deep breathing or visualization to help her detach and center herself after difficult interactions.

4

Review Self-Care Strategies

Help her identify activities that replenish her energy and make them non-negotiable in her schedule.

Crafting Your Feedback

Delivering Encouraging and Actionable Feedback

Acknowledge Her Strengths
"Sarah, your empathy is a real gift. It's clear you deeply care about your clients and their families."
Introduce a New Perspective
"Think of yourself as a conductor of an orchestra. You're there to guide the music, not play every instrument yourself."
Offer Concrete Tools
"Before your next visit, try this: visualize a shield of light surrounding you, deflecting negativity while allowing you to offer support."
Reinforce Self-Care
"What's one thing you can do *today* to nurture yourself? Make it a priority. You can't pour from an empty cup."
