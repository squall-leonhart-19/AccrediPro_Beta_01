Handling Common Objections

Overcoming Objections with Confidence

"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand, Eleanor. Let's explore some options. I have different packages to fit various budgets. We can also prioritize the most critical aspects of your plan and phase in other services later. What's most important is that you get the support you need."
"I need to talk to my children first."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Would you like me to provide you with some information you can share with them? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call together so they can ask any questions they may have? I want to ensure everyone feels comfortable and informed."
"I'm worried it's too morbid to think about death."
"I hear you, Eleanor. It's natural to feel some resistance. However, many of my clients find that facing their mortality actually brings them a greater appreciation for life. It's not about dwelling on death, but about living more fully and intentionally. It's about ensuring your values and wishes are honored."
"I don't have time for this right now."
"I understand you're busy, Eleanor. But think of this as an investment in your future peace of mind. We can work at your pace and schedule sessions that fit your lifestyle. Even a few hours of planning can make a huge difference in reducing stress and ensuring your wishes are respected."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in a death doula discovery call?

Building rapport, exploring client needs, and presenting your services and value.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your services?

By exploring different package options, prioritizing essential services, and emphasizing the long-term value and peace of mind your services provide.

3. What is an effective way to respond when a potential client says they need to talk to their family before making a decision?

Offer to provide information they can share with their family or suggest scheduling a call together to answer any questions.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is crucial for building a sustainable death doula practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you understand client needs and present your services effectively.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence can turn potential clients into paying clients.
  • Clearly communicating your pricing and value is essential for attracting clients who appreciate your expertise.
  • Realistic income goals and consistent effort can lead to a thriving and fulfilling career as a death doula.
