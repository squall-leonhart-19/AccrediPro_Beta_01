Susan, 52

Expresses interest in your "Navigating Grief" workshop after seeing your social media post.

Her Situation
Recently lost her mother after a long illness. Feeling overwhelmed and isolated.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now, but I desperately need support."
Decision Style
Values connection and a safe space to share. Needs to feel understood and supported.
Her Goal
"I want to find healthy ways to cope with my grief and connect with others who understand."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you so much for reaching out and expressing interest in the 'Navigating Grief' workshop. I truly appreciate you taking the time to connect with me. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"I saw that you mentioned the loss of your mother. I'm so sorry for your loss. Would you be open to sharing a bit about what you're experiencing right now?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing as you navigate this grief?"
YOU:
"What kind of support have you been seeking, and what has been helpful or unhelpful so far?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and have the perfect support system, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her pain points]. Is that a fair assessment?"
YOU:
"The 'Navigating Grief' workshop is designed to provide a safe and supportive space for individuals like you to explore their grief, connect with others who understand, and learn practical coping strategies. It's a 4-week program, with weekly 2-hour sessions. We'll cover topics like understanding the grief process, managing difficult emotions, self-care practices, and creating meaningful rituals."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this workshop sound like it could be a good fit for what you're looking for? ... Great! Let's talk about the investment and how you can register."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time commitment right now."
"I understand. Grief can be exhausting. This workshop is designed to be a supportive and rejuvenating experience, not another obligation. The 2-hour sessions are a dedicated time for you to focus on your well-being and connect with others. Plus, the skills and tools you learn can help you manage your grief more effectively in your daily life. How does that sound?"
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Investing in your emotional well-being is an important decision. The workshop is $297 for the entire 4-week program, which includes all materials and resources. Many participants find that the support and tools they gain are invaluable in their healing journey. We also have a payment plan available. Would you like to hear about it?"
"I'm not sure if I'm ready to share my feelings with a group."
"That's completely understandable. Sharing in a group can feel intimidating. The workshop is designed to be a safe and confidential space where you can share as much or as little as you feel comfortable. There's no pressure to speak if you don't want to. Simply being present and listening to others can be incredibly healing. Would you like to hear what some past participants have shared about their experience?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. It's a big decision. What specifically are you needing to think about? Maybe I can provide some more clarity now."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call for a group workshop?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's needs, presenting your workshop as the solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you effectively address the "It's too expensive" objection when selling a workshop?

Highlight the value of the workshop, offer payment plans, and emphasize the long-term benefits of investing in their well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script out loud to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value of your workshop.
  • Remember that you are offering a valuable service that can transform lives.
