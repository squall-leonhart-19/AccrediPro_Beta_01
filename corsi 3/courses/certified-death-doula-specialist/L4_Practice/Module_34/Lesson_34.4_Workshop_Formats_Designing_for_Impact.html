Half-Day Workshops

Half-day workshops are perfect for delivering concise, focused information on a specific topic. These workshops typically run for 3-4 hours, making them accessible for individuals with busy schedules. Common topics include advance care planning, grief support, and understanding the stages of dying.

Key Elements:

  • Focused Content: Choose a single, well-defined topic.
  • Interactive Exercises: Incorporate activities like guided meditations, journaling prompts, or small group discussions.
  • Practical Takeaways: Provide participants with actionable steps and resources they can use immediately.

Case Study: "Planning Ahead" Workshop

Client: Maria Rodriguez, 52, recently launched her death doula practice after a career in education. She felt confident in one-on-one client support but wanted to expand her reach through workshops.

Presenting Need: Maria needed a structured format for a half-day advance care planning workshop.

Intervention: Maria designed a 3-hour workshop covering living wills, durable power of attorney for healthcare, and POLST forms. She included interactive exercises where participants drafted their own values statements and discussed their wishes with a partner.

Outcomes: The workshop was well-received, with 80% of participants reporting increased confidence in completing their advance directives. Maria generated $600 from the workshop and gained three new individual clients. She now earns approximately $2,000/month from these workshops.

Full-Day Workshops

Full-day workshops offer an immersive experience, allowing for a deeper exploration of end-of-life issues. These workshops typically run for 6-8 hours and can incorporate a variety of learning modalities, including lectures, small group discussions, experiential activities, and even creative expression.

Key Elements:

  • Varied Activities: Balance lectures with interactive exercises, discussions, and creative activities.
  • Experiential Learning: Incorporate activities like role-playing, guided visualizations, or art therapy.
  • Community Building: Create opportunities for participants to connect with one another and share their experiences.

Consider integrating elements of the S.O.U.L.S. Framework‚Ñ¢. For example, a full-day workshop could dedicate a segment to the "Opening to Emotions" aspect, providing tools and techniques for emotional exploration.

Multi-Session Workshops

Multi-session workshops provide ongoing support and education over several weeks or months. This format fosters a sense of community and allows participants to delve deeper into complex topics, building on their knowledge and skills over time. They are ideal for grief support groups, caregiver training programs, and exploring the S.O.U.L.S. Framework‚Ñ¢ in depth.

Key Elements:

  • Structured Curriculum: Develop a comprehensive curriculum that builds upon previous sessions.
  • Community Building: Create opportunities for participants to connect with one another and build lasting relationships.
  • Ongoing Support: Provide resources and support between sessions to help participants integrate what they've learned.

Case Study: "Navigating Grief" Series

Client: Sarah Chen, 48, a former social worker, transitioned to death doula work after experiencing the loss of her mother. She wanted to create a supportive space for others navigating grief.

Presenting Need: Sarah needed a structured program to provide ongoing grief support.

Intervention: Sarah developed a 6-week "Navigating Grief" workshop series. Each 2-hour session covered a different aspect of grief, including understanding grief reactions, coping strategies, self-care, and finding meaning after loss. She incorporated group discussions, journaling prompts, and creative expression activities.

Outcomes: The workshop series was highly successful, with participants reporting significant improvements in their emotional well-being and coping skills. Sarah charged $250 per participant and generated $2,000 per series. She now runs multiple series per year and has built a thriving grief support community.

Retreats

Retreats offer an intensive, immersive experience that provides a break from daily routines. They are ideal for self-reflection, healing, and connection with nature or spirituality. Retreats can range from a weekend to a week or longer and often incorporate elements like meditation, yoga, nature walks, and group sharing. Consider how the "Sacred Space & Closure" aspect of the S.O.U.L.S. Framework‚Ñ¢ can be deeply integrated into a retreat setting.

Key Elements:

  • Immersive Environment: Choose a location that is conducive to relaxation, reflection, and connection with nature.
  • Balanced Schedule: Create a schedule that balances structured activities with free time for personal reflection.
  • Accessibility and Inclusivity: Ensure the retreat is accessible to individuals with diverse needs and backgrounds. Consider factors like physical accessibility, dietary restrictions, and cultural sensitivity.

Accessibility and Inclusivity:

When designing any workshop format, it‚Äôs crucial to consider accessibility and inclusivity. This means ensuring that your programs are welcoming and accessible to individuals with diverse backgrounds, abilities, and needs. Consider factors like:

  • Physical Accessibility: Is the venue accessible to individuals with mobility limitations?
  • Sensory Considerations: Can you provide accommodations for individuals with sensory sensitivities?
  • Dietary Restrictions: Can you accommodate dietary restrictions and preferences?
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Are your materials and activities culturally sensitive and inclusive?
  • Financial Accessibility: Can you offer scholarships or sliding scale fees to make your programs more accessible?
