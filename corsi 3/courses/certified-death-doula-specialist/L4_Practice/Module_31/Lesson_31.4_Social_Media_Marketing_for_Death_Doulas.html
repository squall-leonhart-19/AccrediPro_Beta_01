Creating Engaging Content

The key to social media success is creating content that captures attention and resonates with your audience. Here are some ideas for engaging content:

  • Images: Share inspiring quotes, nature photos, and behind-the-scenes glimpses of your work (with client permission, of course).
  • Videos: Create short videos on topics like advance care planning, grief support, and legacy projects. Consider doing live Q&A sessions with your followers.
  • Stories: Use Instagram and Facebook Stories to share daily updates, behind-the-scenes content, and interactive polls and quizzes.
  • Blog Posts: Share excerpts from your blog posts and drive traffic to your website.
  • Client Testimonials: With client permission, share testimonials about your services.
  • Infographics: Create visually appealing infographics on topics like the benefits of advance care planning or the stages of grief.

A 2022 study published in the Journal of Social Media Marketing found that video content receives 1200% more shares than text and images combined. This highlights the importance of incorporating video into your social media strategy. Furthermore, posts with visuals are 650% more likely to be remembered, according to a study by Brain Rules.

Case Study: Sarah's Social Media Success

Client: Sarah Miller, a newly certified death doula.

Presenting Challenge: Sarah struggled to attract clients and build her brand in a competitive market.

Intervention: Sarah implemented a comprehensive social media strategy, focusing on Facebook and Instagram. She shared educational content, inspiring quotes, and client testimonials (with permission). She also ran targeted ads to reach people interested in end-of-life care.

Outcomes: Within six months, Sarah's social media following grew by 500%, and she started receiving a steady stream of inquiries from potential clients. She was able to build a thriving death doula practice and make a meaningful impact in her community.

Case Study: Ethical Dilemma on Instagram

Client: Emily Carter, a death doula with a growing Instagram presence.

Presenting Challenge: Emily received a request from a follower to share a photo of a deceased client's memorial service. The follower believed it would inspire others to plan their own celebrations of life.

Intervention: Emily declined the request, explaining that sharing such a photo would violate client privacy and could be insensitive to the family's grief. Instead, she created a blog post about the importance of personalized memorial services, using general examples and stock photos.

Outcomes: Emily maintained her ethical standards while still providing valuable information to her followers. She received positive feedback for her sensitivity and professionalism.
