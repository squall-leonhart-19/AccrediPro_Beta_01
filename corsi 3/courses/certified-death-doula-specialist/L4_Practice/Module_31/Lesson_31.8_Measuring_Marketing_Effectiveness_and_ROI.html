Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my mother will be open to this."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. I'm happy to schedule a brief, no-pressure introduction visit with your mother so she can meet me and see if she feels comfortable. It's important that she feels safe and at ease."
"I need to talk to my siblings about this first."
"Of course. I can prepare a detailed proposal outlining the services and costs so you can share it with them. I'm also available for a conference call to answer any questions they may have."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I understand. Let's explore some options to make it more manageable. We can start with a smaller package of hours or focus on the most critical areas of support. Remember, investing in this support can prevent burnout and ensure your mother receives the best possible care, which ultimately saves you time, energy, and potential healthcare costs down the road."
"I'm worried about letting a stranger into my mother's home."
"That‚Äôs completely understandable. I undergo thorough background checks, and I can provide references from other families I've worked with. I also encourage you to be present during our initial visits so you can observe our interactions and ensure you feel comfortable."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the initial minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing gratitude for their time, actively listening, and finding common ground or shared connections.

2. How can you address a prospect's concerns about the cost of your services without immediately lowering your price?

Highlight the value and long-term benefits, offer flexible payment options, or suggest a more tailored package to fit their budget.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you understand the prospect's needs and present your services as the solution.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, empathetic responses.
  • Practice stating your prices clearly and confidently, emphasizing the value you provide.
  • Focus on building genuine connections and demonstrating compassion to establish trust.
  • Realistic income is achievable with consistent client acquisition efforts.
