Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify the Primary Concerns
Eleanor is experiencing multiple distressing symptoms due to her underlying conditions and polypharmacy. Her kidney function is declining, and she's showing signs of malnutrition (low albumin). The primary goal is to improve her comfort and quality of life in her remaining months.
Step 2: Analyze Medication Interactions & Side Effects
Several medications can contribute to her symptoms. Opioids (Oxycodone) cause constipation and can suppress appetite. Furosemide can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances, potentially exacerbating kidney dysfunction. Lorazepam, while helpful for anxiety, can cause sedation and confusion, especially in older adults with impaired kidney function.
Step 3: Prioritize Interventions
Address constipation first, as it can significantly impact appetite and comfort. Review all medications with her physician to identify potential deprescribing opportunities (reducing or stopping medications that are no longer beneficial or causing harm). Focus on non-pharmacological approaches for anxiety and insomnia.
Step 4: Consider Ethical Implications
With a limited prognosis, the focus shifts from curative to palliative care. It's crucial to balance symptom relief with potential side effects, always prioritizing Eleanor's wishes and values. Open communication with her and her healthcare team is essential.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Medication-Induced Delirium

Lorazepam and Oxycodone can contribute to delirium, especially in older adults with cognitive impairment. Assess for changes in mental status.

2

Fluid Overload

Despite Furosemide, CHF can still lead to fluid overload, causing shortness of breath and edema. Monitor her weight and edema regularly.

3

Depression & Social Isolation

Living alone in assisted living can exacerbate depression. Assess her mood and social support system.

4

Occult Infection

Infections can present atypically in older adults. Rule out UTI or pneumonia if symptoms worsen.

Your Proposed Protocol

Phased Intervention Plan

ðŸ“… Phase 1: Symptom Management (Weeks 1-4)
  • Constipation: Implement a bowel protocol including daily stool softeners (docusate) and osmotic laxatives (Miralax) as needed. Encourage fluid intake and gentle movement.
  • Pain: Assess pain levels regularly and adjust Oxycodone dosage as needed, in consultation with her physician. Consider non-pharmacological approaches like massage or heat therapy.
  • Anxiety/Insomnia: Implement relaxation techniques (guided imagery, deep breathing). Explore alternative therapies like aromatherapy (lavender). Limit Lorazepam use to breakthrough anxiety only.
  • Appetite: Offer small, frequent meals of her favorite foods. Consider a nutritional supplement if intake remains poor.

  • Medication Review: Collaborate with her physician to review all medications and identify opportunities for deprescribing. Focus on minimizing medications with significant side effects or limited benefit.
  • Emotional Support: Provide regular emotional support and companionship. Encourage social interaction with other residents. Consider a referral to a palliative care social worker for additional support.
  • Advance Care Planning: Review her advance directives and ensure her wishes are documented and communicated to her healthcare team.

  • Hospice Evaluation: If her condition continues to decline, discuss hospice options with her and her family.
  • Legacy Work: Facilitate legacy projects such as creating a memory book or recording a video message for loved ones.
  • Spiritual Support: Connect her with a chaplain or spiritual advisor if desired.
  • Continuous Symptom Management: Continue to monitor and manage her symptoms to ensure maximum comfort and quality of life.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when managing pain in an end-of-life client with COPD and CHF?

Consider the potential respiratory depression from opioids. Start with low doses and titrate slowly. Non-pharmacological approaches should be prioritized.

2. How would you differentiate between medication-induced delirium and worsening dementia in Eleanor's case?

Delirium typically has a sudden onset and fluctuating course. Review her medications for potential culprits. Dementia is a gradual decline. A thorough assessment is needed.

3. What are some ethical considerations when deprescribing medications at the end of life?

The goal is to minimize suffering and maximize comfort, even if it means potentially shortening life slightly. Always respect the client's wishes and involve them in the decision-making process.

Key Takeaways

  • Polypharmacy is common in end-of-life care and requires careful medication review.
  • Prioritize symptom management to improve quality of life.
  • Ethical considerations are paramount when making treatment decisions.
  • Collaboration with the healthcare team is essential.
  • Focus on comfort, dignity, and respecting the client's wishes.
