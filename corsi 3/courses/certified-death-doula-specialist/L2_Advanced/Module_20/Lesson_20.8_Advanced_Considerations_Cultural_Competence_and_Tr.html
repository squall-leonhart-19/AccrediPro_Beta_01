Advanced Clinical Case Study

Complex Case Presentation

Eleanor, 78

Retired librarian, Asheville, NC ‚Ä¢ Widowed, lives in assisted living

Presenting Issue
Increasing anxiety and fear of dying, especially at night. Reports feeling "unprepared" and "incomplete."
Medical History
COPD (mild), osteoarthritis, history of breast cancer (treated with lumpectomy and radiation 10 years ago), mild cognitive impairment.
Medications
Albuterol inhaler (as needed), Tylenol (for pain), occasional Lorazepam (for anxiety).
Cognitive Assessment
Mini-Cog score: 3/5 (indicates possible dementia). Shows deficits in recall and executive function.
Social Support
Limited. Daughter lives out of state. Some interaction with other residents and staff.
Spiritual/Religious Beliefs
Raised Methodist, but hasn't attended church in years. Expresses uncertainty about the afterlife.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Breaking Down Eleanor's Case

Step 1: Prioritize Immediate Needs
Address Eleanor's nighttime anxiety first. Unmanaged anxiety exacerbates other symptoms and impairs quality of life. Consider non-pharmacological interventions initially.
Step 2: Assess Cognitive Capacity
The Mini-Cog score suggests cognitive impairment. This impacts Eleanor's ability to participate in legacy work or complex emotional processing. Tailor interventions accordingly.
Step 3: Explore Existential Concerns
Eleanor's feelings of being "unprepared" and "incomplete" point to unresolved existential issues. Gently explore her beliefs about death and what gives her life meaning.
Step 4: Mobilize Social Support
Limited social support increases feelings of isolation. Facilitate connection with her daughter and encourage participation in activities within the assisted living facility.

Differential Considerations

Considering Other Factors

1

Depression

Anxiety and fear of death can be symptoms of underlying depression. Use the Geriatric Depression Scale to screen for depressive symptoms.

2

Medication Side Effects

Lorazepam, while helpful for anxiety, can worsen cognitive impairment and increase the risk of falls. Review all medications for potential side effects.

3

Unresolved Grief

Eleanor is widowed, and her fear of death may be related to unresolved grief from the loss of her spouse. Explore her grieving process.

4

Spiritual Distress

Eleanor's uncertainty about the afterlife suggests spiritual distress. Assess her spiritual needs and connect her with resources if desired.

When to Refer

Know Your Limits

  • Worsening cognitive impairment ‚Äì Refer to geriatric psychiatrist or neurologist for comprehensive evaluation.
  • Suicidal ideation or intent ‚Äì Immediate referral to mental health services.
  • Severe respiratory distress ‚Äì Requires immediate medical attention due to COPD.
  • Uncontrolled pain ‚Äì Requires pain management specialist consultation.
  • Refusal of care or significant resistance ‚Äì Ethics consultation to navigate complex decision-making.

Proposed Phased Protocol

Eleanor's Care Plan

üìÖ Phase 1: Comfort & Safety (Weeks 1-4)
  • Implement a calming bedtime routine: warm bath, gentle music, aromatherapy (lavender).
  • Introduce guided imagery or progressive muscle relaxation to manage anxiety.
  • Assess and address pain levels to improve comfort.
  • Ensure a safe and familiar environment to reduce disorientation.
üìÖ Phase 2: Meaning & Connection (Weeks 5-8)
  • Facilitate reminiscence therapy using photos and memorabilia.
  • Encourage participation in social activities within the assisted living facility (e.g., book club, gardening).
  • Contact daughter to arrange regular phone calls or visits.
  • Explore Eleanor's values and what brings her joy.
üìÖ Phase 3: Legacy & Closure (Weeks 9-12)
  • Create a simple life review with a focus on positive memories and accomplishments.
  • Explore Eleanor's spiritual beliefs and connect her with a chaplain if desired.
  • Facilitate conversations about her wishes for end-of-life care.
  • Prepare a memory box or scrapbook for her daughter.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a holistic assessment that considers physical, cognitive, emotional, social, and spiritual factors.
  • Cognitive impairment significantly impacts a client's ability to participate in death doula services. Adapt your approach accordingly.
  • Addressing anxiety and ensuring comfort are crucial first steps in supporting clients facing end-of-life issues.
  • Mobilizing social support and exploring existential concerns can help clients find meaning and connection in the face of death.
  • Know your scope of practice and refer to other professionals when necessary. Collaboration is key to providing comprehensive care.
