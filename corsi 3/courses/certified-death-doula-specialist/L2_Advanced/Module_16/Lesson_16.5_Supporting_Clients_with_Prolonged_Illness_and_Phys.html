Understanding the Impact of Prolonged Illness

Prolonged illness can profoundly affect a client's quality of life and emotional well-being. Chronic pain, physical limitations, and the uncertainty of the future can lead to feelings of isolation, depression, and anxiety. It's crucial to recognize these challenges and approach the client with empathy and understanding. A 2021 study published in the Journal of Pain Research found that over 60% of individuals with chronic pain experience symptoms of depression. Understanding these statistics underscores the importance of emotional support in these cases.

Collaborating with the Medical Team

Effective collaboration with the medical team is essential to ensure the client receives appropriate palliative care and pain management. As a death doula, you can advocate for the client's needs, communicate their wishes, and ensure their voice is heard. This requires building a strong rapport with doctors, nurses, and other healthcare professionals. Consider attending medical appointments with the client to provide support and facilitate communication. In some areas, death doulas are seeing incomes of $60,000 - $80,000 per year by offering comprehensive support services that include medical advocacy.

Case Study: Sarah Davis

Sarah Davis

Client: Sarah Davis, 62 years old.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah was diagnosed with advanced rheumatoid arthritis five years ago, leading to chronic pain, limited mobility, and significant fatigue. She also experiences anxiety and feelings of isolation due to her physical limitations.

Intervention:

  1. Collaboration with Medical Team: Attended medical appointments with Sarah to advocate for better pain management strategies.
  2. Adapted S.O.U.L.S. Frameworkâ„¢:
    • Support System Scan: Identified family members and friends who could provide practical and emotional support.
    • Opening to Emotions: Created a safe space for Sarah to express her fears and frustrations about her illness.
    • Legacy & Life Review: Helped Sarah create a video message for her grandchildren, sharing her life lessons and values.
  3. Practical Support: Connected Sarah with a home healthcare aide who assists with daily tasks such as bathing and dressing.
  4. Empowerment: Encouraged Sarah to participate in a virtual support group for individuals with chronic pain.

Outcomes: Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety and feelings of isolation. Her pain management improved, and she felt more in control of her life. The video message project provided her with a sense of purpose and connection to her family.
