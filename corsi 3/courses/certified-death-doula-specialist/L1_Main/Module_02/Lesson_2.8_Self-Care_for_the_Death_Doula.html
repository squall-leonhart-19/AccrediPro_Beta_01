You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Diane expresses a sense of relief: "It feels good to finally talk about it."

  • She schedules a follow-up session: "I'd like to continue exploring this with you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing you can do to create a safe space for a grieving client?

The most important thing is to validate their feelings and assure them that there is no right or wrong way to grieve.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is feeling safe and supported?

Relaxed body language, making eye contact, softer tone of voice, and expressing a sense of relief are all good signs.

You're Becoming a Real Coach!

By practicing these dialogues and focusing on creating a safe space, you're developing the skills to help clients navigate difficult emotions with grace and compassion. Keep practicing, and trust your intuition.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize creating a safe and non-judgmental space for clients to express their emotions.
  • Validate their feelings and assure them that there is no "right" or "wrong" way to feel.
  • Practice active listening and reflect back what you're hearing to ensure understanding.
  • Gently encourage clients to explore their emotions without pushing them beyond their comfort zone.
  • Avoid offering unsolicited advice or minimizing their experience.
