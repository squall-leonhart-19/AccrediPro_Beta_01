Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on the unsaid. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing. It sounds like you're carrying a lot of unspoken concerns about this conversation with your daughter. Before we even talk about *what* you want to say, let's explore what's holding you back. What feels most difficult about bringing this up?"
MARIA:
"I don't want her to think I'm giving up. Or that I'm morbid. I just want her to know my wishes."
YOU:
"That's completely understandable. It‚Äôs common to worry about how loved ones will react. Let's acknowledge those fears. On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being 'terrified,' how afraid are you that she'll react negatively?"
MARIA:
"Probably a 7. I know she loves me, but she's also very sensitive."
YOU:
"Okay, a 7. Let's work with that. What's the *worst* thing she could say or do? Get really specific."
YOU:
"And then, let‚Äôs explore how you could handle that worst-case scenario. Sometimes just having a plan for the 'what ifs' makes the conversation feel less daunting."

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria says, "Yes, that's exactly what I'm afraid of."

  • She's willing to brainstorm solutions for her worst-case scenarios.

You're Becoming a Real Coach!

By practicing these dialogues, you're developing the ability to hear what's *not* being said. This is a powerful skill that will help your clients navigate the most challenging conversations. Keep going!

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge unspoken fears before logistical planning.
  • Validate the client's emotions and concerns.
  • Use gentle, reassuring language.
  • Help clients identify and prepare for worst-case scenarios.
  • Create a safe space for difficult conversations.
