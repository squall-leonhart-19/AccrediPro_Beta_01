Case Study: Personalized Memorial Service

Case Study: Eleanor's Farewell
EA

Eleanor Ainsworth, 78

Diagnosed with terminal cancer

Eleanor, a vibrant 78-year-old woman with a love for gardening and poetry, was diagnosed with terminal cancer. Her family sought the support of a death doula, Sarah, to help create a meaningful farewell.

Sarah utilized the S.O.U.L.S. Frameworkâ„¢ to guide her approach:

  • Support System Scan: Sarah identified Eleanor's closeclose-knit family, gardening club, and book club as key support systems. She involved members from each group in the planning process.
  • Opening to Emotions: Sarah facilitated family meetings where Eleanor and her loved ones could openly share their feelings, fears, and memories. This included creating a "memory jar" where everyone could write down their favorite moments with Eleanor.
  • Understanding the Unsaid: Through gentle conversations, Sarah uncovered Eleanor's unspoken wish to have her memorial service in her beloved garden. She also sensed a desire for her poetry to be shared.
  • Legacy & Life Review: Sarah helped Eleanor compile a collection of her poems and gardening tips into a small booklet to be distributed at the service. Family members also created a slideshow of photos spanning Eleanor's life.
  • Sacred Space & Closure: The memorial service was held in Eleanor's garden, surrounded by her flowers and plants. Her poems were read aloud, and attendees were invited to share their memories. A tree was planted in her honor, symbolizing her enduring legacy.

Outcome: Eleanor's memorial service was deeply personal and meaningful for her and her loved ones. The garden setting, the sharing of her poetry, and the involvement of her support networks created a powerful sense of closure. The family reported feeling comforted and supported in their grief journey.

Addressing Challenges and Ethical Considerations

While the S.O.U.L.S. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a valuable structure, it's important to acknowledge potential challenges and ethical considerations. These may include:

  • Cultural Sensitivity: Ensure that all practices and rituals are culturally appropriate and respectful of the client's beliefs and traditions. A 2021 study in the Journal of Cross-Cultural Gerontology found that culturally tailored end-of-life care significantly improves patient satisfaction (p < 0.01).
  • Family Conflicts: Navigate potential disagreements among family members regarding the sacred space or closure rituals. Facilitate open communication and compromise.
  • Emotional Boundaries: Maintain professional boundaries and avoid becoming overly emotionally involved. Seek supervision or peer support when needed. A 2022 survey by the National Hospice and Palliative Care Organization (NHPCO) reported that 35% of hospice workers experience burnout due to emotional strain.
  • Accessibility: Ensure that the sacred space is accessible to individuals with disabilities or mobility limitations.

Ethical considerations are paramount. Always prioritize the client's autonomy and wishes. Obtain informed consent for all interventions and respect their right to refuse any aspect of the process.
