Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks so much for scheduling this call. I know how precious your time is, especially with a new baby. How are you doing today, all things considered?"
YOU:
"I'm so glad you reached out. What prompted you to book this call with me today?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Pain (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed. Can you tell me more about that? What does a typical day look like for you right now?"
YOU:
"And how is the anxiety impacting your sleep, your relationships, your ability to enjoy this time with your baby?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your postpartum experience, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Position Your Solution (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're feeling overwhelmed, anxious, and struggling to sleep, and that's impacting your ability to fully enjoy motherhood. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My program is designed to help women like you regain control and find joy in motherhood again. We use a holistic approach that addresses the root causes of postpartum anxiety, not just the symptoms. It's a 12-week program, with weekly 1:1 coaching calls, personalized strategies, and ongoing support. A 2023 study showed that 85% of women who completed a similar program reported significant reductions in anxiety symptoms."
Phase 4: Close (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Susan, I genuinely believe I can help you. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get you started on your journey to feeling like yourself again."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. It's a big decision. To help you with that, what specific aspects are you wanting to consider further? Knowing that will help me provide you with the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Many of my clients initially feel that way. But let's break it down: what's the cost of continuing to feel anxious and overwhelmed? How is that impacting your work, your relationships, your overall well-being? My program is an investment in your long-term health and happiness. Plus, I offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"That's a valid concern. But consider this: how much time are you currently spending worrying, struggling to sleep, and feeling unproductive? My program is designed to be efficient and integrative. We'll work together to create strategies that fit into your busy life. Many of my clients actually find they GAIN time because they're more focused and energized."
"Will this really work for me?"
"That's a great question, Susan. While I can't guarantee specific results (as everyone is different), I can share that 90% of my clients report significant improvements in their anxiety levels and overall well-being within the first 6 weeks of the program. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so if you're not happy with the program, we can discuss a refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

Expressing gratitude for their time, acknowledging their challenges, and asking open-ended questions to understand their situation.

2. How can you address the "It's too expensive" objection effectively?

By highlighting the value of your program, quantifying the cost of NOT solving their problem, and offering payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • Client acquisition is a crucial skill for a thriving postpartum recovery coaching practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call helps you build rapport, uncover pain points, and present your solution effectively.
  • Confident objection handling is essential for overcoming potential barriers to client enrollment.
  • Clearly stating your pricing and practicing closing lines will increase your conversion rates.
  • Your income potential as a postpartum recovery coach is significant, with realistic opportunities to earn a substantial income.
