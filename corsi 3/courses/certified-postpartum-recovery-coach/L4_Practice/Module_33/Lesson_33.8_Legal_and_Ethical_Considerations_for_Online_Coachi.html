Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you're looking for postpartum support. How are you doing today, really?"
YOU:
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed. Many new moms experience similar challenges. What prompted you to reach out for coaching specifically?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the sleep deprivation and anxiety. How are they impacting your daily life and your relationship with your baby?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you tried so far to manage these challenges? What has worked, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine six months from now, if things continue as they are. What does that look like? Now, imagine you've successfully overcome these challenges. What's different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, it sounds like you're experiencing [summarize her pain points]. Is that a fair assessment?"
YOU:
"My approach is holistic and personalized. We'll work together to address your sleep, anxiety, nutrition, and overall well-being. I'll provide you with practical tools, support, and accountability to help you reclaim your energy and joy. My 12-week program includes weekly coaching calls, personalized action plans, and ongoing support between sessions. Many of my clients, like Maria (age 45), report significant improvements within the first month. "
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, do you feel like this program could be a good fit for you? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Postpartum life is demanding. My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. The weekly calls are just one hour, and the action steps can be integrated into your daily routine. Think of this as an investment in your well-being, which will ultimately make you a better mom."
"I need to talk to my partner first."
"That's perfectly fine. What are some of the key things you'd like to discuss with them? I'm happy to provide you with information to share or even schedule a brief call together to answer any questions."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"I understand your hesitation. Every client is unique, and I tailor my approach to meet your specific needs. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not seeing progress within the first few weeks, we can adjust the program or offer a refund."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a flexible payment plan. You can choose to pay in three monthly installments, making the program more accessible. This allows you to spread out the investment while still receiving the full benefits of the coaching."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What is a good way to respond to the objection "I need to think about it?"

A good response is: "I completely understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Empathy and active listening build trust and rapport.
  • Addressing objections confidently increases conversion rates.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and value is essential.
  • Consistent practice leads to increased confidence and success.
