Your Ideal Prospect

Susan, 45

Saw your ad on Facebook. Booked a discovery call.

Her Situation
Feeling overwhelmed, postpartum anxiety, sleep-deprived, struggling to balance work and motherhood.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure I can afford this right now."
Decision Style
Needs reassurance, values practical advice, wants a clear plan.
Her Goal
"I want to feel like I have a handle on things and enjoy my baby."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! It's so nice to finally connect. Thank you for scheduling this call. I know you're busy. I saw you found me on Facebook. What caught your eye about my services?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! So, tell me a bit about what's been going on since your baby arrived. What are the biggest challenges you're facing right now?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"I hear you saying you're feeling overwhelmed and anxious. Can you describe what that feels like day-to-day?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage these feelings? Have you spoken with your doctor or tried any self-care techniques?"
YOU:
"Imagine things continue as they are now for the next few months. What impact will that have on you, your baby, and your family? And if you could magically change one thing about your situation, what would it be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're dealing with a lot. It's completely understandable that you're feeling overwhelmed. Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: [Summarize her key challenges and goals]. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because it's tailored specifically to postpartum recovery. I combine evidence-based strategies with personalized support to help you regain your energy, manage your anxiety, and create a sustainable routine. Over 12 weeks, we'll address [mention 2-3 specific areas, e.g., sleep, nutrition, emotional well-being]. Many of my clients report feeling significantly calmer and more in control within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been searching for? ... Excellent! Let me share the details of my program and how we can get you started on the path to feeling better."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"That's perfectly reasonable. I'm happy to answer any questions your partner might have. Would it be helpful to schedule a brief call with both of you, or would you prefer I send you some detailed information you can share?"
"I'm not sure I have the time for this right now."
"I understand. Time is precious, especially with a new baby. Let's look at it this way: investing in your well-being now can actually *create* more time in the long run. When you're less anxious and more energized, you'll be more efficient and present. Plus, our sessions are designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. Would you be open to exploring how we can make it work for you?"
"It's too much money."
"I appreciate that you're being upfront about your budget. Can I ask, what value do you place on feeling like yourself again? Think about the cost of *not* addressing these challenges ‚Äì the impact on your relationships, your career, and your overall well-being. My program is an investment in your long-term health and happiness. We also have flexible payment options to make it more accessible. Would you like to discuss those?"
"I've tried other things, and they haven't worked."
"I hear your frustration. It's disheartening to invest time and energy into something that doesn't deliver results. My program is different because it focuses on a holistic, personalized approach tailored to your specific needs. We'll dig deep to identify the root causes of your challenges and create a plan that's sustainable for you. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so you can feel confident in your investment. Are you willing to give it one more try, with a different approach?"

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are crucial for converting leads into paying clients.
  • A structured approach, including rapport-building, problem identification, solution presentation, and closing, increases your chances of success.
