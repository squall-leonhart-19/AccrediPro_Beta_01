1. Determining Your Value Proposition

Your value proposition is the unique benefit you offer to your clients. It's what sets you apart from other coaches and makes your services worth the investment. Consider these factors:

  • Your Niche: Are you specializing in sleep support, nutritional guidance, or emotional well-being? As discussed in Lesson 26.1, a niche can command higher prices.
  • Your Experience: Do you have certifications, specialized training, or personal experience that adds value?
  • Your Unique Approach: Does your coaching style incorporate mindfulness, specific therapeutic techniques, or personalized plans?
  • The B.L.O.O.M. Framework‚Ñ¢: How does your coaching specifically address each element of the framework?

Translate these factors into tangible benefits for your clients. For example, instead of saying "I offer sleep support," say "I help new mothers achieve restorative sleep, reducing fatigue and improving mood within weeks."

Income Example: A coach specializing in sleep support for postpartum mothers, offering a 6-week program, can realistically charge between $800-$1500 per client.

2. Researching Market Rates

Understanding the going rates for postpartum recovery coaching and related services is crucial for setting competitive prices. Consider these avenues for research:

  • Online Research: Explore websites of other postpartum coaches, doulas, and lactation consultants to get a sense of their pricing structures.
  • Networking: Connect with other professionals in your area to discuss pricing trends and best practices.
  • Client Surveys: Ask potential clients what they would be willing to pay for the services you offer.

Remember to factor in your geographic location, as prices can vary significantly depending on the cost of living. Aim to position yourself competitively within your market.

3. Creating Different Package Options

Offering a variety of package options allows you to cater to different client needs and budgets. Here are some ideas:

  • Basic Package: Includes essential coaching sessions, email support, and access to basic resources.
  • Premium Package: Offers more frequent coaching sessions, personalized meal plans, customized exercise routines, and priority email support.
  • VIP Package: Provides unlimited coaching sessions, home visits, access to exclusive workshops, and 24/7 support.

4. Developing Payment Plans

To make your services more accessible, consider offering payment plans. This can be especially helpful for clients who are on a tight budget. Options include:

  • Installment Plans: Divide the total cost into smaller, more manageable payments.
  • Early Bird Discounts: Offer a discount for clients who pay in full upfront.
  • Sliding Scale Fees: Adjust your fees based on the client's income.

Clearly outline the terms of your payment plans in your contract, including the payment schedule, late fees, and consequences of non-payment.

5. Outlining Terms and Conditions

A clear and comprehensive contract is essential for protecting yourself and your clients. Your terms and conditions should include:

  • Cancellation Policy: Specify the amount of notice required for cancellations and any associated fees.
  • Refund Policy: Outline the conditions under which refunds will be granted.
  • Confidentiality Agreement: Ensure that client information will be kept private and secure.
  • Disclaimer: State that you are not a medical professional and that your services are not a substitute for medical care.

Career Changer Success

Outcome: Maria quickly attracted clients and found that her premium package was the most popular.
