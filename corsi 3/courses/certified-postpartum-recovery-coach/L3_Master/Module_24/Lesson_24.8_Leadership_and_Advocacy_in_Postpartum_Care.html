Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Prioritization Strategies

Teach Sarah how to use the BLOOOM framework to prioritize needs. Explain that addressing sleep is often foundational for improving mood and milk supply.

2

The Interconnectedness of Symptoms

Help Sarah understand that postpartum symptoms rarely exist in isolation. Discuss how hormonal imbalances, sleep deprivation, and emotional distress can create a cascade of issues.

3

Setting Realistic Expectations

Advise Sarah to set realistic expectations for both herself and Emily. Remind her that progress may be gradual and that setbacks are normal.

4

Building Confidence

Encourage Sarah to trust her instincts and training. Remind her that she has the knowledge and skills to help Emily, even if she feels overwhelmed.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, I can see how much you care about Emily, and it's clear you've put a lot of thought into her case. It's admirable that you're seeking guidance to ensure you're providing the best possible support."
Guide Her Thinking
"Let's think about this from a systems perspective. If we improve Emily's sleep, what other areas might be positively impacted? How might that change your approach?"
Offer a Practical Tool
"Have you considered using a sleep diary with Emily? This can help identify patterns and triggers, and it provides a tangible way to track progress. We can use this data to tailor her plan."
Reinforce Her Strengths
"Remember, Sarah, you have a gift for building rapport. Use that to empower Emily. Small steps, consistently applied, can make a huge difference."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to validate when mentoring a new practitioner?

Their feelings of overwhelm and uncertainty are normal and understandable. It's crucial to normalize the learning process.

2. When reviewing a case, should you provide the treatment plan directly?

No, guide them to develop their own plan by asking questions and providing frameworks. This builds their clinical reasoning skills.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize addressing foundational needs like sleep and nutrition.
  • Teach new practitioners to see the interconnectedness of postpartum symptoms.
  • Empower them to trust their instincts and training.
  • Provide specific tools and strategies for treatment planning.
  • Encourage self-care to prevent burnout.
