Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, using techniques we've covered. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Michelle, thank you for being so open. It takes courage to admit these feelings, especially when society puts so much pressure on new mothers. I want to assure you, what you're describing is more common than people realize, and it doesn't mean you're failing."
MICHELLE:
"It feels like everyone else has it figured out. I see these perfect moms on social media, and I feel so inadequate."
YOU:
"Social media rarely reflects reality. Let's focus on YOUR reality, and what *you* need to thrive. Before we dive into strategies, I want to explore your birth experience a bit more. Research shows a link between difficult births and postpartum bonding challenges. Would you be willing to share more about your C-section and how you felt afterward?"
MICHELLE:
"Yes, I'm comfortable with that. It was traumatic. I felt so out of control and disconnected from my body."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. I understand this is difficult. Now, thinking about your baby's cues, can you give me a specific example of a time when you felt particularly lost or confused about what your baby needed?"
MICHELLE:
"Ugh, just yesterday. She was crying, and I tried feeding her, changing her, rocking her...nothing worked. I felt like such a failure."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly frustrating. Let's use that example to explore some techniques for decoding your baby's cues. We'll also discuss strategies for managing your own stress and anxiety, because your emotional state directly impacts your ability to connect with your baby. Does that sound like a good approach?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explore the birth experience with a client who is struggling with bonding?

Research suggests that difficult or traumatic birth experiences can significantly impact postpartum bonding. Understanding the client's birth experience can provide valuable insights into her current challenges.

2. What are some examples of open-ended questions you can ask to help a client explore her feelings about bonding with her baby?

Examples include: "Can you describe a time when you felt particularly disconnected from your baby?" "What are your biggest fears about your ability to bond with your baby?" "What does a 'good' mother-baby relationship look like to you?"
