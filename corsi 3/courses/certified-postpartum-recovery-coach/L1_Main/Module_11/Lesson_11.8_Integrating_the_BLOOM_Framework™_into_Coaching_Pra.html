Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a new mother's feelings of disconnection before offering solutions?

Validating her feelings builds trust and allows her to feel understood, which is crucial for opening her up to receiving coaching. It helps her feel safe and less judged.

2. What are some non-verbal cues that indicate a client is feeling heard and understood during a coaching session?

Relaxed body language, making eye contact, nodding, and verbal affirmations like "yes" or "I understand" are all positive indicators.

Key Takeaways

  • Bonding is a process, not an instant event.
  • Validating a mother's feelings is crucial for building trust.
  • Gentle questioning can reveal underlying emotions and anxieties.
  • Visualizations can help mothers reconnect with their babies.
  • Practical strategies can nurture the mother-infant bond over time.
