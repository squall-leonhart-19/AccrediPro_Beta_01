What You Can and Cannot Do

Welcome back! As you embark on this exciting journey, it‚Äôs crucial to understand the scope of practice for a Certified TCM Practitioner‚Ñ¢. We are educators, not diagnosticians. Our role is to empower clients with knowledge and tools to support their well-being.

We educate, we don't diagnose. This means we provide information, guidance, and support but do not offer medical diagnoses or treatments. We work alongside medical professionals, complementing their expertise, not replacing it. Remember, the power of coaching lies in guiding and motivating clients to make informed decisions about their health.

Legal Considerations

The legality of health coaching varies by location, so always check your local regulations. Generally, health coaching is permissible as long as you stay within the bounds of education and support.

It's wise to consider professional liability insurance to protect yourself. Stay in your lane professionally by avoiding medical advice and always referring clients to medical doctors for diagnoses or treatment plans. This ensures you're providing safe and ethical guidance.

Ethics & Professionalism

Client confidentiality is paramount. Always maintain the privacy of your clients' information. Professional conduct involves treating everyone with respect, maintaining clear boundaries, and acting with integrity.

Knowing when to refer out is key. If a client presents with symptoms or conditions outside your scope, refer them to a qualified healthcare professional. Building trust and credibility comes from ethical practice and genuine care for your clients' well-being.
