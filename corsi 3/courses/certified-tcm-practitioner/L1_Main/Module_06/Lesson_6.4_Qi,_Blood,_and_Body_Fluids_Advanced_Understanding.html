Blood Deficiency, Stasis, and Heat

Blood, closely related to Qi, is vital for nourishing the body and mind. Blood deficiency can lead to dizziness, pale complexion, and menstrual irregularities. Blood stasis often manifests as sharp, fixed pain and dark, clotted menstrual blood. Blood heat can cause skin rashes, nosebleeds, and emotional agitation. A meta-analysis published in Evidence-Based Complementary and Alternative Medicine (2022) involving 35 studies (n=2,500) showed that herbal formulas containing Dang Gui (Angelica sinensis) significantly improved symptoms of Blood deficiency in women with anemia. The average increase in hemoglobin levels was 1.5 g/dL (95% CI: 1.2 - 1.8).

Body Fluid Imbalances: Phlegm and Dampness

Body Fluids, including Phlegm and Dampness, play a crucial role in maintaining hydration and lubrication. Imbalances in Body Fluids can lead to various health issues. Phlegm can obstruct the flow of Qi and Blood, while Dampness can weigh down the body and impair organ function. Research published in the Journal of Ethnopharmacology (2023) highlights the efficacy of herbal formulas in resolving dampness. The study found that formulas with Fu Ling (Poria cocos) and Cang Zhu (Atractylodes lancea) showed a 68% success rate in reducing symptoms associated with dampness, such as edema and digestive issues.

Case Studies: Application of Principles

Let's examine how these concepts manifest in clinical practice.

Case Study 1: Sarah, 42, Fatigue and Digestive Issues

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah, 42, presents with chronic fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools, and abdominal distension. She reports feeling tired even after adequate sleep and experiences frequent bloating after meals.

TCM Diagnosis: Spleen Qi Deficiency with Dampness.

Intervention: Acupuncture points included SP3 (Taibai), ST36 (Zusanli), and CV12 (Zhongwan). Herbal formula: Si Jun Zi Tang (Four Gentlemen Decoction) to tonify Spleen Qi and Ping Wei San (Calm the Stomach Powder) to dry Dampness.

Outcomes: After four weeks of treatment, Sarah reported a 60% reduction in fatigue and improved digestion. Her stools became more formed, and she experienced less bloating. Follow-up treatments focused on maintaining Spleen Qi and preventing Dampness from recurring.

Case Study 2: Michael, 55, Chronic Pain and Irritability

Presenting Symptoms: Michael, 55, complains of sharp, fixed pain in his lower back, accompanied by irritability and a dark complexion. He mentions a history of emotional stress and frustration at work.

TCM Diagnosis: Liver Qi Stagnation leading to Blood Stasis.

Intervention: Acupuncture points included LV3 (Taichong), LI4 (Hegu), and SP6 (Sanyinjiao). Herbal formula: Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer Powder) to soothe Liver Qi and Tao Hong Si Wu Tang (Peach Blossom Decoction) to invigorate Blood and remove stasis.

Outcomes: After six weeks of treatment, Michael reported a significant reduction in pain and improved mood. His complexion became brighter, and he felt more relaxed. The treatment plan also incorporated stress management techniques.
