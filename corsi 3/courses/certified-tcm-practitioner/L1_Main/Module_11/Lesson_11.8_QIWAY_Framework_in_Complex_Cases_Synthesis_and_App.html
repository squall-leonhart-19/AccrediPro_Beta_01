Your Coaching Script

Here's a sample of how to begin a deep-dive coaching session with Maria. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're at a real crossroads. Before we talk solutions, let's explore what 'feeling excited' actually means to you. What does that *look* like in your daily life?"
MARIA:
"Hmm, that's a good question. I guess it means waking up with a sense of purpose, looking forward to something... not just grading papers and watching TV."
YOU:
"Okay, purpose and anticipation. Let's dig a little deeper. Think back to a time in your life when you *did* feel that way. What were you doing? Who were you with? What were your days like?"
MARIA:
"Wow, that takes me back... When I first started teaching, I was so passionate about shaping young minds. I'd spend hours creating lesson plans, collaborating with other teachers... It was exhausting, but fulfilling."
YOU:
"That's powerful. So, passion, collaboration, and creation. It sounds like those are key elements for you. Now, how can we bring some of those elements back into your life today, even if it's not in the same way?"
YOU:
"Considering those elements, let's explore some options that resonate with you. What feels like a small, achievable step you could take this week to reignite that sense of purpose?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to avoid offering solutions too quickly in a deep-dive coaching session?

Offering solutions prematurely can prevent the client from fully exploring their feelings, experiences, and underlying motivations. Deep-dive coaching aims to uncover insights that the client may not be consciously aware of, which requires a more exploratory approach.

2. What is the purpose of asking the client to recall a time when they felt a particular emotion or experience?

Recalling past experiences helps the client reconnect with their values, passions, and sources of fulfillment. It can also provide valuable clues about what they need to reintegrate into their life to feel more aligned and purposeful.

Key Takeaways

  • Deep-dive coaching is about exploration, not prescription.
  • Focus on uncovering the client's values, passions, and past experiences.
  • Ask open-ended questions that encourage reflection and self-discovery.
  • Allow for silence and hold space for the client to process their thoughts.
  • Help the client identify small, achievable steps they can take to move forward.
