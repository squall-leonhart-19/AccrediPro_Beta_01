Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Linda! Thanks for connecting. I saw you were interested in my menopause workshop. I'm Sarah, and I help women navigate this transition with TCM. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"Great! So, what specifically about the workshop caught your eye? What are you hoping to get out of it?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the symptoms you're experiencing. How long have you been dealing with them? What's been the most challenging aspect?"
YOU:
"Have you tried any other approaches to manage your symptoms? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If your symptoms continue, how do you see it impacting your life in the next year? What would it mean to you to find a natural solution that works?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Linda, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for a natural, effective way to manage your menopause symptoms and regain control of your well-being. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My workshop uses TCM principles to address the root causes of menopause symptoms. We'll cover dietary recommendations, lifestyle adjustments, acupressure techniques, and herbal remedies. It's designed to be interactive and supportive, so you'll have the opportunity to connect with other women going through the same thing."
YOU:
"Many participants report a significant reduction in hot flashes, improved sleep, and better mood after implementing these strategies. Plus, you'll receive a comprehensive workbook with all the key information and resources."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Linda, does this workshop sound like a good fit for what you're looking for? ... Great! Let me tell you about the details and how you can register."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. The workshop is designed to be efficient and practical, with strategies you can easily incorporate into your daily routine. Plus, you'll have access to the recordings and materials afterwards, so you can review them at your own pace. How about we discuss your typical week and see where you might be able to carve out a little time for yourself?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"I hear you. Every woman's experience with menopause is unique. However, TCM has been used for centuries to address these types of symptoms. The workshop provides a range of tools and techniques, so you can find what works best for your body. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee ‚Äì if you're not happy with the workshop, I'll offer a full refund."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I understand. When considering the price, think about the value you're receiving: expert guidance, a comprehensive workbook, a supportive community, and practical strategies you can use for years to come. Plus, compare it to the cost of ongoing doctor visits, prescription medications, or supplements that may not be effective. This is an investment in your long-term well-being."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan with two installments to make it more accessible for you. The first installment secures your spot, and the second is due [date]. Would that work better for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call for a group program?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and closing with a clear call-to-action.

2. How can you address the objection that a workshop is "too expensive"?

Highlight the value, compare to other costs, offer a payment plan, and emphasize the long-term investment in their well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs and workshops can be a lucrative and impactful way to expand your practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting interested prospects into paying participants.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence can help overcome potential barriers to registration.
  • Presenting your pricing clearly and highlighting the value of your program is crucial for success.
  • Consistent marketing and promotion are key to attracting a steady stream of participants to your workshops.
