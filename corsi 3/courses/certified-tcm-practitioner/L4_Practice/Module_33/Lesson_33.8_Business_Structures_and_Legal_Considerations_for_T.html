Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. However, the time investment is typically just a few hours per week, including appointments and self-care practices. And the return on investment ‚Äì better sleep, more energy, and improved mood ‚Äì can significantly enhance your productivity and overall quality of life. "I've tried acupuncture before, and it didn't work for me."
"I'm sorry to hear that. It's important to remember that not all practitioners are the same. My approach is highly personalized and based on a deep understanding of TCM principles. Also, we often combine acupuncture with herbal medicine and dietary changes for a more comprehensive approach. Can you tell me more about your previous experience with acupuncture? Perhaps we can identify what didn't work and address it in our approach."
"I need to discuss this with my family first."
"That's perfectly reasonable. I'm happy to provide you with some detailed information about the program that you can share with your family. I'm also available to answer any questions they may have. Would you prefer I send you a brochure or schedule a brief call with you and your family?"
"What if it doesn't work?"
"That's a valid question. While I can't guarantee specific results, I am committed to providing you with the highest quality care and support. I closely monitor your progress and make adjustments to the treatment plan as needed. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee: if you're not satisfied with the program after the first month, we can discuss a partial refund. My goal is to help you achieve your health goals."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include in a discovery call to ensure client conversion?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem deeply, and confidently presenting your unique approach and pricing.

2. How should you handle the objection, "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by highlighting the potential return on investment in terms of improved health, reduced expenses on ineffective treatments, and increased quality of life.
