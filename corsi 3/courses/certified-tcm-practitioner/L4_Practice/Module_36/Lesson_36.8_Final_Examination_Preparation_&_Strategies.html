Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can support you. To start, what resonated with you about my Instagram content that made you want to reach out?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! So, tell me, what's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe the hot flashes? How often do they happen, and how intense are they?"
YOU:
"And what about the insomnia? How many hours of sleep are you getting, and how is that affecting your energy levels during the day?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage these symptoms? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and wake up tomorrow feeling amazing, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, what I'm hearing is that you're struggling with intense menopause symptoms that are impacting your sleep, energy, and overall well-being. You've tried some things, but haven't found lasting relief. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different. We use Traditional Chinese Medicine principles to address the root cause of these imbalances. Over 12 weeks, we'll create a personalized plan using acupuncture, herbal remedies, dietary changes, and lifestyle adjustments. A 2023 study in the 'Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine' found that TCM significantly reduces menopause symptoms in 70% of women. I've helped many women in their 40s and 50s regain control of their health and feel like themselves again."
YOU:
"How does that sound to you?"
Phase 4: The Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I'm confident I can help you find relief and restore your vitality. Are you ready to explore how we can work together?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. What specifically would you like to think about? Is it the program itself, the time commitment, or the investment? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you, Susan. Let's reframe this. You mentioned you're losing sleep, feeling anxious, and your energy is low. How much is that costing you in terms of productivity, enjoyment of life, and overall well-being? My program is an investment in your health and happiness. Plus, I offer payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"That's a valid concern. But think about it this way: how much time are you currently spending dealing with these symptoms? My program is designed to be efficient and effective, and we can tailor it to fit your schedule. Even small changes can make a big difference."
"Will this really work for me?"
"I understand your hesitation, especially if you've tried other things without success. My approach is personalized, addressing the root cause of your symptoms. I have a proven track record of helping women like you find relief. In fact, 85% of my clients report a significant reduction in menopause symptoms within the first 6 weeks. I'm confident we can achieve similar results for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the problem, presenting your solution, and closing the deal.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Reframe the cost as an investment in their health and well-being. Discuss the value of the program and offer payment options.

3. Why is it important to practice your pricing presentation?

To confidently convey the value of your services and avoid hesitation, which can undermine your credibility.
