3. Utilizing the Six Stages (Liu Jing) and Four Levels (Wei Qi Ying Xue)

The Six Stages (Liu Jing) and Four Levels (Wei Qi Ying Xue) are diagnostic frameworks used primarily in acute and infectious conditions. The Six Stages, derived from the Shang Han Lun, describe the progression of disease based on the body's reaction to external pathogens. The Four Levels illustrate the depth of pathogenic invasion, from the superficial Wei level to the deeper Xue level.

Understanding these frameworks allows you to differentiate between conditions like a common cold at the Wei level versus a more severe infection affecting the Ying or Xue levels. For example, a client with a mild fever, aversion to cold, and a floating pulse might be at the Wei level, while a client with high fever, delirium, and skin rashes might be at the Ying or Xue level.

4. Case Study: Liver Qi Stagnation vs. Liver Blood Deficiency

Case Study: Sarah, 35

Client: Sarah, a 35-year-old female.

Presenting Symptoms: Irregular menstrual cycles, premenstrual irritability, fatigue, and mild headaches.

Differentiation:

  • Liver Qi Stagnation: Sarah experiences significant premenstrual irritability, distension in the chest and abdomen, and her tongue has slightly red sides. Her pulse is wiry.
  • Liver Blood Deficiency: Sarah's periods are light and scanty, she experiences dizziness and blurred vision, and her complexion is pale. Her tongue is pale and thin, and her pulse is thready.

Intervention:

  • Liver Qi Stagnation: Acupuncture points such as Liver 3 (Taichong), Liver 14 (Qimen), and GB 34 (Yanglingquan) were used to soothe Liver Qi and regulate menstruation. Herbal formula: Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer Powder).
  • Liver Blood Deficiency: Acupuncture points such as Liver 8 (Ququan), Spleen 6 (Sanyinjiao), and Kidney 3 (Taixi) were used to nourish Liver Blood and tonify Qi. Herbal formula: Si Wu Tang (Four Substance Decoction).

Outcomes:

  • Liver Qi Stagnation: Sarah reported a significant reduction in premenstrual irritability and distension after 4 weeks of treatment. Her menstrual cycles became more regular.
  • Liver Blood Deficiency: Sarah reported reduced dizziness and improved vision after 6 weeks of treatment. Her complexion improved, and her periods became less scanty.

This case highlights the importance of differentiating between similar patterns. While both Liver Qi Stagnation and Liver Blood Deficiency can cause menstrual irregularities, the specific signs and symptoms guide the practitioner towards the correct diagnosis and treatment strategy. A 2021 study in the Journal of Traditional Chinese Medicine found that accurate differentiation of Liver patterns significantly improved treatment outcomes in women with menstrual disorders.
