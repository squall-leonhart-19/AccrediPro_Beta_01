Case Study 1: Fibromyalgia and Emotional Distress

👩
Case Study: Emily, 48

Fibromyalgia and Liver Qi Stagnation

Presenting Symptoms: Emily, a 48-year-old marketing manager, presents with chronic widespread pain characteristic of fibromyalgia. She also reports significant emotional distress, including anxiety, irritability, and occasional insomnia. Her pain is exacerbated by stress and lack of sleep.

TCM Assessment:

  • Qi Assessment: Weak Qi, especially in the Spleen and Liver channels. Her tongue is pale with a thin white coat, and her pulse is wiry and weak.
  • Illness Pattern Identification: Primary pattern is Liver Qi Stagnation affecting the Spleen, leading to Blood Deficiency and affecting the sinews. Secondary pattern is Heart Blood Deficiency due to chronic emotional strain.

Wellness Strategy Weaving:

  • Acupuncture: Points to soothe Liver Qi (LV 3, LV 14), strengthen Spleen Qi (SP 3, SP 6), and nourish Heart Blood (HT 7, BL 15).
  • Herbal Medicine: Xiao Yao San (Rambling Powder) modified to address Blood Deficiency.
  • Dietary Therapy: Warm, cooked foods to support Spleen Qi. Avoidance of caffeine and alcohol.
  • Lifestyle Recommendations: Regular gentle exercise (yoga, Tai Chi) and mindfulness practices.

Action & Adherence Amplification:

  • Educating Emily about the connection between stress and her symptoms.
  • Providing resources for stress management (meditation apps, support groups).
  • Ensuring the herbal formula is palatable and convenient.

Yield & Transformation Yielding:

  • Regularly monitoring pain levels, sleep quality, and emotional state.
  • Adjusting acupuncture points and herbal formula based on progress.
  • Encouraging Emily to actively participate in her healing process.

Treatment Rationale and Alternative Approaches

The treatment plan for Emily focuses on addressing the root cause of her symptoms: Liver Qi Stagnation. By soothing the Liver and strengthening the Spleen, we aim to improve Qi and Blood circulation, thereby alleviating pain and emotional distress. Nourishing Heart Blood addresses her anxiety and insomnia.

Alternative Approaches:

  • If pain is more severe: Consider adding points like GB 34 (influential point of the sinews) and Ah Shi points (tender points).
  • If emotional distress is predominant: Explore points along the Ren Mai to regulate Qi flow and calm the mind.
  • If Blood Deficiency is severe: Use Gui Pi Tang (Restore the Spleen Decoction) instead of modified Xiao Yao San.
