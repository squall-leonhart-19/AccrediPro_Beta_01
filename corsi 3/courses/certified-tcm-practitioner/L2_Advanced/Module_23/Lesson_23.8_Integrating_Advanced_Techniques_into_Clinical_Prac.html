Based on the client's presentation, a TCM assessment reveals the following:

  • Tongue: Pale with a red tip, teeth marks on the sides, thin yellow coating.
  • Pulse: Wiry and thready, especially on the left side.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify Core Imbalances
The primary imbalances are Liver Qi stagnation (due to stress and emotional factors), Blood deficiency (contributing to migraines, insomnia, and brain fog), and Spleen Qi deficiency (leading to digestive issues and Phlegm accumulation). The endometriosis history adds complexity, potentially exacerbating Blood stagnation.
Step 2: Connect the Dots
Stress and Liver Qi stagnation disrupt the normal flow of Qi and Blood, impacting the Spleen's ability to transform and transport nutrients, leading to Blood deficiency and Phlegm accumulation. The Liver Qi stagnation can also lead to Liver Yang rising, contributing to the migraines. The long-term use of oral contraceptives may also impact Liver function and nutrient absorption. Omeprazole further impairs nutrient absorption, exacerbating anemia.
Step 3: Prioritize Treatment Principles
Prioritize soothing the Liver and regulating Qi, nourishing Blood, strengthening the Spleen, and transforming Phlegm. Address the root cause of the migraines by calming Liver Yang and unblocking the channels in the head. Consider the impact of endometriosis and hormonal influences.
Step 4: Select Acupuncture Points & Herbal Formula
Acupuncture points: LV 3, LI 4, SP 6, ST 36, GB 20, Yintang, and auricular points for anxiety and pain management. Herbal formula: A modified Xiao Yao San (Free and Easy Wanderer Powder) with additions to nourish Blood and transform Phlegm, such as Dang Gui (Angelica sinensis) and Chen Pi (Citrus peel).

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Hormonal Imbalance

Irregular cycles, migraines, and endometriosis history suggest significant hormonal fluctuations (estrogen dominance, progesterone deficiency). Further hormone testing could be beneficial.

2

Chronic EBV Reactivation

History of EBV can contribute to fatigue, brain fog, and elevated liver enzymes. Consider supporting immune function and addressing viral load.

3

Cervical Spine Instability

Persistent neck and shoulder pain, especially with migraines, can indicate cervical spine issues. A referral for imaging may be necessary.

4

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Migraines, GERD, and endometriosis can sometimes be associated with MCAS. Consider dietary modifications and referral if symptoms are severe.

Referral triggers include sudden onset of severe headache, vision changes during migraines, unexplained weight loss, severe abdominal pain, and significantly elevated liver enzymes. These symptoms could indicate serious underlying conditions requiring immediate medical attention.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a thorough understanding of both TCM and biomedical perspectives.
  • Prioritize treatment based on the interplay of multiple conditions and identify the root causes.
  • A phased treatment plan allows for gradual and targeted interventions based on patient response.
  • Always be aware of potential referral triggers and scope of practice limitations.
  • Integrate lifestyle modifications, dietary changes, and emotional support for comprehensive care.
