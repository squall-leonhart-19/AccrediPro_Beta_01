Case Study: Qigong for Chronic Pain Management

Case Study: Sarah, 52, Chronic Back Pain

Client: Sarah, 52 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic lower back pain for over 5 years, exacerbated by stress and sedentary work. Limited range of motion and muscle stiffness.

Intervention: Sarah was prescribed a combination of acupuncture, herbal medicine, and a tailored Qigong routine. The Qigong routine included gentle stretching exercises, spinal cord breathing techniques and the Eight Brocades Qigong, modified to accommodate her limitations.

Outcomes: After 8 weeks, Sarah reported a 50% reduction in pain levels. Her range of motion improved significantly, and she experienced reduced muscle stiffness. She also reported feeling more relaxed and empowered to manage her pain.

Safety Precautions and Contraindications

While generally safe, Qigong and Tai Chi have certain precautions and contraindications. Addressing these is the 'A' (Addressing Lifestyle Adjustments) in the Q.I.W.A.Y. Frameworkâ„¢.

  • Acute Injuries: Avoid Qigong and Tai Chi during acute injuries or inflammation.
  • Severe Cardiovascular Conditions: Modify or avoid practices that significantly elevate heart rate or blood pressure.
  • Pregnancy: Certain Qigong and Tai Chi forms may be contraindicated during pregnancy. Consult with a qualified practitioner.
  • Mental Health Conditions: Use caution with individuals with severe mental health conditions, as some practices may trigger emotional release.
