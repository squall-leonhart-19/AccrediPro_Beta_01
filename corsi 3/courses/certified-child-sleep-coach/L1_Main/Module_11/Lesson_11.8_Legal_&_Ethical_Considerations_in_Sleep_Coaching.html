Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing all of that with me. It sounds incredibly frustrating to be dealing with sleep issues for so long, especially when you've already tried some solutions. Let's work together to uncover the root causes and create a personalized plan for you."
SUSAN:
"I really appreciate that. I feel like I'm running out of options."
YOU:
"I understand. Many women experience sleep disturbances, especially during times of transition. We'll explore factors like your daily routines, stress levels, and hormonal balance to get a clearer picture."
YOU:
"To start, I'd like to ask you about your evening routine. What does a typical evening look like for you from the time you finish work until you get into bed?"
SUSAN:
"Well, I usually get home around 6 pm, make dinner, maybe watch some TV, and then try to wind down around 10 or 11."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. Now, when you say 'wind down,' what does that usually involve?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's previous attempts to solve their sleep issues?

Validating their efforts shows empathy, builds trust, and prevents them from feeling like they've failed. It sets the stage for a collaborative approach.

2. What are some key areas to explore when a client is experiencing sleep disturbances?

Consider exploring their daily routines, evening rituals, stress levels, dietary habits, physical activity, and any potential underlying health conditions.

Key Takeaways

  • Actively listen and validate your client's experiences.
  • Avoid making assumptions or jumping to conclusions.
  • Use clear, easy-to-understand language.
  • Create a safe and supportive environment for your client to share their struggles.
  • Focus on finding the root causes of their sleep issues, not just treating the symptoms.
