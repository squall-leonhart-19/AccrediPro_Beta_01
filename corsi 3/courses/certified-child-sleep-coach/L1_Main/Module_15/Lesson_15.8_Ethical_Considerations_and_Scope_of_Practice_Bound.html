Your Coaching Script

Here's a script to guide your final consultation. Remember to adapt it to your own style:

YOU:
"Lisa, congratulations on the progress you've made in the last two weeks! Even small improvements are a sign you're on the right track. Let's review what's working, address the challenges, and solidify our plan for lasting success."
LISA:
"Thank you! It's been better, but some nights are still a disaster. I'm worried I'm not being consistent enough."
YOU:
"That's completely normal. Consistency is key, but it's also about understanding *why* things are inconsistent. Let's troubleshoot those tough nights. Can you describe what happened on the most recent challenging night?"
LISA:
"Well, yesterday, he refused to get into pajamas, then cried for an hour when I put him in bed. I ended up bringing him to our bed at 11 PM because I was exhausted."
YOU:
"Okay, let's break that down. It sounds like bedtime resistance is still a trigger. Have you been using the pre-bedtime routine we discussed? Remember, that routine should be consistent and calming."
YOU:
"Lisa, remember the Done-For-You resources in the portal? Use those client intake forms and assessment templates to really personalize the solutions. Also, don't forget the client agreement templates. These resources alone save you 100+ hours of setup."

  • Lisa expresses renewed confidence: "I feel like I can do this!"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful final consultation?

Acknowledge progress, troubleshoot challenges, reinforce the plan, and empower the client.

2. Why is it important to remind clients of their "why?"

It helps them stay motivated and committed during challenging times.

Key Takeaways

  • Final consultations are about solidifying progress and long-term success.
  • Empathy and encouragement are just as important as practical advice.
  • Use the Done-For-You resources to save time and effort.
  • Focus on empowering clients to become independent problem-solvers.
