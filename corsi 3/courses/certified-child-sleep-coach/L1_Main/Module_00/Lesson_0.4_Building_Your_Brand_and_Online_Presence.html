What You Can and Cannot Do

Welcome back! As a Certified Child Sleep Coachâ„¢, you'll be in a unique position to help families improve their children's sleep. But it's crucial to understand your scope of practice. We're here to educate, not diagnose.

What you CAN do:

  • Provide evidence-based education on sleep hygiene and healthy sleep habits.
  • Develop customized sleep plans based on general best practices and parental preferences.
  • Offer support and encouragement to families implementing their sleep plans.
  • Help parents understand their child's sleep patterns and behaviors.

What you CANNOT do:

  • Diagnose or treat medical conditions related to sleep (e.g., sleep apnea, insomnia).
  • Prescribe medications or supplements.
  • Provide medical advice or replace the guidance of a pediatrician or other healthcare professional.
  • Guarantee specific outcomes or "cure" sleep problems.

Remember, we educate, we don't diagnose. Your role is to work alongside medical professionals, not replace them. The power of coaching lies in education, empowerment, and support, within clearly defined boundaries.

Ethics & Professionalism

Building a thriving coaching practice requires more than just knowledge; it demands a strong ethical foundation.

  • Client Confidentiality: Protect your clients' privacy by keeping all personal information confidential. Obtain written consent before sharing any information with third parties.
  • Professional Conduct: Maintain a professional demeanor in all interactions. Avoid giving unsolicited advice or engaging in gossip.
  • When to Refer Out: Recognize when a client's needs fall outside your scope of practice and refer them to appropriate healthcare professionals. For example, if a child exhibits signs of sleep apnea, recommend a consultation with a pediatrician or sleep specialist.
  • Building Trust and Credibility: Be transparent about your qualifications and experience. Share client testimonials and success stories (with permission) to demonstrate your expertise.

By upholding these ethical principles, you'll build trust with your clients and establish a strong reputation in the coaching community.
