Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on reframing Maria's expectations. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing. It sounds incredibly draining. I want to start by saying that it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when your child isn't sleeping well, and it's also normal for 4-year-olds to still wake up at night. The sleep advice online can be confusing, and not all of it applies to every child."
MARIA:
"But I thought by this age, he should be sleeping through the night. All the other parents seem to have it figured out."
YOU:
"It's easy to think that everyone else has it all together, but often, what we see on the surface isn't the full picture. The reality is, night wakings are common in children even beyond infancy. Let's reframe what 'sleeping through the night' actually means at this age, and then we can create a realistic plan that works for your son and your family."
YOU:
"Instead of aiming for a perfect 12 hours of uninterrupted sleep, let's focus on reducing the frequency and duration of his night wakings. We'll also look at strategies to help him fall back asleep independently. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds more manageable, actually. I was feeling like a failure for not achieving this 'perfect' sleep."
YOU:
"You're definitely not a failure, Maria. You're a caring mom who's actively seeking solutions. Let's start by exploring his bedtime routine and what happens when he wakes up at night. This will give us a clearer picture of what's contributing to the wakings. Is that okay with you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a parent's feelings before reframing their expectations?

Validating their feelings builds trust and makes them more receptive to hearing alternative perspectives. If you dismiss their concerns outright, they're likely to become defensive and less open to your guidance.

2. What are some realistic expectations to set for a 4-year-old's sleep?

Instead of aiming for 10-12 hours of uninterrupted sleep, focus on reducing the frequency and duration of night wakings. Aim for consistent bedtimes and wake times, and work on strategies to help the child fall back asleep independently.

Key Takeaways

  • Reframing expectations is crucial for setting parents up for success.
  • Validate their feelings before offering alternative perspectives.
  • Provide realistic, achievable goals.
  • Focus on small, incremental improvements.
  • Reassure parents that they're not alone and that night wakings are common.
