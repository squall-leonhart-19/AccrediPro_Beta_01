Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a parent's feelings at the beginning of a sleep coaching session?

Validating their feelings helps build trust and rapport, making them more receptive to your guidance. It shows that you understand their struggles and are there to support them.

2. What's a better approach: immediately suggesting a sleep training method or exploring the child's sleep patterns first? Why?

Exploring the child's sleep patterns first is better. This allows you to understand the root causes of the sleep issues and tailor a personalized plan, rather than applying a generic solution.

Key Takeaways

  • Start by validating the parent's feelings and building rapport.
  • Gather information about the child's sleep patterns and daily routines before suggesting solutions.
  • Communicate clearly and simply, avoiding technical jargon.
  • Set clear expectations for the coaching process.
  • Emphasize the importance of consistency in implementing the sleep plan.
