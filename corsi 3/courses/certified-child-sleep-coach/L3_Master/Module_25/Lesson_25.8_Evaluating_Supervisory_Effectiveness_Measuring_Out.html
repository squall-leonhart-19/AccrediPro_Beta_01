Olivia, New L1 Graduate

Former teacher, passionate about helping families, limited business knowledge.

Excellent communication skills, patient, and dedicated to client success.
Growth Areas
Needs help with client management, setting boundaries, and marketing.
Her Question
"How do I handle a client who constantly emails me outside of scheduled hours?"

The Challenge She's Facing

Client Communication Boundaries

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, who is very anxious about her baby's sleep. She emails me multiple times a day with questions, even though we have set call times. I want to be supportive, but it's becoming overwhelming. I'm afraid of losing her as a client if I set boundaries."

Points to Consider:

  • Is Olivia's contract clear about communication protocols?
  • How can Olivia balance support with setting healthy boundaries?
  • What strategies can she use to reassure the client while protecting her time?
  • How can she address her fear of losing the client?

Your Mentoring Approach

Key Points to Discuss with Olivia

1

Emphasize the Importance of Boundaries

Explain that setting boundaries is essential for both her well-being and the client's success. Over-communication can hinder the client's ability to implement strategies independently.

2

Review the Contract

Ensure Olivia's contract clearly outlines communication guidelines, including response times and acceptable methods of contact. A well-defined contract sets expectations from the start.

3

Provide Communication Templates

Offer Olivia templates for responding to out-of-hours emails. These templates can reassure the client while reinforcing boundaries. Example: "Thank you for your email. I will address this during our scheduled call."

4

Address Her Fear of Client Loss

Discuss the reality that some clients may not be a good fit. Emphasize that setting boundaries attracts clients who respect her expertise and time. Share success stories of coaches who successfully set boundaries.

Crafting Your Feedback

How to Deliver Supportive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Olivia, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed by client communication, especially when you're starting out. It shows you care deeply about your clients."
Ask About Her Current Approach
"What have you tried so far to manage Sarah's emails? What were the results?"
Offer Practical Solutions
"One strategy I've found helpful is to create an FAQ document addressing common concerns. You can direct clients to this resource and reassure them that you'll cover everything in detail during your calls."
Reframe Her Perspective
"Remember, setting boundaries isn't about being unsupportive; it's about creating a sustainable practice where you can provide the best possible service without burning out. A well-rested coach is a better coach."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important for new sleep coaches to establish clear communication boundaries with clients?

Establishing clear communication boundaries prevents burnout for the coach, ensures sustainable practice, and promotes client independence.

2. What are some practical strategies a mentor can provide to help a new coach manage excessive client communication?

Practical strategies include reviewing the contract, providing communication templates, creating an FAQ document, and reframing the coach's perspective on setting boundaries.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new sleep coaches involves providing practical guidance and emotional support.
  • Setting boundaries is essential for both the coach's well-being and the client's success.
  • Reviewing contracts and providing communication templates can help new coaches manage client expectations.
  • Addressing fears of client loss and sharing success stories can build confidence in new coaches.
