Her Question

The Challenging Case

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I'm working with Sarah, a mom of 8-month-old Liam. He wakes every 2-3 hours, fights naps, and only falls asleep with rocking. I've suggested a consistent bedtime routine and gentle sleep training, but Sarah is overwhelmed and inconsistent. I'm not sure how to proceed."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • What specific strategies has Aisha suggested?
  • Has Aisha addressed potential underlying issues (teething, reflux, etc.)?
  • Has Aisha considered Sarah's emotional well-being and capacity for change?
  • Is Aisha feeling overwhelmed by the complexity of the case?

Your Teaching Strategy

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Acknowledge the Complexity

"It's normal to feel overwhelmed with multifaceted cases. Liam's challenges require a systematic approach."

2

Prioritize Issues

Guide Aisha to identify the most pressing issues (e.g., frequent night wakings) and address them one at a time.

3

Emphasize Parental Well-being

Remind Aisha to assess Sarah's emotional state and support system. A stressed parent can hinder progress.

4

Review Scope of Practice

Ensure Aisha understands when to refer to other professionals (e.g., pediatrician, lactation consultant).

Constructive Feedback

How to Guide Aisha Effectively

Start with Positive Reinforcement
"Aisha, I admire your dedication to helping Sarah and Liam. It's clear you care deeply about your clients."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What specific strategies have you suggested for Liam's night wakings? What was Sarah's reaction?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Have you considered exploring potential underlying causes, like teething or reflux? Sometimes addressing those can make a big difference."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on what you know about Sarah and Liam, what feels like the most appropriate next step?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address the mentee's feelings of overwhelm before diving into the case details?

Addressing their emotions first creates a safe space for learning and prevents them from feeling judged or inadequate.

2. What are some red flags that might indicate a case is beyond the scope of practice for an L1 sleep coach?

Medical conditions affecting sleep (e.g., sleep apnea), severe behavioral issues, or parental mental health concerns that significantly impact the child's sleep.

You're Cultivating Future Experts!

By sharing your knowledge and experience, you're empowering the next generation of sleep coaches to make a positive impact on families' lives. Your mentorship is invaluable!

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners through complex cases and challenges.
  • Prioritize addressing the mentee's emotional state and building their confidence.
  • Use guiding questions and alternative perspectives to foster critical thinking.
  • Reinforce the importance of staying within the scope of practice and referring when necessary.
  • Celebrate successes and learning moments to encourage continued growth.
