Guiding Principles for Mentorship

Essential Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Emphasize a Holistic Approach

Remind Olivia that sleep is interconnected with other factors. Encourage her to explore medical, developmental, and nutritional influences.

2

Reiterate the Importance of Parental Support

Discuss strategies for assessing and addressing parental anxiety or inconsistency. Suggest role-playing common scenarios to build her confidence.

3

Encourage Critical Thinking

Rather than providing solutions, ask Olivia probing questions: "What other factors might be contributing to Leo's sleep issues? What adjustments could you make?"

4

Affirm Her Expertise

Remind Olivia of her strengths as a former teacher ‚Äì her ability to educate and support parents. Help her translate those skills to sleep coaching.

Crafting Your Feedback

Delivering Constructive and Empowering Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Olivia, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client isn't responding as expected. It shows you care deeply about your clients."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before we dive in, what are your initial thoughts? What's your intuition telling you about this case?"
Offer Guidance, Not Answers
"Have you considered potential medical factors? Sometimes, frequent night wakings can be linked to reflux or allergies. It might be worth suggesting the parents consult with their pediatrician."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on our discussion, what adjustments do you feel comfortable making to Leo's sleep plan? Remember, you have the expertise to tailor the approach to his specific needs."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explore medical rule-outs in a case like Leo's?

Underlying medical conditions like reflux or allergies can significantly impact sleep, and addressing them is crucial for effective sleep training.

2. What is the benefit of asking the mentee for their initial thoughts on a case before offering your own suggestions?

It encourages self-reflection, promotes critical thinking, and empowers the mentee to develop their own clinical reasoning skills.
