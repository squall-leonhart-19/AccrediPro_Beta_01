Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important first step when a mentee presents a challenging case?

The most important first step is to validate their feelings and reassure them that it's okay to feel unsure. This creates a safe space for learning and growth.

2. How can you help a mentee move beyond a purely routine-based approach to sleep coaching?

Encourage them to consider the holistic picture: medical factors, developmental milestones, parental stress, and family dynamics. Help them see sleep as part of a larger system.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding, supporting, and empowering new practitioners.
  • Complex cases are learning opportunities, not signs of failure.
  • A holistic assessment considers all factors impacting a child's sleep.
  • Constructive feedback focuses on the learning process, not just the outcome.
  • Your experience and wisdom are invaluable assets for training others.
