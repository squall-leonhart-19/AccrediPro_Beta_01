Handling Objections with Confidence

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can commit to a sleep training program right now."
"I understand. Sleep training does require consistency. What specific concerns do you have about the commitment? Maybe we can address them together."
"I've tried sleep training before, and it didn't work."
"I'm sorry to hear that. What methods did you try? My program is different because [explain your unique approach]. We'll tailor the plan to your daughter's specific needs."
"It's a lot of money."
"I understand it's an investment. However, think about the long-term benefits: better sleep for your daughter, improved health and well-being for you, and a happier family. How much is that worth to you?"
"Can I think about it and get back to you?"
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? I'm happy to answer any remaining questions. Also, I have limited spots available, so I can't guarantee availability if you wait too long."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's a good way to address the "It's too expensive" objection?

Focus on the long-term value and benefits, not just the price tag. Ask what the VALUE of a good night's sleep is to them.

2. Why is it important to summarize the client's problems during the discovery call?

It shows you were actively listening and understand their needs, building trust and rapport.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections.
  • Believe in the value you offer and communicate it clearly.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice.
