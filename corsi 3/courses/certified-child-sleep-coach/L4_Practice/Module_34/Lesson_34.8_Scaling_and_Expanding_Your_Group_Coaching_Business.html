Discovery Call Script (Post-Info Session)

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Reconnect & Qualify 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for joining the "Sleep Soundly" info session! What resonated most with you?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate your child's sleep right now? What would it take to get to a 10?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the biggest sleep challenges you're facing right now. What have you already tried?"
YOU:
"How is the lack of sleep impacting your daily life and your relationship with your child?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest fears about not addressing these sleep issues?"
Phase 3: Present Group Program as Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling [summarize her pain points]. The "Sleep Soundly" group program is designed to address these exact issues. It provides a structured approach, personalized feedback, and a supportive community of parents facing similar challenges. Many parents see significant improvements within the first two weeks."
YOU:
"Unlike individual coaching, the group setting offers the benefit of shared experiences and peer support, while still providing tailored guidance to meet your child's unique needs."
YOU:
"We cover [mention 2-3 key modules from the program]. How does that sound so far?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I truly believe the "Sleep Soundly" program can help you and your child get the rest you both deserve. Are you ready to join us?"

Handling Objections with Grace

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for a group program."
"I understand your concern. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into busy schedules. Each session is recorded, so you can watch it at your convenience. Plus, the time you invest now will save you countless hours of sleep-deprived frustration in the long run. Think of it as investing in your family's well-being."
"I'm worried my child's sleep issues are too unique for a group program."
"That's a valid point. While every child is unique, the underlying principles of healthy sleep remain the same. The program provides a framework that you can adapt to your child's specific needs. Plus, you'll receive personalized feedback from me and learn from the experiences of other parents in the group. It's often easier to find solutions when you have multiple perspectives."
"It's a bit more expensive than I was expecting."
"I appreciate your honesty. Let's break down the investment. For [Price of Program], you're getting [list key deliverables - e.g., 6 weeks of live sessions, personalized feedback, access to a private community, valuable resources]. Many parents find that the program pays for itself in reduced stress, increased productivity, and improved family harmony. Plus, compared to individual coaching, it's a more affordable way to access expert guidance and support."
"Can I try one session before committing to the whole program?"
"I understand wanting to be sure. While we don't offer single-session trials, I can offer you a 48-hour money-back guarantee. If you attend the first session and don't feel it's a good fit, just let me know within 48 hours, and I'll refund your investment. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call for a group program?

Building rapport, identifying the prospect's challenges, presenting the group program as a solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you address the objection that a group program may not be suitable for a child's unique sleep issues?

Emphasize the underlying principles of healthy sleep that apply to all children, the opportunity for personalized feedback, and the benefit of learning from other parents' experiences.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a scalable way to serve more clients and increase your income.
  • A well-structured discovery call is essential for converting potential clients into group program participants.
  • Anticipate and prepare for common objections to address concerns and build trust.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value and benefits of your group program.
  • Practice your call-to-action to close the sale and encourage prospects to take the next step.
