The Five Pillars of DREAM

The DREAM Frameworkâ„¢ is composed of five interconnected phases, each crucial for achieving holistic and sustainable sleep improvements:

  1. Discover & Define: This initial phase involves gathering comprehensive information about the child's sleep history, environment, and parental practices. It's about understanding the unique challenges and needs of each family.
  2. Reframe Expectations: Here, the focus is on educating parents about realistic infant and child sleep development, debunking common myths, and aligning parental expectations with evidence-based knowledge.
  3. Establish Routines: This phase centers on creating consistent and age-appropriate bedtime routines that promote relaxation and signal to the child that it's time to sleep.
  4. Apply Strategies: This involves selecting and implementing gentle or more direct sleep training methods, tailored to the child's temperament and parental preferences.
  5. Monitor & Maintain: The final phase focuses on tracking sleep data, identifying trends, and providing ongoing support to families to troubleshoot challenges and maintain long-term sleep success.

Interlinking Elements: A Holistic Approach

The true power of the DREAM Frameworkâ„¢ lies in the interconnectedness of its elements. For instance, the data gathered during the "Discover & Define" phase directly informs the "Reframe Expectations" phase, allowing you to tailor your education to specific parental misconceptions. Similarly, the strategies applied in the "Apply Strategies" phase are directly influenced by the routines established in the "Establish Routines" phase. This holistic approach ensures that every aspect of the child's sleep is addressed, leading to more effective and sustainable results.
