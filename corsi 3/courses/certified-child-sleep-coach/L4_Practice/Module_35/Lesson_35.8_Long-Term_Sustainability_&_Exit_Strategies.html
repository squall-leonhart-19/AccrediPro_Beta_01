Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm a Certified Child Sleep Coach. I help families just like yours get the restful sleep they deserve. To start, can you tell me a little bit about [child's name] and what's been going on?"
YOU:
"I saw on your intake form that [mention something specific from the form, e.g., you've tried Ferber]. I appreciate you taking the time to share that already. What's been the hardest part of all of this for you?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"When did these sleep challenges start? What have you already tried, and what were the results? What does a typical night look like right now?"
YOU:
"I understand. It sounds incredibly draining. On a scale of 1-10, with 10 being 'completely unsustainable,' how would you rate your current sleep situation?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and solve this sleep problem, what would your life look like? What would you be able to do that you can't do now?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've told me, it sounds like you're dealing with [summarize her pain points]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because I focus on creating a personalized sleep plan that addresses the root causes of your child's sleep issues. I don't believe in one-size-fits-all solutions. I work with you over [number] weeks to provide daily support, answer your questions, and make adjustments as needed. Many of my clients see significant improvements within the first week."
YOU:
"I also provide resources on sleep hygiene, nutrition, and behavior modification. The goal is to equip you with the tools and knowledge to maintain healthy sleep habits long after we're done working together."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me tell you about my program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections with Confidence

Common Objections & Effective Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand budget is a consideration. Let's look at it this way: how much have you already spent on temporary fixes or products that haven't worked? My program is an investment in a long-term solution. Plus, I offer payment plans to make it more manageable. What feels like a comfortable investment for you?"
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. That's why I take a different approach. I don't just focus on quick fixes; I look at the root causes of the sleep issues. My program is tailored to your specific situation and includes ongoing support to ensure success. Are you willing to try a different approach that focuses on long-term solutions?"
"I need to talk to my partner about it first."
"Of course! Would it be helpful if I sent you some detailed information about the program that you can share with your partner? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions. What works best for you?"
"I'm worried it won't work for my child."
"I understand your concern. Every child is different, but my program is designed to be flexible and adaptable. I'll work closely with you to adjust the plan as needed to ensure it's the right fit for your child's unique needs. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the problem, presenting your solution, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need, ask what specifically they need to think about, and offer to provide more information or schedule a follow-up call.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare effective responses.
  • State your pricing clearly and with conviction.
  • Focus on the value you provide and the transformation you offer.
  • Remember that every "no" gets you closer to a "yes."
