Discovery Call Script

YOU:

YOU:
"Tell me more about these night wakings. How long has this been going on? What have you already tried?"

"I understand. It sounds incredibly draining. On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being completely exhausted, where are you right now?"
YOU:
"What's the biggest impact this lack of sleep is having on your life? Your relationship? Your work?"
Phase 3: Present Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I see a clear path to helping you. I specialize in gentle sleep solutions for babies like yours. My approach focuses on [mention your unique selling points - e.g., attachment-based methods, addressing root causes, parental support]."

YOU:
"Can you see how this approach could be different from what you've tried before?"
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like something you'd like to explore further? ... Great! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get you started on the path to peaceful nights."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand budget is a consideration. Let's reframe that. How much is this lack of sleep costing you in terms of lost productivity, health, and happiness? My program is an investment in your well-being, and I offer flexible payment plans to make it accessible."
"I'm worried it won't work for my baby."
"That's a valid concern. Every baby is different, and I tailor my approach to meet each child's individual needs. I have a proven track record of success with babies facing similar challenges, and I offer ongoing support throughout the program to ensure we achieve your goals. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee."
"I need to talk to my partner first."
"Of course. It's important to be on the same page. To help you have that conversation, I can send you a detailed program overview and answer any questions you both might have. Would you prefer a call with both of you or just the information?"
"I'm just so tired, I don't have the energy to commit."
"I hear you, Susan. It's tough to make decisions when you're exhausted. But think of it this way: my program is designed to *give* you energy. It's a short-term commitment for long-term results. Imagine waking up feeling refreshed and ready to enjoy your baby. That's what I want for you. I will be there to support you every step of the way."

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client enrollment is crucial for building a thriving child sleep coaching practice.

Case Study: From Teacher to Thriving Sleep Coach

Client: Maria, 48, former elementary school teacher

Presenting Problem: Maria felt nervous about sales and struggled to enroll clients. She had the coaching skills but lacked business confidence.

Intervention: Maria practiced the discovery call script, focusing on confident pricing and objection handling. She role-played with a mentor to build her sales muscles.

Outcome: Within two months, Maria doubled her client base and increased her monthly income from $2,000 to $4,500. She gained the confidence to present her services effectively and close more sales.

Case Study: Overcoming Imposter Syndrome

Client: Patricia, 52, Registered Nurse

Presenting Problem: Patricia struggled with imposter syndrome and felt uncomfortable talking about money. She was undervaluing her services and attracting clients who weren't a good fit.

Intervention: Patricia worked on her mindset, focusing on her value and expertise as a nurse and sleep coach. She used the pricing scripts and practiced confident delivery. She also raised her prices to reflect her worth.

Outcome: Patricia started attracting ideal clients who valued her expertise and were willing to invest in her services. Her income increased by 40% in three months, and she felt more confident and fulfilled in her practice.

    Crafting the Perfect Discovery Call

    Discovery Call Structure

    1. Build Rapport: Begin with a warm greeting and genuine interest in the client's situation.
    2. Understand Their Challenges: Ask open-ended questions to uncover their specific sleep-related struggles and goals.
    3. Present Your Solution: Briefly explain how your program addresses their needs and offers a personalized approach.
    4. Address Concerns: Proactively address potential objections and offer solutions.
    5. Invite Enrollment: Confidently invite them to enroll in your program and take the next step.
