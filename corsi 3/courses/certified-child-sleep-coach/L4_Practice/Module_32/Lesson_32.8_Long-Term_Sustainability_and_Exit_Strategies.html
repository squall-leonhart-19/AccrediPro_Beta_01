Susan, 42

Found you on Instagram. Has a 2-year-old who resists bedtime.

Her Situation
Exhausted, back-to-work soon, needs consistent sleep.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure if we can afford a coach right now."
Decision Style
Wants practical solutions, values experience, and needs reassurance.
Her Goal
"I want my child to sleep through the night so I can get some rest before going back to work."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call! I saw your message on Instagram, and I'm happy to chat about your little one's sleep. To start, how is your day going?"
YOU:
"Two-year-olds are a handful! Before we dive into sleep, tell me, what's your favorite thing about being a mom?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Okay, let's talk sleep. What's a typical bedtime routine like right now?"
YOU:
"And what happens after you put your child down? How many times do you get up at night?"
YOU:
"How is this lack of sleep affecting you as you prepare to return to work? On a scale of 1-10, how urgent is solving this problem for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, I specialize in helping families just like yours. Many of my clients are working moms with toddlers who resist bedtime. Is it okay if I share how I can help?"
YOU:
"My approach is gentle and focuses on creating a sustainable sleep plan that works for your family's unique needs. We'll work together over four weeks to establish a consistent bedtime routine, address any sleep regressions, and teach your child independent sleep skills. The goal is for everyone to get a good night's sleep."
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with you, based on what you've tried so far?"
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've told me, I'm confident I can help you achieve your sleep goals. Are you ready to learn more about my coaching program and how we can get started?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if my child is 'coachable' yet."
"That's a great question! My program is designed to work with children of all temperaments. We'll tailor the approach to your child's personality and developmental stage. We can start with small, manageable steps to build confidence and create positive sleep associations. Most children adapt well to a consistent routine and gentle guidance."
"I'm worried about sleep training being too harsh."
"I understand your concern. I do NOT use a 'cry it out' method. My approach is gentle and responsive. We focus on creating a supportive environment where your child feels safe and secure. We'll use techniques like gradual soothing and positive reinforcement to encourage healthy sleep habits, always prioritizing your child's emotional well-being."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. However, consider the long-term benefits of consistent sleep. Think about the cost of sleep deprivation ‚Äì lost productivity at work, increased stress and irritability, and potential health issues. My program is an investment in your family's well-being. Plus, I offer payment plans to make it more manageable."
"I need to discuss it with my partner."
"Absolutely! It's important that you're both on board. How about I send you a program summary that you can share? Also, I'm happy to schedule a quick call with both of you to answer any questions your partner might have."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the "It's too expensive" objection?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the long-term benefits and ROI, and offer payment plans or alternative options.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process can significantly increase your conversion rate.
  • Focus on understanding the client's pain points and demonstrating empathy.
  • Be prepared to address common objections with confidence and compassion.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your services and the positive outcomes clients can expect.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and call-to-action to project confidence and build trust.
