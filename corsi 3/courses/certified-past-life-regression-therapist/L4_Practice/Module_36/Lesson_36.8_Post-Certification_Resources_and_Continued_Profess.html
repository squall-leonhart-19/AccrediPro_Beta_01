    Introduction to the Discovery Call

    The discovery call is a crucial step in converting potential clients into paying clients for your past life regression practice. It's your opportunity to connect with prospects, understand their needs, and demonstrate how your services can help them achieve their goals. This lesson will provide you with the strategies and techniques to master the discovery call and increase your client conversion rate.

    Building Rapport in the First 5 Minutes

    The first few minutes of the call are critical for establishing trust and building rapport. Here are the key steps:

    1. Express Gratitude: Thank the prospect for taking the time to speak with you.
    2. Inquire How They Found You: Ask how they heard about your services. This provides valuable marketing insights.
    3. Ask About Their Interest: Inquire about their initial interest in past life regression.
    4. Set the Agenda: Briefly explain what you'll cover during the call.

    Case Study: Sarah, 34

    Sarah, a 34-year-old marketing manager, contacted a past life regression therapist after struggling with anxiety and recurring nightmares. During the initial discovery call, the therapist began by thanking Sarah for her time and asked how she found her practice. Sarah explained that she had read a blog post about past life regression and its potential to alleviate anxiety. The therapist then inquired about Sarah's specific challenges and what she hoped to achieve through regression therapy. By actively listening and showing genuine interest, the therapist built a strong rapport with Sarah, which ultimately led to her enrolling in a 10-week program.

    Active Listening and Needs Assessment

    Active listening is essential for understanding the prospect's needs and tailoring your approach. Here are some tips:

    • Pay Attention: Focus on what the prospect is saying without interrupting.
    • Show That You're Listening: Use verbal and non-verbal cues to indicate your engagement.
    • Provide Feedback: Paraphrase and summarize their points to ensure understanding.
    • Defer Judgment: Avoid interrupting or imposing your own opinions.
    • Respond Appropriately: Offer thoughtful and relevant responses.

    Ask open-ended questions to encourage the prospect to share their challenges and goals. For example:

    • "What are the biggest challenges you're currently facing in your life?"
    • "What do you hope to achieve through past life regression?"
    • "What have you tried in the past to address these issues?"
