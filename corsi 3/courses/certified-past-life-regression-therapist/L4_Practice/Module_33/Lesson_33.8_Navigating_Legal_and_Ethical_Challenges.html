Intake Call Script: Legal & Compliance Focus

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Introduction & Legal Disclosures 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. Before we discuss your needs, I want to briefly cover some legal and ethical considerations. My role as a Past Life Regression Therapist is [explain your credentials and scope of practice]. This is not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment, and I don't diagnose or treat medical conditions."
YOU:
"I also want to assure you that everything we discuss is confidential, within the limits of the law. I am required to report if you disclose any intent to harm yourself or others, or any instances of child abuse. Do you understand and agree to these terms?"
Phase 2: Data Privacy & Consent 5 min
YOU:
"I want to be transparent about how I handle your data. I use [mention your secure software] to store your information, and it's protected by [mention security measures, e.g., encryption]. I will only use your information to provide you with the best possible service, and I will never share it with third parties without your explicit consent."
YOU:
"I will also send you a consent form outlining these details. Please review and sign it before our first session. Do you have any questions about this?"
Phase 3: Understanding Susan's Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Now that we've covered the legal aspects, can you tell me more about what brings you here? I understand you're dealing with anxiety and relationship issues."
YOU:
"How long have you been experiencing this, and what have you tried so far?"
YOU:
"What are your goals for therapy? What would you like to achieve?"
Phase 4: Explaining Your Approach & Setting Expectations 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Past Life Regression might be helpful. It's a process where we explore past experiences to gain insights and heal current issues. It's not a guarantee of specific outcomes, but many clients find it transformative."
YOU:
"I want to emphasize that this is a collaborative process. Your active participation is essential for success. Are you comfortable with this approach?"
Phase 5: Next Steps & Closing 5 min
YOU:
"If you feel this is a good fit, I'll send you the consent form and intake questionnaire. Once those are completed, we can schedule your first session. Does that sound good?"

Handling Objections: Legal & Ethical Concerns

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm worried about the confidentiality of my information."
"I understand your concern. I take confidentiality very seriously. All your information is stored securely, and I adhere to strict ethical guidelines. I'm happy to answer any specific questions you have about my privacy practices. You'll also receive a detailed confidentiality agreement."
"What if I uncover something traumatic in a past life?"
"That's a valid concern. I'm trained to handle sensitive material with care. We will proceed at your pace, and I'll provide support and grounding techniques throughout the process. If necessary, I can also refer you to a licensed therapist for additional support."
"Is Past Life Regression scientifically proven?"
"Past Life Regression is considered a complementary therapy. While there's not extensive scientific evidence, many people report significant benefits. I always encourage clients to maintain their relationships with their medical and mental health providers and view this as a supportive, not replacement, therapy."
"I'm not sure if this is 'real' or just my imagination."
"That's a common question. Whether the memories are literal or symbolic, they can still provide valuable insights into your current challenges. The goal is to use these experiences to facilitate healing and growth, regardless of their origin."

You're Ready to Onboard Clients Legally & Ethically!

Mastering the legal and compliance aspects of your practice builds trust and protects both you and your clients. Practice these scripts, and you'll be well-prepared to onboard new clients with confidence.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to discuss legal and ethical considerations during the initial intake call?

It establishes clear boundaries, ensures informed consent, and builds trust with the client. It also protects you legally and ethically.

2. What are some key elements to include in a consent form for Past Life Regression?

Scope of practice, confidentiality, data privacy, potential risks and benefits, client's right to withdraw, and a disclaimer that it is not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment.

Key Takeaways

  • Always prioritize informed consent and ethical practice.
  • Clearly communicate your scope of practice and limitations.
  • Address client concerns about confidentiality and data privacy.
  • Use secure systems to protect client information.
  • Remember that building a legally sound and ethical practice protects you and your clients.
