Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation to target the issue. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing this with me. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings. Before we explore solutions, let's clarify what 'inadequacy' means to you in the context of your teaching career. Can you give me a specific example of when you felt this most recently?"
MARIA:
"Well, just last week, I had a parent-teacher conference, and I felt like I wasn't explaining the curriculum well enough. I worried they thought I was incompetent."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. So, the feeling of inadequacy is tied to a specific situation ‚Äì communicating your teaching methods to parents. Is that accurate?"
MARIA:
"Yes, I think so. It's not just that one instance, though. It's a pattern. I often feel like I'm not meeting expectations."
YOU:
"I understand. So, let's explore the root of this pattern. If you were to imagine a younger version of yourself, perhaps a child or teenager, experiencing this same feeling of not meeting expectations, what comes to mind?"
YOU:
"Take your time with this. There's no right or wrong answer. We're simply trying to understand where this feeling originated."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to get a specific example of the client's chief complaint?

Getting a specific example helps to ground the issue and makes it easier to explore potential root causes. It moves the conversation from abstract feelings to concrete situations.

2. What's the risk of immediately jumping into past life regression without properly targeting the issue?

Jumping in too quickly can lead to unfocused sessions and may not address the client's actual needs. Targeting the issue first ensures the regression is relevant and impactful.

Key Takeaways

  • Always start by validating the client's feelings and creating a safe space.
  • Obtain a specific example of the chief complaint to ground the issue.
  • Clarify the client's goals and expectations for the session.
  • Avoid leading questions and allow the client to guide the exploration.
  • Ensure the client is ready and willing to explore past lives before initiating regression.
