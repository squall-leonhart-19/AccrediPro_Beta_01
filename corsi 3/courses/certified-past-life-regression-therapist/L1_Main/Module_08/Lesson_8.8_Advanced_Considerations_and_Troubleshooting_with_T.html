Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you so much for sharing your story with me today. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings of being 'stuck'. What resonates most strongly with you about exploring past lives as a way to understand your current situation?"
DIANE:
"Well, I've tried therapy, but it only goes so far. I feel like there's something deeper driving these patterns, something I can't quite put my finger on."
YOU:
"That's a very common feeling, Diane. Many people find that past life regression can offer a unique perspective, helping to uncover the root causes of these persistent patterns. Are you open to the possibility that these patterns might stem from experiences beyond this lifetime?"
DIANE:
"I'm definitely open. I mean, I wouldn't be here if I wasn't! I'm just a little nervous about what I might find."
YOU:
"That's perfectly normal. Know that you are always in control, and I'll be here to guide you every step of the way. Before we begin, let's set a clear intention for our session. What is the one thing you most hope to gain from this experience?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's nervousness before beginning a past life regression?

Validating their nervousness helps build trust and rapport, assuring the client that their feelings are normal and that you are a supportive guide.

2. What is the primary reason for setting a clear intention at the beginning of a session?

Setting a clear intention helps focus the session, providing a specific goal for the client to work towards and ensuring that the regression is purposeful.

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate the client's feelings before proceeding.
  • Building rapport is crucial for a successful session.
  • Use clear and simple language to avoid confusion.
  • Setting a clear intention provides focus and purpose.
  • Remember that you are a guide, empowering the client to explore their own experiences.
