Former social worker, empathetic, strong theoretical knowledge but limited practical experience.

Excellent listening skills, compassionate, dedicated to client well-being.

Managing intense abreactions, setting boundaries, self-doubt in complex cases.
Her Question
"My client had a major emotional release during a session. I'm not sure if I handled it correctly. Can we review the session?"

The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I worked with Sarah, 52, who wanted to explore feelings of unworthiness. During the regression, she started screaming and crying, saying she was being punished in a past life. It was so intense, I froze. I eventually guided her back to the present, but I felt completely out of my depth."

Key Questions for Olivia:

  • What specific techniques did you use to ground Sarah after the abreaction?
  • Did you explore Sarah's history of trauma or mental health conditions during the intake?
  • What resources did you provide Sarah for aftercare and emotional support?
  • How did you manage your own emotional response during and after the session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Abreaction Management

Emphasize the importance of staying grounded and calm during intense emotional releases. Teach techniques like paced breathing, visualization, and anchoring to the present moment.

2

Trauma-Informed Approach

Highlight the need for thorough intake assessments to identify potential trauma history. Discuss contraindications and modifications for clients with complex trauma.

3

Scope of Practice & Referrals

Clarify the boundaries of past life regression therapy. Explain when it's necessary to refer clients to licensed mental health professionals for additional support.

4

Self-Care for Therapists

Stress the importance of debriefing after intense sessions and engaging in self-care practices to prevent burnout and vicarious trauma.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge and Validate
"Olivia, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client has such a strong reaction. It shows you care deeply. Let's unpack this together."
Inquire About Her Experience
"Before we dive into techniques, can you tell me more about what was going through your mind during the session? What did you notice in your body?"
Offer Guidance and Education
"When a client experiences a powerful abreaction, it's crucial to have grounding techniques ready. Something as simple as focusing on the breath or describing the room can help bring them back to the present."
Empower Her Next Steps
"What steps can you take now to support Sarah and yourself? Consider a follow-up session with a focus on grounding, and explore some self-care strategies for processing the session."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three essential elements of providing effective supervision to a new past life regression therapist?

Creating a safe space, offering trauma-informed guidance, and reinforcing ethical boundaries.

2. How can you help a mentee navigate the emotional challenges of working with clients who experience intense abreactions?

By sharing grounding techniques, normalizing their feelings, and encouraging self-care strategies.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a crucial component of professional development for new practitioners.
  • Creating a safe and supportive environment is essential for effective mentoring.
  • Trauma-informed guidance and ethical considerations are paramount in past life regression therapy.
  • Mentors play a vital role in helping mentees build confidence and navigate complex cases.
  • Self-care is essential for both therapists and mentors to prevent burnout and vicarious trauma.
