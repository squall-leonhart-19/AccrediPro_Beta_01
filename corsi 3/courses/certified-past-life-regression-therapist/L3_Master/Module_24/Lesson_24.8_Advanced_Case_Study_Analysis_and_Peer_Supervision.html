The Case Olivia Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a new client, Sarah, 52, who mentioned in her intake form that she experienced childhood trauma. I'm nervous about triggering something or not being able to handle it. Should I refer her to someone else?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • What is Olivia's understanding of trauma-informed care?
  • What specific aspects of the client's history are making her anxious?
  • Does Olivia have a support system in place if she needs guidance?
  • What resources can Olivia utilize to enhance her skills?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Acknowledge and Normalize Her Concerns

"It's completely normal to feel apprehensive when working with clients who have experienced trauma. It shows you care and are taking the responsibility seriously."

2

Discuss Trauma-Informed Practices

Emphasize the importance of creating a safe and supportive environment, obtaining informed consent, and respecting the client's boundaries. Trauma-informed care is about minimizing the risk of re-traumatization.

3

Review Scope of Practice

Clarify that as a Past Life Regression Therapist, Olivia is not a trauma therapist. Her role is to facilitate exploration and healing within the past life realm, not to provide intensive trauma processing. Reassure her that referral is always an option and a sign of professional responsibility.

4

Provide Resources and Support

Suggest books, articles, or workshops on trauma-informed care. Remind her that she can always reach out for supervision and consultation. Building a support network is crucial.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Olivia, I commend you for recognizing the potential complexity of this case. It's a sign of your ethical awareness and commitment to your client's well-being."
Explore Her Reasoning
"What specific concerns do you have about working with Sarah? What aspects of her history are making you feel uneasy?"
Offer Guidance and Support
"Remember, you don't have to do this alone. I'm here to support you. We can discuss strategies for creating a safe and supportive environment for Sarah. We can also explore referral options if that feels like the best course of action."
Empower Her Decision
"Ultimately, the decision is yours. Trust your intuition. If you feel you're not the right fit for Sarah, it's okay to refer her to someone who is better equipped to meet her needs. What feels right to you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of trauma-informed care in the context of past life regression?

The primary goal is to minimize the risk of re-traumatization by creating a safe, supportive, and predictable environment for the client.

2. When is it appropriate for a Past Life Regression Therapist to refer a client to a trauma specialist?

It's appropriate to refer a client when the therapist feels unequipped to handle the complexity of the client's trauma history, or when the client's needs exceed the therapist's scope of practice.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about guiding and supporting new practitioners, not doing the work for them.
  • Acknowledge and validate their concerns to build trust and rapport.
  • Provide practical tools, resources, and ethical guidance.
  • Encourage them to trust their intuition and seek support when needed.
  • Remember, you are helping them grow into confident and ethical practitioners.
