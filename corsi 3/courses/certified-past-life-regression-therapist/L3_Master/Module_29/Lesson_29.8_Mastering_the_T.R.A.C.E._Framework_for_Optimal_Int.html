Olivia, New L1 Graduate

Eager to start, but feeling overwhelmed with information and client responsibility.

Background
Former elementary school teacher, excellent at creating a safe space, drawn to past life work after her own transformative experience.
Strengths
Strong intuition, compassionate listener, excellent at building rapport with clients.
Growth Areas
Needs help with session structure, struggles with setting boundaries, and managing client expectations.
Her Question
"I had a client burst into tears during our session, and I froze. How do I handle intense emotions?"

The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Scenario

What Olivia Tells You:

"I was working with Sarah, 52, who wanted to explore recurring feelings of anxiety. During the regression, Sarah started sobbing uncontrollably when she described being separated from her family in what felt like a war-torn country. I didn't know what to do! I just sat there and held space, but I felt so helpless and worried I was doing something wrong."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Was Olivia grounded and present during the intense emotional release?
  • Did she have a plan for containment and re-regulation if the client became overwhelmed?
  • Did she debrief with the client after the emotional release, or simply end the session?
  • Did she explore Sarah's resources and coping mechanisms?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Normalize Emotional Release

Explain that intense emotional release is a common occurrence in past life regression. It's not a sign of failure, but rather an indication that deep healing is taking place.

2

Teach Grounding Techniques

Review grounding techniques such as deep breathing, visualization, and body scans that Olivia can use to stay centered and help her client regulate their emotions.

3

Discuss Containment Strategies

Share strategies for containing intense emotions, such as using a "safe word" or visualization to create a protective bubble around the client.

4

Emphasize the Importance of Debriefing

Highlight the importance of debriefing with the client after an emotional release to process the experience, integrate the insights, and ensure they feel safe and supported.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Feelings
"Olivia, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client experiences intense emotions. It shows you care deeply about their well-being."
Validate Her Intuition
"Holding space is a valuable skill. What did you notice about Sarah's body language or energy during the release?"
Introduce New Tools
"Next time, consider using a grounding technique like 'roots to the earth' or offering a comforting object like a blanket. You can also suggest a 'safe word' if things get too intense."
Empower Her Confidence
"Remember, you're not responsible for 'fixing' their emotions. Your role is to create a safe space for them to explore and process their experiences. You've got this!"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing supervision to a new past life regression practitioner?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they can learn, grow, and develop their skills while building confidence.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with setting boundaries with clients?

Role-play different scenarios, provide scripts for difficult conversations, and help them identify their own personal boundaries.
