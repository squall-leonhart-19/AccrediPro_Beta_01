The Case Susan Presents

Susan's Client Case

What Susan Tells You:

"I had a client, Emily, 52, who wanted to explore the root of her anxiety. During the regression, she suddenly started sobbing and hyperventilating. I felt panicked and didn't know what to do. I ended the session early, but I'm worried I didn't handle it well."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Susan have a clear protocol for managing abreactions?
  • Did she maintain a calm presence and reassure the client?
  • Did she debrief Emily and provide grounding techniques?
  • Did she process her own emotional response after the session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Susan

1

Abreaction Management

Explain the importance of having a clear, pre-established protocol for managing strong emotional releases. Review techniques like deep breathing, grounding, and safe-place visualizations. Refer to the course materials on abreaction management.

2

Maintaining Calm Presence

Emphasize the importance of the therapist's calm presence during an abreaction. Explain that the client will mirror the therapist's emotional state. Practice staying grounded and centered even when the client is highly emotional.

3

Debriefing and Grounding

Discuss the importance of debriefing the client after an abreaction. Review grounding techniques like the 5-4-3-2-1 method and explain how to help the client integrate the experience. Ensure the client feels safe and supported before leaving.

4

Therapist Self-Care

Highlight the need for self-care after a session with a strong emotional release. Encourage Susan to process her own feelings, seek support from peers, and practice grounding techniques. Remind her that vicarious trauma is a real possibility.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Validate Her Experience
"Susan, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed when a client has a strong reaction. It shows you care deeply. Let's break down what happened and how you can feel more prepared next time."
Ask About Her Response
"Before I share my thoughts, what did you notice in your own body when Emily started to sob? Where did you feel the panic? Understanding your own reactions is key."
Offer Specific Techniques
"One technique I find helpful is to imagine roots growing from my feet into the earth. It helps me stay grounded and present even when things get intense. You can also try silently repeating a mantra like 'I am safe, she is safe'."
Empower Her Future Actions
"What specific steps will you take to prepare for the possibility of abreactions in future sessions? How will you practice staying calm and grounded? Let's create a plan together."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements of an abreaction management protocol that you would discuss with your mentee?

1. Pre-session preparation (identifying potential triggers), 2. During-session techniques (grounding, reassurance), 3. Post-session debriefing and support.

2. Why is it important for a therapist to have a self-care plan, especially when working with clients who experience trauma?

To prevent vicarious trauma, burnout, and maintain their own emotional well-being, allowing them to provide the best possible care for their clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision involves validating the mentee's experiences and normalizing their anxieties.
  • Abreaction management protocols are crucial for handling strong emotional releases in clients.
  • Maintaining a calm presence is essential for the therapist's and client's well-being.
  • Therapist self-care is paramount to prevent burnout and vicarious trauma.
  • Empowering the mentee to develop their own strategies fosters independence and confidence.
