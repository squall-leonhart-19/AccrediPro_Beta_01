The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 48, recently diagnosed with Hashimoto's. She's experiencing fatigue, weight gain, and brain fog. Her TSH is slightly elevated (3.8), TPO antibodies are high (450), and she also has elevated reverse T3. I recommended a gluten-free diet and selenium, but I'm worried I'm missing something."

Areas to Explore with Aisha:

  • Has Aisha considered other factors influencing reverse T3, such as stress or inflammation?
  • Did she assess Sarah's iron levels, as low iron can impair thyroid hormone conversion?
  • Is Aisha aware of the potential need for thyroid medication, and the importance of working collaboratively with Sarah's physician?
  • Is she aware of the scope of practice considerations when interpreting complex lab results?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Prioritization is Key

Explain the importance of addressing the root cause. With Hashimoto's, focus on inflammation and immune modulation before solely addressing thyroid hormone levels.

2

Deep Dive into Reverse T3

Discuss factors that can elevate reverse T3, such as chronic stress, inflammation, and nutrient deficiencies. Explain the role of inflammation in thyroid hormone conversion.

3

Nutrient Assessment

Emphasize the importance of assessing key nutrients like iron, selenium, zinc, and vitamin D, as they are crucial for thyroid hormone production and conversion. Review the link between nutrient deficiencies and thyroid dysfunction.

4

Scope of Practice & Collaboration

Remind Aisha of the importance of working within her scope of practice and collaborating with Sarah's physician. Reinforce that you cannot prescribe or advise on medication changes.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address the root cause of Hashimoto's before solely focusing on thyroid hormone levels?

Addressing the root cause, such as inflammation and immune dysregulation, can have a more significant and lasting impact on overall thyroid health. Directly targeting thyroid hormone levels without addressing the underlying issues may only provide temporary relief and not resolve the autoimmune process driving Hashimoto's.

2. How can you guide your mentee to work within her scope of practice when dealing with a client with Hashimoto's?

Remind her that her role is to support the client holistically through nutrition, lifestyle modifications, and stress management. Emphasize that she cannot diagnose or treat medical conditions, nor can she advise on medication. Encourage her to collaborate with the client's physician and refer back to them for medical management.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves guiding new practitioners to think critically and prioritize interventions.
  • Understanding the factors influencing reverse T3 is crucial for managing Hashimoto's.
  • Nutrient assessment is essential for supporting thyroid hormone production and conversion.
  • Always reinforce scope of practice and encourage collaboration with other healthcare professionals.
  • Your experience and guidance are invaluable in shaping the next generation of thyroid health specialists.
