Discovery Call Script: Your 30-Minute Roadmap

Your Client Acquisition Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Emily, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about what you're experiencing. To start, what resonated with you about my services that made you book this call?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! I understand [mention something specific from her response]. Before we dive deeper, tell me a bit more about what a typical day looks like for you."
Phase 2: Problem Exploration 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe the brain fog in more detail? When is it most intense, and how does it impact your work and personal life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address your Hashimoto's symptoms? What worked temporarily, and what didn't work at all?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate these symptoms, what would your life look like in six months? How would it impact your career, relationships, and overall happiness?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Emily, based on our conversation, it sounds like you're struggling with brain fog, low energy, and difficulty focusing due to your Hashimoto's, and you're looking for a sustainable solution that goes beyond temporary fixes. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because I focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of your symptoms, not just managing them. We'll use advanced lab testing to uncover hidden imbalances, create a personalized nutrition and lifestyle plan, and provide ongoing support to ensure you achieve lasting results. Many clients experience significant improvements in their energy and focus within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"For example, a 2023 study in the Journal of Thyroid Research found that personalized nutrition plans based on individual lab results led to a 40% reduction in Hashimoto's symptoms compared to standard dietary recommendations."
Phase 4: The Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Emily, does this approach resonate with you and align with what you've been searching for? ... Excellent! Let me share the details of my program and how we can get started on your journey to renewed energy and focus."

Objection Handling: Addressing Concerns with Confidence

Overcoming Common Objections

"I need to think about it."
"I understand completely, Emily. It's a big decision. Just to clarify, is there anything specific you need to think about? Knowing that will help me provide you with the right information, so you can feel confident in your decision."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Many people initially feel that way. However, consider the long-term costs of continuing to struggle with your symptoms ‚Äì lost productivity, expensive supplements that don't work, and the impact on your overall quality of life. My program is an investment in your health and well-being, and most clients find the ROI far outweighs the initial cost."
"I need to ask my husband."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Would it be helpful if I sent you a detailed program overview that you can share with him? Alternatively, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions he may have. Which option would be most helpful?"
"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand that time is a valuable resource. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy schedule. We'll work together to prioritize and implement changes that will have the biggest impact on your health, without overwhelming you. Plus, think about the time you'll save when you're no longer struggling with brain fog and low energy!"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Rapport & Connection, Problem Exploration, Presenting Your Approach, and The Confident Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the program as an investment in their long-term health and well-being, highlighting the potential ROI.

3. What is the purpose of a call-to-action in the discovery call?

The call-to-action is designed to confidently guide the prospect towards enrolling in your program and taking the next step in their health journey.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for client acquisition and building a thriving practice.
  • Emphasize personalized solutions and address the root causes of client symptoms to showcase your unique value.
  • Confidently handle objections by validating concerns and reframing your program as a valuable investment.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines to increase your conversion rates.
  • Remember, your success is achievable with consistent effort and a genuine commitment to helping your clients.
