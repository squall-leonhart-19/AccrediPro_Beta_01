Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connection 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! I'm so glad you reached out about the Thriving Thyroid program. First, tell me a little about yourself. What sparked your interest in this program?"
YOU:
"I understand you have Hashimoto's. I work with so many women with Hashimoto's, and I know it can be isolating. What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing right now?"
Phase 2: Understand Her Needs & Desires 8 min
YOU:
"You mentioned brain fog and low energy. How are those symptoms impacting your daily life? What are you missing out on?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage your Hashimoto's? What has worked, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine you could wave a magic wand and feel like your old self again. What would that look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program as the Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for community, support, and practical strategies to manage your Hashimoto's naturally. Is that right?"
YOU:
"The Thriving Thyroid program is designed to provide exactly that. It's a 12-week program where you'll connect with other women who understand what you're going through, learn evidence-based strategies for managing your symptoms, and receive personalized support from me. We focus on nutrition, lifestyle, and mindset shifts."
YOU:
"In the group, we celebrate small wins, share recipes, and support each other through tough days. It's not just about the information; it's about the transformation that happens within a supportive community."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you see yourself benefiting from this program? ... Great! Let's talk about the investment and how you can join us."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for a group program."
"I understand. Time is valuable. The program is designed to be flexible. The weekly calls are recorded, and you can access the materials anytime. Plus, the time you invest now can save you time and energy in the long run by helping you manage your symptoms more effectively. Think of the time you'll gain back when you're not struggling with brain fog and fatigue."
"I'm on a fixed income, so I'm worried about the cost."
"I understand the concern. Let's look at it this way: how much are you currently spending on supplements, doctor visits, and other things to manage your Hashimoto's? This program can help you reduce those expenses by teaching you how to manage your symptoms naturally. We also have a payment plan available to make it more accessible. Think of it as an investment in your long-term health and well-being."
"I'm not sure if a group setting is right for me."
"That's a valid concern. Some women are hesitant about group programs at first. But what I've found is that the community aspect is one of the most valuable parts of the program. You'll connect with other women who understand what you're going through, share your experiences, and receive support and encouragement. It's a safe and confidential space where you can be yourself and learn from others. Plus, you'll still receive personalized support from me throughout the program."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Maybe I can answer a few more questions now to help you decide."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to highlight when presenting the value of a group program?

The key elements include community support, evidence-based strategies, personalized attention, and the potential for long-term symptom management and improved quality of life.

2. How can you address the objection of a potential client being concerned about the time commitment of a group program?

Emphasize the flexibility of the program (recorded calls, access to materials anytime), and highlight the potential time saved in the long run through effective symptom management and improved energy levels.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a powerful way to support clients and create a thriving practice.
  • Building rapport and understanding your prospect's needs are crucial for successful enrollment.
  • Address objections with empathy and highlight the unique value of your program.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and call-to-action to increase your confidence.
  • Remember, you're offering a community and a transformation, not just a program.
