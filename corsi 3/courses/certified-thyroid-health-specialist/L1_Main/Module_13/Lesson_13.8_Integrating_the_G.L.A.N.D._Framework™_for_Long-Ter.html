Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation about thyroid hormone conversion. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your health history and lab results with me. I understand you're still experiencing hypothyroid symptoms even though your TSH is within the normal range. This is something I see often, and it tells me we need to dig a little deeper into how your body is converting T4 into T3."
MARIA:
"Converting? My doctor never mentioned anything about that. She just keeps saying my TSH is fine."
YOU:
"That's a common perspective, and it's why so many women continue to struggle. While TSH is important, it's not the whole story. Think of it this way: your body needs T3 to function properly. Levothyroxine is T4, which is a precursor to T3. Your body has to convert it."
YOU:
"Your labs show that you have elevated reverse T3. Reverse T3 is like a 'brake' on your thyroid function. It blocks T3 from binding to your cells. So, even if you're taking T4, if your body is producing too much reverse T3, you won't feel better."
MARIA:
"Okay, that makes sense. So what causes the high reverse T3?"
YOU:
"Several factors can contribute. Based on your health history, I suspect stress is playing a significant role. Stress can impair the enzyme needed to convert T4 to T3, and it can increase the production of reverse T3. Inflammation, gut health, and certain nutrient deficiencies can also be factors."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address thyroid hormone conversion, even if TSH is "normal?"

Because TSH doesn't tell the whole story. The body needs T3 to function properly, and if T4 isn't being converted effectively, symptoms can persist despite a normal TSH.

2. What is reverse T3 and how does it impact thyroid function?

Reverse T3 is an inactive form of T3 that blocks T3 receptors. High levels of reverse T3 can prevent T3 from binding to cells, leading to hypothyroid symptoms.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective thyroid coaching goes beyond TSH and considers T4 to T3 conversion.
  • Elevated reverse T3 can be a significant barrier to feeling well, even with medication.
  • Stress, inflammation, and nutrient deficiencies can all impair thyroid hormone conversion.
  • Empathy and clear communication are crucial for empowering clients to understand their thyroid health.
