Persistent Fatigue & Weight Gain

Sarah, a 48-year-old woman, presented with persistent fatigue, unexplained weight gain, and difficulty concentrating. Her TSH was within the "normal" range, according to her primary care physician, who dismissed her concerns.

Intervention: Using the principles learned in this module, you conduct a more comprehensive thyroid panel, including Free T3,Free T4, Reverse T3, and thyroid antibodies. You also assess her adrenal function through a salivary cortisol test.

Findings: Sarah's Free T3 was low, her Reverse T3 was elevated, and she had elevated thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibodies. Her cortisol levels were also dysregulated, with high cortisol in the morning and low cortisol in the evening.

Plan: You implement a multi-faceted approach, including:

  • Nutritional support with selenium, zinc, and iron.
  • Adrenal support with adaptogenic herbs like ashwagandha.
  • Lifestyle modifications to reduce stress and improve sleep.

Outcome: Within three months, Sarah reported significant improvements in her energy levels, weight management, and cognitive function. Her Free T3 increased, her Reverse T3 decreased, and her cortisol levels normalized.

This case study highlights the importance of looking beyond TSH and addressing the underlying factors contributing to thyroid dysfunction.
