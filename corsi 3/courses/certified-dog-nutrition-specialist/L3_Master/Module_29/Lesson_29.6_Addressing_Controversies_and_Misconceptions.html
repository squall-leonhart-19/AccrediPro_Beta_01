Common Misconceptions about Raw Feeding

Raw feeding, while gaining popularity, is often met with skepticism. Letâ€™s address some common misconceptions:

  • Bacterial Contamination: The fear of Salmonella or E. coli is prevalent. However, dogs have shorter digestive tracts and more acidic stomachs than humans, making them more resistant to these bacteria. Proper handling and sourcing of ingredients are crucial.
  • Nutritional Imbalances: Some believe raw diets are inherently unbalanced. The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ ensures appropriate ratios of meat, bone, organs, and plant matter, preventing deficiencies.
  • Safety Issues: Concerns about choking on bones are common. Feeding appropriate-sized, raw bones minimizes this risk. Avoid cooked bones, as they can splinter.

Addressing Concerns about Bone Feeding

Bone feeding is a cornerstone of the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢, providing essential minerals and dental benefits. However, safety is paramount:

  • Appropriate Size: Choose bones that are too large to be swallowed whole.
  • Raw Only: Never feed cooked bones, as they can splinter and cause internal damage.
  • Supervision: Always supervise your dog while they are chewing on a bone.
  • Introduction: Introduce bones gradually to avoid digestive upset.
