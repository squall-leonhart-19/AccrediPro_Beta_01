The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Max, a 7-year-old Labrador with a history of allergies and recurring ear infections. He's currently on a commercial kibble, and his owner wants to switch him to a raw diet. I'm worried about triggering his allergies and ensuring he gets all the nutrients he needs. Where do I even start?"

Key Considerations:

  • Allergy assessment: Have all potential allergens been identified?
  • Gut health: Is Max's gut health optimized to handle the diet transition?
  • Nutrient balance: How will you ensure complete and balanced nutrition on a raw diet, given his allergies?
  • Owner compliance: Is the owner fully committed and educated on safe raw feeding practices?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize a Systematic Approach

Guide Sarah to break down the case into manageable steps: allergy assessment, gut health optimization, diet formulation, and owner education.

2

Review Allergy Management Strategies

Discuss hypoallergenic raw diet options, novel protein sources, and the importance of food trials. Remind her of the BARF principles from Level 1.

3

Address Gut Health Concerns

Explain the role of prebiotics, probiotics, and digestive enzymes in supporting gut health during the transition. Discuss potential contraindications.

4

Promote Confidence in Pricing

Help Sarah understand her value and set appropriate prices for her services, considering her expertise and the time commitment involved.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, I can see you've put a lot of thought into this case. It's great that you're being cautious and considering all the angles. That shows you care deeply about your clients."
Guide Her Thought Process
"Let's start with the allergies. What are your initial thoughts on how to identify and manage potential allergens in Max's case? What assessment tools do you have available to you?"
Offer Practical Solutions
"Have you considered an elimination diet using novel proteins like rabbit or venison? This can help pinpoint specific allergens while providing essential nutrients. Remember, always prioritize safety and a slow transition."
Build Her Confidence
"You have a strong foundation in animal health, Sarah. Trust your instincts and remember the principles we've learned. You've got this! And I'm here to support you every step of the way."

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves active listening, empathy, and understanding the mentee's perspective.
  • Guide mentees to break down complex cases into manageable steps for systematic problem-solving.
  • Provide specific, actionable advice based on established principles and best practices.
  • Encourage critical thinking and empower mentees to develop their own clinical reasoning skills.
  • Celebrate successes and provide constructive feedback to foster confidence and growth.
