The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Buster, a 7-year-old Lab with skin allergies and recurring ear infections. I recommended a raw diet with specific ratios of muscle meat, bone, and organs. I also suggested some supplements. The owner is now saying Buster has diarrhea. What did I miss?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Was the transition to raw done gradually?
  • Were the bone ratios appropriate for Buster's digestive system?
  • Could the supplements be contributing to the diarrhea?
  • Did Aisha consider underlying sensitivities or intolerances?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize Gradual Transition

Explain that a sudden shift to raw can overwhelm a dog's digestive system. A gradual transition over several days or weeks is crucial.

2

Review Bone Ratios

Discuss the importance of appropriate bone ratios. Too much bone can lead to constipation, while too little can cause diarrhea. Explain how to adjust based on stool consistency.

3

Consider Supplement Sensitivities

Remind Aisha that some dogs are sensitive to certain supplements. It's important to introduce them one at a time and monitor for reactions. A 2018 study in the Journal of Animal Physiology and Animal Nutrition found that up to 15% of dogs experience digestive upset from new supplements.

4

Encourage Further Investigation

Guide Aisha to ask the client about any other dietary changes or potential stressors. Suggest considering underlying sensitivities or intolerances that might be contributing to the diarrhea.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Encouragement
"Aisha, it's fantastic that you're proactively seeking guidance! This shows you're committed to providing the best care for your clients. Let's break this down together."
Ask for Her Initial Thoughts
"What are your initial thoughts about why Buster might be experiencing diarrhea? What part of your recommendations are you questioning the most?"
Provide Targeted Education
"One common cause of diarrhea when transitioning to raw is the bone ratio. Too much bone can definitely cause digestive upset. It's also important to transition slowly, especially for dogs with sensitive systems."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"What adjustments do you think would be appropriate to recommend to the client? I'm here to support you, but I want you to feel confident in your ability to assess and adjust the plan."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a gradual transition to a raw diet important, especially for dogs with sensitive systems?

A gradual transition allows the dog's digestive system to adapt to the new food, reducing the risk of digestive upset such as diarrhea or vomiting. It's particularly important for dogs with sensitive systems, as they are more prone to adverse reactions.

2. What are some strategies for addressing a mentee's lack of confidence in their recommendations?

Provide positive reinforcement for their knowledge and skills, encourage them to trust their intuition, and offer guidance and support without taking over the decision-making process. Reviewing successful cases and celebrating small wins can also boost their confidence.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is a powerful way to share your expertise and contribute to the growth of other practitioners.
  • Constructive feedback should be specific, actionable, and delivered with empathy and encouragement.
  • Emphasize the importance of gradual transitions and appropriate ratios when recommending raw diets.
  • Help mentees develop their critical thinking skills by asking questions and guiding them to find solutions.
