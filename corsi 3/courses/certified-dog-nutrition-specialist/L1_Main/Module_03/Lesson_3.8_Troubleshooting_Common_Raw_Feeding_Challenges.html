Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Hi Carol, thanks for reaching out! I understand you're curious about raw feeding for Buddy but also feeling a bit overwhelmed. That's completely normal! Many people feel the same way when they first start exploring raw diets."
CAROL:
"Yes, exactly! I've read so much conflicting information online, and I'm worried about making him sick."
YOU:
"I hear you. The internet can be a scary place when it comes to pet nutrition! My role is to help you sift through the noise and create a safe, balanced, and practical plan for Buddy. We‚Äôll take it one step at a time."
YOU:
"To start, let's talk about your concerns. What are the biggest things holding you back from making the switch?"
CAROL:
"Well, the bacteria thing is huge. And I'm not sure I can balance everything correctly. I don't want him to be deficient in something."
YOU:
"Those are excellent and valid concerns, Carol. We'll address both of those directly. I'll share some simple strategies to minimize bacterial risks and ensure Buddy gets a complete and balanced diet, without requiring you to become a canine nutritionist overnight! How does that sound?"

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge and validate the client's fears about raw feeding.
  • Use simple, easy-to-understand language, avoiding technical jargon.
  • Offer practical solutions and strategies to address their concerns.
  • Emphasize that you'll guide them step-by-step through the process.
  • Set realistic expectations for the transition and potential benefits.
