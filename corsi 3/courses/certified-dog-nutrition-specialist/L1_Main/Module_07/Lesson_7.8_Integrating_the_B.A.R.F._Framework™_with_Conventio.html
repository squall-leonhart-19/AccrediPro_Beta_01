Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to start the conversation about transitioning Buddy to a B.A.R.F. diet. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Carol, thank you for sharing Buddy's history with me. Based on what you've described, a biologically appropriate raw food (B.A.R.F.) diet could be a great option to address his allergies and digestive issues. Are you familiar with raw feeding at all?"
CAROL:
"I've heard of it, but it seems a little intimidating. I'm worried about bacteria and making sure he gets the right nutrients."
YOU:
"Those are valid concerns, and I'm glad you brought them up! I can assure you that with proper handling and a balanced approach, a B.A.R.F. diet can be very safe and incredibly beneficial. We'll work together to create a plan that fits Buddy's specific needs and your comfort level."
YOU:
"The B.A.R.F. framework emphasizes the importance of Balance, Appropriate Ratios, Raw Food Integration, and Functional Follow-up. We'll address each of these systematically to ensure Buddy gets everything he needs."
CAROL:
"Okay, that sounds more manageable when you break it down like that. What does 'balance' mean in this context?"
YOU:
"Great question! Balance refers to ensuring Buddy's diet includes a variety of ingredients to cover all his nutritional bases. We'll include muscle meat, bones, organs, and plant matter, each providing unique benefits. Does that make sense?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key components of the B.A.R.F. framework?

The four key components are Balance, Appropriate Ratios, Raw Food Integration, and Functional Follow-up.

2. How would you address a client's concern about bacterial contamination in a raw food diet?

Acknowledge their concern, explain proper handling and hygiene practices (e.g., washing hands, disinfecting surfaces), and emphasize that a dog's digestive system is designed to handle bacteria.

Key Takeaways

  • The B.A.R.F. framework provides a structured approach to raw feeding.
  • Addressing client concerns with empathy and accurate information is crucial.
  • Breaking down complex concepts into simpler terms increases client understanding.
  • Highlighting the benefits of a B.A.R.F. diet can motivate clients to make changes.
