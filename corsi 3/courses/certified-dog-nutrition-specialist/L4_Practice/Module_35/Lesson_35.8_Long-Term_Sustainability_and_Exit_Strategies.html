Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out! I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can help your dog. To start, tell me a bit about [dog's name] and what you've been experiencing."
YOU:
"I understand it can be frustrating to see your furry friend struggling. What have you already tried to address the allergies?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"How long has [dog's name] been dealing with these allergies? What are the specific symptoms you're seeing?"
YOU:
"You mentioned vet-prescribed medications. What were the side effects you observed, and how did they impact [dog's name]'s quality of life?"
YOU:
"If the allergies continue unresolved, what's the biggest concern for you? On the other hand, if you could wave a magic wand and eliminate the allergies, what would that mean for [dog's name] and for you?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can commit to a 12-week program right now."
"I understand that a 12-week commitment might seem daunting. However, it's designed to give us enough time to address the underlying causes of the allergies and establish lasting changes. What specific concerns do you have about the time commitment?"
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. What I offer is different because it's not a one-size-fits-all approach. We tailor a nutrition plan specifically to [dog's name]'s needs, considering their individual health history and sensitivities. Let's explore why those other approaches didn't work and how we can take a different path."
"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"That's perfectly fine. What information would be most helpful for them to understand the program and its potential benefits for [dog's name]? I can provide a detailed overview or even schedule a brief call to answer any questions together."
"What if it doesn't work?"
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can assure you that I'm committed to providing personalized support and guidance throughout the 12 weeks. Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by highlighting the long-term benefits and potential cost savings (e.g., reduced vet bills). Ask what the value of solving the problem would be to them.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you understand the prospect's needs and present your solution effectively.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Clearly articulating your pricing and the value you provide builds trust and justifies the investment.
  • Consistent effort in client acquisition can lead to a thriving and financially rewarding practice.
