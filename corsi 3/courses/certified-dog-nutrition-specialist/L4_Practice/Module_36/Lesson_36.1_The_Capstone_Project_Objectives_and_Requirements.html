Learning Objectives and the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢

The capstone project is designed to solidify your understanding and application of the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢. The learning objectives are directly aligned with the framework's components:

  • Balance Assessment (B): Demonstrate your ability to thoroughly assess a dog's health history, current diet, lifestyle, and specific nutritional needs.
  • Appropriate Ratios (A): Develop a balanced dietary plan with appropriate macronutrient and micronutrient ratios tailored to the individual dog.
  • Raw Food Integration (R): Incorporate raw food components safely and effectively, considering the dog's individual needs and preferences.
  • Functional Follow-up (F): Establish a monitoring plan to track the dog's progress, make necessary adjustments, and ensure long-term health and well-being.

By successfully completing the capstone, you'll prove your ability to integrate these components into a cohesive and effective nutritional strategy.

Specific Project Requirements

To ensure a comprehensive and standardized evaluation, the capstone project has specific requirements:

  • Case Study Selection: Choose a dog with specific nutritional needs or health challenges. This could be a dog with allergies, digestive issues,or weight management concerns. Preference will be given to case studies that demonstrate the application of the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ to complex or challenging cases.
  • Detailed Assessment: Conduct a thorough assessment of the dog's health history, current diet, lifestyle, and any relevant diagnostic test results. Document this assessment clearly and concisely.
  • Dietary Plan Development: Develop a detailed dietary plan that addresses the dog's specific needs, incorporating appropriate macronutrient and micronutrient ratios, raw food components (if applicable), and any necessary supplements. Explain the rationale behind your choices.
  • Implementation and Monitoring: Implement the dietary plan and monitor the dog's progress over a specified period (typically 8-12 weeks). Track relevant parameters, such as weight, body condition score, digestion, energy levels, and any specific symptoms related to the dog's condition.
  • Outcome Analysis: Analyze the outcomes of the dietary intervention, documenting any improvements or changes in the dog's health. Discuss the strengths and weaknesses of your approach and suggest any potential modifications.
  • Documentation: Prepare a comprehensive written report that details all aspects of the project, including the case study selection, assessment, dietary plan development, implementation, monitoring, and outcome analysis.

Documenting the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ in Your Project

Your capstone project documentation should clearly demonstrate how you applied each component of the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢:

  • Balance Assessment (B): Include a detailed history of the dog, including signalment (age, breed, sex), medical history, current diet, lifestyle, and any relevant lab work. Explain how this information informed your dietary recommendations.
  • Appropriate Ratios (A): Clearly outline the macronutrient and micronutrient ratios in your proposed diet. Justify your choices based on the dog's individual needs and any relevant scientific literature. Provide a detailed breakdown of the ingredients used and their nutrient content.
  • Raw Food Integration (R): If incorporating raw food, explain the rationale behind your choices, including the types of raw foods used, their preparation methods, and any safety precautions taken. Address any potential risks and how you mitigated them. If not incorporating raw food, justify why this approach was not suitable for the specific case.
  • Functional Follow-up (F): Describe your monitoring plan, including the parameters you tracked, the frequency of monitoring, and any adjustments you made to the diet based on the dog's response. Include specific examples of how you adapted the plan to address any challenges or unexpected outcomes.
