Client Profile: 'Shadow'

Shadow is a 2-year-old intact male German Shepherd presenting with a history of chronic diarrhea, frequent vomiting, and noticeable weight loss. He has been diagnosed with Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD) by a veterinarian and is currently being managed with corticosteroids to reduce inflammation. The owner, Sarah, a dedicated dog lover, is seeking a more holistic approach to manage Shadow's condition and reduce his dependence on medication. Sarah, age 45, is a former teacher who is passionate about animal welfare and is looking for a flexible career that aligns with her values.

Balance Assessment

The first step in applying the B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ is conducting a thorough Balance Assessment. This involves gathering comprehensive information about Shadow's current health status, dietary history, and lifestyle factors. A detailed assessment helps identify imbalances contributing to IBD and guides the development of a personalized nutritional plan.

  1. Medical History: Review Shadow's veterinary records, including diagnostic tests, medications, and previous treatments. Note any specific triggers or patterns related to his IBD symptoms.
  2. Dietary History: Obtain a detailed account of Shadow's current diet, including the type of food, feeding schedule, and any supplements. Identify potential allergens or sensitivities that may be exacerbating his condition.
  3. Lifestyle Factors: Assess Shadow's activity level, stress levels, and environmental exposures. Determine whether any lifestyle factors may be contributing to his IBD symptoms.
  4. Physical Examination: Conduct a thorough physical examination, noting any signs of inflammation, dehydration, or nutrient deficiencies. Assess Shadow's body condition score and muscle mass to evaluate his nutritional status.

Based on the Balance Assessment, several key imbalances are identified:

  • Dietary Imbalance: Shadow's current diet consists primarily of processed kibble, which may contain inflammatory ingredients and lack essential nutrients.
  • Gut Dysbiosis: Chronic inflammation has disrupted the balance of bacteria in Shadow's gut, leading to dysbiosis and impaired digestion.
  • Nutrient Deficiencies: Shadow's weight loss and poor coat quality suggest potential nutrient deficiencies, particularly of essential fatty acids, vitamins, and minerals.

Determining Appropriate Ratios

Next, determine appropriate macronutrient ratios to support Shadow's digestion and nutrient absorption. A balanced diet that is tailored to his individual needs can help reduce inflammation and promote gut healing.

For Shadow, a diet consisting of approximately:

  • 70% Raw Meat and Bones: Focus on novel protein sources such as rabbit, venison, or duck to minimize the risk of allergic reactions. Include raw meaty bones for calcium and phosphorus balance.
  • 20% Vegetables and Fruits: Incorporate a variety of non-starchy vegetables and low-glycemic fruits to provide fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants. Consider options like spinach, kale, blueberries, and cranberries.
  • 10% Organ Meats: Include liver, kidney, and heart to provide essential vitamins, minerals, and amino acids.

In addition to macronutrient ratios, consider the following:

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Supplement with fish oil or flaxseed oil to reduce inflammation and support immune function. A 2017 meta-analysis in the Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine, (n=357 dogs) found that omega-3 fatty acids significantly reduced clinical signs of IBD (OR 0.45, 95% CI: 0.28-0.72).
  • Probiotics: Administer a high-quality probiotic supplement to restore the balance of bacteria in Shadow's gut. A 2023 study in the Journal of Animal Science found that probiotic supplementation improved fecal consistency and reduced inflammation in dogs with IBD (p < 0.05).
  • Digestive Enzymes: Add digestive enzymes to Shadow's diet to aid in the breakdown of nutrients and improve digestion.

Raw Food Integration

Gradually introduce raw food into Shadow's diet to minimize digestive upset. Start by replacing a small portion of his current kibble with raw food, gradually increasing the amount over several days.

  1. Day 1-3: Replace 25% of Shadow's kibble with raw food. Monitor his stool for any signs of diarrhea or vomiting.
  2. Day 4-6: Increase the raw food portion to 50%. Continue to monitor his stool and adjust the amount as needed.
  3. Day 7-9: Increase the raw food portion to 75%. If Shadow is tolerating the diet well, proceed to the next step.
  4. Day 10: Transition Shadow to a 100% raw food diet. Continue to monitor his stool and adjust the diet as needed.

When selecting ingredients for Shadow's raw food diet, consider the following:

  • Novel Protein Sources: Choose protein sources that Shadow has not been previously exposed to, such as rabbit, venison, or duck. This can help minimize the risk of allergic reactions.
  • Gut-Supportive Ingredients: Incorporate ingredients that support gut health, such as bone broth, fermented vegetables, and slippery elm bark.
  • Avoid Potential Allergens: Avoid common allergens such as grains, dairy, and soy.

Functional Follow-up

Implement a Functional Follow-up strategy to monitor Shadow's progress and adjust the diet as needed. Regular check-ins with Sarah can help identify any issues and ensure that the diet is effectively managing his IBD symptoms.

  1. Weekly Check-ins: Schedule weekly check-ins with Sarah to monitor Shadow's stool quality, appetite, and energy levels.
  2. Dietary Adjustments: Make dietary adjustments as needed based on Shadow's response to the diet. For example, if he experiences diarrhea, reduce the amount of fat in his diet or add more fiber.
  3. Supplement Adjustments: Adjust the dosage of supplements based on Shadow's individual needs. For example, if he is not responding well to the probiotic supplement, consider switching to a different brand or increasing the dosage.
  4. Veterinary Consultations: Encourage Sarah to continue working with her veterinarian to monitor Shadow's IBD and adjust his medication as needed.
