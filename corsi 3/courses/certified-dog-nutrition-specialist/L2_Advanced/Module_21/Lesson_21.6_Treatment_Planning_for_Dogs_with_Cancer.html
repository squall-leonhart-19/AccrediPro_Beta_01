The Ketogenic Diet and Cancer

The ketogenic diet, characterized by high fat, moderate protein, and very low carbohydrate intake, has garnered attention for its potential role in cancer management. Cancer cells primarily rely on glucose (sugar) for energy. By drastically reducing carbohydrate intake, we aim to deprive cancer cells of their preferred fuel source, potentially slowing tumor growth. A 2019 study in the Journal of Animal Physiology and Animal Nutrition found that a ketogenic diet improved survival times in dogs with cancer (n=25) compared to a standard diet.

However, it's crucial to remember that every dog is unique, and the ketogenic diet isn't a one-size-fits-all solution. Some cancers may not respond to this approach, and careful monitoring is essential. The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ emphasizes the importance of Balance Assessment to determine individual needs.

Nutritional Support During Chemotherapy and Radiation Therapy

Chemotherapy and radiation therapy are common cancer treatments in dogs, but they often come with significant side effects like nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and loss of appetite. Nutritional support is vital to mitigate these effects and maintain the dog's strength. A 2021 review in the Veterinary Clinics of North America: Small Animal Practice highlights the importance of highly digestible protein sources, such as cooked chicken or fish, to support tissue repair and immune function during these treatments.

Here are some specific nutritional strategies to consider:

  • Managing Nausea and Vomiting: Offer small, frequent meals. Ginger, in small amounts, can help soothe the stomach.
  • Combating Diarrhea: Probiotics can help restore gut flora. Consider adding cooked sweet potato or pumpkin to the diet for fiber.
  • Boosting Appetite: Warming the food slightly can enhance its aroma and make it more appealing. Adding bone broth can also stimulate appetite.

Remember, The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ advocates for Raw Feeding Fundamentals, but during chemotherapy or radiation, lightly cooking the food may be safer to minimize the risk of bacterial infections.

Using Raw Foods to Boost Immunity

Even when lightly cooking some foods during treatment, incorporating specific raw food components can still significantly boost immune function. Raw foods contain enzymes, antioxidants, and other bioactive compounds that are often lost during cooking. A 2022 study in Animals demonstrated that dogs fed a raw food diet had higher levels of beneficial gut bacteria and improved immune responses compared to dogs fed a processed kibble diet.

Consider adding these raw food components to your dog's diet:

  • Raw Fermented Vegetables: Rich in probiotics and enzymes.
  • Raw Honey: Contains antibacterial and antioxidant properties.
  • Raw Egg Yolks: A source of essential fatty acids and vitamins.
  • Ground Raw Bone: Provides calcium and other minerals.

When introducing raw foods, start slowly and monitor your dog's stool for any signs of digestive upset. The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ emphasizes Adaptive Feeding, adjusting the diet based on the individual dog's response.

Targeting Cancer Metabolism with Specific Nutrients

Certain nutrients have shown promise in targeting cancer metabolism and potentially inhibiting tumor growth. These include:

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Found in fish oil and flaxseed oil, omega-3s have anti-inflammatory properties and may inhibit cancer cell growth. A 2020 meta-analysis in Prostaglandins, Leukotrienes and Essential Fatty Acids found that omega-3 supplementation was associated with improved survival in dogs with cancer.
  • Curcumin: A compound found in turmeric, curcumin has potent anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. It may also inhibit cancer cell proliferation and angiogenesis (the formation of new blood vessels that feed tumors).
  • Vitamin D: Low vitamin D levels have been linked to increased cancer risk. Supplementation may help support immune function and inhibit cancer cell growth.
  • Medicinal Mushrooms: Certain medicinal mushrooms, such as turkey tail and reishi, contain polysaccharides that have immune-modulating and anti-tumor properties.

Always consult with a veterinarian or veterinary oncologist before adding any supplements to your dog's diet, as some may interact with conventional cancer treatments. The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ stresses the importance of Responsible Supplementation, ensuring that supplements are high-quality and appropriate for the individual dog's needs.

Case Study: Lymphoma

Client: Bella, 8-year-old Golden Retriever

Presenting Symptoms: Generalized lymph node enlargement, lethargy, loss of appetite.

Diagnosis: Lymphoma (confirmed by biopsy).

Intervention: Bella underwent chemotherapy. In addition to conventional treatment, a nutritional plan based on The B.A.R.F. Frameworkâ„¢ was implemented. This included:

  • A ketogenic diet with approximately 70% fat, 20% protein, and 10% carbohydrates.
  • Omega-3 supplementation (fish oil).
  • Curcumin supplementation.
  • Probiotics to support gut health during chemotherapy.
  • Small, frequent meals to manage nausea.

Outcomes: Bella responded well to chemotherapy and the nutritional intervention. Her lymph nodes returned to normal size, and her energy levels improved. She maintained a good quality of life for 18 months following diagnosis, exceeding the average survival time for dogs with lymphoma.
