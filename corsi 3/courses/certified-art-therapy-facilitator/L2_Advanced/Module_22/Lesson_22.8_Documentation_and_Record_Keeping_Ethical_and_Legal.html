Complex Case Presentation

Eleanor, 48

Freelance writer, Asheville, NC ‚Ä¢ Married, two teenage children

Presenting Issue
Anxiety, creative block, feelings of isolation, difficulty balancing work and family responsibilities.
Background
Eleanor sought art therapy to reconnect with her creative self. She's been in therapy for 6 months and has made significant progress in expressing her emotions through art.
The Dilemma
Eleanor invites you to join her book club, which includes other local writers and artists. You are also a writer and feel drawn to the idea of connecting with like-minded individuals.
Potential Benefits
Joining the book club could provide you with valuable networking opportunities and creative inspiration. Eleanor might feel honored and see it as a sign of your genuine care.
Potential Risks
Blurring professional boundaries could compromise the therapeutic relationship. Other book club members might learn about Eleanor's therapy, violating her confidentiality.
Ethical Codes
The American Art Therapy Association (AATA) code of ethics strictly advises against dual relationships that could exploit the client or impair professional judgment.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Issue
The primary ethical issue is the potential for a dual relationship, where you would be both Eleanor's therapist and a fellow book club member.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
The AATA code emphasizes avoiding dual relationships that could impair objectivity, competence, or effectiveness in performing your art therapy functions.
Step 3: Consider Potential Consequences
Joining the book club could lead to a loss of objectivity, difficulty maintaining confidentiality, and potential harm to the therapeutic relationship.
Step 4: Consult with Supervisors or Colleagues
Seeking guidance from experienced colleagues or supervisors can provide valuable insights and alternative perspectives. They can help you assess the risks and benefits more objectively.
Step 5: Develop a Course of Action
After careful consideration, the most ethical course of action is to respectfully decline Eleanor's invitation, explaining your concerns about maintaining professional boundaries.

Differential Considerations

Alternative Scenarios

1

Small Town Dynamics

In a small town where social circles overlap, avoiding all dual relationships might be impossible. Transparency and informed consent are crucial. Document everything.

2

Post-Termination Relationships

Even after therapy ends, caution is needed. A romantic relationship shortly after termination is almost always unethical. Wait at least two years and consult ethical guidelines.

3

Bartering for Services

Bartering (e.g., art supplies for therapy sessions) can be acceptable if it's culturally appropriate, doesn't exploit the client, and is documented clearly. However, cash is always preferable.

4

Social Media Interactions

Avoid "friending" clients on social media. Maintain a professional online presence and be mindful of what you post, as it could impact the therapeutic relationship.

When to Refer

Ethical Violations and Legal Issues

  • Suspected Child Abuse or Neglect: Mandatory reporting laws require immediate referral to child protective services.
  • Threats of Harm to Self or Others: Duty to warn laws necessitate notifying authorities and potential victims.
  • Subpoenaed Records: Consult with an attorney before releasing any client information.
  • Ethical Complaints: If a client files a complaint against you, seek legal counsel and consult with your professional organization (AATA).

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the potential risks of entering into a dual relationship with a client?

Potential risks include impaired objectivity, compromised confidentiality, exploitation of the client, and damage to the therapeutic relationship.

2. How should you respond if a client invites you to a social event?

Respectfully decline the invitation, explaining your concerns about maintaining professional boundaries. Offer alternative suggestions for the client to meet their social needs.

Key Takeaways

  • Dual relationships can compromise the therapeutic relationship and potentially harm clients.
  • Ethical codes provide guidance on avoiding dual relationships and maintaining professional boundaries.
  • Transparency, informed consent, and consultation with colleagues are essential when navigating complex ethical dilemmas.
  • Prioritize the client's well-being and the integrity of the therapeutic process above personal interests.
