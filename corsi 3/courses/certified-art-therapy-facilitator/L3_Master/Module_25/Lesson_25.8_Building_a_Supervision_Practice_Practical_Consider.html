Her Question
"I had a client disclose a past trauma. I'm not sure if I handled it correctly or if I should refer them to a therapist."

The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I was working with Sarah, a 50-year-old woman, in a group art therapy session. We were exploring themes of self-compassion through clay work. Sarah created a small, fragile figure and then started crying, saying it reminded her of a traumatic event from her childhood. I froze. I offered her a tissue and validated her feelings, but I'm not sure if I did enough. Should I have explored it further? Should I refer her to a licensed therapist?"

Key Considerations:

  • Was Olivia prepared for potential trauma disclosures?
  • Did she have a clear understanding of her scope of practice?
  • Did she know how to effectively contain the situation and ensure Sarah's safety?
  • Does Sarah need a referral for deeper trauma processing?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Trauma-Informed Practice

Emphasize the importance of being trauma-informed. Explain that unintentional re-traumatization can occur if disclosures are not handled carefully.

2

Scope of Practice

Clarify the boundaries of her role as an Art Therapy Facilitator. While providing support and validation is within her scope, trauma processing is not.

3

Containment Techniques

Teach practical containment techniques, such as grounding exercises, mindfulness practices, and creating a safe space for emotional expression without delving into details.

4

Referral Process

Explain how to make appropriate referrals to licensed therapists or trauma specialists. Provide her with a list of local resources and a sample referral letter.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Concerns
"Olivia, it's completely understandable that you felt unsure in that situation. It's a common experience for new facilitators. The fact that you're seeking guidance shows your commitment to ethical practice."
Validate Her Actions
"Offering a tissue and validating Sarah's feelings were excellent first steps. You created a space for her to express her emotions."
Guide Her Thinking
"Now, let's think about the potential risks of exploring the trauma further within a group setting, especially without specialized training. What are your thoughts on that?"
Empower Her Future Actions
"Moving forward, how can you create a group agreement that addresses potential disclosures and the limits of your role? And how can you proactively offer resources for clients who may need additional support?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to clarify the scope of practice when mentoring a new Art Therapy Facilitator?

Clarifying the scope of practice ensures that the facilitator understands the boundaries of their role and avoids providing services that they are not qualified to offer, which could potentially harm clients.

2. What are some practical containment techniques you can teach a new facilitator to manage potential trauma disclosures in a group setting?

Practical containment techniques include grounding exercises, mindfulness practices, creating a safe space for emotional expression without delving into details, and offering resources for additional support.
