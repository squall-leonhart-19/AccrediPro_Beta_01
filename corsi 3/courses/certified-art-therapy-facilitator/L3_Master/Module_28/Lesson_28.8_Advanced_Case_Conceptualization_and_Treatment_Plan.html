The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I'm working with Sarah, 52, who is processing childhood trauma. During our last session, she shared a very disturbing memory. I felt paralyzed and didn't know how to respond. I'm worried I retraumatized her by not being supportive enough. I also felt extremely triggered and started to dissociate. What should I do?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • What specific techniques did Aisha use to help Sarah regulate her emotions?
  • Did Aisha have a clear plan for managing potential disclosures of trauma?
  • What are Aisha's personal triggers, and how can she manage them in session?
  • Is this client within Aisha's scope of practice, or does she need a referral?

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Aisha, thank you for being so open and honest about your experience. It's admirable that you are seeking supervision. Your willingness to reflect on your practice demonstrates your commitment to your clients."
Explore Her Experience
"Before I share my thoughts, what did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time? What resources do you have in place for your own self-care?"
Offer Guidance
"One thing I've found helpful is to have a few go-to phrases for when a client shares something difficult. For example, 'Thank you for sharing that with me. It sounds like that was a very painful experience.' This acknowledges their experience without pressuring you to have all the answers."
Empower Next Steps
"What feels like the most important thing to address in your next session with Sarah? What support do you need from me to feel confident in your ability to help her?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key ethical considerations when supervising a new art therapy facilitator working with clients who have experienced trauma?

Maintaining client confidentiality, ensuring the mentee's competence, addressing scope of practice issues, and managing transference and countertransference are essential ethical considerations.

2. How can you help a mentee develop a strong sense of professional identity and confidence in their skills?

Provide regular feedback, acknowledge their strengths, encourage self-reflection, and offer opportunities for professional development. Help them connect with other professionals in the field.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves creating a safe and supportive environment for the mentee to explore their experiences and challenges.
  • It's crucial to address ethical considerations, such as scope of practice, confidentiality, and boundary setting.
  • Providing constructive feedback, acknowledging strengths, and encouraging self-reflection can help build the mentee's confidence and professional identity.
  • Supervision is an ongoing process that requires patience, empathy, and a commitment to the mentee's growth and development.
