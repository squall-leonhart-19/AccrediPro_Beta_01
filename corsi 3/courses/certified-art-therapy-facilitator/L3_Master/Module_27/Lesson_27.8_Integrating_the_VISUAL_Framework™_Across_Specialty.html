Maria, New Graduate

Enthusiastic L1 graduate, seeking guidance on a challenging client case.

Background
Former elementary school teacher, passionate about art's healing potential.
Strengths
Creative, compassionate, excellent at building rapport with clients.
Growth Areas
Needs help with complex trauma cases, struggles with setting boundaries.
Her Question
"How can I better support clients with trauma histories while protecting my own well-being?"

The Case She Presents

Maria's Client Case

What Maria Tells You:

"I'm working with Sarah, 52, who experienced childhood trauma. During our session, she became very agitated after creating a dark, chaotic drawing. I felt overwhelmed and unsure how to help her regulate. I ended the session early, but I'm worried I didn't handle it well."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Maria have a clear safety plan in place?
  • What grounding techniques did she attempt?
  • How did she manage her own emotional response?
  • What are the ethical considerations regarding client safety and transference?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Maria

1

Importance of Safety Planning

Emphasize the need for a comprehensive safety plan, including resources and emergency contacts, before engaging in trauma work.

2

Grounding and Containment Techniques

Teach Maria specific grounding exercises (e.g., 5-4-3-2-1 technique) and containment strategies (e.g., creating a "safe space" image) to help clients manage intense emotions.

3

Managing Transference and Countertransference

Explain the dynamics of transference and countertransference and how to recognize and manage these phenomena in the therapeutic relationship. Encourage Maria to seek supervision to process her own emotional responses.

4

Ethical Considerations and Boundaries

Review ethical guidelines related to client safety, scope of practice, and self-care. Discuss the importance of setting clear boundaries to prevent burnout and maintain professional integrity.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Maria, I commend you for recognizing your discomfort and seeking guidance. It takes courage to acknowledge feeling overwhelmed, especially when working with trauma."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I offer suggestions, what did you learn from this experience? What would you do differently next time, knowing what you know now?"
Provide Education and Resources
"It's essential to have a robust safety plan in place. Let's review some grounding techniques and containment strategies that you can use with clients who become agitated. I can also share some articles on managing transference."
Encourage Self-Care and Supervision
"Remember, your well-being is paramount. Make sure you're engaging in regular self-care practices and seeking supervision to process your own emotional responses to these cases. This will help you maintain your effectiveness and prevent burnout."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a comprehensive safety plan when working with clients who have trauma histories?

A comprehensive safety plan includes identifying triggers, developing coping strategies, listing emergency contacts, and establishing clear protocols for managing crises.

2. How can you help a mentee manage their own emotional responses to challenging client cases?

Encourage the mentee to engage in self-care practices, seek supervision, and process their feelings through journaling or other creative outlets.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize safety planning and risk assessment when working with clients who have trauma histories.
  • Teach grounding and containment techniques to help clients manage intense emotions.
  • Address transference and countertransference dynamics in the therapeutic relationship.
  • Encourage self-care and ongoing supervision to prevent burnout and maintain ethical practice.
