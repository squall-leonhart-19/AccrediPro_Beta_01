Ethical Considerations

As with any therapeutic approach, ethical considerations are paramount when using the VISUAL Frameworkâ„¢. It's crucial to:

  • Ensure Client Safety: Prioritize the client's well-being and emotional stability.
  • Maintain Boundaries: Establish clear professional boundaries to avoid dual relationships.
  • Respect Autonomy: Empower clients to make their own decisions and choices.
  • Obtain Informed Consent: Clearly explain the therapeutic process and obtain the client's consent.
  • Maintain Confidentiality: Protect the client's privacy and confidentiality.

Remember, your role is to facilitate exploration and provide support, not to impose your own interpretations or beliefs. Adhering to these ethical guidelines will create a safe and empowering environment for your clients.

The Art Therapist's Role

Your role as an art therapist is to guide the client through the VISUAL Frameworkâ„¢, fostering exploration, providing support, and promoting self-discovery. This involves:
