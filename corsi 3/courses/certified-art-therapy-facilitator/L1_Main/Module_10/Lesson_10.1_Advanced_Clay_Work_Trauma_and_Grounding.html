Over several sessions, Sarah began to create a small, enclosed structure with the clay, describing it as a "safe space." By the end of the sessions, Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety and improved sleep quality. The clay work had facilitated emotional processing and provided her with a tangible representation of safety and control.

Ethical Considerations

Working with trauma survivors requires a heightened awareness of ethical considerations. Key points include:

  • Boundaries: Establish clear boundaries with clients to ensure their safety and your own well-being.
  • Self-Care: Engage in regular self-care practices to prevent vicarious trauma and burnout.
  • Referral Protocols: Know when to refer clients to other professionals, such as therapists or psychiatrists, for additional support.

Remember, your role is to facilitate healing, not to be a primary therapist. Adhering to these guidelines ensures responsible and ethical practice. The American Art Therapy Association (AATA) provides comprehensive ethical guidelines for art therapists.
