Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, incorporating art therapy principles. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your feelings with me. It takes courage to acknowledge these emotions. Many women in their 50s experience similar feelings of being 'stuck,' and it's often a sign that it's time for a new chapter. I was wondering, would you be open to exploring some of these feelings through a creative process?
MARIA:
"I'm a little nervous. I haven't done anything artistic in years. I'm not very good at it."
YOU:
"That's perfectly normal, Maria. This isn't about creating a masterpiece. It's about using art as a tool to tap into your emotions and gain insights you might not access through words alone. Think of it as a conversation with yourself, using colors and shapes instead of sentences."
YOU:
"I have a few ideas for simple art exercises that can help us explore your feelings of being 'stuck' and what you might want to 'unstick' yourself from. Would you be willing to try one?"
MARIA:
"Okay, I'm willing to try. What did you have in mind?"
YOU:
"Great. We can start with a simple drawing exercise focused on visualizing your current state and your desired future. I'll guide you through it step by step."

Your Turn ‚Äî Practice Out Loud

1

Read the script silently first

Pay attention to the tone and the intention behind each statement. Notice how you're setting the stage for using art as a therapeutic tool.

2

Imagine Maria sitting across from you. Use a gentle, encouraging tone. Convey empathy and understanding.

3

Use your phone to record your voice. Listen back and assess your tone, pacing, and level of confidence. Are you conveying a sense of safety and trust?

4

Personalize the script

Adapt the script to your own style and voice. Maintain the core message, but use language that feels authentic to you.

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Maria expresses a willingness to try the art exercise.
  • She asks clarifying questions about the process.
  • Her body language relaxes, indicating trust.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of using art in this coaching scenario?

The primary goal is to facilitate emotional exploration and self-discovery, not to create aesthetically pleasing artwork.

2. How should you respond if a client expresses resistance to using art?

Acknowledge their feelings, validate their concerns, and offer alternative approaches that align with their comfort level.

Key Takeaways

  • Art therapy techniques can be powerful tools for emotional exploration in coaching.
  • It's crucial to create a safe and supportive environment for clients to express themselves creatively.
  • Focus on the client's feelings and experiences, not on interpreting the art symbolically.
  • Start with simple, accessible exercises to build confidence and reduce anxiety.
