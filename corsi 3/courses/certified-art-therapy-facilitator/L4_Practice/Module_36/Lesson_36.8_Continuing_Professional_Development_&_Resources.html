Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I'm creative enough for art therapy."
"That's a common misconception! You don't need to be an artist. It's about using art as a tool for self-expression and exploration. I provide all the guidance and support you need."
"I'm worried I don't have the time."
"I understand. The program requires a commitment of a few hours per week, but think of it as an investment in your well-being. Many clients find that by reducing their stress, they actually become more productive and have more time overall."
"How is this different from regular therapy?"
"While traditional therapy relies heavily on talk, art therapy accesses different parts of your brain and allows you to express yourself non-verbally. It's often a gentler and more accessible approach for processing emotions and finding solutions."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: Building Rapport, Understanding the Prospect's Needs, Presenting Your Solution, and Closing/Next Steps.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think, then ask clarifying questions: "What specifically do you need to think about?" This helps you address their underlying concerns.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Active listening and empathy are essential for understanding client needs.
  • Confidence in your pricing and value proposition is key to overcoming objections.
  • Consistent practice and refinement of your sales skills will lead to increased success.
