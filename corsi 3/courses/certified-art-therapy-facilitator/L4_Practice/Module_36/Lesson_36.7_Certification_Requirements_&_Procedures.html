3. Maintenance Requirements

Certification as an Art Therapy Facilitatorâ„¢ is not a one-time achievement. To maintain your certification, you are required to engage in ongoing professional development and ethical practice. These requirements ensure that you stay current with best practices and continue to grow as a facilitator.

Here's what you need to know:

  1. Continuing Education: Complete a minimum of 20 hours of continuing education credits every two years. These credits should be related to art therapy, counseling, or related fields.
  2. Ethical Compliance: Maintain adherence to the code of ethics for art therapy facilitators. Any ethical violations may result in suspension or revocation of certification.
  3. Renewal Fee: Pay the biennial renewal fee to maintain active certification status.

5. Q&A Session

Q: What happens if I fail the final certification exam?

A: You will have the opportunity to retake the exam. Contact the certification board for details on retake policies and fees.

You will be allowed one retake of the exam. A retake fee will apply. We recommend reviewing the modules again, focusing on areas where you felt less confident.

Q: How long is the certification valid?

A: The certification is valid for two years, after which you must renew it by meeting the maintenance requirements.

Your certification is valid for two years. Renewal requires completion of continuing education credits and payment of the renewal fee.

Q: What types of continuing education credits are accepted?

A: Continuing education credits must be related to art therapy, counseling, or related fields. Credits from accredited institutions or professional organizations are typically accepted.

We accept credits from workshops, conferences, online courses, and academic coursework related to art therapy, psychology, and counseling. Check with the certification board for pre-approval of specific courses.
