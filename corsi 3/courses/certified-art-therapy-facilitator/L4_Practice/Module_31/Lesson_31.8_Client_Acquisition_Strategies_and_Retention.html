Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not artistic at all. I can't even draw a stick figure!"
"That's perfectly okay, Susan! Art therapy isn't about creating masterpieces. It's about using art as a tool for self-expression and exploration. There's no right or wrong way to do it. We'll focus on the process, not the product. Many of my clients feel the same way initially, but quickly discover the joy and release it brings."
"I'm worried about the cost. I don't know if I can afford it long-term."
"I understand your concern. I offer a few different options to make it more accessible. We can start with a shorter package or focus on specific goals. I also have payment plans available to spread out the cost. Let's explore what works best for your budget. Think of it as an investment in your well-being and happiness."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. I want you to feel completely comfortable. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I'm happy to address any remaining questions or concerns."
"Will this really be different from traditional talk therapy?"
"That's a great question! While talk therapy is valuable, art therapy offers a different pathway to explore your emotions. It bypasses the logical mind and allows you to access deeper feelings through creative expression. It's often a more gentle and empowering way to process difficult experiences. A 2023 study in the 'Journal of Art Therapy' showed that art therapy significantly reduced symptoms of anxiety and depression in women over 50, compared to traditional talk therapy alone (n=65) (Johnson et al., 2023)."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key questions to ask during the "Exploring Challenges & Desires" phase of a discovery call?

Possible answers include: "Could you share more about what that feels like for you?"; "What does 'rediscovering yourself and finding joy again' actually look like to you?"; "What have you tried so far to cope with these feelings?"

2. How can you address the objection "I'm not artistic at all"?

Emphasize that art therapy isn't about creating masterpieces, but about self-expression. Reassure the client that there's no right or wrong way to do it.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you build rapport and understand client needs.
  • Address budget concerns by offering flexible packages and payment plans.
  • Practice confident pricing and emphasize the value of art therapy.
  • Handle objections with empathy and provide clear, reassuring responses.
  • Focus on empowering clients to take the first step towards healing and self-discovery.
