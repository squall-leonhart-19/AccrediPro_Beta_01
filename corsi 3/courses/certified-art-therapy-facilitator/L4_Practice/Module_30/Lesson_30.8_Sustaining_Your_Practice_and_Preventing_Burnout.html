"I'm on a fixed income, so I need to be careful with my spending."
Decision Style
Values connection and authenticity. Needs to feel understood and supported.
Her Goal
"I want to find something meaningful to do with my time and feel connected to others."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! It's so nice to finally connect. Thanks for scheduling this call. I saw you attended my recent online workshop, what resonated with you the most?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! So Susan, tell me a bit about what's been going on since you retired. What are you hoping to create in this next chapter?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What does 'feeling lost' feel like for you? What are some of the specific challenges you're facing right now?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to find that sense of purpose and connection? What did you enjoy, and what didn't work so well?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and create your ideal retirement life, what would that look like? How would you be spending your days? Who would you be connecting with?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking a way to reignite your creativity, find a sense of purpose, and connect with a supportive community. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My art therapy facilitation program is designed to help you do just that. Over 8 weeks, we'll explore different art mediums, tap into your inner wisdom, and connect with other women who are on a similar journey. Many of my clients find a renewed sense of purpose and connection within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like the type of support you've been looking for? ... Wonderful! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get you enrolled."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I'm creative enough."
"Susan, that's a common concern! This program isn't about being an artist, it's about using art as a tool for self-discovery and connection. No prior art experience is necessary. It's about the process, not the product."
"I need to think about it."
"I understand completely. What specifically do you need to think about? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make a confident decision."
"It's a bit more than I was planning to spend."
"I appreciate you being upfront about that. Can I ask, what value would you place on feeling fulfilled, connected, and creatively engaged in your retirement? What's that worth to you?"
"I'm worried I won't have time."
"That's a valid concern. The program is designed to be flexible, with sessions held at [mention days/times] and all materials provided. Many of my clients are surprised by how much time they actually *gain* by prioritizing their well-being and creative expression. It's an investment in yourself, Susan."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the long-term benefits and the return on investment.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Confidence in presenting your pricing is essential.
  • Address objections with empathy and solutions.
  • Practice your script to feel natural and authentic.
  • Building a thriving practice is achievable with consistent effort.
