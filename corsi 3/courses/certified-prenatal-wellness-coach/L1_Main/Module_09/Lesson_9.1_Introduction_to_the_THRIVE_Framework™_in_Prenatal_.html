Ethical Considerations

As a prenatal wellness coach, you have an ethical responsibility to provide safe, evidence-based, and culturally sensitive care. Key ethical considerations when applying the THRIVE Frameworkâ„¢ include:

  • Scope of Practice: Clearly define your role as a coach and avoid providing medical advice or treatment. Refer clients to qualified healthcare professionals when necessary.
  • Informed Consent: Ensure clients understand the nature of coaching, its limitations, and their right to withdraw at any time.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain strict confidentiality regarding client information, except when required by law (e.g., suspected child abuse).
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Be aware of cultural differences and adapt your approach to meet the unique needs of diverse populations.
  • Evidence-Based Practice: Rely on evidence-based information and avoid promoting unproven or harmful practices.

According to the American Psychological Associationâ€™s (APA) Ethical Principles of Psychologists and Code of Conduct (2017), competence, integrity, and respect for people's rights and dignity are paramount.
