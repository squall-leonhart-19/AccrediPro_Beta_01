Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on tuning in to Maria's experience. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you so much for sharing all of that with me. It sounds like you're carrying a lot right now. Before we talk solutions, I want to really hear you. How are you *really* feeling about going back to work?"
MARIA:
"Honestly? Terrified. Excited to get back to my classroom, but also guilty that I'm not going to be with my son all day. And exhausted, even thinking about it."
YOU:
"That mix of emotions is so valid, Maria. Many moms feel that way. So, let's dig a little deeper. If you could wave a magic wand and change *one* thing about this transition, what would it be?"
MARIA:
"I wish I didn't feel so guilty about needing time for myself. I feel like I have to be 'on' all the time."
YOU:
"Okay, that's a great starting point. Let's explore that guilt a bit. Where do you think that feeling is coming from?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to "Tune-In" before offering solutions?

Tuning in builds trust and rapport, allowing you to understand the client's needs and motivations deeply. This leads to more effective and personalized coaching.

2. Give an example of an open-ended question you could use to "Tune-In" with a client struggling with stress.

"What does stress feel like in your body?" or "What are the biggest sources of stress in your life right now?"

Key Takeaways

  • "Tuning-In" is about creating a safe and empathetic space for your client.
  • Resist the urge to offer solutions prematurely.
  • Use open-ended questions to encourage deeper exploration.
  • Pay attention to both verbal and non-verbal cues.
  • Validating your client's feelings is crucial for building trust.
