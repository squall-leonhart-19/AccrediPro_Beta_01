The Program Development Challenge

Sarah's Program Dilemma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I want to create a 6-week prenatal wellness program, but I'm overwhelmed. What topics should I cover? How much should I charge? How do I make it stand out?"

Your Guiding Questions:

  • What are Sarah's strengths and how can she leverage them?
  • What are the essential components of a comprehensive prenatal wellness program?
  • How can Sarah create a unique selling proposition (USP)?
  • What pricing strategies are appropriate for a new practitioner?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Focus on Core Pillars

Guide Sarah to structure her program around essential pillars: nutrition, movement, emotional well-being, and education. Prioritize the most impactful elements.

2

Leverage Her Strengths

Since Sarah is a former teacher, encourage her to incorporate interactive teaching methods, like group discussions and Q&A sessions. This will differentiate her program.

3

Pricing Strategies

Discuss various pricing models: value-based pricing, competitive pricing, and introductory offers. Help Sarah understand her worth and set realistic prices. Consider offering a discount for early sign-ups.

4

Unique Selling Proposition (USP)

Help Sarah identify what makes her program different. Does she specialize in a particular area of prenatal wellness? Does she offer unique resources or experiences? Her USP should be clear and compelling.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Encouragement
"Sarah, it's fantastic that you're taking the initiative to create a prenatal wellness program. That's a huge step! Let's break down the process together."
Ask About Her Vision
"Before we dive into specifics, what's the overall vision for your program? What transformation do you want to create for your clients?"
Share Practical Advice
"One strategy I found helpful was to focus on the 'quick wins' ‚Äì the most impactful changes clients can make early on. This builds momentum and keeps them engaged."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on our discussion, what are your initial thoughts on pricing and program structure? Remember, it's okay to experiment and adjust as you go."

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not dictating their actions.

  • Provide practical advice and resources to help them overcome challenges.
  • Celebrate their progress and empower them to make their own decisions.
