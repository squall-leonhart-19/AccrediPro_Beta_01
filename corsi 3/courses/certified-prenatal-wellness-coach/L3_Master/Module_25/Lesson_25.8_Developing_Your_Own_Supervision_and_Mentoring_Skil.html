The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Prenatal Anxiety

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 32 weeks pregnant, and she's really struggling with anxiety. I suggested a few relaxation techniques, but then I also told her about a specific herbal supplement that's supposed to be safe for pregnancy. Now I'm worried I shouldn't have recommended that!"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Was the herbal recommendation within Sarah's scope of practice?
  • Did Sarah adequately assess Emily's anxiety levels and potential underlying causes?
  • Were safer, evidence-based approaches explored first?
  • How can Sarah address her concerns and provide appropriate support to Emily?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reiterate Scope of Practice

Remind Sarah that as a Prenatal Wellness Coach, she does not prescribe or diagnose. Herbal supplement recommendations may fall outside her scope.

2

Emphasize Evidence-Based Approaches

Discuss the importance of prioritizing well-researched, safe, and non-invasive methods for managing prenatal anxiety, such as mindfulness, yoga, and peer support.

3

Review the Holistic Assessment Process

Guide Sarah to consider other factors contributing to Emily's anxiety, such as sleep patterns, diet, social support, and past trauma. A thorough assessment is key.

4

Teach Collaborative Care

Help Sarah understand the importance of collaborating with other healthcare professionals, such as midwives, doctors, or therapists, to provide comprehensive support for Emily.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for being so proactive and caring about your client's well-being. It's clear you want the best for Emily, and that's a fantastic quality."
Ask About Her Reasoning
"What led you to suggest the herbal supplement? What were you hoping it would achieve for Emily?"
Gently Correct the Course
"As coaches, we want to empower our clients with safe and effective tools. While some herbs might seem helpful, it's crucial to stay within our scope and prioritize evidence-based approaches. In this case, let's focus on relaxation techniques, mindfulness, and connecting Emily with professional mental health support."
Empower Her Next Steps
"How would you like to address this with Emily? What feels like the most authentic and supportive way to guide her towards safer, more appropriate options?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to discuss scope of practice with new coaches?

It protects both the coach and the client, ensuring that advice and interventions are appropriate and safe. It also helps maintain ethical standards within the profession.

2. What is the benefit of asking the mentee about their reasoning before offering advice?

It allows you to understand their thought process, identify any gaps in knowledge, and tailor your feedback to their specific needs. It also encourages critical thinking and self-reflection.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is about guiding, not directing.
  • Reinforce scope of practice and ethical boundaries.
  • Prioritize evidence-based approaches and collaborative care.
  • Empower new coaches to develop their critical thinking skills.
  • Celebrate their strengths and foster confidence.
