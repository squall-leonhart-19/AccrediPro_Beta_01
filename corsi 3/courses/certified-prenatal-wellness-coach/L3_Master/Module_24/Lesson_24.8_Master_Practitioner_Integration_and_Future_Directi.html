The Case Sarah Presents

Sarah's Client Case: Anxiety Management

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have a client, Emily, 35, who is 12 weeks pregnant and has a history of anxiety. She's worried about everything ‚Äì nutrition, exercise, sleep. I've given her some basic tips, but I'm afraid of saying the wrong thing or making her anxiety worse. How do I help her stay calm and confident without giving medical advice?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah clearly defined her scope of practice with Emily?
  • What specific resources has Sarah provided for anxiety management?
  • How is Sarah managing her own anxiety around this client's needs?
  • Is Sarah aware of the local resources available for prenatal mental health?

Your Teaching Approach: Key Points

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Remind Sarah that her role is to provide education, support, and resources, not to diagnose or treat anxiety. Emphasize the importance of clear boundaries.

2

Provide Anxiety-Specific Resources

Discuss evidence-based strategies like mindfulness, gentle yoga, and connecting with support groups. Share reputable websites and books on prenatal anxiety.

3

Address Sarah's Own Anxiety

Help Sarah recognize and manage her own feelings of overwhelm. Remind her that it's okay to refer clients to mental health professionals when needed.

4

Role-Play Communication Techniques

Practice using open-ended questions, reflective listening, and affirmations to help Emily feel heard and supported. For instance, "It sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed. What's one small thing we can focus on today?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Sarah, it's wonderful that you're so attentive to Emily's needs. It shows you're a caring practitioner. Let's explore how to best support her within your scope."
Ask Before Telling
"What are some specific strategies you've considered for helping Emily manage her anxiety? What feels most comfortable and effective for you?"
Teach the Principle
"Remember, darling, our role is to empower clients to make informed decisions and connect them with the right resources. We're not therapists, but we can be valuable sources of support and education."
Empower Next Steps
"What steps would you like to take with Emily in your next session? How can you clearly define your role and boundaries while still providing compassionate support?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to emphasize when mentoring a new practitioner dealing with a client who has anxiety?

The importance of staying within their scope of practice and providing appropriate resources, not attempting to diagnose or treat the anxiety directly.

2. How can you help a mentee manage their own anxiety about working with complex client cases?

Normalize feelings of overwhelm, encourage self-care, and emphasize the importance of setting boundaries and referring clients to other professionals when necessary.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners to develop their skills and confidence.
  • It's crucial to reinforce the importance of scope of practice and ethical boundaries.
  • Provide resources, support, and constructive feedback to help mentees grow.
  • Address the mentee's own feelings of anxiety and overwhelm.
  • Role-playing and communication techniques can be valuable tools in mentoring.
