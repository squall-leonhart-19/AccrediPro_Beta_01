Handling Objections with Confidence

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can commit to 12 weeks."
"I understand that committing to a 12-week program can feel like a big step. Let's break it down. What specifically concerns you about the time commitment? Perhaps we can adjust the schedule or tailor the program to fit your needs better. Remember, investing in your well-being now can prevent bigger challenges down the road."
"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"That's perfectly reasonable. It's important to make this decision together. Would you like me to provide you with a summary of the program that you can share with your partner? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call where all three of us can discuss any questions or concerns."
"I'm worried about the cost."
"I appreciate your candor. Let's talk about the value you'll receive from this program. Think about the potential cost of NOT addressing your concerns ‚Äì the emotional toll, the impact on your relationships, and the potential for postpartum depression. My program is an investment in your long-term well-being, and I offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I've tried other things before, and they didn't work."
"I hear your frustration. It's discouraging when previous attempts haven't yielded results. My approach is different because it's personalized to your specific needs and focuses on addressing the root causes of your challenges. I also provide ongoing support and accountability to help you stay on track. I'm confident that together, we can create a different outcome this time."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's needs, and presenting a clear solution.

2. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your program?

Focus on the value they'll receive, the potential cost of not addressing their concerns, and offer flexible payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your program and its potential impact on your clients' lives.
  • Believe in your ability to help your clients achieve their goals.
  • Remember, every successful practitioner started where you are today.
