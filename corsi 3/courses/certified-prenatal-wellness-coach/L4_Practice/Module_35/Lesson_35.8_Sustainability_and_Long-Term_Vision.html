Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals or new mothers. The sessions are designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find times that work best for you, and the yoga practices can be done at home in as little as 15-20 minutes."
"It's too expensive, especially after IVF."
"I hear you, Susan. The investment in your health and well-being during this critical time is so important. Let's look at it this way: how much would it be worth to you to reduce your stress, have a healthier pregnancy, and feel more prepared for labor and delivery? Investing in this program now can potentially save you money on interventions later, and the peace of mind is invaluable."
"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Would it be helpful if I sent you a detailed outline of the program and its benefits that you can share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions."
"I'm not sure if online coaching is right for me."
"I understand your hesitation. Many of my clients were initially skeptical about online coaching, but they've been pleasantly surprised by how effective and convenient it is. You get personalized support and guidance from the comfort of your own home, and we can still build a strong connection through video calls and regular communication."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport at the start of a discovery call?

Expressing gratitude, actively listening, finding common ground, and genuinely showing interest in the prospect.

2. How can you address the objection "It's too expensive" in a value-driven way?

Reframe the cost as an investment in their health and well-being, highlight the long-term benefits, and offer payment options. Focus on the return on investment (ROI) in terms of improved health, reduced stress, and increased confidence.

You're Ready to Confidently Acquire Clients!

Key Takeaways

  • Building strong rapport is essential for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's problems and goals is crucial for tailoring your approach and demonstrating the value of your services.
  • Handling objections effectively requires empathy, active listening, and a focus on the benefits of your program.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence and offering flexible payment options increases the likelihood of closing the sale.
  • Consistent practice and a positive mindset are key to mastering the art of client acquisition.
