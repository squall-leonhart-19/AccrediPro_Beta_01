Overcoming Objections: Your Confident Responses

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"I understand. It's important to make these decisions together. Would it be helpful if I sent you some information to share with your partner? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions?"
"I'm not sure I have the time for this."
"I hear you. Time is precious, especially with a new baby. My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your busy life. We can tailor the plan to your schedule and priorities, and I'll provide ongoing support to keep you on track. Remember, investing in your well-being will ultimately give you MORE energy and time for your family."
"It's a bit more than I was expecting to spend."
"I appreciate your honesty. Let's break down the investment. When you consider the personalized plan, ongoing support, and potential results, it's truly an investment in your long-term health and well-being. Think of the costs associated with continued struggles ‚Äì doctor visits, supplements that don't work, lost productivity. My program is designed to get you lasting results, so you can avoid those recurring expenses."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes (as results vary based on individual factors), I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the best possible support, a personalized plan tailored to your needs, and evidence-based strategies to help you achieve your goals. My clients consistently report significant improvements in their energy levels, weight management, and overall well-being."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a 30-minute discovery call, and what is the purpose of each phase?

The key phases are: 1) Rapport & Connection (building trust), 2) Uncover the Problem (identifying client's challenges), 3) Present Your Solution (explaining your approach), and 4) Close & Next Steps (inviting client to join your program).

2. How should you handle the objection, "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge the client's concern, break down the value of your program, compare it to the costs of not addressing their challenges, and emphasize the long-term investment in their health and well-being.

3. What are some realistic income goals for a new prenatal wellness coach, and how many clients are needed to achieve those goals?

Realistic income goals can range from $5,000 to $25,000 per month, depending on the number of clients acquired. This can be achieved by securing 2-10 clients per month, assuming an average program price of $2,500.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into paying clients.
  • Effective objection handling builds trust and addresses client concerns.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing demonstrates the value of your services.
  • Consistent client acquisition is essential for building athriving practice.
  • Believe in your ability to make a positive impact and achieve your income goals.
