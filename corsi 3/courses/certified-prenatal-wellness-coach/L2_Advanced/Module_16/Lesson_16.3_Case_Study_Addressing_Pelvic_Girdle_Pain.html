Case Study: Sarah, 35 Years Old

Sarah's Story

Client: Sarah, 35 years old, 24 weeks pregnant with her second child.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reports experiencing sharp, stabbing pain in her lower back and pubic area for the past 4 weeks. The pain is exacerbated by walking, climbing stairs, and turning in bed. She rates her pain as a 7 out of 10 on most days. Sarah also mentions feeling frustrated and anxious about her limitations, as she struggles to keep up with her toddler.

Relevant History: Sarah experienced mild back pain during her first pregnancy, but it resolved after delivery. She has no history of significant musculoskeletal injuries or conditions.

Intervention: Using the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢, we conducted a comprehensive assessment to understand the totality of Sarah's experience. This included evaluating her pain levels, mobility, emotional well-being, sleep patterns, and daily activities. Based on the assessment, we developed an individualized plan that incorporated gentle exercises, postural adjustments, ergonomic modifications, and the use of a pelvic support belt.

Outcomes: After 4 weeks of implementing the plan, Sarah reported a significant reduction in pain levels, with an average rating of 3 out of 10. She also noted improved mobility, sleep quality, and overall mood. Sarah expressed feeling more confident and in control of her body. She was able to resume many of her daily activities with greater ease and comfort.

Applying the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢: Holistic Assessment

Let's break down how the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢ can be applied to assess and support clients with PGP:

  • T - Trauma & Tension: Explore any history of physical or emotional trauma that may be contributing to muscle tension and pain sensitivity.
  • H - Hormones: Understand the role of hormonal changes during pregnancy in joint laxity and pain perception. Relaxin levels, for example, peak in the first trimester, contributing to increased joint mobility (Kristiansson et al., 1999).
  • R - Rest & Recovery: Assess sleep quality, stress levels, and opportunities for rest and relaxation.
  • I - Inflammation: Evaluate dietary habits and lifestyle factors that may be contributing to inflammation in the body.
  • V - Vitals & Biomechanics: Analyze posture, gait, and movement patterns to identify biomechanical imbalances contributing to PGP.
  • E - Emotional Well-being: Address any feelings of anxiety, frustration, or isolation associated with PGP.

A thorough assessment using the T.H.R.I.V.E. Frameworkâ„¢ allows you to create a truly individualized plan that addresses the root causes of PGP and promotes overall well-being.

Individualized Plan: Gentle Exercises & Postural Adjustments

Based on your comprehensive assessment, develop an individualized plan that incorporates the following elements:

  • Gentle Exercises: Focus on core stabilization, pelvic floor strengthening, and hip mobility exercises. Examples include pelvic tilts, bridges, and bird-dog exercises. A 2015 systematic review in the journal Manual Therapy found that exercise therapy significantly reduces pain and improves function in women with PGP (VÃ¸llestad & Stuge, 2015).
  • Postural Adjustments: Educate clients on maintaining proper posture during sitting, standing, and lifting activities. Encourage the use of ergonomic supports, such as lumbar cushions and footrests.
  • Ergonomic Modifications: Advise clients on modifying their home and work environments to reduce strain on the pelvic girdle. This may include adjusting chair height, using assistive devices for reaching, and avoiding prolonged sitting or standing.

Assistive Devices: Pelvic Support Belts

Pelvic support belts can provide external stability and support to the pelvic girdle, reducing pain and improving function. A randomized controlled trial published in the British Medical Journal found that pelvic support belts significantly reduced pain intensity and improved functional capacity in pregnant women with PGP (Stuge et al., 2004). When recommending a pelvic support belt, consider the following:

  • Proper Fit: Ensure the belt fits snugly and comfortably, providing adequate support without restricting movement.
  • Usage Guidelines: Advise clients to wear the belt during activities that exacerbate their pain, such as walking or standing for extended periods.
  • Complementary Therapies: Emphasize that pelvic support belts are most effective when combined with exercise therapy and postural adjustments.

Client Education: Body Mechanics & Pain Management

Empowering clients with knowledge and self-management strategies is a crucial aspect of prenatal wellness coaching. Educate clients on the following:

  • Proper Body Mechanics: Teach clients how to lift, bend, and twist safely to minimize strain on the pelvic girdle.
  • Pain Management Techniques: Introduce clients to various pain management techniques, such as heat or cold therapy, gentle stretching, and relaxation exercises.
  • Activity Modification: Help clients identify activities that aggravate their pain and develop strategies for modifying or avoiding them.
  • Importance of Rest: Emphasize the importance of getting adequate rest and prioritizing self-care activities.
