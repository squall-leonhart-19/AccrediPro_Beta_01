Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to start the "Resource & Reframe" conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for sharing your heart with me. It's clear how much Ginger meant to you. Before we go further, let's pause and acknowledge the depth of your love for her. It's okay to feel this pain; it's a testament to the beautiful bond you shared."
DIANE:
"It just hurts so much. I miss her every minute of every day."
YOU:
"I understand. And it's natural to feel that way. Now, I'd like to help you tap into some of the strength and resilience you already possess. Think back to a time in your life when you faced a significant challenge and overcame it. What helped you through that?"
DIANE:
"Well, when I went through my divorce, I leaned heavily on my friends and my artwork. Painting helped me express what I couldn't put into words."
YOU:
"That's wonderful, Diane. It sounds like you've already developed healthy coping mechanisms. How might you incorporate those resources ‚Äì your friends and your art ‚Äì into your grieving process for Ginger?"
YOU:
"Also, let's reframe some of the guilt you mentioned. Instead of focusing on what you think you *didn't* do for Ginger, let's celebrate all the love, care, and joy you *did* provide her throughout her life. Can you think of a specific happy memory you shared with Ginger?"
