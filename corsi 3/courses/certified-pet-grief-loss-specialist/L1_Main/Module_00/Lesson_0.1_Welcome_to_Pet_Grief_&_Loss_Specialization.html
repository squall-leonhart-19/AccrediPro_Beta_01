What You'll Learn & Become

Over the next few weeks, you'll gain the knowledge, skills, and credentials to confidently support grieving pet parents. This isn't just about learning theory; it's about developing practical tools and techniques that you can use to make a real difference in people's lives. You'll learn:

  1. The unique nuances of pet loss grief and how it differs from other types of grief.
  2. Evidence-based counseling techniques for supporting grieving pet parents, including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT).
  3. How to create a safe and supportive space for clients to share their feelings and experiences.
  4. Strategies for helping clients cope with guilt, shame, and other complex emotions related to pet loss.
  5. The importance of self-care and boundary setting to prevent burnout.
  6. How to market your services and build a successful practice as a Certified Pet Grief & Loss Specialist‚Ñ¢.

Upon completion of this program, you'll not only receive your certification, but you'll also have the confidence and competence to:

  • Provide individual and group counseling to grieving pet parents.
  • Facilitate pet loss support groups.
  • Offer educational workshops and presentations on pet loss grief.
  • Collaborate with veterinary professionals and animal shelters to provide grief support services.
  • Create online resources and content to reach a wider audience of grieving pet parents.

A 2023 meta-analysis published in *PLoS One* examined the effectiveness of various grief interventions for pet loss.   1. Explore the Course Platform: Take some time to familiarize yourself with the course platform. Watch the introductory video, read the course syllabus, and explore the resources available to you.
