Clinical Reasoning Process

Deconstructing the Case

Step 1: Identify Key Issues
Eleanor presents with complicated grief compounded by multiple losses in a short period. Her anxiety, insomnia, and social withdrawal suggest potential major depressive disorder. The use of alcohol as a coping mechanism raises concerns for substance use.
Step 2: Assess Trauma and Attachment
Her husband's sudden death was likely traumatic. Explore her attachment style and relationship history to understand her coping mechanisms and relational patterns. The miscarriage could also be a source of unresolved grief and trauma.
Step 3: Evaluate Resilience Factors
Despite her challenges, Eleanor is a high-functioning professional. Identify her strengths, coping skills, and sources of support (or lack thereof). Assess her motivation for change and willingness to engage in therapy.
Step 4: Formulate a Hypothesis
Eleanor's grief has become chronic and complicated by unresolved trauma, potential depression, and unhealthy coping mechanisms. A comprehensive and integrative approach is needed to address her multiple layers of loss and promote healing.

Differential Considerations

Ruling Out Other Factors

1

Major Depressive Disorder

Differentiate between normal grief and clinical depression. Use standardized assessment tools (e.g., PHQ-9, GAD-7) to evaluate symptom severity and duration. A score of 15+ on the PHQ-9 suggests moderate to severe depression, warranting further evaluation.

2

Substance Use Disorder

Assess Eleanor's alcohol consumption patterns and potential dependence. Use the AUDIT-C questionnaire to screen for problematic drinking. A score of 4+ for women indicates a high likelihood of alcohol misuse.

3

Complex Trauma (C-PTSD)

Explore potential symptoms of C-PTSD related to her husband's sudden death and other losses. Look for difficulties with emotional regulation, distorted self-perception, and relationship challenges. Use the International Trauma Questionnaire (ITQ) to assess for C-PTSD symptoms.

4

Medical Conditions

Rule out underlying medical conditions that could be contributing to her symptoms (e.g., thyroid issues, vitamin deficiencies). Encourage her to consult with her primary care physician for a comprehensive evaluation.

Red Flags â€“ When to Seek External Support

  • Suicidal ideation or intent: Immediate referral to a mental health crisis hotline or emergency services.
  • Severe substance use disorder: Referral to a substance abuse treatment center or addiction specialist.
  • Psychotic symptoms (e.g., hallucinations, delusions): Referral to a psychiatrist for evaluation and medication management.
  • Underlying medical conditions requiring medical intervention: Referral to her primary care physician or a specialist.
  • Lack of progress in therapy despite consistent effort: Consultation with a more experienced therapist or a different therapeutic approach may be warranted.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key considerations when assessing a client presenting with multiple losses and symptoms of complicated grief?

Consider the timing and nature of the losses, assess for trauma and attachment issues, evaluate resilience factors, and rule out other potential mental health conditions (e.g., depression, substance use disorder).

2. How can EMDR be used to address traumatic grief in a client like Eleanor?

EMDR can help process traumatic memories associated with her husband's sudden death, reduce anxiety and emotional distress, and promote adaptive resolution of the grief. It's important to ensure the client is stabilized and has adequate coping skills before initiating EMDR.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex grief requires a comprehensive and integrative approach addressing multiple layers of loss and trauma.
  • Thorough assessment is essential to differentiate between normal grief, complicated grief, and other mental health conditions.
  • Trauma-informed interventions, such as EMDR, can be highly effective in processing traumatic memories and promoting healing.
  • Building social support, fostering resilience, and creating a vision for the future are crucial for long-term recovery and growth.
