Former teacher, excellent communication skills, limited grief experience.

Empathetic listener, quick learner, dedicated to client well-being.
Growth Areas
Needs help setting boundaries, managing transference, and handling complex grief.
Her Question
"I feel overwhelmed by my client's intense emotions. Am I doing enough?"

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 52, whose dog died unexpectedly. Emily is inconsolable, constantly calls me, and says she can't function without her dog. I'm starting to feel drained and guilty that I can't 'fix' her grief."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Is Sarah setting appropriate boundaries with Emily?
  • Is Sarah experiencing countertransference?
  • Has Sarah assessed for complicated grief?
  • Does Emily have a support system beyond Sarah?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Acknowledge Her Feelings

Validate Sarah's feelings of overwhelm. "It's normal to feel drained when working with intense grief."

2

Review Boundary Setting

Discuss setting clear boundaries: session length, communication methods, and availability. Emphasize self-care.

3

Explain Transference/Countertransference

Help Sarah recognize if Emily is projecting feelings onto her (transference) and if Sarah is reacting emotionally (countertransference).

4

Assess for Complicated Grief

Guide Sarah on using assessment tools to identify signs of complicated grief requiring specialized intervention.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, I commend you for seeking supervision. Recognizing your limitations is a strength, not a weakness. Let's break this down together."
Explore Her Perspective
"What strategies have you tried so far to manage Emily's calls and your own emotional response? What feels sustainable for you?"
Teach Boundary Principles
"Remember, boundaries aren't about rejecting the client, but about protecting both of you. Let's role-play how you might gently set limits with Emily."
Empower Her Decisions
"Based on our discussion, what changes will you implement in your next session with Emily? I'm confident you can navigate this ethically and effectively."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate the mentee's feelings before offering solutions?

Validating their feelings builds trust and creates a safe space for open communication. It helps the mentee feel heard and understood, making them more receptive to feedback.

2. What are some signs that a mentee might be experiencing countertransference?

Signs include feeling overly emotionally involved with the client, having difficulty setting boundaries, experiencing intrusive thoughts about the client, or feeling resentful toward the client.
