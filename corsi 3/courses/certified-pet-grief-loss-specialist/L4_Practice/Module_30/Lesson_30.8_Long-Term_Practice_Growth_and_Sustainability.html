Your Prospect

Susan, 52

Found you through your blog. Interested in your "Navigating Pet Loss" program.

Her Situation
Recently lost her golden retriever, Buster. Feeling isolated and overwhelmed with grief. Has adult children who are supportive but don't fully understand.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure I can afford it, but I'm desperate for help."
Decision Style
Emotional decision-maker. Needs to feel understood and supported. Values authenticity and empathy.
Her Goal
"I want to find a way to honor Buster's memory and move forward without feeling so much pain."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Validation 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out and sharing your story. I'm so sorry for your loss of Buster. It sounds like he was a very special part of your life."
YOU:
"I understand how difficult this time can be. Many of my clients feel similarly overwhelmed and lost. It's completely normal to grieve deeply for a pet."
YOU:
"Tell me a bit more about Buster. What are some of your favorite memories with him?"
Phase 2: Uncovering Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"What has been the hardest part of losing Buster?"
YOU:
"What kind of support are you looking for right now?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like in a few months, after you've had time to grieve and heal?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Program 8 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're seeking support to navigate your grief, honor Buster's memory, and find a path forward. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My 'Navigating Pet Loss' program is designed to provide exactly that. Over eight weeks, we'll work together to process your grief, develop coping strategies, create meaningful rituals, and connect with a supportive community. Many of my clients find that they begin to feel a sense of peace and healing within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"The program includes weekly one-on-one sessions, access to a private online forum, and personalized resources to help you through your journey."
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this program sound like it could be a good fit for what you're looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! I'd love to share the details about the investment and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Life can be busy. The weekly sessions are just one hour, and the online forum is available whenever you have a few minutes. Think of this as an investment in your well-being. What if dedicating this time to your healing allowed you to be more present and energized in other areas of your life?"
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I appreciate your honesty. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. This program isn't just about coping with grief; it's about transforming your pain into a celebration of Buster's life and finding renewed joy. Also, I offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make a confident decision. Also, I have a client, Maria, a 48 year old woman who was in the same position as you, she wasn't sure it was the right thing to do, but she ended up joining and found that it was the best decision she could have made."
"I'm not sure online support is right for me."
"I understand. It can feel different than in-person support. However, many of my clients find the online format to be incredibly convenient and supportive. You can participate from the comfort of your own home, and the online forum provides a sense of community and connection with others who understand what you're going through."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, uncovering needs, presenting your solution, and inviting them to take the next step.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the value they'll receive, and offer payment options.
