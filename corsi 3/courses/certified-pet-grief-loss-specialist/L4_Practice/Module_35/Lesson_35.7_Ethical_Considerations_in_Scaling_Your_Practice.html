Conflicts of interest can arise as your practice grows. Case Study: Ethical Dilemma in a Growing Practice

Client: Emily R., 35 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Emily sought grief counseling after the loss of her cat, Whiskers. She had been a client for several months and made significant progress.

The Dilemma: As your practice grew, you hired a new therapist, Sarah, who happened to be a close friend of Emily. You realized this could create a conflict of interest.

Intervention: You immediately disclosed the potential conflict to Emily, explaining that Sarah's personal connection could affect the therapeutic relationship. You offered Emily the option to continue with you or transfer to another therapist in your practice or externally.

Outcomes: Emily appreciated your transparency. She chose to continue with you, feeling more comfortable with the established relationship. You documented the disclosure and Emily's decision in her file, ensuring ethical compliance.

Case Study: Addressing a Complaint

Client: David L., 48 years old

Presenting Issue: David complained that he felt rushed during his sessions and that the therapist seemed distracted.

Intervention: You immediately acknowledged David's complaint and apologized for his experience. You scheduled a meeting with David to discuss his concerns in detail. You also reviewed the therapist's session notes and provided additional training on active listening and time management.

Outcomes: David appreciated the prompt response and the changes you implemented. He continued therapy and reported a significant improvement in his experience. You also used this feedback to improve training for all therapists in your practice.
