Your Prospect

Susan, 52

Lost her beloved dog, Max, 6 months ago. Feeling stuck in her grief. Found you through a local pet group on Facebook.

Her Situation
Difficulty sleeping, loss of appetite, isolating from friends and family. Feels overwhelmed by sadness.
Budget Concern
"I'm not sure I can afford this right now, but I also don't know how much longer I can feel like this."
Decision Style
Values compassion and understanding. Needs to feel safe and supported. A bit hesitant to be vulnerable.
Her Goal
"I want to be able to remember Max with joy, not just pain. I want to feel like myself again."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Validate 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. I understand you're grieving the loss of Max. I'm so sorry for your loss. It takes courage to seek support, and I appreciate you being here. Can you tell me a little bit about Max?"
YOU:
"Six months is a long time to feel this way. What has been the most difficult part of your grief journey so far?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"What does a typical day look like for you now? How has Max's absence changed your routine?"
YOU:
"You mentioned isolating from friends. How has your social life been affected since Max passed?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being 'completely overwhelmed' and 10 being 'feeling at peace,' where would you rate your current state of grief?"
Phase 3: Introduce Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing complex grief, which is completely normal after such a profound loss. My program is designed to help you navigate these intense emotions and find healthy ways to cope."
YOU:
"I offer a 10-week personalized grief support program. We'll explore coping mechanisms, create a memorial project for Max, and help you reconnect with your joy. The program includes weekly 1:1 sessions, email support, and access to guided meditations. Many of my clients find that they start to experience relief within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"Would you be open to learning more about how my program can help you specifically?"
Phase 4: Close with Confidence 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this sound like something that resonates with you and your needs? I truly believe I can help you move forward in your grief journey. Let's discuss the next steps."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I'm ready to talk about Max yet."
"I understand. It's okay to feel hesitant. My program is designed to be gentle and supportive. We'll move at your pace. We can start by focusing on your current feelings and gradually explore your memories of Max when you feel comfortable. There's no pressure to share anything you're not ready for."
"I've tried therapy before, and it didn't help."
"I'm sorry to hear that. My approach is different from traditional therapy. I specialize in pet loss grief, and I provide personalized support and practical tools to help you cope. My program focuses on honoring your bond with Max and finding ways to integrate your loss into your life. It's not just about talking; it's about taking action and creating a meaningful healing journey."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand that the investment is a consideration. Think about what it's costing you to stay stuck in your grief. What is it costing you in terms of your sleep, your appetite, your relationships, your overall quality of life? My program is designed to provide lasting relief and help you reclaim your joy. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial commitment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right decision for yourself. I'm happy to answer any further questions you may have."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call?

Expressing empathy, actively listening, validating the client's feelings, and creating a safe space for them to share their story.

2. How can you address the "It's too expensive" objection effectively?

Acknowledge their concern, help them see the value of your program, offer payment plans, and highlight the long-term benefits of overcoming their grief.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport and validating the client's feelings are crucial for establishing trust.
  • Deeply understanding the client's problem allows you to tailor your solution to their specific needs.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and addressing objections are essential for closing sales.
  • Focus on the value and transformation you provide to help clients make the right decision.
  • Practice your discovery call script and closing lines to feel more comfortable and confident.
