Budget Concern
"I'm willing to invest in help, but I need to know it's going to make a real difference."

Values compassion and expertise. Needs to feel understood and supported.

"I want to find peace and learn to cope with this loss without feeling overwhelmed."

Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Connection & Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you so much for reaching out. I'm Olivia, and I'm so sorry to hear about the loss of Whiskers. Seventeen years is a lifetime of love. To start, can you tell me a little more about what led you to contact me?"
YOU:
"It's completely understandable to feel overwhelmed right now. What have you found most challenging in coping with this loss?"
Phase 2: Explore Susan's Needs & Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe a typical day since Whiskers passed? What's been most different or difficult?"
YOU:
"Have you experienced pet loss before? What kind of support have you sought in the past, if any?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and feel differently about this situation, what would that look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solutions 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing a profound sense of loss and isolation, and you're looking for a way to honor Whiskers' memory while finding peace. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on creating a safe space for you to grieve, validating your emotions, and providing practical tools to cope with your loss. We'll explore ways to memorialize Whiskers, reframe your thoughts, and build a support system. I offer a 10-week program tailored to individual needs, and many clients report feeling significantly better within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
Phase 4: Transition to Program Details & Close 5 min
YOU:
"That's great to hear, Susan. Let me share some more details about the program and how we can work together. It includes weekly one-on-one sessions, personalized resources, and ongoing support."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Losing a pet is already a huge emotional burden, and financial concerns can add to that. Can I ask, what kind of budget did you have in mind for support during this time? We may be able to explore a payment plan or adjust the program to fit your needs. Ultimately, think about the value of finding peace and healing during this difficult time."
"I'm worried it won't really help."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. It's important to find a solution that truly resonates with you. I can share testimonials from other clients who've experienced similar losses and found significant relief through the program. Also, consider that the program provides personalized strategies and ongoing support, which can make a real difference in your healing journey. We can also adjust the program as we go to ensure it meets your needs."
"I need to think about it and discuss it with my family."
"Of course, Susan. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel completely comfortable. What specific questions or concerns do you want to discuss with your family? Perhaps I can provide some information to help with that conversation. Would it be helpful to schedule a brief follow-up call where your family members can ask any questions they may have?"
"I've tried other things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear you, Susan. It can be frustrating when previous attempts haven't provided the relief you were hoping for. My approach is different in that it focuses on [mention key differentiators, e.g., personalized support, holistic approach, specific techniques]. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so if you don't feel like you're getting value from the program, we can discuss a refund. Are you willing to give it another try, knowing that this approach is tailored specifically to your needs?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's needs, presenting your approach, and transitioning to the close.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, ask what specifically they need to think about, and offer additional information or a follow-up call.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for building trust with potential clients.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and handling objections are key to closing the sale.
