Discovery Call Script: Initial Consultation

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Introduction & Clarification 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for reaching out! I understand you're looking to set up your pet grief support practice. Let's start by clarifying your background and goals for the practice."
YOU:
"Can you tell me about your experience as a vet tech and how you envision integrating that into your counseling practice?"
Phase 2: Legal & Ethical Considerations 15 min
YOU:
"Let's discuss the importance of defining your scope of practice. As a pet grief specialist, it's crucial to understand what you can and cannot offer. We need to ensure you're not providing medical advice or psychological therapy if you're not licensed to do so."
YOU:
"We'll also cover informed consent. It's essential that clients understand the nature of your services, your qualifications, and the limitations of pet grief support."
YOU:
"Additionally, we'll explore data privacy and confidentiality. How will you protect client information and ensure compliance with relevant regulations like HIPAA (if applicable)?"
Phase 3: Actionable Steps & Resources 5 min
YOU:
"Based on our discussion, the first step is to create a detailed service agreement that outlines your services, fees, and limitations. I can provide you with a template to get started."
YOU:
"I also recommend consulting with a legal professional to ensure your practice complies with all local and state regulations."
Phase 4: Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, does this sound like the support you're looking for? If so, we can schedule a follow-up session to dive deeper into these areas."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I need all this legal stuff. Can't I just start offering support?"
"I understand the desire to start helping people right away. However, establishing a solid legal and ethical foundation protects both you and your clients. It ensures you're providing services responsibly and avoiding potential legal issues down the road."
"It seems expensive to consult with a lawyer."
"While there is an upfront cost, think of it as an investment in your practice's long-term success and stability. Legal consultations can help you avoid costly mistakes and ensure you're operating within the bounds of the law."
"I'm not sure how to define my scope of practice."
"That's perfectly normal! We can work together to identify your strengths, qualifications, and limitations. I'll help you create a clear and ethical framework for your services."
"I don't have time for all this paperwork."
"I understand that time is precious. We can streamline the process by using templates and focusing on the most critical areas. Remember, investing time in compliance now will save you time and stress in the future."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to define your scope of practice as a pet grief specialist?

Defining your scope of practice ensures you are providing services within your expertise and avoiding practicing outside your qualifications, which could lead to legal and ethical issues.

2. What are some key elements to include in a service agreement with clients?

A service agreement should include a clear description of services, fees, cancellation policies, confidentiality clauses, and a disclaimer outlining the limitations of pet grief support.

Key Takeaways

  • Establish a clear scope of practice to ensure you are providing services within your expertise.
  • Create comprehensive service agreements that outline your services, fees, and limitations.
  • Prioritize data privacy and confidentiality to protect client information.
  • Consult with legal professionals to ensure compliance with local and state regulations.
  • Remember, ethical and legal compliance is an investment in your practice's long-term success.
