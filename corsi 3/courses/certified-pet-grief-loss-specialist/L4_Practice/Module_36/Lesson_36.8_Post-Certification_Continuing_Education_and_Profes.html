Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Acknowledge Loss 7 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out to me during this difficult time. I'm so sorry to hear about the loss of Max. Fourteen years is a lifetime of love and companionship. Before we talk about how I can help, I want to acknowledge your grief and offer my deepest sympathies."
YOU:
"I understand you found me through the Facebook group. Was there something specific that resonated with you in my posts or profile?"
YOU:
"It takes courage to reach out for support. Thank you for trusting me with your story."
Phase 2: Understand the Impact of Grief 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, can you tell me more about how the loss of Max is affecting you day to day? What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing right now?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to cope with your grief? Have you found anything that's been helpful, even in small ways?"
YOU:
"I understand you have other pets. How are they doing? Are they sensing your grief?"

"If you could wave a magic wand and feel differently about this loss, what would that look like? What kind of support would be most meaningful to you?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I'm ready to talk about it yet. It's still so raw."
"I completely understand. There's no pressure to start before you're ready. My program is designed to meet you where you are in your grief journey. We can start slowly, focusing on creating a safe space and building trust. It's okay to take baby steps. The important thing is that you're taking steps towards healing."
"I'm worried it won't work for me. Every pet is different, and my bond with Max was so special."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. And you're right, every pet and every relationship is unique. My program isn't about replacing Max or minimizing your loss. It's about honoring that unique bond and finding healthy ways to integrate Max's memory into your life. I tailor my approach to each individual client, so we'll work together to create a plan that's specific to your needs and your relationship with Max."
"I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I understand that cost is a factor. Let's talk about the investment and what's included in the program. Many people find that investing in their emotional well-being is one of the most valuable things they can do. Think about the cost of staying stuck in your grief ‚Äì the impact on your relationships, your health, your overall quality of life. We can also explore payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. This is a big decision, and I want you to feel completely comfortable. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else? I'm happy to answer any questions you have to help you make the right choice for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport in the first few minutes of a discovery call with a grieving pet owner?

Acknowledge their loss with genuine empathy, express your condolences, and create a safe space for them to share their feelings. Show that you understand the depth of their bond with their pet.

2. How can you confidently address a prospect 's concerns about the cost of your program?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the value and benefits of the program, discuss payment plan options, and reframe the investment as an investment in their emotional well-being and overall quality of life.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process can significantly increase your conversion rates.
  • Empathy and active listening are crucial for building rapport with grieving clients.
  • Addressing objections with confidence and understanding can alleviate client concerns.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and the value of your program is essential.
  • A strong call-to-action encourages clients to take the next step in their healing journey.
