Discovery Call Script: Your 30-Minute Roadmap

Your Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for reaching out. I'm Olivia, and I'm so sorry to hear about Max. It takes courage to seek support during such a difficult time. How are you doing today, all things considered?"
YOU:
"I understand that losing a pet is like losing a member of the family, if you don't mind me asking, what was Max like? What made him so special?"
Phase 2: Exploring Sarah's Needs (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, can you tell me more about what you've been experiencing since Max passed? What does a typical day look like for you right now?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to cope with your grief? Have you spoken to friends, family, or a professional? What has been helpful, and what hasn't?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being completely overwhelmed and 10 being peaceful and content, where would you rate your current level of coping?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and feel differently, what would that look like? What's your ideal outcome from working with someone like me?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Services (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for sharing. It sounds like you're going through immense pain and are looking for a way to navigate this grief. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"The program I offer is designed to provide you with personalized support and practical tools to process your grief in a healthy way. We'll work together over 8 weeks to explore your feelings, identify coping mechanisms, and create a path toward healing and acceptance. Many clients report feeling a sense of relief and renewed hope within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"This involves weekly one-on-one sessions, personalized exercises, and ongoing support between sessions. It's a holistic approach, focusing on your emotional, mental, and spiritual well-being during this transition."
Phase 4: Invitation & Closing (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Sarah, I genuinely believe I can help you through this difficult time. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"

Handling Objections with Grace

Anticipate and Address Concerns

"I'm not sure I can afford this right now."
"I understand. Losing a pet brings unexpected emotional AND financial burdens. Let's explore this a bit. What part of the investment feels most challenging? We can discuss payment plan options or adjust the program to better fit your budget. My priority is ensuring you receive the support you need."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. This is a significant decision. What aspects do you need more time to consider? Perhaps I can provide additional information or address any remaining questions you have right now."
"I'm not sure if therapy/coaching is right for me."
"That's perfectly valid. Many people feel hesitant initially. My approach isn't about 'fixing' you, but rather about providing a safe space for you to process your grief and develop healthy coping strategies. Would you like to hear about some of my previous clients' experiences?"
"I feel like I should be able to handle this on my own."
"It's admirable that you're self-reliant. However, grief can be incredibly isolating. Seeking support doesn't mean you're weak; it means you're proactive about your well-being. Think of it as having a compassionate guide during a difficult journey."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's needs, presenting your services as a solution, handling objections, and confidently stating your price.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your program?

Acknowledge their concern, explore the specific financial challenges, offer payment plan options, and emphasize the value and potential long-term benefits of your services.

3. What is the importance of practicing your closing lines?

Practicing your closing lines allows you to deliver them confidently and naturally, increasing the likelihood of converting a prospect into a paying client.

Key Takeaways

  • Client acquisition calls are crucial for building a sustainable practice.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for establishing trust and understanding client needs.
  • Confidently address objections and present your services as a valuable solution.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines to increase conversion rates.
  • Remember, every successful practitioner started where you are. With practice and dedication, you can build a thriving pet grief and loss support practice.
