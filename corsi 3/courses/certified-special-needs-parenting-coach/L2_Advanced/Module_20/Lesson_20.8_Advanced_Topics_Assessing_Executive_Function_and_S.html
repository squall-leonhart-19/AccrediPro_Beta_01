Clinical Reasoning Process

Work Through the Case

Step 1: Identify Key Systems
This case involves multiple interconnected systems: neurological (migraines, brain fog, ADHD), endocrine (Hashimoto's), gastrointestinal (IBS), and psychological (anxiety, irritability). The key is to understand how these systems influence each other.
Step 2: Consider Underlying Mechanisms
Potential mechanisms include: Chronic inflammation (Hashimoto's), gut-brain axis dysfunction (IBS influencing mood and cognition), hormonal imbalances (potentially related to stress and thyroid), and neurotransmitter imbalances (affecting migraines and ADHD).
Step 3: Prioritize Assessment Tools
Given the complexity, prioritize:
  • Migraine Disability Assessment Scale (MIDAS): Quantifies migraine-related disability.
  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item (GAD-7): Screens for anxiety severity.
  • Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS-v1.1): Assesses ADHD symptoms.
  • Comprehensive Stool Analysis: Evaluates gut microbiome and digestive function.
  • Food Sensitivity Testing: Identifies potential dietary triggers.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Cervical Spine Issues

Migraines can be caused or exacerbated by cervical spine misalignment or muscle tension. Consider a referral to a physical therapist specializing in headache management.

2

Hormonal Imbalances (Beyond Thyroid)

Perimenopause could be contributing to mood swings, migraines, and cognitive issues. Consider hormone testing.

3

Nutrient Deficiencies

Migraines and thyroid function can be affected by deficiencies in magnesium, B vitamins, and iron. Further testing may be warranted.

4

Mold Exposure

Chronic exposure to mold can cause neurological and immune symptoms. Consider environmental testing if there is a history of water damage in her home or office.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a systems-thinking approach, considering the interplay between different bodily systems.
  • Assessment tools are essential for quantifying symptoms and identifying underlying triggers.
  • A phased intervention plan allows for gradual and targeted support, minimizing overwhelm and maximizing effectiveness.
  • Knowing when to refer to other healthcare professionals is crucial for ensuring client safety and optimal outcomes.
  • Empowering clients to take an active role in their health journey is key to long-term success.
