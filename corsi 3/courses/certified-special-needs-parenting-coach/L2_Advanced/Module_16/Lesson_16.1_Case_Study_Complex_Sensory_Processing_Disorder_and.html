Client Profile: Sarah

Let's meet Sarah, an 8-year-old girl diagnosed with Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD) and Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD). Understanding her background is crucial for effective intervention.

Case Study: Sarah

8-year-old with SPD and GAD

Sarah is a bright and articulate child, but her daily life is significantly impacted by her sensory sensitivities and anxiety. She often feels overwhelmed in noisy environments and avoids tactile experiences. Her anxiety manifests as excessive worry about school, social situations, and unexpected changes.

Presenting Challenges

Sarah's challenges span multiple areas. It's essential to identify these challenges to create a targeted intervention plan.

  • Sensory Sensitivities: Over-responsivity to sounds, textures, and visual stimuli.
  • Anxiety: Excessive worry, difficulty concentrating, and avoidance of social situations. A 2021 study in the Journal of Abnormal Child Psychology found that children with SPD are 2.5 times more likely to develop anxiety disorders.
  • Academic Difficulties: Trouble focusing in class, completing assignments, and participating in group activities.
  • Social Isolation: Avoidance of peer interactions due to anxiety and sensory overload.
  • Emotional Regulation: Difficulty managing frustration, anger, and sadness.

Applying the THRIVE Framework

The THRIVE Frameworkâ„¢ provides a structured approach to assess and address Sarah's needs. Each component plays a vital role in her progress.

  • T - Trust & Relationship: Building a strong, trusting relationship with Sarah and her family is paramount. This involves active listening, empathy, and creating a safe space for open communication.
  • H - Holistic Assessment: A comprehensive assessment of Sarah's sensory processing abilities, anxiety levels, academic performance, and social-emotional functioning. This includes standardized assessments, parent interviews, and teacher input.
  • R - Resourcing: Identifying and accessing appropriate resources, such as occupational therapy, psychotherapy, special education services, and parent support groups.
  • I - Intervention Strategies: Developing individualized intervention strategies to address Sarah's specific needs, including sensory integration therapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), and accommodations in the classroom.
  • V - Validation & Progress Monitoring: Regularly monitoring Sarah's progress and validating her achievements. This involves tracking her sensory sensitivities, anxiety symptoms, academic performance, and social-emotional well-being.
  • E - Empowerment & Independence: Empowering Sarah and her family to manage her sensory sensitivities and anxiety independently. This includes teaching her self-regulation techniques, problem-solving skills, and advocacy strategies.

Intervention Strategies

Based on the THRIVEâ„¢ assessment, we can implement targeted intervention strategies. These strategies address both sensory and anxiety components.

  1. Sensory Integration Therapy: Working with an occupational therapist to address Sarah's sensory sensitivities through controlled sensory experiences. A 2022 meta-analysis in the American Journal of Occupational Therapy found that sensory integration therapy significantly improves sensory processing abilities in children with SPD (effect size = 0.72, 95% CI [0.51, 0.93]).
  2. Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Using CBT techniques to help Sarah manage her anxiety by identifying and challenging negative thoughts and developing coping strategies.
  3. Classroom Accommodations: Providing accommodations in the classroom, such as a quiet space for Sarah to retreat to when feeling overwhelmed, preferential seating away from distractions, and extended time on assignments.
  4. Social Skills Training: Participating in social skills training to improve Sarah's ability to interact with peers and navigate social situations.
  5. Mindfulness and Relaxation Techniques: Teaching Sarah mindfulness and relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing exercises and progressive muscle relaxation, to reduce anxiety and promote emotional regulation.
  6. Parent Education and Support: Providing Sarah's parents with education and support to help them understand her sensory sensitivities and anxiety, and to implement strategies at home.

Progress Validation

Regular progress validation is critical to determine if the interventions are effective and to make adjustments as needed.

  • Standardized Assessments: Administering standardized assessments, such as the Sensory Profile and the Spence Children's Anxiety Scale, to track Sarah's sensory processing abilities and anxiety levels over time.
  • Parent and Teacher Feedback: Gathering regular feedback from Sarah's parents and teachers to assess her progress at home and school.
  • Observation: Observing Sarah in various settings to assess her sensory sensitivities, anxiety symptoms, and social-emotional functioning.
  • Self-Report: Encouraging Sarah to self-report her experiences and feelings, using age-appropriate tools and techniques.

Empowering Independence

The ultimate goal is to empower Sarah to manage her sensory sensitivities and anxiety independently. This involves teaching her self-regulation techniques, problem-solving skills, and advocacy strategies.

  • Self-Regulation Techniques: Teaching Sarah self-regulation techniques, such as using fidget toys, taking breaks, and practicing deep breathing exercises, to manage her sensory sensitivities and anxiety.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: Helping Sarah develop problem-solving skills to address challenges and overcome obstacles.
  • Advocacy Strategies: Empowering Sarah to advocate for her needs and preferences in various settings, such as at home, school, and in social situations.
