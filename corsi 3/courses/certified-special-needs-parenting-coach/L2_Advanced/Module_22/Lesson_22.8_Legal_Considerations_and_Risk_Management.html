Introduction

Hello there! Emma Thompson here. In this practice lab, we'll dive into a complex case that highlights the ethical considerations you might encounter as a special needs parenting coach. These situations aren't always straightforward, so let's sharpen our ethical compasses together. It is crucial to remember that as coaches, we are not therapists, doctors, or legal professionals. Maintaining clear boundaries and understanding our scope of practice is paramount to ethical coaching.

  • Recognize situations requiring referral to other professionals.
  • Develop strategies for managing boundary issues with clients.
  • Understand the importance of cultural sensitivity in ethical decision-making.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Steps

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Issues
In this case, the primary ethical issues are: (1) Eleanor's potential violation of informed consent and medical recommendations, (2) confidentiality vs. the need to protect the child's well-being, and (3) the impact of cultural beliefs on medical decisions.
Step 2: Review Relevant Ethical Codes
Refer to the code of ethics you agreed to upon certification. This addresses client autonomy, beneficence (doing good), non-maleficence (avoiding harm), and justice (fairness). Also consider legal obligations regarding child welfare.
Step 3: Consult with Supervisors or Peers
Discuss the situation with a mentor or peer supervision group to gain different perspectives and ensure you're not missing any critical factors. Document the consultation. Experienced coaches in our network earn an average of $75,000 - $120,000 annually by leveraging their expertise and ethical judgment.
Step 4: Develop a Plan of Action
Based on the ethical principles and consultations, create a step-by-step plan. This might involve: (a) exploring Eleanor's reasons for wanting to discontinue medication, (b) educating her about the risks of doing so without medical advice, (c) encouraging open communication with her husband and the pediatrician, and (d) documenting all interactions.

Differential Considerations

What Other Factors Might Be Influencing This?

1

Parental Burnout

Eleanor's stress and feelings of inadequacy may be clouding her judgment. Explore her support system and coping mechanisms.

2

Cultural Beliefs

Her parents' traditional views may be influencing her perception of the medication and its effects on her child.

3

Marital Discord

The conflict with her husband may be contributing to her sense of isolation and need to make decisions independently.

4

Lack of Understanding

Eleanor may not fully understand the potential consequences of stopping the medication or alternative options available.

  • Serious Marital Dysfunction: If the marital conflict is severe and impacting the child's well-being, refer Eleanor and Mark to a qualified marriage and family therapist.

The primary ethical dilemma is Eleanor's potential decision to discontinue her child's medication without consulting medical professionals or her husband, violating informed consent and potentially harming the child.

2. What steps should you take if you suspect Eleanor's actions are placing her child at immediate risk?

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas in special needs parenting coaching often involve conflicts between client autonomy, beneficence, and non-maleficence.
  • Cultural beliefs, parental burnout, and marital discord can significantly influence ethical decision-making.
  • It's essential to know when to refer clients to other professionals, such as therapists, physicians, or legal counsel.
  • Document all consultations, decisions, and actions taken in response to ethical dilemmas.
  • Continual professional development and peer supervision are crucial for maintaining ethical competence.
