Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic, but overwhelmed by complex family dynamics.

Background
Former teacher, now a certified parenting coach.
Strengths
Excellent listener, compassionate, good at building rapport.
Growth Areas
Needs help with setting boundaries, managing expectations, and handling crisis situations.
Her Question
"I'm working with a family in crisis, and I'm not sure how to best support them without getting too involved."

The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with the Miller family. Their 10-year-old son, David, has ADHD and severe behavioral issues. Both parents are exhausted and constantly arguing. The mom, Emily, is reaching out to me multiple times a day with urgent requests, and I feel like I'm on call 24/7. David had a meltdown at school today, and Emily wants me to intervene directly with the school. I'm not sure what to do."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah clearly defined her role and scope of practice with the family?
  • Has she established boundaries regarding communication and availability?
  • Is she equipped to handle crisis situations and connect the family with appropriate resources?
  • Does she understand the importance of self-care and preventing burnout?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Remind Sarah that her role is to coach the parents, not to directly intervene with David or the school. Emphasize the importance of staying within her certification.

2

Establish Clear Boundaries

Help Sarah create a communication policy with specific hours and response times. Teach her how to respectfully decline requests that fall outside her scope of practice.

3

Provide Crisis Management Strategies

Equip Sarah with a list of local resources, including therapists, support groups, and crisis hotlines. Teach her how to help the parents de-escalate situations and access immediate support when needed.

4

Emphasize Self-Care

Discuss the importance of setting healthy boundaries, prioritizing self-care activities, and seeking support from other professionals. Help Sarah recognize the signs of burnout and develop strategies to prevent it.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge the Challenge
"Sarah, I understand that this is a difficult situation, and it's natural to feel overwhelmed. You're doing great by seeking guidance."
Refocus on Core Principles
"Let's revisit the core principles of our certification. Remember, we empower parents to navigate these challenges. We don't become the primary problem-solver."
Offer Specific Strategies
"Here's a script you can use with Emily: 'I understand your concern, and I'm here to support you. However, directly intervening with the school is outside my scope of practice. I can help you prepare for a meeting with the school and develop strategies to advocate for David's needs.'"
Reinforce Self-Care
"Remember, you can't pour from an empty cup. It's essential to prioritize your well-being. What self-care activities can you incorporate into your routine this week?"

Check Your Understanding

The most important thing is to reinforce the coach's scope of practice and help them set clear boundaries.

You're Becoming a Leader!

By mentoring new practitioners, you're shaping the future of special needs parenting support. Your guidance will empower them to provide effective, ethical, and sustainable coaching to families in need.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, reinforcing their scope of practice, and helping them set boundaries.
  • Provide specific examples, scripts, and resources to equip mentees with practical tools.
  • Encourage reflection, problem-solving, and self-care to foster their professional growth and prevent burnout.
  • Validate their feelings, celebrate their strengths, and build their confidence.
  • By mentoring others, you expand your impact and contribute to the advancement of the field.
