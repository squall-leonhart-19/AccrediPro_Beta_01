The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: The Johnson Family

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with the Johnson family. Their son, 6-year-old Ben, has ADHD. I've suggested some strategies, like creating a visual schedule and using positive reinforcement. But Mrs. Johnson keeps calling me with new requests and concerns every day. I feel like I'm constantly putting out fires, and I'm not sure if Ben is actually making progress."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah clearly defined the scope of her coaching with the Johnsons?
  • Is she setting appropriate boundaries around communication and availability?
  • Is she tracking Ben's progress in a measurable way?
  • Is she taking care of her own well-being amidst the demands of the case?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Define Scope & Boundaries

Emphasize the importance of a clear coaching agreement that outlines services, communication protocols, and expected outcomes. Clarity is kindness.

2

Track Progress Objectively

Help Sarah identify measurable indicators of Ben's progress (e.g., reduced outbursts, improved focus). Encourage her to use a simple tracking system to monitor these indicators.

3

Prioritize Self-Care

Remind Sarah that she can't effectively support others if she's running on empty. Encourage her to schedule regular breaks and practice self-compassion.

4

Role-Play Difficult Conversations

Practice assertive communication techniques that Sarah can use to set boundaries with Mrs. Johnson. This can build her confidence and reduce anxiety.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Efforts
"Sarah, it's clear you're deeply committed to your clients. It's admirable that you're so responsive to their needs."
Ask Probing Questions
"What boundaries have you established with the Johnson family regarding communication? What's working, and what's not?"
Offer Specific Suggestions
"Consider scheduling a weekly 'office hours' block for parent communication. This allows you to address their concerns efficiently without being on call 24/7."
Validate Her Feelings
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed, especially when you're starting out. Remember, you're not alone in this. We all face challenges in setting boundaries."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a strong coaching agreement?

A strong coaching agreement clearly defines the services provided, communication protocols, expected outcomes, fees, and cancellation policies. It sets the foundation for a professional and mutually beneficial relationship.

2. Why is it important for new practitioners to track client progress objectively?

Objective tracking allows practitioners to demonstrate the effectiveness of their interventions, identify areas where adjustments are needed, and communicate progress to clients and other stakeholders. It also helps build confidence and credibility.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves guiding new practitioners, not just solving their problems.
  • Setting clear boundaries and expectations is crucial for both the mentor and mentee.
  • Objective progress tracking helps demonstrate the value of coaching services.
  • Prioritizing self-care is essential for sustainable and effective practice.
  • Your experience and wisdom are invaluable assets to share with others.
