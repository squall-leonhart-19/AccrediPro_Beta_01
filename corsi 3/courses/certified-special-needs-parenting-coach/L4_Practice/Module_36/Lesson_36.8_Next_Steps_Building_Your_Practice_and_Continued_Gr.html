Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you so much for scheduling this call. I appreciate you reaching out. To start, I'd love to hear a bit more about what prompted you to connect with me today. What's been going on?"
YOU:
"I understand that you're a member of my Facebook group. What specifically resonated with you about what I share there?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the challenges you're facing with your son. What are the biggest struggles at home and at school?"
YOU:
"What strategies have you already tried to help him manage his ADHD?"
YOU:
"What are your biggest worries about his future if these challenges continue?"
YOU:
"On the flip side, if you could wave a magic wand and transform things, what would that look like for you and your son?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're feeling overwhelmed and are looking for practical strategies to support your son and bring more peace to your home. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is based on the THRIVE method, which we discussed in the Facebook group. We'll work together to Tune-In to your son's specific needs, conduct a Home Assessment, identify Resources, Implement Strategies, Validate Progress, and Empower Independence. This isn't just about managing symptoms; it's about building long-term skills and confidence for both of you."
YOU:
"My program is a structured 12-week journey. You'll receive weekly coaching calls, personalized action plans, and ongoing support. Many of my clients see noticeable improvements in their child's behavior and academic performance within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: The Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, do you feel like this program could be the solution you've been looking for? ... Great! Let me tell you a bit more about the investment and how we can get started."

Handling Objections with Grace

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand that you're busy, Susan. That's why I've designed the program to be efficient and impactful. The weekly calls are just one hour, and the action plans are tailored to fit into your existing routine. Think of it as investing a small amount of time now to save a lot of time and stress in the long run."
"I need to think about it and discuss it with my husband."
"Of course. I want you both to feel completely comfortable. What specific questions do you anticipate he might have? Perhaps I can address them now, or I can send you some detailed information to share with him. We could even schedule a brief call together if that would be helpful."
"I'm worried it won't work. I've tried so many things before."
"I hear your frustration, Susan. What makes my approach different is that it's highly personalized and focuses on addressing the root causes of the challenges, not just the symptoms. I also provide ongoing support and accountability to help you stay on track. I have a proven track record of helping families like yours achieve lasting results. I'm happy to share testimonials and case studies with you."
"It seems like a lot of money."
"I understand that it's an investment. However, think about the cost of continuing to struggle with these challenges. What's the price of ongoing stress, academic difficulties, and family conflict? My program is designed to provide a clear ROI ‚Äì not just in terms of improved behavior and academic performance, but also in terms of reduced stress, increased confidence, and a more harmonious home life. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Rapport & Connection, Uncover the Challenges, Present Your Solution, and The Close.

2. What is a good strategy for handling the objection "It seems like a lot of money?"

A good strategy is to acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment in terms of the cost of continuing to struggle with the problem. Highlight the potential ROI in terms of reduced stress, improved outcomes, and a better quality of life. Also, offer flexible payment options.

3. How can you build trust and credibility during a discovery call?

You can build trust and credibility by actively listening to the prospect, demonstrating empathy, sharing relevant success stories, and clearly explaining your approach and qualifications.

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Thoroughly research your prospect and anticipate their needs and concerns.
  • Focus on connection: Build rapport and establish a genuine connection with your prospect.
  • Address objections with confidence: Be prepared to handle common objections with empathy and clear explanations.
  • Practice your closing: Develop a confident and compelling call-to-action that encourages prospects to take the next step.
  • Believe in your value: Remember the transformative impact you can have on families and price your services accordingly.
