4. Continuing Education

To maintain your certification, you must complete 20 hours of continuing education every two years. This ensures you stay current with the latest research, best practices, and evolving needs of special needs families. Approved continuing education activities include:

  • Attending relevant workshops and conferences
  • Completing advanced training courses
  • Publishing articles or presenting at conferences
  • Participating in peer supervision groups

You'll need to submit documentation of your continuing education activities to AccrediPro Academy for approval. Failure to meet these requirements may result in suspension or revocation of your certification. A 2022 survey found that 92% of certified coaches believe continuing education significantly enhances their coaching skills.

5. Appeals Process

If your certification application is denied, or your certification is suspended or revoked, you have the right to appeal the decision. The appeals process involves:

  1. Submitting a written appeal to the Certification Committee within 30 days of the decision.
  2. Providing any additional information or documentation to support your appeal.
  3. Attending a hearing with the Certification Committee, if requested.

The Certification Committee will review your appeal and make a final decision. Their decision is binding. The appeals process ensures fairness and transparency in certification decisions.
