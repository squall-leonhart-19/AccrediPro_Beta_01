Case Study: Sarah & Alex

Applying the THRIVE Framework‚Ñ¢

Client: Sarah, 42, single mother. Child: Alex, 8, diagnosed with ADHD and mild sensory processing disorder.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah reports feeling overwhelmed, exhausted, and unable to manage Alex's behavior. Alex struggles with focus, impulsivity, and sensory sensitivities, leading to frequent meltdowns and difficulties at school.

Intervention: Applying the THRIVE Framework‚Ñ¢:

  • Tune-In: Active listening to Sarah's concerns and validating her experiences.
  • Home Assessment: Identifying sensory triggers and creating a calming environment.
  • Resource Identification: Connecting Sarah with a support group for parents of children with ADHD and a pediatric occupational therapist.
  • Implement Strategies: Developing a visual schedule for Alex, implementing positive reinforcement techniques, and teaching Sarah strategies for managing meltdowns.
  • Validate Progress: Tracking Alex's behavior and academic performance, celebrating small victories, and adjusting strategies as needed.
  • Empower Independence: Teaching Alex self-regulation techniques and empowering Sarah to advocate for Alex's needs at school.

Outcomes: After three months, Sarah reports feeling less overwhelmed and more confident in her ability to manage Alex's behavior. Alex's meltdowns have decreased in frequency and intensity, and his academic performance has improved. Sarah and Alex have developed a stronger, more positive relationship.

Case Study: Maria & Sofia

Navigating School Accommodations

Client: Maria, 35, working mother. Child: Sofia, 10, recently diagnosed with dyslexia.

Presenting Symptoms: Maria is frustrated and overwhelmed by Sofia's struggles with reading and writing. Sofia is losing confidence and refusing to go to school.

Intervention:

  • Tune-In: Empathetically listening to Maria's concerns and validating her feelings.
  • Home Assessment: Reviewing Sofia's school records and identifying areas of difficulty.
  • Resource Identification: Connecting Maria with a special education advocate and a reading specialist.
  • Implement Strategies: Developing a plan for school accommodations, including assistive technology and extra time for assignments.
  • Validate Progress: Monitoring Sofia's reading progress and celebrating small victories.
  • Empower Independence: Teaching Sofia strategies for managing her dyslexia and advocating for her needs.

Outcomes: Sofia's reading skills have improved, and she is now attending school regularly. Maria feels empowered to advocate for Sofia's needs and has a better understanding of dyslexia.
