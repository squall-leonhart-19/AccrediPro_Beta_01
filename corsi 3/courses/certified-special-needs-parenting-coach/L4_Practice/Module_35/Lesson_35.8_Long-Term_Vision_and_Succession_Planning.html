Your Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Understand (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for reaching out. I understand you're looking for support with your teenage son. Tell me, what's been the biggest challenge lately?"
YOU:
"I appreciate you sharing that, Susan. It sounds like a tough situation. What have you already tried to address this?"
Phase 2: Define Desired Outcomes (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would a successful outcome look like for you and your son?"
YOU:
"What impact would that have on your family life and your own well-being?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to making these changes happen?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, my approach focuses on understanding your son's unique needs and creating a collaborative plan that empowers both of you. It's not about quick fixes, but about building lasting positive change."
YOU:
"I've helped many families in similar situations achieve [mention specific outcomes, e.g., improved communication, reduced conflict]. My program includes [briefly list key components]. Does this resonate with what you're looking for?"
Phase 4: Close (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"So, Susan, considering everything we've discussed, do you see value in exploring how we can work together?"
YOU:
"Great! Let's talk about the next steps and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Addressing Common Concerns

"I'm not sure if my son will be receptive to this."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. My program is designed to be collaborative. We start by understanding his perspective and involving him in the process. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee, so if he's not engaged, we can adjust the approach or offer a refund."
"I'm worried about the time commitment."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's needs and goals, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, then ask what specifically they need to think about to address their concerns and offer support.

3. Why is it important to practice your pricing presentation?

Practicing your pricing presentation builds confidence and helps you articulate the value of your services clearly and persuasively.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Handling objections effectively can turn potential "no's" into "yes's."
  • Confidently presenting your pricing demonstrates the value you offer.
  • Consistent client acquisition leads to a sustainable and thriving coaching practice.
