Defining Roles and Responsibilities

As you expand, clearly defined roles are crucial. Each team member should understand their responsibilities and how they contribute to the overall mission. When defining roles, consider how each position supports the different phases of the THRIVE Frameworkâ„¢: Tune-In, Home Assessment, Resource Identification, Implement Strategies, Validate Progress, and Empower Independence. Think about the skills and expertise needed for each phase and structure your team accordingly.

For example, you might need a team member specializing in Resource Identification who can research and maintain an updated database of local and national resources. Or, you might need someone skilled in Implement Strategies to help parents develop and implement effective support plans.

Case Study: "Empowerment Through Teamwork"

Client: Sarah J., age 42, mother of 8-year-old Ben with autism.

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt overwhelmed and isolated, struggling to manage Ben's challenging behaviors and navigate the complex special education system. She reported high levels of stress and burnout.

Intervention: The coaching team, consisting of a parenting coach, resource coordinator, and behavior specialist, collaborated to develop a comprehensive support plan based on the THRIVE Frameworkâ„¢. The parenting coach provided direct coaching to Sarah, focusing on behavior management strategies and communication techniques. The resource coordinator identified local support groups and respite care options. The behavior specialist conducted a home assessment and provided recommendations for creating a structured and supportive environment.

Outcomes: After six months of intervention, Sarah reported a significant decrease in stress and an increase in self-efficacy. Ben's challenging behaviors decreased, and he made progress in his communication skills. Sarah felt more connected to her community and empowered to advocate for Ben's needs. The team's collaborative approach ensured that Sarah received comprehensive and coordinated support.
