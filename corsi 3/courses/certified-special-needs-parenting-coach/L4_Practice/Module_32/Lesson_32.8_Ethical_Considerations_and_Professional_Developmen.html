Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand your concern about time. The program requires a commitment of a few hours per week, but the strategies and tools you'll learn will save you time in the long run by reducing conflicts and improving communication. Many of my clients find that investing in the program actually frees up more time for themselves."
"I'm worried it won't work for my child."
"I understand your concern. Every child is unique, but the strategies I use are evidence-based and adaptable to different situations. I offer personalized support and tailor the program to your child's specific needs. Plus, I provide ongoing feedback and adjustments to ensure we're on the right track. A 2022 meta-analysis of 18 studies found that parent coaching significantly improved child behavior outcomes across various diagnoses."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a flexible payment plan to make the program more accessible. We can break down the investment into smaller monthly installments to fit your budget. Would you like me to share the details of the payment plan with you?"
"I've tried other programs before, and they didn't work."
"I hear you. It's frustrating when previous attempts haven't yielded results. My program is different because it focuses on creating sustainable change through personalized support and evidence-based strategies. I also provide ongoing accountability to help you stay on track and achieve your goals. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not completely satisfied with the program after the first month, I'll provide a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during a discovery call?

Building rapport involves actively listening, showing empathy, and establishing a connection with the prospect. It's about creating a safe and comfortable space for them to share their challenges and goals.

2. How should you respond to a prospect who says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time to consider, but also try to uncover the specific concerns or questions they have. Offer to provide additional information or schedule a follow-up call to address their questions.

3. What are some effective strategies for presenting your pricing with confidence?

Clearly communicate the value and benefits of your program, emphasizing the return on investment. Practice saying your price out loud and be prepared to address any concerns or objections related to cost.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the prospect's challenges and goals is essential for tailoring your approach and presenting a solution that meets their needs.
  • Handling objections with empathy and providing clear, confident responses can increase your chances of closing the sale.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence and highlighting the value of your program can help prospects see the return on their investment.
  • Consistent effort and practice are key to mastering client acquisition and building a successful coaching practice.
