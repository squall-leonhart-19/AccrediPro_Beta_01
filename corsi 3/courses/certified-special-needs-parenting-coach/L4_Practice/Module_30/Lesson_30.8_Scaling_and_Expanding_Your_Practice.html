Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand completely. Many of my clients are busy parents. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your schedule. We can work together to find realistic ways to incorporate the strategies into your daily routine. Think of it as investing time now to save time and stress later."
"It's more expensive than I expected."
"I appreciate your honesty. Let's break down the value you're receiving. This isn't just about coaching; it's an investment in your family's well-being and your own peace of mind. Consider the cost of *not* addressing these challenges ‚Äì the stress, the frustration, the potential impact on your child's development. Plus, I offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to consider? Is it the financial commitment, the time investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for your family. Perhaps we can schedule a quick follow-up call to address any remaining questions?"
"Will this really work for *my* child? Every child is different."
"That's a valid concern, and I appreciate you bringing it up. While every child is unique, the underlying principles of the program are based on proven strategies and research. The key is that we'll tailor the approach to your child's specific needs and your family's dynamics. I'll provide ongoing support and adjustments to ensure it's effective for *your* situation."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's challenges and goals, presenting your solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How should you handle a prospect who says, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment in terms of the value and long-term benefits they'll receive. Offer flexible payment options if possible.

3. What is the importance of practicing your call-to-action?

Practicing your closing lines helps you deliver them with confidence and conviction, increasing the likelihood of securing the client.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Confidence is key when presenting your pricing and handling objections.
  • Focus on the value you provide and the positive impact you can make on families' lives.
  • Consistent practice will help you master the art of the discovery call.
