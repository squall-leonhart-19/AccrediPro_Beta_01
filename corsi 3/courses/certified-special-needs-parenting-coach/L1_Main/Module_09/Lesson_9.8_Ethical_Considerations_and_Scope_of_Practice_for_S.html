Your Coaching Script

Here's an example of how to start the initial consultation. Practice saying these words out loud, focusing on your tone and body language:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you so much for taking the time to connect with me today. I understand you're feeling overwhelmed, and I want you to know you're not alone. Many parents of children with ADHD experience similar challenges."
MARIA:
"It's just... constant. I feel like I'm always one step behind."
YOU:
"I hear you. To help me understand your specific situation better, can you tell me more about what a typical day looks like for you and your child?"
MARIA:
"Well, it starts with a struggle to get him out of bed... then rushing to get ready for school... homework is a nightmare... and by bedtime, I'm completely drained."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. So, if I'm hearing you correctly, the biggest challenges you're facing are related to morning routines, homework completion, and overall exhaustion. Is that accurate?"
MARIA:
"Yes, that's exactly it. And I feel guilty because I know I should be more patient, but I just run out of steam."
YOU:
"It sounds like you're carrying a heavy load, Maria. We can definitely work together to create strategies that will bring more peace and structure to your home, and help you feel more confident and less overwhelmed. Are you ready to explore some potential solutions?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the initial consultation?

The primary goal is to build rapport, understand the client's challenges, and determine if you're a good fit to work together. It's NOT about providing immediate solutions.

2. What are some key questions to ask during an initial consultation?

Focus on open-ended questions that encourage the client to share their story, such as: "What does a typical day look like?" "What have you already tried?" "What are your biggest challenges?"

Key Takeaways

  • Active listening and empathy are crucial during the initial consultation.
  • Avoid jumping to solutions before fully understanding the client's needs.
  • Use open-ended questions to encourage the client to share their story.
  • Summarize the client's concerns to ensure you're both on the same page.
  • The initial consultation is a two-way street: it's an opportunity for both you and the client to assess the fit.
