Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call! I'm excited to learn more about what brought you here. To start, I'd love to hear a bit about your experience finding me. What caught your eye about my services?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! So, Susan, what's been going on that made you decide to reach out today?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the brain fog and memory issues. When did they start, and how are they impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"And what have you already tried to address these issues? What were the results?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, what will your life look like in a year? Conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and resolve these issues, what would your ideal life look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, here's what I understand: [summarize her key challenges and goals]. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My approach is unique because we focus on identifying the root causes of your symptoms through comprehensive assessments and personalized strategies. I work with clients over 12 weeks to create a tailored plan that addresses their specific needs. Many clients experience significant improvements within the first month."
YOU:
"For example, I worked with Client Jane, a 48-year-old woman experiencing similar post-menopausal symptoms. After 12 weeks, she reported a 60% reduction in brain fog and a 40% decrease in anxiety levels. She now feels more energetic and focused."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you feel like this approach aligns with what you're looking for? Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time right now."
"I understand that time is valuable. The program is designed to be flexible and integrates into your existing routine. Plus, think about the time you're currently losing due to brain fog and lack of focus. This program is an investment in reclaiming that time and energy."
"I need to think about it and do some more research."
"I respect that. What specific areas do you want to research further? I can provide you with additional resources and information to help you make an informed decision. Would it be helpful if I sent you some case studies or testimonials from past clients?"
"I'm concerned about the cost."
"I understand. Let‚Äôs break down the investment. When you compare the cost of this program to other solutions you've tried, and factor in the potential long-term benefits of improved cognitive function and reduced anxiety, how does it measure up? What value do you place on regaining your mental clarity and feeling like yourself again?"
"I'm not sure if this will really work for me."
"That's a valid concern. My program is based on evidence-based strategies and is tailored to your specific needs. Plus, I offer ongoing support and guidance throughout the program. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you fully commit to the program and don't see the results you're looking for, I will [mention your guarantee]."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and closing the call.

2. How should you handle common objections during a discovery call?

Acknowledge the concern, ask clarifying questions, provide evidence-based solutions, and offer a satisfaction guarantee.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for scaling your practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call can significantly increase your conversion rate.
  • Handling objections with empathy and providing evidence-based solutions builds trust.
  • Confidently presenting your pricing and closing the sale are crucial for revenue generation.
  • Focus on the value you provide and the transformative outcomes your clients will achieve.
