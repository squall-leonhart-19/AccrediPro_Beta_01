Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To make the best use of our time, I'd like to briefly understand your current challenges, discuss how my program can help, and answer any questions you have. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Great! To start, what motivated you to reach out to me specifically?"
Phase 2: Explore Challenges & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned struggling with focus and stress. Can you tell me more about that? What does a typical day look like for you?"
YOU:
"What impact is this having on your work, your relationships, your overall well-being?"
YOU:
"And what would be your ideal outcome? If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like in six months?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing that. Based on what you've described, it sounds like you're experiencing [summarize her key challenges]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My Brain Health Program is designed to address these exact issues. We use evidence-based strategies to improve focus, reduce stress, and enhance overall cognitive function. We'll work together over 12 weeks to create a personalized plan that fits your lifestyle and goals. We leverage the BRAIN model: Baseline Assessment, Reframing Beliefs, Action Planning, Integrate Strategies, and Neuroplasticity Nurturing. Many of my clients report significant improvements within the first month."
YOU:
"Specifically, we'll [mention 2-3 relevant techniques or strategies from your program]."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you think my program could be a good fit for you?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your existing schedule. We can work together to prioritize strategies that will give you the biggest impact in the shortest amount of time. Plus, by improving your focus and reducing stress, you'll likely become MORE efficient and have MORE time overall."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can tell you that my program is based on evidence-based principles and tailored to your individual needs. I also offer ongoing support and guidance throughout the program. I am fully committed to your success, and I‚Äôm confident that if you commit to the process, you'll see positive changes."
"I need to do more research."
"I completely understand. What specific information are you looking for? I'm happy to provide you with research articles, testimonials, or answer any specific questions you have. Also, keep in mind that research is helpful, but the best way to know if something works is to experience it yourself."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I do offer a payment plan option. Instead of the full investment of $X, you can pay in three monthly installments of $Y. Would that make it easier for you to get started?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Set Agenda, Explore Challenges & Goals, Present Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.
2. What are some common objections you might encounter, and how can you respond?

Common objections include concerns about time commitment, effectiveness, and cost. You can address these by emphasizing the program's flexibility, evidence-based approach, personalized support, and potential return on investment.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting potential clients into paying clients.
  • A structured approach, including building rapport, exploring challenges, presenting solutions, and closing effectively, is essential.
  • Anticipate and prepare for common objections related to time, cost, and effectiveness.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and its potential impact on clients' lives.
  • Practice your sales script and pricing presentation to build confidence and increase conversion rates.
