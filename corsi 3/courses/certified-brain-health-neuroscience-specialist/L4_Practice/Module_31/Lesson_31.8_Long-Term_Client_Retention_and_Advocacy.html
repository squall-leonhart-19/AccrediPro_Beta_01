Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time. To start, can you tell me a bit about what led you to reach out today?"
YOU:
"I understand you're experiencing brain fog and memory issues. How long has this been going on, and how is it impacting your daily life?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address these issues? What were the results?"
YOU:
"You mentioned your mother had Alzheimer's. How does that influence your concerns about your own cognitive health?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate these challenges, what would your ideal life look like? How would you feel?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for a personalized, evidence-based approach to proactively support your brain health and address these cognitive symptoms. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My BRAIN program is designed to do just that. We use a comprehensive assessment to identify your specific needs and then create a tailored plan incorporating nutrition, lifestyle, and cognitive strategies. We work together over 12 weeks to implement these strategies and track your progress. Many clients report noticeable improvements in their cognitive function, energy levels, and overall well-being within the first few weeks."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you see how this could be a good fit for your needs?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get you started."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport. Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Desires. Phase 3: Present Your Solution. Phase 4: Close & Next Steps.

2. How can you handle the objection "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need for time, then ask clarifying questions like, "What specifically do you need to think about?" or "What additional information would be helpful?"

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script until it feels natural and conversational.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value of your program.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Focus on the client's needs and desires, and how your program can help them achieve their goals.
  • Remember that client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice and persistence.
