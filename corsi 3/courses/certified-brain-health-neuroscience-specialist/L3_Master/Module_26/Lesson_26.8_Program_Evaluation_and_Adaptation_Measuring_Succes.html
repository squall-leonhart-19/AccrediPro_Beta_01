Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Emily

1

Reinforce the Neuroplasticity Timeline

Explain that significant changes in brain function take time, often several weeks to months. Consistency is key.

2

Assess Client Expectations

Discuss the importance of setting realistic expectations at the outset. Explore ways to re-evaluate and adjust expectations as needed.

3

Emphasize Small Wins

Teach Emily to help her client identify and celebrate small improvements, such as better sleep or increased focus, to maintain motivation.

4

Encourage Personalized Strategies

Help Emily understand the importance of tailoring interventions to Sarah‚Äôs unique needs and preferences. Not all strategies work for everyone.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves active listening, empathy, and specific feedback.
  • Setting realistic client expectations is crucial for maintaining motivation and adherence.
  • Neuroplasticity takes time; emphasize consistency and celebrate small wins.
  • Encourage mentees to develop personalized strategies tailored to individual client needs.
  • Your role as a mentor is to empower new practitioners and foster their growth.
