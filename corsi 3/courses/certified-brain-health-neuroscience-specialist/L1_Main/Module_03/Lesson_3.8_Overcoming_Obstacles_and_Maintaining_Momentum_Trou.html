Your Coaching Script

Here's how to guide Maria towards creating a manageable action plan. Practice these lines out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds like you're juggling a lot right now. We're going to create a simple, achievable plan to support your brain health and help you regain that sense of control. How does that sound?"
MARIA:
"That sounds wonderful. I'm just worried I won't be able to stick to it."
YOU:
"I understand. We'll focus on small, consistent steps. Think of it as planting seeds, not building a skyscraper overnight. Let's start by identifying just three things you can realistically commit to this week to support your focus and memory. What comes to mind?"
MARIA:
"Well, I know I should be getting more sleep... and maybe drinking less coffee. And I keep hearing about meditation, but I don't know where to start."
YOU:
"Those are excellent starting points. Let's make them specific. Instead of 'more sleep,' how about aiming for 30 minutes earlier each night? For coffee, can you switch one cup to herbal tea? And for meditation, we can find a simple 5-minute guided session. Does that feel doable?"
MARIA:
"Yes, that feels much less overwhelming. 30 minutes, one tea, 5 minutes... I can do that."
YOU:
"Perfect. So, our action plan for this week is: 30 minutes more sleep, one herbal tea instead of coffee, and 5 minutes of guided meditation. We'll check in next week to see how it's going and adjust as needed. How does that feel as a starting point?"

Key Takeaways

  • Action planning should be collaborative and client-centered.
  • Focus on small, achievable steps to build momentum.
  • Address the client's concerns about sticking to the plan.
  • Tailor advice to the client's specific needs and lifestyle.
  • Celebrate small wins and adjust the plan as needed.
