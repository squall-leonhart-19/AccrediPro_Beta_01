Important Considerations:

Ethics & Professionalism

Upholding high ethical standards is paramount to building a successful and trustworthy practice. Your clients are entrusting you with their well-being, and itâ€™s your responsibility to honor that trust.

Ethical Guidelines:

  • Client confidentiality: Always maintain strict confidentiality regarding your clients' personal information and health history. Respect their privacy and obtain informedconsent before sharing any information.
  • Informed consent and transparency: Ensure your clients fully understand the nature of your services, your qualifications, and the potential benefits and risks. Provide them with a clear and transparent agreement outlining the terms of your engagement.
  • Boundaries and referrals: Establish clear professional boundaries with your clients. Avoid dual relationships and maintain a professional distance. If a client presents with issues outside your scope of practice, refer them to appropriate healthcare professionals.
