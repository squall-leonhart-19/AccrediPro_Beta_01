Understanding the Stages of Grief

Grief is a natural response to loss, and it manifests differently in each individual. The widely recognized KÃ¼bler-Ross model outlines five stages: denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance. However, it's crucial to remember that these stages are not linear. Clients may experience them in varying orders, revisit stages, or not experience all of them.

Understanding these stages provides a framework for recognizing where your client may be in their grieving process. A 2018 study in the *Journal of Palliative Medicine* found that

approximately 60% of individuals experience at least some symptoms of complicated grief following the loss of a loved one

Ethical Considerations

When working with grieving clients, it's essential to maintain ethical boundaries. Avoid making promises of cures or guarantees of specific outcomes. Emphasize that crystal healing is a complementary therapy and should not replace professional medical or psychological care. Always obtain informed consent and respect the client's autonomy.
