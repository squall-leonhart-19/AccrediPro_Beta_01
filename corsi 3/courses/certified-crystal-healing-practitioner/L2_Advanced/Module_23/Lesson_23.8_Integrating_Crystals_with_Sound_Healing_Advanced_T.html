Differential Considerations

Considering Other Possibilities

1

Hormonal Imbalances

At age 48, perimenopause could significantly contribute to migraines, anxiety, and mood swings. Consider testing hormone levels.

2

Nutrient Deficiencies

Chronic stress and medication use can deplete essential nutrients. Assess for deficiencies in magnesium, B vitamins, and vitamin D, all of which play a role in neurological function. A 2018 study in *Headache* found that 41% of migraine sufferers were deficient in Vitamin D.

3

Underlying Autoimmune Condition

Migraines and anxiety can sometimes be associated with underlying autoimmune disorders. Explore this possibility if symptoms persist despite interventions.

4

Sleep Apnea

Irregular sleep and fatigue could indicate sleep apnea, which can worsen migraines and anxiety. Consider a sleep study if there are risk factors.
