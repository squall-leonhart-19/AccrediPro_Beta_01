Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Estrogen Dominance

Endometriosis history, irregular periods, and low libido suggest potential estrogen dominance. Consider testing hormone levels (estradiol, progesterone, FSH, LH) to assess hormonal balance.

2

Thyroid Dysfunction

Brain fog, fatigue, and anxiety can be symptoms of thyroid issues. Rule out hypothyroidism by checking a full thyroid panel (TSH, Free T4, Free T3, thyroid antibodies).

3

Adrenal Fatigue

Chronic stress and feeling overwhelmed can lead to adrenal fatigue. Consider assessing cortisol levels throughout the day to evaluate adrenal function.

4

Food Sensitivities

Migraines, GERD, and endometriosis can be exacerbated by food sensitivities. Consider an elimination diet or food sensitivity testing to identify potential triggers.

1. What are the key considerations when working with a client who has endometriosis and is taking birth control pills?

Consider the nutrient depletion caused by birth control pills, potential estrogen dominance, and the impact of endometriosis on emotional well-being. Focus on nutrient repletion, hormonal balance, and emotional healing.

3. What are some potential red flags that would warrant a referral to a medical doctor in Eleanor's case?

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases require a holistic approach that addresses multiple systems and imbalances.
  • Consider the impact of medications and medical history on the client's overall health.
  • Prioritize interventions based on the client's most pressing symptoms and underlying imbalances.
  • Integrate crystal healing techniques with other modalities, such as nutrition, stress management, and emotional healing.
  • Know your scope of practice and refer clients to other healthcare professionals when necessary.
