The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I had a client, Emily, 52, with chronic anxiety and sleep problems. I recommended amethyst, lepidolite, and howlite. Emily said she felt more anxious after using them. Did I choose the wrong crystals?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Was the client's anxiety rooted in overstimulation, which amethyst can sometimes exacerbate?
  • Did Sarah explore the client's preferred method of using the crystals (placement, meditation, etc.)?
  • Did Sarah discuss the possibility of an initial adjustment period?
  • Were there any external factors contributing to the client's anxiety that need to be addressed?

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Encouragement
"Sarah, you chose crystals known for anxiety relief, which is a great starting point. It's clear you're thinking about your clients."
Probe for Her Thought Process
"Tell me more about why you chose these specific crystals. What qualities in Emily led you to select amethyst, lepidolite, and howlite?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"Sometimes, amethyst can amplify energy before calming it. For someone already anxious, it might feel overwhelming initially. Consider grounding stones like hematite or black tourmaline as a first step."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"Based on this, what adjustments would you make for Emily? How would you explain this to her in a way that builds trust?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing constructive feedback to a new practitioner?

Creating a safe space for learning and growth, emphasizing their strengths while addressing areas for improvement.

2. Why is it crucial to encourage self-reflection in a mentee?

It fosters independent thinking, critical analysis, and confidence in their decision-making abilities.

You're Becoming a Leader!

Mentoring is a powerful way to elevate the entire field. Your guidance helps shape competent, compassionate practitioners who will positively impact countless lives.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring new practitioners is an opportunity to refine your own understanding and skills.
  • Constructive feedback should always be balanced with encouragement and validation.
  • Encourage mentees to trust their intuition while also grounding them in practical knowledge.
  • Ethical boundaries and client safety should be emphasized in every supervision session.
