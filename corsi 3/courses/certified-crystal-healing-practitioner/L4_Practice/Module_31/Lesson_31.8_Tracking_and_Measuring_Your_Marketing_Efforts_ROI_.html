Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if crystal healing really works."
"That's a valid concern, Susan. While crystal healing is not a replacement for conventional medicine, many of my clients find it to be a powerful tool for emotional and spiritual well-being. I encourage you to read some of the testimonials on my website or check out some research on the placebo effect and how belief can influence outcomes. Ultimately, it's about finding what resonates with you."
"It's a lot of money."
"I understand. Can I ask, what value do you place on feeling grounded, peaceful, and in control of your life? This program isn't just about crystals; it's about investing in your overall well-being and creating lasting change. We also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure I've addressed all your concerns so you can make an informed decision."
"I'm too busy right now."
"I understand that life gets busy. However, if you don't prioritize your well-being, you risk burnout and further disconnection. This program is designed to be manageable, with just one weekly session and a few minutes of daily crystal work. We can also adjust the schedule to fit your needs. When *would* be a good time for you to focus on your well-being?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: building rapport, understanding the problem, presenting your approach, and closing.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the long-term benefits and offering payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call helps you qualify leads and build trust.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence is crucial for closing deals.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your program and how it solves your client's problems.
  • Practice your pricing presentation to feel comfortable discussing money.
  • Consistent client acquisition leads to a sustainable and fulfilling practice.
