Sole Proprietorship vs. LLC vs. S-Corp

Choosing the right business structure is a critical first step. Each structure has different legal and financial implications. Let's break down the most common options for crystal healing practices:

  • Sole Proprietorship: This is the simplest structure, where the business is owned and run by one person, and there is no legal distinction between the owner and the business. It's easy to set up, but the owner is personally liable for business debts and obligations.
  • Limited Liability Company (LLC): An LLC provides some liability protection, separating your personal assets from business debts. It's more complex to set up than a sole proprietorship but offers a good balance of simplicity and protection. Many crystal healing practitioners find this structure suitable.
  • S-Corporation (S-Corp): An S-Corp is a more complex structure that can offer tax advantages for established businesses. It requires more administrative work and is generally recommended for practices with higher revenues.
Business Structure Advantages Disadvantages Suitable For
Sole Proprietorship Easy to set up, minimal paperwork, direct control. Personal liability, harder to raise capital. Starting out, testing the waters.
LLC Liability protection, pass-through taxation, credibility. More complex setup, ongoing compliance requirements. Growing practices, seeking liability protection.
S-Corp Potential tax savings, increased credibility. Complex setup, higher administrative costs. Established practices with significant revenue.

For many practitioners like you, transitioning from a fulfilling career into crystal healing, an LLC often provides the best balance of protection and simplicity. As your practice grows, you can always reassess and change your business structure.
