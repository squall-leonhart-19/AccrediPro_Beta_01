Review of Safe Elixir Creation Methods

As we discussed previously, safety is paramount when creating crystal elixirs. The direct method, where crystals are immersed directly in water, carries potential risks due to toxicity or crystal breakdown. Therefore, we will ONLY be focusing on the indirect method. This involves placing the crystal in a separate container (like a glass bowl) within the water, ensuring no direct contact. This method allows the crystal's energy to infuse the water safely.

Dosage Guidelines and Contraindications

While crystal elixirs are generally safe, it's essential to follow appropriate dosage guidelines. A typical dosage is 4 drops under the tongue, 2-4 times per day. However, this can vary depending on the individual's sensitivity and the potency of the elixir. Start with a lower dosage and gradually increase as needed.

There are few absolute contraindications for crystal elixirs, but caution is advised for individuals with severe mental health conditions or those who are highly sensitive to energy. It's also important to be mindful of potential allergies to the water source or any added ingredients (like essential oils). Always advise clients to consult with their healthcare provider if they have any concerns.

It's also important to educate your clients on what to expect. Some individuals may experience a "healing crisis," where symptoms temporarily worsen before improving. This is a sign that the body is releasing old patterns and emotions. Reassure your clients that this is a normal part of the healing process and encourage them to continue with the elixir.
