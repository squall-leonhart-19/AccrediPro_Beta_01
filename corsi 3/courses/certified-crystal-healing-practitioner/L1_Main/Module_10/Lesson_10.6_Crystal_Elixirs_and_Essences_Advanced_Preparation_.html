Safe and Effective Preparation

Creating crystal elixirs involves transferring the vibrational energy of a crystal into water. Itâ€™s crucial to use safe methods to avoid contamination or toxicity. There are two primary methods:

  • Direct Method: Involves placing a crystal directly in water. This method is only safe for crystals that are non-toxic and do not dissolve in water (e.g., Quartz, Amethyst).
  • Indirect Method: Placing the crystal in a separate container that does not come into direct contact with the water. This method is suitable for crystals that are toxic or may degrade in water (e.g., Malachite, Lapis Lazuli).

Water Selection and Sterilization: Always use purified or distilled water. Sterilize your equipment with boiling water or a UV sterilizer to minimize the risk of bacterial growth. Remember, safety is paramount when preparing elixirs for consumption or external use.

Using Crystal Elixirs for Internal and External Applications

Crystal elixirs can be used both internally and externally, depending on the desired effects. Here are some guidelines:

  • Internal Use: Dosage typically ranges from a few drops to a teaspoon, taken one to three times per day. Start with a small dose to assess sensitivity. Elixirs can be taken directly or added to water, tea, or juice.
  • External Use: Elixirs can be applied topically to the skin, added to bathwater, or used as a compress. They can also be incorporated into sprays for aura cleansing or room clearing.

Case Study: Client Sarah, 34, presented with anxiety and insomnia. A blend of Amethyst and Moonstone elixir was recommended (4 drops, twice daily). Within two weeks, Sarah reported improved sleep quality and reduced anxiety levels. Always monitor client responses and adjust dosages as needed.
