Complex Case Presentation

Eleanor, 48

Marketing Executive, Austin, TX ‚Ä¢ Married, two teenage children

Chief Complaints
Chronic fatigue, persistent anxiety, irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), insomnia, difficulty concentrating, and feelings of overwhelm.
Medical History
Diagnosed with Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) 5 years ago, IBS for 8 years, migraines since her 20s. No surgeries.
Medications
Lexapro (10mg daily), occasional Xanax for panic attacks, and birth control pills.
Labs (Recent)
Vitamin D: 28 ng/mL (low), Cortisol: Elevated in the morning, low in the evening, CBC and CMP within normal limits.
Lifestyle
High-stress job with long hours, limited physical activity, relies on coffee to stay energized, struggles to maintain a healthy diet.
Trauma History
Reports a difficult childhood with emotionally unavailable parents. No history of physical or sexual abuse.

Clinical Reasoning Process

Polyvagal-Informed Clinical Reasoning

Step 1: Assess Autonomic State
Eleanor presents with a chronic pattern of sympathetic activation (anxiety, insomnia, difficulty concentrating) combined with dorsal vagal shutdown (fatigue, IBS). Her history suggests a pattern of chronic stress and limited opportunities for safe social engagement, leading to a dysregulated autonomic nervous system.
Step 2: Identify Polyvagal Imbalances
Her system shows an overreliance on sympathetic activation to cope with stress, leading to burnout and fatigue. The IBS symptoms suggest a dysregulated gut-brain axis, with reduced vagal tone impacting digestive function. The elevated morning cortisol and low evening cortisol indicate HPA axis dysregulation, further contributing to autonomic instability.
Step 3: Consider Contributing Factors
Beyond the autonomic state, consider the impact of her lifestyle factors (high stress, limited activity, poor diet) and early childhood experiences on her nervous system development. Lexapro may provide some symptom relief, but does not address the underlying autonomic dysregulation.
Step 4: Prioritize Interventions
Begin with interventions to enhance vagal tone and promote safety. This includes practices that support social engagement, such as gentle movement, breathwork, and mindfulness. Address lifestyle factors by focusing on sleep hygiene, nutrition, and stress management. Consider co-regulation techniques to help Eleanor access her ventral vagal state.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Thyroid Dysfunction

Fatigue and anxiety can be symptoms of thyroid issues. Rule out hypothyroidism with a full thyroid panel (TSH, Free T4, Free T3, and thyroid antibodies).

2

Hormonal Imbalances

Given her age and the use of birth control pills, consider hormonal imbalances (estrogen, progesterone) that could be contributing to anxiety, insomnia, and fatigue.

3

Nutrient Deficiencies

Beyond Vitamin D, assess for other nutrient deficiencies (B vitamins, magnesium, iron) that can impact energy levels and mood.

4

Underlying Autoimmune Condition

Chronic fatigue and IBS can sometimes be associated with autoimmune disorders. Consider screening for common autoimmune markers.

When to Refer

Red Flags ‚Äî Refer to MD or Specialist

  • Sudden or severe abdominal pain could indicate a serious gastrointestinal issue.
  • Unexplained weight loss may suggest an underlying medical condition.
  • Suicidal ideation or self-harm thoughts require immediate mental health intervention.
  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations warrant a cardiac evaluation.
  • Neurological symptoms (e.g., weakness, numbness) necessitate a neurological assessment.

Your Proposed Protocol

Phased Intervention Plan

üìÖ Phase 1: Safety and Stabilization (Weeks 1-4)
  • Establish Safety: Create a safe and trusting therapeutic relationship.
  • Enhance Vagal Tone: Diaphragmatic breathing exercises (5 minutes, 2-3 times per day).
  • Gentle Movement: Short walks in nature (15-20 minutes daily).
  • Sleep Hygiene: Consistent sleep schedule, dark room, no screens before bed.
üìÖ Phase 2: Co-Regulation and Connection (Weeks 5-8)
  • Co-Regulation Exercises: Practice attuned listening and empathetic responses.
  • Mindfulness Practices: Guided meditations to reduce anxiety and increase present moment awareness.
  • Nutritional Support: Focus on whole foods, reduce processed foods, and identify potential food sensitivities.
  • Social Engagement: Encourage participation in supportive social activities.
üìÖ Phase 3: Integration and Resilience (Weeks 9-12)
  • Polyvagal Education: Help Eleanor understand her autonomic nervous system and how to self-regulate.
  • Resilience Building: Develop coping strategies for managing stress and triggers.
  • Lifestyle Optimization: Integrate healthy habits into daily routine.
  • Ongoing Support: Continue to provide support and guidance as needed.

Case Study: Eleanor's Progress

After 12 weeks of Polyvagal-informed therapy, Eleanor reported a 40% reduction in anxiety symptoms and a 50% improvement in sleep quality. She also experienced fewer IBS flare-ups and increased energy levels. She reported feeling more connected to her husband and children, and more confident in her ability to manage stress.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key Polyvagal imbalances present in Eleanor's case?

Eleanor exhibits an overreliance on sympathetic activation (anxiety, insomnia) and dorsal vagal shutdown (fatigue, IBS). This indicates a chronic pattern of stress and limited safe social engagement.

2. What are the initial steps you would take to help Eleanor regulate her autonomic nervous system?

The initial steps involve establishing safety, enhancing vagal tone through diaphragmatic breathing, promoting gentle movement, and improving sleep hygiene. These interventions aim to shift her system towards a more ventral vagal state.

3. When would you consider referring Eleanor to another healthcare provider?

Referral is necessary if Eleanor experiences sudden or severe abdominal pain, unexplained weight loss, suicidal ideation, chest pain, or neurological symptoms. These red flags indicate potential underlying medical conditions that require further evaluation.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases often involve multiple overlapping conditions and autonomic dysregulation.
  • Polyvagal Theory provides a valuable framework for understanding and addressing these presentations.
  • Prioritize interventions that promote safety, enhance vagal tone, and support social engagement.
  • Consider lifestyle factors, trauma history, and potential underlying medical conditions.
  • Collaboration with other healthcare providers is essential for comprehensive care.
