The Case Aisha Presents

Aisha's Client Case: Sarah, 35, Trauma History

What Aisha Tells You:

"Sarah, 35, has a complex trauma history. During our session, she became highly activated while recounting a past event. I tried to use vagal braking techniques, but she seemed to dissociate. I'm not sure if I pushed her too far. Now I feel anxious about our next session."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What specific vagal braking techniques did Aisha use?
  • How did Aisha assess Sarah's capacity for regulation in that moment?
  • Did Aisha co-regulate effectively, or did her own anxiety increase?
  • What safety protocols are in place if dissociation occurs?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Prioritize Safety and Stabilization

Remind Aisha that establishing safety is paramount. Before diving into trauma narratives, ensure the client has resources and coping mechanisms in place.

2

Refine Vagal Braking Technique Application

Discuss the importance of tailoring vagal braking techniques to the client's state. Deep breathing, for example, can be dysregulating for some individuals.

3

Enhance Dissociation Management Skills

Provide Aisha with strategies for recognizing and responding to dissociation, such as grounding techniques and gentle re-orientation to the present moment.

4

Emphasize Self-Regulation for the Practitioner

Help Aisha recognize her own activation patterns and develop strategies for maintaining emotional equilibrium during challenging sessions. Co-regulation starts with self-regulation.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Begin with Affirmation
"Aisha, thank you for bringing this case to supervision. It takes courage to seek guidance, and your willingness to learn is commendable. It sounds like you're deeply committed to Sarah's well-being."
Explore Her Perspective
"Before I offer my thoughts, what did you learn from this session? What would you do differently if you could rewind and start again?"
Offer a Polyvagal Lens
"From a Polyvagal perspective, it's crucial to assess the client's nervous system state before applying any intervention. If Sarah was already in a high state of arousal, vagal braking might have felt overwhelming. It's like slamming on the brakes when the car is already skidding."
Collaborate on Next Steps
"What feels like the most compassionate and effective way to reconnect with Sarah? How can you rebuild trust and create a safer therapeutic space? I'm here to brainstorm with you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the primary goal of supervision in this scenario?

The primary goal is to support the new practitioner in developing her skills, confidence, and ethical practice, while ensuring client safety.

2. Why is it important to explore the mentee's perspective before offering advice?

Exploring the mentee's perspective fosters self-reflection, promotes critical thinking, and allows the supervisor to tailor their guidance to the mentee's specific needs and learning style.

Key Takeaways

  • Supervision is a collaborative process focused on the mentee's professional growth.
  • Prioritize safety, stabilization, and resource-building with clients before exploring trauma narratives.
  • Tailor vagal braking techniques to the client's nervous system state.
  • Enhance dissociation management skills for both the practitioner and the client.
  • Self-regulation is essential for effective co-regulation.
