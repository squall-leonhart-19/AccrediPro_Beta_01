Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for joining me today! I'm Sarah. I help professionals like you manage stress and find more balance. How's your day going so far?"
YOU:
"Great! So, in this call, I want to understand your current challenges, share how my program can help, and see if it's a good fit for both of us. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Uncover Challenges & Desired Outcomes 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed. Can you tell me more about what that looks like in your daily life? What are your biggest stressors?"
YOU:
"How is this affecting your sleep, your relationships, your overall well-being?"
YOU:
"And if you could wave a magic wand and change things, what would your ideal day look like? How would you *feel*?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with significant stress and anxiety that's impacting multiple areas of your life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My program helps you regulate your nervous system using Polyvagal Theory, so you can respond to stress with more calm and resilience. We'll work together over 12 weeks to identify your triggers, develop personalized strategies, and create lasting change. Clients typically report improved sleep, reduced anxiety, and increased focus within the first month."
YOU:
"Does this approach resonate with you?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on our conversation, do you think this program could be a good fit for you?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible, with short, focused sessions that fit into your schedule. Think of it as investing in yourself, so you can be more productive and present in all areas of your life. Where could you find just 30 minutes, 3 times per week to dedicate to your well-being?"
"How is this different from therapy?"
"That's a great question. While therapy often focuses on processing past events, my program is about building present-moment resilience. We use Polyvagal Theory to understand how your nervous system responds to stress and develop practical tools to regulate it. It's more about skills-building and empowerment than traditional talk therapy."
"I need to think about it and discuss it with my spouse."
"Of course! I can send you a detailed program overview to share with your spouse. Would it also be helpful to schedule a brief call together so I can answer any questions they may have? I want you both to feel confident in your decision."
"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand that budget is a consideration. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. How much is your current stress and anxiety costing you in terms of sleep, productivity, and relationships? What if you could significantly reduce that in just 12 weeks? Also, I do offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important goal of the "Build Rapport" phase of a discovery call?

To establish a connection with the prospect and make them feel comfortable sharing their challenges.

2. What's a good strategy for handling the objection, "I need to think about it?"

Ask them what specifically they need to think about (investment, time, etc.) to address their concerns directly.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition and sales is crucial for building a thriving practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call helps you understand your prospect's needs and present your solution effectively.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy builds trust and increases conversion rates.
  • Clearly communicating your pricing and value proposition is essential for closing the sale.
  • Consistent effort and practice lead to predictable income growth.
