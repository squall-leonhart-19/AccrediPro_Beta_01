Discovery Call Script: Legal & Ethical Considerations

Adapting Your Script for Compliance

Phase 1: Introduction & Clarification 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Linda, welcome! Before we start, I want to clarify that I am a Certified Polyvagal Theory Specialist. I'm not a licensed therapist or medical professional. My role is to help you understand your nervous system and build resilience using Polyvagal-informed techniques."
YOU:
"Is that clear, and do you have any questions about my qualifications or scope of practice?"
Phase 2: Informed Consent 10 min
YOU:
"To ensure you're fully informed, let's discuss what our work together will involve. We'll explore how your nervous system responds to stress and use exercises to promote safety and connection. This is an educational and supportive process, not a replacement for medical or psychological treatment."
YOU:
"I'll provide you with a detailed consent form outlining our process, confidentiality, and your right to end our sessions at any time. Please review it carefully and ask any questions."
Phase 3: Scope of Practice & Referrals 10 min
YOU:
"Linda, it's important to stay within my scope of practice. If I identify issues that require medical or psychological intervention, I'll refer you to qualified professionals. I can collaborate with your existing healthcare providers, with your permission."
YOU:
"Do you currently work with any doctors, therapists, or other healthcare providers?"
Phase 4: Setting Expectations & Boundaries 5 min
YOU:
"Finally, let's set clear expectations. Our sessions will be [duration] long, and I'm available for support between sessions via [email/text/platform]. I'm committed to maintaining professional boundaries to ensure a safe and effective therapeutic relationship."

Handling Objections: Ethical & Legal

Navigating Compliance-Related Concerns

"I've heard Polyvagal Theory can cure anxiety. Can you guarantee results?"
"I understand your hope for relief. While Polyvagal-informed practices can significantly reduce anxiety symptoms, I can't guarantee a cure. Our work focuses on regulating your nervous system and building resilience, which can lead to a reduction in anxiety. However, individual results vary, and it's not a substitute for medical treatment."
"Can you prescribe medication if needed?"
"As a Certified Polyvagal Theory Specialist, I am not qualified to prescribe medication. That falls outside my scope of practice. If you feel medication might be helpful, I recommend consulting with a licensed psychiatrist or medical doctor. I'm happy to coordinate with your medical team."
"What if I have a crisis between sessions?"
"While I offer support between sessions via [email/text], I'm not a crisis responder. In case of a crisis, please contact emergency services (911) or a crisis hotline. I can provide you with a list of local resources for immediate support."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of informed consent when offering Polyvagal-informed services?

Key elements include clarifying your role as a Certified Polyvagal Theory Specialist (not a therapist or doctor), explaining the process, potential benefits, limitations, confidentiality, and the client's right to terminate services.

2. How should you handle a client who believes Polyvagal Theory can "cure" their anxiety?

Gently correct their understanding by explaining that Polyvagal-informed practices can significantly reduce anxiety symptoms by regulating the nervous system and building resilience, but it's not a guaranteed "cure" and not a substitute for medical treatment.

Key Takeaways

  • Clear Scope of Practice: Always clarify your role and qualifications to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Informed Consent is Crucial: Provide a detailed consent form and discuss it with clients.
  • Referrals are Essential: Know when to refer clients to qualified medical or mental health professionals.
  • Ethical Marketing: Avoid making guarantees or misleading claims about the effectiveness of Polyvagal-informed practices.
  • Professional Boundaries: Maintain clear boundaries to ensure a safe and effective client relationship.
