Handling Objections: Address Concerns with Confidence

Common Objections & Your Empathetic Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my students are working professionals. The program is designed to be flexible, allowing you to learn at your own pace. Plus, the skills you gain will ultimately save you time and energy in the long run."
"I'm worried I don't have the business skills."
"That's a common concern. The program includes comprehensive business training, covering everything from marketing to client acquisition to pricing strategies. You'll have access to templates, scripts, and ongoing support to build your practice."
"Can I really make money doing this?"
"Absolutely. Many of our graduates are generating $5,000 - $10,000 per month within their first year. Your income potential depends on your commitment and effort, but the demand for Polyvagal-informed practitioners is growing rapidly."
"I need to think about it."
"I totally understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Perhaps I can give you additional information to help you make an informed decision."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Connection & Rapport, Uncover Pain Points & Desires, Present Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.

2. What is a good response to the objection, "I need to think about it?"

A good response is to acknowledge their need to think, and then ask what specifically they need to think about, offering to provide more information.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for building a successful practice.

  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence can increase your enrollment rate.
