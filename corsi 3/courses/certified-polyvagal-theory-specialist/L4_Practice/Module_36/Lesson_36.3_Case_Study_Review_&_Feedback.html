Case Study 1: Sarah, 35, Anxiety and Social Isolation

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah, a 35-year-old woman, presented with chronic anxiety, social isolation, and difficulty forming meaningful connections. She reported feeling constantly on edge, experiencing panic attacks in social situations, and having a pervasive sense of loneliness.

Intervention: The intervention involved a combination of psychoeducation about Polyvagal Theory, SAFE Frameworkâ„¢-based exercises, and somatic experiencing techniques. Sarah was guided through exercises to identify and regulate her vagal tone, focusing on safety cues and social engagement strategies. Heart rate variability (HRV) biofeedback was also incorporated to provide real-time feedback on her autonomic nervous system regulation.

Outcomes: After 12 weeks of intervention, Sarah reported a significant reduction in anxiety symptoms, a decrease in the frequency and intensity of panic attacks, and an increased sense of social connection. Her HRV scores improved, indicating enhanced vagal tone and autonomic flexibility. She began attending social events and reported feeling more comfortable and confident in her interactions. A standardized anxiety scale showed a 40% reduction in her anxiety levels.

Presenting Symptoms: Mark, a 48-year-old man, presented with chronic lower back pain, depression, and a history of childhood trauma. He reported feeling hopeless, withdrawn, and unable to engage in activities he once enjoyed. His pain levels were consistently high, impacting his ability to work and maintain relationships.

Intervention: The intervention focused on addressing Mark's trauma history, regulating his autonomic nervous system, and reducing his pain levels.
