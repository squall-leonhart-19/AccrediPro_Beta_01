Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Many of my clients are busy professionals. The program is designed to be flexible and fit into your existing schedule. We can tailor the exercises to your specific needs, and the time commitment is only about 30 minutes per day. Think of it as an investment in your health and well-being, which will ultimately make you more productive and efficient."
"I need to think about it and discuss it with my husband."
"That's perfectly understandable. What specific questions or concerns do you have that you'd like to discuss with him? Perhaps I can address them now so you both have a clearer picture. Or, I‚Äôd be happy to hop on a 10-minute call with both of you to answer any questions he may have."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate you sharing that. I want to be transparent about the value you'll receive. Can I ask, what price point *would* feel comfortable for you, and what specific results would you need to see to feel that investment was worth it? Let's see if we can align those two."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I understand your frustration. It sounds like you've been through a lot. The Polyvagal-informed approach is different because it focuses on regulating your nervous system, which is the foundation for stress management and sleep. It's not just about treating symptoms; it's about addressing the root cause. I offer a satisfaction guarantee, so if you don't see any improvement within the first 30 days, I'll offer a full refund."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the discovery call structure?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Qualify, Explore the Problem & Desired Outcome, Present Your Solution, and Close & Next Steps.

2. What is a good response to the objection "I need to think about it"?

A good response is to ask what specifically they need to think about and offer to provide more information or answer any questions they have.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for building a thriving practice.
  • A structured discovery call helps you qualify prospects and present your solution effectively.
  • Handling objections with confidence and empathy is crucial for closing the sale.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and its potential return on investment.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines to increase your conversion rate.
