Discovery Call Script (30 Minutes)

Your Call Structure

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks so much for scheduling this call. I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, what sparked your interest in Polyvagal Theory and how do you think it might help you?"
YOU:
"That's interesting! I'm curious, what have you tried in the past to manage your anxiety?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Challenges (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the anxiety. What does it feel like in your body? When does it tend to show up?"
YOU:
"And how is the lack of sleep impacting your daily life? What activities are you missing out on?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the most overwhelmed you've ever felt, where are you right now?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're experiencing a significant level of stress and anxiety that's affecting your sleep and overall well-being. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach uses Polyvagal Theory to help you understand the connection between your nervous system and your emotional state. We'll work together to identify your triggers, build resilience, and create strategies for feeling safe and grounded. I typically work with clients for 3 months. Does this sound interesting to you?"
Phase 4: The Close (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe my program can truly help you regain control and feel more at peace. Are you ready to explore how we can work together?"

Handling Objections Like a Pro

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. It sounds like you're extremely busy. My program requires about 2 hours per week, including our sessions. Think of it as an investment in your well-being. If you continue feeling this anxious and overwhelmed, how will that impact your productivity and relationships?"
"I need to research Polyvagal Theory more."
"That's a great idea! I can send you some resources, including research papers and articles. However, remember that knowledge alone isn't enough. My program provides personalized guidance and support to help you APPLY these principles in your life."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can tell you that my clients typically report significant improvements in their anxiety levels, sleep quality, and overall sense of well-being. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first two weeks, I'll refund your investment."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! I offer a payment plan. You can split the total investment into three monthly installments. Would that make it easier for you to get started?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach as the solution, and closing the call.

2. How can you handle the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the investment by focusing on the potential value and ROI in terms of improved well-being, reduced expenses, and enhanced quality of life. You can also offer payment plans.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call is essential for converting prospects into clients.
  • Address objections with empathy and provide compelling solutions.
  • Confidently present your pricing and highlight the value of your services.
  • Practice your closing lines to increase your conversion rate.
  • Remember, client acquisition is a skill that improves with practice.
