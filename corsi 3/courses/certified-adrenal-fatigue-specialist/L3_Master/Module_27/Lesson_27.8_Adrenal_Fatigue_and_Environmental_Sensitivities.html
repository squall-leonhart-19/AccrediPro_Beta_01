The Case She Presents

Olivia's Client Case

What Olivia Tells You:

"I have a client, Susan, 52, with classic adrenal fatigue symptoms. I put her on a standard adaptogen blend and B vitamins. She says she feels *more* wired and anxious, not better. I‚Äôm following the protocol exactly. What am I missing?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Olivia consider Susan's methylation status before recommending B vitamins?
  • Was the adaptogen blend stimulating (e.g., rhodiola) or calming (e.g., ashwagandha)?
  • Did she assess for underlying anxiety or thyroid issues?
  • Did she explain the concept of paradoxical reactions?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Highlight the Art of Individualization

Explain that protocols are starting points, not rigid rules. Emphasize that the core of this work is adapting recommendations based on individual client needs and responses.

2

Discuss Methylation and B Vitamins

Explain that some individuals are poor methylators and may react negatively to methylated B vitamins. Suggest exploring non-methylated forms or assessing methylation pathways.

3

Review Adaptogen Selection

Discuss the stimulating vs. calming properties of different adaptogens. Explain that rhodiola might exacerbate anxiety in some individuals, while ashwagandha is generally more calming.

4

Introduce the Concept of Paradoxical Reactions

Explain that sometimes, clients experience the opposite of the intended effect. This isn't necessarily a failure, but valuable feedback indicating the need for adjustments.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Olivia, I appreciate you bringing this case forward. It shows you're thinking critically and committed to your clients. That's the mark of a great practitioner."
Encourage Self-Reflection
"Before I offer suggestions, what are your initial thoughts? What did you learn from Susan's reaction? What would you consider changing if you could go back?"
Offer Targeted Education
"One thing to consider: some individuals are sensitive to methylated B vitamins. It might be worth exploring non-methylated forms. Also, remember that adaptogens have different properties. Rhodiola can be stimulating, while ashwagandha is calming."
Empower Future Decisions
"Based on this, what adjustments would you like to make for Susan? Remember, it's about finding the right fit for *her* unique needs. I'm here to support you in that process."

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important aspect of individualizing care for adrenal fatigue clients?

Understanding the client's unique needs, sensitivities, and responses, rather than blindly following a standard protocol.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with a client case where the client experienced a paradoxical reaction?

Explain that paradoxical reactions are valuable feedback, encourage them to explore alternative approaches, and reassure them that this is part of the learning process.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating. Ask questions to stimulate critical thinking.
  • Protocols are guidelines, not rigid rules. Teach the art of individualization.
  • Paradoxical reactions are valuable data points. Use them to refine your approach.
  • Empower your mentees to develop their clinical judgment. Build their confidence.
  • Your experience is a gift. Sharing it elevates the entire field.
