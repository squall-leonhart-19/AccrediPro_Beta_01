Internal Regulation (I)

Internal regulation techniques are crucial for managing stress and supporting adrenal health. For children with ADHD, these techniques can help improve emotional regulation and reduce anxiety. Here are some strategies to implement with Emily:

  • Mindfulness Practices: Introduce simple mindfulness exercises, such as deep breathing and body scan meditations. These practices can help Emily become more aware of her body and emotions, reducing her stress response.
  • Emotional Expression: Encourage Emily to express her emotions in healthy ways, such as through art, journaling, or talking to a trusted adult. Suppressing emotions can exacerbate stress and impact adrenal function.
  • Cognitive Restructuring: Help Emily identify and challenge negative thought patterns that contribute to her anxiety. Cognitive restructuring can improve her emotional resilience and reduce stress.
  • Physical Activity: Encourage regular physical activity, such as playing sports, dancing, or going for walks. Exercise is a natural stress reliever and can improve mood and sleep quality.

A meta-analysis of 30 studies (n=2,456) published in "Complementary Therapies in Medicine" showed that mindfulness-based interventions significantly reduced anxiety symptoms in children with ADHD (Brown et al., 2022).
