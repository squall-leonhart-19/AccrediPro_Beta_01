Scenario 1: Client Boundaries & Scope of Practice

üë©

Susan, 52

Long-term client seeking support beyond adrenal fatigue.

The Situation
Susan, initially seeking help for adrenal fatigue, now confides in you about marital problems and asks for relationship advice.
Your Role
You are NOT a licensed therapist or counselor.
Ethical Concern
Providing advice outside your scope of practice could be harmful and legally problematic.
Your Goal
Maintain professional boundaries and provide appropriate support within your expertise.

Discovery Call Script: Boundary Setting

Navigating the Conversation with Susan

Phase 1: Acknowledge & Validate 2 min
YOU:
"Susan, thank you for sharing this with me. I truly appreciate your trust and openness."
Phase 2: Clearly Define Boundaries 3 min
YOU:
"As much as I care about your well-being, relationship advice falls outside my area of expertise as an Adrenal Fatigue Specialist. It's important for me to stay within my professional boundaries."
Phase 3: Offer Support Within Scope 3 min
YOU:
"I can certainly support you in managing the stress and emotional impact that these challenges might be having on your adrenal health. We can focus on strategies to cope with stress, improve sleep, and nourish your body during this time."
Phase 4: Provide Referral 2 min
YOU:
"For the relationship issues, I highly recommend speaking with a licensed therapist or counselor. I can provide you with a list of qualified professionals in your area who specialize in relationship counseling."

Handling Objections

Potential Objections & Your Responses

"But you know me so well, I'd rather talk to you than a stranger."
"I understand, Susan. And I value our relationship. However, a therapist has specialized training to help you navigate these specific challenges effectively. My role is to support your adrenal health, and I want to ensure you get the best possible care for all aspects of your well-being."
"I can't afford a therapist right now."
"There are often affordable options available. I can help you research community resources, sliding-scale clinics, or therapists who offer payment plans. In the meantime, let's focus on how we can manage your stress levels through our work together."
"I don't want to start all over with someone new."
"I understand that can feel daunting. Think of it as adding another valuable member to your support team. I'll be here to continue supporting your adrenal health, and the therapist will provide specialized guidance for your relationship. We can work together to ensure you receive comprehensive care."

Scenario 2: Testimonials & Endorsements

üë©

Maria, 48

Happy client willing to provide a testimonial.

The Situation
Maria experienced significant improvement in her energy levels and overall well-being after working with you. She offers to provide a glowing testimonial for your website.
Legal & Ethical Consideration
Testimonials must be truthful, non-misleading, and compliant with FTC guidelines regarding endorsements.
Your Responsibility
Ensure the testimonial is accurate, reflects Maria's genuine experience, and includes a disclaimer if results are not typical.
Your Goal
Utilize testimonials ethically and legally to promote your services.

Ethical Testimonial Guidelines

Guideline Description Example
Truthfulness Testimonials must accurately reflect the client's experience. "I felt a significant improvement in my energy levels within a few weeks of starting the program."
Non-Misleading Avoid exaggerating or making unsubstantiated claims. Instead of: "This program cured my adrenal fatigue!" Use: "This program significantly improved my symptoms of adrenal fatigue."
Disclosure Disclose any material connections between you and the client (e.g., if they received a discount for the testimonial). "I received a discounted rate for providing this testimonial, but my experience is genuine."
Typical Results Disclaimer Include a disclaimer stating that results may vary and are not typical. "Results may vary. This testimonial reflects one client's experience and is not a guarantee of similar outcomes."

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. A client offers you a gift worth $500 as a thank you. Is it ethical to accept it?

It depends on your professional code of ethics and your relationship with the client. Accepting a gift of significant value could blur professional boundaries and create a conflict of interest. It's generally advisable to politely decline or accept a smaller, symbolic gift.

2. You suspect a client is not being truthful about their symptoms. How do you handle it?

Address your concerns with empathy and curiosity. Ask open-ended questions to explore their experience further. Avoid accusatory language. If you continue to have concerns, document them and consider seeking guidance from a mentor or legal counsel.

Key Takeaways

  • Clearly define and maintain professional boundaries with clients to avoid ethical conflicts.
  • Ensure all testimonials and endorsements are truthful, non-misleading, and compliant with FTC guidelines.
  • Stay within your scope of practice and refer clients to qualified professionals when necessary.
  • Continuously educate yourself on legal and ethical standards relevant to your practice.
  • Document all client interactions and decisions to protect yourself legally and ethically.
