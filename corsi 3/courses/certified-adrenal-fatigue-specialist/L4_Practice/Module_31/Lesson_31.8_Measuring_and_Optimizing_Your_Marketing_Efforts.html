Your Discovery Call Script

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Rapport & Introduction 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about how I can help you. To start, can you tell me a bit about what led you to reach out to me?"
YOU (Sarah):
"It's great that you found me through a Google search. What specifically caught your eye about my approach?"
Phase 2: Problem Exploration 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"You mentioned fatigue, brain fog, and mood swings. Can you describe these symptoms in more detail? When did they start, and how do they impact your daily life?"
YOU (Sarah):
"Have you consulted with any healthcare professionals or tried any specific interventions to address these issues? What were the results?"
YOU (Sarah):
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the most debilitating, how would you rate the impact of these symptoms on your overall quality of life?"
Phase 3: Solution Presentation 10 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing a cluster of symptoms that are significantly impacting your life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU (Sarah):
"My approach focuses on identifying and addressing the root causes of adrenal fatigue through personalized nutrition, lifestyle modifications, and targeted supplementation. I've helped many women in their 40s regain their energy, improve their focus, and stabilize their moods. My program is 12 weeks long, and most clients start to see improvements within the first month."
YOU (Sarah):
"How does this approach resonate with you? Does it align with what you're looking for?"
Phase 4: Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU (Sarah):
"Susan, based on our conversation, I believe I can help you achieve your goals. Are you interested in learning more about my program and how we can work together?"

Mastering Objection Handling

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need more time to consider? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? Let's address your concerns now so you can make an informed decision."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate your honesty. Let's break down the value you'll receive. My program includes [list key components and benefits]. Think about the cost of continuing to struggle with these symptoms ‚Äì doctor visits, supplements that don't work, lost productivity. What if we could actually solve this in 12 weeks? What would that be worth to you?"
"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand that time is valuable. My program is designed to be flexible and integrate into your existing lifestyle. We'll work together to create a schedule that works for you. Plus, think about how much more energy and focus you'll have once we address your adrenal fatigue. That increased productivity will actually save you time in the long run."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration. It's common to feel discouraged after trying multiple solutions without success. My approach is different because I focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of your symptoms, rather than just masking them. I also provide personalized support and guidance every step of the way. Let's explore what hasn't worked in the past and develop a tailored plan that addresses your specific needs."

Case Study

Client: Sarah M., 48 years old

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic fatigue, difficulty concentrating, and weight gain despite a healthy diet.

Intervention: Sarah enrolled in a 12-week adrenal reset program, which included personalized nutrition plans, stress management techniques, and gentle exercise routines.

Outcomes: After completing the program, Sarah reported a significant increase in energy levels, improved focus, and a loss of 10 pounds. She also gained valuable tools to manage stress and maintain a healthy lifestyle.

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your solution, and closing with a call-to-action.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, break down the value you provide, and compare the cost to the ongoing impact of their symptoms.

Key Takeaways
