Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation about restorative sleep. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your struggles with sleep. I understand how frustrating and exhausting it can be. It sounds like you've already tried some common approaches, but let's dig a little deeper. What does your typical evening routine look like?"
MARIA:
"Well, I usually work until about 9 PM, then maybe watch some TV or scroll through social media to unwind before trying to sleep around 11."
YOU:
"Okay, that's helpful. It sounds like you're trying to relax, but some of those habits might actually be working against your body's natural sleep rhythms. Let's talk about the importance of your circadian rhythm and how we can optimize it for better sleep."
YOU:
"Many people find that the blue light from screens interferes with melatonin production, which is essential for sleep onset. Also, the content you're consuming before bed could be stimulating your mind, making it harder to fall asleep. Are you open to experimenting with some alternative evening routines?"
MARIA:
"I guess so. I'm willing to try anything at this point. What do you suggest?"
YOU:
"Great. We can explore options like a calming bath, reading a physical book, gentle stretching, or listening to relaxing music. The goal is to create a buffer between your workday and bedtime, signaling to your body that it's time to wind down. How does that sound to you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to understand a client's evening routine before suggesting sleep remedies?

Understanding their routine helps identify potential triggers and obstacles to restful sleep, allowing for a more personalized and effective approach.

2. What are some examples of alternative evening routines you might suggest to a client struggling with insomnia?

Examples include a calming bath, reading a physical book, gentle stretching, listening to relaxing music, or practicing mindfulness.

Key Takeaways

  • Prioritize understanding the client's current sleep habits and evening routine.
  • Explain the importance of circadian rhythm in simple terms.
  • Suggest alternative evening routines that promote relaxation and reduce stimulation.
  • Emphasize that improving sleep is a process that requires consistency and patience.
