Step 1: Identify Systems Involved
This case involves multiple overlapping systems: neurological (pain, brain fog, headaches), immune (autoimmune tendencies, allergies), endocrine (potential HPA axis dysregulation due to chronic stress), and environmental (chemical sensitivities). We must consider the interplay between these systems.
Step 2: Look for Root Causes
Potential root causes include: chronic inflammation (elevated CRP, positive ANA), immune dysregulation (autoimmune tendencies, allergies), environmental triggers (chemical sensitivities), and HPA axis dysregulation (chronic stress, insomnia). The history of Lyme disease also warrants consideration for potential lingering effects.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Going On?

1

Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)

Chemical sensitivities, allergies, and autoimmune tendencies can indicate MCAS. Elevated mast cell mediators (histamine, tryptase) may be present. A 2022 review in Clinical Reviews in Allergy & Immunology highlights the overlap between MCAS and fibromyalgia.

2

Mold Toxicity

Exposure to mold can trigger systemic inflammation, neurological symptoms, and chemical sensitivities, mimicking fibromyalgia. Consider environmental assessment and biomarkers of mold exposure.

3

Small Fiber Neuropathy (SFN)

Widespread pain and fatigue can be associated with SFN, a condition affecting small nerve fibers. Skin biopsy can confirm the diagnosis.

4

Heavy Metal Toxicity

Exposure to heavy metals (mercury, lead) can contribute to neurological symptoms and autoimmune dysregulation. Consider assessing heavy metal levels through urine or hair analysis.

Key Takeaways

  • Complex cases like Eleanor's require a systems-thinking approach, considering the interplay between neurological, immune, endocrine, and environmental factors.
  • Prioritize addressing HPA axis dysregulation, reducing inflammation, and supporting immune function in a phased manner.
  • Consider differential diagnoses such as MCAS, mold toxicity, and small fiber neuropathy, and conduct appropriate testing.
  • Always be aware of red flags that warrant referral to a medical doctor, such as progressive neurological deficits or unexplained weight loss.
  • Personalize the intervention plan based on the client's individual needs, lab results, and response to treatment.
