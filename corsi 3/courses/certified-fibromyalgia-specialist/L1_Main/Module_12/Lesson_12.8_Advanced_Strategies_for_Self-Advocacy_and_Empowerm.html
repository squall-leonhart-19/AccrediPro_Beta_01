Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on setting the stage for L1 Deep Dive. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, it sounds like you've been through quite a journey with your fibromyalgia. Before we jump into specific strategies, I want to acknowledge the strength it takes to manage chronic pain and keep pushing forward. Many of my clients with fibromyalgia experience similar challenges, and it's definitely possible to improve your quality of life."
MARIA:
"Thank you. Sometimes I feel like no one really understands what it's like."
YOU:
"I understand. And that's why we're here. To start, I'd like to dive a bit deeper into understanding what might be contributing to your symptoms beyond just the diagnosis. We'll explore different facets of your health and lifestyle to uncover potential root causes."
YOU:
"Specifically, I'm curious about your sleep patterns, your stress levels, and your gut health. These three areas often play a significant role in fibromyalgia symptoms. Does that sound like a good place to start?"
MARIA:
"Yes, definitely. I've always suspected my gut was involved somehow."
YOU:
"Great. Let's begin with your sleep. Can you describe a typical night for me?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's experience with fibromyalgia before suggesting solutions?

Validating their experience builds trust and rapport, making them more receptive to your guidance. It shows you understand their struggles and are not dismissing their pain.

2. What are three key areas to explore with a client during an L1 Deep Dive for fibromyalgia?

Sleep patterns, stress levels, and gut health are three key areas to explore, as they often play a significant role in fibromyalgia symptoms.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial for building trust with clients who have fibromyalgia.
  • An L1 Deep Dive involves exploring various facets of a client's health and lifestyle to identify potential root causes of their symptoms.
  • Sleep, stress, and gut health are often interconnected and can significantly impact fibromyalgia symptoms.
  • Encourage clients to actively participate in the coaching process and explore lifestyle changes.
