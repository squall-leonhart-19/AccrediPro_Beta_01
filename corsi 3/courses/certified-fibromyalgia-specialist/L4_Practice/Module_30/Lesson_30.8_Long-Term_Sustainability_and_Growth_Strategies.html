Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you saw my ad in the local wellness group. What resonated with you that made you book this call?"
YOU:
"I understand you've been dealing with fibromyalgia for 3 years. What's been the most challenging aspect of living with it?"
Phase 2: Uncover Pain Points & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage your fibromyalgia symptoms? What worked, what didn't, and what are you hoping to achieve through our work together?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like without fibromyalgia? What activities would you be able to enjoy again?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1-10, with 1 being no impact and 10 being severely impacted, how would you rate fibromyalgia's impact on your daily life?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I understand you're experiencing widespread pain, fatigue, and brain fog, and you're looking for a way to get back to the activities you love. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on addressing the root causes of fibromyalgia, rather than just managing the symptoms. I offer a personalized 12-week program that includes lifestyle modifications, nutritional guidance, stress management techniques, and gentle movement exercises. A 2023 study in the 'Journal of Pain Research' found that integrative approaches like these can significantly improve pain and quality of life in fibromyalgia patients."
YOU:
"I've helped many women in their 40s and 50s reclaim their lives from fibromyalgia. Most of my clients start experiencing noticeable improvements within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on everything we've discussed, do you feel like this program could be a good fit for you?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand that cost is a concern. Let's talk about the value you'll receive from the program. Many of my clients find that they actually save money in the long run by reducing their reliance on medications and doctor visits. What is your health worth to you?"
"I've tried so many things before that haven't worked."
"I understand your skepticism. My approach is different because it focuses on identifying and addressing the underlying causes of your fibromyalgia, rather than just treating the symptoms. We'll work together to create a personalized plan that's tailored to your specific needs and goals."
"I need to think about it."
"I respect that. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. I also have a client success story I can share."
"I need to discuss this with my family first."
"That's perfectly understandable. What questions do you think they might have? I'm happy to answer them now or schedule a brief call with you and your family to address any concerns."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call for a potential fibromyalgia client?

Building rapport, understanding pain points, presenting a personalized solution, and confidently closing the sale.

2. How can you effectively address the common objection of "I'm not sure if I can afford it?"

Focus on the value and long-term savings, and explore payment plan options if available.

3. What are some realistic income scenarios for a fibromyalgia specialist who is actively acquiring clients?

With a program fee of $2,500, acquiring 2 clients/month can generate $5,000, 5 clients/month can generate $12,500, and 10 clients/month can generate $25,000.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is crucial for building a thriving fibromyalgia practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call can help you connect with potential clients, understand their needs, and present your solution effectively.
  • Confidently handling objections and presenting your pricing are essential skills for closing sales.
  • Realistic income goals can motivate you to take consistent action and build a successful practice.
  • Remember to always focus on the value you provide and the positive impact you can have on your clients' lives.
