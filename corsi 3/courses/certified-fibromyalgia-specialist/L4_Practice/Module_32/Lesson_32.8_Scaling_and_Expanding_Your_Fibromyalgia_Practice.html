Handling Objections

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's important to prioritize. My program is designed to be flexible and fit into your life. We can tailor the sessions to your schedule, and the lifestyle changes are gradual and sustainable. Think of it as investing time now to gain more energy and time in the long run. How does that sound?"
"I've tried so many things before that didn't work."
"I hear your frustration. Many of my clients have had similar experiences. What sets my approach apart is that we focus on identifying and addressing the root causes, rather than just treating symptoms. We use a personalized plan based on your unique needs and circumstances. And I provide ongoing support and accountability to keep you on track. Would you like to hear about some success stories from my other clients?"
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can guarantee that I will provide you with the tools, knowledge, and support you need to make meaningful progress. The success of the program depends on your commitment and active participation. However, I'm confident that if you follow the program and implement the recommendations, you'll see significant improvements in your health and well-being. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first two weeks, I'll refund your investment."
"I need to discuss this with my family first."
"Absolutely! I completely understand. It's important to have their support. Would it be helpful if I sent you some information you could share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a quick call with you and your family to answer any questions they may have. What works best for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important goal of the discovery call?

The primary goal is to determine if you can genuinely help the prospect and if your program is a good fit for their needs. It's not just about making a sale!

2. How should you handle the "It's too expensive" objection?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value of your program in terms of the long-term benefits and return on investment (e.g., reduced medical costs, improved quality of life).

Key Takeaways

  • Preparation is key: Understand your prospect's needs and challenges before the call.
  • Active listening: Truly hear what your prospect is saying and respond thoughtfully.
  • Value-based pricing: Confidently present your prices and highlight the value you provide.
  • Objection handling: Be prepared to address common objections with empathy and solutions.
  • Consistent practice: The more you practice, the more natural and confident you'll become.
