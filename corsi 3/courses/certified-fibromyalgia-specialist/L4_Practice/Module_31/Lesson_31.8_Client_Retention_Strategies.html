Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I understand you're looking for support with your fibromyalgia. To make the best use of our time, I'd like to understand your situation, explain how I can help, and then discuss next steps. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Before we dive in, I'd love to hear a bit about what led you to reach out to me specifically."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, can you tell me more about your experience with fibromyalgia? What are your biggest challenges right now?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to manage your symptoms? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine you wake up three months from now, and your fibromyalgia is significantly better managed. What does that day look like? What are you able to do that you can't do now?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Expertise 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're struggling with [summarize her key pain points: pain, fatigue, inability to work]. Is that accurate?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on identifying and addressing the root causes of your symptoms, not just masking them. We'll use a combination of lifestyle modifications, targeted nutrition, and stress management techniques to create a personalized plan that works for you. Many of my clients experience a 50-70% reduction in their symptoms within the first 8 weeks."
YOU:
"For example, I worked with a client, Maria, age 48, who was unable to work due to fibromyalgia. After 12 weeks, she was back at work part-time and feeling more energetic than she had in years."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, do you have any questions about what I've shared so far?"
YOU:
"Based on our conversation, I believe I can help you significantly improve your fibromyalgia symptoms and get back to living a fuller life. Are you ready to explore how we can work together?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand that cost is a concern. Let's talk about the value of investing in your health. How much have you already spent on treatments that haven't worked? What is it costing you to continue living with these symptoms ‚Äì in terms of lost income, reduced quality of life, and missed opportunities? My program is designed to provide lasting results, which can save you money in the long run. We also have flexible payment options available."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand that time is valuable. The program requires a commitment of a few hours per week, but it's an investment in your long-term health and well-being. Think of it this way: how much time are you currently spending managing your symptoms ‚Äì going to doctor's appointments, resting due to fatigue, and dealing with pain? By addressing the root causes of your fibromyalgia, we can actually help you reclaim your time and energy."
"I need to think about it."
"That's perfectly fine. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the program itself, the investment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. Perhaps we can schedule a brief follow-up call to address any remaining questions."
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked."
"I understand your frustration. Many of my clients have also tried multiple approaches before finding success with my program. The key difference is that we focus on identifying and addressing the underlying causes of your fibromyalgia, rather than just treating the symptoms. We also create a highly personalized plan that's tailored to your specific needs and goals. I'm confident that I can help you achieve significant improvement."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem and goals, presenting your approach and expertise, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "I'm not sure if I can afford it?"

Acknowledge their concern, discuss the value of investing in their health, explore the costs of continuing to live with their symptoms, and offer flexible payment options.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for client acquisition.
  • Focus on understanding the prospect's pain points and goals.
  • Confidently present your approach and expertise.
  • Address objections with empathy and solutions.
  • Practice your pricing and closing lines to increase conversions.
