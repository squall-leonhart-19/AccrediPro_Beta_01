Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time. I remember you were very engaged during the workshop. What resonated most with you?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear. So, Susan, tell me a bit more about what's been going on for you since your fibromyalgia diagnosis."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe the pain you're experiencing? Where is it located, and how intense is it on a scale of 1 to 10?"
YOU:
"And how is this impacting your daily life? What activities are you struggling with?"
YOU:
"What treatments have you tried so far, and what were the results?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and eliminate one symptom, which would it be, and why?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're experiencing significant pain, sleep disturbances, and brain fog, which are impacting your ability to enjoy your life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on addressing the root causes of your fibromyalgia symptoms through lifestyle modifications, targeted nutritional support, and stress management techniques. We'll work together to create a personalized plan that addresses your specific needs and goals."
YOU:
"A 2021 study in the journal *Pain* showed that integrative approaches, similar to mine, resulted in a 40% reduction in pain levels and improved quality of life for fibromyalgia patients. I've personally seen similar results with my clients."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you feel like it could be a good fit for your needs?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you about the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand. Many of my clients have similar concerns. Let's explore some options. Do you have a specific budget in mind? We can also discuss payment plans or explore which program components are most important to you to start with. What's most important is that we find a way for you to get the relief you deserve."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. However, my approach is highly personalized, and we'll be constantly monitoring your progress and making adjustments as needed. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not seeing results within the first 30 days, we can discuss a refund. I am confident that together, we can find a path towards feeling better."
"I need to talk to my family about it."
"Of course. Would you like me to send you a detailed program outline and some testimonials you can share with them? Or perhaps a brief call with all of you to answer any questions?"
"I've tried so many things already that haven't worked."
"I hear you. It sounds like you've been through a lot. My program is different because we focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of your symptoms, rather than just masking them. We'll use evidence-based strategies and personalized interventions to create a plan that's tailored to your unique needs. Are you open to trying something different?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your approach, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection, "It's too expensive?"

Acknowledge their concern, explore their budget, highlight the value of your program, and discuss payment options. Focus on the long-term benefits and ROI.

3. What are some effective closing lines to use during a discovery call?

"Based on everything you've shared, I'm confident that I can help you. Are you ready to take the first step?" or "Does this approach resonate with you? Would you like to get started?"

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering client acquisition is essential for building a thriving practice.
  • A well-structured discovery call can significantly increase your conversion rate.
  • Handling objections with empathy and confidence is crucial for closing deals.
  • Clearly presenting your pricing and value proposition is key to attracting high-quality clients.
  • Consistent effort and effective sales strategies can lead to a substantial income.
