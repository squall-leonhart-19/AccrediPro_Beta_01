Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand. Can we talk about the value you'd receive? Many of my clients find that by addressing the root causes of their symptoms, they spend less on ineffective supplements and doctor visits. We can also discuss payment plan options to make it more accessible."
"I need to research this more."
"Of course! I can provide you with some resources, including testimonials from satisfied clients and information about my certifications. What specific information are you looking for?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I can share that many of my clients experience significant improvements in their symptoms within a few weeks. We'll work closely together to personalize the program to your unique needs, and I'll be there to support you every step of the way."
"Is this covered by insurance?"
"That's a great question! As a Certified Specialist, my services are typically not covered by insurance. However, some clients have successfully used their Health Savings Account (HSA) or Flexible Spending Account (FSA) funds. It's best to check with your insurance provider for clarification."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to disclose your credentials and scope of practice at the beginning of a discovery call?

To ensure transparency and avoid misunderstandings about the services you provide. It's crucial to establish clear boundaries and protect yourself legally.

2. What are some key points to document during a discovery call?

Document the client's symptoms, medical history, previous treatments, and expectations. This information helps you determine if you can ethically and effectively support the client.

3. How can you address a client's concern about the cost of your services?

Focus on the value they'll receive, discuss potential cost savings from reducing ineffective treatments, and offer payment plan options.

Key Takeaways

  • Transparency is key: Always disclose your credentials and scope of practice upfront.
  • Document everything: Keep detailed records of client interactions and information shared.
  • Focus on value: Highlight the benefits of your services and the positive impact they can have on clients' lives.
  • Address objections confidently: Prepare responses to common concerns and be ready to reassure potential clients.
  • Practice makes perfect: Rehearse your discovery call script and pricing presentation to build confidence.
