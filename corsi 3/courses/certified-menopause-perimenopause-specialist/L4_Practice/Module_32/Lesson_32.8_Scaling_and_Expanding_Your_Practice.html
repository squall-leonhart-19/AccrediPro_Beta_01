The Discovery Call: Your Roadmap

30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Warm Welcome & Rapport (5 minutes) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm so glad we connected. To start, could you tell me a little more about what prompted you to reach out?"
YOU:
"I understand you found me through the menopause support group. What were you hoping to find there?"
Phase 2: Uncover the Pain Points (10 minutes) 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned night sweats, insomnia, and mood swings. When did these symptoms start, and how are they impacting your daily life?"
YOU:
"I hear you've tried HRT. What were the side effects you experienced, and what are your thoughts on other options?"
YOU:
"Imagine a day where you wake up refreshed, without night sweats, and feel calm and in control. What would that be worth to you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution (10 minutes) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're dealing with a constellation of symptoms that are significantly affecting your quality of life. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My approach focuses on identifying the root causes of your symptoms, not just masking them. I work with clients using a personalized plan that addresses nutrition, lifestyle, and targeted support to restore hormonal balance. Many clients report significant improvements within a few weeks."
YOU:
"This includes comprehensive lab testing, one-on-one coaching calls, and access to a private support community."
Phase 4: Offer & Close (5 minutes) 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Susan, I'm confident I can help you reclaim your energy and well-being. Does this sound like the kind of support you're looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me share with you the details of my program and how you can get started."

Handling Client Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. Let's break down what you're currently spending on managing your symptoms ‚Äì doctor visits, supplements, lost productivity. What if, in just a few months, we could significantly reduce or eliminate those costs? Also, I have payment plan options available."
"I need to think about it and do some more research."
"Of course. I want you to feel completely comfortable. What specific research are you planning to do? Perhaps I can provide you with some resources or testimonials from past clients to help you make an informed decision. What information would be most helpful for you?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me. I've tried so many things already."
"I hear your frustration. It's common to feel skeptical after trying multiple solutions that haven't worked. My approach is different because it's highly personalized and addresses the root causes of your symptoms. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee - if you don't see improvements within the first month, we can reassess and adjust the plan accordingly."
"I don't have the time for another program."
"I understand that time is valuable. My program is designed to be manageable and integrate seamlessly into your life. The coaching sessions are flexible, and the recommendations are practical and sustainable. What if investing just a few hours a week could dramatically improve your energy and well-being, allowing you to accomplish more in other areas of your life?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's pain points, presenting your solution, and confidently offering the program.

2. How should you handle the objection "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, ask clarifying questions about their concerns, and offer additional resources or support.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call process leads to higher conversion rates.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence builds trust.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your program and its potential ROI.
  • Consistent practice and refinement of your sales skills are essential for success.
