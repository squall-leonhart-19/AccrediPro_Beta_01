Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time for a group program."
"I understand. Time is precious. The live sessions are 90 minutes per week, and all sessions are recorded so you can catch up at your convenience. Many participants find that the time invested actually *saves* them time in the long run, as they become more efficient at managing their symptoms and improving their overall well-being."
"I'm worried about sharing my personal information in a group setting."
"That's a valid concern. I create a safe and supportive environment where everyone feels comfortable sharing. You're always in control of what you share, and all participants agree to maintain confidentiality. Plus, you'll find that connecting with other women who understand what you're going through can be incredibly empowering."
"I've tried other things before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear you. It can be frustrating to feel like you've tried everything. 'Menopause Mastery' is different because it takes a holistic approach, addressing the root causes of your symptoms rather than just masking them. We focus on personalized solutions tailored to your unique needs. In a recent survey of our program graduates, 85% reported significant improvement in their menopause symptoms within 10 weeks."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, absolutely! We offer a payment plan of three monthly installments to make the program more accessible. Would you like me to send you the details?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key benefits of offering a group program versus 1:1 coaching?

Group programs allow you to serve more clients at once, creating a sense of community and peer support, and often lead to increased revenue and impact.

2. How can you effectively address the objection that a potential client doesn't have time for a group program?

Highlight the flexibility of recorded sessions, emphasize the time-saving benefits of managing symptoms effectively, and showcase testimonials from busy clients who have successfully participated.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs provide a scalable way to impact more lives and increase your income.
  • Craft a compelling program name and description that resonates with your target audience.
  • Address common objections with empathy and highlight the unique benefits of your program.
  • Confidently present your pricing and offer payment options to make your program accessible.
  • Remember, building a thriving group program takes time and effort. Stay consistent, adapt to feedback, and celebrate your successes!
