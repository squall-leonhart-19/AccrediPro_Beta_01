Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing your experience with me. It takes courage to reach out, and I want you to know you're not alone. What you're describing is incredibly common during perimenopause, and there are absolutely things we can do to help you feel better."
MARIA:
"I'm so glad to hear that. I was starting to think I was just losing my mind. My mom didn't go through this!"
YOU:
"Everyone's experience is unique, and genetics are only part of the story. Lifestyle and environment play a big role too. Let's focus on what we CAN control to get you back on track."
YOU:
"From what you've said, it sounds like sleep disruption, mood swings, and managing hot flashes are your top priorities. Is that a fair assessment?"
MARIA:
"Yes, definitely. If I could just sleep, I think I could handle everything else."
YOU:
"Okay. Let's start by exploring your sleep hygiene and daily routines. I'm going to ask some detailed questions, and we'll use your answers to create a personalized plan. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's experience at the beginning of a coaching session?

Validating her experience builds trust and rapport. It shows you understand her challenges and creates a safe space for her to share openly.

2. What are some open-ended questions you can ask to gather more information about a client's lifestyle?

Examples include: "What does a typical day look like for you?", "Tell me about your sleep routine", "What are your biggest stressors?"

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate a client's experience before offering solutions.
  • Ask open-ended questions to gather detailed information about her lifestyle.
  • Focus on lifestyle modifications before considering hormone replacement therapy.
  • Encourage clients to express their feelings and concerns.
  • Remind clients that they are not alone and that there are solutions available.
