Identifying Key Stakeholders

Your first step is to identify potential referral sources. Consider professionals who already work with clients experiencing issues related to libido, sexual health, or overall well-being. This might include:

  • Physicians (GPs, gynecologists, urologists, endocrinologists)
  • Therapists and counselors (marriage and family therapists, sex therapists, psychologists)
  • Sex educators and coaches
  • Wellness practitioners (nutritionists, yoga instructors, massage therapists)
  • Other complementary health professionals (e.g., pelvic floor therapists)

Don't limit yourself to local connections. Online communities, professional associations, and social media groups can also be valuable sources of referrals and collaboration.

Collaboration can take many forms, from cross-referrals to joint ventures. Consider partnering with complementary businesses and organizations to offer integrated services. For example:

Creating Referral Programs and Incentives

A well-designed referral program can be a powerful tool for generating new clients. Consider offering incentives to other professionals who refer clients to your practice. This could include:

  • A percentage of the initial consultation fee
  • A free session for the referring professional
  • A discount on your services for their clients

Make sure your referral program is clearly communicated and easy to understand. Track your referrals to measure the effectiveness of your program.
