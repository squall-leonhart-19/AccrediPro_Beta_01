The Boundary Challenge

Alicia's Questionable Scenario

Alicia's Account:

"I started working with Sarah, 52, who is experiencing low libido after menopause. She's really sweet, but she texts me almost every day with questions or updates. I feel bad ignoring her, but it's taking up so much of my time. Am I being too rigid if I set boundaries?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Has Alicia established clear boundaries with Sarah from the beginning?
  • Does Alicia understand the importance of maintaining professional boundaries for ethical practice?
  • Is Alicia experiencing compassion fatigue or burnout?
  • What strategies can Alicia use to address Sarah's texting behavior respectfully?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Alicia

1

Validate Her Feelings

Acknowledge that it's normal to feel conflicted about setting boundaries, especially when working with empathetic clients.

2

Explain the "Why" of Boundaries

Help Alicia understand that boundaries aren't about being rigid, but about protecting her time, energy, and the integrity of the therapeutic relationship.

3

Provide Practical Strategies

Offer specific scripts and techniques for addressing Sarah's texting behavior in a respectful and assertive manner.

4

Discuss Self-Care

Encourage Alicia to prioritize self-care activities to prevent burnout and maintain her well-being.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Empathy
"Alicia, I totally get why you're feeling conflicted. It's natural to want to help our clients, especially when they're going through a tough time."

"What are your thoughts on why setting boundaries is important in our line of work?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of setting boundaries in a therapeutic relationship?

The primary purpose is to protect the practitioner's time and energy, and to maintain the integrity of the therapeutic relationship. This ensures sustainable and effective support for all clients.

2. Give an example of a respectful way to address a client who frequently texts outside of scheduled sessions.

Key Takeaways

  • New practitioners often struggle with setting boundaries and managing client expectations.
  • Effective mentoring involves validating feelings, providing practical strategies, and encouraging self-reflection.
  • Role-playing and sharing personal experiences can help mentees navigate difficult situations.
  • Prioritizing self-care is essential for both mentors and mentees to prevent burnout.
