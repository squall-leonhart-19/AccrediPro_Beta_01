The Case She Presents

Aisha's Client Case

What Aisha Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, experiencing low libido after menopause. I suggested some lifestyle changes like exercise and better sleep. I also recommended a maca supplement. She is still feeling frustrated after a month. Am I missing something?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Aisha explore Sarah's hormone levels with her doctor?
  • Did she assess for relationship dynamics impacting libido?
  • Was the maca dosage and form appropriate?
  • Did she discuss realistic timelines for improvement?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Aisha

1

Emphasize Hormone Testing

Explain the importance of comprehensive hormone testing (estrogen, testosterone, etc.) post-menopause. Lifestyle changes alone may not be sufficient.

2

Explore Relationship Factors

Discuss the impact of relationship satisfaction, communication, and intimacy on libido. Suggest exploring these areas with Sarah.

3

Optimize Supplement Protocol

Review the importance of appropriate maca dosage and form. Also, discuss other potential supplements like Tribulus Terrestris or Fenugreek, and their contraindications.

4

Set Realistic Expectations

Reinforce the importance of setting realistic expectations with clients. Emphasize that improvements may take time and require a multi-faceted approach.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Effort
"Aisha, I can see you're really dedicated to helping Sarah. It's great that you're proactively seeking ways to improve her situation."
Inquire About Her Thought Process
"Tell me more about why you chose those specific recommendations for Sarah. What were you hoping to achieve?"
Offer Alternative Perspectives
"In cases of post-menopausal low libido, hormone levels often play a significant role. Encouraging Sarah to discuss hormone testing with her doctor could provide valuable insights."
Provide Encouragement and Support
"Remember, you're still new to this. It's okay to feel unsure. The key is to keep learning and refining your approach. I'm here to support you every step of the way."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of providing constructive feedback to a mentee?

Creating a safe and supportive environment where they feel comfortable learning and growing.

2. How can you help a mentee develop their clinical reasoning skills?

By asking open-ended questions that encourage them to think critically about the case and consider different perspectives.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves creating a supportive and encouraging environment for mentees.
  • Emphasize the importance of thorough assessment, including hormone testing and relationship dynamics.
  • Help mentees develop their clinical reasoning skills by asking probing questions and encouraging critical thinking.
  • Provide specific and actionable feedback that empowers mentees to improve their practice.
  • Remember that mentoring is a continuous learning process for both the mentor and the mentee.
