The Case Olivia Presents

Olivia's Client: Sarah, 48

What Olivia Tells You:

"Sarah, 48, came in with anxiety and feeling 'stuck'. We explored her childhood, and she had a lot of sadness around her parents' divorce. I felt so bad for her, I let her talk for the entire 90 minutes. I didn't really do any Inner Child Healing techniques. Now I feel like I failed her."

Points to Consider:

  • Was there a clear session structure?
  • Did Olivia maintain professional boundaries?
  • Were Inner Child Healing techniques used appropriately?
  • Did Olivia attend to her own emotional response during the session?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Olivia

1

Empathy vs. Sympathy

Explain the importance of empathy (understanding) without sympathy (over-identifying). Sympathy can blur boundaries.

2

Session Structure

Review the importance of a structured session: check-in, technique, processing, wrap-up. A clear structure provides safety.

3

Technique Integration

Discuss specific Inner Child Healing techniques Olivia could have used in the session, such as re-parenting statements or guided visualization.

4

Self-Care for the Practitioner

Emphasize the importance of Olivia's own self-care to prevent burnout and vicarious trauma. Discuss grounding techniques.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Effort
"Olivia, I commend you for being so open and willing to learn. It takes courage to review sessions that feel challenging."
Validate Feelings
"It's completely normal to feel overwhelmed, especially with clients who trigger our own emotions. It shows you're empathetic."
Offer Guidance
"Let's explore the difference between empathy and sympathy. Empathy is connecting with Sarah's experience; sympathy is taking on her pain. We want to hold space without getting lost."
Collaborative Problem-Solving
"What specific Inner Child Healing techniques do you think might have been helpful for Sarah, even if you only had 15 minutes to use one? Let's brainstorm."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the key difference between empathy and sympathy in a therapeutic relationship?

Empathy is understanding and connecting with the client's experience, while sympathy is taking on their pain. Empathy maintains healthy boundaries, while sympathy can lead to over-involvement.

2. Why is session structure important, especially for new practitioners?

A structured session provides a sense of safety and predictability for both the client and the practitioner. It helps the practitioner stay on track and ensures that key elements (check-in, technique, processing, wrap-up) are addressed.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves creating a safe space for mentees to be vulnerable.
  • Guiding new practitioners requires balancing empathy with professional boundaries.
  • Providing concrete tools and techniques empowers mentees to develop their skills.
  • Encouraging self-reflection and self-care is crucial for preventing burnout.
