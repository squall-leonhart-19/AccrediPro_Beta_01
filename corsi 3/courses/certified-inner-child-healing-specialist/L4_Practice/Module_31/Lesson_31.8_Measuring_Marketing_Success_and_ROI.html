Mastering Objections

Overcoming Concerns with Confidence

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. The program requires a commitment of a few hours per week. However, think about the time you're already spending feeling stuck and unhappy. This program is an investment in reclaiming your time and energy. Plus, the sessions are flexible, and we can work around your schedule."
"I've tried therapy before, and it didn't work."
"I hear you. It's frustrating when things don't work out as planned. My program is different from traditional therapy because it focuses specifically on inner child healing using the NURSE method we discussed, which is a very targeted approach. Also, I offer ongoing support and personalized guidance throughout the 12 weeks, which can make a big difference."
"Can I pay in installments?"
"Yes, I offer a payment plan option. Instead of paying the full amount upfront, you can break it down into smaller monthly installments to make it more manageable for your budget. Let me tell you more about the payment options."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right decision for you."

    Introduction

    The discovery call is a pivotal moment in your journey to building a thriving Inner Child Healing practice. It's your opportunity to connect with potential clients, understand their deepest needs, and showcase how your program can transform their lives. Mastering this skill can significantly increase your client conversion rate and boost your income.

    The Four Phases of an Effective Discovery Call

    A well-structured discovery call follows a clear path, guiding the prospect from initial curiosity to enthusiastic commitment. Here are the four key phases:

    1. Connection & Discovery: Build rapport and understand the prospect's background.
    2. Deep Dive into Pain Points: Uncover their challenges and desired outcomes.
    3. Present Solution & Value: Showcase how your program addresses their needs.
    4. Invitation & Next Steps: Confidently invite them to join your program.

    Case Study: Sarah's Successful Discovery Call

    Client: Emily, 35 years old

    Presenting Symptoms: Emily reported feeling stuck in life, experiencing recurring anxiety, and struggling with low self-esteem. She mentioned a difficult childhood with emotionally unavailable parents.

    Intervention: During the discovery call, Sarah empathetically listened to Emily's concerns, asked probing questions to uncover the root of her pain, and presented her Inner Child Healing program as a solution. She highlighted the program's unique approach and the personalized support offered.

    Outcomes: Emily felt understood and hopeful after the call. She immediately enrolled in Sarah's 12-week program and experienced significant improvements in her self-esteem, anxiety levels, and overall well-being. She later referred two friends to Sarah's program.
