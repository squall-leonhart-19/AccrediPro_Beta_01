Case Study: Emily, 48

Anxiety & Chronic Pain

Emily, a 48-year-old marketing executive, presents with chronic pain and anxiety. She reports feeling overwhelmed and disconnected from her body. Her history reveals a demanding work environment and a tendency to suppress emotions.

Question: How would you apply the C.A.L.M. Frameworkâ„¢ to Emily's case, incorporating Polyvagal Theory and somatic awareness techniques?

  1. Connect & Clarify: Build rapport by listening empathetically to Emily's experience. Explore her history of anxiety and pain, as well as her coping mechanisms.
  2. Assess & Acknowledge: Utilize self-assessment questionnaires (e.g., the PCL-5 for trauma) and body scan techniques to identify areas of tension and dysregulation.
  3. Learn & Liberate: Educate Emily about the autonomic nervous system and the role of the vagus nerve in regulating pain and anxiety. Introduce gentle breathwork exercises (e.g., diaphragmatic breathing) and grounding techniques (e.g., mindful walking).
  4. Maintain & Monitor: Develop a personalized regulation plan that incorporates daily breathwork, somatic awareness practices, and stress management strategies. Encourage Emily to track her symptoms and adjust her plan as needed.

Remember, the goal is not just to memorize facts but to apply your knowledge in a practical and compassionate way. Consider how you would adapt your approach to meet the unique needs of each client.
