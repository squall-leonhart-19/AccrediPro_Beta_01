Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Rapport (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I appreciate you taking the time to explore how I might be able to help. To start, can you tell me a little bit about what led you to reach out to me?"
YOU:
"That makes sense. Many of my clients come to me feeling similarly overwhelmed. I admire you for taking this step to address it. So, what does a typical day look like for you?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Let's talk more about the insomnia and anxiety. When did these issues start, and how do they impact your daily life?"
YOU:
"What have you already tried to address these issues? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and resolve these issues, what would your life look like in six months?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution (10 min) 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're dealing with significant sleep disruption and anxiety, which are impacting your overall well-being and ability to manage your daily life. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because we focus on regulating your nervous system through targeted techniques and personalized strategies. Over 12 weeks, we'll identify the root causes of your insomnia and anxiety, and create a plan to restore balance. Many clients report significant improvements within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"For example, I recently worked with a client, Maria, who is 48 and was experiencing similar issues. After 8 weeks, she reported a 70% reduction in anxiety symptoms and was consistently sleeping 7-8 hours per night."
Phase 4: The Close (5 min) 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on everything we've discussed, do you feel that this approach aligns with what you're looking for? ... Great! Let's talk about the program details and how we can get you started."

Overcoming Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I understand. It's a big decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time commitment, the investment, or something else? Knowing this will help me provide you with the information you need."
"It's too expensive."
"I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask, how much have you already spent on solutions that haven't worked? What if we could actually resolve this in 12 weeks? What would that be worth to you in terms of improved sleep, reduced anxiety, and overall quality of life?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand your concern. However, many of the techniques we use can be integrated into your daily routine in just a few minutes. Plus, think about how much time you're currently losing due to insomnia and anxiety. By addressing those issues, you'll actually free up more time and energy."
"I need to talk to my partner."
"That's perfectly reasonable. Would it be helpful if I sent you some information to share with them? Or, perhaps we could schedule a brief call together so they can ask any questions they might have?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of a successful discovery call?

Building rapport, understanding the client's problem, presenting your solution, and closing the sale.

2. How should you handle the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the value of your program, and ask about their current spending on ineffective solutions.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is essential for converting leads into paying clients.
  • Focus on understanding the client's needs and demonstrating the value of your program.
  • Practice handling common objections with confidence and empathy.
  • Clearly present your pricing and emphasize the return on investment for the client.
  • Believe in your ability to help clients and build a thriving practice.
