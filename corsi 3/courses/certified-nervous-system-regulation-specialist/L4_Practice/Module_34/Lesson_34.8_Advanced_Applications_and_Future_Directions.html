Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I understand. It's a commitment. But the program is designed to fit into busy schedules. We have flexible session times, and the tools you'll learn will actually save you time by reducing stress and improving focus. Plus, you'll have access to recordings if you miss a session. What part of the time commitment feels most challenging to you?"
"I'm not a 'group' person."
"That's perfectly fine. Many participants initially feel the same way. But what they discover is the power of shared experiences and mutual support. It's not just about the content, but about the connections you'll make with women who understand what you're going through. It's a safe and confidential space. What concerns you most about the group setting?"
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. It's a big decision. May I ask, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the program content, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you. Let's look at it this way: what's the cost of not addressing this? What's the impact of ongoing stress and poor sleep on your health, your relationships, and your career? The program is an investment in your well-being and long-term vitality. We also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible. Would you like me to share those options with you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are some key elements to include in an enrollment call for a group program?

Reconnecting with the prospect, understanding their needs, connecting the program to their specific challenges, highlighting community benefits, and confidently presenting pricing.

2. How can you address the objection "I'm not sure I have the time" effectively?

Acknowledge their concern, emphasize the program's flexibility, highlight time-saving benefits, and offer access to recordings for missed sessions. Probe to find out what part of the time commitment feels most challenging.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs offer a powerful way to scale your impact and income.
  • Enrollment calls are about building connection and demonstrating value.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, empathetic responses.
  • Present your pricing clearly and believe in the transformation you offer.
  • Consistent enrollment efforts lead to a thriving practice and a fulfilling career.
