Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic, recently certified, seeking guidance on client interactions.

Background
Former elementary school teacher, passionate about helping children with ADHD.
Strengths
Excellent at explaining concepts to kids, patient, and organized.
Growth Areas
Needs help setting boundaries with clients, struggles with time management.
Her Question
"I'm feeling overwhelmed with my first few clients. Can we discuss strategies for managing my time and setting appropriate boundaries?"

The Challenge She Presents

Sarah's Time Management & Boundary Concerns

What Sarah Tells You:

"I have three new clients, all kids with ADHD. I'm spending so much time outside of sessions answering emails and texts from parents. One parent even called me on the weekend! I want to be helpful, but I'm burning out. What should I do?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Has Sarah clearly communicated her availability and communication policies to clients?
  • Is she spending excessive time on tasks that could be delegated or streamlined?
  • Does she have strategies for setting professional boundaries and managing client expectations?
  • Is Sarah aware of the ethical implications of blurring professional boundaries?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize the Importance of Clear Communication

Explain that setting expectations upfront prevents misunderstandings and protects her time. Discuss creating a welcome packet outlining communication policies.

2

Teach Time Management Strategies

Introduce time-blocking techniques, batching similar tasks, and using productivity tools to maximize efficiency. Share examples of successful strategies used by other practitioners.

3

Role-Play Boundary-Setting Scenarios

Practice responding to common boundary violations, such as weekend calls or excessive emails. Help Sarah develop assertive communication skills.

4

Highlight Ethical Considerations

Discuss the importance of maintaining professional boundaries to protect both the practitioner and the client. Review relevant ethical guidelines and legal considerations.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Sarah's Dedication
"Sarah, I admire your commitment to your clients. It's clear you care deeply about their well-being. Let's work together to find strategies that allow you to be effective without burning out."
Explore Her Current Practices
"Tell me more about your current communication policies. What do you tell parents about your availability and response times?"
Offer Practical Solutions
"One strategy that many practitioners find helpful is to create a welcome packet that outlines communication policies, session schedules, and payment information. This sets clear expectations from the beginning."
Empower Her to Take Action
"What steps can you take this week to implement these strategies? How can I support you in setting these boundaries?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to emphasize clear communication policies when mentoring new practitioners?

Clear communication policies set expectations, prevent misunderstandings, and protect the practitioner's time and energy.

2. What are some effective time management strategies to share with a new practitioner feeling overwhelmed?

Time-blocking, batching similar tasks, using productivity tools, and setting specific times for responding to emails and calls.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective supervision involves validating the mentee's feelings, providing concrete strategies, and fostering a culture of self-care.
  • Clear communication policies are essential for setting boundaries and managing client expectations.
  • Time management strategies can help new practitioners maximize efficiency and prevent burnout.
  • Role-playing scenarios can help mentees develop assertive communication skills and respond to boundary violations.
  • Ongoing support and encouragement are crucial for helping new practitioners build confidence and competence.
