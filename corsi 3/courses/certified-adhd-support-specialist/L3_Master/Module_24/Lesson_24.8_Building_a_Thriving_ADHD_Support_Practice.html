Sarah, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic, but overwhelmed by the practical aspects of running her own practice.

Background
Former teacher, strong understanding of ADHD, limited business experience.
Strengths
Excellent at explaining complex concepts, very patient, compassionate.
Growth Areas
Struggles with setting boundaries, pricing her services, marketing.
Her Question
"How do I balance helping people with actually making a living?"

The Challenge She Presents

Sarah's Business Dilemma

What Sarah Tells You:

"I had a potential client, Emily, who has a child with ADHD. I spent over an hour on the phone with her, giving her tons of free advice. She was so grateful, but then she said she couldn't afford my package. I feel used and resentful. What should I have done?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Intake process and boundary setting.
  • Value proposition and pricing strategy.
  • Marketing and lead generation.
  • Time management and energy levels.

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Validate Her Feelings

Acknowledge that it's normal to feel taken advantage of when giving away too much for free. Emphasize the importance of self-care and sustainable business practices.

2

Reframe the Initial Consultation

Explain that the initial consultation should be a discovery call to assess fit, not a free coaching session. Focus on understanding their needs and explaining your unique value.

3

Teach Value-Based Pricing

Guide her to articulate the tangible benefits clients receive from her services. Calculate the ROI (Return on Investment) for clients. Show her how to confidently present her pricing.

4

Introduce Time Management Strategies

Help Sarah create a schedule that balances client work, marketing, and personal time. Discuss batching tasks, using templates, and setting clear office hours.

5

Discuss Scope of Practice and Referrals

Ensure Sarah understands the boundaries of her certification and when to refer clients to other professionals (therapists, doctors, etc.).

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive, Encouraging Feedback

Start with Affirmation
"Sarah, it's wonderful that you're so generous and passionate about helping people. That's a huge strength. Now, let's channel that energy into building a thriving practice."
Ask Guiding Questions
"What if you had framed that initial call as a 'discovery session' to see if you and Emily were a good fit? What would that look like? What questions would you ask?"
Share Practical Strategies
"One strategy I use is to have a set script for my discovery calls. I ask about their biggest challenges, what they've already tried, and what their ideal outcome would be. Then, I briefly explain how my program can help them achieve that."
Empower Action Steps
"Let's work together to create a pricing package that feels good for you and provides incredible value for your clients. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key areas to address when mentoring a new practitioner struggling with business aspects?

Boundary setting, value-based pricing, and time management are crucial for new practitioners.

2. How can you help a mentee reframe their initial consultations?

Encourage them to see initial calls as "discovery sessions" focused on assessing fit and explaining their value, rather than giving free advice.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves validating feelings, reframing challenges, and teaching practical strategies.
  • Help mentees understand value-based pricing and confidently present their services.
  • Encourage time management, boundary setting, and self-care for sustainable success.
  • Supervision is about empowering mentees to solve problems and build thriving practices.
