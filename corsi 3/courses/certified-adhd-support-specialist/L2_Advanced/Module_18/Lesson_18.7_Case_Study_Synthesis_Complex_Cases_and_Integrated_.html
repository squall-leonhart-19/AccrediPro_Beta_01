Case Study: Emily (16)

ADHD, Dyslexia, and Social Isolation

Emily, a 16-year-old high school student, presents with a diagnosis of ADHD, combined type, and dyslexia. She struggles academically, particularly with reading comprehension and written expression. Socially, Emily feels isolated and has difficulty making and maintaining friendships. Her parents report that she is often overwhelmed, disorganized, and has low self-esteem.

Intervention Plan:

  • F: Frame the Challenge: Identified core challenges as academic struggles due to ADHD and dyslexia, social isolation, and low self-esteem.
  • O: Observe & Organize: Conducted observations in academic and social settings, reviewed school records, and gathered input from parents and teachers.
  • C: Create Strategies: Developed an individualized education program (IEP) with accommodations for dyslexia, implemented social skills training, and incorporated cognitivebehavioral therapy (CBT) techniques to address low self-esteem.
  • U: Utilize Resources: Connected Emily with a reading specialist, a peer support group, and a therapist specializing in ADHD and learning disabilities.
  • S: Support & Sustain: Provided ongoing support and monitoring, adjusted interventions based on Emily's progress, and maintained regular communication with her parents and school staff.

Outcomes:

After six months of intervention, Emily demonstrated significant improvements in reading comprehension and written expression. Her grades improved from failing to passing in most subjects. Socially, she began participating in extracurricular activities and formed meaningful friendships. Her self-esteem also improved, and she reported feeling more confident and less overwhelmed.

Case Study: David (35)

Adult ADHD, Career Instability, and Relationship Strain

David, a 35-year-old professional, seeks help due to chronic career instability and relationship problems. He was diagnosed with ADHD in adulthood and reports difficulties with organization, time management, and emotional regulation. He has a history of frequent job changes and struggles to maintain long-term relationships. His partner reports feeling frustrated by his impulsivity and lack of follow-through.

Intervention Plan:

  • F: Frame the Challenge: Identified core challenges as career instability, relationship strain, and difficulties with organization, time management, and emotional regulation.
  • O: Observe & Organize: Conducted a comprehensive assessment, including a review of David's work history, relationship patterns, and self-report measures of ADHD symptoms.
  • C: Create Strategies: Implemented strategies to improve work performance, such as time management training, organizational tools, and strategies for managing impulsivity. Provided couples counseling to improve communication skills and address relationship issues. Incorporated mindfulness-based techniques to enhance emotional regulation.
  • U: Utilize Resources: Connected David with a career coach, a couples therapist, and a support group for adults with ADHD.
  • S: Support & Sustain: Provided ongoing support and monitoring, adjusted interventions based on David's progress, and maintained regular communication with his partner and other members of his support team.

Outcomes:

After one year of intervention, David secured a stable job and demonstrated improved work performance. His relationship with his partner improved significantly, and they reported feeling more connected and understanding. He also reported feeling more in control of his emotions and better able to manage his ADHD symptoms.

Comparative Analysis

Both Emily and David present with ADHD, but their challenges manifest differently due to their age and life circumstances. Emily's challenges are primarily academic and social, while David's are career-related and interpersonal. However, both individuals benefit from a comprehensive intervention plan that addresses their specific needs and leverages available resources.
