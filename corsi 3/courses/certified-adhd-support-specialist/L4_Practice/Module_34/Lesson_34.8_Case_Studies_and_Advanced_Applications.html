Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. Time is precious. The program requires about 3-4 hours per week, including the live sessions. However, the strategies you'll learn will actually *save* you time in the long run by improving your focus and efficiency. Plus, the sessions are recorded, so you can watch them at your convenience. Think of it as investing time now to gain more time later."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. What specifically makes you think it might not work? We have many testimonials from women just like you who have achieved amazing results. Also, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you participate fully in the program and don't see any improvement, I'll refund your money. How does that sound?"
"It's more than I was expecting to spend."
"I understand. Let's talk about the value you're getting. Think about the cost of *not* addressing your ADHD challenges ‚Äì the lost productivity, the missed opportunities, the stress and frustration. The program is an investment in your future. Plus, we have a payment plan available to make it more accessible. Would you like me to tell you more about that?"
"Can I talk to someone who's already done the program?"
"Absolutely! I can connect you with one of our past participants. I can send you a short video testimonial right now. Would you like to see that?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key components of an effective discovery call for a group program?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's problem, presenting your approach, and closing with a clear call to action.

2. How should you handle the objection "I'm not sure if I have the time"?

Acknowledge their concern, highlight the time-saving benefits of the program, and mention the flexibility of recorded sessions.

3. What is a strong call to action to use when selling a group program?

Ask a direct question like, "Are you ready to join us and start thriving?" and provide clear options (e.g., "Yes, I'm ready!" or "Tell me about the payment plan").

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to feel confident and natural.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your group program.
  • End every conversation with a strong call to action.
  • Remember that selling is about helping people solve their problems.
