Developing a Realistic Business Plan

A well-crafted business plan is your roadmap to financial success. It outlines your financial goals, revenue projections, and expense budgets. Think of it as the "Frame the Challenge" step of The F.O.C.U.S. Frameworkâ„¢, but applied to your business finances. It helps you identify potential financial challenges and opportunities.

Your business plan should include:

  • Executive Summary: A brief overview of your business.
  • Company Description: Details about your services, target audience, and unique selling points.
  • Market Analysis: Research on the demand for ADHD support services in your area.
  • Services Offered: A detailed description of the services you will provide.
  • Marketing and Sales Strategy: How you plan to attract and retain clients (building on Lesson 4).
  • Financial Projections: Revenue forecasts, expense budgets, and cash flow statements.
  • Funding Request (if applicable): Details of any funding you are seeking.

  • Accounting Software: Tools like QuickBooks or Xero offer more advanced features.
  • Mobile Apps: Apps like Expensify can help you track expenses on the go.

Regularly review your financial data to identify trends, manage cash flow, and make informed decisions.

Common pricing models include:

  • Hourly Rates: Charging a set rate for each hour of service.
  • Package Deals: Offering a bundle of services at a discounted rate.
  • Subscription Services: Providing ongoing support for a recurring fee.
  • Sliding Scale: Adjusting fees based on the client's income.

Research the market and set competitive rates that reflect the value of your services.

Running a business involves various financial obligations, including taxes, insurance, and other expenses. This ties into the "Solve & Support" phase of The F.O.C.U.S. Frameworkâ„¢, where you implement your financial plan and provide ongoing support to ensure its success.

Be sure to plan for:

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Sarah's Strategic Business Plan

Client: Sarah, a newly certified ADHD coach, age 32.

Presenting Problem: Sarah was struggling to gain clients and manage her finances effectively after starting her ADHD coaching practice.

Intervention: Sarah developed a detailed business plan, outlining her target market, pricing strategy (package deals for initial consultations), and marketing plan (social media advertising). She also implemented accounting software to track her income and expenses.

Outcomes: Within six months, Sarah saw a 40% increase in her client base and improved her cash flow management. Her strategic business plan provided a clear roadmap for her practice's growth.

Case Study 2: Michael's Financial Turnaround

Client: Michael, an experienced ADHD therapist, age 45.

Presenting Problem: Michael's practice was facing financial difficulties due to inconsistent income and poor expense tracking.

Intervention: Michael transitioned to a subscription-based service model, offering ongoing support for a recurring monthly fee. He also secured a small business loan to cover his operating expenses and invested in marketing to attract new clients.

Outcomes: Within a year, Michael's practice achieved financial stability, with a 60% increase in recurring revenue and improved client retention rates. The subscription model provided a predictable income stream, allowing him to better manage his finances.
