I can, however, help you identify the specific challenges you are facing and brainstorm potential accommodations that might be helpful."
Phase 3: Navigating Ethical Concerns 10 min
SARAH:
"I'm so stressed about all of this and my brother is also struggling. He was diagnosed with \[another condition] and it's really impacting the whole family."
YOU:
"I hear that you're going through a lot, Sarah. While I understand your concern for your brother, it's important that we focus on your needs and goals in our sessions. I'm not qualified to provide support for your brother's condition. If you'd like, I can provide you with resources for finding a qualified professional for him. How does that sound?"
Phase 4: Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on our conversation, I believe I can help you develop strategies to manage your ADHD and improve your overall well-being, while staying within ethical and legal boundaries. Are you interested in learning more about my program and how we can work together?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can trust someone who isn't a therapist."
"I understand your hesitation, Sarah. While I'm not a therapist, my certification as an ADHD Support Specialist‚Ñ¢ means I've completed rigorous training in providing practical strategies and support for individuals with ADHD. My approach is different from therapy; it's more focused on actionable steps and skill-building. Many clients find this approach very effective. I'm happy to share testimonials and success stories with you."
"I'm worried about the confidentiality of our sessions."
"Confidentiality is a top priority for me. I adhere to strict ethical guidelines and legal requirements regarding the privacy of your information. I can provide you with a detailed privacy policy outlining how your data is protected. The only exceptions are legally mandated reporting situations, such as harm to yourself or others, which I mentioned earlier."
"What if I need more than just support? What if I need therapy?"
"That's a valid question. I always encourage clients to have a comprehensive support system. If, during our work together, it becomes clear that you would benefit from therapy, I can provide you with referrals to qualified therapists in your area. I see my role as complementary to therapy, not a replacement for it."
"I'm concerned about the cost, especially if it doesn't include therapy."
"I understand your concern about the investment. My program is designed to provide tangible results in a specific timeframe. Many clients find that the practical strategies and support they receive lead to increased productivity, reduced stress, and improved overall well-being, which ultimately saves them time and money in the long run. I offer different payment options to make it more accessible. Additionally, remember that my support complements, rather than replaces, therapy, potentially reducing the need for frequent therapy sessions once you have effective coping mechanisms in place."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the MOST important thing to clarify at the beginning of a discovery call?

Clarify the scope of your services and your qualifications. Emphasize that you are not a licensed therapist or medical professional.

2. How do you handle a client asking for advice that is outside your scope of practice?

Acknowledge their concern, explain why you cannot provide that specific advice, and offer alternative resources or referrals.

Key Takeaways

  • Always prioritize ethical considerations and legal compliance in your practice.
  • Clearly define your scope of practice and qualifications to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Establish clear boundaries with clients regarding confidentiality and the limits of your services.
  • Address legal and ethical concerns proactively during the discovery call.
  • Build a sustainable and responsible practice by prioritizing ethical and legal compliance.
