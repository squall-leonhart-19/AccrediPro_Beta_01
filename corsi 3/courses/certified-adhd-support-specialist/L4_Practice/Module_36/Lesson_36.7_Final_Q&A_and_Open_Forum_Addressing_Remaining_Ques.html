Check Your Understanding

1. What are some strategies for supporting a client who is resistant to implementing strategies suggested within the F.O.C.U.S. Framework‚Ñ¢?

Focus on building rapport, understanding their concerns, and collaboratively developing strategies that align with their preferences and strengths. Start with small, manageable steps and celebrate successes to build momentum.

2. How can the F.O.C.U.S. Framework‚Ñ¢ be adapted to support clients with co-occurring conditions, such as anxiety or depression?

Integrate strategies that address both ADHD and the co-occurring condition. For example, incorporate mindfulness techniques for anxiety or cognitive reframing exercises for depression. Tailor the intervention plan to meet the client's unique needs and goals.
