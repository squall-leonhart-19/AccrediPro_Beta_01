Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for reaching out! I'm Olivia. I really appreciate you taking the time to connect. I loved hearing that you found my blog post. Before we dive in, tell me a little bit about yourself. What made you decide to schedule this call?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear, Susan. I understand how overwhelming a new diagnosis can be. So, what are your biggest challenges right now?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Tell me more about the disorganization. What does that look like in your daily life? How does it impact your work, relationships, or personal goals?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage your ADHD symptoms? Did anything work? What didn't?"
YOU:
"If things continue as they are, where do you see yourself in a year? What's at stake if you don't get this under control? On the flip side, if you could wave a magic wand, what would your life look like with ADHD managed effectively?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're feeling overwhelmed by your ADHD diagnosis and struggling with disorganization, which is impacting your [mention specific areas]. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My approach is different because it focuses on building personalized strategies tailored to your unique ADHD profile. I work with clients over 12 weeks, providing practical tools, accountability, and support to help them gain control and thrive. Many of my clients experience significant improvements in focus, organization, and overall well-being within the first few weeks. We focus on strengths-based strategies that work *with* your brain, not against it."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Does it feel like something you'd be interested in exploring further? ... Excellent! Let me tell you about the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"Of course, Susan. It's a big decision. Can I ask, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to ensure you have all the information you need to make the right choice for you."
"It's too expensive."
"I understand. Can I ask, what value are you placing on feeling more in control, less overwhelmed, and more productive? Think about the potential impact on your work, relationships, and overall quality of life. What would that be worth to you?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"That's a valid concern. However, consider this: How much time are you currently *losing* due to disorganization and lack of focus? My program is designed to help you become more efficient and productive, ultimately *saving* you time in the long run. We'll work together to prioritize tasks, streamline your schedule, and implement strategies that maximize your time."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a fair question. I understand your hesitation. My program is based on proven strategies and personalized to your specific needs. While I can't guarantee results, I'm committed to providing you with the tools, support, and accountability you need to succeed. Many of my clients report significant improvements within the first few weeks. If you're not satisfied with your progress, we can discuss adjustments to the program or explore alternative solutions."

The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is your first real opportunity to connect with a potential client and determine if your services are a good fit for their needs. It's more than just a sales pitch; it's a chance to build rapport, understand their challenges, and demonstrate the value you can provide. Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into paying clients and building a thriving practice.

Case Study: Sarah's Success Story

Client: Sarah, 35, Entrepreneur

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah felt overwhelmed by her growing business. She struggled to prioritize tasks, manage her time effectively, and maintain a healthy work-life balance. She reported feeling stressed, anxious, and unproductive.

Intervention: During the discovery call, Sarah's coach actively listened to her challenges and asked targeted questions to uncover the root causes of her overwhelm. She then presented a tailored coaching program designed to address Sarah's specific needs. The coach confidently handled Sarah's initial objection about the cost by highlighting the potential return on investment in terms of increased productivity and reduced stress.

Outcomes: Sarah enrolled in the coaching program and experienced significant improvements in her time management, productivity, and overall well-being. She reported feeling more in control of her business and her life. Within three months, Sarah's revenue increased by 25%, and she regained a sense of balance and fulfillment.

Understanding Client Needs

A successful discovery call hinges on your ability to deeply understand the client's needs, pain points, and aspirations. This requires active listening, empathy, and skillful questioning. Avoid making assumptions and focus on uncovering the underlying issues that are driving their desire for change.

Remember, people are more likely to invest in solutions that directly address their specific problems. By taking the time to truly understand their needs, you can tailor your offering to resonate with them on a personal level.

The Importance of Active Listening

Active listening is a crucial skill for any practitioner. It involves paying close attention to what the client is saying, both verbally and nonverbally, and demonstrating that you understand their perspective. Active listening techniques include summarizing, clarifying, and reflecting back their feelings.

By actively listening, you can build trust, create a safe space for the client to share their vulnerabilities, and gain valuable insights into their needs and motivations. This will enable you to tailor your services to their specific circumstances and increase the likelihood of a successful outcome.

Case Study: John's Frustration

Client: John, 48, Executive

Presenting Symptoms: John was experiencing burnout and a lack of fulfillment in his career. He felt stuck in a rut and unsure of how to make a change. He had previously worked with a career coach who focused on resume writing and job searching, but he didn't find it helpful.

Intervention: During the discovery call, John's new coach spent significant time listening to his concerns and asking about his values, interests, and passions. She discovered that John was deeply interested in environmental sustainability but felt that he couldn't pursue it in his current role. She then presented a coaching program designed to help him explore his options and develop a plan to align his career with his values.

Outcomes: John enrolled in the coaching program and gained clarity about his career goals. He developed a plan to transition into a role that focused on environmental sustainability. Within six months, John secured a new position with a company that aligned with his values, and he reported feeling more engaged, fulfilled, and energized.

Check Your Understanding

1. How can you effectively handle a prospect who is hesitant to invest in your services due to a previous negative experience with a similar program?

Acknowledge their past experience and validate their concerns. Emphasize what makes your program different and more effective. Offer testimonials or case studies from satisfied clients. Provide a guarantee or offer a trial period to demonstrate your commitment to their success.

2. What are some ethical considerations to keep in mind during a discovery call?

Be transparent about your qualifications and experience. Avoid making unrealistic promises or guarantees. Respect the prospect's autonomy and allow them to make their own decisions. Maintain confidentiality and protect their personal information. Disclose any potential conflicts of interest.
