Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, thank you for being so open. It sounds like you're at a significant crossroads, feeling the pull of both the past and the future. Many women in their 50s experience a similar sense of questioning and re-evaluation. What resonates most with you when you think about 'connection'?"
DIANE:
"I guess...feeling understood. Like my family sees me for who I really am, not just as 'Mom' or 'the teacher.'"
YOU:
"That's beautiful, Diane. And often, those old roles can overshadow our authentic selves. In Family Constellations, we explore the unspoken dynamics within your family system to uncover hidden loyalties and entanglements. This can provide clarity and a pathway to greater understanding and freedom."
YOU:
"Before we proceed, I want to check in. Does exploring these deeper family dynamics feel like a safe and supportive path for you right now?"
DIANE:
"Yes, I think so. I'm a little nervous, but also hopeful."
YOU:
"That's completely understandable. We'll go at your pace, Diane. Let's start by mapping out your family system. Can you tell me a bit about your parents and siblings, if you have any?"

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Diane expresses feeling heard and understood.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's feelings before explaining Family Constellations?

Validating her feelings builds trust and creates a safe space, making her more receptive to exploring deeper family dynamics.

2. What are some signs that Diane feels safe and supported during the initial conversation?

Open body language, asking questions, expressing hope, and agreeing to a follow-up session are all positive indicators.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and validation are crucial for building trust with clients.
  • Explain Family Constellations in simple, relatable terms.
  • Go at the client's pace and continually check in about their comfort level.
  • Acknowledge the value of their past experiences.
  • Focus on creating a safe and supportive environment for exploration.
