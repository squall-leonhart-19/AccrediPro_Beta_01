Lisa, New L1 Graduate

Enthusiastic graduate, seeking guidance on a challenging case.

Background
Former teacher, strong interest in trauma-informed practices.
Strengths
Excellent listener, compassionate, and dedicated to client well-being.
Growth Areas
Struggles with setting boundaries, needs support in complex systemic cases.
Her Question
"I'm not sure how to proceed with this family constellation. Can you help?"

The Case She Presents

Lisa's Client Case

What Lisa Tells You:

"I worked with a client, Sarah, 52, who wanted to explore recurring financial issues in her family. During the constellation, her representative for 'money' kept collapsing and saying, 'It's not mine.' I felt overwhelmed and ended the session early. I'm not sure what to do next."

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Lisa explore potential hidden dynamics or family secrets related to finances?
  • Was the representative adequately prepared for the role?
  • Did Lisa create a safe and contained space for the constellation?
  • What resources can Lisa use to deepen her understanding of systemic patterns around money?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Lisa

1

Validate Her Experience

"It's normal to feel overwhelmed in complex constellations. Acknowledge that she handled a difficult situation by ending the session when she felt it was beyond her current capacity."

2

Explore Hidden Dynamics

Guide Lisa to consider possible hidden family secrets or traumas related to money. "It's not mine" could indicate hidden inheritance, debts, or unethical financial practices.

3

Deepen Systemic Understanding

Recommend resources and further training on systemic patterns related to finances. Share relevant articles or books on the topic.

4

Emphasize Self-Care and Boundaries

Remind Lisa of the importance of setting boundaries and practicing self-care after challenging sessions. Encourage her to seek support from peers or mentors.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Appreciation
"Lisa, thank you for bringing this case to me. Your willingness to seek support is a sign of a responsible and dedicated practitioner."
Inquire About Her Intuition
"What was your initial feeling when the representative for 'money' said, 'It's not mine'? What did your intuition tell you?"
Offer Systemic Insights
"In family constellations, financial issues often reflect deeper systemic dynamics. 'It's not mine' could indicate a hidden family secret related to money, such as hidden debts, disinheritance, or unethical practices."
Empower Her Next Steps
"What steps would you like to take next with Sarah? How can you create a safer and more contained space for exploring these complex dynamics?"

You're Becoming a Leader!

By guiding and supporting new practitioners, you're contributing to the growth and evolution of Family Constellations. Your wisdom and experience are invaluable assets to our community.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important thing to establish when mentoring a new practitioner?

A safe and supportive space where they feel comfortable sharing their challenges and seeking guidance.

2. How can you help a mentee deepen their understanding of systemic dynamics?

By recommending relevant resources, sharing articles and books, and encouraging them to attend further training.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding and supporting, not judging.
  • Encourage self-reflection and intuition in your mentees.
  • Share relevant resources and training opportunities.
  • Emphasize the importance of self-care and boundaries.
  • Your wisdom and experience are invaluable to the community.
