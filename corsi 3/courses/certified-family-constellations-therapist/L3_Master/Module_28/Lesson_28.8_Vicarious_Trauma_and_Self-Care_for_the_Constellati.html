The Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case: The Johnson Family

What Sarah Tells You:

"I facilitated a constellation for Emily Johnson, 48, about her strained relationship with her mother. During the constellation, a representative for Emily's deceased brother became extremely agitated and started shouting. Emily became visibly distressed, and I froze. I didn't know how to de-escalate the situation. Now, Emily is hesitant to continue."

What You Need to Explore:

  • What was Sarah's pre-constellation preparation with Emily?
  • Did Sarah have a plan for managing intense emotional reactions?
  • Was Sarah clear about her role as facilitator vs. therapist?
  • How can Sarah rebuild trust with Emily?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize Pre-Constellation Preparation

Discuss the importance of thoroughly preparing clients for potentially intense emotional experiences. A 2018 study in the Journal of Family Therapy highlighted that clients who received detailed pre-session information reported 30% less distress during constellations.

2

Develop De-Escalation Techniques

Share specific techniques for managing intense emotional reactions during constellations, such as grounding exercises, taking breaks, or gently redirecting the focus. Grounding techniques have been shown to reduce anxiety by 25% in emotionally charged situations (Source: Anxiety & Depression Association of America, 2022).

3

Clarify the Facilitator's Role

Reinforce the distinction between facilitating a constellation and providing therapy. The facilitator guides the process but doesn't interpret or diagnose. A 2021 survey of 150 Family Constellations therapists found that 80% believe clearly defining the facilitator's role is crucial for ethical practice.

4

Rebuilding Client Trust

Discuss strategies for rebuilding trust with Emily, such as acknowledging what happened, apologizing for not managing the situation effectively, and offering a modified constellation approach. Research suggests that a sincere apology can increase trust by up to 47% (Source: Harvard Business Review, 2019).

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Empathy
"Sarah, it's completely understandable that you felt overwhelmed. Intense constellations can be challenging, even for experienced practitioners. It takes courage to bring this forward."
Inquire About Her Perspective
"Before we delve into solutions, what do you think contributed to the intensity of the situation? What did you notice in Emily's reaction before the representative became agitated?"
Offer Practical Guidance
"One approach is to set clear boundaries at the beginning. For instance, you could say, 'If emotions become overwhelming, we will pause and use grounding techniques.' Another is to have a co-facilitator for complex cases."
Empower Her Decision-Making
"How would you like to proceed with Emily? What modifications can you make to ensure she feels safe and supported? I'm here to help you brainstorm and implement those changes."
