Applying the S.Y.S.T.E.M. Frameworkâ„¢

Let's revisit our core framework, The S.Y.S.T.E.M. Frameworkâ„¢, and see how it applies to organizations:

  • S: Source Identification: In a business context, this means identifying the root cause of the organizational issue. Is it a recent merger, a change in leadership, or a long-standing conflict between departments?
  • Y: Your Position: Understanding the client's role within the organization and how their perspective might be influencing the situation. Arethey a CEO, a middle manager, or an employee?
  • S: Systemic Dynamics: Uncovering the hidden dynamics at play. Are there unspoken rules, power struggles, or loyalties to past leaders?
  • T: Trauma Recognition: Identifying any past traumas that might be impacting the organization. This could be a failed product launch, a major lawsuit, or a period of significant layoffs.
  • E: Entanglements: Recognizing any unhealthy connections or dependencies within the organization. Are departments overly reliant on each other, or are there individuals who are holding onto power unnecessarily?
  • M: Movement & Resolution: Facilitating positive change by creating new perspectives, fostering open communication, and implementing strategies that honor the needs of the entire system.

Remember, organizations are living systems, and the S.Y.S.T.E.M. Frameworkâ„¢ provides a powerful lens through which to understand their complexities.

Identifying Hidden Loyalties and Entanglements

One of the most powerful aspects of Family Constellations is its ability to reveal hidden loyalties and entanglements. In a business context, these can manifest in many ways:

  • Loyalty to a Founder: A company may continue to operate according to the founder's vision, even if it's no longer relevant or effective. This can stifle innovation and prevent the company from adapting to changing market conditions. A 2018 study in the Harvard Business Review found that 33% of family businesses struggle to adapt to new technologies due to a strong attachment to the founder's legacy.
  • Loyalty to a Product: A company may continue to invest in a failing product line out of loyalty, even if it's losing money. This can drain resources and prevent the company from investing in more promising opportunities.
  • Entanglement with a Competitor: A company may be unconsciously entangled with a competitor, perhaps due to a past merger or acquisition. This can lead to self-sabotaging behavior and prevent the company from fully competing in the market.
  • Entanglement with a Family Member: In family-owned businesses, personal family dynamics can significantly impact business decisions. A 2021 study published in the Journal of Family Business Strategy found that 70% of family business failures are due to breakdowns in communication and trust among family members.

By bringing these hidden dynamics to light, you can help organizations break free from unproductive patterns and move towards greater success.

Visualizing and Resolving Conflicts

Conflicts are inevitable in any organization, but they can be particularly damaging if they're not addressed effectively. Family Constellations provides a powerful way to visualize and resolve conflicts between departments, teams, or individuals.

For example, imagine a company where the sales and marketing departments are constantly at odds. The sales team feels that marketing isn't generating enough leads, while the marketing team feels that sales isn't following up on the leads they provide. By setting up a constellation, you can have representatives stand for each department and observe the dynamics between them. You might find that the sales team feels unsupported by the company as a whole, or that the marketing team feels undervalued for their efforts. By bringing these feelings to light, you can help the two departments develop a greater understanding of each other's perspectives and work together more effectively. A 2022 meta-analysis of 32 studies (n=5,487) published in the Journal of Organizational Behavior found that interventions focused on improving interdepartmental communication and collaboration resulted in a 22% increase in overall organizational performance.

Case Study: Stagnation in a Family Business

The Case of "Miller Manufacturing"

Client: Miller Manufacturing, a family-owned business in its third generation.

Presenting Symptoms: Stagnant growth, declining profits, and increasing conflict among family members.

Intervention: A series of Family Constellations sessions were conducted, involving key family members and representatives for different aspects of the business (e.g., sales, production, finance).

Outcomes: The constellations revealed a hidden loyalty to the founder, who had been a highly authoritarian leader. The current CEO, the founder's grandson, felt unconsciously compelled to maintain the founder's rigid management style, even though it was no longer effective. The constellations also revealed a deep-seated resentment between two siblings who were competing for control of the company. Through the constellations, the family members were able to acknowledge these hidden dynamics and begin to develop a more collaborative and forward-looking approach. Within one year, Miller Manufacturing experienced a 15% increase in revenue and a significant improvement in employee morale.

Ethical Considerations

As with any application of Family Constellations, it's crucial to be aware of the ethical considerations when working with businesses and organizations. Here are a few key points to keep in mind:

  • Confidentiality: Maintain strict confidentiality regarding any sensitive information shared during the constellations.
  • Informed Consent: Ensure that all participants understand the nature of the process and have given their informed consent to participate.
  • Boundaries: Be clear about your role as a facilitator and avoid getting drawn into personal conflicts or power struggles within the organization.
  • Objectivity: Maintain objectivity and avoid taking sides in any disputes.
  • Professionalism: Conduct yourself in a professional and ethical manner at all times.

By adhering to these ethical guidelines, you can ensure that you're providing a safe and effective service to your clients.
