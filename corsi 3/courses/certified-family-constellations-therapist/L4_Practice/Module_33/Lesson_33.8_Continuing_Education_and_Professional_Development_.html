Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It requires commitment. However, think about the time you're already spending feeling stuck and unfulfilled. This program is designed to create more time and energy in the long run. Can we explore how to realistically fit this into your schedule?"
"I've tried therapy before, and it didn't work."
"I hear you. Family Constellations is different from traditional therapy. It addresses systemic patterns and unconscious loyalties that may not be addressed in individual therapy. Would you like me to explain the key differences?"
"I'm worried about the intensity of exploring family trauma."
"That's a valid concern. We proceed at your pace, with compassion and support. My role is to guide you safely through this process. We also focus on building resilience and empowerment, not just dwelling on the past."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"While I can't guarantee specific outcomes, I can guarantee my commitment to providing you with the tools, support, and guidance you need. My clients report significant improvements in their relationships, careers, and overall well-being. I encourage you to read their testimonials."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an effective discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection, "I need to think about it?"

Acknowledge their need to think and ask clarifying questions. "I completely understand. This is an important decision. Can I ask ‚Äì what specifically do you need to think about?"

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence.
  • Prepare for common objections and craft thoughtful responses.
  • Confidently present your pricing and payment options.
  • Focus on the value and transformation you provide to clients.
  • Remember your income potential and the impact you can make.
