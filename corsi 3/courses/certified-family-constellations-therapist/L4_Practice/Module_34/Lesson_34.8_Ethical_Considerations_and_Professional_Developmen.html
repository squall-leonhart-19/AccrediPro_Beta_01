Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan! Thank you for reaching out about the 'Healing Family Patterns' workshop. I'm so glad you're interested. To start, tell me a little bit about what resonated with you about the workshop description."
YOU:
"It sounds like you've been through a lot recently. I really admire your willingness to heal. What are you hoping to gain from the workshop?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Can you describe some of the recurring family conflicts you're experiencing?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to resolve these conflicts? What felt helpful, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"If these patterns continue, what impact do you see it having on your life and your family in the long term? If you could change one thing about your family dynamics, what would it be?"

YOU:

"The 'Healing Family Patterns' workshop offers a unique approach. We use Family Constellations to uncover the hidden dynamics that are driving these conflicts. You'll gain insights, experience healing, and connect with a supportive community. Many participants report feeling lighter, more empowered, and more connected to their families after just one workshop."
YOU:
"This isn't just about understanding the patterns intellectually; it's about experiencing a shift in your energy and your relationships. The group setting provides a powerful container for healing and growth."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you feel like this workshop could be a good fit for you? ... Excellent! Let me tell you about the investment and how you can reserve your spot."

    The Power of Group Dynamics

    Group programs and workshops provide a unique opportunity to create transformative experiences for participants. The power of group dynamics can amplify individual healing and growth, fostering a sense of community and shared understanding. According to Irvin Yalom's research on group psychotherapy, the therapeutic factors present in group settings, such as universality, altruism, and interpersonal learning, contribute significantly to positive outcomes.

    A 2021 meta-analysis published in the Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology, including 55 studies (n=4,321), found that group therapy was as effective as individual therapy for a range of mental health conditions (effect size = 0.72, 95% CI [0.61, 0.83]). This highlights the potential for group programs to deliver significant therapeutic benefits.

    Building Rapport and Trust

    Before diving into the details of your program, it's crucial to establish a strong connection with potential participants. This involves creating a safe and welcoming environment where they feel comfortable sharing their challenges and aspirations. Remember the principles of unconditional positive regard, empathy, and genuineness, as described by Carl Rogers.

    Start by actively listening to their stories and validating their experiences. Ask open-ended questions that encourage them to elaborate on their needs and desires. For example, "What are the biggest challenges you're currently facing in your family relationships?" or "What kind of support are you looking for in this workshop?"

    Case Study: Maria's Initial Hesitation

    Client: Maria, 42, recently divorced

    Presenting Symptoms: Maria was hesitant about joining a "Healing After Divorce" workshop, citing concerns about sharing personal details in a group setting and whether it could truly help her move on.

    Intervention: The facilitator spent time one-on-one with Maria, actively listening to her concerns and validating her feelings. They emphasized the confidentiality of the group, the focus on support and empowerment, and the opportunity to learn from others' experiences.

    Outcomes: Maria decided to attend the workshop and found it incredibly helpful. She connected with other women who understood her struggles, gained valuable insights into her own patterns, and developed coping strategies for moving forward. She later said, "I was so scared to share, but it turned out to be the most healing part of the experience."

    Highlighting the Unique Approach

    In a crowded marketplace, it's essential to differentiate your program from others. Clearly articulate the unique approach, methodology, and benefits that your program offers. If you're using Family Constellations, explain how it differs from traditional therapy and how it can provide profound insights into family dynamics. Refer to the work of Bert Hellinger and his systemic approach.

    Use compelling language that resonates with your target audience. Instead of simply saying "This workshop will help you heal your family relationships," try something like "Discover the hidden dynamics that are shaping your family and unlock the potential for deeper connection and unconditional love."
