Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Affirm Her Value

"Sarah, it‚Äôs normal to feel overwhelmed. You're not expected to replace her medical team. Your role is to provide emotional support, lifestyle guidance within your scope, and help her navigate this process. You ARE valuable in this situation."

2

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Emphasize the difference between providing general wellness recommendations and offering medical advice. Supplements should only be suggested with a comprehensive understanding of their potential interactions with Emily's IVF protocol, preferably cleared by her RE.

3

Teach Boundary Setting

Help Sarah develop scripts for setting boundaries: "Emily, that's a medical question, and I'm not qualified to answer it. Please discuss that with your RE."

4

Promote Collaborative Care

Encourage Sarah to offer to collaborate with Emily‚Äôs RE. A simple email introducing herself and her role can be incredibly helpful. This establishes trust and ensures everyone is on the same page.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge Her Feelings
"Sarah, it's completely understandable that you feel overwhelmed. Seeing a complex medical history can be daunting, especially when you're new. Your empathy is a strength, but it's also important to protect yourself."
Reframe the Situation
"Instead of seeing this as a threat, view it as an opportunity to define your role clearly. You're not a replacement for her doctor. You are her advocate, her emotional support, and her guide for lifestyle changes that complement her medical treatment."
Provide Concrete Tools
"Let's work together to create a list of questions you can ask Emily to clarify her needs and boundaries. We can also draft an email you can send to her RE. Having these tools in place will boost your confidence."
Empower Her Next Steps
"What feels like the most manageable first step to you? Let's focus on that. Remember, progress, not perfection, is the goal."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to affirm a new coach's value when they express feeling overwhelmed?

Affirmation helps combat imposter syndrome and reinforces their role within the client's care team, boosting confidence.

2. What are some ways a mentor can help a new coach set clear boundaries with clients?

Developing scripts for redirecting medical questions, practicing assertive communication, and role-playing client interactions.

Key Takeaways

  • New coaches often need reassurance and validation to combat imposter syndrome.
  • Clearly defining scope of practice is crucial for ethical and effective coaching.
  • Providing practical tools and resources empowers new coaches to handle complex situations.
  • Encouraging collaboration with other healthcare providers enhances client care.
  • Mentoring is an opportunity to shape the future of fertility coaching and expand your impact.
