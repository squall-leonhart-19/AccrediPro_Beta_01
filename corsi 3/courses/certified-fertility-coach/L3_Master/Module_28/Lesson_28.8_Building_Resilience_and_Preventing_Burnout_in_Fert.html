Her Question

The Sensitive Case She Presents

Sarah's Client Case

What Sarah Tells You:

"I'm working with Emily, 42, who just had her first IVF cycle fail. She's devastated and keeps asking if there's anything else she can do, any 'secret' to make the next cycle work. I'm worried about giving her false hope, but I also don't want to dismiss her feelings. What's the right approach?"

Key Areas to Explore:

  • How is Sarah managing her own emotions and boundaries in response to Emily's distress?
  • What evidence-based strategies can Sarah offer without promising specific outcomes?
  • How can Sarah reinforce the client's agency and resilience during this difficult time?
  • Is Sarah clear on the distinction between coaching, therapy, and medical advice?

Your Mentoring Strategy

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Emphasize the Importance of Boundaries

Remind Sarah that her role is to support, not to provide medical solutions or guarantees. Setting clear boundaries protects both her and the client.

2

Review Scope of Practice

Ensure Sarah understands what she can and cannot advise within her certification. Focus on lifestyle factors, stress management, and emotional well-being.

3

Teach Emotional Regulation Techniques

Share strategies for Sarah to manage her own emotional responses to client distress. This prevents burnout and ensures she can provide grounded support.

4

Explore Reframing Techniques

Help Sarah learn how to reframe Emily's questions in a way that empowers her to focus on what she *can* control, such as self-care and stress reduction.

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Acknowledge the Difficulty
"Sarah, I know how tough these situations can be. It's natural to want to fix things for your clients, especially when they're hurting."
Probe for Understanding
"What strategies have you considered so far? What feels most aligned with your coaching approach?"
Offer Specific Guidance
"One technique I've found helpful is to validate the client's feelings, then gently redirect the conversation toward manageable actions. For example, 'I hear your disappointment, Emily. Let's explore some self-care practices to nourish your body and mind as you consider your next steps.'"
Reinforce Boundaries
"Remember, Sarah, you're not a magician. Your role is to empower Emily to make informed decisions and cope with the emotional challenges. Refer her to a therapist or counselor if she needs deeper emotional support."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to emphasize boundaries when mentoring a new fertility coach?

Setting boundaries protects both the coach and the client by ensuring the coach operates within their scope of practice and avoids over-involvement in the client's emotional journey.

2. What is one way to help a mentee reframe a client's question when they are seeking a "secret" to fertility success?

Reframing involves acknowledging the client's desire for control, then redirecting the conversation toward manageable actions like self-care, stress reduction, and evidence-based lifestyle changes.
