Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most important aspect of setting boundaries with a client, and why?

The most important aspect is clear communication. Clients need to understand what they can expect in terms of availability, response times, and scope of service. Clear communication helps manage expectations and prevents misunderstandings.

2. Why is it important to encourage self-care for new practitioners?

New practitioners are especially vulnerable to burnout. Encouraging self-care helps them maintain their well-being, prevent compassion fatigue, and provide better care for their clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Effective mentoring involves a balance of support and guidance.
  • Setting boundaries is essential for both the practitioner's well-being and the client's care.
  • Encourage new practitioners to develop their critical thinking skills.
  • Share personal experiences to normalize challenges and build confidence.
