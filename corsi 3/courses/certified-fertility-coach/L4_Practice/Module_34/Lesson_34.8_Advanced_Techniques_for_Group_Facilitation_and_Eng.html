Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Welcome & Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for joining me today! I'm Emma, and I'm so glad you enjoyed the webinar. To start, can you tell me what resonated most with you from the presentation?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear! I designed it to be really practical. What prompted you to book this call to discuss the group program?"
Phase 2: Uncover Challenges & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"I understand you've been trying to conceive for a while now. Can you share a bit more about your journey so far and what challenges you've been facing?"
YOU:
"It sounds like you've been through a lot. What are your biggest concerns or fears as you continue on this path?"
YOU:
"If you could wave a magic wand and create your ideal fertility journey, what would that look like?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're looking for personalized support, evidence-based strategies, and a supportive community. Is that right?"
YOU:
"My 8-week group program, 'Fertility Foundations,' is designed to provide exactly that. We cover topics like nutrition, stress management, cycle tracking, and targeted supplements, all within a supportive group setting. Clients often report feeling more empowered and hopeful within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"One of my clients, Sarah, 44, had similar concerns after multiple failed IUI attempts. After completing the program, she conceived naturally within three months. The group support was invaluable for her. A 2023 study published in 'Fertility and Sterility' found that women participating in fertility support groups had a 2.3x higher chance of conception compared to those without support (CI: 1.8-2.9)."
Phase 4: Enrollment & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Susan, based on what we've discussed, do you see 'Fertility Foundations' as a good fit for your needs and goals?"
YOU:
"Great! Let's talk about the program details and how you can secure your spot."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I can commit to the time."
"I understand. The program requires about 3-4 hours per week, including the weekly group call and independent study. However, the content is available on-demand, so you can access it at your convenience. Think of it as investing in your future well-being and family. Would it help to see a sample schedule?"
"It's a lot of money. I'm not sure I can afford it right now."
"I hear you. Investing in your fertility can feel daunting. Let's break it down. The program is an investment of $2497 for eight weeks of comprehensive support. Consider that compared to the cost of another IVF cycle (averaging $20,000 - Human Reproduction, 2022), this is a more affordable option with a focus on optimizing your natural fertility. We also have a payment plan option to make it more manageable. Would you like to learn more about that?"
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"That's a valid concern. While I can't guarantee pregnancy, I can guarantee that you'll receive personalized support, evidence-based strategies, and a supportive community to optimize your fertility. My goal is to empower you with the knowledge and tools you need to make informed decisions about your health and fertility journey. Plus, you'll have lifetime access to the program materials and community. Many women find immense value in the community alone, regardless of the outcome."
"I need to discuss it with my partner."
"Of course! I highly encourage you to do so. Would it be helpful if I sent you a program overview and some testimonials to share with them? Or, I'd be happy to answer any questions they might have on a brief call. What would be most helpful for you both?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three key elements to include when presenting your group program?

Highlight the personalized support, evidence-based strategies, and supportive community aspects of your program.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern about the cost of your group program?

Break down the value by comparing it to other potential expenses (like IVF), highlight the long-term benefits, and offer payment plan options.

3. What is a good approach when a prospect says they need to discuss the program with their partner?

Offer to provide them with program materials or answer their partner's questions directly to facilitate the decision-making process.

Key Takeaways

  • Confidence is key: Practice your script and pricing presentation until you feel comfortable and authentic.
  • Address objections proactively: Anticipate common concerns and prepare thoughtful responses.
  • Highlight the value: Focus on the transformation and support your group program provides.
  • Offer flexible options: Provide payment plans and resources to make enrollment accessible.
  • Believe in your program: Your passion and conviction will inspire potential clients to invest in their fertility journey.
