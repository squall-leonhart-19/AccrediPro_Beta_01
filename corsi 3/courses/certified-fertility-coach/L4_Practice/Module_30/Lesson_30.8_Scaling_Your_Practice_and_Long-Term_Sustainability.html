Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, thank you for scheduling this call. I know your time is valuable, so I appreciate you taking the time to connect. I'm Emma, and I'm a Certified Fertility Coach. I help women like you navigate their fertility journey with personalized support and evidence-based strategies. Before we get started, can you tell me a little bit about what prompted you to reach out?"
YOU:
"Great. Just so you know, the goal of this call is to understand your situation, see if I can help, and if it feels like a good fit, discuss how we can work together."
Phase 2: Understand Sarah's Fertility Journey 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned you've been trying to conceive for 18 months. Can you tell me more about your journey so far? What kind of testing or treatments have you undergone?"
YOU:
"I understand you had a miscarriage previously. I'm so sorry for your loss. How has that experience affected you emotionally?"
YOU:
"Your doctor says everything looks fine, but you're still not pregnant. What are your biggest concerns or frustrations right now?"
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 1 being not stressed at all and 10 being extremely stressed, how would you rate your stress levels related to your fertility journey?"

YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, it sounds like you're feeling anxious, unsupported, and unsure of what steps to take next. Is that a fair assessment?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's too expensive."
"I understand that investing in your fertility can feel like a big decision, especially when you're already spending money on treatments. However, think of coaching as an investment in your overall well-being and long-term success. Many of my clients find that the personalized support and guidance they receive actually help them reduce unnecessary expenses and make more informed decisions about their fertility care."
"I don't have time for coaching."
"I understand that you're busy, but think of coaching as an investment in your time. By working together, we can help you streamline your efforts, prioritize what's most important, and create a more efficient and effective fertility plan. Plus, our weekly sessions are just one hour, and you can access support from me via email in between sessions."
"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"That's completely understandable. I encourage you to discuss this with your partner. Would it be helpful if I sent you some information about my coaching program that you can share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions you may have."
"I'm not sure if coaching is right for me."
"I understand your hesitation. Coaching isn't for everyone. However, if you're feeling lost, overwhelmed, or unsupported on your fertility journey, coaching can provide you with the personalized guidance and support you need to take control and improve your chances of success. I also offer a complimentary discovery call, so you can experience the coaching process firsthand and see if it's a good fit for you."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of building rapport during the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

The key elements include thanking the prospect for their time, briefly introducing yourself and your role, and asking an open-ended question to understand their reasons for reaching out.

2. How can you effectively address the "It's too expensive" objection during a sales conversation?

Acknowledge the prospect's concern, reframe the coaching program as an investment in their overall well-being and long-term success, and highlight the potential cost savings they may experience as a result of your guidance.

3. What are some effective closing lines you can use to encourage a prospect to enroll in your coaching program?

Examples include: "Based on everything you've shared, I truly believe that my coaching program can help you achieve your fertility goals. Are you ready to take the next step and invest in your success?" or "Are you ready to move forward with the program today?"

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is crucial for establishing trust and creating a connection with potential clients.
  • Asking open-ended questions helps you understand the prospect's needs and challenges, allowing you to tailor your coaching approach accordingly.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and providing value-driven responses can help overcome concerns and encourage enrollment.
  • Presenting your pricing with confidence and highlighting the value of your coaching program can justify the investment and increase conversion rates.
  • Practicing your sales script and role-playing common scenarios can improve your confidence and effectiveness in sales conversations.
