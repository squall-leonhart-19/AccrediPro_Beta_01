Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."
"I understand. Many of my clients have similar concerns. Let's talk about the value you'll receive. My program includes personalized guidance, evidence-based techniques, and ongoing support. How much have you already spent on treatments without the emotional support you need? Think about the potential cost of NOT addressing your emotional well-being during this process. We can also explore payment plan options to make it more manageable."
"I need to research other options first."
"That's a smart approach. What specific information are you hoping to find in your research? I'm happy to provide you with resources or answer any specific questions you have. Keep in mind that my program is unique in its holistic approach and personalized support."
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"I hear your concern. It's natural to feel skeptical after facing challenges. However, my program is tailored to your specific needs and goals. I use the NESTED framework, which is evidence-based. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first two weeks, I'll provide a full refund."
"Can you guarantee I'll get pregnant?"
"While I cannot guarantee pregnancy, as that depends on many factors, I can guarantee that I will provide you with unwavering support, evidence-based techniques, and personalized guidance to optimize your well-being throughout your fertility journey. My focus is on empowering you to make informed decisions and cultivate a sense of peace, regardless of the outcome."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four key phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport & Connection, Uncover Needs & Challenges, Present Your Solution & Value, and Close with Confidence.

2. How should you respond to a prospect who says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, then ask clarifying questions: "I completely understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?"

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into clients.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for building rapport and understanding client needs.
  • Addressing objections with confidence and providing valuable information can overcome resistance.
  • Clearly communicate the value of your program and how it can help clients achieve their goals.
  • Practice your pricing presentation and closing lines to project confidence and professionalism.
