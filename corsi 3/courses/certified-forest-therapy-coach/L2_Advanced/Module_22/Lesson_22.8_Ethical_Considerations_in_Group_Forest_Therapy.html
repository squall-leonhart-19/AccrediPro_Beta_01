Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Decision-Making Steps

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Dilemma
Eleanor's potential action at work presents a conflict between her autonomy and the potential harm to others. Is it your place to intervene, and if so, how?
Step 2: Review Ethical Guidelines
Consider the principles of beneficence (doing good), non-maleficence (avoiding harm), autonomy (respecting client's self-determination), and justice (fairness). Your primary duty is to Eleanor, but you also have a responsibility to the wider community.
Step 3: Consider Legal and Professional Standards
While you are not a mandated reporter in this situation, be aware of any legal implications if Eleanor's actions constitute illegal behavior. Consult with a mentor or ethics board if unsure.
Step 4: Explore Options and Consequences
Possible actions include: (a) explore Eleanor's motivations and potential consequences of her actions; (b) help her identify alternative solutions that align with her values; (c) encourage her to seek legal or ethical counsel; (d) document the disclosure and your actions.
Step 5: Implement and Evaluate
Choose the most ethical course of action, document your reasoning, and be prepared to adjust your approach based on Eleanor's response and any new information.

Differential Considerations

What Else Could Be Influencing This?

1

Underlying Mental Health

Is Eleanor's decision-making impaired by stress, anxiety, or a resurgence of depression? Assess her mental state and consider referral if needed.

2

Values Conflict

Is Eleanor's decision driven by a conflict between her personal values and the values of her workplace? Help her clarify her values and make choices that align with them.

3

External Pressures

Is Eleanor being pressured by others to make this decision? Explore the dynamics of her workplace and identify potential sources of influence.

4

Lack of Awareness

Does Eleanor fully understand the ethical implications of her actions? Provide her with information and resources to help her make an informed decision.

Check Your Understanding

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are complex and require careful consideration of multiple factors.
  • Your primary duty is to your client, but you also have a responsibility to the wider community.
  • It's crucial to know your limits and when to refer to other professionals.
  • Ethical challenges can be opportunities for growth and deeper self-awareness.
  • Document your reasoning and actions to protect yourself and ensure ethical practice.
