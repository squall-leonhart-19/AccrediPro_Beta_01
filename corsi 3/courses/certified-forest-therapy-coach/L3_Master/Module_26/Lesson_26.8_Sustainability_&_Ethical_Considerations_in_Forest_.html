The Pricing Dilemma

Maria's "Pay-What-You-Can" Quandary

Maria's Account:

"I offered a 'pay-what-you-can' forest walk to make it accessible. One participant gave me $5. It feels disrespectful, but I don't know what to say."

Key Discussion Points:

  • The intentions behind "pay-what-you-can" models.
  • Setting clear expectations and suggested ranges.
  • Maria's feelings about money and worth.
  • Alternative accessibility strategies.

Your Mentoring Framework

Guiding Maria Through the Situation

1

Explore Intentions

Help Maria clarify *why* she chose a "pay-what-you-can" model. Was it genuine accessibility, or discomfort with charging?

2

Set Clear Expectations

Discuss how to frame "pay-what-you-can" with suggested amounts or value exchanges (e.g., volunteering, testimonials).

3

Address Money Mindset

Gently explore Maria's beliefs about money and whether they're impacting her business decisions. Is she undervaluing her expertise?

4

Offer Alternative Solutions

Brainstorm other ways to make her services accessible (scholarships, group discounts, sliding scale based on income verification).

Crafting Your Feedback

A Supportive Mentoring Conversation

Acknowledge Maria's Values
"Maria, I admire your desire to make forest therapy accessible. It's a beautiful intention."
Ask Open-Ended Questions
"What were your expectations when you decided to offer a 'pay-what-you-can' walk? What feels sustainable for you in the long run?"
Share Your Experience
"Early on, I struggled with pricing too. I learned that valuing my time and expertise allows me to serve more people effectively."
Empower Her Decision
"How would you like to respond to this participant? Remember, you have the right to set your boundaries and value your work. I'm here to help you find a way that feels good."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explore the mentee's intentions before offering advice?

Understanding their motivations helps you tailor your guidance to their specific needs and values.

2. What are some alternative accessibility strategies Maria could use besides "pay-what-you-can"?

Scholarships, group discounts, sliding scales based on income, and value exchange opportunities (volunteering, testimonials) are all viable options.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring involves understanding the mentee's values and intentions.
