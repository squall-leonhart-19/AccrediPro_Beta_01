The Case She Presents

Lisa's Client Concern

What Lisa Tells You:

"I have a client, Sarah, 52, who is dealing with grief after losing her husband. During our sessions, she constantly shares very intense and personal stories about her loss. I want to be supportive, but it's becoming overwhelming. I'm not sure how to balance being empathetic with maintaining professional boundaries. I‚Äôm starting to dread our sessions."

Key Areas to Explore:

  • Has Lisa established clear boundaries with Sarah regarding the scope of their sessions?
  • Is Lisa aware of the potential for secondary trauma and taking care of herself?
  • Does Lisa have a strategy for redirecting the conversation back to the present moment and the forest?
  • Is Lisa aware of local resources for grief counseling that she could refer Sarah to?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Teaching Points for Lisa

1

Validate and Normalize

Acknowledge Lisa's feelings of being overwhelmed. It's common for new practitioners to struggle with boundaries.

2

Teach Boundary Setting

Explain the importance of setting clear expectations during the initial intake. This includes explaining the focus of forest therapy and gently redirecting excessive personal disclosures.

3

Discuss Scope of Practice

Remind Lisa of the importance of working within her scope of practice. Forest Therapy is not a replacement for grief counseling or psychotherapy.

4

Self-Care Strategies

Encourage Lisa to practice self-care techniques to prevent burnout and secondary trauma. This includes setting boundaries, seeking supervision, and engaging in personal wellness activities.

Your Feedback Dialogue

Delivering Constructive Feedback with Compassion

Start with Affirmation
"Lisa, it's wonderful that you're so caring and want to support your clients. It's a sign of a great practitioner. Let's explore how to do that while also protecting your own well-being."
Inquire About Her Perspective
"What strategies have you considered so far? What feels comfortable and what feels challenging about setting boundaries with Sarah?"
Offer Guidance
"One approach is to gently acknowledge Sarah's feelings and then redirect her attention back to the present moment and the sensory experiences of the forest. For example, you could say, 'I hear that you're going through a lot, Sarah. Let's take a moment to notice the sound of the wind in the trees and how it feels on your skin.'"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the importance of setting boundaries in forest therapy coaching?

Setting boundaries helps maintain a professional relationship, protects the practitioner's well-being, and ensures the client receives appropriate care within the scope of practice.

2. How can you help a mentee who is struggling with a client who is "trauma dumping?"

Guide the mentee to set clear expectations, gently redirect the client back to the present moment and the sensory experiences of the forest, and ensure the mentee practices self-care.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is a crucial aspect of growing the field of forest therapy.
  • New practitioners often need guidance on boundary setting and scope of practice.
  • Providing a safe and supportive space for mentees to share their challenges is essential.
  • Encourage mentees to develop their own solutions and celebrate their successes.
  • Self-care is vital for both the mentor and mentee to prevent burnout and secondary trauma.
