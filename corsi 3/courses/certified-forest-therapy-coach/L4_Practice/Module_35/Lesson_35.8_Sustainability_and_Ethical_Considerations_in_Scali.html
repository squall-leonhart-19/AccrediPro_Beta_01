Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Connection & Intention 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this consultation. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to explore how forest therapy can support you. To start, what resonated with you about the workshop that made you want to connect?"
YOU:
"And what are you hoping to gain from this call today? What would make this a valuable use of your time?"
Phase 2: Understanding & Empathy 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling lost as an empty-nester. Can you tell me more about that? What does 'lost' feel like for you?"
YOU:
"What does your ideal retirement look like? What activities, relationships, and feelings are present in that vision?"
YOU:
"What's stopping you from achieving that vision right now?"
Phase 3: Presenting the Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're at a pivotal point, wanting to embrace this new chapter but feeling uncertain about how. Does that resonate?"
YOU:
"My 6-week Forest Therapy Program helps women like you reconnect with themselves and nature to find clarity, reduce stress, and design a fulfilling retirement. We combine guided forest walks with mindfulness practices and group coaching to create lasting transformation. A 2023 study in *Environmental Health and Preventive Medicine* found that forest therapy significantly reduced anxiety and improved mood in women over 50 (Park et al., 2023)."
YOU:
"Imagine yourself 6 weeks from now, feeling confident, connected, and excited about your future. How would that feel?"
Phase 4: Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Based on our conversation, I genuinely believe the program could be a great fit for you. Are you open to learning more about the investment and how to get started?"

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time."
"I understand. It's about prioritizing what matters. The program requires about 3 hours per week, including the guided walk. Think of it as investing in your well-being. What if dedicating that time actually gave you *more* energy and focus in other areas of your life?"
"It sounds interesting, but I'm not sure if it's for me."
"That's perfectly okay. What specifically makes you hesitant? Is it the group setting, the nature component, or something else? I want to ensure it aligns with your needs and preferences. I offer a satisfaction guarantee, so you can try the first week risk-free."
"I need to think about it."
"Of course. What part do you need more time to consider? Is it the financial investment, the program structure, or the commitment? I'm happy to answer any remaining questions to help you make an informed decision."
"I've tried other things, and nothing seems to work."
"I hear your frustration. Forest therapy is unique because it addresses the root cause of stress and disconnection by immersing you in nature and fostering a sense of belonging. It's not just another quick fix; it's a journey of self-discovery and lasting transformation. Plus, you are not alone, you are part of a group of like-minded women on a similar journey."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements of a successful discovery call for converting prospects into clients?

Building rapport, understanding the prospect's needs and pain points, presenting a tailored solution, confidently stating the price, and providing a clear call to action.

2. How can you effectively handle the common objection, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, inquire about the specific area they need to consider (financial, time commitment, etc.), and offer to provide additional information or answer questions.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call is crucial for converting prospects into paying clients.
  • Empathy and active listening are essential for understanding the prospect's needs and building trust.
  • Confidently presenting your program and pricing is key to demonstrating its value.
  • Handling objections effectively can overcome hesitation and close the sale.
  • Consistent client acquisition leads to a sustainable and thriving forest therapy practice.
