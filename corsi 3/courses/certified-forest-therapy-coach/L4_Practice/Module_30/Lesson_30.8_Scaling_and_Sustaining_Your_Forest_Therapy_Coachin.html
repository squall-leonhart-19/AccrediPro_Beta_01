Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm a Certified Forest Therapy Coach. I‚Äôm so glad you saw my workshop advertisement! To start, could you tell me what resonated with you about the workshop description?"

Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, does this approach resonate with you? Do you feel like this could be a good fit for what you're looking for? ... Great! Let me share the details of the program and how we can get started."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to talk to my partner about it."
"I understand. It's important to make decisions together. Would you like me to send you a detailed outline of the program and its benefits that you can share with them? Or, I'm happy to schedule a brief call with both of you to answer any questions."
"I'm just not sure I have the time."
"I hear you. Many of my clients are busy professionals and parents. The program is designed to be flexible and adaptable to your schedule. We can work together to find times that work for you, and the benefits you'll gain ‚Äì increased energy, reduced stress, and improved focus ‚Äì will actually help you be more efficient with your time."
"What if it doesn't work for me?"
"I understand your concern. While I can't guarantee specific results, I'm confident that if you fully participate in the program and implement the strategies we discuss, you'll experience positive changes. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee ‚Äì if you're not satisfied after the first two sessions, I'll refund your investment."
"I can find similar information online for free."
"That's a fair point. There's a lot of great information out there. What I offer is personalized guidance, accountability, and a structured framework to implement those concepts into your daily life. Forest Therapy is more than just reading about it. It's about experiencing it, and having a coach who can meet you where you are."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of a successful discovery call?

The four phases are: Build Rapport, Understand the Problem, Present Your Approach, and Close.

2. How should you respond to the objection "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the ROI, long-term benefits, and the cost of not solving the problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script to build confidence and fluency.
  • Prepare confident responses to common objections.
  • Clearly articulate the value of your program and its benefits.
  • Believe in your ability to help clients achieve their goals.
