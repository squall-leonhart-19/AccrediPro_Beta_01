Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Qualify 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thank you for scheduling this call. I'm Sarah, and I'm excited to learn more about what you're looking for. To start, could you tell me a bit about what sparked your interest in the 'Forest Bathing for Stress Relief' program?"
YOU:
"I understand you work in a high-stress environment. What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing right now?"
Phase 2: Explore Needs & Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"On a scale of 1 to 10, with 10 being the most stressed you've ever been, where would you rate your stress levels lately? What does that feel like in your body?"
YOU:
"What have you tried in the past to manage your stress? What worked, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine you wake up three months from now, having completed the program. How would you like to feel differently? What would be the biggest changes in your life?"
Phase 3: Present the Group Program 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're experiencing significant stress that's impacting your well-being. The 'Forest Bathing for Stress Relief' program is designed to help people in situations just like yours. It provides a supportive group environment where you'll learn evidence-based techniques to calm your nervous system, reduce stress hormones, and reconnect with nature."
YOU:
"Over the course of 8 weeks, we'll meet weekly for guided forest bathing sessions, where you'll learn to use your senses to attune to the natural world. You'll also receive weekly resources and exercises to integrate these practices into your daily life. A 2023 study in *Environmental Health and Preventative Medicine* found that participants in similar programs reported a 30% reduction in perceived stress."
YOU:
"What questions do you have about the program so far?"
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Susan, I truly believe this program could be a great fit for you. The next cohort starts on [Date]. Are you interested in joining us?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key elements to highlight when presenting the price of a group program?

Highlight the value provided (e.g., guided sessions, resources, community), compare it to the cost of ineffective solutions, and mention payment plan options.

2. How can you address a prospect's concern that they don't have enough time for a group program?

Emphasize that the program can *create* more time and energy by reducing stress and increasing focus. Frame it as a worthwhile investment in their well-being.

Key Takeaways

  • Qualify prospects early in the discovery call to ensure the group program aligns with their needs.
  • Focus on the tangible benefits of the program, such as stress reduction, improved sleep, and increased resilience.
  • Address budget concerns by breaking down the value and offering payment options.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently invite prospects to join the program.
