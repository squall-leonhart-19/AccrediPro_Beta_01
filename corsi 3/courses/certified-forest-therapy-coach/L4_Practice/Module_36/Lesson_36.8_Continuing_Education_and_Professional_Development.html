Handling Objections with Grace

Common Concerns & Confident Responses

"I'm not sure if I have the time for this."
"I understand. Time is precious. The program requires about 2-3 hours per week, including our sessions and your personal practice. But think about the time you're currently spending feeling anxious and unfulfilled. What if this program could help you reclaim that time and energy?"
"I'm worried it won't work for me."
"That's a valid concern. I can't guarantee specific results, as everyone's journey is unique. However, my program is based on evidence-based practices and personalized to your needs. Plus, I offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the program after the first two weeks, I'll offer a full refund."
"I need to talk to my family about it first."
"Of course. It's important to have their support. What questions do you think they might have? Perhaps I could send you some information to share with them, or we could even schedule a brief call together to address any concerns directly."
"I've tried other things before, and they didn't work."
"I hear you. It can be frustrating when things don't work out. What was missing from those previous experiences? My program is different because it focuses on [unique aspects of your program, e.g., nature connection, personalized approach, long-term transformation]. I also provide ongoing support and accountability to help you stay on track."

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the key phases of a successful discovery call?

The key phases are: Connection & Rapport, Exploring Challenges & Goals, Presenting Your Solution, and Invitation & Next Steps.

2. How should you handle the objection, "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern, then reframe the value by focusing on the long-term benefits and potential ROI of your program.

3. What's a powerful closing line you can use to invite a prospect to join your program?

"Based on everything you've shared, I truly believe this program can help you [achieve their desired outcome]. Are you ready to take that first step?"

Key Takeaways

  • Practice your discovery call script out loud to build confidence and fluency.
  • Anticipate common objections and prepare thoughtful, empathetic responses.
  • State your pricing with conviction, highlighting the value and potential ROI of your program.
  • Remember that client acquisition is about building relationships and serving others.
  • Believe in your ability to make a positive impact on your clients' lives.
