Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on sensory re-engagement. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Diane, it sounds like you're longing for a deeper connection with the natural world. Many people in your situation feel similarly. Let's explore how we can reawaken your senses and bring that feeling of peace back into your life. How does that sound?"
DIANE:
"That sounds wonderful. I'm just not sure where to start. I feel so out of touch."
YOU:
"That's perfectly okay. We'll start small. I want to guide you through a simple exercise right now. Close your eyes for a moment, and imagine you're standing in a forest. What's the first thing you notice?"
DIANE:
"Um... I guess I hear birds chirping."
YOU:
"Great! Focus on that sound. What kind of birds do you imagine they are? Are they close by, or far away? Just allow yourself to immerse in that sound for a few breaths."
YOU:
"Now, keeping your eyes closed, what do you smell? Is it earthy, floral, damp, or something else?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use specific sensory prompts instead of general ones?

Specific prompts like "What is the texture of the bark?" help to engage the client's senses more deeply and create a more vivid experience.

2. What should you do if a client says they "don't see anything" when you ask them to visualize a forest?

Gently guide them by asking about other senses: "Okay, if you don't see anything, what do you hear? Or smell?" This helps them find a sensory entry point.

Key Takeaways

  • Deep sensory engagement is crucial for reconnecting clients with nature.
  • Specific prompts are more effective than general ones.
  • Allow ample silence for clients to fully experience the imagery.
  • Validate the client's feelings throughout the process.
  • Start small and build from there.
