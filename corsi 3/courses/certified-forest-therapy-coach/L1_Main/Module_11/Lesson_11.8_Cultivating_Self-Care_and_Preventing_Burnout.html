Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation, focusing on reconnecting with nature. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Beth, thank you for sharing so openly. It takes courage to acknowledge these feelings. Many women in this stage of life feel similarly. One thing that has helped many of my clients is reconnecting with the natural world. How does that resonate with you?"
BETH:
"Nature? I guess I haven't really thought about it. I used to love hiking, but I haven't done that in years."
YOU:
"Sometimes, the things we used to love are the very things we need to revisit. Nature has a powerful way of grounding us and sparking joy. It's not about strenuous activity, but about gentle connection. Would you be open to exploring that a bit?"
BETH:
"I suppose so. I'm willing to try anything at this point."
YOU:
"Great. Let's start with something simple. This week, I'd like you to spend just 15 minutes each day in a natural setting. It could be a park, your backyard, even sitting under a tree. The key is to be present and engage your senses. Notice the colors, sounds, smells, and textures around you. No phone, no distractions, just you and nature. How does that feel?"
BETH:
"Fifteen minutes? I think I can manage that. It sounds...peaceful."
YOU:
"Exactly. Peace is often what we're seeking. And from that peace, inspiration and joy can emerge. We'll check in next week to see what you experienced. Does that sound like a good plan?"

‚ùå Pressuring her to commit to strenuous outdoor activities

‚ùå Giving generic advice ("just get out more!")
‚úì Provide specific, actionable steps (engage your senses, notice colors and sounds).
‚ùå Talking *at* her instead of listening *to* her
‚úì Ask open-ended questions ("How does that resonate with you?") and listen deeply to her responses.

You'll Know It Worked When...

  • Beth expresses relief: "It's good to know I'm not alone."
  • She seems intrigued by the idea of reconnecting with nature.
  • She agrees to try the 15-minute daily practice.
  • Her body language softens, and she makes eye contact.
  • She thanks you for offering a new perspective.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Beth's feelings before suggesting a solution?

Validating her feelings builds trust and makes her more receptive to your suggestions. It shows that you understand her experience.

2. What are some specific ways Beth can engage her senses during her 15 minutes in nature?

She can notice the colors of the flowers, listen to the birds singing, smell the fresh air, and feel the texture of the leaves.

Key Takeaways

  • Acknowledge the client's feelings before offering solutions.
  • Start with small, manageable steps to avoid overwhelm.
  • Focus on sensory engagement to deepen the connection with nature.
  • Help the client envision a positive future.
  • Listen more than you speak.
