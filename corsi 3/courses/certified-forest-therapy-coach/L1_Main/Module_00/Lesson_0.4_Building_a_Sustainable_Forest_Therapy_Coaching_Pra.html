What

What You Can and Cannot Do

As a Certified Forest Therapy Coachâ„¢, it's critical to understand the boundaries of your role. Scope of practice defines the services you are qualified and legally permitted to perform. This ensures client safety and maintains professional integrity.

What You CAN Do:

  • Guide clients through evidence-based forest therapy activities.
  • Provide education on the health benefits of nature exposure.
  • Help clients set achievable wellness goals related to nature connection.
  • Offer support and encouragement for clients to integrate nature into their daily lives.
  • Facilitate group walks and nature-based workshops.

What You CANNOT Do:

  • Diagnose or treat medical conditions.
  • Prescribe medications or supplements.
  • Provide psychotherapy or counseling services unless you are also a licensed mental health professional.
  • Offer advice that contradicts medical recommendations from a client's healthcare provider.

Remember, your role is to empower clients to enhance their well-being through nature, not to replace medical care. A 2021 study in the journal *Environmental Health* found that while forest therapy can significantly reduce stress and improve mood, it should be considered complementary to, not a substitute for, conventional medical treatments (Park et al., 2021).

Legal Considerations

Navigating the legal landscape is crucial for protecting yourself and your clients. Here are some key areas to consider:

  • Informed Consent: Always obtain informed consent from clients before beginning any forest therapy activities. This includes explaining the potential risks and benefits, as well as the scope of your services.
  • Liability Insurance: Secure professional liability insurance to protect yourself in case of accidents or injuries during sessions.
  • Data Privacy: Comply with data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA) when collecting and storing client information.
  • Permits and Regulations: Be aware of any permits or regulations required for conducting forest therapy activities in specific locations (e.g., parks, forests).

A 2022 report by the American Counseling Association emphasized the importance of understanding state and local laws related to professional practice. Failure to comply with these regulations can result in legal penalties and damage your professional reputation (Kaplan et al., 2022).

Ethics & Professionalism

Ethical conduct is the cornerstone of a successful and trustworthy coaching practice. Adhere to the following ethical principles:

  • Confidentiality: Maintain the confidentiality of all client information.
  • Boundaries: Establish and maintain clear professional boundaries with clients.
  • Integrity: Be honest and transparent in all your interactions.
  • Competence: Only offer services within your scope of practice and expertise.
  • Respect: Treat all clients with respect and dignity, regardless of their background or beliefs.

The International Coaching Federation (ICF) Code of Ethics provides a comprehensive framework for ethical coaching practice. Regularly review and adhere to these guidelines to ensure you are providing the highest standard of care (ICF, 2019).
