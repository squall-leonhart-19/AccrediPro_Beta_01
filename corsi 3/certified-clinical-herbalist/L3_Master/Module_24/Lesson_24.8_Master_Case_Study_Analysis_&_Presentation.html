Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Sarah

1

Review Herb Actions

Discuss the potential stimulating effects of black cohosh in some individuals. It's not always calming.

2

Explore Constitution

Guide Sarah to consider Emily's constitution. Is she naturally a "hot" or "cold" type? This influences herb selection.

3

Simplify the Protocol

Suggest starting with *one* herb at a time to isolate the effects. This helps identify the culprit if there's a reaction.

4

Encourage Intuition

Help Sarah trust her gut. Ask: "What does your intuition tell you about Emily? What herb feels most aligned?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What's the most important thing to validate when mentoring a new practitioner?

Their feelings of uncertainty and self-doubt. New practitioners often need reassurance that it's okay to not have all the answers.

2. Why is it helpful to share your own past mistakes with a mentee?

It normalizes the learning process and helps them feel less alone in their challenges. It shows that even experienced practitioners make mistakes.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Focus on building confidence and intuition.
  • Share your experiences, both successes and failures.
  • Empower your mentee to make their own decisions.
  • Celebrate progress and small wins.
