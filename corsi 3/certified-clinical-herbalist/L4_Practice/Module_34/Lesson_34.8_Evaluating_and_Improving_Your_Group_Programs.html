Discovery Call Script

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Susan, thanks for scheduling this call. I'm excited to chat with you! I saw you were interested in the Hormone Harmony program. What resonated with you about it?"
YOU:
"It's so common for women in their 50s to experience hormonal shifts. Tell me, what's been the most challenging part for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Let's dive into those hot flashes. How frequent are they, and how do they impact your day?"
YOU:
"And what about sleep? Are you having trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or both?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to manage these symptoms? What worked, even a little, and what didn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine you woke up tomorrow and felt amazing. What would that day look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Susan, it sounds like you're dealing with a lot. What I'm hearing is [summarize her symptoms and frustrations]. Is that a fair summary?"
YOU:
"My Hormone Harmony program is designed to address these issues holistically. Over 8 weeks, we'll use herbs, nutrition, and lifestyle strategies to rebalance your hormones naturally. You'll also have the support of a community of women going through the same thing. Most participants start seeing noticeable improvements within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"This isn't just about managing symptoms; it's about addressing the root causes of hormonal imbalance and empowering you to take control of your health."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"Susan, based on what you've shared, I truly believe this program can help you feel like yourself again. Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you more about the program details and how you can enroll."

Handling Objections

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm not sure if I can afford it."
"I understand. Investing in your health is a big decision. Can I ask, how much have you already spent on remedies that haven't worked? This program is designed to provide lasting results, potentially saving you money in the long run. We also have a payment plan available."
"I'm worried I won't have time."
"That's a valid concern. The program requires about 3-4 hours per week, including the group sessions and your personal practice. But think about the time you're *already* losing due to sleep deprivation and lack of energy. This program is designed to help you reclaim that time and energy."
"I'm not sure if it will really work for me."
"I get it. It's hard to trust after trying so many things. But this program is different because it's personalized and holistic. We'll address your unique needs and provide ongoing support. Plus, you'll have the encouragement of a supportive community. I also offer a satisfaction guarantee. If you participate fully and don't see results, I'll refund your money."
"Can I think about it and get back to you?"
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think about? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision. Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or something else?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of the discovery call, and how much time should you allocate to each?

The four phases are: 1) Build Rapport (5 min), 2) Understand the Problem (10 min), 3) Present Your Approach (10 min), and 4) Close (5 min).

2. What is a good response to the objection, "I need to think about it"?

A good response is: "I understand. What specifically do you need to think about? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

3. How can you confidently present your pricing?

Practice saying your price out loud, clearly state what's included, and highlight the value and potential ROI of the program.

Key Takeaways

  • Building rapport is essential for creating trust and connection with potential clients.
  • Understanding the client's problems deeply allows you to tailor your program to their needs.
  • Confidently address objections by acknowledging concerns and providing valuable solutions.
  • Clearly and confidently present your pricing, emphasizing the program's value and benefits.
  • Practice your closing lines to confidently guide potential clients toward enrollment.
