Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing all of that. It sounds incredibly disruptive, especially when you're trying to focus on your students. I want to reassure you that what you're experiencing is a very normal transition, and there are effective ways to ease these symptoms naturally."
MARIA:
"I'm glad to hear that. My doctor mentioned hormone replacement therapy, but I'm hesitant about the potential side effects."
YOU:
"That's a valid concern, and it's great that you're exploring all your options. Many women find significant relief with herbal support, often without the side effects associated with HRT. Before we talk about specific herbs, I want to understand a bit more about your overall health and lifestyle. Would that be okay?"
MARIA:
"Yes, absolutely. I'm open to anything that might help."
YOU:
"Perfect. Let's start with a few questions about your diet, stress levels, and sleep habits. This will help us create a personalized plan that addresses the root causes of your symptoms. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Maria's experience before recommending herbs?

Validating her experience builds trust and rapport. It shows that you understand the impact of her symptoms on her daily life, making her more receptive to your recommendations.

2. What's a better alternative to saying "just menopause" when addressing Maria's concerns?

Instead of saying "just menopause," acknowledge the specific challenges she's facing, such as sleep disruption and irritability. For example, you could say, "I understand that these night sweats and mood swings are making it difficult to focus at work."

Key Takeaways

  • Always validate the client's experience before offering solutions.
  • Avoid jargon and use language the client can easily understand.
  • Focus on building trust and rapport.
  • Gather sufficient information before recommending specific herbs.
  • Manage expectations and avoid overpromising results.
