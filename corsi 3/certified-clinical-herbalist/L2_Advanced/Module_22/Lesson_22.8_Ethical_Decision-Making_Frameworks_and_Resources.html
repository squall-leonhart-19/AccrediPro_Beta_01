Ethical Dilemma: Disclosure & Scope

Eleanor comes to you seeking a natural approach to her chronic UTIs and overall well-being. During the intake, she reveals that she has not disclosed her alcohol consumption to her primary care physician because she fears judgment. She asks you not to disclose this information either, as she trusts you more. She states, "I just want something natural that works, and I don't want to be lectured about my lifestyle choices."

Clinical Reasoning Process

Ethical Analysis

Step 1: Identify the Ethical Principles at Play
Several principles are in conflict: Autonomy (Eleanor's right to make decisions about her health), Beneficence (your duty to act in her best interest), Non-maleficence (avoiding harm), and Veracity (honesty and transparency).
Step 2: Assess the Potential Harms
Withholding information about alcohol consumption could lead to inaccurate assessment and ineffective treatment. It could also mask underlying liver issues or medication interactions. Chronic alcohol use is a significant factor in recurrent UTIs, fatigue, and anxiety.
Step 3: Consider the Legal & Professional Boundaries
As a clinical herbalist, you are not legally mandated to report alcohol consumption. However, professional ethics require you to prioritize patient safety and provide informed consent. Deception, even by omission, undermines the therapeutic relationship.
Step 4: Develop a Plan of Action
Address Eleanor's concerns with empathy. Explain the importance of transparency for accurate assessment and treatment. Emphasize that you are a partner in her health journey, not a judge. Discuss strategies for reducing alcohol consumption and encourage her to disclose this information to her physician. Document the conversation thoroughly.

Differential Considerations

Alternative Approaches

1

Motivational Interviewing

Use open-ended questions and reflective listening to explore Eleanor's ambivalence about alcohol consumption. Help her identify her own reasons for change.

2

Harm Reduction Strategy

If Eleanor is unwilling to abstain, focus on reducing the frequency and quantity of alcohol consumption. Provide education on liver-supportive herbs and nutrients.

3

Collaborative Care

Encourage Eleanor to seek therapy for her anxiety and childhood trauma. This could address the root causes of her alcohol use.

4

Informed Refusal

If Eleanor continues to refuse disclosure to her physician, document her informed refusal. Clearly state the potential risks of withholding this information.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary ethical conflict in this case?

The conflict lies between respecting the client's autonomy and the practitioner's duty to beneficence and non-maleficence.

2. What are the potential consequences of not addressing the client's alcohol consumption?

Failure to address alcohol consumption could lead to ineffective treatment, masked underlying conditions, and potential harm from medication interactions.

3. What is the most ethical course of action in this scenario?

The most ethical approach involves open communication, empathetic exploration of the client's concerns, and encouragement of transparency with her primary care physician, while respecting her right to make informed decisions.

Key Takeaways

  • Ethical dilemmas are inherent in clinical practice, requiring careful consideration of competing principles.
  • Transparency and honesty are crucial for building trust and providing effective care.
  • Motivational interviewing can be a powerful tool for addressing sensitive topics like alcohol consumption.
  • It is essential to know your scope of practice and when to refer to other healthcare professionals.
  • Document all ethical considerations and decisions thoroughly in the client's record.
