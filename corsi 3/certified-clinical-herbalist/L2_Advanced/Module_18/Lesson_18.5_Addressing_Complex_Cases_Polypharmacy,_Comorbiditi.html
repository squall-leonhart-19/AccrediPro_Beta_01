Understanding Polypharmacy

Polypharmacy is generally defined as the concurrent use of five or more medications by an individual. This is increasingly common, especially in older adults. According to a 2021 study in the Journal of the American Geriatrics Society, approximately 40% of older adults in the US take five or more medications (Qato et al., 2021). This increases the risk of adverse drug events, drug-drug interactions, and non-adherence.

As herbalists, we need to be aware of potential herb-drug interactions. Some herbs can alter the absorption, distribution, metabolism, or excretion of medications, leading to either increased or decreased drug effects. Always encourage clients to inform their prescribing physicians about their herbal regimens.

Navigating Comorbidities

Comorbidities refer to the presence of two or more chronic diseases or conditions in a patient. These conditions can interact with each other, complicating diagnosis and treatment. For example, someone might have both type 2 diabetes and hypertension.

Identifying Contraindications

A contraindication is a specific situation in which a drug, procedure, or surgery should not be used because it may be harmful to the person. In herbalism, contraindications can arise due to pregnancy, breastfeeding, specific medical conditions, or interactions with medications.

Always prioritize safety. Thoroughly research any herb you plan to use, especially in clients with complex health histories. If you are ever unsure, err on the side of caution and consult with a more experienced herbalist or healthcare professional.
