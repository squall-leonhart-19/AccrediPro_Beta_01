# FM-CP™ Curriculum
# Functional Medicine Certified Professional

---

## Program Overview

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Credential** | FM-CP™ (Functional Medicine - Certified Professional) |
| **Level** | Professional (Tier 2) |
| **Total Hours** | 75 contact hours |
| **Modules** | 12 core + 3 electives |
| **Practical** | 3 case study submissions |
| **Examination** | 100-question proctored exam |
| **Pass Score** | 75% (75/100) |
| **Timeline** | 3-6 months typical |
| **Prerequisite** | FM-FC™ or equivalent |

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, candidates will be able to:

1. Apply advanced knowledge of gut, hormonal, metabolic, and immune health
2. Conduct comprehensive client assessments using FM frameworks
3. Develop evidence-based, individualized protocols
4. Utilize coaching techniques to support behavior change
5. Work effectively with special populations
6. Maintain ethical, professional practice standards
7. Document appropriately and manage a client practice
8. Know when and how to refer to other practitioners

## Module 6: Advanced Gut Health
### 5 Hours

### Lesson 6.1: Microbiome Deep Dive (60 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain microbiome composition and function
- Describe factors affecting microbiome health
- Apply microbiome-focused interventions

**Content:**
- Microbiome ecology and diversity
- Beneficial vs. pathogenic species
- The gut-immune connection
- The gut-brain-microbiome axis
- Factors affecting microbiome balance:
  - Diet
  - Antibiotics
  - Stress
  - Environment
- Supporting microbiome health
- Prebiotics, probiotics, postbiotics

---

### Lesson 6.2: Leaky Gut Syndrome (45 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain intestinal permeability
- Identify causes and consequences
- Apply gut healing protocols

**Content:**
- What is intestinal permeability?
- The tight junction barrier
- Causes of increased permeability:
  - Zonulin and gluten
  - Dysbiosis
  - Stress
  - Medications
  - Toxins
- Systemic consequences:
  - Food sensitivities
  - Autoimmunity
  - Inflammation
- Assessment approaches
- The 5R gut healing protocol

---

### Lesson 6.3: SIBO, SIFO, and Dysbiosis (60 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Differentiate SIBO, SIFO, and dysbiosis
- Recognize signs and symptoms
- Understand treatment approaches

**Content:**
- Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO):
  - Hydrogen vs. methane dominant
  - Risk factors
  - Symptoms
  - Testing
- Small Intestinal Fungal Overgrowth (SIFO):
  - Candida overgrowth
  - Risk factors
  - Symptoms
- Large intestine dysbiosis
- Treatment approaches:
  - Antimicrobial protocols
  - Diet modifications
  - Prokinetics
  - Prevention of recurrence

---

### Lesson 6.4: GI Testing & Interpretation (60 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Describe functional GI testing options
- Interpret basic test results
- Know testing limitations

**Content:**
- Comprehensive stool analysis:
  - Markers evaluated
  - Interpretation basics
  - Limitations
- SIBO breath testing:
  - Lactulose vs. glucose
  - Reading results
  - False positives/negatives
- Food sensitivity testing:
  - IgG vs. IgE
  - Limitations and considerations
- Organic acids testing (gut markers)
- When to recommend testing
- Scope considerations

---

### Lesson 6.5: Gut Healing Protocols (60 min)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply the 5R framework
- Design gut healing protocols
- Support long-term gut health

**Content:**
- The 5R Framework:
  1. **Remove**: Triggers, pathogens, inflammatory foods
  2. **Replace**: Digestive support (enzymes, HCl)
  3. **Reinoculate**: Probiotics, prebiotics
  4. **Repair**: Gut-healing nutrients
  5. **Rebalance**: Lifestyle factors
- Protocol design considerations
- Timeline expectations
- Reintroduction strategies
- Maintenance protocols
- Case examples
