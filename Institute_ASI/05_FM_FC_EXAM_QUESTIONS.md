## Module 1: Introduction to Functional Medicine
### Questions 1-10

---

**Question 1**
What is the primary focus of functional medicine?

A) Treating symptoms with medication
B) Identifying and addressing root causes of disease
C) Performing surgical interventions
D) Specializing in one organ system

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Functional medicine focuses on identifying and addressing the root causes of disease rather than just treating symptoms.*

---

**Question 2**
Which of the following best describes the functional medicine approach to patient care?

A) Disease-centered, organ-specific
B) Provider-directed, protocol-driven
C) Patient-centered, systems-based
D) Symptom-focused, medication-based

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Functional medicine uses a patient-centered, systems-based approach that considers the whole person.*

---

**Question 3**
The Functional Medicine Matrix includes how many core clinical imbalances?

A) 5
B) 6
C) 7
D) 8

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: The FM Matrix identifies seven core clinical imbalances: Assimilation, Defense & Repair, Energy, Biotransformation & Elimination, Transport, Communication, and Structural Integrity.*

---

**Question 4**
In functional medicine, "ATM" stands for:

A) Automated Testing Methods
B) Antecedents, Triggers, Mediators
C) Assessment, Treatment, Maintenance
D) Analysis, Testing, Monitoring

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: ATM stands for Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators - the factors that contribute to disease development.*

---

**Question 5**
Which of the following is considered an "antecedent" in functional medicine?

A) A stressful divorce that occurred 6 months ago
B) Genetic predisposition to autoimmune disease
C) Current inflammation levels
D) Ongoing exposure to mold

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Antecedents are predisposing factors such as genetics or early life experiences that set the stage for disease.*

---

**Question 6**
The principle of "biochemical individuality" in functional medicine means:

A) All patients should receive the same treatment
B) Each person has unique nutritional and health needs
C) Biochemistry is the only factor in health
D) Individual preferences don't affect treatment

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Biochemical individuality recognizes that each person has unique genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors affecting their health.*

---

**Question 7**
Which statement about the scope of practice for functional medicine practitioners is TRUE?

A) FM practitioners can diagnose medical conditions
B) FM practitioners can prescribe medications
C) FM practitioners work within defined scope limitations
D) FM practitioners replace medical doctors

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Functional medicine practitioners must work within their defined scope and cannot diagnose or prescribe medications.*

---

**Question 8**
The "therapeutic partnership" in functional medicine refers to:

A) Partnership between two practitioners
B) Collaborative relationship between practitioner and patient
C) Partnership with pharmaceutical companies
D) Relationship between FM and conventional medicine

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The therapeutic partnership is the collaborative relationship between practitioner and patient where both actively participate in the healing process.*

---

**Question 9**
Which core clinical imbalance in the FM Matrix relates to digestive function?

A) Transport
B) Communication
C) Assimilation
D) Energy

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Assimilation refers to digestion, absorption, and the gut microbiome.*

---

**Question 10**
According to functional medicine principles, which approach is most appropriate when a patient presents with symptoms outside your scope?

A) Attempt to treat them anyway
B) Refer to an appropriate medical professional
C) Tell them functional medicine can't help
D) Recommend supplements for all symptoms

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: When symptoms are outside scope of practice, appropriate referral to qualified medical professionals is essential.*

## Module 2: Foundations of Human Physiology
### Questions 11-20

---

**Question 11**
Systems biology in functional medicine emphasizes that:

A) Each body system operates independently
B) Body systems are interconnected and influence each other
C) Only the nervous system controls health
D) Systems should be treated in isolation

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Systems biology recognizes that body systems are interconnected and dysfunction in one area affects others.*

---

**Question 12**
The gut-brain axis primarily communicates through which nerve?

A) Trigeminal nerve
B) Vagus nerve
C) Sciatic nerve
D) Optic nerve

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The vagus nerve is the primary communication pathway between the gut and brain.*

---

**Question 13**
Approximately what percentage of the body's serotonin is produced in the gut?

A) 10%
B) 50%
C) 70-90%
D) 100%

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Approximately 70-90% of serotonin is produced in the gut, highlighting the gut-brain connection.*

---

**Question 14**
The HPA axis involves which three components?

A) Heart, Pancreas, Adrenals
B) Hypothalamus, Pituitary, Adrenals
C) Hippocampus, Pituitary, Adrenals
D) Hypothalamus, Pancreas, Adrenals

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: HPA stands for Hypothalamus-Pituitary-Adrenal axis, the central stress response system.*

---

**Question 15**
Phase I liver detoxification involves:

A) Conjugation reactions
B) Cytochrome P450 enzymes
C) Elimination through kidneys
D) Storage in fat tissue

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Phase I detoxification uses cytochrome P450 enzymes to transform toxins.*

---

**Question 16**
Chronic inflammation differs from acute inflammation in that chronic inflammation:

A) Is a short-term healing response
B) Can contribute to disease development over time
C) Is always beneficial to the body
D) Only occurs after injury

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Unlike acute inflammation which is a healing response, chronic inflammation can persist and contribute to disease development.*

---

**Question 17**
The microbiome refers to:

A) Small organisms visible under microscope
B) The community of microorganisms living in and on the body
C) Vitamins and minerals in the body
D) Small molecules in the bloodstream

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The microbiome is the community of bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms living in and on the body.*

---

**Question 18**
Which of the following is a sign of potential thyroid dysfunction?

A) Unexplained weight changes
B) Improved energy levels
C) Better hair growth
D) Increased metabolism

**Correct Answer: A**
*Rationale: Unexplained weight changes (gain or loss) can be a sign of thyroid dysfunction.*

---

**Question 19**
Autoimmunity occurs when:

A) The immune system is too weak to function
B) The immune system attacks the body's own tissues
C) Bacteria invade the body
D) The body produces too many antibodies against pathogens

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Autoimmunity is when the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own healthy tissues.*

---

**Question 20**
Which organs are primarily responsible for Phase II detoxification conjugation?

A) Kidneys and bladder
B) Liver and intestines
C) Lungs and skin
D) Heart and blood vessels

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Phase II conjugation primarily occurs in the liver, with some activity in the intestines.*

## Module 3: Nutrition Fundamentals
### Questions 21-30

---

**Question 21**
Which macronutrient is the body's preferred quick energy source?

A) Protein
B) Fat
C) Carbohydrates
D) Fiber

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Carbohydrates are the body's preferred and quickest source of energy.*

---

**Question 22**
Essential fatty acids are called "essential" because:

A) They provide the most calories
B) The body cannot produce them and must obtain them from food
C) They are the most important nutrient
D) They are only found in essential oils

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Essential fatty acids cannot be synthesized by the body and must be obtained through diet.*

---

**Question 23**
Which of the following is considered an anti-inflammatory food?

A) Refined sugar
B) Processed vegetable oils
C) Wild-caught fatty fish
D) Trans fats

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Wild-caught fatty fish contains omega-3 fatty acids which have anti-inflammatory properties.*

---

**Question 24**
The glycemic index measures:

A) How much fiber is in a food
B) How quickly a food raises blood sugar
C) The total calories in a food
D) The protein content of a food

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The glycemic index measures how quickly a carbohydrate-containing food raises blood glucose levels.*

---

**Question 25**
Common foods eliminated in an elimination diet include all EXCEPT:

A) Gluten
B) Dairy
C) Leafy green vegetables
D) Soy

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Leafy green vegetables are not typically eliminated; common eliminations include gluten, dairy, soy, eggs, and corn.*

---

**Question 26**
Insulin resistance is characterized by:

A) Cells responding normally to insulin
B) Cells becoming less responsive to insulin's signals
C) The pancreas producing no insulin
D) Too little glucose in the blood

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Insulin resistance occurs when cells become less responsive to insulin, requiring more insulin to achieve the same effect.*

---

**Question 27**
Which vitamin is known as the "sunshine vitamin"?

A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin D
D) Vitamin E

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Vitamin D is called the sunshine vitamin because it is synthesized in the skin upon exposure to sunlight.*

---

**Question 28**
Adequate hydration is important for all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Cellular function
B) Detoxification
C) Increasing body temperature
D) Nutrient transport

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Hydration helps regulate (not increase) body temperature and supports cellular function, detoxification, and nutrient transport.*

---

**Question 29**
The reintroduction phase of an elimination diet is important because:

A) It allows eating all foods immediately
B) It helps identify which specific foods cause reactions
C) It is not actually necessary
D) It allows eating larger portions

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The reintroduction phase systematically adds foods back to identify which specific foods cause symptoms.*

---

**Question 30**
Magnesium deficiency may contribute to all of the following EXCEPT:

A) Muscle cramps
B) Sleep difficulties
C) Improved bone density
D) Fatigue

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Magnesium deficiency can cause muscle cramps, sleep issues, and fatigue; it does not improve bone density (deficiency harms bones).*

## Module 4: Lifestyle Factors
### Questions 31-40

---

**Question 31**
Which hormone is primarily responsible for regulating the sleep-wake cycle?

A) Cortisol
B) Insulin
C) Melatonin
D) Estrogen

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Melatonin is the primary hormone regulating the circadian rhythm and sleep-wake cycle.*

---

**Question 32**
Chronic stress can lead to elevated levels of which hormone?

A) Melatonin
B) Cortisol
C) Testosterone
D) Growth hormone

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Chronic stress leads to elevated cortisol levels, which can have widespread health effects.*

---

**Question 33**
"Sleep hygiene" refers to:

A) Cleaning your bedroom
B) Practices and habits that promote good sleep
C) Using sanitizer before bed
D) Sleeping in a hospital

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Sleep hygiene refers to practices and environmental factors that support quality sleep.*

---

**Question 34**
Which type of exercise is most associated with building muscle mass?

A) Yoga
B) Swimming
C) Resistance training
D) Walking

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Resistance training (strength training) is most effective for building muscle mass.*

---

**Question 35**
BPA is a toxin commonly found in:

A) Fresh vegetables
B) Plastics and food container linings
C) Organic meat
D) Filtered water

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: BPA (bisphenol A) is commonly found in plastics and the lining of food containers.*

---

**Question 36**
The circadian rhythm is approximately how long?

A) 12 hours
B) 24 hours
C) 48 hours
D) 1 week

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The circadian rhythm follows an approximately 24-hour cycle aligned with the light-dark cycle.*

---

**Question 37**
Research on Blue Zones suggests that which factor contributes to longevity?

A) Working longer hours
B) Strong social connections and community
C) Eating more processed foods
D) Avoiding all physical activity

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Blue Zone research shows that strong social connections and sense of community contribute to longevity.*

---

**Question 38**
Which practice has been shown to activate the parasympathetic nervous system?

A) Intense exercise
B) Deep breathing exercises
C) Drinking caffeine
D) Watching stressful news

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Deep breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, promoting relaxation.*

---

**Question 39**
Heavy metals that can accumulate in the body include all EXCEPT:

A) Lead
B) Mercury
C) Calcium
D) Cadmium

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Calcium is an essential mineral, not a toxic heavy metal. Lead, mercury, and cadmium are toxic heavy metals.*

---

**Question 40**
Overtraining can negatively impact health by:

A) Building too much muscle
B) Increasing chronic inflammation and stress hormones
C) Improving recovery time
D) Enhancing immune function

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Overtraining can increase inflammation, elevate stress hormones, and suppress immune function.*

## Module 5: Putting It Together
### Questions 41-50

---

**Question 41**
The FM Timeline tool is used to:

A) Schedule appointments
B) Track the chronological development of health issues
C) Time how long consultations take
D) Plan future treatments

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The FM Timeline tracks the chronological sequence of life events, symptoms, and health changes.*

---

**Question 42**
A comprehensive health history should include all EXCEPT:

A) Chief concerns
B) Family medical history
C) Financial information
D) Lifestyle factors

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: A comprehensive health history includes chief concerns, medical/family history, and lifestyle factors, but not financial information.*

---

**Question 43**
The "5 Whys" technique in functional medicine is used to:

A) Ask five questions per visit
B) Dig deeper to find root causes
C) Limit conversation to five topics
D) Schedule five follow-up appointments

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: The "5 Whys" technique involves asking "why" repeatedly to drill down to root causes.*

---

**Question 44**
When creating a protocol, which approach is recommended?

A) Implement all changes at once
B) Start with multiple supplements
C) Begin with foundational changes, one at a time
D) Focus only on advanced interventions

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Starting with foundational changes and implementing one change at a time allows for assessment of what's working.*

---

**Question 45**
Motivational interviewing is a technique used to:

A) Force clients to change behavior
B) Support client autonomy and behavior change
C) Interview clients for a job
D) Determine client motivation levels

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Motivational interviewing is a collaborative conversation style that supports client autonomy and motivation for change.*

---

**Question 46**
When a client presents with symptoms suggesting a serious medical condition, you should:

A) Recommend supplements
B) Attempt to treat the symptoms
C) Refer them to an appropriate medical professional
D) Wait to see if symptoms improve

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: When serious medical conditions are suspected, immediate referral to appropriate medical professionals is essential.*

---

**Question 47**
Documentation of client interactions is important because:

A) It is optional for FM practitioners
B) It provides continuity of care and legal protection
C) It is only needed for medical doctors
D) It slows down the consultation process

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: Documentation ensures continuity of care, tracks progress, and provides legal protection.*

---

**Question 48**
The DFSM order for protocol development stands for:

A) Diagnosis, Formula, Supplements, Medicine
B) Diet, Foundational, Specific, Maintenance
C) Detox, Food, Sleep, Movement
D) Drugs, Foods, Sleep, Meditation

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: DFSM stands for Diet modifications, Foundational supports, Specific interventions, and Maintenance.*

---

**Question 49**
When a client shows resistance to recommended changes, the best approach is to:

A) Insist they follow your recommendations exactly
B) Give up on making recommendations
C) Explore their concerns and meet them where they are
D) Terminate the client relationship

**Correct Answer: C**
*Rationale: Exploring client concerns and meeting them where they are supports better outcomes and adherence.*

---

**Question 50**
The pathway after completing FM-FC™ Foundation certification includes:

A) No further certification options
B) FM-CP™ Professional certification, then BC-FMP™ Board certification
C) Immediate ability to diagnose conditions
D) Automatic medical license

**Correct Answer: B**
*Rationale: After FM-FC™, the pathway continues to FM-CP™ (Professional) and then BC-FMP™ (Board Certified).*
