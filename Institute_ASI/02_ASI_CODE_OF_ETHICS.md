# AccrediPro Standards Institute
# Code of Ethics

---

## Preamble

The AccrediPro Standards Institute (ASI) Code of Ethics establishes the standards of professional conduct for all ASI-certified professionals. This Code reflects our commitment to protecting the public, advancing the profession, and maintaining the integrity of ASI credentials.

All individuals holding ASI credentials agree to uphold this Code as a condition of certification.

## Core Ethical Principles

### 1. Beneficence
Act in the best interest of clients and the public. Strive to contribute positively to client well-being.

### 2. Non-Maleficence
Do no harm. Avoid actions that risk harm to clients, whether physical, emotional, or financial.

### 3. Autonomy
Respect client autonomy and their right to make informed decisions about their own health and wellness.

### 4. Justice
Treat all clients fairly and equitably, without discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age, disability, or socioeconomic status.

### 5. Fidelity
Honor commitments and maintain trust in professional relationships.

### 6. Veracity
Be truthful in all professional communications and representations.

## Professional Competence

### 2.1 Maintaining Competence

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Practice within competence** - Only provide services for which they have adequate training, education, and experience.

b) **Pursue ongoing education** - Fulfill continuing education requirements and stay current with developments in their specialty.

c) **Recognize limitations** - Acknowledge the boundaries of their expertise and refer clients when needs exceed their competence.

d) **Seek supervision** - Obtain appropriate supervision or consultation when working in new areas or with challenging cases.

### 2.2 Accurate Representation

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Represent credentials accurately** - Only claim credentials they currently hold and maintain.

b) **Use credential designations properly** - Follow ASI guidelines for displaying credentials.

c) **Avoid misleading titles** - Not use titles or designations that imply qualifications they do not possess.

d) **Correct misunderstandings** - Clarify when clients or others misunderstand their qualifications or scope.

### 2.3 Impairment

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Monitor personal wellness** - Be aware of personal issues that may impair professional judgment.

b) **Refrain when impaired** - Not practice when physical, mental, or emotional impairment could harm clients.

c) **Seek help** - Obtain appropriate assistance when personal issues affect professional functioning.

## Integrity & Honesty

### 3.1 Truthfulness

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Be honest** - Provide accurate information to clients, colleagues, and the public.

b) **Avoid deception** - Not engage in fraud, misrepresentation, or deceptive practices.

c) **Disclose conflicts** - Reveal any conflicts of interest that could affect professional judgment.

d) **Honor agreements** - Fulfill commitments and contractual obligations.

### 3.2 Credential Integrity

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Earn credentials honestly** - Not engage in cheating, plagiarism, or misrepresentation in obtaining certification.

b) **Report accurately** - Provide truthful information on applications, renewals, and CE reporting.

c) **Protect exam security** - Not share exam content or assist others in cheating.

d) **Report violations** - Report known violations of credential integrity.

## Client Welfare

### 4.1 Prioritizing Client Interests

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Put clients first** - Prioritize client welfare over personal or financial interests.

b) **Provide quality services** - Deliver services that meet professional standards.

c) **Avoid exploitation** - Not exploit clients emotionally, financially, sexually, or in any other manner.

d) **Support informed decisions** - Provide information clients need to make informed choices.

### 4.2 Informed Consent

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Explain services** - Clearly describe the nature and purpose of services offered.

b) **Discuss limitations** - Explain the limitations of services and realistic expected outcomes.

c) **Disclose fees** - Provide clear information about fees before services begin.

d) **Explain confidentiality** - Describe confidentiality policies and limitations.

e) **Obtain consent** - Obtain appropriate consent before providing services.

f) **Document consent** - Maintain records of informed consent.

### 4.3 Termination & Referral

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Refer appropriately** - Refer clients to other professionals when client needs exceed their scope or competence.

b) **Avoid abandonment** - Not abandon clients and ensure appropriate transition when ending services.

c) **Terminate ethically** - End professional relationships when they are no longer beneficial or client safety is at risk.

d) **Provide resources** - Offer referrals and resources when terminating services.

## Confidentiality

### 5.1 Protecting Client Information

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Maintain confidentiality** - Keep all client information confidential unless authorized to disclose.

b) **Secure records** - Store client records securely and protect electronic information.

c) **Limit disclosure** - Share only information necessary and only with appropriate parties.

d) **Obtain authorization** - Get written consent before sharing client information.

### 5.2 Limits of Confidentiality

ASI-certified professionals may disclose confidential information when:

a) **Client consent** - Client provides informed consent for disclosure.

b) **Legal requirement** - Disclosure is required by law (subpoena, court order).

c) **Imminent danger** - Disclosure is necessary to prevent serious and foreseeable harm to client or others.

d) **Abuse reporting** - Required by mandatory reporting laws for abuse or neglect.

e) **Consultation** - Consulting with other professionals, with appropriate precautions.

### 5.3 Electronic Communications

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Secure communications** - Use appropriate security measures for electronic communications.

b) **Inform clients** - Advise clients of risks associated with electronic communication.

c) **Protect data** - Implement appropriate data protection measures.

d) **Comply with laws** - Follow applicable privacy laws and regulations.

## Professional Boundaries

### 6.1 Maintaining Boundaries

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Establish clear boundaries** - Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with clients.

b) **Avoid dual relationships** - Avoid relationships that could impair professional judgment or exploit clients.

c) **Manage boundary crossings** - If boundary crossings occur, document and address appropriately.

d) **Seek consultation** - Consult with colleagues when boundary issues arise.

### 6.2 Prohibited Relationships

ASI-certified professionals shall NOT:

a) **Sexual relationships** - Engage in sexual or romantic relationships with current clients.

b) **Former clients** - Engage in sexual relationships with former clients for minimum of 2 years after termination, and only if not exploitative.

c) **Client family members** - Engage in sexual relationships with client family members or significant others.

d) **Financial exploitation** - Enter into financial arrangements that exploit clients.

### 6.3 Physical Contact

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Obtain consent** - Obtain consent before any physical contact.

b) **Maintain appropriateness** - Ensure any physical contact is professionally appropriate.

c) **Respect preferences** - Respect client preferences regarding physical contact.

d) **Document as needed** - Document when physical contact is part of services.

## Scope of Practice

### 7.1 Practicing Within Scope

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Know their scope** - Understand the scope of practice for their credential and specialty.

b) **Stay within bounds** - Not practice outside their defined scope.

c) **Refer when needed** - Refer to appropriate professionals for services outside their scope.

d) **Respect other professions** - Not disparage or undermine other licensed professionals.

### 7.2 Medical Scope Limitations

ASI-certified professionals shall NOT:

a) **Diagnose** - Diagnose medical conditions or diseases.

b) **Prescribe** - Prescribe medications, including controlled substances.

c) **Treat medical conditions** - Provide treatment for medical conditions.

d) **Order medical tests** - Order laboratory tests or imaging studies (unless within professional license).

e) **Replace medical care** - Substitute for or advise against appropriate medical care.

### 7.3 Appropriate Language

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Use appropriate terminology** - Use terms like "work with," "support," "guide" rather than "treat," "cure," "diagnose."

b) **Make appropriate recommendations** - Offer "suggestions" or "recommendations" rather than "prescriptions."

c) **Clarify role** - Ensure clients understand the distinction between wellness coaching and medical care.

## Business Practices

### 8.1 Ethical Business Conduct

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Operate honestly** - Conduct business affairs with honesty and integrity.

b) **Honor contracts** - Fulfill contractual obligations with clients and business partners.

c) **Maintain accurate records** - Keep accurate business and financial records.

d) **Pay obligations** - Meet financial obligations in a timely manner.

### 8.2 Fees & Financial Practices

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Disclose fees clearly** - Provide clear, upfront information about all fees.

b) **Avoid unreasonable fees** - Charge fees that are reasonable and commensurate with services.

c) **Document agreements** - Use written agreements specifying fees and services.

d) **Handle refunds fairly** - Maintain clear, fair refund policies.

e) **Avoid fee splitting** - Not engage in fee splitting except for legitimate referral relationships.

### 8.3 Advertising & Marketing

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Advertise honestly** - Ensure all advertising is truthful and not misleading.

b) **Avoid false claims** - Not make false or exaggerated claims about services or outcomes.

c) **Use testimonials appropriately** - Ensure testimonials are genuine and appropriately obtained.

d) **Represent credentials accurately** - Accurately represent credentials and qualifications.

e) **Avoid guarantees** - Not guarantee specific outcomes.

### 8.4 Products & Supplements

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Disclose financial interests** - Disclose any financial interest in products recommended.

b) **Base recommendations on need** - Recommend products based on client need, not personal profit.

c) **Provide alternatives** - Inform clients of alternatives to products sold.

d) **Avoid pressure** - Not pressure clients to purchase products.

## Professional Relationships

### 9.1 Relationships with Colleagues

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Treat colleagues respectfully** - Interact with colleagues with respect and professionalism.

b) **Collaborate appropriately** - Work collaboratively with other professionals when it serves client welfare.

c) **Avoid disparagement** - Not make false or malicious statements about colleagues.

d) **Report concerns** - Report ethical concerns about colleagues through appropriate channels.

### 9.2 Relationships with ASI

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Maintain requirements** - Fulfill all certification maintenance requirements.

b) **Respond promptly** - Respond promptly to communications from ASI.

c) **Cooperate with inquiries** - Cooperate fully with ethics investigations.

d) **Report changes** - Report changes in contact information, legal status, or other relevant information.

### 9.3 Supervision & Training

ASI-certified professionals who supervise or train others shall:

a) **Provide competent supervision** - Ensure adequate competence to supervise in the area.

b) **Maintain boundaries** - Maintain appropriate professional boundaries with supervisees.

c) **Evaluate fairly** - Provide honest, constructive feedback and evaluation.

d) **Model ethical behavior** - Demonstrate ethical behavior in all professional activities.

## Social Media & Public Statements

### 10.1 Social Media Conduct

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Maintain professionalism** - Present themselves professionally on social media.

b) **Protect confidentiality** - Never share client information on social media.

c) **Use accurate credentials** - Accurately represent credentials on social profiles.

d) **Separate personal/professional** - Consider separation of personal and professional social media presence.

### 10.2 Public Statements

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Base statements on evidence** - Ensure public statements are based on accurate information and evidence.

b) **Clarify opinion vs. fact** - Distinguish between personal opinion and established fact.

c) **Represent the profession well** - Recognize that public statements reflect on the profession.

d) **Avoid misleading information** - Not spread misleading health information.

## Reporting Obligations

### 11.1 Self-Reporting

ASI-certified professionals shall report to ASI:

a) **Criminal conviction** - Any felony or misdemeanor conviction related to professional practice.

b) **License action** - Any action against a professional license by a regulatory body.

c) **Malpractice claims** - Any malpractice claims or lawsuits.

d) **Ethics complaints** - Ethics complaints filed against them with other organizations.

### 11.2 Reporting Others

ASI-certified professionals shall:

a) **Report violations** - Report known violations of this Code by other ASI certificants.

b) **Report through proper channels** - Use ASI's formal reporting process.

c) **Report in good faith** - Make reports based on genuine belief of violation.

d) **Cooperate with investigations** - Cooperate with ethics investigations when requested.

### 11.3 Mandatory Reporting

ASI-certified professionals shall comply with all legal mandatory reporting requirements, including:

a) Child abuse or neglect
b) Elder abuse or neglect
c) Dependent adult abuse
d) Threats of harm to self or others

## Disciplinary Process

### 12.1 Grounds for Discipline

Grounds for disciplinary action include:

a) Violation of this Code of Ethics
b) Fraud in obtaining or maintaining certification
c) Incompetence or gross negligence
d) Impairment affecting practice
e) Criminal conviction related to practice
f) Violation of applicable laws or regulations
g) Failure to cooperate with ASI investigation
h) Failure to maintain certification requirements

### 12.2 Disciplinary Actions

Possible disciplinary actions include:

| Level | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Written warning |
| 2 | Required remedial education |
| 3 | Supervised practice |
| 4 | Probation |
| 5 | Suspension |
| 6 | Revocation |

### 12.3 Disciplinary Procedure

1. **Complaint Filed** - Written complaint submitted to ASI Ethics Committee
2. **Initial Review** - Committee determines if complaint warrants investigation
3. **Investigation** - If warranted, investigation conducted
4. **Notice** - Respondent notified of charges and evidence
5. **Response** - Respondent given opportunity to respond
6. **Hearing** - If needed, hearing conducted
7. **Decision** - Committee renders decision
8. **Appeal** - Respondent may appeal within 30 days
9. **Final Action** - Final determination made
