## Mission & Purpose

### Mission Statement

AccrediPro Standards Institute (ASI) is committed to advancing the health and wellness profession by establishing rigorous certification standards that protect the public and elevate professional practice.

### Purpose of Certification

ASI certification serves to:

1. **Protect the Public** - Ensure practitioners meet minimum competency standards
2. **Elevate the Profession** - Establish recognized professional credentials
3. **Guide Professional Development** - Provide clear pathways for career advancement
4. **Ensure Accountability** - Maintain ongoing competency through CE requirements
5. **Enable Recognition** - Provide verifiable credentials for employers and clients

### Core Values

- **Integrity** - Maintaining honest, transparent standards
- **Excellence** - Pursuing the highest quality in certification
- **Accessibility** - Making certification attainable for qualified candidates
- **Relevance** - Keeping standards current with industry needs
- **Accountability** - Holding certificants to ethical standards

## Credential Framework Overview

### Three-Tier System

ASI offers three levels of certification, each building upon the previous:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│   TIER 3: BOARD CERTIFIED (BC-™)                                │
│   ══════════════════════════════                                │
│   Advanced practitioners establishing practices                 │
│   150+ hours | Board Exam | 40 CE/2yr | $249 renewal            │
│                                                                 │
│   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────     │
│                                                                 │
│   TIER 2: CERTIFIED PROFESSIONAL (CP™)                          │
│   ════════════════════════════════════                          │
│   Professionals authorized for client practice                  │
│   75+ hours | Professional Exam | 20 CE/2yr | $149 renewal      │
│                                                                 │
│   ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────     │
│                                                                 │
│   TIER 1: FOUNDATION CERTIFICATE (FC™)                          │
│   ════════════════════════════════════                          │
│   Entry-level demonstrating foundational knowledge              │
│   25+ hours | Assessment | No renewal required                  │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Credential Naming Convention

All ASI credentials follow this format:

- **Foundation**: `[SPECIALTY]-FC™` (e.g., FM-FC™)
- **Professional**: `[SPECIALTY]-CP™` (e.g., FM-CP™)
- **Board Certified**: `BC-[SPECIALTY]™` (e.g., BC-FMP™)

## Foundation Certificate (FC™) Standards

### Purpose

The Foundation Certificate demonstrates that an individual has acquired foundational knowledge in a specialty area. This credential is ideal for:

- Career explorers assessing fit for the profession
- Professionals seeking foundational knowledge
- Support staff in wellness practices
- Those preparing for professional certification

### Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Training Hours** | Minimum 25 contact hours |
| **Curriculum** | 5 core modules |
| **Assessment** | Knowledge assessment |
| **Pass Score** | 70% (35/50 questions) |
| **Prerequisites** | None - open enrollment |
| **Time Limit** | Complete within 12 months of enrollment |
| **Renewal** | Not required (lifetime credential) |

### Curriculum Structure (25 Hours)

| Module | Title | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 1 | Introduction to [Specialty] | 5 |
| 2 | Foundational Principles | 5 |
| 3 | Core Concepts & Methodology | 5 |
| 4 | Practical Applications | 5 |
| 5 | Professional Pathway | 5 |
| **Total** | | **25** |

### Assessment Format

- **Type**: Online knowledge assessment
- **Questions**: 50 multiple choice
- **Time Limit**: 60 minutes (or untimed)
- **Pass Score**: 70% (35 correct)
- **Proctoring**: Honor code (not proctored)
- **Retakes**: Unlimited (48-hour wait between attempts)
- **Cost**: Included in program fee

### Credential Awarded

Upon successful completion:
- Digital credential badge
- PDF certificate
- Credential ID number
- Entry in ASI database
- Pathway to Professional certification

### Limitations

The Foundation Certificate:
- Does NOT authorize independent client practice
- Does NOT permit use of "Certified Professional" title
- Is NOT sufficient for insurance credentialing
- Is intended as a stepping stone to professional certification

## Certified Professional (CP™) Standards

### Purpose

The Certified Professional credential authorizes individuals to work with clients in their specialty area. This is the primary professional certification offered by ASI.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Training Hours** | Minimum 75 contact hours |
| **Curriculum** | 12 core modules + 3 electives |
| **Examination** | Proctored certification exam |
| **Pass Score** | 75% (75/100 questions) |
| **Practical** | 3 case study submissions |
| **Prerequisites** | Foundation Certificate OR equivalent experience |
| **Time Limit** | Complete within 18 months of enrollment |
| **Renewal** | 20 CE credits every 2 years + $149 fee |

### Prerequisite Options

Candidates must meet ONE of the following:
1. Hold the corresponding Foundation Certificate (FC™)
2. Hold a related degree (Bachelor's or higher in health/wellness field)
3. Document 2+ years professional experience in the field
4. Complete equivalency assessment

### Curriculum Structure (75 Hours)

**Part 1: Advanced Knowledge (25 hours)**
| Module | Title | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 6 | Advanced [Topic 1] | 5 |
| 7 | Advanced [Topic 2] | 5 |
| 8 | Advanced [Topic 3] | 5 |
| 9 | Advanced [Topic 4] | 5 |
| 10 | Advanced [Topic 5] | 5 |

**Part 2: Clinical Skills (25 hours)**
| Module | Title | Hours |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 11 | Client Assessment | 5 |
| 12 | Protocol Development | 5 |
| 13 | Coaching & Behavior Change | 5 |
| 14 | Special Populations | 5 |
| 15 | Professional Practice | 5 |

**Part 3: Electives (15 hours)**
Choose 3 from available specialty electives (5 hours each)

**Part 4: Practical Requirements (10 hours)**
- Case Study 1: Initial Assessment
- Case Study 2: Protocol Development
- Case Study 3: Progress & Adaptation

### Examination Format

- **Type**: Online proctored examination
- **Questions**: 100 multiple choice
- **Time Limit**: 2 hours
- **Pass Score**: 75% (75 correct)
- **Proctoring**: Required (online proctoring service)
- **Retakes**: 2 included, then $99 per additional attempt
- **Scheduling**: On-demand within testing windows

### Examination Content Domains

| Domain | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Foundational Knowledge | 20% |
| Assessment & Evaluation | 25% |
| Protocol Development | 25% |
| Coaching & Communication | 15% |
| Ethics & Professional Practice | 15% |

### Case Study Requirements

Each case study must include:
1. Client background and health history
2. Assessment findings
3. Root cause analysis
4. Protocol recommendations
5. Expected outcomes
6. Follow-up plan

Case studies are reviewed and must receive "Satisfactory" rating.

### Credential Awarded

Upon successful completion:
- Professional credential designation
- Digital credential badge
- Framed certificate (optional)
- Credential ID number
- Listing in ASI Professional Directory
- Authorization for client practice
- Use of credential letters (e.g., "Jane Smith, FM-CP™")

### Scope of Practice

Certified Professionals are authorized to:
- Conduct client assessments
- Develop wellness protocols
- Provide coaching and guidance
- Make lifestyle recommendations
- Work within specialty scope

Certified Professionals may NOT:
- Diagnose medical conditions
- Prescribe medications
- Replace licensed medical care
- Practice outside specialty scope

## Board Certified (BC-™) Standards

### Purpose

Board Certification represents the highest level of ASI credential, designed for practitioners establishing independent practices and demonstrating mastery in their specialty.

### Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Training Hours** | Minimum 150 contact hours |
| **Curriculum** | 20 core modules + 5 electives + practicum |
| **Examination** | Comprehensive board examination |
| **Pass Score** | 80% (120/150 questions) |
| **Practical** | 10 supervised client cases + portfolio |
| **Prerequisites** | Certified Professional (CP™) credential |
| **Time Limit** | Complete within 24 months of enrollment |
| **Renewal** | 40 CE credits every 2 years + $249 fee |

### Prerequisites

Candidates MUST:
1. Hold active CP™ credential in the specialty
2. Be in good standing with ASI
3. Complete application and be approved

### Curriculum Structure (150 Hours)

**Part 1: Advanced Clinical Mastery (50 hours)**
10 advanced modules (5 hours each)

**Part 2: Practice Building (25 hours)**
5 business modules (5 hours each)
- Building Your Practice
- Marketing Your Practice
- Client Acquisition
- Scaling Your Practice
- Leadership & Legacy

**Part 3: Electives (25 hours)**
Choose 5 specialty electives (5 hours each)

**Part 4: Practicum (50 hours)**
- 10 supervised client cases (5 hours each minimum)
- Group mentorship calls (12 hours)
- Individual mentor sessions (3 hours)
- Peer practice sessions (5 hours)
- Portfolio development

### Examination Format

- **Type**: Comprehensive board examination
- **Questions**: 150 multiple choice
- **Time Limit**: 3 hours
- **Pass Score**: 80% (120 correct)
- **Proctoring**: Required
- **Oral Component**: 30-minute defense (optional by specialty)
- **Retakes**: 1 included, then $199 per additional attempt

### Supervised Practice Requirements

Each of 10 cases must include:
1. Initial consultation and assessment
2. Complete protocol development
3. Minimum 90-day engagement
4. Progress documentation
5. Outcome measurement
6. Supervisor sign-off

### Portfolio Requirements

Final portfolio must include:
1. 10 case study summaries
2. Personal practice philosophy statement
3. Marketing materials sample
4. Practice launch plan
5. Professional development plan

### Credential Awarded

Upon successful completion:
- Board Certified designation
- Premium digital credential badge
- Framed certificate with seal
- Credential ID number
- Featured listing in ASI Directory
- Use of "Board Certified" title
- Speaking/teaching opportunities
- Mentorship eligibility

## Examination Standards

### Examination Development

All ASI examinations are developed following psychometric standards:

1. **Job Task Analysis** - Based on actual practitioner competencies
2. **Item Development** - Questions written by subject matter experts
3. **Item Review** - Reviewed for accuracy, bias, and clarity
4. **Statistical Analysis** - Items analyzed for performance
5. **Standard Setting** - Pass scores set using modified Angoff method
6. **Regular Updates** - Examinations updated annually

### Examination Security

- Unique exam forms for each candidate
- Question pools (candidates see subset)
- Online proctoring with ID verification
- Recorded sessions
- AI-powered monitoring
- Incident investigation process

### Testing Accommodations

ASI provides accommodations for candidates with documented disabilities:
- Extended time (50% additional)
- Separate testing room
- Screen readers
- Other accommodations as appropriate

Request accommodations at least 14 days before testing.

### Examination Results

- Foundation: Immediate results
- Professional: Results within 5 business days
- Board: Results within 10 business days

### Appeals Process

Candidates may appeal examination results within 30 days:
1. Submit written appeal with rationale
2. $50 appeal fee (refunded if successful)
3. Review by Examination Committee
4. Decision within 15 business days

## Continuing Education Requirements

### Purpose

Continuing Education (CE) ensures certificants maintain current knowledge and skills.

### Requirements by Credential

| Credential | CE Credits | Cycle |
|------------|------------|-------|
| Foundation (FC™) | None | N/A |
| Professional (CP™) | 20 credits | 2 years |
| Board Certified (BC-™) | 40 credits | 2 years |

### Approved CE Activities

| Activity | Credits | Maximum |
|----------|---------|---------|
| ASI-approved courses (per hour) | 1 | Unlimited |
| ASI Annual Conference | 10 | 10/cycle |
| College/university courses | 15/course | 30/cycle |
| Publishing article/research | 5 | 10/cycle |
| Presenting at approved event | 5 | 10/cycle |
| Peer supervision (per hour) | 0.5 | 5/cycle |
| Self-study with assessment | 0.5/hour | 10/cycle |
| Specialty certification add-on | 10 | 20/cycle |

### CE Reporting

- Track CE in your ASI portal
- Upload certificates/documentation
- CE audits conducted randomly (10% of certificants)
- Maintain records for 4 years

### Non-Compliance

Failure to complete CE by renewal date:
1. 30-day grace period
2. Credential placed on "Inactive" status
3. Must complete CE before reactivation
4. $50 late fee applies

## Renewal & Maintenance

### Renewal Requirements

| Credential | Fee | CE Required | Cycle |
|------------|-----|-------------|-------|
| FC™ | None | None | Lifetime |
| CP™ | $149 | 20 credits | 2 years |
| BC-™ | $249 | 40 credits | 2 years |

### Renewal Process

1. Receive reminder 90 days before expiration
2. Complete CE requirements
3. Pay renewal fee
4. Attest to continued adherence to Code of Ethics
5. Credential renewed for 2 years

### Lapsed Credentials

If credential lapses:
- **Within 1 year**: Reinstate with fee + late fee + CE completion
- **1-3 years**: Reinstatement exam required
- **Over 3 years**: Must recertify (retake full exam)

### Voluntary Resignation

Certificants may voluntarily resign their credential:
- Submit written request
- Credential marked as "Resigned - Good Standing"
- May reinstate within 3 years with CE + fees

### Credential Revocation

Credentials may be revoked for:
- Ethics violations
- Fraud in obtaining certification
- Failure to maintain requirements
- Criminal conviction related to practice
- Gross negligence or malpractice

See Code of Ethics for disciplinary procedures.
