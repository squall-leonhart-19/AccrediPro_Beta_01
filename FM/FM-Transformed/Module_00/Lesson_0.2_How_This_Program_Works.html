At what point in the program can students typically begin accepting paid clients?

Answer: After completing Phase 2 (Modules 0-8), many students begin accepting paid clients at $75-150 per session. What's the difference between the FM-FC‚Ñ¢ and FM-CP‚Ñ¢ credentials?

Answer: The FM-FC‚Ñ¢ (Functional Medicine Foundations Certified) is awarded after completing Phases 1-2 (Modules 0-8) and allows you to legally practice as a health coach. The FM-CP‚Ñ¢ (Functional Medicine Certified Practitioner) is the full certification earned after completing all 21 modules plus a comprehensive final exam. The FM-CP‚Ñ¢ commands higher rates ($150-250/session) and represents mastery of specialized clinical knowledge, lab literacy, and business-building systems.

3

Why is Phase 3 described as providing "niching opportunities"?

Answer: Phase 3 contains nine specialized clinical modules (women's hormones, perimenopause/menopause, thyroid, metabolic health, autoimmunity, mental health, cardiometabolic health, energy/mitochondria, and detoxification). These specializations allow coaches to develop deep expertise in specific health areas, which justifies premium pricing. Clients will pay more for specialized knowledge‚Äîfor example, a coach specializing in perimenopause and thyroid health can charge significantly higher rates than a generalist. Each module also earns a Specialization Certificate for marketing purposes.

4

What study schedule do most successful students follow, and why?

Answer: Most successful students complete the program in 4-6 months by studying 5-7 hours per week (typically spread across 2-3 study sessions). This timeline is optimal because it maintains learning momentum, improves information retention through consistent exposure, and allows students to start earning income sooner. Daily small progress beats occasional marathon study sessions. The key is consistency‚Äîprotecting study time like it's already income-generating time‚Äîbecause building this habit now establishes the discipline needed for a successful coaching practice.
