Why Packages Work Better Than Sessions

Selling coaching by the session seems straightforward and flexible, but it actually undermines both client results and practice sustainability. Understanding why packages work better is the foundation for structuring your offerings effectively.

Session-by-Session Model
  • Client decides each time whether to continue
  • Inconsistent scheduling disrupts momentum
  • Each session feels like a transaction
  • Unpredictable income for coach
  • Limited outcomes‚Äîclients quit before results
Package-Based Model
  • Commitment made upfront‚Äîclient stays through challenges
  • Regular cadence builds habits and accountability
  • Relationship-based container for transformation
  • Stable, predictable revenue
  • Clients stay long enough for meaningful change
The Transformation Reality

Real health transformation takes time‚Äîtypically 3-6 months minimum for meaningful, sustainable change. Packages create the container for this transformation. When clients can drop out after one session that felt hard, they never experience the breakthrough that comes from persisting through difficulty.

Coach Tip

When transitioning from sessions to packages, frame it as "I've found that my clients get the best results when we work together for at least three months. This gives us time to assess, implement, adjust, and really see sustainable change happen." Position packages as in service of their success, not your business model.

Designing Effective Coaching Packages

The most effective coaching packages balance comprehensive support with practical sustainability. Let's break down the key components and explore sample structures that work across different practice models.

Essential Package Components

1

Duration

3-Month Package: Good starting point. Allows time for assessment, implementation, and initial results. Popular for focused concerns.

6-Month Package: Better for complex cases, multiple issues, or deeper transformation. Allows for complete protocol implementation and adjustment.

12-Month Package: For comprehensive transformation or ongoing support. Often priced at a discount. Common for VIP offerings.

2

Session Frequency

Weekly 60-min: High-touch, intensive support. Good for complex situations or clients needing more accountability.

Biweekly 60-min: Most common and effective. Allows time for implementation between sessions.

Monthly 90-min: Less intensive, good for maintenance phase or experienced clients.

3

Additional Support

‚Ä¢ Email/message support: Between-session questions and check-ins
‚Ä¢ Resource library: Handouts, guides, recipes, trackers
‚Ä¢ Emergency support: Brief calls for urgent needs
‚Ä¢ Lab review: If within scope and training
‚Ä¢ Meal planning: If you offer nutrition guidance

Coach Tip

Offer 2-3 package options at different investment levels. This serves different client needs and budgets while giving you flexibility. Many coaches find that offering a middle-tier option increases overall revenue‚Äîclients who might have chosen the lowest tier often "upgrade" when they see a middle option that offers more value.

Pricing Your Services

Pricing triggers more anxiety than almost any other business decision. Let's address it head-on with practical frameworks that honor both your value and your clients' investment.

Pricing Approaches

Value-Based Pricing

Price based on the value of the transformation, not your time. Ask: What is solving this problem worth to the client?

  • Energy and quality of life: Priceless to someone who's been exhausted for years
  • Reduced healthcare costs: Thousands per year in medications, specialists, ER visits
  • Improved work performance: Career advancement and income impact
  • Better relationships: Can't put a price on restored connection with family
  • Prevention of disease: Decades of life quality and independence
Sustainability-Based Pricing

Calculate what you need to charge to sustain yourself and your practice:

  • Annual income goal: What do you need to earn?
  • Business expenses: Technology, insurance, continuing education, marketing, office space
  • Working weeks/year: Account for vacation, sick time, continuing education
  • Client capacity: How many clients can you effectively serve at once?
  • Calculate: (Income Goal + Business Expenses) √∑ (Number of Clients √ó Packages per Year) = Minimum package price

Investment Creates Commitment

Clients who invest meaningfully show up, do the work, and get results. Free or cheap coaching often produces free or cheap results. The investment is part of the transformation.

Presenting Your Pricing

How you present pricing matters as much as the number itself. Confident, clear communication about your packages and investment creates trust and helps ideal clients say yes.

Pricing Presentation Best Practices
  • Lead with transformation: Describe what they'll achieve before mentioning price
  • Anchor to value: "For the price of daily coffee, you get your health back"
  • State price confidently: No apologizing, minimizing, or over-explaining
  • Offer payment plans: Make investment accessible without discounting
  • Give space: State the price, then pause. Let them respond.
  • Have written materials: Follow up verbal conversations with proposal/email

Handling Price Objections

Common Objections & Effective Responses

üí¨ "I can't afford it."

Explore first: "I understand investment is a consideration. Can you share more about what's holding you back?"

Often there are other factors beneath the surface‚Äîfear of failure, uncertainty about results, other priorities. If truly financial, discuss payment plans or whether the timing is right. Never discount‚Äîit devalues your work and attracts clients who won't fully commit.

ü§î "I need to think about it."

Explore deeper: "Of course‚Äîthis is a significant decision. What specifically would you like to think through? Sometimes talking it out helps."

Often they have an unstated concern you can address‚Äîresults uncertainty, time commitment, spouse buy-in. Getting it into the open helps you address the real objection.

üòÆ "It costs more than I expected."

Explore expectations: "What were you expecting?"

This opens conversation about what they're comparing to, what value they see, and whether you've communicated the transformation effectively. It also helps you understand if they're an ideal fit.

Coach Tip

Practice stating your prices out loud until you can say them without flinching, apologizing, or rushing. Try this exercise: Stand in front of a mirror and say, "The investment for the 6-month Transformation Package is $4,500." Keep saying it until it feels natural. Your confidence (or lack of it) about your pricing is contagious.

Payment Options

Offering flexible payment options makes your services accessible to more ideal clients without discounting. Here's how to structure payment terms that serve both you and your clients.

Pay in Full

Pros:

  • Maximum client commitment
  • Cash flow upfront
  • Can offer small discount (5-10%)
  • No payment chasing

Cons:

  • Higher barrier for some clients
  • Fewer clients may choose this option
Monthly Payments

Pros:

  • More accessible to clients
  • Recurring revenue stream
  • Predictable cash flow

Cons:

  • Payment chasing if cards fail
  • Lower perceived commitment
  • Need automated billing system
Critical Distinction

Payment Plans ‚â† Discounts. Offer the same total price with payment options‚Äîdon't discount for payment plans. If you offer a pay-in-full discount (typically 5-10%), the payment plan price is your actual price, and pay-in-full is the discounted option.

Example: 6-month package = $4,500 in 6 monthly payments OR $4,200 pay-in-full (7% discount). The $4,500 is your standard price.

Why do package-based coaching models generally produce better client outcomes than session-by-session models?

Answer: Package-based models create commitment and consistency that allows real transformation. Clients commit upfront and stay through challenges rather than dropping out when things get difficult. The regular cadence builds habits and accountability. Real health transformation typically takes 3-6 months minimum‚Äîpackages create the container for this timeline. What are the three main approaches to pricing coaching services, and what does each prioritize?

Answer: The three main approaches are:

  • Value-Based Pricing: Prioritizes the worth of the transformation to the client‚Äîwhat is solving their problem actually worth in terms of health, quality of life, and long-term wellbeing?
  • Sustainability-Based Pricing: Prioritizes what you need to charge to sustain yourself and your practice‚Äîcalculated from income goals, business expenses, and realistic client capacity.
  • Market-Based Pricing: Prioritizes understanding the competitive landscape and positioning yourself appropriately within market ranges for your niche, experience level, and location.
3

A potential client says, "I need to think about it." What's an effective coaching response, and why?

Answer: An effective response is: "Of course‚Äîthis is a significant decision. What specifically would you like to think through? Sometimes talking it out helps."

Why this works: This response validates their need while gently exploring what's really behind the hesitation. Often "I need to think about it" masks a specific unstated concern‚Äîuncertainty about results, time commitment concerns, needing spouse buy-in, or financial questions. By getting the real concern into the open, you can address it directly rather than letting the potential client leave with unresolved doubts.

What's the difference between offering payment plans and discounting, and why does it matter?

Answer: Payment plans divide the same total investment into multiple payments‚Äîthey don't reduce the price. Discounting reduces the total amount paid. This matters because:

  • Payment plans make your services accessible without devaluing your work
  • Discounting attracts less committed clients and trains them to expect lower prices
  • If you offer a pay-in-full discount (5-10%), the payment plan price is your standard price‚Äîpay-in-full is the discounted option
  • Payment plans maintain the value of your services while honoring different financial situations
