The Marketing Mindset

Before tactics, address the beliefs that block effective marketing:

Reframing Marketing

  • Marketing = Reaching people who need help: You can't serve people who don't know you exist
  • Visibility = Service: Hiding doesn't help anyoneâ€”your knowledge benefits people
  • Content = Public coaching: Share freely what you would tell clients
  • Promotion = Invitation: Inviting people to solutions isn't pushyâ€”it's generous
  • Consistency = Reliability: Showing up regularly builds trust

Understanding the Client Journey

People don't go from stranger to paying client instantly. Understanding the journey helps you create appropriate content for each stage:

Stage Client's Mindset What They Need
Awareness "I have a problem but don't know solutions exist" Educational content that names their problem and hints at solutions
Interest "I'm curious about this approach" Deeper content, free resources, proof of expertise
Consideration "Is this the right solution for me?" Case studies, testimonials, connection with you
Decision "I'm ready to take action" Clear offer, consultation, easy next step
Action "I'm enrolling in coaching" Simple enrollment process, welcome experience

Choosing Your Marketing Channels

You don't need to be everywhere. Choose 2-3 channels where your ideal clients hang out and where you can show up consistently.

Core Channel Options

Email Marketing

Why it works: You own your list (unlike social media), direct communication, high conversion rates, builds relationship over time

Best for: All coachesâ€”this should be foundational

Strategy: Offer valuable free resource (lead magnet) to build list; send regular newsletters with helpful content; occasional invitations to work together

Instagram

Why it works: Visual platform, personal connection, large health-interested audience, reels and stories for reach

Best for: Coaches comfortable on camera, niches where visual content works (food, lifestyle)

Strategy: Mix of educational posts, personal stories, client wins, behind-the-scenes; engage authentically with followers

Facebook

Why it works: Groups create community, older demographic than Instagram, good for local connections

Best for: Coaches targeting 40+, local practice, community building

Strategy: Join and contribute to groups where ideal clients gather; consider starting your own community; use personal profile authentically

LinkedIn

Why it works: Professional audience, B2B opportunities, thought leadership

Best for: Corporate wellness, executive coaching, professional niches

Strategy: Thought leadership posts, professional articles, networking with complementary professionals

In-Person/Local

Why it works: High trust, personal connection, less competition, referral-friendly

Best for: Local practice, community-oriented coaches, those uncomfortable with social media

Strategy: Workshops at local venues, partnerships with gyms/studios/clinics, networking groups, community events

Content That Attracts

Effective content educates, connects, and positions you as the solution:

Content Categories

The Content Mix

Educational (50-60%):

Teach what you know. Tips, explanations, myth-busting, how-tos, research simplified. "Here's why you're not sleeping well and what to do about it."

Connection/Story (20-30%):

Your journey, behind-the-scenes, personal experiences, values. "Why I became a health coach after my own health crisis."

Social Proof (10-15%):

Client testimonials, transformations, results. "Sarah came to me exhausted and skeptical. Six months later..."

Invitation (5-10%):

Direct invitations to work with you. "I have two spots opening in my spring program. Here's how to apply."

Content Creation Tips

Speak to One Person

Write/speak as if addressing your ideal client directly. "You" is more powerful than "people." "Are you exhausted but your doctor says you're fine?" creates more connection than "Many people experience unexplained fatigue."

Answer Real Questions

What do clients ask you? What do people in your niche search for? Create content that answers actual questions. Keep a running list of questions and concerns you hear.

Give Your Best Stuff Free

Don't hold back. Share valuable information generously. People who can implement on their own will; those who need support will hire you. Your best content builds trust and demonstrates expertise.

Building Your Lead Magnet

A lead magnet is a free valuable resource offered in exchange for email signup. It moves strangers into your world.

Effective Lead Magnet Types

Guides and Checklists

"The Perimenopause Survival Guide: 7 Steps to Take Today"

"Gut Health Starter Checklist: 30 Days to Better Digestion"

Quizzes and Assessments

"What's Your Hormone Type? Take the Quiz"

"Discover Your Energy Depletion Pattern"

Mini-Courses or Challenges

"5-Day Reset Your Sleep Challenge"

"7-Day Anti-Inflammatory Eating Kickstart"

Webinars and Trainings

"Free Masterclass: The 3 Hidden Causes of Midlife Weight Gain"

"Workshop: Understanding Your Thyroid Results"

The Sustainable Marketing System

Sporadic marketing doesn't work. Consistent, sustainable systems do:

Simple Weekly Marketing System

  • Weekly Email: One valuable email to your list (30-60 min to write)
  • Social Posts: 3-5 posts per week on primary platform (can batch-create)
  • Engagement: 15 min/day engaging with others' content
  • Connection: 2-3 personal outreach messages to referral sources or potential collaborators
  • Total Time: 3-5 hours per week

Batch Content Creation

Monthly Content Day

Dedicate one day per month to creating content in batches:

  • Write 4 weekly emails
  • Create 15-20 social media posts
  • Plan any live content (workshops, webinars)
  • Update lead magnet or create new one quarterly

Marketing Mistakes to Avoid

Inconsistency

Posting intensely for two weeks, then disappearing for a month. Consistency beats intensity. Better to post three times a week forever than daily for a month then nothing.

Being Everywhere

Trying to maintain presence on every platform. Choose 2-3 and do them well. You can always expand later.

All Education, No Invitation

Giving endlessly without ever inviting people to work with you. It's not pushy to tell people how to take the next stepâ€”it's helpful.

Comparing Yourself

Looking at coaches with 50,000 followers and feeling inadequate. You need clients, not followers. Focus on connection with your audience, not size.
