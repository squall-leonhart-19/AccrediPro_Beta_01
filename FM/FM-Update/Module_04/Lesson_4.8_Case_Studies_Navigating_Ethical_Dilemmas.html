Case Study 1: The Supplement Suggestion

Your client asks if she should take B12 supplements for her fatigue. You know B12 deficiency is common and supplementation is generally safe. However, recommending specific supplements for symptom treatment crosses into territory that requires clinical assessment.

Case Study 2: The Cry for Help

A client discloses suicidal ideation. This is clearly outside health coaching scope and requires professional mental health assessment. You must respond with compassion while ensuring the client gets appropriate help.

Case Study 3: The Skeptical Spouse

A client's husband calls demanding information about your sessions. Even though he pays for them, you cannot share information without explicit written consent from the client.

Case Study 4: The Friend Referral

A close friend's neighbor wants to become your client. This creates a dual relationship that could complicate boundaries. Professional practice requires proper intake processes.

Case Study 5: The Urgent Text

A client texts late at night about severe abdominal pain. Assessing symptoms and advising on emergency care is not within your scope. Direct them to seek immediate medical attention.

The PAUSE Framework for Ethical Decisions

When facing uncertain situations, use this framework:

  • Pause: Don't react immediately. Recognize you're facing an ethical decision.
  • Analyze: Identify competing values and considerations.
  • Understand: Consider different perspectives and professional guidance.
  • Select: Choose a course of action that prioritizes client welfare.
  • Evaluate: Reflect on the outcome to inform future decisions.
