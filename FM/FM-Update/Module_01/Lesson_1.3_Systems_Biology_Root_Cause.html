The Seven Core Physiological Systems

Functional medicine organizes the body into seven interconnected core systems. Understanding these helps you see patterns across seemingly unrelated symptoms:

1. Assimilation

Digestion, absorption, microbiome, gut barrier function. The foundationâ€”if you can't absorb nutrients, nothing else works optimally.

Related: bloating, reflux, food sensitivities, nutrient deficiencies

2. Defense & Repair

Immune function, inflammation, infection response. Balancing protection without excess inflammation or autoimmunity.

Related: frequent illness, allergies, autoimmune conditions, chronic inflammation

3. Energy

Mitochondrial function, energy production, oxidative stress. The cellular engines that power everything.

Related: fatigue, brain fog, muscle weakness, exercise intolerance

4. Biotransformation & Elimination

Detoxification pathways, liver function, toxin elimination. Processing and removing what doesn't belong.

Related: chemical sensitivities, hormone imbalances, skin issues

5. Transport

Cardiovascular and lymphatic circulation. Delivering nutrients, oxygen, and immune cells; removing waste.

Related: blood pressure, cholesterol, circulation problems

6. Communication

Hormones, neurotransmitters, immune messengers. The signaling systems that coordinate everything.

Related: thyroid issues, mood disorders, menstrual problems, stress response

7. Structural Integrity

Musculoskeletal system, cell membranes, from subcellular to whole-body structure.

Related: joint pain, muscle issues, cell membrane function
