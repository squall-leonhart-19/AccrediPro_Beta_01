Why History Matters

Chronic disease doesn't appear out of nowhere. It develops over months, years, or decades through a series of events, exposures, and shifts that gradually move the body toward dysfunction.

A conventional medical history focuses on:

  • Current symptoms
  • Family history of disease
  • Current medications
  • Allergies

A functional medicine timeline adds crucial dimensions:

  • When did symptoms first appear? What preceded them?
  • What exposures has this person had? (Infections, toxins, traumas)
  • What major life events correlate with health changes?
  • What has helped or worsened symptoms over time?

Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators

The ATM framework helps organize timeline information into three categories:

The ATM Framework

Antecedents

Factors that predispose someone to illness. These exist before symptoms appear and create vulnerability.

Examples: Genetics, early life events, prenatal exposures, family environment

Triggers

Events that initiate symptoms or disease. The "match that lights the fire" in a predisposed individual.

Examples: Infection, trauma, toxic exposure, major stress, surgery

Mediators

Factors that perpetuate the condition, keeping the dysfunction going even after the trigger is gone.

Examples: Ongoing inflammation, poor diet, chronic stress, gut dysfunction

Understanding ATM helps identify what to address:

  • Antecedents can't be changed, but awareness helps manage risk
  • Triggers may need specific treatment or removal
  • Mediators are often the key intervention pointâ€”address these to break the cycle

Coach's Insight

As a health coach, you won't diagnose. But you can help clients identify potential mediators they can address: sleep quality, stress management, dietary patterns, movement, relationships. These lifestyle factors often perpetuate dysfunctionâ€”and are within your scope to support.

Connecting the Dots

The power of the timeline emerges when you see patterns across time:

  • "You had mono in college, then your fatigue started a year later..."
  • "The antibiotics for your sinus infection came right before your digestive issues began..."
  • "Your symptoms started within months of moving into that house with water damage..."
  • "The divorce was 18 months before your autoimmune diagnosis..."

These connections don't prove causation, but they generate hypotheses worth exploring with the healthcare team.
