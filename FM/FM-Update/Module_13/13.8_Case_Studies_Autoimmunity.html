Case Study 1: Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

Client Profile: Michelle, 42

Diagnosis: Hashimoto's thyroiditis, diagnosed 3 years ago

Medications: Levothyroxine 75mcg daily

Chief concerns: Persistent fatigue despite "normal" TSH, weight gain (20 lbs in 2 years), brain fog, hair loss, constipation

Recent labs: TSH 2.8 (normal range), TPO antibodies 342 IU/mL (elevated), vitamin D 24 ng/mL (low), ferritin 18 ng/mL (low)

Coaching Discovery Process

Michelle's coach explored her history and lifestyle, discovering several relevant factors:

  • High-stress corporate job with frequent travel
  • Chronic digestive issues (bloating, gas, irregular bowels) for 10+ years
  • Diet heavy in processed foods and gluten-containing grains
  • Poor sleep (5-6 hours, often interrupted)
  • History of yo-yo dieting
  • Symptoms worsened significantly after a stressful divorce 4 years ago

Coaching Approach

Phase 1: Foundation Building (Weeks 1-4)

  • Worked with functional medicine provider to optimize thyroid medication (added T3) and address vitamin D and iron deficiencies
  • Initiated 30-day gluten elimination trial with anti-inflammatory Mediterranean-style eating
  • Established consistent sleep schedule with 10pm bedtime goal
  • Introduced morning sunlight exposure and evening wind-down routine
  • Started daily 10-minute stress reduction practice (breathing exercises)

Phase 2: Gut Focus (Weeks 5-12)

  • Comprehensive stool testing revealed low beneficial bacteria and markers of intestinal inflammation
  • Implemented 5R protocol with practitioner guidance: removed remaining trigger foods, added digestive enzymes, introduced targeted probiotics
  • Emphasized gut-healing foods: bone broth, fermented vegetables, omega-3 rich fish
  • Supplementation: L-glutamine, zinc carnosine, omega-3s, selenium (per practitioner)

Phase 3: Optimization (Months 4-6)

  • Reintroduced some eliminated foods systematically; identified dairy as a trigger
  • Established sustainable eating pattern (gluten-free, dairy-free, anti-inflammatory)
  • Developed stress management toolkit including yoga and nature walks
  • Created strategies for maintaining habits during work travel

Outcomes at 6 Months

  • TPO antibodies decreased from 342 to 156 IU/mL (54% reduction)
  • Energy improved from 3/10 to 7/10
  • Lost 12 pounds without restrictive dieting
  • Brain fog largely resolved
  • Digestive symptoms 80% improved
  • Hair loss stopped; new growth visible
  • Sleeping 7-8 hours consistently

Coaching Insight: Michelle's case illustrates how addressing root causes (gut health, nutrient deficiencies, stress, sleep) can significantly improve autoimmune markers and symptomsâ€”even when thyroid medication is already in place. The coach focused on sustainable lifestyle changes while working collaboratively with her functional medicine provider on testing and supplementation.

Case Study 2: Rheumatoid Arthritis

Client Profile: Patricia, 56

Diagnosis: Rheumatoid arthritis, diagnosed 5 years ago

Medications: Methotrexate weekly, folic acid, occasional prednisone during flares

Chief concerns: Morning stiffness (45+ minutes daily), joint pain in hands and feet, fatigue, concern about long-term medication use

Recent labs: CRP 8.2 mg/L (elevated), ESR 38 mm/hr (elevated), vitamin D 31 ng/mL (suboptimal), RF positive

Coaching Discovery Process

Patricia's coach uncovered these contributing factors:

  • Standard American diet with daily sugar intake, processed foods, refined carbohydrates
  • Sedentary lifestyle due to pain (afraid to exercise)
  • Poor relationship with her body after RA diagnosis ("my body is attacking itself")
  • History of chronic sinus infections treated with multiple antibiotic courses
  • High-stress caregiver role for elderly mother
  • Using wine nightly to relax (2-3 glasses)

Coaching Approach

Phase 1: Nutrition Transition (Weeks 1-6)

  • Gradual transition to Mediterranean anti-inflammatory diet (started with additions before subtractions)
  • Week 1-2: Added fatty fish 3x/week, 6+ servings vegetables daily
  • Week 3-4: Replaced industrial seed oils with olive oil; reduced sugar by 50%
  • Week 5-6: Eliminated processed foods; reduced alcohol to 1 glass, 2 nights/week
  • Started omega-3 supplementation (3g/day) and vitamin D optimization per practitioner

Phase 2: Movement and Stress (Weeks 7-12)

  • Introduced gentle movement: warm water pool exercise 2x/week, gentle yoga 1x/week
  • Addressed fear around exercise; reframed movement as medicine for joints
  • Developed stress management plan: daily breathing exercises, weekly nature walks
  • Explored caregiver stress; connected Patricia with local support resources
  • Replaced wine habit with herbal tea ritual and evening stretching

Phase 3: Refinement and Expansion (Months 4-8)

  • Completed 30-day elimination of gluten and dairy; dairy identified as trigger (increased stiffness)
  • Added curcumin supplementation (with rheumatologist approval)
  • Progressed to gentle strength training with physical therapist guidance
  • Worked on body image and relationship with diagnosis through journaling and coaching conversations

Outcomes at 8 Months

  • Morning stiffness reduced from 45+ minutes to 10-15 minutes
  • CRP decreased from 8.2 to 3.1 mg/L
  • Joint pain reduced by 60% (patient rating)
  • Energy improved; afternoon fatigue largely resolved
  • Rheumatologist reduced methotrexate dose by 25%
  • No prednisone use needed in past 5 months
  • Exercising regularly without increased pain
  • Improved relationship with body and sense of agency

Coaching Insight: Patricia's transformation demonstrates the power of comprehensive lifestyle intervention for inflammatory autoimmune conditions. The gradual approach honored her readiness for change. Addressing alcohol, stress, and movement fear alongside diet created synergistic effects. The coach maintained close communication with her rheumatologist, ensuring complementary rather than conflicting approaches.

Case Study 3: Multiple Autoimmune Conditions

Client Profile: Angela, 49

Diagnoses: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (8 years), psoriasis (5 years), newly diagnosed celiac disease

Medications: Levothyroxine 100mcg, topical steroids for psoriasis flares

Chief concerns: Overwhelming fatigue, widespread skin plaques despite topical treatment, digestive distress, anxiety about having multiple conditions, feeling "broken"

Recent labs: Multiple nutrient deficiencies (B12, iron, vitamin D, zinc), elevated tissue transglutaminase antibodies

Coaching Discovery Process

Angela's case revealed the "multiple autoimmune syndrome"â€”when one autoimmune condition increases risk for others. Her history showed:

  • Lifelong digestive issues, now explained by undiagnosed celiac disease
  • Chronic nutrient malabsorption affecting thyroid, skin, and energy
  • Family history of autoimmunity (mother with lupus, sister with RA)
  • High-stress nursing career with night shifts disrupting circadian rhythm
  • Emotional eating patterns, especially during psoriasis flares
  • Perfectionist tendencies leading to "all-or-nothing" approaches

Coaching Approach

Phase 1: Celiac Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

  • Prioritized strict gluten elimination as medical necessity (not optional)
  • Extensive education on hidden gluten sources and cross-contamination
  • Developed meal planning and preparation skills for safe gluten-free cooking
  • Addressed emotional aspects of dietary restriction
  • Practitioner managed nutrient repletion (B12 injections, iron, vitamin D, zinc)

Phase 2: Expanding Anti-Inflammatory Focus (Weeks 9-16)

  • Once gluten-free was established, expanded to full anti-inflammatory protocol
  • Considered Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) but chose modified approach given multiple restrictions
  • Eliminated dairy, nightshades (for psoriasis), and alcohol
  • Emphasized omega-3 rich foods, colorful vegetables, bone broth
  • Addressed emotional eating through mindfulness and self-compassion practices

Phase 3: Circadian and Stress Restoration (Months 5-9)

  • Angela negotiated with employer for more consistent shifts
  • Developed strategies for sleep when night shifts unavoidable
  • Built regular stress management practices into routine
  • Explored identity beyond illness; reconnected with hobbies and relationships
  • Worked on releasing perfectionism; embraced "progress, not perfection"

Outcomes at 9 Months

  • tTG antibodies normalized (celiac in remission with strict gluten-free diet)
  • Psoriasis plaques reduced by 70%; rarely needs topical steroids
  • Energy improved from 2/10 to 6/10 (ongoing improvement expected as gut heals)
  • Nutrient levels normalizing on follow-up testing
  • Anxiety significantly reduced; feels hopeful rather than hopeless
  • Thyroid antibodies trending down (from 280 to 195)
  • Digestive symptoms largely resolved
  • Developed sustainable eating pattern she can maintain long-term

Coaching Insight: Angela's case illustrates coaching someone with multiple autoimmune conditions. The coach prioritized interventions (celiac first), paced changes appropriately, and addressed the emotional burden of multiple diagnoses. The interconnected nature of her conditions meant that healing the gut and reducing inflammation helped all three conditions. The coach's empathetic support through the identity shift of chronic illness was as important as the practical dietary guidance.

Common Themes Across Autoimmune Coaching

Patterns That Emerge

  • Gut involvement: Nearly every autoimmune case benefits from gut-focused interventions
  • Stress connection: A major stressor often precedes diagnosis by 1-2 years
  • Nutrient deficiencies: Common and contribute to both symptoms and immune dysregulation
  • Diet matters: Anti-inflammatory eating consistently improves outcomes
  • Sleep is critical: Poor sleep perpetuates inflammation and prevents healing
  • Emotional component: Addressing the psychological impact of autoimmunity is essential
  • Time and patience: Meaningful improvement typically takes 3-6+ months
  • Team approach: Best outcomes come from collaboration with medical providers

Coaching Wisdom for Autoimmune Clients

Practical Coaching Strategies

1. Meet clients where they are

Some clients are ready for an intensive protocol; others need to start with one small change. Assess readiness and adjust your approach accordingly.

2. Emphasize additions before subtractions

Adding anti-inflammatory foods feels more positive than focusing on restrictions. Often, as healthy foods increase, less healthy choices naturally decrease.

3. Address the emotional journey

Autoimmune diagnoses carry grief, anger, and fear. Create space for these emotions while building hope through evidence-based interventions.

4. Celebrate non-scale victories

Energy improvements, better sleep, reduced pain, clearer thinkingâ€”these matter as much or more than lab values or weight.

5. Prepare for setbacks

Autoimmune conditions fluctuate. Help clients understand that flares don't mean failureâ€”they're information about triggers and timing.

6. Build a sustainable identity

Help clients see themselves as people managing their health, not as their diagnosis. Support them in living fully despite their condition.

Key Takeaways

  • Autoimmune coaching requires a comprehensive approach addressing gut health, nutrition, stress, sleep, and environmental factors
  • Phased approaches allow clients to build sustainable changes progressively
  • Working collaboratively with medical providers ensures safety and synergy
  • Each autoimmune condition is unique, but underlying principles (reduce inflammation, heal gut, manage stress) apply broadly
  • Meaningful improvement typically requires 3-6+ months of consistent intervention
  • The emotional and identity aspects of autoimmunity are as important as the physical interventions
  • Clients can often significantly improve symptoms and markers even while maintaining necessary medications
