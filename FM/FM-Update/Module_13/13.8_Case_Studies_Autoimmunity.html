Case Study 1: Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

üìã

Client Profile: Michelle, Age 42

Managing Persistent Symptoms Despite Medication

üë©

Michelle, 42

Diagnosis: Hashimoto's thyroiditis, diagnosed 3 years ago

Medications: Levothyroxine 75mcg daily

Chief concerns: Persistent fatigue despite "normal" TSH, weight gain (20 lbs in 2 years), brain fog, hair loss, constipation

Recent labs: TSH 2.8 (normal range), TPO antibodies 342 IU/mL (elevated), vitamin D 24 ng/mL (low), ferritin 18 ng/mL (low)

Coaching Discovery Process

Michelle's coach explored her history and lifestyle, discovering several relevant factors:

  • High-stress corporate job with frequent travel
  • Chronic digestive issues (bloating, gas, irregular bowels) for 10+ years
  • Diet heavy in processed foods and gluten-containing grains
  • Poor sleep (5-6 hours, often interrupted)
  • History of yo-yo dieting
  • Symptoms worsened significantly after a stressful divorce 4 years ago
Coach Tip

When clients present with "normal" labs but persistent symptoms, dig deeper into their history. Ask: "When did your symptoms start? What was happening in your life at that time? Tell me about your digestion‚ÄîI know it might not seem related to your thyroid, but it often is." This kind of curiosity often reveals the interconnected root causes conventional medicine misses.

Coaching Approach

Phase 1

Foundation Building (Weeks 1-4)

Worked with functional medicine provider to optimize thyroid medication (added T3) and address vitamin D and iron deficiencies

  • Initiated 30-day gluten elimination trial with anti-inflammatory Mediterranean-style eating
  • Established consistent sleep schedule with 10pm bedtime goal
  • Introduced morning sunlight exposure and evening wind-down routine
  • Started daily 10-minute stress reduction practice (breathing exercises)
Phase 2

Gut Focus (Weeks 5-12)

Comprehensive stool testing revealed low beneficial bacteria and markers of intestinal inflammation

  • Implemented 5R protocol with practitioner guidance: removed remaining trigger foods, added digestive enzymes, introduced targeted probiotics
  • Emphasized gut-healing foods: bone broth, fermented vegetables, omega-3 rich fish
  • Supplementation: L-glutamine, zinc carnosine, omega-3s, selenium (per practitioner)
Phase 3

Optimization (Months 4-6)

Reintroduced some eliminated foods systematically; identified dairy as a trigger

  • Established sustainable eating pattern (gluten-free, dairy-free, anti-inflammatory)
  • Developed stress management toolkit including yoga and nature walks
  • Created strategies for maintaining habits during work travel
Outcomes at 6 Months
  • TPO antibodies decreased from 342 to 156 IU/mL (54% reduction)
  • Energy improved from 3/10 to 7/10
  • Lost 12 pounds without restrictive dieting
  • Brain fog largely resolved
  • Digestive symptoms 80% improved
  • Hair loss stopped; new growth visible
  • Sleeping 7-8 hours consistently
Coaching Insight

Michelle's case illustrates how addressing root causes (gut health, nutrient deficiencies, stress, sleep) can significantly improve autoimmune markers and symptoms‚Äîeven when thyroid medication is already in place. The coach focused on sustainable lifestyle changes while working collaboratively with her functional medicine provider on testing and supplementation.

Case Study 2: Rheumatoid Arthritis

üìã

Client Profile: Patricia, Age 56

Reducing Inflammation Through Lifestyle

üë©

Patricia, 56

Diagnosis: Rheumatoid arthritis, diagnosed 5 years ago

Medications: Methotrexate weekly, folic acid, occasional prednisone during flares

Chief concerns: Morning stiffness (45+ minutes daily), joint pain in hands and feet, fatigue, concern about long-term medication use

Recent labs: CRP 8.2 mg/L (elevated), ESR 38 mm/hr (elevated), vitamin D 31 ng/mL (suboptimal), RF positive

Coaching Discovery Process

Patricia's coach uncovered these contributing factors:

  • Standard American diet with daily sugar intake, processed foods, refined carbohydrates
  • Sedentary lifestyle due to pain (afraid to exercise)
  • Poor relationship with her body after RA diagnosis ("my body is attacking itself")
  • History of chronic sinus infections treated with multiple antibiotic courses
  • High-stress caregiver role for elderly mother
  • Using wine nightly to relax (2-3 glasses)
Coach Tip

When clients express fear around exercise due to pain, start with the concept of "movement as medicine." Explain: "Gentle, appropriate movement actually reduces inflammation in your joints and prevents stiffness from getting worse. We'll start slowly, in ways that feel good to your body. You're in control of the pace." This reframes exercise from something scary to something healing.

Coaching Approach

Phase 1

Nutrition Transition (Weeks 1-6)

Gradual transition to Mediterranean anti-inflammatory diet (started with additions before subtractions)

  • Week 1-2: Added fatty fish 3x/week, 6+ servings vegetables daily
  • Week 3-4: Replaced industrial seed oils with olive oil; reduced sugar by 50%
  • Week 5-6: Eliminated processed foods; reduced alcohol to 1 glass, 2 nights/week
  • Started omega-3 supplementation (3g/day) and vitamin D optimization per practitioner
Phase 2

Movement and Stress (Weeks 7-12)

Introduced gentle movement: warm water pool exercise 2x/week, gentle yoga 1x/week

  • Addressed fear around exercise; reframed movement as medicine for joints
  • Developed stress management plan: daily breathing exercises, weekly nature walks
  • Explored caregiver stress; connected Patricia with local support resources
  • Replaced wine habit with herbal tea ritual and evening stretching
Phase 3

Refinement and Expansion (Months 4-8)

Completed 30-day elimination of gluten and dairy; dairy identified as trigger (increased stiffness)

  • Added curcumin supplementation (with rheumatologist approval)
  • Progressed to gentle strength training with physical therapist guidance
  • Worked on body image and relationship with diagnosis through journaling and coaching conversations
Outcomes at 8 Months
  • Morning stiffness reduced from 45+ minutes to 10-15 minutes
  • CRP decreased from 8.2 to 3.1 mg/L
  • Joint pain reduced by 60% (patient rating)
  • Energy improved; afternoon fatigue largely resolved
  • Rheumatologist reduced methotrexate dose by 25%
  • No prednisone use needed in past 5 months
  • Exercising regularly without increased pain
  • Improved relationship with body and sense of agency
Coach Tip

Patricia's transformation demonstrates the power of gradual, addition-focused change. When clients feel overwhelmed by restrictions, start by adding anti-inflammatory foods. As they feel better from the additions, they become more motivated to reduce inflammatory foods. This approach honors readiness and builds sustainable momentum.

Coaching Insight

Patricia's transformation demonstrates the power of comprehensive lifestyle intervention for inflammatory autoimmune conditions. The gradual approach honored her readiness for change. Addressing alcohol, stress, and movement fear alongside diet created synergistic effects. The coach maintained close communication with her rheumatologist, ensuring complementary rather than conflicting approaches.

Case Study 3: Multiple Autoimmune Conditions

üìã

Client Profile: Angela, Age 49

Navigating Multiple Autoimmune Syndrome

üë©

Angela, 49

Diagnoses: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (8 years), psoriasis (5 years), newly diagnosed celiac disease

Medications: Levothyroxine 100mcg, topical steroids for psoriasis flares

Chief concerns: Overwhelming fatigue, widespread skin plaques despite topical treatment, digestive distress, anxiety about having multiple conditions, feeling "broken"

Recent labs: Multiple nutrient deficiencies (B12, iron, vitamin D, zinc), elevated tissue transglutaminase antibodies

Coaching Discovery Process

Angela's case revealed the "multiple autoimmune syndrome"‚Äîwhen one autoimmune condition increases risk for others. Her history showed:

  • Lifelong digestive issues, now explained by undiagnosed celiac disease
  • Chronic nutrient malabsorption affecting thyroid, skin, and energy
  • Family history of autoimmunity (mother with lupus, sister with RA)
  • High-stress nursing career with night shifts disrupting circadian rhythm
  • Emotional eating patterns, especially during psoriasis flares
  • Perfectionist tendencies leading to "all-or-nothing" approaches

Coaching Approach

Phase 1

Celiac Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

Prioritized strict gluten elimination as medical necessity (not optional)

  • Extensive education on hidden gluten sources and cross-contamination
  • Developed meal planning and preparation skills for safe gluten-free cooking
  • Addressed emotional aspects of dietary restriction
  • Practitioner managed nutrient repletion (B12 injections, iron, vitamin D, zinc)
Phase 2

Expanding Anti-Inflammatory Focus (Weeks 9-16)

Once gluten-free was established, expanded to full anti-inflammatory protocol

  • Considered Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) but chose modified approach given multiple restrictions
  • Eliminated dairy, nightshades (for psoriasis), and alcohol
  • Emphasized omega-3 rich foods, colorful vegetables, bone broth
  • Addressed emotional eating through mindfulness and self-compassion practices
Phase 3

Circadian and Stress Restoration (Months 5-9)

Angela negotiated with employer for more consistent shifts

  • Developed strategies for sleep when night shifts unavoidable
  • Built regular stress management practices into routine
  • Explored identity beyond illness; reconnected with hobbies and relationships
  • Worked on releasing perfectionism; embraced "progress, not perfection"
Coach Tip

When coaching clients with multiple conditions, prioritization is critical. Ask yourself: "What's the most medically important intervention? What will create the foundation for everything else?" For Angela, strict gluten elimination was non-negotiable due to celiac disease. Once that was solid, other interventions could be layered in. Don't try to change everything at once‚Äîit's overwhelming and unsustainable.

Outcomes at 9 Months
  • tTG antibodies normalized (celiac in remission with strict gluten-free diet)
  • Psoriasis plaques reduced by 70%; rarely needs topical steroids
  • Energy improved from 2/10 to 6/10 (ongoing improvement expected as gut heals)
  • Nutrient levels normalizing on follow-up testing
  • Anxiety significantly reduced; feels hopeful rather than hopeless
  • Thyroid antibodies trending down (from 280 to 195)
  • Digestive symptoms largely resolved
  • Developed sustainable eating pattern she can maintain long-term
Coaching Insight

Angela's case illustrates coaching someone with multiple autoimmune conditions. The coach prioritized interventions (celiac first), paced changes appropriately, and addressed the emotional burden of multiple diagnoses. The interconnected nature of her conditions meant that healing the gut and reducing inflammation helped all three conditions. The coach's empathetic support through the identity shift of chronic illness was as important as the practical dietary guidance.

Common Themes Across Autoimmune Coaching

Patterns That Emerge Across Cases

ü¶† Gut Involvement

Nearly every autoimmune case benefits from gut-focused interventions. The gut-immune connection is fundamental.

üò∞ Stress Connection

A major stressor often precedes diagnosis by 1-2 years. Stress management is therapeutic, not optional.

üíä Nutrient Deficiencies

Common and contribute to both symptoms and immune dysregulation. Testing and repletion are essential.

ü•ó Diet Matters

Anti-inflammatory eating consistently improves outcomes across different autoimmune conditions.

üò¥ Sleep is Critical

Poor sleep perpetuates inflammation and prevents healing. Sleep optimization is foundational.

üí≠ Emotional Component

Addressing the psychological impact of autoimmunity is as essential as the physical interventions.

‚è∞ Time and Patience

Meaningful improvement typically takes 3-6+ months. Set realistic expectations from the start.

ü§ù Team Approach

Best outcomes come from collaboration with medical providers. You are part of a healthcare team.

Coaching Wisdom for Autoimmune Clients

1

Meet Clients Where They Are

Some clients are ready for an intensive protocol; others need to start with one small change. Assess readiness and adjust your approach accordingly. Pushing too hard too fast leads to overwhelm and dropout.

2

Emphasize Additions Before Subtractions

Adding anti-inflammatory foods feels more positive than focusing on restrictions. Often, as healthy foods increase, less healthy choices naturally decrease without feeling like deprivation.

3

Address the Emotional Journey

Autoimmune diagnoses carry grief, anger, and fear. Create space for these emotions while building hope through evidence-based interventions. Don't skip the emotional work.

4

Celebrate Non-Scale Victories

Energy improvements, better sleep, reduced pain, clearer thinking‚Äîthese matter as much or more than lab values or weight. Help clients recognize and celebrate these wins.

5

Prepare for Setbacks

Autoimmune conditions fluctuate. Help clients understand that flares don't mean failure‚Äîthey're information about triggers and timing. Build resilience for the long journey.

6

Build a Sustainable Identity

Help clients see themselves as people managing their health, not as their diagnosis. Support them in living fully despite their condition. Identity work is transformative.

Scope of Practice Reminder

As a health coach, you do not diagnose, prescribe, or adjust medications. Your role is to support lifestyle modifications, facilitate behavior change, and work collaboratively with the client's medical team. Always encourage clients to discuss significant changes with their healthcare providers, especially around supplementation and dietary protocols that may affect medication needs.

Check Your Understanding

Test Your Knowledge
1

In Michelle's Hashimoto's case, what were the key root causes identified beyond her thyroid diagnosis?

Answer: Key root causes included chronic gut issues (bloating, gas, irregular bowels), nutrient deficiencies (vitamin D, iron), poor sleep (5-6 hours), high stress from work and divorce, and a diet high in processed foods and gluten. These interconnected factors were perpetuating her symptoms despite medication.

2

Why did Patricia's coach start with "additions" rather than "restrictions" when addressing her diet?

Answer: Starting with additions (adding fatty fish, vegetables, olive oil) feels more positive and sustainable than focusing on restrictions. This approach honors the client's readiness for change, builds momentum through feeling better, and often naturally crowds out less healthy choices without the psychological burden of deprivation. Patricia was more likely to stick with changes that felt empowering rather than punishing.

3

In Angela's case with multiple autoimmune conditions, why was celiac disease prioritized as the first intervention?

Answer: Celiac disease required strict gluten elimination as a medical necessity, not an optional lifestyle choice. Continued gluten exposure causes ongoing intestinal damage and prevents nutrient absorption. Addressing celiac first created the foundation for healing‚Äîit allowed nutrient repletion, reduced intestinal inflammation, and created a stable base from which to address her other conditions. Trying to change everything at once would have been overwhelming and unsustainable.

4

What common themes emerged across all three case studies that can guide your approach to autoimmune coaching?

Answer: Common themes include: (1) Gut involvement in nearly all cases, (2) Stress as a significant contributing factor, (3) Nutrient deficiencies requiring repletion, (4) Anti-inflammatory diet consistently improving outcomes, (5) Poor sleep perpetuating inflammation, (6) Emotional component needing attention, (7) Time and patience required (3-6+ months for meaningful improvement), and (8) Team approach with medical providers producing best results.

Key Takeaways
  • Autoimmune coaching requires a comprehensive approach addressing gut health, nutrition, stress, sleep, and environmental factors simultaneously
  • Phased approaches allow clients to build sustainable changes progressively without overwhelm
  • Working collaboratively with medical providers ensures safety, appropriate testing, and synergistic interventions
  • Each autoimmune condition is unique, but underlying principles (reduce inflammation, heal gut, manage stress) apply broadly
  • Meaningful improvement typically requires 3-6+ months of consistent intervention‚Äîset realistic expectations
  • The emotional and identity aspects of autoimmunity are as important as the physical interventions
  • Clients can often significantly improve symptoms and markers even while maintaining necessary medications‚Äîthis is complementary, not alternative care
  • Start with additions before subtractions to build sustainable momentum and avoid the deprivation mindset
