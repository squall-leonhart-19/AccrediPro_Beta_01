The First Session Structure: 75-90 Minutes

Here's a proven structure for initial intake sessions. Adjust timing based on your session length and client needs:

ðŸ“‹ Initial Session Structure (90-minute example)

5-10 min
1. Welcome & Connection

Warm greeting, brief chat to settle in, thank them for completing paperwork. Set a positive tone and help them relax.

5-10 min
2. Set the Agenda & Expectations

Explain what you'll cover today, how the session will flow, and what coaching is/isn't. Get their buy-in on the plan.

10-15 min
3. Explore Their Vision & Goals

What brought them here? What do they hope to achieve? What would be different if their health improved? Understand their "why."

30-40 min
4. Health History Deep Dive

This is the heart of the intake. Review questionnaire responses, ask clarifying questions, build the timeline, identify patterns. Listen more than you speak.

10-15 min
5. Synthesis & Initial Observations

Share what you're noticingâ€”patterns, connections, areas of focus. Check their reaction. Don't prescribe yet; offer observations.

10-15 min
6. Next Steps & Action Planning

Collaborate on 1-3 initial action items. Make them specific and achievable. Identify any referrals needed. Discuss homework or tracking.

5 min
7. Closing & Scheduling

Summarize the session, schedule next appointment, express confidence in their journey. Leave them feeling hopeful and clear.

Key Phrases for Each Section

Having prepared language helps you navigate the session smoothly:

Setting the Agenda:

"Here's what I'm thinking for today: I'd love to hear what brought you here and what you're hoping to achieve. Then we'll walk through your health history together, and I'll share some initial observations. We'll wrap up with next steps. Does that sound good?"

Exploring Their Vision:

"If you imagine yourself 6 months from now, and things have really improvedâ€”what's different? What are you doing that you can't do now? How do you feel?"

Transitioning to History:

"Thank you for sharing that. Now I'd love to understand your health journeyâ€”how we got here. I've reviewed your questionnaire, but I want to hear the story in your own words. Where would you say your health challenges really began?"

Sharing Observations:

"Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm noticing... [pattern]. Does that resonate with you? What stands out to you as we look at this together?"

Closing:

"We've covered a lot today. You've got [summarize action items]. I want you to knowâ€”based on what we've discussed, I really believe you can make meaningful progress. What's one thing you're taking away from today?"

Common First-Session Challenges (and Solutions)

Challenge: Client Goes Off-Topic Repeatedly

Solution: Gently redirect with empathy. "That's really important, and I want to make sure we come back to it. Right now, I want to make sure we have time to cover [X]. Can we note that and return to it?" Use the agenda you set as an anchor.

Challenge: Client is Overwhelmed and Emotional

Solution: Slow down. Acknowledge the emotion. "I can see this is bringing up a lot. Let's pause for a moment. This matters." Give them space. Sometimes the most valuable thing is being witnessed, not assessed.

Challenge: Running Out of Time

Solution: Give a time check at the halfway point. "We have about 30 minutes left. Let me make sure we cover the most important things." Prioritize closing with clarity and next stepsâ€”don't just end abruptly.

Challenge: Client Wants Immediate Answers

Solution: Validate their eagerness while setting realistic expectations. "I can see you're ready to take actionâ€”that's great. Today is about understanding the full picture so we can make the right moves. I'll have more specific recommendations after we complete our assessment."

Challenge: Client is Skeptical or Guarded

Solution: Don't push. Ask about their previous experiences. "It sounds like you've tried a lot of things before. What's worked? What hasn't? What would make this experience different for you?" Meet skepticism with curiosity, not defense.
