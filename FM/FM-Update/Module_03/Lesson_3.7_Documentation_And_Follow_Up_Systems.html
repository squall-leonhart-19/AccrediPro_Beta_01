Why Documentation Matters

Many new coaches see documentation as administrative burden. In reality, it's an essential professional practice that serves multiple purposes:

Continuity of Care

Remember details session-to-session. Track what's working, what's not, and adjust accordingly.

Legal Protection

If questions ever arise, your records document what was discussed, recommended, and agreed upon.

Progress Tracking

Show clients (and yourself) how far they've come. Objective data motivates continued effort.

Professional Standards

Documentation is expected in healthcare settings and demonstrates your credibility as a practitioner.

Team Communication

If you work with other providers, clear records enable effective collaboration and coordinated care.

Your Own Learning

Reviewing records helps you identify patterns in your practice and improve your coaching skills.

The SOAP Note Format

The SOAP note is a standard documentation format used across healthcare. It provides a consistent structure for recording session information:

üìã SOAP Note Structure

S
Subjective

What the client reports. Their symptoms, experiences, feelings, concerns, and self-assessments. Use their words when possible.

Example: "Client reports feeling 'more energetic' this week. States she slept 7 hours most nights (up from 5-6). Still experiencing bloating after dinner but notes it's 'less intense.' Expresses frustration with sugar cravings in the afternoon."

O
Objective

Observable and measurable data. Tracking results, completed assessments, vital signs (if applicable), lab results reviewed.

Example: "Food diary reviewed: 4/7 days completed. Average protein intake ~50g/day (target: 80g). Exercise: walked 3x this week. Weight: 165 lbs (down 2 lbs from baseline). MSQ score: 42 (down from 58 at intake)."

A
Assessment

Your clinical interpretation. What you notice, patterns identified, progress toward goals. (Note: as a coach, this is assessment, not diagnosis.)

Example: "Client showing positive response to sleep prioritization‚Äîenergy improving correlates with better sleep. Bloating pattern suggests possible food sensitivity; may warrant elimination approach. Afternoon sugar cravings likely linked to inadequate protein at lunch."

P
Plan

Action items and next steps. What was agreed upon, homework assigned, goals set, referrals made, next session scheduled.

Example: "1) Continue sleep protocol (7+ hours goal). 2) Increase lunch protein to 30g minimum. 3) Client to track bloating patterns with food diary focus. 4) Discuss elimination protocol next session if bloating persists. Next session: Thursday 2/15 at 10am."

Essential Client Records

Beyond session notes, maintain organized records for each client:

üìÅ Client File Contents

Intake Documents

Completed health history questionnaire, informed consent, HIPAA authorization, coaching agreement

Assessment Results

Symptom questionnaires (MSQ, etc.), baseline measurements, any validated assessments used

Functional Timeline

The chronological health map created during assessment

Session Notes

SOAP notes from each session, dated and organized chronologically

Progress Tracking

Food diaries, sleep logs, symptom trackers, measurement logs submitted by client

Communications

Significant emails, referral letters sent/received, provider communication

Lab Results (if applicable)

Copies of labs client has shared, with notes on what was discussed

Progress Tracking Tools

Systematic tracking helps you and your clients see progress over time. Consider using:

Medical Symptoms Questionnaire (MSQ)

A validated tool that scores symptoms across body systems. Repeat monthly to track overall symptom burden. Significant decreases indicate progress.

Goal Progress Tracking

Simple spreadsheet or form tracking each goal, target metrics, current status, and trend over time. Review at each session.

Food & Lifestyle Diaries

Have clients track food, sleep, movement, stress, and symptoms. Apps like Cronometer or simple templates work well. Review patterns together.

Energy & Mood Tracking

Daily 1-10 ratings of energy and mood can reveal patterns and correlations with interventions. Simple but powerful.

Confidentiality & Security

Client records contain sensitive health information. Maintain professional standards:

üîí Record-Keeping Best Practices

  • Secure storage: Use encrypted digital systems or locked physical files. Don't store client info on unprotected devices.
  • Access control: Only authorized personnel should access client records. Use strong passwords.
  • HIPAA awareness: If you're a covered entity or work with healthcare providers, understand HIPAA requirements.
  • Informed consent: Have clients sign acknowledgment of how their information will be stored and used.
  • Minimum necessary: Only document what's relevant to care. Avoid unnecessary personal details.
  • Retention policy: Keep records for 7 years minimum (longer for minors). Know your state requirements.

Follow-Up Systems

Great coaching extends beyond sessions. Build systems for ongoing client support:

üìß Between-Session Check-Ins

Brief email or message check-ins mid-week for clients who benefit from accountability. Keep it simple: "How's [specific goal] going?"

üìù Pre-Session Forms

Send a brief form 24-48 hours before sessions: "What's going well? What's challenging? What do you want to focus on today?"

üìä Monthly Progress Reviews

Periodic "big picture" reviews comparing current status to baseline. Celebrate wins, identify patterns, adjust strategies.

üéÅ Resource Delivery

Follow up sessions with relevant resources: recipes, articles, guided meditations, tracking templates. Shows you're invested.

üîë Key Takeaways

  • Documentation serves continuity of care, legal protection, progress tracking, and professional standards
  • Use SOAP format for session notes: Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan
  • Maintain organized client files with intake docs, assessments, session notes, and progress tracking
  • Track progress systematically using tools like MSQ, goal tracking, and food/lifestyle diaries
  • Protect client confidentiality with secure storage and appropriate consent
  • Build follow-up systems (check-ins, pre-session forms, progress reviews) that extend your impact
