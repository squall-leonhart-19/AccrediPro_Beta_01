The Power of Network

You can't do everything alone. A strong network means better client outcomes and professional growth.

Network Benefits

Referral sources for clients you can't serve. Referral partners to receive clients from. Mentors for challenging cases. Peers for discussion and support. Collaborative care for complex clients.

Key Network Connections

  • Gastroenterologists: For colonoscopies, IBD management, red flags
  • Primary care physicians: For labs, prescriptions, collaboration
  • Naturopathic doctors: For expanded testing and prescriptions
  • Mental health practitioners: For gut-brain axis clients
  • Dietitians/Nutritionists: For specialized dietary support
  • Acupuncturists/Chiropractors: For complementary care

Professional Communities

  • AccrediPro practitioner community
  • Functional medicine practitioner groups
  • Condition-specific communities (SIBO, IBD)
  • Local health practitioner networks
  • Online mastermind groups

Finding Mentorship

  • Identify practitioners you admire
  • Offer to assist or shadow
  • Join formal mentorship programs
  • Engage with experts at conferences
  • Consider paid coaching or supervision

1. Why is having a GI referral relationship important?

Gastroenterologists can perform colonoscopies/endoscopies to rule out serious conditions, manage IBD medications, and handle red flag situations. Having a trusted GI to refer to ensures clients get appropriate care when it's beyond your scope.

2. How can you build relationships with referring practitioners?

Introduce yourself and your services. Offer to present at their practice. Send detailed, professional referral notes. Follow up on shared clients. Make referrals reciprocal. Be reliably professional. Over time, trust and referrals build.
