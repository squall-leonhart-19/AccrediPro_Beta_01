Module 6 Review

Lifestyle interventions address the terrain in which healing occurs. No supplement protocol can overcome chronic stress, poor sleep, or sedentary lifestyle. Let's consolidate these foundations into practical frameworks.

Stress and Gut-Brain (6.1)

  • Gut-brain axis is bidirectional
  • Chronic stress damages gut lining, microbiome, motility
  • Many gut issues start with stress trigger
  • Stress assessment essential in all intakes

Vagus Nerve (6.2)

  • Master switch between stress and healing modes
  • Techniques: deep breathing, cold exposure, humming, gargling
  • Pre-meal rituals enhance digestion
  • Vagal tone strengthens with daily practice

Sleep Optimization (6.3)

  • Sleep and gut health bidirectionally connected
  • Consistent sleep timing most important
  • Finish eating 3-4 hours before bed
  • Light exposure sets circadian rhythm

Movement (6.4)

  • Moderate exercise improves motility and microbiome
  • Intense exercise can worsen active gut issues
  • Post-meal walks highly effective
  • Daily NEAT as important as formal exercise

Environmental Toxins (6.5)

  • Glyphosate disrupts microbiome and tight junctions
  • Dirty Dozen: buy organic; Clean Fifteen: conventional OK
  • Avoid heating food in plastic
  • Support detox with crucifers, elimination, sweating

Medications (6.6)

  • Antibiotics: effects persist 6-12 months+
  • PPIs increase SIBO risk; support with enzymes
  • NSAIDs damage gut lining within hours
  • Never advise stopping medications; support alongside

Behavior Change (6.7)

  • Start embarrassingly small
  • Habit stacking: attach to existing routines
  • Design environment for success
  • Focus on identity, not just outcomes

Quick Reference: Lifestyle Prescription Template

Foundation Level (Everyone)

3 deep breaths before meals. Consistent sleep/wake times. 10-minute post-meal walk. No screens during meals. 4-5 hours between meals (no snacking).

Stress-Dominant Presentations

Add: Daily vagal activation (5-10 min breathing practice). Evening wind-down routine. Nature exposure. Social connection. Consider referral for therapy/counseling if needed.

Toxic Load Concerns

Add: Dirty Dozen organic shopping. Water filtration. Glass food storage. Cruciferous vegetable focus. Sweat regularly (sauna or exercise).
