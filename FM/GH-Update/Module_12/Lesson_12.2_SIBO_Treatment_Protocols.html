SIBO Treatment Overview

SIBO treatment requires eliminating overgrowth AND addressing why it developed. Without addressing root cause, recurrence rates are 45% or higher.

Treatment Phases

Phase 1: Preparation (2-4 weeks). Phase 2: Antimicrobial treatment (4-6 weeks). Phase 3: Prokinetic therapy (3+ months). Phase 4: Reintroduction and maintenance. Each phase is critical‚Äîdon't skip.

Antimicrobial Options

  • Pharmaceutical: Rifaximin (hydrogen), Rifaximin + Neomycin or Metronidazole (methane)
  • Herbal (equally effective): Berberine, oregano oil, neem, allicin
  • Duration: 2-4 weeks per round; may need 2-3 rounds
  • Biofilm disruptors: NAC, enzymes; use before antimicrobials

Prokinetic Therapy

  • Why essential: SIBO often results from impaired MMC (motility)
  • Pharmaceutical: Low-dose erythromycin, prucalopride
  • Herbal: Ginger, Iberogast, MotilPro
  • Duration: 3+ months minimum; some need indefinitely
  • Timing: Start immediately after antimicrobial phase

Preventing Recurrence

  • Address underlying motility issues
  • Meal spacing (4-5 hours between meals)
  • Avoid snacking (allows MMC to function)
  • Treat adhesions if present (visceral manipulation)
  • Manage stress (impacts vagus nerve and motility)

1. Why is prokinetic therapy essential for SIBO?

SIBO often results from impaired MMC (migrating motor complex)‚Äîthe "cleaning wave" that sweeps bacteria from the small intestine. Without addressing this underlying motility issue, SIBO recurs. Prokinetics restore proper motility.

2. How does meal spacing help prevent SIBO recurrence?

The MMC (cleaning wave) only activates in a fasted state‚Äîabout 90 minutes after a meal. Constant snacking prevents the MMC from ever activating. Spacing meals 4-5 hours apart allows the MMC to function and sweep bacteria out.
