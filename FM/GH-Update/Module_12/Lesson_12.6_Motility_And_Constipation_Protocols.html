Types of Constipation

Not all constipation is treated the same way. Identifying the type determines the intervention.

Constipation Categories

Slow transit: Weak peristalsis, whole colon sluggish. Outlet dysfunction: Pelvic floor issues, can't evacuate. Mixed: Both components. SIBO-related: Methane slows motility. Each requires different approach.

Causes of Impaired Motility

  • Hypothyroidism: Always check thyroid in constipation
  • Methane SIBO: Archaea produce methane that slows gut
  • Vagus nerve dysfunction: Stress, trauma, head injuries
  • Medications: Opioids, anticholinergics, iron
  • Magnesium deficiency: Muscles can't relax properly
  • Dehydration: Insufficient water for stool consistency

Natural Interventions

  • Magnesium: Citrate or oxide draws water; start 200-400mg at night
  • Fiber: Increase gradually; some can't tolerate (SIBO)
  • Prokinetics: Ginger, Iberogast, artichoke for motility
  • Vitamin C: High doses have laxative effect
  • Coffee: Stimulates gastrocolic reflex
  • Movement: Physical activity improves motility

Red Flags for Referral

  • New-onset constipation over age 50
  • Blood in stool
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Family history of colon cancer
  • No response to standard interventions

1. What is methane SIBO's relationship to constipation?

Methane-producing archaea in the small intestine produce methane gas that directly slows gut motility. This leads to constipation, bloating, and incomplete evacuation. Treating methane SIBO often resolves chronic constipation.

2. What is the difference between slow transit and outlet dysfunction constipation?

Slow transit: The whole colon moves slowly due to weak peristalsis. Stool takes too long to travel through. Outlet dysfunction: Stool reaches the rectum but can't be evacuated due to pelvic floor issues. Different treatments needed for each.
