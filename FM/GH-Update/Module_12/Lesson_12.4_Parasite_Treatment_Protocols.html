Parasite Treatment Overview

Parasites have life cycles that affect treatment timing. Treatment often needs to span 2-3 full lunar cycles (60-90 days) to catch all life stages.

Lunar Cycle Consideration

Parasites are more active around the full moon (egg laying, movement). Some practitioners intensify treatment around full moon and support around new moon. While controversial, many find improved results with cycle-aligned protocols.

Antiparasitic Approaches

  • Prescription: Mebendazole, Albendazole, Ivermectin, Nitazoxanide (based on organism)
  • Herbal: Wormwood, black walnut, cloves, mimosa pudica, olive leaf
  • Duration: 60-90 days minimum to cover life cycles
  • Pulsing: Some protocols pulse treatment (on days around full moon)

Protocol Phases

  • Prep (2 weeks): Open drainage, binders, liver support
  • Treatment (8-12 weeks): Antiparasitics, rotate or cycle
  • Maintenance: Monthly pulses may be needed
  • Rebuild: Probiotics, gut repair after clearance

Managing Die-Off

  • Binders essential: Mimosa pudica seed, charcoal, clay
  • Coffee enemas may help clear debris
  • Liver support: NAC, milk thistle, castor oil packs
  • Go slower if reactions too intense

1. Why does parasite treatment need 60-90 days?

Parasites have life cycles with eggs, larvae, and adults. Treatment may kill adults but egg forms survive. Multiple cycles (60-90 days = 2-3 lunar cycles) are needed to catch all life stages as they hatch and mature.

2. What is the lunar cycle theory in parasite treatment?

Parasites may be more active around the full moon (egg laying, movement). Some practitioners intensify treatment around full moon and provide support/rest around new moon. While not universally accepted, many report improved results with cycle-aligned protocols.
