Leaky Gut Repair Principles

You can't heal a leaky gut while still damaging it. Remove the cause first (gluten, pathogens, NSAIDs, stress), then repair.

Repair Timeline

Intestinal cells turn over every 3-5 days. Full mucosal repair takes 3-6 months depending on severity. Some autoimmune cases take longer. Chronic damage means longer repair.

Key Repair Nutrients

  • L-Glutamine: Primary fuel for enterocytes; 5-30g daily
  • Zinc carnosine: Repairs tight junctions; 75-150mg daily
  • Collagen/gelatin: Provides glycine, proline for tissue repair
  • DGL (licorice): Stimulates mucus production
  • Aloe vera: Anti-inflammatory, promotes healing
  • Butyrate: Fuel for colonocytes; especially for colon repair

Dietary Strategies

  • Bone broth: Rich in collagen, glycine, glutamine
  • Elimination diet: Remove triggers (gluten, dairy, soy 100%)
  • Easy-to-digest foods: Soups, stews, cooked vegetables
  • Omega-3s: Anti-inflammatory (wild salmon, sardines)
  • Avoid: Alcohol, NSAIDs, processed foods

Integration Timing

  • Address pathogens before heavy repair (infections damage lining)
  • Basic repair support during antimicrobial phase is OK
  • Intensive repair phase comes AFTER "Remove" phase
  • Continue repair during reintroduction phase

1. How long does full mucosal repair typically take?

While intestinal cells turn over every 3-5 days, full mucosal repair takes 3-6 months depending on severity. Some autoimmune cases take longer. The tighter junctions and deeper tissue layers need more time.

2. Why is L-glutamine important for leaky gut repair?

L-glutamine is the primary fuel source for enterocytes (intestinal cells). Without adequate glutamine, these cells can't function or repair properly. Therapeutic doses are 5-30g daily, often divided throughout the day.
