Clinical Diet Selection Algorithm

Step 1: Identify Primary Condition

IBS/SIBO â†’ Low-FODMAP. Food sensitivities â†’ Elimination. IBD â†’ Consider SCD. Candida â†’ Low-sugar/anti-Candida. General inflammation â†’ Anti-inflammatory foundation.

Step 2: Assess Client Capacity

Time for cooking? Budget? Family dynamics? History of disordered eating? These factors determine how restrictive you can realistically go. The "best" diet that isn't followed doesn't work.

Step 3: Start Conservatively

Begin with simplest intervention likely to help. Elimination diet or modified low-FODMAP covers most situations. Escalate only if needed. More restrictive protocols require demonstrated need.

Step 4: Plan for Expansion

Set timeline for reintroduction from day one. Explain that restriction is temporary. Goal is always the most diverse diet that maintains symptom control.

Common Clinical Scenarios

  • New client with IBS: Start with low-FODMAP elimination. If good response, proceed to FODMAP reintroduction. If poor response, consider SIBO testing and/or standard elimination.
  • Post-food poisoning SIBO: Low-FODMAP during treatment. Biofilm disruptors and antimicrobials. Slowly expand diet as treatment progresses.
  • Multiple food reactions: Standard elimination suggests leaky gut. Prioritize gut healing (bone broth, glutamine, zinc). Reintroduce foods as permeability improves.
  • Histamine symptoms: Low-histamine elimination for symptom relief. Investigate DAO-damaging conditions (SIBO, dysbiosis). DAO support while addressing root cause.
