Consultation Structure

A well-structured initial consultation achieves multiple goals: gathering essential information, building trust, and setting expectations. Plan for 60-90 minutes.

Phase 1: Connection (5-10 min)

Welcome the client. Review how the session will flow. Explain confidentiality. Ask what they're hoping to get from working together. This sets the stage and helps the client feel comfortable sharing.

Phase 2: Story (15-20 min)

"Tell me your story. How did you get here?" Let them talk without interruption. Listen for key themes, timeline of events, and what they believe is causing their issues. Take notes but maintain eye contact.

Phase 3: Targeted Questions (20-30 min)

Fill in gaps from the story. Ask about specific symptoms, diet, lifestyle, medications, and history. Use the comprehensive question categories below.

Phase 4: Summary and Next Steps (10-15 min)

Summarize what you've heard. Share initial impressions (without diagnosing). Explain recommended testing or next steps. Set expectations for timeline and process.

Essential Question Categories

Current Symptoms

  • Describe your main digestive symptoms
  • How often? How severe (1-10)?
  • What makes them better? Worse?
  • Stool frequency, consistency (Bristol Scale), any blood or mucus?

Timeline

  • When did symptoms first begin?
  • What was happening in your life at that time?
  • Any food poisoning, travel illness, or infection around that time?
  • How have symptoms changed over time?

Medical History

  • Previous diagnoses (IBS, IBD, GERD, etc.)?
  • Previous testing‚Äîwhat's been done, what were results?
  • Surgeries, especially abdominal (appendix, gallbladder, C-section)?
  • History of eating disorders?

Medications and Supplements

  • Current prescription medications
  • OTC medications (especially NSAIDs, antacids, laxatives)
  • Current supplements
  • Antibiotic history‚Äîhow many courses, when?

Red Flags Requiring Medical Referral

Immediate Medical Attention

Refer to physician if any of the following are present: unexplained weight loss (>10 lbs without trying), blood in stool (especially dark/tarry), fever with GI symptoms, severe abdominal pain, family history of colon cancer with new symptoms, symptoms starting after age 50 without prior workup, any symptoms that feel "different" or concerning to you.

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What are the four phases of an initial consultation?

1) Connection (5-10 min): Welcome, explain session flow, build rapport. 2) Story (15-20 min): Let client tell their story without interruption. 3) Targeted Questions (20-30 min): Fill in gaps with specific questions. 4) Summary and Next Steps (10-15 min): Reflect back, share impressions, explain next steps.

2. Why is the timeline question important in gut health assessment?

The timeline reveals potential triggers and patterns. Many gut issues begin after: food poisoning (post-infectious IBS/SIBO), major stress, travel, antibiotics, or surgery. Understanding when symptoms began and what was happening helps identify root causes and guide treatment. It also helps clients connect the dots.

3. Name three red flags that require immediate medical referral.

Red flags include: unexplained weight loss (>10 lbs without trying), blood in stool (especially dark/tarry), fever with GI symptoms, severe abdominal pain, family history of colon cancer with new symptoms, symptoms starting after age 50 without prior workup. When in doubt, refer out.
