Why Documentation Matters

Good documentation protects you and serves your clients. It tracks progress, supports continuity of care, provides legal protection, and enables quality improvement.

The SOAP Note Format

S (Subjective): What client reports‚Äîsymptoms, concerns, progress. O (Objective): Observable findings, test results. A (Assessment): Your interpretation. P (Plan): Recommendations, next steps.

Essential Records

  • Initial intake forms and health history
  • Signed informed consent and policies
  • Session notes for each visit
  • Test results and interpretation
  • Treatment plans provided
  • All communications

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What does SOAP stand for?

S = Subjective (what client reports), O = Objective (observable/test findings), A = Assessment (your interpretation), P = Plan (recommendations and next steps).

2. Why does documentation matter?

Good documentation: tracks progress over time, supports continuity of care, provides legal protection if issues arise, enables quality improvement, and helps you remember details between sessions.
