Understanding Scope

Scope of practice varies by credential and jurisdiction. Know your limits‚Äîworking outside them puts clients at risk and exposes you to liability.

Generally Within Scope

Education about health topics. Lifestyle and dietary recommendations. Supplement suggestions (check local laws). Functional testing interpretation. Health coaching and accountability.

Generally Outside Scope

Diagnosing medical conditions. Prescribing medications. Advising clients to stop prescribed medications. Ordering conventional lab tests (varies). Treating emergencies. Mental health treatment (unless licensed).

Building Referral Relationships

  • Primary care physicians for medical oversight
  • GI specialists for colonoscopies and complex conditions
  • Mental health professionals for anxiety, trauma, eating disorders
  • Registered dietitians where required by law

2. Why are referral relationships important?

Clients need a complete care team. Referrals ensure they get medical oversight, specialized testing, mental health support, and services outside your scope‚Äîproviding comprehensive care without overstepping your boundaries.
