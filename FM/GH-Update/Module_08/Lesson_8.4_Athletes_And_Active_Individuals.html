Exercise and the Gut

Intense exercise affects gut function‚Äîblood diverts from GI tract to muscles, core temperature rises, and stress hormones increase. This can create acute symptoms and long-term issues if not managed.

Exercise-Induced GI Distress

Up to 70% of endurance athletes experience GI issues during training/competition: cramping, nausea, diarrhea (runner's trots), side stitches, reflux. Caused by: blood flow diversion, mechanical jostling, heat, dehydration, inappropriate pre-exercise nutrition.

Common Athlete Gut Issues

  • GI distress during exercise: Timing of meals, food choices, hydration
  • Leaky gut from overtraining: Chronic intense exercise can increase permeability
  • Iron deficiency: GI blood loss during running is common
  • NSAID overuse: Athletes often use ibuprofen, damaging gut lining

Athletic Gut Support

  • Train the gut: Practice race-day nutrition during training
  • Avoid high-fiber, high-fat, high-FODMAP before training
  • Ensure adequate recovery between intense sessions
  • Address any underlying gut issues (SIBO common in endurance athletes)
  • Avoid NSAIDs‚Äîespecially before/during exercise

1. Why do athletes experience GI symptoms during intense exercise?

Blood flow diverts from GI tract to working muscles (up to 80% reduction), core temperature rises, dehydration occurs, and stress hormones increase. The mechanical jostling of running and inappropriate pre-exercise nutrition also contribute.

2. Why should athletes avoid NSAIDs before exercise?

NSAIDs damage gut lining and increase intestinal permeability. Combined with exercise-induced blood flow diversion, they significantly worsen leaky gut and increase inflammation. Many athletes overuse ibuprofen, creating chronic gut damage.
