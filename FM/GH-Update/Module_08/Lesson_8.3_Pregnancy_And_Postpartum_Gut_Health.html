Pregnancy Gut Changes

Pregnancy naturally alters gut function through hormonal changes, physical compression, and immune shifts‚Äîall designed to support the baby but often causing discomfort.

Why GI Symptoms Increase

Progesterone relaxes smooth muscle, slowing motility. Growing uterus compresses intestines and stomach. Immune system shifts to tolerate baby. Microbiome changes to support pregnancy. Result: constipation, reflux, nausea, bloating.

Common Pregnancy Concerns

  • Nausea/morning sickness: Ginger, B6, small frequent meals
  • Constipation: Magnesium, fiber, hydration, movement
  • Reflux: Small meals, elevate head of bed, avoid eating before lying down
  • Hemorrhoids: Fiber, hydration, squatting position

Pregnancy Safety

Many herbs and supplements are contraindicated in pregnancy. Always verify safety before recommending. Avoid: high-dose vitamin A, many herbs (especially in first trimester). Safe generally: probiotics, ginger (moderate), fiber, magnesium. Refer to prenatal team for medication questions.

Postpartum Considerations

  • Gut healing after C-section (surgery affects gut)
  • Rebuilding microbiome after any antibiotics during delivery
  • Breastfeeding impacts: what mom eats affects baby
  • Stress and sleep deprivation affecting gut

1. Why does constipation increase during pregnancy?

Progesterone relaxes smooth muscle throughout the body, including the intestines, which slows motility. The growing uterus also compresses the intestines physically. Result: slower transit and constipation.

2. What supplements are generally safe in pregnancy for gut support?

Generally safe: probiotics, ginger (in moderate amounts), fiber, magnesium (especially for constipation). Always verify safety before recommending‚Äîmany herbs are contraindicated, especially in first trimester. Refer to prenatal team for questions.
