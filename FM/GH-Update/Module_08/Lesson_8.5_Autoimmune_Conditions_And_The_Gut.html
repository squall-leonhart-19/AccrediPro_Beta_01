The Gut-Autoimmune Connection

All autoimmune conditions have a gut component. 70-80% of immune tissue resides in the gut. Intestinal permeability (leaky gut) is considered a necessary precondition for autoimmunity to develop.

Alessio Fasano's Triad

Autoimmunity requires 3 factors: 1) Genetic predisposition, 2) Environmental trigger, 3) Intestinal permeability. Addressing the gut (permeability) is one factor we can influence, even when genetics can't be changed.

Common Autoimmune-Gut Connections

  • Hashimoto's: Strong gluten connection; often co-occurs with celiac
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis: Microbiome shifts precede disease onset
  • Multiple Sclerosis: Gut dysbiosis linked; certain bacteria protective
  • Lupus: Increased permeability; leaky gut worsens flares

Autoimmune Protocol Approach

  • Elimination diet (AIP) removes common triggers: grains, dairy, eggs, nightshades, nuts
  • Heal gut lining with L-glutamine, bone broth, collagen
  • Address dysbiosis and SIBO if present
  • Reduce inflammation with omega-3s, curcumin
  • Support with vitamin D (often low in autoimmune)
