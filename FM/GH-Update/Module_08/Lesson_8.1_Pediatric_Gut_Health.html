The Developing Microbiome

A child's microbiome is still developing until around age 3. Birth method, feeding, antibiotics, and environment all shape this critical window.

Key Influencers

Vaginal birth vs. C-section. Breastfeeding vs. formula. Antibiotic exposure (especially in first year). Introduction of solid foods. Pet exposure. Outdoor play and soil contact.

Common Pediatric Concerns

  • Colic: Often gut-related; microbial imbalance, feeding issues
  • Constipation: Very common; fiber, hydration, dairy removal often helpful
  • Food sensitivities: Can manifest as eczema, behavior, not just GI symptoms
  • Recurrent infections: 70% of immune function is in gut

Dosing Modifications

General rule: Reduce adult doses based on weight. Infants/toddlers: typically 10-25% adult dose. Children 4-12: typically 25-50% adult dose. Always check product-specific pediatric recommendations.
