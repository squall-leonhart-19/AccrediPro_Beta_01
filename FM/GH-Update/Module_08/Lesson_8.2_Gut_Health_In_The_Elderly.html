Age-Related Changes

Aging naturally affects gut function‚Äîreduced stomach acid, slower motility, decreased microbiome diversity, and reduced digestive enzyme production.

Common Changes with Aging

Decreased stomach acid production. Slower intestinal transit (constipation). Reduced microbiome diversity. Decreased muscle tone in digestive tract. Reduced ability to absorb nutrients (B12, calcium, iron). Increased medication use affecting the gut.

Common Elderly Concerns

  • Constipation: Extremely common; low fluid, movement, fiber, medications
  • Nutrient deficiencies: B12, D, calcium, iron due to reduced absorption
  • Polypharmacy effects: Multiple medications stacking gut side effects
  • C. diff risk: Higher with antibiotics and PPI use

Protocol Modifications

  • Start with lower doses‚Äî"start low, go slow"
  • Consider digestive support (enzymes, HCl if appropriate)
  • Emphasize hydration and movement for motility
  • Review all medications for gut effects
  • Always coordinate with medical team

1. What gut changes occur with aging?

Decreased stomach acid, slower motility, reduced microbiome diversity, decreased digestive enzymes, reduced nutrient absorption (B12, calcium, iron), and often increased medication use that affects gut function.

2. Why is "start low, go slow" important with elderly clients?

Older adults often have reduced metabolism and clearance of supplements, increased sensitivity to changes, and potential interactions with medications. Starting with lower doses and increasing slowly minimizes risks and adverse reactions.
