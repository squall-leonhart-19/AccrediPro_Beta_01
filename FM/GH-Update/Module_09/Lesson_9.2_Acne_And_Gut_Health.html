Acne Mechanisms

Acne is inflammation visible on the skin. While traditionally treated topically, research shows gut dysfunction is present in over half of acne patients.

Gut-Acne Links

Intestinal permeability allows LPS into bloodstream, triggering inflammation. Dysbiosis shifts cytokine balance toward inflammation. Poor detoxification: toxins exit through skin. Blood sugar spikes increase IGF-1 and androgens. Stress affects both gut and skin via cortisol.

Key Dietary Factors

  • Dairy: Increases IGF-1; whey protein especially problematic
  • High glycemic foods: Sugar, refined carbs spike insulin/IGF-1
  • Gluten: In sensitive individuals, increases permeability
  • Chocolate: Some evidence of direct effect; may be cocoa butter or sugar

Gut-Based Protocol

  • Remove dairy and high-glycemic foods for 4 weeks
  • Support gut healing (L-glutamine, collagen, zinc)
  • Address any dysbiosis or SIBO
  • Support liver detoxification (cruciferous, bitters)
  • Probiotics: Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains

1. Why is dairy often problematic for acne?

Dairy increases IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor), which promotes sebum production and skin cell proliferation. Whey protein is especially problematic. Skim milk may be worse than whole milk due to processing.

2. How does gut permeability contribute to acne?

Leaky gut allows LPS (lipopolysaccharides) and other inflammatory particles into the bloodstream. This triggers systemic inflammation that manifests in the skin. Additionally, impaired detoxification forces toxins to exit through the skin pores.
