Eczema and the Gut

Eczema is often the first sign of immune dysfunction that begins in the gut. The "atopic march" describes progression from eczema ‚Üí food allergies ‚Üí asthma ‚Üí allergic rhinitis.

The Atopic March

Eczema typically appears in infancy. Without addressing root causes, the immune dysfunction progresses: food allergies develop, then asthma, then environmental allergies. This march can be interrupted by addressing gut health early.

Common Food Triggers

  • Dairy: Most common trigger, especially in children
  • Eggs: Second most common pediatric trigger
  • Gluten/wheat: Often implicated in adult eczema
  • Soy: Common trigger, especially with formula history
  • Histamine-rich foods: Fermented foods, aged cheese, wine

Gut-Focused Protocol

  • Elimination diet: Remove dairy, eggs, gluten, soy for 4 weeks
  • Heal gut lining: L-glutamine, zinc, vitamin A, collagen
  • Probiotics: Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG shown beneficial
  • Address dysbiosis if present
  • Omega-3s: Anti-inflammatory, support skin barrier
