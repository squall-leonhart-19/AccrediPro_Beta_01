Overview of Stool Testing

Comprehensive stool testing is the cornerstone of functional gut health assessment. Unlike basic stool culture, functional stool tests provide a comprehensive view of gut ecology, digestion, inflammation, and immune function.

Major Stool Testing Platforms

GI-MAP (Diagnostic Solutions)

Uses qPCR technology for quantitative pathogen detection. Excellent for identifying bacteria, parasites, viruses, and fungi. Includes H. pylori with virulence factors, antibiotic resistance genes, and immune markers. Industry favorite for pathogen detection.

GI Effects (Genova)

Combines culture, PCR, and advanced microbiome analysis. Strong for microbiome diversity assessment and digestive function markers. Includes metabolomic markers like short-chain fatty acids.

Comprehensive Stool Analysis (Doctor's Data)

Culture-based with PCR for select pathogens. Good digestive markers and includes antimicrobial sensitivity testing to guide antifungal/antibiotic selection.

Test Components

Pathogen Detection

  • Bacteria: H. pylori (with virulence factors), pathogenic E. coli strains, Campylobacter, C. difficile
  • Parasites: Giardia, Blastocystis, Cryptosporidium, Entamoeba
  • Fungi: Candida species and other yeast
  • Viruses: Some tests include norovirus, rotavirus (less common)

Commensal Bacteria

  • Beneficial bacteria: Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Akkermansia
  • Opportunistic bacteria: Organisms that cause problems when overgrown
  • Diversity markers: Overall microbiome health indicators

Digestive Markers

  • Pancreatic elastase: <200 ¬µg/g suggests pancreatic insufficiency
  • Fecal fat (steatocrit): Elevated indicates fat malabsorption
  • Meat/vegetable fibers: Presence indicates incomplete digestion

Inflammatory Markers

  • Calprotectin: Distinguishes IBD from IBS; elevated >50-200 ¬µg/g suggests inflammation
  • Lactoferrin: Another inflammation marker
  • Zonulin: Marker of intestinal permeability (available on some tests)

Immune Markers

  • Secretory IgA (sIgA): Low = suppressed mucosal immunity; High = acute immune response
  • Beta-glucuronidase: Elevated indicates estrogen recirculation issues

Clinical Pearl

Chronically low sIgA often indicates adrenal fatigue/HPA axis dysfunction, chronic stress, or longstanding infection. Extremely high sIgA can indicate acute infection or immune activation.

Interpreting Results

Important Framework

Always interpret results in the context of the client's symptoms and history. A positive finding on a test doesn't always require treatment, and a negative test doesn't mean there's no problem. Tests are data points, not prescriptions.

Key Interpretation Principles

  • Treat the patient, not the test ‚Äî Findings must correlate with symptoms
  • Consider quantification ‚Äî High levels are more significant than trace amounts
  • Look for patterns ‚Äî Multiple dysbiotic findings together paint a picture
  • Prioritize pathogens ‚Äî Address infections before optimizing commensals

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What does fecal calprotectin help distinguish?

Calprotectin helps distinguish IBD (inflammatory bowel disease) from IBS (irritable bowel syndrome). Elevated calprotectin (>50-200 ¬µg/g) indicates intestinal inflammation‚Äîsuggesting IBD or infection. Normal calprotectin in a symptomatic patient suggests functional disorder like IBS, which is appropriate for practitioner support.

2. What does low pancreatic elastase indicate?

Low pancreatic elastase (<200 ¬µg/g) indicates pancreatic exocrine insufficiency‚Äîthe pancreas isn't producing enough digestive enzymes. This leads to maldigestion, especially of fats and proteins, and warrants digestive enzyme supplementation and possibly further investigation.

3. Why should you "treat the patient, not the test"?

A positive finding doesn't always require treatment‚Äîsome organisms may be present without causing symptoms. Similarly, a negative test doesn't mean there's no problem. Test results must be interpreted in the context of the client's symptoms, history, and clinical picture. Tests are data points that inform decisions, not automatic prescriptions for treatment.
