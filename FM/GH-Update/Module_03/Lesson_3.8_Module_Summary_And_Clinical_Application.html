Step 3: Interpret in Context

No test is definitive. Results must correlate with symptoms and history. A positive finding in an asymptomatic area may not need treatment. A negative test doesn't rule out the condition.

Step 4: Track Progress

Retesting is valuable for: SIBO (post-treatment breath test), severe dysbiosis (follow-up stool), nutrient repletion (blood work at 3-6 months). Symptom tracking through questionnaires is equally important.

Cost-Effective Testing Strategy

  • Budget-conscious clients: Rely more heavily on clinical assessment, elimination diets, and empirical treatment. Test only when treatment fails.
  • Complex cases: Comprehensive testing upfront saves time and money long-term by identifying multiple issues simultaneously.
  • Progress monitoring: Symptom questionnaires are free and effective. Reserve expensive retesting for documenting resolution of serious findings.
