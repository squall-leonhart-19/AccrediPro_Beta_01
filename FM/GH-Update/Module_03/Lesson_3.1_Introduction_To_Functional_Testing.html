Functional Range

Narrower optimal ranges based on health optimization rather than just disease detection.

Pathological Range

Standard lab ranges designed to identify disease states, often missing subclinical dysfunction.

Direct Access Testing

Lab tests that can be ordered directly by clients without a practitioner prescription.

Biomarker

A measurable indicator of a biological state or condition.

Why Functional Testing?

Conventional medicine excels at diagnosing pathology‚Äîidentifying disease states that require medical intervention. But many of your clients will come to you after conventional testing showed "everything is normal" despite significant symptoms.

The Functional Approach

Functional testing looks beyond pathology to identify dysfunction‚Äîthe space between optimal health and diagnosable disease. It asks not just "Is there disease?" but "How well are these systems functioning?"

Functional vs. Pathological Ranges

Standard lab reference ranges are based on the population of people who get tested‚Äîoften including many with subclinical issues. Functional ranges are narrower, based on optimal function:

  • Example: Fasting glucose ‚Äî Lab normal: 70-99 mg/dL; Functional optimal: 75-86 mg/dL
  • Example: TSH ‚Äî Lab normal: 0.5-4.5 mIU/L; Functional optimal: 1.0-2.0 mIU/L
  • Example: Vitamin D ‚Äî Lab normal: 30-100 ng/mL; Functional optimal: 50-70 ng/mL

Clinical Application

A client with a TSH of 3.5 is "normal" by lab standards but may have subclinical thyroid dysfunction affecting gut motility. Functional ranges help identify these borderline cases.

Categories of Gut Health Testing

Comprehensive Stool Analysis

Tests like GI-MAP, GI Effects, and Comprehensive Stool Analysis evaluate the microbiome, pathogens, digestive markers, and inflammation. The cornerstone of gut health assessment.

Breath Testing

SIBO breath tests measure hydrogen and methane produced by bacterial fermentation. Essential for diagnosing small intestinal bacterial overgrowth.

Organic Acids Testing (OAT)

Urine metabolites indicate bacterial and fungal activity, nutrient status, neurotransmitter metabolism, and mitochondrial function. Provides metabolic insights.

Food Sensitivity Testing

IgG and other antibody panels. Useful with limitations‚Äîshould complement, not replace, elimination diets.

Intestinal Permeability Testing

Tests like lactulose/mannitol or zonulin can assess gut barrier function.

When to Test vs. When to Treat Empirically

  • Test when: Diagnosis will significantly change treatment, previous treatments failed, complex case, client wants objective data
  • Treat empirically when: Strong clinical suspicion, cost constraints, straightforward presentation, dietary/lifestyle interventions are low-risk

Important Consideration

Testing can be expensive and isn't always necessary. A thorough history and symptom assessment often provides enough information to begin interventions. Reserve testing for complex cases or when empirical treatment fails.

Scope of Practice

Your ability to order tests depends on your credentials and jurisdiction:

  • Direct access labs ‚Äî Many functional labs allow practitioners to order tests without being licensed clinicians
  • Client self-ordering ‚Äî Some labs allow clients to order their own tests
  • Medical collaboration ‚Äî Working with MDs or NDs who can order tests

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What is the difference between functional and pathological lab ranges?

Pathological ranges are designed to detect disease and are based on the population of people who get tested‚Äîoften including those with subclinical issues. Functional ranges are narrower, based on optimal function rather than just absence of disease. This helps identify dysfunction before it becomes pathology.

2. When might you treat empirically rather than testing first?

Treat empirically when: there's strong clinical suspicion, cost is a constraint, the presentation is straightforward, and interventions are low-risk (dietary changes, lifestyle modifications). Test when diagnosis will significantly change treatment, previous treatments failed, or the case is complex.
