üî¨ Key Terms

Intake Form

Comprehensive questionnaire completed before initial consultation, capturing health history, symptoms, and lifestyle.

Symptom Tracker

Ongoing tool for monitoring symptom frequency and severity over time during treatment.

Validated Questionnaire

A tool that has been scientifically validated for accuracy (e.g., Bristol Stool Scale, IBS-SSS).

Pattern Recognition

Using symptom clusters to identify likely underlying conditions without testing.

Why Questionnaires Matter

A well-designed questionnaire is your most powerful diagnostic tool. It provides systematic data collection, reveals patterns invisible in casual conversation, and guides clinical decisions about testing and treatment.

Components of Comprehensive Intake

Chief Complaint & Timeline

What brings them in? When did it start? What triggered it (food poisoning, stress event, antibiotic use)? How has it evolved? This establishes the "story" of their condition.

Symptom Inventory

Detailed GI symptoms: bloating, gas, reflux, constipation, diarrhea, pain location and timing. Also: systemic symptoms like fatigue, brain fog, skin issues, joint pain, mood changes that may connect to gut dysfunction.

Medical History

Previous diagnoses, surgeries (especially abdominal), current medications (PPIs, antibiotics, hormones), family history of GI conditions.

Dietary Assessment

Current eating patterns, known food reactions, dietary restrictions, supplement use, alcohol/caffeine intake, eating behaviors (speed, stress, timing).

Lifestyle Factors

Sleep quality, stress levels, exercise, work environment, relationship stress‚Äîall impact gut function significantly.

Key Diagnostic Questions

SIBO Indicators

  • "Does eating make your symptoms worse, especially bloating?"
  • "Do symptoms improve when you don't eat (fasting)?"
  • "Did symptoms start after food poisoning or stomach bug?"
  • "Do probiotics make you feel worse?"

Low Acid Indicators

  • "Do you feel heavy or full long after eating?"
  • "Do you burp frequently after meals?"
  • "Did reflux start after age 35-40?"
  • "Are you on proton pump inhibitors?"

Fungal/Candida Indicators

  • "Do you crave sugar or carbohydrates intensely?"
  • "Have you taken multiple courses of antibiotics?"
  • "Do you have recurring yeast infections?"
  • "Did symptoms start after antibiotic use?"

Validated Tools

Bristol Stool Scale

Visual scale of stool types 1-7. Essential for determining IBS subtype and tracking response to treatment. Include in all intake forms.

IBS-SSS (Severity Scoring System)

Validated questionnaire scoring IBS severity. Useful for baseline assessment and tracking improvement over time.

Creating Scoring Systems

Scoring allows objective tracking of progress:

  • Frequency scale: Never (0) - Rarely (1) - Sometimes (2) - Often (3) - Always (4)
  • Severity scale: None (0) - Mild (1) - Moderate (2) - Severe (3)
  • Total score: Calculate baseline total; repeat monthly to track improvement
  • Subsection scores: Score different symptom categories separately (GI, energy, mood)

Clinical Application

A client's total symptom score of 45 drops to 28 after 6 weeks of intervention‚Äîthis provides objective evidence of improvement, even if the client feels "about the same." Numbers don't lie and help both you and clients see progress.

Using Questionnaires to Guide Testing

Questionnaire patterns can determine testing priorities:

  • Strong SIBO pattern ‚Üí Prioritize breath testing
  • Multiple food reactions ‚Üí Consider comprehensive stool test for permeability/dysbiosis
  • Post-antibiotic onset ‚Üí Candida focus, consider OAT
  • Nutrient deficiency symptoms ‚Üí Blood chemistry panel

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What key questions help identify SIBO on intake?

Key SIBO questions: Does eating make symptoms worse (especially bloating)? Do symptoms improve when fasting? Did symptoms start after food poisoning/stomach bug? Do probiotics make you feel worse? "Yes" to these strongly suggests SIBO and prioritizes breath testing.

2. Why is using a scoring system valuable?

Scoring provides objective tracking‚Äîa total score dropping from 45 to 28 demonstrates improvement even if the client feels "about the same." Numbers reveal gradual progress that's easily missed subjectively. It validates your work, motivates clients, and provides documentation for outcomes.

3. How do questionnaire patterns help guide testing decisions?

Patterns from questionnaires direct which tests are most valuable: Strong SIBO pattern ‚Üí breath testing. Multiple food reactions ‚Üí stool test for permeability. Post-antibiotic onset ‚Üí consider OAT for Candida. Nutrient deficiency symptoms ‚Üí blood chemistry. This targeted approach is more cost-effective than broad testing.
