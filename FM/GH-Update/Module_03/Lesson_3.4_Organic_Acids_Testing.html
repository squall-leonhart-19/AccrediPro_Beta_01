What Is Organic Acids Testing?

The Organic Acids Test (OAT) is a urine test measuring over 70 metabolites produced by normal cellular function and microbial activity. It provides a metabolic snapshot that complements direct microbiome testing.

Why OAT Is Valuable

While stool tests show WHAT organisms are present, OAT shows WHAT THEY'RE DOING metabolically. You can have Candida on a stool test that's inactive, or miss overgrowth that's producing high levels of problematic metabolites. OAT captures metabolic activity.

Gut-Related Markers

Yeast/Fungal Markers

Arabinose

Primary marker for Candida/yeast overgrowth. Elevated arabinose indicates active yeast producing metabolites in the gut, even if stool culture is negative. One of the most clinically useful OAT markers.

Tartaric Acid

Another yeast metabolite. Elevated levels support a finding of fungal overgrowth, especially when combined with high arabinose.

Bacterial Markers

HPHPA (DHPPA)

Marker of pathogenic Clostridia bacteria. Elevated HPHPA is associated with neurotoxicity and can affect neurotransmitter function. Often elevated in dysbiosis.

Hippuric Acid

Bacterial metabolite that can indicate dysbiosis when elevated. Also affected by benzoate-containing foods.

Oxalate Markers

Oxalic Acid

Can be elevated due to yeast overgrowth (yeast produce oxalates), dietary intake, or metabolic issues. High oxalates are associated with kidney stones, vulvodynia, and fibromyalgia-like symptoms.

OAT vs. Stool Testing

Complementary Tests

Stool testing tells you WHAT organisms are present. OAT tells you about metabolic ACTIVITY. A Candida finding on stool might be colonization; elevated arabinose on OAT confirms active overgrowth producing metabolites. Best results come from using both together.

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What does elevated arabinose on an OAT indicate?

Elevated arabinose indicates active yeast/Candida overgrowth in the gut. It's a metabolite specifically produced by yeast, so its presence confirms metabolic activity‚Äînot just colonization. This is valuable because stool tests might miss yeast or show colonization without indicating activity level.

2. How does OAT complement stool testing?

Stool testing shows WHAT organisms are present (identity and quantity). OAT shows metabolic ACTIVITY‚Äîwhat those organisms are actually doing and producing. Together they provide a complete picture: is Candida just colonizing or actively producing problematic metabolites? Is dysbiosis affecting neurotransmitter metabolism?

3. What is HPHPA and what does elevation suggest?

HPHPA is a metabolite produced by pathogenic Clostridia bacteria. Elevated levels indicate Clostridia overgrowth and are associated with neurotoxicity‚ÄîHPHPA can affect neurotransmitter function, potentially contributing to mood, behavior, and cognitive issues. It's particularly watched in autism spectrum protocols.
