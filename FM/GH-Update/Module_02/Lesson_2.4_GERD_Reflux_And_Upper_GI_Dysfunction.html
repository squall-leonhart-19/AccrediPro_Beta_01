Understanding Reflux

While conventional medicine treats reflux as "too much acid," the functional perspective recognizes that reflux is often about acid in the wrong place, not too much acid.

The Functional View

Reflux is often the RESULT of dysfunction‚Äîlow acid, poor LES function, delayed gastric emptying, food intolerances, H. pylori, or SIBO. The question isn't "how do we suppress symptoms?" but "why is acid going where it shouldn't?"

The Low Acid Paradox

Low stomach acid (hypochlorhydria) often causes reflux-like symptoms‚Äîespecially common in those over 40.

How Low Acid Causes Reflux

  • Delayed gastric emptying ‚Äî Food sits longer, creating upward pressure
  • Bacterial fermentation ‚Äî Underdigested food ferments, producing gas
  • Poor LES signaling ‚Äî Adequate acid needed for proper LES closure
  • SIBO development ‚Äî Unsterilized food allows bacterial overgrowth

Clinical Pattern

The client on long-term PPIs with persistent reflux PLUS bloating, constipation, nutrient deficiencies, or SIBO likely never needed acid suppression‚Äîand PPIs created additional problems.

Signs of Low vs. High Acid

Low Acid Signs

Heavy feeling after eating, bloating soon after meals, undigested food in stool, burping within 30 minutes, multiple food sensitivities, brittle nails, reflux starting after age 35-40.

High Acid Signs

Burning that improves with food, symptoms worse on empty stomach, history of ulcers, worse with stress, younger age with symptoms.

Cascade Effects

Upper GI problems cascade throughout the digestive system:

  • Protein maldigestion ‚Äî Pepsin needs acid to activate
  • Mineral deficiency ‚Äî Iron, zinc, calcium require acid
  • B12 deficiency ‚Äî Acid releases B12 from food
  • Bile/pancreatic impairment ‚Äî Acid triggers CCK release
  • SIBO risk ‚Äî Unsterilized food feeds bacteria

Functional Approaches

  • Dietary modifications ‚Äî Eliminate triggers, smaller meals, chew thoroughly
  • Supporting digestion ‚Äî ACV, bitters, enzymes, Betaine HCl if appropriate
  • Mucosal support ‚Äî DGL, slippery elm, aloe vera, zinc carnosine

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. How can LOW stomach acid cause reflux symptoms?

Low acid causes delayed gastric emptying creating pressure, food fermentation producing gas, and poor LES signaling. Result: acid in wrong place, not too much acid.

2. Why might long-term PPI use lead to SIBO?

PPIs reduce stomach acid, which normally sterilizes food. Without this acid barrier, bacteria survive passage into small intestine where they overgrow, causing SIBO.
