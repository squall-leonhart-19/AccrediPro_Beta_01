Gut and Blood Sugar

Your microbiome affects how you respond to food. Research shows different people can have vastly different blood sugar responses to identical foods‚Äîand their microbiome predicts the response better than the food itself.

Personalized Glycemic Response

The Weizmann Institute study showed massive variation in glycemic responses to identical foods. Your microbiome composition predicts your response better than the food's stated glycemic index. This is why one diet doesn't work for everyone.

How Gut Affects Insulin Resistance

  • Metabolic endotoxemia: LPS from leaky gut triggers inflammation
  • SCFA production: Butyrate improves insulin sensitivity
  • Bile acid signaling: Microbiome modifies bile acids affecting glucose
  • GLP-1: Some bacteria promote GLP-1 production, improving glucose control

Gut-Based Blood Sugar Support

  • Increase fiber for SCFA production (30+ grams daily)
  • Heal leaky gut to reduce LPS-driven inflammation
  • Probiotics: some strains improve insulin sensitivity
  • Berberine: antimicrobial that also lowers blood sugar
  • Vinegar: acetic acid improves post-meal glucose response

1. What is metabolic endotoxemia?

Metabolic endotoxemia refers to chronic low levels of LPS (lipopolysaccharides) in the bloodstream due to leaky gut. Even small amounts of LPS trigger inflammation and promote insulin resistance, contributing to metabolic syndrome and type 2 diabetes risk.

2. How do SCFAs affect blood sugar?

Short-chain fatty acids, especially butyrate, improve insulin sensitivity. They also stimulate GLP-1 release (which improves glucose control), reduce inflammation, and feed beneficial gut bacteria. Fiber intake is key because bacteria produce SCFAs from fiber.
