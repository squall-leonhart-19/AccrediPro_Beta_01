Why Diets Fail

95% of diets fail long-term. The microbiome partially explains this: it adapts slowly and can push you back to previous eating patterns.

The Set Point Theory

Your body has a set point it defends. Rapid weight loss triggers metabolic adaptations. The microbiome "remembers" your previous diet and can influence regain. Sustainable change requires changing the microbiome itself‚Äîwhich takes 3-6 months.

Sustainable Approach

  • Gradual changes: 1-2 lbs/week maximum
  • Microbiome remodeling: High fiber, diverse plants
  • Protein priority: Prevents muscle loss, improves satiety
  • Time-restricted eating: 12-16 hour overnight fast
  • Address inflammation: Heal gut before aggressive calorie restriction

Maintenance Phase

  • Maintain new eating patterns for 3-6+ months to reset set point
  • Continue high fiber to maintain microbiome changes
  • Regular movement to preserve metabolic rate
  • Weekly check-ins to catch regain early
  • Stress management‚Äîcortisol drives regain

1. Why do 95% of diets fail long-term?

2. How long should new eating patterns be maintained to reset the set point?

New eating patterns should be maintained for 3-6+ months to allow the microbiome to fully adapt and help reset the body's set point. This is why quick diets don't work‚Äîthe microbiome hasn't had time to change.
