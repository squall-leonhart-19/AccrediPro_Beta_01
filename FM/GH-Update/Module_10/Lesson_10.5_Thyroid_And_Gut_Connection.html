The Gut-Thyroid Axis

How Gut Affects Thyroid

20% of inactive T4 converts to active T3 in the gut. Inflammation from leaky gut impairs conversion. Dysbiosis affects conversion enzymes. SIBO can impair nutrient absorption (selenium, zinc, iodine). Constipation from low thyroid worsens gut issues.

Hashimoto's and Gluten

  • Gliadin (gluten protein) structurally resembles thyroid tissue
  • Molecular mimicry: immune system attacks both
  • Strong association: Hashimoto's and celiac co-occur frequently
  • Gluten-free diet reduces thyroid antibodies in many patients

Gut-Based Thyroid Support

  • Gluten-free trial: Especially for Hashimoto's
  • Heal leaky gut: Reduces inflammation and autoimmune activity
  • Address SIBO: Common in hypothyroid due to slow motility
  • Optimize nutrients: Selenium, zinc, iodine, B12
  • Probiotics: Support conversion and reduce inflammation

1. What percentage of T4-to-T3 conversion happens in the gut?

About 20% of T4 to T3 conversion happens in the gut. This means gut health directly affects how much active thyroid hormone is available, regardless of what the thyroid gland produces.

2. Why is gluten problematic for Hashimoto's patients?

Gliadin (a protein in gluten) structurally resembles thyroid tissue. Through molecular mimicry, the immune system may attack both. Hashimoto's and celiac frequently co-occur, and gluten-free diets reduce thyroid antibodies in many patients.
