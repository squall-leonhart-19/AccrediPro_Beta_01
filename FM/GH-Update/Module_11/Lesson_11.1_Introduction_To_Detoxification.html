What is Detoxification?

Detoxification is the body's natural process of neutralizing and eliminating toxins. It's not a fad‚Äîit's a continuous biochemical process happening every second in your body.

Key Organs of Detoxification

Liver: Primary detox organ, runs Phase I and II. Gut: Eliminates toxins via bile and feces; Phase III. Kidneys: Filter blood, eliminate water-soluble toxins. Skin: Secondary elimination via sweat. Lungs: Eliminate volatile compounds.

The Three Phases of Liver Detox

  • Phase I (Functionalization): Cytochrome P450 enzymes modify toxins, sometimes creating more reactive intermediates
  • Phase II (Conjugation): Adds molecules to make toxins water-soluble (glutathione, sulfation, glucuronidation, methylation, acetylation, amino acid conjugation)
  • Phase III (Transport): Transports conjugated toxins out via bile or urine

Signs of Impaired Detoxification

  • Chemical sensitivity (perfumes, cleaning products)
  • Caffeine intolerance (slow caffeine metabolism)
  • Adverse reactions to medications
  • Chronic fatigue, brain fog
  • Hormonal imbalances (estrogen dominance)

1. What are the three phases of liver detoxification?

Phase I (Functionalization): CYP450 enzymes modify toxins. Phase II (Conjugation): Makes toxins water-soluble. Phase III (Transport): Moves conjugated toxins out via bile or urine.

2. What might caffeine intolerance indicate about detoxification?

Caffeine intolerance (jitters, insomnia from small amounts) indicates slow Phase I (CYP1A2) metabolism. This is a sign that Phase I detoxification pathways may be sluggish or genetically slower.
