Principles of Safe Detox

"Open the drains before you release the water." Ensure elimination pathways are working BEFORE mobilizing toxins.

The Safe Detox Sequence

1) Ensure regular bowel movements (1-2 daily). 2) Support bile flow. 3) Optimize hydration. 4) Ensure kidney function. 5) THEN add Phase I/II support. 6) Add binders as needed. 7) Go slow‚Äîespecially with sensitive clients.

Supporting Elimination Pathways

  • Bowels: Magnesium, fiber, hydration, movement
  • Bile: Bitter foods, ox bile, TUDCA, artichoke
  • Kidneys: Adequate hydration, avoid nephrotoxins
  • Skin: Sweating (sauna, exercise), dry brushing
  • Lymph: Movement, rebounding, massage

Recognizing Detox Reactions

  • Headaches, fatigue, brain fog worsening
  • Skin breakouts, rashes
  • Digestive upset, nausea
  • Mood changes, irritability
  • Flu-like symptoms

Managing Detox Reactions

  • Slow down: Reduce doses, take days off
  • Add binders: To catch mobilized toxins
  • Increase bowel movements: Magnesium, enemas if needed
  • Increase hydration: Supports kidney elimination
  • Epsom salt baths: Magnesium absorption, relaxation

1. What should be the first step in any detox protocol?

"Open the drains before releasing the water." Ensure elimination pathways are working FIRST: regular bowel movements (1-2 daily), bile flow, hydration, kidney function. Only THEN add detox support to mobilize toxins.

2. What should you do if a client has strong detox reactions?

Slow down: reduce doses, take days off. Add binders to catch mobilized toxins. Increase bowel movements with magnesium. Increase hydration. Use Epsom salt baths. The goal is gentle progress, not aggressive detox.
