The Estrobolome

The estrobolome is the collection of gut bacteria that metabolize estrogen. These bacteria directly influence circulating estrogen levels.

How the Gut Affects Estrogen

Estrogen is conjugated in the liver ‚Üí excreted in bile ‚Üí enters gut. Beta-glucuronidase from certain bacteria can break apart conjugated estrogen. Free estrogen is reabsorbed ‚Üí elevated circulating estrogen. High beta-glucuronidase = estrogen dominance.

Estrogen Dominance Signs

  • PMS, heavy periods, breast tenderness
  • Fibroids, endometriosis, fibrocystic breasts
  • Weight gain around hips and thighs
  • Mood swings, irritability
  • Increased risk of estrogen-positive cancers

Supporting Healthy Estrogen Metabolism

  • Address dysbiosis: Reduce beta-glucuronidase-producing bacteria
  • Calcium-D-glucarate: Inhibits beta-glucuronidase
  • DIM/I3C: Promotes healthy estrogen metabolites (2-OH pathway)
  • Fiber: Binds estrogen for elimination; 30+ grams daily
  • Regular bowel movements: Constipation recirculates estrogen
  • Cruciferous vegetables: Natural source of I3C/DIM

1. What is the estrobolome?

The estrobolome is the collection of gut bacteria that metabolize estrogen. These bacteria produce beta-glucuronidase which can break apart conjugated estrogen, allowing it to be reabsorbed. This directly influences circulating estrogen levels.

2. How does calcium-D-glucarate help with estrogen?

Calcium-D-glucarate inhibits beta-glucuronidase, the enzyme that breaks apart conjugated estrogen in the gut. By inhibiting this enzyme, estrogen stays conjugated and is eliminated in feces rather than being reabsorbed.
