Phase I Support

Phase I can create more reactive intermediates than the original toxin. It must be balanced with Phase II or oxidative damage occurs.

Phase I Nutrients

B vitamins (B2, B3, B6, B12, folate): Essential cofactors. Glutathione: Protects against Phase I oxidative damage. Iron, magnesium, zinc: Additional cofactors. Cruciferous vegetables: Indole-3-carbinol supports healthy estrogen metabolism.

Phase II Pathways and Support

  • Glutathione conjugation: NAC, glycine, glutamine, selenium
  • Sulfation: Sulfur foods (garlic, onions, eggs), MSM
  • Glucuronidation: Calcium-D-glucarate, citrus
  • Methylation: B12, folate, B6, methionine, betaine
  • Acetylation: B5 (pantothenic acid), vitamin C
  • Amino acid conjugation: Glycine, taurine, glutamine

Balancing the Phases

  • Fast Phase I + slow Phase II = accumulation of reactive intermediates
  • This causes oxidative damage, chemical sensitivity
  • Solution: Prioritize Phase II support before upregulating Phase I
  • Cruciferous vegetables upregulate both phases safely

1. Why is it dangerous to have fast Phase I and slow Phase II?

Fast Phase I + slow Phase II = accumulation of reactive intermediates. Phase I often creates MORE reactive compounds than the original toxin. Without Phase II keeping up, these reactive intermediates cause oxidative damage.

2. What nutrients support glutathione conjugation?

NAC (N-acetyl cysteine) provides cysteine for glutathione synthesis. Glycine and glutamine are the other amino acids needed. Selenium supports the glutathione enzyme system.
