L-Glutamine

L-glutamine is the most well-researched gut-healing nutrient. It's the primary fuel for enterocytes and is essential for maintaining the intestinal barrier.

How It Works

Enterocytes preferentially use glutamine (not glucose) for energy. Glutamine supports tight junction proteins that maintain intestinal barrier integrity. It also supports mucus production and immune function in the gut.

Therapeutic Dosing

  • General support: 3-5 grams daily
  • Active gut healing: 5-10 grams daily, divided doses
  • Intensive protocols: 10-20 grams daily for short periods (2-4 weeks)
  • Timing: Away from meals or in shakes/bone broth. Powder form is most economical at therapeutic doses.

Duration

Gut-healing protocols with L-glutamine typically run 4-12 weeks. Reassess symptoms and adjust. Some clients benefit from ongoing lower-dose maintenance (3-5g). High doses (>15g) are short-term intensive interventions.

Zinc Carnosine

Zinc carnosine (brand name: PepZin GI) has specific research on gut healing and H. pylori gastritis. It's more than just zinc‚Äîthe carnosine compound has its own benefits.

Mechanisms

Stabilizes the gastric mucosa. Supports tight junction integrity. Has anti-inflammatory effects locally. May inhibit H. pylori adhesion. Supports healing of gastric and intestinal ulcers.

Dosing

  • Standard: 75-150mg zinc carnosine daily (equivalent to 16-32mg elemental zinc)
  • Divided: Usually taken twice daily, 30 minutes before meals
  • Duration: 8-12 weeks for healing protocols

Collagen and Gelatin

Amino Acid Profile

Collagen provides glycine, proline, and hydroxyproline‚Äîamino acids specifically used for connective tissue repair, including the gut lining. Also provides glutamine. Can be obtained from bone broth or supplements.

Collagen Supplement Options

  • Collagen peptides: Hydrolyzed, dissolves in liquid, flavorless
  • Gelatin: Thickens when cold, good for cooking
  • Bone broth: Food-based, additional minerals
  • Dosing: 10-20g collagen peptides daily or 1-2 cups bone broth

Soothing Botanicals

DGL (Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice)

Licorice root with glycyrrhizin removed (prevents blood pressure elevation). Increases mucus production, protects gastric lining. 380-760mg chewed before meals. Especially good for heartburn and gastritis.

Slippery Elm

Mucilaginous bark that coats and soothes irritated mucous membranes. Mix powder in water or take capsules. 400-800mg 3x daily. Safe long-term for ongoing irritation.

Marshmallow Root

Similar mucilaginous properties to slippery elm. Soothes from mouth to colon. Often combined with slippery elm in formulas. 300-500mg 3x daily or as tea.

Aloe Vera

Inner leaf gel has anti-inflammatory and healing properties. Use products with aloin removed (laxative component). 50-200mg inner leaf gel standardized or 1-2 oz juice daily. Helps with reflux and IBD symptoms.

Sample Gut-Healing Protocol

  • Morning: L-glutamine 5g in water, collagen peptides 10g in coffee/smoothie
  • Before meals: Zinc carnosine 75mg, DGL chew
  • Evening: L-glutamine 5g, slippery elm tea or capsules
  • Duration: 8-12 weeks minimum
  • Reassess: Reduction in symptoms indicates healing; consider maintenance dosing

1. Why is L-glutamine considered the most important gut-healing nutrient?

2. What is unique about DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice)?

DGL has had glycyrrhizin removed‚Äîthe compound in regular licorice that can elevate blood pressure and cause potassium loss. This makes it safe for long-term use while maintaining licorice's gut-soothing benefits: increasing mucus production and protecting the gastric lining. It's especially effective for heartburn and gastritis.

3. What amino acids does collagen provide that support gut healing?

Collagen provides glycine, proline, hydroxyproline, and glutamine. These amino acids are specifically used for connective tissue repair, including the gut lining. Glycine also has calming effects. Collagen can be obtained from bone broth or collagen peptide supplements (10-20g daily recommended for gut healing).
