The 5R Framework

The 5R Framework provides a logical sequence for gut restoration. While not every client needs all phases, understanding the order prevents common mistakes.

1. Remove (2-8 weeks)

Remove inflammatory triggers (food sensitivities via elimination diet), pathogens (SIBO, Candida, parasites via antimicrobials), and stressors. This creates the clean environment for healing. You can't restore a gut that's still under assault.

2. Replace (Concurrent + Ongoing)

Replace what's missing for digestion: stomach acid (HCl), digestive enzymes, bile salts. Often starts during the Remove phase and continues long-term. Improves nutrient absorption immediately.

3. Reinoculate (After Remove, 1-3 months)

Restore beneficial bacteria with probiotics and prebiotics. Wait until after antimicrobial phase‚Äîdon't add probiotics while killing bacteria. Exception: S. boulardii and spore-based can be used during antimicrobials.

4. Repair (1-3 months)

Heal the gut lining with L-glutamine, zinc carnosine, collagen, soothing herbs. Can begin during Reinoculate or after. Some practitioners start Repair alongside Remove to support tolerance.

5. Rebalance (Ongoing)

Address lifestyle factors: stress management, sleep, meal timing, exercise. Prevent recurrence with prokinetics and ongoing maintenance. This phase continues indefinitely.

Sample Protocol Timeline: SIBO Case

Weeks 1-2: Assessment and Preparation

  • Complete intake, order breath test
  • Start dietary changes (low-FODMAP)
  • Begin digestive support (enzymes, HCl if indicated)
  • Introduce biofilm disruptors

Weeks 3-8: Remove Phase

  • Antimicrobial protocol (berberine, oregano, allicin)
  • Continue low-FODMAP diet
  • S. boulardii for gut support
  • Gentle gut repair can begin (L-glutamine, bone broth)

Weeks 9-12: Transition and Reinoculate

  • Stop antimicrobials, retest breath test at week 10
  • Begin multi-strain probiotic
  • Start FODMAP reintroduction
  • Intensify gut repair (zinc carnosine, collagen)
  • Begin prokinetic at bedtime

Weeks 13+: Repair and Rebalance

  • Continue gut repair protocol
  • Expand diet based on reintroduction results
  • Maintain prokinetic and meal spacing
  • Transition to maintenance probiotic dose
  • Address remaining lifestyle factors

Avoiding Protocol Stacking

Don't start 10 supplements on day one. Introduce 1-2 new elements per week maximum. If a reaction occurs, you'll know what caused it. If improvement occurs, you'll know what helped. Sequential introduction also reduces overwhelm for clients.

Common Sequencing Mistakes

  • Starting probiotics during active SIBO: Add fuel to the fire; wait until after antimicrobials
  • Skipping the Remove phase: Can't repair a gut that's still under attack
  • Intensive repair before clearing pathogens: Healing over infection doesn't work
  • Stopping prokinetics too soon: MMC support is often needed long-term to prevent recurrence
  • Not addressing root causes: Treating symptoms without fixing underlying motility, stress, or thyroid issues

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What are the 5 Rs in order?

Remove, Replace, Reinoculate, Repair, Rebalance. Remove inflammatory foods and pathogens first. Replace digestive factors. Reinoculate with probiotics after antimicrobials. Repair the gut lining. Rebalance lifestyle for long-term maintenance.

2. Why is protocol stacking problematic?

Starting too many supplements simultaneously makes it impossible to identify what's helping or causing reactions. If symptoms improve or worsen, you don't know why. It's also overwhelming for clients and expensive. Better: introduce 1-2 new elements per week maximum.

3. When should probiotics be introduced in a SIBO protocol?

After the antimicrobial (Remove) phase is complete‚Äîtypically during the Reinoculate phase. Starting standard probiotics during active SIBO adds more bacteria to an already overgrown small intestine. Exception: S. boulardii (yeast) and spore-based probiotics can be used during antimicrobial treatment.
