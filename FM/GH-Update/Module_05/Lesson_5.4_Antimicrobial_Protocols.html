üî¨ Key Terms

Herbal Antimicrobials

Botanical compounds with antibacterial, antifungal, or antiparasitic properties.

Die-Off (Herxheimer)

Temporary worsening of symptoms as pathogens die and release toxins.

Biofilm

Protective matrix pathogens create that shields them from antimicrobials.

Pulse Dosing

Alternating on/off periods of antimicrobials to prevent resistance and catch emerging organisms.

When Are Antimicrobials Indicated?

Antimicrobial protocols are appropriate when testing or clinical presentation indicates pathogenic overgrowth that won't resolve with diet alone.

  • Positive SIBO breath test
  • Stool test showing pathogens: H. pylori, Candida, parasites, pathogenic bacteria
  • OAT markers elevated: Arabinose (Candida), HPHPA (Clostridia)
  • Clinical pattern strongly suggesting infection with failed dietary intervention

Key Herbal Antimicrobials

Broad-Spectrum (Bacteria + Some Fungal)

Berberine

Found in goldenseal, Oregon grape, barberry. Effective against SIBO (comparable to rifaximin in studies). Also anti-inflammatory and blood sugar regulating. Dosing: 500mg 2-3x daily. Duration: 4-8 weeks for SIBO.

Oregano Oil

Contains carvacrol and thymol with potent antimicrobial activity. Effective against bacteria, yeast, and some parasites. Emulsified forms better tolerated. Dosing: 50-200mg 2-3x daily. Can be irritating‚Äîuse with food.

Allicin (Garlic Extract)

Stabilized form of garlic's active compound. Effective against methane SIBO (IMO), bacteria, yeast. Also has cardiovascular benefits. Dosing: 450-900mg daily. Look for "allicin yield" on label.

Antifungal Focus

Caprylic Acid

Medium-chain fatty acid from coconut. Specifically targets Candida cell walls. Well-tolerated. Dosing: 500-1000mg 3x daily with meals. Works synergistically with other antifungals.

Undecylenic Acid

Antifungal fatty acid, particularly effective against Candida. Often combined with caprylic acid. Dosing: 150-250mg 3x daily. May cause GI upset initially.

Antiparasitic Focus

Wormwood (Artemisia)

Classic antiparasitic herb, particularly for intestinal parasites. Contains artemisinin. Not for long-term use. Dosing: 200-300mg 2x daily. Cycle: 2 weeks on, 1 week off. Contraindicated in pregnancy.

Black Walnut Hull

Contains juglone with antiparasitic and antifungal properties. Often combined with wormwood and cloves in traditional formulas. Dosing: 500mg 2-3x daily.

Die-Off Reactions

When pathogens die, they release toxins causing temporary symptom worsening: fatigue, headache, nausea, skin eruptions, flu-like symptoms, mood changes. Manage by: reducing antimicrobial dose temporarily, supporting detox pathways (binders, water, liver support), going slowly ("low and slow" approach). Severe die-off may indicate too-aggressive protocol.

Protocol Structure

Typical SIBO Protocol

Biofilm disruptor phase (1-2 weeks) ‚Üí Combined antimicrobial phase (4-8 weeks) ‚Üí Repair/rebuild phase (4-8 weeks) ‚Üí Retest breath test to confirm clearance.

Typical Candida Protocol

Biofilm phase (2 weeks) ‚Üí Antifungal rotation (6-12 weeks, rotating agents every 2-3 weeks to prevent adaptation) ‚Üí Probiotic restoration ‚Üí Dietary maintenance.

Biofilm Disruptors

  • NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine): 600-900mg 2x daily on empty stomach
  • Biofilm-disrupting enzymes: Nattokinase, serrapeptase, lumbrokinase
  • EDTA: Chelates minerals pathogens use to build biofilms
  • Timing: Start biofilm disruptors 1-2 weeks before antimicrobials for better penetration

Rotation Strategy

For persistent or Candida protocols, rotate antimicrobial agents every 2-4 weeks. This prevents pathogen adaptation and addresses organisms with different sensitivities. Example: Oregano oil weeks 1-2 ‚Üí Berberine weeks 3-4 ‚Üí Allicin weeks 5-6 ‚Üí Repeat cycle.

1. What is a die-off (Herxheimer) reaction and how should it be managed?

Die-off is temporary symptom worsening as pathogens die and release toxins‚Äîfatigue, headache, nausea, skin issues, mood changes. Manage by: reducing antimicrobial dose temporarily, supporting detox (binders, water, liver support), and going "low and slow." Severe die-off indicates too-aggressive protocol‚Äîslow down.

2. Why are biofilm disruptors used before antimicrobials?

Pathogens create protective biofilm matrices that shield them from antimicrobials. Biofilm disruptors (NAC, enzymes, EDTA) break down these protective structures, allowing antimicrobials to penetrate and reach the organisms. Starting disruptors 1-2 weeks before antimicrobials improves treatment effectiveness.

3. Which herbal antimicrobial is particularly effective for methane SIBO (IMO)?

Allicin (stabilized garlic extract) is particularly effective for methane-dominant SIBO (now called IMO‚ÄîIntestinal Methanogen Overgrowth). It targets methane-producing archaea. Dose: 450-900mg daily. Look for products that list "allicin yield" on the label for standardization.
