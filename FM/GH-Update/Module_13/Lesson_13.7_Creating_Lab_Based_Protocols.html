From Labs to Action

Labs don't provide protocols‚Äîyou do. The skill is translating multiple data points into prioritized, sequenced interventions.

Interpretation Process

1. Identify key abnormalities. 2. Group by system (digestion, pathogens, inflammation, etc.). 3. Prioritize based on severity and interconnection. 4. Match interventions to findings. 5. Sequence based on 5R framework.

Matching Interventions to Markers

  • Low elastase: Digestive enzymes with meals
  • Low sIgA: Mucosal support (colostrum, SBI)
  • High beta-glucuronidase: Calcium-D-glucarate, probiotics
  • Candida on stool + high arabinose: Antifungal protocol
  • Positive H. pylori: Triple therapy or herbal protocol

Prioritization Framework

  • First: Address active pathogens (parasites, significant overgrowth)
  • Second: Support digestion (enzymes, HCl, bile)
  • Third: Address inflammation and permeability
  • Fourth: Rebalance microbiome (probiotics, prebiotics)
  • Ongoing: Address nutrient deficiencies throughout

Retest Timing

  • Stool test: 3-6 months after treatment complete
  • SIBO breath test: 2-4 weeks after antimicrobial phase
  • OAT: 3-6 months after protocol
  • Blood chemistry: 3-4 months for trending

‚úì Check Your Understanding

1. What should be addressed first when labs show pathogens AND low enzymes?

Address pathogens first (Remove phase of 5R). However, you can support digestion with enzymes concurrently. The key is not delaying pathogen clearance. Full repair comes after the Remove phase.

2. When should you retest SIBO breath test after treatment?

Retest SIBO breath test 2-4 weeks after completing the antimicrobial phase. This allows time for gases to normalize but catches any remaining overgrowth before it regrows. If positive, another round may be needed.
