Key Stool Test Sections

Comprehensive stool tests assess the microbiome, pathogens, digestion, and inflammation. Each section tells a different part of the story.

Major Test Categories

1. Microbiome analysis (diversity, keystone species). 2. Pathogens (bacteria, yeast, parasites). 3. Digestion markers (elastase, fat, meat fibers). 4. Inflammation (calprotectin, lactoferrin, sIgA). 5. Intestinal health (zonulin, occludin, beta-glucuronidase).

Microbiome Markers

  • Diversity score: Higher is generally better; low = dysbiosis
  • Akkermansia: Keystone species; low common in metabolic issues
  • Faecalibacterium: Anti-inflammatory; low in IBD
  • Firmicutes:Bacteroidetes ratio: Elevated in obesity
  • Proteobacteria: Elevated = dysbiosis marker

Pathogen Findings

  • H. pylori: Virulence factors matter (CagA, VacA)
  • Candida species: Some is normal; overgrowth if symptoms
  • Parasites: Always pathological if found
  • Opportunistic bacteria: Context-dependent

Digestion and Inflammation

  • Pancreatic elastase: Low = pancreatic insufficiency; enzymes needed
  • Fecal fat: High = malabsorption; bile issue or enzyme issue
  • Calprotectin: Elevated = intestinal inflammation; rule out IBD if very high
  • sIgA: Low = poor mucosal immunity; high = active infection

1. What does low pancreatic elastase indicate?

Low pancreatic elastase indicates pancreatic insufficiency‚Äîthe pancreas isn't producing enough digestive enzymes. This leads to malabsorption, especially of fats and proteins. Digestive enzyme supplementation is usually needed.

2. What does elevated calprotectin indicate?

Elevated calprotectin indicates intestinal inflammation. Mildly elevated may suggest infection or food reactions. Very high levels (>250) require ruling out IBD (Crohn's, ulcerative colitis). It's a sensitive marker that responds quickly to treatment.
