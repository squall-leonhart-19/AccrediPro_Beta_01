Pricing Psychology

Clients pay for transformation, not time. Value-based pricing focuses on outcomes, not hourly rates.

Value > Time

Trading time for money limits income. Package your expertise into outcomes. Clients want results‚Äîpackage accordingly. Premium pricing attracts committed clients. Undercharging attracts uncommitted clients.

Package Structures

  • Single session: Initial consult or one-off (limit these)
  • 3-month program: Basic gut reset or specific protocol
  • 6-month program: Comprehensive gut restoration
  • VIP intensive: High-touch, accelerated program
  • Maintenance: Ongoing support after primary program

What to Include

  • Initial comprehensive assessment
  • Lab test interpretation (costs may be separate)
  • Customized protocol and meal plans
  • Follow-up sessions (frequency varies by package)
  • Email/messaging support between sessions
  • Educational materials and resources

Pricing Ranges (Examples)

  • Single session: $150-300
  • 3-month program: $1,500-3,000
  • 6-month program: $2,500-5,000
  • VIP intensive: $5,000-10,000+
  • Rates vary by location, experience, and niche

1. Why is value-based pricing better than hourly rates?

Trading time for money limits income potential. Clients pay for transformation and outcomes, not hours. Value-based pricing focuses on results delivered. Premium pricing attracts more committed clients who value the service.

2. Why should you limit single session offerings?

Single sessions limit your ability to create real results (gut healing takes time). They trade time for money. They attract less committed clients. Packages create better outcomes and more sustainable income.
