The Consultation Framework

A structured consultation builds trust and ensures thorough assessment. Clients feel heard when you follow a systematic process.

Consultation Flow

1. Welcome and rapport building. 2. Review intake forms and health history. 3. Deep-dive questioning. 4. Physical assessment if applicable. 5. Education and findings discussion. 6. Recommendations and next steps. 7. Q&A and commitment.

Comprehensive Health History

  • Chief complaint: What brought them to you today?
  • Symptom timeline: When did it start? What makes it better/worse?
  • Medical history: Diagnoses, surgeries, hospitalizations
  • Medications/supplements: Everything they take
  • Family history: Relevant genetic patterns
  • Lifestyle: Diet, sleep, stress, exercise, relationships

Gut-Specific Questions

  • Bowel frequency, consistency (Bristol scale), pain
  • Bloating: timing, triggers, severity
  • Food reactions and known sensitivities
  • Antibiotic history and past gut infections
  • History of H. pylori, SIBO, Candida, parasites
  • Stress and gut-brain symptoms connection

Setting Expectations

  • Explain your approach and philosophy
  • Discuss realistic timelines (gut healing takes time)
  • Clarify scope of practice and what you can/can't do
  • Set communication boundaries (when you're available)
  • Discuss investment and package options

1. Why is a structured consultation process important?

A structured consultation builds trust, ensures thorough assessment (nothing missed), makes clients feel heard and understood, positions you as professional, and creates consistency across all client interactions.

2. What gut-specific questions should always be asked?

Bowel habits (frequency, consistency, pain), bloating (timing, triggers), food reactions, antibiotic history, past gut infections (H. pylori, SIBO, Candida, parasites), and stress-gut connection patterns.
