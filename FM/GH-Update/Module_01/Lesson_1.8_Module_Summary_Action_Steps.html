Lesson 1.1: Introduction to Gut Health & The Microbiome

The gut is far more than a digestive organ‚Äîit's home to trillions of microorganisms that influence immunity, mood, hormones, and metabolism. The G.U.T.S. Method (Gather, Understand, Treat, Sustain) provides a systematic framework for addressing gut issues.

Lesson 1.2: Understanding Dysbiosis

Dysbiosis (microbial imbalance) occurs in three forms: loss of beneficial organisms, overgrowth of opportunistics, and loss of diversity. It progresses through five stages from initial insult to disease manifestation. Early intervention produces the best outcomes.

Lesson 1.3: Intestinal Permeability (Leaky Gut)

Tight junctions control gut permeability. When compromised by NSAIDs, gluten, stress, or dysbiosis, substances leak through triggering inflammation. Zonulin is the master regulator‚Äîelevated levels indicate increased permeability.

Lesson 1.4: The Gut-Brain Axis

The gut and brain communicate through neural, hormonal, immune, and microbial pathways. The gut produces 95% of serotonin and contains 500 million neurons. Mental health conditions often have gut origins.

Lesson 1.5: The Gut-Immune Connection

70-80% of immune cells reside in the gut (GALT). The microbiome trains immune function and regulates tolerance. The "triad of autoimmunity" includes genetics, triggers, AND intestinal permeability.

Lesson 1.6: The Gut-Hormone Connection

The gut produces and metabolizes hormones. The estrobolome regulates estrogen levels‚Äîdysbiosis can cause estrogen dominance. 20% of T4 to T3 conversion occurs in the gut. Stress and gut health form a bidirectional feedback loop.

Lesson 1.7: Recognizing Gut Dysfunction Patterns

Key patterns include: Inflammatory Cascade, Multiple Food Sensitivity, Upper GI Dysfunction, SIBO/SIFO, and Gut-Brain. Pattern recognition guides assessment and protocols. Red flags require medical referral.
