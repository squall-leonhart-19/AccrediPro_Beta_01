Defining Your Practice Vision

Your practice should reflect your passion and expertise. The most successful practitioners specialize rather than generalize.

Key Questions

What gut conditions excite you most? What population do you connect with? What's your unique background or story? Where do you want to practice (online, in-person, hybrid)? What income and lifestyle do you want?

Practice Models

  • Solo practitioner: Full control, all responsibility
  • Group practice: Shared overhead, referral opportunities
  • Virtual practice: Low overhead, geographic freedom
  • Hybrid: Combine in-person and virtual
  • Within existing practice: Add gut health to your current work

Identifying Your Ideal Client

  • Demographics: Age, gender, location, income level
  • Psychographics: Values, motivations, frustrations
  • Health concerns: Specific conditions you help with
  • Where they are: Social media, forums, specific communities

Niche Examples

  • SIBO specialist for women with chronic bloating
  • Gut health for autoimmune conditions
  • Pediatric digestive wellness
  • Gut-brain connection for anxiety
  • Sports performance and gut optimization

1. Why is niching important for practice success?

Niching helps you stand out, become the go-to expert in your area, attract ideal clients who are specifically looking for your expertise, charge premium prices, and create targeted marketing that resonates deeply with your audience.

2. What are the key components of defining your ideal client?

Demographics (age, gender, location, income), Psychographics (values, motivations, frustrations), specific health concerns they have, and where they spend time online or in-person so you can reach them.
