The Case She Presents

Jennifer's Client Case

What Jennifer Tells You:

"I had a client, Maria, 44, who came to me with fatigue and digestive issues. I did an intake, recommended she cut out gluten and dairy, take probiotics, and come back in 4 weeks. But she emailed me saying she feels worse ‚Äî more bloated than before. Did I do something wrong?"

What You Need to Explore:

  • Did Jennifer assess for contraindications to probiotics (SIBO, histamine issues)?
  • Was the elimination too aggressive without proper guidance?
  • Did she set realistic expectations about the timeline?
  • Is there a scope of practice issue here?

Your Teaching Approach

Key Points to Cover with Jennifer

1

Normalize the Learning Experience

"This is exactly why case review is valuable. Every practitioner has cases that don't go as expected. It's how we learn."

2

Teach the 'Die-Off' Concept

Explain that probiotics can cause temporary worsening if there's dysbiosis. This isn't failure ‚Äî it's information.

3

Review Assessment Depth

Gently explore whether the initial intake was thorough enough. Did she ask about SIBO symptoms? Stool history?

4

Build Her Clinical Reasoning

Instead of giving the answer, ask: "What might be causing this reaction? What would you do differently next time?"

Your Feedback Dialogue

How to Deliver Constructive Feedback

Start with Validation
"Jennifer, I really appreciate you bringing this to me. The fact that you're questioning and wanting to learn tells me you're going to be an excellent practitioner. Let's look at this together."
Ask Before Telling
"Before I share my thoughts, what's YOUR instinct about what might be happening? What did your gut tell you when you read Maria's email?"
Teach the Principle
"One thing I learned early on: when someone gets worse on probiotics, it's often a sign of SIBO or significant dysbiosis. The 'good bugs' are fighting the 'bad bugs' and it can get messy before it gets better. This is actually valuable clinical information."
Empower Next Steps
"What would you like to do next with Maria? I'm here to support you, but I want you to develop your own clinical thinking. What feels right to you?"
