### Sarah (Coach) â€” post after 28 min

great question Cheryl and yes it absolutely matters which type they have ðŸ’•

here's the breakdown:

**Hydrogen-dominant SIBO** (most common):
- typically causes more diarrhea, urgency, gas
- responds well to herbal antimicrobials like oregano, berberine
- usually clears faster with treatment

**Methane-dominant SIBO** (technically IMO - Intestinal Methanogen Overgrowth):
- causes more constipation and bloating
- harder to treat, needs specific antimicrobials that target archaea (different organisms than bacteria)
- often needs longer treatment protocols
- allicin (garlic extract) and specific herbals work better here

**Hydrogen + Methane** (mixed type):
- need to address both populations
- usually treat hydrogen first, then methane
- requires more complex herbal combinations

this is why testing matters. if you just guess and give someone berberine when they have methane-dominant, it might not work well and they'll think nothing helps

the test results literally tell you which protocol to use

make sense?
