### Sarah (Coach) â€” post after 35 min

not a dumb question at all Lisa! this is actually one of the most important concepts to understand ðŸ’•

so yes there are tests:
- CRP (C-Reactive Protein) is the most common one
- ESR (sed rate) 
- sometimes homocysteine or ferritin can give clues too

but here's the thing... a lot of people with chronic low-grade inflammation have "normal" labs because the reference ranges are so wide

so we also look at symptoms:
- fatigue that doesn't go away with sleep
- brain fog
- joint pain that moves around
- skin issues
- digestive problems
- mood issues

when someone has several of these plus elevated markers, that's when we're pretty confident it's chronic inflammation

you'll learn more about this in the lab interpretation module but you're asking exactly the right questions! ðŸŒ¿
