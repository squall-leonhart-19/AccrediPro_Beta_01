2. The Post-Traumatic Growth Inventory (PTGI)

The PTGI, developed by Tedeschi and Calhoun, is the gold standard for measuring growth. As a N.A.R.C. Coach, you will adapt this inventory to focus on the specific liberation from narcissistic enmeshment. We measure growth across five distinct pillars:

PTG Pillar Narcissistic Abuse Application Metric of Success
New Possibilities Pursuing interests previously forbidden or mocked by the narcissist. Number of autonomous "firsts" in the last 30 days.
Relating to Others Moving from trauma-bonding to secure, reciprocal attachments. Identification and rejection of "Red Flags" within 3 interactions.
Personal Strength The realization: "If I survived that, I can survive anything." Reduction in "freeze" response during conflict.
Spiritual Change A deeper connection to purpose or a sense of universal justice. Shift from "Why me?" to "What is this calling me to become?"
Appreciation of Life Finding joy in simple, peaceful, "boring" moments without chaos. Daily score of "Nervous System Calm" (1-10 scale).

3. Resilience Quotient (RQ) Assessment

Resilience in the context of narcissistic abuse is specifically the capacity to maintain emotional homeostasis in the face of "Hoovering" or "Smear Campaigns." We measure the RQ by assessing four domains: Emotional Regulation, Impulse Control, Causal Analysis, and Self-Efficacy.

4. Values-Congruence Testing

During narcissistic abuse, the survivorâ€™s values are often "colonized" by the abuser. Recovery requires the discovery of the N.A.R.C. Values Compass. Values-Congruence Testing measures the gap between what a client says they value (e.g., "Integrity," "Peace") and their actual behavior.

A high gap (Incongruence) often indicates residual "FOG" (Fear, Obligation, Guilt). A low gap indicates the "Claim Your Power" phase has been successfully integrated. We use a 7-day "Values Audit" where the client logs every major decision and rates its alignment with their top 3 core values.

5. Relational Intelligence (RI) Markers

Relational Intelligence is the objective ability to vet new connections without the interference of "Betrayal Blindness." We track RI through three specific markers:

  • The Pacing Marker: Can the client intentionally slow down the "Love Bombing" phase of a new friendship or romance?
  • The Boundary Test Marker: Does the client issue a small "No" early in a relationship to observe the other person's reaction?
  • The Pattern Recognition Marker: Can the client articulate the specific cluster of behaviors (e.g., lack of empathy, grandiosity) rather than just "feeling" something is wrong?

Key Assessment Terms

Refractory Period

The duration of time between a traumatic trigger and the return to a regulated nervous system state.

Values-Congruence

The degree of alignment between an individual's core beliefs and their daily actions/decisions.

Relational Discernment

The cognitive ability to distinguish between healthy, safe individuals and predatory or toxic personality types.

6. The 'Claim Your Power' Milestone Tracker

As the final stage of the N.A.R.C. Methodâ„¢, "Claiming Your Power" must be evidenced by objective milestones. Coaches should look for these four "Sovereignty Signifiers":

  1. Indifference: The shift from hatred or obsession to a lack of emotional charge regarding the narcissist.

  4. Future-Orientation: Planning for a life that is no longer defined as a "reaction" to the abuse.

Key Takeaways

  • Growth is Quantifiable: Use the PTGI to show clients that their struggle has produced measurable psychological dividends.
  • Resilience is a Muscle: Track the "Refractory Period" to prove to clients that their nervous system is becoming more robust.
  • Values are the Compass: Recovery is complete when daily actions align with rediscovered internal values, not external demands.
  • RI is the Shield: Moving from hypervigilance to Relational Intelligence is the hallmark of the "Claim Your Power" phase.
