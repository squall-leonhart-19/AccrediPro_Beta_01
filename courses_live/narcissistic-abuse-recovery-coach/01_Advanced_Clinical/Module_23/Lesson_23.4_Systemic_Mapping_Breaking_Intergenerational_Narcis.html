Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Enabler" often considered more difficult for a survivor to process than the Narcissist themselves?

The Enabler represents a betrayal of the protective bond. While the Narcissist is the source of the wound, the Enabler is the one who allowed the wound to happen while claiming to be the "safe" parent, creating profound cognitive dissonance.

2. What is the primary difference between a standard genogram and a Narcissistic Systemic Map?

A standard genogram tracks biological and legal relationships; a Systemic Map tracks the flow of narcissistic supply, the location of the Scapegoat/Golden Child roles, and the "Legacy of Silence" (family secrets).
