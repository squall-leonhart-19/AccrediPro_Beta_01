4. Navigating Crisis Hoovering: Health & Family Emergencies

Perhaps the most difficult challenge for a survivor is the "Emergency Hoover." This occurs when the abuser (or a flying monkey) reaches out regarding a genuine or fabricated crisis: a death in the family, a terminal diagnosis, or a major accident. The survivor is often paralyzed by the fear of being "heartless" or "unforgiving."

Coaching Insight: The Guilt Trap

Remind the client that No Contact is not a punishment for the narcissist; it is a protection for the survivor. A health crisis does not magically grant an abuser the capacity for empathy or safe interaction. In fact, narcissists in physical pain often become more abusive as their sense of control diminishes.

When coaching a client through a crisis hoover, utilize the Sovereignty Decision Matrix:

  1. Verification: Is the crisis verified by a neutral, objective source?
  2. Necessity: Does the survivor's presence actually change the outcome of the crisis? (Usually, the answer is no).
  3. Safety: What is the psychological cost of breaking No Contact?
  4. Alternative: Can a message be sent through a legal representative or a neutral third party if communication is absolutely necessary?
