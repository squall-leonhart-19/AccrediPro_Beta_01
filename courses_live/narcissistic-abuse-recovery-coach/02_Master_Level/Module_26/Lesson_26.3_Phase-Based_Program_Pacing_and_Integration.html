4. Phase-Based Bridge Exercises

The most dangerous point in a recovery program is the transition between phases, specifically from Acknowledge (Wounds) to Rebuild (Identity). If a client stays in the "Acknowledge" phase too long, they may develop a "victim identity" or become stuck in ruminative loops. If they move to "Rebuild" too fast, they are building on a foundation of unaddressed trauma.

The "Values-Wound Bridge" Exercise

To bridge these phases, use an exercise that connects a specific wound to a future value.
Example: "Because I was never allowed to speak (Wound), I now value Radical Authenticity (Value)."

  • The Grief-to-Growth Pivot: A 15-minute somatic exercise where the client locates grief in the body and then visualizes that same energy being "re-commissioned" for a personal boundary.
  • The Introject Audit: Identifying the "Narcissistic Introject" (the abuser's voice) and replacing it with the "Sovereign Voice" before moving into Identity work.

5. Crisis Support Architectures

A premium recovery program must account for the "Extinction Burst" of the narcissist. When a client begins to heal, the abuser often escalates manipulation (Hoovering, Smear Campaigns, or Legal Threats). A 2023 survey found that 68% of survivors experienced a significant hoovering attempt during their first 3 months of active recovery coaching.

Your program should include "In-Case-Of-Emergency" (ICE) modules that are triggered by specific events:

  • The Hoover Shield: A pre-recorded module and worksheet for when the "I've changed" email arrives.
  • The Deposition Prep: A somatic grounding protocol for legal proceedings.
  • The Smear Campaign Counter-Measure: A linguistic strategy for maintaining "Grey Rock" while others are being manipulated.

1. Physiological Markers

2. Cognitive Markers (The "Fog" Index)

Key Takeaways

  • Pacing is Strategy: Move fast in "Navigate" (Stabilization) and slow in "Acknowledge" (Processing).
  • Gaps are Growth: Use 7-14 day Integration Windows to allow the brain to re-wire.
  • Bridge the Gap: Use "Values-Wound" exercises to transition from victimhood to agency.
  • Anticipate the Burst: Build crisis modules for hoovering and legal escalations.
