4. Closing the Coaching Container: Mastery of Termination

For a survivor of narcissistic abuse, "goodbyes" are often associated with discard, abandonment, or trauma. The termination of the coaching relationship is a critical therapeutic opportunity to model a Secure Attachment ending. This is where the coach must resist the "Hero Archetype" and fully hand the power back to the client.

The Termination Protocol

Effective termination involves four distinct phases:

  1. Review of Progress: Quantifying the shift from the N (Navigate) phase to the C (Claim) phase.
  2. Addressing the "Final Fear": Discussing the client's anxiety about being "on their own" without the coach's mirror.
  3. The Handover: Formally acknowledging the client as the "Primary Expert" of their own life.
  4. The Open-Door Boundary: Establishing if and how future "tune-up" sessions may occur, preventing a sense of abrupt abandonment.
