1. The Mechanics of Intuition Loss

In the context of narcissistic abuse, the loss of intuition is not an accidental byproduct; it is a survival adaptation. Throughout the devaluation phase (Module 2), the survivor was conditioned to believe that their perception of reality was flawed, crazy, or "sensitive." This chronic gaslighting creates a physiological severance between the brain and the body's warning systems.

When a client says, "I don't trust my gut anymore," they are describing a state of Interoceptive Dissociation. As a coach in the Rebuild phase, your task is not to "give" the client intuition, but to help them clear the "static" that prevents them from hearing the signal that is already there.

The Somatic Gut-Check

Intuition is housed in the Enteric Nervous System (ENS), often called the "Second Brain." To help a client re-tune, use the following coaching exercise:

  1. The Baseline: Ask the client to think of something undeniably true (e.g., "My name is [Name]"). Ask them where they feel that "truth" in their body.
  2. The Falsehood: Ask them to say something undeniably false (e.g., "I am a 10-foot tall giraffe"). Notice the subtle shiftâ€”often a tightening or a "rejecting" feeling in the solar plexus.
  3. The Neutral: Practice this with low-stakes daily decisions to strengthen the neural pathways between the gut and the conscious mind.

4. Small 'Self-Trust' Experiments

Self-trust is a muscle that has atrophied. You cannot ask a client to trust their intuition on a new romantic partner if they don't yet trust their intuition on what to eat for lunch. We utilize Micro-Decisional Experiments to rebuild this foundation.

The Three-Tier Experiment Framework:

  • Tier 1: Sensory Preferences. Choosing music, food, or clothing based solely on immediate "pull" without analyzing "Is this the best choice?"
  • Tier 2: Boundary Micro-Tests. Saying "no" to a small request (e.g., "Can you grab me a water while you're up?") if the internal feeling is one of tiredness, even if it feels "mean."
  • Tier 3: Social Intuition. Observing a stranger or a new acquaintance and writing down a "gut feeling" about them, then waiting to see if evidence supports it over timeâ€”without acting on the feeling yet.

5. The 'Evidence of Truth' Log

The "Evidence of Truth" log is a cognitive-behavioral tool designed to bridge the gap between Phase 2 (Acknowledge) and Phase 3 (Rebuild). It serves as a physical record that the client's intuition is, in fact, functional.

Instruct the client to create a four-column log:

  1. The Situation: (e.g., "Met a new neighbor today.")
  2. The Gut Feeling: (e.g., "Felt a flash of heat/unease when he mentioned his ex.")
  3. The Rationalization: (e.g., "I'm just being paranoid because of my trauma.")
  4. The Eventual Evidence: (e.g., "Two weeks later, found out he was banned from the neighborhood pool for aggressive behavior.")

Over time, this log provides undeniable data that the client's "internal compass" is not broken; it was simply being ignored.

This is likely Anxiety (Trauma Response). Intuition is typically neutral, quiet, and present-oriented, whereas anxiety is loud, frantic, and future-oriented (fear of a repeat of past abuse).

2. What is the primary purpose of "Micro-Decisional Experiments" in Phase 3 of recovery?

The purpose is to rebuild the "self-trust muscle" in low-stakes environments, allowing the client to reconnect with their Enteric Nervous System signals without the paralysis of high-stakes consequences.
