1. Meta & Google Strategy: Navigating Policy Restrictions

For the N.A.R.C. Recovery Methodâ„¢ practitioner, paid advertising is a double-edged sword. While it offers unparalleled reach, platforms like Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google have stringent policies regarding "Personal Hardship" and "Sensitive Interests."

Directly targeting "victims of domestic abuse" or using "Are you being gaslighted?" in ad copy often triggers immediate account flags or bans. At Level 4, your strategy must pivot from direct call-outs to interest-based proxies and educational value. Instead of targeting the trauma directly, we target the symptoms and the search for solutions.

Compliance Alert

Meta's policy on "Personal Attributes" prohibits ads that assert or imply that you know a user's personal characteristics (including physical or mental health). Phrases like "I know you're struggling with a narcissist" can lead to account suspension. Use "The 3 signs of toxic relationship dynamics" instead.

  • Broad Interests: Self-help, Psychology, Mindfulness, Personal Development.
  • Life Events: Recently Divorced, Separated, or "Moving" (often correlates with leaving toxic environments).

2. Budget Allocation: The TOFU to BOFU Pipeline

A common mistake for coaches is spending 100% of their budget on "Book a Call" ads. In the recovery niche, trust is the primary currency. A cold lead who has just realized they are in a trauma bond is rarely ready to book a $5,000 coaching package from a single ad.

60% TOFU (Awareness)
30% MOFU (Nurture)
10% BOFU (Conversion)

The 60/30/10 model ensures a constant stream of new survivors entering your ecosystem. Top of Funnel (TOFU) ads should promote your Lead Magnet (e.g., the "Boundaries Architecture Checklist" discussed in L4). Middle of Funnel (MOFU) retargets those who engaged with educational content. Bottom of Funnel (BOFU) invites warm leads to a Discovery Call or Webinar.

5. Scaling from 1-on-1 to High-Ticket Groups

Level 4 coaching is defined by leverage. You cannot scale a 1-on-1 model indefinitely without burnout. Group programs allow you to impact more survivors while increasing your hourly effective rate. Check Your Understanding

1

Why is a high ad frequency (e.g., seeing an ad 10 times a day) potentially harmful in the narcissistic abuse recovery niche?

Survivors of narcissistic abuse are often hyper-vigilant and have had their boundaries violated via stalking or "hoovering." High-frequency ads can mimic this behavior, triggering a "fight-or-flight" response and causing the survivor to associate your brand with intrusion rather than safety.
2

What is the primary benefit of interest-based proxies (e.g., targeting "Mindfulness") over direct targeting of "abuse victims"?

Interest-based proxies ensure compliance with platform policies (Meta/Google) regarding sensitive health data, while also reaching survivors who may be seeking solutions (like stress relief or psychology) but haven't yet labeled their experience as "abuse."

Key Takeaways

  • Compliance is King: Use educational, solution-oriented copy to avoid account bans on Meta and Google.
  • The 60/30/10 Rule: Diversify your budget to build trust before asking for a high-ticket sale.
  • Ethics in Retargeting: Prioritize survivor safety and privacy in your digital footprint.
  • Know Your Numbers: Track CAC and CPL to ensure your "scaling engine" is profitable and sustainable.
  • Leverage Group Models: Transitioning to group coaching is the only way to scale impact without sacrificing personal well-being.
