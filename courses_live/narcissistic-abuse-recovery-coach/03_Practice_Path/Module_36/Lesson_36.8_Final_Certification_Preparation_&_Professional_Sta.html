3. AccrediPro Code of Conduct & Ethical Standards

Professionalism in the recovery field is the shield that protects both the coach and the client. As a certified N.A.R.C. Master Coach, you are bound by the AccrediPro Professional Standards.

Standard Area Professional Requirement Ethical Violation
Scope of Practice Coaching for recovery and future-focused growth. Diagnosing personality disorders or treating acute psychosis.
Confidentiality Strict data protection and privacy protocols. Sharing client "success stories" without written consent or identifying details.
Dual Relationships Maintaining clear coach-client boundaries. Engaging in romantic or business partnerships with active/past clients.
Informed Consent Clear disclosure of methods, fees, and limitations. Using "experimental" techniques without disclosure or clinical backing.

Critical Warning: Mandatory Reporting

Certified coaches must adhere to local jurisdiction laws regarding mandatory reporting. While coaching is confidential, threats of self-harm, harm to others, or child/elder abuse must be reported to the appropriate authorities immediately. Failure to do so results in immediate revocation of certification.

5. The Post-Certification Roadmap

Once the digital certificate is issued, your journey as a business owner and community leader begins. The transition from student to practitioner involves three key pillars:

1. Credentialing & Insurance

Secure Professional Liability Insurance (Errors & Omissions). Even with the best training, insurance is a non-negotiable standard for professional practice. Register your business entity (LLC/Ltd) to separate personal and professional assets.

2. Digital Sovereignty

Establish a secure, HIPAA-compliant (or GDPR-compliant) client portal. Your website should clearly state your "Scope of Practice" and include a "Not Therapy" disclaimer to manage expectations and legal risk.

3. Community Leadership

Narcissistic abuse recovery is often a lonely path for survivors. As a Master Coach, you are encouraged to lead support groups or contribute to public awareness. Use the AccrediPro "Certified Badge" to build trust and authority.

6. Final Q&A: Integration of the N.A.R.C. Method‚Ñ¢

Throughout this course, we have covered the spectrum from neurobiology to business scaling. The most common final question is: "How do I know when to refer a client out?"

Referral is a sign of professional strength, not failure. You should refer to a licensed mental health professional (LMHP) if the client exhibits:

  • Active suicidal ideation with intent or plan.
  • Severe Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID) symptoms.
  • Acute substance use disorders that prevent cognitive engagement.
  • Inability to perform basic activities of daily living (ADLs).
