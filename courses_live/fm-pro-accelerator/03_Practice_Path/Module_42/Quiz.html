DEPTH Practice Path - Income Mastery Quiz

1. **Case Study:** Dr. Anya Sharma, a solo FM practitioner, consistently generates $300,000 in gross revenue annually but struggles with high overhead (65%). She desires to increase her net income while maintaining her current patient volume. Which of the following strategies would be the MOST effective INITIAL step to improve her income mastery?

Correct Answer: B. A thorough expense audit is the most logical first step. Reducing overhead immediately and directly impacts net income. Options A and C involve risk and upfront investment, while option D could lead to decreased patient satisfaction and potentially burnout.

2. A functional medicine practice is considering offering a comprehensive gut health program priced at $2500. Market research suggests a potential demand of 20 patients per month. However, the program requires hiring a health coach at a salary of $60,000/year and purchasing specialized lab testing equipment costing $10,000 upfront. What is the break-even point (in number of patients) for this program in the FIRST year?

Correct Answer: A. Break-even point = (Fixed Costs) / (Price per unit - Variable cost per unit). Fixed Costs = $60,000 (coach salary) + $10,000 (equipment) = $70,000. Assuming lab costs are included in the $2500 price per unit and no other variable costs are present, the break-even point = $70,000 / $2500 = 28 patients.

3. You are negotiating a contract with a new supplement vendor. They offer a 20% discount on all orders over $5,000, with net 60 payment terms. Your current vendor offers a 15% discount on all orders, but requires net 30 payment terms. Which factor is MOST critical to consider when evaluating these offers?

Correct Answer: B. While all factors are important, cash flow is paramount. Longer payment terms (net 60) can significantly improve cash flow, allowing for reinvestment in the practice. A small discount difference may be outweighed by the cash flow benefits.

4. Which of the following Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) is MOST directly related to the financial health and sustainability of a functional medicine practice?

Correct Answer: C. Average revenue per patient directly impacts the practice's profitability. While the other options are important for marketing and patient retention, they don't directly translate to financial health as effectively as revenue per patient.

5. A practitioner is considering hiring a virtual assistant (VA) to handle administrative tasks. The VA's hourly rate is $25, and the practitioner estimates they will need 20 hours of VA support per week. What is the MOST important consideration when determining the ROI of hiring this VA?

Correct Answer: B. The ROI is directly tied to the practitioner's increased billable hours. The cost of the VA must be offset by the revenue generated from the time the practitioner gains by delegating tasks.

6. A functional medicine practice is experiencing a high rate of no-shows for initial consultations. Which of the following strategies is MOST likely to reduce no-show rates and improve revenue?

Correct Answer: B. A non-refundable deposit creates a financial commitment, making patients more likely to attend. While reminders are helpful, a deposit adds a significant incentive.

7. You are considering offering group coaching programs in addition to individual consultations. What is the PRIMARY benefit of group coaching from an income mastery perspective?

Correct Answer: C. Group coaching allows you to see multiple patients simultaneously, increasing your hourly revenue potential. This is a key principle of leveraging your time for increased income.

8. A functional medicine practice is consistently booked 3 months in advance. Which of the following strategies would be MOST effective in maximizing revenue without increasing the number of appointments?

Correct Answer: B. High demand (being booked 3 months out) indicates price inelasticity. Increasing prices will likely increase revenue without significantly affecting patient volume.

9. **Scenario:** Dr. Patel wants to implement a recurring revenue model in her practice. Which of the following offerings would BEST align with this goal?

Correct Answer: B. A subscription model provides consistent, predictable income each month. The other options are transactional and do not guarantee recurring revenue.

10. A practice is considering investing in a new piece of diagnostic equipment costing $50,000. The equipment is expected to generate an additional $20,000 in revenue per year but will also require $5,000 per year in maintenance and supplies. What is the payback period (in years) for this investment?

Correct Answer: C. Payback period = Initial Investment / Annual Net Cash Flow. Annual Net Cash Flow = $20,000 (revenue) - $5,000 (expenses) = $15,000. Payback period = $50,000 / $15,000 = 3.33 years.
