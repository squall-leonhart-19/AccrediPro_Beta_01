Learn with Others

Peer groups provide mutual support, diverse perspectives, and accountability.

Benefits of Peer Practice

Why Peers Matter

  • Multiple perspectives: Others see what you miss
  • Normalization: Everyone struggles with similar things
  • Accountability: Show up prepared
  • Community: You're not alone

Peer Group Structure

Effective Format

  • Size: 3-6 people ideal
  • Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly
  • Duration: 60-90 minutes
  • Format: Rotating case presenter

Session Agenda

  • Check-in (5 min): Quick wins and challenges
  • Case 1 (25 min): Present, discuss, feedback
  • Case 2 (25 min): Second presenter
  • Wrap-up (5 min): Key takeaways, next meeting

Peer Feedback Guidelines

Give Great Peer Feedback

  • Be supportive: We're all learning
  • Be specific: Not just "good job"
  • Ask questions: Before giving advice
  • Share experiences: What worked for you

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Peers provide accountability and perspective
  • 3-6 people, weekly meetings, rotating presenter
  • Structured agenda keeps it productive
  • Supportive feedback: We're all learning together
