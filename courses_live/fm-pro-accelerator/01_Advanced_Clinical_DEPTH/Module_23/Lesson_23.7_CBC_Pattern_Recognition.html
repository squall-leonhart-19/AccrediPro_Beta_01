Pattern: Microcytic Anemia (Low MCV <80)

  • Most common cause: Iron deficiency
  • Confirm with: Ferritin, iron, TIBC, transferrin saturation
  • Other causes: Thalassemia trait, chronic disease

Pattern: Macrocytic Anemia (High MCV >100)

  • Common causes: B12 deficiency, folate deficiency
  • Confirm with: Serum B12, methylmalonic acid, homocysteine
  • Other causes: Hypothyroidism, liver disease, alcohol, medications

White Blood Cell Differential

Neutrophils (40-70% of WBC)

  • Elevated: Bacterial infection, inflammation, stress, steroids
  • Low: Viral infection, autoimmunity, bone marrow suppression, severe infection

Lymphocytes (20-40% of WBC)

  • Elevated: Viral infection, chronic infection, autoimmunity
  • Low: Immune suppression, HIV, severe stress, early infection

Eosinophils (1-4% of WBC)

  • Elevated: Parasites, allergies, asthma, eczema, drug reactions
  • Eosinophils >500 cells/ŒºL = investigate parasites, allergy burden

Basophils (<1% of WBC)

  • Rarely significantly elevated
  • May increase with allergic conditions, hypothyroidism

Monocytes (2-8% of WBC)

  • Elevated: Chronic infection, recovery from acute infection, autoimmunity
  • Persistently elevated: Investigate underlying chronic condition

Clinical Pattern Recognition

Pattern: Neutrophil:Lymphocyte Ratio Elevated

  • Calculate: Neutrophil % √∑ Lymphocyte %
  • Optimal: 1.5 - 2.5
  • Elevated (>3): Stress, infection, inflammation, cardiovascular risk marker

Pattern: Low WBC + Low Neutrophils + High Lymphocytes

  • Classic viral infection pattern
  • May also indicate: Chronic viral load (EBV, CMV reactivation)

Pattern: High RDW + Normal MCV

  • May indicate early nutritional deficiency before MCV changes
  • Also seen in: Mixed deficiencies, chronic inflammation

Key Terms

MCV

Mean Corpuscular Volume - average size of red blood cells; low = microcytic, high = macrocytic

RDW

Red cell Distribution Width - variation in RBC size; elevated indicates mixed or developing deficiency

Neutrophil:Lymphocyte Ratio

Calculated ratio used as inflammatory and cardiovascular risk marker
