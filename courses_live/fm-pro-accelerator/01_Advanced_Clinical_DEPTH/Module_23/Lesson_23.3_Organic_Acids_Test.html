The Metabolic Snapshot

The Organic Acids Test measures metabolic byproducts in urine that reveal cellular function you can't see any other way: mitochondrial health, neurotransmitter metabolism, yeast overgrowth, nutrient status, and detox capacity. It's like running diagnostics on the body's operating system.

Mitochondrial Markers

Citric Acid Cycle Metabolites

  • Elevated citrate, isocitrate, Œ±-ketoglutarate: May indicate amino acid insufficiency, B vitamin deficiency
  • Elevated succinate, fumarate, malate: CoQ10 deficiency, mitochondrial dysfunction
  • Clinical correlation: Fatigue, exercise intolerance, brain fog
  • Support: B vitamins, CoQ10, carnitine, ribose

Ketone & Fatty Acid Markers

  • Elevated adipate, suberate: Impaired fatty acid oxidation, carnitine deficiency
  • Elevated ethylmalonate: Often carnitine or CoQ10 insufficiency
  • Support: L-carnitine, CoQ10, B2 (riboflavin)

Low HVA + Low VMA + Low 5-HIAA = Classic "neurotransmitter depletion" pattern seen in chronic stress, burnout, and long-term illness. These clients need amino acid precursor support, B vitamins, and stress recovery‚Äînot just antidepressants.

Microbial/Yeast Markers

Yeast/Fungal Metabolites

  • Arabinose: Classic Candida marker
  • Tartaric acid: Yeast overgrowth indicator
  • Clinical correlation: Brain fog, sugar cravings, bloating, history of antibiotics
  • Protocol: Antifungal herbs, low-sugar diet, probiotic support

Bacterial Markers

  • HPHPA (Clostridia marker): Elevated in Clostridia overgrowth; affects dopamine
  • D-lactate: Bacterial overgrowth, often SIBO-related
  • Benzoate, hippurate: Bacterial metabolites; may indicate dysbiosis

Nutritional Markers

B Vitamin Status

  • Methylmalonate high: B12 deficiency (functional)
  • Formiminoglutamate high: Folate deficiency
  • Xanthurenate high: B6 deficiency
  • Note: These may be elevated even when serum levels look "normal"

Antioxidant/Detox Status

  • Pyroglutamate high: Glutathione depletion (oxidative stress)
  • 2-methylhippurate: Xylene exposure (environmental toxin)
  • Orotate high: Ammonia clearance issues, possible urea cycle stress

When to Order OAT

Ideal OAT Candidates

  • Unexplained chronic fatigue despite normal bloodwork
  • Mood disorders not responding to conventional treatment
  • Suspected yeast overgrowth or chronic dysbiosis
  • Children with developmental/behavioral concerns
  • Complex cases needing metabolic assessment

Key Terms

Organic Acids

Metabolic byproducts measured in urine that reflect cellular function and biochemical pathways

HVA

Homovanillic acid - dopamine metabolite indicating dopamine production/turnover

Arabinose

Organic acid produced by Candida/yeast; elevated indicates fungal overgrowth

Methylmalonate

Elevated indicates functional B12 deficiency even if serum B12 appears normal
