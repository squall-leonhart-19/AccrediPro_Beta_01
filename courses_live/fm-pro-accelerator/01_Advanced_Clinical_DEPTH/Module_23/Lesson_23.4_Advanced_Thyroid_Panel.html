Pattern Recognition

Pattern 1: Classic Hypothyroid

  • TSH: Elevated (>3.0)
  • FT4: Low-normal or low
  • FT3: Low
  • Action: Medical referral for medication; support conversion with selenium, zinc

Pattern 2: Poor T4‚ÜíT3 Conversion

  • TSH: Normal (1-2)
  • FT4: Normal or high-normal
  • FT3: Low (below 2.8)
  • Causes: Stress, inflammation, nutrient deficiencies (selenium, zinc, iron)
  • Action: Address root cause; T3 medication may eventually be needed

Pattern 3: High Reverse T3

  • RT3: Elevated (>15-20)
  • FT3:RT3 ratio: <0.2 (problematic)
  • Causes: Chronic stress, inflammation, illness, caloric restriction, toxins
  • Action: Address underlying stressor; RT3 blocks T3 at receptor

Pattern 4: Hashimoto's (Autoimmune)

  • TPO Antibodies: Elevated (>34, often hundreds)
  • TgAb: May also be elevated
  • TSH/T4/T3: May be normal initially, fluctuates over time
  • Action: Immune modulation, gluten-free trial, gut healing, selenium

The Conversion Equation

What Impairs T4‚ÜíT3 Conversion?

  • Nutrient deficiencies: Selenium, zinc, iron, iodine, B vitamins
  • Chronic stress: Cortisol impairs deiodinase enzyme
  • Inflammation: Cytokines block conversion
  • Gut issues: 20% of conversion happens in gut
  • Liver dysfunction: 60% of conversion happens in liver
  • Caloric restriction: Body down-regulates as survival mechanism

Support Conversion

  • Selenium: 200mcg daily (Brazil nuts or supplement)
  • Zinc: 15-30mg daily
  • Iron: Optimize ferritin to 70-90 ng/mL
  • Reduce inflammation: Anti-inflammatory diet, gut healing
  • Manage stress: Cortisol management protocols

Functional Ranges vs. Conventional

Why Functional Ranges Matter

  • Conventional ranges include sick population data
  • A TSH of 4.0 may be "normal" but absolutely not optimal
  • Many clients with TSH 2.5-4.5 have clear hypothyroid symptoms
  • FT3 is often the most clinically relevant marker for symptoms

Key Terms

Free T3

The active form of thyroid hormone that enters cells and drives metabolism

Reverse T3

Inactive form of T3 that blocks thyroid receptors; elevated in stress/illness

TPO Antibodies

Thyroid peroxidase antibodies - primary marker for Hashimoto's autoimmunity

T4‚ÜíT3 Conversion

Enzymatic process converting inactive T4 to active T3; requires selenium, zinc, iron
