The Silent Fire

Chronic low-grade inflammation underlies virtually every modern disease: autoimmunity, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, cancer, neurodegeneration, and depression. Inflammation markers tell you whether the fire is burning‚Äîand how hot.

hs-CRP Deep Dive

Cardiovascular Risk Stratification

  • <1.0 mg/L: Low cardiovascular risk
  • 1.0 - 3.0 mg/L: Moderate risk
  • >3.0 mg/L: High risk (investigate source)
  • >10 mg/L: Acute inflammation (infection, injury); retest when resolved

What Elevates hs-CRP?

  • Chronic infections (dental, sinus, gut)
  • Gut permeability ("leaky gut")
  • Obesity/visceral fat (adipose tissue is inflammatory)
  • Blood sugar dysregulation
  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Poor diet (processed foods, seed oils, sugar)
  • Chronic stress

Homocysteine: The Multi-Marker

Why Homocysteine Matters

  • Cardiovascular: Damages blood vessel walls
  • Neurological: Associated with cognitive decline, dementia
  • Methylation: High levels indicate poor methylation (B12, folate, B6 status)
  • Detox: Needed for glutathione production

Lowering Homocysteine

  • Methylfolate: 400-800mcg (not folic acid)
  • Methylcobalamin (B12): 1000-2000mcg
  • B6 (P5P): 25-50mg
  • Riboflavin (B2): Supports MTHFR function
  • Betaine (TMG): Alternative methylation pathway

Ferritin: Not Just Iron

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Distinction

Ferritin is an acute phase reactant‚Äîit rises with inflammation. A "normal" ferritin of 150 may actually indicate inflammation masking iron deficiency. If hs-CRP is elevated, ferritin may be falsely elevated. Check iron, TIBC, and transferrin saturation too.

Ferritin Interpretation

  • <30 ng/mL: Iron deficiency (even without anemia)
  • 70-100 ng/mL: Optimal for most
  • >150 ng/mL (women) / >200 (men): May indicate inflammation OR hemochromatosis
  • >300 ng/mL: Investigate iron overload or significant inflammation

Anti-Inflammatory Protocol Foundations

Diet Interventions

  • Eliminate processed foods, refined sugar, seed oils
  • Increase omega-3s (fatty fish, quality fish oil)
  • Colorful vegetables (polyphenols are anti-inflammatory)
  • Consider elimination of inflammatory triggers (gluten, dairy for some)

Supplement Considerations

  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): 2-4g combined daily for therapeutic effect
  • Curcumin: 500-1000mg (bioavailable form) twice daily
  • Vitamin D: Optimize to 50-70 ng/mL
  • SPMs (Specialized Pro-resolving Mediators): For resolution phase

Key Terms

hs-CRP

High-sensitivity C-reactive protein - primary measure of systemic inflammation

Homocysteine

Amino acid metabolite; elevated indicates poor methylation and cardiovascular/neurological risk

Acute Phase Reactant

Marker that rises in response to inflammation (ferritin, fibrinogen, CRP)
