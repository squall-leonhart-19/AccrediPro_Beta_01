Types of Group Programs

Format Options

  • Cohort-based: Fixed start/end dates, everyone progresses together
  • Evergreen: Ongoing, clients join anytime, self-paced with group calls
  • Hybrid: 1:1 kick-off + group calls + self-paced modules
  • Challenge: Short-term (5-21 days), specific outcome
  • Membership: Ongoing access, monthly fee

Group Size Considerations

  • 4-8 people: Intimate, high-touch, more interaction
  • 10-20 people: Good balance of scalability and connection
  • 20+ people: Requires more structure, less individual attention
  • Start small and grow‚Äîeasier to manage and iterate

Designing Your Curriculum

Curriculum Structure

  • Clear outcome: What transformation will they achieve?
  • Modules/weeks: Break into digestible chunks (typically 4-12 weeks)
  • Core content: Videos, workbooks, resources
  • Live calls: Q&A, coaching, hot seats
  • Community: Facebook group, Slack, Circle
  • Assignments: Implementation between modules

Common Mistakes

  • Too much content: Overwhelm kills completion‚Äîless is more
  • No implementation time: Content without action doesn't transform
  • Trying to cover everything: Niche down the topic
  • No community: Peer support is half the value

Pricing Groups

Pricing Strategy

  • Lower than 1:1: Accessible entry point
  • Value-based: What is the transformation worth?
  • Examples: 6-week group $400-800, 12-week intensive $1,200-2,400
  • Payment plans: Make it accessible (installments)
  • Beta pricing: Launch first round at 50% for testimonials

Launching Your Group

Launch Strategy

  • Warm audience: Offer to email list first
  • Webinar: Free training that leads to enrollment
  • Deadline: Clear start date creates urgency
  • Social proof: Use testimonials from beta or 1:1
  • Personal outreach: DM past clients or warm leads
