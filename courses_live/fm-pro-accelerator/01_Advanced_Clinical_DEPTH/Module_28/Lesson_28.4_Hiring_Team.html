When to Hire

Signs You're Ready

  • You're at capacity: Can't take more clients
  • Revenue supports it: Can afford help
  • Spending time on low-value tasks: Admin eating client time
  • Burnout risk: Working unsustainable hours
  • Growth ceiling: Can't grow without help

The "Buy Back Your Time" Mindset

  • Calculate your hourly rate: Target income √∑ billable hours
  • Identify tasks below your rate: Admin, social scheduling, email
  • Outsource those first: Pay $25/hr to free up $150+/hr work
  • Focus on revenue-generating activities: Client work, marketing, sales

What to Delegate First

First Hires for Practitioners

  • Virtual assistant (VA): Email, scheduling, admin tasks
  • Social media manager: Content posting, engagement
  • Tech support: Website, course platform, automations
  • Bookkeeper: Invoicing, expense tracking, taxes

Keep for Yourself

  • Client sessions (initially)
  • Content creation (your expertise)
  • Sales calls (until you train someone)
  • Vision and strategy

Types of Hires

Contractor vs. Employee

  • Contractor: Project-based, flexible, no benefits/taxes to manage
  • Employee: Dedicated, more control, legal requirements
  • Most solopreneurs start with contractors: Lower risk, easier
  • Consider agencies: For specialized work (design, ads)

Where to Find Help

  • VAs: Belay, Time Etc, Upwork, referrals
  • Social media: Agencies, Facebook groups for your niche
  • Tech: Fiverr, Upwork, specialized agencies
  • Referrals: Ask your network who they use

Hiring Process

Steps to Hire Well

  • Define the role: Exactly what tasks, hours, deliverables
  • Create job post: Be specific about requirements
  • Review applications: Look for relevant experience, attention to detail
  • Interview: Video call to assess fit and communication
  • Paid test project: Small paid task before committing
  • Trial period: 30-90 day trial with clear expectations

Managing Contractors

Management Best Practices

  • Clear expectations: Written SOPs for every task
  • Regular check-ins: Weekly sync, even if brief
  • Tools: Slack, Asana, Loom for async communication
  • Feedback: Praise publicly, correct privately, be specific
  • Trust but verify: Review work initially, build trust over time

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Hire when at capacity and admin is eating your time
  • First hires: VA, social media, tech support, bookkeeper
  • Contractors first: Lower risk, easier to start
  • Use paid test projects before committing
  • SOPs and clear expectations are essential for success
