Membership Models

Types of Memberships

  • Community-focused: Access to group, Q&As, peer support
  • Content library: Ongoing access to resources, templates, training
  • Coaching access: Monthly group calls, hot seats
  • Maintenance: For past clients‚Äîquarterly check-ins, support
  • Hybrid: Combination of above

Who Memberships Are For

  • Past clients: Who want ongoing support but don't need intensive work
  • Lower-budget audience: Who can't afford 1:1 or higher-ticket programs
  • DIYers: Who want resources and community, not hand-holding
  • Topic enthusiasts: Who want to stay current on a subject

What to Include

Membership Value Elements

  • Monthly live calls: Q&A, hot seat coaching, teaching
  • Community access: Facebook group, Slack, Circle
  • Resource library: Templates, guides, checklists
  • New content: Monthly training, challenges, themes
  • Bonuses: Discounts on other services, early access

Keeping It Sustainable

  • Don't over-deliver: Creates burnout and member overwhelm
  • Batch content: Create themes or monthly focuses
  • Community runs itself: Foster peer-to-peer support
  • Automate where possible: Email onboarding, resource delivery

Pricing Memberships

Pricing Strategies

  • Low-ticket: $19-49/month (high volume, lower touch)
  • Mid-tier: $47-97/month (balanced value and access)
  • Premium: $97-197/month (high touch, direct access)
  • Annual option: Offer discount for yearly prepay (10-17% off)

Retention: The Key to Memberships

Why Members Leave

  • Not using it (engagement problem)
  • No results (value problem)
  • Overwhelmed (too much content)
  • Financial (life circumstances)
  • Achieved goal (success, but not retained)

Retention Strategies

  • Strong onboarding: First 30 days critical for engagement
  • Quick wins: Immediate value in week 1
  • Community connection: Help them make friends
  • Progress tracking: Show them their wins
  • Regular touchpoints: Keep them engaged monthly
  • Renewal bonuses: Incentivize staying

When to Launch

Membership Readiness

  • You have an audience: Memberships need volume to work
  • Proven offers: Courses/programs that demonstrate demand
  • Content engine: You can create ongoing value
  • Time capacity: Monthly calls/community management
  • Start small: Founding member group, iterate before scaling

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Memberships = predictable monthly revenue
  • Types: Community, content library, coaching access, maintenance
  • Don't over-deliver: Sustainable for you, digestible for them
  • Retention is everything: Onboarding, engagement, quick wins
  • Wait until you have audience and proven offers before launching
