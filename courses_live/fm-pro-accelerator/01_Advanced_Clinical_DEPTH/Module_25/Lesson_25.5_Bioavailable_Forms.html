Not All Forms Are Created Equal

The form of a nutrient dramatically affects its absorption and effectiveness. Chelated minerals, methylated vitamins, and active forms can make the difference between a supplement that works and one that doesn't.

B Vitamins: Methylated Forms

Vitamin Preferred Form Basic Form Why It Matters
B12 Methylcobalamin, Adenosylcobalamin, Hydroxocobalamin Cyanocobalamin Methyl forms are active; cyano requires conversion and releases cyanide
Folate L-Methylfolate (5-MTHF), Folinic Acid Folic Acid 40-60% of population has MTHFR variants affecting folic acid conversion
B6 Pyridoxal-5-Phosphate (P5P) Pyridoxine HCl P5P is active form; pyridoxine requires liver conversion
B2 Riboflavin-5-Phosphate (R5P) Riboflavin R5P is active coenzyme form

Minerals: Chelated Forms

Mineral Preferred Form Basic Form Why It Matters
Magnesium Glycinate, Malate, Taurate, Threonate, Citrate Oxide, Carbonate Oxide is only 4% absorbed; glycinate is ~80% absorbed
Iron Iron Bisglycinate, Ferrous Fumarate Ferrous Sulfate Bisglycinate is gentler; less constipation and GI distress
Zinc Zinc Picolinate, Citrate, Glycinate Zinc Oxide, Sulfate Chelated forms absorbed 2-3x better
Calcium Calcium Citrate, Malate, MCHC Calcium Carbonate Citrate doesn't require stomach acid; better for elderly

Other Key Form Considerations

CoQ10: Ubiquinol vs. Ubiquinone

  • Ubiquinol: Active, reduced form; better absorbed, especially for 40+
  • Ubiquinone: Oxidized form; must be converted to ubiquinol
  • Recommendation: Ubiquinol for most clients; ubiquinone acceptable for young, healthy individuals

Omega-3: Forms and Sources

  • Triglyceride form: Natural form, best absorbed, but lower EPA/DHA per gram
  • Ethyl ester form: Concentrated, but lower absorption (take with fat)
  • Re-esterified triglyceride: Best of both‚Äîconcentrated and well-absorbed
  • Phospholipid form (krill): Well-absorbed but expensive, lower EPA/DHA

Vitamin D: D3 vs. D2

  • Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol): Preferred; raises levels 2x more effectively than D2
  • Vitamin D2 (Ergocalciferol): Plant-derived; less effective at raising levels
  • Vegan option: D3 from lichen is available

Vitamin E: Natural vs. Synthetic

  • d-alpha-tocopherol: Natural form; better retained in body
  • dl-alpha-tocopherol: Synthetic; half as biologically active
  • Mixed tocopherols + tocotrienols: Full spectrum; most complete

Curcumin: Absorption Matters

  • Standard curcumin: Poorly absorbed (~1%)
  • With piperine: 20x absorption increase
  • Phytosome (Meriva): 29x absorption
  • Theracurmin, Longvida: Highly bioavailable patented forms

Reading Labels for Quality

Red Flags on Labels

  • Folic acid instead of methylfolate
  • Cyanocobalamin instead of methylcobalamin
  • Magnesium oxide instead of chelated forms
  • "Proprietary blend" hiding amounts
  • Many artificial colors, flavors, sweeteners
  • DL-form (synthetic) of vitamin E

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Methylated B vitamins (methylfolate, methylcobalamin, P5P) for MTHFR and optimal absorption
  • Chelated minerals (glycinate, citrate) absorb 5-20x better than oxides
  • Ubiquinol over ubiquinone for CoQ10, especially 40+
  • Vitamin D3 over D2; natural vitamin E (d-) over synthetic (dl-)
  • Bioavailable curcumin requires special formulation (phytosome, piperine)
