Physiological Changes with Aging

Digestive Changes

  • Decreased stomach acid: Reduced B12, iron, calcium, and protein absorption
  • Slower motility: Increased constipation risk
  • Reduced enzyme production: May need digestive enzyme support
  • Decreased appetite: Risk of undernutrition

Metabolic Changes

  • Lower basal metabolic rate: But nutrient needs stay same or increase
  • Insulin resistance: Blood sugar management more critical
  • Decreased kidney function: Impacts drug/supplement clearance
  • Reduced vitamin D synthesis: Skin becomes less efficient

Sarcopenia: The Silent Epidemic

What is Sarcopenia?

  • Age-related loss of muscle mass and strength
  • Begins around age 30; accelerates after 60
  • Leads to: falls, fractures, loss of independence, increased mortality

Prevention and Treatment

  • Protein timing: 25-30g protein per meal (not just at dinner)
  • Leucine: 2.5g per meal triggers muscle protein synthesis
  • Resistance exercise: Essential‚Äîrefer to appropriate professional
  • Creatine: 3-5g daily shown to help preserve muscle in elderly
  • Vitamin D: Deficiency accelerates muscle loss

Polypharmacy Challenges

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Awareness

Many elderly clients take 5+ medications. Common drug-nutrient depletions: PPIs deplete B12/magnesium, statins deplete CoQ10, metformin depletes B12, diuretics deplete potassium/magnesium. Always review medication list!

Common Medication Interactions

  • Blood thinners (warfarin): Vitamin K consistency critical; watch supplements
  • Thyroid medication: Take away from calcium, iron, coffee
  • Blood pressure meds: Grapefruit interaction; potassium levels matter
  • Antibiotics: Give probiotics 2 hours apart

Cognitive Health Support

Brain-Protective Nutrition

  • Mediterranean/MIND diet: Best evidence for cognitive protection
  • Omega-3 (DHA): Critical for brain structure
  • B vitamins: B12, folate, B6 for homocysteine control
  • Antioxidants: Vitamin E, C, polyphenols
  • Blood sugar control: Diabetes significantly increases dementia risk

Lifestyle Factors

  • Social engagement: Loneliness accelerates cognitive decline
  • Sleep: Deep sleep clears brain toxins (glymphatic system)
  • Exercise: Best evidence for brain health after diet
  • Cognitive challenge: Learning new skills, not just crosswords

Practical Considerations

Making Nutrition Accessible

  • Smaller, frequent meals: If appetite is low
  • Nutrient-dense choices: Every bite counts when eating less
  • Soft textures: If dental issues present
  • Easy preparation: Pre-cut vegetables, rotisserie chicken, canned fish
  • Hydration focus: Thirst sensation decreases with age
