More Than Just a Logo

Your brand is how people feel about you before they ever meet you. A strong brand creates recognition, builds trust, and differentiates you from competitors. This lesson covers creating a cohesive brand identity.

üéØ What You'll Master

  • Defining your brand values and mission
  • Visual identity elements (colors, fonts, logo)
  • Creating a brand voice
  • Building brand consistency across platforms
  • Standing out through positioning

What Is a Brand?

Brand = Feeling + Perception

  • Not just visuals: Logo, colors, fonts are expressions of the brand‚Äînot the brand itself
  • Emotional response: How do people feel when they encounter you?
  • Promise: What do clients expect from working with you?
  • Reputation: What do people say about you when you're not in the room?

Brand Values and Mission

Core Values (3-5 max)

  • What principles guide your work?
  • Examples: Integrity, Evidence-based, Empowerment, Compassion, Holistic
  • These inform every decision‚Äîfrom marketing to client interaction
  • Clients resonate with your values, not just your services

Mission Statement

  • Formula: I help [who] [do what] so they can [transformation]
  • Example: "I help overwhelmed moms overcome chronic fatigue so they can show up fully for their families without sacrificing their own health."
  • Keep it specific, clear, and client-focused

Visual Identity

Color Palette

  • Primary color: Main brand color (1-2)
  • Secondary colors: Complementary (2-3)
  • Neutrals: For text, backgrounds
  • Psychology matters: Green = health, Blue = trust, Warm tones = approachable
  • Consistency: Use same hex codes everywhere

Typography

  • Heading font: Personality, character
  • Body font: Clean, readable
  • Limit to 2-3 fonts maximum
  • Free options: Google Fonts (Roboto, Open Sans, Playfair Display)

Logo

  • Simple is better: Must work at small sizes
  • Options: DIY (Canva), freelancer (Fiverr, 99designs), agency
  • Formats needed: Full color, one color, dark background, light background
  • Don't overthink: Logo matters less than consistency

Brand Voice

How You Sound

  • Personality traits: Warm, clinical, casual, authoritative, playful?
  • Vocabulary: Technical terms vs. plain language?
  • Tone: Encouraging, direct, nurturing, edgy?
  • Consistency: Same voice across all platforms

Voice Exercise

  • I am: [3 words describing your brand personality]
  • I am not: [3 words your brand avoids]
  • Example: "I am warm, evidence-based, empowering. I am not clinical, cold, preachy."

Positioning: Standing Out

Differentiation Strategies

  • Niche: Specific population or condition
  • Methodology: Your unique framework or approach
  • Values: What you stand for that others don't emphasize
  • Story: Your personal Why
  • Experience: Specific credentials or background

  • Brand = feeling + perception, not just visuals
  • Define 3-5 core values that guide everything
  • Visual identity: Colors, fonts, logo‚Äîkept consistent
  • Brand voice: How you sound across all communications
  • Position yourself through niche, methodology, story, or values
