Business Entity Options

Common Structures

  • Sole Proprietorship: Simplest, no separation from personal assets
  • LLC (Limited Liability Company): Personal asset protection, flexible taxation
  • S-Corporation: Potential tax benefits, more complexity
  • Most solopreneurs: LLC is often the sweet spot

Why LLC is Popular

  • Liability protection: Business debts don't affect personal assets
  • Pass-through taxation: Profits taxed once on personal return
  • Credibility: Looks more professional
  • Relatively simple: To set up and maintain

Essential Contracts

Client Agreement Must-Haves

  • Scope of services: What you will and won't do
  • Scope of practice disclosure: You're not a doctor
  • Payment terms: Fees, refund policy, late fees
  • Cancellation policy: Notice required, fees
  • Confidentiality: How you'll handle their information
  • Release of liability: Limited to services provided
  • Dispute resolution: How issues will be resolved

Other Important Contracts

  • Independent contractor agreement: When you hire help
  • Terms of service: For website and digital products
  • Privacy policy: Required by law (GDPR, CCPA)
  • Non-disclosure agreement (NDA): When sharing proprietary info

Scope of Practice

Stay in Your Lane

  • Know your state laws: What can you legally do?
  • Don't diagnose: Only licensed providers diagnose
  • Don't prescribe: Recommendations vs. prescriptions
  • Use proper language: "Suggest" vs. "prescribe"
  • Refer when needed: Know when to send to medical providers

Insurance

Coverage to Consider

  • Professional liability (E&O): Covers claims of negligence
  • General liability: Slip and fall, property damage
  • Cyber liability: Data breaches, cyber attacks
  • Product liability: If you sell supplements
  • Business property: Equipment, inventory

HIPAA and Privacy

Privacy Considerations

  • HIPAA: May apply if you work with providers or accept insurance
  • Even if not covered: Best practice to protect client data
  • Secure systems: HIPAA-compliant platforms when possible
  • Encrypted communication: Avoid regular email for sensitive info
  • Data retention: Know how long to keep records

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • LLC offers liability protection and simplicity for most
  • Client agreements: Scope, payment, cancellation, liability
  • Stay within scope: Don't diagnose or prescribe
  • Professional liability insurance: Non-negotiable
  • Protect client data even if HIPAA doesn't technically apply
