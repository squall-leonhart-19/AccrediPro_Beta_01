Time Blocking

Design Your Ideal Week

  • Client days: Stack sessions on specific days (e.g., Tue/Wed/Thu)
  • Admin days: Dedicate time for admin, documentation
  • CEO time: Work ON the business (strategy, planning)
  • Content days: Batch content creation
  • Off days: Non-negotiable time off

Sample Week Structure

  • Monday: CEO day (planning, strategy), content creation
  • Tuesday-Thursday: Client sessions
  • Friday: Admin, documentation, weekly review
  • Weekend: OFF or minimal (batch social)

Batching for Efficiency

What to Batch

  • Content creation: Write a month's posts in one sitting
  • Email: Check 2-3 times/day, not constantly
  • Documentation: Complete all notes end of day
  • Calls: Stack calls on specific days
  • Admin: Weekly admin block vs. daily interruptions

Context Switching Costs

  • Every switch takes 15-25 minutes to regain focus
  • Batching similar tasks reduces mental load
  • Deep work requires uninterrupted blocks (2+ hours)

Energy Management

Work With Your Energy

  • Peak hours: Do demanding work when energy is highest
  • Low energy: Admin, routine tasks, email
  • Know your patterns: Morning person? Night owl?
  • Protect peak time: Don't schedule low-value meetings then

Energy Builders

  • Sleep: Non-negotiable 7-8 hours
  • Movement: Exercise protects mental clarity
  • Nutrition: Practice what you preach
  • Breaks: Pomodoro or timed breaks
  • Nature/outdoors: Reset nervous system

Boundaries

Business Boundaries

  • Set office hours: Communicate when you're available
  • Response time: 24-48 hours is reasonable
  • No after-hours: Don't respond to client messages at night
  • Vacation: Schedule time off in advance, block calendar
  • Device-free time: Meal times, evenings, mornings

Avoiding Burnout

Warning Signs

  • Dreading client sessions
  • Exhaustion that doesn't resolve with rest
  • Cynicism about clients or work
  • Decreased effectiveness
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, insomnia, illness)

Prevention Strategies

  • Cap client load: Know your sustainable maximum
  • Regular time off: Weekly day off, quarterly breaks
  • Supportive community: Peers who understand
  • Supervision/coaching: Your own support
  • Diverse activities: Life outside of work

‚ú® Key Takeaways

  • Time block your ideal week with dedicated days for different activities
  • Batch similar tasks to reduce context switching
  • Work with your energy: Peak hours for demanding work
  • Set boundaries: Office hours, response time, time off
  • Burnout prevention is essential for sustainability
