What Is Case Stacking?

Case stacking is the strategic management of multiple co-existing health conditions by identifying their interconnections and addressing shared root causes‚Äîrather than treating each condition separately.

Traditional Approach vs. Case Stacking

Traditional: Client has Hashimoto's, IBS, and anxiety ‚Üí Three separate protocols, 15+ supplements, conflicting advice

Case Stacking: Recognize all three share gut dysfunction as a common driver ‚Üí One focused gut protocol addresses all three

The Economics of Case Stacking

Case stacking isn't just clinically superior‚Äîit's economically smart. Fewer supplements = lower client cost, higher compliance, and better margins on your packages. A 6-supplement protocol that addresses 4 conditions is more valuable than a 20-supplement protocol that overwhelms the client.

The LAYER Framework

Use LAYER to organize and prioritize stacked conditions:

L

List All Conditions

Start by listing every diagnosed condition, undiagnosed symptom pattern, and functional imbalance. Don't judge or prioritize yet‚Äîjust capture everything.

A

Analyze Connections

Draw lines between conditions that share triggers, mediators, or pathophysiology. Look for the "hubs" where multiple conditions connect.

Y

Yield the Priority

Identify the 1-2 upstream causes that, if addressed, would create the most downstream benefit. This is your therapeutic leverage point.

E

Execute Strategically

Design your intervention plan starting with the highest-leverage upstream targets. Document expected "downstream ripples" to track.

R

Reassess & Refine

At each check-in, reassess ALL conditions‚Äînot just the ones you targeted. Unexpected improvements validate your mapping; no improvement suggests revision.

  • Objective measure: Sleep tracking, symptom diary

  • Expected ripples: IBS resolves, Joint pain ‚Üì, Anxiety ‚Üì, Thyroid function improves
  • Testing: Consider GI-MAP if Phase 1 response incomplete

  • Target: Assess T4‚ÜíT3 conversion, support with selenium, retest labs
  • Expected ripples: Fatigue resolves, weight loss begins
  • Coordination: May need to discuss medication adjustment with prescriber

Maintenance Phase (Ongoing)

  • Focus: Lifestyle sustainability, stress management, prevent relapse
  • Check-ins: Monthly for 3 months, then quarterly

Sequencing Mistakes to Avoid

Don't: Try to address everything in Phase 1. Don't: Skip foundations to jump to "exciting" interventions like advanced testing. Don't: Add new interventions before assessing response to current ones. Each phase gives you diagnostic information for the next.

Common Condition Stacks

You'll see these patterns repeatedly. Knowing them accelerates your assessment:

The Autoimmune-Gut-Mood Stack

  • Conditions: Any autoimmune + IBS/SIBO + Anxiety or Depression
  • Shared driver: Gut permeability ("leaky gut") + Dysbiosis
  • Key intervention: Comprehensive gut repair, elimination diet, probiotic therapy
  • Common labs: Food sensitivity panel, GI-MAP, zonulin

The Stress-Sleep-Hormone Stack

  • Conditions: Insomnia + Weight gain/loss resistance + Irregular menstrual cycles or low libido
  • Shared driver: HPA axis dysfunction, cortisol dysregulation
  • Key intervention: Stress management, sleep hygiene, adaptogens, circadian rhythm optimization
  • Common labs: DUTCH Complete, salivary cortisol, fasting insulin

The Fatigue-Brain Fog-Pain Stack

  • Conditions: Chronic fatigue + Cognitive dysfunction + Widespread pain (fibromyalgia-like)
  • Shared drivers: Mitochondrial dysfunction, systemic inflammation, possible occult infection or toxin
  • Key intervention: Mitochondrial support, anti-inflammatory diet, toxin assessment
  • Common labs: OAT, inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, homocysteine), mold/mycotoxin panel

The Metabolic-Cardiovascular Stack

  • Conditions: Prediabetes/Insulin resistance + High blood pressure + Elevated cholesterol + Central obesity
  • Shared drivers: Insulin resistance, inflammation, often underlying gut dysfunction
  • Key intervention: Blood sugar management, dietary modification, movement, gut health
  • Common labs: Fasting insulin, HOMA-IR, hs-CRP, lipid particle analysis

Practical Application: Client Communication

How you explain case stacking to clients matters. They need to understand why you're not giving them 7 different protocols.

Client Script: Explaining the Approach

"I know you came in with a list of 7 things that feel like separate problems. But here's what I see: they're all connected like branches of the same tree. The conventional approach would give you 7 different specialists treating 7 branches. My approach is different‚Äîwe're going after the roots. When we heal the roots, many of those branches start improving on their own. That's why we're focusing on [gut health/sleep/stress] first. Don't be surprised when problems we're not even targeting start getting better."

Key Terms to Remember

Case Stacking

Managing multiple conditions as an interconnected system by addressing shared root causes rather than treating each separately.

LAYER Framework

List, Analyze connections, Yield priority, Execute strategically, Reassess & refine.

Therapeutic Leverage Point

The upstream target that, when addressed, creates the most downstream benefit across multiple conditions.

Downstream Ripples

Improvements in conditions you're not directly targeting, resulting from addressing upstream root causes.
