What's In Your Scope

Generally Within Scope (for non-licensed practitioners)

  • Education about lifestyle, nutrition, stress, sleep
  • Recommending OTC supplements within appropriate guidelines
  • Interpreting functional lab patterns (with appropriate disclaimers)
  • Designing lifestyle and wellness protocols
  • Coaching and accountability support

Requires Licensed Provider

  • Diagnosing medical conditions
  • Prescribing medication or changing medication
  • Ordering and interpreting diagnostic imaging
  • Invasive procedures
  • Managing acute medical conditions

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Know Your State/Country Laws

Scope varies by credential, state, and country. What's legal in one jurisdiction may not be in another. Consult your local regulations and consider liability insurance. When in doubt, refer.

Red Flags: When to Refer

Immediate Medical Referral

  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, sudden severe symptoms
  • Unintended weight loss >10% in 6 months
  • Blood in stool, black tarry stools
  • Suicidal ideation or severe mental health crisis
  • Any symptoms suggesting cancer, stroke, or emergency

Building Your Referral Network

Key Referral Partners to Cultivate

  • FM/Integrative physician (for prescriptions, complex cases)
  • Gastroenterologist (GI red flags)
  • Endocrinologist (complex thyroid, diabetes)
  • Therapist/psychologist (mental health component)
  • Physical therapist/chiropractor (structural issues)
  • Naturopath or acupuncturist (complementary modalities)
