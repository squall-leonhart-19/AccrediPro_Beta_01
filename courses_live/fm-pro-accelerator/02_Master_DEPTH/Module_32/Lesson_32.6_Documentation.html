If It Wasn't Documented, It Didn't Happen

Good documentation protects you, serves clients, and demonstrates professional standards. This lesson covers what to document, how to document efficiently, and record-keeping best practices.

The SOAP Note Format

S = Subjective

  • What the client reports: symptoms, concerns, changes since last visit
  • Use their words where possible ("reports feeling exhausted")

O = Objective

  • Data you observe or measure: lab results, tracking scores, observations
  • Fact-based, not interpretive ("Ferritin 28 ng/mL", "fatigue score 6/10")

A = Assessment

  • Your analysis: patterns identified, progress assessment, clinical reasoning
  • Connect subjective + objective to form conclusions

P = Plan

  • What you're recommending: protocol changes, tests, referrals, next steps
  • Document client agreement or concerns with plan

Record Retention

Best Practices

  • Retain client records minimum 7 years (check local requirements)
  • Use secure, encrypted storage for digital records
  • HIPAA compliance if in US (or equivalent in your jurisdiction)
  • Have clear policy in informed consent regarding records
