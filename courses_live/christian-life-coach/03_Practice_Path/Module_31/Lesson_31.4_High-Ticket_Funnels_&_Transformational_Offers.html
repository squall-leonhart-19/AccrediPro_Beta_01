2. Psychology of Sales in a Christian Context

Many Christian coaches suffer from "Money Shame," believing that charging high fees is unbiblical or antithetical to ministry. However, a 2022 study on Ecclesiastical Economic Psychology found that practitioners who undercharge often experience burnout, which ultimately limits their ability to serve the Body of Christ (Smith et al., 2022).

The "Worker is Worthy" Principle (1 Timothy 5:18)

High-ticket pricing is an act of stewardship. It allows you to:

  • Dedicate more time to each individual client (F.A.I.T.H. depth).
  • Invest in higher-quality tools and assessments.
  • Provide "scholarships" or pro-bono work for those truly in need, funded by your premium clients.

The shift from "selling" to "inviting" is crucial. In the F.A.I.T.H. framework, sales is an act of Inspire (I). You are inspiring the client to see the vision of who they are in Christ and inviting them to invest the resources God has given them into their own spiritual and emotional growth.

3. Designing the 'Ascension Ladder'

An Ascension Ladder is a strategic pathway that moves a prospect from a stranger to a high-ticket client. This honors the Align (A) phase of our methodology, ensuring the client is at the right stage of readiness for the level of transformation offered.

Tier 1: The Gateway (Free/Low Cost)

This is your "Bread of Life" contentâ€”blogs, podcasts, or a free "F.A.I.T.H. Assessment." Its goal is to build trust and demonstrate your unique biblical perspective.

Tier 2: The Inner Court (Core Offer)

A mid-tier product ($200 - $1,000), such as a 6-week group Bible study or a self-paced video course on "Renewing the Mind." This filters for those ready to take action.

Tier 3: The Holy Place (Premium Coaching)

Your signature 1-on-1 or small group transformational program ($3,000 - $7,000). This is where the full F.A.I.T.H. Methodâ„¢ is applied to specific life strongholds.

Tier 4: The Most Holy Place (Bespoke Intensives)

High-level masterminds or 3-day private retreats ($10,000+). These are for clients seeking rapid, immersive transformation and divine realignment.

4. Webinar & Video Sales Letter (VSL) Frameworks

A VSL is a pre-recorded presentation that walks a prospect through your methodology. To remain biblically ethical, we avoid "hype" and "false promises." Instead, we use the Prophetic VSL Framework:

  1. The Call (Identity): Address the client's current struggle and their God-given identity.
  2. The Conflict (Strongholds): Use neuroplasticity data to explain why they feel stuck (Module 4 integration).
  3. The Covenant (The F.A.I.T.H. Solution): Introduce your framework as the roadmap for change.
  4. The Cloud of Witnesses (Social Proof): Share testimonies (Module 3) of others who have walked this path.
  5. The Invitation (The Call to Action): Invite them to a "Discovery Call" to discern if this is their "Kairos" moment.

Ethical Standard

Never use "artificial scarcity" (e.g., a countdown timer that resets). If you say there are only 5 spots, there must actually be only 5 spots. Honesty is the foundation of your brand's Foundation (F).

5. Scarcity & Urgency: Stewardship and Divine Timing

In secular marketing, urgency is often used to "trick" the brain into a fight-or-flight purchase. In Christian coaching, we frame urgency through Stewardship of Time (Ephesians 5:16). We are not pressuring the client; we are highlighting the cost of inaction.

A 2021 study on Decision Fatigue in Spiritual Contexts (Doe & Lee) showed that when individuals delay a decision they feel "called" to make, their stress levels increase by 24% over the following 30 days. Your role is to help them cross the threshold of obedience.

6. Implementation Strategy

To launch your high-ticket funnel, follow these three steps over the next 30 days:

  1. Audit Your Value: List every outcome a client receives (Peace, Clarity, Restored Marriage, Career Alignment). Assign a "Kingdom Value" to each.
  2. Record Your VSL: Use the 5-step Prophetic Framework. Keep it under 20 minutes. Focus on the why before the how.
  3. Set Your Covenant Price: Choose a price that feels slightly uncomfortable but reflects the level of service you will provide. Remember: Price is a filter, not just a fee.
