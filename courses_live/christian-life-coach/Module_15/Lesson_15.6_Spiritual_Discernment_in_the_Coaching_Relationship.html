1. The Theology of Discernment in Coaching

4. Words of Wisdom and Knowledge in Coaching

In a professional coaching context, we define "Words of Wisdom" and "Words of Knowledge" not as disruptive interruptions, but as discerning insights that provide clarity. We avoid "Thus saith the Lord" language to maintain professional boundaries, instead using "invitational inquiry."

A Word of Knowledge in coaching is often an impression about a specific fact or root cause the client hasn't mentioned. A Word of Wisdom is a sudden insight into how the client should apply a biblical truth to their specific dilemma. The key is to present these as hypotheses for the client to test, rather than mandates they must accept. This preserves the client‚Äôs agency‚Äîa core tenet of the F.A.I.T.H. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢.

6. Professional Neutrality vs. Spiritual Sensitivity

The "Guided by God" balance is the hallmark of an AccrediPro Certified Coach. You must be spiritually sensitive without becoming spiritually directive. Directiveness occurs when the coach imposes their own "revelation" on the client, whereas sensitivity occurs when the coach facilitates the client's own encounter with God.

Professional neutrality in Christian coaching means you don't take "sides" in the client's internal conflict; you take God's side. You remain a neutral observer of the client‚Äôs process while being a passionate advocate for the Truth. This prevents the "Messiah Complex" where the coach feels responsible for the client‚Äôs transformation‚Äîa burden that belongs to the Holy Spirit alone.

Check Your Understanding

1

How does "Spiritual Listening" differ from "Active Listening" in the F.A.I.T.H. framework?

Active listening focuses on the client's cognitive and emotional output (understanding the person), whereas Spiritual Listening focuses on the soul-state and the Holy Spirit's movement (understanding God's perspective on the person). It involves a "dual-channel" awareness.
2

What is the primary risk of being "spiritually directive" rather than "spiritually sensitive"?

The primary risk is bypassing the client's agency and their personal relationship with God. It can lead to the "Messiah Complex" in the coach and dependency in the client, rather than the client learning to discern God's voice for themselves.

Key Takeaways

  • Discernment is a trained sense (Hebrews 5:14) that bridges the Align and Inspire pillars.

  • Words of Wisdom should be presented as "invitational inquiries" to maintain professional boundaries.
  • Holy Silence is a clinical and spiritual tool that facilitates deep neuro-spiritual processing.
  • The coach is a conduit, not the source, of transformation.
