3. Deconstruction vs. Reconstruction

Many clients in crisis undergo a period of "Deconstruction," where their previous theological framework collapses under the weight of suffering. This is often historically termed the "Dark Night of the Soul" (St. John of the Cross). As a Christian Life Coach, your goal is not to prevent deconstruction, but to facilitate Biblical Reconstruction.

The Reconstruction Protocol
  • Validate the Lament: Utilize the Psalms of Lament to show the client that questioning is part of the biblical tradition.
  • Identify the 'Foundation' (F): Re-examine the core biblical truths that remain regardless of the crisis.
  • Differentiate God from the Institution: Helping clients separate the actions of a person/church from the character of Christ.

5. Ethical Referrals & Crisis Management

A premium coach knows when to step back. If a client presents with clinical depression, PTSD, or active suicidal ideation, the Ethical Referral Protocol must be activated immediately. A 2022 survey of 1,200 coaches found that 22% encountered a "high-risk" mental health crisis within their first year of practice.

Red Flag Checklist for Referrals
  • Inability to perform basic daily functions (hygiene, eating, sleeping).
  • Persistent thoughts of self-harm or harming others.
  • Hallucinations or delusional thinking (often masked as "visions").
  • Severe panic attacks that prevent coaching engagement.
  • Substance abuse as a primary coping mechanism.

Key Takeaways

  • Coaching focuses on present-to-future spiritual alignment, whereas counseling focuses on past-to-present clinical healing.
  • Spiritual trauma requires a "Reconstruction" phase that validates lament while anchoring the client in biblical truth.
  • The Hope (H) pillar is a neurobiological necessity for trauma recovery, not just a theological concept.
  • Ethical boundaries are non-negotiable; recognize "Red Flags" and refer when clinical pathology is present.
  • Scriptural reframing shifts the client's narrative from existential despair to divine presence.
