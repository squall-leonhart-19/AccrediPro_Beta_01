4. Trans-disciplinary Integration

Effective supervision in the L3 tier requires trans-disciplinary integration. This is the art of using psychological insights (neuroplasticity, attachment theory, cognitive restructuring) alongside theological reflection (sanctification, imago Dei, spiritual warfare). We do not choose one over the other; we see them as complementary layers of God's truth.

The Integration Framework:

  • Psychological Lens: Are there cognitive distortions or trauma-informed responses (e.g., amygdala hijack) preventing the client from moving forward?
  • Theological Lens: Is there a misalignment with Scriptural authority or a lack of surrender to the Holy Spirit's leading?
  • Synthesis: How does the "renewing of the mind" (Romans 12:2) manifest both neurologically and spiritually in this specific case?

5. Clinical Review via Verbatim & Audio

To move beyond vague "reporting" in supervision, we utilize Verbatim Transcripts and Audio Recordings. This provides empirical evidence of the coaching process, allowing the supervisor to see the "Eye 2" (Interventions) and "Eye 3" (Relationship) with clarity.

A "Verbatim" is a word-for-word written record of a 5-10 minute segment of a session. It includes:

  1. C: (Client's words)
  2. CO: (Coach's words)
  3. Internal Reflection: What the coach was thinking/feeling but didn't say (The Internal Supervisorâ€™s notes).

Pro Tip: The 5-Minute Audit

Research suggests that the most critical data in a coaching session often occurs in the first 5 minutes of the "Inspire" phase. When submitting recordings for supervision, focus on these transition points where the conversation shifts from "problem-solving" to "spiritual discernment."

6. Reflective Cycles for Mastery

Reflective practice is not a one-time event but a cycle. We use the Gibbs Reflective Cycle adapted for the Christian Life Coach:

  1. Description: What happened?
  2. Feelings: What was my spiritual/emotional state?
  3. Evaluation: What was good/bad about the F.A.I.T.H. application?
  4. Analysis: What theological/psychological sense can I make of the situation?
  5. Conclusion: What else could I have done to facilitate Metanoia?
  6. Action Plan: How will I align with the Spirit in the next session?
