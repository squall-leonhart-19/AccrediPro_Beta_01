4. Reconstructing Hope: Finding Meaning

Victor Frankl, a psychiatrist and Holocaust survivor, noted that "meaning-making" is the primary protection against psychological collapse.

The Meaning-Making Framework

Help your client navigate the transition from "Why did this happen?" to "What does this mean for my future walk with God?" using the following prompts:

5. Addressing Disappointment with God

A significant "Hope Deficit" (Module 5) occurs when a client feels their prayers were ignored. Statistically, 38% of believers report a "weakened faith" immediately following a traumatic loss (Barna, 2022). As a coach, you must navigate this without becoming a "Job's Comforter."

Strategy: The "Lament to Trust" Pivot

Do not correct their theology in the heat of their pain. Use the T (Transform) phase to gently reframe. Ask: "If God is big enough to handle your anger, what would you say to Him right now?" This validates their emotion while maintaining the Foundation (F) of God's sovereignty.

Clinical Insight: Research on "Spiritual Struggle" indicates that clients who are encouraged to express their disappointment with God actually show 22% higher long-term spiritual resilience than those who suppress it.
