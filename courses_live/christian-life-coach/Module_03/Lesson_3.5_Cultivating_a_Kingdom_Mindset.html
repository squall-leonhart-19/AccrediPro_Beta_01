2. Eternal vs. Temporal: The Value Audit

A Kingdom Mindset requires the ability to distinguish between the temporal (that which is passing away) and the eternal (that which has lasting spiritual significance). In coaching, we use a "Biblical Lens Audit" to help clients evaluate their current time management and priorities.

Focus Area Temporal/Worldly View Eternal/Kingdom View
Success Accumulation of wealth, status, and praise. Faithfulness to God's calling and fruitfulness.
Time A resource to be spent on self-interest. An investment in people and God's purposes.
Trials Obstacles to be avoided or overcome quickly. Refining fire for character and testimony.
Identity Defined by roles, income, and appearance. Defined by "Identity in Christ" (Module 1).

3. The Stewardship Mindset: From Ownership to Management

One of the most profound psychological shifts in Christian coaching is moving from ownership to stewardship. Ownership carries the burden of total responsibility and the fear of loss. Stewardship carries the responsibility of faithfulness but recognizes that the "Owner" (God) provides the resources.

As a coach, you must help clients realize they are "under-managers" of God's assetsâ€”their talents, time, money, and even their children. When a client says, "I have to make my business succeed," they are operating in ownership. When they say, "I am managing this business for the glory of God," they are operating in stewardship.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of the Kingdom Mindset concepts.

1. What is the primary psychological difference between an "Ownership" mindset and a "Stewardship" mindset?

Ownership places the burden of ultimate outcome on the individual, leading to anxiety. Stewardship recognizes God as the owner and the individual as a manager, leading to peace and faithfulness-based motivation.

2. How does a "Kingdom Vision Statement" differ from a standard corporate vision statement?

While corporate visions focus on market dominance or revenue, a Kingdom Vision Statement aligns personal calling with God's redemptive work, focusing on eternal impact and divine assignment.

6. Vision Casting: The Kingdom Vision Statement

To conclude the "I: Inspire" module, we help the client synthesize everything into a Kingdom Vision Statement. This is a concise declaration of how their life, career, and resources will serve the King.

The Formula:
"I am called to [Specific Action] for [Specific People/Area] so that [Kingdom Impact/God's Glory]."

Example: "I am called to steward my architectural firm to create restorative spaces for urban communities so that they experience the beauty and order of God's Kingdom."
