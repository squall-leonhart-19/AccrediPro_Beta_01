2. The Spiritual Resilience Scale (SRS)

The Spiritual Resilience Scale (SRS) is a specialized assessment designed to measure a client's "spiritual grit." It evaluates three primary domains: Divine Connection, Adaptive Coping, and Purposeful Endurance. Unlike standard resilience scales, the SRS assumes that the source of resilience is external (God) rather than internal (self-will).

3. Lament vs. Despair Assessment

A critical skill for the Christian Life Coach is distinguishing between Biblical Lament and Hopeless Despair. Lament is an active, faith-filled turning toward God in pain, whereas despair is a passive turning away from God. Assessment tools must help the coach discern if the client is processing grief with God or sinking into a "Hope Deficit."

Feature Biblical Lament Hopeless Despair
Direction Vertical (Directed toward God) Inward (Directed toward self/void)
Core Belief "God is there and He hears me." "God has forgotten me or doesn't care."
Action Honest complaint followed by trust. Resignation and withdrawal.
Outcome Spiritual resilience and renewed hope. Spiritual atrophy and isolation.
