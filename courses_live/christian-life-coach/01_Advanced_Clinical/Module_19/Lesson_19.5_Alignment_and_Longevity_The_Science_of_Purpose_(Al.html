2. The Psychology of Calling: Beyond Job Satisfaction

Organizational psychology has long sought to understand why some individuals thrive in high-stress environments while others burn out. Research by Dr. Amy Wrzesniewski (Yale University) suggests that the way we align with our work determines our psychological and physical health outcomes.

Orientation Primary Motivation Health & Longevity Impact
Job Orientation Financial reward; work is a necessity. Higher cortisol levels; increased risk of weekend "burnout" syndrome.
Career Orientation Advancement, status, and prestige. High achievement but prone to "existential vacuum" during transitions.
Calling Orientation Alignment with higher purpose/service. Higher resilience; lower systemic inflammation; superior cardiovascular health.

A 2018 study published in the Journal of Career Assessment found that individuals who perceived their work as a "Calling" reported significantly higher life satisfaction and lower levels of depression, even when controlling for income levels. For the Christian Life Coach, the goal of the Align phase is to transition the client from a "Job" mindset to a "Divine Vocation" mindset, leveraging these protective psychological mechanisms.

5. The Biology of Prosocial Behavior: Serving Your Way to Health

The Align pillar often directs clients toward service (Prosocial Behavior). Modern immunology shows that serving othersâ€”a core tenet of the Christian lifeâ€”actually optimizes the helper's immune system. This is often called the "Helper's High," but the science goes deeper.

A landmark study by Cole et al. (2013) found that individuals who lived a life of Eudaimonic Well-being (purpose-driven, service-oriented) showed a distinct gene expression profile in their immune cells. They had lower levels of pro-inflammatory genes and higher levels of antiviral and antibody-producing genes compared to those seeking purely "Hedonic" (pleasure-based) happiness.

The IL-6 Marker

Interleukin-6 (IL-6) is a key pro-inflammatory cytokine associated with Alzheimer's, heart disease, and certain cancers. Research has shown that individuals with a high "Sense of Purpose" have significantly lower circulating levels of IL-6. By helping a client Align with their God-given purpose, you are literally prescribing a biological anti-inflammatory.

6. Practical Application: Using the Purpose-in-Life (PIL) Scale

To move from theory to evidence-based practice, the F.A.I.T.H. Coaching Methodâ„¢ utilizes the Purpose-in-Life (PIL) Scale, originally developed by Crumbaugh and Maholick. This 20-item psychometric tool allows you to quantify your client's sense of meaning.

Coaching Protocol:

  1. Baseline: Administer the PIL scale during the Foundation (F) phase.
  2. Identification: Use the Align (A) phase to address the specific sub-sections where the client scores low (e.g., "My personal existence is: Utterly meaningless / Very purposeful").
  3. Re-assessment: Administer again after 90 days of coaching to demonstrate the "Evidence of Transformation."
