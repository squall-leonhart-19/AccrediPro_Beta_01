3. The Power of the Rhema Word in Session

In the F.A.I.T.H. Coaching Methodâ„¢, we distinguish between Logos and Rhema. While the entire Bible is the inspired Logos (the written Word), a Rhema word is a specific passage that the Holy Spirit "breathes on" for a client in a specific moment of need.

Concept Definition Coaching Application
Logos The objective, written Word of God. Establishing boundaries, ethics, and general identity.
Rhema An "utterance" or specific word for a situation. Breaking a specific stronghold or providing direction in a crisis.

As a coach, your role is to facilitate the client's hearing of the Rhema. You don't "prophesy" over them; you guide them through the Logos until a specific truth "pierces" the situation (Hebrews 4:12).

5. Practical Tools: Selecting Anchor Verses

Transformation requires the "renewing of the mind" (Romans 12:2). In the coaching process, Anchor Verses are specific scriptures assigned as homework for meditation, visualization, and cognitive reframing. This is not just "homework"; it is spiritual neuroplasticity.

Criteria for a Strong Anchor Verse:

  • Directly Challenges the Lie: If the client believes "I am alone," the anchor should be Matthew 28:20 or Hebrews 13:5.
  • Action-Oriented: Verses that prescribe a specific posture or behavior (e.g., Philippians 4:6-7 for anxiety).
  • Memorizable: Short enough to be recalled in the heat of a "trigger" moment.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the principle of "Sola Scriptura" vital for a coach when a client is experiencing high emotional distress?

Emotional distress often skews a client's perception of reality. Sola Scriptura provides an objective, unchanging "True North" to ground the client's identity and decisions, preventing the coach from relying on subjective or potentially deceptive feelings.

2. What is the primary difference between Exegesis and Eisegesis in the coaching context?

Exegesis is drawing the meaning out of the text based on context (Biblical authority), while Eisegesis is reading your own meaning into the text to support a pre-conceived coaching goal (Scriptural malpractice).

  • Reframing a client's struggle within the Biblical Metanarrative provides eternal perspective and hope.
  • Anchor Verses are practical tools for the "renewing of the mind" and cognitive behavioral change.
