2. Setting S.M.A.R.T.G. Goals

Case Study: The Misaligned Executive

Client: Mark, 52 | Presenting Symptom: Chronic Burnout & Spiritual Dryness

Intervention: Using the F.A.I.T.H. Method, Mark identified that while he was a high-capacity steward (Talent), his current role as a VP of Sales for a gambling firm violated his core value of Biblical Integrity. This was a massive Alignment Gap.

His burnout dissipated as his "work" aligned with his "worship."

4. Establishing Accountability: Iron Sharpening Iron

The Alignment Action Plan is only as strong as its implementation. Biblical accountability is not about "policing" the client; it is about "partnering" in their sanctification. Proverbs 27:17 notes that "as iron sharpens iron, so one person sharpens another."

In a meta-analysis of 38 coaching studies, researchers found that accountability structures were the single most significant predictor of long-term behavior change, exceeding the impact of the initial motivation or the complexity of the plan itself. For the Christian coach, this includes:

  • The Coaching Relationship: Regular check-ins on S.M.A.R.T.G. goals.
  • Ecclesiastical Accountability: Ensuring the client is submitted to a local church body.
  • Spiritual Disciplines: Accountability to the "First Love"â€”daily time in the Word and prayer.

5. Defining Success: Faithfulness vs. Visible Fruit

One of the most critical shifts in the "Align" module is redefining success. In the parable of the talents (Matthew 25), the master did not commend the servants for the amount they made, but for their faithfulness with what they were given. Secular coaching often focuses exclusively on visible outcomes (revenue, weight loss, promotions).

The Alignment Action Plan must include Faithfulness Metrics. These ask: "Was I obedient to the promptings of the Holy Spirit today?" rather than "Did I achieve the result I wanted?" This protects the client from the "idolatry of the outcome" and anchors them in the peace of God.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of the Alignment Action Plan principles.

1. What is the primary difference between a SMART goal and a S.M.A.R.T.G. goal?

The S.M.A.R.T.G. framework adds "Scriptural" and "God-centered" criteria, ensuring the goal is not just effective, but biblically sound and designed for God's glory rather than self-promotion.

2. How does a coach handle an "Alignment Gap" involving a client's character?

The coach helps the client identify the specific spiritual stronghold or character deficit (e.g., fear, pride) and incorporates spiritual disciplines and repentance into the Action Plan to bridge the gap through the power of the Holy Spirit.

Key Takeaways

  • The Life Alignment Map (LAM) is the visual synthesis of passions, gifts, and values.
  • Alignment requires moving from abstract vision to Scriptural, God-centered goals.
  • Incremental steps are the primary tool for bridging the gap between current reality and divine calling.
  • True success in Kingdom coaching is measured by faithfulness and obedience, not just visible fruit.
  • Accountability is a grace-filled structure that ensures the client remains on the narrow path.
