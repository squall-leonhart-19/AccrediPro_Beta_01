2. The Mechanics of Synaptic Pruning

Renewal is a two-fold process: the creation of the new and the removal of the old. In neuroscience, the removal of the old is called synaptic pruning. This is the process by which the brain eliminates extra synapses to increase the efficiency of neural transmissions.

4. The Default Mode Network (DMN) and Spiritual Disruptors

The Default Mode Network (DMN) is a large-scale brain network known for being active when a person is not focused on the outside world. It is the seat of the "wandering mind" and is often where rumination, worry, and self-criticism reside. In a "carnal" mind, the DMN is hyper-active, constantly replaying past failures or future fears.

Spiritual disciplinesâ€”specifically those emphasized in the "Inspire" (I) and "Transform" (T) phasesâ€”act as DMN disruptors. When a client engages in Lectio Divina or focused worship, they shift the brain from the DMN to the Task-Positive Network (TPN), effectively silencing the "inner critic."
