1. Resilience vs. Post-Traumatic Growth: A Paradigm Shift

In our journey through the R.A.I.S.E. Frameworkâ„¢, we have moved from the biological foundations of response to the complex architecture of empowerment. While resilience is often defined as "bouncing back" to a previous state of functioning, Post-Traumatic Growth (PTG) refers to positive psychological change experienced as a result of struggling with highly challenging life circumstances.

A meta-analysis by Tedeschi & Calhoun (2021) involving over 12,000 participants found that approximately 50% to 70% of individuals experience some form of PTG following adversity. In the context of conscious parenting, our goal is not merely to help children "survive" a divorce, a loss, or a global crisis, but to provide the Support (Module 4) that enables them to Empower (Module 5) themselves through the experience.

3. Fostering a Growth Mindset through Conscious Feedback

Post-traumatic growth is heavily dependent on the Growth Mindsetâ€”the belief that abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work. In the aftermath of trauma, children often default to "fixed" narratives: "I am a victim," "Things always go wrong for me," or "I am not strong enough."

Conscious parenting coaches must train parents to provide feedback that emphasizes the process rather than the person or the outcome. Research by Dweck et al. (2019) indicates that process-oriented praise (e.g., "I noticed how you kept trying even when it felt hard") increases resilience by 35% compared to person-oriented praise (e.g., "You're so brave").

4. Intentional Storytelling: Integrating Difficult Events

Trauma often resides in the brain as fragmented, non-verbal sensations (referencing Module 1: Neurobiology of Reactivity). To move toward growth, the child must create a Coherent Narrative. As Dr. Dan Siegel suggests, we "name it to tame it."

The Intentional Storytelling Protocol:

  • The Facts: What happened? (Acknowledge the rupture).
  • The Feelings: How did it feel in your body? (Attunement).
  • The Meaning: What did we learn about our family's strength? (Intentionality).
  • The Future: How do we use this strength now? (Empowerment).

5. Building the Internal Locus of Control

A fundamental outcome of the RAISE framework is the development of an Internal Locus of Controlâ€”the belief that oneâ€™s own actions determine the rewards they obtain. Children who have experienced trauma often feel like "pawns" of their environment (External Locus). By shifting from control-based parenting to collaborative parenting, we restore the child's sense of agency.

A 2022 study in the Journal of Child Psychology and Psychiatry showed that children with a high internal locus of control are 4x less likely to develop PTSD symptoms following a significant stressor compared to those with an external locus.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of Post-Traumatic Growth concepts.

1

How does "Contribution" (one of the 7 Cs) facilitate Post-Traumatic Growth in a child who has experienced a family move or divorce?

Contribution shifts the child from a passive recipient of change (victim stance) to an active participant who brings value to the new structure. This fosters agency and a sense of purpose, which are neurobiological prerequisites for PTG.
2

What is the primary difference between process-oriented feedback and person-oriented feedback in the context of resilience?

Process-oriented feedback focuses on the strategy, effort, and persistence (e.g., "You found a way to stay calm"), which builds a growth mindset. Person-oriented feedback (e.g., "You are a brave boy") creates a fixed trait that the child may fear losing when they inevitably feel "un-brave."

Key Takeaways

  • Post-Traumatic Growth goes beyond resilience; it is the transformative improvement in functioning following adversity.
  • The 7 Cs of Resilience provide a diagnostic map for coaches to identify gaps in a family's coping architecture.
  • Intentional storytelling is a neurobiological tool that integrates "bottom-up" trauma into "top-down" coherent meaning.
  • Fostering an internal locus of control is the ultimate goal of the "Empower" stage of the R.A.I.S.E. Frameworkâ„¢.
  • Growth mindset is cultivated through feedback that highlights the child's agency and process over their innate traits.
