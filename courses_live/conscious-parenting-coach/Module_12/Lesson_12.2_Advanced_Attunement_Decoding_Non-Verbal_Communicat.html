4. R.A.I.S.E. and the 'Double Empathy' Problem

In neurodivergent households (Autism, ADHD, etc.), attunement is often complicated by the Double Empathy Problem. This theory suggests that communication breakdowns occur not because of a deficit in the neurodivergent person, but because of a mismatch in communication styles between different neurotypes.

Using the R.A.I.S.E. Framework to bridge this gap:

  • R (Respond): The parent must first respond to their own bias about what "listening" looks like (e.g., eye contact).
  • A (Attune): Attuning to the child's unique somatic language (e.g., stimming as a sign of joy or regulation).
  • I (Intentional): Setting the intention to bridge the neurotype gap rather than forcing neurotypical compliance.
  • S (Support): Providing sensory scaffolding rather than just behavioral correction.
  • E (Empower): Empowering the child to use their natural communication style.

5. Tools for Coaches: Developing 'Reflective Functioning'

Reflective Functioning (RF) is the capacity to imagine the mental states (feelings, thoughts, intentions) that underlie behavior. It is "Attunement in action." As a coach, you can use the following exercises to help parents build this "mentalizing" muscle during high-conflict moments.

The "Third Eye" Visualization

Ask the parent to visualize a "third eye" on their childâ€™s chest. Instead of looking at the childâ€™s angry face (the behavior), they must "look" through the third eye at the heart (the feeling). Ask: "If that heart could speak without using angry words, what would it be asking for right now?"

The Somatic Echo

During a conflict, have the parent pause and name where they feel the child's energy in their own body. If the parent feels a "tightness in the throat," it often mirrors the child's feeling of being unheard. This is the first step toward Affective Resonance.

6. Clinical Application: The Coach as the 'Co-Regulator'

In your coaching sessions, you are the "parent" to the parent. You must model the very attunement you are teaching. If a parent is struggling to grasp a concept, notice their non-verbal cues. Are they leaning back (withdrawal/shame)? Are they talking faster (anxiety/overwhelm)?

Your ability to maintain Affective Resonance with the parent allows them to experience the safety of being attuned to, which in turn increases their capacity to attune to their child. This is the "parallel process" in professional coaching.
