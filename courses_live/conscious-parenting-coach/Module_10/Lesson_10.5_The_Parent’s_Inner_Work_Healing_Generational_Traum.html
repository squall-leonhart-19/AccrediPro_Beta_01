4. Shadow Work in Parenting: Projection & Integration

The "Shadow Self" (a Jungian concept) consists of the parts of ourselves we have suppressed because they were deemed "unacceptable" by our early caregivers. In parenting, we often project our shadow onto our children.

The Shadow Mirror

The trait in your child that irritates you the most is often a trait you were forced to suppress in yourself. If your childâ€™s "loudness" triggers you, ask yourself: "When was I told to be quiet? Who would I have been if I were allowed to be loud?"

Shadow integration involves acknowledging these parts without judgment. When we integrate our shadow, we no longer need our children to be "different" so that we can feel comfortable. We move from Control (managing the child to manage our own anxiety) to Connection (accepting the child as they are).
