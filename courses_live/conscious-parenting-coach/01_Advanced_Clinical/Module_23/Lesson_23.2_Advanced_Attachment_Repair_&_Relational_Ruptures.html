The advanced 4-step repair process requires the parent to move from their own amygdala-driven reactivity back into the prefrontal cortex to lead the reconnection:

  1. Acknowledge & Name: The parent must explicitly name the rupture without defensiveness. "I realize I raised my voice and my face looked angry."
  2. Assume Radical Responsibility: Removing "if" and "but" from the narrative. "I am responsible for how I reacted, regardless of what happened before."

  4. Co-Regulated Reconnection: A somatic invitation to return to safety (e.g., eye contact, soft tone, or physical proximity).

4. Proactive Repair: The Intentional Pillar

For families with chronic ruptures, the 'Empower' pillar (Module 5) is utilized by giving the child control over the pace of reconnection. This builds trust by proving the parent is no longer "intruding" or "abandoning."
