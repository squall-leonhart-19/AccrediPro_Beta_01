2. The Secure Attachment Checklist

Longitudinal indicators of health are rooted in Secure Attachment. As we conclude this Deep Dive, we look at how the RAISE pillars cultivate the "Secure Base" and "Safe Haven" described by Bowlby and Ainsworth. A secure attachment is not the absence of conflict; it is the presence of repair.

The 5-Point Secure Attachment Indicator Checklist:

  • Proximity Seeking: Does the child turn toward the parent during times of genuine distress, rather than withdrawing?
  • The "Free to Explore" Marker: Does the child demonstrate curiosity and age-appropriate independence, knowing the parent is a stable "home base"?
  • Emotional Literacy: Can the child name at least three distinct emotions during a de-escalation phase? (Evidence of the Attune and Empower pillars).
  • The Repair Rate: Does the family have a "Recovery Time Objective"? Success is defined as decreasing the time between a rupture (yelling/disconnection) and a repair (apology/reconnection).
  • Parental Reflective Functioning: Can the parent imagine the internal state of the child during a meltdown? ("He isn't being bad; he is feeling overwhelmed.")

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of the RAISE Outcome Roadmap

1. Why is "Child-Initiated Disclosure" considered a high-level qualitative metric of success in Conscious Parenting?

It indicates a shift from a fear-based relationship to a safety-based one. When a child shares voluntarily, it proves they view the parent as a "Safe Haven" (Secure Attachment) rather than a source of judgment or punishment.

2. In the 90-Day Integration Roadmap, what is the primary focus of the first 30 days?

The focus is on "Respond" (R) and Deconstruction. This involves identifying somatic triggers, mastering the "Power of the Pause," and reducing immediate reactivity before layering on advanced communication skills.

Key Takeaways

  • Success in the RAISE Framework is measured by relational health and nervous system regulation, not just compliance.
  • The Secure Attachment Checklist provides a scientific baseline for tracking family progress over time.
  • A 90-day roadmap prevents "information overload" by focusing on one or two pillars at a time.
  • The framework must be developmentally adapted, shifting from sensory co-regulation (toddlers) to collaborative autonomy (middle childhood).
  • The ultimate goal of the Deep Dive is to move the parent from "doing" conscious parenting to "being" a conscious presence.
