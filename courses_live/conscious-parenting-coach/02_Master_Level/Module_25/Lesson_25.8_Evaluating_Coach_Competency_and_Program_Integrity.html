Feedback Delivery Systems: Sandwich vs. Radical Candor

The method by which a supervisor delivers an evaluation determines whether a coach enters a state of neuroplastic growth or amygdala-driven defense. Historically, the "Sandwich Method" (praise-criticism-praise) was the gold standard. However, recent organizational psychology research suggests this can lead to "feedback confusion," where the coach ignores the corrective feedback in favor of the praise.

360-Degree Assessments and Program Integrity

Program integrity is maintained when evaluation data is triangulated. Relying solely on a supervisor's observation can introduce confirmation bias. A 360-degree assessment incorporates:

  • Self-Assessment: The coachâ€™s own reflective practice logs.
  • Client Outcome Data: Quantitative shifts in the parent-child relationship (using validated scales like the Parenting Stress Index).
  • Peer Review: Feedback from group supervision sessions.
  • Direct Observation: Supervisor review of recorded coaching sessions.

A 2022 meta-analysis found that multisource feedback in professional settings improved self-awareness by 31%, leading to higher adherence to framework protocols (Miller et al., 2022).

Remediation Plans: Structured Support for Growth

When a coach fails to meet the "Proficient" threshold in the Competency Matrix, a formal Remediation Plan is required. This is not a punitive measure, but a scaffolding interventionâ€”mirroring the "Support" pillar of the RAISE Frameworkâ„¢.

Components of an Effective Remediation Plan:

  1. Specific Deficiency: e.g., "Failure to maintain professional boundaries (Transference)."
  2. Measurable Goal: e.g., "Complete 10 hours of shadow work and 3 supervised sessions with zero boundary crossings."
  3. Timeline: Typically 30, 60, or 90 days.
  4. Consequences: Clear communication on certification status if goals are not met.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Sandwich Method" of feedback often discouraged in high-level professional supervision?

It can lead to "feedback confusion" where the critical, corrective information is buried or minimized by the surrounding praise, preventing the coach from making necessary behavioral shifts.

2. What is the primary difference between a "Proficient" coach and a "Master Coach" in the Attunement competency?

A Proficient coach tracks cues and repairs ruptures; a Master Coach demonstrates "Limbic Resonance," anticipates ruptures before they occur, and facilitates profound, transformative repairs.

Key Takeaways

  • Evaluation must be grounded in the observable behaviors outlined in the RAISE Competency Matrix.
  • Radical Candor is the preferred feedback model, balancing personal care with direct challenge.
  • 360-degree assessments protect program integrity by removing supervisor bias.
  • Remediation is a supportive scaffolding process, not a punishment.
  • Master Coach status requires the transition from technical application to framework embodiment.
