4. Limitations of Energy-Measuring Devices

Practitioners must understand the limitations of current technology to maintain professional integrity. Most "energy" devices are actually measuring secondary physiological markers (like Galvanic Skin Response, Heart Rate Variability, or Biophoton emission) and then inferring the state of the biofield through software models.

Key limitations include:

  • The Observer Effect: Quantum physics dictates that the act of observation changes the observed. The practitionerâ€™s own state of coherence can influence the device's readings.
  • Environmental Interference: Electromagnetic smog (EMF) from Wi-Fi or cellular towers can create "noise" in sensitive bio-resonance equipment.
  • Snapshot Bias: A scan is a single moment in time. A client who just drank caffeine or had an argument in the parking lot will show a distorted field that may not represent their baseline.
