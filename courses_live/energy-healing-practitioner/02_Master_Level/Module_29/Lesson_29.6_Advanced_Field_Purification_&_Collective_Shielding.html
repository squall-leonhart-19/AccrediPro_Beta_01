5. Differential Diagnosis: Debris vs. Influence

One of the most critical skills at this level is the ability to differentiate between Psychic Debris (the client's own unprocessed emotional "trash") and External Influence (environmental or collective loads). Treating an environmental load as personal trauma can lead to "therapeutic looping," where the client tries to "heal" something that isn't theirs.

Diagnostic Markers:

  • Psychic Debris: Usually has a "sticky" or "heavy" quality; resonates with specific personal memories; located deep within the emotional or mental bodies.
  • Environmental Influence: Usually feels "sharp," "static-like," or "superficial"; lacks a narrative or memory component; often disappears when the client changes locations.

Master Practitioners use the Pulse-Resonance Test: if the energetic signature remains unchanged after a deep emotional release session, it is likely environmental or collective in nature and requires shielding rather than processing.
