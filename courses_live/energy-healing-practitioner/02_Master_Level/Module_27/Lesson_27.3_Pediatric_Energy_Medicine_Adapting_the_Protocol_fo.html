6. Clinical Safety, Consent, and Ethics

Working with minors requires a specialized ethical framework. Practitioners must navigate the "Triad of Consent": the practitioner, the parent, and the child.

  • Assent vs. Consent: While the parent provides legal consent, the child must provide *assent*. If a child's biofield shows strong resistance or they verbally refuse, the session must stop immediately.
  • Physical Boundaries: Whenever possible, use "off-body" techniques. The pediatric biofield is highly sensitive; the practitioner's hands should remain 2-4 inches from the body unless a specific "A" (Align) or "L" (Lifeforce) technique requires contact.
  • Documentation: Note not just the child's response, but the parent's observations over the following 48 hours, as pediatric shifts often manifest in sleep patterns and appetite.
