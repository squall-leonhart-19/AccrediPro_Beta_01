6. Safety Protocols & Contraindications

Breathwork is a powerful intervention and must be applied with clinical caution. Rapid or intensive breathwork (Holotropic or Wim Hof style) can cause significant shifts in blood pH (Respiratory Alkalosis), which can be destabilizing for certain clients.

  • Cardiovascular Issues: Uncontrolled high blood pressure or history of aneurysms.
  • Pregnancy: Avoid any breath retention or forceful abdominal breathing.

  • Glaucoma: Breath retention can increase intraocular pressure.

For most maintenance purposes, the "Gentle Path" of 5.5-second inhales and 5.5-second exhales is the safest and most effective recommendation for long-term client empowerment.
