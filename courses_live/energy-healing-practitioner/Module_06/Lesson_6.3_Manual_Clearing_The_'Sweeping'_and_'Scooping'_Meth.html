2. The General Sweep Technique

The General Sweep is the foundational movement of the 'C' phase. It is a rhythmic, downward motion designed to remove surface-level debris and prepare the field for deeper work. Think of this as "energetic dusting."

Execution of the General Sweep:

  • The Hand Position: Fingers are slightly cupped and relaxed, as if holding a large orange. The palms should face the client.
  • The Motion: Start approximately 6-10 inches above the crown. Move the hands in a slow, steady, downward stroke toward the feet.
  • The Rhythm: Maintain a cadence of approximately 3-5 seconds per full-body stroke.
