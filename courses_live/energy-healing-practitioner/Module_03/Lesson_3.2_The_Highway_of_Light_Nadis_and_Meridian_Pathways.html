3. The 12 Primary Meridians and the Organ Clock

While Nadis are often associated with the nervous system, the 12 Primary Meridians are the energetic blueprints for the organ systems. Qi flows through these meridians in a specific 24-hour cycle known as the Circadian Organ Clock.

Each meridian has a two-hour window of peak activity. For example:

  • Liver (1 AM - 3 AM): Detoxification and blood renewal. Stagnation here often manifests as waking up during these hours with "racing thoughts."
  • Heart (11 AM - 1 PM): Circulation and emotional joy. Depletion here presents as midday fatigue or social withdrawal.
  • Lung (3 AM - 5 AM): Respiratory health and the processing of grief.

As an Energy Healing Practitioner, you must look for patterns. If a client consistently feels a "dip" in energy at 3 PM, you should immediately assess the Small Intestine Meridian. This level of specificity is what elevates your practice from general "hand-waving" to clinical energy medicine.
