4. Differential Diagnosis: Depletion vs. Structural Damage

One of the most critical skills in advanced biofield mapping is distinguishing between Energetic Depletion (temporary loss of charge) and Structural Damage (tears, leaks, or "scarring" in the biofield fabric).

Depletion (Temporary)

Depletion is akin to a low battery. The structure of the field is intact, but the amplitude is low. This responds rapidly to 'L' (Lifeforce Activation) and 'A' (Align Chakras).

Structural Damage (Chronic)

Structural damage involves a breach in the toroidal flow. This often results from acute shock or long-term systemic abuse. If you attempt to activate lifeforce (L) in a damaged field without first repairing the structure, the energy will simply "leak out," leading to client exhaustion post-session.

This indicates Structural Damage (Field Leak). You filled the "tank" (L), but because the "container" (B/C) has a structural tear, the energy dissipated rapidly once the practitioner's influence was removed. Level 2 intervention requires "field knitting" before activation.
