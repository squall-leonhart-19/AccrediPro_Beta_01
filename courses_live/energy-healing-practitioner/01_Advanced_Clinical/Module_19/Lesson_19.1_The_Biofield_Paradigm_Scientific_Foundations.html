1. Defining the 'Human Biofield'

For decades, energy healing was relegated to the periphery of "pseudoscience" due to a lack of shared nomenclature. This changed in 1992 when the National Institutes of Health (NIH) convened a panel of scientists to establish a formal definition for the energetic phenomena observed in clinical settings. They settled on the term "Biofield."

The Biofield is defined as a massless field, not necessarily electromagnetic, that surrounds and permeates living bodies. However, modern biophysics expands this: it is an endogenous, complex ensemble of electromagnetic and non-electromagnetic fields that regulate the biochemical and physiological processes of the organism. Essentially, the Biofield is the "operating system" that tells the "hardware" (cells and organs) how to function.
