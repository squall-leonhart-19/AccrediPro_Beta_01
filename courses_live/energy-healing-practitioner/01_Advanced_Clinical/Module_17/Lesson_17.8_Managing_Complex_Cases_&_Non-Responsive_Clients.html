5. Professional Referral Protocols

As an Energy Healing Practitioner, your scope of practice is energetic, not psychiatric or medical. Complex cases often involve deep-seated clinical pathology that requires a multi-disciplinary approach. You must refer a client to a licensed mental health professional if you observe:

  1. Dissociative Fugue: The client frequently "checks out" or loses time during energetic work.
  2. Psychotic Features: Energetic sensations are interpreted as "voices" or external entities in a way that is disorganized or paranoid.
  3. Transference/Counter-transference: The client becomes obsessively attached to the practitioner as their "only hope," or the practitioner feels an overwhelming need to "save" the client.
