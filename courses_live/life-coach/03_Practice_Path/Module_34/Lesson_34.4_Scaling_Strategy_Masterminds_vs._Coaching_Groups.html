1. Structural Archetypes: Teaching vs. Facilitating

In the evolution of a professional coaching practice, scaling beyond 1-on-1 sessions requires a strategic choice between two primary group models: Curriculum-based Coaching Groups and Peer-led Masterminds. While both involve groups, their neurological and structural impact on the client differs significantly.

A Coaching Group is typically "top-down." The coach is the primary source of authority, delivering a specific curriculum (often derived from the L.I.F.E. Blueprintâ„¢ phases) to multiple people at once. This model is ideal for clients in the Focus or Execute phases who need specific skills or a roadmap to achieve a common goal.

A Mastermind, conversely, is "lateral." The coach shifts from "Teacher" to "Facilitator." The value lies in the "Collective Intelligence" of the room. Here, the coach curates the environment and manages the dynamics, but the breakthroughs often come from peer-to-peer interaction. This is the pinnacle of the Identify phaseâ€”identifying not just individual blocks, but systemic opportunities through diverse perspectives.

4. The Art of Selection & Curation

In L4 scaling, your most important work happens before the program starts. The "Identify" phase is used as a rigorous screening tool. A single "toxic" or "misaligned" member can destroy the psychological safety of a $100k group investment.

When curating a mastermind, you must look for Complementary Diversity. If every member is a marketing expert, the group lacks the "Identify" breadth needed for holistic growth. You want a mix of disciplines but a uniformity of ambition and values.

The Vetting Process:

  • Psychological Readiness: Are they willing to be vulnerable in front of peers?
  • Value Alignment: Do their core drivers (Module 2, L2) match the group's ethos?
  • The "Give/Get" Ratio: Can they contribute as much value as they expect to receive?

5. Pricing Tiers and the Economics of Scale

Pricing is a reflection of the Psychological Contract you establish with the client. A $2,000 coaching group signals a "learning environment." A $15,000 mastermind signals a "partnership and performance environment."
