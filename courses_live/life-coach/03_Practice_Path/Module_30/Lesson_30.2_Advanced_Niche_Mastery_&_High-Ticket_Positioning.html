To command premium rates, you must move beyond demographic targeting (age, location, job title) into psychographic market segmentation.

Using the Identify phase of the L.I.F.E. Blueprint Methodâ„¢, we don't just identify who the client is; we identify the specific Cognitive Architecture of their problem. 3. Pricing Psychology: Hourly vs. Value-Based Models

Hourly billing creates a conflict of interest: the faster you help the client, the less you get paid. Value-based pricing, conversely, aligns your compensation with the client's results. If your coaching helps a CEO save a $2M partnership, or helps a couple avoid a $50k divorce, the "value" of that coaching is significantly higher than the hours spent on Zoom.

5. High-Ticket Program Architecture (6-12 Months)

A transformative journey requires time for neuroplasticity to take effect. High-ticket programs should be structured to support the client through the inevitable "dip" in motivation that occurs after the initial excitement wears off. This is where the Execute phase of our methodology becomes paramount.

The 12-Month "Transformative Arc" Structure
  • Month 1: Deep Listen & Identify. Intensive 1-on-1 "Deep Dive" session (3-4 hours) to map the blueprint.
  • Months 2-4: The Focus Phase. Bi-weekly sessions focused on cognitive reframing and strategic prioritization.
  • Months 5-11: The Execute & Sustain Phase. Monthly "Neuro-Accountability" check-ins, unlimited text support, and quarterly "Course Correction" intensives.
  • Month 12: Integration & Future-Mapping. Final review of metrics (ROI/VOI) and transition to a maintenance retainer.

Check Your Understanding

Test your mastery of high-ticket positioning concepts.

1

Why is "psychographic segmentation" superior to "demographic segmentation" for high-ticket coaching?

Demographics (age, gender, income) only tell you *who* the person is. Psychographics (values, beliefs, fears, aspirations) tell you *why* they are stuck. High-ticket coaching solves the "why," making psychographics essential for identifying the deep-seated pain points that justify premium investments.
2

In value-based pricing, what is the primary factor determining the fee?

The primary factor is the economic or emotional impact of the result for the client (the "ROI" or "Value of Investment"). It is not based on the coach's hours, the number of sessions, or the coach's internal costs.
