2. The VIA Character Strengths Framework

The VIA (Values in Action) Classification is the gold standard in coaching for identifying internal resources. Developed by Dr. Christopher Peterson and Dr. Martin Seligman, it identifies 24 universal character strengths organized under six virtues.

Virtue Associated Strengths (Examples) Coaching Application
Wisdom Creativity, Curiosity, Perspective Used for problem-solving and strategic planning.
Courage Bravery, Perseverance, Zest Essential for taking risks and overcoming fear of failure.
Humanity Love, Kindness, Social Intelligence Key for relationship goals and leadership.
Justice Teamwork, Fairness, Leadership Utilized in workplace dynamics and community impact.
Temperance Forgiveness, Humility, Self-Regulation Vital for habit change and emotional management.
Transcendence Gratitude, Hope, Humor, Spirituality Used for maintaining motivation and long-term vision.

When coaching, look for the client's Signature Strengthsâ€”the top 5â€“7 strengths that feel "essential" to who they are. Using these strengths feels energizing (intrinsic motivation), whereas using "bottom" strengths feels draining, even if the client is competent in them.

Key Terms to Master

Signature Strengths

The character strengths that are most representative of an individual's "true self" and provide energy when used.

Phronesis (Practical Wisdom)

The ability to use the right strength, in the right amount, in the right situation.

3. Resource Mining: Extracting Past Success

Clients often enter coaching feeling like they are "starting from zero." Resource Mining is a narrative technique used to prove they have already demonstrated the very skills they believe they lack. This involves a deep dive into "Peak Experiences"â€”moments where the client felt proud, effective, or "in flow."

Case Study: Sarah's Career Pivot

Age: 42 | Presenting Issue: Lack of confidence to start a business.

The Situation: Sarah felt she had "no business experience" despite 15 years as a head nurse. She was paralyzed by the idea of managing finances and marketing.

The Intervention: Using Resource Mining, the coach asked Sarah to describe her most stressful week at the hospital. She detailed managing a staff of 40, coordinating with different departments, and remaining calm during a crisis.

The Outcome: The coach mapped "Crisis Nursing" to "Business Operations." Sarah realized her Perseverance and Social Intelligence were already at an elite level. Her confidence score rose from 3/10 to 8/10 in one session.

5. The 'Internal Ally' Exercise

Every client has a "resilient self"â€”the part of them that has survived 100% of their worst days. The Internal Ally exercise personifies this resilient trait to make it accessible during times of stress. This builds on the "Neuroscience of Focus" (which we will cover in Module 3) by creating a mental anchor.

How to Facilitate:

  1. Identify the Trait: Ask, "When you were at your lowest, what kept you going?" (e.g., Grit, Humility, Stubbornness).
  2. Personify: Give this trait a name or an image. If "Grit" were a person or an object, what would it look like?
  3. Dialogue: Ask the client, "What does your Internal Ally say to your Limiting Belief (from Lesson 2.3)?"

6. Mapping Transferable Wins

A "Transferable Win" is the application of a success strategy from a mastered life area to a struggling life area. For example, a client who is highly disciplined in their fitness (Area A) but disorganized in their finances (Area B) can "transfer" the habit-tracking strategy from fitness to finance.

This creates cognitive ease. Instead of learning a "new" skill, the client is simply "re-deploying" an existing one. This is a core component of the L.I.F.E. Blueprint Methodâ„¢ because it reduces the friction of behavioral change.

Check Your Understanding

Test your knowledge of strengths-based coaching interventions.

1

A client is competent at detail-oriented tasks but finds them exhausting. According to the VIA framework, is this likely a Signature Strength?

No. A Signature Strength is characterized by being "energizing" and "authentic." Competence without energy often indicates a "learned behavior" rather than a core character strength.
2

What is the primary purpose of "Resource Mining"?

To provide empirical evidence to the client that they already possess the internal resources (skills, traits, resilience) necessary to achieve their current goals, based on past successes.

Key Takeaways

  • Strengths > Weaknesses: Focusing on strengths leads to higher engagement, faster growth, and better psychological well-being.
  • VIA Framework: Use the 24 character strengths to help clients identify their unique "Signature" traits.
  • Energy is the Indicator: True strengths provide energy; learned behaviors (even if done well) often drain energy.
  • Resource Mapping: Always inventory tangible assets (Social, Structural, Temporal) alongside internal traits.
  • Transferable Wins: Bridge the gap by applying successful strategies from one life domain to another.
