I: Identify â€“ Cognitive Mapping and the Values Gap

Once the alliance is established, we move to Identify. This phase is rooted in Cognitive-Behavioral Theory (CBT). The objective is to map the "Gap" between the client's current reality and their desired future.

During this phase, the coach helps the client elicit their Core Values. Research shows that goals aligned with intrinsic values have a 72% higher completion rate than those driven by external pressures (Deci & Ryan, 2000). We identify not just what the client wants, but the belief systems that have prevented them from getting it until now.

CASE STUDY: The Disconnected Executive
M

Marcus, 48

VP of Operations, experiencing "burnout" despite high salary.

Intervention: Using the Identify phase, the coach moved past the surface goal ("I need more energy") to uncover a core value conflict. Marcus valued Connection and Creativity, but his current role demanded 14 hours a day of Compliance and Conflict Management.

Outcome: By mapping this values gap, Marcus recognized that "burnout" was actually "value-erosion." This identification allowed for precision targeting in the Focus phase.

In Focus, we apply Cognitive Restructuring. Most clients suffer from "Choice Overload." The Focus phase uses the Pareto Principle (the 80/20 rule) to determine which 20% of actions will yield 80% of the results.
