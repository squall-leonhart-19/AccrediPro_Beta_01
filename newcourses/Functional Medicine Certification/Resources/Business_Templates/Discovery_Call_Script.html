Discovery Call Script

Consultation Framework for Prospective Clients

About This Script

This script provides a framework for conducting discovery calls with prospective clients. Use it as a guide—not a rigid script—to create natural, meaningful conversations that help you understand the prospect's needs and determine if you're a good fit to work together.

Recommended Duration: 20-30 minutes
Purpose: Connect, assess fit, invite to work together

Part 1: Welcome & Rapport (2-3 minutes)

Goal: Create connection and set expectations

Sample Opening:

"Hi [Name]! Thank you so much for taking the time to connect today. I'm really looking forward to learning more about you and what brought you to explore health coaching.

Before we dive in, let me share how I'd like to use our time together. We have about [20-30] minutes, and I want to make sure we use it well. I'll start by learning about what's going on with your health and what you're hoping to achieve. Then I'll share a bit about how I work and we can explore whether coaching might be a good fit for you. How does that sound?"

Light Rapport Building:

"How has your day been so far?" / "Where are you calling from today?" / "How did you hear about me?"

"So if I'm hearing you correctly, you're dealing with [summarize main challenges]. You've tried [what they've tried] but haven't gotten lasting results. What you really want is [their desired outcome] so that you can [deeper motivation]. Does that sound right?"

Part 3: Share How You Can Help (5-7 minutes)

Goal: Connect your approach to their specific needs

Key: Make it about THEM, not a generic pitch.

Bridge Statement:

"Thank you for sharing all of that with me, [Name]. I really appreciate your openness. Based on what you've told me, I think I can help. Let me share a bit about my approach and how we might work together..."

Your Approach (customize to your style):

"My approach is rooted in functional medicine principles. Rather than just treating symptoms, we look at the whole picture—your nutrition, sleep, stress, movement, relationships—and find the root causes of what's keeping you stuck.

What makes coaching different from just reading a book or following a diet plan is the personalization and accountability. We create a plan specifically for YOUR life, and I'm there to support you, troubleshoot with you, and keep you on track.

For someone in your situation dealing with [their specific challenges], we'd likely focus on [relevant areas]. You mentioned wanting [their desired outcome]—that's absolutely achievable when we take a systematic approach."

Part 4: Address Questions & Concerns (3-5 minutes)

Open the Floor:

"What questions do you have about working together?"

Common Concerns & Responses:

"What's the investment?"

"Great question. My [program] is $________. [Explain value, what's included, payment options if applicable]. When you consider what it costs you to stay stuck—in energy, missed opportunities, how you feel every day—most clients find this is one of the best investments they've made in themselves. What are your thoughts?"

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand—this is an important decision. What specifically would you like to think about? Is it the investment, the timing, or something else? [Listen and address]. What would help you feel confident in your decision?"

"I'm not sure I have time."

"I hear that—life is busy, especially when you're not feeling your best. Here's the thing: the lack of energy and symptoms you're dealing with are actually costing you time and capacity. Many clients find that as their health improves, they actually have MORE time and energy for what matters. The sessions are [duration] and the practices we'll add fit into your existing day. What does your schedule typically look like?"

"How do I know this will work?"

"That's such a fair question, especially if you've tried things before that didn't work. The difference with coaching is that it's personalized to YOU and you have support along the way. We're not following a one-size-fits-all plan—we're figuring out what works for your unique body and life. I've helped women with similar challenges achieve [relevant results]. Are you willing to fully commit to the process if we work together?"

Part 5: The Invitation (2-3 minutes)

Goal: Invite them to take the next step (if it's a good fit)

If Good Fit - Direct Invitation:

"[Name], based on everything you've shared, I really believe I can help you [achieve their desired outcome]. I'd love to work with you. How do you feel about moving forward?"

[If yes] "Wonderful! Let's get you scheduled for your first session. I have availability on [dates/times]. What works best for you? I'll send you the welcome packet and intake forms today so you can complete them before we meet."

If They Need Time:

"I understand. This is an important decision. I'll follow up with an email that includes everything we discussed and the program details. When would be a good time for us to reconnect? I want to be respectful of your process while also making sure you don't lose momentum on something that clearly matters to you."

If Not a Good Fit:

"[Name], I've really enjoyed our conversation. Based on what you've shared, I think you might benefit more from [appropriate referral—therapist, dietitian, doctor, etc.]. That's outside my scope as a health coach, but I'd be happy to recommend some resources if helpful. [Provide referral if appropriate]."

Closing

"Thank you so much for taking the time to connect today, [Name]. Regardless of what you decide, I hope this conversation has given you some clarity about what's possible for your health. [If enrolled: I'm so excited to begin this journey with you!] [If thinking: I'll be in touch, and please reach out if any other questions come up.] Take care!"

Discovery Call Tips

  • Listen more than you speak — Aim for 70/30 (them/you)
  • Get curious — Ask follow-up questions to go deeper
  • Reflect back — Show you've heard and understood
  • Be genuine — This is a conversation, not a performance
  • Trust the process — Not everyone is your ideal client, and that's okay
  • Don't be attached to the outcome — Your job is to serve and invite
