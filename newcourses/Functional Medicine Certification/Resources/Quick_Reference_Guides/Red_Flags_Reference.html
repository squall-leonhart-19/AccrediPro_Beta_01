Red Flags Quick Reference

When to Refer for Medical Evaluation

CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY FOR:

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Suicidal ideation with plan/intent
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Seizure (first time or prolonged)

General Red Flags

Recommend immediate medical evaluation for any of these:

Unexplained Weight Changes

  • Unintentional weight loss > 10 lbs or 5% body weight in 6-12 months
  • Rapid unexplained weight gain
  • Weight loss with increased appetite

Fatigue

  • Severe fatigue unrelieved by rest
  • Sudden onset of extreme fatigue
  • Fatigue with other concerning symptoms (fever, pain)

Pain

  • Severe or sudden onset pain
  • Pain that wakes from sleep
  • Pain unresponsive to OTC pain relief
  • Pain with fever or other symptoms

Fever

  • Fever > 103°F (39.4°C)
  • Fever lasting > 3 days
  • Fever with stiff neck, confusion, or rash
  • Unexplained recurring fevers

System-Specific Red Flags

Cardiovascular

  • Chest pain, pressure, or tightness (especially with exertion)
  • Shortness of breath at rest or with minimal exertion
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat (new onset)
  • Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet (especially one-sided)
  • Fainting or near-fainting episodes
  • Persistent dizziness or lightheadedness

Digestive System

  • Blood in stool (red or black/tarry)
  • Persistent vomiting or vomiting blood
  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Unexplained change in bowel habits lasting > 2 weeks
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes)
  • Abdominal mass or unexplained bloating
  • Unintentional weight loss with GI symptoms

Neurological

  • Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of life")
  • Headache with fever and stiff neck
  • New onset seizures
  • Sudden vision changes or vision loss
  • Sudden weakness or numbness (especially one-sided)
  • Slurred speech or difficulty speaking
  • Confusion or sudden personality changes
  • Balance or coordination problems (sudden onset)
  • Progressive memory loss or cognitive decline

Respiratory

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Coughing up blood
  • Wheezing (new onset)
  • Persistent cough > 3 weeks
  • Chest pain with breathing
  • Blue lips or fingernails

Endocrine/Metabolic

  • Excessive thirst with frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss with increased appetite
  • Heat or cold intolerance (significant change)
  • Significant hair loss
  • Skin changes (darkening, thickening)
  • Signs of blood sugar extremes (confusion, shakiness, sweating)

Musculoskeletal

  • Joint swelling with redness and heat
  • Bone pain (especially at night)
  • Back pain with bladder/bowel changes
  • Back pain with fever
  • Progressive weakness
  • Pain after recent trauma/injury

Skin

  • New moles or changes in existing moles (ABCDE: asymmetry, border, color, diameter, evolution)
  • Non-healing wounds or sores
  • Spreading rash with fever
  • Signs of infection (increasing redness, warmth, swelling, pus)
  • Unexplained bruising

Women's Health Red Flags

Menstrual/Reproductive

  • Heavy bleeding (soaking through pad/tampon hourly)
  • Bleeding between periods or after menopause
  • Severe pelvic pain
  • Missed periods with possibility of pregnancy
  • Unusual vaginal discharge with fever or pain
  • New breast lump or nipple discharge

Pregnancy-Related

  • Vaginal bleeding during pregnancy
  • Severe abdominal pain during pregnancy
  • Sudden swelling of face/hands
  • Severe headache with vision changes
  • Decreased fetal movement
  • Signs of preterm labor

Mental Health Red Flags

Immediate Mental Health Referral/Crisis Resources:

  • Suicidal thoughts or statements (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
  • Thoughts of harming others
  • Active psychosis (hallucinations, delusions)
  • Severe self-harm or self-injury
  • Inability to care for self due to mental health

Recommend Mental Health Evaluation For:

  • Persistent depressed mood > 2 weeks
  • Severe anxiety interfering with daily function
  • Panic attacks
  • Signs of eating disorder (restriction, binging, purging)
  • Significant sleep disturbance with mood changes
  • Substance use affecting health or function
  • Trauma symptoms affecting daily life
  • Significant personality or behavior changes

Eating Disorder Warning Signs

Eating disorders require specialized treatment. Refer to eating disorder specialist if you observe:

  • Extreme restriction of food intake
  • Obsessive calorie counting or food rules
  • Binge eating episodes
  • Purging behaviors (vomiting, laxative use, excessive exercise)
  • Distorted body image
  • Significant weight loss or underweight
  • Preoccupation with food, weight, or body size
  • Avoiding eating with others
  • Excessive exercise despite injury or illness
  • Loss of menstrual period (in those who should be menstruating)

Note: Health coaching for nutrition should be suspended and client referred to appropriate treatment team (MD, therapist, RD specializing in eating disorders).

Age-Specific Considerations (Women 35-55+)

Additional Screening Reminders

Encourage clients to stay current on age-appropriate screenings:

  • Annual mammogram (typically starting age 40-50, discuss with provider)
  • Cervical cancer screening (Pap smear/HPV testing)
  • Colorectal cancer screening (starting age 45)
  • Bone density screening (typically age 65, earlier with risk factors)
  • Cardiovascular risk assessment
  • Skin cancer screening (especially with risk factors)

How to Communicate Referrals

Sample Language:

"Based on what you're describing, I think it would be really valuable to have your doctor take a look at this. [Specific symptom] warrants medical evaluation to rule out anything that needs medical attention. I want to make sure you're getting the best care possible. Would you like help figuring out next steps?"

Key Points:

  • Express care and concern, not alarm
  • Be clear about what warrants evaluation
  • Empower them to advocate for themselves
  • Offer to help them prepare questions for their provider
  • Document that you made the referral recommendation
