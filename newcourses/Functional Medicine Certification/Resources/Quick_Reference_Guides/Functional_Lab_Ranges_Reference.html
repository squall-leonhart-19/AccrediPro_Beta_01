Functional Lab Ranges

Quick Reference Guide for Health Coaches

Scope of Practice Reminder

As health coaches, we do not interpret labs diagnostically or make treatment recommendations based on lab values. This reference is for educational purposes only—to help you understand what markers mean so you can have informed conversations. Always refer clients to their healthcare provider for lab interpretation and medical decisions.

How to Use This Guide

  • Conventional ranges are based on statistical averages (often 95% of the population) and are used for diagnosing disease
  • Functional/optimal ranges are narrower and aim for optimal function, not just absence of disease
  • Use this information to educate clients and help them prepare questions for their providers
  • Encourage clients to request copies of their labs and discuss results with their doctors

Key Takeaways for Coaching Conversations

  • Empower clients to request comprehensive testing and copies of their results
  • Encourage questions—help clients prepare questions for their providers
  • Look at trends—a single value matters less than patterns over time
  • Consider the whole picture—labs are one piece of the health puzzle
  • Stay in scope—always refer to providers for interpretation and treatment decisions
