Designing Effective Coaching Packages

Package Components

Duration

3-Month Package: Good starting point. Allows time for assessment, implementation, and initial results. Popular for focused concerns.

6-Month Package: Better for complex cases, multiple issues, or deeper transformation. Allows for complete protocol implementation and adjustment.

12-Month Package: For comprehensive transformation or ongoing support. Often priced at a discount. Common for VIP or premium offerings.

Session Frequency and Length

Weekly 60-min: High-touch, intensive support. Good for complex situations or clients who need more accountability.

Biweekly 60-min: Common and effective. Allows time for implementation between sessions.

Monthly 90-min: Less intensive, good for maintenance phase or experienced clients.

Additional Support

  • Email/message support: Between-session questions and check-ins
  • Resource library: Handouts, guides, recipes, trackers
  • Emergency support: Brief calls for urgent needs
  • Lab review: If within scope and training
  • Meal planning: If you offer nutrition guidance

Sample Package Structures

Foundation Package (3 Months)

  • Initial comprehensive assessment (90 min)
  • 6 biweekly coaching sessions (60 min each)
  • Personalized protocol and resources
  • Email support between sessions
  • Progress tracking tools

Price Range: $997 - $2,500

Transformation Package (6 Months)

  • Initial comprehensive assessment (90 min)
  • 12 biweekly coaching sessions (60 min each)
  • Personalized protocol with quarterly adjustments
  • Unlimited email support
  • Resource library access
  • 2 brief check-in calls between sessions if needed

Price Range: $2,500 - $5,000

Premium/VIP Package (6-12 Months)

  • Initial deep-dive assessment (2 hours)
  • Weekly coaching sessions (first 3 months), then biweekly
  • Voxer/WhatsApp access for between-session support
  • Lab interpretation support (as appropriate to scope)
  • Customized meal plans and resources
  • Priority scheduling

Price Range: $5,000 - $15,000+

Pricing Your Services

Pricing triggers more anxiety than almost any other business decision. Let's address it head-on.

Pricing Approaches

Value-Based Pricing

Price based on the value of the transformation, not your time. Ask: What is solving this problem worth to the client?

  • Energy and quality of life: Priceless
  • Reduced healthcare costs: Thousands per year
  • Improved work performance: Career and income impact
  • Better relationships: Can't put a price on it
  • Prevention of disease: Decades of life quality

Sustainability-Based Pricing

Calculate what you need to charge to sustain yourself and your practice:

  • Annual income goal: $X
  • Business expenses: Technology, insurance, continuing education, etc.
  • Working weeks/year: Account for vacation, sick time
  • Client capacity: How many clients can you effectively serve?

Pricing Mindset

Your Price Reflects Your Value, Not Your Worth

You're not selling your hoursâ€”you're selling transformation. A client isn't buying 60 minutes of your time; they're buying the ability to have energy, clarity, and health. Price the transformation.

Underpricing Harms Everyone

When you underprice, you can't sustain your practice, you attract less committed clients, and you devalue the entire coaching profession. Sustainable pricing serves everyone.

Investment Creates Commitment

Clients who invest meaningfully show up, do the work, and get results. Free or cheap coaching often produces free or cheap results. The investment is part of the transformation.

Presenting Your Pricing

How you present pricing matters as much as the number itself:

Pricing Presentation Best Practices

  • Lead with transformation: Describe what they'll achieve before mentioning price
  • Anchor to value: "For the price of a daily coffee, you get your health back"
  • State price confidently: No apologizing, minimizing, or over-explaining
  • Offer payment plans: Make investment accessible without discounting
  • Give space: State the price, then pause. Let them respond.
  • Have written materials: Follow up verbal conversations with proposal/email

Handling Price Objections

"I can't afford it."

Explore: "I understand investment is a consideration. Can you share more about what's holding you back?" Often there are other factors. If truly financial, discuss payment plans or whether the timing is right. Never discountâ€”it devalues your work and attracts the wrong clients.

"I need to think about it."

Explore: "Of courseâ€”this is a significant decision. What specifically would you like to think through? Sometimes talking it out helps." Often they have an unstated concern you can address.

"It costs more than I expected."

Explore: "What were you expecting?" This opens conversation about what they're comparing to, what value they see, and whether you've communicated the transformation effectively.
