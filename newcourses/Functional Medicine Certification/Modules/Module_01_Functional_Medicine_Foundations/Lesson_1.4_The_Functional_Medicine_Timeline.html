Case Study: Rachel's Autoimmune Mystery

Rachel, 44, was diagnosed with Hashimoto's thyroiditis two years ago. But when we constructed her timeline, a fascinating pattern emerged:

  • Age 16: Mono (Epstein-Barr virus)
  • Age 22: Started birth control pills
  • Age 28: Severe food poisoning while traveling
  • Age 32: Divorce and job loss (major life stress)
  • Age 34: Started noticing fatigue, weight gain
  • Age 42: Finally diagnosed with Hashimoto's

The disease didn't appear from nowhere at age 42. The timeline revealed years of accumulating insults that finally tipped into autoimmunity.

What Is the Functional Medicine Timeline?

The timeline is a chronological map of significant events in a client's life—from preconception through the present. It captures:

Health events Life stressors Environmental exposures Medications Surgeries & treatments Dietary changes Symptom onset

The timeline answers the most important question in functional medicine: "When did you last feel well, and what changed?"

Why This Matters

Chronic conditions don't appear suddenly. They develop over years—sometimes decades—through accumulated insults, triggers, and mediating factors. The timeline makes this invisible process visible, revealing patterns that point directly to root causes.

The Structure of a Timeline

A comprehensive timeline covers three major periods:

1. Pre-Birth & Early Life (Antecedents)

  • Mother's health during pregnancy
  • Birth type (vaginal vs. C-section)
  • Infant feeding (breast vs. formula)
  • Early antibiotic exposure
  • Childhood illnesses and treatments
  • Family environment and stress

2. Key Events Through Life (Triggers)

  • Infections (mono, Lyme, GI infections, etc.)
  • Physical or emotional trauma
  • Major life stressors (divorce, job loss, death)
  • Surgeries and hospitalizations
  • Medications (especially antibiotics, hormones, PPIs)
  • Environmental exposures (mold, toxins, moves)

3. Symptom Development & Current State (Mediators)

  • When symptoms first appeared
  • How symptoms evolved over time
  • What makes symptoms better or worse
  • Current diet and lifestyle habits
  • Current medications and supplements
  • Current stressors and support systems

Key Events to Capture

Not every life event matters equally. Here are the high-impact events that most often connect to chronic health issues:

Category Examples Why It Matters
Infections Mono (EBV), Lyme, strep, food poisoning, viral illnesses Can trigger autoimmunity, chronic fatigue, gut dysfunction
Antibiotics Courses, frequency, especially in childhood Disrupts microbiome, sets stage for dysbiosis
Hormonal Events Birth control, pregnancy, perimenopause, HRT Major shifts in hormonal balance and metabolism
GI Events Food poisoning, traveler's diarrhea, colonoscopies Can trigger SIBO, IBS, leaky gut
Stress Events Divorce, death, job loss, caregiving, trauma Dysregulates HPA axis, suppresses immunity
Environmental Mold exposure, new home/job, chemical exposure Toxic burden, inflammatory triggers
Physical Trauma Accidents, concussions, surgeries Inflammation, nervous system impact, anesthesia effects
Dietary Changes Going vegan, keto, extreme diets, eating disorders Nutrient status, metabolic shifts

Building the Timeline: Questions to Ask

Constructing a timeline requires skillful questioning. Here are the key questions organized by life stage:

Early Life

  • "Do you know anything about your mother's health or stress during pregnancy?"
  • "Were you born vaginally or by C-section?"
  • "Were you breastfed? For how long?"
  • "Did you have frequent ear infections or strep as a child? Antibiotics?"
  • "How would you describe your childhood—stable, stressful, traumatic?"

Adolescence & Young Adulthood

  • "Did you have any significant illnesses—mono, strep, viral infections?"
  • "When did you start birth control, if applicable?"
  • "Any eating disorders or extreme dieting?"
  • "Major stressors—family issues, academic pressure, relationships?"
  • "When do you remember first not feeling 'right'?"

Adulthood

  • "Have you ever had food poisoning or traveler's diarrhea?"
  • "Any surgeries, hospitalizations, or significant injuries?"
  • "Major life stressors—divorce, job changes, deaths, moves?"
  • "Pregnancies? How did you feel during and after?"
  • "Any known toxic exposures—mold, chemicals, heavy metals?"

The Key Question

"When did you last feel truly well—like yourself? And what happened around that time?"

Pro Tip

Clients often don't connect events to symptoms. They'll say "I've always had digestive issues" but won't mention the food poisoning in Mexico 10 years ago. Ask specifically. Don't assume they'll volunteer information—they don't know what's relevant.

Interpreting the Timeline

Once you have the timeline, look for patterns and connections:

1

Temporal Clusters

Did multiple events happen around the same time? Stacking insults often tips the system over.

2

Symptom Onset Timing

What happened 6-24 months before symptoms started? This is often where triggers hide.

3

The "Perfect Storm"

Chronic disease often results from multiple factors combining. Look for the convergence point.

4

Known Connections

EBV → autoimmunity. Food poisoning → IBS/SIBO. Stress → hormonal imbalance. Look for these patterns.

5

Ongoing Mediators

What current factors are keeping the condition active? These are your intervention targets.

What Rachel's Timeline Reveals

Applying this analysis to Rachel:

  • EBV at 16 — Epstein-Barr virus is strongly associated with Hashimoto's. It may have been silently reactivating over the years.
  • Birth control — Can deplete nutrients (B vitamins, zinc, selenium) essential for thyroid function and increase intestinal permeability.
  • Food poisoning — May have triggered gut dysfunction/leaky gut, increasing systemic inflammation.
  • Major stress at 32 — Likely the final trigger. Chronic stress is a known precipitant of autoimmune flares.
  • 8 years to diagnosis — The disease process was happening long before labs became abnormal enough to diagnose.

The intervention? Don't just prescribe thyroid medication. Address the mediators: heal the gut, support detoxification, balance the stress response, optimize nutrients. This comprehensive approach—informed by the timeline—produces far better outcomes than treating the thyroid in isolation.

Client-Friendly Language

"Your Hashimoto's didn't come from nowhere. Looking at your timeline, we can see how different events over the years—that mono infection, the stress of your divorce, potentially some gut issues—all contributed. This is actually good news because it means there are multiple places we can intervene to support your healing."

Common Timeline Patterns

After constructing many timelines, certain patterns emerge repeatedly:

"Post-Infectious" Pattern

Health was fine → significant infection → never quite the same. Think: post-viral fatigue, post-infectious IBS, post-Lyme syndrome.

"Stress Cascade" Pattern

Major life stress → sleep issues → weight gain → mood changes → multiple symptoms. The stress dominoes fell.

"Antibiotic Aftermath" Pattern

Multiple antibiotic courses (especially in childhood) → gut issues → food sensitivities → immune dysregulation.

"Hormonal Shift" Pattern

Health changed with puberty, birth control, pregnancy, or perimenopause. Hormonal transitions can unmask latent issues.

"Environmental Exposure" Pattern

Moved to new home or job → new symptoms appeared. Think: mold, water damage, sick building syndrome.
