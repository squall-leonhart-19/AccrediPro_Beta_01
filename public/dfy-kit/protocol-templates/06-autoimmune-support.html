Autoimmune Basics: In autoimmune disease, the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues. While we can't "cure" autoimmunity, we can reduce triggers that drive inflammation and support immune regulation through diet, lifestyle, and targeted support.
The 5 Pillars of Autoimmune Support

1 Heal the Gut (70% of immune system lives there)

  • Address intestinal permeability ("leaky gut")
  • Remove inflammatory foods
  • Support gut lining repair (see Gut Healing Protocol)
  • Restore beneficial bacteria
  • Consider testing for infections, SIBO, parasites

2 Remove Inflammatory Triggers

  • Eliminate common inflammatory foods
  • Identify personal food sensitivities
  • Reduce environmental toxins
  • Address chronic infections
  • Minimize stress (major trigger)

3 Optimize Nutrient Status

  • Test and correct deficiencies (D, B12, iron, zinc)
  • Focus on nutrient-dense whole foods
  • Support methylation pathways
  • Ensure adequate protein for tissue repair

4 Balance the Immune System

  • Support regulatory T cells (Tregs)
  • Reduce Th1/Th2 imbalances
  • Address chronic infections that drive inflammation
  • Use immunomodulating supplements wisely

5 Manage Stress & Support Adrenals

  • Chronic stress = immune dysregulation
  • Daily stress reduction practices
  • Adequate sleep (non-negotiable)
  • Gentle, not intense exercise
Autoimmune Protocol (AIP) Diet

The AIP is an elimination diet that removes common immune triggers, then strategically reintroduces foods to identify personal triggers.

‚úì AIP-Compliant Foods

  • All vegetables (except nightshades)
  • Fruits (in moderation)
  • Quality meats (grass-fed, pasture-raised)
  • Wild-caught fish and seafood
  • Organ meats (nutrient-dense)
  • Bone broth
  • Healthy fats (olive oil, coconut, avocado)
  • Fermented foods (coconut yogurt, sauerkraut)
  • Herbs and non-seed spices
  • Coconut products

‚ùå Eliminate During AIP

  • Grains: All (including gluten-free)
  • Legumes: Beans, lentils, peanuts, soy
  • Dairy: All forms
  • Eggs: Especially whites
  • Nightshades: Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, potatoes
  • Nuts & seeds: All (including seed-based spices)
  • Refined sugars: All
  • Alcohol: All
  • Processed foods: All
  • Food additives: MSG, sulfites, gums
Reintroduction: After 30-90 days strict elimination, systematically reintroduce one food at a time, waiting 3-5 days between foods to observe reactions. Keep a detailed symptom journal.

Recommended Testing
  • Basic labs: CBC, CMP, inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR)
  • Nutrient levels: Vitamin D, B12, iron panel, zinc, magnesium RBC
  • Thyroid panel: TSH, Free T4, Free T3, TPO, TG antibodies
  • Gut testing: Comprehensive stool analysis, SIBO test if indicated
  • Food sensitivity testing: Consider if elimination diet inconclusive
  • Infection panels: EBV, Lyme, other chronic infections if suspected
