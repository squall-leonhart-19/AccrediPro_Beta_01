Adrenal Recovery Protocol

HPA Axis Support & Stress Recovery

Understanding Adrenal Dysfunction: "Adrenal fatigue" is more accurately described as HPA (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal) axis dysfunction. The adrenals don't actually "fatigue" but communication between the brain and adrenals becomes dysregulated due to chronic stress.
Stages of HPA Dysfunction

Stage 1: Alarm (High Cortisol)

Symptoms: Wired but tired, anxiety, insomnia, difficulty calming down, weight gain (especially belly), high blood sugar, frequent illness

Focus: Calming practices, reduce stimulants, blood sugar balance, sleep support

Stage 2: Resistance (Fluctuating Cortisol)

Symptoms: Energy crashes, afternoon slump, need caffeine to function, sleep issues, brain fog, irritability

Focus: Stabilize cortisol curve, adaptogens, consistent routine, gentle exercise

Stage 3: Exhaustion (Low Cortisol)

Symptoms: Profound fatigue, can't get out of bed, depression, low motivation, salt cravings, dizziness, frequent sickness

Focus: Rest, nourishment, very gentle movement only, foundational supplements

Mindset & Boundaries
Recovery requires lifestyle changes, not just supplements. The habits that led to HPA dysfunction must be addressed. This often means:
  • Saying "no" more often
  • Delegating responsibilities
  • Reducing work hours if possible
  • Addressing toxic relationships
  • Making rest a priority, not a luxury
  • Accepting that recovery takes time (3-24 months)
