Hormone Balance Protocol

Women's Hormonal Wellness Plan

Foundation: Blood Sugar Balance

Blood sugar regulation is foundational to hormone balance. Insulin affects every other hormone.

  • Eat protein with every meal (20-30g)
  • Include healthy fats to slow glucose absorption
  • Choose complex carbohydrates over refined
  • Avoid skipping meals - especially breakfast
  • Limit sugar and refined carbohydrates
  • Consider eating carbs with dinner vs. morning

Seed Cycling Protocol
Seed cycling uses specific seeds during each phase of the menstrual cycle to support natural hormone balance.

Follicular Phase (Days 1-14)

  • 1 tbsp flaxseeds (ground)
  • 1 tbsp pumpkin seeds
  • Supports estrogen production
  • Lignans help balance estrogen

Luteal Phase (Days 15-28)

  • 1 tbsp sunflower seeds
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • Supports progesterone production
  • Selenium and zinc for hormone synthesis

Liver & Estrogen Detoxification

The liver metabolizes and eliminates excess estrogen. Support with:

  • Cruciferous vegetables daily (broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower)
  • Beets, carrots, artichokes for bile flow
  • Lemon water in the morning
  • Dandelion root tea
  • Minimize alcohol (competes for liver enzymes)
  • Adequate protein for phase 2 detox pathways
Reduce Endocrine Disruptors

In Your Home

  • Switch to glass/stainless containers
  • Avoid heating food in plastic
  • Filter drinking water
  • Choose organic when possible
  • Avoid non-stick cookware
  • Use natural cleaning products

Personal Care

  • Clean beauty products
  • Avoid fragrance/parfum
  • Natural deodorant
  • Clean period products
  • Read labels - avoid parabens
  • Use EWG Skin Deep database
Stress & Cortisol Management

Chronic stress steals pregnenolone from sex hormone production ("pregnenolone steal"):

  • Daily stress reduction practice (meditation, deep breathing, yoga)
  • Prioritize 7-9 hours of sleep
  • Set boundaries around work and obligations
  • Consider adaptogens (ashwagandha, rhodiola)
  • Reduce high-intensity exercise if overtrained
  • Address caffeine intake
