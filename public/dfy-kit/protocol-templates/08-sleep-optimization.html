Sleep Optimization Protocol

Deep, Restorative Sleep for Health & Healing

Why Sleep Matters: Sleep is when your body repairs, detoxifies, consolidates memories, and regulates hormones. Poor sleep is linked to weight gain, insulin resistance, inflammation, weakened immunity, and cognitive decline. Optimizing sleep is foundational to all other health goals.

Light Exposure Protocol

‚òÄÔ∏è Morning (Critical!)

  • Get bright light within 30 min of waking
  • 10-30 minutes of outdoor light
  • No sunglasses during this time
  • Light box if dark climate (10,000 lux)
  • Sets circadian rhythm for the day
  • Helps you feel sleepy 14-16 hours later

üåô Evening (Equally Critical!)

  • Dim lights after sunset
  • Blue light blocking glasses after 8 PM
  • Night shift mode on all devices
  • Red/amber bulbs in bedroom
  • Avoid bright bathroom lights
  • Complete darkness for sleep
Nutrition for Sleep

‚úì Sleep-Promoting Foods

  • Tryptophan: Turkey, eggs, pumpkin seeds
  • Magnesium: Leafy greens, nuts, seeds
  • Complex carbs: Sweet potato, rice (at dinner)
  • Tart cherry: Natural melatonin source
  • Kiwi: Shown to improve sleep
  • Fatty fish: Omega-3s and vitamin D
  • Chamomile/passionflower tea: Calming
  • Warm milk: (if tolerated) Tryptophan + ritual

‚ùå Avoid for Better Sleep

  • Caffeine after 2 PM: 6+ hour half-life
  • Alcohol: Disrupts REM and deep sleep
  • Sugar before bed: Blood sugar crashes
  • Heavy/late meals: Finish 2-3 hours before bed
  • Spicy foods: Can cause reflux and heat
  • Too much liquid: Bathroom waking
  • Chocolate: Contains caffeine
