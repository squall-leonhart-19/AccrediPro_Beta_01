Anti-Inflammatory Protocol

Reducing Chronic Inflammation Naturally

Understanding Inflammation: Acute inflammation is protective (healing a cut). Chronic, low-grade inflammation is harmful, driving conditions like heart disease, diabetes, autoimmunity, obesity, depression, and accelerated aging. This protocol addresses root causes while providing anti-inflammatory support.

Pro-Inflammatory Foods to Eliminate

‚ùå Major Inflammatory Triggers

  • Industrial seed oils: Canola, soybean, corn, cottonseed, sunflower, safflower
  • Added sugars: All forms - white, brown, HFCS, agave
  • Refined carbs: White bread, pasta, crackers, pastries
  • Trans fats: Partially hydrogenated oils, margarine
  • Processed meats: Hot dogs, sausages, deli meats
  • Deep fried foods: All types

  • Artificial additives: Colors, flavors, preservatives
  • Excess omega-6: Imbalances omega ratio
  • Factory-farmed meats: Higher inflammatory markers
Optimizing Omega-6:Omega-3 Ratio
The Goal: Move from typical Western ratio (20:1) toward ideal (2:1 to 4:1). This requires both reducing omega-6 AND increasing omega-3.

‚Üë Increase Omega-3

  • Fatty fish 3-4x per week
  • Fish oil supplement 2-4g EPA/DHA
  • Grass-fed beef (better ratio)
  • Pastured eggs (omega-3 enriched)
  • Walnuts, flax, chia, hemp daily

‚Üì Decrease Omega-6

  • Eliminate seed/vegetable oils
  • Cook with olive oil, coconut, butter
  • Avoid restaurant/fast food (seed oil heavy)
  • Read labels - oils hidden everywhere
  • Choose grass-fed over grain-fed

Monitoring Inflammation
  • hs-CRP: High-sensitivity C-reactive protein (goal: <1.0 mg/L)
  • ESR: Erythrocyte sedimentation rate
  • Ferritin: Can indicate inflammation when elevated
  • Homocysteine: Associated with vascular inflammation
  • Omega-3 Index: Goal >8% (reflects cell membrane composition)
  • Fasting insulin: Elevated insulin drives inflammation
