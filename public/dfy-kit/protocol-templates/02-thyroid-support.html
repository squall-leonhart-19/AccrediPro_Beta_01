Gut-Thyroid Connection

About 20% of thyroid hormone conversion happens in the gut. Supporting gut health is essential:

  • Address intestinal permeability (see Gut Healing Protocol)
  • Support healthy microbiome with probiotics and fermented foods
  • Consider testing for and removing food sensitivities
  • Ensure adequate fiber from vegetables
  • Address constipation (common in hypothyroidism)

Medication Considerations
If taking thyroid medication:
  • Take thyroid medication on an empty stomach, 30-60 minutes before food
  • Wait 4 hours before taking calcium, iron, or fiber supplements
  • Coffee should be consumed after medication has absorbed
  • Be consistent with timing every day
  • Never adjust medication without physician guidance
Recommended Testing
  • Full thyroid panel: TSH, Free T4, Free T3, Reverse T3, TPO antibodies, TG antibodies
  • Nutrient levels: Vitamin D, Iron/ferritin, B12, Selenium
  • Frequency: Every 6-8 weeks when adjusting, then every 3-6 months
