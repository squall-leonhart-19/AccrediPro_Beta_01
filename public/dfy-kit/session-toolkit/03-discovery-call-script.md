# Discovery Call Script
# A Guided Framework for Enrollment Conversations

---

## PRE-CALL CHECKLIST

Before the call:
- [ ] Review their intake form/application (if submitted)
- [ ] Research their social media/background (if applicable)
- [ ] Prepare your energy - be present and grounded
- [ ] Have your offer details ready (pricing, schedule, etc.)
- [ ] Set up your space (quiet, good lighting if video)
- [ ] Open your notes document

---

## CALL STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

| Section | Time | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| Welcome & Rapport | 3-5 min | Build connection |
| Their Story | 10-15 min | Understand situation |
| Goals & Vision | 5-7 min | Clarify desired outcome |
| Present Solution | 5-7 min | Share how you help |
| Address Questions | 5-10 min | Handle objections |
| Invitation | 3-5 min | Make the offer |

**Total: 30-45 minutes**

---

## SECTION 1: WELCOME & RAPPORT (3-5 min)

**Start warm and welcoming. Set the tone.**

### Opening Script:

> "Hi [Name]! It's so great to meet you. Thank you so much for taking the time to chat today."
>
> "Before we dive in, how are you doing? How has your day been?"

[Small talk - 1-2 minutes max. Be genuine but keep it moving.]

### Set the Agenda:

> "So I'm really excited to learn more about you and what's going on with your health. Here's what I thought we'd cover today:"
>
> "First, I'd love to hear about your story - what's brought you to this point. Then we'll talk about what you're hoping to achieve. And finally, I'll share a bit about how I work and we can see if it might be a good fit. Sound good?"
>
> "And just to set expectations - this is YOUR call. My goal is to understand if and how I can help you. There's no pressure here, okay?"

*[Get their buy-in before proceeding]*

---

## SECTION 2: THEIR STORY (10-15 min)

**Goal: Deeply understand their current situation and struggles.**

### Opening Question:

> "So tell me, [Name] - what's going on that made you want to book this call? What's happening with your health right now?"

*[Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take notes.]*

### Follow-Up Questions:

**Go deeper with these questions as needed:**

**About their symptoms/struggles:**
> "Can you tell me more about that?"
> "How long has this been going on?"
> "How is this affecting your daily life?"
> "What does a typical day look like for you right now?"

**About what they've tried:**
> "What have you already tried to address this?"
> "How did that work for you?"
> "Why do you think it didn't give lasting results?"

**About the impact:**
> "How is this affecting your work/relationships/quality of life?"
> "On a scale of 1-10, how much is this impacting you?"
> "What is this costing you - in time, energy, happiness?"

**About the emotional side:**
> "How does it FEEL to be dealing with this?"
> "What emotions come up when you think about your health?"

### Reflective Listening:

**Mirror back what you've heard:**

> "So what I'm hearing is... [summarize their situation]. Is that right?"
>
> "It sounds like you're feeling really [emotion] about this."

*[Make them feel heard and understood]*

---

## SECTION 3: GOALS & VISION (5-7 min)

**Goal: Clarify their desired outcome and connect emotionally to it.**

### Core Questions:

> "If we fast-forward 3-6 months from now and everything has changed for the better... what does that look like for you?"

> "If you woke up tomorrow and this was no longer a problem, what would be different?"

> "What would it FEEL like to [achieve their goal]?"

### Dig into the Why:

> "Why is this important to you right now?"
> "What would this mean for your [family/career/quality of life]?"
> "Why now? What made you decide to take action?"

### Check for Commitment:

> "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to making this change?"
> "What would it take for you to get to a 10?"

---

## SECTION 4: PRESENT YOUR SOLUTION (5-7 min)

**Goal: Position your offer as the bridge from where they are to where they want to be.**

### Transition:

> "Thank you for sharing all of that with me, [Name]. I really appreciate your openness."
>
> "Based on everything you've told me, I think I can help. Would you like to hear a bit about how I work?"

*[Get permission before presenting]*

### Present Your Approach:

> "So here's the thing - what you're experiencing [problem] isn't something you have to just live with. And the reason other approaches haven't worked is likely because [reason - didn't address root cause, wasn't personalized, lacked support, etc.]."
>
> "My approach is different. Here's what I do..."

**Share your unique methodology:**

> "I work with clients like you to [transformation] through [your methodology/approach]."
>
> "What makes this different is:
> 1. [Unique differentiator 1]
> 2. [Unique differentiator 2]  
> 3. [Unique differentiator 3]"

### Paint the Picture:

> "So for you, specifically, here's what I see..."
>
> "[Describe what working together might look like for their specific situation]"
>
> "[Share relevant client success story if appropriate]"

### Present the Offer:

> "The way I work with clients is through my [Program Name]."
>
> "It's a [X-week/month] program that includes:
> - [Component 1]
> - [Component 2]
> - [Component 3]
> - [Component 4]"
>
> "The investment is [price] - and I do offer [payment plan if applicable]."

*[Pause and let them absorb]*

---

## SECTION 5: ADDRESS QUESTIONS & OBJECTIONS (5-10 min)

**Goal: Handle concerns with empathy and clarity.**

### Open the Floor:

> "What questions do you have for me?"
>
> "What's coming up for you as I share this?"

### Common Objections & Responses:

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Absolutely, this is an important decision. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?"
>
> "What information would help you make this decision?"
>
> "Can I ask - what would be different in a week or a month that would help you decide? [Gently explore if thinking = avoiding]"

**"It's too expensive"**
> "I totally understand - it's an investment. Can I ask what you've already spent on [supplements, doctors, programs] that haven't worked?"
>
> "What do you think it's costing you NOT to solve this - in energy, productivity, happiness?"
>
> "I offer [payment plan option] - would breaking it into payments help?"

**"I don't have time"**
> "I hear you - time is so precious. Can I ask how much time you're currently losing to [their symptoms/struggles]?"
>
> "The program is designed to [fit into busy schedules / be efficient / X hours per week]. What does your schedule look like?"
>
> "What if investing this time now could give you MORE time and energy in the long run?"

**"I should ask my spouse/partner"**
> "Of course - that's so important. What do you think they'd say?"
>
> "What concerns might they have that I could help address?"
>
> "Would it help if I provided information you could share with them?"

**"I've tried everything and nothing works"**
> "I completely understand that frustration. Can I ask - has anything you've tried included [your unique approach]?"
>
> "The reason most approaches don't work is [explain root cause]. That's exactly what we address."
>
> "Would it help to share a story of a client who felt the same way?"

**"I'm not sure this will work for me"**
> "What makes you feel that way?"
>
> "[Address their specific concern]"
>
> "I have a [guarantee/approach to tracking progress] to ensure we're on the right track."

---

## SECTION 6: THE INVITATION (3-5 min)

**Goal: Make a clear invitation and get a decision.**

### If They're Ready:

> "It sounds like this is really resonating with you. Are you ready to get started?"
>
> "I'm excited to work with you. Here's what happens next..."
>
> [Walk them through enrollment process]

### If They're Hesitant:

> "[Name], what's your gut telling you right now?"
>
> "What would need to be true for you to say yes today?"
>
> "If you were 100% ready, what would that look like?"

### Create Gentle Urgency (if true):

> "I want to be transparent - I only take [X clients at a time] to ensure everyone gets personal attention. Right now I have [X spots available]."
>
> "The [bonus/pricing] is available until [date]."

### If It's a No (for now):

> "I completely understand. Thank you for being honest with me."
>
> "Would it be okay if I stayed in touch? I'll keep sending helpful content, and the door is always open if things change."
>
> "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"
