7 Strategies for Stable Blood Sugar

1

Protein at Every Meal

Include 20-30g protein at each meal. Protein slows glucose absorption and promotes satiety.

2

Never Eat Carbs Naked

Always pair carbs with protein, fat, or fiber. Naked carbs spike blood sugar rapidly.

3

Eat in the Right Order

Vegetables first, then protein/fat, carbs last. This can reduce glucose spikes by 75%!

4

Move After Meals

A 10-15 min walk after eating helps muscles use glucose, flattening the spike.

5

Start with Savory Breakfast

Protein-rich breakfast sets blood sugar tone for the whole day. Skip the sugar/carbs in AM.

6

Add Vinegar Before Meals

1 tbsp apple cider vinegar in water before meals can reduce glucose response.

7

Manage Stress & Sleep

Both cortisol and sleep deprivation raise blood sugar. These aren't optional for balance.

‚úì Blood Sugar Friendly

  • Proteins: eggs, fish, poultry, meat
  • Healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts
  • Non-starchy veggies: leafy greens, broccoli
  • Complex carbs: sweet potato, quinoa, beans
  • Low-glycemic fruits: berries, green apple
  • Fiber: vegetables, flax, chia
  • Cinnamon: helps insulin sensitivity

‚úó Blood Sugar Spikes

  • Refined sugar: candy, pastries, desserts
  • Simple carbs: white bread, pasta, rice
  • Sugary drinks: soda, juice, sweet coffee
  • Breakfast cereals: most are high-glycemic
  • Dried fruit: concentrated sugar
  • Alcohol: especially on empty stomach
  • Hidden sugars: sauces, dressings, bars
