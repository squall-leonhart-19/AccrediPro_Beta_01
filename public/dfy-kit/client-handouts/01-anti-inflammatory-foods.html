Anti-Inflammatory Foods Guide

Chronic inflammation is linked to most health issues. Use this guide to reduce inflammatory foods and increase anti-inflammatory foods in your diet.

âœ“ ENJOY FREELY

Vegetables

  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale, chard)
  • Cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts)
  • Colorful veggies (bell peppers, tomatoes, beets)
  • Alliums (garlic, onions, leeks)

Fruits

  • Berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • Cherries
  • Citrus fruits
  • Pomegranate

Proteins

  • Wild-caught fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel)
  • Grass-fed beef (in moderation)
  • Pasture-raised poultry
  • Free-range eggs

Healthy Fats

  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Avocados
  • Coconut oil
  • Walnuts, almonds, other raw nuts

Herbs & Spices

  • Turmeric (with black pepper)
  • Ginger
  • Cinnamon
  • Rosemary, oregano, basil

Beverages

  • Green tea / Matcha
  • Bone broth
  • Filtered water
  • Herbal teas

âœ— MINIMIZE OR AVOID

Oils

  • Canola oil
  • Soybean oil
  • Corn oil
  • Vegetable oil blends
  • Margarine

Sugars

  • White sugar
  • High fructose corn syrup
  • Agave nectar
  • Artificial sweeteners

Refined Carbs

  • White bread, pasta, rice
  • Crackers, chips
  • Pastries, baked goods
  • Most breakfast cereals

Processed Foods

  • Fast food
  • Packaged snacks
  • Processed meats (hot dogs, deli meat)
  • Frozen convenience meals

Common Triggers

  • Gluten (for sensitive individuals)
  • Conventional dairy
  • Alcohol (especially excess)
  • Deep-fried foods

Additives

  • MSG
  • Artificial colors/flavors
  • Preservatives
  • Trans fats
