Reframing the Resistance

If a client says "I can't find a memory," don't panic! Simply say: "That's okay. If you *could* imagine what a confident person feels like, what would that be like?" This bypasses the analytical mind.

3. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New practitioners often fall into these traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

The Pitfall Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Rushing the Elicitation Nervousness or wanting to "get to the fix." Slow down. Wait for the client's breathing to change before anchoring.
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome (trying to prove you're an expert). Keep instructions simple. "Close your eyes and remember..." is enough.
Losing Rapport Focusing too much on your notes. Maintain "Soft Eyes" and match the client's vocal tempo.
Weak Future Pacing Forgetting to link the new feeling to a specific future event. Always ask: "When is the next time you'll use this new feeling?"

4. Success Markers

How do you know the session worked? Look for these physiological shifts in your client:

  • Skin Color: A slight flush or change in tone (signaling blood flow change).
  • Breathing: A shift from shallow chest breathing to deeper abdominal breathing.
  • Muscle Tension: Visible relaxation in the shoulders and jaw.
  • Congruence: When they say "I feel better," their voice sounds steady and certain.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Future Pacing" a critical final step in an NLP session?

It bridges the gap between the coaching office and real life. By imagining a future scenario using the new resource, the brain creates a "neural pathway" that makes the new behavior automatic when the situation actually occurs.

2. What should you do if the client's physiology doesn't change during the anchor?

The memory isn't "vivid" enough. Use VAK (Visual, Auditory, Kinesthetic) prompts: "What are you seeing? What do you hear? Where in your body do you feel that?" to intensify the state before re-anchoring.

3. How does rapport help during a Deep Dive session?

Rapport creates a "container" of safety. When a client feels deeply understood (through your mirroring and matching), their nervous system moves out of "fight or flight" and into a state where change is possible.

4. What is a "congruent" response from a client?

It is when the client's words, tone of voice, and body language all match. If they say "I feel great" while frowning and crossing their arms, they are incongruent, and more work is needed.
