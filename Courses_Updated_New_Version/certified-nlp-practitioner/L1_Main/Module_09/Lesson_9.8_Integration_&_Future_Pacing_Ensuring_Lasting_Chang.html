Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Watch out for these four common mistakes that new practitioners make during identity shift sessions:

  • Over-Sympathizing: If you say, "Oh, I know, it's so scary to charge that much," you are agreeing with her limiting belief. Instead, be empathetic but maintain your position.
  • Rushing the Reframe: If you jump to the "solution" before Diane feels heard, she will resist. Use backtracking (repeating her words) before offering the reframe.
  • Ignoring Physiology: If Diane is slumped over while saying she wants to be a coach, her body is lying. Ask her to "Sit the way a $2,500/month coach sits" before continuing the script.
  • The "Why" Trap: Don't ask "Why do you feel like a fraud?" This forces her to find more reasons to feel bad. Ask "How do you know you're feeling that way?" to uncover the structure of the feeling.
Sarah's Insight

In my first year, I used to "over-talk" because I was nervous. If you find yourself talking more than 30% of the time, stop. Use the "Power of the Pause." Let the NLP reframe land in her silence.

Success Markers

How do you know the session worked? Look for these "Green Lights" in your client:

  1. Physiological Shift: Shoulders drop, breathing deepens, or she let out a "big sigh."
  2. Language Change: She stops saying "I'm just a..." and starts saying "I am..." or "I am becoming..."
  3. Insight (The "Aha" Moment): She makes a connection you didn't prompt, such as "Oh! Managing teachers was actually harder than this will be!"
  4. Action Orientation: She asks, "What's our next step?" rather than "Do you think I can do this?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to "backtrack" (repeat) Diane's words before offering a reframe?

Backtracking builds deep rapport and ensures the client feels fully "heard" and "seen." Without this, a reframe can feel like a correction or an argument, causing the client to move into a defensive state.

2. What is the structural difference between asking "Why" and "How" in an NLP context?

"Why" looks for justifications and stories (which reinforces the problem). "How" looks for the process and structure (how they are creating the feeling), which allows the coach to intervene and change the sequence.

3. If Diane says "I'm just a retired teacher," what NLP concept is she demonstrating?

She is demonstrating a Limiting Identity Belief anchored by a linguistic minimizer ("just"). This suggests her self-concept is stuck in a previous role rather than integrated into her current potential.

4. What does "Future Pacing" achieve in this practice lab?

Future pacing allows the client's neurology to "pre-experience" success in the new identity. By mentally rehearsing a positive outcome, the brain creates a neural pathway that makes the actual event feel familiar and achievable.
