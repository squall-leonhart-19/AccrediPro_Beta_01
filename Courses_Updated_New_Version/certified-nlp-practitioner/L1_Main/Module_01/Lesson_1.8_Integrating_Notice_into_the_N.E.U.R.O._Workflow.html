Common Calibration Mistakes

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps. Being aware of them is 50% of the battle.

  • Mind Reading: Assuming you know why a client's skin turned red. (Instead, ask: "I notice a change in color in your face, what is that representing for you right now?")
  • Over-Calibration: Staring so intently at the client that they feel like a lab rat. Keep your gaze relaxed.
  • Ignoring the Baseline: Forgetting that some people just breathe fast naturally. Always calibrate their "normal" state first.
  • Breaking Rapport to Calibrate: Stopping the flow of a deep emotional moment to point out a minor muscle twitch.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "Observation" and "Calibration" in NLP?

Observation is simply seeing a behavior. Calibration is the process of linking that specific behavior (like a lip twitch) to a specific internal state (like doubt) for that specific individual.

2. If Elena's eyes move to her bottom right (her kinesthetic/feeling lead), what should you do?

Pause and give her space. She is processing a feeling or internal dialogue. Interrupting her now would break her internal process and rapport.

3. Why is "Pacing" breathing important before "Leading" a client?

Pacing builds a "neurological bridge" of rapport. Once the client's nervous system feels you are "like them," they will unconsciously follow your lead to a calmer, deeper breathing pattern.

4. What is a "Baseline" in calibration?

A baseline is the client's "normal" stateâ€”their resting breathing rate, posture, and skin tone when they are NOT under stress. You must know the baseline to notice the deviations.
