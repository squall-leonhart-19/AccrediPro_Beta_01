Establishing the 'N' Signal (Notice & Calibrate)

The 6-Step Reframe relies on Unconscious Signaling. This is where your calibration skills (Module 1) become critical. We are looking for an involuntary signal that the unconscious mind uses to say "Yes" or "No."

Common signals include:

  • Ideomotor Responses: A subtle twitch in a finger or a shift in head tilt.
  • Internal Sensations: A sudden warmth in the chest, a "click" in the mind, or a specific visual flash.
  • Auditory Tones: An internal "hum" or a specific word that bubbles up.

The Creative Generation Step

In Step 4, we ask the client's Creative Part to generate alternatives. This is a "U" (Utilize Language) step. We don't suggest the behaviors; we facilitate the unconscious mind to find them. This ensures the solutions are biochemically and ecologically specific to the client.

Coach Tip: The Power of Three

Always insist on at least three alternative behaviors. One is a fixation, two is a dilemma, but three is a choice. Giving the unconscious mind a menu of options prevents it from defaulting back to the old habit when stress levels rise.

The 6-Step Reframe Protocol

Follow this exact sequence to ensure the N.E.U.R.O. integration is successful:

1

Identify the Behavior

Ask the client to identify the behavior or symptom they wish to change. Ensure they describe it as a part of them, not their entire identity.

2

Establish Communication

Ask the part responsible for the behavior to give a "Yes" signal. Calibrate the response (The 'N').

3

Distinguish Intention

Ask the part: "Would you be willing to let me know what you are trying to do for me that is positive?" Wait for the signal.

4

Engage the Creative Part

Ask the Creative Part to generate 3 alternative behaviors that satisfy the intention as well or better than the old behavior.

5

Ecological Agreement

Ask the original part if it is willing to use these new alternatives instead of the old behavior for a trial period.

6

Future Pacing (The 'O')

Optimize the integration by having the client imagine a future situation where the old behavior would have occurred and see themselves using the new choices.

Coach Tip: Handling "No" Signals

If the part gives a "No" signal in Step 5 (refusing the new behaviors), it usually means the alternatives aren't satisfying enough. Simply go back to Step 4 and ask the Creative Part for even better options. Never force the unconscious mind; it‚Äôs a negotiation, not a command.

Ensuring Systemic Harmony (Optimize & Integrate)

A reframe is only as good as its Ecology. In the N.E.U.R.O. framework, "O" (Optimize) requires us to check if the new behavior will disrupt the client's family, career, or self-image.

The Golden Question: "Does any other part of you have any objection to you using these new behaviors?"

If a client is a nurse transitioning to coaching (like many of our students), a new behavior of "setting strict boundaries" might satisfy their need for rest, but another part might object because "nurses are supposed to be selfless." We must integrate both before the change sticks.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it essential to establish a "Yes/No" signal before starting the reframe?

Establishing a signal ensures you are communicating directly with the unconscious mind rather than the client's conscious logical mind. This bypasses conscious resistance and ensures the "part" responsible for the behavior is actually participating in the negotiation.

2. What is the difference between the "Behavior" and the "Positive Intention" in Step 3?

The behavior is the "What" (e.g., overeating, smoking, procrastination). The positive intention is the "Why" (e.g., seeking comfort, social connection, protection from failure). We change the behavior while keeping the intention satisfied.

3. Which part of the N.E.U.R.O. framework corresponds to the 6-Step Reframe's "Future Pacing"?

The "O" (Optimize & Integrate). Future pacing ensures the new behavioral choices are "installed" in the client's future timeline, making the new response automatic when the trigger occurs.

4. What should you do if a client's "part" objects during the Ecology Check (Step 6)?

You must treat the objecting part as a new "part" with its own positive intention. You either re-run the 6-step process for that part or facilitate a Parts Integration to ensure both parts' needs are met by the new behaviors.
