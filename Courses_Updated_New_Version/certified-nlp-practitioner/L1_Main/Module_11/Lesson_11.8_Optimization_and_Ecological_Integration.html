Your Coaching Scenario

In this lab, you are coaching Elena. Elena is a 52-year-old former school teacher who is trying to launch her own consulting business but is "paralyzed" by the fear that she isn't an expert. She feels like a "fraud" every time she tries to post on social media or reach out to potential clients.

Client Profile: Elena

Category Details
Presenting Problem Intense imposter syndrome and "procrastination" disguised as research.
NLP Meta-Program Internal Reference (she looks to others for validation of her worth).
Desired Outcome To feel "legitimate" and take consistent action on her business.
Current State High anxiety, shallow breathing, visual-construct thinking (imagining failure).
Sarah's Insight

When a client like Elena says she "feels like a fraud," she is actually describing a mental movie she's playing in her head. Your job isn't to tell her she's wrong; it's to help her change the editing of that movie.

The Coaching Script

Read the following script out loud. Pay attention to the bolded NLP techniques being used.

YOU (The Coach):
"Elena, I hear you saying that you feel like a fraud when you go to post your services. (Pacing) If I'm hearing you correctly, it's as if there's a specific image of a 'real expert' in your mind, and you're comparing yourself to that image and finding a gap. Is that right?"
ELENA:
"Yes! Exactly. I see these women online who have it all together, and I just feel like a teacher who's trying to play dress-up."
YOU (The Coach):
"I appreciate that honesty. (Validation) Now, just for a moment, I want you to notice... where in your body do you feel that 'dress-up' feeling? (Accessing Kinesthetic)"
ELENA:
"It's like a tightness in my chest. Like I'm holding my breath."
YOU (The Coach):
"Okay, notice that tightness. Now, Elena, you spent 20 years leading a classroom. (Resource Anchoring) When you were in front of those students, did you have to 'play dress-up,' or did you simply possess the knowledge they needed?"
ELENA:
"I just knew my stuff. I didn't think about it."
YOU (The Coach):
"Exactly. So, what if the 'expert' isn't someone who 'has it all together,' but simply someone who has a solution for someone else's problem? (Reframing) How does that shift the tightness in your chest right now?"
Sarah's Insight

Notice how we moved from her internal feeling to her external history. This is a classic NLP pattern of "Resource Retrieval." We are reminding her brain that she already knows how to be an authority.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my first few years, I made all of these! Watch out for these "beginner traps":

  • Over-Explaining the NLP: Don't tell the client "I'm reframing you now." Just do it. The magic is in the experience, not the terminology.
  • Rushing to the Solution: If you pivot Elena too fast, she won't feel heard. You must pace (match) her feeling before you lead her out of it.
  • Taking on the Client's Anxiety: If Elena is anxious, stay calm. Your calm is the "anchor" she needs to steady herself.
  • Ignoring Physiology: If the client is slumped over, no amount of "positive thinking" will work. Ask them to adjust their posture before doing deep work.
Sarah's Insight

A 2022 study on coaching efficacy showed that the strength of the working alliance (rapport) accounted for 30% of the successful outcome. Use your NLP pacing tools to build that alliance early!

Check Your Understanding

1. Why did we ask Elena where she felt the "dress-up" feeling in her body?

To access the Kinesthetic component of her state. NLP teaches that every emotion has a physical location. By identifying it, we can then work to "interrupt" or shift that physical pattern.

2. What is the primary goal of "Pacing and Leading" in this scenario?

To build rapport by first meeting the client where they are (Pacing) so they feel understood, which then gives you the "permission" to guide them toward a new perspective (Leading).

3. Elena looks up and to her right when imagining her failure. What does this suggest in NLP?

This typically indicates Visual Constructed (Vc) eye patternsâ€”she is literally "constructing" or creating a future image of failure that hasn't happened yet.

4. How does "Resource Retrieval" help with imposter syndrome?

It reminds the client of a time they were successful or confident in a different context (like Elena's teaching), proving to their subconscious that they already possess the "code" for confidence.

Lab Summary & Takeaways

  • Acknowledge the Feeling: Always pace the client's current reality before trying to change it.
  • Use Resource Anchors: Help clients "borrow" confidence from their past successes (nursing, teaching, parenting).
  • Posture Matters: You cannot coach a "stuck" mind in a "stuck" body. Check physiology first.
  • Authority is Earned through Presence: Your tone and confidence create the "container" for the client's transformation.
