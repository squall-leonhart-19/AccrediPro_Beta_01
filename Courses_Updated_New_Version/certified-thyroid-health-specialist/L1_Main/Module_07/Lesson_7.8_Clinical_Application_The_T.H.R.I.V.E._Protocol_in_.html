Common Mistakes for New Coaches

  • Over-Explaining Science: Don't spend 20 minutes explaining the Krebs cycle. Keep it focused on her symptoms.
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Trying to solve 10 problems in the first session. Pick the "Lead Domino" (usually sleep or digestion).
  • Ignoring the Emotional Load: If she starts to cry when talking about her fatigue, stop the clinical talk. Acknowledge the emotion before moving back to the plan.
  • Sounding Uncertain: Avoid saying "I think maybe..." or "We could try..." Instead, use "Based on your data, the next logical step is..."

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these three signs:

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address the HPA axis (adrenals) in a client like Diane who has thyroid issues?

High cortisol (stress) can inhibit the conversion of T4 to T3 and increase the production of Reverse T3, making thyroid medication less effective even if TSH looks "normal."

2. What is the "Lead Domino" concept in complex coaching?

It is the one intervention that, when addressed, makes all other symptoms easier to manage. For many thyroid clients, this is sleep or blood sugar stability.

3. If a client says, "My doctor says my labs are fine, but I feel terrible," what is the best coaching response?

Validate her experience first ("I believe you"). Then explain the difference between "clinical ranges" (absence of disease) and "optimal ranges" (presence of health).

4. Which analogy is best for explaining the interaction of different hormone systems?

The "Three-Legged Stool" (Thyroid, Adrenals, Sex Hormones) or a "Symphony Orchestra" where one out-of-tune instrument affects the whole sound.
