The ATM Framework: Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators

To identify a root cause, we must first speak the language of functional investigation. The ATM Framework allows us to categorize every piece of a client's history into a logical map of their current health state.

Category Definition Thyroid-Specific Examples
Antecedents Factors that predispose a person to a condition (Genetics/History). HLA-DRB1 gene, family history of Graves', C-section birth (low microbiome diversity).
Triggers Discrete events that "flip the switch" or initiate the symptoms. Pregnancy, severe viral infection (EBV), acute emotional trauma, mold exposure.
Mediators Factors that keep the symptoms going (the "fuel" on the fire). Chronic stress, poor sleep, ongoing gluten consumption, nutrient deficiencies.
Specialist Insight

When interviewing a new client, listen for the "Triggers." Ask: "When was the last time you felt truly well, and what happened right before that changed?" This often reveals the missing link that standard doctors overlook.

The Three-Legged Stool of Autoimmunity

Since approximately 90% of hypothyroidism in developed nations is Hashimoto's, we must understand the mechanics of autoimmunity. Dr. Alessio Fasano's landmark research established that for autoimmunity to manifest, three specific "legs" must be present:

  1. Genetic Predisposition: The genes that make the immune system "trigger-happy."
  2. Environmental Triggers: Toxins, pathogens, or foods that the immune system reacts to.
  3. Intestinal Permeability (Leaky Gut): The gateway that allows triggers to enter the bloodstream and meet the immune system.

As a specialist, you cannot change a client's genes (Leg 1). However, you have total control over identifying environmental triggers (Leg 2) and healing the gut (Leg 3). This is how we put Hashimoto's into remission.

The Total Toxic Burden & Halogen Displacement

Our modern world is a "chemical soup." A 2022 study found that the average American is exposed to over 700,000 chemicals daily. For the thyroid, the most critical concept is Halogen Displacement.

Iodine is a halogen required to make thyroid hormone (T4 has 4 iodine atoms). However, other halogens like Fluoride (water), Chlorine (pools/water), and Bromine (bread/flame retardants) can "kick" iodine off its receptors because they look similar to the body. This creates a state of functional iodine deficiency even if the client is consuming enough iodine.

Why Standard Hormone Replacement Often Fails

Why do millions of women take their thyroid medication every morning but still feel like "zombies"? The answer lies in the Cellular Resistance caused by root causes. If the "room" (the cell) is full of trash (inflammation/toxins), the "mailman" (thyroid hormone) can't get through the door.

  • Inflammation: Cytokines block the conversion of T4 to T3.
  • Gut Dysbiosis: 20% of T4 to T3 conversion happens in the gut.
  • Cortisol: High stress drives the production of Reverse T3 (the "brake pedal").

Giving more Levothyroxine (T4) to a body that can't convert it or a cell that can't receive it is like shouting at a person who is wearing earplugs. We must remove the earplugs first.
