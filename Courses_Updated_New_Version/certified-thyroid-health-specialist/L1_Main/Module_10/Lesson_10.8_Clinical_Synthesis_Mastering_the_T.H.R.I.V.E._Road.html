Success Markers: How You Know It Went Well

A successful coaching session isn't measured by how much you talk, but by how the client feels at the end. Look for these signs:

  • The "Shoulder Drop": The client physically relaxes as they realize they are finally being heard.
  • Active Questions: Instead of passive nodding, the client asks, "So, what can we do about my nutrition?"
  • Hopeful Language: The client starts talking about the future (e.g., "I'd love to be able to garden again this spring").
  • Commitment: The client asks about the next steps or how to sign up for your full program.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's "normal" lab results immediately?

Validation builds immediate trust. Many women feel "gaslit" by the medical system; by confirming that a TSH of 4.2 can indeed cause symptoms, you establish yourself as a specialist who understands the nuances of thyroid health beyond basic lab ranges.

2. What is the "Functional Lens" analogy used for?

It explains the difference between conventional medicine (detecting disease) and functional coaching (optimizing health) without disparaging the medical profession. It helps the client understand why they still feel poorly despite "normal" results.

3. If a client asks, "Can you cure my Hashimoto's?", what is the best specialist response?

Avoid the word "cure." Instead, say: "While we don't use the word 'cure' for autoimmune conditions, our goal is to achieve remission and significantly reduce your symptoms by addressing the root triggers of your immune response."

4. What is the primary purpose of a Discovery Session?

The primary purpose is to determine "fit" and build rapport. It is an opportunity for the client to feel heard and for you to demonstrate your expertise and empathy, leading to a long-term coaching relationship.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Empathy is your greatest tool: Use phrases like "I believe you" and "It makes sense that you feel this way."
  • Function over Labs: Educate your clients on the difference between "standard" and "optimal" thyroid ranges.
  • Practice makes Professional: Recording your sessions (even mock ones) is the fastest way to eliminate "umms" and "ahhs."
  • You are the Guide: You don't need to have all the answers instantly; you just need to have a reliable process to find them.
