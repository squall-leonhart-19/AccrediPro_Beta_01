Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's ferritin level of 18 a priority in her thyroid protocol?

Iron is a required cofactor for the enzyme Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO). Without adequate ferritin (ideally >70), the body cannot effectively produce thyroid hormone or convert T4 to T3, regardless of how much medication is taken.

2. What is the likely connection between Elena's irregular periods and her hives?

This suggests "Estrogen Dominance" common in perimenopause. High estrogen levels stimulate mast cells to release histamine and inhibit the DAO enzyme, leading to histamine intolerance symptoms like hives.

3. Elena's TSH is 4.2 (within the "normal" range). Why might this still be problematic?

Functional medicine "optimal" ranges for TSH are typically between 0.5 and 2.5. A TSH of 4.2, combined with low Free T3 and high antibodies, indicates the pituitary is working hard to signal a struggling thyroid.

4. Which symptom would trigger an immediate referral back to Elena's physician?

Persistent heart palpitations (resting HR > 100 BPM), a visible lump/nodule in the neck, or ferritin levels dropping below 10 ng/mL.
