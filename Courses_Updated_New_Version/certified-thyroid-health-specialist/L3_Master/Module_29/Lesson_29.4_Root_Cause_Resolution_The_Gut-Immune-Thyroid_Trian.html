Molecular Mimicry & TPO Antibodies

Why does the immune system attack the thyroid specifically? The answer lies in Molecular Mimicry. This occurs when the amino acid sequences of certain triggers (like gluten or certain bacteria) closely resemble the proteins in the thyroid gland (Thyroid Peroxidase and Thyroglobulin).

The immune system creates "Wanted" posters (antibodies) for the invader. However, because the thyroid tissue looks so similar, the immune system begins "friendly fire" attacks on the gland itself. Biofilm Disruption: Breaking the Invisibility Cloak

Pathogens are smart. To survive the immune system and anti-microbial treatments, they create Biofilms. These are protective, slimy barriers made of polysaccharides and minerals (calcium, magnesium, iron) that shield bacteria and fungi.

If you don't disrupt the biofilm, your herbal anti-microbials or the client's own immune cells cannot reach the pathogen. This is why many "gut cleanses" fail. We utilize specific enzymes (like Serrapeptase or Nattokinase) and chelators (like Bismuth or EDTA) to "dissolve" these shields before introducing anti-microbial agents.
