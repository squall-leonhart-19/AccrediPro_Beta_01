2. The Discovery Call Blueprint: A 30-Minute Guide

A well-structured discovery call isn't about rigid adherence to a script, but rather a framework that ensures you cover all essential points while allowing for authentic conversation. This 30-minute blueprint is designed to guide you from introduction to a clear next step.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Intentions 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Sarah, it's so lovely to connect with you today! Thank you for taking the time. I'm Grace. Before we dive into what brought you here, could you share how you heard about my coaching and what prompted you to reach out now?"
SARAH:
"Hi Grace! Yes, it's nice to meet you too. I heard you speak at the Women's Ministry event last month, and my friend, Brenda, who was there, suggested I reach out. I've been feeling really lost lately, especially with my eating and just a general disconnect from my faith. Your message really resonated with me."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear, Sarah. Brenda is a dear. It sounds like you're carrying a lot right now. My intention for our call today is to deeply understand your current situation, explore your goals for healing and spiritual growth, and determine if my Faith-Based Recovery Coaching program is truly the right fit to support you on this journey. Does that sound good to you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deep Dive 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you, Sarah. Let's dive in. You mentioned feeling lost, particularly with emotional eating and spiritual disconnect. Can you tell me more about how this has been showing up in your daily life? When did you first notice these patterns, and what does a typical day look like when you're feeling this way?"
SARAH:
"It's been a few years, but it got much worse after my mother's illness last year. I just feel so drained. I turn to food for comfort, especially at night. Then I feel guilty, and it just makes me pull away from everything, even church. I pray, but it feels like my prayers just hit the ceiling."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging, Sarah. My heart goes out to you for what you've been through. What steps have you already taken to address these feelings and patterns? What has worked, and perhaps more importantly, what hasn't, and why do you think that is?"
SARAH:
"I've tried so many diets, weight loss programs, even a few sessions with a therapist. The diets always fail, because they don't address the emotional side. Therapy helped a bit with coping, but I felt like something was missing ‚Äì the spiritual piece. It never felt truly integrated."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that vulnerability. It takes courage. If nothing changes in your current situation, how do you envision your life unfolding over the next year? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly overcome these challenges, what would your life look like? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on everything you've so bravely shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a woman of faith, deeply desiring to reconnect with God and find peace, but emotional eating and a sense of spiritual emptiness are holding you back. You've tried various methods that haven't fully integrated your faith, leaving you feeling stuck and searching for a holistic solution. Does that feel accurate?"
SARAH:
"Yes, absolutely. You've hit it right on the head."
YOU:
"Wonderful. What I offer through my Faith-Based Recovery Coaching is truly different from what you've experienced. We don't just address symptoms; we go to the root causes, integrating clinical insights with deep spiritual wisdom. My signature F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢ helps clients like you build an unshakeable foundation for recovery, addressing emotional triggers, renewing your mind through scripture, and strengthening your connection with God. We work together over a structured 12-week program, providing personalized support, practical tools, and spiritual guidance. Most of my clients begin to experience significant shifts in their peace and clarity within the first 4-6 weeks, often seeing breakthroughs in their relationship with food and their faith within that time."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on our conversation, does this approach resonate with what you've been looking for‚Äîa path that truly integrates your faith into your healing journey?"
SARAH:
"It really does. It sounds like exactly what I need."
YOU:
"Excellent. Then let's discuss how we can get started and what the investment looks like. My 12-week Faith-Based Recovery Coaching program is designed for deep transformation. The full investment for this comprehensive program is [Your Price Here]. This includes weekly one-on-one coaching sessions, personalized resources, and unlimited Voxer support. Coach Tip

During the "understand the problem" phase, utilize active listening techniques like mirroring, paraphrasing, and summarizing. Use phrases like, "So, if I'm hearing you correctly, you're feeling..." This not only confirms your understanding but also makes the prospect feel deeply seen and heard.

3. Mastering Objections: Turning "No" into "Know"

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They aren't rejections; they're simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, confidence, and a servant's heart, understanding that you are helping them overcome their own internal barriers to healing.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"It's too expensive. I'm on a fixed income." (Sarah's likely objection)
"I completely understand, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. Investing in yourself, especially when on a fixed income, is a significant decision. What I want to assure you is that this isn't just an expense; it's an investment in your holistic well-being and spiritual foundation, designed to create lasting change. Many of my clients, including those on a fixed income, find the payment plan option makes it much more manageable. We can break the investment into [e.g., three monthly payments of X]. When we consider the true cost of staying stuck ‚Äì the emotional toll, the impact on your spiritual life, and the continued spending on solutions that haven't worked ‚Äì this program offers a clear path to lasting peace and wholeness. How does the payment plan sound to you?"
"I need to think about it."
"Absolutely, this is an important decision, and I want you to feel completely confident. To help you in your reflection, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the program itself? Knowing this helps me ensure I've addressed all your concerns."
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked. How is this different?"
"That's a very valid concern, and I hear that a lot from clients who come to me feeling frustrated. The key difference with Faith-Based Recovery Coaching, particularly my F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢, is its holistic and integrated approach. Unlike diets or traditional therapy alone, we intentionally weave your spiritual beliefs into every aspect of your recovery. We address the root of emotional patterns through a lens of faith, helping you cultivate spiritual resilience and connect with God's healing power in a way that truly transforms from the inside out. This isn't just a band-aid; it's about building a sustainable foundation for lasting freedom. Does that distinction resonate with your experience?"
"I'm not sure I have the time right now."
"I understand. Life is incredibly busy, and adding something new can feel overwhelming. However, I often find that when clients are feeling overwhelmed, it's precisely when they need this structured support the most. My program is designed to fit into your life, with weekly sessions and actionable steps that integrate seamlessly. Think of it as creating dedicated time to invest in your own peace and well-being, which ultimately frees up mental and emotional energy in other areas of your life. What kind of time commitment are you concerned about?"

4. Confidently Presenting Your Value & Pricing

Presenting your price confidently is a crucial skill. It‚Äôs not just about the number; it‚Äôs about the value you provide, the transformation you facilitate, and the belief you have in your ability to help. Frame your pricing as an investment in a future where your client feels whole, connected, and free.

Value-Driven Pricing Statement

After explaining your program's unique methodology and benefits, transition smoothly into pricing:

"Sarah, based on everything we've discussed and your clear desire to reconnect with your faith and find peace with food, I truly believe my 12-week Faith-Based Recovery Coaching program is precisely what you've been searching for. This program is a comprehensive journey designed to help you build an unshakeable spiritual foundation, overcome emotional eating, and reclaim your joy and purpose. The full investment for this deep transformation is $2,500. This includes weekly 60-minute one-on-one coaching sessions, personalized spiritual and practical resources, and direct support between sessions via a secure messaging app. For your convenience, I also offer a payment plan of three monthly payments of $850. My goal is to make this life-changing support accessible while honoring the significant value and expertise I bring to your journey. Which option feels best for you?"

Key takeaways for pricing:

  • State the full price first: This anchors the value.
  • Follow immediately with payment options: Makes it more accessible.
  • Connect back to value and transformation: Remind them what they are investing in.
  • Be silent after stating the price: Let them process and respond. The first one to speak often loses.
Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud, to yourself in the mirror. The more comfortable you are articulating your value and the investment, the more confident and credible you will sound to your prospects.

5. Practice Your Close: Guiding to the Next Step

The close is not about pressuring; it's about guiding. You've established rapport, understood their needs, and presented a solution. Now, it's time to help them make a decision that serves their highest good.

Here are some closing lines to practice:

  1. "Sarah, I truly believe we could achieve amazing things together. Are you ready to take this step and begin your journey toward spiritual wholeness and freedom from emotional eating?"
  2. "Given what you've shared and how well my program aligns with your goals, what feels like your next best step forward today?"
  3. "Based on our conversation, I'm confident my program can help you achieve [reiterate their desired outcome]. Shall we get you enrolled so we can start building that foundation next week?"
  4. "How would it feel to finally have the integrated support you've been looking for, beginning this week? If you're ready, we can finalize the details now."

The key is to be direct, confident, and provide a clear path forward. Offer to send a link to enroll or schedule the first session. Always end with an action item.

8. Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem & Deep Dive" phase of a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to actively listen and deeply understand the prospect's challenges, pain points, past attempts at solutions, and their desired future state. This phase is crucial for building empathy and gathering the information needed to tailor your solution.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective immediate response?

The most effective response is to acknowledge their need to think, then gently inquire what specifically they need to think about (e.g., "I completely understand. To help you in your reflection, could you share what specifically you need to think about?"). This helps uncover the true objection.

3. How should you frame your pricing when presenting it to a prospect like Sarah?

Frame your pricing as an "investment" in their transformation and well-being, not just an expense. Clearly state the full price first to anchor value, then immediately follow with payment plan options to address affordability concerns. Connect the price back to the profound value and lasting change your program offers.

4. Why is it important to remain silent after stating your program's price?

Remaining silent after stating the price allows the prospect to process the information without interruption or feeling pressured. It demonstrates confidence in your value and gives them space to respond, often revealing their initial thoughts or objections. The first person to speak often feels compelled to fill the silence, which can lead to unintended concessions.

9. Key Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • Structure for Success: A well-defined discovery call blueprint ensures you guide the conversation effectively, building rapport, understanding needs, and presenting solutions with clarity.
  • Listen with Intention: True connection and effective problem-solving stem from deep, active listening, allowing you to truly understand a prospect's spiritual and practical needs.
  • Objections are Opportunities: View objections not as rejections, but as requests for more information or reassurance, allowing you to address underlying concerns with empathy and confidence.
  • Value-Driven Pricing: Confidently communicate your program's investment, anchoring it to the profound transformation and lasting spiritual freedom you provide, and always offer payment options.
  • Practice Makes Perfect: Regularly practicing your script, objection handling, and closing statements will build your confidence and significantly improve your conversion rates, leading to a thriving and impactful coaching practice.
