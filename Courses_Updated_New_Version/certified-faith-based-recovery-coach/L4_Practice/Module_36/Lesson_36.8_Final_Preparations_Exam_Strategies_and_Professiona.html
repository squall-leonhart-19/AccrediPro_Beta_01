Your Ideal Prospect: Sarah's Story

Imagine this scenario: you receive a message from Sarah, a woman in her late 40s, who found you through a trusted referral. She's tired, feels spiritually disconnected, and is searching for a different path to recovery. Let's dive into her profile.

üë©

Sarah Jenkins, 49

Referred by a past client, seeking a "deeper" approach to her ongoing struggle with anxiety and a past alcohol dependency.

Her Situation
Sarah has maintained sobriety for 3 years after a conventional program, but feels a profound sense of spiritual emptiness and persistent anxiety. She's a single mother, works as a paralegal, and feels overwhelmed.
Spiritual State
Raised in faith, but feels distant from God. "I go to church, but it feels hollow. I need something real to fill this void."
Budget Concern
"I've invested a lot in therapy and programs already. I worry about spending more money without a guarantee it will work."
Her Deepest Desire
"I want peace. I want to feel connected to God again. I want to truly heal, not just manage symptoms."
Coach Tip

Always approach a discovery call with a mindset of service and discernment. Your goal isn't just to "sell" a program, but to genuinely understand if you are uniquely positioned to help this person. If not, a graceful referral is a testament to your integrity and professionalism.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Remember to personalize it with your authentic voice and allow for organic conversation. Active listening is key!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Sarah, it's so wonderful to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. How are you doing this afternoon?"
YOU:
"I understand you were referred by [client's name]. She's a remarkable woman. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear a bit about what you're hoping to get out of our conversation today."
YOU:
"Excellent. My goal for this call is to understand your situation, share a bit about how faith-based recovery coaching works, and see if it feels like a good fit for you. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Discern Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, tell me in your own words, what's been weighing on your heart lately? What prompted you to reach out for a faith-based approach at this time?"
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling a spiritual emptiness despite your sobriety. Can you describe what that feels like for you day-to-day? How does it impact your energy, your relationships, your sense of purpose?"
YOU:
"What steps have you taken so far to address this? What has worked, and perhaps more importantly, what hasn't quite met your needs?"
YOU:
"If we were sitting here a year from now, and you felt truly healed and connected, what would be different in your life? What would that peace look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing so openly, Sarah. I'm hearing that you're seeking a deeper spiritual connection, a way to move beyond just managing anxiety and past dependencies, and to truly find sustainable peace and purpose. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The approach I use in faith-based recovery coaching is different because we don't just look at behaviors or mental patterns. We integrate your faith journey into every step. We explore the spiritual roots of your struggles, drawing on God's Word and prayer to cultivate true inner healing and resilience. It's about rebuilding your foundation on Christ. My 12-week 'Renewed Spirit Pathway' program is designed specifically for women like you, helping you bridge that gap between where you are and where God wants you to be."
YOU:
"Many of my clients, often women in their 40s and 50s who've felt stuck for years, experience significant shifts in their peace and spiritual vitality within the first 6-8 weeks, truly feeling God's presence in their daily lives again. We work one-on-one, with personalized spiritual practices and actionable strategies."
Phase 4: Confident Enrollment & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared and what I've explained about the 'Renewed Spirit Pathway,' does this sound like the kind of support and transformation you've been seeking?"
YOU:
"Wonderful. If you're ready to explore this path further, the next step would be to enroll in the 'Renewed Spirit Pathway' program. It's a 12-week journey, and I typically work with clients for a minimum of three months to ensure lasting change. Let me share how we can get started."
Coach Tip

Your passion for your mission is contagious. When describing your program, speak from the heart about the transformation you offer, not just the features. Paint a vivid picture of the peace, connection, and freedom your clients will experience through God's grace.

Confidently Handling Objections

Objections are a natural part of any enrollment conversation. They are often simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, understanding, and unwavering belief in the value of your service.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to pray about it."

"Sarah, I completely honor that. Prayer is foundational to any significant decision, especially one concerning your spiritual well-being. What specifically would you be praying about? Is it the timing, the investment, or perhaps if I'm the right coach for you? My hope is that our conversation today has given you clarity on how God might be working through this opportunity. I'd be happy to answer any further questions that might help guide your prayers."

"It's too expensive. I've already spent so much."

"I completely understand that concern, Sarah. Many of my clients have felt the same way, having invested in various approaches without finding lasting peace. What I've found is that the true cost isn't just financial; it's the cost of staying stuck, of spiritual emptiness, of missing out on the abundant life God intends for you. My program is an investment in transformational, faith-integrated healing, designed to address the root causes you've described. Can I ask, what value do you place on finally experiencing that deep peace and connection with God you're longing for?"

(Pause for her response. You can then gently re-emphasize the unique value and long-term savings of true healing.)

"I've tried so many things already, I'm not sure this will be different."

"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, and I hear your frustration. You've been courageous in seeking help. What makes faith-based recovery coaching truly different is its foundation. We're not just offering coping mechanisms or temporary fixes; we're inviting God into every aspect of your recovery. We address the spiritual brokenness that often underlies anxiety and addiction, integrating prayer, scripture, and a Christ-centered perspective that other programs may miss. It's about inviting God to do what only He can do, through a structured, supportive coaching relationship. This isn't just another program; it's a partnership with God for your complete restoration."

"I don't have time for another commitment right now."

"I completely empathize, Sarah. Life as a single mother and paralegal is incredibly demanding. Many of my clients are busy women who initially feel the same way. What they quickly realize, however, is that this program isn't just another 'thing to do' but an investment that actually creates more time and peace in their lives. By addressing the root causes of your anxiety and spiritual emptiness, you'll find newfound energy, clarity, and focus, ultimately making you more effective in all areas of your life. We design the sessions and practices to integrate seamlessly into your life, not add another burden. Imagine the time you'll gain back when you're no longer battling this internal struggle."

Coach Tip

When an objection arises, resist the urge to immediately defend or explain. Instead, validate their concern, then gently inquire further to understand the deeper meaning behind it. This creates trust and opens the door for a truly helpful response.

Presenting Your Program & Investment

Presenting your pricing is a moment to stand in your value and the transformative power of your coaching. Do so with confidence, clarity, and conviction. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, not just a commodity.

Confidence in Your Value

Before you even state your price, ensure your prospect understands the immense value they will receive. Reiterate the transformation, the personalized support, and the unique faith-based approach. Frame your pricing as an investment in their spiritual and emotional well-being.

When it's time to state the price, do so clearly and then pause. Allow the client to process the information. Avoid immediately filling the silence with justifications or discounts.

Example Pricing Presentation Script

YOU:
"Sarah, the 'Renewed Spirit Pathway' is a 12-week, one-on-one coaching journey. It includes weekly 60-minute sessions, personalized spiritual practices, unlimited email support between sessions, and access to my exclusive resource library. This comprehensive program is designed to guide you from spiritual emptiness to profound connection and lasting peace. The investment for the full 12-week program is $2,997. We also offer a two-part payment plan of $1,550 per month for two months."
YOU:
"My goal is to make this accessible while honoring the deep level of transformation we'll achieve together. What questions do you have about the program or the investment?"
Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and comfortable. Your tone of voice conveys your confidence. If you hesitate, so will your potential client. Remember, you are worth the investment, and so is their healing.

Closing Lines to Practice

After presenting the investment and addressing any final questions, it‚Äôs time to invite them to begin their journey. Here are a few options:

  • "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the 'Renewed Spirit Pathway' is exactly what you need to find that lasting peace and spiritual connection you're longing for. Are you ready to take this step of faith and begin your transformation?"
  • "Given your desire for a deeper, faith-integrated approach, does moving forward with the 'Renewed Spirit Pathway' feel like the right next step for you?"
  • "If you're ready to commit to this journey of healing and renewal, I'd be honored to walk alongside you. How would you like to proceed?"

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1

During the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call, what is the primary goal a Faith-Based Recovery Coach should aim for?

The primary goal is to deeply listen and discern the prospect's spiritual and emotional needs, understand their current struggles, past attempts at recovery, and their deepest desires for transformation, especially related to their faith journey. It's about uncovering their "why."
2

When a potential client raises the objection, "I need to pray about it," what is the most effective and faith-affirming way to respond?

Acknowledge and honor their commitment to prayer. Then, gently inquire what specifically they would be praying about (e.g., timing, investment, fit) to uncover any underlying concerns. Offer to answer any questions that might help guide their prayer, reinforcing your role as a supportive guide.
3

What is a key principle for confidently presenting your program's investment to a prospect?

Present the investment clearly and confidently, then pause. Avoid immediately justifying or discounting. Ensure the prospect understands the immense value and transformation they will receive before the price is stated, framing it as an investment in their healing and spiritual well-being.
4

What is the significance of the "Renewed Spirit Pathway" program's focus on "integrating your faith journey into every step" when presenting your approach?

This highlights the unique value proposition of faith-based coaching, differentiating it from conventional approaches. It emphasizes addressing spiritual roots of struggle, using divine guidance (God's Word, prayer) for holistic healing, and building resilience on a Christ-centered foundation, which resonates deeply with clients seeking more than just symptom management.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a sacred space for discernment and connection, not just a sales pitch.
  • Active listening and empathetic inquiry are crucial for truly understanding a prospect's spiritual and emotional needs.
  • Confidently articulate the unique, transformative value of faith-based recovery coaching.
  • Address objections with grace, validation, and a clear re-framing of your program's benefits.
  • Present your program's investment with conviction and clarity, standing firm in the value you provide.
  • Your coaching practice offers significant income potential and flexibility, empowering you to serve God and others abundantly.
