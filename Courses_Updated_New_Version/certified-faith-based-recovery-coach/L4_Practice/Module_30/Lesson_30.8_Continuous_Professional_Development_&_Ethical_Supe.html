2. The Art of the Discovery Call: A Sacred Conversation

A discovery call is your opportunity to understand a potential client's needs, articulate how your faith-based approach can help, and determine if there's a good fit for a coaching partnership. It's not about hard selling, but about heart-centered service.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Building Authentic Connection 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's so good to connect with you. Thank you for taking the time today. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to know a little about you. How did you hear about my practice?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear that [ministry leader's name] recommended me. She's a true blessing. So Sarah, tell me, in your own words, what led you to seek out faith-based recovery coaching at this time?"
Coach Tip: Active Listening

Remember that listening is a ministry. Your goal in this phase is to make Sarah feel seen, heard, and valued. Use open-ended questions and resist the urge to interrupt or offer solutions too early. Let her share her story fully.

Phase 2: Deeply Understanding Their Needs (and Faith) 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned emotional eating and feeling a spiritual void. Can you tell me more about how these challenges impact your daily life, your relationships, and your walk with God?"
YOU:
"What steps have you already taken to address these issues? What worked, even a little bit, and what felt like it just didn't stick?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in six months or a year? And if you could wave a magic wand and truly overcome these challenges, what would your life look like? How would you feel spiritually and emotionally?"
Coach Tip: Empathy and Validation

Validate her feelings and experiences. Phrases like "That sounds incredibly challenging" or "I can hear how much this is weighing on you" build trust. Connect her struggles to universal human experiences, especially within a faith context, showing she's not alone.

Phase 3: Presenting Your Transformative Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're deeply desiring peace with food, a stronger connection to God, and freedom from the shame of emotional eating. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"My approach is different from what you've likely tried before. We don't just focus on food; we look at the root spiritual and emotional triggers behind emotional eating. Through our 12-week 'Renewed Spirit, Restored Body' program, we'll journey together, integrating biblical principles with practical strategies to help you cultivate a healthy relationship with food, yourself, and God. Clients typically begin to experience significant shifts in their mindset and habits within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"We'll explore topics like identifying false beliefs, practicing self-compassion, discerning God's voice for your body, and building sustainable habits rooted in grace, not guilt."
Coach Tip: Bridge to Faith

Explicitly connect your program to faith-based principles. For Sarah, mentioning "biblical principles," "false beliefs," "self-compassion," and "grace, not guilt" speaks directly to her stated needs and values. This reinforces your unique position as a Faith-Based Recovery Coach.

Phase 4: The Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about my 'Renewed Spirit, Restored Body' program, does this sound like the kind of supportive, faith-centered guidance you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me walk you through the program details and how we can get started on this journey together."

3. Confidently Presenting Your Value & Pricing

The transition from discussing needs to presenting your program and its investment can feel daunting. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, and your confidence in its value is paramount. Clients invest in transformation, not just information.

Structuring Your Programs

As a Faith-Based Recovery Coach, you'll typically offer packages or programs rather than single sessions. This allows for deeper, more sustainable transformation, which is what clients like Sarah truly seek. Common structures include:

  • Entry-Level Package (e.g., 6-week foundational): Focuses on initial awareness and basic tools.
  • Signature Program (e.g., 12-week comprehensive): Your core offering, designed for significant change. This is what you presented to Sarah.
  • Premium/Extended Program (e.g., 6-month deep dive): For clients desiring continued support and advanced work.
Program Level Duration Key Focus Investment Range (Example)
Foundational 6 Weeks (3 sessions) Initial assessment, goal setting, spiritual grounding $800 - $1,500
Signature (Most Popular) 12 Weeks (6-8 sessions) Comprehensive recovery, spiritual growth, habit formation $2,500 - $4,500
Deep Dive 6 Months (10-12 sessions) Sustained transformation, advanced inner healing, community building $5,000 - $8,000+

Overcoming Price Objections

When discussing investment, always state the full program price first, then offer payment options. This anchors the value. For Sarah's "Renewed Spirit, Restored Body" 12-week program, you might say:

YOU:
"The investment for the full 12-week 'Renewed Spirit, Restored Body' program is $3,500. This includes our 8 personalized coaching sessions, between-session support, and all program materials. For your convenience, I also offer a payment plan of three monthly installments of $1,200."

Pause. Let the number land. Then, when she inevitably brings up her concern:

SARAH:
"That's a significant investment, Grace. I'm just not sure I can afford that right now, especially with retirement planning."
YOU:
"I completely understand, Sarah. It is an investment, and it's natural to pause when considering something so important. Many of my clients, like yourself, have felt that initial hesitation. What they've found, however, is that this program isn't just another expense; it's an investment in their long-term spiritual and emotional well-being, freeing them from years of struggle. Think about the cost of continuing to feel stuck, or the money spent on programs that haven't delivered lasting change. This is about finally creating that peace with food and renewed connection with God you spoke about. How would that impact your retirement years, knowing you're living in freedom?"

4. Navigating Common Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, confidence, and a strong belief in your program's ability to help.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or are you perhaps still wondering if this approach is truly different for you? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make a prayerful decision."
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"I hear you, Sarah. Life is busy, especially when you're managing so much. The program is designed to fit into a busy schedule, with our sessions being just one hour every other week. Beyond that, the 'homework' is really about integrating practices into your daily life, not adding more to your plate. Many clients find that by investing this time now, they actually gain more time and energy back in the long run as they find freedom from old patterns. How much more time is emotional eating currently taking from your life and peace?"
"I've tried everything, and nothing has worked."
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage to keep trying after past disappointments. I understand why you might feel that way. What makes our faith-based recovery coaching different is that we go beyond just diet plans or willpower. We address the spiritual and emotional roots of the struggle, inviting God into every aspect of your healing journey. This holistic approach often brings breakthroughs where other methods fall short because it addresses the whole person â€“ spirit, soul, and body. Are you open to exploring a path that truly integrates your faith?"
"I need to talk to my spouse/partner."
"That's absolutely a wise and important step, Sarah. I encourage you to involve your husband in this decision. To help you communicate the value of this investment, would it be helpful if I sent you a brief summary of our conversation today, outlining the program and how it addresses the goals you shared? Perhaps we could even schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you, so I can answer any questions he might have directly?"

5. The Power of the Faith-Based Close

As a Faith-Based Recovery Coach, your closing isn't just about securing a client; it's about extending an invitation to transformation rooted in God's grace. After addressing objections, bring it back to their core desire and God's calling.

YOU:
"Sarah, I truly believe that God has led you to this conversation today for a reason. You've expressed a deep desire for peace, for spiritual connection, and for freedom in your relationship with food. My 'Renewed Spirit, Restored Body' program is designed to help women like you step into that freedom, aligning your health journey with your faith journey. Are you ready to take this step of faith and begin your transformation?"

Then, be silent. Let her answer. This moment of silence is powerful; it allows her to reflect and respond from a place of prayerful consideration.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Building Authentic Connection" phase in a discovery call for a Faith-Based Recovery Coach?

The primary purpose is to establish rapport and create a safe, welcoming space for the prospect. For a Faith-Based Recovery Coach, this also means acknowledging and respecting their spiritual journey from the outset, allowing them to feel seen and understood on a deeper level.

2. Sarah expresses concern about the investment. What is an effective strategy to address this objection, specifically tailored to her situation?

An effective strategy is to reframe the investment as a path to long-term spiritual and emotional freedom, contrasting it with the ongoing "cost" of her current struggles. Connect it to her desire for peace with food, renewed connection with God, and how that impacts her future, including retirement years, reinforcing that this is an investment in her overall well-being and purpose.

3. If a prospect says, "I've tried everything, and nothing has worked," how should a Faith-Based Recovery Coach differentiate their approach?

A Faith-Based Recovery Coach should emphasize that their approach goes beyond conventional methods by addressing the spiritual and emotional roots of the struggle, not just symptoms. Highlight the integration of biblical principles, grace, and a holistic focus on spirit, soul, and body, which can lead to breakthroughs where other approaches have failed.

4. What is the significance of integrating a "Faith-Based Close" into your discovery call?

The Faith-Based Close transforms the sales conversation into a spiritual invitation. It connects the decision to work with you to God's purpose for their life and their desire for transformation, grounding the commitment in faith rather than just a transaction. This resonates deeply with prospects seeking a faith-centered path to recovery.
