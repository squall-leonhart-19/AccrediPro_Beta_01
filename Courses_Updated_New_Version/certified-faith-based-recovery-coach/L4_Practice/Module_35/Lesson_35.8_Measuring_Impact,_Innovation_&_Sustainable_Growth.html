Sarah, 52

Her Situation
Struggling with emotional eating and reliance on alcohol after a difficult divorce and children leaving for college. Feels a deep spiritual emptiness despite church attendance.

The Faith-Based Discovery Call Script

This 30-minute script provides a framework. Be flexible, listen intently, and allow the Holy Spirit to guide your conversation. Your authenticity is your greatest asset.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Intentions 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's so wonderful to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear a little about how you found me."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [Ministry/Friend's Name] connected us. I truly believe God works through these connections. So, Sarah, in your own words, what led you to seek out faith-based recovery coaching at this time? What's on your heart?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understand Her Struggle & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage. Could you tell me more about this spiritual emptiness you mentioned, and how it connects with the emotional eating and alcohol use? When did these patterns begin to feel overwhelming?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these challenges? What brought temporary relief, and what felt like it missed the mark?"
YOU:
"Sarah, if nothing changes, what does life look like for you in six months or a year? And conversely, if you could wave a divine wand and everything shifted, what would your ideal life — spiritually, emotionally, physically — truly look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Faith-Based Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've so bravely shared, I'm hearing a deep desire to reconnect with God, to break free from habits that no longer serve you, and to embrace a season of joy and purpose. You're looking for a holistic approach that integrates your faith. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The AccrediPro Faith-Based Recovery Coaching Method™ is designed specifically for women like you. It's different from traditional therapy because we don't just address symptoms; we seek the root spiritual and emotional causes, inviting God into every step of the healing process. Over our 12-week journey, we'll uncover these roots, rebuild your connection with Christ, and equip you with practical tools rooted in biblical principles. My clients typically begin to experience significant breakthroughs and renewed hope within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"We'll work through tailored modules focusing on spiritual formation, emotional regulation, habit transformation, and discovering God's purpose for your new season. This isn't a quick fix, but a transformative partnership designed for lasting change."
Phase 4: Guide to Next Steps & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, after hearing about my approach, does this sound like the comprehensive, faith-centered support you've been looking for to achieve your vision of freedom and joy?"
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me share a bit about how my Signature 12-Week Faith-Based Recovery Program works and how we can get started on this journey together. I'll outline the investment and what's included, and then you can ask any questions that come to mind."
Coach's Insight

Your passion for helping others through faith-based principles should shine through. When discussing your program, speak from a place of conviction and belief in the transformative power of God and your unique methodology. This isn't just a service; it's a ministry.

Handling Objections with Grace and Confidence

It's natural for prospects to have concerns. Your role is to listen, validate, and address them with empathy and clarity, gently guiding them back to their stated desires.

Common Objections & Your Faith-Centered Responses

"I need to pray about it / talk to my husband."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is a significant decision, and prayerful consideration is essential, as is involving your husband. Many of my clients have shared similar sentiments. To help you both make an informed decision, what specific questions or concerns might arise during your prayer and conversation that I could address right now? I want you to feel fully equipped and at peace."
"The investment is too high / I can't afford it right now."
"I hear you, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. It's true that investing in your transformation is a commitment. What I often find is that clients discover the cost of *not* addressing these issues — in terms of lost peace, strained relationships, and continued struggle — is far greater in the long run. My program is designed for deep, lasting change. Can I walk you through the specific value and ROI you'll receive, or perhaps discuss our flexible payment options?"
"I'm not sure I have the time / I'm already so busy."
"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, especially with everything you're juggling. My program is structured to integrate seamlessly into a busy life, requiring about 3-5 hours per week of focused work, which includes our coaching calls. Many clients find that by dedicating this time, they actually gain more time and energy in other areas as they overcome their struggles. What parts of your schedule feel most challenging, and how might we creatively make space for your healing?"
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It sounds like you've been on a long and frustrating journey, and it's completely understandable to feel weary. The key difference with faith-based recovery coaching is our holistic approach — we address not just behaviors, but the underlying spiritual and emotional roots, inviting God's power into every step. We focus on sustainable transformation, not just temporary fixes. Are you open to exploring an approach that prioritizes your spiritual foundation?"

Confident Pricing Presentation

Presenting your pricing is a moment of truth. Speak with confidence, conviction, and a clear understanding of the immense value you provide. Remember, you're not just selling hours; you're offering transformation.

Grace's Tip: Always state the full price first, then break down payment options. This anchors the value.

Presenting Your Signature Program

Your Pricing Dialogue
YOU:
"My Signature 12-Week Faith-Based Recovery Program is a deep dive into lasting transformation. It includes twelve weekly 60-minute one-on-one coaching sessions, personalized biblical resources and meditations, unlimited email support between sessions, and access to our private online community for ongoing encouragement. The full investment for this comprehensive program is $3,997."
YOU:
"For those who prefer, we also offer a flexible payment plan of 3 monthly payments of $1,399. Most clients find this manageable. My goal is to make this transformative journey accessible to those who are truly ready to commit."
YOU:
"Sarah, considering everything we've discussed today — your desire for spiritual renewal, freedom from emotional eating and alcohol, and a life filled with purpose — do you feel this program aligns with the breakthrough you're seeking?"
Coach's Insight

After stating your price, pause. Let the information land. Resist the urge to fill the silence. Your confidence in your value speaks volumes. This pause gives the client space to process and respond.

Case Study: From Imposter to Income

Empowering a Career Changer to Thrive

S

Susan, 49

Former teacher, felt called to faith-based coaching but struggled with sales confidence.

Presenting Challenge: Susan had completed her certification and was passionate about helping women find freedom in Christ. However, she dreaded discovery calls, feeling like she was "asking for money" rather than offering a solution. This imposter syndrome led to low conversion rates (only 1 out of 10 calls resulted in a client) and significant financial stress, hindering her ability to leave her teaching job.

Intervention: We worked with Susan on reframing discovery calls as sacred conversations, focusing on deep listening and discerning divine alignment. We role-played objection handling, emphasizing empathy and redirecting to the client's stated desires. We also practiced confident pricing presentation, anchoring it in the transformative value she offered, not just her time. Susan developed a clear understanding of her ideal client and their pain points, which helped her tailor her messaging.

Outcomes: Within 8 weeks, Susan's discovery call conversion rate jumped to 4 out of 10 calls (a 300% improvement). She signed 5 new clients in a single month, generating over $15,000 in program revenue. This newfound confidence allowed her to transition fully into her coaching practice, enabling her to serve more women and experience the financial freedom she desired, all while honoring her faith. She reported feeling "empowered by God to be a vessel of healing and a successful businesswoman."

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the initial 5 minutes of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to build rapport, make the prospect feel comfortable, and set a positive, engaging tone for the conversation. It's about connecting human-to-human.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is an effective next step for a Faith-Based Recovery Coach?

Acknowledge and validate their need for reflection, then gently inquire about the specific aspects they need to think about (e.g., investment, time, feeling understood). This helps uncover underlying concerns and allows you to address them directly.

3. How should you frame the discussion of your program's pricing to emphasize value?

State the full investment first, anchoring the value of the comprehensive transformation. Then, if applicable, offer flexible payment options. Frame the price as an "investment in transformation" rather than a "cost."

4. Why is it important to ask a prospect about their ideal life if their challenges were resolved?

This question helps the prospect visualize their desired future and articulate their deepest desires. It connects their pain points to a positive outcome, allowing you to position your coaching program as the bridge to that ideal future, making the value proposition much stronger.

Key Takeaways

  • The Discovery Call is a sacred conversation focused on deep listening and discerning divine alignment, not a high-pressure sales pitch.
  • Follow a clear structure: build rapport, understand their struggle and vision, present your faith-based solution, and guide to next steps.
  • Handle objections with empathy, validation, and clarity, gently redirecting the conversation back to the prospect's stated desires and the value you offer.
  • Present your program's investment with unwavering confidence, anchoring it in the transformative value and addressing financial concerns with flexible solutions.
  • Embrace the business side of coaching; it empowers you to serve more clients and achieve the financial freedom God desires for you.
