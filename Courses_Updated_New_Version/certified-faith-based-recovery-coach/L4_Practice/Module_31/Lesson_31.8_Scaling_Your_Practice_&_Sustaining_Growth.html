Your Prospect: Meet Sarah

A referral from a past client, ready for a change.

S

Sarah, 52

Found you through a friend's referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Struggling with anxiety and feeling "stuck" after her children left for college. She's been a stay-at-home mom for 25 years and now feels a loss of purpose. She's also noticed a decline in her consistent spiritual practices.
Budget Concern
"I've never invested in myself like this before. My husband handles the finances, and I'd need to discuss it with him. Is this really worth the investment?"
Decision Style
Needs to feel understood and supported. Values faith-based approaches but is wary of anything that feels too "preachy." Seeks practical tools and emotional support.
Her Goal
"I just want to find my purpose again, connect with God like I used to, and feel confident in this new season of my life. I want to feel joy."

Sarah represents many women in our target demographic: at a life transition, seeking renewed purpose and spiritual connection, and often hesitant about investing in themselves. Your ability to connect with her on a deeper, faith-aligned level will be key.

Coach Tip

Remember, the discovery call is a two-way street. You're not just assessing if the client is a good fit for your program; they are also assessing if you are the right coach for them. Authenticity, empathy, and a genuine desire to serve will shine through more than any perfectly memorized script.

The Faith-Based Discovery Call Script: Your Roadmap to Connection

A well-structured discovery call allows you to guide the conversation purposefully, ensuring you gather all necessary information while building rapport and demonstrating your value. This 30-minute framework is designed for efficiency and impact.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself. So Sarah, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on in your life that led you to consider coaching?"
YOU:
"My goal for our call today is to see if Faith-Based Recovery Coaching feels like the right next step for you, and if I'm the right coach to support you. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Desires (The Deep Dive) 10 min

This is where your active listening skills are paramount. Ask open-ended questions and truly listen to understand her pain, her hopes, and her spiritual perspective.

YOU:
"You mentioned feeling 'stuck' and a loss of purpose. Can you tell me more about that? When did you first start feeling this way, and how does it impact your daily life and your spiritual well-being?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, what didn't, and what's your experience been with integrating your faith into finding solutions?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What would your relationship with God feel like?"
Coach Tip

Listen for pain points and passion points. Pain points reveal where they're suffering; passion points reveal what they truly desire. Connect these back to how your coaching can bridge the gap. For Sarah, her pain is loss of purpose and spiritual decline, her passion is renewed connection and joy.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach (The Solution) 10 min

Based on what you've heard, summarize her situation and introduce your unique Faith-Based Recovery Coaching approach.

YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're feeling a deep sense of loss and uncertainty in this new season, longing to rediscover your purpose and strengthen your spiritual foundation. You've tried various things, but haven't found a holistic path that truly integrates your faith with practical steps for moving forward. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different. It's built on the F.A.I.T.H. Framework‚Ñ¢, which means we don't just look at surface-level challenges. We explore the deeper spiritual roots of your feelings, integrating prayer, scripture, and evidence-based coaching techniques to help you reconnect with God's purpose for your life. Over our 12-week journey, we'll work to identify your unique calling, develop daily spiritual practices, and build confidence in this new chapter. Most of my clients, especially women navigating similar life transitions, start feeling a profound sense of peace and clarity within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"This isn't a quick fix, but a transformative journey rooted in faith, designed to equip you with lasting tools and a renewed sense of self. How does that sound to you?"
Coach Tip

When presenting your solution, avoid jargon. Speak in terms of benefits and outcomes for the client. Instead of "I use the F.A.I.T.H. Framework," say "The F.A.I.T.H. Framework helps you..." and explain the direct positive impact on their life.

Phase 4: Close with Clarity (The Call-to-Action) 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on our conversation, does Faith-Based Recovery Coaching sound like what you've been looking for to find your purpose and deepen your faith in this season? ... Great! Let me tell you about the program details and how we can get started."

Handling Objections Gracefully and with Faith

Objections are not rejections; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence, anchoring your responses in both practical value and your faith-based approach.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and I respect that. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed, prayerful decision."

(Listen carefully to her answer. If it's about finances, move to the financial objection. If time, address time commitment. If general, reiterate benefits.)

"The investment is too high / I need to talk to my husband."

"I hear you, Sarah. Investing in yourself is a significant step, and it's wise to discuss it with your husband, especially when it comes to family finances. Many of my clients, particularly women who've prioritized others for years, initially feel this way. However, they often find that the return on investment in terms of renewed purpose, spiritual vitality, and overall well-being is immeasurable. Consider the cost of NOT making a change ‚Äì the continued anxiety, the feeling of being stuck. What would it mean for your family and your spiritual legacy if you were living fully in God's purpose?

Perhaps you and your husband could review the program details together, and I'd be happy to answer any questions he might have. I also offer a flexible payment plan to make it more accessible. Would that be helpful?"

"I'm not sure I have the time."

"That's a very valid concern, Sarah. Life is busy, especially during transitions. The program is designed to be flexible, requiring about 1-2 hours per week for our sessions and practical exercises. My clients often tell me that by investing this small amount of time, they actually gain more time and energy back because they're operating with greater clarity and purpose. We're not just adding another task; we're streamlining your focus and helping you steward your time in alignment with God's will. How much time are you currently spending feeling overwhelmed or searching for answers?"

"I've tried other programs before, and nothing has worked."

"I can certainly appreciate that, Sarah. It sounds like you've been dedicated to finding solutions, and it's frustrating when efforts don't yield results. What makes Faith-Based Recovery Coaching unique is its holistic, Christ-centered approach. We don't just address symptoms; we go to the spiritual root, seeking God's wisdom and integrating proven strategies for lasting transformation. This isn't just another self-help program; it's a journey of discipleship and empowerment. What felt missing or ineffective in those other programs for you?"

Confident Pricing Presentation

Presenting your pricing is a moment that can feel daunting, but remember your value. You are offering a transformational service rooted in faith. Speak clearly, confidently, and without apology.

Your Investment in Transformation

After you've presented your program and addressed any initial objections, it's time to state the price. Here‚Äôs a sample script:

YOU:
"Sarah, my 12-week Faith-Based Recovery Coaching program is an intensive, personalized journey designed to help you rediscover your purpose, deepen your spiritual connection, and step into this new season with joy and confidence. The investment for this program is a one-time payment of $2,997. I also offer a convenient 3-month payment plan of $1,050 per month."

Then, pause. Let the number land. The next thing you say should be a question, inviting her to respond.

YOU:
"Does that sound like something that would work for you, Sarah?"

Key Principles for Pricing:

  • State it Clearly: No hedging, no apologies.
  • Provide Options: A single payment and a payment plan.
  • Anchor Value: Remind them of the transformation, not just the cost.
  • Pause and Listen: Allow the client to respond. Their next words will reveal their true concerns.
Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident. Record yourself. If you hesitate or sound apologetic, it signals to the client that you're not fully confident in your value. Your conviction is contagious!

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Uncover Needs & Desires" phase in a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to actively listen and deeply understand the prospect's pain points, desires, and spiritual context, identifying their core motivations for seeking coaching.

2. When a prospect states, "I need to think about it," what is the recommended next step?

Acknowledge their need to think, then gently inquire what specifically they need to consider (e.g., investment, time, or other concerns) to address the underlying hesitation.

3. How should you present your program's price to a prospect?

State the price clearly and confidently, without apology. Offer both a full payment option and a payment plan, then pause and allow the prospect to respond.

4. What is a key benefit of Faith-Based Recovery Coaching to highlight when a prospect expresses skepticism about programs they've tried before?

Emphasize the holistic, Christ-centered approach that addresses spiritual roots and integrates proven strategies for lasting transformation, distinguishing it from secular or less comprehensive programs.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call is crucial for building rapport, understanding needs, and presenting your Faith-Based Recovery Coaching solution effectively.
  • Active listening and empathetic questioning are more valuable than a rigid script; adapt to the client's unique story.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, not roadblocks. Address them with calm confidence and faith-aligned responses.
  • Present your pricing clearly, confidently, and without apology, always linking it back to the transformative value you provide.
