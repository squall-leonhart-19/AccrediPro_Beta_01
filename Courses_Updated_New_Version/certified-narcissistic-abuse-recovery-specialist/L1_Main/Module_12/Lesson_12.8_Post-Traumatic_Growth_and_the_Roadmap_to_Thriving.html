Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: The Mirror Rehearsal

Stand in front of a mirror and say the phrase: "It is not your fault that you were lied to, but it is your responsibility to choose what happens next." Watch your facial expressions. Do you look confident? Or do you look hesitant? Your client will mirror your level of certainty.

Exercise 2: Identifying Gaslighting

Listen to Elena's statement: "He said I'm just stressed because of menopause and that's why I'm seeing things that aren't there." Practice your response out loud. Goal: Validate the medical reality (menopause doesn't cause bank statements to appear) without being aggressive toward the husband.

Common Pitfalls for New Specialists

The Mistake Why It Happens The Pro Correction
Giving Legal Advice Wanting to "save" the client. "I'm your recovery specialist; let's find you a specialist attorney."
Over-Validating Anger Empathizing too much with the client. Focus on the client's *healing*, not the narcissist's *evil*.
Rushing the Exit Impatience with the client's "circular" thinking. Understand that trauma bonding takes time to break.

Check Your Understanding

1. When Elena says "Maybe I am just senile," what is the most effective coaching response?

Redirect to the evidence. "Let's look at the bank statements again. Does 'senility' create physical documents, or are these documents a reflection of a reality you're being told to ignore?"

2. Why is the "Pattern Interrupt" crucial in Module 12 deep-dive sessions?

It stops the "rumination loop." Narcissistic abuse victims often get stuck in circular logic; the coach must interrupt the loop to bring the client back to the present moment and their own agency.

3. What is the danger of a coach "working harder than the client"?

It creates a new dependency. The goal of recovery is to restore the client's autonomy, not to replace the husband's control with the coach's direction.

4. True or False: A successful session always ends with the client deciding to leave the relationship.

False. Success is defined by the client's increased clarity, emotional regulation, and self-trust, regardless of their immediate relationship status.
