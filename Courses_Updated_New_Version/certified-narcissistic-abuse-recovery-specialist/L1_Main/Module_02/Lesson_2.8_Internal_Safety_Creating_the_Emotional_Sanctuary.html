Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we prioritize a "Safety Audit" over emotional processing in Module 2?

Because the brain cannot process trauma or engage in "healing" while the amygdala is in a state of active threat. Establishing safety (The "E" in RECOVERY) is the physiological prerequisite for recovery.

2. What is a "Safety Leak" in a digital context?

A safety leak is any unintentional way the abuser can access the client. Examples: Shared iCloud accounts, "Find My" app, shared Amazon/Netflix logins, or children's devices with location enabled.

3. How should you respond if a client says, "I feel like I'm going crazy because I'm so paranoid"?

Normalize and validate. Explain that hypervigilance is a biological survival mechanism (the "Security Guard") that served them well during the abuse and needs to be de-escalated through tangible safety measures.

4. What is the "Calm-Confident" tone?

A coaching presence characterized by a slower speaking pace, lower pitch, and steady breathing. This helps the client's nervous system co-regulate with the coach.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Validation is the first step of safety: Telling the client they aren't "crazy" immediately lowers cortisol.
  • The Safety Audit: Use a checklist approach to identify digital, physical, and social vulnerabilities.
  • Your Voice is a Tool: Use "Low and Slow" speech to act as a regulation anchor for the client.
  • Stay in Scope: Coaches provide safety planning and education; we refer out for legal advice or active crisis intervention.
