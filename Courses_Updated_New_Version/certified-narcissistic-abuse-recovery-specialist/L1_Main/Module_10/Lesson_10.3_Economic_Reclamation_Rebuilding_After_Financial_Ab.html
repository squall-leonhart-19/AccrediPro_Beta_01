Reclaiming the Purse Strings

Financial abuse is present in 99% of domestic violence cases, yet it is often the most overlooked component of recovery. For the high-achieving woman, the realization that her finances have been weaponized can trigger intense shame. The Mechanics of Financial Narcissism

Financial abuse is not always about withholding money; it is about the centralization of power. In narcissistic dynamics, money is used as a tool for idealization (love bombing through lavish gifts) and a weapon for devaluation (restricting access to basic needs).

Financial Infidelity refers to the secret manipulation of marital or shared assets. This may include:

  • Secret Debt: Opening credit lines in the survivorâ€™s name without their knowledge.
  • Asset Siphoning: Funneling communal income into private accounts or "shell" investments.
  • The Allowance Trap: Forcing a professional woman to "ask" for money for groceries while the abuser spends freely.
Coach Tip: The Professional Reframe

Many of your clients will be women who were once high-earners. They may say, "How could I let this happen?" Your role is to reframe this as systemic grooming. The narcissist didn't steal their money on day one; they eroded their confidence over a decade.

Navigating Litigation Abuse & Hidden Assets

When a survivor decides to leave, the narcissist often shifts to Litigation Abuse. This is the use of legal proceedings to harass, deplete, and demoralize the survivor. A 2021 study indicated that narcissists are 4x more likely to ignore court orders regarding financial disclosures than non-personality disordered litigants.

Paper Terrorism Drowning the survivor in frivolous motions to spike legal fees. Requesting "Conduct-Based Costs" from the court.

Client: Elena, 52, former corporate executive who became a stay-at-home mother. After 20 years of marriage, her husband "discarded" her, leaving her with a frozen joint account and $14,000 in secret credit card debt in her name.

The Restoration Protocol: Step-by-Step

For survivors, a credit score is more than a numberâ€”it is a Passport to Freedom. Without it, they cannot rent an apartment, buy a car, or sometimes even secure employment in certain sectors.

  1. Audit the Damage: Pull reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). Identify "ghost" accounts.
  2. The Credit Freeze: Immediately freeze credit to prevent the narcissist from opening new accounts during the separation.
  3. Fraud Affidavits: If the abuser opened accounts in the survivor's name, file a police report for identity theft. This is a hard step for many, but necessary for legal protection.
  4. Secured Lines: If credit is destroyed, start with a secured credit card to begin the rebuilding process.
Coach Tip: The "New Bank" Rule

Always advise clients to open their new accounts at an entirely different financial institution. Narcissists often use "social engineering" to gain access to accounts at banks where the couple was well-known.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological purpose of "Financial Infidelity" in a narcissistic relationship?

The primary purpose is the centralization of power and the erosion of the survivor's autonomy. By keeping secrets or creating debt in the survivor's name, the narcissist ensures the survivor feels "incapable" and "trapped" by their economic circumstances.

2. Why is a "Credit Freeze" recommended immediately upon separation?

A credit freeze prevents the narcissist from opening new fraudulent accounts or credit lines in the survivor's name, which is a common tactic used to sabotage the survivor's ability to rent a home or secure a car after leaving.

3. Define "Litigation Abuse" in the context of financial reclamation.

Litigation abuse is the use of the legal system to harass the survivor, typically by filing endless motions or ignoring discovery requests, with the intent to deplete the survivor's financial resources and force them into a disadvantageous settlement.

4. How does Economic Safety relate to the "E" phase of RECLAIM?

Economic safety provides the material foundation required to maintain physical and psychological safety. Without financial resources, a survivor is at high risk of returning to the abuser for survival (hoovering success), making economic reclamation a core safety priority.

Key Takeaways

  • A "Passport to Freedom" (healthy credit) is the most critical asset for a survivor's long-term independence.
