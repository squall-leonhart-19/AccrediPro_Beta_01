Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

As you start your practice, watch out for these four common mistakes that can hinder a client's integration phase:

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to "prove" the client isn't a narcissist with 20 minutes of theory. Keep it simple; use their own empathy as proof.
  • Diagnosing the Ex: While you know the ex was likely narcissistic, focus on the client's reaction. If you spend the whole hour talking about the ex, the client doesn't heal.
  • Taking it Personally: If the client stays stuck in shame for several sessions, don't feel like a "bad coach." Recovery is non-linear.
  • Breaking Professional Boundaries: Sharing too much of your own story. A little "I've been there" is fine, but Elena needs the focus on Elena.
Olivia's Practitioner Tip

If you find yourself wanting to "save" the client, take a step back. You are a coach, not a savior. Your job is to hand them the flashlight, but they have to walk the path.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a narcissist's behavior and "Reactive Abuse"?

The intent and the context. Narcissism is a pattern of power and control; reactive abuse is a defensive reaction to being mistreated. Additionally, survivors feel deep guilt for reactive abuse, whereas narcissists rarely feel genuine remorse for their actions.

2. Why is "The Mirror Test" effective for a client like Elena?

It uses logic to interrupt a shame spiral. It highlights that the client's current actions (seeking help, worrying about being a good person) are fundamentally incompatible with a narcissistic personality.

3. If Elena continues to obsess over the email, what should the coach do?

Pivot to context. Ask the client to describe the events leading up to the email to help them see the external pressure that triggered the internal reaction.

4. Which success marker indicates the client is moving from the "Past" to the "Future"?

A shift in focus toward handling future interactions and setting boundaries, rather than ruminating on past mistakes.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation is the First Step: Before Elena can learn, she must feel safe and understood.
  • Language Matters: Differentiating between "being" and "reacting" is crucial for de-shaming.
  • Confidence is a Muscle: Practicing scripts out loud reduces the "fraud" feeling for new coaches.
  • Focus on the Client: Keep the coaching session centered on the client's growth, not the narcissist's diagnosis.
