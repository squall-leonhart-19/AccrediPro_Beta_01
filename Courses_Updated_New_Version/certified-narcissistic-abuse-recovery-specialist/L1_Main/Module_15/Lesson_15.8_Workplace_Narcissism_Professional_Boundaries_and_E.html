The Enrollment Conversation (Clarity Call)

The "Clarity Call" is not a sales pitch; it is a professional assessment to see if your services align with the client’s needs. Your goal is to lead with authority and empathy.

Coaching Script: The First Consultation

Practice these words out loud. Imagine a 54-year-old woman named Ellen who is nervous about reaching out.

YOU:
"Ellen, I want to start by acknowledging how much courage it took to book this call. Most people suffer in silence for years, but you’ve taken the first step toward reclaiming your life. Tell me, what was the specific moment that made you say, 'I need support now'?"
ELLEN:
"I just... I realized I don't know who I am anymore. My husband has controlled every thought for thirty years. I'm scared I'm too old to change."
YOU:
"I hear you, and I want you to know—you are exactly where you need to be. Healing has no expiration date. Based on what you’ve shared, we would focus on three pillars: breaking the trauma bond, rebuilding your identity, and establishing iron-clad boundaries. Does that sound like the path you want to take?"
ELLEN:
"Yes. That’s exactly what I need. How does it work?"
YOU:
"We will work together over 12 weeks using the proprietary recovery framework I've been trained in. I’ll provide the tools, the structure, and the support so you never have to guess what to do next. My professional fee for this transformation is [Your Price]. Shall we look at the calendar and get your first official session scheduled?"
Olivia’s Insight

In my practice, I’ve seen that the most successful coaches aren’t the ones with the most "flashy" marketing—they are the ones who listen the most deeply. When you repeat back a client's needs, they feel seen for the first time in years.

Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you transition into your new career, watch for these common pitfalls that new coaches often encounter.

4 Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

  • Over-Coaching on the Clarity Call: Trying to "fix" the client in the first 15 minutes instead of showing them the path to the fix.
  • Underpricing Services: Pricing based on "hourly rates" instead of the value of the transformation.
  • Waiting for "The Perfect Website": Delaying client acquisition because your logo isn't perfect. Your first clients care about your heart, not your hex codes.
  • Ignoring Self-Care: Forgetting that you must be regulated to help others regulate.

Success Markers: How You Know You’re Ready

  • You can explain the cycle of narcissistic abuse clearly and calmly.
  • You feel comfortable using the DFY intake forms to vet clients.
  • You have a designated, quiet space for your virtual or in-person sessions.
  • You feel a sense of "rightness" when you think about helping others heal.
Olivia’s Insight

Success is not a straight line. Some weeks you will feel like a superhero, and some weeks you'll feel like a beginner. Both are part of the journey toward becoming a Master Practitioner.
