Check Your Understanding

1. Why does the coach reframe Elena's identity loss as a "survival skill"?

To remove the burden of shame. When a client views their "Adapted Self" as a brilliant survival mechanism rather than a personal failure, they feel safe enough to begin exploring their Authentic Self.

2. What is a "Mirroring" technique used in the script?

The coach pointed out that Elena's voice got stronger when she talked about teaching. Mirroring involves reflecting back the client's physiological and emotional "sparks" that they might not notice themselves.

3. True or False: You should give the client a list of hobbies to try to help them find themselves faster.

False. This is the "Fix-It" Trap. Recovery is about the process of choosing, not the choice itself. Giving them a list just creates a new "authority figure" for them to please.

4. What is the significance of "Micro-Decisions"?

They are the building blocks of autonomy. Choosing a book genre or a coffee flavor based on personal preference is a "win" that strengthens the identity muscle.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Identity is dormant, not dead: The Authentic Self is a foundation that cannot be destroyed by abuse, only covered by rubble.
  • Language Matters: Use terms like "flexing the preference muscle" and "Self-Stewardship" to empower clients.
  • Reframing is Healing: Turn their "weakness" (adaptation) into their "strength" (survival intelligence).
  • Watch for the Sparks: Your job is to notice when the client "lights up" and reflect that back to them.
