Practice Exercise: Say It Out Loud

The neural pathways for coaching are built through vocalization. Do not just read this; speak it.

Step 1: The Validation Statement

Speak this out loud: "Diane, I want to validate that what you've been through is a documented global crisis. Millions of women are being dismissed right now, but as a specialist, I see the patterns they are missing."

Step 2: The "Why" Transition

Speak this out loud: "The reason your labs look normal is that they are looking for broken bones or tumors, but your crisis is happening in the way your nervous system processes signals. That requires a different set of tools."

Step 3: The Call to Action

Speak this out loud: "In our first 90 days together, we are going to focus on turning down that pain volume. Does that sound like the support you've been looking for?"

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Pitfall Why It Fails The Specialist Fix
Over-Apologizing "I'm sorry you feel that way" sounds like pity. Use Validation: "It makes sense that you feel frustrated given the statistics."
Vague Promises "I'll help you feel better" lacks authority. Use Specifics: "We will use the Fibro-Flow protocol to address sleep first."
Downplaying Training "I'm just a coach" creates imposter syndrome. Use Credentials: "As a Certified Fibromyalgia Specialist, I am trained in..."
Doctor-Bashing Unprofessional and creates anxiety. Use Systemic Context: "Doctors are trained for acute care; I am trained for the chronic crisis."

Success Markers

How do you know if you've successfully navigated this "Crisis Conversation"? Look for these cues from the client:

  • The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes or sighs with relief when you validate their pain.
  • The "Me Too" Moment: They say, "I've never heard anyone explain it that way before."
  • Shift in Tone: Their voice moves from defensive or hopeless to curious.
  • Agreement on the "Crisis": They acknowledge that the current medical system has failed them.
Sarah's Tip

Practice recording yourself on your phone. Listen back. Do you sound like someone Diane can trust? If you sound hesitant, keep practicing the script until the words "Central Sensitization" and "Global Crisis" roll off your tongue naturally.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use statistics (like the 5-year average for diagnosis) during a discovery call?

It moves the conversation from personal opinion to professional authority. It validates the client's struggle as a systemic issue rather than a personal failure or "craziness."

2. What is the "Specialist Fix" for the pitfall of over-apologizing?

Replacing pity with validation. Instead of saying "I'm sorry," say "It makes sense that you feel this way given the lack of support for fibromyalgia in conventional medicine."

3. What is the "Exhale" marker in a coaching session?

A physical sign of relief from the client, indicating they finally feel heard and understood by someone who recognizes the reality of their condition.

4. How should a Specialist describe the difference between their work and a doctor's work?

By framing it as a difference in training: Doctors are trained for acute disease and diagnostics, while Specialists are trained for chronic nervous system regulation and lifestyle triggers.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Validation is the first "Treatment": Before a client can change their diet or lifestyle, they must feel believed.
  • Use the Crisis as Context: Framing Fibromyalgia as a global crisis removes the "shame" from the individual client.
  • Authority is Earned: Using specific data and terminology (like central sensitization) builds immediate trust.
  • Practice Vocalization: Speaking the script out loud is the only way to overcome "imposter syndrome" and sound like the expert you are.
