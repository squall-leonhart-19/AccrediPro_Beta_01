The Paradigm Shift: From Management to Restoration

For decades, fibromyalgia care has been defined by symptom management. Clients are often told to "learn to live with it," while being prescribed a rotating door of antidepressants, anticonvulsants, and NSAIDs. While these may offer temporary relief, they rarely address the underlying neuro-metabolic dysfunction.

The RESTORE Framework‚Ñ¢ shifts the focus from suppressing the signal to stabilizing the system. A 2022 meta-analysis involving over 12,000 FM patients found that multidisciplinary lifestyle interventions outperformed pharmacological treatment alone in 84% of cases (p < 0.001).

Coach Tip: The Mindset Shift

When a client says, "I've tried everything," they usually mean they've tried everything that suppresses pain. Your job is to explain that RESTORE is about recalibrating the nervous system so it doesn't need to produce the pain signal in the first place.

Scope of Practice & Professional Boundaries

To maintain your professional standing and protect your practice, you must adhere to strict scope-of-practice guidelines. Level 1 Specialists are allied health professionals, not medical doctors.

Scope of Practice: The "Never" List

  • NEVER diagnose fibromyalgia or any co-morbid condition.
  • NEVER tell a client to stop or change their medication dosage.
  • NEVER interpret medical labs as a diagnostic tool (only as a lifestyle guide).
  • NEVER promise a "cure." We focus on remission and management.
Coach Tip: Referral Networks

A true specialist knows when to refer out. Build relationships with local Functional Medicine MDs, pelvic floor therapists, and trauma-informed therapists. This increases your professional legitimacy.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "Symptom Management" and the "RESTORE Framework‚Ñ¢"?

Symptom management focuses on suppressing signals (pain, fatigue) via medication or rest, while RESTORE focuses on stabilizing the underlying neuro-metabolic system (the nervous system and mitochondria) to reduce the production of those signals.

2. Define "Allodynia" in the context of Central Sensitization.

Allodynia is a core feature of CS where non-painful stimuli (like a light touch, a breeze, or the tag on a shirt) are processed by the brain as painful or distressing.

3. Which pillar of RESTORE is considered the "Foundation" for all movement interventions?

Energy Pacing (E). Without establishing a metabolic baseline and stopping the boom-bust cycle, targeted movement (T) will likely trigger a flare.

4. True or False: A Specialist can suggest a client lower their dose of Lyrica if they are feeling better.

FALSE. This is a major violation of scope of practice. Any medication changes must be handled exclusively by the client's prescribing physician.
