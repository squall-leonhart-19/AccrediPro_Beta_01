Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced coaches can stumble when discussing the medical crisis. Watch out for these 4 pitfalls:

  • Bad-mouthing Doctors: Avoid saying "Doctors are incompetent." Instead, say "The system is designed for acute care, not chronic support."
  • Over-Promising: Don't say "I will cure you." Say "We will work together to improve your quality of life and reduce your symptom burden."
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Jumping into supplements or diet changes before validating her pain. Validation must come first.
  • Toxic Positivity: Avoid "Just think positive!" Fibromyalgia is a physiological crisis, not just a mindset issue.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to mention the "Global Crisis" or systemic failure to a new client?

It removes the burden of guilt from the client. By explaining that the system is not built for chronic, complex conditions, you help them realize they aren't "failing" and that their lack of progress isn't their fault.

2. What is the "Fibro Sigh" a sign of in a coaching session?

It is a physiological marker of safety. It indicates that the client feels validated and heard, which lowers their sympathetic nervous system (fight/flight) responseâ€”a crucial first step in fibromyalgia recovery.

3. Which of the following is the best way to handle a client's "normal" lab results?

Tell the client: "Normal labs mean your organs aren't in acute failure, but they don't measure how your system is functioning day-to-day. Your symptoms are the most important data we have."

4. How does a "Specialist" voice differ from a "Generalist" voice?

A specialist speaks with calm authority about the specific mechanisms of the condition and doesn't shy away from the difficult reality of the healthcare crisis. They offer a specific roadmap rather than vague wellness advice.
