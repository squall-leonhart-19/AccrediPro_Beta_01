Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Sleep Runway" metaphor effective for fibromyalgia clients?

It visualizes the physiological need for the nervous system to transition from sympathetic (alert) to parasympathetic (rest) dominance, reducing resistance to changing evening habits.

2. Diane is resistant to giving up her phone in bed. What is the best coaching response?

Acknowledge her need for "me time" first. Then, explain the blue light/melatonin suppression link and offer a "bridge" activity like an e-reader with a warm filter or a relaxing podcast.

3. What is the "Tired but Wired" state biologically indicative of?

It indicates HPA-axis dysregulation where cortisol remains inappropriately high in the evening, preventing the natural dip needed for deep sleep onset.

4. How many sleep hygiene changes should you recommend in a single session?

Ideally just 1 or 2. Fibromyalgia clients often suffer from cognitive fatigue; too many changes lead to non-compliance and a sense of failure.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation First: Always validate the frustration of non-restorative sleep before offering solutions.
  • The 3 AM Signal: Recognize middle-of-the-night waking as a nervous system/blood sugar signal, not a character flaw.
  • Small Wins: A 15-minute shift in the "Sleep Runway" is a major victory for a chronic pain client.
  • You are the Guide: Your role is to provide the science-backed "why" so the client feels empowered to change the "how."
