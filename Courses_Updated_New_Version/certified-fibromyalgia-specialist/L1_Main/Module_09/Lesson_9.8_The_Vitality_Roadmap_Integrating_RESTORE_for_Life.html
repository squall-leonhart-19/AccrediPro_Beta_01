Common Pitfalls for New Specialists

When you're starting out, it's easy to fall into these traps during the long-term management phase:

  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Being too positive and dismissing the client's very real fear of relapse. Acknowledge the fear before pivoting to the plan.
  • Protocol Rigidity: Forcing a client to stay on a 100% strict elimination diet forever. This leads to burnout and "hidden" non-compliance. Vitality is about flexibility.
  • Lack of Future Pacing: Failing to talk about what life looks like 6 months from now. If the client doesn't see a "Level 2," they will stop seeing the value in your coaching.
  • Under-charging for Maintenance: Thinking that because the "hard work" is done, the sessions should be cheaper. Maintenance is where the real life transformation happens!
