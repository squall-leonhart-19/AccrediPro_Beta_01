Common Mistakes for New Coaches

When you're starting out, it's easy to fall into these patterns. Be mindful of them as you practice.

  • The "Fixer" Mentality: Jumping straight to supplements or diet changes before the client feels fully "heard" in the assessment phase.
  • Over-Explaining Science: Using too much jargon. Keep it simple: "Your body is in defensive mode" is better than a 10-minute lecture on cytokine storms.
  • Ignoring the Timeline: Failing to ask "What happened right before you got sick?" This is the most important question in a Root Assessment.
  • Imposter Shrinking: Using "filler" words like "I just think..." or "Maybe we could..." Speak with the authority of your training!

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Health Timeline" critical in a Root Assessment?

It helps identify the "Initial Insult" (viral, traumatic, or toxic) that triggered the nervous system's defensive state, allowing you to target the cause rather than just the symptom.

2. What is the danger of the "Fixer Mentality" during this phase?

If you jump to "fixing" too early, the client may feel unheard, and you may miss secondary root causes that are essential for long-term recovery.

3. Elena mentions her labs are "normal." How should a Specialist respond?

Validate her experience. Explain that standard labs often miss functional imbalances and that her pain is real regardless of what a basic blood panel shows.

4. Which "Root" did the coach identify in Elena's script?

The "Viral Root" (shingles) combined with the "Stress Root" (teaching/caregiving), which led to mitochondrial dysfunction.
