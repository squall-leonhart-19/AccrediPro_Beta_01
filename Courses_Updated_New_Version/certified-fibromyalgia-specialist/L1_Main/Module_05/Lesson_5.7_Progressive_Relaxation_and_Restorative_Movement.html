Proprioception, Interoception, and the Overdrive Loop

In a state of Central Sensitization, the brain's internal map of the body becomes "blurry." This is due to two key neurological processes: Proprioception (knowing where your body is in space) and Interoception (sensing the internal state of the body).

A 2022 study published in the Journal of Clinical Medicine found that fibromyalgia patients demonstrate significantly decreased proprioceptive accuracy compared to healthy controls (p < 0.001). When the brain cannot accurately "hear" the body, it defaults to a "high-alert" state, assuming danger is present. The Body Scan: Releasing Subconscious Micro-Tension

Fibromyalgia clients often suffer from "holding patterns"â€”subconscious muscle contractions they aren't even aware of. Common sites include the Masseter (jaw), the Pelvic Floor, and the Intercostals (ribs).

A systematic Body Scan protocol should move from the periphery to the core. Use the following script elements with your clients:

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is traditional high-intensity PMR contraindicated for most fibromyalgia clients?

High-intensity contraction can be interpreted by a sensitized CNS as a threat or injury signal, potentially triggering a sympathetic "surge" and a subsequent pain flare.

2. What is the "20% Tension" rule in Micro-PMR?

It is the practice of only engaging a muscle to 20% of its maximum capacity to provide proprioceptive feedback to the brain without overwhelming the sensory system.

3. Which restorative posture is specifically designed to neutralize the psoas and lower back?

The Constructive Rest Position (lying on the back with knees bent and feet flat).

4. What is the neurological difference between proprioception and interoception?

Proprioception is the sense of the body's position in space (outer), while interoception is the sense of the internal physiological state of the body (inner). Both are often impaired in fibromyalgia.
