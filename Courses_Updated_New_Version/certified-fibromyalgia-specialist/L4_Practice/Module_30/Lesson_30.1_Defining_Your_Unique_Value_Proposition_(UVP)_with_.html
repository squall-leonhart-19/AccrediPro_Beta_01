Analyzing the Competitive Landscape

The chronic pain market is underserved and overwhelmed. While there are over 100,000 general health coaches in the US, there are fewer than 1,000 practitioners specifically trained in the neurobiology of central sensitization. This creates a massive supply-demand imbalance.

Fibromyalgia patients are often "medical refugees"‚Äîindividuals who have been dismissed by conventional doctors and feel misunderstood by general wellness coaches who suggest "just eating more kale" or "trying yoga." They are looking for a Specialist who understands the Alpha-Delta sleep anomaly, the Boom-Bust cycle, and the vagus nerve.

Coach Tip: The Authority Gap

Your clients aren't buying "coaching"; they are buying a solution to their pain. When you position yourself as a Specialist, you move from being a "nice-to-have" expense to a "must-have" investment. This allows you to command professional rates (typically $150-$300/hour) rather than generalist rates ($50-$75/hour).

Defining Your Ideal Client Avatar (ICA)

In marketing, "if you speak to everyone, you speak to no one." Within the fibromyalgia spectrum, there are three primary ICAs you might choose to serve:

  • The High-Achieving Professional: The woman in her 40s or 50s who is trying to maintain a high-stress career while her body is failing her. She values efficiency, science, and results.
  • The Newly Diagnosed: The individual who just received a diagnosis and is terrified. They need foundational education and "Root Assessment" to understand their new reality.
  • The "Treatment-Resistant" Case: The client who has tried everything‚Äîmeds, supplements, physical therapy‚Äîand nothing worked. They need the deep CNS regulation and "Resilience Cultivation" aspects of RESTORE.
