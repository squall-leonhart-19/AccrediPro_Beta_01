The Physician Outreach Packet

Physicians, particularly rheumatologists and GPs, are often overwhelmed by fibromyalgia patients. These consultations are time-consuming, and doctors frequently feel they have few tools beyond pharmaceutical management. Your Physician Outreach Packet must present the RESTORE Framework‚Ñ¢ as a complementary, evidence-based support system.

Your packet should include:

  • A Cover Letter: Brief, professional, and focused on their pain points (patient adherence and time management).
  • The RESTORE Methodology Brief: A one-page executive summary of the 7 pillars, citing the neurobiological basis of each.
  • Evidence of Outcomes: Anonymized data or a sample progress report showing how you track pain baselines and activity levels.
  • Scope of Practice Statement: Explicitly stating you do not diagnose or treat, but provide behavioral implementation and CNS regulation support.
Coach Tip: The Imposter Syndrome Antidote

Remember: You are not a "subordinate" to the doctor; you are a specialist in a field they likely had less than 4 hours of training on in medical school. You are the expert in lifestyle implementation, which is the very thing they need most for their fibromyalgia patients.

The Art of the Professional Progress Report

To maintain a referral network, you must prove your value through data. Doctors value objective metrics and concise communication. When a client is referred to you, or when you wish to update their primary care provider (with client consent), use a standardized report format.

Client-Facing Language Clinical/Physician Language
"I feel less tired today." "Improved metabolic pacing; 20% increase in activity baseline."
"The pain isn't as scary now." "Reduction in pain catastrophizing scores (PCS)."
"I'm sleeping through the night." "Stabilization of circadian rhythm; improved sleep architecture."
"I can walk to the mailbox again." "Increased functional mobility and kinesiophobia reduction."

A 2022 survey found that 84% of primary care physicians would be more likely to refer to a health specialist if they received regular, concise clinical updates regarding their patients' progress (n=1,200).

Networking for Integrative and Multidisciplinary Centers

Multidisciplinary pain centers are your "Gold Mine." These centers already believe in a team-based approach. Your goal is to fill the gap between the Physical Therapist (who handles movement) and the Physician (who handles medicine).

Strategic Targets:

  • Integrative Medicine Clinics: These practitioners are already looking for "Root Cause" specialists.
  • Pelvic Floor Physical Therapists: High comorbidity between fibromyalgia and pelvic pain.
  • Sleep Labs: Since sleep optimization is Pillar 3 of RESTORE, you are a natural ally for patients who "fail" CPAP or have non-apnea sleep disturbances.
Coach Tip: The 2-Minute Pitch

Avoid saying "I help people feel better." Instead, say: "I specialize in the RESTORE Framework for fibromyalgia, helping patients stabilize their central nervous system through energy pacing and sleep optimization. My goal is to improve their adherence to your clinical recommendations."

Positioning as an 'Adherence Specialist'

The greatest frustration for a physician is the non-adherent patient. A doctor may tell a patient to "reduce stress" or "start walking," but the patient, paralyzed by fear (kinesiophobia) and brain fog, rarely follows through. This is where you shine.

By positioning yourself as an Adherence Specialist, you are telling the doctor: "You give the orders; I ensure they have the tools, mindset, and pacing strategy to actually follow them." This reduces the doctor's "emotional labor" and improves their clinical outcomes, making you an indispensable asset to their practice.

Navigating Case Conferencing Logistics

Case conferencing is the formal process of discussing a client's care with their medical team. This requires strict adherence to privacy laws (HIPAA in the US, GDPR in Europe).

Key Logistics:

  • Release of Information (ROI): Never speak to a doctor without a signed ROI from your client.
  • The "SBAR" Method: Use the clinical communication standard: Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation.
  • Billing/Time: While you may not bill insurance, the doctor might be able to bill for "Coordinated Care" time. Mentioning this can sometimes make them more eager to jump on a 10-minute call.
Coach Tip: Persistence Pays

It often takes 5 to 7 "touches" before a busy medical office remembers who you are. Don't be discouraged if your first packet is ignored. Follow up with a handwritten note or a relevant research article on fibromyalgia pacing.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Adherence Specialist" positioning so effective with physicians?

It addresses the physician's primary pain point: patients who fail to implement lifestyle recommendations due to lack of time, fear, or complexity. It positions you as the "bridge" that ensures clinical orders are executed.

2. What does the "A" in the SBAR communication method stand for?

Assessment. In this context, it refers to your professional observation of the client's current status regarding the RESTORE pillars (e.g., "The client is currently struggling with Pillar 2: Energy Pacing, leading to frequent boom-bust cycles").

3. True or False: You should wait for a doctor to call you before sending a progress report.

False. Proactively sending a concise, professional report (with client consent) demonstrates your value and keeps your name in front of the physician for future referrals.

4. Which clinical term would you use in a report to describe a client who is afraid to exercise?

Kinesiophobia. Using clinical terms like this demonstrates your specialized training and helps the doctor quickly understand the behavioral barrier.
