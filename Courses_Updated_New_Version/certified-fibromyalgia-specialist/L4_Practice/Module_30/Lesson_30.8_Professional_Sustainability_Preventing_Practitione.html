The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A successful call is 80% listening and 20% leading. Use this structure to ensure Diane feels heard and understood.

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (0-5 mins)
YOU:

"Hi Diane! I've been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive in, I want to let you know how this call works. My goal today is to understand exactly what you're going through. If I feel I can help you reach your goals, I'll tell you how. If not, I'll point you to someone who can. Does that sound fair?"

Phase 2: Deep Discovery (5-15 mins)
YOU:

"You mentioned in your form that the brain fog is the hardest part right now. Tell me, how does that affect your day-to-day life? What is it costing you emotionally?"

YOU:

"And what have you tried so far? Why do you think those approaches didn't give you the lasting relief you're looking for?"

Phase 3: The Bridge (15-25 mins)
YOU:

"Diane, based on what you've shared, I can see why you're frustrated. Most protocols ignore the mitochondrial dysfunction and central sensitization that are driving your symptoms. In my 12-week 'Fibromyalgia Freedom' program, we address these root causes directly. Would you like to hear how that works?"

Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 mins)
YOU:

"The investment for the full 12-week intensive is $2,400. We can do that in one payment or a monthly plan. Based on everything we've discussed, do you feel this is the support you need to finally get your life back?"

Handling Common Objections

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy and authority.

The Objection What They Are Really Asking Your Professional Response
"It's too expensive." Is the result worth the cost? "I understand. If this program could reduce your pain by 50% and give you back your energy, what would that be worth to you?"
"I need to talk to my husband." I'm afraid to fail again. "Of course. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Is he supportive of you finding a solution?"
"I've tried so many things." Why is this different? "Exactly. You've tried symptom-masking. We are doing root-cause repair using the CFS‚Ñ¢ methodology. That's why this is different."
Sarah's Tip

Never lower your price on the spot. If a client has a genuine financial hardship, offer a longer payment plan, but keep the total value of your expertise intact. You are a specialist, not a commodity.

Pricing Confidence & The "No-Flinch" Method

Most new practitioners fail because they whisper their price or apologize for it. A 2022 study on consumer psychology found that confidence in pricing is directly correlated with perceived competence of the provider.

Practice saying these lines out loud until they feel like second nature:

  • "My 90-day intensive is a $3,000 investment."
  • "I only take on 4 new clients a month to ensure this level of deep support."
  • "Which payment option works best for your budget?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Discovery" phase (Phase 2)?

The goal is to understand the emotional and physical "cost" of the client's condition. You are looking for the "why" behind their desire for change, which builds the necessity for your solution.

2. If a client says, "I've tried everything and nothing works," how should you respond?

Validate their frustration, then differentiate your approach. Explain that they likely tried managing symptoms, whereas your CFS‚Ñ¢ training allows you to address the underlying nervous system and mitochondrial dysfunction.

3. Why is "package pricing" (e.g., $2,500 for 12 weeks) better than "per-session" pricing?

Package pricing ensures client commitment to the full healing journey, provides predictable income for the practitioner, and shifts the focus from "buying time" to "buying a result."

4. True or False: You should wait until the end of the call to mention your price.

True. You must first establish the value of the transformation and ensure the client feels understood before the price is introduced. If price comes first, it's a cost; if it comes after value, it's an investment.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are a service, not a sales pitch; focus on active listening and empathy.
  • Use the 4-phase script to lead the prospect from pain to a potential solution.
  • Handle objections by uncovering the "hidden" fear and addressing it with authority.
  • Specialized "Package Pricing" allows for higher income with fewer hours and better client outcomes.
  • Confidence in stating your fees is a learned skill that improves with vocal practice.
