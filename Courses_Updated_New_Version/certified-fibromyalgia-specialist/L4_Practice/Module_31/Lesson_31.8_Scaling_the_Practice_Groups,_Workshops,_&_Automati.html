1. The Prospect Profile: Meet Diane

Before you pick up the phone, you must understand who is on the other end. In this lab, we are working with Diane, a classic "ideal client" for a Fibromyalgia Specialist.

Prospect: Diane Miller, 51

Background: Former elementary school teacher. Diagnosed with Fibromyalgia 4 years ago. Currently working part-time because she can't handle the full-time physical demand.

The Pain: "Invisible" pain, morning stiffness that lasts until noon, and "fibro-fog" that makes her feel like she's losing her mind. She's tried Lyrica (side effects were too much) and physical therapy (which left her flared up).

The Motivation: Her daughter is getting married in 6 months. Diane is terrified she won't be able to dance or even stand for the photos.

The Fear: "Is this just another thing I'm going to spend money on that won't work?"

Coach Tip

Diane isn't buying "nutrition" or "lifestyle coaching." She is buying the ability to dance at her daughter's wedding. Always anchor your conversation to her deepest "Why."

A discovery call is not a coaching session. It is a structured conversation to see if you are a fit. Follow this 4-phase structure.

Phase 1: Rapport & Setting the Agenda (0-5 min)

3. Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are not "no's." They are requests for more information or a manifestation of fear. Practice these responses out loud.

The Objection The "Sarah" Response (The Script)
"It's too expensive." "I completely understand. It is an investment. But let me askâ€”what is the cost of not doing this? What have you already spent on things that didn't work?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I love that you support each other in big decisions! What do you think he'll be most concerned aboutâ€”the cost, or the time commitment?"
"I've tried so many things..." "I hear you. Most of my clients felt the same way. The difference here is we are using a functional, root-cause approach, not just masking symptoms."
Coach Tip

When you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak usually loses their confidence. State the price and wait for her to process it. Silence is your friend.

4. Confident Pricing & Income Potential

As a specialist, your time is valuable. Do not charge by the hour; charge by the transformation. A 12-week program allows you to see real results and provides you with stable, predictable income.

Realistic Income Scenarios

Let's look at what is possible for a practitioner working from home, part-time or full-time, using a standard 12-week package priced at $1,500 (a conservative starting point).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (Discovery) in the call script?

The purpose is to identify the "Gap"â€”the distance between where the client is (pain) and where they want to be (goals/events like a wedding). This creates the emotional necessity for the solution.

2. If a client says, "I need to think about it," what is the most professional follow-up?

Acknowledge the decision, then ask: "I understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's because they have a specific concern about the money, the time, or the program itself. Which one is it for you?" This allows you to address the real root of their hesitation.

3. Why is "Package Pricing" better than "Hourly Pricing" for a Fibromyalgia Specialist?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome/transformation rather than the time spent. It also ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see physiological changes (usually 12 weeks) and provides predictable income for the practitioner.

4. How should you present your price on a call?

State the price clearly, offer payment options (e.g., pay-in-full vs. monthly), and then remain silent to allow the prospect to respond.

Key Takeaways

  • Invite, Don't Sell: View every call as an invitation to a better life, not a high-pressure sales pitch.
  • Anchor to the "Why": Focus on the client's emotional goals (weddings, playing with grandkids) rather than just clinical markers.
  • Specialization Equals Value: Because you are a Specialist, you can charge premium prices that reflect your unique expertise.
  • Consistency is Key: Income growth comes from a consistent enrollment process and a structured 12-week program delivery.
