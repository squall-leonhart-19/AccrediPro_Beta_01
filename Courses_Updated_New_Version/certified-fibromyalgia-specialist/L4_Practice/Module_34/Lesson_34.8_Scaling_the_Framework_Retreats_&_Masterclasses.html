4. Pricing with Confidence

When it's time to state the price, do not lower your voice and do not rush. Silence is your friend.

The Pricing Script

YOU: "The investment for the 8-week Fibro-Freedom Intensive, including all materials, the community, and our weekly live sessions, is a one-time payment of $1,200, or three monthly installments of $450. Which of those works better for your budget?"

(Stop talking. Wait for her to speak first.)

Sarah's Lesson

If you apologize for your price, you are telling the client your service isn't worth it. Own your value. You have a specialized certification that 99% of health coaches don't have.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of a group program for a fibromyalgia client?

The primary benefit is the reduction of isolation. Fibromyalgia is an "invisible illness," and being in a community of others who validate their experience is often as therapeutic as the clinical protocols themselves.

2. If a prospect says, "I need to talk to my husband first," what is the best transition?

"I completely respect that. When you talk to him, what do you think his biggest concern will be? And how can I help you explain the value of you finally feeling like yourself again?" (This identifies the real objection‚Äîusually money or skepticism).

3. Why should you stop talking immediately after stating your price?

To allow the prospect to process the information. Silence demonstrates confidence. If you keep talking, you often end up "discounting" yourself out of nervousness before they even object.

4. True or False: You should wait until you have 20 people to start your first group.

False. A "Beta Group" of 3-5 people is a fantastic way to start. It feels intimate, allows for high-touch support, and provides the testimonials you need to grow to 10-20 people later.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Community is Medicine: Frame your group as a support system, not just a set of lessons.
  • The 30-Minute Rule: Keep discovery calls focused. Spend 20 minutes on their pain/goals and only 10 minutes on your program details.
  • Posture Over Price: Your confidence in the price is more important than the actual dollar amount.
  • Scaling is Freedom: Groups allow you to help more people in less time, preventing practitioner burnout‚Äîwhich is vital when working with chronic illness populations.
