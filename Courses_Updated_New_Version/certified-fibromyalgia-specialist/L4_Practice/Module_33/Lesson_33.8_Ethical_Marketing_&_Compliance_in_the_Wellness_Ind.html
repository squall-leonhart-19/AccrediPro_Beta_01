Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a sign of fear. Your job is to lead the client through that fear.

Objection The "Sarah" Response The Goal
"I can't afford it." "I understand. If money wasn't an issue, is this the program you feel would actually help you?" Determine if it's a money issue or a value issue.
"I need to talk to my husband." "I love that you support each other. What do you think he will be most concerned about‚Äîthe cost or the time?" Identify the real barrier so you can help her address it with him.
"I've tried so many things." "That‚Äôs exactly why we‚Äôre talking. Most things treat symptoms. We are addressing the root nervous system safety." Reiterate the Unique Selling Proposition (USP).

Coach Sarah's Tip

Never lower your price on the call. If they truly have a budget constraint, offer a longer payment plan (e.g., 6 months instead of 3), but keep the total value the same. This maintains your professional integrity.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it legally critical to provide a disclaimer at the start of the Discovery Call?

It establishes your "Scope of Practice" immediately, ensuring the client knows you are not providing medical advice, which protects you from liability and malpractice claims while building professional authority.

2. If a client says, "I need to think about it," what is the most professional follow-up?

Ask, "Of course. To help you think through it, what specifically are you feeling unsure about‚Äîthe program structure, the time, or the investment?" This allows you to address the root of the hesitation.

3. True or False: You should give Donna 3-4 tips she can start today during the Discovery Call.

False. Giving "tips" turns the call into a free coaching session, which often leads to the client feeling "full" and not signing up. The call should focus on the problem and the solution (your program).

4. What is the "emotional goal" you should look for in Donna's story?

The emotional goal is the impact of the symptoms‚Äîin Donna's case, it's the ability to play with her grandkids. People buy the result, not the process.

Key Takeaways

  • Lead with Ethics: Your legal disclaimer is a tool for professional authority, not just a "shield."
  • Listen More than Talk: In a 30-minute call, the prospect should be talking for at least 18-20 minutes.
  • Value over Price: Always frame the investment in the context of what they are currently losing (time, money on failed meds, quality of life).
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Use the script provided to practice out loud at least 5 times before your first real call.
  • Income is Scalable: A boutique practice with just 5 new clients a month can out-earn many corporate management positions.
