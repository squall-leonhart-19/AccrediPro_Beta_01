The Cross-State Conundrum: Where Does Practice Occur?

In the world of telehealth, the most critical legal principle to remember is this: The practice occurs where the client sits, not where the specialist sits. If you are in Florida and your client is in California, you are technically practicing in California.

For licensed medical professionals, this often requires holding a license in every state where they have clients. However, as a Certified Fibromyalgia Specialist‚Ñ¢, you operate in the realm of non-clinical health coaching and education. This provides more flexibility, but it does not exempt you from state-specific "Scope of Practice" laws. Some states have stricter definitions of what constitutes "nutrition advice" or "mental health counseling" than others.

Coach Tip

Before accepting a client in a new state, spend 20 minutes researching that state's Health Freedom Laws. States like Arizona, Colorado, and Minnesota have "Safe Harbor" laws that explicitly protect the rights of non-licensed practitioners to provide wellness services.

Identity & Location Verification

In a digital environment, you cannot assume the person on the screen is who they say they are, or that they are where they claim to be. For legal record-keeping, you must verify both.

The "Digital Handshake" Protocol

During the initial intake session, you must perform the following:

  • Visual ID Check: Ask the client to hold a government-issued ID to the camera.
  • Location Confirmation: Ask the client to state their current physical address at the start of every session. This is vital because if they have traveled to a state where you are not permitted to practice, you must reschedule.
  • IP Verification: Professional telehealth platforms often log the IP address, providing a secondary layer of location verification for your records.

Emergency Mandates: Remote Crisis Management

What happens if a client experiences a severe panic attack, a physical fall, or a mental health crisis during a Zoom session? Because you are not physically present, you must have a pre-defined Emergency Protocol Mandate.

Coach Tip

Never rely on "911" for a remote client. If you call 911 from your phone, it will route to your local dispatch. You must have the phone number for the local police precinct in the client's specific town on file.

Required Emergency File Elements:

  1. Local Emergency Number: The direct line to the client's local emergency services.
  2. Physical Address: The exact location where the client usually conducts sessions.
  3. Emergency Contact: A person in the client's vicinity who can be reached immediately.
  4. "Safe Word" System: A word the client can use if they feel they are entering a state of overwhelm where they can no longer follow instructions.

Tax & Business Registration: The Location-Independent Specialist

If you are a "digital nomad" or simply practice across multiple states, you encounter the concept of Nexus. Nexus is a legal term for having a "sufficient physical or economic presence" in a state to be subject to its taxes.

  • Income Tax: Generally, you pay income tax in the state where you are physically located.
  • Sales Tax: Some states consider "health coaching" or "educational services" as taxable services. If you have enough clients in a specific state (e.g., 200 transactions or $100k in sales), you may have "Economic Nexus" and be required to collect sales tax.
  • Business Registration: If you are physically living in a state for more than 30-90 days (depending on the state), you may be required to register your business as a "Foreign Entity" in that state.
Coach Tip

Use an automated tax service like TaxJar or Avalara if your practice grows beyond 50 clients. It automates the "Nexus" tracking so you don't have to worry about missing a state-specific filing requirement.

Check Your Understanding

1. If you are located in Texas and your client is in London, where is the "practice" legally considered to be taking place?

The practice is legally considered to be taking place in London (the location of the client). You must ensure your services comply with UK regulations regarding health coaching and education.

2. Why is a standard 911 call insufficient for a remote client crisis?

Emergency dispatch (911) is geolocated to the caller's physical location. To help a remote client, you need the direct number for the emergency services in their specific town or city.

3. What is the minimum video quality standard for conducting 'Overdrive Regulation' (O) sessions?

The minimum standard is 720p HD. This is necessary to accurately observe the client's physical state, respiratory rate, and potential signs of distress during nervous system work.

4. What is 'Nexus' in the context of a location-independent practice?

Nexus refers to the sufficient connection (physical or economic) between your business and a state that triggers tax obligations (income or sales tax) in that state.

Key Takeaways

  • Client Location Rules: Always assume the laws of the client's state/country apply to the session.
  • Digital Handshake: Verify ID and physical location at the start of every session to maintain legal records.
  • Tech is Compliance: High-quality audio/video is not just for "vibe"‚Äîit's a requirement for safe nervous system regulation.
  • Emergency Preparedness: Keep local emergency numbers for every client on file; don't rely on 911.
  • Financial Nexus: Monitor your growth in different states to ensure you meet tax and registration obligations.
