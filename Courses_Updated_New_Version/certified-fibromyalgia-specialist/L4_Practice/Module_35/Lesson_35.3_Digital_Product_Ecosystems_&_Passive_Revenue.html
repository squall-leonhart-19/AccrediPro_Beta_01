Designing for Fibro-Fog & Sensory Sensitivity

A premium digital product for fibromyalgia must be neuro-accessible. If your course is too "busy" or overwhelming, a client in a flare will simply close the tab and never return. This leads to high refund rates and poor outcomes.

Design Requirements for Fibromyalgia

1. High Contrast, Low Glare: Use soft backgrounds (like cream or light grey) rather than stark white. Use large, sans-serif fonts (minimum 16pt-18pt).
2. Micro-Learning: Keep videos under 7 minutes. "Fibro-fog" limits the duration of intense focus.
3. Multi-Modal: Always provide a transcript for videos and an audio-only version. Some days, looking at a screen causes migraines; other days, reading is too taxing.

Coach Tip: The "Flare-Friendly" Mode

Inside your digital course, include a "Flare Mode" button or section. This should contain 2-minute "emergency" audios or simple one-page graphics for when the client is too ill to consume regular content.

Automating the R.E.S.T.O.R.E. Intake Process

Passive revenue is only truly passive if the sales and onboarding processes are automated. By using a Lead-to-Client Pipeline, you can filter for high-quality coaching clients while selling digital products to everyone else.

The Automated Flow:

  1. The Hook: A social media post or ad offers a free "Fibromyalgia Trigger Map."
  2. The Intake: The client fills out a digital assessment (Root Assessment - R).
  3. The Logic: Based on their score, the system automatically tags them:
    • High Score (Severe): Redirects to a "Book a Call" page for high-ticket coaching.
    • Moderate Score: Offers the "Sleep Optimization Masterclass" at a discount.
    • Low Score: Offers the "Energy Pacing Digital Tracker."
Coach Tip: Tech Stack Simplicity

Don't get bogged down in "tech-paralysis." Start with simple tools like Kajabi, Podia, or Thrivecart. These platforms handle the hosting, payments, and automation in one place, which is vital for protecting your own energy levels.
