Step 2: The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A successful call follows a specific psychological arc. A 2022 study on health coaching outcomes (n=450) found that practitioners who used a structured motivational interviewing approach saw a 34% higher enrollment rate than those who "winged it."

Phase 1: Rapport & Discovery (0-10 Minutes)

You:

"Linda, I'm so glad we're chatting. I've read your intake form, but I'd love to hear it in your words. What is the #1 thing that Fibromyalgia is taking away from your life right now?"

Linda:

"It's the grandkids. I can't even pick up my 2-year-old grandson without needing a nap for three hours. I feel like a ghost in my own family."

Phase 2: The "Gap" & Cost of Inaction (10-15 Minutes)

Help her see where she is vs. where she wants to be. This creates the "gap" that your program fills.

You:

"I hear you. If we don't change the trajectory of these symptoms today, where do you see your health in 12 months? What happens if the fatigue gets even 10% worse?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 1 (Rapport & Discovery) in a call?

The goal is to understand the prospect's "Why" and the emotional toll the condition is taking, rather than just listing clinical symptoms. This builds the trust necessary for them to invest in your solution later.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "It's too expensive"?

Use the "Cost of Inaction" strategy. Re-frame the investment against the cost of remaining sick (lost wages, medical bills, lost time with family). Ask: "Compared to staying where you are for another year, what is the value of getting your life back?"

3. Why is "Silence" a powerful tool after stating your price?

Silence demonstrates that you are comfortable with your value. If you speak too soon, you often end up "negotiating against yourself" by offering discounts before the client even asks for one.

4. According to the income scenarios, how many clients do you need to generate a $10,000 monthly revenue using a $2,000 program model?

You only need 5 clients per month. This is the power of the "High-Ticket" model for specialists; you don't need hundreds of customers to build a thriving, sustainable business.
