Check Your Understanding

1. Why do many clients resist starting legacy work?

Most resistance stems from the "ordinariness" mythâ€”the belief that one's life wasn't extraordinary enough to be documented. As a doula, your job is to reframe legacy as a transmission of love and values rather than a list of achievements.

2. What is the "Heart-to-Paper" method?

It is a technique where the doula uses sensory-based questions (smells, sounds, feelings) to bypass the logical brain and tap into emotional memories that form the core of a meaningful legacy.

3. How should you handle a client who becomes emotional during an interview?

Pause the recording/interview. Offer a tissue or a glass of water. Validate the emotion: "It's okay to feel this; it shows how much this memory matters." Only continue when the client gives a verbal or non-verbal cue of readiness.

4. True or False: A legacy project must be a written book.

False. Legacy can take many forms: video messages, audio recordings, photo albums with captions, recipe collections, or even "ethical wills" (documents of values).

Key Takeaways

  • Reframing is Key: Your primary role is helping the client see the "extraordinary" in their "ordinary" life.
  • Tone Matters: A warm, slow, and steady voice creates the safety needed for deep reflection.
  • Start Small: One vivid memory is better than ten vague years. Use sensory prompts to spark recall.
  • Professional Legitimacy: Using structured frameworks (like the Ethical Will) builds trust and justifies your professional fees.
  • Witnessing: Sometimes the most important part of legacy work isn't the final product, but the fact that someone listened.
