1. Welcome to the Lab: Your Client Acquisition Blueprint

As biblical counseling coaches, our ultimate goal is to steward the gifts God has given us to serve others. To do that effectively, we must also embrace the practicalities of building a sustainable practice. The discovery call is often the first genuine interaction a potential client has with you beyond your marketing. It's where connection happens, trust begins to form, and the seed of transformation is planted. This isn't about "selling"; it's about serving through clear communication and discerning fit.

The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is more than just a free consultation. It's a structured conversation designed to achieve several critical objectives:

  • Build Rapport: Establish a warm, empathetic connection, demonstrating that you genuinely care.
  • Uncover Needs: Deeply listen to understand their spiritual, emotional, and practical challenges.
  • Assess Fit: Determine if their needs align with your expertise and if your program is truly the best solution.
  • Educate: Gently introduce your unique, biblically-rooted approach and how it differs from what they've tried.
  • Present Solution: Confidently articulate the value of your program and its potential for transformation.
  • Invite to Partnership: Guide them toward making a decision to move forward, or offer alternative resources if not a fit.
Coach's Insight

Remember, the discovery call is a two-way street. You're not just being interviewed; you're also interviewing them. You want to ensure that this is a client you can genuinely serve well and who is ready to commit to the process. Pray before every call, asking the Holy Spirit to guide your words and discernment.

3. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script: A Step-by-Step Guide

This script provides a framework. Let the Holy Spirit lead, and always prioritize genuine connection and active listening over rigidly following lines. Adapt it to your natural style!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's truly a pleasure to connect. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my biblical counseling coaching?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] shared about my work. She's a dear sister in Christ. So Sarah, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on in your heart and life that prompted you to seek support?"
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. My goal for our time today is simply to listen, understand your situation, and explore if biblical counseling coaching might be a good fit for you. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed by anxiety and guilt. Can you tell me more about how that manifests in your daily life? When did these feelings start to become particularly heavy?"
YOU:
"And how does this impact your faith or your relationship with God? You mentioned feeling disconnected and not 'spiritual enough.' Could you elaborate on that?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year, emotionally and spiritually? And if you could wave a magic wand and truly experience God's peace and freedom from this worry and guilt, what would your life look like? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for being so open and vulnerable. Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: [summarize her pain points, fears, and desires, using her own words, e.g., 'You're carrying a heavy burden of anxiety and guilt, feeling distant from God, and longing for His peace and confidence in your faith.'] Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use in biblical counseling coaching is distinct from traditional methods you might have tried. We don't just focus on coping strategies; we go to the very root of these struggles by examining them through the lens of God's Word. We'll identify specific thoughts, beliefs, or patterns that are keeping you stuck, and then prayerfully apply biblical truth to bring lasting transformation and freedom in Christ. My clients often experience significant shifts in their peace and spiritual clarity within the first few weeks as we begin to align their hearts with God's truth."
YOU:
"My 12-week 'Peace in Christ' program is designed specifically for women like you who are seeking to overcome anxiety and guilt through a deeper relationship with God and practical application of His Word. It includes weekly 60-minute coaching sessions, personalized action steps, and ongoing prayer support. Would you like me to share more about how this program works?"
Phase 4: The Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about my 'Peace in Christ' program, does this sound like the kind of biblically-grounded support you've been looking for to find the peace and freedom you desire?"
YOU:
"Wonderful. I believe this program could truly help you experience the transformation you're seeking. Let me explain the investment and how we can get started."
Coach's Insight

Practice summarizing the client's pain points and desires back to them. This demonstrates deep listening and ensures they feel truly heard. When they affirm your summary, you've built a powerful bridge to presenting your solution.

4. Confidently Presenting Your Program and Pricing

This is often where imposter syndrome can creep in for new coaches. Remember, you are offering immense value ‚Äì not just your time, but your expertise, your heart for ministry, and the transformative power of God's Word. Your pricing reflects the depth of your training and the life-changing results you help clients achieve.

When discussing pricing, always:

  1. State the value first: Reiterate the benefits and transformation your program offers.
  2. Be direct and clear: State your price with confidence, without hesitation or apology.
  3. Offer payment options: Make it accessible if possible (e.g., pay-in-full discount, payment plan).
  4. Pause and listen: Allow them to process and respond. Resist the urge to fill the silence.

Grace's Pricing Script Example:

YOU:
"Sarah, my 'Peace in Christ' 12-week program is an intensive, personalized journey designed to help you deeply root yourself in God's truth and experience lasting freedom from anxiety and guilt. Clients who commit to this process often describe it as life-changing, finding a peace they've long yearned for."
YOU:
"The investment for the full 12-week program, including our weekly 60-minute sessions, personalized resources, and ongoing prayer support, is $2,500. Many clients choose our convenient payment plan of three monthly payments of $850. We can discuss which option works best for you. How does that sound?"
YOU:
(Pause. Allow Sarah to respond.)

5. Mastering Objections: Turning "No" into "Not Yet"

Objections are rarely a hard "no" to you or your value; they are often expressions of fear, uncertainty, or a need for more information. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and a desire to serve, always pointing back to the core transformation you offer.

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"The investment is too high / I can't afford it right now."
"I completely understand that this is a significant investment, and it's wise to consider it carefully. Many of my clients initially feel this way. Can I ask, what value do you place on finally finding peace and freedom from this anxiety and guilt? What would it be worth to you to truly feel connected to God again? I often find that the cost of staying stuck, emotionally and spiritually, far outweighs the investment in transformation. We can also discuss payment plan options if that helps."
"I need to think about it / Discuss it with my spouse."
"I completely understand. This is an important decision, especially when it involves your spiritual well-being and finances. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else about the program? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make a prayerful decision. If it's about your spouse, I'm happy to provide you with materials or even a brief follow-up call with both of you to address any questions."
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing seems to work."
"Sarah, I hear your frustration, and it's completely valid to feel that way after trying various approaches. What makes biblical counseling coaching different is our unwavering foundation in God's Word, which is 'living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword' (Hebrews 4:12). We're not just offering coping mechanisms; we're seeking God's truth to transform your heart and mind at the deepest level. My clients find this approach brings a lasting peace that other methods couldn't provide because it addresses the spiritual roots of their struggles. Are you open to exploring God's solution for your anxiety?"
"I'm not sure I have the time for this right now."
"I appreciate your honesty, and I know life can be incredibly busy. Many of my clients come to me feeling overwhelmed and strapped for time. However, they often find that investing this time now actually creates more peace and capacity in the long run. Imagine the mental energy you'd free up if you weren't constantly battling anxiety and guilt. Our sessions are structured to be impactful, and the action steps are practical and integrate into your daily life. What would a life with less anxiety mean for your time and energy?"
Coach's Insight

When handling objections, lean into curiosity, not defensiveness. Ask open-ended questions like, "What specifically concerns you?" or "What would need to happen for you to feel ready?" This invites further dialogue and helps you uncover the true root of their hesitation.

6. Call-to-Action Practice: Guiding Them to the Next Step

After addressing any objections, it's crucial to confidently guide the client to the next step. This demonstrates leadership and clarity. Practice these closing lines until they feel natural and empowering:

  • "Sarah, based on our conversation today, I truly believe my 'Peace in Christ' program is exactly what you need to find the freedom and peace you're longing for. Are you ready to take this step of faith and begin your journey?"
  • "Considering everything we've discussed, does moving forward with the 'Peace in Christ' program feel like the right next step for you?"
  • "If you're ready to commit to this transformative journey, we can get you enrolled today. How would you like to proceed?"
  • "I'm excited about the possibility of working with you. Shall we get your first session scheduled?"
Coach's Insight

A clear call to action is a gift to your potential client. It removes ambiguity and helps them make a decision. Don't be afraid to ask for the enrollment; it shows you believe in your program's ability to help them.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem & Vision" phase of a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to deeply listen to the prospective client, understand their challenges (spiritual, emotional, practical), and help them articulate their desired future state or vision for transformation. This helps assess fit and builds a foundation for presenting your solution.

2. When a prospective client says, "I need to think about it," what is a biblically-informed and effective coaching response?

A biblically-informed response would be empathetic and curious, inviting deeper conversation. For example: "I completely understand that this is an important decision, and it's wise to take it to the Lord in prayer. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically are you praying about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else about the program that you're seeking clarity on?" This helps uncover the true objection and allows you to address it with truth and grace.

3. Why is it important to state your program's value before presenting its price?

Stating the value first frames the price as an investment in a desirable outcome rather than just a cost. It helps the client connect the financial commitment to the significant transformation and benefits they will receive, making the price seem more reasonable and justifiable in their minds.

4. According to Grace, how does biblical counseling coaching differ from traditional therapy in addressing clients' core struggles?

Grace emphasizes that biblical counseling coaching goes beyond coping mechanisms to address the root causes of struggles by examining them through the lens of God's Word. It focuses on identifying specific thoughts, beliefs, or patterns that are misaligned with biblical truth and applying God's Word for lasting transformation and freedom in Christ, addressing the spiritual core often untouched by traditional methods.
