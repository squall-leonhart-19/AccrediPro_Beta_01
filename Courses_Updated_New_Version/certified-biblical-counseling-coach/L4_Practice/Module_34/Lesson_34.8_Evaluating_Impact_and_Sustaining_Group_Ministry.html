2. The Group Program Discovery Call

A discovery call for a group program differs slightly from a one-on-one call. While you still build rapport and understand needs, you're also assessing if a group dynamic is the right fit and highlighting the unique benefits of community. Aim for 30-45 minutes.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Eleanor

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Eleanor, it's so wonderful to connect with you today! Thank you for taking the time. I'm Grace. I understand our mutual friend, Sarah, suggested you reach out. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"That's lovely to hear. Sarah spoke so highly of you. So, Eleanor, what prompted you to consider a group program like 'Journey to Renewed Purpose' at this particular time in your life?"
Phase 2: Understand Her Needs & Group Fit 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling lost and a bit stagnant spiritually. Can you share a little more about what that feels like day-to-day? How is this impacting your joy and sense of purpose?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to address these feelings? And what specifically are you hoping a supportive group setting might offer that perhaps individual coaching or self-study hasn't?"
YOU:
"Imagine six months from now, having gone through a journey of renewed purpose. What would your life look like? What would be different?"
Coach Tip

In this phase, your primary role is to listen deeply. Resist the urge to offer solutions too early. Eleanor needs to feel truly heard and understood. Use open-ended questions and reflective listening to draw out her deepest desires and concerns. The more she talks, the more you understand, and the more she feels connected.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on everything you've so thoughtfully shared, what I'm hearing is a deep desire for renewed purpose, spiritual vitality, and a supportive community to navigate this new chapter. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"Our 'Journey to Renewed Purpose' group program is designed exactly for women like you. It's an 8-week Christ-centered journey where we explore biblical principles for identity, purpose, and community. We meet weekly for live coaching, engage in guided reflections, and have a private online space for ongoing support and prayer. The beauty of a group is the shared wisdom, accountability, and genuine sisterhood that forms. Most participants report feeling significantly more connected and purposeful within the first few weeks."
YOU:
"We limit our groups to 8-10 women to ensure personalized attention and deep connection. You'd be walking alongside women who are also seeking God's path for their lives, sharing insights, and praying for one another."

3. Handling Common Objections

Objections are natural. They are often simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence, reiterating the value of your program and how it specifically addresses their concerns.

Grace's Insight

Remember, an objection is rarely a "no." It's often a "tell me more," or "I need reassurance." Your role is to listen, empathize, and confidently clarify how your program addresses their underlying concern.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

Eleanor: "I'm not sure a group is right for me; I've always preferred one-on-one attention."

YOU: "That's a very common feeling, Eleanor, and I appreciate your honesty. Many women initially feel that way. What I've found, and what our participants consistently share, is that while individual attention is valuable, there's a unique power in a group setting that 1:1 can't always replicate. Imagine having 7-9 other women who genuinely understand what you're going through, who can offer diverse perspectives rooted in their own faith journeys, and who are there to pray for you and hold you accountable. It's like having a dedicated spiritual community rallying around you. We ensure personalized attention by keeping groups small, and many find the collective wisdom and encouragement incredibly enriching. Does that shift your perspective a bit on what a group could offer?"

Eleanor: "The investment feels significant, especially on a fixed income."

YOU: "I completely understand, Eleanor. Investing in yourself, especially after a major life transition, requires thoughtful consideration. When we talk about investment, we're not just talking about money, but also your time, energy, and commitment to yourself and your walk with God. Consider the cost of NOT addressing these feelings of being lost or stagnant ‚Äì what is the spiritual, emotional, and even relational cost of staying where you are? This program is designed to be a profound catalyst for change, helping you rediscover purpose and joy. Many of my clients find the transformation invaluable. We also have flexible payment options to make it more accessible. Would exploring those options be helpful?"

Coach Tip

When handling financial objections, reframe the cost as an investment in transformation. Connect it back to their stated desires and the pain points they want to resolve. Be prepared with payment plan details to offer flexibility.

Eleanor: "I need to talk to my children about this before I commit."

YOU: "Of course, Eleanor, that makes perfect sense. It's wise to involve your family in significant decisions. What specifically would you like to discuss with them? Is it the time commitment, the financial aspect, or perhaps just sharing your desire for this new journey? I can provide you with a summary of the program details and benefits that you could share with them, so they understand the positive impact this could have on you. How does that sound?"

4. Presenting Your Investment Confidently

When it's time to discuss the financial investment, do so with unwavering confidence. Your pricing reflects the immense value, transformation, and expertise you provide. Never apologize for your rates.

Structuring Your Pricing Presentation

  • State the Investment Clearly: "The investment for the 8-week 'Journey to Renewed Purpose' group program is $997."
  • Reiterate Value: "This investment includes 8 live weekly group coaching sessions, a comprehensive workbook with guided reflections, access to our private online community for daily support and prayer, and personalized feedback from me throughout the program. It's a holistic experience designed to provide you with clarity, connection, and a renewed sense of God's purpose."
  • Offer Payment Options: "To make this accessible, we offer a full payment option, or two monthly payments of $525." (Always quote the higher price first, then the payment plan).
  • Pause and Listen: After stating the investment and options, pause. Allow them to process and respond. Resist the urge to fill the silence.
Coach Tip

Your energy around your pricing is contagious. If you hesitate or sound unsure, your prospect will pick up on it. Practice stating your fees out loud until it feels completely natural and confident. Believe in the value you offer.

5. Closing the Enrollment

The close is not about being pushy; it's about guiding your prospect to the next logical step if the program is a good fit. It's about empowering them to make a decision that serves their highest good.

Practice Your Closing Lines

  1. "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed today, and your desire for renewed purpose and community, does this 'Journey to Renewed Purpose' program sound like the supportive, Christ-centered experience you've been looking for?" (Pause for her answer).
  2. If yes: "Wonderful! I truly believe this group is a perfect fit for you. We have just a few spots remaining. Are you ready to secure your place and begin this journey with us?"
  3. If she still has hesitations: "I completely understand. If this isn't the right time, that's perfectly okay. But if it is, I want to ensure you have all the support you need. What feels like the most natural next step for you right now?"
  4. "We start on [Date], and I'd love to have you join us. I can send you the enrollment link right now, and we can get you set up for this transformative experience."
Coach Tip

A confident close comes from a place of service. You're not selling; you're offering a solution rooted in biblical truth that you know can transform lives. Trust in the Holy Spirit's leading and the value of your program.

6. Income Potential with Group Programs

One of the most exciting aspects of group programs is their scalability. You can impact more lives and generate significant income without proportionally increasing your time investment, unlike 1:1 coaching.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference in a discovery call for a group program compared to a one-on-one coaching call?

While both require rapport building and understanding client needs, a group program discovery call also focuses on assessing if a group dynamic is a good fit for the prospect and highlighting the unique benefits of community, shared wisdom, and collective accountability.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the investment. What is an effective way to reframe this objection?

Reframe the cost as an investment in transformation. Connect it back to her stated desires (renewed purpose, joy, spiritual vitality) and the pain points she wishes to resolve. Emphasize the long-term benefits and the potential cost of *not* addressing her current challenges.

3. When presenting the investment for your group program, what is a best practice regarding payment options?

Always state the full payment option first, then offer payment plans. For example, "The investment is $997, or two monthly payments of $525." This anchors the higher value first.

4. What is the underlying principle behind confidently closing a group program enrollment?

The underlying principle is service. You are not "selling" but rather offering a valuable, Christ-centered solution that you genuinely believe will benefit the client. Your confidence stems from your conviction in the program's ability to deliver transformation and impact lives for God's glory.

Key Takeaways
  • Strategic Discovery Calls: Tailor your discovery calls for group programs to assess client fit within a community dynamic, highlighting shared benefits alongside individual transformation.
  • Empathetic Objection Handling: View objections as opportunities to clarify and reinforce value, connecting back to the client's core needs and desires.
  • Confident Investment Presentation: State your group program's investment clearly and confidently, anchoring the full price before offering flexible payment options.
  • Service-Oriented Closing: Approach the close as a natural next step in guiding clients towards a transformative experience, trusting in the value of your Christ-centered offering.
  • Scalable Impact & Income: Group programs provide a powerful avenue to significantly scale your impact and income, allowing you to serve more women effectively and build a robust practice.
