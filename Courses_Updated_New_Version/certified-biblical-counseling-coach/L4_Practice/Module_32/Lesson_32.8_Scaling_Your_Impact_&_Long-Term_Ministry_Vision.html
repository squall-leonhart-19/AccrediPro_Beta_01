The Art of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is more than just a sales pitch; it's a sacred space where you discern if you are the right guide for a potential client's journey. It's an opportunity to listen deeply, ask insightful questions, and present your Christ-centered solution with clarity and compassion. My goal for you is to approach these calls not with anxiety, but with an anticipation of serving. Remember, you are offering transformation, not just a service.

Coach Tip

Approach every discovery call with a posture of servant leadership. Your primary goal is to understand if you can genuinely help this individual, not just to "close a sale." This mindset shift reduces pressure and allows for authentic connection, which is far more powerful in converting prospects into clients.

Structuring Your 30-Minute Call

A structured approach ensures you cover all critical points while remaining flexible enough to truly listen. Hereâ€™s a breakdown of how to guide Sarah through a productive discovery call, adapted from a proven framework used by many successful coaches.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Sarah

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's so good to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to know how you heard about my coaching. I believe it was through our friend, Emily?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! Emily has truly experienced transformation. So Sarah, tell me, in your own words, what specifically prompted you to reach out for biblical counseling coaching now? What's on your heart?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Pain Points 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling 'stuck' and overwhelmed. Can you elaborate on what that looks like in your daily life? When do you notice the anxiety most, and how does it impact your family or your sense of purpose?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, even a little bit, and what truly felt ineffective or left you feeling more discouraged?"
YOU:
"Imagine fast-forwarding six months from now, and nothing has changed. How would that impact you and your family? Conversely, if we could wave a magic wand and you achieved the spiritual and emotional breakthrough you desire, what would your life look like? What would you be doing, feeling, and believing?"
Phase 3: Position Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is a deep desire for God-centered peace and practical tools to overcome anxiety and guilt, so you can live out your faith with confidence and joy. You're seeking a lasting breakthrough, not just a temporary fix. Does that resonate with where you are?"
YOU:
"My approach to biblical counseling coaching is designed specifically for women like you. It's different from traditional counseling because it's deeply rooted in God's Word, empowering you with spiritual truths and practical strategies for lasting change. We work together over a structured 12-week program, focusing on identifying the root causes of your spiritual and emotional struggles, and equipping you with the WORD Framework™ to apply God's wisdom to every area of your life. My clients often report significant shifts in their peace and confidence within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: Confident Closing 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Sarah, does this Christ-centered, practical approach sound like the kind of support and guidance you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me share a bit more about how the program works, what's included, and how we can get started on this transformative journey together."
Coach Tip

During Phase 2, actively listen for emotionally charged words. Phrases like "overwhelmed," "guilty," "disconnected," or "longing" reveal deeper pain points. Mirror these words back to the client in Phase 3 to show empathy and demonstrate that you truly understand their struggle.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing

Confidently presenting your program and its investment is a crucial skill. You are not just selling sessions; you are offering a structured path to biblical transformation. Your pricing should reflect the immense value, expertise, and lasting change you provide.

Coach Tip

Always communicate your pricing with a "value-first" approach. Before stating the number, reiterate the benefits and transformation the client will experience. This frames the investment as a pathway to their desired outcome, rather than just a cost.

Call-to-Action Practice

The close is where you invite the client to take the next step. It should be clear, confident, and leave no room for ambiguity. Practice these lines out loud until they feel natural and empowering.

  1. "Sarah, based on everything you've shared and the transformation we've discussed, I truly believe this program is exactly what you need to find the peace and confidence you're longing for. Are you ready to take this step and begin your journey?"
  2. "Considering your desire for lasting spiritual and emotional breakthrough, and my proven process, does it make sense to get you started today?"
  3. "If you're feeling a 'yes' in your spirit about moving forward, the next step is simple: we'll get your enrollment complete, schedule your first session, and begin creating your personalized plan. How does that sound?"
  4. "I'm genuinely excited about the possibility of walking alongside you, Sarah. If this feels like the right path for you, let's secure your spot and begin building that foundation of peace. What questions do you have for me about moving forward?"
Coach Tip

After asking a closing question, stop talking. The silence can feel uncomfortable, but it's crucial to give the client space to process and respond. Resist the urge to fill the void; let them be the first to speak.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Uncover Needs & Pain Points" phase in a discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply understand the prospective client's current struggles, their emotional impact, and what they have already attempted (and failed) to resolve, creating a clear picture of their desired transformation.

2. Sarah expresses concern about the "significant investment." How should you frame your response to address this objection effectively?

Frame the response by acknowledging her concern about past ineffective spending, then pivot to the unique, lasting value of your biblically-rooted, personalized program. Emphasize that it's an investment in transformation and peace, not just an expense, and connect it to her stated desire for lasting change.

3. When presenting your coaching program, what key elements should you always include to ensure clarity and value?

Always include the duration of the program, the number and length of 1:1 sessions, the specific framework or methodology used (e.g., WORD Framework™), the types of support included (e.g., email, resources), and a clear statement of the investment with any payment options.

4. After asking a closing question, what is a critical non-verbal technique to employ, and why is it effective?

A critical non-verbal technique is to remain silent after asking a closing question. This is effective because it creates space for the client to process and articulate their decision, rather than being influenced or interrupted by the coach. It demonstrates confidence and respect for their decision-making process.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a structured conversation, not a sales pitch, aimed at discerning fit and presenting transformative value.
  • Effective calls involve deep listening, empathetic questioning, and clearly articulating your Christ-centered solution.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, addressed with grace, clarity, and conviction.
  • Present your program's investment with a "value-first" approach, highlighting the lasting transformation over the monetary cost.
  • Confident closing techniques invite the client to take the next step, empowering them to begin their biblical counseling coaching journey.
