The Art of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is your opportunity to serve by listening, discerning, and offering hope. It's not about being pushy, but about presenting a clear path forward, rooted in biblical truth. Here's a structured approach for your 30-45 minute call with Sarah:

Your 30-45 Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5-7 min
YOU:
"Hello Sarah, it's so wonderful to connect with you! Thank you for taking the time today. I truly appreciate it. I saw that [friend's name] referred you â€“ she's a dear sister. Before we dive into what brought you here, how are you doing today?"
YOU:
"That's so good to hear. I'm really looking forward to our conversation. My goal for our time is simply to understand what you're hoping for, and to see if my Biblical Counseling Coaching approach might be the right fit to help you get there. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understand Her Needs (The "Pain & Vision" Phase) 15-20 min
YOU:
"Sarah, in your inquiry, you mentioned feeling a loss of purpose and struggling with anxiety since your children left home. Could you tell me more about what that's been like for you? What does a typical day look like when you're feeling this way?"
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing so openly. It sounds like a deeply challenging season. You also mentioned feeling disconnected from God. How has this impacted your faith walk and your sense of His presence?"
YOU:
"If we were to fast forward six months, and you've found the peace and purpose you're longing for, what would your life look like? What would be different?"
YOU:
"And why is it important to address this now, Sarah? What's at stake if nothing changes?"
Phase 3: Present Your Christ-Centered Solution 10-12 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for sharing your heart so vulnerably. What I'm hearing is a deep desire to rediscover your identity and purpose in Christ, to overcome this anxiety, and to reconnect with God's plan for your life in this new season. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I truly believe Biblical Counseling Coaching can offer the unique support you're seeking. My approach, rooted in God's Word, isn't about quick fixes, but about transformational growth. We'd work together over a [e.g., 12-week] period, diving deep into Scripture to understand your identity in Christ, renew your mind, and equip you with practical, biblical strategies to navigate this season. Many women I've worked with, who felt just like you, have found renewed joy, clarity, and a deeper walk with the Lord."
YOU:
"We would explore specific passages, develop personalized action plans, and focus on practical application of biblical truths to your daily life. My role is to come alongside you as a guide, providing encouragement, accountability, and a safe space to grow in grace. How does that sound to you?"

Coach Tip

When presenting your solution, avoid jargon. Speak in terms of outcomes and benefits that directly address their stated pain points and align with their desired future. For Sarah, this means emphasizing peace, purpose, renewed faith, and practical biblical guidance. Share a brief, relevant client story (anonymously, of course!) if it feels natural and encouraging.

Confidently Handling Objections

It's natural for prospects to have questions or concerns. View objections as opportunities for further conversation, not as rejections. They often indicate a desire for more information or reassurance. Hereâ€™s how you might respond to Sarah's potential objections:

"This sounds wonderful, but I need to talk to my husband about the investment."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and discussing it with your husband is wise. To help you with that conversation, what specific questions do you anticipate he might have? I can provide you with some details to share, or perhaps we could schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you if that would be helpful? My goal is for you both to feel fully confident in this decision."
"I've tried so many things â€“ counseling, Bible studies â€“ and nothing seems to stick. How is this different?"
"That's a very fair question, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've been diligently seeking solutions, and that shows a deep commitment to your growth. What makes Biblical Counseling Coaching unique is its intentional focus on application and transformation. We don't just study the Word; we actively work to integrate it into your daily life, addressing specific thought patterns, behaviors, and spiritual disciplines. It's a partnership, with me guiding you through personalized, actionable steps, rooted in the sufficiency of Scripture, for lasting change. It's less about information, and more about formation."
"I'm not sure I have the time right now. My schedule is already so full."
"I hear you, Sarah. It often feels like there's never enough time, especially when you're navigating a new season. And honestly, this program *does* require a commitment of your time and heart. However, consider what not addressing these feelings is costing you in terms of peace, joy, and spiritual vitality. Many of my clients find that by investing this time upfront, they actually gain more clarity and efficiency in other areas of their lives. We'll work together to make this manageable within your schedule, focusing on progress over perfection. What would it feel like to reclaim your peace and purpose?"

Scripting Your Pricing Presentation

After you've addressed her concerns and she's expressed enthusiasm:

Presenting the Program & Investment 2-3 min
YOU:
"Sarah, I'm so excited about the possibility of working with you and seeing God move in your life. My primary coaching program is a 12-week transformational journey. It includes [mention key components, e.g., 6 one-on-one sessions, personalized action plans, scripture study guides, unlimited email support between sessions]. The investment for this program is $2,997. I also offer a flexible payment plan, breaking it into three monthly payments of $1,050, if that makes it more accessible for you."
YOU:
"I believe this is a profound investment in your spiritual well-being, your peace of mind, and your ability to step into God's purpose for this new chapter. It's an investment in the woman God is calling you to be. How does that sound?"

Pause here. Allow her to process. Her immediate reaction will guide your next steps.

Coach Tip

When stating your price, do so clearly and confidently. Avoid hedging or apologizing. Then, stop talking. The first person to speak often loses. Let the prospect absorb the information and respond. This demonstrates confidence in your value.

Practice Your Call to Action

The close is not about pressuring, but about guiding your prospect to a decision that serves their highest good. Your call to action should be clear, simple, and empowering.

Empowering Closing Lines

If she needs more time to think or discuss with her husband:

Gracious Follow-Up 1-2 min
YOU:
"Absolutely, take the time you need. What would be a good next step for us? Would you like me to follow up with you by , or would you prefer to reach out once you've had a chance to discuss it? I'm here to support you."

Coach Tip

Always end with a clear next step, whether it's enrolling, scheduling a follow-up, or sending more information. This maintains momentum and shows professionalism. Don't leave the call open-ended without a defined action.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a sacred conversation, focused on listening, discerning, and offering Christ-centered hope.
  • Thoroughly prepare for each call by reviewing prospect information to build immediate rapport and tailor your approach.
  • Master the art of asking powerful questions to uncover deep pain points and inspire a vision for transformation.
  • Present your Biblical Counseling Coaching solution with confidence and clarity, focusing on the unique, lasting benefits and scriptural foundation.
  • View objections as opportunities to serve with further clarity and reassurance, not as personal rejections.
  • Always conclude with a clear, empowering call-to-action and a defined next step, maintaining professionalism and momentum.
