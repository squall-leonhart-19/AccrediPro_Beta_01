Welcome to Your Practice Lab!

As biblical counseling coaches, our calling is to guide clients toward God's wisdom in every area of their lives. This Practice Lab is designed to give you a hands-on experience in facilitating spiritual discernment. You'll engage with a client who is seeking clarity and peace amidst life's demands. Remember, practice is where confidence is built. Lean into this opportunity to hone your skills!

Learning Objectives
  • Accurately identify a client's core spiritual discernment needs from a brief intake.
  • Practice initiating a coaching conversation using empathetic, Christ-centered language.
  • Apply biblical principles of wisdom and guidance within a coaching dialogue.
  • Formulate discerning questions that encourage deeper spiritual reflection in the client.
  • Guide a client towards actionable steps rooted in biblical truth and personal responsibility.
  • Identify and avoid common pitfalls when coaching clients through spiritual decision-making.

Meet Your Client

Eleanor, 52

Former Elementary School Teacher, recently retired. Married, two adult children. Atlanta, GA

Chief Complaint
"I'm retired now, and I feel lost. I thought I'd have clarity on my 'next chapter,' but I just feel overwhelmed and unsure of God's will. I pray, read my Bible, but still feel stuck."
Duration
6 months since retirement; a general feeling of spiritual stagnation for the past year.
What She's Tried
More devotional time, volunteering at church (feels like busywork, not calling), talking to friends (conflicting advice), attending a "purpose workshop" (didn't resonate).
Her Goal
"I want to understand God's unique calling for me in this season. I want peace and direction."
Coach Tip

Before you even begin the conversation, take a moment to pray for your client. Ask the Holy Spirit to guide your words and reveal the root issues behind their presenting concerns. This sets a truly Christ-centered tone for your session.

The Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud, embodying Grace's warm and discerning approach.

Your Coaching Script

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for being so open and sharing what's truly on your heart. It takes real courage to step into a new season and admit you're feeling a bit adrift. What you're experiencing is incredibly common, especially after such a significant life transition like retirement. Many women in your shoes find themselves asking these very deep questions about purpose and direction."
ELEANOR:
"It's true. I feel like I should have it all figured out by now, but I just don't. And I feel guilty, like I'm failing God somehow."
YOU:
"That feeling of guilt is heavy, isn't it? But Eleanor, I want to reassure you: God's love for you isn't dependent on your 'figuring it out.' In fact, this season of seeking can be a profound opportunity for deeper intimacy with Him. The journey of discernment is less about finding a single, perfect answer and more about learning to walk closely with Him in trust. Does that resonate with you?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, it does. I've been so focused on the 'answer' that I think I've missed the process."
YOU:
"Absolutely. And that's where we can begin. Based on what you've shared, I sense a desire to reconnect with God's peace, clarify your unique gifts and passions, and then explore how those might align with His purposes for this new season. How does that sound as a starting point for our work together?"
ELEANOR:
"That sounds wonderful, Grace. Exactly what I need."
YOU:
"Excellent. Let's start by exploring what 'peace' truly means to you in this context, and where you're currently experiencing a lack of it. We'll draw from Scripture as our guide, because God's Word is the ultimate source of wisdom for discernment. Is there a particular verse or passage that comes to mind when you think about seeking God's will?"
Coach Tip

Notice how Grace validates Eleanor's feelings ("That feeling of guilt is heavy, isn't it?") before reframing the situation with biblical truth. This validation-then-reframe approach builds trust and gently shifts the client's perspective towards hope.

Use your phone's voice recorder. Listen back to how you sound. Are you empathetic? Do you sound confident? Is your pace appropriate? This self-assessment is invaluable for refinement.

Imagine Eleanor just responded, "Well, the verse 'Be still, and know that I am God' (Psalm 46:10) comes to mind, but I struggle to actually be still." What would you say next to deepen the conversation and move towards practical application of discernment?

Coach Tip

Always bring the conversation back to biblical truth and practical application. When Eleanor mentions Psalm 46:10, a good next step might be to explore *what makes it hard for her to be still* and *how God's character (knowing He is God) connects to her struggle*.

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

As you practice, be mindful of these common missteps. Avoiding them will elevate your coaching impact.

Avoid These Mistakes

  • Offering Quick Fixes or Prescriptive Advice: For example, "You just need to pray more" or "You should volunteer with X ministry." Biblical counseling coaching is about guiding, not dictating.
  • Over-Spiritualizing Every Problem: While spiritual, not every struggle is purely a spiritual battle. Acknowledge practical realities alongside spiritual truths.
  • Failing to Listen Actively: Interrupting, formulating your next question instead of truly hearing the client's heart, or missing emotional cues.
  • Neglecting Scripture or Misapplying It: While not a Bible study, biblical counseling coaching must be grounded in God's Word. Ensure Scripture is integrated thoughtfully and accurately.
  • Focusing Only on the "What" Instead of the "Why": Don't just help them find a new activity; help them understand *why* they feel lost and *how* God's character informs their calling.
Coach Tip

Remember the "O" in our O.D.D. framework: Observe & Orient. This means not just hearing words, but observing body language, emotional tone, and the underlying spiritual landscape. It's crucial for effective discernment coaching.

Markers of a Successful Session

How do you know if you've done well? Look for these indicators:

Signs of a Great Coaching Session

  • Client Feels Heard and Understood: They express a sense of relief and connection.
  • Shift in Perspective: The client moves from feeling "stuck" or "guilty" to seeing possibilities or understanding God's grace in their process.
  • Increased Clarity on Next Steps: Even if small, the client has a tangible, biblically-informed action to take.
  • Empowerment, Not Dependence: The client feels equipped to engage in their own discernment process with God, rather than relying solely on you for answers.
  • Deepening of Spiritual Reflection: The conversation naturally leads to discussion of God's character, Scripture, and the Holy Spirit's role.
  • Client Expresses Hope: They leave the session with renewed hope and a sense of peace about their journey.
Coach Tip

Always conclude a session by inviting the client to reflect on what they've learned and to commit to a specific, prayerful action. This reinforces personal responsibility and spiritual growth, key tenets of biblical coaching.

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary role of the Biblical Counseling Coach in guiding a client through spiritual discernment?

The primary role is to guide the client towards God's wisdom by facilitating deeper spiritual reflection, applying biblical principles, and empowering them to hear from God for themselves, rather than providing direct answers or solutions.

2. Eleanor expressed feeling "guilty" about not having her purpose figured out. How did Grace respond to effectively reframe this feeling?

Grace validated Eleanor's feeling of guilt but immediately reframed it by stating that God's love is not dependent on her "figuring it out" and that this season of seeking can be an opportunity for deeper intimacy with Him.

3. Why is it important for a Biblical Counseling Coach to avoid offering quick fixes or prescriptive advice, especially in discernment?

Offering quick fixes can undermine the client's own journey of seeking God's wisdom, create dependence on the coach, and bypass the process of spiritual formation that discernment is intended to foster. It also risks providing human wisdom over divine guidance.

4. Eleanor mentioned Psalm 46:10. What would be a biblically sound next step a coach might take to help her apply this verse to her struggle with "being still"?

A biblically sound next step would be to explore *why* Eleanor struggles to be still (e.g., fear, anxiety, busyness) and how the truth of "knowing that I am God" (His sovereignty, faithfulness, and character) can directly address those struggles, leading to practical steps for intentional stillness.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy and Validation are Foundational: Always start by validating your client's emotions and experiences before introducing new perspectives.

  • Discernment is a Relational Journey: Frame the discernment process as an opportunity for deeper intimacy and trust with God, not just problem-solving.

  • Scripture is the Ultimate Guide: Ground your coaching in biblical truth, inviting clients to engage with God's Word as their primary source of wisdom.

  • Focus on Empowerment and Action: Equip clients to actively participate in their spiritual growth and discernment, leading to concrete, prayerful next steps.

  • Practice Builds Confidence: Regular practice with realistic scenarios is crucial for developing your skills as a Christ-centered coach.
