A Personal Welcome from Grace Williams

My dearest student, I'm Grace Williams, and I am SO excited you're here. More than that, I believe you're here for a reason far beyond coincidence. God has a powerful plan for you, and I believe this program is a vital part of it.

Let me share a little bit about my own journey, because it might resonate with yours. For 20 years, I was a pastor's wife. On the outside, I was the picture of the 'perfect' Christian woman, always smiling in the front pew. But inside, I was silently battling a deep depression. My faith felt like a performance, and my joy was a mask.

Then, my world shattered. My husband had an affair with a worship leader. At 45, I found myself divorced, jobless, and completely lost. I didn't know who I was outside of being 'Pastor's wife.' My faith, once my anchor, felt utterly betrayed by my husband, my church, and in my darkest moments, by God Himself.

In the rubble of that brokenness, I began to rebuild. Not on the church's version of faith, but on my own authentic relationship with God. I realized I wasn't alone; countless Christian women were suffering in silence, afraid to admit their marital struggles, their doubts, their profound pain. They needed a space where they could be real, without judgment.

That's when God called me to become the support I never had. I trained as a life coach, specifically to serve Christian women navigating crisis, divorce, and the challenging journey of faith reconstruction. Today, I am blessed to coach women through their deepest wounds, earning an average of $10,100 per month. I've held sacred space for over 175 women, helping them find God in their mess and rebuild their lives. My private online community now has over 400 women healing and thriving together.

My personal quote, which I live by, is: "God isn't found in perfect church attendance. He meets you in your mess‚Äîand that's where real faith begins." And that, my friend, is what we will equip you to do for others.

Your First Steps: Setting the Stage for Success

You've taken the courageous step of enrolling, and that's a huge victory! Now, let's set you up for success from the very beginning. Here are your first steps:

  1. Explore the Course Platform: Take some time to navigate the AccrediPro Academy platform. Familiarize yourself with where lessons are located, how to submit assignments, and where to find resources.
  2. Review Your Syllabus: Read through the complete course syllabus for an overview of all modules, learning outcomes, and key milestones. This will give you a clear roadmap of your journey.
  3. Set Up Your Study Space: Create a dedicated, peaceful space where you can focus on your studies without distraction. This small act signals to yourself and your family that this new career is a priority.
  4. Commit to a Schedule: Look at your calendar and block out consistent time each week for your studies. Consistency is key to building momentum and avoiding overwhelm.
  5. Introduce Yourself: Head over to our private community forum (details will be in Lesson 0.2) and introduce yourself! Share your story, your hopes, and what you're most excited about. We can't wait to meet you!

This journey will challenge you, inspire you, and ultimately, transform you. I am here to walk alongside you every step of the way, cheering you on and guiding you towards your calling. I believe in your potential, and I know you have what it takes to become an exceptional Biblical Counseling Coach.

Check Your Understanding

1. What was Grace Williams's primary motivation for creating this Biblical Counseling Coach program?

Grace was motivated by her own experience of battling depression and faith crisis after her divorce, realizing that many Christian women suffer in silence without adequate support. She created the program to provide the guidance she wished she had received.

2. According to Grace, why is a woman in her 40s or 50s uniquely suited to become a Biblical Counseling Coach?

Grace emphasizes that women in this age range possess invaluable life experience, wisdom, empathy, and resilience honed over years of navigating personal, family, and professional challenges. These qualities are considered superpowers for connecting with and guiding clients.

3. What is a "POD" in the context of this certification program, and what is its purpose?

A "POD" (Private Practice Group) is a small, dedicated group of fellow students who will journey together through the program. Its purpose is to provide a supportive community for sharing insights, practicing skills, offering encouragement, and building accountability.

4. What is one recommended "first step" to maximize your learning experience in this program?

Several first steps are recommended, including: exploring the course platform, reviewing the syllabus, setting up a dedicated study space, committing to a consistent study schedule, and introducing yourself in the private community forum.
