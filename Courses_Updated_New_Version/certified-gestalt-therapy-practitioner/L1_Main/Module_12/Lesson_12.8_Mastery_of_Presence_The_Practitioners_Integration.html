Coach Tip

Don't be afraid of silence during the integration phase. After you ask an integration question, wait at least 10 seconds. Let the client's brain bridge the gap between the emotion and the action.

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

Common Mistake The Gestalt Success Marker
Rushing the end: Trying to "fix" the client's emotion because time is running out. Pacing: Slowing down to ensure the client feels grounded before leaving.
Giving advice: "You should do X this week." Co-creation: Asking, "What experiment feels right for you?"
Ignoring the body: Staying purely in the intellectual "why." Embodiment: Asking, "Where do you feel that relief in your body right now?"
Over-explaining: Using too much theory/jargon. Simplicity: Using "I" statements and direct observations.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Integration Phase" considered the most important part of a Gestalt session?

Because awareness alone doesn't create change. Integration bridges the gap between the session's insights and the client's real-world behavior, ensuring the "unfinished business" doesn't simply return the next day.

2. In the script, why did the coach ask Elena to "sit as if you are the expert"?

This is an "Embodiment" technique. By changing her physical posture, Elena can access the internal feeling of confidence, making the insight more "real" and accessible for her upcoming discovery call.

3. Elena suggests "telling herself" she is an expert. Why might a Gestalt coach want to move beyond just positive affirmations?

Affirmations are often "top-down" (intellectual). Gestalt is "bottom-up" (experiential). We want the client to *experience* the truth of the statement in their body and through action/experiments, not just repeat words.

4. What is a sign that a session has NOT been properly integrated?

If the client leaves feeling scattered, highly emotional without a sense of "next steps," or "stuck in their head" about the realization, the integration phase was likely rushed or missed.
