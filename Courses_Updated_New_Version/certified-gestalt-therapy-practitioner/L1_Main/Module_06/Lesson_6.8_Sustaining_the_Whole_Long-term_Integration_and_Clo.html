The Context: Linda has been "planning" her new business for 18 months but hasn't launched. She is articulate and smart, but when she talks about her goals, she tends to look away, fidget with her ring, and talk about what "people might think."

Income Note: Practitioners like you, specializing in high-level transitions for women 45+, often command $200 - $350 per session once certified, as this demographic values the deep, transformative nature of Gestalt work over "surface-level" life coaching.

Maya's Tip

Watch for Deflection. When Linda says "people might think," she is moving the focus away from her own feelings and onto an anonymous crowd. Your job is to bring the "I" back into the room.

The Practice Script

LINDA:
"I just don't know if the market is ready for another consultant. I mean, you see all these people on Instagram, and it feels like everyone is already doing it. It makes one feel a bit silly for even trying."
YOU (The Practitioner):
"Linda, I notice as you say the word 'silly,' youâ€™re twisting your wedding ring quite fast. What are you experiencing in your hand right now?"
LINDA:
"Oh... I didn't even realize I was doing that. It feels... tight. My hand feels very tense."
YOU (The Practitioner):
"Stay with that tension for a moment. If that tightness in your hand had a voice, what would it be saying to you right now?"
LINDA:
"It's saying... 'Hold on. Don't let go. If you let go, you might fall.'"
Maya's Tip

Notice how we didn't ask "Why are you twisting your ring?" That would lead to a mental explanation. By asking "What are you experiencing?" we moved into the somatic reality of the moment.

Practice Exercise: Say It Out Loud

Step 1: Record yourself on your phone saying this line: "Linda, I notice you've moved from talking about your dream to talking about 'people on Instagram.' I'm curious, what is happening for you right here, with me, in this moment?"

Step 2: Listen to the recording. Is your voice warm? Is it steady? Does it sound like an invitation rather than a challenge?

Step 3: Try again, but this time, slow down your pace by 20%. In Gestalt, the silence between the words is where the client's awareness grows.

Common Practitioner Pitfalls

The Pitfall Why It Happens The Gestalt Shift
Problem Solving We want to "fix" the client's business launch. Focus on the process of how she is stopping herself.
Asking "Why" Natural curiosity about the past. Ask "How" or "What" to stay in the present.
Ignoring the Body Being too "in our heads" as coaches. Notice breath, posture, and micro-movements.
Filling the Silence Discomfort with the "void." Let the silence sit; it's the "fertile void" of change.
Maya's Tip

If you feel stuck, simply describe what you see. "I see you looking at the floor" or "I hear your voice getting softer." This simple act of Phenomenological Observation is often enough to shift the session.

Check Your Understanding

1. When Linda says "It makes one feel silly," which contact style is she likely using to avoid her own feelings?

She is using Deflection (and specifically Egotism or Proflection depending on context). By using the word "one" instead of "I," she distances herself from the immediate emotional impact of her statement.

2. Why did the practitioner ask about the ring instead of the Instagram market?

Because the Instagram market is "out there" (the environment), while the ring-twisting is "in here" (the contact boundary). Gestalt focuses on how the client is currently manifesting their anxiety in the room.

3. What is the goal of asking "What would that tightness say?"

This is a Gestalt Experiment. It gives a voice to a "disowned" part of the self (the part that is afraid), allowing the client to integrate that fear rather than just fighting it.

4. How does slowing down the pace help a client like Linda?

Slowing down reduces the "noise" and allows the client to notice subtle physical sensations that are usually drowned out by rapid, defensive speech.
Maya's Tip

You are becoming a real coach! Don't worry about being "perfect." Gestalt is about Authenticity, not a script. If you feel confused in a session, you can even say, "I'm feeling a bit confused right now; where are we?" That's being a present practitioner.

Key Takeaways

  • Process over Content: Itâ€™s not about *what* the client says, but *how* they say it and how they move.
  • The Somatic Bridge: Physical fidgets or tension are direct pathways to the client's internal conflict.
  • The "I" Statement: Always gently encourage the client to move from "you" or "one" to "I."
  • Safe Emergency: Gestalt experiments create a "safe emergency" where the client can face their resistance in a controlled environment.
