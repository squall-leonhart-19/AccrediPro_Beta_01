Background & Symptoms

Elena retired from teaching 6 months ago to start a boutique wellness business. She is struggling with "imposter syndrome." She says, "I feel like a fraud. Part of me still feels like the teacher who follows rules, and the other part wants to be this creative entrepreneur. I'm stuck in the middle."

The Somatic Marker: When Elena talks about her business, she leans forward but her breath becomes shallow. When she talks about her teaching career, her shoulders slump and she looks at the floor.

Income Note: Practitioners working with "career pivoters" like Elena often charge $175 - $250 per session for high-level integration coaching, as the ROI for the client is a successful new business launch.

Maya's Tip

Watch Elena's hands. In Gestalt, the hands often tell the story the mouth is trying to hide. If she clenches her fist while talking about her "teacher self," that is a doorway to integration. Ask her: "What is your left hand saying right now?"

The Practice Script: Two-Chair Dialogue

Read this script out loud. Imagine Elena is sitting across from you. There are two chairs: one for "Teacher Elena" (The Old Identity) and one for "Entrepreneur Elena" (The New Identity).

Integration Dialogue Script

YOU:
"Elena, I hear the conflict between these two parts of you. Iâ€™d like to try something. I want you to sit in this chair and speak *as* the Teacher who feels like she must follow the rules. Tell the 'Entrepreneur' in that other chair why sheâ€™s being a 'fraud'."
ELENA:
(Sitting in Chair 1, shoulders slumped) "You don't know what you're doing. You've spent 30 years following a curriculum. You can't just make your own rules now. People will see through you."
YOU:
"Stay with that feeling in your shoulders, Elena. Now, move to the other chair. Sit as the Entrepreneur, and respond to what you just heard. What does this part of you want the Teacher to know?"
ELENA:
(Moving to Chair 2, leaning forward) "I want to tell her that I've learned so much! I'm not a fraud, I'm just new. I have a right to be here."
YOU:
"Say that again, but start with 'I am...' and notice your breath. 'I am an entrepreneur, and I have a right to be here.'"
Maya's Tip

Notice how I asked her to use "I am" statements. This is a core Gestalt technique. It moves the client from talking *about* a concept to *owning* the identity in the present moment.

Practical Exercise: Your Turn

Set up two chairs in your room. If you don't have two chairs, use two cushions on the floor. Practice the following prompts out loud:

1

The Invitation: Practice saying: "I notice there's a part of you that feels [X] and a part that feels [Y]. Would you be willing to give them each a voice today?"

2

The Somatic Bridge: Practice saying: "As you say those words, I notice your chest tightening. If that tightness had a voice, what would it say to you right now?"

3

The Integration Question: Practice saying: "How do these two parts feel about each other now that they've spoken? Is there a way they can work together?"

Common Practice Pitfalls

The Mistake Why It Happens The Gestalt Fix
Over-Intellectualizing The coach stays in "problem-solving" mode. Ask: "Where do you feel that in your body right now?"
Rushing the Shift Coach is uncomfortable with the client's conflict. Slow down. Let the client stay in the "uncomfortable" chair longer.
Using "Why" Questions Curiosity leads to searching for past causes. Switch "Why" to "How" or "What." (e.g., "What are you experiencing now?")
Ignoring Non-Verbals Coach is too focused on the script/dialogue. Comment on what you see: "I notice you just took a deep breath."
Maya's Tip

In my first year, I used to try to "reconcile" the parts for the client. I'd say, "See? You can be both!" Don't do that. The integration must happen *inside* them. Your job is just to hold the space for the conversation.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these "Integration Markers" in your clients:

  • Breath Deepening: The client moves from shallow "chest breathing" to deep "belly breathing."
  • Softening of Features: The jaw relaxes, the forehead unfurrows, and the eyes brighten.
  • Congruence: Their words match their body language. (e.g., they say "I feel powerful" and their spine actually straightens).
  • The "Aha" Moment: A sudden realization that the "Old Self" isn't an enemy, but a foundation for the "New Self."
Maya's Tip

A 2021 study on somatic integration showed that when clients physically move between chairs, their physiological stress response (measured by heart rate variability) actually stabilizes faster than through talk therapy alone. This is the power of the work you are doing!

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena says, "I know I should feel confident, but I just don't." What is the best Gestalt response?

Instead of agreeing or giving advice, ask: "As you say 'I don't feel confident,' what do you notice happening in your body right now?" This brings her back to the present experience.

2. What is the primary purpose of the Two-Chair technique in Module 5?

To facilitate a dialogue between fragmented parts of the self so they can reach a state of "Embodied Integration."

3. If a client becomes very emotional during the dialogue, what should the coach do?

Stay present. Encourage them to "Stay with that feeling" and breathe. Avoid the urge to "comfort" them out of the emotion, as the emotion is often the path to integration.

4. Why do we use "I" statements instead of "It" or "You" statements?

"I" statements encourage ownership and presence. Saying "I am angry" is an embodied experience; saying "It is frustrating" is a detached observation.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Integration is Somatic: Watch for shifts in breath, posture, and tone as the "split" parts begin to communicate.
  • The Coach as Mirror: Your role is to reflect back the client's non-verbal cues (e.g., "I notice your hand is shaking").
  • The Power of "I am": Moving from abstract talk to present-moment identification is the catalyst for change.
  • Respect the Resistance: If a client isn't ready to move chairs, don't force it. Explore the "stuckness" as its own part.
  • Wholeness is the Goal: We aren't trying to eliminate the "Old Self," but to integrate its wisdom into the "New Self."
