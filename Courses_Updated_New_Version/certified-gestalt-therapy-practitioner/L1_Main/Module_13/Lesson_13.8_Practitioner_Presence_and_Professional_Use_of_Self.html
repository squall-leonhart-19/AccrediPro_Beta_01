Common Mistakes for New Practitioners

Mistake Why it Hinders Growth Gestalt Correction
Analyzing "Why" Keeps the client in their head and out of their experience. Ask "How" and "What" instead of "Why."
Fixing the Problem Steals the client's opportunity for self-regulation. Trust the client's process; stay in the "Void."
Ignoring the Body Misses 90% of the communication. Comment on non-verbal cues (fidgeting, breathing).
Over-explaining Theory Interrupts the flow of awareness. Keep interventions short and experiential.

Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

A successful Gestalt session isn't always "happy," but it is always alive. Look for these signs:

  • The "Aha" Shift: The client has a visible physical release (sighing, shoulders dropping).
  • Ownership: The client moves from "It makes me feel..." to "I feel..."
  • Increased Vitality: Even if the topic is sad, the client seems more "present" and energized in the room.
  • Integration: The client recognizes that the "Principal" was a role they played, not the totality of who they are.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask Elena "What are you noticing in your body?" instead of "Why do you think you're stiff?"

"Why" questions invite intellectualization and rationalization. "What" questions in the body ground the client in the immediate phenomenological reality, which is the only place where change can occur in Gestalt therapy.

2. What is an "introject" in the context of Elena's career change?

An introject is a belief or behavior (like "being professional means being rigid") that Elena "swallowed whole" from her previous career environment without evaluating if it actually fits her current needs or personality.

3. What is the primary goal of the "Empty Chair" experiment in this lab?

The goal is externalization. By speaking to the "Principal" version of herself, Elena can differentiate her current needs from her past roles, facilitating the "Contact Boundary" work needed for growth.

4. If Elena begins to cry during the experiment, what is the most "Gestalt" response?

The most Gestalt response is to stay with the feeling. You might say, "Stay with those tears, Elena. What are they saying right now?" Avoid the urge to hand her a tissue immediately, as that can be a subtle way of telling the client to stop expressing.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • The Body Never Lies: When a client's words and body language don't match, follow the body.
  • Respect the Mask: Introjects served a purpose once. We don't "attack" them; we help the client outgrow them.
  • Experiment, Don't Prescribe: Gestalt is about discovery through doing, not following a set of instructions.
  • You Are the Instrument: Your own awareness of the client's energy is your most powerful coaching tool.
