Maya's Insight

Notice Elena's "deflection." In Gestalt, talking about the past is often a way to avoid the intensity of the present. Your job isn't to fix the past, but to bring her energy into the room right now.

The Coaching Script

In this scenario, Elena is rambling about her old teaching job. You are going to use the W.H.O.L.E. Frameworkâ„¢ to bring her back to the present.

ELENA:
"And then there was this one student, back in 2014... I just wonder if I should have stayed. Maybe I'm not cut out for this new business thing. It's just so much easier to stay where people know you..."
YOU:
"Elena, I'm going to invite you to pause for just a moment. I notice that as you talk about 2014, your hand is gripping the edge of your chair quite tightly."
ELENA:
"Oh... I didn't even realize. I guess I'm just nervous."
YOU:
"Stay with that 'nervous' feeling right now. If that grip on the chair could speak, what would it be saying?"
ELENA:
"It's saying... 'Don't let go. If you let go, you'll fall.'"
YOU:
"I hear that. 'Don't let go.' Let's breathe into that 'Don't let go' right here with me. What do you feel in your chest as you say that?"
Technique Check

This is the Awareness of Body technique. By shifting from her story (the past) to her hand (the present), you've bypassed her intellectual defenses.

Practice Exercise: Say It Out Loud

1

The "I Notice" Prompt: Record yourself saying: "Elena, I notice your voice gets softer when you mention your new business. What are you experiencing in this moment as you tell me that?"

Goal: Keep your tone neutral but warm. No judgment.

2

The "Enactment" Prompt: Say this out loud: "Try saying that again, but this time, start the sentence with 'I am choosing to stay stuck because...' and see how that feels in your body."

Goal: This encourages client ownership (The 'O' in W.H.O.L.E.).

Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

The Mistake Why It Happens The Gestalt Shift
Problem Solving We want to "fix" the client's career anxiety. Focus on the process of her anxiety, not the solution.
Asking "Why?" Natural curiosity leads to seeking causes. Ask "How?" or "What?" to keep them in the How of the present.
Ignoring the Body We get caught up in the client's interesting story. The body never lies; the story often obscures.
Talking Too Much Fear of silence or needing to "prove" value. Use silence as a "fertile void" for the client's awareness.
Maya's Advice

In my early days, I felt I had to give "advice" to justify my fee. In Gestalt, your presence is the value. When you stop trying to fix, the client starts to heal.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why did the coach ask Elena what her "grip" would say if it could speak?

This is a "prosopopoeia" or "giving voice" experiment. It helps the client access the subconscious wisdom held in their physical tension, bypassing their intellectual "story."

2. What is the primary danger of asking a client "Why are you feeling stuck?"

Asking "Why" leads the client into their head to search for justifications and history. Gestalt focuses on "How" and "What," which keeps the client in the experiential "Here and Now."

3. Elena deflects by talking about 2014. What is this called in Gestalt terminology?

This is often called "Deflection" or "Evasion"â€”a boundary disturbance where the client steers away from direct contact with the present moment or the practitioner.

4. How does the W.H.O.L.E. Frameworkâ„¢ help a career-changer like Elena?

It provides a structured way to move from Witnessing (W) her patterns to helping her take Ownership (O) of her new life path, ensuring her transition is "Whole" and not fragmented by past regrets.

Key Takeaways

  • Presence over Pacing: Your ability to stay present with the client's discomfort is more important than "getting through" a script.
  • The Body is the Map: Always return to physical cues (breath, tension, gaze) when the client gets lost in their story.
  • Safe Experiments: Gestalt is about "trying on" new behaviors in the safety of the coaching relationship.
  • You Are Ready: Your life experience (as a nurse, teacher, or mom) is your greatest asset in sensing these subtle human shifts.
