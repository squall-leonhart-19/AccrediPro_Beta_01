The Coaching Script

Read this script to understand the flow. We are moving Elena from talking about her fear to experiencing the physical sensation of it.

YOU:
"Elena, I notice that as you talk about 'researching more,' your voice gets higher and you're tapping your pen quite fast. What are you aware of in your body right now?"
ELENA:
"I don't know... I just think I need to be professional. If I don't have every answer, I'll look like a fraud. It's just logical to be prepared."
YOU:
"I hear the logic. But let's try an experiment. Would you be willing to put the pen down for a moment? Just for thirty seconds."
ELENA:
"(Sighs, puts pen down) Okay. Now what?"
YOU:
"Stay with that sigh. What happens in your chest when you aren't holding that penâ€”that 'shield' of research? Just describe the sensation, not the thought."
ELENA:
"It feels... hollow. And a bit shaky. I feel like I'm 15 again, waiting for a grade."

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

When you are starting out, it is easy to fall into these traps. Use this table as your checklist for self-supervision.

The Pitfall The Gestalt Success Marker
"Fixing" the client: Trying to take away their anxiety. Deepening the experience: Allowing the anxiety to be felt fully.
Asking "Why": Leading the client back into intellectualizing. Asking "How/What": Keeping the client in the "Now."
Rushing the silence: Feeling uncomfortable when the client is quiet. Honoring the Void: Trusting that the silence is where the integration happens.
Staying "Professional": Being a cold, distant observer. The I-Thou Bond: Being a real human being in the room with the client.
Maya's Wisdom

In Gestalt, we say "Change occurs when one becomes what he is, not when he tries to become what he is not." Your job is simply to help Elena be 100% Elena in this moment.

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why did we ask Elena to put down her pen during the experiment?

In Gestalt, objects can act as "creative adjustments" or shields that help a client avoid contact with their immediate feelings. Removing the physical distraction brings the awareness back to the body.

2. Elena says, "I feel like I'm 15 again." What is the best Gestalt response?

Avoid asking "Why did that happen at 15?" Instead, ask: "How are you experiencing that 15-year-old version of you right now in this chair?" This keeps the work in the present moment.

3. What is a physiological marker that the experiment is working?

A "deepening" of the breath, a change in skin color (flushing), or a spontaneous emotional release (tears or laughter). These indicate the client has moved from "head" to "body."

4. If Elena refuses the experiment, what should you do?

Respect the resistance. In Gestalt, resistance is just another form of energy. You might say, "I notice you're hesitant to put the pen down. What is that hesitancy like for you right now?"

Practice Lab Key Takeaways

  • Observation is Intervention: Simply noticing a client's physical habit (like tapping a pen) is a powerful way to start an experiment.
  • Stay in the "How": Focus on how the client is avoiding the present, rather than why they are doing it.
  • The Body Never Lies: While the mind can create logical excuses for months, the body will reveal the truth in seconds.
  • The Impasse is the Way: When a client feels "stuck" or "hollow," don't rush to fill it. That is the fertile void where new growth begins.
