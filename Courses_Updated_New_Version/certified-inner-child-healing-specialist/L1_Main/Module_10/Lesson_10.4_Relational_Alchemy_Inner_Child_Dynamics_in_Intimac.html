The Safe Harbor Agreement

To manifest a truly authentic partnership, clients must create a Safe Harbor Agreement. This is a pre-negotiated protocol for when the "Adaptive Child" takes the wheel.

  1. The "Pause" Button: Either partner can call a "Somatic Timeout" if they feel flooded.
  2. The Identification: "I am currently in an Adaptive Child space. I need 20 minutes to regulate."
  3. The Safe Re-entry: Re-engaging only when the Functional Adult is back online.
  4. The Request: Asking for what the Inner Child needs (e.g., "I need to hear I'm not in trouble").

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "Functional Adult" and an "Adaptive Child" in conflict?

The Functional Adult is regulated and seeks connection/solutions, whereas the Adaptive Child is reactive, flooded, and seeks to "win" or protect itself through survival strategies like blaming or withdrawing.

2. How does a "Relational Projection" function?

It occurs when a client overlays their childhood trauma or caregiver dynamic onto their partner, treating the partner as if they were the original source of the wound (e.g., seeing a partner's silence as a parent's neglect).

3. What is the goal of the 'Safe Harbor' agreement?

To provide a pre-negotiated protocol that allows partners to pause, co-regulate, and return to the "Functional Adult" state before addressing the conflict, ensuring the Inner Child feels safe.
