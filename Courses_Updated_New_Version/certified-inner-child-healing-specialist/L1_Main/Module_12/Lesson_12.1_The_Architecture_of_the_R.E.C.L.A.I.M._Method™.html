Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "fixing" and "reclaiming" in the R.E.C.L.A.I.M. Methodâ„¢?

Fixing implies the client/inner child is broken and needs to be returned to a "correct" state, which can reinforce shame. Reclaiming is about integrating parts of the self that were frozen or exiled, moving toward wholeness and authentic self-alignment.

2. What does it mean for a practitioner to act as an "External Regulator"?

It means the practitioner uses their own regulated nervous system to help stabilize the client's dysregulated nervous system through interpersonal neurobiology, providing the safety needed for the client to explore deep emotional wounds.

3. Which brain structure is primarily calmed during the "Listen" stage of the method?

The Amygdala. By providing radical attunement and listening, the practitioner helps lower the "alarm" response, signaling to the brain that the environment is now safe.

4. Why is a "Psychological Container" essential for inner child work?

It prevents emotional "flooding" and re-traumatization. By establishing boundaries, somatic anchors, and informed consent, the practitioner ensures the client remains in a "Window of Tolerance" during regression work.
