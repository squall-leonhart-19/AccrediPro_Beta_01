Application Exercise: Speak it Out Loud

Healing happens in the vocal vibration and the emotional resonance of the coach. It is not enough to read the script; you must inhabit it.

Step Action Goal
1. Mirroring Stand in front of a mirror and say the first line. Watch your facial expressions. Ensure your face looks warm and empathetic, not judgmental.
2. Recording Record yourself reading the "Inner Child" explanation on your phone. Listen for "umms," "ahhs," or a rushed pace that indicates your own anxiety.
3. The "Sarah" Check Imagine you are talking to a dear friend. Soften your voice. Shift from "Professional Authority" to "Compassionate Guide."
Sarah's Insight

I used to record my sessions (with permission!) and listen back while driving. I realized I was talking 20% too fast because I was nervous about 'proving' my worth. If you feel that urge to rush, take a breath. Your silence is often more healing than your words.

Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you transition into this career, you might fall into "Fixer Mode." Let's look at how to pivot toward "Healer Mode."

4 Common Novice Mistakes

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to teach a 20-minute lecture on neuroscience when the client just needs to feel heard.
  • The "Right Answer" Trap: Fearing you'll say the "wrong" thing. Remember, the relationship heals more than the specific technique.
  • Fixing the Feeling: Trying to make the client stop crying or stop feeling anxious. In Inner Child work, we want them to feel it so they can heal it.
  • Ignoring the Body: Focusing only on the client's story and ignoring their clenched fists or shallow breathing.
Sarah's Insight

When I first pivoted from nursing, I wanted to 'treat' the client. I had to learn that in coaching, the client is the expert on their lifeâ€”I am just the expert on the process. Trust Elena's inner child to know the way home.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explain the "Science" (neuroscience) behind the Inner Child to a client like Elena?

It helps remove the "shame" and "self-blame." When a client understands that their reactions are a biological survival mechanism, they can move from self-criticism to self-compassion.

2. What is the primary goal of the "First Session" dialogue used in the script?

The goal is to establish safety and rapport while introducing the concept of the Inner Child as a "protective part" rather than a "problem to be fixed."

3. If Elena starts to cry when talking about her father, what is the best "Coach" response?

Hold a compassionate silence. Avoid rushing in with tissues or "it's okay" immediately. Allow the emotion to exist in the space, perhaps saying, "It's safe to let that out here. I'm right here with you."

4. What does "The Principal's Office" metaphor tell you about Elena's Inner Child?

It indicates a "Power-Under" dynamic where she feels small, judged, and in trouble. Her Inner Child is stuck in a state of "Performance Anxiety" related to authority figures.

Practice Lab Takeaways

  • Validation First: Always validate the client's adult struggle before diving into childhood roots.
  • Tone Matters: A warm, steady, and compassionate voice is your most powerful coaching tool.
  • Neuro-Normalization: Use the "Science" to explain that the Inner Child is a neural pathway formed for protection.
  • Application: Practice out loud until the concepts of "Adult Self" and "Inner Child" feel like a natural part of your vocabulary.
  • You are Ready: Imposter syndrome is just your own inner child feeling "new." Acknowledge her, and keep going!
