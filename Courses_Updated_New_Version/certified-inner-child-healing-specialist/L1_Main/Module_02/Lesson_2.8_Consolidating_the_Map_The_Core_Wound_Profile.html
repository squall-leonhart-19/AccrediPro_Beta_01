4. Common Pitfalls in the Explore Phase

Mistake Why it Happens The Professional Fix
Moving to "Healing" too fast We want to fix the client's pain quickly. Stay in Explore. The client must fully see the pattern before they can change it.
Over-analyzing Imposter syndrome makes us want to "prove" we know the science. Ask, don't tell. Use: "Does that resonate?" instead of "This is because of X."
Ignoring Body Cues Focusing too much on our next question. Watch for: sighing, looking away, or fidgeting. Call it out gently.

6. Check Your Understanding

Knowledge Check

1. Diane says, "I don't remember much of my childhood, it was just normal." How do you respond in the Explore phase?

Gently explain that "not remembering" is often a protective mechanism. You might say: "That's very common. Our minds sometimes 'file away' the details to keep us moving forward. Let's focus on how you feel today and see where those feelings lead us."

2. What is the primary goal of the "Explore" phase?

The goal is Awareness. It is helping the client see the direct link between their current adult "stuckness" and the adaptive survival strategies they developed as a child.

3. If a client becomes visibly distressed (crying heavily) during the Explore phase, what is your first step?

Pause and ground them. Use a "Safe Container" technique: "I see you, and you are safe here. Let's take a deep breath together. You don't have to go any further than you're ready to go."

It creates psychological distance. Itâ€™s often easier for a client to feel compassion for a "little girl" than it is to feel compassion for their current adult self. This reduces shame.

Key Takeaways

  • The "Explore" phase is about making connections, not "fixing" problems yet.
  • Use the client's own language (e.g., "Good Girl," "Easy Child") to build the bridge to the past.
  • Body cues (chest tightness, sighing) are often more accurate than the client's words.
  • Specializing in a specific client archetype (like the burnt-out executive) allows for higher rates and better results.
