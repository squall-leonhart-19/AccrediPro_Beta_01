Success Markers vs. Common Pitfalls

Use this table to audit your practice sessions. Remember, growth happens in the "correcting" phase!

The Pitfall (Avoid This) The Success Marker (Aim for This)
The "Fixer" Trap: Trying to solve the client's problem in the first 10 minutes. The "Witness" Approach: Allowing the client to feel fully seen and heard before moving to tools.
Over-Explaining: Using too much jargon about the nervous system. Relatable Metaphors: Using simple language like "The Protector" or "The Little Girl."

Dealing with Imposter Syndrome

If you feel like an imposter, remember: You don't have to be "healed" to be a healer. You just have to be one step further on the path than your client. Your own journey is your greatest credential.

Check Your Skills

1. A client says, "I feel like a total failure for crying in our session." What is the best coaching response?

The best response is to validate and normalize: "Actually, those tears are a sign that your Inner Child finally feels safe enough to express what she's been holding. You aren't failing; you're healing."

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Safe Container" in the first session?

To establish a secure attachment between coach and client, allowing the client's nervous system to shift from "Defensive/Protective" mode to "Open/Healing" mode.

3. If a client becomes overwhelmed (hyper-aroused) during a somatic exercise, what should you do?

Immediately pause the exercise. Use grounding techniques: "Let's open our eyes and name three things you see in the room. Feel your feet on the floor." Always prioritize stabilization over deep processing.

4. Why is it important to ask "Where do you feel that in your body?"

Because Inner Child wounds are stored in the body's implicit memory. We cannot heal through "talk" alone; we must engage the somatic experience to release stored trauma.
Final Practice Tip

In your next practice session, try to leave 5 seconds of silence after the client finishes speaking. This "Golden Silence" often allows the client's Inner Child to come forward with a deeper truth that the Adult Self was trying to hide.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Safety First: You cannot do deep inner child work until the client's "Protector" parts feel safe with you.
  • Validation is Medicine: Simply telling a client "it makes sense why you feel this way" can be more healing than any advanced technique.
  • Somatic Awareness: Always bridge the gap between the client's story and their physical sensations.
  • Professional Presence: Your calm, regulated nervous system is your most powerful coaching tool.
