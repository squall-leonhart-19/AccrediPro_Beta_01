Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps during a deep dive session:

  • Over-intellectualizing: Explaining the science of the amygdala for 20 minutes instead of letting the client feel their emotions.
  • Rushing the "Fix": Trying to get the client to "stop feeling scared" before they've fully acknowledged the inner child's valid fear.
  • Losing the Professional Container: Becoming "too much of a friend" and losing the authority needed to guide the client through deep work.
  • Ignoring the Body: Focusing only on the thoughts/words and missing the physical cues (fidgeting, shallow breathing, looking away).

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these signs:

  • Somatic Release: The client takes a deep, spontaneous sigh or their shoulders visibly drop.
  • Shift in Perspective: The client stops saying "I am lazy" and starts saying "A part of me is scared."
  • Actionable Clarity: By the end of the session, the "impossible" task (like the website) feels manageable again.
  • Increased Self-Compassion: The client‚Äôs tone toward themselves softens.
Sarah's Insight

Remember, your presence is the most powerful tool you have. If you stay calm and grounded, your client's nervous system will begin to co-regulate with yours.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask the client where they feel a block in their body?

The body (soma) holds the emotional memory of the inner child. By focusing on the physical sensation, we bypass the logical mind's defenses and access the root emotion directly.

2. If a client like Diane insists she just needs "more information," what is likely happening?

This is often a "protective strategy" used by the inner child to avoid the perceived danger of failure or judgment. Seeking more information provides a temporary (but false) sense of safety.

3. What is the primary goal of the "Safe Space Anchor"?

The goal is to provide the inner child with the reassurance that the "Adult Self" is now present and capable of handling the situation, which lowers the nervous system's threat response.

4. What should you do if a client becomes highly emotional during the practice?

Stay grounded, maintain a calm voice, and hold the space. Use phrases like "I'm right here with you" and "It's safe to feel this now." Do not rush to "fix" the crying; let the release happen.

Key Takeaways

  • Inner child blocks often masquerade as business problems like procrastination or "imposter syndrome."
  • Somatic cues (body sensations) are the fastest way to identify the age of the inner child part that is triggered.
  • The coach's primary role is to provide a "regulated container" so the client's adult self can reintegrate.
  • Practice out loud to build the professional authority needed for high-impact coaching.
