Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

The Mistake Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Giving Advice Too Soon Wanting to "fix" the client's pain quickly. Stay in the "Curiosity Phase" longer. Ask "Where do you feel that?"
Over-identifying Your own inner child wants to "save" them. Maintain a "Compassionate Witness" stance. Use breathing to ground yourself.
Rationalizing Trying to use logic to solve an emotional wound. The inner child doesn't speak "logic." Use somatic (body) cues instead.
Rushing the Process Feeling pressure to get a "breakthrough" in Session 1. Trust the process. Sometimes just identifying the age of the wound is a massive win.
Sarah's Insight

In your first few sessions, you might feel like you need to charge less because you're "new." Don't. Many of my students, even during their certification, start at $125-$150 per hour because this work is deep and transformational. Your value is in the transformation, not your years of experience.

How to Know the Session Worked

You don't need the client to cry for a session to be successful. Look for these "Micro-Shifts":

  • Somatic Release: The clientâ€™s shoulders drop, or they take a spontaneous deep sigh.
  • Language Shift: They move from saying "I am a procrastinator" to "A part of me is feeling scared."
  • Integration: They can name the age or the specific memory associated with the current stressor.
  • Empowerment: They express a desire to "reparent" that younger part rather than "get rid of it."

Always end with a "Reparenting Commitment." Ask the client: "What is one small thing you can do this week to show your inner child that they are safe and loved, regardless of their performance?"

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. If a client is stuck in a logical "loop" and won't drop into their feelings, what is the best intervention?

Acknowledge the "Protector" part of them. Say: "I notice there's a part of you that's very analytical and trying to figure this out logically. Can we thank that part for its hard work, and ask if it's okay if we check in with your body for just a moment?"

2. What is the primary difference between traditional life coaching and Inner Child Healing?

Traditional coaching is often forward-facing and goal-oriented (the "What"). Inner Child Healing looks at the developmental origins of current patterns (the "Why" and "Who") to create lasting neurological change.

3. A client says, "I feel like I'm 5 years old right now." What is your next step?

Validate and somaticize. Say: "I hear that. When you're 5, what do you notice happening in your body? And if that 5-year-old could say one thing to us right now, what would it be?"

4. How do you handle a client who begins to heavily "trauma dump" during a practice session?

Gently "Contain" the session. Say: "I want to honor everything you're sharing, and I can see how much is there. To make sure we stay safe and grounded today, let's pick just one of those threads to look at through the lens of your inner child. Which one feels most present in your body right now?"

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • The Body Never Lies: Always pivot from the "story" the client is telling to the "sensation" they are feeling.
  • Safety First: The inner child will not reveal itself if the coaching environment feels judgmental or rushed.
  • Reparenting is the Goal: Your job is to teach the client how to be their own "Good Parent," not to become the parent for them.
  • Confidence follows Action: You don't get confident to start coaching; you start coaching to get confident.
