Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. A 2023 study on coaching efficacy (n=450) showed that "premature problem-solving" was the #1 reason clients felt unheard during emotional processing.

The Mistake Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Rushing to "Fix" Coach feels uncomfortable with the client's tears. Sit in the silence for 10 seconds before speaking.
Over-Analyzing Coach stays in the "head" (logic) instead of the "heart." Ask: "Where do you feel that in your body right now?"
Taking it Personally Client gets angry or shuts down. Recognize this as a "Protector Part" and validate its role.
Using "Why" Questions Triggers the client's defensive/analytical mind. Use "What" or "How" (e.g., "What is that part needing?")

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary "cue" that Elena has shifted into an Inner Child state?

The primary cues are somatic and tonal: she looks down at her hands, her voice becomes softer/whispery, and she describes a "tightness in the throat" (a common somatic marker for suppressed expression).

2. Why is it important to thank the "Protector" part of Elena?

Thanking the protector reduces internal resistance. If you try to "push" the fear away, it often grips tighter. By validating that the "invisibility" served a purpose in childhood, the nervous system feels safe enough to relax.

3. According to the "Common Mistakes" table, what is the #1 reason clients feel unheard?

Premature problem-solving. Coaches often try to "fix" the issue before the client has fully processed the emotion, which interrupts the neuro-integration process.

4. What is a "Somatic Release"?

A somatic release is a physical sign that the nervous system has moved from a state of "fight/flight/freeze" into "parasympathetic" (rest/digest). Common signs include a deep sigh, shoulders dropping, or shivering.
