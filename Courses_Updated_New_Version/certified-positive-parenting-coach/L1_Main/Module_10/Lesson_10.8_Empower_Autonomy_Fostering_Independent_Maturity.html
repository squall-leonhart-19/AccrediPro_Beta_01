Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Initial Mirroring" phase with a discouraged client?

The goal is to dissolve shame and build safety. By validating the client's feelings without judgment, you open their "upstairs brain" to learning new strategies.

2. If a client says "I've tried everything and nothing works," how should a coach respond?

Avoid listing more things to try. Instead, pivot to: "It sounds like you've put in so much effort and feel exhausted. Let's look at the energy behind those attempts rather than the actions themselves."

3. Why is "out loud" practice essential for new coaches?

Verbal practice builds muscle memory. It allows you to find your "coach voice" so that when you are with a real client, the words flow naturally even when you are nervous.

4. What is a "Success Marker" in a coaching session?

A success marker is when a client has an "Aha!" moment where they take ownership of a new perspective or commit to one small, manageable action step for the week.

Key Takeaways

  • Connection is Currency: Your relationship with the client mirrors their relationship with their child. Model the connection you want them to have.
  • Silence is a Tool: Don't be afraid of pauses. Let the client sit with a powerful question.
  • Progress, Not Perfection: Just as we teach parents to give their kids grace, you must give yourself grace as you learn this craft.
  • Practice is Mandatory: The difference between a $20/hour hobbyist and a $200/hour professional is the hours spent practicing the "soft skills" of coaching.
