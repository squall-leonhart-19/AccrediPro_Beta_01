Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

As you begin implementing the N.U.R.T.U.R.E. foundations, watch out for these four common mistakes that can stall a client's progress:

  • The "Fix-It" Rush: Trying to give Sarah a "sticker chart" solution before she has learned to Notice her own triggers.
  • Over-Educating: Spending 20 minutes explaining brain science instead of letting the client feel the shift in the session.
  • Joining the Chaos: Becoming as anxious as the client. If Sarah is at a level 10 anxiety, you must remain at a level 2.
  • Skipping the Body: Focusing only on thoughts. Positive parenting is a somatic (body-based) experience first.

Success Markers: How Do You Know the Session Worked?

A successful "Foundations" session doesn't mean the child never misbehaves again. It means the parent has shifted. Look for these markers:

  • Softening: The clientâ€™s shoulders drop, and their voice loses its sharp edge.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we focus on "Notice" before "Understand" in the framework?

Because a parent cannot intellectually "understand" their child's needs if their own nervous system is in a state of high arousal (Fight/Flight). We must notice and regulate the self first.

2. Sarah says, "My son is just manipulative." How do you use the 'Understand' pillar here?

Gently challenge the label by reframing it as a developmental need. "I hear how it feels that way. Let's look at the 'Understand' pillarâ€”is it possible he's seeking 'connection' or 'agency' rather than trying to manipulate you?"

3. What is the primary goal of the "Foundations" phase of coaching?

To shift the parent from a reactive state to a responsive state by building awareness of their own triggers and the child's developmental stages.

4. True or False: You should provide a full discipline plan in the very first session.

False. Jumping to "Empowerment" (the E in NURTURE) without the foundations of Notice and Understand usually leads to failed implementation and client frustration.
