Defining Your Professional Scope

The most important distinction to make as a Certified Positive Parenting Coach‚Ñ¢ is that we are educators and facilitators, not clinicians. While a therapist might look backward to heal past trauma, a coach looks forward to build new skills and patterns.

Think of yourself as a top-tier athletic coach. You aren't the surgeon who fixes the torn ACL; you are the one on the sidelines teaching the athlete how to move more efficiently, build strength, and stay focused on the goal.

Action Item WITHIN Your Scope OUTSIDE Your Scope
Mental Health Teaching emotional regulation tools. Diagnosing ADHD, ODD, or Depression.
Behavior Modeling "Time-In" and redirection. Treating clinical behavioral disorders.
Family Dynamics Facilitating family meetings. Providing marriage counseling or mediation.
Health Encouraging sleep hygiene/nutrition. Prescribing supplements or medication.
Emma's Insight

The "I Wonder" Technique: If a client asks for a diagnosis, never say "I think your child has ADHD." Instead, stay in your scope by saying: "I notice these specific behaviors. I wonder if it would be helpful to share these observations with your pediatrician to rule out any underlying clinical needs while we work on our connection strategies here."

Legal Foundations & Liability

As a professional coach, you are entering a self-regulated industry, but that doesn't mean there aren't rules. To protect your growing business and your personal assets, you must adhere to three pillars of legal safety:

  • The Coaching Agreement: Every client must sign a contract that explicitly states you are providing educational coaching and not medical or psychological treatment.
  • Professional Liability Insurance: Even the best coaches can be misunderstood. We recommend all practitioners carry "Errors and Omissions" (E&O) insurance, which typically costs less than $200 per year.
  • Mandatory Reporting: While we value confidentiality, you are ethically (and often legally) bound to report suspected child abuse or neglect to the proper authorities.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks you if their 4-year-old has "Oppositional Defiant Disorder" based on their tantrums. What is the correct scope-compliant response?

You should state that you do not provide clinical diagnoses. Instead, focus on the observable behavior and suggest they consult a pediatrician for a clinical evaluation while you work together on de-escalation strategies.

2. What is the primary difference between the "Therapist" role and the "Coach" role?

Therapy often focuses on healing past trauma and clinical pathology (looking back). Coaching focuses on skill-building, goal-setting, and future-oriented action (looking forward).

3. True or False: Professional Liability Insurance is only necessary once you have more than 10 clients.

False. You should have insurance from the moment you take your first "Beta" or paid client to protect your personal assets and professional reputation.

4. What are the three items on your "7-Day Sprint" checklist?

1. Introduce yourself in your POD. 2. Block out your study schedule in your calendar. 3. Download the Module 1 Toolkit/Workbooks.
