1. The Power of Discovery Calls

The discovery call, sometimes called a consultation or clarity call, is arguably the most important sales tool in your aromatherapy practice. It's not just a chat; it's a structured conversation designed to understand a potential client's needs, determine if your services are a good fit, and ultimately, invite them to work with you. For many practitioners, including myself, it's the bridge between interest and enrollment.

A well-executed discovery call can lead to a conversion rate of 50-70% for qualified leads, significantly impacting your practice's growth (Smith & Jones, 2021). It allows you to build rapport, establish trust, and showcase your expertise before any financial commitment is made, making it invaluable for individuals who may feel skeptical or overwhelmed by past health experiences.

Coach Tip

Remember, the discovery call is a two-way street. You're not just selling; you're also assessing if this client is a good fit for you and your practice. It's okay to say no if you feel you can't genuinely help them or if their expectations don't align with your offerings. This protects your energy and the integrity of your practice.

3. Structuring Your Discovery Call

A clear structure ensures you cover all necessary points and guide the conversation effectively. Aim for 30 minutes, respecting both your time and the prospect's.

Phase 1: Build Rapport (5 minutes)

Your goal here is to create a warm, safe space. Start with genuine curiosity.

  • Opening: "Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
  • Connection: "That's wonderful that [mutual connection/platform] led you here. So Eleanor, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's been going on for you?"

Phase 2: Deep Dive into Needs & Desires (10 minutes)

This is where you listen more than you speak. Ask open-ended questions to uncover her pain points, desires, and underlying motivations.

  • Symptoms: "Tell me more about the bloating and digestive discomfort. When did it start? How does it affect your daily life?" (Listen for keywords, emotional impact).
  • Past Experiences: "And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't? What was your experience with other practitioners?" (Identifies past failures, builds empathy).
  • Vision: "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What does 'feeling like yourself again' truly mean to you?" (Connects to her deepest desires).

Coach Tip

Listen actively and take notes. Reflect back what you hear in your own words to show you're truly listening. Phrases like "So what I'm hearing is..." or "It sounds like you're feeling..." can be very powerful in building trust and making your prospect feel understood.

Phase 3: Introduce Your Solution (10 minutes)

Now, you articulate how your unique approach can address her specific challenges, without giving away all your intellectual property.

  • Summarize & Validate: "Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing chronic digestive issues and fatigue that steal your energy and joy, you've tried many things without lasting success, and what you truly desire is to feel comfortable, energetic, and understand your body better. Does that feel accurate?"
  • Your Unique Approach: "The approach I use is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes of your discomfort. Through a comprehensive holistic assessment, including detailed lifestyle analysis and targeted aromatherapy protocols, we identify exactly what's driving your symptoms. Then, we create a personalized plan — empowering you with tools like essential oil blends, dietary recommendations, and stress management techniques. Most of my clients, like you, start feeling significantly better within the first 4-6 weeks."
  • Client Success: "For example, I recently worked with a client, Maria, also in her 50s, who was struggling with similar fatigue and digestive issues. After 8 weeks, she reported a 60% reduction in bloating and felt she had enough energy to start gardening again."

Phase 4: Transition to the Offer & Close (5 minutes)

This is where you invite them to take the next step. It should feel natural and confident.

  • Check for Alignment: "So Eleanor, does this holistic, root-cause approach sound like what you've been looking for — a different path to finally feeling like yourself again?" (Wait for her response.)
  • The Invitation: "Great! Based on what you've shared, I believe I can absolutely help you achieve those goals. I'd love to tell you more about my signature 12-week 'Aromatherapy Renewal Program' and how we can get started."

Coach Tip

Your confidence in your ability to help is contagious. If you truly believe in your services, it will shine through. Don't be afraid to be enthusiastic about the transformation you can offer.

4. Navigating Common Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and a problem-solving mindset.

Objection Confident & Empathetic Response
"I need to think about it." "I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for your health." (This helps uncover the real objection.)
"It's too expensive. I've spent so much money already." "I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot without seeing the results you hoped for, which is incredibly frustrating. This program is an investment, yes, but it's an investment in a long-term solution, not a quick fix. My clients typically save money in the long run by reducing reliance on various supplements and quick fixes that don't address the root cause. Think about the cost of staying stuck — the missed joy, the energy drain. What would it be worth to you to truly feel like yourself again?"
"I've tried everything before, nothing works for me." "It sounds like you've had a really tough journey, Eleanor, and I completely empathize with that feeling of discouragement. What I've found is that many approaches focus on symptoms, or offer generic advice. My method is different because it's highly personalized, delving into your unique biochemistry and lifestyle to uncover the specific imbalances. We use targeted aromatherapy and holistic strategies tailored just for you. This isn't a one-size-fits-all approach; it's about finding your unique path to wellness."
"I need to talk to my spouse/partner." "Absolutely, that's a very wise decision, and I encourage you to discuss it. What specific questions do you think your spouse might have that I could help answer now? Perhaps we could even schedule a brief follow-up call with them if that would be helpful? I want to ensure you both feel fully comfortable and informed about this journey."

5. Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

When it's time to talk about your programs and pricing, do so with unwavering confidence. Remember, you are offering a solution to a significant problem, and your expertise is valuable.

Clarity is Key: Clearly outline what your program includes (e.g., number of sessions, length, specific deliverables like custom blends, personalized protocols, ongoing support). Then, state your price clearly and confidently. Avoid apologetic language or hesitation.

  • Example Presentation: "My signature program, the 'Aromatherapy Renewal Journey', is a 12-week personalized experience. It includes an initial 90-minute deep dive assessment, followed by five 60-minute one-on-one sessions, custom essential oil blends tailored to your needs, a personalized lifestyle and dietary guide, and unlimited email support between sessions. The investment for this comprehensive program is $2,497."
  • Payment Options: "I also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly installments of $850."
  • Reinforce Value: "This program is designed to not just alleviate your symptoms, but to empower you with lasting knowledge and tools for vibrant health, preventing future issues and giving you back your energy and joy."

6. Mastering the Close

The close is simply guiding your prospect to the next logical step. It should feel like a natural extension of the value you've already presented.

  • Direct Close: "So Eleanor, are you ready to embark on your Aromatherapy Renewal Journey and start feeling like yourself again?"
  • Choice Close: "Would you prefer to pay in full today and take advantage of the savings, or would the three-month payment plan work better for you?"
  • Assumptive Close: "Wonderful! I'll send over the enrollment agreement and payment link right after this call. What's the best email address for that?"
  • Next Steps Close: "The next step is to get you officially enrolled so we can schedule your initial 90-minute deep dive session. How does your calendar look for next week?"

Coach Tip

Silence is powerful. After you present your offer and ask for the sale, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the prospect think and respond. It can feel uncomfortable, but it's crucial for them to process the information.

8. Practice Scenario: Live Role-Play

Now it's your turn to practice! Below are some prompts to help you internalize the discovery call process. Find a practice partner, record yourself, or simply speak these responses out loud.

Check Your Understanding

1. Eleanor mentions, "I'm just so tired all the time, and the bloating is constant. It makes me dread social events." How would you reflect this back to her to show empathy and understanding?

"Eleanor, it sounds like this constant fatigue and bloating isn't just a physical discomfort, but it's really impacting your quality of life and even your social confidence. That must be incredibly frustrating."

2. Eleanor says, "My doctor just told me to eat more fiber and gave me a prescription for reflux, but it hasn't helped my energy or the bloating." How would you frame your unique aromatherapy approach as a solution?

"I hear you, Eleanor. It's common for conventional approaches to address individual symptoms. Our approach is different: we'd delve into the root causes of your fatigue and bloating, using a holistic assessment and personalized aromatherapy protocols to support your entire system — not just mask the symptoms. This is about lasting change."

3. After you present your $2,497 program, Eleanor hesitates and says, "That's a lot of money. I'm not sure I can afford that right now." How do you respond to this financial objection?

"I completely understand that this is a significant investment, Eleanor. Many of my clients felt similarly. However, they found that by addressing the root cause, they saved money in the long run by avoiding endless cycles of temporary fixes and unnecessary supplements. What would it be worth to you to gain back your energy and digestive comfort, and truly understand how to support your body for years to come? We also offer a payment plan to make it more accessible."

4. You've answered her questions and she seems ready. How would you confidently close the call and guide her to the next step?

"Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the Aromatherapy Renewal Journey is exactly what you need to achieve your goals. Are you ready to take this step and begin feeling like yourself again? If so, I can send you the enrollment link and we can schedule your first session."

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are essential for building rapport, assessing fit, and enrolling clients.
  • A structured 30-minute call guides the conversation from rapport to a clear call-to-action.
  • Active listening and empathy are crucial for understanding client needs and building trust.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reinforce value, not rejections.
  • Confidently articulate your program's value and pricing without hesitation.
  • Practice your script and objection handling to build confidence and fluency.
