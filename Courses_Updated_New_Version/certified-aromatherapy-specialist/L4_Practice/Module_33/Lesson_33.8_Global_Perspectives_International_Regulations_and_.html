Your Ideal Prospect: Rachel's Journey

Rachel, 47

Found you through a friend's referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Chronic fatigue, persistent weight issues (despite diet/exercise), feeling overwhelmed and unheard by conventional medicine.
Budget Concern
"I've spent so much money on doctors and supplements already. Is this really going to be different, or just another expense?"
Decision Style
Needs to feel deeply heard and understood. Skeptical of quick fixes or generic advice. Values genuine expertise, a personalized approach, and clear results.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel like myself again. I miss having energy to keep up with my kids and enjoy life. I want to feel lighter and healthier."

As you approach this call, remember Rachel's core desire: to be seen, heard, and to finally find a solution that works after years of frustration. Your role is to offer hope and a clear path forward.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Flow

This script provides a framework. Remember to listen more than you speak, allow for natural conversation, and adapt based on Rachel's responses.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Sarah! Yes, my friend [friend's name] recommended you. She's been raving about how much better she feels."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself, and it's always so rewarding to see her progress. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on that you're hoping I might be able to help with?"

Coach Tip: Active Listening is Key

In Phase 1, focus on making Rachel feel comfortable and heard. Ask open-ended questions and truly listen to her answers, not just waiting for your turn to speak. This builds trust and shows genuine care, which is invaluable for a career changer like yourself who values meaningful connections.

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned chronic fatigue and weight concerns. Could you tell me more about the fatigue? When did it start? How does it affect your daily life and what you can do?"
RACHEL:
"It started about five years ago, after my second child. I'm constantly tired, even after a full night's sleep. It makes it hard to focus at work, to exercise, and honestly, to enjoy time with my family. I just feel so heavy and sluggish all the time."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging, Rachel. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and what were your frustrations with those approaches?"
RACHEL:
"Oh, everything! Diets, cleanses, different doctors... they just tell me I'm stressed or need to try harder. I've taken supplements, but nothing really sticks. I feel like I'm just guessing."
YOU:
"I hear that frustration, Rachel. It's common to feel that way when conventional approaches don't address the underlying causes. Now, if nothing changes — if you continue on this path — where do you see yourself in a year? And conversely, if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What would you be able to do, feel, or experience that you can't now?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're exhausted, frustrated with generic advice, and longing for your energy and vitality back so you can fully engage in your life. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, exactly! You really get it."
YOU:
"The approach I use as an Aromatherapy Specialist is different from what you've tried before, Rachel. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes of your fatigue and weight concerns. We'd start with a comprehensive holistic assessment, diving deep into your lifestyle, diet, stress, and emotional well-being to identify the specific imbalances contributing to how you feel."
YOU:
"Then, we'd craft a highly personalized aromatherapy protocol, integrating therapeutic essential oils, dietary adjustments, and lifestyle shifts. I work with clients typically over 12 weeks to systematically address these root causes. Most of my clients, especially those struggling with chronic fatigue and hormonal imbalances, start feeling significantly better — more energy, better sleep, improved mood — within the first 4-6 weeks, and see sustainable changes by the end of our program."
YOU:
"My goal is to empower you with the knowledge and tools to not only reclaim your energy now but to maintain it long-term, so you never feel like you're just 'guessing' again. How does that sound to you?"
Phase 4: Close & Call to Action 5 min
RACHEL:
"That sounds amazing, Sarah. It's what I've been searching for."
YOU:
"So Rachel, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive, personalized support you've been looking for to finally get to the bottom of your fatigue and feel like yourself again?"
RACHEL:
"It really does."
YOU:
"Great! In that case, let me tell you about my signature 12-week 'Revitalize & Reclaim' program and how we can get started. This program is designed specifically for women like you who are ready to transform their energy and well-being..."

Handling Objections with Confidence

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your role isn't to convince, but to understand and address their concerns with empathy and clarity. Here are common objections and how to respond:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision about your health and well-being. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed choice." (This helps you uncover the real objection.)
"It's too expensive / I can't afford it."
"I hear you, Rachel, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. What they realized, though, is that continuing to feel exhausted and unwell, constantly searching for answers, also comes at a significant cost — to your energy, your joy, and even financially with all the things you've already tried. This program is an investment in finally getting lasting results. We can discuss payment plans, but first, let's ensure this is truly the right solution for you." (Reframe the cost, highlight the cost of inaction, and offer solutions.)
"I'm not sure if aromatherapy will actually work for me, I've tried so many things."
"That's a very valid concern, Rachel, especially given your past experiences. What makes my approach unique is that it's not a generic solution; it's deeply personalized. We don't just use essential oils; we integrate them into a holistic plan tailored to your body's specific needs, identified through a thorough assessment. We're addressing the root causes, not just symptoms. My clients often find that this comprehensive, individualized strategy is what finally brings them the results they've been seeking. Are you open to exploring a new, personalized path?" (Validate their concern, emphasize personalization and root cause, and reiterate the unique value.)
"I don't have enough time for a 12-week program."
"I completely understand that life gets busy, Rachel. The beautiful thing about this program is that it's designed to fit into your life, not overhaul it. We start with small, manageable steps — often just 10-15 minutes a day for your aromatherapy practices. Our sessions are weekly, but the real work happens in the small, consistent actions you take. What's more, by regaining your energy, you'll actually find you have MORE time and mental clarity for everything else. Many clients find that investing this time now saves them countless hours of struggle later. How does that sound?" (Acknowledge, reframe, highlight benefits, and emphasize small steps.)

Coach Tip: The Power of Empathy

Remember, objections are often an expression of fear or past disappointment. Approach them with empathy and understanding, not defensiveness. Your confidence in your process and your belief in their potential for transformation will shine through and be far more persuasive than any hard sell.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing

When it comes to discussing the investment for your programs, confidence is key. Frame your pricing as an investment in transformation, not just a cost. Always state your pricing clearly and then be silent, allowing the client to process.

Structuring Your Signature Program

For Rachel, you might present a 12-week signature program. A common structure involves:

  • Initial Comprehensive Assessment: 90-minute deep dive.
  • Follow-up Sessions: 10 bi-weekly 45-minute sessions.
  • Personalized Aromatherapy Protocol: Tailored essential oil blends and application guidance.
  • Lifestyle & Nutritional Guidance: Complementary recommendations.
  • Ongoing Support: Email or messaging support between sessions.
  • Educational Resources: Custom handouts, recipes, and resource library access.

Let's consider how you might present pricing for a program like this. For a 12-week program, a price point of $2,500 - $3,500 is common for certified specialists offering comprehensive support. You can also offer payment plans to make it more accessible.

Program Tier Investment Key Features
Revitalize & Reclaim (12-Week Signature Program) $2,997 (or 3 payments of $1,050) 1 Initial Assessment, 10 Follow-up Sessions, Personalized Protocols, Email Support, Resource Library.
Momentum Monthly (Ongoing Support) $497/month (after initial program) 2 monthly 30-minute sessions, advanced protocol adjustments, priority support.
Single Session Focus $297/session One 60-minute targeted session (typically not recommended for complex issues like Rachel's).

When you present the price, say it clearly: "The investment for the 12-week Revitalize & Reclaim program is $2,997. We also have a payment plan of three monthly installments of $1,050. Which option works best for you?" Then, pause and let them respond.

Mastering the Call to Action

The Call to Action (CTA) is not about pressuring, but about guiding. If you've done your job well — listened, empathized, and presented a clear solution — the CTA should feel like the natural next step for Rachel.

Practice These Closing Lines:

  1. "Based on everything we've discussed, Rachel, I truly believe my 'Revitalize & Reclaim' program is exactly what you need to get your energy back and feel like yourself again. Are you ready to take this next step together?"
  2. "You've shared how much you want to feel energetic and lighter. My program is designed to help you achieve that. What feels like the best way for you to move forward?"
  3. "Given your goals and my expertise in helping women like you, I'm confident we can create significant change. If you're ready to commit to yourself, I have a space open next week to begin your initial assessment. Shall we schedule that now?"
  4. "The next step would be to enroll in the 'Revitalize & Reclaim' program. I'll send you a link with all the details and the enrollment form. Do you have any final questions before I send that over?"

Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

After you deliver your pricing or your call to action, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the client speak first. This gives them space to process and voice any remaining concerns, which you can then address.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (Understand the Problem & Vision) in a discovery call?

The primary purpose of Phase 2 is to deeply understand the prospect's current challenges (pain points), their past attempts at solutions and frustrations, and their desired future state (vision). This phase is critical for building empathy and demonstrating that you truly understand their situation.

2. When a prospect raises the objection, "It's too expensive," what is the most effective initial response strategy?

The most effective initial response is to acknowledge their concern with empathy ("I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment") and then reframe the cost by highlighting the greater "cost" of inaction or continuing to struggle. Offering payment plans can also be a solution, but first, ensure they see the value of the transformation.

3. Why is it important to pause and remain silent after stating your program's investment during a discovery call?

Pausing after stating the investment allows the prospect time to process the information without feeling pressured. It gives them the opportunity to be the first to speak, which can reveal their true feelings or any underlying objections, rather than you guessing or prematurely addressing non-existent concerns.

4. What is a key characteristic of an effective Call to Action (CTA) in the context of an aromatherapy discovery call?

An effective CTA should feel like a natural, logical next step for the prospect, not a high-pressure sales tactic. It should clearly guide them towards enrollment, reiterate the benefits of working with you, and be delivered with confidence and belief in your ability to help them achieve their desired transformation.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a structured conversation designed to build rapport, understand needs, present your solution, and invite enrollment.
  • Active listening and empathy are your most powerful tools for uncovering core pain points and building trust.
  • Frame your aromatherapy approach as a unique, personalized solution that addresses root causes, not just symptoms.
  • Handle objections with grace, acknowledging concerns while confidently reiterating value and offering solutions.
  • Present your program's investment clearly and confidently, focusing on the transformative results your clients will achieve.
  • Practice your call structure and closing lines to build confidence, knowing that your expertise can truly change lives and build a fulfilling career.
