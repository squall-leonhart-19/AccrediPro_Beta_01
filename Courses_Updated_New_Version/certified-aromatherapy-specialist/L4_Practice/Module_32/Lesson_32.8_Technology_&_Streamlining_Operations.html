Eleanor Vance, 52

Former elementary school teacher, now part-time consultant. Found you via a local wellness group's Facebook page.

Presenting Situation: Eleanor is a highly organized individual who has struggled with persistent stress-related headaches and sleep disturbances for the past three years. She's tried various over-the-counter remedies, acupuncture, and even conventional sleep aids, but nothing has provided lasting relief. She feels "wired and tired" and is desperate for a natural, holistic solution.

Underlying Concerns: She's worried about the long-term effects of poor sleep and constant headaches on her cognitive function and overall well-being. She also expresses concern about the financial investment, having spent a lot on previous unsuccessful treatments.

Decision Style: Eleanor is analytical and needs to understand the "why" behind an approach. She values expertise, clear steps, and evidence-based methods. She's looking for a comprehensive plan, not just a quick fix.

Her Goal: "I just want to wake up feeling refreshed and get through a day without a pounding head. I miss feeling calm and in control."

Coach Sarah's Insight

Your ability to truly listen and reflect Eleanor's concerns back to her is paramount. It builds trust and shows her you understand her unique journey. Remember, she's not just seeking symptom relief; she's seeking a return to her vibrant self. Connect with that deeper desire.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Your goal is to make it a natural, empathetic conversation, not a rigid interrogation. Practice internalizing the flow, so you can adapt it genuinely to each unique individual.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 min)

YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my practice?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear you found me through the wellness group. So Eleanor, what made you decide to reach out now? What's been going on that you're looking for support with?"

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deepen the Pain (10 min)

This is where you ask open-ended questions and listen intently. Let her talk. Your role is to understand her reality.

YOU:
"Tell me more about these headaches and sleep issues. When did they start? How do they truly impact your daily life ‚Äì your work, your energy, your mood?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these? What worked, even a little, and what felt like a dead end?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year with these symptoms? And on the flip side, if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly resolve these issues, what would your life look and feel like?"

Phase 3: Present Your Unique Approach & Solution (10 min)

Once you understand her needs, it's time to briefly introduce how your unique Certified Aromatherapy Specialist‚Ñ¢ approach can help.

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing all of that. What I'm hearing is that you're exhausted by chronic headaches and restless nights, feeling 'wired and tired,' and you're ready for a holistic, lasting solution, not just another temporary fix. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe my AROMA Framework approach to addressing stress and sleep could be incredibly helpful for you. Unlike many conventional methods that only mask symptoms, we'll dive deep to identify the root causes of your discomfort. We'll work together over 12 weeks to create a personalized aromatherapy protocol tailored specifically to your body's needs, incorporating lifestyle adjustments and stress reduction techniques. My clients often report significant improvement in their sleep quality and a reduction in headache frequency within the first 4-6 weeks."
Coach Sarah's Insight

Notice how I used Eleanor's own words ("wired and tired") when summarizing her pain points. This validates her experience and shows you've been actively listening. Also, emphasize your unique framework (AROMA) and the process, not just products. This positions you as an expert, not just a seller of oils.

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

This is often the most challenging part for new practitioners. Remember, you are offering immense value, and your expertise is worth the investment. Practice this part out loud until it feels natural and confident.

Transitioning to Pricing

YOU:
"So Eleanor, does this holistic, root-cause approach sound like what you've been looking for to finally get relief from your headaches and improve your sleep?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, it really does. I'm tired of feeling this way."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. Let me tell you a bit about how we work together and what the investment looks like. My signature 12-week 'Rest & Rejuvenate' program is designed specifically for individuals like you, struggling with stress and sleep. It includes six one-on-one sessions, a personalized aromatherapy protocol with custom blends, a comprehensive lifestyle assessment, and unlimited email support. The investment for this transformative program is $1,800. Many clients choose our flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $650."

That program is $3,200, or six monthly payments of $550. Which of these sounds like it might be a better fit for where you are right now?"

Notice the confidence in stating the price. Don't waiver or apologize. Present it as an investment in her health. Offering a payment plan makes it more accessible. Always offer two options ‚Äì a primary one and a slightly higher one ‚Äì this often makes the primary option seem more reasonable.

Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They aren't rejections; they're requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"This sounds great, but I need to think about it."
YOU:
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and it's wise to consider it carefully. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure I've answered all your questions."
"I've spent so much money on things that haven't worked before. How is this different?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, that's a very fair concern, and I hear that frustration. Many of my clients come to me feeling exactly the same way. The key difference with my approach, and why I'm confident we can achieve results, is that we're not guessing. We're not just throwing remedies at symptoms. We're systematically identifying the root causes of your headaches and sleep issues using a comprehensive assessment and then creating a truly personalized plan. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution; it's a partnership to uncover what your unique body needs to heal. And I'm here to guide you every step of the way."
"It's a lot of money right now."
YOU:
"I completely appreciate that, Eleanor. Investing in your health is a significant decision. Let me ask you this: What is the cost of NOT addressing these headaches and sleep issues? What is the impact on your energy, your focus, your quality of life a year from now if nothing changes? My clients find that the investment pays for itself many times over in improved well-being, productivity, and peace of mind. Remember, we also have flexible payment options to make it more manageable."
"I'm not sure if I have the time for a 12-week program."
YOU:
"I understand you have a busy schedule, Eleanor, and that's a common concern. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life. The sessions are bi-weekly, and the daily aromatherapy practices are typically very quick ‚Äì often just a few minutes morning and night. We focus on sustainable habits, not overwhelming tasks. Think of it as investing a small amount of time now to reclaim significant time and energy in the future, free from headaches and fatigue. How much time are you currently losing due to poor sleep or pain?"

Practicing the Close: Your Call to Action

After addressing objections, it's time to guide your prospect to the next step. Confidence and clarity are key.

The Confident Close

YOU:
"Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the 'Rest & Rejuvenate' program is exactly what you need to finally get relief from your headaches and improve your sleep. Are you ready to take this important step towards feeling like yourself again?"
IF YES:
"Fantastic! I'm so excited for you. I'll send you an email right after this call with a link to enroll and schedule your first session. We can begin as early as next week. Do you have any questions about the enrollment process?"
IF STILL HESITANT:
"I understand. What feels like the biggest hurdle for you right now? Perhaps we can schedule a quick 15-minute follow-up call in a couple of days to discuss any remaining questions once you've had time to process. How does that sound?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary objective of Phase 2 of the Discovery Call (Understand the Problem)?

The primary objective is to actively listen and uncover the prospect's core pain points, desires, and the emotional impact of their health challenges. This phase focuses on deep empathy and understanding their unique story.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the recommended first response to uncover their true concern?

The recommended first response is to empathize and then gently inquire: "I completely understand. This is an important decision. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps something else that's giving you pause?"

3. Sarah suggests offering two program options during pricing. What is the strategic benefit of this approach?

Offering two program options (a primary and a slightly higher-tier one) strategically makes the primary option seem more reasonable and manageable to the prospect. It also caters to different levels of commitment and financial capacity.

4. According to the income potential table, how many active clients (on a 12-week program, 3 monthly payments) would a "Thriving Practice" typically have to achieve a monthly income of $6,000?

A "Thriving Practice" would typically have 10 active clients per month to achieve a monthly income of $6,000 (assuming an average program price of $1,800 and three monthly payments of $600 per client).

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is your opportunity to build rapport and deeply understand a prospect's needs before presenting your solution.
  • Practice active listening and ask open-ended questions to uncover emotional pain points and desired outcomes.
  • Confidently present your programs as a transformative investment in their health, emphasizing your unique framework and personalized approach.
  • Handle objections with empathy and clear information, viewing them as requests for more understanding, not rejections.
  • Always conclude with a clear call to action, guiding the prospect to the next step in their journey with you.
  • Consistent client acquisition can lead to significant financial freedom and a deeply rewarding practice.
