The Strategic Importance of Your Discovery Call

Think of the discovery call not as a sales pitch, but as a genuine conversation. It's your opportunity to truly listen, understand a potential client's challenges, and determine if your specialized aromatherapy programs are the right fit. For someone like you, a career changer often driven by a desire for meaningful work, this call is where you build trust and demonstrate your unique value.

A well-executed discovery call can significantly increase your client conversion rates. Research indicates that wellness practitioners who use a structured, empathetic discovery call process can see conversion rates of 40-60%, compared to 10-20% for those who rely solely on brochures or passive inquiries (Wellness Business Magazine, 2022). This isn't just about numbers; it's about connecting with individuals who genuinely need your help and are ready to invest in their well-being.

Coach Tip

Remember, the goal of a discovery call is not to diagnose or provide a mini-consultation. It's to understand their needs, share your framework, and invite them into a deeper working relationship. Focus on listening more than talking.

Eleanor, 52

Found you through a local wellness event where you gave a talk on stress management. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Former high school teacher, recently retired. Experiencing chronic stress, difficulty sleeping, feeling overwhelmed and "lost" without her routine. Tried meditation apps and over-the-counter sleep aids with limited success.

Her Goal
"I just want to feel calm again, sleep through the night, and find a new sense of purpose. I miss feeling balanced."

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Here's a proven structure to guide your conversation with Eleanor. Remember, this is a guide, not a rigid script. Your authenticity and empathy are your greatest assets.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Eleanor

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It was a pleasure meeting you at the wellness event. How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear! So, Eleanor, tell meâ€”what prompted you to schedule this call today? What's been on your mind regarding your well-being?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Client Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling stressed and having trouble sleeping. Can you tell me more about that? When did these challenges start, and how are they impacting your daily life, especially now that you're retired?"
YOU:
"I understand. It sounds like a significant shift. What strategies or remedies have you explored so far to address your stress and sleep issues? What worked, and what didn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"If we fast forward a year from now, and nothing has changed with your stress and sleep, how would that make you feel? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel balanced, energized, and rested, what would your ideal day look like?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on everything you've shared, what I'm hearing is that you're seeking a natural, sustainable way to manage stress, improve your sleep quality, and regain a sense of balance and purpose in your new phase of life. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Aromatherapy Specialist™ is different from quick fixes. We don't just mask symptoms; we delve into your unique physiological and emotional landscape to identify the root causes of your imbalances. My 12-week program, 'AromaReset for Retirement,' is designed specifically for women like you, helping you create personalized aromatherapy protocols, integrate mindful practices, and establish routines that support deep rest and emotional resilience. Clients typically begin to experience noticeable improvements in sleep and stress levels within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: The Confident Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, does this structured, personalized approach sound like what you've been searching for to finally achieve the calm and restful nights you desire?"
YOU:
"Wonderful! If you feel this is a good fit, I'd love to share more about the 'AromaReset for Retirement' program and how we can get started on your journey to feeling balanced and vibrant again. Shall I walk you through the program details and investment?"

Coach Tip

Active listening is paramount. Nod, make eye contact (if video call), and summarize their points frequently. This shows you're engaged and truly understand their pain, building immense trust and rapport.

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

This is where many new practitioners stumble. Remember, you're offering a transformation, not just bottles of essential oils. Your certification signifies deep expertise, and your programs provide structured support for lasting change. When discussing pricing, focus on the value and the outcomes Eleanor will achieve.

For a comprehensive program like "AromaReset for Retirement," which might include 1:1 sessions, personalized blend formulations, educational resources, and ongoing support, a common price range for a 12-week program is $1500 - $3500. You might offer payment plans to make it more accessible, but always state the full investment first.

Example Pricing Presentation:

"Eleanor, my 'AromaReset for Retirement' program is a 12-week journey designed to help you regain your calm, improve your sleep, and find balance. It includes six 60-minute personalized aromatherapy consultations, custom essential oil blends tailored to your unique needs, a comprehensive digital resource library, and unlimited email support between sessions. The full investment for this transformative program is $2497. I also offer a convenient three-month payment plan of $850 per month, if that would make it easier for you to get started."

Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud, in front of a mirror, until it feels completely natural. Your confidence in your value will translate into your client's confidence in investing in themselves through your program.

Gracefully Overcoming Common Objections

Eleanor's concerns about budget and skepticism are common. Anticipating and preparing for these objections will empower you to respond with empathy and conviction. Frame objections not as rejections, but as opportunities to provide more clarity and reassurance.

Common Objection Empathetic Response Strategy
"It's too expensive. I'm on a fixed income now." "I completely understand, Eleanor, and I appreciate your honesty. This is an investment in your long-term well-being. Think about the cost of continued sleepless nights, the impact of chronic stress on your health, and the missed joy of retirement. My clients often find that the lasting relief and tools they gain far outweigh the initial investment. We can discuss payment options that might ease the financial commitment."
"I need to think about it." "Absolutely, Eleanor, this is an important decision. To help you reflect, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have lingering questions about the program itself? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked." "I hear your frustration, Eleanor, and it's completely valid. Many of my clients come to me feeling the same way. What makes this different is my personalized, holistic approach as a Certified Aromatherapy Specialist™. We're not just trying a single remedy; we're building a comprehensive, evidence-informed protocol tailored specifically to your body's unique needs, addressing the underlying factors of your stress and sleep issues. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution."
"I'm not sure if aromatherapy can help with *my* specific issues." "That's a fair question, Eleanor. While aromatherapy is incredibly versatile, my role is to ensure it's the right fit for you. Based on what you've shared about your stress, sleep patterns, and desire for natural solutions, I am confident that a targeted aromatherapy program, combined with supportive lifestyle practices, can offer significant relief and promote deep healing. My certification has equipped me with advanced knowledge in nervous system support and stress modulation through essential oils."

Coach Tip

An objection is often just a request for more information or reassurance. Don't take it personally. Listen carefully to the underlying concern and respond with empathy, clarity, and confidence in your program's ability to deliver results.

The Art of the Confident Call-to-Action

After presenting your program and addressing any questions or objections, it's crucial to guide Eleanor to the next step. Ambiguity kills conversions. Be clear, direct, and confident in inviting her to move forward.

Practice Call-to-Action Lines:

  • "Eleanor, I truly believe my 'AromaReset for Retirement' program is exactly what you need to reclaim your peace and restorative sleep. How would you like to move forward today?"
  • "Given everything we've discussed, if you're ready to commit to transforming your well-being, I recommend we reserve your spot in the program. We can schedule your first session right now."
  • "Does this sound like the path you're ready to embark on? If so, I can send you the enrollment link immediately, and we can set up our initial deep-dive session."

Understanding Your Income Potential

One of the most empowering aspects of becoming a Certified Aromatherapy Specialist™ is the potential for financial freedom and flexibility. Let's look at some realistic income scenarios based on the typical program pricing we discussed (e.g., a 12-week program at $2497).

Realistic Monthly Income Scenarios:

  • Scenario 1: Part-Time Practice (2 new clients/month)
    • If your average program price is $2497, securing just two new clients per month would generate approximately $4,994/month in revenue. This is highly achievable for a part-time practitioner, allowing for significant supplemental income or a comfortable transition from a previous career.
  • Scenario 2: Growing Full-Time Practice (5 new clients/month)
    • With five new clients per month, your monthly revenue could reach around $12,485/month. This represents a solid full-time income, offering substantial financial independence and the ability to scale your offerings. Many practitioners achieve this level within their first 1-3 years.
  • Scenario 3: Thriving Practice (10 new clients/month)
    • For a well-established or rapidly growing practice, ten new clients monthly could generate $24,970/month or more. This level often involves leveraging group programs, workshops, or hiring support staff, allowing you to amplify your impact and earnings.

Remember, these figures are based on program enrollment. Many practitioners also offer complementary services, sell products, or host workshops, further diversifying their income streams. A 2023 industry survey of wellness coaches found that certified practitioners with structured programs reported average annual earnings between $60,000 and $150,000, with top earners exceeding $200,000 (Global Wellness Institute, 2023).

Coach Tip

Start with a realistic goal, perhaps 1-2 clients per month, and build from there. Success isn't about instant millions; it's about consistent, strategic action and providing exceptional value. Your confidence will grow with every client you serve.

Now it's your turn to put it all into practice. Find a trusted friend, family member, or fellow student to role-play with. Use Eleanor's profile and the script above as a foundation. Focus on:

  • Your Tone: Warm, empathetic, and professional.

  • Objection Handling: Practice the responses we discussed.

Record yourself if possible and listen back. Notice where you sound confident and where you might hesitate. This self-reflection is invaluable for growth!

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of a discovery call, according to Sarah's coaching?

The primary goal is to build trust, genuinely listen to the potential client's challenges, understand their needs, and determine if your aromatherapy program is the right fit, rather than just making a sale or giving a mini-consultation.

2. When Eleanor expresses concern about her budget, what is the recommended strategy for addressing this objection?

Acknowledge and validate her concern ("I completely understand..."), then reframe the investment in terms of the long-term value and transformation she will receive. Offer to discuss flexible payment options.

3. What is a key difference between your aromatherapy program and the "quick fixes" Eleanor might have tried before?

Your program offers a personalized, holistic, and evidence-informed approach that delves into root causes, rather than just masking symptoms or applying a one-size-fits-all solution. It's a structured journey for lasting change.

4. Sarah emphasizes the importance of a clear call-to-action. Why is ambiguity detrimental at the close of a discovery call?

Ambiguity can lead to hesitation, confusion, and ultimately, lost opportunities. A clear, confident call-to-action guides the client to the next logical step, making it easy for them to commit and preventing them from getting stuck in indecision.
