Rachel, 47

Background: Elementary school teacher, busy mother of two. Has tried various self-help books and meditation apps for stress. Found your website through a local wellness fair. Has an upcoming discovery call.

Presenting Symptoms/Concerns: Rachel is constantly overwhelmed and exhausted. She struggles to "switch off" at night, leading to restless sleep and daytime fatigue. She feels a constant pressure to be "on" for her students and family, leaving little time for herself. She's looking for a natural, sustainable way to manage stress and improve her sleep quality.

Motivation for a Group Program: Rachel is intrigued by the idea of a group program because she feels isolated in her struggles and believes a community aspect could provide accountability and shared learning. Understanding the Group Program Discovery Call

A discovery call for a group program shares many similarities with a one-on-one intake, but it has distinct nuances. Your goal isn't just to see if the client is a good fit for *your* services, but also if they're a good fit for the group dynamic and structure of your program.

The "Why" Behind Discovery Calls for Groups

While you might think group programs are easier to sell without individual calls, they are crucial for several reasons:

  • Qualification: Ensures the prospective client's needs align with what the group program offers.
  • Personal Connection: Even in a group, people want to feel seen. A brief personal connection builds trust.
  • Addressing Specific Concerns: Allows you to pre-emptively address unique questions about group dynamics, time commitment, or privacy.
  • Building Community from the Start: You can hint at the supportive environment and identify potential leaders or highly engaged members.
  • Value Articulation: It's an opportunity to clearly differentiate your group program and highlight its unique benefits, especially the collective learning and support.

Key Differences from 1:1 Calls

When selling a group program, your discovery call shifts focus slightly:

  • Emphasis on Community: Highlight the benefits of shared experience, peer support, and diverse perspectives.
  • Structured Curriculum: Emphasize the clear, progressive learning path and the specific outcomes the group will collectively achieve.
  • Program Fit vs. Individual Fit: While individual needs are important, ensure the client understands and values the group format and curriculum.
  • Managing Expectations: Be clear about the level of individual attention they will receive versus what is expected in a one-on-one setting.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Remember, your energy is contagious! If you're excited and confident about your group program, that enthusiasm will naturally transfer to your prospective clients. Frame the group experience as a journey they won't have to take alone, with you as their expert guide.

Structuring Your Group Program Discovery Call

Let's walk through a 30-minute call with Rachel, applying the principles of effective discovery calls to a group program context. This script provides a framework; adapt it to your authentic voice.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

1

Phase 1: Building Rapport & Setting the Stage (5 min)

YOU: "Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my 'Aromatherapy for Stress & Sleep' program?"

YOU: "That's wonderful! I'm so glad the wellness fair sparked your interest. So Rachel, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on that has you considering a group program like this?"

2

Phase 2: Uncovering Group-Specific Needs & Desires (10 min)

YOU: "Tell me more about the stress and sleep challenges you're experiencing. When did they start? How do they impact your daily life, especially as a busy teacher and mom?"

YOU: "And what have you tried so far to manage this? What worked, what didn't? And what makes you feel like a group program might be a good next step for you?"

YOU: "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly master your stress and sleep, what would your life look like? What kind of support would help you get there?"

(Listen for keywords like 'community,' 'accountability,' 'learning together,' 'structured approach.')

Coach Tip from Sarah

During Phase 2, actively listen for cues that indicate a desire for group support. Rachel specifically mentioned feeling isolated and wanting a community. Echo these back to her later when you present your program to show you truly heard her needs.

3

Phase 3: Presenting Your Group Program Solution (10 min)

YOU: "Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're feeling overwhelmed and exhausted, struggling with sleep, and you're looking for natural, sustainable solutions with the added benefit of a supportive community. Does that feel accurate?"

YOU: "My 'Aromatherapy for Stress & Sleep' group program is designed exactly for women like you. Over 8 weeks, we meet weekly as a small, supportive group of 8-12 women. We'll dive deep into specific aromatherapy protocols for stress reduction, sleep enhancement, and emotional balance. You'll learn how to blend your own remedies, create calming rituals, and connect with peers who share similar experiences. Most of my past participants report significant improvements in sleep quality and a noticeable reduction in daily stress within the first few weeks, often feeling a renewed sense of energy and control."

YOU: "The power of this program lies in the shared journey. You'll not only learn from me, but also from the experiences and insights of other women, creating a truly enriching and accountable environment. We have dedicated Q&A time in each session, and a private online forum for ongoing support."

Coach Tip from Sarah

When you summarize their pain points, use their exact words where possible. This demonstrates deep listening and makes them feel truly understood. Then, pivot directly into how your group program is the perfect solution for those specific problems, highlighting the unique group benefits.

4

Phase 4: Confident Closing & Handling Objections (5 min)

YOU: "So Rachel, does this sound like the kind of supportive, structured program you've been looking for to finally get control over your stress and sleep?"

(Pause and listen for her response. If positive, proceed to pricing.)

Confident Pricing Presentation for Group Programs

This is often where practitioners hesitate. Remember, you're offering immense value. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident.

Presenting the Investment

YOU: "Fantastic! I'm thrilled this resonates with you. The investment for the 8-week 'Aromatherapy for Stress & Sleep' group program, including all live weekly sessions, a comprehensive digital workbook, access to our private community forum, and a curated essential oil starter kit, is $597. We also offer a flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $210, making it more accessible."

YOU: "When you break that down, it's less than the cost of a daily coffee for 8 weeks, yet it provides lifelong tools for managing your well-being, plus the incredible support of a community. Many of my clients find this investment pays for itself multiple times over in improved health, energy, and peace of mind."

(Pause. Let the number land. Do not fill the silence.)

YOU: "How does that sound to you, Rachel?"

Coach Tip from Sarah

When stating your price, use a clear, even tone. Avoid apologizing or rushing. Follow it with a brief explanation of the value and then, most importantly, a question that invites a response. This allows the prospect to voice any immediate concerns or objections.

Handling Common Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Be prepared with confident, empathetic responses.

Common Objection Your Confident Response (Practice Out Loud!)
"I need to think about it." "I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"I'm not sure I have time for a group program with my schedule." "I hear you, Rachel, and many busy women share that concern. The program is designed to be flexible, with recordings of all live sessions available. Think of this as carving out dedicated 'me time' that will actually give you MORE energy and clarity in the long run. What small shift could you make to prioritize this investment in yourself?"
"Will I really get personalized attention in a group setting?" "That's a valid question. While it's not one-on-one, I've structured the program to ensure you feel supported. Each live session includes dedicated Q&A time, and our private online forum allows for individual questions and guidance from me throughout the week. Plus, the shared experiences within the group often provide insights you might not get in a solo setting."
"The investment is a bit high for me right now." "I appreciate your honesty, Rachel. I understand that investing in yourself is a significant decision. Consider the cost of NOT addressing your stress and sleep ‚Äì the impact on your energy, mood, and overall well-being. We do have a payment plan available to spread out the cost. What would feel more manageable for you, or what would need to shift for this to feel like a truly worthwhile investment for your health?"

Reflect and Practice

Record yourself practicing these responses. Listen back to your tone and confidence. The more you practice, the more natural and authoritative you'll sound. Your conviction in your program's value is your strongest asset.

Income Potential: Thriving with Group Programs

Group programs are a fantastic way to leverage your expertise, serve more clients, and create a sustainable income stream without burning out on one-on-one sessions. Let's look at some realistic income scenarios.

Scaling Your Aromatherapy Practice

Imagine your 8-week "Aromatherapy for Stress & Sleep" group program, priced at $597 per participant. Sarah shares her experience:

  • Starting Small: "When I first launched, I aimed for a small group. Enrolling just 2 participants generated an immediate income of $1,194. This was a fantastic confidence booster and covered my initial material costs."
  • Building Momentum: "As I refined my marketing and saw testimonials roll in, enrolling 5 participants became my next goal. That's $2,985 for one group program! This allowed me to reinvest in my business and offer more."
  • Thriving Practice: "Now, with established programs and a strong reputation, I consistently enroll 10-12 participants per group. A group of 10 participants brings in $5,970 from a single program run. Running 2-3 such groups annually significantly boosts my annual revenue, freeing up time for other ventures or deeper dives with private clients."

Consider the time efficiency: you deliver content once to many, rather than repeating it in individual sessions. This dramatically increases your hourly earning potential.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Don't be afraid to start small with your first group. The experience, testimonials, and confidence you gain from even 2-3 participants are invaluable. Each successful group builds your reputation and makes subsequent enrollments easier. Focus on delivering exceptional value, and the income will follow.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is a primary objective of a discovery call specifically for a group program, beyond just client qualification?

A primary objective is to assess if the prospective client is a good fit for the group dynamic and structure, and to highlight the benefits of shared learning and community support. It's about collective transformation as much as individual needs.

2. When presenting your group program's value, what key elements should you emphasize that differentiate it from one-on-one coaching?

Emphasize the community aspect (peer support, shared experiences), the structured curriculum (clear learning path, collective outcomes), and the accountability that comes from a group journey.

3. Rachel expresses concern about not having enough time for a group program. How might you reframe this objection effectively?

You could reframe it by acknowledging her busy schedule ("I hear you, many busy women share that concern") and then positioning the program as a dedicated investment in her well-being that will ultimately give her more energy and clarity, with flexible options like recorded sessions.

4. If your 8-week group program is $597 per participant, and you successfully enroll 7 clients, what is the total revenue generated from that single group?

Total revenue would be $597 (price per participant) × 7 (participants) = $4,179.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls for group programs are essential for qualifying participants and building initial connection.
  • Structure your calls to build rapport, uncover group-specific needs, present your solution, and confidently close.
  • Highlight the unique benefits of group dynamics: community, shared learning, and accountability.
  • Practice confident pricing presentation, clearly articulating value and offering payment flexibility.
  • Prepare empathetic responses to common objections, viewing them as opportunities for clarification.
  • Embrace group programs to scale your impact, serve more clients, and create a robust, sustainable income.
