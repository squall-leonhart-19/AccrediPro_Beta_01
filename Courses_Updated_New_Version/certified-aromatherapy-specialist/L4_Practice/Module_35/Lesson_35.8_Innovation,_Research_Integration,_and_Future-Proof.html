The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework for your 30-minute discovery call with Eleanor. Remember, this is a conversation, not an interrogation. Listen actively, empathize, and guide her towards understanding how you can help.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure: Building Trust & Value

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. How did you hear about my practice?"
YOU:
"That's fantastic that [friend's name] referred you. She's made incredible progress herself! So, Eleanor, to start, I'd love to hear in your own words what brought you here today. What's currently going on with your health and well-being?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her Challenges & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned chronic stress and sleep issues. Can you tell me more about how these affect your daily life? For example, what does a typical night of sleep look like for you?"
ELEANOR:
"It's awful. I lie awake for hours, my mind racing about school, my to-do list. Then I wake up multiple times. I'm exhausted by noon, and sometimes I snap at my students. I just can't focus."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly draining, Eleanor. I hear you. What solutions have you explored so far to address these challenges?"
ELEANOR:
"I've tried melatonin, herbal teas, meditation apps... they help a little for a night or two, but it never sticks. My doctor just suggested stronger medication, but I don't want to rely on that."
YOU:
"I completely understand your hesitation with medication. If we fast forward six months, and nothing about your stress or sleep changes, what does that future look like for you? And on the flip side, if we could wave a magic wand and truly resolve these issues, what would your ideal day, your ideal life, feel like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a dedicated teacher struggling with persistent stress and sleep deprivation, leading to exhaustion and difficulty focusing. You've tried various short-term solutions but are looking for something that addresses the root cause and offers sustainable relief, ideally without relying on medication. Does that resonate with your experience?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, exactly. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Wonderful. The approach I use as a Certified Aromatherapy Specialist is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just masking symptoms, we delve into understanding your unique physiological and emotional landscape to pinpoint the specific stressors and imbalances contributing to your sleep and stress issues. I work with clients like you through a personalized 12-week program, where we create bespoke aromatic blends, integrate targeted aromatherapy practices, and develop holistic lifestyle strategies. This isn't about quick fixes; it's about empowering you with tools and knowledge for lasting well-being. Most clients report significant improvements in sleep quality and a noticeable reduction in stress within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"My program includes in-depth consultations, customized essential oil formulations delivered to your door, guided mindfulness practices with aromatherapy, and ongoing support. It's designed to bring your nervous system back into balance, calm your mind, and restore your natural sleep rhythms."
Phase 4: Close with a Clear Call-to-Action 5 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, does this structured, personalized approach to addressing your stress and sleep sound like what you've been looking for?"
ELEANOR:
"It sounds promising, yes. It's comprehensive."
YOU:
"Excellent! Then the next step would be for us to begin your journey with my Signature 12-Week Stress & Sleep Reset Program. Let me share how we can get started and what the investment looks like."

Coach Tip from Sarah

Remember, the discovery call isn't about selling, it's about qualifying and educating. Your role is to determine if your program is the right fit for their needs and to help them see the value. Be genuinely curious and empathetic, and let their answers guide the conversation. Your confidence in your expertise will shine through.

Confident Objection Handling

It's natural for prospects to have questions or concerns before making a commitment. Your ability to address these calmly and effectively is key to building trust and moving forward. Here are common objections and strategic responses:

Presenting Your Signature Offer & Pricing

After addressing any initial concerns, it's time to clearly and confidently present your signature program and its investment. Speak with conviction about the value you provide.

Presenting Your Signature 12-Week Stress & Sleep Reset Program

YOU:
"Eleanor, my Signature 12-Week Stress & Sleep Reset Program is designed to guide you from feeling overwhelmed and sleep-deprived to experiencing profound calm, improved focus, and restorative sleep. It includes:
  • A comprehensive initial assessment and personalized aromatherapy protocol.
  • Three custom-blended essential oil formulations, delivered to your home (one every 4 weeks).
  • Six bi-weekly 60-minute one-on-one virtual coaching sessions.
  • Guided aromatherapy mindfulness exercises and relaxation techniques.
  • Ongoing email support between sessions.
  • Access to my exclusive client resource library.
The total investment for this transformative 12-week journey is $2,497. For your convenience, we also offer a flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $897."

Coach Tip from Sarah

When stating your price, state it clearly and then stop talking. Let the number hang in the air. Don't immediately jump to justifications or discounts. Your confidence in your value is contagious.

Practice Your Call-to-Action

After stating the price, pause. Then, use one of these closing lines to invite her to take the next step. Practice them until they feel natural:

  • "So Eleanor, are you ready to finally experience the deep calm and restorative sleep you deserve?"
  • "Given what we've discussed, Eleanor, does this sound like the comprehensive solution you've been searching for to reclaim your well-being?"
  • "What feels like the best next step for you, Eleanor, to get started on your journey to better sleep and less stress?"
  • "I'd be thrilled to support you in achieving these goals. How would you like to move forward?"

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1

During the discovery call, what is the primary goal of Phase 2: "Deep Dive into Her Challenges & Desires"?

The primary goal is to actively listen, ask open-ended questions, and empathetically uncover the prospect's pain points, past attempts at solutions, and their deepest desires for transformation. This helps you understand their needs beyond surface-level symptoms.
2

Eleanor expresses, "I've tried so many things, and nothing has worked. How is this different?" What is the most effective way to respond to this objection, according to the lesson?

The most effective response is to acknowledge her concern, then highlight the unique, holistic, and individualized nature of your approach. Emphasize that you delve into root causes and create bespoke plans, differentiating it from generic solutions she may have tried.
3

When presenting your program's investment, what critical action should you take immediately after stating the price?

Immediately after stating the price, you should pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect to process the information without you undermining your own value by rushing to justify or offer discounts.
4

Which of the following is NOT a recommended component of a strong call-to-action after presenting your offer?

  1. Asking, "Are you ready to commit now?"
  2. Asking, "What feels like the best next step for you?"
  3. Inviting them to confirm if the solution resonates with their needs.
  4. Stating, "How would you like to move forward?"
The answer is A. Asking "Are you ready to commit now?" can be too pushy and may put the prospect on the defensive. The other options are open-ended and invite the prospect to consider the next steps in a collaborative way.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call focuses on building rapport, deeply understanding client needs, and clearly articulating your unique value.
  • Practice active listening and empathetic questioning to make clients feel heard and understood, which builds trust.
  • Your signature program should address root causes and offer a clear path to transformation, differentiating you from generic solutions.
  • Confident objection handling involves acknowledging concerns, validating feelings, and strategically re-framing the value of your services.
  • When discussing pricing, state your fee clearly and then pause for the client to process, demonstrating confidence in your value.
  • Real-world income potential for Certified Aromatherapy Specialists is substantial, offering both financial freedom and meaningful impact.
