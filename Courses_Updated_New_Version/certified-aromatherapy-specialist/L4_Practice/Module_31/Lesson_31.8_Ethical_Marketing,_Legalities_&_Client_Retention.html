Understanding the Discovery Call

The discovery call is a strategic, free consultation designed to establish rapport, deeply understand a prospective client's health goals and challenges, and determine if your services are the right fit. It's not a sales pitch, but a compassionate conversation aimed at uncovering how you can best serve them.

Why Discovery Calls are Crucial

  • Build Trust: It's the first real human connection, allowing you to establish credibility and empathy.
  • Qualify Prospects: You assess if their needs align with your expertise and if they are genuinely ready for commitment.
  • Educate & Inspire: You introduce your unique approach and demonstrate the potential for transformation.
  • Overcome Objections Proactively: Many concerns can be addressed before they become barriers.
  • Differentiate Yourself: In a crowded market, a personalized conversation sets you apart.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Think of the discovery call as a first date! Your goal isn't to get married, but to see if there's enough connection and shared vision for a second date (your program). Be genuinely curious, listen more than you speak, and let your passion for helping others shine through. Authenticity is your most powerful tool.

Setting the Stage: Pre-Call Preparation

Before every call, take a few minutes to prepare:

  1. Review Intake Form: What did they share? Look for clues about their primary concerns and motivations.
  2. Clear Your Space: Ensure you have a quiet, professional environment, free from distractions.
  3. Mental Preparation: Take a deep breath. Center yourself. Remember your intention: to serve.
  4. Have Your Calendar Ready: Be prepared to schedule the next step immediately.
  5. Know Your Pricing: Be clear and confident about your program structure and investment.

Navigating Objections with Grace and Confidence

It's natural for prospects to have concerns. Your role isn't to push, but to listen and address those concerns with empathy and clarity. Here are common objections Eleanor might raise, and how to respond:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"This sounds great, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some questions about the process itself? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"The investment is more than I expected/I've spent so much money already."
"I appreciate your honesty, Eleanor. Investing in your health is a significant decision, especially when you've tried many things without success. Many of my clients felt the same way. What they found, however, was that by addressing the root causes and creating sustainable changes, they saved money in the long run on remedies that didn't work and gained back invaluable energy and peace of mind. What would it be worth to you to finally sleep soundly, feel balanced, and enjoy your grandkids without constant hot flashes?"
"I'm worried about the time commitment. My schedule is already so full."
"That's a very valid concern, Eleanor. Many of my clients have incredibly busy lives. The 'Harmonious Hormones' program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your routine. Our sessions are structured and efficient, and the protocols are practical. We start with small, manageable steps to ensure you don't feel overwhelmed. In fact, many clients find that as their energy improves, they actually gain time back because they're more efficient and less drained. How much time per week do you feel you could realistically dedicate to feeling better?"
"I'm just not sure aromatherapy can really help with something like menopause."
"That's a common question, Eleanor, and I'm glad you asked. While conventional approaches often focus on symptom suppression, aromatherapy, when used therapeutically and holistically, can be incredibly supportive for hormonal balance and symptom management. Essential oils like Clary Sage, Geranium, and Peppermint have well-documented properties that can help regulate hormone levels, reduce hot flashes, and improve sleep quality. For instance, a 2017 meta-analysis published in Complementary Therapies in Clinical Practice showed significant positive effects of aromatherapy on menopausal symptoms. My approach combines these powerful botanical tools with lifestyle strategies for a truly comprehensive impact. We're not just masking symptoms; we're supporting your body's innate ability to find balance."

Check Your Understanding

1

During Phase 2 of the discovery call, what is the primary objective when asking questions like, "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year?"

The primary objective is to help the prospect articulate their pain points more deeply and visualize the negative consequences of inaction. This builds urgency and highlights the value of your solution by showing the contrast with their desired future.
2

Eleanor expresses concern about the program's investment, stating she's "hesitant to spend more unless it's truly effective." How should you best address this objection?

Address the underlying concern of past disappointments by empathizing with her experience. Reframe the investment as a solution to long-term issues, emphasizing the sustainable results and the value of regaining health and vitality, rather than just the dollar amount.
3

When presenting your program's pricing, what is a critical non-verbal technique to employ immediately after stating the investment amount?

It is critical to pause and remain silent after stating the investment. This allows the prospect to process the information without feeling pressured, and it communicates your confidence in the value of your offering. Avoid immediately justifying the price.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a structured, empathetic conversation crucial for building trust and qualifying prospects.

  • Follow a 4-phase structure: Rapport, Problem Deep Dive, Solution Presentation, and Confident Close.

  • Active listening and asking powerful questions (e.g., "magic wand" questions) uncover deeper motivations.

  • Address objections by empathizing, reframing, and validating concerns, always linking back to their goals.

  • Present your program and its investment with unwavering confidence, followed by a clear, practiced call-to-action.

  • Your aromatherapy practice has significant income potential, achievable through consistent client acquisition and value-driven services.
