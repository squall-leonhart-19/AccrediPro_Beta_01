The Golden Rule: We Educate, We Don't Diagnose

The most important distinction in your new career is the difference between clinical treatment and nervous system education. As a Certified Polyvagal Theory Specialist‚Ñ¢, you are a guide to the human nervous system. You are not a mechanic fixing a broken machine; you are a navigator helping a captain understand their ship.

To stay within your scope of practice, you must avoid the "Big Three" of clinical practice:

  • Diagnosing: You do not name a disease or mental health disorder (e.g., "You have Clinical Depression").
  • Treating: You do not prescribe a cure for a specific medical condition (e.g., "Use this technique to cure your PTSD").
  • Prescribing: You do not recommend specific dosages of medication or medical interventions.
Coach Tip from Sarah

When a client asks, "Do I have anxiety?", your response should be: "I don't provide clinical diagnoses, but what I can see is that your nervous system is currently showing signs of Sympathetic Activation. Let's look at how we can shift that state together." This keeps you 100% safe and 100% professional.

Legal & Ethical Foundations

Building a "Premium" practice means having premium protections. Even if you are just starting out, you should view your business through a professional lens. We recommend the 4-Legged Stool of Protection:

  1. Certification: Your AccrediPro certification provides the foundational "proof of competency."
  2. Professional Liability Insurance: Often called "Errors & Omissions" insurance. For coaches, this is typically very affordable (approx. $150-$250/year).
  3. Client Agreement: A signed document where the client acknowledges that you are a coach, not a doctor or therapist.
  4. Referral Network: A list of trusted therapists or doctors you can send clients to if they present with issues beyond your scope (e.g., active suicidal ideation).
Coach Tip from Sarah

Don't let the legal talk scare you! Having these in place actually removes the imposter syndrome. When you know you're insured and have a clear contract, you feel like the true professional you are. It‚Äôs a gift to yourself and your clients.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which of the following is considered "In Scope" for a Polyvagal Specialist?

Teaching a client how the Vagus nerve influences their heart rate and breathing during stress. (This is educational, not diagnostic).

2. What is the "Golden Rule" of coaching language?

We Educate, we do not Diagnose.

3. True or False: A coach should never work with someone who has a clinical diagnosis like Generalized Anxiety Disorder.

False. You can work with them, provided you are clear that you are supporting their nervous system regulation and not "treating" their clinical disorder. It is often best to work alongside their therapist.
