Common Coaching Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches can stumble when dealing with Dorsal Vagal states. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Trying to "pump up" a client in shutdown. High-energy Sympathetic energy can feel like a threat to a Dorsal system, causing them to withdraw further.
  • Over-Explaining: Giving a 20-minute lecture on the Vagus nerve. When a client is in freeze, their prefrontal cortex is offline. Keep explanations short and somatic.
  • Ignoring Your Own State: If you are feeling anxious about the client's progress, they will neurocept your anxiety as a lack of safety. Check your own Ventral state first.
  • Rushing the "Fix": Trying to get the client to "do" something before they "feel" safe. Safety must precede action.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "pumping up" a client in a Dorsal Vagal state (shutdown) often counterproductive?

High-energy "cheerleading" is a Sympathetic state. To a system in Dorsal shutdown, this sudden energy can be neurocepted as a threat or "predatory" energy, causing the client to retreat deeper into protection.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Safety Sandwich" technique?

The goal is to provide co-regulation by validating the current state (Safety), providing a non-shaming biological explanation (Education), and offering a low-stakes way to re-engage (Invitation).

3. Name one physiological marker that Diane has shifted toward a Ventral Vagal state.

Markers include a spontaneous sigh, return of facial prosody/expression, increased eye contact, or a softening of the vocal tone.

4. How should a coach handle their own "imposter syndrome" during a session?

The coach should recognize the "imposter" feeling as a Sympathetic/Dorsal blend and use their own anchors (breath, glimmers) to return to a Ventral state, ensuring they remain a "safe harbor" for the client.

Key Takeaways

  • Biology Over Character: Always reframe "laziness" or "procrastination" as protective autonomic responses.
  • Voice is a Tool: Your vocal prosody is a direct signal of safety to the client's middle ear and nervous system.
  • Small Wins Matter: In a Dorsal state, moving toward a 1% shift is a massive victory. Don't rush the process.
  • Co-Regulation is the Intervention: Often, your calm, grounded presence is more healing than the specific words you say.
