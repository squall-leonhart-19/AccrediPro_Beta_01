Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it critical to use "biological language" (e.g., HPA-axis, nervous system) with high-achievers like Diane?

It shifts the narrative from "personal weakness" or "mental failure" to a physiological event. This reduces shame and establishes your professional authority as a Specialist.

2. What is the primary danger of "over-empathizing" in a burnout recovery session?

Over-empathizing can keep the client stuck in a "victim" or "crisis" loop. A Specialist must acknowledge the pain but quickly pivot to the recovery framework to provide hope and direction.

3. How should a coach respond when a client says their doctor found "nothing wrong" with their labs?

Validate that standard labs look for pathology (disease), while burnout recovery looks for "subclinical dysregulation" (function). Explain that "not sick" is not the same as "thriving."

4. What is the most important "Success Marker" of an initial integration session?

The client moves from a state of "unexplained shame" to a state of "biological understanding." They should leave with a framework that explains their symptoms.
