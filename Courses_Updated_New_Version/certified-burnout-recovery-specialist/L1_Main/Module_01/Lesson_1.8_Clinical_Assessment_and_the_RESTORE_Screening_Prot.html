Common Pitfalls in the Recognition Phase

New coaches often struggle with the transition from "listening" to "recognizing." Avoid these four common mistakes to ensure you maintain your professional standing (and justify your $150+ per hour rates).

  • Mistake 1: Rushing to "Fix-It" Mode. If you suggest a vacation or a supplement before Diane feels 100% recognized, she will feel misunderstood.
  • Mistake 2: Over-Identifying. Avoid saying "Oh my god, me too!" for twenty minutes. A brief "Iâ€™ve seen this in many high-achievers I work with" is better.
  • Mistake 3: Fearing the Word "Burnout." Some coaches use "stress" because it feels safer. "Stress" is something you manage; "Burnout" is something you recover from. Use the stronger word.
  • Mistake 4: Ignoring the Body. If you don't mention physical symptoms (sleep, digestion, brain fog), the client will think it's all "in their head."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use physiological terms (like "nervous system" or "biology") during the Recognition phase?

It shifts the narrative from a personal or moral failing (shame) to a biological reality (science). This makes the client more open to intervention and establishes your authority as a Specialist.

2. What is the "hidden" fear many 40-55 year old women have when experiencing burnout-related brain fog?

Many fear they are experiencing early-onset dementia or that they are simply "past their prime" due to age. Validating this as a burnout symptom is crucial for their mental peace.

3. A client says, "I just need a better time-management system." How do you respond in the Recognition phase?

Gently pivot by saying: "A system can help, but right now your 'engine' is overheated. Adding a better 'GPS' (time management) won't help if the engine can't turn over. We need to focus on the engine first."

4. What is the primary goal of the "Recognize" dialogue?

To move the client from a state of confused suffering and shame to a state of clear understanding and hope, while establishing the Coach-Client "Expert Alliance."
