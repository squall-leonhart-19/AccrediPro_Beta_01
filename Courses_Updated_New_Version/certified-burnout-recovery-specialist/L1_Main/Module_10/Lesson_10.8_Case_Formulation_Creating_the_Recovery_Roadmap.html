Common Mistakes for New Coaches

When working with Level 1 clients (the most fragile stage), avoid these common pitfalls:

  • Over-Planning: Giving the client a 10-step plan in the first call. (They are already overwhelmed; 10 steps feels like 1000).
  • Premature Problem Solving: Trying to fix their business or their marriage before fixing their cortisol levels.
  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Saying "You can do it!" or "Just stay positive!" This invalidates their very real physiological exhaustion.
  • Ignoring the Body: Staying purely in "heady" or "mindset" talk. Burnout is a physical condition that manifests in the mind.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to tell a client they are "biologically depleted" rather than just "stressed"?

It shifts the blame from their character (I'm lazy/weak) to their physiology (my body is out of resources). This removes the shame that prevents recovery.

2. What is the "Recovery Bridge" in coaching?

It is the conceptual transition from "Survival Mode" to "Stability." It helps the client see that there is a middle ground between burnout and their ultimate goals.

3. Brenda mentions that a vacation feels like "more work." What does this indicate?

This indicates a high level of decision fatigue and executive dysfunction, common in late-stage burnout. It means the recovery must start with passive rest and nervous system regulation, not active planning.

4. How many seconds should you wait after asking a client how they feel in their body?

At least 5 seconds. Burned-out clients often have a delayed "interoceptive" response‚Äîit takes time for the signal to travel from the body to the conscious mind.
