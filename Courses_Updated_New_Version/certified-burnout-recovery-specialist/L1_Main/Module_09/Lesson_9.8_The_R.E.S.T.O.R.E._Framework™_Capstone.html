Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you begin your practice, watch out for these common "imposter syndrome" traps that new coaches often fall into.

1

Mistake: The "Fix-It" Reflex

Trying to give Elena a 10-step plan in the first 5 minutes. Burnout recovery requires spaciousness, not more tasks.

2

Mistake: Over-Explaining Science

Spending 20 minutes explaining the HPA axis. Use simple metaphors (like the "emergency mode") to keep the focus on her experience.

3

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Elena's exhaustion as a "protective mechanism" rather than a failure?

It shifts the narrative from shame (I'm broken) to biology (my body is trying to keep me safe). Shame is a major driver of burnout; removing it is essential for nervous system regulation.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Identity Reclamation" phase in coaching?

To help the client decouple their self-worth from their professional output or caretaking roles, allowing them to build a more resilient and sustainable sense of self.

3. Elena says, "I feel like I'm being lazy." How should a coach respond using the framework from this module?

Reframing "laziness" as "biological necessity." You might say, "Your body isn't being lazy; it's in a forced rest state because the 'battery' is depleted. Rest is your most productive task right now."

4. What is a key sign that a coaching session is moving toward recovery rather than just "stress management"?

The conversation moves away from "how to do more" toward "how to be different." It focuses on internal shifts in perspective and nervous system state rather than just external time-management tips.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Language Matters: Use words like "protection," "spaciousness," and "reclamation" to set a healing tone.
  • The Body is Wise: Always frame burnout symptoms as the body's attempt to survive an unsustainable environment.
  • Identity is Core: Most high-achieving women (nurses, teachers, executives) need help separating "who they are" from "what they do."
  • Patience is a Skill: Don't rush the client into a plan. The "plan" is often just learning how to breathe and exist without a deadline.
