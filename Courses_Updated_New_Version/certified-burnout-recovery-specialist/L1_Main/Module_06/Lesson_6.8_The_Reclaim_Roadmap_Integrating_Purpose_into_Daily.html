Common Coaching Pitfalls in Module 6

New Burnout Recovery Specialists often make these four mistakes when working through the Reclaim phase:

  • Mistake 1: Validating the Guilt. If you say, "I know, it is hard to say no to family," you are reinforcing the idea that saying no is a problem. Instead, validate the growth.
  • Mistake 2: Becoming a "Life Admin." Don't spend the whole session helping them organize their calendar. Spend the session helping them reclaim the identity that makes the calendar possible.
  • Mistake 3: Fearing the Client's Discomfort. When Elena says she's afraid of being called selfish, don't rush to comfort her. Let her sit with that fear. Ask, "What's the cost of avoiding that label?"
  • Mistake 4: Moving Too Fast to Joy. You can't reclaim joy if the "house is still on fire" with boundary violations. Secure the boundaries first, then hunt for the joy.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "selfishness" a common theme in the Reclaim phase for women aged 40-55?

Society often socializes women in this demographic to be "caregivers" first. Reclaiming identity feels like a betrayal of that role, even though it is actually necessary for long-term health and professional success.

2. What is the primary difference between "healing" and "reclaiming"?

Healing is internal and often passive (rest, nutrition, nervous system regulation). Reclaiming is external and active (setting boundaries, asserting identity, and pursuing goals).

3. If a client like Elena says, "My family will be upset if I stop doing their laundry," what is the most professional coaching response?

A professional response would be: "They might be. How long are you willing to trade your professional dreams to keep them from being temporarily upset?" This puts the power of choice back on the client.

4. What is the "Office Hours" strategy in burnout recovery?

It is a boundary-setting tool where the client designates specific times when they are "at work" and unavailable for domestic or family requests, regardless of whether they are physically at home.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Identity is the Engine: Without a clear sense of "who I am now," a client will always default back to "who I was for everyone else."
  • Boundaries are the Walls: You cannot build a new life (Reclaim) if you don't have walls to protect your time and energy.
  • Guilt is a Compass: In the Reclaim phase, feeling "guilty" often means the client is finally doing something for themselves. Use it as a sign of progress.
  • Words Matter: Practice "Command Language" (e.g., "I am unavailable") versus "Compliance Language" (e.g., "I'm sorry, I can't right now").
