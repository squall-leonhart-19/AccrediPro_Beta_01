Case Study: Rekindling Purpose

Eleanor & David, Mid-50s, 30 Years Married

Clients: Eleanor (54, retired teacher) & David (56, small business owner)

Presenting Situation: Eleanor and David came to coaching feeling a sense of "drift" in their marriage after their children left home. They loved each other deeply but felt they were living parallel lives, lacking the vibrant connection and shared direction they once had. They described their faith as important but rarely discussed how it shaped their daily decisions as a couple. Eleanor felt unfulfilled, yearning for a new purpose, while David was focused on his business. They rarely prayed together and felt their spiritual intimacy had dwindled.

Initial Assessment: My intake revealed that while both held strong personal faith, they had never explicitly articulated their individual core values to each other, nor had they intentionally crafted a shared marital vision beyond raising their family. Their communication was respectful but lacked depth regarding their spiritual convictions and future aspirations. This misalignment was causing a subtle but pervasive sense of disconnect and aimlessness.

"We realized we'd spent decades building a family, but we hadn't built a blueprint for our 'next chapter' as a couple. Our faith was personal, but we hadn't made it truly shared. It felt like we were waiting for purpose to find us, instead of actively seeking God's purpose for us." - Eleanor

Eleanor and David's situation is common among couples transitioning through life stages. Their journey highlights the critical need for coaches to facilitate values and vision alignment to reignite purpose and deepen connection, moving beyond mere coexistence to a truly covenantal partnership. We will revisit their progress later in this lesson.

The Sacred Foundation: Individual Faith Values

Every individual carries a unique tapestry of core faith values‚Äîthe deeply held beliefs and principles derived from their spiritual walk that dictate their choices, priorities, and reactions. In marriage, these individual values profoundly influence expectations, communication patterns, conflict resolution styles, and even financial decisions.

For example, a wife whose core value is "service to others" might feel unfulfilled if her husband prioritizes personal leisure, while a husband whose core value is "financial stewardship" might struggle with his wife's spontaneous generosity if not understood within a shared framework. Unarticulated or unaligned values often become the silent saboteurs of marital harmony.

Facilitating Individual Values Discovery

As a coach, your role is to create a safe space for each spouse to explore and articulate their personal faith values, free from judgment or the pressure to immediately conform to their partner's. This often involves:

  1. Values Inventory: Provide a comprehensive list of values (e.g., integrity, compassion, justice, family, growth, security, faith, community) and ask each spouse to select their top 5-7.
  2. Scriptural Reflection: Encourage them to identify biblical passages or characters that exemplify these values, grounding them in a faith context. For instance, a value of "justice" might resonate with Micah 6:8, while "compassion" aligns with Colossians 3:12.
  3. Life Story Exploration: Ask questions like: "Think of a time you felt most alive and purposeful. What values were you living out?" or "What causes stir your heart most deeply?"
  4. Prioritization Exercise: Guide them to rank their values, understanding that while many values are important, some are more central to their identity and decision-making.

This individual discernment is a crucial first step. It helps each spouse understand themselves better and provides a foundation for sharing with their partner. Research indicates that individuals who clearly understand their personal values experience greater life satisfaction and make more congruent decisions (Schwartz et al., 2012).

Coach's Tip

Navigating "Spiritual Differences": Sometimes, one spouse identifies more overtly "faith-based" values than the other. Frame this not as a deficit, but as an opportunity to explore the underlying principles. For example, "success" for one might be driven by a desire for "stewardship" and "generosity" for the other. Help them find the common ground in purpose, even if the language differs slightly.

Bridging Hearts: Discovering Shared Spiritual Values

Once individual values are articulated, the next powerful step is to bridge these into shared spiritual values. This is where individual paths begin to weave into a common journey, strengthening the marital bond and providing a compass for their union. The goal is not to erase individual values, but to find the overlapping, unifying principles that will serve as the foundation for their shared life.

For Eleanor and David, this process was transformative. Eleanor's top values were "service," "spiritual growth," and "community," while David prioritized "integrity," "provision," and "legacy." At first glance, they seemed disparate. However, through guided dialogue, they recognized that "service" could be expressed through "provision" for their community, and "legacy" could encompass both "spiritual growth" and fostering a "community" impact.

Exercises for Shared Values Identification

Here are methods to help couples identify their shared spiritual values:

  1. Collaborative Values Mapping: Have couples share their individual top values. Use a large sheet of paper or whiteboard to visually map where their values overlap or complement each other. Discuss similarities and differences respectfully.
  2. "Our Covenant Pillars" Exercise: Guide them to articulate 3-5 core values that will serve as the "pillars" of their marriage. These should be values they both commit to upholding and living by. Frame this as a covenantal agreement, reflecting biblical principles of mutual commitment (Malachi 2:14-15).
  3. Scripture as a Guide: Encourage couples to collectively search Scripture for verses that speak to their identified shared values. This anchors their values in divine truth and provides a common reference point.
  4. "What Do We Stand For?" Dialogue: Ask questions like: "If our marriage were a public testimony, what would we want it to represent?" or "What principles do we want our children and grandchildren to see lived out in our home?"
Coach's Tip

Actionable Values: Shared values are more than abstract ideas; they are living principles. Help couples translate their values into actionable behaviors. If "generosity" is a shared value, how will they practically live that out? Through tithing, volunteering, hospitality, or supporting specific ministries? Concrete actions solidify commitment.

Crafting a God-Honoring Marital Vision

With shared values established, couples are ready to articulate their marital vision‚Äîa compelling, faith-infused picture of their future together, aligned with God's design for marriage. This vision moves beyond individual aspirations to a collective destination, providing direction, inspiration, and a unified sense of purpose. Proverbs 29:18 (KJV) reminds us, "Where there is no vision, the people perish." This is profoundly true for marriages as well.

A God-honoring marital vision is not merely a list of goals; it's a declaration of who they aspire to be as a couple, what they desire to achieve, and the impact they wish to make, all within the context of their faith. It answers questions like: "What is God calling our marriage to be?" and "What legacy do we want our union to leave?"

The Visioning Process: From Concept to Covenant

Guiding couples through visioning involves several steps:

  1. Dreaming Together: Encourage open, prayerful brainstorming. What does their ideal marriage look like in 5, 10, 20 years? What kind of spiritual life do they share? How do they serve their family and community?
  2. Articulating the Vision Statement: Help them condense their dreams into a concise, inspiring statement. It should be positive, present-tense, and reflect their shared values. For example: "Our marriage is a vibrant testimony of God's grace, marked by unwavering love, intentional spiritual growth, and joyful service to our community."
  3. Aligning with Biblical Principles: Review their vision against scriptural teachings on marriage, such as unity (Genesis 2:24), love (Ephesians 5:25), mutual submission (Ephesians 5:21), and partnership in ministry (1 Peter 4:10). Ensure it reflects God's heart for their union.
  4. Making it Visible and Actionable: Encourage couples to write down their vision, perhaps frame it, and revisit it regularly. Break the vision down into SMART+F goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound + Faith-centered) that translate the vision into daily practice.

A recent study on Christian couples found that those who regularly discussed and intentionally aligned their marital vision reported significantly higher levels of marital satisfaction and resilience in the face of challenges (Johnson & Miller, 2018). This proactive approach provides a powerful antidote to marital drift.

Coach's Tip

Addressing Imposter Syndrome in Visioning: Many women, especially career changers, may feel their "dreams" for their marriage or life purpose aren't grand enough or are selfish. Reassure them that God's plan is unique for every couple. Small, consistent acts of shared purpose, like daily prayer or intentional acts of kindness, are just as profound as starting a large ministry. The authenticity of the heart is what matters.

Living a Shared Purpose: Beyond the Wedding Day

The ultimate fruit of values and vision alignment is the discovery and living out of a shared life purpose. This is where their marriage transcends being merely a partnership for personal happiness and becomes a vessel for God's work in the world. It‚Äôs about understanding that their union is part of a greater calling, a testament to Christ's love, and a force for good.

For Eleanor and David, their shared vision led them to a beautiful shared purpose. They discovered a mutual passion for mentoring younger couples in their church, leveraging their decades of marital experience and Eleanor's teaching background. David's business acumen helped them organize workshops, and Eleanor's nurturing spirit created a welcoming environment. Their marriage became a beacon of hope and guidance, fulfilling their values of "service," "spiritual growth," and "legacy" in a tangible way. This newfound purpose infused their daily interactions with renewed energy and intimacy.

Integrating Values & Vision into Daily Life

Your role as a coach extends to helping couples embed their shared values and vision into the fabric of their everyday lives:

  • Decision-Making Filter: Teach them to ask, "Does this decision align with our shared values and vision?" This applies to big choices (career, relocation) and small ones (weekend activities, budget allocations).
  • Conflict Resolution Framework: When disagreements arise, remind them of their shared values and vision. How would a couple living out their vision handle this challenge? This elevates the conversation beyond personal preference.
  • Spiritual Disciplines: Encourage shared prayer, Bible study, and worship that reinforces their vision. This cultivates spiritual intimacy, a cornerstone of The C.O.V.E.N.A.N.T. Framework‚Ñ¢.
  • Regular Review: Suggest quarterly or annual "vision check-ins" where they review their progress, celebrate victories, and adjust their action steps as needed.

A 2021 study by the Barna Group found that Christian couples who regularly engage in shared spiritual practices and articulate a common purpose for their marriage report 35% higher satisfaction rates and 40% greater resilience during difficult seasons compared to those who do not. This underscores the profound impact of intentional values and vision alignment.

Coach's Tip

Empowering the "Empty Nesters": For women like Eleanor, who may be re-evaluating their identity post-children, guiding her and her husband to find a shared purpose can be incredibly empowering. It shifts the focus from "what now?" to "what next, together?" This can unlock new avenues for personal fulfillment and shared ministry, often leading to a second wave of marital vitality.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important for a couple to first explore their individual core faith values before attempting to identify shared values?

Exploring individual core faith values first is crucial because it helps each spouse gain self-awareness and articulate their unique convictions without the immediate pressure of alignment. This foundational understanding prevents one partner's values from dominating and ensures that shared values are built on genuine, recognized individual principles, leading to a stronger, more authentic marital foundation.

2. What is the primary difference between a "shared spiritual value" and a "marital vision"?

A shared spiritual value is a fundamental principle or belief (e.g., generosity, integrity, compassion) that both spouses commit to upholding and living by. A marital vision, on the other hand, is a forward-looking, inspiring statement that describes the overall desired future state or destination of their marriage, encompassing who they aspire to be and what they aim to achieve together, guided by their shared values. Values are the "what we stand for," while vision is the "where we are going."

3. How does aligning a marital vision with biblical principles strengthen the marriage, according to the lesson?

Aligning a marital vision with biblical principles strengthens the marriage by anchoring it in divine truth and God's design. This provides a transcendent framework that offers clarity, stability, and purpose beyond personal preferences. It ensures their vision is God-honoring, drawing on scriptural wisdom for unity, love, and partnership, which has been linked to higher marital satisfaction and resilience.

4. Consider Eleanor and David's case study. How did discovering a shared purpose help them overcome their sense of "drift" and rekindle their connection?

Eleanor and David's shared purpose (mentoring younger couples) gave their marriage a renewed, outward focus beyond their individual lives. This collaborative mission leveraged their individual strengths and values ("service," "spiritual growth," "integrity," "legacy") in a meaningful way. By working together towards a common, God-honoring goal, they re-engaged in shared spiritual practices, deepened their communication, and reignited the sense of unity and vitality that had been missing, transforming their "empty nest" into a purposeful new chapter.

  • A God-honoring marital vision is a compelling, faith-infused blueprint for the future, guiding decisions and actions while aligning the couple's aspirations with divine purpose.
  • Coaches empower couples to move beyond personal happiness to a shared life purpose, transforming their marriage into a vessel for God's work and a testimony to His love.
