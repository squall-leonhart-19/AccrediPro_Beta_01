Before every discovery call, take a few minutes to review your prospect's intake form and any notes you have. This shows you value their time and helps you tailor the conversation. A personalized approach, even in the first few minutes, can significantly increase their trust and engagement. Remember, they are looking for a guide who truly understands their unique journey.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework for a powerful 30-minute discovery call. The goal is not to "sell" but to deeply understand their needs, introduce your unique faith-based solution, and guide them to their next best step. Practice this out loud, internalizing the flow and key phrases.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Intentions 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's so wonderful to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. I really enjoyed seeing you at the 'Rekindling the Spark' workshop. Before we dive into what brought you here, how are you doing today?"
YOU:
"Excellent. Well, the purpose of our call today is for me to understand what's currently happening in your marriage, what you're hoping for, and for you to learn more about how faith-based marriage coaching works. By the end, we'll both have a clear idea if this is the right next step for you. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Wonderful. So, Sarah, you mentioned in your intake that you're feeling a disconnection from your husband, and a desire to rekindle that spark. Could you tell me more about what that disconnection feels like for you day-to-day? What are you experiencing?"
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage. And what have you and your husband tried so far to address these challenges? What worked, and what felt like it didn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes in your marriage, where do you see yourselves in 6 months or a year from now? And, on the flip side, if you could wave a magic wand and your marriage was exactly as you desire, what would that look, sound, and feel like? What kind of example would that set for your children?"
Phase 3: Present Your Faith-Based Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you. What I'm hearing is a deep longing for reconnection, intimacy, and a stronger, more vibrant faith-centered marriage, especially for your children. Does that resonate with what you're feeling?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe my COVENANT Marriage Coaching Program could be a powerful next step for you. Unlike traditional counseling, we don't just focus on conflict resolution; we build upon your shared faith as the foundation. Over 12 weeks, we'll work through a proven framework to help you and your husband: Communicate with grace, Open your hearts to intimacy, Validate each other's needs, Engage in spiritual practices together, Nurture forgiveness, Act in love, and Navigate challenges with God's wisdom. Many clients like you, even those who felt stuck for years, begin to see significant shifts within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"We'll meet weekly for 60-minute sessions, and you'll have access to resources, exercises, and direct support between sessions. This isn't just about fixing problems; it's about transforming your marriage into a vibrant, living testament to God's love."
Phase 4: Transition to Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about the COVENANT program and what you're looking for, does this sound like the kind of support and transformation you've been seeking for your marriage?"

Coach Tip

Notice the emphasis on asking open-ended questions and active listening. Your prospect needs to feel truly heard and understood. When you summarize their pain points and desired outcomes, you're not just repeating; you're demonstrating empathy and confirming that you grasp the depth of their situation. This builds trust, which is the foundation for any successful coaching relationship.

Confident Objection Handling

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your role is to address these with empathy, clarity, and confidence, reinforcing the value of your program. Here are common objections and effective faith-based responses.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to pray about it / talk to my husband."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is a significant decision, and prayer and discussion with your husband are exactly what I would encourage. In fact, a core part of faith-based coaching is discerning God's will together. What specifically would you like to pray about or discuss with him? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps what the program entails? I can provide you with a summary of our discussion and the program details to share, and we can schedule a brief follow-up call if that would be helpful after your discussion."
"The investment is a bit high right now."
"I appreciate your honesty, Sarah. I know investing in your marriage is a big decision, and itâ€™s natural to consider the financial aspect. Many of my clients initially feel this way. Consider the cost of *not* addressing these issues â€“ the emotional toll, the potential impact on your children, the long-term strain on your relationship. My program is structured to provide transformative results, not just temporary fixes, by rooting your marriage in God's principles. We also have payment plans available to make it more accessible. Would exploring a payment plan make this more feasible for you?"
"I'm not sure we have the time for this right now."
"I hear you, Sarah. Life with teenagers can be incredibly busy, and it often feels like there's no extra time. However, what I've found is that when couples prioritize their marriage, it actually creates more peace and efficiency in other areas of their lives. Our sessions are weekly, 60 minutes, which is a dedicated time to focus on your most important relationship. The tools and strategies you'll learn will actually *save* you time in the long run by reducing conflict and improving communication. How much longer are you willing to live with the current level of disconnection before making time for change?"
"We've tried counseling before, and it didn't really help."
"I understand your skepticism, Sarah, and that's a common experience for many couples. It sounds like you've been proactive in seeking help, which is commendable. My approach is different because it's specifically faith-based and focuses on proactive growth rather than just crisis management. We integrate biblical principles into every strategy, and the coaching model is highly action-oriented, providing practical tools you can implement immediately. We're not just talking about problems; we're actively building a stronger, God-centered future for your marriage. What specifically didn't work for you in previous experiences?"

Coach Tip

When handling objections, avoid getting defensive. Instead, validate their concern, reframe it in a positive light if possible, and then offer a solution or a deeper explanation of your program's value. Using phrases like "I understand," "I hear you," or "That's a common concern" shows empathy and keeps the conversation collaborative, not confrontational.

Presenting Your Program & Pricing

This is where many new coaches hesitate. Remember, you are offering immense value that can transform lives. Present your pricing with confidence, clarity, and conviction. Avoid apologies or wavering.

Following the "Does this sound like what you're looking for?" question (Phase 4), if Sarah says "Yes," move directly into this:

YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear, Sarah! I'm genuinely excited about the possibility of working with you and your husband. My signature COVENANT Marriage Coaching Program is a 12-week journey, and the investment for this comprehensive transformation is $3,500. This includes 12 weekly 60-minute coaching sessions, personalized resources, between-session support, and access to our exclusive client community. For couples who prefer, we also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly payments of $1,200. Which option would work best for you?"

Key elements of this presentation:

  • State the full value: Reiterate what the program includes.
  • Confident tone: Deliver the price clearly, without hesitation.
  • Two options: Provide a paid-in-full option and a payment plan. This anchors the higher price and makes the payment plan seem more accessible.
  • Direct question: End with a question that prompts a decision, not an "if."

Coach Tip

Your energy and belief in your program are contagious. If you hesitate or sound apologetic when stating your price, your prospect will pick up on that. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels completely natural and confident. Remember the value you provide â€“ a transformed marriage for God's glory is priceless.

Case Study: From Hesitation to Hope

Empowering a Client to Say "Yes" to Faith-Based Marriage Coaching

Suzanne, 49

Career woman, mother of three, feeling overwhelmed and disconnected in her 20-year marriage.

Presenting Situation: Suzanne scheduled a discovery call after attending a free webinar on "Building a God-Centered Home." She expressed significant fatigue, a feeling of being "on autopilot" in her marriage, and a desire for more spiritual connection with her husband. Her primary concern was the financial investment, as they had recently incurred unexpected medical expenses for one of their children.

Coach's Intervention: During the discovery call, Coach Grace (you!) actively listened to Suzanne's deep desire for a revitalized marriage and her fears about financial strain. Instead of immediately presenting the full price, Grace focused on validating Suzanne's feelings and reiterating the profound value of a faith-based approach. When the objection about cost arose, Grace responded with empathy, acknowledging the medical expenses, and then skillfully reframed the investment as prioritizing long-term marital health and family stability, a legacy for her children. Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem & Vision" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply understand the prospect's current marital challenges (pain points), what they have tried previously, and their desired future state or vision for their marriage. This phase is crucial for building empathy and demonstrating that you truly hear and grasp their situation.

2. A prospect states, "My husband is skeptical about coaching, especially investing more money." How should you, as a Faith-Based Marriage Coach, respond?

A confident response would be to validate their husband's skepticism ("I understand his hesitation, many partners feel that way initially.") then reframe the investment as a spiritual and relational priority. Emphasize the unique faith-based approach and its proven effectiveness, offering to send specific program details for him to review or even suggesting a brief follow-up call with both spouses.

3. When presenting your program pricing, what are two key elements to include to maximize client conversion?

The two key elements are: 1) Reiterating the full value proposition of your program (what it includes and the transformation it offers) before stating the price, and 2) Offering two distinct pricing options (e.g., paid-in-full discount vs. a payment plan) to provide flexibility and anchor the value.

4. According to the lesson, what is a realistic conversion rate from discovery call to client for a faith-based marriage coach?

A realistic conversion rate from discovery call to client for a faith-based marriage coach is typically between 25-35%, especially with consistent practice of effective communication and sales strategies.
