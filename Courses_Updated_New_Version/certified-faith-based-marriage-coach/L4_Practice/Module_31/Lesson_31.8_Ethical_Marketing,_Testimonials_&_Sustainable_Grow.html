Business Practice Lab: Your First Discovery Call

It's time to step into your role as a Certified Faith-Based Marriage Coach‚Ñ¢ and experience the client acquisition process firsthand. This lab walks you through a discovery call from start to close, providing scripts and strategies to ensure you feel prepared and empowered.

Your Ideal Prospect: Sarah

Imagine you've just received a notification: a prospective client, Sarah, has scheduled a 30-minute discovery call through your website. She found you via a testimonial from a friend in your local church community. This is your opportunity to connect, understand her needs, and offer the transformative power of faith-based marriage coaching.

üë©

Sarah, 48

Found you through a friend's referral at church. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Married for 22 years. Feeling disconnected from her husband, John, after kids left for college. They've grown apart, communicate poorly, and she misses the spiritual intimacy they once shared.
Budget Concern
"We've invested in so many things for our kids, but never really for us. I'm worried about the cost, especially if John isn't fully on board."
Decision Style
Values trust and authenticity. Needs to feel understood and see a clear path forward. Highly values faith-based solutions.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel close to John again. I want us to pray together, laugh together, and feel like we're on the same team, especially spiritually."
Coach's Insight

Remember, the discovery call is a conversation, not a sales pitch. Your primary goal is to deeply understand their needs and determine if you are the right person to help them. Approach it with curiosity, empathy, and a genuine desire to serve.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Adapt it to your natural conversational style, always keeping Sarah's needs and your faith-based approach at the forefront.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's so wonderful to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. I'm Grace. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear how you found me."
SARAH:
"Hi Grace! Yes, [friend's name] from church spoke so highly of you. She said you really helped her and Mark reconnect."
YOU:
"That's so lovely to hear! [Friend's name] and Mark are truly a testament to God's restorative power. So Sarah, tell me, in your own words, what inspired you to reach out for support for your marriage right now?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned feeling disconnected and missing the spiritual intimacy with John. Can you tell me more about what that feels like day-to-day? When did you first notice this shift?"
SARAH:
"Well, it's just... we used to pray together, read scripture, but now it's like we're roommates. We talk about logistics, but not our hearts. I feel lonely in the marriage. It started slowly after the kids left, but it's really hit me hard lately."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging, Sarah, and many couples experience similar shifts. What have you and John tried so far to bridge this gap, and what were the outcomes?"
SARAH:
"We tried a date night once, but it felt forced. We also tried reading a devotional together, but we kept getting distracted. It just feels like we don't know where to start."
YOU:
"I understand. If nothing changes, and you continue feeling this way, what do you imagine your marriage will look like a year from now? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and achieve your ideal marriage, what would that look and feel like for you and John?"
Phase 3: Present Your Faith-Based Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for being so open and vulnerable. Based on everything you've shared, what I'm hearing is that you deeply desire to rekindle the emotional and spiritual intimacy with John, to move beyond being roommates and truly feel like partners in faith and life again. You're looking for guidance to communicate more effectively and rediscover shared purpose. Does that resonate with you?"
SARAH:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Wonderful. The unique approach I use, the COVENANT Framework‚Ñ¢, is specifically designed for couples like you. It's built on biblical principles, helping you to not just address symptoms, but to rebuild a strong foundation rooted in God's design for marriage. We'll work together over 12 weeks, focusing on areas like Communication, Openness, Vision, and Nurturing Intimacy. My clients often report significant shifts in connection and understanding within the first few weeks, and a profound sense of renewed hope and purpose by the end of the program."
YOU:
"For example, one client, Eleanor, similar to your situation, felt her marriage was just coasting. Through our work, she and her husband re-engaged in daily prayer, discovered new shared hobbies, and truly felt 'seen' by each other again. They now feel more connected than they have in a decade."
Phase 4: The Invitation & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, and knowing the transformative power of the COVENANT Framework, I truly believe I can help you and John cultivate the deeply connected, faith-centered marriage you're longing for. Does this sound like the kind of comprehensive support you've been looking for?"
SARAH:
"It really does, Grace. It sounds exactly like what we need. But I'm not sure if John would be fully on board with coaching, and honestly, the investment is a concern."
YOU:
"Those are very common and valid concerns, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. Let's talk about them..."
Coach's Insight

Always summarize and validate before presenting your solution. This shows you've truly listened. When you transition to your solution, paint a vivid picture of the transformation, not just the features. Share a quick, relatable success story if you have one.

Graceful Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, confidence, and a servant's heart.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to talk to my spouse / John isn't fully on board."
"That's completely understandable, Sarah. Marriage coaching is a journey you embark on together, and it's vital that John feels included. What specifically do you think his hesitations might be? Often, when couples hear about the specific, faith-based tools we'll use to strengthen their connection, their perspective shifts. Perhaps we could schedule a brief 15-minute call with both of you, where I can answer any questions John might have and explain how this truly benefits both partners and honors God's design for your marriage. It's a low-pressure conversation, simply for clarity."
"It's too expensive / I'm worried about the cost."
"I completely hear you, Sarah, and I understand that investing in yourselves can feel like a big step, especially when you've prioritized so much for your family. Think of this not as an expense, but as an investment in the most important relationship in your life ‚Äì your marriage. What is the cost of staying where you are? The cost of continued disconnection, missed spiritual intimacy, and potential regret? My program is designed to deliver lasting transformation, providing tools and spiritual principles that will serve your marriage for decades. Many clients find the return on this investment immeasurable, far outweighing the financial commitment. We can discuss payment options if that would be helpful."
"I need to think about it."
"Absolutely, Sarah, this is an important decision. To help you reflect, can I ask what specifically you need more time to consider? Is it the program structure, the time commitment, the investment, or perhaps something else that hasn't been fully clarified? My goal is for you to feel completely confident and at peace with your decision, knowing you're taking a faithful step towards the marriage God intends for you."
"I've tried counseling before, and it didn't work."
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage to try again after a challenging experience. Many clients come to me after traditional counseling hasn't met their needs, especially in the area of faith integration. My approach as a Faith-Based Marriage Coach‚Ñ¢ is distinct. We don't just address past wounds; we focus on building a future, equipping you with proactive tools rooted in scripture, and empowering you to apply God's wisdom daily. It's a forward-looking, action-oriented process, centered on spiritual growth and practical skills, which is often a very different experience. I'm here to provide a safe space where your faith is not just acknowledged but is the cornerstone of our work."
Coach's Insight

When handling objections, lean into curiosity. Ask open-ended questions to understand the root of their concern. Validate their feelings, then reframe the objection by highlighting the unique value of your faith-based approach and the potential for transformational change.

Confident Pricing Presentation

Presenting your pricing is about confidence in your value and the transformation you offer. Frame it as an investment, not a cost.

After addressing objections and reiterating the value:

Presenting Your Program & Pricing
YOU:
"Sarah, I'm so excited about the possibility of working with you and John. My signature program, 'The COVENANT Connection Journey,' is a 12-week intensive coaching experience, delivered through 6 bi-weekly sessions. It includes personalized curriculum, direct access to me for support between sessions, and resources to deepen your spiritual practices as a couple. The investment for this transformative journey is $3,500. Many clients choose our flexible payment plan, which is three monthly payments of $1,200. My next program begins on [Date], and I have capacity for two new couples. If this feels right, we can secure your spot today."
YOU:
"I truly believe this is an investment in your marriage's future, a testament to its value in God's eyes, and a step towards the joy and intimacy you desire. What questions do you have about getting started?"
Coach's Insight

State your price clearly and confidently. Then, pause. Let the client process the information. Avoid filling the silence with extra justifications. Your confidence in your value speaks volumes.

Case Study: Eleanor's Journey to a Thriving Practice

Eleanor, 52: From Teacher to Transformational Coach

Building a Full-Time Faith-Based Marriage Coaching Practice

üë©

Eleanor Vance, 52

Former High School Teacher, Aspiring Coach

Presenting Situation: Eleanor, a dedicated high school teacher for 25 years, felt a deep calling to serve couples in her church community. She had witnessed countless marriages struggle and believed her faith and life experience could make a difference. After completing her AccrediPro certification, she was eager to start her practice but felt significant imposter syndrome and anxiety about client acquisition, especially the "sales" aspect of discovery calls. She worried about confidently stating her prices and handling objections, especially regarding the investment.

Intervention & Strategy: Eleanor focused heavily on the client acquisition modules, particularly practicing discovery calls. She utilized mock calls with accountability partners and recorded herself to refine her script. She practiced:

  • Active Listening: Focusing on understanding the client's spiritual and marital pain points.
  • Value-Based Language: Shifting from "what I do" to "the transformation clients experience" (e.g., "rekindling spiritual intimacy" instead of "communication techniques").
  • Confident Pricing: Practicing stating her $3,200 program fee with conviction, followed by a confident pause.
  • Empathy in Objections: Learning to validate concerns like "It's too expensive" with grace, then linking back to the cost of inaction and the eternal value of a strong, faith-based marriage.

Outcomes: Within her first six months, Eleanor enrolled 4 paying clients, generating an initial income of $12,800. By the end of her first year, she had consistently maintained 8-10 active clients, achieving a monthly income ranging from $5,000 to $6,000. She successfully transitioned out of teaching within 18 months, now enjoying the flexibility and profound fulfillment of her coaching practice. Eleanor often shares, "The discovery call used to be my biggest fear, but now it's my favorite part. It's where I get to hear God's call on a couple's life and offer them a path back to Him and each other." Her confidence grew exponentially with each successful enrollment, proving that genuine connection and faith-aligned service are powerful sales tools.

Coach's Insight

Your personal story and testimonials from clients who've experienced transformation are incredibly powerful. Don't be afraid to share brief, relevant examples during your discovery calls to illustrate the impact of your coaching.

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. During Phase 2 of the discovery call (Deep Dive into Needs & Desires), which type of questions are most effective for uncovering a prospect's true pain points and desires?

Answer: Open-ended questions are most effective, as they encourage the prospect to share more deeply about their experiences, feelings, and aspirations. Examples include: "How does that impact your daily life?" or "What would your ideal marriage look like if this issue were resolved?"

2. A prospect tells you, "I need to pray about it before deciding." How should you respond, aligning with a faith-based coaching approach?

Answer: Acknowledge and affirm their desire to seek God's guidance: "That is a beautiful and wise response, and I fully support your desire to pray about such an important decision. To help you in your discernment, do you have any remaining questions about the program or how we might specifically address your needs through a faith-based lens? I'm happy to provide any clarity that might aid your prayerful consideration." This respects their faith while gently inviting further engagement and clarification.

3. When presenting your coaching investment, what is a key strategy to convey value and confidence, rather than focusing solely on the monetary figure?

Answer: Frame it as an investment in transformation, not just a cost. Connect the price directly to the specific, lasting benefits and the profound change they will experience in their marriage (e.g., renewed spiritual intimacy, deeper connection, tools for a lifetime). State the price clearly and confidently, then pause to let them absorb it, avoiding immediate justification.

4. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call for a Faith-Based Marriage Coach‚Ñ¢?

Answer: The primary purpose is to discern if there is a mutual fit ‚Äì to understand the prospect's needs, determine if your faith-based coaching solution is appropriate for them, and to offer an invitation to a transformative journey. It's about serving and matching needs with solutions, not just "selling."
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is your sacred space to connect, listen, and discern if God is calling you to serve a couple.
  • Follow a clear, structured script (Rapport, Needs, Solution, Invitation) but always remain flexible and authentic.
  • Empathy and active listening are your most powerful tools for understanding a client's heart and pain.
  • Objections are opportunities for deeper understanding and reassurance; handle them with grace, clarity, and conviction in your faith-based value.
  • Present your pricing confidently, focusing on the transformational investment rather than just the dollar amount.
