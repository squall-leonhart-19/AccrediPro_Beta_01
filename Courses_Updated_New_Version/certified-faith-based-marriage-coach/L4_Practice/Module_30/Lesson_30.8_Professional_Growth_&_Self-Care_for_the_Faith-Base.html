1. Your Ideal Prospect: Meet Sarah

Before we dive into the script, let's meet our prospect for this lab. Imagine Sarah is someone very much like the clients you aspire to serve ‚Äì she's ready for change, but perhaps a bit hesitant about the investment or skeptical after past disappointments. Your role is to guide her with empathy, clarity, and unwavering faith in the transformation you offer.

üë©

Sarah Jenkins, 49

Found you through your church's bulletin board. Scheduled a free 30-minute discovery call.

Her Situation
Married for 25 years. Feeling distant from her husband, John. Communication has broken down, and they've grown apart. Worries about her adult children seeing their parents unhappy.
Budget Concern
"We've spent money on counseling before, and it didn't really stick. Is this different? We're on a fixed income with college tuition coming up."
Decision Style
Values trust and authenticity. Needs to feel understood and that the approach is grounded in faith. Wants practical tools, not just talk.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel connected to John again, like we used to be. I want our home to be a place of peace and joy, especially for our kids."
Coach's Insight

Remember, Sarah isn't just seeking solutions; she's seeking hope. Her "budget concern" isn't always about the money itself, but about the perceived value and the risk of disappointment. Focus on the transformation you offer, not just the features of your program.

2. The Discovery Call Blueprint: Your 30-Minute Script

This script provides a framework. Your authentic self, guided by the Holy Spirit, is your best tool. Adapt these lines to your natural voice, but ensure you hit these key points to create a powerful, connection-driven conversation.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Intentions 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Sarah, it's so lovely to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. Before we dive in, how did you hear about my coaching services?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! I'm so glad the bulletin board caught your eye. So, Sarah, my intention for our time together is to truly understand what's been happening in your marriage, what you're hoping for, and to see if my faith-based marriage coaching program might be a good fit to help you achieve those goals. Does that sound good to you?"
YOU:
"Great. So, in your own words, what led you to reach out for support at this particular time?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned feeling distant from John. Can you tell me more about what that feels like day-to-day? What does that distance look like in your interactions?"
YOU:
"And how does this situation impact you personally, Sarah? How does it affect your sense of peace, your joy, or even your faith walk?"
YOU:
"What have you and John tried so far to address these challenges? What worked, even a little, and what didn't seem to make a lasting difference?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, and you continue on this path for another 6-12 months, what does that future look like for you and John? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and your marriage was exactly as you desired, what would that look and feel like? How would your home environment change for your children?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Transformative Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for being so open and vulnerable. What I'm hearing is that you're feeling a deep longing for reconnection with John, a desire for renewed peace and joy in your home, and a wish for your children to witness a thriving, God-honoring marriage. You've tried traditional counseling, but it hasn't quite addressed the deeper spiritual and practical needs you both have. Does that resonate with what you're experiencing?"
YOU:
"My approach to faith-based marriage coaching is designed to be truly transformative. Unlike generic counseling, we integrate biblical principles with practical communication tools and emotional intelligence strategies. We don't just talk about problems; we actively work to rebuild foundations and equip you both with skills rooted in God's design for marriage. My 12-week 'Covenant Connection' program helps couples like you move from distance to deep intimacy, often seeing significant shifts within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"Imagine what it would feel like to have those tools, that renewed connection, and the peace you desire. That's what we aim to achieve together."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Based on everything we've discussed, Sarah, does this 'Covenant Connection' program sound like the kind of comprehensive support and guidance you've been looking for to transform your marriage?"
YOU:
"Wonderful. Let's talk about how we can get started and the investment involved."

3. Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

This is often where imposter syndrome can creep in, but remember your value! You are offering a life-changing service. Your pricing reflects your expertise, your time, and the profound transformation you facilitate. Always state your prices with confidence and clarity, without apology.

Structuring Your Program & Pricing

For Sarah, you might offer a 12-week program, which is a common and effective duration for marriage coaching. Let's assume your signature 12-week "Covenant Connection" program includes:

  • 12 weekly 60-minute coaching sessions (via Zoom or in-person).
  • Customized resources, worksheets, and biblical study guides.
  • Unlimited email support between sessions.
  • Access to a private client portal with session recordings and progress tracking.
  • A personalized "Marriage Mission Statement" development session.

When presenting your pricing, clearly state the investment and then immediately anchor it to the value and transformation. Avoid a long pause that invites doubt.

YOU:
"The investment for the 12-week 'Covenant Connection' program is $3,000. This includes everything we just discussed: 12 personalized sessions, all your resources, and ongoing support. I also offer a payment plan of three monthly installments of $1,100."
YOU:
"Think about the cost of divorce, the emotional toll of an unhappy marriage, or even the cumulative cost of repeated, ineffective counseling. This investment is not just in coaching; it's an investment in the future of your family and your spiritual well-being. It's about regaining the joy and peace that you both deeply desire."

4. Navigating Objections with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, understanding, and a confident reiteration of your value. Here are common objections and how to respond:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That's a lot of money. I need to talk to my husband/think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is a significant investment in your marriage, and it's wise to discuss it with John. Can I ask, what specifically about the investment gives you pause? Is it the amount itself, or perhaps concern about whether this program will truly deliver the transformation you're hoping for after past experiences? I want to make sure I've addressed any lingering questions you might have."
"We're too busy right now. I don't know if we have the time."
"I hear you, Sarah. Life is incredibly busy, especially with family responsibilities. Many of my clients feel the same way initially. However, what they often discover is that by dedicating this focused time now, they actually gain back more time and energy in the long run by resolving ongoing conflicts and improving communication. Think about how much time and emotional energy is currently consumed by marital stress. This program is designed to be efficient and impactful, fitting into busy lives because it prioritizes what truly matters. What would be the cost of not making time for your marriage now?"
"We tried counseling before, and it didn't work. How is this different?"
"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, and I appreciate you sharing your past experience. Traditional counseling often focuses on problem-solving or past issues. My faith-based marriage coaching, on the other hand, is a proactive, future-focused approach rooted in biblical principles. We don't just talk about problems; we implement actionable strategies, spiritual disciplines, and communication tools that empower you both to rebuild your covenant. It's about actively co-creating the marriage God intends for you, with practical steps and ongoing support tailored to your unique spiritual journey. It's less about therapy and more about intentional growth and skill-building."
Coach's Insight

When an objection comes up, pause. Listen. Validate their concern. Then, reframe it by connecting back to their stated desires and the unique value of your faith-based approach. Remember, you're not trying to convince them; you're helping them see the path to their desired future through your guidance.

5. Mastering the Close and Follow-Up

The close isn't about pushing; it's about inviting. If you've genuinely connected with Sarah and believe you can help, it's your responsibility to offer her the clear next step. Sometimes, clients need a gentle nudge or a structured follow-up plan.

Powerful Closing Lines to Practice:

  • "Sarah, based on everything you've shared and what I know my 'Covenant Connection' program offers, I truly believe this is the path to the renewed intimacy and peace you're seeking. Are you ready to take this step of faith and commit to transforming your marriage?"
  • "Considering the pain you've described and your deep desire for connection, how does moving forward with the 'Covenant Connection' program feel to you right now?"
  • "If you're feeling a spiritual nudge and believe this is the right next step for your marriage, I'd love to help you get started today. We can secure your spot with the first payment and schedule our initial session."

The Art of the Follow-Up:

Not every client will say "yes" on the spot, and that's perfectly normal. Have a clear follow-up strategy:

  1. Send a Recap Email (within 24 hours): Summarize what was discussed, reiterate the program benefits, and include a direct link to your enrollment page or scheduling tool.
  2. Offer a Resource: "I'd love to send you a short article on [relevant topic] that might provide further insight as you consider our conversation." This keeps you top of mind and provides value.
  3. Schedule a Follow-Up Call: If they need to discuss with their spouse, suggest a brief 15-minute follow-up call in a few days to answer any remaining questions. "How about we schedule a quick chat for Thursday at 2 PM, after you've had a chance to speak with John?"
Coach's Insight

Your follow-up is not about being pushy; it's about being persistent in your service. Many clients need a little space to process and pray. A well-timed, value-driven follow-up can be the difference between a lost opportunity and a transformed marriage. Remember, you are a professional, and professionals follow up.

Check Your Understanding

1. During Phase 2 of the Discovery Call, what is the primary goal you aim to achieve with your prospect?

The primary goal of Phase 2 is to deeply understand the prospect's problem by asking open-ended questions about their current struggles, emotional impact, past attempts at solutions, and their vision for a transformed future. This helps uncover their core desires and pain points.

2. When a prospect expresses a budget concern, what is an effective strategy to address it, as discussed in this lesson?

An effective strategy is to validate their concern ("I completely understand...") and then gently probe deeper to understand the true nature of the objection (e.g., "Is it the amount itself, or concern about whether this program will truly deliver?"). Reframe the investment in terms of the long-term value and transformation, rather than just the dollar amount.

3. What is the recommended approach for confidently stating your program's price?

State the investment clearly and confidently, without apology or hesitation. Immediately follow it by anchoring the price to the immense value and transformative outcomes the client will receive, connecting it back to their stated desires and problems.

4. Why is a structured follow-up plan important for client acquisition, even if a prospect doesn't commit immediately?

A structured follow-up demonstrates professionalism, provides additional value, and allows the prospect space to process the information, discuss with their spouse, and pray about the decision. Many clients need multiple touchpoints before committing, and a thoughtful follow-up can be the crucial step that leads to enrollment.

Key Takeaways

  • A discovery call is a sacred conversation focused on deep listening and discerning fit, not just a sales pitch.
  • Follow a structured 4-phase approach: Rapport, Problem Deep Dive, Solution Presentation, and Clear Close.
  • Confidently articulate your program's value and pricing, anchoring it to the transformational outcomes you provide.
  • View objections as opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, responding with empathy and clarity.
  • Master the close by offering clear next steps and implement a professional, value-driven follow-up strategy.
