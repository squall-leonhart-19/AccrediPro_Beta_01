Found you through your workshop on "Rekindling Intimacy in Christian Marriages." Scheduled a discovery call.

Married 22 years. Feels disconnected from husband, John. Communication has dwindled. Both are active in their church, but struggle to apply faith principles to their day-to-day marital issues. Fears they are drifting apart.

Coach Tip: Empathy is Your Superpower

Remember, your prospect is coming to you with vulnerability and a deeply personal struggle. Your ability to listen, empathize, and reflect their pain back to them will build trust far more effectively than any sales pitch. Focus on understanding their world before offering yours.

The Enrollment Call Script: Your 30-Minute Blueprint

This script provides a structured yet flexible framework for your discovery calls. Practice it, internalize it, and then make it your own. The goal is a natural, authentic conversation that leads to clarity for both you and your prospect.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to connect today. I really appreciate you being here. Before we dive into what brought you to me, I'd love to just hear a little about you. How did you hear about my work?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that you attended the workshop! I'm glad it resonated. So, Sarah, in your own words, what inspired you to reach out for a conversation like this now? What's going on in your marriage that you'd like to explore?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Client Needs 10 min

(Listen actively, take notes, ask clarifying questions. Allow pauses.)

YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage to speak so openly. You mentioned feeling disconnected from John. Can you tell me more about what that feels like for you on a day-to-day basis? When did you first notice this shift?"
YOU:
"And what have you and John tried so far to address these challenges? What worked, even a little, and what felt like it just didn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, and you continue on this path for another year, what does that look and feel like for your marriage, and for you personally? And, on the flip side, if you could wave a magic wand and instantly have the marriage you truly desire, what would that look, feel, and sound like? What role would your faith play in that renewed connection?"
Coach Tip: The Power of "If Nothing Changes..."

This question, "If nothing changes...", is incredibly powerful. It helps the client visualize the cost of inaction and creates a sense of urgency. Follow it up with the "magic wand" question to help them articulate their deepest desires, connecting to their spiritual longing for a thriving marriage.

Phase 3: Presenting Your Transformative Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you so much for sharing your heart. Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'm hearing: You're a woman of deep faith, longing to reconnect with John, to feel that intimacy and joy you once shared, and to truly live out God's design for your marriage. You've tried various approaches, but they haven't provided the lasting transformation you seek. Does that feel accurate?" (Pause for confirmation)
YOU:
"The approach I use is rooted in biblical principles and proven coaching methodologies, and it's designed to be different from what you've tried before. We don't just address symptoms; we dive deep into the root causes of marital disconnection, integrating your faith at every step. My 12-week 'Covenant Connection' program is a personalized journey where we work together to rebuild communication, reignite intimacy, and strengthen your spiritual foundation as a couple. Most of my clients experience significant shifts in their marital dynamic and personal peace within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"We'll cover areas like effective communication strategies, understanding each other's needs, conflict resolution through a faith lens, and spiritual intimacy practices. This isn't a quick fix; it's a commitment to transformational change, guided by God's Word and tailored specifically to you and John's unique situation."
Phase 4: Confident Pricing & Call to Action 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about the 'Covenant Connection' program, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive, faith-based support you and John have been looking for to truly transform your marriage?" (Wait for her response, ideally a 'yes' or positive affirmation)
YOU:
"Wonderful. Let me tell you how we can get started. The 'Covenant Connection' program is a 12-week commitment, and the investment for this personalized transformation is [State your price confidently, e.g., '$4,500' or 'three payments of $1,600']. This includes [list 2-3 key deliverables, e.g., '12 weekly 60-minute coaching sessions, personalized action plans, and unlimited email support between sessions']. I also offer a complimentary session with John to ensure he's aligned and comfortable with the process before we begin."
YOU:
"Given your desire to rekindle that connection and strengthen your spiritual bond, I truly believe this program could be a profound turning point for you both. How does that sound to you, Sarah? Are you ready to take this step towards a more joyful, God-honoring marriage?"

Navigating Objections with Grace and Conviction

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, understanding, and a clear articulation of your value, always anchoring back to their stated desires and your faith-based approach.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it / I need to pray about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and prayer is foundational to any significant step in faith. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think or pray about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that gives you pause? I want to make sure I've answered all your questions clearly so you can pray with full clarity." (This helps uncover the real objection beneath the surface.)
"It's too expensive / We've spent so much on retreats already."
"I hear you, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. It's true that investing in your marriage is a significant decision. You mentioned that previous retreats didn't 'stick.' My program is different because it's not a one-time event; it's a sustained, personalized transformation over 12 weeks, with consistent support and accountability. Consider the long-term cost of not addressing these issues ‚Äì the emotional toll, the spiritual impact, and the potential for greater marital strain. Many clients find that this focused, intensive approach yields lasting results that far outweigh the investment, saving them from years of continued struggle. We can also explore a payment plan if that makes it more accessible."
"I need to talk to John first."
"Absolutely, Sarah. John's buy-in is incredibly important for this journey. You mentioned he's active in church, and I believe he'll appreciate the faith-based foundation of this program. How about this: I'm happy to offer a brief, complimentary 15-minute call with both you and John, where he can meet me, ask any questions he has, and hear directly about how this program is designed to support both of you in building a stronger, more Christ-centered marriage. Would that be helpful to you both?" (This brings John into the conversation early.)
"I'm not sure if coaching is right for us / We're not 'that bad off'."
"That's a very common thought, Sarah. Many couples come to me not because their marriage is in crisis, but because they're simply feeling a drift, a lack of connection, or a desire to move from 'good' to 'great' in their relationship with God at the center. Coaching is about proactively building strength, developing new skills, and deepening intimacy before small issues become big ones. It's about investing in the marriage God designed for you, and creating a legacy of love and faith for your family. If you're feeling a longing for more, that's often God's gentle nudge towards growth."
Coach Tip: Your Authority and Faith

As a Faith-Based Marriage Coach, you carry a unique authority. When handling objections, speak from a place of conviction in your calling and the transformative power of God's design for marriage. Frame your responses not just as business logic, but as an invitation to greater spiritual alignment and marital flourishing.

Confident Pricing Presentation: Articulating Your Value

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but it's a critical step in establishing your value. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, not just a commodity. Your confidence in your pricing reflects your belief in the transformation you provide.

When stating your price, use these guidelines:

  1. State it Clearly and Concisely: No hedging, no apologies. "The investment for the 12-week 'Covenant Connection' program is $X."
  2. Anchor it to Value: Immediately follow the price with the core benefits and deliverables. "This includes 12 personalized coaching sessions, a customized marriage growth plan, and direct support between sessions."
  3. Offer Options (if applicable): If you have payment plans, mention them. "I also offer a convenient payment plan of three installments of $Y."
  4. Pause and Listen: After stating the price and value, pause. Let the client process. The next words should ideally come from them.

Case Study: Emily's Financial Breakthrough

From Imposter Syndrome to Confident Enrollment

Emily, 51

Certified Faith-Based Marriage Coach, former teacher

Emily, a newly certified coach, struggled with charging for her services. She felt her previous career as a teacher meant she should offer services for free or very low cost, leading to significant imposter syndrome around pricing. Her initial discovery calls often ended with her apologizing for her rates or offering deep discounts.

Intervention: We worked on reframing her value proposition, recognizing that her deep faith and specialized training provided unique, life-altering solutions. We practiced confident pricing scripts, focusing on the biblical concept of value and stewardship. She developed a clear understanding of her own worth and the investment required for true transformation.

Outcome: Within three months, Emily confidently signed four new clients at her full rate of $3,500 for a 10-week program. Her confidence soared, and she reported feeling a profound sense of alignment between her calling and her financial abundance. She stated, "I realized that by undercharging, I was actually devaluing the Holy Spirit's work through me. Now, I present my prices as an invitation to a powerful, sacred investment."

Coach Tip: Practice Out Loud!

The only way to become comfortable with your enrollment conversation is to practice. Record yourself, role-play with a fellow coach, or even practice in front of a mirror. The more you verbalize your script, your value proposition, and your pricing, the more natural and confident you will become.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive into Client Needs" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to actively listen and understand the client's current struggles, their emotional experience, what they've tried previously, and their deepest desires for their marriage, both in its current state and its ideal future. This helps you grasp their unique pain points and aspirations.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to talk to my spouse first," what is an effective strategy to address this objection?

An effective strategy is to offer a brief, complimentary call with both the prospect and their spouse. This allows the spouse to meet you, ask questions, and hear directly about the program, addressing their potential concerns and getting their buy-in early in the process.

3. According to the lesson, what is a crucial element to include immediately after stating your program's price during an enrollment conversation?

Immediately after stating your program's price, it is crucial to anchor it to value by clearly articulating the core benefits and deliverables included in the program (e.g., number of sessions, personalized plans, support). This helps the client understand what they are investing in.

4. What is a realistic annual income range for a Faith-Based Marriage Coach with a "Growing Practice" (approximately 5 active clients)?

For a "Growing Practice" with approximately 5 active clients, a realistic annual income range is estimated between $70,000 - $100,000, depending on program pricing.
