Your Prospect: Sarah, 49

Referral from a mutual friend. Scheduled a discovery call after visiting your website.

Her Situation
Married for 22 years. Feeling disconnected, communication breakdowns, children leaving home (empty nest syndrome creating new marital stress). Expressed a desire to "reignite the spark" and reconnect spiritually with her husband.
Budget Concern
"We've invested in marriage retreats before, but they never stuck. I'm worried about spending more money if it's not going to make a lasting difference."
Decision Style
Analytical, needs clear steps and a logical progression. Values evidence of effectiveness. Also deeply values faith-based principles.
Her Goal
"I want to feel close to my husband again, like we did when we first got married. And I want God to be at the center of our renewed connection."
Coach's Insight

Remember, a discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a discovery conversation. Your primary role is to listen deeply, understand their pain, and help them articulate their desired future. This builds trust and positions you as a compassionate problem-solver, not just a service provider.

Crafting Your Discovery Call Structure

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all necessary points while keeping the conversation natural and client-focused. Here's a proven 30-minute framework:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, it's wonderful to finally connect! Thank you for taking the time today. Before we dive in, how did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"Ah, [mutual friend's name] is a dear client! So glad she connected us. My goal for our chat today is to learn more about what's bringing you here, for you to understand how I work, and to see if my faith-based marriage coaching program is the right fit for you and your husband. Does that sound good?"
YOU:
"Great. So, Sarah, tell me ‚Äì what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on in your marriage that you're hoping to address?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned feeling disconnected and communication breakdowns, especially with the kids leaving. Can you tell me more about what that disconnection feels like day-to-day? How does it impact your spiritual life together?"
YOU:
"And what have you and your husband tried so far to address these issues? What worked, and what didn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see your marriage in a year, or even five years from now? And if you could wave a magic wand and truly reignite that spark and spiritual connection, what would your life and your marriage look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You and your husband are experiencing significant disconnection and communication challenges, particularly as you navigate the empty nest phase. You've tried various approaches, but they haven't provided lasting change, and you deeply desire to reconnect on a spiritual and emotional level, with God at the center. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different. My Faith-Based Marriage Renewal Program is a structured 12-week journey designed specifically for couples like you. We don't just talk about symptoms; we uncover the root causes of disconnection through a biblical lens, provide practical communication tools, and guide you in reigniting spiritual intimacy. Most of my clients report significant shifts in connection and understanding within the first few weeks, and lasting transformation by the end of the program."
YOU:
"We'd work together in weekly sessions, focusing on areas like Biblical Communication Principles, Rebuilding Emotional Intimacy, and Cultivating Shared Spiritual Practices. You'll also receive resources and exercises to complete between sessions, ensuring consistent progress."
Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive and faith-centered support you and your husband have been looking for to achieve that deep reconnection?"
YOU:
"Fantastic! Let me tell you about the program investment and how we can get started."
Coach's Insight

Practice summarizing the client's problem in their own words. This validates their feelings and shows you truly heard them. Use phrases like, "What I'm hearing is..." or "It sounds like you're experiencing..." This is powerful for building trust and setting up your solution.

Navigating Common Objections

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your ability to address these with empathy and confidence is key. Remember, an objection is often a request for more information or reassurance, not a definitive "no."

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to talk to my husband/spouse about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is a significant decision for both of you, and it's essential that you're both on board. What specifically do you think your husband might want to know or discuss? Perhaps we can cover those points now so you're fully equipped for that conversation, or even better, if he's open to it, we could schedule a brief follow-up call with him to answer any questions directly?"
"The investment is a bit high for us right now. We've spent so much already."
"I hear you, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot in your marriage already, and you want to ensure this investment yields lasting results. My program is structured to provide a deep, transformative experience that addresses the root causes, unlike superficial retreats. What would it cost you and your husband to continue feeling disconnected and drifting apart over the next year? Often, the emotional and relational cost far outweighs the financial investment in truly effective support. We can discuss flexible payment plans if that helps make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure we have the time for a 12-week commitment."
"That's a valid concern, Sarah. Life is busy, especially with the changes you're experiencing. However, consider what happens if you don't commit to dedicated time for your marriage now. Often, postponing essential work leads to deeper issues down the line. My program is designed to integrate into busy lives, with weekly sessions and practical, manageable exercises. We're talking about investing an hour or two a week to transform a foundational relationship ‚Äì a small commitment for a lifetime of renewed connection. How much time are you currently spending trying to 'fix' things without a clear path?"
"How is this different from marriage counseling or previous retreats?"
"That's an excellent question, Sarah, and it's important to understand the distinction. While traditional counseling often focuses on past issues and coping mechanisms, my faith-based coaching program is future-focused and action-oriented. We leverage biblical principles not just as guidance, but as practical tools for transformation. Retreats offer a temporary high, but often lack the sustained support and personalized accountability needed for lasting change. My 12-week program provides consistent, weekly guidance, personalized strategies, and ongoing support to ensure you build new habits and foundational understanding that truly sticks, all grounded in your faith."
Coach's Insight

When handling objections, always lead with empathy ("I understand," "That's a valid concern"). Then, reframe the objection to highlight the value of your solution. Finally, ask a clarifying question to uncover the deeper concern or move the conversation forward.

Confidently Presenting Your Investment

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but it's a critical moment. Approach it with the same confidence and conviction you have in your coaching abilities. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, and your fees reflect your expertise and the profound value you deliver.

Presenting Your Program Investment

After summarizing your program, confidently state your price. For example:

12-Week Faith-Based Marriage Renewal Program
$3,000 (or 3 monthly payments of $1,100)
  • 12 x 60-minute private coaching sessions (weekly)
  • Personalized action plan and resources
  • Direct messaging support between sessions
  • Access to exclusive client portal with additional tools
  • Spiritual discernment and prayer integration

"Sarah, the investment for the 12-week Faith-Based Marriage Renewal Program is $3,000. Many clients choose our flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $1,100. This investment covers comprehensive support, personalized guidance, and the tools you need to create lasting change and deeply reconnect your marriage, with God at the center."

Pause after stating the price. Let them react. Avoid filling the silence. Your confidence in your value will speak volumes.

Mastering the Call to Action

After presenting your program and addressing any initial questions, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Your Call to Action (CTA) should be clear, simple, and direct.

Powerful Closing Lines to Practice

  1. "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed today, I truly believe my program is exactly what you and your husband need to reignite your connection. Are you ready to get started?"
  2. "Given your goals to rekindle intimacy and strengthen your spiritual bond, the 12-week program is designed to deliver those results. How does moving forward with enrollment sound to you?"
  3. "I'm confident we can help you and your husband achieve the marriage you envision. What's the best way for us to move forward and secure your spot in the program?"
  4. "It sounds like you're ready for lasting change. If you're ready to commit to this journey, we can complete the enrollment process today, and I'll send you your welcome materials immediately."

Always have your next step ready: a link to enroll, a payment portal, or a calendar link to book the first session. Make it easy for them to say "yes."

Coach's Insight

A strong "Why Now?" is often the final push clients need. During your discovery call, subtly highlight the cost of inaction ‚Äì what they stand to lose by *not* addressing their marital issues now. This creates a gentle urgency.

Check Your Understanding

1. During the "Understand the Problem" phase, what is a key question to ask that helps a prospect envision their future?

A key question is: "If you could wave a magic wand and truly reignite that spark and spiritual connection, what would your life and your marriage look like?" This helps them connect with their desired outcome and builds motivation.

2. Sarah expresses concern about the program's investment, stating, "We've spent so much already." How should you initially respond to this objection?

Start with empathy: "I hear you, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot in your marriage already, and you want to ensure this investment yields lasting results." Then, reframe the cost of inaction.

3. What is a crucial element to include when presenting your program's pricing to convey confidence and value?

Confidence in your voice and a clear, concise statement of the price, followed by a pause. Connect the investment directly to the transformative results and the comprehensive support provided. Avoid apologies or immediate justifications.

4. Why is it important to have a clear and direct Call to Action (CTA) at the end of a discovery call?

A clear CTA removes ambiguity and guides the prospect to the next logical step. It demonstrates your confidence in your solution and makes it easy for them to enroll, preventing indecision or "thinking about it" indefinitely.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured yet empathetic conversation focused on listening, understanding, and serving.
  • A strong call structure (Rapport, Problem, Solution, Close) ensures you cover all bases and guide the prospect effectively.
  • Objections are opportunities to reframe value and build deeper trust, not roadblocks.
  • Present your pricing with unwavering confidence, anchoring it to the transformative results you offer.
  • Always conclude with a clear, direct Call to Action, making it simple for clients to enroll.
  • Your faith-based marriage coaching practice holds significant income potential, allowing you to align purpose with prosperity.
