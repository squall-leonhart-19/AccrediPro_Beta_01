Opening Case Study

Sarah's Pricing Predicament

Client Profile: Sarah, 48-year-old former teacher, Certified Faith-Based Marriage Coach

Challenge: Sarah launched her coaching practice six months ago, offering a single 1:1 package at $1500 for three months. She loves her clients but finds her income inconsistent and feels overwhelmed. She's hesitant to raise prices because she wants to help everyone, but her current model isn't sustainable for her family or her desire to grow.

Goal: Create a financial strategy that allows her to serve a wider audience, achieve financial stability, and grow her practice without burnout, all while honoring her faith values.

Sarah's experience is common for coaches transitioning from other careers. The desire to serve often conflicts with the need for financial sustainability. This lesson will provide Sarah, and you, with the tools to navigate this delicate balance, transforming her pricing predicament into a pathway for growth and greater impact.

1. Understanding Revenue Diversification Strategies

In the entrepreneurial journey, particularly in purpose-driven fields like faith-based coaching, relying on a single income stream can create instability. Revenue diversification is about creating multiple pathways for income, which not only stabilizes your business but also expands your reach and impact.

Think of your coaching practice not just as a service, but as a ministry with a mission. Just as a church relies on various forms of giving and outreach, your practice can thrive through diverse offerings.

Here are key strategies to consider:

  • Premium 1:1 Coaching Packages: Your foundational offering, providing deep, personalized transformation. These are high-value, high-touch services.
  • Group Coaching Programs: As discussed in Module 34, Lesson 4, these allow you to serve multiple couples simultaneously, leveraging your time and reducing the cost per client.
  • Online Courses & Digital Products: Self-paced programs, workbooks, devotionals, or meditation bundles that offer accessible entry points and passive income.
  • Workshops & Retreats: Intensive, in-person or virtual events that provide immersive experiences and higher price points.
  • Speaking Engagements: Sharing your expertise at churches, conferences, or community events, which can also serve as powerful lead generators.
  • Affiliate Partnerships: Recommending trusted resources (books, assessments, other services) and earning a commission.

A diversified revenue model can insulate you from market fluctuations and allow you to serve clients across different financial capacities, aligning with a holistic approach to Kingdom service.

Coach's Tip

Start small, scale smart: Don't try to launch all revenue streams at once. Begin with your core 1:1 offering, perfect it, then gradually introduce a group program or a simple digital product. Many successful coaches, like Maria (52, former nurse), started with 1:1, then added a "Rekindle Your Flame" group coaching program, which now brings in over $5,000 per launch with 10-15 couples. Focus on quality over quantity.

2. Developing Tiered Pricing Structures

A well-designed tiered pricing model allows you to serve a broader spectrum of clients while ensuring you are fairly compensated for your expertise and time. This strategy helps overcome the "either/or" dilemma of being affordable or profitable.

Consider the "Good, Better, Best" approach:

Tier Offering Value Proposition Typical Price Range
Good (Entry-Level) Digital Products (e.g., "7-Day Prayer Challenge for Couples" workbook, mini-course) Accessible, self-paced, foundational knowledge, low commitment. $27 - $197
Better (Mid-Range) Group Coaching Programs (e.g., "The C.O.V.E.N.A.N.T. Connection Journey" 6-week program) Community support, structured curriculum, direct access to coach, significant transformation. $497 - $1,497 per couple
Best (Premium) 1:1 Tailored Coaching Packages (e.g., 3-6 month "Covenant Renewal Immersion") Highly personalized, deep dive, direct and intensive support, accelerated results. $2,500 - $10,000+ per package

Crafting Your Premium 1:1 Packages

Your premium packages should reflect the immense value and personalized attention you provide. They are not just about hours, but about transformation. When structuring these, consider:

  • Duration: Typically 3, 6, or 12 months. Longer commitments lead to deeper change.
  • Inclusions: Number of 60-90 minute sessions, email/messaging support, personalized resources, assessments, follow-up check-ins.
  • Exclusivity: Limited spots, direct access to you.
  • The "Why": Focus on the outcome, not just the features. What life-altering results will couples experience? (e.g., "Reignite Intimacy & Communication," "Build an Unshakeable God-Centered Marriage").

For example, a 6-month premium package might include 12 bi-weekly 75-minute sessions, unlimited email support, personalized C.O.V.E.N.A.N.T. framework action plans, and access to a private resource library. This comprehensive support justifies a higher investment.

Coach's Tip

Overcoming pricing fears: Many coaches, especially women pivoting later in life, struggle with charging their worth. Remember your expertise, your investment in this certification, and the profound impact you have. Frame your pricing as an investment in their marriage, not an expense. When you honor your value, clients will too. Sarah (our case study) realized her $1500 package was significantly undervalued for the transformation she provided, and later successfully launched a 6-month premium package at $4,500, attracting committed clients.

3. Calculating Your Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and Profit Margins

To ensure sustainability, you must understand the true cost of delivering your services. This involves calculating your Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and subsequently your profit margins.

For a service-based business, COGS typically includes direct expenses tied to delivering a specific service or product. For a marriage coach, this might include:

  • Direct Labor: Your time spent coaching (if you pay yourself an hourly rate for this, or if you outsource part of the delivery).
  • Materials/Resources per client: Cost of any assessments, workbooks, or specialized software subscriptions directly used for that client/group.
  • Payment Processing Fees: A percentage of each transaction.
  • Platform Fees for Digital Products: If using a platform like Teachable or Kajabi, the per-sale fee or a portion of the monthly fee attributed to that product.

Example Calculation:

Let's say you offer a 6-week group coaching program for $797 per couple. You aim for 10 couples per group.

  • Revenue per group: 10 couples x $797 = $7,970
  • COGS for the group:
    • Your direct time (e.g., 20 hours x $50/hour internal rate): $1,000
    • Online platform fee per group: $100
    • Workbook printing/digital access fee per couple ($10 x 10 couples): $100
    • Payment processing fees (approx. 3% of revenue): $7,970 x 0.03 = $239.10
  • Total COGS: $1,000 + $100 + $100 + $239.10 = $1,439.10
  • Gross Profit: $7,970 - $1,439.10 = $6,530.90
  • Gross Profit Margin: ($6,530.90 / $7,970) x 100% = 81.9%

Understanding these numbers allows you to price strategically, ensuring each offering contributes meaningfully to your overall financial health.

4. Implementing Accurate Financial Forecasting and Budgeting

Just as you help couples create a vision for their future, you need a financial vision for your practice. Financial forecasting involves estimating future income and expenses, while budgeting is the process of planning how you will spend and save your money over a specific period.

This is where your professional legitimacy shines. A well-managed business fosters trust and allows you to plan for growth, new offerings, and even charitable giving.

Key Steps:

  1. Review Past Performance: Analyze your income and expenses from previous months/quarters.
  2. Project Income: Based on your tiered pricing, anticipate how many 1:1 clients, group coaching spots, and digital product sales you expect. Be realistic but also allow for growth.
  3. Estimate Expenses: Categorize your fixed costs (e.g., website hosting, CRM, insurance) and variable costs (e.g., marketing spend, new software).
  4. Create a Profit & Loss (P&L) Statement: A simple spreadsheet can track your revenue, COGS, operating expenses, and net profit.
  5. Set Financial Goals: What is your target monthly or annual income? How much do you want to save or reinvest?

Regularly reviewing your budget (monthly or quarterly) allows you to make informed decisions, identify areas for cost reduction, and allocate resources effectively for strategic expansion. For instance, if your forecast shows strong interest in a specific group program, you might budget more for targeted social media ads.

Coach's Tip

Leverage simple tools: You don't need complex accounting software initially. A simple spreadsheet for tracking income and expenses is a great start. Tools like QuickBooks Self-Employed or FreshBooks can automate much of this. Remember, good stewardship involves diligence and transparency, both to yourself and to God, in how you manage your resources.

5. Principles of Reinvesting Profits for Sustainable Growth

True financial stewardship isn't just about accumulating wealth; it's about strategically utilizing resources to further your mission and impact. Reinvesting profits back into your business is a biblical principle of "sowing" to reap a greater harvest (2 Corinthians 9:6).

Where can you wisely reinvest?

  • Professional Development: Advanced certifications, specialized workshops, business coaching.
  • Marketing & Sales: Professional branding, targeted advertising, SEO, social media management.
  • Technology & Systems: Upgrading your CRM, scheduling software, course delivery platforms for better efficiency and client experience.
  • Team Expansion: Hiring a virtual assistant, social media manager, or copywriter to free up your time for coaching and strategic vision.
  • Creating New Offerings: Investing in the development of a new online course or high-value retreat.
  • Emergency Fund: Building a buffer for unexpected challenges, demonstrating prudence.

By reinvesting, you create a virtuous cycle: more resources lead to better services, broader reach, and ultimately, greater impact.

6. Maximizing Kingdom Impact Through Financial Stewardship

As a faith-based marriage coach, your financial success is intrinsically linked to your ability to make a Kingdom impact. When your practice is financially sound, you are better positioned to:

  • Offer Scholarships or Pro Bono Work: Provide services to couples in genuine financial need, extending grace and support.
  • Support Ministry Initiatives: Tithe or donate to ministries, missions, or charities aligned with your values.
  • Create Accessible Resources: Invest in developing free or low-cost resources (blogs, podcasts, free workshops) that bless a wider audience.
  • Be a Generous Employer: If you grow to hire staff, you can provide fair wages and a supportive work environment.
  • Model Generosity: Your financial discipline and generosity can inspire others in your community.

Financial stewardship is not just about personal gain; it's about being a conduit for God's blessings. It empowers you to build a legacy of transformation, one marriage at a time, while also supporting your family and community. Your business is a tool for God's glory, and managing it well is an act of worship.

Coach's Tip

Beyond the bottom line: Regularly reflect on how your financial decisions align with your faith values and your mission. Ask yourself: "How can this financial choice enable me to serve more deeply, give more generously, and honor God more fully?" This perspective shifts profit from a selfish pursuit to a powerful engine for Kingdom work.

Case Study Conclusion

Sarah's Sustainable Success

After implementing the strategies learned, Sarah transformed her practice:

  • She redesigned her 1:1 coaching into three-tiered packages (3-month "Covenant Foundation" at $2,700, 6-month "Covenant Renewal" at $4,500, and a premium 9-month "Legacy Builder" at $6,000).
  • She launched a "Prayer & Purpose for Couples" mini-course as a digital product for $97, generating consistent passive income.

  • She hired a part-time virtual assistant to handle administrative tasks, freeing up 10 hours a week for coaching and strategic planning.

Sarah now earns an average of $7,500-$10,000 per month, working fewer hours than before. She feels confident, financially stable, and deeply fulfilled, knowing her sustainable pricing allows her to serve more couples effectively and even offer a scholarship spot in each group program. Her imposter syndrome has been replaced with a deep sense of purpose and professional confidence.

Key Takeaways

  • Diversify Revenue Streams: Build business stability and expand impact by offering a mix of 1:1 coaching, group programs, and digital products.
  • Implement Tiered Pricing: Design "Good, Better, Best" models to serve a broad client base, ensuring accessibility and valuing your expertise appropriately.
  • Understand Your Costs: Accurately calculate COGS and profit margins for each offering to ensure sustainable and profitable growth.
  • Practice Financial Forecasting: Use budgeting and forecasting to make informed decisions, plan for expansion, and maintain financial health.
  • Reinvest Strategically: Reallocate profits into professional development, marketing, systems, or team expansion to foster sustainable growth and maximize Kingdom impact.
