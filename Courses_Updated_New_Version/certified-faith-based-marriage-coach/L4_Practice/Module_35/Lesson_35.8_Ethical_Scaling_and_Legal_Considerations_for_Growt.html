1. The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is arguably the most vital step in your client acquisition process. It's where potential clients transition from being "interested" to "committed." For a faith-based marriage coach, this call is more than just a business transaction; it's a ministry opportunity. It's where you listen with empathy, discern their spiritual and relational needs, and gently guide them toward the transformative power of a God-centered marriage.

Think of it as a sacred conversation. You're not just selling a service; you're offering hope, guidance, and a pathway to healing rooted in divine wisdom. Approaching it with this mindset shifts the energy from transactional to transformational.

Coach Tip: Your Mindset Matters

Before every discovery call, take a moment to pray. Ask for discernment, wisdom, and the ability to truly hear your prospect. Remember, you are a vessel for God's work. This shifts your focus from "getting a client" to "serving God's children," which naturally builds confidence and authenticity.

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all necessary points while maintaining a natural, empathetic flow. This 30-minute structure is a guideline, allowing flexibility for deeper conversations where needed.

4. Navigating Common Objections

It's natural for potential clients to have reservations. Your role isn't to dismiss them, but to address them with empathy, confidence, and a clear understanding of the value you offer. Here are some common objections and how to respond.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's too expensive. I'm not sure we can afford it right now."
"I completely understand that investing in your marriage is a significant decision, and it's wise to consider your finances. Many couples I work with have felt the same way. What they've found, however, is that the cost of inaction â€“ the continued emotional distance, the spiritual void, the potential for greater marital distress â€“ far outweighs the investment in creating a thriving, God-honoring marriage. This isn't just a cost; it's an investment in your spiritual well-being, your family's foundation, and your legacy. We also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible. Does that help alleviate some concern?"
"I need to think about it / Discuss with my husband."
"Absolutely, this is an important decision that impacts both of you. To help you in that discussion, what specifically do you need to think about, or what questions do you anticipate your husband might have? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps whether this faith-based approach is truly different from what you've tried before? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed choice together."
"We've tried counseling before, and it didn't work. I'm not sure coaching will be any different."
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It takes courage to open up again after a disappointing experience. I hear your skepticism, and it's valid. Many of my clients come to me after trying traditional counseling. The key difference with faith-based marriage coaching, and specifically with the COVENANT Frameworkâ„¢, is our explicit integration of biblical principles and spiritual disciplines. We don't just address communication techniques; we help you align your marriage with God's design, inviting His presence into your relationship in a way secular approaches cannot. It's a holistic, proactive, and spiritually guided journey. Does that distinction resonate with what you're seeking?"
Coach Tip: Reframe & Validate

When facing objections, always start by validating their concern ("I understand," "That's a valid point"). Then, reframe the objection into a question or an opportunity to clarify value. Don't be afraid to ask deeper questions to uncover the root of their hesitation.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call in faith-based marriage coaching, beyond simply "selling"?

The primary purpose is to connect with the prospect, listen to their needs with empathy, discern if faith-based marriage coaching is the right fit, and gently guide them toward a transformative, God-centered solution. It's a sacred conversation focused on service and discernment.

2. When a prospect expresses a budget concern, what is an effective strategy for addressing it, as demonstrated in the lab?

An effective strategy is to validate their concern, then reframe the "cost" as an "investment" in their marriage's spiritual and emotional future. Highlight the cost of inaction and the long-term benefits, and mention flexible payment plans if available.

3. What is a key question to ask a prospect during Phase 2 (Deep Dive) to help them visualize their desired outcome?

A key question is: "If you could wave a 'spiritual' magic wand and truly transform this, what would your marriage look and feel like? What role would faith play in that transformation?" This helps them articulate their vision and connects it to your faith-based solution.

4. How should you present your pricing during the closing phase of a discovery call?

Present your pricing confidently and clearly, immediately after outlining the value and inclusions of your program. State the full investment, followed by any payment plan options, and then pause to allow the prospect to respond. Avoid hesitation or apologizing for your fees.
Key Takeaways
  • Approach discovery calls with a ministry mindset, focusing on serving and discerning, not just selling.
  • Utilize a structured 30-minute call to build rapport, deeply understand client needs, present your faith-based solution, and close with confidence.
  • Practice active listening and ask open-ended questions to uncover emotional and spiritual pain points, as well as desired outcomes.
  • Address objections by validating, reframing, and clarifying the unique value of your faith-based marriage coaching.
  • Present your pricing clearly and confidently, anchoring it to the transformative value and spiritual investment of your program.
