Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you begin practicing, watch out for these "New Coach" traps that can derail a therapeutic session.

The Mistake Why It Happens The Mindfulness Fix
Rushing the Silence We feel uncomfortable when the client is quiet and try to fill it. Count to five slowly after a client finishes speaking before you respond.
"Fixing" the Emotion We want the client to feel better immediately. Allow the emotion to exist. Use: "It makes sense that you feel this way."
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome makes us want to prove we know the science. Keep explanations brief. Focus 80% on the client's direct experience.
Using "Why" Questions We want to analyze the past. Use "What" or "How" to stay in the present. (e.g., "How does that feel now?")

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask Elena where she feels "lost" in her body?

It moves the experience from an abstract, overwhelming thought ("I am a failure") to a manageable physical sensation. This is the core of "somatic grounding" in mindfulness therapy.

2. What is the "liminal space" mentioned in the script?

It is the "in-between" state where a person has left one identity but hasn't fully integrated into the next. Mindfulness helps clients stay present in this space without panicking.

3. If Elena starts crying, what is the most "mindful" response?

Simply stay present and silent for a moment. You might say, "I'm right here with you. Let the tears come; they are just energy moving through." Avoid handing her a tissue immediately, as that can sometimes signal "please stop crying."

4. How does labeling the feeling as "a transition" help the client?

It provides "Cognitive Defusion." It separates the person from the problem. She is no longer "a failure"; she is someone "experiencing a transition."

Lab Key Takeaways
  • Validation over Fixation: Your primary job is to validate the client's current state, not to rush them into "positivity."
  • Somatic Awareness: Always bring the conversation back to the body to ground the client in the present moment.
  • Identity Labeling: Use mindfulness terms like "liminal space" to give clients a framework for their confusion.
  • Confidence through Practice: Speaking the coaching language out loud is the only way to overcome imposter syndrome.
