To navigate this complexity, we use a hierarchical reasoning framework. We don't just treat where it hurts; we treat where the system is most restricted.

3. Differential Considerations

As an advanced practitioner, you must rank your considerations. What is the most likely driver of her symptoms?

5. The Phased Intervention Protocol

For a client this complex, we do not attempt to fix everything in Session 1. We use a phased approach to prevent "healing crises" or system overwhelm.

Phase 1: Stabilization (Sessions 1-3)

Goal: Down-regulate the sympathetic nervous system and build trust in the tissues.
Tools: Stillpoint induction (CV4), Pelvic Diaphragm release, and Occipital-Cranial Base release. We want to lower that CSR rate from 14 down to a healthy 8-10.

Phase 2: Decompression (Sessions 4-7)

Goal: Address the structural "Global" restrictions.
Tools: Sphenoid decompression, Temporal ear pulls (to clear the Jugular Foramen), and Dural Tube glides. This phase targets the migraines and brain fog directly.

Phase 3: Integration & SER (Sessions 8+)

Goal: Address the "disconnection" and emotional components.
Tools: SomatoEmotional Release (SER) techniques, Mouthwork (if appropriate for TMJ tension), and long-distance integration.
