Managing 'Flare-Up' Risks

A critical skill for the premium practitioner is knowing when to back off. During an active autoimmune flare, the client's system is already overwhelmed. Aggressive fascial unwinding can release more inflammatory mediators into the bloodstream, potentially worsening the flare.

"Less is more" is the golden rule here. If you palpate high systemic heat (a feeling of radiation or vibration under your hands), prioritize Stillpoint induction. A Stillpoint acts as a "reboot" for the autonomic nervous system, allowing the immune system to "stand down" temporarily.
