Common Practitioner Pitfalls

As you transition to a paid professional, avoid these four common mistakes that I see many new practitioners make during their first month.

Mistake Why It Hurts Your Practice The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Confuses the client and sounds like you're "selling" theory. Focus on the benefit (relief, sleep, calm) rather than the anatomy.
Under-Pricing Attracts clients who don't value the work; leads to burnout. Set your rate between $120-$200 based on your local market.
Ignoring Forms Creates legal liability and looks unprofessional. Use the DFY Intake Forms for every single client.
Skipping Follow-up Clients feel like "just a number" and don't rebook. Send a "How are you feeling?" email 24 hours after the session.
