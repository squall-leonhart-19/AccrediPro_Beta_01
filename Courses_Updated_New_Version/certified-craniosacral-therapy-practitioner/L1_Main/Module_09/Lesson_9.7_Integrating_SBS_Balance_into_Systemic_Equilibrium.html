The SBS-Sacrum Link: The Dural Pump

The relationship between the Occiput (the posterior half of the SBS) and the Sacrum is known as the Core Link. They are physically connected by the dural tube, which is firmly attached at the Foramen Magnum and the second sacral segment (S2).

During the Flexion phase of the CRI, the SBS rises (elevates) and the Sacral base moves posteriorly (nutation). This synchronous movement acts as a "pump" for Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF). If the SBS is restricted, the sacral rhythm often becomes sluggish or asynchronous, leading to systemic issues like digestive sluggishness or pelvic congestion.

Systemic Integration Techniques

To move from SBS correction to systemic equilibrium, use the Synchronicity Hold. This technique involves one hand at the Occiput (cradle) and the other hand under the Sacrum.

  1. Establish the Core Link: Feel the connection through the dural tube between your hands.
  2. Monitor Synchrony: Are the Occiput and Sacrum moving in harmony? Are they both entering flexion at the same time?
  3. Invite Stillpoint: If they are out of sync, gently "wait" at the end-range of the easier phase until a systemic stillpoint occurs.
  4. Follow the Reorganization: As the system restarts, you will often feel a "surge" of fluid (the PRM) that feels more powerful and symmetrical than before.
Coach Tip: Longevity and Ergonomics

When performing long integration holds, ensure your own posture is supported. Sit with your feet flat, spine neutral, and elbows tucked. If you are strained, your client will palpate your tension, which inhibits their equilibrium. Your "neutral presence" is your most powerful tool.

Functional Post-Correction Assessment

How do you know you're "done"? A premium practitioner never assumes; they verify. After integrating the SBS, perform a final global assessment of the CRI. You are looking for the "Three S's":

  • Symmetry: Does the left side of the vault move with the same timing and range as the right?
  • Smoothness: Is the transition between flexion and extension fluid, or is there a "stutter" or "jerkiness"?
  • Strength (Amplitude): Has the vitality of the rhythm increased? A successful SBS integration usually results in a more robust, "fuller" feeling pulse.
