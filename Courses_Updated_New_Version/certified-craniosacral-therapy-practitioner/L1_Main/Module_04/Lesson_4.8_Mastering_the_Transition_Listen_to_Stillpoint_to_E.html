Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Mistake Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome; feeling the need to "prove" your knowledge. Use 1-2 simple sentences, then return to therapeutic silence.
Breaking Contact The practitioner gets distracted or thinks the "work" is done during the pause. Maintain precise, gentle contact. The stillpoint is the peak of the work.
Rushing the Exit Feeling awkward during the silence. A stillpoint can last 30 seconds or 5 minutes. Wait for the rhythm to restart naturally.
Clinical Jargon Using terms like "ventricular compression" with a stressed client. Use metaphors like "the reset button" or "the quiet between breaths."
Maya's Presence Tip

If you feel nervous during the silence, focus on your own feet on the floor. Grounding yourself helps the client feel safe enough to stay in the "void" of the stillpoint without anxiety.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why might a client feel anxious when a stillpoint occurs?

Clients, especially those in high-stress roles, are conditioned to be "active." The sudden cessation of the craniosacral rhythm can feel like a loss of control or a "void," triggering a minor stress response if not properly guided.

2. What is the most important "tool" a practitioner uses during the actual stillpoint?

Silence and Presence. While hand position is the physical tool, your ability to remain grounded and silent allows the client's nervous system to complete its reorganization without external interruption.

3. A 2021 study on autonomic regulation showed that stillpoint induction specifically impacts which part of the nervous system?

It shifts the balance toward the Parasympathetic Nervous System (PNS), reducing cortisol levels and heart rate variability (HRV) markers associated with high stress.

4. How should you respond if a client asks, "Did I fall asleep?" after a stillpoint?

Validate them: "It can certainly feel like that! You entered a state of very deep relaxation where your brain waves slowed downâ€”similar to deep sleep, but your body was actually busy doing some great 'housekeeping' work."
