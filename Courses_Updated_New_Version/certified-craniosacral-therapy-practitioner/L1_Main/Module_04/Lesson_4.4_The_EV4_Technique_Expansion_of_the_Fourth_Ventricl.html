The Anatomical Vector of Expansion

The fourth ventricle lies anterior to the cerebellum and posterior to the pons and medulla oblongata. When we perform the EV4, our goal is to increase the transverse diameter of this space. Unlike the CV4, where we follow the occiput as it moves medially, in the EV4, we wait for the flexion phase.

During flexion, the paired bones of the cranium externally rotate, and the occiput widens. To perform the EV4, the practitioner places their hands in the "Cradle" position (similar to CV4) but instead of resisting the widening, we subtly encourage it. This encourages the lateral recesses of the fourth ventricle to expand, drawing CSF into the space.

Step-by-Step EV4 Protocol

Success with the EV4 requires a level of tactile sensitivity that you have been developing since Module 1. Follow these steps with exquisite presence:

  1. Positioning: Sit at the head of the table. Place your thenar eminences (the meaty part of your thumbs) on the lateral aspects of the occipital squama, just medial to the occipitomastoid sutures.
  2. Listening: Tune in to the Cranial Rhythmic Impulse. Identify the expansion (flexion) and narrowing (extension) phases.
  3. The Invitation: As the client's system enters the flexion phase, follow the lateral movement. When the movement reaches its natural end, provide a micro-ounce of further lateral encouragement.
  4. The Resistance: As the system tries to return to extension (narrowing), do not allow it to narrow. Maintain the wide position.
  5. Maximum Fill: You will feel a build-up of "fluid pressure." It may feel like holding a balloon that is being slowly inflated.
  6. The Stillpoint: Eventually, the system will stop struggling to narrow and will enter a Stillpoint in the expanded state.
  7. The Release: Hold this stillness until you feel the "re-ignition"‚Äîa strong, wide, and vital surge of fluid. Gently release your hands as the new rhythm establishes itself.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which phase of the Cranial Rhythmic Impulse does the EV4 technique primarily encourage?

The EV4 primarily encourages the flexion phase (the expansion or widening phase) of the CRI.

2. How does the hand vector of the EV4 differ from the CV4?

In the CV4, the vector is medial (encouraging narrowing/extension). In the EV4, the vector is lateral (encouraging widening/flexion).

3. What is the physiological goal of the "Maximum Fill" state in EV4?

The goal is to create a "negative pressure" effect that draws an optimal volume of Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) into the fourth ventricle to replenish and vitalize the nervous system.

4. For which client presentation is EV4 usually more appropriate than CV4?

EV4 is best for clients with chronic depletion, fatigue, burnout, or low vitality, whereas CV4 is often better for high-stress, inflammatory, or acute pain states.
