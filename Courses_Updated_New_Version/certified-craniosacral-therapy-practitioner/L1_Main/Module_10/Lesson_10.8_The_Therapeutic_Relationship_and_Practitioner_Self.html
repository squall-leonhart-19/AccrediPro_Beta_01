Coach Tip #2: Silence is a Tool

Don't feel the need to fill every silence. In CST, the "Stillpoint" is where the healing happens. If the client falls silent, your job is to maintain the "hold" and your own presence. Your quiet confidence is more healing than any explanation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. Awareness is the first step toward mastery.

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to use too much "science" during the session can pull the client out of their body and back into their logical brain. Save the deep theory for the post-session talk.
  • Breaking Contact Abruptly: Always "feather off" your touch. A sudden break in contact can feel like a shock to a deeply relaxed nervous system.
  • Projecting Outcomes: Avoid saying "This will definitely cure your headache today." Instead, use: "We're creating the environment for your body to begin its own healing process."
  • Ignoring Your Own Body: If you are tense, the client will feel it. Remember to ground yourself through your feet before you place your hands on the client.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we use "light touch" (5 grams) instead of deep pressure in Craniosacral Therapy?

To bypass the body's natural resistance/defense mechanisms and communicate directly with the autonomic nervous system and fascia without triggering a "guarding" response.

2. Elena tells you she feels "nothing is happening" 20 minutes into the session. What is the best coaching response?

"I hear you. Sometimes the most profound changes are the quietest. Your system is currently processing at a very deep level. Let's just stay with this stillness for a few more minutes."

3. What is the "feathering off" technique?

Slowly and gently reducing pressure and surface area of contact until you are no longer touching the client, preventing a "startle" response in their nervous system.

4. How does grounding yourself benefit the client?

It allows you to act as a "neutral observer" and provides a stable, calm energetic presence that the client's nervous system can co-regulate with.
Coach Tip #4: The Follow-Up

Always send a short text or email 24 hours after the first session. Ask: "How is your system integrating the work today?" This small gesture builds the "Meaningful Work" and "Legitimacy" you're striving for in your new career.

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Communication is Treatment: How you explain the work is just as important as how you perform the work.
  • Safety First: The primary goal of the first session is to make the client's nervous system feel safe enough to let go.
  • Validate Sensations: Use the "Client Says/Your Response" guide to normalize the unique experiences of CST.
  • Professional Presence: Ground yourself, speak slowly, and embrace the therapeutic power of silence.
  • Value Your Expertise: Remember that you are providing a specialized service that helps people like Elena reclaim their lives from chronic stress.
