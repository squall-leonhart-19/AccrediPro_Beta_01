Mitigating Practitioner Projection

Practitioner projection occurs when our own physical tensions, emotional states, or clinical expectations are "projected" onto the client‚Äôs tissue. If you have a sore shoulder, you might "find" shoulder tension in every client you touch.

To mitigate this, we use the Fulcrum of Neutrality. This involves three specific checks:

  1. Physical Check: Are my shoulders relaxed? Is my breath shallow?
  2. Emotional Check: Am I carrying the stress of my last client into this room?
  3. Intellectual Check: Have I already decided what is wrong before I‚Äôve finished the Global Assessment?

Maintaining the 'Wide-Angle Lens'

In Lesson 3.3, we learned about listening stations. The "Wide-Angle Lens" is the ability to keep your hands on one station (e.g., the ankles) while your internal awareness encompasses the entire body. A 2021 study on therapeutic touch suggested that practitioners who maintained a "global focus" noted 30% more subtle rhythmic shifts than those who focused solely on the local contact point.

Clinical Boundaries: Listen vs. Unwind

One of the most common mistakes is jumping into "fixing" (Unwinding) before the "listening" is complete. Within the P.U.L.S.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, Listening is the diagnostic phase that informs the treatment.

When to stay in Listen mode:

  • The rhythm is still disorganized or chaotic.
  • The client‚Äôs system is still "sizing you up" (sympathetic dominance).
  • You haven't yet identified the Primary Fulcrum (the source of the tension).

When to transition to Unwind mode:

  • The tissue begins to "melt" or soften under your hands.
  • A clear direction of ease is revealed.
  • The client takes a spontaneous deep "cleansing" breath.
Coach Tip

If you aren't sure whether to move into Unwinding, stay in Listening. You can never listen too much, but you can certainly intervene too early.
