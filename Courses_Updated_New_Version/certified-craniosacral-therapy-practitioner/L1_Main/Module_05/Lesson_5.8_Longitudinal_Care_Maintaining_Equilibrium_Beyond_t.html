Common Practitioner Pitfalls

New practitioners often struggle with the "quiet" nature of equilibrium. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to explain the entire anatomy of the sphenoid bone during the first five minutes. Keep it focused on the *feeling* of balance.
  • Fidgeting: Moving your hands because you think the client is bored. In CST, movement often interrupts the very Stillpoint you are trying to facilitate.
  • Matching the Client's Energy: If Diane is talking fast, you might start talking fast. Practice entrainmentâ€”keep your speech slow and your breath deep to pull her into your calm.
  • Ignoring Your Own Equilibrium: If your feet aren't flat on the floor and your back isn't supported, your system will feel "unstable" to the client.
