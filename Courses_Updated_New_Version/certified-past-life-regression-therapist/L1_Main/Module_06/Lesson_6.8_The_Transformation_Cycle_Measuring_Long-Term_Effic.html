Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced coaches fall into these traps. Being aware of them is 90% of the battle.

  • Rushing the Integration: New coaches often end the session too quickly after the regression. Spend at least 15-20 minutes on integration.
  • Playing "The Oracle": Telling the client what their past life means instead of asking them. "This means you have a fear of water" is less powerful than "What does that water represent to you?"
  • Ignoring the Body: If the client is crying or shaking, don't just talk. Help them ground themselves physically first.
  • Over-Spiritualizing: Sometimes a past life is just a story the subconscious uses to process trauma. Don't force a "cosmic" explanation if the client needs a "practical" one.

Success Markers

How do you know you've done a great job? Look for these signs:

The "Sigh"

The client has an audible release of breath as the connection between the past and present clicks into place.

Shift in Perspective

The client stops saying "I am a perfectionist" and starts saying "I have been carrying Eliza's perfectionism."

Concrete Action

The client identifies a specific, real-world change they will make in the next 72 hours.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask Diane about her physical sensations during integration?

Physical sensations act as an anchor for the subconscious. By connecting the "tight chest" of the past life to the "tight chest" of her current anxiety, Diane realizes that her body is reacting to an old memory, not a current threat, allowing for a somatic release.

2. What is the danger of "playing the oracle" as a coach?

It strips the client of their agency. When a coach provides the interpretation, the client may agree out of politeness but won't experience the "internal click" of a self-generated realization, which is required for permanent neuroplastic change.

3. If Diane says "I don't know what this means," what is your best response?

Use clinical curiosity: "That's okay. If you *did* know, or if your subconscious was sending you a metaphor for your current life, what might be the first thing that comes to mind?" or "How does the *feeling* of that life show up in your week?"

4. What is a "Success Marker" in an integration session?

A success marker includes an audible sigh of relief, a shift from "identity" language to "behavioral" language, and the identification of a concrete action step for the coming week.
