The Historical Evolution of PLRT

Past Life Regression Therapy (PLRT) has evolved from a fringe metaphysical curiosity into a recognized transpersonal therapeutic modality. While early iterations were often linked to occultism, the mid-20th century saw a shift toward clinical observation. Pioneers like Dr. Morris Netherton and Dr. Roger Woolger began documenting cases where patients experienced spontaneous memories of previous lifetimes that led to immediate symptomatic relief.

In the late 1980s, Dr. Brian Weiss, a traditional psychiatrist, brought PLRT into the mainstream with his work Many Lives, Many Masters. Today, we view PLRT through the lens of Narrative Therapy and Somatic Experiencing, recognizing that whether these memories are literal historical facts or subconscious archetypal metaphors, the healing outcome is quantifiable.

The Neurobiology of Regression

To be a premium practitioner, you must understand the science behind the "woo." During a S.O.U.R.C.E. session, we are intentionally manipulating brainwave states. The goal is to move the client from Beta (alert/analytical) through Alpha (relaxed/creative) and into Theta (deeply subconscious/reminiscent).

Theta waves (4-7 Hz) are the gateway to cellular memory. In this state, the brain is highly plastic and suggestible, allowing for the rapid rewriting of traumatic imprints. This is why PLRT often achieves in 3 sessions what traditional talk therapy might take 3 years to address.
