Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps during the L1 transition. A 2022 survey of regression students found that 68% struggled with "over-explaining" during the induction phase.

1

Asking "Why" Questions

Never ask "Why are you there?" in the first 5 minutes. The client doesn't know why yet. Ask "What do you see/feel?" to keep them in the experience.

2

Filling the Silence

New coaches often get nervous when the client is quiet. Silence is where the subconscious retrieves data. Give them 10-15 seconds before prompting again.

3

Leading the Witness

Avoid saying, "Are you in a forest?" Instead, ask, "Describe your surroundings." Let them build the world.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask about the client's feet or the ground first?

It "grounds" the client in the physical reality of the past life scene and bypasses the analytical mind's attempt to figure out the "story" too quickly.

2. What is the best way to respond if Elena says, "I think I'm just making this up"?

Validate and redirect: "That's a very common feeling. For now, let's just stay with the images and see where they lead. What's happening now?"

3. How long should you wait for a client to respond during a transition?

Ideally 10-20 seconds. This allows the subconscious to "render" the scene without the pressure of immediate verbalization.

4. What is the primary goal of the L1 Pre-Talk?

To build rapport, manage expectations, and lower the client's "analytical wall" so they feel safe to explore.

Success Markers

How do you know if your L1 transition was successful? Look for these signs in your "client":

  • Physical Shifts: Rapid Eye Movement (REM) behind closed lids, change in breathing patterns, or muscle relaxation.
  • Emotional Resonance: The client describes feelings that seem "too big" for the simple scene they are seeing.
  • Specific Details: Instead of saying "I'm in a house," they say "I see a rough wooden table with a cracked bowl."
