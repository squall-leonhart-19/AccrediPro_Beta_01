2. The Resolution Script

In this scenario, Diane has just finished describing the scene of her death as the governess. You are now moving into the Resolution (R) phase.

Dialogue: The Karmic Release

YOU:
"Diane, as you look back at that governess from your higher perspective now... what does she need to know that she didn't understand then?"
DIANE:
"She needs to know it wasn't her fault. She did everything right. But she still feels... so heavy. Like she failed."
YOU:
"I want you to speak directly to her. Tell her: 'The debt is paid. You no longer have to carry the burden of being perfect to be worthy.' Say those words out loud for me now."
DIANE (Whispering):
"The debt is paid. You don't have to be perfect anymore."
YOU:
"Good. Now, look at the employer who dismissed you. From this soul level, do you see the lesson he was playing for you? Can you release the anger so you don't have to carry him into your next life?"
Sarah's Tip: The Power of the Pause

After a client says a release statement like "The debt is paid," wait at least 10 seconds. Let the emotional resonance settle in their body before moving to the next question.

3. Comparison: Surface vs. Deep Resolution

New therapists often mistake "venting" for "resolution." Use this table to ensure you are going deep enough.

Feature Surface Forgiveness (Venting) Deep Karmic Resolution
Client Emotion Anger or sadness remains present. A sense of "lightness" or neutrality.

Vocal Tone High-pitched, rapid, or shaky. Deep, resonant, and calm.
Outcome Temporary relief; pattern repeats. Permanent shift in current-life behavior.

5. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even seasoned pros can trip up during the Resolution phase. Here are the top 4 mistakes I see:

  1. Rushing to Forgiveness: If the client is still furious, forcing them to "forgive" will create resistance. Let them express the anger fully first.
  2. Taking on the Weight: You are the guide, not the sponge. If you feel exhausted after a session, you are likely "carrying" the client's karma. Visualize a glass wall between you.
  3. Over-Explaining: Don't lecture the client on what their karma means. Ask them what it means. Their answer is the only one that heals.
  4. Skipping the Body: Always ask, "Where do you feel that release in your body?" This anchors the spiritual work into the physical self.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary indicator that a karmic resolution has been successful?

The primary indicator is a physical sense of "lightness" or "shift" in the client, accompanied by a neutral or compassionate view of the previous antagonist.

2. Why should you avoid "over-explaining" the karma to the client?

Because the healing comes from the client's own subconscious realization. If you provide the answer, it remains an intellectual concept; if they find it, it becomes a soul-level truth.

3. What should you do if a client refuses to forgive a person from a past life?

Shift the focus to "releasing the debt" rather than "forgiving the person." Sometimes a client isn't ready to forgive, but they are ready to stop carrying the heavy burden of the debt.

4. How does a "Karmic Contract Rewrite" differ from a simple affirmation?

A contract rewrite is done in a state of deep trance (theta brainwaves) and specifically addresses a perceived "soul obligation" discovered during regression, making it much more profound than a conscious-level affirmation.
