Your Coaching Practice Scenario

In this lab, you will practice a consultation with a potential client. This is the "bridge" between someone being curious about your work and someone handing you their credit card to book a session.

Client Profile: Brenda, 54

Background: Brenda is a retired nurse who has "tried everything" for her chronic shoulder pain and a recurring feeling of "not being safe" that has no root in her current life. She is curious but skeptical. She's worried she will "make it up" or that "nothing will happen."

Your Goal: Use the Professional Consultation Framework to build rapport, validate her concerns, and explain the process so she feels safe enough to book.

Sarah's Professional Insight

When clients say they are "skeptical," they are usually just afraid of failing at the process. Your job isn't to prove past lives are real; it's to prove that the process is safe and that their subconscious knows exactly where to go.

The Professional Consultation Script

Practice these lines out loud. Notice how the tone is warm but authoritative.

You:
"Brenda, I hear you. As a nurse, you're used to looking for logical causes. What we do in Past Life Regression isn't about ignoring logic; it's about accessing a different part of your internal library. Whether what you see is a literal past life or a symbolic story your mind created to help you heal, the relief you feel in your shoulder is very real. Does that make sense?"
Brenda:
"Yes, I suppose. But what if I just sit there and see a blank screen? I'm not very 'visual'."
You:
"That is the #1 fear people have! Here is the secret: You don't have to 'see' anything. Some people just 'know' or 'feel' things. It's like a memory of a vacation‚Äîyou might not see a high-def movie, but you know you were there. My job as your therapist is to guide you so gently that your mind has no choice but to relax into the story. We'll start with some simple relaxation exercises first so you can see how easy it is. Shall we look at the calendar for next Tuesday?"
Practice Prompt

Record yourself saying the "You" lines on your phone. Listen back. Do you sound like a confident professional or are you "asking" for permission? Aim for a calm, steady "doctor's office" tone mixed with "best friend" warmth.
