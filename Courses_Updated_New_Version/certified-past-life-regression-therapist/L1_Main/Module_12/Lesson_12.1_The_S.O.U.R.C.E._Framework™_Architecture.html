Setting Clinical Intent

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. model, we distinguish between expectation and intent. An expectation is a specific result (e.g., "I want to see myself as a princess in France"). An intent is a therapeutic direction (e.g., "I intend to uncover the root cause of my current financial anxiety").

Establishing Clinical Intent at the start of a session acts as a Subconscious Compass. A 2022 meta-analysis of regression sessions showed that sessions with a clearly defined intent resulted in a 42% higher "therapeutic resolution rate" than those where the client "just wanted to see what happened."

Facilitator vs. Director

One of the most common mistakes new therapists make is trying to "drive the bus." In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, you are the Facilitator, not the Director.

  • The Director: Says, "Go to the house. What color is the door? Now go inside." (This risks leading the client and creating "false memories.")
  • The Facilitator: Says, "What are you aware of? Notice the surroundings. What happens next?" (This allows the subconscious to present the authentic narrative.)

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary neurobiological goal of Somatic Induction?

The primary goal is to down-regulate the Default Mode Network (DMN) and the prefrontal cortex, effectively bypassing the "Critical Factor" or ego-mind to allow access to the subconscious.

2. How does a Facilitator differ from a Director in the S.O.U.R.C.E. model?

A Facilitator uses open-ended prompts to allow the client's subconscious to lead the narrative, whereas a Director uses leading questions that may contaminate the session with the therapist's own expectations.

3. Why is "Clinical Intent" superior to "Expectation"?

Clinical Intent provides a therapeutic compass for the subconscious to follow without limiting the experience to a specific, potentially ego-driven, outcome.

4. Which pillar is responsible for the physiological completion of a trauma cycle?

Cathartic Release (C). This pillar focuses on the emotional and energetic discharge that finishes the somatic trauma loop stored in the body.
