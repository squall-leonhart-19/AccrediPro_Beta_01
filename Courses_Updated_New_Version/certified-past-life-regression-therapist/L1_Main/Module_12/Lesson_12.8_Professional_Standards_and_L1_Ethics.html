Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. Watch out for these during your practice:

  • Rushing the Silence: New coaches often feel awkward during pauses. Silence is where the client's subconscious speaks. Let it breathe.
  • Using "Sharp" Language: Avoid words like "Focus" or "Concentrate." Use softer terms like "Allow," "Notice," or "Invite."
  • Over-Explaining: You don't need to explain the science of brainwaves *during* the induction. Keep the instructions simple.
  • Ignoring Physical Cues: If you see Dianeâ€™s eyelids fluttering (REM), she is likely entering the state. Donâ€™t interrupt with a new instruction!
