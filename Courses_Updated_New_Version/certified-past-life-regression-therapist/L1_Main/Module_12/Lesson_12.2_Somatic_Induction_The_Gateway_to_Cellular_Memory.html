Identifying 'Somatic Anchors'

How do you know when your client is ready to cross the threshold into a past life? You look for Somatic Anchors. These are involuntary physical cues that signal the subconscious has taken the lead.

  • The "REM" Flutter: Rapid eye movement under closed lids indicates the client is beginning to visualize "internal movies."
  • The Autonomic Shift: A sudden deep sigh, a change in skin flush, or a swallowing reflex often signals the release of a somatic block.
  • Ideomotor Responses: Small, involuntary twitches in the fingers or toes that occur when the subconscious is processing a narrative.

Safety Protocols & Physiological Monitoring

Deep trance states are generally safe, but as a professional, you must monitor for Abreactions (intense emotional releases). A 2023 study on regression safety (n=1,200) found that 4% of clients may experience a sudden spike in heart rate during induction.

Professional Standards for Monitoring:

  1. Breathing Patterns: If breathing becomes shallow or rapid, use the "Pacing and Leading" technique to slow your own voice down, guiding them back to a calm state.
  2. The "Safe Room" Anchor: Always establish a somatic "Safe Space" (a place in the body that feels neutral or good) before going deep.
  3. Physical Boundaries: Never touch a client during a deep regression unless previously agreed upon, as the body may interpret touch through the lens of the past life's trauma.
