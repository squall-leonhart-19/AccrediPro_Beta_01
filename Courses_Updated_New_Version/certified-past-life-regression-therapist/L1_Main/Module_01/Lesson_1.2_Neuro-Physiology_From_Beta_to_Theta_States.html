The Amygdala: Lowering the Shield

The Amygdala is the brain's "smoke detector." Its job is to protect the individual from pain. In many cases, the subconscious "hides" past life memories because the Amygdala perceives the emotional intensity of those memories as a threat to the client's current stability.

Somatic induction works because it bypasses the Amygdala's guard. When the body is profoundly comfortable, the Amygdala "stands down." It concludes that since the body is safe, the memories are safe to view. This allows for Objective Observation (Module 2) without the client becoming overwhelmed or "snapping out" of the trance.
