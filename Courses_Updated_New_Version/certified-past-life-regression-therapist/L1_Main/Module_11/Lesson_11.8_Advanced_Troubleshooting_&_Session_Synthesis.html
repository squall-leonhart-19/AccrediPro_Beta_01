Common Mistakes to Avoid

The Mistake Why It Stalls Progress The Professional Correction
Leading the Witness Asking "Are you in a forest?" forces your imagery on them. "Describe your surroundings."
Filling the Silence Talking too much interrupts the client's internal "movie." Wait at least 5-10 seconds after a client speaks.
Validating Too Early Saying "Wow, amazing!" can snap them out of trance. Use neutral grounding: "I see," or "Go on."
Ignoring the Body Focusing only on visuals leaves the experience "flat." Ask about temperature, smells, and physical sensations.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most effective way to handle a client who says "I'm just seeing blackness"?

Ask them to focus on a non-visual sense, such as the feeling of the ground under their feet or the temperature of the air. This bypasses the visual "block."

2. Why is "therapeutic silence" critical during the entry phase?

It allows the client's subconscious mind time to retrieve and "render" the memory. Over-talking can distract the client and pull them back into their analytical mind.

3. If a client doubts their memory, what is the best "Sarah-approved" response?

Tell them to "just play along" or "trust your imagination for now." This lowers the stakes and allows the subconscious to continue flowing without the "inner critic" interfering.

4. Which sensory detail is often the strongest "anchor" for a past life memory?

The feet. Asking "What is on your feet?" or "What are you standing on?" is the most grounding way to initiate a regression entry.
