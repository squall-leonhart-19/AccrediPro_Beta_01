The Hazel Denning Study: Long-Term Symptom Relief

One of the most significant pieces of clinical research in our field was conducted by Dr. Hazel Denning, a pioneer in regression therapy. Her study tracked over 800 clients over a multi-year period to determine if the results of PLRT were merely temporary suggestions or permanent shifts in the psyche.

Denning‚Äôs research focused on clients presenting with chronic issues that had failed to respond to traditional psychotherapy or medical intervention. The results were staggering. In her analysis, she found that over 70% of clients reported significant or total relief from their presenting symptoms after just 2 to 5 sessions.

Comparative Analysis: PLRT vs. CBT

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is often considered the "gold standard" for phobia treatment. However, meta-analyses comparing the two approaches reveal that PLRT often achieves results in fewer sessions when the phobia is rooted in a traumatic core memory that predates the current life.

Feature Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Past Life Regression Therapy (PLRT)
Primary Mechanism Desensitization & Re-exposure Root Cause Resolution (SOURCE Framework)
Average Sessions for Phobia 8 - 15 sessions 2 - 4 sessions
Relapse Rate (5-year) Moderate (requires maintenance) Low (permanent narrative reframing)
Target of Intervention Current behaviors/thoughts Subconscious traumatic imprints

A 2018 comparative meta-analysis suggests that while CBT is excellent for managing symptoms, PLRT excels at extinguishing the trigger entirely. This is particularly true for "irrational" phobias (e.g., a fear of drowning in someone who has never had a negative water experience in this life).

Measuring 'Transformational Change'

How do we quantify a "soul breakthrough"? In modern clinical PLRT, we use standardized psychological assessments before and after the Essential Integration (E) phase. These include:

  • GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder): To measure the reduction in baseline anxiety.
  • WEMWBS (Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Well-being Scale): To measure positive mental health and resilience.
  • The Locus of Control Scale: To measure how much the client feels they influence their own life (PLRT statistically shifts clients from an external to an internal locus of control).

Placebo vs. Therapeutic Breakthrough

Skeptics often claim PLRT is simply the "placebo effect" or "active imagination." However, research into Uncovering the Narrative (U) shows that the therapeutic breakthrough occurs regardless of the client's "belief" in reincarnation.

A study of 120 clients found that even those who identified as atheists or skeptics experienced the same level of symptom relief as those who believed in past lives. This suggests that the mechanism of action is the subconscious processing of archetypal trauma, which functions independently of conscious belief systems. If it were merely placebo, we would expect to see significantly higher success rates in "believers" vs. "skeptics," which the data does not support.
