The Case Weighting Scale (CWS)

One of Tucker's most significant contributions is the Case Weighting Scale (CWS). This tool allows researchers to assign a numerical value to the strength of a case, moving away from subjective "feelings" about a story's validity. The CWS evaluates cases based on four distinct categories:

CWS Category Description Point Value Significance
Birthmarks/Defects Physical marks on the child that correspond to wounds on the deceased. High: Provides biological "hard" evidence.
Statements The number and specificity of verifiable facts (names, locations). Variable: Based on the "rarity" of the information.
Behaviors Phobias, skills (xenoglossy), or habits related to the previous life. Medium: Demonstrates "cellular memory" or personality carry-over.
Distance/Contact The geographical and social distance between the two families. Critical: Rules out "leakage" of information through normal means.

A 2013 analysis of the UVA database found that cases with a CWS score higher than 12 were statistically impossible to explain through chance alone. As a therapist, understanding this scale helps you identify Somatic Markers (Module 5) in your adult clients that may correspond to these quantitative categories.
