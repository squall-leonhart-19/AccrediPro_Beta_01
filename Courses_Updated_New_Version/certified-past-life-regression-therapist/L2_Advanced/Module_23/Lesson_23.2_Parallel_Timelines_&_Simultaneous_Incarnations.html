Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "linear" past life and a "simultaneous" incarnation?

A linear life is perceived as occurring "before" the current life in a chronological sequence. A simultaneous incarnation is a projection of the Oversoul occurring at the same "now" moment in the soul's perspective, though it may appear in a different historical era in 3D time.

2. In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, which pillar is most vital when a client is confused by seeing two lives at once?

Objective Observation (O). The practitioner must help the client maintain the "Observer Stance" to prevent emotional flooding and to map the two narratives clearly.

3. How does "Genetic Memory" differ from "Soul Memory"?

Genetic memory is passed through DNA and usually involves survival instincts or ancestral traumas without a specific personal soul-identity. Soul memory is the individual's unique journey across different bodies and lifetimes.

4. Why is the Inter-life transition important for advanced regression?

It allows the client to access the "Soul's Perspective" or "Oversoul Hub," where they can understand the higher purpose and contracts governing multiple concurrent timelines.
