The Clinical Challenge: Differential Diagnosis

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, we prioritize Objective Observation (O). When a client reports a memory or a "voice," we must determine its origin. A 2021 study on transpersonal clinical experiences (n=450) suggested that up to 18% of clients in deep regression report sensations of being "influenced" by an external presence.

We categorize these "foreign" influences into three primary domains:

Phenomenon Source Clinical Presentation
Past Life Memory Internal (Soul History) Resonant, emotional, "I" statements, chronological flow.
Sub-Personality Internal (Ego Fragment) Defensive, child-like or parental, protects a trauma.
Energetic Attachment External (Hitchhiker) Colder temperature, intrusive thoughts, "It" or "He/She" statements.
Coach Tip: Clinical Confidence

Don't let the word "attachment" intimidate you. Think of it as a "static signal" on a radio. Your job isn't to be an exorcist; it's to help the client reclaim their frequency. As a professional charging $200+ per session, your calm neutrality is your client's greatest anchor.

Somatic Markers (S) of Non-Self Energy

The Somatic Induction (S) phase is where the most reliable data is found. Cellular memories of your own past lives typically integrate into the nervous system's "baseline." In contrast, external attachments create localized somatic disruptions.

Key Somatic Indicators:

  • Temperature Drops: A sudden, localized chill in the solar plexus or back of the neck that does not resolve with breathing.
  • Weight/Pressure: A sensation of a "heavy coat" or "something sitting on the shoulders" that feels distinct from muscle tension.
  • Divergent Eye Movement: Rapid, erratic REM during trance that doesn't match the emotional content of the narrative.
  • "The Foreign Pulse": A secondary rhythmic sensation in the body that is out of sync with the client's heartbeat.

The Sovereignty Protocol (R Phase)

When you identify an attachment, we move into Resolving the Karma (R), but with a specific modification called the Sovereignty Protocol. This is not a battle; it is a boundary setting exercise.

Step-by-Step Protocol:

  1. Acknowledge without Fear: "I notice a heavy energy in your solar plexus. Let's look at it objectively."
  2. Identify the Contract: Ask the energy, "Why are you here?" (Often it is for protection, warmth, or confusion).
  3. Revoke Permission: Guide the client to say: "I recognize you are not me. I thank you for what you thought you were doing, but I revoke all permission for you to remain in my field."
  4. The Light Bridge: Direct the client to visualize a "Somatic Bridge" of their own light pushing the energy out into the universal source.
Coach Tip: Language Matters

Avoid using "dark" or "evil." These terms trigger the amygdala and can cause the client to snap out of trance. Use clinical terms like "non-resonant energy," "interference," or "unauthorized frequency."

The 3-Pillar Hygiene System:

  • Pre-Session Shielding: Visualize a burgundy-gold (the colors of authority) sphere around your chair.
  • Post-Session Disconnection: A physical act of "washing the hands" or "shaking out the limbs" to signal to your nervous system that the client's energy is no longer your responsibility.
  • Environmental Clearing: Use sound (tuning forks or bells) between clients to reset the room's frequency.

Reframing "Possession" as Soul Retrieval

In the Essential Integration (E) phase, we help the client make sense of the experience. We move away from medieval concepts of "possession" and toward modern transpersonal psychology.

Often, an attachment is only able to "hook" into a client because there is a soul fragment missing from a previous trauma. The "hole" in the client's energy field allows the attachment in. Therefore, the ultimate "clearing" is actually Soul Retrieval‚Äîbringing the client's own power back into the space the attachment once occupied.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which somatic marker is most indicative of an external attachment versus a past life memory?

Localized, unresolved temperature drops (chills) and the sensation of a "foreign pulse" that doesn't match the client's heart rate.

2. In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, during which phase do we primarily implement the Sovereignty Protocol?

The Resolving the Karma (R) phase, where we address the "contracts" or permissions that allowed the attachment to remain.

3. Why is it clinically safer to use terms like "non-resonant energy" instead of "evil spirit"?

To avoid triggering the client's amygdala (fear response), which can cause a sudden exit from the Theta state and prevent the therapeutic clearing.

4. What is the ultimate goal of reframing an attachment scenario?

To move toward "Soul Retrieval," filling the energetic gaps with the client's own power so that future attachments have no "hook" to latch onto.

Key Takeaways

  • Discernment is Key: Use the "I" vs. "It" linguistic test to distinguish internal memories from external attachments.
  • Somatic Data: Trust the body's temperature and pressure signals over the narrative's drama.
  • Sovereignty First: The client is the ultimate authority in their own energetic field; your role is to facilitate their command.
  • Professional Hygiene: Protect your own energy to ensure long-term career sustainability and clinical neutrality.
  • Integration: Always end with "filling the space" with the client's own light to prevent re-attachment.
