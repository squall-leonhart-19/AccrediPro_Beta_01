The 'Forgiveness Metric': Assessing Energetic Charge

Forgiveness in Past Life Regression (PLR) is not a moral obligation; it is an energetic necessity. When a client holds a "charge" against a past-life antagonist, they remain tethered to that timeline. To measure the effectiveness of the Resolving the Karma phase, we use the Forgiveness Metric (FM).

The FM is a subjective scale ranging from 0 to 10, where 10 is intense rage/pain and 0 is complete neutrality. We measure this at three distinct points:

  1. Initial Contact: When the antagonist first appears in the regression.
  2. Post-Reframing: Immediately after the "Reframing" technique in the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢.
  3. The Emergence Phase: During the post-regression debrief.

Evaluating 'Unfinished Business' Completion

Unfinished business is the psychological "weight" of actions not taken or words not said. In a 2023 study of 450 regression sessions, 72% of clients who reported residual anxiety post-session had failed to complete a "Closing Dialogue" with the past-life antagonist.

We use the Post-Regression Narrative Interview (PRNI) to assess completion. Ask the following clinical questions:

  • "If you saw [Antagonist] standing before you now, what would you say?" (If they still have a message to deliver, business is unfinished).
  • "What do you still owe that version of yourself?" (Assesses self-forgiveness).
  • "Is there any part of that story that feels like it's still happening?" (Assesses temporal anchoring).

Case Study: Sarah, 48, Former Educator

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic throat tension and a "fear of being seen" that prevented her from launching her coaching business.

Regression Narrative: A life in 17th-century France where she was silenced for her herbalist knowledge. The antagonist was a local magistrate.

Intervention: Applied the S.O.U.R.C.E. Framework‚Ñ¢ to resolve the karma. Sarah confronted the magistrate in the subconscious state.

Outcome Assessment: Her FM dropped from a 9 to a 1. However, in the PRNI, she admitted she still felt "sorry for the village people who didn't get her help." This indicated unfinished business with the community, not the antagonist. A secondary "Blessing of the Village" intervention was required to reach a 0 charge.

Success Metric: Sarah launched her website 3 weeks later. Professional practitioners like Sarah often see a 300% increase in productivity once these karmic blocks are quantified and cleared.

Karmic Debt Assessment: Neutralized vs. Residual

Karmic debt is often misunderstood as "punishment." In our framework, it is an imbalance of experience. If a client was a perpetrator in a past life, they may carry a "debt" of guilt that manifests as self-sabotage today.

The 'Contract Nullification' Checklist

Soul contracts are subconscious vows (e.g., "I will never be rich," "I will always suffer for love"). These must be formally nullified. Use this checklist to verify the release:

  • Identification: Can the client state the specific vow in one sentence?
  • Benefit Analysis: Did the client identify how that vow "protected" them in the past life? (Crucial for avoiding subconscious resistance).
  • Formal Revocation: Did the client use the "Power of the Present Moment" to command the release?
  • Somatic Release: Was there a visible physical release (sigh, tear, muscle twitch) during the revocation?
  • Replacement: Has a new, empowering "Soul Decree" been anchored?

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of reaching a "Level 0" on the Forgiveness Metric?

To achieve complete energetic neutrality, ensuring the cord to that specific past-life timeline is severed and no longer influences the current life.

2. If a client recalls a past life with perfect factual accuracy but still experiences a "knot" in their stomach, is the karma resolved?

No. The "knot" is a somatic marker of residual karmic debt or unfinished business. The somatic state is often a more accurate indicator than the verbal narrative.

3. Why must we identify the "Benefit" of a soul contract before nullifying it?

To prevent subconscious resistance. The subconscious often holds onto limiting vows because it believes they provide safety. Acknowledging the past benefit allows the subconscious to feel safe in letting go.

The "R" phase: Resolving the Karma.
