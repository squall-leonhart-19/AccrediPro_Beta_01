Introduction to the Discovery Call

The discovery call is arguably the most critical step in your client acquisition process. Itâ€™s a no-obligation conversation where you and a potential client explore if working together is a good fit. It's your opportunity to:

  • Build Rapport: Establish trust and connection.
  • Understand Needs: Deeply listen to their health challenges and aspirations.
  • Educate & Empower: Briefly explain your unique approach and how it can help.
  • Assess Fit: Determine if you can genuinely help them and if they are ready for commitment.
  • Introduce Your Solution: Present your program as the logical next step.

Remember, this isn't a free consultation where you give away all your expertise. It's a conversation designed to understand their problem and determine if your paid programs offer the solution they desperately need.

Coach Tip: Mindset Matters

Approach every discovery call not as a sales pitch, but as a diagnostic conversation. Your goal is to understand if you can truly help this person. If you focus on serving, the "sale" becomes a natural outcome of demonstrating value and genuine care. This shift in mindset will alleviate imposter syndrome and boost your confidence immensely!

Scenario: Meet Your Prospect, Rachel

Let's dive into a real-world scenario. Imagine you've just received a booking for a 30-minute discovery call. Your prospect is Rachel.

Your Prospect Profile: Rachel, 47

Rachel is a 47-year-old former teacher, now working part-time as an administrative assistant. She found you through a friend's referral (a past client of yours!). She scheduled a discovery call because she's feeling increasingly frustrated with her health.

  • Presenting Symptoms: Chronic fatigue (especially mid-afternoon), persistent weight gain around her midsection despite trying diets, brain fog, and disrupted sleep.
  • Previous Attempts: She's tried various popular diets, seen her primary care doctor multiple times who told her "it's just aging" or "stress," and feels dismissed. She's spent money on supplements without clear guidance.
  • Biggest Concern: "I've spent so much money and time already. Is this really going to be different? I'm worried about wasting more resources."
  • Decision Style: Needs to feel heard and understood. Skeptical of quick fixes. Values expertise, credentials, and a personalized approach.
  • Her Goal: "I just want to feel like myself again. I miss having energy, feeling clear-headed, and fitting into my clothes without a struggle."

Phase-by-Phase Discovery Call Script

Now, let's walk through a structured 30-minute discovery call with Rachel. Pay attention to the flow and the intention behind each question.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Oh, your client, Maria, she couldn't stop raving about you! Said you helped her turn her energy around completely. I've been struggling for years, and she insisted I talk to you."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that Maria referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min

This is where you listen, listen, listen. Ask open-ended questions.

YOU:
"Tell me more about the fatigue. When did it start? How does it affect your daily life? What does a typical day look like when you're feeling your worst?"
RACHEL:
"It's been a gradual decline over the last 5-7 years, but it's gotten really bad since I hit 40. I wake up tired, hit a wall by 2 PM, and just want to nap. I can't keep up with my kids, and my brain feels foggy, like I'm constantly searching for words. I used to be so active."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and why do you think it didn't stick?"
RACHEL:
"Everything! Keto, intermittent fasting, counting calories. I'd lose a few pounds, then it would all come back, plus more. My doctor just says my labs are 'normal' and suggested antidepressants for the fatigue. I've bought so many supplements online, but it's just a guessing game."
YOU:
"I hear your frustration. It's exhausting when you're trying so hard and not getting answers. One final question for this part: If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like?"
RACHEL:
"Honestly, if nothing changes, I'm scared I'll just keep gaining weight, lose all my energy, and become a shadow of myself. If I could wave a wand, I'd wake up refreshed, have energy to play with my grandkids, feel confident in my clothes, and have a clear mind again. I just want to feel like me."
Coach Tip: Ask "Why?" (Respectfully!)

When a client mentions trying something that didn't work, gently ask "Why do you think that didn't work for you?" or "What was missing from that approach?" This helps them articulate their past frustrations, which builds empathy and highlights where your approach can offer a different, more effective solution.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min

Summarize her pain, then bridge to your solution. Focus on your unique methodology.

YOU:
"Rachel, thank you for sharing all of that. I'm hearing that you're experiencing chronic fatigue, persistent weight struggles, brain fog, and a deep desire to feel like your vibrant self again. You've tried various diets and conventional medical approaches that haven't provided lasting solutions, leaving you feeling frustrated and dismissed. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, exactly! You just summarized everything perfectly."
YOU:
"The approach I use is quite different from what you've tried before, Rachel. As a Certified Adaptogen Specialist, I focus on identifying the root causes of these symptoms, rather than just managing them. We use a personalized, holistic framework that integrates advanced functional assessments, targeted adaptogenic protocols, and comprehensive lifestyle adjustments."
YOU:
"My 12-week 'Reclaim Your Vitality' program is designed specifically for women like you who are ready to uncover exactly what's driving their fatigue and weight issues. We work together to create a plan that addresses your unique biochemistry and lifestyle. Most of my clients, especially those struggling with energy and metabolism, start feeling significantly better — more energetic, clearer thinking, and noticing positive shifts in their body composition — within the first 4-6 weeks."

Navigating Objections with Grace

Rachel is clearly interested, but she has concerns. This is where your confidence and empathy truly shine. Remember, objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance.

Practice Challenge

Read Rachel's objection, then practice saying your response out loud. Notice how it feels to confidently address her concerns.

RACHEL: "I need to think about it."

YOU: "I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision about your health. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for yourself."

(Coach's Note: This helps you uncover the real objection. Be prepared to address it directly.)

RACHEL: "It sounds great, but it's a lot of money. I've spent so much already."

YOU: "I completely empathize, Rachel. You've invested a lot of time and money without getting the results you deserve, and that's incredibly frustrating. What I can tell you is that my program is designed to be the last investment you'll need to make in this area. We get to the root cause, so you're not endlessly chasing symptoms or buying random supplements. Think of this not as an expense, but as an investment in reclaiming your health, energy, and quality of life for years to come. What's the cost of staying stuck for another year?"

RACHEL: "I've tried everything. What makes this different?"

YOU: "That's a very fair question, Rachel, and it's why I emphasized my unique approach. You've tried general diets and conventional medicine, which often miss the nuanced individual factors. As a Certified Adaptogen Specialist, my expertise lies in understanding your unique stress response, hormonal balance, and metabolic health at a foundational level. We don't guess; we test and create a truly personalized plan using the power of adaptogens and targeted nutrition. This comprehensive, root-cause methodology is precisely what differentiates my program from the 'everything' you've tried before."

Coach Tip: Reframe Objections

Instead of hearing "no," hear "I need more information" or "I need reassurance." Your job is to calmly and confidently provide that. Validate their feelings, then pivot to how your program specifically addresses their concern. Empathy + Solution = Trust.

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

This is often where practitioners feel most uncomfortable. But remember, you are offering immense value — the transformation of someone's health and life. Your pricing reflects your expertise, your time, and the significant results you help clients achieve. Here's how to do it effectively:

Stating the Investment

After you've addressed any initial objections and confirmed they are still interested, it's time to present your program and pricing. Do this clearly and without hesitation.

YOU:
"Based on everything you've shared, Rachel, I truly believe my 12-week 'Reclaim Your Vitality' program is exactly what you need to get your energy back, address your weight concerns, and feel like yourself again. The investment for this comprehensive 12-week program is $X,XXX. This includes weekly coaching calls, personalized adaptogen and nutrition protocols, functional lab interpretation, and ongoing support."

(Coach's Note: Pause here. Let the number land. Don't immediately fill the silence. Rachel might respond with an objection, which you are now prepared to handle.)

YOU:
"I also offer flexible payment plans, including a 3-pay option of $Y,YYY per month, to make this accessible for you. My clients typically see a significant return on this investment in terms of improved health, reduced reliance on medications, and a renewed sense of well-being."

Mastering the Close: Your Call to Action

Once you've presented your program and pricing, and addressed any further questions, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Don't leave it open-ended. Guide them clearly.

YOU:
"So Rachel, given everything we've discussed, and knowing how much you want to reclaim your energy and feel vibrant again, are you ready to get started on your 'Reclaim Your Vitality' journey with me?"

(Coach's Note: This is a direct, clear question. Again, allow for silence. Your confidence here is key.)

YOU:
"Excellent! The next step is to complete your enrollment. I'll send you an email right after this call with the link to sign up and schedule your first in-depth consultation. It typically takes about 10 minutes to complete. How does that sound?"
Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

After asking a closing question or stating your price, be silent. Let the client process the information. The natural inclination is to fill the void, but resist it. The first person to speak often loses. Your silence communicates confidence in your value.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call, according to the lesson?

The primary purpose of a discovery call is to determine if there is a mutual fit between the practitioner and the potential client, to understand the client's needs, and to present the practitioner's program as a potential solution, rather than providing a free consultation.

2. When Rachel says, "I need to think about it," what is the recommended next step for the practitioner?

The recommended next step is to gently inquire what specifically Rachel needs to think about (e.g., investment, time commitment) to uncover the underlying objection and address it directly.

3. How should you frame your pricing during a discovery call to avoid sounding "salesy"?

Frame your pricing as an "investment" rather than a "cost." Emphasize the long-term transformation, health benefits, and return on investment in terms of improved quality of life, rather than just the monetary figure.

4. What is the "Power of Silence" technique, and when should you use it?

The "Power of Silence" is a technique where you pause and remain silent after asking a closing question or stating your price. This allows the client time to process the information and respond, demonstrating your confidence and avoiding the urge to fill the void, which can weaken your position.
Key Takeaways
  • Discovery calls are crucial for client acquisition, focusing on building rapport and assessing mutual fit.
  • Active listening and open-ended questions are essential to deeply understand a prospect's pain points and aspirations.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, not rejections. Address them with empathy and confidence.
  • Present your program's value and pricing clearly, framing it as an investment in transformation.
  • Mastering the close involves a clear call-to-action and the strategic use of silence to empower the client's decision.
