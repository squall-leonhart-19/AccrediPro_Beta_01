Introduction to the Discovery Call

The Discovery Call is a no-cost, no-obligation conversation designed to determine if you and a prospective client are a good fit. It's your opportunity to:

  • Build rapport and establish trust.
  • Understand their primary health concerns and goals.
  • Educate them on your unique approach as an Adaptogen Specialist.
  • Determine if your services can genuinely help them.
  • Transition them into a paid program if there's alignment.

Think of it as a mutual interview. You're assessing their commitment and readiness, just as they're assessing your expertise and ability to deliver results. A well-structured discovery call can lead to a conversion rate of 50-70% for experienced practitioners, according to industry benchmarks (Mindbody, 2023).

Coach Tip

Remember, the discovery call is primarily about listening. Aim for an 80/20 rule: 80% listening to the client, 20% talking about your approach. When a client feels truly heard, they are far more likely to trust you with their health journey.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Here's a structured script for a 30-minute discovery call. Practice saying these lines out loud, internalizing the flow, and making it sound natural and authentic to your voice. This is a guide, not a rigid mandate; adapt it to your style!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Sarah! Yes, I heard about you from my friend, Jessica. She said you really helped her with her energy. I'm so tired of feeling tired all the time."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that Jessica referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on that prompted this call?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
RACHEL:
"Well, for the last few years, I've just had no energy. I drag myself out of bed, I hit a wall by 3 PM, and I can't lose weight no matter what I do. My doctor says my labs are 'normal,' but I don't feel normal."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Rachel. That sounds incredibly frustrating. Tell me more about the fatigue. When did it start? How does it specifically affect your daily life and what you want to do?"
RACHEL:
"It started gradually after my second child, but it's gotten so much worse in the last 5 years. I used to love hiking, but now I can barely walk a mile. I feel like I'm missing out on my kids' lives because I'm always too tired."
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't work, and what was your experience with those attempts?"
RACHEL:
"Oh, everything! Keto, paleo, cutting out sugar, endless supplements — B vitamins, magnesium, iron. I even tried a detox last year. Nothing sticks, and I just gain the weight back. It's so disheartening."
YOU:
"I hear how much effort you've put in, Rachel, and how frustrating it's been to not see lasting change. Let me ask you this: If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And conversely, if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What would you be doing?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing chronic fatigue and weight challenges that are deeply impacting your quality of life, despite trying many different approaches. You feel unheard by conventional medicine and are looking for sustainable energy and vitality. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, exactly! You've really captured it."
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Adaptogen Specialist is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes of your fatigue and metabolic imbalance, often linked to chronic stress, adrenal health, and gut function. I work with you over a structured 12-week program, using the A.D.A.P.T. Frameworkâ„¢, to identify exactly what's driving your symptoms and create a personalized plan involving targeted nutrition, lifestyle shifts, and specific adaptogenic herbs. Most of my clients, especially those struggling with energy and weight like you, start feeling significantly better and seeing noticeable changes within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"My goal is not just to help you feel better temporarily, but to equip you with the knowledge and tools to maintain that vitality long-term, so you can get back to hiking and truly enjoying life with your family."
Coach Tip

When you summarize their problem, use their exact words where possible. This shows you were truly listening. When presenting your solution, connect it directly back to their stated goals and frustrations. This makes your service highly relevant and desirable.

Confidence in Pricing

Pricing can be a sensitive topic, but it doesn't have to be. Your confidence in stating your fees reflects your confidence in the value you provide. Remember these principles:

  • State it clearly and without apology: Your services are valuable.
  • Connect it to transformation: The price is for the result they want, not just your time.
  • Offer options: A payment plan can make a significant investment more accessible.
  • Justify the length: Explain why your program duration is necessary for results.

Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That sounds like a lot of money. I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision and a significant investment in your health. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need." (Listen for the real objection: finances, skepticism, partner approval, fear of failure. Address that directly.)
"I've tried so many things, and nothing has worked. How do I know this will be different?"
"That's a very valid concern, Rachel, especially given your past experiences. What makes my approach different is its personalized, root-cause focus, specifically leveraging adaptogens to restore your body's natural resilience. We don't just guess; we use detailed assessments to identify *your* unique imbalances. Plus, the ongoing support and accountability over 12 weeks are designed to ensure you implement and sustain these changes, unlike quick fixes. My goal is to empower you with lasting solutions, not temporary relief."
"I need to talk to my husband/partner first."
"Of course, that's completely understandable. It's important to be aligned on such an important decision. When you speak with him, what questions do you anticipate he might have, or what information would be most helpful for him to understand the value of this program?" (Offer to schedule a brief call with both of them, or provide a concise summary of the program benefits they can review together.)
"I don't have the time for a 12-week program right now."
"I hear that, Rachel. Life is busy, and adding something new can feel overwhelming. However, many clients find that investing this time now actually saves them time and energy in the long run, because they're no longer battling chronic fatigue and brain fog. My program is structured to integrate seamlessly into a busy life, with actionable steps and flexible session scheduling. What specifically about the time commitment feels challenging for you?"
Coach Tip

Never argue with an objection. Validate their feelings, then pivot to education or a deeper question. Your goal is to uncover the underlying concern and address it head-on, not to "win" a debate.

Call to Action Practice

Once you've addressed objections and they're ready to move forward, make it easy for them:

  • Direct Close: "Great, Rachel! I'm so excited to help you reclaim your energy. To get started, I'll send you a link to enroll in the 'Reclaim Your Vitality with Adaptogens' program, where you can choose your payment option and schedule your first session. How does that sound?"
  • Soft Close (if some hesitation remains): "Rachel, it sounds like this program is a really good fit for what you're looking for. What feels like the next best step for you — would you like me to send you the enrollment link, or do you have one more question I can answer right now?"
  • Urgency/Scarcity (use sparingly and genuinely): "I only take on a limited number of new 1:1 clients each month to ensure personalized attention. If you're ready to commit to your health, I have two spots remaining for next month. Shall we secure one for you?"
Coach Tip

Always end with a clear, single call to action. Don't leave them wondering what to do next. Guide them confidently to the next step, whether that's sending an enrollment link or answering one final question.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem" phase of a Discovery Call?

The primary purpose is to actively listen to the prospect, understand their specific challenges, frustrations, and desired outcomes, and identify their "why" for seeking help. This phase is crucial for building empathy and tailoring your solution.

2. When Rachel expresses concern about the program's cost, what is an effective initial response?

An effective initial response is to validate her concern ("I completely understand, this is an important investment...") and then ask a clarifying question to uncover the root of the objection ("What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time, or something else?").

3. How can you confidently present your program's price without sounding apologetic?

State the price clearly and directly, connecting it to the value and transformation the client will receive. For example, "The investment for this program is $X, and it's designed to help you achieve over [program length]." Offer payment options if available.

4. Why is it important to have a clear "Call to Action" at the end of the Discovery Call?

A clear Call to Action (CTA) guides the prospect to the next logical step, preventing confusion or inaction. It demonstrates your confidence and professionalism, making the enrollment process seamless and increasing the likelihood of conversion.
Key Takeaways
  • The Discovery Call is a structured conversation focused on mutual fit and service, not just sales.
  • Active listening and deep empathy are paramount to understanding a prospect's true needs and motivations.
  • Confidently summarize their pain points and link your specialized Adaptogen Specialist approach to their desired transformation.
  • Present your program and pricing clearly, connecting the investment to the significant value and lasting results you provide.
  • View objections as opportunities for further understanding and reassurance, rather than barriers to enrollment.
  • Always provide a clear, single call to action to guide prospects to the next step in their journey with you.
