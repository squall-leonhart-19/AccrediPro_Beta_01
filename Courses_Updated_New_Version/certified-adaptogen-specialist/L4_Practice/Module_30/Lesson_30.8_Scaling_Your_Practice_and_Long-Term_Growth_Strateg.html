Understanding Your Prospect: Meet Eleanor

Eleanor, 49

Found you through a local wellness fair. Booked a 30-minute discovery call.

Her Situation
Perimenopausal symptoms (hot flashes, night sweats), chronic fatigue, weight gain around the middle, mild anxiety. Feels dismissed by conventional doctors.
Budget Concern
"I've spent so much on supplements and doctor visits already, with no real results. I'm hesitant to invest more unless it's truly different."
Decision Style
Analytical, needs clear explanations and evidence. Values feeling heard and a personalized plan. Wants to trust your expertise.
Her Goal
"I want to feel vibrant and energetic again, manage my perimenopause symptoms naturally, and lose this stubborn weight. I just want to feel like myself again, not this tired, anxious version."

Eleanor represents a common client profile for Adaptogen Specialists. She's frustrated, has tried various conventional and alternative approaches, and is seeking a personalized, evidence-based solution. Your role in this discovery call is to be that solution.

Coach's Tip

Remember, the discovery call is not a diagnostic session. Your goal is to determine if you can help the prospect, if they are a good fit for your program, and if they are ready to invest in their health. It's about connection and qualification, not prescription.

The Discovery Call Blueprint: Your 30-Minute Script

This 30-minute structure is designed to be efficient, empathetic, and effective. Practice these phases out loud to build confidence.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah, a Certified Adaptogen Specialist. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to know how you heard about me, and what piqued your interest in adaptogens?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful! I loved meeting so many people at the wellness fair. So, Eleanor, tell me ‚Äî what's currently going on for you? What prompted you to reach out for support now?"

(Listen actively. Let her speak without interruption. Take notes.)

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Eleanor. It sounds incredibly frustrating. Can you tell me more about the fatigue and hot flashes? When did they start? How do these symptoms impact your daily life, your energy, your mood?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, and what didn't feel effective for you?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see your health in 6 months or a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly resolve these challenges, what would your life look like? What would be different?"

(This phase is about deep listening and asking open-ended questions to uncover her core motivations and desired future state.)

Coach's Tip

When Eleanor describes her problems, listen for emotional language. Words like "frustrated," "tired of," "helpless," "miss being myself" are powerful indicators of her pain points. Reflect these feelings back to her to show empathy: "It sounds like you're feeling really frustrated with the lack of progress."

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for being so open. Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing significant fatigue, disruptive perimenopausal symptoms like hot flashes, and anxiety, which are deeply impacting your quality of life. You've tried various approaches, but haven't found a lasting solution that addresses the root causes. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Adaptogen Specialist is different from what you've likely experienced before. Instead of just managing symptoms, we utilize the A.D.A.P.T. Framework‚Ñ¢ to look at your body as an interconnected system. We'll identify the underlying imbalances contributing to your fatigue, hormonal shifts, and anxiety, and then create a personalized plan using evidence-based adaptogens, nutrition, and lifestyle strategies. Most of my clients, especially those struggling with similar issues, start feeling significantly better and seeing measurable improvements within the first 4-6 weeks of our 12-week program."
YOU:
"My goal is to empower you with the tools and knowledge to not only feel like yourself again, but to build lasting resilience and vitality. We focus on sustainable transformation, not just quick fixes."
Phase 4: Confident Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on what I've shared about my approach and what you're looking for, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive, personalized support you've been seeking?" (Pause for her response. Listen for a 'yes' or an objection.)
YOU:
"Great! I'm confident we can help you achieve your goals. Let me tell you a bit more about how my program works and how we can get started."

Navigating Objections with Confidence

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're often a request for more information or reassurance, not a definitive "no." Your ability to respond with empathy and confidence is key.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's too expensive. I can't afford it right now."
"I completely understand that this is an investment, Eleanor. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. What I've found, though, is that continuing to manage symptoms with temporary solutions often ends up being more expensive in the long run, both financially and in terms of your quality of life. My program is an investment in lasting change and foundational health. When you consider the cost of ongoing fatigue, mood issues, and feeling unwell, what would it be worth to you to truly feel vibrant and like yourself again? We can discuss payment options to make it more manageable."
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel 100% confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for your health."
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked. How is this different?"
"That's a very valid concern, Eleanor, and I hear that often from women like you who have felt let down by previous approaches. The key difference with my Adaptogen Specialist program is our holistic, root-cause approach using the A.D.A.P.T. Framework‚Ñ¢. We don't just throw supplements at symptoms; we conduct a thorough assessment to understand your unique biochemical individuality. We then create a highly personalized plan, integrating specific adaptogens, nutrition, and lifestyle shifts tailored to your body's needs. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution; it's about addressing your specific imbalances to create sustainable health from the inside out. My clients consistently report feeling heard and seeing results where other methods have failed."

Coach's Tip

When handling objections, always validate the client's feeling first ("I understand," "That's a valid concern"). Then, reframe the objection to highlight the unique value of your program. Don't be afraid to ask clarifying questions to uncover the true root of their hesitation.

Presenting Your Program & Pricing

Once you've addressed objections and confirmed the client's interest, it's time to confidently present your program details and investment. Be clear, concise, and focused on the value.

Your Program Presentation Outline

  • Name Your Program: "My Signature 12-Week Adaptogen Resilience Program"
  • Key Components:
    • Initial 90-minute deep dive consultation (holistic assessment, goal setting).
    • Bi-weekly 45-minute coaching sessions (6 sessions total over 12 weeks).
    • Personalized Adaptogen & Supplement Protocol.
    • Tailored Nutritional & Lifestyle Recommendations.
    • Access to a client portal with resources, recipes, and tracking tools.
    • Ongoing email support between sessions.
  • Expected Outcomes: "Clients typically experience significant improvements in energy, mood stability, hormonal balance, and overall well-being. My aim is for you to feel empowered, resilient, and in control of your health."
  • The Investment: "The total investment for the 12-week program is [Your Price Here]. Many clients find it helpful to break this into [2 or 3] monthly payments of [Payment Amount]. Which option works best for you?"

Coach's Tip

Always state your price with confidence and then pause. The first person to speak after the price is stated often loses. Allow the client to process and respond. Have payment options ready to offer immediately.

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the discovery call, beyond simply gathering information?

The primary goal is to determine if you can genuinely help the prospect, if they are a good fit for your program, and if they are ready and willing to invest in their health and the transformation you offer. It's about qualifying the lead and building a foundational connection.

2. Eleanor mentions, "I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked." How should you respond to this common objection?

First, validate her concern: "That's a very valid concern, Eleanor, and I hear that often." Then, clearly articulate how your approach (e.g., the A.D.A.P.T. Framework‚Ñ¢, root-cause focus, personalized plans) is fundamentally different from what she's likely tried before, emphasizing the unique value proposition of an Adaptogen Specialist.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is a crucial non-verbal technique to employ immediately after stating the price?

After confidently stating your price, pause and remain silent. Allow the client to be the first to speak. This gives them space to process the information and prevents you from potentially talking yourself out of the sale.

4. What is one way to empower a prospect who expresses a budget concern, without immediately reducing your price?

Reframe the investment from a cost to a long-term value in their health and well-being. Ask them to consider the ongoing "cost" of their current symptoms. You can also offer payment plan options to make the investment more accessible.
