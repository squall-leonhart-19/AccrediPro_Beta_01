The 4-Phase Discovery Call Script

This 30-minute structure is designed to be natural, client-focused, and highly effective. Practice this out loud, imagining Rachel on the other end of the line.

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 minutes)

The goal here is to make Rachel feel comfortable, seen, and heard. You want to establish yourself as a warm, professional, and trustworthy guide.

  • YOU: "Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
  • RACHEL: "Oh, my friend Jessica raved about you! She said you helped her get her energy back."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful that Jessica referred you; she's been on quite a journey herself. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you?"

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deepen the Pain (10 minutes)

This is where you listen, listen, listen. Ask open-ended questions that encourage Rachel to elaborate on her experience, frustrations, and desires. Connect her symptoms to her life.

  • YOU: "Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned chronic fatigue and weight issues. Let's start with the fatigue. When did it really start for you? How does it affect your daily life — your work, your family, your personal joy?"
  • RACHEL: "It started a few years ago, after my second child. It's just gotten worse. I can't focus at work, I snap at my kids, and I have no energy for my husband. I just feel like a shadow of myself."
  • YOU: "That sounds incredibly frustrating, Rachel. It sounds like this isn't just about feeling tired, but about how it's impacting your quality of life and your relationships. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and why do you think that was?"
  • RACHEL: "I've tried everything! Keto, detox teas, endless gym memberships... I lose a few pounds, then it all comes back. Nothing lasts. My doctor just pushes pills."
  • YOU: "It sounds like you've been on a long and disheartening journey. Imagine for a moment, if nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And conversely, if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What would be different?"

Coach Tip from Sarah

During Phase 2, your job isn't to diagnose or offer solutions yet. It's to be a compassionate investigator. The "magic wand" question is powerful because it helps clients articulate their deepest desires, allowing you to connect your future solution to their vision. This creates intrinsic motivation.

Phase 3: Present Your Unique Approach & Value (10 minutes)

Now, you'll summarize her pain, introduce your unique Adaptogen Specialist‚Ñ¢ approach, and explain how it addresses her specific concerns. This is where your Unique Value Proposition (UVP) shines.

  • YOU: "Rachel, based on what you've so bravely shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing debilitating fatigue and persistent weight gain that's impacting your energy, your relationships, and your overall sense of self. You've tried conventional approaches and various diets, but they haven't provided lasting relief because they haven't addressed the underlying causes. Does that feel accurate?"
  • RACHEL: "Yes, exactly! You just get it."
  • YOU: "The approach I use as a Certified Adaptogen Specialist‚Ñ¢ is fundamentally different from what you've tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we delve into the ROOT causes of your fatigue and weight challenges — often related to stress, adrenal health, metabolic balance, and inflammation. I work with you over 12 weeks, using advanced adaptogenic strategies and personalized nutrition, to identify exactly what's driving your symptoms and create a plan tailored to your body. Most of my clients, like Jessica, start feeling significantly better within the first 4-6 weeks, experiencing more energy, better sleep, and sustainable weight management."

Phase 4: Close with Confidence & Call to Action (5 minutes)

This is where you determine if she's ready to take the next step. Confidently present your program and guide her to enrollment.

  • RACHEL: "It does sound promising, but..."
  • YOU: "Great! I'm glad to hear that. Let me briefly tell you about the program options and how we can get started..."

Navigating Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're not rejections; they're simply requests for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy, validate their concern, and then reframe or provide clarity.

Practice Scenario

Imagine Rachel pauses after you've presented your approach. She says, "It sounds good, but I've spent so much money already, and I'm just not sure I can afford another program right now."

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses:

  • Objection 1: "I need to think about it."
    • Your Response: "I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and you deserve to feel confident. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the information you need." (This helps uncover the real objection.)
  • Objection 2: "It's too expensive."
    • Your Response: "I appreciate your honesty, Rachel. I know investing in your health is a significant decision. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. What they've found, however, is that the cost of *not* addressing their health — the lost productivity at work, the impact on family life, the ongoing expenses of quick fixes — often far outweighs the investment in a long-term solution. What value do you place on finally feeling like yourself again, with sustainable energy and confidence?" (Shift focus from cost to value and consequences of inaction.)
  • Objection 3: "I've tried everything, and nothing works."
    • Your Response: "Rachel, I completely hear your frustration, and it's understandable to feel that way given your past experiences. The key difference with the Adaptogen Specialist‚Ñ¢ approach is that we don't just throw solutions at symptoms. We spend the time to truly uncover your unique root causes, using a holistic, personalized strategy that adapts to your body's specific needs. It's not about trying 'everything'; it's about finding the *right* thing for *you*. Are you open to exploring a truly personalized path this time?" (Validate, differentiate, and re-engage.)

Coach Tip from Sarah

When handling objections, remember that empathy disarms, and questions clarify. Never argue. Instead, validate their feelings, then gently guide them to see how your unique approach addresses their specific concern. Your confidence in your program is contagious.

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

This is often where practitioners feel the most uncomfortable, but it doesn't have to be. Your programs are valuable, and you are worthy of being compensated for your expertise. Practice saying these phrases out loud until they feel natural.

After Rachel expresses interest, or you've addressed an initial objection:

  • YOU: "Based on what we've discussed, Rachel, I believe my Signature 12-Week Adaptogen Resilience Program is exactly what you need to regain your energy and achieve your health goals. This program includes:
    • Six 1-on-1 coaching sessions (bi-weekly)
    • Personalized adaptogen protocols & supplement recommendations
    • Customized meal plans & lifestyle adjustments
    • Direct messaging support between sessions
    • Access to my exclusive client resource library
    The investment for this transformative 12-week program is $2,500. I also offer a convenient 3-month payment plan of $850 per month."
  • YOU (Optional, if needed): "Many clients find that by committing to this level of support, they save money in the long run by avoiding ineffective treatments and gaining sustainable health."

Coach Tip from Sarah

When stating your price, do so calmly and clearly. Then, stop talking. Let the number land. The first person to speak often feels the pressure. Allow Rachel to process the information. Your silence gives her space to consider, not to feel pressured.

Sealing the Partnership: The Call to Action

Once you've presented your pricing and addressed any final questions, it's time to invite Rachel to commit. Make it easy and clear.

  • YOU: "How does that sound to you, Rachel? Are you ready to take this step towards reclaiming your energy and vitality?"
  • RACHEL (If ready): "Yes, I think I am! Where do I sign up?"
  • YOU: "Wonderful! I'm so excited for you. I'll send you an email right after this call with a link to enroll and select your first session time. It's a simple online process, and you'll receive immediate access to our client portal. What's the best email for that?"
  • YOU (If hesitant, after addressing concerns): "Given what you've shared about wanting to feel like yourself again and how this program aligns with your goals, what feels like the best next step for you today — enrolling in the 12-week program, or do you have any other questions I can answer right now?"

Coach Tip from Sarah

Always assume the sale. Your confidence in your program and its ability to help Rachel should be unwavering. Your role is to guide her to a decision that will benefit her health and well-being. A clear, single call-to-action is far more effective than offering too many choices.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem & Deepen the Pain" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to actively listen and ask open-ended questions to genuinely understand the prospect's challenges, how these challenges impact their life, and what their deepest desires for change are. It's about empathy and gathering information, not offering solutions yet.

2. How should you generally respond to a client's objection like "It's too expensive"?

You should respond with empathy, validate their concern, and then reframe the conversation from "cost" to "value" and the "cost of inaction." Highlight the long-term benefits and savings of investing in a sustainable solution compared to the ongoing expenses and impact of their current health issues.

3. Why is it important to "stop talking" immediately after stating your program's price?

Stopping talking after stating the price allows the prospect to process the information without feeling rushed or pressured. It demonstrates your confidence in your value and gives them space to respond, rather than you filling the silence with justifications or further explanations, which can undermine your position.

4. What is the role of your Unique Value Proposition (UVP) during the "Present Your Unique Approach & Value" phase?

Your UVP is critical here to differentiate your Adaptogen Specialist‚Ñ¢ approach from anything the prospect has tried before. It highlights what makes your methods unique (e.g., root-cause focus, personalized adaptogen protocols) and directly connects how these unique aspects will solve their specific pain points and help them achieve their desired outcomes.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a structured, compassionate conversation designed to determine client fit and readiness for transformation.
  • Mastering the 4 phases — Rapport, Problem Understanding, Solution Presentation, and Closing — builds client trust and confidence.
  • Objections are opportunities for deeper understanding and clarification, not rejections. Respond with empathy and strategic reframing.
  • Presenting your programs and pricing requires confidence in your value and a clear, concise delivery.
  • A clear call-to-action guides your prospect to take the next step towards their health journey with you.
  • Building a thriving practice with consistent client acquisition can lead to significant and sustainable income.
