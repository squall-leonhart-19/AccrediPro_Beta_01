Handling Objections with Grace

An objection is not a "No." It is a request for more information or a sign of the client's own fear. According to a 2023 study on wellness consumer behavior, 68% of clients express a price concern before committing to a premium service.

"It's a lot of money right now."
"I completely hear you, Linda. Investing in ourselves can feel scary. But let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not changing? If you stay in this burnout for another six months, what does that cost your health or your relationships? I also offer a 3-part payment plan to make this more accessible."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

The goal is to build rapport and establish the agenda. You want the client to feel safe and heard, while also signaling that you are the professional leader of the conversation.

2. If a client says "I can't afford it," what is the most professional first response?

Empathize first ("I hear you, it's a big investment"), then pivot to the "cost of inaction." Ask them what happens if their life stays exactly the same for another year. This shifts the focus from "price" to "value."

3. True or False: You should explain the client's entire birth chart during the discovery call to prove you know what you're doing.

False. This is a common mistake! A discovery call is for sales and connection, not teaching. If you give them all the answers for free, they have no reason to hire you for the journey. Save the chart analysis for the paid sessions.

4. Why is a "Package" better for your business than a "Single Reading"?

Packages provide recurring, predictable income for you and better results for the client. Transformation takes time. One session is a "high"; a package is a "habit change."

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Questions are Power: Spend 70% of the call listening and 30% speaking.
  • State the Price, Then Silence: After you say your fee, do not speak until the client does. The first person to speak usually "loses" the negotiation.
  • Solve the Pain: Astrology is the tool, but "Clarity," "Peace," and "Direction" are what people actually buy.
  • Professionalism Over Perfection: You don't need to be the world's best astrologer to be a great practitioner; you need to be the person who truly listens and cares.
