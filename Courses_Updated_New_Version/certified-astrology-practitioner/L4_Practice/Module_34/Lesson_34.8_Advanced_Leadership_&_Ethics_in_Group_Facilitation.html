2. The Group Discovery Call Script

A discovery call for a group program should last about 20-25 minutes. It is less about a deep dive into her chart and more about alignment.

Phase 1: The "Why Group?" Bridge 4 min
YOU:
"Elena, I'm so glad we're chatting. I saw you mentioned on the application that you're feeling a bit isolated in your current career pivot. What made you look for a group experience specifically, rather than just a one-off reading?"
ELENA:
"Honestly, I think I just need to know I'm not crazy for wanting to leave a stable job at 52."
Phase 2: Validating the Vision 8 min
YOU:
"You aren't crazy‚Äîyou're likely feeling that second Saturn trine. In this 6-week program, we don't just look at your chart in a vacuum. We look at how these cycles show up for everyone in the group. When you see another woman navigating the same transit, the 'aha' moment hits twice as hard. Does that resonate with you?"
Phase 3: The Program Structure 5 min
YOU:
"Here is how we work: We meet every Tuesday for 90 minutes. The first 45 is teaching the transit mechanics, and the second 45 is 'Hot Seat' coaching where I pull up individual charts‚Äîincluding yours‚Äîto apply the lesson directly. You get the privacy of a small group (only 8-10 women) but the wisdom of the collective."
Phase 4: The Confident Close 3 min
YOU:
"Based on our talk, you're a perfect fit for this cohort. The investment for the 6-week journey is $1,200, or three monthly payments of $450. I have two spots left. Would you like one of them?"

3. Handling Group-Specific Objections

In a 2023 survey of wellness practitioners, 68% of potential clients cited "lack of personal attention" as their primary reason for hesitating on group programs. You must address this head-on.

The Objection The Confident Response
"I'm worried I won't get my personal questions answered." "I hear you. That's why I cap the group at 10. Every session includes a dedicated Q&A where we look at your specific degrees and houses. You actually get 9 hours of access to me for the price of 2 private sessions."
"I can't make all the live sessions." "Life happens! Every session is recorded and uploaded to our private portal within 2 hours. You can even submit your chart questions in advance, and I'll answer them on the recording for you to watch later."
"I'm not sure I want to share my chart with strangers." "I take confidentiality seriously. We sign a group agreement at the start. Most women find that the 'strangers' in the group become their closest confidants because you're all speaking the same soul-language."
Sarah's Insight

Never lower your price when someone objects. Instead, increase the perceived value. Remind them of the "Bonus" elements, like the private Slack group or the workbook, which make the experience feel high-touch.

Case Study: Linda's "Midlife Transition" Group

From $150 readings to $10k months

Practitioner: Linda (Age 49, former Special Ed Teacher)

The Challenge: Linda was exhausted doing five 1-on-1 readings a week. She was capped at $3,000/month and had no time for her own family.

The Intervention: Linda launched a 4-week workshop series called "The Astrology of the Second Act." She priced it at $497 per person.

The Outcome: Her first cohort filled with 12 women ($5,964). She spent 6 hours total in live sessions. She now runs this group quarterly, combined with a few high-end VIP clients, and consistently earns $8,500 - $11,000 per month while working 15 hours a week.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of a group program over a 1-on-1 session?

The primary benefit is normalization and collective validation. Clients realize they aren't alone in their transits, which reduces shame and increases the speed of transformation through shared experience.

2. If Elena says, "I'm a private person," how should you pivot the conversation?

Acknowledge her concern, then explain the safety measures (confidentiality agreements) and the benefit of the 'Hot Seat'‚Äîwhere she can choose how much to share while still receiving personalized chart application.

3. Why is it better to state the full price ($1,200) before the payment plan ($450 x 3)?

This is called Price Anchoring. By stating the higher number first, the payment plan feels like a more accessible and manageable "yes" by comparison.

4. According to the income table, which model provides the highest hourly rate?

The Scaled Leader model. By serving 12 people in a single block of time, the practitioner earns over $1,000 per hour of live delivery.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs are the most effective way to scale an astrology practice without burnout.
  • Conversion relies on selling the community and the environment, not just the astrological content.
  • Address objections about "personal attention" by highlighting small group caps and dedicated Q&A time.
  • Use price anchoring to make your program accessible while maintaining high-ticket value.
  • Start with a low-stakes workshop to build confidence before launching a flagship group cohort.
