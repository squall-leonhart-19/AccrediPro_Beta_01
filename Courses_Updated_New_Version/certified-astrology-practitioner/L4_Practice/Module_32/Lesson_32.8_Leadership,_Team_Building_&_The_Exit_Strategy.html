Handling Objections with Grace

When Elena says, "I need to think about it," she is usually experiencing a fear of the unknown. Your job is to help her process that fear.

The Objection The Reframing Response
"It's too expensive." "I understand. Is it that the total is out of reach, or are you unsure if the transformation is worth the investment right now?"
"I need to talk to my spouse." "I support that. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Let's see if we can address that now."
"I've tried readings before." "Most readings are 'information only.' My 3-month mentorship is about integration. We don't just look at the chart; we change your life using it."
Sarah's Pro Tip

Never defend your price. If they say it's expensive, agree! "Yes, it is a significant investment because we are doing significant work." Confidence is contagious.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The purpose is to understand the prospect's current pain, their desired future state, and why they need to solve the problem specifically now. It is diagnostic, not educational.

2. How should you respond when a prospect says, "I need to talk to my spouse"?

Support their decision, but ask what they think the spouse's main concern will be. This allows you to address potential objections (like money or time) while the prospect is still on the phone with you.

3. Why is it recommended to stay silent after stating your price?

Silence demonstrates confidence in your value and gives the prospect the necessary space to process the investment without feeling pressured or "sold" to by a rambling practitioner.

4. Based on the income table, how many clients per month do you need to reach a six-figure annual revenue at a $2,500 price point?

You would need approximately 3.33 clients per month (4 clients puts you well over at $120,000/year).

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a professional consultation, not a social chat or a free reading.
  • Rapport is built by listening, not by talking about your own credentials.
  • Objections are a request for more information; they are not a "no."
  • Premium pricing ($2,500+) allows you to work with fewer clients and provide deeper, more impactful transformations.
  • Consistency in your "Price Drop" delivery is the fastest way to overcome imposter syndrome.
