Evaluating Business Structures for Efficiency

Legitimacy requires a legal foundation. For the US-based practitioner, the choice usually falls between an LLC and an S-Corp.

  • Sole Proprietorship: Simple, but offers zero liability protection. If a client sues, your personal assets (house, car) are at risk. Not recommended for professionals.
  • LLC (Limited Liability Company): The "Gold Standard" for the $50k-$100k earner. It separates your personal assets from your business liabilities.
  • S-Corp Election: Often beneficial once you consistently net over $70k-$80k. It allows you to save significantly on self-employment taxes by paying yourself a "reasonable salary" and taking the rest as a distribution.
Coach Tip

Always consult with a CPA who understands "Knowledge-Based Businesses." Tell them you are a consultant/coach using a proprietary methodology. This often opens up better tax deduction categories than "fortune teller" or "entertainer."
