3. Handling Objections Confidently

When a client hesitates, it is usually a fear of the unknown, not a rejection of you. Use these "Reframing" responses.

The Objection The "Imposter" Response (Avoid) The "Professional" Response (Use)
"Is this really scientific?" "Um, well, many people believe in it and it's very old..." "Astrology is a sophisticated system of archetypal psychology and cycles. We use astronomical data to map out psychological patterns that have been observed for centuries."
"I need to talk to my husband." "Oh, okay, no problem. Let me know what he says." "I completely respect that. When you speak with him, what is the main thing you want to convey about why you feel this is the right time for your growth?"
"It's a bit more than I expected." "I can give you a 20% discount if that helps?" "I understand. This is an investment in your clarity. We can break this into two monthly payments to make it more accessible. Would that help you move forward?"
Sarah's Insight

Never discount your price on the first call. It signals that you don't value your own time. Instead, offer a payment plan. Professionals have fixed rates; hobbyists have "whatever you can pay."

4. Stating Your Rates with Authority

The moment you state your price is when your energy matters most. Use the "Anchor and Pause" technique.

The Technique: State the price clearly. Do not add "if that's okay" or "I know it's a lot." Just state it and stop talking. The silence is where the client processes the value.

  • Script A: "The 'Celestial Path' package, which includes your 90-minute reading and a follow-up integration call, is a one-time investment of $350."
  • Script B: "For the full 3-month mentorship, where we track your transits in real-time as you launch your business, the investment is $1,200."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a 30-minute discovery call?

The purpose is to determine if the practitioner and prospect are a good fit for a working relationship, identify the prospect's pain points, and present a professional solution‚ÄîNOT to give a free reading.

2. If a client says "It's too expensive," what is the most professional response?

Acknowledge the investment and offer a payment plan. Avoid immediately discounting your price, as this devalues your professional expertise.

3. Why is it important to "stop talking" after stating your price?

It allows the client the psychological space to process the value and prevents the practitioner from "talking themselves out of the sale" due to nervous energy or over-explaining.

4. What is "authentic scarcity" in a professional practice?

It is the honest communication of your limited availability (e.g., "I only take 5 clients a month"), which highlights the premium nature of your focused attention and encourages the prospect to make a timely decision.
