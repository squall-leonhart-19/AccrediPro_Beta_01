Recruitment and Vetting Standards

Hiring for an astrology agency is fundamentally different from hiring for a standard service business. You aren't just looking for "knowledgeable" people; you are looking for practitioners who can execute the A.S.P.E.C.T. Framework‚Ñ¢ with clinical precision.

The Junior Practitioner Profile

When vetting candidates, look for the "Three Pillars of Agency Fit":

  • Technical Proficiency: Can they synthesize a chart without relying on "cookbook" definitions? (The 'S' in ASPECT).
  • Methodological Alignment: Are they willing to follow your specific system, or are they "rogue" interpreters?
  • Communication Empathy: Can they deliver challenging news (The 'C' in ASPECT) with the professional warmth your brand requires?
Founder Insight

Don't hire based on social media following. Hire based on "interpretive stamina." A junior practitioner needs to be able to handle 10-15 chart syntheses a week without losing depth or accuracy.

Legal & Contractual Frameworks

As you scale, your Intellectual Property (IP) is your most valuable asset. Your contracts must reflect this. Professional agencies typically utilize 1099 Independent Contractor agreements with specific riders.

Critical Contract Clauses

  1. Work for Hire: Ensures that any chart interpretations or reports written by the practitioner belong to the Agency, not the individual.
  2. Non-Solicitation: Prevents practitioners from "poaching" agency clients for their private practice for a period of 12-24 months post-contract.
  3. Methodology Confidentiality: Protects the proprietary way you apply the A.S.P.E.C.T. Framework‚Ñ¢ from being taught elsewhere.
Legal Tip

Always have a local attorney review your contracts. While the A.S.P.E.C.T. methodology is global, labor laws regarding contractors vs. employees vary significantly by state and country.

Developing a 'House Style' Guide

A "House Style" is the voice of your agency. It ensures that whether a client sees you or your junior associate, the experience feels consistent. This is essential for maintaining premium pricing.

Your House Style Guide should include:

  • Visual Standards: Which software is used for charts? What colors/fonts are used in PDF reports?
  • Linguistic Standards: Do we use "traditional" or "modern" terminology? Do we use psychological or predictive language?
  • The "Forbidden" List: Are there certain words or fatalistic phrases your agency never uses? (e.g., "You will never get married").

Ethical Supervision and Growth

In a high-growth environment, practitioners can burn out. Ethical supervision involves bi-weekly "Chart Rounds" where the team discusses complex cases.

The Supervision Model:
1. Review: The junior practitioner presents a challenging chart.
2. Analysis: The founder applies the A.S.P.E.C.T. lens to find missed connections.
3. Resolution: A collective strategy is formed for the client.
4. Support: Assessing the practitioner's emotional state regarding the client's heavy themes.

Leadership Tip

Encourage your team to specialize. One practitioner might become the agency‚Äôs "Relationship Specialist" while another focuses on "Career Transitions." This increases the agency's overall authority.
