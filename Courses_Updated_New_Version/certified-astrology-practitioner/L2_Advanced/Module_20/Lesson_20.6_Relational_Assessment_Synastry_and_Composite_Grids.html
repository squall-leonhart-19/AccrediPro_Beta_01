Synastry Mechanics: The Interaction of Blueprints

Synastry is the diagnostic art of overlaying one person's Natal Blueprint onto another's. While individual analysis tells us who someone is, synastry tells us how they experience another person. In the professional setting, we look for the "intermeshing" of planets and houses.

A key mistake made by novice practitioners is focusing solely on the "soft" aspects (trines and sextiles). In professional assessment, we recognize that growth requires friction. However, too much friction without an elemental "safety valve" leads to burnout. We use the A.S.P.E.C.T. Framework‚Ñ¢ to analyze the "Analyze" (A) and "Synthesize" (S) phases of both charts simultaneously.

Practitioner Insight

When assessing synastry for a client (e.g., a 45-year-old woman entering a second marriage), prioritize Saturn contacts. While Jupiter feels good, Saturn provides the "glue" for longevity. A relationship without Saturn aspects often lacks the structural integrity to survive the "Plotting Temporal Transits" (P) phase of life cycles.

Composite Grids: The Third Entity

While synastry is about interaction, the Composite Chart is about the union itself. It is a mathematical average of two natal charts, creating a "Third Entity." If Chart A is Hydrogen and Chart B is Oxygen, the Composite Chart is Water.

In the professional assessment of a composite chart, we focus on:

  • The Composite Sun: The central purpose of the relationship. Why does this union exist?
  • The Composite Moon: The "internal climate" of the relationship. How do they feel when they are together?
  • The Composite Ascendant: How the world perceives the couple or partnership.

Davison vs. Midpoint: Which Tool to Use?

Professional practitioners must choose between two primary methods of composite calculation. Each has a specific diagnostic application.

Midpoint Composite

Calculates the mathematical midpoint between each planet (e.g., Midpoint of Sun A and Sun B). It is a symbolic chart of the internal psychological "vibe" of the relationship.

Best for: Feeling-based relationship coaching.

Davison Relationship Chart

Calculates a "real" time and place midpoint. It produces a chart with actual planetary positions that can be progressed and transited like a natal chart.

Best for: Business entities and predictive timing.

The 'Soul Purpose' of the Partnership

In Level 2 practice, we look beyond survival to Evolutionary Purpose. Every relationship serves a developmental goal. By analyzing the Composite North Node and the 8th/12th house placements, the practitioner can identify what the two souls are "working on" together.

For example, a Composite Sun in the 10th House suggests the relationship's purpose is Public Achievement or building a legacy together. A Composite Sun in the 4th House suggests the purpose is Private Healing and establishing a secure foundation.

Practitioner Insight

A "difficult" synastry (lots of squares) with a "strong" composite (Sun trine Jupiter) often indicates a couple that bickers constantly but accomplishes great things together. The synastry is the process; the composite is the product.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between Synastry and a Composite Chart?

Synastry assesses the interaction between two people (how they affect each other), while a Composite Chart represents the relationship itself as a third entity or "offspring" of the union.

2. Which "Red Flag" aspect is most associated with gaslighting and deception?

Neptune square Sun or Mercury. This aspect creates a "fog" where one or both parties may misinterpret facts or intentionally deceive to maintain an idealization.

3. When would a practitioner choose a Davison Chart over a Midpoint Composite?

When predictive timing is required (transits and progressions) or when assessing a business entity that has a "physical" presence in time and space.

4. Why is Saturn important in long-term synastry assessment?

Saturn provides the "structural glue." Without Saturn aspects, a relationship may be exciting (Uranus) or pleasant (Venus) but will lack the commitment and endurance required to weather life's challenges.
