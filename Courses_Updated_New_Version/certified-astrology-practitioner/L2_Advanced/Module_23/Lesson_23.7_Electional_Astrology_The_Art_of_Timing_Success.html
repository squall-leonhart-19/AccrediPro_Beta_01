The Concept of 'Radicality' in Electional Astrology

In Electional Astrology, we are creating a "birth chart" for an event. The most critical rule is that the chart must be Radical‚Äîmeaning it must accurately reflect the nature of the event being timed. If you are timing a wedding, Venus must be strong. If you are timing the launch of a law firm, Jupiter and Saturn must be prominent.

A common mistake among intermediate practitioners is looking for a "good day" in a general sense. In professional practice, there is no such thing as a universally good day; there is only a day that is aligned with the specific goal. We look for resonance between the Electional Chart and the Natal Blueprint of the individual performing the action.

The 'Moon is Queen' Rule

In the hierarchy of Electional Astrology, the Moon is the most important factor. While the Sun represents the core purpose, the Moon represents the unfolding of events on the physical plane. She acts as the "transmitter" of planetary energy to Earth.

The Three Pillars of Lunar Strength:

  • The Sign: The Moon should be in a sign that supports the activity. For business growth, the Moon in Taurus or Leo is often preferred. For quick communication, Gemini or Libra.
  • The Phase: Generally, we elect for an Increasing Moon (New to Full) for growth, expansion, and launches. We use a Decreasing Moon for ending things, surgery (to reduce swelling), or debt repayment.
  • Upcoming Aspects: The last aspect the Moon makes before leaving her current sign describes the final outcome of the endeavor. We always strive for the last aspect to be a conjunction, sextile, or trine to a benefic (Venus or Jupiter).

Essential Dignities and Planetary Hours

To give an election "teeth," we look for the ruler of the event to be in high Essential Dignity. If timing a financial transaction, we want the ruler of the 2nd house or Jupiter to be in its own sign (Domicile) or sign of Exaltation.

Furthermore, we utilize Planetary Hours to add a layer of micro-timing. Each day is ruled by a planet, and each hour of that day is also ruled by a planet. Launching a creative project during a Venus Hour on a Friday (Venus's day) provides a double layer of archetypal support.

Avoiding the 'Void of Course' Moon and Pitfalls

Even the most beautiful chart can be rendered ineffective by the Void of Course (VOC) Moon. A Moon is "void" when it makes no further major aspects to other planets before changing signs. Traditionally, actions initiated during a VOC Moon lead to "nothing coming of the matter."

Common Electional Red Flags:

  • Mercury Retrograde: Avoid for signing contracts or launching tech-heavy projects.
  • Mars in the 1st House: Can indicate conflict, accidents, or "too much heat" in the beginning of a venture.
  • Saturn on the Ascendant: Often brings delays, heavy burdens, or early failure to a new project.
  • Combust Ruler: If the planet ruling the event is within 8.5 degrees of the Sun, it is "burnt up" and loses its power to act.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the Moon considered the "Queen" of Electional Astrology?

The Moon acts as the transmitter of planetary energies to the physical plane. While other planets represent the "will" or "potential," the Moon governs the actual unfolding of events and the final outcome.

2. What does it mean for an electional chart to be "Radical"?

Radicality means the chart's planetary placements match the nature of the intent. For example, a "radical" chart for a legal battle would require a strong Mars or Jupiter, whereas a radical chart for a spa opening would require a strong Venus.

3. What is the primary risk of initiating a launch during a Void of Course Moon?

The traditional aphorism is that "nothing will come of the matter." It suggests that the energy is dissipated, leading to lack of results, lack of momentum, or the project simply fizzling out.

4. How do Planetary Hours enhance an election?

Planetary hours provide micro-timing that aligns the specific hour with the ruling planet of the activity, adding a layer of archetypal "potency" and resonance to the chosen moment.
