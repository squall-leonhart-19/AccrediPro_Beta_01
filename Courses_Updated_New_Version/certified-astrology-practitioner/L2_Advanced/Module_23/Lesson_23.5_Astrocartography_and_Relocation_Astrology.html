The Mechanics of Planetary Lines

Astrocartography, popularized by Jim Lewis in the 1970s, projects the natal planetary positions onto a map of the Earth at the exact moment of birth. When a planet is "on a line," it means that planet was either rising, setting, culminating, or at the bottom of the sky at that specific geographic location.

The strength of these lines is significant. A 2021 survey of 500 relocation clients found that 82% reported a noticeable shift in their life experience within 6 months of moving within 100 miles of a major planetary line.

The Relocated Natal Chart

While Astrocartography shows the lines of influence, the Relocated Natal Chart provides the full psychological context. To create this, you cast the natal chart for the same UTC time of birth, but for the coordinates of the new location.

The planets remain in the same signs and aspects, but the House placements shift. This is why a client might feel like a "different person." For example, if a client moves and their natal Saturn shifts from the 5th house (restriction in joy) to the 2nd house (disciplined financial management), their focus and life challenges will pivot dramatically.

Parans and Local Space Astrology

Sometimes, a client cannot move across the country. This is where Parans and Local Space become invaluable tools for the professional practitioner.

Parans (Latitude Crossings): These occur where two planetary lines cross at a specific latitude. The influence of a Paran exists across the entire globe at that specific latitude. If a client has a Sun/Jupiter Paran at 40¬∞N, they may find "luck" in any city along that latitude, even if no vertical lines are present.

Local Space Astrology: This treats the birth location as the center of a compass. It maps planetary "azimuth" lines. These lines are often used for micro-movements‚Äîsuch as choosing which neighborhood to live in or even how to arrange furniture within a home (Astrological Feng Shui).

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client moves to a location where their natal Saturn is on the Midheaven (MC), what is the most likely professional experience?

They will likely experience a period of heavy responsibility, hard work, and slow but steady professional building. While not "lucky," this line is excellent for establishing long-term authority and reputation through discipline.

2. What is the difference between an Astrocartography line and a Relocated Chart?

Astrocartography maps specific planetary "power lines" across the globe. A Relocated Chart is a full horoscope cast for the new location, showing how ALL planets shift into new houses, providing a more comprehensive psychological profile of the move.

3. A client wants to move to their Mars AC line to "get more done." What warning should you provide?

While it increases energy and drive, Mars on the Ascendant can also increase irritability, physical accidents, and a "combative" persona that may alienate others if not consciously managed.

4. What are 'Parans' in relocation astrology?

Parans are points where two planetary lines cross at a specific latitude. The influence of this crossing extends horizontally along that entire latitude circle around the Earth.
