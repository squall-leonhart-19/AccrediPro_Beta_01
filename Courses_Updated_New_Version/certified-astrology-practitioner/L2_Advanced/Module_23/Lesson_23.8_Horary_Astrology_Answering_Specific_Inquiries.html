1. Why is a Neptune square to a 6th House Sun particularly challenging for a medical professional?

The 6th House governs the physical body and work systems. A medical professional relies on "objective facts." Neptune dissolves these facts, creating "idiopathic" symptoms (symptoms with no known cause) that can lead to health anxiety or professional burnout.

2. What is the "Critical Node" in Deborah's case?

The Critical Node is the intersection of her Virgo Sun (need for order) and her Scorpio Moon (need for deep transformation). The conflict between "safety/routine" and "rebirth/risk" is the root of her physical tension.

3. When should you trigger a medical referral for a client with Neptune/Sun transits?

When somatic symptoms (like joint pain or brain fog) persist despite lifestyle changes, or if the client shows signs of clinical depression/inability to function. Astrology supports medical care; it does not replace it.

It forces her to evaluate her "tribe." She must leave the "hospital peer group" (which may be draining her) and find a "professional wellness peer group" that recognizes her new authority as a practitioner.
