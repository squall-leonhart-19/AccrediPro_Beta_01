Legitimacy comes from knowing where your work ends and another professional's begins. For Diane, we must be vigilant.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Diane's Chiron Return particularly significant given her background as a Nurse Practitioner?

Chiron represents the "Wounded Healer." In a clinical professional, the Chiron Return often triggers a crisis where the "healer" realizes they have been wounded by the system they worked in, requiring a shift from "institutional healing" to "personal/soul-based healing."

2. What does an "intercepted axis" in the 6th/12th houses usually indicate in a clinical scenario?

It indicates that the themes of work/health (6th) and subconscious/rest (12th) are "locked" or difficult to access directly. The client often feels like they are "going through the motions" without being able to take independent action (Aries) or find balance (Libra) in those areas.

3. Which natal aspect in Diane's chart suggests her history of high-level corporate/medical responsibility?

The Saturn-Pluto conjunction on the Midheaven (MC). Saturn represents structure and duty, while Pluto represents power and intensity. On the MC (the point of career), this suggests a life of heavy public responsibility and high-stakes work.

4. If Diane reports she is unable to sleep for 4 days and is hearing "whispers," what is your immediate professional response?

This is a clear Referral Trigger. You must immediately refer her to a medical doctor or mental health professional, as these are signs of acute psychological distress that fall outside the scope of astrological practice.
