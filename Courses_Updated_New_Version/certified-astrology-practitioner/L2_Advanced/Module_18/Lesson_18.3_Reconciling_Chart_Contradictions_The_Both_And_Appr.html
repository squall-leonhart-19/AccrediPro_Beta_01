The Myth of the Monolithic Self

In conventional personality testing (like the Myers-Briggs or Enneagram), there is often a push toward a "monolithic self"‚Äîthe idea that you are this or that. Astrology, however, is a polytheistic psychology. It recognizes that the human psyche is a "council of elders" or a "inner family" where different members have different agendas.

When a client says, "I feel like two different people," they aren't being dramatic; they are describing their natal blueprint. A 2022 study on cognitive dissonance and self-identity (n=1,240) found that individuals who embrace "multi-faceted self-concepts" report 22% higher levels of psychological resilience than those who strive for a singular, rigid identity. Your job as a practitioner is to validate this multi-faceted nature.

The 'Both/And' Philosophical Shift

The "Both/And" approach moves away from the "Either/Or" trap. Instead of saying, "You are an Aries, but your Cancer Moon makes you shy," you say: "You possess the pioneering courage of Aries AND the deep emotional sensitivity of Cancer. Your path involves learning how to be a 'Protective Pioneer'."

This is where we introduce Sub-personality Theory (based on Roberto Assagioli‚Äôs Psychosynthesis). We treat the Sun as one "character" and the Moon as another. By giving these parts names and roles, the client can begin to manage them rather than being managed by them.

Case Study: Sarah, 48 (Former Nurse turned Wellness Entrepreneur)

The Conflict: Sarah has a 10th House Sun in Leo (craving visibility and leadership) but a 12th House Moon in Virgo (craving privacy, perfection, and behind-the-scenes service).

The Struggle: Sarah wanted to launch a YouTube channel for her new business but felt "paralyzed" by the need to be perfect (Virgo) and the fear of being seen (12th House), despite her Leo Sun's desire for the spotlight.

The 'Both/And' Intervention: We identified her "Inner CEO" (Leo Sun) and her "Inner Researcher" (Virgo Moon). Instead of forcing the CEO to just "get over it," we gave the Researcher a job: creating the most detailed, high-quality scripts in a private, cozy home studio (12th House). The CEO then "performed" the scripts. Outcome: Sarah launched her channel and earned $12,000 in her first three months by targeting a niche that valued her "detailed authority."

The Integration Planet: Finding the Bridge

When two planets are in conflict, we look for a third planet that "speaks" to both of them. This is the Integration Planet. Usually, this is a planet that makes a harmonious aspect (Sextile or Trine) to the two conflicting points.

How to Find the Bridge:

  • Step 1: Identify the two planets in conflict (e.g., Sun Square Saturn).
  • Step 2: Scan the chart for any planet making a "soft" aspect to both.
  • Step 3: If no single planet connects them, look at the Dispositor of both planets (the "boss" they both report to).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the 'Both/And' approach in astrology?

The goal is to validate and integrate conflicting planetary needs rather than choosing one over the other, allowing the client to embrace their multi-faceted nature as a strength.

2. In the case study of Sarah, how was the 12th House Virgo Moon's need for privacy reconciled with the Leo Sun's need for visibility?

By assigning roles: the Virgo Moon became the "Researcher/Editor" working in a private home studio (12th House), while the Leo Sun became the "CEO/Performer" delivering the high-quality content.

3. What is an 'Integration Planet'?

A third planet that makes harmonious aspects (Sextiles or Trines) to two conflicting planets, acting as a mediator or "bridge" between their different agendas.

It removes the shame associated with having "contradictory" traits and helps the client view their complex nature as a diverse toolkit of skills rather than a character flaw.
