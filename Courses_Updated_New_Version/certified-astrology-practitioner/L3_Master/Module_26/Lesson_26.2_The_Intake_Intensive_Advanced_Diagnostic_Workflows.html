Stage 1: The Multi-Stage Intake Process

In a $997+ certification-level practice, the intake is not a form; it is an experience. High-ticket clients expect a level of rigor that justifies their investment. By splitting your intake into stages, you achieve two goals: you collect superior data, and you subconsciously signal that your work is deep, professional, and exclusive.

A professional multi-stage intake follows this sequence:

  1. The Quantitative Capture: Standard birth data, contact info, and "Top 3 Challenges."
  2. The Qualitative Discovery: A deep-dive narrative workbook (discussed in Section 3).
  3. The Psychological Overlay: Integration of standardized personality assessments.
Coach Tip

For women over 40 transitioning into this career, your professional background (nursing, teaching, HR) is your greatest asset here. Use the same intake rigor you used in your previous career. It builds instant legitimacy and calms "imposter syndrome" by showing the client you have a proven system.

Designing Pre-Session Discovery Workbooks

The "Discovery Workbook" is a 10-15 page PDF or digital form that the client must complete 72 hours before the intensive. This isn't just for you; it's a priming mechanism. By asking the client to reflect on their life history through an archetypal lens, you begin the Synthesis phase before the Zoom call even starts.

Key Questions for the Workbook:

  • "Describe a recurring 'brick wall' you hit every 7-9 years." (Saturn Cycle diagnostic)
  • "Which family member's voice do you hear when you're about to take a risk?" (4th House/Moon diagnostic)
  • "If your current life was a movie, what would the title of this current chapter be?" (Transit/Progressed Moon diagnostic)

Identifying "Core Narrative Themes"

During the first 30 days, your job is to identify the Core Narrative Theme (CNT). This is the "meta-story" that links the client's natal chart to their current life crisis. Using the A.S.P.E.C.T. Framework‚Ñ¢, you look for three repeating patterns:

  1. The Dominant Element: Is their life currently "too dry" (too much Earth/Air, not enough Water)?
  2. The Aspect Cluster: Are they currently re-living a natal T-Square through their current transits?
  3. The Rulership Chain: Who is the "CEO" of their chart right now? (The Chart Ruler or a prominent Time-Lord).
Coach Tip

Don't overwhelm the client with 50 themes. Professionalism is found in reduction. Aim to present the client with three core themes that explain 90% of their current friction. This creates the "Aha!" moment that secures their long-term trust.

Establishing Baseline Metrics

If you can't measure it, you can't prove your value. In astrology coaching, we use the Archetypal Alignment Scale (AAS). During the intake, have the client rate themselves (1-10) on various archetypal expressions:

  • Solar Vitality: "How much do I feel like the 'Sun' of my own life right now?"
  • Lunar Safety: "How well am I meeting my emotional and somatic needs?"
  • Saturnian Authority: "How much agency do I feel over my boundaries and structures?"

By capturing these numbers on Day 1, you can show a 40% increase in "Solar Vitality" by Month 3. This is how you turn a "woo-woo" session into a legitimate professional intervention.
