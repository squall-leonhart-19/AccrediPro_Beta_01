Common Practitioner Pitfalls

As you begin your career, watch out for these four common mistakes that can undermine your authority as a $200+/hour practitioner:

  • The "Dictionary" Approach: Listing definitions (e.g., "Leo means bold, Capricorn means hard-working") instead of weaving them into Elena's specific story about retirement.
  • Over-Apologizing: Saying "I think..." or "I might be wrong, but..." Astrology is an interpretive art; own your insights with confidence.
  • Using Too Much Jargon: Saying "Your Sun is square your Ascendant" to a client who doesn't know what a square is. Instead, say "There is a productive tension between your public image and your inner desires."
  • Ignoring the Clientâ€™s Age: A 52-year-old woman has lived through her Chiron Return and two Saturn Returns. Speak to her wisdom, not just her "potential."
Sarah's Practitioner Tip

Clients in their 40s and 50s are often looking for validation of what they already know deep down. When you see a placement that matches their lived experience, use the phrase: "Your chart confirms what your intuition has been telling you." This builds massive trust.

Success Markers

How do you know if your blueprint analysis was successful? Look for these signs:

  1. The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes or sighs when you describe their inner tension.
  2. The "How did you know?": The client asks how you knew a specific detail about their private feelings (usually the Moon sign).
  3. Actionable Insight: The client says, "Now I understand why I've been feeling so guilty."
  4. Future Focus: The conversation moves from "Why am I like this?" to "What should I do next?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's Sun in the 10th House particularly relevant to her retirement?

The 10th House governs career, public reputation, and legacy. Even though she is "retired," her Sun in this house suggests she still has a deep need to be recognized for her contributions and to lead in a public way.

2. How does Scorpio Rising affect the way Elena presents her Leo Sun?

Scorpio Rising often creates a "poker face" or a private exterior. This can create a conflict where she wants to be seen (Leo Sun) but her first instinct is to hide or protect herself (Scorpio Rising).

3. What is a "Vitality Source" in astrology?

The Sun placement. It represents the activities and energies that "recharge" the client's battery and give them a sense of purpose.

4. What is the danger of using "Astrological Jargon" with a new client?

It creates a barrier between the practitioner and the client. It can make the client feel confused or excluded, rather than empowered by the information.
