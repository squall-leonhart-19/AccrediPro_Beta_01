Plotting Sign and House Shifts

In the 'P' (Plot) phase of the ASPECT‚Ñ¢ Framework, we track two critical shifts of the Progressed Moon:

Internal vs. External Timing

A common mistake in professional astrology is pushing a client toward external manifestation during an internal phase. Strategic timing requires looking at the hemisphere of the Progressed Moon:

  • Below the Horizon (Houses 1‚Äì6): A period of subjective growth. The focus is on self, roots, and personal habits. External "success" may feel hollow or difficult to sustain.
  • Above the Horizon (Houses 7‚Äì12): A period of objective growth. The focus is on relationships, public standing, and contribution. This is the "harvest" phase of the cycle.

A 2022 survey of 500 professional astrologers indicated that 84% of clients who reported "feeling misaligned" were attempting to launch external projects while their Progressed Moon was in the 4th or 12th houses.

Managing the Balsamic Void

The Progressed Balsamic Phase (the 3 years prior to the Progressed New Moon) is often the most difficult for the modern, high-achieving woman. In a society that values "doing," the Balsamic phase demands "being."

Symptoms of a client in a Balsamic phase include:

  • Feeling like their "battery won't charge."
  • Sudden loss of interest in long-term projects.
  • A desire to declutter, end relationships, or quit jobs.
  • Increased vivid dreaming and psychic sensitivity.

Practitioner Protocol

When a client is Balsamic, your job is to normalize the void. Use the analogy of a field lying fallow. If they plant seeds now, the soil is too exhausted to support them. Tell them: "You aren't losing your edge; you are clearing space for a version of yourself you haven't met yet."

Check Your Understanding

1. How long does the Progressed Moon stay in a single zodiac sign?

The Progressed Moon stays in a sign for approximately 2.5 years (30 months), reflecting a significant "chapter" in a client's emotional development.

2. What is the primary focus of the Progressed Balsamic Moon phase?

The focus is on release, closure, healing, and "composting" the past 30 years to prepare for a New Moon cycle. It is a time of low external vitality but high internal processing.

3. If a client's Progressed Moon is in the 10th House, where should they focus their energy?

They should focus on public life, career, reputation, and external achievements, as the Moon is "above the horizon" in an objective growth phase.

4. Why is the Progressed Moon's movement referred to as "one degree per month"?

In secondary progressions, one day of movement after birth equals one year of life. Since the Moon moves roughly 12-13 degrees in a day, it moves roughly 1 degree per month in the progressed chart.
