Common Mistakes to Avoid

When you're starting out, it's easy to fall into these traps. Awareness is the first step to professional mastery.

  • The "Doom and Gloom" Trap: Telling a client a transit will be "hard" without providing the growth purpose. (e.g., "Saturn is going to crush your career" vs. "Saturn is helping you build a more solid foundation.")
  • Over-Technical Jargon: Using terms like "square," "quincunx," or "secondary progressions" without explaining what they mean for the client's life.
  • Ignoring Free Will: Acting like the planets are "doing" things to the client. Always frame it as "This is the energy available to you; how do you want to use it?"
  • The "Crystal Ball" Syndrome: Trying to predict exact events (e.g., "You will get a check for $5,000 on Tuesday") instead of describing the quality of the time.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological purpose of the Chiron Return (age 50-52)?

The Chiron Return is about healing the "core wounds" of identity and moving from a place of "doing" for others to "being" who you truly are. It often triggers a career or lifestyle shift that aligns more closely with one's soul purpose.

2. If a client is anxious about a "Saturn Square," how should you reframe it?

Reframe it as a "structural integrity check." Saturn isn't there to punish; it's there to show where the foundation is weak so the client can strengthen it before building higher.

3. Why is it important to use "Progressed Moon" cycles in coaching?

The Progressed Moon describes the client's internal "emotional weather." It helps explain why they might feel a shift in focus (e.g., from career to home) even if their external circumstances haven't changed yet.
