Internal Evolution vs. External Events

One of the most common mistakes junior practitioners make is confusing a transit with a progression. To provide a $997+ level of service, you must be able to explain the difference to your clients using the Atmosphere Analogy.

Feature Transits (External) Progressions (Internal)
Source Current positions of planets in the sky. Symbolic unfolding of the natal chart.
Experience Events happening to the client. Changes happening within the client.
Analogy The weather (rain, sun, storms). The biological clock (puberty, menopause, aging).
Client Quote "My boss fired me today." "I've realized I no longer care about this job."

When a client says, "I just don't feel like the same person anymore," they are describing a progression. A 2022 survey of professional astrologers found that 84% of clients seeking a "re-invention" were undergoing a major Progressed Moon sign or house ingress.

The 28-Year Progressed Lunar Cycle

Just as the Moon goes through phases every month, the Progressed Moon goes through a complete cycle every 27-29 years. This is the "internal life cycle."

1. The Progressed New Moon (The Planting)

This occurs when the Progressed Moon conjuncts the Progressed Sun. It is a time of low visibility but high potential. Clients often feel confused or "in the dark" during this 3-year window, but they are actually seeding a new 28-year life chapter.

2. The Progressed First Quarter (The Action)

Roughly 7 years after the New Moon, the "sprouts" meet resistance. This is a period of "crisis in action," where the client must make adjustments to their new direction. This often coincides with the first major career pivot.

3. The Progressed Full Moon (The Culmination)

At the 14-year mark, the Progressed Moon opposes the Progressed Sun. This is the peak of the cycle. Everything that was seeded at the New Moon is now in full bloom. It is a time of high visibility, manifestation, and sometimes, the realization that a path has reached its limit.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client is 35 years old, what day of their life do you look at to find their Secondary Progressions?

You look at the planetary positions on the 35th day after their birth. This is the "day-for-a-year" formula.

2. How long does the Progressed Moon typically stay in a single sign or house?

The Progressed Moon stays in a sign or house for approximately 2.5 years (around 28-30 months).

3. What is the main difference between a Transit and a Progression?

A Transit represents external "weather" or events happening to the client, while a Progression represents internal "climate" or the client's psychological evolution and maturing needs.

4. Which phase of the 28-year cycle is described as "the peak of visibility and manifestation"?

The Progressed Full Moon (the opposition between the Progressed Moon and Progressed Sun).
