The Uranus Opposition: Radical Authenticity

Between the ages of 38 and 44, transiting Uranus moves into an opposition (180¬∞) with its natal position. This is the astrological "Mid-life Crisis," but professionally, we call it the Great Awakening.

This transit acts as a "lightning bolt" to the chart. If a client has been living a life based on "shoulds" (I should be a lawyer, I should stay in this marriage), Uranus will create an internal‚Äîand often external‚Äîrebellion. Statistics show that career pivots during this window have a 65% higher satisfaction rate when they are aligned with the client's North Node (see Lesson 2).

Key Markers of the Uranus Opposition:

  • Sudden Restlessness: An inability to tolerate boredom or routine.
  • The "Now or Never" Feeling: A sense that time is running out to be "who I really am."
  • Breakthroughs vs. Breakdowns: Depending on the client's resistance, this manifests as a genius invention or a total collapse of current structures.
Coach Tip: Authenticity over Stability

During a Uranus Opposition, do NOT advise the client to "calm down" or "be sensible." Instead, help them identify which parts of their life are authentic and which are performative. The goal is to help them "break out" without "burning it all down" (unless the fire is necessary).

The 12-Year Jupiter Pulse: Expansion Windows

Jupiter returns to its natal position every 12 years (ages 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72). These are the "Golden Years" of the 'P' (Plot) phase. While Saturn contracts, Jupiter expands.

A professional practitioner uses the Jupiter Return to help clients Target Aligned Actions (Phase T). If a client wants to launch a course, write a book, or expand their practice, doing so during a Jupiter Return year provides a "cosmic tailwind."

Converting 'Heavy' Transit Years

A "Heavy" transit year usually involves outer planets (Pluto, Neptune, or Saturn) making hard aspects (Squares, Oppositions, Conjunctions) to the client's Sun, Moon, or Ascendant. Many clients come to astrology out of fear during these times.

Your job is to apply the 'C' (Convert Challenging Aspects) mindset. We do not use the word "bad." We use the word Evolutionary Intensity.

  • Pluto Transits: Theme is Metamorphosis. (Example: "You are being asked to let go of the ego-identity that no longer serves you.")
  • Neptune Transits: Theme is Dissolution. (Example: "The fog is here so you stop relying on your eyes and start relying on your intuition.")
  • Saturn Transits: Theme is Consolidation. (Example: "It's time to trim the fat and focus on what is essential.")
Coach Tip: Holding the Container

During heavy years, the client needs a "container." Schedule monthly check-ins rather than one-off readings. This provides the client with emotional stability and provides you with a predictable recurring revenue model (e.g., $3,000 for a 6-month "Transit Transformation" package).
