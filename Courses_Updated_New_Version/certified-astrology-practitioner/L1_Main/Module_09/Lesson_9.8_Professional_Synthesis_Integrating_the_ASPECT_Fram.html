Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

In my first year, I used to apologize for "bad" transits. Iâ€™ve learned that clients donâ€™t want an apology; they want a strategy. Here is what to watch for as you practice:

Avoid the "Encyclopedia" Trap

New coaches try to explain every single aspect. Professional practitioners focus on the 3 most impactful themes that solve the client's current problem.

2

Watch Your "Filler" Words

"Ums," "likes," and "sort ofs" drain your authority. Practice sitting in silence for 3 seconds while you look at the chart instead of filling the air with noise.

3

The "Wait, What?" Check

Every 15 minutes, ask: "Does that resonate with how you've been feeling?" This keeps the session a coaching dialogue rather than a lecture.
