Electional Foundations: The "Target" Phase

Electional astrology is the practice of choosing a specific time to begin an endeavor. In the A.S.P.E.C.T. Framework‚Ñ¢, this represents the T: Target Aligned Actions phase. While natal astrology tells us about the person, and transits tell us about the weather, electional astrology allows us to choose the best day to set sail.

For a professional practitioner, offering electional services‚Äîsuch as timing a wedding, a home purchase, or a business launch‚Äîis a high-value skill. Practitioners like Sarah, a 48-year-old former teacher, have found that adding "Strategic Launch Timing" packages to their offerings allows them to charge $350 to $500 per consultation, as the ROI for the client is tangible and significant.

The Rule of the Four Pillars

When targeting a window for a major launch or investment, we look for four primary "pillars" of strength:

  • The Moon: Must be well-placed, waxing (if growing something), and making a positive applying aspect to a beneficial planet (Venus or Jupiter).
  • The Ascendant: The sign rising at the moment of the action represents the "body" of the endeavor. The ruler of the Ascendant should be strong and unafflicted.
  • The Midheaven (MC): For business, the MC must be strong. It represents the reputation and public outcome of the action.
  • Planetary Dignity: The "hero" planet of the action (e.g., Mercury for a contract, Venus for a launch) should be in its home sign or exaltation.
Coach Tip: The Practitioner's Edge

Don't wait for perfection. In electional astrology, a "perfect" chart doesn't exist. Your job is to find the best available window within the client's practical timeline. Acknowledge the "least bad" compromises and focus on strengthening the Moon above all else.

Avoiding the Void-of-Course Moon

The Void-of-Course (VOC) Moon occurs when the Moon has made its last major aspect in its current sign and is waiting to enter the next sign. In traditional astrology, this is a period where "nothing will come of the matter."

For your clients, the VOC Moon is a "dead zone" for significant decision-making. If a client signs a contract or launches a website during a VOC Moon, the project often fails to gain traction, gets lost in the shuffle, or requires a total "do-over" later.

Planetary Days and Hours: Micro-Targeting

To truly differentiate yourself as a premium practitioner, you must master Planetary Hours. This ancient system divides the day into 24 "hours" (not 60 minutes, but calculated based on sunrise/sunset) ruled by the seven visible planets.

When a client says, "I have a difficult conversation with my boss today," you don't just look at the day; you look at the hour.

  • Mars Hour: Best for competitive sports, demanding boundaries, or physical labor. Avoid for delicate negotiations.
  • Mercury Hour: Best for emails, writing, teaching, and signing minor documents.
  • Jupiter Hour: Best for asking for a raise, filing legal paperwork, or seeking a mentor.
  • Venus Hour: Best for social media posting, beauty appointments, or romantic dates.

Coach Tip: Modern Tools

You don't need to do the math by hand! Use apps like 'TimeNomad' or 'Planetary Times' to help your clients find their "Power Hours." Teaching a client to send a high-stakes pitch during a Jupiter hour on a Thursday (Jupiter's day) builds incredible trust in your expertise.

The Moon acts as the "transmitter" of planetary energies to Earth. Its speed and proximity mean it triggers transits and brings potential into physical manifestation faster than any other body.

The primary risk is that "nothing will come of the matter." The action fails to produce a tangible result, or the circumstances change so significantly that the action becomes irrelevant.

A Jupiter Hour on a Thursday (Jupiter's Day) is the gold standard for financial expansion and seeking favor from institutions.
