Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

In my first year, I made these mistakes constantly. Avoiding them will instantly elevate you to the top 5% of practitioners.

  • The "Information Dump": Telling the client everything about their Mars, Saturn, and Jupiter without ever asking, "How does this apply to your Monday morning?"
  • Predicting vs. Empowering: Saying "You will get a client in June" (Predicting) vs. "The transits in June support your Mars; how can we prepare your systems now?" (Empowering).
  • Ignoring the Client's Age: A 52-year-old woman like Diane often has "Saturn baggage"â€”years of being told to follow the rules. Don't just give her more rules; use her chart to help her rewrite them.
  • Failing to Set a Target: Ending a session with "That was a great reading" instead of "Based on your Mars in Taurus, what is the one tangible task you are committing to by Friday?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Diane (Mars in Taurus) struggling with "hustle culture"?

Mars in Taurus is a Fixed Earth placement. It values stability, thoroughness, and tangible results over speed. Hustle culture's emphasis on rapid-fire action creates "mechanical stress" for this placement, leading to burnout rather than progress.

2. How does Saturn in the 10th house influence Diane's fear of starting?

Saturn in the 10th house creates a high "professional ego." The client fears looking like an amateur or being "messy" in public. She needs structured systems (Saturn) to feel that her reputation (10th House) is protected before she takes action.

3. What is the danger of focusing only on Diane's Jupiter in Aries?

Focusing only on Jupiter provides "visionary high" but no "engine." She will keep dreaming big (Aries vision) but will feel more and more like a failure when her Mars in Taurus engine doesn't keep up with the imaginary pace.

4. What is the most important component of an "Aligned Action"?

It must honor the planetary mechanics. An action is only "aligned" if it uses the client's natural drive (Mars) and respects their psychological boundaries/needs for structure (Saturn).

Success Markers: How You Know You Nailed It

If you finish a session and the following things happen, you have successfully applied the Target Aligned Action framework:

The "Sigh of Relief"

The client physically relaxes because you've stopped telling them to be someone they aren't (e.g., telling a Taurus Mars to "just move fast").

The Tangible Task

The client names a specific, small action they feel 100% confident they can complete within 48 hours.

Language Shift

The client stops saying "I should" and starts saying "My chart suggests I work best when..."

Key Takeaways

  • Mars is the Engine: Always look to Mars to see how a client should take action (speed, style, and duration).
  • Saturn is the Guardrail: Use Saturn to build the systems that make the client feel safe enough to take risks.
  • Permission is Power: Using the chart to "allow" a client to work in their natural rhythm is one of the most healing things you can do.
  • Action over Information: A professional practitioner always ends with a specific, chart-aligned homework task.
