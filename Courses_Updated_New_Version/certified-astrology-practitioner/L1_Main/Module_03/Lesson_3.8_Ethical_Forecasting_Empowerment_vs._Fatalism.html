Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New practitioners often fall into these traps when discussing transits. Watch out for these:

  • Fatalistic Language: Saying "Something bad will happen on this date" instead of "This is a period for heightened awareness in [Area of Life]."
  • Over-Technical Jargon: Spending 10 minutes explaining what a "Square" is instead of explaining how the square feels to the client.
  • Ignoring the Natal Chart: Forgetting that a transit only triggers what is already promised in the birth chart.
  • Missing the "Exit Date": Leaving the client in the "heavy" energy without showing them when the transit ends.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to give Elena an "exit date" for her Saturn transit?

Providing an exit date prevents "transit despair." It gives the client a psychological light at the end of the tunnel and allows them to plan their energy expenditure effectively.

2. If Elena asks, "Is this transit going to ruin my new business?", how should a professional coach respond?

Reframe the transit as an "integrity check." Explain that Saturn doesn't ruin things that are built well; it only exposes cracks in the foundation so they can be fixed before the business grows.

3. What is the "Anchor Technique" mentioned in the objectives?

It is the practice of linking a current transit (the "weather") back to the natal chart (the "seed"). It reminds the client that the current challenge is actually helping them fulfill their original life purpose.

4. Why should you avoid using the term "bad transits" with clients?

Because "bad" implies a lack of agency. Professional practitioners use "challenging," "dynamic," or "refining" to describe difficult aspects, which keeps the client in the driver's seat of their own life.
