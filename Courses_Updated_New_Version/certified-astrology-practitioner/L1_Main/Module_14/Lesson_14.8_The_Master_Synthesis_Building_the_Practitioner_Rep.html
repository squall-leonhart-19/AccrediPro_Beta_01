Common Pitfalls to Avoid

When you're first starting out, it's easy to get lost in the math. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • The "Doom and Gloom" Trap: Telling a client a planet is "bad" or "weak" without offering a way to work with it.
  • Over-Technicality: Using terms like "Peregrine" or "Mutual Reception" without explaining what they mean for the client's actual life.
  • Ignoring the Sun/Moon: Even if other planets are stronger, the Sun and Moon are the "Lights." Always start there.
  • Linear Reading: Reading each planet in isolation. Synthesis is about how the planets talk to each other.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client has a planet in its "Fall," what is the best coaching approach?

Frame the planet as being "out of its element" or "unsupported," and look for a stronger planet in the chart to act as a "mentor" or "support system" for that debilitated energy.

2. Why is the Sun in Leo considered "Dignified"?

Because Leo is the Domicile (home sign) of the Sun. In this sign, the Sun's natural qualities of visibility, leadership, and vitality are fully supported by the environment.

3. What is the difference between Essential Dignity and Accidental Strength?

Essential Dignity is about the planet's quality based on its sign (zodiacal state), while Accidental Strength is about its power to act based on its house position (angularity).

4. How does synthesis help a client with "Imposter Syndrome"?

Synthesis allows you to show the client that their doubt (Saturn) is a real, observable pattern in their chart, but it is countered by their inherent strengths (Sun/Jupiter), giving them a logical reason to trust their potential.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Strength is Contextual: A planet's dignity tells you how well it functions, but its house tells you where it acts.
  • Synthesis is Storytelling: Your job is to weave the data points into a cohesive narrative that the client can actually use.
  • Empowerment First: Always translate technical debilities into "growth opportunities" or "resource gaps."
  • Identify the Anchor: Every chart has at least one "strong" planet. Use that planet as the anchor for your coaching advice.
