Reading vs. Professional Coaching

A common mistake is simply "reading" the chart at the client. A professional practitioner uses the chart as a diagnostic tool to facilitate a coaching conversation. Note the difference in the table below:

The "Amateur" Approach The Professional Coaching Approach
"You have Uranus in your 2nd house, so you like weird money." "Your chart shows a deep need for financial independence and non-traditional work."
"Uranus is opposing your Uranus, so expect big changes now." "You're in a cycle that only happens once in a lifetimeâ€”it's designed to help you break free from old security patterns."
"You should quit your job because the stars say so." "Based on this cycle, what feels more risky: staying in a role that drains you, or exploring this creative path?"
Sarah's Pro Tip

Never tell a client what to do. The chart shows the weather; the client is the pilot. Your job is to give them the most accurate weather report so they can fly safely. This builds trust and keeps your practice ethically sound.

The Power of "Unreasonable"

Using the word "unreasonable" is a powerful coaching technique. It bypasses the client's logical/corporate brain and allows them to access their 8th-house intuition without feeling "irresponsible."

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're starting out, your desire to be "right" can sometimes get in the way of being helpful. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • Over-Explaining the Tech: The client doesn't need to know what a "quincunx" is. They need to know why they feel frustrated. Keep the jargon to a minimum.
  • The "Fortune Teller" Trap: Avoid saying "You will get a new job in October." Instead, say "The energy for career expansion is strongest in Octoberâ€”how can we prepare you to take advantage of that window?"
  • Ignoring the Body: 40-55 year old women are often dealing with physical shifts (perimenopause/menopause). If the chart shows a hard Saturn transit, acknowledge the need for physical rest, not just mental shifts.
  • Undercharging: If you charge $50, clients treat it like a "reading" for fun. If you charge $200+, they show up ready to do the work.
