Techniques for Acoustic Probing

Acoustic Probing is the physical act of using an instrument to "ping" the biofield. For the Resonance Scan, we typically use a high-quality Tuning Fork (Unweighted) or a small Tibetan Singing Bowl.

  1. The Clearing Strike: Strike the instrument away from the client to establish a clean baseline.
  2. The Descent: Slowly bring the instrument toward the client's body, starting 24 inches away.
  3. The Sweep: Move the vibrating instrument in a slow, linear path from the crown of the head to the feet.
  4. The Pause: When you hear a change in the sound (dissonance), stop and hold the instrument there for three breaths.
Coach Tip

Keep your wrist loose. A stiff wrist creates a "stiff" sound. Your body must be as resonant as the instrument you are holding.
