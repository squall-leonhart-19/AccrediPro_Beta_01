High-Frequency Instrumentation: Tingshas and Pyramids

For the Auric Alignment phase, we move away from the heavy mallets and large gongs toward instruments that produce pure, high-overtone profiles. These tools act as "scalpels" for the energy field, allowing for precision work.

Crystal Pyramids

Crystal pyramids, usually made of 99.9% pure quartz, produce a unique "spinning" sound. This is due to the geometry of the instrument, which creates complex interference patterns. In the "A" phase, the pyramid is used to "seal" the auric field, creating a sense of containment for the client.

Tingshas (Cymbals)

Traditional Tibetan Tingshas are used to "clear" the space. In a clinical setting, we use them to identify sonic stagnation. If the Tingsha sound "dies" quickly or sounds dull in a specific area of the client's body, it typically indicates a density or peak in the biofield that requires further sweeping.

Sensing Energetic Voids and Peaks

Advanced facilitators develop the ability to "read" the biofield through sound. This is known as Sonic Palpation. When you play a Tingsha or a small crystal bowl over the body, listen for changes in the decay of the sound.

1. Energetic Peaks (Densitites)

A "peak" is an area of excess energy, often manifesting as physical tension or acute stress. The Sound: The instrument may sound sharp, metallic, or the decay may be abruptly shortened.

2. Energetic Voids (Depletions)

A "void" is an area where energy is lacking, often seen in clients with chronic fatigue or grief. The Sound: The instrument may sound "hollow" or "thin," as if the sound is being "sucked in" rather than vibrating through the space.

Coach Tip

If you find a void, do not "sweep" it away. Instead, pause the instrument over that area and play gently until the sound begins to resonate clearly, indicating the field has "filled" or "rebalanced."
