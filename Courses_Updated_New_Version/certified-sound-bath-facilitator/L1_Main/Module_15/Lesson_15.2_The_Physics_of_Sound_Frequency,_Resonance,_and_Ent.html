The Anatomy of a Wave: Frequency and Amplitude

Sound is essentially a mechanical wave that travels through a medium (like air or water) by vibrating the molecules. For the sound bath facilitator, two primary measurements dictate the therapeutic outcome: Frequency and Amplitude.

Frequency is measured in Hertz (Hz) and refers to the number of vibrations per second. In the R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. Method‚Ñ¢, frequency is our primary tool for targeting specific brainwave states. Lower frequencies (like a 38" Gong) ground the client, while higher frequencies (like a 4.5" Crystal Bowl) often target the upper energy centers.

Amplitude is measured in Decibels (dB) and refers to the "height" of the wave‚Äîor the volume. While frequency determines the pitch, amplitude determines the intensity of the physical sensation.
