Transitioning from Clearing to Sealing

The shift from dispersing energy to consolidating it requires a change in your physical movement and the "shape" of the sound. During clearing, your movements are often outward, pulling energy away from the body. During sealing, your movements must be circular and enveloping.

1

Change the Tempo

Move from the erratic or intense rhythms used to break up blockages to a steady, rhythmic, and predictable pulse. This signals to the nervous system that the "work" is done and "integration" has begun.

2

The Clockwise Spiral

Always move your instruments in a clockwise direction around the client. In bioenergetics, clockwise movement is considered "building" or "strengthening," whereas counter-clockwise is "cleansing" or "releasing."

3

Volume Decrescendo

Gradually reduce the volume of the sealing instruments. This pulls the client's attention from the external sound back into their own physical center, anchoring the energy within.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it clinically dangerous to omit the sealing phase after a deep auric clearing?

Omitting the sealing phase leaves the client's biofield "porous" or "unzipped." This can lead to sensory overwhelm, emotional volatility, and a feeling of vulnerability as the client absorbs external environmental "noise" without their natural energetic protection.

2. What is the primary difference in movement between "Clearing" and "Sealing"?

Clearing movements are often outward and sometimes counter-clockwise to disperse stagnation. Sealing movements are circular, clockwise, and enveloping to consolidate and strengthen the field.

Koshi Chimes (specifically Aria or Ignis elements) are ideal because they produce a high-frequency, complex, yet coherent set of overtones that feel protective and light.

Key markers include profound physical stillness, a healthy flush in the skin tonality, and verbal feedback using words like "contained," "solid," or "whole."
