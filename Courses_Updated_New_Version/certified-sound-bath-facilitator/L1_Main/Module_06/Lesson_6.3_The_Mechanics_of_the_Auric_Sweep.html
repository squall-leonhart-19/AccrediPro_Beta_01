Directionality and Flow: The Intentional Path

The direction in which you move an instrument through the client‚Äôs field dictates the energetic outcome of the session. In sound therapy, we operate on the principle that energy follows sound. By moving a singing bowl or a chime in a specific direction, you are literally "herding" the client's subtle energy.

1. Crown to Root (Descending Flow)

This is the most common direction used in the Auric Sweep. It is primarily used for clearing, grounding, and sedating. When a client arrives with high anxiety, racing thoughts, or "top-heavy" energy, moving the sound from the crown down toward the feet helps anchor their awareness back into the physical body.

2. Root to Crown (Ascending Flow)

This direction is used for energizing, uplifting, and activating. If a client is experiencing lethargy, depression, or a sense of being "stuck," moving the sound from the feet toward the head encourages the upward flow of Prana or Qi, stimulating the nervous system and refreshing the auric field.

Linear vs. Spiral Sweeping Patterns

How you move your hand and the instrument determines how the energy is redistributed. We categorize these into two primary mechanical patterns.

1. Linear Sweeping (The "Flush")

Linear sweeps move in straight lines parallel to the body. This is used for movement and evacuation. If you identify a "clump" of energy in the solar plexus, you use a linear sweep to pull that energy down toward the feet to be grounded. It is efficient, fast, and highly effective for general clearing.

2. Spiral Sweeping (The "Drill")

Spiral sweeps involve moving the instrument in a clockwise or counter-clockwise circular motion while simultaneously moving along the body.

  • Clockwise Spirals: Used to infuse energy or "tighten" a leak in the auric field.
  • Counter-Clockwise Spirals: Used to draw out or "unscrew" deep-seated stagnation that won't budge with a linear sweep.

Facilitator Tip

üí° Use the "Spiral" to loosen the energy first, then follow with a "Linear" sweep to move it out of the field entirely. This two-step approach is significantly more effective than either method alone.

The 'Shatter and Sweep' Method

In the R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we often encounter "dense" energy‚Äîenergy that feels heavy, cold, or resistant. This is common in clients dealing with chronic grief or physical injury. For these areas, we use the Shatter and Sweep protocol.

  1. Identify: Locate the density (often felt as a "push back" or a change in the instrument's tone).
  2. Shatter: Use a high-intensity, high-frequency instrument (like a Crystal Tuner or Zaphir Chime) held directly over the area. Use rapid, short oscillations to "break up" the density.
  3. Sweep: Immediately follow the shattering with a long, slow linear sweep toward the feet using a lower-frequency instrument (like a weighted tuning fork or a small Tibetan bowl).

Combing Energetic Tangles and Static

Think of the auric field like long hair that has become knotted. When the field has "tangles" (disharmonious interference patterns), the sound will often sound "sour" or "warbled."

The Combing Technique: Using a high-frequency instrument, move slowly through the field. When you hear the sound "warble," stop. Move the instrument back and forth over that specific spot (about 3-4 inches of travel) until the tone clears and becomes steady again. This is "combing" the field back into a coherent state.
