Proper Striking and Handling Techniques

The way you hold and strike your instruments determines the purity of the frequency. In Auric Alignment, a "clunky" strike can actually cause a jarring response in the client‚Äôs nervous system.

  1. The "Feather" Strike: For chimes, avoid aggressive shaking. Use a gentle, circular wrist motion to let the internal striker dance against the glass or metal rods.
  2. The "Crystalline" Tingsha Strike: Hold the leather cord about 2 inches above the cymbals. Strike the edges together at a 45-degree angle. Never strike them flat-on, as this creates a "thud" instead of a "ring."
  3. The Auric Sweep: Move the instrument in a slow, continuous arc about 12-18 inches above the client's body. Imagine you are "combing" the energy field.
Coach Tip

Practice your "sustain" timing. The silence after the high-frequency strike is just as important as the sound itself. Allow the sound to fully dissipate into the field before striking again.
