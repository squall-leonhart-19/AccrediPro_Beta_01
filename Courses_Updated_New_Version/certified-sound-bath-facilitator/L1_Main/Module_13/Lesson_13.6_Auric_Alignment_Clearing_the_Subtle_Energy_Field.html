The 'Halo Sweep': Sealing the Field

The "Halo Sweep" is the final technical maneuver of the Auric Alignment phase. After clearing debris, the field is often "open" and vulnerable. The Halo Sweep uses a high-frequency, sustained tone to create a protective energetic boundary.

How to Execute the Halo Sweep:

  1. Select a high-vibration instrument (Crystal Pyramid or high-octave chime).
  2. Start at the Crown (head) of the client.
  3. Move the instrument in a slow, continuous circle around the head, approximately 12 inches above the physical body.
  4. Slowly spiral the sound outward, moving down the length of the body toward the feet.
  5. Imagine you are "weaving" a golden thread of sound around the client.

Localized Clearing vs. Systemic Alignment

It is crucial to understand the difference between these two approaches to ensure your sessions are both efficient and effective.

Localized Clearing is "spot treatment." You identify a specific area of congestion (like Sarah's shoulder) and apply sound until the sustain returns. This is often necessary for clients with physical injuries or specific emotional traumas.

Systemic Alignment is the "general maintenance" of the entire field. It involves moving through the Chakra Map (C through B) to ensure the entire biofield is vibrating in harmonic resonance. A professional session should include both: systemic alignment as the foundation, and localized clearing as needed.
