Identifying Sonic Blockages

A sonic blockage is an area of the body where energy is no longer flowing in a coherent wave pattern. Physically, this often manifests in the fascial network. Fascia is piezo-electric; it generates an electric charge when compressed or stretched. When fascia becomes "bound" due to trauma or repetitive stress, it loses its ability to conduct frequency efficiently.

Common locations for blockages include:

  • The Jaw (TMJ): Often holds "unspoken" emotional frequency or acute anger.
  • The Diaphragm: The primary site for "fear-based" frequency, leading to shallow breathing.
  • The Pelvic Bowl: Frequently stores chronic stagnation related to creative or ancestral trauma.
