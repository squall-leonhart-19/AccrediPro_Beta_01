Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you build your confidence, watch out for these common "rookie" mistakes that can leave a client feeling unsupported.

Mistake: Rushing the "Exit"

New coaches often feel awkward in the silence after a session and start talking too much or packing up their bowls. Correction: Maintain the "container" for at least 5-10 minutes after the last sound.

!

Mistake: High-Beta Questions

Asking "How did that feel?" or "What did you see?" too early forces the brain into analytical thinking. Correction: Focus on physical sensations first ("How does your body feel?") before emotional ones.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it recommended to offer water or a small snack after a sound bath?

It stimulates the digestive system and swallowing reflex, which are physical "rest and digest" functions that help transition the client from a meditative state back into their physical body.

2. What is a "High-Beta" question, and why should it be avoided immediately after a session?

A High-Beta question is one that requires analytical or complex thinking (e.g., "What did that vision mean?"). It should be avoided because it forces the brain to jump too quickly from a relaxed state to a stressed/analytical state, which can cause headaches or irritability.

3. A client says, "I don't think I can drive yet." What is your professional response?

Acknowledge that it's a normal feeling, offer them more water, and have them sit in a designated "integration area" for another 15 minutes. Never let a disoriented client leave your space.

4. Which of the following is a "Business Grounding" task?

Ensuring you have a signed liability waiver and tracking your session income. These tasks ground your practice in professional reality and legal safety.
