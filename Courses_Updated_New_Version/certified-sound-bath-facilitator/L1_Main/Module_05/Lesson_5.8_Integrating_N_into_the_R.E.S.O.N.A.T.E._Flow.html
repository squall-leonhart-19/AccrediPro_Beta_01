Common Mistakes to Avoid

When I was starting out, I made these mistakes often. Here is how to avoid them to ensure you look like the expert you are:

  • Over-Explaining: Don't give a 20-minute lecture on neurobiology. Keep the "science talk" under 3 minutes.
  • The "Woo" Trap: Avoid saying "this will clear your aura" to a client like Diane. Stick to "this will lower your cortisol."
  • Ignoring the Body: Always ask where they feel stress in their body (neck, jaw, etc.) to anchor the neural reset to a physical sensation.
  • Rushing the Transition: Don't jump straight from the script to the first loud gong. Use a "bridge" of silence or soft humming.
