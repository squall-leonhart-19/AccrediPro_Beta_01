Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. Being aware of them sets you apart as an AccrediPro Certified Professional.

  • Rushing the Intake: New coaches often want to get to the "music" quickly. If you rush the intent, the sound bath will be less effective.
  • Telling vs. Guiding: Don't tell the client what their intent should be. Instead, use their own words to reflect it back to them.
  • Using "Try": Avoid saying "Try to relax." Instead, use active language: "Allow yourself to relax" or "Invite a sense of ease."
  • Ignoring the Body: Intent isn't just mental. Always ask where they feel the stress or the relief in their physical body.
Sarah's Insight

In my first year, I used to talk too much. I realized that the less I said, the more room the client had to heal. Aim for "Minimal Effective Dose" of coaching.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to move from a vague goal like "relaxation" to a specific intention?

Specific intentions provide a "hook" for the mind to return to during the sound bath. While relaxation is a general byproduct, a specific intention (like "trusting my wisdom") addresses the root emotional cause of the client's stress, leading to a more profound and lasting transformation.

2. What is the "Bridge Technique" in the context of establishing intent?

The Bridge Technique involves taking the client's vague or negative complaint (e.g., "I'm stressed about work") and bridging it to a positive, present-tense intention (e.g., "I am calm and capable in my career") using their own vocabulary.

3. If a client struggles to find an intention, what is the best "Sarah-approved" question to ask?

"If we could quiet all of the noise for just the next 60 minutes, what is the one feeling you‚Äôd like to invite into that space?" This removes the pressure of "solving" a problem and focuses on the immediate emotional need.

4. A 2019 study on "Implementation Intentions" suggests that specific mental framing increases what?

It increases the likelihood of goal attainment and emotional regulation. By framing the sound bath with a specific intent, the brain's reticular activating system (RAS) is primed to notice and integrate the healing frequencies more effectively.
