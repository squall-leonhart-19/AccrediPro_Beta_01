Auric Alignment (A): Sweeping the Biofield

While Neural Reset focuses on the physical nervous system, Auric Alignment addresses the subtle energy field, often referred to as the Biofield. This field extends approximately 3 to 5 feet from the physical body and can hold "sonic static"‚Äîresidual emotional or environmental energy.

To clear this field, we use high-frequency instruments such as Tingshas, Koshi Chimes, or high-pitched bells. The technique is known as the "Auric Sweep."

The Auric Sweep Technique:

  1. Initial Strike: Strike the Tingsha or bell near the crown of the client's head.
  2. Movement: Slowly move the instrument in a zig-zag or spiral pattern down the length of the body, approximately 12 inches above the skin.
  3. Listening for Dissonance: Notice where the sound "dips" or becomes dull. This indicates an area of energetic congestion.
  4. Resolution: Repeat the sweep over congested areas until the tone rings clear and bright.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why are pure sine-wave tones preferred for the 'Neural Reset' phase?

Pure sine waves provide a predictable, stable frequency that the brain can easily entrain to. This stability signals safety to the nervous system, allowing the HPA axis to downregulate without the "startle response" often triggered by complex or unpredictable overtones.

2. What is the primary biological marker of a successful parasympathetic shift during a sound bath?

Heart Rate Variability (HRV). An increase in HRV indicates that the autonomic nervous system is flexible and that the parasympathetic "braking system" is functioning correctly.

3. In Auric Alignment, what does a "dull" or "flat" sound during a sweep indicate?

A dull or flat tone indicates an area of energetic congestion or "static" in the biofield. This is where the facilitator should spend extra time, sweeping the area until the instrument's tone returns to its natural, clear resonance.

4. Which musical interval is recommended to stimulate Nitric Oxide production and vasodilation?

The Perfect Fifth (e.g., C and G, or F and C). This interval is mathematically harmonious and has been shown in clinical studies to trigger the release of Nitric Oxide in the tissues.
