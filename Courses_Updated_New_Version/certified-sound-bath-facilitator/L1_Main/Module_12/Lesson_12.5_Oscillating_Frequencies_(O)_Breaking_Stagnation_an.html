In the context of the R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. Method‚Ñ¢, oscillation refers to the rhythmic movement of sound waves that creates a "push and pull" effect on biological tissue. Harmonic Intervals: Perfect 5ths vs. Tritones

The choice of musical interval determines the type of movement you create in the client's body. In sound therapy, we categorize intervals by their "consonance" (stability) and "dissonance" (tension).

While digital binaural beats require headphones, a skilled sound facilitator can create acoustic beats in a physical space using two instruments (such as two tuning forks or two singing bowls) with slightly different frequencies.

This creates a Theta-wave pulse that is felt not just in the ears, but as a physical vibration throughout the skull and spine. Managing Intensity: The "Swell" Technique

A common mistake for new facilitators is staying at one volume level. To break stagnation, you must master the Swell. This involves increasing the volume and complexity of the sound to a "peak" of intensity, then suddenly receding into silence or a soft, stable tone.

This "peak" pushes the client's nervous system to the edge of its comfort zone (hormetic stress), which triggers the Transmutive Release (T) phase. For your clients‚Äîmany of whom may be "people pleasers" or high-achievers‚Äîthis controlled intensity provides a safe container to finally "let go" without having to explain why.
