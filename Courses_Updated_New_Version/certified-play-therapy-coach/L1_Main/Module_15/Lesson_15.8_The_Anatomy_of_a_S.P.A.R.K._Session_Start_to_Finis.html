Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Pitfall Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome; trying to prove you know enough. Keep explanations brief. Focus on the client's results, not the theory.
Discounting Pricing Fear of rejection or "feeling bad" for the client. Remember: A committed client (who pays) gets better results. Stick to your rates.
Rushing Regulation Wanting to get to the "fun play part" too fast. The brain can't learn if it isn't regulated. Always start with 'R' (Regulation).
