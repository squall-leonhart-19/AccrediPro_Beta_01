Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

Common Mistakes for New Coaches

  • Over-explaining the science: Turning the session into a biology lecture instead of a coaching conversation.
  • Agreeing with the client's limitations: Saying "Yeah, technology is hard at our age" reinforces the fixed pathway.
  • Moving too fast: Not letting the metaphor "land" before moving to the next point.
Sarah's Insight

If you feel imposter syndrome creeping in, remember: You don't need to be a neurosurgeon. You just need to know more than the client and have the tools to help them apply it.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "dirt road" metaphor effective for a client like Diane?

It externalizes the problem. It shows Diane that she isn't "broken," but rather that her brain has simply "paved" a specific path over time that can be redirected.

2. What biological process are you describing when you talk about "paving the new road"?

Long-Term Potentiation (LTP). This is the strengthening of synapses based on recent patterns of activity.

3. If Diane says "I'm too old for this," how should a Brain Health Coach respond?

Reframe by citing that neuroplasticity occurs throughout the entire lifespan, provided the right stimulus and environment are present.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Establish Plasticity" phase in coaching?

To move the client from a "fixed" mindset to a "growth/plastic" mindset so they are biologically primed for the habit changes in the following modules.

Lab Summary & Key Takeaways

  • Biology is Hope: Explaining that the brain can change is the first step to establishing plasticity.
  • Metaphors Matter: Use simple, relatable imagery (roads, muscles, hiking trails) to bypass client resistance.
  • Listen for "Fixed" Language: Your ears must be tuned to hear phrases like "I've always been this way" as neural grooves.
  • Confidence is Key: Your belief in the client's neuroplasticity will often precede their own belief.
