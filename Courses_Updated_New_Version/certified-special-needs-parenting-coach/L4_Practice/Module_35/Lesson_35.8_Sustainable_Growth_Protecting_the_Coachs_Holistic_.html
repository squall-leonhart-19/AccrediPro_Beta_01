Handling Scaling Objections

When you scale to group programs, you will hear different objections than 1-on-1 coaching. Here is how to handle them with authority.

"Will I get enough personal time with you in a group?"
"That's a great question. In fact, most of my clients find they learn faster in the group because they hear answers to questions they didn't even know they had yet. However, I also include a private 1:1 'Kickstart Session' to ensure your specific family plan is solid."
"I'm so busy, I don't know if I can keep up with a group."
"I completely understand. That‚Äôs actually why the program is structured this way‚Äîit‚Äôs designed for the 'overwhelmed parent.' All sessions are recorded, and the 'Action Steps' are broken down into 15-minute daily bites. We aren't adding to your plate; we're clearing the clutter off of it."
Emma's Pro Tip

Never lower your price the moment someone hesitates. Instead, increase the perceived value. Remind them of the cost of inaction‚Äîthe cost of another six months of burnout and family tension.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in the discovery call?

The purpose is to uncover the prospect's "pain points" and the "cost of inaction." By asking where they will be in six months if nothing changes, you help them realize that the investment in coaching is smaller than the emotional cost of staying stuck.

2. How should you respond when a prospect says, "I'm worried I won't get enough 1:1 time in a group"?

Validate their concern, then pivot to the unique benefits of group coaching (shared wisdom, community, seeing others succeed). You can also mention "Hybrid" elements like a private kickstart call to provide extra reassurance.

3. According to the data-table, which model offers the highest revenue potential with the lowest time commitment for the coach?

The Signature Group Program. While the price point is lower than 1:1, the ability to coach many people in the same hour makes it the most scalable and profitable model per hour worked.

4. Why is it important to present the price confidently without offering an immediate discount?

Confidence in your pricing reflects confidence in your results. If you discount immediately, you signal that your program might not be worth the full amount. Instead, focus on increasing the perceived value and solving their specific problem.

Key Takeaways

  • Scaling is a mindset shift from "selling hours" to "selling a signature transformation."
  • The 4-Phase Discovery Call (Connection, Deep Dive, Invitation, Close) is your primary tool for growth.
  • Objections are not "Nos"‚Äîthey are requests for more information and safety.
  • Hybrid and Group models allow you to break the $10k/month barrier without increasing your workload.
  • Your background (as a nurse, teacher, or mom) is your greatest asset in building trust during the sales process.
