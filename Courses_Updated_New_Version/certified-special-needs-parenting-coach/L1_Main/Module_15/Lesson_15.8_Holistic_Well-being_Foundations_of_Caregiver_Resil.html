Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-Explaining the Science: In an enrollment call, the client cares about outcomes, not the specific neurobiology of the prefrontal cortex. Keep it focused on their relief.
  • The "Helper" Trap: Avoid giving away all your best advice for free in the first 20 minutes. If you solve their immediate problem, they won't feel the need to commit to the long-term transformation they actually need.
  • Ignoring the Spouse: If a client says "I need to talk to my husband," don't see it as a 'no.' Say: "I'd love for him to be on board. What information can I give you to help him understand the ROI of this investment?"
  • Fearing the Price: When you state your fee, stop talking. Silence is your friend. Let them process the value.

It establishes professional authority and "legitimacy" immediately. It shows the client you have a structured, proven system rather than just "winging it."

3. What is the "Helper Trap" in a discovery call?

The tendency to give away all the solutions for free, which prevents the client from committing to the full 12-week transformation they actually need for long-term success.
