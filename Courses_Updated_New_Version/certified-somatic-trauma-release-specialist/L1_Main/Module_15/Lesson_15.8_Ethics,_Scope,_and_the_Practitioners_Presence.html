Common Pitfalls for New Specialists

As you transition into your practice, watch out for these common "imposter syndrome" traps:

1

Over-Explaining the Science

You don't need to prove how smart you are by quoting every study on the Vagus nerve. Focus on Elena's experience, not your textbook knowledge.

2

Rushing the Release

If Elena's jaw doesn't relax in session one, that's okay. Pushing for a "result" creates more bracing. Trust the process.

3

Ignoring Your Own Body

If you feel tense while coaching, she will feel it too. Use the grounding techniques you've learned while you listen to her.
