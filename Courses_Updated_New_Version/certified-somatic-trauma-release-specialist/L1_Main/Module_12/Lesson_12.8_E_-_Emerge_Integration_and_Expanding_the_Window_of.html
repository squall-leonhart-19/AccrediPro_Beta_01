Clients like Elena often have a very strong "Protector" part. They are afraid that if they drop the tension, they will stop being successful. Your job isn't to take the tension away; it's to help her body realize the "threat" is over so the tension can naturally soften.

Your goal in this script is to move Elena from her Narrative (the story of her stress) into her Soma (the physical experience of it).

Practice Exercise: Out-Loud Prompts

Somatic coaching is 50% what you say and 50% how you say it. Your voice is a tool for co-regulation.

Say This Out Loud (Recording Exercise)

Find a quiet space. Record yourself saying the following lines. Listen back for a warm, slow, and grounded tone.

Prompt 1: Validating the Body's Wisdom

"Elena, thank you for sharing that. It makes so much sense that your body is holding on so tightly. It's been your armor for a long time. We aren't here to rip that armor off; we're just here to see if it's okay to loosen the straps a little bit."

Prompt 2: The Shift to Sensation

"As you talk about that deadline, I'm noticing your shoulders moved up toward your ears. Just notice that with me. What does that 'up-ness' feel like from the inside? Is it a tightness? A buzzing? A coldness?"

Olivia's Tip for Imposter Syndrome

When I started, I felt like I had to have all the answers. I didn't! The most powerful thing you can say is: "I'm right here with you. Let's just notice what happens next." You are a witness, not a mechanic.

Common Pitfalls for New Somatic Coaches

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps when they are first transitioning into somatic work.

The Mistake Why it Happens The Somatic Correction
Rushing to "Release" Wanting the client to feel better immediately to prove your worth. Slow down. Release cannot be forced; it must be invited through safety.
Over-Explaining Science Using "brain talk" to feel like a legitimate expert. Keep education brief (2 mins max). Get back into the body's sensations.
Ignoring Your Own Body Focusing so hard on the client that you hold your own breath. Self-regulate first. If you are tense, the client's nervous system will mirror you.
"Fixing" the Emotion Trying to make the client stop crying or stop being angry. Allow the "Somatic Wave" to complete. Emotions are just energy in motion.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to start with "Grounding" (like feeling the chair) before addressing the "Trauma" (the neck tension)?

It establishes "Felt Safety." If we go straight to the pain, the nervous system may perceive the coaching as a threat and tighten further. Grounding provides a "resource" the client can return to if the somatic release becomes too intense.

2. If Elena says, "I don't feel anything, I'm just numb," what is your best response?

Validate the numbness. "Numbness is a sensation, too. Itâ€™s the bodyâ€™s way of buffering. Letâ€™s just notice the 'nothingness' together. Where does the 'nothing' start and end?" This prevents the client from feeling like they are failing at the exercise.

3. What is "Vocal Prosody" and why does it matter in this lab?

Vocal prosody is the rhythm, pitch, and tone of your voice. In somatic work, a melodic, calm, and low-pitched voice signals to the client's Vagus nerve that they are safe (the Social Engagement System), allowing their muscles to relax.

4. Elena starts to shake slightly during the session. What should you do?

Encourage it gently. "I see that little bit of shaking. That's your nervous system discharging old energy. It's safe to let that happen. Just breathe and stay with it." Shaking is a classic sign of somatic release.

Key Takeaways for Professional Practice

  • Safety First: You cannot release trauma in a body that feels under threat. Co-regulation is your primary tool.
  • Language Shift: Move from "Why do you feel this?" (Cognitive) to "What does it feel like?" (Somatic).
  • The Power of Slow: In the somatic world, "Slow is fast." The slower you go, the deeper the nervous system can integrate.
  • Professional Legitimacy: Your value isn't in "fixing" people; it's in facilitating a process that their body already knows how to do.
