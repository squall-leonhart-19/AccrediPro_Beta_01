The Somatic Coaching Script

"Elena, I'm going to invite you to pause there for just a moment. I hear the weight of that story, and I can see how much you've been carrying. Right now, as you're talking about that meeting... where do you feel that in your body?"

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're starting out, it's easy to fall back into "problem-solving" mode. Avoid these common mistakes:

The Mistake Why it Hinders Progress The Somatic Fix
Joining the Story You start asking questions about the ex-husband or the job. Acknowledge the story, but pivot immediately to the sensation.
Rushing the Release Trying to make the "rock" go away in the first 5 minutes. Allow the sensation to exist. Trauma releases when it feels safe, not forced.
Over-Explaining Giving a 10-minute lecture on the nervous system mid-session. Keep explanations brief. "Your body is holding this for protection."
Ignoring Your Own Body Holding your breath while the client is struggling. Model regulated breathing. If you are calm, their nervous system will co-regulate.
Practice Prompt

Stand in front of a mirror and practice the phrase: "I hear the story, but let's see what the body is saying." Say it until it feels like a natural part of your vocabulary.

Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

In a somatic session, success isn't measured by the client saying "I feel better." It's measured by Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) shifts. Watch for these markers in Elena:

  • Spontaneous Sighing: A deep, involuntary breath that signifies the diaphragm is relaxing.
  • Softening of the Eyes: Moving from a "hard stare" or "darting eyes" to a soft, present gaze.
  • Skin Color Changes: Flushing or "pinking" of the skin as blood flow returns to the periphery.
  • Stomach Gurgling: A sign that the body has moved from Sympathetic (Fight/Flight) to Parasympathetic (Rest/Digest).
Professional Confidence

Remember, Elena isn't looking for you to be a guru. She's looking for a safe container. If you can stay calm while she feels that "rock" in her stomach, you have already succeeded.

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena starts crying while talking about her ex-husband. What is your first somatic priority?

Ensure she stays "present" in her body. Ask: "As those tears come, can you feel your feet on the floor?" This prevents her from spiraling into a past memory and keeps her in the safety of the current moment.

2. Why is "stomach gurgling" considered a success marker in a session?

It indicates a shift into the Parasympathetic Nervous System. When we are in "survival mode," digestion shuts down. Gurgling means the body finally feels safe enough to resume normal metabolic functions.

3. What should you do if a client says, "I don't feel anything in my body"?

Validate it! This is called "numbness" or "dissociation." Say: "That's a very common way the body protects itself. Let's just notice the 'nothingness.' What does that 'nothing' feel like? Is it heavy, or airy?"

4. How does the "Somatic Pivot" differ from traditional life coaching?

Traditional coaching often focuses on mindset and future actions. The Somatic Pivot focuses on the physiological state in the present moment, believing that a regulated body is a prerequisite for a changed mind.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • The Story is the Door, Not the Destination: Use the client's narrative to find the physical entry point, then move deeper.
  • Language Matters: Use invitational language ("I invite you to notice") rather than directive language ("Do this").
  • Safety is the Medicine: A client will only release trauma when their nervous system perceives the environment (and the coach) as safe.
  • Watch the ANS: Look for physical shifts (sighs, gurgles, softening) as indicators of progress.
  • You are the Container: Your own regulation is your most powerful coaching tool.
