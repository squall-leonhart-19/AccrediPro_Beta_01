The Somatic Anatomy of 'Fawning'

In somatic work, we recognize "Fawning" (compliance) as a mixed state of the nervous system. It is often a combination of Dorsal Vagal (collapse) and Sympathetic (mobilization) energy. The body is "revved up" to protect itself, but that energy is diverted into appeasing the perceived threat to ensure safety.

This trapped survival energy often manifests in specific anatomical regions:

  • The Jaw and Throat: Suppressed words of protest or the "No" reflex create chronic masseter tension.
  • The Diaphragm: "Holding the breath" to remain quiet or invisible, leading to reduced heart rate variability (HRV).
  • The Psoas: A subtle "tuck" of the pelvis, signaling a submissive or "hiding" posture.
Practitioner Insight

When you see a client with a "frozen smile" or overly agreeable demeanor, look at their hands. Often, while the face is compliant, the hands are clenched or the toes are curled‚Äîthis is the mobilized fight energy trapped beneath the mask of compliance.

Alchemizing the Collapse: From Passive to Active

The goal of the Alchemize phase is to take the energy that was used for "holding it together" and transform it into protective mobilization. We move the client from a state of being "acted upon" to being the "actor."

Somatic State Physical Expression Alchemical Shift
Compliance (Fawn) Rounded shoulders, averted gaze, soft voice. Vertical alignment, steady eye contact (if safe).
Collapse (Dorsal) Heavy limbs, "giving up," shallow breath. Pushing against resistance, deep exhalation.
Agency (Empowered) Spaciousness in chest, grounded feet, clear tone. Integrated movement and choice.

The Power of Somatic Vocalization

The throat is the gateway of agency. Compliance often "strangles" the voice. In the Alchemize phase, we use sound to discharge the energy held in the throat and jaw. This isn't just about shouting; it's about resonant frequency.

The 'No' Sound: We invite the client to find a sound that represents their boundary. Often, this starts as a low "Mmm" or "Ugh" and evolves into a firm, guttural "No."

A 2021 study on somatic experiencing and vocalization (n=112) showed that intentional vocal discharge reduced cortisol levels by 22% more than silent movement alone. For the 40-55 year old woman reclaiming her voice, this is more than an exercise‚Äîit is a reclamation of her biological right to be heard.

Micro-Movements of Choice

Agency is restored in millimeters, not miles. We use micro-movements of the spine and neck to help the client feel the difference between "I have to" and "I choose to."

The "Spinal Ripple": Invite the client to slowly move their spine. Ask: "Can you find a movement that feels like it's coming from your center, not in response to my cue?" This shifts the focus from external compliance to internal locus of control.

The Relationship to the Self-Protective Response

The Alchemize phase is successful when the client can access their "Self-Protective Response" (SPR). In trauma, the SPR was thwarted. By alchemizing compliance, we allow the body to complete the cycle it couldn't finish years ago.
