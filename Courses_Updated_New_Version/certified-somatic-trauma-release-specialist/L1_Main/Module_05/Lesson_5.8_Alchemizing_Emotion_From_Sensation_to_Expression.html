Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

In my first year of practice, I made almost all of these mistakes. They usually come from a place of wanting to "help" too much. Letâ€™s look at what to avoid:

  • The "Rush to Positive": Trying to make the client feel "happy" before they have fully processed the release. This can feel like spiritual bypassing.
  • Over-Explaining: Using too much "brainy" science during a somatic moment. It pulls the client out of their body and into their head.
  • Ignoring the "Rawness": Failing to acknowledge that release can feel scary or vulnerable. Always validate the "weirdness."
  • Filling the Silence: Silence is where the body does its deep integration. If the client is quiet, let them be quiet for 15-30 seconds.
Olivia's Pro Tip

If you feel the urge to speak because the silence is awkward, take a deep breath yourself. Your calm nervous system is the best tool in the room.

Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

A successful Somatic Alchemy session doesn't always end with "rainbows and butterflies." Look for these subtle, physiological shifts in your client:

  1. Spontaneous Deep Breath: The client takes a "sigh of relief" without being prompted.
  2. Softened Gaze: Their eyes look less "darty" or intense; they look present and "soft."
  3. Postural Shift: They naturally sit taller or lean back into the chair, showing increased "environmental trust."
  4. Cognitive Clarity: They start making new connections, like "Oh, if I'm not carrying that grief, I can actually start that business I wanted."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask Elena where she feels the "amber light" in her body?

It anchors the new positive state into her actual physiology (neuroplasticity), rather than just keeping it as a mental concept or a nice thought.

2. What is the "Golden Void"?

The "Golden Void" is the temporary state of emptiness or "weirdness" a client feels after a major somatic release. It is the fertile ground where new, healthier patterns are integrated.

3. Which "Success Marker" indicates the nervous system is shifting into the Parasympathetic (rest/digest) state?

A spontaneous deep breath or sigh of relief is a primary indicator that the nervous system has shifted from a "high-alert" state to a "settled" state.

4. What is the danger of "The Rush to Positive"?

It can feel like "spiritual bypassing" or invalidation to the client. It prevents the body from fully experiencing the recalibration process and can make the client feel like they have to "perform" being happy.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Embrace the Void: Emptiness after release isn't a problem to be solved; it's a recalibration to be guided.
  • Pacing is Presence: Use slow, grounded pacing to signal to the client's nervous system that they are safe to change.
  • Anchor the Positive: Always move from the "release" to an "embodied anchor" (like Elena's amber light).
  • Validate the "Weird": Use phrases like "That 'weirdness' is a sign of profound change" to build client confidence.
  • Silence is a Tool: Give the client's body time to "speak" before you jump in with words.
