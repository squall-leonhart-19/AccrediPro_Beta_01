The Coaching Script: Integration & Future-Pacing

Read the following script out loud. Pay attention to the pauses. In somatic work, the silence is where the body integrates the new information.

YOU:
"Elena, as you think about this interview next week, I want you to notice where you feel that 'readiness' in your body right now. Not the nerves, but the part of you that knows you are capable."
ELENA:
"I feel it in my chest, I think. It feels... solid. But then my mind starts saying, 'What if you freeze like you did three years ago?'"
YOU:
"That's such a natural protective thought. Let's acknowledge that old 'freeze' part‚Äîit kept you safe back then. But right now, feel that 'solid' sensation in your chest. Can you let that solidity grow just 10% more? (Pause) What does your breath do when you focus there?"
ELENA:
"My breath gets deeper. It feels like I have more room."
YOU:
"Exactly. Now, imagine yourself walking into that interview room. As you sit down, you feel this same 'roomy' breath and 'solid' chest. If a difficult question comes, you feel your feet on the floor. Just imagine that for a moment... how does your body respond to that image?"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

In the "Emerge" phase, it is easy to slip back into traditional life coaching and forget the body. Watch out for these pitfalls:

  • Rushing to "Positive Thinking": If the client expresses fear, don't just tell them to "be positive." Always go back to the body first.
  • Over-Planning: Don't give them a 20-step to-do list. In the Emerge phase, we want embodied action, not cognitive overwhelm.
  • Ignoring the "Vulnerability Hangover": After a big release, clients often feel raw. Ensure you check in on their self-care routines.
  • Taking Credit: The Emerge phase is about the client's agency. Use "You" statements ("You found that strength") rather than "We."

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these somatic cues in your client:

Spontaneous Sighing

A sign that the autonomic nervous system has shifted from "high alert" to "rest and digest."

Postural Shift

The client moves from a collapsed or rigid posture to one that is upright yet fluid.

Future-Oriented Language

The client begins using "When I..." instead of "If I..." or "I can't..."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of "future-pacing" in a somatic context?

To allow the client's nervous system to "rehearse" a future event while maintaining a state of somatic safety and regulation, thereby reducing the likelihood of a future trigger response.

2. If Elena says, "I'm afraid I'll freeze," what is your first somatic move?

Acknowledge the fear, then immediately redirect her attention to a "resource" or a part of the body that feels neutral or strong (like her feet on the floor or the solidity in her chest).

3. Why is the "Emerge" phase considered the most delicate?

Because the client is stepping out of the "therapeutic container" and into the real world. The fear of "losing" their progress can trigger old survival patterns.

4. What is a "Somatic Anchor"?

A physical touch or specific sensation that the client can consciously return to in order to stabilize their nervous system during times of stress.
