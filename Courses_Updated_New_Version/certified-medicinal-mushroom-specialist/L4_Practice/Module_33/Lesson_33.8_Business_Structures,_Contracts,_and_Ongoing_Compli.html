Coach Sarah's Tip

Remember, your prospect is likely feeling vulnerable and frustrated. Approach every call with empathy and genuine curiosity. Your goal isn't to push a sale, but to listen deeply and determine if you are the right person to support their journey. If you're not, that's okay! A good referral builds trust and reputation.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a structured framework for your call with Eleanor. Practice it out loud, adapting it to your natural voice. Remember, it's a guide, not a rigid mandate.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah. How are you doing this afternoon?"
ELEANOR:
"I'm alright, Sarah, thanks. A bit tired, as usual, but hopeful to learn more."
YOU:
"I completely understand. My goal for our chat today is to learn more about what's been going on for you, what you're hoping to achieve, and then explain a bit about how I work. At the end, we can decide if it feels like a good fit to move forward. Does that sound good?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, that sounds perfect."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Wonderful. So Eleanor, you mentioned brain fog and low energy. Can you tell me more about when these started and how they're truly impacting your daily life? What does a typical day look like with these challenges?"

"It's awful. I used to be so sharp at work, but now I forget things, struggle to focus. By 3 PM, I'm completely drained, and then I lie awake at night. My husband asks if I'm okay, and I just feel‚Ä¶dimmed."

"That sounds incredibly frustrating. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, even a little, and what really didn't resonate?"
ELEANOR:
"I've tried cutting out sugar, taking B vitamins, even a short course of melatonin. Some things helped for a day or two, but then it's back to square one. My doctor just offered sleeping pills, and I don't want to go down that road."
YOU:
"I hear you. If nothing changes and you continue on this path for another year, where do you imagine yourself? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel like yourself again, what would your life look and feel like?"
ELEANOR:
"Honestly, I'd probably be burnt out, maybe even depressed. But if I could wave that wand? I'd be waking up refreshed, clear-headed, enjoying my morning coffee, being present with my family, and tackling work projects with my old energy. I just miss feeling vibrant."

Check Your Understanding

Let's test your grasp of the key concepts from this practice lab. Take a moment to answer these questions.

Check Your Understanding

1

During the "Understand the Problem" phase, which question is most effective for uncovering a client's deepest motivations and potential for transformation?

The question, "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like?" is highly effective. It helps the client visualize both the negative consequences of inaction and the positive outcomes of working with you, tapping into their core desires.

2

Eleanor says, "I'm worried about the time commitment." How should you respond to validate her concern while still moving the conversation forward?

A good response would be: "That's a very valid concern, especially with your busy schedule. My program is structured to integrate seamlessly into your life. While it does require dedication, we focus on sustainable, incremental changes. Our sessions are typically 60 minutes, and the daily protocols are designed to be practical. We prioritize efficiency and impact. Many of my clients find that the time invested upfront saves them far more time and energy in the long run, as they regain their vitality. What does your ideal time commitment look like?" This validates, explains the solution, and asks for more information.

3

When presenting your program's investment, what is the best practice immediately after stating the price?

The best practice is to state the price clearly and then pause. Allow the client to process the information. Avoid immediately justifying or over-explaining the cost. Your confidence in your value is conveyed through this pause, inviting the client to ask questions if they have them.

4

What is the primary goal of the "Present Your Approach & Solution" phase of the discovery call?

The primary goal is to clearly and concisely articulate how your unique methodology and program specifically address the client's pain points and help them achieve their desired outcomes. It's about connecting their problem to your solution, highlighting the personalization and difference from what they've tried before, without overwhelming them with clinical details.
