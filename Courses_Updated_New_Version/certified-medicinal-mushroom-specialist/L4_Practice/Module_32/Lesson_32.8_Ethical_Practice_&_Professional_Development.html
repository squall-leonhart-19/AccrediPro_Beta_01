1. Your Prospect Profile: Meet Eleanor

Before any call, it's helpful to visualize who you're speaking with. This allows you to tailor your approach and anticipate their needs and concerns. Let's meet Eleanor:

Eleanor, 52

Found you through a local women's wellness group. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Struggling with menopausal symptoms (hot flashes, sleep disturbances, brain fog) for 3 years. Recently gained 15 lbs, despite diet changes. Feels "invisible" and frustrated with her doctor's suggestion of HRT as the only option.
Budget Concern
"I've spent so much on supplements and doctor visits already that haven't worked. I'm hesitant to invest more unless I truly believe it will be different."
Decision Style
Values evidence-based solutions but is open to natural alternatives. Needs to feel heard and understood. Looks for a structured plan and clear steps.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel like myself again. I miss having energy, sleeping through the night, and feeling confident in my body."

Coach Tip from Sarah

Always do your homework! If a prospect mentions how they found you, quickly check if there's a common connection or interest you can reference. This immediately builds rapport and shows you pay attention. A simple "I'm so glad [mutual friend/group] connected us" can make a big difference.

2. Structuring Your 30-Minute Discovery Call

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all essential points while maintaining a natural, conversational flow. Here's a proven four-phase structure:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure: Eleanor's Scenario

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor, thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today! I saw you found me through the women's wellness group â€“ I love that community. Before we dive in, is this still a good time for our conversation?"
YOU:
"Wonderful. So Eleanor, what specifically made you decide to reach out now? What's been going on for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deep Dive 10 min

(Listen actively, take notes, ask open-ended questions.)

YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned struggling with menopausal symptoms, especially hot flashes and sleep. Can you tell me more about how these affect your daily life and energy levels?"
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly frustrating. What approaches have you tried so far to manage these symptoms, and what were the outcomes?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, if nothing changes, where do you envision yourself in six months or a year? And conversely, if we could wave a magic wand and truly resolve these issues, what would your ideal day or life look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Unique Approach 10 min

(Summarize, validate, then introduce your method.)

YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a busy woman who's been battling significant menopausal symptoms â€“ the hot flashes, disrupted sleep, brain fog, and weight gain â€“ all of which are impacting your confidence and quality of life. You've tried various solutions without lasting success and are now seeking a natural, holistic approach to feel like yourself again. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different. Instead of just managing symptoms, we focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of your hormonal imbalances and systemic inflammation, often using targeted medicinal mushrooms and lifestyle adjustments. My 12-week program is designed to create a personalized roadmap for you, supporting your body's natural ability to rebalance. Many of my clients, especially women in their 40s and 50s experiencing similar challenges, report significant improvements in energy and sleep within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: Transition to Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on what I've described about my approach, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive and personalized support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Excellent! Let me briefly tell you how my signature 'Menopause Rebalance Program' works and how we can get started together."

Coach Tip from Sarah

The "magic wand" question is powerful. It helps prospects articulate their deepest desires and gives you insight into their true motivations. When you later describe your program, you can directly link its benefits back to their "magic wand" vision, showing them how you can help them achieve it.

3. Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're often a sign of interest, not rejection, indicating the prospect needs more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the program itself? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"It's too expensive/I've spent so much money already."
"I hear your concern about the investment, Eleanor, especially given past experiences. And I agree, investing in your health is a significant decision. What I can tell you is that my program is designed to provide lasting, foundational change, not just temporary fixes. We're addressing the root causes, which ultimately saves you money and suffering in the long run. Many clients find the return on investment in their energy, sleep, and overall well-being to be invaluable. How much is feeling like yourself again worth to you?"
"I've tried everything, and nothing has worked."
"I completely empathize with feeling that way, Eleanor. It sounds like you've been on a long and frustrating journey. My approach is unique because it's deeply personalized and focuses on functional testing and a holistic perspective that traditional medicine often misses. We don't just guess; we investigate. This often uncovers underlying imbalances that other methods haven't addressed. I'm confident we can find solutions where others haven't, because we're looking at your unique body blueprint."
"I need to talk to my partner."
"Of course, Eleanor, open communication with your partner is essential. What specific aspects of the program or investment do you anticipate your partner will want to discuss? Perhaps we can address those questions now so you feel fully equipped to have that conversation. Or, if it's helpful, I'm happy to schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you to answer any questions directly."

4. Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

When it comes to pricing, confidence is key. Present your programs and investment clearly, linking them directly to the value and transformation you offer. Avoid hesitation or apologizing for your fees.

Presenting Eleanor's Program: The Menopause Rebalance

When you reach Phase 4, you'll transition to explaining your signature program. For Eleanor, this would be a 12-week program designed for women experiencing menopausal symptoms.

YOU:
"Eleanor, my signature 'Menopause Rebalance Program' is a 12-week journey designed to help you regain your energy, improve your sleep, and find relief from those frustrating hot flashes. It includes a comprehensive initial assessment, personalized protocols utilizing medicinal mushrooms, targeted nutrition, and lifestyle recommendations, plus six bi-weekly coaching sessions to ensure you're supported every step of the way. You'll also have access to my private client portal with resources and direct messaging support."
YOU:
"The investment for this transformative 12-week program is $2,500. I also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly installments of $850."

Notice the clear, concise language. You state the value, then the price, and then offer options. Don't speak further immediately; allow the client to process the information.

Coach Tip from Sarah

After stating your price, pause and be silent. This can feel uncomfortable, but it gives the client space to process the information without you filling the void. The first one to speak often "loses." Let them respond. Your confidence in your value will shine through this silence.

6. Practice Your Call to Action

The call to action (CTA) is the final, clear instruction that guides your prospect to the next step. Be direct and make it easy for them to say "yes."

Effective Closing Lines to Practice:

  • "Eleanor, I'm confident we can achieve significant improvements in your well-being. Are you ready to get started and take the first step towards feeling like yourself again?"
  • "Based on what you've shared, I truly believe my program is exactly what you need to address your menopausal symptoms holistically. How would you like to move forward?"
  • "If you're ready to commit to this transformation, we can get your initial paperwork sent over today and schedule your first in-depth session. Does that sound good?"
  • "The next step is to complete your intake forms and schedule your initial 90-minute consultation where we'll dive deep into your health history. Would you prefer to handle the enrollment now, or should I send you the details via email?"

Coach Tip from Sarah

Don't be afraid to ask for the sale. You're offering a valuable service that can genuinely transform lives. Your confidence in your abilities and your program will empower your clients to say "yes" to their own healing journey. Remember, you're not selling; you're inviting them to a solution.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase in a discovery call?

The primary goal is to actively listen and gather detailed information about the prospect's challenges, their impact on daily life, what they've tried previously, and their desired outcomes. This helps to deeply understand their pain points and motivations.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the program's cost, stating, "It's too expensive." How should you respond effectively?

Acknowledge her concern empathetically ("I hear your concern about the investment..."). Reframe the cost by emphasizing the long-term value and transformation, linking it to saving money and suffering in the long run. Ask a question that helps her reconsider the value, like "How much is feeling like yourself again worth to you?"

3. After confidently stating your program's price, what crucial step should you take, and why?

After stating the price, pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect time to process the information and formulate their response. Breaking the silence too soon can undermine your confidence and invite unnecessary objections.

4. You've just finished presenting your program to a prospect. Provide two strong, clear calls-to-action to guide them to the next step.

  1. "Are you ready to take the next step and begin your journey to better health? We can get you enrolled today."
  2. "The next step is to schedule your initial consultation. When would be a good time for us to book that?"

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a foundational business skill for client acquisition, focusing on connection, understanding, and qualification.
  • A structured call with phases for rapport, problem understanding, solution presentation, and closing ensures efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Empathetic listening and validating a prospect's experience are critical for building trust and uncovering their true needs.
  • Confidently addressing objections involves active listening, reframing, and reiterating value.
  • Presenting your programs and pricing with clarity and conviction, followed by a strategic pause, reinforces your professional value.
  • Clear and direct calls-to-action are essential to guide prospects towards enrollment and beginning their transformative journey with you.
