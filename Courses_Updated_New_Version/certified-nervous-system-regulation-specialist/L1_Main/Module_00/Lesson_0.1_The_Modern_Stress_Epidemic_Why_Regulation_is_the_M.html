Check Your Understanding

1. Why is life experience considered a "clinical advantage" in this field?

Life experience provides the foundation for co-regulation. Wisdom, empathy, and personal resilience allow a practitioner to provide a grounded presence that helps clients feel safe enough to engage their ventral vagal (safe/social) state.

2. What is the primary difference between a "wellness enthusiast" and a "Specialist"?

A Specialist holds a professional credential, understands the underlying neurobiology (the "why"), and has a specific toolkit of evidence-based interventions to produce predictable results.

3. True or False: You need a medical degree to be successful in this program.

False. While we have many nurses and therapists, this program is designed for career changers. We provide all the science and professional training you need to practice safely and effectively.

4. What are the three phases of the transformation journey in this course?

1. The Science of Safety (Neurobiology), 2. The Practitioner's Toolkit (Interventions), and 3. The Professional Launch (Business Building).
