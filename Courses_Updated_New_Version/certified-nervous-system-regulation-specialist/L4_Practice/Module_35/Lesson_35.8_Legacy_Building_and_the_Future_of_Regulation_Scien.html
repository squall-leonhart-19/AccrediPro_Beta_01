Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "Session" mindset and a "Scaling" mindset?

A session mindset sells time (hourly rate), while a scaling mindset sells a transformation (package price). Scaling focuses on the outcome, not the minutes spent on Zoom.

2. Why is "The Frame" (Phase 1) critical in a discovery call with a high-value prospect?

It establishes you as the authority and the "expert" in the room. It sets expectations, reduces sales pressure, and ensures the prospect knows you are evaluating them just as much as they are evaluating you.

3. How should you respond when a prospect says, "I've tried therapy/meditation and it didn't work"?

Validate their experience and explain the physiological difference. "Therapy is top-down (mind to body), but nervous system regulation is bottom-up (body to mind). We are working with the hardware, not just the software."

4. If you want to earn $10,000 per month, how many clients do you need if your 12-week package is $2,500?

You need 4 new enrollments per month. This allows you to maintain a high level of care without the burnout associated with high-volume, low-price models.
Sarah's Tip

Imposter syndrome usually disappears once you see your first $2,500 wire transfer and realize that the client is actually more grateful than the one who paid $50. High-ticket clients are often the most joy-filled to work with!

Key Takeaways

  • Confidence is Currency: High-value prospects invest in your certainty, not just your knowledge.
  • Package the Result: Move away from hourly billing to 12-week transformational containers.
  • Diagnostic Sales: Use your discovery calls to diagnose the physiological "stuckness" and prescribe the regulation solution.
  • Sustainable Math: Scaling requires fewer clients paying higher rates, which prevents practitioner burnout.
