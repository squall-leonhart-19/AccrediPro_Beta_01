Sarah's Pro Tip

Never lower your price on the spot. If they truly have a budget constraint, offer a longer payment plan. Lowering your price immediately signals that you don't believe in the value of the transformation.

4. Presenting Price with Authority

When you state your price, stop talking. This is the "Golden Silence." Many new practitioners feel the urge to keep talking to justify the price, which actually creates anxiety in the prospect.

Practice this out loud:
"The investment for the Nervous System Reset is $1,500. We can get started as early as Tuesday. Would you like me to send over the agreement and the first invoice now?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Discovery" phase (Phase 2) of the call?

To identify the "Gap" between where the client is (pain/dysregulation) and where they want to be, and to help them see the cost of staying the same.

2. Why is it important to stop talking after stating your price?

It demonstrates confidence and gives the prospect the space to process the information without feeling pressured or sensing your own insecurity.

3. How should you view a client's objection about money?

As a nervous system response seeking safety and reassurance of value, rather than a personal rejection or a final "no."

4. What is the "Legal Backbone" mentioned in this lesson?

The confidence that comes from having professional contracts and compliance in place, allowing you to lead the enrollment process with authority.
