Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Mistake Why It Fails The Better Approach
"Fixing" the Email Focuses on the external problem, ignoring the internal trauma trigger. Somatic anchoring first; problem-solving second.
Asking "Why?" "Why do you feel this way?" pulls the client into the cognitive brain and away from the body. Ask "Where?" (Where do you feel that in your body?)
Rushing the Breath Telling a panicked client to "just breathe" can actually increase anxiety. Invite them to feel their feet first, then offer a structured 4-6 breath.
Over-Validating Saying "Oh my god, that client is so mean!" keeps the client in victim mode. Validate the internal experience, not the external drama.
