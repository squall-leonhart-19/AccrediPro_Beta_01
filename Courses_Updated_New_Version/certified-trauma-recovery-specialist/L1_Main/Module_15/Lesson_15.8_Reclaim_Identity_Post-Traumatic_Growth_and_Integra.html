Success Markers: How You Know It Worked

A successful ANCHOR-based session doesn't always end with the client being "cured," but it always ends with the client being more connected. Look for these signs:

  • Spontaneous Sighing: A sign the nervous system is shifting from sympathetic (fight/flight) to parasympathetic (rest/digest).
  • Softening of Facial Features: The jaw relaxes or the brow unfurrows.
  • Shift in Language: Moving from "I am broken" to "My system is protecting me."
  • Increased Agency: The client makes a small choice (e.g., "I'd like to sit differently" or "I want to stop here for a moment").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it critical to validate "neck tension" as a protective mechanism instead of a problem to be solved?

Validating the tension reduces the "threat" response in the brain. When we label a body sensation as a "problem," the brain stays in a state of alarm. Labeling it as "protection" fosters self-compassion and allows the nervous system to feel safe enough to relax.

2. What is the "Fix-It Trap" and how does it impact the coaching relationship?

The Fix-It Trap is when a coach rushes to provide solutions or "healing" before the client feels safe. It can make the client feel unheard, misunderstood, or pressured, which often triggers further resistance or "freezing" in trauma survivors.

3. According to the ANCHOR Frameworkâ„¢, what should you do if a client becomes "frozen" during a session?

Move to the 'N' (Nervous System) phase. Slow down the pace, use co-regulation (breathing with them), and acknowledge the freeze as a valid survival response. Do not push for more information; prioritize grounding.

4. What is a "Success Marker" that indicates a nervous system shift?

A spontaneous sigh, softening of the jaw, deeper breathing, or the client moving from self-judgmental language to self-curious language.
