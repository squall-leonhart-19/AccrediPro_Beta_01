A Personal Message from My Heart to Yours

I am so incredibly happy you're here. My heart is full as I write this, because I know exactly what it feels like to be standing where you are‚Äîon the precipice of a massive life change, feeling a mix of fire-in-your-belly excitement and that nagging, quiet voice of imposter syndrome.

I'm Olivia Reyes, your lead coach. Before we dive into the science of neurobiology and the mechanics of recovery, I want you to know who I am and why I created this program for you.

For twelve years, I lived a life that looked perfect on the outside but was a nightmare behind closed doors. I was married to a covert narcissist. The gaslighting was so insidious that I genuinely thought I was losing my mind. He isolated me from my friends, my family, and eventually, from my own sense of self. When I finally found the courage to leave, I was a shell of a woman. I had C-PTSD, debilitating panic attacks, and a profound inability to trust my own perception of reality.

In the middle of that wreckage, I lost my father‚Äîmy only remaining safe person‚Äîto cancer. The grief didn't just break my heart; it broke my entire system. I was later diagnosed with ADHD, and I realized that what I had called "emotional dysregulation" my whole life was actually a neurodivergent brain trying to survive in a traumatized environment.

I didn't just "get over it." I fought for my recovery. And through that fight, I found my calling. I became a coach because I refused to let other survivors suffer in silence like I did. I wanted to create the professional, evidence-based, yet deeply compassionate path that I couldn't find when I was drowning.

Olivia's Insight

You aren't here by accident. Your "mess" is often the very thing that will make you a magnificent coach. We don't hide our scars here; we use them as a map to guide others out of the woods. I believe in you because I am you.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is life experience considered a "superpower" in this field?

Life experience provides the empathy, resilience, and pattern recognition that are essential for trauma coaching. It allows you to connect with clients on a deeper, more authentic level than clinical training alone.

2. What is a "POD" in the context of this program?

A POD is a small Private Practice Group of fellow students who provide community, peer support, and a safe space to practice coaching skills throughout the certification.

3. According to the income table, what is the estimated monthly revenue for an established CTRS?

An established specialist (2-4 years) can typically earn between $7,000 and $10,000 per month, based on 15-20 client hours per week.

4. What is the first suggested step for getting started?

The first step is to complete your profile and introduce yourself in the community hub, sharing your "why" with your peers and mentors.
