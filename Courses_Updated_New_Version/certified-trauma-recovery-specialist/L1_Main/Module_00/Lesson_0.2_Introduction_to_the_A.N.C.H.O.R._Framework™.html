The "Can I Actually Make Money?" Conversation

Let's address the elephant in the room: Imposter Syndrome. Many women entering this field feel that because their work is "heart-centered," they shouldn't focus on the money. I am here to tell you that financial abundance is the fuel for your impact.

When you are financially secure, you don't show up to sessions stressed about your mortgage. You show up fully present. You can afford continuing education, better tools for your clients, and the self-care that prevents your own burnout. A 2023 industry report found that trauma-informed specialists earn, on average, 34% more than general life coaches because of their specialized, high-demand skill set.

Olivia's Insight

High-value clients aren't looking for the cheapest option; they are looking for the right option. When someone is recovering from trauma, they want a specialist, not a generalist. Your certification is what allows you to command professional rates from day one.

The Math of a $100K Practice

Many people view "six figures" as a mystical number. It isn't. It's just simple math. Let's look at how a Certified Trauma Recovery Specialist‚Ñ¢ reaches $100,000 per year (approx. $8,333/month):

  • Option A (The Purist): 12 private clients paying $700/month (weekly sessions) = $8,400/month.
  • Option B (The Scaler): 5 private clients ($3,500) + 1 Group Program with 10 people at $500/month ($5,000) = $8,500/month.
  • Option C (The Hybrid): 4 private clients ($2,800) + 1 Corporate Retainer ($3,000) + Digital Course Sales ($2,600) = $8,400/month.
Olivia's Insight

Don't try to do all three at once! Start with Option A to master your craft, then move to Option B once you have a waitlist. This is how you build a business that supports your life rather than consuming it.

Realistic 12-Month Timeline

Success doesn't happen overnight, but it happens faster than you think when you have a roadmap. Here is what your first year typically looks like:

Months 1-3: The Foundation
Complete certification, identify your specific niche, and work with 2-3 "beta" clients to build confidence and gather testimonials.

Months 4-6: The Launch
Set your professional rates ($150-$250/hr). Secure your first 5 paying clients. Monthly income: $1,500 - $3,500.

Months 7-12: The Momentum
Referrals start kicking in. You launch your first small group or workshop. Monthly income: $5,000 - $8,000.

Year 2+: The Scale
Raise your rates for new clients. Introduce passive income (courses) or larger group models. Monthly income: $10,000+.

Olivia's Insight

The biggest hurdle is the "Gap of Doubt" in months 4-5. Stick with the process. Most practitioners quit right before the referral momentum starts. Consistency is the secret sauce.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do trauma-informed specialists typically earn more than general life coaches?

Because they possess a high-demand, specialized skill set that addresses complex issues. Clients view them as "specialists" rather than "generalists," which allows for professional, premium pricing.

2. What is the "Option B" scaling model for reaching $100k?

The "Scaler" model involves a mix of 1-on-1 private clients (e.g., 5 clients) and a scalable group program (e.g., 10 people), allowing for higher income without increasing 1-on-1 hours.

3. According to the 12-month timeline, when does "referral momentum" typically begin to take over?

Referral momentum typically kicks in during months 7-12, after you have established a track record with initial paying clients and your network begins to grow.

4. What is the primary benefit of the "Corporate Wellness" path?

It offers consistent, high-value retainers (often $2k-$10k/month) for providing trauma-informed training and leadership support to organizations, providing a stable income base.
