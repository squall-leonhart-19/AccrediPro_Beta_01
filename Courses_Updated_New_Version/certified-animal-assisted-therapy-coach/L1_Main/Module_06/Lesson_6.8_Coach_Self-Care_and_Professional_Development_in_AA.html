Coach Tip

The Foundational AAT Coaching Script

Here's a script for your initial conversation with Eleanor. Practice it out loud, focusing on your tone, pacing, and genuine empathy. Imagine her sitting across from you.

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly with me. It takes incredible courage to articulate such deep feelings of loss and a desire for new purpose, especially after such significant life changes. What you're experiencing is very real, and I want you to know you're not alone."
ELEANOR:
"It's just... I thought retirement would be peaceful, but it feels empty. And everyone says 'get out there,' but I just don't feel like it. Except for Max. He's my rock."
YOU:
"I hear you, Eleanor. That feeling of emptiness after a fulfilling career, coupled with your children moving away, is a significant emotional transition. It's completely natural to feel overwhelmed. And it's wonderful that you have Max. It sounds like he provides a deep sense of comfort and connection for you."
ELEANOR:
"He does. He's the only one who truly listens without judgment."
YOU:
"That connection you share with Max is incredibly powerful, Eleanor. As an Animal-Assisted Therapy Coach, my role is to help you consciously harness that bond, and the inherent wisdom of animals, to help you rediscover joy, build new routines, and find that renewed sense of purpose you're seeking. We'll explore how Max can be an active partner in your well-being journey, beyond just companionship. Does that resonate with what you're hoping for?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes! That's exactly it. I just... I don't know where to start."
YOU:
"That's where we come in. Together, we'll design simple yet profound practices that integrate Max into your personal growth. We might focus on mindful walks, interactive play that fosters presence, or even exploring community engagement opportunities with Max, if that feels right for you. Our first step is to explore your current routines and what brings you even small moments of peace or joy with him. How does that sound as a starting point?"
ELEANOR:
"That sounds... hopeful. I'm ready."
Coach Tip

Notice how the coach introduces AAT coaching not as a rigid therapy, but as a way to "harness that bond" and "partner" with the animal. This collaborative language empowers the client and positions the animal as an active participant, not just a passive presence.

Common Coaching Mistakes to Avoid

As you practice, be mindful of these common pitfalls new coaches often encounter during initial client sessions:

  • Over-explaining AAT: While enthusiasm is great, avoid lengthy academic explanations. Focus on the client's experience and how AAT can help *them*, as demonstrated in the script.
  • Minimizing client's feelings: Phrases like "It's not so bad" or "Look on the bright side" can invalidate their experience. Stick to empathetic validation: "I hear you," "That sounds challenging."
  • Jumping straight to solutions: Resist the urge to fix everything immediately. The initial session is for listening, building rapport, and understanding. Solutions come later, collaboratively.
  • Using jargon: Avoid clinical or overly technical AAT terms. Keep your language accessible and relatable to the client's everyday experience.
  • Lack of clear next steps: Even in an initial session, give the client a sense of what to expect next and a simple, manageable "first step" to consider.

Markers of a Successful Initial Session

How do you know if your first session went well? Look for these key indicators:

  • Client feels heard and understood: They express a sense of relief or validation.
  • Client expresses hope: They feel a glimmer of possibility for their goals.
  • Client is engaged and asks questions: They show curiosity about the AAT coaching process.
  • Clear alignment on goals: You and the client are on the same page about what they want to achieve.
  • Client agrees to next steps: They are willing to schedule a follow-up or try a suggested initial practice.
  • Strong rapport built: The client feels comfortable and trusts you.
Coach Tip

A truly successful coaching session isn't just about what you say, but how you make the client feel. Aim to leave them feeling respected, empowered, and genuinely excited about the path forward with you and their animal companion.

Key Takeaways
  • Empathetic validation is crucial for building immediate rapport and trust with new clients.
  • Introduce AAT coaching by focusing on its unique benefits and how it aligns with the client's specific goals.
  • Practice active listening and collaborative language to empower clients and position their animal companions as active partners.
  • Avoid common pitfalls like over-explaining, minimizing feelings, and premature solution-giving.
  • A successful initial session leaves the client feeling heard, hopeful, and engaged for future sessions.
