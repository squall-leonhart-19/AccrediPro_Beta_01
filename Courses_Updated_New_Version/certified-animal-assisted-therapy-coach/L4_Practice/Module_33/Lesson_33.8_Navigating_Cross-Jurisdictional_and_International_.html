1. Setting the Stage: Your Prospect Profile

Before any discovery call, it's beneficial to visualize your prospect. Understanding their potential mindset, challenges, and aspirations allows you to tailor your approach and empathy. Meet Emily, a potential client who's reached out to explore Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching.

Emily, 52

Referral from a local wellness group. Seeking support for stress and emotional well-being.

Her Situation
High-stress corporate job, feeling overwhelmed and disconnected. Her beloved dog, Max, is her main source of comfort.
Primary Concern
"I'm worried about committing to another program that won't make a lasting difference. I'm so busy already."
Decision Style
Values practical, actionable steps and a compassionate approach. Needs to feel a genuine connection and trust.
Her Goal
"I want to find a way to manage my stress better, feel more present, and deepen my bond with Max."
Coach Tip

Always do a quick scan of any publicly available information (LinkedIn, shared groups) before a call. It's not about being intrusive, but about finding common ground or understanding their professional background to build rapport more effectively. A simple acknowledgment of their field can make a significant difference.

2. The Discovery Call Blueprint

A well-structured discovery call isn't about selling; it's about listening, understanding, and determining if there's a mutual fit. This 30-minute framework will guide you through connecting with Emily, uncovering her needs, and gently introducing the transformative potential of AAT Coaching.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Emily

Phase 1: Building Rapport & Setting the Tone 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Emily! It's wonderful to finally connect. Thank you for making the time today. I understand you were referred by [wellness group/mutual acquaintance]? How are you doing today?"
YOU:
"That's fantastic. So, Emily, I'm genuinely curious ‚Äì what prompted you to explore Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching at this particular moment? What's on your mind?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Their World 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed and disconnected. Can you tell me more about how that manifests in your daily life? How does it impact your energy, your relationships, or even your time with Max?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried previously to manage this stress or to feel more connected? What were the outcomes, both positive and negative?"
YOU:
"Let's imagine a year from now, if nothing changes. What does that picture look like? Now, flip that ‚Äì if we could wave a magic wand and you achieved everything you desired, how would your life be different? How would you feel?"
Phase 3: Positioning Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Emily, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is that you're an incredibly dedicated professional, feeling the weight of daily stressors, and longing for a deeper sense of peace and connection, especially with Max. Does that resonate with what you're experiencing?"
YOU:
"The unique power of Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching, particularly with your own companion animal like Max, is that we don't just talk about stress. We actively engage in practices that leverage the innate human-animal bond to regulate your nervous system, enhance mindfulness, and foster emotional resilience. We'd work together over 12 weeks to build sustainable strategies, utilizing Max's calming presence and your existing bond as powerful tools for transformation. Many of my clients report feeling significantly calmer and more present within the first 3-4 weeks."
Phase 4: The Transition to the Offer 5 min
YOU:
"Based on what we've discussed, Emily, does this approach sound like the supportive, transformative path you've been seeking? ... Wonderful. If you feel this could be the right fit for you and Max, I'd love to share more about how my Bond & Thrive coaching program works and how we can get started."
Coach Tip

When summarizing their pain points, use their own language where possible. This demonstrates active listening and makes them feel truly heard. Your role isn't to diagnose, but to reflect their experience and offer a solution aligned with their stated desires.

3. Navigating Objections Like a Pro

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. As a confident coach, you'll learn to anticipate and address common concerns with empathy and clarity, transforming hesitation into commitment.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand, Emily. This is a significant decision. To help me understand better, what specifically do you need more time to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps whether this approach truly feels like the right fit for you and Max? Knowing this helps me ensure I've addressed all your questions."

(Listen carefully to their response. This opens the door to address their core concern directly.)

"It's too expensive / I've already spent so much money."

"I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. Many of my clients, like yourself, have invested in various solutions before without seeing lasting results. What makes the Bond & Thrive program different is its personalized, holistic approach that leverages the science of the human-animal bond for deep, sustainable change. Consider the cost of not addressing this stress ‚Äì the impact on your well-being, your productivity, and even your relationship with Max. This is an investment in your long-term peace and connection, designed to give you tools that last a lifetime."

"I'm too busy, I don't have the time."

"That's a very common concern, especially with your demanding schedule. In fact, many clients come to me precisely because they feel overwhelmed and lack time. Our program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, with focused sessions and practical exercises that you can do with Max in short bursts. The goal isn't to add more to your plate, but to teach you how to reclaim your time and energy by reducing stress and increasing your presence, ultimately making you more efficient and joyful in everything you do."

"I've tried therapy/coaching before, and it didn't work."

"Thank you for sharing that, Emily. It's important to acknowledge past experiences. The key differentiator with Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching is the active, integral role your animal companion plays. We move beyond traditional talk-based methods to engage in experiential practices with Max that directly impact your emotional regulation and connection. This approach often unlocks breakthroughs where conventional methods may have fallen short, because it taps into a primal, non-judgmental bond that is uniquely healing. It's a truly different pathway to well-being."

Coach Tip

When handling objections, lean into empathy. Phrases like "I hear you," "I understand that," or "That's a very common concern" validate their feelings before you offer your solution. This builds trust and lowers their defenses, making them more receptive.

4. Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

Once you've addressed objections and confirmed a mutual fit, it's time to present your program and its investment. This is not just about numbers; it's about reiterating the immense value and transformation your coaching provides.

Presenting Your Bond & Thrive Coaching Program

Your program should be structured to deliver significant, lasting results. A typical AAT coaching program runs for 12 weeks, allowing enough time for deep work and habit formation.

The Bond & Thrive 12-Week Signature Program

This comprehensive program is designed for individuals seeking profound transformation in stress management, emotional well-being, and deepening their bond with their animal companion. It includes:

  • Six bi-weekly 60-minute one-on-one virtual coaching sessions.
  • Personalized AAT exercises and guided meditations to practice with your animal.
  • Unlimited email support between sessions.
  • Access to exclusive client resources and a private community forum.
  • A personalized "Bond & Thrive" action plan after each session.

$2,997 (or 3 monthly payments of $1,050)

"Emily, this program is an investment in your well-being, your peace of mind, and the incredible connection you share with Max. It's about creating a life where you feel centered, joyful, and truly present."

Key Principles for Pricing Presentation:

  • State the Price with Confidence: Deliver the price clearly and without hesitation. Your confidence in your value translates to their confidence in your services.
  • Connect to Value: Always link the investment back to the transformation they desire. Remind them of their "magic wand" vision.
  • Offer Payment Options: Breaking down the total into monthly installments can make it more accessible, but always present the full payment option first.
  • Silence is Golden: After stating the price, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let them process the information.
Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural. If you stumble or sound uncertain, it signals a lack of confidence in your own value. Your voice inflection and posture during this moment are just as important as the words themselves.

5. Sealing the Deal: Your Call to Action

The call to action is where you guide your prospect to the next step. It should be clear, concise, and easy to follow. Remember, you're not pushing them; you're inviting them to take a transformative journey.

Practice Your Closing Lines

After presenting your program and addressing any final questions, use one of these clear calls to action:

  • "Emily, if you're ready to embark on this journey to greater peace and connection with Max, the next step is to finalize your enrollment. We can do that right now, or I can send you a secure link to complete your registration."
  • "Based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the Bond & Thrive program is exactly what you need to achieve [reiterate their main goal]. Are you ready to take that first step with me?"
  • "How does that sound, Emily? Are you excited to get started and bring more calm and joy into your life with Max?"
  • "If you're feeling a 'yes' in your heart, let's get you enrolled today so we can begin your transformative journey. What questions do you have about moving forward?"

Remember: Be present, listen for their response, and be ready to answer any lingering questions or guide them through the enrollment process.

Coach Tip

A strong closing isn't about being aggressive; it's about clarity and conviction. Your prospect is looking to you for guidance. Provide a clear path forward, and don't be afraid to ask for the commitment.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 1 in the discovery call blueprint?

The primary purpose of Phase 1 is to build rapport and set a comfortable, open tone for the conversation. It helps the prospect feel at ease and willing to share.

2. How should you respond when a prospect says, "It's too expensive"?

Acknowledge their concern with empathy ("I appreciate your honesty about the investment"). Then, pivot to reiterating the unique value and long-term transformation your program offers, contrasting it with the cost of inaction or previous ineffective solutions.

3. Why is it important to use a clear and direct call to action at the end of the call?

A clear and direct call to action removes ambiguity and guides the prospect on the next steps. It demonstrates your confidence and leadership, inviting them to commit to their transformation rather than leaving them to wonder what to do next.
