Crafting Your Discovery Call Structure

A successful discovery call isn't about selling; it's about serving. It's a structured conversation designed to understand your prospect's needs, determine if you can genuinely help them, and then, if there's a good fit, invite them to work with you. Here‚Äôs a proven 30-minute structure:

Phase 1: Build Rapport (5 minutes)

The goal here is to create a safe, comfortable space. Remember, people buy from those they know, like, and trust. Start with genuine curiosity.

  • YOU: "Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into your vision, I'd love to hear a little about you. What brought you to explore Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching?"
  • ELEANOR: (Shares about her teaching career, Sunny, and desire for new purpose.)
  • YOU: "That's a beautiful story, Eleanor. It sounds like you have a deep connection with animals and a natural gift for nurturing others. What specifically made you decide to reach out to AccrediPro now for further guidance on building your practice?"

Phase 2: Uncover Vision & Challenges (10 minutes)

This is the most critical phase. Ask open-ended questions to understand her deepest desires and frustrations. Listen actively, reflecting her feelings and words.

  • YOU: "Tell me more about your vision for an AAT coaching practice. What does your ideal day look like? Who are you serving? What kind of impact do you dream of making?"
  • ELEANOR: (Describes her dream of working with Sunny, helping people, but then pivots to her fears.) "It all sounds wonderful, but honestly, I just don't know where to start. The business side feels like a mountain. I've never had to market myself or set prices."
  • YOU: "I hear you, Eleanor. It sounds like you have this incredible passion and a clear vision for helping others, but the practicalities of building the business feel daunting, almost paralyzing. Is that right?"
  • ELEANOR: "Exactly! And I worry about charging for something I'm just starting. What if I'm not good enough?"
  • YOU: "That's a very common feeling, Eleanor, especially for those of us transitioning into entrepreneurship. If nothing changes, and you don't get the support you need, where do you see yourself and your AAT coaching dream in a year from now?"
  • ELEANOR: "Probably still retired, still dreaming, maybe volunteering, but not building my own legacy. That thought makes me sad."
Coach Tip

During Phase 2, practice empathetic listening. Don't interrupt. Let silence hang. Your goal isn't just to hear her words, but to understand her underlying emotions and motivations. Reflecting her feelings back to her ("It sounds like you feel...") builds immense trust.

Phase 3: Position Your Solution (10 minutes)

Once you fully understand her problem, it's time to briefly introduce how your program can bridge the gap between her current reality and her desired future. Focus on transformation, not features.

  • YOU: "Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is that you have a powerful calling to AAT coaching, a wonderful partner in Sunny, but you're struggling with the business blueprint ‚Äì things like client acquisition, pricing, and overcoming that very real imposter syndrome. Does that resonate with you?"
  • ELEANOR: "Yes, absolutely. You've hit the nail on the head."
  • YOU: "Great. Well, this is exactly what our Certified Animal-Assisted Therapy Coach‚Ñ¢ L4 Certification track is designed for. It's not just about advanced AAT skills; it's a comprehensive business accelerator. We provide the step-by-step framework to build your practice, from clarifying your niche to mastering enrollment calls, and even overcoming those confidence hurdles. Many coaches, like Maria, a former nurse who graduated last year, found her stride and enrolled her first 3 clients within a month of completing this program."
  • YOU: "We focus on practical strategies, personalized mentorship, and building a supportive community so you never feel alone. My goal is to equip you with the clarity, confidence, and proven systems to launch a successful, sustainable practice."
Coach Tip

When positioning your solution, use social proof. Mentioning a successful past client (like Maria) who shares a similar background or challenge as your prospect can be incredibly powerful for building credibility and belief.

Confidently Presenting Your Investment

This is often where new coaches stumble. Remember, you are offering immense value ‚Äì a transformation that will change your client's life and livelihood. Your fees reflect that value.

Value-Based Pricing

AccrediPro advocates for value-based pricing, focusing on the outcomes and transformation you provide, rather than an hourly rate. This positions you as an expert, not a commodity.

When discussing pricing, be confident, clear, and concise. State your price and then be silent. Let the prospect process it.

Example Script:

YOU: "Based on everything you've shared, Eleanor, I believe our L4 Certification track is the perfect fit to help you launch your dream practice with confidence and clarity. The program is a 6-month immersive experience, including personalized coaching, advanced business modules, and ongoing community support. The investment for this program is $X,XXX. This can be paid in full for a discount, or through a flexible payment plan of $Y/month over 6 months."

(PAUSE. SILENCE.)

YOU: "Does that sound like something that aligns with your goals and what you're looking for?"

Coach Tip

When you state your price, your voice should remain steady and confident. Avoid hedging or apologizing. After stating the price, embrace the silence. The first person to speak often loses. Let the prospect absorb the information and respond.

Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or a deeper understanding. Approach them with empathy and a problem-solving mindset.

Common Objections & Confident Responses:

Objection: "It's too expensive."

Your Response: "I completely understand that this is a significant investment, Eleanor, and it's wise to consider it carefully. Many of our most successful coaches felt the same way initially. What I'd invite you to consider is the cost of not investing in your dream. What is the value to you of finally launching your practice, having financial independence, and making the impact you envision with Sunny? This program is designed to pay for itself many times over by giving you the tools to consistently attract clients and generate income."

Objection: "I need to think about it."

Your Response: "I completely respect that, Eleanor. This is an important decision. To help me understand better, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps something else that's giving you pause? My goal is to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for you."

Objection: "I'm not sure I have the time."

Your Response: "I appreciate your honesty, Eleanor. Time is a precious commodity, especially when starting something new. Many of our coaches are busy professionals, often juggling family or other commitments. The beauty of this program is its flexibility; it's designed to fit into your life, not take it over. We break down the steps into manageable actions, and the support system is there to keep you accountable without overwhelming you. How much time per week do you realistically feel you could dedicate to building this dream?"

Objection: "What if it doesn't work for me?"

Your Response: "That's a very fair question, Eleanor, and one I often hear from ambitious individuals who are ready for change but cautious. My commitment is to provide you with a proven framework, personalized mentorship, and all the tools you need. Your success, however, also depends on your commitment to implement what you learn. What would 'not working' look like to you? And what would it feel like to finally overcome these hurdles and have the thriving practice you envision?"

Coach Tip

When handling objections, always seek to understand the root cause behind the objection. Is it truly about money, or is it fear of failure, lack of belief in themselves, or a past negative experience? Address the underlying emotion, not just the surface-level statement.

Practice Your Call to Action (CTA)

The discovery call concludes with a clear call to action. Assuming there's a good fit and the prospect is interested, guide them on the next steps. This removes ambiguity and empowers them to move forward.

Example Closing Lines:

  1. "Eleanor, based on our conversation, I truly believe the L4 Certification track is exactly what you need to build the confident, impactful AAT coaching practice you're dreaming of. Are you ready to take that next step and enroll today?"
  2. "Given your passion and your specific challenges with the business side, I'm confident our program will provide the roadmap you're looking for. What feels like the best next step for you ‚Äì reviewing the program details I can send, or are you ready to discuss enrollment?"
  3. "My mission is to help dedicated coaches like you turn their passion into a profession. If you're ready to commit to building your dream, I'd love to welcome you into our community. How would you like to proceed?"
  4. "It sounds like you're excited about the possibilities, Eleanor, and I am too! The next step would be to finalize your enrollment and get you started with your first module. I can walk you through that process now if you'd like."

Practice these lines out loud. The more comfortable you become with inviting people into your programs, the more natural and confident you'll sound, and the more clients you'll enroll.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Uncover Vision & Challenges" phase in a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to deeply understand the prospect's current struggles, desired future, and the emotional gap between them. It's about listening to their story, identifying pain points, and uncovering their core motivations and aspirations.

2. When Eleanor says, "It's too expensive," what is the most effective approach to respond, according to Sarah's coaching?

The most effective approach is to acknowledge her concern with empathy, then pivot to the "cost of not investing" or the value of the transformation. It's about reframing the investment in terms of long-term gain and the impact on her goals, rather than just the dollar amount.

3. Why is it important to "embrace the silence" after stating your program's investment?

Embracing the silence allows the prospect time to process the information without feeling pressured. It demonstrates confidence in your value and gives them space to formulate their thoughts or questions, rather than you filling the void with justifications or further explanations.

4. According to the income potential table, what is a realistic annual gross income for a Certified AAT Coach with a growing practice enrolling 5 clients per month?

For a growing practice enrolling 5 clients per month, the realistic annual gross income ranges from $90,000 to $180,000, assuming an average program price of $1,500 - $3,000 for a 3-month package.
