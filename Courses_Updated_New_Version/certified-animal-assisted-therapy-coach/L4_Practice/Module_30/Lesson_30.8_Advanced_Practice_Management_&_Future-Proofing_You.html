1. The Foundation: Your Discovery Call Blueprint

The discovery call is your chance to shine, to demonstrate your expertise, and to build trust. It's a structured conversation, but it must feel organic and client-centered. Remember, this is about them, not about you. Your role is to listen more than you speak, to ask powerful questions, and to reflect their needs back to them.

Practice Scenario: Your Prospect

Eleanor, 52

Former corporate manager, recently retired, feeling lost and experiencing significant anxiety and social isolation. Loves animals but has no prior experience with AAT. Referred by a friend who saw your social media.

  • Presenting Challenge: "I feel completely adrift since retiring. My days lack purpose, and I'm struggling with anxiety, especially about social interactions. I just feel so disconnected."
  • Desired Outcome: "I want to feel engaged again, find purpose, and reduce this constant feeling of unease. My friend mentioned how much joy her dog brings her, and I've always loved animals. Could this help me find my way back?"
  • Budget Concern: "I'm on a fixed income now, so I need to be careful with my investments. How does this work financially?"
  • Decision Style: Analytical, needs clear structure, evidence-based approach, and reassurance that this isn't just "fluffy animal time."

Now, let's walk through the script with Eleanor in mind. Read these lines aloud, picturing her on the other end of the call.

Phase 1: Building Authentic Rapport (5 minutes)

This phase is about creating a safe, open space. Start with genuine warmth.

  • YOU: "Hi Eleanor, thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah, it's lovely to connect! How are you doing this afternoon?"
  • ELEANOR: (responds)
  • YOU: "That's wonderful. Before we dive into what brought you here, could you share a little about how you heard about Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching?"
  • ELEANOR: (explains the referral from a friend)
  • YOU: "It's so kind of [Friend's Name] to share my work! I truly appreciate that. So Eleanor, tell me, what prompted you to reach out now? What's been on your mind lately?"
Coach Tip

Remember to use the client's name frequently. It personalizes the conversation and demonstrates you're fully present. A genuine smile, even on a phone call, translates through your voice.

Phase 2: Deep Dive into Client Needs (10 minutes)

This is where you listen. Ask open-ended questions and let Eleanor elaborate. Your goal is to understand her pain points, her aspirations, and the gap between where she is and where she wants to be.

  • YOU: "You mentioned feeling adrift and struggling with anxiety. Can you tell me more about that? When did these feelings start, and how are they impacting your daily life, your energy, your relationships?"
  • ELEANOR: (shares details about retirement, lack of structure, social withdrawal)
  • YOU: "Thank you for sharing that, Eleanor, it sounds incredibly challenging. And what have you tried so far to address these feelings of anxiety or to find new purpose? What worked, and what didn't feel quite right?"
  • ELEANOR: (discusses conventional therapy, hobbies that didn't stick)
  • YOU: (Reflect back) "So, if I'm hearing you correctly, you've been grappling with a sense of loss of purpose and increasing anxiety since retirement, and traditional approaches haven't quite resonated. Is that right?"
  • ELEANOR: "Yes, exactly."
  • YOU: "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in six months or a year? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel vibrant and purposeful again, what would your life look like? What would be different?"
Coach Tip

The "magic wand" question is incredibly powerful. It helps clients articulate their deepest desires and creates a vision that you can then connect to your program's outcomes.

Phase 3: Articulating Your Unique Value (10 minutes)

Now that you deeply understand Eleanor's needs, it's time to connect them to your unique solution. This is not a sales pitch; it's an explanation of how your AAT Coaching program can bridge her gap.

  • YOU: "Eleanor, based on everything you've so openly shared, here‚Äôs what I'm hearing: a deep desire to rediscover purpose, manage anxiety, and reconnect with life in a meaningful way, perhaps with the unique companionship of animals. Does that feel accurate?"
  • ELEANOR: "Yes, that's exactly it. It's like you've read my mind."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful. The approach I use in Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching is quite different from what you've described trying before. Instead of just talking about feelings, we actively engage in a structured process, often alongside trained animals, to help you rebuild confidence, reduce anxiety, and find joy and purpose. We focus on building new routines, developing coping strategies, and fostering connection. For someone like you, who values structure and tangible results, we'd work through the BOND Framework‚Ñ¢, which provides a clear path to achieve your goals."
  • YOU: "For instance, clients often find that the presence of an animal during our sessions helps to lower stress, facilitate open communication, and even encourages playful engagement that naturally reduces anxiety. We would work together over a typical 12-week program, with weekly or bi-weekly sessions, to tailor a plan specifically to your goals. Most of my clients, especially those seeking renewed purpose and anxiety relief, start experiencing significant shifts within the first 4-6 weeks."

Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

This is where many coaches falter. Remember, you are offering immense value. State your pricing clearly and confidently, without apology.

  • YOU: "So Eleanor, does this unique, structured, and animal-inclusive approach sound like what you've been looking for to help you move forward?"
  • ELEANOR: "It does sound intriguing, yes. How does it work, financially?"
  • YOU: "Great question. My signature 12-week Purpose & Connection Program is an investment of $4,500. This includes twelve 60-minute coaching sessions, personalized resources and exercises, direct access to me between sessions, and the unique opportunity to engage with our certified therapy animals. I also offer a flexible payment plan of three monthly installments of $1,550 to make it more accessible. My goal is to ensure this transformative journey is available to those truly committed to their well-being."
Coach Tip

Always state the full price first, then offer payment plans. This anchors the perceived value. Practice saying your price out loud until it feels completely natural and confident.

2. Mastering Objection Handling with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Your goal is to listen, empathize, and reframe. Here are common objections and how to respond.

Common Objection Empathetic Response Strategy
"It's too expensive. I'm on a fixed income." Acknowledge their concern, reiterate the long-term value and ROI (return on investment) in terms of well-being, purpose, and reduced anxiety. Discuss payment plans. "I completely understand that the investment is a significant consideration, especially on a fixed income. This program is designed as an investment in your long-term well-being and finding renewed purpose, which truly has an invaluable return. Many clients find the payment plan helpful, or we can discuss a shorter, foundational package if that's a better fit for now."
"I need to think about it." Gently uncover the real hesitation. "I completely understand. This is an important decision. Can I ask, Eleanor, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that gives you pause? I want to make sure all your questions are answered."
"I've tried everything, and nothing seems to work." Validate their past experiences, then highlight the unique differentiator of AAT Coaching. "I truly hear your frustration, Eleanor. It sounds like you've invested a lot of effort without seeing the lasting changes you desire. What makes our Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching different is the dynamic, experiential element of working with animals, coupled with a structured framework. This often creates breakthroughs where traditional methods haven't, by tapping into different emotional and physiological pathways. It‚Äôs not just another talk therapy; it‚Äôs an active engagement in your healing."

Check Your Understanding

1. During the "Deep Dive into Client Needs" phase, what is the primary goal of asking the "magic wand" question?

The primary goal of the "magic wand" question is to help the client articulate their deepest desires and envision their ideal future, free from their current challenges. This creates a powerful vision that the coach can then connect to the outcomes of their program, making the solution more tangible and desirable.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the financial investment. How should you confidently present your pricing to address this?

Confidently state the full price of your program first to anchor its value. Then, immediately follow up with details about any flexible payment plans you offer. Emphasize that it's an investment in their long-term well-being and a transformative journey, rather than just a cost.

3. A potential client says, "I've tried everything, and nothing seems to work." What is an effective strategy for responding to this objection?

Validate their frustration and past efforts. Then, pivot to highlight the unique, experiential, and structured nature of Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching, emphasizing how it differs from traditional methods they may have tried. Focus on how the animal interaction and BOND Framework‚Ñ¢ can create breakthroughs they haven't experienced before.

4. If your signature 12-week AAT Coaching program is priced at $4,500, what is the average monthly gross income if you consistently enroll 5 new clients per month (assuming a 3-month program cycle)?

If 5 new clients enroll per month for a 3-month program at $4,500 each, this means each client represents $1,500 per month ($4,500 / 3 months). Therefore, 5 clients would generate an average monthly gross income of 5 clients * $1,500/client = $7,500.
Key Takeaways
  • The Discovery Call is a structured, client-centered conversation focused on deep listening and empathetic understanding.
  • Building authentic rapport and clearly understanding a client's needs are paramount before presenting your solution.
  • Confidently articulate the unique value of your Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching, connecting it directly to the client's desired outcomes.
  • Objections are opportunities for clarification and reassurance; respond with empathy, validate concerns, and reframe value.
  • Consistently enrolling even a few clients each month can build a significant and sustainable income stream for your practice.
