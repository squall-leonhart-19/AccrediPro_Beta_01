1. The Art of the Discovery Call: Beyond the Sales Pitch

As Animal-Assisted Therapy coaches, our work is deeply personal and transformative. A discovery call isn't a high-pressure sales tactic; it's a mutual exploration. It's your opportunity to understand a potential client's world, their challenges, and their aspirations, and for them to understand how your unique AAT coaching approach can guide them toward profound change.

Think of it as the first step in building a therapeutic alliance, even before a contract is signed. Your goal is to determine if there's a good fit ‚Äì for both of you. A well-executed discovery call can lead to a client feeling heard, understood, and genuinely excited about the possibility of working with you and your animal partner.

Coach Tip: Your Authenticity is Your Superpower

Remember, your unique story and genuine passion for Animal-Assisted Therapy coaching are your biggest assets. Don't try to be someone you're not. Clients are drawn to authenticity. Let your true self shine through, and focus on truly listening rather than just waiting for your turn to speak. This builds trust, which is the foundation of every successful coaching relationship.

2. Prospect Profile: Meet Sarah

Sarah, 52

Referred by a colleague. Scheduled a 30-minute discovery call seeking support for her daughter, Emily.

Her Situation
Sarah is a busy, divorced mother of two. Her 15-year-old daughter, Emily, struggles with anxiety and social withdrawal, especially after the family dog, a beloved Golden Retriever, passed away six months ago. Emily has seen a therapist, but Sarah feels it hasn't quite "clicked."
Budget Concern
"I'm willing to invest in Emily's well-being, but we've already spent a lot on therapy that didn't seem to help much. How is this different, and what's the real cost?"
Decision Style
Analytical, needs clear information and a tangible plan. Values evidence-based approaches but is open to new ideas if they are well-explained and feel practical. Wants to feel a strong sense of hope and confidence in the solution.
Her Goal
"I just want Emily to find her joy again, to feel less anxious, and maybe even start connecting with friends again. And I miss seeing her light up around animals."

As you prepare for this call, remember Sarah's emotional investment in her daughter's happiness, and her past experiences with other interventions. Your role is to offer a compassionate, clear, and compelling path forward.

3. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Blueprint

A structured approach ensures you cover all essential points while maintaining a natural, conversational flow. This blueprint will guide you through the key phases, allowing you to confidently lead the conversation.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, it's wonderful to connect with you today. Thank you for taking the time. I'm , an Animal-Assisted Therapy Coach, and I'm really looking forward to learning more about how I might support Emily. How did you hear about my work?"
SARAH:
"My colleague, Jessica, mentioned you. She said you did wonders for her son's confidence."
YOU:
"That's so kind of Jessica! I love hearing that. So Sarah, for our time together today, my goal is to really understand what's going on with Emily, what you've tried, and what your hopes are. Then, if it feels like a good fit, I can share a bit about how Animal-Assisted Therapy coaching works and how I might be able to help. Does that sound good?"
SARAH:
"Yes, that sounds perfect."
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Wonderful. So, Sarah, tell me in your own words, what made you decide to reach out now? What's been happening with Emily?"
SARAH:
"It's mainly her anxiety and how withdrawn she's become. After our dog passed, it got much worse. She barely leaves her room, and her grades are slipping. She used to be so outgoing."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly tough for both Emily and for you as her mom. You mentioned her anxiety and withdrawal have worsened since your family dog passed. Can you tell me more about how that loss impacted her, and how her anxiety manifests now?"
SARAH:
"She gets panic attacks, avoids school events, and just seems to have lost her sparkle. She misses having a companion, I think. She's tried traditional therapy, but it felt too clinical for her."
YOU:
"I hear you. It sounds like Emily is really struggling with a profound sense of loss and the resulting anxiety, and the previous therapy wasn't quite the right fit for her unique needs. If you could wave a magic wand and fast-forward six months, what would you hope to see for Emily? What would her life look like?"
SARAH:
"I'd want her to be laughing again, enjoying school, maybe even talking about getting another pet. Just feeling like herself, vibrant and connected."
Coach Tip: Listen to the "Why"

Beyond the surface-level problem, dig into the emotional 'why.' Sarah isn't just seeking to reduce anxiety; she wants Emily to "find her joy again" and "feel vibrant and connected." These are the deep desires that drive action. Reflect these back to her to show you truly understand her core motivation.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that so openly, Sarah. I hear how much you want Emily to thrive again. Based on what you've shared ‚Äì Emily's anxiety, her social withdrawal, the impact of losing her beloved dog, and her previous experience with therapy feeling too clinical ‚Äì it sounds like she's longing for connection and a different kind of support. Does that feel accurate?"
SARAH:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"In Animal-Assisted Therapy coaching, we approach things uniquely. Instead of just talking about feelings, we engage with a specially trained animal partner ‚Äì in my case, my gentle Labrador, Luna ‚Äì to create a safe, non-judgmental space. This often helps teens like Emily open up more easily, reduce stress, and rebuild confidence. We'd focus on building coping strategies for her anxiety, re-engaging her social skills, and helping her process grief, all through interactive, fun, and empowering activities with Luna."
YOU:
"Our program is typically 12 weeks, with weekly 60-minute sessions. We start by co-creating a personalized plan with Emily's input, focusing on her goals. Many of my clients, especially teens, report feeling a renewed sense of purpose and connection within the first few weeks, and we often see significant shifts in their confidence and anxiety levels by the end of the program. It's about empowering her to rediscover her inner strength with the unique support of a canine companion."
Phase 4: The Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what I've shared, does this sound like the kind of supportive and engaging approach you've been looking for to help Emily?"
SARAH:
"It sounds really promising, actually. Emily loves dogs, and I can see how that could make a difference."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. I truly believe this approach can offer Emily the unique pathway she needs. If you're ready to explore this further, I'd love to share the specifics of our program and how we can get started. Would you like me to walk you through the investment and next steps?"

4. Navigating Objections with Confidence

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and a problem-solving mindset. Here's how you might respond to Sarah's potential concerns:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"This sounds interesting, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, especially when it concerns Emily's well-being. To help you think it through, could I ask what specifically you need to consider? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have more questions about how Emily might engage with Luna? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"We've already spent so much on therapy that didn't work. How is this really different, and what's the guarantee?"
"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. It's frustrating to invest time and money without seeing the desired results. The key difference with AAT coaching is the active, experiential engagement with the animal partner. It moves beyond traditional talk therapy by naturally reducing defensiveness, fostering connection, and providing immediate, non-judgmental feedback. While I can't offer a 'guarantee' of specific outcomes, I can share that my clients typically experience significant improvements in areas like emotional regulation, social confidence, and overall well-being. We work together to set clear, measurable goals, and I provide continuous support and adjustments to ensure Emily is progressing effectively. My success rate with teens experiencing anxiety and social withdrawal is over 85% in seeing measurable improvements within a 12-week program, based on client feedback and assessment tools."
"It sounds expensive. What's the investment?"
"I completely understand that financial considerations are important. Before we dive into the numbers, Sarah, let's talk about the value. What would it mean for you and Emily if she could truly overcome her anxiety, find her joy again, and confidently re-engage with life? What's the cost of staying where she is now, in terms of her happiness, her development, and your peace of mind? When you're ready, I'd be happy to outline the different program options and their investments, as well as any payment plans we offer."
Coach Tip: Embrace "No" as "Not Yet"

Not every discovery call will result in a client. And that's perfectly okay. A 'no' often means 'not right now' or 'I need more information.' Stay gracious, offer to keep in touch, and remember that every call is an opportunity to refine your skills and connect with someone who might be ready in the future.

5. Presenting Your Value & Pricing with Poise

This is where many coaches feel uncomfortable, but it doesn't have to be. When you truly believe in the transformative power of your AAT coaching, presenting your pricing becomes an act of confidence and service. Remember, you're not just selling hours; you're selling a solution, a transformation, and a pathway to a better life for Emily and her family.

Pre-Framing the Investment

Before you state a number, remind the client of the value. You've spent time understanding their pain and painting a picture of their desired future. Now, connect your program directly to that desired outcome.

  • "Sarah, imagine Emily feeling confident enough to join a school club, laughing with friends, and finding comfort and connection with an animal again. That's the transformation we work towards in our 12-week 'Joyful Paws' program."
  • "This isn't just about weekly sessions; it's about a comprehensive, personalized journey with ongoing support, resources, and the unique therapeutic bond with Luna, designed to create lasting change for Emily."

Confidently Stating Your Fees

When you state your price, do so clearly, confidently, and then pause. Let the number land. Avoid rambling or justifying. Your confidence in your value will resonate with the client.

  • "The investment for our 12-week 'Joyful Paws' program, which includes 12 weekly one-on-one AAT coaching sessions with Luna, personalized resources, and direct support, is $2,997."

Discussing Payment Options

Offer flexibility to make your program accessible. This shows you're committed to helping them, not just making a sale.

  • "To make this accessible, we offer two payment options: a one-time payment of $2,997, or three monthly payments of $1,050."

Focus on Transformation, Not Cost

Always bring the conversation back to the benefits and the outcome. The cost is simply the investment required for the desired transformation.

Coach Tip: Practice Your Pricing Script

Practice saying your pricing out loud, to yourself, to a mirror, or to a friend. The more comfortable you are articulating your fees, the more confident and persuasive you will sound to your potential clients. It‚Äôs not about being pushy, but about owning the value you provide.

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing with Clarity

Once you've presented the program and pricing, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Be clear, direct, and empowering.

  • "Sarah, knowing what you now know about the 'Joyful Paws' program and how it can help Emily rediscover her joy and confidence, what feels like the best next step for you?"
  • "Are you ready to enroll Emily in the 'Joyful Paws' program and begin this transformative journey with Luna and me?"
  • "I have space for one new client starting next month. If you're ready to move forward, we can get Emily's initial intake scheduled today. How does that sound?"
  • "I'm truly excited about the possibility of working with Emily. If you're ready to commit to this powerful change, we can finalize the details now."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call in Animal-Assisted Therapy coaching, beyond simply selling a program?

The primary purpose is to establish a mutual fit, build rapport, deeply understand the potential client's challenges and aspirations, and determine if AAT coaching is the right solution for their unique needs. It's about connection and exploration, not just a sales pitch.

2. When a potential client like Sarah says, "It sounds expensive. What's the investment?", what is the most effective initial response strategy?

The most effective initial response is to re-frame the conversation around the value and transformation, rather than immediately stating the price. Ask about the cost of inaction or the value of achieving their desired outcome before discussing specific numbers, then confidently state the price and payment options.

3. What are two key elements to include when presenting your pricing to a potential client?

Two key elements are: 1) Pre-framing the investment by reminding the client of the value and transformation your program offers, and 2) Confidently stating your fees clearly and concisely, followed by a pause, and then offering payment options.

4. How can a new AAT coach realistically achieve an annual income of $60,000 based on the example program pricing of $2,997 for a 12-week program?

A new AAT coach can realistically achieve an annual income of $60,000 by consistently acquiring approximately 1-2 new clients per month for their 12-week program. This would maintain an average of 5 active clients at any given time, leading to an illustrative monthly income of $5,000.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a vital, empathetic conversation to connect with potential clients, understand their needs, and determine mutual fit for AAT coaching.
  • A structured 30-minute blueprint ensures you cover rapport-building, problem identification, solution presentation, and a clear call-to-action.
  • Active listening and mirroring client language are crucial for building trust and demonstrating understanding.
  • Approach objections as opportunities for clarification and reassurance, focusing on the unique value and transformative nature of AAT coaching.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and clarity, always anchoring it to the profound transformation and value your program provides.
  • A sustainable AAT coaching practice is achievable with consistent client acquisition, offering significant income potential that reflects your expertise and impact.
