Business Practice Lab: Your First Discovery Call

It's time to put your client acquisition skills into practice! This lab walks you through a simulated discovery call from start to close, giving you the confidence to convert interest into enrollment.

Your Ideal Prospect: Sarah's Client Avatar

Before any call, it's vital to have a clear understanding of who you're speaking to. This isn't about rigid scripting, but about being prepared to meet your client where they are, empathize with their challenges, and speak to their deepest desires. Let's introduce our prospect for today's practice call.

Maria, 52

Found you through a local wellness event where you presented on stress reduction with companion animals. Scheduled a discovery call after your talk.

Her Situation
Executive assistant, single mom of two adult children. Feels overwhelmed, constantly stressed, struggles with sleep, and has recently felt a loss of joy. Her beloved dog, Max, is her main source of comfort, but she feels guilty she doesn't spend enough quality time with him due to work demands.
Emotional State
Exhausted, a bit cynical about "self-care fads," but desperate for a real change. Intrigued by the AAT approach because of her bond with Max.
Budget Concern
"I've invested in so many programs that didn't work. I need to know this is a worthwhile investment. My finances are tight with college tuitions."
Her Goal
"I just want to feel less stressed, sleep better, and actually enjoy my life again. And I want to reconnect with Max in a meaningful way, maybe even help him with his own anxiety."

Coach Tip

Remember, your prospect isn't just buying a service; they're buying a solution to a painful problem and a vision of a better future. The more clearly you understand their pain and desired outcome, the more effectively you can position your AAT coaching as the perfect fit.

The Art of the AAT Discovery Call: A Structured Approach

A well-structured discovery call guides the conversation naturally, ensuring you gather necessary information, build trust, and present your solution effectively. This isn't about being rigid, but about having a roadmap. Here's a 30-minute framework:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Uncover Needs 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Maria! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It was wonderful meeting you at the wellness event. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. What resonated with you most from my talk that made you decide to reach out?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. So Maria, tell me ‚Äî what specifically is going on in your life right now that made you feel it was time to explore support?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Desires & Challenges 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed and a loss of joy. Can you tell me more about how that manifests in your daily life? What does a typical day look like when you're feeling this way?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings of stress and lack of joy? What worked, what didn't, and why do you think that was?"
YOU:
"I hear how much Max means to you. How do you see your relationship with him fitting into this? If nothing changes in your current situation, where do you imagine yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel less stressed, sleep better, and find your joy again, what would that look like for you and Max?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your AAT Coaching Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Maria, thank you for sharing so openly. Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a dedicated single mom and professional feeling overwhelmed and disconnected from your joy, impacting your sleep and even your bond with Max. You've tried various self-care methods, but they haven't provided the sustainable relief you're looking for. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The Animal-Assisted Therapy Coaching approach I use is distinct from what you've tried. Instead of just managing symptoms, we work together to uncover the root causes of your stress and emotional fatigue. We'll leverage the profound human-animal bond, specifically with Max, as a powerful catalyst for your well-being. My 12-week 'Joyful Connection' program is designed to create personalized strategies that reduce stress, improve sleep, and reignite your joy, all while deepening your bond with Max. Clients typically begin experiencing noticeable shifts within the first 4-6 weeks, reporting a 30-40% reduction in perceived stress and improved sleep quality by an average of 1.5 hours per night. We also focus on enhancing the human-animal bond, which often leads to positive changes in the animal's well-being as well."
Phase 4: Confident Pricing & Call-to-Action 5 min
YOU:
"So Maria, based on what I've shared about how we work and what you're looking for, does this sound like the kind of supportive, results-oriented approach you've been seeking?"
YOU:
"Excellent! My 'Joyful Connection' 12-week program is an intensive, personalized journey. The investment for this program is $3,000, which can be paid in full or in three monthly installments of $1,050. This includes weekly 60-minute coaching sessions, personalized resources, and unlimited email support between sessions. Many of my clients find this investment pays for itself in improved energy, productivity, and overall well-being. How does that sound to you?"
YOU:
"If you're ready to start regaining your joy and strengthening your bond with Max, we can schedule our first session this week. I'll send you an intake form and the agreement right after this call. How would you like to proceed?"

Coach Tip

When presenting your pricing, state it clearly and confidently. Do not waver or apologize. Frame the cost as an investment in their transformation, not an expense. This shifts the perception and reinforces the value you provide.

Navigating Objections with Grace and Confidence

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your role isn't to hard-sell, but to listen, empathize, and address their concerns with clarity and conviction. Here are some common objections and how to respond:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That sounds like a lot of money. I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Maria. This is a significant investment, and it's natural to want to consider it carefully. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or perhaps feeling unsure if this is truly the right solution for you? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make an informed decision."
"I'm not sure if I have enough time for a 12-week program right now."
"That's a very valid concern, especially with your busy schedule. Many of my clients, especially those in demanding roles like yours, feel the same way at first. What they often discover is that by dedicating this focused time to their well-being, they actually gain more time and energy in the long run. We're not adding more to your plate; we're creating strategies that integrate seamlessly into your life, often simplifying things. How much time per week do you realistically feel you could commit to yourself and Max if you knew it would lead to significant relief?"
"I've tried so many things, and nothing has really worked. How do I know this will be different?"
"That's a very honest and understandable feeling, Maria, and I appreciate you sharing it. It sounds like you've been through a lot of trial and error, which can be incredibly frustrating. What makes AAT Coaching different is our holistic, personalized approach that leverages the science of the human-animal bond. We're not offering a quick fix; we're building sustainable habits and addressing underlying stressors. Plus, the presence of Max in your journey provides a unique source of comfort and motivation that often isn't present in traditional coaching. My clients consistently report long-term shifts in emotional regulation and stress resilience (75% sustained improvement at 6 months post-program) because we co-create a plan that truly fits their life and their bond with their animal. How does incorporating Max into your journey feel different to you?"

Coach Tip

Don't be afraid of objections; see them as opportunities to deepen your understanding and reinforce your value. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and a belief in your program's ability to help.

Intervention: Eleanor joined a business mentorship program focused on sales psychology and discovery call mastery. Outcomes: Within three months, Eleanor's confidence soared. She reported a 60% increase in her discovery call conversion rate, going from 20% to 80%. She attributes her success to mastering the discovery call, realizing it's an act of service, not sales.

Coach Tip

The best way to overcome imposter syndrome in sales is through consistent practice and belief in the transformative power of your service. Every "no" brings you closer to a "yes," and every call is a learning opportunity.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 in a discovery call, and what key question should you ask to facilitate this?

The primary purpose of Phase 2 is to deeply understand the prospect's desires and challenges. A key question is: "If you could wave a magic wand and truly resolve this issue, what would your life look like?" This helps uncover their ideal future and emotional drivers.

2. When a prospect expresses concern about the investment, what is an effective strategy for addressing this objection?

An effective strategy is to empathize, then gently probe to understand the root of the concern. For example: "I completely understand. This is an investment. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the financial investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This allows you to address their specific concern rather than making assumptions.

3. How should you confidently present your pricing during a discovery call?

Present your pricing clearly, confidently, and without apology. State the full investment first, then offer payment options. Frame it as an investment in their transformation, not just a cost. For example: "The investment for this program is $X, which can be paid in full or in monthly installments of $Y."

4. According to Sarah's advice, what is the best way to overcome imposter syndrome in sales?

The best way to overcome imposter syndrome in sales is through consistent practice and a strong belief in the transformative power of your service. Viewing each call as a learning opportunity and an act of service, rather than a sales pitch, also helps build confidence.

Key Takeaways

  • Mastering the discovery call is crucial for converting interested prospects into enrolled clients, building a sustainable AAT coaching practice.
  • A structured four-phase approach (Rapport, Deep Dive, Present Solution, Close) provides a roadmap for effective and authentic conversations.
  • Active listening and empathetic questioning are paramount for understanding a client's deepest pain points and desired transformations.
  • Confidently articulating your unique value proposition and framing your pricing as an investment are key to successful enrollment.
  • Objections are opportunities to deepen understanding; address them with empathy, clarity, and a strong belief in your program's impact.
  • Consistent practice and a clear understanding of your value will empower you to build a thriving, financially rewarding AAT coaching business.
