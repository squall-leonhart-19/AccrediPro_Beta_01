ELENA:

"No, I haven't. I just feel like I should be able to remember where I put things. I was a teacher for 25 years!"

"In the classroom, you had bells, schedules, and visual aids everywhere. Your brain thrived there because the environment did the heavy lifting. "That‚Äôs a great observation. That tells us the system was too broad. What if we used a single-purpose hook for the keys and a charging station for the phone? No space for mail. We want to reduce the 'friction' of the habit. What's the one thing you lose most often?"

Coach Tip

When a client says "I've tried that," they are usually describing a system that wasn't specific enough. Always dig deeper into why it failed. Was it too far from the door? Did it require too many steps to use?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Over-Organizing" Trap: Recommending a complex 12-step filing system. ADHD brains need low-friction solutions (e.g., "drop zones").
  • Ignoring Sensory Needs: Suggesting a bright, open office for a client who is easily distracted by movement or light.
  • Character over Environment: Focusing on "trying harder" rather than "changing the room."
  • Missing the "Launchpad": Forgetting that a successful day starts with a successful exit from the house.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Point of Performance" in ADHD coaching?

It is the exact time and place where a task needs to be performed. Restructuring the environment means placing the tools/cues for that task at that specific point (e.g., putting meds next to the coffee maker).

2. Why do "Out of Sight, Out of Mind" clients struggle with traditional filing cabinets?

Because once the paper is inside a closed drawer, the visual cue that the task exists is gone. ADHD coaches often recommend vertical, open-faced organizers or transparent bins instead.

3. How should a coach respond when a client feels "shame" about their environment?

By externalizing the problem. Use language like: "Your brain is highly capable, but your environment is currently speaking a different language. Let's translate it so it works for you."

4. What is the primary goal of a "Launchpad"?

To reduce the cognitive load and decision fatigue of leaving the house by keeping all essential items in one consistent, visible location.

Lab Summary & Takeaways

  • Environment > Willpower: A restructured room will always beat a "try harder" mindset.
  • Friction is the Enemy: Every extra step in a system (opening a lid, walking to another room) increases the chance of ADHD task failure.
  • Visual Cues are Essential: Help clients move from "hidden" storage to "visible" organization.
  • Collaborative Design: The client is the expert on their home; you are the expert on ADHD mechanics. Design systems with them, not for them.
