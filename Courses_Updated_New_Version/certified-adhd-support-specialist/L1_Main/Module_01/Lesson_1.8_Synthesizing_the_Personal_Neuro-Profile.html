Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to look at childhood symptoms when tracing an adult woman's ADHD profile?

Because ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that must be present (even if masked) during development. This helps differentiate it from adult-onset conditions like burnout or thyroid issues.

2. What is a "masking" behavior Elena might have used as a teacher?

Working late hours to compensate for daytime distractions, using extreme "over-organization" to prevent failure, or relying on the rigid school schedule to provide the structure she couldn't create herself.

3. If a client says, "I'm just failing at being an adult," what is the best coaching response?

A validating response that shifts the focus to physiology: "It sounds like you're judging yourself for a struggle that might actually be about how your brain processes executive tasks."

4. What role does estrogen play in the ADHD profile for women in Elena's age group?

Estrogen supports dopamine production. When estrogen levels drop during perimenopause or menopause, ADHD symptoms often become significantly more severe and harder to mask.
