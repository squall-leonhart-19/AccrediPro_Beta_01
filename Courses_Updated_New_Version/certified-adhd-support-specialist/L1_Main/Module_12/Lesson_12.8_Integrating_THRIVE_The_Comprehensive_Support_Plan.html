Common Mistakes vs. Professional Moves

New coaches often fall into "empathy traps" where they agree with the client's limitations rather than challenging them. Review the table below:

The Mistake The Professional Move Why It Matters
Over-explaining your credentials when you feel nervous. Asking a powerful question to bring the focus back to the client. It establishes you as the guide, not the person seeking approval.
Offering a discount as soon as the client mentions price. Holding the silence and exploring the "cost of inaction." Discounting devalues the transformation you provide.
Giving "tips" for free during the enrollment call. Identifying the pattern but saving the "how-to" for the sessions. Clients need a container (the coaching relationship) to implement change.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of an enrollment conversation with a late-diagnosed client like Diane?

The goal is to help the client move from a state of "survival/grief" to a state of "possibility/investment," validating their past while showing the value of a different future.

2. Why should you avoid giving "quick tips" during a discovery call?

Quick tips provide a temporary "dopamine hit" of feeling helped, which can actually decrease the client's motivation to commit to the long-term work required for real change.

3. How does the "Cost of Inaction" framework help handle price objections?

It shifts the focus from the "price of the coach" to the "cost of the problem" (e.g., lost productivity, medical bills from burnout, strained relationships).

4. What is a key marker of a "Professional Move" during a call?

Using powerful silence and asking deep questions rather than filling the space with nervous chatter or over-explaining.
