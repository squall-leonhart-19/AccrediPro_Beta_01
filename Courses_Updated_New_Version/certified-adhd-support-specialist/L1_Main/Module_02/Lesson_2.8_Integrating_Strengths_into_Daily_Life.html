Practice these transitions. The goal is to acknowledge the pain while pivoting to the possibility. Say these words out loud to get the "feel" of the professional coaching flow.

"Diane, I hear the frustration, and it makes sense that taxes feel heavy right now. But I want to pause on what you called 'lazy.' You mentioned spending three hours on those swatches. Common Coaching Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches can stumble when first applying strength-based models. Avoid these four common mistakes:

  • Toxic Positivity: Ignoring the fact that ADHD is genuinely hard. Always validate the struggle before pivoting to the strength.
  • The "Fixer" Mentality: Trying to give Diane a list of 10 apps to fix her focus. Remember: Coaching is about discovery, not just advice.
  • Labeling for the Client: Telling her "You are creative" before she feels it. Use her own words (e.g., "You mentioned fixing broken pieces in 'wild' ways‚Äîthat sounds like a unique creative spark.")
  • Overwhelming the Session: Trying to find 50 strengths at once. Focus on one dominant strength per session to ensure it sticks.

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why is it critical to validate the struggle before offering a strength-based reframe?

Validation builds rapport and prevents "Toxic Positivity." If the client doesn't feel heard in their pain, they won't be open to seeing their potential.

2. What ADHD trait is Diane demonstrating when she works on furniture for 6 hours straight?

Hyperfocus. This is a state of intense concentration often triggered by high interest or novelty, common in ADHD brains.

3. A client says, "I'm a procrastinator." What is a possible strength-based reframe?

"You might be an Incubator who processes information deeply before acting, or a Crisis-Motivated High Performer who thrives on the dopamine of a deadline."

4. What is the "Agency Spark" in a coaching session?

It is the moment the client takes ownership and suggests their own next step or solution based on their newfound understanding of their strengths.

Lab Summary & Takeaways

  • Reframing is a Skill: It takes practice to hear a "deficit" and instantly see the "strength" flip-side.
  • Focus on Interest: ADHD brains are interest-based; helping clients align tasks with their natural interests is the key to harnessing strengths.
  • Validate First: Empathy is the bridge to empowerment. Never skip the validation step.
  • Your Voice Matters: Your calm, professional, and strength-focused presence is often the most powerful tool in the room.
