The Realistic Income Timeline

Building a career takes time, but it doesn't have to take years. Here is the trajectory most of our successful 40+ career-changers follow:

Phase 1: The Foundation (Months 1-3)

Activity: Completing your certification, setting up your basic website/socials, and working with 2-3 "Beta" clients at a reduced rate for testimonials.

Income: $500 - $1,500 total. Your focus is proof, not profit.

Phase 2: The Launch (Months 4-6)

Activity: Taking on your first 5-8 full-fee clients. Refining your "signature method." Networking with local therapists or doctors.

Income: $2,500 - $4,500 / month. You are now earning more than many part-time professional roles.

Phase 3: The Momentum (Months 7-12)

Activity: Reaching a full roster (12-15 clients) or launching your first small group program. Raising your rates based on demand.

Income: $6,000 - $10,000 / month. This is the "Full-Time Freedom" zone.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is specializing in ADHD more profitable than being a general life coach?

Specialists solve specific, high-pain problems. Because the "Support Gap" for ADHD is so large, specialists can command higher fees (often 40% more) and build waiting lists more easily than generalists who compete with everyone.

2. According to the "Rule of 15," how many clients per week are needed to potentially reach a six-figure income?

15 clients per week at a rate of $150 per session equals roughly $108,000 per year (assuming 48 weeks of work).

3. What is the primary focus of the first 3 months of your career transition?

The primary focus is building your foundation: completing certification, gathering testimonials from "beta" clients, and establishing your professional presence. Profit comes after proof.

4. Which career model is often best for those who want high income with fewer hours?

The Hybrid Model (combining 1-on-1 private clients with leveraged group programs) allows you to scale your income without linearly increasing your working hours.
