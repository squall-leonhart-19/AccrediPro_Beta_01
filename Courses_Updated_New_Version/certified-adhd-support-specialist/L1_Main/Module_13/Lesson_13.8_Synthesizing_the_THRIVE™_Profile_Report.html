Check Your Understanding

1. When Diane says she is "lazy," what is the most effective first response for a coach?

The most effective response is validation and reframing. You must gently challenge the "lazy" label by introducing the concept of executive dysfunction or brain-wiring differences to reduce shame.

2. What is the primary purpose of "Body Doubling"?

Body doubling provides an external "anchor" for the ADHD nervous system. The presence of another person (even virtually) helps regulate the client's focus and provides the necessary stimulation to initiate and sustain tasks.

3. Why is "The Fix-It Trap" dangerous in ADHD coaching?

It reinforces the idea that the client is "broken" and needs someone else to provide the answers. True ADHD support empowers the client to discover their own unique workarounds and build self-efficacy.

4. How do you know a coaching session with a late-diagnosis client was successful?

Success is marked by a shift in the client's internal narrativeâ€”moving from "I am the problem" to "I have a brain that requires a specific strategy."

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Validation is Intervention: For ADHD clients, reducing shame is often 80% of the work.
  • Metaphors Matter: Use relatable stories (like the sports car) to explain complex neurological concepts.
  • Small Wins First: Focus on one practical strategy (like Body Doubling) before overhauling their entire life.
  • Professional Identity: You are a partner in their journey, not a boss or a doctor. Your warmth is your credential.
