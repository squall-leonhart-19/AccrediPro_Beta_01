Moving Beyond the DSM-5: Internalized Hyperactivity

The traditional diagnostic criteria for ADHD were largely developed by observing young boys in classroom settings. This led to a focus on externalized hyperactivity‚Äîfidgeting, running, and climbing. However, in adults, and particularly in women, hyperactivity often moves inward.

Internalized hyperactivity manifests as a "racing mind" or a persistent sense of inner restlessness. While the client may appear calm and composed on the outside, their internal experience is one of cognitive exhaustion. A 2022 study published in The Lancet Psychiatry highlighted that internalized symptoms are frequently misdiagnosed as Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) or Dysthymia.

Coach Tip

When a client says, "I can't be hyperactive, I'm a couch potato," ask them about their mental chatter. If they describe their brain as having "20 browser tabs open at once" or a "radio that never turns off," you are likely observing internalized hyperactivity.

Gender-Specific Manifestations and Masking

Societal expectations for women‚Äîto be organized, nurturing, and socially attuned‚Äîcreate a high-pressure environment for the ADHD brain. To survive, many women develop masking or "social camouflaging."

Masking is the conscious or unconscious suppression of ADHD traits to appear neurotypical. While it may help a woman succeed professionally, it comes at a staggering metabolic and emotional cost. Statistics show that women with ADHD are 3.5 times more likely to experience burnout compared to neurotypical peers due to the cognitive load of masking.

Lifespan Development: The Hormonal Connection

ADHD is not static. As a specialist, you must understand how estrogen levels directly influence dopamine signaling. Estrogen acts as a "dopamine modulator"‚Äîwhen estrogen is high, ADHD symptoms are often more manageable. When estrogen drops, executive function often plummets.

Coach Tip

For clients in their 40s and 50s, always ask about their menstrual cycle or menopausal status. A client who was "fine" for 40 years may suddenly feel "broken" during perimenopause. This is a biological shift, not a personal failure.

The 'Spiky Profile': IQ vs. Executive Function

The Spiky Profile is a hallmark of the twice-exceptional (2e) ADHDer. This refers to the vast discrepancy between high intellectual ability (IQ) and low executive function (EF).

Imagine a client who can solve complex architectural problems or lead a multi-million dollar company but cannot remember to move the wet laundry to the dryer. This "gap" creates intense shame. The client thinks, "If I'm so smart, why can't I do this simple thing?" A 2023 meta-analysis of 42 studies confirmed that high IQ does not "cure" ADHD; it only makes it easier to hide‚Äîuntil it isn't.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "Externalized" and "Internalized" hyperactivity?

Externalized hyperactivity involves physical movement (fidgeting, pacing), while internalized hyperactivity involves a racing mind, intrusive thoughts, and a persistent sense of mental restlessness, often appearing calm on the outside.

2. Why are women with ADHD frequently misdiagnosed with Anxiety or Depression?

Due to societal masking, women often suppress their ADHD traits. The chronic effort of masking leads to burnout and anxiety, which are then treated as the primary conditions rather than symptoms of an unsupported ADHD brain.

3. How does estrogen impact ADHD symptoms in women?

Estrogen modulates dopamine. When estrogen levels drop (pre-menstrually or during perimenopause), dopamine signaling becomes less efficient, often leading to a significant increase in ADHD symptom severity and cognitive fog.

4. What does the "Spiky Profile" represent in a neuro-affirming context?

It represents the discrepancy between high cognitive/intellectual ability and lower executive functioning skills (like organization, time management, and task initiation).
