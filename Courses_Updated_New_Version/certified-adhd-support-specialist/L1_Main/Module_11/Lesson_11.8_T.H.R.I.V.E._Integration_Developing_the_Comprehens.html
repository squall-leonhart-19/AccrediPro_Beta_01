Common Pitfalls to Avoid

When you are new to the field, imposter syndrome often manifests in these four specific ways:

  • The "Just" Trap: Saying "I'm just starting out" or "I just got my certification." This immediately devalues your expertise.
  • Over-Sharing: Sharing too much of your own ADHD struggle. While empathy is good, the session must remain client-focused.
  • Apologizing for Fees: Feeling the need to explain why you charge professional rates. Your training and the transformation you provide are the justification.
  • Advice-Giving Too Early: Trying to "fix" the client in the first 5 minutes. Professionals listen and assess before they offer interventions.
