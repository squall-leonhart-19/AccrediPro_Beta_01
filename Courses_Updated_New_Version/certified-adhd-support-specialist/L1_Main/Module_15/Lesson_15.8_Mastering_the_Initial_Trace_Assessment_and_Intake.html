Section 4: Preparing for the Certification Exam

You have done the work. The exam is simply a formal recognition of the expertise you've gained. Here is what to expect:

  • Format: 20 Multiple Choice Questions.
  • Passing Score: 80% (16/20 correct).
  • Focus Areas: Executive Functioning, Neurobiology basics, Coaching Ethics, and the "Support Specialist" boundaries.
  • Strategy: Read each question twice. Often, the "best" answer is the one that prioritizes client autonomy and neuro-affirming care.

Check Your Readiness

1. A client is struggling with "Paralysis by Analysis." What is the first practitioner response?

Validate the nervous system response first. Then, use "Body Doubling" or "Micro-Tasking" to lower the barrier to entry.

2. What is the primary purpose of the DFY Client Intake form?

To identify the client's specific "Executive Function Gaps" so you can tailor your support strategy effectively from Session 1.

3. True or False: As a Support Specialist, you should provide medical advice regarding ADHD medication.

False. We support the lifestyle and systems around the diagnosis. Medical advice must always come from their prescribing physician.

4. What is the "Gold Standard" for session frequency in early ADHD support?

Weekly sessions are recommended to maintain momentum and provide the necessary "external scaffolding" for the ADHD brain.
