Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-Scaffolding: Giving the client a 20-step plan when they can only handle 2. This triggers the "ADHD Shutdown."
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Focusing only on the calendar and ignoring the grief of leaving a former professional identity.
  • Being Too "Soft": Late-diagnosed high-achievers often appreciate directness. Don't be afraid to point out the "loops" they are stuck in.
  • Failing to Externalize: Just talking about a plan isn't enough. It must be written, drawn, or physically anchored.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these three signs:

  1. The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes when they realize their struggle is biological/structural, not a character flaw.
  2. The "Micro-Action": The client identifies a task that takes less than 10 minutes and feels confident they can do it immediately.
  3. The "Visual Shift": The client moves from looking "up and left" (searching their brain) to "down and forward" (focusing on the external scaffold).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do high-achievers like Elena often struggle more with "simple" scaffolding?

They often feel "shame" that they need basic tools, and they may over-intellectualize the problem rather than applying the physical solution. They also may have relied on high-stress environments to "mask" their ADHD for years.

2. What is a "Constraint Scaffold"?

It is a technique where the coach artificially limits the client's choices (e.g., "Choose between these two options only") to prevent decision paralysis and executive function depletion.

3. What is the difference between a "to-do list" and a "scaffold"?

A to-do list is just a series of tasks. A scaffold is a supportive structure that includes the how, when, and where, along with environmental cues and emotional support to ensure the tasks can be completed.

4. How does "Identity-Based Scaffolding" help Elena?

It anchors her actions in her new professional role, helping to overcome imposter syndrome. By acting "as if" she is already the wellness coach, the executive function tasks associated with that role become more congruent and less taxing.
