1. Setting the Stage: Your Practice's Foundation

Before you even pick up the phone for a discovery call, it's vital to have your business foundations in place. Remember our discussions on legal structures, client agreements, and ethical guidelines? These aren't just paperwork; they're the bedrock of your confidence. When you know your business is compliant, insured, and professionally structured, you project an unwavering sense of authority and trustworthiness, which is incredibly attractive to potential clients.

A well-prepared coach understands that the discovery call isn't just a sales pitch; it's a mutual exploration. You're assessing if you're the right fit for them, just as they are for you. This mindset shift from "selling" to "serving" is key to authentic client acquisition.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Always review your client agreement template and FAQ document before a discovery call. 2. Understanding Your Ideal Client

Who is your ideal client? For many Certified Breastfeeding Support Coaches, it's often women in their 30s, 40s, or even 50s, navigating the complexities of breastfeeding while balancing career, family, and personal well-being. They're seeking not just information, but holistic, empathetic support that acknowledges their unique life stage and challenges.

Let's meet a potential client who embodies this demographic. Understanding her situation will help us tailor our approach during the discovery call.

Budget Concern
"My husband thinks we should just stick with the free hospital lactation consultant. I've already spent so much on baby gear."

3. The Art of the Discovery Call

A discovery call is a structured conversation designed to build rapport, understand the client's core problem, determine if you can help, and then present your solution. It's not about hard selling; it's about genuine connection and demonstrating your expertise.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Maria

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Maria! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that the mom's group referred you. So many women find their community there. For our chat today, I'd love to spend the first 10-15 minutes hearing about your current situation and challenges, then I can share a bit about how I work and we can see if it feels like a good fit. Does that sound good to you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deep Dive 10-12 min
YOU:
"Fantastic. So Maria, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you and your baby right now?"
YOU:
"You mentioned painful latch and nipple damage. Can you describe that a bit more? When does it hurt the most? What have you tried so far to alleviate it? And how is this impacting your daily life, especially now that you're back at work?"
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly challenging, Maria. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself and your breastfeeding journey in a month? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your ideal breastfeeding experience look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 8-10 min
YOU:
"Maria, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're experiencing significant pain and worry about your milk supply, feeling overwhelmed by the demands of a newborn and returning to work, and you're longing for a confident, joyful breastfeeding experience. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different. Many standard consultations offer quick fixes, but we'll take a comprehensive look at the root causes of your painful latch and supply concerns. We'll assess everything from positioning and anatomy to your daily routine and stress levels. My L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ is a personalized, step-by-step process that addresses your unique needs over several weeks. Most of my clients, especially those balancing work, find significant relief and confidence within the first 2-3 sessions."
Phase 4: Transition to Close 3-5 min
YOU:
"Maria, does this comprehensive, personalized approach sound like what you've been looking for to finally get the support you need?"
Coach Tip from Sarah

Practice active listening! Nod, reflect their feelings ("That sounds incredibly frustrating"), and ask open-ended questions ("Tell me more about..."). This isn't just polite; it makes the client feel truly seen and heard, which is paramount for women feeling vulnerable and overwhelmed.

4. Navigating Common Objections

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your ability to address these with empathy and confidence is a cornerstone of client acquisition. Remember Maria's budget concern? Let's tackle that and others.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Maria. This is an important decision, and you want to feel confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for you and your baby." (This opens the door to uncover the real objection.)
"My husband thinks we should just stick with the free hospital lactation consultant."
"That's a very common thought, and hospital lactation consultants provide valuable services. The key difference in working with me is the depth and continuity of personalized care. Hospital visits are often limited in time and follow-up. My program offers multiple, extended sessions, direct access for questions, and a consistent relationship over weeks, allowing us to deeply understand and adapt to your unique challenges as they evolve. This comprehensive support often leads to quicker, more sustainable results, saving you time, stress, and potentially more money in the long run if issues persist."
"I've already spent so much money on baby gear/other things."
"I hear you, Maria. Babies come with a lot of expenses! It's easy to see these investments as separate, but think about the value of successful breastfeeding. What is it worth to you to be pain-free, confident in your supply, and truly enjoy this precious time with your baby? Many clients tell me that investing in expert, personalized support was the most valuable decision they made, preventing months of struggle and the potential cost of formula or further medical interventions. This is an investment in your well-being and your baby's health."
"I'm not sure I have the time with returning to work."
"That's a completely valid concern, Maria. Returning to work adds a whole new layer of complexity. My program is designed with busy moms in mind. We'll work together to create a plan that fits into your schedule, whether that's virtual sessions, flexible scheduling, or specific strategies for pumping at work. In fact, many working moms find that dedicated support actually saves them time and stress by streamlining their breastfeeding routine and quickly resolving issues that would otherwise consume their energy."
Coach Tip from Sarah

When handling objections, always validate their feeling first ("I understand," "That's a common concern"). Then, reframe the objection by highlighting the unique value of your service and how it addresses their specific pain point. Never argue; always educate and empathize.

5. Confidently Presenting Your Value & Pricing

This is where many coaches, especially those new to entrepreneurship, feel most uncomfortable. But remember, you are offering a life-changing service. Your pricing reflects your expertise, time, and the significant positive impact you'll have on families.

When discussing pricing, always present your comprehensive package first, emphasizing the value and transformation, not just the cost. Avoid apologetic language. Be clear, concise, and confident.

Presenting Your Program to Maria

YOU:
"Maria, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe my 3-Month L.A.T.C.H. Support Immersion Program is exactly what you need to overcome these challenges and achieve your breastfeeding goals. This program includes:
  • Four 60-minute personalized virtual consultations, strategically spaced to support you through critical phases.
  • Unlimited text and email support between sessions for quick questions and reassurance.
  • Customized resources, including a feeding plan, pumping schedule for work, and latch technique videos.
  • Access to my exclusive online community for ongoing peer support.
The investment for this comprehensive support, which provides you with lasting confidence and peace of mind, is $1,200. We also offer a payment plan of three monthly installments of $425."

Notice how the benefits are clearly articulated, and the price is stated firmly. This is an investment in health and well-being, not just a service fee.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Always lead with value before price. List out the deliverables and transformation, then state the investment. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident. Your energy around your pricing is contagious!

6. Sealing the Partnership: Call to Action

After presenting your program and addressing any objections, it's time to guide your client toward the next step. A clear, confident call to action is essential. Don't leave them guessing.

Call-to-Action Practice

Choose one of these closing lines to practice out loud:

  1. "Maria, I'm confident we can get you to a place of pain-free, joyful breastfeeding. Are you ready to take this step and get started?"
  2. "Given what you've shared about your goals and my expertise in supporting working mothers like you, I truly feel this is the right path. What feels like the next best step for you?"
  3. "I have a few openings in the coming weeks. If you're ready to move forward, we can get your first session scheduled today and begin building your personalized plan."
  4. "How does that sound? Are you ready to invest in yourself and your baby's breastfeeding journey?"

The goal is to invite them to commit, not to pressure them. Allow space for their response, and be prepared to reiterate value or address any lingering questions.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Silence is powerful. Once you've delivered your call to action, stop talking. Let the client process and respond. It can feel uncomfortable, but it gives them the space to make their decision. Resist the urge to fill the silence with more information.

8. Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply listen and empathize, uncovering the client's core challenges, their emotional impact, what they've tried previously, and their desired future state if the problem were resolved. This helps you understand their motivation and how your service aligns.

2. When a client raises a budget concern, what is the most effective initial response strategy?

Validate their concern ("I understand, it's an investment"), then reframe the conversation around the value and transformation your service provides, emphasizing the long-term benefits and potential savings compared to ongoing struggles or alternative solutions.

3. Why is it important to lead with "value" before stating your pricing during a discovery call?

Leading with value helps the client understand the comprehensive benefits and transformation they will receive before hearing the cost. This frames the price as an investment in their desired outcome, rather than just an expense, making them more receptive to your fees.

4. What is the significance of "silence" after delivering a call to action?

Allowing silence after a call to action provides the client with the necessary space to process the information, consider their options, and formulate their response without feeling pressured. It demonstrates confidence and respect for their decision-making process.
