1. Introduction to Client Acquisition

Before we jump into the call, let's set the stage. The discovery call is arguably the most critical step in your client acquisition journey. It's not a sales pitch; it's a conversation. Your goal is to understand the prospect's challenges, determine if you're a good fit to help them, and then, if appropriate, invite them to work with you. This call is where you transition from being a theoretical expert to a practical solution-provider.

Remember, clients aren't just buying your knowledge; they're buying transformation, support, and the belief that you can help them achieve their breastfeeding goals. Your authenticity and confidence are your most powerful tools.

Coach Sarah's Tip

The most successful discovery calls I've had always started with genuine curiosity. Your prospect is inviting you into their world of challenges. Approach it with the mindset of a compassionate investigator, not a salesperson. Ask open-ended questions and truly listen to understand their underlying emotions and desires. This builds trust, which is the bedrock of any coaching relationship.

3. The Discovery Call Script

A structured approach helps you stay on track and ensures you cover all critical points, while still allowing for natural conversation. This 30-minute framework is a powerful tool to guide your calls.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Sarah! Yes, of course. My friend, Emily, recommended you. She said you helped her so much with her latch issues."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that Emily referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
RACHEL:
"Well, my baby, Leo, is three months old, and I've been struggling with low milk supply since the beginning. I've seen two lactation consultants, tried fenugreek, blessed thistle, power pumping... you name it. My supply just won't increase, and I feel like I'm constantly topping up with formula. I'm so exhausted and feel like a failure."
YOU:
"Rachel, I hear the exhaustion and frustration in your voice, and that feeling of failure is incredibly common when you're doing everything you can. Tell me more about your typical day. When did you first notice the low supply? How does it affect your daily life and your connection with Leo?"
RACHEL:
"It started pretty much immediately after birth. Every feeding is a battle. I dread them, honestly. I feel like I'm always watching the clock, wondering if he's getting enough. I just want to relax and enjoy him, but this constant worry is draining me."
YOU:
"I completely understand. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in six months? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this — what would your breastfeeding journey look like?"
RACHEL:
"In six months? Probably dried up and feeling even worse. With a magic wand, I'd be confidently breastfeeding, knowing Leo is nourished, and I'd actually look forward to our feeding times. I want to feel capable and connected, not stressed."
Coach Sarah's Tip

Asking "magic wand" questions helps uncover the client's deepest desires and paints a picture of their ideal future. This isn't just about milk supply; it's about their identity as a mother and their emotional well-being. Listen for those emotional cues and reflect them back to show you truly understand.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a dedicated mom, exhausted and frustrated by persistent low milk supply, despite trying many solutions. This is impacting your joy and connection with Leo, and you're longing for confidence and peace of mind in your breastfeeding journey. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, absolutely. You've hit it right on the head."
YOU:
"The approach I use is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just focusing on symptoms like milk volume, we utilize the L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ to look at the root causes of your specific challenges. We consider everything from your unique physiology and infant feeding mechanics to your stress levels and nutritional support. I work with you over 12 weeks, providing personalized, step-by-step guidance to identify exactly what's driving your low supply and create a comprehensive plan. Most of my clients, especially those with persistent issues, start seeing significant improvements in confidence and supply within the first 4-6 weeks, leading to a much more joyful feeding experience."
Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Rachel, does this comprehensive, personalized approach sound like what you've been looking for — a way to finally get to the bottom of your supply issues and regain confidence?"
RACHEL:
"It really does. It sounds like a breath of fresh air, honestly. But..."

4. Navigating Objections with Confidence

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Your ability to handle them gracefully and empathetically will define your success.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. This is an important decision. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for you and Leo."

(This opens the door to uncover the real objection.)
"I've already spent so much money on lactation consultants; how is this different?"
"That's a very fair question, Rachel, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot without getting the lasting results you hoped for. The key difference with my 12-week L.A.T.C.H. Support Program‚Ñ¢ is its holistic and sustained nature. We don't just troubleshoot one feeding session; we implement a comprehensive plan that evolves with you, addressing not just immediate symptoms but also underlying factors like nutrition, stress, and long-term strategies for maintaining supply. Think of it as a dedicated partner walking with you for three months, ensuring you build lasting confidence and practical skills, rather than just getting quick fixes."
"I'm not sure I have the time for a 12-week program with a new baby."
"I completely get that, Rachel. Time is precious with a newborn. The beauty of this program is its flexibility. Our sessions are structured to be impactful, and the support between sessions is tailored to fit into your life, not overwhelm it. We focus on implementable steps that integrate into your daily routine. Many of my clients find that by investing this time upfront, they actually *save* time and emotional energy in the long run, because they're no longer constantly worrying or trying ineffective strategies. Imagine the time you'll gain back when breastfeeding becomes a source of calm, not stress."
Coach Sarah's Tip

When handling objections, always validate the client's concern first. "I completely understand," or "That's a very fair question," disarms them and shows empathy. Then, pivot to how your program uniquely addresses that specific concern. Don't be afraid to reiterate your value proposition.

5. Presenting Your Investment

This is where many coaches stumble. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, not just a commodity. Your pricing reflects your expertise, your time, and the significant transformation you provide. Present it with confidence and clarity.

How to Confidently State Your Price:

  1. Anchor with Value: Always reiterate the value they will receive before stating the price. "Based on everything we've discussed, Rachel, and your desire to finally feel confident and connected in your breastfeeding journey, my 12-week L.A.T.C.H. Support Program‚Ñ¢ is designed to give you exactly that transformation."
  2. State the Full Investment Clearly: "The full investment for the 12-week program, which includes six one-on-one coaching sessions, unlimited messaging support, and personalized resources, is $2,500."
  3. Offer Payment Options (if applicable): "I also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly payments of $850."
  4. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, stop talking. Let the client process. The first person to speak often feels the pressure.

It's crucial to practice this out loud. The more comfortable you are saying your prices, the more confident you will sound to your potential clients. Your tone of voice, posture, and conviction communicate more than the numbers themselves.

6. Mastering the Close

After presenting your program and investment, it's time to guide Rachel towards a decision. This isn't about pushing; it's about inviting her to take the next step towards her desired outcome.

Practice These Closing Lines:

  • "Rachel, does this sound like the right path for you to finally achieve the breastfeeding confidence and joy you've been looking for?"
  • "Given everything you've shared, I truly believe the L.A.T.C.H. Support Program‚Ñ¢ is exactly what you need right now. Are you ready to get started?"
  • "What feels like the best next step for you, Rachel? Are you ready to enroll today, or would you prefer the payment plan option?"
  • "Imagine feeling truly confident and peaceful with Leo's feedings in just a few weeks. If that's what you want, let's make it happen. Are you in?"

Once she says yes, be ready to move quickly to the next steps: sending the agreement, collecting payment, and scheduling the first session. Strike while the iron is hot!

Coach Sarah's Tip

Don't be afraid of the silence after you ask for the sale. It's a natural processing time for the client. Resist the urge to fill the silence with more talking or offering discounts. Let them sit with the decision. Your confidence in your offer will speak volumes.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem" phase in a discovery call, and what type of questions are most effective here?

The primary purpose is to deeply uncover the prospect's pain points, challenges, and desired outcomes. Open-ended, empathetic questions focusing on their emotions, daily impact, and "magic wand" scenarios are most effective to build rapport and truly understand their needs beyond surface-level symptoms.

2. Rachel expresses concern about having already spent a lot of money without results. How would you reframe her past experiences to highlight the unique value of your program?

You would validate her frustration, then pivot to emphasize the holistic, sustained, and personalized nature of your 12-week L.A.T.C.H. Support Program‚Ñ¢. Explain that it addresses root causes and long-term transformation, unlike previous piecemeal approaches, positioning it as a lasting solution rather than another temporary fix.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is the crucial step immediately after stating the price and payment options?

The crucial step is to pause and listen. Resist the urge to fill the silence or elaborate further. This allows the client time to process the information and indicates your confidence in your offer.

4. A client says, "I need to talk to my partner before deciding." How can you respond to this objection while maintaining control of the sales process?

You can respond by validating their need for discussion: "That's completely understandable, this is an important decision for your family." Then, ask to understand their partner's potential concerns: "What specific questions or concerns do you anticipate your partner might have that I could help address now?" or "Would it be helpful for me to join a brief call with you and your partner to answer any questions they might have about the program?" This keeps the conversation moving forward and allows you to proactively address potential hesitations.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a structured conversation designed to understand client needs, establish fit, and invite enrollment.
  • Building rapport and actively listening to uncover emotional pain points are critical for trust and connection.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reinforce your unique value proposition.
  • Present your program's investment with clarity and confidence, always anchoring it to the transformation you provide.
  • Mastering simple closing lines and embracing silence empowers clients to make a decision.
  • Your expertise as a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach is valuable and can lead to a financially rewarding practice.
