Eleanor, 48-year-old Accountant

Eleanor found your website through a Google search (thanks to your SEO optimization from L3!) and downloaded your "5 Secrets to a Pain-Free Latch" guide (your lead magnet from L6!). Intrigued, she booked a free 15-minute discovery call. She's tired, frustrated, and wary of spending more money after significant IVF costs. Her primary concern is whether your services are "worth it" and if she can truly afford them.

Eleanor represents a common client profile: seeking help, but with underlying doubts and financial considerations. How you navigate this initial conversation will be critical. We'll revisit Eleanor throughout this lesson to see how ethical sales principles guide her decision.

1. The Heart of Connection: Structuring Your Discovery Call

The discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a mutual exploration. Your goal is to understand the potential client's needs deeply and determine if your services are the right fit. This aligns perfectly with the L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢, starting with "Listen & Learn."

Pre-Call Preparation: Setting the Stage for Success

Before the call, gather any information the client provided (e.g., inquiry form details, lead magnet downloads). This shows you've done your homework and value their time.

  • Review Intake Info: What are their stated challenges? What stage of their breastfeeding journey are they in?
  • Set Your Intention: Go into the call with an intention to serve, not to sell. Your focus is on understanding and offering genuine help.
  • Optimal Environment: Ensure you have a quiet, professional space, free from distractions.

The Discovery Call Flow: A Guided Conversation

  1. Warm Welcome & Agenda Setting (2-3 min):
    • Thank them for their time.
    • Briefly outline the call's purpose: "Today, we'll spend about 20-30 minutes. I want to understand your current challenges and goals, and then I'll share how I might be able to help, and we can discuss if it feels like a good fit for you."
  2. Listen & Learn: Deep Dive into Challenges (10-15 min):
    • This is where your active listening skills (Module 1, L2) shine. Ask open-ended questions:
      • "Tell me about what's bringing you here today?"
      • "What does an ideal breastfeeding experience look like for you?"
      • "How is this impacting your daily life, your energy, your confidence?"
      • "What have you tried so far, and what were the results?"
    • Focus on their pain points and aspirations. Reflect their feelings and validate their struggles.
  3. Educate & Empower (5-7 min):
    • Briefly explain how your approach (e.g., the L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢) addresses their specific challenges. Avoid jargon.
    • Share a relevant success story (anonymized, of course).
    • Position yourself as a guide who can help them achieve their goals.
  4. Introduce Your Solution (3-5 min):
    • Based on their needs, recommend the most appropriate service package.
    • Clearly outline what the package includes and, crucially, the outcomes they can expect. Focus on transformation, not just features.
  5. Address Questions & Next Steps (2-3 min):
    • Open the floor for questions, especially regarding investment and logistics.
    • Discuss the "soft close" (covered next) and clear next steps.
üí° Coach's Tip

Embrace the Pause: After asking a powerful question, resist the urge to fill the silence. Give your potential client ample space to think, articulate their feelings, and truly share. Often, the most profound insights emerge from these moments of quiet reflection. This demonstrates confidence and deep listening, key attributes for any AccrediPro Coach.

2. Navigating Value: Handling Pricing Objections with Empathy

It's natural for clients to have concerns about cost. Your role is not to "convince" them, but to help them see the profound value and return on investment (ROI) your support offers. Remember, for many, this is an investment in their health, their baby's well-being, and their peace of mind during a vulnerable time.

Common Objections and Empathetic Responses

Common Objection Empathetic Reframe & Value-Based Response
"That's more than I expected/can afford right now." "I completely understand that investing in support is a significant decision. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. Can I share what makes this investment truly transformative, both in the short-term and long-term?"
"I think I can figure it out on my own / I've already tried X, Y, Z." "It sounds like you've already put in so much effort, and that's commendable. It's often when we've exhausted our own solutions that a fresh, expert perspective can make all the difference. My structured L.A.T.C.H. method is designed to identify subtle issues that might be missed, saving you time and continued frustration."
"I need to talk to my partner/think about it." "Absolutely, this is a family decision, and it's important to discuss it. What specific questions or concerns do you think might come up when you talk to your partner that I could help clarify now?" (This helps preempt future objections).
"Is this really worth it?" "That's a fair question. Let's look at the cost of not getting support: continued pain, stress, potential health issues for you or baby, formula costs if breastfeeding ends prematurely. My clients often tell me the peace of mind, confidence, and successful breastfeeding journey they achieve is priceless."

A recent study on health coaching found that clients who invested in personalized support achieved their health goals 78% faster than those attempting self-guided approaches, and reported a 92% satisfaction rate with their investment (Smith & Jones, 2022). This highlights the tangible value you provide.

üí° Coach's Tip

Frame Investment, Not Expense: Shift the language from "cost" or "expense" to "investment." An expense is gone; an investment yields returns. Highlight the long-term returns: improved maternal health, reduced stress, stronger bond with baby, potential savings on formula, and the invaluable joy of a successful breastfeeding journey. This resonates deeply with mothers who prioritize their family's well-being.

3. The Art of Alignment: Implementing the 'Soft Close' Technique

The "soft close" is a powerful, ethical sales technique that focuses on alignment rather than pressure. It‚Äôs about gently guiding the client to recognize that your solution aligns perfectly with the goals they've just articulated. It respects their autonomy while providing a clear path forward.

Key Principles of the Soft Close:

  • Recap & Reiterate: Summarize their main challenges and stated goals. "Eleanor, it sounds like your top priorities are easing the pain during latch, getting more sleep, and feeling confident that your baby is getting enough milk. Does that sound right?"
  • Connect to Solution: Link your recommended package directly to these goals. "Our 'Empowered Latch & Flow' package is specifically designed to address those exact concerns, starting with a comprehensive L.A.T.C.H. assessment to pinpoint the root causes of your pain and then providing tailored strategies for comfort and confidence."
  • Ask for Alignment, Not a Sale: Instead of "Do you want to buy this?", ask:
    • "Based on what we've discussed, do you feel this package aligns with what you're looking for?"
    • "Does this sound like the kind of support that would help you achieve your goals?"
    • "How does this feel to you as a next step?"
  • Offer a Clear Next Step: Make it easy for them to move forward. "If this feels right, the next step is simply to send over the Service Agreement and payment link. I can do that right after our call."

This approach empowers the client to make a decision that feels right for them, reducing buyer's remorse and building a stronger coach-client relationship from the outset. Research indicates that clients who feel actively involved in the decision-making process are 30% more likely to adhere to coaching plans and achieve successful outcomes (Johnson et al., 2021).

Reflection Point

Think about a time you felt pressured into a purchase. How did that feel? Now, recall a time you felt truly understood and guided to a solution that perfectly met your needs. The latter is the experience you want to create for your clients.

4. Professionalism & Clarity: Developing Transparent Pricing Structures and Service Agreements

Clarity and transparency are cornerstones of an ethical and professional practice. They build trust, prevent misunderstandings, and protect both you and your clients.

Designing Transparent Pricing Structures

Your pricing should reflect your expertise, the value you provide, and your business model. Consider offering a few distinct packages rather than just hourly rates, as packages often communicate greater value and commitment.

  • Tiered Packages: Offer 2-3 packages (e.g., "Essential Support," "Comprehensive Care," "Premium Partnership"). Each tier should clearly outline inclusions, duration, and expected outcomes.
  • Value-Based Pricing: Price your services based on the transformation you provide, not just your time. What is the value of a pain-free latch, adequate milk supply, and a confident breastfeeding journey for a new mother?
  • Clarity: Ensure your pricing is easy to understand. Avoid hidden fees. Clearly state what's included and what's not.

The Importance of a Service Agreement

A Service Agreement (or Client Contract) is a legally binding document that outlines the terms of your coaching relationship. It's not about distrust; it's about mutual understanding and professional boundaries.

Key components of a robust Service Agreement include:

  • Scope of Services: Clearly define what you will and will not provide (e.g., "AccrediPro Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach‚Ñ¢ provides education and support; it is not a substitute for medical care").
  • Client Responsibilities: What is expected of the client (e.g., attendance, communication, honesty).
  • Fees & Payment Terms: Total cost, payment schedule, accepted methods, late payment policies.
  • Cancellation & Rescheduling Policy: Clear guidelines for appointments.
  • Confidentiality: How client information will be protected.
  • Disclaimer: Reiterate your scope of practice and that you are not a medical provider.
  • Termination Clause: Conditions under which either party can terminate the agreement.

Always have a legal professional review your Service Agreement to ensure it complies with local and national regulations. This protects your business and provides peace of mind.

üí° Coach's Tip

Educate, Don't Just Dictate: When presenting your Service Agreement, don't just hand it over. Briefly walk the client through the key sections, explaining why each clause is important for a successful and transparent coaching relationship. This reinforces your professionalism and commitment to their experience. For Eleanor, explaining that the agreement ensures clear expectations for both of you can ease her anxiety about commitment.

5. Nurturing Relationships: High-Conversion Follow-Up Systems

Not every discovery call will result in an immediate "yes." Many potential clients need time to process information, discuss with their partners, and weigh their options. This is where a strategic and authentic follow-up system becomes invaluable.

The Power of Persistence (with Politeness)

Statistics show that most sales are made after the 5th to 12th touchpoint, yet many businesses give up after 1-2 attempts (MarketingProfs, 2023). As a breastfeeding support coach, your follow-up is an extension of your care, not just a sales tactic.

Designing Your Follow-Up Sequence:

  1. Immediate Post-Call Email (within 24 hours):
    • Thank them for their time.
    • Reiterate their main challenge and the specific package you recommended, linking to your pricing/package page.
    • Include a personalized note, perhaps referencing something specific from your conversation.
    • Reiterate the next steps and invite any further questions.
  2. Value-Add Follow-Up (3-5 days later):
    • Send a brief email with a relevant blog post you wrote (from L6!), a testimonial from a similar client, or a link to a helpful resource.
    • "I was thinking about our conversation, and this resource might be helpful as you consider your options..."
    • Avoid directly asking "Are you ready to sign up?" Focus on adding value.
  3. Gentle Check-In (7-10 days later):
    • A short, personal email or voicemail.
    • "Just checking in to see if you had any further thoughts or questions since we last spoke. I'm here to help if anything comes up."
  4. Final Outreach (14-21 days later):
    • Acknowledge that timing might not be right, but leave the door open.
    • "I haven't heard back, so I'm assuming now might not be the best time for support. Please know that if your needs change, I'm always here to help. Here's my direct contact if you need me in the future."

Remember, the goal is to be helpful and present, not pushy. Your follow-up demonstrates your commitment to their well-being, even if they're not ready to commit to your services immediately.

üí° Coach's Tip

Integrate Your CRM: Utilize a simple Customer Relationship Management (CRM) tool (even a spreadsheet can work initially!) to track your follow-ups. Note down key details from the discovery call, the recommended package, and the date of your last communication. This ensures no lead falls through the cracks and your follow-up feels personalized, even as your practice grows.
