Coach in Focus: Evelyn's Service Transformation

From Hourly Hopes to Packaged Prosperity

E

Evelyn, 52

Former High School Teacher, Newly Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach

Evelyn, a recent AccrediPro graduate, was passionate about supporting new mothers but found herself overwhelmed. She offered hourly consultations at a rate of $75/hour, feeling guilty charging more. Clients would book one session, then disappear, often still struggling. Her income was inconsistent, and she felt undervalued.

"I knew my knowledge was powerful, but I didn't know how to translate that into a business model that felt fair to me and my clients. I was constantly worried about my next booking and felt like I was giving away too much for too little, which led to burnout," Evelyn shared.

Evelyn's experience is common for many career changers entering the coaching world. She needed to shift her mindset from trading time for money to offering transformative solutions through structured service packages. We'll revisit Evelyn's journey and her eventual success in designing a robust service menu.

The Client Journey: From Prenatal Preparation to Postpartum Partnership

Understanding your client's journey is fundamental to designing services that truly resonate. Breastfeeding support needs evolve significantly from pregnancy through the postpartum period. Your service menu should reflect this progression, offering targeted support at each critical stage.

Structuring Prenatal 'Listen & Learn' Packages

Prenatal support focuses on proactive education, confidence-building, and setting realistic expectations. This is where you leverage the L.A.T.C.H. method's 'Listen & Learn' phase to empower expectant parents with knowledge and skills before challenges arise. These packages are often less intensive but crucial for laying a strong foundation.

  • Focus: Education, anticipation, preparation, emotional support.
  • Content: Understanding lactation physiology, optimal positioning and latch techniques (theory), common challenges and how to prevent them, partner involvement, building a postpartum support plan.
  • Format: Group workshops, online courses, 1-2 personalized virtual sessions.
  • Goal: Reduce anxiety, increase self-efficacy, equip parents with foundational knowledge.

Designing Postpartum 'Assess & Align' Interventions

Postpartum support typically requires more immediate, hands-on, and problem-solving interventions. This aligns with the L.A.T.C.H. method's 'Assess & Align' and 'Troubleshoot & Target' phases, where you are actively addressing specific concerns, providing real-time guidance, and optimizing physical mechanics.

  • Focus: Problem-solving, hands-on assistance, clinical assessment, immediate relief, personalized strategies.
  • Content: Latch assessment, positioning adjustments, milk transfer evaluation, pain management, supply concerns, infant weight gain, pump flange fitting.
  • Format: In-home visits, extended virtual consultations, follow-up text/email support.
  • Goal: Resolve acute issues, optimize breastfeeding mechanics, ensure maternal and infant well-being.
Coach's Tip

Overcoming Imposter Syndrome in Pricing: Many coaches, especially those transitioning from service-oriented careers, struggle with charging their worth. Remember, your fee isn't just for your time; it's for your specialized knowledge, years of study, the transformation you facilitate, and the confidence and peace of mind you provide. Research suggests that clients who invest more are often more committed to the process and achieve better outcomes (Grant, 2013).

Crafting Your Signature Service Packages

Moving beyond hourly rates is essential for a sustainable and profitable practice. Packages allow you to offer comprehensive support, ensure continuity of care, and provide greater value to your clients. They also offer you more predictable income and less administrative burden.

Service Model Description Pros for Coach Pros for Client
Hourly Consultations Single, time-based sessions (e.g., 60-90 minutes). Low entry barrier. Perceived flexibility, low initial commitment.
Value-Based Packages Bundled services focused on a specific outcome (e.g., "Latch Optimization Package" includes initial consult + 2 follow-ups + 2 weeks text support). Predictable income, higher client lifetime value, deeper impact. Comprehensive support, clear path to resolution, better value for money.
Retainer-Style Support Ongoing access to coach for a defined period (e.g., "First 3 Months Support"). Stable recurring revenue, deep client relationships. On-demand support, peace of mind, continuous guidance.

When designing packages, think about the client's desired transformation, not just a list of services. What problem are you solving? What outcome are they hoping to achieve?

For example, instead of "2-hour consult + 1-hour follow-up," consider "The Confident Latch Journey: Achieve pain-free breastfeeding and optimal milk transfer within 3 weeks."

Pricing Strategies: Valuing Your Expertise

Pricing is often the most challenging aspect for new coaches. It requires a blend of market research, self-worth, and a clear understanding of your value proposition. Remember, you're not just selling time; you're selling results, peace of mind, and the fundamental health and well-being of a mother and baby.

Industry data indicates that certified lactation professionals and breastfeeding coaches typically charge between $75-$150 per hour for individual consultations (ILCA, 2022). However, packages significantly increase your average client value and income potential.

Examples of Package Structures & Income Potential:

  • Basic Postpartum Package: Initial 90-min visit + one 60-min follow-up + 1 week email support. Priced at $350-$550.
  • Premium L.A.T.C.H. Transformation: Initial 90-min visit + two 60-min follow-ups + 2 weeks text support + personalized plan. Priced at $750-$1200.
  • VIP Concierge Support: Initial 2-hour visit + four 60-min follow-ups (over 2 months) + 6 weeks unlimited text/email support + partner education session. Priced at $1500-$2500+.

A coach like Evelyn, who previously charged $75/hour, might serve 8-10 clients per month on a Basic Package, generating $2,800 - $5,500. With a mix of Premium and VIP clients, a similar number of clients could yield $5,000 - $8,000+ per month, significantly increasing her income and impact without necessarily increasing her workload.

Coach's Tip

Communicate Value, Not Just Services: When presenting your packages, focus on the benefits and outcomes for the client. Instead of listing "90-minute visit," say "a comprehensive assessment to identify the root cause of discomfort and optimize milk transfer." Emphasize the long-term gains: confidence, reduced stress, successful breastfeeding journey. This helps clients understand the true investment.

Integrating The L.A.T.C.H. Method into Tiered Services

The L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ provides a perfect framework for structuring tiered services, ensuring a logical progression of support and clear value at each level.

Basic Support: Foundations of L.A.T.C.H.

This tier often covers the foundational elements, especially for prenatal education or initial postpartum assessment.

  • L: Listen & Learn: Comprehensive intake, maternal/infant history review, understanding client concerns.
  • A: Assess & Align: Initial visual assessment, basic positioning guidance, identifying common challenges.
  • C: Comfort & Connection: Basic strategies for maternal comfort and promoting skin-to-skin.
  • Target Client: Those needing foundational knowledge, initial problem identification, or seeking to prevent common issues.

Premium Support: Deep Dive & Targeted Intervention

This tier integrates more intensive application of the L.A.T.C.H. framework, offering deeper intervention and personalized strategies.

  • L: Listen & Learn: Detailed history, emotional landscape, deeper understanding of parent goals.
  • A: Assess & Align: Advanced positioning, functional anatomy assessment, hands-on latch support.
  • T: Troubleshoot & Target: Clinical problem-solving for specific issues (pain, supply, transfer), developing targeted intervention plans.
  • C: Comfort & Connection: Personalized comfort strategies, neurobiology of let-down, stress reduction techniques.
  • Target Client: Those experiencing specific challenges (pain, low supply, unsettled baby) requiring hands-on and strategic support.

VIP Support: Holistic Integration & Ongoing Partnership

The highest tier offers comprehensive, ongoing support, embracing the full spectrum of the L.A.T.C.H. method for holistic well-being.

  • L.A.T.C.H. Integration: Comprehensive application of all phases, including in-depth follow-up and adjustment.
  • H: Holistic Habits: Integration of nutrition, sleep, routine, and stress management into the breastfeeding journey.
  • Ongoing Access: Extended period of direct access for questions, adjustments, and emotional support.
  • Target Client: Those desiring extensive, personalized, and long-term support for their entire breastfeeding journey, including lifestyle integration.

Reflection Point

Consider your ideal client's needs and budget. How can you structure 3-4 distinct packages that genuinely serve different segments of your audience, while still providing exceptional value and reflecting your expertise?

Specialized Support: Emergency & Group Models

Beyond traditional 1:1 packages, consider how you can expand your offerings to include immediate, urgent support and scalable group solutions.

Creating 'Emergency Support' Protocols

New parents often face urgent concerns that can't wait for a scheduled appointment. Offering clear emergency support can be a lifeline for them and a valuable, premium service for your practice. However, clear boundaries are paramount to prevent burnout.

  • Define Scope: What constitutes an "emergency"? (e.g., sudden severe pain, baby refusing to latch, significant engorgement with fever).
  • Set Availability: Clearly communicate hours of availability (e.g., 8 AM - 8 PM daily).
  • Pricing: Position this as a premium service with a higher fee (e.g., $150-$250 for a 30-minute urgent virtual call) to reflect its immediate nature and your flexibility.
  • Protocol: How do clients access this? (e.g., dedicated emergency phone number, specific email subject line).

'Quick-Response' Text/Email Add-Ons

For non-urgent but time-sensitive questions, offering quick-response text or email support as an add-on to packages can enhance client satisfaction and provide a small, consistent revenue stream.

  • Define Boundaries: Clearly state what types of questions are appropriate for this channel and expected response times (e.g., "response within 4 hours during business hours").
  • Limit Scope: Emphasize that this is for quick guidance, not complex problem-solving.
  • Pricing: Offer as a monthly add-on (e.g., $50-$100/month) or included in higher-tier packages.
Coach's Tip

Setting Boundaries for Emergency Support: While you want to be responsive, it's vital to protect your personal time. Clearly outline your emergency hours and response times. Consider using a dedicated business phone number for client communications. Remember, a well-rested coach provides better support. Your well-being is part of your professional sustainability.

Designing Group Coaching Models for Cost-Effective Community Support

Group coaching is an excellent way to serve more clients, create a supportive community, and generate scalable income. This is particularly effective for topics related to the L.A.T.C.H. method's 'Holistic Habits' phase or prenatal 'Listen & Learn' preparation.

  • Target Topics: Prenatal Breastfeeding Prep, Returning to Work & Pumping, Sleep Strategies for Breastfeeding Families, Nutrition for Lactation, Stress Management for New Moms.
  • Format Ideas:
    • Webinar Series: 3-4 sessions on a specific topic, live Q&A.
    • Structured Programs: 4-6 week program with weekly live calls, a private online community, and resources.
    • Support Circles: Monthly facilitated discussions for ongoing community and peer support.
  • Benefits:
    • For Clients: More affordable access to expert guidance, peer support, reduced isolation.
    • For Coaches: Scalable income (serve 10-20+ clients simultaneously), leverages your time, builds community around your brand.

Imagine running a 6-week "Holistic Habits for Breastfeeding Success" group program for 15 participants at $297 each. That's nearly $4,500 for a single program, often with less direct hour-for-hour time commitment than 1:1 coaching.

Building Your Financial Foundation with a Clear Menu

Evelyn, our coach in focus, eventually transformed her practice by implementing a clear service menu. After deep reflection on her unique value and market needs, she launched three core packages:

  1. "Prenatal L.A.T.C.H. Ready" (2 virtual sessions + digital guide) at $395.
  2. "Postpartum L.A.T.C.H. Restore" (1 in-home + 2 virtual follow-ups + 2 weeks text support) at $950.
  3. "Holistic Habits Group Coaching" (6-week online program) at $297.

Within three months, Evelyn had consistently booked 3-4 "L.A.T.C.H. Ready" clients, 5-6 "L.A.T.C.H. Restore" clients, and filled her first "Holistic Habits" group with 12 participants. Her monthly income stabilized at $6,000-$7,500, providing the financial freedom she craved. More importantly, her clients felt deeply supported, understood the clear path forward, and achieved lasting success.

"I finally feel confident and valued," Evelyn shared. "My clients know exactly what they're getting, and I know I'm delivering exceptional, comprehensive care. It's a win-win."

Coach's Tip

Leverage Testimonials for Package Marketing: Once clients complete a package, ask for detailed testimonials that speak to the specific transformation they experienced. "Thanks to Evelyn's 'L.A.T.C.H. Restore' package, I went from painful, tearful feeds to confident, joyful breastfeeding in just two weeks!" These stories are powerful social proof for future clients.

Check Your Understanding

1

What is a primary difference in focus between prenatal 'Listen & Learn' packages and postpartum 'Assess & Align' interventions?

Prenatal 'Listen & Learn' packages primarily focus on proactive education, anticipation, and confidence-building to prepare expectant parents for breastfeeding. Postpartum 'Assess & Align' interventions, however, focus on reactive problem-solving, hands-on assistance, and clinical assessment to address immediate challenges and optimize breastfeeding mechanics once the baby has arrived.

2

How do value-based packages benefit both the Breastfeeding Support Coach and the client compared to hourly rates?

For the coach, value-based packages offer more predictable income, higher client lifetime value, and less administrative burden, allowing for deeper impact. For the client, they provide comprehensive support, a clear path to resolution, and better perceived value, as the focus is on achieving a specific outcome rather than just accumulating time.

3

Provide an example of how the 'Holistic Habits' component of the L.A.T.C.H. method could be integrated into a group coaching model.

The 'Holistic Habits' component, which covers nutrition, sleep, routines, and stress management, is ideal for a group coaching model. An example could be a 4-6 week online program titled "Nourish & Thrive: Holistic Habits for Breastfeeding Parents," featuring weekly live sessions on topics like "Optimal Nutrition for Lactation," "Mastering Maternal & Infant Sleep," and "Stress Reduction Techniques," alongside a private community forum for peer support and Q&A.

4

What are two critical considerations when designing an 'Emergency Support' protocol for your service menu?

Two critical considerations are: 1) Clearly defining the scope of what constitutes an "emergency" (e.g., severe pain, baby refusing to latch, not just general questions) to manage expectations and prevent misuse. 2) Setting clear boundaries for availability and pricing (e.g., specific hours, higher fee) to ensure the service is sustainable for the coach and reflects its premium, urgent nature, preventing burnout.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure your service menu to align with the evolving needs of clients through their prenatal and postpartum breastfeeding journeys.
  • Shift from hourly rates to value-based packages to provide comprehensive support, ensure client success, and generate predictable, sustainable income.
  • Integrate the L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ into tiered service offerings (Basic, Premium, VIP) to clearly define value at each level.
  • Establish clear protocols for 'Emergency Support' and 'Quick-Response' add-ons, balancing client needs with your professional boundaries.
  • Explore group coaching models, especially for 'Holistic Habits' or prenatal preparation, to serve a broader audience and scale your impact.
