Understanding the Discovery Call

The discovery call, often referred to as a "consultation" or "introductory call," is your opportunity to do two critical things: first, truly understand a potential client's challenges and goals, and second, determine if your services are the right fit to help them. It's not a sales pitch; it's a conversation of mutual discovery. A well-executed discovery call builds trust, establishes your expertise, and paves the way for a successful client relationship.

Research indicates that clients who engage in a comprehensive discovery process with a coach are 3.5 times more likely to convert into long-term clients compared to those who receive only a brief informational exchange (Coach Federation Study, 2022). This highlights the importance of investing time in this initial interaction.

Coach Tip

Always remember, the discovery call is primarily about the client. Your role is to listen more than you speak, ask insightful questions, and genuinely seek to understand their world. When they feel truly heard, trust is built, and they become open to your guidance.

Mastering the Discovery Call Script

Here's a structured approach to your 30-minute discovery call with Elena. Remember, this is a guide, not a rigid script. Adapt it to the flow of conversation, but ensure you hit these key points.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Elena

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Elena, thanks so much for connecting with me today. How are you doing, and how's your little one?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful. I understand you found me through the 'Moms of [City Name]' group, which is fantastic. So, what specifically prompted you to reach out to me today? What's going on with your breastfeeding journey?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Goals 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing, Elena. You mentioned significant pain. Could you tell me more about it? When does it happen during a feed? How would you rate it on a scale of 1 to 10?"
YOU:
"And regarding your concerns about low milk supply, what makes you feel that your supply might be low? How often is your baby feeding, and what are you noticing after feeds?"
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly challenging, especially balancing this with your other responsibilities and knowing you'll return to work soon. If we could wave a magic wand and solve these issues, what would your ideal breastfeeding experience look like? What's most important to you right now?"
Coach Tip

During Phase 2, practice mirroring and validating. Phrases like "I hear you, that sounds incredibly frustrating" or "It's completely understandable to feel overwhelmed when..." show empathy and reinforce that you're truly listening.

Phase 3: Present Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Elena, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is that you're experiencing significant pain, you're concerned about your milk supply and baby's satisfaction, and you desperately want to breastfeed successfully and confidently before returning to work, without feeling overwhelmed. Does that capture the essence of what you're facing?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've described, my L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ is designed precisely for situations like yours. We'd start with a comprehensive assessment to understand the root causes of the pain and supply issues. Then, we'd develop a personalized, step-by-step plan focusing on optimal Latch, effective Alignment, comfortable Timing, consistent Consumption, and holistic Healing for you. My typical program is 8 weeks, with weekly 60-minute virtual sessions and unlimited text support, to ensure we get you to a confident, pain-free, and sustainable breastfeeding experience."
Phase 4: Call to Action & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Does this comprehensive, personalized approach resonate with what you're looking for, Elena? Does it sound like the kind of support you need right now?"
YOU:
"Excellent! Let's talk about how we can get started."

Confident Pricing & Program Presentation

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but it's a moment to embody confidence in the value you provide. After Elena indicates interest in your approach, transition smoothly into discussing your program and its investment.

Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud, in front of a mirror, until it feels natural and confident. Avoid hesitation or apologetic tones. Your conviction in your value will be contagious.

Pricing Presentation Example:

YOU: "My signature 'Confident Breastfeeding Journey' program is an 8-week commitment, designed to transform your experience. The investment for this personalized support, including weekly sessions, customized action plans, and unlimited text support, is $1,200. I also offer a convenient 3-month payment plan of $450 per month. Many of my clients find that resolving these issues early saves them significant costs on formula, multiple specialist visits, and the immense emotional toll of ongoing struggles. How does that sound to you, Elena?"

  • Pause and Listen: After stating the price, pause. Let Elena process the information. Do not fill the silence. Her response will guide your next steps.
  • Connect to Value: Always link the investment back to the value and transformation she expressed wanting. Remind her of her goals.
  • Offer Options: Payment plans can significantly reduce the barrier to entry for many clients. Be clear about what they entail.

Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They are rarely a hard 'no' but often an invitation for more information or reassurance. Your goal is to listen, validate, and then reframe or provide clarity.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That sounds like a lot of money."
"I completely understand that it feels like a significant investment, Elena. And it is. Many of my clients initially feel the same. What they often realize, however, is the long-term cost of not addressing these issues. Think about the potential formula costs over months (which can easily exceed $150-200/month for a newborn), multiple visits to different specialists, and the emotional toll of daily pain and worry. My program is designed to provide a comprehensive, lasting solution, often preventing those ongoing expenses and giving you peace of mind. What value do you place on a pain-free, confident breastfeeding experience?"
"I need to talk to my partner."
"Absolutely, Elena, this is a big decision for your family, and it's important that you and your partner are aligned. To help with that conversation, what specific questions do you think your partner might have? I can provide you with some information, perhaps a brief summary of our discussion and the program's benefits, to share with them. Would it be helpful for me to send you an email with these details?"
"I've tried everything, I'm not sure this will work."
"I completely hear your frustration, Elena. It sounds like you've been through a lot already, and that can be incredibly disheartening. My approach is different because it's not just about quick fixes; it's about a holistic, personalized plan using the L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢. We dive deep into the specific mechanics, your body's unique needs, and emotional well-being. My clients often tell me they've tried 'everything' too, but hadn't found a coach who looked at the whole picture and provided consistent, dedicated support. I'm confident we can find solutions together. Would you be open to hearing about a client similar to you who felt the same way initially?"
"I don't have enough time right now."
"I understand completely. Being a new mom is incredibly demanding, and adding another commitment can feel overwhelming. However, what I've found is that by investing time strategically now, we can actually save you significant time and energy in the long run. Imagine the time you'll save once breastfeeding is pain-free and efficient, and you're not constantly worrying or troubleshooting. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, with focused weekly sessions and actionable steps that fit your schedule. How much more time is currently being consumed by the difficulties you're facing?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to mutually discover if there's a good fit between the client's needs and your services, by deeply understanding their challenges and goals, and then presenting how you can help. It's not a hard sell, but a conversation of exploration.

2. When Elena expresses concern about the cost, what is an effective strategy to respond?

An effective strategy is to validate her concern, then reframe the cost as an investment by comparing it to the long-term expenses of not addressing the issue (e.g., formula, other specialist visits) and the value of resolving pain and worry. Connect it back to her stated goals.

3. What are the four key phases of the 30-minute discovery call outlined in this lesson?

The four key phases are: 1) Build Rapport, 2) Uncover Needs & Goals, 3) Present Your Solution, and 4) Call to Action & Next Steps.

4. If a client tells you they "need to talk to their partner" before deciding, what is a helpful next step you can offer?

Offer to provide them with information (e.g., a brief email summary of the program and benefits) to facilitate their conversation with their partner. You can also ask what specific questions their partner might have to better equip them.
