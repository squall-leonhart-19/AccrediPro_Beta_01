2. The Discovery Call Framework: Your Roadmap to Connection

The discovery call isn't about selling; it's about connecting, understanding, and determining if you're the right solution for their unique challenges. It's a structured conversation designed to build rapport, uncover needs, and introduce your approach. A typical call lasts 20-30 minutes.

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 minutes)

Your primary goal here is to make the client feel comfortable and safe. Begin by thanking them for their time and acknowledging how they found you. This builds immediate connection.

Your Dialogue Example

YOU: "Hi Maria! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into what's bringing you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"

MARIA: "Oh, a few of the moms in my local Facebook group mentioned you. They said you were incredibly helpful and really listened."

YOU: "That's wonderful to hear! I'm so glad they had a positive experience. So Maria, in your own words, what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you and Leo?"

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision (10-12 minutes)

This is where your active listening and empathetic questioning skills shine. Ask open-ended questions that encourage Maria to share her story, her struggles, and her hopes. Don't interrupt; let her speak.

Your Dialogue Example

YOU: "Thank you for sharing that, Maria. It sounds incredibly challenging. Can you tell me more about Leo's weight gain? When did the concerns start, and how has it been affecting your daily life and your emotional well-being?"

MARIA: "It started around six weeks. The pediatrician brought it up, and ever since, every feeding is just anxiety. I cry almost daily. I feel like a failure. I can't enjoy him because I'm constantly worried."

YOU: "I can only imagine how heavy that must feel. And what have you already tried to address Leo's weight gain and your feeding concerns? What worked, and what didn't feel quite right?"

MARIA: "I've tried different holds, power pumping, even some herbal teas. The hospital LC gave me a handout, but it felt so generic. Nothing has made a lasting difference, and I'm just so tired of guessing."

YOU: "If nothing changes with Leo's feeding and weight, where do you see yourself in a year? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly resolve this, what would your life and your feeding journey with Leo look like?"

These questions help Maria articulate her deepest pain and her desired future state, creating emotional leverage for her to seek a solution.

Coach Tip

As Sarah, I learned that the most powerful tool in a discovery call is not your knowledge, but your ability to listen without judgment. Many clients have felt dismissed before. By truly hearing their story, you build trust, which is the bedrock of any successful coaching relationship. Aim for an 80/20 listening-to-talking ratio.

Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Value (8-10 minutes)

After deeply understanding their needs, it's time to briefly introduce your unique approach and how it directly addresses their specific challenges. Focus on outcomes and transformation, not just features.

Your Dialogue Example

YOU: "Maria, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're feeling overwhelmed, anxious, and guilty about Leo's weight gain, and you're exhausted from trying generic advice that hasn't worked. You desperately want to enjoy feeding your baby and see him thrive. Does that feel accurate?"

MARIA: "Yes, exactly! You've really captured it."

YOU: "The approach I use is different from what you've experienced before. Instead of just treating symptoms or offering generic tips, we look at the root causes of Leo's feeding challenges. I work with you over a period of 8-12 weeks to identify exactly what's contributing to his slow weight gain ‚Äì whether it's related to latch mechanics, milk transfer efficiency, or other factors ‚Äì and we create a personalized, step-by-step plan. Most of my clients, like Maria, who was struggling with low supply and nipple pain, start feeling significantly more confident and seeing positive changes within the first 3-4 weeks. My goal is to empower you with the knowledge and practical skills to feel in control and joyful about breastfeeding again."

Coach Tip

Remember to frame your solution in terms of the client's desired outcome. Maria wants to enjoy feeding and see Leo thrive, not just 'fix a latch'. Connect your methods to her vision. Use language that speaks to her emotional needs and offers hope.

3. Confidently Presenting Your Investment

This is often the most nerve-wracking part for new coaches. Remember, you are offering a life-changing service, and your fees reflect your expertise, time, and the transformative results you deliver. Practice makes perfect.

Transparent Pricing Strategies

Always present your services as a package, not hourly rates. This emphasizes the comprehensive, long-term support you provide and the holistic nature of your work. State your price clearly and then pause, allowing the client to process the information.

Your Dialogue Example

YOU: "So Maria, does this sound like the comprehensive, personalized support you've been looking for?"

MARIA: "It really does sound like what I need. I just wish I'd found you sooner."

YOU: "That's wonderful to hear. My signature 10-week 'Thriving Together' program, which includes weekly 60-minute virtual sessions, unlimited text/email support between sessions, and a personalized resource library, is an investment of $1,800. We can break that into two payments if that's helpful. This program is designed to get you from worry to confidence, ensuring Leo thrives and you reclaim your joy in motherhood."

Notice the pause after stating the price. This is crucial. It gives the client space to react. Don't fill the silence.

Coach Tip

When stating your price, use confident, clear language. Avoid phrases like "it's only" or "my fee is just." Your service is valuable. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural. If you're not confident in your pricing, your client won't be either.

4. Mastering Objections & Closing the Call

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Anticipate common objections and prepare confident, empathetic responses. The goal is to address their concerns and guide them toward a decision.

Common Objections & Your Responses

  • "I need to think about it."

    Your Response: "I completely understand, Maria. This is an important decision. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure I've addressed all your questions."
    (This helps uncover the real objection.)

  • "It's too expensive / I can't afford it right now."

    Your Response: "I appreciate your honesty, Maria. I know this is a significant investment. However, I invite you to consider the cost of NOT addressing this ‚Äì the continued stress, the guilt, the impact on your mental health, and Leo's potential health challenges. Many of my clients find that the peace of mind and the lasting solution we achieve quickly outweigh the initial investment. We do have payment plans available to make it more manageable. What feels most challenging about the investment for you right now?"
    (Reframe the cost, offer solutions, and ask for specifics.)

  • "I'm not sure if it will work for me, I've tried other things."

    Your Response: "That's a very valid concern, Maria, especially given your past experiences. What makes my approach different is its highly personalized nature and our focus on root causes. We don't just offer generic advice; we actively work together to understand your unique situation and implement strategies tailored specifically for you and Leo. I'm committed to finding solutions that work. What specifically makes you feel uncertain?"
    (Acknowledge, differentiate, and re-engage.)

Call-to-Action Practice

Always end with a clear call to action. Make it easy for them to say "yes" or to take the next step.

Your Dialogue Example

YOU: "Maria, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe I can help you achieve your goal of joyful, stress-free feeding for Leo. Are you ready to take this step and start feeling confident in your breastfeeding journey?"

IF YES: "Fantastic! I'm so excited to work with you. I'll send over the enrollment details and contract right after this call. Let's get your first session scheduled."

IF THEY NEED MORE TIME: "No problem at all. How about I send you a summary of our conversation, the program details, and my availability for next week? Would it be helpful to schedule a brief follow-up call on [Day] at [Time] to answer any further questions you might have?"

Coach Tip

Don't be afraid to ask for the sale. You've provided value, built rapport, and offered a solution. It's your responsibility to guide the client to the next step. Confidence in your offer is contagious.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Build Rapport" phase in a discovery call, and what is a key question to ask?

The primary purpose is to make the client feel comfortable, safe, and heard, establishing trust. A key question is: "How did you hear about me?" or "What made you decide to reach out now?"

2. When a client says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective next step you should take, and why?

The most effective next step is to ask, "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This helps uncover the client's true underlying objection, allowing you to address it directly.

3. Why is it recommended to present your services as a package rather than hourly rates?

Presenting services as a package emphasizes the comprehensive, holistic, and long-term support you provide, focusing on the transformative outcome rather than just the time spent. It also helps clients see the full value of your expertise and commitment.

4. How can you ethically and effectively address a client's "It's too expensive" objection?

Acknowledge their honesty and validate their feeling. Then, reframe the cost by highlighting the cost of inaction (continued suffering, missed opportunities for joy). Differentiate your value from cheaper, less effective alternatives. Offer solutions like payment plans, and then ask clarifying questions to understand what makes the investment challenging for them.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a connection call, not a sales pitch, focused on understanding the client's deepest needs and desired transformation.
  • Active listening and empathetic questioning are your most potent tools for building trust and uncovering true pain points.
  • Confidently articulate your unique value proposition and present your fees as an investment in a lasting solution.
  • Anticipate and prepare for common objections, viewing them as opportunities to provide more information and reassurance.
  • Your business has significant income potential; consistent client acquisition and confident sales conversations are key to achieving financial freedom and impact.
