Elena, 42

Found you through a local mom's group, interested in your "Thriving Together" breastfeeding circle.

Her Situation
6 weeks postpartum, struggling with frequent nipple pain and low confidence in milk supply. Feels isolated and overwhelmed by conflicting advice online.
Budget Concern
"I've spent money on a few 1:1 consults already and it felt expensive and didn't fully solve things. Is this group really going to be different?"
Decision Style
Seeks community, practical, step-by-step solutions. Values feeling heard and supported. Skeptical of quick fixes, but open to structured help.
Her Goal
"I want to breastfeed comfortably and confidently for at least 6 months. I need practical tips and to feel like I'm not alone in this."
Coach Tip

Remember, a sales call isn't about manipulation; it's about empathetic inquiry. Your primary role is to listen more than you speak. Aim for an 80/20 split: 80% listening to their story, 20% guiding the conversation and offering solutions. This builds trust and makes them feel truly seen.

The Group Program Sales Call Script: "Thriving Together"

This script outlines a 30-minute call for your hypothetical 6-week group program, "Thriving Together: Postpartum Breastfeeding Circle," priced at $297. Adapt it to your own program details!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Elena! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's lovely to connect. I understand you heard about the 'Thriving Together' circle through your mom's group? That's fantastic! How are things going for you today?"
YOU:
"Great. So, before we dive into the details of the group, I'd love to hear a bit more about what specifically prompted you to reach out about group support. What's on your mind regarding your breastfeeding journey right now?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Elena. Tell me more about the nipple pain you're experiencing â€“ when does it happen? How intense is it? And what makes you concerned about your milk supply?"
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly challenging to feel isolated and overwhelmed with conflicting advice. How is all of this impacting you emotionally and physically? What's the biggest struggle day-to-day?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these challenges? What worked, and what didn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"Let's fast-forward three months. If your breastfeeding journey was exactly where you wanted it to be, what would that look like for you and your baby? How would you feel?"
Coach Tip

When prospects share their struggles, resist the urge to immediately jump to solutions. Instead, validate their feelings. Phrases like "That sounds incredibly tough," or "It's completely understandable you feel that way" can create a safe space and deepen trust, making them more receptive to your solutions later.

Phase 3: Present Your Group Program as THE Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Elena, based on everything you've shared, what I'm hearing is that you're an exhausted new mom, dealing with nipple pain and milk supply worries, feeling isolated, and craving practical, step-by-step guidance within a supportive community. Does that feel accurate?" (Wait for affirmation)
YOU:
"That's exactly why I created the 'Thriving Together: Postpartum Breastfeeding Circle.' It's designed specifically for moms like you who need comprehensive, consistent support without the high cost of ongoing 1:1 sessions. Over 6 weeks, we'll cover advanced latch techniques, proven strategies for building and maintaining supply, effective pain management, and practical tips for navigating common challenges. But just as importantly, you'll connect with other moms facing similar journeys, sharing experiences and celebrating wins in a non-judgmental space. It's like having a lactation expert and a supportive village in your pocket every week."
YOU:
"Many of my past group members, like Sarah (45, mom of two), initially felt overwhelmed, but by Week 3, she reported feeling a profound shift in her confidence and comfort. By the end, she was not only meeting her breastfeeding goals but had also built lasting friendships. We focus on empowering you with knowledge and practical tools, combined with the emotional scaffolding of peer support."
Coach Tip

When presenting your solution, don't just list features. Translate features into benefits and outcomes. Instead of "weekly Q&A," say "weekly live Q&A where you get personalized answers to your specific questions, saving you hours of frantic Googling." Paint a picture of their desired future state.

Phase 4: Confident Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Elena, does this sound like the comprehensive, supportive environment you've been looking for to achieve that confident, comfortable breastfeeding journey we talked about?" (Pause and listen)
YOU:
"Wonderful! The investment for the full 6-week 'Thriving Together' program is $297. This includes 6 live weekly sessions, access to our private community forum, downloadable resources, and recordings of all sessions. We start on [Date], and I'd love to have you join us. How does that sound, and do you have any questions about getting started?"

Handling Common Objections

Even with a perfect script, prospects will have questions and concerns. Here's how to confidently address them, turning potential roadblocks into opportunities for deeper connection and clarification.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"The $297 is a bit much right now. I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Elena. Investing in yourself and your breastfeeding journey is a significant decision. Can I ask, what specifically are you needing to think about? Is it the investment itself, the time commitment, or perhaps you're wondering if a group setting is truly right for you? I want to make sure I address any concerns you have now." (Listen for the real objection. If it's the investment, you can then offer a payment plan if available, or reiterate the value compared to ongoing 1:1 support.)
"I'm not sure a group setting is for me. I prefer 1:1 support."
"That's a very valid point, and I hear that often. While 1:1 support is wonderful for acute, highly personalized issues, the power of a group program like 'Thriving Together' is truly unique. It offers a structured curriculum that covers all the essential topics, combined with the invaluable benefit of shared experience and peer support. Many clients find that hearing others' questions and insights can be even more beneficial than just one perspective. Plus, you still get personalized attention during our live Q&A sessions. Itâ€™s a blend of personalized guidance and community wisdom. What specifically makes you hesitant about a group environment?"
"I'm worried about the time commitment. I'm already so busy with a newborn."
"Elena, I completely empathize! New motherhood is incredibly demanding. That's precisely why I've designed 'Thriving Together' to be accessible. Each live session is 60-75 minutes, and if you miss one, you get the recording. The 'homework' is practical, actionable steps you'd be doing anyway, like practicing a new latch or observing feeding cues. Think of it as investing a small amount of time now to gain confidence and ease that will actually save you time and stress in the long run. Many moms find that dedicating this focused time actually helps them feel more organized and less overwhelmed."
"Can't I just find all this information online for free?"
"You absolutely can find a lot of information online, Elena. The internet is a vast resource! However, what's often missing is the structure, personalized guidance, and accountability that a program like 'Thriving Together' provides. It's one thing to read about something; it's another to apply it effectively with expert support and the encouragement of a community. We filter through the noise, provide evidence-based strategies, and ensure you're implementing them correctly for your unique situation. Think of it as having a roadmap and a guide, rather than just a pile of ingredients."
Coach Tip

Practice these responses out loud until they feel natural. Your confidence in handling objections isn't about having a perfect script, but about genuinely believing in the value of your program and being prepared to address concerns with empathy and clarity. This is where your expertise shines!

Confident Pricing Presentation

Presenting your pricing isn't just about stating a number; it's about anchoring that number in the immense value your program provides. Here's how to do it with confidence.

Step 1: Reiterate Value. Before you say the price, briefly remind them of the transformation they desire and how your program delivers it. For Elena, this might be: "Elena, imagine feeling confident, comfortable, and connected in your breastfeeding journey, supported by both expert guidance and a loving community. That's the transformation we create in 'Thriving Together.'"

Step 2: State the Price Clearly. "The investment for the full 6-week 'Thriving Together: Postpartum Breastfeeding Circle' is $297." Say it with a smile, without hesitation or apology.

Step 3: Break Down the Value. "To put that into perspective, that's less than the cost of two individual 1:1 lactation consultations, yet you receive 6 weeks of comprehensive, structured support, weekly live interaction, a private community, and all the resources you need to succeed. It's designed to be an accessible, high-value investment in your peace of mind and your baby's nutrition."

Step 4: Offer Payment Options (if applicable). "For those who prefer, we also offer a convenient 2-pay option of $150 per month."

Step 5: Call to Action. "How does that sound to you, and are you ready to claim your spot in our next circle, starting [Date]?"

Golden Rule of Pricing

Never apologize for your prices. Your expertise, time, and the transformation you offer are valuable. Your confidence in your pricing directly reflects your belief in your program's ability to deliver results.

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase of a group program sales call?

The primary goal is to deeply uncover the prospect's pain points, struggles, and desired outcomes, validating their feelings and building rapport. This understanding allows you to tailor your program presentation as the ideal solution.

2. When Elena states, "I'm not sure a group setting is for me," what is an effective strategy to address this objection?

Validate her concern ("That's a very valid point..."), then highlight the unique benefits of a group program that 1:1 might not offer (e.g., peer support, shared experiences, structured curriculum, cost-effectiveness), while reassuring her that personalized attention is still available. You might ask, "What specifically makes you hesitant about a group environment?" to dig deeper.

3. How should you ideally frame your program's price to a prospect?

Frame the price by first reiterating the immense value and transformation the program offers. State the price clearly and confidently, then immediately break down the value by comparing it to alternatives (e.g., fewer 1:1 sessions) and highlighting the comprehensive nature of the program. Offer payment plans if available, and end with a clear call to action.

4. What is a key emotional benefit of a group program that you should emphasize during a sales call for a new mom struggling with breastfeeding?

A key emotional benefit to emphasize is the sense of community, reduced isolation, and shared support. For a new mom feeling overwhelmed and alone, knowing she's connecting with others facing similar challenges can be incredibly powerful and is a major differentiator from individual support. This fosters connection and validation.
Key Takeaways
  • Effective sales calls are about empathetic listening and clearly articulating how your group program solves a prospect's specific problems.
  • Follow a structured call framework (Rapport, Problem, Solution, Close) to maintain control and guide the conversation.
  • Anticipate and confidently address objections by validating concerns and reframing them with the unique benefits of your program.
  • Present your pricing with conviction, always anchoring it in the significant value and transformation your program delivers.
  • Group programs offer substantial income potential, allowing you to scale your impact and create financial freedom.
