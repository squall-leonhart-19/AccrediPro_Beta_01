Designing High-Ticket Group Coaching Programs

Group coaching offers a powerful middle ground between intensive 1-on-1 support and self-paced digital courses. It allows you to leverage your time more effectively while still providing personalized attention within a supportive community. For many coaches, this is the first step beyond 1-on-1.

Identifying Your Niche for Group Programs

The key to a successful, high-ticket group program is specificity. Instead of "General Breastfeeding Support," focus on a distinct challenge that a cohort of parents faces simultaneously. This allows for targeted curriculum and fosters a strong sense of community.

  • New Parent Nerves: "Confident Latch & Early Days Survival Guide" for expectant parents.
  • Return to Work: "Seamless Transition: Breastfeeding & Back to the Office" for parents preparing to go back to work.
  • Weaning Journeys: "Gentle Weaning: A Mindful Transition for Mother & Baby."
  • Tandem Nursing: "Nourishing Two: Navigating Tandem Breastfeeding with Ease."
  • Pumping & Storing: "Pumping Power: Maximizing Your Milk Supply & Storage."

These specific niches allow you to command higher prices because you're solving a very particular, often urgent, problem for clients.

Structuring Your Group Coaching Program

A typical high-ticket group program might include:

  • Duration: 4-8 weeks is common, providing enough time for transformation without overwhelming participants.
  • Live Sessions: Weekly or bi-weekly live calls (e.g., Zoom) for teaching, Q&A, and hot-seat coaching. Record these for participants who can't attend live.
  • Community Hub: A private Facebook group, Slack channel, or dedicated forum on your LMS for ongoing support, questions, and peer connection.
  • Resources: Supplementary guides, worksheets, video tutorials, and checklists that align with your L.A.T.C.H. Method‚Ñ¢ principles.
  • Price Point: High-ticket typically ranges from $500-$2,500+ per participant, depending on duration, exclusivity, and your level of direct interaction.
Coach's Tip

Leverage Your Existing Expertise: Don't try to create something entirely new. Look at the top 3-5 challenges your 1-on-1 clients consistently bring to you. Those are your golden opportunities for group programs. You already have the solutions; now it's about packaging them for a collective audience.

Creating Evergreen Digital Courses: Passive Income Potential

Evergreen digital courses are the ultimate expression of scaling your expertise. Once created, they can be sold continuously without requiring your direct, active time for each sale. This is where true passive income generation begins.

Identifying Evergreen Course Topics

Think about topics that have broad appeal and are not time-sensitive. These are often foundational, preparatory, or transitional phases of the breastfeeding journey.

  • Prenatal Preparation: "The Confident Latch: Your Prenatal Breastfeeding Blueprint."
  • Weaning: "Navigating Weaning: A Gentle Approach for You & Your Child."
  • Pumping Mastery: "Pumping & Storing with Confidence: Essential Skills for Breastfeeding Parents."
  • Common Challenges: "Troubleshooting Early Breastfeeding: Solutions for Sore Nipples, Engorgement & More."

These courses should address universal needs and provide actionable solutions, making them valuable at any point in a parent's journey.

Course Content & Structure

Digital courses typically consist of pre-recorded video lessons, downloadable PDFs (worksheets, checklists, guides), audio files, and quizzes. Structure your course logically, breaking down complex topics into digestible modules.

Consider:

  • Introduction: Welcome, course overview, setting expectations.
  • Core Modules: 3-7 modules, each focusing on a specific learning outcome.
  • Lesson Breakdown: Each module contains 3-5 short (5-20 minute) video lessons.
  • Actionable Exercises: Encourage application of knowledge.
  • Resources: Curated links, recommended products, further reading.
  • Conclusion: Summary, next steps, encouragement.

The goal is to provide comprehensive value that clients can access and complete at their own pace, whenever and wherever they are.

Coach's Tip

Start Small, Iterate Often: Don't feel pressured to build a massive, all-encompassing course initially. Begin with a smaller, highly focused course (e.g., 3 modules, 10 lessons) that solves one specific problem. Get it out there, gather feedback, and then iterate or expand. Perfection is the enemy of progress in digital product creation.

Pricing Strategies for Tiered Digital Offerings

A well-designed tiered pricing strategy maximizes both accessibility and profit. It creates a "value ladder" where clients can enter your ecosystem at different price points and ascend to higher-value (and higher-priced) offerings as their needs evolve.

Tier Offering Type Typical Price Range Value Proposition Client Benefit
Free/Lead Magnet E-books, checklists, webinars, mini-guides $0 Introduces your expertise, builds trust, collects leads Quick wins, initial education, low commitment
Low-Ticket Evergreen digital courses, workshops (recorded) $47 - $297 Addresses specific pain points, foundational knowledge Affordable solutions, self-paced learning, high ROI
Mid-Ticket Group coaching programs, specialized workshops (live) $497 - $1,997 Community support, direct access to coach, deeper transformation Personalized guidance, accountability, peer connection
High-Ticket 1-on-1 VIP coaching, intensive retreats, advanced programs $2,500 - $10,000+ Exclusive access, bespoke solutions, accelerated results Deep dive, tailored strategy, maximum support

This ladder allows you to serve a wider audience. A new parent might start with your free "L.A.T.C.H. Checklist," then purchase your "$147 Prenatal Latch Course." Later, when returning to work, they might invest in your "$797 Return to Work Group Coaching Program."

Factors Influencing Pricing

  • Perceived Value: How much transformation does your offering provide?
  • Your Expertise: Years of experience, credentials, unique methodology (like L.A.T.C.H.‚Ñ¢).
  • Market Demand: What are competitors charging? What are clients willing to pay for this specific solution?
  • Delivery Method: Live interaction (higher price) vs. self-paced (lower price).
  • Support Level: Direct access to you, community access, email support.
Coach's Tip

Embrace Value-Based Pricing: Instead of pricing based on your time, price based on the value and transformation you provide. A solution to persistent nipple pain, for example, is priceless to a struggling parent. Your digital products offer solutions that save parents time, pain, and stress‚Äîquantify that value when setting prices.

Integrating the L.A.T.C.H. Method‚Ñ¢ into Self-Paced Learning Modules

The AccrediPro L.A.T.C.H. Support Method‚Ñ¢ is a powerful framework that can be seamlessly adapted for digital consumption. The structured, step-by-step nature of L.A.T.C.H. makes it ideal for guiding clients through self-paced learning.

Translating L.A.T.C.H. for Digital Delivery

Consider how each component of L.A.T.C.H. can be broken down into video lessons, downloadable guides, and actionable exercises:

  • L: Listen & Learn:
    • Digital Module: "Understanding Your Unique Story & Goals."
    • Content: Video lessons on reflective listening, guided journaling prompts, downloadable intake forms for self-reflection.
  • A: Assess & Align:
    • Digital Module: "Visualizing Optimal Latch & Positioning."
    • Content: Video demonstrations of various positions, anatomical graphics, self-assessment checklists for latch indicators.
  • T: Troubleshoot & Target:
    • Digital Module: "Pinpointing & Solving Common Challenges."
    • Content: Short video tutorials on common issues (e.g., shallow latch, engorgement), decision trees for problem-solving, downloadable action plans.
  • C: Comfort & Connection:
    • Digital Module: "Nurturing Your Bond & Promoting Let-Down."
    • Content: Guided meditations for relaxation, video on skin-to-skin benefits, exercises for biological nurturing positions.
  • H: Holistic Habits:
    • Digital Module: "Sustainable Wellness for You & Baby."
    • Content: Nutrition guides, sleep hygiene tips, stress management techniques, routine planners.

Each "L.A.T.C.H. Letter" can become a distinct module in your course, guiding clients through a systematic process, just as you would in a 1-on-1 session.

Ensuring Engagement in Self-Paced Learning

Without your direct presence, it's crucial to design for engagement:

  • Interactive Elements: Quizzes, polls, fill-in-the-blank worksheets.
  • Progress Tracking: Most LMS platforms allow students to track their progress, fostering a sense of accomplishment.
  • Clear Calls to Action: After each lesson, tell students exactly what to do next.
  • Sense of Community: Even in evergreen courses, consider adding a private Facebook group for alumni to connect and ask questions (monitored by you or a VA).
  • Regular Updates: Keep content fresh and relevant.

Selecting and Managing Learning Management Systems (LMS) for Growth

A Learning Management System (LMS) is the digital home for your courses and potentially your group programs. Choosing the right platform is critical for ease of use, scalability, and seamless client experience.

Key Features to Look For in an LMS

  • Ease of Use: For both you (course creator) and your students. Intuitive interface is paramount.
  • Course Creation Tools: Video hosting, text editor, ability to upload PDFs, quizzes, assignments.
  • Payment Gateway Integration: Stripe, PayPal, etc., for smooth transactions.
  • Marketing & Sales Features: Landing page builders, affiliate programs, coupon codes, email marketing integrations.
  • Community Features: Forums, comments, private groups.
  • Analytics: Track student progress, completion rates, sales data.
  • Customer Support: Responsive and helpful support is invaluable.
  • Scalability: Can it grow with you as you add more courses and students?
  • Pricing: Understand monthly fees, transaction fees, and any hidden costs.

Managing Your Digital Ecosystem

Once your products are live, active management is key:

  • Marketing & Promotion: Regularly promote your offerings through your website, social media, email list, and collaborations.
  • Student Support: Even with self-paced courses, be available for questions (e.g., dedicated email, weekly office hours).
  • Content Updates: Periodically review and update your course content to ensure it remains accurate and relevant.
  • Feedback Loop: Collect feedback from students to continuously improve your offerings.
  • Automation: Utilize email sequences, marketing funnels, and customer service automation to streamline operations.
Coach's Tip

Don't Let Tech Be a Barrier: Many coaches, especially career changers, fear the technology involved in digital products. Start with an LMS known for its user-friendliness (like Teachable or Podia). Remember, you don't need to be a tech wizard; you just need to be willing to learn and leverage the tools available. Your message is more important than perfect production value.
