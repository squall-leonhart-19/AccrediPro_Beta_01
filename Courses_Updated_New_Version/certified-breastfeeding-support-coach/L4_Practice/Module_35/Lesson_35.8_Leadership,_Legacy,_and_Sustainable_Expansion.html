Welcome to Your Growth Frontier

As a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach‚Ñ¢, you possess invaluable expertise. Many coaches begin with one-on-one consultations, which is a fantastic foundation. However, to achieve true financial freedom, flexibility, and broader impact, diversifying your income streams is not just an option, but a strategic imperative. Think of it as building a robust financial ecosystem for your practice.

A 2022 survey by the Small Business Administration found that businesses with multiple income streams reported 30% higher revenue growth and significantly greater resilience during economic fluctuations compared to those reliant on a single source (SBA, 2022). For a solopreneur, this means less stress about feast-or-famine cycles and more predictability.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Remember, your unique blend of clinical knowledge and compassionate support is a superpower. When you think about diversifying, don't just think about what you can sell, but how you can package your expertise to serve more people in different ways. It's about scaling your impact, not just your hours.

Identifying Your Unique Value & New Opportunities

Your journey begins by identifying how your core expertise can be repackaged. Consider your ideal client's journey: what support do they need before, during, and after 1:1 sessions? What common questions do you answer repeatedly? These are clues to potential new offerings.

Brainstorming Potential Income Streams:

  • Group Programs/Workshops: Offer themed workshops (e.g., "Preparing for Breastfeeding Success," "Returning to Work While Breastfeeding") or longer group coaching programs. This leverages your time more efficiently.
  • Digital Products: Create downloadable guides, e-books, online courses, or recorded masterclasses. These are passive income generators once created.
  • Membership Community: Build a subscription-based community for ongoing support, Q&A sessions, and exclusive content.
  • Affiliate Partnerships: Recommend trusted products (pumps, nursing pillows, supplements) and earn a commission.
  • Speaking Engagements: Offer paid presentations to local parenting groups, hospitals, or corporate wellness programs.
  • Supervision/Mentorship: If you're an experienced coach, mentor newer practitioners (as we discussed in Module 25).

Pricing Your Premium Offerings

Pricing new services can feel daunting, especially if you're used to hourly rates. The key is to shift to value-based pricing. What is the transformation you provide? What is the client's desired outcome worth to them?

Consider a client paying $150 for a 1-hour 1:1 session. If your group program helps them achieve similar or even better results over 6 weeks, with community support, it's worth significantly more than 6 individual sessions. A common pricing strategy for group programs is to price them at 25-50% of the cost of equivalent 1:1 support, making them accessible yet highly valuable.

Strategies for Value-Based Pricing:

  1. Anchor Pricing: Present your premium 1:1 service first, then introduce your group program or digital product as a more accessible alternative that still delivers significant value.
  2. Tiered Packages: Offer different levels (e.g., Basic, Premium, VIP) within your group program, perhaps with varying levels of access to you or bonus materials.
  3. Bundle Services: Combine a digital product with a mini-group coaching session, or a group program with a single 1:1 check-in.
  4. Early Bird Discounts: Create urgency for initial launches.
Coach Tip from Sarah

Don't undervalue your expertise. Many women, especially career changers, struggle with pricing. Research shows that women are more likely to underprice their services by up to 30% compared to men (Harvard Business Review, 2019). Stand firm in the value you provide. Your credentials and experience are worth investing in.

Strategy Call Script: Selling Your "Scale & Soar" Coaching Program

"Imagine in 12 weeks, you've successfully launched a new group program, easing your 1:1 load, and creating new revenue streams. How would that feel?"

Handling Objections with Grace and Authority

Objections are a natural part of any sales conversation. They are often not a "no," but a request for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy, validate their concern, and then reframe or offer a solution.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"The investment is a lot. I've already spent so much on my clinical training."
"I completely understand. Investing in your business can feel significant, especially after your clinical training. However, think of this not as another expense, but as an investment in your future earning potential and freedom. Your clinical skills are the engine, but business strategy is the fuel and the map. Without it, you risk continued burnout and capped income. My coaching helps you leverage that clinical investment to generate income in new, sustainable ways. What kind of return would you need to see to feel this investment was worthwhile?"
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision. To help you think, can I ask what specifically you need more time to consider? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps clarifying how this fits into your overall vision? I want to make sure I've addressed all your questions." (This helps uncover the real objection.)
"I'm not sure I have the time to create new programs right now with my full client load."
"That's precisely why many coaches seek my support. The 'Scale & Soar' program is designed to be highly structured to make the most of your limited time. We break down the process into manageable steps, and my goal is to help you create time by building scalable assets. Imagine dedicating a few focused hours now to create a group program that then serves many, freeing up future 1:1 hours. We prioritize what will give you the biggest return on your time investment."
"I'm not tech-savvy. Creating an online course or managing group calls feels overwhelming."
"Many of my clients share that concern! And that's perfectly normal. My program offers practical, step-by-step guidance on the essential tech tools you'll need, focusing on simplicity and efficiency. We don't delve into complex coding; we focus on user-friendly platforms and strategies that make technology work for you, not against you. You don't need to be a tech guru; you just need to know how to use the right tools effectively, and I'll show you how."

The key shift is from hourly rates to value-based pricing. This is important because a group program offers a transformation and outcome that is worth more than the sum of its hours. Value-based pricing reflects the significant benefits clients receive (e.g., community, structured curriculum, sustained support), allowing the coach to earn more for their expertise and time investment.

After stating your price, it's crucial to pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect time to process the information without feeling pressured or interrupted. The first person to speak often feels the need to fill the silence, which can lead to lowering the price or over-explaining. A confident pause demonstrates your belief in the value of your offer.
