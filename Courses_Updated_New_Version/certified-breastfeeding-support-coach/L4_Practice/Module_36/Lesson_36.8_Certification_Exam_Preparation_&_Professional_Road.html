Your Ideal Prospect: Understanding Who You Serve

Before you even pick up the phone, understanding your ideal client is paramount. For a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach, this often means a parent who is feeling overwhelmed, exhausted, and perhaps a bit defeated by their breastfeeding journey. They've likely tried various solutions and are seeking not just advice, but genuine support and a personalized plan. Let's meet our prospect for today's practice call.

üë©

Eleanor, 42

Found you through a local mom's group referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
First-time mom, 6 weeks postpartum. Struggling with painful latch, low milk supply concerns, and extreme nipple soreness. Feeling constant anxiety and guilt.
Budget Concern
"My partner and I are on a tight budget with the new baby. I've heard coaches can be expensive, and I'm not sure if we can afford another expense right now."
Decision Style
Needs reassurance and empathy. Values practical solutions and a clear path forward. Skeptical of generic advice, as she's received a lot already. Wants to feel understood and supported.
Her Goal
"I just want to breastfeed without pain and feel confident I'm giving my baby enough. I truly want this to work, but I'm losing hope."
Coach Tip

Always do a quick "pre-call" review of any information the client provided when booking. Even a few notes about their stated challenge or how they found you can help you tailor your opening and show you've already invested in understanding them. This builds instant rapport.

Your Discovery Call Blueprint: A 30-Minute Structure

A well-structured discovery call isn't about rigid scripting, but about having a clear framework that allows you to guide the conversation effectively, ensuring you cover all essential points while remaining authentic and client-centered. Here‚Äôs a blueprint for a typical 30-minute call:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into your specific situation, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you. How did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] referred you. I truly appreciate that. So Eleanor, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with your breastfeeding journey?"
Phase 2: Uncover Needs & Deepen Understanding 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Eleanor. It sounds incredibly challenging. Can you tell me more about the painful latch? When did it start, and how does it feel? And what are your concerns about your milk supply?"
YOU:
"I hear the frustration in your voice. What have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, and what felt like it didn't make a difference?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself and your baby's feeding journey in a month, or six months? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and achieve your ideal breastfeeding experience, what would that look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Solution & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a dedicated mom struggling with painful latch, worried about supply, and feeling overwhelmed. You want to breastfeed comfortably and confidently. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use is different from what you might have experienced before. As a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach, I don't just offer generic advice. We work together over a structured period ‚Äì typically an 8 or 12-week program ‚Äì to get to the root causes of your challenges. This includes a comprehensive assessment, personalized adjustments for latch and positioning, and a tailored plan to build your confidence and address supply concerns. My clients often report significant improvement in comfort and confidence within the first 2-3 weeks."
YOU:
"My goal is to empower you with the knowledge and practical skills to breastfeed successfully and joyfully, without the pain and anxiety you're currently experiencing. We'll focus on your unique situation, providing consistent support, and celebrating every milestone."
Phase 4: Confident Closing & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on what I've described about my coaching approach, does this sound like the kind of personalized support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Great! If you feel this is a good fit, I'd love to share more about my signature program and how we can get started. Are you open to hearing about the details now?"
Coach Tip

Remember that you are not just selling a service; you are offering a solution to a significant problem in a parent's life. Approach the call with genuine empathy and a desire to help. Your confidence in your abilities and your process will naturally shine through, making the "sale" feel like a natural progression of the conversation.

Handling Objections Like a Pro

It's natural for potential clients to have reservations. Your ability to address these objections with empathy, clarity, and confidence is a key indicator of your professionalism and commitment. Here are common objections and how to navigate them:

Common Objections & Confident Responses

"It's too expensive / I'm not sure we can afford this right now."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. Investing in support for yourself and your baby is a significant decision. Many of my clients initially feel this way. What they often find is that the cost of NOT getting support ‚Äì the ongoing pain, the anxiety, the potential for early weaning, or even costly formula ‚Äì far outweighs the investment in a personalized program. My goal is to help you achieve your breastfeeding goals sustainably, which can actually save you money and emotional energy in the long run. We can discuss payment plan options too. Does that help address your concern?"
"I need to talk to my partner about this."
"Absolutely, this is a family decision, and it's important that your partner is on board. What specific questions or concerns do you anticipate your partner might have that I could help you address now, so you feel fully prepared for that conversation? Perhaps we could even schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you to ensure all questions are answered?"
"I've tried everything, and nothing has worked."
"I hear that, and it sounds incredibly frustrating to have put in so much effort without seeing the results you hoped for. That's precisely why my approach is different. We don't just try generic solutions; we delve deep into your unique physiology, baby's cues, and your specific challenges to create a truly personalized plan, often incorporating elements you haven't considered. My certification ensures I bring an advanced, evidence-based perspective. What specifically have you tried that left you feeling like nothing worked?"
"I'm not sure if coaching is really what I need / I don't know if this will work."
"That's a very valid concern. Many parents aren't familiar with the depth of support a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach provides. Unlike quick appointments, my programs offer ongoing, consistent guidance, troubleshooting, and emotional support. We work together to build your confidence and implement practical strategies. My clients typically see significant progress because of this sustained, personalized attention. If you're ready to commit to making a change, I'm confident we can work towards your goals."
Coach Tip

When handling objections, always validate the client's feeling first ("I understand," "That's a valid concern"). Then, reframe their objection by reiterating your value or offering a solution. Never argue or dismiss their concerns.

The Art of Confident Pricing

Presenting your prices can feel daunting, but it's an essential part of the discovery call. Your confidence in your value directly impacts how the client perceives your investment. Always present your prices clearly, concisely, and with conviction. Avoid apologies or hesitation.

Structuring Your Offering

Most successful coaches offer packages rather than hourly rates. This provides comprehensive support and signals a commitment to results. For example:

  • The "LATCH & Learn" Starter Package (4 weeks): Ideal for new moms needing foundational support. Includes initial comprehensive assessment, 3 follow-up sessions, and unlimited messaging support.
  • The "Empowered Feeding" Signature Program (8 weeks): For more complex challenges or sustained support. Includes initial assessment, 6 follow-up sessions, comprehensive resources, and priority messaging.
  • The "Thrive & Flourish" Premium Program (12 weeks): For ongoing, in-depth support, especially for return-to-work transitions or long-term goals. Includes initial assessment, 9 follow-up sessions, advanced resources, and bespoke support.

Example Pricing Presentation

After Eleanor expresses interest in moving forward:

YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've shared, I believe the Empowered Feeding Signature Program would be the best fit for you. This 8-week program is designed to address painful latch, build your supply confidence, and provide the consistent support you need to feel truly empowered. The investment for this program is $1,200. This includes a comprehensive initial consultation, six personalized follow-up sessions, access to my exclusive resource library, and unlimited messaging support between sessions. I also offer a flexible payment plan of two installments of $600."

Then, pause. Let the client absorb the information. Your next move is to listen for their response, which may be another objection or a move towards commitment.

Coach Tip

Always state the value before the price. Explain what's included and the transformation they'll experience. This frames the price as an investment in a solution, not just a cost. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels completely natural and confident.

Case Study: Maria's Scaling Success

From Part-Time Passion to Thriving Full-Time Practice

Maria, 48

Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach, former high school teacher.

Presenting Situation: Maria, a former high school teacher, completed her AccrediPro certification at age 46, driven by a passion to support new mothers. She initially started her coaching practice part-time, working with 2-3 clients a month, generating around $2,500-$3,500 monthly. While fulfilling, she felt limited by her time and desired to fully replace her previous income of $60,000/year.

Intervention: Maria focused on refining her discovery call process, practicing objection handling, and confidently presenting her 12-week "Nourish & Nurture" signature program ($1,500). She leveraged her teaching skills to create a small group workshop on "Returning to Work While Breastfeeding," priced at $297 per participant. She actively sought referrals from local pediatricians and doulas, and optimized her online presence based on AccrediPro's marketing modules.

Outcomes: Within 18 months, Maria transitioned to full-time coaching. Her client load for one-on-one programs increased to an average of 6-7 clients per month, bringing in $9,000-$10,500. Her quarterly group workshops consistently attracted 8-10 participants, adding another $2,376-$2,970 per quarter. Her annual gross income now comfortably exceeds $120,000, providing her with the financial freedom and flexibility she desired, while making a profound impact on countless families. Her success was largely attributed to her mastery of the discovery call and confident pricing strategy.

Coach Tip

Your journey is unique, but the principles of building a successful practice are universal. Embody confidence, practice your conversations, and remember the life-changing impact you have. Every "yes" on a discovery call is a step towards realizing your vision and helping a family thrive.

Action Plan: Practice Makes Perfect

The best way to master your discovery calls is through practice. Here‚Äôs your action plan:

  1. Role-Play: Find a fellow student, friend, or even practice in front of a mirror. Play both the coach and the client. Record yourself and listen back for areas of improvement.
  2. Refine Your Summary: Practice summarizing the client's pain points and goals in a clear, concise, and empathetic way.
  3. Memorize Your Value Proposition: Be able to articulate what makes your coaching unique and valuable without hesitation.
  4. Practice Objection Handling: Go through the common objections listed above and confidently deliver your responses out loud.
  5. State Your Price: Practice stating your package price and payment options confidently, without apology or hesitation.
  6. Develop Your Closing Lines: Have 2-3 confident closing statements ready to guide the client towards the next step.

Remember, every call is a learning opportunity. Embrace the process, stay client-centered, and trust in the incredible value you bring as a Certified Breastfeeding Support Coach.

Check Your Understanding

When Eleanor (the prospect) brings up budget concerns during the discovery call, what is the most appropriate initial response?

The most appropriate initial response is to validate her concern ("I completely understand, investing in support is a significant decision") and then gently reframe the value of your services by explaining how the investment can prevent greater costs (emotional, financial) in the long run. Offering payment plans can also be a helpful next step.
2

During the "Uncover Needs" phase, what is a key question to ask that helps a client visualize their desired future state?

A key question is: "If you COULD wave a magic wand and achieve your ideal breastfeeding experience, what would that look like for you?" This helps them articulate their vision and connects their current pain to your potential solution.
3

What is the primary purpose of offering coaching in packages rather than hourly rates?

The primary purpose is to provide comprehensive and sustained support, which leads to better client outcomes. It also signals a commitment to results, simplifies pricing, and often leads to higher client satisfaction and retention compared to ad-hoc hourly sessions.
4

According to the lesson, what is a critical action step for coaches to take to gain confidence in presenting their prices?

A critical action step is to practice saying your prices out loud repeatedly until it feels completely natural and confident. This builds muscle memory and reduces hesitation during actual calls.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured, empathetic conversation designed to understand client needs and present your value.
  • Building rapport, actively listening, and asking powerful questions are crucial for uncovering deep client pain points.
  • Confidently summarize the client's situation and present your coaching solution as the tailored answer to their specific challenges.
  • Anticipate and gracefully handle objections by validating feelings, reframing value, and offering solutions.
  • Present your package pricing with conviction, focusing on the transformation and long-term investment rather than just the cost.
  • Consistent practice and self-reflection are essential for mastering your discovery calls and building a thriving coaching business.
