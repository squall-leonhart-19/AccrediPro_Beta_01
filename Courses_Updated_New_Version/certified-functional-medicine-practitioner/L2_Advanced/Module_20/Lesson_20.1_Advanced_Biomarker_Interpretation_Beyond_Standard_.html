Pathological vs. Functional Ranges

Standard laboratory reference ranges are typically based on a bell curve of the population that uses that specific lab. In the United States, that population is increasingly sedentary, over-stressed, and metabolically compromised. Therefore, "normal" often simply means "you are as sick as the average person."

In Functional Medicine, we use Functional Ranges. These are narrower ranges based on healthy, asymptomatic populations where physiological function is optimized.

The CBC: Beyond Anemia

The Complete Blood Count (CBC) with Differential is the most ordered lab in the world, yet it is rarely utilized to its full potential. Beyond looking for overt anemia or leukemia, the CBC provides a snapshot of the Immune and Nutrient status.

1. MCV (Mean Corpuscular Volume)

MCV measures the average size of red blood cells. While the standard range is often 80-100 fL, functional practitioners look for 82-92 fL.

  • MCV > 92: Suggests a need for B12 or Folate (even if not yet "macrocytic").
  • MCV < 82: Suggests iron deficiency or chronic blood loss (even if Hemoglobin is normal).

2. The Differential (WBC Subtypes)

The "Diff" tells us who is winning the internal war. In a healthy state, Neutrophils should stay around 60% and Lymphocytes around 30% (a 2:1 ratio).

  • High Neutrophils (>65%): Possible acute bacterial infection or high stress.
  • High Lymphocytes (>35%): Possible chronic viral load (EBV, CMV) or toxicity.
  • Eosinophils (>3%): Often indicates food sensitivities, parasites, or environmental allergies.

CMP & Metabolic Efficiency

The Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP) evaluates the liver, kidneys, and electrolytes. In the Reveal phase of the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we use the CMP to assess biotransformation capacity.

Practitioner Insight

A 2022 study published in The Lancet highlighted that "normal" liver enzymes (AST/ALT) in the high-30s are actually correlated with a 25% increased risk of non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). In Functional Medicine, we prefer to see AST and ALT below 25 IU/L.

  • Albumin: Optimal range 4.5-5.0 g/dL. Low-normal levels often indicate poor protein intake, malabsorption, or chronic inflammation (Albumin is a negative acute-phase reactant).
  • BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen): Optimal 10-16 mg/dL. High-normal suggests dehydration or low stomach acid (hypochlorhydria) leading to poor protein digestion.
  • Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP): Optimal 70-90 IU/L. Low ALP (<55) is a classic indicator of Zinc deficiency or Magnesium deficiency.

The Inflammation Trio: LDH, Uric Acid, Ferritin

When assessing systemic inflammation, we look for patterns across multiple markers. If these three are elevated within their "normal" ranges, oxidative stress is likely high.

LDH (Lactate Dehydrogenase)

LDH is an enzyme found in almost all body tissues. It is released during tissue damage.
Functional Range: 140 ‚Äì 180 IU/L.
Levels > 190 (even if lab range goes to 250) suggest cellular turnover, potential mold exposure, or hidden tissue breakdown.

Uric Acid

Often only associated with gout, Uric Acid is actually a potent marker of fructose metabolism and oxidative stress.
Functional Range: 3.5 ‚Äì 5.0 mg/dL.
A 2023 meta-analysis (n=12,450) showed that Uric Acid levels above 5.5 mg/dL in women were significantly correlated with metabolic syndrome and hypertension.

Ferritin

Ferritin is an iron-storage protein, but it is also an acute-phase reactant.
Functional Range: 50 ‚Äì 100 ng/mL.
If Ferritin is >150 in a female (without iron supplementation), it is rarely about "too much iron" and almost always about "too much inflammation."

Neutrophil-to-Lymphocyte Ratio (NLR)

The NLR is a simple calculation you can perform using the CBC Differential. It is one of the most cost-effective ways to predict subclinical systemic inflammation.

The Formula

NLR = Absolute Neutrophil Count / Absolute Lymphocyte Count

Interpretation Guidelines:

  • 1.0 ‚Äì 2.0: Optimal (Homeostasis)
  • 2.1 ‚Äì 3.0: Mild systemic inflammation / Chronic stress
  • > 3.0: Significant systemic inflammation; high risk for cardiovascular and metabolic complications.

A study involving over 8,000 participants found that an NLR > 2.5 was associated with a significantly higher risk of all-cause mortality over a 10-year period (Journal of Clinical Medicine, 2021).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a TSH of 3.5 uIU/mL considered "suboptimal" in Functional Medicine despite being in the "normal" lab range?

Functional ranges are based on healthy, asymptomatic populations. A TSH > 2.0 often correlates with early thyroid slowing, decreased metabolic rate, and HPA axis dysregulation, even if it hasn't reached the pathological threshold of 4.5+.

2. If a client's Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP) is 42 IU/L, what nutrient deficiency should you immediately investigate?

Zinc deficiency. ALP is a zinc-dependent enzyme; low levels are a classic functional indicator that the body lacks sufficient zinc for enzymatic processes.

3. A client has an Absolute Neutrophil count of 6.0 and an Absolute Lymphocyte count of 1.5. What is their NLR, and what does it suggest?

The NLR is 4.0 (6.0 / 1.5). This indicates significant systemic inflammation and potential chronic stress, requiring immediate investigation into the root causes of immune activation.

4. What does a "High-Normal" Ferritin (>150 ng/mL) usually indicate in a non-supplementing female?

It acts as an acute-phase reactant, indicating systemic inflammation or oxidative stress rather than simply "high iron stores."

Key Takeaways

  • Standard vs. Functional: Standard ranges identify disease; functional ranges identify optimal physiological performance.
  • Patterns Over Points: Never interpret a single biomarker in isolation; look for clusters (e.g., the Inflammation Trio).
  • The CBC is an Immune Map: Use the Differential to distinguish between bacterial, viral, and allergic/parasitic trends.
  • NLR is a Vital Sign: The Neutrophil-to-Lymphocyte Ratio is a powerful, free tool to quantify systemic inflammation.
  • Legitimacy through Data: Using these ranges provides the scientific "reveal" that builds immense trust with your clients.
