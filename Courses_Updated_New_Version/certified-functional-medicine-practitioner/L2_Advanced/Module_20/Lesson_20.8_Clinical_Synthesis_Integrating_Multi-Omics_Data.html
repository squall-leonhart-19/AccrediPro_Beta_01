Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's ferritin of 18 a "Priority #1" intervention?

Iron is a required cofactor for the enzyme Thyroid Peroxidase (TPO). Without adequate iron, the body cannot produce thyroid hormone effectively, and the conversion of T4 to T3 is impaired. Additionally, low iron causes fatigue that mimics and worsens Hashimoto's symptoms.

2. What does Elena's HbA1c of 5.8 indicate in a functional context?

While conventionally "pre-diabetic," functionally it indicates significant Insulin Resistance. This state increases systemic inflammation and elevates cortisol, both of which inhibit the conversion of T4 to the active T3 hormone.

3. Why is "Night Sweats" listed as a referral trigger?

While night sweats can be perimenopausal, they are also a "B-symptom" in oncology (e.g., lymphoma) and can indicate serious infection. A practitioner must ensure a medical doctor has ruled out these pathologies before assuming it is purely hormonal.

4. What is the clinical significance of Elena's "PRN Omeprazole" use?

Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs) like Omeprazole lower stomach acid, which is required to absorb iron, B12, and magnesium. Her low ferritin is likely exacerbated by this medication, and her reflux may actually be caused by *low* stomach acid or SIBO, not high acid.

Key Takeaways

  • Synthesis over Analysis: Don't just look at labs in isolation; look for the connections (e.g., Iron + Thyroid + Insulin).
  • Phased Approach: Stabilize the foundation (nutrients/sleep) before moving to advanced protocols like gut weeding or detox.
  • Scope Awareness: Always keep a high index of suspicion for red flags; your role is part of a collaborative care team.
