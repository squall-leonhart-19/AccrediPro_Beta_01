The "Metabolic Gridlock" Mechanism

In the functional medicine paradigm, we view the brain as the most energy-demanding organ in the body, consuming roughly 20% of total glucose despite being only 2% of body weight. When systemic insulin resistance develops, the blood-brain barrier (BBB) begins to downregulate insulin receptors.

This creates a paradox: blood glucose levels are high, but the neurons cannot transport that glucose into the cell for energy. This state of cerebral glucose hypometabolism leads to synaptic loss and the accumulation of beta-amyloid plaquesâ€”the brain's attempt to protect itself from oxidative stress.
