Check Your Understanding

1. Which marker on the GI-MAP is the most direct indicator of "Leaky Gut" or Intestinal Permeability?

Zonulin. High levels of fecal zonulin indicate that the tight junctions of the intestinal lining are opening, allowing substances to leak into the bloodstream.

2. Why is brain fog common in patients with intestinal permeability (Leaky Gut)?

This is due to Neuroinflammation. Bacterial toxins like LPS cross the leaky gut, enter the bloodstream, and can cross the blood-brain barrier, activating microglia (the brain's immune cells).

3. What is the clinical significance of "Molecular Mimicry" in Sarah's case?

Molecular mimicry occurs when the immune system confuses foreign proteins (like gluten or certain bacteria) with the body's own tissues (like the thyroid) because their amino acid sequences are similar.

4. How does Selenium assist in Hashimoto's management?

Selenium is a cofactor for glutathione peroxidase, which protects the thyroid from oxidative stress, and it has been clinically shown to reduce TPO antibody levels.
