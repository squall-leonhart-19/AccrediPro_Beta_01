This brings us to a really important tool for your mentor toolkit: the Identify-Validate-Challenge framework, or IVC. Constructive feedback is an art form, especially with career changers like Lisa. For someone who has had a successful first career, like teaching, their identity is often very tied to being competent. If you give harsh criticism, you might accidentally shut down their clinical intuition because they’re so afraid of being "wrong."

So, we use IVC. First, you Identify. You specifically name what she did correctly. You might say, "Lisa, your initial assessment of the thyroid labs was spot on. You identified the subclinical markers perfectly, and your gluten-free recommendation was the right foundational move. You got the basics exactly right." This lowers her defenses.

Next, you Validate. You normalize the difficulty of the situation. You say, "It is incredibly common for clients to feel 'raced' or anxious when we support the thyroid without looking at the adrenals first. In fact, I’ve had this happen in my own practice several times early on. It’s a tricky balance to strike." Now she doesn't feel like a failure; she feels like she’s just encountered a common professional hurdle.

Finally, you Challenge. This is where you push her to the next level of thinking. You ask, "Based on Maria's new symptoms of palpitations, what does that suggest about her sympathetic nervous system? What would happen if we paused the thyroid support and focused purely on the HPA axis for the next 30 days?"
