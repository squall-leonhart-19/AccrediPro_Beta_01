The "Observe, Reflect, Direct" Framework

As a supervisor, your goal is to build Lisa's clinical reasoning, not just give her a protocol to copy. We use a three-step framework for these sessions:

  1. Observe: Ask Lisa to present the facts. "What did you see? What did the client say?"
  2. Reflect: Ask Lisa to interpret. "What do you think is the underlying mechanism here? Why did you choose that initial recommendation?"
  3. Direct: Provide the missing piece. "Have you considered how Susan's perimenopause might be driving the insulin resistance we see here?"
Coach Tip #2

Avoid the "Fix-It" Trap. If you give Lisa the answer immediately, she remains dependent on you. If you guide her to the answer, she becomes a peer.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Reflect" stage in the supervision framework?

The goal is to encourage the mentee to interpret the data and explain their clinical reasoning, rather than just reciting facts or waiting for an answer.

2. Why is it important to validate a mentee's feelings of overwhelm?

Validation reduces the "threat response" in the brain, allowing the mentee to move out of "fight or flight" and back into "executive function" where they can actually learn and problem-solve.

3. If a mentee suggests a protocol that is outside their scope of practice, what is your responsibility?

You must firmly but kindly redirect them to their scope of practice, explaining the legal and ethical reasons why the recommendation is inappropriate and helping them find a "within-scope" alternative.

4. How does mentoring benefit the Master Practitioner (Mentor) financially?

It creates a diversified income stream (supervision fees) and establishes the mentor as a "Key Opinion Leader" (KOL) in the field, which often leads to higher-tier client referrals.

Key Takeaways

  • Mentoring is about guiding clinical reasoning, not just correcting protocols.
  • Use the Observe, Reflect, Direct model to structure your supervision sessions.
  • New practitioners often suffer from imposter syndrome; your role is to provide "clinical scaffolding" to build their confidence.
  • Mentoring represents a legacy phase in your career, offering both professional fulfillment and a premium revenue stream.
  • Always maintain professional boundaries while being a warm and encouraging "Sarah Mitchell" for your mentees.
