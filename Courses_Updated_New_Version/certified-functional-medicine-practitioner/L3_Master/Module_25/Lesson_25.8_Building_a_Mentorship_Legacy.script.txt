So, how do you actually do this? We use a three-step framework called Observe, Reflect, Direct. Your goal is to build Lisa’s clinical reasoning. You don’t want her to just copy your protocols. If she just does what you say, she isn't learning. She’s just a transcript. 

The first step is Observe. You ask Lisa to present the facts. What did you see? What did the client actually say? This forces Lisa to look at the data without the cloud of her own anxiety. The second step is Reflect. This is where the magic happens. You ask Lisa to interpret the data. You might ask, what do you think is the underlying mechanism here? Why did you choose that initial recommendation? You’re asking her to show her work, like a math teacher would. You want to see the logic she used. Even if the logic is flawed, you need to see how she got there.

The third step is Direct. This is where you finally provide the missing piece. You might say, have you considered how Susan’s perimenopause might be driving the insulin resistance we see here? You aren't giving her the answer on a silver platter. You’re pointing her in the right direction so she can make the final connection herself.
