Now, let’s talk about the scientific rationales for re-testing frequency. This is where a lot of practitioners get tripped up. If you re-test too early, the results might not show the full impact of your work, which leads to disappointment. If you re-test too late, you might miss a critical window where you needed to adjust the protocol. 

The frequency of your laboratory follow-ups must be grounded in biological half-lives. Take HbA1c, for example. I see coaches wanting to re-test this every four weeks. But that’s biologically futile. Red blood cells have an average lifespan of one hundred and twenty days. You need at least three months to see a true shift in that marker. On the flip side, something like hs-CRP can shift within fourteen to twenty-one days of a significant dietary intervention. 

In our premium programs, we teach a tiered re-testing schedule. In the early phase, which is weeks four to six, we look at those high-sensitivity inflammation markers like CRP. We might also look at hydration and basic electrolytes, especially if the client is using a ketogenic or detox protocol. In the mid-phase, around weeks twelve to sixteen, that’s when we bring back the glycemic markers like HbA1c, lipid sub-fractions, and nutrient status like Vitamin D or B12. And for the deep tissue or gut work, we usually wait six to nine months. You shouldn’t be re-testing a GI-MAP or an Organic Acids Test every month. It takes time to eradicate dysbiosis and restore those metabolic pathways.
