Biochemical Sequencing: The "Drainage First" Rule

One of the most common mistakes in detoxification programming is starting with Phase I Biotransformation (induction of P450 enzymes) before ensuring Phase III Elimination (drainage) is functional. This results in the "re-circulation" of toxins, often in a more reactive, intermediate state.

The Three Phases of Detoxification

  1. Phase I (Modification): Enzymes (CYP450) transform fat-soluble toxins into intermediate metabolites. Requirement: B-Vitamins, Glutathione.
  2. Phase II (Conjugation): Toxins are attached to a carrier (sulfate, glycine, glucuronide) to become water-soluble. Requirement: Amino acids, Sulfur.
  3. Phase III (Elimination/Drainage): The transport of water-soluble toxins out of the cell and out of the body via bile, urine, and sweat.

The Clinical Rule: You must open the "exit doors" (Phase III) before you start the "cleaning crew" (Phase I/II). If a client is constipated, has stagnant lymph, or is dehydrated, a detox protocol will make them sicker.

Biotoxin Illness (CIRS) Protocols
