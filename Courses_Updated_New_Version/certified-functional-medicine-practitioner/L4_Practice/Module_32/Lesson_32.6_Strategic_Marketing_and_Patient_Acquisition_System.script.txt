While digital marketing is incredibly powerful, we cannot overlook the power of referral networks. Reciprocal referral networks often provide the highest quality patients with the lowest acquisition cost. The goal here is to become the functional specialist for your local conventional doctors.

I call this the Reciprocity Protocol. The first step is to identify the gap. Conventional MDs are often very frustrated by patients who have vague symptoms like chronic fatigue or IBS. These doctors often don't have the time or the tools to manage these lifestyle-heavy cases. They want these patients to get better, but they can't do it in a ten-minute appointment.

The second step is professional outreach. Please, do not go in and ask for referrals first. That is the quickest way to get a door closed in your face. Instead, send a professional letter. Introduce your clinical focus. Offer to take those lifestyle-heavy cases off their plate. Tell them you are there to support their patients with the deep-dive work that they don't have time for.

The third step is to close the loop. This is the most important part. When a doctor refers a patient to you, and you have the patient’s consent, you must send a professional Clinical Summary Report back to that doctor. Show them your findings. Show them the protocol you are using. When a conventional doctor sees a report that is clinically rigorous and evidence-based, it changes how they see you. It demonstrates your clinical rigor and ensures they keep referring to you.

When you are speaking to these MDs, you have to use their language. This is not the time to talk about vibrations or energy. You need to talk about HPA axis dysregulation. Now, let's talk about the money side of things. At the Level Four level, you have to treat your marketing budget like an investment portfolio. If you put one dollar into an ad, you should expect five to ten dollars back in program fees. To do this, you have to track your metrics.

There are three key metrics you need to know. First is CPL, or Cost Per Lead. This is how much it costs you to get an email address. In our industry, a good target is between three and ten dollars. If it is higher than that, your messaging might be too broad or your offer might not be compelling enough.

Second is CPA, or Cost Per Acquisition. This is how much it costs you to actually get a paying client. For a three-thousand-dollar program, you should aim for a CPA between two hundred and six hundred dollars. If you spend six hundred dollars to make three thousand, that is a very healthy business.

Third is ROAS, or Return on Ad Spend. This is your total revenue divided by your total ad spend. This tells you the overall efficiency of your marketing.

When it comes to where to spend your money, Meta—which is Facebook and Instagram—is still the leader for what we call interruption marketing. This is where you target people based on their interests. Google Search, on the other hand, is superior for intent marketing. This is where you target people who are actively searching for things like functional medicine near me. Both have their place, but Meta is often better for scaling high-ticket programs because you can find people before they even know they are looking for functional medicine.
