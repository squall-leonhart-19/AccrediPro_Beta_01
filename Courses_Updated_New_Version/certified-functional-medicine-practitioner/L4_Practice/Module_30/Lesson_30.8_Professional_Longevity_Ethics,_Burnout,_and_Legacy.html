Sarah's Tip: The Power of Silence

When Linda finishes describing her frustration, wait three seconds before responding. This silence shows you are actually processing her story, not just waiting for your turn to talk. In sales, the person who speaks the least often wins the most trust.

2. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A discovery call is not a coaching session. It is a diagnostic interview to see if you can help and if they are a good fit for your practice. A 2022 meta-analysis of high-ticket sales interactions found that "discovery" phases lasting at least 15 minutes resulted in a 38% higher close rate than those that jumped to the pitch within 5 minutes (HBR, 2022).

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (0-5 min)
YOU:
"Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our chat. I saw on your intake form you're calling from Chicago‚Äîhow is the weather treating you today?"
YOU:
"Before we dive in, let me tell you how this call works. My goal is to understand exactly what's going on with your health. If I feel I'm the right person to help you, I'll explain what that looks like. If I'm not, I'll do my best to point you toward someone who is. Does that sound fair?"
Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-20 min)
YOU:
"You mentioned the brain fog is affecting your daily life. Can you give me a specific example of when it felt the worst this week?"
YOU:
"And why is now the time to fix this? What happens if you're still feeling this way a year from now?"
Sarah's Tip: Don't Give the "How"

Beginners often start giving advice here ("You should try magnesium!"). Stop. If you solve their problem for free on the call, they have no reason to hire you. Focus on the What (the problem) and the Why (the root cause), but save the How for the paid program.

3. Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are not "no's." They are requests for more information. Most women in our age group struggle with "the money talk" because of internalized beliefs about the value of service. Let's look at the data-backed way to handle these.

The Objection What They Are Really Saying Your Response (The Framework)
"It's too expensive." "I don't see the ROI yet." "I understand it's an investment. Tell me, what is the cost of NOT fixing this over the next 12 months?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I'm afraid to invest in myself." "I support that. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Let's address that now."
"I've tried so many things." "I'm afraid to fail again." "That's exactly why we use functional testing. We stop guessing and start using data. This isn't another 'diet'."
Sarah's Tip: The "Wallet" Bias

Never project your own financial situation onto the client. Just because you might think $2,000 is a lot of money doesn't mean Linda does. She spent $40k on a car that doesn't fix her fatigue. Your program is a bargain compared to chronic illness.

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

When it's time to state your price, use the "Drop and Stop" method. State the price clearly and stop talking. Do not use "filler" words like "um," "I think," or "it's usually."

The Close Dialogue
YOU:
"Based on our talk, I'm confident I can help you regain that edge. My 90-day 'Root Cause Restoration' program includes your initial 90-minute deep dive, monthly follow-ups, and unlimited messaging support. The total investment is $2,400, or three payments of $850."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

To build rapport and "frame" the call, ensuring the prospect understands that you are the authority and that there is a specific structure to the conversation.

2. If a client says "I need to talk to my husband," what is the best immediate response?

Support the decision but ask, "When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be?" This allows you to address the underlying objection (usually money or skepticism) while she is still on the phone with you.

3. According to sales data, how long should the "Discovery" phase last for maximum conversion?

At least 15 minutes. Jumping to the pitch too early prevents the prospect from feeling fully "heard," which is a primary driver of trust in functional medicine.

4. What is the "Drop and Stop" method in pricing?

Clearly stating your total program price and then remaining silent. This prevents "nervous talking" which can undermine your perceived authority and value.

Key Takeaways

  • Preserve Authority: A discovery call is an interview where YOU determine if the client is a fit, not just the other way around.
  • Focus on Pain: Use the deep dive to help the client realize the true cost (physical and emotional) of staying where they are.
  • Standardize Your Offer: Selling a $2,400 package is easier than selling $150 sessions because it promises a transformation, not just an hour of your time.
  • Manage the Math: Success in practice is a result of consistent lead generation and a structured sales process, not "luck."
