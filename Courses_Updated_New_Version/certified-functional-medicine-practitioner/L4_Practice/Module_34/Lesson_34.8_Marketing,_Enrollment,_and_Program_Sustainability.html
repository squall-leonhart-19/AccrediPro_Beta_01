4. The "Value Stack" Pricing Presentation

One of the biggest mistakes practitioners make is apologizing for the price. Instead, use the Value Stack method to show how much they are receiving for a fraction of the 1:1 cost.

How to State the Price

"Linda, if we did this 1:1, the investment would be over $2,500 for the three months. Because we are doing this in a group format, you get the same curriculum, the same lab review sessions, and the community support for just one payment of $597 (or three payments of $225). Does that feel like a manageable investment for getting your energy back?"

Sarah's Secret

Always mention the 1:1 price first. It sets a "price anchor" in their mind, making the group price feel like an incredible deal (which it is!).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of the "Price Anchor" (mentioning 1:1 costs first)?

It establishes the high value of your expertise, making the group price seem like a significant discount for the same outcome, reducing "sticker shock."

2. How should you respond if a prospect says they are "too private" for a group?

Validate their concern, explain that sharing is optional, and highlight that they will benefit from hearing others' questions without having to disclose their own data.

3. Why is "Validation" the first phase of the enrollment script?

It builds rapport and helps the prospect feel "seen" and "heard," which is often the missing piece in their previous healthcare experiences.

4. True or False: You should wait until you have 20 people before launching your first group.

False. Starting with a small "Beta" group of 5-8 people allows you to refine your materials and get testimonials while earning a high hourly rate.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Sell the Container: People pay for the accountability and community as much as the clinical protocols.
  • Confidence is Currency: State your group price with the same authority as your 1:1 price.
  • Intimacy Scales: You don't need hundreds of people to have a successful group; 10-12 committed participants create a thriving practice.
  • The Bridge Technique: Always connect a prospect's specific pain point to a specific week in your curriculum.
