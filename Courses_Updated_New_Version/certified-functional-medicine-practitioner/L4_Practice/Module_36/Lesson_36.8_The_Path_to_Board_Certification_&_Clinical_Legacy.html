The Psychology of the Discovery Call

A discovery call is not a sales pitch; it is a diagnostic interview for the relationship. Your goal is to determine if the client is a good fit for your methodology and if you are the right person to help them achieve their goals. Research shows that practitioners who follow a structured enrollment process have a 42% higher conversion rate than those who "wing it."

Sarah's Tip

Stop trying to "sell" and start trying to "serve." If you truly believe your functional approach can help them, it is your professional duty to lead them toward a decision.

Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are rarely about the money; they are about certainty. The client is asking: "Is this the thing that will finally work?"

The Objection The Reframing Response
"It's too expensive." "I understand. It is an investment. But let's look at the cost of not doing this. How much have you already spent on supplements and co-pays that didn't work?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I completely support that. What do you think he'll be most concerned aboutâ€”the cost, the time, or whether this will actually work for you?"
"I'm too busy right now." "I hear you. But will life be less busy in three months? If we start now, you'll have the energy to handle your busy life much better."
Sarah's Tip

Never lower your price on the call. If they have a genuine budget constraint, offer a payment plan (e.g., 3 payments of $875), but keep the total value of your expertise intact.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in the discovery call?

The goal is to understand the client's emotional "why," their desired future state, and the consequences of inaction. This builds the "value gap" that your program will eventually fill.

2. If a client says, "I've tried everything and nothing works," how should you respond?

Acknowledge their frustration first ("I hear how exhausting that has been"). Then, differentiate your approach by explaining that while they've tried "remedies," you provide a "system" based on functional data to find the root cause.

3. Why is a "Program Model" better than an "Hourly Model" for your practice?

The program model focuses on outcomes rather than time. It ensures client commitment (better results), provides predictable income for you, and positions you as a specialist rather than a commodity.

4. How should you handle the "Talk to my spouse" objection to keep the momentum?

Validate the need for the conversation, then ask what their spouse's specific concerns might be. Offer to send a "Spouse FAQ" or a summary of the call to help them explain the value of the investment.

Key Takeaways

  • Objections are Questions: View objections as a request for more certainty, not a rejection of your service.
