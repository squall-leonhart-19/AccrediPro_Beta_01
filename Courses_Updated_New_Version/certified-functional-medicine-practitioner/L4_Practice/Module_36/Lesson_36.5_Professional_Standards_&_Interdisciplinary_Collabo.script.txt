Next, I want to discuss the expert-partner dynamic. In traditional medicine, we often see the expert-patient model. It is very hierarchical. The doctor is the boss, and the patient follows orders. In the coaching world, we often see a peer-peer model. But as a professional functional practitioner, you want to land right in the middle. You want the expert-partner dynamic. 

In this model, you are the expert in the system. You understand the physiology and the protocols. But the client is the expert in their experience. They are the ones living in their body. This dynamic is essential for fostering self-efficacy. Self-efficacy is simply the client’s belief in their own ability to succeed. Research by Albert Bandura and others has shown that clients with high self-efficacy have a 60% higher rate of following their protocols. 

You don't need to have all the answers right away. That is a trap many new practitioners fall into. They feel like they have to be a walking encyclopedia. But you don't.
