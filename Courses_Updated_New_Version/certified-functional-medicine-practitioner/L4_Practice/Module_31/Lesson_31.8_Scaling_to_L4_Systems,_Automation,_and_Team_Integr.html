1. The Mindset of Service vs. Sales

Many practitioners struggle with "sales" because they feel like they are asking for something. In Functional Medicine, we must flip that script. You are not taking their money; you are offering a solution to a problem that is costing them their quality of life, their productivity, and perhaps even their future.

Sarah's Insight

If you knew you had the cure for someone's chronic pain, would you be "embarrassed" to tell them about it? Of course not. Your program is the bridge between where they are and where they want to be. Selling is an act of service.

Traditional "Sales" Approach Service-Based Enrollment

Convincing the prospect to buy Determining if the prospect is a good fit
Talking 70% of the time Listening 70% of the time
Handling objections with "rebuttals" Handling objections with empathy and clarity

3. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This structure ensures you stay in control of the conversation while making the prospect feel heard. Goal: 30 Minutes Total.

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (3 Minutes)
YOU:
"Hi Diane! It‚Äôs so great to finally connect. I‚Äôve been looking forward to our call. Phase 2: The Deep Dive (Discovery) (12 Minutes)
YOU:
"Tell me, Diane... what was the 'breaking point' that made you schedule this call today? What is the #1 thing you want to change?"
YOU:
"And how is that fatigue affecting your life outside of work? What is it costing you?" (Wait for the emotional answer).
Phase 3: The Gap & The Bridge (10 Minutes)
YOU:
"Based on what you‚Äôve told me, it sounds like your body is stuck in a 'stress-response' loop. Sarah's Insight

Always ask permission before sharing the price. When they say "Yes," they have mentally opened the door to the transaction. It shifts the power dynamic from you "pushing" to them "requesting."

4. Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are rarely about the money itself; they are about the perceived value or fear of failure. A 2022 survey of health coaching clients found that 84% of prospects who raised a price objection actually bought once the "long-term cost of inaction" was clarified.

Objection 1: "It‚Äôs too expensive."

The Response: "I completely understand that this is an investment. Let me ask‚Äîwhat is it costing you not to fix this? If we don't address this brain fog, what does your career look like in 12 months? My goal is to make this the last money you ever have to spend on this problem."

Objection 2: "I need to talk to my husband."

The Response: "I love that you want his support! What do you think he‚Äôll be most concerned about? Is it the cost, or the time you'll be spending on yourself? Let‚Äôs make sure you have all the info he needs to see why this is so important for your family's future."

5. Confident Pricing Presentation

When you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak after the price is mentioned is usually the one who feels the most pressure. Let the silence do the work.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive" phase of the discovery call?

The goal is to understand the prospect's "pain" and "desire," and to help them realize the "cost of inaction"‚Äîwhat happens if they don't change their current path.

2. True or False: You should explain every single clinical detail of your program before mentioning the price.

False. Over-explaining clinical details (features) can lead to "analysis paralysis." Focus on the transformation and outcomes instead.

3. How should you handle the "I need to talk to my spouse" objection?

Validate their desire for support, then ask what the spouse's specific concerns might be. This helps the prospect prepare for that conversation and reinforces their own "why."

4. According to industry standards, what is a realistic conversion rate for discovery calls?

While it varies, a well-structured call typically converts at 30-50%. If you are below 20%, you likely need to refine your "Discovery" phase or your lead qualifying process.

Key Takeaways

  • Listen More, Talk Less: Aim for a 70/30 listening-to-talking ratio to build deep rapport.
  • Structure is Safety: Use the 4-phase script to ensure you lead the prospect toward a decision.
  • Price with Confidence: State your investment clearly and embrace the silence that follows.
  • Focus on ROI: Help the client see that health is an investment that pays dividends in every other area of life.
  • Start Small: Two clients a month at $1,500 creates a solid $36k/year foundation while you learn.
