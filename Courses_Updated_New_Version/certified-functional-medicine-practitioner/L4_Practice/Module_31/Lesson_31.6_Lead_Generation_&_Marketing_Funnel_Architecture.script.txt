Once someone clicks your ad, they land on your landing page. Think of this page as your 24/7 digital salesperson. Now, let's talk about the numbers. I know math isn't always the favorite part of the job for practitioners. But you cannot manage what you do not measure. If you want to scale to a six-figure practice, you must understand your numbers. This is how you move from being a struggling practitioner to being a true business owner.

There are four main numbers to track. First is your Cost Per Lead, or CPL. This is how much you pay for an email address. A good goal is between $3 and $8. Next is your Cost Per Call. This is what you pay to get someone on a discovery call. Ideally, you want this to be between $50 and $150. 

Then we have the Client Acquisition Cost, or CAC. You calculate this by taking your total ad spend and dividing it by your number of new clients. Your goal here is to keep this under 20% of your program price. Finally, there is your Return on Ad Spend, or ROAS. This is your total revenue divided by your total ad spend. You want to see a 3x to 5x return.

If your program is $3,000 and your acquisition cost is $300, you are essentially buying $3,000 for $300. When you see it that way, marketing stops being an expense. It becomes an investment in your mission. It is the vehicle that allows you to help more people.
