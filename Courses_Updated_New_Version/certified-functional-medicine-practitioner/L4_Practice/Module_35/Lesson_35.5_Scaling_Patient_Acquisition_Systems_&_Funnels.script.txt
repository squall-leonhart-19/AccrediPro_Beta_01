Now, let’s talk about the math. To scale a Level 4 practice, you must treat your marketing spend as an investment, not an expense. If you spend money on a dinner out, that money is gone. To know if this is working, you have to track two critical metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost, or CAC, and Lifetime Value, or LTV.

Your CAC is the total amount you spend on marketing divided by the number of new patients you get. Your LTV is the total revenue a patient generates over their entire relationship with you. In a healthy, scaling practice, you want your LTV to CAC ratio to be at least five to one. So, if it costs you six hundred dollars in ads to acquire one patient, but that patient enrolls in a three-thousand-dollar program, you have a sustainable and profitable model.
