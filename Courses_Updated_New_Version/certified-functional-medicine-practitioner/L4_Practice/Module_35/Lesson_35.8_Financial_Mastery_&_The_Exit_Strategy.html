The 30-Minute Enrollment Script

A professional discovery call is not a "chat." It is a structured clinical interview that transitions into a business proposal.

Phase 1: Rapport & Discovery(0-10 min)

You: "Diane, I‚Äôve reviewed your intake form. You mentioned that the brain fog is starting to affect your performance in board meetings. Tell me, on a scale of 1-10, how much is this impacting your quality of life right now?"

Diane: "It's an 8. I feel like I'm losing my mind some days."

You: "I hear you. And if we don't get this sorted out, where do you see your health‚Äîand your career‚Äîin twelve months?"

Phase 2: The Gap & The Root Cause(10-20 min)

You: "Based on what you've shared, it's clear why the 'normal' labs didn't help. They weren't looking at your HPA-axis dysfunction or your gut-brain connection. We need to stop guessing and start testing. Does it make sense why we need to look at the 'why' behind the fog, rather than just masking it?"

Phase 3: The Recommendation(20-25 min)

You: "Diane, I‚Äôm confident I can help you. I recommend my 90-day Executive Restoration Program. We‚Äôll do deep-dive functional testing, bi-weekly clinical sessions, and a custom protocol. We aren't just fixing sleep; we're restoring your metabolic edge."

Phase 4: The Investment(25-30 min)

You: "The investment for the 90-day comprehensive program is $3,500. We can do that in one payment, or we have a monthly installment plan. Which of those works best for your budgeting?"

Coach Tip

Notice the phrasing: "Which of those works best?" This is an assumptive close. You are assuming she wants to move forward and are simply helping her decide how to pay.

Mastering Objections with Confidence

Objections are not "nos." They are requests for more information or a manifestation of the prospect's fear of failure.

Objection The Underlying Fear Your Response (The Script)
"It's too expensive." "I'm afraid this won't work and I'll lose my money." "I understand. It is a significant investment. But let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not fixing this? If the weight gain and brain fog continue for another year, what will that cost you in medical bills or lost productivity?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I don't feel empowered to make this decision alone." "I completely respect that. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Is it the time I'll be taking from the family, or the financial investment?" (This identifies the real objection).
"Can I just buy the labs first?" "I want to dip my toe in without full commitment." "The labs are just data. The value is in the clinical interpretation and the 90 days of support to actually implement the changes. Data without a plan is just more confusion. Shall we get you started on the full path?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological goal of Phase 1 (Rapport & Discovery) in the enrollment call?

The goal is to establish the "Gap"‚Äîthe distance between where the client is (pain) and where they want to be (goal), and to confirm that the cost of staying the same is higher than the cost of the program.

2. Why is the "Assumptive Close" effective in a clinical setting?

It positions you as the authority and the leader. Clients who are sick or tired are often suffering from "decision fatigue." By assuming the next step, you provide the leadership and certainty they are looking for.

3. How should a practitioner respond when a client says, "I've already spent $5,000 on other doctors and I'm still sick"?

Acknowledge their frustration (empathy) and then pivot to the clinical difference: "I understand. Most of my clients have been through that. The reason they didn't succeed is they were using a 'symptom-suppression' model. We are using a 'systems-biology' model. We are looking at the soil, not just the leaves."

4. What is the main financial benefit of the Package Model over the Hourly Model?

The Package Model provides predictable cash flow, higher client commitment/compliance, and allows the practitioner to scale income without linearly increasing work hours.
Coach Tip

Your first 5 calls will be the hardest. Record them. Listen to them. You'll hear exactly where you lost confidence or where you talked too much. Sales is a clinical skill‚Äîpractice it with the same rigor you practice biochemistry.

Key Takeaways for Scaling

  • Sell Certainty, Not Coaching: High-level clients are buying the result (energy, clarity, vitality), not the number of sessions.
  • The 30-Minute Rule: Keep discovery calls focused. If you spend 60 minutes, you are likely "over-coaching" and giving away the solution for free, which lowers the incentive to enroll.
  • Objections are Opportunities: View an objection as a sign of interest. If they didn't care, they wouldn't bother objecting.
  • Price with Authority: State your investment clearly and then stop talking. The first person to speak after the price is stated is often the one who is most uncomfortable.
