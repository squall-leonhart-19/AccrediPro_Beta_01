The Co-Management Model

Many practitioners view conventional doctors as "competition." In reality, they are your best potential referral sources. The Co-Management Model positions you as the "lifestyle and root-cause specialist" who handles the 90% of health that happens outside the doctor's office.

Specialists like cardiologists, endocrinologists, and fertility experts are often frustrated because they lack the time to implement the R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢ with their patients. Licensing & White-Labeling Your Protocols

As you refine your practice, you will develop proprietary ways of applying the R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢ to specific niches (e.g., "The 90-Day Menopause Mastery Protocol"). This is Intellectual Property (IP). Scaling via licensing means allowing other practitioners or clinics to use your system in exchange for a fee.

Types of Licensing:

  • White-Labeling: Selling your digital course or curriculum to other coaches who put their own logo on it.
  • Affiliate Licensing: Allowing other practitioners to run your group program for a percentage of the revenue.
  • Clinical Licensing: Training other clinics' staff to implement your specific R.O.O.T.S. workflow.
Coach Tip: Protect Your Assets

Before licensing, ensure your protocols are documented in a standard operating procedure (SOP). Use a "Learning Management System" (LMS) to deliver the training so it remains consistent and scalable.

Affiliate & JV Strategies

Strategic alliances with lab companies and nutraceutical brands can create a significant passive revenue stream. However, in functional medicine, transparency is key. You should only partner with brands that meet the high clinical standards of the AccrediPro certification.

Joint Venture (JV) Opportunities:

  • Lab Bundling: Partnering with a lab company to create a "custom panel" unique to your practice, which they help market to their practitioner network.
  • Webinar Swaps: Guest speaking for a supplement brand's audience (practitioners or consumers) to drive traffic to your high-level certification or group programs.
  • Co-Branded Products: Developing a specific "R.O.O.T.S. Starter Kit" with a trusted supplement manufacturer.

National Authority Positioning

Public speaking and media appearances move you from "local practitioner" to "national authority." This allows you to command higher prices and attracts B2B partners who want to be associated with your brand.

The Authority Ladder:

  1. Local Authority: Speaking at local networking events or libraries.
  2. Digital Authority: Guesting on top-tier health podcasts (e.g., The Doctor's Farmacy, The Model Health Show).
  3. Industry Authority: Speaking at functional medicine conferences (IFM, A4M, etc.).
  4. National Authority: TV appearances and publishing a book with a major house.
Coach Tip: The "One-Sheet"

Create a professional "Speaker One-Sheet" that highlights your expertise in the R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢, your target audience, and 3-5 specific talk titles. This is your "business card" for media and event planners.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Corporate Wellness 2.0 more effective for scaling than general public marketing?

It targets high-value decision-makers (Executives) with a data-driven ROI, allowing for large multi-person contracts rather than individual sales cycles.

2. What is the primary benefit of the "Co-Management Model" for a practitioner?

It creates a "referral bridge" with conventional specialists, providing a steady stream of highly qualified leads while establishing professional legitimacy.

3. What is the difference between white-labeling and clinical licensing?

White-labeling allows others to put their branding on your content/products; clinical licensing allows others to use your specific workflow/methodology within their practice.

4. How does media positioning impact your pricing?

It increases perceived authority and "social proof," allowing you to shift from commodity pricing to authority-based premium pricing.
