Now, let's talk about the gold standard for risk mitigation. It’s called Shared Decision-Making, or SDM. This is a shift in mindset. In the old model of medicine, the practitioner was in a "command-and-control" position. They told the patient what to do, and the patient did it. In functional medicine, we move to a "collaborative partnership." 

The statistics on this are incredible. When a client is an active participant in choosing their protocol, they are forty percent less likely to pursue litigation if something goes wrong. Why? We use a specific framework for this. It starts with "Choice Talk." This is where you tell the client that there are multiple paths they could take. Finally, there is "Decision Talk." You let the client lead the final choice. You ask them, "Based on what we’ve discussed, which path feels right for you today?" First, you must provide written notice. I recommend sending this via certified mail so you have proof it was received. It should be a formal letter stating that the professional relationship is ending. You don't need to give a long, emotional explanation. Second, you have to offer a window for emergency care. Usually, this is a thirty-day window.
