Check Your Understanding

1. If a prospect asks, "Can you cure my Hashimoto's?", what is the most compliant response?

"As a Functional Medicine Practitioner, I don't 'cure' or 'treat' medical conditions. Instead, we look at the underlying physiological functions and lifestyle factors to help your body return to a state of balance and optimal function."

2. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) of the discovery call?

The goal is to understand the prospect's pain points, the emotional cost of their health issues, and the "Gap" between where they are and where they want to be. This builds the value for your solution.

3. How should you respond to the "I need to talk to my husband" objection?

Acknowledge and respect the decision-making process, then ask a clarifying question to uncover the root concern (e.g., "What do you think his biggest concern will be?") to help her navigate that conversation.

4. Why is the "Package Model" superior to the "Hourly Model" for functional practitioners?

It shifts the focus from "buying time" to "buying a result," ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see physiological changes, and provides the practitioner with predictable, sustainable income.

Key Takeaways

  • Rapport is King: People buy from those they trust and feel understood by. Spend the first 15 minutes listening.
  • Scope is Safety: Always pivot language from "treating disease" to "optimizing function" during the sales call.
  • Silence is Golden: State your price clearly and wait. Don't talk your way out of a sale by over-explaining.
  • Packages over Hours: Selling transformations instead of time leads to better client results and higher practitioner revenue.
