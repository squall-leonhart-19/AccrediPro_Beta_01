Now, let’s talk about where you are practicing. Regulatory frameworks vary wildly depending on your location. In the United States, we deal with a patchwork of state laws. They allow non-licensed practitioners to provide wellness services as long as you do two things: provide specific disclosures to your clients and avoid "prohibited acts" like surgery or prescribing drugs. If you are in a Green state, you have a clear path forward as long as you follow the disclosure rules.

Then we have the Yellow States. These are neutral or a bit unclear. In these states, the law might be silent on functional medicine, or it might depend heavily on how someone interprets "nutrition practice." If you are in a Yellow state, you must operate under very strict "Informed Consent" and "Disclaimer" protocols. You want to be incredibly transparent about what you are—and what you are not.

Finally, we have the Red States. These are the restrictive ones. They often have strict "Dietetics Practice Acts." In these states, the law might say that only a Registered Dietitian or a Medical Doctor can provide individualized nutrition counseling. If you are in a Red state, you have to be very careful about how you structure your business and how you market your services.
