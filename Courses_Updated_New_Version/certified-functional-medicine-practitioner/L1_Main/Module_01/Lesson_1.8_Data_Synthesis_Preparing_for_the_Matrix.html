Section 4: Success Markers

How do you know your "Reveal" session was successful? Look for these three signs:

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask Diane about her life transitions (like retirement) during the Reveal phase?

Life transitions are often "antecedents" or "triggers" in the Functional Medicine timeline. They represent periods of high physiological and psychological stress that can shift the body from a state of health to a state of chronic inflammation or dysfunction.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Reveal" conversation compared to a conventional medical intake?

While a conventional intake seeks to match a symptom to a drug, the Reveal conversation seeks to connect multiple symptoms across different systems to identify a common root cause (e.g., connecting joint pain, fatigue, and weight gain to systemic inflammation).

3. If a client like Diane says, "I've tried everything and nothing works," how should you respond?

Validate her frustration first. Then, explain that most "solutions" focus on the surface, while your work together will "Reveal" the underlying terrain that hasn't been addressed yet, making her previous efforts finally effective.

4. Which success marker indicates the highest level of practitioner-client rapport?

When the client shares a "secret" or sensitive symptom they omitted from their written intake. This indicates they feel safe and trust your clinical authority enough to be fully transparent.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • The "Reveal" phase is about connection, not just collection. You are connecting the dots of their life story.
  • Your voice and tone are clinical tools. Use a warm, calm, and authoritative tone to lower the client's stress response.
  • Avoid the "Fixer Trap"‚Äîdon't rush to suggest supplements before the client feels fully heard.
  • Practice out loud! The more you say these words, the more they become your natural professional language.
