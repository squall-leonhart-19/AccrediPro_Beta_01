Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my early years, I made the mistake of trying to sound "smart" by using too much science. Here is what to avoid:

The Mistake Why It Fails The Professional Correction
Over-explaining Cytokines Clients get "glazed eyes" and feel disconnected from the plan. Use analogies (security team, fire alarm, overflowing bucket).
Ignoring Stress Inflammation is often driven by the HPA-axis, not just food. Acknowledge her high-stress job as a primary "immune trigger."
Promising Instant Results Chronic inflammation takes time to "cool down." Set expectations: "We are cooling the fire, not just blowing out a candle."
Being Too Clinical Diane feels like a "patient" rather than a partner in her health. Use "we" and "us" language to build a collaborative bond.

Final Knowledge Check

1. Why is the "Security Team" analogy effective for explaining immunology to a client?

It shifts the narrative from the body "attacking itself" (which causes fear) to the body "protecting itself" (which fosters trust), while making complex cytokine activity easy to visualize.

Focusing only on diet while ignoring the fact that hormonal shifts (declining estrogen/progesterone) are significant drivers of immune dysregulation and "inflammaging."
