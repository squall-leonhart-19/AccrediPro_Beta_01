Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using too much jargon: Avoid saying "NF-kappaB up-regulation" to a client. Use "turning on the inflammation switch" instead.
  • Promising a "Cure": As Functional Medicine Practitioners, we support the body's function; we do not claim to "cure" autoimmune or inflammatory diseases.
  • Ignoring the Gut: 70-80% of the immune system is in the gut. If you talk about inflammation without mentioning the microbiome, you're missing the root.
  • Being Alarmist: High CRP is a marker for action, not a death sentence. Keep your energy grounded and solution-oriented.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Smoke Alarm" metaphor better than saying "Your immune system is attacking you"?

The "Smoke Alarm" metaphor implies the system is working correctly (trying to protect) but is over-sensitive. Saying the "body is attacking itself" creates a sense of betrayal and fear, which increases cortisol and further fuels inflammation.

2. Diane asks if she should take an "Immune Booster" supplement. How should you respond?

Explain that her immune system is already "over-active" (hyper-responsive). We want to "modulate" or "balance" it, not "boost" it further. Focus on resolving the triggers (like gut permeability) rather than just adding more stimulation.
