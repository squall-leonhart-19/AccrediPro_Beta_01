Common Optimization Mistakes

Even experienced coaches can stumble in the "O" phase. Watch out for these four pitfalls:

  • The "Supplement Shotgun": Adding 10 new supplements at once. (Optimize one system at a time).
  • Ignoring the "Reveal" Data: Forgetting that stress (cortisol) can "un-optimize" even the best diet.
  • Settling for "Better": Allowing the client to stop the program because they feel 50% better, rather than 100% optimized.
  • Over-complicating: Using too much "science-speak" that confuses the client instead of empowering them.

    Check Your Understanding

    1. Why is the "Optimize" phase often the most high-value part of a coaching program?

    Because it addresses the "grey area" where clients are told they are healthy by conventional standards but still feel sub-optimal. This is where personalized, data-driven coaching provides the most visible results in vitality and performance.

    2. What is a "Functional Range" compared to a "Pathological Range"?

    A pathological range (conventional) identifies disease states. A functional range (ours) identifies the narrow window where the body operates with the least amount of stress and the most efficiency.

    3. How should you respond if a client says, "I'm already eating healthy, why do I need to optimize?"

    Validate their effort, then explain that "eating healthy" is general, but "optimization" is bio-individual. Itâ€™s about ensuring their specific cells are getting exactly what they need based on their unique biology and life stage.

    4. Which analogy is best for explaining the Optimize phase to a client?

    The "High-Performance Car" or "Tuning an Instrument" analogies work best because they imply that the foundation is already there, but fine-tuning is required for the best "sound" or "speed."
