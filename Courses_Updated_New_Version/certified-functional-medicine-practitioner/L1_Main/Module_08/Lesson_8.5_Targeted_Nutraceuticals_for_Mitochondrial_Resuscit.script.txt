So, let’s start with the first big tool in our kit: Coenzyme Q10, specifically Ubiquinol. CoQ10 is a fat-soluble antioxidant. You can find it in every cell membrane in the body, but it is most concentrated in the inner membrane of the mitochondria. Think of it as a critical shuttle. Its job is to move electrons from Complexes One and Two over to Complex Three in the electron transport chain. Without enough CoQ10, that conveyor belt of energy production just grinds to a halt. Now, as a practitioner, you have to know the difference between the two forms of CoQ10. There’s Ubiquinone and Ubiquinol. Ubiquinone is the oxidized form. It’s been around for a long time and is generally cheaper. But to use it, the body has to convert it into Ubiquinol, which is the reduced, active form. As we age, or when we’re under a lot of oxidative stress, our ability to make that conversion drops significantly.

If your client is under 35 or 40 and relatively healthy, Ubiquinone might be fine for general wellness. Now, we have to talk about NAD+. You’ve probably heard a lot about this lately. Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide is essentially the currency of cellular communication.
