Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological goal of the "Sustain" phase for a client like Sandra?

The primary goal is to build self-efficacyâ€”the client's belief in their own ability to maintain their health results without constant supervision.

2. Why are "Anchor Habits" superior to "Full Adherence" in the maintenance phase?

Full adherence is often unsustainable long-term and leads to burnout. Anchor habits identify the highest-leverage actions that provide the most protection for the least effort during times of stress.

3. If a client is "terrified" of finishing the intensive program, what is the best first coaching response?

Validation. Acknowledge that the fear is normal and actually a positive sign that they value their health, then pivot to the empowerment of the Sustainability Plan.

4. True or False: You should stop all supplement recommendations during the Sustain phase.

False. You should transition from "therapeutic/repair" doses to "maintenance/support" doses, simplifying the regimen to ensure long-term compliance.

Key Takeaways

  • The "Sustain" phase is about transitioning from repair to resilience.
  • Anchor Habits act as a physiological safety net during periods of high stress.
  • Success in this phase is measured by the client's ability to self-correct without shame.
  • Evolving your role from "Coach" to "Consultant" empowers the client and creates a sustainable business model for you.
  • You are becoming a real coach when you can let go of the client's hand and watch them walk confidently on their own.
