Common Mistakes in Immune Coaching

Even experienced nurses and teachers make these mistakes when they first pivot into functional coaching:

  • Mistake 1: Over-Supplementing. Adding more "immune support" to an already reactive system often triggers more inflammation.
  • Mistake 2: Using War Imagery. Words like "fight," "attack," and "defense" reinforce the client's internal stress state.
  • Mistake 3: Ignoring the "Drain." Focusing only on what to *remove* from the bucket (gluten, dairy) without focusing on how to *drain* it (sleep, vagus nerve tone).
  • Mistake 4: Taking the "Attack" Literally. Explaining that the body is "destroying itself" rather than explaining it as a "miscommunication."

Check Your Understanding

1. A client wants to take a high-dose Echinacea supplement to "strengthen" her Hashimoto's. What is the functional coaching response?

Explain that in autoimmunity, the immune system is already "strong" (overactive). Adding a stimulant like Echinacea can sometimes "fuel the fire." Suggest focusing on immunomodulators (like Vitamin D or Omega-3s) that balance the system instead.

2. What does "draining the bucket" mean in a lifestyle context?

It means supporting the body's natural regulatory mechanisms: deep sleep, lymphatic drainage, stress reduction (vagus nerve support), and nutrient density that supports T-regulatory cell function.

3. Why is Elena's joint pain "moving around"?

This is often a sign of systemic cytokine circulation. The inflammation isn't localized to a "broken" joint; it's a systemic immune signal manifesting where the "overflow" happens to land that day.

4. What is the primary goal of the first immune coaching session?

To establish psychological safety and shift the narrative from "body betrayal" to "body protection," reducing the stress response that drives immune dysregulation.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Regulation Over Stimulation: Always prioritize "calming" the immune system over "boosting" it in cases of dysregulation.
  • Metaphor is Medicine: Use the "Immune Bucket" to make complex immunology accessible and less scary for the client.
  • The Vagus Connection: You cannot fix an immune system that is stuck in "Fight or Flight" mode.
  • Empty the Bucket: Identify 1-2 "drops" to remove (e.g., a specific food trigger) and 1 way to "drain" (e.g., 8 hours of sleep).
