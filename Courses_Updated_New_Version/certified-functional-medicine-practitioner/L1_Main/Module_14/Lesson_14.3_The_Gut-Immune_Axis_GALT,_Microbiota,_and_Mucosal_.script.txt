These are organized lymphoid follicles located mostly in the lower part of the small intestine, the ileum. I think of Peyer’s Patches as the "intelligence centers." Now, let’s talk more about that "pre-check" pass: Secretory IgA, or sIgA. This is the primary antibody found in our mucosal secretions. It’s in your saliva, your tears, and most importantly, the lining of your gut. Unlike the antibodies we find in the blood, like IgG or IgM, sIgA is built for combat in the trenches. It’s designed to survive the harsh, acidic, enzyme-filled environment of the digestive tract.

The main job of sIgA is something called "Immune Exclusion." This is a fancy way of saying it "tags" the bad guys. It binds to pathogens, toxins, and undigested food particles. By binding to them, it prevents them from ever touching the gut wall. If they can’t touch the wall, they can’t get into your bloodstream.
