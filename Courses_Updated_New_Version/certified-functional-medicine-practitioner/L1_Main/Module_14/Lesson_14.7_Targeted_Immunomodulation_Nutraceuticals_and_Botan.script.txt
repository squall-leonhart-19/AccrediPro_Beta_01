And the third part of this trinity is Zinc. Zinc is involved in over three hundred enzymatic reactions in the body, but its most critical role for us is supporting the thymus gland. The thymus is a small gland behind your breastbone, and it’s essentially where T-cells go to school. It’s where they learn the difference between a dangerous virus and your own thyroid tissue. As we age, the thymus naturally starts to shrink, but zinc deficiency accelerates this process. When the thymus atrophies, we see a rise in pro-inflammatory cells called Th17 cells, which are heavily linked to tissue destruction in autoimmune disease. Zinc provides the fuel for that thymic school to stay open and keep training T-cells properly.
