Common Coaching Pitfalls

Avoid these four mistakes that new coaches often make during the Reveal phase:

  • The "Science Dump": Explaining the entire NF-kB pathway to a client who just wants to know why her knees hurt. Keep it high-level unless they ask for more.
  • The "Fear Factor": Making the immune system sound like a monster. Remind them that inflammation is a natural process that has simply lost its "off switch."
  • Over-Promising: Saying "We'll have you feeling perfect in two weeks." Immunology takes time. Set expectations for a 3-6 month journey.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Load: Inflammation is often tied to chronic stress. If you only talk about supplements and ignore her high-stress job, the bucket will never empty.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Immune Bucket" analogy effective for a client like Diane?

It shifts the narrative from "my body is broken" to "my body is overwhelmed," which reduces shame and provides a clear, actionable path for recovery (removing triggers).

2. If a client asks for the "science" behind why her brain feels foggy due to inflammation, what is a simple way to explain it?

You can explain that when the body is inflamed, the immune cells in the brain (microglia) get "activated." This creates a sort of "brain smoke" that slows down communication between neurons.

3. What is the danger of the "Science Dump" in a coaching session?

It can trigger a "stress response" in the client, making them feel overwhelmed and incompetent, which actually adds more "stress" to their immune bucket.

4. Diane's doctor said her labs are "normal." How do you validate her without disparaging her doctor?

Explain that conventional labs look for "acute disease," while functional medicine looks for "optimal function." Her labs show she isn't in a crisis, but they don't mean she is thriving.
