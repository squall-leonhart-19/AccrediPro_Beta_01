Next, we have to talk about the "Barrier Breach." You probably know this as "Leaky Gut." The scientific term is intestinal permeability. The lining of your gut is only one cell layer thick. It is incredibly fragile. These cells are held together by "tight junctions." Think of these as little protein zippers. They are made of proteins like occludin and zonulin.

When these zippers stay closed, everything is fine. But when they start to fray, we have a problem. Gaps open up between the cells. Now, things that should stay in the gut start leaking into the blood. This includes undigested food particles. It includes fragments of bacteria called LPS. LPS is highly inflammatory.

When these particles enter the bloodstream, the alarm goes off. The innate immune system sees them and panics. It sounds the siren. This leads to systemic inflammation. This is the "hidden" driver behind so many conditions. We see it in Hashimoto’s and Rheumatoid Arthritis. We even see it in depression. The brain is very sensitive to these "leaks" in the gut.
