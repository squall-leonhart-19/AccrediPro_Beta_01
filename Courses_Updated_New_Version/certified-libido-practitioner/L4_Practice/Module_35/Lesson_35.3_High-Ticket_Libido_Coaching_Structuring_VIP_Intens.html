Screening Protocols for High-Net-Worth Clients

Not every client is a fit for a $10,000 intensive. Premium coaching requires a rigorous Application-Only process. This protects your reputation and ensures you are working with clients likely to succeed.

Screening Criteria

The High-Ticket "Green Lights"

  • Readiness for Change: Scoring 8/10 or higher on a "Motivation to Implement" scale.
  • Biological Stability: Client has already completed basic blood work or is willing to do so before the intensive.
  • Psychological Safety: No active unmanaged trauma or severe personality disorders (these require long-term clinical therapy, not a 2-day intensive).
  • Investment Capacity: The fee represents a significant but non-debilitating investment.

Practitioners should utilize a 15-minute "Discovery Call" to verify these criteria. If a client is looking for a "magic pill" or refuses to engage in the Somatic Awareness portion of the framework, they are a poor fit for an intensive.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is high-ticket pricing considered a "psychological signal" for high-achieving clients?

High-achieving clients often associate price with value, privacy, and speed. A $5,000+ price point signals that the practitioner is a specialist who can provide rapid, expert results, which increases the client's psychological commitment and "skin in the game."

2. What is the primary focus of Day 1 in a 2-Day Ignition Intensive?

Day 1 focuses on the Discovery and Exploration phases of the D.E.S.I.R.E. Framework‚Ñ¢. This includes biological lab reviews, HPA-axis analysis, identifying psychological "brakes," and deconstructing limiting beliefs or shame.

3. Which type of client is generally a "Red Light" for a VIP Intensive?

Clients with active, unmanaged trauma, severe personality disorders, or those looking for a "magic pill" without engaging in somatic work are poor fits. These individuals require long-term therapeutic support rather than a compressed coaching intensive.

4. How does the 'Evolution' Retainer benefit the practitioner's business model?

It creates predictable recurring revenue and ensures long-term client success. By moving a VIP client into a monthly retainer, the practitioner provides ongoing maintenance while stabilizing their own monthly income.
