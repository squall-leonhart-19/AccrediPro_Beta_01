Funnel Architecture for Sensitive Topics

A "cold" prospect is rarely ready to book a libido intensive after one ad. They require a Discovery-to-Conversion journey that builds safety. Because sexual wellness is a "vulnerable" purchase, your funnel must prioritize anonymity and education in the early stages.

The 3-Stage Libido Funnel

  1. Top of Funnel (Discovery): A low-threat "Libido Audit" or "Hormonal Spark Quiz." This allows the client to engage privately without speaking to anyone.
  2. Middle of Funnel (Exploration): A 5-day email sequence or a pre-recorded masterclass titled "The 3 Biological Brakes Killing Your Desire." This establishes your expertise and the D.E.S.I.R.E. Framework‚Ñ¢.
  3. Bottom of Funnel (Ignition): A "Somatic Discovery Call" where the client feels heard and safe to book a high-ticket program.
Coach Tip

In your email sequences, use "The Sunday Evening Effect." Send your most empathetic, value-driven content on Sunday evenings. This is when women are often reflecting on the week ahead and feeling the "mental load" most acutely, making them highly receptive to solutions for vitality and connection.

Trust & HIPAA Compliance: Testimonials

Sharing "Somatic" success stories is vital for social proof, but sexual wellness practitioners must be 100% compliant with privacy standards. In the US, HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) provides the gold standard for privacy, even for non-medical coaches who want to maintain high ethical standing.

The "Safe Testimonial" Framework:

  • De-identification: Change names, specific locations, and identifying career details (e.g., "A 45-year-old educator in the Midwest").
  • Focus on Mechanism, Not Act: Instead of "She had better sex," use "She reclaimed her responsive desire and felt a renewed somatic connection with her partner."
  • Written Consent: Always have a signed "Marketing Release Form" that specifies exactly where and how the story will be used.
