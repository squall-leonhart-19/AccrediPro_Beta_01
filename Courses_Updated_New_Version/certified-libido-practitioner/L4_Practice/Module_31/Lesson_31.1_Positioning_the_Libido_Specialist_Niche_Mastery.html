Defining Your Unique Selling Proposition (USP)

In a saturated wellness market, "Health Coach" is a commodity. A Unique Selling Proposition (USP) is the specific benefit that makes your practice the logical choice for a client. As a Certified Libido Practitionerâ„¢, your USP is built on the intersection of clinical science and somatic empathy.

To define your USP, you must answer the "Why You?" question through three lenses:

  • The Mechanism: You don't just "talk about sex"; you use the D.E.S.I.R.E. Frameworkâ„¢ to address the bio-psycho-social roots of dysfunction.
  • The Specificity: You don't help "everyone"; you help a specific person solve a specific pain point.
  • The Outcome: You don't sell "sessions"; you sell the restoration of intimacy, confidence, and vitality.
Coach Tip

Don't be afraid to exclude people. A USP that tries to attract everyone ends up attracting no one. If you are the "Postpartum Libido Specialist," a woman in menopause may not hire you, but every new mother with low desire will feel like you are reading her mind.

The 'Discovery' Phase of Market Research

In Module 1, you learned 'Discovery' as a clinical tool. In marketing, Discovery is your market research phase. You must audit the "Social and Environmental" landscape of your ideal client avatar.

Ask yourself these Avatar Audit questions:

  1. Where does she spend her "mental energy"? (e.g., LinkedIn, parenting forums, menopause Facebook groups).
  2. What are the "Brakes" in her life? (e.g., household chores, 50+ hour work weeks, caring for aging parents).
  3. What is the "Cost of Inaction"? If she doesn't fix her libido in the next 12 months, what happens to her marriage or her self-worth?
Coach Tip

Use the exact language your clients use. If they say they feel "touched out" or "like a roommate," use those exact phrases in your marketing. This creates instant Relational Alignment before they even book a discovery call.
