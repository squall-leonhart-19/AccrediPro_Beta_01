Confidently Presenting the Investment

This is where most new practitioners stumble. The key is to state the price and then stop talking. Silence is your best friend here.

Practice This Out Loud

"The investment for the 12-week Radiant Desire intensive is a one-time payment of $2,500, or three monthly installments of $900. Which of those works best for your flow?"

The Golden Rule: Do not apologize for your price. Do not say "I know it's a lot." You are offering her the chance to save her marriage and find herself again. That is priceless.

Mastering Common Objections

An objection is rarely a "no." It is usually a request for more information or a manifestation of fear.

Objection The Real Meaning Your Confident Response
"I need to talk to my husband." She's afraid of being judged for spending money on herself. "I completely respect that. Libido is a 'we' issue. What do you think he would say if he knew this could bring the 'old you' back?"
"It's too expensive." She doesn't see the value vs. the cost yet. "I understand. If this program successfully restored your intimacy and confidence, what would that be worth to you?"
"Will this work for me?" She has failed before and has "hope fatigue." "I hear that skepticism. The reason this is different is because we aren't just guessing; we are using a science-backed protocol tailored to your specific libido blockers."
Sarah's Insight

I used to lower my price the second someone hesitated. Don't do it! When you lower your price instantly, you tell the client your value is negotiable. Stand firm. The right clients will find the way to pay for what they truly value.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive" phase of the discovery call?

To identify the "Gap" between where the client is (pain) and where she wants to be (pleasure), and to help her realize the "cost of inaction."

2. How should you handle the silence after stating your price?

Maintain the silence. Allow the client to process the information. The first person to speak usually "loses" the negotiation or weakens their position.

3. What does the objection "I need to talk to my husband" usually signify?

It often signifies fear of self-investment or a lack of perceived "permission" to spend family resources on her own sexual wellness.

4. Based on the income table, how many clients per month are needed to reach a $120,000 annual income at a $2,500 price point?

4 clients per month.
Lab Summary: Key Takeaways
  • Discovery calls are a service, not a sales pitch; you are helping her make a decision for her future.
  • Authority is built by asking deep, insightful questions that show you understand her problem better than she does.
  • Pricing should be stated clearly and without apology; your transformational work justifies a premium investment.
  • Objections are natural; use them as opportunities to coach the client through her fears.
  • A six-figure practice is achievable by serving just 4 high-value clients per month.
