The Psychology of Value-Based Pricing

In the sexual wellness sector, pricing is more than a financial transaction; it is a clinical signal. When a client pays a premium for a libido restoration program, they are psychologically "buying in" to the process. A 2021 study on behavioral economics in health coaching found that clients who invested in high-ticket packages (>$2,000) demonstrated a 42% higher completion rate of behavioral protocols compared to those on pay-as-you-go models.

Low pricing often triggers a "commodity mindset," where the practitioner is viewed as a disposable resource. Premium pricing, conversely, positions you as a specialist. For the 40-55 year old woman who has spent years feeling disconnected from her body, a $150 hourly session feels like an expense. A $3,500 "Relational Vitality Transformation" feels like a solution.

Coach Tip: The Imposter Syndrome Antidote

If you feel guilty charging premium rates, remember: You aren't charging for the hour. You are charging for the 200+ hours of training, the biological expertise, and the years of emotional labor it took to master the D.E.S.I.R.E. Framework‚Ñ¢. Your price is a reflection of the result, not the clock.

Tiered Service Architecture

While high-ticket 1:1 work is the fastest path to revenue, a sustainable practice offers levels of access. This allows you to serve clients at different price points while protecting your energy.

1. The Platinum Tier (1:1 Intensive)

Price Range: $3,000 - $7,000.
Inclusions: Bi-weekly 1:1 calls, unlimited Voxer/text support, customized lab interpretation, and partner-inclusion sessions. This is for the client who wants high-touch, rapid results.

2. The Gold Tier (Hybrid Group)

Price Range: $1,200 - $2,500.
Inclusions: Weekly group coaching calls, access to a private community, and the D.E.S.I.R.E. online curriculum. This leverages your time by coaching 10-20 people simultaneously while maintaining a premium feel.

3. The Silver Tier (Self-Paced)

Price Range: $297 - $597.
Inclusions: Digital course access only. This is "passive" income that serves those not yet ready for the financial or emotional intensity of coaching.

Managing Financial Objections

When a client says, "I can't afford this," they are rarely talking about the balance in their bank account. They are talking about perceived value vs. perceived risk. In libido work, the objection is often rooted in a belief that their pleasure is not a "necessity."

Coach Tip: The "Relational Health" Reframe

When met with cost objections, gently pivot the conversation: "I hear you. It is a significant investment. I‚Äôd love to ask‚Äîwhat is the current cost of not addressing this? What is it costing your marriage, your confidence, and your daily joy to remain in this state of disconnection?"

Research suggests that 74% of women in midlife prioritize the needs of children, parents, and partners over their own sexual health (The Midlife Wellness Survey, 2022). Your job is to help them see that investing in their libido is an investment in the "heart of the home."

Operationalizing Payments & Accessibility

To maintain the "Premium" feel, your billing must be seamless. Clunky PayPal invoices sent manually detract from the professional experience. Use a dedicated Practice Management Software (like Practice Better, Dubsado, or HoneyBook) to automate the following:

  • Automated Billing: Payments should be deducted automatically on a set schedule.
  • Flexible Payment Plans: Offer a "Pay in Full" discount (usually 10-15%) or a monthly installment plan. A $3,500 package becomes much more accessible at $625/month over 6 months.
  • The Scholarship Model: To satisfy the desire for social impact, many practitioners reserve 10% of their "seats" for scholarship or sliding-scale clients. This allows you to maintain premium rates while remaining ethically aligned with accessibility.
