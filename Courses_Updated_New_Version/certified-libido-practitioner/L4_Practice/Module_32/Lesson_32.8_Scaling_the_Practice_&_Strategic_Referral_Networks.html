Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (Deep Discovery) in the call?

The goal is to understand the emotional and relational impact of the problem and the "cost of inaction"â€”helping the client realize why they need to solve this now.

2. How should you handle the "I need to talk to my husband" objection?

Acknowledge the importance of his support, but first confirm if the client herself is 100% committed to the change. This helps identify if the objection is about him or her own fear.

3. True or False: You should explain every single module of your program before mentioning the price.

False. Focus on the outcomes and the 3-pillar approach. Over-explaining features can lead to "information overload," making the client more likely to say they need to "think about it."

4. What is the "Sarah-approved" way to state your price?

State the price clearly and confidently, then pause. Do not rush to justify the cost or offer discounts immediately.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation is the Cure for Shame: Always start by normalizing the client's experience.
  • Outcome > Process: Clients pay for the "vibrant woman" they want to become, not the number of Zoom calls you provide.
  • The 30-Minute Rule: Keep calls tight. If they go over 45 minutes, you are coaching, not enrolling.
  • Financial Freedom is Simple: High-ticket packages ($1,500+) allow for a sustainable practice with fewer, higher-quality clients.
