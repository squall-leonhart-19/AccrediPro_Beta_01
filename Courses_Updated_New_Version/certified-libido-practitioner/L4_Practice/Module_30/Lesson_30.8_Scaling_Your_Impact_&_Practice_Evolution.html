Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

The goal is to build rapport and gain explicit permission to ask deep, personal questions. This establishes safety and authority.

2. Why is "selling the gap" more effective than "selling the features"?

Clients buy the bridge between their current pain and their desired future. Features (like "6 calls") are just logistics; the "gap" represents the emotional transformation they are actually paying for.

3. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Use empathy first: "I completely understand, it's a big decision." Then, ask a clarifying question: "Is there a specific part of the program or the investment you'd like to dive deeper into while we're on the phone?"

4. According to industry data, how much more do practitioners earn when selling packages vs. hourly?

Practitioners selling outcome-based packages earn 2.4x more than those billing by the hour.

Key Takeaways

  • Listen More, Talk Less: The prospect should be talking 60-70% of the time during the discovery call.
  • Focus on Outcomes: You are selling "vibrant intimacy" and "confidence," not "coaching minutes."
  • Own Your Price: $2,500 is a standard, professional rate for a life-changing 12-week transformation.
  • Consistency is Key: Practice your script out loud at least 10 times before your first real call.
