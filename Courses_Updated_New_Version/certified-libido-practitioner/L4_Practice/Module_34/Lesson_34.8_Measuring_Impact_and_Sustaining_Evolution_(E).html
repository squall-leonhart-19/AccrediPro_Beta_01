The 30-Minute Enrollment Script

Unlike a 1:1 discovery call, a group enrollment call focuses on the Curriculum and the Community. Your goal is to see if she is a fit for the dynamic of the group.

Phase 1: Setting the Container 3 min
YOU:
"Elena, I'm so glad we're connecting. My goal today is to hear about your journey and see if the 'Radiant Reclamation' group is the right next step for you. If it is, I'll tell you how to join. If not, I'll point you toward another resource. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: The "Gap" Discovery 12 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling 'invisible' in your intake form. Tell me, when did that start? How is that affecting your confidence when you're out in the world?"
YOU:
"And if we don't address this sense of disconnection now, where do you think you'll be in six months?"
Phase 3: The Group Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Based on what you've said, you're a perfect fit for this cohort. We have 8 other women, all in that 45-55 age range, dealing with exactly this. We spend 8 weeks together. We cover the physical hormonal shifts, but we also do the deep somatic work to get you back into your body."
Phase 4: The Invitation 5 min
YOU:
"The program is a $1,500 investment, or we have a monthly payment plan. Does this feel like the community you've been looking for?"
The Pricing Pause

After you say the price, stop talking. The silence is where the prospect processes the value. If you keep talking, you signal that you are nervous about the cost.

Handling Group-Specific Objections

In the libido space, privacy is a concern. You must be prepared to address why a group is actually safer and more effective than working alone.

Objection The "Libido Practitioner" Response

Confidently Stating Your Prices

Your price is a reflection of the transformation, not your hourly rate. Use these scripts to practice stating your price out loud until it feels natural.

  • The Direct Approach: "The investment for the 8-week group experience is $1,200. We also offer a 3-part payment plan of $450 to make it more accessible."
  • The Value-Stack Approach: "You get the 8 weekly sessions, the private community, and the 'Libido Blueprint' workbook. The total value is over $3,000, but the group tuition is just $1,500."
  • The "Early Bird" Incentive: "The program is $1,500, but since you're enrolling during this first week of the launch, I‚Äôm able to offer it for $1,200."
Imposter Syndrome Check

If $1,500 feels "too high," remember that your clients are likely spending that on a single vacation or a new designer bag. They are investing in their marriage, their health, and their joy. That is priceless.

Closing Practice: The Final Invitation

The "Close" is simply an invitation to take action. If you have done the discovery work correctly, the close should feel like a relief to the prospect.

Practice these lines out loud:

  • "Elena, I would love to have your energy in this group. Shall we get you registered?"
  • "It sounds like you're ready to stop feeling 'invisible.' Would you like to take one of the remaining 4 spots?"
  • "I'll send over the enrollment link now, and as soon as you're in, you'll get immediate access to the Pre-Work module. How does that sound?"
The "Next Step" Rule

Never end a call without a clear "Yes" or a scheduled "Follow-up." If they need to talk to a spouse, book a 10-minute check-in call for 48 hours later right then and there.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Silence" after stating your price so important?

It allows the prospect to process the value and prevents the practitioner from "talking them out of it" or appearing desperate/nervous about the cost.

2. What is the primary benefit of a group program for a libido client?

The dissolution of shame through shared experience. Hearing that other women have similar struggles is a powerful catalyst for healing.

3. How should you respond if a prospect says they prefer 1:1 work?

Acknowledge the preference, then explain the "Mirror Effect" of groups‚Äîhow seeing others' patterns helps them identify their own blind spots faster than in 1:1 work.

4. What is a realistic revenue goal for a first-time group of 10 women?

At a professional rate of $997 to $1,500, a practitioner can expect $10,000 to $15,000 in gross revenue for a single launch.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs allow you to impact more lives while increasing your hourly "effective rate."
  • The enrollment call is about finding a fit for the community, not just solving a symptom.
  • Objections about privacy are common but can be handled by emphasizing the power of "shame-busting" in a safe group.
  • State your price with a "neutral" tone‚Äîit is a fair exchange for a life-changing result.
  • Always lead with an invitation; you are offering a solution to a problem they desperately want to solve.
