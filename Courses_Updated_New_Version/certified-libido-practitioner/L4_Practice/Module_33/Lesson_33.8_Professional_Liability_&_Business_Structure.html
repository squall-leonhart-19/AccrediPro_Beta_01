Brenda's Challenge

A client insisted Brenda tell her to stop taking her antidepressants because "they killed my sex drive."

The Intervention: Brenda used the "Referral Bridge." She said, "I hear how frustrated you are with that side effect. Legally and ethically, I cannot advise on medication. Handling Legal & Compliance Objections

The Objection/Question The Compliant Response
"Are you a doctor or a therapist?" "I'm a Certified Libido Practitioner. Think of me as a specialized consultant for your sexual vitality. I bridge the gap between medical care and daily life."
"Can you guarantee I'll get my libido back?" "Legally, I can't guarantee health outcomes. What I can guarantee is a proven framework and my full support in implementing the lifestyle changes that have helped 90% of my clients see improvement."
"My doctor is useless. Can I just work with you instead?" "I appreciate your trust! However, for your safety, I require all my clients to maintain a relationship with a primary care provider. We work as a team‚Äîthey handle the 'illness' side, and I handle the 'wellness' side."
Sarah's Tip

If a client pushes your boundaries three times in one call, they are a high-risk client. It is better to decline the sale than to risk your certification and peace of mind. A "No" today protects your "Yes" tomorrow.

Check Your Understanding

1. A prospect asks: "Should I stop taking my birth control to help my libido?" What is the most compliant response?

"I cannot advise you to start or stop any medication. However, I can provide you with research on how hormonal contraceptives affect libido so you can have an informed conversation with your prescribing physician."

2. When should you first mention your legal disclaimer in a discovery call?

Ideally during the "Safety Frame" at the very beginning of the call (Phase 1). This sets the professional tone and prevents misunderstandings later.

3. Why is "guaranteeing" a result a legal risk?

In most jurisdictions, guaranteeing health outcomes is considered a deceptive trade practice. Since you cannot control the client's biology or their compliance with the program, a guarantee is legally unenforceable and risky.

4. How does having professional liability insurance help your sales process?

It acts as a "Trust Signal." Mentioning that you maintain professional standards and insurance shows the client you are a legitimate business entity, justifying higher premium fees.
Sarah's Tip

Never apologize for your boundaries. If a client gets annoyed that you won't give medical advice, they are showing you they will be a difficult, high-liability client. Thank them for their time and move on to the next prospect!

Key Takeaways

  • Compliance = Authority: Clear boundaries make you look more professional, not less.
  • The Safety Frame: Always open your calls by defining your scope of practice.
  • The Referral Bridge: Never just say "I can't help with that." Instead, say "I can't advise on that, but here is how I can support your conversation with your doctor."
  • Value-Based Pricing: Your $2,500+ fee is a reflection of your specialized expertise and professional standards.
