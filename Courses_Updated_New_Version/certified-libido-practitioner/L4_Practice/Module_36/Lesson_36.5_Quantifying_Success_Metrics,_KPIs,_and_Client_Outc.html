Moving Beyond 'Subjective Desire': Standardized Scales

While a client's narrative is vital, narratives are subject to "recency bias"‚Äîwhere a client forgets three weeks of progress because of one bad day. Standardized scales provide an objective anchor. In the D.E.S.I.R.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, we use these during the Discovery (D) and Evolution (E) phases.

The Female Sexual Function Index (FSFI)

The FSFI is the gold standard in clinical research. It consists of 19 items across six domains. A 2021 meta-analysis confirmed that the FSFI remains the most reliable tool for assessing female sexual dysfunction (FSD) with a high internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha > 0.9).

The 'Libido Log' Methodology: Tracking Ignition

The Ignition (I) pillar focuses on triggers and the dual control model. To quantify this, we use the Libido Log‚Äîa 14-day tracking tool where clients record "Desire Events." This moves the focus from "I never want sex" to "I felt a spark 3 times this week, and here is what triggered it."

A data-driven practitioner looks for the Ignition Efficiency Ratio (IER). If a client attempts 5 Ignition protocols (e.g., Sensate Focus or Novelty triggers) and 4 result in successful arousal, their IER is 80%. This data is invaluable for refining the client's custom protocol.

Measuring Relational Alignment: The Gottman Influence

We cannot measure libido in a vacuum. The Relational Alignment (R) pillar utilizes metrics inspired by the Sound Relationship House. We specifically track the "Love Map" scores and the "Turning Toward vs. Turning Away" ratio.

A Certified Libido Practitioner‚Ñ¢ tracks Bid Acceptance Rates. If a client makes a bid for connection (non-sexual) and the partner accepts, the relational foundation for libido strengthens. Research by the Gottman Institute shows that couples who stay together have a 5:1 ratio of positive to negative interactions during conflict, and a 20:1 ratio during everyday life.

Coach Tip: The 5:1 Ratio

If your client reports high desire but low relational satisfaction, their "brakes" are likely relational. Use a simple weekly "Connection KPI" where they track 5 positive interactions for every 1 grievance. This data often "unlocks" the libido faster than any supplement.

Analyzing 'Somatic Milestones'

How do we quantify "feeling more in your body"? We use the MAIA (Multidimensional Assessment of Interoceptive Awareness) scale. This tracks the Somatic Awareness (S) pillar.

Key Somatic KPIs include:

  • Noticing: Awareness of uncomfortable, comfortable, and neutral body sensations.
  • Not-Distracting: The tendency not to ignore or distract oneself from sensations of pain or discomfort.
  • Emotional Awareness: Awareness of the connection between body sensations and emotional states.
  • Body Trusting: Experience of one's body as safe and trustworthy.

A client who moves from "Body Trusting" 1/5 to 4/5 is significantly less likely to experience the "spectatoring" (self-critique during sex) that kills arousal.

Reporting Outcomes to Medical Partners

As you graduate to a professional practitioner, you will often work alongside OBGYNs, Urologists, and Functional Medicine Doctors. They do not want to hear that a client "feels more sensual." They want Clinical Data Points.

A professional Outcome Summary should include:

  1. Baseline vs. Current FSFI Scores: (e.g., "Client showed a 42% increase in Arousal domain scores").
  2. Symptom Resolution: (e.g., "Dyspareunia reduced from 'Severe' to 'Mild' following Somatic Pelvic protocols").
  3. Adherence Metrics: (e.g., "Client maintained 90% adherence to the Circadian Biology/Evolution protocol").
  4. Referral Recommendations: (e.g., "Despite 20% improvement in desire, lubrication remains low; recommend reassessment of topical estradiol levels").
Coach Tip: Professionalism = Referrals

Sending a one-page data report to a client's doctor is the fastest way to build a referral network. Doctors are busy; they love practitioners who provide objective evidence that their patients are improving under your care.
