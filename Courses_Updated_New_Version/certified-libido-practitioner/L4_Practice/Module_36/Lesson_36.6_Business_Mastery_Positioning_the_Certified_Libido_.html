Legal & Liability Foundations

Professionalism requires protection. As a libido practitioner, you are dealing with sensitive, personal data. You must have three foundational legal pillars in place:

  1. The Practitioner-Client Agreement: This must clearly state your Scope of Practice. You are a practitioner, not a licensed medical doctor or therapist (unless you hold those licenses). It must include a "No Guarantee" clause regarding specific medical outcomes.
  2. Informed Consent: Clients must sign a document acknowledging that sexual wellness involves somatic and psychological exploration and that they are participating voluntarily.
  3. Professional Liability Insurance: Ensure your policy specifically covers "Sexual Health Wellness" or "Life Coaching." Companies like Alternative Balance or HPSO offer specialized policies for wellness practitioners.
Coach Tip: Referral Networks as Legal Protection

Always have a "Red Flag" list. If a client presents with symptoms of undiagnosed clinical depression or physical pain that suggests a medical pathology, your legal protection (and ethical duty) is to refer them out immediately. Documenting this referral is your best liability defense.
