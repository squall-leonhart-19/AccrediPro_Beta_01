Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or reassurance. Statistics show that 80% of sales require 5 follow-up attempts, but most practitioners quit after the first objection.

The Objection The "Sarah" Response (Empathy + Question)
"I need to talk to my husband." "I completely respect that. Libido is a team sport! When you talk to him, what do you think his biggest concern will be: the cost, or the fear that this might not work?"
"It's just so much money right now." "I understand. It is an investment. Let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not doing this? What is the value of feeling like yourself again in your marriage?"
"I've tried so many things before." "I hear you. Most 'fixes' are band-aids. This is the first time you've looked at the somatic and relational roots. How would it feel to finally have a framework that connects the dots?"
Coach Sarah's Tip

Silence is your best friend after you state your price. Do not speak first. Let the prospect process the number. The first one to speak usually loses the lead's internal "buy-in" moment.

Pricing Presentation Mastery

When you present your price, your voice must remain steady. Avoid using "filler words" like "it's just..." or "I hope that's okay."

The "Anchor" Technique

Before stating your price, "anchor" the value by comparing it to the alternatives:

  • Therapy: $200/session x 2 years = $19,000+ (without specific libido focus).
  • Medical interventions: Thousands in tests and hormones that often fail to address the "spark."
  • Divorce: The average cost of divorce in the US is $15,000 - $20,000 per person.

Your $2,500 - $5,000 package is actually the most affordable way to save a marriage and reclaim a life.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The goal is to understand the prospect's current pain points and, more importantly, the "cost of inaction"‚Äîwhat happens if they don't solve this problem now.

2. If a client says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective follow-up question?

"I understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either the money, the time, or they aren't sure I'm the right fit. Which one is it for you?" (This forces the real objection to the surface).

3. Why is "anchoring" important when presenting your price?

Anchoring provides a frame of reference. By comparing your package to more expensive or less effective alternatives (like divorce or years of general therapy), your price is seen as a high-value investment rather than just a cost.

4. How many hours a week does a "Starter" level practitioner typically work to earn $5,000/month?

Typically 5-8 hours per week (including 2 hours of coaching and 3-6 hours of marketing/admin). This is why this career is so popular for those seeking flexibility.
