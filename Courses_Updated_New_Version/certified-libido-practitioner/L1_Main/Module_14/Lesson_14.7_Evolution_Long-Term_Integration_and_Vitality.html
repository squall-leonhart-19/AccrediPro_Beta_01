The Libido Life Cycle: Managing Transitions

Libido is not a static line; it is a cycle. Practitioners must educate clients on the biological "seasons" of desire:

  • Postpartum: Prolactin (the desire-suppressor) remains high during breastfeeding. Evolution here means prioritizing emotional intimacy and oxytocin over physical arousal.
  • Perimenopause/Menopause: The decline in DHEA and Testosterone requires a shift toward Responsive Desire protocols. Clients must learn that "waiting for the spark" is no longer a viable biological strategy; they must learn to "invite the spark."
  • Andropause: For male partners, the focus shifts to cardiovascular health and nitric oxide optimization to maintain erectile function and confidence.
Practitioner Insight

Use the "Seasons of Desire" analogy with your clients. It removes shame. Tell them: "You wouldn't expect a garden to bloom in December. Right now, your body is in a 'Resting Season.' Our job is to nourish the soil so the bloom is even stronger when it returns."
