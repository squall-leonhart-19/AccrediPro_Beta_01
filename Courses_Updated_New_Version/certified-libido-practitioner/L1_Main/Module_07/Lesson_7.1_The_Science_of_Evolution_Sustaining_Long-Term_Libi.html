Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between the "Ignition" stage and the "Evolution" stage?

Ignition focuses on immediate triggers and "sparking" desire (often corrective), while Evolution focuses on integrating these changes into a sustainable, permanent lifestyle and biological state (integrative).

2. How does the "Set Point" theory explain why some libido "boosters" fail in the long run?

The body's Sexual Inhibition System (SIS) acts like a thermostat. If the underlying biological "Set Point" isn't raised, the body will eventually pull the desire level back down to its previous baseline once the external booster is removed.

3. True or False: Epigenetic changes are permanent and cannot be reversed.

False. Epigenetic "marks" (like methylation or acetylation) are dynamic and can be influenced by lifestyle, environment, and behavior, allowing us to "turn on" or "turn off" specific gene expressions for health.

4. Why is mitochondrial health essential for raising the libido set point?

Desire and sexual function are metabolically "expensive." If the mitochondria are inefficient, the body will prioritize survival over reproduction/desire. High mitochondrial reserve provides the "energy excess" needed for vitality.
