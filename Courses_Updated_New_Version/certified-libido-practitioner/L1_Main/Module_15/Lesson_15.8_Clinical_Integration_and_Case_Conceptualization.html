Common Practitioner Pitfalls (And How to Avoid Them)

As you transition into your new career, keep these four common mistakes in mind. Avoiding these will keep your practice profitable and your stress levels low.

1

Over-Explaining the Science

New practitioners often try to prove their worth by lecturing clients on HPA-axis dysfunction. The Fix: Focus on the result (more energy, better sex) rather than the mechanism, unless the client asks.

2

Charging Too Little

Imposter syndrome leads to "discounting." The Fix: Remember that you are solving a problem that affects their health, marriage, and happiness. A $2,500-$5,000 package is standard for this level of specialized care.

3

Ignoring Your Own Boundaries

Answering client texts at 10 PM leads to burnout. The Fix: Use the Communication Policy in your DFY packet to set "office hours" from day one.

4

Waiting to be "Perfect"
