The Dual Control Model: Science of Arousal

Central to the "Ignition" pillar is the Dual Control Model of Sexual Response, developed by researchers at the Kinsey Institute (Bancroft & Janssen). This model posits that the brain has two systems:

  • The Sexual Excitation System (SES): The "Accelerator." It scans the environment for erotic stimuli and turns on the arousal response.
  • The Sexual Inhibition System (SIS): The "Brakes." It scans the environment for reasons not to be aroused (stress, threat, distraction, shame).

In most women seeking your help, the problem isn't that their "Accelerator" is broken; it's that their "Brakes" are slammed to the floor. A 2021 study found that for 75% of women, reducing "brakes" was more effective for libido recovery than increasing "accelerators."
