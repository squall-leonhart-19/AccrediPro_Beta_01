4. Common Practitioner Pitfalls

Even seasoned coaches make these mistakes when starting with libido-specific work. Avoid these to maintain your professional authority.

  • Over-Pathologizing: Jumping straight to "it's your hormones" (the Rhythm phase) before addressing the "Discovery" of her current emotional state.
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Offering supplements or toys in the first 10 minutes. Connection must precede correction.
  • Avoiding the "S-Word": Using euphemisms like "intimacy" when the client clearly means "sex." Use the client's language to build rapport.
  • Nervous Talking: Filling the silence when a client gets emotional. Silence is where the client's internal Somatics (S) are processed.
Self-Correction

If you catch yourself "over-talking," simply stop and say: "I'm going to pause there for a second because I want to make sure I'm giving you space to process that." It shows immense confidence.

5. Success Markers: How You Know the Session Worked

A successful D.E.S.I.R.E. intake doesn't always end with a "cured" libido. It ends with a shifted perspective. Look for these signs:

  • Physical Release: The client's shoulders drop, or they take a deep "sigh of relief."
  • Shift from Shame to Curiosity: The client moves from "What is wrong with me?" to "Oh, I see how my environment is affecting me."
  • Specific Action: The client agrees to one small "Somatic" check-in daily, rather than a massive lifestyle overhaul.

At least 5 seconds. This silence is crucial for clients who are disconnected from their somatic sensations to actually "find" the feeling.
