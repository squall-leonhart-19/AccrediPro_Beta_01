Common Practitioner Pitfalls

When I first started coaching women in their 50s, I made several mistakes that I see new practitioners make today. Avoid these to maintain your professional standing:

  • Over-Apologizing: Saying "I'm sorry if this is awkward" actually makes it awkward. Assume the topic is as clinical and normal as a heart rate check.
  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Avoid saying "You're still so beautiful!" It can feel dismissive of her genuine struggle with her changing body. Instead, validate her current experience.
  • Moving Too Fast to Supplements: If you suggest Maca or Ashwagandha in the first 5 minutes, you've missed the chance to hear her emotional story. Listen first, prescribe later.
  • Ignoring the Partner: While the focus is on the woman, the "partner pressure" is often the #1 source of her stress. Address it early.
