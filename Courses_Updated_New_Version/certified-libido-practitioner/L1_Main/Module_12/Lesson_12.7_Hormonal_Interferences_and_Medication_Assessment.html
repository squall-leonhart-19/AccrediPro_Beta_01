The 'Libido Killers': Medication Assessment

Many of the most commonly prescribed medications in the United States have a direct, often devastating impact on sexual function. As a practitioner, your role is not to prescribe or de-prescribe, but to assess and educate.

1. SSRIs and SNRIs

Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) are notorious for causing sexual dysfunction in 30% to 60% of users. The mechanism is two-fold: increased serotonin inhibits dopamine (the "ignition" neurotransmitter) and can lead to "genital anesthesia"‚Äîa physical numbing of the tissues.

2. Hormonal Contraceptives

Oral contraceptives often work by suppressing ovulation, which in turn suppresses the natural mid-cycle testosterone surge. Furthermore, they increase Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG). Think of SHBG as a "sponge" that soaks up free testosterone, leaving very little available for the brain and tissues to use for desire and arousal.

Perimenopause and Menopause: The Androgen Shift

While estrogen gets the most attention during the menopause transition, the Androgen-to-Estrogen ratio is the true driver of libido. Women produce testosterone in their ovaries and adrenal glands. During perimenopause, the fluctuating levels of estrogen can mask a steady decline in androgens.

The "Androgen Cushion" is what provides the motivation, energy, and "assertive desire" many women feel in their 30s. When this drops, the result isn't just a loss of libido; it‚Äôs a loss of vitality. Symptoms include:

  • Loss of muscle mass and "sarcopenia"
  • Brain fog and lack of competitive "edge"
  • Reduced sensitivity of the clitoris and nipples
  • Decreased frequency of sexual fantasies

Low Testosterone: Beyond the Male Stereotype

Testosterone is a human hormone, not just a male one. In fact, healthy women have more circulating testosterone than they do estrogen. When assessing for low T, we must look at the "Free" vs. "Total" levels.

Lifestyle 'Evolution' Strategies for Testosterone:

  • Resistance Training: Lifting heavy weights (relative to the individual) signals the body to maintain androgen production.
  • Zinc and Magnesium: Essential co-factors for the HPG axis.
  • Stress Management: High cortisol "steals" the precursors needed for testosterone (the Pregnenolone Steal).

Thyroid Dysfunction: The Overlooked Metabolic Brake

The thyroid is the "master regulator" of metabolism. If the thyroid is sluggish (hypothyroidism), every system in the body slows down‚Äîincluding blood flow to the genitals and the production of sex hormones.

Many clients are told their labs are "normal" because their TSH is under 4.5. However, Functional Libido Practitioners look for "Optimal" ranges (TSH between 1.0 and 2.0). Symptoms of subclinical hypothyroidism that mimic low libido include:

  • Chronic fatigue (too tired for intimacy)
  • Feeling cold all the time (reduced peripheral blood flow)
  • Thinning hair and dry skin (reduced tissue health)

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do hormonal contraceptives often lower free testosterone?

They increase Sex Hormone Binding Globulin (SHBG), which binds to testosterone and makes it unavailable for use by the body's tissues.

2. What is the "Pregnenolone Steal"?

It is a process where chronic stress causes the body to divert hormonal precursors (like pregnenolone) toward cortisol production instead of sex hormones like testosterone and progesterone.

3. What is the "Optimal" TSH range for sexual and metabolic vitality?

While conventional "normal" can go up to 4.5, functional practitioners look for an optimal TSH range between 1.0 and 2.0 mIU/L.

4. How do SSRIs cause "genital anesthesia"?

By increasing serotonin levels, which can have an inhibitory effect on the dopamine-driven arousal pathways and physically desensitize the nerve endings in sexual tissues.

  • Professional Partnership: Use data and symptom logs to help clients advocate for themselves in the medical system.
  • The Androgen Cushion: Libido in the second half of life is heavily dependent on maintaining healthy testosterone levels through lifestyle and movement.
