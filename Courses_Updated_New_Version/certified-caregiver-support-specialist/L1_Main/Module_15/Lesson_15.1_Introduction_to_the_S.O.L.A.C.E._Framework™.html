Defining the Level 1 Scope of Practice

As a Level 1 Certified Caregiver Support Specialist‚Ñ¢, your role is distinct from medical or legal professionals. Clarity on your Scope of Practice is essential for professional legitimacy and liability protection.

What You ARE:

  • A Strategic Consultant for the primary caregiver.
  • An Educational Resource for system navigation.
  • A Resilience Coach for emotional regulation.
  • A Facilitator of family communication.

What You ARE NOT:

  • You do not provide medical diagnoses or clinical treatment.
  • You do not provide legal or financial advice (you refer to experts).
  • You do not perform hands-on nursing care (ADLs).

Establishing the Support Alliance

The "Support Alliance" is the professional bond between you and the caregiver. Unlike a traditional "helper" relationship, the Alliance is a partnership of equals. You bring the methodology; they bring the intimate knowledge of the care recipient.

To establish this in your first session, use the Triple-A Protocol:

  • Acknowledge: "I see the immense labor you are doing that the world doesn't see."
  • Audit: "We are going to audit your current situation through the S.O.L.A.C.E. lens to find where you are leaking energy."
  • Align: "My goal is to protect your health and career while ensuring high-quality care for your loved one."

Communication Tip

Avoid using the word "help." Instead, use "strategize" or "optimize." For a 45-year-old high-achieving woman, being "helped" can feel like a loss of autonomy. Being "optimized" feels like professional development.
