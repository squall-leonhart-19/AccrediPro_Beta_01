Realistic Income Timeline

Success doesn't happen overnight, but in this industry, it happens faster than you might think due to the massive demand. Here is a typical trajectory for a 40+ career changer:

  • Months 1-3: The Foundation. Complete your certification, set up your basic website/LinkedIn, and take on 2 "beta" clients at a reduced rate to build testimonials. Income: $500 - $1,500/mo.
  • Months 4-6: The Launch. Begin regular networking and social media education. Sign your first 3-5 full-fee clients. Income: $2,500 - $5,000/mo.
  • Months 7-12: The Momentum. Referrals start coming in. You might run your first small group program or workshop. Income: $5,000 - $10,000/mo.
  • Year 2+: The Scale. You increase your rates, launch a digital product, or sign a corporate contract. Income: $10,000 - $25,000+/mo.

Success Case Studies: Women Like You

Case Study: Elena (The Former Nurse)

Profile: Elena, 52, burnt out from 25 years of floor nursing. She wanted to stay in healthcare but leave the 12-hour shifts and physical toll.

The Strategy: She launched a 1-on-1 "Care Transition" service, helping families move their parents from hospital to home safely.

The Outcome: Within 8 months, Elena was fully booked with 12 clients at $1,200/month each. She now earns $14,400/month while working from her home office in her pajamas.

"I realized my nursing degree gave me the 'what,' but this certification gave me the 'how' to build a business. I make more now than I did at the hospital, with half the stress."

Case Study: Martha (The Career Pivot)

Profile: Martha, 48, was a high school teacher and stay-at-home mom who had cared for her own mother with Alzheimer's for 5 years.

The Strategy: She specialized in "Dementia Caregiver Resilience," offering a 6-week group program for 10 women at a time.

The Outcome: She runs 4 groups a year ($15,000 per launch) and sees 4 private clients at $150/hour. Her annual income is approximately $88,000 working part-time hours.

Coach Tip

Notice that Elena and Martha didn't invent a new world; they just packaged their existing life experience with their new certification. What "hidden" skills are you bringing to the table?

The Math of Success

Let's make this feel achievable. To reach a $100,000 per year income (approx. $8,300/month), what does that actually look like in terms of humans served?

Option A: The Private Practitioner
8 clients per month on a $1,000/mo comprehensive support package = $8,000/mo.

Option B: The Group Specialist
20 students in a $500 group program = $10,000. Do this every other month = $5,000/mo avg. Add 4 private clients at $800/mo = $3,200/mo. Total = $8,200/mo.

Option C: The Corporate Consultant
One $3,000/mo corporate retainer (educating their HR team) + 6 private clients at $900/mo = $8,400/mo.

Coach Tip

Most of our students find that 8-10 private clients is the "Sweet Spot"â€”it's enough to feel very financially secure but few enough that you can still give every family your absolute best energy.
