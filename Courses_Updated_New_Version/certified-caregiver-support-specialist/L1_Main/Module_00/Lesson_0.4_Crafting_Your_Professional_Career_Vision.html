What You Can and Cannot Do

The most important distinction in your new career is understanding that we are educators, strategists, and advocates. We do not provide clinical treatment. This distinction is not a limitation; it is your protection.

Caregiver Support Specialist (YOU) Medical/Clinical Professional
Educate on disease progression and care strategies. Diagnose medical or mental health conditions.
Empower the caregiver with coping tools. Prescribe medications or clinical treatments.
Navigate healthcare systems and resources. Perform medical procedures or therapy.
Facilitate family communication and planning. Treat clinical depression or anxiety.
Coach Sarah's Tip

If a client asks, "Do you think my mom has stage 3 Alzheimer's?" your response should be: "As a Support Specialist, I can't provide a diagnosis. However, I can help you prepare the right questions for her neurologist so you get the clarity you need." This keeps you in your lane while remaining incredibly valuable.

Legal Foundations & Liability

Operating a professional practice requires a foundation of legal legitimacy. This ensures you are protected and your clients feel secure in your services. A 2022 survey found that 88% of professional coaches who carried liability insurance felt significantly more confident in charging premium rates.

  • Professional Liability Insurance: Often called "Errors and Omissions" (E&O) insurance. This is essential for any practitioner.
  • Client Agreements: Every client should sign a waiver clearly stating that you are not a medical professional or licensed therapist.
  • Business Registration: Most practitioners start as an LLC to separate personal assets from their business.
Coach Sarah's Tip

Don't let the legal "paperwork" stop your momentum. We provide templates for client agreements in Module 4. For now, focus on the learning; the business structure will follow!

Ethics & Professionalism

Trust is the currency of the caregiver support industry. Because caregivers are often in a vulnerable state, your ethical conduct must be beyond reproach. We follow the ASI Professional Code of Ethics, which centers on four main pillars:

  1. Confidentiality: Protecting the privacy of the caregiver and the care recipient at all times.
  2. Integrity: Being honest about your qualifications and the expected outcomes of your coaching.
  3. Referral Excellence: Knowing exactly when a client's needs exceed your scope and having a network of professionals to refer them to.
  4. Objectivity: Remaining a neutral party in family caregiving disputes to facilitate the best outcome for the senior.
Coach Sarah's Tip

Ethical boundaries also include your self-care. You cannot support a caregiver if you are burnt out yourself. We will discuss "The Oxygen Mask Principle" in Module 2.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which of the following is WITHIN the scope of practice for a Caregiver Support Specialist?

Educating a family on how to navigate a hospital discharge and setting up a home care schedule. (Diagnosing depression or prescribing meds is strictly outside of scope).

2. What is the primary purpose of a Client Service Agreement?

To clearly define the relationship, protect you legally, and ensure the client understands you are not providing medical or psychological treatment.

3. When should you refer a client to a licensed therapist?

When the client shows signs of clinical mental health issues, such as suicidal ideation, severe clinical depression, or unresolved deep-seated trauma.

4. Why is joining a POD (Private Practice Group) highly recommended?

It provides accountability, peer support, and has been shown to quadruple the likelihood of successfully launching your practice.
