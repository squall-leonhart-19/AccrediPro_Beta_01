2. What is the primary difference between a "Respite Helper" and a "Support Specialist"?

A Respite Helper provides temporary relief (time), whereas a Support Specialist provides infrastructure and strategy (systems). One is a band-aid; the other is a cure for the systemic chaos of caregiving.

Key Takeaways

  • You are the Bridge: Your role is to move the client from "Crisis Management" to "Sustainable Planning."
  • The Client is the Caregiver: While you care about the senior, your professional focus is the health and viability of the person providing the care.
  • Guilt is the Barrier: Most clients will resist help due to "Daughter/Son Guilt." Your role is to reframe support as an act of love for the senior.
  • Authority + Empathy: Success in this role requires balancing a warm heart with a firm professional boundary.
