Evolution of Suggestibility

The history of our field is a journey from "magic" to "mechanism." Understanding this evolution helps you explain the process to skeptical clients:

  • Franz Mesmer (1770s): Believed in "Animal Magnetism." While his theory was wrong, his results were real because he inadvertently induced trance.
  • James Braid (1840s): Coined the term "Hypnosis" (from Hypnos, the Greek god of sleep). He realized it was a psychological phenomenon, not a magnetic one.
  • Milton Erickson (1950s): The father of modern hypnotherapy. He introduced indirect suggestion and the idea that the subconscious is a reservoir of resources, not a dark basement of repressed trauma.
  • Modern Era (Today): We now use EEG, fMRI, and the T.R.A.N.C.E. Protocol‚Ñ¢ to provide evidence-based interventions for everything from chronic pain to smoking cessation.
