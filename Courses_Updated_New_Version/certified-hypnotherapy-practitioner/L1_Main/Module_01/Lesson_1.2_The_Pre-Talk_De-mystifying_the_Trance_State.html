The Conscious-Subconscious Model: The Critical Faculty

To explain hypnosis to a layperson, we use the model of the Critical Faculty. Think of the mind as a high-security building:

  • The Conscious Mind (The Lobby): This is where you handle your 5-9 bits of daily information. Itâ€™s logical, analytical, and very limited.
  • The Subconscious Mind (The Vault): This is where your habits, emotions, and long-term memories live. Itâ€™s the powerhouse of your personality.
  • The Critical Faculty (The Security Guard): This is the filter between the two. Its job is to reject any information that doesn't match what is already in the "Vault."

If a client believes "I am a smoker," and you suggest "You are a non-smoker," the Security Guard (Critical Faculty) says, "That's not true!" and throws the suggestion out. Hypnosis is simply the process of asking the Security Guard to take a coffee break, so we can walk into the Vault and update the records.
