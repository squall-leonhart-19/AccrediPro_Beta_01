Surface Goals vs. Core Targets

A key part of the "Target" phase is distinguishing between what the client says they want and the mechanism we need to address.

Client Statement (Surface) The Real Target (Core) Why it Matters
"I want to stop being anxious." The physical "freeze" at 9:00 AM. Gives the subconscious a specific "trigger" to rewire.
"I need more confidence." The internal "imposter" dialogue. Targets the specific thought pattern causing the lack of confidence.
"I want to be successful." The fear of being "seen" or judged. Addresses the underlying emotional block to success.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing to the "Fix": New practitioners often try to start the hypnosis before the client feels truly heard. If Trust isn't 100%, the hypnosis will be 50% less effective.
  • Accepting Vague Targets: If a client says "I want to be happy," and you start there, you'll wander. You must find the Target (e.g., "I want to stop the self-criticism I feel when I look in the mirror").
  • Over-Explaining the Science: While some logic is good for skeptics, don't turn the session into a lecture. Use "Professional Empathy" instead of a PowerPoint.
  • Matching the Client's Anxiety: If Elena is speaking fast, you must speak slightly slower to lead her into a calmer state.
Maya's Insight

Success Markers: How You Know It's Working

When you've successfully established Trust and identified the Target, you will notice these shifts in the client:

  • The Sigh: The client takes a deep, audible breath (a sign of the nervous system shifting from sympathetic to parasympathetic).
  • Softened Gaze: Their eyes may stop darting around and settle, or they may look down in a reflective state.
  • "That's It!": They use an affirmative phrase when you summarize their Target.
  • Physical Stillness: Fidgeting stops as they begin to focus inward.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to move from a "Surface Goal" (like anxiety) to a "Specific Target" (like the 9:00 AM freeze)?

Specific targets provide the subconscious mind with a clear "anchor" or trigger to work with, making the hypnotic suggestions much more potent and measurable.

2. What is the "Sigh" a marker of in a coaching session?

The "Sigh" indicates a physiological shift from the "Fight or Flight" (sympathetic) nervous system to the "Rest and Digest" (parasympathetic) system, signaling that Trust has been established.

3. How should your speaking pace compare to a client who is anxious and speaking quickly?

You should speak slightly slower and more rhythmically than the client. This is called "pacing and leading"â€”you meet them where they are, then lead them into a calmer state.

4. According to research, what percentage of treatment outcome variance is attributed to the therapeutic alliance (Trust)?

Research suggests the therapeutic alliance accounts for approximately 30% of the variance in treatment outcomes, making it one of the most critical factors in success.
