Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Hypnotic Comma" and why is it used?

The Hypnotic Comma is a deliberate, slightly elongated pause where a client expects a word. It creates a "pattern interrupt" that encourages the brain to drop deeper into the subconscious to find meaning, effectively deepening the trance.

2. If Elena says "my mind is racing," what is the best "Maya Chen" style response?

Validate and incorporate. You might say: "That's perfectly fine. Even a racing mind can begin to notice the speed slowing down, just like a car coasting to a stop after a long journey." Never tell the client they are doing it wrong.

3. According to the data table, why do new coaches often fall into the "Speed Trap"?

It is usually caused by practitioner nervousness or a fear of "losing" the client's attention during silences. The correction is to take a full breath before every sentence to regulate your own nervous system.

4. Which physiological sign is a primary indicator of a successful trance state?

A respiratory shift (moving from shallow chest breathing to deep abdominal breathing) and muscle laxity (specifically in the jaw or hands) are key indicators.
