Safety First

If a client seems "spacey" after the count of five, ask them to tell you their phone number backward. This "Pattern Interrupt" forces the logical brain to engage fully.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Trance Hangover": Not giving enough energy on the count of 5, leaving the client feeling groggy or with a slight headache.
  • Over-Talking: Talking too much after they open their eyes. Give them 10 seconds of "sacred silence" before asking your integration question.
  • Neglecting Physicality: Forgetting to tell them to move their body. Movement is the fastest way to break the hypnotic state.
  • Ending on a Negative: Ensure the last suggestion before emergence is positive (e.g., "feeling wonderful" rather than "you won't feel tired").

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why do we include physical movement (fingers and toes) in the count-up?

Physical movement helps re-orient the client to their body and the physical environment, facilitating the shift from internal focus to external alertness.

2. What should you do if Diane opens her eyes at the count of three?

Continue the count to five anyway. Even if their eyes are open, their brain chemistry is still transitioning. Finishing the script ensures full alertness.

3. What is the primary purpose of the "Integration Bridge" question?

To link the subconscious insights to the conscious mind, making the change "sticky" and actionable in the client's waking life.

4. How should your voice change from number 1 to number 5?

It should move from a low, slow, melodic tone to a higher, faster, and more energetic "conversational" tone.
