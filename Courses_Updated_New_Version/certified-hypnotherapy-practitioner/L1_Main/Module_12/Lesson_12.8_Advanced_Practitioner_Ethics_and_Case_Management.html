Common Mistakes to Avoid

As you practice, watch out for these "New Coach" traps that can break rapport:

  • Over-Explaining: Don't give a 20-minute lecture on biology. Keep explanations under 2 minutes.
  • Agreeing with the Shame: Never say "Yeah, chips are hard to stop." Instead, say "Your body is seeking a specific type of sensory relief."
  • Rushing the Intake: If you move to hypnosis before the client feels "heard," the suggestions won't take as deeply.
  • Sounding "Clinical": Avoid sounding like a doctor. Use a warm, "Maya Chen" style of professional empathy.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to reframe Elena's snacking as a "physiological response" rather than a "lack of willpower"?

Reframing removes the burden of shame. When a client feels ashamed, their nervous system stays in "fight or flight," making it nearly impossible to enter the suggestible alpha/theta states required for hypnotherapy.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Pre-Talk" phase demonstrated in the script?

The goal is to build rapport, establish authority, and begin lowering the client's "Critical Factor" by providing logical yet comforting explanations for their behavior.

3. If Elena says, "I've tried hypnosis before and it didn't work," how should you respond?

Use the "Feel-Felt-Found" technique: "I understand how that feels. Many of my clients felt that way after trying generic recordings. What we found is that personalized, deep-dive sessions targeting your specific stress triggers make all the difference."
