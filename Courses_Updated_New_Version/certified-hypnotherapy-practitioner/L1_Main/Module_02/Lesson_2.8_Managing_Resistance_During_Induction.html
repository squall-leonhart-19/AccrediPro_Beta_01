Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it recommended to speak primarily during the client's exhalation?

The exhalation is naturally tied to the Parasympathetic Nervous System (the "brake" of the body). Speaking on the exhale reinforces the physical release the client is already experiencing.

2. Deborah keeps opening her eyes during the induction. What should you do?

Don't stop! Simply incorporate it: "And you can keep your eyes open or closed, whichever feels more comfortable for you as you notice that relaxation growing..." This removes the "failure" and keeps the flow.

3. What is the primary indicator that the Relaxation Induction is working?

Physical markers: Smoothing of the forehead, lower lip becoming heavy, visible slowing of the breath, and the "hypnotic sigh."

4. How long should a standard Relaxation Induction last for a high-beta client?

Typically 5-10 minutes. Any shorter and they may not fully "drop in"; any longer and they may become restless or fall into actual sleep.
