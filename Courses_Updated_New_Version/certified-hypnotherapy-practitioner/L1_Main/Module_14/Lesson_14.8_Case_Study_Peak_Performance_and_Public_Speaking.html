Deep Dive vs. Surface Level

It is crucial to understand why this "Parts Work" approach is more effective than standard positive affirmations.

Approach Example Dialogue Long-Term Result
Surface Level "You are confident. You are a successful coach." Temporary boost; the Critic usually fights back harder later.
Deep Dive (Parts) "What is your positive intention for Diane?" Integration; the Critic stops sabotaging and becomes a "Safety Monitor."
Affirmation Only "I am worthy of success." Often creates "Cognitive Dissonance" if the subconscious doesn't believe it.

Common Practitioner Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches can stumble when moving into deep-dive work. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • The "Fix-It" Reflex: Trying to argue with the Inner Critic. Correction: Always validate the part's protective intent first.
  • Moving Too Fast: Not giving the client enough "processing time" in silence. Correction: Count to five in your head after every question.
  • Using "Why" Questions: "Why are you doing this?" can feel like an attack. Correction: Use "What" or "How" (e.g., "What is your goal for Diane?").
  • Ignoring the Body: Forgetting to ask where the client feels the "part" physically. Correction: Always anchor the part in a physical sensation (e.g., "the heavy chest").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask for the "Positive Intention" of a negative part?

To bypass the subconscious resistance. When a part feels "heard" and validated for its protective role, it is much more likely to step aside or negotiate a new role.

2. What is the "T.R.A.N.C.E. Protocolâ„¢" focus in this specific lesson?

The focus is on the "N" (Negotiation) and "C" (Change Work). We are negotiating with a protective ego state to allow for a behavioral change.

3. If a client says their Inner Critic "doesn't have a shape," what should you do?

Ask for a sound, a temperature, or a feeling. "If it were a sound, what would it sound like?" or "Does it feel hot or cold?" This helps the subconscious personify the abstract feeling.

4. Diane's fear of "embarrassment" is a sign of what subconscious driver?

The "Safety/Security" driver. Her subconscious views social rejection (embarrassment) as a threat to her survival, so it uses "Imposter Syndrome" to keep her invisible and "safe."
