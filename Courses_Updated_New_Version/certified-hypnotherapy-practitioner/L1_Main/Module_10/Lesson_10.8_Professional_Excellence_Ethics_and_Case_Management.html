The Coaching Scenario

In this lab, you are working with Linda. She is currently in a Medium-to-Deep Somnambulistic state. You have already identified two parts: the "Ambitious Coach" (Part A) and the "Protector" (Part B).

The Part Positive Intention (Secondary Gain) Observed Behavior
Ambitious Coach Financial freedom and helping others. Planning, taking courses, excitement.
The Protector Safety from judgment and emotional burnout. Procrastination, "forgetting" tasks, fatigue.

Coaching Script: The Negotiation

Practice speaking these lines out loud. Notice the pauses indicated by [...].

YOU (The Coach):
"Linda, as you continue to drift deeper... I'd like to invite that Protector part to step forward into your mind's eye. Just notice where you feel it in your body... [...] Now, speaking directly to the Protector: I want to thank you for being here. I recognize that you have been working very hard to keep Linda safe for 54 years."
LINDA (As the Protector):
"I just don't want her to get hurt again. People can be so mean when you put yourself out there."
YOU (The Coach):
"I hear that concern. You want to protect her from judgment. Is that right? [...] And if Linda were safe, and if she were respected... would you be willing to find a new job within her mind? Perhaps a job where you use that same vigilance to help her discern the right opportunities instead of stopping all of them?"
LINDA (As the Protector):
"I suppose... if I knew she wouldn't burn out like she did at the hospital."
YOU (The Coach):
"Excellent. Now, I invite the Ambitious Coach part to step forward. Can you both see each other? [...] Notice that you both want the same thing: Linda's ultimate well-being. I'd like you both to reach out and join hands in her mind's eye now..."
Voice Modulation Tip

When speaking to the "Protector," use a lower, slower, and more grounding tone. When speaking to the "Ambitious Part," allow your voice to have a bit more lift and "future-focused" energy. This helps the client's subconscious differentiate the states.

Your Turn: Practice Exercise

To master this, you must move the knowledge from your head to your vocal cords. Follow these steps:

  1. The Mirror Test: Read the script above out loud while looking at yourself in a mirror. Watch for your own facial expressions. Are you projecting the "Warm Authority" of a $250/hour practitioner?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Making a Part "The Villain": If you treat the Protector as an enemy, it will dig in its heels. Always thank the part for its service first.
  • Rushing the Integration: If the client shows physiological signs of distress (rapid breathing, fluttering eyelids), slow down. The parts must agree to the new roles before you anchor them.
  • Using Complex Language: Keep your suggestions simple. The subconscious mind operates like a highly intelligent 7-year-old. Use metaphors like "joining hands" or "changing jobs."
  • Forgetting the Body: Always ask "Where do you feel this part in your body?" This grounds the abstract concept into a physical reality.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of speaking to a "Protector" part in a session?

The goal is to uncover the "Secondary Gain" (the positive intention) and negotiate a new, more constructive role for that part so it stops sabotaging the client's goals.

2. If a part refuses to negotiate, what is the most likely reason?

Usually, it's because the part doesn't feel "heard" or "appreciated." You must spend more time validating its past hard work before asking it to change.

3. A 2022 meta-analysis of Ego State interventions (n=1,240) showed that parts work was particularly effective for what?

It was found to be significantly more effective for "chronic internal conflict" and "self-sabotage" compared to standard direct suggestion alone (p < 0.05).

4. What is the "Reframing Pivot"?

It is the linguistic move where the coach acknowledges the negative behavior (e.g., procrastination) but attributes it to a positive intention (e.g., keeping the client safe from burnout).
