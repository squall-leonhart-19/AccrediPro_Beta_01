4. Referral Triggers (Scope of Practice)

As an Integrative Medicine Practitioner, your role is to collaborate, not replace. You must identify Red Flags that require an MD's immediate attention.

  • Suicidal Ideation: Given Elena's use of Escitalopram and chronic pain, any mention of self-harm requires immediate referral to a mental health professional.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's Free T3 low despite a "normal" TSH?

High systemic inflammation (evidenced by joint pain and high TPO antibodies) and high cortisol (from stress and poor sleep) inhibit the 5'-deiodinase enzyme, which converts T4 into the active T3 hormone.

2. What is the primary risk of Elena's long-term Omeprazole use in the context of her Hashimoto's?

Low stomach acid (hypochlorhydria) leads to incomplete protein digestion and potential SIBO. These undigested proteins and bacterial toxins can cross the gut barrier (Leaky Gut), triggering the immune system to produce more TPO antibodies through molecular mimicry.

3. Which lab marker suggests Elena is at high risk for cardiovascular complications despite her focus on "eating less"?

Her HbA1c of 5.8 indicates pre-diabetes. Insulin resistance is a major driver of systemic inflammation and cardiovascular disease, especially in post-menopausal women.

4. What is the rationale for starting with "Stabilization" rather than a "Gut Detox" in Phase 1?

Elena is already depleted (low Vit D, low T3, high stress). A rigorous detox could overwhelm her biotransformation pathways, leading to a "Herxheimer" reaction or a flare in her fibromyalgia pain, which would likely cause her to quit the program.
