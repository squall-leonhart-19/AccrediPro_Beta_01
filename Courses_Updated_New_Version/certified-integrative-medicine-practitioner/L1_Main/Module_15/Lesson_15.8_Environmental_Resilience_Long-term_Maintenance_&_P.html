Common Mistakes to Avoid

As you begin your practice, watch out for these four common pitfalls that new coaches often encounter:

  • The "Toxin Terrorist" Approach: Scaring the client about every chemical in their house. This causes a cortisol spike, which actually inhibits detoxification.
  • Skipping the Gut: Trying to detox a client who is constipated. (Rule: You must have 1-2 bowel movements daily before starting any detox protocol).
  • Ignoring Protein: Forgetting that Phase II detoxification is amino acid dependent. You cannot detox effectively on a juice-only fast.
  • Over-Supplementing: Giving 10 different "liver support" pills at once. Start with food and one or two targeted nutrients.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it dangerous to start a "detox" protocol if a client is constipated?

If elimination pathways (the gut) are blocked, biotransformed toxins from the liver can be reabsorbed into the bloodstream, often causing a "healing crisis" or worsening symptoms.

2. Which phase of liver detoxification is dependent on amino acids (protein)?

Phase II conjugation requires various amino acids (like glycine, taurine, and glutamine) to turn toxins into water-soluble forms for excretion.

3. What is the "Total Toxic Burden"?

The cumulative effect of all environmental, dietary, and endogenous toxins that the body's detoxification systems must process at any given time.

4. How should you handle a client who is resistant to removing a specific environmental toxin?

Focus on "adding" supportive habits and nutrients first. This builds the client's confidence and physiological resilience before asking them to "subtract" something they value.
