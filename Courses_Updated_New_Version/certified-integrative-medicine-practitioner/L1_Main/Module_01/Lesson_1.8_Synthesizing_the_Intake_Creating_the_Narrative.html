Common Intake Mistakes

  • The "Interrogator" Vibe: Rushing through questions without acknowledging the client's emotional response.
  • Ignoring the Social Pillar: Giving a meal plan that is impossible for a night-shift worker or a single parent to follow.
  • Fixing Too Fast: Trying to give "tips" before the client has finished explaining their symptoms.
  • Over-Pathologizing: Making the client feel "broken" rather than seeing their symptoms as adaptations to stress.
Success Marker

You know the intake was successful when the client says: "No one has ever asked me that before," or "I never realized those two things were connected." That is the moment you have established authority and trust.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it critical to ask about a client's "Social" pillar (work, family, community) during a health intake?

Because social factors are often the "root cause" drivers of biological symptoms. For example, a high-stress job (Social) can lead to high cortisol (Bio), which causes weight gain. Without addressing the social context, biological interventions (like diets) often fail.

2. Linda (the client) feels guilty about her health. How should an Integrative Practitioner handle this?

By reframing the symptoms as "protective signals." This removes the moral weight of the "failure" and allows the client to view their body with curiosity rather than shame.

3. What is a "Bridge Statement" in the context of an intake?

A verbal transition that connects two different pillars, such as linking a physical symptom (Bio) to a life stressor (Social) or an emotional state (Psych).

4. A 2022 study found that deep-dive intakes improve what specific outcome?

Client adherence. When a client understands the biopsychosocial connection of their symptoms, they are significantly more likely to follow through with the recommended lifestyle changes.
