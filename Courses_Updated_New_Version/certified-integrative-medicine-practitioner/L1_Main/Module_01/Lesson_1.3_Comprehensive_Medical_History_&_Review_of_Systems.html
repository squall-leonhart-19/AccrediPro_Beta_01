The Integrative Review of Systems (ROS)

In conventional medicine, the Review of Systems is often a "check-the-box" exercise designed to ensure no major organ system is failing. In Integrative Medicine, we view the ROS as a map of biological interconnectedness.

We aren't just looking for symptoms in isolation; we are looking for the Skin-Gut-Brain axis or the HPA-Thyroid-Gonadal axis. For example, if a client reports acne, bloating, and anxiety, a conventional approach might lead to three different specialists. An integrative practitioner sees one root cause: Intestinal Permeability (Leaky Gut) driving systemic inflammation.

Evaluating Past Medical Interventions

We must look at past surgeries and hospitalizations not just as historical facts, but as metabolic turning points. A surgery is a major physical trauma that requires significant nutrient resources for healing and can permanently alter physiology.

Common Surgical Impacts to Assess:

  • Cholecystectomy (Gallbladder Removal): Permanent impairment in fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K) and fatty acid balance.
  • Hysterectomy: Even if ovaries are spared, blood flow may be altered, leading to premature perimenopausal symptoms.
  • Tonsillectomy/Appendectomy: Potential long-term shifts in the mucosal immune system and microbiome diversity.
  • Multiple Antibiotic Rounds: A 2022 study showed that even one course of broad-spectrum antibiotics can disrupt the microbiome for up to 6 months, increasing the risk of future metabolic dysfunction.

Pharmaceuticals & Nutrient Depletions

One of the most overlooked areas of medical history is Drug-Induced Nutrient Depletion (DIND). As an Integrative Practitioner, you must understand that the very medications used to "manage" a condition may be creating the next one.

Case Study: Elena, 52 (Former Corporate Executive)

Elena presented with muscle pain (myalgia), extreme fatigue, and "memory slips." She had been on a high-dose Statin for cholesterol for 5 years and a Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) for acid reflux for 3 years.

The Integrative Analysis:

  • Statins deplete Coenzyme Q10, essential for mitochondrial energy and muscle health.
  • PPIs block stomach acid, which is required to cleave Vitamin B12 from protein and absorb Magnesium.

Outcome: By identifying these depletions and working with her physician to support her nutrient status, Elena's "aging" symptoms vanished within 60 days. This level of insight is why clients pay $400+ for an initial integrative intake.

Let's work on supporting your B12 levels and share this data with your doctor to see if your dosage can be optimized." This protects you legally while providing immense value.

Prioritizing the Chief Complaint

While we look at the whole system, the client comes to you because of a specific pain point‚Äîtheir Chief Complaint. If you ignore the complaint to focus on "root causes" they don't care about yet, you will lose them.

The "Anchor & Expand" Strategy:

  1. Anchor: Acknowledge and address the Chief Complaint immediately (e.g., "I hear that your migraines are stopping you from playing with your kids").
  2. Expand: Connect that complaint to the systems you've identified (e.g., "Based on your history, I suspect these migraines are connected to the hormone shifts and gut inflammation we saw in your ROS").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a history of gallbladder removal (cholecystectomy) significant in an integrative intake?

It indicates a permanent impairment in the ability to emulsify fats, which leads to deficiencies in fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) and essential fatty acids, potentially affecting bone health, immunity, and hormone production.

2. What is the "Anchor & Expand" strategy for the Chief Complaint?

It involves acknowledging the client's primary concern (Anchor) to build rapport, then connecting that concern to the underlying systemic imbalances identified in the ROS (Expand) to justify the root-cause intervention.

3. Which nutrient is most commonly depleted by long-term use of Statins?

Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10), which is vital for mitochondrial function and energy production in muscle tissues.

4. According to epigenetic research, what percentage of chronic disease risk is typically attributed to lifestyle/environment vs. pure genetics?

Approximately 70-90% is attributed to lifestyle and environment, while only 10-30% is attributed to pure genetics.
Key Takeaways
  • The ROS is a tool for identifying interconnected biological axes, not just isolated symptoms.
  • Past surgeries (like gallbladder or appendix removal) are biological milestones that alter long-term nutrient needs.
  • Drug-Induced Nutrient Depletion is a primary driver of "secondary" symptoms that are often mistaken for aging.
  • Family history should be viewed through the lens of epigenetics, empowering clients to change their health trajectory.
  • Always anchor your clinical findings to the client's chief complaint to ensure engagement and compliance.
