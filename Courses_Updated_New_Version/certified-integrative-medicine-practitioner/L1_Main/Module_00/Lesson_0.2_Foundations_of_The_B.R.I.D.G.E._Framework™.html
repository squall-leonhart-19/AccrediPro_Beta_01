Realistic Income Timeline

Building a practice is a marathon, not a sprint, but you should see financial "wins" early. Here is the typical trajectory for our graduates:

Months 1-3: The Foundation

During your training, you begin working with 2-3 "Beta Clients" (often friends or family) for a reduced fee. This builds your confidence and provides your first testimonials.
Target: $500 - $1,500 / month.

Months 4-6: The Launch

You graduate and launch your first official 3-month signature program. You transition from hourly rates to package-based pricing (e.g., $1,500 for a 90-day transformation).
Target: $2,500 - $5,000 / month.

Months 7-12: The Momentum

Referrals begin to kick in. You may add a small group program or a corporate workshop. You are now a recognized authority in your niche.
Target: $6,000 - $10,000 / month.

Coach Sarah's Tip

The biggest mistake new practitioners make is charging by the hour. You aren't selling "time"; you are selling an outcome. A client will gladly pay $2,000 to fix their chronic fatigue, but they might balk at $150 an hour. Sell the result!

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Package Pricing" generally superior to "Hourly Rates" for an Integrative Practitioner?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome and the value of the transformation rather than just time. It also ensures better client commitment and more stable, predictable income for the practitioner.

2. What is a "Freedom Number" in the context of career planning?

It is the specific monthly income required to cover your expenses and feel successful in your new career. Knowing this number allows you to calculate exactly how many clients you need to reach your goals.

3. Which career path offers the highest potential for "Passive" or "Scalable" income?

Online Courses and Group Programs. These models allow you to serve many people simultaneously (one-to-many) without a linear increase in your working hours.

4. True or False: You should wait until you are 100% finished with your certification before talking to potential clients.

False. You should begin working with "Beta Clients" during your training (Months 1-3) to build confidence, gain experience, and collect testimonials.
