Practice Drills: Say It Out Loud

Expertise is built through repetition. Follow these steps to build your "coaching muscles."

1
The Mirror Check

Stand in front of a mirror. Deliver the "Three Pillar" section of the script. Watch your facial expressions. Are you smiling? Is your brow furrowed? Aim for a "calm authority" look.

2
The Voice Memo Challenge

Record yourself reading the script on your phone. Listen back. Do you sound rushed? Do you use "filler words" like "um" or "like"? Practice until you can deliver the script in a steady, rhythmic tone.

3
The "Elevator Pitch" Strategy

Try to explain Diane's 3-pillar strategy to a friend (or a pet!) in under 60 seconds. If you can't explain it simply, the strategy is too complex.

Coach Sarah's Insight

In my first year, I used to over-explain the science because I was afraid they wouldn't think I was "smart enough." I've learned that clarity is the ultimate professional credential. If they understand the plan, they trust the practitioner.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. Being aware of them is half the battle.

The Mistake Why it Fails The "Integrative" Fix
The Data Dump Overwhelms the client with too many biomarkers and "whys." Use the 3-Pillar method to categorize information.
Ignoring the "Why" The client feels like a project, not a person. Always tie the strategy back to their personal goal (e.g., "Finding your spark").
The Dictator Tone Creates resistance and lowers compliance. Ask: "How does this land with you?" to ensure buy-in.
Too Many Supplements Expensive and difficult to maintain. Focus on lifestyle pillars first; use supplements as "scaffolding."
Coach Sarah's Insight

If you feel imposter syndrome creeping in during a strategy session, remember: You are the guide, not the hero. The client is the hero. Your job is just to hand them the map you've carefully drawn.

Success Markers

How do you know if your Strategy Presentation went well? Look for these signs:

  • The "Exhale": The client literally sighs or relaxes their shoulders when you present the plan.
  • Clarifying Questions: They ask about how to implement, rather than why they are doing it.
  • Immediate Buy-In: They say things like, "That makes so much sense," or "I've never had it explained that way before."
  • Collaborative Spirit: They offer suggestions on how to fit a pillar into their specific schedule.
Coach Sarah's Insight

When I see a client exhale during a strategy session, I know I've won. It means their nervous system has shifted from "fight or flight" to "rest and repair." That is the first step of healing.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask "How does this land with you?" after presenting the strategy?

It ensures client "buy-in" and creates a collaborative partnership. If a client feels the plan is being forced on them, compliance will be low. It also allows you to catch any hidden objections early.

2. What is the primary benefit of the "3-Pillar" approach?

It reduces cognitive load and prevent overwhelm. By categorizing dozens of small actions into three main strategic themes, the plan becomes memorable and manageable for the client.

3. If Diane asks for a 10-step plan immediately, how should you respond?

Acknowledge her enthusiasm but redirect to the strategy first. Explain that the steps are the "what," but the strategy is the "why." This ensures that the steps actually lead to the desired outcome rather than just being "busy work."

4. True or False: You should explain every single scientific mechanism behind your recommendations during the strategy session.

False. While you should understand the science, "data dumping" can overwhelm the client. Provide just enough science to build trust and explain the "why," then focus on the implementation.
