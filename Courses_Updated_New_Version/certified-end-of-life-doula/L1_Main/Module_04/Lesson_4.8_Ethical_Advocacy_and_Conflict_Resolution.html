Common Mistakes for New Doulas

The "Checklist" Trap

Focusing only on the forms and ignoring the emotional cues. If Arthur looks away, stop talking about the form and ask about his feelings.

Siding with the Adult Child

Itâ€™s easy to align with Grace because she hired you, but your primary advocacy is for Arthurâ€™s wishes. Avoid "teaming up" against him.

Rushing the Silence

When you ask a hard question, wait. Silence is where the client processes their mortality. Don't fill it with nervous chatter.

Using Medical Jargon

Terms like "DNR" or "Intubation" can be scary. Explain them in plain language: "Helping your heart restart" or "Breathing machines."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Gentle Invitation" technique used in the script?

The goal is to frame planning as a way to preserve the client's autonomy and "voice," rather than focusing on the clinical reality of death.

2. If a family member becomes visibly upset during a planning session, what is the doula's first priority?

The priority is to "pause the paperwork" and hold space for the emotion. Advocacy involves recognizing that emotional safety is required for clear decision-making.

3. Why is it a mistake to "side" with the person who hired you (the adult child)?

It creates a "2-against-1" dynamic that makes the dying person feel defensive and lose their sense of agency, which is the opposite of good advocacy.

4. How does framing planning as a "gift" change the conversation?

It shifts the motivation from an obligation or a "to-do list" to an act of love and legacy, making it easier for resistant family members to engage.

Key Takeaways

  • Advocacy is about ensuring the client's voice remains the "loudest in the room."
  • Practice scripts help build the "calm authority" needed for high-stakes family meetings.
  • Your role is to facilitate the conversation, not just to complete the paperwork.
  • Silence is a powerful tool; allow clients the time to process difficult topics.
  • Success is measured by emotional relief and clarity, not just signed documents.
