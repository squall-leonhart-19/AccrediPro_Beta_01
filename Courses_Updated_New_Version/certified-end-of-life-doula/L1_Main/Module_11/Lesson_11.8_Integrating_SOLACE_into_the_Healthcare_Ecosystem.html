Comparison: Doula vs. Hospice Role

Responsibility Hospice Care (Medical) Doula Care (Non-Medical)
Medication Prescribes and adjusts dosages. Education on comfort measures (non-drug).
Physical Care Wound care, vitals, medical exams. Repositioning, moistening lips, touch.
Time Spent Short, intermittent visits (1-2 hours). Extended presence, vigil support (8-24+ hours).
Legacy Work Minimal focus due to medical needs. Primary focus: letters, videos, life review.

Common Mistakes & Success Markers

A 2022 study in the Journal of Palliative Medicine noted that role confusion is the #1 cause of friction between doulas and medical staff. Avoiding these mistakes will set you apart as a professional.

4 Common Mistakes New Doulas Make

  • The "Mini-Doctor" Trap: Using medical terminology or giving advice on medication dosages. This can lead to legal liability.
  • Over-Promising Availability: Saying "Call me anytime" without defining "anytime." This leads to burnout in less than 6 months.
  • Apologizing for the Fee: Feeling guilty about charging for "kindness." Remember: you are providing a professional service that requires specialized training and emotional labor.
  • Ignoring the Hospice Nurse: Failing to introduce yourself to the medical team. A successful doula works with hospice, not around them.
Emma's Success Marker

You know the session went well when the client's shoulders visibly drop. If they stop asking "What do I do about the meds?" and start saying "Can we talk about the letters I want to leave for my kids?", you have successfully transitioned them into the Doula space.

Check Your Practice

1. A client asks, "Can you tell me if my dad is in pain? I don't know if I should give him more medicine." What is the correct scope-aligned response?

"I can't assess his medical pain levels or advise on medication, but I can help you look for the non-verbal signs of discomfort we discussed, and we can call the hospice nurse together to get their professional guidance."

2. According to industry standards, what percentage of a Doula's work is typically focused on legacy and emotional support vs. physical comfort?

Approximately 80-90%. While physical comfort (non-medical) is part of the vigil, the bulk of the work involves life review, legacy projects, and emotional processing for the family.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Confidence is Comfort: Your ability to clearly define your scope gives the family a sense of safety.
  • Language Matters: Use phrases like "I manage the environment" and "I support the daughter role" to differentiate yourself from medical staff.
  • Hospice is Your Partner: Always position yourself as a complementary addition to the hospice team, never a replacement.
  • You are a Professional: Treat your initial consultation like a professional intake, not just a friendly chat. This justifies your premium pricing.
