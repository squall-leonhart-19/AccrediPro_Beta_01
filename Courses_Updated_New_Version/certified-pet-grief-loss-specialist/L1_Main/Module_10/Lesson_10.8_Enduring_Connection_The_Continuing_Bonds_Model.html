Check Your Understanding

1. Diane says, "My husband thinks I'm being dramatic." According to the B.R.I.D.G.E. Framework, what is your primary goal in this moment?

The primary goal is Build Safety (B). You must validate her experience to counteract the disenfranchisement she is feeling at home.

2. Why is the phrase "At least he lived a long life" considered a coaching pitfall?

It is a "minimizer." It suggests that because the dog was old, the grief should be less intense. In reality, a longer life often means a more deeply ingrained daily habit and a stronger bond.

3. What is "Warm Authority" in a coaching context?

It is the balance of being deeply empathetic and kind (Warm) while remaining the confident leader of the session (Authority) who has a framework to help the client navigate their pain.

4. True or False: If a client cries through the entire session, the session was a failure.

False. For many clients, your session is the only place they feel safe enough to cry. This release is a vital part of the "R" (Recognize the Bond) and "I" (Identify Emotions) phases.
