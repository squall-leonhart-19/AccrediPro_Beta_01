Kenyoku-ho: The Art of Energetic Purification

Kenyoku-ho, or "Dry Bathing," is a traditional technique used to clear the aura of stagnant energy. It is essential after you have completed your Scan and before you begin the Restore phase.

Step Action Energetic Purpose
The Shoulder Sweep Right hand to left shoulder, sweep diagonally to right hip. Repeat on opposite side. Clears the "weight of the world" and disconnects from external stressors.
The Arm Sweep Right hand to left shoulder, sweep down the outside of the arm to the fingertips. Repeat. Purifies the minor chakras in the palms, preparing them for transmission.
The Breath Exhale forcefully through the mouth during each sweep. Uses the breath as a "vacuum" to pull stagnant Ki out of the field.

Gassho Meiso: Heart-Mind Unity

Gassho means "two hands coming together." This is more than a prayer pose; it is a sophisticated method of hemispheric synchronization. By pressing the pads of the middle fingers together, you stimulate the heart meridian and quiet the "monkey mind."

The Master Technique:

  • Sit with a straight spine, hands in front of the heart.
  • Focus all attention on the point where the two middle fingers meet.
  • If a thought arises, acknowledge it and gently bring your focus back to the pressure of the fingers.
  • Continue for 5-10 minutes until you feel a "shift" in your perception‚Äîthis is the Alpha state necessary for Reiki mastery.

The Energetic Contract and Space Maintenance

Opening the channel isn't just about you; it's about the space between you and the client. As a professional, you must establish an "Energetic Contract." This is an unspoken (or spoken) agreement that defines the boundaries of the session.

Elements of a Professional Contract:

  1. Intentional Privacy: Ensuring no physical or energetic interruptions (phones off, door locked).
  2. Permission: Explicitly asking the client's higher self for permission to enter their biofield.
  3. Non-Attachment: Releasing the need for a specific outcome, allowing the Reiki to go where it is needed most.

Coach Tip

To maintain a high-vibrational state during the session, keep your tongue touching the roof of your mouth. This completes the "Microcosmic Orbit" of energy within your own body, preventing leaks.

Mastery Check

1. Why is the "middle finger focus" in Gassho Meiso significant?

The middle finger is associated with the heart meridian in traditional Japanese medicine. Focusing here helps bypass the egoic mind and centers the practitioner in the heart-space, facilitating a more coherent energetic flow.

2. What is the primary risk of skipping the "Open" phase?

Skipping the "Open" phase often leads to "Practitioner Depletion." Without becoming an "Empty Vessel," the practitioner may inadvertently use their own vital energy (Ki) to heal the client, leading to fatigue, headaches, and burnout.

3. When should Kenyoku-ho be performed in a professional session?

Kenyoku-ho should be performed after the initial Scan (to clear any picked-up disharmony) and before the Restore phase (to ensure the conduit is pure). It can also be done at the very end of the session to disconnect from the client's field.

4. What does the term "Reiji-ho" literally translate to?

It translates to "Indication of Spirit" or "Sign of the Soul." It represents the practitioner's surrender to higher guidance rather than relying on a rigid, pre-set list of hand positions.
