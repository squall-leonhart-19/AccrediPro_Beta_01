Shoden: The Original "First Teachings"

The term Shoden literally translates from Japanese as "First Teachings" or "Beginning Level." In the original Usui Reiki Ryoho Gakkai (the society founded by Mikao Usui), Shoden was not just about hand positions; it was a path of self-purification and developing the capacity to hold energy.

Historically, the Shoden level focused heavily on the physical body. Usui Sensei taught that before one could work on the deep emotional or spiritual subconscious of another (Level 2/Okuden), the practitioner must first become a stable conduit for Ki. This involved learning to detect Hibiki (sensations) in the body, which we now categorize under the "Scan" phase of our framework.

The Empty Vessel: Practitioner as a Pure Conduit

One of the most common mistakes new practitioners make is "trying too hard." They attempt to push their own energy into the client, leading to practitioner exhaustion (burnout).

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. framework, we teach the Philosophy of the Empty Vessel. You are not the "healer"; you are the conduit.

  • Ego Dissolution: Removing the desire for a specific outcome.
  • Non-Attachment: Understanding that the client's body knows how to use the Ki better than your conscious mind does.
  • Hollow Bamboo: A traditional metaphor for being open at both ends, allowing energy to flow through without being colored by your personal stress or opinions.

Coach Tip

If you feel tired after a session, you were likely using your own "battery" (personal Ki) rather than the "wall outlet" (Universal Ki). Re-read the "Open" phase of the SOURCE method to ensure you are properly connecting to the source before starting.
