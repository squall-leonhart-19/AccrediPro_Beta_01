The Anatomy of the 12-15 Standard Level 1 Hand Positions

While Reiki is an intuitive art, the foundation of Level 1 (Shoden) mastery lies in the structured application of hand positions. These positions are strategically designed to cover the endocrine system, major organ groups, and the primary meridian pathways.

By following a standardized sequence, you ensure that no area of the client's biofield is neglected. This structure is particularly helpful for practitioners transitioning from structured careers like nursing or teaching, providing a "clinical map" for the session.

The Sequence Breakdown

  • The Head (4 Positions): Focuses on the pituitary/pineal glands, the eyes, and the cerebral cortex. This addresses mental fatigue, anxiety, and sensory overload.
  • The Front Body (4-5 Positions): Covers the throat, heart, solar plexus, upper abdomen, and lower abdomen. This targets the thyroid, heart, liver, stomach, and reproductive organs.
  • The Back Body (4 Positions): Targets the shoulders (trapezius), the mid-back (kidneys/adrenals), the lower back (lumbar), and the sacrum.

Techniques for Releasing 'Stuck' Energy

When your Scan (Byosen Reikan) reveals a "Hibiki" (sensation) of coldness, intense heat, or a "sticky" vibration, you have encountered stagnation. To unblock this, we use the U-Phase techniques:

  1. The Gentle Pulse: Placing the hands on the area and slightly increasing the flow of Ki with intention, acting like a "plunger" to move the blockage.
  2. The Aura Sweep: Moving the hands 3-5 inches above the body in a downward motion to clear the "debris" from the etheric layer.
  3. Somatic Release: Encouraging the client to "breathe into" the area where you are holding your hands.
Coach Tip

If you encounter a particularly stubborn blockage, don't force it. Reiki is not about "pushing" energy; it is about providing the high-frequency environment where the client's own body chooses to release. If it doesn't move in 5 minutes, move to the next position and return later.

Managing Emotional Catharsis

Emotional congestion often manifests as tears, shaking, or sudden temperature shifts during the Unblock phase. This is a healing crisis‚Äîa positive sign that the body is safe enough to let go.

Professional Protocol for Catharsis:

  • Maintain Presence: Do not pull your hands away abruptly. This can make the client feel "abandoned" mid-release.
  • Offer Grounding: If the client is overwhelmed, move one hand to their feet or knees to help "pull" the energy down.
  • Minimize Verbalization: Don't ask "What are you feeling?" This pulls them out of their somatic experience and into their analytical mind. Simply say, "You are safe. Let it move."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "U: Unblock" phase performed before the "R: Restore" phase in the SOURCE Method‚Ñ¢?

You must clear the "stagnant" or "congested" energy first to create space. Trying to "Restore" or "Replenish" a field that is still blocked is like trying to pour fresh water into a pipe that is clogged with debris; the new energy cannot be fully integrated until the blockage is resolved.

2. What is the primary physiological link to a blockage in the Throat Chakra?

The primary physiological link is the Thyroid and Parathyroid glands, as well as the cervical spine. Stagnation here often manifests as jaw tension, thyroid issues, or chronic neck pain.

3. How does "Kenyoku-ho" benefit the practitioner after a session?

Kenyoku-ho (Dry Bathing) helps to disconnect the practitioner's energy from the client's, clearing any lingering "debris" and ensuring the practitioner does not carry the client's emotional or physical "weight" after the session ends.

4. What should a practitioner do if a client begins to cry during the Unblock phase?

Maintain a calm, grounded presence. Do not pull your hands away. Offer a brief statement of safety ("You are safe, let it move"), and if necessary, move one hand to the feet to provide grounding. Avoid asking analytical questions.
