From Intuition to Protocol: Why Structure Matters

The Power of Sankalpa: Setting Intentions

In the Open (O) phase of the SOURCE method, we utilize the concept of Sankalpa‚Äîa Sanskrit term for a "vow from the heart." While Reiki goes where it is needed most, the Power of Intent acts as a compass.

A Sankalpa is different from a goal. A goal is "I want to fix my back pain." A Sankalpa is "I am a vessel for restorative ease."

Criteria for a Powerful Sankalpa:

  • Present Tense: State it as if it is already happening (e.g., "Healing is flowing now").
  • Positive Phrasing: Focus on what you want, not what you want to remove (e.g., "Peace resides here" vs. "No more anxiety").
  • Brief: 5-7 words maximum.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary clinical purpose of the 'Scan' (S) phase in the SOURCE Method‚Ñ¢?

The Scan phase (Byosen Reikan) is for assessment. It allows the practitioner to detect 'Hibiki' (sensations) and identify areas of energetic density or depletion before the active restoration begins.

2. Why is the 'Conduit' mindset essential for preventing practitioner burnout?

It shifts the responsibility of 'healing' away from the practitioner's ego and personal energy. By acting as a channel for Universal Ki rather than using personal Ki, the practitioner remains replenished rather than depleted.

3. How does a 'Sankalpa' differ from a standard goal?

A Sankalpa is a present-tense, heart-centered vow (e.g., "I am peace") rather than a future-oriented desire to "fix" a specific problem. It sets the frequency for the session.

4. Which phase of the SOURCE Method‚Ñ¢ focuses on stabilizing the client's energy in the Lower Hara?

The 'Center' (C) phase. This ensures the client is properly grounded and energetically stable before the session concludes.
