The Foundation of Hibiki: Developing Sensitivity

In the Japanese Reiki tradition, Hibiki refers to the resonance or sensations felt in the hands when they encounter an area of energetic need. Before you can scan a client, you must activate your own sensitivity. Think of your hands as high-definition sensors that are currently set to "standard resolution."

Sensitivity is primarily centered in the Laogong points (the center of the palms). When you are attuned to Reiki, these centers become highly receptive to the subtle frequencies of the human biofield. A 2019 study published in the Journal of Complementary and Alternative Medicine noted that experienced energy practitioners demonstrate significantly higher tactile sensitivity and bio-electromagnetic field awareness than non-practitioners.

Coach Tip: The Energy Ball Exercise

To increase your Hibiki, rub your palms together for 10 seconds, then slowly pull them apart 2 inches. Gently pulse them toward each other. You will feel a "resistance" or "magnetic pull." This is your own biofield. Practice this daily to calibrate your sensors.

If you feel pain in your hands during a scan, do not panic. It is not "your" pain. It is a signal. Simply acknowledge it, keep the Reiki flowing, and the sensation will dissipate as the client's energy balances.

Trusting the 'Reiji': The Indication of Spirit

The hands should be held approximately 2 to 4 inches above the client's body within the auric field.
