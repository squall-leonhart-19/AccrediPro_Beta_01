Advanced Techniques: Sweeping & Pulsing

Sometimes, simply holding a position is not enough to move dense stagnation. In these cases, we employ two specific techniques derived from traditional Japanese lineages but refined for the modern practitioner.

1. Sweeping (Kekki-ho)

Sweeping is a non-touch or light-touch technique used to "brush" the aura. It is particularly effective for clients who feel "heavy" or "clouded." You use your hands as if they were energetic brooms, moving from the center of the body outward to the extremities. This clears the peripheral biofield, making the deep unblocking more effective.

2. Pulsing (Hado-resonance)

Pulsing involves a subtle, rhythmic "push and pull" of the energy flowing through your palms. Instead of a steady stream, you create a wave-like frequency. This is used for stubborn blockages (Hibiki Level 4-5) where the energy feels like a solid wall. The pulse helps "break up" the density, similar to how an ultrasonic cleaner uses vibration to remove grime.

Coach Tip: Sensory Cues

When a blockage begins to release during a pulse, you will often feel a sudden "cool breeze" or a "dropping" sensation in your own palms. This is your cue that the Unblock phase for that area is complete and you can move to the Restore phase.

Managing Healing Crises & Detox

Occasionally, unblocking energy can lead to what is known as a Healing Crisis (or Herxheimer-like reaction in energy work). Because you are moving stagnant Ki, the body's physical detoxification systems (lymphatic, digestive, emotional) may be temporarily overwhelmed.

Common Symptoms of a Healing Crisis:

  • Physical: Temporary fatigue, mild headache, increased urination, or slight "flu-like" achiness.
  • Emotional: Vivid dreams, irritability, or unexplained mood swings for 24-48 hours.
Coach Tip: Client Education

Always frame a healing crisis as a positive sign of progress. Tell your client: "If you feel a bit tired or emotional tomorrow, celebrate it. It means your body is finally processing and letting go of things it has carried for a long time. Drink extra water and allow yourself to rest." This professional framing prevents the client from feeling "sick" and instead makes them feel "healing."
