The Biofield: More Than Mysticism

While traditional Reiki refers to the "aura," modern science increasingly utilizes the term Human Biofield. In 1992, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) officially recognized the biofield as a "massless field, not necessarily electromagnetic, that surrounds and permeates living bodies."

As a practitioner, viewing the biofield as a biological blueprint is essential. Before a physical symptom appears in the body, it often manifests as a disturbance in the field. By scanning the biofield, you are effectively performing an "energetic early warning" check. Practitioners who master this level of scanning often command premium rates (averaging $150-$225 per session) because they offer insights that transcend basic relaxation.

Scanning Distances: Close-Range vs. Distant

A master practitioner uses two distinct scanning modes to gather a complete data set of the client's condition.

1. The Micro Scan (Close-Range)

Performed 1-3 inches from the skin. This focuses on the Etheric Body. You are looking for Byosen (sensations) related to physical inflammation, injury, or organ fatigue. Sensation here is often very "hot" or "tingly."

2. The Macro Scan (Distant)

Performed 12-24 inches from the skin. This focuses on the Mental and Spiritual bodies. Here, you may feel "voids" or "pockets" where the client has disconnected from their higher purpose or is experiencing significant mental exhaustion.

Coach Tip

Always perform a Macro Scan first to get the "big picture" before moving into the Micro Scan for details. This prevents you from getting "lost" in a specific physical symptom before seeing the emotional cause.

Recognizing Leaks, Tears, and Congestion

When scanning, you are looking for deviations from a smooth, egg-shaped field. In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we classify these as:

  • Energetic Leaks: Feels like a "cool breeze" blowing out from the body. Usually found near the Solar Plexus or Sacral chakras in clients who feel "drained" by others.
  • Auric Tears: Feels like a "jagged" or "sharp" edge in the field. Often the result of sudden trauma or surgery.
  • Congestion: Feels like "heavy mud" or "thick soup." This is stagnant energy that needs to be "combed" out before Reiki can flow effectively.
