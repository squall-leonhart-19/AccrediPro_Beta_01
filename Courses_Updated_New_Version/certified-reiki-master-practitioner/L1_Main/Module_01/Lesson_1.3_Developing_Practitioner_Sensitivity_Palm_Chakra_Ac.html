The Anatomy of the Palm Chakras (Laogong Points)

While the traditional Reiki system focuses on the flow of energy from the Crown to the Hands, the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢ emphasizes the hands as bidirectional tools. The palms are not just "exits" for energy; they are sophisticated "receivers."

The primary energetic gateway in the hand is known in Traditional Chinese Medicine as the Laogong point (Pericardium 8). Located in the center of the palm, where the tip of the middle finger touches when you make a fist, this point is the "Palace of Toil." In Reiki Master practice, we refer to this as the Palm Chakra.

Gassho Meiso: Centering for Perception

Before you can scan a client, you must calibrate your own system. Gassho Meiso (the Gassho meditation) is not merely a ritual of respect; it is a neurological centering technique. By placing the hands together at the heart center, you create a "closed circuit" of energy that balances the left and right hemispheres of the brain.

Research into biofield practitioners suggests that 10 minutes of Gassho Meiso increases heart rate variability (HRV) and shifts the practitioner into a parasympathetic state. This state is critical because a "stressed" hand is a "numb" hand. When the practitioner is in a state of fight or flight, the brain deprioritizes subtle sensory input in favor of survival signals.

Daily Exercises for Increasing Hand Sensitivity

Sensitivity is a muscle; it must be trained. To reach a Master level of proficiency, incorporate these three protocols into your daily routine:

1. The "Etheric Ball" Compression

Hold your hands 12 inches apart, palms facing each other. Slowly bring them closer until you feel a slight resistance, as if pressing against a soft balloon. Bounce the hands gently against this resistance. This trains the Palm Chakras to recognize the boundary of an energetic field.

2. Texture Discernment

Place your hand 2 inches above different materials (wood, metal, water, a living plant). Close your eyes and notice the "flavor" of the sensation. Does the metal feel "sharp"? Does the plant feel "pulsating"? This builds your energetic vocabulary.

3. The "Cold Scan" Drill

Practice scanning an inanimate object, like a chair, after someone has sat in it and then left. Try to find exactly where they were sitting using only the heat/sensation in your palms. This hones your residual energy detection.

Coach Tip

Consistency beats intensity. Five minutes of sensitivity training every morning is more effective for your neural pathways than a two-hour session once a week. Think of this as "tuning your instrument" before the concert.

Utilizing Reiji-ho: The Indication of Spirit

While Byosen Reikan is the art of scanning, Reiji-ho is the spiritual guidance behind it. Reiji-ho translates to "indication of the spirit." At the Master level, you do not just move your hand because you "think" it should move; you move because you are being led.

To practice Reiji-ho:

  • Bring your hands to the "Third Eye" position after Gassho.
  • Silently ask: "May the Reiki energy guide my hands to where they are most needed."
  • Wait for a "nudge"‚Äîa subtle physical or intuitive pull‚Äîthat directs your hand to a specific location on the client's body.

Clearing Practitioner 'Energetic Noise'

A high-quality scan requires an objective observer. If you are worried about your mortgage, feeling physically ill, or holding a specific "hope" for the client's outcome, you create energetic noise. This noise distorts the Hibiki signals you receive.

The Neutrality Protocol: Before beginning a scan, visualize a clear glass wall between your personal emotions and the client's field. Remind yourself: "I am the vessel, not the source." This detachment is what allows for the 100% accuracy required of a Master Practitioner.

Coach Tip

If you find yourself "guessing" what the client needs based on their intake form, you are in your head, not your hands. Close your eyes for 3 seconds, exhale deeply, and return to the physical sensation in your Laogong points. The body never lies; the intake form sometimes does.

Check Your Understanding

1. Where is the Laogong point (Palm Chakra) physically located?

In the center of the palm, specifically where the tip of the middle finger touches the palm when making a fist. It is the primary receiver for energetic signals.

2. Why is Gassho Meiso considered a "calibration" tool for practitioners?

It creates a closed energetic circuit, balances the brain hemispheres, and shifts the practitioner into a parasympathetic (relaxed) state, which is necessary for detecting subtle biofield signals.

3. What is the fundamental difference between Byosen Reikan and Reiji-ho?

Byosen Reikan is the technical act of scanning and interpreting sensations (Hibiki), while Reiji-ho is the intuitive/spiritual guidance that directs the practitioner's hands to the area of need.

4. How does "energetic noise" affect a Reiki scan?

It acts as interference, distorting the signals from the client's biofield with the practitioner's own emotions, physical tensions, or biases, leading to an inaccurate scan.
