The Step-by-Step Visualization Protocol

To perform Joshin Kokyu Ho correctly, follow this sequence. This is the "Gold Standard" for the professional practitioner:

  1. Preparation: Sit comfortably with a straight spine. Place your hands in your lap, palms up (or in Gassho).
  2. Inhalation (The Descent of Light): As you breathe in through the nose, visualize a brilliant white light entering your Crown Chakra. See it traveling down your spine, filling your entire body, and settling into the Tanden.
  3. The Expansion: Hold the breath for a comfortable 2-3 seconds. Visualize the light in the Tanden becoming incredibly bright, like a miniature sun.
  4. Exhalation (The Purification): Breathe out through the mouth. Visualize the light expanding from the Tanden, moving through every cell, and radiating out through your skin into infinity. As the light moves out, it carries away any tension, stagnant energy, or Byosen.
Coach Tip: Mindful Mouth Breathing

When exhaling through the mouth, keep the jaw relaxed. Imagine you are gently breathing out through a straw. This slow release helps maintain the "Inner Sun" visualization in the Tanden.

Practical Application: Palm Activation and Self-Clearing

One of the most powerful uses of Joshin Kokyu Ho is increasing the sensitivity of your palms (the Laogong points). This is essential for the Scan phase (Module 1), where you must detect subtle Hibiki (sensations).

The Technique: During the exhalation phase, instead of visualizing the light radiating in all directions, direct the flow of light specifically down your arms and out through the centers of your palms. You will often feel a physical sensation of heat, tingling, or "magnetic" pressure. This is a sign that your energetic "pumps" are primed and ready for the session.

Coach Tip: Clearing Between Clients

If you have back-to-back clients, use 2 minutes of Joshin Kokyu Ho to "wash" your channel. This prevents the energetic signature of Client A from influencing your scan of Client B.

Coach Tip: Managing Lightheadedness

If you feel dizzy, you are likely over-breathing. Return to a natural rhythm. Professional mastery is about intent and visualization, not the volume of air moved.
