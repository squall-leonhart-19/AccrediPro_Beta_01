Aura Smoothing: The First Contact

Before any targeted hand placements begin, the practitioner must address the "static" present in the client's immediate energetic environment. Aura Smoothing is a non-invasive, sweeping motion performed 2-4 inches above the physical body. It serves to "comb" the energetic fibers and signal to the client's nervous system that the session has officially begun.

In professional practice, this is often where the client transitions from their busy, external world into their internal sanctuary. Think of this as smoothing a wrinkled silk sheet; you are removing the superficial energetic "clutter" collected throughout the client's day.

Coach Tip

Many practitioners in their 40s and 50s find that clients in high-stress corporate roles respond best to aura smoothing. It provides a "buffer zone" that allows them to feel safe before direct touch or proximity to sensitive chakras occurs. Use long, slow, rhythmic strokes from crown to feet.

Identifying and Addressing Energetic Resistance

Not every client opens immediately. Energetic Resistance is a natural protective mechanism. It is often not a conscious choice by the client but a subconscious "shielding" resulting from past trauma or current stress. As a professional, you must recognize the "push-back" sensation‚Äîa feeling of coldness, density, or a subtle "magnetic repulsion" when your hands enter their field.

Coach Tip

If you encounter resistance, do not try to "force" the Reiki through. This is a common mistake that leads to practitioner exhaustion. Instead, move your hands slightly further away from the body (into the outer layers of the aura) and wait. Patience is the hallmark of a Master.

The 'Invitation of Light'

Reiki is a collaborative process. While you have the client's verbal consent, the Invitation of Light is the process of seeking permission from the client's Higher Self. This is a silent, internal protocol performed after the Aura Smoothing.

Mentally, the practitioner asks: "May the highest frequencies of Light be accepted by this soul for their greatest good?" This ensures that you are not "imposing" healing, but "offering" it. This subtle shift in intent transforms the session from a "treatment" into a "sacred ceremony."

The 'Opening Sigh': Physiological Indicators

How do you know the field is truly open? The body provides clear, objective markers. When the parasympathetic nervous system takes over, several physical shifts occur:

  • The Opening Sigh: A spontaneous, deep inhalation followed by a long, audible exhalation.
  • REM-like Eye Movement: Rapid fluttering of the eyelids (indicating an Alpha or Theta state).
  • Stomach Gurgling (Borborygmus): A sign that the "rest and digest" system has activated.
  • Temperature Shift: The client may report a sudden feeling of warmth or a localized "flush."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Aura Smoothing at the beginning of a session?

The primary purpose is to soothe the client's initial sensory field, remove superficial energetic "static," and signal the nervous system to transition from an external to an internal focus.

2. Which brainwave state is considered the "healing frequency" for Reiki receptivity?

The Alpha state. It represents a relaxed, receptive mind where the analytical filters of the Beta state are lowered.

3. What should a practitioner do if they feel energetic "push-back" or resistance?

The practitioner should avoid forcing the energy. Instead, they should move their hands further away into the outer layers of the aura, maintain a patient presence, and allow the client's field to soften naturally.

4. Why is the 'Invitation of Light' performed mentally?

It is a sacred protocol to seek permission from the client's Higher Self, ensuring the healing is offered as a gift for the greatest good rather than imposed upon the client.
