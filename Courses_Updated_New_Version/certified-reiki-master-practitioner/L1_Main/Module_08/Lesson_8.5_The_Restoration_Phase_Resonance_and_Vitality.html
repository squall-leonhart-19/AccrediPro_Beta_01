Reiki-ho Techniques: Koki-ho and Gyoshi-ho

In traditional Japanese Reiki (Usui Reiki Ryoho), restoration isn't just done through the hands. Master practitioners utilize the breath and the eyes to direct concentrated Ki.

Koki-ho (Healing with Breath)

The breath is a powerful carrier of Ki. During restoration, you can use Koki-ho to "blow" energy into a depleted area.

  • Process: Inhale deeply into the Hara, visualize the Reiki symbol or pure light filling your lungs, and exhale a gentle, steady breath onto the client's body (or just above it).
  • Application: Excellent for small, specific areas like a joint or the "Third Eye" center.

Gyoshi-ho (Healing with Eyes)

The eyes are windows to the soul, but in Reiki, they are also emitters of energy. Gyoshi-ho involves softening your gaze and "beaming" Reiki through your eyes.

  • Process: Look at the area requiring restoration with a gaze of pure compassion (Karuna). Do not stare; allow the energy to flow through your visual field.
  • Application: Ideal for clients who are sensitive to touch or for infusing the outer layers of the astral body.

Assessing Fullness: Knowing When to Finish

One of the most common questions for Level 1 Deep Dive students is: "How do I know when an area is done?" In the Scan phase, you look for Hibiki (sensations). In the Restore phase, you look for the Shift.

Signs of Replenishment:

  • The "Push-Back": The energy, which previously felt like it was being "sucked in" by the client, now feels like it is gently pushing your hands away.
  • Temperature Equalization: The extreme heat or cold felt during the scan has neutralized to a warm, pleasant glow.
  • Client Physiological Shift: A change in breathing pattern (usually a very deep "reset" breath) or a softening of facial muscles.
  • Visual Clarity: Practitioners often report that the area "looks" brighter or more "solid" in their mind's eye.
