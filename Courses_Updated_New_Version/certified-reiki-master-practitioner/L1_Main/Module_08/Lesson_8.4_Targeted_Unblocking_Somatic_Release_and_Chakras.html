The Anatomy of Stagnation

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we define stagnation as Ki that has lost its frequency of flow. Physiologically, this often manifests in the fascial system. Fascia is highly conductive; when we suppress emotions like grief or anger, the nervous system signals the fascia to contract, creating a "holding pattern."

When you encounter a blockage during the 'Scan' phase, you are likely feeling the Hibiki (vibration) of this contracted tissue. In the 'Unblock' phase, your hands act as a thermal and energetic catalyst. By providing a consistent, high-frequency Ki field, you encourage the client's system to "relax" out of its defensive posture.

Practitioner Insight

Remember that blockages are often protective mechanisms. Never "force" Ki into a blockage. Instead, hold the position with the intent of invitation. Let the client's body decide when it is safe to let go of the stagnation.

Facilitating Somatic Release

As you progress in your Reiki Master journey, you will encounter Somatic Release. This occurs when the physical body releases stored emotional energy. It may look like:

  • Involuntary twitching or "unwinding" of limbs.
  • Sudden changes in breathing patterns (deep sighs or rapid breath).
  • Emotional outbursts (tears or laughter).
  • Temperature spikes or "hot flashes" as Ki moves through a previously cold area.
Professional Standards

If a client begins to cry, do not stop the Reiki flow to grab a tissue immediately. Maintain the connection. Your presence and the Ki flow provide the "container" for their release. Once the peak of the release passes, you can offer support.

The 'U' in SOURCE: Front and Back Balancing

A common mistake among novice practitioners is focusing only on the front of the body. In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, the 'U' (Unblock) phase emphasizes that chakras are three-dimensional "cones" of energy that extend both forward and backward.

The Front of the chakra typically relates to our feeling and will in the present world. The Back of the chakra often relates to our past, our subconscious, and our support systems. To truly unblock a client, you must treat both sides. For example, a "Heart Blockage" often requires as much time on the shoulder blades (back of the heart) as it does on the sternum.
