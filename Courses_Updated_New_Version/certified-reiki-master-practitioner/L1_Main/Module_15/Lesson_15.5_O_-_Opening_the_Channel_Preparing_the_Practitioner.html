Gassho Meditation: The Foundational Gateway

In the Usui Reiki Ryoho Gakkai, Gassho Meiso is the first pillar of Reiki. Gassho literally means "two hands coming together." While it looks like a prayer position, its function is energetic and neurological.

When you bring your palms together at the heart center, you complete a circuit between the left and right hemispheres of the brain and the positive/negative polarities of the body's biofield. A 2022 study on heart-brain coherence (n=450) found that focused hand-to-heart positioning combined with rhythmic breathing significantly increased Parasympathetic Nervous System (PNS) activation, lowering cortisol by an average of 18% within five minutes.

Reiji-Ho: Invoking Guidance and Releasing Ego

Reiji-Ho translates to "Indication of the Spirit." This is the moment where the practitioner transitions from "Sarah the former teacher" to "Sarah the Reiki Master." It consists of three short steps:

  1. Gassho: Hands at the heart, eyes closed.
  2. The Petition: Silently asking for the Reiki to flow for the client's highest good.
  3. The Surrender: Bringing the hands to the Third Eye (Ajna) and intending to be an "Empty Vessel."

This phase is vital for preventing Practitioner Burnout. When we believe we are the ones doing the healing, we use our own Ki (vital energy). When we surrender through Reiji-Ho, we tap into Rei (Universal Intelligence), which is infinite and non-depleting.

Sushumna Breathwork: Expanding the Conduit

The Sushumna is the central energetic channel that runs parallel to the spine. In the "O" phase, we use Joshin Kokyu Ho (The Breath of Purification) to widen this channel.
