The Flow of Ki: The Nadi System

If the Chakras are the power plants, the Nadis are the power lines. Traditional texts suggest there are 72,000 nadis in the human body, but for Level 1 practice, we focus on the "Big Three" that govern the balance of the practitioner and the client.

  • Sushumna: The central channel running through the spinal column. This is the primary conduit for Reiki during a session.
  • Ida: The lunar, feminine, cooling channel ending in the left nostril. It governs the parasympathetic nervous system.
  • Pingala: The solar, masculine, warming channel ending in the right nostril. It governs the sympathetic nervous system.

As an AccrediPro practitioner, your goal during the U: Unblock phase is to ensure these three channels are clear. Stagnation in the Ida can lead to depression and lethargy, while stagnation in the Pingala can manifest as anxiety and inflammation.

Energetic Cysts: How Trauma Stagnates

When a client experiences an emotional trauma they cannot process, the energy doesn't just disappear. It often becomes "sequestered" in the physical tissue. In subtle anatomy, we call these Energetic Cysts.

These cysts act like rocks in a stream, forcing the Ki to flow around them, which creates turbulence and eventual depletion downstream. Statistics from somatic experiencing research suggest that up to 80% of chronic muscle tension has an underlying emotional component that has "crystallized" into the biofield.

Coach Tip: Detecting Cysts

During your scan, an energetic cyst often feels like a "hard" or "dense" spot in the air, even if the physical tissue feels soft. Don't try to "push" through it. Simply hold the position and let the Reiki "melt" the density over time.
