    The Bridge: Hayashi & Takata

    Reiki might have remained a small Japanese tradition if not for Chujiro Hayashi. As a retired naval officer and physician, Hayashi brought a clinical structure to Reiki. He developed the standardized hand positions and opened a Reiki clinic in Tokyo.

    In 1935, Hawayo Takata, a Japanese-American woman from Hawaii, arrived at Hayashi's clinic seeking healing for several serious ailments. Her recovery was so profound that she became the first woman and the first person to bring Reiki to the West.

    The Master's Perspective

    Hawayo Takata is often criticized for "Westernizing" Reiki by adding Christian-friendly backstories to Usui's life. As a Master, view this through the lens of cultural survival. In the post-WWII era, Takata's adaptations allowed Reiki to flourish in a Western world that might have otherwise rejected a purely Japanese spiritual practice.
