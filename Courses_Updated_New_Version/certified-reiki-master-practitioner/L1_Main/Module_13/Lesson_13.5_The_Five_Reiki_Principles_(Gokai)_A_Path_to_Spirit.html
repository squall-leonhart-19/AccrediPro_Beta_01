The Esoteric Roots of the Gokai

Mikao Usui did not create the Gokai in a vacuum. They were inspired by the Meiji Emperor's poems and deep Buddhist philosophy. In Japanese, they begin with the phrase "Kyo dake wa," which translates to "Just for today." This is a profound psychological anchor that prevents the practitioner from becoming overwhelmed by the past or future.

When we look at the original kanji, we see that Usui-Sensei intended for these to be spiritual medicine. He believed that if the mind is not healed, the body cannot remain healthy. This aligns with modern psychoneuroimmunology, which shows that chronic mental stress (anger and worry) directly suppresses the immune system.

Gassho: The Gateway Practice

The Gassho Meditation is the foundational practice for mental stillness. In Japanese, "Gassho" means "two hands coming together." This mudra balances the left and right hemispheres of the brain and centers the practitioner in the Heart Chakra.

The Technique for Masters:

  1. Position: Sit comfortably. Bring your hands together in front of your chest (the heart center).
  2. Focus: Close your eyes. Direct your entire focus to the point where your two middle fingers meet.
  3. Breath: Use Joshin Kokyu Ho (the breath of purification) to inhale Ki into the hara and exhale through the fingertips.
  4. Recitation: Silently or aloud, recite the Gokai. Feel the vibration of each word in your palms.
Coach Tip

Science shows that focusing on a single point of tactile contact (like the middle fingers) stimulates the somatosensory cortex, which helps quiet the "Default Mode Network" (DMN) associated with rumination and anxiety. This is why Gassho is so effective for "clearing the vessel" before a session.

Right Livelihood & Energy Flow

The fourth principle, Gyo wo hage-me, is often translated as "work hard." However, in the context of Reiki Mastery, it refers to Right Livelihood. This is the concept that our work should be an extension of our spiritual values and contribute positively to the world.

Many women entering this field struggle with the "money vs. spirituality" paradigm. They feel that charging for healing is "wrong." However, the Gokai teaches us that diligent work is a spiritual requirement. When you charge a professional fee, you are engaging in a fair exchange of energy that allows you to sustain your practice and continue serving others.
