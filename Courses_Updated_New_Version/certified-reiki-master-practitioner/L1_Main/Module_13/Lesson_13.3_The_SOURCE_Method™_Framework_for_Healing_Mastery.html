O: Opening the Sacred Conduit

The "O" in SOURCE stands for Open. This is the most critical phase for the practitioner's longevity. Without a proper opening, you risk "taking on" the client's energy or feeling drained after a session.

Opening involves two distinct actions:

As a Level 1 practitioner, your primary goal is to be a clear conduit. You are not "giving" your own energy; you are opening a doorway for Universal Ki to flow through you. S: Mastery of Byosen Reikan

The "S" stands for Scan. In traditional Japanese Reiki, this is known as Byosen Reikan. This is the "diagnostic" phase where you use your hands to feel the client's biofield without making physical contact.

During the scan, you are looking for Hibiki (sensations). These sensations indicate where the energy is most needed. Mastery of the scan is what makes you a "Master Practitioner" rather than a novice.

The 4 Primary Hibiki Sensations:

  • Heat (Atataki): Indicates a high demand for energy or active inflammation.
  • Tingling (Hibiki): Often feels like "pins and needles," indicating energy movement or breakthrough.
  • Cold (Hiyari): Suggests a deep, chronic blockage or "emptiness" in the area.
  • Throbbing (Atsu-on): Indicates strong congestion or emotional "pressure" waiting to be released.
Coach Tip: Trusting Your Hands

In the beginning, you might only feel "heat." That is perfectly normal! As your palm chakras activate through daily self-practice, your sensitivity will refine. Don't rush the process; the "Scan" is a conversation between your hands and the client's soul.

U & R: The Engine of Change

The Unblock and Restore phases are where the "heavy lifting" of the session occurs.

Unblock: This involves specific hand placements designed to break up stagnant Ki. We focus on the "Heart Gateway" and the "Solar Plexus" during Level 1, as these are the primary sites for emotional and survival-based congestion.

Restore: Once the block is cleared, the field is "empty." You must then fill that space with high-frequency Ki. Think of it like a garden: first, you pull the weeds (Unblock), then you plant the seeds and water them (Restore).

C & E: Ensuring Lasting Results

The final two phases are often skipped by untrained practitioners, leading to clients feeling "spacey" or "ungrounded" after a session.

Center: This phase focuses on the Hara (the lower dantian). By placing your hands on the client's lower abdomen and feet, you pull their awareness back into their physical body. This is essential for safety, especially if the client has to drive home after the session.

Embody: This is the integration phase. It involves sharing the Gokai (Five Principles) with the client and providing them with a simple "homework" task to maintain their energetic state. This transforms a 60-minute session into a 24/7 lifestyle change.

Coach Tip: The Professional Hand-Off

Always give your client a glass of water and 5 minutes of quiet time during the 'Embody' phase. This demonstrates professional care and ensures they have fully "landed" back in the physical world before they leave your sanctuary.
