Common Mistakes to Avoid

New practitioners often fall into these traps. Being aware of them is the first step toward mastery.

Mistake Why It Happens The Master's Correction
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome; feeling the need to "prove" Reiki works. Keep it simple. Focus on relaxation and stress reduction.
Apologizing Fear that the client isn't having a "magical" experience. Never apologize for the energy. Trust the process.
Rushing the Intake Anxiety to get to the "real work" on the table. The intake is where the client decides to trust you. Take your time.
Using Too Much Jargon Trying to sound like an expert. Use relatable terms like "recharging your batteries" or "unwinding."
Luna's Income Insight

Practitioners who offer a "Introductory Package" (3 sessions for $250) often have a 70% higher retention rate than those who sell single sessions. It takes time for the body to learn how to receive energy.

Success Markers

How do you know your coaching and session were successful? Look for these signs:

  • Physical Release: The client's breathing deepens, or they experience "stomach gurgling" (a sign the parasympathetic nervous system has activated).
  • The "Reiki Glow": The client's face looks softer and less tense after sitting up.
  • Follow-up Questions: The client asks, "When can I come back?" instead of "Did it work?"
  • Personal Vulnerability: The client shares a personal emotion or realization they had during the silence.
Final Thought

You are not "fixing" the client. You are holding the space for them to heal themselves. That shift in perspective will cure your imposter syndrome faster than any book ever could.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client says, "I don't believe in this stuff, but my wife made me come." How do you respond?

"I appreciate your honesty! The great thing about Reiki is that it doesn't require 'belief' to work. It's simply a way to help your body enter a state of deep relaxation. Why don't we just treat this as an hour of quiet time for you to rest?"

2. What is the benefit of using the term "nervous system regulation" with professional clients?

It provides a bridge between holistic energy work and modern science, making the practice feel more grounded, legitimate, and safe for those with academic or professional backgrounds.

3. Why should you avoid over-explaining the history of Reiki during the first intake?

Over-explaining can overwhelm a client who is already stressed. The first session should focus on the client's needs and goals, not a history lesson. Save the deep history for when they ask or for a Level 1 class!

4. If a client falls asleep and snores, what does this indicate?

This is a major success marker! It indicates the client has fully surrendered and their body has moved into the delta or theta brainwave state, which is where the most profound physical healing occurs.

Key Takeaways

  • Trust the Silence: You don't need to fill every gap with words. Your presence is the primary tool.
  • Normalize the Experience: Use scripts to let clients know their feelings (or lack thereof) are normal.
  • Professionalism Sells: Clear boundaries, intake forms, and authoritative speech command higher rates.
  • Focus on Results: Speak to the "headaches" or "stress" the client mentioned, connecting Reiki back to their specific goals.
