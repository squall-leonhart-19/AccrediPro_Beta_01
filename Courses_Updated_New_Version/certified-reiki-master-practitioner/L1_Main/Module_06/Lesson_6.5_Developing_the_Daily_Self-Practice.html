Habit Stacking for Spiritual Integration

The secret to consistency is not willpower; it is environmental design. Using the concept of "Habit Stacking" (popularized by James Clear), we attach the new Reiki habit to an existing, non-negotiable anchor.

Coach Tip: The Anchor Method

Ask your client: "What is one thing you do every single day without fail?" (e.g., brushing teeth, checking email, waiting for the shower to warm up). That is their anchor. Tell them: "After I [Anchor], I will [5-Minute Reiki Ritual]."

Common Stacks for Reiki Practitioners:

  • The Commuter Stack: "While I sit at red lights, I will place one hand on my heart and recite 'Just for today, I will not anger.'"
  • The Shower Stack: "While the water hits my shoulders, I will visualize the Reiki light performing Joshin Kokyu Ho (Purification Breath)."
  • The Pillow Stack: "As soon as my head hits the pillow, I will place my hands on my eyes for self-healing until I drift off."

By attaching the Reiki practice to an existing, automatic habit (like brewing coffee), it bypasses the need for high willpower and makes the practice a seamless part of the daily routine.

The subconscious belief that self-care is selfish, which often prevents women in the 40-55 age bracket from prioritizing their daily energetic maintenance.
