Common Signs of a Release Point:

  • Stomach Gurgling (Borborygmus): A classic sign that the parasympathetic nervous system has engaged and energy is moving through the digestive tract.

Managing Emotional Releases

As an expert practitioner, you must be prepared for emotional catharsis. When "unblocking," clients may suddenly cry, laugh, or feel a surge of anger. This is normal and desirable.

The Professional Response:

  1. Maintain the Connection: Do not pull your hands away abruptly. This can "shock" the client's field.
  2. Provide a "Container": Continue to channel Reiki with a calm, steady presence. Your stability allows them to feel safe in their vulnerability.
  3. Normalize the Experience: After the session, simply say, "It's very common for the body to release emotions during Reiki. How do you feel now?"
Coach Tip

Always have high-quality tissues within reach, but do not offer them immediately. Sometimes offering a tissue can signal to the client that they should "stop" crying. Let the release happen fully first.

The Sweep Technique (Kenyoku-ho Variation)

The "Sweep" is an active Level 1 technique used to clear "auric debris" that has been loosened during the hand positions. It is particularly effective for clients who feel "heavy" or "cluttered."

How to Perform the Sweep:

  • Stand at the client's side (or head if working distally).
  • Cup your hands slightly, about 3-5 inches above the client's physical body.
  • With a smooth, rhythmic motion, "sweep" your hands from the Crown down toward the feet.
  • Imagine you are brushing away gray mist or dust.
  • Flick your hands toward the ground at the end of each stroke to ground the energy.
