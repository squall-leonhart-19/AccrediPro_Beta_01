Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

Even experienced career-changers often fall into these traps when they first start their Reiki business:

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to explain the entire history of Mikao Usui during the intake. Keep it focused on the client's needs.
  • Using "Small" Language: Saying "I'm just a beginner" or "I'm trying this out." Instead, say "I am a Certified Reiki Practitioner."
  • Lack of Physical Boundaries: Not asking for permission before touching the client or failing to explain that they stay fully clothed.
  • Apologizing for the Price: Feeling guilty about charging professional rates. Remember: You are providing a service that saves them time, stress, and potentially medical costs.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to mention clinical data (like heart rate reduction) to a client like Elena?

It bridges the gap between the "mystical" and the "practical," making the client feel safe and validating their investment in a professional service.

2. What is the recommended duration for a professional intake?

10 to 15 minutes. This is long enough to build rapport but short enough to ensure the majority of the hour is spent on the healing table.

3. What is a "Success Marker" in a first-time session with a skeptic?

The client shifting from a "questioning" posture to a "relaxed/receiving" posture, and their willingness to try a simple "homework" task post-session.

4. How should you handle a client who starts talking about their problems mid-session?

Gently acknowledge them and say, "Let's hold that thought for our post-session chat. For now, just focus on your breath and allow yourself to receive." This maintains the professional boundary.
