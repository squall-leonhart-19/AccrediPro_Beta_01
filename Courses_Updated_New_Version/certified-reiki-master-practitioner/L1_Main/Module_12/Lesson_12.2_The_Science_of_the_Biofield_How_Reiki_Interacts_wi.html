Defining the Human Biofield

For decades, the concept of an "aura" was dismissed as pseudoscience. However, modern physics now recognizes the Human Biofield as a complex, extremely weak electromagnetic field that surrounds and permeates the living body. This is not a "belief"; it is a measurable reality of biological systems.

The biofield consists of two primary components:

  • The Endogenous Field: Measurable electromagnetic fields produced by the heart (ECG), brain (EEG), and muscles (EMG). The heart's magnetic field is roughly 5,000 times stronger than the brain's and can be detected several feet away from the body.
  • The Subtle Field: Non-classical fields that interact with matter at the subatomic level, often referred to as Ki, Prana, or Zero-Point Energy.
