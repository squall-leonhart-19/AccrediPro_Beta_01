The Three Pillars of Reiki

Traditional Usui Reiki is built upon three foundational practices, often called the "Three Pillars." These are the internal mechanics that power the S.O.U.R.C.E. Methodâ„¢.

1. Gassho (The Meditative Pillar)

Gassho literally means "two hands coming together." This is more than a gesture; it is a meditation practiced to clear the mind and center the practitioner. In the Open phase of our method, Gassho creates the "Empty Vessel" state necessary for high-frequency transmission.

2. Reiji-ho (The Intuitive Pillar)

Reiji-ho translates to "indication of the Reiki power." This is the moment where the practitioner asks the energy to guide their hands. It is the bridge between the Scan and Unblock phases, allowing the energy to dictate the treatment rather than the practitioner's ego.

3. Chiryo (The Treatment Pillar)

Chiryo is the actual delivery of Reiki. In Level 1, this focuses heavily on the 12-15 standard hand positions that cover the major endocrine glands and vital organs. This aligns with the Restore phase of our framework.
