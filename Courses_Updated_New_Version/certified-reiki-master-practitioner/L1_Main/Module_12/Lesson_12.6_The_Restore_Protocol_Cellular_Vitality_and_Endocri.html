Protocols for Acute vs. Chronic Conditions

One of the hallmarks of a Master Practitioner is the ability to adapt the Restore phase based on the client's physiological state. Not all depletion is created equal.

Condition Type Characteristics Reiki Protocol / Dwell Time
Acute (Sudden onset, injury, flu) High intensity, localized pain, rapid Ki drainage. Short, frequent sessions (20-30 min). Focus on the site of injury and the immune-related glands (Thymus).
Chronic (Long-term fatigue, autoimmune) Low vitality, deep depletion, systemic imbalance. Long, deep sessions (60-90 min). High dwell time (7-10 min) on kidneys, adrenals, and hara.

In chronic cases, the body's "Ki tank" is effectively empty. The first few sessions may not show immediate results because the energy is going toward foundational cellular repair before it manifests as "feeling better."
