Distinguishing Practitioner Energy vs. Client Byosen

One of the biggest hurdles for new practitioners is the "Feedback Loop." You must distinguish between:

  • Active Giving: The heat coming from your hands as Reiki flows.
  • Byosen Detection: The sensation being drawn out of the client or pushing against your palms.
Coach Tip: The Friction Test

If you aren't sure if the heat is yours or theirs, briefly move your hand away from the client (about 2 feet). If the sensation remains in your palm, it is your own energy flow. If the sensation stops and only reappears when you move back over the client, you have found Byosen.

Check Your Understanding

1. You feel a "pins and needles" sensation over a client's shoulder. What Level of Hibiki is this, and what does it usually signify?

This is Level 3 (Tingling/Prickling). It typically signifies nerve stagnation, chronic tension, or suppressed emotions (like anger) that are "stuck" in the tissues.

2. Why do we recommend scanning 2-4 inches above the body rather than touching the skin?

Scanning in the Etheric Layer (2-4 inches) allows the practitioner to sense energetic variances without being confused by the client's actual skin temperature (physical body heat). It also maintains professional boundaries.

3. What is the difference between Byosen and Hibiki?

Byosen is the actual "toxic accumulation" or blockage in the client's body. Hibiki is the "echo" or sensation that the practitioner feels in their hands as a result of that Byosen.

4. If a practitioner feels "Pain" in their own hand while scanning a client's knee, what should they do?

This is Level 5 Hibiki. The practitioner should not pull away in fear; instead, they should stay in that position (moving into the 'U' - Unblock phase) to allow Reiki to neutralize the intense stagnation, while ensuring they are "grounded" to avoid absorbing the energy.
