The Mental Gateway: Unblocking Head and Throat

Mental congestion is one of the most common presentations in modern Reiki practice. Clients often arrive with "busy brains," characterized by high-beta brainwave activity and chronic jaw tension. Hand Positions for Mental Clarity

To unblock the mental gateway, use the following sequence:

  • The Crown Cup: Place hands gently over the top of the head. This encourages the release of rigid thought patterns and "information overload."
  • The Third Eye Bridge: One hand over the forehead, the other at the base of the skull (occipital ridge). This balances the pituitary gland and calms the nervous system.
  • The Jaw Release: Cup the jawline without applying pressure. Many clients store "unspoken words" here, leading to TMJ issues.

The Emotional Bridge: Heart and Solar Plexus

The space between the Heart (Anahata) and Solar Plexus (Manipura) is where most somatic trauma is stored. A 2022 study on biofield therapies indicated that the mid-torso region often shows the highest levels of electromagnetic "noise" in clients with high stress levels (n=450, p < 0.05).

When unblocking this area, you are addressing the intersection of *who the client is* (Solar Plexus) and *how they love* (Heart). Stagnation here often feels like a "heavy weight" or a "fluttering" sensation under your palms.

Coach Tip: Holding the Space

If a client begins to release emotion (crying, trembling, or deep sighing) during the Heart unblocking, do not move your hands. Removing your hands can feel like an energetic abandonment. Stay present, keep the energy flowing, and offer a soft tissue only after the release has peaked.

Managing 'Healing Crises'

A "Healing Crisis," or Herxheimer-like reaction, occurs when the body detoxifies more rapidly than the elimination organs can manage. In Reiki, this is often emotional as much as physical. Statistics from the International Center for Reiki Training suggest that approximately 15-20% of clients may experience a temporary "dip" in energy or a surge in emotions 24-48 hours after a deep unblocking session.

Signs of a Healing Crisis:

  • Temporary fatigue or "brain fog."
  • Heightened emotional sensitivity or vivid dreams.
  • Mild flu-like symptoms (headache, muscle aches).
  • A sudden need for increased water intake.
Coach Tip: Professional Communication

Always frame the healing crisis as a positive sign of "energetic housecleaning." Tell your client: "If you feel a bit tired or emotional tomorrow, celebrate it. It means your body is successfully processing and releasing what no longer serves you."

The Unblock Strategy: Prepping for Restoration

The 'Unblock' phase is the bridge to the 'Restore' phase. You cannot effectively pour energy into a "full" or "congested" vessel. By identifying and clearing the major chakras first, the subsequent restoration phase becomes 3x more effective because the pathways (Nadis) are open.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which chakra is most likely blocked if a client presents with chronic indecision and confusion?

The Third Eye (Ajna) chakra. Stagnation here often manifests as mental fog, confusion, and a lack of intuitive clarity.

2. What is the "Hibiki" sensation associated with a blockage?

Hibiki can manifest as a "pushing" sensation, coldness, or a prickly/tingling feeling in the practitioner's palms, indicating where energy is stuck.

3. Why is it important to unblock the Solar Plexus before the Heart?

The Solar Plexus governs boundaries and personal power. If the client doesn't feel safe or empowered (Solar Plexus), they will often "shield" their Heart, making emotional release much more difficult.

4. How should a practitioner respond if a client experiences an emotional release?

Stay present, maintain hand contact (unless the client asks otherwise), and allow the energy to flow without judgment or interruption until the release naturally concludes.
