The 'Nurturing Hold': A Specialized Technique

The Nurturing Hold is a Master-level hand position specifically designed for the Restore phase. Unlike standard hand positions that focus on general flow, the Nurturing Hold creates an "energetic cocoon" around the heart center.

How to Perform the Nurturing Hold:

  1. Positioning: Stand at the client's side. Place your dominant hand approximately 2 inches above the physical heart (the Anahata).
  2. The "Anchor" Hand: Place your non-dominant hand gently on the client's shoulder or the "Higher Heart" area (just below the collarbone).
  3. The Visualization: Visualize Cho Ku Rei (The Power Symbol) spinning in a soft, rose-gold light between your palms, sandwiching the heart center.
  4. The Intent: Silently state the intent: "I fill this space with the infinite love of the Universe. You are held. You are safe. You are whole."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Restore" phase critical after an emotional release in the "Unblock" phase?

Because releasing a blockage creates an energetic vacuum. If not intentionally filled with Reiki (emotional resupply), the client may feel "empty," "raw," or "vulnerable," and may inadvertently return to old emotional patterns to fill the void.

2. What is the primary focus of the "Nurturing Hold" technique?

The Nurturing Hold creates an "energetic cocoon" around the heart center to infuse it with rose-gold light and the intent of safety, wholeness, and infinite love, moving the session from "clearing" to "filling."

3. Which two centers are bridged to help a client move from "I am loved" to "I am powerful"?

The Heart (or Higher Heart) and the Solar Plexus. This bridge integrates compassion with personal power and self-worth.

4. What does the term "Emotional Thirst" refer to in a Reiki context?

A state of etheric depletion where the client's biofield lacks the "spiritual nutrients" required for emotional resilience, often manifesting as a "hollow" feeling in the chest or post-session shakiness.
