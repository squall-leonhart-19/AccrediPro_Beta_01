Common Mistakes & Success Markers

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps during the "Restore" phase of a session.

The Mistake Why It Hinders Restoration The Correction
Over-Explaining Keeps the client in their "thinking brain" (Beta waves). Use fewer words; let the energy do the talking.
Rushing the Intake Makes the client feel like just another "appointment." Spend 5 extra minutes on the "connection" before the table.
Predicting Outcomes Creates "performance anxiety" for the client. Use open-ended language: "Let's see what your body needs."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "validating the depletion" important for a client like Diane?

It removes the "guilt" of being tired. When the client stops fighting their fatigue, the parasympathetic nervous system can finally engage, allowing restoration to begin.

2. What is the "Restoration Gap"?

It is the period between a major energy output (like a 25-year career) and the availability of new creative energy. It is a necessary "re-filling" phase that cannot be skipped.

3. If a coach feels "imposter syndrome" during a session, what should they focus on?

Focus on being a "calm harbor." The client needs your grounded presence more than they need your expertise or "perfect" performance.

4. Which brain wave state are we trying to move the client toward for deep restoration?

We are moving them away from high-Beta (stress/thinking) and toward Alpha and Theta states, where deep cellular and energetic restoration occurs.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Validation is Healing: Telling a client their fatigue is "not their fault" is a powerful restorative act.
  • Slow the Pace: Your speaking rhythm should mirror the restorative state you want the client to achieve.
  • Silence is a Tool: Don't feel the need to fill every gap with words; the Reiki energy fills the silence.
  • Success is Subjective: A successful restorative session is one where the client feels "re-connected" to their own body.
