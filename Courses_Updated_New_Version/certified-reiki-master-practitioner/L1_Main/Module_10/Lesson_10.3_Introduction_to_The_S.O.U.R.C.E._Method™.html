The Professional Session Flow

A professional session is more than just hand placements. It is an energetic arc that begins before the client enters the room and continues after they leave.

1. Preparation (The "O" Phase)

This involves Gassho Meiso (meditation) and Joshin Kokyu Ho (cleansing breath). You are preparing your nervous system to be a clear conduit. For a Level 1 practitioner, this phase is critical to prevent taking on the client's "stuff."

2. The Assessment (The "S" Phase)

Before applying energy, you scan the biofield. You are looking for heat, cold, tingling, or "pulling" sensations. This allows you to create a personalized "map" for the session rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach.

Coach Tip

Always document your scan findings! Clients are deeply impressed when you can say, "I noticed some heat over your solar plexus," and they respond, "That's exactly where I've been feeling anxious." Documentation builds massive trust.

Ethics & The "Do No Harm" Principle

As a Level 1 practitioner, your primary ethical responsibility is to operate within your Scope of Practice. Reiki is a complementary therapy, not a replacement for medical or psychological care.

  • No Diagnosis: Never tell a client they have a specific disease based on your scan. Instead, describe the energetic sensation (e.g., "I'm sensing some stagnation here").
  • Informed Consent: Always explain where you will be placing your hands (or if you will be working off-body).
  • Confidentiality: Treat every session as a sacred and private clinical encounter.
  • Practitioner Well-being: You cannot pour from an empty cup. Self-Reiki is an ethical requirement of the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢.
Coach Tip

If a client asks for medical advice, use this script: "As a Reiki practitioner, I work with the energetic biofield to support your body's natural healing process. For medical concerns, it is vital that you consult with your primary physician. I am happy to work alongside their treatment plan."

Preparing for the Level 1 Attunement

The attunement (or Reiju) is the process by which your Reiki Master-Teacher opens your energetic channels to the Reiki frequency. While the Master performs the attunement, your internal state determines the depth of the experience.

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we recommend a 3-day preparation period:

  1. Hydration: Ki moves through the water in our cells. Increase your water intake by 20%.
  2. Intention: Write down your "Why." Why are you stepping onto this path?
  3. Detoxification: Minimize caffeine, alcohol, and heavy processed foods to allow the nervous system to settle.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢ and traditional intuitive Reiki?

The S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢ provides a systematic, 6-stage clinical framework that standardizes the session protocol, whereas traditional Reiki often relies primarily on the practitioner's moment-to-moment intuition without a structured sequence.

2. What does the "S" in S.O.U.R.C.E. stand for, and what is its objective?

"S" stands for Scan (Byosen Reikan). Its objective is to detect Hibiki (sensations) in the client's biofield to identify areas of imbalance or stagnation before beginning the energetic application.

3. True or False: A Level 1 practitioner should diagnose a client's physical ailments if they feel a strong heat sensation during the scan.

False. Ethics and Scope of Practice dictate that practitioners never diagnose. They should only describe the energetic sensations and refer the client to a medical professional for diagnosis.

4. Why is the "Embody" phase (the "E") included in the framework?

The Embody phase focuses on integration and the Five Principles (Gokai). It ensures the energetic shifts from the session are grounded into the client's daily life and somatic awareness.
