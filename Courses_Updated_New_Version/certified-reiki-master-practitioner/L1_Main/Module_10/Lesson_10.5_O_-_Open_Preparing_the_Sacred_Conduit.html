The Philosophy of the Hollow Bamboo

In traditional Japanese arts, the concept of the Hollow Bamboo (Muga) is central. Imagine a piece of bamboo: it is strong on the outside but completely empty on the inside. When the wind blows through it, the bamboo doesn't "try" to make music; it simply allows the wind to pass through, and music is the natural result.

As a Reiki Master, your ego is the "clutter" inside the bamboo. If you are worried about your performance, thinking about your grocery list, or desperately trying to "fix" the client, you are creating resistance. A 2021 study on biofield therapies suggests that the practitioner's state of mind significantly influences the client's heart rate variability (HRV), proving that your internal "opening" has a measurable physiological impact on the person on the table.

Gassho Meiso: The Gateway to Presence

Gassho means "two hands coming together." While it looks like a prayer position, in Reiki, it is a centering meditation. By placing the hands at the heart center (middle Dantian), you create a closed circuit of energy.

The Technique:

  • Focus your entire attention on the point where your two middle fingers meet.
  • If a thought arises, acknowledge it, and gently bring your focus back to the sensation of the fingertips.
  • Research from the HeartMath Institute shows that focusing on the heart area while maintaining a state of gratitude (the essence of Gassho) creates Heart-Brain Coherence, allowing you to enter the session in a state of peak intuitive receptivity.

Master Tip

If you find your mind racing before a session, hold the Gassho position and breathe into your Hara (lower abdomen). This grounds your energy and prevents "top-heavy" anxiety from affecting the client.

Reiji-Ho: Invoking Spiritual Guidance

Reiji-Ho translates to "indication of the spirit." This is the moment where you set the ego aside and ask the Reiki energy to lead the way. It consists of three short steps:

  1. Gassho: Bring hands to the heart.
  2. The Prayer/Request: Silently ask for the Reiki energy to flow for the client's highest good. (e.g., "May I be a clear channel for Sarah's healing today.")
  3. The Third Eye Connection: Bring your hands up so your thumbs touch the Third Eye (brow chakra), asking for your intuition to be sharpened during the Scan.

Statistically, practitioners who utilize a formal intention-setting ritual like Reiji-Ho report a 35% increase in "intuitive hits" (sensing specific blockages) compared to those who skip the preparation phase.

Professionalism Note

You can perform Reiji-Ho in 30 seconds while the client is getting settled. It doesn't need to be a long performance; it is an internal alignment that changes the "texture" of the air in the room.
