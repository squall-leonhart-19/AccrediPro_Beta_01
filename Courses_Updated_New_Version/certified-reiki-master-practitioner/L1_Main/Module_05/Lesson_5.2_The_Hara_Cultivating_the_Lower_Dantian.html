Anatomy of the Hara: Locating the Lower Dantian

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we recognize the Hara as the primary battery of the human system. While Western anatomy focuses on the physical organs of the lower abdomen, energetic anatomy identifies the Seika Tanden (often called the Lower Dantian in Chinese traditions).

Physically, the Hara is located approximately two to three finger-widths below the navel and about two inches deep toward the spine. It corresponds roughly with the body's physical center of gravity. Energetically, it is the reservoir where Ki (life force) is gathered, stored, and distributed.

Coach Tip

When teaching clients to find their Hara, have them place their dominant hand over their lower belly and take a deep breath. Tell them: "Imagine there is a glowing golden sphere the size of a tennis ball deep behind your palm. This is your center of power."

The Japanese Reiki Perspective

Traditional Japanese Reiki, or Usui Reiki Ryoho, places significantly more emphasis on the Hara than many Western lineages. Mikao Usui taught that a practitioner must be "anchored in the Tanden" to facilitate deep healing without becoming depleted.

In Japanese culture, the Hara represents more than just a chakra; it represents one's character and soul. A person with "great Hara" is considered stable, courageous, and wise. For the Reiki Master, the Hara is the furnace that refines the energy before it is channeled through the hands.

Practical Exercise: Joshin Kokyu-Ho

The Joshin Kokyu-Ho (Spirit Cleansing Breath) is the gold standard for Hara cultivation. It trains the body to pull Reiki into the Tanden and expand it through the entire biofield.

  1. Preparation: Sit comfortably with a straight spine. Place your hands in Gassho (prayer position) at your heart, then rest them palm-up on your thighs.
  2. Inhalation: Breathe in through the nose. Imagine a stream of white light entering through your Crown Chakra, moving down the center of your body, and settling deep into your Hara.
  3. Retention: Hold the breath for a comfortable 2-3 seconds, feeling the energy "condense" and glow brighter in the Tanden.
  4. Exhalation: Breathe out through the mouth. Imagine the energy expanding from your Hara, filling every cell of your body, and then radiating out through your skin into your aura.
  5. Repeat: Continue for 5-10 minutes.
Coach Tip

If you find your mind wandering during Joshin Kokyu-Ho, gently touch your lower abdomen. The physical sensation will help "magnetize" your focus back to the Hara.
