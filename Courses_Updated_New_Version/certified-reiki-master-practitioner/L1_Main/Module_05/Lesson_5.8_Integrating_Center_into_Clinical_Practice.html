Common Master Pitfalls

Even experienced practitioners can lose their center. Watch out for these 4 common mistakes during the client intake:

1

Mirroring the Client's Speed

If the client talks fast and you talk fast to "keep up," you've lost your center. You must remain the "slow" variable in the equation.

2

Over-Explaining the Process

Masters don't need to prove how it works. Using too much "science talk" can actually keep the client stuck in their analytical mind.

3

Apologizing for the Client's Discomfort

Don't say "I'm sorry you're stressed." Instead, use "I acknowledge that you're carrying a lot." Empathy is centered; pity is uncentered.

4

Fidgeting

Adjusting your clothes, clicking a pen, or shifting in your chair signals to the client that you aren't fully present.
