Common Mistakes & Success Markers

When I was starting out, I used to talk too much to fill the "awkward" silence. Here is what to watch for as you apply the S.O.L.A.C.E. Methodâ„¢.

4 Common Pitfalls

  • The "Silver Lining" Trap: Trying to find something positive too early (e.g., "At least your son is doing well!"). This shuts down the Lamentation phase.
  • Rushing to Empowerment: Moving to the "E" (Evolution) before the client has felt fully "S" (Safe) and "A" (Affirmed).
  • Over-Identifying: Saying "I know exactly how you feel." Instead, use: "I can see how deeply that affects you."
  • Fixing the Feeling: Treating grief like a problem to be solved rather than an experience to be witnessed.
Expert Wisdom

If you find yourself getting anxious and wanting to "fix" the client, take a deep breath and return to Phase O: Observation. Simply describe what you see: "I see tears in your eyes right now. Let's just stay with that for a moment."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Safety & Space" phase the first step in the S.O.L.A.C.E. Methodâ„¢?

Grief creates a state of hyper-vigilance in the nervous system. Without establishing a felt sense of safety, the client's brain cannot move out of "survival mode" to process the deeper emotional work required in later phases.

2. Diane says, "I should be over this by now." Which phase of S.O.L.A.C.E. is most critical here?

Affirmation & Compassion (A/C). The coach must affirm that grief has no timeline and provide the compassionate container that allows Diane to drop her self-judgment.

3. What is a "Success Marker" in the Listening & Lamentation phase?

A success marker is when the client stops "performing" or "polishing" their story and begins to share the raw, messy, or "unacceptable" feelings (like anger, relief, or deep despair) without the coach flinching.

4. True or False: You should always reach the "Evolution" phase in the first session.

False. In many cases, especially with compounded grief, the first several sessions may stay entirely within the S, O, and L phases. Rushing to Evolution can feel like a betrayal to the client's pain.
