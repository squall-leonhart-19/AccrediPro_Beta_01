Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Discovery" phase of the enrollment call?

The goal is to help the client articulate their current pain and their desired future state (the gap), while identifying the barriers that have kept them stuck. This builds the "need" for your specific framework.

2. How should you respond if a client asks, "Why is this so much more expensive than the grief group at my church?"

Highlight the difference between support and transformation. A church group provides community support (which is vital), but your program provides a specialized, proprietary framework (S.O.L.A.C.E.â„¢) designed for individual integration and specific legacy outcomes.

3. True or False: You should wait for the client to ask for the price before mentioning it.

False. You should lead the conversation. After presenting your approach, you should transition naturally into the investment as part of the "Prescription" phase. Leading the price conversation demonstrates authority.

4. What is the most effective way to handle the "I need to think about it" objection?

Acknowledge the importance of the decision, then ask a clarifying question: "I completely understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either the time, the money, or a doubt that this will work for them. Which one is it for you?" This allows you to address the real concern.
