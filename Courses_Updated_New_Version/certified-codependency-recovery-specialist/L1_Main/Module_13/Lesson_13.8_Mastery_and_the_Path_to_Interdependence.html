Check Your Understanding

1. Why is asking "What did you buy for yourself?" more effective than "Why did you give her the money?"

"Why" often triggers defensiveness and shame. Asking what they "bought" (e.g., relief from guilt, temporary peace) invites the client to see their behavior as a transactional coping mechanism rather than a character flaw.

2. What is a physical sign that you, as the coach, might be over-functioning in a session?

Signs include leaning forward excessively, heart rate increasing, feeling a "rush" to solve the client's problem, or feeling physically drained after the call.

3. According to the data mentioned, how much higher is cortisol in "over-functioning" parents?

A 2022 study found 42% higher levels of chronic cortisol elevation in parents who score high in over-functioning behaviors.

4. What is the "Coachâ€™s Pause"?

It is the intentional act of waiting 3-5 seconds after a client finishes speaking before responding. This allows the client to process their own words and prevents the coach from jumping in to "rescue" the silence.
