The Coaching Script: Reclaiming the Core

Practice these transitions. Your goal is to move the conversation from external tasks to internal essence.

Coaching Dialogue: The Mirroring Technique

ELENA:
"I just don't know what to do with my Saturdays anymore. I used to spend the whole day meal prepping for the kids and running them to games. Now I just sit there. I feel so guilty for being unproductive."
YOU (Coach):
"I hear that heavy sense of guilt, Elena. It sounds like for a long time, 'being Elena' was synonymous with 'being productive for others.' If we took away the to-do list for just a moment, who is the woman underneath all that doing?"
ELENA:
"I... I don't know. That's a scary question. I feel like there's nothing there."
YOU (Coach):
"It's okay to not know yet. That 'nothingness' is actually a blank canvas. In our work together, we aren't going to find a 'new' you; we're going to uncover the original you that got tucked away. What is one thing you loved to do when you were ten years old, before the world told you who you had to be?"
Pro Tip

Silence is your best friend here. After you ask "Who is the woman underneath?", wait at least 5-8 seconds. Let her sit with the discomfort of the question‚Äîthat's where the Core begins to stir.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New coaches often fall into these traps when doing Core Identity work. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

The Pitfall What it Sounds Like The Specialist Correction
The "Fix-It" Trap "You should join a book club or start gardening!" Asking: "What activities make you lose track of time?"
Over-Validating the Mask "You're such a good mom, they're lucky to have you." "You've given so much. What would it look like to give to yourself?"
Rushing the Process "We need to find your passion by next week." "We are in the 'discovery phase.' There is no rush to have answers."
Fear of Client Tears Changing the subject when the client gets emotional. "I see those tears. They are a sign that we're touching something important."
Sarah's Secret

If you get stuck, simply say: "Tell me more about that." It buys you time to think and keeps the focus on the client's internal world.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these "Core Shift" indicators:

  • Shift in Language: The client starts using "I want" instead of "I should."
  • Body Language: A visible relaxation of the shoulders or a deep, spontaneous sigh (release of the Mask).
  • Boundary Setting: The client reports saying "no" to a request that would have previously drained them.
  • Internal Validation: The client expresses a feeling of "coming home" to themselves.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is silence particularly important in Core Identity coaching?

Silence allows the client to move past their "reflexive" codependent answers and listen to the quieter voice of their Core Self. It prevents the coach from "filling the gap" with their own suggestions.

2. What is the primary difference between a "Role" and a "Core"?

A Role is defined by external relationships and duties (Mother, Teacher, Wife). The Core is the internal essence, values, and desires that exist even when those roles are absent.

3. If Elena says, "I feel guilty for spending money on this coaching," how should a Specialist respond?

Address the guilt as a symptom of the Mask. You might say: "That guilt is the old 'Helper' part of you trying to protect the status quo. What does the 'Core' part of you think about this investment in your future?"

4. What is a "Core Inquiry" question?

An open-ended question designed to bypass the Mask, such as "What brings you joy when no one is watching?" or "What parts of yourself did you leave behind to keep others happy?"

Key Takeaways

  • The Core Self is the "original blueprint" of the client, often buried under years of codependent role-playing.
  • Your job is not to "build" a new identity for the client, but to facilitate the "uncovering" of their existing Core.
  • Professionalism in this niche comes from your ability to hold space for the client's "not knowing" without rushing to fix it.
  • Successful practice requires vocalizing these scripts until they feel natural and authoritative.
  • You are becoming a real coach‚Äîevery time you practice these dialogues, you are integrating your expertise.
