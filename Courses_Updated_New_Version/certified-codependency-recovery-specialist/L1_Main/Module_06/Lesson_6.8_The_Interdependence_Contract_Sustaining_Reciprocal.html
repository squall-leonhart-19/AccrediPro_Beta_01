4. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Giving Tactical Advice: Don't tell Diane to "buy Chloe a louder alarm." Thatâ€™s just helping her fix more efficiently. Focus on Diane's internal shift.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it a mistake to give Diane tactical advice on how to help her daughter more effectively?

Tactical advice on "better fixing" reinforces the codependent cycle. The goal of interdependence coaching is to shift the focus from the other person's behavior to the client's own boundaries and self-regulation.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Interdependence Pivot" in a coaching conversation?

The pivot shifts the conversation from the "problem person" (the daughter) back to the client's agency and the impact their over-functioning has on their own well-being.

3. According to the lesson, a 2022 review suggests that moving from codependency to interdependence requires what specific shift?

It requires a shift from "shielding others from pain" to "allowing others to experience the weight of their own lives," which fosters growth for both parties.

4. How does the "Soft Mirror" technique help when a client becomes defensive?

It reflects the client's underlying emotion (usually fear or anxiety) without judgment, which lowers defenses and allows for deeper exploration of the root cause of their over-functioning.
