"It feels terrible now, but for many of us, over-functioning was the only way we knew how to stay 'attached' or safe in our early relationships. When you're fixing his life, you're actually trying to soothe a very old fear of disconnection. Does that resonate at all?"

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

When you're starting out, your "helper" instincts might actually get in the way. Watch out for these common rookie mistakes:

  • The "Expert" Trap: Trying to diagnose the client's mother or son too early. Keep the focus on Elena's current nervous system.
  • Rushing the Reframe: If Elena is crying, don't jump to the "Attachment Model" too fast. Validate the pain first: "I can see how heavy this is for you."
  • Fear of Silence: New coaches often talk too much. After you deliver a "Power Pivot," wait. Let the client process.
  • Taking Responsibility: If Elena doesn't "get it" in the first session, that's okay. Attachment styles took decades to form; they won't un-form in 20 minutes.

Check Your Understanding

1. When Elena calls herself an "addict," why is it important to reframe this to "attachment"?

Reframing to attachment removes the moral "shame" associated with addiction. It shifts the focus from Elena being "broken" to Elena having a "survival strategy" that can be updated. This lowers her cortisol and makes her more open to change.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Secure Base" coaching script?

The goal is to build immediate rapport and safety. By validating her intent (love and protection) while identifying the strategy (over-functioning), you become a "Secure Base" for the client to explore their own patterns without fear of judgment.

3. A client says, "I don't want to talk about my childhood, I just want to know how to get my son to get a job." How do you respond?

Validate and Pivot: "I hear you, and we definitely want him to find his footing. However, I've found that until we address why his unemployment feels like a 'life-or-death' emergency for your nervous system, any advice I give you won't stick. Can we look at what happens in your body when he's struggling?"

4. Which "Success Marker" indicates the client is moving from Anxious to Secure attachment?

When the client shows "Curiosity" over "Control." Instead of trying to fix the external situation immediately, they begin to ask how to regulate their internal state first.

Practice Lab Key Takeaways

  • Language Matters: Swapping "addiction" for "attachment" is the most powerful tool for reducing client shame.
  • Biology over Character: Always remind the client that their codependent behaviors were originally adaptive survival strategies.
  • The Goal is Interdependence: We aren't teaching clients to stop caring; we're teaching them to care without losing their own sense of safety.
  • Practice is Mastery: Your confidence as a practitioner comes from having these "Power Pivots" ready in your back pocket.
