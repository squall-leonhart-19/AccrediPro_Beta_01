The Strategic Power of Your Discovery Call

The discovery call is arguably the most important step in your client acquisition process. It's your chance to move beyond a website visit or a casual inquiry and forge a genuine human connection. Think of it as a mutual discovery process, not a one-sided sales pitch.

Research indicates that personalized interactions significantly increase conversion rates. A 2021 study by HubSpot found that companies prioritizing discovery calls saw a 23% higher client retention rate and a 15% higher average deal size compared to those who didn't. Your goal isn't to "convince" someone to buy, but to deeply understand their challenges and clearly articulate how your equine therapy programs can be the solution they've been searching for.

Coach Tip

Approach every discovery call with a mindset of service. Your primary objective is to listen, empathize, and determine if you can genuinely help. This authentic approach builds trust and positions you as a true partner in their journey, rather than just a service provider.

Crafting Your 30-Minute Discovery Call Flow

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all necessary points while maintaining a natural, conversational flow. Here's a proven framework for a 30-minute call:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Context 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my work with equine therapy, and what prompted you to reach out after the wellness talk?"
YOU:
"That's fantastic. I'm glad the talk resonated with you. So, Eleanor, tell me â€” what's been on your mind lately? What made you decide to explore equine-assisted therapy now?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Needs & Desires 10-12 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling a loss of purpose and some anxiety. Could you tell me more about how that manifests for you day-to-day? What does that feel like?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, what didn't, and how did those experiences leave you feeling?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel that sense of purpose and connection you're longing for, what would your ideal life look like?"
Phase 3: Educate & Connect Your Solution 8-10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've so openly shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a thoughtful woman navigating a significant life transition, seeking renewed purpose and a deeper connection, while also managing feelings of anxiety and restlessness. Does that feel accurate?" (Pause for confirmation)
YOU:
"The approach I use in equine-assisted therapy is unique. It's not about traditional talk therapy or riding horses. Instead, we engage in ground-based activities with our incredible equine partners. They act as mirrors, providing immediate, honest feedback that helps you gain profound insights into your emotions, patterns, and strengths. For clients like you, who are seeking purpose and connection, the horses offer a non-judgmental space to explore new ways of being, build confidence, and rediscover inner resources. Many clients find this process incredibly empowering, leading to a renewed sense of self and direction within a few sessions."
YOU:
"My 'Finding Your Footing' program, for example, is a 10-week journey specifically designed for women in transition. We work together to clarify your values, build resilience, and establish new routines that support your well-being and sense of purpose. Would you be open to hearing more about how that program works and what it entails?"
Phase 4: Next Steps & Closing 3-5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on our conversation today, does this sound like the kind of transformative experience you've been looking for to find that purpose and connection?" (Wait for her answer)
YOU:
"Excellent! Let me tell you about the investment for the 'Finding Your Footing' program and how we can get started. The 10-week program is an investment of $2,500. This includes 10 private, 60-minute equine therapy sessions, personalized journaling prompts, and email support between sessions. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible. How does that sound to you?"
Coach Tip

After you state your price, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the prospect process the information. Often, the first person to speak after the price is mentioned loses some leverage. Practice this silence until it feels comfortable.

Navigating Common Objections with Grace

It's natural for prospects to have reservations. Your ability to address these objections with empathy and confidence is key. Remember, an objection is often just a request for more information or reassurance, not a definitive "no."

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, whether this approach is truly for you, or something else? I want to make sure I've answered all your questions."
"It's too expensive / I'm on a fixed income."
"I appreciate your honesty about the investment, Eleanor. I know it's a significant commitment, especially after retiring. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. What they've found, however, is that the transformation they experience â€“ rediscovering purpose, reducing anxiety, and building deep connections â€“ is truly priceless. Consider the cost of *not* addressing these feelings: the continued anxiety, the lack of fulfillment. My payment plans are designed to help make this accessible, spreading the investment over several months. Is that something that would be helpful?"
"I'm not sure if equine therapy is right for me / I've never done anything like this before."
"That's a very common feeling, Eleanor, and it's completely valid. Equine-assisted therapy is certainly different. What I can tell you is that you don't need any prior horse experience, and all activities are ground-based. My role is to guide you safely through the process, and the horses meet you exactly where you are. Many clients, like Sarah (a former nurse at 55), were initially skeptical but found it to be the most impactful work they'd ever done because of the horses' unique ability to facilitate self-discovery. We start with simple, gentle introductions, and you set the pace. Does that help ease some of those concerns?"
"I don't have enough time."
"I hear you, Eleanor. Even in retirement, our schedules can feel full, and it's easy for our own well-being to take a backseat. However, this program is designed to be a dedicated space for your personal growth. It's just one hour a week, a small but powerful commitment that creates ripple effects throughout your entire life. Think of it as investing in your future self â€“ the self that feels purposeful, connected, and free from anxiety. What would it mean for you to carve out this dedicated time for your transformation?"
Coach Tip

Always validate the prospect's feeling before offering your response. Phrases like "I understand," "That's a common concern," or "I appreciate your honesty" build empathy and keep the conversation open.

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Investment

Presenting your pricing can feel intimidating, but remember the value you offer. You are not just selling sessions; you are offering a transformative experience that addresses deep-seated needs. Your confidence in your value is paramount.

When discussing your programs and investment, follow these principles:

  1. Lead with Value: Reiterate the benefits and transformation the client will experience, linking back to their stated goals.
  2. State the Price Clearly: Say the full investment amount directly. Avoid hedging or apologizing.
  3. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, stop talking. Let the client process. Their reaction (or lack thereof) will guide your next steps.
  4. Offer Options (if applicable): If you have payment plans or different program tiers, present them clearly after the initial price.
  5. Address Objections: Be prepared to calmly and confidently address any financial concerns, as practiced above.
Coach Tip

Practice saying your program prices out loud, to yourself or a friend, until it feels completely natural and confident. The energy you bring to this part of the conversation significantly impacts how the client perceives the value.

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing Lines

Once you've presented your program and addressed any initial concerns, it's time to invite the client to take the next step. Here are some effective closing lines to practice:

  • "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the 'Finding Your Footing' program is exactly what you need to rediscover your purpose and ease that anxiety. Are you ready to take this powerful step forward and begin your journey?"
  • "Given your desire for connection and a new path, and how well this program aligns with those goals, would you like to move forward with enrollment today?"
  • "What feels like the best next step for you, Eleanor, to get started with the 'Finding Your Footing' program?"
  • "I have an opening next Tuesday at 10 AM for our first session. Does that work for you to begin your transformation?"

These lines are direct but inviting, giving the client a clear path to commit.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call in your equine therapy practice?

The primary purpose is to establish rapport, deeply understand the prospective client's needs and desires, determine if your services are a good fit, and clearly articulate the value of your programs as a solution. It's a mutual discovery process.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective next step to take?

The most effective step is to gently inquire what specifically they need to think about. This helps uncover the root of the objection (e.g., investment, time, uncertainty about the therapy) so you can address it directly.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is a crucial non-verbal technique to employ immediately after stating the price?

A crucial technique is to pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect time to process the information without feeling pressured, and their reaction will often provide valuable insight into their true feelings.

4. According to the lesson, what is a key mindset shift to adopt when approaching discovery calls?

Instead of viewing it as a "sales call," adopt a mindset of service and genuine curiosity. Focus on listening and determining if you can truly help the individual, which builds authentic trust and connection.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a strategic, empathetic conversation designed for mutual fit, not a hard sell.
  • A structured 30-minute call flow helps you build rapport, uncover client needs, and present your solution effectively.
  • Active listening and asking open-ended questions are critical for understanding a prospect's deeper motivations.
  • Confident objection handling involves validating feelings, clarifying the concern, and reframing value.
  • Present your program's investment clearly, confidently, and then pause, allowing the prospect to respond.
  • Visualizing your income potential reinforces the viability and reward of building your equine therapy practice.
