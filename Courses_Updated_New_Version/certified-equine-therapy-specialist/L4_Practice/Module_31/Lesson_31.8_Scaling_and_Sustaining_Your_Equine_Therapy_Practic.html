Understanding Your Prospect: Rachel's Profile

Before any call, understanding your prospect is paramount. It allows you to tailor your approach, anticipate concerns, and speak directly to their needs. Meet Rachel, our simulated prospect for this lab.

üë©

Rachel, 47

Found you through a friend's referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Chronic fatigue, weight issues, tried everything, frustrated with conventional medicine. Feels stuck and overwhelmed.
Budget Concern
"I've spent so much money already on various treatments and diets. Is this really going to be different, and is it worth the investment?"
Decision Style
Needs to feel deeply heard and understood. Skeptical of quick fixes or generic advice. Values genuine expertise, a clear plan, and emotional support.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel like myself again. I miss having energy to enjoy my life and my family. I want to feel vibrant and in control of my health."

Coach Tip

Always remember, your prospects are often coming to you after trying many other solutions. They are seeking hope and a different approach. Your role is not just to offer a service, but to offer a pathway to transformation. Hold space for their frustration and validate their experiences.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Structure

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all essential points while maintaining a natural, empathetic flow. Here‚Äôs a tried-and-true 30-minute framework, designed to build trust and clarity.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Sarah! Yes, my friend Emily raved about her experience with your equine therapy program. She said you helped her so much with her stress."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that Emily referred you; she's been on quite a journey herself. I'm glad you reached out. So Rachel, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's been going on for you?"
RACHEL:
"Well, for years now, I've just been so tired. I drag myself through the day, my clothes don't fit, and I feel like I'm constantly fighting myself. I just want to feel like myself again."
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Their World 10 min

This is where your active listening skills shine. Ask open-ended questions and truly listen to understand their deepest pain points and desires.

YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Rachel. It sounds incredibly challenging. Tell me more about the fatigue. When did it start? How does it affect your daily life and your ability to do the things you love?"
RACHEL:
"It started gradually after my second child, but it's gotten so much worse in the last five years. I can barely keep up at work, and by the time I get home, I have no energy for my kids or husband. I used to love hiking, but now even a short walk feels like a marathon."
YOU:
"That sounds truly disheartening. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and what's left you feeling frustrated?"
RACHEL:
"Oh, everything! Doctors just tell me my labs are 'normal,' or suggest antidepressants. I've tried countless diets, supplements, exercise programs... I lose a little weight, then gain it back. Nothing sticks. I feel like I'm just wasting money and time."
YOU:
"I hear that frustration, Rachel. It's tough when you're putting in so much effort without seeing lasting results. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this completely, what would your life look like? What would you be doing, and how would you feel?"
RACHEL:
"Honestly, if nothing changes, I'm scared I'll just keep getting worse, feeling more isolated. But with a magic wand? I'd be waking up feeling refreshed, having energy to play with my kids, going on those hikes again, feeling confident in my body. I'd feel like myself again."

Coach Tip

Allow for silence. After asking a powerful question, resist the urge to fill the quiet. Give your prospect time to think, feel, and articulate their deepest thoughts. Often, the most profound insights emerge from these moments of reflection.

Phase 3: Positioning Your Solution 10 min

Now, connect their pain points to your unique solution. Explain your approach and how it specifically addresses their challenges, without giving away all your intellectual property.

YOU:
"Rachel, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're experiencing chronic fatigue and weight struggles that are impacting your joy, your family life, and your sense of self. You've tried conventional routes and various programs, feeling frustrated by the lack of lasting results and a personalized approach. You deeply desire to regain your energy, feel confident, and get back to living fully. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Thank you. The approach I use in equine-assisted therapy is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we work together to understand the root causes of your fatigue and emotional blocks, often linked to stress and past experiences. My program involves working with our horses in a unique, non-riding way that helps you build self-awareness, emotional resilience, and discover inner resources you didn't know you had. We'll create a truly personalized plan over 12 weeks. Most of my clients, like Emily, start feeling significantly better, more grounded, and more energetic within the first 4-6 weeks as they begin to shift their internal landscape."
RACHEL:
"That sounds... intriguing. I've never considered anything like that."

Coach Tip

When summarizing, make sure to use your client's own words and validate their feelings. This demonstrates empathy and shows them you've truly listened, building immense trust. It‚Äôs also an opportunity to confirm your understanding before you present your solution.

Phase 4: The Confident Close 5 min

This phase is about clarity and guiding your prospect to the next logical step. Don't be afraid to ask for the commitment.

YOU:
"So Rachel, based on what we've discussed, does this unique, personalized approach to regaining your energy and vitality sound like what you've been looking for?"
RACHEL:
"It does, yes. It sounds refreshing to try something so different and holistic."
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me tell you about the program structure, the investment, and how we can get started right away so you can begin feeling like yourself again."

Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel 100% confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for you."

(This opens a dialogue to uncover the real objection. Be prepared to address it directly.)
"It's too expensive/I can't afford it right now."
"I appreciate you bringing that up, Rachel. I understand that investing in your health is a significant decision. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. What they found, however, was that the cost of not addressing their issues ‚Äì the lost energy, the impact on family, the continuous spending on things that don't work ‚Äì was far greater. My program is an investment in lasting transformation, not just a temporary fix. How much is it worth to you to wake up feeling vibrant and energetic again, to truly feel like yourself?"

(Shift focus from cost to value and the cost of inaction. You can also briefly mention payment plans if you offer them.)
"I don't have time for a 12-week program."
"I hear you, Rachel. It sounds like your schedule is already very full, and adding one more thing feels daunting. What I've found is that dedicating this focused time now actually creates more time and energy in the long run. Imagine having the vitality to truly enjoy your weekends, without the crushing fatigue. My clients often report that the structure of the program helps them prioritize their well-being, leading to greater efficiency in other areas of their lives. How much time are you currently spending feeling unwell or trying things that don't work?"

(Reframe the time commitment as an investment that yields more time and energy.)
"I'm not sure if equine therapy is right for me. I've never done anything like it."
"That's a very natural feeling, Rachel, and many clients share that initial uncertainty. The beauty of equine-assisted therapy is its gentle yet profound nature. You don't need any prior horse experience, and it's not about riding. It's about connecting with these incredible, intuitive animals in a safe, guided environment, which often reveals insights and shifts perspectives in ways traditional talk therapy cannot. It's an experiential journey that allows for deep, lasting change. How does the idea of exploring new pathways to healing resonate with you?"

(Validate their feeling, educate briefly, and highlight the unique benefits.)

Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

This is where your belief in your program's value truly shines. Present your program with enthusiasm and state your pricing clearly and without hesitation.

Value-Based Pricing

Remember that your pricing reflects the transformation you offer, not just the hours you spend. Your comprehensive 12-week program, for example, is designed to deliver specific outcomes. Focus on these outcomes when discussing investment.

  • Outcome-Oriented: Frame your program around the results clients achieve (e.g., "regain energy," "reduce stress by 40%," "feel confident again").
  • Personalized Support: Emphasize the individualized attention, expert guidance, and unique equine interactions that set your program apart.
  • Long-Term Value: Contrast your program with short-term fixes they've tried. This is a lasting investment in their well-being.

Stating Your Fees

When it's time to state your fees, be direct, confident, and then pause. Let the number land. Avoid rambling or justifying. For example:

YOU:
"Rachel, my 12-week 'Equine Path to Vitality' program, which includes weekly one-on-one sessions with the horses, personalized support materials, and direct access to me, is an investment of $X,XXX. This includes everything you need to transform your energy and reclaim your vitality."

(Pause. Let her respond.)

If you offer payment plans, you can follow up with: "I also offer flexible payment plans, breaking the investment into three monthly payments of $Y,YYY to make it more accessible."

Coach Tip

Your confidence in your pricing directly reflects your belief in your program's value. If you hesitate or sound apologetic, your prospect will pick up on it. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and firm. You are offering a solution to their deepest desires, and that is invaluable.

Your Call to Action: Guiding Enrollment

After presenting your program and pricing, your call to action (CTA) should be clear and simple. Guide them to the next step.

  • Direct & Clear: "Are you ready to commit to yourself and start your journey to lasting vitality with the horses?"
  • Next Steps: "If you're ready, we can get you scheduled for your first session next week. I'll send you the enrollment link right after this call, and we can book your initial assessment."
  • Address Logistics: Briefly explain the onboarding process (e.g., initial forms, scheduling, payment).

Practice Your Close

Practice these closing lines out loud:

  1. "Rachel, are you ready to take this powerful step towards feeling like yourself again?"
  2. "Based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe this program is exactly what you need to achieve your goals. Shall we get you enrolled today?"
  3. "The next step is to secure your spot and schedule your initial session. I'll walk you through the simple enrollment process now."

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 1 (Building Rapport) in a discovery call, and why is it crucial?

The primary goal of Phase 1 is to establish a personal connection and build trust. It's crucial because prospects are more likely to open up and consider your solution if they feel a genuine connection and believe you understand them.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the recommended next step, and why?

The recommended next step is to ask, "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This helps uncover the underlying objection, allowing you to address their specific concerns directly rather than making assumptions.

3. How should your pricing be presented to a prospect like Rachel, who has expressed budget concerns?

Pricing should be presented confidently and framed around the value and transformation the program offers, rather than just the cost. Emphasize the long-term benefits and the cost of inaction. Briefly mention payment plans if available, but prioritize the value proposition first.

4. What is a key characteristic of an effective call-to-action (CTA) at the end of a discovery call?

A key characteristic of an effective CTA is clarity and directness. It should clearly guide the prospect to the very next step, making it easy for them to commit and know what to expect (e.g., "Let's get you scheduled," "I'll send the enrollment link now").

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is your opportunity to build genuine connection and demonstrate empathetic leadership, not just to sell.
  • A structured approach, moving from rapport to problem understanding, solution positioning, and confident closing, ensures effectiveness.
  • Objections are natural; view them as opportunities to provide further clarity and reinforce the value of your equine therapy program.
  • Present your program and its investment with unwavering confidence, focusing on the transformative outcomes you offer.
  • A clear, direct call-to-action empowers prospects to take the next step and begin their healing journey with you.
