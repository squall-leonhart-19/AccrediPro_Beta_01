The Discovery Call: Script & Strategy (30 Minutes)

A well-structured discovery call isn't about coercion; it's about qualified connection. Your goal is to determine if your program is the right fit for the client, and if the client is the right fit for your program. This 30-minute framework helps you guide the conversation effectively.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Eleanor

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor, it's wonderful to connect with you today! Thank you for taking the time. I understand [Therapist friend's name] referred you ‚Äì she spoke so highly of you. Before we dive into what brought you here, how are you doing today?"
YOU:
"That's great to hear. So Eleanor, what prompted you to reach out to explore equine-assisted therapy now? What's been on your mind?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Her World (Understanding the Problem) 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling lost and struggling with anxiety. Can you tell me more about what that feels like for you day-to-day? How does it impact your energy, your relationships, your sense of self?"
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly challenging. And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, what didn't feel quite right?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you imagine yourself in a year, Eleanor? And, if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel like yourself again, what would that look like for you? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Educate & Present Your Unique Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing so openly, Eleanor. What I'm hearing is a deep desire to reconnect with your inner strength, manage anxiety, and find clarity after a significant life transition. You're looking for an approach that offers more than just talking, something experiential and empowering. Does that resonate with you?"
YOU:
"That's exactly why equine-assisted therapy is so powerful. Unlike traditional therapy, we're not just talking about your challenges; we're actively engaging with them alongside a horse, who acts as an honest mirror. My Equine Empowerment Program is a 12-week journey designed to help women like you rebuild confidence, process emotions in a safe space, and gain profound insights. Clients often tell me they experience breakthroughs in 4-6 sessions that took years in conventional settings."
YOU:
"We focus on practical tools for emotional regulation, boundary setting, and rediscovering your authentic self, all facilitated by the unique wisdom of our equine partners. We'll utilize elements of the T.R.U.S.T. Framework‚Ñ¢ to build a strong foundation for your growth."
Phase 4: Qualify & Transition to the Next Step (The Close) 5 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what we've discussed, does this sound like the kind of supportive and transformative experience you've been searching for?"
YOU:
"Excellent! If you're ready to explore how this program can specifically support you, I'd love to share the details of how we can get started and answer any questions you have about the investment. How does that sound?"
Coach Tip: The Power of the Pause

After asking a question, especially one designed to elicit a 'yes' or a commitment, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the client process and respond. This demonstrates confidence and gives them space to truly consider their decision. This reflective pause is a therapeutic tool that also translates powerfully into sales conversations.

Confidently Handling Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and a problem-solving mindset. Here's how you might respond to Eleanor's potential concerns:

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"This sounds amazing, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is a significant investment in yourself and your well-being, and it deserves careful thought. To help you with that, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process itself? Knowing what's on your mind will help me provide the information you need."
"I'm on a tighter budget right now; the investment feels high."
"I hear you, Eleanor, and I appreciate your honesty about your financial situation. Many of my clients have similar concerns, especially when they're making a big change. What I can tell you is that the Equine Empowerment Program is designed for lasting transformation, not just a temporary fix. It's an investment in rebuilding your foundation. We can discuss payment plan options, but more importantly, let's look at the return: what is the cost to you, emotionally and physically, of staying where you are? What would it be worth to truly find your strength and joy again?"
"I'm not sure I have the time for a 12-week commitment right now."
"That's a very valid concern, Eleanor, especially with everything you're navigating. The 12-week structure is designed to create sustainable change, allowing for integration between sessions. We typically meet once a week for 60-75 minutes. My clients often find that while it's a commitment, the clarity and energy they gain actually free up more time and mental space in their lives overall. How much longer are you willing to feel overwhelmed and lost, before prioritizing this time for yourself?"
Coach Tip: The 3 F's of Objection Handling

A classic sales technique, the "Feel, Felt, Found" method can be very effective: "I understand how you feel. Other clients have felt that way too. What they found was..." This validates their emotion, normalizes their concern, and then pivots to a positive outcome experienced by others. It builds connection and trust.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing with Confidence

When it comes to discussing your fees, confidence is key. Remember, you are offering a high-value, specialized service that creates profound, lasting change. Your pricing reflects your expertise, the unique environment, and the transformative outcomes. Never apologize for your prices.

Structuring Your Pricing Conversation:

  1. Reiterate Value: "Eleanor, as we discussed, this program is about reconnecting with your inner strength, managing anxiety, and finding clarity using the powerful wisdom of horses. This isn't just about feeling better temporarily; it's about equipping you with tools for life."
  2. Present the Package: "The Equine Empowerment Program is a comprehensive 12-week journey that includes 12 one-on-one equine-assisted therapy sessions, personalized resources, and direct support between sessions."
  3. State the Investment Clearly: "The investment for this complete program is [Your Price, e.g., $3,600 USD]."
  4. Offer Payment Options (if applicable): "To make this accessible, we offer two payment options: a single upfront payment of $3,600, or three monthly payments of $1,250 each." (Note: Payment plans often have a slightly higher total cost to account for administrative effort and risk.)
  5. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, allow for silence. Let them absorb the information.
  6. Address the "Why": Be prepared to gently link back to the value and their stated goals. "Eleanor, considering your desire to truly find your strength and joy again, what feels like a bigger investment: continuing to struggle, or committing to a proven path for change?"
Coach Tip: Your Pricing Reflects Your Worth

As a Certified Equine Therapy Specialist‚Ñ¢, you bring a unique, powerful modality to the world. Your pricing should reflect the specialized training, the costs associated with maintaining your equine partners, and the profound results you facilitate. If you don't value your services, neither will your clients. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident.

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing Lines

The close is where you invite the client to take the next step. It should be clear, confident, and focused on their benefit. Practice these lines until they feel natural:

  1. "Eleanor, are you ready to embark on this journey and start feeling empowered and joyful again?"
  2. "Given everything we've discussed and your desire for lasting change, does the Equine Empowerment Program feel like the right next step for you?"
  3. "If you're truly committed to transforming your life and finding your strength, I'm confident this program can help. Shall we get you enrolled today?"
  4. "I have a couple of spots opening up next month. Would you like to reserve yours and begin your journey?"
  5. "What questions do you have for me that, if answered, would make you feel completely confident moving forward?"

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of a discovery call, beyond simply "selling a program"?

The primary goal is to determine if there is a mutual fit between the client's needs and your program's offerings, and to build rapport and trust. It's about qualifying the client and allowing them to qualify you, ensuring your program is the best solution for their specific challenges.

2. When a client expresses a budget concern, what is an effective strategy to address it without devaluing your services?

Acknowledge their concern with empathy ("I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty..."). Reiterate the long-term value and transformative nature of your program, emphasizing that it's an investment in lasting change. Offer payment plan options if available, and gently pivot to the "cost of inaction" ‚Äì the emotional and physical cost of not addressing their problem.

3. What is the "Power of the Pause" and why is it important during a discovery call?

The "Power of the Pause" refers to intentionally allowing silence after asking a question or stating a price. It's crucial because it gives the client space to process information, formulate their thoughts, and respond without feeling rushed. It demonstrates your confidence and respect for their decision-making process, preventing you from filling the silence with unnecessary talking.

4. According to the lesson, what is a realistic monthly gross income for an established Equine Therapy Specialist‚Ñ¢ with approximately 5 clients enrolled in a 12-week program priced at $3,600?

For 5 clients enrolled in a 12-week program priced at $3,600, the realistic monthly gross income would be approximately $18,000. This assumes clients pay in full or on a monthly plan that averages to this amount over the program duration.

Key Takeaways

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery Calls are for Connection, Not Coercion: Approach calls with genuine curiosity and a desire to understand, not just to sell. Your authenticity builds trust.
  • Structure Empowers Your Sales: A clear, empathetic call framework (rapport, problem, solution, close) ensures you cover all key points and guide the conversation effectively.
  • Objections are Opportunities: View client objections as requests for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy, value-reiteration, and solutions.
  • Confidence in Pricing Reflects Value: Clearly and confidently state your program's investment, linking it directly to the transformative outcomes and your specialized expertise.
  • Your Practice is Your Financial Freedom: Building a sustainable client base offers significant income potential, empowering you to create meaningful work and financial independence.
