"I want to find my passion again, feel more confident, and understand myself better. I just want to feel like I belong."

Eleanor's referral expressed how much equine therapy helped them find their voice, which intrigued Eleanor, but she's still very much in the "exploring" phase.

The Discovery Call Blueprint

The discovery call is your opportunity to connect with a potential client, understand their unique needs, and determine if your equine therapy services are the right fit. It's a two-way conversation, not a sales pitch. Remember, you're assessing them as much as they're assessing you. Here's a proven structure, designed for a 30-45 minute call, with Eleanor in mind.

Your 30-45 Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5-7 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about AccrediPro Equine Wellness?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] shared her experience with you. She's been on quite a journey. So Eleanor, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you?"

(Allow her to share, listen actively, use reflective listening. Avoid interrupting.)

Coach Tip

Remember that your primary goal in Phase 1 is to make the prospect feel seen and heard. Authenticity is key. People buy from people they trust. Share a brief, relatable personal anecdote if appropriate, but keep the focus on them. For Eleanor, acknowledging her friend's journey can build immediate trust.

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 15-20 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Eleanor. You mentioned feeling 'stuck' and a lack of purpose. Can you tell me more about how that manifests in your daily life? What does that feeling actually look like for you?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, what didn't, and what makes you feel hesitant about exploring new paths?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? What's the cost of staying where you are? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel connected and purposeful again, what would your life look like? What would that sense of 'belonging' feel like?"

(Dig deep here. Ask open-ended questions. Listen for emotional drivers, not just surface-level issues. This is where you uncover their deepest desires and pain points.)

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10-12 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've so openly shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a thoughtful woman who has given so much, and now you're seeking to reclaim your sense of purpose and belonging, to move past feeling stuck and anxious in new situations. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use here at AccrediPro Equine Wellness is different from conventional therapy. Instead of just talking about feelings, we engage with horses as co-facilitators to provide immediate, honest feedback. This experiential process helps you uncover deep-seated patterns and build new ways of relating ‚Äì to yourself, to others, and to your purpose. It's about 'doing' and 'being' rather than just 'talking.' Many of my clients, especially those in mid-life transitions, find this accelerates their journey towards clarity and confidence. We work together over a [e.g., 8-week or 12-week] program, tailored to your specific goals, with tangible outcomes like increased self-awareness, improved confidence, and a clearer path forward."

(Connect her problem directly to your unique solution. Emphasize the unique benefits of equine therapy and your program's structure. Use specific examples if possible.)

Coach Tip

When summarizing, use Eleanor's exact words and emotions. This shows you were truly listening. When presenting your approach, focus on the transformation she will experience, not just the features of your program. For Eleanor, emphasize how the horses can help her find her voice and sense of belonging without the pressure of direct human interaction initially.

Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5-8 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, does what I've described about our equine-assisted approach sound like the kind of supportive and transformative path you've been looking for to help you move forward?"

(Pause and wait for her answer. If yes, proceed to pricing. If hesitant, explore objections gently.)

Navigating Common Objections

It's natural for potential clients to have reservations. Your role is to address these with empathy, confidence, and transparency. Think of objections as requests for more information or reassurance, not rejections.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it / Discuss with my spouse."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and it's wise to consider it carefully. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that you'd like more clarity on? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best decision for yourself."

(If she mentions discussing with a spouse): "Absolutely, I encourage it! What information would be most helpful for you to share with them? Perhaps a summary of our program, or even a brief follow-up call with all of you? I'm happy to provide whatever support helps you both feel confident."
"It's too expensive / I can't afford it right now."
"I hear you, Eleanor. Investing in ourselves, especially when it comes to personal growth and well-being, is a significant decision. My program is structured as an investment in your transformation, designed to create lasting change rather than temporary fixes. Many clients find the return on this investment, in terms of renewed confidence and purpose, far outweighs the initial cost. We can discuss flexible payment options, and I also encourage you to consider the 'cost' of staying where you are ‚Äì the impact on your well-being, energy, and joy. What feels like the biggest financial hurdle for you at this moment?"
"I'm not sure if equine therapy is right for me / if it really works."
"That's a very valid question, Eleanor, especially if this is a new concept for you. What specifically makes you feel uncertain? Many of my clients felt similarly before experiencing it firsthand. What I can tell you is that the unique, non-judgmental interactions with horses often create breakthroughs that traditional talk therapy struggles to achieve, especially for those feeling stuck or disconnected. We don't just talk about change; we experience it. Perhaps starting with a single introductory session could help you determine if this resonates with you without committing to the full program?"
"I don't have enough time."
"Time is precious, and I completely respect that, Eleanor. Our [e.g., 8-week] program involves [X number] of sessions, each lasting [Y minutes]. Many of my clients, often busy professionals or parents, integrate this into their schedules by viewing it as dedicated time for their own growth ‚Äì an essential investment, not just another appointment. What does your current schedule look like, and what would need to shift for you to prioritize this time for yourself?"
Coach Tip

When handling objections, lead with empathy and validation. Avoid getting defensive. Reframe the objection as an opportunity to provide more value and clarify misunderstandings. Never pressure, always empower the client to make an informed decision.

Confidently Presenting Your Investment

This is often the most challenging part for new practitioners, but it doesn't have to be. Remember the value you provide. You are offering a transformative experience, not just a service. When you believe in the profound impact of your work, presenting your pricing becomes a natural extension of your value proposition.

Strategies for Pricing Presentation:

  • Anchor High, Present Value: Always state the total value of your program first, then break down the investment. For example, "The complete 8-week Equine Connection Program, which includes 8 one-on-one sessions, personalized integration exercises, and email support, is valued at $X. Today, your investment for this transformative journey is $Y."
  • Offer Options: Provide 2-3 payment options (e.g., pay-in-full discount, 2-part payment plan, 3-part payment plan). This makes it more accessible.
  • Focus on Outcomes: Reiterate the specific results and transformations your client will achieve. For Eleanor, this means renewed confidence, clarity, and a sense of belonging.
  • Be Prepared for Silence: After stating the price, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the client process. Often, they will speak first.
  • Confidence is Contagious: Your tone, posture, and belief in your pricing will reflect directly onto the client. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural.
Gold Standard Practice

Always have a clear, concise, and written outline of your program and pricing to send to the client immediately after the call. This reinforces your professionalism and provides a tangible reference point for their decision-making process.

Sealing the Connection: Call-to-Action Practice

After you've presented your program and addressed any questions, it's time to guide the client to the next step. This is not about being pushy, but about providing a clear path forward for someone you believe you can genuinely help.

Practice These Closing Lines Out Loud:

  1. "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe our Equine Connection Program is exactly what you've been looking for to find your purpose and regain your confidence. How would you like to move forward?"
  2. "Given your desire to feel more connected and find clarity, and my confidence that our program can deliver that, are you ready to take the first step and begin your journey with us?"
  3. "I have a few spots opening next month. If you're ready to commit to this transformation, we can get you scheduled for your initial session. How does that sound?"
  4. "What feels like the best next step for you, Eleanor? Would you like to schedule your first session, or would it be helpful to review the program details one more time?"

The key is to be direct yet gentle, offering a clear path without overwhelming them. Your confidence in their potential for transformation, facilitated by your expertise and equine partners, should shine through.

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem & Vision" phase in a discovery call, especially when dealing with a client like Eleanor?

The primary purpose is to move beyond surface-level complaints to uncover the client's deepest pain points, emotional drivers, and their desired future vision. For Eleanor, it's about understanding her yearning for purpose and belonging, and the anxiety she feels, connecting her current state to her desired transformation. This deeper understanding allows the practitioner to tailor their solution effectively.

2. Eleanor expresses a budget concern ("Is this a significant investment?"). What is the most effective way to address this, based on the strategies discussed?

The most effective way is to acknowledge and validate her concern with empathy ("I hear you, Eleanor..."), then reframe the cost as an investment in her transformation, focusing on the long-term return (renewed confidence, purpose). Offer flexible payment options and gently prompt her to consider the "cost" of *not* addressing her issues (the impact on her well-being, energy, and joy).

3. You've just presented your program and pricing. Eleanor says, "I need to think about it." How should you respond to this common objection?

Respond with empathy and an open-ended question to uncover the specific hesitation: "I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that you'd like more clarity on?" This approach helps you address the root of the objection rather than making assumptions.

4. Why is it important to practice your closing lines out loud, and what should be the underlying tone of your call-to-action?

Practicing closing lines out loud builds confidence and helps them feel natural, preventing hesitation or awkwardness during the actual call. The underlying tone of your call-to-action should be direct yet gentle, offering a clear path forward from a place of genuine belief in your ability to help, rather than being pushy or sales-focused. It's about empowering the client to take the next step towards their desired transformation.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured, empathetic conversation focused on understanding the client's needs and determining mutual fit.
  • Building strong rapport and actively listening to uncover deep emotional drivers are crucial for effective client connection.
  • Common objections are opportunities to clarify value and build trust, requiring empathetic and confident responses.
  • Presenting your program's value and pricing confidently reflects your belief in your services and their transformative impact.
  • A clear, gentle call-to-action guides potential clients to commit, turning interest into tangible progress.
  • Understanding your income potential empowers you to price appropriately and build a sustainable, fulfilling equine therapy practice.
