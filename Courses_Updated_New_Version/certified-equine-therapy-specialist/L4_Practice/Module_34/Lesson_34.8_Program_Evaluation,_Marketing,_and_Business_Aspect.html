The Art of the Equine Therapy Discovery Call

The discovery call is your opportunity to connect deeply with a potential client, understand their needs, and determine if your equine-assisted services are the right fit. It's less about selling and more about serving through clarity and connection. Remember, you are offering a unique, powerful pathway to healing and growth. Your confidence in this offering will resonate with your prospect.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Think of the discovery call as a mutual exploration. You're not just interviewing them; they're interviewing you! Your genuine curiosity about their journey, combined with your professional expertise, builds immediate trust. People want to feel heard and understood, especially when they're seeking support for deeply personal challenges.

The Discovery Call Blueprint: A 30-Minute Guide

Here's a proven structure for your discovery calls. Practice these phases out loud, adapting them to your unique style. Remember, authenticity is key!

Phase 1: Building Authentic Connection (5 min)

This is where you make them feel seen, heard, and safe. Start warm and genuine.

  • Opening: "Hello Eleanor, thank you so much for taking the time to connect today. I'm Sarah. How are you doing this afternoon?"
  • Acknowledge Referral/Source: "I saw you found me through my 'Finding Your Herd' workshop post. What was it about that post that resonated with you?"
  • Set the Stage: "This call is really for us to get to know each other, for me to understand what you're hoping for, and for you to ask any questions about how equine therapy might support you. Does that sound good?"
Coach Tip from Sarah

Your enthusiasm is contagious! Let your passion for horses and their ability to help shine through. A simple, "I'm so glad we're connecting!" can set a positive, welcoming tone. Make eye contact if it's a video call, and truly listen.

Phase 2: Deepening Understanding & Uncovering Needs (10 min)

This is where you become a detective, asking open-ended questions and listening deeply. Identify their core pain points and aspirations.

  • Explore the "Why Now?": "Eleanor, you mentioned feeling a need to 'find your spark again.' Can you tell me more about what that feels like for you right now? What's prompted you to seek something new at this stage?"
  • Impact & Desired Future: "How does feeling disconnected impact your daily life? And if you could wave a magic wand and achieve that 'spark' and 'new direction,' what would your life look like in six months?"
  • Past Experiences: "You mentioned trying traditional therapy. What did you find challenging about that experience, and what are you hoping will be different with an experiential approach like equine therapy?"

Phase 3: Presenting Your Unique Equine-Assisted Solution (10 min)

Based on their needs, clearly articulate how your program addresses their challenges. Focus on benefits, not just features.

  • Summarize & Validate: "Eleanor, what I'm hearing is that you're seeking renewed purpose, deeper connection, and a way to build confidence, especially after feeling a bit lost post-retirement. You're drawn to horses and a more hands-on approach, but have some natural hesitations about group vulnerability. Does that sound accurate?"
  • Bridge to Your Program: "Many women I work with, especially those navigating life transitions like retirement, share similar feelings. Our 'Finding Your Herd' workshop is specifically designed for this. It's a small, supportive group where the horses act as incredible mirrors, helping you uncover your innate strengths without needing to 'talk it out' in a traditional way. We focus on building non-verbal communication skills, emotional regulation, and a sense of belonging, which often translates to more confidence and clarity in your daily life."
  • Highlight Key Differentiators: "Unlike traditional settings, the horses offer immediate, honest feedback, creating a unique space for self-discovery and connection. The group dynamic, while initially daunting for some, often becomes the most powerful part, as women realize they're not alone in their journey."
Coach Tip from Sarah

Use client testimonials (without naming names) or brief anecdotes to illustrate the power of your work. For example, "I recently had a client, a former educator like yourself, who felt a similar lack of direction. Through our horse-assisted sessions, she discovered a new passion for volunteer work and regained her sense of purpose."

Phase 4: Confident Closing & Next Steps (5 min)

This is where you invite them to take the next step. Be clear, calm, and confident.

  • Check for Alignment: "Eleanor, based on what we've discussed, does the 'Finding Your Herd' workshop sound like the kind of transformative experience you've been looking for?" (Pause and listen for their response).
  • Address Initial Questions: "Do you have any initial questions about the workshop format, the horses, or anything else that comes to mind?"
  • Transition to Logistics: "Wonderful. If this feels like a good fit, I'd love to share more about the program logistics, including dates, what to expect, and the investment to participate. Would you like me to walk you through that now?"

Navigating Common Objections with Confidence

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and a calm, confident demeanor.

"It's too expensive."

This is often a value objection, not just a price objection. Reframe the investment.

  • Empathy & Validation: "I completely understand, Eleanor. Investing in ourselves is a significant decision, especially on a fixed income. Many of my clients initially feel the same way."
  • Reframe Value: "What I've found is that the cost of not addressing these feelings of disconnection and lack of purpose often has a much higher price â€“ in terms of lost joy, missed opportunities, and continued emotional strain. This workshop is designed as a focused, powerful intervention, helping you create lasting change in a way that traditional approaches often can't. Think of it as an investment in your renewed sense of self and future happiness."
  • Highlight Unique ROI: "The experiential nature of working with horses provides insights and shifts that are often profound and accelerate personal growth. Clients frequently report feeling more confident and clear on their path within weeks, leading to greater fulfillment that far outweighs the financial investment."
Coach Tip from Sarah

Be prepared to discuss payment plans if you offer them. Having a clear, structured payment option can often alleviate financial concerns for clients like Eleanor. Mention it proactively if appropriate: "We also have flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."

"I need to think about it."

This could mean many things. Gently uncover the real hesitation.

  • Acknowledge & Probe: "Of course, Eleanor, this is a big decision and it's important you feel fully confident. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps whether this is truly the right approach for you?"
  • Address the Specific Concern: (Once she shares, address it directly. E.g., if it's the group aspect) "I hear your concern about opening up in a group. What I can tell you is that the horses create such a unique, non-judgmental space that often clients find it easier to connect and be vulnerable than they ever expected. The focus is on your interaction with the horses, and sharing is always at your comfort level. The support from other women often becomes a powerful unexpected benefit."
  • Create Urgency (Softly): "The next 'Finding Your Herd' workshop starts on [Date], and we keep groups small to ensure a personal experience, so spots fill quickly. If you feel a genuine pull towards this, I encourage you to consider moving forward so you don't miss out on this opportunity."

"Will this really work for me?"

This is a request for reassurance and proof of concept.

  • Validate Skepticism: "That's a very fair question, Eleanor, especially given your past experiences. It's natural to wonder if something new will truly deliver."
  • Explain the Mechanism (briefly): "What makes equine therapy so effective is that it bypasses the 'thinking' mind and engages your somatic wisdom. Horses are incredibly sensitive and respond authentically to your non-verbal cues. This allows you to experience shifts in confidence, boundaries, and self-awareness in a way that just talking about it often doesn't achieve. You're not just learning about change; you're experiencing it in real-time, with a powerful, non-judgmental partner."
  • Social Proof: "I've seen countless women, many with stories similar to yours, find profound breakthroughs in these programs. For example, [brief, anonymized success story that mirrors Eleanor's goals]."

Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

When it comes to discussing money, clarity and confidence are your best allies. Avoid hesitation or apologetic tones.

Structuring Your Offers

For group programs, you typically offer a single, comprehensive package. For Eleanor, your "Finding Your Herd" workshop might be structured as:

  • Duration: A 6-week program, meeting once a week for 3 hours.
  • Inclusions: All equine sessions, guided journaling prompts, a private online group for support, and a follow-up individual call.
  • Value Proposition: "This comprehensive package is designed to not only help you reconnect with your inner strength but also to build a supportive community and integrate your learnings into your daily life."

Stating Your Investment Clearly

Once you've presented the value, state the price directly and then pause. Let the number land.

  • The Lead-In: "The investment for the 6-week 'Finding Your Herd' workshop, including all sessions, materials, and support, is..."
  • The Price: "...$1,800."
  • Payment Options: "We offer a single payment option, or for those who prefer, three monthly payments of $625."
  • The Pause: After stating the price and options, stop talking. Let her process. The next person to speak often buys.
Coach Tip from Sarah

Your tone when stating the price should be the same as when you discuss the program's benefits â€“ confident, clear, and matter-of-fact. If you sound hesitant, it signals a lack of belief in your own value. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels completely natural.

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing Lines

Once you've addressed questions and presented pricing, it's time for a clear call to action.

  • Direct Close: "How does that sound to you, Eleanor? Are you ready to reserve your spot in the 'Finding Your Herd' workshop and begin this journey?"
  • Benefit-Oriented Close: "If you're ready to rediscover your purpose and connect with a supportive community, securing your spot today means you'll be joining us for the next workshop starting [Date]. What feels like the best next step for you?"
  • Assumptive Close: "Great! I can send you the enrollment link right after this call, which includes all the details and payment options. Does that work for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1

During the discovery call, Eleanor expresses concern about the cost of the "Finding Your Herd" workshop. What is the most effective way to address this objection?

The most effective approach is to validate her concern and then immediately reframe the cost as an investment in her long-term well-being and personal growth, highlighting the unique value and transformative outcomes of equine-assisted therapy compared to the "cost" of inaction. You could also mention flexible payment plans if available.
2

Eleanor mentions she needs to "find her spark again" and feels "disconnected." In which phase of the discovery call should you focus on exploring these core needs in depth?

These core needs should be explored in depth during Phase 2: Deepening Understanding & Uncovering Needs. This phase is dedicated to asking open-ended questions to fully grasp the client's pain points, aspirations, and the emotional impact of their current situation.
3

When presenting the program's investment, what is a crucial non-verbal technique to employ after stating the price?

After confidently stating the price and payment options, it is crucial to pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect to process the information without feeling pressured or interrupted, inviting them to speak next and often revealing their true thoughts or questions.
4

What is the primary purpose of the "Call-to-Action" phase in a discovery call?

The primary purpose of the Call-to-Action phase is to clearly and confidently invite the prospect to take the next concrete step towards enrollment, whether it's reserving their spot, reviewing the enrollment link, or confirming their commitment to the program. It's about guiding them forward in their decision-making process.
