The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Your authentic self is your best tool. Adapt these lines to your natural voice, but ensure you hit these key points to guide the conversation effectively.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about AccrediPro Equine Therapy?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that you connected with us through the wellness group. It's a great community. So Sarah, tell me â€” what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling overwhelmed and burnt out. Can you tell me more about how that shows up in your daily life? How does it affect your energy, your mood, your relationships?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings? What worked, what didn't, and what makes you feel like you need a different approach now?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel like 'yourself' again, what would that look and feel like? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're feeling overwhelmed and disconnected, yearning for a sense of calm and purpose, and you're ready for a unique, holistic path to reclaim your well-being. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach we use here at AccrediPro Equine Therapy is truly different. Instead of just talking about feelings, we engage with horses as partners. This unique interaction helps you build self-awareness, resilience, and emotional regulation in a profound, embodied way. Our 8-week 'Reclaim Your Calm' program is specifically designed for women like you who are ready to reconnect with their inner strength and find peace. Many of my clients, often busy professionals or mothers, report feeling significantly more grounded and confident within the first few sessions."
YOU:
"We'll work together one-on-one, typically once a week, and each session is highly personalized to your goals. The horses provide immediate, honest feedback, helping you unlock insights and practice new ways of being in a safe, non-judgmental space."
Phase 4: Call-to-Action & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Sarah, does this unique approach to reclaiming your calm and purpose sound like what you've been searching for? ... Wonderful! Let me tell you a little more about the 'Reclaim Your Calm' program and how we can get started."
Coach Tip from Sarah

Always pause after asking a question and allow for silence. It gives the prospect space to think and respond authentically. Don't be afraid of silence; it's often where the most meaningful insights emerge.

Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're rarely a "no," but rather a request for more information, reassurance, or clarification. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"The investment is more than I expected/It's a lot of money."
"I appreciate your honesty, and I know investing in yourself can feel like a big step, especially when balancing family needs. Many of my clients, like yourself, initially feel this way. What they often find is that the transformation they experience â€“ the renewed energy, reduced anxiety, and stronger sense of self â€“ creates immense value that impacts every area of their lives. What's the cost of staying where you are, feeling overwhelmed and burnt out? Our program is an investment in your long-term well-being and a return to the vibrant woman you know you can be. We also have payment plans available to make it more accessible."
"I'm not sure if equine therapy is right for me/I've never worked with horses."
"That's a very common concern, Sarah! And it's exactly why our approach is so effective. You don't need any prior horse experience. In fact, many clients find the novelty of working with horses without riding them to be incredibly liberating. The horses are intuitive mirrors; they don't judge or expect anything from you, creating a safe space for authentic self-discovery. We start with very gentle, ground-based activities, always ensuring your comfort and safety. It's about the unique relational experience, not horsemanship."
"I don't have enough time."
"I hear you, Sarah. As a busy mom and former teacher, your schedule is likely packed. What my clients often tell me is that dedicating this one hour a week to themselves, away from their daily demands, actually creates more time and energy in the long run. By reducing anxiety and building resilience, they become more efficient and present in other areas of their lives. Think of it as an essential self-care appointment that gives back tenfold, rather than another thing on your to-do list. We can explore scheduling options to find a time that truly works for you."
Coach Tip from Sarah

Never argue with an objection. Validate their concern, then reframe it through the lens of your program's benefits. Use phrases like, "I understand that..." or "Many of my clients feel that way, and what they found was..."

Confidently Presenting Your Pricing

This is often the most nerve-wracking part, but it doesn't have to be. Confidence in your value translates directly into confidence in your pricing. Clearly state your program fee, then immediately follow up with the value and next steps.

Presenting Your Investment

After Sarah expresses interest and you've addressed initial questions:

YOU:
"The investment for the 8-week 'Reclaim Your Calm' program is $2,400. This includes eight personalized one-on-one equine therapy sessions, a comprehensive workbook, and ongoing email support between sessions. We also offer a flexible payment plan of three monthly installments of $850. Which option works best for you?"

Key Principles:

  • State the full price first: Don't apologize or hesitate.
  • Immediately follow with value: Remind them what they're getting for that investment.
  • Offer options: Payment plans can significantly reduce the barrier to entry.
  • Ask a closing question: This shifts the conversation from "if" to "how."
Coach Tip from Sarah

Practice saying your prices out loud, confidently and without hesitation, until it feels natural. Your energy around pricing significantly impacts how clients perceive its value. Remember, you're offering transformation, not just sessions.

Practicing Your Call-to-Action

The call-to-action (CTA) is where you clearly outline the next steps. Make it easy for your prospect to say "yes" and move forward.

Closing Lines to Practice Out Loud

If they're ready to move forward:
"That's wonderful! I'm so excited for you to begin this journey. The next step is to complete our enrollment form and schedule your first session. I can send you the link right now, or we can walk through it together if you prefer."
If they need a little more time (after addressing objections):
"I completely understand. I'm confident this program can help you find the calm and purpose you're seeking. How about I send you a summary of what we discussed, along with the program details and payment options? I'll follow up with you by to answer any further questions you might have. Does that work for you?"
If it's not a good fit:
"Based on our conversation, it sounds like [mention specific reasons, e.g., 'your needs might be better met by X, Y, or Z']. While my program might not be the right fit at this moment, I truly wish you the best on your journey to well-being. Please feel free to reach out if anything changes, or if you have any questions in the future."

Check Your Understanding

1. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective immediate response?

The most effective response is to validate their need for thought, then gently probe for the specific concern: "I completely understand. This is an important decision. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This helps uncover the true objection.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem & Vision" phase of the discovery call?

The primary purpose is to deeply listen and uncover the prospect's current challenges (pain points), what they've tried previously, and their desired future state (their "magic wand" vision). This allows you to tailor your solution to their specific needs.

3. When presenting your pricing, what two key elements should immediately follow the stated program fee?

After stating the program fee, you should immediately follow with: 1) a clear articulation of the value they will receive (what's included, the transformation), and 2) a clear call-to-action or next step, often involving payment options or scheduling.

4. How does the Equine Therapy Specialist certification contribute to overcoming prospect skepticism during a discovery call?

Your certification provides a strong foundation of legitimacy and expertise. It demonstrates that you have undergone rigorous training and adhere to professional standards, which can significantly build trust and alleviate skepticism, particularly for prospects new to equine-assisted therapy. You can confidently reference your specialized training and the evidence-based principles you apply.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured conversation focused on connection, understanding, and guiding the client to a solution.
  • Effective rapport building, deep listening, and understanding the client's pain points and desired future are critical for successful conversion.
  • Anticipate and empathetically address objections by validating concerns and reframing them through the benefits of your program.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and clarity, immediately following with the value proposition and clear next steps.
  • Regular practice of your script, objection handling, and closing lines will build your confidence and conversion rates, leading to a thriving practice.
