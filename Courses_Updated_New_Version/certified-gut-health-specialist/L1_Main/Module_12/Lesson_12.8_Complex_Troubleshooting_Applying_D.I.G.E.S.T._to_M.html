4 Common Mistakes New Coaches Make

  • Over-Explaining Science: Don't give a 20-minute lecture on the Firmicutes/Bacteroidetes ratio. Diane wants to know how she'll feel, not the Latin names of every bacteria.
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Jumping to supplements in the first 5 minutes. You must build the relationship and the "Why" first.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Diane is stressed. If you don't acknowledge her stress as a principal, your gut protocol will likely fail.
  • Apologizing for the Price: When you say "I know it's expensive, but...", you undermine your own value. State your fee with a neutral, confident tone.
Sarah's Insight

If you feel nervous about the "sales" part, remember: you are offering Diane a solution she hasn't found anywhere else. You are a Specialist. Specialists don't apologize for their fees; they provide results that change lives.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use Diane's own words (like "broken") back to her during the intake?

It builds "Neural Resonance" and rapport. It makes the client feel deeply heard and understood, which is the foundation of the coaching relationship.

2. What is the "Authority of Empathy"?

It is the balance of showing deep compassion for the client's struggle while maintaining the professional boundary and knowledge of a clinical specialist.

3. True or False: You should explain the exact microbial species involved in dysbiosis during the first 10 minutes of a discovery call.

False. Over-explaining science too early can overwhelm the client. Focus on analogies and the "Why" behind their symptoms first.

4. According to the "Window Screen" analogy, what represents "the flies"?

Undigested food particles, toxins, and pathogenic bacteria that should stay inside the digestive tract but "leak" through a damaged gut lining.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Connection Before Correction: Establish rapport and validate the client's experience before diving into the protocol.
  • Use High-Value Analogies: Translate complex gut biology into simple, visual concepts like the "window screen."
  • Bridge Symptoms to Biology: Always explain how a gut issue is causing a non-gut symptom (like brain fog).
  • Own Your Value: Present your program as the specific solution to their high-pain problem without apologizing for the investment.
