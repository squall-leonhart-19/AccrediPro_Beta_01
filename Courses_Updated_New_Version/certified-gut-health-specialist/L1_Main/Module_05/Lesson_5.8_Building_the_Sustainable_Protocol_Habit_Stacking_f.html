Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

As you transition into your new career, watch out for these common "new coach" mistakes during the Sustain phase:

Common Mistake

Over-prescribing: Adding even more supplements to "make sure" the progress stays. This leads to client burnout.

The Pro Move

Simplifying: Identifying the "Minimum Effective Dose" of habits that keep the client symptom-free.

Sarah's Tip

Many of my students feel they aren't "providing enough value" if they aren't changing the protocol every week. Remember: In the Sustain phase, boredom is a sign of success. If the client is bored, it means the habits have become second nature.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological hurdle for clients in the Sustain phase?

Relapse anxiety and an identity crisis (still seeing themselves as a "sick person").

2. Why is "habit stacking" more effective than "willpower" for long-term gut health?

Habit stacking utilizes existing neural pathways (anchors), making the new behavior automatic and reducing the cognitive load on the client.

3. What does the "80/20 rule" mean in the context of gut maintenance?

It means that if a client maintains their core gut-supportive habits 80% of the time, the microbiome is resilient enough to handle the other 20% (occasional stressors or dietary diversions).

4. How do you know a Sustain session has been successful?

The client leaves feeling a sense of "lightness" and clarity, with 1-3 specific, automated actions rather than a long list of new rules.

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Validate the client's fear of relapse before moving into clinical solutions.
  • Use professional language like "automating progress" to build client confidence.
  • Anchor new habits to existing routines (Habit Stacking) to ensure longevity.
  • Focus on the "Minimum Effective Dose" of habits to prevent client burnout.
