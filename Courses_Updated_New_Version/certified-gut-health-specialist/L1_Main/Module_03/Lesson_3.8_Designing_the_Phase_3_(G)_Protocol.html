Common Mistakes for New Coaches

When I first started, I made all of these! Watch out for these pitfalls during your sessions:

  • Over-Explaining the Science: You don't need to explain the entire Krebs cycle. Focus on how the supplement or food helps the client's specific symptom (e.g., "This helps calm the bloating").
  • Promising Instant Results: Gut healing takes time. A 2022 study showed that significant intestinal barrier repair often takes 4-12 weeks of consistent support. Set expectations early.
  • Ignoring Lifestyle: You can give all the L-Glutamine in the world, but if the client is under high stress, the gut won't heal. Always mention the "Gut-Brain" connection.
  • Sounding Like a Prescription: Remember, you are a coach. Use phrases like "I suggest we explore..." or "Based on the research, many clients find success with..."

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks why they can't just take a probiotic to fix their "leaky gut." What is the best coaching response?

Explain that while probiotics are great for the "neighborhood" (the microbiome), the "house" (the gut lining) needs structural repairs first. You can't plant a garden (probiotics) if the soil (gut lining) is washed away.

2. What is the primary role of L-Glutamine in the "G" phase of gut healing?

L-Glutamine serves as the primary fuel source for enterocytes (gut cells), helping them regenerate and maintain the integrity of the tight junctions.

3. If a client is skeptical about "leaky gut," what clinical term should you use instead?

Increased Intestinal Permeability. This term is widely recognized in medical literature and peer-reviewed studies.

4. Why is stress management included in a gut-healing protocol?

Cortisol (the stress hormone) can directly increase intestinal permeability. Without managing stress, the physical "patches" (supplements) may not hold.

Key Takeaways

  • Analogies are your best friend: Use the "cheesecloth" or "window screen" analogy to explain permeability.
  • Add, don't just subtract: Focus on the healing raw materials (L-Glutamine, Zinc Carnosine, Collagen).
  • Validate the struggle: Acknowledge the client's frustration to build an alliance.
  • Set a 4-12 week timeline: Be honest about how long biological repair takes.
