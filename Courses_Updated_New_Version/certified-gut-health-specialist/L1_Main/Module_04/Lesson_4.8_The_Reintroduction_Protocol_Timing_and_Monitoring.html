Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Avoid these common mistakes during the "Establish" phase to ensure your client feels supported rather than burdened.

  • The Information Dump: Providing a 40-page PDF on the first day. Better: Give a 2-page "Quick Start" guide and drip the rest.
  • Ignoring the Emotional State: Diving into the protocol before checking how the client is feeling that day.
  • Lack of Specificity: Saying "eat more vegetables" instead of "add one cup of leafy greens to your lunch."
  • Under-selling the "Why": If they don't understand the science (simplified), they won't value the habit.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to link every protocol suggestion to the client's specific history?

It creates "buy-in." When a client sees that a recommendation (like bitter tonics) is a direct response to their symptom (bloating), they perceive it as a solution rather than just another chore.

2. What is the "80/20 Lifestyle Rule" in the context of gut health coaching?

It means following the protocol 80% of the time while allowing for 20% flexibility (social events, travel). This prevents the "all-or-nothing" mentality that leads to burnout and quitting.

3. If a client like Linda feels overwhelmed by the plan, what should be your first response?

Validation. Acknowledge that it is a lot of change, then offer to "scale back" to the most essential 1 or 2 habits to ensure success.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Establish" phase?

To create a clear, actionable, and agreed-upon roadmap that the client feels confident they can execute over the next 30 days.

Key Takeaways

  • Presentation is everything; lead with empathy and validation before data.
  • Limit initial changes to 3 core pillars to prevent client overwhelm.
  • Use the "Expert Partner" tone to build trust and authority.
  • Always explain the why behind dietary and lifestyle shifts.
  • Success in the "Establish" phase is the foundation for long-term client results and practitioner income.
