Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive" phase of the Discovery Call?

The goal is to move beyond surface-level symptoms and uncover the "Deep Why" (emotional drivers) and the "Cost of Inaction" (what happens if they don't fix the problem).

2. If a prospect says "I've tried everything and nothing works," how should you respond?

Acknowledge their frustration (empathy) and then explain how your approach is fundamentally different (e.g., "I understand. Most programs use a 'one-size-fits-all' approach, but we use the DIGEST framework to find YOUR specific triggers.")

3. Why is "Package Pricing" superior to "Hourly Pricing" for a Gut Health Specialist?

Package pricing focuses on the value of the transformation rather than the time spent. It also ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see real biological changes (usually 12 weeks).

4. What is the "Feel, Felt, Found" technique used for?

It is a non-confrontational way to handle objections by validating the prospect's feelings, sharing that others have felt the same, and revealing the positive outcome they found.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure Equals Sales: A 30-minute call with a clear 4-phase structure prevents rambling and leads to higher conversion rates.
  • Listen More, Talk Less: You should be listening 70% of the time. The more the prospect talks about their pain, the more they realize they need your help.
  • Own Your Value: You are not a "coach"; you are a Specialist. Your pricing should reflect the years of study and the depth of the AccrediPro curriculum.
  • Objections are Opportunities: Treat concerns about money or time as a chance to reinforce the value of the health transformation.
