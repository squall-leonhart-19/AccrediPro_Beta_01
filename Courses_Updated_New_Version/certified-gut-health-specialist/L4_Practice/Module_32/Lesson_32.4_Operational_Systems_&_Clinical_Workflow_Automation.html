The Gut Health SOP Framework

A Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is simply a "recipe" for a recurring task in your business. Without SOPs, you are reinventing the wheel every time a new client signs up. For a Gut Health Specialist, there are three critical SOPs that must be documented:

  1. The Onboarding SOP: From payment to the first "Detect" session. This includes the welcome packet, portal access, and intake form triggers.
  2. The Lab Coordination SOP: How kits are ordered, how clients are notified to fast/prepare, and how results are uploaded to the EHR.
  3. The Follow-up SOP: The 48-hour post-session protocol, including sending the summary, supplement links, and scheduling the next touchpoint.
Coach Tip: The 3-Time Rule

If you perform a task more than three times, it deserves an SOP. Record a quick "Loom" video of yourself doing the task once, and that becomes your training manual for your future Virtual Assistant (VA).

Sustain Phase & Retention Automation

The 'Sustain' phase is where many practitioners lose revenue. Once a client feels better, they often drift away. However, true gut health requires long-term maintenance. Automation allows you to provide "High-Touch" support with "Low-Labor" input.

Implementing an Automated Sustain Sequence might look like this:

  • Month 4 (Post-Healing): Automated email checking in on Vagal Tone exercises.
  • Month 6: A "Re-Detect" symptom survey to catch any minor dysbiosis before it becomes a flare.
  • Month 9: Seasonal "Microbiome Refresh" recipes sent automatically via your EHR.

A study of wellness practitioners found that implementing automated check-ins increased client retention by 42% over a 12-month period. This is the difference between a "one-off" client and a lifelong advocate.

Project Management Tools: Asana & Trello

While an EHR handles clinical data, a Project Management (PM) tool handles business tasks. In a complex gut health case, you may be tracking multiple labs, physician referrals, and supplement shipments simultaneously.

How to use PM tools in your practice:

  • The "Client Board": Use a Trello board with columns: Lead -> Onboarding -> Detect -> Identify -> Healing -> Sustain. Move client "cards" through the workflow.
  • The "Lab Tracker": Use Asana to set due dates for lab result reviews. Never let a GI-MAP result sit in your inbox for 5 days without a review.
  • Team Collaboration: If you hire a VA or a junior coach, the PM tool becomes the "Single Source of Truth" for the practice.
Coach Tip: Start Simple

Don't over-complicate your PM tool. A simple Trello board with five columns is often more effective than a complex Asana setup that you never look at. The goal is visibility, not complexity.
