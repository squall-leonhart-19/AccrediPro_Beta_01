3. Handling Objections with Confidence

A 2023 industry report found that 74% of prospective clients will raise at least one objection regarding price or timing. This isn't a "no"â€”it's a request for more information.

Objection 1: The "I Need to Think About It"

The Strategy: Identify if itâ€™s a "no" or a "fear."

Your Response: "I completely understand. This is a commitment. Usually, when people say they need to think, it's because I haven't clarified something. Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or are you just wondering if your body can actually heal?"

Objection 2: The "Spouse" Objection

The Strategy: Bring the spouse into the "vision."

Your Response: "I love that you value your partner's input. When you talk to him tonight, are you going to tell him about the 'gut protocols,' or are you going to tell him that you found a way to finally have the energy to go on those weekend hikes again?"

Sarah's Secret

Never defend your price. Defend the result. If they say it's expensive, remind them of the cost of not fixing it (missed work, doctor copays, and low quality of life).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Discovery Phase" in a call?

The primary purpose is to identify the "Gap"â€”the distance between where the client is now (pain) and where they want to be (vision)â€”and to determine if you have the clinical tools to bridge that gap.

2. Linda says, "I've tried a gluten-free diet and it didn't work." How should you respond?

Validate her effort, then pivot to authority: "I'm glad you tried that, as it's a great first step. However, for most people with chronic bloating, 'just gluten-free' is like putting a band-aid on a deep wound. We need to look at the underlying microbiome balance to see WHY your body is reacting."

3. Why is it recommended to sell packages rather than hourly sessions?

Packages ensure client "buy-in" for the full duration of the healing process (gut health isn't fixed in one hour), provide predictable income for the practitioner, and shift the focus from "buying time" to "buying a result."

4. What should you do immediately after stating your program price?

Stay silent. This allows the prospect to process the investment and come to their own conclusion without feeling pressured by "nervous chatter" from the practitioner.
