Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The goal is to understand the client's pain points and, more importantly, the impact those symptoms have on their daily life and future goals. This builds the "case for change."

2. What should you do immediately after stating your price?

You should pause and remain silent. This is the "Golden Silence," which allows the client to process the information and respond without feeling pressured by "filler" talk.

3. How should you handle the "I need to talk to my spouse" objection?

Respect the boundary, but offer to provide a summary or a "spouse-friendly" PDF that outlines the value and plan, ensuring you stay in the loop during their decision-making process.

4. Why is it important to mention a specific event (like Diane's daughter's wedding) during the call?

It connects the health outcome to a high-stakes emotional driver. It moves the conversation from "fixing bloating" to "being a confident mother-of-the-bride."

Key Takeaways

  • Listen More, Talk Less: Aim for a 30/70 split where the prospect does most of the talking.
  • Outcome-Based Pricing: Price your services based on the transformation (relief, energy, confidence) rather than your hourly rate.
  • Empathy Over Sales: A discovery call is a service in itself. Even if they don't buy, they should leave feeling heard and understood.
  • Professionalism is Compliance: Staying within your scope of practice during the call builds trust and protects your business legally.
