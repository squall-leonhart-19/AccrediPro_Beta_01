2. The 5-Phase Discovery Script

A discovery call isn't an interrogation; it's a guided journey from where they are to where they want to be. Use this structure to maintain control of the conversation while building deep trust.

Phase 1: The Frame (3 Minutes)

YOU:

"Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our chat. My goal for today is to hear about what you've been going through, share a bit about how I work, and by the end, we'll both know if this is the right fit for you. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (12 Minutes)

YOU:

"You mentioned the bloating and brain fog in your intake form. Tell me, how is that actually affecting your day-to-day life right now?"

LINDA:

"I just feel so heavy. I avoid going out to dinner because I know I'll have to unbutton my pants. I feel like I'm losing my edge."

YOU:

"I hear you. And if we don't get this sorted out, where do you see your health in a year from now?"

Phase 3: The Bridge (5 Minutes)

YOU:

"Linda, based on what you've shared, I'm confident I can help. The reason those 'normal' labs didn't help is that they weren't looking at the functional root of your gut-brain axis. In my 12-week program, we don't just guess; we rebuild your microbiome from the ground up."

Phase 4: The Offer (5 Minutes)

YOU:

"Iâ€™d love to invite you into my 'Gut Restoration Intensive.' Itâ€™s a 3-month partnership where we handle the testing, the personalized nutrition, and the weekly support to get your energy back. Does that sound like the support youâ€™ve been looking for?"

Phase 5: The Logistics (5 Minutes)

YOU:

"The investment for the full 3-month program is $1,800. We can get you started as early as Tuesday. Would you like to use a credit card or set up a payment plan?"

Sarah's Insight

Notice that I didn't say "Is that okay?" after the price. I stated the price and asked how she wanted to pay. Silence after the price is your best friend. Let her process it.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) the most important part of the call?

It connects the client's physical symptoms to their emotional pain and future consequences. Without this emotional connection, the client will see your price as a "cost" rather than an "investment" in their future self.

2. What is the most effective way to handle the "I need to talk to my husband" objection?

Acknowledge the respect for the partner, then ask what his specific concerns might be. This helps the client process her own fears and prepares her to have a productive conversation with him, rather than just "asking permission."

3. Based on the income table, how many clients per month do you need at a $1,800 price point to reach a six-figure income?

Approximately 5 clients per month ($9,000/mo x 12 = $108,000/year). This illustrates that you don't need a massive audience to have a highly successful practice.

4. Why is silence important after stating your price?

Silence demonstrates confidence and authority. If you speak too soon, you often end up "discounting against yourself" out of nervousness. It gives the client space to make a firm internal decision.
