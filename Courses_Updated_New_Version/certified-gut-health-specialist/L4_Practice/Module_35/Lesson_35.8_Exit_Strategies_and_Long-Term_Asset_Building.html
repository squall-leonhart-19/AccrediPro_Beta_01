The "Scaling" Discovery Call Script

A successful discovery call is 80% listening and 20% strategic questioning. Use this structure to guide Deborah from skepticism to "When can we start?"

Phase 1: The "Why Now?" Hook (0-5 mins)
YOU:
"Deborah, I've read your intake form. You've clearly been through the ringer with your GI health. But tell me‚Äîwhy is today the day you decided to hop on a call with me? What was the final straw?"

(Listen for the emotional trigger‚Äîthis is what you will circle back to during the close.)

Phase 2: The Gap Analysis (5-15 mins)
YOU:
"You mentioned the brain fog is affecting your meetings. If we don't get this gut-brain axis sorted, what does your work life look like six months from now? Conversely, if you woke up tomorrow with 100% clarity, what's the first thing you'd do?"
Phase 3: The Specialized Solution (15-25 mins)
YOU:
"The reason your labs look 'normal' is that conventional tests look for disease, not function. In my 12-week Protocol, we use functional markers to see where the system is breaking down. We don't guess; we map."
Phase 4: The Confident Close (25-30 mins)
YOU:
"Based on everything you‚Äôve told me, you are a perfect fit for the 'Gut Restoration Elite' program. I‚Äôd love to walk you through how we get started."
Sarah's Insight

Notice I didn't say "Does that sound okay?" I said "I'd love to walk you through how we get started." Assume the sale. You are the expert prescribing a solution, not a salesperson asking for a favor.

Handling Objections with Authority

When Deborah says "That's a lot of money," she isn't saying no. She is asking for certainty. Use these scripts to pivot.

1. The "Investment" Pivot

Deborah: "I wasn't expecting it to be $2,900."

You: "I completely understand. It is a significant investment. But let's look at what the brain fog is costing you‚Äîmissed opportunities, medical co-pays that lead nowhere, and your quality of life. Is it worth $2,900 to have the next 20 years of your life back?"

2. The "Time" Objection

Deborah: "I'm so busy right now, I don't know if I can do 12 weeks."

You: "That's exactly why you need this. We aren't adding more to your plate; we are optimizing your energy so your 'busy' feels effortless. If we wait until you're 'not busy,' we might be waiting until a health crisis forces you to stop."

Sarah's Insight

Never apologize for your price. If you lower your price the moment they flinch, you are telling them that your results aren't actually worth what you claimed.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Gap Analysis" the most important part of the discovery call?

It connects the client's current pain to their future goals, making the cost of "staying the same" higher than the cost of the program.

2. What is the "Sweet Spot" price for a 12-week transformation package based on industry standards?

Typically between $2,500 and $3,500, which allows for high-touch support without requiring a massive volume of clients.

3. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to talk to my spouse"?

"I completely respect that. What do you think they will be most concerned about‚Äîthe time, the cost, or the approach? Let's make sure you have the answers they'll need." (This keeps you in the consultant role).

4. What is the primary benefit of package-based pricing over hourly billing?

It focuses on outcomes rather than time, increases client commitment, and allows the practitioner to scale income without working more hours.

Lab Key Takeaways

  • Assume the Sale: High-value clients want a leader, not a negotiator. Speak with the authority of your credentials.
  • Focus on ROI: Health is the ultimate investment. Always frame the price in the context of what they are "buying back" (energy, focus, time).
  • Tier Your Offers: Use three price points to help clients find the "middle" option, which is usually your most profitable.
  • Practice the Silence: After you state your price, stop talking. Let the client process. The first one to speak often loses the leverage.
