The Group Enrollment Script

Unlike 1-on-1 calls, group enrollment calls focus on the shared journey and the proven system. You are selling the outcome of the collective.

Phase 1: Validating the Connection (3 Mins)

YOU:

"Elena, it was so great to see your questions in the webinar chat last night. You mentioned you've been struggling with that afternoon brain fog for about two years now. What would it mean for your new consulting business if we could clear that up in the next 6 weeks?"

Phase 2: The Power of the Collective (7 Mins)

YOU:

"One reason I created the 'Gut Renewal Collective' is because I saw so many women like you‚Äîambitious, busy, but feeling held back by their digestion‚Äîtrying to solve this alone. In the group, you aren't just getting my expertise; you're getting the accountability of 10 other women on the exact same protocol. Does that community aspect sound like it would help you stay on track?"

Sarah's Tip: Use "The Collective" Language

Always refer to the group as a "Collective," "Mastermind," or "Cohort." This elevates the perceived value from a "cheap class" to a premium, shared professional experience.

Phase 3: Presenting the Solution (10 Mins)

YOU:

"The program is 8 weeks. We meet every Tuesday for a deep-dive clinical session, and you have a private portal with your meal plans and supplement protocols. Because this is a group format, I'm able to offer it at $897, which is about a third of the investment of my 1-on-1 private packages, while giving you the exact same clinical roadmap."

Handling Common Group Objections

Objection 1: "I'm worried I need 1-on-1 attention for my specific issues."

Your Response: "I completely understand. That's why I've designed this with a 'Hybrid' approach. Sarah's Tip: The "VIP Upgrade"

If they are truly insistent on 1-on-1 time, offer a "VIP Upgrade." For an extra $300, they get the group program PLUS two 30-minute private sessions. This is an easy way to increase your average order value!

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of a group program for the client?

The primary benefit is social support and accountability. Clients realize they aren't alone in their struggle, which reduces shame and increases the likelihood of sticking to the protocol (compliance).

2. How should you respond if a client says the group price is too high?

Compare it to the alternative. Explain that the group investment is significantly lower than 1-on-1 private coaching, yet they receive the exact same clinical framework and materials. It is the most cost-effective way to access your expertise.

3. What is a "VIP Upgrade" in the context of a group program?

A VIP Upgrade is an add-on (usually $200-$500 extra) that includes the group program plus a limited amount of private 1-on-1 time (e.g., two 30-minute calls) for those who want extra personalized attention.

4. Why is a "Beta Group" recommended for your first launch?

A Beta Group allows you to test your curriculum with a smaller group at a lower price point in exchange for honest feedback and testimonials. This builds the "social proof" needed to charge premium prices for future cohorts.
