Hypochlorhydria: The Silent Trigger of Skin Flares

The link between stomach acid and skin health was noted as early as the 1930s, when researchers found that 40% of acne patients suffered from hypochlorhydria. When stomach acid is low, the body fails to "disinfect" the upper GI tract and cannot properly break down proteins.

This failure in the Detect and Identify phases leads to two major skin-disrupting events:

  • Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO): Without the "acid barrier," bacteria migrate upward, leading to systemic inflammation that manifests on the face.
  • Mineral Malabsorption: Zinc and Magnesium‚Äîtwo critical nutrients for skin repair‚Äîrequire an acidic environment for absorption.

The Estrobolome: Why Jawline Acne is Rarely Just "Skin"

Elena‚Äôs acne was primarily concentrated on her jawline, a classic sign of hormonal involvement. However, the problem wasn't just her ovaries; it was her Estrobolome‚Äîthe collection of gut bacteria responsible for metabolizing estrogen.

Substance P: The Neuro-Immune Link

Why does stress make acne and rosacea worse? It's not just "in the head." Stress triggers the release of Substance P, a neuropeptide produced in the gut and the brain. Substance P acts directly on the sebaceous (oil) glands, increasing oil production and triggering mast cell degranulation (redness and swelling).

This is where the Sustain phase of our method is vital. By using Vagal Tone optimization and mindful eating, we lower the "Substance P load," preventing the stress-induced flares that often derail a client's progress.
