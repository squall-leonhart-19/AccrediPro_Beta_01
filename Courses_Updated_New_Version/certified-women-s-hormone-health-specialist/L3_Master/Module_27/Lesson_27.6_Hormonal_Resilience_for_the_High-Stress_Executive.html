The Cortisol:DHEA-S Ratio

DHEA-S is the "youth and vitality" hormone that counterbalances cortisol. In chronic stress, the body prioritizes cortisol production (the "survival" hormone) over DHEA-S. A high ratio indicates that the client is cannibalizing her own tissues and neurochemistry to maintain her career output.

Sleep Architecture & Circadian Alignment

Executives often view sleep as a "luxury" rather than a metabolic necessity. However, Regulating the Nervous System (R) is impossible without restorative sleep architecture. For women over 40, the transition into perimenopause makes the brain more sensitive to sleep disruption.

The primary disruptor for the executive is blue light exposure from late-night emails. This suppresses melatonin, the hormone that signals the "ovarian-adrenal-thyroid" (OAT) axis to repair during the night. A single hour of blue light exposure in the evening can delay melatonin onset by up to 90 minutes.

Coach Tip: The 3-2-1 Rule

Teach your executive clients the 3-2-1 rule for hormonal recovery: 3 hours before bed, stop eating (to prevent insulin spikes); 2 hours before bed, stop work (to lower cortisol); 1 hour before bed, no screens (to allow melatonin rise).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the Cortisol:DHEA-S ratio more important than just measuring cortisol alone in an executive?

Because DHEA-S acts as the "anabolic buffer." High cortisol might be necessary for the job, but if DHEA-S is low, the client has no protection against tissue breakdown, neuro-inflammation, and burnout. The ratio shows if the stress is "sustainable" or "destructive."

2. What is the primary hormonal reason for "Luteal Phase Brain Fog" in high-stress women?

The progesterone-GABA mismatch. Stress "steals" progesterone precursors to make cortisol (the 'Pregnenolone Steal' concept), leading to insufficient GABA stimulation in the brain, which causes the fog and irritability.

3. How does blue light exposure specifically affect the OAT axis?

Blue light suppresses melatonin. Melatonin is not just for sleep; it is a master regulator that signals the Ovarian-Adrenal-Thyroid axis to enter "repair mode." Without it, the body stays in a catabolic "stress mode" even during sleep.

4. Which adaptogen is considered the "Executive's Choice" for morning focus?

Rhodiola Rosea, due to its ability to improve mental performance and reduce fatigue without being overly stimulatory or sedative.
