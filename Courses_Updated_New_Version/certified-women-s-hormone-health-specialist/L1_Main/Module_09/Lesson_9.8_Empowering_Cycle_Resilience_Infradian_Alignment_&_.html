Common Coaching Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps. Awareness is the first step to mastery.

  • 1. The "Information Dump": Giving the client a 20-minute lecture on the HPA axis. The Fix: Give one piece of science, then ask, "How does that land with you?"
  • 2. Being Too Rigid: Telling a high-stress executive she must meditate for 30 minutes daily. The Fix: Suggest "micro-habits," like three deep belly breaths before opening her laptop.
  • 3. Ignoring the "Why": Recommending a supplement without explaining which hormone it supports. The Fix: "We're using Magnesium Glycinate to help support that progesterone dip we talked about."
  • 4. Solving Too Many Problems: Giving a list of 10 changes at once. The Fix: Focus on the "Lead Domino"â€”usually blood sugar or sleep hygiene.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Elena's stress before giving hormone advice?

Validation lowers the client's cortisol and builds the "therapeutic alliance." A client who feels judged or overwhelmed will not be able to implement physiological changes.

2. What is the "Lead Domino" for a client with 3 AM wakeups and midsection weight gain?

Usually, it is Blood Sugar Stability. By fixing her "skipping breakfast/light lunch" habit, you stabilize insulin and reduce the cortisol spikes that cause nighttime wakeups.

3. If Elena says "I don't have time to cook breakfast," how should a coach respond?

4. What hormone is often the first to decline in perimenopause, leading to increased anxiety?

Progesterone. Its decline reduces the production of GABA in the brain, making women more susceptible to stress and "racing heart" sensations.
