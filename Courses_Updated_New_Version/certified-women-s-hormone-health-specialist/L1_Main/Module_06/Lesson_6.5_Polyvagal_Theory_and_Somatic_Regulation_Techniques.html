The Three States of the Nervous System

Developed by Dr. Stephen Porges, Polyvagal Theory revolutionized our understanding of the autonomic nervous system (ANS). It posits that the ANS is not a simple "on/off" switch between stress and rest, but a three-part hierarchy based on evolutionary development.

State Evolutionary Age Biological Function Hormonal Impact
Ventral Vagal Newest (Mammalian) Safety, Social Connection, Rest/Digest Optimal Progesterone, High Oxytocin
Sympathetic Middle (Reptilian) Mobilization, Fight or Flight High Cortisol, High Adrenaline
Dorsal Vagal Oldest (Primitive) Immobilization, Shutdown, Freeze Low Energy, Thyroid Suppression

For the perimenopausal woman, the goal is to cultivate Ventral Vagal Tone. This is the "state of safety" where the body feels secure enough to prioritize reproduction, thyroid function, and metabolic repair. When a client is stuck in Sympathetic (anxiety/high-stress) or Dorsal (depression/burnout), her hormones will reflect that state of emergency.

When explaining this to clients, use the "Traffic Light" analogy: Ventral is Green (Go), Sympathetic is Yellow (Caution/Fast), and Dorsal is Red (Stop/Shutdown). Most women in their 40s are oscillating between Yellow and Red, never reaching Green.

Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS)

The Vagus Nerve (Cranial Nerve X) is the "highway" of the parasympathetic nervous system. It connects the brainstem to the heart, lungs, and digestive tract. We can stimulate this nerve through physical actions that vibrate the larynx or activate the pharyngeal branch.

1. Gargling and Humming

The Vagus nerve passes through the area of the vocal cords. Strong vibrations in this area directly stimulate the nerve. Research indicates that vagus nerve stimulation can increase heart rate variability (HRV) and lower systemic inflammation.

  • Gargling: Have your client gargle water aggressively for 30-60 seconds twice daily. The intensity of the gargle is key‚Äîit should make their eyes water slightly to ensure the muscles are fully engaged.
  • Humming/Chanting: Humming a low-frequency "Voo" sound or chanting "Om" creates a resonance that activates the Ventral Vagal complex.

Ocular Exercises and the Oculocardiac Reflex

The eyes are an embroyological extension of the brain. By moving the eyes in specific patterns, we can trigger the oculocardiac reflex, which slows the heart rate and shifts the ANS into a parasympathetic state.

The Basic Exercise (Stanley Rosenberg Method)

  1. Lie on your back with your hands interlaced behind your head.
  2. Keeping your head still, look as far to the right as you can with your eyes only.
  3. Hold this position until you feel a spontaneous sigh, yawn, or swallow (usually 30-60 seconds). This signal indicates the nervous system has shifted.
  4. Repeat on the left side.
Coach Tip

This is a "ninja" move for clients who have high-stress jobs. They can do a version of this at their desk or in a bathroom stall to reset their nervous system before a big meeting, preventing the "Cortisol Spike" that leads to mid-afternoon sugar cravings.

Breathwork Protocols for Hormonal Health

Breath is the only autonomic function we can consciously control. It is the "remote control" for the nervous system.

The Physiological Sigh

Popularized by neurobiologist Dr. Andrew Huberman, this is the fastest way to offload carbon dioxide and reduce autonomic arousal in under 60 seconds.

  • Mechanism: A double inhale followed by a long, slow exhale. The second short inhale "re-inflates" the alveoli in the lungs, allowing for maximal CO2 exchange.
  • Protocol: Inhale through the nose, then "pop" a tiny second inhale on top, followed by a long exhale through the mouth as if through a straw. Repeat 3 times.

Coherent Breathing

Coherent breathing (5 seconds in, 5 seconds out) creates resonance between the heart and lungs. A 2017 study found that this specific rhythm optimizes Heart Rate Variability and improves mood in patients with depressive disorders.

The Role of Oxytocin: The Tend-and-Befriend Response

While the "Fight or Flight" response is well-documented, researcher Shelley Taylor identified a female-specific stress response called "Tend-and-Befriend." This response is mediated by Oxytocin.

Oxytocin is a powerful antagonist to cortisol. When oxytocin levels rise, the HPA axis is inhibited. For our clients, this means that social connection and safe touch are not just "nice to haves"‚Äîthey are clinical interventions for hormone balance.

  • Safe Touch: Hugging a loved one for 20 seconds or even self-soothing touch (placing a hand over the heart) triggers oxytocin release.
  • Social Connection: Meaningful conversation with a trusted friend reduces the perception of threat, shifting the client out of Sympathetic Dominance.

Encourage perimenopausal clients to join "Women's Circles" or community groups. The isolation of midlife is a significant driver of chronic stress. Community is medicine for the OAT axis.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which state of the Polyvagal hierarchy is associated with "Social Engagement" and optimal hormone production?

The Ventral Vagal state. This is the newest evolutionary state where the body feels safe enough to prioritize long-term health functions like reproduction and digestion.

2. What is the physiological purpose of the "second inhale" in the Physiological Sigh?

The second inhale re-inflates the alveoli (air sacs) in the lungs, which often collapse under stress. This allows for more efficient offloading of CO2 during the long exhale, rapidly calming the nervous system.

3. How does gargling water stimulate the Vagus nerve?

Gargling activates the muscles in the back of the throat (pharynx) and the vocal cords, which are directly innervated by branches of the Vagus nerve. The vibration and muscle contraction provide direct stimulation.

4. Why is oxytocin considered a "hormonal antagonist" to cortisol?

Oxytocin inhibits the activation of the HPA axis. When oxytocin is high (due to connection or touch), it signals to the brain that the environment is safe, thereby reducing the production of CRH, ACTH, and ultimately, cortisol.
