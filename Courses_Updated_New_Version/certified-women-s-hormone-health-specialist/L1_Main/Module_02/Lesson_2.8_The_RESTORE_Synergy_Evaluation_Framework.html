Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're first starting, it's easy to fall into these traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure!

1

The "Expert" Trap

Using too much medical jargon (e.g., "Downregulation of the 5'-deiodinase enzyme"). It intimidates the client and breaks the coaching bond.

2

Ignoring the Adrenals

Trying to fix sex hormones (menopause/PMS) without addressing the stress response first. Synergy always starts with safety/HPA-axis.

3

The "Normal Lab" Dismissal

Agreeing with the client that their doctor is "wrong." Instead, frame it as the doctor looking for disease, while you are looking for vitality.

Success Markers

How do you know if your Synergy Evaluation was successful? Look for these "Gold Standard" indicators:

  • The "Aha!" Moment: The client says, "Nobody has ever explained it to me like that before."
  • The Shift in Blame: The client stops saying "I'm lazy" and starts saying "My system is overwhelmed."
  • Collaborative Goal Setting: The client is eager to try one small change (like eating breakfast) because they understand the why.
  • Trust: The client feels comfortable sharing the "messy" parts of their life (stress, libido, digestion) because they see you as a safe harbor.

Check Your Understanding

1. Diane asks, "Why should I care about my adrenals if it's my hair that's falling out?" What is the best synergy-based response?

Explain that when the body is in a state of chronic stress (adrenals), it prioritizes survival over "luxury" functions like hair growth. The adrenals can also signal the thyroid to slow down, which directly impacts hair follicle health.

2. What is the "Emergency Brake" in our synergy analogy?

The Adrenal/Stress response. When cortisol is chronically high or the HPA-axis is dysregulated, it acts as a brake on the metabolism (thyroid) and reproductive system (sex hormones) to conserve energy.

3. True or False: You should tell a client their doctor is wrong if labs are "normal" but symptoms are present.

False. Instead, explain that medical doctors use "reference ranges" to screen for pathology (disease), while hormone coaches look at "optimal ranges" and symptom clusters to support functional wellness.

4. Why is "warmth" emphasized in the coaching script?

Because the endocrine system (specifically the HPA-axis) is highly sensitive to safety. A warm, supportive environment lowers the client's stress response during the session, making them more receptive to education and change.
