Check Your Understanding

1. When Elena says she is "wired but tired," what is the likely hormonal pattern you should look for in her data?

You should look for elevated evening or nighttime cortisol (the "wired" part) and potentially low morning cortisol or suboptimal Free T3 levels (the "tired" part). This indicates HPA-axis dysfunction.

2. Why is it important to use analogies like the "gas and brake pedals" when explaining labs?

Analogies reduce cognitive load for stressed clients. It helps them understand the functional impact of their hormones without needing a medical degree, which increases their confidence in your plan.

3. What is the "Triple Threat" mentioned in this module?

The "Triple Threat" refers to the interconnectedness of the Adrenals (Cortisol), the Thyroid (Metabolism), and the Ovaries (Sex Hormones). Dysfunction in one usually impacts the other two.
