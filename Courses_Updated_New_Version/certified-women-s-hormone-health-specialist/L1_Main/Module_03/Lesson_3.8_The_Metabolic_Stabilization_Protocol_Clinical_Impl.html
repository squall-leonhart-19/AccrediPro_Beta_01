Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Avoid these four common mistakes that can stall a client's progress and undermine your professional authority:

1. Over-Explaining Science

Don't spend 20 minutes on the Krebs cycle. Give them just enough science to feel "bought in," then move to the practical action step.

2. Being the "Food Police"

If Michelle ate a brownie, don't judge. Ask: "How did your energy feel an hour later?" Let the data be the teacher, not your disapproval.

3. Ignoring Stress

You can't "diet" your way out of high cortisol. If you don't address her 12-hour workdays, the PFF rule will only go so far.

4. Guaranteeing Rapid Loss

Metabolic healing takes time. A 2023 meta-analysis showed that hormonal recalibration often takes 12 to 16 weeks of consistent habits before significant weight shifts occur.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a "just salad" lunch problematic for a woman with Michelle's symptoms?

Without adequate protein and fat, a salad (mostly carbohydrates/fiber) can still cause a glucose spike and subsequent crash, leading to afternoon fatigue and sugar cravings. We need the "Metabolic Anchor" of protein and fat to slow digestion.

2. What is the "PFF" rule used for in coaching?

PFF stands for Protein, Fat, and Fiber. It is a simple tool for clients to ensure every meal stabilizes blood sugar and supports insulin sensitivity.

3. How should you respond when a client says she's "failing" because she hasn't lost weight in week one?

Validate her feelings, then pivot to "Non-Scale Victories" (NSVs). Ask about her energy, sleep, and "hangry" episodes. Remind her that metabolic healing (internal) precedes weight loss (external).

4. What is the physiological danger of "chronic calorie cutting" in perimenopause?

It signals "famine" to the body, which can further elevate cortisol, suppress thyroid function (T4 to T3 conversion), and actually increase insulin resistance as the body tries to preserve fat stores.
