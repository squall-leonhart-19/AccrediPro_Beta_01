Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

  • The "Information Dump": Trying to explain the entire HPA-axis in the first 10 minutes. Keep it simple: focus on how they feel.
  • Fixing Instead of Facilitating: Don't jump to a supplement list immediately. Validate the struggle first.
  • Ignoring the "Why": If you don't connect the 3:00 AM waking to her blood sugar/cortisol, she won't be motivated to change her bedtime snack.
  • Under-selling the Result: Don't be afraid to say, "We can absolutely improve this." Confidence builds compliance.

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena mentions her doctor said her labs were "normal." How should a Specialist respond?

Acknowledge that "normal" is a statistical average, but "optimal" is where she will feel her best. Explain that symptoms are valid data points even when bloodwork is within range.

2. What is the primary reason for Elena's 3:00 AM waking in this scenario?

Likely a combination of a cortisol spike due to low blood sugar (no protein at dinner) and declining progesterone, which is a natural sedative for the brain.
