Common Coaching Mistakes

The Mistake Why It Fails The Resilience Fix
Over-Explaining Science Clients feel overwhelmed and "stupid." Use metaphors (Dashboard, Seasons, Battery).
The "Fixer" Impulse You give 10 supplements in the first 10 minutes. Focus on 1 lifestyle shift for cycle resilience first.
Dismissing the Emotional Client feels you are ignoring her lived experience. Validate the frustration before explaining the biology.
Ignoring the "Why" Client doesn't see the benefit of tracking. Connect resilience to her specific goals (e.g., career clarity).
Professionalism Check

Always remember: You are a Coach, not a Doctor. Your job is to empower her lifestyle and mindset. If symptoms are acute, your resilience plan should include a referral to a hormone-literate MD.

Check Your Understanding

1. When Sandra says she feels "broken," what is the primary goal of your response?

The goal is to provide immediate validation and a reframe. You want to shift her perspective from "broken machine" to "changing system" to reduce the stress response associated with her symptoms.

2. Why is the "negotiate vs. dictate" metaphor effective for perimenopausal clients?

It honors their ambition and agency while acknowledging the biological reality that the body no longer responds to "forcing" things. It sets the stage for lifestyle adjustments like rest and nutrition.

3. According to the "Common Mistakes" table, what happens when you over-explain science?

Clients often feel overwhelmed or "stupid," which increases their stress and decreases their confidence in following your plan. Clarity and simplicity are the hallmarks of an expert coach.

4. What is a "Success Marker" in a resilience coaching session?

A success marker is when the client shifts from asking "How do I stop this?" to "What is my body telling me right now?" This indicates she has moved into an empowered, resilient mindset.

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Validation First: Never move to solutions until the client feels her frustration has been fully witnessed.
  • Narrative Power: Your most valuable tool isn't a supplement; it's the new story you give the client about her body.
  • Simplicity Wins: Use metaphors like "dashboards" and "seasons" to make complex hormonal shifts actionable.
  • Agency is the Goal: A resilient client believes she has the tools to navigate her shifts, even when they are uncomfortable.
  • Practice Makes Professional: Speaking these scripts out loud builds the "muscle memory" needed for high-stakes client calls.
