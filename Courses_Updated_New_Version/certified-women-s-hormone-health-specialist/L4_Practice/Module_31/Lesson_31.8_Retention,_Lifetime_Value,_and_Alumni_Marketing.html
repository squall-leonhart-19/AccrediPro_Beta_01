2. The 30-Minute Discovery Script

Use this structure to maintain control of the call while making the client feel completely heard.

Phase 1: Rapport & Setting the Stage (0-5 mins)

YOU: "Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal today is to hear about what you're going through, see if my framework is the right fit to help you reach your goals, and if so, I'll explain how we can work together. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: Discovery & The "Deep Why" (5-15 mins)

YOU: "You mentioned on your form that you're feeling 'wired but tired.' Tell me, on a Tuesday afternoon at 3:00 PM, how does that actually show up for you? How is that affecting your life?"

YOU: "And if we don't get this sorted out, where do you see your health‚Äîand your career‚Äîa year from now?"

Phase 3: Building the Bridge (15-22 mins)

YOU: "Linda, what I‚Äôm hearing is that your body is stuck in a stress-response loop that‚Äôs tanking your progesterone and spiking your cortisol. The reason Keto and HIIT didn't work is that they actually added more stress to an already taxed system. In my Hormone Resilience Program, we do the opposite. We use the RESTORE framework to calm the nervous system first so your metabolism can actually function again."

Sarah's Tip

Wait for the "breath of relief." When you explain why her previous attempts failed, her shoulders will drop. That is the moment she trusts you as the expert.

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

Never "drop" the price and then keep talking. State it, and then be silent.

The Script: "Based on everything we've discussed, the best path forward is my 90-day Hormone Harmony Intensive. This includes your functional evaluation, 6 bi-weekly sessions, and daily messaging support. The investment for the full 90 days is $1,800, or three monthly payments of $650. Which of those options works best for you?"

(Then... STOP TALKING. Wait for her to speak first.)

Sarah's Tip

The first person to speak after the price is mentioned usually loses their position. If you keep talking, you sound defensive. Silence demonstrates confidence in your value.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Discovery" phase (Phase 2) of the call?

The goal is to find the "Deep Why"‚Äîthe emotional impact of the symptoms. You want to understand not just that she has brain fog, but how that brain fog is threatening her career or relationships.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge the decision, then ask a clarifying question: "I understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either because they aren't sure the program will work, or the investment is a stretch. Which one is it for you?" This allows you to address the real concern.

3. True or False: You should explain all 7 steps of the RESTORE framework in detail during the first 10 minutes of the call.

False. The first 15 minutes should be focused entirely on her. If you spend too much time on clinical details early on, you lose the emotional connection. Save the framework for the "Bridge" phase (Phase 3).

4. Why is silence important after stating your price?

Silence signals authority and gives the prospect space to process the information. Filling the silence often comes across as insecurity or "selling," which can trigger skepticism in the prospect.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Listen 70%, Talk 30%: A successful discovery call is one where the client feels more understood than they ever have by a doctor.
  • Focus on the Gap: Clearly define the distance between where she is now (fatigued/frustrated) and where she wants to be (vibrant/focused).
  • Own Your Value: You are providing a specialized solution to a complex problem. Your pricing should reflect the years of study and the transformation you provide.
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Record your first few calls (with permission) and listen back to identify where you might be rushing or sounding hesitant.
