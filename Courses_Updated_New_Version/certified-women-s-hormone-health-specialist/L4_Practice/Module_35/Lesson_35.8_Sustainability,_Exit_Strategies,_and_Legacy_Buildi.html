Meet Your Prospect: The High-Ticket Lead

Before we pick up the phone, we need to understand who we are talking to. In a scaling model, your leads are often women who have "tried everything" and are ready to invest in a permanent solution.

Her Situation
Experiencing severe perimenopausal brain fog, night sweats, and "stubborn" weight gain (15 lbs in 6 months). She is terrified her performance at work is slipping.
The "Gap"
Her doctor told her she's "just aging" and offered antidepressants. She wants a root-cause approach but doesn't have time to research it herself.

Results-oriented. She values efficiency over "discounts." She needs to know you have a proven system.

Coach Tip: The Silent Objection

Clients like Linda aren't usually worried about the money itself; they are worried about wasting the money. Your job is to prove that your process is the "last stop" on their journey.

The Psychology of the Discovery Call

A discovery call is not a "free coaching session." It is a diagnostic interview. If you spend the whole time giving advice, the prospect feels "full" and doesn't feel the need to hire you. Instead, you must focus on the Gap‚Äîthe distance between where she is now and where she wants to be.

Case Study: Jennifer's Shift to Premium

Practitioner: Jennifer (52), former high school teacher turned Hormone Specialist.
The Problem: Jennifer was charging $150 per session. She was exhausted, seeing 15 clients a week, and barely making $6,000/month after expenses.
The Intervention: She switched to a 4-month "Hormone Harmony" package for $2,400. She learned to lead discovery calls by asking "What is the cost of NOT fixing this?"
The Outcome: Jennifer now sees 6 clients at a time, earns $14,400 per package cycle, and works 10 hours a week. Her clients get better results because they are fully committed.

Phase-by-Phase Call Script

Use the following structure for your 30-minute call. Practice these lines out loud to build muscle memory.

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (0-5 min)
YOU: "Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our chat. My goal today is to hear about your health goals, share how I work, and see if we're a perfect fit. If we are, I‚Äôll show you what that looks like. If not, I‚Äôll point you to the best next resource. Sound good?"
Phase 2: Deep Discovery (5-15 min)
YOU: "You mentioned brain fog is impacting your work. Can you give me a specific example of how that showed up this week?"
YOU: "And if we don't get this under control, where do you see your health‚Äîand your career‚Äîsix months from now?"
Phase 3: The Prescription (15-25 min)
YOU: "Based on what you've told me, you don't need another 'diet.' You need to stabilize your insulin response and support your adrenal reserves. My 4-month Hormone Reset is designed specifically for women in your position. We do [Step A], then [Step B]..."
Phase 4: The Close (25-30 min)
YOU: "The investment for the full 4-month partnership is $2,800. We can do that in one payment, or a monthly plan. Does that feel like the support you've been looking for?"
Coach Tip: Silence is Golden

After you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak usually loses their confidence. Let her process the number. If she stays silent for 10 seconds, that's okay!

Mastering the Art of Objection Handling

An objection is rarely a "no." It is usually a request for more information or a manifestation of fear. We use the "Acknowledge, Explore, Resolve" framework.

The Objection The Reframing Response
"I need to talk to my husband." "I completely understand. When you talk to him, what do you think his biggest concern will be? Is it the cost, or the time commitment?"
"It's just so much money right now." "I hear you. If we look at what you‚Äôre currently spending on supplements and doctors that aren't working, how does this compare over the next 4 months?"
"Can I just buy one session first?" "I wish I could say one session would fix this, but your hormones didn't get out of balance in an hour. To get you the results you want, we need the full container."

Presenting Your Premium Investment

When you scale, you must stop thinking about "hourly rates." A $2,500 package might involve 10 hours of direct work, but it provides $10,000+ of value in avoided medical bills, increased productivity, and quality of life.

The "Value Stack" Method

When presenting your price, always "stack" what is included so the price feels small in comparison:

  • Comprehensive Lab Review: (Value: $500)
  • 8 Bi-Weekly Coaching Sessions: (Value: $1,600)
  • Customized Nutritional & Supplement Protocols: (Value: $400)
  • Private Messaging Support: (Value: $1,000)
  • Total Value: $3,500 | Your Investment: $2,500
Coach Tip: The "Nurse/Teacher" Trap

Many of you come from service backgrounds where you were paid $30-$50/hour. If you try to run your practice that way, you will burn out. High-ticket isn't about greed; it's about sustainability.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it detrimental to give health advice during a discovery call?

Giving advice makes the prospect feel "full" or satisfied temporarily, which reduces their perceived need for a long-term package. It also shifts the focus from "transformation" to "information," which is harder to sell at a premium price.

2. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (Deep Discovery) in the script?

The purpose is to identify the "Gap"‚Äîthe emotional and physical cost of the client staying exactly where they are. By highlighting the pain of inaction, the investment in your program becomes a logical solution.

3. How should you respond to the "I need to talk to my husband" objection?

Acknowledge the need for partnership, but then ask an exploratory question to find the real concern (e.g., "What do you think his biggest hesitation will be?"). This helps you address the underlying fear rather than just the "permission" issue.

4. What is the "Value Stack" and why is it used?

The Value Stack is a list of every component of your program and its individual market value. It is used to show that the total value of the transformation is significantly higher than the actual price being charged, making the investment feel like a "win" for the client.

Key Takeaways

  • Stop Selling Hours: High-ticket scaling requires selling a specific, time-bound transformation rather than individual sessions.
  • Master the Gap: Successful conversion happens when the client realizes the cost of their health problem is higher than the cost of your program.
  • Practice the Price: Speak your investment amount out loud until it feels like a neutral fact, not a scary request.
  • Scale with Systems: As your price increases, your number of clients can decrease, allowing you to provide higher quality care and avoid burnout.
  • Empathy Over Sales: Objection handling is an act of service; you are helping the client overcome the fears that are keeping them stuck in poor health.
