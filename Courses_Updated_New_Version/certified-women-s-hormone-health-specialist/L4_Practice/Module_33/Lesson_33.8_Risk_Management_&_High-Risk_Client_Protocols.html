The Compliance-First Strategy

The biggest fear for new practitioners is the "Medical Advice" line. To stay compliant while selling, you must master educational language.

Avoid (Non-Compliant) Use (Compliant & Empowering)
"I will treat your PCOS." "We will support the underlying metabolic foundations to help manage PCOS symptoms."
"You should take 200mg of Progesterone." "Based on these markers, we can discuss how certain nutrients and herbs support natural progesterone production."
"I'll diagnose your thyroid issue." "We‚Äôll look at your functional labs to identify where your system needs the most foundational support."

Handling Objections with Compliance

Objections are rarely about the money; they are usually about certainty. Susan needs to know you can actually help her without overstepping your legal bounds.

Objection 1: "I need to ask my doctor if this is okay."
Your Response: "I absolutely encourage that! In fact, I provide a 'Provider Collaboration' sheet that you can take to your next appointment. My work supports the lifestyle changes that make your doctor's treatments even more effective."

Objection 2: "That‚Äôs more expensive than the health coach I saw last year."

However, what I can guarantee is that we will address the hormonal imbalances that often make weight loss impossible, creating the right environment for your body to release that weight."

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks, "Will this program cure my Hashimoto's?" What is the most compliant response?

"We don't use the word 'cure.' Instead, say: 'My program focuses on supporting your immune system and gut health to help reduce the inflammatory load associated with Hashimoto's.'"

2. When should you state your pricing during the discovery call?

Only after you have clearly established the "Gap" between where the client is and where they want to be, and after you've explained how your specific methodology (RESTORE) solves their problem.

3. What is the "Legal Handshake"?

It is the verbal disclaimer at the beginning of the call where you clarify your role as a health specialist/coach and state that you are not providing medical advice or diagnosis.

4. If a client says "I need to talk to my husband," how do you handle it?

Validate them: "I completely understand, I'm the same way with my spouse! Can I ask, what parts of the program do you think he'll be most excited about, and what concerns do you think he might have that I can help you clarify?"
Coach Tip: You are the Prize

Remember, Susan is lucky to have found you. You have the specialized knowledge she‚Äôs been searching for. When you speak from a place of service rather than "selling," the legal and financial pieces fall into place naturally.

Key Takeaways

  • Compliance is Confidence: Using educational language protects you legally and positions you as an expert educator.
  • Structure the Call: Follow the 4-phase structure to ensure the client feels heard before you ever mention a price.
  • Own Your Value: Pricing should reflect the life-changing results you provide, not the hours you spend.
  • Objections are Questions: View objections as the client asking for more certainty that your program will work for them.
