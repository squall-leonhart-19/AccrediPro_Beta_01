Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Pain Discovery" phase of the discovery call?

To help the prospect realize the "cost of inaction"â€”how much their current health struggles are affecting their life, career, and relationshipsâ€”so they understand that the investment in your program is smaller than the cost of staying sick.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

With empathy and a follow-up question. "I completely understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either the money, the time, or they aren't sure I'm the right fit. Which one is it for you so I can clarify?"

3. Why is the "Stop Talking" rule important after stating your price?

It demonstrates confidence and authority. If you keep talking, you often start "backpedaling" or offering discounts before the prospect even asks, which lowers your perceived value.

4. Based on the income scenarios, how many clients per month do you need to reach a $10,000/month revenue goal at a $2,000 package price?

You only need 5 new clients per month. This allows for deep, high-touch work without the burnout of traditional 1-on-1 hourly coaching.

Key Takeaways
