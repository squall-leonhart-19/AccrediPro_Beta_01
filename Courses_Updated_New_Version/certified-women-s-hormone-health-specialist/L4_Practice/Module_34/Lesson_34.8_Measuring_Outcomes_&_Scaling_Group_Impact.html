Handling Group Objections

Objections are not "nos"â€”they are requests for more information. In a 2023 study of health coaching outcomes, 82% of clients who voiced objections but had them addressed felt more committed to the program than those who had none.

Objection The Fear Behind It Your Professional Response
"I'm too busy for a weekly call." Lack of priority/Fear of falling behind. "I totally get it. All calls are recorded and uploaded to your portal within 2 hours. You can even submit questions in advance!"
"I prefer 1:1 attention." Fear that their case is "too complex." "I love 1:1 work, but I've found that the 'community effect' actually leads to better compliance and faster results for this specific protocol."
"I need to talk to my husband." Financial hesitation or lack of agency. "I support that. What information can I give you to help explain the value of resolving these symptoms to him?"
Coach Sarah's Tip

If someone says "I need to think about it," don't just say "Okay." Ask: "Of course. Just so I can help you process, is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or are you just not sure if this is the right fit for your symptoms?"

Call-to-Action Practice

Closing the call is about leadership. You are the expert; you are guiding them to the finish line. Use these three "Direct Close" options:

  • The "Assumptive" Close: "It sounds like this is exactly what you need. Shall we get you registered so you can access the pre-work today?"
  • The "Urgency" Close: "I only have 2 spots left for this cohort because I keep the groups small. Would you like to claim one of those now?"
  • The "Inquiry" Close: "On a scale of 1 to 10, how ready are you to start feeling better? (If they say 8+) Great, let's get you signed up."
Coach Sarah's Tip

Silence is your friend. After you state the price and ask for the sale, stop talking. Let the prospect speak first. The first person to speak usually "loses" the negotiationâ€”or in this case, interrupts the prospect's decision-making process.

It is a closing technique where you speak as if the prospect has already decided to join, focusing on the next steps (e.g., "Shall we get you registered?") rather than asking "Do you want to join?"
