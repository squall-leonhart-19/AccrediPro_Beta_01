Workshop Architecture: The 90-Minute Blueprint

A high-impact workshop isn't just a lecture; it's a transformation. To move a client from "confused and symptomatic" to "empowered and curious," you must follow a specific psychological arc. For a standard 90-minute session, use the following timing:

Phase Timing Core Objective
The Hook & Validation 0-15 min Share the "Why" and validate their symptoms using the Root Cause paradigm (R).
The Interactive Audit 15-45 min The "Hormone Audit" using Endocrine Synergy (E). Attendees self-assess their OAT axis.
The Quick-Win Teaching 45-70 min The "Metabolic Quick-Start" (S). Practical steps to stabilize blood sugar today.
The Path Forward 70-90 min Q&A and the invitation to the next level of support (Conversion).
Coach Tip: The "Aha!" Moment

The goal of the first 30 minutes is not to teach everything you know. It is to give the client an "Aha!" moment where they finally understand why they feel the way they do. Once they have clarity, they will trust your solution.

Integrating the Endocrine Synergy Evaluation (E)

In a 1:1 setting, you spend hours on the Endocrine Synergy Evaluation. In a workshop, we condense this into a "Hormone Audit." This is an interactive assessment where attendees use a scorecard to track their symptoms across the Ovarian, Adrenal, and Thyroid (OAT) axis.

The Hormone Scorecard Method

Provide a physical handout with symptom clusters. Ask attendees to rate themselves 1-5 on specific markers:

  • Adrenal Markers: Afternoon energy crashes, salt cravings, "tired but wired" at night.
  • Thyroid Markers: Cold hands/feet, thinning outer eyebrows, stubborn weight gain.
  • Ovarian Markers: Night sweats, cycle irregularities, breast tenderness.

By the end of this 20-minute section, the attendee shouldn't just know they have "hormone issues"‚Äîthey should know they have Adrenal Dominance or Thyroid Sluggishness. This specificity creates immediate authority for you as the practitioner.

The 'Metabolic Quick-Start': Teaching (S)

Once the attendee understands their "Hormone Type" (E), you must provide a solution. We focus on Stabilizing Metabolic Foundations (S) because it provides the fastest symptomatic relief. A 2022 study published in the Journal of Endocrine Society noted that even minor glycemic variability can trigger cortisol spikes that disrupt progesterone production within hours.

The "PFF" Teaching Framework

Teach the "Protein, Fat, Fiber" (PFF) rule for every meal. This is a simplified version of the R.E.S.T.O.R.E. metabolic protocols.

Workshop Activity: Have attendees "audit" their breakfast from that morning. Ask: "Where was your fiber? Where was your protein?" This immediate application of the (S) pillar builds confidence that your methods are practical and achievable.

Coach Tip: Sensory Teaching

If hosting an in-person workshop, bring "Metabolic Quick-Start" samples. A small cup of a blood-sugar-stabilizing smoothie or a handful of specific nuts/seeds makes the teaching "real" and memorable.

Conversion Strategy: The Gateway to High-Tier Programs

A common mistake is treating the workshop as a standalone product. Instead, view it as Module 0 of your larger program. You are not "selling" at the end; you are offering the "logical next step" for those who want to go deeper.

The "Seed and Feed" Method

  • Seeding: Throughout the workshop, mention your 1:1 or group program. "When I work with my private clients in the R.E.S.T.O.R.E. Intensive, we take this thyroid audit and look at the specific labs to match..."
  • Feeding: At the end, offer a "Workshop Only" incentive. This could be a $200 discount or a bonus 1:1 "Strategy Call" if they join your program within 48 hours.
Coach Tip: The Power of "Limited"

Always limit the number of conversion spots. "I only have 4 spots opening up for my 1:1 intensive this month." This creates authentic urgency for the women who are ready to change.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the 'Metabolic Quick-Start' (S) the preferred teaching topic for a short workshop?

It provides the fastest symptomatic relief through blood sugar stabilization, proving the efficacy of your methods quickly and building trust for longer-term programs.

2. What is the primary purpose of the 'Hormone Audit' during the workshop?

To move the attendee from general confusion to a specific understanding of their hormonal imbalances (OAT axis), establishing the practitioner's authority and the client's need for a targeted solution.

3. According to the 90-minute blueprint, when should the 'Conversion' phase begin?

In the final 20 minutes (minutes 70-90), following the teaching phase and preceding or during the final Q&A.

4. What is the 'Seed and Feed' method?

'Seeding' involves mentioning your long-term programs throughout the teaching to build familiarity, while 'Feeding' is providing a specific, time-limited incentive at the end to encourage immediate enrollment.
