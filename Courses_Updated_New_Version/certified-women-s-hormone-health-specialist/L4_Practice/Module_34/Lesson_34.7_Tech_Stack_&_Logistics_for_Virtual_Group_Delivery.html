Platform Ecosystems: LMS vs. Community

Where you host your content determines the "vibe" and functionality of your program. Broadly, you have two choices: a Learning Management System (LMS) or a Community-First Platform.

Platform Type Examples Best For... Pros/Cons
LMS (Course-First) Kajabi, Teachable, Thinkific Structured 12-week curricula with heavy video content. + Professional look, - Harder to foster peer-to-peer chat.
Community-First Circle, Mighty Networks Interactive workshops where peer support is the "secret sauce." + High engagement, - Content can sometimes feel less "linear."
Practice Management Practice Better, Healthie Clinical groups requiring intense HIPAA security and food journaling. + Secure, - Community features are often less "sleek."
Coach Tip

Don't fall into "shiny object syndrome." If you are just starting, Practice Better is often the best choice because it handles your clinical intake, waivers, and group chat all in one HIPAA-compliant place. You can add a "prettier" platform like Kajabi once you've hit your first $10k month.

Automating the Onboarding Sequence

The "First 48 Hours" after a client pays for your group program are critical for reducing buyer's remorse and ensuring clinical safety. Your tech stack must handle this automatically.

The Ideal Automated Flow:

  • Step 1: Payment & Trigger. Client pays via Stripe/PayPal. This triggers an automated email.
  • Step 2: The Welcome Kit. A PDF or video that explains "How to get the most out of this program" and the schedule.
  • Step 3: Clinical Intake & Waiver. Crucial: They must sign a "Group Informed Consent" stating they understand this is education, not medical advice, and that their data will be visible to others in the group.
  • Step 4: Platform Invitation. Automated invite to your Circle or Kajabi portal.

Privacy & Compliance in Digital Spaces

When women share details about their cycles, libido, or thyroid symptoms in a group chat, you are handling Protected Health Information (PHI). Even if you are "coaching," the ethical standard remains high.

To remain compliant (HIPAA in the US, GDPR in Europe):

  1. Use Group Disclaimers: Ensure every group session starts with a slide stating the educational nature of the call.
  2. Moderate Discussion: If a client posts lab results with their full name and SSN visible, you must delete it and message them privately about privacy.
  3. Recording Storage: Never host recordings on "Public" YouTube. Use Vimeo with domain-level privacy or the native hosting in your LMS.
Coach Tip

In your Group Agreement, include a "Vegas Rule" clause: What is said in the hormone group, stays in the hormone group. This builds the psychological safety necessary for women to discuss sensitive issues like vaginal dryness or PMDD.

Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Efficiency comes from having a "Run of Show." You should not be "winging it" every week. Create a simple SOP for every live session:

  • T-Minus 30 Mins: Test Zoom link, lighting, and microphone. Open your slide deck.
  • During Session: Start recording immediately. Have a "Chat Monitor" (even if it's just a trusted student or VA) to flag urgent questions.
  • Post-Session (within 4 hours): Upload the recording, add the "Time Stamps" (so women can find specific topics), and link any mentioned resources.
Coach Tip

Use an AI transcription tool like Otter.ai or Fathom. This allows you to provide a searchable transcript of every workshop, which is a massive value-add for busy moms who can't watch a full 60-minute video but want to find the section on "magnesium for sleep."

Check Your Understanding

1. Which platform type is best suited for a workshop where peer-to-peer interaction and community support are the primary goals?

Community-First Platforms (like Circle or Mighty Networks) are best for high-engagement workshops. LMS platforms like Kajabi are better for structured, linear course content.

2. What is the most critical document a client must sign during the automated onboarding process for a group program?

The Group Informed Consent/Waiver. This document must clarify that the program is educational, not medical advice, and that privacy is limited in a group setting.

3. How can you provide "searchability" for your group recordings to increase value for busy clients?

By using AI transcription tools (like Otter.ai or Fathom) and providing Time Stamps for key topics within the video description.

4. True or False: It is acceptable to host sensitive hormone health group recordings on a "Public" YouTube channel as long as you don't share the link.

False. Even "Unlisted" YouTube videos are not considered secure enough for sensitive health data. You should use password-protected or domain-restricted hosting like Vimeo or your LMS's internal hosting.

Key Takeaways
