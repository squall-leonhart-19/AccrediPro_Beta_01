Section 2: The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A successful discovery call isn't an interrogation; it's a guided conversation where Jennifer realizes that you are the bridge to her desired future.

Phase 1: The Opener (5 Minutes)

Goal: Establish rapport and set the agenda.

YOU: "Hi Jennifer! It's so good to finally connect. I've been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our time. My goal today is to hear about what you've been going through, share how my approach works, and by the end, we'll both know if we're a great fit to work together. How does that sound?"

Phase 2: Discovery & Pain Points (12 Minutes)

Goal: Let her feel heard and identify the root cause.

YOU: "You mentioned in your intake form that you're feeling 'wired but tired.' Can you tell me what a typical Tuesday feels like for you, from the moment you wake up?"

JENNIFER: (Describes the 3 AM wake-up, the coffee reliance, the afternoon crash, and the frustration with her doctor.)

YOU: "I hear how frustrating that is, Jennifer. Especially when you're told everything is 'normal' but you don't feel normal. Aside from the energy, how is this affecting your life outside of work?"

Phase 3: The Bridge (8 Minutes)

Goal: Show her why her previous attempts failed and why your method works.

YOU: "Jennifer, the reason those supplements and the 'normal' labs haven't helped is that they aren't looking at the synergy between your cortisol and your progesterone. At 49, your body is shifting, and we need to stabilize your metabolic foundation before we can fix the sleep. My 12-week Hormone Resilience Program is designed specifically to do exactly that."

Section 6: Closing Practice

The "Close" is simply an invitation to the next step. Practice these lines out loud until they feel natural.

Practice These Lines
  • "Jennifer, based on everything you've shared, I am 100% confident I can help you get your energy back. Would you like to hear how we get started?"
  • "We can start your onboarding as early as Monday. Which payment option works best for you, the pay-in-full or the monthly plan?"
  • "I only take on 4 new clients a month to ensure everyone gets my full attention. I have one spot left for Octoberâ€”would you like to claim it?"
Sarah's Tip

Silence is your friend. After you ask for the sale, wait. Give her space to say yes. The first person to speak usually loses the lead.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (Discovery) in the call?

The goal is to let the client feel heard and to identify the emotional and physical "pain points" that make the problem urgent for them to solve.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge the importance of the decision, then gently ask, "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the time, the investment, or the approach?" This helps you address the real concern.

3. Why is package pricing generally better than hourly pricing for hormone health?

Hormone health requires time and consistency. A package ensures the client is committed to the full 12-week process needed to see results, and it positions you as a solution-provider rather than a commodity.

4. What is the "Bridge" in a sales conversation?

The Bridge is the section of the call where you connect the client's specific problems to your unique methodology, showing them exactly how your program leads to their desired outcome.

Key Takeaways

  • Enrollment is Service: If you don't close the sale, the woman remains in pain. Approach sales as the first step of her healing.
  • Structure Equals Confidence: Following a 4-phase script prevents rambling and keeps you in the "expert" seat.
  • Value Over Price: Always anchor your price to the cost of the problem (lost productivity, medical bills, poor quality of life).
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Role-play your discovery calls with a peer or in the mirror until the transitions feel seamless.
