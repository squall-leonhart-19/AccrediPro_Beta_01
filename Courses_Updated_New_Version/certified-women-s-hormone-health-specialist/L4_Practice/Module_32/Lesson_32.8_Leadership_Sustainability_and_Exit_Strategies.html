A successful call is 80% listening and 20% speaking. Your goal is to guide Linda to the realization that her current path isn't working and that you have the solution.

Phase 1: Rapport & The "Why Now" (5 Minutes)

3. Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a sign of fear. As a specialist, your job is to coach them through that fear.

The Objection The Reframing Response
"It's too expensive." "I hear you. It is an investment. But let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not fixing this? If you can't finish that consulting project, what does that cost you financially and professionally?"
"I need to ask my husband." "I completely respect that. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Is it the cost, or is it that he's seen you try things before that didn't work?"
"I've tried everything." "I understand the frustration. Most of my clients felt the same way. The difference here is we are using a Root Cause Discovery method, not just masking symptoms with more pills."
Sarah's Tip

Silence is your best friend after you state your price or answer an objection. Don't "fill the air" with nervous chatter. Let them process.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

The goal is to build rapport and identify the "Why Now"‚Äîthe specific emotional or practical trigger that motivated the prospect to seek help today.

2. If a client says "I need to think about it," what is the most effective follow-up question?

"I completely understand. Usually, when someone needs to think about it, it's either because of the time commitment, the financial investment, or they aren't sure this will work for them. Which one of those is it for you?" (This forces clarity).

3. Why is package pricing superior to hourly billing for hormone specialists?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome (e.g., "Hormone Harmony") rather than the time spent. It also ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see physiological changes (usually 12 weeks).

4. How does "Root Cause Discovery" help in handling the "I've tried everything" objection?

It differentiates your service by explaining that previous failures were likely due to symptom-masking, whereas your method looks at the underlying endocrine synergy and metabolic foundations.
Sarah's Tip

Practice your pricing out loud in the mirror. Say, "The investment is $1,800" fifty times until it feels as natural as saying your own name. Confidence is contagious!

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Listen More, Talk Less: Use the 80/20 rule to ensure the client feels heard and understood.
  • Focus on the "Gap": Highlight the difference between where they are now and where they want to be in 3-6 months.
  • Own Your Value: You are a specialist solving a high-value problem; your pricing should reflect that expertise.
  • Systematize the Close: Use a proven script to reduce anxiety and increase your enrollment conversion rate.
