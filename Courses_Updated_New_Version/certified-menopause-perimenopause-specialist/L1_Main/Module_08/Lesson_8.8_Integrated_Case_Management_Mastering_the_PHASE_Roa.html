Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're starting out, your imposter syndrome might lead you into these traps. Awareness is the first step to avoidance.

1. Over-Explaining

Don't give a 20-minute lecture on the Krebs cycle. Keep the science relevant to her specific symptom.

2. The "Fix-It" Trap

Avoid saying "I will fix your anxiety." Instead, use "We will work together to support your nervous system."

3. Ignoring the "E" (Emotions)

If you only focus on the Physiology and Hormones, you miss the emotional weight of Elena's career transition.

4. Rushing the Results

Remind the client that hormonal recalibration takes 3-6 months, not 3-6 days. Manage expectations early.

Sarah's Insight

If you don't know an answer, say: "That's a great question. Let me consult our specialist database and get back to you with the most current research." This builds more trust than faking an answer ever will.

Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

A successful PHASE session isn't defined by the client "feeling better" immediately. Look for these markers:

  • The "Sigh of Relief": The client visibly relaxes when they realize their symptoms have a biological name and cause.
  • The "Aha!" Moment: The client connects two pillars (e.g., "Oh, so my 3 PM coffee is why I'm waking up at 3 AM?").
  • Actionable Clarity: The client can name ONE specific change they will make in the next 48 hours.
  • Shift in Narrative: The client stops saying "My body is failing" and starts saying "My body is changing."

Practice Lab: Check Your Understanding

1. Elena describes a "buzzing" anxiety. Based on our Advanced PHASE training, what is the likely hormonal driver?

The "buzzing" anxiety in late perimenopause is often driven by a crash in Progesterone. Progesterone metabolites (like allopregnanolone) act on GABA receptors in the brain, providing a natural calming effect. When progesterone drops, the "GABA brakes" are removed from the nervous system.

2. Why is it critical to address Elena's 4 cups of coffee before adjusting her exercise?

Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours. Excessive caffeine spikes cortisol and can exacerbate hot flashes and anxiety. If her nervous system is already "red-lined" by caffeine, adding the stress of a new exercise routine may lead to further burnout and "menopause belly" fat storage.

3. Which PHASE pillar addresses Elena's feeling of "losing her edge" in her career?

This primarily falls under Emotions/Environment, though it is influenced by the Physiology of brain fog. Addressing the emotional transition of aging and career shifts is vital for the "High-Achiever" client.

4. What is the most professional way to handle a question about HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy) if you are not a medical provider?

"I can certainly share the current research and guidelines on HRT so you can have an informed conversation with your doctor. As a coach, I don't prescribe, but I can help you track your symptoms so your physician has the best data to work with."
