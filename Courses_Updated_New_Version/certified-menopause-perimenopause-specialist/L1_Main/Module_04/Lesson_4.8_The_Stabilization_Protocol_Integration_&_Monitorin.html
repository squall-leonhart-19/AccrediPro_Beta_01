5 Common Mistakes New Coaches Make

  • Mistake 1: Over-explaining the Science. Don't give a 20-minute lecture on the Krebs cycle. Keep it focused on the client's symptoms (e.g., "This is why you're tired").
  • Mistake 2: Changing Too Much at Once. If you give Elena 10 new habits, she will fail. Pick 2 (e.g., Protein at breakfast and no caffeine after 11 AM).
  • Mistake 3: Fearing Resistance. When a client says "I can't give up my HIIT," don't back down. Explain the cortisol cost of that workout.
  • Mistake 4: Ignoring the "Why." Always connect the habit back to their specific goal (Sleep, Energy, Mood).
  • Mistake 5: Sounding Unsure. Use "We are going to..." instead of "Maybe we could try..."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why does skipping meals often lead to 3:00 AM insomnia in perimenopausal women?

Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) triggers a stress response. The adrenals release cortisol and adrenaline to stimulate glucose production, which simultaneously alerts the brain and causes a mid-night wakeup.

2. What is the "Specialist framing" for why HIIT might be counterproductive in the Stabilize phase?

In a body that is already "unstable" (low energy, high stress), high-intensity exercise adds a massive cortisol load that the body cannot recover from, leading to further fat storage and exhaustion.

3. Which macronutrient is most critical for stabilizing blood sugar during the transition?

Protein. Consuming high-quality protein (25-30g) at every meal, especially breakfast, slows glucose absorption and provides the amino acids necessary for neurotransmitter and hormone production.

4. How many new habits should a coach typically introduce in the first stabilization session?

1 to 2. Overwhelming the client with too many changes increases their stress response, which is exactly what the Stabilize phase is designed to reduce.
