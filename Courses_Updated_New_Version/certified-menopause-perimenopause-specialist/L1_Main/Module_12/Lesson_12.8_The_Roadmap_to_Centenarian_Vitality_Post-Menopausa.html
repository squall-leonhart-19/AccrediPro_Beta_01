Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to acknowledge Elena's hard work before suggesting a pivot to "doing less"?

It validates her effort and prevents her from feeling like she has "failed." This maintains the coaching alliance and makes her more open to a counter-intuitive strategy like reducing exercise intensity.

2. What is the physiological rationale for trading long walks for restorative breathwork in a high-stress client?

Chronic high stress elevates cortisol. In perimenopause, high cortisol can lead to insulin resistance and abdominal fat storage. Restorative practices activate the parasympathetic nervous system, signaling "safety" to the body and allowing for better metabolic function.

3. If Elena asks, "Will I gain more weight if I stop my long walks?" how should you respond?

Reassure her that this is a time-bound experiment (e.g., 2 weeks) to gather data. Frame it as "optimizing for capacity" rather than "quitting," and remind her of the successes she‚Äôs already achieved.

4. What is a "Success Marker" for this specific coaching session?

A success marker is when the client expresses "relief" rather than "anxiety" about the plan change, and when she agrees to a specific, manageable action step (like 15 minutes of breathwork).
