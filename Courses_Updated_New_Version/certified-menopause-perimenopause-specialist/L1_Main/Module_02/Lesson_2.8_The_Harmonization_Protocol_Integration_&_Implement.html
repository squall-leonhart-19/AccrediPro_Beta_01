Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

A 2022 survey of wellness practitioners found that 68% of new coaches struggle with "The Expert Trap." Here is how to avoid it:

  • The Expert Trap: Trying to explain the entire Krebs cycle to a client who just wants to know why she's tired. Keep it simple.
  • Fixing Too Fast: Giving 10 recommendations in the first 10 minutes. Harmonization takes time. Aim for 2-3 "big wins" per session.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Elena feels like she is "failing." If you don't address that shame, the best nutrition plan in the world won't stick.
  • Using "You Should": This triggers a resistance response. Instead, use: "What would it look like if we experimented with..."
Coach Sarah's Tip

In my early days, I felt I had to justify my $150/hour rate by giving too much information. I realized later that clients pay for clarity and results, not a biology lecture. Less is often more.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "eating less" often the wrong advice for a client like Elena?

In a high-cortisol state, extreme calorie restriction acts as a further stressor, signaling the body to slow the metabolism even further and store fat (insulin resistance) to survive the perceived "famine."

2. What is the primary goal of the "Harmonize" framework during an initial session?

To validate the client's experience, reduce shame, and identify the primary hormonal "loudest drummer" (usually cortisol or insulin) that needs support through safety signals.

3. Which of the following is a "safety signal" for the endocrine system?

Consistent protein intake at breakfast, adequate hydration, and nervous system regulation (like box breathing) all signal to the brain that the environment is safe for hormone production rather than survival.

4. How should a coach respond when a client says, "I feel like I'm failing"?

By using empathetic validation: "I hear you, and it's understandable to feel that way when you're working so hard without results. But what if your body isn't failing, it's just trying to protect you from stress?"

Key Takeaways for your practice

  • Validation is Intervention: Simply explaining the "Why" behind symptoms can lower a client's cortisol immediately.
  • Safety Over Scarcity: Focus on what the client can add (protein, rest, minerals) rather than what they must remove.
  • The 3-AM Wakeup: This is a classic sign of blood sugar drops and cortisol spikes; address it through evening "Harmonize" snacks.
