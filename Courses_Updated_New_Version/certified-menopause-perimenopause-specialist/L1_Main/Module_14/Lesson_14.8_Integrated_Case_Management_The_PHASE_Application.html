Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why it Fails The "Pro" Correction
Over-Explaining Science Overwhelms a client already suffering from brain fog. Use simple metaphors (like the roller coaster).
Dismissing the Doctor Creates fear and conflict for the client. "Your doctor checked for disease; we are checking for optimal function."
Jumping to Supplements Treats the symptom, not the root cause. Focus on lifestyle foundations (sleep/stress) first.
Fixing Instead of Coaching The client becomes dependent on you. Ask: "Are you open to tracking these markers together?"

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these "Green Lights":

  • The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes their shoulders or sighs after you validate their experience.
  • "You're the first person who...": The client states that they finally feel heard or understood.
  • Shift to Curiosity: The client moves from "Why is this happening to me?" to "What can we do about it?"
  • Actionable Buy-In: The client agrees to the tracking or lifestyle change without hesitation.
Sarah's Insight

Don't be afraid of silence. After you say, "You are not crazy," wait for 5 seconds. Let it sink in. That silence is where the healing starts.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is validation the first step in coaching a client like Diane?

Validation builds trust and safety. Most perimenopausal women have been told their symptoms are "just stress" or "normal aging." By acknowledging their reality, you lower their cortisol response and make them more receptive to your coaching plan.

2. What is the benefit of the "Roller Coaster" metaphor?

It explains the difference between "static" lab results (a snapshot) and "dynamic" hormonal fluctuations (the ride). It helps the client understand why they feel bad even if their labs are "normal."

3. How should you handle a client's "Normal" bloodwork from their GP?

Do not dismiss the doctor. Instead, explain that medical labs look for pathology (disease), while coaching looks for physiology (how well the body is functioning). This positions you as a complementary partner to their medical care.

4. What is a "Success Marker" to look for in a client's body language?

The "Exhale"â€”a visible release of tension in the shoulders, neck, or jaw. This indicates the client has moved from a "fight or flight" state into a "rest and digest" state, allowing for better cognitive processing of your coaching advice.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation First: Before you give a single piece of advice, ensure the client feels 100% heard.
  • Simplify the Science: Use metaphors to explain complex hormonal shifts to avoid overwhelming the client.
  • Coach, Don't Fix: Guide the client toward their own tracking and discovery rather than just handing over a protocol.
  • Monitor the Response: Watch for the "Exhale" as a sign that your coaching is landing effectively.
