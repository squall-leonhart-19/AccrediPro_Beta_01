Common Mistakes to Avoid

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is HIIT cardio often counter-productive for a client like Monica?

In a state of low estrogen and high stress (insomnia), HIIT can spike cortisol excessively. Since the body is already "wired," it perceives the intense exercise as a threat, triggering fat storage (especially visceral fat) and further depleting the adrenals.

2. What is the primary goal of using "Validation" in the opening of a session?

Validation removes the "shame" the client feels for "failing" at her health. By explaining that her body is behaving logically to a hormonal shift, you lower her stress response and build the trust necessary for her to try counter-intuitive strategies like "resting more."

3. How does the "Cortisol-Insulin-Estrogen" triad explain midsection weight gain?

Lower estrogen increases insulin resistance and cortisol sensitivity. High cortisol triggers the release of glucose (energy), but if the body is insulin resistant, that glucose is stored as fatâ€”specifically visceral fat around the organs, which has more cortisol receptors.

4. What is a "safety signal" in the context of menopause coaching?

A safety signal is any intervention that lowers the sympathetic nervous system (fight or flight) response. Examples include restorative sleep, adequate (not low) caloric intake, slow strength training, and deep breathing.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation is Intervention: Simply telling a client "it's not your fault" can physically lower their cortisol.
  • The Pivot: Transition clients from "stress-based" fitness to "resiliency-based" fitness (lifting heavy + resting more).
  • The Triad: Always connect symptoms back to the interplay of Cortisol, Insulin, and Estrogen.
  • Simplicity Wins: Your value isn't in your complex knowledge, but in your ability to make that knowledge actionable for the client.
