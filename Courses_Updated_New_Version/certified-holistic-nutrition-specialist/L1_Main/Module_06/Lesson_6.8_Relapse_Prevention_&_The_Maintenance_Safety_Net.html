4. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Pitfall Why it Hurts Your Business The Sustainable Fix
The Information Dump Overwhelms the client; they feel they have "enough" info and don't need the coach. Focus on outcomes and systems, not just teaching facts.
Pre-emptive Discounting Devalues your expertise and sets a precedent for "cheap" coaching. State your professional fee with confidence. Offer payment plans, not discounts.
Lack of a "Next Step" The client leaves the call inspired but with no clear path to action. Always end with a specific enrollment link or a follow-up date.
Sarah's Insight

In my first year, I used to give away all my best tips in the first call for free. I thought I was being helpful, but I was actually robbing them of the 90-day container they needed to actually implement those tips!

5. Success Markers: How You Know You Nailed It

A successful enrollment call isn't just about the "Yes." It's about the quality of the connection. Look for these signs:

  • The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes when they realize you have a system to handle their problem.
  • Clarifying Questions: They ask about the logistics (e.g., "How do we meet?") rather than just the price.
  • Future-Pacing: They start talking about what they will do once they have more energy (e.g., "I'll finally be able to hike with my grandkids").
Sarah's Insight

If you get a "No," it's rarely about you. It's usually about their own fear of change. Bless them, release them, and keep your system open for the clients who are ready to say "Yes."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is silence important after stating your program price?

It allows the client the mental space to process the investment and commit. Filling the silence often comes from the coach's own anxiety and can lead to unnecessary discounting or over-explaining.

2. What is the difference between a "diet" and a "sustainable system" in a coaching conversation?

A diet is a temporary set of rules that relies on willpower. A sustainable system (like the N.O.U.R.I.S.H. Methodâ„¢) is a lifestyle framework that automates habits and addresses the root causes of health issues for long-term success.

3. How should you handle the "I need to think about it" objection?

Acknowledge their need for space, offer a summary of the discussed roadmap to help them decide, and immediately set a firm time for a follow-up "check-in" call.

4. What is "uptalking" and why should a coach avoid it?

Uptalking is ending a statement with a rising inflection, making it sound like a question. It signals a lack of confidence and can undermine the client's trust in your expertise.

Key Takeaways

  • Systems Sell Solutions: Clients aren't buying "nutrition tips"; they are buying a sustainable path to their desired future.
  • Confidence is Contagious: If you believe in your $1,800+ value, they will too. Practice your delivery until it feels natural.
  • The 90-Day Container: Use the 3-month timeframe to emphasize that real, sustainable change takes time and consistent support.
  • Listen More, Teach Less: In enrollment calls, your job is to understand their "Why" and show how your system bridges the gap.
  • Professionalism is Kindness: Setting clear boundaries, prices, and systems is the kindest thing you can do for a client who is currently in chaos.
