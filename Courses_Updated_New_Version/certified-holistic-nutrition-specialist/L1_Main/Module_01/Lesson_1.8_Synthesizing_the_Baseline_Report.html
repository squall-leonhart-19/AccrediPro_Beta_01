The "Why" Behind the Script

Notice how you didn't tell her to "stop drinking coffee." Instead, you validated her experience and explained why you were asking. This builds trust and positions you as a specialist, not a "food police" officer.

Section 3: Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New coaches often fall into these traps during the assessment phase. Being aware of them is the first step toward professional mastery.

The Pitfall Why It Happens The Professional Correction
The Interrogator Feeling like you need to "find the mistake" in their diet. Use open-ended questions: "Tell me more about how you feel after that lunch."
The Premature Fixer Wanting to prove your value by giving advice too early. Wait until the end of the session to offer "Next Steps." Focus on discovery first.
The Sympathy Drowner Getting too emotional about the client's struggles. Practice empathy (understanding) without losing your authority as a practitioner.
Ignoring the Wins Focusing only on symptoms and problems. Highlight what they ARE doing well (e.g., Diane's consistent breakfast).
Income Insight

Practitioners who master the "Discovery Phase" often see 30% higher client retention. When a client feels truly *seen* during an assessment, they are far more likely to commit to a 3-month or 6-month high-ticket program.

Section 4: Success Markers

How do you know if your assessment session was successful? Look for these three signs:

  • The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes or says, "Wow, nobody has ever asked me that before."
  • The Shift in Ownership: The client starts connecting the dots themselves (e.g., "Wait, maybe that's why I'm so bloated after the crackers!").
  • Clear Next Steps: You have identified 1-2 "Low Hanging Fruit" changes that don't overwhelm the client but provide immediate relief.

Check Your Practice Lab Knowledge

1. Why is it important to validate Diane's "picking" at crackers rather than scolding her?

Validation builds a "safe container" for the client. If they feel judged, they will stop being honest with their journal, and you will lose the data you need to help them find the root cause.

2. What is the danger of the "Premature Fixer" pitfall?

Giving advice before a full assessment often leads to "surface-level" solutions. It also diminishes your professional valueâ€”clients pay for a comprehensive plan, not just quick tips they could find on Google.

3. In Diane's case, what does the high coffee intake suggest beyond just a "bad habit"?

It suggests a physiological need for stimulation, likely due to HPA-axis (adrenal) dysfunction or significant blood sugar dips. It's a "crutch" for a deeper metabolic issue.

4. What is the "Expert-Partner" dynamic?

It is a coaching relationship where you provide the clinical expertise and framework, while the client provides the "expertise" on their own life and body. You work together to find sustainable solutions.
