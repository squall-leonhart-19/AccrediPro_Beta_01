Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-explaining the Biochemistry: Don't get lost in the Krebs cycle. Diane doesn't need a biology lecture; she needs to know why she's tired.
  • Being Too "Clinical": Avoid saying "You have adrenal fatigue." Instead, say "Your stress response system is currently overtaxed." (Stay within your scope!).
  • Promising a Quick Fix: Hormonal synergy takes time. Set expectations that 90 days is the minimum for sustainable change.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Hormones dictate mood. If she's crying, don't rush to the next nutrition tip. Hold space.

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why is the "orchestra" analogy effective for explaining endocrine health?

It helps the client understand that hormones don't act in isolation. Even if one gland (the thyroid) is "normal," the overall "sound" (health) can be off if other hormones are out of balance.

2. What is the primary physiological reason for Diane's "3:00 AM wake-ups"?

It is often a cortisol spike caused by a drop in blood sugar or an overtaxed adrenal system, signaling the body to wake up and find fuel.

3. What should a coach do when a client says her labs are "normal" but she feels symptomatic?

Validate her experience first. Then explain the difference between "clinical normal" (absence of disease) and "functional optimal" (thriving health).

4. Why is "eating less" often counterproductive for a perimenopausal client?

Severe calorie restriction is a stressor that can further elevate cortisol and downregulate thyroid function, leading to more weight retention and fatigue.
