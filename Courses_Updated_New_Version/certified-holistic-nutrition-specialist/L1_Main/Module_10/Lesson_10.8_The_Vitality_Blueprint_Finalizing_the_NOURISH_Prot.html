Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

4 Mistakes New Coaches Make

  • The "Information Dump": Trying to explain the entire Krebs cycle to a client who just wants to know why she's tired. Keep science high-level.
  • Fixing Too Fast: Jumping to "take this supplement" before the client feels fully heard.
  • Apologizing for Price: Hesitating when mentioning your program fee. Remember: You are solving a problem she hasn't been able to solve in 2 years.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Forgetting to connect the nutrition plan back to her goal (e.g., "having the energy to start your new business").
Financial Insight

Practitioners who master this "Integrative Consultation" often charge between $150 and $250 for an initial 60-minute deep dive. When you can explain the why behind the what, your value in the market triples.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate Elena's feeling of being a "fraud"?

Validation builds psychological safety. By acknowledging her internal struggle, you lower her cortisol (stress response) and make her more receptive to your nutritional advice.

2. What is the "3 PM wall" usually a symptom of in this scenario?

It is typically a sign of reactive hypoglycemia (a blood sugar crash) caused by an inadequate lunch or excessive caffeine consumption earlier in the day.

3. What does the "N.O.U.R.I.S.H. Method‚Ñ¢" prioritize over restriction?

It prioritizes nutrient density and metabolic support. Instead of "eating less," the focus is on "nourishing differently" to meet the body's hormonal demands.

4. How should a coach handle a client saying they have "no willpower"?

A coach should reframe "willpower" as a physiological state. Explain that cravings are often the brain's way of seeking quick glucose because the previous meal didn't provide sustained energy.
