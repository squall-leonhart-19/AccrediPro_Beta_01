Common Coaching Pitfalls

In my first year of practice, I made all of these! Avoid these mistakes to build faster rapport with your clients.

  • Over-Explaining Science: Don't spend 20 minutes explaining the Krebs cycle. Focus on how the science feels to the client (e.g., "more energy," "better sleep").
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Jumping to supplements before validating the client's emotional struggle. Validate first, then protocol.
  • Using "You Should": This triggers the client's inner critic. Instead, use "What if we tried..." or "I invite you to consider..."
  • Ignoring the "Scary" Factor: For a chronic dieter, eating more is terrifying. Acknowledge the fear rather than brushing it off.
Professionalism Check

Notice how the script uses "We" instead of "I." "We are going to move..." This creates a partnership. You are the guide, but she is the hero of her own journey.

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these three signs:

  1. The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes their shoulders when you tell them it's not their fault.
  2. Shift in Language: The client stops saying "I can't have X" and starts asking "How will X make me feel?"
  3. Commitment: The client agrees to one small, manageable change (like adding protein to breakfast) rather than asking for a 30-day overhaul.

3. How should you handle a client's fear of "eating more"?
