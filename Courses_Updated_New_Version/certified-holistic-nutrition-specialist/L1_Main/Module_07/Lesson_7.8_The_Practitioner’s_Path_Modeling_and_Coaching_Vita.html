Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to reframe Diane's fatigue as a "biological signal" rather than "just aging"?

Reframing gives the client agency. If it's "just aging," there's nothing she can do. If it's a signal, it means there are inputs (nutrition, light, stress management) that can be changed to produce a different outcome.

2. What is the danger of recommending high-intensity interval training (HIIT) to a client in Diane's "depleted" state?

HIIT can spike cortisol in a body that is already struggling with HPA-axis dysregulation. For a client who is "wired but tired," this can lead to further burnout and "crashing" rather than vitality.

3. Which "N.O.U.R.I.S.H." Method pillar does vitality primarily fall under?

It falls under the "H" - Holistic Vitality, though it is supported by all other pillars, especially "U" (Underlying Root Causes) and "S" (Stress Resilience).

4. How does the "Vitality Audit" scale of 1-10 help the coach?

It provides a subjective baseline that can be measured over time. When a client moves from a "3" to a "6" in four weeks, it provides powerful evidence of the program's success, even if their weight hasn't changed yet.

Key Takeaways

  • Validation is the first step: Ensure the client knows their depletion is real and addressable.
  • Language matters: Shift from "fixing symptoms" to "restoring vitality."
  • The "Audit" is essential: Use specific questions to identify the timing and nature of energy crashes.
  • Avoid the overwhelm: Focus on small, restorative wins to build client momentum.
  • Professionalism: Stand in your authority as a Holistic Nutrition Specialist who understands the science of energy.
