Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (CIRS)

For the elite practitioner, understanding CIRS is a game-changer. CIRS is a multi-system, multi-symptom illness caused by exposure to biotoxins (mold from water-damaged buildings, Lyme disease, or certain algae).

In roughly 25% of the population, a genetic defect in the HLA-DR gene prevents the body from "seeing" and clearing these toxins. The immune system stays in a state of permanent "red alert," leading to systemic failure. Symptoms often include:

  • Static shocks: Due to electrolyte imbalances.
  • Ice-pick pains: Sharp, stabbing pains that move around the body.
  • Executive dysfunction: Difficulty with word-finding and organization.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between an IgE allergy and an IgG sensitivity?

IgE allergies are immediate hypersensitivities (hives, anaphylaxis), while IgG sensitivities are delayed (hours to days) and drive chronic, systemic inflammation through immune complex formation.

2. How does Lipopolysaccharide (LPS) drive insulin resistance?

LPS enters the blood via a permeable gut barrier (Metabolic Endotoxemia) and binds to TLR4 receptors, triggering pro-inflammatory cytokines that interfere with the insulin signaling pathway in muscle and fat cells.

3. What makes CIRS different from standard inflammation?

CIRS is a biotoxin-driven illness where the immune system cannot clear the toxin (often due to HLA-DR genetic variations), leading to a permanent, multi-system inflammatory state that doesn't respond to standard diet changes alone.

4. Why might a client need SPMs instead of just standard Omega-3 fish oil?

Chronically inflamed individuals may lack the specific enzymes required to convert EPA/DHA into resolvins. SPMs are pre-converted molecules that actively "resolve" or turn off the inflammatory response.
