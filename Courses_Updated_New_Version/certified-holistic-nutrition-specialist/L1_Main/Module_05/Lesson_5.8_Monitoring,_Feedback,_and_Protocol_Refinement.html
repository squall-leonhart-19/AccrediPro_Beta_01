The Coaching Script

Read this dialogue out loud. Focus on your tone‚Äîaim for authoritative yet empathetic.

YOU:
"Diane, I've reviewed your assessment and your goals. I can see how much effort you've put into researching this on your own. But I want to give you permission right now to close those browser tabs. We aren't going to do everything at once. We're going to do what works for your biology."
DIANE:
"That's a relief. I just feel like I'm behind. I see people on Instagram doing 12-step morning routines and I can't even get my water in."
YOU:
"Instagram isn't your life, Diane. Your life is what happens between 4 PM and 7 PM when you're exhausted. That's where we start. Instead of a 12-step routine, we're going to focus on one 'Anchor Habit' this week: stabilizing your blood sugar before that afternoon crash. How does that sound?"
DIANE:
"It sounds doable. But what about the supplements and the gluten-free thing I read about?"
YOU:
"We will get there. But implementation science tells us that if we change too much, we change nothing. We are building a foundation first. If we fix your afternoon energy, you'll have the willpower to make better dinner choices. It's a domino effect. Ready to look at what that one snack looks like?"
Practice Prompt

Record yourself saying the last line of the script. Listen back. Do you sound confident? If you hesitate, the client will hesitate. Practice until it feels like a natural conversation.

Common Implementation Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches fall into these traps. Awareness is the first step to avoidance.

The Pitfall Why it Fails The Integrative Fix
The Information Dump Causes "Analysis Paralysis" and client overwhelm. Provide only the "Next Best Step."
Ignoring the Environment Expects willpower to overcome a kitchen full of triggers. Focus on "Environmental Design" first.
Vague Goals "Eat better" isn't actionable or measurable. Use "Micro-Actions" (e.g., 20g protein at breakfast).
Lack of Support The client feels alone until the next session. Set up a mid-week check-in or "win" text.
Sarah's Practitioner Tip

I used to give my clients 5-page PDF summaries. I realized they never read them! Now, I send a 3-sentence email after our session: 1. The Goal, 2. The Action, 3. The 'Why'. My client success rate tripled.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to "close the browser tabs" for a client like Diane?

To reduce cognitive load and prevent analysis paralysis. High-stress clients need a narrowed focus to begin taking successful action.

2. What is an "Anchor Habit"?

A foundational habit that, when implemented, makes other healthy choices easier or more likely to occur (the "domino effect").

3. If a client asks about a complex topic (like supplements) before they've mastered the basics, how should you respond?

Acknowledge the importance ("We will get there"), but redirect focus to the foundational foundation to ensure long-term success and prevent overwhelm.

4. What is the "Environmental Design" fix for implementation?

Modifying the client's physical surroundings (kitchen, office, car) so that the healthy choice becomes the easiest or "default" choice.

Key Takeaways

  • Implementation is a Skill: It is the bridge between clinical knowledge and client transformation.
  • Less is More: Success in the first week is more important than a "perfect" plan.
  • Master the Dialogue: Use scripts to build your confidence and establish your authority as a specialist.
  • Watch for Overwhelm: If a client's energy drops during a session, you are likely giving too much information.
  • Celebrate the Micro-Wins: Every small action taken by the client is a victory for their long-term health.
