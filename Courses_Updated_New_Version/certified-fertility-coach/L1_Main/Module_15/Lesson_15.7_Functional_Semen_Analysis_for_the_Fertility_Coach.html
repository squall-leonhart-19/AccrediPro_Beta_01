WHO 6th Edition: Beyond the Minimums

The World Health Organization (WHO) released the 6th edition of the Laboratory Manual for the Examination and Processing of Human Semen in 2021. While these represent the global standard, coaches must understand that these numbers are based on the 5th centile of men whose partners achieved pregnancy within 12 months. This means 95% of fertile men have numbers higher than these minimums.

The DNA Fragmentation Index (DFI): The Hidden Barrier

A standard SA tells us about the "packaging" (count, shape, movement), but it tells us nothing about the "cargo" (the DNA). A man can have a massive sperm count and perfect motility, yet possess high levels of DNA Fragmentation.

High DFI is strongly correlated with:

  • Recurrent Miscarriage: Even if fertilization occurs, the embryo may stop developing at the 4-8 cell stage when the paternal genome "switches on."
  • IVF Failure: High fragmentation reduces blastocyst conversion rates.
  • Unexplained Infertility: Up to 25% of men with "normal" SA parameters have high DFI.

Functional Ranges for DFI

Excellent: < 15% fragmentation
Fair/Good: 15% - 25% fragmentation
Poor: > 25% fragmentation (High risk for miscarriage and failed ART)

Seminal Oxidative Stress: The Root Cause

Why does DNA break? The primary culprit is Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS). Sperm are uniquely vulnerable to oxidative damage because their membranes are rich in polyunsaturated fatty acids and they have very little cytoplasm to house protective antioxidant enzymes.

In your functional assessment, if you see high DFI or low motility, you must screen for these lifestyle "oxidants":

  1. Scrotal Hyperthermia: Laptops on laps, hot tubs, heated car seats, or prolonged sitting (truck drivers/IT professionals).
  2. Environmental Toxins: Phthalates, BPA, and pesticides (EDCs) mimic estrogen and disrupt the HPG axis.
  3. Infection/Inflammation: Silent STIs or prostatitis can flood the seminal fluid with white blood cells, which produce ROS.
  4. Varicocele: Dilated veins in the scrotum that increase temperature and pool toxins.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client's husband has a morphology of 5%. According to WHO 6th Edition, he is "normal." How does a functional coach interpret this?

The coach recognizes that 5% is very close to the 4% minimum (5th centile). While clinically "normal," it is not functionally optimal. We should investigate potential causes of oxidative stress or nutrient deficiencies to improve the percentage of normal forms.

2. Why might a man with a "perfect" standard semen analysis still be the cause of recurrent miscarriage?

He likely has high DNA Fragmentation (DFI). A standard SA only looks at the external features of the sperm. High DFI means the genetic material inside is damaged, which often leads to embryo development failure after fertilization.

3. What is the minimum time frame for a male lifestyle intervention to show results on a repeat lab?

Approximately 90 days. This is the duration of the spermatogenesis cycle‚Äîthe time it takes for a new sperm cell to be created and fully matured.

4. Which lifestyle factor is most commonly associated with a "silent" increase in seminal ROS (Reactive Oxygen Species)?

Scrotal hyperthermia (heat). This includes laptops, hot tubs, tight clothing, and prolonged sitting, all of which raise the temperature of the testes and trigger oxidative stress.
