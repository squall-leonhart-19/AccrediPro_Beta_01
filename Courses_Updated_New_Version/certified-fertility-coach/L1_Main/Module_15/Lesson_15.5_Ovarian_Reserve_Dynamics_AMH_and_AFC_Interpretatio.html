AMH Mechanics: The Follicle Factory

Anti-MÃ¼llerian Hormone (AMH) is a protein secreted by the granulosa cells of small, growing follicles (pre-antral and small antral follicles). Because these growing follicles are recruited from the "sleeping" primordial pool, AMH serves as a biochemical proxy for the total number of eggs remaining.

It is vital to understand that AMH is a marker of quantity, not quality. While a low AMH suggests fewer eggs, it does not dictate the chromosomal health of those eggs. In a 2017 study published in JAMA, researchers found that women with low AMH levels were no less likely to conceive naturally within 6 to 12 months than women with normal levels.

Correlation & Discordance Analysis

In a perfect clinical scenario, AMH and AFC correlate. However, as a Functional Fertility Coach, you must look for Discordance. If AMH is very low but AFC is moderate, several factors may be suppressing the AMH signal:

  • Vitamin D Deficiency: AMH production is Vitamin D dependent. A 2021 meta-analysis showed that correcting Vitamin D deficiency can artificially "raise" AMH levels by up to 25%.
  • Recent Hormonal Contraception: Use of the pill can suppress follicular recruitment, leading to temporarily lower AMH and AFC.
  • Endometriomas: "Chocolate cysts" can interfere with the ultrasound's ability to see all follicles, or the inflammation can suppress granulosa cell function.
Coach Tip: Lab Timing

While AMH can be tested any day of the cycle, AFC must be done in the early follicular phase. If a client brings you an AFC count taken on Day 14, it is clinically invalid for reserve assessment as the dominant follicle obscures the count.

Pathology vs. Physiological Decline

It is normal for a 45-year-old to have an AMH of 0.3 ng/mL. This is physiological. However, if a 29-year-old has an AMH of 0.3 ng/mL, this is pathological. As a practitioner, you must investigate the "Why" behind premature decline:

  1. Endometriosis: Chronic inflammation and oxidative stress in the pelvic cavity can "burn through" follicles faster than normal.
  2. Autoimmunity: Anti-ovarian antibodies can attack the follicle pool. Check for concurrent Hashimoto's or Celiac disease.
  3. Environmental Toxins: Exposure to endocrine disruptors (BPA, Phthalates) can accelerate follicular atresia (death).
  4. Ovarian Surgery: Even minor cyst removals can damage the delicate ovarian cortex where follicles reside.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which cells in the ovary are responsible for secreting AMH?

AMH is secreted by the granulosa cells of pre-antral and small antral follicles.

2. True or False: A woman with an AMH of 0.5 ng/mL cannot conceive naturally.

False. AMH is a marker of quantity, not quality. Research shows women with low AMH have similar natural conception rates in the short term compared to women with normal AMH.

3. What common nutrient deficiency can lead to a falsely low AMH reading?

Vitamin D deficiency. Correcting Vitamin D levels has been shown to improve the AMH signal in several clinical studies.

4. Why is AFC (Antral Follicle Count) performed specifically on Day 2-4 of the cycle?

To visualize the "resting" follicles before a dominant follicle is selected and grows, which would otherwise suppress or hide the other small follicles.
