4 Common Mistakes to Avoid

As a new coach, it's natural to feel like you have to prove yourself. Watch out for these common "new coach" traps:

  • The "Data Dump": Explaining every single marker on a 15-page lab report. Fix: Focus only on the 3-4 markers that explain her primary symptoms.
  • The "Mini-Doctor": Saying "You have hypothyroidism" or "You have adrenal fatigue." Fix: Use descriptive language: "Your thyroid markers are outside of the optimal range for fertility."
  • Ignoring the Emotions: Moving straight to supplements without acknowledging her 18 months of heartbreak. Fix: Always start with empathy.
  • The "Fix-It" Pressure: Feeling like you have to have all the answers immediately. Fix: It's okay to say, "That's a great question, let me look into that specific marker and get back to you by tomorrow."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we use metaphors like "cellular battery" or "velvet lining" when explaining labs?

Metaphors bridge the gap between complex science and a client's lived experience, making the information "sticky," reducing overwhelm, and helping the client feel empowered rather than confused.

2. If a client's lab is in the "normal" range but they have symptoms, what is the coach's role?

The coach's role is to look at the "optimal" or "functional" range for fertility, validate the client's symptoms, and explain that "normal" often just means "absence of disease," not "presence of vitality."

3. What is the "Root Cause Narrative"?

It is the cohesive story that connects a client's history, symptoms, and lab data into a single explanation that points toward a clear path for healing.

4. How should a coach respond if they don't know what a specific lab marker means?

With honesty and professionalism. Say, "That's an excellent question. I want to give you the most accurate insight, so let me consult my resources on that specific marker and follow up with you in our session notes."
