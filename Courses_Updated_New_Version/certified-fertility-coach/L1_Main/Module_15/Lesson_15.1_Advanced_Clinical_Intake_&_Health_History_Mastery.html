The Art of the Functional Intake

A standard medical intake asks: "What is your diagnosis?" A Functional Fertility Intake asks: "Why did your body lose its balance in the first place?" As a coach, your goal is to identify Antecedents, Triggers, and Mediators (ATMs).

  • Antecedents: Pre-existing factors (genetics, birth history, childhood nutrition).
  • Triggers: Events that "tipped" the system (a period of high stress, an infection, or stopping hormonal birth control).
  • Mediators: Factors keeping the dysfunction alive (poor sleep, ongoing inflammation, or nutrient deficiencies).
Coach Tip: The Silence Between the Lines

When a client says "my cycles have always been irregular," don't just check a box. Ask: "When did they start being irregular? What was happening in your life at age 13?" Often, the emotional or physical stress of puberty sets the epigenetic tone for adult fertility.

Environmental Toxic Load: The EDC Screening

Endocrine Disrupting Chemicals (EDCs) are "fertility hijackers." Your intake must screen for occupational and household exposures. Research published in Human Reproduction (2022) demonstrated that women with the highest levels of phthalates in their urine had a 25% lower probability of clinical pregnancy per cycle.

Key Areas for EDC Screening:

  • Personal Care: Use of fragranced lotions, perfumes, and conventional makeup (Phthalates/Parabens).
  • Household: Use of plastic food storage, non-stick cookware (PFAS), and "air fresheners."
  • Occupational: Hairdressers (solvents), dental assistants (mercury), or agricultural workers (pesticides).

Clinical Red Flags: When to Triage

As a coach, you must know when a symptom requires immediate medical referral. While we work holistically, we never ignore "Red Flags."

Severe Dysmenorrhea

Pain that prevents daily activities or requires high-dose NSAIDs. Risk: Endometriosis (refer to specialist).

Sudden Cycle Shortening

Cycles dropping from 28 days to 23-24 days. Risk: Rapidly Diminishing Ovarian Reserve (DOR).

Galactorrhea

Nipple discharge when not breastfeeding. Risk: Prolactinoma (Pituitary tumor).

Check Your Understanding

1. A client reports large clots (>1 inch) and dark purple blood. What is the most likely functional interpretation?

This pattern typically indicates Estrogen Dominance or low uterine circulation. In functional terms, the blood is "stagnating" and the lining is becoming too thick, often due to an imbalance between estrogen and progesterone.

2. What is the difference between an "Antecedent" and a "Trigger" in a clinical intake?

An Antecedent is a pre-existing factor (like genetics or being born via C-section), while a Trigger is the specific event that provoked the current symptoms (like a bout of food poisoning or a period of intense grief).

3. Why is it important to ask about a client's grandmother's health history?

Because of Epigenetics. A woman's eggs are formed while she is in her mother's womb. Therefore, the grandmother's environmental exposures and nutritional status directly impacted the "blueprint" of the client's eggs.

4. A client has nipple discharge but is not pregnant or nursing. What is the appropriate coaching action?

This is a Clinical Red Flag (Galactorrhea). You should immediately refer the client to a physician or endocrinologist to rule out a prolactinoma (pituitary tumor) before continuing with coaching protocols.
