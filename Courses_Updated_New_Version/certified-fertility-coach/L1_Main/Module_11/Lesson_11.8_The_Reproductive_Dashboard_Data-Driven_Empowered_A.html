Coaching Language

Avoid saying "Your hormones are a mess." Instead, use words like "tempo," "synchronization," or "rhythm." This keeps the client in an empowered state rather than a broken one.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client's cycle shortens from 30 days to 24 days, which hormone is likely "shouting" louder at the start of the cycle?

FSH (Follicle Stimulating Hormone). In the late reproductive years, the pituitary gland increases FSH to stimulate the ovaries, which often leads to faster follicle development and a shorter follicular phase.

2. What does pre-menstrual spotting (3-4 days before the flow) typically indicate in perimenopause?

It typically indicates Luteal Phase Insufficiency or premature Progesterone withdrawal. The corpus luteum is not sustaining progesterone levels long enough to keep the uterine lining intact until the full menses.

3. Why is the "Hormonal Symphony" metaphor effective for women ages 40-55?

It moves the conversation away from "broken parts" to "timing and coordination." It validates that the components (ovaries, brain) are working, but the rhythm has changed, which reduces the client's anxiety.

4. True or False: You should tell a client her doctor is wrong if they say her labs are "normal."

False. Her labs may be clinically "normal" (within range), but not optimal for her symptoms. Your role is to help her understand the sub-clinical shifts that occur before labs become "abnormal."
