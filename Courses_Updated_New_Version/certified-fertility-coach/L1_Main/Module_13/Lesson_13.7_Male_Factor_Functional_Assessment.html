DNA Fragmentation: The Hidden Barrier

DNA Fragmentation refers to breaks or lesions in the genetic material within the sperm head. High DNA Fragmentation Index (DFI) is one of the leading causes of "unexplained" infertility and recurrent early miscarriage.

While the egg has some capacity to "repair" fragmented sperm DNA after fertilization, this capacity is limited, especially in women over 35. If the damage is too extensive, the embryo may stop developing around Day 3, leading to a failed IVF cycle or a chemical pregnancy.

Root Causes of High DNA Fragmentation:

  • Oxidative Stress: The most common cause (ROS damage).
  • Infection: Subclinical prostatitis or leukocytospermia (white blood cells in semen).
  • Heat: Hot tubs, saunas, tight underwear, or prolonged sitting.
  • Varicocele: Dilated veins causing blood pooling and heat.

Oxidative Stress Markers

Sperm cells are uniquely vulnerable to oxidative stress because their cell membranes are rich in polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs), and they have very little cytoplasm to house protective antioxidant enzymes. This makes them the "canary in the coal mine" for systemic inflammation.

Functional Hormonal Markers for Men

We often think of hormones as a "female issue," but the male hormonal environment is the soil in which sperm grows. In functional assessment, we look for Subclinical Imbalances:

  • Estradiol (E2): High levels in men (often from aromatization in belly fat) can suppress the HPG axis, lowering testosterone and sperm production.
  • SHBG: Sex Hormone Binding Globulin. If too high, it "locks up" testosterone, leaving little Free Testosterone for sperm development.
  • Prolactin: High prolactin in men can indicate high stress or a pituitary issue, directly inhibiting GnRH and causing low libido and poor sperm motility.
Coach Tip: The Aromatase Effect

If you see a male client with a "spare tire" (abdominal adiposity) and low sperm count, his adipose tissue is likely acting as an endocrine organ, converting his testosterone into estrogen. Functional nutrition focusing on blood sugar and cruciferous vegetables (DIM/I3C) can be transformative here.

Varicoceles & Structural Health

A varicocele is essentially a "varicose vein" in the scrotum. It is found in approximately 15% of the general male population but up to 40% of men with primary infertility.

From a functional perspective, varicoceles cause two major problems:

  1. Thermal Stress: They raise the temperature of the testes, which must remain 1-2 degrees cooler than body temperature for spermatogenesis.
  2. Hypoxia: Poor blood drainage leads to a buildup of metabolic waste and low oxygen in the testicular tissue.

Environmental Toxin Screening

Men are often exposed to higher levels of industrial endocrine disruptors. When conducting a functional intake, ask about:

  • Phthalates: Found in "fragrance," personal care products, and plastics. They are known anti-androgens.
  • Glyphosate: Pesticide exposure (common in landscapers or those living near farms) is linked to poor morphology.
  • Radiofrequency (RF) Radiation: Cell phones in pockets and laptops on laps.
