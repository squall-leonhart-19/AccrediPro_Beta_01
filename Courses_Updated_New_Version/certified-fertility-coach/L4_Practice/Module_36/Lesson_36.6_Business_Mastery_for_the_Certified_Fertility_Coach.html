Building a High-Value Coaching Package

The most common mistake new coaches make is selling "sessions." When you sell by the hour, you are a commodity. When you sell a transformation, you are a specialist. High-value fertility coaching requires time for the biological changes (egg quality maturation takes ~90 days) and lifestyle integration to occur.

Feature Standard Health Coaching Certified Fertility Coach‚Ñ¢ (High-Value)
Pricing Model Hourly ($100-$150/hr) Package-Based ($2,500 - $6,000+)
Duration Pay-as-you-go 90 Days to 6 Months
Deliverables Generic advice Functional Assessment, EAP, Lab Review, Advocacy Support
Client Commitment Low (Easy to quit) High (Invested in the outcome)
Coach Tip: The 90-Day Minimum

Always structure your primary package around a minimum of 90 days. This aligns with the folliculogenesis timeline you learned in Module 3. It ensures the client understands that biological change isn't overnight, which manages expectations and improves your success rates.

Strategic Niche Identification

In a crowded wellness market, specialization is the key to premium pricing. While you are trained in the full F.E.R.T.I.L.E. Method‚Ñ¢, your marketing should speak to a specific "pain point."

  • The Male Factor Advocate: Working with couples to improve sperm morphology and motility through targeted nutrition.

Marketing with Integrity

As a Certified Fertility Coach‚Ñ¢, you must navigate the line between inspiration and compliance. A 2022 survey of the coaching industry found that 68% of consumers are wary of "over-promising" in the health space.

Compliance Gold Standard

NEVER: Guarantee a pregnancy, "cure" infertility, or tell a client to stop medical treatment.
ALWAYS: Focus on "optimizing reproductive health," "improving egg quality," "reducing inflammatory burden," and "building emotional resilience."

The Discovery Call Framework

The Discovery Call is not a coaching session; it is a consultation to determine fit. Using the F.E.R.T.I.L.E. Method‚Ñ¢ value proposition, follow this 5-step flow:

  1. The Opening (5 mins): Set the agenda. "Today is about seeing where you are and if my method can help you reach your goal."
  2. The "Gap" Analysis (15 mins): Ask deep questions. "What have you tried? What is the biggest emotional hurdle right now?"
  3. The Method Bridge (10 mins): Connect their problems to your solution. "Based on your PCOS symptoms, we would focus heavily on the Functional and Targeted Nutrition pillars of my method."
  4. The Future State (5 mins): Help them visualize. "Imagine going into your next cycle feeling advocate-ready and physically optimized."
  5. The Invitation (5 mins): Present the package and price. Do not apologize for your rates.
Coach Tip: Handle Objections with Empathy

When a client says "It's too expensive," they are usually saying "I'm afraid it won't work for me." Reframe the investment: "I understand. Compared to the $15,000 cost of a failed IVF cycle, our work together is designed to ensure that $15,000 is an investment that actually pays off."

Retention & Referral Mastery

In fertility coaching, your best marketing comes from "BFP" (Big Fat Positive) announcements. However, your business shouldn't end at conception. A sustainable practice includes Integrative Advocacy for the first trimester.

The Referral Loop:

  • The "Success" Gift: Send a small, non-toxic pregnancy gift to successful clients with a note.
  • The Professional Referral: Build relationships with local acupuncturists and REIs. When they see your clients are "easier to treat" because they are prepared, they will refer back to you.
  • The Alumni Program: Offer a "Healthy Pregnancy" transition package to keep clients for an additional 3 months post-conception.
Coach Tip: Collect Video Testimonials

Ask for testimonials during the process, not just at the end. A client saying "I finally feel like I have a plan" after 30 days is just as powerful as a pregnancy announcement for a prospective client who currently feels lost.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Hourly Rate" model considered detrimental to a fertility coaching practice?

Hourly rates commoditize the coach and make it easy for clients to cancel when they don't see immediate results. Because biological changes (like egg quality) take 90+ days, a package-based model ensures the client stays committed long enough to see actual physiological transformation.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Gap Analysis" phase in a Discovery Call?

The goal is to identify the distance between where the client is now (frustrated, symptomatic, confused) and where they want to be (empowered, optimized, pregnant). This allows the coach to "bridge" that gap using the specific pillars of the F.E.R.T.I.L.E. Method‚Ñ¢.

3. Which of the following is a compliant marketing statement?

"My program is designed to optimize your reproductive environment and improve egg quality through functional lifestyle alignment." (Statements promising to "cure" infertility or "guarantee" pregnancy are non-compliant and legally risky).

4. How does the "Success Gift" serve as a business strategy?

It cements the emotional bond between coach and client, increases the likelihood of a glowing referral, and provides a natural touchpoint to transition the client into a "First Trimester Support" package.
