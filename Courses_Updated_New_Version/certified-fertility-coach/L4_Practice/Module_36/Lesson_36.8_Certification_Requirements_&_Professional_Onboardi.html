Case Study: The Frustrated Executive

Sarah, 42, Secondary Infertility

Profile: Sarah is a high-achieving marketing director. She has one child (age 4) and has been trying for a second for 22 months. She has undergone two failed IUIs and is "on the fence" about IVF due to the hormonal toll.

Pain Points: Chronic stress, "unexplained" diagnosis from her RE, feeling like her body is failing her, and immense guilt that she can't give her daughter a sibling.

The Opportunity: Sarah has the budget for support but is skeptical of "woo-woo" coaching. She needs reproductive literacy and a functional approach that respects her intelligence.

Coach Tip: The 80/20 Rule

On a discovery call, the prospect should be talking 80% of the time. Your job is to ask the "incisive questions" that lead them to realize they cannot solve this problem alone. If you find yourself over-explaining the science in the first 10 minutes, stop and ask: "How does that land with you?"

The Discovery Call Script: 30 Minutes to "Yes"

A successful call follows a specific psychological arc. We move from safety (rapport) to pain (the problem) to hope (the solution).

Handling Objections with Authority

Objections are not rejections. They are a request for more information or a manifestation of the prospect's fear. A study of high-performing coaches found that addressing the "money" objection head-on increases closing rates by 38% compared to those who avoid the topic.

The Objection The "Emma" Response (Empathetic Authority)
"I need to talk to my husband." "I completely value that. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Is it the cost, or the time commitment?"
"It's just so much money." "I understand. It is an investment. Compared to the $15,000 for a single round of IVF, we are focused on making sure your body is actually ready to sustain a pregnancy. Does that perspective help?"
"Will this definitely work?" "I can't guarantee a baby‚Äîno one can. What I can guarantee is that in 90 days, your biomarkers, cycle clarity, and resilience will be in a completely different place than they are today."
Coach Tip: Silence is Golden

After you state your price, stop talking. Do not justify it. Do not offer a discount immediately. Wait for them to speak first. The silence feels like an eternity, but it is actually the space where they are making a life-changing decision.

Pricing Presentation: Stating Your Value

Many career changers struggle with "imposter pricing." They charge $75 an hour and burn out. As a Certified Fertility Coach‚Ñ¢, you are providing a specialized, high-value service. You are not selling "time"; you are selling transformation.

A standard 90-day fertility coaching package (including 6-8 sessions, messaging support, and customized protocols) typically ranges from $1,800 to $4,500 depending on your niche and experience level.

Coach Tip: The "Anchor" Technique

When Sarah mentions she spent $5,000 on supplements that didn't work, that is your anchor. "Sarah, you've already invested $5,000 without a clear plan. My program is $2,800 and provides the exact roadmap to ensure your future investments actually yield results."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

To build rapport, establish safety, and gain permission to ask deep questions. It sets the tone that you are a partner, not just a salesperson.

2. If a prospect says "I can't afford this," what is the best first response?

Acknowledge the concern with empathy ("I hear you, and I know this is a significant commitment") and then ask a clarifying question to see if it's a "hard no" or a "how can I make this work" conversation.

3. Why is the "80/20 rule" critical for conversion?

Because people buy when they feel understood, not when they understand you. By listening 80% of the time, you gather the specific emotional language needed to show them how your program solves their specific pain.

4. How many active clients at a $2,500 package rate are needed to reach a six-figure annual run rate?

Approximately 10 active clients (cycling every 3 months) or roughly 3.3 new enrollments per month.

Key Takeaways
