The Discovery Call: The Empowered Action Framework

A discovery call is not a "free coaching session." It is a structured interview designed to determine if you can help the client and if they are ready to be helped. Using the Empowered Action Framework, you lead the client through their current pain points toward a vision of their future success.

Coach Tip #1

Stop over-explaining the science on the discovery call. Your goal is to show them you understand their problem better than they do. When they feel understood, they automatically trust that you have the solution.

The 6 Steps of the Empowered Action Call

  1. The Frame: Set the agenda immediately. "The goal of today is to see where you are, where you want to go, and if my F.E.R.T.I.L.E. Method‚Ñ¢ is the right bridge to get you there."
  2. Current Reality (The Gap): Ask, "What have you tried that hasn't worked?" This identifies their frustration with the conventional "one-size-fits-all" model.
  3. The Vision: "If we could balance your hormones and optimize your egg quality over the next 4 months, how would your life look different?"
  4. The Cost of Inaction: Gently explore what happens if they don't change their approach. This builds the internal "why" for the investment.
  5. The Solution (The Bridge): Briefly explain how your signature program addresses their specific gaps (e.g., "Because you have PCOS-related insulin resistance, we will focus heavily on the 'Functional Assessment' and 'Targeted Nutrition' pillars").
  6. The Invitation: Do not "pitch." Invite them. "Based on what you've told me, you are a perfect fit for this container. Would you like to hear how we can get started?"

Case Study: Transitioning from Nurse to Coach

Practitioner: Sarah, 48, former Labor & Delivery Nurse.

The Challenge: Sarah felt "guilty" charging for her time. She was doing 90-minute "discovery calls" that were actually free coaching sessions. Her conversion rate was 10%.

The Intervention: Sarah implemented the Empowered Action Framework. She limited calls to 45 minutes and stopped giving "tips" during the call. Instead, she focused on identifying the client's "Functional Gaps."

The Outcome: Sarah's conversion rate jumped to 65%. She enrolled 4 clients in one month into her $4,500 signature program, generating $18,000 in revenue while working fewer hours than her nursing shifts.

Assessing Coachability & Clinical Fit

Not everyone who can pay is a good client. Enrolling the "wrong" client leads to poor outcomes and practitioner burnout. As a Certified Fertility Coach‚Ñ¢, you must screen for two things:

1. Clinical Fit (Scope of Practice)

Is the client's situation within your scope? If they have active, unmanaged Stage IV endometriosis or a history of 5+ recurrent miscarriages without medical workup, they need a Reproductive Endocrinologist (RE) first. Your role is Integrative Advocacy, not medical replacement.

2. Coachability (The Mindset)

Ask yourself these questions during the call:

  • Are they taking responsibility for their lifestyle, or blaming their doctor/partner?
  • Do they follow the "pre-call" instructions (e.g., filling out an intake form)?
  • Are they open to the "Functional" approach, or are they skeptical of everything that isn't a drug?
Coach Tip #2

If a lead says, "I've worked with 5 coaches and none of them helped," be very careful. This is a red flag for a "Difficult Client" profile. Ensure you set strict boundaries during the enrollment call to see how they respond.

Overcoming Objections with Integrity

Objections are rarely about the money. They are usually about fear or perceived value. When a client says "It's too expensive," they are often saying "I'm afraid this won't work for me like the other things didn't."

The "Cost of the Alternative" Strategy

When faced with a financial objection, help the client look at the math of their current path:

  • IVF Cycle: $15,000 - $25,000 per attempt.
  • Lost Wages: From stress, appointments, and mental health days.
  • Supplements: $200/month spent on things that might not be working.

Your program (e.g., $3,500) is an investment to increase the success rate of those expensive medical interventions or to avoid them altogether by optimizing natural fertility.

Coach Tip #3

Handle "Emotional Fatigue" by acknowledging it. "I hear how exhausted you are. My program is actually designed to take the thinking off your plate. You follow the roadmap; I provide the strategy. Does that sound like it would lower your stress?"

Streamlined Onboarding Systems

The moment a client pays, they experience a spike in dopamine followed by potential "Buyer's Remorse." Your onboarding must be immediate and professional to solidify trust.

The "Instant Trust" Onboarding Sequence:

  • Minute 1: Automated "Welcome" email with a video from you.
  • Minute 5: Access to the client portal and the "First Steps" module.
  • Hour 1: Link to schedule their 90-minute Deep Dive Assessment.
  • Day 1: A physical or digital "Welcome Kit" (e.g., a PDF guide on "What to do while you wait for our first call").
Coach Tip #4

Use a practice management software (like Practice Better or Healthie) to automate this. For a woman in her 40s or 50s pivoting into this career, professional tech systems are the fastest way to overcome "imposter syndrome" and look like an established expert.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Vision" step in the Empowered Action Framework?

To move the client from focusing on their current pain to the emotional and physical benefits of the desired outcome, making the investment feel like a bridge to that future.

2. Which statistic represents the percentage of fertility patients who feel "overwhelmed and powerless"?

According to industry surveys, approximately 78% of fertility patients feel overwhelmed by the conventional medical process.

3. How should a coach handle the "It's too expensive" objection?

By reframing the cost as an "Investment in Outcome" and comparing it to the high cost of IVF failure or "blind" supplement spending.

4. What is a "red flag" for coachability during an enrollment call?

Clients who blame all external factors (doctors, partners) without taking personal responsibility or who fail to complete simple pre-call intake tasks.
