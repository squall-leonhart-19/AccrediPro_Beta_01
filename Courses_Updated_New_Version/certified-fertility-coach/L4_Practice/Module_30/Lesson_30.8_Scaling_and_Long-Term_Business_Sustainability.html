The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

The goal is not to "sell," but to disqualify or qualify. You are interviewing her to see if she is a good fit for your high-level support.

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (0-5 Minutes)
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It‚Äôs so good to finally connect. I read your intake form, and I want to start by saying I hear how much you‚Äôve been through. Before we dive in, my goal today is to understand your journey and see if my Empowered Fertility program is the right bridge to get you to your goal. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Deep Discovery (5-15 Minutes)
YOU:
"You mentioned the failed IVF cycles. Beyond the physical toll, how has this impacted your daily life and your marriage? What is the one thing you feel is 'missing' from the care you've received so far?"
YOU:
(Listen for the 'Gap') "I see. So you have the medical side covered, but you feel like your nutrition, stress, and egg quality support are being ignored. Is that right?"
Phase 3: The Prescription (15-25 Minutes)
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you‚Äôve told me, you are a perfect fit for what I do. We don't just 'try things.' We use a 12-week functional approach to optimize your terrain. I work with women exactly like you to ensure that if you go for a third IVF, your body is in the best possible state to receive it."
Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 Minutes)
YOU:
"My 3-month intensive is $2,400, or we can do a monthly plan. Based on our talk, would you like to hear more about how we get started next week?"
Emma's Strategy

Never apologize for your price. When you state your fee, stop talking. The silence that follows is where the client decides to invest in herself. Let her have that moment.

Handling Objections with Empathy

Objections are rarely about the money; they are usually about fear of another failure. Use these "Turn-Around" responses.

The Objection The "Emma" Response The Psychology
"I need to talk to my husband." "I love that you're a team. What do you think he'll be most concerned about‚Äîthe time, or the investment?" Isolates the real concern so you can address it together.
"It's just so much money after IVF." "I completely understand. Compared to another $15k IVF, this $2k investment is about making sure that $15k isn't wasted. Does that make sense?" Reframes the cost as "Insurance" for her medical success.
"Can I just think about it?" "Of course. Usually, when people say that, it means they have a lingering doubt. What's the one thing you're still unsure about?" Invites honesty and uncovers hidden fears.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Discovery" phase of the call?

The goal is to identify the "gap" between where the client is and where they want to be, and to understand the emotional and practical cost of staying where they are.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I've already spent so much on IVF"?

Acknowledge the pain, then reframe your coaching as "success insurance" to optimize their physical terrain so that their medical investments have a higher chance of yielding a result.

3. True or False: You should wait until the end of the call to mention your price.

True. You must first establish the value and the "fit" before the price has any context. Stating price too early leads to being viewed as a commodity rather than a solution.

4. Why is the "Invitation" phase crucial?

Because it shifts the dynamic from "providing information" to "offering a transformation." Without a clear invitation to work together, you are just a friendly resource, not a professional practitioner.
Key Takeaways
  • Authority + Empathy: Successful coaching sales come from leading the call while making the client feel deeply heard.
  • Structure is Safety: Using a 30-minute framework prevents calls from turning into "free coaching sessions" that don't lead to a commitment.
  • Objections are Information: View objections as the client asking for more certainty, not as a rejection of you personally.
  • Financial Freedom is Possible: With just 5 clients a month, you can build a career that exceeds the average US professional salary while working flexible hours.
