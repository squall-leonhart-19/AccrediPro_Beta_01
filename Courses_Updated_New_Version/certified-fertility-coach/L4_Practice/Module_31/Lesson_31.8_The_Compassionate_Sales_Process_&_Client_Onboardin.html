2. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A successful call follows a specific rhythm. If you talk too much, you lose the lead. If you talk too little, you lose the authority. Follow this 4-phase structure.

3. Handling Objections with Integrity

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a manifestation of the prospect's fear. As a coach, your coaching starts during the sales call.

Objection 1: "I need to talk to my husband."

The Response: "I completely respect that; big decisions should be made together. Usually, when someone says that, it‚Äôs because they‚Äôre either not sure about the investment or they aren't sure if this will actually work for them. Which one is it for you?" (This gets to the real truth).

Objection 2: "It‚Äôs too expensive."

The Response: "I understand. It is a significant investment. But let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not solving this? If you continue down the path of unguided supplements and stress, what will that cost you in terms of time and further medical interventions? My goal is to save you the $20k you might otherwise spend on IVF by getting the foundations right now."

Emma's Strategy Tip

Never lower your price on the spot. If you do, you communicate that your original price was arbitrary. Instead, offer a longer payment plan to make the monthly "bite" smaller.

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

Imposter syndrome often shows up right when you state your price. You might find yourself "upspeaking" (making the price sound like a question) or rushing past the number. Practice saying these out loud until they feel like a fact, not a request.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in the discovery call?

To identify "The Gap" and help the prospect realize the emotional and physical cost of staying where they are. It is about building the "need" for a solution.

2. If a prospect says "I've tried everything," how should a coach respond?

Acknowledge their frustration, then pivot to why your methodology is different (e.g., "You've tried symptoms-based approaches; we are doing root-cause systems work").

3. True or False: You should wait until the very end of the call to mention the price.

True. You must establish the value and the prospect's desire for the outcome before the price is introduced, otherwise, it is seen as a "cost" rather than an "investment."

4. How many discovery calls per month are typically needed to enroll 3 clients at a 30% conversion rate?

Approximately 10 calls. This helps you plan your marketing effort (how many leads you need to generate).
Emma's Strategy Tip

Record your first 5 discovery calls. Listen back to them. You will hear exactly where you hesitated or where you missed an opportunity to ask a deeper question. This is the fastest way to double your income.

Key Takeaways

  • Sales is an act of service; if you believe you can help Sarah conceive, it is your duty to be as persuasive as possible.
  • The Discovery Call is a structured 30-minute conversation, not an informal chat.
  • Confidence in pricing comes from practice and internalizing the "ROI" (Return on Investment) of a healthy baby.
  • Objections are a natural part of the process and should be handled with curiosity rather than defensiveness.
  • A six-figure practice is achievable with just 3-4 high-ticket enrollments per month.
