3. Navigating Objections with Integrity

Objections aren't rejections; they are requests for more information. Handle them with empathy and legal clarity.

The Objection The Compliant Response Strategy
"Can you guarantee I'll get pregnant?" "I cannot guarantee a clinical outcome like pregnancy. What I can guarantee is that you will have a personalized, evidence-based plan to optimize every factor within your control."
"Is this covered by my health insurance?" "Typically, coaching is not covered by insurance as it is a wellness service, not a medical treatment. However, many clients use their HSA/FSA funds‚Äîyou'll just need to check with your provider."
"I need to ask my husband first." "I completely respect that. Fertility is a team sport! Would it be helpful if I sent over a PDF summary of our plan that you two can review together tonight?"
Emma's Strategy

Never lower your price on the spot. If a client has a budget issue, offer a smaller "starter" package or a longer payment plan. Lowering your price immediately devalues your certification and expertise.

Check Your Understanding

1. When should you deliver your "Scope of Practice" disclaimer?

Ideally within the first 5 minutes of the discovery call. This sets the professional tone and protects you before the client shares sensitive medical questions.

2. A client asks: "Which thyroid medication is best for fertility?" How do you respond?

"That is a medical question that must be answered by your doctor or endocrinologist. As your coach, I can help you track your symptoms and prepare a list of questions for your next appointment so you can advocate for the best care."

3. True or False: You should wait until after the first paid session to have the client sign a contract.

False. The contract must be signed and the first payment received BEFORE the first coaching session begins to ensure legal protection for both parties.

4. Why is it important to offer a "PDF Summary" when a client needs to talk to a partner?

It ensures your value proposition and your legal boundaries are communicated clearly to the partner, who wasn't on the call to hear your verbal disclaimers.

Key Takeaways

  • Compliance is a trust-builder; it shows the client you are a trained professional, not an amateur.
  • Always lead with your Scope of Practice disclaimer to set boundaries early.
  • Pivot medical questions back to the client's doctor while offering "coaching support" (e.g., advocacy, lifestyle implementation).
  • Price your services based on the transformation you provide, not the hours you spend.
  • A compliant practice is a scalable practice; getting the legal side right allows for significant income growth.
