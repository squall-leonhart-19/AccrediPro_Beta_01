Welcome to the Lab, Coach!

I’m Emma Thompson. When I first started, I was terrified that no one would want to work in a group. I thought women needed 100% of my attention to see results. I was wrong. A 2022 study of fertility support groups found that peer connection actually reduced cortisol levels by 18% more than individual counseling alone. Today, we’re going to practice how to sell that value.

Learning Objectives
  • Master the transition from "free workshop" to "paid group program."
  • Identify the 3 core psychological drivers for group participation.
  • Practice confident pricing delivery for mid-tier group offers.
  • Develop a rebuttal strategy for common group-specific objections.

The Prospect Profile: Sarah

Name: Sarah, 42

Background: Elementary school teacher, trying for her second child for 18 months. She is "information-overloaded" and feels isolated because her friends all finished having kids years ago.

The Scenario: Sarah just finished your free 60-minute webinar, "The 3 Egg Quality Essentials." She has booked a 15-minute "Fit Call" to see if your 8-week group program, The Fertile Reset, is right for her.

Her Secret Fear: "Will I just be another number in a group? Can I really get results if the coach isn't focusing only on me?"

Coach Tip #1

Sarah doesn't need more information; she needs a curated path and community. When speaking to women 40+, emphasize that the group provides the "sisterhood" they are missing in their current social circles.

The Discovery Call Script: Group Program Close

Phase 1: Validation & Connection (3 Mins)
YOU:
"Sarah, it was so great having you on the webinar! You asked such a poignant question about egg quality and stress. What was the biggest 'aha' moment for you during the presentation?"
SARAH:
"I think just realizing that I'm not the only one at 42 struggling with this. I felt so much less alone just seeing the other women in the chat."
Phase 2: The Gap Analysis (5 Mins)
YOU:
"That feeling of isolation is exactly why I created The Fertile Reset. Sarah, if you keep doing what you're doing—searching Google at 2 AM and trying random supplements—where do you think you'll be in six months?"
SARAH:
"Probably still here, just more tired and more broke."
Phase 3: Presenting the Solution (5 Mins)
YOU:
"The Reset is designed to take that guesswork off your plate. We meet once a week for 8 weeks. You get the exact protocol we discussed, but more importantly, you get the daily support of 9 other women who 'get it.' We move as a pack. Does that sound like the kind of support you need right now?"
Coach Tip #2

Notice the use of "We move as a pack." This frames the group as an advantage, not a compromise. In sales, this is called Reframing the Feature.

Handling Group-Specific Objections

In group sales, objections usually center around privacy or personalization. Use the "Feel, Felt, Found" method to normalize their concerns.

Objection The Confident Response
"I'm a private person." "I completely understand. Many of my most successful clients felt the same way initially. What they found was that listening to others' questions actually answered things they didn't even know to ask."
"Is it personalized?" "While the curriculum is structured, our weekly Q&A sessions are 100% focused on your specific labs and life. You get the 'best of both worlds'—a proven system and direct access to me."
"I can't afford it." "I hear you. That’s why I created this group—it’s about 1/3 the investment of my 1-on-1 private coaching, but with the added benefit of the community support which often leads to better compliance."
Coach Tip #3

Always state your price with a "period" at the end, not a question mark. "The investment for the 8-week program is $997." Stop talking. Let them process. The first person to speak usually loses the lead in the negotiation.

The Math of Meaningful Income

For many career changers, the goal is to replace a salary of $60k-$80k. Group programs make this possible without working 40 hours a week. A 2023 survey of wellness practitioners (n=1,200) showed that those with at least one group offer earned 42% more annual revenue than those offering only 1-on-1 services.

Income Scenario: The Teacher-to-Coach Pivot

Meet Linda (51), a former nurse. She runs one group program per quarter.

  • Program: 10-Week "Conception Preparation"
  • Price: $1,200 per person
  • Capacity: 12 women
  • Revenue: $14,400 per launch
  • Effort: 2 hours of live coaching per week + 3 hours of admin.

By running this 3 times a year, Linda earns $43,200 from just 5 hours of work per week, leaving her ample time for high-ticket 1-on-1 clients or family.

Coach Tip #4

Don't wait until you have "enough" followers to launch a group. Start with a "Beta" group of 4-6 people at a lower price point to gather testimonials. Social proof is the currency of group sales.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of a group program for a 40+ fertility client?

The reduction of isolation and the "sisterhood" effect, which has been shown to physiologically lower stress markers like cortisol.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I'm worried I won't get enough attention in a group"?

Reframe the group as an advantage: explain that hearing others' questions provides insights they wouldn't get alone, and highlight the dedicated Q&A time for their specific needs.

3. What is the "Feel, Felt, Found" method?

It is an empathy-based objection handling tool: "I understand how you feel; other clients have felt the same way; but what they found was [positive result]."

4. Why is a group program more "scalable" than 1-on-1 coaching?

Because your time investment remains fixed (e.g., 90 minutes for a call) whether you have 5 students or 25 students, allowing you to decouple your income from your hours worked.
Key Takeaways
  • Group programs are not a "lesser" version of coaching; they are a different, often more powerful, modality due to peer support.
  • Confident pricing delivery requires a neutral tone and a "period" at the end of the sentence.
  • Replacing a full-time income is mathematically simpler with group programs (e.g., 10 women at $1k is a $10k month).
  • Use free workshops or webinars as the primary "feeder" for your group programs to build trust and authority first.
