The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A professional discovery call follows a specific cadence. Research suggests that high-converting calls (above 35% conversion) involve the prospect talking for at least 60% of the time.

Phase 1: Rapport & Framing (0-5 Minutes)

You:
"Hi Sarah! It‚Äôs so good to finally connect. I‚Äôve been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal today is to understand exactly where you are, where you want to be, and see if my Fertility Reset Program is the right bridge to get you there. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: Deep Discovery (5-15 Minutes)

You:
"Sarah, tell me a bit about your journey so far. What has been the biggest challenge for you over these last 18 months?"
Sarah:
"I just feel like the doctors aren't listening. They say my FSH is high and I should just go to donor eggs. I'm not ready for that yet."
You:
"I hear you. It feels like you're being rushed into a decision without looking at the whole picture. If we could improve your egg quality and lower that stress over the next 90 days, how would that change things for you?"
Emma's Insight

Use "active listening" cues. When she says something emotional, pause. Say, "That sounds incredibly heavy." Let her feel heard before moving to the solution.

Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are not "no's"‚Äîthey are requests for more information. In the fertility space, objections are usually rooted in fear of another failure.

The Objection The Fear Root Your Confident Response
"I need to talk to my husband." Lack of safety in solo decision-making. "I completely understand. Fertility is a team sport! Would it be helpful if I sent you a summary of our talk for him to look at?"
"It's a lot of money." Fear of low ROI (Return on Investment). "I hear you. It is an investment. Compared to another $15k IVF cycle, our goal is to prep your body so your next steps are as successful as possible."
"Will this work at my age?" Biological hopelessness. "While I can't guarantee a baby, I can guarantee we will optimize every factor within our control‚Äîegg quality, lining, and stress‚Äîto give you the best possible odds."

Practitioner: Linda, former High School Teacher.

Intervention: Linda switched from hourly coaching to a $2,500 12-week "Over 40 Fertility" package. She practiced her discovery call script 20 times with her daughter.

Stating Your Price (The "No-Blink" Rule)

The moment you state your price is the moment you demonstrate your own belief in your value. If you stutter, Sarah will feel unsafe.

You:
"The investment for the 12-week Fertility Foundation Program is $2,400. This includes your functional lab reviews, weekly 1-on-1 sessions, and daily messaging support. We can do that in one payment, or we have a 3-month payment plan of $850. Which works best for your family?"
Emma's Insight

After you say the price, STOP TALKING. The first person to speak usually loses their leverage. Let her process the number in silence.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the recommended percentage of time the prospect should be talking during a discovery call?

The prospect should talk for at least 60% of the call. Your role is to ask powerful questions and listen deeply.

2. If a client says, "I need to think about it," what is the most professional follow-up?

Acknowledge the weight of the decision and ask, "Of course. To help you think through it, is there a specific part of the program or the investment that you're feeling unsure about?" This opens a dialogue rather than ending the call.

3. Why is it important to state the price and then remain silent?

Silence demonstrates confidence. Filling the silence with justifications or "discounts" before they've even asked signals that you don't believe your service is worth the price.

4. Based on the "Steady State" income scenario, what is the estimated revenue for a coach with 5 clients?

$10,000 per month (assuming a $2,000 package rate). This allows for a significant income with a very manageable part-time workload.

Key Takeaways

  • The Discovery Call is a professional consultation, not a casual chat.
  • Rapport and deep listening are more important than your technical knowledge in the first 15 minutes.
  • Objections are signs of interest; handle them with empathy and biological facts.
  • High-ticket packaging ($2,000+) allows you to provide better support and achieve financial freedom faster.
  • Consistency in your "Discovery Call" process leads to a predictable, thriving business.
