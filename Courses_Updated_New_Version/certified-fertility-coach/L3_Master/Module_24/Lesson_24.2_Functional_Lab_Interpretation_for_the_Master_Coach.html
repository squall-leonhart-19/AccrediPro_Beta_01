Ovarian Reserve: Ratios Over Raw Numbers

In conventional medicine, a high FSH or low AMH is often treated as a final sentence. However, the Master Coach understands that these markers are dynamic, not static. We must look at the relationship between hormones.

1. The FSH/LH Ratio

On Day 3 of the cycle, FSH and LH should ideally be in a 1:1 ratio (e.g., FSH 6, LH 6). If FSH is significantly higher than LH, it indicates the brain is screaming at the ovaries to wake up (diminished reserve). However, if LH is significantly higher than FSH (e.g., LH 12, FSH 4), this is a classic hallmark of PCOS or metabolic dysfunction, regardless of the client's weight.

2. AMH and Biological Age

AMH (Anti-M√ºllerian Hormone) reflects the "fuel in the tank." While low AMH is concerning, it does not measure egg quality. A 42-year-old with an AMH of 1.0 has better odds than a 25-year-old with an AMH of 1.0 because the 42-year-old's eggs have had more time to accumulate mitochondrial wisdom (though also more potential for DNA damage). We use AMH to set expectations for IVF response, not to determine if a natural pregnancy is possible.

Coach Tip: The AMH Reframe

When a client is devastated by a low AMH score, remind them: "AMH tells us how many eggs are 'knocking on the door' this month, but it only takes one high-quality egg to make a baby. Our focus is on the quality of the environment that egg matures in."

Functional Thyroid Optimization

Conventional labs often only test TSH. If TSH is 4.0, the doctor says it's "normal." But for fertility, Normal is not Optimal.

1
TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)

Optimal Fertility Range: 0.5 ‚Äì 2.5 mIU/L. Anything above 2.5 increases the risk of miscarriage by 60% in the first trimester.

2
Free T3 (The "Gas Pedal")

This is the active hormone that tells the ovaries to produce progesterone. We want this in the upper 25% of the lab range.

3
Reverse T3 (The "Brake Pedal")

If Reverse T3 is high (usually due to stress or inflammation), it blocks T3 from entering the cells. A client can have "normal" TSH but "hypothyroid" ovaries.

Coach Tip: The Stress Connection

If you see a high Reverse T3, don't just reach for supplements. Ask about their Module 2: Emotional Resilience practices. High cortisol is the primary driver of T4 converting into Reverse T3 instead of active T3.

A standard semen analysis only looks at "count, move, and shape." It does not look at the cargo inside the sperm. Sperm DNA Fragmentation (SDF) is a critical Master-level marker.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is an LH/FSH ratio of 3:1 (e.g., LH 15, FSH 5) significant in a fertility assessment?

It is a strong indicator of PCOS or insulin resistance. High LH prevents the "FSH window" from opening properly, which can lead to poor egg maturation or lack of ovulation altogether.

2. A client's TSH is 3.8. Her doctor says she is "fine." What is the Master Coach's concern?

For fertility and pregnancy maintenance, the optimal TSH is below 2.5. A TSH of 3.8 is "subclinical" but associated with a significantly higher risk of miscarriage and impaired follicular development.

3. Which DUTCH metabolite is known as the "toxic" pathway that can damage egg DNA?

The 4-OH (4-hydroxyestrone) pathway. If not properly methylated, it can create quinones that directly damage the DNA of the developing oocyte.

4. When should a Sperm DNA Fragmentation (DFI) test be prioritized?

It should be prioritized in cases of recurrent pregnancy loss (RPL), "unexplained" infertility, or multiple failed IVF/IUI cycles where embryos stop developing at the blastocyst stage (Day 3 to Day 5).

Key Takeaways for the Master Coach

  • Ratios Reveal the Truth: Never look at a single hormone in isolation. The relationship between FSH, LH, and AMH tells the real story of the ovarian environment.
  • Metabolism Matters: Use the DUTCH test to identify why a client has estrogen dominance (is it poor Phase I detox or poor Phase II methylation?).
  • Thyroid is Non-Negotiable: Demand "Optimal" over "Normal." Target a TSH < 2.5 and robust Free T3 levels.
  • Include the Partner: DNA fragmentation is the "hidden" male factor. If the cargo is damaged, the pregnancy will likely not sustain.
  • Advocacy is a Skill: Empower your clients with scripts that use clinical terminology to bridge the gap with their medical providers.
