Section 3: Your Teaching Approach

In supervision, we use the Socratic Method. Instead of telling Sarah what to do, we ask questions that lead her to the answer. This builds her clinical self-efficacy.

1

Validate the Emotion

Acknowledge her care for the client. "Sarah, I can hear how much you want Maya to succeed. That empathy is your greatest strength."

2

Query the Assessment

"Before we look at supplements, let's look at the data. Have we seen Maya's latest Vitamin D or fasting insulin levels? What about her thyroid panel?"

3

Reinforce Scope of Practice

Remind her that if we are "guessing" with aggressive herbs, it's time to refer for updated labs or collaborate with an ND/MD.

Section 5: Supervision Best Practices

Professional supervision is a distinct skill set from coaching. To be an effective mentor, you must adhere to these industry standards:

  • Contractual Clarity: Always have a written agreement for supervision that defines your role, fees (Master Practitioners often charge $200-$350/hour for this), and confidentiality.
  • Safe Space: Ensure the mentee feels safe admitting "I don't know" or "I made a mistake."
  • Evidence-Based Focus: Always pull the conversation back to the AccrediPro protocols and peer-reviewed science.
  • Boundary Setting: If a mentee begins treating the session like their own personal therapy, gently redirect them back to the client's case.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the Socratic Method in clinical supervision?

The goal is to guide the mentee to discover the answer themselves through targeted questioning, which builds their own clinical reasoning skills and self-efficacy rather than creating dependency on the mentor.

2. If a mentee wants to suggest a high-risk herb without proper lab data, what is your primary responsibility?

Your responsibility is to reinforce Scope of Practice and client safety. You must guide the mentee to recognize that "guessing" is not professional practice and suggest referring for labs or collaborating with a medical professional first.

3. True or False: Supervision should focus exclusively on the mentee's clinical mistakes.

False. Effective supervision must balance critical inquiry with validation of the mentee's strengths and "wins" to prevent burnout and build confidence.

4. What is a "Feedback Sandwich" in a Master-level context?

It is a structured way to deliver feedback: Start with Validation (praising what went well), move to Critical Inquiry (addressing the gap/mistake), and end with Strategic Empowerment (creating a clear, confident plan for next steps).
