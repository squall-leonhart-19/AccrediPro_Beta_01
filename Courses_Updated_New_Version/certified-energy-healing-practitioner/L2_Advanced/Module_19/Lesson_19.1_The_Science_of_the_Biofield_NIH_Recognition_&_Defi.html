The 1994 NIH Landmark Conference

The turning point for energy medicine occurred in 1994. Prior to this date, terms like "aura," "energy healing," or "laying on of hands" were largely dismissed by the scientific establishment as unmeasurable and therefore non-existent. However, a panel of scientists convened by the Office of Alternative Medicine (now the NCCIH) at the National Institutes of Health sought to establish a rigorous framework for studying these phenomena.

This conference didn't just discuss techniques; it established a new category of medicine. By creating the term "Biofield," the NIH provided a scientific "home" for therapies like Reiki, Therapeutic Touch, and the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢. This was a strategic move to move away from culturally specific terms like Qi or Prana toward a more neutral, biophysical descriptor.

Defining the Biofield: The NCCIH Standard

According to the National Center for Complementary and Integrative Health (NCCIH), the biofield is defined as:

"A massless field, not necessarily electromagnetic, that surrounds and permeates living bodies and affects the body's health."

This definition is profound for three reasons:

  • "Massless Field": It acknowledges that energy doesn't require physical "stuff" to exist or exert influence.
  • "Not Necessarily Electromagnetic": It leaves the door open for "subtle energies" that current instruments may not yet be sensitive enough to measure.
  • "Surrounds and Permeates": It confirms the holographic nature of the human energy system‚Äîit is both inside us and around us.
