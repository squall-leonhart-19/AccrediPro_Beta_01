Assessing the 12 Primary Meridians: Excess vs. Deficiency

In advanced profiling, we look beyond whether a meridian is "blocked." We must determine the quality of the flow. In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) philosophy, which underpins our meridian work, we categorize these states as Shi (Excess) and Xu (Deficiency).

An Excess state is characterized by too much energy, often manifesting as heat, inflammation, or acute pain. A Deficiency state is characterized by "hollowness," lack of vitality, and chronic, dull symptoms. As a practitioner, your hands (or pendulum) will feel a distinct difference: Excess feels "pushy" or dense, while Deficiency feels like a "sinkhole" or cold spot.

Meridian State Tactile Sensation Common Manifestations Energetic Goal
Excess (Shi) Repelling, hot, buzzing, hard Irritability, acute pain, inflammation Sedate / Disperse
Deficiency (Xu) Pulling, cold, empty, soft Fatigue, depression, chronic weakness Tonify / Nourish

When profiling the meridians, always start with the Triple Warmer. In our modern high-stress society, the Triple Warmer is almost always in a state of Excess. If you don't address this first, the other meridians will not hold their calibration.

Quantifying Chakra 'Texture'

Profiling is not just about "open" or "closed." We use Textural Analysis to determine how the energy is behaving within the vortex. This requires a high degree of sensory perception (The Clairs).

  • Congested Texture: Feels thick, like moving your hand through molasses. This indicates suppressed emotions or stagnant "old" energy that hasn't been processed.
  • Depleted Texture: Feels "thin" or "wispy." The chakra is spinning, but there is no "fuel." Common in cases of burnout or long-term illness.
  • Fragmented Texture: Feels jagged, static-y, or "broken." This is often a sign of trauma (recent or ancestral). The energy isn't a cohesive vortex; it's scattered.

Using the 24-Hour 'Organ Clock'

Temporal patterns are the "secret weapon" of the master profiler. If a client tells you they wake up every night between 1:00 AM and 3:00 AM, you don't even need to touch them to know their Liver Meridian is likely in a state of Excess.

Coach Tip

Always ask your clients: "What time of day do you feel your lowest energy?" and "Do you wake up at the same time every night?" This data point is often more accurate than a manual scan because it reflects the body's natural circadian rhythm.

Key Temporal Markers:

  • 3 AM - 5 AM (Lung): Grief, sadness, or respiratory issues.
  • 7 AM - 9 AM (Stomach): Anxiety about the day, "disgust," or digestive upset.
  • 1 PM - 3 PM (Small Intestine): Difficulty "sorting" through life's choices; afternoon slump.
  • 9 PM - 11 PM (Triple Warmer): Difficulty winding down; "tired but wired" state.
