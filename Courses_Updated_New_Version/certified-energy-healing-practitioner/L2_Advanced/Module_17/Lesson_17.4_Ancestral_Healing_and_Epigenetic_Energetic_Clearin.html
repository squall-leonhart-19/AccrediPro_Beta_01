The Science of Epigenetic Memory

For decades, the idea of "ancestral healing" was relegated to the realm of mysticism. However, modern epigenetics provides a biological mechanism for what energy healers have observed for centuries. Epigenetics is the study of how behaviors and environment can cause changes that affect the way your genes work.

A landmark 2013 study by Dias and Ressler demonstrated that mice trained to fear a specific odor passed that fear response down to two subsequent generationsâ€”even though the offspring had never encountered the odor themselves. In humans, similar patterns are observed in the descendants of Holocaust survivors and those who lived through the Dutch Hunger Winter.

Did You Know? A meta-analysis of transgenerational trauma suggests that energetic signatures of extreme stress can alter the methylation patterns of the DNA, effectively "tagging" the biofield with a predisposition for hyper-vigilance or chronic depletion.
