The Neurobiology of Sensitivity

Sensitivity is not a "condition" or a "disorder"; it is a distinct neurobiological trait known as Sensory Processing Sensitivity (SPS). Research by Dr. Elaine Aron and others has shown that HSPs possess a more reactive central nervous system and a hyper-perceptive thalamus‚Äîthe "switchboard" of the brain.

In a 2014 study published in Brain and Behavior, researchers used fMRI to show that HSPs have significantly higher activation in brain regions associated with empathy, awareness, and action planning when exposed to emotional stimuli. For the energy practitioner, this means the HSP client isn't "imagining" the energy; their brain is literally wired to process it with 10x the intensity of a non-HSP.

Refining the 'Open' Step for HSPs

In Module 2, we learned to Open the pathways (Sushumna Nadi). For a standard client, this might be a vigorous clearing. For an HSP, a sudden opening can cause a "healing crisis" (nausea, dizziness, or emotional flooding).

The 'Slow-Release Gate' Protocol:

  1. Peripheral First: Clear the hands and feet chakras before touching the central pillar. This creates "exit points" for the energy.
  2. Micro-Dosing Energy: Use 30-second bursts of Open techniques followed by 60 seconds of Unify (smoothing).
  3. Vagal Toning: Have the client hum or use "Om" during the opening to keep the parasympathetic nervous system engaged.
