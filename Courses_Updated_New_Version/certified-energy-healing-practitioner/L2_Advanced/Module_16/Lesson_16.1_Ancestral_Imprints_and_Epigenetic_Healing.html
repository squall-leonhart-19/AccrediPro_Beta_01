Research has demonstrated that trauma can leave chemical marks on genes, which are then passed down through generations. A landmark study by Rachel Yehuda found that children of Holocaust survivors had altered cortisol levels, predisposing them to anxiety disorders despite never experiencing the trauma themselves.

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we view these epigenetic markers as energetic "tags" in the biofield. These tags act as filters, coloring the client's perception of reality and attracting specific life circumstances that mirror the original ancestral trauma.
