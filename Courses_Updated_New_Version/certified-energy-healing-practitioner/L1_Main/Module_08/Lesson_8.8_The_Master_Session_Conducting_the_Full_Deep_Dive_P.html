Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

  • Over-Explaining: Don't give a 20-minute lecture on quantum physics. Answer the client's immediate pain point first.
  • Dismissing Medical Care: Never say "You don't need your doctor." Instead, use "This is a perfect complement to the care you're already receiving."
  • Using "Woo-Woo" Jargon Too Early: Avoid terms like "ascension," "starseed," or "lightbody" unless the client uses them first.
  • Apologizing for the Price: If Elena asks the cost, state it clearly without adding "I know it's a lot." Your work has immense value.

The goal is to bridge the client's physical symptoms (tension/fatigue) to their energetic state, showing them that you are addressing the root cause, not just the symptom.

While massage works on the physical tissue, energy medicine works on the field that informs that tissue. It helps the relaxation "stick" by addressing the nervous system's stress patterns.
