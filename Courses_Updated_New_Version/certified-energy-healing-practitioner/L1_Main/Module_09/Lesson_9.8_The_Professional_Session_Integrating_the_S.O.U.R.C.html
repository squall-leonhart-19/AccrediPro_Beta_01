Common Pitfalls to Avoid

As you transition into advanced energy work, watch out for these four common mistakes that can undermine your legitimacy and drain your battery:

  • Over-Explaining: You don't need to give a 20-minute lecture on quantum physics. Keep your explanations grounded and client-focused.
  • The "Healer" Trap: Avoid saying "I will heal you." Instead, use "We will facilitate the clearing of these energetic blocks." It places the power back with the client.
  • Ignoring Your Own "S": If you feel your energy dropping during a practice session, pause. Take a breath. Re-shield. Your client will respect the authenticity.
  • Apologizing for Price: Never say "I know it's expensive, but..." State your fee ($150, $250, $500) as a neutral fact, just like a doctor or an attorney would.

Check Your Understanding

1. What does it mean when a client like Elena feels her client's anxiety in her own chest?

It indicates a lack of the "S" (Shielding) dynamic, where the practitioner is "absorbing" energy rather than "observing" it. This leads to empathetic burnout.

2. Why is "over-explaining" considered a coaching pitfall?

It often stems from the coach's own imposter syndrome and can overwhelm the client, shifting the focus from the client's healing to the coach's need for validation.

3. What is the benefit of using downward inflection at the end of your sentences?

Downward inflection projects authority, confidence, and professional legitimacy, helping the client feel safe in your leadership.

4. How does the S.O.U.R.C.E. framework assist in justifying professional rates?

It provides a structured, repeatable, and "expert" methodology that demonstrates a higher level of skill and predictable outcomes compared to unstructured "energy work."
