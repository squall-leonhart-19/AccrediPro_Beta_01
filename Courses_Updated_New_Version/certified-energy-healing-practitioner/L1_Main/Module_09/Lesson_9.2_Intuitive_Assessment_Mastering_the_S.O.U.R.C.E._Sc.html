Scanning the 7 Primary Chakras

A professional scan requires more than just knowing if a chakra is "open" or "closed." We must assess Structural Integrity. Think of a chakra as a high-precision turbine.

The Assessment Criteria

When your hand hovers 3-5 inches above a chakra, look for these three metrics:

  1. Rotational Velocity: Is the spin sluggish or hyper-active? Sluggishness often correlates with physical fatigue, while hyper-activity correlates with anxiety.
  2. Directionality: While "clockwise" is the standard for healthy intake, "counter-clockwise" spin often indicates the body is trying to release or purge energy (common during detox or emotional processing).
  3. Structural Integrity: Does the chakra feel "wobbly" or off-center? A wobbly Heart Chakra often appears in clients experiencing grief or relationship instability.
Coach Tip

Always scan from the Root up to the Crown first to establish a baseline, then scan from Crown down to Root to identify where the "flow" gets interrupted. This dual-direction scan is a hallmark of the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢.
