The Seven Layers: A Structural Overview

The human biofield is not a monolithic cloud of energy; it is a highly organized, interpenetrating system of seven distinct layers. Each layer serves a specific purpose in the client's overall health, ranging from physical maintenance to spiritual connection.

As a practitioner, you must understand that these layers are not like "onion skins" that sit on top of each other. Instead, they occupy the same space but vibrate at different frequencies. The higher-frequency layers penetrate through the lower-frequency layers and the physical body.

Structural vs. Fluid Energetic Imbalances

One of the most critical distinctions you will make during the Scan phase is whether an imbalance is structural or fluid.

Fluid Imbalances

These are transient. They are the "weather" of the biofield. An argument with a spouse, a stressful commute, or a temporary illness creates fluid stagnation. These often clear easily during the Open (O) phase of the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢.

Structural Imbalances

These are the "geology" of the biofield. They are deep-seated patterns often held in the Etheric Template (5th layer) or the Mental Layer (3rd layer). They feel "harder," "colder," or more "fixed" to the practitioner's hand or intuitive sight. These require repeated Calibration (C) and Rejuvenation (R) to shift.

Coach Tip

Don't get discouraged if a client's "heavy spot" returns after the first session. If it's a structural imbalance, you are essentially "re-training" the energy field to hold a new shape. Consistency is the key to professional-grade results.

The Impact of Auric Tears

An auric tear is a localized rupture in the protective membranes of the biofield layers. These are most common in the 1st (Etheric) and 7th (Ketheric) layers.

Causes of Auric Tears:

  • Sudden physical trauma or surgery.
  • Severe emotional shock or grief.
  • Prolonged use of certain medications or anesthesia.
  • Extreme "empathic leakage" (common in nurses and teachers).

When a client has an auric tear, they cannot hold a Calibrated (C) frequency. It is like trying to fill a bucket with a hole in the bottom. You may give them a high-vibrational boost, but they "leak" it out within hours, leading to the "rollercoaster" effect where they feel great after a session but crash shortly after.
