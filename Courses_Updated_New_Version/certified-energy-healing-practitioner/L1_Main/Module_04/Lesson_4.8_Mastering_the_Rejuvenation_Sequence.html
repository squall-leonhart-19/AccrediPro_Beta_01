Common Coaching Pitfalls in Module 4

Even experienced practitioners fall into these traps when working in the Rejuvenate phase:

  • The "Action" Trap: Assigning too many "homework" tasks. Rejuvenation requires less, not more.
  • The "Fix-It" Reflex: Trying to "clear" the feeling of emptiness instead of honoring it as a stage of growth.
  • Vocal Over-Stimulation: Speaking too quickly or too loudly, which keeps the client's nervous system in a state of "alertness."
  • Ignoring the Body: Staying too much in the "mind" (talking about feelings) rather than the "soma" (feeling the energy in the body).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the feeling of "emptiness" common in the Rejuvenate phase?

It occurs because the stagnant, heavy energy has been cleared, and the client's system hasn't yet integrated the new, higher-frequency energy. It is a transitional "void" that allows for deep restorative rest.

2. What is the primary role of the coach's voice during a restorative session?

The voice acts as a frequency generator to induce parasympathetic dominance. By slowing down, lowering pitch, and using pauses, the coach helps the client's nervous system shift into "Rest and Digest" mode.

3. Which physiological sign indicates a successful nervous system reset?

Spontaneous sighing or deep, involuntary breaths are primary indicators that the autonomic nervous system has shifted from sympathetic (alert) to parasympathetic (restorative) mode.

4. How should "homework" be handled in Module 4?

Homework should be minimized or reframed as "non-doing" activities, such as 5 minutes of silent sitting, restorative baths, or gentle grounding, to avoid triggering the client's "achievement" or "doing" reflex.

Key Takeaways

  • Rejuvenation is an allowing process, not a doing process.
  • Your vocal frequency and presence are your primary coaching tools in this phase.
  • Always look for physiological cues (sighing, softening) to gauge session depth.
  • Help clients reframe "emptiness" as "peaceful space" for integration.
  • Protect the client's newly cleared energy by discouraging immediate over-activity.
