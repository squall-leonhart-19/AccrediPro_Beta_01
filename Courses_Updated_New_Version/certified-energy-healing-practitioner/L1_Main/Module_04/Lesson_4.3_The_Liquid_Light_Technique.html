Identifying the 'Saturation Point'

A common mistake among novice practitioners is over-channeling. Just as a plant can only absorb so much water before the soil becomes waterlogged, the human biofield has a Saturation Point. This is the moment when the clientâ€™s field has reached its maximum capacity for that session.

How to Sense Saturation:

  • Tactile Repulsion: Your hands may feel a slight "push back" or resistance from the client's field.
  • Temperature Change: The area being treated may suddenly shift from "drawing in" heat to feeling neutral or even slightly cool.
  • The "Sponge" Effect: In the beginning, the field feels "thirsty" (absorptive). At saturation, it feels "slippery" or "bouncy."

Sensing the 'Density Shift'

As you progress from the Open phase (Module 2) to the Rejuvenate phase, you must develop the sensory acuity to detect the Density Shift. Clearing energy (Open) creates a sense of "lightness" and "space." Rejuvenating energy (Liquid Light) creates a sense of "substance" and "fullness."

Imagine the difference between a room that has just been dusted (Open) and a room that has been filled with comfortable, beautiful furniture (Rejuvenate). One is about absence; the other is about presence.

Hands-On vs. Hands-Off Techniques

The delivery method of Liquid Light changes its "flavor" and absorption rate. Depending on your client's sensitivity and the specific organ being treated, you will toggle between these two modes.

1. Hands-On (The Anchor): Placing hands directly on the body (with permission). This is best for grounding the light into the physical template. It feels "heavier" and more comforting. Use this for the adrenals and the lower abdomen.

2. Hands-Off (The Radiator): Hovering 3-6 inches above the body. This allows the Liquid Light to saturate the emotional and mental layers of the auric field first. It feels "lighter" and more expansive. Use this for the brow chakra (Pituitary) and the heart.

Coach Tip: Technique Refinement

If a client feels "jittery" during the infusion, pull your hands further away. If they feel nothing, move closer or use light physical contact to "bridge" the energy into their nervous system.
