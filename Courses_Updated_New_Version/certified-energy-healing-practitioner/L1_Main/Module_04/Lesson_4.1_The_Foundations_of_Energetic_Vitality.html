The Physiology of Prana and Qi Storage

To understand vitality, we must look at how the body stores energy. In Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) and Ayurvedic science, energy is not just "present"; it is stored in specific reservoirs. These are the bio-energetic equivalent of biological batteries.

The Dantians and the Sushumna

The human biofield utilizes three primary storage centers known as the Dantians (in the Taoist tradition) or specific Chakra junctions (in the Vedic tradition). These centers hold Jing (essence), Qi (energy), and Shen (spirit).

  • Lower Dantian (The Battery): Located approximately two inches below the navel, this is the primary reservoir for physical vitality.
  • Middle Dantian (The Hearth): Located at the heart center, this stores emotional and relational energy.
  • Upper Dantian (The Beacon): Located at the "third eye," this stores cognitive and spiritual energy.

When a client is "burnt out," it is often because these storage centers have been depleted to critical levels, leaving the physical body to run on metabolic reserves alone, which leads to rapid aging and cellular oxidative stress.

Personal Energy vs. Universal Life Force

This is perhaps the most important distinction in professional energy medicine. Personal energy is finite; Universal Life Force is infinite.

Personal Energy (ATP/Metabolic): This is generated by the mitochondria through food, water, and oxygen. It is meant for physical movement, digestion, and thinking. If you use this for healing work, you will burn out.

Universal Life Force (Prana/Qi): This is the underlying "fabric" of the universe. It is the frequency that sustains all life. As a SOURCE practitioner, your job is to be a conduit, not a generator. The Rejuvenate phase teaches you how to plug the client into the "Universal Grid" so their own "batteries" can recharge.

Preventing Healing Crises via Stabilization

A "healing crisis" (or Herxheimer-like reaction in the biofield) occurs when a client releases more "energetic sludge" than their system can process. This often happens when a practitioner clears a major block (Open) but fails to Rejuvenate the surrounding field.

The Rejuvenate phase acts as a stabilizer. By filling the field with high-frequency energy, we raise the client's "energetic floor." This makes the release of old traumas or toxins much smoother. A rejuvenated field has the "cushion" necessary to handle deep transformation without crashing.
