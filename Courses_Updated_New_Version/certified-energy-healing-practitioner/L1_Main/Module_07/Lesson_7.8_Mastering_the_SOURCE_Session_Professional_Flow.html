4. Common Pitfalls

New practitioners often fall into these traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Trying to solve the client's life problems (like her divorce) instead of focusing on the energetic clearing.
  • Over-Explaining: Using too much jargon to prove your legitimacy. This usually stems from imposter syndrome and can overwhelm the client.
  • Rushing the Intake: Skipping the grounding portion of the conversation to get to the "hands-on" work.
  • Matching the Client's Vibration: If Elena is anxious, you might start speaking faster. Practice staying one octave lower and slower than your client.
Luna's Insight

In my first year, I used to talk way too much because I was afraid of the silence. Now, I know that the silence is where the energy actually shifts. Embrace the quiet.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Energetic Consent" a vital part of the coaching script?

It establishes professional boundaries, empowers the client, and ensures they are an active participant in their own healing, which increases the efficacy of the session.

2. What is the "Fix-It" Trap in energy coaching?

It occurs when a practitioner tries to provide life advice or solve external problems instead of focusing on the energetic blockages and allowing the client's own wisdom to emerge.

3. If a client is skeptical of "energy," what is a more professional way to describe the work?

Focus on the "biofield," "nervous system regulation," or "electromagnetic stress." Using scientific-adjacent language helps bridge the gap for analytical clients.

4. What does it mean to speak "one octave lower" than your client?

It is a grounding technique where the practitioner intentionally keeps their tone calm, low, and steady to help regulate the client's potentially anxious or frantic energy.

Key Takeaways

  • Your professional presence is just as important as your energetic technique.
  • Always ask for energetic consent before beginning any clearing work.
  • Avoid the "Fix-It" trap by focusing on the client's energetic field rather than their life drama.
  • Silence is a tool; use it to allow energy shifts to integrate during the conversation.
  • Success is measured by the client's physical release and your own energetic maintenance.
