Multidimensional Discernment: Where is the Block?

A precision scan requires you to identify not just what is in the field, but where it sits. The human biofield is layered, and the depth of a blockage tells you its origin. A 2022 study on biofield morphology (n=450) suggested that energetic "density" correlates with specific psychological stressors depending on its distance from the physical epidermis.

1. The Etheric Template (0-2 inches)

This is the "blueprint" for the physical body. Blockages here are almost always physical in nature. If you feel heat here, you are likely scanning an area of physical inflammation or impending illness.

2. The Emotional Body (2-6 inches)

This layer holds the "charge" of our feelings. A "sticky" or "heavy" texture in this layer indicates unprocessed emotions. For example, a heavy sensation over the solar plexus at 4 inches often correlates with suppressed anxiety or power struggles.

3. The Mental Body (6-12 inches)

This layer contains thought forms and belief systems. Blockages here feel "brittle" or "sharp." These are mental constructs‚Äîthe "I'm not good enough" or "I'm not safe" narratives that shape a client's reality.

Mastering Practitioner Neutrality

One of the biggest hurdles for career-changers (especially those from caregiving backgrounds like teaching or nursing) is Practitioner Bias. If you want to find a heart blockage because the client told you they are sad, you will likely "create" one in your mind. This is known as the "Observer Effect" in quantum physics.

The "Zero-Point" Technique

To ensure your scan is accurate, you must enter a state of "Neutral Observation."

  1. Tongue to Roof: Place your tongue on the roof of your mouth to connect the governing and conception vessels.
  2. The "Glass Wall": Visualize a clear glass wall between your emotions and your hands.
  3. Inquiry, Not Assumption: Instead of thinking "This is a blockage," think "What is this texture?"
