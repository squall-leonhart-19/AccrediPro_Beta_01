Clearing (Open) vs. Integrating (Unify)

It is vital for the professional practitioner to distinguish between these two phases. If you continue to "clear" a field that is already open but lacks cohesion, you may actually cause the client to feel depleted or ungrounded. This is a common pitfall for novice practitioners.

Clearing (Module 2): Focuses on the removal of Vrittis (fluctuations), stagnant Qi, and emotional congestion. It is subtractive in nature.

Integrating (Module 3): Focuses on the connection between points. It is additive and structural. We are building "bridges" between the heart and the gut, the crown and the root, and the inner self and the outer environment.

Identifying Markers of Fragmentation

Before you can unify a field, you must be able to recognize where it is broken. During your Scan (Module 1), you are now looking for specific "Cohesion Markers."

  • The "Split" Field: The upper body feels hot or buzzing, while the lower body feels cold or empty. This indicates a lack of vertical integration.
  • The "Leaky" Boundary: The client feels "too much" of other people's energy. Their field is not unified enough to maintain a protective barrier.
  • Asymmetric Flow: One side of the body (often the left/feminine or right/masculine) feels significantly denser than the other.
  • Disjointed Layers: The emotional layer feels "far away" from the physical body, often seen in clients with past trauma.
