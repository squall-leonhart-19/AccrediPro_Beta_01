Section 5: Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

New coaches often struggle with "over-explaining" or taking on the client's energy. Watch for these signals.

4 Common Practitioner Mistakes

  • The "Woo-Woo" Trap: Using too much jargon (e.g., "Your astral body is leaking into the 5th dimension") before the client is ready.
  • Energetic Sponging: Feeling Elena's "chest weight" in your own body and not clearing yourself afterward.
  • Fixing vs. Holding: Trying to "fix" her divorce instead of clearing the energy around the divorce.
  • Weak Boundaries: Letting the intake go for 45 minutes, leaving no time for the actual energy work.

Check Your Understanding

1. If Elena says she feels "foggy" and "indecisive," which chakra should you investigate first?

The Solar Plexus (Manipura). This is the seat of our "Will" and personal power. When it is deficient, clients feel "stuck," "foggy," or unable to take action.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Vulnerability Bridge" in a coaching session?

To normalize the client's experience and reduce the shame or "imposter syndrome" they feel about their symptoms, which immediately lowers their energetic defenses.

3. True or False: You should feel the client's pain in your own body to be an effective healer.

False. This is "Energetic Sponging." An effective practitioner remains an "Objective Witness," holding a stable frequency so the client can match it, rather than drowning with them.

4. How should you respond to a client who says their GP found nothing physically wrong?

Validate them by explaining that energy often shows imbalances before they manifest as physical pathology, and that their feelings are a valid "early warning system."
