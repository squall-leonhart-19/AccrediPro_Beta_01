Techniques for 'Tracing' and 'Flushing'

There is a vital distinction between tracing a meridian and flushing it. Tracing is maintenance; flushing is intervention.

Technique Direction Purpose When to Use
Tracing With the natural flow Maintenance & Strengthening Daily health, grounding, ending a session.
Flushing Against flow, then with flow Clearing "Dams" & Stagnation During the "Open" phase for chronic blocks.

The 3-Step Flushing Protocol

  1. Locate the Stagnation: Use the scanning techniques from Module 1 to feel for "cold" or "heavy" spots along the meridian path.
  2. The Reverse Flush: Move your hand 2-3 inches above the body backwards against the natural flow of the meridian one time. This "loosens" the energetic debris.
  3. The Strengthening Trace: Trace the meridian in its correct direction three times with intention and light, sweeping motions.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between tracing and flushing a meridian?

Tracing follows the natural direction of the meridian to strengthen and maintain health. Flushing involves moving backwards against the flow once to loosen stagnation, followed by tracing forward three times to clear and restore flow.

2. What is the TCM term for energetic stagnation or constraint?

The term is "Yu." It represents the "dams" in the river of Qi that lead to physical pain and emotional blocks.

3. Why is it important to clear the central pillar BEFORE flushing meridians?

The central pillar (Sushumna) is the primary source of energy. If the "power station" is blocked, flushing the "wires" (meridians) will have limited effect because there is no power to fill the newly opened pathways.

4. Where are "Source Points" typically located?

Source Points (Yuan Points) are generally found in the extremities, specifically around the wrists and ankles, where the meridian's primordial energy is most accessible.
