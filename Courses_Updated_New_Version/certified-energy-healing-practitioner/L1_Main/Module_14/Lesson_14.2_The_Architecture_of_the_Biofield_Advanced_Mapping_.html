Structural vs. Functional Imbalance

As an advanced practitioner, you must differentiate between a "temporary cloud" and a "permanent blockage." This distinction is critical for setting client expectations and choosing the right S.O.U.R.C.E. intervention.

Functional Imbalances (Acute): These are transient. They are often the result of a bad day, a temporary stressor, or a minor illness. In your scan, they feel like "surface static" or a light mist. They clear easily with a basic O: Open technique.

Structural Imbalances (Chronic): These are deep-seated. They represent years of repressed emotion, ancestral imprints, or long-term physical trauma. In your scan, they feel like dense clay, cold voids, or sharp "shards." These require the U: Unify and R: Rejuvenate phases to fully resolve.

Categorizing Energy Density and Quality

To document your findings professionally, you need a standardized vocabulary. A 2022 study on biofield perception (n=150) found that trained practitioners consistently identified four primary "energetic textures."

  1. Congested/Dense: Feels like moving your hand through honey or thick fog. Indicates stagnant emotions or physical toxins.
  2. Empty/Depleted: Feels like a "hole" in the field or a sudden drop in temperature. Indicates burnout or a "leaking" aura.
  3. Hyper-Electric: Feels like "pins and needles" or static electricity. Indicates acute anxiety, inflammation, or over-active nervous system.
  4. Coherent/Fluid: Feels like a gentle, warm breeze or a smooth magnetic field. This is the goal of the C: Calibrate phase.
