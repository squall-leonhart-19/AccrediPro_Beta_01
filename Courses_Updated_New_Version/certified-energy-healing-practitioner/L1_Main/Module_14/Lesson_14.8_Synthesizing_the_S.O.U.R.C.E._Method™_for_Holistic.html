4. Common Mistakes for New Practitioners

In my practice, I've noticed that most "income plateaus" for new coaches happen because of these four energetic errors:

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to prove energy healing works by talking for 30 minutes. Let the *results* prove it.
  • Taking on the Client's "Stuff": Forgetting to ground yourself before the session, leading to you feeling exhausted afterward.
  • The "Discount Trap": Charging too little because you feel "new." Remember, you are providing a specialized service that saves the client months of traditional talk-therapy fatigue.
  • Vague Goal Setting: Not asking the client exactly what they want to feel by the end of the 60 minutes.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use professional terminology like "Biofield" with a client like Elena?

It bridges the gap between traditional wellness and energy medicine, building legitimacy and reducing the client's skepticism while justifying professional coaching rates.

2. What is the "Practitioner's Role" as defined in the script?

The practitioner is a "facilitator," while the client remains the "primary healer." This prevents the coach from taking on the client's energetic burden.

The Solar Plexus (power, identity, and confidence) and the Heart (emotional processing and grief).

A state where the client's energetic field feels "heavy" or "gray" due to prolonged survival mode, often resulting in physical exhaustion.

5. Success Markers: How to Know You Nailed It

A successful Level 1 assessment doesn't mean the client is "cured" in 10 minutes. It means:

  1. The client feels seen and validated (The "She gets me" moment).
  2. You feel energized, not drained, after the dialogue.
  3. The client understands the roadmap for the rest of the session.
  4. There is a clear energetic boundary established between you and the client.
