Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: The Mirror Scan (Solo)

Stand in front of a mirror. Perform the "S" scan on yourself. Move your hands 3-6 inches away from your body. Note where you feel heat, cold, or tingling. Speak your findings out loud: "I am sensing a coolness over my heart center."

Exercise 2: The Audio Audit

Record yourself reading the script above using your phone's voice memo app. Listen back. Do you sound rushed? Do you sound confident? Practice until your voice sounds like a "warm blanket"‚Äîcalm, steady, and authoritative.

Luna's Insight

Don't skip the audio audit! Many of us (especially women) have a tendency to end sentences with a rising pitch, making them sound like questions. Practice making your statements land with a flat or slightly downward pitch to convey authority.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client asks, "What if you find something bad during the scan?" what is the most professional response?

Reframe the findings as "information" or "weather patterns" rather than "good" or "bad." This reduces client anxiety and keeps the session focused on flow rather than judgment.

2. What is the recommended distance for your hands from the client's body during a biofield scan?

Generally 3 to 6 inches. This is where the human biofield is most easily detected by the nerve endings in the practitioner's palms.

3. Why is it important to ask "Does that resonate with you?" after sharing a finding?

It creates a "feedback loop" that involves the client in their own healing process and validates your energetic findings with their physical/emotional experience.

4. What is the "Psychic Trap" in energy scanning?

The tendency to make definitive "predictions" or "diagnoses" rather than reporting energetic sensations. Our role is to facilitate flow, not to diagnose medical or psychological conditions.

Key Takeaways

  • Scanning is about gathering information, not making judgments.
  • Use grounded language (heat, static, density) to help clients feel safe and understood.
  • The solar plexus is a common area for "held" energy in women aged 40-55 due to lifetime caretaking roles.
  • Your voice and presence are as much a part of the "Scan" as your hands are.
  • Confidence in the Scan leads to higher rebooking rates and professional legitimacy.
