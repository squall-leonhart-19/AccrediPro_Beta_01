Proper Hand Positioning and Optimal Distance

The success of an energetic scan depends on your "receptive state." If your hands are tense, your own nervous system creates "noise" that drowns out the client's signal. We use the Soft Hand Technique.

To achieve this, relax your wrists and slightly cup your palms, as if holding a delicate bird. The fingers should be close but not touching. This maximizes the surface area of the minor chakras in the palms and the nerve endings in the fingertips.

Coach Tip

Think of your hands like satellite dishes. If the dish is bent or rigid, the signal is distorted. Keep your breathing deep and rhythmic; as your parasympathetic nervous system activates, your piezoelectric sensitivity increases, making the biofield feel "thicker" and easier to read.

The Layering Technique: Depth Assessment

Effective practitioners don't just scan once; they scan in "layers." This is crucial for distinguishing between a physical symptom and an emotional root cause.

Step 1: The Outer Layer (12 inches). Scan for the overall "shape" of the aura. Are there leaks? Is the energy expanded or contracted?

Step 2: The Functional Layer (6 inches). This is where emotional patterns reside. Look for "static" or "vibrations" that feel like a buzzing phone.

Step 3: The Physical Interface (2 inches). Scan slowly over the organs and joints. This is where you find the "Hot" and "Cold" spots mentioned earlier.

Coach Tip

If you feel a "Cold Spot" at 2 inches but a "Hot Spot" at 6 inches, you are likely seeing a chronic physical depletion that the client is trying to "over-power" with emotional willpower. This insight alone establishes you as an expert in your client's eyes.

The Crown-to-Root Protocol

To ensure you don't miss anything, always follow a systematic path. Random scanning leads to random results. Professional practitioners use a standardized "Scan Path."

  1. Crown & Third Eye: Start at the head. Check for "mental fog" (density) or "overactive thoughts" (heat).
  2. Throat & Shoulders: A common area for "stickiness" (unspoken words).
  3. Heart & Lungs: Feel for "hollows" (grief) or "pulsing" (anxiety).
  4. Solar Plexus & Upper GI: The "power center." Look for "knots" or "resistance."
  5. Sacral & Root: Lower abdomen and hips. Check for "coldness" (lack of grounding or vitality).
  6. Feet & Grounding: Always finish by scanning the feet to see if the energy is "connecting" to the earth.

Calibrating Your Hands: Establishing a Baseline

How do you know if what you're feeling is "normal"? You must calibrate. Before the client arrives, spend 2 minutes scanning your own energy or even the energy of a neutral object (like a crystal or a plant).

Calibration Exercise: Rub your hands together for 10 seconds to activate the nerve endings. Pull them slowly apart. Feel that "cushion" of air? That is your baseline. During a session, anything that feels denser, thinner, hotter, or colder than that cushion is a "finding" that requires your attention.
