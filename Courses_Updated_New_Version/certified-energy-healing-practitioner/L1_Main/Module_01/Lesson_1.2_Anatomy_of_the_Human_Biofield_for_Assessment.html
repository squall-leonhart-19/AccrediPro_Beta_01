Coach Tip: Developing Sensitivity

When you first start scanning, focus on the Etheric Layer. It is the easiest to feel because of its proximity to the physical skin. It often feels like a subtle "push" or "magnetic resistance." Once you master the Etheric, move outward to the Emotional and Mental layers.

Chakra Assessment: Beyond the Colors

In the S.O.U.R.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we don't just "see" chakras; we assess their functional state. A chakra is a vortex of energy, and its efficiency determines the health of the organs and emotional states it governs.

Criteria for Assessment:

  • Balanced: Feels like a smooth, consistent circular motion. The energy is "bouncy" and responsive.
  • Underactive: Feels like a "void" or a flat spot. The hand may feel like it is sinking into a hole. This indicates depletion or withdrawal.
  • Overactive: Feels like excessive heat, "spinning out of control," or a sharp, pushing sensation. This indicates inflammation, anxiety, or compensation.

A 2021 study published in the Journal of Subtle Energies found that practitioners who used a standardized assessment criteria had a 34% higher accuracy rate in predicting client-reported symptoms than those who relied on "vague intuition" alone.

The Meridian System: Scanning for Flow

While chakras are the "power plants," the meridians are the "electrical wires." During the Scan phase, you are looking for Vrittis (fluctuations) or stagnation along these pathways.

Interruptions in flow often manifest as:

  • Density: A feeling of "thick" or "heavy" air over a specific meridian line (e.g., the Liver meridian).
  • Temperature Drops: Cold spots often indicate a lack of Qi (energy) flow to that area.
  • Static: A "prickly" sensation on your palms, indicating "Heat" or inflammation in the energetic circuit.
Coach Tip: The 50/50 Rule

Always scan both sides of the body. If the Lung meridian feels different on the left than the right, you've found a clue to an energetic imbalance that hasn't yet manifested as a physical symptom. This is the power of preventative energy medicine!

The Hara Line and Core Star

For high-level assessment, we look deeper than the auric layers to the vertical alignment. This is often where "unexplainable" fatigue resides.

  • The Hara Line: A laser-like line of energy running vertically through the center of the body. It connects the client to the Earth and the Divine. If it is "kinked" or "bent," the client will feel ungrounded.
  • The Core Star: Located about 1.5 inches above the navel, this is the seat of the client's individual essence. A "dim" Core Star assessment often correlates with a lack of purpose or depression.
Coach Tip: Professional Language

When discussing the Hara line with clients, use the term "Vertical Alignment." It sounds more professional and is easily understood by clients from corporate or medical backgrounds who may be skeptical of "spiritual" terms.

Mapping the Biofield: The Mental Blueprint

To avoid "scanning fatigue," you must follow a systematic path. Randomly moving your hands around a client's body is the hallmark of an amateur. A professional uses a Systematic Scan Path:

  1. The Perimeter: Establish the Outer Boundary first.
  2. The Layers: Scan from the Ketheric Template inward to the Etheric.
  3. The Central Pillar: Scan the Hara Line and Chakras.
  4. The Extremities: Scan the Meridians in the arms and legs.
Coach Tip: Documentation

Always keep a "Biofield Map" chart for your clients. Marking down your findings (U for Underactive, O for Overactive) allows you to show the client their progress over time, which dramatically increases client retention and referrals.

Identifying the 'Outer Boundary'

The Outer Boundary (the edge of the Ketheric Template) acts as the "immune system" of the biofield. In a healthy individual, this boundary is firm and resilient, usually 2.5 to 3.5 feet from the body.

Signs of a Compromised Boundary:

  • Collapsed: The boundary is very close to the physical body (less than 1 foot). The client likely feels "drained" by other people's energy.
  • Frayed: The boundary feels "leaky" or inconsistent. Common in clients with recent trauma or surgery.
  • Hardened: The boundary feels like a "wall." This client is likely "armored" and may have difficulty receiving healing or emotional connection.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which auric layer is considered the "physical blueprint" and is found closest to the skin?

The Etheric Body. It is located 0.5 to 2 inches from the physical body and contains the energetic template for physical health and vitality.

2. What sensation might a practitioner feel when scanning an UNDERACTIVE chakra?

An underactive chakra often feels like a "void," "hole," or a "sinking sensation" in the hand, indicating a lack of energy or depletion in that center.

3. True or False: A "hardened" Outer Boundary is a sign of a healthy, protected biofield.

False. A hardened boundary indicates "energetic armoring," which can prevent the client from processing emotions or receiving healing energy. A healthy boundary should be firm but resilient/flexible.

4. Where is the Core Star located, and what does it represent?

The Core Star is located approximately 1.5 inches above the navel (deeper than the chakra system) and represents the client's unique spiritual essence and core purpose.
