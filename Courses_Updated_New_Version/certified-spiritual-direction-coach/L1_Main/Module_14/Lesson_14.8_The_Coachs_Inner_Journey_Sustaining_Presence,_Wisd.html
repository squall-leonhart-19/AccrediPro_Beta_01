Eleanor, 52

Former teacher, now works part-time at a non-profit. Resides in Portland, OR. Married, two adult children.

Presenting Issue: Eleanor describes a profound sense of spiritual drift. She feels a "deep quiet ache in my soul" and is questioning the spiritual path she's followed for decades. She feels disconnected from her long-standing faith community and believes her daily life lacks a sense of sacredness. She mentions feeling "stuck" and guilty for her questioning.

Spiritual History: Raised in a traditional Christian faith, Eleanor has always been a dutiful and active participant. After her children left home for college, she found herself with more time but also more existential questions. Her fulfilling work at a non-profit doesn't fully address her inner longing.

Desired Outcome: "I want to reconnect with a sense of purpose and meaning. I'm open to exploring new ways of spiritual expression, and I long for peace amidst my questions. I want my spiritual life to feel integrated, not separate, from my everyday."

Key Statement: "I feel like I'm standing at a crossroads. The old paths don't feel right anymore, but I don't know where to turn. I feel guilty for even thinking this way."

Coach Tip

As you read Eleanor's profile, notice not just the words she uses, but the underlying emotions and spiritual longings. What is she truly seeking beyond the stated goals? This discernment of deeper currents is a hallmark of effective spiritual direction.

The Coaching Script

This script provides a framework for the initial part of a session with Eleanor. Your task is to embody the coach, Sarah, and practice these exchanges out loud. Focus on your tone, presence, and the intention behind each statement.

Your Coaching Script

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU (Sarah):
"Welcome, Eleanor. Thank you for trusting me with what feels like a very tender and significant time in your spiritual journey. I hear your longing for meaning and connection, and I want to assure you that these feelings, this questioning, are a natural and often profound part of spiritual growth."
ELEANOR:
"It's a relief to hear you say that. I've felt so much guilt, like I'm doing something wrong by questioning everything."
YOU (Sarah):
"That guilt is a heavy burden, and it's understandable given your background. But imagine for a moment that this period of questioning isn't a sign of failure, but an invitation. An invitation from your deepest self, perhaps even from the Divine, to explore new landscapes within. How does that idea resonate with you?"
ELEANOR:
"An invitation... I hadn't thought of it that way. It still feels scary, but... a little hopeful too."
YOU (Sarah):
"Scary and hopeful ‚Äì both can exist together. My role here isn't to give you answers, but to walk alongside you, helping you listen more deeply to your own inner wisdom, to what's stirring within you. What is it like to simply sit with that feeling of 'spiritual drift' right now, without needing to fix it?"
ELEANOR:
"It's... quiet. A little empty. But also, oddly peaceful, just acknowledging it."
YOU (Sarah):
"Yes, that quiet space can hold so much. Sometimes, our souls need a period of fallow ground before new growth can emerge. What are you noticing in that quiet, peaceful emptiness?"
Coach Tip

Notice how I used the client's own words, like "invitation," "scary and hopeful," and "quiet, peaceful emptiness." This technique, known as reflective listening, validates their experience and encourages deeper exploration. It shows you're truly hearing them.

Coach Tip

One of the most powerful tools in spiritual direction is holding silence. After a client shares something profound, resist the urge to fill the void immediately. A pause allows them to sit with their own words, to feel their own emotions, and to listen for deeper insights. Aim for a comfortable, receptive silence.

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

As you practice, it's helpful to be aware of common challenges new spiritual direction coaches face, and what indicates a truly impactful session.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid:

  • Offering Theological or Spiritual Advice: Your role is not to preach or teach doctrine, but to facilitate the client's own encounter with the Divine. Avoid statements like, "You should try daily meditation" or "Perhaps you need to read more scripture."
  • Minimizing the Client's Struggle: Phrases like "It'll get better" or "Everyone goes through this" can invalidate their unique pain. Instead, acknowledge and sit with their discomfort.
  • Rushing to Solutions or Practices: Spiritual journeys unfold organically. Resist the urge to quickly suggest new spiritual practices. The goal is discernment first, action second.
  • Imposing Your Own Spiritual Framework: Be mindful of your own beliefs. Your client's path may look very different from yours, and your role is to honor their unique journey, not direct it to your preferred destination.
  • Failing to Hold Silence: Silence is fertile ground in spiritual direction. New coaches often feel compelled to fill every pause. Practice allowing silence to deepen the space for reflection.
Coach Tip

Remember, you are a companion, not a guru. Your expertise lies in creating a safe, non-judgmental space where the client can hear their own inner voice and discern the movements of the Spirit within their life. Trust that they hold their own answers.

Markers of a Successful Spiritual Direction Session:

  • Client Feels Heard and Validated: They express a sense of relief, understanding, or simply feel deeply seen.
  • Client Articulates New Insight or Question: They leave with a deeper understanding of their inner landscape, or a new, more profound question to ponder.
  • A Sense of Openness and Possibility: The session fosters a feeling of spaciousness, hope, or a gentle shift in perspective, rather than a definitive "answer."
  • Increased Self-Compassion: The client shows signs of being kinder to themselves regarding their spiritual journey, releasing guilt or self-judgment.
  • Deepening Connection to Inner Wisdom: They feel more attuned to their own intuition or the gentle nudges of their spiritual experience.

Check Your Understanding

Test your comprehension of the core principles applied in this practice lab. Reflect on Eleanor's scenario and the coaching approach.

Check Your Understanding

1. Based on Eleanor's profile, what is the primary spiritual theme she is grappling with, beyond just "spiritual drift"?

Eleanor is primarily grappling with disorientation and a crisis of meaning, particularly as she navigates a new life stage (empty nest) and the questioning of long-held beliefs. Her guilt indicates a conflict between her authentic inner experience and external expectations or past conditioning.

2. If Eleanor were to ask you, "Sarah, what is your spiritual practice?" how would you respond as a Spiritual Direction Coach?

As a Spiritual Direction Coach, my response would gently redirect the focus back to Eleanor. I might say something like, "That's a thoughtful question, Eleanor. While I do have my own practices that sustain me, my role here is to focus entirely on your unique journey. What makes you ask about my practice?" This maintains the client-centered focus and avoids imposing my path.

3. What is the significance of "holding sacred space" in Eleanor's scenario, particularly given her feelings of guilt and questioning?

Holding sacred space for Eleanor means creating an environment of unconditional acceptance, safety, and non-judgment. Given her guilt and fear of questioning, this space allows her to be vulnerable and authentic without fear of condemnation. It validates her experience as sacred, enabling her to explore doubts and longings that might be suppressed elsewhere.

4. How can you encourage Eleanor to explore her "quiet, peaceful emptiness" without offering direct solutions or rushing her process?

To encourage exploration without rushing, use open-ended, invitational questions and deep listening. For example: "What does that quietness feel like in your body?" or "If that emptiness had a message for you, what might it be?" or "What new possibilities might emerge from this fallow time?" The goal is to invite curiosity, not to push for answers.

Key Takeaways

Key Takeaways
  • Spiritual direction coaching thrives on deep presence and active listening, allowing you to discern the subtle spiritual currents beneath the client's narrative.
  • Your role is to facilitate the client's own discernment, not to provide answers or impose your spiritual framework.
  • Embrace the power of silence; it is a sacred space for reflection, insight, and the unfolding of inner wisdom.
  • Normalize the client's spiritual struggles, guilt, or questioning as natural and potentially transformative phases of their journey.
  • Success in spiritual direction is marked by the client feeling heard, validated, and empowered to engage with their own spiritual path with greater self-compassion and openness.
