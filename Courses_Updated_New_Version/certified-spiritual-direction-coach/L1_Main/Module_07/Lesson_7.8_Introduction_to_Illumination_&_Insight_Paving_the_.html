Deepening the Discernment Process

After this initial exchange, your role is to guide Eleanor through a structured discernment process. This involves several key stages, drawing directly from the principles in Module 4.

Key Discernment Steps to Guide Eleanor Through:

  1. Clarifying the Call: Help Eleanor articulate the specific "calls" or longings she feels (e.g., creative expression, service, travel). Encourage her to describe them in detail, without judgment.
  2. Gathering Information: Explore what she already knows about these paths. What are the practical considerations? What are the emotional and spiritual implications?
  3. Seeking Inner Wisdom: Introduce practices like Imaginative Contemplation (from Module 4) or "Consolation and Desolation" to help her feel into each option. How does her body respond? What spiritual fruits or barrenness does each path evoke?
  4. Consulting Trusted Sources: Encourage her to speak with mentors, friends, or spiritual advisors (if she has them) who can offer objective perspectives, ensuring she doesn't rely solely on external validation.
  5. Testing the Spirits: If possible, suggest small, low-stakes experiments for each option (e.g., attending a writing workshop, volunteering for a day, researching a travel destination).
  6. Making a Provisional Choice: Guide her to make a tentative decision, holding it lightly and remaining open to further confirmation or redirection.
Coach Tip

When discussing "consolation and desolation" with a client like Eleanor, emphasize that these aren't about 'good' or 'bad' feelings, but about movements of the spirit that offer clarity or confusion regarding a path. It's a tool for spiritual intelligence, not emotional judgment.

Facilitating Embodiment and Integration

Once Eleanor gains clarity on a direction, the next crucial step is helping her to embody that insight. It's not enough to intellectually 'know' what to do; she needs to integrate it into her being and daily life (Module 5).

For Eleanor, this might look like:

  • Setting Intentions: Helping her articulate a clear, heart-centered intention for her chosen path.
  • Creating Rituals: Suggesting small daily rituals that reinforce her new direction (e.g., a morning meditation focused on her creative writing, a weekly walk in nature to connect with her spiritual self).
  • Body Awareness: Guiding her to notice how her body feels when she engages in activities aligned with her discerned path. Does it bring energy or drain it?
  • Journaling: Encouraging reflective journaling to track her experiences, challenges, and moments of spiritual connection as she moves forward.
  • Accountability: Offering to hold her gently accountable to her chosen path and to celebrate her small steps of progress.
Coach Tip

Embodiment often involves moving beyond planning to sensing and feeling. Ask questions like, "Where do you feel this calling in your body?" or "What does it feel like to imagine yourself fully living this purpose?"

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers in Discernment Coaching

As you practice, it's helpful to be aware of common challenges and what indicates a successful session.

Common Pitfalls for New Spiritual Direction Coaches:

  1. Giving Advice: Telling the client what you think they *should* do, rather than facilitating their own discovery. Eleanor is looking for her own answers.
  2. Rushing the Process: Pressuring the client to make a decision quickly. Discernment takes time and patience, especially for significant life changes.
  3. Over-intellectualizing: Focusing too much on logical pros and cons, neglecting the emotional, intuitive, and spiritual dimensions. Remember consolation/desolation.
  4. Ignoring Resistance: Not acknowledging or exploring the client's fears, doubts, or internal conflicts that might arise during discernment.
  5. Lack of Structure: Without a clear framework for discernment, the session can feel aimless, leaving the client more confused.

Markers of a Successful Discernment & Embodiment Session:

  • Client Clarity: Eleanor expresses a clearer understanding of her inner longings or a specific path forward.
  • Increased Confidence: She feels more confident in her ability to listen to her inner guidance and make choices.
  • Emotional Shift: She moves from feeling overwhelmed or anxious to a sense of peace, hope, or calm excitement.
  • Embodied Awareness: She can describe where she feels her spiritual connection or chosen path in her body.
  • Defined Next Steps: Eleanor identifies small, actionable steps she can take to begin embodying her discerned direction.
  • Empowerment: She feels empowered by her own insights, not dependent on your guidance for the answers.
Coach Tip

Trust the process. Spiritual discernment is not a linear path. There will be twists and turns, moments of clarity, and moments of doubt. Your unwavering, compassionate presence is the most powerful tool you offer.

1. What is Eleanor's primary presenting concern, and how does Sarah initially reframe it?

Eleanor's primary concern is feeling restless and lost after retirement, questioning her purpose. Sarah reframes this as "a profound invitation for spiritual growth" and "a deep longing for meaning and purpose," shifting the perspective from a problem to an opportunity.

2. What are two key principles of spiritual discernment that Sarah implicitly introduces in the initial script?

Sarah implicitly introduces the principles of "slowing down enough to truly listen" (active listening to inner guidance) and "discerning which paths truly align with your deepest spiritual self" (alignment with core values and spiritual identity).

3. If Eleanor expresses confusion about "consolation and desolation," how would you, as her coach, explain these concepts in a simple, relatable way?

"Eleanor, think of consolation as a feeling of peace, clarity, energy, and closeness to your spiritual center when you consider a path. Desolation, on the other hand, might feel like confusion, restlessness, a draining of energy, or a sense of spiritual distance. These aren't judgments, but signals from your spirit about what aligns or misaligns with your truest self. We use them to listen more deeply."

4. Eleanor decides she wants to pursue creative writing. What is one practical embodiment practice you might suggest to help her integrate this decision into her daily life?

One practical embodiment practice could be to establish a simple, non-negotiable "sacred writing time" each day, even if it's just for 15-30 minutes. This creates a ritual that physically and spiritually reinforces her commitment, signaling to herself and the universe that this path is real and valued.
Key Takeaways
  • Spiritual Direction Coaching empowers clients to discover their own inner wisdom and divine guidance, rather than receiving direct advice.
  • Effective discernment involves creating sacred space for listening, exploring various spiritual "calls," and using tools like consolation and desolation.
  • The transition from intellectual insight to embodied living is crucial, requiring practical rituals and body awareness practices.
  • New coaches should avoid common pitfalls such as giving advice, rushing the process, over-intellectualizing, or ignoring client resistance.
  • Markers of a successful session include increased client clarity, confidence, emotional shifts towards peace, and defined, actionable next steps for embodiment.
