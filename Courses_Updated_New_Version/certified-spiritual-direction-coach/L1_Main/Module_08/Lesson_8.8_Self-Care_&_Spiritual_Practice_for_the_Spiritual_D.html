The Coaching Script: Guiding Deep Discernment

Hereâ€™s a sample dialogue to help you initiate a discernment session with Eleanor. Pay close attention to the tone, pacing, and types of questions asked. This script is designed to establish trust, validate her experience, and gently open the door to deeper spiritual exploration.

Your Coaching Script

Practice saying these words out loud, imagining Eleanor is with you:

YOU:
"Welcome, Eleanor. Thank you for sharing your story with me. I hear a profound longing for meaning and purpose, and a courageous spirit seeking its next path. What you're experiencing is a sacred invitation, and I'm honored to walk with you as you explore it."
ELEANOR:
"A sacred invitation? I've mostly felt like I'm just lost. It's comforting to hear it described that way."
YOU:
"It is. Often, these feelings of restlessness or emptiness are not signs that something is wrong with you, but rather that something deeper is calling for your attention. It's your soul stirring, seeking alignment. In my practice, I've seen so many women, especially at this stage of life, find incredible clarity and renewed vitality when they answer this call."
YOU:
"Today, I'd like us to create a quiet, reflective space together. We'll begin by simply acknowledging where you are right now, without judgment. Then, we'll gently explore some of those inner stirrings you mentioned. Does that sound like a good starting point for our time?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, that sounds perfect. I'm ready for that kind of space."
YOU:
"Wonderful. Let's start with a moment of silence, inviting a sense of presence. Then, I'll ask you to simply share what comes to mind when you think about this 'call to something more meaningful.' There's no right or wrong answer, just what feels true for you in this moment. How does that feel?"
ELEANOR:
"It feels... hopeful. And a little bit scary, but mostly hopeful."
YOU:
"That's a very honest response, Eleanor, and perfectly natural. Discernment can feel like stepping into the unknown, but you don't have to do it alone. We'll navigate it together, one gentle step at a time. What is one small way you've noticed your spiritual connection trying to get your attention recently?"

Common Pitfalls for New Spiritual Directors

As you embark on your journey as a Certified Spiritual Direction Coach, it's natural to encounter challenges. Awareness of these common pitfalls can help you navigate them with grace and professionalism.

Coach Tip

Remember that spiritual direction is distinct from therapy or counseling. While therapeutic benefits may arise, your primary role is to help the client attend to their spiritual life and discern the movement of the Divine within their experience, not to diagnose or treat mental health conditions.

Pitfall Description Impact on Client/Session
1. Premature Advice Giving Offering solutions or telling the client what they "should" do, rather than facilitating their own discovery. Can shut down client's internal wisdom, create dependency, or make the client feel unheard.
2. Imposing Your Own Spiritual Framework Guiding the client toward your own beliefs, practices, or understanding of the Divine, rather than meeting them where they are. Alienates the client, undermines their spiritual autonomy, and can lead to inauthentic spiritual experiences.
3. Focusing Solely on the Practical Diving into logistical solutions or surface-level problems without exploring the deeper spiritual or existential questions. Misses the core purpose of spiritual direction, leaving the client feeling unfulfilled and their deeper longings unaddressed.
4. Lack of Comfort with Silence Filling every pause with words, questions, or affirmations, preventing the client (and coach) from deep listening and reflection. Rushes the process, inhibits introspection, and can prevent deeper insights from emerging. Silence is a powerful tool in spiritual direction.
5. Inadequate Boundary Setting Blurring the lines between coach and friend, extending sessions, or allowing the conversation to veer significantly off spiritual discernment. Can lead to burnout for the coach, confusion for the client, and compromise the professional integrity of the relationship.
Coach Tip

Embrace the "beginner's mind". Even as an experienced coach, approaching each client with curiosity and openness, assuming you have something new to learn, will deepen your presence and effectiveness.

Markers of a Powerful Discernment Session

How do you know if a session has been impactful? Beyond the client's direct feedback, there are subtle yet powerful indicators that you've facilitated a meaningful discernment experience.

  • Client Feels Deeply Heard and Validated: They express a sense of being understood, not just on an intellectual level, but in the depths of their being.
  • Emergence of New Language: The client starts to use new metaphors, images, or insights to describe their inner experience, indicating a shift in perspective.
  • Identification of "Stirrings" or "Invitations": The client can articulate specific feelings, intuitions, or recurring themes that suggest a deeper spiritual movement. This is the core of discernment.
  • Increased Self-Awareness: The client gains a clearer understanding of their values, longings, resistances, or patterns related to their spiritual journey.
  • Sense of Empowerment, Not Dependency: The client leaves feeling equipped to continue their discernment journey, not reliant on your answers, but trusting their own inner compass.
  • Commitment to a Next Small Step: Even if clarity isn't fully achieved, the client identifies a tangible, small action they can take to continue their exploration (e.g., journaling, spending time in nature, reflective prayer).
  • Sacred Space Felt: Both you and the client experience a sense of reverence, presence, and calm during the session, indicating that spiritual space was created.
Coach Tip

A truly transformative spiritual direction session often doesn't end with definitive answers, but with deeper, more meaningful questions. Guide your clients to embrace the journey of inquiry.

Check Your Understanding

1. In the practice scenario with Eleanor, what is the primary distinction between the coach's approach and what Eleanor previously experienced with her doctor or friends?

The coach's approach validates Eleanor's experience as a "sacred invitation" and focuses on creating a reflective space for spiritual exploration and discernment, rather than offering immediate practical solutions or dismissing her concerns as simple stress. This contrasts with advice to "eat less, move more" or other surface-level suggestions.

2. Why is it important for a spiritual director to be comfortable with silence during a session?

Silence is a crucial element in spiritual direction. It allows both the client and the director to engage in deep listening, process information, connect with inner wisdom, and create space for the Divine to speak. Filling every pause can rush the process, inhibit introspection, and prevent deeper insights from emerging.

3. Eleanor expressed feeling "hopeful and a little bit scary." How did the coach acknowledge and validate this dual emotion, and why is this important?

The coach responded by saying, "That's a very honest response, Eleanor, and perfectly natural. Discernment can feel like stepping into the unknown, but you don't have to do it alone." This validation normalizes Eleanor's feelings, builds trust, and reassures her that fear is a natural part of the discernment journey, while emphasizing the coach's supportive presence.

4. A client asks you, "What do you think I should do about my career? Should I quit and pursue my passion for art?" How would a spiritual director typically respond to avoid a common pitfall?

A spiritual director would avoid the pitfall of "premature advice giving." Instead of telling the client what to do, they might respond with a question that invites deeper reflection, such as: "What is your intuition telling you about pursuing art? What feelings arise when you consider that path, and what feelings arise when you consider staying in your current role?" or "What has your spiritual practice been inviting you to explore regarding your career?" The goal is to facilitate the client's own discernment, not provide answers.
Coach Tip

Your unique life experiences and spiritual journey are your greatest assets as a spiritual director. They allow you to hold space with authenticity and empathy, resonating with clients like Eleanor because you understand the human quest for meaning.

Key Takeaways

Your Practice Lab Essentials
  • Validation and Sacred Space: Begin by validating the client's experience and framing their search as a "sacred invitation," creating a safe, non-judgmental environment.
  • Empathetic Inquiry: Use open-ended questions that encourage deep spiritual reflection and help the client explore their inner landscape, rather than seeking quick answers.
  • Embrace Silence: Recognize silence as a powerful tool for introspection and divine connection, allowing insights to emerge naturally.
  • Focus on Spiritual Discernment: Keep the conversation rooted in the client's spiritual journey and inner stirrings, distinguishing it from practical problem-solving.
  • Trust the Client's Inner Wisdom: Your role is to guide, not to dictate. Empower the client to connect with their own spiritual authority and discern their path forward.
