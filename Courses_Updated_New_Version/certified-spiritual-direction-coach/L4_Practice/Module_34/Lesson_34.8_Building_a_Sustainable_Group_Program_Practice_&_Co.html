1. Your Ideal Group Program Prospect

Before you can enroll anyone, you need to know exactly who you're speaking to. For group programs, this means understanding not just individual needs, but also the collective desires and shared challenges that make a group setting appealing and effective. Presenting Situation: Eleanor feels a deep longing for spiritual growth and a more consistent practice since retiring, but she's feeling isolated. She's open to investing in herself but is cautious about one-on-one commitments due to past experiences feeling overwhelmed.

Key Motivators for a Group Program: Community, shared learning, accountability, structured guidance, affordability compared to 1:1, and a sense of belonging.

2. Crafting Your Group Program Pitch

Your pitch isn't about selling; it's about clearly articulating the transformational value of your group program and how it uniquely addresses the needs of someone like Eleanor. A strong pitch answers the unspoken questions in your prospect's mind.

  • Identify the Core Problem: What common spiritual challenge does your group address? (e.g., isolation, lack of consistent practice, difficulty discerning divine guidance).
  • Articulate the Unique Solution: How does your group program solve this problem in a way that individual coaching or self-study might not? Emphasize community, shared wisdom, and collective energy.
  • Highlight the Transformation: What will participants gain? (e.g., deeper spiritual connection, a supportive community, clarity on their path, renewed purpose, practical tools for daily spiritual living).
  • State the Structure & Logistics: Briefly mention format (e.g., 8-week online program, weekly live calls, private community group), duration, and what's included.
  • Call to Deeper Exploration: End with an invitation to discuss further, rather than a hard sell.

Coach Tip: Emphasize Community

For group programs, the sense of community is often a primary draw. Highlight how participants will benefit from shared experiences, diverse perspectives, and mutual support. This can be a powerful differentiator from 1:1 coaching.

3. The Group Program Discovery Call Script

This is where you practice leading a conversation, not just giving information. Your goal is to understand if your program is truly a good fit for the individual, and for them to understand if it's a good fit for them. Here‚Äôs a framework for a 30-minute discovery call:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure for Group Programs

1

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 min)

YOU: "Welcome, Eleanor! Thank you for scheduling this time. I'm excited to learn more about what you're seeking. To start, how did you come across my work, and what prompted you to explore a group spiritual direction program now?"
(Listen actively, acknowledge her journey, connect on shared values if possible.)

2

Phase 2: Understand Needs & Desires (10 min)

YOU: "Tell me more about your spiritual journey currently. What feels challenging or missing? What does 'deeper spiritual connection' look like for you, ideally?"
YOU: "You mentioned feeling isolated and wanting community. Can you elaborate on what kind of support and connection you're hoping to find in a group setting?"
YOU: "If you could fast-forward a few months and feel truly supported and connected in your spiritual life, what would be different?"
(Probe for specific examples, emotional impact, and future desires. Identify common themes that resonate with your group program's focus.)

3

Phase 3: Present Your Group Program Solution (10 min)

YOU: "Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is a deep desire for structured guidance, a supportive community, and practical tools to deepen your spiritual practice. Does that resonate with you?"
(Wait for confirmation.)
YOU: "Based on what you've shared, I believe my 'Awaken & Connect' group program could be a wonderful fit. It's an 8-week online journey designed specifically for spiritual seekers like you who crave community and consistent practice. We meet weekly on Zoom for guided spiritual exercises, shared reflection, and deep discussion, all within a confidential and sacred container. Participants consistently tell me they find profound connection and clarity."
(Briefly mention 2-3 key benefits, focusing on how it addresses HER stated needs.)

4

Phase 4: Transition to Enrollment & Next Steps (5 min)

YOU: "How does that sound to you, Eleanor? Does this align with what you've been looking for in a group program?"
(Pause for her response. Address initial questions.)
YOU: "If it feels like a good fit, I'd be happy to share the investment details and how to enroll. Would you like to hear more about that now?"
(This is a soft close, inviting her to the next step.)

Coach Tip: Active Listening is Key

During the discovery call, your primary role is to listen more than you speak. Ask open-ended questions and truly hear their answers. This not only builds trust but also gives you the language to describe your program in a way that resonates directly with their needs and desires.

4. Mastering Common Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

  • "I'm not sure a group is right for me; I prefer one-on-one."

    Your Response: "I completely understand that preference, and many people feel that way initially. What specifically makes you hesitant about a group setting? (Listen.) What many of my clients find surprising is the unique power of a group ‚Äì the diverse perspectives, the shared wisdom, and the deep sense of not being alone on their journey. While it's not one-on-one, the 'Awaken & Connect' program is intentionally kept small (typically 6-8 participants) to ensure everyone receives personalized attention within the group container. It offers a rich tapestry of support that 1:1 can't always replicate. Would you be open to experiencing that?"

  • "The price is a bit high for a group program."

    Your Response: "I appreciate your honesty, and investing in yourself is a significant decision. To help me understand better, are you comparing this to other group programs you've seen, or perhaps to other types of investments? (Listen.) Our 'Awaken & Connect' program is designed as a comprehensive 8-week journey, not just a series of calls. It includes weekly live sessions, a dedicated private community space, curated resources, and direct access to my guidance. Many participants consider it an investment in profound, lasting spiritual transformation and community support that truly changes their daily lives. What value would you place on feeling deeply connected and clear in your spiritual path?"

  • "I'm worried about confidentiality in a group setting."

    Your Response: "That's a very valid and important concern, Eleanor, and I want to assure you that creating a safe and confidential space is paramount in all my group programs. We begin every program by co-creating a sacred container and setting clear group agreements, with confidentiality being at the very top. Participants commit to honoring each other's privacy and sharing only their own experiences outside the group. My role as the facilitator is to uphold and protect that container fiercely. We cultivate a space of deep trust and respect, which is foundational to the transformative work we do together."

Coach Tip: Handle Objections with Curiosity

Instead of immediately defending your program, respond to objections with genuine curiosity. Ask clarifying questions to understand the root of their concern. This approach validates their feelings and allows you to address their specific hesitation more effectively.

5. Confidently Presenting Your Investment

This is often the most challenging part for new coaches. Remember, you are offering immense value and transformation. State your price clearly, confidently, and without apology.

  • Lead with Value: Reiterate the transformation and benefits they will receive.

    YOU: "Eleanor, if this sounds like the supportive community and guided spiritual journey you've been looking for, the investment for the entire 8-week 'Awaken & Connect' program, including all live sessions, the private community, and resources, is $597."

  • Offer Payment Options (Optional but Recommended): This can make it more accessible.

    YOU: "We also offer a convenient payment plan of two monthly payments of $310, making it easier to fit into your budget."

  • Pause and Listen: After stating the price, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let them process.

    YOU: "Does that feel accessible to you?"

  • Address Investment Objections: Refer to the strategies in the previous section. Focus on the long-term value and return on investment in their spiritual well-being.

Coach Tip: Practice Your Pricing Statement

Say your pricing statement out loud, confidently, many times. Practice in front of a mirror or with a peer. The more comfortable you are saying it, the more confident you'll sound to your potential clients. This isn't about being pushy; it's about owning the value of your work.

The Art of the Enrollment Call Close

The close is simply guiding your prospect to the next logical step. It should feel natural and empowering, not manipulative.

  • Confirm Readiness:

    YOU: "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, do you feel this 'Awaken & Connect' program is the right next step for you to find that community and deepen your spiritual practice?"

  • Clear Call to Action:

    YOU: "Wonderful! The next step is simple. I can send you the enrollment link right after this call. You can review all the details and complete your registration there. We start on [Date], and spots are limited to ensure an intimate group experience."

  • Address Any Remaining Questions:

    YOU: "Do you have any final questions for me about the program or the enrollment process?"

Not every enrollment happens on the first call. A thoughtful follow-up email with the program details, a summary of your conversation, and a clear call to action can significantly increase your conversion rates. Persistence, delivered with grace, pays off.

Check Your Understanding

1. Eleanor is hesitant about joining a group program. What is the most effective initial response to her concern, according to the lesson?

The most effective initial response is to validate her concern and then ask a clarifying question, such as, "What specifically makes you hesitant about a group setting?" This allows you to understand the root of her apprehension before offering solutions.

2. When presenting the investment for your group program, what is the recommended approach to ensure confidence and clarity?

The recommended approach is to lead with the value and transformation the client will receive, state the price clearly and confidently without apology, and then pause to allow the client to process the information. Offering payment options can also enhance accessibility.

3. You've just completed a discovery call for your 6-week "Inner Compass" group program (5 participants at $397 each). How much gross revenue would this single offering generate?

Gross revenue for this offering would be 5 participants * $397/participant = $1,985.

4. During a group program discovery call, what is the primary objective of Phase 2 ("Understand Needs & Desires")?

The primary objective of Phase 2 is to deeply listen and understand the prospect's current spiritual journey, their specific challenges, what they feel is missing, and what their ideal spiritual life or community experience would look like. This helps you tailor your program's presentation to their unique needs.

Key Takeaways

  • Understanding your ideal group program prospect is foundational to effective client acquisition, focusing on shared needs and the desire for community.
  • A compelling group program pitch highlights the transformational value and unique benefits of a group setting, addressing common spiritual challenges.
  • Structured discovery calls for group programs involve building rapport, deeply understanding collective and individual needs, presenting your solution, and guiding the prospect to the next step.
  • Handle objections with empathy, curiosity, and confidence, viewing them as opportunities to provide more information rather than outright rejections.
  • Confidently presenting your program's investment by leading with value and offering flexible payment options is crucial for successful enrollment.
  • Group programs offer significant income potential and scalability, allowing you to serve more clients and build a sustainable practice.
