1. Your Ideal Prospect Profile: Meet Eleanor

Before any call, it's essential to understand who you're speaking with. This helps you tailor your approach and speak directly to their deepest needs. For this lab, let's meet Eleanor.

Eleanor Vance, 52

Referral from a mutual acquaintance. Scheduled a discovery call after visiting your website.

Her Situation
Recently took early retirement from a demanding teaching career. Feeling a profound sense of loss of purpose and spiritual emptiness. Attends church but feels disconnected. Seeking "something more."
Budget Concern
"I'm on a fixed income now, so I need to be careful with my investments. Is this truly worth it?"
Decision Style
Analytical, values authenticity and deep listening. Needs to feel understood and see a clear path forward. Skeptical of "fluffy" spiritual talk.
Her Goal
"I want to rediscover my purpose, feel spiritually alive again, and find peace in this new chapter of my life."

Eleanor represents a common client archetype for spiritual direction coaches ‚Äì someone at a crossroads, seeking deeper meaning and connection. Your role is to meet her where she is, listen deeply, and offer a clear vision for how your guidance can support her journey.

Coach Tip

Always do a quick scan of your prospect's public profiles (LinkedIn, professional website) if available, before a discovery call. This isn't about judgment, but about understanding their background and potential values to better connect and tailor your initial questions. Remember, preparation breeds confidence.

2. Mastering the Discovery Call: A Step-by-Step Script

A well-structured discovery call isn't rigid; it's a framework that allows for authentic conversation while ensuring you cover all essential points. Here's a 30-minute structure to practice with Eleanor.

Your 30-Minute Spiritual Direction Discovery Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hello Eleanor, it's wonderful to connect! Thank you for making the time today. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear how you found me?"
YOU:
"Ah, [acquaintance's name] is a dear person. I'm glad she thought of me. So, Eleanor, tell me in your own words, what inspired you to seek out spiritual direction coaching at this particular time in your life?"
Phase 2: Understand Her Deepest Needs 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling a loss of purpose since retirement and a spiritual emptiness. Can you tell me more about what that feels like for you day-to-day? What does 'spiritual emptiness' mean in your experience?"
YOU:
"What have you tried so far to address this feeling? What has helped, even a little, and what hasn't resonated?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, if nothing were to change, what does life look like for you a year from now? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly feel spiritually alive and purposeful again, what would that look and feel like?"
Phase 3: Introduce Your Approach & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is a deep longing for renewed purpose, spiritual connection, and peace in this new life chapter. Does that resonate with your experience?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe my G.U.I.D.E. Framework‚Ñ¢ for spiritual direction coaching is precisely what you're looking for. We'd embark on a journey together, typically over 3-6 months, where we'd gently explore your inner landscape, discern divine whispers, and co-create practices to help you reconnect with your deepest self and purpose. Most clients begin experiencing a profound shift in clarity and inner peace within the first few sessions."
YOU:
"My role is not to tell you what to believe, but to create a sacred, confidential space where you can hear your own inner wisdom and God's call more clearly. It's a journey of deep listening, reflection, and intentional action."
Phase 4: Transition to Next Steps & Close 5 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on our conversation, do you feel this approach aligns with what you're seeking to achieve?"
YOU:
"Wonderful. If you're ready to explore this further, I can share details about my program structure, the investment, and how we can begin this transformative work together."

Coach Tip

Practice your summary in Phase 3 until it feels natural. This isn't just about showing you've listened; it's about giving the client the gift of being truly heard and understood, which builds immense trust. It also allows them to correct any misunderstandings before you offer your solution.

3. Navigating Common Objections with Confidence

It's natural for prospects to have questions or reservations. Viewing objections as opportunities for clarity, rather than roadblocks, is key. Here's how you might respond to Eleanor's potential concerns and others.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it. It's a big decision, especially with my new fixed income."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is indeed an important investment in yourself and your spiritual well-being. To help you reflect, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the program structure, the financial investment, or perhaps something else that's giving you pause? Knowing this helps me ensure I've provided all the clarity you need."
"I can just pray on my own, or talk to my pastor. How is this different?"
"That's a very valid question, and yes, personal prayer and pastoral care are incredibly valuable. Spiritual direction, however, offers a unique, dedicated space with a trained professional whose sole focus is to accompany you in discerning God's presence and action in your life. Unlike pastoral counseling, which often focuses on problem-solving, spiritual direction is about deep listening to your inner life and the Divine, without agenda. It's a consistent, confidential container for your spiritual growth that complements your existing practices, rather than replacing them. Many clients find this dedicated, unbiased companionship accelerates their journey in profound ways."
"I'm not sure I can commit to a long-term program right now."
"I appreciate your honesty about commitment, Eleanor. Spiritual growth is a journey, not a sprint, and my programs are designed to provide sustained support for lasting transformation. However, I do offer different engagement options, including an initial 3-month foundational package to help you experience the depth of this work. We can explore what feels most comfortable and sustainable for you. The key is consistent, intentional engagement to truly see shifts in your spiritual landscape."
"I've tried other 'spiritual' programs before, and they felt very superficial or didn't last."
"Thank you for sharing that, Eleanor. It sounds like you've had experiences where programs didn't meet your expectations for depth or lasting impact, and that's frustrating. What I offer is not a 'quick fix' or a program of prescribed beliefs. Instead, it's a deeply personalized, co-creative process rooted in ancient contemplative practices and modern coaching principles. We focus on your unique spiritual path, exploring your questions, doubts, and longings with reverence and discernment. My commitment is to genuine, sustainable transformation, not just temporary inspiration."

4. Presenting Your Investment Confidently

Once a prospect expresses interest, it's time to discuss the financial investment. This is often an area where new coaches feel hesitant. Remember, you are offering a profound, life-changing service, and your value is undeniable. Confidently stating your fees reflects your belief in your own expertise and the transformation you provide.

Key Principles for Presenting Pricing:

  1. Anchor High, Present Value: Always state the full value of your offering before the price. Reiterate the transformation, the benefits, and what's included.
  2. Be Clear and Concise: State the price directly, without apology or hesitation.
  3. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, stop talking. Let the prospect process the information.
  4. Offer Payment Options (if applicable): If you have payment plans, mention them as a way to make the investment more accessible.

Practice Script: Presenting Your Program to Eleanor

YOU:
"Eleanor, based on our conversation, I recommend starting with my Pathfinder Program. This is a 3-month journey where we meet bi-weekly for 6 sessions, each lasting 60 minutes. It includes personalized spiritual exercises, resources tailored to your unique path, and unlimited email support between sessions. The investment for the Pathfinder Program is $2,400. Many clients choose our convenient monthly payment plan of $800 over three months."
YOU:
(Pause. Allow Eleanor to respond.)
YOU:
"Does that sound like something that would work for you, Eleanor?"

Coach Tip

When stating your price, maintain eye contact (if on video) and a calm, confident demeanor. Avoid using phrases like "It's only..." or "My rates are..." Instead, frame it as "The investment for this transformative journey is..." This subtle shift in language reinforces value.

5. The Art of the Call-to-Action

A discovery call isn't complete without a clear Call-to-Action (CTA). You've built rapport, understood their needs, presented your solution, and addressed objections. Now, guide them to the logical next step.

Effective CTAs are:

  • Clear: No ambiguity about what to do next.
  • Direct: Ask for the commitment.
  • Low-Friction: Make it easy for them to say yes.

Practice Script: Closing the Call with Eleanor

YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for such an open and meaningful conversation today. I truly feel I can support you in rediscovering your purpose and spiritual vitality. If you're feeling ready to begin this journey, the next step is to complete our simple client agreement and make your initial payment. I can send you all the details immediately after this call, and we can schedule your first session. How does that sound?"
YOU:
(If she agrees) "Wonderful! I'm so excited for you. I'll send that over right away. Do you have any questions about getting started?"
YOU:
(If she needs more time) "I completely respect that. What would be a good timeframe for you to review the materials and make a decision? I'd be happy to follow up with you on to answer any further questions. Does that work?"

Coach Tip

Always aim to schedule the next interaction before ending the current one. Whether it's sending an onboarding link, booking the first session, or scheduling a follow-up call, having a concrete next step significantly increases the likelihood of conversion.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand Her Deepest Needs" phase of the discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply listen and uncover the prospect's core spiritual longings, challenges, and desired transformation. This phase focuses on empathy and understanding, allowing the coach to identify how their services align with the client's needs.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about her fixed income. How should you address this when presenting your program's investment?

When addressing Eleanor's concern, you should confidently state the full investment, then immediately offer payment plan options (e.g., monthly installments). Frame the cost as an "investment" in her spiritual well-being and reiterate the value and transformation she will receive, without hesitation or apology.

3. Eleanor asks, "I can just pray on my own, or talk to my pastor. How is this different?" What are key distinctions you would highlight?

Key distinctions include: spiritual direction is a dedicated, confidential, and unbiased space with a trained professional focused solely on discerning the Divine in her life; it's about deep listening and companionship, not problem-solving (like counseling); and it complements, rather than replaces, personal prayer or pastoral care, offering a unique container for accelerated spiritual growth.

4. After presenting your program and investment, what is the most crucial next step to take, even if the client doesn't immediately say "yes"?

The most crucial next step is to establish a clear, low-friction Call-to-Action (CTA). This might involve sending program details and a client agreement immediately, or, if they need time, scheduling a specific follow-up call or email to continue the conversation. The goal is to avoid an open-ended "I'll get back to you" and instead create a defined path forward.

Key Takeaways

  • A successful discovery call hinges on empathetic listening, understanding the prospect's deep spiritual longings, and clearly articulating how your spiritual direction coaching can fulfill those needs.
  • Follow a structured script (Rapport, Needs, Approach, Close) to ensure a comprehensive and professional conversation, allowing for authentic connection within a defined timeframe.
  • Address objections as opportunities for clarity and deeper connection, responding with confidence, empathy, and a clear explanation of the unique value of spiritual direction.
  • Present your program's investment with conviction, anchoring it in the profound transformation offered and providing clear payment options.
  • Always end with a clear Call-to-Action, guiding the prospect to the next logical step in their journey with you, whether it's enrollment or a scheduled follow-up.
  • Building a thriving practice with significant income potential is achievable through consistent client acquisition, driven by effective discovery calls and strong client relationships.
