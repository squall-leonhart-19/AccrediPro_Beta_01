Welcome, Future Spiritual Direction Coach!

Welcome to your Business Practice Lab! I'm Sarah, and I'm thrilled to guide you through one of the most crucial aspects of building a thriving Spiritual Direction practice: the Discovery Call. This isn't just about "selling"; it's about serving. It's about creating a safe, understanding space where potential clients can articulate their deepest spiritual longings and challenges, and you can confidently present how your unique gifts and training can support them.

For many of us, especially those pivoting from care-focused professions like nursing or teaching, the idea of a "sales call" can feel daunting. But I want you to reframe it. A discovery call is a sacred conversation, an initial step in discernment for both you and your potential directee. It's where you both explore the fit, the potential for a transformative journey together. Let's make you feel empowered and authentic in every step of this process!

  • Practice handling common objections with empathy and conviction.

  • Understand the income potential of a scalable Spiritual Direction practice.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script: A Sacred Conversation

This script provides a framework. Remember, authenticity is key. Adapt it to your natural conversational style, but ensure you hit these crucial points. The goal is to build rapport, understand their needs, position your service, and invite them to take the next step.

Your Discovery Call Flow with Eleanor

Phase 1: Welcome & Rapport Building 5 min
YOU:
"Welcome, Eleanor! Thank you for taking the time to connect today. I'm Sarah. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear a little about you. How did you first hear about spiritual direction, or about my practice specifically?"
ELEANOR:
"Hello, Sarah. Yes, my friend Margaret mentioned you. She spoke so highly of her experience with spiritual direction, and I've been feeling a bit lost since retiring, so I thought I'd explore it."
YOU:
"It's wonderful that Margaret shared her experience with you. Many women, especially after significant life transitions like retirement, find themselves at a crossroads, seeking new ways to connect with their inner life. Can you tell me a bit more about what 'feeling lost' means for you right now?"
Coach Tip: Active Listening

In this phase, your primary role is to listen deeply. Use phrases like "Tell me more about that..." or "What does that feel like for you?" to encourage Eleanor to elaborate. Avoid interrupting or offering solutions too early. Your presence is the most powerful tool.

Phase 2: Deep Dive into Spiritual Needs 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing that. It sounds like a significant shift. What spiritual questions or longings are most present for you in this new chapter of life?"
ELEANOR:
"I miss the sense of purpose I had as a teacher. Now, I wake up and wonder, 'What's next for me spiritually?' I feel a disconnect, like there's a part of me waiting to be reawakened, but I don't know how to reach it. I also worry about feeling isolated in my spiritual journey."
YOU:
"That's a very common experience, Eleanor, and a powerful space to be in. Many find this period of searching to be profoundly fertile ground for growth. If you could wave a magic wand and have your spiritual life feel exactly as you desire, what would that look like for you? What would be different?"
ELEANOR:
"I'd feel a deep sense of peace, clarity, and connection. I'd have a clearer sense of my purpose beyond my career, and perhaps some spiritual practices that truly nourish me. I'd love to feel less alone in this exploration."

"Yes, absolutely. That's exactly it."

My role is not to tell you what to believe, but to listen deeply, ask clarifying questions, and help you notice the movements of the Spirit in your life. We'll explore practices that resonate with you, helping you cultivate that inner peace and connection you're seeking. Confident Objection Handling with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and confidence. Here are common objections you might encounter and how to address them.

Common Objections & Empathetic Responses

"The investment is a bit higher than I expected."
"I completely understand that. Spiritual direction is an investment in your deepest self and your long-term spiritual well-being. Many of my directees, especially those like you who are navigating significant life changes, find that this dedicated space and guidance accelerate their growth and bring a profound sense of peace and purpose that is truly priceless. What part of the investment feels challenging for you right now?"
"I need to think about it / Discuss it with my spouse."
"Of course, this is an important decision, and it's wise to consider it carefully. To help you in that process, could you share what specific aspects you'd like to think over or discuss? Is it the time commitment, the financial investment, or perhaps something else about the process itself? Knowing this helps me ensure I've answered all your questions."
"I'm not sure I'm 'spiritual enough' / I don't know what to talk about."
"Eleanor, that's a very common feeling, and it's precisely why spiritual direction is so valuable. You don't need to be 'spiritual enough' or have all the answers. My role is to meet you exactly where you are, with all your questions and uncertainties. The content of our sessions emerges from your life and your experiences â€“ your joys, your challenges, your quiet moments. We simply pay attention to how the divine might be moving within it all. My directees often tell me they're surprised by how naturally the conversation flows once we begin."
"How is this different from therapy or coaching?"
"That's a great question! While there can be some overlap, spiritual direction focuses specifically on your relationship with the divine, however you understand that. It's not about solving problems (like therapy) or achieving specific goals (like coaching), but about deepening your awareness of God's presence, exploring your spiritual identity, and discerning your path. We focus on the 'why' and the 'how' of your spiritual journey, nurturing your soul's growth and wisdom. It's a unique form of companionship for your spiritual quest."

Presenting Your Pricing with Poise and Confidence

This is often where imposter syndrome can creep in. Remember, your training, expertise, and the transformative value you offer are worth every penny. When presenting your pricing:

  1. Be Clear and Direct: State the price without hesitation, apology, or rambling. Use a firm, confident tone.
  2. Frame as an Investment: Emphasize that it's an investment in their spiritual well-being, growth, and long-term fulfillment.
  3. Connect to Value: Briefly reiterate the benefits they will receive (e.g., clarity, peace, connection, discernment) and how your structured program delivers that value.
  4. Offer Payment Options (if applicable): If you offer payment plans, mention them briefly. "The investment for the 6-month journey is $1500, with an option for monthly payments of $250."
  5. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, pause. Allow them to process and respond. Do not fill the silence.

Practice this phrase out loud:

Pricing Presentation Practice

"My 6-month Spiritual Direction program, which includes six private sessions and tailored resources, is an investment of $1500. This can be paid in full or through monthly installments of $250. This commitment allows us to build a deep, meaningful relationship and ensures you experience lasting spiritual growth. Does that work for you, or do you have any questions about the structure of the program?"

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing with Intention

The close of your discovery call should be natural, clear, and invite a decision. It's not about pressure, but about inviting them to take the next step on their spiritual journey with you. Practice these phrases out loud:

  1. "Eleanor, based on everything you've shared, I truly believe spiritual direction can offer you the clarity and connection you're seeking in this new chapter. If you're ready to embark on this journey, we can schedule our first session now."
  2. "I feel a strong sense of resonance with your spiritual longing, Eleanor, and I'd be honored to walk alongside you. What feels like the best next step for you?"
  3. "My next available openings are [mention specific days/times]. Would one of those work for your first session?"
  4. "I'm confident we can help you find that renewed sense of purpose. Shall we take the first step together?"

Remember, a confident close comes from a place of genuine belief in the value of your service and a desire to help your prospective directee.

Check Your Understanding

1. When Eleanor expresses concern about the investment, what is an empathetic yet confident response you could offer?

An empathetic response acknowledges her concern, then reframes the investment in terms of the profound, lasting value of spiritual growth and personal transformation. For example: "I completely understand that. Spiritual direction is an investment in your deepest self and your long-term spiritual well-being. Many find that this dedicated space and guidance bring a profound sense of peace and purpose that is truly priceless. What part of the investment feels challenging for you right now?"

2. Eleanor mentions feeling "not spiritual enough." How would you address this common concern to make her feel comfortable?

Reassure her that this is a common feeling and that spiritual direction meets her exactly where she is. Emphasize that it's not about having all the answers but about exploring her authentic spiritual experience. For example: "That's a very common feeling, Eleanor, and it's precisely why spiritual direction is so valuable. You don't need to be 'spiritual enough' or have all the answers. My role is to meet you exactly where you are, with all your questions and uncertainties."

3. What is the primary purpose of summarizing the prospect's pain points before presenting your solution in a discovery call?

Summarizing the prospect's pain points validates their experience, shows you've been actively listening, and builds rapport. It also allows you to directly connect your spiritual direction services to their specific needs, making your solution more relevant and compelling.

4. What is a key principle to remember when confidently stating your pricing during a discovery call?

A key principle is to state the price clearly and directly, without hesitation or apology. Frame it as an investment in their transformation and connect it to the value they will receive, then pause and listen for their response.
Key Takeaways
  • The Discovery Call is a sacred conversation focused on deep listening and discerning fit.
  • Structure your calls to build rapport, understand needs, present your value, and invite next steps.
  • Approach objections with empathy and curiosity, not defensiveness.
  • Present your pricing with confidence and clarity, framing it as an investment.
  • Practice your calls-to-action to ensure a natural and intentional close.
  • A spiritual direction practice offers both profound impact and viable income potential, even with a manageable number of clients.
