Navigating the Discovery Call: A Step-by-Step Script

This 30-minute script is designed to guide you through a powerful, authentic conversation. Remember, it's a dance of listening and leading, always with the client's best interest at heart.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Building Authentic Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's a pleasure to connect. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my practice?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Sarah! Yes, it's great to connect. My friend, Emily, spoke so highly of her experience with you. She said you helped her through a really tough time of transition."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear about Emily! I'm so glad she found clarity. So Rachel, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on in your spiritual life or inner world that led you to seek out spiritual direction?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understanding Their Spiritual Journey 10 min
RACHEL:
"Well, for a while now, I've just felt... empty. Like I'm going through the motions. I have a great family, a good job, but inside, there's this persistent restlessness, a feeling that I'm missing something profound. I used to feel so connected, but now it's just... silence."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that so openly, Rachel. 'Empty,' 'restlessness,' 'silence' ‚Äì those are powerful words. Can you tell me more about what that inner restlessness feels like for you? When did it start, and how does it manifest in your daily life or your spiritual practices, if you have any?"
RACHEL:
"It's like a dull ache. I try to meditate, but my mind just races. I feel guilty that I'm not appreciating what I have. I've read books, tried journaling, but it feels like I'm just scratching the surface. I miss having that sense of purpose, that divine guidance I used to feel."
YOU:
"I hear you, Rachel. It sounds like you're truly seeking a deeper reconnection and a renewed sense of meaning. If nothing changes in the next year, how do you imagine you'd be feeling? And conversely, if we could wave a magic wand and you felt completely reconnected and purposeful, what would your life look like then?"
Phase 3: Articulating Your Unique Approach 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a deeply reflective person who is experiencing a profound sense of spiritual disconnection and a longing for purpose, despite your outwardly successful life. You've tried various self-help methods, but they haven't brought the lasting transformation you seek. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"The approach I use in spiritual direction is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just seeking answers, we create a sacred, confidential space for you to listen deeply to your own inner wisdom and the stirrings of the Divine within you. Over our 12-week journey, we'll explore practices tailored to your unique spiritual path, helping you discern blockages, rediscover your authentic self, and reconnect with your sense of purpose. Many of my clients, like Emily, report feeling a significant shift in clarity and peace within the first 4-6 sessions, often experiencing a renewed sense of joy and direction."
Phase 4: Guiding Towards the Next Step 5 min
YOU:
"So Rachel, based on what we've discussed, does this sound like the kind of support and guidance you've been looking for to address your spiritual longing and find that reconnection you desire?"
RACHEL:
"It really does. It sounds like exactly what I need right now."
YOU:
"Excellent! I'm thrilled to hear that. Let me tell you a bit about the program and how we can get started on this journey together."
Coach Tip: Active Listening

Remember, your primary role during the "Understand the Problem" phase is to listen, not to offer solutions. Use reflective listening and open-ended questions. Phrases like "Tell me more about that..." or "What does that feel like for you?" invite deeper sharing and build immense trust. Aim for 80% listening, 20% speaking.

Mastering Objection Handling with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely aligned. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

"It's a big investment right now."

"I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. Many of my clients felt the same way initially. What I've found is that the true cost isn't just financial; it's the cost of staying stuck, of continuing to feel that spiritual emptiness. What is the cost to you of not addressing this longing for connection and purpose? When we consider the profound transformation in peace, clarity, and joyful living, clients often see this as an invaluable investment in their spiritual well-being."

"I'm not sure I have enough time for this commitment."

"That's a very valid concern, Rachel, especially with your busy life. Spiritual direction is indeed a commitment, but it's designed to integrate seamlessly, not add more burden. Our sessions are 60 minutes every other week, and the 'homework' is often reflective practices that can be woven into your existing routine, like mindful moments or journaling. Many clients actually find that by creating this dedicated time for their spiritual well-being, they become more efficient and focused in other areas of their lives. How much time per week do you feel you could realistically dedicate to your spiritual growth?"

Coach Tip: Empathy First

Always validate the client's feelings before addressing the objection. Phrases like "I understand," "That's a valid point," or "Many clients share that concern" create a bridge of understanding rather than a wall of defense. This shifts the dynamic from confrontation to collaboration.

Coach Tip: Value-Based Pricing

Don't just state your price; reiterate the value and transformation the client will receive. Frame the investment in terms of the spiritual growth, peace, and clarity they will gain. For example, "This 12-week journey is an investment in rediscovering your soul's purpose, which is truly priceless."

Practicing Your Call-to-Action (CTA)

The CTA is your invitation for the client to take the next step. It should be clear, confident, and action-oriented. Practice these phrases out loud until they feel natural.

Key Call-to-Action Phrases

  • Direct & Clear: "So, Rachel, are you ready to begin your Deep Dive Journey with me?"
  • Option-Based: "Given everything we've discussed, I believe the Deep Dive Journey would be the perfect fit for you. Would you like to enroll in the 12-week program today, or do you have any further questions about getting started?"
  • Addressing Logistics: "If you're ready to move forward, I can walk you through the enrollment process right now, which takes about five minutes. We can then schedule our first session. How does that sound?"
  • Assumptive Close: "Fantastic! I'm excited to support you on this journey. My next availability is [Date] at [Time] or [Date] at [Time]. Which works best for our first session?"
Coach Tip: Silence is Golden

After you deliver your CTA, be comfortable with silence. It gives the client space to process and respond. Resist the urge to fill the silence with more talking or questions. Let them speak first.

Check Your Understanding

1

During Phase 1 of the discovery call, what is the primary goal when asking "How did you hear about my practice?"

The primary goal is to build rapport and understand their entry point. It helps to personalize the conversation and acknowledge any referrals, strengthening the initial connection.
2

Rachel expresses concern about the financial investment. What is an effective way to reframe this objection, as discussed in the lesson?

An effective reframe is to shift the focus from cost to value and the cost of inaction. Acknowledge the financial concern, then highlight the spiritual transformation, peace, and clarity gained, and ask, "What is the cost to you of not addressing this spiritual longing?"
3

When articulating your unique approach (Phase 3), what is a key differentiator of spiritual direction coaching compared to self-help books or therapy?

A key differentiator is creating a sacred, confidential space for the client to listen deeply to their own inner wisdom and the stirrings of the Divine within them, rather than just seeking external answers or symptom relief. It's about discerning their unique spiritual path.
4

After delivering a clear Call-to-Action, what crucial technique should you employ to give the client space to respond?

The crucial technique is to be comfortable with silence. After presenting the CTA, pause and allow the client to process and respond without feeling pressured to fill the quiet.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured yet authentic conversation designed to build rapport, understand the client's spiritual needs, present your unique approach, and invite them into a transformative journey.
  • Effective objection handling involves empathy, curiosity, and reframing concerns into opportunities to highlight the value and necessity of spiritual growth.
  • Confidently present your programs by clearly articulating the benefits and transformation, emphasizing that the investment is in their profound spiritual well-being.
  • Practice clear, confident, and action-oriented Calls-to-Action (CTAs), and embrace silence to allow clients space for decision-making.
  • Building a thriving spiritual direction practice offers significant income potential, with many coaches achieving substantial earnings through consistent client acquisition and diversified offerings.
