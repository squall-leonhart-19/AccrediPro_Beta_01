The Spiritual Direction Client Journey

Before we simulate a discovery call, let's map out the typical journey a client takes from initial awareness to becoming a committed directee. Understanding this journey helps you position your discovery call effectively.

  1. Awareness: The individual recognizes a need for deeper spiritual exploration, guidance, or support. This might stem from a life transition, a sense of spiritual stagnation, or a desire for greater meaning.
  2. Exploration: They begin researching solutions. This could involve searching online for "spiritual director near me," asking for referrals, or exploring different modalities (coaching, therapy, spiritual direction).
  3. Engagement: They encounter your presence ‚Äì perhaps through your website, social media, a workshop, or a referral. They resonate with your message and decide to take a small step, often scheduling a discovery call.
  4. Discernment (The Discovery Call): This is where you meet. It's a low-stakes, high-impact conversation where both parties assess fit.
  5. Commitment: If there's alignment, the individual commits to a spiritual direction program or series of sessions with you.
  6. Transformation: The ongoing work of spiritual direction unfolds.

The discovery call is not just a sales call; it's a critical point of mutual discernment. Your role is to listen deeply, offer clarity, and invite them into a sacred partnership.

Understanding Your Ideal Prospect

Knowing who you serve deeply informs how you communicate and connect. For spiritual direction, your ideal directee is often someone seeking profound personal and spiritual growth, not just problem-solving. They are typically reflective, open to inner work, and ready for commitment.

Mastering the Discovery Call Structure

A structured approach to your discovery call ensures you cover all necessary ground, demonstrate your professionalism, and guide the conversation effectively. This is a 30-minute framework.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Intentions 5 min
YOU:
"Hi [Prospect Name]! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Sarah, and I'm looking forward to learning more about what brought you here. Before we dive in, how are you doing today? And how did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear. My intention for this call is to understand what's currently happening in your spiritual journey, what you're seeking, and to see if spiritual direction is the right path for you at this time. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understand Their Needs 10 min
YOU:
"So, tell me ‚Äì what has prompted you to seek spiritual direction or guidance right now? What's stirring within you?"
YOU:
"When you think about your spiritual life, what does a sense of fulfillment or peace look like for you? What are you longing for?"
YOU:
"Have you tried other forms of spiritual support or guidance before? What was that experience like for you?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes in your spiritual life over the next six months, how would that feel? And if you could gain complete clarity or peace, what would that enable you to do or be?"
Phase 3: Articulate Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing so openly, [Prospect Name]. What I'm hearing is [summarize their core spiritual longing, challenge, and desired outcome]. Does that resonate with your experience?"
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, I believe spiritual direction could be incredibly supportive for you. My approach, rooted in the G.U.I.D.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, involves [briefly explain 1-2 key aspects, e.g., 'creating a sacred space for deep listening,' 'exploring your inner wisdom,' or 'discerning divine movements in your everyday life']. We typically work together over [e.g., 3 or 6 months] to allow for sustained, transformative growth. My directees often report experiencing [1-2 key benefits, e.g., 'greater clarity and peace,' 'a stronger connection to their inner guide,' or 'renewed sense of purpose'] within the first few months."
YOU:
"How does that sound to you? Does this resonate with what you're looking for?"
Phase 4: Invite to Partnership (Call to Action) 5 min
YOU:
"Given what we've discussed, I truly feel we could create a powerful partnership to support your journey. Are you ready to explore how we can begin working together?"
YOU:
"Excellent! Let me walk you through my spiritual direction programs and how we can get started."
Coach Tip: Deep Listening

Your primary tool during a discovery call is not your script, but your ability to listen. Practice active listening, asking open-ended questions, and reflecting back what you hear. This builds trust and makes the prospect feel truly seen and understood, which is invaluable in spiritual work.

Navigating Objections with Grace

It's natural for potential directees to have concerns. Objections are not rejections; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"This sounds wonderful, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel fully aligned. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"The investment is more than I expected/can afford right now."
"Thank you for being transparent about that. I appreciate it. I understand that investing in spiritual growth is a significant decision. What I've found is that directees who commit to this work often see it as an investment in their overall well-being and purpose, yielding profound long-term returns far beyond the financial cost. How do you currently value your spiritual well-being and personal growth?" (Pause for their answer.) "While I don't offer discounts, we can explore payment plans, or perhaps a shorter foundational program might be a good starting point to experience the value."
"I'm not sure I have enough time for this commitment."
"I hear you. Our lives are incredibly busy, and adding another commitment can feel challenging. Many of my directees are busy professionals or parents. What they find is that the dedicated time for spiritual direction actually helps them reclaim time and energy by bringing greater clarity and intention to their daily lives. Our sessions are [e.g., 60 minutes bi-weekly], and the 'homework' is often reflective and integrates into your existing routines. How much time do you currently dedicate to your spiritual well-being?"
Coach Tip: Embrace Your Value

Imposter syndrome can creep in when discussing pricing. Remember your training, your unique gifts, and the profound transformation you facilitate. Your expertise is valuable. When you stand confidently in your worth, clients are more likely to trust in the value of your offerings. You are not asking for money; you are inviting them to invest in their spiritual flourishing.

Confidently Presenting Your Investment

After addressing any initial concerns, it's time to clearly and confidently present your spiritual direction programs and their associated investment. Always lead with the value and transformation, not just the price.

Here‚Äôs a typical way to frame your offerings:

"Based on what we've discussed, I recommend my Deepening Presence Program. This is a 3-month journey where we meet bi-weekly for 60-minute sessions. Throughout our time, you'll receive personalized guidance, reflective exercises, and email support between sessions. The investment for this program is $X, and payment plans are available."

Consider offering structured programs rather than hourly rates. This encourages commitment and allows for deeper, more sustained spiritual work, which is the essence of spiritual direction.

Coach Tip: Own Your Pricing

State your prices clearly and confidently, then allow for silence. Don't rush to fill the void. This pause gives the prospect time to process and allows you to hold space for their reaction. Your calm demeanor reinforces your belief in the value of your service.

Sealing the Sacred Partnership: The Call to Action

Once you've presented your programs, the next step is to guide them toward commitment. Make it easy for them to say "yes."

Example Closing Lines:

  • "Based on everything we've discussed, I genuinely feel that the [Program Name] would be a perfect fit for you to achieve . Are you ready to begin this journey together?"
  • "If you're feeling a 'yes' in your heart, the next step is to [e.g., send you an enrollment link, schedule our first session, send the agreement]. How would you like to move forward?"
  • "I'm truly excited about the possibility of working with you. What feels like the right next step for you?"

Always have your next steps clearly defined: whether it's sending a welcome email with payment links, scheduling the first session, or providing a detailed program agreement.

Coach Tip: The Power of Follow-Up

Not everyone says yes on the first call, and that's perfectly fine. A respectful, value-driven follow-up can often convert a hesitant prospect. Send a personalized email within 24 hours, summarizing your conversation, reiterating the program details, and inviting them to ask any further questions. Often, a gentle nudge is all that's needed.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call in spiritual direction, beyond simply "selling" a program?

The primary purpose is mutual discernment. It's an opportunity for both the spiritual director and the potential directee to explore alignment, understand needs, and determine if a sacred partnership is the right fit at this time. It's about deeply listening and inviting, not just selling.

2. A potential client says, "I love this, but I'm not sure I have enough time for the commitment." How should you respond, focusing on empathy and value?

A good response would be: "I hear you. Our lives are incredibly busy, and adding another commitment can feel challenging. What many of my directees find is that the dedicated time for spiritual direction actually helps them reclaim time and energy by bringing greater clarity and intention to their daily lives. Our sessions are [e.g., 60 minutes bi-weekly], and the 'homework' is often reflective and integrates into your existing routines. How much time do you currently dedicate to your spiritual well-being?" This validates their concern while reframing the commitment as a benefit.

3. When presenting your program investment, what crucial element should you lead with before stating the price?

You should always lead with the value and transformation the client will receive. Articulate the specific benefits and outcomes of your program before stating the financial investment. This helps the client understand what they are gaining, not just what they are paying.

4. If a spiritual director has 5 active clients enrolled in a 6-month program priced at $2,400, what is their approximate monthly income from these clients?

If a 6-month program costs $2,400, that's $400 per client per month ($2400 / 6 months). With 5 active clients, the monthly income would be 5 * $400 = $2,000 per month.
