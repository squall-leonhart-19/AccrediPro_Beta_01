Section 3: Practice Exercise

Exercise: The "Red Flag" Inquiry

Practice asking these three questions out loud, focusing on a warm, inquisitive tone (not an interrogative one):

  1. The Sleep Inquiry: "Linda, when Max is sleeping, does he seem to rest deeply, or does he shift positions frequently, as if he can't get comfortable?"
  2. The Nutrition Inquiry: "I love that Max gets to share family time at dinner. When he gets those table scraps, are they usually lean proteins, or more of the 'starchy' variety like bread or pasta?"
  3. The Vision Inquiry: "Have you noticed Max hesitating at the top of the stairs or in low-light conditions, like during your evening walk?"
The "Why" Behind the Question

We ask about sleep because restlessness is often a hidden sign of chronic pain. We ask about starch because it fuels inflammation. We ask about stairs because vision and mobility are often linked in senior profiles.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask about a pet's flooring during the Profiling (P) phase?

Hardwood or slick floors can cause "micro-slips" that lead to chronic muscle strain and joint instability, particularly in senior pets. Identifying this allows for environmental modifications (like rugs or toe grips) that provide immediate relief.

2. What is the "70/30 Rule" in a coaching session?

The client should speak 70% of the time, and the coach should speak 30%. This ensures you are gathering sufficient data and the client feels heard and understood.

3. According to the lesson, what is a "Red Flag" regarding a pet's sleep?

Frequent shifting or inability to get comfortable during sleep. This is often a subtle indicator of chronic pain or discomfort that owners may overlook as "just getting old."

4. How does a coach avoid "shaming" a client who feeds table scraps?

By using a "pivot" strategy: acknowledge the bond (sharing food) and suggest "upgrading" the scraps to functional foods (like lean proteins) rather than simply telling them to stop.
Key Takeaways
  • Profiling is the Foundation: You cannot build a wellness plan without deep, accurate data from the "P" phase.
  • Environment Matters: Always audit the physical space (flooring, stairs, lighting) as it directly impacts physical health.
  • Old is Not a Disease: Part of your job is to reframe "aging" as a manageable life stage for the client.
  • Professionalism via Detail: High-level data collection (like the 1-10 energy scale) establishes your authority and justifies premium pricing.
