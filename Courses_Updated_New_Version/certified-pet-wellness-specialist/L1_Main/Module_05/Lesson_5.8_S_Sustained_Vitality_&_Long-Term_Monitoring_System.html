Practice Exercise: Your Turn

To build your "coaching muscles," complete the following steps:

  1. The "Mirror Test": Stand in front of a mirror and say the first "YOU" line from the script. Watch your facial expressions. Do you look like someone Diane can trust?
  2. Voice Memo Challenge: Record yourself reading the entire script on your phone. Play it back. Listen for "ums," "likes," or "justs." Aim for a steady, confident pace.
  3. The "Why" Pivot: Practice explaining why a full assessment is better than a quick tip. Example: "A quick tip is like a band-aid; an assessment is like finding out why the wound happened in the first place."

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Avoid these four common mistakes to ensure your discovery sessions are successful:

  • The "Advice Trap": Giving away all your solutions in the first 5 minutes. If you give the "answer" before the assessment, the client won't see the value in the full process.
  • Over-Explaining Science: Using too much "vet-speak." Keep it simple. Instead of "mitochondrial dysfunction," try "his cells are running low on battery."
  • Ignoring the Human: Forgetting that Diane is stressed. Acknowledge the owner's feelings as much as the pet's symptoms.
  • Weak Closing: Ending with "Let me know if you're interested." Instead, use: "The next step is the full assessment. Should we get that on the calendar for Tuesday or Wednesday?"
Bella's Wisdom

In my experience, the most successful coaches are the ones who listen 70% of the time and speak 30%. Let the client tell you exactly what they need.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Discovery Session" script used with Diane?

The goal is to validate the owner's concerns, establish your expertise by explaining the "Whole Dog" approach, and transition the client into a full, paid assessment.

2. Why should you avoid the "Advice Trap" during a discovery call?

Giving quick advice before a full assessment often results in "band-aid" solutions that don't address root causes. It also diminishes the perceived value of your professional expertise and comprehensive assessment process.

3. How does Sarah (the practitioner in the spotlight) use assessments to drive her business?

She uses the assessment as a high-value entry point ($197) that demonstrates the need for her 3-month package ($597), leading to a 70% conversion rate without high-pressure sales.

4. What is a more effective way to close a session than saying "Let me know if you're interested"?

Use a "presumptive close" by offering two specific time slots for the next step, such as: "The next step is the full assessment. Should we get that on the calendar for Tuesday or Wednesday?"

Key Takeaways

  • Assessment is a structured act of caring that builds immediate trust with clients.
  • Use "Whole Dog" language to differentiate your services from generic advice.
  • Practice your script out loud to eliminate filler words and project confidence.
  • Acknowledge the client's emotional state to build a lasting professional relationship.
  • Always lead with a clear "Next Step" to maintain momentum and professional authority.
