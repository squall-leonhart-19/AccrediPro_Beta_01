The Psychology of the Enrollment Call

In the world of professional sound healing, the discovery call is your first clinical intervention. You are not just checking if they can afford you; you are assessing their readiness for resonance. Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Phase 2 (The Gap Analysis) the most important part of the call?

It helps the client realize the "cost of inaction." By identifying how much their problem is costing them (health, money, relationships), the price of your program becomes a small investment compared to the cost of staying the same.

2. What should you do immediately after stating your price?

Remain silent. This allows the client to process the information and demonstrates that you are confident in your value. Breaking the silence often signals insecurity or a willingness to discount prematurely.

3. How do you handle the "I need to talk to my spouse" objection?

Acknowledge it as valid, then ask a clarifying question to identify the real concern (time or money). Offer to hop on a 5-minute "bridge call" with the spouse to answer technical questions about the sound healing science.

4. Why is a 12-week program better than "pay-per-session"?

It ensures client commitment and better clinical outcomes. Transformation takes time (neural plasticity and entrainment). It also provides you with predictable income and allows you to work with fewer clients more deeply.
