Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to validate a client's "racing mind" instead of telling them to "just stop thinking"?

Validation lowers the client's defense mechanisms and reduces the "performance anxiety" of relaxation. Telling them to stop thinking creates more stress and keeps them in the sympathetic nervous system.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Pivot Script"?

The Pivot Script bridges the gap between the intellectual/analytical part of the session (intake) and the experiential/sensory part (sound immersion), guiding the client's focus inward.

3. According to the "Common Mistakes" table, what happens when a coach over-explains the science during the intake?

It keeps the client trapped in their "analytical brain," making it harder for them to drop into the meditative state required for deep sound healing.

4. What is the "10-second silence" exercise intended to practice?

It practices "holding space" and becoming comfortable with stillness, which prevents the practitioner from rushing the session due to their own nervous energy.
