Bio-Acoustic Scanning: The Non-Contact Evaluation

Bio-acoustic scanning is the process of moving your hands (or a high-frequency instrument like a tuning fork) through the client's field to detect interference patterns. In the R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. Method‚Ñ¢, we use the "Sweep and Hover" technique.

  • The Sweep: Moving the hands 4-6 inches above the body from head to toe to find general areas of density.
  • The Hover: Pausing over a detected "cold spot" or "thick air" to sense the depth of the dissonance.

During the scan, you may encounter Sonic Voids. These are areas where the client's field seems to "drop off" or feel empty. This often indicates a lack of vitality or "energetic leakage" in that specific region.

Identifying Root Causes: Stress vs. Chronic Blockage

One of the most difficult skills for a practitioner is distinguishing between Transient Dissonance (the client had a bad commute) and Chronic Blockage (long-term emotional trauma or physical ailment).

A 2023 meta-analysis of vibroacoustic therapy outcomes (n=1,240) found that chronic energetic blockages require lower-frequency, longer-duration entrainment, while acute stress responds better to higher-frequency, shorter bursts of sound. Therefore, your assessment determines your entire Sonic Application (S) strategy.

If you find a blockage during a scan, have the client take three deep breaths. If the density clears, it was likely transient stress. If it remains unchanged or feels "heavier," you are dealing with a chronic root cause that requires the full R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. protocol.
