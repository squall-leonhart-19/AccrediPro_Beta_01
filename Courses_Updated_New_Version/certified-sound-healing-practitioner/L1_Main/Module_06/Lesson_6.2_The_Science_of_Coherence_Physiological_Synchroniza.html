The Physics of Wave Synchronization

In the study of acoustics, coherence refers to the orderly relationship between waves. When we apply sound to the human body, we are essentially introducing a coherent wave pattern into a system that may be experiencing dissonance (biological noise).

The primary mechanism at play is Constructive Interference. When two waves of the same frequency meet and their peaks and troughs align, they combine to form a wave with greater amplitude. In a sound healing context, this means that the "pure" tone of a singing bowl can amplify the healthy resonant frequencies within the body's tissues, "overpowering" the chaotic frequencies of stress.

Coach Tip: Explaining Coherence

Think of coherence like a laser vs. a lightbulb. A lightbulb scatters light in all directions (incoherent). A laser focuses light into a single, powerful beam (coherent). Your role is to help the client's "scattered" energy focus into a laser-like state of biological efficiency.
