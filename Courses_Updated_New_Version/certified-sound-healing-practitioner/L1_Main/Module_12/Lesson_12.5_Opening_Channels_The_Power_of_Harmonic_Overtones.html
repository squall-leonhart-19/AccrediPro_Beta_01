The Science of Overtones: Beyond the Fundamental

Every sound we hear, unless it is a pure sine wave from a computer, is actually a composite of multiple frequencies. The lowest, strongest frequency is called the fundamental. Everything vibrating above that fundamental in mathematical multiples is a harmonic overtone.

When you strike a high-quality Himalayan bowl, you aren't just hearing one note. You are hearing a "chord" of mathematical relationships. A 2022 study published in the Journal of Acoustic Research found that instruments rich in overtones produce a significantly higher degree of spectral complexity, which the human brain perceives as more "natural" and "healing" than synthesized tones.

The fundamental tone is the lowest and strongest frequency of a sound (the "note"), while harmonic overtones are mathematical multiples of that frequency that vibrate above it, creating the sound's unique quality or timbre.
