The Science of Manual Muscle Testing (MMT)

Applied Kinesiology, or Manual Muscle Testing (MMT), is based on the principle that the body's central nervous system responds instantly to external stimuli. In sound healing, we use MMT not for diagnostic purposes, but as a validator for resonance.

When a client is exposed to a sound frequency that is "coherent" with their biofield, the nervous system maintains its integrity, and the muscle remains "strong" under light pressure. Conversely, a frequency that is "dissonant" or causes stress to the system will result in a momentary "weakening" of the muscle's resistance.

HRV: The Modern Gold Standard

If Kinesiology is the "soft" science of bio-feedback, Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the "hard" metric. HRV measures the variation in time between each heartbeat. A high HRV indicates a flexible, resilient autonomic nervous system (ANS).
