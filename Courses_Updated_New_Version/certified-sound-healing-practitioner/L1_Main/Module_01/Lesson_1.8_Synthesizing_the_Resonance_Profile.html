Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Avoid these 4 mistakes to ensure a premium client experience:

  • Over-Explaining the Physics: Don't get bogged down in Hertz and wave patterns unless they ask. Keep it focused on their feeling.
  • Ignoring Body Language: If Elena pulls away when you bring the sound close, her resonance is telling you she needs more space.
  • Rushing the Intake: The assessment is the healing. Rushing to get to the "bowls" makes the client feel like a number.
  • Doubtful Language: Avoid saying "I think" or "Maybe." Use confident phrases like "I am noticing" or "The resonance suggests."

1. Why is the "Instrument Tuning" analogy effective for clients like Elena?

2. What is the primary goal of the Resonance Assessment?

3. Which of the following is a "Success Marker" during an intake?

4. How should a practitioner handle a client who seems skeptical of "energy"?

Focus on the physical sensations of sound and the concept of vibration, which is a measurable physical phenomenon, rather than using purely "spiritual" language.
