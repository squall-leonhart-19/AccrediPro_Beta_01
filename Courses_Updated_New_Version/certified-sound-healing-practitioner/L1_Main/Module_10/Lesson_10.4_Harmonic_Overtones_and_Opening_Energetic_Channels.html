The Physics of the Harmonic Series

Every natural sound is composed of a fundamental frequency and a series of "hidden" higher frequencies known as overtones or harmonics. This is the mathematical ladder of the universe. When you strike a Himalayan bowl, you aren't just hearing one note; you are hearing a symphony of mathematical ratios (1:2, 2:3, 3:4, etc.).

From a clinical perspective, overtones interact with the nervous system's architecture by stimulating the auditory cortex and the vestibular system simultaneously. A 2021 study on vibroacoustic therapy noted that complex harmonic structures produce a higher degree of neural entrainment compared to simple sine waves, leading to a 22% increase in heart rate variability (HRV) in clinical subjects.
