Techniques for Scanning the Biofield

Scanning is the diagnostic phase of the R.E.S.O.N.A.T.E. Method‚Ñ¢. While instruments like tuning forks are vital, the practitioner‚Äôs own vocal toning acts as a biological sonar. By projecting a neutral tone into the client's field and listening for how that tone "bounces" back, you can identify areas of density or depletion.

The Vocal Sonar Protocol

  1. Positioning: Stand 2-3 feet from the client, who is ideally supine.
  2. The Neutral Tone: Produce a steady, mid-range "Ah" or "Oh" sound.
  3. The Scan: Move your voice from the crown of the head down to the feet, maintaining a consistent volume.
  4. The Feedback Loop: Listen for changes in your own voice. Does the tone suddenly sound "thin"? Does it feel "blocked"? Does it become "harsh"?
