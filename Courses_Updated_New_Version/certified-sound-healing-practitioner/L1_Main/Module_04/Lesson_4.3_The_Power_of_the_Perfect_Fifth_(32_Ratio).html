The Geometry of Harmony: The 3:2 Ratio

In music theory, the Perfect Fifth is the interval between two notes where the higher frequency is exactly 1.5 times the lower frequency. This creates a 3:2 mathematical ratio. For example, if note A is vibrating at 200 Hz, the Perfect Fifth above it vibrates at 300 Hz.

Why does this matter to the human body? Pythagoras, the ancient Greek philosopher, discovered that this ratio is fundamental to the structure of the universe. In modern sound therapy, we recognize that the human ear and nervous system are hard-wired to respond to this specific proportion. When we hear the 3:2 ratio, the brain experiences a state of consonance‚Äîa feeling of stability and resolution.
