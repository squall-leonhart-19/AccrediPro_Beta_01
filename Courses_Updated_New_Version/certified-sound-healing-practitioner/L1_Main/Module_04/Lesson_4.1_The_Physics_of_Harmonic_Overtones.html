The Harmonic Series: Nature‚Äôs Hidden Ladder

In the world of physics, a "pure" sound‚Äîa single frequency without any other accompanying tones‚Äîis actually a rarity. In nature, when a string vibrates or a bowl is struck, it vibrates at multiple frequencies simultaneously. This collection of frequencies is called the Harmonic Series.

The lowest frequency is called the Fundamental. This is the note your ear identifies (e.g., a Middle C). However, layered on top of that fundamental are Overtones (also called partials). These overtones follow a strict mathematical progression: the first overtone is twice the frequency of the fundamental (the octave), the second is three times the frequency (the perfect fifth), and so on.

While the trunk provides the structure, the branches and leaves provide the complexity, life, and interaction with the environment. In sound healing, overtones are what allow the sound to "branch out" into the client's energetic pathways.
