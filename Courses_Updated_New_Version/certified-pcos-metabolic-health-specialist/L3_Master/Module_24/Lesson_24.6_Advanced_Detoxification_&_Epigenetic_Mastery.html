Estrogen Metabolism: Phase I & Phase II Clearance

Many PCOS clients present with "estrogen dominance" patterns (heavy periods, breast tenderness, mood swings) even when their total estrogen is "normal" on labs. This is often a clearance failure rather than an overproduction issue.

  • Phase I (Functionalization): The liver uses CYP enzymes to turn estrogen into metabolites (2-OH, 4-OH, or 16-OH). We want to favor the 2-OH pathway.
  • Phase II (Conjugation): The liver adds a "tag" (like a methyl group or glucuronic acid) to make the toxin water-soluble for excretion.

The Lymphatic-Metabolic Link

We often focus on the liver and kidneys, but the Lymphatic System is the "metabolic drain" of the body. If the lymph is stagnant, metabolic waste (what we call "metabolic sludge") accumulates around cells, increasing insulin resistance.
