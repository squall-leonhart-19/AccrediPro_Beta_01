Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

As you transition from a "wellness enthusiast" to a Certified Specialist, you must avoid these common mistakes that I see new coaches make every day.

4 Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Information Dump": Explaining every single lab value in the first 10 minutes. (Result: Client feels overwhelmed and paralyzed).
  • Being the "Food Police": Telling her what she can't eat before she knows what she can add.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Giving a protocol without explaining how it lowers her insulin. (Result: Low compliance).
  • Imposter Apologizing: Using phrases like "I think..." or "Maybe we could try..." instead of "Based on the evidence, we will..."

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena's HbA1c is 5.9%. Why is her HIIT workout potentially making this worse?

Intense HIIT can trigger a significant cortisol response. Cortisol stimulates gluconeogenesis (the production of glucose by the liver), which raises blood sugar levels even in a fasted state, exacerbating insulin resistance in a stressed perimenopausal body.

2. What is the "Success Marker" of a high-value initial consultation?

The client moves from a state of "self-blame" (e.g., "I'm a failure") to "clinical curiosity" (e.g., "Oh, my cortisol is driving my insulin"). When the client understands the mechanism, they are 3x more likely to follow the protocol.
