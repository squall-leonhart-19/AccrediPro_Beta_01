Check Your Understanding

1. Why is HIIT exercise often counterproductive for a client like Linda?

In a state of HPA-axis dysfunction (PCOS + Perimenopause), HIIT can drive cortisol too high. High cortisol triggers the liver to release glucose, which raises insulin, signaling the body to store fatâ€”especially in the abdominal area.

2. What is the primary purpose of using analogies like "Falling Dominoes" with clients?

Analogies reduce the cognitive load on the client. It helps them visualize the "Endocrine Cascade" without needing a medical degree, making the plan feel achievable and logical.

3. Linda wakes up at 3:00 AM regularly. From an endocrine perspective, what is likely happening?

This is often a "Cortisol Spike" caused by a drop in blood sugar or chronic HPA-axis activation. The body perceives low blood sugar as a crisis and releases cortisol to mobilize energy, which wakes the brain up.

4. What is a "Success Marker" in a coaching session?

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Validation is Clinical: Lowering a client's shame lowers their cortisol. Never skip the empathy step.
