Handling Resistance

If a client stays stuck in shame, use the "5-Year Reframe." Ask: "Diane, in five years, will this week of donuts matter, or will the fact that you showed up to this coaching call today matter more?" This shifts them back into the "Growth Mindset."

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New coaches often struggle with the transition from "Teacher" to "Partner" during maintenance. Avoid these four common mistakes:

  • Mistake 1: Getting "Sciencey" Too Fast. When a client is in a shame spiral, they don't need a lecture on the glucose-insulin spike. They need emotional regulation first.
  • Mistake 2: Lowering the Goal. Don't say "It's okay, we can just aim for weight maintenance now." This can feel like you've given up on their bigger vision.
  • Mistake 3: Taking It Personally. If a client "fails," it's not a reflection of your coaching. It's a reflection of their current capacity. Stay objective.
  • Mistake 4: Over-Prescribing. Don't add 5 new habits to "fix" the lapse. Simplify back to the "Big Three" (Protein, Sleep, Movement).
Professional Confidence

You are the expert. When you don't panic, the client doesn't panic. Your calm is their most powerful supplement.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological goal of the Maintenance Phase (Months 4+)?

The primary goal is building Psychological Resilience and self-efficacy, moving the client from "all-or-nothing" thinking to "lifestyle integration."

2. Why is the "Data Collection Point" reframe effective for PCOS clients?

It removes the moral judgment (good/bad) from eating choices and turns a "lapse" into a neutral piece of information that can be used to improve future strategies.

3. According to the "Bank Account" analogy, what happens during a week of poor choices?

It is seen as a "withdrawal" from a healthy account. It doesn't erase previous "deposits" (metabolic repairs), provided the client returns to consistent habits.

4. What is a "Non-Negotiable Minimum"?

A simplified, low-effort habit that the client commits to even during their most stressful times to maintain a baseline of metabolic health and psychological momentum.
