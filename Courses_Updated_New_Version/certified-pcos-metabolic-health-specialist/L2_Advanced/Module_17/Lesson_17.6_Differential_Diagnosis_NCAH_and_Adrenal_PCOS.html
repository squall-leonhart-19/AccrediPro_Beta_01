The Great Mimicker: Non-Classic Adrenal Hyperplasia (NCAH)

Non-Classic Adrenal Hyperplasia (NCAH) is a genetic condition caused by a partial deficiency in the 21-hydroxylase enzyme. Unlike the classic form (diagnosed at birth), NCAH manifests in puberty or adulthood with symptoms that are virtually identical to PCOS: hirsutism, cystic acne, and irregular menstrual cycles.

Statistics show that between 1% to 10% of women presenting with PCOS symptoms actually have NCAH, depending on their ethnic background (with higher prevalence in Ashkenazi Jewish, Hispanic, and Mediterranean populations). If you miss this diagnosis, the standard "low carb and metformin" approach may yield frustratingly minimal results.

The Adrenal Phenotype: DHEA-S Dominance

Even without a genetic enzyme deficiency like NCAH, many clients present with what we call Adrenal PCOS. In these cases, the "E" (Endocrine) component of the S.T.E.A.D.Y. Method‚Ñ¢ is the primary lever. These clients often have normal or only slightly elevated Total Testosterone, but significantly elevated DHEA-S.

DHEA-S is produced exclusively in the adrenal glands. When it is high, it suggests that the hyperandrogenism is not being driven by the ovaries (via insulin-stimulated Theca cells), but rather by the HPA-axis. These clients are often "lean" and may not respond to aggressive glucose tuning because their insulin sensitivity is already high.

Stress-Induced Insulin Resistance

In the Adrenal Phenotype, chronic HPA-axis activation creates a unique metabolic environment. Cortisol, the "stress hormone," is gluconeogenic‚Äîit tells the liver to dump glucose into the bloodstream to prepare for a "fight or flight" response. If this stress is chronic, the client experiences "stress-induced insulin resistance."

Standard "T" (Therapeutic Glucose Tuning) efforts like cutting carbs may actually worsen the situation by increasing the stress load on the body, leading to further cortisol spikes and higher adrenal androgen output. This is why the Adrenal PCOS client often feels "tired but wired" and struggles with insomnia despite a "perfect" diet.

S.T.E.A.D.Y. Method‚Ñ¢ Adjustment

For Adrenal PCOS, prioritize 'A' (Activity) that is low-impact (walking, restorative yoga) rather than HIIT, which can further spike DHEA-S and cortisol. Your goal is to signal safety to the HPA-axis, not more stress.

Supplements for Adrenal Androgen Control

When the differential diagnosis points toward an adrenal driver, our supplement strategy must pivot from insulin sensitizers to HPA-axis modulators and direct adrenal androgen blockers.

  • Reishi Mushroom (Ganoderma lucidum): A potent 5-alpha reductase inhibitor. It helps prevent the conversion of androgens into their more potent forms (like DHT) which drive hair loss and acne.
  • Licorice Root (Glycyrrhiza glabra): Contains glycyrrhizin, which can spare cortisol (helping with HPA-axis burnout) and has been shown in studies to reduce serum testosterone and DHEA-S levels in women. Note: Monitor blood pressure as it can cause sodium retention.
  • Phosphatidylserine: A phospholipid that helps "blunt" the ACTH and cortisol response to physical and emotional stress, effectively lowering the stimulus for adrenal androgen production.

Case Study: The "Low Carb" Failure

Client: Sarah, age 44, former athlete. Presenting with adult acne, thinning hair, and regular but very painful cycles. She had been on a strict ketogenic diet for 18 months but her acne was worsening.

Initial Labs: Fasting Insulin: 4.2 uIU/mL (Excellent), Total Testosterone: 45 ng/dL (Normal), DHEA-S: 580 ug/dL (High), 17-OHP: 340 ng/dL (Elevated screening).

Intervention: Because her 17-OHP was >200 ng/dL, we suspected a genetic NCAH variant. We transitioned her from Keto to a Moderate-Carb Anti-Inflammatory diet to reduce HPA stress. We added 1,000mg Reishi and 400mg Phosphatidylserine at night.

Outcome: Within 12 weeks, her DHEA-S dropped to 310 ug/dL, her acne cleared completely, and her "wired" feeling subsided. Sarah represents the classic NCAH/Adrenal client who was accidentally "stressing" her way into worse symptoms through over-restriction.

Specialist Protocol

Always test 17-OH Progesterone in the early morning (before 8 AM) during the follicular phase (days 3-5). Testing outside of this window can lead to false positives or missed diagnoses.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which enzyme is typically deficient in Non-Classic Adrenal Hyperplasia (NCAH)?

The 21-hydroxylase enzyme. A deficiency here causes a backup of precursors, leading to an overproduction of 17-OH Progesterone and adrenal androgens.

2. If a client has high DHEA-S but normal Total Testosterone and normal fasting insulin, which PCOS phenotype are they likely displaying?

The Adrenal Phenotype. This suggests the hyperandrogenism is driven by the HPA-axis rather than the ovaries or insulin resistance.

3. Why might a strict ketogenic diet be contraindicated for an Adrenal PCOS client?

Ketosis is a metabolic stressor. For an already overactive HPA-axis, the lack of carbohydrates can trigger higher cortisol and ACTH production, further driving adrenal androgen (DHEA-S) output.

4. What is the standard screening threshold for 17-OH Progesterone that warrants a referral for genetic testing?

A value greater than 200 ng/dL (or 6 nmol/L) in the early morning follicular phase is generally considered the threshold for further investigation into NCAH.
