Presenting Symptoms: Intense hot flashes, irritability, and "wirey" anxiety. Sarah has been taking cooling herbs (Sage, Motherwort) with only 20% relief.

Defining Constitution (Prakriti): The Baseline

In clinical herbalism, the Constitution is the inherent energetic makeup an individual is born with. Often referred to in Ayurvedic tradition as Prakriti, it is the "Individual Blueprint." It is influenced by genetics, the health of the parents at conception, and early developmental environment.

Think of the constitution as the "Home Base." It is where the client's body functions most efficiently. A naturally "hot" person (Choleric) isn't necessarily ill because they are warm; they are simply operating at a higher metabolic rate. Problems arise when they move too far away from this base.

Current State (Vikriti): The Imbalance

If Prakriti is the blueprint, Vikriti is the current state of the "house." It represents the imbalances accumulated through diet, lifestyle, stress, and environment. As a Clinical Herbalist, your job is to look through the Vikriti to see the Prakriti underneath.

Feature Prakriti (Constitution) Vikriti (Current State)
Duration Lifelong; permanent baseline. Temporary; changes based on life factors.
Indicators Bone structure, eye color, natural temperament. Tongue coating, pulse, current symptoms (e.g., rash).
Clinical Goal Support and nourish this nature. Clear and balance the deviation.

The Four Temperaments in Modern Clinical Context

Derived from Galenic medicine but validated by modern metabolic observations, the four temperaments provide a map for understanding how different people process stress, food, and medicine.

1. The Sanguine (Hot & Damp)

The "Air" element. Sanguine individuals are often social, optimistic, and physically robust. They tend toward Damp-Heat.

  • Clinical Vulnerability: Congestion, high blood pressure, inflammatory skin conditions.
  • Herbal Focus: Alteratives and Lymphatics (e.g., Red Clover, Burdock).

2. The Choleric (Hot & Dry)

The "Fire" element. These are the high-achieving, sharp-witted "Type A" personalities. They process things quickly.

  • Clinical Vulnerability: Acid reflux, "burnout," hypertension, liver tension.
  • Herbal Focus: Cooling Sedatives and Bitters (e.g., Skullcap, Dandelion Root).

Coach Tip: Identifying the Choleric

A Choleric client will likely show up to their appointment 5 minutes early with a spreadsheet of their symptoms. They want efficiency. Don't give them a 10-step protocol; give them the 3 most potent actions they can take today.

3. The Melancholic (Cold & Dry)

The "Earth" element. Often thin-framed, analytical, and sensitive. They tend toward Tension.

  • Clinical Vulnerability: Constipation, "brain fog," dry skin, brittle bones.
  • Herbal Focus: Warming Warming Carminatives and Demulcents (e.g., Ginger, Marshmallow).

4. The Phlegmatic (Cold & Damp)

The "Water" element. Calm, steady, and physically sturdy. They have a slower metabolism.

  • Clinical Vulnerability: Weight gain, edema, mucus congestion, lethargy.
  • Herbal Focus: Warming Stimulants and Diuretics (e.g., Rosemary, Juniper).

Physical Archetypes: Clues in the Body

While we rely on the Holistic Intake (H) for history, Energetic Assessment (E) uses observation. A 2021 study in the Journal of Personalized Medicine noted that certain morphotypes (body shapes) correlate significantly with metabolic rates and hormonal dominant patterns.

  • Bone Structure: Large, heavy bones often indicate a Phlegmatic or Sanguine baseline. Narrow, fine bones suggest Melancholic.
  • Skin Texture: Naturally oily skin (Damp) vs. naturally parchment-like skin (Dry).
  • Eye Color/Depth: Deep-set eyes often correlate with an inward-focused, Melancholic nature. Bright, protruding eyes can signal a "hot" or hyper-metabolic state.
Coach Tip: The "Why" Behind the "What"

Always explain to your client why you are looking at their nails or tongue. Say: "I'm looking for clues about how your body processes moisture. This helps me pick the exact preparation of the herb that will work best for your unique system." This builds immense trust and legitimacy.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client presents with a red, itchy rash (Heat) but has a lifelong history of being thin, cold, and anxious (Melancholic/Cold-Dry). Is the rash their Prakriti or Vikriti?

The rash is the Vikriti (Current Imbalance). Their Prakriti is Melancholic. To treat this effectively, you must clear the heat of the rash while nourishing the cold/dry baseline so the body doesn't "re-balance" into another inflammatory state.

2. Which temperament is most likely to suffer from "Damp-Heat" conditions like gout or inflammatory skin issues?

The Sanguine temperament. Because they are naturally Hot and Damp, they easily accumulate "excess" which manifests as stagnant heat in the blood and tissues.

3. Why is bone structure considered a reliable indicator of Prakriti?

Bone structure is determined by genetics and early development; it does not change based on temporary illness or diet. It represents the "fixed" physical foundation of the individual's constitution.
