Action & Formulation: Pediatric Dosing Strategies

One of the biggest hurdles for the pediatric herbalist is Compliance. If it tastes like "dirt," the child won't take it, and the parent will give up. Our Action & Formulation (A) must prioritize palatability.

Pediatric Dosing Formulas

While weight-based dosing is gold-standard, two common clinical formulas help determine the percentage of an adult dose:

  • Clark‚Äôs Rule: (Weight of child in lbs / 150) x Adult Dose.
  • Fried‚Äôs Rule (for infants under 2): (Age in months / 150) x Adult Dose.

Palatable Delivery Methods

1. Glycerites: Using vegetable glycerin instead of alcohol. It is naturally sweet and has a low glycemic index. Excellent for nervines and mild immune support.

2. Herbal Honeys & Electuaries: Mixing powdered herbs into raw honey (ensure child is >1 year old). Sambucus nigra (Elderberry) honey is a pediatric staple.

3. Oxymels: A combination of apple cider vinegar and honey. This is the perfect vehicle for pungent herbs like Garlic or Ginger that would otherwise be rejected.

Coach Tip: The "Apple Juice Trick"

For bitter tinctures that must be used (like Echinacea or Gentian), have the parent mix the dose into a single tablespoon of organic apple or grape juice. Instruct the child to "shoot it" and follow with a water chaser. Never mix herbs into a full glass of juice, as the child may not finish the glass, leading to under-dosing.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Triad of Care" important during the Holistic Intake for a child?

The Triad of Care includes the child, the parent, and the practitioner. It is crucial because the parent provides the narrative and manages compliance, while the child provides behavioral clues. Furthermore, the parent's stress levels can directly influence the child's healing environment and the success of the protocol.

2. A 50lb child needs a dose of a tincture where the adult dose is 5ml. Using Clark's Rule, what is the pediatric dose?

Calculation: (50 lbs / 150) = 0.33. Then, 0.33 x 5ml = 1.65ml. The pediatric dose is approximately 1.6ml to 1.7ml.

3. Which tissue state is most commonly associated with Sensory Processing Disorder meltdowns and how should it be addressed?

The "Tension" (or Wind) tissue state. It should be addressed with relaxant nervines and antispasmodics such as Scutellaria lateriflora (Skullcap) or Melissa officinalis (Lemon Balm) to modulate the overactive nervous system.

4. What is the energetic rationale for removing dairy in cases of recurrent ear infections?

Dairy is energetically "Damp" and "Cold." In children with recurrent ear infections, the tissue state is often Damp/Stagnation, leading to fluid backup in the Eustachian tubes. Removing dairy helps reduce mucus production and clears the lymphatic "dampness."
