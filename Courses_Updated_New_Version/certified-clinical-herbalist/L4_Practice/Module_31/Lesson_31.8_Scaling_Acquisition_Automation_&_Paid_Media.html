Handling Objections with Grace

An objection is rarely a "no." It is usually a request for more information or a manifestation of the prospect's fear of failing again.

Confident Pricing Presentation

Never end a call with "I'll email you the prices." State them clearly while you are still on the phone. Use the Package Hierarchy to provide options.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

To build rapport, establish the agenda, and get permission to lead the call. This ensures you maintain authority throughout the conversation.

2. If a prospect says, "I need to talk to my husband," what is the best professional response?

"I completely understand. It‚Äôs important to be on the same page. Aside from his input, is there anything about the program itself that you‚Äôre feeling unsure about?" (This identifies if the husband is the real objection or just a polite way to stall).

3. Why is "Price Anchoring" an effective strategy in clinical practice?

By presenting a high-value, high-cost option first (the "Concierge" level), it makes the standard transformation package feel more affordable and accessible by comparison.

4. What is the recommended ratio of listening vs. talking during the discovery phase?

80% listening, 20% talking. You must fully understand their "pain" and "goals" before you can bridge them to your herbal solution.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a consultation, not a sales pitch; focus on the client's needs.
  • Always use a structured script to maintain professional authority and ensure no details are missed.
  • Handle objections by validating the concern and then refocusing on the "cost of inaction."
  • Present packages rather than hourly rates to emphasize the value of the outcome over the time spent.
  • Consistency in discovery calls is the engine of a six-figure herbal practice.
