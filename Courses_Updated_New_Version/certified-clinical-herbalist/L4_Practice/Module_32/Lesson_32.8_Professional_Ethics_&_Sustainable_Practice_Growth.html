The Discovery Call Script

A discovery call is not a consultation. You are not giving advice yet; you are determining if you are a good fit to work together. Use this 30-minute structure.

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Agenda (0-5 Minutes)

You:

"Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal today is to hear about what you're going through, share a bit about how I work, and if it feels like a fit, we can talk about next steps. How does that sound?"

Phase 2: Identify the "Gap" (5-15 Minutes)

You:

"You mentioned in your form that you're struggling with brain fog and night sweats. Tell me, how is that affecting your day-to-day life right now?"

Linda:

"Honestly, I feel like I'm failing at my job. I can't remember names in meetings, and I'm so exhausted from the night sweats that I'm snappy with my husband."

You:

"I hear you. If we could fast-forward three months and these symptoms were 80% improved, what would that change for you?"

Phase 3: Present Your Approach (15-25 Minutes)

You:

"Linda, based on what you‚Äôve shared, I am very confident I can help. In my Hormonal Harmony Program, we don't just throw Black Cohosh at the problem. We look at your stress response, your liver clearance of hormones, and your mineral status. We work together over 12 weeks to build a foundation so these results actually last."

Sage's Insight

When you name your process (e.g., "The Hormonal Harmony Program"), it creates legitimacy. It shows you have a proven path, which calms the prospect's anxiety about "trying one more thing."

Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are rarely a "no." They are usually a request for more information or a manifestation of fear. As a Clinical Herbalist, your job is to coach them through that fear.

Pricing Presentation: The "State and Wait"

This is where most new practitioners stumble. They state their price and then immediately start justifying it because they feel uncomfortable. Practice this out loud:

The Confident Close

"The 12-week Hormonal Harmony Program is an investment of $1,200. This includes your deep-dive intake, five follow-up sessions, and unlimited messaging support between calls. [STOP TALKING]."

Sage's Insight

Silence is your best friend. After you state the price, wait for them to speak first. If you keep talking, you sound desperate or unsure of your own value. Let them process the number.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Gap" phase in a discovery call?

The goal is to help the prospect realize the distance between their current suffering and their desired health state. This builds the "emotional bridge" that makes the investment feel necessary and urgent.

2. Why should you name your service (e.g., "The Digestive Reset") instead of just saying "herbal consultation"?

Naming your service creates a "proprietary process" feel. it moves you from being a generalist (commodity) to a specialist (expert), which justifies higher pricing and builds trust in your methodology.

3. If a client says "I need to think about it," what is the most professional follow-up?

Acknowledge their need for space, but ask a clarifying question: "I completely understand. Just so I can be helpful, is there a specific part of the program you‚Äôre unsure about‚Äîthe cost, the time, or the approach?" This helps uncover the real objection.

4. What is the "State and Wait" technique?

It is the practice of stating your price clearly and then remaining silent. This demonstrates confidence in your value and gives the prospect the necessary psychological space to make a decision without feeling pressured by "salesy" talk.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy First: A discovery call is about listening more than talking. Linda needs to feel heard before she will trust your herbs.
  • Structure = Confidence: Following a set script prevents you from rambling and ensures you lead the conversation like a professional.
  • Own Your Value: Pricing is a reflection of the transformation you provide, not just the minutes you spend on Zoom.
  • Programs Over Sessions: Selling 3-month packages provides better clinical outcomes and more predictable business income.
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Record yourself practicing the script. The more you say the numbers out loud, the more natural they become.
