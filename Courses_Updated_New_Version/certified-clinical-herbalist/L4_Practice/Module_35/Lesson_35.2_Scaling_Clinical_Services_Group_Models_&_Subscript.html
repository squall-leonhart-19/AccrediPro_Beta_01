Designing Group H.E.R.B.A.L. Intensives

A Group Intensive is a cohort-based program (usually 6‚Äì12 weeks) that focuses on a specific pathology or wellness goal. Instead of repeating the same foundational education to 20 individual clients, you teach the foundations once and use group time for nuance and community support.

By applying the H.E.R.B.A.L. Method‚Ñ¢ to a group, you create a structured journey:

  • H (Holistic Intake): Pre-recorded modules on tracking symptoms and history.
  • E (Energetic Assessment): Teaching the group how to recognize their own tissue states (e.g., "Is your inflammation 'hot' or 'cold'?").
  • R & B (Remedy & Biology): Group education on the materia medica for their specific condition.
  • A (Action): Guided formulation workshops.
  • L (Longevity): Transitioning the cohort into a long-term maintenance community.
Coach Tip: Pricing for Profit

A group intensive should be priced between $600 and $1,500 per person. If you have 10 people in a 12-week program, that is $6,000‚Äì$15,000 for roughly 15‚Äì20 hours of total work. Compare this to the 120 hours required for 10 individual clients over the same period.

Developing Subscription-Based 'Longevity' (L) Programs

The greatest failure in clinical herbalism is the "one-and-done" consultation. True healing happens in the Longevity & Follow-up (L) phase. Subscriptions stabilize your revenue while ensuring the client stays on their protocol.

Tiered Service Architecture: The Ascension Ladder

Scaling requires a clear path for the client to follow. You should never "sell" a single session; you sell a result through a tiered journey.

Step 1: The Entry Point (Low Friction)
A $27‚Äì$47 workshop (e.g., "Herbs for Menopause Sleep"). This builds trust and proves your expertise to a wide audience.

Step 2: The Mid-Tier (The Intensive)
The 8-week group program ($800). This is where the bulk of your impact and profit margin lies.

Step 3: The High-Ticket (Private Clinical Package)
A 3-month private "VIP" experience ($2,500+). Reserved for those who need highly individualized Energetic Assessment (E) and Biological Integration (B).

Case Study: Sarah's Scaling Success

Practitioner: Sarah, 51, former Registered Nurse turned Clinical Herbalist.

The Problem: Sarah was capped at $4,000/month, working 40 hours a week in 1-on-1 sessions. She was exhausted and felt she wasn't "present" for her family.

The Intervention: Sarah launched a 12-week "Metabolic Harmony" group intensive for women 45+. She automated her intake (H) and used 90-minute weekly group Zoom calls for the Energetic (E) and Action (A) phases.

The Outcome: Her first cohort had 12 women at $997 each. She earned $11,964 in 3 months while reducing her working hours by 60%. She then transitioned 8 of those women into a $79/month "Longevity" subscription, creating $632/month in passive recurring revenue.

Ethics & Logistics: Maintaining Quality in Groups

The primary concern with scaling is losing the precision of the Energetic Assessment (E). How do you assess the "tissue state" of 15 people at once?

The Solution: Self-Assessment Education. Instead of you telling them their state, you teach them the indicators. Use high-quality visual aids and symptom checklists to help them identify if they are "Damp/Stagnant" or "Dry/Atrophic." You then provide the "Remedy Selection (R)" options for each state, and they choose with your guidance during the live call.

Coach Tip: HIPAA and Privacy

In group settings, always have clients sign a Group Informed Consent. This document must state that while you are a clinical professional, the group setting does not guarantee private confidentiality among peers, and they should only share what they are comfortable with.

Utilizing Discovery Calls & Automated Screening

As you scale, your time becomes your most expensive asset. You cannot afford to spend 60 minutes with someone who isn't a fit for your high-value programs.

The 3-Step Qualification Funnel:

  1. The Application: A Google Form or Typeform that asks about their health goals, their budget for wellness, and their readiness to change.
  2. The Scorecard: If they don't meet your "readiness" threshold, they are automatically directed to your low-cost workshops or a pre-recorded course.
  3. The 15-Minute Discovery Call: Only for those who qualify for your Intensive or Private Package. This call is not for "free advice"‚Äîit is to determine if you can help them achieve their specific goal.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary financial benefit of a "Longevity" (L) subscription model?

It stabilizes recurring revenue and reduces the "churn" of clients who stop protocols too early, leading to better long-term clinical outcomes and financial predictability.

2. How do you maintain the "E" (Energetic Assessment) in a group setting?

By shifting from "Doing for the client" to "Teaching the client." You provide tools for self-assessment of tissue states, which empowers the client and allows you to oversee multiple assessments simultaneously.

3. What is the purpose of the "Ascension Ladder" in service architecture?

To create a clear pathway for clients to move from low-barrier entry points (workshops) to high-impact, higher-revenue services (Intensives and Private Packages) as trust increases.

4. Why is an automated application form necessary for scaling?

It protects the practitioner's time by pre-screening clients for readiness, budget, and clinical fit before a discovery call is ever scheduled.
