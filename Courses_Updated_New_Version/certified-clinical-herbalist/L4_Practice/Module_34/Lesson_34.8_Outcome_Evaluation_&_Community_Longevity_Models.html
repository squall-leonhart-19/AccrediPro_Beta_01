The Group Enrollment Script

This is a 30-minute call designed to see if Sarah is a fit for your 8-week program, "The Radiant Menopause Method."

Phase 1: Rapport & Permission (0-5 min)

YOU: "Hi Sarah! I've been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal is to hear about your goals and see if my Radiant Menopause group is the right fit. If it is, I'll tell you how it works. If not, I'll point you toward another resource. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: The "Current State" Discovery (5-15 min)

YOU: "You mentioned in your application that the brain fog is affecting your new consulting business. Can you tell me what a 'bad day' looks like right now?"

SARAH: "It's just... I feel like I'm losing my edge. I've tried Black Cohosh from the drugstore, but nothing changes."

YOU: "I hear you. And if we don't get this handled now, where do you see your energy levels in six months?"

Phase 3: The Group Bridge (15-25 min)

YOU: "Sarah, based on what you've said, you're a perfect fit for the Radiant Menopause Method. We actually have three other women joining who are also navigating career shifts while dealing with these exact symptoms. The reason this is a group format is because the 'collective wisdom' speeds up the healing. You'll get the exact herbal protocols I use with private clients, but with a support system that ensures you actually follow through. Does that sound like the kind of support you need?"

Handling Group-Specific Objections

In group programs, objections usually center around privacy or personalization. Use these proven reframes.

Case Study: Elena's "Privacy" Pivot

Practitioner: Elena (48), Clinical Herbalist.

The Challenge: A prospect was hesitant to share her digestive issues in a group call.

The Intervention: Elena explained: "I completely respect your privacy. In our group, we focus on the 'How-To' of the herbal protocols. For deeply personal medical history, you have a private portal to message me directly. The group is for the inspiration and the shared wins."

Outcome: The prospect felt safe and enrolled, later stating the group support was her favorite part of the program.

Sage's Insight

Never defend the group format. Celebrate it! Group programs are not a "cheaper version" of 1-on-1; they are a premium experience of community-led healing. When you believe that, your prospects will too.

Call-to-Action Practice

The "Close" isn't something you do to someone; it's an invitation you extend for them. Practice these three closing lines out loud:

  • The Direct Invite: "Sarah, I'd love to have your energy in this group. Shall we get you registered today?"
  • The Timeline Invite: "We start on the 15th, and I only have 2 spots left for this cohort. Would you like one of them?"
  • The Vision Invite: "Imagine where you'll be in 8 weeks when the hot flashes are gone and you have your 'edge' back. Are you ready to start that journey with us?"
Sage's Insight

If they say "I need to think about it," respond with: "I understand. Usually, when people need to think, it's about the time, the money, or the fear that it won't work for them. Which one is it for you?" This opens up the real conversation.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological benefit of a group program for a woman in mid-life?

The primary benefit is normalization‚Äîrealizing she is not alone in her symptoms, which reduces the stress and isolation that often exacerbate hormonal imbalances.

2. How should you handle a prospect who says they are "too private" for a group?

Acknowledge their concern, then explain the boundaries: group calls focus on education and implementation, while personal health data is handled through private forms or direct messaging.

3. True or False: You should wait until the end of the call to mention that the program is a group format.

False. Transparency is key to building trust. Mention the group format early as a benefit (the "Group Bridge") to frame it as a premium support feature.

4. What is the "Value-First" pricing method?

It is stating the price only after you have clearly summarized the transformation (e.g., getting her energy back) so the prospect weighs the cost against the outcome, not just the number of hours.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs allow for scalable income without increasing your clinical hours exponentially.
  • The Discovery Call for groups focuses on community fit and collective transformation.
  • Objections are usually masks for fear; address them with empathy and clear boundaries.
  • Confidence in pricing comes from repetition and belief in the herbal protocols you've mastered.
  • A successful "Close" is an invitation to a better future, not a sales pitch.
