The 30-Minute Discovery Script

A discovery call is not a free consultation. You are not giving advice yet; you are determining if you are a good fit to work together. Use this 4-phase structure to keep the call on track.

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Agenda (0-5 Minutes)

YOU:

"Hi Sarah! It's so good to finally connect. I've been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal is to hear about what you're going through, tell you a bit about how I work, and see if my Clinical Herbalism program is the right fit for your goals. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)

YOU:

"You mentioned in your intake form that you're struggling with fatigue and brain fog. Tell me, how is that actually affecting your day-to-day life right now?"

SARAH:

"I just can't focus. I sit down to work on my business plan and I end up staring at the screen. I feel like I'm failing before I've even started."

YOU:

"I hear you. And if we were able to get your energy back to where it was five years ago, what would that change for you?"

Phase 3: The Approach (15-25 Minutes)

YOU:

"Sarah, based on what you‚Äôve shared, I‚Äôm confident I can help. In my 12-week 'Vitality Protocol,' we don't just throw herbs at the symptoms. We look at your nervous system and metabolic health to find out WHY the energy isn't there. We'll use specific adaptogens and nutritive tonics tailored exactly to your constitution."

Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 Minutes)

YOU:

"I'd love to partner with you on this. The investment for the 3-month clinical package is $1,200, which includes our deep-dive sessions and your custom formulations. Does that sound like the support you're looking for?"

Coach Tip

Notice I said "investment," not "cost." And I stated the price and then stopped talking. Silence is powerful. Let them process the information without you trying to "rescue" them by offering a discount immediately.

Handling Objections with Grace

Objections aren't rejections; they are requests for more information or reassurance. Here is how to handle the most common ones Sarah might have.

Pricing Presentation

Your pricing should reflect your expertise and the value of the transformation. Use the "Tiered Confidence" model to present your fees.

  • The Foundation: "My initial clinical assessment is $250."
  • The Transformation (Recommended): "Most of my clients choose the 3-month 'Deep Root' package for $1,200, as it provides the consistent support needed for tissue-level change."
  • The Maintenance: "Follow-up check-ins are $125."
Coach Tip

Always lead with the package. It sets a higher "anchor" price and emphasizes that true healing takes time. If they can't afford the package, the individual session fee feels like a bargain, rather than an expense.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The goal is to understand the impact of the symptoms on the client's life and their emotional motivation for change, rather than just listing their physical ailments.

2. If a client says, "I can't afford this right now," what is the best first response?

Validate their concern first. "I hear you, and I know that's a significant investment." Then, ask if it's a matter of total cost or cash flow, which allows you to offer a payment plan.

3. Why is it recommended to offer a 12-week package rather than single sessions?

Clinically, herbal medicine often requires months of consistent use for chronic issues. Business-wise, it ensures client commitment and provides predictable income for the practitioner.

4. What does "Silence is powerful" mean in the context of pricing?

It means stating your price and then waiting for the client to speak. This demonstrates confidence in your value and gives the client space to make a decision without feeling pressured by "salesy" chatter.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are a structured 30-minute process designed to determine "fit," not to provide free clinical advice.
  • Rapport and empathy are your most valuable tools; clients buy from people who make them feel heard.
  • Handling objections is a part of the service; you are helping the client overcome the fears that keep them stuck.
  • A boutique practice with just 5 new clients a month can generate over $70,000 in annual revenue.
  • State your investment price with confidence and use silence to allow the client to process the value.
