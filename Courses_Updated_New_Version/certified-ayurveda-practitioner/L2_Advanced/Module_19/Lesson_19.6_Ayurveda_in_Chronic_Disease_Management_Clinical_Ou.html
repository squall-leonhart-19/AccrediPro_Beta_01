Metabolic Health & Type 2 Diabetes (Madhumeha)

In Ayurveda, Type 2 Diabetes is traditionally categorized under Madhumeha. Modern clinical research has focused heavily on this area due to the global metabolic crisis. A 2021 meta-analysis involving over 2,500 participants demonstrated that Ayurvedic interventions, when combined with standard care, resulted in a significant reduction in HbA1c levels compared to standard care alone.

Researching Mental Health: Anxiety & Neuroplasticity

The "Mind-Body" connection is not just a catchphrase in Ayurveda; it is a clinical reality. Research into Medhya Rasayanas (nootropic herbs) like Ashwagandha and Brahmi has shown profound effects on the HPA axis. A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study found that high-concentration Ashwagandha root extract safely and effectively improves an individual's resistance towards stress and thereby improves self-assessed quality of life.

  • Cortisol Reduction: Studies show up to a 28% decrease in serum cortisol levels after 60 days of Ashwagandha supplementation.
  • Neuroplasticity: Bacopa monnieri (Brahmi) has been shown to increase dendritic proliferation, enhancing communication between neurons.
  • Anxiety Scores: Ayurvedic protocols typically show a significant reduction on the Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale (HAM-A).
