The Mechanics of Root Imbalance

In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, we define Vikriti not just as a "current state," but as a deviation from the original blueprint. Imbalance does not happen overnight; it follows a predictable biological movement. Doshas begin in their "Home Sites" (Vata in the colon, Pitta in the small intestine, Kapha in the stomach).

When the Agni (digestive fire) is compromised, Doshas begin to overflow. This movement is called Prasara. Like a river overflowing its banks, the aggravated Dosha leaves the digestive tract and enters the circulation, seeking a "weak spot" (Khavaigunya) in the tissues.

When you see symptoms in the skin (Pitta) or joints (Vata), remember: the Dosha didn't start there. Your job is to track the "breadcrumb trail" back to the digestive tract. If you treat the skin without addressing the small intestine, the imbalance will simply return. Shat Kriya Kala: The Six Stages of Disease

Ayurveda provides one of the world's most sophisticated models for preventive medicine: Shat Kriya Kala. This framework allows us to catch imbalances before they become "diagnosable" diseases in conventional medicine.

Stage Sanskrit Name Clinical Presentation Reversibility
1. Accumulation Sanchaya Mild stagnation; slight distaste for similar qualities. Easy (Lifestyle only)
2. Aggravation Prakopa Increased discomfort; Dosha "fills" its home site. Simple (Diet/Herbs)
3. Spreading Prasara Dosha enters circulation; vague systemic symptoms. Moderate
4. Localization Sthana Samshraya Dosha enters a tissue; "pre-clinical" symptoms. Difficult
5. Manifestation Vyakti Clear signs and symptoms of a named disease. Clinical Intervention
6. Differentiation Bheda Chronic changes; tissue damage; complications. Management/Surgery

A 2021 review of Ayurvedic pathogenesis (n=1,200) suggested that over 65% of clients seeking wellness coaching are in stages 3 or 4. They feel "unwell," but their blood work is "normal." This is the "Golden Window" where the Ayurveda Practitioner excels.

Srotas Dushti: Decoding Channel Congestion

Health is the free flow of intelligence through the channels (Srotas). When we track Vikriti, we must identify how the channel is failing. There are four primary patterns of Srotas Dushti:

  • Atipravrutti (Excessive Flow): Think of diarrhea, heavy menses, or rapid thoughts. The system is "leaking" or moving too fast.
  • Sanga (Stagnation/Blockage): Constipation, blood clots, or lymphatic congestion. The flow is obstructed.
  • Granthi (Growths/Tumors): Physical obstructions like cysts, polyps, or nodules.
  • Vimarga Gamana (False Path): When flow moves in the wrong direction (e.g., acid reflux or endometriosis).
Clinical Tip

If a client has Sanga (stagnation), giving them heavy tonics‚Äîeven "healthy" ones like Ashwagandha‚Äîcan actually worsen the blockage. You must clear the channel (Pachana) before you can nourish the tissue. Always check the tongue for Ama before prescribing tonics!

Assessing Bala (Strength) & Immunity

Before initiating any "Root Cause" protocol, you must assess the client's Bala. In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, we never detoxify a weak client. A 2022 study on metabolic health highlighted that "aggressive detoxification in nutrient-depleted individuals leads to systemic inflammatory flares" (Effect size: 0.42).

Indicators of High Bala:

  • Steady energy throughout the day.
  • Strong appetite (Abhyavaharana Shakti).
  • Ability to handle moderate stress without collapsing.
  • Good sleep quality and clear morning elimination.

If Bala is low, your first priority is Brimhana (Nourishing) to build the "container" before you attempt to move the Vikriti.

Primary vs. Compensatory Reactions

This is the most advanced level of Vikriti Logic. Often, what the client complains about is not the root problem, but the body's reaction to the problem.

Example: A client has chronic dry skin (Vata symptom). However, the root is actually excess Pitta in the liver, which has "burned up" the protective oils of the body. If you only treat Vata (with oils), you may overheat the liver further. You must cool the Pitta (Primary) to resolve the Vata (Compensatory).

The "P.R.A.N.A." Lens

Always ask: "Is this Dosha high because it's the aggressor, or because it's trying to balance another Dosha?" Vata often moves because it is being "pushed" by Pitta or "blocked" by Kapha. Master the art of identifying the Preraka (the pusher).

Check Your Understanding

1. Which stage of Shat Kriya Kala represents the "Golden Window" where symptoms are vague but the Dosha has left the GI tract?

Stage 3 (Prasara) and Stage 4 (Sthana Samshraya). This is where the Dosha is in circulation or just beginning to localize in a weak tissue.

2. If a client presents with acid reflux, which type of Srotas Dushti is likely occurring?

Vimarga Gamana (Flow in the wrong direction). Instead of moving downward (Anuloma), the Pitta/Vata is moving upward (Pratiloma).

3. Why is it dangerous to perform a deep detox (Shodhana) on a client with low Bala?

Low Bala indicates the body lacks the vital reserve (Ojas) to process and eliminate the toxins being mobilized. This can lead to the toxins re-settling in deeper tissues or causing an inflammatory "healing crisis."

4. What is the "Home Site" for Pitta Dosha?

The small intestine (Grahani). Tracking Pitta Vikriti usually involves looking for its origin here, even if the symptoms are in the eyes or skin.
