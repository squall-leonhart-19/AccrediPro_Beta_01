The 6 Stages of Disease: Shat Kriya Kala

In Ayurveda, disease is not an event, but a process. Shat Kriya Kala provides a chronological framework for understanding how a subtle imbalance in the Doshas evolves into a clinical diagnosis. For a practitioner, identifying where a client is on this map determines the intensity and focus of the intervention.

Stage Ayurvedic Name Description PRANA Action Step
1. Accumulation Sanchaya Dosha increases in its home site (e.g., Vata in colon). A: Agni Optimization
2. Aggravation Prakopa Dosha overflows its site but remains in the GI tract. R: Root Identification
3. Spreading Prasara Dosha enters the circulation and travels through Srotas. R: Root Identification
4. Localization Sthana Samshraya Dosha lodges in weak tissues (Khavaigunya). N: Nature-Synced Nutrition
5. Manifestation Vyakti Full clinical symptoms appear (e.g., Arthritis). A: Awakened Vitality
6. Differentiation Bheda Chronic changes or structural damage occurs. A: Awakened Vitality
Practitioner Insight

Most clients come to you at stages 4 or 5. Your value lies in your ability to "reverse map" their symptoms back to stage 1 or 2. By addressing the Sanchaya (accumulation) phase, you prevent recurrence even after the manifest symptoms disappear.

Srotas & Channel Blockage: The Highway of Health

Health is defined by the free flow of biological intelligence through the Srotas (channels). When these channels are compromised, even the best nutrition (Ahara) cannot reach the tissues (Dhatus). In complex pathophysiology, we look for four specific Srotas abnormalities:

  • Atipravrutti (Excessive Flow): Seen in conditions like diarrhea, polyuria, or heavy menstrual bleeding.
  • Sanga (Stagnation/Blockage): The most common pathology, where Ama (toxins) blocks the channel, leading to constipation, blood clots, or lymphatic congestion.
  • Siragranthi (Structural Growth): The formation of tumors, cysts, or nodules within the channel.
  • Vimarga Gamana (False Passage): When flow moves into the wrong channel, such as bile entering the blood (jaundice) or vomit (upward flow of downward-moving energy).

In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, we use the R (Root Imbalance) phase to determine which Srotas is the primary site of blockage. Often, a client with skin issues (Raktavaha Srotas) actually has a primary blockage in the digestive tract (Annavaha Srotas).

The Interplay of Prana, Tejas, and Ojas

While the Doshas are the gross energies, Prana, Tejas, and Ojas are their subtle counterparts. In chronic, complex cases‚Äîespecially those involving the immune system or nervous system‚Äîwe must assess these three "Essences of Life."

A 2021 study on chronic stress markers (n=450) aligns with Ayurvedic theory: persistent HPA-axis activation (high Vata) eventually depletes the "buffer" of the body. In Ayurveda, this is the depletion of Ojas.

  • Prana (Vata's Subtle Form): Governs the coordination of the breath, senses, and mind. Depletion leads to anxiety and lack of creativity.
  • Tejas (Pitta's Subtle Form): Governs cellular intelligence and "burning" of toxins. If Tejas is too high, it "burns out" Ojas (autoimmunity). If too low, Ama accumulates.
  • Ojas (Kapha's Subtle Form): The ultimate reserve of immunity and resilience. Depletion leads to chronic fatigue and susceptibility to infection.

Advanced Root Identification (R): The "Why" Behind the "What"

When a client presents with a recalcitrant condition (one that doesn't respond to standard treatments), master practitioners look for Khavaigunya‚Äîthe "weak space."

Khavaigunya can be caused by:

  1. Genetic Predisposition: Familial weaknesses in certain organs.
  2. Past Trauma: Physical injuries or emotional shocks that leave a tissue vulnerable.
  3. Repetitive Strain: Lifestyle habits that constantly tax one specific channel (e.g., staring at screens taxing the Majjavaha Srotas).

By identifying the "weak space," you can predict where the next imbalance will land. This is preventative pathophysiology, a premium service that allows you to charge professional rates (often $400+ per session) for high-level health strategy.

Dosha-Dhatu-Mala Synthesis

The final layer of complex mapping is the relationship between the Doshas (energies), Dhatus (tissues), and Malas (waste products). Disease occurs when a Dosha enters a Dhatu and disrupts the Mala production.

Example: Pitta entering Rasa Dhatu (plasma) results in fever and inflammatory skin rashes. The "waste" of Rasa Dhatu is Kapha (mucus). If Rasa is inflamed, mucus production becomes pathological, leading to "dampness" in the system.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which stage of Shat Kriya Kala involves the Dosha leaving the GI tract and entering general circulation?

The answer is Prasara (Spreading). At this stage, the Dosha is no longer localized in its home site or the digestive tract, making systemic intervention necessary.

2. What is the term for a "weak space" in the tissues where a Dosha is likely to lodge?

The term is Khavaigunya. It represents the site of least resistance where pathology localizes during the Sthana Samshraya stage.

3. If a client has tumors or cysts forming in a channel, which Srotas abnormality is present?

The answer is Siragranthi, which refers to structural growths or nodules within the Srotas.

4. How does depleted Ojas manifest in a modern clinical setting?

Depleted Ojas typically manifests as chronic fatigue, low immunity (frequent colds), anxiety, and a feeling of being "brittle" or unable to handle stress.
