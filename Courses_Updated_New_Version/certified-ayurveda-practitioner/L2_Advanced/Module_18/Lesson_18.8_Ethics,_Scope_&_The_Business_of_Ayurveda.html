Check Your Understanding

1. Why must we address Ama before giving Rasayanas like Ashwagandha in Elena's case?

If you give heavy, nourishing Rasayanas while Ama (toxins) is present and Srotas (channels) are blocked, the Rasayana cannot reach the tissues. Instead, it "feeds" the Ama, potentially worsening the bloating, brain fog, and joint pain.

2. Which specific Ayurvedic vital sign suggests Elena has Ama?

Her migratory joint pain (Amavata), bloating after every meal (Agni Mandya), and persistent brain fog (Srotorodha) are classic clinical indicators of Ama.

3. What is the clinical significance of Elena's 3 AM waking?

3 AM is the transition into Vata time. In menopause, this often indicates Vata-Pitta displacement or a blood sugar drop (Metabolic Vata). It suggests the need for nervous system stabilization and a small, protein-rich snack before bed during the early phases.

4. How should you handle Elena's Levothyroxine and Metformin during treatment?

You must NEVER adjust these medications. Your role is to support her body so the medications work better, and then instruct her to work with her MD to adjust dosages based on updated lab results.
