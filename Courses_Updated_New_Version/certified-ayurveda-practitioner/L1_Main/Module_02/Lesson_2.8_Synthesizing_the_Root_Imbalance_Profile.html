Check Your Understanding

1. If Diane (Pitta-Kapha) has Vata symptoms, which dosha should your initial protocol focus on balancing?

Vata. We always treat the Vikriti (current imbalance) first, regardless of the Prakriti (birth constitution).

2. What is a "Root Imbalance" in the context of the P.R.A.N.A. Methodâ„¢?

It is the underlying doshic or digestive disturbance that is generating the surface symptoms. By treating the root, multiple symptoms often resolve simultaneously.

3. Why is Diane's move to a new city relevant to her digestive bloating?

Movement, change, and travel are inherently Vata-aggravating. This environmental stressor is likely the catalyst for her Vata-type digestive issues (Vishamagni).

4. What is a more "client-friendly" way to describe Agni?

"Digestive fire," "metabolic engine," or "the body's ability to transform food into energy."

Key Takeaways

  • Vikriti First: Always address the current state of imbalance before trying to balance the birth constitution.
  • Metaphors Matter: Use language like "wind," "fire," or "stability" to help clients visualize their health.
  • The Detective Mindset: Look for the common thread (like Vata) that connects seemingly unrelated symptoms (like bloating and racing thoughts).
  • Validation is Healing: Simply explaining the "why" behind a symptom can reduce a client's stress significantly.
