Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-explaining the Doshas: In an initial Agni session, don't get bogged down in Vata/Pitta/Kapha. Focus purely on the fire.
  • Being too rigid: If a client loves their smoothie, suggest a "Warm Smoothie" (no ice, add ginger/cinnamon) rather than banning it entirely on day one.
  • Ignoring the "When": Agni isn't just about what we eat, but when. If you don't mention the 10am-2pm Pitta window for the largest meal, you're missing 50% of the solution.
  • Sounding Unsure: When you suggest ginger water, don't say "Maybe you could try..." Say, "I want you to start..." You are the expert.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a raw green smoothie considered "ice on the fire" for a client with Manda Agni?

Raw, cold, and liquid foods require significant metabolic energy to "cook" and digest. If the Agni is already weak (Manda), these foods further dampen the fire, leading to incomplete digestion and bloating.

2. What is the most important "timing" rule to share with a client struggling with slow digestion?

Eating the largest, most complex meal during the "Pitta window" (10:00 AM ‚Äì 2:00 PM) when the environmental and internal Agni is at its peak.

3. If a client says, "I'm not hungry for lunch," what does that tell you about their Agni?

It indicates that their previous meal hasn't been fully digested (low Agni) or that their "appetite signals" are suppressed. In Ayurveda, we wait for "true hunger" before eating, but we use spices (Deepana) to rekindle that hunger.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Ginger Water" ritual?

It acts as a "Deepana" (appetizer/kindler) to stimulate digestive enzymes and stomach acid before food arrives, ensuring the "campfire" is hot enough to handle the meal.
