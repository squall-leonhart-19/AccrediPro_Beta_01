Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

1

The "Sanskrit Dump"

Using too many foreign terms without explaining them. This triggers the client's "imposter syndrome" or makes them feel like this is too "woo-woo" for them.

2

Ignoring the Physical

Focusing only on "energy" while ignoring the fact that Elena has bloating and dry skin. Always ground the subtle in the physical.

3

Rushing the "Build"

Trying to increase Tejas (clarity/action) before refilling Ojas (rest/stability). If you push a burnt-out client to "be more creative" too soon, you'll deplete them further.

Check Your Understanding

1. If Elena complains of "brain fog" and "lack of direction," which subtle energy is likely dimmed?

Tejas. Tejas is the subtle energy of fire that governs mental digestion, clarity, and the "spark" of intelligence.

2. Why is it a mistake to give a "stimulating" protocol to a client with severely depleted Ojas?

Because stimulation (increasing Tejas/Prana) without a foundation of Ojas is like trying to turn up the flame on a lamp with no oil. It leads to deeper burnout. You must build before you burn.

3. What is the "Honey" metaphor used to describe?

Ojas. It represents the sweet, stable, and nourishing essence of the body that provides immunity and resilience.

4. How does mirroring Elena's language help with her "imposter syndrome" regarding her own health?

It validates her experience. By using her words ("hollowed out") and connecting them to Ayurvedic principles, you show her that her symptoms are real, understandable, and solvable.

Key Takeaways

  • Translate, Don't Just Transcribe: Always follow a Sanskrit term with a modern, relatable metaphor (e.g., Ojas = Reservoir).
  • Prioritize Ojas: In midlife burnout, always focus on stabilizing and refilling the "oil" before trying to brighten the "flame."
  • Root in the Physical: Connect subtle energy deficiencies to tangible symptoms like frequent colds or dry skin.
  • Confidence is Contagious: Practice your script out loud until the concepts of Prana, Tejas, and Ojas feel as natural as talking about vitamins.
