Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

As you step into this role, watch out for these 4 common mistakes I see in my mentorship programs:

  • The "Information Dump": Trying to teach the client the entire history of Ayurveda in 60 minutes. (Keep it simple!)
  • Solving Before Listening: Formulating the herb protocol in your head while the client is still crying or sharing their heart.
  • Apologizing for Your Price: Feeling guilty about charging professional rates. Remember: You are providing a clinical transformation, not just "advice."
  • Ignoring the Agni: Recommending heavy lifestyle changes when the client doesn't even have the "digestive fire" to process their current life.
Coach Sarah's Tip

Imposter syndrome is just Vata moving in the mind. When you feel it, come back to your breath and remember: You have the tools to help Elena. You don't need to be perfect; you just need to be present.

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these signs:

  1. The "Exhale": The client physically drops their shoulders or takes a deep, audible breath during the session.
  2. Collaborative Goals: The client says, "I think I could try that," rather than "You're the expert, tell me what to do."
  3. Clarity: The client can repeat back the one main thing they are focusing on this week.
  4. Connection: You feel energized, not drained, after the session.
Coach Sarah's Tip

In my practice, I always end with: "On a scale of 1-10, how confident do you feel about this plan?" If they say anything less than an 8, we simplify the plan further. Compliance is the only path to results.
