Success Markers: How You Know It Worked

A successful session doesn't mean the client is "cured" instantly. It means the relationship and momentum are established. Look for these signs:

  • The "Exhale": The client physically sighs or drops their shoulders when you explain their depletion.
  • Specific Commitment: Instead of saying "I'll try," they say "I can definitely swap my salad for soup."
  • Clarity: They can repeat back to you why they are making the change (e.g., "To protect my Ojas").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a cold kale salad often the wrong choice for a client like Elena?

In Ayurveda, cold and raw foods are difficult to digest and increase Vata (cold/dry qualities). For a depleted client, this extinguishes Agni (digestive fire) and prevents the creation of Ojas, further contributing to fatigue and anxiety.

2. What is the primary metaphorical concept used to describe Ojas to a layperson?

The "Internal Battery." It helps the client understand that vitality is a finite resource that must be recharged through rest and proper nourishment, rather than just "pushed" through.

3. According to the P.R.A.N.A. Methodâ„¢, what should a coach do if a client feels overwhelmed?

Reduce the protocol to its simplest form. Prioritize the most "grounding" action (like warm meals or oil massage) and remove the rest until the nervous system stabilizes.

4. Why is "Package Pricing" recommended over "Hourly Pricing" for vitality coaching?

Package pricing emphasizes the *result* (Awakened Vitality) rather than the time spent. It positions the coach as a professional expert and ensures the client stays committed for the full duration required to see physiological changes.

Key Takeaways

  • Ojas is the Goal: Every intervention for a depleted client should ask, "Does this build or drain Ojas?"
  • Warmth is Medicine: For mid-life women with Vata-type burnout, warmth (in food, drinks, and tone) is the first line of therapy.
  • Authority via Empathy: You gain legitimacy not by knowing every herb, but by deeply understanding the client's experience.
  • Simplicity over Complexity: A single, consistently followed Ayurvedic habit is worth more than a complex 10-step routine.
