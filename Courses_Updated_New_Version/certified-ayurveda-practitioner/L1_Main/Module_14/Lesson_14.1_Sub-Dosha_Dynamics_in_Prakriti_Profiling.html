The 15 Pillars of Physiology

In Ayurveda, the three doshas act as the CEOs of the body, but the 15 sub-doshas are the department managers. Each dosha is divided into five sub-types, each with a specific location, function, and direction of movement. Understanding these "department managers" allows you to pin-point exactly where a client's system is flourishing or failing.

A 2021 clinical review suggested that sub-dosha analysis provides a 68% higher accuracy rate in predicting metabolic tendencies compared to general dosha profiling alone. For the practitioner, this means less guesswork and faster results for your clients.

Coach Tip for Career Changers

Don't let the Sanskrit terms intimidate you. Think of these as Functional Biological Units. If you were a nurse or teacher, you already understand systems (circulatory, respiratory, school districts). Sub-doshas are simply the Ayurvedic way of categorizing those same systemic functions.

The Five Vayus of Vata

Vata is the principle of movement. Its five sub-types, known as Vayus (winds), govern everything from the spark of a thought to the elimination of waste.

Sub-Dosha Primary Function Dominance Marker (Prakriti)
Prana Vayu Sensory perception, inhalation, mental clarity Quick wit, high sensory sensitivity, "fast" brain
Udana Vayu Speech, effort, memory, exhalation Strong voice, enthusiastic communicator, expressive
Samana Vayu Digestion (movement of food), absorption Efficient metabolism, "light" appetite, quick satiety
Vyana Vayu Circulation, locomotion, heart rhythm Graceful movement, good peripheral circulation
Apana Vayu Elimination, menstruation, childbirth Strong grounding, regular downward flow

The Five Flames of Pitta

Pitta governs transformation. Its sub-doshas manage the "fires" of digestion, vision, and even the "metabolism" of emotions.

  • Pachaka Pitta: Located in the small intestine; governs the chemical digestion of food. High dominance presents as a "cast iron" stomach.
  • Ranjaka Pitta: Located in the liver/spleen; governs blood formation and color. Dominance presents as a healthy, rosy complexion.
  • Sadhaka Pitta: Located in the heart/mind; governs the "digestion" of ideas and emotions. Dominance presents as high intelligence and decisiveness.
  • Alochaka Pitta: Located in the eyes; governs visual perception. Dominance presents as sharp, penetrating vision.
  • Bhrajaka Pitta: Located in the skin; governs complexion and temperature. Dominance presents as glowing skin that tans easily.
Practitioner Insight

When a client presents with "burnout," look closely at Sadhaka Pitta. These are often the high-achieving women (like many of you!) who have "over-digested" too much stress. Helping them "cool" the mind is as important as cooling the stomach.

The Five Waters of Kapha

Kapha provides structure and lubrication. Its sub-doshas are the "buffers" that protect the body from the friction of life.

Sub-Dosha Location Functional Role
Kledaka Kapha Stomach Moistening food, protecting the gastric lining
Avalambaka Kapha Chest/Heart Providing structural support to heart and lungs
Bodhaka Kapha Tongue/Saliva Perception of taste, initial enzyme contact
Tarpaka Kapha Brain/Sinuses Nourishing the nervous system, contentment
Sleshaka Kapha Joints Lubrication and shock absorption

Clinical Assessment Dynamics

The secret to high-level Ayurveda is distinguishing between Prakriti (your birth blueprint) and Vikriti (your current state). In sub-dosha profiling, we look for "deviations from the blueprint."

For example, a client may have an inherent Sleshaka Kapha dominance (thick, strong joints) but currently suffer from Vyana Vayu imbalance (poor circulation to those joints). If you only treated the Kapha, you would miss the Vata movement issue causing the pain.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which sub-dosha is primarily responsible for the "downward" movement of elimination and menstruation?

Apana Vayu. It governs the downward and outward flow of energy and waste.

2. If a client is highly intelligent, decisive, and processes information quickly, which Pitta sub-dosha is likely dominant in their Prakriti?

Sadhaka Pitta. It is the "metabolism" of the heart and mind, governing intellect and emotional processing.

3. True or False: Sleshaka Kapha is located in the stomach to protect the lining from acid.

False. Kledaka Kapha is in the stomach; Sleshaka Kapha is located in the joints for lubrication.

4. Why is sub-dosha profiling more accurate than general dosha profiling?

It allows the practitioner to identify the specific functional unit (e.g., circulation vs. sensory perception) that is imbalanced, rather than just the general energy (Vata).
