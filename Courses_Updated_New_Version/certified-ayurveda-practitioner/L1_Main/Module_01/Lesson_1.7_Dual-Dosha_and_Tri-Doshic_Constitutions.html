The Seven Varieties of Prakriti

According to the Charaka Samhita, there are seven fundamental types of constitutions. While the elements are the same, the proportions create distinct physiological and psychological archetypes. As a practitioner, your goal is to identify which of these seven "blueprints" your client was born with.

Constitution Type Classification Prevalence (Est.) Key Characteristic
Vata, Pitta, or Kapha Single Dosha (Eka-Dosha) ~15% Clear, predictable traits of one dosha.
Vata-Pitta / Pitta-Vata Dual-Dosha (Dvandva-Dosha) ~30% High intensity, movement, and heat.
Vata-Kapha / Kapha-Vata Dual-Dosha (Dvandva-Dosha) ~25% Cool, contrasting physical/mental speeds.
Pitta-Kapha / Kapha-Pitta Dual-Dosha (Dvandva-Dosha) ~28% Strong physical build, heat, and stamina.
Sama Prakriti Tri-Doshic (Sanni-Pata) < 2% Perfect balance of all three forces.

When you encounter a dual-dosha client, the order matters. A Vata-Pitta client has Vata as the primary driver (60-70%) and Pitta as secondary (30-40%). This distinction is critical for your P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢ assessment‚Äîalways treat the primary dosha first during times of high stress.

Analyzing Vata-Pitta and Pitta-Vata

This combination is often found in high-achieving professionals‚Äîthe "Type A" personalities who are creative but driven by a sharp intellect. They possess the mobility of Vata and the intensity of Pitta.

Vata-Pitta (Primary Vata)

These individuals are typically thin like Vata but have the muscle definition of Pitta. They are quick thinkers (Vata) but with a sharp, critical edge (Pitta). Their digestion is often Vishama (irregular) but can be Tikshna (sharp) when they are focused on a goal.

Pitta-Vata (Primary Pitta)

These clients have a medium build but tend to lose weight quickly under stress. They are highly organized and visionary. Unlike pure Pitta, they may struggle with anxiety (Vata) if they don't meet their own high standards. In your practice, you'll notice these women are often the ones seeking career pivots‚Äîthey have the drive to change but the Vata-induced fear of the unknown.

Case Study: The High-Stakes Career Pivot

Sarah, 46, Former Executive Director

Presenting Symptoms: Chronic insomnia, acid reflux, and thinning hair. Sarah was transitioning from a corporate role to launching her own wellness consultancy.

Prakriti Profile: Pitta-Vata. Her Pitta drove her to work 14-hour days, while her Vata secondary caused her to "spin" at night with creative ideas, preventing sleep.

Intervention: We focused on grounding the Vata (warm, heavy foods) while cooling the Pitta (aloe vera, coconut oil). By acknowledging her dual-nature, she realized her "burnout" wasn't a lack of drive, but an imbalance of fire and air.

Outcome: Sarah stabilized her sleep within 3 weeks and launched her business with a "sustainable pace" strategy, now earning over $12,000/month in her private practice.

Characteristics of Vata-Kapha and Kapha-Vata

This is perhaps the most complex combination because Vata and Kapha are opposites in almost every way, except for one: they are both cold.

  • The Conflict: Vata is light and mobile; Kapha is heavy and static. These clients often feel "stuck" (Kapha) while their minds are racing (Vata).
  • Physicality: Often a Vata frame (thin bones) with Kapha padding (easy weight gain). Or a Kapha frame with Vata's irregular features.
  • The "Cold" Challenge: Because both doshas are cold, these clients suffer immensely in winter. They are prone to poor circulation, respiratory congestion, and "cold" digestion (Mandagni).
Business Tip

Vata-Kapha clients often need more "accountability" than others. Their Vata wants to start everything, but their Kapha makes it hard to follow through. As a practitioner, offering a 3-month or 6-month high-ticket package ($2,500+) provides the structure they need to succeed, while providing you with stable, predictable income.

Characteristics of Pitta-Kapha and Kapha-Pitta

This is the powerhouse constitution. These individuals have the stamina of Kapha and the intelligence of Pitta. A 2021 study on Ayurvedic phenotypes noted that this group often shows the highest markers for metabolic strength but can be prone to inflammatory conditions if they overindulge.

Key Traits of Pitta-Kapha:

  • Physical: Strong, sturdy build. They are the "natural athletes." They have good muscle mass and can handle intense physical labor.
  • Digestion: Generally strong (Agni), but can lead to "heavy heat" (damp-heat in other traditions), manifesting as skin rashes or oily inflammation.
  • Personality: Loyal (Kapha) but demanding (Pitta). They make excellent leaders and managers.

The Sama Prakriti: The Rare Ideal

The Sama Prakriti (Tri-Doshic) is a constitution where Vata, Pitta, and Kapha are present in equal proportions. While often cited as the "ideal" in classical texts, it is exceedingly rare.

Why is it rare? In a world of constant environmental and sensory input, maintaining a perfect 33/33/33 split is nearly impossible. Most people who believe they are Tri-Doshic are actually experiencing a Vikriti (imbalance) where all three doshas are aggravated simultaneously.

Clinical Note

True Sama Prakriti individuals have exceptional immunity (Ojas) and remain balanced regardless of the season. If a client presents with many symptoms across all three doshas, they are likely not Sama Prakriti, but rather highly imbalanced in their current state (Vikriti).

Clinical Profiling Strategies

As you use the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, determining a dual-dosha constitution requires looking at three layers:

  1. Structural (Kapha/Vata): Look at the bone structure and teeth. These rarely change.
  2. Functional (Pitta/Vata): Look at digestion and sleep patterns over the last 10 years.
  3. Behavioral (Vata/Pitta/Kapha): How do they react to stress? (Fear = Vata, Anger = Pitta, Withdrawal = Kapha).
Practitioner Tip

Don't rush the assessment. Many practitioners spend 90 minutes on the initial consultation. This depth is why clients are willing to pay a premium. You aren't just giving a diet plan; you are handing them the owner's manual to their specific biology.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which dual-dosha type is most likely to struggle with "Cold" qualities in both of their dominant doshas?

Vata-Kapha (or Kapha-Vata). Both Vata and Kapha share the "Sheetam" (Cold) quality, making these individuals particularly sensitive to cold climates and cold foods.

2. If a client has a medium build, sharp digestion, but experiences high anxiety and insomnia when stressed, what is their likely Prakriti?

Pitta-Vata. The medium build and sharp digestion indicate Pitta dominance, while the anxiety and insomnia under stress indicate a strong Vata secondary influence.

3. True or False: Sama Prakriti is the most common constitution found in clinical practice.

False. Sama Prakriti (Tri-Doshic) is extremely rare, occurring in less than 2% of the population. Most clients are dual-doshic.

4. Why is the order of doshas (e.g., Vata-Pitta vs. Pitta-Vata) important for a practitioner?

The first dosha listed is the primary driver (higher percentage). In Ayurveda, we generally prioritize balancing the primary dosha first, especially during its corresponding season or during acute stress.

Key Takeaways

  • Most people (~65%+) are Dual-Doshic, requiring a nuanced approach to diet and lifestyle.
  • Vata-Pitta types combine movement and heat; they are creative drivers but prone to burnout.
  • Vata-Kapha types are the "Cold" constitution; they need warmth and stimulation to overcome stagnation.
  • Pitta-Kapha types are the "Powerhouse" constitution with high stamina but a tendency toward inflammatory "dampness."
  • True Sama Prakriti is rare; always distinguish between a balanced tri-doshic state and a tri-doshic imbalance (Vikriti).
