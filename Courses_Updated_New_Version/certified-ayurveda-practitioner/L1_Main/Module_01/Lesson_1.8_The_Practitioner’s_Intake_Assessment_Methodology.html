Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

Avoid these four mistakes to maintain your professional authority during the profiling phase:

  • The "Dosha Box" Trap: Don't tell a client "You are a Vata." Instead, say "You have a predominantly Vata constitution." This leaves room for the complexity of the human experience.
  • Over-Sanskritizing: Using too many Sanskrit terms (like Tikshna Agni or Anulomana) in the first session can overwhelm the client. Stick to "sharp digestion" or "downward flow."
  • The Diagnostic Rush: New coaches often try to guess the Dosha within the first 5 minutes. Wait. Let the client speak. The most important clues often come in the last 10 minutes of the session.
  • Ignoring the Prakriti/Vikriti Distinction: Don't mistake a temporary imbalance (Vikriti) for their permanent nature (Prakriti). Just because Diane is angry today doesn't mean she is a Pitta Prakriti.

Success Markers

How do you know if your Prakriti profiling session was successful? Look for these three signs:

The "Aha" Moment

The client says something like, "That explains why I've always hated cold weather!" or "I finally understand why I get so irritable when I miss lunch."

Visible Relaxation

You notice the client's shoulders drop. Understanding their Prakriti removes the guilt of "not being like everyone else."

Compliance Buy-In

The client is eager for the plan because they understand why it's specifically for them, not just a generic health tip.

Check Your Understanding

1. Diane mentions she has always been a "perfectionist" and gets very frustrated when things aren't organized. Which Dosha does this trait primarily associate with?

This is a primary trait of Pitta. While Vata may want things organized to calm their anxiety, the drive for perfection and the resulting frustration/anger are classic Pitta markers.

2. If Diane starts crying during the session because she feels overwhelmed, what is the best "Sarah-approved" coaching response?

Pause, offer a tissue, and validate her. Say: "It's okay to feel this way. This is your Vata energy feeling over-extended. We are going to find the grounding you need." Avoid rushing to the next question.

3. What is the main difference between Prakriti and Vikriti that you should explain to a client?

Prakriti is your permanent nature (your blueprint), while Vikriti is your current state of imbalance (the dust on the blueprint). We profile Prakriti to know the goal, and we address Vikriti to get there.

4. Why is it important to ask about a client's childhood traits during a Prakriti assessment?

Childhood traits are less likely to be influenced by current stressors or lifestyle choices. They provide the clearest window into the client's original Prakriti before life's imbalances (Vikriti) took over.
