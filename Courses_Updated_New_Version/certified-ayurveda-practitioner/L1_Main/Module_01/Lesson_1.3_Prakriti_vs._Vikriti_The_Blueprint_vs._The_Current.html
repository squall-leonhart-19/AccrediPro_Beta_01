Defining Prakriti: The Original Nature

In Ayurveda, Prakriti (literally "nature" or "original creation") is the specific combination of Vata, Pitta, and Kapha that is set at the moment of conception. Think of it as your client's genetic "hard-wiring."

This constitution remains constant throughout a person's life. While a person's behavior, weight, or health may fluctuate, their baseline tendencies‚Äîsuch as bone structure, eye color, and primary metabolic speed‚Äîare dictated by Prakriti. In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, we view Prakriti as the gold standard for health; it represents the state of perfect balance for that specific individual.

Defining Vikriti: The Deviation

If Prakriti is the blueprint, Vikriti is the current state of the building. It represents the "modified" state of the doshas. In almost every client who seeks your help, there will be a gap between these two states. This gap is where disease lives.

Vikriti is influenced by everything we do: diet, lifestyle, seasons, age, and environment. When we say a client is "Vata aggravated," we are describing their Vikriti. Our goal as practitioners is not to change their Prakriti, but to bring their Vikriti back into alignment with their Prakriti.

The Role of Parental Health (Shukra and Artava)

Ayurveda was thousands of years ahead of modern epigenetics. It teaches that Prakriti is determined by four primary factors at the time of conception:

  • Shukra (Sperm): The health and dominant dosha of the father.
  • Artava (Ovum): The health and dominant dosha of the mother.
  • Kala (Time/Season): The environmental energetics at the moment of conception.
  • Matur-Ahara (Maternal Diet/Lifestyle): The environment of the womb (Garbhashaya) during gestation.

This is why we often see a "career changer" practitioner in her 50s find so much success working with young couples for preconception care. By optimizing the parents' health, we are literally influencing the "Prakriti" of the next generation.

Presenting Symptoms: High anxiety, insomnia, dry skin, and racing thoughts. Elena had diagnosed herself as "Vata" using a 3-minute online quiz.

The Discovery: Elena‚Äôs Prakriti was actually Pitta-dominant. Her anxiety and dryness were Vikriti (Vata imbalance) caused by 20 years of high-stress work and a caffeine-heavy diet. Differentiating Trait vs. Symptom

One of the most vital clinical skills you will develop is the ability to distinguish a Trait (Prakriti) from a Symptom (Vikriti). This requires "Clinical Listening."

1. Constitutional Traits (Prakriti)

These are long-term, consistent patterns. If a client says, "I have always had a slow metabolism, even as a child," that is likely a Kapha Prakriti trait. If they say, "I have always been a light sleeper," that is a Vata Prakriti trait.

2. Temporary Symptoms (Vikriti)

These are recent changes or "aggravations." If a client says, "I‚Äôve been feeling really bloated lately, but I used to be able to eat anything," the bloating is Vikriti. We do not use the bloating to define their Prakriti; we use it to identify their root imbalance (the "R" in P.R.A.N.A.).

üí° Assessment Strategy: Always ask your clients: "Is this how you have always been, or is this how you are feeling lately?" This simple question is the fastest way to separate Prakriti from Vikriti.

Key Takeaways

  • Prakriti is the Blueprint: It is the fixed energetic DNA of the client that never changes.
  • Vikriti is the Deviation: It is the current state of imbalance that practitioners seek to resolve.
  • Conception is Key: Parental health at the moment of conception determines the child's lifelong constitution.
  • P.R.A.N.A. Priority: We must profile Prakriti first to establish the goal of the clinical intervention.
  • Clinical Wisdom: Distinguishing between a lifelong trait and a recent symptom is the hallmark of an expert practitioner.
