Mapping the 'Gap': Quantifying the Distance

The "Gap" is the numerical or qualitative distance between a clientâ€™s natural state and their current state. For example, if a client is naturally Kapha-dominant (stable, calm, slow metabolism) but is currently experiencing Vata-type anxiety and insomnia, the "Gap" is a Vata-driven deviation from a Kapha baseline.

Why the Gap Matters: If you treat a Kapha client for their Vata anxiety using heavy, grounding foods (which normally balance Vata), you might inadvertently crash their Kapha metabolism, leading to weight gain and lethargy. You must bridge the gap without compromising the baseline.

The Causes of Deviation: Kala, Artha, and Karma

In Ayurveda, Vikriti doesn't happen by accident. It is caused by Trividha Hetuâ€”the three primary causes of disease:

1. Kala (Time & Season)

This refers to the effects of chronological age (e.g., Vata increases as we enter the 50+ stage of life) and the impact of the seasons. A Pitta-dominant woman in her 40s will experience a much higher "Gap" during a hot July than she will in January.

2. Artha (Sensory Contact)

Known as Asatmyendriyartha Samyoga, this is the unwholesome contact of senses with their objects. Examples include excessive screen time (Vata), loud environments, or eating food that is "too hot" for your constitution.

3. Karma (Action/Volition)

Prajnaparadha, or "crimes against wisdom." This is when we know what is good for us but do the opposite. For many of your clientsâ€”women in their 40s and 50sâ€”this often manifests as over-working or neglecting self-care in favor of family demands.

Prioritization Logic: The 2-Dosha Rule

What happens when a client has multiple doshas out of balance? This is the most common scenario for premium practitioners. You must follow the Ayurvedic Hierarchy of Intervention:

  • Step 1: Address the "Emergency" (Agni & Ama): Before balancing doshas, you must clear toxins (Ama) and stoke the digestive fire (Agni). We cover this in the 'A' of PRANA.
  • Step 2: Balance the Aggravated Dosha: Treat the Vikriti first, even if it contradicts the Prakriti.
  • Step 3: Respect the Season: If a client has Vata Vikriti during Pitta season (Summer), you must use "Cooling Grounding" techniques rather than "Heating Grounding."

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client has a Pitta Prakriti but is currently suffering from Vata-type constipation, which dosha should you address first?

You address the Vikriti (Vata) first. In clinical practice, the current imbalance takes priority over the baseline constitution to prevent further pathological progression.

2. What does "Kala" refer to in the context of doshic deviation?

Kala refers to Time, including seasonal changes, time of day, and the chronological stage of life (e.g., the Vata stage of life after age 50).

3. True or False: Prakriti can change throughout a person's life depending on their diet.

False. Prakriti is the genetic blueprint fixed at conception. Vikriti is what changes based on diet, lifestyle, and environment.

4. Which cause of deviation is described as a "crime against wisdom" (Prajnaparadha)?

Karma (Action/Volition). This occurs when an individual consciously makes choices that they know will aggravate their doshas or harm their health.
