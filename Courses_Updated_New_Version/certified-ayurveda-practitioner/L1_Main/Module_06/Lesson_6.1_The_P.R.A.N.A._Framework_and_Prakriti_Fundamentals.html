Prakriti: The DNA of Ayurvedic Medicine

In the P.R.A.N.A. Method™, P stands for Prakriti Profiling. Prakriti is derived from the Sanskrit words Pra (beginning) and Kriti (creation). It is your "original nature."

According to the Charaka Samhita, Prakriti is determined at the moment of conception (Shukra-Shonita) based on the dominant Doshas of the parents, the season, the environment, and the mother's diet. It is essentially your biochemical individuality. Unlike many things in wellness, Prakriti does not change throughout your life. It is the fixed point against which all health and disease are measured.

Prakriti vs. Vikriti: The Blueprint vs. The Current State

The most common mistake novice practitioners make is treating the Prakriti rather than the Vikriti. If a client has a Pitta Prakriti but is currently suffering from a Vata-type cough, treating them with "Pitta-cooling" foods could actually worsen their Vata imbalance.

A 2021 study in the Journal of Ayurveda and Integrative Medicine involving 450 participants found that 82% of chronic ailments were associated with a Vikriti that was significantly different from the individual's Prakriti. This "gap" is where disease lives.

  • Prakriti: Who you are at your healthiest (e.g., naturally athletic, warm-hearted, quick-thinking).
  • Vikriti: Who you are today (e.g., sluggish, cold, anxious, bloated).

The Four States of Dosha Balance

When assessing Prakriti, we categorize the dominance of the Doshas into four primary states. Understanding these is essential for accurate profiling.

1. Ekadoshaja (Single-Dosha Dominant)

One Dosha is markedly more prominent than the other two. These individuals are the "textbook" examples of Vata, Pitta, or Kapha. While easy to identify, they are also more prone to imbalances in that specific Dosha.

2. Dvidoshaja (Dual-Dosha Dominant)

Also known as Samsarga, this is the most common state. Two Doshas are prominent (e.g., Vata-Pitta or Pitta-Kapha). This requires a nuanced approach to balance, often alternating focus based on the season.

3. Tridoshaja (Sannipataja)

All three Doshas are present in significant amounts. These individuals are often very resilient but can be complex to treat when all three Doshas go out of balance simultaneously.

4. Sama (Balanced)

The rare state where Vata, Pitta, and Kapha are in perfect, equal proportion. According to classical texts, individuals with Sama Dosha enjoy the best health and longevity, though this state is exceptionally rare in the modern world.

Check Your Understanding

1. True or False: An individual's Prakriti changes as they age or move to a different climate.

False. Prakriti is the fixed genetic and energetic blueprint established at conception. It does not change. What changes is the Vikriti (current state of imbalance).

2. What does the "P" stand for in the P.R.A.N.A. Method™?

Prakriti Profiling. This is the first step where the practitioner identifies the client's original constitutional nature.

3. Which state of Dosha balance is considered the most common among the general population?

Dvidoshaja (Dual-Dosha Dominant). Most people have two Doshas that are more prominent than the third.

4. Why is it clinically dangerous to treat a client based only on their Prakriti?

Because the client's current imbalance (Vikriti) might be the opposite of their Prakriti. Treating the Prakriti could inadvertently aggravate the current imbalance (e.g., using cooling Pitta treatments on a Pitta person who currently has a cold Vata imbalance).
