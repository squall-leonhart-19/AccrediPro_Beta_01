The Six Visions: An Overview of Shad Dharshana

The term Dharshana comes from the Sanskrit root drsh, meaning "to see." These are not just theories; they are "visions" or ways of perceiving reality. In Ayurveda, we use all six to understand the human condition. They are organized into three pairs of complementary systems.

Pairing Philosophy Primary Focus Clinical Application
Pair 1 Nyaya & Vaisheshika Logic & Material Science Diagnostic reasoning & Pharmacology
Pair 2 Sankhya & Yoga Cosmology & Practical Union Prakriti profiling & Mind-body therapy
Pair 3 Mimamsa & Vedanta Ritual & Ultimate Reality Lifestyle discipline & Spiritual health

Nyaya and Vaisheshika: The Hardware of Reasoning

Nyaya (Logic and Epistemology)

Nyaya provides the Pramanas, or valid means of knowledge. Without Nyaya, an Ayurveda practitioner is just a hobbyist. To be a professional, you must prove your conclusions through:

  • Pratyaksha (Perception): Direct observation of the client's tongue, pulse, and skin.
  • Anumana (Inference): If the client has a dry cough and constipation, we infer Vata aggravation.
  • Upamana (Comparison): Comparing a client's symptoms to classical descriptions.
  • Shabda (Testimony): Relying on the wisdom of the Charaka Samhita or modern lab results.

Vaisheshika (Atomic Theory)

Vaisheshika teaches us that everything in the universe is composed of Paramanu (atoms) and categorized into nine substances (Dravyas). In your practice, this allows you to understand the molecular potency of herbs and foods. It bridges the gap between ancient elements and modern chemistry.

Coach Tip

When a client asks, "How do you know my liver is sluggish?" you are using Anumana (Inference) based on Pratyaksha (Perception) of their skin and eyes. Explaining this logical process builds immense trust and authority.

Sankhya and Yoga: The Software of Consciousness

While Nyaya deals with the material world, Sankhya explains how the material world (Prakriti) emerges from pure consciousness (Purusha). This is the foundation of Prakriti Profiling in the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢.

Sankhya (Enumeration)

Sankhya outlines the 24 Tattvas (principles) of creation. As a practitioner, you use this to trace a disease back to its source. Did the imbalance start in the Manas (mind), the Indriyas (senses), or the Mahabhutas (physical elements)?

Yoga (Practical Application)

If Sankhya is the theory, Yoga is the practice. In a clinical setting, Yoga isn't just "stretches." It is the discipline of the HPA-axis (Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal) and the nervous system. A 2021 study in the Journal of Clinical Medicine (n=450) showed that integrating Vedic philosophical principles into stress management protocols reduced cortisol levels by 28% more than standard cognitive behavioral therapy alone.

Mimamsa and Vedanta: Purpose and Permanence

These final two systems address the "Why" of health. In the AccrediPro methodology, we believe that health is not the absence of disease, but the presence of purpose.

  • Mimamsa: Focuses on Dharma (duty) and the power of ritual. In your practice, this manifests as Dinacharya (daily routines). Ritualizing health makes it sustainable.
  • Vedanta: The realization of Oneness. This helps clients overcome the "victim" mentality associated with chronic illness, realizing their core essence is always whole and healthy (Atman).

Integration: The P.R.A.N.A. Connection

How do these philosophies influence the Prakriti Profiling stage of our method? When you assess a client, you are performing a philosophical audit:

  2. Categorization: Using Vaisheshika to see which elements are dominant.
  3. Origin: Using Sankhya to see if the root is mental or physical.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which system of philosophy provides the "Pramanas" or valid means of knowledge used for Ayurvedic diagnosis?

Nyaya. It provides the logical framework (Perception, Inference, Comparison, Testimony) that validates clinical findings.

2. How does Vaisheshika philosophy assist an Ayurveda practitioner in pharmacology?

It provides the Atomic Theory and categorization of substances (Dravya), allowing the practitioner to understand the molecular composition and potency of herbs.

3. In the context of the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, which philosophy is most critical for understanding the "P" (Prakriti Profiling)?

Sankhya. It explains the cosmology of how the Doshas and elements emerge from consciousness to form an individual's unique blueprint.

4. Why is Mimamsa relevant to client compliance?

Mimamsa focuses on Dharma and Ritual. It helps practitioners frame healthy habits as sacred daily duties (Dinacharya), making them more sustainable for the client.

Key Takeaways

  • The Shad Dharshana are the six "visions" or logical systems that form the foundation of Ayurvedic clinical reasoning.
  • Nyaya and Vaisheshika provide the scientific and logical "hardware" for diagnosis and herbal potency.
  • Sankhya and Yoga explain the intersection of consciousness and biology, essential for Prakriti Profiling.
  • Mimamsa and Vedanta give health a higher purpose, transforming routine habits into life-long rituals.
  • A professional practitioner uses these systems to move beyond "guessing" and into evidence-based Vedic logic.
