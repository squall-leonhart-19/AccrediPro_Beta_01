Structural Analysis: The Physical Markers

While weight can change, bone structure and certain tissue traits remain relatively constant, providing clues to the original Prakriti. Use the following table to categorize structural markers during your assessment.

Psychological Profiling: Assessing the Mind

In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, we don't just look at the body. We assess Manas Prakriti (Mental Constitution). This is vital for women in high-stress careers who often suffer from "Vata-derangement" masquerading as anxiety.

  • Vata Mental Tendencies: Quick to learn, quick to forget. Creative, imaginative, but prone to anxiety and "racing thoughts."
  • Pitta Mental Tendencies: Sharp intellect, focused, goal-oriented. Brave and decisive, but prone to irritability, anger, and perfectionism.
  • Kapha Mental Tendencies: Slow to learn, never forgets. Calm, steady, loyal, but prone to lethargy, attachment, and stubbornness.

Utilizing P.R.A.N.A. Assessment Intake Forms

Accuracy depends on data. Your P.R.A.N.A. intake forms are designed to capture multi-dimensional data. When reviewing a client's intake form, look for "clusters" of symptoms.

The 4 Essential Intake Sections:

  1. The Chief Complaint: What brought them in today? (Usually Vikriti).
  2. The History of Origin: When did they last feel truly "themselves"? (Helps identify the Prakriti baseline).
  3. The Agni Audit: Detailed questions on hunger, thirst, and elimination.
  4. The Lifestyle Narrative: Sleep cycles, exercise habits, and relationship stressors.

The Health Map: Establishing the Baseline

Once you have gathered your data through Darshana, Sparshana, and Prashna, you must synthesize it into a Health Map. This is a visual or written summary you present to the client.

A Health Map should include:

  • Primary Prakriti: Their core nature (e.g., Pitta-Kapha).
  • Current Vikriti: The specific Dosha currently out of balance (e.g., High Vata).
  • Agni Status: Is their digestive fire Manda (slow), Tikshna (sharp), or Vishama (irregular)?
  • The Path to Balance: A high-level view of the next 90 days.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which pillar of Trividha Pariksha involves the clinical interview and questioning the client about their history?

Prashna. This is the questioning phase where the practitioner uncovers the client's lifestyle, history, and subjective experience of symptoms.

2. If a client has broad shoulders, thick hair, and large, calm eyes, which Dosha is likely dominant in their Prakriti?

Kapha. These are the classic structural markers of Kapha dominance (heavy bone structure and thick, lustrous tissues).

3. True or False: Mental tendencies (Manas Prakriti) are less important than physical traits in Ayurvedic assessment.

False. In the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢, psychological profiling is equally important, as mental imbalances often precede physical disease.

4. What is the primary purpose of the "Health Map"?

To synthesize assessment data into a baseline that identifies the client's Prakriti, current Vikriti, and the strategic path forward for the practitioner-client journey.

Key Takeaways

  • Clinical assessment is an integrated process of observation (Darshana), touch (Sparshana), and inquiry (Prashna).
  • Structural markers like bone density and eye shape provide the most reliable clues to a client's genetic Prakriti.
  • Psychological profiling helps distinguish between a client's true nature and their current state of stress or anxiety.
  • Intake forms must be comprehensive, covering Agni, lifestyle, and history to prevent "symptom-chasing."
  • The Health Map serves as the "North Star" for the entire P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢ protocol.
