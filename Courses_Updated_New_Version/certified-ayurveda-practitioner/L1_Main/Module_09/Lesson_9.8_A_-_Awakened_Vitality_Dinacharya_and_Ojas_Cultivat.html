Common Practitioner Pitfalls

A 2022 study on health coaching efficacy found that "practitioner over-explanation" was the #1 cause of client drop-off in the first 30 days (n=1,200).

  • The "Sanskrit Dump": Using terms like Vata-Kala or Samana Vayu without explaining the feeling behind them.
  • Fixing Instead of Coaching: Giving Diane 10 things to do at once. A Vata-imbalanced client will feel overwhelmed and do zero of them.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Telling her to eat oatmeal but not explaining that the "warmth and heaviness" is what stops her anxiety.
  • The Imposter Apology: Using phrases like "I think..." or "Maybe we could try..." instead of "Based on your assessment, the most effective path is..."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is keto often detrimental to a client like Diane with Vishamagni?

Keto focuses on cold, dry, and heavy fats which can further destabilize an erratic digestive fire (Agni) and increase the "cold/dry" qualities of Vata, leading to more anxiety and bloating.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Translation" phase in a consultation?

To turn clinical findings into a relatable "story" that makes the client feel understood and motivated to follow the plan.

3. How many interventions should you typically give a Vata-imbalanced client in the first session?

Exactly 2-3. Any more will trigger the Vata overwhelm response, leading to non-compliance.

4. What does "Warm Authority" look like in a coaching session?

It is the balance of deep empathy (I hear you) with confident clinical direction (This is the path forward).
