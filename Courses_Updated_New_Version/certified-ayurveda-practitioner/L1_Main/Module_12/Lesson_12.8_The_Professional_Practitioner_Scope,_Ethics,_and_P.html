Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my first year, I made all of these. You don't have to!

  • The "Information Dump": Trying to teach the client the entire history of Ayurveda in session one. They don't want a history lesson; they want to sleep.
  • Ignoring the "Win": Forgetting to tell the client what they are doing *right*. Lisa likely feels like a failure; highlight her resilience.
  • The Jargon Trap: Using words like *Srotas* or *Ojas* before the client even understands what a Dosha is.
  • Lack of Clear Next Steps: Ending the session with "Let me know if you want to work together." Instead, use: "Based on today, I recommend we start with a 3-week Agni-reset. Shall we get that on the calendar?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use metaphors like "operating systems" with a client like Lisa?

It builds a bridge of legitimacy by using language familiar to her background (corporate/HR), reducing the "woo-woo" barrier and establishing trust.

2. What is the difference between Prakriti and Vikriti in a coaching context?

Prakriti is the client's original healthy blueprint; Vikriti is their current state of imbalance. Explaining this helps the client realize they aren't "broken," just "out of alignment."

3. According to the lesson, what is the "Information Dump" mistake?

Overwhelming the client with too much theory or history in the first session instead of focusing on their immediate needs (like sleep or digestion).

4. How should you handle a client's skepticism?

Validate it as "discernment" and suggest that the results of the protocol (better sleep/energy) will provide the necessary proof.
