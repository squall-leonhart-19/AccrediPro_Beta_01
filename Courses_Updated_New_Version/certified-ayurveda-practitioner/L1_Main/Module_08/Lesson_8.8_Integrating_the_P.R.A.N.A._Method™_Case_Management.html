Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When discussing subtle essences, it is easy to lose the client. Avoid these four common mistakes:

  • Over-Sanskritizing: Using terms like Prana without explaining them. Always follow a Sanskrit term with a modern metaphor.
  • Being Too Clinical: Talking about "mitochondrial function" when the client wants to talk about "feeling their glow." Match their emotional language.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Telling a client to take Ashwagandha (an Ojas-builder) without explaining that it's "protecting their savings account."
  • Rushing the Replenishment: Ojas takes time to build (classically 30 days). Don't promise a 24-hour fix. Be honest about the Sneha (oiliness/love) required for the process.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client describes themselves as "burnt out" but "wired." Which essence is likely overactive, and which is depleted?

Tejas (the fire) is overactive/burning too hot, and Ojas (the stability/juice) is depleted. This is the classic "tired but wired" state.

2. What is the most effective modern metaphor for explaining Ojas to a corporate-minded client?

A "Biological Savings Account" or a "Safety Buffer." It represents the reservoir of energy the body keeps for emergencies and long-term health.

3. True or False: Prana is only about the breath.

False. While the breath is the primary vehicle, Prana is the master intelligence that coordinates all cellular communication and vitality.

4. Why is caffeine particularly damaging to a client with low Ojas?

Caffeine artificially stimulates Tejas (fire), which "cooks" or dries out the remaining Ojas (juice), leading to deeper depletion and "brittleness" over time.
