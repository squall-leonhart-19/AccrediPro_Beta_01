The Forbidden Three: Diagnosis, Treatment, & Prescription

In the eyes of the law (specifically in the US, UK, and Canada), the practice of medicine is defined by three specific actions. If you perform these without a medical license, you are practicing medicine without a license‚Äîa serious legal violation. To protect your practice, you must understand the "Non-Doctor" Protocol.

Medical Action Legal Definition The Practitioner Alternative
Diagnosis Identifying a disease or condition from its signs and symptoms. Assessment & Profiling: Identifying bio-energetic imbalances (Vikriti).
Treatment Medical care given to a patient for an illness or injury. Support & Education: Providing lifestyle and nutritional guidance to restore balance.
Prescription An instruction written by a medical practitioner for a medicine. Recommendation: Suggesting specific foods, herbs, or rituals for wellness.

Reframing Clinical Language

The words you use in your marketing, intake forms, and sessions are your primary legal defense. Reframing is not about "hiding" what you do; it is about accurately describing the nature of Ayurvedic wellness coaching, which is fundamentally different from allopathic medicine.

Linguistic Reframing Examples

  • Instead of: "I can treat your insomnia."
    Use: "We can explore Ayurvedic rituals to support a healthy sleep-wake cycle."
  • Instead of: "This herb is a cure for your anxiety."
    Use: "This botanical support is traditionally used to calm Vata and promote mental clarity."
  • Instead of: "I am diagnosing you with a Pitta disorder."
    Use: "Our assessment shows a current Pitta imbalance (Vikriti) in the digestive channel."

Medical 'Red Flags' & Mandatory Referral

A "Premium" practitioner knows when not to work alone. Establishing a referral protocol is not a sign of weakness; it is a hallmark of professional integrity. If a client presents with "Red Flags," your protocol must be: Immediate Referral to a Medical Professional.

Critical Red Flags (Refer Immediately):

  • Unexplained Weight Loss: More than 10 lbs in a month without trying.
  • Sudden Neurological Changes: Slurred speech, numbness, or loss of balance.
  • Severe Abdominal Pain: Acute, sharp, or localized pain that suggests appendicitis or obstruction.
  • Suicidal Ideation: Any mention of self-harm requires immediate mental health referral.
  • Lumps or Unusual Growths: Any new masses must be cleared by an oncologist or PCP.

The Legalities of Pulse (Nadi) and Tongue (Jihva)

In Ayurveda, the pulse and tongue are diagnostic tools. In a coaching environment, they must be used as Educational Assessment Tools.

Legal Safeguard

When performing Nadi Vigyan (Pulse Assessment), never say: "Your pulse shows you have a heart condition." Instead, say: "I am noticing a 'frog-like' quality (Manduka Gati) in the Pitta position, which suggests your system is processing a high level of heat right now."

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks, "Can you prescribe something for my chronic migraines?" What is the correct legal response?

"I cannot prescribe medication or treat migraines as a medical condition. However, we can look at the Vata-Pitta imbalances that may be contributing to your discomfort and recommend specific dietary changes and herbs to support systemic balance."

2. Which of the 'Forbidden Three' involves identifying a disease from its symptoms?

Diagnosis. Practitioners should instead use "Assessment" or "Profiling" to identify energetic imbalances (Vikriti).

3. True or False: If a client shows sudden slurred speech, you should suggest a Vata-pacifying tea.

False. Sudden slurred speech is a "Red Flag" for a stroke. You must refer them to emergency medical services immediately.

4. How should you describe Nadi Vigyan (Pulse Assessment) to a new client?

As an "educational assessment tool" used to determine the current state of their bio-energies (Doshas) and digestive fire (Agni).
