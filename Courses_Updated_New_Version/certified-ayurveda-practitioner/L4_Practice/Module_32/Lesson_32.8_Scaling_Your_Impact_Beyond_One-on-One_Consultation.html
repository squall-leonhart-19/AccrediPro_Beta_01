2. The 4-Phase Discovery Call Script

A successful call is not a lecture; it is a diagnostic journey. According to sales psychology research, the person asking the questions controls the conversation.

Phase 1: Rapport & Permission (0-5 Minutes)

YOU:

"Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, I'd love to hear‚Äîwhat was it about my practice that made you say 'I need to talk to her' right now?"

LINDA:

"Honestly, I saw your post about Vata-imbalance and insomnia. It felt like you were reading my diary."

YOU:

"I hear that so often. My goal today is to understand exactly what's going on with you. If I feel I can help, I'll show you what that looks like. If not, I'll point you to someone who can. Does that sound fair?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)

Your goal here is to find the emotional cost of her symptoms. Use open-ended questions.

  • "When you wake up at 3 AM and can't get back to sleep, how does that affect your work the next day?"
  • "You mentioned the weight gain‚Äîwhat have you had to stop doing because of it?"
  • "On a scale of 1-10, how committed are you to fixing this right now?"

3. Handling Common Objections

Objections are not "No." They are requests for more information. A 2022 study on health coaching found that 68% of clients who initially objected to price eventually signed up when the value was clarified.

Objection The "Imposter" Response The "Professional" Response
"It's too expensive." "Oh, okay. I can give you a discount?" "I understand. Aside from the investment, is there anything else holding you back from the transformation we discussed?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "Sure, let me know what he says." "I support that! What do you think he'll be most concerned about‚Äîthe cost, or the time commitment?"
"Can I just do one session?" "Yes, that's fine." "I actually don't offer single sessions because your imbalance took years to create; we can't reverse it in 60 minutes. I'm committed to your results, not just a chat."
Sarah's Pro Tip

When they say "I need to think about it," they usually mean "I'm afraid I'll fail again." Address the fear, not just the finances. Remind them that doing nothing is also a choice with a high cost.

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

Never end a call with "So, do you want to do it?" Use a Prescriptive Close. You are the practitioner; you tell them what they need.

YOU:

"The investment for the full 90-day transformation is $1,800, or three monthly payments of $650. Which of those works better for your budget?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask "What made you reach out now?" at the start of the call?

This identifies the prospect's "Trigger Event." People don't buy when they have a problem; they buy when the problem becomes unbearable. Understanding the "why now" gives you the emotional leverage to help them commit to change.

2. If a client says "I can't afford it," what is the most professional first step?

Isolate the objection. Ask: "I understand. Aside from the finances, is there anything else about the program that you're unsure of?" This ensures you aren't fighting a money battle when the real issue is a lack of trust in the results.

3. What is the "Prescriptive Close"?

It is shifting from a "seeker" (asking for a sale) to a "provider" (recommending a solution). You state exactly what the client needs based on your expertise, which builds authority and reduces the client's decision fatigue.

4. True or False: Charging $1,500 for a 3-month program is "taking advantage" of people in need.

False. High-ticket pricing ensures "skin in the game." Clients who invest more are statistically 4x more likely to follow the protocol and achieve results. It also prevents practitioner burnout, allowing you to serve your clients with 100% of your energy.
Sarah's Pro Tip

Practice your pricing out loud in the mirror. Say: "The investment is fifteen hundred dollars." Keep saying it until your voice doesn't shake. If you aren't confident in your price, they won't be confident in your results.

Practice Lab Takeaways

  • Control the Conversation: The person asking the questions is the expert. Use the 4-phase script to guide the prospect.
  • Sell the Destination: People don't buy "Ayurveda"; they buy sleep, energy, and confidence. Focus your language on outcomes.
  • Isolate Objections: Always check if there are hidden concerns before addressing price.
  • Prescribe, Don't Pitch: Tell the client what they need based on your diagnostic findings.
  • Value Your Time: High-ticket packages ($1,500+) are the most sustainable way to build a $100k+ practice while maintaining high client success rates.
