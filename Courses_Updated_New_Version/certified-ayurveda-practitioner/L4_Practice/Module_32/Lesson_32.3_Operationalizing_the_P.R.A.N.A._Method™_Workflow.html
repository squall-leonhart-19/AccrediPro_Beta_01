The 90-Minute Gold Standard: Prakriti Assessment SOP

The initial 90-minute assessment is the most critical touchpoint in the P.R.A.N.A. Methodâ„¢. To prevent it from spilling over into two hours or feeling rushed, you must follow a strict Standard Operating Procedure (SOP).

A 2023 study on practitioner-patient communication found that practitioners who followed a structured intake protocol reported a 32% increase in client satisfaction and a 25% reduction in "administrative fatigue" (Health Systems Journal, 2023).

Phase Time Allotment Key Deliverable
Connection & Opening 0-10 mins Rapport building and setting the agenda.
Prakriti Profiling (P) 10-30 mins Physical, mental, and behavioral constitution analysis.
Root Imbalance History (R) 30-55 mins Deep dive into current symptoms and pathogenesis (Samprapti).
Agni & Nutrition Scan (A/N) 55-75 mins Evaluating digestive fire and current dietary habits.
Initial Protocol & Closing 75-90 mins High-level recommendations and scheduling follow-up.
Coach Tip

The "Buffer 15": Always schedule a 15-minute buffer after your 90-minute assessments. Use this time to finalize your notes while the information is fresh. Never jump immediately into another call; your brain needs the "clinical reset" to maintain the high-level energy required for the P.R.A.N.A. framework.

Streamlining 'Root Imbalance' Analysis

In the Root Imbalance (R) phase, your goal is to identify the Vikriti. Doing this entirely during the session is inefficient. Modern practitioners use digital intake forms to gather 70% of the data before the client arrives.

Digital Intake Strategy:

  • Smart Forms: Use tools like Practice Better or Typeform to create logic-based forms. If a client checks "Bloating," the form should automatically expand to ask about timing, frequency, and association with specific foods.
  • Symptom Burden Assessment: Ask clients to rate 20 key Ayurvedic indicators (e.g., tongue coating, bowel frequency, sleep quality) on a scale of 1-10.
  • Visual Data: Request photos of the tongue and fingernails to be uploaded 24 hours prior to the session.

Case Study: Sarah, 48 (Former Registered Nurse)

Challenge: Sarah transitioned from nursing to Ayurveda but found herself spending 3 hours on "post-session notes" for every 90-minute client, leading to a 60-hour work week with only 10 clients.

Intervention: We implemented an automated "Root Imbalance" digital intake. Clients now provide 80% of their history via a HIPAA-compliant portal 48 hours before the call. Sarah reviews this for 15 minutes pre-session.

Outcome: Sarah reduced her post-session admin time to 20 minutes. She now sees 15 clients per week in just 25 total working hours, generating $9,000/month while having her afternoons free for her family.

Automating Follow-up Sequences (Agni & Nutrition)

The Agni Optimization (A) and Nature-Synced Nutrition (N) phases require consistent reinforcement. You shouldn't be manually emailing "How is your CCF tea going?" every Tuesday.

The Automation Stack:

  1. The 48-Hour Recap: An automated email triggered 48 hours after the assessment, containing their primary Prakriti profile and a PDF of "First Steps for Agni."
  2. The Day 7 Check-in: A simple automated text or email: "Hi [Name], you're one week into your Agni priming. How is your digestion feeling today?"
  3. Educational Drip: A 4-week sequence that sends one "Nature-Synced Nutrition" tip per week (e.g., Week 1: The 6 Tastes; Week 2: Seasonal Eating).

Coach Tip

Automation doesn't mean "impersonal." Write your automated emails in your natural voice. Use phrases like "I was thinking about our session today..." Even though it's automated, the content is what provides the value and maintains the therapeutic bond.

The Practitioner's Time Map: Clinical vs. Admin

To avoid the "Imposter Syndrome" trap of feeling like you aren't doing enough, you must categorize your time. A successful Ayurveda practitioner uses a 60/20/20 Time Split:

  • 60% Clinical Hours: Face-to-face (or Zoom) assessments and follow-ups. These are your high-value, revenue-generating hours.
  • 20% Administrative/Marketing: Managing the "machine"â€”social media, billing, and responding to inquiries.
  • 20% Preparation & Study: Herbal formulation, reviewing complex cases, and continuing education.

Example Weekly Schedule for a Part-Time Practitioner (20 hours):

  • Tuesday/Thursday: Clinical Days (6 hours each = 12 hours).
  • Wednesday: Admin & Marketing (4 hours).
  • Friday: Case Review & Herbal Prep (4 hours).

Mapping the Client Journey: From Lead to Vitality

Operationalizing your workflow means knowing exactly where a client is at all times. This is the P.R.A.N.A. Journey Mapâ„¢.

1. The Awareness Phase: Potential client sees your "What's Your Dosha?" quiz or social post.
2. The Assessment Phase (P & R): The 90-minute deep dive where the blueprint and imbalance are identified.
3. The Optimization Phase (A & N): 3-6 months of active coaching to reset Agni and align nutrition.
4. The Vitality Phase (A): The "Awakened Vitality" stage where the client moves to a maintenance membership (e.g., quarterly seasonal resets).

Coach Tip

Don't let clients "finish" after 3 months. Ayurveda is a lifestyle. Operationalize a "Maintenance Membership" at a lower price point ($97-$147/month) that includes one 30-minute seasonal check-in and access to a community group. This creates recurring revenue and ensures long-term client success.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the recommended time allotment for 'Prakriti Profiling' within a 90-minute assessment?

20 minutes (from the 10-minute to 30-minute mark). This ensures you have enough time for the complex Root Imbalance (R) analysis while still establishing the client's foundational blueprint.

2. Why is a digital intake form considered an "operational multiplier"?

It allows the practitioner to gather 70-80% of the client's history before the session, reducing administrative burden, shortening note-taking time, and allowing the live session to focus on high-level clinical synthesis rather than data entry.

3. What does the "60/20/20" time management rule represent?

60% Clinical (Revenue-generating), 20% Admin/Marketing (Practice-building), and 20% Prep/Study (Quality-maintaining). This balance prevents burnout and ensures the business stays healthy.

4. How does a "Vitality Maintenance" phase benefit the business operations?

It creates recurring revenue, reduces the constant need for new lead generation, and provides the client with long-term support for their Ayurvedic lifestyle, improving overall outcomes.

  • Protect Your Time: Use the 60/20/20 rule to ensure your business is sustainable and your clinical skills remain sharp.
