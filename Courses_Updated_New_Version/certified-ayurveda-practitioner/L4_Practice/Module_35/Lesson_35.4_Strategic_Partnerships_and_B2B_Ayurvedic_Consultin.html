Structuring Referral Agreements

Transparency is the foundation of professional growth. When you recommend herbal supplements or refer to a specialist, you should be compensated for your expertise and the "warm lead" you provide.

Common Models:

  • Affiliate (Digital): Standard 10-20% commission via tracking links (e.g., Banyan Botanicals or organic herbal suppliers).
  • Cross-Referral (Service): You send a client to a Chiropractor; they send one to you. No money changes hands, but "social capital" is built.
  • Revenue Sharing (Clinical): A percentage of the total package price (usually 15-25%) paid to the referring party.
Coach Tip: The Transparency Rule

Always disclose your affiliate relationships to your clients. Say: "I receive a small commission from this company. I only partner with them because their heavy-metal testing standards meet my P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢ criteria."

Legal & Ethical Guidelines

Collaborations require clear boundaries to protect your professional reputation and your clients.

  1. Business Associate Agreements (BAA): If you are sharing client data with a clinic, ensure you have a BAA in place for HIPAA compliance (in the US).
  2. Scope of Practice: Ensure your partners respect your role as a practitioner and do not ask you to perform medical tasks (unless you hold the appropriate license).
  3. Written Contracts: Never rely on a "handshake deal" for B2B work. Use a Professional Services Agreement that outlines deliverables, payment terms, and termination clauses.
