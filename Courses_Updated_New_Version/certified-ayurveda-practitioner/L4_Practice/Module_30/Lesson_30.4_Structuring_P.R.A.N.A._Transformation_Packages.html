The Session Trap vs. The Transformation Container

In the early stages of practice, it is common to offer single consultations. However, Ayurvedic healing is a process of tissue regeneration (Dhatu Sarata), which takes time. A single session often leaves the client with a long list of changes but no support system to implement them, leading to a 70% drop-off rate after the first follow-up.

Always schedule a "Celebration Call" at the 45-day mark. Clients often forget how bad they felt on Day 1. Reviewing their initial intake forms at this point reinforces the value of the package and secures their commitment to the final 45 days.

Logistics of Herbal Support: To Bundle or Not?

One of the biggest hurdles for practitioners is whether to include herbs in the package price. There are three primary models:

  • The All-Inclusive Model: You include foundational herbs (e.g., Triphala, CCF Tea, and one custom formula) in the total price. Pros: High compliance. Cons: Lower margins if you don't calculate correctly.
  • The Affiliate Model: You provide a link to a third-party supplier (like Banyan Botanicals) and the client pays them directly. Pros: Zero inventory. Cons: Client may procrastinate on ordering.
  • The Hybrid Model: You provide the "Starter Kit" (Day 1-14) and they purchase refills.
Coach Tip: Legal Clarity

In the United States, ensure your herbs are labeled as "Dietary Supplements." Never claim they "cure" or "treat" a specific disease. Use Ayurvedic terminology: "Supports healthy Agni" or "Balances Vata in the digestive tract."

Continuity: From Root to Vitality

What happens after the 90 days? A professional practice has an Ascension Path. You should never "discharge" a client; you transition them to a different level of support.

  1. The Reset (90 Days): Intensive support, bi-weekly calls.
  2. The Maintenance (6 Months): Monthly "Tune-up" calls, seasonal cleanses.
  3. The Community (Annual): Access to a private group, quarterly workshops.
Coach Tip: The Re-Enrollment Conversation

Start the conversation about "What's Next" at Day 75. Say: "We've done incredible work clearing the Ama. Now, I'd love to move into the 'Awakened Vitality' phase where we focus on longevity and anti-aging. Would you like to hear how that works?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a 90-day container clinically superior to a single session in Ayurveda?

It aligns with the Dhatu Sarata (tissue development) cycles, specifically the 30-day cycle of Rasa Dhatu. It allows for three full cycles of renewal and provides the support needed to navigate the "healing crisis" that often occurs early in treatment.

2. What is the primary focus of Phase 2 in the 90-Day Agni Reset?

Phase 2 focuses on Agni Optimization (A) and Nature-Synced Nutrition (N). Once the foundation is set, this phase ignites the digestive fire and implements specific nutritional protocols to nourish the tissues.

3. What is the difference between hourly and value-based pricing?

Hourly pricing charges for the practitioner's time (e.g., $100/hr). Value-based pricing charges for the outcome or transformation provided to the client (e.g., $2,000 to resolve chronic IBS), regardless of the exact minutes spent.

4. When should the "What's Next" conversation begin with a package client?

The conversation should begin around Day 75 of a 90-day program. This ensures a seamless transition into a maintenance or longevity program before the momentum of the initial transformation is lost.

Key Takeaways

  • Package over Price: Selling 90-day transformations ensures better client results and more stable practitioner income.

  • Seamless Ascension: Always have a "Maintenance" or "Vitality" offer ready for clients completing their initial transformation.
