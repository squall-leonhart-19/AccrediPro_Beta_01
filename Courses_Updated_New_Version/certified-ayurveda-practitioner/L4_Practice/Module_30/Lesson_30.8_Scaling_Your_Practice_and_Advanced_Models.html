The 30-Minute Discovery Script

A Discovery Call should be a structured container. If you let it wander, you lose authority. Follow this 4-phase P.R.A.N.A. approach.

Phase 1: The Connection (0-5 Minutes)

YOU:

"Hi Diane! I've been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive into the clinical side, tell me‚Äîwhat was the 'final straw' that made you book this call today?"

Why this works: It skips the small talk and goes straight to the emotional driver.

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)

YOU:

"I hear you. You're exhausted but your mind won't shut off at 3 AM. If we don't address this now, where do you see your health in six months? And conversely, if you woke up tomorrow with 100% energy, what‚Äôs the first thing you‚Äôd do?"

Why this works: It establishes the "Cost of Inaction" and the "Value of the Result."

Handling Common Objections

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or reassurance. A 2023 study on health coaching sales found that practitioners who addressed concerns directly had a 42% higher conversion rate than those who avoided them.

The Objection The Fear Behind It Your Professional Response
"It's too expensive." "I've wasted money before and I'm scared to fail again." "I understand. When we look at the cost of your current medications and lost productivity, can you afford not to fix this?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I don't feel empowered to spend money on myself." "Absolutely. Would it be helpful if I sent you a summary of our plan so you can show him exactly what we're doing?"
"Is this scientific?" "I need legitimacy to feel safe." "Ayurveda is a 5,000-year-old science, and our P.R.A.N.A. method integrates modern circadian biology. We track your biomarkers throughout."
Sarah's Tip

Never lower your price on the call. If they truly have a budget constraint, offer a longer payment plan, not a discount. Discounts devalue the sacred exchange of healing.

Confident Pricing Presentation

One of the biggest mistakes new practitioners make is "pricing by the hour." This creates a "sickness treadmill" where you only get paid if the client stays unwell. Instead, sell The Result.

Package Pricing vs. Hourly Rates

Imagine Diane. If you charge $150/hour, she might come twice and quit when she feels "okay." If you sell a $2,000 12-week package, she is invested in the full transformation of her Agni and Dhatus.

Sarah's Tip

When you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak after the price is mentioned is usually the one who feels uncomfortable. Silence is a professional tool.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 1 (The Connection) in a Discovery Call?

The goal is to identify the emotional driver or "final straw" that motivated the client to seek help, establishing immediate empathy and rapport.

2. Why is "Package Pricing" superior to "Hourly Billing" in an Ayurvedic practice?

Package pricing focuses on the "Result" and ensures client commitment to the full 12-week biological transformation, rather than just temporary symptom relief.

3. How should you respond to the "I need to talk to my husband" objection?

Acknowledge the importance of the decision and offer a professional summary or "case for support" that the client can use to communicate the value of the investment to their partner.

4. What is the "Cost of Inaction" technique?

It involves asking the client where their health will be in 6-12 months if they DON'T make a change, helping them realize that the cost of staying sick is higher than the cost of the program.

Key Takeaways

  • A Discovery Call is a professional clinical container, not an informal chat.
  • Focus on the "Why" (Emotion) before the "How" (Doshas/Herbs).
  • Objections are requests for reassurance; address them with authority and data.
  • Price for results, not for hours, to ensure client compliance and financial stability.
  • Your teaching or nursing background is an asset, not a liability‚Äîuse it to build legitimacy.
