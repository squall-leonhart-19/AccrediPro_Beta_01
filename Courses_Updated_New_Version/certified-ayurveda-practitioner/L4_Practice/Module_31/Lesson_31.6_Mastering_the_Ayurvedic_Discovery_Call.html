The Psychology of the Ayurvedic Discovery Call

A discovery call is a psychological bridge. On one side, the prospect is in a state of Dukha (suffering) and confusion. On the other side is the promise of Svastha (established in self). Your role is to be the guide across that bridge.

Statistics show that in the wellness industry, 72% of high-ticket sales (packages over $1,500) occur only after a live interaction. For a woman in her 40s or 50s looking for a holistic solution, she isn't just buying "Ayurveda"‚Äîshe is buying you and the certainty you provide.

Coach Tip: The 80/20 Rule

In a discovery call, the client should speak 80% of the time. Your job is to ask the right questions that lead them to realize their current path is no longer working. Silence is your most powerful closing tool.

This framework is designed to move a prospect from "I have a problem" to "You have my solution" in exactly 20 minutes.

Handling Objections with Ayurvedic Empathy

When a prospect says "it's too expensive," they are rarely talking about money. They are talking about perceived value or fear of failure. Use these scripts to navigate the three big hurdles:

1. The "Time" Objection

"I'm too busy for a complex Ayurvedic routine."

The Pivot: "I hear you. The P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢ isn't about adding 2 hours to your day; it's about reclaiming the 3 hours you currently lose to fatigue and brain fog. We don't do 'complex'; we do 'effective.' Are you willing to trade 15 minutes of scrolling for 15 minutes of vitality?"

2. The "Cost" Objection

"I didn't realize it would be this much."

The Pivot: "I understand. This is an investment. But let's look at the cost of not doing this. How much are you currently spending on supplements that don't work, co-pays, and lost productivity? My goal is to make this the last money you ever spend on this specific problem."

Coach Tip: The Anchor

Always state your price clearly and then stop talking. The first person to speak after the price is revealed is usually the one who feels the most pressure. Let the value of your work hang in the air.

The 'Transition to Treatment' Close

The most awkward part of the call for new practitioners is the transition from "chatting" to "charging." You must avoid the "friend zone."

The Seamless Close Script:
"We‚Äôve identified that your Agni is low and it‚Äôs driving this exhaustion. I am 100% confident I can help you resolve this using my 3-month P.R.A.N.A. protocol. The investment for that is $2,800, or three payments of $995. Shall we get your Initial Assessment on the calendar now?"

If they say yes, take the payment or the deposit on the call. Sending a link "later" results in a 30% drop-off rate. Be the professional who handles the business so they can focus on the healing.

Post-Call Follow-Up: The 40% Growth Engine

A 2023 study on wellness consulting found that 40% of clients who initially said "I need to think about it" eventually signed up‚Äîbut only if they received at least 3 follow-ups within 7 days.

The 48-Hour Sequence:

  • 24 Hours Later: A case study or testimonial of a woman similar to them who achieved the result they want.
  • 48 Hours Later: The "Gentle Nudge." "I'm finalizing my roster for next month and wanted to see if you wanted that 2:00 PM slot for our kickoff."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the 'Vikriti Deep-Dive' phase of the call?

The goal is to help the client understand the depth of their current imbalance and the consequences of remaining in that state (the "cost of inaction").

2. What is the "80/20 Rule" in discovery calls?

The client should speak 80% of the time, while the practitioner speaks 20% of the time, primarily asking guiding questions.

3. How should a practitioner handle the "Time" objection?

By reframing Ayurveda not as an "addition" to a busy schedule, but as a way to reclaim time currently lost to low energy and poor health.

4. Why is taking a deposit on the call critical?

Because sending a link "later" results in a 30% drop-off rate due to lost momentum and "buyer's remorse" or distractions.
