The Psychology of the Discovery Call

Most practitioners fail because they talk too much. In a successful discovery call, the prospect should be talking 70% of the time. According to a 2023 industry analysis of 1,200 wellness practices, calls where the practitioner listened for at least 18 minutes had a 52% higher close rate than those where the practitioner dominated the conversation.

Coach Tip

Your goal isn't to prove how smart you are. It's to make the client feel heard and understood. When they feel understood, they automatically trust that you have the solution.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

Use this structure to guide Susan from "skeptical" to "signed up."

Phase 1: Rapport & Permission (0-5 Mins)

"Hi Susan! I've been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive in, my goal today is to understand what's going on with your health and see if my P.R.A.N.A. Methodâ„¢ is the right fit to get you back on track. If it is, I'll show you how we can work together. If not, I'll point you toward someone who can help. Does that sound fair?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-20 Mins)

Ask these specific questions to uncover the "Gap":

  • The Current State: "You mentioned the brain fog and weight gain. On a scale of 1-10, how is this affecting your ability to enjoy your new freedom after leaving your HR job?"
  • The Failed Attempts: "You've tried Keto and fasting. Why do you think those didn't provide the long-term shift you're looking for?"
  • The Cost of Inaction: "If we're sitting here a year from now and nothing has changed, what does your life look like?"
Coach Tip

Wait for the "sigh." Usually, after 15 minutes of deep questioning, the client will sigh or say, "Nobody has ever asked me that." That is the moment they have opened up to change.

Handling Objections with Grace

An objection is not a "No." It is a request for more information or a manifestation of fear. We use the A.C.E. Formula: Acknowledge, Clarify, Explore.

Coach Tip

If they say "It's too expensive," they are actually saying "I don't yet see how the value of the result outweighs the cost of the program." Re-link the price to their specific pain point (e.g., "The cost of another year of insomnia").

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the recommended ratio of listening to talking during a discovery call?

You should listen 70% of the time and talk 30% of the time. This ensures the client feels heard and allows you to gather the necessary data to bridge "The Gap."

2. What is the "Cost of Inaction" question and why is it important?

It asks: "If nothing changes in a year, where will you be?" It is vital because it helps the client realize that the "cost" of staying sick is higher than the "investment" in your program.

3. How should you respond to "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge their need for time, then clarify: "I completely understand. Usually, when people need to think, it's about the money, the time, or the fear it won't work. Which one is it for you?" This opens an honest dialogue.

4. Why is package pricing superior to hourly billing for an Ayurveda practitioner?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome (healing) rather than the time. It ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see Ayurvedic results (usually 3-6 months) and provides predictable income for the practitioner.

Key Takeaways

  • The Diagnostic Mindset: Approach every call as a helper, not a salesperson. If you can help, it's your duty to invite them to work with you.
  • The 70/30 Rule: Let the prospect lead the narrative to build deep psychological trust.
  • Bridge the Gap: Clearly define the distance between their current pain and their future health goals.
  • Own Your Value: State your package prices clearly and without apology. You are selling a transformation, not just "hours."
  • Consistency is Key: Success in client acquisition is a numbers game. Aim for 2-3 discovery calls per week to maintain a thriving practice.
