Prospect: Elena, 52

Presenting Symptoms: Menopausal weight gain (+20 lbs), "brain fog" that makes her feel incompetent at her new job, and chronic 3 PM exhaustion.

Don't focus on the weight loss. Focus on the identity. Elena doesn't just want to lose 20 pounds; she wants to feel competent and visible again. Listen for those "identity words" during your call.

2. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A successful call isn't a random conversation; it's a diagnostic journey. Use this 4-phase structure to lead Elena to her own conclusion that she needs your help.

Phase 1: Rapport & Permission 0:00 - 0:05
YOU: "Hi Elena! It's so good to finally connect. I‚Äôve been looking forward to this. Before we dive in, my goal today is to see if I‚Äôm the right person to help you get your energy back. If I am, I'll show you what that looks like. If not, I‚Äôll point you to someone who can. Does that sound fair?"

Why this works: It removes the "sales pressure" immediately by establishing you as an expert who only takes on the right fit.

Phase 2: The Deep Discovery (The Gap) 0:05 - 0:15
YOU: "You mentioned in your intake form that you're struggling with brain fog. Tell me, how is that actually showing up in your day-to-day work?"
YOU: "And if we don't get this handled now, where do you see your health in 12 months?"

The Goal: You are looking for the Current State vs. the Desired State. The distance between them is the "Gap" your program fills.

Sarah's Tip

If they say "I just want to be healthy," dig deeper. Ask: "What does 'healthy' allow you to do that you can't do today?" This is where the emotional sale happens.

Phase 3: The Prescription (Your Offer) 0:15 - 0:25
YOU: "Elena, based on everything you've shared, I am 100% confident I can help you. The reason Keto didn't work is that it was aggravating your Vata-Pitta imbalance. In my 90-day 'Awakened Vitality' program, we aren't just dieting; we are resetting your metabolic fire (Agni)."

3. Handling Objections with Authority

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a sign of the client's internal fear of failure. A 2022 study on health coaching outcomes showed that clients who voiced concerns and had them addressed by the practitioner were 40% more likely to complete the 12-week program than those who had no initial objections.

The Objection The Practitioner's Response (The "Sarah" Way)
"It's too expensive." "I understand. Is it that the total is out of reach, or are you just unsure if this will finally be the thing that works for you?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I support that. When you talk to him, what do you think he'll be most concerned about‚Äîthe cost or the time you'll be spending on yourself?"
"I'm too busy right now." "I hear you. But let me ask: Is there ever a 'perfect' time to stop feeling exhausted? If we wait, will the fog get better or worse?"

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

When you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak after the price is revealed often loses the "authority" in the room. Practice these lines out loud:

  • The Package: "The investment for the 90-day Awakened Vitality transformation is $2,500."
  • The Value: "This includes your deep-dive Prakriti analysis, 12 weekly coaching sessions, your custom herbal protocol, and daily Voxer support so you're never alone."
  • The Close: "Would you like to take care of that with a one-time payment, or would the monthly installment plan work better for you?"
Sarah's Tip

Never say "It's just $2,500." The word "just" minimizes the investment and makes you sound defensive. State the number as a fact, like the weather.

Let's look at the math. For a woman pivoting from a $60,000/year teaching or nursing job, the "freedom number" is usually around $5,000 - $8,000 per month in gross revenue.

Scenario: The "Awakened Vitality" Program ($2,500 per client)

  • 4 Clients/Month: $10,000/mo ($120k/year) - The "Full-Time" Expert

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Permission" step at the start of the discovery call?

It establishes you as the authority and removes sales pressure by stating that you are both evaluating if the partnership is a "right fit," rather than you simply trying to "get" a client.

2. If a client says "I need to think about it," what is the most effective follow-up question?

"Of course. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either the money, the time, or they aren't sure I'm the right person. Which one of those is it for you?" (This forces the real objection to the surface).

3. Why is "The Gap" essential to the sales process?

The Gap defines the cost of inaction. By contrasting where they are (fatigued, brain fog) with where they want to be (vibrant, confident), the price of the program becomes an investment in a solution rather than an expense.

4. True or False: You should explain all the clinical details of Ayurveda (Doshas, Sub-doshas, Dhatus) during the discovery call presentation.

False. The discovery call is about the client's OUTCOMES. Over-explaining clinical theory can lead to "information overwhelm," causing the prospect to say they need to "think about it" because their brain is tired.

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • The sale is service: If you believe your Ayurveda practice can change a life, it is your duty to lead the client to a "yes."
  • Silence is power: State your price ($2,500+) and wait for the client to respond. Do not lower your price just because there is a pause.
  • Focus on the "Why Now": Use Elena's daughter's wedding as the anchor for why she cannot wait another six months.
  • Model for $10k: Aiming for 4 new clients a month at $2,500 creates a $120,000/year business with plenty of time for your own self-care.
