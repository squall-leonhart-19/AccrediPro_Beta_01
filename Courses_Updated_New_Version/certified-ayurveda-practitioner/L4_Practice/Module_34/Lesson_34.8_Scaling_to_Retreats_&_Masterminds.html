Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological driver for a client choosing a group program over 1-on-1?

The primary driver is community and accountability. Clients often feel isolated in their health journey and find strength in knowing others are facing similar challenges.

2. How should you respond if a prospect says the group price is still too high?

Shift the focus from "cost" to "investment" and "consequence." Ask: "What is the cost of NOT fixing your sleep/energy over the next year?" and highlight the value of the 8 weeks of support.

3. What is the "Vegas Rule" in group coaching?

It is a confidentiality agreement: "What happens in the group stays in the group." This builds the safety and trust necessary for deep Ayurvedic healing.

4. Why is the "Founding Member" strategy effective for new practitioners?

It creates urgency and incentive for people to join while acknowledging it's a new program, which lowers the practitioner's stress regarding "perfection."

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs allow you to impact more lives while protecting your own energy (preventing burnout).
  • The Discovery Call for groups focuses on "fit" and "community contribution" rather than just clinical history.
  • Use "Founding Member" pricing to launch quickly and gather testimonials.
  • Always have a clear transition (CTA) at the end of your call to move them to payment.
  • Hybrid models (Group + VIP 1-on-1) are the fastest path to a $10k/month practice.
