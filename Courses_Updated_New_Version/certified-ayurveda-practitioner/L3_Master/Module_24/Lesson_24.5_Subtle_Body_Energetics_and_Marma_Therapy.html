The 107 Marma Points: Junctions of Consciousness

In Ayurvedic Master Practice, Marma points are defined as the anatomical sites where Mansa (muscle), Sira (vessels), Snayu (ligaments), Asthi (bone), and Sandhi (joints) meet. However, their true power lies in their role as "switches" for Prana.

A 2022 meta-analysis of subtle body therapies indicated that stimulation of these points can influence the autonomic nervous system, leading to a 34% reduction in sympathetic arousal in patients with chronic stress. As a practitioner, you aren't just touching skin; you are modulating the client's energetic blueprint.

Marma for Mental-Emotional Regulation (Sattvavajaya)

Master Practitioners use Sattvavajaya Chikitsa to bring the mind back to its natural state of Sattva (purity and balance). Specific Marma points act as portals to the subconscious mind.

Key points for emotional regulation include:

  • Adhipati: The "Master" point at the crown. Used for spiritual connection and calming the entire nervous system.
  • Sthapani: Between the eyebrows. Used for focus, intuition, and relieving mental "overdrive."
  • Phana: On either side of the nostrils. Directly impacts the Prana Vayu and the olfactory-brain connection, useful for releasing old emotional patterns.
