Section 3: The Socratic Teaching Method

A Master Practitioner doesn't just give the answer. You use the Socratic Method‚Äîasking targeted questions that lead the mentee to the realization themselves. This builds their clinical muscle.

Avoid This (Direct Answer) Try This (Socratic Question)
"You gave too much Ashwagandha for a Pitta imbalance." "Looking at Susan's 'wired and itchy' response, which Dosha might be reacting to the heating quality of the herbs?"
"She has Ama, you shouldn't have given tonics yet." "If the digestion shut down after starting tonics, what does that tell us about her current digestive fire (Agni)?"
"Stop the Ashwagandha immediately." "Based on this new feedback, how would you pivot the protocol to address the immediate discomfort?"
Sarah's Insight

In my experience, 90% of "failed" cases in the first year are simply a result of giving Brimhana (nourishing) therapy before Langhana (lightening/clearing) therapy. Remind Elena that Ayurveda is a dance of clearing before building.

Section 5: Ethics & Scope in Supervision

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the Socratic Method in mentoring?

To lead the mentee to discover the clinical solution themselves, thereby building their own clinical reasoning and confidence rather than just providing a "quick fix."

2. If a mentee's client feels "wired and itchy" after taking a heating tonic like Ashwagandha, what should the supervisor suggest investigating?

Investigate "hidden heat" or Pitta aggravation. The heating quality of the herb may be too intense for the client's current state, even if they have a Vata imbalance.

3. True or False: A supervisor is responsible for ensuring the mentee stays within their Scope of Practice.

True. As a mentor, you act as a secondary gatekeeper to ensure client safety and professional ethics are maintained.

4. What is the "Sandwich Method" of feedback?

Validation (Positive) ‚Üí Correction (Constructive) ‚Üí Empowerment (Positive). This ensures the mentee feels supported while learning from their mistakes.
