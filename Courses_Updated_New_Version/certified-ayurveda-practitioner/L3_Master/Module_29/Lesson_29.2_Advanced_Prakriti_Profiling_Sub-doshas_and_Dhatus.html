The 15 Sub-Dosha Dynamics

While the three doshas provide a broad overview, the 15 sub-doshas represent the directional and functional intelligence of the body. In advanced profiling, we don't just say "Vata is high"; we specify if it is Apana Vata (downward flow) or Prana Vata (sensory input).

Dosha Sub-Dosha Primary Function Master Profiling Indicator
Vata Vyana Circulation / Heart Cold extremities, heart palpitations, uneven gait.
Pitta Sadhaka Emotional Processing Mental clarity, decisiveness, "heart-fire."
Kapha Tarpaka Nervous System Lubrication Memory retention, emotional stability, CSF health.
Vata Samana Absorption/Assimilation Mid-gut gas, nutrient malabsorption (Dhatu depletion).
Pitta Ranjaka Blood Chemistry/Liver Anemia, bile production, skin pigmentation.

Dhatu Sarata: Assessing Tissue Excellence

In the P.R.A.N.A. Methodâ„¢, we use Dhatu Sarata to determine a client's "reserve." Sarata is the excellence of a tissue. A person may have a Vata Prakriti but possess Mamsa Sarata (excellent muscle tissue), which gives them more stamina than a typical Vata.

The 7 Tissues of Vitality

  1. Rasa (Plasma): Indicated by skin health and emotional satisfaction.
  2. Rakta (Blood): Indicated by the luster of the eyes and warmth of the body.
  3. Mamsa (Muscle): Indicated by the strength of the joints and physical "groundedness."
  4. Meda (Fat): Indicated by the health of hair, nails, and voice.
  5. Asthi (Bone): Indicated by the strength of teeth and large joints.
  6. Majja (Marrow/Nerve): Indicated by deep-seated intelligence and compassion.
  7. Shukra (Reproductive): Indicated by overall charisma (Tejas) and creative drive.

Dhatu Ksaya (Deficiency) vs. Vriddhi (Excess)

Understanding the state of the tissues allows you to predict the Samprapti (pathogenesis). In your 40+ female demographic, Dhatu Ksaya (depletion) is the most common clinical finding due to stress and hormonal shifts.

Tissue Ksaya (Deficiency) Symptoms Vriddhi (Excess) Symptoms
Rasa Dry skin, sound sensitivity, fatigue. Congestion, salivation, loss of appetite.
Asthi Joint pain, hair loss, brittle nails. Extra teeth, bony outgrowths, calcification.
Majja Hollowness in bones, dizziness, anxiety. Heaviness in eyes/joints, slow mentation.

Srotas Integrity: The Living Channels

No profiling is complete without assessing the Srotas. If the Prakriti is the "blueprint" and the Dhatus are the "building materials," the Srotas are the "delivery trucks." Even with perfect nutrition (Ahara), if the Pranavaha Srotas (respiratory/vitality channel) is blocked, the client will feel stagnant.

Signs of Srotas Dysfunction:

  • Atipravrutti: Excessive flow (e.g., diarrhea, excessive sweating).
  • Sanga: Stagnation/Blockage (e.g., constipation, tumors, blood clots).
  • Siragranthi: Dilatation/Growths (e.g., varicose veins, cysts).
  • Vimarga Gamana: Flow in the wrong direction (e.g., acid reflux).

Advanced Pulse and Tongue Verification

To verify sub-doshic involvement, we use the Seven Levels of the Pulse. While Level 1 shows the current Vikriti, Level 7 reveals the Prakriti and the subtle state of the Dhatus.

Tongue Indicators for Dhatus:

  • Rasa: A thick white coating (Ama) indicates Rasa-Vaha Srotas congestion.
  • Rakta: A very red tongue tip or edges indicates Pitta in the Rakta Dhatu.
  • Mamsa: Deep cracks in the center indicate depletion of muscle and digestive tissue.
  • Asthi: Teeth marks (scalloping) on the edges indicate malabsorption affecting the bones.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which sub-dosha is primarily responsible for the circulation of blood and nutrients throughout the entire body?

Vyana Vata. It acts as the "distributor" from the heart to the periphery. Imbalances often manifest as cold hands/feet or irregular heart rhythms.

2. If a client presents with brittle nails, thinning hair, and joint cracking, which Dhatu is likely in a state of Ksaya (deficiency)?

Asthi Dhatu (Bone tissue). In Ayurveda, hair and nails are considered the "Mala" (byproducts) of bone tissue metabolism.

3. What does "Sanga" in a Srotas indicate?

Stagnation or Blockage. This is a common precursor to "Ama" (toxin) accumulation and structural disease.

4. Why is Dhatu Sarata assessment critical for "Master" level profiling?

Because it identifies a client's inherent resilience. It tells you which systems are naturally strong (even if currently imbalanced) and which systems require lifelong protective support.
