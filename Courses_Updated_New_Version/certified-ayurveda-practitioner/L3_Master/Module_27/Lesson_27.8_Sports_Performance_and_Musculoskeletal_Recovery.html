Section 3: Your Teaching Approach

As a supervisor, your goal is not to simply "fix" the client case for Elena. It is to teach her how to think, not what to do. A 2022 study on clinical supervision found that collaborative inquiry (asking questions) leads to 40% higher practitioner retention than directive supervision (giving answers).

Step Action Why it Works
1. Validate Acknowledge the difficulty of the case. Lowers cortisol and opens the "learning brain."
2. Inquire Ask: "What does the 'wired' feeling tell us about her Agni?" Builds clinical reasoning and pattern recognition.
3. Theoretical Pivot Introduce the concept of Ama blocking the channels. Connects the symptom to the root cause (Samprapti).
4. Empower Have her draft the revised plan first. Builds the "muscle" of clinical confidence.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of clinical supervision?

The primary goal is to foster the mentee's clinical reasoning, ensure client safety, and build the practitioner's confidence through collaborative inquiry, rather than just providing the "correct" answer.

2. Why might a Vata-pacifying herb like Ashwagandha make a client feel "wired but tired"?

If the client has significant Ama (toxins) or blocked Srotas (channels), heavy tonics cannot be properly digested (Agni) or circulated, leading to further stagnation and a "wired" nervous system response.

3. According to the lesson, what is a financial benefit of offering supervision?

Supervision allows senior practitioners to create a high-value revenue stream (typically $150-$250/hr) that leverages their expertise without the overhead of direct client care.

4. What feedback model is suggested in the P.R.A.N.A. Method‚Ñ¢?

The Sattva-Rajas-Sattva model: Start with clarity/validation (Sattva), move to constructive action/correction (Rajas), and end with integration/empowerment (Sattva).
