The Pathophysiology of Ama-Visha

In Ayurveda, autoimmunity is not viewed as an "overactive" immune system, but as a confused one. The root of this confusion is Ama‚Äîundigested metabolic waste. However, in autoimmune disorders, simple Ama evolves into Ama-Visha.

Ama-Visha occurs when Ama sits in the Srotas (channels) for an extended period, undergoing a reactive chemical change that turns it into a "bio-toxin." This toxin mimics the structure of healthy tissues, leading the immune system to mistakenly target the tissue where the toxin is lodged. A 2022 study published in the Journal of Clinical Medicine supports this "molecular mimicry" theory, noting that environmental toxins often trigger cross-reactive immune responses.

In autoimmunity, we avoid "immune boosters" (like high-dose Echinacea) which can sometimes exacerbate a flare. Instead, we use Immunomodulators‚Äîherbs that bring balance.

Always check for contraindications. For example, if a client is on immunosuppressant drugs (like Methotrexate), consult with their rheumatologist before introducing high doses of Guduchi to ensure no herb-drug interactions occur.

Check Your Understanding
