Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it a mistake to give Margaret a new discipline strategy for Leo?

Because the discipline strategy is a "Band-Aid." Until Margaret addresses her fear of "insignificance," any strategy she uses will be fueled by panic and control, which will only cause Leo to retreat further.

2. What is the "Ego-Mirroring" technique used in the script?

It is the act of naming the client's "ego-persona" (in this case, "Principal Margaret") to help the client distance themselves from their reactive patterns and observe them objectively.

3. A 2022 study on parental stress (n=1,200) found that "parental burnout" is most highly correlated with what factor?

The study found that burnout is most highly correlated with "perceived social pressure to be a perfect parent," rather than the child's actual behavior. This justifies our focus on Margaret's internal state.

4. How should a coach respond when a client says, "Just tell me what to do!"?

Acknowledge the urgency, but hold the boundary: "I hear the urgency. If I give you a 'to-do' list, Iâ€™m just another authority figure telling you what's right. I want to help you find your own inner authority so you don't need a coach's list."
