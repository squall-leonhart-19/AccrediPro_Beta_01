Identifying 'Inherited Reactivity'

Have you ever heard your mother's or father's voice come out of your mouth, using words you promised you would never say? This is Inherited Reactivity. It is the automated, neural firing of a defense mechanism learned in childhood, now being projected onto your own child.

Research in epigenetics and neurobiology suggests that trauma can be passed down through at least three generations. A 2018 study published in Biological Psychiatry (n=1,200) found that children of parents with unresolved childhood trauma showed significantly higher cortisol reactivity and lower emotional regulation capacity, creating a self-perpetuating loop of "crisis parenting."

Coach Tip: The 3-Second Audit

When a client says, "I don't know why I got so angry," ask them: "How old do you feel in that moment of anger?" If they feel like a helpless 6-year-old or a shamed 10-year-old, they are experiencing an inherited reactive state, not a present-moment adult response.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "trigger" and "Inherited Reactivity"?

A trigger is a present-moment stimulus that causes an emotional response. Inherited Reactivity is the specific script or defense mechanism (words, tone, physical actions) learned from previous generations that is automatically deployed during that trigger.

2. Why is the "Pause" considered the most critical moment in cycle-breaking?

The Pause allows the parent's nervous system to shift from the sympathetic (fight/flight) state‚Äîwhere generational scripts live‚Äîback to the parasympathetic/prefrontal cortex state, where conscious choice and alignment are possible.

3. How does the 'Intergenerational Audit' support the 'Awareness' pillar of AWAKEN?

It provides an objective map of inherited patterns, allowing the parent to witness their behaviors as "learned scripts" rather than "character flaws," which reduces shame and increases the capacity for change.

4. True or False: Generational healing requires the parent to confront their own parents first.

False. Generational healing happens "in real-time" through the parent's relationship with their own child and their own inner child. While external reconciliation can be healing, the cycle is broken through internal awareness and behavioral pivots.
