Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Fixing instead of Aligning: Don't jump into "homework tips" (like using a timer). That‚Äôs a surface fix. Stay with the internal alignment.
  • Joining the Judgment: If Elena says "I'm a bad mom," and you say "Oh, don't say that!", you are dismissing her feeling. Instead, say "I hear that story you're telling yourself."
  • Over-Explaining the Theory: Avoid saying "According to the neurobiology of the brain..." unless she asks. Keep her in her heart and body.
  • Ignoring the Body: Alignment is physical. If you don't ask "What does that feel like in your body?", you're staying in the "intellectual" trap.
Practice Wisdom

Remember, many of our clients (like Elena) are high-achievers. They are used to being "the best." Alignment coaching can feel vulnerable for them because it requires admitting they don't have it all figured out. Your warmth is their safety net.

Markers of Success: How Do You Know the Session Worked?

A successful alignment session doesn't mean Elena will never yell again. It means she has a new awareness. Look for these signs:

  1. Softening: Elena‚Äôs shoulders drop, or her voice slows down. This indicates her nervous system is moving out of "fight or flight."
  2. Ownership without Shame: She says, "I see now that I yell when I feel like a failure," rather than "I yell because I'm a bad person."
  3. The "Aha" Moment: She identifies the physical sensation (the chest tightness) that precedes the reaction.
  4. Actionable Alignment: She decides on a "re-alignment" phrase to use next time, like "I am safe, and he is learning."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "surface fix" and an "alignment intervention" in Elena's case?

A surface fix addresses the behavior (e.g., "Use a homework timer"), while an alignment intervention addresses the internal disconnect between the parent's values (patience) and the underlying belief (e.g., "I am failing if he doesn't finish").

2. Why is it important to ask Elena what she feels in her body?

Reactivity is a nervous system response. By identifying the physical sensation (tightness in the chest), Elena can learn to recognize the "warning signs" before she yells, giving her the space to choose a different action.

3. If a client says "I'm a fraud," what is the most conscious coaching response?

Instead of disagreeing, validate the feeling while separating it from their identity. Example: "I hear that you feel like a fraud right now because your actions didn't match your values. Let's look at what pulled you away from your center."

4. What is the "Alignment Bridge" phrase Elena chose to use?

"I am safe, and he is learning." This phrase shifts the focus from "failure" to "growth" and calms the nervous system.
