Check Your Understanding

1. When Lisa says, "I can't manage my own son," what paradigm is she operating from?

She is operating from the Control Paradigm. This mindset views parenting as a management task where the child's obedience is the primary metric of success.

2. What is the primary goal of the "Conscious Reframe" in this scenario?

The goal is to shift Lisa's perspective from seeing Leo's behavior as "defiance" (a personal attack) to seeing it as "information" (a signal of an unmet need or internal struggle).

3. Why is the "Teacher Trap" dangerous in conscious coaching?

Because it focuses on external behavioral modification (fixing the child) rather than internal parent transformation. Conscious coaching is about the parent's evolution, not the child's compliance.

4. What is a physical sign that Lisa is moving into a more conscious state during your session?

A drop in shoulder tension, a deep sigh, or a softening of the voice. These indicate a shift in the autonomic nervous system from sympathetic (stress) to parasympathetic (connection).

Lab Key Takeaways

  • Listen for "management" language to identify the Control Paradigm.
  • Your role is to offer a "Conscious Reframe" that humanizes the child's struggle.
  • Self-regulation in the coach is the foundation for regulation in the client.
  • Success is measured by the parent's internal shift, not immediate behavioral changes in the child.
  • Avoid the "Fix-it" mentality; instead, hold space for the client's self-discovery.
