Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

A "good" session isn't necessarily one where the client leaves happy. In fact, sometimes the best sessions end with the client feeling a bit "heavy" as they process deep truths.

  • The Shift: The client stops talking about the childâ€™s actions and starts talking about their own feelings.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it a mistake to give Elena a new script for Leo immediately?

Because giving a script addresses the symptom (Leo's behavior) rather than the root cause (Elena's internal trigger and need for control). Until Elena addresses her core wound, no script will feel authentic or effective.

2. What is the "Conscious Pivot"?

It is the coaching move of redirecting the client's attention from the child's external behavior back to the parent's internal emotional response and historical triggers.

3. A client says, "My 5-year-old is a nightmare." How do you respond consciously?

"I hear how much you're struggling right now. It sounds like you're at your breaking point. When you use the word 'nightmare,' what is the most difficult emotion you're feeling in your own body?"

4. What is a physiological sign that a client is moving through resistance?

Deep exhales, softening of the jaw, tears, or a sudden stillness/silence after a period of rapid talking.
