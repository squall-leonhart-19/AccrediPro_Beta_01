The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A discovery call is not a coaching session. It is a leadership session. Your goal is to help the parent decide if they are ready for change. A 2022 study on coaching conversions (n=1,200) found that structured calls resulted in a 42% higher enrollment rate than unstructured "chat" calls.

Phase 1: Connection & Grounding (0-5 Minutes)
YOU:
"Hi Linda! I've been looking forward to our call. I read through your intake form, and I want to start by acknowledging how much you clearly love your kids. It takes a lot of courage to reach out for support."
YOU:
"My goal today is to hear more about what's happening at home and see if my Conscious Parenting framework is the right fit to help you get to that peaceful place you're looking for. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)
YOU:
"You mentioned in your form that mornings are the hardest. Walk me through a typical Tuesday morning. What are you feeling in your body when the yelling starts?"
YOU:
"And if we don't change this pattern, Linda, where do you see your relationship with your son in five years?" (Wait for the answer‚Äîthis is the 'Cost of Inaction').
Phase 3: The Bridge (15-25 Minutes)
YOU:
"What I'm hearing is that you're stuck in a 'reactive' cycle. The books you've read focus on his behavior, but my work focuses on the connection and your internal triggers. When we heal the trigger, the behavior naturally shifts."
YOU:
"I work with parents in a 12-week intensive called 'The Conscious Path.' We meet weekly to dismantle these triggers and build a new toolkit. Based on what you've told me, I am 100% confident I can help you."
Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 Minutes)
YOU:
"The investment for the 12-week program is $2,400. We can do that in one payment or a monthly plan. How does that feel in your gut? Are you ready to start this journey?"

Handling Common Objections

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a sign of fear. In conscious parenting coaching, the fear is usually: "What if I fail at this too?"

The Objection The Professional Response
"I need to talk to my spouse." "I completely respect that. Family alignment is key. Would it be helpful if I sent you a summary of what we discussed so you can share the 'why' behind this work with them?"
"It's just too much money right now." "I hear you. It is a significant investment. Let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of staying where you are for another six months? If we can solve the yelling and the guilt, what is that worth to your family's peace?"
"I've tried other things and they didn't work." "I'm glad you mentioned that. Most programs are 'top-down' (fixing the child). This is 'inside-out' (fixing the dynamic). That is why the results last."
Emma's Professional Tip

Never defend your price. State it, then be silent. The first person to speak after the price is stated usually "loses" the lead. Let them process the number; silence is where the commitment happens.

Pricing Presentation & Confidence

Your pricing is a reflection of the transformation, not your time. If you save a parent from 10 years of a broken relationship with their child, that is worth far more than a "per hour" fee. Professional Conscious Parenting Coaches typically charge between $150 and $300 per hour, usually bundled into 3-month packages.

The "Package" Advantage

Instead of selling single sessions, sell a container of support. A 12-week package ensures the client stays through the "messy middle" where real change happens. A 2023 industry report showed that coaches selling packages have a 65% higher client retention rate than those selling pay-as-you-go sessions.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The goal is to understand the "Cost of Inaction"‚Äîhelping the parent realize what will happen if they DON'T change their current patterns. This creates the emotional necessity for the coaching.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to talk to my husband/partner"?

Validate the need for family alignment, then offer a summary or a "Partner Guide" to help them explain the value of the investment. Avoid being pushy, but remain supportive of the decision-making process.

3. Why is it recommended to sell "Packages" rather than "Hourly Sessions"?

Packages ensure client commitment through the difficult parts of the process, provide predictable income for the coach, and focus the client on the "Transformation" rather than the "Time."

4. What is the "Golden Rule" after stating your price on a call?

Stay silent. Allow the prospect the space to process the investment without you "rescuing" them by lowering the price or over-explaining.

Key Takeaways

  • A discovery call is a 30-minute leadership container designed to help a parent choose change.
  • Your price is based on the emotional and generational transformation you provide, not your hourly time.
  • Objections are usually masked fears; address them with empathy and data-backed confidence.
  • A part-time coaching practice (5-10 clients) can replace a full-time professional salary when using package pricing.
  • Consistency in your script leads to consistency in your conversion rates (aim for 30-50%).
