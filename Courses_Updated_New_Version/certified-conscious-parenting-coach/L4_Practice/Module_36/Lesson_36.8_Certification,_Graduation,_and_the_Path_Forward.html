1. Your Ideal Prospect Profile

In this lab, we are going to work with a high-value prospect. Understanding who you are talking to is 80% of the sale. Meet Linda.

Prospect Profile: The Overwhelmed High-Achiever

Name: Linda, 44
Background: Marketing Executive, mother of two (ages 9 and 12).
The Pain: Linda is successful at work but feels like a "failure" at home. Her 12-year-old is withdrawing, and her 9-year-old has explosive outbursts. She’s read every book but can’t implement the advice because she’s too exhausted.
The Desire: She wants a home that feels like a sanctuary, not a battlefield. She values expertise and is willing to invest if she believes the person "gets" her unique pressure.
The Budget: Has the funds, but is skeptical of "fluff." She needs to see a structured path to results.

Coach Tip: Pivot from Professional to Practitioner

If you’re coming from nursing or teaching, you’re used to being an "expert" in a system. As a coach, you are the guide. Your value isn't just in what you know, but in how you hold space for the client to discover their own alignment. Don't over-explain the science; focus on the transformation.

2. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

A discovery call is not a free coaching session. It is a diagnostic interview to see if you can help and if they are ready to commit.

Phase 1: The Connection (0-5 mins)

"Hi Linda! I’ve been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive in, I’d love to know—what was it about my profile or our mutual friend’s recommendation that made you feel like now was the right time to reach out?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 mins)

"You mentioned things feel like a 'battlefield' at home. Can you give me a specific example of a moment this week where you felt completely out of alignment? How did that impact your relationship with your 12-year-old?"

Phase 3: The Gap (15-20 mins)

"If we don't change this dynamic, Linda—if we're sitting here a year from now and nothing has shifted—what does your relationship with your kids look like then? And conversely, what is the 'Dream Home' version of your family?"

Phase 4: The Invitation (20-30 mins)

"Based on what you’ve shared, you are exactly the kind of mother I work with. We need to move from reactive 'firefighting' to conscious 'alignment.' I’d love to walk you through how my 12-week Conscious Parenting Intensive can get you there. Would you like to hear how that works?"

3. Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are rarely about the money; they are usually about fear of failure. Use the "Feel, Felt, Found" method or "The Empathy Bridge."

The Objection The Conscious Response
"It’s just too expensive right now." "I completely hear you. It is a significant investment. Can I ask—is it the total amount, or are you wondering if the ROI on your family's peace will actually be worth it?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I love that you want him on board. Usually, when clients say this, they’re worried about explaining the value. Would it be helpful if I sent you a 'Partner PDF' that outlines exactly what we’ll be achieving?"
"I don't have time for weekly calls." "I hear the overwhelm, Linda. Actually, the reason you don't have time is because of the current chaos. My goal is to give you 5 hours of 'peace time' back per week. Can we find 45 minutes to save those 5 hours?"
Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

After you state your price, stop talking. The first person to speak usually loses the lead. Let them process. Silence is where the client decides if they are ready to value themselves enough to invest. Don't "discount" away your worth because of a three-second pause.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a Discovery Call?

The primary purpose is a diagnostic interview to determine if the client is a good fit for your methodology and if they are ready to commit to the transformation. It is NOT a free coaching session.

2. What is the "Empathy Bridge" in objection handling?

It is the act of validating the client's fear or concern (Empathy) before showing them how the coaching program provides the solution to that very concern (The Bridge).

3. Why is "Package Pricing" superior to "Hourly Pricing" for conscious parenting?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome (transformation) rather than the time spent. It ensures client commitment for the duration needed to see real results and positions you as a specialist.

4. What should you do immediately after stating your price?

Remain silent. This allows the prospect space to process the investment and shows that you are confident in the value you provide.
Coach Tip: Follow-Up is Where the Fortune Is

48% of coaches never follow up after a discovery call. If they say "not yet," put a reminder in your calendar to check in with a helpful resource (a blog post or podcast) in 2 weeks. It shows you genuinely care about their family, not just their credit card.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Structure Equals Safety: A 30-minute structured call makes the prospect feel safe and shows you are a professional.
  • Value the Transformation: You are selling peace, connection, and a child’s future—not "60 minutes of talking."
  • Objections are Invitations: View every "no" or "maybe" as a request for more information or a need for reassurance.
  • Financial Freedom is Possible: With just 4 new clients a month, you can exceed a typical teacher or nurse's salary while working fewer hours.
