Handling Objections with Empathy

Presenting Your Price with Confidence

When it's time to state your price, stop talking. Silence is your most powerful tool. Practice these lines out loud until they feel like second nature.

The Pricing Script

"The investment for the 12-week Conscious Parenting Intensive is $2,800. This includes our weekly deep-dives, voxer support between sessions, and the lifetime resource library. We can do that in one payment, or we have a 3-month payment plan of $995. Which of those works better for your family's budget?"

Emma's Insight

If you find yourself saying "It's only $2,800" or "I know it's a lot, but...", you are projecting your own money blocks. State the number as a neutral fact, like the price of a gallon of milk.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary objective of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in a discovery call?

The objective is to uncover the client's "pain points" and their "vision" for the future. You want them to articulate the cost of staying the same and the emotional value of the transformation.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge the importance of the decision, then ask a clarifying question: "I completely understand. Usually, when people need to think about it, it's either the money, the time, or they aren't sure I'm the right coach. Which one is it for you?"

3. Why is it important to ask permission before stating your price?

It creates a "micro-commitment" and ensures the prospect is actually ready to hear the logistics. It shifts the dynamic from you "pushing" a price to them "inviting" the information.

4. True or False: You should provide a mini-coaching session during the discovery call to prove your worth.

False. Providing coaching during the call often gives the prospect a "quick fix" feeling, which actually reduces their motivation to sign up for the full program. Focus on diagnosis and solution-matching instead.

Key Takeaways

  • Structure Equals Safety: Using a timed script prevents you from rambling and establishes you as the expert leader of the conversation.
  • Diagnosis Over Sales: Approach every call like a doctor; your job is to find the "root cause" and see if your "medicine" (coaching) can fix it.
  • Silence is Golden: After you state your price, wait for the prospect to speak first. Breaking the silence often leads to unnecessary discounting.
  • High-Value Positioning: Focus on the ROI (Return on Investment) of a peaceful home, which is priceless compared to the monetary cost of the program.
