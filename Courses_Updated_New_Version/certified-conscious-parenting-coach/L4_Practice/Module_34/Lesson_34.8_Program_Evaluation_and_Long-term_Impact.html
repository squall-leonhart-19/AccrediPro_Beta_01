4. Pricing & Income Potential

One of the biggest hurdles for new coaches is stating the price without a "waver" in their voice. Practice these numbers out loud until they feel like a statement of fact, not a question.

Coach Tip

Always state the price, then stop talking. The silence that follows is where the client decides. If you speak first, you are usually trying to "rescue" them from the investment, which undermines your value.

5. The Final Close: Call-to-Action Practice

Closing a group sale is about invitation, not persuasion. Use the following lines to practice your "invitation" voice.

The Invitation: "Diane, based on our talk, I know the 'Conscious Circle' is the right home for you. We start on Tuesday at 7:00 PM. The investment is $597, which covers all 8 weeks, the workbook, and our private community. Would you like me to send over the enrollment link so you can secure one of the 10 spots?"
Coach Tip

Notice the use of "secure one of the 10 spots." This isn't a fake sales tactic; it's a clinical boundary. A group only works if it's intimate. You are protecting the integrity of the container by limiting space.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a group program often clinically superior for a client who feels "isolated" or "ashamed"?

Group coaching provides "social proof" that the client is not alone. Witnessing others' struggles and successes dissolves shame faster than 1-on-1 work, as the client sees their own patterns reflected in a safe community (The "Mirroring Effect").

2. What is the "Group Multiplier" effect in terms of business revenue?

It is the ability to increase your hourly rate by coaching multiple people at once. For example, instead of one client at $150/hr, you coach 10 people at $50/hr each, effectively raising your hourly revenue to $500 while making the service more affordable for the clients.

3. How should you respond when a client says, "I'm worried I'm too private for a group"?

Acknowledge their concern, explain the "Sacred Confidentiality" agreement, and highlight that many private people find the group safer because they can "listen and learn" from others' coaching before they feel ready to share themselves.

4. What is the most important thing to do after stating your price?

Stop talking. Allow the client the space to process the investment. Silence demonstrates your confidence in the value of your work and respects the client's decision-making process.

Key Takeaways

  • Group programs allow you to help more families while protecting your own energy from burnout.
  • The "Isolation Objection" is solved by highlighting the power of community and shared wisdom.
  • Workshops (low ticket) serve as a "bridge" to your higher-priced group intensives or 1-on-1 work.
  • Confidence in pricing comes from practice; state your fees as a neutral fact, not a request for permission.
  • Clinically, groups offer unique benefits (mirroring, social support) that 1-on-1 coaching cannot replicate.
