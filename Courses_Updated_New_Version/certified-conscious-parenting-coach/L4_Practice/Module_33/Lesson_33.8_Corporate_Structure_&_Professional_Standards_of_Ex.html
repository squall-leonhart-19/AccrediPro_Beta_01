Check Your Understanding

1. A prospect asks if you can help their child with "suspected ADHD." What is the compliant response?

You must state that you do not diagnose or treat ADHD. However, you can support the parent in creating a conscious environment that helps a neurodivergent child thrive, provided they are also working with a medical professional for the diagnosis.

2. Why is a signed coaching agreement necessary before the first session?

It establishes the legal scope of practice, protects you from liability, ensures the client understands the "no-refund" or "cancellation" policies, and reinforces the professional nature of the relationship.

3. If a client begins discussing severe, clinical depression during a session, what is your professional obligation?

You must pause and acknowledge that this falls outside the scope of coaching. You should refer them to a licensed mental health professional while clarifying that coaching can continue as a complementary support once they are under clinical care.

4. How does stating your price with confidence impact the client's perception of your "legal" legitimacy?

Confidence in pricing signals that you are a business professional with established systems, certifications, and insurance. It differentiates you from a "hobbyist" and builds immediate trust in your expertise.

Key Takeaways

  • Clarity is Kindness: Setting legal boundaries during the discovery call protects both you and the client.
  • Scope Mastery: Always distinguish coaching (future/action-oriented) from therapy (past/healing-oriented).
  • Contracts Build Trust: Presenting a professional agreement is a sign of a high-level, legitimate practitioner.
  • Value Your Pivot: Your previous career skills (nursing, teaching) combined with this certification justify premium pricing.
