The 30-Minute Discovery Call Flow

A professional discovery call is not a "chat." It is a structured diagnostic process. Use this exact timeline to stay on track.

Phase 1: Connection & Agenda (0-5 Minutes)
YOU:
"Sarah, I‚Äôm so glad we‚Äôre connecting today. I‚Äôve reviewed your intake form, and I want to make sure we make the most of our 30 minutes. My goal is to understand what‚Äôs happening in your home, where you want to be, and see if my Conscious Parenting framework is the right fit to bridge that gap. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)
YOU:
"You mentioned that things feel 'fractured' with your daughter right now. Can you give me a specific example of a moment this week where you felt that disconnect most deeply?"
YOU:
"And Sarah, if we don't change this dynamic now, where do you see your relationship with her in three years when she's seventeen?" (This identifies the cost of inaction.)
Phase 3: The Vision & Solution (15-25 Minutes)
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, what you're experiencing isn't a 'bad kid' or 'bad parenting'‚Äîit's a breakdown in the Kinship stage of our methodology. My 12-week program specifically targets the internal triggers that cause these power struggles. We move from 'Control' to 'Connection'."
Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 Minutes)
YOU:
"I am confident I can help you navigate this. The investment for the 12-week 'Conscious Connection' package is $2,400. We can get started as early as next Tuesday. How does that feel in your gut?"

Handling Objections with Grace

Objections are rarely about the money; they are usually about fear of failure. As a coach, your job is to coach them through the decision.

The Objection The Hidden Fear Your Professional Response
"It's a lot of money." "What if I spend this and nothing changes?" "I hear you. It is an investment. But let's look at the cost of the status quo. What is the 'price' of the daily screaming matches over the next year?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I'm afraid he won't support my growth." "I completely respect that. Would it be helpful if I sent you a 'Partner PDF' that explains the science behind the Conscious Parenting approach we discussed?"
"I don't have the time." "I'm already drowning; I can't add one more thing." "That's exactly why we need this. We aren't adding to your plate; we are changing how you handle the plate so you actually reclaim hours of peace."
Emma's Insight

Never defend your price. State it, then be silent. The first person to speak usually loses the lead. Let them process the value you've just established.

Confident Pricing Strategies

Many new coaches undercharge because they think they are selling "time." You are selling transformation. A 2023 industry analysis showed that coaches who charge by the package rather than the hour have a 62% higher retention rate.

The "Anchor" Presentation

When presenting your price, use the "Top-Down" approach. Mention your most comprehensive package first. This makes your standard package feel like an accessible middle ground.

  • The VIP Intensive ($5,000): 6 months of support + weekend retreat.
  • The Core Transformation ($2,400): 12 weeks of 1:1 coaching (Your sweet spot).
  • The Foundation ($900): 4-week intensive.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of Phase 2 (The Deep Dive) in the discovery call?

The purpose is to identify the "Gap"‚Äîthe distance between where the parent is now (pain) and where they want to be (vision). It also establishes the "cost of inaction," helping the prospect realize why they need to solve the problem now.

2. How should a coach respond when a prospect says, "I can't afford this right now"?

Use the "Feel-Felt-Found" method or pivot to the cost of the problem. Example: "I understand how that feels. Many of my most successful clients felt that way initially. What they found was that the cost of the constant household stress was actually higher than the investment in coaching."

3. Why is "Package Pricing" superior to "Hourly Pricing" for a Conscious Parenting Coach?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome and transformation rather than just time. It ensures the client is committed to the full 12-week process required for lasting neurological and behavioral change in the family dynamic.

4. What is the "Anchor" technique in pricing?

It is the practice of presenting your highest-value (and highest-priced) package first. This sets a psychological "anchor" for the value of your work, making your standard packages appear more reasonable and accessible by comparison.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Structure Equals Safety: A well-led discovery call makes the prospect feel safe and held before the coaching even begins.
  • Sell the Destination: Don't sell the 12 sessions; sell the peaceful morning and the teenager who actually wants to talk to their mom.
  • Objections are Requests for Information: Treat every "No" or "Maybe" as a coaching moment to explore the prospect's fears.
  • Financial Freedom is a Choice: By setting professional rates ($2k+), you ensure you don't burn out and can provide the highest level of care to your clients.
