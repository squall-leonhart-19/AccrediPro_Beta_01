The Systemic Shift: From "Me" to "Us"

Traditional psychotherapy often operates under the "Individual Paradigm." It asks: What happened to you? What are your thoughts? How can you change your behavior? While valuable, this approach often hits a ceiling when dealing with deep-seated patterns that seem immune to willpower.

The Family Soul and Group Conscience

Bert Hellinger, the founder of this work, observed that every family possesses a Family Soul (Anima). This is not a religious concept, but a biological and systemic regulatory force. Its primary directive is simple: Survival of the System.

Within this Family Soul lives the Group Conscience. Unlike our individual conscience (which tells us what is "good" or "bad" based on our personal values), the Group Conscience is an archaic force that demands total belonging. It ensures that no one is excluded. If a member is forgotten, cast out, or shamed, the Group Conscience will "entangle" a later member to represent that person, forcing the system to look at what was lost.

The Three Orders of Love

Love is the fuel of the family, but the Orders are the pipes. If the pipes are broken, the fuel cannot reach the members. As a Certified Constellator, you will look for where these three laws are being violated:

  1. The Law of Belonging: Everyone has an equal right to belong. No one‚Äînot the criminal, the addict, the aborted child, or the "black sheep"‚Äîcan be excluded without consequence.
  2. The Law of Precedence (Hierarchy): Those who came before have priority over those who came after. Parents give; children take. When a child tries to "save" a parent, the order is violated.
  3. The Law of Balance: In relationships between equals (like partners), there must be a fair exchange of giving and taking.
