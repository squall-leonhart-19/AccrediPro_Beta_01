The Mother: Gateway to Life and Success

Bert Hellinger, the father of Family Constellations, famously stated: "Success has the face of the mother." To the systemic mind, the way we took in life from our mother is the exact way we take in success, money, and professional opportunities.

If there is a disturbed movement toward the mother—whether due to early separation, judgment, or rejection—the individual often struggles to "take" what the world offers. They may find themselves perpetually chasing success but never quite reaching it, or reaching it only to find it "slips through their fingers."

Coach Tip

When a client complains of "money running away," look first at their somatic response when they visualize their mother. If they cannot take her as she is, they will struggle to take the "harvest" of their own professional labor. Prosperity is the adult version of the infant receiving milk.

The 'Loyalty to Failure' Dynamic

Many women in their 40s and 50s pivoting into this career carry a hidden "Loyalty to Failure." This is a subconscious contract that says: "If you suffered, I will not enjoy my life. If you lost the farm, I will not own a home. If you were poor, I will not be wealthy."

A 2021 study on intergenerational financial trauma found that descendants of individuals who experienced significant economic displacement (such as the Great Depression or forced migration) were 42% more likely to exhibit chronic under-earning behaviors, even when highly educated.

Case Study: Sarah’s Invisible Ceiling
Client: Sarah, 49, former teacher turned wellness coach.
Issue: Unable to charge more than $50/hour despite 15 years of expertise.

During a constellation, we Revealed (R) that Sarah’s grandmother had lost her small business during a political upheaval and lived in poverty for the rest of her life. Sarah felt a "blind loyalty" to her grandmother's struggle. By Observing (O) the field, Sarah saw that her success felt like a "betrayal" to her grandmother.

Intervention: Sarah performed a deep bow to her grandmother and said: "Dear Grandma, I see your struggle. I honor your loss. Please look kindly upon me if I allow myself to be well-paid for my work. I do it in your honor, not to leave you behind."

Outcome: Within three months, Sarah restructured her packages and signed three clients at a $2,500 premium rate, finally matching her professional value with her income.

The Systemic Imposter Syndrome

For the career-changing woman, "Imposter Syndrome" is rarely a lack of skill. It is often a lack of systemic precedence. If you are the first woman in your lineage to own a business or earn a six-figure income, you are standing on "unmapped territory."

Without the "permission" of the ancestors, the nervous system interprets success as a threat to belonging. We feel like "imposters" because we are doing something our ancestors never could. To resolve this, we don't need more certifications; we need to bring our ancestors with us into our success.

Coach Tip

When you feel like an imposter, stand up and feel your ancestors behind you. Say to them: "I am the one who carries our name into this new light. I take my place so that our lineage may flourish." This shifts the energy from "I am a fraud" to "I am a representative of my line."

Ordering the Professional System

Success also depends on respecting the Order of Precedence in your business. This applies to how you treat your mentors, your previous careers, and even your clients.

The Orders of Success in Business:

  • Respect for the Founder: The one who started the business (or the idea) has priority.
  • Respect for the Service: The service exists to serve life. If the ego becomes larger than the service, the flow stops.
  • Respect for Previous Careers: If you "hate" your old job as a nurse or teacher, you cannot take the strength of that experience into your new practice. You must "include" your professional past to have a future.
Coach Tip

Many practitioners struggle because they reject their "corporate past." Use a healing sentence: "Everything I learned there, I take with me now. It was the foundation for what I do today." This integrates the energy of your 20+ years of experience into your new certification.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the relationship with the mother considered the "prototype" for success?

Because the mother is our first source of "taking" life. The movement toward the mother—receiving her milk, her care, and her presence—mirrors our adult capacity to receive money, opportunities, and the "harvest" of our work.

2. What is "Loyalty to Failure"?

It is a subconscious systemic dynamic where a descendant stays small, poor, or unsuccessful out of a "blind loyalty" to ancestors who suffered, lost wealth, or were displaced. It is an attempt to "belong" through shared suffering.

3. How does the "Order of Precedence" apply to a career changer?

It requires the practitioner to honor and include their previous careers. If a practitioner rejects their past professional life, they lose the "roots" and strength of that experience, which weakens their current professional standing.

4. From a systemic perspective, what is the root of Imposter Syndrome?

It is often a lack of systemic "permission" to surpass the status or achievements of the ancestors. The individual feels like an imposter because they are breaking a family "norm" of staying small or invisible.
Key Takeaways
  • Success is a systemic flow that begins with the "taking" of the mother as the source of life.
  • Financial blockages are often "Reveal" (R) moments for ancestral losses and unresolved guilt.
  • Money is an energy that honors the balance of giving and taking; it flows where life is respected.
  • To move into a new career, one must "Order" (O) the system by honoring all previous professional chapters.
  • Permission to prosper is granted when we bow to the past and agree to carry the lineage forward into light.
