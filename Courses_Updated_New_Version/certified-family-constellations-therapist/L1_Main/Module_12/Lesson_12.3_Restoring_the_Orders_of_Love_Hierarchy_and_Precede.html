The 'Order' in ROOTS: Architecture of the Soul

In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, "Order" follows "Reveal" and "Observe." Once we have revealed the entanglement and observed the field's reactions, we must actively intervene to restore the Orders of Love. This is not a moral order, but a natural order of priority.

The fundamental rule of systemic order is Chronological Priority. Those who came before have priority over those who came after. However, there is a crucial distinction between the Family of Origin and the Current Family:

System Type Priority Rule The Impact of Disorder
Family of Origin Parents come before children; older siblings before younger. Children try to "save" parents, leading to burnout and failure.
Current Family The current family (partner/children) takes priority over the family of origin. Marital strife; partners feel "second" to their in-laws.
New Relationships A second marriage/family has priority over the first, BUT only if the first is honored. The new family feels unstable or "haunted" by the previous partner.
Coach Tip

When a client says, "I have to put my mother's needs before my husband's," they are violating the Order of the Current Family. As a practitioner, your role is to help them see that while the mother came first in time, the husband has priority in importance for the client's current life stage.

The Right to Belong: Beyond Moral Judgment

The first "Order of Love" is that every member of the system has an equal right to belong. In systemic constellations, we do not look at "good" or "bad" people; we look at "included" or "excluded" members. When we judge a family member‚Äîthe alcoholic uncle, the runaway father, the "black sheep" sister‚Äîwe effectively exclude them from our hearts.

The system, however, does not tolerate a vacuum. If a member is excluded, a later member (often a grandchild) will "step into" that person's energy, unconsciously repeating their fate to ensure they are remembered. Restoring order begins with giving everyone their place.

Hierarchy: The "Big" and the "Small"

The most common hierarchical disruption is Parentification. This occurs when a child, out of "blind love," tries to take care of a parent's emotional or physical needs. Systemically, the parent is "Big" (the giver) and the child is "Small" (the receiver).

Triangulation is a specific form of this disorder where a child is pulled into the conflict between two parents, often becoming a "confidante" for one parent against the other. This places the child in a position of power they cannot handle, leading to lifelong anxiety and "imposter syndrome" in adulthood.

Case Study: Sarah, 48 (Former Teacher)

Presenting Issue: Sarah felt "heavy" and unable to launch her new coaching business, despite having all the credentials. She felt like she was constantly carrying the weight of the world.

The Constellation: In the field, Sarah's representative stood behind her mother, holding her mother's shoulders. Her mother was looking down at a "dead" representative (Sarah's grandmother who died young).

Intervention: The facilitator had Sarah move in front of her mother. Sarah was asked to say: "Mom, I am the child, you are the adult. I am small, you are big. I leave the burden of your mother with you; it is too heavy for me."

Outcome: Sarah felt an immediate physical release in her shoulders. By returning to the "Small" position, she regained the energy needed to focus on her own "Big" life (her business).

The Law of Precedence and Previous Partners

One of the most overlooked orders is the role of the Predecessor. For a second marriage to succeed, the first husband or wife must be honored. They "made room" for the current partner, often through great pain. If they are dismissed as "the crazy ex" or "the mistake," the current relationship remains systemically unstable.

This also applies to:

  • Aborted or miscarried children (they are the first/second/third child).
  • Business partners who were pushed out.
  • Previous owners of land or homes.

Practitioner Insight

If a client is struggling with a "step-child" dynamic, check if the biological mother (the predecessor) has been honored. Often, the step-parent tries to take the "first" position, which causes the child to rebel systemically to protect the biological mother's place.

Techniques for Re-ordering

As a Certified Family Constellations Therapist‚Ñ¢, you use Spatial Movements and Healing Sentences to restore order. The goal is to move representatives until the "Knowing Field" feels "right" or "light."

Key Healing Sentences for Order:

  • To a Parent: "I take the life from you, all of it, at the full price it cost you and that it costs me. I will make something good out of it."
  • To a Predecessor: "I acknowledge you were here first. You have a place in my heart. I am the second wife/husband, and I take my place now."
  • To an Excluded Member: "Now I see you. You belong. You are one of us."

Check Your Understanding

1. Which system takes priority: The Family of Origin or the Current Family?

The Current Family takes priority in importance, although the Family of Origin came first in time. Failure to recognize this often leads to marital conflict.

2. What is the systemic consequence of "Parentification"?

The child becomes "Big" and the parent "Small." This drains the child's energy, making it difficult for them to succeed in their own adult life, as they are "busy" carrying the parent's burden.

3. Why must a previous partner be honored for a current marriage to be stable?

Because the previous partner "made room" for the current one. If they are excluded or judged, the system will often cause the current partner or a child to "re-present" the excluded person's pain or characteristics.

4. What is the "Full Price" mentioned in healing sentences to parents?

The "Full Price" refers to the reality that life comes with both the good and the bad‚Äîthe trauma, the joy, and the history. To accept life is to accept the parents exactly as they are, without wishing they were different.
Final Thought

Order is not about control; it is about peace. When everyone is in their rightful place, the struggle to "be someone else" or "carry something else" vanishes. Your clients will describe this feeling as "finally being able to breathe."

Key Takeaways

  • Chronological Priority: Those who came before have a specific rank that must be respected for the system to be at peace.
  • Hierarchy of Giving: Parents give and children receive. Any reversal of this (Parentification) leads to systemic exhaustion.
  • The Law of Precedence: Current systems can only be healthy if the previous systems (and partners) are acknowledged and honored.
  • Belonging is Absolute: No one can be excluded based on moral behavior without the system attempting to "re-balance" through a later member.
  • The Power of Rank: Simply moving a client to the "Small" position in relation to their ancestors can unlock years of "stuck" energy.
