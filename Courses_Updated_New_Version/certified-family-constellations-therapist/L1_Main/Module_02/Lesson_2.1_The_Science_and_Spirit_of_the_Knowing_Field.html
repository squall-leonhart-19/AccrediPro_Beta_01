Memory vs. Information: The Systemic Record

It is vital to distinguish between Personal Memory and Systemic Information.

  • Personal Memory: Stored in the brain (hippocampus/cortex). It is subjective, often flawed, and limited to what we have personally experienced.
  • Systemic Information: Stored in the Field. It is objective (within the system), timeless, and accessible even if the individual has no conscious knowledge of the events.

Think of the Knowing Field as a "Cloud Storage" for the family. Even if your personal computer (the brain) is wiped or never had the file, the data remains in the Cloud, influencing the current "software" (your life) until it is acknowledged and integrated.
