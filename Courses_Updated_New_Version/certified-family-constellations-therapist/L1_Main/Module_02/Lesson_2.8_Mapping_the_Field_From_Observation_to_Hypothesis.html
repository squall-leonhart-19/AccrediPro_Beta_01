Sarah's Insight

When a client looks down and to the side, they are often accessing an internal "dialogue" or a felt sense of a family member. Don't rush them. That "look" is the field opening up.

The Coaching Script: The Power of Observation

Practice these lines out loud. Notice how the statements are descriptive, not interpretive. We aren't telling Diane what's wrong; we are telling her what we see.

YOU: "Diane, as you speak about this 'heaviness,' I notice that your hand moves to your throat. What do you feel there right now?"
DIANE: "It feels... tight. Like I'm holding something back."
YOU: (Pause for 5 seconds) "I also notice that when you mentioned your mother's retirement, your gaze moved to the floor on your left. Just stay with that for a moment. What do you see there?"
DIANE: (Quietly) "I see her... but she looks so small. Like a child."
YOU: "I see. And as you see her looking so small, what happens to the 'stones' in your backpack?"
DIANE: "They feel heavier. I want to pick her up."
YOU: "I observe that your right shoulder has lifted even higher. You are ready to carry her."
Income Tip

The ability to point out these "micro-movements" is what separates a $50/hour coach from a $250/hour Systemic Constellations Professional. It creates a "wow" moment for the client because they feel truly seen for the first time.

Sarah's Practice Secret

I used to be terrified of silence. I thought I was failing the client. Now, I know that the longer the silence lasts (within reason), the deeper the field is working. Breathe through the urge to fill the gap!

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Interpreting too early: Saying "You're carrying your mother's grief" instead of "I notice you look toward your mother when you mention grief." Let the client reach the conclusion.
  • Missing the "Gaze": Forgetting to watch where the client's eyes go. The eyes always point toward the entanglement.
  • Fixing the posture: Telling the client to "sit up straight" or "relax your shoulder." We want the unconscious posture, not a corrected one.
  • Fear of the "Heavy": Pulling the client out of a difficult emotion too quickly because it makes you uncomfortable.

Success Markers

How do you know you've successfully "Observed the Field"? Look for these indicators:

  • The "Shift": The client takes a deep, spontaneous breath (a "soul breath").
  • The "Aha": The client says, "I never realized I did that with my hand/eyes before."
  • The "Stillness": The room feels suddenly quiet and "charged" with meaning.
  • The "Truth": A statement is made that feels undeniably true to both of you, without needing explanation.
Final Encouragement

You are doing beautifully. Remember, Diane (and all your future clients) doesn't need you to be a superhero. She needs you to be a witness. Your "O" (Observation) is her path to freedom.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we use "Observation Statements" (e.g., "I see your hand is shaking") instead of interpretations?

Because interpretations come from the therapist's mind, while observation statements keep the focus on the "Phenomena" of the field, allowing the client's own soul-movement to reveal the truth.

2. What does a client's gaze toward the floor usually indicate in a constellation context?

It often indicates a connection to someone who is "below" or "gone"â€”frequently an ancestor, a deceased family member, or someone forgotten/excluded from the system.

3. What is the "Soft Focus" or "Phenomenological Gaze"?

It is a way of looking that takes in the whole picture (the client, the space around them, and the felt sense of the room) without focusing sharply on any one detail, allowing the field's movements to become visible.

4. Diane mentions she feels "heaviness" and looks to her left. Which side of the family does the "left" typically represent in systemic work?

In the standard systemic spatial configuration, the left side typically represents the maternal line (the mother and her ancestors).
