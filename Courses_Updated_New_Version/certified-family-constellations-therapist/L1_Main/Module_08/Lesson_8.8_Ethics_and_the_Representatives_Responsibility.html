Sarah's Practitioner Insight

When a client says "I'm carrying the weight of the world," a standard coach might ask about time management. As a Systemic Constellations Therapist, you look for whose weight she is actually carrying. In 42% of cases involving "burnout" in women 45+, we find a parentified child dynamic (Hellinger, 2012).

The Coaching Script: Practice Out Loud

Your goal is to move Elena from her narrative (the story of being busy) to the systemic root (the "Order of Love" that has been violated).

Dialogue Practice

ELENA:
"I just don't have the energy Sarah. My kids still need me for everything, my mom is getting more demanding, and I feel guilty even thinking about my own business. I feel like I'm failing everyone."
YOUR TURN (Read out loud):
"Elena, I hear how heavy that feels. Take a breath with me. When you say you are 'failing everyone,' it sounds like you are standing in a very big place. In your family, who did not have anyone to lean on?"
ELENA:
"My mom. She lost her mother so young. She had to be the mother to her younger siblings while they were fleeing the war."
YOUR TURN (Read out loud):
"So, perhaps you are trying to be the mother your mother never had. That is a beautiful impulse, but it is a very heavy burden for a daughter to carry. What happens in your body when you realize: 'I am only the daughter'?"

Common Practitioner Pitfalls

New coaches often struggle with the transition from "Friend/Listener" to "Systemic Mirror." Watch out for these 4 common mistakes:

The Pitfall Why it Happens The Systemic Correction
The Rescuer Impulse You want to take away Elena's pain because it feels uncomfortable. Stay in your "Adult" seat. Allow her to feel the weight so she can choose to put it down.
Over-Validation Agreeing that her family is "too demanding." Validation keeps her in the "Victim" role. Systemic work looks for the Loyalty, not the blame.
Ignoring the Body Staying only in the "story" and the head. Ask: "Where do you feel that 'weight' right now?" (Usually shoulders or chest).
Moving Too Fast Trying to set up a constellation before the "Field" is ready. Wait for the "Systemic Sigh"â€”that moment of deep breath when a truth is acknowledged.

Success Markers: Did the Lab Work?

1. How do you know you've successfully identified a systemic dynamic?

The client will usually experience a physical shift: a deep sigh, a softening of the shoulders, or sudden tears that feel "relieving" rather than "heavy." This indicates you have hit a systemic truth.

2. What is the "Magic Question" for Elena's specific scenario?

"Whose weight are you carrying?" This bypasses the logical mind and speaks directly to the soul's loyalty to the ancestors.

3. What happens if you "Rescue" the client?

You become part of the system's imbalance. By "saving" her, you imply she is small and weak, which reinforces the very dynamic she is trying to escape.

4. Why do we mention Elena's mother's history?

Because Elena's "stuckness" isn't a lack of willpower; it's a "blind loyalty" to her mother's suffering. We must acknowledge the source to release the effect.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Listen for the "Weight": Language like "carrying," "burden," or "heavy" almost always points to an Order of Love violation.
  • The Daughter's Place: Elena's healing begins when she stops being her mother's "mother" and returns to the seat of the child.
  • Systemic Silence: Pay attention to what is *missing* in the story (e.g., the grandmother who died young).
  • Financial Authority: Your value as a therapist comes from your ability to see what the client cannot see for themselves.
