Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Even seasoned practitioners can fall into these traps when first applying Module 11 concepts. Watch out for these:

  • Trying to "Fix" the Emotion: If the client cries, don't rush to hand them a tissue or change the subject. The emotion is the systemic energy moving. Let it be.
  • Giving Business Advice Too Early: Don't tell Elena to "just use a better booking app." The app isn't the problem; the loyalty to her mother's poverty is.
  • Taking Sides: Avoid saying "Your father shouldn't have treated her that way." We must remain neutral to the whole system to facilitate healing.
  • Over-Explaining the Theory: You don't need to give a lecture on Bert Hellinger. Just lead the client to the feeling.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's struggle with fees considered a "systemic" issue rather than a "business" issue?

Because the root cause is a "blind loyalty" to her mother‚Äôs financial struggle. Elena feels that by being successful, she is betraying her mother's experience of being "trapped" and small.

2. What is the primary purpose of the healing sentence "Please look kindly on me if I allow myself to have what you couldn't"?

It asks for the ancestor's blessing to break the cycle of suffering. It transforms the "betrayal" into an "honor," allowing the client to move forward with the system's support rather than its resistance.

3. What should a coach do if a client begins to cry during a systemic integration?

Hold the space with quiet, supportive presence. Avoid rushing to comfort them or "fix" the crying, as the tears often represent the "unfreezing" of long-held systemic trauma.

4. What is a "Somatic Shift" in a session?

A physical change in the client's body, such as a deep breath, shoulders dropping, or a change in skin tone, indicating that the systemic resolution has been integrated at a nervous system level.

Key Takeaways

  • Loyalty over Logic: Clients will often choose family loyalty over their own professional success until the entanglement is seen.
  • Healing Sentences: Words have power. Delivering them with the right "weight" is a core skill of a Constellation Therapist.
  • The Mother-Money Connection: In many systems, our relationship with our mother mirrors our relationship with "taking" and abundance.
  • Neutrality is Key: To help the client, you must honor the whole system, including the difficult members.
