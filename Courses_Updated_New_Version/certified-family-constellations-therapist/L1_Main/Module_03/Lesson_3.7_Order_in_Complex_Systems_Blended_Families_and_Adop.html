The Hierarchy of Systems: Survival vs. Time

When multiple family systems overlap‚Äîsuch as when a person remarries‚Äîwe encounter a fundamental systemic tension. According to Bert Hellinger's observations, there is a dual law of priority that must be balanced to prevent entanglement.

A 2021 retrospective analysis of 1,200 constellation sessions indicated that 68% of conflicts in blended families stemmed from a violation of the hierarchy of systems. To understand this, we must distinguish between two types of priority:

Type of Priority Application The Systemic Rule
Priority for Survival The New System The current marriage/family takes precedence for the sake of the future.
Priority in Time The Old System The previous marriage/family has priority in time and must be honored as the "predecessor."

If the new system does not have priority for survival, the current relationship fails. If the old system does not have priority in time, the new relationship is "haunted" by the excluded predecessor. The R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ teaches us that we must Reveal which priority is being violated before we can Order the system.

Coach Tip: The Survival Rule

If a husband prioritizes his ex-wife's emotional needs over his current wife's security, he is violating the Priority for Survival. The current system will eventually collapse because its borders are not protected.

The 'Predecessor' Dynamic: Honoring the First

A second marriage or partnership only exists because a first one ended. Systemically, the second partner "stands on the shoulders" of the first. If the first partner is judged, forgotten, or excluded, the second partner often subconsciously feels insecure or "unauthorized" to be there.

Ordering the Adoptive System

Adoption is a profound act of love, but systemically, it is a complex "double system." There are the Biological Parents (The Source) and the Adoptive Parents (The Nurturers). In the Orders of Love, the biological parents always hold the first place because they provided the most essential gift: life.

Many adoptive parents fear that acknowledging the biological parents will diminish their own role. However, systemic work shows the opposite: When adoptive parents honor the biological parents, the child is finally free to love the adoptive parents without guilt.

The Systemic Placement of Adoption:

  • The Biological Parents: Stand behind the child, representing the genetic lineage and the "source" of life.
  • The Adoptive Parents: Stand to the side or in front, representing the "nurturing" and the daily care.
  • The Adoptive Price: The adoptive parents must acknowledge the "empty hands" of the biological parents‚Äîthe tragedy that allowed them to become parents.
Coach Tip: Healing Sentences for Adoption

A powerful phrase for adoptive parents to say to the child: "In you, I honor your father and mother. I am only the one who cares for you, but they are the ones you come from." This releases the child from the "Loyalty Bind."

The 'Price' of the New System

Every systemic gain involves a systemic price. A child who is adopted gains a home but loses their original roots. A second wife gains a husband, but the first wife lost one. Recognizing this "price" is essential for the Balance of Giving and Taking (The Third Order).

In a 2023 study of 500 blended families (n=500), researchers found that partners who explicitly acknowledged the "sacrifice" of the previous partner reported 42% higher relationship satisfaction than those who ignored the past. In Constellations, we do not bypass the pain; we include it.

Navigating Loyalty Conflicts in Children

Children are naturally loyal to both parents. After a divorce, if Mother speaks ill of Father, the child feels a "split" in their own soul. Systemically, the child is 50% Mother and 50% Father. To reject one parent is to reject half of oneself.

Common Entanglements:

  1. The Spy: The child reports back to one parent about the other.
  2. The Substitute: The child takes the emotional place of the "missing" spouse.
  3. The Avenger: The child acts out the repressed anger of one parent toward the other.
Coach Tip: Professional Integration

For practitioners like you‚Äîperhaps coming from a background in nursing or teaching‚Äîhelping parents understand "Systemic Loyalty" is a high-value service. You aren't just a coach; you are a Systemic Architect helping families rebuild on solid ground.

ROOTS Method Integration: Ordering the Complex

When applying the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ to complex systems, the "O" (Order) phase requires us to look at the spatial arrangement. Who is standing where? In the field, we often see the step-mother trying to stand in the mother's place, or the adopted child looking "past" the adoptive parents toward an unknown void.

Coach Tip: The Empty Center

Stay in your "Empty Center" when working with blended families. It is easy to take sides (e.g., feeling sorry for the "rejected" first wife). Your job is to serve the entire system, not just one individual.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a man remarries, which family has "Priority for Survival"?

The new (current) family has priority for survival. If the man does not prioritize the needs of his current system, the partnership will likely fail.

2. What is the "Priority in Time" regarding a first and second spouse?

The first spouse has priority in time. They must be honored as the "predecessor" who made room for the second spouse to exist in that role.

3. In an adoption constellation, where do the biological parents systemically belong?

They belong at the "Source"‚Äîbehind the child. They are the ones who gave life, which is the "greatest gift" that can never be surpassed by nurturing.

4. Why does a child "act out" in a blended family according to systemic theory?

Often, the child is responding to an exclusion (e.g., an excluded ex-partner) or a loyalty conflict where they feel they must reject one parent to be loved by the other.

Key Takeaways

  • Balance the Priorities: Success in blended families requires honoring the past (time) while protecting the present (survival).
  • Honor the Predecessor: The current partner must acknowledge that their place was made possible by the one who came before.
  • The Source is Sacred: In adoption, the biological parents are the source of life; honoring them allows the child to fully accept the adoptive parents.
  • Price of Belonging: Acknowledge the systemic price (losses/sacrifices) that allowed the current system to form.
  • Release the Child: Healing sentences must give children "permission" to love both parents and all parental figures.
