The Mechanics of "Identification"

In Systemic Constellations, identification occurs when a person in the current generation unconsciously takes on the feelings, behaviors, or even the tragic fate of an ancestor. This is not a psychological "choice" but a systemic necessity driven by the family soul‚Äôs demand for wholeness.

A 2021 study on transgenerational trauma (n=450) suggested that individuals carrying "inherited" emotional patterns often exhibit higher cortisol reactivity, even when their own lives are stable. This is the physiological echo of an identification.

Tracing Multi-Generational Trauma Loops

Mapping entanglements requires the practitioner to look for loops‚Äîpatterns that repeat across generations. We use the Genogram as our primary tool, but we look for "Systemic Facts" rather than personality traits.

The Interview Checklist for "Reveal":

  • Early Deaths: Who died before age 25? Who left behind young children?
  • Misfortunes: Who lost a home, a country (migration), or a business?
  • Injustices: Was someone cheated of an inheritance? Did someone commit a crime?
  • Secret Members: Are there "illegitimate" children or hidden adoptions?

The Paradox of "Blind Love"

Most clients suffer not because of a lack of love, but because of Blind Love. This is the child's primitive desire to save their parents or ancestors by suffering in their place. It is a "magical thinking" of the soul: "Better me than you."

As a Certified Family Constellations Therapist‚Ñ¢, you must help the client transition from Blind Love (which leads to illness and failure) to Conscious Love (which leads to strength and resolution). Conscious love says: "I see your fate, and I honor it by living my life to the fullest."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the definition of "Identification" in a systemic context?

Identification is the unconscious process where a member of the current generation takes on the feelings, fate, or behaviors of an ancestor to ensure that ancestor is "represented" in the family system.

2. Why is "Blind Love" considered destructive?

Because it involves the unconscious belief that "I will suffer so you don't have to" or "I will follow you into death/misfortune." It does not actually help the ancestor and only repeats the trauma loop.

3. Which type of "Systemic Fact" is most likely to cause an entanglement?

Exclusion. When a member is forgotten, shamed, or erased (e.g., a "black sheep" or a lost child), the system demands they be seen, usually through the life of a later descendant.

4. How does a practitioner identify the "Primary Entanglement"?

By looking for the "erased" or "missing" member in the family history whose fate most closely mirrors the client's current struggle.
