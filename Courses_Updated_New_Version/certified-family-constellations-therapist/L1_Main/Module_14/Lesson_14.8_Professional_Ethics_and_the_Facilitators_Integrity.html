Sarah's Insight

When a client says "I feel guilty making money," they are often saying "I feel like I'm leaving my family behind." In Constellation work, we don't fix the guilt; we acknowledge the love that drives it.

The Coaching Script

Practice these lines out loud. Imagine Elena is sitting across from you, her shoulders hunched, feeling the weight of her family's struggle.

YOU (The Facilitator):
"Elena, I'd like you to close your eyes and imagine your father standing behind you. Can you feel his presence?"
ELENA:
"Yes. He looks tired. Like he's always carrying something heavy."
YOU:
"Look at him and say these words: 'Dad, I see how hard you worked. I see the price you paid.'"
ELENA:
"Dad, I see how hard you worked. I see the price you paid... (She starts to tear up)."
YOU:
"Now say: 'I have been staying small to stay close to you. I thought if I struggled, I wouldn't lose you. But now, I choose to honor your hard work by being successful. Please look kindly on me if I have enough.'"
Sarah's Insight

The phrase "Please look kindly on me" is one of the most powerful tools in our kit. It asks for the ancestor's blessing to do something different than they did, which dissolves the systemic guilt.

Common Mistakes & Professional Adjustments

Even seasoned practitioners can fall into these traps. Use this table to self-correct during your practice sessions.

Common Mistake Professional Adjustment The Result
Over-Explaining: Trying to explain the psychology of the block. Less is More: Use short, impactful "Healing Sentences." The client stays in their body rather than their head.
Rushing the Shift: Moving to the next step before the client has felt the shift. Wait for the Breath: Wait until the client takes a deep, spontaneous breath. The systemic movement fully integrates into the nervous system.
Taking Sides: Agreeing that the father's business deal was "bad." Radical Neutrality: Honor the fate exactly as it happened. The client finds peace with reality instead of staying in resentment.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask the client to imagine their father "behind" them?

In systemic work, the ancestors stand behind us to provide support and "back" us. If the client feels they must face their father, they are often out of their proper place in the hierarchy.

2. What is the primary indicator that a "Healing Sentence" has worked?

A physiological release, most commonly a deep "sigh of relief," a softening of the shoulders, or a change in skin color (blood flow returning to the face).

3. What does "Invisible Loyalty" mean in a financial context?

It is the unconscious drive to remain at the same economic or social level as one's parents to avoid the feeling of "betraying" them by being more successful.

4. What should you do if a client says "I don't feel anything" during the script?

Don't panic! Simply say, "That's okay. We are just planting a seed. Let the words sit in the room for a moment." Avoid the urge to push for an emotional reaction.
