Your Life Experience is Your Superpower

If you are in your 40s, 50s, or beyond, you might worry that you're "behind." I want to challenge that immediately. In the world of Family Constellations, your age is your greatest clinical asset.

Unlike other fields where "youthful energy" is prized, therapeutic work requires gravitas. You have lived through transitions. You have likely navigated grief, parenting, career shifts, or health challenges. This lived wisdom allows you to hold space for a client in a way a 22-year-old simply cannot. You don't just "learn" empathy; you've earned it.

The Business of Healing

Let's talk about the part many people shy away from: money. Financial freedom is a form of self-care. It allows you to show up for your clients without the stress of "making ends meet" clouding your presence.

Our graduates typically fall into these income brackets within their first 18 months:

  • The Part-Time Practitioner: 5-8 clients/week @ $150/session = $3,000 - $4,800/month.
  • The Full-Time Professional: 15-20 clients/week @ $200/session = $12,000 - $16,000/month.
  • The Workshop Leader: Monthly group intensives (10 people @ $300 each) = $3,000/weekend.

This isn't just "pie in the sky" dreaming. It is the reality for women who treat their certification as the launchpad for a legitimate business.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is life experience considered a "superpower" in this field?

Because Family Constellations work requires the gravitas and lived wisdom that comes with navigating life's transitions, allowing you to hold deeper space for clients.

2. What is the primary purpose of the Private Practice PODs?

To provide a small, supportive community where you can practice skills, find accountability, and combat the isolation of career changing.

3. True or False: You need a prior degree in psychology to succeed as a Family Constellations Therapist.

False. While helpful, the certification is designed to provide you with the specific clinical methodology and professional legitimacy needed to practice effectively.

4. What is the #1 predictor of student success in this program?

Active engagement with the community and mentorship structures, such as attending live calls and participating in PODs.
