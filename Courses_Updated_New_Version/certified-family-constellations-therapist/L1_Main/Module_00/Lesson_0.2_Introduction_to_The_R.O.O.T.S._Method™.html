Your Realistic Timeline to Income

Success doesn't happen overnight, but it happens faster than you think when you have a structured plan. Here is the typical trajectory for a career-changer in our program:

  • Months 1-3: The Learning Phase. You are absorbing the methodology and practicing on "beta" clients (friends, family, fellow students) for free or low-cost testimonials.
  • Months 4-6: The Launch Phase. You secure your first 3-5 paying clients. At $150-$200 per session, you‚Äôre seeing $1,000-$3,000/month while still honing your craft.
  • Months 7-12: The Momentum Phase. Word of mouth spreads. You raise your rates to $250+ per session or launch your first small group program. Income stabilizes at $5,000-$8,000/month.
  • Year 2 and Beyond: The Scaling Phase. You move into group intensives, online programs, or high-ticket retreats. This is where $15,000-$25,000+ months become your new normal.

The Strategy: Elena focused on "Ancestral Healing for Healthcare Workers." She created a 3-month private package priced at $2,400.

The Outcome: Within 14 months of certification, Elena had a consistent roster of 8 private clients and ran one weekend workshop per month. Her average monthly take-home is now $12,500‚Äîmore than she ever made as a nurse, with half the hours.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is selling "packages" recommended over single sessions?

Packages offer better transformational results for the client and provide financial predictability and higher "per-hour" value for the practitioner.

2. What is the "Momentum Phase" in the income timeline?

Occurring between months 7-12, this is when word-of-mouth spreads, rates increase, and income typically stabilizes between $5,000-$8,000/month.

3. True or False: To make $10,000/month, you must work 40+ hours of client sessions per week.

False. By using the "Success Math" of high-value packages (e.g., 12 clients on a $2,500 package), you can reach this goal with significantly fewer hours of direct client work.

4. What is the primary benefit of "niching down" your practice?

Niching allows you to be seen as an expert rather than a commodity, which enables you to charge higher rates and attracts more committed clients.

Key Takeaways

  • Your experience is your edge. As a career changer, your "previous life" skills make you a more relatable and effective therapist.
