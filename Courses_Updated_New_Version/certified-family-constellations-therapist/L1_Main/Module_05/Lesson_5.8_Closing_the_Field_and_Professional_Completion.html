Your goal is to prevent Elena from over-analyzing the session. Use these exact words to help the resolution Settle.

"Good. We are finished for today. For the next 72 hours, I want you to protect this image. Try not to talk about this session with anyone. Let it work in the dark, like a seed."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why it's harmful The Correction
The "How do you feel?" Trap Forces the client into the cognitive brain, away from the felt sense. Observe their body instead of asking for a verbal report.
Rushing to the door The client leaves in a "high" state without being grounded. Ensure feet are flat on the floor before ending.
Over-explaining the symbols The coach's ego takes over the client's internal image. Say less. Let the image speak for itself.
Seeking validation Asking "Was that helpful?" makes the session about your performance. Trust the systemic movement. The work is done.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we discourage the client from "storytelling" immediately after the resolution image is set?

Storytelling moves the energy from the somatic/nervous system (where healing happens) to the cognitive/analytical brain (where the old patterns live). Silence keeps the energy in the body.

2. What is the "72-hour rule"?

It is the instruction to the client not to discuss the constellation with others for three days. This prevents "leaking" the energy of the resolution before it has fully integrated.

3. If a client begins to cry heavily during the "Settle" phase, what should you do?

Hold the space. Do not rush to provide tissues or comfort. Allow the emotional release to complete itself naturally while maintaining a grounded presence.

4. True or False: You should always ask the client for feedback on the session before they leave.

False. Feedback-seeking often breaks the "spell" of the resolution. Trust the work and let the client leave in their quiet, integrated state.

Key Takeaways

  • The "Settle" phase is the most critical step for long-term integration of the healing image.
  • Silence is a therapeutic tool; use it to allow the client's nervous system to discharge old trauma.
  • Protect the work by enforcing the 72-hour "no-talk" rule.
  • Watch for physical success markers like deep sighs, softening shoulders, and steady gazes.
  • You are becoming a real coach when you can sit comfortably in the silence of a profound shift.
