The Language of Symptoms

In systemic work, we do not view illness as a biological error or a "failure" of the body. Instead, we view the symptom as a loyal servant. It is a physical manifestation of a systemic imbalance‚Äîusually an exclusion or a violation of the Orders of Love.

When a family member is excluded, forgotten, or judged, the system seeks balance. If the story cannot be told through words, it is often "told" through the body of a descendant. A 2021 study on transgenerational epigenetics suggests that stress responses can be inherited for up to three generations; in constellations, we see this manifest as specific physiological markers.

Practitioner Insight

If a client presents with a symptom that has "no medical explanation" or is "resistant to all treatment," your systemic radar should be on high alert. These are often the symptoms that carry the heaviest systemic messages.

The "I Follow You" Dynamic

The most common systemic root of serious illness is the "I follow you" (Ich folge dir) dynamic. This is an unconscious, "blind" love where a child or descendant sees the suffering or death of an ancestor and says, in their heart: "To show my love and loyalty, I will be as sick as you," or "I will die like you."

Symptoms as Placeholders

Sometimes, a symptom doesn't just "follow" an ancestor; it acts as the ancestor. This is the "Placeholder" dynamic. If a family member was excluded because of a "shameful" illness (like tuberculosis or mental illness in previous generations), a descendant might develop a symptom that forces the family to look at that exact type of suffering again.

Symptom Category Potential Systemic Messenger
Autoimmune Issues Internalized conflict; "fighting oneself" as a surrogate for a family feud.
Chronic Back Pain Carrying the "weight" or "burden" of a parent's unexpressed grief.
Addiction A "search" for an excluded father or a way to numb the pain of an excluded mother.
Skin Conditions Boundary issues; representing someone who was "untouchable" or shamed.

Spatial Mapping: Differentiating the Symptom

In the Observe phase of the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we use spatial representation to see the relationship between the client and their illness. We set up a representative for the Client and a representative for the Symptom.

  • Observation A: If the Symptom representative looks away from the Client, they are likely looking at the ancestor the illness represents.
  • Observation B: If the Client representative tries to "hug" or "clash" with the Symptom, it shows an entanglement of identity.
  • Observation C: If the Symptom representative feels "heavy" or "cold," it often indicates a connection to the deceased.
Language Tip

Avoid saying "Your illness is caused by your grandfather." Instead, use systemic language: "Your body seems to be carrying a memory that belongs to your grandfather. Shall we see if your grandfather would prefer you to be healthy?"

Transmutation Rituals: Returning the Weight

The Transmute phase involves a "Deep Bow" or a ritual of return. We help the client realize that their suffering does not actually help the ancestor. In fact, most ancestors‚Äîwhen represented in the Field‚Äîdesire their descendants to live fully.

The Healing Sentence:

Ethical Medical Integration

As a Certified Family Constellations Therapist‚Ñ¢, you must adhere to strict ethical guidelines regarding physical illness. Systemic work is complementary, never a replacement for clinical care.

The Golden Rule of Systemic Health

Never advise a client to stop medication, skip surgery, or ignore medical advice. The constellation works on the informational and systemic level, while the doctor works on the biological level. True healing often requires both.

Check Your Understanding

1. What does the "I Follow You" dynamic typically represent in a constellation?

It represents an unconscious loyalty where a descendant takes on the illness or fate of an ancestor to stay connected to them or to "share" their burden out of blind love.

2. If a representative for a "Symptom" is looking past the client into the distance, what is the likely systemic interpretation?

The symptom is likely a "placeholder" for an excluded or forgotten family member (often deceased) whom the system is trying to bring back into awareness.

3. How should a practitioner handle a client who wants to stop their medical treatment because of a breakthrough in a constellation?

The practitioner must ethically insist that the client continues their medical protocol and consults their physician. Constellation work addresses the systemic root, but the physical body still requires biological support.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Bow" in a health-related constellation?

To honor the ancestor's fate and "return" the burden to them, acknowledging that the descendant's suffering does not lessen the ancestor's pain, thereby freeing the descendant to live their own life.

Key Takeaways

  • Symptoms are systemic messengers, not biological accidents.
  • The "I Follow You" dynamic is a form of "blind love" that manifests as physical illness.
  • In the Field, differentiating the "Self" from the "Symptom" allows for the observation of hidden loyalties.
  • Healing occurs when the client gives the symptom's "message" a place in their heart, allowing the physical body to release the burden.
  • Systemic health work must always be integrated with professional medical care to ensure client safety.
