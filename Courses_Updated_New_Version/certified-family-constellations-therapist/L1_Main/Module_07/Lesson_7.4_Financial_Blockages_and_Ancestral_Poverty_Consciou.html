The Link Between 'Mother' and 'Success'

In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we often say: "Success has the face of the mother." This isn't just a poetic sentiment; it is a foundational systemic observation. Bert Hellinger noted that our primary relationship with the mother serves as the blueprint for how we relate to Life itself‚Äîand money is a form of life energy.

If a client rejects their mother, judges her, or feels "above" her, they are unconsciously rejecting the source from which they came. In the Knowing Field, this rejection often manifests as a "turning away" from money or success. When we cannot take our mother as she is, we struggle to take the "nourishment" the world offers in the form of profit and prosperity.

Coach Tip: The Pricing Mirror

If a client is struggling to charge professional rates (e.g., $150-$250/hour), look first at her ability to "receive" from her mother. Imposter syndrome is often a systemic symptom of being "out of order" with the maternal source. When she settles into being the "small one" receiving from the "big one" (Mother), she can finally receive from her clients.

Reveal Entanglements: Ancestral Poverty Consciousness

Poverty consciousness is rarely about the present-day economy. It is an epigenetic echo of survival. If your ancestors survived the Great Depression, the Irish Potato Famine, or systemic dispossession, their nervous systems were "wired" for scarcity. This wiring is passed down as a loyalty to their suffering.

Common Symptoms of Ancestral Scarcity Loops:

  • The "Just Enough" Ceiling: Earning exactly what is needed for bills, but never a cent more.
  • Self-Sabotage at Thresholds: Gaining a large sum of money only to have an "emergency" expense (car breakdown, medical bill) immediately drain it.
  • The Guilt of Having More: Feeling a deep, inexplicable shame when earning more than one's parents or siblings.

Coach Tip: The "Why Me?" Inquiry

When a client consistently loses money despite high earnings, ask: "Who in your family gained something they didn't earn?" or "Who lost everything so your family could have something?" This often reveals the "Blood Money" entanglement.

The 'Right to Prosper' and Realigning with Flow

Prosperity requires Systemic Permission. In many families, there is a "script" that says: "We are hard-working, simple people. We aren't like those greedy rich people." Breaking this script feels like a betrayal of the family soul. To heal this, we must shift the client's perspective from "betrayal" to "contribution."

The R.O.O.T.S. Ritual for Prosperity:

  1. Observe the Field: Place a representative for "The Ancestors" and "The Client's Wealth."
  2. Acknowledge the Scarcity: The client says to the ancestors: "You had so little, and you did so much with it. I honor your strength."
  3. Request Permission: "Please look kindly upon me as I do it differently."
  4. The Turn: The client physically turns their back to the ancestors (with their blessing) and faces the representative of Wealth.

Business Failures as a Reflection of Exclusions

A business is a living system. If a business consistently fails or "leaks" money, it often indicates that an excluded member of the family is being represented by the "loss."

For example, if a grandfather was disowned for his financial failures, his grandson may unconsciously repeat those failures to "bring the grandfather back" into the family's awareness. In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we don't fix the business; we fix the inclusion.

Coach Tip: Business as a Representative

In a constellation, let the "Business" be a representative. Watch where it looks. If the Business looks at the floor, it is looking at a "dead" or "excluded" family member. The business cannot thrive until that person is honored.

Check Your Understanding

1. According to systemic principles, why is the relationship with the mother linked to financial success?

The mother is the primary source of life. Systemically, our ability to "take" from our mother mirrors our ability to "take" and receive the nourishment, profit, and abundance that life offers. Rejection of the mother often manifests as a rejection of success.

2. What is "Blood Money" in a systemic context?

Blood Money refers to wealth or assets gained through unethical means, harm to others, or the exclusion of rightful heirs. The systemic conscience often "balances" this by causing descendants to lose money or suffer financial blockages as a form of unconscious compensation.

3. How does "Ancestral Poverty Consciousness" manifest in a modern client's behavior?

It often manifests as a "glass ceiling" on income, self-sabotage when reaching new financial thresholds, or an inexplicable sense of guilt or shame when the client has more than their ancestors or siblings. It is a blind loyalty to the family's history of scarcity.

4. If a business is consistently failing, what is the first systemic question a practitioner should ask?

"Who is being excluded?" or "Who lost something so this business could exist?" We look for excluded family members or victims of ancestral financial crimes who are "taking" the profit to gain visibility in the system.
