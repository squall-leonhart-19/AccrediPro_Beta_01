Interpreting Behavior as 'Blind Love'

In Systemic Constellations, we operate under the premise that children are the most sensitive members of a family system. When there is an exclusion, a forgotten ancestor, or an injustice in the previous generations, the child unconsciously steps in to "represent" the missing piece. This is what Bert Hellinger termed "Blind Love."

A child who is hyperactive, aggressive, or withdrawn is often looking at someone the parents have forgotten. For example, a son who is disruptive in class may be unconsciously imitating an excluded grandfather who was "the black sheep." By being "difficult," the child ensures that the family energy of the grandfather remains present in the system.

Coach Tip: The Practitioner's Gaze

When a parent comes to you saying, "My child has ADHD," your first systemic question should be: "Who is the child looking at that you are not?" This shifts the burden off the child and back to the systemic order where it belongs.

The Hierarchy in Schools: Parents, Teachers, Students

The school is a secondary system that must respect the primary system (the family). For a child to learn effectively, there must be a clear Order of Precedence. If a teacher looks down on a child's parentsâ€”even subtlyâ€”the child will unconsciously "defend" the parents by refusing to learn from that teacher.

Systemic Order Relationship Dynamic Impact on the Child
Order 1 Teacher respects Parents as the primary source of life. Child feels safe to learn and grow.
Disorder 1 Teacher feels "better" or more "educated" than the Parents. Child becomes defiant or fails to show their intelligence.
Order 2 Parents support the Teacher's authority in the classroom. Child respects boundaries and school rules.
Disorder 2 Parents criticize the Teacher in front of the child. Child feels superior to the teacher and loses focus.

Using the R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢ in Pedagogy

The R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢ provides a structured way to navigate these complex dynamics without resorting to labels or medication as a first resort.

  • Reveal: Identify who or what the child is representing. Is it a deceased sibling? An excluded former partner of the father?
  • Observe: Use the Knowing Field to see where the child's gaze is directed. Are they looking at the teacher, or past the teacher toward a family trauma?
  • Order: Restore the hierarchy. The teacher must say (internally), "I see your parents behind you, and I honor them as the big ones."
  • Transmute: Use healing sentences to release the child. "I am just the child. I leave the heavy things with you, Mom/Dad."
  • Settle: Allow the child to return to their "small" place, free from the adult burdens of the system.

The 'Gift of Life' in Education

Learning is essentially an act of taking. We take knowledge from the teacher just as we take life from our parents. However, if a child has a "taking block" with their parents, they will have a "learning block" at school.

A child who judges their mother as "weak" or their father as "bad" cannot fully take the "Gift of Life" from them. Because they cannot take from the source, they cannot take from the teacher. Successful systemic pedagogy involves helping the child (and the parents) accept the parents exactly as they are. When the child says "Yes" to their parents, they say "Yes" to life and "Yes" to learning.

Practical Tools: Parents Taking Their Place

The greatest gift a parent can give a child is to be "the big one." When parents are entangled in their own ancestral trauma, they often unconsciously look to the child for emotional support (parentification). To free the child, the parent must "take their place."

Coach Tip: The Daily Ritual

Encourage parents to spend 2 minutes every morning visualizing their own parents standing behind them, hands on their shoulders. This "fills" the parent so they don't need to "drain" the child for emotional stability.

Healing Sentences for Pedagogy

In your sessions, use these sentences to help restore order between parents, children, and teachers:

  • For the Parent to the Teacher: "I give you my child for these hours. Please teach them what they need to know. I remain the parent."
  • For the Teacher to the Parent: "I see you as the right parents for this child. I am only the teacher."
  • For the Child to the Parent: "You are the big ones, I am the small one. You give, I take."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the systemic definition of 'Blind Love' in a child?

Blind Love is the unconscious loyalty a child feels toward the family system, leading them to represent excluded members or carry burdens that belong to previous generations through their own behavior.

2. If a child refuses to learn from a specific teacher, what is the most likely systemic cause?

The most likely cause is that the teacher (unconsciously) lacks respect for the child's parents, or the parents have criticized the teacher's authority, disrupting the Systemic Order of Precedence.

3. How does the R.O.O.T.S. Methodâ„¢ 'Reveal' phase apply to a 'difficult' child?

It involves looking past the child's symptoms to identify who or what in the family system is being excluded or forgotten that the child is now representing through their behavior.

4. Why is the 'Gift of Life' essential for academic success?

Because learning is an act of 'taking.' If a child cannot 'take' life and love from their parents due to judgment or entanglement, they will often have a blocked capacity to 'take' knowledge from teachers.
