Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

New practitioners often fall into these traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

4 Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-Explaining: Trying to "teach" the client the theory during the session. (Let the experience do the teaching).
  • Rushing the Silence: Feeling uncomfortable when the client is processing. (Silence is where the soul moves).
  • Taking the Burden: Feeling like you have to fix the client's family. (You are just the facilitator).
  • Using "Why" Questions: "Why do you feel this way?" (In constellations, we ask "What" and "Who," not "Why").

How to Know You Succeeded

  • The client experiences a Physical Shift (shoulders drop, color returns to face).
  • The "Field" feels lighter or more peaceful.
  • The client stops talking about the "problem" and starts looking toward the "solution."
  • You feel energized, not drained, after the session.
Sarah's Insight

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Survivor's Guilt" in a systemic context?

It is the subconscious feeling that being happy, healthy, or wealthy is a betrayal of ancestors who suffered or died. We resolve it by honoring their sacrifice through our own success.

2. Why do we ask the client to speak "The Sentences" out loud?

Speaking the truth out loud creates a vibration in the body that bypasses the logical mind and speaks directly to the "Family Soul" or the subconscious field.

3. What should you do if a client says a sentence "doesn't feel true"?

Stop. Do not force it. Acknowledge the resistance and say, "Let's just look at that for a moment." Forcing a resolution before the client is ready can cause them to close off.

4. What is the primary indicator of a successful systemic shift?

A visible physical release in the client, such as a deep breath, a change in skin tone, or a softening of the facial muscles.

Key Takeaways

  • Systemic blocks often manifest as "self-sabotage" in career and finances.
  • The practitioner's role is to facilitate the client's connection to their ancestors' strength, not just their pain.
  • "The Sentences" are tools of resonance; their power lies in their truth, not their complexity.
  • Success is measured by the client's ability to turn away from the past and look toward their own life.
