Background & Presenting Issue

Elena recently left a 25-year career in education to start her own consulting business. Despite having the credentials and a clear business plan, she feels a profound "heaviness" and "guilt" whenever she starts to succeed.

Systemic Context: Elenaâ€™s mother was a refugee who lost everything during a war. Her mother spent her life working menial jobs and often said, "People like us shouldn't get too comfortable; it can all be taken away."

Elena's Goal: "I want to feel 'allowed' to be successful and wealthy without feeling like I'm betraying my family's struggle."

Sarah's Insight

When a client mentions "guilt" regarding success, your systemic radar should immediately look for a loyalty to suffering. Elena is subconsciously staying "small" to remain in solidarity with her mother's hardship.

The Coaching Script

Read this script out loud. Pay attention to the pacing. Systemic work requires pauses to let the field "settle."

YOU:
"Elena, I hear how much you want this business to thrive. And I also hear the heaviness you feel when things start going well. If we look behind you, at your mother's journey... does this 'heaviness' feel like it belongs only to you, or does it feel older?"
ELENA:
"It feels... heavy. Like I'm carrying her fear. She always said we shouldn't get too comfortable. If I'm successful, I feel like I'm leaving her behind in that struggle."
YOU:
"That is a very deep, loving loyalty. You are trying to stay with her by sharing her burden. But let's look at it systemicly: If you stay small and struggling, does that make her past hardship any easier? Or would she, as a mother, want her daughter to have the life she couldn't?"
ELENA:
(Pauses, takes a breath) "I think she'd want me to be happy. But it feels wrong to have so much when she had so little."
YOU:
"I invite you to try a sentence with me. Imagine her standing before you, and say: 'Dear Mom, I see your struggle. I honor it by doing something good with my life. Please look kindly on me if I am successful.'"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why does Elena feel "guilty" about her business success?

Systemically, Elena is experiencing a "blind loyalty" to her mother's suffering. By succeeding, she subconsciously feels she is "betraying" her mother's hardship.

2. What is the purpose of the "Healing Sentence" used in the script?

The sentence "I honor your struggle by doing something good with my life" allows the client to remain connected to their parent while also giving themselves permission to thrive. It transforms loyalty from "suffering with" to "honoring through success."

3. What is a common mistake for new systemic coaches during an intake?

A common mistake is rushing into "problem-solving" mode or trying to analyze the psychology, rather than pausing to feel the systemic "weight" or "field" of the family history.

4. How does systemic coaching differ from standard mindset coaching regarding "limiting beliefs"?

While standard coaching treats beliefs as individual errors in thinking, systemic coaching views them as "functional loyalties" that serve to keep the individual connected to their family system.

Key Takeaways

  • Systemic Intake: Always look for the "older" story behind the current "stuck" feeling.
  • The Orders of Love: Success is only possible when we have our parents' "blessing" (internalized) behind us.
  • Healing Sentences: Words have power. Use them slowly and let them land in the client's heart.
  • Professional Presence: Ground yourself before every session; you are the "anchor" for the client's family field.
