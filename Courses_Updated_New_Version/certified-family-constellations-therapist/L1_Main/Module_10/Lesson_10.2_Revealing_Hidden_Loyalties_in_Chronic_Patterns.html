"I Follow You" vs. "I Do It For You"

These two phrases represent the core mechanics of systemic entanglement. Understanding the nuance between them is critical for a successful resolution.

"I Follow You" A blind loyalty to an ancestor who died young, was excluded, or suffered a tragic fate. Chronic illness, suicidal ideation, "accidental" injuries, inability to feel alive.
"I Do It For You" An attempt to carry the guilt, grief, or burden of a parent or grandparent to "lighten their load." Depression, heavy emotional "weight," financial struggle, taking on others' responsibilities.
