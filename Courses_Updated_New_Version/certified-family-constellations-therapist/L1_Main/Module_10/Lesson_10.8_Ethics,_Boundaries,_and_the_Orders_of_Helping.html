Your Coaching Script

In this scenario, you have already identified the link between her business block and her early separation from her mother. Practice these restorative sentences with a slow, rhythmic pace.

Script: The Restorative Movement

YOU:
"Elena, I'd like you to close your eyes for a moment. Imagine your mother standing a few feet in front of you. See her not as she is now, but as she was when you were a little girl."
ELENA:
(Breathing shallowly) "Okay. I see her. She looks tired."
YOU:
"Yes. And notice that little 3-year-old Elena inside you. She wanted to reach out, but the hands weren't there to catch her. I want you to say these words to her: 'Dear Mom. I was so small, and I missed you so much.'"
ELENA:
(Voice trembling) "Mom... I was so small... and I missed you so much."
YOU:
"Wait for the breath. Good. Now say: 'The reach was broken, but the love stayed. I am ready to complete that reach now. I take my life from you, at the full price it cost you and me. I will do something good with it.'"
ELENA:
(Takes a deep, audible breath) "I take my life from you... I will do something good with it."

Sarah's Insight

I remember when I first started, I was so afraid of the client's tears. I would try to "fix" them or hand them a tissue immediately. I realized later that by doing that, I was interrupting their movement again. Let them cry. Your job is to be the steady mountain while they weather the storm.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

The Mistake Why It Fails The Professional Pivot
Rushing the Sentences The client needs time to process the "weight" of the systemic truth. Wait for the client to take a deep breath (the "sigh of relief") before moving on.
Focusing on "Blame" Blaming the mother for being sick weakens the client's connection to their source. Focus on the event (the separation) rather than the mother's "fault."
Over-Explaining Too much "heady" talk pulls the client out of their body. Use fewer words. Let the restorative sentences do the heavy lifting.
Seeking "Closure" Systemic work isn't about a happy ending; it's about inclusion. Accept whatever emotions arise, even if the client still feels "sad" at the end.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was effective? Look for these "Shifts in the Field":

  • Somatic Shift: The client's color returns to their face, or their posture naturally straightens.
  • The "Full Breath": A deep, spontaneous inhalation that reaches the belly.
  • Softened Eyes: The "hard" look of resentment or the "blank" look of trauma is replaced by a softer, more present gaze.
  • A Shift in Perspective: The client mentions their business or goal with a sense of "I can" rather than "I should."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Interrupted Reach" specifically linked to business success and marketing?

Because the mother represents our first experience of "the world." If we could not safely reach for her, we unconsciously feel it is unsafe to reach for clients, success, or visibility in the professional world.

2. What is the primary purpose of restorative sentences like "I take my life from you at the full price"?

These sentences acknowledge the reality of the systemic cost and allow the client to stop trying to "repay" a debt they can't pay, allowing them to finally move forward into their own life.

3. If a client starts sobbing hysterically during the reach, what should the coach do?

Maintain a calm, grounded presence. Do not rush to comfort them. Allow the emotion to move through. Your "holding" of the space is what allows the trauma to resolve.

4. What is the "Full Price" Elena refers to in the script?

The "Full Price" includes the pain of the separation, the difficulties the mother faced, and the challenges Elena endured. Acknowledging the price makes the life received more "valuable" and real.

Practice Lab Key Takeaways

  • The Interrupted Reach is a somatic and systemic block, not a logical one.
  • Restorative sentences must be spoken slowly to allow the nervous system to "digest" them.
  • Success is measured by the client's physical and energetic shift, not just their verbal agreement.
  • As a coach, your most powerful tool is your ability to remain grounded while the client processes deep systemic grief.
  • By completing the reach to the mother, the client gains the "permission" to reach for success in the world.
