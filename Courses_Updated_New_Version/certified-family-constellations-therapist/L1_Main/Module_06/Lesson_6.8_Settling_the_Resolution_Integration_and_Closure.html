Your Coaching Scenario

In Family Constellations, we often see clients who are "doing everything right" on the surface, yet they remain stuck. This is frequently a sign of a systemic entanglementâ€”an unconscious loyalty to someone in the family system who suffered or was excluded.

Client Profile: Elena

Elena, 52. A former elementary school teacher from Ohio. She is transitioning into a wellness coaching career. She has the certifications, the website, and the passion, but she "cannot seem to charge what she's worth" and feels a strange guilt every time she gains a new client.

Symptom Elena's Description Systemic Clue
Financial Block "I feel like a fraud taking money for helping people." Guilt associated with "having more."
Career Stalling "I start things but stop right before I succeed." Fear of outshining a family member.
Family History Father was a coal miner who died young; Mother struggled in poverty. Loyalty to the parents' hardship.
Sarah's Insight

When a client like Elena says she feels like a "fraud," she's often unconsciously saying: "How can I be successful and wealthy when my father worked so hard and had so little?" This is the "loyalty of the heart."

Your Coaching Script

Practice these words out loud. Pay attention to your breath and your pacing. In Constellation work, we speak slowly and let the words land.

YOU (The Coach):
"Elena, I hear how much you want this new career to work. But I also hear a heavy guilt when you talk about your success. When you think about your father and his hard life in the mines... does it feel 'right' for you to have an easy, successful life?"
ELENA:
"No... it feels like I'm betraying him. Like I'm saying his life didn't matter because I have it so much better."
YOU:
"I understand. This is a very deep love. We call it 'blind loyalty.' You are trying to stay close to him by sharing his struggle. But I want to try something. Imagine your father is standing in front of you now. Look into his eyes. Can you say these words to him?"
YOU (The Resolution Sentence):
"Dear Papa. I see how hard you worked. I honor your struggle. And now, I ask you to look kindly upon me if I am successful. I do it in your honor, not to leave you behind."
Sarah's Insight

Watch for the "shift." After a client says a resolution sentence, they often take a deep breath or their shoulders drop. That is the systemic weight lifting. Don't rush into the next question; let the silence do the work.

Practice Exercise: The Facilitator's Voice

Itâ€™s time to move these concepts from your head to your heart. Follow these steps to build your confidence:

  1. The Grounding Breath: Before you speak, place both feet on the floor. Imagine you are supported by your own ancestors. This prevents you from "leaning into" the client's energy.
  2. Speak to the Mirror: Read the "Resolution Sentence" from the script above while looking at yourself in the mirror. Do you look certain? Or do you look like you're asking for permission? Aim for a loving authority.
  3. Record and Review: Use your phone to record yourself saying the script. Listen back. Are you speaking too fast? (Common for new coaches). Practice slowing down by 20%.
Sarah's Insight

I used to record my sessions (with permission) and listen to them while driving. I realized I was interrupting the client's silence because I was uncomfortable with the "heavy" energy. Learning to sit in that silence was my biggest breakthrough as a practitioner.

Common Facilitation Mistakes

As you move into this work, your desire to help is your greatest assetâ€”and sometimes your biggest hurdle. Avoid these 4 common pitfalls:

  • Taking the Burden: If you feel exhausted after a 20-minute intake, you are likely trying to "carry" Elena's father for her. Remember: You are the facilitator, not the savior.
  • Rushing the Sentence: Giving the resolution sentence too early, before the client feels the "pinch" of the entanglement, makes it just words. Wait for the emotion.
  • Over-Explaining: You don't need to explain the theory of Constellations during the session. The experience of the shift is what heals, not the intellectual understanding.
  • Fixing the Mother/Father: New coaches often try to "make the father happy" in the visualization. In systemic work, we accept the father exactly as he wasâ€”hardship and all.
Sarah's Insight

You'll know you're doing well when you feel "light" at the end of a session. If you feel heavy, go back to your own "Orders of Love" practice. Are you trying to be "bigger" than the client?

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these indicators in your practice sessions:

Marker What it looks like What it means
Somatic Shift Client takes a spontaneous deep breath (sigh). The nervous system is releasing the systemic tension.
The "Soft Eye" Client's gaze softens; they stop looking "at" you and look "inward." They have connected with the internal image of the ancestor.
Sentence Ownership Client repeats the sentence with their own emphasis or tears. The words have become their own truth.
Facilitator Neutrality You feel calm and "behind" the client, not in front of them. You have successfully maintained the facilitator's boundaries.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why did Elena feel like a "fraud" when charging for her wellness coaching?

She was experiencing "blind loyalty" to her father's hardship. To have "easy" money felt like a betrayal of his difficult life in the coal mines.

2. What is the primary purpose of a "Resolution Sentence" in this scenario?

To acknowledge the ancestor's fate (the struggle) and ask for their "blessing" to do things differently, thereby breaking the unconscious cycle of suffering.

3. If you feel physically exhausted after speaking with Elena, what is likely happening?

You have fallen into the "Healer's Trap" and are unconsciously trying to carry the client's systemic burden instead of remaining a neutral facilitator.

4. What should you do immediately after a client speaks a Resolution Sentence?

Remain silent. Allow the client to process the somatic shift and let the words settle into their soul without intellectual interference.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Presence Over Perfection: Your grounded presence is more healing than "perfect" technique.
  • The Gift of Success: Remind clients like Elena that their success is the best way to honor ancestors who struggled.
  • Slow is Fast: In systemic work, the slower you go, the deeper the transformation occurs.
  • You are Ready: Imposter syndrome is common for career changers; trust the process you've learned.
