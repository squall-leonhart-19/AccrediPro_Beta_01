Autoimmune Dynamics: The Proxy War

In conventional medicine, autoimmune disorders are defined as the immune system‚Äôs failure to distinguish between "self" and "non-self." In Family Constellations, we view this as a proxy war. The aggression that rightfully belongs toward a systemic injustice or a perpetrator is turned inward.

Bert Hellinger often observed that autoimmune symptoms appear when a child (even as an adult) feels a deep, unconscious aggression toward a parent but suppresses it out of "blind love." Because it is "safer" to attack the self than to risk the bond with the parent, the body takes on the burden of the conflict.

Practitioner Insight

When working with autoimmune clients, look for the "I do it for you" dynamic. The client may be carrying the guilt or the suppressed anger of a parent. By attacking their own tissue, they are unconsciously attempting to "atone" for a systemic sin they didn't commit.

Cancer: The Movement Toward Death

Cancer is often viewed systemically as a "Movement toward Death" (Hinbewegung zum Tod). This is not a conscious wish to die, but a deep systemic pull toward someone who has been lost, forgotten, or excluded. The malignant growth can be seen as a physical manifestation of an "excluded presence" that is demanding to be seen.

Identifying the 'Excluded One'

In many cancer cases, the tumor acts as a placeholder for someone who was cast out of the family soul. This could be a child given up for adoption, a perpetrator, a victim of the family, or an ancestor who died in "disgrace."

The biological process of cancer‚Äîcells that grow without regard for the boundaries of the whole‚Äîmirrors the systemic reality of an individual who was not allowed to belong. When the excluded person is "brought home" to the heart of the client, the biological "need" for the symptom to represent them often diminishes.

Practitioner Insight

The Role of Grandmother/Grandfather Energy

When a client is in a life-threatening crisis, the immediate parents often feel "too close" or are part of the entanglement. We look further back to the Grandparents. This energy is typically more stable, providing a "buffer" of ancestral strength.

  • Stability: Grandparents represent the survival of the line through greater challenges (wars, depressions).
  • Perspective: They see the "long view" of the family, beyond the immediate drama of the parents.
  • Resolution: Often, the "Excluded One" is from the grandparents' generation, making their presence essential for reconciliation.
