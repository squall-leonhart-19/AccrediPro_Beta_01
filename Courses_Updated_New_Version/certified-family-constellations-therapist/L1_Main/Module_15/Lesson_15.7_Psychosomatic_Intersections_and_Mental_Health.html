Depression: The Weight of the "Interrupted Reach"

In conventional psychiatry, depression is often viewed as a serotonin deficiency or a cognitive distortion. In Family Constellations, we look for the movement of the soul that has been frozen. Bert Hellinger famously identified that many forms of chronic depression stem from an interrupted reach out to a parent (usually the mother).

Anxiety as a Systemic Alarm

Anxiety is rarely "about" the present moment. Systemically, anxiety is often a somatic alarm triggered by a hidden exclusion. A 2021 study on transgenerational trauma suggested that "unexplained" anxiety in descendants often correlates with high-stress events in the grandparental generation (n=1,200).

We ask: "Whose fear is this?"

  • The Refugee Echo: A client feels constant panic about "not having enough," even with a high-paying job. We find a grandfather who lost everything during a war.
  • The Displaced Grief: A client has panic attacks in crowded places. We find an aunt who died in a crowded hospital, forgotten by the family.

Psychosis: The Murderer and Victim Dynamic

Perhaps the most controversial and profound discovery in systemic work is the dynamic behind schizophrenia and psychosis. Hellinger observed that in these systems, there is often a perpetrator and a victim from a previous generation who have both been excluded or forgotten.

The "split" in the schizophrenic mind often reflects a split in the family soul. The individual tries to hold both the murderer and the victim in their own heart. Because these two cannot be reconciled in the past, they fragment the consciousness of the descendant in the present.

Chemical Imbalance vs. Systemic Entanglement

It is vital to maintain professional ethics. We do not deny the reality of neurochemistry. However, we view chemistry as the ink and the systemic entanglement as the story. Changing the ink (medication) can make the story easier to read, but it doesn't change the plot.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Interrupted Reach" and how does it manifest in adults?

The Interrupted Reach occurs when early bonding with a parent is broken. In adults, it manifests as chronic depression, a lack of "vitality," and a feeling of being unable to "take" or succeed in life.

2. How does the systemic view explain the "split" in schizophrenia?

It is often seen as the individual attempting to represent both a perpetrator and a victim from the family's history who have both been excluded. The mental fragmentation reflects the unreconciled conflict in the family soul.

3. True or False: Anxiety is always caused by the client's own life experiences.

False. Systemically, anxiety is often a "systemic alarm" or inherited fear from an ancestor who experienced trauma, displacement, or loss.

4. What is the primary goal of "Order the System" in mental health cases?

To stabilize the client by restoring hierarchical integrity, ensuring they feel supported by their ancestors (behind them), and separating their fate from the fates of those who came before.

Key Takeaways for the Practitioner

  • Symptoms are Language: Mental health diagnoses are not "broken brains" but "burdened souls" speaking through the body.
  • The Mother Connection: Depression almost always requires a restorative movement toward the mother.
  • Perpetrator Inclusion: For severe psychological fragmentation, the "murderer" in the system must be acknowledged and given a place to find peace.
  • Empowerment through Roots: By revealing the entanglement, you move the client from "victim of chemistry" to "agent of systemic resolution."
