The 'I Follow You' Dynamic: Blind Love in the Body

The most profound and often tragic dynamic in systemic work is blind loyalty, specifically the movement known as "I follow you." This occurs when a child (or the child within an adult) unconsciously observes the suffering, illness, or early death of a parent or ancestor and decides‚Äîout of a deep, archaic love‚Äîto share that fate.

This is not a conscious decision. It is a movement of the family soul that whispers: "If you suffered, I will suffer too. If you died young, I will not allow myself to live fully." In the context of chronic illness, this dynamic manifests as a biological resonance where the client's body recreates the "climate" of the ancestor's pain.

Chronic Illness as a Systemic Messenger

Chronic conditions like fibromyalgia and migraines are often referred to as "functional" disorders in conventional medicine‚Äîmeaning the structure is fine, but the function is impaired. In Family Constellations, we view these as systemic "reminders."

Fibromyalgia and the Weight of the Ancestors

A 2021 study on transgenerational trauma suggests that unexplained widespread pain often correlates with high ACE (Adverse Childhood Experiences) scores in previous generations. Systemically, fibromyalgia often represents the "carrying" of multiple burdens that do not belong to the individual. The client's muscles and fascia are literally "holding" the tension of a past trauma that was never resolved.

Migraines: The Pressure of Secret Loyalties

Migraines often appear when there is a conflict between the client's desire for individual autonomy and an unconscious loyalty to a family secret or an excluded person. The "pressure" in the head mirrors the systemic pressure of a truth trying to emerge from the field.

The R.O.O.T.S. Symptom Map

To integrate this into your professional practice, use this framework to map a client‚Äôs physical symptom back to its systemic origin:

  • R - Reveal: When did the symptom start? What was happening in the system at that time? (e.g., "It started when my daughter turned 12‚Äîthe same age my mother lost her father.")
  • O - Observe: Who in the family had a similar fate? Who was excluded, forgotten, or shamed?
  • O - Order: Restore the hierarchy. Is the client "parenting" their parent? Are they standing in the place of a dead sibling?
  • T - Transmute: Use the "Deep Bow" and healing sentences to return the burden to the source with love.
  • S - Settle: Allow the body to rest in the new image. Integration requires silence and time.

Healing Sentences for Release

Healing sentences are the "medicine" of the constellation. They must be spoken slowly, allowing the body to absorb the vibration of the words. For ancestral loyalties, the goal is to separate love from suffering.

To a suffering parent: "I see your pain, and I honor it. But I leave it with you, with respect. You are the big one, I am the small one. I can be healthy and still love you."

To an excluded ancestor: "You are one of us. You belong. I give you a place in my heart. Look kindly on me as I live my life differently than yours."

The "I Follow You" Release: "I followed you for a long time out of love. Now, I stay a little longer out of love. I will make something good out of my life in your memory."
