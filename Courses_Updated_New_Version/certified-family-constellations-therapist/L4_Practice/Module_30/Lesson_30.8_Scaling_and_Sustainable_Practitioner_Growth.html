Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Why" phase of the discovery call?

To move the client from surface-level complaints to the emotional and systemic impact of their problem, helping them realize that "knowing" the problem hasn't solved it.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I've tried therapy and it didn't work"?

Validate them. Explain that therapy works with the conscious mind, but Family Constellations work with the "Family Soul" and the subconscious, which is why it often succeeds where talk therapy plateaus.

3. Why is "Packaging" better than "Per-Session" billing?

Packages ensure client commitment to the integration process, provide you with predictable income, and position you as a specialist focused on a result rather than a commodity focused on time.

4. True or False: You should explain the history of Bert Hellinger during a discovery call.

False. The discovery call should focus on the client's pain and your solution. Save the history and theory for the first session or your website.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Authority is Kindness: Leading the call and stating your price clearly is an act of service that makes the client feel safe.
  • Sell the Destination: Clients pay for the "unburdening," not the "constellation process."
  • Handle Objections Systemically: Look for the fear behind the "no" and address it with compassion.
  • Financial Sustainability: Aim for a mix of high-value packages and accessible workshops to build a $5k-$10k monthly practice.
