    Check Your Understanding

    1. Why is it legally important to state "I am a Facilitator, not a Therapist" during the enrollment call?

    It establishes your Scope of Practice and prevents "holding out" (misrepresenting your credentials), which is a primary legal requirement to avoid practicing medicine/psychology without a license.

    2. What is the "Systemic" reason for encouraging a 3-session package over a single session?

    Systemic movements often require an "integration period." A single session can be "too fast" for the soul. A package creates a container of safety and commitment, allowing the client to fully honor the ancestors' movements.

    3. If a client says "I can't afford it," what is the most professional first response?

    Acknowledge the investment, then ask: "I understand. Is it that the total is out of reach right now, or would a payment plan make this accessible for you?" This maintains the value of your work while offering a bridge.

    4. When should the Informed Consent and Liability Waiver be signed?

    After the discovery call is successful but BEFORE the first official session begins. Most practitioners include it as part of the "Welcome Pack" or onboarding email.

    Key Takeaways for Your Practice

    • Professionalism is Protection: Clear pricing and legal disclosures protect you from liability and protect the client from confusion.
    • Lead the Call: You are the facilitator of the system. If you can't lead a 30-minute phone call, the client won't trust you to lead their family soul.
    • Value over Hours: Charge based on the systemic shift you facilitate, not just the minutes you spend on Zoom.
    • Boundaries Start Early: The enrollment process is the first "Constellation" you do with the client. Set the orders of helping from the very first minute.
