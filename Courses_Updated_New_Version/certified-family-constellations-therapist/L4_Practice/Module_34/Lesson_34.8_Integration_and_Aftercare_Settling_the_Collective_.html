The Prospect Profile

Before you pick up the phone or open Zoom, you must understand who you are speaking to. In the world of systemic work, our "ideal" group participant is often someone who has reached a plateau in 1:1 therapy or coaching.

Sarah's Insight

When someone like Linda says they are "private," they are actually expressing a systemic fear of being excluded if they reveal their family "secrets." Your job is to show them that the group is actually a safe container for inclusion.

The 30-Minute Enrollment Script

This isn't a "sales" call; it's a resonance check. You are determining if Linda is ready for the field, and she is determining if she trusts you to hold the space.

Phase 1: Deep Listening 0-10 Minutes
YOU:
"Linda, I'm so glad we're connecting. I saw you reached out about the 'Healing the Mother Line' workshop. Tell me, what was happening in your life the moment you decided to click that link?"
LINDA:
"I just had another blow-up with my daughter. It‚Äôs the same pattern my mother had with me. I'm tired of carrying it."
Phase 2: The Systemic Pivot 10-20 Minutes
YOU:
"It sounds like you've done a lot of work to understand this intellectually. In a Constellation group, we move past the 'story' and look at the energetic knot. Have you ever experienced a group field where others represent your family members?"
LINDA:
"No, and honestly, it sounds a little intimidating."
Phase 3: The Invitation 20-30 Minutes
YOU:
"That's a very normal feeling. The beauty of the group is that you aren't just doing your own work; by witnessing others, you often find the missing piece of your own puzzle. Based on what you've told me about your daughter, I think this workshop is exactly the right container for you. Would you like to hear how the day is structured and the investment?"

Handling Common Group Objections

In group programs, objections usually fall into three categories: Privacy, Participation, and Price.

Practice Your Responses

1. Objection: "I'm worried about my privacy. What if I know someone in the group?"

"I completely understand. Confidentiality is the first 'Order' of our group. We set a sacred contract at the start that what happens in the field stays in the field. Also, the work is 'content-minimal'‚Äîyou don't have to share the details of your story for the constellation to be effective."

2. Objection: "What if I don't get 'picked' to do my own constellation?"

"In systemic work, there are no 'bystanders.' Every person in the room is chosen by the field for a reason. Whether you are an observer, a representative, or the 'seeker,' the healing movements affect everyone. Most clients find that representing someone else's 'Mother' gives them the exact breakthrough they needed for their own."

3. Objection: "I've never done anything like this. I'm afraid I won't 'feel' anything."

"You don't need to be 'intuitive' or 'spiritual' for this to work. We simply follow the physical sensations in the body. If you can feel your feet on the floor, you can participate in a constellation. I will guide you every step of the way."
Sarah's Insight

Always validate the fear before providing the solution. Use phrases like, "I hear your concern about privacy," or "That's a very common question." This keeps the client's nervous system regulated, which is essential for them to say 'Yes' to the work.

Case Study: Ellen's Transition

From Nurse Practitioner to Group Facilitator

Ellen (54) was a nurse practitioner who felt burnt out by the medical system. She transitioned into Family Constellations but struggled with her 1:1 income. She decided to launch a monthly "Systemic Wellness Circle."

The Strategy: She priced her 1-day workshop at $225. She needed 8 people to make it feel "energetically full."

The Outcome: Her first workshop had 10 participants ($2,250 for one day of work). Three of those participants then signed up for her $1,200 private coaching package. By leading with a group, she established herself as the authority and "warmed up" her high-end clients.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Resonance Over Selling: Use the enrollment call to check for systemic readiness, not just to "close" a deal.
  • Normalize the Fear: Address privacy and group vulnerability as a natural part of the systemic process.
  • Anchor Your Value: Price your workshops based on the transformation (the resolution of generational trauma), not the hours spent.
  • Start Small, Scale Fast: Use evening circles to fill your day-long workshops, and workshops to fill your premium group programs.
