The Single Session Trap

Many new facilitators fall into the "Constellation-as-an-Event" trap. They sell one 90-minute session for $150 or $250. While this provides immediate relief, it often fails to address the long-term integration required for permanent systemic change. From a business perspective, it forces the practitioner into a "hamster wheel" of constant client acquisition.

A 2022 survey of wellness practitioners found that those who sold 3-month packages reported 42% higher client satisfaction scores and 3x more predictable monthly revenue than those selling single sessions. Why? Because systemic resolution is a process, not a singular moment in time.

Coach Tip

üí° Mindset Shift: You are not selling a 90-minute constellation. You are selling the resolution of a 30-year-old pattern of scarcity, or the restoration of a relationship with a parent. Price the outcome, not the clock.

Designing R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ Containers

To provide a premium experience, your packages should mirror the natural progression of the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢. A typical 3-month transformation (The "Systemic Alignment Journey") might look like this:

Phase 1: Reveal & Observe (Month 1)

The focus is on the intake, genogram construction, and the first major constellation. We are identifying the exclusions and entanglements that have been "running the show" from the shadows.

Phase 2: Order & Transmute (Month 2)

Here, we move into the heavy lifting of the Orders of Love. We utilize healing sentences and somatic transmutation rituals to return burdens to the ancestors. This often involves a second, deeper constellation or 1-on-1 intensive work.

Phase 3: Settle the Resolution (Month 3)

This is where most single-session practitioners fail. In a package, you dedicate the final month to post-session integration protocols, ensuring the client doesn't "snap back" into old loyalties when they return to their family environment.

Case Study: Sarah, 48, Former Special Education Teacher

Challenge: Sarah was charging $175 per constellation. She was exhausted and only making $2,800/month after expenses. She felt like a "commodity."

Intervention: We restructured her offer into a 4-month "Ancestral Wealth & Worth" program for $2,400. This included 4 constellations, 2 integration calls, and a custom recorded meditation for the "Settle" phase.

Outcome: Sarah enrolled 3 clients in the first month. She worked less, earned more ($7,200), and her clients reported profound shifts in their businesses that they attributed to the sustained support.

Value-Based vs. Hourly Pricing

As a Certified Family Constellations Therapist‚Ñ¢, you are a specialist. When you price by the hour, you are essentially telling the client's subconscious that your time is the most valuable thing. In the Field, time is non-linear.

Value-based pricing considers the Cost of Inaction (COI). If a client doesn't resolve their systemic entanglement with money, they may lose $10,000s over their lifetime. If they don't resolve a pattern of toxic relationships, the emotional cost is immeasurable. Your package price should reflect a fraction of the value of the freedom they gain.

Coach Tip

üí° The "Rule of Three": Always offer three tiers. Tier 1: The Core Resolution (Basic). Tier 2: The Transformation Journey (Recommended). Tier 3: The VIP Legacy Intensive (Premium). 70% of clients will choose the middle option.

The Hybrid Workshop Bundle

One of the most effective ways to "Package Transformation" is to combine the O (Observe) and S (Settle) phases through group workshops. Individual sessions are intimate and deep, but the "Knowing Field" is often most palpable in a group setting.

The "Field Mastery" Bundle:

  • 3 Private Individual Constellations (R & T phases).
  • Unlimited access to your monthly Group Workshops as a "Representative" (O phase).
  • 1 "Resolution Anchor" session (S phase).

This model allows clients to see their patterns mirrored in others, which accelerates the "de-identification" process necessary for healing.

Post-Resolution Maintenance

Once a client has completed the "Settle" (S) phase, they often desire ongoing alignment. Systemic work is like a garden; once the large boulders (major entanglements) are removed, the soil still needs tending.

Maintenance programs (e.g., "The Quarterly Alignment") provide a "check-in" with the Field every 90 days. This prevents new entanglements from forming and keeps the client in a state of precedence and belonging. It also provides you with a source of "Recurring Revenue," which is the bedrock of a stable business.

Coach Tip

üí° Timing the Offer: Offer the maintenance program in the final session of their transformation package. Say: "We have cleared the main path. To keep this garden blooming, most of my clients find a quarterly check-in keeps them aligned. Would you like to set that up today?"

The Psychology of High-Ticket Commitment

In systemic work, the Balance of Giving and Taking (The Third Order of Love) is paramount. If a client pays a very low fee for a life-changing constellation, they may feel a subconscious "debt" to the practitioner, which can actually hinder their resolution.

When a client invests significantly ($2,000+), they are making a "Sacred Contract" with themselves and their system. This "skin in the game" signals to the Field that they are ready for the weight of the resolution. High-ticket packages often result in better outcomes because the client is more likely to do the integration work and respect the "Restorative Image."

Coach Tip

üí° Handling Objections: When a client says "That's expensive," they are usually comparing it to a massage or a therapy co-pay. Reframe it: "This is an investment in ending a multi-generational cycle of [Problem]. What is it worth to ensure your children don't carry this burden?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Settle" phase often missed in single-session models?

In a single session, the practitioner often says goodbye as soon as the constellation ends. Without a structured container, there is no follow-up to ensure the client is successfully internalizing the new systemic order and resisting the "pull" of old entanglements.

2. What is the primary benefit of a "Hybrid" bundle?

It allows the client to experience the Field from two perspectives: the deep personal work of individual sessions and the broad, observational learning of being a representative in group workshops (The "O" in R.O.O.T.S.).

3. How does high-ticket pricing relate to the Third Order of Love?

It maintains the Balance of Giving and Taking. A significant investment prevents the client from feeling "indebted" to the therapist and ensures they value the resolution enough to sustain it.

4. What is the "Rule of Three" in pricing?

Offering three tiers (Basic, Recommended, VIP). This provides a frame of reference for the client and typically guides them toward the middle "Transformation" package.
