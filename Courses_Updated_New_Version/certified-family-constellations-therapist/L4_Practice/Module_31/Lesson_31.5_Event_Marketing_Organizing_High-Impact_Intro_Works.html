The 'Introductory Evening' Blueprint

The "Intro Evening" is your most effective tool for Revealing (R) the power of systemic work to a cold audience. For many 40-55 year old women, the concept of "ancestral trauma" can feel abstract until they see it in motion. Your goal is to move them from skepticism to somatic resonance.

A successful blueprint follows a specific energetic arc:

Phase Duration Focus R.O.O.T.S. Connection
The Opening Circle 20 Mins Grounding, safety, and the "Orders of Love" overview. Establish the Container
The Educational "Hook" 15 Mins Explaining systemic entanglements via a common niche issue. Reveal (R)
The Mini-Constellation 45 Mins One full piece of work for a pre-selected "Seeker." Observe (O)
Group Exercise 20 Mins Small-group spatial exercises for all participants. Direct Experience
The Integration 20 Mins Closing, Q&A, and the "Invitation to Deeper Work." Settle (S)
Coach Tip

Don't try to explain the "science" of the Field for 60 minutes. People come for a shift, not a lecture. Keep your intro under 15 minutes and get into the movements as quickly as possible. The Field is your best salesperson.

Pricing Strategies for a Balanced Field

In Family Constellations, we must honor the Third Order of Love: The Balance of Giving and Taking. If you charge too little, the work is devalued; if you charge too much for an intro, the barrier to entry is too high.

The industry standard for high-impact workshops utilizes a tiered pricing model:

  • Representatives (Observers): Usually $25‚Äì$50. These participants provide the "human hardware" for the constellations. They receive the healing benefits of being in the Field without the vulnerability of having their own issue worked.
  • Seekers (Issue Holders): Usually $150‚Äì$300. These participants have a guaranteed spot to have their specific entanglement addressed.

Income Example: A 3-hour evening workshop with 15 Representatives ($35 each) and 2 Seekers ($175 each) generates $875. Beyond the immediate revenue, this event serves as a funnel for 1-on-1 packages (typically $1,500+).

Using "Observe the Field" as a Conversion Tool

In our R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, Observe the Field (O) is the stage where the client witnesses the hidden dynamics. In a workshop setting, this is the most powerful "sales" moment. When a participant watches a representative step into the role of a "Grandmother they never met" and speak with eerie accuracy, their skepticism evaporates.

To maximize conversion during the workshop:

  • Highlight the Universality: After a constellation, point out how the "Exclusion" or "Entanglement" seen in the circle is something many of us carry.
  • The Somatic Check-in: Ask the observers, "Where did you feel that in your body?" This anchors the experience in their own nervous system.
  • The Soft Close: At the end of the night, say: "The Field has revealed much tonight. For some of you, this was enough. For others, you realize your roots need deeper tending. I have three spots opening for private work this month."

Ancestral Healing Retreats: The Multi-Day Intensive

Once you have mastered the 3-hour intro, the Ancestral Healing Retreat is your high-ticket backend offer. These typically range from 2 to 4 days and are priced between $1,200 and $3,500 per person.

Logistics Checklist:

  • Venue: Must be private and "energetically quiet." Avoid busy hotels; prefer retreat centers or large Airbnbs with a "circle room."
  • The Field Load: Do not schedule more than 3-4 full constellations per day. The Field becomes "heavy," and participants need time to Settle (S).
  • Marketing: These are rarely sold to "cold" audiences. They are marketed to your email list and past workshop attendees who have already experienced the "O" (Observe) stage.
Coach Tip

For retreats, always include a "Somatic Integration" hour each afternoon (walking in nature, journaling, or silent rest). This prevents "healing fatigue" and allows the new systemic order to take root.

The 'Settle the Resolution' Follow-Up Sequence

The work doesn't end when the circle closes. In fact, the Settle (S) phase is where the long-term neural and systemic shifts happen. Your follow-up sequence should mirror this.

The 3-Part Follow-Up Protocol:

  1. The 24-Hour "Golden Window" Email: A brief message thanking them for their presence. Include a grounding exercise (like the "Deep Bow" learned in Module 4) to help them stay centered.
  2. The 72-Hour "Integration" Email: Provide a piece of educational content that explains the "Post-Constellation Fatigue" or "The Power of Silence." Remind them not to talk about the work too much yet.
  3. The Day 7 "Next Step" Email: A direct invitation to a discovery call or your next event. By day 7, the initial "Field High" has worn off, and they are ready to commit to the actual labor of change.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Representative" price kept low ($25-$50) compared to the "Seeker" price?

It lowers the barrier to entry for new clients to experience the Field (Observe) while ensuring the practitioner is compensated for the high-intensity work of facilitating a personal constellation for the Seeker.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Educational Hook" in the workshop blueprint?

To "Reveal" (R) how systemic entanglements relate to the specific problems the audience is facing (e.g., money blocks, relationship patterns), moving the concept from "woo-woo" to practical.

3. According to the ROOTS Method‚Ñ¢, what should you avoid doing immediately after a constellation?

Avoid over-analyzing or "talking it to death." The "Settle" (S) phase requires silence and somatic integration for the new image to take hold in the soul.

Key Takeaways for the Facilitator

  • The Field Sells Itself: Your primary job in an intro workshop is to get people to Observe (O) the Field in action as quickly as possible.
  • Balance the Exchange: Use tiered pricing to honor the "Giving and Taking" between you, the Seekers, and the Representatives.
  • Structure is Safety: A 2-3 hour blueprint prevents you from over-working and keeps the energy of the group contained and professional.
  • The Money is in the Follow-up: The "Settle" (S) sequence converts "one-time attendees" into "lifetime clients" by supporting their integration.
