The Systemic Intake Landing Page (The 'R' Process)

Most practitioners use a simple "Contact Me" form. To stand out, you should create a Systemic Intake Landing Page. This page begins the Reveal (R) process before the client even pays you.

By asking 3-5 high-level systemic questions on your intake form, you demonstrate expertise and immediately shift the client's perspective from "What is wrong with me?" to "What is happening in my system?"

Sample Intake Questions:

  • "Is there a known history of early death, exclusion, or significant loss in your parents' or grandparents' generations?"
  • "Do you feel you are carrying a burden that belongs to someone else?"
  • "What is the 'repeating pattern' you've noticed in your life that seems to resist traditional therapy?"
Coach Tip: Automation as Ease

Use tools like Typeform or Jotform to make this intake look elegant. When a client finishes, redirect them to a "Thank You" page with a short 2-minute video of you explaining that their journey of Observation (O) has already begun.

Email Marketing: Settling the Field

Email is not about "selling"‚Äîit's about Settling (S). In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, settling is the phase where the new resolution takes root in the soul. Your emails should provide a "restorative rhythm" for your community.

Statistics show that for every $1 spent on email marketing, the average return is $36. For a systemic coach, this is where you nurture the "Virtual Knowing Field."

The 3-Part Email Strategy

  1. The Insight Email: A short systemic reflection (e.g., "The Power of the Bow") that offers immediate value.
  2. The Case Study Email: Sharing a (de-identified) story of a resolution, helping the reader see themselves in the "Restorative Image."
  3. The Invitation: A clear call to action for a discovery call or an upcoming workshop.
