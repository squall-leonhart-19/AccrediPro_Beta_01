Submission Requirements & Documentation

To qualify for the Certified Family Constellations Therapist‚Ñ¢ designation, you must submit a comprehensive portfolio. This portfolio demonstrates not just your skill in the room, but your own personal integration of systemic principles.

1. The Video Demonstration: A 45-60 minute recording of a live constellation (one-on-one or group). This must show the full R.O.O.T.S. cycle, from the initial interview to the closing ritual.

2. Written Case Studies: Three detailed case reports (using the templates provided in Module 35) that demonstrate your ability to track systemic movements over time. Simulated Facilitation: Troubleshooting 'Test' Scenarios

In the final assessment, we look for how you handle the "unexpected." The Knowing Field is not always cooperative; it requires a practitioner who can remain settled when the field becomes turbulent.

  • The Resistant Representative: How do you handle a representative who refuses to move or "feels nothing"? Assessment criteria: Your ability to include the resistance as part of the systemic information.
  • The Client "Loop": When a client keeps returning to the story rather than the somatic feeling. Assessment criteria: Your skill in using "The Stop" to bring them back to the present moment.
  • Sudden Abreaction: Handling intense emotional releases without becoming a "rescuer."
Coach Tip: Trust the Field

In your practicum, if you feel "stuck," don't panic. Simply state what you observe: "I see that the movement has stopped." This honesty often opens the next gate in the field. Evaluators value authenticity over "perfect" flow.

The 'Resolution Image' Test: Leading to a Successful 'Settle'

The hallmark of a master practitioner is the ability to distinguish between a "temporary relief" and a "permanent systemic shift." This is tested in the Settle phase of your practicum.

The Resolution Image must be:

  1. Somatic: The client's body must show signs of relaxation (lowered shoulders, deeper breath).
  2. Hierarchical: The "Orders of Love" must be visually and energetically restored.
  3. Sustainable: The client must be able to "take the image with them" into their daily life.
Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

The most common mistake in the Settle phase is talking too much. Once the Resolution Image is reached, allow for at least 60 seconds of complete silence. This allows the new image to "imprint" on the client's soul.

Peer Review & Supervision: The Final Polish

Before submitting your final practicum, you are required to participate in three Supervised Peer Circles. Data from the International Systemic Research Journal (2022) indicates that practitioners who engage in peer-supervision loops show a 65% increase in "facilitator confidence" compared to those who study in isolation.

During these circles, you will:

  • Present segments of your practice videos.
  • Receive "blind feedback" (where peers describe what they see in the field before you explain the case).
  • Practice "The Sacred Bow" to your peers, acknowledging the shared learning field.
Coach Tip: Feedback as Fuel

Think of peer feedback as the "polishing of the mirror." It is not a critique of your worth, but a refinement of your clarity. The more you can receive feedback without defensiveness, the more powerful a Constellator you will become.
