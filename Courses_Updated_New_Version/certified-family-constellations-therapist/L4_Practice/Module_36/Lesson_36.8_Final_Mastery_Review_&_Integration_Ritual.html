Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

To establish rapport and "Safety in the Field." You want the client to feel seen and to understand the "container" of the call.

2. Why is "Package Pricing" recommended over "Hourly Pricing" for Family Constellations?

Systemic work requires integration time. Packages ensure the client commits to the full ROOTSâ„¢ process, leading to better clinical outcomes and more stable practitioner income.

3. How should a practitioner respond when a client says, "I've tried everything else and nothing worked"?

Acknowledge their effort, then pivot to the systemic perspective: "Most therapies work with the individual; we are going to work with the whole system. That is why this is the missing piece."

4. What is the "Silence Rule" in pricing?

After stating the investment amount, stay silent. This allows the client to process the value and prevents the practitioner from "talking them out of it" due to their own money blocks.
