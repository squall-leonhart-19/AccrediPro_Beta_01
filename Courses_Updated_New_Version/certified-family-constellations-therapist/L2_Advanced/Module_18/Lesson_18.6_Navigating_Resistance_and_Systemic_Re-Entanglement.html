The 'Pull of the Old': Systemic Gravity

Even after a client witnesses a restorative image, they may experience what Bert Hellinger described as "blind loyalty." This is an unconscious urge to return to suffering because suffering feels like "belonging." In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we call this Systemic Gravity.

Statistics from a 2022 longitudinal study of systemic practitioners suggest that up to 65% of clients experience a "crisis of belonging" within 14 days of a major systemic shift. This is not a failure of the work; it is the system attempting to maintain homeostasis.

Advanced Transmutation of 'Secondary Gains'

Sometimes, a client remains tethered to a burden because that burden provides a hidden benefit. This is the Secondary Gain. In systemic work, common gains include:

  • The Right to Complain: Suffering provides a ticket to community sympathy.
  • Innocence: Staying "small" or "victim-like" ensures the client never has to face the responsibility of their own power.
  • Moral Superiority: Carrying the burden of a parent can make a child feel "better" than the parent.

Sensing the Wall: Handling Dissociation

When a client "checks out" or becomes numb during the integration phase, they have hit "The Wall." This is often a protective mechanism against a truth that feels too big for the nervous system to handle.

As a practitioner, you must sense this somatically. If the field feels "flat" or the client's eyes glaze over, stop the narrative work. Shift to Somatic Grounding:

  1. The Weighted Gaze: Ask the client to name three physical objects in the room.
  2. The Ancestral Floor: Ask them to feel the weight of their feet and imagine their ancestors' hands supporting their soles.
  3. The Breath of the System: Slow the pace. In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we prioritize safety over speed.
Coach Tip: The Distance Dial

üí° If a client is dissociating, ask them: "On a scale of 1 to 10, how far away do you feel right now?" If they say 8, ask them what they need to move to a 6. Usually, it's a physical adjustment or a moment of silence.

Re-Ordering in Toxic Environments

The greatest challenge for many clients is returning home to a family that hasn't done "the work." Systemic re-entanglement is common when the family system tries to force the client back into their old role (the "scapegoat," the "caretaker," etc.).

The Strategy of the "Invisible Order": Teach your client that they do not need to explain the constellation to their family. They simply need to hold the internal image. When a mother criticizes, the client internally says: "I see you as the big one, and I am the small one. I leave your criticism with you."

Healing Sentences as Shields

In the final synthesis, Healing Sentences act as a "linguistic immune system." They prevent re-entanglement by reinforcing the new boundaries in daily life.

  • For the Caretaker: "I love you, and I leave your fate with you."
  • For the Scapegoat: "I am just like you, and I am also different. I take my own path now."
  • For the Parentified Child: "You are the big one, I am the small one. You give, I take."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary cause of the "Pull of the Old" according to systemic theory?

The primary cause is "blind loyalty" or "systemic gravity"‚Äîthe unconscious urge to suffer in order to maintain a sense of belonging within a dysfunctional family system.

2. How does a "Secondary Gain" differ from a systemic entanglement?

An entanglement is the "what" (being caught in someone else's fate), while a secondary gain is the "why" (the hidden benefit, like innocence or moral superiority, that makes the client unconsciously want to stay entangled).

3. What somatic sign indicates a client has hit "The Wall"?

Signs include glazed eyes, a "flat" feeling in the field, sudden numbness, or a complete inability to feel the representatives in the field (dissociation).

4. Why is the "72-Hour Rule" critical for integration?

It prevents the "leakage" of the restorative energy. Discussing the work prematurely allows the rational mind and the old system's influence to dismantle the soul-level shift achieved in the constellation.
Coach Tip: The Professional Pivot

Key Takeaways

  • Systemic Gravity is a natural part of integration; it is not a sign of failure.
  • Secondary gains must be transmuted into "Honoring through Thriving" to ensure long-term change.
  • Dissociation requires immediate somatic grounding rather than continued narrative exploration.
  • Healing sentences serve as a protective "shield" when clients return to un-ordered family systems.
  • Silence and the 72-hour rule are the practitioner's best tools for protecting the restorative image.
