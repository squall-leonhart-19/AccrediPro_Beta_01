Phased Intervention Plan

For a client like Elena, we do not jump into a full-scale "Grandfather Constellation" in session one. We use a Phased Synthesis Approach.

Phase 1: Stabilization & Resource Building (Weeks 1-3)

Focus on "The Mother." Before we can address the grandfather's bankruptcy, Elena must find her "place" as the child. We use small-figure constellations to help her "take" life from her mother, regardless of its brevity.

Phase 2: The Core Constellation (Weeks 4-6)

Address the Identification. Here, we work with the refugee grandmother. Elena says: "Dear Grandma, I see your heavy burden. I carried it for a while out of love, but now I leave it with you."

Phase 3: Integration & Future Pacing (Weeks 7-10)

The "Career Pivot." We set up the "Business" as a representative. Elena must look at the business through the eyes of her successful ancestors, not through the eyes of the bankrupt grandfather.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Elena's fibromyalgia considered a "Systemic Primary" in this case reasoning?

Because the physical pain acts as a somatic "anchor" that keeps her energetically tied to the "heavy" unresolved trauma of her maternal ancestors (the refugee grandmother), preventing her from moving forward into her own life and success.

2. What is the "Orders of Love" violation when Elena tries to "fix" her father's shame?

It is a violation of Hierarchy. As the child, she is attempting to "carry" something for the parent, which places her "above" the father in the systemic order, leading to exhaustion and "paralysis" in her own life.

3. When should a Constellations practitioner immediately refer Elena to a medical professional?

If she presents with red flags such as suicidal ideation, psychotic symptoms, or if her fibromyalgia pain suddenly changes in a way that suggests a new, acute medical pathology (neurological deficits, etc.).

4. In the Phased Protocol, why do we work with the Mother before the Business?

Because "Success has the face of the Mother." Systemically, if a client cannot fully accept and "take" life from their primary source (the mother), they will struggle to "take" and sustain success in their professional endeavors.
