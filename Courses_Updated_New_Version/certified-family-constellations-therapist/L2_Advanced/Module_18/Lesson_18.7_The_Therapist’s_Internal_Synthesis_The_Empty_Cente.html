Defining the Empty Center: Mastery of Non-Intention

Bert Hellinger often spoke of the facilitator needing to be "without intention, without fear, and without memory." In Level 2 practice, we call this the Empty Center. It is a state of active presence where the therapist‚Äôs personality steps back so the Systemic Soul can step forward.

When you are in your Empty Center, you are not trying to "fix" the client. You are not searching for a specific outcome. You are simply observing what is. The Systemic Witness vs. The Observing Ego

In clinical psychology, the "Observing Ego" is the part of the self that watches one's own thoughts and feelings. In Systemic Constellations, we go further to the Systemic Witness. While the Observing Ego is still centered in the "I," the Systemic Witness is centered in the "Field."

The Systemic Witness perceives the client not as an isolated individual, but as the "front-line" of a vast ancestral line. When you shift from Ego to Witness, you stop looking at the client‚Äôs face and start looking at the space behind them. This is where the true power of the Knowing Field resides.
