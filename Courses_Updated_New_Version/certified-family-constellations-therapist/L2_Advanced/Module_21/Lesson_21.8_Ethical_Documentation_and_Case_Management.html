3. Differential Considerations

Sarah's Clinical Insight

Always ask: "To whom does this pain belong?" If the client describes the pain as "heavy" or "old," it is likely systemic. If it is "sharp" and "recent," look at personal trauma or biological factors first.

4. Referral Triggers (Scope of Practice)

Because Elena is 52 and has chronic pain, we must maintain clinical boundaries. A 2022 study in the Journal of Systemic Therapies noted that 15% of "somatic loyalties" have underlying medical pathologies that require concurrent treatment.

Red Flags for Elena:

  • Sudden change in migraine pattern: Requires a Neurologist to rule out vascular issues.
  • Pelvic Pain: Must be cleared by a Gynecologist to rule out endometriosis or fibroids before systemic work continues.
  • Suicidal Ideation: If the "identification with the dead" becomes an active desire to die, refer immediately to a crisis specialist.

5. Phased Protocol Plan

For a client like Elena, we do not jump into a "Grand Constellation" immediately. We use a Phased Approach to ensure integration.

Phase 1: Stabilization & Resource Building (Weeks 1-3)

Focus on "Taking the Mother." We use small, floor-marker exercises to help Elena visualize her mother behind her, and her grandmother behind her mother. We do not "work" the trauma yet; we simply "look at" the line of life.

Phase 2: The Core Resolution (Weeks 4-8)

The "Orphan's Acknowledgement." We set up a constellation where Elena finally meets the "Excluded Parents" of her grandmother. Key Phrase: "I see you now. You were lost, but you are part of us. I bow to your fate, and I choose to do something good with the life that came through you."
