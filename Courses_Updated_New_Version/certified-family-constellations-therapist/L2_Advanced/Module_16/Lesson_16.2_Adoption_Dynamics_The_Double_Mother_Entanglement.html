Welcome, Practitioner

Adoption is often viewed through a lens of "saving" or "choosing" a child. However, in the Knowing Field, adoption represents a profound systemic rupture. As a facilitator, your role is not to judge the circumstances of the adoption, but to restore the Order of Love by acknowledging the biological parents as the primary source of life. Today, we dive into the "Double Mother" entanglement‚Äîa complex dynamic where the soul of the child is torn between the mother who gave life and the mother who gave care.

The Hierarchy of Source vs. Nurture

In Systemic Constellations, we distinguish between the Biological Parents (the Source) and the Social/Adoptive Parents (the Nurturers). A common mistake in therapeutic settings is to prioritize the adoptive parents because they provided the safety, education, and love. However, the Orders of Love are governed by the flow of life, not the quality of care.

The biological parents are always first. They are the ones through whom the life force reached the child. Without them, the child does not exist. The adoptive parents come second; they take over a task that the biological parents could not fulfill. When this order is ignored‚Äîwhen the biological parents are "erased" or "replaced"‚Äîthe child often experiences deep-seated systemic anxiety, feeling like a "ghost" in their own life.

Coach Tip: The Practitioner's Stance

When working with adoptive parents, they may feel threatened by the idea that the biological parents are "first." Your job is to gently explain that this is a biological truth, not a legal or emotional one. Acknowledging the bio-parents actually strengthens the bond with the adoptive parents by removing the child's unconscious need to "search" for the missing pieces.

The 'Double Mother' Concept

The "Double Mother" entanglement occurs when a child feels an unconscious loyalty to the biological mother (the one who "left") while simultaneously feeling a conscious loyalty to the adoptive mother (the one who "stayed"). This creates a systemic "split" in the psyche.

Case Study: Sarah, 48

Former ER Nurse & Aspiring Wellness Coach

Presenting Symptoms: Sarah, a high-achieving woman in her late 40s, sought help for a recurring pattern of "emotional withdrawal" in her marriage. Despite a loving husband, she felt a physical barrier whenever he tried to get close. She was adopted at 4 days old and had a "wonderful" relationship with her adoptive parents.

The Reveal (R): During the constellation, we placed a representative for Sarah and her biological mother. Sarah‚Äôs representative immediately looked at the floor and began to tremble. She could not look at her husband (representative) because her soul was focused on the "missing" mother.

The Entanglement: Sarah was unconsciously following her biological mother into the "shame" of the abandonment. She felt that if she fully accepted love from her husband, she would be "betraying" the mother who had to give her up. This is the Double Mother trap: "If I am happy here, I am leaving you behind there."

The Intervention (T/S): We used the ritual of the Deep Bow toward the biological mother. Sarah said: "You are the right one for me to come from. I take my life from you, at the full price it cost you and me."

Outcome: Two months later, Sarah reported a "softening" in her chest. For the first time, she felt "solid" enough to let her husband in without the fear of disappearing.

R.O.O.T.S. Ritual Interventions

Using the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we navigate adoption dynamics through specific spatial and linguistic steps:

1. Reveal (R) the Hidden Loyalty

We look for the "interrupted reaching out." In the Knowing Field, we often see the adoptee representative frozen or facing away from the current family. This indicates the soul is still searching for the Source.

2. Observe (O) the Somatic Split

Watch for clients who have "half-body" tension. Often, the left side (maternal) feels heavy or numb. This is the physical manifestation of the excluded biological mother.

3. Order (O) the System

We place the biological parents behind the adoptive parents. This creates a "double layer" of support. The life flows from the bio-parents, through the adoptive parents, to the child.

Settling the Resolution: The Unified Image

The goal of the constellation is to move from a "split" image (Bio vs. Adoptive) to a "unified" image (Bio + Adoptive). In the final restorative image, we want the client to feel the weight of four parents behind them.

Healing Sentences for the Adoptee:

  • To the Biological Parents: "I take my life from you. It was enough. The rest I will make something of."
  • To the Adoptive Parents: "You took me in when my parents couldn't. I honor you for the care you gave. You are the right ones to raise me."
  • To the Self: "I have two sets of parents. One gave me life; the other gave me a home. I am the child of both."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why must biological parents be placed in the "first" position in a constellation?

According to the Order of Precedence, the ones who came first have priority. Biological parents are the "Source" of life. If they are not honored as the primary source, the flow of life to the child is systemically blocked, regardless of how "good" the adoptive parents are.

2. What is a common somatic marker of an adoptee's entanglement with a missing parent?

Common markers include the "interrupted reaching out" (frozen hands/arms), eyes fixed on the floor (looking for the "dead" or "lost"), and a sense of "not being fully present" or feeling like a "ghost."

3. How does the 'Double Mother' entanglement manifest in adult relationships?

It often manifests as an inability to form deep intimacy or a "barrier" to love. The individual unconsciously feels that being happy or loved in the present is a betrayal of the biological mother who suffered loss or abandonment.

4. What is the goal of the "Deep Bow" ritual in adoption constellations?

The Deep Bow is a physical movement of humility that acknowledges the "price" of life. It helps the client move from "judgment" or "victimhood" into "acceptance" of the life force exactly as it was given.

Key Takeaways

  • Source vs. Nurture: Biological parents are the source of life; adoptive parents are the providers of care. Both must be honored in their specific roles.
  • Systemic Anxiety: Ruptures in the biological line often manifest as chronic anxiety or a "missing" sense of self in the adoptee.
  • The Split Psyche: The Double Mother entanglement creates a loyalty conflict that can sabotage adult intimacy and success.
  • Resolution: Healing comes from a unified internal image where the bio-parents stand behind the adoptive parents, all supporting the child.
