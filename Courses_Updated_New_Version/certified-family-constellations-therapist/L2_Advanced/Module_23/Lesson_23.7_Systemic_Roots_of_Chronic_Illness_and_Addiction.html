The 'Symptom as a Messenger'

In the R.O.O.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we begin by Revealing the entanglement. When a client presents with a chronic illness‚Äîbe it autoimmune disease, chronic pain, or cancer‚Äîthe systemic lens asks: "Who is this illness looking at?"

Bert Hellinger famously observed that many illnesses are a form of "blind love." A child (even as an adult) may unconsciously say to an excluded or suffering ancestor, "I will suffer like you," or "I will follow you into death." The symptom becomes a physical bridge to the excluded person.

Addiction: The Movement toward the Father

While every case is unique, systemic work has identified a recurring pattern in addiction: the missing father. Addiction is often a desperate, unconscious movement to fill a vacuum left by an excluded or devalued father.

If a mother tells a child, "Your father is no good," or if the father is physically present but emotionally "excluded" by the mother‚Äôs family, the child cannot fully take the father's strength. The substance (alcohol, drugs, gambling) becomes a substitute for the father's presence. It provides a temporary, albeit destructive, sense of "fullness."

Practitioner Insight

When working with addiction, never criticize the substance. In the field, the substance is often the only thing keeping the client connected to life. Instead, focus on Ordering the System so the client can "take" their father exactly as he is, without the mother's filter.

Advanced Orders of Love in Health

Chronic illness often arises when the Second Order (Precedence) is violated. This usually manifests as Parentification‚Äîwhere the child attempts to "carry" the emotional or physical burden of the parent to keep the parent alive or functional.

To restore order, the practitioner must facilitate the Ritual of the Deep Bow. The client must acknowledge: "You are the big one, I am the small one. You carry your destiny, and I leave it with you." This is not an act of abandonment, but an act of profound respect for the parent's soul-path.

The 'Facing Death' Ritual

In cases of terminal illness or when a client is "stuck" in a movement toward a deceased loved one, we use the Facing Death Ritual. This is an advanced Transmute (T) technique.

We place a representative for "Death" at a distance. Often, the client is surprised to find that Death is not scary in the Field, but rather a quiet, neutral presence. The ritual involves:

  • Acknowledgment: "I see you. I know you are coming for me one day."
  • The Choice: The client is asked to turn their back to Death (with Death's permission) and look toward "Life" (represented by another person or a bright light).
  • The Sentence: "I stay a little longer, then I come."

Working with illness and addiction can trigger Helper‚Äôs Syndrome‚Äîthe practitioner‚Äôs unconscious need to "save" the client to validate their own worth. This is a violation of the Third Order (Balance).

The Golden Rule of Systemic Help: You can only help as much as the client's system allows. If you try to pull a client out of an illness that their soul is using to stay connected to a parent, you will fail, and both you and the client will be exhausted.

As a career changer (especially if you come from nursing or teaching), your "saving" muscle is overdeveloped. Practice the "Empty Center" technique before every health-related session to ensure you aren't carrying the client's fate.

Key Takeaways
