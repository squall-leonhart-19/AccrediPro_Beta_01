Eleanor, 52

Retired Teacher, Seattle, WA • Widowed, lives with her rescue Golden Retriever, "Daisy"

  • Chief Complaint: Persistent, debilitating fatigue (rating 8/10 daily), significant hair loss, brittle nails, poor wound healing, heightened anxiety, and chronic digestive issues (bloating, gas, irregular bowel movements).
  • Duration: Gradually worsening over 3-4 years. "I just feel like I'm falling apart. My doctor said it's probably just aging or stress, but I know something is wrong."
  • What She's Tried: Over-the-counter multivitamins, increased fiber, various probiotics recommended by health food store staff, and a "clean eating" diet focused on salads and lean protein, which exacerbated her digestive issues.
  • Relevant History: History of mild anemia in her 30s, recent blood work showed "low-normal" ferritin (35 ng/mL), slightly elevated homocysteine (14 μmol/L), and low Vitamin D (25 ng/mL). She also takes a daily antacid for occasional heartburn.
  • Her Goal: "I want my energy back, to feel vibrant again, and to understand why my body isn't working like it used to. I want to be active with Daisy again."
Coach Tip

Eleanor's case highlights the importance of looking beyond "normal" lab ranges. A ferritin of 35 ng/mL, while technically in range, is often insufficient for optimal hair growth and energy in women. Similarly, her elevated homocysteine suggests potential B vitamin deficiencies, even if standard B12/folate levels are "normal." This is where your deep dive knowledge becomes crucial.

3. Coaching Script: Navigating Complexity

This dialogue focuses on validating Eleanor's experience, gently challenging conventional advice, and introducing a more comprehensive approach. Practice this out loud, embodying empathy and confidence.

Reflection Prompt

As you read this script, consider how you would convey warmth and understanding to Eleanor. How would your tone of voice and pacing reflect your empathy for her frustration?

Speaker Dialogue
YOU: "Eleanor, thank you so much for sharing your story with me. I hear how frustrating and disheartening it's been to feel this way, especially when you've been told it's just 'aging' or 'stress.' I want you to know, what you're experiencing is incredibly real, and it's absolutely not your fault. Your body is sending you important signals, and the fact that you're here seeking deeper answers shows incredible strength."
ELEANOR: "It's such a relief to hear you say that. I've felt so dismissed, like I'm just complaining about getting older. My doctor just repeated that my labs were 'normal' and suggested meditation."
YOU: "That's a very common experience, Eleanor, and one I hear from many women in your age group. While meditation can be helpful, it doesn't address the underlying physiological imbalances that can contribute to these symptoms. The good news is, by looking closer at your unique biochemistry, we can often uncover the root causes that are being missed."
YOU: "Based on what you've shared, particularly your persistent fatigue, hair loss, and digestive issues, I'd like us to focus on three key areas: optimizing your nutrient status, supporting your digestive health, and understanding your stress resilience. Does that feel like a good starting point for you?"
ELEANOR: "Yes, absolutely. Nutrient status... that's something I haven't really focused on beyond a general multivitamin. And digestion has been a nightmare. This sounds much more comprehensive."
YOU: "Wonderful. My approach involves a deeper dive into your current and historical health data. This includes reviewing your recent lab work with a functional lens, and discussing your diet in detail, not just what you eat, but how your body is processing it. We'll identify specific areas where your body might need more targeted support, such as addressing potential iron deficiency for your fatigue and hair loss, or B vitamin support for homocysteine and energy. Does this sound like the thorough approach you're looking for?"
ELEANOR: "It sounds exactly like what I need. I'm ready to understand what's truly going on."
YOU: "Excellent. We'll start with a comprehensive dietary recall and a deeper questionnaire to gather more pieces of your health puzzle. This will allow us to create a truly personalized plan that works with your body to restore your energy and vitality. How does that sound for our next steps?"
Coach Tip

Notice how I validated Eleanor's feelings and then pivoted to explaining the "why" behind her symptoms and my approach. This builds trust and positions you as the expert who truly understands her complex situation. Never dismiss a client's prior experiences, but gently introduce a new perspective.

4. Practice Exercise: Your Turn

Your Coaching Practice

This is your opportunity to internalize this dialogue and make it your own. Repetition builds confidence!

  • Read Silently First: Go through the script again, paying attention to the flow and the underlying intention of each statement.
  • Speak Out Loud: Pretend Eleanor is sitting across from you. Deliver your lines with a warm, calm, and confident tone. Practice making eye contact (even with an imaginary client!).
  • Record Yourself: Use your phone or computer to record your dialogue. Listen back to your pacing, tone, and clarity. Are you speaking too fast? Is your voice conveying empathy? Are you using jargon unnecessarily?
  • Focus on Key Phrases: Pay special attention to phrases like, "It's absolutely not your fault," "looking closer at your unique biochemistry," and "root causes." These are powerful in shifting a client's perspective.
  • Practice Explaining the "Why": In your own words, practice explaining why low-normal ferritin or elevated homocysteine matters, even if a doctor says it's "normal." This is your unique value proposition.
Coach Tip

Don't be afraid to pause and breathe. A slight pause can convey thoughtfulness and allow the client to absorb what you've said. It also gives you a moment to collect your thoughts and respond intentionally.

5. Common Coaching Pitfalls for Advanced Cases

Even experienced coaches can stumble when dealing with complex client scenarios. Be mindful of these common mistakes:

  • Overwhelming with Information: While you have deep knowledge, resist the urge to explain every biomarker in detail in the first session. Focus on the most relevant insights and how they connect to the client's symptoms.
  • Dismissing Prior Experiences: Never invalidate a client's previous attempts or medical advice. Acknowledge their journey ("I understand your doctor said X, and that's a common perspective...") before introducing your functional approach.
  • Jumping to Solutions Too Quickly: Even with clear diagnostic insights, a phased approach is best. Focus on building rapport and establishing the "why" before diving into specific dietary changes.
  • Using Excessive Jargon: Translate complex terms like "homocysteine" or "functional ranges" into understandable language. Explain the concept, not just the word.
  • Failing to Set Expectations: Clearly communicate that complex issues take time to resolve. Manage expectations about the timeline for seeing results and the commitment required.

6. Markers of a Successful Coaching Session

How do you know if your advanced coaching session went well? Look for these indicators:

  • Client Feels Heard & Validated: They express relief or gratitude for being understood.
  • Client Expresses Hope & Empowerment: They feel a sense of optimism that their issues can be addressed, rather than resignation.
  • Clear Understanding of Next Steps: They know exactly what to expect in subsequent sessions and what actions they might need to take.
  • Increased Trust & Rapport: You've established yourself as a knowledgeable and empathetic guide.
  • Client Asks Clarifying Questions: This indicates engagement and a desire to understand, rather than passive acceptance.
  • Client is Engaged and Participatory: They actively contribute to the conversation and feel like a partner in their health journey.
Coach Tip

In my practice, I've found that clients often come in feeling defeated. Your ability to offer a fresh, hopeful perspective, grounded in science, is incredibly powerful. Remember, you're not just offering nutritional advice; you're offering renewed hope and a path forward.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it crucial to validate Eleanor's feelings about being dismissed by her doctor?

Validating Eleanor's feelings builds immediate trust and rapport. It shows empathy, acknowledges her frustration, and positions you as someone who truly understands her experience, differentiating your approach from prior dismissive encounters.

2. What is the benefit of discussing "nutrient status" as a key area of focus, rather than just "diet"?

"Nutrient status" implies a deeper, more comprehensive look at absorption, utilization, and specific deficiencies beyond just food intake. It broadens the scope to include advanced assessment results (like ferritin, homocysteine) and sets the stage for targeted interventions, which resonates with clients seeking root causes.

3. Eleanor's ferritin is "low-normal." How would you explain to her why this might still be problematic for her symptoms?

You would explain that while her ferritin is within the standard lab "normal" range, for optimal health, energy, and specific functions like hair growth, many individuals feel best when their ferritin is in a higher, more optimal range (e.g., above 70-100 ng/mL). You can liken it to a car's fuel tank: "normal" might mean it's not empty, but "optimal" means it's full enough to perform at its best.

4. What is one practical step you can recommend to a client like Eleanor to start addressing her digestive issues, even before a full plan is developed?

A simple, yet powerful initial recommendation is to focus on mindful eating: chewing food thoroughly (e.g., 20-30 times per bite), eating in a relaxed environment, and avoiding drinking large amounts of water with meals. This supports the initial stages of digestion and can significantly reduce bloating and gas, preparing the digestive system for more targeted interventions.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy is Paramount: Always start by validating your client's experience and frustrations, especially when they've felt dismissed by conventional approaches.
  • Translate Complexity: Your advanced knowledge of diagnostics and functional ranges is powerful, but it must be communicated in an accessible, empowering way, not as jargon.
  • Root Cause Focus: Frame your coaching around identifying and addressing the underlying imbalances, rather than just managing symptoms.
  • Set Clear Expectations: Guide your client through the process, outlining next steps and managing expectations for the timeline of results.
  • Practice Builds Confidence: Regularly practicing your coaching dialogue out loud will help you internalize your expertise and deliver it with natural authority.
