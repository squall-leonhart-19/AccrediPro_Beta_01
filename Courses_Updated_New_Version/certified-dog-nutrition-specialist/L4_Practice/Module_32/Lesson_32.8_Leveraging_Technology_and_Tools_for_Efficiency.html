The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is more than just a sales pitch; it's a diagnostic tool, a rapport-builder, and a powerful opportunity to establish your expertise and empathy. This 20-30 minute conversation is where you determine if a client is a good fit for your services and, equally important, if you are the right fit for them. A well-executed discovery call can lead to conversion rates of 50-70% for established practitioners, turning casual inquiries into committed clients (HubSpot, 2023).

Your goal isn't to convince, but to understand. When you genuinely listen and ask incisive questions, you empower the client to articulate their needs and envision a solution, with you as their guide.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Remember, the discovery call is a two-way street. You're assessing if you can truly help this dog and owner, and if they're ready to commit to the process. It's perfectly okay if it's not a fit; sometimes, declining a client who isn't ready saves both of you time and frustration, and allows you to serve clients who are fully aligned.

Structuring Your Discovery Call

A well-structured call guides the conversation naturally, ensuring you cover all critical points while making the client feel heard and supported. Hereâ€™s a blueprint for your 30-minute discovery call with Eleanor:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Eleanor

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's great to connect. Before we dive into Buddy's situation, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me, and what prompted you to reach out now?"
ELEANOR:
"Hi! Yes, I saw your post in the 'Healthy Hounds' group about gut health, and it really resonated. Buddy's been struggling for so long, and I'm just at my wit's end."
YOU:
"I totally understand. Many owners feel that way when their beloved pets are unwell. So, for our call today, my goal is to understand Buddy's unique health challenges, share a bit about how I work, and see if my approach is the right fit to help you both. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Impact 10 min
YOU:
"Fantastic. Let's talk about Buddy. You mentioned chronic diarrhea, skin itchiness, and ear infections. Could you tell me more about when these issues started, how often they occur, and how they truly impact Buddy's quality of life and your daily routine?"
ELEANOR:
"Oh, it's been about two years now. The diarrhea is constant, some days worse than others. He wakes me up several times a night. The itching is relentless, especially his paws, and he's always shaking his head. It breaks my heart. We can't go on long walks anymore; he's just not himself."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly tough for both of you. It's clear how much you love Buddy. What steps have you already taken to address these issues? What worked, even if temporarily, and what didn't seem to make a difference?"
ELEANOR:
"We've tried prescription hydrolyzed protein diets, antibiotics for the ears, steroids for the itching... they'd help for a week or two, then everything comes back. I even tried a raw diet for a month, but it seemed to make the diarrhea worse."
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see Buddy's health and your life with him in another year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly resolve these issues, what would Buddy's life look like? What would your ideal outcome be?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You've been tirelessly seeking solutions for Buddy's chronic digestive and skin issues, experiencing frustration and guilt with temporary fixes, and what you truly desire is for Buddy to be comfortable, happy, and regain his zest for life, without constant suffering. Does that feel accurate?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Wonderful. The approach I use, the F.U.E.L. Frameworkâ„¢, is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just managing symptoms, we focus on identifying and addressing the root causes of Buddy's issues. We start with a deep dive into his history, current diet, and lifestyle, often integrating advanced diagnostic tools like gut microbiome testing if appropriate. Then, we craft a highly personalized nutritional and lifestyle plan designed to restore balance and promote true healing. My clients often see significant improvements in energy, digestion, and coat quality within the first 4-6 weeks, with lasting changes over our 12-week program."
Phase 4: Close & Call to Action 5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on what I've described about my process and what you're hoping for Buddy, does this sound like the comprehensive, root-cause approach you've been looking for?"
ELEANOR:
"It really does. It sounds much more thorough than anything we've done."
YOU:
"Great! Then I'd be delighted to discuss how we can get started. My signature program is a 12-week commitment, as true healing takes time. Would you like me to walk you through the details and investment?"
Coach Tip from Sarah

Practice summarizing the client's problem in their own words. Phrases like "So what I'm hearing is..." or "It sounds like you're feeling..." demonstrate deep listening and build immense trust. This validation makes them feel understood and more open to your solution.

Navigating Common Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, understanding, and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"This sounds great, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel 100% confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need more time to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the information you need." (This helps uncover the real objection.)
"It's too expensive. I've already spent so much money."
"I hear you, Eleanor, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot without getting the lasting results you hoped for, which is incredibly frustrating. My program is an investment, yes, but it's an investment in a long-term solution â€” not another temporary fix. We aim to get to the root of Buddy's issues so you can finally stop the cycle of vet visits, medications, and expensive 'special' diets that don't work. What would it be worth to you to see Buddy truly thrive, free from discomfort, and for you to have peace of mind?" (Reframe cost as investment, emphasize long-term value, connect to their desired outcome.)
"I can probably find all this information online for free."
"You're right, Eleanor, there's a vast amount of information out there online. The challenge often isn't finding information, but finding the right information that's relevant to Buddy's unique needs, and knowing how to implement it safely and effectively. My role is to cut through the noise, provide a personalized roadmap based on proven science, and offer ongoing support and accountability. Think of me as your expert guide, saving you countless hours of research and potential missteps, ensuring Buddy gets exactly what he needs to heal." (Acknowledge, then highlight your unique value: personalization, guidance, safety, accountability.)
"I need to talk to my spouse/partner."
"That makes perfect sense, Eleanor, it's a family decision. To help you have that conversation, what specific questions do you think your partner might have that I could answer now? Perhaps we could schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you, so I can ensure all their questions are addressed directly? My goal is to make sure you both feel completely comfortable with this path forward for Buddy." (Offer to help facilitate the conversation, remove obstacles.)

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

When it comes to discussing your fees, confidence is key. Remember, you are offering a high-value, specialized service that provides transformative results. Your pricing reflects your expertise, your time, and the profound impact you will have on a dog's health and an owner's peace of mind.

Always present your programs and pricing clearly, concisely, and without apology. Lead with the value, then state the price. Avoid phrases like "It's just..." or "My fee is only..."

Example: "My signature F.U.E.L. Transformation Program is a 12-week intensive package designed to completely overhaul Buddy's nutrition and support his healing journey. This includes a comprehensive initial assessment, personalized meal plans, weekly check-ins, direct access to me for questions, and tailored supplementation recommendations. The investment for this complete program is $X,XXX."

Follow up with: "How does that sound to you?" This open-ended question invites a response and allows you to address any immediate concerns.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Practice saying your prices out loud, even to yourself in the mirror. The more comfortable you become articulating your fees, the more confident you'll sound during actual client calls. Your energy around your pricing directly influences how clients perceive its value.

The Art of the Enrollment Close

The close isn't about pressure; it's about guiding the client to take the next logical step towards their desired outcome. After presenting your program and addressing any questions or objections, it's time to ask for the commitment.

  • Direct Close: "Eleanor, are you ready to get started and help Buddy finally find relief with the F.U.E.L. Transformation Program?"
  • Assumptive Close: "Great, so the next step is to get Buddy's initial assessment scheduled. How does next Tuesday or Wednesday work for you?"
  • Option Close: "We can either get started with the full 12-week program today, or if you prefer, we can begin with a foundational 4-week jumpstart package to address his immediate discomfort. Which feels like a better fit for you right now?"

After asking, stop talking. Let the client respond. The silence can feel uncomfortable, but it's crucial for them to process and make their decision.

Coach Tip from Sarah

Don't be afraid of the silence after you ask for the close. It's the client's turn to speak. Resist the urge to fill it with more information or to try to "sell" harder. Let them process and make their decision.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem & Impact" phase of a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to deeply listen and uncover the client's dog's specific health challenges, understand the emotional and practical impact these issues have on both the dog and the owner, and identify their desired outcomes. This phase is crucial for building empathy and tailoring your solution.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the cost, stating, "I've already spent so much money." How should you best address this objection?

Acknowledge her frustration ("I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot without getting the lasting results you hoped for..."). Then, reframe the cost as an investment in a long-term solution, contrasting it with past temporary fixes. Connect the investment to her desired outcome for Buddy and her peace of mind.

3. When presenting your program and pricing, what is a crucial verbal cue to maintain confidence and allow the client to process?

After clearly stating your program's value and price, ask an open-ended question like "How does that sound to you?" or "Are you ready to get started?" and then stop talking. Allow silence for the client to process and respond.

4. What is the key difference between a "Direct Close" and an "Assumptive Close" in the enrollment phase?

A Direct Close directly asks for the client's commitment (e.g., "Are you ready to get started?"). An Assumptive Close assumes the client is ready to move forward and asks about logistics (e.g., "How does next Tuesday work for your initial assessment?") to guide them to the next step.
