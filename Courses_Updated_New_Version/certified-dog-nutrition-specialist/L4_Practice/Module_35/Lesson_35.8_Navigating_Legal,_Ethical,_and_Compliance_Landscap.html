The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Your goal is to be authentic, empathetic, and guide the conversation naturally. Remember, this is a two-way street; you're also assessing if they're a good fit for your support.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. I understand Susan referred you â€“ she's been a truly dedicated client. How are you doing today?"

"I'm good, thank you. Susan raves about you, so I was keen to reach out."
YOU:
"That's so kind of her! I really appreciate her trust. So, on our call today, my goal is really just to listen to what's going on with Max, understand your concerns, and then share a bit about how I work. If it feels like a good fit for both of us, we can discuss next steps. Does that sound good?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, that sounds perfect."
Coach Tip: Active Listening

Your primary role in the first two phases is to listen, not to diagnose or offer solutions. Use empathetic mirroring and open-ended questions. Nod, make eye contact (if video), and let them feel heard. This builds immense trust, which is the foundation of any successful client relationship.

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"Wonderful. So Eleanor, tell me â€” what's been happening with Max? What made you decide to reach out now?"
ELEANOR:
"Well, Max has been dealing with constant ear infections, he's always scratching, and his joints seem to be getting worse. The vet gives him antibiotics and steroids, which help temporarily, but it always comes back. I just feel so helpless."
YOU:
"It sounds incredibly frustrating to see Max suffer and not find a lasting solution. When did these issues first start, and how are they impacting his daily life, and yours?"
ELEANOR:
"It started about two years ago, but it's gotten much worse in the last six months. He's less active, he whimpers sometimes, and I'm constantly cleaning his ears. It breaks my heart. I worry about his quality of life."
YOU:
"I can absolutely hear how much you love Max and how distressing this must be. You mentioned you've tried different things. What approaches have you explored so far, and what were the outcomes?"
ELEANOR:
"We've done special prescription kibbles, limited ingredient diets, allergy tests... nothing seems to stick. I'm just so tired of trying things that don't work."
YOU:
"If nothing changes with Max's health in the next year, what does that look like for you and him? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and truly resolve these issues, what would Max's life, and your life, look like?"
ELEANOR:
"Oh, I hate to think of nothing changing. It would mean more pain for him, more worry for me. But if I could fix it... Max would be running around, playing fetch like he used to, no more scratching, no more ear gunk. I'd feel so much relief, knowing he's truly healthy and happy."
Coach Tip: The "Magic Wand" Question

This powerful question helps clients articulate their desired future state and connect emotionally with the potential outcome of your work. It moves them beyond their pain points to their aspirations, making them more receptive to solutions.

Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, confidence, and a clear understanding of your value.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"This sounds great, but I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision for Max's health. Can I ask, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"The investment is a bit higher than I expected."
"I appreciate your honesty, and I know financial considerations are important. Many of my clients initially feel that way. However, they often find that investing in a root-cause solution now actually saves them money in the long run by reducing recurring vet visits, expensive prescription foods that don't work, and medications. This program is an investment in Max's lasting health and your peace of mind. What value do you place on Max being comfortable and free from his current struggles?"
"I'm not sure I have the time for a 12-week commitment."
"That's a valid concern, Eleanor. I design my programs to integrate seamlessly into your life. The initial phase is more intensive as we gather information and create the plan, but after that, it's about consistent, manageable steps. We break it down into actionable weekly strategies, and our check-ins are designed to be efficient and supportive. Imagine the time you'll save not constantly managing flare-ups and vet visits. What would it mean to you to have a clear roadmap and dedicated support for Max's health?"
"I've tried so many things, I'm just not sure anything will really work."
"I completely empathize with feeling that way, especially after trying so many different avenues without lasting success. That's precisely why my approach is different. We don't just try another 'thing'; we conduct a deep investigation into Max's unique biology and pinpoint the specific imbalances. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution; it's a personalized blueprint for Max's health. My clients, like Susan, come to me feeling exactly as you do, and they are consistently amazed by the results once we address the true underlying causes. What would it feel like to finally have a clear, effective plan that works for Max?"

Presenting Your Value & Pricing

Confidence in your pricing comes from confidence in your value. Remember, you're offering a transformative solution, not just a service. Frame your pricing as an investment in a desired outcome.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Magic Wand" question in a discovery call?

The "Magic Wand" question helps the prospect articulate their desired future state and connect emotionally with the potential positive outcomes of your program, moving them beyond just their current pain points.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective immediate response?

An effective response is to acknowledge their need to think, then gently inquire what specifically they need to think about (e.g., "What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time, or something else?"). This helps uncover the real objection.

3. How should you frame your pricing during a discovery call?

Frame your pricing as an "investment" in the desired outcome (e.g., "The investment for this complete program is..."). Emphasize the long-term value, potential savings from avoiding ineffective treatments, and the immeasurable benefit of their dog's improved health.

4. According to the lesson, how does consistent client acquisition on discovery calls impact your business?

Consistent client acquisition directly drives significant income growth, enabling practitioners to achieve financial freedom and scale their practice. It's the mechanism by which marketing efforts translate into tangible business success.
