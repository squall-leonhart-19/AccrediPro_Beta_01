Understanding the Sales Process: Beyond the Clinical

As a Certified Dog Nutrition Specialist, your primary goal is to improve the health and well-being of dogs. However, to do that, you first need clients. This means embracing the role of a business owner, which includes understanding and confidently executing your sales process. Think of it not as "selling," but as educating and empowering clients to make the best decision for their beloved pets.

Many practitioners, myself included, initially feel a disconnect between their passion for helping and the necessity of charging for services. It's important to reframe this: your expertise is valuable, and charging for it allows you to sustain your practice, invest in further education, and ultimately help more dogs. A clear, confident sales process is a pathway to delivering your invaluable services.

Mastering the Discovery Call: Your 30-Minute Blueprint

The discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a conversation. Your goal is to understand if you can genuinely help the prospect, and if they are ready and willing to commit to the process. Use the following script as a framework, but remember to let your natural empathy and listening skills guide you.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Building Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Emily! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's lovely to connect. Before we dive into Buddy's situation, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
EMILY:
"I found your website after searching for 'holistic dog nutrition.' Your client testimonials really resonated with me, especially the one about the Golden Retriever with skin issues. It sounded so much like Buddy."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear, Emily. I'm so glad our paths crossed. So, tell me, what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Buddy that prompted this call?"

Coach Tip: Genuine Connection

Start with genuine curiosity, not an agenda. Ask about their day, how they found you, or something brief to build a human connection before diving into the problem. This makes them feel seen and heard, not just like another lead.

Phase 2: Uncovering the Core Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing, Emily. Let's talk more about Buddy. You mentioned chronic ear infections and hot spots. When did these issues first start, and how do they impact his daily life and yours?"
EMILY:
"Oh, it feels like forever. The ear infections started about three years ago, and the hot spots followed. He's constantly scratching, he's lethargic, and the house always smells faintly of yeast despite my best efforts. I'm constantly cleaning his ears, applying creams, and rushing him to the vet for another round of antibiotics. It's heartbreaking to see him so uncomfortable."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly stressful for both of you. And what have you tried so far to help Buddy? What worked, even temporarily, and what didn't seem to make a difference?"
EMILY:
"We've done countless vet visits, different antibiotics, steroid shots, special shampoos, and he's been on a prescription hypoallergenic kibble for the last year. The antibiotics help for a few weeks, but then it all comes back. The hypoallergenic food hasn't changed anything, and honestly, he barely touches it now."
YOU:
"I hear you. It sounds like you've been on a very long and frustrating journey, trying everything you can for Buddy. If nothing changes for Buddy in the next year, what does that look like for you both? And, if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would Buddy's life, and your life, look like?"

Coach Tip: The "Magic Wand" Question

This powerful question helps clients articulate their deepest desires and imagine a solution, moving them from problem-focused to solution-oriented. It also highlights the emotional impact of the problem, which is often a key motivator.

Phase 3: Presenting Your Tailored Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Emily, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're exhausted from seeing Buddy suffer from chronic ear infections, hot spots, and digestive issues. You've tried conventional methods like antibiotics, steroids, and prescription foods, but they only offer temporary relief, leaving you feeling helpless and worried about his future. Does that feel accurate?"
EMILY:
"Yes, absolutely. You've hit the nail on the head."
YOU:
"Thank you. Now, here's where my approach as a Certified Dog Nutrition Specialist is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes of Buddy's discomfort. We'll dive deep into his diet history, environment, and lifestyle to identify exactly what's driving these recurring issues. I work with clients like you over 12 weeks to create a truly personalized nutrition and lifestyle plan. Most of my clients see significant improvements in their dog's coat, skin, and digestion within the first 4-6 weeks, leading to a happier, healthier dog and a much less stressed owner."
YOU:
"My goal is to empower you with the knowledge and tools to confidently manage Buddy's health long-term, reducing those constant vet visits for recurring issues and giving you back the joy of having a vibrant, energetic companion."

Coach Tip: Summarize & Validate

Before presenting your solution, summarize the client's pain points. This shows you've listened and understood, building trust. Then, clearly articulate how your unique approach addresses those specific pains, focusing on outcomes and benefits, not just features.

Phase 4: Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Emily, based on what I've described about my program and how we work to address the root causes of Buddy's issues, does this sound like the comprehensive, lasting solution you've been looking for?"
EMILY:
"It really does sound promising. It's exactly what I've been hoping to find."
YOU:
"Fantastic! Let me tell you a bit more about the program structure and how we can get started to bring Buddy that lasting relief and vitality."

Navigating Objections with Confidence

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're rarely a "no," but rather a request for more information, reassurance, or clarification. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Emily. This is an important decision, especially when it comes to Buddy's health. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment involved, or perhaps something else you'd like more clarity on? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"It's too expensive. I've already spent so much money."
"I absolutely hear you, Emily, and I understand your concern about the investment, especially given what you've already spent with limited results. Many of my clients come to me after spending thousands on recurring vet bills, medications, and prescription foods that don't solve the underlying problem. My program is designed to be a one-time investment in a lasting solution, potentially saving you money in the long run by reducing those ongoing costs and giving Buddy true, sustainable health. What value would it be to you to see Buddy free from chronic itching and ear infections, and for you to have peace of mind?"
"I've tried everything. How is this going to be any different?"
"That's a very fair question, Emily, and it speaks to the frustration you've experienced. The key difference lies in our holistic, root-cause approach. While conventional medicine often focuses on managing symptoms with medications, we delve into Buddy's unique biochemistry, diet, and lifestyle to uncover the underlying imbalances. We don't just stop the itching; we work to understand *why* he's itching. My comprehensive 12-week program allows us to implement and adjust a personalized plan, educating you every step of the way, so you become the expert in Buddy's health. It's a journey towards true wellness, not just temporary relief."
"I need to talk to my partner first."
"Of course, that makes perfect sense, especially when it involves a family decision about Buddy's health. I'm happy to provide you with a brief summary of what we discussed, or even better, if your partner has questions, I'd be happy to schedule a quick 10-15 minute follow-up call with both of you to answer any concerns they might have. How does that sound?"

Coach Tip: Empathy and Confidence

Listen actively to the objection, validate their feelings, and then confidently pivot to how your program addresses their specific concern. Don't be defensive. Your calm, informed response builds trust.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing with Authority

When it's time to talk about your program and its cost, it's crucial to do so with unwavering confidence. Remember, you're not just quoting a number; you're presenting the investment in a transformation. Always lead with the value and outcomes before stating the price.

Sealing the Deal: The Call to Action

After presenting your program and addressing any lingering questions, it's time to guide the client to the next step. A clear call to action (CTA) is essential; don't leave them wondering what to do next.

Practice Your Closing Lines

Here are a few ways to confidently move forward:

  • "So, Emily, does this sound like the right step for Buddy and for you to finally get the lasting change you're looking for?" (Pause for response) "Great! The next step is to send you the enrollment link and schedule our official onboarding call. How does that sound?"
  • "Considering everything we've discussed, I truly believe the Pawsitive Wellness Transformation is exactly what Buddy needs to regain his health. Are you ready to get started and begin this journey together?"
  • "Based on Buddy's symptoms and your goals, I'm confident we can achieve significant improvements. My next availability for an onboarding call is [Date] at [Time] or [Date] at [Time]. Which of those works best for you to officially kick off Buddy's transformation?"
  • "I'll send you a summary of our conversation and the program details, along with the link to enroll. If you have any further questions as you review it, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'm here to support you."

Remember, the goal is to make the next step clear and easy for them. Sometimes, a direct question about readiness or next steps is all it takes.

Check Your Understanding

The primary goal of the discovery call is to understand if you can genuinely help the prospect and if they are ready and willing to commit to the process, rather than just delivering a sales pitch.

Sarah suggests reframing "selling" as "educating and empowering clients" to make the best decision for their beloved pets, focusing on how you can best serve their needs.

Key Takeaways

  • Approaching sales with authenticity and empathy transforms it into a helpful conversation, not a daunting task.
  • A structured discovery call is crucial for building rapport, understanding deep-seated client problems, and presenting your unique solution.
  • Objections are opportunities for clarification and reassurance; respond with empathy, curiosity, and confidence.
  • Always lead with the value and desired outcomes of your program before confidently stating your pricing.
