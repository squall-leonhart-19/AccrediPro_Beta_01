The Discovery Call Script: Guiding the Conversation

This 30-minute call structure is designed to build rapport, identify core problems, present your unique solution, and guide the prospect towards a decision. Practice these phases out loud!

Phase Time Your Dialogue (Example) Key Objective
1. Build Rapport & Set Agenda 5 min "Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat today. Before we dive into Buster's health, I'd love to hear how you found me and what made you decide to reach out now. Then, I'll ask some questions about Buster, share a bit about my approach, and see if we're a good fit. Does that sound good?" Establish trust, make them comfortable, confirm purpose of call.
2. Understand the Problem (Deep Dive) 10 min "Thank you for sharing, Eleanor. Tell me more about Buster's digestive issues. When did they start? How do they impact his daily life? What have you already tried, and what were the outcomes? If nothing changes, what does life look like for Buster and for you in 6 months? If you could wave a magic wand, what would Buster's health look like?" Uncover pain points, emotional drivers, previous attempts, and desired future state. Listen more than you speak.
3. Present Your Approach & Value 10 min "Eleanor, based on what you've shared, I'm hearing that Buster is struggling with [summarize symptoms] and you're feeling [summarize emotions]. Does that feel accurate? My approach is different. Instead of just managing symptoms, we identify and address the root causes of digestive distress and declining vitality. I work with clients over 12 weeks to create a truly personalized nutritional plan. Most clients see significant improvements in energy and digestion within 4-6 weeks." Summarize their problem (shows you listened), introduce your unique solution (F.U.E.L. Framework‚Ñ¢), and highlight benefits/results.
4. Close & Next Steps 5 min "So Eleanor, does this sound like the comprehensive, personalized approach you've been searching for to help Buster? ... Wonderful! If you're ready to explore this further, I'd suggest starting with my Canine Nutritional Revitalization Program. Let me tell you a bit about what's included and how we can get started." Gauge interest, offer a clear path forward.

Confident Objection Handling: Turning "No" into "Know More"

Objections are not rejections; they are requests for more information. Your confidence and empathy are your greatest assets here.

Common Objections & Effective Responses:

  • "I need to think about it."

    Your Response: "I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision for Buster. To help me understand, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have more questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

    (This helps you identify the real objection. Be prepared to address it specifically.)

  • "It's too expensive/I've already spent so much money."

    Your Response: "I hear you, Eleanor, and I know you've invested significantly in Buster's health already. Many of my clients come to me after spending thousands on treatments that didn't get to the root cause. My program is an investment in finding a lasting solution, not just managing symptoms. What's the cost of *not* addressing this? What's the value of seeing Buster comfortable and energetic again? We can discuss payment plans, but I encourage you to consider the long-term benefit of a truly revitalized Buster."

    (Shift focus from cost to value and long-term benefits. Acknowledge their concern, then reframe.)

  • "I've tried everything; I don't know if this will work."

    Your Response: "I completely empathize, Eleanor. It sounds like you've been on a long and frustrating journey, and it's natural to feel skeptical. The difference with my approach, the F.U.E.L. Framework‚Ñ¢, is its highly individualized nature. We use advanced assessment tools to pinpoint exactly what's going on with Buster's unique biochemistry, rather than a generic 'one-size-fits-all' solution. This isn't just another diet; it's a precise, science-backed strategy tailored just for him. Are you open to exploring an approach that looks beyond conventional methods?"

    (Validate their frustration, then highlight your unique, personalized, and scientific approach.)

Coach's Insight

When handling objections, maintain a calm, empathetic tone. Remember, you're not arguing; you're educating and reassuring. Your confidence in your process is contagious. If you believe in your value, they will too.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing with Confidence

Once you've addressed concerns, it's time to confidently present your program and its investment. Never apologize for your prices.

Structuring Your Program Offer:

For Eleanor, you might offer your Canine Nutritional Revitalization Program, a 12-week intensive program designed for dogs with complex, chronic issues.

  • Program Components:
    • Initial Comprehensive Nutritional Assessment (including detailed history, current diet analysis, lifestyle factors).
    • Two 60-minute 1:1 consultation calls (initial strategy, mid-program review).
    • Four 30-minute follow-up calls (bi-weekly check-ins).
    • Personalized Nutritional Plan (including ingredient recommendations, recipes, supplement guidance).
    • Email support between sessions.
    • Access to exclusive client resources.
  • Investment: "The investment for Buster's 12-week Canine Nutritional Revitalization Program is $2,497. This includes everything we just discussed, designed to give Buster the best chance at significant improvement. We can also discuss a 3-month payment plan of $897/month."
Coach's Insight

Always state the full value first, then the price. Present payment options clearly. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident. Your energy around money impacts how clients perceive your value.

Call-to-Action Practice: Guiding the Enrollment

After presenting your program and pricing, it's essential to clearly ask for the enrollment. Practice these lines to make them your own.

  • "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed today, I truly believe we can make a significant difference for Buster. Are you ready to take this next step and get him started on his path to feeling better?"
  • "Given Buster's current situation, the sooner we begin, the sooner he can find relief. What feels like a good next step for you?"
  • "I have a few spots available in my program starting next month. If you're feeling a strong 'yes' to helping Buster, we can get you enrolled today and schedule our first assessment call."
  • "How does this sound to you, Eleanor? Are you ready to move forward?"
Coach's Insight

Silence is powerful. Once you've asked the closing question, stop talking and let the client respond. It can feel uncomfortable, but it gives them space to process and make a decision. Be patient, be present.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply uncover the prospect's pain points, emotional drivers, previous attempts at solutions, and their desired future state for their dog's health. It's about active listening and empathetic inquiry, not offering solutions yet.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the cost, stating, "I've already spent so much money." How should you best respond to this objection?

Acknowledge her concern empathetically ("I hear you, Eleanor, and I know you've invested significantly..."). Then, pivot to the value and long-term benefits of your unique, root-cause approach, asking her to consider the cost of *not* solving the problem and the value of a lasting solution for Buster. Mention payment plans as an option.

3. When presenting your program and pricing, what is a crucial principle to follow?

Always present the full value and benefits of your program *before* stating the price. Speak with confidence and conviction, without apologizing for your fees. Be ready to clearly explain what's included and any payment options.

4. Sarah emphasizes the importance of silence after asking a closing question. Why is this strategy effective?

Silence gives the prospect the necessary space to process the information, consider their options, and formulate their response without feeling rushed or pressured. It demonstrates confidence and respect, allowing them to make an uncoerced decision.
Key Takeaways
  • Discovery calls are about connection: Focus on building rapport and genuinely understanding your prospect's needs and emotional drivers.
  • Listen more than you speak: Ask open-ended questions and allow the client to elaborate on their challenges and desired outcomes.
  • Objections are opportunities: View objections as requests for more information, and respond with empathy, education, and confidence in your unique approach.
  • Present your value, then your price: Confidently articulate the benefits of your program before stating the investment, and offer clear next steps.
  • Your expertise is valuable: Embrace the sales process as a way to connect clients with the life-changing solutions you provide, building a financially rewarding practice.
