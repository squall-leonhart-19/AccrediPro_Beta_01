Practitioner Spotlight: From Teacher to Thriving CDNS

How Maria, 52, transformed her career and confidence

Maria, a former high school teacher in her early 50s, enrolled in the CDNS program seeking a flexible, meaningful career. While she excelled in the nutritional science, she initially struggled with the business aspect, particularly client discovery calls. "I felt like I was asking for money, not offering a solution," she confessed. After completing her certification, Maria dedicated herself to practicing discovery calls, using scripts and role-playing with her study group.

Her breakthrough came when she realized the call wasn't about selling, but about listening and identifying a genuine need. She started focusing on asking open-ended questions about her prospect's dog's health journey and their owner's frustrations. By truly understanding their pain points, she could then articulate how her services directly addressed those needs.

Outcome: Within six months, Maria went from zero clients to a consistent caseload of 5-7 clients per month. Her confidence soared, and she reported earning a net income of approximately $3,500-$5,000 per month, allowing her to work from home and enjoy the financial freedom she desired. Maria's success underscores that mastering the discovery call is a skill, not an innate talent, and it's one you can absolutely develop.

The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework. Practice internalizing the flow, so you sound natural, not robotic. Your authenticity is your biggest asset!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Agenda 5 min

"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that the [local pet community] helped you find me. I'm so glad you reached out. So, for our call today, my goal is to understand what's going on with Buddy, discuss what you've tried so far, and determine if my approach is the right fit to help you achieve your goals. Does that sound good?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Emotional Impact 10 min
YOU:
"Fantastic. So Eleanor, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's been happening with Buddy?"
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. It sounds incredibly frustrating to see Buddy struggling with [digestive upset, itchiness, low energy]. When did these issues start? How does it affect his daily life, and yours?"

Confident Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Practice these responses to feel prepared.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand. This is an important decision. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for Buddy."
"It's too expensive. I've already spent so much money."
"I hear you, Eleanor, and I know you've invested a lot already. Many of my clients come to me after trying countless solutions that didn't work. While the initial investment might seem significant, consider the cost of ongoing vet visits, prescription foods, and supplements that aren't truly addressing the problem. My program is designed to get to the root cause, offering a lasting solution that saves you money and worry in the long run. What is the cost of Buddy's ongoing discomfort and your stress?"
"I'm not sure if I have the time to implement a new diet."
"That's a valid concern, and I want to assure you that my program is designed to fit into busy lives. We start with small, manageable steps, and I provide clear, easy-to-follow instructions and ongoing support. My goal is to make this process as smooth and stress-free as possible for you. Many clients find that the time invested upfront saves them significant time and worry down the road when their dog is thriving."
"I need to talk to my spouse/partner."
"Absolutely, it's a family decision. What specific questions or concerns do you anticipate your spouse might have that I could help you address now, so you feel fully prepared for that conversation?" (Alternatively: "Would it be helpful to schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you so I can answer any questions directly?")

Coach Tip

Silence is golden in sales. After you ask a question or present an option, pause and let the prospect speak. Don't rush to fill the silence. This gives them space to process and articulate their thoughts, often revealing their true objections or readiness to move forward.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing Confidently

Confidence in your pricing comes from confidence in your value. You are offering a specialized, transformative service. Do not apologize for your rates.

Strategies for Presenting Your Investment

  • Anchor High: Always state the full price first, then offer payment plans if applicable. For example, "The full investment for the 12-week 'Thriving Canine Transformation' program is $2,500."
  • Focus on Transformation, Not Transactions: Frame the price in terms of the results and relief they will gain. "This investment provides Buddy with a customized plan to address the root causes of his discomfort, leading to lasting health and vitality, and giving you peace of mind."
  • Offer Payment Options (Optional but Recommended): "I also offer a convenient 3-month payment plan of $850 per month." This makes it more accessible.
  • Be Silent After Stating the Price: Let the number land. The next person to speak often loses.

Example Dialogue:

YOU:
"Eleanor, the full investment for Buddy's 12-week 'Thriving Canine Transformation' program is $2,500. This comprehensive program is designed to finally get to the root of Buddy's issues, giving him lasting comfort and energy, and bringing you the joy of seeing him thrive again. I also offer a convenient 3-month payment plan of $850 per month. Which option works best for you?"

Mastering Your Call-to-Action (CTA)

The CTA is where you guide the prospect to the next step. It should be clear, confident, and direct.

Practice These Closing Lines

Option 1 (Direct): "Eleanor, are you ready to get started on Buddy's journey to better health today?"
Option 2 (Benefit-Oriented): "If you're ready to see Buddy regain his energy and comfort, the next step is to enroll in the 'Thriving Canine Transformation' program. We can get you set up right now."
Option 3 (Assumptive Close): "Great! So, what's the best email to send over the enrollment agreement and your welcome packet?"
Option 4 (Addressing a soft 'yes'): "It sounds like you're ready to move forward. What would be the ideal start date for you and Buddy?"

Remember: After your CTA, be silent. Let Eleanor respond.

Coach Tip

Don't undervalue your time or expertise. As a Certified Dog Nutrition Specialist, you offer a unique, specialized service that can profoundly impact a dog's life and an owner's peace of mind. Your pricing reflects the depth of your knowledge, the personalized attention you provide, and the transformative results you deliver. Own your value!

Check Your Understanding

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to deeply understand the prospect's pain points, their dog's specific issues, what they've tried, and the emotional impact these challenges have on both the dog and the owner. This helps tailor your solution and build empathy.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the program being "too expensive." How should you generally respond to this objection?

Acknowledge her concern, validate her feelings, then reframe the investment in terms of long-term value and the cost of inaction. Emphasize that your program offers a lasting solution to the root cause, potentially saving money and stress over time.

3. When presenting your pricing, what is a recommended strategy to make the investment more digestible for the client?

A recommended strategy is to "anchor high" by stating the full price first, then offering a payment plan (e.g., 3-month installment option). This makes the payment plan seem more affordable in comparison.

4. After delivering a Call-to-Action (CTA), what crucial action should you take?

Remain silent. After asking a closing question or stating the next step, pause and allow the prospect to respond. This gives them space to process and articulate their decision without feeling pressured or interrupted.

Key Takeaways

  • The Discovery Call is a structured conversation designed to build rapport, understand needs, and determine fit.
  • Effective calls prioritize listening and empathizing with the client's emotional and practical challenges.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, not personal rejections.
  • Present your pricing with confidence, focusing on the transformative value and long-term benefits of your service.
  • Clear, direct Calls-to-Action guide prospects to the next step, turning interest into enrollment.
  • Consistent client acquisition efforts can lead to significant and flexible income as a CDNS.
