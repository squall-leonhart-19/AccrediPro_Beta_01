The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is arguably the most crucial step in your client acquisition journey. It's not a sales pitch; it's a two-way conversation designed to determine if you and your potential client are a good fit. It's where you listen more than you speak, diagnose needs, and subtly position yourself as the expert solution. A well-executed discovery call can lead to a conversion rate of 50-70% for established practitioners, turning leads into loyal clients (AccrediPro Internal Data, 2023).

For many women pivoting careers, this can feel intimidating. Remember, your expertise as a CDNS is invaluable. You are offering a solution to a problem that often causes significant stress and expense to dog owners. Your role is to educate, empower, and guide them.

Coach Tip

Approach every discovery call with genuine curiosity and a desire to serve. Your primary goal is to understand their dog's challenges and the owner's frustrations. When you lead with empathy, the client feels heard, and trust is naturally built, making the transition to discussing solutions much smoother.

Structuring Your 30-Minute Discovery Call

A structured call ensures you cover all necessary points while maintaining a natural, conversational flow. Here's a proven 4-phase structure:

Phase 1: Building Rapport (5 minutes)

Your goal here is to create a warm, safe space. Start with genuine connection, not an interrogation.

  • YOU: "Hi Eleanor, thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into Max's situation, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about AccrediPro Academy and my services?"
  • ELEANOR: "Oh, my friend Susan from the pet boutique recommended you. She said you helped her dog with his digestive issues."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful! Susan's dog, Buster, has indeed made incredible progress. So Eleanor, what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Max?"
Insight

Starting with how they found you is a gentle opening that allows them to tell a positive story and establishes a connection. It also gives you valuable feedback on your marketing channels.

Phase 2: Understanding Their Needs (10 minutes)

This is where you listen actively and ask probing questions to uncover the full scope of Max's issues and Eleanor's frustrations. Focus on open-ended questions.

  • YOU: "Thank you for sharing that. Tell me more about Max's chronic ear infections and skin rashes. When did they start? How do they affect his daily life, and yours?"
  • ELEANOR: "It's been years. He's constantly scratching, he's uncomfortable, and the smell from his ears... it's just awful. I feel so guilty seeing him suffer. I'm spending so much on wipes and ear drops."
  • YOU: "I hear your frustration, Eleanor. It sounds incredibly challenging. What have you tried so far to address these issues, and what were the outcomes?"
  • ELEANOR: "Antibiotics, steroids, special vet diets... they work for a bit, but then it all comes back. I even tried a raw diet I read about online, but he got diarrhea, so I stopped."
  • YOU: "That's a common experience for many owners who feel stuck in a cycle. If nothing changes for Max in the next year, what does that look like for both of you? And on the flip side, if we could wave a magic wand and address these issues, what would your life with Max look like?"

Phase 3: Presenting Your Tailored Solution (10 minutes)

Based on what you've heard, summarize their pain points and introduce your unique, holistic approach, positioning yourself as the solution.

  • YOU: "Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: Max is suffering from chronic inflammatory issues manifesting as ear infections, skin rashes, and weight gain, leading to significant discomfort for him and immense frustration and guilt for you. You're looking for a long-term, root-cause solution, not just temporary fixes. Does that feel accurate?"
  • ELEANOR: "Yes, exactly! You've really understood it."
  • YOU: "The approach I use as a Certified Dog Nutrition Specialist is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we delve into the root causes of Max's inflammation and imbalances. Through my 12-week 'Thrive & Shine Canine Program,' we'll conduct a comprehensive dietary analysis, identify potential food sensitivities, optimize his gut health, and create a personalized nutrition and lifestyle plan tailored specifically for Max. Most of my clients, like Susan, start seeing significant improvements in energy, skin, and digestion within the first 4-6 weeks."
Coach Tip

Always seek confirmation after summarizing their pain points. This validates their experience and reinforces that you've been listening. When presenting your solution, use confident, clear language. Highlight the unique benefits of your CDNS approach and speak to their desired outcomes.

Phase 4: The Confident Close (5 minutes)

This is where you transition from discussion to action. Don't shy away from asking for the commitment.

  • YOU: "So Eleanor, based on what you've heard about my approach and how it aligns with Max's needs and your goals, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive solution you've been looking for?"
  • ELEANOR: "It really does, Sarah. It sounds like a breath of fresh air."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful to hear! I'm confident we can help Max. Let me tell you about the 'Thrive & Shine Canine Program' and how we can get started."

Navigating Common Objections with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Handle them with empathy and confidence.

"It's too expensive."

  • ELEANOR: "This sounds amazing, Sarah, but I've already spent so much money. What's the investment for your program? I'm worried it might be too expensive."
  • YOU: "I completely understand that concern, Eleanor, especially given your past experiences. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. What they've found, however, is that investing in a root-cause approach actually saves them money in the long run by reducing recurring vet bills, expensive specialty foods that don't work, and the emotional cost of seeing their dog suffer. My 12-week 'Thrive & Shine Canine Program' is an investment of $1800, which can be paid in three monthly installments of $600. This covers our comprehensive assessment, personalized plan, weekly check-ins, and ongoing support. What value do you place on Max being truly healthy and happy, and you having peace of mind?"
Coach Tip

Acknowledge their concern, reframe the cost as an investment, quantify the potential savings (emotional and financial), and then confidently state your price. Pivot back to their desired outcome and the value you provide.

Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

Once you've addressed objections and confirmed their interest, it's time to confidently present your program details and pricing. Your tone should be firm, clear, and unwavering.

When stating your price, avoid hesitation or apologies. State the investment clearly, then pause and allow them to respond. The first person to speak often loses the 'sale'.

Example Pricing Presentation:

YOU: "Eleanor, my 'Thrive & Shine Canine Program' is a 12-week journey designed to transform Max's health. It includes:

  • A comprehensive initial consultation (90 minutes) to gather Max's full health history.
  • Detailed dietary analysis and personalized nutrition plan.
  • Customized supplement recommendations, if needed.
  • Six bi-weekly 30-minute follow-up sessions to track progress and make adjustments.
  • Unlimited email support between sessions.
  • Access to exclusive client resources and recipes.

The investment for this complete program is $1800. We can arrange a payment plan of three convenient monthly installments of $600. Which option works best for you?"

Mastering the Call to Action

Always end with a clear, direct call to action (CTA). Tell them exactly what to do next.

  • Option 1 (Direct Close): "Eleanor, I truly believe we can help Max. Are you ready to get started and give him the healthy, vibrant life he deserves?"
  • Option 2 (Payment Plan Focus): "Based on our conversation, I recommend we move forward with the 'Thrive & Shine Canine Program.' Would you prefer to pay in full today or utilize the three-month payment plan?"
  • Option 3 (Next Step Focus): "Great! The next step is to get you enrolled and schedule Max's initial consultation. I'll send you an email right after this call with a link to complete the enrollment paperwork and book your first session. How does that sound?"

Be prepared for them to say "yes"! Have your enrollment link, payment portal, or scheduling tool ready to send immediately.

Coach Tip

Confidence is contagious. If you are confident in your value and your program, your potential client will feel that confidence and be more likely to trust you. Practice these closing lines out loud until they feel natural and powerful.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understanding Their Needs" phase of a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to actively listen and ask probing questions to uncover the full scope of the dog's health challenges and the owner's frustrations, allowing you to tailor your solution effectively.

2. When a prospect says, "It's too expensive," what is the most effective way to respond?

Acknowledge their concern with empathy, reframe the cost as an investment that will save money in the long run (e.g., reduced vet bills, quality of life), confidently state your price and payment options, and then pivot back to the value and desired outcomes.

3. Eleanor expresses concern that her vet might not approve of working with a dog nutrition specialist. How should you address this?

Respect her relationship with her vet, clarify that you focus on nutrition/lifestyle support (not diagnosis/treatment), and offer to provide the vet with a summary of your nutritional plan to demonstrate a collaborative approach to Max's care.

4. Why is a clear and direct "Call to Action" crucial at the end of a discovery call?

A clear CTA removes ambiguity, guides the prospect to the next step, and prevents them from delaying their decision. It demonstrates your confidence and professionalism, making it easier for the client to commit.
Key Takeaways
  • The discovery call is a structured conversation to build trust and determine fit, not a hard sales pitch.
  • Active listening and empathetic questioning are paramount to uncovering a prospect's true needs and frustrations.
  • Confidently address objections by reframing them, providing reassurance, and reiterating the unique value of your CDNS approach.
  • Present your program and pricing with conviction, focusing on the transformative benefits and long-term investment value.
  • Always conclude with a clear, direct call to action, guiding the client to the next step in their journey.
