The Coaching Script: The Movie Technique

Practice these words out loud. Imagine Elena is sitting on your screen or across from your desk. Your tone should be warm, steady, and empathetic.

YOU (The Coach):
"Elena, I hear how much that board meeting is still weighing on you. Even though it happened months ago, your body is still reacting as if itâ€™s happening right now. Are you willing to try a technique called the 'Movie Technique' to help clear that charge?"
ELENA:
"Yes, please. I just want to stop feeling like a failure every time I think about it."
YOU (The Coach):
"I understand. Let's start at the side of the hand. Repeat after me: 'Even though I have this board meeting memory, and it makes my chest feel tight, I deeply and completely accept myself and how I feel.'"
ELENA:
"Even though I have this board meeting memory... and my chest is so tight... I accept myself."
YOU (The Coach):
"Great. Now, as we tap through the points, I want you to give this 'movie' a title. What would you call this short clip of the meeting?"
ELENA:
"I'd call it... 'The Day I Lost My Voice.'"
The Power of the Title

Giving the memory a "Title" creates a healthy distance. It allows the client to look at the memory rather than being in the memory, which prevents flooding.

Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

Even seasoned coaches can stumble. Here is what to watch for in your Level 1 applications:

The Pitfall The Solution
Being Too Vague Instead of "this stress," use Elena's specific words: "this tightness in my chest."
Leading the Client Don't say "You feel sad, right?" Ask: "What emotion is coming up as you watch this movie?"
Tapping Too Fast Slow down. Ensure the client has time to repeat the phrase and feel the point.
Missing the Re-test Always ask for a new SUDs score after each round. Never assume it went down.
Success Marker

You know the session is working when Elena's shoulders drop, she takes a spontaneous deep breath (the "sigh of relief"), or she starts to laugh at how "silly" the memory now seems. That is the Cognitive Shift.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask Elena to give her memory a "Movie Title"?

It creates psychological distance (dissociation), allowing the client to process the memory without becoming overwhelmed or "flooded" by the emotion.

2. If Elena's SUDs score stays at an 8 after two rounds, what should you do?

Check for "Aspects." Ask if a different part of the memory is now bothering her, or if the physical sensation has moved to a different part of the body.

3. What is the primary goal of the Level 1 Movie Technique?

To reduce the emotional intensity of a specific past event to a 0 or a very low number, so it no longer triggers a stress response in the present.

4. True or False: You should always use your own words for the tapping phrases to show your expertise.

False. You should prioritize the client's exact language to ensure the tapping resonates with their subconscious experience.

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Specific is King: Level 1 success depends on narrowing the focus to one "scene" or one "sensation."
  • Presence Over Script: Your calm, regulated nervous system is your most powerful coaching tool.
