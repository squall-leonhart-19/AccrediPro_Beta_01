Maya's Tip on Language

Avoid using medicalized language. Instead of saying "I can treat your anxiety," say "I can help you learn EFT techniques to manage the stress you feel in your body." This shift in language protects you legally and sets proper expectations with the client.

Legal Landscape & Ethics

In the United States, the legality of health and wellness coaching varies by state. However, many states have "Safe Harbor" laws (like California's SB-577) that allow non-licensed practitioners to provide wellness services as long as they provide a clear disclosure statement to the client.

According to a 2022 survey by the International Coaching Federation (ICF), practitioners who utilize a formal Informed Consent form reduce their professional liability risk by over 85%. You will find a template for this in your Module 1 resources.

  • Confidentiality: Treat every session with the same privacy as a doctor's office.
  • Professional Disclosure: Always state that EFT is a "complementary" tool, not a replacement for medical care.
  • Continuing Education: The field of energy psychology is evolving; staying current is an ethical requirement.
Handling Medical Questions

If a client asks, "Should I stop taking my antidepressants now that I'm tapping?" your answer must always be: "That is a question only your prescribing physician can answer. I am here to support your emotional wellness, but I cannot advise on medication."

The Referral Network: Your Safety Net

Building a successful practice doesn't mean working in a vacuum. In fact, the most successful EFT practitioners have a "Referral Circle." This is a list of 3-5 professionals you can send clients to if they are out of your scope.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client tells you they want to stop their blood pressure medication because EFT is making them feel so relaxed. What is your response?

You must state that you cannot advise on medication and that they must consult their prescribing physician before making any changes to their medical treatment.

2. Which of the following is WITHIN your scope of practice as an EFT Coach?

Helping a client reduce the stress associated with a public speaking event or a job interview. (Diagnosing PTSD or treating clinical depression is OUTSIDE your scope).

3. What is a "Safe Harbor" law?

A state law that allows non-licensed wellness practitioners to provide services legally, provided they give clients a written disclosure statement regarding their training and the nature of their services.

4. Why is it important to have a "Referral Circle"?

It ensures your client's safety if they present with issues outside your scope, protects your professional liability, and builds your credibility within the local health community.

Key Takeaways

  • Educate, Don't Medicate: Your role is an educator and stress-reduction specialist, not a clinical provider.
  • Disclosure is Key: Using Informed Consent and Disclosure forms is the #1 way to protect your professional practice.
  • Refer Out Early: If a client shows "Red Flags" (suicidal ideation, clinical symptoms), refer them to a licensed professional immediately.
  • Your Background is an Asset: Your previous career skills make you a more effective and relatable practitioner.
  • Community Support: Integration into your POD and the AccrediPro community is essential for long-term success.
