The Coaching Script

Read this script out loud. Pay attention to the transitions between the Setup Statement and the Reminder Phrases. As a certified professional, your ability to guide this flow is what clients are paying for (often $150-$250 per session).

YOU (The Coach):
"Elena, I hear how much this wellness business means to you, and I also hear that 'tightness' in your throat. Let's start right there. Even though you have this tight, constricted feeling in your throat because you feel like a 'fraud'... you deeply and completely accept yourself."

"(Tapping on the Karate Chop point) Even though I feel like a fraud... and my throat is so tight... I accept how I feel."

"Great. Now, let's go to the points. (Top of Head) This tight throat. (Eyebrow) This fear of being found out. (Side of Eye) I'm just a teacher pretending. (Under Eye) This constriction in my throat..."

"Stay with that breath. (Under Nose) This tight throat. (Chin) This 'pretender' feeling. (Collarbone) It's not safe to be seen. (Under Arm) This tightness in my throat."

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

A 2022 study on practitioner competency found that 64% of new EFT students struggle with "Refinement of the Problem." Avoid these four traps:

1
Being Too General

Don't tap on "My anxiety." Tap on "This fluttering in my chest when I see my inbox." Specificity is the secret sauce of EFT.

2
Leading the Client

Don't say, "You feel sad, right?" Instead, ask, "What is the emotion attached to that tightness?" Use their words, not yours.

3
Forgetting the SUDs Score

Without a 0-10 score, you have no baseline. Always check the intensity before and after a round of tapping.

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena says her throat tightness is a 4, but sheâ€™s crying. What should you do?

Trust the physical cues over the number. If she's crying, the intensity is likely higher than a 4. Gently acknowledge the tears and continue tapping on the "emotional release" she is experiencing.

2. When should you use the "Karate Chop" point in a Level 1 session?

The Karate Chop point is used during the "Setup Statement" to acknowledge the problem and set the intention for the session, typically at the very beginning of a new tapping round.

3. If the client's SUDs score doesn't move after two rounds, what is the most likely issue?

The phrase is likely too general. You need to "get specific." Ask the client to describe the sensation in more detail or identify a specific memory where they felt this exact way.

4. Why is it important to use the client's exact words (e.g., "pretender" instead of "imposter")?

The client's own words are neurologically linked to their specific emotional experience. Using your own "professional" terms can actually disconnect them from the feeling you are trying to clear.

Key Takeaways

  • Voice is a Tool: Your calm, confident tone is as therapeutic as the tapping points themselves.
  • Specificity is King: Always drill down from a general feeling to a specific physical sensation or "snapshot" memory.
  • The SUDs Compass: Use the 0-10 scale to track progress and decide when to shift the tapping phrase.
  • Rapport First: Use the client's language to ensure they feel heard, seen, and safe.
  • Practice Makes Professional: Mastery comes from speaking these scripts out loud until they feel like second nature.
