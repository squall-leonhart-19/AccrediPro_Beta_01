Practice Exercise: Out Loud

Now, it's your turn. Follow these steps to build muscle memory:

Step Action Goal
1. The Setup Tap your "Karate Chop" point and say the Setup Statement 3 times with feeling. Establish the "Problem" and "Acceptance."
2. The Points Go through all 8 points (Head to Underarm) using only 3-word reminder phrases. Keep the client focused on the physical sensation.
3. The Re-Check Stop, take a breath, and ask: "What is that number now?" Measure progress (SUDs).
Pro Tip

If the number doesn't move, don't panic! In Level 1, we often find that the "knot" moves or changes sensation. Ask the client: "Is it still a knot, or is it something else now?" This is the Chasing the Pain technique we discussed in Lesson 4.

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

Avoid these four mistakes that often stem from the "imposter syndrome" many of us feel when starting out:

  • Over-Explaining: Spending 20 minutes explaining the science of EFT instead of actually tapping. Let the results do the talking.
  • Being Too "Polite": If the client is in pain, don't be afraid to use their exact words (even if they use "strong" language).
  • Forgetting the Breath: Always prompt a deep, cleansing breath between rounds to help the nervous system reset.
  • Leading the Witness: Avoid saying, "It feels better now, right?" Instead, ask, "What do you notice now?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we repeat the Setup Statement exactly three times on the Side of Hand point?

In Level 1 protocol, the three-fold repetition helps neutralize the "Psychological Reversal" (subconscious resistance) and ensures the client is fully tuned into the issue before moving to the reminder points.

2. Elena's SUDs score drops from an 8 to a 5. What should your next step be?

Acknowledge the shift ("That's a great shift, Elena") and do another round, adjusting the wording to: "Even though I still have some of this remaining tension..." to address the remaining 5 points of intensity.

3. What should you do if Elena starts crying during the tapping?

Maintain your tapping rhythm. Keep tapping with her silently or use very gentle reminder phrases. Crying is a "processing shift." Stay calm, stay present, and don't stop the tapping‚Äîit's helping her regulate the emotion.

4. Why is it important to use the client's exact words (e.g., "heavy knot") rather than your own (e.g., "tight muscle")?

Using the client's specific language ensures they stay "tuned in" to their unique experience. Your words might not resonate with their internal map of the problem, which can reduce the effectiveness of the session.

Key Takeaways

  • Rapport First: A client must feel safe before their nervous system will allow a SUDs drop.
  • SUDs are Vital: Always get a number before you start. Without a metric, neither you nor the client can objectively see the progress.
  • Follow the Protocol: In Level 1, stick to the basic points and the standard Setup Statement format.
  • Practice Out Loud: Speaking the words builds the neural pathways of a confident coach.
  • Validation: Mirroring the client‚Äôs language builds an instant "bridge of trust" between you.
