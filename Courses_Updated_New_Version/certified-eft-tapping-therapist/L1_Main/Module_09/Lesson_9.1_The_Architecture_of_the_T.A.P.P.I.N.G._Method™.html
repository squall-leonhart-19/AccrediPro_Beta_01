The Art of Specificity: Global vs. Specific

The most common mistake new practitioners make is tapping on Global Issues. Examples of global issues include:

  • "I have low self-esteem."
  • "I'm stressed about money."
  • "My relationship is failing."

In Level 1 Deep Dive, we use the Table Top Analogy. The global issue is the "table top." It is held up by "table legs"‚Äîwhich are specific, one-time events. If you try to tap on the table top, nothing happens. If you tap on the legs (the specific events) until they break, the entire table top collapses.

Pro Tip

Always ask: "If this were a movie scene, what would I see?" If the client can't describe a specific scene with a beginning, middle, and end, you are still too global. Dig deeper into the Targeting phase.

The Sacred Container: Practitioner-Client Safety

Safety is the precursor to healing. For women in their 40s and 50s entering this field, your natural empathy is your greatest asset, but it must be structured. The "Container" consists of:

  1. Physical Safety: Ensuring the client is in a private, quiet space (especially in Zoom sessions).
  2. Emotional Permission: Explicitly stating, "You are in control. We can stop or slow down at any time."
  3. Non-Judgmental Calibration: Observing the client's breathing, skin flush, and eye movements without "fixing" them prematurely.

Managing the Apex Effect

The Apex Effect is a psychological phenomenon where, after a successful shift, the client dismisses the effectiveness of the treatment. They might say, "I guess it just went away on its own" or "It wasn't that big of a deal anyway."

This happens because the neural reconsolidation is so complete that the brain literally "forgets" how the old distress felt. To manage this as a professional:

  • Always get a baseline SUDs score before tapping.
  • Document somatic markers (e.g., "You mentioned your throat felt like it was closing; how is that now?").
  • The "Wait, what?" Moment: When they dismiss the change, gently remind them of their initial intensity. This reinforces the value of your work.
Business Insight

Managing the Apex Effect is crucial for client retention. If they think they "just got better on their own," they won't rebook. If they recognize your method facilitated the shift, they become a client for life.
