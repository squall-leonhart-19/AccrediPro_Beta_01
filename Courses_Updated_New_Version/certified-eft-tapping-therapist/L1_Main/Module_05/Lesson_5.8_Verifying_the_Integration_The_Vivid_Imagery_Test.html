Common Pitfalls in the Integration Phase

Mistake Why it's a Problem The Fix
Ending too abruptly The client leaves in a "floaty" state but has no bridge to reality. Spend at least 10 minutes on Future Pacing and Homework.
Ignoring "Yeah, but..." Unaddressed doubts act as "reverse tapping," bringing the SUDs back up. Tap specifically on the "Fear the change won't last."
Giving too much homework Overwhelms the client, leading to a sense of failure. Suggest ONE 2-minute tapping task per day.
Maya's Practitioner Tip

I once had a client, a former nurse, who was incredibly diligent. I gave her 5 things to do, and she did none of them because she was afraid of doing them 'wrong.' Now, I only ever give one simple integration task. Success breeds success!

Success Markers

How do you know if your Integration phase was successful? Look for these three signs:

  • Cognitive Shift: The client uses different language (e.g., from "I can't" to "I'm learning how to").
  • Physiological Grounding: The client's breathing is deep and rhythmic, and they feel "back in their body" rather than "floaty."
  • Specific Action Plan: The client can name the exact time and place they will apply their new skill or tapping anchor.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of "Future Pacing" in the Integrate phase?

To mentally rehearse a future challenging situation while in a calm, post-tapping state, thereby creating new neural pathways that associate the situation with safety rather than fear.

2. Why should you address a client's "Reversion Anxiety"?

If left unaddressed, the fear that "this won't last" can trigger the amygdala, potentially bringing back the emotional distress the session worked to clear.

3. What is a "SUDs" score of 2 usually indicative of at the end of a session?

It indicates a successful clearing of the primary emotional charge, making it the perfect time to move into the Integration phase.

4. How much homework is recommended for a new client?

One simple, manageable task (like a 2-minute morning tapping sequence) to ensure compliance and build the client's confidence in their own self-healing ability.

Key Takeaways

  • The Integrate phase bridges the gap between the therapy room and the real world.
  • Future Pacing is a non-negotiable tool for solidifying breakthroughs in the client's nervous system.
  • Always look for physiological signs of grounding before ending the session.
  • Keep homework simple; your goal is to empower the client, not overwhelm them.
  • You are becoming a real coach! Your ability to lead this phase with confidence is what separates a professional from an amateur.
