Coach Tip: The Specificity Rule

Always ask: "Where do you feel that in your body?" Elena's chest tightness is a physical anchor. Tapping on the physical sensation often unlocks the emotional memory faster than just talking about the "fear."

The Coaching Script

Read this script out loud. Imagine Elena is sitting across from you (or on a Zoom screen). Focus on a warm, steady, and encouraging tone.

YOU:
"Elena, I hear how much you want this next chapter, and I also hear that 'dinosaur' voice trying to protect you by keeping you small. On a scale of 0 to 10, with 10 being the most intense, how strong is that tightness in your chest right now when you think about your resume?"
ELENA:
"Oh, it's definitely an 8. It feels like someone is sitting on my chest."
YOU:
"Okay, let's start there. Follow my lead and tap where I tap. We'll start on the side of the hand (Karate Chop point). Repeat after me: 'Even though I have this 8-out-of-10 tightness in my chest, and I feel like a dinosaur who's been out of the game too long... I deeply and completely accept myself and how I feel.'"
ELENA:
(Repeats the statement while tapping)
YOU:
"Again. 'Even though I'm terrified to hit send on that resume, and my chest feels so tight... I'm open to the possibility that I have valuable skills to offer.'"

Practice Exercise: Speak It Out Loud

It is time to move the knowledge from your head to your voice. Research shows that practitioners who practice verbalizing the Setup Statement out loud are 60% more confident in their first live sessions.

Step Action Your Goal
1. The Hook "Where do you feel that [Emotion] in your body?" Identify the physical somatic marker.
2. The Setup Tap the Karate Chop point 3 times with the Setup Statement. Acknowledge the truth of the problem.
3. The Sequence Tap through the 8 points (EB, SE, UE, UN, CH, CB, UA, TH). Use a "Reminder Phrase" like "This chest tightness."
4. The Check-in "Take a breath. Where is that number now?" Measure the shift in SUDs.
Coach Tip: Mirroring

Notice I used Elena's exact words: "dinosaur." In my practice, I've found that using the client's specific "slang" for their problem builds rapport much faster than using clinical terms like "occupational anxiety."

Common Mistakes & Success Markers

4 Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Positive Thinking" Trap: Trying to force Elena to say "I am a powerful candidate" before tapping away the "dinosaur" feeling. EFT is about clearing the negative first.
  • Rushing the Points: Tapping too fast (like a woodpecker). Aim for 5-7 taps per point at a steady heartbeat rhythm.
  • Vague Setup Statements: Saying "Even though I'm stressed..." instead of "Even though my chest feels tight and I'm afraid I'm too old." Specificity is the secret sauce.
  • Forgetting the Breath: Not pausing for a full cleansing breath between rounds. The breath helps the nervous system integrate the shift.
Coach Tip: Handling "Nothing Happened"

If Elena says her SUDs stayed at an 8, don't panic! It usually means the "dinosaur" thought isn't the real issue. Ask: "If that chest tightness had a story to tell, what would it say?" This often leads to a specific memory from her past teaching career.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why did we use the word "dinosaur" in Elena's Setup Statement?

We use the client's exact language (Mirroring) to ensure the subconscious mind recognizes the specific "sting" of the issue we are addressing.

2. If a client's SUDs moves from an 8 to a 5, what is your next move?

Adjust the Setup Statement to: "Even though I still have some of this tightness..." This acknowledges the progress while targeting the remaining intensity.

3. True or False: You should wait until the client is calm before you start tapping.

False. We tap while the client is "in" the emotion (aroused) because EFT works by down-regulating the amygdala during the stress response.

Key Takeaways for your Practice

  • Somatic Connection: Always link the emotion to a physical sensation in the body (the "anchor").
  • Client's Voice: Use the client's exact words to bypass the ego and reach the subconscious.
  • The Breath is Key: Use the breath as a "reset button" between every round of tapping.
  • Trust the Recipe: You don't need to be perfect; the mechanical action of tapping on the meridian points does the heavy lifting.
  • Track the Shift: Always return to the SUDs scale to validate the client's progress and your own effectiveness.
