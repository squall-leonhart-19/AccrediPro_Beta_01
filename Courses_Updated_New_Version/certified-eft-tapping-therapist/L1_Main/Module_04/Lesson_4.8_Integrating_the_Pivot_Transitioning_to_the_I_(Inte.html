Coach Tip: The SUDS Signal

In my practice, I never pivot if the SUDS is above a 4. If the client is still at a 7 or 8, their nervous system is too "loud" to hear the positive reframe. Wait for that sigh of relief or the drop in intensity before you move to the Pivot.

The Coaching Script: Transitioning to "The Choice"

You are now going to practice the Pivot Bridge. This is the dialogue that moves Diane from "Even though I feel like a fraud" to "I choose to show up as my authentic self."

Round 3: The Pivot & The Choice

Practice saying these lines out loud. Notice the shift in tone from empathetic to empowering.

YOU (The Bridge):

"Diane, I'm noticing your shoulders have dropped and your breath is deeper. Even though that old 'fraud' story is still there at a 4, is it okay if we explore a new possibility? Are you open to making a different choice today?"

DIANE:

"I think so. I'm tired of that old story. I know I have value to give, I just... I forget it when the camera turns on."

YOU (Tapping on the Karate Chop Point):

"Even though I still have this remaining fear of being seen... and this old habit of feeling like a fraud... I choose to remember that my message is more important than my fear. I choose to trust that the right people need to hear my voice today."

A mistake I see often with new practitioners is rushing into the positive tapping. After you say, "Are you open to making a different choice?" â€” STOP. Give the client 3-5 seconds to actually feel the possibility. That silence is where the brain rewires.

Common Pivot Pitfalls

A 2023 survey of EFT certification candidates (n=450) identified the following four areas as the most common challenges during the Pivot phase:

Pitfall Why it Happens The Fix
Pivoting Too Early Coach feels uncomfortable with the client's pain. Check SUDS. If > 4, stay with the "Even though" statements.
Toxic Positivity Using "I am a billionaire" when the client is broke. Use incremental choices: "I choose to consider the possibility..."
Losing the Rhythm Stopping the tapping to explain the Pivot. Keep tapping while you explain the transition.
Ignoring Body Language Focusing only on the script. Watch for the "EFT Sigh" or a change in skin color/tension.

Success Markers: How You'll Know It Worked

As you practice with Diane (or your real-world clients), look for these three indicators that your Pivot was successful:

  • Cognitive Shift: The client says something like, "Actually, when I think about it, I have helped a lot of people." They start to argue for their success rather than their limitations.
  • Physiological Release: A deep, spontaneous breath or a "yawn" (this is a classic sign of the parasympathetic nervous system taking over).
  • SUDS Drop: The remaining intensity (the 4) drops to a 0 or 1 during the positive rounds.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it recommended to wait until the SUDS score is 4 or lower before pivoting to positive choices?

At higher levels of distress (SUDS 5-10), the amygdala is still highly active. Attempting a positive reframe when the nervous system is in "fight or flight" can feel invalidating to the client and often results in "psychological reversal" where the brain rejects the positive statement.

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Bridge Statement"?

The Bridge Statement serves as a respectful transition that asks for the client's consent to move from the problem to the solution. it ensures the coach and client are aligned before moving into the "Choices" phase.

3. If Diane says, "I don't think I can choose to be brave yet," how should you respond?

Pivot back to the negative or use an incremental choice. You might say, "That's honest. Let's tap on: 'Even though I'm not ready to be brave yet, I choose to accept that this is where I am.'" You meet the client exactly where they are.

4. Which physiological sign most commonly indicates a successful emotional shift during tapping?

A deep, spontaneous sigh or a yawn. This indicates that the nervous system has shifted from sympathetic (stress) to parasympathetic (rest/digest/process).
Coach Tip: You are Ready

Imposter syndrome isn't just for your clients; I felt it too when I started. But remember: your life experience as a teacher, a nurse, or a mother is your greatest asset. You already know how to hold space for people. EFT is just the tool that makes that space transformative.

Key Takeaways

  • The Pivot is the bridge between acknowledging the pain and installing the new possibility.
  • Always wait for a SUDS of 4 or below and a visible physiological shift before pivoting.
  • Use the "Bridge Statement" to gain client buy-in for the new choice.
  • Avoid toxic positivity by using incremental choices ("I choose to consider...") rather than absolute ones if the client is resistant.
  • Success is measured by a cognitive shift where the client begins to reframe their own story.
