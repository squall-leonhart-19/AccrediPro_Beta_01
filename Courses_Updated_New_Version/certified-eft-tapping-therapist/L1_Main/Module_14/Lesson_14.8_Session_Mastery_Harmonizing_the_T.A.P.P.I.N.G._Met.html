Your Coaching Script

Read the following script out loud. Imagine Elena is sitting across from you, looking for your guidance.

YOU (Coach):
"Elena, I can hear how frustrating that meeting was for you. Let's use EFT to take the edge off that intensity. Even though your boss interrupted you and you felt that heat in your chest, you're here now, and you're safe. Let's start tapping on the side of the hand."
YOU (Setup Statement):
"Even though I felt so invisible when Mark interrupted me yesterday, I deeply and completely accept myself. Even though my chest felt tight and I felt like I didn't belong in that room, I'm okay right now. Even though he just moved on like I hadn't said a word, I choose to be calm anyway."
ELENA:
"It just felt so disrespectful. I'm 52 years old, I shouldn't feel like a student in the principal's office again."
YOU (Reminder Phrases):
"Tapping through the points now... This feeling of being invisible... That heat in my chest... He just cut me off... I felt so small... That principal's office feeling... All this disrespect... Tightness in my chest."
YOU (Check-in):
"Take a deep breath, Elena. When you think about that moment he interrupted you now, what's the number from 0 to 10?"
Coach Tip

Notice how we used Elena's exact words ("principal's office"). Using the client's own language creates a deeper neurological connection to the memory, making the tapping more effective.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even seasoned practitioners can fall into these traps. Being aware of them is half the battle.

1
Being Too Global

Tapping on "my work stress" is L1 error #1. Always narrow it down to the specific meeting, the specific person, and the specific second the feeling hit.

2
Leading the Client

Avoid saying, "You must have felt angry." Instead, ask, "What did you feel in that moment?" Let them provide the emotional labels.

3
Forgetting the SUDs

Without a 0-10 score, neither you nor the client can objectively track progress. Always get a number before and after a round.

4
Tapping Too Fast

Give the client time to repeat the phrase and actually feel the sensation. Rushing through points is a sign of practitioner anxiety.

Success Markers

How do you know if your session with "Elena" was successful? Look for these signs:

  • The Shift: Elena sighs, yawns, or her shoulders visibly drop. This indicates a nervous system shift from sympathetic (fight/flight) to parasympathetic (rest/digest).
  • SUDs Reduction: The number moves from an 8 to a 4 or lower.
  • Cognitive Reframing: Elena says something like, "Actually, Mark interrupts everyone, not just me. It's his bad habit, not my lack of skill."
  • New Aspects: Elena remembers another time this happened, which gives you a new "doorway" to work through.
Maya's Insight

Don't panic if the SUDs number goes UP. This often means the client is becoming more present to the emotion rather than numbing it. Just keep tapping!

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use Elena's phrase "principal's office" rather than just saying "you felt like a kid"?

Using the client's exact words ensures we are targeting their specific neurological encoding of the memory. Our own interpretations (paraphrasing) might not resonate with their subconscious experience as effectively.

2. What should you do if Elena's SUDs score stays at an 8 after two rounds of tapping?

You should check for "specificity." Are we tapping on the meeting (global) or the exact moment of interruption (specific)? Also, check for "aspects"â€”perhaps the heat in her chest has moved to a lump in her throat.

3. Elena starts crying during the tapping. What is your immediate next step?

Continue tapping! Crying is a form of emotional release. Stay calm, maintain a warm presence, and keep tapping through the points until she reaches a state of relative calm.

4. Which L1 technique is best for a client who is too overwhelmed to talk about the event?

The "Sneaking Up" technique or simply tapping on the physical sensations (e.g., "this tightness") without mentioning the story until the intensity lowers.
