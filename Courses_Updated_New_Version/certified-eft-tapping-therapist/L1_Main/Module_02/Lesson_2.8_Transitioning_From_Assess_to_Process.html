The Coaching Script

Read the following script out loud. Pay attention to the bolded transition phrasesâ€”these are designed to establish you as a Certified EFT Therapist while maintaining a warm connection.

Coach (You):
"Elena, I'm so glad we're connecting today. I've reviewed your intake form, but I'd love to hear in your own wordsâ€”when you sit down to send a proposal and that 'fluttery' feeling starts, what is the specific thought that goes through your mind?"
Elena:
"It's just... I think, 'Who am I to charge this much?' I feel like a fraud, even though I've been doing this for twenty years in corporate."
Coach (You):
"That 'Who am I' voice is very common during big transitions. Based on my experience working with women in career pivots, that feeling in your chest is actually a safety response. Your brain is trying to protect you from a perceived risk. Does that resonate with you?"
Elena:
"Yes! It feels like if I send the proposal, I'm 'out there' and people can judge me."
Coach (You):
"Exactly. So, our goal in our work together isn't just to 'push through' the fear, but to use EFT to reprogram that safety response. When we clear the emotional charge, the proposals become just another task, rather than a threat. Shall we try a brief assessment tap to see how your body responds?"
Coach Tip

Avoid the "interrogation" style. Instead of asking "Why do you feel that?", use the phrase "What is the specific thought...?" This helps the client find the Target for tapping without getting lost in a long story.

Practice Exercise: Speak it Out Loud

Application is the bridge between knowing and doing. Perform the following steps now:

  1. The Mirror Test: Stand in front of a mirror. Practice the "Transition Statement" from the script: "Based on my experience working with women in career pivots..." Say it three times until it feels natural and authoritative.
  2. Vocal Tone Check: Record yourself saying the final line of the script on your phone. Listen back. Do you sound like a peer or a professional? Aim for a "Calm Authority" toneâ€”warm but steady.
  3. The "SUDs" Check: Practice asking for the intensity level. Out loud, say: "On a scale of 0 to 10, where 10 is the most intense, how strong is that fluttery feeling in your stomach right now as we talk about it?"

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps during the Assessment phase. Watch for these in your own practice:

  • The "Fix-It" Rush: Trying to start tapping before you've actually identified the specific emotional target.
  • Over-Explaining: Spending 20 minutes explaining the science of the amygdala instead of letting the client talk.
  • Missing the Body: Forgetting to ask where the client feels the emotion in their physical body.
  • The "Why" Trap: Asking "Why do you feel that?" which leads to intellectualizing, rather than "How do you know you're feeling that?" which leads to the sensation.
Coach Tip

If a client starts telling a 10-minute story, gently interrupt with: "Hold that thought for just a secondâ€”I want to make sure I catch the exact feeling you had in that moment." This is called Protective Interruption, and it's a vital skill for a Certified EFT Therapist.

Success Markers

How do you know your Assessment was successful? Look for these three signs:

Marker What it Looks Like
The "Aha" Breath The client takes a deep, spontaneous sigh when you name the core issue.
Physical Location The client can pinpoint exactly where the feeling lives (e.g., "A heavy stone in my throat").
Specific Target You have a clear "Setup Statement" ready (e.g., "Even though I have this 'Who am I' fear in my chest...").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask Elena where she feels the "fraud" sensation in her body?

EFT is a somatic (body-based) therapy. By identifying the physical location, we anchor the emotional work in the nervous system, making the tapping more effective and measurable.

2. What is the danger of asking Elena "Why" she feels like a fraud?

Asking "Why" encourages the client to go into their "thinking brain" (prefrontal cortex). EFT works best when we stay in the "feeling brain" (limbic system). We want the sensation, not the theory.

3. Elena says, "I've always been this way." How do you handle this as a coach?

Use a transition: "I hear that it feels like a permanent part of you. In EFT, we view that as a 'Global' statement. Let's look for one specific time this week when that feeling was strongest so we have a place to start."

4. What is the primary goal of the "Assess" phase in the Discovery Call?

To identify a specific emotional target and its physical sensation, while establishing your authority as a practitioner who understands the root cause.

Key Takeaways

  • Assessment is an intervention: Simply asking the right questions begins to shift the client's perspective.
  • Specifics are gold: "General" tapping gets "general" results. Always hunt for the specific thought and sensation.
  • Authority + Empathy: Use transition statements to show you've seen this before and know the way out.
  • The Body Never Lies: Always ground the assessment in the client's physical experience (SUDs and location).
