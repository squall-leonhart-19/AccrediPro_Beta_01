The SUDs Plateau: When the Needle Won't Move

In a standard EFT session, we expect to see the Subjective Units of Distress (SUDs) decline with each round. However, clinical data suggests that in approximately 15-20% of cases, the score remains static after the initial rounds. This is rarely because "tapping doesn't work" for that client; rather, it indicates that the Process has encountered a neurological or psychological roadblock.

Coach Tip: Professional Confidence

When SUDs don't move, your client will look to you for a reaction. If you look panicked, they will lose safety. Instead, smile and say, "That‚Äôs interesting! The Apex Effect: Cognitive Denial of Change

The Apex Effect is a fascinating phenomenon where a client experiences significant relief but cognitively dismisses the tapping as the cause. They may say, "I just feel better because I'm tired," or "It probably wasn't that big of a deal to begin with."

This happens because the brain attempts to resolve the Cognitive Dissonance between having a lifelong problem and it suddenly vanishing. To troubleshoot this:

  • Calibration: Remind the client of their physical state 10 minutes ago (e.g., "Remember how your hands were shaking?").
  • Validation: Acknowledge that the change feels "weird" or "impossible."
  • Testing: Ask the client to try to get the feeling back. If they can't, the Apex Effect is confirmed.

Psychological Reversal (PR): The Subconscious Handbrake

Psychological Reversal is a state where the subconscious mind perceives that it is safer to keep the problem than to solve it. This is often linked to Secondary Gain.

Type of Block Underlying Belief Troubleshooting Strategy
Safety Block "If I get over this, I'll be vulnerable." Add "It's safe to let this go" to the Setup.
Identity Block "Who am I without this struggle?" Tap on the fear of the unknown.
Deserving Block "I don't deserve to be happy." Focus on self-forgiveness phrases.
Coach Tip: Income Potential

Practitioners who master troubleshooting Psychological Reversal often command fees of $200-$350 per session. Why? Because you become the "specialist" who succeeds with clients who have failed with traditional talk therapy or general life coaching.

The Specificity Problem: Narrowing the Target

If the SUDs aren't moving, the target is likely too Global. In Module 1, we learned that "The Art of Specificity" is key. If you are tapping on "This anxiety," and it's not moving, you must narrow the focus.

The "Zoom In" Technique:

  1. Time: Don't tap on "the car accident." Tap on "the sound of the glass breaking."
  2. Sensory: What is the exact color, smell, or sound?
  3. Internal Dialogue: What is the exact sentence you are telling yourself? (e.g., "I'm going to die" vs. "This is embarrassing").

Chasing the Pain: Following the Somatic Shift

Sometimes the SUDs stay the same because the emotion has moved, but the client is still reporting the score for the original location. This is called Chasing the Pain.

Example:
Round 1: "Tightness in my chest" (SUDs 8)
Round 2: "The chest feels fine, but now my throat feels constricted" (SUDs 8)

The client says "It's still an 8," but the target has changed. You must pivot the Reminder Phrase immediately to the new sensation: "This constriction in my throat."

Coach Tip: Somatic Awareness

Always ask: "Is that 8 still in your chest, or has it moved?" This simple question prevents 15 minutes of wasted tapping on a target that has already cleared.

Advanced Setup Adjustments

For stubborn issues, the standard "I deeply and completely accept myself" might trigger a "Tail-Ender" (an internal 'Yeah, right!'). Try these modifications:

  • The "Possibility" Setup: "Even though I have this block, I am open to the possibility of letting it go."
  • The "Neutral" Setup: "Even though I have this fear, I accept that this is what's happening right now."
  • The "Choice" Setup: "Even though I've had this for 20 years, I choose to consider a different way of feeling."

Check Your Understanding

1. A client says their SUDs is still a 7, but you notice they are laughing and their breathing has deepened. What is likely occurring?

This is the Apex Effect. The client has experienced a shift but their conscious mind is denying the change or attributing it to something else.

2. What should you do if the SUDs score stays at a 5 for three consecutive rounds of tapping?

You should Narrow the Target. The issue is likely too global. Break the event down into smaller "movie scenes" or specific sensory details.

3. Define 'Chasing the Pain' in the context of an EFT session.

It is the process of following a somatic sensation as it moves or changes quality (e.g., from a 'sharp pain in the back' to a 'dull ache in the shoulder').

4. When is a "Possibility Setup" more effective than the standard Setup Statement?

When the client has a strong "Tail-Ender" or resistance to the phrase "I deeply and completely accept myself," making the standard statement feel like a lie.

Key Takeaways

  • Stalled progress is a diagnostic tool, not a failure of the method.
  • The Apex Effect requires the practitioner to use "Calibration" to prove the shift to the client.
  • Psychological Reversal is often a safety mechanism that requires a modified Setup Statement.
  • Specificity is the "antidote" to the SUDs plateau; always zoom in on sensory details.
  • "Chasing the Pain" ensures you are always tapping on the active neurological signal.
