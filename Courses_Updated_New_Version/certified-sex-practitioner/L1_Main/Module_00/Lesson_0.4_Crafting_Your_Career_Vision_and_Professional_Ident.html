Legal Safety & Your Practice

Legitimacy comes from structure. To move from "hobbyist" to "professional," you must treat your practice like a business. This protects you and gives your clients confidence in your services.

  • Professional Service Agreements: Never start a session without a signed contract that clearly states you are providing coaching and education, not medical treatment.
  • Professional Liability Insurance: Even as a coach, you need "Errors and Omissions" insurance. It is surprisingly affordable (often under $200/year) and provides massive peace of mind.
  • LLC Formation: Separating your personal assets from your business assets is a standard move for any practitioner earning a professional income.

The Ethics of Intimacy

Working in the realm of sexuality requires a higher standard of ethics than almost any other coaching niche. We follow the AccrediPro Ethical Code, which includes:

  1. Strict Confidentiality: What is said in the "sacred space" stays there, unless there is a risk of harm to self or others.
  2. No Physical Contact: As a practitioner, your work is verbal and educational. We maintain a "hands-off" policy to ensure the safety of both practitioner and client.
  3. Unconditional Positive Regard: We hold a non-judgmental space for all sexual orientations, gender identities, and relationship structures.

When to Refer Out

Knowing when to refer out is a sign of expertise, not weakness. You should have a "referral network" of local or virtual professionals. Refer out immediately if a client presents with:

  • Active suicidal ideation or self-harm
  • Severe, untreated mental health disorders (Bipolar, Schizophrenia, etc.)
  • Acute physical pain that hasn't been cleared by a medical doctor
  • Severe addiction issues that interfere with the coaching process
