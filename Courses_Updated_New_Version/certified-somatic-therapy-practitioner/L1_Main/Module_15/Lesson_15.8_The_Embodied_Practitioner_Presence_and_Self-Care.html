Step 6: Common New Practitioner Mistakes

As you launch, watch out for these four common pitfalls that I see new coaches make:

  1. Over-Explaining the Science: You don't need to teach a biology lesson. Just help the client feel the shift.
  2. Under-Pricing Your Services: Somatic work is a specialized, high-impact modality. Pricing yourself at $50/hour signals "amateur." Professional practitioners typically start at $150-$250/session.
  3. Ignoring Your Own Regulation: You cannot lead a client where you haven't gone. Practice your own somatic tools before every session.
  4. Waiting for "Perfection": Your practice will evolve. The best way to learn is by working with real bodies.
