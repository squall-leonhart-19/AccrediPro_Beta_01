When a client like Elena "freezes," it's not a lack of willpower. It's her Dorsal Vagal system trying to protect her from the perceived danger of failure. Our job is to invite the body back into safety, one micro-movement at a time.

The Coaching Script: Navigating the Freeze

Practice these lines out loud. Notice the pacingâ€”allow for at least 3 seconds of silence where indicated.

Elena: "I just don't get it. I know I want this business. I know I'm capable. But every time I sit down, I feel like I'm made of lead. I feel so lazy."
You: "Elena, I want to stop you right there. 'Lazy' is a label the mind uses when it doesn't understand the body. What if we looked at that 'lead' feeling not as laziness, but as a very loyal protector?"
Elena: (Pauses, looks down) "A protector? It's protecting me from succeeding."
You: "It might be protecting you from the *vulnerability* of being seen. Let's try something. As you feel that 'lead' in your limbs right now... can you just notice where it's heaviest? Don't try to change it. Just acknowledge it's there."
Elena: "It's in my shoulders. They feel like they're being pulled down."
You: "Good noticing. Let's offer those shoulders a micro-movement. What if you let them drop just one millimeter more? Let the lead be heavy. I'm right here with you." (Wait 5 seconds)

Common Pitfalls in Advanced Integration

The Mistake Why It Happens The Somatic Correction
"Why" Questions We want to help the client understand the "root" intellectually. Switch to "How" or "Where". (e.g., "How does that feel in your chest?")
Rushing the Silence Silence feels awkward or like "nothing is happening." Trust the Neurobiological Processing. Silence is where the shift happens.
Fixing the Symptom Imposter syndrome makes us feel we must "solve" the tension immediately. Titrate. Work with 5% of the tension, not 100%.
Coach Tip

If you feel your own heart rate rising when a client is stuck, that's Counter-Transference. Gently press your feet into the floor and notice the chair supporting you. Your groundedness is the client's greatest resource.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful, even if the client still feels some tension?

  • The Physiological Sigh: The client takes a spontaneous, deep breath followed by a long exhale.
  • Softening of the Eyes: The "thousand-yard stare" of the freeze response shifts to a present, softer gaze.
  • Increased Agency: The client moves from "I am stuck" to "I notice my body is feeling stuck." (This is Self-Observation).
  • Skin Color Shift: You see a return of color to the face as the blood flows back from the core to the extremities.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is asking "Why do you think you feel frozen?" less effective in a somatic session?

"Why" questions pull the client out of their felt sense and into their analytical mind (Prefrontal Cortex). Somatic integration happens in the "lower" brain centers (Limbic/Brainstem). We want to stay with the *experience*, not the *explanation*.

2. What is the primary goal of "Titration" during a deep dive?

The goal is to break down overwhelming sensations into small, manageable "bites." This prevents the nervous system from becoming flooded (re-traumatized) and allows the client to build "somatic capacity."

3. If Elena starts to shake or tremble slightly during the session, what should you do?

Usually, this is a "discharge" of trapped energy. You should normalize it by saying, "It's okay to let that movement happen. Your body is just releasing some old energy. I'm right here." Monitor her to ensure she stays present.

Key Takeaways

  • Resistance is a biological protection, not a personal failure or "laziness."
  • Your primary tool in a deep dive is your own regulated nervous system (Co-regulation).
  • Success is measured by physiological shifts (breathing, softening), not just intellectual "aha" moments.
  • Small, slow movements (titration) lead to faster, permanent integration than big, forced breakthroughs.
  • You don't need to have all the answers; you just need to have the presence to stay with the client's "now."
