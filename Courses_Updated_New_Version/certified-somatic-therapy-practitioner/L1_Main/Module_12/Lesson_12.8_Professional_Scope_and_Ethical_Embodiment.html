Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Rushing the Release: Trying to get the client to a "10 out of 10" relaxation too fast. This can cause a "rebound" of anxiety.
  • Intellectualizing: Asking the client "Why do you think you feel this way?" This pulls them out of their body and into their head.
  • Missing the Signs: Ignoring a client's "yawn" or "sigh." These are signs of the nervous system shifting from Sympathetic to Parasympathetic.
  • Over-Talking: Somatic work requires silence. Give the client's brain time to process the new bodily data.
Maya's Practitioner Tip

If a client starts to cry, don't rush for the tissues. Tissues are often a "stop signal" for emotions. Just stay present, keep your own breath steady, and say: "I'm right here with you. Let that move through."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why did the coach ask Elena to focus on her feet when she was feeling "buzzing" in her chest?

This is the technique of Pendulation. By moving the attention to a "resource" (the feet/floor), we prevent the nervous system from becoming overwhelmed by the "activation" (the chest buzzing).

2. What is a physical marker that Elena's nervous system is beginning to "settle"?

In the script, Elena took a spontaneous deeper breath. Other markers include softening of the eyes, swallowing, stomach gurgling, or a change in skin color (flushing or evening out).

3. A 2021 study on somatic interventions found that titration reduces what specific risk in trauma-informed coaching?

It reduces the risk of re-traumatization or "flooding," where the client becomes so overwhelmed by bodily sensations that they shut down or panic.

4. Why should you avoid asking "Why" during a somatic tracking session?

"Why" is an analytical question that activates the prefrontal cortex. Somatic therapy focuses on the bottom-up process (body to brain). We want to stay in the "felt sense," not the "thought sense."

Lab Key Takeaways

  • Titrate Everything: Small steps lead to big shifts. Never rush a client's bodily process.
  • Pendulation is a Safety Valve: Always have a "resource" (like the feet or a happy memory) ready if a sensation becomes too intense.
  • Your Voice is a Tool: Use a calm, melodic prosody to signal safety to the client's amygdala.
  • Watch for "Glimmers": Small sighs, eye flutters, and deeper breaths are the "gold" of somatic coaching‚Äîthey prove the method is working.
