Presenting Concern

Jordan is experiencing "profound burnout." They feel like they are failing at their career and their health. They describe their brain as a "browser with 50 tabs open," and they've stopped exercising because the gym feels "overstimulating and performative."

Intersectionality Context

As a Black non-binary person, Jordan often feels they have to "mask" both their neurodivergence and their gender identity to be taken seriously at work. This double-masking is exhausting. They want wellness, but most wellness spaces feel "white, thin, and neurotypical."

Coaching Goal

To find a way to move their body and eat well that doesn't feel like "another chore" or a "sensory nightmare."

Coach Tip

When working with neurodivergent clients, traditional "discipline-based" coaching often fails. Instead, focus on dopamine-based strategiesâ€”finding what feels rewarding and sensory-safe.

Your Coaching Script

Practice these words out loud. Notice how the coach validates the intersectional experience before moving to goal-setting.

YOU:
"Jordan, I hear how much energy it takes just to show up every day when you're managing burnout, navigating a world not built for your neurotype, and doing the work of self-advocacy as a Black non-binary person. That is a lot for one human to carry."
JORDAN:
"It really is. I feel like I'm constantly performing. Even 'wellness' feels like a performance I'm failing."
YOU:
"What if we took 'performance' off the table entirely? In this space, we don't have to follow neurotypical rules. If the gym feels overstimulating, that's not a failureâ€”it's valuable sensory data. What would movement look like if it was designed purely for your comfort and your joy?"
JORDAN:
"I honestly don't know. I've always been told I just need to be more 'disciplined' with a 5 AM workout."
YOU:
"The '5 AM discipline' model was built for a very specific type of person, and it often ignores how ADHD brains work. Let's look at what gives you a 'dopamine hit' instead. What's one thing you do where you feel completely at ease in your body?"
Coach Tip

Notice the use of the term "Sensory Data." This reframe helps the client stop seeing their sensitivity as a weakness and start seeing it as a biological reality to be navigated.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "discipline" model often harmful for a client like Jordan?

It ignores the reality of executive dysfunction in ADHD brains and the added exhaustion (allostatic load) of masking multiple marginalized identities. It frames a biological/systemic struggle as a personal moral failure.

2. What does "double-masking" refer to in Jordan's context?

Double-masking refers to the simultaneous effort of hiding neurodivergent traits (to appear neurotypical) and hiding or "softening" gender identity or cultural expression (to appear "professional" in white/cis-centric spaces).

3. How can a coach help a client move from "failure" to "data"?

By reframing a "failed" attempt (like not going to the gym) as a sensory or executive function mismatch. The coach asks: "What did we learn about that environment that didn't work for your brain?"

4. What is a "dopamine-based" wellness strategy?

A strategy that focuses on activities the client finds inherently stimulating, interesting, or pleasurable, rather than activities they feel they "should" do. This works with the ADHD brain's natural reward system.

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Validation First: Always acknowledge the systemic and biological barriers a client faces before moving to action.
  • Sensory Safety: For neurodivergent clients, the environment (light, sound, crowd) is often more important than the exercise itself.
  • Intersectional Awareness: A client's race and gender identity significantly impact their available energy and safety in wellness spaces.
  • Neuro-Affirming Tools: Replace "discipline" with "dopamine" and "consistency" with "flexibility."
