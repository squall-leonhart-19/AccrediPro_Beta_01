Common Coaching Mistakes

Even experienced coaches can stumble when working with late-blooming LGBTQ+ clients. Watch out for these four pitfalls:

  • Rushing the "Coming Out" Phase: Don't push a client to come out before they are ready. Authenticity is a marathon, not a sprint.
  • Toxic Positivity: Avoid phrases like "Everything will be fine!" Instead, use: "I am here with you, regardless of the outcome."
  • Over-Identifying: If you are also a late-bloomer, be careful not to make the session about your story. Keep the focus on the client's unique journey.
  • Ignoring the Body: Authenticity isn't just a mental concept; it's a physical state. If the client is disconnected from their body, they cannot manifest their truth.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address Elena's "vice-like" chest sensation before discussing her family?

Addressing somatic (body) sensations first helps regulate the client's nervous system. A client in a "fight or flight" state cannot think clearly or manifest authentic actions. We must move them from survival mode to a state of safety.

2. What is the danger of a coach telling Elena, "Don't worry, your kids will definitely understand"?

This is toxic positivity and false reassurance. We cannot guarantee how others will react. Instead, we should coach her to be okay with herself regardless of their reaction, which is the core of true authenticity.

3. How does the "Manifesting Authenticity" framework differ from traditional goal-setting?

Traditional goal-setting is often about external achievements. Manifesting Authenticity is about internal alignmentâ€”ensuring that the client's outward life is a true reflection of their inward identity.

4. If Elena says she feels "too old," what is the most affirming coaching response?

Validate the feeling without agreeing with the premise. Use language like: "I hear that fear of lost time. At the same time, your wisdom at 52 is a powerful asset in this journey. What does this 'older, wiser' Elena know that her younger self didn't?"
