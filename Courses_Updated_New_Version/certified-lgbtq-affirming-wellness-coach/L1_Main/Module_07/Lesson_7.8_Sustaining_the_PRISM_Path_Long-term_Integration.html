Common Mistakes to Avoid

New coaches often stumble in these four areas when dealing with authenticity and advocacy:

  • Rushing to Advocacy: Trying to push a client into community leadership before they feel safe in their own skin. Wait for the client's internal "yes."
  • The "Expert" Trap: Feeling like you need to know every queer term or historical event. Your expertise is in the coaching process, not the client's specific identity labels.
  • Dismissing "Late-Bloomer" Grief: Telling a client "Age is just a number!" when they are actually grieving lost decades. Validate the grief before moving to manifesting.
  • Over-Identifying: If you are also a late-bloomer, be careful not to make the session about your story. Use your experience to inform your empathy, not to dominate the dialogue.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary reason Elena feels like a "fraud" in her advocacy?

Identity Lag/Late-Bloomer Grief. She feels she lacks the "time-in-community" to be a legitimate leader, despite having decades of life and leadership experience.

2. Which coaching technique was used to help Elena see her 52 years as an asset?

3. Why is it important to validate Elena's "tightness" in her body?

Somatic Awareness. LGBTQ+ clients often carry trauma and "closeting" stress in their bodies. Acknowledging it creates a safe space for the nervous system to regulate.

4. What is a "Micro-Action" for a client like Elena?

A small, authentic step toward advocacy, such as writing a single blog paragraph or choosing a blog name, rather than launching the entire site at once.
