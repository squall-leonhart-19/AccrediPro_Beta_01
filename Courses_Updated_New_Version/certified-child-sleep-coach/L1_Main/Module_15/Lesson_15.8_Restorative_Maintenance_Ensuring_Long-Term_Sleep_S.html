The Final Client Simulation: The "Confidence Close"

In my practice, I’ve found that the hardest part for new coaches isn't the sleep science—it's the moment they have to say their price and ask for the commitment. Let's practice that now.

Scenario: The Discovery Call Wrap-Up

Client Profile: Diane (49), a former HR Manager and mom to 8-month-old Leo. Leo wakes 6 times a night. Diane is exhausted and skeptical but desperate for help. You have just finished explaining your 3-week "Restorative Sleep Journey" package.

The Objective: Diane asks, "It sounds great, but I've read so many books... how do I know this will actually work for us?"

Your Coaching Script: The Value Affirmation

Read this out loud. Feel the weight and authority of your words.

DIANE:

"It sounds great, but I've read so many books... how do I know this will actually work for us?"

YOU (Warm and Steady):

"Diane, I completely understand that skepticism. The difference between a book and what we’re going to do together is customization and support. Books can't see how Leo reacts to a schedule change, and they can't support you when you're tired at 2:00 AM. Based on the intake forms you've already filled out, I can see exactly where the biological 'sleep pressure' is dropping off for Leo. We aren't going to guess; we are going to use a proven framework to align his biology with his environment. Within 72 hours, most of my clients see a 50% reduction in night wakings. Are you ready to get started so we can get you both sleeping through the night by next weekend?"

Sarah's Pro Tip

Notice I didn't defend the price. I defended the outcome. When you speak to the biological "why" (sleep pressure, circadian rhythms), you move from being a "helper" to being a "specialist." Specialists get paid premium rates.

Certification Exam: Format & Prep

You’ve done the work. The exam is simply a formal validation of your expertise. Here is what to expect:

  • Format: 20 Multiple Choice Questions.
  • Passing Score: 80% (16/20 correct).
  • Focus Areas: Sleep Cycles (NREM/REM), Safe Sleep Guidelines (AAP), Circadian Rhythms, and Coaching Boundaries.
  • Duration: 45 minutes (most students finish in 20).

Final Exam Prep Check

1. What is the most critical factor in ensuring a client's success during the first 72 hours of a sleep plan?

Consistency and Parental Confidence. Your role as a coach is to provide the "emotional scaffolding" so the parent doesn't revert to old habits when the child protests the change.

2. True or False: You should wait until you have a perfect website before booking your first client.

False. Use the DFY Email Scripts and your personal network. Most coaches book their first 3 clients through word-of-mouth or social media before their website is even live.
