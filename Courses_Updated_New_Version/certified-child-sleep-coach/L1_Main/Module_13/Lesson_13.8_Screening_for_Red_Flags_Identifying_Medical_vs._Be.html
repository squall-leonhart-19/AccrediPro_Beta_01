Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Mistake Why It Happens The Professional Correction
Over-Apologizing Imposter syndrome makes you feel "bad" for charging or setting rules. Use declarative statements: "This is the plan that will get Jack sleeping."
Giving Too Much Advice Early Wanting to prove your value immediately. Listen for 15 minutes before offering a single solution.
Ignoring the Parent's Psychology Focusing only on the child's sleep cycles. Address Karen's guilt and exhaustion directly.
Vague Timelines Fear of being "wrong" about when it will work. Provide a range: "Most families see a shift in 4-7 nights if we stay consistent."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address Karen's guilt before giving sleep advice?

Guilt creates a "stress barrier" that prevents parents from being consistent. If Karen feels like a 'bad mom' for setting boundaries, she will likely give in to Jack's protests, causing the plan to fail.

2. What does Sarah mean by being a "Sturdy Leader"?

A sturdy leader is a coach or parent who remains calm, confident, and consistent even when the child is upset. It shows the child they are safe, even though things are changing.

3. A client says, "I've tried everything and nothing works." What is the best coaching response?

"I hear how frustrating that is. Usually, when 'everything' hasn't worked, it's because the pieces weren't put together in the right order or for long enough. We are going to look at the whole puzzle, not just the pieces."

4. What is the primary goal of the "Discovery Phase" of a consultation?

To establish trust, identify the root causes (biological and behavioral), and demonstrate that you have a clear, professional path to the solution.

Key Takeaways

  • Empathy + Authority: The "Golden Ratio" for successful coaching is being 70% empathetic and 30% authoritative.
  • Root Cause Identification: Always distinguish between biological timing issues (circadian rhythm) and behavioral associations (snuggle trap).
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Speaking your scripts out loud builds the "muscle memory" needed to handle high-stress client calls.
  • High Value: Complex toddler cases are premium services. Your ability to navigate these makes you a top-tier specialist.
