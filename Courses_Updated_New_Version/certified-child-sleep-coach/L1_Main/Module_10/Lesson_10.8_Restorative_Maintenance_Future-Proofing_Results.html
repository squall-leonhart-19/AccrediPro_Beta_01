Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it vital to validate Elena's feelings before explaining Leo's biology?

A shamed or stressed parent is in "fight or flight" mode, which makes it cognitively difficult for them to absorb new information or follow a complex plan. Validation lowers their cortisol, allowing them to actually "hear" your coaching.

2. What is "reconnection seeking" in the context of a 2-year-old?

It is the biological drive for a child to stay awake or wake up at night to seek the physical and emotional proximity of a primary caregiver, often triggered by a change in daycare or a parent returning to work.

3. How should you handle a parent who asks for a "quick fix" like sleep medication?

Acknowledge the desperation, but pivot to the root cause. Explain that your role is to create sustainable, long-term sleep health by adjusting the biological clock and environment, rather than using temporary sedatives.

4. What is the "Exhale Moment" in a coaching call?

It is a physical and emotional indicator that the client feels heard, validated, and safe. It marks the transition from the parent being "the problem" to the parent being "the partner" in the solution.
