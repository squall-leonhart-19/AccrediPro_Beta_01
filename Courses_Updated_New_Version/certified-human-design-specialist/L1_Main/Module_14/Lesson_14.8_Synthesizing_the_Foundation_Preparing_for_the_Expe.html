Common Mistakes to Avoid

The Mistake Why It Happens The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Insecurity; wanting to prove you know the "science." Give the client one small "experiment" to try this week.
Using Too Much Jargon Forgetting the client doesn't know what a "Defined G-Center" is. Translate the chart into "Life Language" (e.g., "Your sense of direction").
Fixing Instead of Coaching The desire to be a "hero" for the client. Ask: "How does that feel in your body?" instead of "You should do X."
Ignoring the "Not-Self" Focusing only on the positive traits. Gently point out the Bitterness or Frustration as a helpful navigation tool.

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena is a Projector with Splenic Authority. What is the key characteristic of her decision-making process?

Splenic Authority is an instantaneous, "in the moment" hit of intuition. Unlike Emotional Authority, there is no "clarity over time." She needs to trust her immediate gut instinct when an invitation arrives.

2. If Elena feels "Bitter" after a networking event, what does this tell you as her coach?

Bitterness is the Not-Self theme for a Projector. It indicates she was likely initiating, trying to force a result, or sharing her wisdom without being invited/recognized first.

3. Why is it important to ask Elena about her corporate past during a Human Design session?

To identify conditioning. Most Projectors in corporate roles have survived by mimicking Generators. Understanding her past helps her "decondition" and see that her previous success came at a high biological cost (burnout).

4. What is a practical "experiment" you could give Elena to test her Strategy?

Ask her to stop cold-calling for one week and instead spend that time perfecting her craft or posting one piece of valuable content, then notice who reaches out to HER.
