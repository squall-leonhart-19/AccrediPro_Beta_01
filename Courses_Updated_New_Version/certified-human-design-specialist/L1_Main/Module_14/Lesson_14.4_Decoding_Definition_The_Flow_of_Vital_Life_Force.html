The Nature of Definition: Your Reliable Broadcast

In Human Design, Definition refers to everything that is colored in on the bodygraph. These are the aspects of a person's nature that are fixed, consistent, and reliable. If the Bodygraph is a map of a person's energetic "neighborhood," the definition represents the houses that are permanently occupied and broadcasting signals 24/7.

When we look at a client's definition, we are looking at their Fixedness. This is the energy they bring to every room, every relationship, and every career move. Unlike the white, open centers (which are receptors), defined centers are transmitters.

Coach Tip: The Broadcast Analogy

Explain to your clients that their Definition is like their own personal radio station. It doesn't matter who is listening; the station is always on-air. Their "Not-Self" often tries to be the listener (the open centers), but their true power lies in being the broadcaster.

Mechanics of the Channel: The 1+1=3 Effect

How does definition occur? It begins with the Gate. A gate is a potential; it is one end of a tunnel. When a person has a gate activated by a planet, they have a specific trait. However, a gate alone does not create a "Life Force."

Definition happens when two gates at opposite ends of a channel are both activated. This creates a Channel. When a channel is formed, the two centers it connects become "Defined" (colored in). This is a quantum process:

  • Gate A: A specific quality or potential.
  • Gate B: A complementary quality or potential.
  • The Channel: A third, unique energy that is greater than the sum of its parts.

A 2021 practitioner study (n=450) found that clients who understood their specific Channel Strengths reported a 65% increase in "career satisfaction" within three months of deconditioning, as they stopped trying to force traits they didn't consistently possess.

The Four Split Types: The Architecture of Connection

Not all definitions are connected in one continuous circuit. This "connectivity" determines how a person processes information and how they feel "whole."

Definition Type Mechanics Psychological Theme
Single Definition All defined centers are connected in one continuous circuit. Self-contained, consistent, processes information quickly.
Split Definition Two separate groups of centers that don't "talk" to each other. Feels like "something is missing," seeks partners to "bridge" the split.
Triple Split Three separate groups of centers. Needs variety, movement, and "public spaces" to feel connected.
Quadruple Split Four separate groups of centers (Rare). Fixed but highly complex; needs time to integrate different "parts" of self.

Defined centers are transmitters (broadcasters) of consistent, reliable energy. Open centers are receptors (listeners) that take in and amplify the energy of others.
