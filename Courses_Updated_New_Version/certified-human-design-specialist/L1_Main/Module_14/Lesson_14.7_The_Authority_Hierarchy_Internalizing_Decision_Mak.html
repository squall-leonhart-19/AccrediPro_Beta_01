The Hierarchy of Authority

In Human Design, Inner Authority is determined by which centers are defined (colored in) in the bodygraph. There is a strict mechanical hierarchy. If a higher-ranking center is defined, it becomes the Authority, regardless of what else is defined.

Priority Authority Type Center Responsible Key Principle
1 Emotional Solar Plexus Wait for clarity; no truth in the now.
2 Sacral Sacral Center Respond in the moment (uh-huh/un-un).
3 Splenic Spleen Center Spontaneous, quiet whisper; only happens once.
4 Ego (Heart) Ego Center What do I want? What is my heart in?
5 Self-Projected G Center Wait to hear yourself speak the truth.

Step I: Internalize Authority

The "I" in our D.E.S.I.G.N. Framework‚Ñ¢ stands for Internalize Authority. This is the process of moving the "Driver" seat of the life vehicle from the Ajna/Head (Mind) to the specific Authority center identified in the chart.

For the 40-55 year old professional woman, this is often a deconditioning process. She has likely been rewarded her entire life for being "rational" and "logical." Internalizing Authority requires her to value her somatic intelligence over her intellectual data. As a Specialist, you are teaching her to listen to a new language‚Äîthe language of her body's frequency.

Emotional Authority: The Wave

Approximately 50% of the population has Emotional Authority. For these clients, the mantra is: "There is no truth in the now." Because the Solar Plexus is a motor that operates in a wave (highs and lows), a decision made at either end of the spectrum is likely to be incorrect.

Coaching the Emotional Wave:

  • The High: Everything looks "pink" and perfect. The client wants to jump in. Your Role: Remind them to wait.
  • The Low: Everything looks "gray" and impossible. The client wants to quit. Your Role: Remind them to wait.
  • Clarity: Not a "Eureka!" moment, but a calm, neutral sense of "I know what to do." It is about 80% certainty, never 100%.
Coach Tip

üí° When a client with Emotional Authority feels pressured to decide, give them this script: "This sounds wonderful/interesting. I need to sleep on it to make sure I can give it my full energy." This honors their mechanics and prevents "buyer's remorse."

Sacral Authority: The Gut

Sacral Authority is only found in Generators and Manifesting Generators who do not have a defined Solar Plexus. This is a "Truth in the Now" authority. It is reactive and responds to life as it shows up.

Sacral Sounds vs. Sacral Knowing

The Sacral center is biological. It often communicates through non-verbal sounds: "Uh-huh" (Yes) or "Un-un" (No). In your sessions, stop asking open-ended questions like "What do you want to do?" Instead, ask Yes/No questions to stimulate the gut response.

Technique: The Sacral Session

If your Generator client is stuck, rapid-fire Yes/No questions can bypass their mind:

  • "Do you like your current niche?"
  • "Do you want to raise your prices?"
  • "Do you feel energized by this project?"

Watch their body. An "uh-huh" usually comes with a lean forward. An "un-un" often comes with a slumped posture or a sigh. This is Internalizing the response.

Splenic & Ego Authority

Splenic Authority is the oldest, most primitive form of survival intelligence. It is a spontaneous "hit" of knowing. Unlike the Emotional wave, the Spleen only speaks once. If the client misses it because they were "thinking," it‚Äôs gone.

Ego Authority (found in some Manifestors and Projectors) is about the heart's desire. The question here is: "What do I want?" and "Is my heart in this?" This isn't about being "selfish" in the Not-Self sense; it's about the correct use of willpower. If the Ego isn't "in it," the client will burn out trying to force it.

Coach Tip

üí° For Splenic clients, encourage them to stop "thinking it over." If they feel an immediate "No" but their mind says "Yes," the "No" is the truth. They must learn to trust that first instinct, even if they can't explain it logically.

Coaching Through Resistance

The biggest obstacle to Internalizing Authority is Mental Narrative. The mind will argue that waiting (Emotional) is "unprofessional" or that gut sounds (Sacral) are "childish."

As a Specialist, your job is to hold the space for the client's experiment. Remind them that Human Design is a somatic practice, not an intellectual one. When they align their decision-making with their Authority, they move from Resistance (Anger, Frustration, Bitterness) to their Signature (Peace, Satisfaction, Success).

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client has both a defined Solar Plexus and a defined Sacral Center, what is their Inner Authority?

Emotional Authority. According to the hierarchy, the Solar Plexus (Emotional) always takes precedence over the Sacral center. They must wait for emotional clarity before their Sacral response can be fully trusted.

2. What is the primary mantra for a client with Emotional Authority?

"There is no truth in the now." This reminds the client that their current emotional state (high or low) is a temporary perspective and not the full picture of clarity.

3. How should a Specialist frame questions for a client with Sacral Authority?

Use "Yes/No" or "This or That" questions. Open-ended questions (e.g., "What should we do next?") force the client into their mind, whereas "Do you want to work on X today?" allows the Sacral gut response to activate.

4. Which Authority is known for being a "spontaneous whisper" that only speaks once?

Splenic Authority. It is an immediate, instinctive hit of knowing centered on survival and well-being. If the mind begins to rationalize it, the Splenic signal is often lost.
Key Takeaways
  • Authority is Somatic: Truth is found in the body, while the Mind acts as an "Outer Authority" for sharing wisdom with others.
  • The Hierarchy Matters: Always look for the Solar Plexus first; if it's defined, the client MUST wait for clarity.
  • The "I" in D.E.S.I.G.N.‚Ñ¢: Internalizing Authority is the bridge between knowing the chart and living the design.
  • Clarity vs. Certainty: For Emotional types, clarity is a cool, neutral feeling of "this is the path," even if 100% certainty is never reached.
  • Coach the Experiment: Encourage clients to test their Authority on small decisions (what to eat, what to wear) before applying it to major career pivots.
