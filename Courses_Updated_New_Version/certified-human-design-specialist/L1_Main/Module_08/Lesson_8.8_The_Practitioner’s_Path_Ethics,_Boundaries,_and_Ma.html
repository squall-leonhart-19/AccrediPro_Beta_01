Handling Imposter Syndrome

If you feel like you're "making it up," remember: The chart is the map. You are just the navigator. If you get stuck, it's okay to say, "Let's look at what the mechanics say about this specific feeling you're having."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

New specialists often fall into these four traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

  • The Info-Dump: Trying to explain all 64 gates in the first 20 minutes. (Stick to Type/Strategy/Authority!)
  • Predicting the Future: Saying "You will get a job in June." We coach the process, not the outcome.
  • Using "HD-Speak": Saying "Your Sacral is undefined" instead of "You don't have a consistent internal battery for labor."
  • Fixing Instead of Empowering: Trying to solve Margaret's problems for her instead of teaching her how to use her Authority to solve them.

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why is it important to explain "Waiting for the Invitation" to a Projector like Margaret before diving into her career options?

Because if she uses her mind to force a career choice without an invitation, she will likely experience bitterness and further burnout. Her Strategy is the "how" of her life.

2. What is the danger of using "HD-Speak" (jargon) in an initial reading?

It creates a barrier between the coach and client. The client may feel overwhelmed or confused, which prevents them from actually applying the Strategy to their life.

3. Margaret asks, "Should I take the job offer I got yesterday?" How should an Emotional Authority coach respond?

Ask her: "How do you feel about it right now? And how did you feel about it yesterday?" Remind her that she needs to wait for emotional clarity and that there is no rush in the "now."

4. What is the primary goal of a Level 1 reading?

To give the client the tools (Strategy and Authority) to begin their own experiment and move from their "Not-Self" theme to their "Signature" theme (e.g., Success for Projectors).

Key Takeaways for Practice

  • Connection over Content: Your ability to make the client feel "seen" is more important than memorizing every line.
  • The Big Three: For Level 1, focus 90% of your energy on Type, Strategy, and Authority.
  • Metaphors Matter: Use real-world analogies to explain complex HD mechanics.
  • Practice Out Loud: Verbalizing the concepts is the only way to build professional confidence.
  • You Are a Guide: Your job is to facilitate the client's experiment, not to have all the answers.
