Outer Authority vs. Inner Authority

In the Human Design system, the mind (the Head and Ajna centers) is designated as Outer Authority. It is meant to process information, store data, and share insights with others. However, for 100% of the population, the mind is NOT the place where personal decisions should be made.

A 2022 survey of 1,200 wellness practitioners found that 84% of clients struggle with "decision paralysis" because they rely solely on cognitive logic. When we shift a client to their Inner Authority, we bypass the "Not-Self" mind and tap into the body‚Äôs somatic intelligence.

Practitioner Insight

When a client says, "I think I should do this," they are in their mind. When they say, "I feel a pull toward this" or "My gut says yes," they are touching their authority. Your job is to gently move them from "I think" to "I feel/sense."

Emotional (Solar Plexus) Authority

Approximately 47% of the population has Emotional Authority. This occurs whenever the Solar Plexus center is defined. This is the highest authority in the hierarchy; if the Solar Plexus is colored in, it overrides everything else.

The Principle of "No Truth in the Now"

For those with Emotional Authority, clarity is a process, not a moment. They experience "emotional waves"‚Äîhighs of hope and lows of nervousness. If they make a decision at the peak of a high or the pit of a low, they are making an impulsive choice based on a temporary state.

Sacral and Splenic Authorities

If the Solar Plexus is undefined, the next potential authorities are the Sacral or the Spleen.

Authority Type Center Involved The "Voice" Timing
Sacral Sacral Center Gut sounds (Uh-huh / Un-unh) Instant response to a stimulus
Splenic Splenic Center Quiet "knowing," intuition, survival sense Instant, but speaks only once

Sacral Authority (Generators/MGs only): This is a physical response of energy. It requires something to respond to. It is the most reliable "Yes/No" engine in the world, representing about 35% of the population who don't have Emotional definition.

Splenic Authority (Projectors/Manifestors): This is the oldest form of intelligence. It is about health and safety. Unlike the Sacral, which is a "vroom vroom" of energy, the Spleen is a quiet whisper. If the client misses it, the mind will quickly talk them out of it.

Coach's Tip

For Splenic clients, emphasize that their authority is un-logical. If they get a bad vibe about a "perfect" business deal, they must trust it, even if they can't explain why to their partners.

Minority Authorities: The Rare 10%

When the Solar Plexus, Sacral, and Spleen are all undefined, we look to the "Minority" authorities. These are often the most misunderstood by conventional society.

  • Ego-Manifested/Projected: The decision comes from the "Heart." The client must ask: "What do I actually want? What is in it for me?" This isn't selfish; it's their correct mechanic.
  • Self-Projected (Projectors only): The authority is in the G-Center. These clients must talk it out. They don't need advice; they need to hear themselves speak to recognize the truth in their own voice.
  • Mental (Environmental) Authority: These Projectors have definition only above the throat. They need to use their environment as a sounding board. If the "vibe" of the room is wrong, they cannot make a clear decision.
  • Lunar Authority (Reflectors only): The rarest (1%). They must wait a full 28-day lunar cycle. They sample the decision through different "transits" before reaching clarity.

The Hierarchy of Authority

Determining which authority "wins" is simple if you follow the hierarchy. Even if a client has a "defined gut" (Sacral), if they also have a "defined Solar Plexus," they are Emotional Authority. The wave always takes precedence.

  1. Solar Plexus (Emotional) - Always 1st.
  2. Sacral - 2nd (If Solar Plexus is open).
  3. Spleen - 3rd (If Solar Plexus/Sacral are open).
  4. Heart (Ego) - 4th.
  5. G-Center (Self) - 5th.
  6. Environment/Lunar - If everything above is open.
