Welcome to your first Practice Lab!

We aren't just reading data; we are reflecting someone's soul back to them.

Learning Objectives
  • Demonstrate a professional opening for a Human Design "Discovery" session.
  • Practice explaining a client's Type and Strategy using non-technical, empowering language.
  • Identify the "Low-Hanging Fruit" in a Bodygraph to provide immediate value.
  • Navigate a client's emotional reaction to their chart with empathy and authority.
  • Avoid "Information Overload" by focusing on the core Strategy and Authority.

Your Coaching Scenario

In this scenario, you are meeting with Elena, who has booked a "Human Design Deep Dive" because she feels "stuck" in her career transition. She is curious but slightly skeptical.

Client Profile: Elena, 52

Background: Elena was a high school principal for 25 years. She recently "retired" but wants to start a consulting business. She is exhausted and feels like she has to "push" to get anything started.

Chart Component Elena's Data Coaching Significance
Type Projector She is designed to guide, not to "do" the heavy lifting.
Strategy Wait for the Invitation Her "pushing" is causing the burnout and bitterness.
Authority Splenic (Intuitive) She needs to trust her "gut" in the moment, not overthink.
Defined Centers Spleen, Root, Ajna Steady intuition, pressure to move, fixed way of thinking.

The Goal: Help Elena understand why her old "Principal" way of working (pushing, initiating) is no longer working for her business, and how her HD chart offers a new path.

Sarah's Practitioner Tip

When working with women in their 50s, remember they have often spent decades "conditioning" themselves to be like Generators (doers). Be gentle. When you tell a Projector they don't have to initiate, it often feels like a permission slip they've been waiting for their whole lives.

The Coaching Script

Read the following script out loud. Pay attention to the transitions between technical HD terms and practical life application.

You (The Coach):
"Elena, it's so good to be with you today. I've looked over your Bodygraph, and the first thing I want to say is: I see why you're tired. You've spent years in a role that required you to be the 'initiator,' but your chart shows you are actually a Projector."
Elena:
"A Projector? That sounds... passive. I've always been the one in charge."
You (The Coach):
"I hear you. But in Human Design, a Projector isn't passive‚Äîyou are a Guide. You have this incredible gift for seeing the big picture and knowing how to make things more efficient. The reason you're feeling 'stuck' in your new business is likely because you're trying to use your old 'Principal' energy to force things to happen. Your chart suggests your magic actually happens when you wait for the invitation."
Elena:
"Wait for an invitation? If I just sit around, nothing will get done!"
You (The Coach):
"It feels that way, doesn't it? But for you, 'waiting' isn't doing nothing. It's about making yourself visible, sharing your wisdom, and then letting the right people recognize you. When you push, you hit resistance. When you are invited, you are unstoppable. Does that resonate with how your most successful projects in the past felt?"
Sarah's Practitioner Tip

Notice how the script uses the word "resistance." This is a key Human Design concept, but using it in a sentence about her life makes it more relatable than just saying "the Not-Self theme."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my first year, I made all of these! Keep this list handy so you can stay in the "Specialist" mindset.

  • The "Dictionary" Trap: Explaining every gate and line in the first 10 minutes. (Result: Client gets overwhelmed and forgets their Strategy).
  • Being Too "Woo-Woo": Using terms like "Aura" or "Transits" without explaining what they mean for the client's bank account or energy levels.
  • Predicting the Future: Saying "You will get a big invitation on Tuesday." (HD is about mechanics, not fortune-telling).
  • Ignoring the Body: Forgetting to ask the client how the information *feels* in their body right now.
Sarah's Practitioner Tip

A successful reading is one where the client says, "Oh my god, I thought I was just broken, but that's actually my design." That relief is worth more than any technical data point.

Success Markers

How do you know if your practice session (or a real session) went well? Look for these signs:

Sign of Success What it Looks Like
Physical Release The client sighs deeply or their shoulders drop. They are "letting go" of conditioning.
Personal Examples The client starts saying, "Oh! That's like when I did [X] and it felt so easy!"
Strategy Clarity The client can repeat back to you exactly how they should make their next big decision.
Empowerment The client moves from "What is wrong with me?" to "How can I use this gift?"

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to focus on Elena's "Type" before her "Gates"?

Type and Strategy are the foundation. If a client doesn't follow their Strategy, the specific Gates won't function correctly anyway. It's the "big lever" that moves everything else.

2. If Elena (a Projector) says she's afraid of "doing nothing," how should you respond?

Validate her fear, then reframe "waiting" as "active preparation." Remind her that her value is in her perspective, not her labor.

3. What is the "Not-Self" theme for a Projector like Elena?

Bitterness. If she feels bitter, it's a sign she is pushing or initiating without an invitation.

4. How does Splenic Authority differ from Emotional Authority in a session?

Splenic is "in the moment" and spontaneous. Emotional requires a "waiting for clarity" period. You would encourage Elena to trust her immediate hit, rather than waiting for a wave.
Sarah's Practitioner Tip

Key Lab Takeaways

  • Start with Type: It is the most transformative piece of information for 90% of clients.
  • Translate the Tech: Use real-world words like "efficiency," "instinct," and "burnout" instead of just HD jargon.
  • Watch for the Sigh: Physical relaxation in the client is the best indicator of a successful "decoding."
  • Practice Out Loud: Your confidence grows when the words feel familiar in your mouth.
