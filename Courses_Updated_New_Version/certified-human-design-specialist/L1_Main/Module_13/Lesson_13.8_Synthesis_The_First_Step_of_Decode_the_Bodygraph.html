Success Markers

How do you know if your session with "Diane" was a success? Look for these cues:

  • The "Body Release": She physically exhales or drops her shoulders when she realizes she doesn't have to "initiate."
  • The "Aha" Moment: She says, "Oh, that explains why I loved my job 10 years ago when things were just falling into my lap!"
  • The Commitment: She agrees to a specific "experiment" for the coming week.
  • The Energy Shift: She moves from "restless/anxious" to "curious."

Coach Sarah's Tip

A successful session isn't one where you were "perfect." It's one where the client felt seen. If you can make Diane feel seen in her exhaustion, you've already done 90% of the work.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use metaphors like "GPS" or "Battery" instead of technical terms like "Defined Sacral Center"?

Metaphors bridge the gap between abstract mechanics and lived experience. They help the client integrate the information into their daily life without needing a degree in Human Design.

2. If Diane says, "Waiting sounds passive and lazy," how should a Specialist respond?

Explain that "Waiting to Respond" is an active state of presence. Use the analogy of a predator waiting for the right moment to strike, or a gardener waiting for the season to plant. It's about efficiency, not laziness.

3. What is the primary indicator of a Generator out of alignment?

Frustration. When a Generator pushes or initiates, they hit resistance, which manifests as a deep, bone-weary frustration.

4. What is the "60/40 Rule" in coaching sessions?

Aiming for the client to speak/process for 60% of the time while the coach facilitates for 40%. This ensures the client is actively engaging with their own design.
