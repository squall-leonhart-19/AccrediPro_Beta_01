Common Mistakes for New Specialists

When you are starting your practice, it‚Äôs easy to fall into these traps. Awareness is 90% of the cure.

  • The "Information Dump": Trying to explain the gates and channels instead of focusing on the client's immediate frustration. Keep it simple: Strategy and Authority first.
  • Acting as the Authority: Telling the client what to do instead of asking them what their body feels. Ask: "What does your Sacral say about that?"
  • Ignoring the Age Factor: A 52-year-old has 50+ years of conditioning. You cannot "fix" it in one session. Frame it as a 7-year experiment.
  • Fearing the Silence: New coaches often talk to fill the gap. Give the client's Sacral time to rev up.

Check Your Understanding

1. Brenda (Generator) is frustrated because she is cold-calling clients. What is the mechanical reason for her frustration?

She is initiating from the mind (Not-Self) instead of waiting for something to respond to. This bypasses her Sacral energy, leading to resistance and exhaustion.

2. If Brenda asks, "But how will I make money if I just wait?", how should you respond?

Remind her that "Waiting to Respond" isn't "Doing Nothing." It's being active in her life while staying alert for the "spark" to respond to. Encourage her to experiment with small things first to build trust.

3. Why is it important to ask Brenda how her body felt during the "hustle"?

It anchors the HD theory in her actual physical experience. It moves the conversation from a mental concept to a somatic (body-based) truth she can't argue with.

4. What is the primary role of a Human Design Specialist during the "Experiment" phase?

To act as a mirror and a guide, helping the client recognize their own Strategy and Authority in action, and providing permission to decondition from societal "shoulds."

Key Takeaways

  • Strategy is the Engine: For Generators, initiating is like trying to drive in neutral. Help them find their "spark."
  • Somatic Over Semantic: Always bring the client back to how their body feels, rather than what their mind thinks.
  • Permission to Pivot: High-value coaching often involves giving the client permission to stop behaviors that are draining them.
  • The 7-Year Journey: Remind clients (and yourself) that deconditioning is a process, not an event.
  • Expertise is Presence: You don't need to know every gate to be a life-changing coach; you need to be present with the client's experiment.
