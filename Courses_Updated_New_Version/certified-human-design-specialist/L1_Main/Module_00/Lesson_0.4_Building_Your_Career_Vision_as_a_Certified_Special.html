Legal & Liability Basics

To protect your practice and your family's assets, you must operate with professional legitimacy. This isn't just about "staying out of trouble"‚Äîit's about the confidence that comes from having your "house in order."

Every practitioner should have these three elements in place:

  • Professional Disclaimer: A clear statement on your website and intake forms stating that you are not a medical professional and your services are for educational purposes.
  • Client Agreement: A signed document outlining the nature of the work, refund policies, and the "no-guarantee" clause regarding specific life outcomes.
  • Liability Insurance: Even as a coach, professional liability (Errors & Omissions) insurance is affordable (often under $200/year) and provides immense peace of mind.
