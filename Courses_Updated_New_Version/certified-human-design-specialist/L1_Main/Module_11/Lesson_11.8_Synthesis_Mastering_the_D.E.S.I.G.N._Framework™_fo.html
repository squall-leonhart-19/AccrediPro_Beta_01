Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating the Root like a Motor: The Root is pressure, not sustainable energy. Don't tell a client they have "Root energy" to get things done; tell them they have "Root drive" to get things started.
  • Over-identifying with the "Open" centers: Don't make the client feel "broken" because they are undefined. Frame it as "flexibility" and "wisdom."
  • Ignoring the Heart Center: If a client has a defined Heart (like Diane), they will "will" themselves into a hospital bed. You must address the Willpower motor specifically.
  • Rushing the Session: If you have an undefined Root, you might pick up the client's pressure and start talking faster. Notice it, pause, and slow down.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client says, "I have so much to do, I'm just going to push through and finish it all tonight," which motor center are they likely over-using?

They are likely over-using the Heart/Will Center to override the lack of Sacral energy. This leads to deep burnout because the Heart center requires rest cycles (it's a "pulsing" motor).

2. What is the primary difference between the Sacral Center and the Root Center?

The Sacral is a Motor Center (sustainable energy for work and life), while the Root is a Pressure Center (the adrenaline/stress drive to get things moving).

3. How should you advise a client with an Undefined Root who feels "rushed" at work?

Advise them to recognize that the "rush" is external. They should pause, breathe, and ask: "Am I actually in a hurry, or am I just feeling everyone else's stress?"

4. Why is the Solar Plexus unique among the centers we studied in this module?

The Solar Plexus is both a Motor Center (emotional energy) and an Awareness Center (emotional intelligence/clarity).

Key Takeaways

  • Pressure is not Energy: Help clients distinguish between the drive to start (Root) and the fuel to finish (Sacral).
  • The "Not-Self" of the Root: Always look for the client trying to "hurry up to be free" of pressure.
  • Projector Exhaustion: In women 40+, burnout is often caused by undefined Sacral centers trying to mimic Generator motors.
  • Your Presence is the Lesson: As a coach, maintaining a calm, non-pressured presence is the most powerful "deconditioning" tool you have.
