Common Mistakes for New Specialists

I've mentored hundreds of women transitioning into this career, and I see these four mistakes over and over again. Avoiding these will immediately put you in the top 10% of practitioners.

1
The "Data Dump"

Giving the client the names of all 4 gates in their Cross. Instead: Focus on the theme of the Cross. Diane doesn't need to know Gate 52; she needs to know she brings 'stillness' to her clients.

2
Predicting the Future

Telling a client "You will be a famous author." Instead: Say "You have the design of someone whose voice carries great weight." Leave the 'form' of the purpose to their Strategy and Authority.

3
Ignoring the Profile

Focusing only on the G-Center and missing how the Profile (e.g., 4/6) influences how that purpose is delivered. A 4th line purpose is always about community.

4
Lack of Income Context

Failing to show how "Purpose" leads to "Prosperity." Instead: Help the client see that their unique frequency is their "Unique Selling Proposition" (USP) in business.

Sarah's Practice Secret

In my first year, I made $45k. By my third year, focusing on "Purpose Strategy," I crossed $150k. Clients will pay a premium for clarity. Don't be afraid to charge for the value of the 'aha' moment!

Success Markers

How do you know if your "Purpose Session" was successful? Look for these three signs in your client:

  • Physical Relaxation: Their shoulders drop. They exhale deeply. The "search" is over.
  • The "Of Course" Moment: They say, "I've always felt that, but I never had a name for it."
  • Actionable Excitement: They stop asking "What is my purpose?" and start asking "How can I start using this energy today?"

Check Your Understanding

1. When Diane asks for a specific job title for her purpose, what is the best Specialist response?

Redirect her to the Quality of Being or Frequency of her design. Explain that the specific 'form' (job title) will emerge naturally when she follows her Strategy and Authority.

2. What is a key characteristic of a 4/6 Profile in midlife (age 50+) regarding purpose?

They are moving into the "Role Model" phase (off the roof), where their purpose is about leading through wisdom and being an authentic example for their community.

3. Why is it a mistake to "Data Dump" gate names during a purpose session?

It overwhelms the client's mind with technical jargon, moving them away from the feeling and embodiment of their purpose, which is the goal of the session.

4. For a Generator, what is the "litmus test" for whether they are on-purpose?

Satisfaction. If they are satisfied with their daily work, they are fulfilling their design's purpose of generating high-quality life-force energy.

Key Takeaways

  • Purpose is a frequency, not a destination or a specific job title.
  • The Defined G-Center provides a reliable internal compass, even if the client feels lost.
  • Your vocal delivery should be grounded, warm, and authoritative to build trust.
  • Success is measured by the client's physical shift from seeking to embodying.
  • Avoid technical jargon; focus on the lived experience of the chart mechanics.
