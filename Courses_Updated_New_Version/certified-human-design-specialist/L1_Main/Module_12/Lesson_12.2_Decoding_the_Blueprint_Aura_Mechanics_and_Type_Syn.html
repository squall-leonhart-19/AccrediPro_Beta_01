The Chemistry of Signature

One of the most profound aspects of the "D" in D.E.S.I.G.N. is the link between the spirit and the physical body. Each type has a Signature (the feeling of alignment) and a Not-Self Theme (the feeling of resistance). These are not just emotions; they are chemical states.

The Hierarchy of Decoding

In the D.E.S.I.G.N. Framework‚Ñ¢, we never start with the "small stuff" like gates or lines. Professional decoding follows a strict hierarchy to ensure the client isn't overwhelmed and the foundation is solid.

  1. Type & Aura: The mechanical foundation. If they don't get this, nothing else matters.
  2. Strategy: The behavioral alignment with the aura.
  3. Authority: The decision-making compass.
  4. Centers (Defined vs. Undefined): Where they are consistent vs. where they are open to conditioning.
  5. Profile & Incarnation Cross: The "role" and the "purpose."

A common mistake for new specialists is jumping to the Incarnation Cross. This is like trying to install a luxury roof on a house with no foundation. Use the 80/20 Rule: 80% of a client's transformation comes from the first three steps of the hierarchy.

Bridging Personality & Design

The Bodygraph is a synthesis of two distinct data sets: the Personality (Black) and the Design (Red). This is the ultimate "D" in Decoding.

The Personality (Black): This is "Who you think you are." It is the conscious mind, the passenger in the vehicle. It is calculated at the moment of birth.

The Design (Red): This is "Your body's intelligence." It is the unconscious, the vehicle itself. It is calculated approximately 88 days before birth. It represents your genetic inheritance from your bloodline.

The "Bridge" between these two is the Magnetic Monopole, located in the G-Center. It holds these two together in the "illusion" of a single life. As a specialist, you will often see a client's mind (Black) wanting one thing, while their body (Red) is naturally designed for another. Helping a client "marry" these two aspects is the hallmark of a Master Specialist.
