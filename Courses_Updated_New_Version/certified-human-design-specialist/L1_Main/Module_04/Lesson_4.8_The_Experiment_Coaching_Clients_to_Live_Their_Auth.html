Mind vs. Authority

Before we coach Linda, we must be clear on what we are looking for. Most clients confuse "mental anxiety" with "inner authority."

Feature The Mind (Not-Self) Inner Authority (True-Self)
Speed Fast, frantic, urgent. Steady, slow, rhythmic.
Language "I should," "It makes sense to..." "I feel," "My gut says," "I need time."
Feeling Anxiety, pressure in the chest/head. A sense of "settling" or physical "yes/no."
Consistency Changes based on new information. Remains consistent over time (clarity).
Sarah's Insight

When a client like Linda says she is "paralyzed," she is actually in a perfect state for coaching. Her mind has failed her, and she is finally open to a new way. This is where your expertise as a Specialist shines.

Your Coaching Script

Practice these words out loud. Imagine you are on a Zoom call with Linda. Your tone should be warm, grounded, and unhurried.

YOU:
"Linda, I can hear how much pressure you're putting on yourself to 'figure this out.' But here is the most liberating thing I can tell you: As an Emotional Authority, you are actually not designed to have clarity in the moment. Your mind is a brilliant tool for consulting, but it's a terrible boss for your life."
LINDA:
"But if I don't think it through, how will I know? I feel like I'm just wasting time waiting."
YOU:
"I hear that. That's the 'Not-Self' talk we discussed. For you, clarity is like a fine wineâ€”it needs to breathe. When you feel that 'rush' to decide, that's usually just an emotional wave. LINDA:

"The right opportunities for a Manifesting Generator will always wait for your clarity. If it disappears because you took time to feel into it, it wasn't yours to begin with. Let's practice 'sleeping on it' for the next three days. No pro-con lists. Just notice how the idea of the new business feels on Tuesday versus Thursday."

Practice Exercise

It is time to move this from your eyes to your voice. Follow these steps to internalize the coaching flow.

1
The "Mirror" Test: Stand in front of a mirror and say the script above. Notice if you look "tense" when you talk about waiting. Your body must model the ease you are teaching.
2
Record & Listen: Use your phone to record yourself saying: "Your mind is a brilliant tool, but a terrible boss." Listen back. Do you sound like a confident professional, or are you asking for permission? Aim for a "warm authority" tone.
3
The "Why" Explanation: Practice explaining *why* an Emotional MG needs time. Use the "ocean wave" analogy. If you can explain it to a 10-year-old, you can explain it to a CEO.
Sarah's Insight

Imposter syndrome often hits right here. You might think, "Who am I to tell a Principal how to make decisions?" Remember: You aren't teaching her how to be a Principal; you're teaching her how to be a Human. You have the map (the chart) that she doesn't.

Common Coaching Mistakes

Even experienced coaches can fall into these traps when teaching Authority. Watch for these in your own practice:

  • Rushing the Client: Trying to get them to a "yes" or "no" before the end of the session. (Irony: You're violating their authority to prove a point!)
  • Over-Explaining Mechanics: Spending 40 minutes on the Solar Plexus motor and only 5 minutes on how it feels. Focus on the experience.
  • Agreeing with the Mind: When the client says "But my mortgage is due!", don't get sucked into the logic. Bring them back to the body.
  • Ignoring the "Gulp": Missing the physical signs of resistance (tight throat, shallow breathing) in favor of what the client is saying.
Success Tip

Always end an Authority session with a "Low-Stakes Experiment." Ask the client to practice their authority on something small, like what to eat for dinner, before applying it to their career change.

Success Markers

How do you know the session worked? Look for these three signs:

  1. The Physical Release: The client exhales, their shoulders drop, or they stop fidgeting.
  2. The Language Shift: They stop saying "I think" and start saying "It feels like..." or "I need to sit with that."
  3. Relief: They express a sense of relief that they don't have to "force" an answer right now.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is a pros-and-cons list detrimental for an Emotional Authority client like Linda?

It keeps the decision-making process in the mind (the Not-Self), which lacks the capacity to see the "whole" emotional picture. It forces a logical conclusion on a biological process.

2. What is the "Not-Self" trap for a client who feels urgent about a decision?

The trap is the belief that "if I don't decide now, I'll miss out." This urgency is almost always a mental pressure, not a true inner authority.

3. What should you do if a client becomes frustrated that they "can't feel" their authority?

Validate the frustration as part of the deconditioning process. Remind them that they've spent decades ignoring their body, and it takes time to rebuild that neural connection.

4. What is the primary role of the coach in an Authority-focused session?

To be a "mirror" and a "permission-giver." You are there to hold space for their body's truth to emerge and to protect them from the pressure of their own mind.

Lab Summary

  • Authority is Experiential: You cannot "think" your way into your authority; you have to feel your way into it.
  • Mind = Tool, Body = Boss: Reiterate this frequently to help clients shift their internal hierarchy.
  • Validate the Wait: For Emotional Authorities, the "wait" is where the magic happens. It is not wasted time.
  • Watch for the Shift: Look for physical signs of relaxation as the indicator of a successful session.
  • Confidence is Key: Your belief in their design gives them the courage to try a new way of living.
