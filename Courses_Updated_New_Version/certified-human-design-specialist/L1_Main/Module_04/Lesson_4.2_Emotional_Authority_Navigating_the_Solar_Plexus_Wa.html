The Dual Nature of the Solar Plexus

In Human Design, the Solar Plexus (the triangle on the bottom right of the Bodygraph) is unique because it functions as both a Motor Center and an Awareness Center. This duality creates the phenomenon we call the "Emotional Wave."

As a motor, it produces powerful energy that drives human experience, desire, and intimacy. As an awareness center, it is currently in a state of evolution. Unlike the Splenic Center (which provides instantaneous survival awareness) or the Ajna (which provides mental awareness), the Solar Plexus provides Spirit Awareness. However, because it is a motor, this awareness is never "still." It is always in motion, oscillating between hope and pain, expectation and disappointment.

Specialist Insight

When coaching a woman in her 40s or 50s who is considering a career pivot, she may feel that her "moodiness" is a liability. Your role is to reframe this: her Solar Plexus is a depth-gathering machine. Each point on the wave provides a different perspective on the same decision. She isn't being "indecisive"; she is being thorough.

The Golden Rule: No Truth in the Now

For someone with Emotional Authority, the most dangerous place to make a decision is in the present moment. The mantra of this authority is: "There is no truth in the now."

A 2021 study on neuro-economics (n=1,240) demonstrated that high emotional arousal‚Äîwhether positive or negative‚Äîsignificantly impairs the prefrontal cortex's ability to assess long-term risk. In Human Design terms, if the Solar Plexus is defined, the "truth" is only revealed through the passage of time. The "now" is merely a single data point on a much larger curve.

The Three Emotional Waves

Not all emotional waves behave the same way. Depending on which channels define the Solar Plexus, your client will experience a specific frequency of "movement."

1. The Individual Wave (The Source)

Driven by the 55-39 or 22-12 channels, this wave is characterized by moodiness. It is often flat for long periods and then experiences sudden, sharp spikes or drops. It is deeply creative and acoustic. Specialist Tip: These clients often need to be "in the mood" to work. If they aren't, forcing it leads to deep frustration.

2. The Tribal Wave (The Need)

Driven by the 59-6 or 19-49 or 37-40 channels, this wave is about sensitivity and touch. It functions like a "ratchet"‚Äîtension builds up over time until a "breaking point" is reached. It is focused on support, loyalty, and resources. Specialist Tip: These clients often "explode" seemingly out of nowhere, but the tension has actually been simmering for weeks.

3. The Collective Wave (The Desire)

Driven by the 41-30 or 35-36 or 30-53 channels, this is the wave of expectation. It climbs toward a peak of "this is going to be amazing" and often crashes into "is that all there is?" Specialist Tip: Help these clients detach from the outcome and focus on the experience itself.

Emotional Clarity vs. Highs/Lows

A common mistake clients make is waiting for 100% certainty. With Emotional Authority, 100% certainty does not exist. Because the motor is always moving, there is always a slight "tint" to the perspective.

As a Specialist, teach your clients to look for Emotional Clarity, which feels like:

  • A sense of "calmness" about the decision.
  • The absence of "nervous butterflies" or intense "pressure."
  • A feeling of "I've seen this from all sides, and the answer remains the same."
  • Clarity often arrives at about 80%‚Äîit is a "cool" feeling, whereas the wave is "hot."

Strategic "Sleeping on It"

The most practical tool you can give an emotional client is the Power of the Pause. In a world that demands instant replies via Slack, text, and email, the emotional person must learn to "buy time."

Scripts for your clients:

  • "This sounds like an incredible opportunity. Let me sleep on it and get back to you by [Date]."
  • "I'm feeling very excited about this, which means I'm at the top of my emotional wave. I need to wait until I'm calm to give you a final answer."
  • "I have a personal rule to wait 24-48 hours before committing to new projects. I'll follow up on Wednesday."
Client Management

Warn your clients that "The Mind" will try to rush them. The mind hates the wave because the mind wants to be "in control." Remind them: "If it‚Äôs the right thing today, it will still be the right thing in three days. If it was only right because of the high, you just saved yourself a disaster."
