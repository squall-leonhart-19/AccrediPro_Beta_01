The Ethics of 'Reading' Without Permission

One of the most common ethical dilemmas for the Human Design Specialist is the "Peeping Tom" phenomenon‚Äîthe temptation to pull and analyze charts of people who have not requested a reading. While curiosity is natural, a Master Practitioner understands that energy follows attention.

Analyzing a third party's chart (a client‚Äôs husband, a boss, or a friend) without their consent can lead to "energetic projection." You begin to see them through the lens of their mechanics rather than their humanity, potentially creating a "Not-Self" narrative for them before they‚Äôve even begun their experiment.

Coach Tip: The Permission Protocol

Always ask your client: "Does this person know you are looking at their chart?" If the answer is no, shift the focus. Instead of "Reading his chart," frame it as "Understanding the mechanics of your relationship." This places the authority back on the client's experience rather than a psychic-style analysis of an absent person.
