The Foundational Coaching Script

This script is designed to help you navigate the crucial first few minutes of an intake session. It emphasizes empathy, active listening, and setting a positive, client-centered tone. Read through it, then prepare to practice it out loud.

Your Coaching Dialogue with Maria

Here's exactly how to open this conversation. Practice saying these words out loud:

YOU:
"Welcome, Maria! It's so wonderful to finally connect with you. Thank you for taking the time to come in today."
MARIA:
"Thank you! I'm really glad to be here. I've been looking forward to this."
YOU:
"I appreciate that. Before we dive into specifics, I just want to say how much I admire you for taking this proactive step. Planning for a healthy pregnancy is such a profound journey, and seeking support shows incredible dedication to yourself and your future family. Many women in their 40s, just like you, come to me with similar desires to optimize their health before conception, so you're certainly not alone in this."
MARIA:
"That's good to hear. Sometimes I feel like I'm behind or that I should already know all this."
YOU:
"I completely understand that feeling, Maria. It's easy to get caught up in expectations, especially with so much information out there. My role as your prenatal wellness coach is to cut through that overwhelm, provide you with clear, evidence-based guidance, and empower you to feel confident and vibrant throughout your journey. My goal is to help you create a sustainable plan that feels good for you."
YOU:
"From what you shared in your intake form, it sounds like you're looking to boost your energy, manage stress, and refine your nutrition as you prepare for conception. Does that broadly capture what you're hoping to achieve with our coaching?"
MARIA:
"Yes, absolutely. Especially the energy and nutrition part. I just feel so drained sometimes, and then I reach for sweets, which I know isn't helping."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Maria. It gives us a great starting point. Today, I'd love for us to explore a bit more about your daily rhythms, what a typical day looks like for you, and where you feel your biggest challenges and opportunities lie. We'll start by building a clear picture of your current wellness landscape, and then we can begin to pinpoint some initial, achievable steps. How does that sound as a plan for our first session?"
MARIA:
"That sounds perfect. I'm ready."

Coach Tip: Your Authenticity is Your Superpower

Don't try to sound like a textbook or mimic someone else's style perfectly. Your clients are seeking your unique blend of knowledge and empathy. Allow your authentic voice to shine through. Practice until the words feel natural, not rehearsed. This builds genuine connection.

Your Turn: Practice Out Loud

Now it's time to bring this script to life! The more you practice, the more natural and confident you'll feel when you're speaking with real clients. Remember, mastery comes from doing.

Practice Steps

1

Read the script silently first.

Familiarize yourself with the flow, the key phrases, and the intent behind each statement. Notice Emma's (your) empathetic tone and open-ended questions.

2

Now, speak your lines out loud.

Pretend Maria is sitting across from you. Use a warm, calm, and confident tone. Make eye contact with an imaginary client or even a mirror. Pay attention to your pacing and inflection.

3

Record yourself (optional, but highly recommended!).

Use your phone's voice recorder. Listen back to how you sound. Are you clear? Do you sound empathetic? Is your language empowering? This is a game-changer for self-improvement.

4

Focus on the "Why."

As you speak, think about *why* you're saying each line. What is your intention? Is it to gather information, build rapport, or set expectations? Understanding your intention will make your delivery more impactful.

5

Repeat until it feels natural.

Don't stop after one try. Go through the script multiple times. The more you practice, the more these foundational phrases will become second nature, allowing you to focus more on listening and less on what to say next.

Coach Tip: The Power of Validation

Notice how the script validates Maria's feelings ("I completely understand that feeling"). This is crucial in building trust. When clients feel truly heard and understood, they are much more likely to open up and engage deeply in the coaching process. Validation doesn't mean agreeing with a negative belief, but acknowledging their experience.

Common Coaching Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

Even seasoned coaches make mistakes, especially in the early days! Being aware of common pitfalls can help you navigate your initial client sessions with greater ease and effectiveness. Here are a few I've seen over the years:

Things to Watch Out For

  • Jumping to Solutions Too Soon: It's tempting to offer advice when you hear a problem, but foundational coaching is about guiding the client to their own solutions. Resist the urge to fix!
  • Not Actively Listening: Listening isn't just about waiting for your turn to speak. It's about truly absorbing what your client says, and perhaps more importantly, what they don't say.
  • Using Jargon: Avoid overly scientific or technical terms unless the client specifically asks for clarification. Keep your language accessible and empowering.
  • Making Assumptions: Never assume you know what a client needs or feels. Always ask clarifying questions and let them lead the discovery.
  • Over-Talking: Your client should be doing most of the talking, especially in the initial sessions. Aim for an 80/20 split, with the client speaking 80% of the time.

Coach Tip: Embrace Silence

It can feel uncomfortable, but silence is a powerful coaching tool. After asking a question, give your client ample time to think and respond. Often, the most profound insights emerge from these moments of quiet reflection. Don't rush to fill the void.

Markers of a Successful Initial Session

How do you know if your first session went well? It's not about solving all their problems, but about laying a strong foundation. Here are some key indicators:

Indicators of a Strong Start

  • Client Feels Heard and Understood: They express a sense of relief or validation.
  • Clear Understanding of Coaching Role: The client grasps that you're a guide, not a prescriber.
  • Mutual Trust and Rapport: You feel a genuine connection has been established.
  • Client Feels Empowered: They leave feeling hopeful and motivated, not overwhelmed.
  • Clear Next Steps: Both you and the client know what the immediate next actions or focus areas will be.
  • Client Expresses Readiness to Continue: They are enthusiastic about scheduling the next session.

Coach Tip: Manage Expectations

At the end of your initial session, briefly reiterate what coaching is (and isn't). Clearly state that lasting change takes time and consistent effort. This sets realistic expectations and prevents clients from feeling discouraged if immediate results aren't apparent. Your role is to support their journey, not to provide a quick fix.

Check Your Understanding

Let's test your grasp of these foundational coaching principles. Think through each question before revealing the answer.

Check Your Understanding

1. Maria mentions feeling "behind" and overwhelmed by information. What is the most effective coaching response to validate her feelings while also empowering her?

The most effective response is to acknowledge her feelings directly and normalize them. For example: "Maria, I completely understand that feeling. It's so common to feel overwhelmed with all the information out there, and it's easy to feel like you 'should' know it all. But you're here, taking a proactive step, and that shows incredible strength. My role is to help you navigate this, not to judge where you're starting from." This validates, normalizes, and reframes her action as a positive step.

2. During the initial session, Maria starts listing every single food she believes she "shouldn't" eat. As her coach, what is your primary objective in this moment?

Your primary objective is to listen actively without judgment, gather information about her current beliefs and habits, and gently redirect the conversation back to her overall goals and feelings, rather than getting bogged down in a detailed list of "forbidden" foods. You might say, "Thank you for sharing that, Maria. It sounds like you've put a lot of thought into your diet. What feelings come up for you when you think about these 'shouldn't' foods?" This encourages deeper reflection.

3. What is one key difference between a prenatal wellness coach and a medical doctor when addressing a client's concerns like fatigue and stress-eating?

A medical doctor typically focuses on diagnosing and treating medical conditions, often through medication or specific medical interventions. A prenatal wellness coach, while respecting medical advice, focuses on holistic lifestyle modifications, behavioral change, and empowering the client to find sustainable solutions through a client-centered approach. For fatigue and stress-eating, a coach would explore root causes through daily habits, mindset, and environmental factors, guiding the client to implement practical strategies.

4. Maria mentions her goal is to "feel energetic and confident." How can you ensure this goal is both empowering and actionable in the initial session?

To make it empowering and actionable, you would use SMART goal principles (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) and break it down. In the initial session, you might ask: "Maria, when you say 'energetic,' what does that look like in your daily life? How would you know you're feeling more confident? What's one small step you could take this week that would move you even 1% closer to feeling more energetic?" This helps define the abstract goal and identify immediate, tangible actions.

Key Takeaways

Your Path to Confident Coaching

  • Empathy is Foundational: Always start by validating your client's experience and making them feel seen and understood.
  • Active Listening is Non-Negotiable: Allow your client to do most of the talking, truly absorbing their words and underlying emotions.
  • Empowerment Over Prescription: Your role is to guide clients to their own solutions, fostering self-efficacy rather than providing quick fixes.
  • Set Clear Expectations: Define the coaching relationship, its scope, and how progress will be measured to ensure alignment.
  • Practice Makes Proficient: Regularly rehearse your initial client interactions to build confidence and refine your authentic coaching voice.
