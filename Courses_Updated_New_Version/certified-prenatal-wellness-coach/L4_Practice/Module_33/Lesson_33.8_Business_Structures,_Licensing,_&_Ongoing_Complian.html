Business Practice Lab: Your First Discovery Call

It's time to practice the business side of coaching. This lab walks you through a discovery call from start to close, preparing you for real-world client interactions.

Setting the Stage: Your Prospect Profile

Before any call, it's essential to understand who you're speaking with. Imagine this scenario:

ðŸ‘©

Rachel, 47

Found you through a friend's referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation
Rachel is a busy marketing executive, a mother of two teenagers, and recently started experiencing perimenopausal symptoms. She's struggling with chronic fatigue, unexplained weight gain, disturbed sleep, and mood swings. She's tried various diets and exercise programs without lasting success and feels frustrated and unheard by conventional doctors.
Budget Concern
"I've spent so much money already on supplements and programs that didn't work. Is this really going to be different? I need to be sure before I invest more."
Decision Style
Needs to feel deeply heard and understood. Skeptical of quick fixes or "magic pills." Values evidence-based approaches, professional credentials, and a personalized plan. She will research you.
Her Goal
"I just want to feel like myself again. I miss having energy to keep up with my kids and my career. I want to understand what's really going on in my body and get a clear path forward."
Coach Tip: Empathy First

Remember, Rachel isn't just a "prospect"; she's a woman in a similar life stage to many of you, seeking genuine help. Approach every call with deep empathy and a desire to understand her unique story. Your authenticity is your greatest asset.

The Art of the Discovery Call: A Step-by-Step Script

A typical discovery call lasts 30-45 minutes and follows a natural progression. This script provides a framework; remember to listen actively and let the conversation flow organically, using these prompts as guides.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Rachel

Phase 1: Build Authentic Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Emma! Yes, [friend's name] has been raving about you. She said you helped her so much with her energy levels."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear! [Friend's name] has indeed made incredible progress. So Rachel, tell me â€” what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you right now?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understanding Their Pain 10 min
RACHEL:
"Well, for the past year, I've just feltâ€¦ off. The fatigue is relentless, even after a full night's sleep. And the weight gain around my middle, despite my best efforts, is so frustrating. I feel like I'm constantly battling my own body."
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Rachel. It sounds incredibly challenging. Tell me more about the fatigue. When did it start, and how does it affect your daily life and your ability to do the things you love?"
RACHEL:
"It started subtly, but now it's constant. I used to be so active, but now I dread my morning workout. I snap at my kids more, and my focus at work is suffering. I feel like I'm constantly running on fumes."
YOU:
"I hear you. That sounds isolating. And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and what were your expectations?"
RACHEL:
"Oh, everything! Keto, intermittent fasting, cutting out dairy, supplements recommended by a friend... I even saw an endocrinologist who said my labs were 'normal.' Nothing has stuck, and I just feel more confused and defeated."
YOU:
"That's a common experience, Rachel, and it's completely understandable to feel that way. Let me ask you this: if nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like? What would be different?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Unique Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a driven woman feeling exhausted and frustrated by persistent fatigue and weight gain, despite trying many solutions. You're looking for answers and a personalized approach that truly addresses the root cause, so you can regain your energy and feel like yourself again. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, exactly! You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Excellent. The approach I use as an AccrediPro Certified Prenatal Wellness Coach is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes of your fatigue, hormonal imbalances, and weight issues. We use an evidence-based framework to identify exactly what's driving your symptoms â€“ whether it's nutritional deficiencies, stress, gut health, or sleep patterns. I work with clients like you over 12 weeks to create a truly personalized plan. Most of my clients, especially those in their 40s and 50s, start feeling significantly better and noticing shifts in their energy and clarity within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: Transition to Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Rachel, based on what I've described about my approach, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive support and clarity you've been looking for?"
RACHEL:
"It really does. It sounds much more thorough than anything I've tried."
YOU:
"Great! Let me tell you a bit about how the program works, what's included, and how we can get started."
Coach Tip: Mirroring Language

Pay close attention to the words your prospect uses to describe their pain and desires. When you summarize their situation, use their exact language. This demonstrates that you've truly listened and builds a deeper connection and trust. For example, Rachel used "relentless fatigue" and "battling my own body." Incorporate these phrases.

Confidently Handling Objections

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Your ability to respond with empathy and confidence is key. Here are common objections you might encounter and how to address them.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and it's wise to consider it carefully. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."

(Listen carefully to her response. This helps you uncover the real objection. If it's about a partner, offer to send an email summary she can share. If it's time, discuss flexibility. If it's still unclear, reiterate the value and ask about her commitment to change.)

"It's too expensive / I've already spent so much money."

"I completely hear you, Rachel, and it's a valid concern, especially given your past experiences. Many of my clients, often women like us in their 40s and 50s, have felt the same way after investing in things that didn't deliver. The way I see it, this isn't just an expense; it's an investment in your long-term health and quality of life. What is the cost of NOT addressing this fatigue and weight gain right now? What impact does it have on your career, your family, your happiness?

My program is designed to provide lasting results by addressing root causes, which is why it's a 12-week commitment rather than a quick fix. We also have flexible payment options to make it more accessible. Would you like to hear more about those?"

(Shift focus from cost to value and the cost of inaction. Offer solutions like payment plans.)

"I've tried everything before, and nothing works for me."

"I understand why you feel that way, Rachel. It sounds incredibly disheartening to put in so much effort without seeing results. What I can tell you is that the AccrediPro approach is fundamentally different because it's highly personalized and evidence-based. We don't just recommend generic diets; we delve deep into your unique physiology, lifestyle, and goals to build a plan that's truly tailored for YOU. We focus on sustainable changes and empowering you with knowledge, not just temporary fixes.

My role is to be your dedicated guide, providing accountability and expertise that's been missing from your previous attempts. We will uncover what's truly holding you back. Are you open to discovering a new path that offers lasting change?"

(Validate their frustration, then differentiate your unique, personalized, and evidence-based approach, emphasizing your role as a guide.)

Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

After you've addressed an objection, resist the urge to keep talking. Allow for a moment of silence. This gives the prospect time to process your response and often prompts them to share more or ask another question, moving the conversation forward.

Presenting Your Value & Pricing with Confidence

Once you've addressed objections, it's time to confidently present your coaching packages. Remember, you're not just selling a service; you're offering a transformative experience. Focus on the benefits and outcomes, not just the features.

Structuring Your Packages

Consider offering a tiered approach or a single signature program. For new coaches, a single, comprehensive 3-month or 6-month program can simplify your offerings. Let's imagine a 12-week signature program for Rachel.

  • Name: "Revitalize & Thrive: Your 12-Week Prenatal Wellness Journey"
  • Investment: $2,500 - $4,500 (depending on your market and experience level)
  • What's Included:
    • (1) 90-minute Deep Dive Initial Consultation
    • (6) Bi-weekly 60-minute Coaching Sessions
    • Personalized Wellness Plan (Nutrition, Lifestyle, Stress Management)
    • Customized Supplement Recommendations (if applicable)
    • Access to a Private Client Portal with resources & recipes
    • Unlimited Email/Chat Support between sessions
    • Post-Program Integration & Maintenance Guide

Confidently Stating Your Price

When you state your price, do so clearly and then pause. Allow the prospect to absorb the information. Avoid apologies or immediately justifying the cost. Your confidence in your value is contagious.

YOU: "Rachel, my signature 12-week 'Revitalize & Thrive' program is designed to help you regain your energy, balance your hormones, and truly feel like yourself again. The investment for this comprehensive program is $3,500. This includes everything we just discussed, ensuring you have personalized support every step of the way."

(Pause. Let her respond.)

Coach Tip: Value Over Cost

Always anchor your pricing discussion in the immense value and transformation you provide. When you talk about the investment, connect it back to their expressed desires: "This investment will allow you to finally get the energy back to keep up with your kids and excel in your career, which you told me is so important."

Sealing the Deal: Effective Calls to Action

After presenting your program and addressing any remaining questions, it's time for a clear and confident call to action. This is not about being pushy, but about guiding your prospect to take the next logical step towards their desired outcome.

Practice These Closing Lines:

  1. "Rachel, based on everything we've discussed today, I truly believe my 'Revitalize & Thrive' program is exactly what you need to finally reclaim your energy and feel fantastic. Are you ready to commit to yourself and take this first step towards that transformation?"
  2. "You've shared how much you want to feel like yourself again, and I'm confident we can achieve that together. How does this sound for getting started?"
  3. "Given what you've experienced and where you want to be, does enrolling in this program feel like the right next step for you?"
  4. "My next available spots for new clients are in [Month]. If you're ready to move forward, we can get you scheduled for your Deep Dive session today."

Always have your enrollment process clear: whether it's sending a contract, an invoice, or directing them to a sign-up page on your website. Make it as seamless as possible.

Check Your Understanding

1

What is the primary goal of the "Understand the Problem" phase in a discovery call?

The primary goal is to actively listen and deeply understand the prospect's pain points, their current struggles, what they've tried, and their desired future state if their problem were solved. This builds empathy and informs your solution.
2

When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective immediate response?

An effective response is to acknowledge their need to think, then gently inquire about the specific concerns: "I completely understand. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This helps uncover the true objection.
3

How should you confidently state your coaching program's price during a discovery call?

State the price clearly and directly, then pause. For example: "The investment for this comprehensive program is $X. This includes everything we discussed, ensuring you have personalized support every step of the way." Avoid immediately justifying the cost or apologizing.
4

What is a realistic annualized income potential for a Prenatal Wellness Coach with an established practice (10 clients at $3,500 for a 12-week program)?

For 10 clients on a 12-week (3-month) program at $3,500 each, the annualized income potential is approximately $46,666. This is calculated as (10 clients / 3 months) * $3,500 per client * 12 months = $46,666. This demonstrates strong earning potential for a thriving practice.
Key Takeaways
  • Discovery calls are powerful conversations for connection and assessment, not just sales.
  • Building rapport and deeply understanding a prospect's pain are crucial before presenting your solution.
  • Approach objections with empathy, curiosity, and a focus on clarifying underlying concerns.
  • Confidently present your coaching program's value and price, anchoring it to the transformation you offer.
  • Clear and direct calls to action empower prospects to make decisions and move forward.
  • A well-structured coaching practice offers significant income potential and financial freedom.
