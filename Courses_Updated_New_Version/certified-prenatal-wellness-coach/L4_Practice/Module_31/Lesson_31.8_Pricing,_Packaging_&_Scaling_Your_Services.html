1. Introduction: The Power of the Discovery Call

As a Certified Prenatal Wellness Coach, your expertise is invaluable. But how do you bridge the gap between someone discovering your services and becoming a committed client? The discovery call is that bridge. It's not a sales pitch; it's a diagnostic conversation, a chance for you to be a detective, a confidante, and a guide. When done right, it establishes trust, clarifies expectations, and powerfully demonstrates the unique value you offer.

Think of it as the first step in a collaborative journey. Your goal is to understand their story, their pain points, and their desired future state. Only then can you genuinely assess if your program is the solution they need, and if you are the right person to deliver it.

Coach Tip from Emma

Remember, the discovery call is a two-way street. You're assessing them just as much as they're assessing you. You want to work with clients who are truly ready for change, who are aligned with your values, and who you feel you can genuinely help. Don't be afraid to say "no" if it's not a good fit â€“ it saves both of you time and energy in the long run.

3. Structuring Your Discovery Call

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all essential points while maintaining a natural, empathetic flow. Hereâ€™s a proven 30-minute structure to guide your conversation.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's lovely to connect. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Emma! Yes, [friend's name] raved about you. She said you really helped her feel so much better during her pregnancy."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear! [Friend's name] has indeed been on a transformative journey. So Rachel, tell me â€” what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you at this stage of your pregnancy?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deep Dive 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned chronic fatigue and persistent nausea. Can you tell me more about when these symptoms started? How do they truly affect your daily life and your ability to enjoy this pregnancy?"
RACHEL:
"It started around week 6, and it's just relentless. I can barely get through the workday, and by the time I pick up my first child, I have nothing left. The nausea makes it hard to eat, but then I feel worse. I just feel so disconnected from this experience, and guilty."
YOU:
"I hear you, Rachel. That sounds incredibly challenging, and it's completely understandable to feel guilty when you're not feeling your best. What have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and what left you feeling frustrated?"
RACHEL:
"My OB suggested B6 for nausea, and I've tried all the ginger teas and crackers. For fatigue, I've tried to get more sleep, but it feels impossible. I've even tried a few expensive prenatal vitamins, but I don't feel any different. It just feels like I'm hitting a wall."
YOU:
"Thank you for being so open. This is crucial for me to understand. Now, let's look forward. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself and your pregnancy journey in a year? And conversely, if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel vibrant and energetic, what would your daily life, and this pregnancy experience, look like?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Rachel, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're an ambitious woman, deeply committed to your family, currently navigating significant fatigue and nausea during your pregnancy, feeling frustrated by conventional approaches, and yearning to feel vibrant and present, not just for yourself but for your growing family. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, absolutely. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Excellent. The approach I use is different from what you've likely experienced before. Instead of just managing symptoms, we delve deeper to identify the root causes of your fatigue, nausea, and other prenatal challenges. I work with you over a period of 12 weeks to create a truly personalized plan, integrating advanced nutritional strategies, lifestyle adjustments, and mindful practices, all specifically tailored to support your body's unique needs during pregnancy. My goal is to empower you to reclaim your energy, reduce discomfort, and truly thrive. Most of my clients, especially those with chronic fatigue, start feeling significantly better and more in control within the first 4-6 weeks of our collaboration."
Phase 4: Transition to Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Rachel, having heard about my approach and how it differs, does this sound like the comprehensive, personalized support you've been looking for to truly transform your pregnancy experience?"
RACHEL:
"It really does sound promising, Emma. It's exactly what I've been hoping for."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear! If you're ready to explore how we can work together, I'd love to share the details of my signature 12-week Prenatal Vitality Program and how we can get started."
Coach Tip from Emma

During the "Understand the Problem" phase, focus intensely on listening. Ask open-ended questions and allow for silence. Your prospect needs to feel heard and seen. Validate their feelings. This builds immense trust and helps them connect their pain to the potential solution you offer.

4. Navigating Common Objections

It's natural for prospective clients to have reservations. Your role is to address these with empathy and confidence, reframing them as opportunities to clarify value. Here are some common objections and how to respond.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need more time to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, whether this approach is truly different, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for your health."
"It's too expensive / I've already spent so much money."
"I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested a lot already without seeing the results you hoped for, which can be incredibly frustrating. What I offer is a personalized, evidence-based program designed to get to the root of your issues, preventing future challenges and truly transforming your pregnancy experience. Many of my clients find that investing in comprehensive support now saves them significant time, money, and emotional energy in the long run, compared to continuing with fragmented solutions. What would it be worth to you to truly feel vibrant and energetic throughout your pregnancy, avoiding the depletion you experienced before?"
"I don't have enough time."
"I understand completely, Rachel. You're a busy mom, working full-time, and growing a human! My program is designed with busy women in mind. We meet for virtual sessions, and the recommendations are integrated into your existing routine, not added on top of it. In fact, addressing your fatigue effectively will likely free up more time and energy than the program requires. We'll focus on efficiency and high-impact strategies. How much time are you currently spending feeling exhausted or trying things that don't work?"
"I'm not sure if this will work for me, given everything else I've tried."
"That's a very valid concern, Rachel, especially given your past experiences. What makes my approach unique is its highly individualized nature. We don't use a one-size-fits-all protocol. We start with a deep dive into your unique physiology, lifestyle, and specific needs during pregnancy. This allows us to uncover what's truly holding you back and create a plan that is truly tailored to you. My certification ensures I'm applying the latest evidence-based practices, and my track record with clients like [friend's name] demonstrates the effectiveness of this personalized approach. What specific concerns do you have about whether this will work for you?"
Coach Tip from Emma

Don't interrupt. Let them finish expressing their objection fully. Then, validate their feeling ("I hear you," "I understand that concern") before you respond. This shows empathy and builds connection, making them more receptive to your solution.

5. Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

Once you've addressed objections and confirmed their interest, it's time to talk specifics. Present your programs and pricing with clarity, confidence, and a focus on the transformation, not just the features.

Structuring Your Program Offer

A typical prenatal wellness coaching program might be 3-6 months long, offering consistent support. For Rachel, a 12-week (3-month) program would be a great starting point.

Hereâ€™s how you might present your "Prenatal Vitality Program":

  • Duration: "My signature 12-week Prenatal Vitality Program is designed to guide you through a complete transformation, providing consistent support during this crucial period."
  • Components: "It includes six bi-weekly 60-minute one-on-one coaching sessions with me, where we dive deep into your progress and next steps. You'll also receive a personalized nutritional blueprint, curated recipes, lifestyle recommendations, and unlimited email support between sessions for any questions that come up."
  • Outcomes: "By the end of our 12 weeks, you can expect to experience significantly increased energy, reduced nausea and discomfort, a clear understanding of optimal prenatal nutrition for you and your baby, and a profound sense of empowerment and confidence in your pregnancy journey. We'll also lay the groundwork for a smoother postpartum recovery."

Confidently Stating Your Price

When stating your price, do so clearly and without hesitation. Follow the "state, pause, explain" method.

YOU:
"The investment for the 12-week Prenatal Vitality Program is $3,000. [PAUSE] This includes everything we just discussed, designed to give you comprehensive, personalized support and the transformation you're looking for."

If you offer payment plans, present them immediately after the full price to make it more accessible.

YOU:
"I also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly installments of $1,100, making it easier to get started today."

Notice how the payment plan total is slightly higher. This is a common strategy to incentivize upfront payment, while still offering flexibility.

Coach Tip from Emma

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural. Eliminate any 'ums' or 'ahs' before the number. Your confidence in your value directly impacts a client's willingness to invest. Remember, you're offering a life-changing service, not just a commodity.

6. Mastering the Call to Action

After presenting your program and pricing, the next step is to guide your prospect towards a decision. This is where many new coaches hesitate, but itâ€™s crucial for helping clients move forward.

Clear and Direct Invitation

Don't leave it ambiguous. Directly ask if they are ready to begin.

YOU:
"Rachel, based on our conversation today and the transformation you're seeking, do you feel ready to get started with the Prenatal Vitality Program and begin feeling truly vibrant throughout your pregnancy?"

Handling "Yes"

If they say yes, immediately move to logistics.

RACHEL:
"Yes, Emma, I'm ready! I really need this."
YOU:
"Fantastic! I'm so excited to work with you, Rachel. I'll send you an email right after this call with the enrollment link, where you can choose your payment option and schedule our first session. How does next Tuesday at 10 AM sound for our initial deep-dive session?"

Handling "Not Right Now" or "Still Need to Think"

Refer back to your objection handling, or offer a clear next step.

RACHEL:
"It sounds amazing, but I still need to discuss it with my husband."
YOU:
"I completely understand. It's important to be aligned with your partner on such an investment. What specific questions do you anticipate your husband might have that I could help you answer before you speak with him? Perhaps we could schedule a brief follow-up call, or I can send you some information to share with him?"

Always aim for a clear next step, even if it's not an immediate enrollment. Leave the conversation with a plan.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call, and how does it differ from a traditional sales pitch?

A discovery call's primary purpose is to serve as a diagnostic conversation to genuinely connect with a prospective client, understand their needs, and determine if your services are the right fit. It differs from a traditional sales pitch by focusing on deep listening, empathy, and assessing mutual fit, rather than just presenting features and benefits.

2. When a prospect says, "I need to think about it," what is the most effective immediate response, and why?

The most effective immediate response is to validate their feeling ("I completely understand") and then ask a clarifying question: "Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need more time to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else?" This helps uncover the true objection, allowing you to address it directly.

3. Describe the "state, pause, explain" method for confidently presenting your pricing.

The "state, pause, explain" method involves clearly stating the full price without hesitation (e.g., "The investment is $X"), then pausing briefly to allow the prospect to process the number, and finally, explaining the value or what's included in that investment (e.g., "This includes everything we discussed, designed to give you the transformation you're looking for").

4. Why is it important for a coach to confidently guide a prospect to a clear call to action, even if it's not an immediate "yes"?

It's important because it demonstrates your professionalism, confidence in your services, and respect for the client's time. A clear call to action helps the prospect move forward, whether that's enrolling, scheduling a follow-up, or clarifying a specific concern. It prevents ambiguity and ensures a productive next step, even if it's not an immediate enrollment.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is a foundational client acquisition tool, focusing on empathetic listening and mutual fit.
  • A structured 30-minute call, moving from rapport to problem understanding, solution presentation, and closing, ensures effectiveness.
  • Active listening, validation, and asking clarifying questions are crucial for understanding prospect needs and building trust.
  • Addressing objections with empathy and confidence transforms potential barriers into opportunities to reinforce value.
  • Present your programs and pricing with clarity and conviction, always linking the investment to the client's desired transformation.
  • A clear call to action is essential to guide prospects toward commitment and a defined next step.
