The Art of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is arguably the most critical step in your client acquisition journey. It's not a sales pitch; it's a conversation of curiosity and connection. Your goal is to listen more than you speak, to understand the client's deepest desires and frustrations, and to ascertain if your services are a genuine fit for their needs. When done well, it builds trust and positions you as a compassionate expert.

A strong discovery call aims to:

  • Build Rapport: Establish a warm, safe space.
  • Uncover Needs: Go beyond surface-level complaints to understand root challenges.
  • Educate (Briefly): Explain your unique approach without overwhelming.
  • Assess Fit: Determine if you can truly help and if the client is ready for change.
  • Present Solution: Confidently offer your program as the vehicle for their transformation.
  • Facilitate Decision: Guide them towards taking the next step.
Coach Tip

Remember, a discovery call is a two-way street. You're not just evaluating the client; they're evaluating you. Be authentic, be present, and trust your intuition. If it doesn't feel like a good fit for either of you, it's okay to gently recommend alternative resources.

The Discovery Call Script: Your Guiding Framework

Here's a structured script to guide your 30-minute discovery call with Sarah. Remember, this is a framework, not a rigid dialogue. Your authenticity and ability to listen are paramount.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Sarah

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's wonderful to connect. How are you doing this morning?"
YOU:
"I saw you connected with my article on the mothers' forum ‚Äì I'm so glad that resonated with you. What prompted you to reach out for a discovery call today?"
SARAH:
"Well, I'm just so tired. My first pregnancy wasn't like this. I'm 14 weeks now, and I feel like I'm constantly battling nausea and fatigue, and I'm already worried about how I'll manage everything with my toddler and work. I need help."
Phase 2: Deep Dive & Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that, Sarah. It sounds incredibly challenging. Could you tell me more about this fatigue? When did it start, and how does it specifically impact your daily life, your work, or your ability to enjoy time with your toddler?"
SARAH:
"It started around week 6, and it's just relentless. I crash by 3 PM, can barely keep my eyes open. My husband has to pick up most of the slack, and I feel guilty. I used to be so organized and energetic, and now I just feel... depleted. I'm also worried I'm not eating well enough for the baby because I just crave carbs and feel sick at the thought of healthy food."
YOU:
"I hear you. It's common for energy levels to fluctuate, especially in the first trimester, but what you're describing sounds beyond typical. What have you tried so far to manage these symptoms or improve your nutrition?"
SARAH:
"I tried taking naps, but with my toddler, it's impossible. I force myself to eat some greens, but honestly, it's a battle. My doctor just said it's 'normal pregnancy stuff' and to rest more, but that's not helping me feel better."
YOU:
"If nothing changes, Sarah, and you continue to feel this way throughout your pregnancy, what does that look like for you and your family? And conversely, if we could wave a magic wand and you felt exactly how you wanted to, what would your daily life look like?"
SARAH:
"Honestly, it scares me. I don't want to be a tired, grumpy mom. I want to have energy to play with my son, to feel excited about this baby, and to be present. I want to feel strong and nourished, not just surviving."
Coach Tip

Listen for emotional language and the "why" behind their goals. Sarah's fear of being a "tired, grumpy mom" and desire to feel "strong and nourished" are powerful motivators. Reflecting these back shows you truly understand.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Solution 8 min
YOU:
"Sarah, thank you for being so open. Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a busy, professional mom, 14 weeks into your second pregnancy, feeling overwhelmed by chronic fatigue, nausea, and anxiety. You want to regain your energy, improve your nutrition for a healthier pregnancy, and feel empowered to manage the demands of work and family, so you can truly enjoy this special time. My focus is on empowering you with practical, evidence-based tools to feel vibrant and prepared. Many of my clients start feeling a significant shift in energy and mood within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"My program, The Prenatal Vitality Journey, is a 3-month comprehensive coaching experience designed to help you transform your energy, nourish your body, and build resilience for a joyful pregnancy and postpartum period. It includes bi-weekly 60-minute coaching calls, personalized nutrition and lifestyle plans, unlimited email support, and access to exclusive resources."

"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about my approach and what you're looking for, does The Prenatal Vitality Journey sound like the kind of support you've been seeking?"

Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the enrollment process. They aren't rejections; they're requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and it's essential you feel confident moving forward. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure I've addressed all your concerns."
"It's too expensive / I can't afford it right now."
"I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. Let's consider what the cost of not addressing these challenges might be ‚Äì the impact on your energy, your well-being, and your ability to fully enjoy your pregnancy and new baby. My clients often find that the investment pays for itself many times over in improved health, reduced stress, and increased confidence. I also offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible. Would you like to explore those options?"
"I'm not sure I have the time."
"That's a very common concern, especially for busy moms like you. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, not add more burden. We meet bi-weekly for an hour, and the personalized strategies are practical and efficient. In fact, many clients find that by investing this time upfront, they actually gain more time and energy overall because they're no longer battling fatigue or feeling overwhelmed. What does 'having time' look like for you, and how can we make this work within your current schedule?"
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked."
"I completely empathize with feeling frustrated after trying various approaches without success. What makes my program different is its holistic and personalized nature. We don't just address symptoms; we dive deep into your unique physiology, lifestyle, and mindset to uncover the root causes of your challenges. This isn't a one-size-fits-all solution; it's a collaborative journey designed specifically for you. My certifications and expertise in prenatal wellness ensure a truly evidence-based and comprehensive approach. Are you open to exploring a different path that focuses on sustainable change?"
Coach Tip

Frame objections as opportunities. Each objection is a chance to provide clarity, demonstrate empathy, and reinforce the value of your program. Practice these responses out loud until they feel natural and confident.

Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

Confidence in your pricing comes from confidence in your value. When you present your programs, do so with conviction, focusing on the transformation you offer, not just the features.

For Sarah, you might present your signature 3-month program:

The Prenatal Vitality Journey: 3-Month Coaching Program

  • Six (6) bi-weekly 60-minute 1:1 coaching sessions.
  • Personalized Prenatal Nutrition & Lifestyle Plan, tailored to your unique needs and preferences.
  • Unlimited email/messaging support between sessions for questions and accountability.
  • Curated resources, recipes, and mindfulness exercises.
  • Investment: $1,800 (or 3 monthly payments of $650).

Always state the full value first, then offer payment plan options. This anchors the perceived value at the higher amount.

Sealing the Enrollment: Your Call-to-Action Practice

After presenting your program and addressing any questions or objections, it's time for a clear Call-to-Action (CTA). This isn't about pressure; it's about guiding your client to take the next step towards their desired transformation.

Practice these closing lines out loud:

  1. "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed today, I truly believe The Prenatal Vitality Journey is exactly what you need to regain your energy and have the vibrant pregnancy you deserve. Are you ready to get started?"
  2. "Given your goals to feel more energized and prepared, and the personalized support my program offers, it feels like a perfect fit. How would you like to move forward?"
  3. "The next step is simple: I'll send you an enrollment link which includes the agreement and payment options. Once that's complete, we can schedule your first official coaching session and begin your transformation. Does that sound good?"
  4. "I'm excited about the possibility of working with you, Sarah. If you're ready to commit to feeling your best during this pregnancy, I'd love to welcome you into the program. What feels like the best next step for you?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a discovery call in prenatal wellness coaching?

The primary purpose of a discovery call is to build rapport, deeply understand the prospect's needs and challenges, determine if your coaching program is a good fit, and then confidently present your solution as the vehicle for their transformation. It's about serving and assessing fit, not just selling.

2. Sarah expresses concern about the "investment." How should you initially respond to this objection?

You should acknowledge and validate her concern with empathy, then pivot to offering to explain the investment details and payment options. A good response is: "I completely understand that, and it's a valid consideration. Many of my clients initially have that concern. Would you like me to walk you through the investment details and payment options, and then we can discuss any other questions you have?"

3. When presenting your program's pricing, what is the recommended strategy for stating the investment?

Always state the full program investment first, clearly linking it to the value and transformation offered. Then, and only then, present any available payment plan options. This anchors the perceived value at the higher, full investment amount.

4. Maria, a former teacher in the case study, significantly increased her discovery call conversion rate. What key shifts did she make?

Maria shifted from "information dumping" to structured discovery calls, focusing on active listening and value-based pricing. She extensively practiced objection handling and developed a confident script for program presentation, coupled with a mindset shift from "selling" to "inviting clients to transform."
