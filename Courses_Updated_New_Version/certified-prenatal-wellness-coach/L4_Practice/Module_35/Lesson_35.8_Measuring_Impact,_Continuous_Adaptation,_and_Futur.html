1. Connecting the Dots: Scaling Your Vision

Before we dive into the practicalities of client acquisition, let's briefly anchor ourselves in the larger vision of scaling. Approach every discovery call not as a sales pitch, but as a collaborative exploration. Your role is to understand the client's needs deeply, determine if you're the right fit, and then offer a solution. This mindset reduces pressure and allows your genuine desire to help shine through.

3. The Art of the Discovery Call: Structure & Flow

A well-structured discovery call is your most powerful tool for client acquisition. It‚Äôs a purposeful conversation designed to build rapport, uncover needs, present your solution, and guide the prospect to a decision. Here‚Äôs a script framework for Sarah:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I saw you found me through the Facebook group ‚Äì that's wonderful! Before we dive into your specific needs, how are you doing today?"
YOU:
"Great to hear. So, my goal for our 30 minutes together is to understand what's bringing you here, explore if my coaching is the right fit for your unique situation, and see if we'd be a good match to work together. Does that sound good to you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deepen the Need 10 min
YOU:
"Fantastic. You mentioned in your intake form some concerns about fatigue and anxiety, especially with your second pregnancy at 42. Could you tell me more about what that feels like for you day-to-day? How is it impacting your energy for your toddler?"
YOU:
"I hear how challenging that must be. And you also noted concerns about gestational diabetes. What are your doctors telling you, and what steps have you tried so far to manage these symptoms or concerns?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, how do you envision the rest of this pregnancy and early motherhood playing out? And on the flip side, if we could wave a magic wand and you felt exactly how you wanted to feel, what would that look like for you and your family?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 8 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've so openly shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're feeling overwhelmed by fatigue and anxiety, concerned about gestational diabetes, and you deeply desire more energy and peace to truly enjoy this pregnancy and be present for both your children. Does that resonate with your experience?"
YOU:
"My approach as a Certified Prenatal Wellness Coach is unique. Unlike a quick fix or general advice, I work with you over 12 weeks to craft a personalized plan that addresses the root causes of your fatigue and supports optimal blood sugar balance, tailored specifically for pregnancy after 40. We'll focus on nutrition, stress management, and lifestyle adjustments that empower you to feel energetic, confident, and truly supported."
YOU:
"My goal is for you to not just survive this pregnancy, but to thrive, feeling connected to your body and empowered in your choices. Many of my clients, especially those navigating similar challenges, report significant improvements in energy and anxiety within the first 4-6 weeks."
Phase 4: Transition to Next Steps & Close 7 min
YOU:
"Given what we've discussed, Sarah, does this sound like the comprehensive and personalized support you've been looking for?"
YOU:
"Excellent! Let me share a bit about what working together looks like and how we can get started. My signature 12-week 'Thriving Mama' program includes..."
Coach Tip: Listen More, Talk Less

During the "Understand the Problem" phase, aim for an 80/20 listening-to-talking ratio. Ask open-ended questions and truly listen to Sarah's responses. This not only builds rapport but also provides you with the specific language and pain points to use when presenting your solution.

4. Navigating Objections: Confidence in Conversation

It's natural for prospects to have hesitations. Your ability to address these with empathy and confidence is crucial. Here are common objections and how you might respond to Sarah:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I'm worried about the cost right now, with all the baby expenses."
"I completely understand, Sarah. Investing in yourself during pregnancy is a significant decision. What I hear is that you're concerned about making a wise financial choice. Many of my clients initially feel this way, but they find that the long-term benefits of improved energy, reduced anxiety, and better health for both mom and baby far outweigh the investment. How would feeling truly vibrant and confident impact your overall budget and well-being in the coming months?"
"I need to talk to my partner first."
"That's absolutely fair, Sarah. This is a family decision. What specific questions do you anticipate your partner will have that I can help you answer now? Perhaps we could schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you, so I can explain the program directly and address any concerns you both might have? This ensures he also fully understands the value and support you'd receive."
"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has really worked long-term."
"I completely empathize, Sarah. It's frustrating when you've invested time and effort without seeing lasting results. That's precisely why my approach is different. We don't just layer on more 'things to try'; we dig deep to understand the unique underlying factors contributing to your fatigue and blood sugar imbalances. My program is highly personalized and provides consistent, structured support to implement sustainable changes, not just temporary fixes. How does the idea of a truly customized plan resonate with you?"
"I don't know if I have the time to commit to a program right now."
"I hear you, Sarah. With a toddler and another baby on the way, time is incredibly precious. The irony is, often when we feel we have the least time, that's precisely when we need support to optimize our energy and efficiency. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, focusing on small, impactful steps. We meet weekly for 45 minutes, and the 'homework' is practical and designed to save you time and energy in the long run, not add to your plate. Imagine having more energy for your kids ‚Äì wouldn't that free up more time than you think?"
Coach Tip: Validate & Reframe

The core of objection handling is to first validate the client's concern ("I understand," "That's fair") and then reframe it by highlighting the value or solution your coaching provides. Never dismiss their feelings; acknowledge them, then pivot to how you can help.

5. Presenting Your Value: Pricing & Packages

When it comes to pricing, confidence is your superpower. State your prices clearly, concisely, and with conviction. Avoid rambling or apologizing for your fees. Remember, your pricing reflects the significant value and transformation you provide.

Example Pricing Presentation for Sarah:

"Sarah, my signature 'Thriving Mama' 12-week program is an intensive, personalized journey designed for women like you. It includes:

  • Weekly 45-minute 1:1 coaching sessions via Zoom.
  • Personalized nutrition and lifestyle plan tailored to your pregnancy, energy levels, and blood sugar management.
  • Unlimited email support between sessions for questions and accountability.
  • Custom resource library with recipes, meal guides, and stress-reduction techniques.
  • Access to my exclusive community forum for peer support.

The investment for this comprehensive 12-week program is $2,500. I also offer a payment plan of three monthly installments of $850. Which option feels better for you?"

GOLD STANDARD PRACTICE

Always state the full value of your program first, then offer payment options. This frames the investment in terms of its comprehensive benefits before breaking it down. Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident.

6. Closing with Confidence: The Next Steps

After presenting your program and addressing any final questions, it‚Äôs time to ask for the commitment. A clear, direct, and supportive call-to-action is essential. Don't leave the client wondering what to do next.

Practice Closing Lines:

  1. "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe my 'Thriving Mama' program is exactly what you need to feel your best during this pregnancy. Are you ready to get started and reclaim your energy?"
  2. "Considering your goals to reduce fatigue and anxiety and manage your gestational diabetes effectively, what feels like the best next step for you ‚Äì enrolling in the full program or starting with the payment plan?"
  3. "I'm excited about the possibility of working with you, Sarah. If you're ready to move forward, I can send you the enrollment link right now, and we can schedule your first session. How does that sound?"
  4. "If you're feeling confident that this program is right for you, let's take the first step. I can send over the agreement and invoice, and we can book your initial session for next week."
Coach Tip: Silence is Golden

After you deliver your closing line, be silent. Let the prospect process and respond. It can feel uncomfortable, but it's crucial to give them space to make their decision. The first one to speak often loses the 'sale'.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem" phase in a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to deeply listen to the client, uncover their specific pain points, challenges, and desires, and understand the emotional impact of their current situation. This helps the coach tailor their solution to the client's unique needs.

2. When a client says, "I'm worried about the cost," what is the recommended first step in handling this objection?

The first recommended step is to validate their concern ("I completely understand," "That's fair") and then reframe the investment by highlighting the long-term value and transformation your coaching provides, linking it back to their stated goals.

3. Why is it important to use clear, direct language when presenting your pricing and packages?

Clear, direct language conveys confidence in your value and expertise. It prevents ambiguity, reduces the likelihood of miscommunication, and helps the client perceive your services as professional and worth the investment. Hesitation or apology undermines perceived value.

4. After delivering a closing line, what crucial action should the coach take?

After delivering a closing line, the coach should remain silent and allow the prospect to respond. This gives the client space to process the information and make their decision without feeling pressured, demonstrating respect for their autonomy.

  • Effective closing involves a clear call-to-action, allowing the client to make an empowered decision.
