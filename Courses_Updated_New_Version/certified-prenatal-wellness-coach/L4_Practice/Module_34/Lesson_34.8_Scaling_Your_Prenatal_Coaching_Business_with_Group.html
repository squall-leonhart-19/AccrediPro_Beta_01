The Power of Group Programs in Prenatal Wellness

Group programs are a game-changer for both coaches and clients. For you, they offer scalability, leveraged income, and the joy of creating a supportive community. For your clients, they provide peer support, shared learning, and often a more accessible price point than one-on-one coaching.

As we discussed in previous lessons, the demand for community-based wellness solutions is growing. A 2023 survey found that 78% of expectant mothers expressed interest in prenatal wellness programs that offered peer interaction and support (Prenatal Health Journal, 2023). This highlights a significant market opportunity for coaches like you.

Crafting Your Group Program Offer

Before you can enroll clients, you must have an offer that truly resonates. Your group program isn't just a series of sessions; it's a transformative journey. It should clearly articulate:

  • The Problem it Solves: What specific challenges do pregnant women face that your program addresses? (e.g., "overwhelmed by conflicting advice," "feeling isolated," "struggling with prenatal nutrition").
  • The Solution it Provides: How does your program uniquely address these problems? (e.g., "evidence-based nutritional guidance," "stress reduction techniques," "a supportive sisterhood").
  • The Outcome it Delivers: What tangible results or feelings will participants experience? (e.g., "confidence in birth choices," "sustained energy throughout pregnancy," "a peaceful postpartum transition").
Coach Tip

Always frame your group program's value in terms of client transformation, not just features. Instead of "8 modules on nutrition," say "8 weeks to confidently nourish your body and baby for optimal health and energy." This language connects directly to their desires.

Mastering the Group Program Discovery Call

The discovery call is your primary tool for enrolling clients into your group programs. It's not a sales pitch; it's a conversation of curiosity and connection. Your goal is to understand if your program is the right fit for the prospect's needs, and if they are a good fit for your group dynamic.

Handling Objections & Closing

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They are rarely a "no," but often a request for more information, reassurance, or a deeper understanding of value. Approach them with empathy and a problem-solving mindset.

Common Objections & Strategic Responses

  • "It's too expensive / I need to think about the investment."

    Your Response: "I completely understand that this is an investment, Maria, both of time and resources. Can I ask, what specifically about the investment gives you pause? Is it the overall amount, or are you questioning if this program will truly deliver the transformation you're seeking?" (Pause and listen.) "Think about the cost of NOT addressing these challenges â€“ the continued fatigue, anxiety, and feeling isolated. What's the cost of that to your well-being and your family? My clients often tell me the investment pays for itself many times over in peace of mind, energy, and a joyful pregnancy."

  • "I need to talk to my partner."

    Your Response: "That's perfectly natural, Maria. This is a family decision. What specific questions do you anticipate your partner might have that I could help you answer now, so you feel fully prepared for that conversation?" (Help her articulate the value. Offer to schedule a brief follow-up call with her and her partner if appropriate.)

  • "I don't have the time."

    Your Response: "I hear you, Maria. As a busy mom, time is precious. What I've learned is that often, investing a small amount of time upfront in a structured program like this actually creates more time and energy in the long run. We focus on efficient, high-impact strategies, and the weekly sessions are recorded so you can catch up if life happens. How much longer are you willing to feel this level of fatigue and anxiety before making a change that could give you more time back?"

  • "Can I just do one-on-one coaching instead?"

    Your Response: "That's a great question, and one-on-one coaching is certainly an option for some. The unique power of the group program, however, lies in the shared experience. You'll gain insights not just from me, but from other incredible women on a similar journey. The collective energy and support are truly transformative, and it often comes at a more accessible investment than individual coaching. For your desire for connection, the group dynamic would be invaluable. Would you like to explore the one-on-one option as well, or does the community aspect of the group program feel more aligned with your goal of connecting with other moms?"

Coach Tip

When an objection arises, acknowledge it, validate the feeling, and then reframe it by linking back to their stated desires and the program's unique solution. Never argue or dismiss their concerns.

Call-to-Action Practice: Closing the Enrollment

Once you've addressed concerns and reaffirmed value, it's time to invite them into the program. Be clear, confident, and direct.

  • YOU: "Maria, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the 'Mindful Mama Group Program' would provide you with the community, energy strategies, and peace of mind you're seeking for this pregnancy. Are you ready to take this step and join us?"
  • YOU (Alternative if hesitant): "Given your desire for X, Y, and Z, does this sound like the right path for you to achieve those goals?"
  • YOU (After a "yes"): "Wonderful! I'm so excited to welcome you. The investment for the 8-week 'Mindful Mama Group Program' is $797. We can get you enrolled today, and I'll send you all the details to get started. How would you like to handle payment?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a group program discovery call?

The primary purpose is to understand the prospect's needs and desires, determine if the group program is a good fit for them, and if they are a good fit for the group dynamic, rather than just delivering a sales pitch.

2. When a prospect says, "It's too expensive," what is a recommended first step in your response?

Acknowledge their concern and ask a clarifying question to understand the root of the objection, such as "What specifically about the investment gives you pause? Is it the overall amount, or are you questioning if this program will truly deliver the transformation you're seeking?"

3. How should you frame the benefits of a group program during the discovery call?

Frame benefits in terms of client transformation and outcomes, directly linking them to the prospect's stated needs and desires. Emphasize community, shared experience, and the specific problems the program solves for them.

4. What is the approximate annual revenue for a Prenatal Wellness Coach who consistently enrolls 5 clients per month into an 8-week group program priced at $797?

With 5 enrollments per month at $797 each, the monthly revenue is $3,985. Annually, this totals approximately $47,820.
Key Takeaways
  • Mastering discovery calls for group programs requires a balance of empathy, active listening, and confident presentation of value.
  • Frame your group program's benefits in terms of the client's desired transformation and specific solutions to their challenges.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide clarity and reinforce value; approach them with a problem-solving, rather than defensive, mindset.
  • Confident pricing reflects the immense value you provide and can be supported by flexible payment options.
  • Group programs offer significant scalability and income potential, allowing you to serve more clients and build a thriving practice.
