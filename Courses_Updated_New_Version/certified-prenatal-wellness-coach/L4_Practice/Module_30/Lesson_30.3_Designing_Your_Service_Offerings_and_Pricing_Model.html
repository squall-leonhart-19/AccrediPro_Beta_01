1. Crafting Your Signature Service Portfolio

Your service offerings are the vehicles through which you deliver your coaching expertise. A well-designed portfolio provides clarity for clients, maximizes your impact, and ensures a sustainable income stream. Think beyond just "sessions" and consider a range of options that cater to different needs, budgets, and commitment levels, all while embodying your BLOOM Framework‚Ñ¢ approach.

1.1. One-on-One Coaching: The Personalized Journey

This is often the core offering for many coaches, providing deep, individualized support. It's ideal for clients who require highly personalized attention for specific challenges or goals during their prenatal journey.

  • Structure: Typically 3, 6, or 12-month packages, including a set number of bi-weekly or monthly calls, direct messaging support, and personalized resources.
  • Benefits for Client: Tailored support, deep accountability, privacy, accelerated progress.
  • Benefits for Coach: Higher per-client revenue, deeper client relationships, predictable income, opportunity for profound impact.
  • Example: "The BLOOM Signature 12-Week Transformation" ‚Äì includes 6 bi-weekly 60-minute sessions, unlimited email support, personalized nutrition plans, movement recommendations, and mindset tools.

1.2. Group Programs: Community & Scalability

Group programs offer a powerful way to serve multiple clients simultaneously, fostering community and making your services more accessible. This is an excellent step towards scalability for coaches who want to move beyond purely 1:1 models.

  • Structure: Defined duration (e.g., 6-8 weeks) with weekly live group calls, a private online community (e.g., Facebook group), and shared resources.
  • Benefits for Client: Peer support, shared learning, lower investment, diverse perspectives.
  • Benefits for Coach: Increased revenue per hour, leveraged time, builds community and social proof.
  • Example: "Prenatal Power Circle: Navigating Trimester Two with Confidence" ‚Äì an 8-week program for 8-12 expectant mothers, featuring weekly group coaching calls, guest expert speakers, and a dedicated Slack channel.
Coach's Tip

When designing group programs, ensure they align with your niche. For prenatal wellness, consider trimester-specific programs, or programs focused on specific challenges like "Optimal Nutrition for Gestational Diabetes" or "Mindful Movement for Third Trimester Comfort." This specificity attracts your ideal client.

1.3. Workshops & Webinars: Lead Generation & Value Delivery

These are shorter, focused events that can be offered live (online or in-person) or as recorded content. They are excellent for attracting new leads, building your email list, and showcasing your expertise without a significant time commitment from the client.

  • Structure: 60-90 minute live sessions, often with a Q&A, or pre-recorded modules. Can be free or low-cost (e.g., $27-$97).
  • Benefits for Client: Quick, actionable insights, low commitment, introduction to your coaching style.
  • Benefits for Coach: Brand visibility, lead generation, authority building, potential upsell to higher-tier services.
  • Example: "Prep for Success: Your Prenatal Nutrition Essentials Workshop" ‚Äì a 90-minute live webinar covering key prenatal nutrition principles, ending with an invitation to a full group program or a discovery call.

1.4. Digital Products & Online Courses: Passive Income Potential

For coaches looking to scale significantly, digital products and self-paced online courses offer an opportunity for passive income. These leverage your expertise without requiring your active, real-time presence for every client interaction.

  • Structure: Pre-recorded video lessons, downloadable workbooks, templates, checklists, audio guides.
  • Benefits for Client: Self-paced learning, affordable access to expertise, immediate solutions.
  • Benefits for Coach: High scalability, passive income stream, global reach, minimal ongoing time commitment after creation.
  • Example: "The BLOOM Prenatal Planning Toolkit" ‚Äì a downloadable PDF guide with customizable birth plan templates, hospital bag checklists, and postpartum recovery tips. Or a self-paced video course: "Gentle Prenatal Yoga Flows for Every Trimester."

2. Market Research: Knowing Your Value & Your Competition

Before you set your prices, you need to understand the landscape. This involves both understanding what your ideal client is willing to pay and what similar services are priced at in your market. Don't guess; investigate!

2.1. Identifying Your Ideal Client's Willingness to Pay

Your ideal client, identified in the previous lesson, has specific needs and a perceived value for solutions. Their willingness to pay is directly tied to the transformation you offer.

  • Surveys & Interviews: Conduct informal surveys or interviews with potential clients. Ask about their biggest prenatal challenges, what solutions they've tried, and what they'd be willing to invest for a lasting transformation.
  • Pain Points vs. Price: The more acute the pain point and the more comprehensive your solution, the higher the perceived value. For instance, a coach helping a client manage severe prenatal anxiety may command a higher price than one offering general wellness tips.
  • Demographics: Consider the income levels and discretionary spending habits of your niche. A busy professional in a high-income bracket might expect and be willing to pay for premium, comprehensive support.

2.2. Analyzing Competitor Offerings and Pricing

This isn't about copying, but about understanding market benchmarks and identifying opportunities to differentiate.

  1. Identify Direct Competitors: Other prenatal coaches, doulas offering coaching, or even relevant health professionals (e.g., nutritionists specializing in pregnancy).
  2. Identify Indirect Competitors: Online courses, books, free resources (e.g., pregnancy apps, YouTube channels) that clients might use instead of hiring a coach.
  3. Research Their Offerings: What services do they provide? What's included? What's their pricing structure? Look for package deals, hourly rates, group program costs.
  4. Note Differentiation: What makes them unique? What are their strengths and weaknesses? Where can you offer something different or better?

3. Calculating Your Worth: Cost of Doing Business (CODB) & Desired Income

Many coaches underprice because they don't fully understand their Cost of Doing Business (CODB) or clearly define their income goals. This is a critical step to ensure your practice is not just impactful, but also financially viable.

3.1. Understanding Your Fixed and Variable Costs

Calculating your CODB involves itemizing all expenses necessary to run your business, both those that stay consistent and those that fluctuate.

  • Fixed Costs (Monthly/Annually):
    • Website hosting & domain: $10-$50/month
    • CRM/Scheduling software (e.g., Acuity, Practice Better): $20-$100/month
    • Email marketing software (e.g., ConvertKit, Mailchimp): $0-$50/month (depends on list size)
    • Professional insurance: $30-$60/month
    • Professional memberships/certifications: $10-$50/month
    • Continuing education/supervision: $50-$200/month (average)
    • Marketing tools/ads: Variable, but budget $100-$500+ initially
    • Virtual assistant/bookkeeper (if applicable): $100-$500+
  • Variable Costs (Per Client/Per Program):
    • Specialized software for client resources (e.g., meal planning app subscription): $10-$30/month per client if applicable
    • Physical materials (e.g., welcome kits, handouts): $5-$20 per client
    • Payment processing fees (e.g., Stripe, PayPal): 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction

Action: Tally your estimated monthly fixed costs. This is your baseline for staying open.

3.2. Setting Your Income Goals

What do you *need* and *desire* to earn from your coaching business? Be realistic, but also aspirational. Many career changers are looking for meaningful work that also provides financial freedom and flexibility.

  • Personal Living Expenses: Calculate your monthly personal expenses (mortgage, groceries, utilities, savings, etc.).
  • Desired Profit: How much extra do you want for savings, investments, or discretionary spending?

Example: If your monthly personal expenses are $3,500 and you desire an additional $1,500 in profit, your target net income is $5,000/month.

3.3. The Pricing Formula: Value + Costs + Profit

Now, combine your CODB and income goals to determine your revenue target, and subsequently, your pricing.

Total Monthly Revenue Goal = Monthly CODB + Desired Monthly Net Income

If your monthly CODB is $500 and your desired net income is $5,000, your total monthly revenue goal is $5,500. If you aim to work with 5 one-on-one clients per month, each client package needs to generate $1,100 (after accounting for payment processing fees and variable costs).

This simple calculation reveals that Maria, from our opening case study, charging $75/hour for single sessions, was likely generating insufficient revenue to cover her CODB and personal needs. A typical 3-month package with 6 sessions at $1,500, for example, would instantly elevate her income potential and provide more value for her clients.

4. Strategic Pricing Models for Prenatal Wellness Coaches

Choosing the right pricing model is crucial. It impacts client perception, your administrative load, and your bottom line. We recommend moving away from hourly rates towards value-based packages.

4.1. Package-Based Pricing: Value-Driven & Predictable

This is often the most effective model for coaches. Instead of selling time, you sell a comprehensive solution and a transformative journey.

  • How it Works: Offer multi-session packages (e.g., 3-month, 6-month) that include a specific number of coaching calls, support between calls, resources, and a clear outcome.
  • Why it's Effective:
    • For Clients: Clarity on the entire journey, focus on transformation, better value for money, commitment to results.
    • For Coaches: Predictable income, less administrative hassle (no hourly tracking), encourages deeper client commitment, positions you as a solution provider, not a time-seller.
  • Example: "The BLOOM Second Trimester Thrive Package" ‚Äì a 3-month program including 6 bi-weekly 60-minute calls, personalized meal ideas, movement routines, a stress reduction toolkit, and unlimited text/email support for $1,800.

4.2. Tiered Pricing: Meeting Diverse Needs

Offering different tiers of your services allows you to cater to a wider range of clients while still providing clear value at each level.

  • How it Works: Create 2-3 distinct packages (e.g., Basic, Standard, Premium) with varying levels of access, support, and deliverables.
  • Why it's Effective:
    • For Clients: Provides choice, allows entry at different price points, feels empowering.
    • For Coaches: Captures clients with different budgets, encourages upsells, maximizes revenue.
  • Example:
    • Bronze BLOOM: 3-month package, 4 sessions, email support (e.g., $1,200)
    • Silver BLOOM: 3-month package, 6 sessions, email + text support, personalized resources (e.g., $1,800)
    • Gold BLOOM: 3-month package, 6 sessions, unlimited support, VIP access to workshops, partner integration (e.g., $2,500)

4.3. Retainer Models: Long-Term Engagement

This model is particularly suitable for clients seeking ongoing, comprehensive support throughout their entire prenatal journey and potentially into postpartum. It provides stability for both coach and client.

  • How it Works: A fixed monthly fee for a pre-defined level of service, often including a certain number of calls, ongoing check-ins, and flexible support.
  • Why it's Effective:
    • For Clients: Continuous support, peace of mind, seamless transition through trimesters.
    • For Coaches: Highly predictable recurring revenue, fosters deep, long-term relationships.
  • Example: "The Year of BLOOM: Full Prenatal & Postpartum Support" ‚Äì a 12-month retainer program for $500/month, including bi-weekly calls, immediate text support, and access to all digital resources.

5. Articulating Value: Compelling Service Descriptions

Once you've designed your services and set your prices, the final step is to communicate their value effectively. Your descriptions should not just list features; they must paint a picture of the transformation your client will experience.

5.1. Focus on Transformation and Benefits

Clients don't buy coaching sessions; they buy solutions to their problems and a better future. Shift your language from "what it is" to "what it does for them."

  • Weak: "Six 60-minute coaching calls."
  • Strong: "Experience 6 personalized coaching calls designed to reduce prenatal stress by 40%, boost your energy, and empower you with a clear birth vision, leaving you feeling calm and confident for motherhood."
  • Weak: "A prenatal nutrition plan."
  • Strong: "Receive a customized prenatal nutrition plan that eliminates guesswork, supports optimal fetal development, and helps you manage common pregnancy discomforts like nausea and fatigue, ensuring you feel vibrant throughout your pregnancy."

Think about the emotional and practical benefits. How will they *feel*? What tangible results will they *achieve*?

5.2. Clear Deliverables and Expectations

While focusing on transformation, it's also crucial to be clear about what clients will actually receive. This builds trust and manages expectations.

  • Be Specific: Clearly list what's included: number of sessions, duration, type of support (email, text, portal), specific resources (workbooks, guides, templates), access to communities.
  • Set Boundaries: Clearly state your availability for support between sessions (e.g., "email support during business hours, Monday-Friday"). This prevents burnout and ensures a professional relationship.
  • Outline the Process: Briefly explain the journey. "Our journey begins with a comprehensive intake, followed by bi-weekly deep-dive sessions, and concludes with a personalized postpartum plan."
Coach's Tip

Use client-centric language: Avoid jargon. Speak directly to your ideal client's desires and fears. Use words like "you," "your," "empower," "confident," "peace of mind," "thrive," and "supported." This creates an immediate connection and helps them envision themselves benefiting from your services.

Key Takeaways

  • Your service portfolio should be diverse, including one-on-one, group programs, workshops, and digital products, to cater to varied client needs and maximize your reach and income potential.
  • Thorough market research is essential to understand both what your ideal client values and what the competitive landscape dictates, informing your pricing strategy.
  • Accurately calculating your Cost of Doing Business (CODB) and defining clear income goals are foundational steps to ensure your practice is not only impactful but also financially sustainable and profitable.
  • Adopt strategic pricing models like package-based or tiered pricing instead of hourly rates, as these better reflect the comprehensive value you provide and offer greater income predictability.
  • Craft compelling service descriptions that emphasize client transformation, benefits, and clear deliverables, using client-centric language to resonate deeply with their desires and needs.
