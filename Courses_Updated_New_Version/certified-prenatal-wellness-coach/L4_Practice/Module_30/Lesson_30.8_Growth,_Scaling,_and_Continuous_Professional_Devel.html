The Discovery Call Script: Your 30-Minute Framework

A well-structured discovery call isn't about being rigid; it's about having a roadmap that ensures you cover all essential points while remaining authentic and client-centered. Here‚Äôs a proven 30-minute framework:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah, it's wonderful to connect! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I really appreciate you reaching out. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear a little bit about you. How did you hear about my work?"
SARAH:
"Hi Emma! Yes, I'm Sarah. My friend, Emily, mentioned you in our moms' group. She said you helped her so much with her second pregnancy. I also read your blog post on perinatal anxiety, which really resonated."
YOU:
"That's fantastic! Emily is a wonderful woman, and I'm so glad my work resonated with you. So Sarah, tell me ‚Äî in your own words, what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understand the Problem & Desired Outcome 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. You mentioned feeling overwhelmed and anxious, especially with managing work and your toddler. Can you tell me more about how this anxiety manifests for you? What does a typical day feel like?"
SARAH:
"Well, I wake up already tired, and the nausea is constant. I dread bedtime because I know I'll just lie there, worrying about everything ‚Äì the baby, work deadlines, being a good mom to my son. It's just a constant loop of 'what ifs'."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging, Sarah. It takes so much courage to acknowledge and share those feelings. What have you tried so far to manage these symptoms or feelings of overwhelm? What worked, what didn't?"
SARAH:
"I've tried meditation apps, my doctor suggested more rest (which is impossible!), and I've cut back on caffeine. Nothing really sticks, or the relief is very temporary. I feel like I'm just pushing through each day."
YOU:
"I hear you. Many women in your position feel that way. Let me ask you, if nothing changes, and you continue feeling this overwhelmed and anxious, where do you see yourself in a year, after the baby arrives? And conversely, if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel supported and empowered throughout this pregnancy, what would your life look like? What would you be doing, and how would you feel?"
Coach Tip

The "magic wand" question is powerful. It shifts the client from problem-focused to solution-focused, allowing them to articulate their deepest desires. This helps you understand their true motivation and gives you anchors to refer back to when discussing your program's benefits.

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value Proposition 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a devoted mom embarking on a second pregnancy, feeling overwhelmed by anxiety, persistent nausea, and fatigue, all while trying to balance work and family life. You've tried various approaches but haven't found sustainable relief, and you deeply desire to genuinely enjoy this pregnancy and prevent a recurrence of postpartum depression. Does that feel accurate?"
SARAH:
"Yes, that's exactly it. You've really captured it."
YOU:
"Wonderful. The approach I use is different from what you've tried before. As a Certified Prenatal Wellness Coach, I don't just offer quick fixes or generic advice. We work together over a structured period ‚Äì typically 12 to 16 weeks ‚Äì to create a truly personalized roadmap. This involves diving deep into your unique physiological needs, lifestyle, and emotional landscape to identify the root causes of your anxiety and fatigue. We'll implement evidence-based strategies covering nutrition, stress management, sleep optimization, and emotional resilience techniques. My clients often report feeling a significant shift in energy and emotional balance within the first 4-6 weeks, allowing them to approach their pregnancy with confidence and joy."
YOU:
"This isn't just about managing symptoms; it's about empowering you with tools and knowledge for a thriving pregnancy and a smoother transition into motherhood. We'll focus on building your resilience so you can feel present, energetic, and truly enjoy this special time, avoiding the pitfalls you experienced before."
Coach Tip

Always summarize their pain points before presenting your solution. This demonstrates deep listening and ensures the client feels heard and understood. Then, clearly articulate how your unique approach directly addresses their specific challenges and leads them to their desired "magic wand" outcome.

Handling Common Objections

It's rare for a client to say "yes" immediately without any hesitation. Objections are a natural part of the sales process; they're often signs of genuine interest tempered by fear, uncertainty, or a need for more information. Your ability to address them confidently and empathetically is crucial.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and you deserve to feel confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure I've addressed all your concerns." (Listen intently, then address the specific concern. If it's about the investment, reiterate the long-term value and transformation. If time, discuss flexibility or breaking down the commitment.)
"It's too expensive. I've spent so much money already."
"I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty. It sounds like you've invested quite a bit in your wellness journey already without getting the lasting results you hoped for. My program is an investment in a different kind of outcome ‚Äì a truly personalized, evidence-based approach designed to address the root causes of your symptoms, not just manage them. Consider the cost of continuing to feel overwhelmed, anxious, and unwell. What is the value of truly enjoying your pregnancy and preventing PPD? Many clients find this investment pays for itself in peace of mind, improved health, and long-term well-being. We can also discuss payment plan options if that helps." (Pivot to value, highlight the cost of inaction, and offer solutions.)
"I'm not sure I have the time with my toddler and work."
"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, and many busy moms feel the same way. What I've found is that dedicating a focused amount of time now, with structured support, actually saves you time and energy in the long run. Imagine the time and mental space you'll gain when your anxiety lessens, your energy improves, and you're not constantly searching for solutions. My program is designed to integrate into your life, not add more stress. Our sessions are bi-weekly, and the strategies we implement are practical and efficient. We're building sustainable habits, not adding more to your to-do list." (Acknowledge, reframe as a time-saver, emphasize practical integration.)
"How is this different from therapy or what my doctor has told me?"
"That's an excellent question. While therapists and doctors are vital, our roles are complementary. A therapist focuses on mental health diagnosis and treatment. Your doctor provides medical care. As a Prenatal Wellness Coach, my focus is on empowering you with proactive, holistic strategies for your physical and emotional well-being throughout pregnancy. I bridge the gap by providing personalized guidance on nutrition, stress resilience, sleep, and lifestyle changes that often aren't covered in routine medical appointments. We're actively building habits and a support system to help you thrive, not just manage symptoms. It's about empowering you to be an active participant in your wellness journey." (Clearly define your unique scope and value proposition.)

Confidently Presenting Your Pricing

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but it's a moment of truth for both you and your client. Approach it with confidence, clarity, and conviction. Remember, you are offering immense value that can transform someone's prenatal experience.

Key Principles for Pricing Presentation:

  1. State the Price Clearly: Don't mumble or rush. State the full price of your package or program directly.
  2. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, stop talking. Allow the client to process and respond. The first one to speak often loses.
  3. Connect to Value: Always link the price back to the transformation and benefits you discussed. "The investment for this 12-week program is X, and it's designed to give you Y results."
  4. Offer Options: If appropriate, have a clear payment plan ready. This can significantly reduce budget-related objections.
  5. Believe in Your Worth: Your confidence in your pricing reflects your belief in the value of your services.

Example Dialogue for Pricing:

YOU:
"Sarah, based on everything we've discussed, I believe my 'Thriving Mama' 12-week Prenatal Wellness Program is exactly what you need to achieve your goals of a calmer, more energetic pregnancy and prevent PPD. This comprehensive program includes bi-weekly 60-minute coaching sessions, a personalized wellness plan, tailored resources, and unlimited email support between sessions. The investment for this transformative journey is $2,800. We also offer a flexible three-month payment plan of $950 per month. How does that sound, and what questions do you have about getting started?"

Notice how the price is stated, followed by a brief summary of value, and then an open-ended question that invites dialogue rather than a simple "yes" or "no."

Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels completely natural. The more comfortable you are, the more confident you'll appear. Record yourself and listen back to identify any hesitation or lack of conviction.
