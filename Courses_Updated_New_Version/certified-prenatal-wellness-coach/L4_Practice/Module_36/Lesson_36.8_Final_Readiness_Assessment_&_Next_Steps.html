1. Your Prospect: Sarah ‚Äì A Case for Connection

Meet Sarah. She represents a typical client you might encounter ‚Äì someone who has tried many things, feels frustrated, and is looking for a genuine solution. Your role in the discovery call is to be her guide, her empathetic listener, and her potential solution.

üë©

Sarah, 42

Found you via a local mom's Facebook group recommendation. Scheduled a discovery call after reading your blog post on "Beyond Morning Sickness: Holistic Energy Boosts."

Her Situation
Second pregnancy, 16 weeks along. Chronic fatigue, significant weight gain despite "eating healthy," overwhelmed by conflicting advice, struggling with sleep. Feels isolated.
Budget Concern
"My husband and I are watching our finances with a new baby coming. Is this a big investment? I've spent money on so many things that didn't work."
Decision Style
Analytical, needs data and evidence. Values trust and connection. Wants to feel understood, not just prescribed to.
Her Goal
"I want to enjoy this pregnancy, not just survive it. I want more energy, better sleep, and to feel confident in my body and choices. I don't want to feel this tired anymore."

Coach Tip

Remember, a discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a discovery conversation. Your primary goal is to listen deeply, ask insightful questions, and genuinely understand your prospect's pain points and aspirations. The more you listen, the more you can tailor your message to their specific needs.

2. The 30-Minute Discovery Call Script: Your Roadmap to Connection

This script provides a structured framework, but remember to let the conversation flow naturally. Authenticity is key!

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Emma, and I'm really looking forward to learning more about you. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about my work?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name/Facebook group] led you here. I love that community! So Sarah, tell me ‚Äî what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on for you?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Deepen Empathy 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned chronic fatigue and feeling overwhelmed. Can you tell me more about that? When did it start, how does it affect your daily life and your ability to enjoy this pregnancy?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these issues? What worked, what didn't, and what's your experience been with conventional advice?"
YOU:
"I hear how frustrating that must be. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a few months, or when the baby arrives? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel supported and energized throughout this pregnancy, what would that look like for you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Sarah, based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a busy mom, 16 weeks pregnant, feeling exhausted and overwhelmed, struggling with weight, and you're looking for personalized support to truly enjoy this pregnancy and feel like yourself again. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Prenatal Wellness Coach is quite different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just managing symptoms, we focus on identifying the root causes of your fatigue and discomfort. We'll work together over 12 weeks to create a personalized plan ‚Äì integrating nutrition, stress management, movement, and mindset ‚Äì specifically tailored to your unique body and pregnancy stage. Many of my clients, like Rachel, a 40-year-old expecting her third, start feeling significantly more energized and confident within the first 4-6 weeks."
YOU:
"My goal is to empower you with tools and knowledge that will serve you not just now, but throughout your entire motherhood journey, ensuring you have the energy and vitality you deserve."
Phase 4: Transition to Offer & Close 5 min
YOU:
"So Sarah, based on what I've shared about my approach and what you're looking for, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive support you've been looking for to achieve your goals?"

Coach Tip

Practice active listening! Nod, summarize, and reflect back what you hear. Phrases like "What I'm hearing is..." or "It sounds like you're feeling..." validate their experience and build trust. This is critical for connecting with clients and demonstrating empathy.

3. Confident Pricing Presentation: Articulating Value

Once you've confirmed your approach resonates, it's time to talk about the investment. Deliver your pricing with confidence, focusing on the transformative value, not just the cost.

YOU: "Wonderful! So, let me tell you a bit about how my programs work and the investment involved. My core offering is a 12-week 'Radiant Pregnancy' program, designed to provide comprehensive, personalized support through your unique journey."

YOU: "This program includes weekly 60-minute coaching calls, a personalized wellness plan updated bi-weekly, unlimited email support between sessions, and access to my exclusive resource library. The investment for this 12-week transformation is $2,500. I also offer a 6-month 'Empowered Motherhood' program for $4,500, which provides extended support and deeper integration of sustainable habits."

YOU: "I understand this is an investment, and many of my clients see it as an investment in their health, their baby's well-being, and their peace of mind during this critical time. I do offer flexible payment plans to make it more accessible."

Coach Tip

State your price clearly and then pause. Don't rush to fill the silence. Let the client process the information. This demonstrates your confidence in your value and allows them space to consider.

4. Mastering Objections: Your Compassionate Responses

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy, validate their concerns, and pivot back to value.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That's a lot of money. I need to talk to my husband/think about it."
"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and it's natural to want to discuss it with your partner. Can I ask, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment itself, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the program that I can clarify for you now? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
"I've spent so much money on things that haven't worked before. How is this different?"
"That's a very valid concern, Sarah, and I hear that frustration often. What makes my approach different is its personalized, root-cause methodology. We don't just treat symptoms; we uncover what's truly driving your fatigue and challenges. Unlike generic diets or advice, your plan is custom-built for YOUR body, YOUR pregnancy, and YOUR lifestyle. Plus, as your dedicated coach, I provide accountability and continuous support, which is often the missing piece in other programs."
"I'm already so busy and overwhelmed. I don't think I have the time for this."
"I completely empathize, Sarah, especially with a busy family and another baby on the way. The beauty of this program is that it's designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, not add more stress. We focus on efficient, impactful changes. In fact, many clients find that by gaining more energy and clarity, they actually SAVE time and reduce overwhelm in other areas of their lives. Our weekly calls are structured, and the personalized plan streamlines your efforts, so you're not wasting time on things that don't work."

5. The Confident Close: Your Call to Action

After addressing objections, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Be clear, calm, and confident.

YOU: "Given everything we've discussed, Sarah, and your desire to feel energized and truly enjoy this pregnancy, I genuinely believe my 'Radiant Pregnancy' program is exactly what you need. How would you like to move forward today?"

YOU: "If you're ready to start feeling better and more confident in your prenatal journey, we can get you enrolled today. I have a few openings for new clients starting next week. Does that sound like a good fit for you?"

YOU: "I'm excited about the possibility of working with you, Sarah. What are your thoughts on getting started?"

Coach Tip

Always end with a clear call to action. Don't leave it vague. Whether it's "Are you ready to enroll?" or "Shall we book your first session?", provide a direct path forward. This shows leadership and confidence.

Check Your Understanding

1

During the "Understand the Problem" phase, what is the most critical question to ask to uncover a client's deepest motivations?

The most critical question is often about the future: "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like?" This helps the client articulate the cost of inaction and the profound desire for change.
2

When a client raises the objection, "It's too expensive," what is the most effective initial response strategy?

The most effective initial response is to validate their concern ("I completely understand, it's an investment") and then immediately pivot to value by asking, "Can I ask, what specifically about the investment concerns you? Or, what would it be worth to you to feel ?" This helps uncover if it's a true financial barrier or a perceived lack of value.
3

Emma recommends a 12-week 'Radiant Pregnancy' program for $2,500. If she enrolls 3 clients per month, what would her estimated annual income be from this program?

If Emma enrolls 3 clients per month at $2,500 each, her monthly income would be 3 * $2,500 = $7,500. Her estimated annual income would then be $7,500 * 12 months = $90,000.
4

Why is it important to provide a clear Call to Action (CTA) at the end of a discovery call?

A clear CTA is crucial because it removes ambiguity and guides the client to the next step. It demonstrates your confidence and leadership, making it easier for the client to make a decision. Without a clear CTA, clients might feel uncertain, leading to procrastination or inaction.
