Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Normalizing Technique" critical in the first 15 minutes of a session?

It lowers the client's cortisol and shame levels, making them more receptive to coaching and behavioral changes. Shame is a "stopper" for sexual wellness; normalization is the "key."

2. If Diane asks, "Do I need hormone replacement therapy?", what is the correct practitioner response?

"That is a great question for your medical provider. As your Sexual Wellness Practitioner, I can help you prepare a list of questions for them and support you in the lifestyle and emotional aspects of that transition."

3. What is the primary purpose of using a "Pleasure Snack" vs. a "Sexual Goal"?

It removes the pressure of "performance" and focuses on small, achievable wins that rebuild the neural pathways for pleasure without the anxiety of a "finish line."

4. How does stating your role clearly help with imposter syndrome?

It defines the scope of your expertise. You don't have to be a doctor or a therapist; you just have to be an expert in the Practitioner Role you are being trained for right now.
