Common Mistakes & Success Markers

4 Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Lecture" Trap: Talking for 10 minutes straight without checking in. Use the "Ask-Provide-Ask" method.
  • Avoiding the "O" Word: Being too shy to discuss orgasm or pleasure directly. If you're uncomfortable, they will be too.
  • Over-Medicalizing: Focusing only on hormones and forgetting the relationship or psychological context.
  • Giving Advice Too Soon: Trying to "fix" it before the client fully understands the "why." Education must come before the action plan.

How You Know You Succeeded

  • The "Exhale": The client physically relaxes their shoulders when they realize they aren't "broken."
  • Curiosity: The client starts asking follow-up questions like, "So, how do we work with responsive desire?"
  • Language Shift: The client starts using the correct terms you taught them instead of vague euphemisms.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Ask-Provide-Ask" framework in education?

Ask for permission to share info, Provide the education in bite-sized pieces, then Ask the client how that information resonates with them.

2. Why is "Responsive Desire" a critical concept for women in long-term relationships?

It normalizes the fact that desire often follows physical arousal/stimulation rather than preceding it, which reduces the "I'm not in the mood" shame.

3. If a client uses a euphemism like "down there," how should the coach respond?

Acknowledge their point while gently and professionally using the correct term (e.g., "vulva" or "pelvic floor") to model comfort and clarity.

4. What is the primary indicator that your educational intervention worked?

The client moves from a state of shame/failure ("I'm broken") to a state of curiosity and understanding ("My body is changing and I can adapt").
