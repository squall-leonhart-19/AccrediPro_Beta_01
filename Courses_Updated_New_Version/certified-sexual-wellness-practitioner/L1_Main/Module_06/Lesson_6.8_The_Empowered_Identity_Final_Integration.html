Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced practitioners can fall into these traps when focusing on empowerment.

  • The "Fix-It" Trap: Giving a list of supplements or toys before the client has reclaimed their agency. (Empowerment must come before tools).
  • Toxic Positivity: Saying "You're still beautiful!" when the client is expressing genuine grief over aging. (Acknowledge the grief first).
  • Over-Talking: Empowerment requires space. If you are talking more than 30% of the time, the client isn't finding their own voice.
  • Ignoring the "No": Forgetting to validate that "No" is a powerful, empowered sexual choice.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to take "going along with it" off the table for a client like Elena?

2. What is the danger of the "Fix-It" trap in empowerment coaching?

It reinforces the idea that the client is "broken" and needs external tools to be "fixed," rather than recognizing they have internal agency.

4. What is a "10% shift" in coaching?
