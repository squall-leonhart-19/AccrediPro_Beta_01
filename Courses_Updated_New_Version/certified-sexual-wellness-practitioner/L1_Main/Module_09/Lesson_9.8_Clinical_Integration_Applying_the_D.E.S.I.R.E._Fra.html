The Power of the Pause

Notice the pause in the script. In somatic coaching, silence is an intervention. If you talk too much, you pull the client back into their cognitive brain. Give them at least 5-10 seconds to find the words for their physical sensations.

Practice Exercise: Speak It Out Loud

Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

When I first started, I wanted to "fix" the client's numbness immediately. Here is what to avoid:

  • Leading the Witness: Don't ask, "Does it feel like a tight knot?" Instead, ask, "What does it feel like there?" Let them choose the metaphor.
  • Over-Intellectualizing: If the client says "I think it's because of my mother," gently bring them back: "I hear that thought, but what is your stomach feeling right now as you say that?"
  • Ignoring Your Own Body: If you feel anxious or rushed, the client will sense it and their "protective fog" will thicken. Check your own breath every 5 minutes.

Success Markers: How Do You Know It Worked?

A successful somatic session doesn't always end with a "breakthrough." Sometimes, success looks like small physiological shifts:

Spontaneous Sigh

The client takes a deep, involuntary breath. This indicates the nervous system is shifting from Sympathetic (fight/flight) to Parasympathetic (rest/digest).

Softening of Features

The jaw relaxes, or the "11 lines" between the eyebrows smooth out. This is a sign of increased safety.

Vivid Imagery

When a client moves from "I'm fine" to "I feel like a lead weight in my hips," they have successfully accessed their felt sense.

Check Your Understanding

1. If Elena says, "I don't know why I feel this way," what is the best somatic response?

Instead of analyzing the "why," redirect her to the "what." Say: "The 'why' might come later, but for now, what is the physical sensation of 'not knowing' in your body?"

2. What is the primary purpose of the "Somatic Pause"?

To allow the client's slower interoceptive pathways to catch up with their fast cognitive thoughts, facilitating a deeper connection to physical sensations.

3. Elena describes her numbness as a "heavy grey fog." What is this an example of?

This is a "Felt Sense" metaphor. It is the body's way of communicating complex internal states through symbolic physical descriptions.

4. Why should a practitioner monitor their own nervous system during a session?

Because of "co-regulation." A client's nervous system mirrors the practitioner's. If you are grounded and calm, it signals to the client's brain that it is safe to drop their defenses.
