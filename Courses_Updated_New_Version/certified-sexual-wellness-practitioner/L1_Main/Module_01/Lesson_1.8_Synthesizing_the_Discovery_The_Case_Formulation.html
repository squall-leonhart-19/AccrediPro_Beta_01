Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the Discovery phase?

The primary goal is to build rapport, identify the client's root desires/concerns, and determine if there is a fit for a professional coaching relationship. It is about uncovering, not fixing.

2. Why should you use the client's specific language (like "numb") back to them?

This is called "Mirroring." It demonstrates that you are listening deeply and validates their unique experience, making them feel seen and understood.

3. When is the appropriate time to transition to your "offer" or program details?

You transition only after the client has expressed their pain points and goals, and you have validated them. You should always ask permission first (e.g., "Would you like to hear how we could work together on that?").

4. How long should you allow silence to last after a client shares something emotional?

A good rule of thumb is 3-5 seconds. This "sacred pause" allows the client to process their own words and feel the safety of your presence.
