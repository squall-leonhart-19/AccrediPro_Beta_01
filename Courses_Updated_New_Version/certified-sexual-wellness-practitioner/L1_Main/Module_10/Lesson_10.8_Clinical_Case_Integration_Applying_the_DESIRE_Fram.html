Success Markers: How Did You Do?

How do you know if your practice session (or real call) was a success? Look for these four indicators:

  1. Exhale Moment: Did the client visibly relax or sigh? This indicates they feel safe.
  2. Shift in Language: Did the client move from "I don't know" to "I feel"?
  3. Clear Boundaries: Did you manage to stay warm without becoming their "friend"?
  4. Next Steps: Did you end the call with a clear professional recommendation rather than a "think about it"?
Pro Tip

If you feel your face getting hot when a client mentions sex, that's okay! Just take a breath and focus on your notes. Your professional curiosity is your best shield against awkwardness.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Transition Statement" (e.g., "In this practice, we believe...") so important for career changers?

It establishes professional legitimacy and moves the conversation from a casual/personal exchange to a structured, clinical/coaching framework. This helps overcome imposter syndrome by leaning on the methodology of the practice.

2. What should you do if a client uses humor to deflect a serious question about their intimacy?

Acknowledge the humor briefly (to maintain rapport) but then gently "circle back" to the underlying emotion. Example: "I love your sense of humor, Elena, but I also hear a bit of frustration behind that joke. Is that fair to say?"

3. True or False: You should share your own personal sexual history to make the client feel more comfortable.

False. This is the "BFF Trap." While brief, high-level relatability is okay, your role is to remain the professional guide. Extensive self-disclosure shifts the focus away from the client's needs.

4. What is the "Exhale Moment" in a discovery call?

It is a physiological sign that the client's nervous system has shifted from "fight or flight" (anxiety about the topic) to "rest and digest" (feeling safe and heard). It is a key indicator of successful rapport building.
