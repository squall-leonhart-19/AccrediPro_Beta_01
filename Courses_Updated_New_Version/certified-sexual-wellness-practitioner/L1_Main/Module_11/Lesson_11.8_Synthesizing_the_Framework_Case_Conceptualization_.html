Check Your Understanding

1. Why did the practitioner frame Elena's "pulling away" as "intelligent protection" rather than a problem?

To neutralize the shame narrative. When a client feels "broken," their nervous system is in a state of threat. Reframing the symptom as a "protective mechanism" allows the client to feel safe enough to explore change.

2. Elena mentions feeling like a "ghost." What coaching technique is best used here?

Empathic Mirroring. Using the client's exact metaphor ("ghost") shows them they are deeply heard and validated, which is the foundation of the coaching container.

3. What is the danger of "Over-Explaining Science" in a sexual wellness session?

It causes "cognitive bypass." Sexual wellness is an embodied experience; too much technical data shifts the client into their analytical mind, making it harder to connect with their physical sensations and emotions.

4. How many seconds should you ideally wait after a client finishes a vulnerable share?

At least 5 seconds. This "Golden Silence" allows the client to process their own words and often leads to a deeper, secondary realization that wouldn't happen if the coach spoke immediately.
