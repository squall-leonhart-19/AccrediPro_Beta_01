Referral Triggers & Scope of Practice

Even with advanced training, knowing when to refer is the hallmark of a professional. For Elena, we must monitor for "Red Flags" that indicate her Hashimoto's or Hypertension is poorly managed.

  • Cardiovascular Risk: If Elena reports sudden chest pressure or a resting heart rate over 100 bpm (tachycardia), refer to her MD immediately (potential Levothyroxine over-medication or hypertensive crisis).
  • Neurological Changes: Sudden, severe "brain fog" that includes loss of orientation or slurred speech requires an ER referral.
  • Autoimmune Flare: If her joint pain becomes accompanied by a "butterfly rash" on the face or significant swelling, refer for a Lupus (SLE) screening.

1. Why is it contraindicated to give Elena heavy Yin-nourishing herbs (like Rehmannia) in Phase 1?

Monitoring for a chronic dry cough (a common side effect of ACE inhibitors like Lisinopril) is vital. In TCM, this can be mistaken for Lung Yin Deficiency, but it is actually a drug-induced "Branch" symptom that requires MD consultation for medication adjustment.
