The Holographic Principle of Microsystems

The concept of a microsystem is rooted in the holographic nature of the human body: the idea that a part of the body reflects the whole. While traditional acupuncture focuses on the 12 primary meridians, microsystems like the ear (auricular) and the scalp provide a concentrated map of the entire organism.

Safety and Needling Mastery

Precision is paramount when working with the head and ears. The scalp is highly vascular, and the ear contains sensitive cartilage.

  • Scalp Depth: Needles should be inserted at a 15-30 degree angle, sliding 0.5 to 1.5 inches into the subaponeurotic space. Never needle perpendicular to the skull.
  • Auricular Caution: Avoid needling through the cartilage whenever possible to prevent perichondritis (infection of the cartilage). Use a "pinch" technique to isolate the skin.
  • Bleeding: The scalp bleeds easily. Always have clean cotton balls ready and apply firm pressure for 30 seconds upon needle withdrawal.
