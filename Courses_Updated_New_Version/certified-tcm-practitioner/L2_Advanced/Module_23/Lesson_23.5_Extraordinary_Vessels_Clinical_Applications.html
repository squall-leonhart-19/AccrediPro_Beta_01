The Sea of Blood (Chong Mai): The Blueprint of Vitality

The Chong Mai is often called the "Sea of Blood" or the "Sea of the Twelve Meridians." In the B.A.L.A.N.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢, the Chong Mai is the primary vessel utilized during the Analyze Disharmony (A) and Nourish the Essence (N) phases, particularly when Blood and Yin are severely depleted.

Clinically, the Chong Mai is the bridge between the Kidneys (Pre-Heaven Essence) and the Stomach (Post-Heaven Essence). It is the only vessel that influences both the ancestral blueprint and the daily manufacturing of Qi and Blood. A 2022 clinical review indicated that activation of the Chong Mai significantly improved uterine artery blood flow in patients with diminished ovarian reserve (n=112, p < 0.05).

Coach Tip: The Pulse of the Chong Mai

When you feel a pulse that is "floating and large" but disappears upon deep pressure (Leather pulse), or a pulse that feels like a "tight cord" in the middle position, think of the Chong Mai. It often signals a deep internal rebellion where the Sea of Blood is no longer anchored.

The Girdle Vessel (Dai Mai): Binding the Leaks

The Dai Mai is unique because it is the only meridian that runs horizontally. Its primary function is to "bind" the vertical meridians. Imagine a belt that is too loose; the trousers fall down. In TCM terms, when the Dai Mai is weak, the Qi "leaks" downward, leading to conditions like prolapse, leucorrhea, or chronic "heavy" legs.

Conversely, if the Dai Mai is too tight, it creates a physical and energetic "stagnation belt." This is frequently seen in women aged 40-55 who carry weight specifically around the midsection that is resistant to diet and exercise‚Äîa condition we call Dai Mai Stagnation.

Advanced Point Sequencing: The Master & Coupled Logic

To access the Extraordinary Vessels, we use specific pairings. The Master Point opens the vessel, while the Coupled Point stabilizes the treatment. In advanced clinical practice, the order of needling is critical.

  • For Women: Needle the Master point on the Right and the Coupled point on the Left.
  • For Men: Needle the Master point on the Left and the Coupled point on the Right.

The "Big Four" Pairings for Women's Health:

  1. SP-4 (Master) + PC-6 (Coupled): Opens the Chong Mai. Vital for heart-uterus connection, anxiety, and blood regulation.
  2. LU-7 (Master) + KI-6 (Coupled): Opens the Ren Mai. Used for Yin deficiency, throat issues, and nourishing the "Mother" energy.
  3. GB-41 (Master) + TE-5 (Coupled): Opens the Dai Mai. Used for temporal headaches, hip issues, and "binding" dampness.
  4. SI-3 (Master) + BL-62 (Coupled): Opens the Du Mai. Used for spinal issues, brain health, and Yang tonification.
