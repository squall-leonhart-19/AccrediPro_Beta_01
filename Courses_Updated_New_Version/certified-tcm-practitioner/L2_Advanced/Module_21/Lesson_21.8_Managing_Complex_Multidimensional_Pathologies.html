  • Formula Strategy: Transition to Shen Qi Wan (Kidney Qi Pill) or Li Zhong Wan to rebuild the fire.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it contraindicated to give Elena heavy Yin-tonifying herbs (like Shu Di Huang) in Phase 1?

Heavy Yin tonics are "cloying" and greasy. Because Elena has Spleen Qi Deficiency and Dampness (swollen tongue, bloating), these herbs would "drown" her Spleen, worsening her bloating and loose stools. You must clear dampness before adding heavy tonics.

2. What does the "Cun" position wiry pulse indicate in this specific case?

The wiry pulse in the Cun (Heart/Lung) position suggests that Liver Qi Stagnation is "rising" and affecting the Upper Jiao, contributing to her anxiety and potentially her night sweats (Heart Fire).

3. Which B.A.L.A.N.C.E. Method‚Ñ¢ pillar is most critical for Elena's compliance?

The "E" (Education/Empowerment). Elena is a high-level executive; she needs to understand the "why" behind the phases. Explaining the "Heat Above, Cold Below" concept helps her understand why we aren't just giving her cooling herbs for her sweats.

4. If Elena's night sweats were accompanied by a red, peeled tongue, how would your strategy change?

A red, peeled tongue indicates true Yin Deficiency with Heat, rather than just Empty Heat from Yang Def. You would need to prioritize nourishing Yin and clearing Heat much earlier in the protocol, though still being mindful of her Spleen's strength.
