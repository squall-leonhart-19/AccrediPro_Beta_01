Wood Overacting on Earth: Liver-Spleen Disharmony

In the Five Element theory (which we will cover deeper in Module 4), the Liver belongs to the Wood element and the Spleen to the Earth element. Naturally, Wood (roots) penetrates Earth. However, when Liver Qi stagnates, it becomes aggressive‚Äîthis is known as Wood overacting on Earth.

This is the primary mechanism behind "stress-induced" digestive issues. The Liver's stagnant energy "attacks" the Spleen's ability to transform and transport nutrients.

Physical Manifestations: The Map of Constraint

Liver Qi Stagnation doesn't just stay in the abdomen; it follows the Liver and Gallbladder channel pathways. For your female clients, this often manifests in three specific areas:

  • Breast Distension: The Liver channel passes through the breast area. Stagnation here causes tenderness and swelling, often fluctuating with the hormonal cycle.
  • Menstrual Irregularities: Because the Liver stores Blood and moves Qi, stagnation leads to painful periods (dysmenorrhea) and dark, clotted menstrual flow.
  • The "Plum Pit" Sensation (Mei He Qi): A feeling of something stuck in the throat that cannot be swallowed or coughed up, which worsens with emotional stress.

  2. LV3 (Taichong): Located on the foot, it is the "Source Point" of the Liver and the primary point for resolving stagnation.
