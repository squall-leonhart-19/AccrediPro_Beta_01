Common Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced nurses or teachers transitioning into coaching make these common "rookie" errors when locating stagnation:

  • Over-Explaining Theory: Don't spend 20 minutes talking about the Liver Meridian. Keep it focused on the client's feeling of being stuck.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Stagnation is almost always tied to suppressed frustration or "unspoken" words. If you ignore the emotion, the Qi won't move.
  • Rushing to the "Fix": New coaches often want to suggest herbs or acupuncture immediately. In the L (Locate) phase, your only job is to make the client feel understood.
  • Sounding Uncertain: Using words like "I think maybe it could be..." weakens your authority. Use "Based on what you've shared, we are looking at..."
