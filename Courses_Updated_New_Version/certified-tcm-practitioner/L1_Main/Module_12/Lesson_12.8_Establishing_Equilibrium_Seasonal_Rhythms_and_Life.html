Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • The "Firehose" Effect: Trying to explain the entire history of TCM in the first 10 minutes. Keep it simple.
  • Being Too Clinical: Using terms like "Zang-Fu" or "Damp-Heat" without explaining them. This triggers imposter syndrome in you and confusion in the client.
  • Fixing Instead of Coaching: Don't just give a list of herbs. Ask, "How does this plan feel to you?" to ensure buy-in.
  • Ignoring the Emotional Component: Diane is a retired teacher; she likely feels a loss of identity. Acknowledge the person, not just the Spleen.
Sarah's Insight

If you feel stuck, just breathe and say: "Let me make sure I'm understanding you correctly..." This gives you 10 seconds to think while making the client feel heard. It's my favorite "pro" trick!

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these signs:

  • The "Sigh of Relief": The client physically relaxes their shoulders when you validate their fatigue.
  • Active Participation: They start using your metaphors (e.g., "Oh, so my campfire is low!").
  • Low Resistance: They agree to the first small step (like the warm breakfast) without listing 10 reasons why they can't.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use metaphors like "campfire" instead of just saying "Spleen Qi Deficiency"?

Metaphors bridge the gap between complex theory and the client's lived experience. It increases compliance because the client understands the "why" behind the recommendation.

3. What is the primary goal of the "Integration Session"?

To move from assessment to a collaborative plan that the client feels confident and empowered to start immediately.
