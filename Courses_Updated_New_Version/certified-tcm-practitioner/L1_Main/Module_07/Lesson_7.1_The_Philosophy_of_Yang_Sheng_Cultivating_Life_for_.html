The Three Treasures: The Foundation of Equilibrium

To establish equilibrium, a practitioner must manage the San Bao or "Three Treasures." These are the three essential energies that sustain human life:

  • Jing (Essence): Your "biological battery." It governs growth, reproduction, and constitutional strength. It is stored in the Kidneys.
  • Qi (Vital Energy): The "electricity" that powers your organs. It is the day-to-day energy derived from food and breath.
  • Shen (Spirit/Mind): The "light" in the eyes. It encompasses consciousness, emotions, and mental clarity.

In the 40-55 year old demographic, we often see a "Jing-Qi Gap." The client has used up their "Post-Heaven Qi" (lifestyle energy) and is now tapping into their "Pre-Heaven Jing" (inherited reserves). This leads to rapid aging and burnout.

Coach Tip: Assessing the "Shen"

You can assess a client's Shen in the first 30 seconds. Does their gaze wander? Is their speech pressured? A scattered Shen will eventually deplete the Qi. Teach your clients that mental quietude is a physiological necessity, not a luxury.

Every person is born with a specific "constitutional bank account." Some are born with a "trust fund" of Jing, while others must be more frugal. Establishing equilibrium requires identifying where the client is overspending.
