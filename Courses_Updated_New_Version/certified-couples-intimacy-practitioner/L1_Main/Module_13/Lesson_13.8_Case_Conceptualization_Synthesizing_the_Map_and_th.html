Common Mistakes to Avoid

In our 2023 practitioner survey, we identified four key areas where new coaches struggle during the Deep Dive phase:

  • Fixing instead of Facilitating: Jumping in with "You should go on a date night!" before Elena has even processed her loneliness.
  • Colluding with the Client: Agreeing that Mark is "the problem." A coach remains a neutral third party who focuses on the dynamic, not the person.
  • Ignoring the Body: Failing to notice when Elena crosses her arms or looks away. Always ask: "I noticed your voice got a bit quieter just thenâ€”what was happening for you?"
  • Over-sharing: Telling Elena your own marriage story for 10 minutes. Use self-disclosure sparingly and only if it serves the client's growth.

    Check Your Understanding

    1. Why is it important to ask Elena "How does it feel to say that out loud?" instead of "Why do you feel that way?"

    Asking "How" keeps the client in their emotional experience (somatic), while "Why" often forces them into their head to intellectualize or justify their feelings, which stalls the intimacy-building process.

    2. What is the "Vulnerability Bridge" in the context of this lesson?

    It is a communication technique that allows a client to share a deep need or fear with their partner in a way that invites connection rather than defensiveness or "sounding needy."

    3. According to the Success Markers, what is a sign of "Ownership Language"?

    When the client shifts from blaming the partner (e.g., "He never talks") to recognizing their own role in the dynamic (e.g., "I've stopped inviting him in").

    4. What is a "Fixing" mistake in this scenario?

    Giving surface-level advice like "go on a date night" before the client has had the chance to process the underlying emotional distance and loneliness.
