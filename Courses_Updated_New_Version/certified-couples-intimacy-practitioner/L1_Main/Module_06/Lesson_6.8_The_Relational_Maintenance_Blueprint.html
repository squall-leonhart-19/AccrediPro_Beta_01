Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even experienced coaches can stumble. Watch out for these four traps:

  • The "Fixer" Trap: Trying to solve Diane's problems for her. Instead, give her the tools (like the script) to solve them herself.
  • Taking Sides: It's easy to sympathize with Diane and view Mark as the "villain." Remember: The relationship is your client, not just the person in front of you.
  • Ignoring the Body: Intimacy isn't just talk. If you don't eventually address physical touch and somatic connection, the mastery is incomplete.
  • Over-Explaining: New coaches often talk too much to prove their value. Practice the "80/20 Rule"â€”the client should speak 80% of the time.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of a "Softened Startup" in relational coaching?

The goal is to raise a concern or need without triggering the partner's defensiveness, allowing for a productive conversation rather than an escalation into conflict.

2. According to the lesson, what is the "80/20 Rule" in professional coaching?

The client should be speaking 80% of the time, while the coach speaks only 20% of the time, primarily to ask powerful questions and provide framework pivots.

3. Why is the "Empty Nest" period considered a "high-opportunity" time for intimacy coaches?

Couples often lose their primary "distraction" (parenting) and are forced to face their underlying relationship dynamics, making them highly motivated to seek professional help to reconnect.

4. How should a coach respond when a client like Diane says, "He never listens"?

A coach should validate the feeling but pivot to a "Softened Startup" exercise, helping the client rephrase the global complaint ("never listens") into a specific need ("I miss feeling heard").
