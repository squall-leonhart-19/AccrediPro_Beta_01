Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary neurobiological goal of the 'Soft Start' protocol?

The goal is to prevent the "Amygdala Hijack" and physiological flooding, keeping the prefrontal cortex online so the partner can remain empathetic and receptive rather than defensive.

2. According to the Translation Matrix, what is the underlying wish behind the criticism: "You're always working late"?

The underlying wish is usually a desire for Somatic Presence or Ritualized Connection. A vulnerable translation would be: "I feel lonely in the evenings and I really value our time together; I would love to have dinner together at least three nights this week."

3. How does Empathetic Resonance differ from standard Active Listening?

Active listening is cognitive and involves paraphrasing words. Empathetic Resonance is somatic and involves attuning to and mirroring the partner's emotional state, activating mirror neurons for deeper connection.

4. At what heart rate is a partner considered "flooded," requiring a somatic time-out?

When the heart rate exceeds 100 BPM (beats per minute), the individual is physiologically flooded and unable to process information rationally.
