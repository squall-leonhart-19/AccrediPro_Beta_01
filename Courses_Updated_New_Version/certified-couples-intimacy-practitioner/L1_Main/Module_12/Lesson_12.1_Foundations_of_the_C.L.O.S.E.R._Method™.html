Scope of Practice & Ethical Boundaries

As a Practitioner, clarity on your scope is your greatest protection and your greatest professional asset. We do not diagnose mental health disorders, and we do not provide "therapy" for acute trauma.

  • We ARE: Educators, facilitators, and somatic guides who provide structured protocols for healthy couples to deepen their connection.
  • We ARE NOT: Licensed psychologists, medical doctors, or sex therapists (unless you hold those separate licenses).
Coach Tip: The Referral Network

If a client discloses active domestic violence or severe untreated PTSD, your ethical duty is to refer them to a clinical specialist. Professionalism is knowing when to say, "This is outside my scope, but I can help you find someone who can support you there."

The Practitioner's Mindset: Facilitating vs. Fixing

One of the hardest shifts for career changersâ€”especially former teachers and nursesâ€”is moving away from the "fixer" role. In the C.L.O.S.E.R. Methodâ„¢, we believe the couple is the expert on their own relationship. You are the guide who provides the map and the compass.

The three pillars of the Practitioner Mindset are:

  1. Radical Neutrality: You do not take sides. You are "on the side" of the relationship itself.
  2. Somatic Presence: You model a regulated nervous system. If you are anxious, the couple will feel unsafe.
  3. Clinical Empathy: You validate their experience without becoming enmeshed in their drama.
