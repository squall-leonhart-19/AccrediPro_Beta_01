The Art of the Discovery Call: Beyond the Script

The discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a conversation. It's about connection, empathy, and co-creation. Your goal is to determine if you are the right guide for their journey and to empower them to take the next step. A successful call leaves the prospect feeling heard, understood, and hopeful about their future.

Research indicates that clients are four times more likely to purchase when they feel deeply understood by a service provider (Gartner, 2022). This isn't about memorizing lines, but internalizing a framework that allows your authentic self, and the power of Forest Therapy, to shine through.

Mastering the Discovery Call Script: A 30-Minute Blueprint

This is a suggested structure for a 30-minute discovery call. Remember, it's a guide, not a rigid script. Be present, listen intently, and let the conversation flow naturally.

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 minutes)

Your primary goal here is to create a safe, welcoming space and establish a human connection.

  • Opening: "Hi Eleanor, thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's lovely to connect. How are you doing today?"
  • Gratitude & Connection: "I'm so glad [referring friend's name] connected us. She's been on a wonderful journey with Forest Therapy. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to hear a little about what sparked your interest in Forest Therapy Coaching specifically."
  • Permission: "My intention for this call is to understand what's going on for you, share a bit about how Forest Therapy Coaching works, and see if it feels like a good fit for us to work together. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: Deep Dive into Their World (10 minutes)

This is the listening phase. Ask open-ended questions and truly hear Eleanor's story, pain points, and aspirations.

  • Current Challenges: "Eleanor, you mentioned feeling overwhelmed and disconnected. Can you tell me more about what that feels like on a day-to-day basis? When did you first notice these feelings?"
  • Impact: "How does this impact your life? Your energy, your relationships, your ability to do the things you used to enjoy?"
  • Past Attempts: "What have you tried so far to address these challenges? What worked, and what didn't feel quite right?"
  • Vision of the Future: "If you could wave a magic wand and wake up feeling exactly how you want to feel, what would that look like? What would a 'connected' and 'joyful' Eleanor be doing?"
Coach Sarah's Insight

Listen for their emotional language ‚Äì frustration, longing, exhaustion. Reflect it back to them ("It sounds like you're feeling a deep sense of longing to reconnect with that joy"). This builds profound trust and shows you truly understand. Avoid interrupting or offering solutions yet.

Phase 3: Presenting Your Unique Solution (10 minutes)

Bridge the gap between their problem and your solution. Explain how Forest Therapy Coaching is uniquely positioned to help them achieve their desired future.

  • Summarize & Validate: "Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly. What I'm hearing is that you're feeling deeply exhausted and disconnected, longing to rediscover your spark and joy, especially through nature. You've tried various approaches, but nothing has quite addressed this deep-seated burnout. Does that resonate with you?" (Wait for confirmation).
  • Introduce Your Approach: "The beautiful thing about Forest Therapy Coaching is that it's fundamentally different. Instead of just managing stress, we tap into your innate wisdom and the profound healing power of nature to address the root causes of disconnection and burnout. We don't just talk about nature; we immerse in it, cultivating mindfulness, sensory awareness, and a profound sense of belonging."
  • Outline Your Program (briefly): "My signature program, 'Reclaiming Your Wild Heart,' is a 12-week journey where we meet weekly for guided outdoor sessions, explore practices that bring you back into balance, and build sustainable habits for well-being. My clients often report significant shifts in energy, sleep, and overall joy within the first 4-6 weeks."
  • Connect to Their Vision: "Imagine feeling that sense of calm and clarity you described, experiencing renewed energy, and reconnecting with the natural world that used to bring you so much joy. That's the transformation we work towards."

Phase 4: The Confident Close & Next Steps (5 minutes)

This is where you invite them to commit to their transformation.

  • Check for Fit: "Eleanor, based on what I've shared, does this approach sound like what you've been searching for? Does it feel like a path that could truly help you reclaim your spark?"
  • Present the Offer: "Wonderful! Let me tell you how my 12-week 'Reclaiming Your Wild Heart' program works and what the investment is. The program includes 12 weekly 90-minute guided Forest Therapy sessions, personalized nature-based practices, a digital journal, and ongoing support. The investment for this transformative 12-week journey is $2,400. I also offer a payment plan of three monthly installments of $850."
  • Call to Action: "Typically, clients are ready to get started once they feel this alignment. How does that sound to you, Eleanor?"
Coach Sarah's Insight

Practice saying your prices out loud, confidently and without hesitation. Your energy around your pricing speaks volumes. Remember the immense value you provide ‚Äì a journey of profound healing and reconnection that can't be quantified in dollars alone.

Navigating Objections with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses:

  1. "I need to think about it."

    Your Response: "I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision. To help you reflect, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the clarity you need."

    (This helps uncover the root of the hesitation. Address it directly.)

  2. "It's too expensive / I've spent so much money already."

    Your Response: "I hear you, Eleanor. It sounds like you've made significant investments in your well-being already, and you want to ensure this is truly different. Many of my clients come to me after trying various things. What sets Forest Therapy Coaching apart is its holistic, nature-immersed approach that addresses the underlying patterns of stress and disconnection, leading to sustainable change. When you consider the cost of ongoing fatigue, anxiety, and feeling disconnected from your life, what would it be worth to you to finally reclaim your vitality and joy?"

    (Reframe the cost in terms of value and the cost of inaction.)

  3. "I'm not sure I have the time."

    Your Response: "That's a very valid concern, especially with your current responsibilities. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, with weekly sessions and practices that are easily woven into your routine. In fact, many clients find that by committing to this process, they actually gain more time and energy because they become more efficient and less overwhelmed. What does freeing up your time and energy mean for your daily life?"

    (Acknowledge, validate, and reframe the time investment as a time-saver in the long run.)

  4. "Forest Therapy sounds lovely, but can it really help with deep-seated stress and anxiety?"

    Your Response: "That's an excellent question, and it's one I hear often. While Forest Therapy Coaching might sound gentle, its impact is profound. We're talking about scientifically-backed practices that reduce cortisol, boost mood, and enhance cognitive function. A meta-analysis of over 70 studies (n=15,000+) on nature immersion found significant reductions in stress hormones and improved mental well-being (Twohig-Bennett & Jones, 2018). My approach combines these proven methods with personalized coaching to create lasting change. It's not just 'being in nature'; it's a guided, intentional process of healing and growth."

    (Provide evidence and emphasize the structured, intentional nature of your coaching.)

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive into Their World" phase of a discovery call?

The primary goal is to actively listen and understand the prospect's current challenges, pain points, and their desired future state. It's about gathering information and building empathy, not yet offering solutions.

2. Eleanor expresses a budget concern, stating, "It's too expensive / I've spent so much money already." How should you generally respond to this objection?

Acknowledge and validate their concern, then reframe the investment in terms of the value and long-term transformation your program offers, and the cost of *not* addressing their issues. Avoid immediately lowering your price.

3. When presenting your program, what is a crucial element to include that connects back to the client's initial sharing?

It's crucial to connect your program's benefits directly to their stated desires and vision of the future. Help them visualize how your coaching will help them achieve the transformation they articulated earlier in the call.

4. According to Coach Sarah, why is it important to practice stating your prices out loud confidently?

Practicing your pricing confidently helps convey your belief in the value of your services. Your energy and conviction around your prices can significantly influence a prospect's perception of your program's worth and their willingness to invest.
Key Takeaways
  • The Discovery Call is a conversation of connection and empathy, not a sales pitch, focused on understanding the client's needs.
  • Structuring your call into phases (Rapport, Deep Dive, Solution, Close) provides a clear, effective framework.
  • Active listening and validating client emotions are paramount for building trust and demonstrating understanding.
  • Confidently articulate the unique value of Forest Therapy Coaching, linking it directly to the client's desired transformation.
  • Approach objections with grace and curiosity, seeing them as opportunities to provide more clarity and reinforce value.
  • Your income potential as a Forest Therapy Coach is substantial and flexible, growing with your confidence and client base.
