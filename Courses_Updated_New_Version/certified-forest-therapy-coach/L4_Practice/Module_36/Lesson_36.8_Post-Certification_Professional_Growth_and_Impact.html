Business Practice Lab: Your First Discovery Call

This lab is designed to give you hands-on experience with one of the most vital aspects of client acquisition: the discovery call. We'll walk through a simulated call, providing you with a framework to confidently engage, assess, and enroll your ideal clients.

The Power of the Discovery Call: Beyond the Conversation

A discovery call is often the first real interaction a potential client has with you. It's not a sales pitch; it's a mutual exploration. For you, it's an opportunity to understand their challenges, assess their readiness for change, and determine if your Forest Therapy Coaching approach is the right fit. For them, it's a chance to feel heard, understand your methodology, and envision a path forward.

The primary goals of a discovery call are:

  1. Build Rapport: Create a safe, empathetic space where the prospect feels comfortable sharing.
  2. Uncover Needs: Listen deeply to understand their pain points, aspirations, and what they've tried before.
  3. Educate & Empower: Briefly explain how Forest Therapy Coaching works and how it can address their specific challenges.
  4. Qualify & Align: Determine if they are genuinely ready to invest time, energy, and resources into their well-being.
  5. Present Your Solution: Offer your program as the logical next step, if there's a good fit.
Coach Tip: Active Listening is Your Superpower

During a discovery call, aim for an 80/20 split: listen 80% of the time, speak 20%. Ask open-ended questions like, "Tell me more about that," or "How does that impact your daily life?" This isn't just about gathering information; it builds trust and makes the client feel truly seen and understood.

Crafting Your Irresistible Offer: Packaging Your Value

Before you get on a discovery call, you need to clearly define what you offer. This isn't just a list of services; it's a transformation. How do you package your expertise into a program that delivers tangible results? Consider structuring your offerings into signature programs that address specific client journeys, often spanning 3 to 6 months.

A well-defined program typically includes:

  • Duration: A clear timeframe (e.g., 12-week journey, 6-month immersion).
  • Session Frequency: How often you meet (e.g., bi-weekly 60-minute sessions).
  • Core Methodology: Emphasize your unique application of the ROOTS Frameworkâ„¢.
  • Support Mechanisms: What additional support do they get (e.g., email support, nature-based reflections, custom invitations)?
  • Desired Outcomes: Focus on the transformation, not just the activities. (e.g., "rekindle inner peace," "reduce stress by 30%," "deepen connection to self and nature").

Navigating Objections with Grace: Building Trust, Not Resistance

It's natural for potential clients to have reservations. Your role isn't to dismiss these objections but to acknowledge them with empathy and address them with confidence. Common objections often revolve around time, money, and skepticism.

When an objection arises, remember the "Feel, Felt, Found" framework:

  1. Feel: Acknowledge their feeling ("I understand how you feel...").
  2. Felt: Relate to it ("Many of my clients have felt similarly...").
  3. Found: Share the positive outcome ("...but what they've found is that...").

This approach validates their experience while gently guiding them toward a new perspective.

Coach Tip: Empathy Dissolves Resistance

Instead of countering objections, lean into them. "That's a valid concern." "I hear you, this is a significant commitment." When clients feel understood, their guard lowers, opening the door for genuine conversation and trust-building.

Confident Pricing & Closing Strategies: Owning Your Worth

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but remember the value you provide. You are offering transformation, not just hours of your time. Frame your pricing as an investment in their well-being, not an expense.

When discussing pricing:

  1. Be Direct and Clear: State your program name and the investment required without hesitation. "My 12-week Roots Reawakening program is an investment of $X."
  2. Offer Payment Options: Make it accessible. "I offer a payment plan of 3 monthly installments of $Y."
  3. Reiterate Value: Briefly connect the investment back to the transformation they desire. "This investment not only provides you with deep nature connection but also equips you with lifelong tools to manage stress and cultivate inner peace."
  4. Pause and Listen: After stating the price, resist the urge to fill the silence. Let them process and respond.

For closing, use a soft, invitational approach. "How does that sound to you?" or "Are you ready to begin this journey?"

Coach Tip: Practice Your Pitch Aloud

The more you rehearse discussing your programs and pricing out loud, the more natural and confident you'll sound. Practice in front of a mirror, record yourself, or role-play with a friend. This builds muscle memory for your message.

Handling Brenda's Potential Objections

Brenda's profile suggests she's analytical and budget-conscious. Here are some likely objections and how you might respond:

Common Objections & Your Responses

"That's a significant investment, especially on a fixed income."
"I completely understand how you feel, Brenda. Many of my clients, especially those in retirement, have felt concerned about the financial commitment. What they've found is that by investing in their well-being now, they actually save money in the long run by reducing stress-related health issues, improving their sleep quality, and gaining tools to navigate life's transitions more gracefully. This isn't just an expense; it's an investment in your peace of mind and quality of life for years to come. We can explore the payment plan if that makes it more comfortable."
"I'm not sure if 'walking in the woods' will really solve my anxiety. I need something practical."

What they've found, however, is that Forest Therapy Coaching is a highly structured and evidence-based practice. We use specific invitations and sensory exercises that are incredibly practical for training your nervous system to relax, focus your mind, and build resilience. Coach Tip: The Power of Silence

After you present your program or respond to an objection, resist the urge to keep talking. Let silence hang in the air. This gives the client space to process, think, and respond. Often, they will fill the silence with their true thoughts, giving you valuable insight.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of a Forest Therapy Coach discovery call?

The primary goal is mutual exploration: for the coach to understand the prospect's needs and determine fit, and for the prospect to feel heard, understand the methodology, and envision a path forward. It's about serving, not just selling.

2. When a client expresses a financial concern, what is an effective framework to use in your response?

The "Feel, Felt, Found" framework: "I understand how you feel (acknowledging their emotion). Many of my clients have felt similarly (relating to their experience). What they've found is that... (sharing the positive outcome or reframing)."

3. How should you present your program pricing to a potential client?

Present pricing directly and clearly, stating the program name and investment without hesitation. Offer payment options to increase accessibility, and briefly reiterate the value and transformation the investment provides. Then, pause and allow the client to respond.

4. What is a key non-verbal communication strategy to employ after presenting your program or responding to an objection?

Utilize the power of silence. After you've spoken, resist the urge to fill the void. This allows the client time to process, think, and articulate their true thoughts or questions, often leading to deeper insights and better engagement.
