1. The Power of the Discovery Call

As a Forest Therapy Coach, your services offer profound, life-changing benefits. The discovery call is the essential bridge between a curious prospect and a committed client. It's a structured conversation designed to understand their challenges, articulate the value of your approach, and determine if there's a mutual fit.

Understanding its Purpose

A discovery call serves multiple critical functions:

  • Build Rapport: Establish trust and connection. People work with coaches they like and trust.
  • Qualify the Prospect: Determine if their needs align with your services and if they are genuinely ready for change.
  • Uncover Pain Points and Desires: Go beyond surface-level issues to understand their deepest motivations and what a successful outcome looks like for them.
  • Educate and Inform: Briefly introduce the unique benefits of Forest Therapy and your coaching methodology.
  • Position Yourself as an Expert: Demonstrate your knowledge and ability to guide them.
  • Present Your Solution: Articulate how your programs can help them achieve their desired outcomes.
  • Determine Fit: Ensure that both you and the prospect feel confident and excited about working together.

Coach Tip

Remember, you're not just offering walks in the woods. You're offering a path to profound well-being, stress reduction, and reconnection. Approach each call with the intention to serve, not to sell, and the energy will shift dramatically. Your genuine desire to help will be palpable.

3. Structuring Your Discovery Call: The 4-Phase Framework

Here's a proven structure for a 30-minute discovery call. Practice speaking these lines aloud, imagining Eleanor on the other end of the line.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for carving out this time to chat with me today. It's lovely to connect. To start, how are you doing today, and how did you hear about Forest Therapy coaching?"
ELEANOR:
"I'm doing okay, thanks. A friend of mine mentioned it after seeing a post on social media. I've been feeling a bit lost since retiring, and she said it might help me find some calm."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear you're exploring new avenues for well-being. Many people feel a similar shift in identity and purpose after a big life change like retirement. So, Eleanor, what specifically made you decide to reach out to me today? What's been on your mind?"
Phase 2: Deep Dive into Needs & Desires 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling a bit lost and struggling with anxiety. Can you tell me more about that? When did these feelings start, and how do they impact your daily life, your sleep, or your relationships?"
ELEANOR:
"Well, honestly, it's been building. I used to thrive on the structure of teaching, but now I wake up and sometimes feel... adrift. My mind races, especially at night, and I just can't seem to relax. I snap at my husband more often, and I just don't feel like myself."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly challenging, Eleanor. It takes a lot of courage to acknowledge these feelings. What have you tried so far to manage this anxiety or find that sense of connection you're looking for?"
ELEANOR:
"I've tried meditation apps, joining a book club, even some gentle yoga. They help a little, but it's like a temporary fix. I feel like I need something deeper, something that truly shifts my perspective. I just want to feel calm, focused, and excited about life again."
YOU:
"If we fast forward six months, and you've found that calm and excitement, what does your life look like then? What are you doing, and how do you feel?"
Phase 3: Connect to Forest Therapy & Your Solution 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, thank you for sharing so openly. Based on what you've described ‚Äì the desire for calm, renewed purpose, and deeper connection ‚Äì it sounds like you're really seeking a way to re-center yourself and embrace this new chapter with vitality. Does that resonate with you?"
ELEANOR:
"Yes, absolutely. That's exactly it."
YOU:
"Wonderful. My approach as a Certified Forest Therapy Coach is designed precisely for this. We don't just 'take walks'; we engage in mindful, sensory practices in nature that are scientifically proven to reduce stress, improve sleep, and foster a profound sense of connection and clarity. We'll work together over a structured 12-week program, blending guided outdoor experiences with personalized coaching to help you cultivate resilience, rediscover your inner wisdom, and define your next meaningful steps. Many of my clients, especially those navigating life transitions, report feeling significantly more grounded and purposeful within the first month or two."
Phase 4: Confident Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"Given what you've shared and how Forest Therapy coaching can address those needs, does this sound like the kind of supportive guidance and transformative experience you've been looking for, Eleanor?"
ELEANOR:
"It really does sound appealing. I just... I'm not sure about the cost."
YOU:
"I completely understand that. Let's talk about the investment, and I'll explain exactly what's included. Then we can explore if this is the right next step for you."

4. Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are a natural part of any sales conversation. They are not rejections of you or your service, but rather expressions of concern or a need for more information. Approach them with empathy, validation, and clarity.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's too expensive, especially now that I'm retired."
"I completely understand your concern about the investment, Eleanor, especially with a fixed income. Many of my clients feel the same initially. What they've found, however, is that this is an investment in their long-term well-being and peace of mind ‚Äì something that truly pays dividends. Think about the cost of continued anxiety, sleepless nights, or feeling adrift. My 12-week program is designed to create sustainable shifts, offering tools and practices you'll use for a lifetime. We can discuss payment plans if that helps make it more accessible."
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand that this is an important decision, and I want you to feel confident. To help you reflect, could you share what specifically you need to think about? Is it the program structure, the time commitment, or perhaps the investment? Knowing this helps me ensure I've addressed all your questions."
"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has really worked long-term."
"I hear your frustration, Eleanor, and it's valid to feel that way after trying various approaches. What makes Forest Therapy coaching different is its holistic, experiential nature combined with personalized guidance. We're not just offering another technique; we're cultivating a deep connection with nature that inherently promotes healing, and we're building sustainable practices into your daily life. This isn't a quick fix, but a journey of profound self-discovery and lasting change. My clients often tell me it's the most impactful thing they've ever done for their well-being because it addresses the root of their disconnection."

Coach Tip

When an objection comes up, pause. Breathe. Validate their feeling. Then, gently inquire to understand the root of the objection. This shows empathy and gives you the information needed to address their specific concern, rather than making assumptions.

5. Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

After addressing objections, if the client expresses continued interest, it's time to clearly and confidently present your program details and pricing. Your confidence here is key; it signals the value you place on your own services.

The AccrediPro 12-Week Signature Program

(Imagine you are speaking to Eleanor)

"Eleanor, my signature program is a 12-week immersive journey designed to help you deeply reconnect with yourself and nature, finding that calm and clarity you're seeking. It includes:

  1. Six 60-minute 1:1 Coaching Sessions: Held every other week, these are personalized sessions to explore your progress, integrate practices, and address specific challenges.
  2. Four Guided Forest Therapy Experiences: These are 2-3 hour immersive sessions in a local natural area, designed to deepen your sensory awareness and connection to the living world.
  3. Personalized Nature-Based Practices: Tailored 'invitations' for you to explore between sessions, building a consistent practice.
  4. Resource Library Access: Exclusive access to meditations, journal prompts, and guided audio walks.
  5. Ongoing Support: Direct messaging support between sessions for questions and encouragement.

The investment for this transformative 12-week program is $1,800. Many clients choose to pay in full, or we can arrange a flexible payment plan of three monthly installments of $625.

Coach Tip

State your price clearly and then stop talking. Let the silence hang. This allows the prospect to process the information. Avoid immediately justifying the price or adding more details; you've already presented the value.

Call-to-Action Practice

Once you've presented your offer and addressed any final questions, it's time for the Call-to-Action (CTA). This is where you guide them to the next step. Practice these closing lines:

  • "So Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, how does this feel to you? Are you ready to begin your journey of reconnection and calm?"
  • "If you're feeling ready to move forward and truly invest in your well-being, the next step is to secure your spot with a deposit. We can do that right now, or I can send you the enrollment link immediately after this call."
  • "What questions do you have that I haven't answered, and are you prepared to take this step towards feeling more grounded and purposeful?"

Be confident, but also be prepared for them to say 'no' for now. A 'no' is rarely a 'never,' and often just means 'not right now.' Offer to keep them on your newsletter or follow up in a few months if appropriate.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of a discovery call for a Forest Therapy Coach?

The primary goal is to build rapport, understand the prospect's needs and desires, determine if Forest Therapy coaching is a good fit, and confidently present your solution as an expert.

2. When handling the objection "It's too expensive," what key elements should your response include?

Your response should validate their concern, reframe the cost as an investment in long-term well-being, highlight the value and benefits of your program over the cost of their current struggles, and potentially offer flexible payment options.

3. Eleanor mentioned feeling "adrift" and seeking "calm, re-energized, and clarity." How would you connect Forest Therapy to these specific needs during Phase 3 of the call?

You would explicitly link Forest Therapy to cultivating resilience, rediscovering inner wisdom, and defining meaningful next steps. Emphasize how mindful, sensory practices in nature are scientifically proven to reduce stress and foster connection, directly addressing her desire for calm and clarity.

4. What is the importance of "stopping talking" after stating your program's price?

Stopping talking after stating the price allows the prospect to process the information without feeling pressured or overwhelmed. It demonstrates your confidence in the value of your offer and prevents you from inadvertently undermining your own pricing by over-explaining or justifying.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are about genuine connection and deep listening, not hard selling.
  • A structured 4-phase approach helps you confidently guide the conversation from rapport to close.
  • Empathy and validation are crucial when handling objections; seek to understand the root concern.
  • Present your programs and pricing with unwavering confidence, clearly articulating the value.
  • Consistent client acquisition, even with a few clients per month, can build a financially rewarding practice.
