2. The Discovery Call Framework

A well-structured discovery call ensures you cover all necessary points while maintaining a natural flow. Here's a 30-minute framework designed for clarity and connection.

Phase 1: Building Rapport (5 minutes)

The first few minutes are critical for establishing trust and making your prospect feel comfortable. It's about genuine human connection before diving into problems.

  • YOU: "Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
  • RACHEL: "Hi Sarah! Yes, [friend's name] recommended you. She said you really helped her with her stress."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful that [friend's name] referred you. She's been on quite a journey herself. So Rachel, tell me — what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on?"

Key takeaway: Acknowledge the referral, express gratitude, and open with a broad, inviting question.

Phase 2: Deepening Understanding (10 minutes)

This is where you listen, listen, listen. Ask open-ended questions to uncover her pain points, what she's tried, and her desired future state. Your goal is to understand her deeply, not to diagnose.

  • YOU: "Tell me more about the fatigue. When did it start? How does it affect your daily life and what brings you joy?"
  • RACHEL: "It started subtly after a particularly stressful period at work a couple of years ago. Now, I wake up tired, I crash in the afternoons, and by evening, I have no energy for anything but the couch. I used to love hiking, but I just can't find the motivation."
  • YOU: "And what have you tried so far to address this? What worked, what didn't? What did you like or dislike about those approaches?"
  • RACHEL: "I've tried different diets, more exercise — which just made me more tired. My doctor ran tests, said everything was 'normal,' and suggested I manage stress. It feels like no one really understands."
  • YOU: "That sounds incredibly frustrating, Rachel. It's disheartening when you're seeking help and feel unheard. If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life look like?"
  • RACHEL: "Honestly, I worry I'll just keep getting worse, more withdrawn. If I could wave a wand, I'd have my energy back, feel vibrant, be out in nature like I used to, and just feel like myself again."

Key takeaway: Use empathic language ("That sounds incredibly frustrating"), ask about impact, past attempts, and future desires to paint a full picture of her struggle and aspiration.

Coach Tip

Practice reflective listening. After Rachel shares something significant, paraphrase it back to her. For example, "So, what I'm hearing is that the chronic fatigue isn't just physical, but it's also stealing your joy and sense of connection to activities you love, like hiking. Is that right?" This validates her experience and shows you're truly engaged.

Phase 3: Presenting Your Solution (10 minutes)

Now, you transition from listening to explaining how Forest Therapy Coaching can bridge the gap between her current pain and desired future. Focus on the transformation, not just the features.

  • YOU: "Rachel, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're exhausted, frustrated with conventional approaches, and deeply miss feeling vibrant and connected to nature. You're looking for a holistic path to reclaim your energy and joy. Does that feel accurate?"
  • RACHEL: "Yes, exactly."
  • YOU: "The approach I use, Forest Therapy Coaching, is different from what you've tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we delve into the ROOTS Frameworkâ„¢ to help you reconnect with your innate well-being through nature. We don't just talk about being outside; we guide you through intentional practices that calm your nervous system, reduce stress hormones, and foster a deep sense of presence and vitality. I work with clients over a 12-week program to identify exactly what's driving your fatigue and create a personalized plan that integrates nature connection into your life. Most of my clients, especially those struggling with energy, start feeling significantly better and more grounded within the first 4-6 weeks."
  • YOU: "Think about the impact of consistent, guided nature immersion on your stress levels, sleep quality, and even your body's inflammatory response. This isn't a quick fix, but a sustainable pathway to long-term well-being."

Key takeaway: Summarize her pain points, validate them, then introduce your solution. Emphasize the unique benefits of Forest Therapy Coaching and the transformation it offers, linking it directly to her stated desires.

Phase 4: The Confident Close (5 minutes)

This is where you invite her to take the next step. Confidence is key here. Avoid hesitations.

  • YOU: "So Rachel, based on what we've discussed, and how Forest Therapy Coaching can support your desire to reclaim your energy and connection, does this sound like the kind of supportive, holistic approach you've been looking for?"
  • RACHEL: "It sounds really appealing, actually. I like that it's natural and focused on real change."
  • YOU: "Wonderful! I truly believe this can make a profound difference for you. Let me tell you about the program and how we can get started."

Key takeaway: Ask a direct question to gauge interest, and then, assuming a positive response, smoothly transition to discussing the program details and next steps.

3. Navigating Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're often requests for more information or reassurance, not outright rejections. Approach them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objection Empathetic & Confident Response
"I need to think about it." "I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for yourself."
"It's too expensive." "I appreciate your honesty, Rachel. It's true that investing in your well-being is a significant decision. My clients often find that the cost of inaction — the continued fatigue, the lost joy, the ongoing frustration — far outweighs the investment in a program that delivers lasting change. What value would you place on truly feeling like yourself again, with renewed energy and connection? We can discuss payment plans, but I encourage you to consider the long-term return on this investment in your health and happiness."
"I've tried everything else, how is this different?" "That's a very fair question, Rachel, and I completely hear your skepticism given your past experiences. The key difference with Forest Therapy Coaching is our focus on your unique connection to nature, combined with the structured ROOTS Frameworkâ„¢. We don't just offer generic advice; we co-create a personalized journey that leverages the profound physiological and psychological benefits of guided nature immersion. Unlike quick fixes, this is about cultivating a sustainable relationship with well-being that you carry with you long after our program concludes. It's about deep, internal shifts, not just external symptom management."
"I don't have enough time." "I understand that feeling completely, Rachel. Many of my clients come to me feeling overwhelmed and time-starved. The beauty of Forest Therapy is that we integrate it into your life in manageable, impactful ways. We start small, often with just 15-20 minutes of intentional nature connection daily, building up to longer immersions. The goal isn't to add more to your plate, but to introduce practices that actually create more energy and mental clarity, ultimately freeing up your time and reducing your sense of overwhelm. How would having more energy impact your current schedule?"

Coach Tip

When handling objections, use the A.L.A.R.M. method: Acknowledge, Listen, Ask, Reframe, Move forward. Acknowledge their concern, listen to their underlying fear, ask clarifying questions, reframe the objection into a benefit, and then move towards the next step.

4. Confidently Presenting Your Investment

This is often where new coaches feel the most discomfort. Remember, you are offering immense value and a transformative experience. Your pricing reflects your expertise and the profound impact you can have. State your price with confidence and clarity, then pause and let the client respond.

The Pricing Presentation Script

  • YOU: "Rachel, the 12-week Forest Therapy Transformation Program is designed to guide you from where you are now — feeling exhausted and disconnected — to a place of renewed energy, mental clarity, and deep connection with yourself and the natural world. This program includes weekly personalized coaching sessions, guided nature immersion practices, customized resources, and ongoing support."
  • YOU: "The investment for this comprehensive 12-week program is $2,497." (Pause. Let the number land.)
  • YOU: "For those who prefer, we also offer a convenient payment plan of three monthly installments of $897."
  • YOU: "This investment covers 12 weeks of dedicated, personalized guidance to help you reclaim your vitality and joy, and build sustainable practices for lifelong well-being. What questions do you have about the program or the investment?"

Key takeaway: Frame the price as an "investment," not a "cost." Connect it directly to the transformation and value she will receive. Offer payment options clearly. Then, invite questions.

Coach Tip

Your energy around pricing is contagious. If you hesitate, stammer, or sound unsure, your client will pick up on that. Practice saying your price out loud until it feels completely natural and confident. You are worth it, and so is the transformation you offer!

5. Crafting Your Call-to-Action

Once you've presented your program and handled any objections, it's time to clearly articulate the next steps. Make it easy for them to say "yes."

Closing Lines to Practice Out Loud:

  1. "Based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe this program is exactly what you need to achieve [her desired outcome, e.g., 'reclaim your energy and feel vibrant again']. Are you ready to take this step and begin your journey?"
  2. "If you're ready to start experiencing the transformative power of nature connection and personalized support, we can get you enrolled today. How does that sound?"
  3. "Given your goals and my expertise, I'm confident we can achieve significant results together. Shall we secure your spot in the program?"
  4. "The next step is simple: we'll get you set up with your first session, and I'll send over the enrollment details. Does that work for you?"

Remember: A strong call-to-action is direct, confident, and assumes the client is ready to move forward. It's not pushy; it's guiding them to make a beneficial decision.

Check Your Understanding

1. During Phase 2 of the Discovery Call, what is the primary goal when asking about a prospect's past attempts to solve their problem?

The primary goal is to understand what they've tried, what worked/didn't, and to identify their frustrations with previous approaches, not to critique them. This helps you position Forest Therapy Coaching as a unique and effective alternative.

2. Rachel states, "It's too expensive." According to the recommended objection handling, what should be your immediate next step after she voices this concern?

After she says it's too expensive, you should acknowledge her honesty and ask clarifying questions to understand the root of her concern (e.g., "I appreciate your honesty. What specifically about the investment is giving you pause?"). This allows you to address her specific worry rather than making assumptions.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is a crucial non-verbal cue or technique to employ after stating the price?

After stating the price, it's crucial to pause and remain silent. This allows the number to land, gives the prospect time to process, and prevents you from unconsciously filling the silence with unnecessary justifications.

4. What is the key difference between a strong Call-to-Action (CTA) and a weak one?

A strong CTA is direct, clear, and confidently assumes the prospect is ready to move forward, guiding them to the next step. A weak CTA is hesitant, vague, or leaves the decision entirely up to the prospect without clear direction, often leading to indecision.

Key Takeaways

  • Follow a structured framework (Rapport, Understanding, Solution, Close) to ensure a comprehensive and effective conversation.

  • Present your program's investment with unwavering confidence, focusing on the profound value and transformation your clients will receive.
  • Craft clear, direct calls-to-action that empower prospects to confidently take the next step in their well-being journey.
