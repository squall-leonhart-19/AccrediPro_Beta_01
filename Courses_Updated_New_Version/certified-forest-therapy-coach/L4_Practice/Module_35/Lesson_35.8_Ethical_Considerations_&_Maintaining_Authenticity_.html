Meet Your Prospect: Eleanor Vance

Your goal isn't just to "sell" her, but to genuinely understand her needs and determine if Forest Therapy Coaching is the right fit.

Eleanor Vance, 52

Former high school teacher, feeling burnt out. Found you through a local wellness event flyer.

Her Situation
Recently retired from teaching after 28 years, feels a loss of purpose, overwhelmed by free time, mild anxiety, disconnected from nature, and generally "stuck."
Budget Concern
"I'm living on a teacher's pension now, so I need to be mindful of my investments. Is this really worth it?"

Analytical, values structure and clear outcomes. Needs to feel heard and respected. Appreciates credentials and a professional approach.

Coach Tip

Remember, Eleanor isn't just a set of problems; she's a person with hopes and fears. Approach this call with genuine curiosity and empathy. Your ability to connect on a human level is your greatest asset.

The Discovery Call Script: Your 30-Minute Roadmap

A structured approach helps you stay on track, ensuring you cover all essential points while remaining authentic. This script provides a framework; adapt it to your unique style, but ensure you hit these key phases.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into what brought you here, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about Forest Therapy Coaching and AccrediPro Academy?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear you picked up a flyer at the wellness event! I'm so glad you reached out. So Eleanor, tell me â€” what prompted you to schedule this call now? What's going on for you?"
Coach Tip

Listen for emotional cues and underlying desires. Eleanor's "stuck" feeling isn't just about retirement; it's about a deeper need for purpose and connection.

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision 10 min
YOU:
"You mentioned feeling 'stuck' and a loss of purpose. Can you tell me more about how that manifests in your daily life? How does it feel to be disconnected from nature, and what impact is it having on your well-being?"
YOU:
"And what have you tried so far to address these feelings of overwhelm or disconnection? What worked, and what didn't feel quite right for you?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, if nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and truly feel connected, purposeful, and peaceful again, what would your life look like? What does that 'second chapter' truly mean to you?"
Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Value 10 min
YOU:
"Eleanor, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a woman who's given so much, now at a crossroads, seeking a deeply meaningful transition into retirement, wanting to shed feelings of anxiety and find joy and connection in nature. Does that feel accurate?"
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Forest Therapy Coachâ„¢ is quite different from quick fixes or generic wellness programs. We don't just talk about your feelings; we actively engage with the healing power of nature to foster deep introspection, reduce stress, and ignite a renewed sense of purpose. My 12-week 'Roots & Renewal' program is designed to guide you through a personalized journey, helping you build a profound connection with the natural world and, in turn, with your own inner wisdom. Clients often tell me they feel a significant shift in clarity and peace within the first 4-6 weeks."
Coach Tip

When presenting your approach, speak with conviction about the unique benefits of Forest Therapy Coaching. Emphasize the transformation, not just the activities. Use phrases that resonate with your prospect's stated goals.

Phase 4: Close & Next Steps 5 min
YOU:
"So Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, does the 'Roots & Renewal' program sound like the kind of supportive and transformative journey you've been looking for to find your peace and purpose in this new chapter?"
YOU:
"Wonderful! Let me tell you a little more about how the program works and how we can get started..."

Mastering Objection Handling

It's normal for prospects to have reservations. Your role isn't to push, but to listen, validate, and gently guide them towards clarity. Here are common objections and confident responses.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. To help me understand better, can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you're still wondering if Forest Therapy is the right fit for you?"
"It's a lot of money right now, especially on a pension."
"I hear you, Eleanor, and I appreciate your honesty. Investing in yourself is a significant decision. Many of my clients, especially those transitioning careers, have similar concerns. What I've found is that the true cost isn't just the monetary investment, but the cost of staying stuck â€“ the emotional toll of feeling disconnected, the missed opportunities for joy and purpose. Consider what it's costing you to not address these feelings. This program is an investment in your long-term well-being and a fulfilling next chapter. We can also discuss flexible payment plans to make it more manageable."
"I'm not sure if Forest Therapy is really for me. I'm not a 'nature person' necessarily."
"That's a very common thought, Eleanor, and it's perfectly okay! You don't need to be an avid hiker or a 'nature person' to benefit. Forest Therapy is about slowing down, engaging your senses, and simply being present in nature, in a way that feels comfortable and accessible to you. We start gently, and the focus is on your internal experience and connection, not on strenuous activity. Many clients, like Sarah, 48, a busy executive, initially felt similar but quickly discovered a profound sense of peace and clarity they didn't know they were missing. It's about finding your way to connect."
"I'm worried about the time commitment. I feel so busy already."
"I completely understand that feeling, Eleanor. It often feels like we're adding another thing to an already full plate. However, this program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, not add more stress. Our sessions are carefully planned, and the 'homework' is often about simple, mindful moments in nature, which actually create more time and mental space by reducing overwhelm. Think of it as reclaiming your time and energy through intentional connection, rather than adding another task. My clients find that by prioritizing this, they actually feel more productive and less rushed overall."

Confidently Presenting Your Programs & Pricing

This is often where imposter syndrome can creep in for new coaches. Remember the immense value you offer. You are providing a pathway to profound well-being and transformation. State your prices clearly, confidently, and without apology.

Structuring Your Program Offerings

Most successful Forest Therapy Coaches offer package deals, as consistent engagement yields the best results. Consider 3-month or 6-month programs for individual clients.

When you get to Phase 4 of the discovery call, you might say:

  • "The 'Roots & Renewal' 12-week program includes 6 personalized 1:1 Forest Therapy coaching sessions, a custom 'Nature Prescription' plan, guided audio meditations, and unlimited email support. The investment for this transformative journey is $2,497."
  • "For those seeking deeper, sustained transformation, I also offer the 'Seasonal Immersion' 6-month program, which includes 12 sessions, advanced nature-based practices, and a private retreat day. The investment for this comprehensive experience is $4,797."

Always follow up by addressing payment options:

  • "I also offer a flexible payment plan, allowing you to pay in three monthly installments of $850 for the 12-week program, or six monthly installments of $825 for the 6-month program."
  • "For clients who prefer to pay in full, there's a 10% discount, making the 12-week program $2,247."
Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural. Your confidence in your value directly impacts a client's confidence in investing in themselves through your program. Avoid hesitations or justifications.

Mastering the Call to Action

After presenting your program and pricing, it's time to invite your prospect to take the next step. Be clear and confident.

  • "Eleanor, are you ready to embark on this journey to rediscover your purpose and connection through nature?"
  • "Does the 'Roots & Renewal' program feel like the right next step for you?"
  • "If you're ready to start, we can schedule your first session right now. How does [mention a specific date/time] sound?"
  • "I have space for two new clients next month. If you're excited to move forward, let's secure your spot."

Silence is your friend after a call to action. Give them space to respond.

Check Your Understanding

1. During Phase 2 of the discovery call, what is the primary goal when asking about a prospect's past attempts to solve their problem?

The primary goal is to understand what approaches haven't worked for them, which helps you position Forest Therapy Coaching as a unique and effective solution, avoiding past pitfalls. It also helps you assess their level of commitment and frustration.

2. Eleanor expresses concern about the financial investment. What is an effective strategy to address this objection, according to our lesson?

Acknowledge and validate her concern. Then, reframe the cost as an investment, highlighting the "cost of staying stuck" (emotional, time, missed opportunities) and the long-term transformation your program offers. Mention flexible payment plans.

3. When presenting your program pricing, what is a key element of confident communication?

State your prices clearly and confidently, without apology or hesitation. Emphasize the value and transformation the client will receive, and be prepared to discuss payment options.

4. If Eleanor says, "I'm not sure if Forest Therapy is for me, I'm not a 'nature person'," how should you respond?

Validate her feeling, explaining that many clients feel similarly. Clarify that Forest Therapy isn't about being an "outdoor enthusiast" but about gentle, mindful connection through the senses, customized to her comfort level. Share a brief example of someone similar who benefited.

Key Takeaways

  • A well-structured discovery call focuses on building rapport, understanding needs, presenting value, and inviting commitment.
  • Listen actively to uncover core motivations and tailor your program presentation to the prospect's specific goals.
  • Objections are opportunities to clarify value and build trust, not roadblocks. Address them with empathy and confidence.
  • Present your programs and pricing with conviction, emphasizing the transformative investment in the client's well-being.
  • Practice your script and objection handling regularly to build confidence and refine your unique coaching voice.
