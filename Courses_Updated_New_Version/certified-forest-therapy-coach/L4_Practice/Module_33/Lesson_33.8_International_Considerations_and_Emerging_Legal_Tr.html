The Discovery Call Blueprint: Connecting with Purpose

A well-structured discovery call isn't about rigid scripting, but about having a clear roadmap to guide the conversation. Think of it as a journey you're taking with your prospect, designed to uncover their needs, position your solution, and invite them to take the next step.

Phase 1: Building Authentic Rapport (5 minutes)

The goal here is to create a safe, welcoming space. People work with coaches they know, like, and trust. Start by being genuinely curious about them, beyond their presenting problem.

  • Warm Welcome: Thank them for their time.
  • Personal Connection: Ask how they heard about you or what prompted them to reach out now.
  • Set the Stage: Briefly explain the call's purpose ‚Äì to explore their needs and see if your program is a good fit.

Coach Tip

Remember, this is a conversation, not an interrogation. Your tone should be warm, curious, and empathetic. Focus on making them feel seen and heard right from the start. A genuine smile, even over the phone, can make a huge difference!

Phase 2: Deep Dive into Client Needs (10-12 minutes)

This is where you become a detective, uncovering their pain points, desires, and the emotional impact of their current situation. Ask open-ended questions that encourage them to share their story.

  • The Problem: "Tell me more about what's bringing you here today?" or "What does a typical day look like when you're feeling [their stated pain point]?"
  • Impact: "How does this impact your life, your energy, your relationships?"
  • Past Attempts: "What have you tried so far to address this, and what were the results?"
  • Future Vision: "If nothing changes, where do you see yourself in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand, what would your life look like?"

Phase 3: Presenting Your Transformative Solution (8-10 minutes)

Once you understand their needs, it's time to connect the dots between their problem and your unique Forest Therapy Coaching program. Frame your solution as the bridge to their desired future.

  • Acknowledge & Summarize: Reflect back what you've heard. "So, what I'm hearing, Sarah, is that you're feeling a bit adrift after your teaching career, seeking new purpose and a deeper connection to nature, but you're unsure how to make that a reality and have some hesitation about investing in yourself."
  • Introduce Your Approach: Explain how Forest Therapy Coaching specifically addresses their challenges, emphasizing the root-cause or holistic nature of your work. Highlight the transformation, not just the features.
  • Share Success Stories: Briefly mention how other clients (similar to them) have benefited.

Coach Tip

Avoid jargon. Speak in terms of benefits and outcomes for them. Instead of saying, "We'll do a series of sensory invitations," say, "You'll discover simple practices to quiet your mind and tap into nature's calming presence, helping you feel more grounded and clear about your next steps."

Phase 4: Guiding Towards the Next Step (3-5 minutes)

This is the closing phase. Be confident and clear about how they can move forward. Remember, you're offering a solution, not asking for a favor.

  • Check for Alignment: "Does this sound like what you've been looking for?"
  • Present Your Program: Briefly outline your core program (e.g., 8-week journey, 12-week package).
  • Call to Action: Explain the next steps and discuss investment.

Navigating the Waters: Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are not rejections; they are simply requests for more information or reassurance. When a prospect raises an objection, it means they're engaged enough to voice their concerns. Your job is to listen, validate, and address their underlying worries.

Common Objections & Confident Responses

Reflect & Practice

As you read through these, imagine Sarah saying them. How would you respond with genuine empathy and confidence?

"I need to think about it."

Underlying concern: Unsure, needs more information, feeling pressured, or needs to discuss with partner.

Your Response: "I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. To help me understand better, what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else entirely? Knowing that helps me ensure I've answered all your questions."

(Listen carefully to their answer. This reveals the true objection.)

"It's too expensive." / "I've spent so much money already."

Underlying concern: Value perception, financial constraints, fear of wasting money again.

Your Response: "I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty about the investment. Many of my clients, especially those who have tried many things before, feel that way initially. However, what they often find is that the cost of *not* addressing these feelings of being lost or disconnected is far greater in the long run ‚Äì in terms of missed opportunities, continued stress, and diminished joy. Think of this as an investment in a new chapter, in truly rediscovering your purpose and peace. What value do you place on that?"

Coach Tip

When discussing investment, stand firm in the value you offer. You're not just selling sessions; you're selling transformation, renewed purpose, and a deeper connection to life. Your conviction in your own value is contagious.

"I've tried so many things before, and nothing has worked."

Underlying concern: Past failures, skepticism, fear of disappointment.

Your Response: "It sounds like you've been on quite a journey, Sarah, and it's completely understandable to feel that way given your past experiences. What makes Forest Therapy Coaching different is its deeply holistic and individualized approach. We're not just addressing symptoms; we're reconnecting you with your innate wisdom and the healing power of nature, something often overlooked in conventional approaches. My program is designed to create sustainable shifts, not just temporary fixes, by building practices that become a part of your daily life. It's about cultivating a relationship, not just following a protocol."

"I'm not sure I have the time."

Underlying concern: Overwhelm, lack of prioritization, fear of adding another commitment.

Your Response: "I understand completely. Life as a former teacher, even in retirement, can still be incredibly busy, and it often feels like there aren't enough hours in the day. However, what many of my clients discover is that by dedicating this focused time to themselves and their well-being, they actually gain more time and energy back in the long run. We'll work together to integrate these practices into your existing schedule in a way that feels supportive, not overwhelming. What would it look like if you prioritized finding your purpose and peace for just a few hours a week?"

Confidently Presenting Your Investment

Presenting your pricing can feel daunting, but it's a critical skill for a successful practice. Remember, you are offering immense value, and your prices reflect your expertise, time, and the significant transformation you facilitate.

Articulating Value, Not Just Cost

Before stating a number, reiterate the value. Remind them of their desired outcome and how your program bridges the gap. Frame your fees as an investment, not an expense.

Your dialogue might sound like this: "Sarah, based on everything you've shared ‚Äì your desire to find purpose, reconnect with nature, and overcome that sense of being lost ‚Äì my 'Awaken Your Inner Guide' program is designed precisely for you. It's an 8-week journey that includes weekly 60-minute one-on-one sessions, personalized nature invitations, guided meditations, and ongoing email support. We'll dive deep into creating a sustainable practice that brings you peace and clarity."

Presenting Your Program Structure & Investment

Be clear, concise, and confident. State the price without hesitation, then pause and let them process. Avoid rambling or justifying.

Your dialogue: "The investment for the 'Awaken Your Inner Guide' 8-week program is $1,800. Many clients choose our convenient payment plan of two monthly payments of $950. This includes everything we just discussed, and I only work with a limited number of clients at a time to ensure you receive my full attention and support."

Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural and confident. The more comfortable you are with your fees, the more comfortable your prospects will be. Your energy speaks volumes!

From Conversation to Client: The Call to Action

After presenting your program and investment, it's crucial to have a clear call to action. You've guided them through the journey; now tell them how to take the next step.

Example Closing Lines:

  • "Sarah, based on our conversation, I truly believe the 'Awaken Your Inner Guide' program is exactly what you need to find that renewed purpose and connection to nature. How would you like to move forward today?"
  • "If you're ready to commit to this journey, we can get you started right away. I have space for one new client next week. Does that sound like a good fit for you?"
  • "What questions do you have for me, Sarah, that would help you feel confident in taking this next step?"

Be prepared to send a follow-up email with program details and a link to your booking/payment page. A strong discovery call makes the follow-up a natural next step.

Check Your Understanding

1. During Phase 2 of the discovery call (Deep Dive), what type of questions are most effective for uncovering a prospect's true needs and motivations?

Open-ended questions are most effective. They encourage the prospect to share their story, feelings, and experiences in detail, allowing you to understand the emotional impact of their problem and their desired future. Examples: "Tell me more about...", "How does that impact you...?", "What would life look like if...?"

2. Sarah raises the objection, "It's too expensive." According to the lesson, what is the underlying concern she is likely expressing, and how should you initially respond?

The underlying concern is often a value perception or fear of wasting money. Your initial response should validate her feeling ("I hear you, and I appreciate your honesty...") and then pivot to articulating the long-term value and transformation your program offers, rather than just focusing on the monetary cost.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is a key psychological strategy to employ immediately after stating the price?

After stating the price, the key strategy is to pause and remain silent. This allows the prospect time to process the information without feeling pressured by your immediate justification or further explanation. Your confidence in your price should be conveyed through your composure.

4. According to the income potential table, approximately how many active clients (on an 8-week program) would a Forest Therapy Coach need to consistently serve to achieve an annual revenue of over $50,000 USD?

To achieve an annual revenue of over $50,000 USD, a coach would need to consistently serve approximately 10 active clients (on an 8-week program), which projects an annual revenue potential between $54,000 - $66,000 USD.

Key Takeaways

  • The discovery call is your opportunity to build genuine connection and demonstrate empathy, not just to sell.
  • Listen actively to uncover your prospect's core pain points and their deepest desires for transformation.
  • Frame your Forest Therapy Coaching program as the unique solution that bridges their current challenges to their desired future.
  • Objections are opportunities for further conversation and clarification; address them with confidence and unwavering belief in your value.
  • Practice articulating your program's investment clearly and confidently, allowing your fees to reflect your expertise and the profound impact you offer.
