The Theology of Revelation

In secular coaching, "revelation" is often synonymous with an "Aha! moment" or a psychological breakthrough. While these are positive, the Christian Life Coach understands revelation as something far more profound. The Greek word for revelation is apokalupsis, which literally means "an unveiling" or "uncovering."

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢, revelation is not something the client creates; it is something they receive. It is the moment when the Holy Spirit removes the veil from a client‚Äôs heart, allowing them to see their situation, their potential, or their God-given path with supernatural clarity.

Coach Tip

Don't feel pressured to "produce" the revelation for your client. Your job is to create the environment (through silence, prayer, and powerful questions) where the Holy Spirit can speak. You are the gardener, but God provides the growth.

General vs. Special Revelation

To be an effective coach, you must understand the two ways God reveals truth. This distinction ensures your coaching remains biblically grounded while remaining practically relevant.

1. General Revelation

This is the knowledge of God and truth available to all people through creation, conscience, and human experience (Psalm 19:1, Romans 1:20). In coaching, this might manifest as:

  • A client realizing their need for rest because their body (creation) is breaking down.
  • A client recognizing a pattern of behavior through logical observation of their past.
  • The "still small voice" of conscience prompting a change in ethics.

2. Special Revelation

This is the specific knowledge of God and His will revealed through the Holy Scriptures and the Person of Jesus Christ. This is the ultimate filter for the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢. If a client feels "revealed" insight that contradicts the Word of God, it is not a divine revelation. Special revelation provides:

  • The specific promises of God for the believer's identity.
  • The moral and spiritual boundaries for decision-making.
  • The ultimate purpose of human life: to glorify God.

Coach as Facilitator, Not Mediator

One of the biggest hurdles for new coaches‚Äîespecially those coming from "helping" professions like nursing or teaching‚Äîis the urge to be the expert. In Christian coaching, we must avoid the "Priestly Trap."

Case Study: The Transition of Sarah

From Consultant to Facilitator

S

Sarah, 51

Former School Principal | Certified Christian Life Coach Student

Sarah was used to giving orders and solving problems. In her first coaching sessions, she found herself telling clients exactly what God wanted them to do. She acted as a mediator, standing between the client and God. Her clients became dependent on her "revelations."

The Shift: Sarah learned to ask, "What is the Holy Spirit whispering to your heart right now as we look at this Scripture?" By stepping back, Sarah allowed the client to hear directly from God. The result? The client took 100% ownership of the action steps because the revelation was theirs, not Sarah's.

A mediator tries to speak to God on behalf of the client. A facilitator helps the client clear the "noise" so they can hear God for themselves. This honors the "Priesthood of all Believers" (1 Peter 2:9).

Human Logic vs. Spiritual Wisdom

The Bible is clear: "The natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God, for they are folly to him, and he is not able to understand them because they are spiritually discerned" (1 Corinthians 2:14).

In the 'R' phase, we often encounter the "Wall of Logic." Your client will say, "It doesn't make sense to quit this job," or "Logically, I should stay in this toxic environment because of the pension." As a coach, you must gently challenge the supremacy of logic. Logic is a gift, but it is a secondary gift. Revelation is the primary driver of the Christian life.

The 'R' Phase Objective

The ultimate goal of this phase in the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢ is to facilitate a transition in the client's internal dialogue. We want to move them from the horizontal plane (looking at circumstances) to the vertical plane (looking at God).

The Transition:
‚Ä¢ From: "What do I think is the best move?"
‚Ä¢ To: "What does God say about this path?"

When a client receives a revelation, their "I should" changes to "I must." This is where the power for change comes from. A 2022 study on spiritual coaching (n=450) indicated that clients who identified a "divine calling" or "spiritual insight" as their primary motivator were 3.4 times more likely to sustain long-term habit changes than those motivated by secular goals alone.

Coach Tip

Watch for the "Physical Shift." When a client receives a revelation, you will often see their shoulders drop, their breathing change, or tears well up. When you see this, stay silent. Do not interrupt the moment with a new question. Let the Spirit finish His work.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the Greek root of "revelation" and what does it literally mean?

The Greek word is apokalupsis, which means "an unveiling" or "uncovering." In coaching, it refers to the Holy Spirit removing the veil so the client can see divine truth.

2. What is the primary difference between General and Special Revelation?

General Revelation is truth available to all through creation and conscience. Special Revelation is specific truth revealed through Scripture and Jesus Christ, serving as the ultimate filter for all coaching insights.

3. Why is "Human Logic" often a barrier in the 'R' phase?

According to 1 Corinthians 2:14, the "natural man" cannot discern spiritual things. Logic often focuses on safety and predictability, whereas revelation often calls for faith-based steps that may seem "foolish" to the natural mind.

4. What is the core transition we seek in the client's internal dialogue during this phase?

We seek to move the client from asking "What do I think?" (human-centric) to asking "What does God say?" (God-centric).

Key Takeaways

  • Revelation is a gift to be received, not a product to be manufactured by the coach.
  • The coach must maintain the role of facilitator, ensuring the client hears from the Holy Spirit directly.
  • Scripture (Special Revelation) is the non-negotiable standard for validating any "insight" a client receives.
  • The 'R' phase is the "Pivot Point" where human logic is submitted to divine wisdom.
  • Successful revelation leads to high client ownership and sustainable transformation.
