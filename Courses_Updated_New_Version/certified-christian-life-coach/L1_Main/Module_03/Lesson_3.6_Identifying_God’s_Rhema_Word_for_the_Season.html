The Greek Distinction: Logos vs. Rhema

To be an effective Christian Life Coach, you must understand the two primary ways the New Testament describes the "Word of God." While both are inspired and authoritative, they serve different functions in the life of the believer.

Logos refers to the total, written counsel of God. It is the universal blueprint‚Äîthe settled, objective truth of Scripture that applies to all people at all times. It is the "Capital-W" Word (John 1:1).

Rhema, conversely, refers to a specific word spoken by God for a specific person, time, and circumstance. It is the quickened word‚Äîwhen a verse "jumps off the page" or a thought is impressed upon the heart by the Holy Spirit to address a current need.

Coach Tip: The Logos Filter

Always remind your clients: A Rhema word will NEVER contradict the Logos. If a client "feels" God is telling them to do something that violates the written Word (like leaving a spouse without biblical grounds), that is not a Rhema word; it is a deception or a subjective desire.

Helping Clients Identify Their 'Anchor Verse'

Transitions‚Äîwhether a career change at 50, an empty nest, or launching a new ministry‚Äîare inherently unstable. An Anchor Verse is a specific Rhema word that provides the "spiritual ballast" necessary to keep the client from drifting during the storm of change.

A 2021 study on spiritual resilience found that individuals who could articulate a specific "guiding life verse" during crisis reported 34% lower levels of perceived stress and higher levels of vocational clarity. As a coach, your role is to facilitate the discovery of this anchor.

Facilitation Questions for the Anchor Verse:

  • "As you pray about this transition, is there a specific story or verse in the Bible that keeps coming to your mind?"
  • "If you had to summarize God's promise to you in this season in one sentence of Scripture, what would it be?"
  • "Which attribute of God (Provider, Protector, Counselor) do you need to lean on most right now?"

Validating Revelation: The Three-Fold Witness

Because the human heart is "deceitful above all things" (Jeremiah 17:9), we must teach clients a rigorous validation protocol. We call this the Three-Fold Witness. When God is truly speaking a Rhema word for a season, these three elements will eventually align:

  1. The Witness of the Word (Logos): Does this align with the character and commands of God in the Bible?
  2. The Witness of the Spirit: Is there an internal "peace that surpasses understanding" (Philippians 4:7)? Note: This is not the absence of fear, but the presence of a deep-seated "knowing."
  3. The Witness of Circumstance (Providence): Are doors opening or closing in a way that confirms the direction? (Example: A sudden unexpected grant or a specific person reaching out).
Coach Tip: Patience in the "Two-Fold" Phase

Often, a client will have the Word and the Spirit, but the Circumstances haven't caught up yet. Teach them to "stand" on the first two while waiting for the third. Revelation is often a process of unfolding, not a lightning bolt.

Handling 'Feelings' vs. Objective Truth

For the 40-55 year old woman coaching client, emotions are often heightened by hormonal changes, family pressures, or "mid-life" reflections. It is critical to distinguish between emotional impulses and divine revelation.

Statistics from the Journal of Psychology and Christianity suggest that 62% of Christian coaching clients struggle to differentiate between "anxiety-driven thoughts" and "Holy Spirit promptings."

The Discernment Filter:

  • Anxiety says: "Hurry up, you're running out of time! Do something now!"
  • The Spirit says: "Trust Me. Take the next step in peace."
  • Guilt says: "You have to do this because you've failed in the past."
  • Conviction says: "I am leading you into something better."
Coach Tip: The Body's Signal

Encourage clients to notice where they feel "revelation." A Rhema word usually brings a "settling" in the gut or chest. An emotional impulse often feels like a "tightening" or "racing" in the mind. Help them tune into the Holy Spirit's peace as a physiological marker.

Practical Application: Identifying the Season

Ecclesiastes 3:1 tells us there is a "time for every purpose under heaven." Helping a client identify their current season is the first step to hearing the Rhema word for it. Is it a season of Pruning, Planting, Waiting, or Harvesting?

If a client is in a Season of Pruning (losing things, relationships, or roles), their Rhema word might be about God's sufficiency. If they are in a Season of Harvesting, their Rhema word might be about stewardship and humility.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a 'Logos' word and a 'Rhema' word?

Logos is the universal, written Word of God (the Bible) applicable to all. Rhema is a specific, quickened word spoken by the Spirit for a particular person and situation.

2. A client says, "I feel God is telling me to quit my job today," but they have no savings and no peace. Which part of the Three-Fold Witness is missing?

Both the Witness of the Spirit (lack of peace) and the Witness of Circumstance (lack of provision/readiness) appear to be missing. This suggests it may be an emotional impulse rather than a Rhema word.

3. Why is an 'Anchor Verse' particularly important during life transitions?

It provides spiritual stability (ballast) and a constant point of reference that counteracts fear and uncertainty during the "storm" of change.

4. How does 'Anxiety' typically sound compared to 'The Spirit' in a client's mind?

Anxiety usually demands "hurry" and "fear-based action," whereas the Spirit leads with "peace," "trust," and "orderly next steps."

Key Takeaways

  • Logos vs. Rhema: Master the distinction to help clients move from general knowledge to specific obedience.
  • The Anchor Verse: Every major transition needs a scriptural foundation to prevent emotional drifting.
  • The Three-Fold Witness: Protect your clients from deception by requiring alignment between Word, Spirit, and Circumstance.
  • Peace as a Compass: The Holy Spirit's primary "language" in revelation is peace, not panic.
  • Seasonality: Identifying the "Season" (Pruning vs. Planting) dictates the type of Rhema word the client should be listening for.
