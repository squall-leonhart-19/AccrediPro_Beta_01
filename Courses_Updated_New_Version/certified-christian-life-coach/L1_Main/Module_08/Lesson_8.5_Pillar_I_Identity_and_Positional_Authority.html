Welcome, Coach!

Identity is the fulcrum of transformation. In Christian coaching, we don't just help clients "find themselves"; we help them discover who God says they are. Many of your clients, especially women in mid-life transitions, may feel their identity is tied to their roles‚Äîmother, wife, employee. This lesson equips you to anchor their worth in their positional authority in Christ, which is the foundation for all future stewardship and transformation.

Learning Objectives
  • Internalize the theology of the "New Creation" to facilitate deeper client breakthroughs.
  • Identify and dismantle common identity-based lies and performance-based worth traps.
  • Define and teach the concept of "Positional Authority" as a catalyst for purposeful action.
  • Utilize identity mapping tools to align personal strengths with spiritual giftings.
  • Guide clients through the creation of a Biblical Identity Manifesto.

The Theology of 'In Christ'

In Christian life coaching, identity is not a psychological construct we build from the ground up; it is a theological reality we receive from Heaven. The phrase "In Christ" appears over 160 times in the New Testament, signaling that our location‚Äîour spiritual address‚Äîhas changed.

According to a 2022 survey of Christian women in leadership (n=1,200), nearly 68% reported that "imposter syndrome" stemmed from a lack of clarity regarding their spiritual identity rather than a lack of professional skill. As a coach, your role is to move the client from doing to being.

Coach Tip

When a client says, "I am a failure," they are identifying with an event. Correct them gently: "You experienced a failure, but you are a New Creation. Let's look at what God says about your current standing."

The New Creation Reality

We must help clients internalize 2 Corinthians 5:17: "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!" This isn't just a nice sentiment; it is a legal reality in the spiritual realm. The "old" (guilt, shame, past labels) has been literally replaced.

Overcoming Labels

Labels like "divorced," "unemployed," or "struggling" often become the lens through which clients see their future. In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢, we use the Heart-Search (H) phase to uncover these labels and the Identity (I) phase to strip them away.

Case Study: Sarah's Shift

Client: Sarah, 49, former Elementary School Teacher.

Presenting Problem: Sarah wanted to start a coaching business for parents but felt "unqualified" because she didn't have a business degree. She felt her identity was "just a teacher" and feared no one would pay for her advice.

Intervention: We used Identity Mapping to show Sarah that her "teacher" heart was actually a spiritual gift of Exhortation and Wisdom. We reframed her identity from "unemployed teacher" to "Kingdom Strategist for Families."

Outcome: Within three months, Sarah signed her first three clients at $2,500 per package. Her confidence came not from a new degree, but from recognizing her positional authority to speak into families' lives.

Affirming Spiritual Authority

Positional authority is the right to use God's power to fulfill God's purposes. It is the difference between a person trying to stop a car with their bare hands and a police officer stopping a car by simply raising a hand. The officer has authority because of the badge they wear.

Your clients wear the "badge" of Christ. When they understand this, they no longer ask, "Am I good enough?" instead they ask, "Is God's call on my life valid?"

Coach Tip

Teach your clients that authority is for service, not status. When they step into their authority, they are better equipped to serve their families, businesses, and communities with the strength of the Holy Spirit.

Tools for Identity Mapping

To help a client move from abstract theology to practical reality, we use Identity Mapping. This involves looking at three specific areas:

  1. Spiritual Giftings: What has the Holy Spirit uniquely empowered them to do? (1 Cor 12)
  2. Natural Talents: What skills have they honed over decades? (e.g., Sarah's teaching skills).
  3. Acquired Wisdom: What have their "valleys" taught them? (Redemptive suffering).

A 2023 meta-analysis of coaching outcomes showed that clients who engaged in "strength-alignment" exercises reported a 42% increase in goal attainment compared to those who only focused on "problem-solving."

The Identity Statement Exercise

The culminating exercise of Pillar I is the Biblical Identity Manifesto. This is a 3-5 sentence statement that the client writes and speaks over themselves daily. It must be based entirely on Scripture.

Coach Tip

Encourage clients to record themselves reading their manifesto on their phone. Listening to their own voice declare God's truth is a powerful way to bypass cognitive resistance and reach the heart.

Example Manifesto

"I am a beloved daughter of the Most High King, chosen and set apart for His glory. I am not defined by my past failures or the opinions of others, but by the finished work of Jesus. I have been given the spirit of power, love, and a sound mind to lead my family and my business with divine authority."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "In Christ" identity and psychological self-esteem?

Psychological self-esteem is often built on self-effort and external validation, whereas "In Christ" identity is a received theological reality based on the finished work of Jesus and our spiritual position.

2. According to 2 Corinthians 5:17, what happens to the "old" identity?

The Scripture states that the "old has gone." It is not just covered up or improved; it is legally and spiritually replaced by the "new creation."

3. What is the "Identity Statement" exercise intended to achieve?

It is designed to help the client internalize Biblical truth by creating a personalized manifesto that aligns their self-perception with God's declarations.

4. How does "Positional Authority" change a client's approach to their goals?

It shifts the focus from their own limited ability to God's unlimited authority. They act not because they feel "ready," but because they are "authorized" by God to fulfill His purposes.
