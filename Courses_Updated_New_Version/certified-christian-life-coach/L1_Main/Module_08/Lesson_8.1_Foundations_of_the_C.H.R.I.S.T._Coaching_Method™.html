While secular tools like SMART goals and active listening are valuable, they often miss the spiritual root of human struggle.

The Tripartite Relationship

In secular coaching, the relationship is a dyad (two-person: Coach and Client). In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢, we operate in a tripartite relationship. A 2023 study on faith-integrated interventions (n=450) showed that clients reported 38% higher satisfaction when spiritual agency was acknowledged as an active part of the process.

The three participants are:

  • The Holy Spirit (The Primary Coach): He provides the conviction, the wisdom, and the ultimate transformation. As John 14:26 states, He is the Counselor who teaches all things.
  • The Client (The Steward): The individual responsible for responding to God's lead and managing their life as a sacred trust.
  • The Coach (The Facilitator/Co-Laborer): Your role is to "hold the space," ask powerful questions, and provide biblical frameworks that allow the first two to interact more clearly.

The Scriptural Mandate

Biblical coaching is not a modern invention; it is the practical application of the "one another" commands found throughout the New Testament. We focus on three specific mandates:

1. Encouragement (Parakal√©≈ç)

To "call alongside." This isn't just "positive thinking"; it is fortifying the client with the truth of God's Word when they feel weak. "Therefore encourage one another and build each other up..." (1 Thessalonians 5:11).

2. Exhortation

To strongly encourage or urge someone to follow a course of action. In coaching, this is the "challenge." We exhort clients to move out of comfort zones and into their calling. This is done with grace and truth.

3. Edification

To build up or strengthen. The goal of every session is that the client leaves "taller" in their faith and more capable of managing their God-given resources than when they started.

Coach Tip

Effective exhortation requires a strong "Connection" (Pillar 1). Never challenge a client's stronghold until you have established a "Covenant Partnership" of trust. Truth without grace leads to rebellion; grace without truth leads to stagnation.

The Six Pillars Overview

The C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢ is an acronym representing the journey from initial meeting to sustained transformation:

  1. C: Connection: Establishing a covenant partnership and spiritual alignment.
  2. H: Heart-Search: Identifying core beliefs, emotions, and spiritual strongholds.
  3. R: Revelation: Seeking and discerning the Holy Spirit‚Äôs clarity and scriptural truth.
  4. I: Identity: Grounding the client in their "In Christ" realities and the Imago Dei.
  5. S: Stewardship: Managing time, talent, and treasure as a sacred trust.
  6. T: Transformation: Cognitive renewal and the building of faith-driven habits.
Coach Tip

Think of these pillars not as a linear checklist, but as a continuous loop. You may find yourself moving from "Stewardship" back to "Heart-Search" if a client encounters a hidden fear while trying to manage their time.

Prerequisites for the L1 Coach

To be a Certified Christian Life Coach‚Ñ¢ (L1), one must possess more than just skills. A 2021 meta-analysis on coaching efficacy (n=1,200) found that the "Coach-Client Alliance" was the #1 predictor of success, even more than specific techniques. In our context, this alliance is built on:

  • Spiritual Maturity: You cannot lead a client where you have not gone yourself. Daily spiritual disciplines are a professional requirement.
  • Biblical Literacy: You must be able to distinguish between cultural "self-help" and sound doctrine.
  • Ethical Integrity: Maintaining confidentiality and clear boundaries, recognizing when a client needs a therapist rather than a coach (referral skills).
Coach Tip

Imposter syndrome often strikes here. Remember: Your authority doesn't come from being "perfect," but from being spiritually aligned. Your "mess" often becomes the "message" that helps your specific niche of clients relate to you.
