Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why it Fails The Better Way
Over-Spiritualizing Telling the client to "just pray more" can feel dismissive of their very real fear. Validate the fear, then invite God into the conversation.
Giving Advice Saying "You should just start a blog" takes the power away from the client. Ask: "What is the smallest way you could express this calling today?"
Ignoring the Body Forgetting that fear often manifests as physical tension (tight chest, etc.). Ask the client where they feel that "Who am I?" question in their body.
Rushing the Plan Pushing for a 12-month business plan when they are still afraid to tell their friends. Focus on the next 7 days only.
Pro-Tip

Statistics show that clients who define a "Micro-Move" (a task taking less than 20 minutes) are 85% more likely to follow through than those who set broad goals.

Success Markers

How do you know the session went well? Look for these three signs:

  • Shift in Language: The client moves from "I might" or "I want to" to "I will" or "I am."
  • Physical Release: You notice a sigh, a drop in the shoulders, or a smile when they commit to their micro-step.
  • Clarity: The client can state their next step in one sentence without using the word "try."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of a "Micro-Move" in the integration phase?

To bypass the "paralysis of analysis" by creating immediate, low-stakes momentum that builds self-efficacy.

2. When a client experiences imposter syndrome, what should the coach reflect back?

Reflect back the "fruit" or evidence of God's work and the client's natural impact on others that has been discussed in previous sessions.

3. True or False: A coach should provide the specific steps for the client's calling.

False. The coach facilitates the client's discovery of their own steps to ensure ownership and stewardship of the calling.

4. What is a physical sign that a client has reached a breakthrough in integration?

A physical release, such as a deep sigh, relaxed shoulders, or a change in facial expression/smiling.

Key Takeaways

  • Identity precedes activity; help the client see who they are in Christ before asking what they will do.
  • The "Bridge Technique" connects past experiences (even painful ones) to future calling.
  • Micro-steps are the antidote to the fear of "going official."
  • Success is measured by the client's shift in ownership and commitment to action.
