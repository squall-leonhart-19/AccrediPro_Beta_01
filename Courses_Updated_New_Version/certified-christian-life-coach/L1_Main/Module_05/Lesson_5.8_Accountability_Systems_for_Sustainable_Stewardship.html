Common Pitfalls to Avoid

New coaches often stumble in these four areas when discussing stewardship:

  • Over-Empathizing: Agreeing that "yes, life is just too busy" instead of challenging the client to manage their time better.
  • Money Shyness: Feeling awkward talking about the client's budget or their willingness to pay for coaching/training.
  • Preaching vs. Coaching: Telling the client they are a "bad steward" instead of asking questions that help them realize it.
  • Ignoring the Body: Forgetting that stewarding our health is the foundation for stewarding our calling.

Success Markers

How do you know this session went well? Look for these signs in your "client":

  • The "Aha" Moment: The client stops saying "I have to" and starts saying "I am called to."
  • Boundary Setting: The client identifies at least one "Good" thing they will stop doing to make room for the "Great."
  • Financial Clarity: The client views an investment in their growth as a business/ministry expense, not a personal luxury.
  • Shift in Energy: The client moves from a state of "overwhelm" to a state of "focused intentionality."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "self-care" and "stewardship" in a Christian coaching context?

Self-care is often framed as an end in itself (feeling better), whereas stewardship is managing one's health and energy as a means to fulfill a God-given calling more effectively.

2. Why do women in the 40-55 age bracket often struggle with "financial stewardship" regarding their own growth?

Many have been socialized to put everyone else's needs first (children, aging parents, spouse), leading to guilt when "spending" money on their own development, even if it's for a ministry or career pivot.

3. If a client says, "I just don't have any time to work on my goals," what is a powerful stewardship-based coaching question?

"If we look at your time as a gift from God that has already been distributed, where is it currently being 'leaked' into areas that don't align with your calling?"

4. According to the "Success Markers," what is a key sign that a client has shifted their mindset?

A shift from "overwhelm" to "focused intentionality," and moving from "I have to" language to "I am called to" language.
