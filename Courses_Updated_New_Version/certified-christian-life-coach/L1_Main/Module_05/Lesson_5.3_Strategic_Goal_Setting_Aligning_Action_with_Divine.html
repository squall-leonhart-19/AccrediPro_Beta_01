The Bridge from Revelation to Stewardship

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Coaching Methodâ„¢, Revelation (R) provides the "What" and the "Why," but Stewardship (S) provides the "How" and the "When." Without stewardship, revelation becomes a source of frustration rather than transformation.

A 2022 study on goal attainment found that individuals who wrote down their goals and shared weekly progress reports with a partner were 33% more successful in reaching them than those who merely had a vision in their heads (Matthews, 2022). For the Christian client, this "progress report" is an act of accountability to God.

Coach Tip: The Revelation Gap

If a client is stuck, ask: "Is the vision God gave you clear, or are we trying to build a house without the blueprints?" Stewardship requires the blueprints of revelation. If they lack clarity, go back to Module 3 (Revelation) before attempting strategic goal setting.

The S.M.A.R.T.G. Framework

Traditional coaching uses the S.M.A.R.T. criteria. However, as Christian coaches, we add two critical dimensions to ensure our goals aren't just high-achieving, but high-calling.

Specific Clear and defined. Is this specific to the calling God gave you?

The S.M.A.R.T.G. Framework ensures that the goal isn't just a human ambition dressed in spiritual language. It forces the client to ask: "If God doesn't show up, will this goal fail?" If the answer is no, the goal might be too small or too self-reliant.

The greatest enemy of the "Best" (Godâ€™s specific assignment) is often the "Good" (distractions that look like opportunities). Stewardship is about choosing where to invest limited resources for maximum eternal return.

Coach Tip: The "Yes" Filter

Teach your clients to use a 24-hour "prayer pause" before saying yes to any new opportunity. Ask them: "Does this 'Good' thing align with the 'Best' thing God revealed in our last session?"

Micro-Stepping God-Sized Visions

Biblical stewardship is often demonstrated through the "Mustard Seed" principle (Matthew 13:31-32). We help clients value the small start. Strategic goal setting involves deconstructing a 5-year vision into 90-day objectives, and then into weekly "stewardship tasks."

The Deconstruction Ladder:

  • The Revelation: "I am called to write a book that heals marriages."
  • The 1-Year Goal: "Complete a 50,000-word manuscript."
  • The 90-Day Objective: "Outline 10 chapters and write the first 3."
  • The Weekly Stewardship Task: "Write 500 words every Tuesday and Thursday morning."

By focusing on the weekly stewardship task, the client moves from feeling overwhelmed by the book to feeling empowered by the morning writing session.

The Sacred Pruning Process

In John 15:2, Jesus says, "...every branch that does bear fruit he prunes so that it will be even more fruitful." Stewardship isn't just about adding goals; it's about removing distractions. Strategic goal setting must include a "Stop Doing" list.

Common areas for pruning include:

  • Unproductive Commitments: Roles they are keeping out of guilt, not calling.
  • Digital Clutter: Excessive social media use that drains time and mental energy.
  • Comparison Traps: Goals they set because they saw someone else doing it on Instagram.
Coach Tip: The Pruning Question

Ask your client: "What are you currently carrying that God never asked you to pick up?" This is often the most liberating question a coach can ask.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a S.M.A.R.T. goal and a S.M.A.R.T.G. goal?

The "G" stands for God-Centered and Grace-Based, ensuring the goal requires divine partnership and aligns with spiritual revelation rather than just human ambition.

2. According to the "Mustard Seed" principle, how should a coach handle a client's "God-sized" vision?

By deconstructing the massive vision into manageable micro-steps or "weekly stewardship tasks" to prevent overwhelm and build momentum through small acts of faithfulness.

3. Why is "pruning" considered a strategic part of stewardship?

Pruning removes "good" distractions and unproductive commitments, allowing the client's energy and resources to be focused on their "best" divine assignments for maximum impact.

4. What does the "R" in S.M.A.R.T.G. specifically emphasize for the Christian client?

It emphasizes "Relevance" to the client's current *season* of life, ensuring they aren't striving for a goal that conflicts with their primary God-given responsibilities (e.g., family or health).

Key Takeaways

  • Stewardship is the bridge that turns abstract revelation into concrete, kingdom-building action.
  • The S.M.A.R.T.G. framework integrates professional goal-setting with spiritual dependency.
  • Effective prioritization requires saying "no" to the Good to protect the "Best."
  • Faithfulness in micro-steps is the biblical prerequisite for being entrusted with "much."
  • Pruning is not a sign of failure, but a divine strategy for increased fruitfulness.
