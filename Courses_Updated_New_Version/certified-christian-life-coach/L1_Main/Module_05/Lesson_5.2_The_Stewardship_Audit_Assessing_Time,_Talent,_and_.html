The 168-Hour Audit: Revealing Priorities

Time is the only resource that is distributed equally to everyoneâ€”24 hours a day, 168 hours a week. However, many clients feel "time-starved." The 168-Hour Audit is a diagnostic tool that reveals the gap between a clientâ€™s stated priorities and their actual behaviors.

Research by the Bureau of Labor Statistics (2022) shows that the average American spends approximately 2.8 hours per day on television and social media alone. For a coaching client, this represents nearly 20 hours a week that could be re-stewarded toward their calling.

Steps for the 168-Hour Audit:

  • Tracking: The client tracks every 30-minute block for 7 consecutive days.
  • Categorization: Blocks are labeled (e.g., Essential Work, Sleep, Spiritual Growth, Time Leaks).
  • The "Leak" Analysis: Identifying "mindless" timeâ€”activities that neither rest nor restore.
  • The Redemptive Shift: Choosing 3-5 hours of "leaked" time to re-allocate to their coaching goals.

Case Study: Sarah's Time Redemption

Client: Sarah, 48, former nurse and mother of three.

The Challenge: Sarah felt she had "zero time" to start her coaching practice. She was exhausted and felt her life was a series of endless chores.

The Audit: Sarah's 168-hour audit revealed she was spending 14 hours a week "doom-scrolling" news sites and 6 hours a week on "unnecessary" errands due to a lack of planning.

The Outcome: By batching errands and setting a 15-minute news limit, Sarah "found" 12 hours a week. She dedicated 8 of those hours to her certification and 4 to deep prayer and rest. Her stress levels dropped by 40% within three weeks.

Identifying Spiritual Gifts and Natural Talents

Stewardship includes the management of our Internal Capital. This is the combination of our natural-born talents and our Holy Spirit-empowered spiritual gifts. As a Christian Life Coach, you help clients distinguish between what they can do (skill) and what they are anointed to do (gift).

A meta-analysis of vocational psychology (2021) suggests that individuals who work within their "strength zone" are 6x more likely to be engaged in their work. For the Christian, this "strength zone" is often where their spiritual gifts intersect with their natural passions.

Coach Tip

Use the "Internal Capital Assessment" during your sessions. Ask: "What is a task that others find difficult but feels like breathing to you?" Often, our greatest talents are invisible to us because they come so naturally. These are the "talents" the Master expects us to multiply.

Financial Stewardship: Aligning the Treasure

While life coaching is not financial planning, a client's "Treasure" is a primary indicator of their heart's alignment. "For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also" (Matthew 6:21). Stewardship coaching involves helping clients assess if their spending reflects their God-given vision.

Key Audit Questions for Treasure:

  • Does your current spending support the vision God has given you for this season?
  • Are there financial "weights" (debt, overspending) hindering your ability to say "Yes" to God's calling?
  • Is the "First Fruits" principle (tithing/generosity) a foundation or an afterthought?
Coach Tip

Many women in transition face "financial fear." Remind them that stewardship is not about having enough; it's about being faithful with what is present. Small shifts in spendingâ€”like reallocating a "coffee budget" to a "personal development fund"â€”can be a powerful act of faith.

Evaluating Influence Capital

Influence capital is often the most overlooked resource. It consists of the trust, respect, and access a client has within their social and professional circles. For a woman pivoting careers at 50, her 25 years of "influence capital" in her previous industry is a massive asset for her new coaching practice.

Mapping the Sphere of Influence:

  1. Inner Circle: Family and close friends (Support and encouragement).
  2. Community Circle: Church, neighbors, local groups (Service and local impact).
  3. Professional Circle: Colleagues, clients, industry peers (Authority and expertise).
  4. Digital Circle: Social media followers, email lists (Broad reach).
Coach Tip

Help your client see their "Influence Capital" as a platform for the Gospel. When they steward their reputation and relationships well, they create a bridge for their coaching message to travel across. This is "Kingdom Networking."
