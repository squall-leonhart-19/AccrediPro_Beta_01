Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to address "being" before "doing" with a client like Diane?

If you focus on "doing" (hobbies, new jobs) before "being" (identity in Christ), the client will simply find a new role to hide behind, and the cycle of performance-based anxiety will continue when that role eventually changes.

2. What does the term "poiema" (from Ephesians 2:10) imply about Diane's identity?

It implies she is a "workmanship" or "masterpiece." It shifts the focus from her being the worker to her being the work of a Divine Artist, which places her value in the Artist's skill, not her own performance.

3. How should a coach handle a client's grief over a lost role (like retirement)?

Validate it. Grief is the natural response to losing a significant role. Acknowledge the loss first ("It makes sense that you feel like a ghost right now") before pivoting to the unchanging identity of the Imago Dei.

4. What is the "Sermon Trap" in coaching?

The Sermon Trap occurs when a coach talks at the client, explaining theology rather than asking powerful questions that allow the client to experience the truth of that theology personally.
