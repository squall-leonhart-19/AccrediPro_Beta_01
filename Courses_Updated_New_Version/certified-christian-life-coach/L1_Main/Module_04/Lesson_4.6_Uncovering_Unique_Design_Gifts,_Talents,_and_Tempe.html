The D.E.S.I.G.N. Framework

To help clients move from a vague sense of "I think I'm good at this" to a clear "I was made for this," we use the D.E.S.I.G.N. Framework. This holistic assessment ensures we aren't just looking at what they do, but how God has woven their entire life story together.

A 2022 study on vocational clarity found that individuals who could articulate their "unique contribution" reported 43% higher levels of life satisfaction and 31% higher productivity. In Christian coaching, this clarity comes from recognizing Divine craftsmanship (Ephesians 2:10).

Coach Tip: The "E" Factor

For your 40-55 year old clients, the "E" (Experiences) is often where the greatest coaching gold is found. Many women dismiss their years of parenting, nursing, or teaching as "just what I did." Your job is to help them see these as strategic training grounds for their next chapter.

Spiritual Gifts (Charismata) in Coaching

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢, we define Spiritual Gifts as supernatural empowerments given by the Holy Spirit to believers for the common good. Unlike natural talents, which are present from birth, spiritual gifts are often activated or clarified at the point of salvation or during specific seasons of ministry.

When coaching a client, help them distinguish between:

  • Motivational Gifts (Romans 12): How we are "wired" to serve (e.g., Mercy, Giving, Prophecy, Teaching).
  • Manifestation Gifts (1 Cor 12): How the Spirit moves in a specific moment (e.g., Wisdom, Knowledge, Discernment).

Identifying these gifts allows a client to stop "performing" in areas they aren't gifted for. For example, a client who feels guilty for not being a "great evangelist" may find total freedom when she realizes her primary gift is Hospitality or Encouragement. This shift reduces burnout and increases effectiveness.

The Role of Personality: Redemptive Assessments

Many Christian circles are wary of personality assessments like the Enneagram or DISC. However, in coaching, we view these as descriptive tools, not prescriptive identities. We use them to understand the vessel that holds the Spirit.

The DISC Model through a Biblical Lens:

  • D (Dominance): Like Nehemiah‚Äîdriven, task-oriented, visionary. Coach Tip: Watch for "Identity in Achievement."
  • I (Influence): Like Peter‚Äîenthusiastic, relational, persuasive. Coach Tip: Watch for "People-Pleasing."
  • S (Steadiness): Like Ruth‚Äîloyal, consistent, supportive. Coach Tip: Watch for "Fear of Change."
  • C (Conscientiousness): Like Luke‚Äîdetailed, accurate, systematic. Coach Tip: Watch for "Legalism/Perfectionism."
Coach Tip: Personality vs. Spirit

Always remind your client: "Personality is how you are wired; Character is how you are being transformed." We never use personality as an excuse for sin (e.g., "I'm just a High D, that's why I'm rude"). We use it to understand how we best communicate and process information.

The 'Body of Christ' Principle

Identity is never meant to be lived in isolation. Romans 12:4-5 states, "For as in one body we have many members, and the members do not all have the same function, so we, though many, are one body in Christ..."

A major breakthrough for coaching clients occurs when they realize they are part of a whole. This removes the pressure to be "everything to everyone." In your coaching, help the client identify their "Body Fit":

  • Are they an Eye (Visionary/Discerning)?
  • Are they a Hand (Serving/Implementing)?
  • Are they a Heart (Mercy/Encouragement)?

When a client finds their fit, they stop competing and start contributing. This is the essence of Kingdom synergy.

Practical Application: Coaching for Discovery

As a professional Christian Life Coach, you can package "Discovery Intensives." These are high-value, short-term engagements (often 3-4 sessions) specifically focused on the D.E.S.I.G.N. framework.

Coach Tip: Pricing Your Value

A "Unique Design Intensive" can be priced between $497 and $997 as a standalone package. This provides immediate value to the client and establishes your authority as a specialist in Identity coaching.

  3. "In what environments do you feel most 'alive' and least drained?" (Individual Style)

Check Your Understanding

1. What does the "E" in the D.E.S.I.G.N. framework represent, and why is it vital for career changers?

"E" stands for Experiences. It is vital because it helps clients see that their past‚Äîeven their secular careers or painful trials‚Äîhas been a strategic training ground for their current calling (Romans 8:28).

2. True or False: Spiritual gifts are the same as natural talents.

False. Natural talents are innate abilities present from birth; Spiritual Gifts (Charismata) are supernatural empowerments given by the Holy Spirit for the benefit of the Body of Christ.

3. How should a Christian coach view personality assessments?

As descriptive tools to understand the "vessel," not as prescriptive identities. They help understand communication styles and processing methods but do not define the person‚Äôs worth or excuse character flaws.

4. What is the primary purpose of identifying a client's "fit" in the Body of Christ?

To remove the pressure of being "everything to everyone," reduce burnout, and move the client toward Kingdom synergy where they contribute their specific part to the whole.
