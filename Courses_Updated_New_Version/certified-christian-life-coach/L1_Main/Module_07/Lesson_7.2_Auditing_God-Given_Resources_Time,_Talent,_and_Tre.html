Case Study: The Overwhelmed Servant

Sarah, 48-Year-Old Former Nurse

Sarah's Profile

Sarah left a 20-year nursing career to pursue coaching and ministry. Despite having more "free time," she felt more exhausted than ever and had made zero progress on her business plan.

The Audit Discovery: Through the Stewardship Audit, Sarah realized she was spending 18 hours a week on church committees that did not align with her specific gifts, simply because she felt "guilty" saying no. She was also "leaking" 12 hours a week to mindless scrolling as a trauma-response to her previous high-stress job.

Outcome: By reallocating those 30 hours, Sarah launched her coaching practice within 90 days, securing her first three clients at $1,500 each for a 3-month package.

The Spiritual Resource Inventory (SRI)

The Spiritual Resource Inventory is a diagnostic tool used within the C.H.R.I.S.T. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢ to move a client from vague frustration to concrete clarity. It is based on the premise that God provides exactly what is needed for the assignment He has given (2 Peter 1:3).

When a client says, "I don't have enough time" or "I don't have enough money," the SRI asks: "Are you managing what you have in a way that invites God to give you more?"

Coach Tip: The Guilt Filter

Many Christian women, especially those in the 40-55 age bracket, struggle with "Christian Guilt"‚Äîthe belief that saying 'no' to a church request is saying 'no' to God. Help them see that stewardship requires focus. Saying 'no' to the good allows them to say 'yes' to the God-ordained.

Time: The Currency of Calling

In Christian coaching, we distinguish between Chronos (quantitative time) and Kairos (the appointed time/opportunity). Stewardship of time is the art of fitting our Chronos into God's Kairos.

A 2022 study on productivity found that the average professional loses 2.1 hours per day to interruptions and "low-value" tasks. For your clients, this represents over 14 hours a week that could be dedicated to their God-given vision.

The 168-Hour Audit Technique

Have your client track every 30-minute block for one full week. You are looking for three specific leaks:

  • The "Social" Leak: Passive consumption of others' lives rather than active creation of their own.
  • The "Approval" Leak: Time spent doing things they don't want to do, for people they don't particularly like, to gain approval they don't actually need.
  • The "Perfectionism" Leak: Spending 5 hours on a task that required 1 hour, because of the fear of being judged.

Talent: Unearthing Buried Gifts

The Parable of the Talents (Matthew 25) warns against the "wicked and slothful" servant who hid his talent out of fear. Many clients have "buried" their talents under layers of corporate demand, motherhood, or trauma.

As a coach, you must use Incarnational Listening to hear the gifts beneath the surface. Use these deep questioning prompts:

  1. "What is the one thing you do where you lose all track of time?"
  2. "What problem in the world makes you the most righteously angry?" (Anger often points to an area of giftedness/calling).
  3. "If money were no object and failure was impossible, how would you serve the Body of Christ today?"
Coach Tip: Identifying "Latent" Skills

A former teacher has the talent of Curriculum Design. A former nurse has the talent of Triage and Empathy. Help your clients translate their "old" career skills into their "new" coaching identity. This bridges the gap of imposter syndrome.

Treasure: Financial Stewardship as Spiritual Discipline

Money is often the most difficult resource to audit because it is tied to Identity and Security. In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢, we coach the client to see money as a seed, not just a harvest.

A 2023 survey indicated that 64% of aspiring female entrepreneurs cite "lack of capital" as their primary barrier. However, a Stewardship Audit often reveals that the barrier is actually misaligned spending. Coaching a client through their "Treasure" involves:

  • Breaking the Scarcity Mindset: Moving from "I can't afford it" to "How can I steward my resources to make this possible?"
  • The Investment Principle: Distinguishing between an expense (money gone) and an investment (money that produces a return, like coaching certification).
  • Kingdom ROI: Evaluating spending based on eternal impact rather than temporary comfort.

Energy: Managing the Temple

You cannot steward a calling if you have destroyed the vessel. Energy Management is the stewardship of the physical body (1 Corinthians 6:19). High-achieving women often treat their bodies like machines rather than temples.

The Four Dimensions of Energy Stewardship:

  • Physical: Sleep, nutrition, and movement. (A client sleeping 5 hours a night is not a "dedicated servant"; they are a "compromised steward").
  • Emotional: Maintaining boundaries and "emotional margin."
  • Mental: Focusing on "The One Thing" rather than multi-tasking.
  • Spiritual: The "Sabbath" principle‚Äîresting as an act of trust in God's providence.
Coach Tip: The Sabbath Audit

Ask your client: "When was the last time you rested without feeling guilty?" If they can't remember, their stewardship of energy is in crisis. True stewardship includes the stewardship of rest.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "Chronos" and "Kairos" in time stewardship?

Chronos refers to chronological, quantitative time (the clock), while Kairos refers to the "appointed time" or opportune moments of God's favor. Stewardship involves aligning our daily Chronos with God's Kairos opportunities.

2. Why is "Righteous Anger" a valuable indicator during a Talent Audit?

Righteous anger often points to a specific area of brokenness that a client is uniquely gifted and called to address. It reveals a passion that, when combined with talent, creates powerful Kingdom impact.

3. According to the lesson, what is the "Approval Leak" in time management?

The Approval Leak is time wasted on tasks or commitments the client doesn't truly value or feel called to, but performs solely to gain the approval of others or avoid the discomfort of saying 'no.'

4. How does the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢ reframe "Investment" vs. "Expense" in financial coaching?

An expense is money spent for immediate consumption with no expected return. An investment is a strategic use of "seed" money (like education or coaching) intended to produce a future harvest in skills, revenue, or spiritual fruit.

Key Takeaways

  • Audit Before Action: You cannot effectively strategize for a client until you have audited the resources currently in their hands.
  • Ownership vs. Management: Stewardship coaching shifts the client's mindset from "My stuff" to "His stuff in my hands."
  • The 168-Hour Reality: Everyone has the same amount of time; the difference in fruitfulness lies in the stewardship of the 30-minute blocks.
  • Energy is the Engine: Physical health is not a secondary concern; it is the fundamental resource required to carry out a spiritual calling.
  • Translation of Talent: Help clients see how their past career "talents" are actually "seeds" for their future coaching ministry.
