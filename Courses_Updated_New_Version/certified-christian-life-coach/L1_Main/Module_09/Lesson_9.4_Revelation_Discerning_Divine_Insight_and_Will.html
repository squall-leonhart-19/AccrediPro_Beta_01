Case Study: Sarah's Career Pivot

Client: Sarah, 48, former ICU nurse transitioning into wellness coaching.

The Challenge: Sarah felt "stuck" and paralyzed by the fear that she was making a mistake by leaving her stable nursing career. She had analyzed the pros and cons for months (Human Logic) but felt no peace.

The Revelation Intervention: During a session, the coach invited Sarah into a "God-Moment." Instead of asking Sarah what she thought, the coach asked, "Sarah, let's invite the Holy Spirit into this space. What does He want you to know about your nursing career?"

The Outcome: Sarah sat in silence for two minutes. She suddenly began to weep, saying, "I just saw a picture of a bridge. God told me nursing wasn't a mistake; it was the bridge to get me to where I am now, but the bridge has reached the other side. I'm not 'quitting'; I'm 'arriving'." This revelation broke her paralysis instantly.

Facilitating 'God-Moments'

A "God-Moment" in coaching is a pivot point where the focus shifts from the coach-client dialogue to the Spirit-client dialogue. Your role changes from a facilitator of conversation to a steward of silence and presence.

To facilitate these moments effectively, you must master the art of the Spirit-Led Pause. Research suggests that the average person waits only 1.5 seconds before filling silence. In Christian coaching, we extend that silence to allow the "still, small voice" to be heard.

Techniques for Divine Facilitation:

  • The Invitation: Explicitly ask permission. "May we take a moment to ask the Holy Spirit for His perspective on this?"
  • Directed Listening: Give the client a specific question to ask God. "Holy Spirit, what is the truth about this situation?"
  • Body Awareness: Encourage the client to notice where they feel peace or tension. God often speaks through the "peace that surpasses understanding" (Philippians 4:7).
Coach Tip

Don't be afraid of the "uncomfortable" silence. When a client is processing a revelation, the silence is where the "cement" of transformation dries. If you speak too soon, you may interrupt a divine download.

The 'Scripture Test': Validating Insight

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢, we believe that God will never tell a client something that contradicts His written Word. Subjective revelation must always bow to objective Scripture.

As a coach, you must be well-versed in the Word to act as a "Biblical Guardrail." If a client says, "I feel God is telling me to leave my husband because I'm not happy," you must be prepared to gently use the Scripture Test, as God's revealed will in the Bible prioritizes covenant over temporary happiness.

Type of Insight Subjective Feeling Scriptural Filter (The Test)
Career Move "I feel a sense of excitement." Does this use my God-given gifts for His glory? (1 Peter 4:10)
Interpersonal Conflict "I feel I should cut them off." Does this align with the ministry of reconciliation? (2 Cor 5:18)
Financial Decision "I want to take this risk." Is this based on greed or stewardship? (Luke 12:15)
Coach Tip

Always have a Bible (digital or physical) nearby. If a client receives a "word," ask them: "Where do we see this principle reflected in the character of Jesus or the teachings of Scripture?" This empowers them to become self-feeders of the Word.

Distinguishing the Three Voices

Discernment is the ability to distinguish between the three primary voices that speak into a client's mind during a coaching session. A 2021 study on spiritual discernment in counseling found that clients who could distinguish "self-talk" from "perceived divine guidance" reported 40% higher levels of decisional certainty.

1. The Voice of Self (Human Intuition)

This voice is often rooted in past experiences, ego, or survival instincts. It sounds like: "I should," "I must," or "What will people think?" It is often logical but lacks supernatural peace.

2. The Voice of the Enemy (Deception)

The enemy‚Äôs voice is characterized by condemnation, confusion, and hurry. If the "revelation" makes the client feel hopeless or pressured to act impulsively without prayer, it is likely not from God.

3. The Voice of the Spirit (Revelation)

The Holy Spirit‚Äôs voice brings conviction (not condemnation), clarity, and peace. It often says things that human logic wouldn't think of, yet it feels "right" in the deepest part of the soul.

Coach Tip

Teach your clients the "Fruit Test" (Galatians 5:22-23). Does this insight produce love, joy, and peace? Or does it produce anxiety, pride, and strife? The fruit reveals the root.

The Ethics of 'Prophetic Coaching'

As you grow in your spiritual gifts, you may receive insights for the client. However, professional Christian coaching requires strict ethical boundaries to avoid "spiritual abuse" or over-dependence on the coach.

The Golden Rule of Revelation: The coach‚Äôs insight should only confirm what God is already speaking to the client. We never use "God told me to tell you" as a way to bypass the client‚Äôs own agency or the coaching process.

  • Avoid Directive Prophecy: Never tell a client who to marry, what job to take, or where to move based on your "revelation."
  • Use "Humble Inquiry": Instead of "God says," try "I'm sensing a theme of [X], does that resonate with what you're hearing from the Lord?"
  • Maintain the Partnership: Remember that you are a coach, not a guru. The Holy Spirit is the Counselor; you are the facilitator.

From Human Logic to God's Perspective

Human logic is horizontal; it looks at resources, obstacles, and past data. Divine Revelation is vertical; it looks at God's sovereignty, timing, and purpose. Coaching helps the client "set their minds on things above" (Colossians 3:2).

Consider the difference in perspective:
Human Logic: "I am 50 years old; it's too late to start a business. I don't have enough savings."
God's Perspective: "I am the God of the eleventh hour. Your years of experience are the fuel for your new assignment. I am your Provider."

Coach Tip

When a client is stuck in human logic, use the "God-Sized Question": "If resources, age, and fear were not factors, and you knew God was backing you 100%, what would your next step be?" This bypasses the logical brain and taps into the spirit.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between the voice of the Spirit and the voice of the Enemy in a session?

The Spirit brings conviction, peace, and clarity, while the Enemy brings condemnation, confusion, and a sense of urgent pressure or hurry.

2. Why is the "Scripture Test" essential in the Revelation pillar?

It provides an objective standard to validate subjective feelings or insights. Since God never contradicts His Word, Scripture is the ultimate filter for any perceived revelation.

3. What is the ethical danger of "Prophetic Coaching"?

The danger is creating over-dependence on the coach or using "spiritual authority" to bypass the client's own discernment, which can lead to spiritual manipulation or directive coaching.

4. How does a "God-Moment" differ from standard coaching dialogue?

A God-Moment shifts the focus from the coach/client interaction to the client's direct interaction with the Holy Spirit, often involving intentional silence and listening.

Key Takeaways

  • Revelation is the Engine: While Connection and Heart-Search prepare the ground, Revelation provides the divine "seed" for true transformation.
  • Silence is Productive: Extending the pause allows the Holy Spirit space to speak to the client's heart.
  • Word and Spirit: Effective coaching always balances the subjective leading of the Spirit with the objective truth of the Word.
  • Confirm, Don't Command: Your insights as a coach should serve to confirm what the client is already sensing from God.
  • Shift Perspectives: Moving from human logic to God's perspective is the key to breaking through "impossible" obstacles.
