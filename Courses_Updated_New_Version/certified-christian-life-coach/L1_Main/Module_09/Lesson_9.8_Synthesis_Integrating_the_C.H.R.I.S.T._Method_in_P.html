Practice Exercises

Exercise 1: The "Say This Out Loud" Prompt

Common Mistakes New Coaches Make

In this scenario, it is easy to fall into these traps. Watch out for them in your practice:

  • Over-Spiritualizing: Agreeing that the client should "just pray more" without exploring if prayer is being used as an escape from action.
  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Saying "Oh, you're great! You'll be fine!" This dismisses the client's very real internal struggle.
  • Becoming the Teacher: Launching into a 10-minute sermon on identity instead of asking powerful questions that let the client discover the truth.
  • Taking it Personally: Feeling like you've failed if the client doesn't immediately "get over" their imposter syndrome in one session.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between "Humility" and "Spiritual Imposter Syndrome"?

Humility recognizes that God is the source of strength while still stepping into the calling. Spiritual Imposter Syndrome uses "unworthiness" as a reason to disobey or delay the calling.

2. Why is it dangerous to simply "cheerlead" a client like Beth?

Cheerleading is a surface-level fix. It doesn't address the root limiting belief, meaning the imposter syndrome will simply return the next time the client faces a challenge.

3. According to the Master Script, what is the definition of a coach?

A coach is described as a "partner with a flashlight," not an expert with a megaphone. This emphasizes partnership and guidance over authority.

4. What is the first thing you should do when a client expresses deep insecurity?

Validate the honesty and the emotion behind the statement before attempting to pivot to a solution or a truth.

Key Takeaways

  • Imposter Syndrome is common: Especially among high-achieving women in their 40s and 50s transitioning to coaching.
  • Validate then Pivot: Acknowledge the feeling, then challenge the logic using Biblical alignment.
  • Wounded Healers: Remind clients that their struggles often provide the very empathy needed to be an effective coach.
  • Practice Out Loud: Coaching is a vocal skill. Use the scripts provided to build your professional presence.
  • Focus on the "One Step": Success isn't "curing" the client; it's helping them take the next obedient step.
