The 'Three Lights' of Discernment

Adapted from the classic teachings of F.B. Meyer, the Three Lights of Discernment provides a balanced approach to seeking divine will. For the Christian Life Coach, this serves as a safeguard against spiritual impulsivity.

The Light Source Coaching Inquiry
The Word Scriptural Alignment "Does this decision align with the timeless principles of the Bible?"
The Spirit Inward Peace/Prompting "Do you have a 'settled peace' or a 'restless urgency' in your spirit?"
Circumstances Providential Openings "Are the doors actually opening, or are we trying to kick them down?"

When all three lights align‚ÄîScripture permits it, the Spirit confirms it with peace, and circumstances provide the path‚Äîthe client can move forward with Covenant Confidence. If even one light is "red," it is a signal for further Heart-Search (H).

Coach Tip: The Urgency Red Flag

God rarely speaks in a voice of panicked urgency. If a client says, "I have to do this by Friday or I'll miss God's best," they are likely operating out of a Stronghold of Scarcity rather than Revelation. Use the Heart-Search pillar to explore the fear behind the deadline.

Unmasking Confirmation Bias

In psychology, confirmation bias is the tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information in a way that confirms one's preexisting beliefs. In coaching, this often looks like "Spiritual Projection"‚Äîwhere the client projects their own desires onto God's voice.

According to a 2022 study on cognitive biases in decision-making (n=1,200), over 74% of participants failed to recognize their own bias even when presented with contradictory evidence. In a spiritual context, this can lead to "Christianized" mistakes.

Techniques to Address Bias:

  • The "What If" Null Hypothesis: Ask, "If God were to say 'No' to this plan today, what would be your immediate emotional reaction?" (This uncovers attachment).
  • The Outsider Perspective: "If your most trusted, spiritually mature friend saw this situation, what concerns might they raise?"
  • Evidence Tracking: Ask the client to list 3 reasons not to move forward. If they cannot find any, they are likely in a bias loop.

The Revelation (R) Pivot

One of the most advanced skills in the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢ is knowing when to pivot. Sometimes, the initial "Revelation" was simply a "Connection" (C) point to get the client moving, but as they walk it out, a deeper truth is revealed.

A Revelation Pivot occurs when the coaching goal changes because the client receives a higher-order insight. For example, a client may come to you to "Save their business," but through Heart-Search, they realize God is actually calling them to "Close the business to save their family."

Coach Tip: Navigating the Pivot

When a goal shifts mid-contract, don't view it as a failure of the initial plan. Celebrate it! Say: "We have successfully used the Revelation pillar to find the Real Root. Our new goal is even more aligned with your calling."

Practical Discernment Exercises

For high-stakes decisions (career shifts, $10k+ investments, relocation), use the Discernment Grid exercise:

  1. The Silence Audit: Have the client sit in 10 minutes of silence, then write down the first three fears that arise. (Discernment is often blocked by unaddressed fear).
  2. The Legacy Filter: "Ten years from now, looking back at this decision through the lens of your Kingdom legacy, which path feels like it has the most 'weight' of glory?"
  3. The Fruit Forecast: Examine the potential "fruit" of the decision. Does it produce the Fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23) or the Fruit of the Flesh (Striving, Anxiety, Pride)?

The Coach as 'Discernment Partner'

It is tempting, especially as you grow in spiritual authority, to tell the client what you think God is saying. Avoid this. Your role is a Partner, not a Director.

A Director says: "I feel like the Lord is telling you to quit your job."
A Partner says: "As you were speaking about your job, I noticed your body language shifted and you mentioned a lack of peace. What do you think the Holy Spirit is highlighting in that lack of peace?"

Coach Tip: The Power of "I Notice"

Use "I notice" statements to mirror back the client's own spiritual frequency. It keeps the responsibility of revelation on the client and the Holy Spirit, which is the core of the R Pillar.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which "Light" of discernment is most commonly ignored by impulsive clients?

Circumstances. Clients often feel a "spiritual" pull but ignore the practical "closed doors" or lack of resources, mistaking persistence for faith.

2. How does confirmation bias manifest in a Christian coaching session?

It manifests as "Spiritual Projection," where the client only notices scriptures or "signs" that support what they already want to do, while ignoring contradictory evidence.

3. What is the primary difference between a "Director" and a "Discernment Partner"?

A Director gives the "answer" or "word" to the client, while a Discernment Partner asks powerful questions and mirrors observations to help the client hear from God themselves.

4. When should a coach facilitate a "Revelation Pivot"?

When a higher-order insight reveals that the current coaching goal is no longer aligned with God's revealed will for the client's current season.

Key Takeaways

  • Discernment is a tri-fold process involving the Word, the Spirit, and Circumstances.
  • Addressing confirmation bias is essential for objective, spiritually-grounded coaching.
  • The coach must remain a facilitator of the client's revelation, not the source of it.
  • High-stakes decisions require a "slow-down" process to bypass the "urgency trap" of the flesh.
  • Pivoting is a sign of coaching mastery, not a failure of the original strategy.
