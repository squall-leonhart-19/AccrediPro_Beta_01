Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're starting out (and I remember this well!), it's tempting to want to "help" God by speaking for Him. Avoid these four common mistakes:

Mistake Why It Happens The Better Way
Rushing the Silence Feeling awkward or "unproductive" during quiet moments. Wait. The Holy Spirit often speaks in the silence after the noise stops.
Preaching vs. Coaching Wanting to give the client the "right" Bible verse immediately. Ask the Revelation Question. Let the Spirit bring the verse to them.
Analyzing the Lie Trying to figure out "why" they believe the lie (psychological focus). Focus on the revelation. The "why" is less important than the Truth.
Leading the Witness Asking, "Don't you think God wants you to be brave?" Ask open questions: "What is God saying to you right now?"
Grace's Insight

In my first year, I used to talk to fill the silence because I was afraid the client would think I didn't know what I was doing. Now, I know that silence is my most powerful tool. It's the "waiting room" for revelation.

Success Markers

How do you know the session was successful? Look for these "Revelation Indicators":

  • The "Sigh of Relief": A physical release of tension in the client's shoulders or breath.
  • Shift in Language: Moving from "I should" or "I must" to "He says" or "I am."
  • Emotional Resonance: Tears are common, but so is a quiet, deep peace.
  • Alignment: The revelation matches the character of God as revealed in Scripture.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Revelation Question" in a Heart-Search?

The purpose is to shift the client's focus from their own logic to the Holy Spirit's voice, allowing for a direct spiritual breakthrough rather than just intellectual understanding.

2. If a client remains silent for more than 60 seconds after you ask a question, what should you do?

Remain in prayerful silence. Do not interrupt the process. If the silence feels stuck after several minutes, you can gently ask, "What are you noticing in your heart right now?"

3. Why is "Preaching" considered a pitfall in this specific coaching module?

Preaching provides an external truth, whereas Heart-Search coaching facilitates an internal revelation. When the client "hears" it from the Spirit, the level of ownership and transformation is significantly higher.

4. How do you handle a revelation that seems to contradict Scripture?

Gently bring it back to the Word. You might say, "That's interesting. Let's look at what God says about [topic] in His Word. How does that revelation align with His promise in [Scripture]?"

Key Takeaways

  • The "Heart-Search" is a transition from horizontal problem-solving to vertical revelation.
  • Your primary role is a facilitator of the conversation between the client and God.
  • Silence is not an absence of progress; it is the space where the Spirit works.
  • Always conclude a revelation by asking for the "Counter-Truth" to the identified lie.
  • Success is measured by the client's internal peace and biblical alignment, not your "performance."
