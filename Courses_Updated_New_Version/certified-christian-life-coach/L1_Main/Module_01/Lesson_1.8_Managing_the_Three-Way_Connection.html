Practice Exercise: Your Turn

The "Imago Dei" Breath: Before you speak, take a breath and silently say: "This person is made in the image of God." This shifts your brain from "problem-solving mode" to "connection mode."

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • The "Scripture Band-Aid": Using a verse to shut down a client's pain instead of walking through it with them.
  • The "Me Too" Trap: Spending 10 minutes talking about your own empty nest instead of listening to Deborah's.
  • Premature Problem Solving: Trying to give Deborah a business plan before she feels emotionally safe.
  • Ignoring Non-Verbals: Looking at your notes instead of maintaining soft, encouraging eye contact.
Grace's Insight

In my practice, I've found that the most powerful connection happens in the silence. If you ask a deep question, wait at least 5-8 seconds before speaking again. Let the Holy Spirit work in the space between your words.

Check Your Understanding

1. Deborah says, "I feel like I'm invisible." What is the first priority of a Christian Life Coach in this moment?

The priority is Validation and Presence. You must acknowledge the reality of her feeling without trying to "correct" it immediately, affirming her value as a person made in God's image.

2. What is the "Fixer Trap" in the context of Deborah's anxiety?

The Fixer Trap is jumping straight to business advice or "keeping busy" tips. This breaks connection because it signals to the client that her feelings are a problem to be solved rather than an experience to be understood.

Key Takeaways

  • Connection is the Foundation: Without a safe emotional container, no amount of coaching strategy will stick.
  • Validate Before You Navigate: Always acknowledge the client's current emotional state before moving toward goals.
  • Identify the Imago Dei: See the client as God sees themâ€”complete, valuable, and full of potential, even in their "empty" seasons.
  • Practice Silence: Master the art of the "Comfortable Pause" to allow the client space to process deep emotions.
