Assessing Spiritual Readiness

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢, the "C" (Connection) requires us to determine if the client is ready for the transformation that God offers. Coaching is a forward-moving process, but spiritual growth requires a specific kind of fertility in the heart.

A client may be "successful" by world standards‚Äîperhaps a 45-year-old executive or a retired teacher looking for their next chapter‚Äîbut if they are spiritually resistant, the coaching process will stall. We are looking for spiritual coachability: a combination of humility, hunger, and a willingness to be honest before God.

Coach Tip

Listen for the "But": When a client says, "I want God's will, but I'm not willing to change my [habit/relationship/view]," they are signaling a lack of alignment. Your job isn't to force them, but to discern if they are ready for the Heart-Search (Module 2) that coaching requires.

Indicator Aligned & Ready Misaligned / Not Ready
Motivation Desires to honor God and fulfill calling. Seeking a "magic wand" to fix problems without effort.
Accountability Welcomes biblical truth and gentle correction. Defensive or evasive when spiritual fruit is discussed.
Spiritual Agency Takes responsibility for their own walk with Christ. Blames others or God for their current circumstances.

Navigating Denominational Diversity

As a Certified Christian Life Coach‚Ñ¢, you will encounter clients from various backgrounds‚ÄîBaptist, Catholic, Pentecostal, Anglican, and non-denominational. One of the most common fears for new coaches is: "What if my client believes something different than I do about secondary issues?"

The principle here is found in the famous maxim often attributed to Augustine: "In essentials, unity; in non-essentials, liberty; in all things, charity."

Your connection must be built on the Essentials of the Faith (the authority of Scripture, the Lordship of Jesus Christ, and the work of the Holy Spirit). However, you must respect Denominational Diversity regarding styles of worship, views on specific spiritual gifts, or liturgical preferences. If you cannot coach someone outside your specific "tribe" with genuine love and respect, you limit the reach of your calling.

Case Study: The Denominational Bridge

Coach: Deborah (51, former Nurse). Client: Maria (44, stay-at-home mom). Deborah is from a charismatic background; Maria is from a traditional, liturgical background. During their second session, Maria expressed discomfort with Deborah‚Äôs use of the phrase "God told me."

Intervention: Instead of defending her terminology, Deborah pivoted to their shared value: The Word of God. She asked Maria, "How do you typically sense God‚Äôs guidance in your life?" Maria shared about her love for the Psalms and the liturgy. Deborah aligned her coaching language to Maria's "spiritual mother tongue," focusing on "scriptural resonance."

Outcome: The connection deepened because Maria felt respected, not judged. Deborah now earns a consistent $3,500/month coaching women from various liturgical backgrounds by focusing on biblical alignment rather than denominational jargon.

Identifying Red Flags & Referrals

Discernment also means knowing when not to coach. Because coaching is focused on the future and personal growth, it is not a substitute for clinical therapy or deep pastoral deliverance. A 2022 industry report noted that 22% of coaching clients actually present with issues that require a mental health professional.

As a professional, you must recognize these "Red Flags":

  • Active Trauma: The client is currently in a state of crisis or unable to function due to recent trauma.
  • Clinical Depression/Anxiety: The client‚Äôs symptoms are debilitating and require medical or psychological intervention.
  • Addiction: Active, unmanaged substance abuse or behavioral addictions.
  • Abusive Situations: If a client is in immediate physical danger, your role shifts from coach to advocate for their safety (referral to local authorities or shelters).
Coach Tip

The "Past vs. Future" Test: If a client spends 90% of the session stuck in the pain of the past without the ability to look forward, they likely need a therapist. If they acknowledge the past but are hungry to move forward, they are ready for a coach.

The Discernment Filter

How do you know if a partnership is "God-ordained"? We use the Discernment Filter during the initial consultation. This is a three-pronged approach to seeking the Holy Spirit's "green light."

1. The Peace of the Spirit

Colossians 3:15 tells us to "let the peace of Christ rule in your hearts." As you talk to a potential client, do you feel a sense of "settledness," or is there a nagging "check" in your spirit? Do not ignore your intuition; it is often the Holy Spirit's warning system.

2. Scriptural Alignment

Does the client‚Äôs goal align with biblical principles? For example, if a client wants coaching on how to "discreetly" leave their spouse without biblical grounds, you cannot align with that goal. We only connect with goals that can be invited into the light of God's Word.

3. The Witness of the "Third Party"

In Christian coaching, there is always a third party in the room: The Holy Spirit. During the Discovery Call, pray silently: "Lord, is this a person You have assigned to me?" Look for "God-winks"‚Äîshared scriptures, common themes, or a sudden clarity of the client's core need.

The Discovery Call Framework

The Discovery Call is your professional "first date." It is where you move from Contact to Connection. For many coaches in their 40s and 50s, this feels like a "sales call," which can trigger imposter syndrome. Reframe this: It is a Discernment Call.

The Professional Discovery Flow:

  1. The Opening (5 mins): Build rapport and set the "sacred space" with a brief prayer.
  2. The Heart-Search (15 mins): Ask powerful questions. "What is the one thing that, if it changed, would transform your walk with God?"
  3. The Alignment Check (5 mins): Discuss values and your coaching style. "I use the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢‚Äîhow does that resonate with you?"
  4. The Invitation (5 mins): If you sense alignment, invite them into a covenant. If not, provide a "Blessed Referral."
Coach Tip

Income Insight: High-level Christian coaches often convert 50-70% of their discovery calls. If you are charging $1,500 for a 3-month package, just two "aligned" clients a month creates a $3,000/month revenue stream. Focus on quality of alignment over quantity of leads.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Essentials vs. Non-Essentials" principle used for in coaching?

It is used to navigate denominational diversity. We maintain unity on core biblical truths (essentials) while allowing for liberty and respect regarding secondary denominational practices (non-essentials).

2. Which of the following is a "Red Flag" that requires a referral rather than coaching?

Active trauma, clinical depression/anxiety, active addiction, or an immediate abusive situation. These require clinical or specialized care that goes beyond the scope of life coaching.

3. What are the three components of the "Discernment Filter"?

1. The Peace of the Spirit, 2. Scriptural Alignment, and 3. The Witness of the "Third Party" (the Holy Spirit).

4. How should you reframe the "Discovery Call" to avoid feeling like a salesperson?

Reframe it as a "Discernment Call." Your primary goal is not to "sell" but to determine if the partnership is God-ordained and if the client is spiritually ready for the coaching journey.
Coach Tip

The Power of the Referral: Never be afraid to say, "I don't think I'm the right coach for you right now." A "Blessed Referral" protects your reputation, the client's well-being, and keeps your practice aligned with God's best for both of you.

Key Takeaways

  • Spiritual readiness is marked by humility, hunger, and a willingness to take spiritual agency.
  • Professionalism in Christian coaching involves respecting denominational differences while standing firm on the Essentials of the Faith.
  • Safety and ethics require a clear understanding of when to refer a client to a therapist or pastor.
  • The Holy Spirit is an active participant in the discernment process; listen for His "check" or His "peace."
  • The Discovery Call is a sacred gate-keeping exercise to ensure a vision-match between coach and client.
