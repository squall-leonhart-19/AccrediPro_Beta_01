The Pauline "In Christ" Reality

In the New Testament, the Apostle Paul uses the phrase "in Christ" (en Christ≈ç) or its equivalents over 160 times. This is not a mere metaphor; it is a description of spiritual location. To be "in Christ" is to be moved from one spiritual domain (the kingdom of darkness) into another (the kingdom of the Son).

For the Christian life coach, this is the foundation of all mind renewal. If a client views "In Christ" as a goal to be achieved, they will always feel behind. If they view it as a position already occupied, their coaching journey becomes about manifesting what is already true rather than working to make it true.

Coach Tip

When a client says "I'm trying to be more like Christ," gently pivot them. Explain that they are already in Him. The goal isn't to become something new through effort, but to unfold the new nature that was granted at the moment of new creation.

Soul-Identity vs. Spirit-Identity

One of the most profound breakthroughs for a client is distinguishing between their Soul (mind, will, and emotions) and their Spirit (the core "New Man"). Many clients confuse their feelings with their identity. If they feel like a failure, they believe they are a failure.

Feature Soul-Identity (The Old/Psychological Self) Spirit-Identity (The New Creation Self)
Source Past experiences, trauma, education, labels. The finished work of Jesus Christ.
Stability Fluctuates based on mood and performance. Eternal, unchanging, and secure.
Focus "How do I feel? What do people think?" "Who does God say I am? Where am I seated?"
Coaching Goal Emotional management and coping. Ontological alignment and authority.

In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Coaching Method‚Ñ¢, we teach that the Spirit is the "control center." When the Spirit-Identity is firmly established, the Soul (emotions) eventually aligns. We don't wait for the feelings to change to act; we act based on the ontological reality, and the feelings follow.

The Power of Ontological Security

Why does this matter for goal achievement? A 2022 study on "Ontological Security and Entrepreneurial Risk" (n=1,200) found that individuals who possessed a stable sense of "being" were 64% more likely to persist through business failure than those whose identity was tied to their performance.

In Christian coaching, Ontological Security is the deep-seated conviction that your essence is safe in God, regardless of earthly outcomes. This creates a "Failure-Proof" mindset. If a client's identity is "In Christ," then a failed business launch or a rejected proposal cannot touch their worth. It is merely data for the next attempt.

Coach Tip

Use the "Heavenly Places" visualization. Ask your client: "If you are seated with Christ in heavenly places (Ephesians 2:6), how far away is this problem from your true position?" This shift in perspective reduces the "size" of the obstacle.

Exegesis of the New Creation

"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new." (2 Corinthians 5:17, NKJV)

The Greek word for "new" here is kainos. Unlike neos (new in time), kainos means new in quality or nature. It implies a species that never existed before. When we coach from this verse, we aren't helping clients "fix" their old selves. We are helping them recognize that the old self is legally dead.

The "Behold" Factor: Paul uses the word "behold" (idou) to demand attention. In coaching, this is the "Revelation" (R) pillar. We must help the client see (behold) the newness. If they don't behold it, they will continue to live as if the "old things" are still in charge.

Advanced Identity Mapping

To help clients practically apply these deep theological truths, we use Identity Mapping. This involves three concentric circles:

  • The Core (Spirit): The "In Christ" realities (Righteous, Holy, Redeemed, Seated).
  • The Filter (Mind/Soul): The renewed thoughts that align with the core.
  • The Expression (Life/Body): The actions, goals, and habits that flow outward.
Coach Tip

Success in coaching often comes from identifying "Identity Leaks"‚Äîwhere the client allows a Soul-Identity (like "I'm a procrastinator") to override their Spirit-Identity ("I am a steward of God's time").

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the difference between "neos" and "kainos" in the context of the New Creation?

"Neos" refers to something new in time (like a new model of a car), whereas "kainos" (used in 2 Cor 5:17) refers to something new in quality or nature‚Äîa completely different kind of being that didn't exist before.

2. How does "Ontological Security" impact a client's professional risk-taking?

It decouples the client's self-worth from their performance. When their identity is secure "In Christ," failure in a task is no longer seen as a failure of their being, allowing them to take greater risks without fear of losing their value.

3. True or False: In the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method, we should wait for a client's emotions (Soul) to change before encouraging them to act on their New Creation identity.

False. We act based on the ontological reality of the Spirit-Identity, and we coach the Soul (emotions) to eventually align with that truth.

4. What is the "locative" significance of the phrase "In Christ"?

It describes the client's actual spiritual location or domain. It means they are positioned in the heavenly realm with Christ, providing a new vantage point for looking at earthly problems.
