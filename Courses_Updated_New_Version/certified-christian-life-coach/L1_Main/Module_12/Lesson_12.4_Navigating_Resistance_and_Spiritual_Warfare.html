Identifying the Three Sources of Resistance

In Christian life coaching, we must recognize that resistance is rarely one-dimensional. A 2021 study on behavioral change in faith-based contexts suggested that 68% of participants who failed to sustain change cited "unforeseen internal or external pressures" that they couldn't name. We name them as: The World, The Flesh, and The Devil.

1. The World (External Pressure)

This is the resistance that comes from the client's environment. It includes cultural norms, unsupportive family members, or the "hustle culture" that contradicts biblical rest. When a client tries to change, the "system" around them often tries to pull them back into their old role to maintain the status quo.

2. The Flesh (Internal Biological/Psychological Resistance)

As we learned in Lesson 12.1, the brain loves efficiency. The "Flesh" is often the scriptural term for our fallen nature and the entrenched neural pathways that seek comfort over growth. It is the part of the client that still craves the "leeks and onions of Egypt" even while walking toward the Promised Land.

3. The Devil (Spiritual Opposition)

Spiritual warfare is a reality for the Christian coach. If a client is moving toward their God-given purpose, they become a threat to the kingdom of darkness. This resistance often manifests as sudden, irrational fear, intense self-doubt, or "accidental" obstacles that seem timed to thwart progress.

Coach Tip: Identifying the Source

Don't assume every obstacle is a demon. If a client is tired because they aren't sleeping, that's "The Flesh." If they are discouraged because of a critical spouse, that's "The World." Discernment is the ability to see which source is most active so you can apply the right remedy.

Coaching Through the "Law of Sin" (Romans 7)

One of the greatest barriers to growth is shame. When clients know what to do but find themselves doing the opposite, they often conclude they are "bad Christians." As a coach, you must lead them to the theology of Romans 7.

The Apostle Paul describes a universal human experience: "For I do not do the good I want to do, but the evil I do not want to do‚Äîthis I keep on doing" (Romans 7:19). Paul identifies a "law" at work in his members. In coaching, we frame this as the Residual Resistance of the Old Self.

Discerning Burnout vs. Spiritual Oppression

Many clients (especially high-achieving women in their 40s and 50s) suffer from burnout, yet they often mislabel it as spiritual failure. Conversely, some face spiritual oppression but try to treat it with a nap and a green juice. You must help them discern the difference.

Psychological Burnout:

  • Source: Overextension of resources (time, energy, emotions).
  • Symptoms: Physical exhaustion, cynicism, reduced professional efficacy, "flat" emotions.
  • Remedy: Stewardship of the body, boundaries, rest, and Sabbath (Module 7).

Spiritual Oppression:

  • Source: Targeted attacks on identity or calling.
  • Symptoms: Sudden "darkness," irrational fear, persistent intrusive thoughts of "God has left me," spiritual apathy despite physical rest.
  • Remedy: Prayer, Scripture declaration, community intercession, and the Armor of God.

Coach Tip: The "Rest Test"

If a client takes a full weekend for rest and Sabbath and still feels an inexplicable, heavy "darkness" or "fog," start looking for spiritual roots. Burnout responds to rest; oppression requires spiritual authority.

The Armor of God as a Coaching Diagnostic

Ephesians 6:10-18 is often taught as a defensive list, but for the C.H.R.I.S.T. Method‚Ñ¢ coach, it serves as a Diagnostic Framework. When a client is stalling, ask where the "gap" in their armor might be.

  • The Belt of Truth: Is the client believing a lie about their identity or the situation? (The "H" in Heart-Search).
  • The Breastplate of Righteousness: Is there unconfessed sin or a lack of integrity that is making them feel vulnerable?
  • The Shoes of Peace: Is the client's "footing" unstable due to anxiety or a lack of preparation?
  • The Shield of Faith: Are they failing to "extinguish the flaming arrows" of the enemy's suggestions?
  • The Helmet of Salvation: Is their mind protected from thoughts of hopelessness or loss of security in Christ?
  • The Sword of the Spirit: Are they actively using the Word of God, or just thinking about it?

Maintaining Momentum When the "High" Fades

Sustainable growth requires moving from Inspiration to Integration. The "emotional high" of a coaching breakthrough usually lasts between 48 to 72 hours. After this, the "Valley of Change" begins.

To help clients maintain momentum during resistance:

  1. Normalize the Dip: Warn them that the "Monday Morning" after a "Sunday Mountain" experience is when the enemy strikes.
  2. The 1% Rule: In times of high resistance, don't ask for 100% effort. Ask for 1%‚Äîthe smallest possible step of obedience.
  3. Covenant Accountability: Remind them of the "C" in C.H.R.I.S.T.‚ÄîConnection. They are not fighting alone.
  4. Identity Anchors: Have the client write down their "Revelation" (Pillar R) while they are in the light, so they can read it when they are in the dark.
Coach Tip: Anticipatory Guidance

At the end of a powerful session, always ask: "When the resistance comes tomorrow‚Äîand it will‚Äîwhat is the one Truth you will stand on?" This prepares the client for the battle before it starts.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which source of resistance is characterized by the internal conflict described in Romans 7?

The Flesh. This is the internal resistance caused by our fallen nature and old neural pathways that conflict with our new desires in Christ.

2. If a client feels "flat" and physically exhausted but their mood improves significantly after a weekend of rest, were they likely experiencing spiritual oppression or burnout?

Burnout. Burnout typically responds to physiological and boundary-related remedies like rest and Sabbath, whereas spiritual oppression often persists despite physical rest.

3. In the "Armor of God" diagnostic, which piece of armor addresses believing lies about one's identity?

The Belt of Truth. It is the foundational piece that holds everything else together, ensuring the client is grounded in biblical reality rather than deceptive narratives.

4. According to the lesson, how long does the "emotional high" of a breakthrough typically last?

48 to 72 hours. This is why it is critical to have "Integration" strategies in place before the client leaves the coaching session.

Key Takeaways

  • Resistance is a multi-source phenomenon involving the World, the Flesh, and the Devil.
  • Romans 7 provides a "shame-free" framework for understanding the internal battle of transformation.
  • Discernment is required to distinguish between the need for physical rest (burnout) and spiritual authority (oppression).
  • The Armor of God serves as a comprehensive diagnostic tool for identifying gaps in a client's spiritual and psychological defense.
  • Sustainability is achieved by normalizing the "dip" and moving from emotional inspiration to disciplined integration.
