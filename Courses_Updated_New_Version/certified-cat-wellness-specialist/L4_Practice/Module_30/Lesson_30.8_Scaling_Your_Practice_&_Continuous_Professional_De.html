The Power of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is more than just an initial consultation; it's a foundational element of your client acquisition strategy. It's your opportunity to move beyond a transactional interaction and establish a genuine connection, demonstrating empathy and expertise. For many of your prospects, like you once were, this is a significant investment in their cat's health and their own peace of mind. Your role is to guide them through their concerns and present a clear path forward.

Research indicates that clients are 67% more likely to commit to a service when they feel genuinely heard and understood during the initial consultation, rather than simply being presented with a solution (Smith & Jones, 2022). This call is your chance to showcase your value, differentiate yourself from generic advice, and build the trust necessary for a successful partnership.

Coach Tip

Remember, the goal isn't to sell, but to serve. Approach every discovery call with a genuine desire to understand the client's challenges and determine if your services are the right fit. This authentic approach builds trust and makes the sales process feel natural and collaborative.

The 4-Phase Discovery Call Script

This script provides a framework for a 30-minute discovery call. Remember to personalize it with your own authentic voice and adapt it to the client's responses. The key is active listening and thoughtful questioning.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure with Eleanor Vance

Phase 1: Build Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Eleanor! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. It's a pleasure to meet you. How did you hear about my services?"
YOU:
"That's wonderful that [friend's name] referred you. She truly cares about her pets. So Eleanor, tell me â€” what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Mittens?"
Phase 2: Understand the Problem 10 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. It sounds really stressful for both you and Mittens. Can you tell me more about the over-grooming? When did it start, what triggers it, and how does it affect her daily life and yours?"
YOU:
"And the litter box issues â€“ when do those typically happen? What have you tried so far to address these behaviors? What worked, and what didn't seem to make a difference?"
YOU:
"Eleanor, if nothing changes, where do you see Mittens' health and your peace of mind in six months or a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this completely, what would Mittens' life look like? What would YOUR life look like?"
Coach Tip

Asking open-ended questions like "Tell me more..." and "What would that look like...?" encourages the client to elaborate, helping you uncover their deepest pain points and desires. This isn't just about Mittens' symptoms; it's about Eleanor's emotional investment.

Navigating Common Objections

Even with a perfect script, prospects will often voice objections. This is normal. View objections not as rejections, but as requests for more information or reassurance. Your confidence in handling them is key.

Common Objections & Your Confident Responses

"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is an important decision for both you and Mittens. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice." (This opens a dialogue to uncover the real concern.)
"It's too expensive."
"I hear you, Eleanor. Investing in your cat's well-being is a significant decision, especially given what you've already spent. Many of my clients initially feel the same way. What they've found is that addressing the root causes through a structured program actually saves money in the long run, avoiding continuous vet visits for behavioral issues, damaged furniture, or costly products that don't work. What value do you place on Mittens' happiness and your peace of mind?" (Reframe the cost as an investment and connect it to their stated desires.)
"I've tried everything before, and nothing has worked."
"I can certainly appreciate that feeling of frustration, Eleanor. It sounds like you've been incredibly diligent in trying to help Mittens. My approach, however, focuses on a holistic, evidence-based methodology, like the P.A.W.S. Methodâ„¢, that looks beyond individual symptoms to understand the entire feline environment and behavior. This personalized, long-term strategy is often what's missing from other solutions. Are you open to exploring a different, more comprehensive path?" (Acknowledge their experience, differentiate your approach, and invite them to consider a new perspective.)
"I need to discuss it with my spouse."
"That's perfectly natural, Eleanor, especially when it's a family decision. What specific questions do you anticipate your spouse might have that I could help answer now? Perhaps we could even schedule a brief follow-up call with both of you, so I can explain the program directly and address any concerns?" (Offer to help facilitate the conversation and remove obstacles.)
Coach Tip

Practice these responses out loud until they feel natural. Your tone of voice, body language (even on a video call), and confidence are just as important as the words themselves. Remember to pause, listen, and empathize before responding.

Confidently Presenting Your Program & Pricing

After you've presented your approach and addressed initial questions, it's time to talk about your program structure and investment. Confidence here is paramount. Avoid hesitation or apologizing for your fees.

When stating your price, do so clearly and then pause. Allow the client to process the information. Do not immediately fill the silence with justifications or further explanations. This pause demonstrates your confidence in the value you provide.

Example Pricing Presentation:

"Eleanor, my 12-week Feline Harmony Program is designed to provide you with comprehensive support and lasting results for Mittens. It includes weekly 60-minute coaching calls, a personalized assessment and action plan, unlimited email support between sessions, and exclusive access to my client resource library. The investment for this transformative program is $1,200. I also offer a flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $425."

After stating the price, you can follow up with a benefit-oriented question:

"How does that sound to you, and what questions do you have about the program structure or investment?"

Coach Tip

Always present your program's value before stating the price. Frame the investment in terms of the transformation and relief the client will experience, not just the features. This helps them connect the cost to the desired outcome.

Mastering the Close

The close is simply the natural next step in the conversation. It's about guiding the client to make a decision. A strong close is clear, direct, and offers a clear path forward.

Call-to-Action Practice:

  1. The Direct Close: "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the Feline Harmony Program is exactly what you and Mittens need. Are you ready to get started and bring peace back to your home?"
  2. The Assumptive Close: "Wonderful. So, to move forward, I'll send over the enrollment agreement and the link to schedule your first session. How does next Tuesday at 10 AM work for you?"
  3. The Alternative Close: "Would you prefer to pay the full program investment upfront and receive a bonus resource, or would the three-month payment plan be more suitable for your budget?"
  4. The Summary Close: "We've talked about Mittens' over-grooming and litter box issues, your frustration, and how my program provides a root-cause solution. This program will give you the tools and support to create a harmonious environment. Given all of that, what feels like the best next step for you?"

Practice these phrases until they feel comfortable and confident coming from you. The goal is to make the transition from discovery to enrollment seamless.

Practice Exercise: Role-Playing

The best way to master the discovery call is through practice. Find a trusted friend, family member, or fellow AccrediPro student to role-play with. Use the Eleanor Vance scenario and the 4-phase script. Focus on:

  • Active Listening: Truly hearing and reflecting what the "client" says.
  • Empathy: Connecting with their emotional state.
  • Confident Delivery: Speaking clearly and with conviction.
  • Handling Objections: Using the responses we discussed.
  • Clear Call-to-Action: Guiding the conversation towards the next step.

Record yourself if possible, and listen back for areas of improvement. Pay attention to your tone, pacing, and how you handle silences.

Check Your Understanding

1

During Phase 2 of the discovery call, "Understand the Problem," what is the primary goal beyond just listing symptoms?

The primary goal is to uncover the client's emotional investment, their frustrations, past attempts, and their vision for a transformed future. This helps connect their pain points to the value of your solution.
2

When a client raises the objection, "It's too expensive," what is an effective strategy to reframe the conversation?

Reframe the cost as an investment in a lasting solution that saves money in the long run (e.g., fewer vet visits for behavioral issues, no wasted money on ineffective products). Connect the investment directly to the value of their cat's happiness and their own peace of mind.
3

Why is it important to pause after stating your program's price during the discovery call?

Pausing demonstrates confidence in your value and allows the client to process the information without feeling pressured or overwhelmed. It also prevents you from immediately justifying the price, which can inadvertently signal insecurity.
4

What is the key difference between a "sales pitch" and a "discovery call" in your practice?

A sales pitch is often one-sided, focused on listing features and benefits. A discovery call, conversely, is a two-way conversation focused on listening, understanding the client's specific needs and desires, building rapport, and collaboratively determining if your service is the right solution for their unique situation. It's about serving, not just selling.
