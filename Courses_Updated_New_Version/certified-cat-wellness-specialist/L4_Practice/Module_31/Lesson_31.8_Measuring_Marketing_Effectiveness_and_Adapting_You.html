Structuring Your Discovery Call: A 30-Minute Blueprint

A successful discovery call isn't about improvisation; it's about following a clear, empathetic structure that guides the conversation naturally from introduction to a clear next step. Here's a proven blueprint:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Trust 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Sarah! It's wonderful to finally connect. Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. Before we dive into Luna's situation, I'd love to hear a bit more about how you found me and what sparked your interest in feline wellness."
YOU:
"That's fantastic that you connected with my message at the expo and on social media! It sounds like you're really dedicated to Luna's well-being. So Sarah, tell me ‚Äî what specifically made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Luna that brought you here?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understanding the Feline & Client Needs 10-12 min
YOU:
"Thank you for sharing that. Let's talk more about Luna. You mentioned weight gain and occasional vomiting. Can you tell me when these issues started? How do they affect Luna's daily life, and how do they impact you, seeing her struggle?"
YOU:
"And what steps have you already taken to address these concerns? What worked, what didn't, and what did your vet say?"
YOU:
"If nothing changes, where do you see Luna in a year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would Luna's life look like, and how would that make you feel?"

Phase 1: Building Rapport & Trust

The first few minutes of any discovery call are crucial for establishing a connection. Your goal is to make the client feel heard, understood, and comfortable. Remember, people buy from those they know, like, and trust.

  • Warm Welcome: Always start with a genuine thank you for their time.
  • Personal Connection: Ask how they found you. This not only gathers data but also helps you understand their journey.
  • Empathetic Listening: Listen more than you speak. Let them tell their story about their cat's struggles.
Coach Tip

As Bella, I always remind myself that clients are often coming to me after feeling frustrated and unheard by others. Your job in this phase is to validate their experience. Phrases like, "That sounds incredibly frustrating," or "I can only imagine how worried you must be," build instant empathy and trust.

Phase 2: Deeply Understanding the Feline & Client Needs

This is where you become a detective. Your role is to ask open-ended questions that allow the client to elaborate on their cat's issues, their previous attempts at solutions, and their deepest desires for their cat's health.

  • Probe for Details: Don't just accept surface-level answers. Ask "when," "how," and "what does that look like?"
  • Identify Pain Points: What are the biggest challenges or worries they have about their cat's health? How does this impact their own life?
  • Uncover Desired Outcomes: The "magic wand" question helps them visualize the ideal future, which becomes your shared goal.
  • Connect to Your Expertise: Listen for keywords that align with your Certified Cat Wellness Specialist‚Ñ¢ training, such as dietary imbalances, environmental stressors, or behavioral changes.
Coach Tip

Focus on the emotional impact. It's not just about Luna's weight; it's about Sarah missing her playful cat and worrying about her future. When you connect with their emotions, you demonstrate a deeper understanding that goes beyond symptoms. This is where your holistic approach shines.

Phase 3: Confidently Presenting Your Solution

Now that you understand their needs, it's time to position yourself as the solution. This isn't about a hard sell; it's about clearly explaining how your unique approach can bridge the gap between their current struggles and their desired outcomes.

  • Summarize & Validate: Reiterate their pain points to show you've truly listened. "So, if I'm understanding correctly, Luna's weight and vomiting are really impacting her energy, and you're looking for a comprehensive plan..."
  • Introduce Your Method: Explain how your P.A.W.S. Method‚Ñ¢ or signature program differs from what they've tried. Emphasize the holistic, root-cause approach.
  • Highlight Benefits, Not Just Features: Instead of "I offer dietary analysis," say "We'll identify specific foods that might be contributing to Luna's digestive upset, leading to less vomiting and more comfortable digestion."
  • Set Expectations: Give a realistic timeline for results, like "most clients see improvements within 4-6 weeks," to build confidence.

Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They aren't rejections; they're usually requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with confidence and empathy.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"I need to think about it."

"I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else that's giving you pause? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for Luna."

(Listen carefully to their answer. If it's about cost, move to the pricing discussion. If it's about time, explain the flexibility of your program. If it's about whether it will work, reinforce your unique approach and past client successes.)

"It's too expensive / I've already spent so much money."

"I hear you, Sarah, and I appreciate your honesty. Investing in Luna's long-term health is a significant decision. Many of my clients come to me after spending hundreds, even thousands, on treatments that only address symptoms or provide temporary relief. My program, while an investment upfront, aims to get to the root cause, potentially saving you more money and stress in the long run by preventing recurring issues and providing lasting solutions. What value do you place on Luna being vibrant and healthy again?"

(You can also offer payment plans if that's part of your business model.)

"I've tried everything, and nothing has worked."

"That's precisely why many clients come to me, Sarah. You've tried conventional approaches, and they haven't delivered the lasting results you're looking for. My approach as a Certified Cat Wellness Specialist‚Ñ¢ is fundamentally different. We're not just throwing solutions at symptoms; we're meticulously investigating Luna's entire health ecosystem ‚Äì diet, environment, stress, behavior ‚Äì to uncover the interconnected factors. This holistic, detective-like work is what often reveals the missing pieces and leads to breakthroughs where other methods have fallen short."

Coach Tip

Remember, an objection is often a hidden question. Instead of getting defensive, reframe it. If they say "It's too expensive," they're really asking, "Is the value worth the investment?" Your job is to confidently articulate that value and address their underlying concern.

The Art of Pricing Presentation

When it comes to discussing your fees, confidence is key. State your price clearly, without hesitation or apology. Remember the value you provide and the transformation you offer.

After you've presented your solution and addressed initial questions, transition to pricing:

YOU: "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed, I believe my Signature 12-Week Feline Wellness Program is exactly what Luna needs to get back to her healthy, playful self. This program includes a comprehensive initial assessment, weekly personalized action plans, direct access to me for support, and educational resources tailored to Luna's specific needs."

YOU: "The investment for this transformative 12-week journey is $1,800. Many clients choose our flexible payment plan of three monthly payments of $650. This includes everything we need to achieve lasting results for Luna."

Pause. Let the number sink in. Don't speak immediately after stating the price. Your confidence will speak volumes.

Coach Tip

Don't just state the price; connect it back to the transformation they desire. Remind them of the "magic wand" vision they shared. If Luna is back to her playful self, what is that worth to Sarah? Anchor the investment in that profound value.

Practice Your Call to Action

Once you've presented your pricing and addressed any remaining questions, it's time to guide them to the next step. Be clear and direct.

YOU: "So Sarah, are you ready to commit to Luna's health and start her journey towards optimal wellness with my Signature 12-Week Feline Wellness Program?"

If yes:

YOU: "Fantastic! The next step is to get you onboarded. I'll send over the enrollment agreement and a link to schedule our first in-depth assessment session. We can aim to get started as early as next week. How does that sound?"

If they need more time (revisit objection handling):

YOU: "I understand. What feels like the appropriate next step for you? Would you like a recap of the program details, or perhaps a day to discuss it with your family? I'm happy to follow up with you by ."

Coach Tip

Your belief in your program and its ability to help clients is your strongest asset. When you truly believe in the value you offer as a Certified Cat Wellness Specialist‚Ñ¢, your confidence becomes infectious. This isn't about selling; it's about empowering clients to make the best decision for their cat's health.

Check Your Understanding

1

What is the primary goal of the "Build Rapport & Trust" phase of a Discovery Call, and what key emotion should you aim to evoke in the client?

The primary goal is to establish a personal connection and make the client feel heard and comfortable. You should aim to evoke a sense of trust and validation, showing empathy for their situation.
2

When a client expresses the objection, "It's too expensive," how should you confidently reframe their concern, and what key aspect of your service should you emphasize?

You should reframe it as a question about value versus cost, acknowledging their previous spending. Emphasize the long-term savings and lasting solutions provided by your root-cause, holistic approach, contrasting it with temporary fixes.
3

During the "Presenting Your Solution" phase, what is the importance of summarizing the client's pain points before introducing your program?

Summarizing pain points demonstrates active listening and validates the client's experience. It shows you truly understand their needs, building credibility and making them more receptive to your proposed solution as it directly addresses their articulated problems.
4

When stating your program's price, what is a crucial non-verbal technique to employ immediately after quoting the investment?

Immediately after stating the price, you should pause and remain silent. This allows the client to process the information without interruption and demonstrates your confidence in the value you provide.
