Lives in a suburban home with her two cats, Luna (8 years, female DSH) and Orion (6 years, male Maine Coon mix).

Presenting Situation: Sarah reached out after finding your website through a local pet store referral. She's concerned about Luna, who has recently started urinating outside the litter box, despite a recent vet checkup showing no obvious medical issues. Luna also seems more withdrawn and less playful than usual. Sarah describes herself as "exhausted" from cleaning and worried about Luna's well-being. She's tried different litter, litter boxes, and even a Feliway diffuser with no lasting success.

Underlying Emotions & Concerns: Sarah feels guilty and frustrated. She loves Luna deeply but is at her wit's end. She's skeptical about "another expense" after investing in vet visits and various products, but her primary motivation is Luna's happiness and her own peace of mind. She values practical, sustainable solutions and wants to understand *why* this is happening, not just stop the symptom. She also worries about Orion being affected by Luna's stress.

Desired Outcome: Sarah wants Luna to be happy, healthy, and using the litter box consistently. She wants to feel like a good cat parent again and restore harmony in her home. She's looking for an expert who can offer a personalized plan and guide her through the process.

Understanding the Discovery Call: Your First Step to Impact

The discovery call is your initial, no-obligation conversation with a prospective client. It's not a sales pitch in the traditional sense, but rather a structured dialogue designed to achieve three key objectives:

  1. Build Rapport: Establish trust and a genuine connection. People work with those they like and trust.
  2. Understand Needs: Deeply listen to their challenges, frustrations, and desires for their cat's wellness. This is where you identify if you can truly help.
  3. Determine Fit: Assess if your services are the right solution for their specific situation and if they are ready to commit to the process.

Think of it as a mutual interview. You're assessing if they are a good fit for your programs, just as they are assessing if you are the right specialist for their needs. A well-executed discovery call has a conversion rate of 50-70% for experienced practitioners (Forbes, 2022), demonstrating its power in client acquisition.

Coach Tip

Remember, your prospect is likely feeling vulnerable and overwhelmed. Approach every discovery call with genuine curiosity and empathy. Your goal isn't to diagnose or solve their problem on the call, but to validate their feelings and show them that you understand their struggle. This builds immense trust!

The 4-Phase Discovery Call Script: Your Roadmap to Enrollment

A typical discovery call lasts 20-30 minutes. Here's a proven structure to guide your conversation with Sarah, or any other prospect:

Phase 1: Build Rapport & Set the Stage (5 minutes)

This phase is about making a warm connection and setting expectations for the call. You want them to feel comfortable sharing.

  • YOU: "Hi Sarah! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Bella. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
  • SARAH: "Hi Bella! Nice to meet you too. My friend Jessica from the pet store mentioned you. She said you helped her with her cat's anxiety."
  • YOU: "That's wonderful that Jessica referred you, she's been on quite a journey with her cat! So Sarah, tell me ‚Äì what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Luna?"

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Explore Desires (10-12 minutes)

This is where you listen, listen, listen. Ask open-ended questions to uncover the depth of their problem and what they truly desire. Avoid interrupting or offering solutions yet.

  • YOU: "Thank you for sharing that. It sounds incredibly frustrating and worrying when your cat isn't herself. Tell me more about Luna's litter box issues and her changes in behavior. When did this start, and how does it affect your daily life and Luna's well-being?"
  • SARAH: "Well, it started about two months ago... I feel like I'm constantly cleaning. I'm worried she's stressed, and honestly, it's making me stressed too. I just want her to be happy again."
  • YOU: "I completely understand. And what have you already tried to help Luna? What worked, and what didn't seem to make a difference?"
  • SARAH: "I've tried special litter, different boxes, even a calming diffuser. The vet said she's physically fine, which is a relief but also frustrating because I don't know what else to do."
  • YOU: "That's a common experience, Sarah. Many conventional approaches miss the subtle behavioral and environmental factors. Now, let's imagine for a moment: if nothing changes, where do you see yourself and Luna in six months or a year? And conversely, if you could wave a magic wand and fix this, what would your life and Luna's life look like?"

(Listen for emotional drivers, impact on her life, and her vision of success.)

Phase 3: Present Your Approach & Establish Value (8-10 minutes)

Now, it's your turn to speak. Summarize their pain points, then introduce your unique approach (like the P.A.W.S. Method‚Ñ¢) as the solution, focusing on benefits, not just features.

  • YOU: "Sarah, based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: You're a dedicated cat parent, deeply concerned about Luna's sudden litter box issues and withdrawal, despite trying various solutions. You feel frustrated and guilty, and you're looking for a clear path to help Luna feel happy and healthy again, and restore peace to your home. Does that feel accurate?"
  • SARAH: "Yes, absolutely. You've hit it right on the head."
  • YOU: "Great. The approach I use is different from what you've likely experienced before. Instead of just trying to stop the symptom, we look at the ROOT causes ‚Äì combining behavioral, environmental, nutritional, and emotional factors. I use my signature P.A.W.S. Method‚Ñ¢, a comprehensive 12-week program, to identify exactly what's driving Luna's stress and create a personalized plan. Most of my clients, like Jessica, start seeing significant positive changes within the first 4-6 weeks, leading to lasting harmony."
  • YOU: "We'll work together one-on-one, with weekly check-ins and a detailed action plan tailored specifically for Luna and your home environment. My role is to be your guide, providing expert insights and unwavering support every step of the way."

Coach Tip

When summarizing their problem, use their exact words and phrases where possible. This makes them feel deeply understood and heard. When presenting your solution, always tie it back to the benefits they expressed in Phase 2. For example, if they want "peace of mind," explain how your program delivers that.

Confident Pricing Presentation

This is often the most nerve-wracking part for new practitioners. Remember, you are offering immense value and expertise. State your price clearly, confidently, and then be silent. Let them process.

  • YOU: "So Sarah, does this sound like the comprehensive, personalized support you've been looking for to help Luna?"
  • SARAH: "It really does sound promising."
  • YOU: "Wonderful. My 12-week P.A.W.S. Method‚Ñ¢ Cat Wellness Program is an investment of $2,500. This includes everything we've discussed: your initial comprehensive assessment, 12 weeks of personalized coaching, weekly check-ins, custom action plans, and ongoing support. I also offer a flexible payment plan, breaking it into three monthly payments of $850."

(PAUSE. Let the silence hang. Do not fill it.)

Coach Tip

Practice saying your prices out loud until it feels natural. Your confidence in your pricing reflects your confidence in your value. When you state the price, keep your tone even and calm. Avoid apologizing or immediately justifying it. The value has already been established in Phase 3.

Mastering Objection Handling

Objections are rarely about the true problem; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Respond with empathy, validate their concern, and then reframe or provide a solution.

Practice Scenario: Handling Objections

Objection 1: "I need to think about it."

  • YOUR RESPONSE: "I completely understand, Sarah. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel fully confident. Can I ask ‚Äî what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or something else? I want to make sure you have all the information you need."
  • (Listen to her answer, then address the specific concern. If it's budget, re-emphasize the long-term cost of inaction or the payment plan. If it's time, discuss how the program integrates into her schedule.)

Objection 2: "That's a lot of money right now."

  • YOUR RESPONSE: "I hear you, Sarah. Investing in your cat's health is a significant decision. When clients tell me this, it's often because they're weighing the cost against the potential results. Think about what you've already spent on vet visits, special litter, and products that haven't fully solved the problem. My program is designed to be a lasting solution, preventing future issues and saving you money and stress in the long run. Plus, we do have that flexible payment plan available. What's the cost of *not* solving this for Luna and for your peace of mind?"

Objection 3: "I'm not sure if this will really work for Luna."

  • YOUR RESPONSE: "That's a very fair concern, Sarah, especially given what you've already tried. What makes my approach unique is its holistic and individualized nature. We're not just guessing; we're systematically investigating all potential factors impacting Luna. Every step is tailored to her unique personality, environment, and needs. My track record with clients like Jessica shows that this method consistently brings results because it addresses the core issues. What would need to happen for you to feel confident that this would work?"

Phase 4: Closing the Call & Call-to-Action (3-5 minutes)

If you've addressed their concerns, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Be clear and confident.

  • YOU: "Sarah, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe the P.A.W.S. Method‚Ñ¢ can bring the lasting change you're looking for with Luna. I'm excited about the possibility of working with you. Are you ready to move forward and get Luna on the path to feeling like herself again?"
  • (If yes) YOU: "Fantastic! I'll send over the enrollment agreement and a link to schedule our first session. We can begin as early as next week. I'm really looking forward to this!"
  • (If no, and you've addressed objections) YOU: "I completely respect your decision. Even if we don't work together, I hope our conversation today has provided some valuable insights for you and Luna. Please feel free to reach out if anything changes."

Coach Tip

Always end the call with a clear next step, regardless of whether they enroll. If they say yes, tell them exactly what to expect. If they say no, maintain professionalism and leave the door open. Your integrity and professionalism build your reputation.

The primary purpose of Phase 1 is to establish trust and a genuine connection with the prospective client, making them feel comfortable sharing their story. A key question is: "How did you hear about me?" or "What made you decide to reach out now?"

Key Takeaways

  • The Discovery Call is a structured conversation focused on connection, understanding, and determining mutual fit, not a hard sell.
  • Employ a 4-phase framework (Rapport, Problem, Solution, Close) to guide your calls effectively and professionally.
  • Active listening and empathetic questioning are paramount to uncovering a prospect's true pain points and desired outcomes.
  • Present your pricing with unwavering confidence, clearly articulating the value of your comprehensive programs.
  • Mastering objection handling involves validating concerns, reframing, and providing targeted solutions, seeing objections as opportunities for clarity.
  • Your certification empowers you to build a practice with significant income potential, allowing for both financial freedom and meaningful impact.
