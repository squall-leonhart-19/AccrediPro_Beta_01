Meet Rachel, a potential client who's reached out for help with her beloved cat, Luna. Understanding her situation, concerns, and goals is paramount to a successful discovery call. Remember, empathy is your superpower.

Rachel, 47

Found you through a friend's referral. Scheduled a discovery call.

Her Situation with Luna
Luna, an 8-year-old domestic shorthair, has recently developed chronic diarrhea, coat dullness, and increased anxiety. Rachel has tried multiple vet visits, dietary changes suggested by the vet (prescription foods), and even a calming diffuser, all with minimal lasting improvement. She feels frustrated and helpless.
Rachel's Personal Background
Rachel is a former teacher, now working part-time. She's a dedicated pet parent, often putting Luna's needs before her own. She's spent a considerable amount on Luna's health already, leading to budget concerns. She values thoroughness and genuine care.

"I've spent so much money on vet bills and special foods already that haven't worked. Is this really going to be different, and how much more will it cost?"

Needs to feel deeply heard and understood. Skeptical of quick fixes or generic advice. Values expertise, credentials, and a personalized, holistic approach. She'll need clear steps and reassurance.
Her Ultimate Goal for Luna
"I just want Luna to be comfortable, happy, and vibrant again. I miss seeing her playful self. I want to understand what's truly going on, not just mask symptoms."

Coach Tip from Bella

Before every discovery call, take 5 minutes to review the prospect's intake form or referral notes. This helps you tailor your opening, show you've listened, and identify potential pain points and aspirations. It's about making them feel seen from the very first minute.

Mastering the Discovery Call: A Step-by-Step Script

A structured approach ensures you cover all key points while allowing for authentic conversation. This 30-minute framework is designed to build trust, uncover needs, and position you as the ideal solution.

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

Phase 1: Building Authentic Rapport 5 min
YOU:
"Hi Rachel! Thank you so much for taking the time to chat with me today. I'm Bella Martinez, and I'm really looking forward to learning more about you and Luna. Before we dive in, I'd love to just take a moment to get to know you a bit. How did you hear about me?"
RACHEL:
"Hi Bella! Yes, I heard about you from Sarah, my friend. She raved about how much you helped her cat, Jasper. I'm really hoping you can do the same for Luna."
YOU:
"That's wonderful that Sarah referred you. She's been on quite a journey with Jasper, and I'm so glad he's doing better. So Rachel, thank you for sharing a bit about Luna on your intake form. Tell me â€” what made you decide to reach out now? What's going on with Luna that's prompted this call?"
Phase 2: Deeply Understanding the Feline & Human Needs 10 min

This is where you listen, listen, listen. Ask open-ended questions and let Rachel share her story.

YOU:
"Thank you for sharing, Rachel. Tell me more about Luna's chronic diarrhea and anxiety. When did these issues start? How do they affect her daily life, and how do they impact you as her devoted parent?"
RACHEL:
"It's been about six months now. The diarrhea is constant, and she's just not herself. She used to greet me at the door, but now she hides. I'm constantly worried about her, cleaning up messes, and feeling so guilty that I can't fix it. It's exhausting."
YOU:
"That sounds incredibly stressful and heartbreaking, Rachel. I hear how deeply you care for Luna. What have you tried so far to help her? What worked, even a little, and what didn't seem to make any difference?"
RACHEL:
"We've been to the vet so many times. She's had tests, been on antibiotics, tried several prescription diets... I even bought one of those calming pheromone diffusers. Nothing sticks. The vet just says 'keep trying different foods' or 'it might be IBS.' I feel like I'm just guessing."
YOU:
"I understand completely how frustrating that must be. It sounds like you've been incredibly diligent, but haven't found the answers you're looking for. Let me ask you this, Rachel: If nothing changes, where do you see Luna and yourself in another year? And if you COULD wave a magic wand and fix this, what would Luna's life look like, and how would that change things for you?"
Phase 3: Presenting Your Unique Solution 10 min

This is where you articulate your value, showing how your unique approach addresses her specific pain points.

YOU:
"Rachel, thank you for being so open. Based on everything you've shared, here's what I'm hearing: you're a devoted cat parent whose Luna is suffering from chronic digestive and behavioral issues, despite extensive conventional efforts. You feel frustrated and want a deeper understanding and a lasting solution, not just symptom management. Does that feel accurate?"
RACHEL:
"Yes, absolutely. You've hit it exactly."
YOU:
"The approach I use as a Certified Cat Wellness Specialist is different from what you've likely tried before. Instead of just treating symptoms, we look at the ROOT causes. I work with you over a period of 12 weeks to identify exactly what's driving Luna's digestive upset and anxiety â€“ whether it's diet, environmental stressors, microbiome imbalances, or a combination. We use a comprehensive, holistic framework, the P.A.W.S. Method™, to create a personalized plan. My clients typically start seeing significant improvements in their cat's energy, digestion, and overall well-being within the first 4-6 weeks, and often experience a complete turnaround by the end of our program."
YOU:
"This isn't about quick fixes; it's about lasting transformation, giving you the knowledge and tools to ensure Luna thrives long-term. How does that sound to you?"
Phase 4: Guiding Towards a Decision 5 min
YOU:
"So Rachel, based on what I've described about my approach, does this sound like the kind of comprehensive, personalized solution you've been looking for to help Luna feel like herself again?"
RACHEL:
"It really does, Bella. It sounds like you actually understand what I'm going through, and you're talking about finding answers, not just managing symptoms."
YOU:
"That's wonderful to hear, Rachel. I'm confident we can help Luna. Let me tell you a little more about how the program works and what the investment looks like, so you have all the information you need to decide if this is the right next step for you and Luna."

Confidently Handling Common Objections

Objections are a natural part of the sales process. They're often a request for more information or reassurance, not a definitive "no." Your role is to address them with empathy and confidence.

Common Objections & Your Responses

"It's too expensive. I've already spent so much money."
"I completely understand, Rachel. You've invested a lot already without getting the results you hoped for, and that's incredibly frustrating. My program is an investment in lasting solutions and Luna's long-term health, which ultimately saves you money and heartache in the long run. Think of the ongoing vet visits, prescription foods, and stress you've endured. This program is designed to get to the root cause, providing a clear path forward and peace of mind. Many clients find that addressing the underlying issues prevents future, more costly problems."
"I need to think about it."
"I completely understand, Rachel. This is an important decision, and I want you to feel absolutely confident. Can I ask â€” what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the time commitment, or perhaps you have some lingering questions about the process? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the best choice for Luna and yourself."
"I've tried everything before, and nothing has worked."
"I hear your frustration, Rachel, and it's completely valid given your experience. It sounds like you've been incredibly proactive, which tells me how dedicated you are to Luna's well-being. What makes my approach different is its holistic and individualized nature. We don't just throw solutions at symptoms; we conduct a deep dive into every aspect of Luna's life â€“ from diet specifics to environmental factors and emotional well-being â€“ to uncover the interconnected root causes. This tailored strategy, guided by the P.A.W.S. Method™, often reveals answers that conventional approaches might miss. You're not just 'trying another thing'; you're embarking on a guided, proven path to understanding and healing."
"I need to discuss this with my partner."
"That's absolutely fair, Rachel. It's important that you and your partner are on the same page, especially for such an important decision for Luna. Would it be helpful if I put together a brief summary of what we discussed, including the program details and investment, for you to share with them? Or perhaps, if they have questions, we could schedule a quick follow-up call with all three of us?"

Coach Tip from Bella

Silence is your friend. After asking a question or presenting your program, let the client speak. Don't rush to fill the void. Often, they will reveal more about their true concerns or decision-making process if you allow them the space to do so. This shows confidence and respect.

The Art of the Call to Action

After presenting your program and addressing any questions, it's time to invite them to take the next step. Be clear, direct, and supportive.

Closing Lines to Practice

Imagine Rachel has expressed enthusiasm and you've addressed her questions. Now, it's time to close.

  • Direct Close: "Rachel, does this sound like exactly what you and Luna need right now? If so, I'd love to welcome you into the program. We can get started today."
  • Benefit-Oriented Close: "Considering how much you want Luna to feel vibrant again, and how frustrated you've been with other approaches, do you see this program as the path to achieving those lasting results for her?"
  • Next Steps Close: "If you're ready to move forward, the next step is simple. I'll send you a link to enroll, and we can schedule our first deep-dive session. How does that sound?"
  • Addressing Hesitation Proactively: "Given everything we've discussed, if there's anything holding you back, please share it. My goal is to ensure you feel completely comfortable and confident in this decision."

Practice this out loud! The more you vocalize these closing statements, the more natural and confident you'll sound when speaking to a real prospect.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "Understand the Problem" phase in a discovery call?

The primary purpose is to actively listen and deeply understand the prospect's pain points, frustrations, and aspirations regarding their cat's health. It's about uncovering the emotional impact of the problem and what a successful outcome would mean for them.

2. Rachel expresses concern about the cost, stating, "I've already spent so much money." How should you respond to this objection?

Acknowledge her frustration and validate her feelings. Then, pivot to the long-term value and effectiveness of your program as an investment in a lasting solution that addresses root causes, ultimately saving money and stress compared to ongoing, ineffective treatments.

3. When presenting your program's investment, what is a critical non-verbal technique to employ?

State the price with confidence and then stop talking. Allow the client to process the information without you nervously filling the silence. This demonstrates your belief in your value.

4. What is the value of visualizing potential income scenarios for a new Cat Wellness Specialist?

Visualizing income potential helps combat imposter syndrome, provides motivation, and offers a tangible goal for business growth. It demonstrates that financial freedom and meaningful work are achievable through client acquisition.

Key Takeaways

  • A structured discovery call allows you to build rapport, deeply understand client needs, and confidently present your unique solutions.
  • Empathy and active listening are crucial for connecting with prospects and uncovering their true pain points and desires for their feline companions.
  • Objections are opportunities to provide more information and reassurance, strengthening the client's confidence in your services.
  • Present your program's investment by emphasizing the long-term value and transformative results, not just the cost.
  • Practice your closing lines and maintain confidence to guide prospects smoothly to the next step in their journey with you.
