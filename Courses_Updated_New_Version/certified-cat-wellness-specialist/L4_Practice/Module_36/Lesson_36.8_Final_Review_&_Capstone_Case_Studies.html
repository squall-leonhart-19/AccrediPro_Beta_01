2. The Art of the Discovery Call

The discovery call is not a sales pitch; it's a conversation of curiosity and connection. Your goal is to listen deeply, understand your prospect's pain points, and determine if you are the right fit to help them. This approach builds trust and positions you as an expert, not just a service provider.

A well-executed discovery call can convert up to 70% of qualified leads into paying clients, especially for high-ticket services like comprehensive wellness programs (HubSpot, 2023). It's where your empathy meets your expertise.

Coach's Insight from Bella Martinez

Remember, your ideal client, like Eleanor, isn't just buying a service; they're buying peace of mind and a solution to a deeply felt problem. Approach every call with genuine care. Your authenticity is your most powerful selling tool. When you truly listen, clients feel seen, and that connection is invaluable.

3. Structuring Your 30-Minute Call

A structured approach ensures you cover all essential points while maintaining a natural flow. Here‚Äôs a proven framework for your discovery calls:

Your 30-Minute Call Structure

1

Phase 1: Build Rapport (5 min)

Start by creating a warm, welcoming atmosphere. Ask about their day, how they found you, and acknowledge their effort in reaching out. This eases tension and opens the door for honest conversation.

2

Phase 2: Understand the Problem & Vision (10-12 min)

This is where you listen. Ask open-ended questions about their cat's health, their concerns, what they've tried, and what they hope to achieve. Dig deep into their emotional drivers. What would success look like? What are they afraid will happen if nothing changes?

3

Phase 3: Educate & Offer Your Solution (8-10 min)

Briefly explain your holistic approach and how it differs from what they've tried. Connect their pain points to your solution. Describe your program's benefits and transformation, not just features. This is where you introduce your specific Cat Wellness program.

4

Phase 4: Close & Next Steps (3-5 min)

Clearly state your pricing, answer any questions, and provide a clear call-to-action. Guide them on how to enroll or what the very next step is. Remember, you're guiding them to a solution.

4. Confident Pricing Presentation

Many practitioners hesitate when it comes to pricing, but your confidence is key. Remember, you are offering a high-value, transformative service. Your pricing reflects your expertise, the depth of your program, and the significant results you help clients achieve.

When presenting your fees, be direct and clear. State the investment amount, then immediately follow with the value and benefits the client will receive. Avoid hedging or apologizing for your prices.

5. Navigating Objections with Grace

Objections are not rejections; they are often requests for more information or reassurance. Approach them with empathy, curiosity, and a problem-solving mindset. Your role is to understand the root of the objection and address it effectively.

Common Objections & Confident Responses

1

"It's too expensive / I need to think about the investment."

"I completely understand, Eleanor. This is a significant investment in Luna's health and your peace of mind. Can I ask, what specifically about the investment gives you pause? Is it the total amount, or are you questioning if this program will truly deliver the results you're hoping for?" (Listen, then reiterate value, payment plans, or highlight the cost of inaction.)

2

"I've tried so many things already, and nothing has worked."

"That's a very valid concern, and I hear your frustration. Many of my clients come to me after trying multiple approaches. What makes my method different is our holistic, root-cause approach, combined with personalized implementation and ongoing support. We don't just treat symptoms; we uncover the underlying imbalances. This is a comprehensive partnership, not just another quick fix."

3

"I need to talk to my spouse/partner."

"Of course, that's an important conversation. To help you explain it to your partner, what specific questions do you think they might have about the program? Or, what aspects of Luna's wellness are most important for them to understand that we'd be addressing?" (Offer to send an email summarizing key points or even a brief follow-up call with both of them if appropriate.)

4

"I don't have enough time."

"I appreciate your honesty about your time constraints. My program is designed to integrate seamlessly into your life, with actionable steps that build over time. We start small, focusing on the highest impact changes first. Many clients find that by addressing their cat's health proactively, they actually save time and stress in the long run. How much time per day or week do you feel you could realistically dedicate to Luna's wellness plan?"

6. Sealing the Deal: Your Call-to-Action

After addressing questions and objections, it's time to guide your prospect to the next step. A clear, confident call-to-action (CTA) is crucial. Avoid ambiguity; tell them exactly what to do.

For Eleanor, if she seems ready, you might say:

  • "Eleanor, based on everything we've discussed, I truly believe my 12-week program is exactly what Luna needs to reduce her anxiety and improve her kidney support. I'm excited about the possibility of working with you both. Are you ready to move forward and get Luna started on her path to better health?"
  • "Wonderful! The next step is to complete the enrollment form and select your payment option. I can send you the link right now, and we can schedule our first in-depth session for next week. How does that sound?"

If she needs more time, you can offer:

  • "I understand you need to discuss this with your partner. How about I send you an email summarizing what we discussed, including the program details and investment, and we schedule a brief follow-up call for to answer any further questions?"
