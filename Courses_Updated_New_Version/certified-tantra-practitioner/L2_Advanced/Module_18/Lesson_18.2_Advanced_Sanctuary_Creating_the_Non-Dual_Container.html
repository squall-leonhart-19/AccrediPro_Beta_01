The Concept of the 'Void' in Sanctuary

In Tantric philosophy, the "Void" (Sunyata) is not emptiness, but the pregnant possibility from which all creation arises. In a session, the Void manifests as moments of profound silence, stillness, or "not-knowing."

Most practitioners feel the urge to "fill" the silence with a technique or a question. Advanced Sanctuary involves Creating space for the unexpressed. By resisting the urge to "do," you allow the client's own wisdom to emerge from the Void. This is a hallmark of the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path Methodâ„¢: we do not fix; we facilitate the return to wholeness.

Check Your Understanding

1. How does Level 2 Sanctuary differ from Level 1 in its primary focus?

The Void is a space of stillness and "not-knowing" where the practitioner resists the urge to fill the silence, allowing the client's subconscious wisdom and unexpressed emotions to emerge.

3. Why is the practitioner's self-regulation considered a "professional tool"?

Because of co-regulation and mirror neurons, the client's nervous system scans the practitioner's for safety. If the practitioner is regulated and steady (the "Mountain"), the client feels safe enough to enter deep catharsis or expansion.

4. What is "The Third Field"?

The Third Field is the shared energetic space created by the resonance between practitioner and client. It is a transpersonal container that exists beyond the individual egos of either person.
