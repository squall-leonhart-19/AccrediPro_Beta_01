Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Evelyn's low HRV (28ms) a critical factor in her "sexual shutdown"?

Low HRV indicates a lack of vagal tone and a dominant Sympathetic (fight/flight) or Dorsal (shutdown) state. Since the body prioritizes survival over reproduction, her nervous system has "turned off" the desire pathways to conserve energy and protect against perceived threat.

2. What is the "Double-Bind" in Evelyn's somatic presentation?

The Double-Bind is her cognitive desire for intimacy versus her body's somatic memory of medical trauma. Her mind says "yes," but her pelvic floor says "danger," leading to guarding, pain, and eventual dissociation (feeling like a ghost).

3. Which medication in Evelyn's profile is a known evidence-based inhibitor of sexual function?

Lexapro (an SSRI). Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors are clinically documented to cause "SSRI-Induced Sexual Dysfunction," including low libido and anorgasmia, by altering the dopamine/serotonin balance in the brain's reward centers.

4. What is the primary goal of Phase 1 in the clinical protocol?

Nervous system stabilization. Before any intimacy work can happen, we must move the client out of a high-alert state and increase her "vagal brake" capacity (HRV) so her body can tolerate the "thaw" of somatic sensations.

Key Takeaways for the Advanced Practitioner

  • Data Validates Experience: Using metrics like HRV and cortisol levels allows you to provide a "Clinical Mirror" to the client, reducing their shame through biological normalization.
  • Safety is Physiological: Intimacy cannot be forced in a Dorsal Vagal state. Stabilization must always precede activation.
  • Team-Based Care: High-end clinical Tantra requires a network of MDs, PTs, and therapists. Knowing your triggers for referral is a mark of professional maturity.
  • The 3-Phase Approach: Always move from the "Periphery" (nervous system) to the "Core" (pelvic/intimacy work) to avoid re-traumatization.
