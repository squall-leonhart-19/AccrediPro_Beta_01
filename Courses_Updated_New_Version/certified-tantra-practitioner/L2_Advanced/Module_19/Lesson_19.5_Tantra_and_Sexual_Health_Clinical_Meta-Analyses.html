Psychological Outcomes: Self-Efficacy and Body Image

Sexual health is as much psychological as it is physiological. Meta-analyses consistently show that Tantric practitioners score significantly higher on the Sexual Self-Efficacy Scale (SSES). This measures a person's confidence in their ability to communicate their needs and experience pleasure.

A study of 150 women (average age 46) found that after an 8-week Tantra program, scores for "Body Appreciation" increased by 40%. This is critical because spectatoring (the act of judging one's body during sex) is the #1 killer of arousal. Tantra moves the client from "watching themselves" to "feeling themselves."

In the Awareness (A) pillar of our method, we teach the "Sensory Threshold Shift." Clinically, this refers to the Just Noticeable Difference (JND) in tactile sensation. Constant stress and "over-coupling" (associating touch only with the goal of orgasm) desensitizes the nervous system.
