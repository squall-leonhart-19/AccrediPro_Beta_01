Referral Triggers & Scope of Practice

Knowing when to stop is as important as knowing how to start. In Lesson 8, we emphasize the Safety First Protocol. A 2023 survey of somatic practitioners found that 15% of clients present with undiagnosed medical conditions that manifest as "sexual blocks."

Red Flag: Unexplained Bleeding

If external or internal de-armoring triggers post-coital or non-menstrual bleeding, pause all somatic work and refer to a GYN immediately. This is outside our scope.

Red Flag: Psychotic Break / Severe Dissociation

If a client cannot "return" to the room after a session or exhibits disorganized speech, they require a trauma-informed psychologist. We facilitate, we do not diagnose mental illness.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the jaw (TMJ) considered a primary entry point for a client with a "frozen" pelvis?

Due to the fascial and embryological connection between the jaw and the pelvic floor (the "pelvic-throat axis"), tension in the jaw often mirrors tension in the pelvis. Releasing the jaw provides a "safe" way to begin releasing the pelvic floor without triggering the client's defensive responses.

2. What is the most likely cause of Elena's numbness based on her medication?

SSRI medications (like Lexapro) are known to cause "SSRI-induced sexual dysfunction," which includes genital numbness and delayed or absent peak. This is a pharmacological layer that requires a more patient, sensory-focused approach.

3. If a client exhibits "glazed eyes" and "loss of time" during a session, what state are they in?

They are experiencing dissociation, a form of Dorsal Vagal shutdown. The practitioner should immediately stop the deep work and use grounding techniques (e.g., having the client name 5 things they see in the room).

4. When should you refer a client to a Pelvic Floor Physical Therapist?

When there is significant medical hypertonicity, history of complex surgeries, or when the pain is localized and sharp rather than a general energetic block. A collaborative approach between a Tantra Practitioner and a Pelvic PT is the "Gold Standard" of care.
