Mastering the Trinity of Bandhas

Bandha translates to "lock" or "bind." In the context of sexual alchemy, these are physical contractions that have profound energetic consequences. They are the primary tools for the "E" (Expansion) phase of our methodology.

Bandha Physical Action Energetic Function Application in Alchemy
Mula Bandha (Root) Contraction of the perineum/pelvic floor. Prevents downward leakage (Apana) and redirects energy upward. Engaged during arousal to prevent premature ejaculation or "energy drop."
Uddiyana Bandha (Abdominal) Drawing the navel toward the spine and upward. The "pump" that moves energy from the solar plexus to the heart. Used in the "Big Draw" to vacuum energy up the spine.
Jalandhara Bandha (Throat) Tucking the chin slightly toward the chest. Prevents energy from flooding the brain too quickly; protects the heart. Engaged during high-intensity peaks to contain the "ecstatic charge."

The Maha Bandha (The Great Lock)

When all three locks are engaged simultaneously, usually during breath retention (Kumbhaka), it is known as Maha Bandha. In clinical practice, teaching a client to hold Maha Bandha for even 5-10 seconds during an energetic peak can trigger a full-body energetic orgasm that is non-ejaculatory and deeply restorative for the nervous system.

Mudras as Energetic Navigators

If Bandhas are the locks, Mudras are the switches. Hasta Mudras (hand gestures) utilize the high density of nerve endings in the fingertips to stimulate specific brain regions. In sexual alchemy, we use them to anchor the state of Devotion (Module 6) and Awareness (Module 2).

  • Jnana Mudra (Wisdom Gesture): Touching the tip of the index finger to the thumb. This creates a circuit that grounds the energy. Used when a client feels "over-amped" or anxious during expansion.
  • Yoni Mudra: Interlacing the fingers with index fingers and thumbs touching to form a triangle over the lower abdomen. This directs Prana specifically to the reproductive organs for healing and awakening.
  • Kechari Mudra (The King of Mudras): While technically a Mana (head) mudra, placing the tongue on the soft palate completes the Microcosmic Orbit. Without this "bridge," energy often gets stuck in the throat, leading to "pressure headaches" during intense Tantric practice.
Coach Tip: Physical vs. Subtle

Remind your clients: "Softness is power." A common mistake is clenching the muscles so hard that the energy cannot flow. The Bandha should be a firm but supple engagement, like the closing of a velvet curtain, not the slamming of a wooden door. If the body is too tense, the energy (Prana) cannot move through the Nadis.

The 'Big Draw': Upward Sublimation

The "Big Draw" is the signature technique of the Advanced Tantra Practitioner. It is the process of taking the raw, potent energy of arousal (Jing) and drawing it up the Sushumna Nadi (spinal channel) to be processed by the higher centers.

Step-by-Step Execution:

  1. Arousal Building: Use the Dyad Breath (Module 3) to build a high charge in the pelvic bowl.
  2. The Engagement: At the point of 8/10 intensity, exhale fully and engage Mula Bandha.
  3. The Vacuum: As you begin a slow, deep inhalation, engage Uddiyana Bandha. Visualize a golden liquid being vacuumed up the spine.
  4. The Lock: At the top of the breath, tuck the chin (Jalandhara Bandha) and press the tongue to the roof of the mouth (Kechari Mudra).
  5. The Circulation: Hold the breath (Kumbhaka) for 5-10 seconds, sensing the energy "blooming" in the Third Eye or Crown.
  6. The Release: Exhale slowly, releasing the locks from top to bottom, and feel the energy cascading down the front of the body (completing the orbit).
Coach Tip: Client Progression

Don't teach the "Big Draw" in the first session. This is a Level 2 technique. A client must first have a regulated nervous system (Module 9) and a clear "Release" phase (Module 4). If you try to pump energy through a "blocked" system, it can cause emotional overwhelm. Legitimacy comes from knowing when NOT to use a technique.
