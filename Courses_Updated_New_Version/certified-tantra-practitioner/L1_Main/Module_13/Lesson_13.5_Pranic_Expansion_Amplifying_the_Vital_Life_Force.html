Mastery of Bandhas: The Energetic Valves

Expansion without containment is dissipation. To amplify life force, we use Bandhas (Locks). In the Deep Dive of Module 13, we focus on how these locks act as hydraulic pumps for Prana.

1. Mula Bandha (Root Lock)

This is the contraction of the pelvic floor. It prevents the downward leak (Apana) of energy. For your female clients, this is not a Kegel; it is a subtle, energetic lifting of the cervix and perineum. It creates the "floor" for expansion.

2. Uddiyana Bandha (Abdominal Lock)

Performed on an exhale, this lock pulls the energy from the lower Dantians or Chakras up into the heart center. It is the primary engine of transmutation.

3. Jalandhara Bandha (Throat Lock)

This prevents energy from flooding the brain too quickly, which can cause dizziness or "ungroundedness." It acts as a pressure valve, ensuring the expansion is distributed evenly.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between 'Release' and 'Expansion' in the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path?

Release focuses on clearing blockages and "subtraction" of somatic armoring, while Expansion focuses on "multiplication" and the active amplification and upward circulation of Prana through the Sushumna Nadi.

2. Which Bandha acts as the primary "engine" for transmuting energy from the lower centers to the heart?

Uddiyana Bandha (the Abdominal Lock). When performed correctly, it creates a vacuum effect that pulls energy upward toward the Anahata (heart) center.

3. In the Microcosmic Orbit, which channel is considered 'Yang' and 'Ascending'?

The Du Mai (Governor Channel), which runs along the spine from the perineum to the crown.

4. What is a "Tantric Headache" usually a symptom of?

Energy stagnating in the head due to a lack of proper circulation (not completing the orbit) or over-application of expansion techniques without sufficient grounding or the use of Jalandhara Bandha.
