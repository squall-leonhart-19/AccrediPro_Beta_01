The Neurobiology of Mirror Neurons

Discovered in the 1990s by Giacomo Rizzolatti, mirror neurons are a class of cells in the brain that fire both when we perform an action and when we observe someone else performing that same action. In Tantra, mirror neurons allow us to "simulate" the client's internal experience within our own nervous system.

When your client‚Äôs breath hitches in a moment of release, your mirror neurons fire as if your own breath were hitching. This creates Somatic Empathy. However, as a professional, you must learn to observe this firing without being swept away by the emotion. This is the difference between feeling with someone and becoming their pain.

Distinguishing Projection vs. Reception

A common pitfall for new practitioners is projection‚Äîassuming the client is feeling what you are feeling. For example, if you are tired, you might project that the client is unmotivated.

  • Genuine Reception: Feels like a "download" of information. It is neutral, clear, and usually disappears as soon as the session ends.
  • Projection: Feels heavy, personal, and is often tied to your own past stories or current stressors.
