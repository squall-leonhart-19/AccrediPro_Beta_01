Opening the 'Back of the Heart'

Most people associate the heart chakra with the front of the chest. However, in advanced Tantric practice, the Back of the Heart (the space between the shoulder blades) is considered the "Inlet" or the "Receiver."

When practitioners only focus on the front of the heart, they often "over-give," leading to burnout. By consciously breathing into the back of the heart, you create a 360-degree field of awareness. This allows you to:

  • Receive without absorbing: You can feel the client's pain without taking it into your own body.
  • Remain Supported: Energetically, the back of the heart connects you to your lineage and the "universal support" of the practice.
  • Maintain Boundaries: It prevents the "leaning forward" energy that can feel invasive to a sensitive client.

Clearing the 'Heart Shield'

The "Heart Shield" is a somatic defense mechanism. It manifests as a physical tightening of the pectoral muscles, a rounding of the shoulders, and a shallowing of the breath in the upper chest. As a practitioner, you must first clear your own shield before you can assist a client in clearing theirs.

Somatic Markers of the Heart Shield:

  • The "Hollow" Chest: The client appears to be caving in on themselves (protection).
  • The "Puffed" Chest: The client appears overly rigid (aggression as defense).
  • Restricted Ribcage: Lack of lateral expansion during breathwork.

The Heart-to-Heart Hug: A Stabilization Ritual

This is one of the most powerful grounding tools in the Certified Tantra Practitioner‚Ñ¢ toolkit. It is often used at the beginning of a session to establish the container or at the end to integrate expansion.

Step-by-Step: The Heart-to-Heart Hug

  1. Alignment: Stand facing the client. Ask for consent.
  2. The Shift: Both step slightly to the left, so your hearts are directly aligned (chest to chest).
  3. The Contact: Wrap arms around each other. Ensure the contact is firm but not restrictive.
  4. The Breathing: Synchronize the breath. On the exhale, imagine your heart fields melting into one.
  5. The Duration: Hold for at least 3-5 full respiratory cycles until a "sigh" or "drop" is felt in both bodies.
Safety First

Never perform a Heart-to-Heart hug if the client is in a state of active "freeze" or "dissociation." Check their eyes first. If they are not "home," use the grounding techniques from Module 2 before attempting heart-centered contact.
