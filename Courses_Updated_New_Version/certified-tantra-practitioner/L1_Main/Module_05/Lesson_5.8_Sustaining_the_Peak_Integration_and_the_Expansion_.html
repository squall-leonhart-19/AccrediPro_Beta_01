Common Practitioner Mistakes

Avoid these common "rookie" errors when facilitating expansion sessions:

  • Over-Explaining: Using too many "scientific" or "spiritual" justifications during the practice. Let the experience speak for itself.
  • Rushing the Silence: Silence is where expansion actually happens. After a cue, wait at least 15-20 seconds.
  • Using "Try": Never say "Try to feel." Say "Notice if you can feel" or "Invite the feeling." The word "try" implies effort, which causes contraction.
  • Mismatched Energy: If your client is in a deep, expanded state, don't talk in a high-pitched, excited voice. Match their resonance.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Ah" sound recommended during the Expansion phase?

The "Ah" sound (vocalization) helps drop the client out of the analytical mind and into the body, breaking the habit of "holding" or contracting the throat and chest.

2. What should you do if a client says they "don't feel anything" during an expansion exercise?

Validate them immediately. Frame "numbness" or "not feeling" as a valid somatic experience. Ask where that numbness lives in the body to maintain presence without pressure.

3. What is the danger of using the word "Try" in your coaching cues?

The word "try" triggers the "efforting" mind, which creates physical and mental contraction. Using words like "invite" or "allow" encourages the parasympathetic nervous system to engage.

4. How long should you wait after giving a cue during the Expansion phase?

At least 15-20 seconds of silence. This allows the client to actually experience the sensation rather than just listening to your voice.
