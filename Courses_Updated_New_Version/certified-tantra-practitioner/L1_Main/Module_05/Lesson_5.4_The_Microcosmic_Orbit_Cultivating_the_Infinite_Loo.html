Step-by-Step Orbit Initiation

When teaching this to clients, use the "Somatic Visualization" technique. We don't just imagine the energy; we feel the micro-sensations of heat, tingling, or pressure along the path.

The 5-Stage Practice:

  1. The Foundation (Hui Yin): Sit upright. Bring your awareness to the perineum. On an inhale, gently contract the pelvic floor (Mula Bandha). Feel the "spark" of energy igniting at the base.
  2. The Ascent (The Spine): As you inhale, draw that spark up the tailbone, through the sacrum, and up the spine. Visualize it as a golden thread of light.
  3. The Crown (Bai Hui): Draw the energy over the top of the skull. Pause briefly at the crown. Feel the "expansion" here‚Äîthis is the peak of the Yang phase.
  4. The Descent (The Front): Place the tongue on the roof of the mouth. As you exhale, let the energy "spill over" like a waterfall down the face, throat, heart, and solar plexus.
  5. The Return (Lower Dantian): Let the energy pool in the belly (2 inches below the navel) before returning to the perineum to start the next loop.

Troubleshooting Common "Leaks"

As a practitioner, you must be able to spot where a client‚Äôs circuit is broken. If the energy isn't moving, the "Infinite Loop" becomes a "Stagnant Pool."

  • The Throat Block: If a client has "unspoken truths" or somatic armoring in the neck, energy gets stuck in the head. Solution: Use vocal toning during the descent.
  • The Heart Leak: Energy often dissipates outward toward others rather than continuing down the circuit. Solution: Focus on "Internalizing the Gaze" (Pratyahara).
  • The Perineum Leak: If the pelvic floor is too lax, energy "drops" out the bottom. Solution: Consistent, gentle Mula Bandha engagement.
