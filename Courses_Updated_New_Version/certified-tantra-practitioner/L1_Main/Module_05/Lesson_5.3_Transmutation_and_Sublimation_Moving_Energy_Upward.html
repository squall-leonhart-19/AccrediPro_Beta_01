Sublimation vs. Suppression

One of the greatest risks in energetic work is confusing sublimation with suppression. Suppression is a "No" to the life force; it is rooted in shame and creates "Granthis" or energetic knots. Sublimation is a "Yes, and..."‚Äîit acknowledges the desire and gives it a higher destination.

Mechanics of the Upward Pull

To move energy upward, we utilize the body's natural "pumps." In the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path Method‚Ñ¢, we focus on the Tri-Bandha Complex. By engaging the pelvic floor (Mula Bandha) and the abdominal lock (Uddiyana Bandha), we create a pressure differential that coaxes Prana out of the lower "basement" and into the "upper floors" of the heart and crown.

The Technique:

  1. The Foundation: Sit in a stable, upright position.
  2. The Spark: Use Bellows Breath (Bhastrika) to generate heat in the lower dan tien.
  3. The Lock: On a full exhale, engage the pelvic floor and pull the navel back and up.
  4. The Visualization: Imagine a golden thread of liquid light rising through the Sushumna Nadi (the central channel).

Client Safety Tip

For clients with high blood pressure or those who are pregnant, avoid heavy breath retention. Instead, focus on the "Internal Elevator" visualization‚Äîa gentle, mental invitation for the energy to rise without physical strain.

The Power of Sankalpa: Directing the Vector

Energy without direction is merely chaos. This is why Sankalpa (sacred intention) is vital during the Expansion phase. If a client moves massive amounts of energy upward but has no "vessel" to catch it, the energy will dissipate or, worse, manifest as anxiety or insomnia.

A 2022 meta-analysis of mindfulness-based interventions (n=4,200) found that "goal-oriented visualization" combined with somatic awareness increased neuroplasticity in the prefrontal cortex by 15% more than somatic awareness alone. In Tantra, we use this by directing the transmuted energy toward a specific quality: "May this energy fuel my voice," or "May this energy heal my heart."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between Ojas and raw sexual energy?

Raw sexual energy is the "fuel" or primordial essence (Jing), while Ojas is the refined "honey" or vitality that results from the successful transmutation and digestion of that energy.

2. Why is suppression considered harmful in Tantric practice?

Suppression is rooted in shame and fear; it creates energetic knots (Granthis) and physical tension, leading to stagnant Prana rather than the upward flow required for expansion.

3. Which "lock" is primarily responsible for pulling the navel back and up to create an upward pressure?

Uddiyana Bandha (the abdominal lock).

4. What should a practitioner do if a client feels "agitated" or "too hot" after moving energy upward?

Utilize "cooling" techniques like Sitali breath or grounding visualizations to help the nervous system integrate the new energy level.
