The Multi-Dimensional Intake Process

A premium Tantric intake goes far beyond a medical checklist. While we must collect health history, our primary goal is Somatic Mapping. We are looking for where energy is flowing and where the "Granthis" (blocks) reside.

Professional practitioners use a three-tier intake approach:

  • Physical History: Surgeries, injuries, chronic pain, and sexual health.
  • Emotional/Somatic History: Patterns of emotional suppression, past trauma (assessed for stability), and current stress levels.
  • Energetic Readiness: The client's ability to maintain presence (Awareness) and their current relationship with pleasure and expansion.
Coach Tip

During the intake, observe the client's breath patterns as they speak. If they hold their breath when discussing a specific topic, you have just found a somatic marker for a potential blockage. Note this in your assessment without interrupting their flow.

Managing the 'Healing Crisis'

In somatic work, a Healing Crisis (sometimes called a Herxheimer-like emotional reaction) occurs when the body releases stored tension faster than the conscious mind can process it. A 2021 study on somatic experiencing found that nearly 22% of participants experienced "temporary emotional intensification" during the release phase.

Your role as a practitioner is to normalize this. Symptoms of a healing crisis include:

  • Temporary fatigue or "emotional hangover"
  • Vivid dreaming or disrupted sleep patterns
  • Spontaneous crying or irritation without a clear "story"
  • Flu-like somatic sensations as the nervous system recalibrates
Coach Tip

Always provide "Integration Support" within 24-48 hours of a deep Release session. A simple check-in text or email can prevent the client's ego from "looping" into fear during a healing crisis.

Screening for Alignment and Resonance

Not every client who can pay is a client you should play with. Energetic Sovereignty requires you to screen for "Resonance." If you feel a "drain" or a "knot" in your stomach during the intake call, this is a somatic signal that the resonance is off.

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • The client seeks a "guru" to fix them rather than taking agency.
  • Boundaries are pushed during the very first interaction.
  • The client is seeking sexual gratification rather than energetic expansion.
  • A history of "practitioner hopping" without doing the integration work.
Coach Tip

Trust your interoception. If your body says "No," the answer is "No." Refer the client to another practitioner or a different modality. Your professional reputation depends on the quality of your containers, not the quantity of your clients.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a medical intake and a Tantric Somatic Mapping?

A medical intake focuses on pathology and history, while Somatic Mapping focuses on where energy is flowing or blocked (Granthis) and the client's current capacity for presence and pleasure.

2. When should a practitioner refer a client to a licensed therapist?

When the client presents with active, unstable PTSD, suicidal ideation, Bipolar I, Schizophrenia, or any condition that impairs their ability to remain grounded in consensus reality.

3. What percentage of clients might experience a "Healing Crisis" or emotional intensification?

Research suggests approximately 22% of clients engaging in deep somatic release work may experience a temporary intensification of symptoms as the nervous system recalibrates.

4. Why is a "Roadmap" preferred over single sessions in a professional practice?

Roadmaps provide a structured container for long-term transformation, ensure the client commits to the integration (Devotion) phase, and allow the practitioner to charge premium rates based on outcomes rather than time.
