Trauma-Informed Touch: Discomfort vs. Re-traumatization

As a Certified Tantra Practitionerâ„¢, you must master the "Edge." There is a difference between the discomfort of growth and the pain of violation.

  • Healthy Discomfort: The client feels "stretched," perhaps emotional, but remains present in their body. They can breathe through the sensation.
  • Re-traumatization: The client "leaves" their body (dissociation), stops breathing, or becomes hyper-vigilant. Their nervous system has moved from Release back into Defense.

Post-Release Stabilization: The Integration Protocol

A massive release without stabilization is like an electrical surge without a surge protector; it can "fry" the client's system, leading to a "healing crisis" where they feel exhausted or depressed the next day. Always leave 15 minutes at the end of a session for Integration.

The 3-Step Stabilization Protocol:

  1. Physical Grounding: Have the client press their hands and feet firmly into the floor or mat. Use weighted blankets if necessary.
  2. Hydration & Electrolytes: Somatic release is metabolically demanding. Provide water or tea.
  3. The "Container" Check: Ask the client, "On a scale of 1-10, how 'back' in the room are you?" Do not let them drive home until they are at least a 9.
