Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Ventral Vagal" state critical for a Tantra session?

The Ventral Vagal state is the "social engagement" part of the nervous system. Without it, the client remains in "fight or flight" (Sympathetic) or "freeze" (Dorsal Vagal), making energetic expansion and deep connection impossible.

2. What is the primary difference between a "soft no" and a "hard no"?

A "hard no" is a verbal boundary. A "soft no" is a non-verbal somatic cue (tightening muscles, holding breath, looking away) that indicates the client is reaching their limit, even if they haven't spoken yet.

3. How does "Prosody" affect the sanctuary?

Prosody is the rhythm and pitch of your voice. Warm, melodic, and calm prosody sends a direct signal of safety to the client's neuroception, allowing their "armoring" to relax.

4. Why is a post-session "Cord-Cutting" ritual necessary for the practitioner?

It maintains "Energetic Sovereignty." It prevents the practitioner from carrying the client's emotional "load" home, which is the leading cause of burnout in the healing arts.
