Common Practitioner Mistakes

The Mistake Why It Fails The Professional Pivot
Using too much Sanskrit jargon Alienates the client; sounds "cultish" to skeptics. Use modern terms like "nervous system," "presence," or "vitality."
Over-explaining the history Bores the client; feels like a history lecture. Keep it brief: "It's an ancient system for modern wholeness."
Becoming defensive Breaks the "holding space" and shows your own insecurity. Validate the concern: "That's a very common and fair question."
Focusing only on sexuality Reinforces the very myth the client is afraid of. Broaden the scope to "life force," "energy," and "emotional clarity."

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why is it important to use terms like "Somatic Psychology" when speaking to a skeptical professional client?

It provides a "logic bridge" that anchors the ancient practice in modern, credible terminology, helping the client feel safe and intellectually respected.

2. What is the "Professional Pivot" when a client asks if Tantra is just about sex?

Acknowledge the misconception as a Hollywood myth, then pivot to explaining Tantra as a system for "Evolution of Presence" and "Nervous System Regulation."

3. If a client is visibly nervous, what should your first priority be in the conversation?

Establishing safety and rapport. You do this through a calm tone, validating their concerns, and maintaining professional boundaries.

4. True or False: You should explain the entire 5,000-year history of Tantra in the first 10 minutes of a discovery call.

False. Keep historical context brief and focused only on how it serves the client's modern goals.
