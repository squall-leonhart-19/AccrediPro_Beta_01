Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "mechanical" practice and a "devotional" practice?

Mechanical practice focuses on technique and objective observation (Solar). Devotional practice focuses on the relationship, love, and receptivity toward the object of focus (Lunar).

2. Why is "over-explaining" a pitfall in Module 6 coaching?

Over-explaining keeps the client in their analytical mind (prefrontal cortex), which prevents them from dropping into the felt-sense of the heart and the emotional state of devotion.

3. What is a "warm honey" tone of voice?

It is a vocal quality that is slow, thick, rhythmic, and nourishing. It signals safety to the client's nervous system, allowing for deeper vulnerability.

4. If a client feels "vulnerable" when asked to love their breath, what should the coach do?

Validate the feeling immediately. Vulnerability is a sign that the "glass" between the observer and the experience is breaking, which is a necessary step toward devotion.
