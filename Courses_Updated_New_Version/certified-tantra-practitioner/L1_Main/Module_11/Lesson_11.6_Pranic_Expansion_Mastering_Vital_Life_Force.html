The Mechanics of Prana: Nadis and the Sushumna

In the Tantric tradition, the physical body is supported by a "subtle body" (Sukshma Sharira), a complex network of 72,000 energetic channels known as Nadis. As a practitioner, your goal is to help clients move energy from the peripheral nadis into the central channel, the Sushumna.

The three primary nadis you must understand are:

  • Ida Nadi: The lunar, cooling, feminine channel. It relates to the parasympathetic nervous system and the left nostril.
  • Pingala Nadi: The solar, warming, masculine channel. It relates to the sympathetic nervous system and the right nostril.
  • Sushumna Nadi: The central pillar. When Ida and Pingala are balanced, energy enters the Sushumna, leading to the state of Samadhi or expanded awareness.

The Microcosmic Orbit: Circulating the Infinite Loop

Stagnation is the enemy of expansion. If energy rises but does not circulate, it can cause "energy sickness" (headaches, irritability, or insomnia). The Microcosmic Orbit is a Taoist-Tantric synthesis that creates a continuous loop of life force.

The orbit consists of two main channels:

  1. The Governor Channel (Du Mai): Runs from the perineum, up the spine, over the head to the upper lip.
  2. The Conception Channel (Ren Mai): Runs from the tip of the tongue (touching the roof of the mouth), down the front of the body to the perineum.

By connecting the tongue to the palate, the practitioner "closes the circuit," allowing prana to flow in a self-sustaining loop. This circulation is what allows for the Expansion phase to last for extended periods without exhaustion.

Managing 'Energy Spikes': Safety in Ecstatic States

As a Certified Tantra Practitioner‚Ñ¢, you will eventually facilitate states where a client experiences an Energy Spike‚Äîa sudden, intense surge of prana that can manifest as shaking, heat, or emotional outbursts. This is often a sign of Kundalini activity.

Protocol for Energy Spikes:

  • Grounding (The Earth Anchor): Have the client place their bare hands and feet on the floor.
  • Exhalation: Encourage long, audible "Ssss" or "Haaa" exhales to vent excess heat.
  • Physical Touch: If consent is established, a firm hand on the sacrum or upper back can help "contain" the surge.
  • The "Cooling Breath" (Sitali): Curling the tongue and inhaling through it like a straw to cool the blood.
Safety First

Never "push" a client into an energy spike. True expansion is a gradual stretching of the nervous system's capacity. Think of it like upgrading the wiring in a house before installing a high-voltage appliance.
