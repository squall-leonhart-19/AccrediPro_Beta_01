Practice Exercise: The Out-Loud Lab

Professionalism is built through repetition. Follow these steps to anchor this skill.

  1. The Mirror Check: Stand in front of a mirror. Deliver the first line of the script. Notice your facial expression. Is it warm and inviting, or are you "thinking" too hard?
  2. The Voice Memo: Record yourself reading the final line: "No need to fix them. Just let them know you're listening." Play it back. Does your voice sound rushed? Aim for a "bass" toneâ€”deeper and slower than your normal speaking voice.
  3. The "Silence" Challenge: Set a timer for 30 seconds. Sit in silence, imagining Diane is across from you. Get comfortable with the quiet. In Tantra coaching, the breakthrough happens in the silence after the question.

Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you begin your practice, watch out for these common "new coach" pitfalls.

The Pitfall Why It Happens The Tantric Shift
The "Fix-It" Reflex We want the client to feel better immediately. Allow the discomfort to exist. Awareness is the cure.
Filling the Silence Silence feels awkward or like "dead air." Silence is where the client's body finally speaks.
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome makes us want to prove we know "science." Use 50% fewer words. Let the experience lead.
Leading the Witness Asking "Do you feel relaxed?" instead of "What do you feel?" Keep questions open-ended and sensation-based.

How do you know the session went well?

  • Physiological Shift: The client takes a spontaneous deep sigh or their shoulders drop.
  • Slower Speech: The client begins to speak more slowly as they "check in" with their body before answering.
  • Specific Sensations: The client moves from vague words ("I'm stressed") to specific somatic markers ("My stomach feels like a tight knot").

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to ask for the client's permission before an awareness check-in?

It establishes somatic consent and safety. For a client like Diane who lives in her head, a sudden shift to the body can feel invasive. Permission-giving puts her in the driver's seat.

2. What is the primary role of "The Witness" in a coaching session?

To observe sensations, thoughts, and emotions without judgment or the need to change them. This creates the "neutral space" necessary for transformation.

3. If a client says, "I don't feel anything, I'm just numb," what is the best practitioner response?

Validate the numbness as a sensation itself. "Numbness is a very clear signal from the body. Let's just witness the numbness together. Where in your body do you feel the 'nothingness' most clearly?"

4. How does slowing down your own speech affect the client?

Through co-regulation. When the coach's nervous system is slow and grounded, the client's mirror neurons pick up on that safety, allowing their own system to down-shift from "fight or flight" into "awareness."

Key Takeaways

  • Presence over Performance: Your ability to stay present with the client's "numbness" is more valuable than any advice you could give.
  • Somatic Language: Shift the conversation from "Why do you feel this?" (analytical) to "Where do you feel this?" (somatic).
  • The Power of Silence: Silence is a professional tool. Use it to allow the client's internal awareness to surface.
  • Consent-Based Coaching: Always ask permission before moving into deeper body-centered work.
