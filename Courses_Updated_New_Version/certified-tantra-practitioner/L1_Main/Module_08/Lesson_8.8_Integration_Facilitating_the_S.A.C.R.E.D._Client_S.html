The Coaching Script

This script focuses on the Somatic Bridge. Your goal is to gently interrupt her "storytelling" and invite her into her "feeling." Practice these words out loud to get the rhythm in your body.

Script: The Somatic Bridge

ELENA:
"I just don't understand why I can't relax. I've read all the books, I do the yoga, but when I'm with my husband, I'm just thinking about the school budget or the grocery list. It's like I'm a machine."
YOU:
"Elena, I hear how frustrating that 'machine' feeling is. I want to pause the story for just a second. Would it be okay if we took a breath together and just noticed what is happening in your body right now as you talk about being a machine?"
ELENA:
"Oh... okay. I guess I feel... tight? In my shoulders?"
YOU:
"Beautiful noticing. Just stay with that tightness in the shoulders. Give it permission to be there. You don't need to change it. If that tightness had a color or a temperature, what would it be?"

"It feels... grey. And cold. Like a piece of lead."
YOU:
"Grey, cold lead. Thank you for that. Letâ€™s just breathe into the edges of that 'lead' for three breaths. We aren't trying to melt it; we're just acknowledging it's there."
Practitioner Tip

Notice how the script doesn't ask "Why do you feel tight?" Asking "Why" sends the client back into their head. Asking "What color/temperature is it?" keeps them in the sensory experience.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

As you begin your practice, watch out for these "Head-Space" traps that new coaches often fall into:

  • The "Fixer" Trap: Trying to make the client's "cold lead" feeling go away immediately. In Tantra, we sit with what is.
  • The "Teacher" Trap: Giving a 10-minute lecture on the Vagus nerve instead of staying in the feeling.
  • The "Fear of Silence" Trap: Filling the quiet moments with more questions. Silence is where the body speaks.
  • The "Approval" Trap: Nodding too much or saying "Good" after everything they say. Use neutral validation like "I hear you" or "Thank you for sharing that."

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask the client for the "color or temperature" of a sensation?

It engages the sensory-motor cortex and keeps the client in their "felt sense" (the body) rather than the "analytical mind" (the head).

2. What is the "Low and Slow" technique?

It refers to using a lower vocal register and a slower speaking pace to help co-regulate the client's nervous system into a state of safety.

3. If Elena starts crying when she feels the "cold lead" in her shoulders, what is your first move?

First, breathe. Then, offer a supportive, neutral statement like, "I'm right here with you. That's a lot of lead to carry. Just let the breath move through it." Do not rush to hand her a tissue or stop the crying.

4. What is the "Permission-Lead"?

It is asking for consent before shifting the focus (e.g., "Would it be okay if we paused for a breath?"). It builds trust and ensures the client feels in control of the process.

Key Takeaways

  • Somatic Inquiry is the bridge from the "story" to the "soul."
  • Your regulated nervous system is your primary coaching tool.
  • Avoid "Why" questions; focus on "What" and "How" regarding physical sensations.
  • Success is measured by physiological shifts (sighs, softening, slowing down).
  • Professionalism in Tantra is rooted in constant consent and permission.
