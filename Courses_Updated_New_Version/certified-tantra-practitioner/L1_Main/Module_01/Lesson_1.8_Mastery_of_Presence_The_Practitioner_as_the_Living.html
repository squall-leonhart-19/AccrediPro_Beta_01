Common Beginner Mistakes

We all make them! Here is what to avoid as you build your practice:

  • Over-explaining: You don't need to explain the 5,000-year history of Tantra. Elena just needs to know she is safe now.
  • Apologizing for boundaries: Never say, "I'm sorry, but I have to tell you this is non-sexual." Say it as a confident promise of safety.
  • Ignoring the room: If the room is messy or the lighting is harsh, the "Sanctuary" is broken before you speak.
  • Rushing the Intake: New coaches often rush to the "exercises." The intake is the work.
