Environmental Psychology & Neuroception

The term Neuroception, coined by Dr. Stephen Porges, describes how our neural circuits distinguish whether situations or people are safe, dangerous, or life-threatening. Before a client ever engages in breathwork or touch, their nervous system is scanning your room.

In Tantric practice, we aim to trigger a deep Parasympathetic Response. If the room is cluttered, the lighting is harsh (fluorescent), or the temperature is slightly too cold, the client‚Äôs nervous system remains in a state of "low-grade vigilance." This vigilance prevents the "dropping in" required for energetic expansion.

Coach Tip: The 5-Minute Scan

Before every client arrives, lie down on your treatment mat or cushions exactly where the client will be. Look at the ceiling. Is there dust on the fan? Is there a harsh bulb visible? What do you hear? If you find a distraction, your client certainly will.
