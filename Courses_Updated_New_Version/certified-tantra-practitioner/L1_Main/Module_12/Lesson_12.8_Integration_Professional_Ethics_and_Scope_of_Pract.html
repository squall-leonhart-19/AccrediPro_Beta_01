The Coaching Script

In Tantra coaching, we use Directive Presence. This means we don't just ask "How are you?"; we guide the client's attention. Practice the following script out loud to build your muscle memory.

Embodiment Opening Script

YOU:

"Elena, I'm so glad you're here. Before we talk about your week, I'd like to invite us both to arrive fully in this space. Would you be willing to close your eyes for a moment and just notice the weight of your body against the chair?"

ELENA:

"Sure. It's just... I have so much on my mind today. I feel like I should be telling you about my retirement plan."

YOU:

"I hear that busy mind, and it's welcome here. But for the next hour, your mind doesn't have to 'manage' anything. Let's try something: as you breathe, can you feel the air touching the back of your throat? Just that one sensation."

ELENA:

(Takes a breath) "Oh... I actually haven't noticed my breath all day. It feels a bit tight."

YOU:

"Beautiful. 'Tight' is a perfect place to start. Don't try to change it. Just breathe into that tightness and let it know you're listening. In Tantra, we don't fix sensations; we befriend them."

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

The Pitfall Why it Fails The Tantric Correction
Over-Explaining Keeps the client in their head (intellectualizing). Use fewer words. Direct them to a sensation.
"Fixing" the Numbness Creates a goal-oriented pressure that blocks flow. Invite them to "be with" the numbness as a valid state.
Rushing the Silence Silence is where the body speaks. Wait 5-10 seconds after a client speaks before responding.
Clinical Distance Prevents the energetic resonance required for Tantra. Model the embodiment you want them to feel. Breathe with them.

Success Markers

How do you know if your practice session (or real session) was successful? Look for these Non-Verbal Cues:

  • Spontaneous Sighing: The client's nervous system is shifting from Sympathetic (fight/flight) to Parasympathetic (rest/digest).
  • Softening of the Face: Especially around the jaw and brow.
  • Lower Pitch in Voice: As the client drops into their body, their voice often becomes deeper and more resonant.
  • Increased "I feel" vs "I think" statements: A shift in linguistic focus toward somatic reality.

Check Your Understanding

1. If Elena says, "I think I'm just frustrated because retirement isn't what I thought," what is the best Tantric coaching response?

Instead of asking "Why?", say: "I hear that frustration. Where do you feel that frustration in your body right now?" This pivots her from the story to the sensation.

2. True or False: A Tantric coach should always try to make the client feel "happy" or "blissful" by the end of the session.

False. The goal of Tantra is wholeness and presence, not just "positive" emotions. Being present with numbness or sadness is a massive success in embodiment work.

3. What is a "Sensory Pivot"?

A sensory pivot is a verbal technique used to interrupt a client's intellectual narrative and redirect their attention to a current physical sensation or breath pattern.

4. Why is "modeling embodiment" important for the coach?

Because of mirror neurons and energetic resonance. If you are breathing deeply and present in your body, the client's nervous system will naturally begin to regulate and follow your lead (co-regulation).

Key Takeaways

  • Presence Over Process: Your ability to stay embodied is more important than following a perfect script.
  • The Body is the Map: Always redirect the client from their "story" to their "sensations."
  • Safety First: Use breathing and grounding to ensure the client feels safe enough to open up.
  • Practice Out Loud: Verbalizing these scripts is essential for developing your professional "Coach Voice."
