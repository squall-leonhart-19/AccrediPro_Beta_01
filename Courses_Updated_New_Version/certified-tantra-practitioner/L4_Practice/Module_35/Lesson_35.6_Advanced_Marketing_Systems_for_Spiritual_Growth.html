The Metrics of Devotion: CAC and LTV

As a Practitioner-CEO, you must move from "hoping" to "knowing." This requires tracking two critical metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and Lifetime Value (LTV).

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): This is the total cost of your marketing and sales efforts divided by the number of new clients. If you spend $500 on Facebook ads and get 5 clients, your CAC is $100.

Lifetime Value (LTV): This is the total revenue you expect from a single client over the duration of your relationship. If a client does a $2,000 program, then joins a $500/month continuity group for a year, their LTV is $8,000.

The Golden Ratio: For a healthy, scaling business, your LTV should be at least 3x your CAC. In premium Tantra practices, we often see ratios of 10:1 or higher because the "Devotion" pillar creates extreme client loyalty and referrals.
