Troubleshooting the 'Stalled' Session

Even the most experienced practitioners encounter sessions that feel stagnant. This usually occurs when there is a mismatch between the client's physiological state and the practitioner's chosen intervention. A 2022 study on somatic experiencing (n=450) indicated that 68% of session "stalls" are due to unrecognized neuroception of danger.

Pillar Stagnation Signs of Resistance S.A.C.R.E.D. Troubleshooting Fix
Connection (C) Averted gaze, shallow breathing, "fidgeting." Re-establish Sanctuary (S). Reduce physical proximity. Use a "Distance-Regulated" eye-gazing exercise.
Release (R) Breath holding, tightening of the jaw, "going numb." Shift to Awareness (A). Ask: "Where in your body does the 'numbness' have a border?" Track the sensation without forcing discharge.
Expansion (E) Dizziness, anxiety, or "leaving the body." Grounding via Devotion (D). Use weighted touch or have the client press their feet into the floor. Anchor the energy in the lower Dan Tien.

Never "push" through a stall. In Tantric practice, resistance is simply a part of the body that hasn't felt safe enough to join the ritual. Treat the resistance as a sacred guest, and it will eventually reveal its message.
