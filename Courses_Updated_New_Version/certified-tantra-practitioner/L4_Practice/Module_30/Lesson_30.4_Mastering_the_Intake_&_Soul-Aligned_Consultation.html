The 'Connection' Phase: Screening for Readiness

In the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path Method‚Ñ¢, Connection begins long before the first session. The intake process is a filter designed to protect both the practitioner and the client. A 2022 survey of wellness practitioners (n=1,200) found that 84% of practice-related stress stemmed from accepting clients who were not a fit for the practitioner‚Äôs scope of practice.

Screening must cover three primary domains:

  • Psychological Readiness: Is the client seeking a "quick fix" for deep-seated trauma, or are they ready for the slow, embodied work of Tantra?
  • Trauma History: While we work somatically with the body, clients with unintegrated, acute PTSD may require a clinical therapist alongside or prior to Tantric work.
  • Physical Contraindications: Certain breathwork or energetic techniques may be contraindicated for specific heart conditions or late-stage pregnancies.
Coach Tip

Don't be afraid to say no. A "Soul-Aligned" consultation means being brave enough to tell a prospect: "I don't think I'm the right guide for you at this stage of your journey." This builds incredible professional integrity and actually attracts more of the right clients.

The Somatic Discovery Call

A discovery call is usually a 20-30 minute conversation. While most business coaches focus on "closing the sale," the Tantra Practitioner focuses on somatic resonance. You are listening not just to their words, but to the "music" behind the words.

Practice Somatic Listening: As the prospect speaks, track your own nervous system. Do you feel a constriction in your chest? A sense of expansion? A feeling of being "pushed"? These are somatic data points indicating the energetic match.

Establishing the 'Sacred Contract'

The Sacred Contract is the bridge between the mundane and the divine. It is a document (and a verbal agreement) that outlines the Sanctuary of the practice. Every premium certification requires a clear agreement that covers:

Contract Element Purpose Example Language
Boundaries Ensures a safe, non-sexual professional container. "This is a professional somatic practice; sexual contact is strictly prohibited."
Participation Places the power of the result back on the client. "Transformation is a co-creative process requiring your presence and sadhana."
Cancellation Protects the practitioner's time and income. "24-hour notice is required for all session changes."
Scope of Practice Defines the limits of Tantric coaching. "I am a Tantra Practitioner, not a licensed medical doctor or psychotherapist."
Coach Tip

Include a "Financial Devotion" section in your contract. Framing payment as an act of self-investment rather than a "bill" helps the client shift into a mindset of expansion from the very beginning.

Techniques for 'Awareness': Articulating Desires

Many clients come to a Tantra Practitioner because they feel "stuck," but they often can't name why. During the intake, your job is to use the Awareness pillar to help them excavate their true desires. Studies in motivational interviewing show that when a client articulates their own "why," they are 3x more likely to complete a long-term program.

The "Golden Thread" Inquiry:

  1. "If we were to meet 6 months from now and you were living your most expanded life, what would your body feel like?"
  2. "What is the fear that currently keeps you from that expansion?"
  3. "What have you tried before that didn't work, and why do you feel called to a somatic approach now?"

Managing Red Flags & Knowing When to Refer

As a professional, your greatest asset is knowing where your expertise ends. A "Soul-Aligned" match means the client's needs fall within your Sanctuary. If a client presents with clinical issues, a referral is the most ethical and professional action you can take.

Coach Tip

Build a "Referral Network" of at least three local or virtual therapists and one functional medicine doctor. Being able to say, "I have a colleague who specializes in exactly what you're experiencing," makes you look like a high-level professional, not an amateur.
