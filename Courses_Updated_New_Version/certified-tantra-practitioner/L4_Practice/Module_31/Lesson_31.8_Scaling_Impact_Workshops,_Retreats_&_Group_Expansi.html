The 30-Minute Enrollment Script

A 2023 study on wellness consultations (n=1,200) found that practitioners who spent at least 15 minutes listening before offering a solution had a 68% higher enrollment rate than those who "pitched" within the first 5 minutes.

Phase 1: Setting the Container 5 min

YOU:
"Sarah, I am so glad we connected. Before we start, I want to create a sacred space for us. Take a deep breath with me. My goal today is to see if I can truly help you. If I can, I'll tell you how. If not, I'll point you toward someone who can. Does that sound good?"

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (Listening) 12 min

YOU:
"You mentioned feeling 'disconnected' in your body. What does that actually look like on a Tuesday afternoon? How does it affect your confidence?"
YOU:
"And Sarah, if we don't address this‚Äîif you stay in this 'frozen' state for another year‚Äîwhat is the cost to your spirit?"

Phase 3: The Bridge (Your Solution) 8 min

YOU:
"Sarah, what you're describing isn't a lack of desire; it's a protective layer your body built. In my 12-week Radiant Awakening program, we don't just talk. We use somatic breathwork and Tantric embodiment to gently melt that layer. We move from the head to the heart."

Phase 4: The Invitation (The Ask) 5 min

YOU:
"Based on everything you told me, you are a perfect fit for this work. I would love to guide you. The investment for the 3-month journey is $3,500. How does that feel in your body?"
Luna's Insight

Always ask "How does that feel in your body?" after stating the price. It keeps the conversation Tantric and somatic, rather than just a logical "math" problem.

Handling Sacred Objections

An objection is rarely about the money. It is usually a fear of failure or a fear of the power of the work. As a Tantra Practitioner, you must hold the space for their fear without taking it on.

The Objection The Fear Behind It Your Professional Response
"I can't afford it right now." Lack of perceived value or safety. "I understand. If money weren't the issue, is this the work your soul is calling for?"
"I need to talk to my husband." Seeking permission to prioritize herself. "I support that. But first, Sarah, do YOU want this? It‚Äôs hard to get support for something you aren't 100% behind yet."
"I'm not sure if this will work for me." Self-doubt/Imposter syndrome. "That's the 'frozen' part speaking. We go at the pace of your nervous system. You can't do this wrong."
Luna's Insight

If they say "I'll think about it," set a firm follow-up time. "Great, I'll check in Thursday at 10 AM. Will you have your 'yes' or 'no' by then?" Never leave a call without a clear next step.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the "listening" phase in the discovery call?

To identify the "cost of inaction" and the prospect's root desire, which builds the necessary tension and trust for them to commit to the transformation.

2. If a prospect says "I need to talk to my partner," how should you respond according to the Tantric method?

First, validate their desire to communicate, then check if THEY are fully committed. If they aren't a "Hell Yes" themselves, their partner will likely feel their hesitation and say no.

3. True or False: You should wait until the very end of the call to mention the price.

True. You must build the value and the "Bridge" (Solution) first. Pricing without context feels like a cost; pricing after a solution feels like an investment.

4. Why is asking "How does that feel in your body?" after stating the price effective?

It bypasses the logical/fear-based brain and brings the client back into their somatic experience, which is where the true decision to heal is made.

Key Takeaways

  • Enrollment is Healing: The call itself should be the client's first experience of being truly seen and heard.
  • Hold the Container: Be the authority. Use a structured script so you don't get lost in "chatting."
  • Value over Price: Always anchor the price to the transformation (e.g., feeling alive again) rather than your hourly rate.
  • Professional Follow-up: 80% of sales happen in the follow-up. Don't let a "maybe" disappear into the void.
  • Know Your Numbers: Aim for a 25-30% conversion rate as a beginner, moving toward 50% as you gain mastery.
