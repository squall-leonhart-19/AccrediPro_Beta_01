The Discovery Call: From Awareness to Devotion

In the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path, the discovery call is the bridge. The client arrives with Awareness (they know they have a block) and leaves with Devotion (they are committed to the work). This is not about convincing; it is about Resonance.

A sacred enrollment call follows a specific energetic arc:

  • Establish Sanctuary: Begin with 60 seconds of synchronized breathing. This moves the client out of their "logical/skeptical" mind and into their "somatic/feeling" body.
  • Deep Inquiry: Ask questions that mirror the Awareness pillar. "Where in your body do you feel the most restricted?" rather than "What are your goals?"
  • The Mirroring: Reflect back their words. When a client hears their own somatic truth spoken back to them with reverence, the Connection is forged.
Coach Tip

üí° Track the Breath: During the call, watch the client's chest or listen to their breathing rhythm. If they hold their breath when you mention the price or the commitment, they are experiencing "Somatic Armoring." Address it immediately: "I notice your breath caught just then. Let's breathe into that sensation together before we continue." This builds instant trust.

Pricing for Expansion: The Life-Force Exchange

Many practitioners, especially women in their 40s and 50s who have spent years in service-based roles (nursing, teaching), struggle with high-ticket pricing. In Tantra, money is simply Lakshmi energy‚Äîa flow of abundance that mirrors the Expansion you facilitate.

A 2023 industry report found that clients who invest $3,000+ in wellness programs show a 64% higher rate of long-term habit integration compared to those paying session-by-session.

Coach Tip

Navigating Resistance: The Alchemy of Release

When a client says "It's too expensive" or "I need to talk to my husband," they are rarely talking about money. They are experiencing the Release phase of the S.A.C.R.E.D. Path‚Äîthe ego's resistance to change.

Common Resistances & Re-frames:

  • The Money Objection: Re-frame as "Investment in Sovereignty." Ask: "If money were not the obstacle, would your soul say yes to this work?"
  • The Time Objection: Re-frame as "Prioritizing Devotion." Ask: "What is the cost to your life-force if you stay in this current state for another year?"
  • The Partner Objection: Re-frame as "Individual Sovereignty." Ask: "How would your relationship expand if you showed up as your most embodied, ecstatic self?"
Coach Tip

üí° Silence is Sanctuary: After you state your price, do not speak. Wait for the client to respond. This silence is a "Release" container. If you speak first, you are rescuing them from their own growth process. Let them sit in the expansion of the "Yes."

Onboarding as Ritual: Establishing the Container

The moment the client pays, the Sanctuary must be established. This is the "Onboarding Ritual." It should feel like the entrance to a temple, not a digital transaction.

The L4 Onboarding Checklist:

  1. The Welcome Invocation: A personalized video or voice note welcoming them into the sacred container.
  2. The Intake Ceremony: A detailed questionnaire that asks about their lineage, their desires, and their "shadow" (Release).
  3. The First Sadhana: Give them a simple breathwork or "Awareness" practice to begin immediately. This anchors the commitment.
  4. Clear Boundaries: Establishing communication hours and "Sanctuary" rules (e.g., no sessions during menstruation, or specific preparation rituals).
Coach Tip

üí° The "Sacred Welcome" Gift: Many top-tier practitioners send a physical "Sanctuary Box" (incense, a specific crystal, a journal). This tactile experience bridges the digital marketing world with the physical embodiment of Tantra.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is synchronized breathing recommended at the start of an enrollment call?

It moves the client out of the analytical/logical mind and into the somatic/feeling body, establishing "Sanctuary" and allowing them to experience "Awareness" of their true desires rather than their ego-based fears.

2. What is the "Expansion" test for pricing?

It is a somatic check where the practitioner says the price out loud to see if it feels "contracted" (too low/burdensome) or "expansive" (aligned with the value of the transformation). The price must be a frequency the practitioner can hold with confidence.

3. How should a "Money Objection" be handled in the S.A.C.R.E.D. framework?

By identifying it as a "Release" phase resistance. The practitioner should ask if the soul says "Yes" regardless of money, helping the client distinguish between a genuine financial impossibility and a fear-based somatic contraction.

4. What is the primary purpose of the "Onboarding Ritual"?

To establish the "Sanctuary" pillar immediately after the investment. It anchors the client's commitment (Devotion) and ensures they feel held in a high-integrity container before the first session even begins.
