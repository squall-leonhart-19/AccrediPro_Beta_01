Handling Group Objections

Objection 1: "I'm not sure I want to share my secrets with strangers."
Response: "I completely respect that. In our workshops, 'sharing' is always optional. We focus on your internal experience. You can participate fully without ever having to disclose anything you aren't comfortable with."

Objection 2: "The price seems high for a 3-hour event."
Response: "I understand. I view this not as a 'class,' but as a professional intervention. You're getting the distilled tools I've used to help dozens of women move from numbness to vitality. Most clients find the breakthrough they get in these 3 hours saves them months of traditional talk therapy."

Luna's Insight

Never apologize for your price. When you drop your price, you drop the perceived energetic value of the transformation. If they can't afford it, offer a payment plan, but keep the total price firm.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the most effective way to handle a prospect's fear of group vulnerability?

Validate their feeling as normal, explain the professional "container" and safety protocols, and emphasize that sharing is always optional.

2. Why is a "Ladder of Services" important for a new practitioner?

It allows prospects to "sample" your work at a lower price point ($197) before committing to high-ticket transformations ($1,500+), increasing overall conversion rates.

3. If you host a workshop with 12 people at $147, what is your gross revenue for those 3 hours?

$1,764. This demonstrates the high hourly leverage of the group model compared to 1-on-1 sessions.

4. What is the "Invitation" phase of the sales call?

The final stage where you clearly state the price and ask the prospect if they would like to claim one of the limited remaining spots.

Key Takeaways

  • Groups provide leverage: You can earn more in 3 hours of a workshop than in 10 hours of private sessions.
  • Safety first: For women 40-55, emphasize the professional, high-integrity nature of your group container.
  • Price with confidence: Your certification and life experience make you a premium provider; do not discount your value.
  • Follow the script: Use a structured 30-minute call to lead the prospect from their "pain" to your "solution."
