Handling Objections with Confidence

Objections are rarely about money; they are usually about fear of failure. Use these "Turn-Around" responses.

The Objection The "Tantric" Reframe Your Professional Response
"It's too expensive." Value vs. Cost "I understand. If we look at the cost of staying where you are‚Äîthe stress, the disconnection‚Äîwhat is that costing you? This is an investment in the rest of your life."
"I need to ask my partner." Self-Sovereignty "I support that. Is there anything I can provide to help them understand the professional nature of this work? Or is there a part of you that feels hesitant to claim this for yourself?"
"I'm not sure I'm ready." Resistance Check "The mind is never ready for transformation, Elena. The body, however, usually is. What is your body telling you right now as we speak?"
Coach Tip

Notice how we don't argue? We ask questions. A 2022 study on sales psychology showed that practitioners who asked 15-18 questions per call had a 40% higher closing rate than those who "pitched" more.

Confident Pricing Presentation

Imposter syndrome often shows up as "the stutter" when stating your price. Practice these lines out loud until they feel like a fact, not a request.

"The program is $5,000." (Stop talking. Do not say "if that's okay" or "I know it's a lot.")

The "Anchor" Technique: If you are nervous about your price, mention a higher-tier option first. "My private 6-month immersion is $10,000, but for what you need right now, the 12-week foundation at $3,500 is actually the better fit."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of Phase 2 (Deep Discovery) in the enrollment call?

The goal is to move the prospect from their "head" (logic) to their "heart/body" (desire) by identifying the emotional cost of their current situation and the vision of their future.

2. If a client says "I need to think about it," what is the best professional response?

Ask a clarifying question: "I respect that. Usually, when someone needs to think about it, it‚Äôs about the money, the time, or a fear that it won't work for them. Which one is it for you?" This allows you to address the real concern.

3. How many active clients would you need to manage to earn $10,000/month if your 12-week program is $3,000?

You would need to enroll 3.33 (effectively 4) new clients per month. Over a 12-week (3 month) period, you would be managing approximately 10-12 active clients at once.

4. Why is "silence" considered a powerful tool after stating your price?

Silence demonstrates confidence and high status. It allows the prospect to process the investment and prevents the practitioner from "talking themselves out of the sale" by offering premature discounts.
