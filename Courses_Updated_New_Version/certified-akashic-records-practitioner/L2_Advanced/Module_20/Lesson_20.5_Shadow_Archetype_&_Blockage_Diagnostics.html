The Gatekeeper Archetypes

The "Gatekeeper" is an energetic construct of the ego designed to protect the client from information it perceives as threatening to their current identity. In the Akasha, these manifest as specific archetypal behaviors that "muddy the water" during a session.

Archetype Behavioral Symptom Akashic Blockage Style
The Skeptic Constant questioning of the practitioner's validity. Mental static; prevents the "Accessing" phase from deepening.
The Martyr Focusing entirely on others' needs; "Why can't I help them?" Deflection; prevents the Records from showing the client's own soul path.
The Perfectionist Anxiety about "doing the Records right" or getting the "right" answer. Performance pressure; shuts down the intuitive flow.
The Silent Guardian Emotional numbness; "I don't feel anything." The "Wall of Glass"; allows seeing but prevents feeling or integration.
Coach Tip: Identifying the Gatekeeper

When you encounter a Gatekeeper, do not fight it. Instead, name it. Say: "I am sensing a very strong Guardian energy here that wants to keep you safe from the intensity of this truth. Can we ask the Records why this Guardian feels it's necessary to stand watch today?" This bypasses the ego by honoring its protective intent.

The Psychology of Secondary Gain

One of the most difficult blocks to diagnose is Secondary Gain. This occurs when the client‚Äôs soul or ego receives a "benefit" from staying sick, broke, or lonely. As a practitioner charging premium rates, your value lies in identifying these hidden payoffs that the client cannot see for themselves.

Common Secondary Gains in the Akasha:

  • Protection from Responsibility: If I am "broken," I don't have to step into my full power (which feels scary).
  • Relatability: If I stay at the same vibration as my family/friends, I won't be abandoned.
  • Validation: Suffering provides a constant stream of sympathy and attention from others.
  • Safety in Smallness: Staying hidden prevents the "predatory" attention the soul might have experienced in past lives (Karmic Loop).
Coach Tip: The Secondary Gain Inquiry

Ask the Records: "What is the hidden safety net that this struggle provides for [Client Name]?" The answer is often surprising and provides the key to the entire healing transmutation phase.

The Mirror Assessment

The Mirror Assessment uses the Akasha to see how the client‚Äôs external conflicts reflect internal disharmony. This is based on the Hermetic principle of "As Above, So Below; As Within, So Without."

In a professional diagnostic session, you will look at the client's three main "Mirrors":

  1. The Relationship Mirror: What are the recurring complaints about partners? (Reflects Soul Contracts).
  2. The Financial Mirror: What is the "flow" of money? (Reflects Self-Worth and Abundance Alignment).
  3. The Somatic Mirror: Where does the body hold tension? (Reflects suppressed Akashic memories).

Diagnostic Key Inquiries
