The Sacred Invitation Technique

In advanced work, we do not "pull" fragments back. We use the Sacred Invitation. This respects the fragment's autonomy and ensures the client's nervous system is ready to receive.

Step 1: Alignment (A): Ensure the practitioner and client are in a high-vibrational state. The fragment will not return to a "hostile" or "unstable" environment.

Step 2: The Gateway (A): Open the Records and locate the fragment. View it with Compassionate Neutrality.

Step 3: The Invitation: Instead of a command, use the following syntax: "We recognize your service in protecting this soul. The danger has passed. We invite you to bring your wisdom and light back to the whole, at the pace of grace."

Long-term Anchoring (A) Strategies

Retrieval is only 50% of the work. The remaining 50% is Anchoring. A fragment that has been away for 30 years needs to be "re-educated" on how to live in a 3D body.

Somatic Anchoring: Encourage the client to engage in physical activities that the fragment enjoys. If it's a childhood fragment, suggest swinging on a swing set or coloring. This signals to the nervous system that the fragment is "home."

Practitioner Boundary

Clients may feel "raw" after retrieval. Set a boundary: No major life decisions for 72 hours. The soul is recalibrating, and the ego may try to make impulsive moves to "compensate" for the new energy.
