Identifying 'Lineage Loops'

A Lineage Loop is a recurring energetic pattern that replicates across generations within a family tree. In the Akasha, these appear as "knots" or "distortions" in the ancestral records. When you are building a treatment plan, you must look for these repetitions during the Phase K (Key Inquiry) of your protocol.

Common Lineage Loops include:

  • Physical Predispositions: Patterns of illness (e.g., heart issues, autoimmune conditions) that manifest at similar ages across generations.
  • Financial Scarcity: "Poverty consciousness" where ancestors experienced sudden loss, famine, or war, leading to a biological drive to remain "small" or "safe" financially.
  • Relationship Failure: Cycles of abandonment, betrayal, or early widowhood that seem to follow the women or men of a specific line.
Coach Tip

When a client says, "Everyone in my family has this," or "It's just our luck," your practitioner ears should perk up. These are the verbal markers of a Lineage Loop. The 'Seven Generations' Rule

In professional treatment planning, we utilize the Seven Generations Rule. This principle states that a clearing is only complete when its resonance reaches seven generations back (to the root of the trauma) and seven generations forward (to ensure the lineage is clear for descendants).

When sequencing your sessions in the A.K.A.S.H.A. Protocol‚Ñ¢, you should plan the ancestral work as follows:

  1. Session 1 (The Root): Identifying which ancestor the pattern originated with.
  2. Session 2 (The Transmutation): Clearing the "Root Seed" trauma in that ancestor's record.
  3. Session 3 (The Ripple): Clearing the residual "echoes" in the living descendants.
  4. Session 4 (The Shield): Coding the future lineage records with the new, healed vibration.

During Phase S (Soul Mapping), it is critical to differentiate between debt the client's soul accrued in its own past lives and debt they are carrying on behalf of their family bloodline. Treating the wrong one leads to stalled progress.
