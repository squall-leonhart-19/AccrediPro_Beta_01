The Science of Inherited Trauma

For decades, the idea that a trauma suffered by an ancestor could affect a descendant was considered "mystical." However, the field of epigenetics‚Äîthe study of changes in organisms caused by modification of gene expression rather than alteration of the genetic code itself‚Äîhas provided the biological "receipts" for what we call Soul Mapping.

The pioneering research of Dr. Rachel Yehuda, a neuroscientist and director of the Traumatic Stress Studies Division at the Icahn School of Medicine, has been instrumental. Her 2015 study of Holocaust survivors and their children found that both groups shared a specific epigenetic tag on the FKBP5 gene, which is linked to post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and depression.
