Neurobiology: Intuition vs. Imagination

A common fear for new practitioners is: "Am I just making this up?" Neuropsychology provides a clear distinction between the two. Imagination is a "top-down" process, where the brain actively constructs images from stored memories in the hippocampus. Intuition (Akashic reception) is a "bottom-up" or "non-local" process.

Research using fMRI has shown that imagination primarily activates the visual cortex and prefrontal regions as the brain "works" to create a scene. In contrast, authentic intuition shows higher activation in the insula and anterior cingulate cortex‚Äîareas associated with interoception and "direct knowing."
