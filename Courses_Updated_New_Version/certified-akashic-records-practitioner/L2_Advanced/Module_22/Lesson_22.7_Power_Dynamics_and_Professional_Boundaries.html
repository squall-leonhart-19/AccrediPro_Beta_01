The Inherent Power Imbalance in "Accessing"

When we utilize the A.K.A.S.H.A. Protocol‚Ñ¢, specifically during the Accessing (A) phase, we are entering a "fiduciary-like" relationship. The client is vulnerable, often sharing trauma, deep-seated fears, or soul secrets they have never told anyone else. Because you hold the "keys" to this information, the client may subconsciously view you as an authority figure or a "savior."

A 2022 study on spiritual counseling dynamics found that 68% of clients in alternative healing modalities initially perceive their practitioner as having "superior wisdom," which can lead to a suspension of the client's own critical thinking. As a professional, your job is to bridge this gap by constantly pointing the client back to their own sovereignty.

Coach Tip: The Authority Trap

Avoid using language like "The Records say you MUST..." Instead, use "The Records are inviting you to consider..." This subtle shift in syntax preserves the client's power and prevents them from abdicating their free will to you.

Practitioner: Elena (52), former School Teacher

Client: "Julie," 45, undergoing a high-conflict divorce.
Intervention: Julie began texting Elena daily for "quick Record checks" on legal decisions.
The Outcome: Elena felt drained and Julie stopped making independent choices. Elena had to implement a "No Texting for Readings" policy, moving Julie back to scheduled Soul-Led Action Plans (SAP).

Elena learned that by being "too available," she was actually hindering Julie's growth. Professionalism is a form of love; it provides the structure the client needs to feel safe enough to change.

Dual Relationships and the Digital Boundary

For many of our practitioners‚Äîespecially those transitioning from careers in nursing or teaching‚Äîthe desire to be "friends" with clients is strong. However, in the Akashic field, dual relationships (being a client's practitioner and their friend/business partner/relative) can cloud your neutrality.

Guidelines for Social Interaction:

  • Physical Touch: In a professional setting, touch should be limited to a brief handshake or a light touch on the shoulder/hand only if permission is granted. Avoid "healing hugs" unless the session has completely closed and the client initiates it.
  • Social Media: It is highly recommended to have a professional page separate from your personal life. If a client follows your personal page, avoid engaging in deep "Record talk" in the comments or DMs.
  • Public Encounters: If you see a client in public, let them acknowledge you first. This protects their privacy regarding the fact that they are seeing a practitioner.

Managing Client Dependency

Success in your practice isn't measured by how many years a client stays with you, but by how much they evolve. If a client cannot make a decision without "checking the Records" with you, they have moved into dependency. This is common among women 40-55 who are seeking a sense of purpose and may cling to the practitioner for validation.

Coach Tip: Income Sustainability

A professional practitioner who sets boundaries actually makes more money. Why? Because you avoid burnout. Practitioners who allow dependency often quit within 18 months. Those who maintain boundaries build a referral-based practice charging $200+ per session.

The Ethics of Referral: Knowing Your Scope

As you perform Healing Transmutation (H), you may uncover deep clinical depression, active addiction, or suicidal ideation. The Akashic Records are not a substitute for psychiatric care.

When to Refer Out:

  1. The "Looping" Client: If a client brings the same trauma to every session for 3 months without any movement toward the SAP (Soul-Led Action Plan), they likely need a therapist to process the "3D" psychological aspects.
  2. Mental Health Red Flags: Any mention of self-harm or harm to others requires an immediate referral to a crisis line or professional.
  3. Medical Claims: Never "diagnose" a physical illness in the Records. You may see "energetic congestion in the liver area," but you must say: "I'm seeing some energetic density here; I recommend seeing your physician for a standard check-up."

Compassionate Distance in Healing Transmutation

During the Healing Transmutation (H) phase, you are holding space for the client's soul to clear karmic loops. It is easy to "take on" the client's energy. Professional distance does not mean being cold; it means being a clear vessel.

Maintaining distance allows you to see the "Soul Map" clearly. If you get emotionally "in the trenches" with the client, you lose the 30,000-foot view required to guide them effectively through the A.K.A.S.H.A. Protocol‚Ñ¢.

Coach Tip: The Post-Session Ritual

Always perform a "cord-cutting" or energetic wash after a session. This signals to your nervous system that the professional interaction has ended and you are returning to your own sovereignty.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks if they should quit their job during an Akashic reading. What is the most ethical response?

The most ethical response is to share the energetic insights provided by the Records (e.g., "The Records show a high level of misalignment in your current role") but explicitly state that the final decision rests with the client's free will. You might say, "The Records provide the map, but you are the driver."

2. What is a "Dual Relationship" in the context of professional practice?

A dual relationship occurs when a practitioner holds two or more roles with a client simultaneously (e.g., being their Akashic reader and their best friend). This is generally discouraged because it compromises the practitioner's objectivity and creates "egoic filters" during the reading.

3. You notice a client is becoming dependent on you for daily decisions. What is the best course of action?

Gently address the pattern during a session. Reiterate that the goal of the A.K.A.S.H.A. Protocol‚Ñ¢ is to empower their own intuition. You may need to space out sessions further (e.g., once a month instead of once a week) to encourage self-governance.

4. True or False: It is ethical to give medical advice if the "MTLOs" (Masters, Teachers, and Loved Ones) suggest a specific supplement.

False. Even if you receive specific information, unless you are a licensed medical professional, you must frame it as "information to discuss with your doctor" rather than a prescription or medical advice.

Key Takeaways

  • Power Awareness: Always acknowledge the vulnerability of the client in the "Accessing" phase and actively work to minimize the power gap.
  • Boundary Clarity: Clear boundaries (no texting, specific session times, professional social media) create a "Sacred Container" that facilitates deeper healing.
  • Empowerment Goal: The ultimate goal of the A.K.A.S.H.A. Protocol‚Ñ¢ is the client's sovereignty, not their reliance on you.
  • Referral Knowledge: Knowing when to refer a client to a therapist or doctor is a sign of professional mastery, not a lack of skill.
  • Energetic Hygiene: Maintain professional distance and perform post-session clearing to prevent "empathic burnout."
