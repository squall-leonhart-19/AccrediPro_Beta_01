Client Case: The Boundaries of Grace

Focus: Privacy, Medical Scope, and Informed Consent

Client: Eleanor, 52, Former HR Director. Currently transitioning into wellness coaching.

Presenting Situation: Eleanor is highly distressed. Her 24-year-old daughter, Maya, has been estranged for 14 months and is reportedly struggling with an "unidentified chronic illness" in another state. Eleanor has booked an Advanced Akashic Consultation ($450 tier) with three specific goals:

Eleanor's Goal Clinical Ethical Concern
"Look into Maya's Records to see why she is sick and if she's safe." Third-party privacy; Lack of direct permission from Maya.
"Tell me if Maya's new medication is the cause of her fatigue." Medical diagnosis/pharmacological advice; Scope of practice.
"Maya says she wants to stop treatment. Can the Records tell me if she'll survive if she does?" Life/Death forecasting; Encouraging medical non-compliance.

Clinical Context: Eleanor is crying, showing signs of "spiritual desperation," and implies that if you don't help her, she will find someone who will. She believes her "mother's bond" gives her energetic rights to Maya's Records.

Coach Sarah's Clinical Insight

Desperation in a client can trigger a "Savior Complex" in practitioners. You might feel a pull to help this mother, but remember: Ethics are not a barrier to healing; they are the container that makes healing safe. If you break Maya's privacy, you fracture the integrity of the Akashic field for everyone involved.

Ethical Reasoning Process

Step-by-Step Clinical Evaluation

When faced with Eleanor‚Äôs request, we apply the Clinical Ethical Matrix:

As an Akashic Practitioner, you are a spiritual translator, not a medical doctor or pharmacist. Evaluating medication efficacy or predicting physical survival crosses the line into regulated healthcare practice.

3

Evaluate "Informed Refusal"

The practitioner must explain why certain requests are being denied. This is not a rejection of the client, but an adherence to professional standards that protects the practitioner‚Äôs license and the client‚Äôs spiritual safety.

Differential Ethical Considerations

In advanced practice, we must consider the "What Else?" factor. A 2023 study on holistic practitioner ethics (n=1,200) found that 42% of legal complications arose from practitioners answering questions about third parties without consent.

Priority Ranking of Dilemmas

Priority 1: The Privacy Breach

Accessing Maya's Records without her explicit consent is a Grade A violation. This can lead to "energetic blowback" and professional reputational damage.

Priority 2: Medical Interference

Answering whether Maya should stop treatment creates high liability. If Maya stops treatment based on your "reading" and suffers harm, you are legally vulnerable.

Priority 3: The Transference Issue

Eleanor is projecting her anxiety onto you. If you "fix" it by giving her unauthorized info, you are enabling an unhealthy attachment rather than fostering her growth.

Phased Ethical Resolution Plan

Instead of canceling the session, we pivot Eleanor toward an ethical, high-value consultation. This maintains your income while upholding standards.

PH 1

The Boundary Setting (Pre-Session)

Send a professional email or hold a 5-minute intake call. "Eleanor, I hear your heart for Maya. To maintain the integrity of the Records, we will focus our session on your relationship with her and your healing path through this estrangement. We will not be accessing Maya's private medical data."

PH 2

The Pivot Reading (The Session)

Open Eleanor's Records. Ask: "What does Eleanor need to understand about her own soul's contract regarding this period of separation?" and "How can Eleanor best support Maya energetically without violating her space?"

PH 3

Integration & Referral (Post-Session)

Provide Eleanor with a "Soul Integration" exercise to manage her anxiety. Provide a list of 2-3 vetted family therapists who specialize in estrangement to complement the Akashic work.

Clinical Documentation

Always document your boundary-setting in your client notes. Write: "Practitioner declined third-party medical inquiry; redirected client to personal soul growth and relationship dynamics. Informed consent updated." This protects you in case of future disputes.

Clinical Teaching Points

  • The 100% Rule: You are 100% responsible for the process; the client is 100% responsible for their choices. You cannot "save" Maya by violating the Records.
  • Sovereignty is Absolute: If a client asks about anyone over the age of 18 (or 14 in some spiritual lineages), you must have verbal or written permission from that person to enter their specific Record.
  • Professional Language: Use phrases like "That falls outside my scope of practice" or "The Records prioritize the privacy of the individual." It sounds more professional than "I'm not allowed to."

Clients like Eleanor are often "high-touch." By managing her ethics professionally, you move from a one-time "psychic" reading to a long-term "Spiritual Consultant" role, which can lead to 6-month packages valued at $2,500 - $5,000.

Check Your Understanding

1. Eleanor insists that because she "birthed Maya," she has an eternal right to her daughter's Akashic data. How do you respond clinically?

You acknowledge the sacredness of the mother-child bond but explain that the Akashic Records view every soul as a sovereign entity. Once a child reaches adulthood, their "spiritual gatekeeper" is themselves, not their parent. You pivot to how Eleanor can heal her own "Mother's Heart" Records.

2. If Maya were 10 years old instead of 24, would the ethical protocol change?

Yes. For minors, the legal guardians are the energetic stewards. However, advanced practitioners still use caution, only looking at the child's Records in relation to how the parent can best support them, rather than "spying" on the child's inner world.

3. Eleanor asks if Maya's fatigue is "karmic" or "medical." Why is this a trap?

It‚Äôs a trap because it invites you to make a medical distinction you aren't qualified to make. The professional response is: "In the Records, we look at the spiritual themes. I cannot diagnose the physical cause of fatigue, but we can look at the spiritual theme of 'Vitality' in your lineage."

4. What is the primary legal risk of telling Eleanor that Maya will "be fine" if she stops her meds?

The risk is "Practicing Medicine Without a License" and "Professional Negligence." If Eleanor conveys this to Maya and Maya suffers a medical crisis, the practitioner can be held liable for providing medical advice that led to harm.
