Handling Silence

If you don't "hear" or "see" anything immediately after opening, stay in the silence. Don't rush to fill it. Usually, the information is waiting for you to relax your shoulders.

Common Practitioner Pitfalls

The Pitfall What it Looks Like The Professional Correction
Censoring Thinking "That's too weird to say" or "That doesn't make sense." Say it anyway. The "weird" detail is often the specific validation the client needs.
Over-Explaining Spending 20 minutes explaining the history of the Records. Keep the intro brief (2-3 mins). Get into the Records where the actual healing happens.
Energy Leaking Taking on the client's sadness or anxiety as your own. Use the "Golden Pillar" visualization to stay centered while being empathetic.
Seeking Approval Asking "Does that make sense?" after every single sentence. Deliver the information with confidence. Wait for the client to offer feedback naturally.

Your Success Markers

Check Your Understanding

1. Deborah asks, "Am I just making this up?" during the reading. What is the best practitioner response?

Gently remind her that the Records often feel like our own thoughts because they communicate through our existing mental framework. Suggest she focus on how the information feels in her body rather than analyzing it with her mind.

2. What should you do if you receive information that feels negative or frightening?

The Records are a realm of Akasha (Primary Substance/Love). If information feels frightening, you have likely dropped out of the Records and into the astral plane or your own ego. Close the Records, ground yourself, and re-open with a focus on Divine Love.

3. True or False: You must wait until you are "certified" to practice these scripts out loud.

False. Practice is the only way to build the "intuitive muscle." You should practice on yourself and willing friends/family throughout this module to gain the necessary experience for certification.
