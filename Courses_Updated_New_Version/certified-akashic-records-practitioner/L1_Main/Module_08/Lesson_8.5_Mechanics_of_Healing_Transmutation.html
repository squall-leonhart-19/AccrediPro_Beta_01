The Sacred Vow of Revocation

The Vow of Revocation is the formal legal process of the soul. It is used when a client is bound by an oath that was once protective but is now restrictive. These vows are often "hidden" in the subconscious and manifest as recurring patterns of lack, loneliness, or illness.

Managing the Integration Lag

One of the most critical aspects of professional practice is educating your client on Integration Lag. While the shift in the Akasha is instantaneous (5D), the physical body and the 3D life take time to catch up. This lag can last anywhere from 3 days to 3 weeks.

During this time, the client may experience:

  • Physical Detox: Headaches, fatigue, or changes in sleep as the cells release old "frequency markers."
  • Emotional Purging: Unexpected crying or bouts of anger as the old narrative leaves the system.
  • Reality Testing: Small challenges in the 3D world that "test" if the client will revert to the old vow.

The 'Echo Effect' in Physical Reality

The Echo Effect is the phenomenon where a client sees a ghost of their old problem right after a major clearing. For example, a client who cleared a "Vow of Invisibility" might suddenly get a critical email from a boss. This is not a sign that the healing failed; it is the 3D shadow passing through their field as it exits.

As a practitioner, your job is to help the client recognize the echo so they don't re-attach to it. If they react with their old pattern, they "re-sign" the contract. If they observe it with neutrality, the echo fades, and the new reality anchors permanently.
