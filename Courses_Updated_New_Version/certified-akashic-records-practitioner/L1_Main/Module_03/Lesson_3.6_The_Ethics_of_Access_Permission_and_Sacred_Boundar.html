As a Certified Akashic Records Practitioner‚Ñ¢, your mandate is to redirect the inquiry. You cannot access a third party's record without their direct permission. However, you CAN access the Client‚Äôs Record to see how that relationship is affecting them.

Case Study: The Boundary of Curiosity

Practitioner: Sarah (51), former HR Executive

Scenario: A client, Deborah, asked Sarah to look into her ex-husband's records to see if he was planning to sue for more custody. Deborah was anxious and willing to pay a premium for "insider info."

Intervention: Sarah declined the direct request, explaining the Law of Consent. Instead, she opened Deborah's records and asked: "What does Deborah need to know to feel secure and empowered during this legal transition?"

Outcome: The Records revealed a deep-seated fear of abandonment in Deborah. By addressing the root cause, Deborah showed up to mediation calm and centered. The ex-husband, sensing her lack of reactive "hooks," dropped the suit. Sarah maintained her professional integrity and earned a lifelong client who now pays $250 per session.

Coach Tip

When a client pushes for third-party info, say: "I honor your concern, but I cannot enter another's record without their soul's consent. Let's look at your record to see what you need to know to navigate this relationship with peace." This maintains authority and provides more value.

Protocol for 'Emergency Access'

There are rare exceptions where permission cannot be obtained verbally. This typically involves children under 18 (where the parent/guardian holds the energetic "proxy") or individuals in a coma/crisis where they cannot speak.

The Emergency Protocol:

  1. Open your own records first.
  2. Ask your MTLOs: "Is it in the highest good of [Name] for me to access their records for healing purposes?"
  3. Wait for a clear Sensory Signature of Peace. If you feel any resistance, contraction, or "grayness," the answer is NO.
  4. Proceed only with the intention of healing and support, never for information-gathering or curiosity.

Coach Tip

For children, always remind the parent that once the child reaches 18 (or sometimes 16, depending on soul maturity), the parent's proxy expires. The child then becomes the sole gatekeeper of their own records.
