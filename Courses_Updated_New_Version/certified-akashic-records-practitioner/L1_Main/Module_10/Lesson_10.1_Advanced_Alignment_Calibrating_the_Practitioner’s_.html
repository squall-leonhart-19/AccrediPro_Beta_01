Neutralizing the 'Static': Clearing Egoic Filters

As a practitioner, you are a translator. Information enters your field as pure energy and is translated by your brain into words, images, or feelings. If your "translation software" (the ego) is cluttered, the message becomes skewed.

Identify these three common professional filters:

  1. The 'Fixer' Filter: The need to make the client feel better immediately. This leads to "sugar-coating" the Records' message.
  2. The 'Expert' Filter: The need to sound smart or profound. This leads to adding "extra" details that weren't in the original transmission.
  3. The 'Personal' Filter: Projecting your own life experiences (e.g., your views on divorce or money) onto the client's data.
Coach Tip: The Ego-Hook Check

Before every session, say internally: "I am the hollow bone. I have no investment in the outcome, only in the accuracy of the transmission." This simple mantra helps collapse the 'Fixer' filter.

The 'Internal Compass' Technique

Your physical body is a bio-resonance feedback tool. In advanced alignment, we use somatic markers to verify the "truth" of the information being received. This is what we call the Internal Compass.

When you receive information from the Akasha, notice where it "lands" in your body:

  • The 'Yes' Resonance: Usually felt as an expansion in the chest, a cooling sensation, or a sense of "rightness" in the solar plexus.
  • The 'Static' Resonance: Felt as a tightening in the throat, a "buzzing" in the forehead (ego over-activity), or a sudden drop in body temperature.

Pro Tip: If you receive a message that feels "scary" or "doom-laden," check your compass. The Akashic Records always resonate with the frequency of unconditional love. If the message feels heavy or restrictive, it is likely coming from the practitioner's ego-filter, not the Records.
