Common Practitioner Pitfalls

Even the most talented students can trip up in their first few professional sessions. Watch out for these common mistakes:

The Mistake Why it Happens The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome; trying to "prove" the Records are real. Trust the silence. Let the message land before adding more words.
Giving "Human" Advice Wanting to help so badly you stop listening to the Records. Prefix your statements with "The Records are showing me..." to stay in the channel.
Rushing the Opening Anxiety about the client's time or "dead air." Take 3 deep breaths before the prayer. The silence is where the healing begins.
Ignoring "No" Trying to force an image to fit the client's life. If a client says "that doesn't resonate," ask the Records for a different perspective immediately.
