What You Can and Cannot Do

As a Certified Akashic Records Practitioner‚Ñ¢, your role is that of a spiritual facilitator and intuitive educator. You are not a doctor, a licensed therapist, or a financial advisor. Understanding this distinction is the key to your professional legitimacy.

Coach Sarah's Tip

Think of yourself as a translator. You are translating the language of the soul (the Records) into the language of the human experience. You aren't "fixing" the client; you are providing them with the map so they can fix themselves.

Legal Foundations & Liability

According to a 2023 industry report, the wellness coaching market (which includes spiritual practitioners) is growing at a CAGR of 6.7%. With this growth comes a need for professional standards. Protecting your business allows you to serve more people.

To stay legally safe, you must implement the "Triangle of Protection":

  • The Professional Disclaimer: A clear statement on your website and intake forms stating that your services are for educational and spiritual purposes only.
  • Client Agreement: A signed document where the client acknowledges they are responsible for their own well-being and that you are not a medical professional.
  • Professional Liability Insurance: Even for spiritual work, insurance is affordable (often under $200/year) and provides peace of mind.
Coach Sarah's Tip

Don't let the legal talk scare you! It's actually very empowering. When you present a client with a professional agreement, it tells them: "I take my work‚Äîand your soul‚Äîseriously." It immediately elevates you above the "hobbyists."

The Ethics of Energy

Ethics in the Akashic Records are different from clinical ethics. We are dealing with Sacred Privacy.

1. Informed Consent: Never open someone's Records without their explicit permission. This includes "checking in" on an ex-partner or a family member. It is an energetic violation.

2. Confidentiality: What happens in the Records stays in the Records. The only exception is if a client expresses a clear intent to harm themselves or others.

3. Referral Responsibility: If a client comes to you in a state of active crisis, your most ethical move is to refer them to a licensed professional. "I can see the spiritual root here, but I want to ensure you have the grounded, clinical support you deserve while we do this work."
