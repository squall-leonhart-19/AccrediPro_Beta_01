The Intersubjective Field: The Third Energy

As a supervisor, you must be able to "read" this field. Is the practitioner providing a pure stream of data from the Masters, Teachers, and Loved Ones (MTLOs), or are they unknowingly filtering the information through their own unresolved wounds? This is the core of Countertransference.

Projective Identification in the Akasha

Projective Identification is a sophisticated defense mechanism where a client unconsciously "deposits" a part of their experience into the practitioner. In an Akashic session, a client who feels powerless might project that powerlessness so strongly that the practitioner begins to feel "stuck" or "unable to see the Records clearly."

Signs of Projective Identification for Supervisors to Watch For:

  • The practitioner reports feeling "drained" or "heavy" only after sessions with a specific client.
  • The practitioner expresses uncharacteristic anger or frustration toward a client's "lack of progress."
  • The practitioner begins to use language that mirrors the client's exact emotional state (e.g., "I felt just as hopeless as she did").

Recognizing Past-Life Over-Identification

This is a unique risk for Akashic Practitioners. When a practitioner accesses a client's past-life trauma, they may encounter a narrative that mirrors their own soul history. If the practitioner has not fully integrated their own Phase H: Healing Transmutation, they may "over-identify" with the client's past-life character.

Maintaining Energetic Neutrality

As a supervisor, you will often review "heavy" case files involving abuse, loss, or deep soul-trauma. To maintain your own health and the integrity of your mentorship, you must master Quantum Decoupling.

Techniques for Clearing the Intersubjective Field:

  1. The Golden Cord Visualization: Before a supervision session, visualize a cord of light connecting you to the Great Central Sun, and another connecting the mentee. See a clear space between you where only the "Work" exists.
  2. Somatic Discharge: After reviewing a sensitive file, use physical movement (shaking the hands, stretching) to release any empathetic resonance stored in the nervous system.
  3. The "Not My Story" Mantra: Silently affirm, "I am a witness to this soul's journey, but I am not the author of its pain."
