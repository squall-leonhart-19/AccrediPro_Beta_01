5. Supervision Best Practices

To be an effective Master Practitioner, you must adhere to the Supervision Ethics Code. This ensures the safety of both the practitioner and the public.

  • Maintain Dual Boundary: You are her mentor, not her therapist. If Elena‚Äôs personal trauma is the primary issue, refer her to a therapist.
  • Documentation: Keep brief notes of your supervision sessions. Professionalism is the hallmark of a Master Practitioner.
  • Financial Transparency: As a Master Practitioner, you may charge for supervision. Typical rates for experienced mentors range from $200 to $450 per hour. This is a significant revenue stream that honors your years of study.

Check Your Understanding

1. A mentee reports that they forgot the closing prayer because they were crying with the client. What is the priority in your feedback?

The priority is to address the "boundary leak" (over-identifying with the client) and normalize the technical error (forgetting the prayer) as a symptom of energetic overwhelm, rather than a spiritual failure.

2. What is the "Socratic Method" in the context of Akashic supervision?

It is the practice of asking the mentee guiding questions (e.g., "What did your intuition tell you in that moment?") to help them discover their own clinical reasoning, rather than simply telling them what they did wrong.

3. When should you refer a mentee to a therapist instead of continuing supervision?

When the mentee's personal trauma or psychological issues are consistently interfering with their ability to practice, and the supervision sessions have turned into therapy sessions. This maintains professional boundaries.

4. Why is a "Healing Crisis" during a reading considered a success rather than a failure?

Because it indicates that the Records have successfully bypassed the client's ego-defenses to touch a deep-seated block that is now ready to be released and integrated.
