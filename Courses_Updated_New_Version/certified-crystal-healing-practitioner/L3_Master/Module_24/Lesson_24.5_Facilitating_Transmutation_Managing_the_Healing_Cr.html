Defining the Healing Crisis in Mineral Therapy

A healing crisis, often referred to in biological terms as a Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction, occurs when the body's detoxification or energetic purging processes temporarily outpace its ability to eliminate the waste. In the context of The F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, this happens during the T (Transmutation) phase when a high-frequency stone forces a low-frequency blockage to surface.

For many practitioners, this phase can be intimidating. However, for a Master Practitioner, a healing crisis is a sign of profound success. It indicates that the mineral‚Äôs lattice structure has successfully entrained the client's bio-field to a level where stagnation can no longer exist.

Coach Tip: Clinical Differentiation

It is vital to distinguish between a healing crisis and an adverse reaction. A healing crisis usually involves a brief intensification of existing symptoms followed by a sense of lightness. An adverse reaction often presents as new, sharp pain or extreme anxiety that does not dissipate when stones are moved. Always trust your tactile scanning to check for auric tears during these moments.

Advanced Extraction: Obsidian & Black Tourmaline

The Obsidian Draw

Obsidian, being a volcanic glass with no crystalline structure, is uniquely suited for absorption. It does not reflect energy; it pulls it into its amorphous void. To manage a crisis, a Master Practitioner uses an Obsidian Point held roughly 2-3 inches above the site of the crisis (often the solar plexus or heart), using a counter-clockwise spiraling motion to "draw" the stagnant frequency out of the bio-field.

Black Tourmaline Grounding Rods

While Obsidian extracts, Black Tourmaline transmutes through its complex borosilicate structure. In a crisis, placing Black Tourmaline rods at the "Exit Points" (the soles of the feet) ensures that the energy being stirred up has a clear, grounded path out of the physical vessel. This prevents the "re-absorption" of emotional toxins.
