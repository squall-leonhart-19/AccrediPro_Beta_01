The Chemistry of Interaction: Silicates vs. Carbonates

At the Master level, we must understand that crystals are chemical compounds. When placed on the body, they don't just "vibrate"‚Äîthey interact via Ionic Exchange and electromagnetic signaling.

1. Silicates (The Conductors)

Silicates (Quartz, Tourmaline, Mica) are composed primarily of Silicon and Oxygen. Silicon is the second most abundant element in the Earth's crust and a vital trace mineral in human skin, hair, and connective tissue. Silicates act as the "broadband" of the mineral world, capable of carrying vast amounts of information and energy.

2. Carbonates (The Builders)

Carbonates (Calcite, Malachite, Rhodochrosite) contain the carbonate ion (CO3). These minerals are chemically softer and often resonate with the body's calcium-based structures (bones and teeth). Carbonates are "absorptive" in nature, making them ideal for the Transmutation phase of the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢.

3. Oxides (The Protectors)

Oxides (Hematite, Magnetite) are often iron-rich. These minerals interact with the hemoglobin in our blood, influencing circulation and the "vital heat" of the body. A 2021 study on magnetoreception suggests that humans possess trace amounts of magnetite in the brain, explaining our deep resonance with iron-based stones.
