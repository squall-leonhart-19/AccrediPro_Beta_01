Legal Considerations & Liability

Transitioning to mentorship increases your "vicarious liability." If a mentee you are supervising commits an ethical breach, you may be held partially responsible if your oversight was deemed negligent.

Professional Indemnity for Mentors

Standard practitioner insurance often does not cover Supervisory Acts. L3 Practitioners should ensure their policy includes "Professional Liability for Educators/Supervisors." This is especially important for career-changers (like former nurses or teachers) who understand the value of protecting their personal assets and professional reputation.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between a "Crystal Assessment" and a "Medical Diagnosis"?

A Crystal Assessment focuses on bio-field frequencies and auric imbalances (energy), whereas a Medical Diagnosis identifies biological pathology and clinical diseases (matter). L3 practitioners must ensure mentees never cross this line.

2. What does "Vicarious Liability" mean for an L3 Mentor?

It means the Mentor may be held legally responsible for the actions or negligence of the mentee they are supervising, highlighting the need for specialized supervisor insurance and strict oversight protocols.

3. Why is "Energetic Sovereignty" a mandatory part of Informed Consent?

It establishes that the client is the ultimate authority over their own energy field, preventing "energetic coercion" and protecting the practitioner from claims that they influenced the client's state without permission.

4. How does DEI impact the "Stabilization" phase of the FACETS Method?

By acknowledging a client's unique cultural and physical reality, a practitioner can select grounding minerals (Module 6) that resonate with the client's specific lived experience, leading to more effective anchoring of the vibrational shift.
