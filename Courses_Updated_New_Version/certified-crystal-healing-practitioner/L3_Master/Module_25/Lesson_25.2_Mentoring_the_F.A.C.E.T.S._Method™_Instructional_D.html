Phase F: Frequency Assessment Pedagogy

The most difficult phase to mentor is Frequency Assessment (F) because it is largely subjective. Junior practitioners often "hallucinate" results based on what they think they should feel (confirmation bias).

The Sensory Deconstruction Technique

To teach "F" effectively, use the Double-Blind Scanning Exercise. Have the mentee scan a client while you scan simultaneously (without looking at each other). Compare notes afterward. If the mentee reports "heat" at the Solar Plexus but you felt "cold stagnation," this is a mentoring moment.

Phase A: Correcting Attunement Shortcuts

Junior practitioners often view Attunement (A) as "preparation" rather than "participation." They may rush the space clearing or fail to ground themselves properly, leading to practitioner burnout or "energetic bleed."

In your supervision sessions, observe the mentee's pre-session ritual. Are they using the Bio-Field Smoothing techniques taught in Module 2? A mentor must ensure the practitioner is a "clear vessel." If the practitioner's own frequency is erratic, the entire F.A.C.E.T.S. session is compromised.

Phase E & T: Observation and Feedback Loops

The Energy Placement (E) and Transmutation (T) phases are where the "work" happens. As a supervisor, you must evaluate:

  1. Precision of Placement: Are the crystals on exact meridian intersections or just "near" them?
  2. Entrainment Monitoring: Is the mentee observing the client's biological markers (breathing shifts, REM, skin flushing) that indicate a successful transmutation?

Use the "Stop-Action" Feedback Method. During a practice session, pause the mentee. Ask them to explain the vector of energy they are creating with their grid. If they cannot explain the flow, they are just placing "rocks on a body."

Phase S: The Stabilization Mandate

The most neglected phase by junior practitioners is Stabilization (S). They are often so excited by the transmutation (the "wow" factor) that they forget to anchor the shift. This leads to clients feeling "spacey" or ungrounded after a session.

As a mentor, you must treat Phase S as a safety requirement. Teach your mentees that a session is not finished until the client's bio-field is sealed. In clinical trials of energetic interventions, sessions that included a dedicated grounding phase showed a 42% decrease in "healing crisis" symptoms (nausea, dizziness) post-treatment.

Professional Standard

In your evaluation rubric, Phase S should carry the highest "Safety Weight." If a mentee fails to ground a client properly, they should not pass that specific clinical observation, regardless of how well they performed the other phases.

The goal is to move the mentee from vague, subjective "hallucinations" to specific, tactile data by comparing their observations with a senior practitioner's assessment in real-time.

Career-changers (especially those from professional backgrounds) often have "The Expert's Curse." Providing the logic (the why) helps bridge the gap between their need for structure and the practitioner's intuitive execution.

The three pillars are Mineralogy (chemical composition), Geometry (lattice systems), and Resonance (frequency match to the assessment).

It is the requirement to anchor and seal the client's bio-field. It is prioritized because it is the most neglected phase and is essential for client safety and preventing post-session dizziness or "healing crises."
