Analyzing Chemical Compositions: Silicates vs. Oxides

In Level 2 work, we recognize that a crystal is not merely a "stone," but a sophisticated chemical delivery system. The chemical class of a mineral determines its energetic density and how it interacts with the human bio-field.

1. The Silicate Class: Structural Complexity

Silicates make up over 90% of the Earth's crust. They are characterized by the silicon-oxygen tetrahedron (SiO4). In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, silicates are used for structural integrity in the auric field. Because of their complex branching structures, they are ideal for clients dealing with "scattered" energy or lack of focus.

2. The Oxide Class: Direct Force

Oxides (minerals where oxygen is combined with one or more metals) are energetically "heavier." Examples include Hematite and Magnetite. These are your primary tools for grounding and protection. While silicates branch and expand, oxides condense and protect.
