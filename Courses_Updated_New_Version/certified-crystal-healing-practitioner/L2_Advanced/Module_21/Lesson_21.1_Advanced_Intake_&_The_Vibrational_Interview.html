The Art of the Vibrational Interview

In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, the intake is not a passive collection of facts; it is the first phase of Frequency Assessment (F). While a conventional intake asks "Where does it hurt?", a Vibrational Interview asks "How is your energy currently manifesting in this space?"

This process begins the moment the client enters your field. You are assessing the Bio-Field Resonance‚Äîthe subtle energetic signature that surrounds the physical body. A 2021 study on bio-field interactions suggests that practitioners who establish "resonant rapport" during intake can actually influence the client's heart rate variability (HRV) before a single crystal is placed.

Coach Tip: The 70/30 Rule

In a professional intake, the client should speak 70% of the time. Your role is to hold the Attunement (A) state, acting as a "calibrated receiver." If you talk too much, you introduce your own frequency into their assessment, muddying the data.

Mapping the Energetic State

Once the interview is complete, you must map the data. This is where the Analytical vs. Intuitive balance comes into play. You are looking for patterns. If a client reports insomnia (Physical), fear of the future (Emotional), and a sudden interest in blue lace agate (Intuitive), you are seeing a pattern of Hyper-Active High-Frequency energy that needs Stabilization.

Ethical Boundaries & Expectation Management

As a practitioner, you occupy a sacred space, but it is also a professional space. You must never diagnose medical conditions. Your language should always reflect energetic states. Instead of saying "You have an overactive thyroid," you say "I am sensing a high-velocity frequency in the throat region that we will aim to stabilize."

Coach Tip: The Scope of Practice

Always include a "Professional Disclosure" in your intake forms. This establishes you as a legitimate practitioner and protects both you and the client. Legitimacy is built on clear boundaries.

Balancing Intuition with Analytical Data

The "Vibrational Interview" is a double-blind process. You collect Analytical Data (the client's words, history, and physical cues) and Intuitive Data (your bio-field scanning and "clairsentient" impressions).

The Golden Rule of Treatment Planning: If your intuition says Amethyst but the analytical data shows the client is already ungrounded and "spacey," the analytical data wins. You must stabilize the client before you can expand them. This is the difference between a hobbyist and a professional.

Coach Tip: The "Double-Check"

Before finalizing a plan, ask yourself: "Am I choosing this crystal because it's my favorite, or because the client's frequency assessment specifically demands it?"
