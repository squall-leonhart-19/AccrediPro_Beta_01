1. The Cubic (Isometric) System

2. The Hexagonal System

3. The Monoclinic System

These structures are skewed, representing flexibility and hidden potential. They are excellent for clients who are stuck in "black and white" thinking or need to see a situation from a new perspective. Example: Selenite, Kunzite.

Physical Integrity & Mohs Scale

Professionalism in crystal healing requires a deep respect for the physical properties of your tools. The Mohs Scale of Mineral Hardness (1-10) is vital for the E: Energy Placement phase.

Safety Protocol

Never place minerals with a Mohs hardness below 4 directly on the skin if they are being used for long-duration gridding. Soft stones like Selenite (Mohs 2) can flake, causing skin irritation. Furthermore, be aware of chemical toxicity: Malachite contains high levels of copper and should never be used in elixirs or placed on broken skin.

Client Safety Tip

Always check the Solubility of your stones. Some minerals, like Halite or certain Calcites, will dissolve or lose their luster if cleansed in water. A professional practitioner always uses dry cleansing methods (like sound or smoke) for delicate chemical structures.

The Science of Piezoelectricity

One of the most compelling scientific arguments for crystal therapy is the piezoelectric effect. This occurs when mechanical stress (like pressure or heat) is applied to a crystal, resulting in the generation of an electric charge.

Quartz is the most famous piezoelectric mineral. In a clinical setting, when you place a Quartz point on a client's meridian point and apply light pressure or use the warmth of your hands (A: Attunement), you are literally activating a micro-current that interacts with the client's nervous system. A 2021 study on bio-electric interfaces showed that crystalline structures can influence cellular signaling through these subtle electrical discharges.
