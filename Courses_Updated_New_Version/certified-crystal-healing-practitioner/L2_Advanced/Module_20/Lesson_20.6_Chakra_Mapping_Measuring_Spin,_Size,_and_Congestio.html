Measuring Spin & Rotational Velocity

The speed at which a chakra rotates indicates the metabolic rate of that energy center. A high-velocity spin suggests a center that is processing information and energy rapidly‚Äîcommon in the Crown or Third Eye during meditation. However, high velocity in the Root chakra may manifest as anxiety or "fight or flight" dominance.

To measure velocity, observe the pendulum's arc. A wide, fast circle indicates high velocity. A tight, fast circle indicates high intensity but low volume. A slow, wide circle indicates high volume but low processing speed (congestion).

Front vs. Rear Chakra Dynamics

Most practitioners only assess the front of the body. However, the chakras (excluding the Root and Crown) are double-headed vortices that extend through the back. These two sides have distinct functions:

  • Front (Feeling/Emotional): How the client relates to the world and processes emotions.
  • Rear (Will/Action): How the client executes their desires and asserts themselves in the world.

The 'Chakra Balance Ratio'

Energy is a closed system. If one chakra is under-functioning, another will often over-compensate. This is the Chakra Balance Ratio. A common pattern is a "blown out" Heart chakra (20cm) over-compensating for a "collapsed" Root chakra (5cm). The client is "all heart" but has no boundaries or stability.

Coach Tip: Identifying the "Lead" Chakra

Always look for the chakra with the most extreme measurement first. This is often the "Lead" chakra that is driving the imbalances in the others. Correcting the Lead chakra often causes the others to self-regulate without further intervention.

Identifying and Correcting 'Spin Reversal'

In a healthy state, the chakras should rotate clockwise (from the practitioner's perspective looking at the client). This represents the intake of universal life force. A counter-clockwise spin, or Spin Reversal, indicates that the chakra is in "output only" mode or is actively pushing energy away (repulsion).

Clinical Implications of Reversal:

  • Root: Feelings of not belonging on Earth; dissociation.
  • Sacral: Rejection of pleasure or creative expression.
  • Throat: Habitual dishonesty or "swallowing" one's truth.

Check Your Understanding

1. What does a counter-clockwise spin (Spin Reversal) typically indicate in a chakra assessment?

It indicates the chakra is in an "output only" or "repulsion" mode, often reflecting a subconscious rejection of the themes associated with that center (e.g., rejecting pleasure in the Sacral).

2. If a client is unable to take action on their goals despite having a strong emotional desire, which part of the chakra system should you investigate?

You should investigate the Rear (Will/Action) centers of the chakras, particularly the Solar Plexus and Heart, as these govern execution and assertion.

3. What is considered a healthy diameter range for a primary chakra vortex in a balanced adult?

A healthy diameter typically ranges between 10cm and 15cm. Measurements below 8cm suggest depletion, while those above 18cm suggest over-expansion.

4. Why is the Chakra Balance Ratio important to measure?

It identifies compensatory patterns, showing how one chakra might be over-functioning to make up for a neighbor's deficiency, allowing you to find the "Lead" imbalance.
