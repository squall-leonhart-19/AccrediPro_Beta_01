The Art of the Interview: Triggering the Field

The interview is not just for information gathering; it is a diagnostic tool. As you ask open-ended questions, you must observe the client's bio-field (using the hand scanning techniques from Module 1) or their subtle body language. When a client speaks about a "Vibrational Anchor," their field will often react before they even realize it.

Effective Questioning Strategies:

  • The Timeline Probe: "If this sensation had a starting note in the song of your life, when did that note first play?"
  • The Color/Texture Query: "When you focus on the tension in your throat, what color or density does it feel like to you?"
  • The Relational Map: "Who or what in your current environment feels like it 'drains' your battery?"

Practitioner-Client Resonance & Ethics

During the initial Frequency Assessment (F), a phenomenon known as Resonance Coupling occurs. Your field and the client's field begin to interact. This is why self-attunement (Module 2) is critical. If your field is cluttered, you will "misread" the client's anamnesis as your own issues.

Professional Boundaries: As a Certified Practitioner, you are a facilitator, not a "fixer." Your role is to provide the mineral frequencies necessary for the client's own system to rebalance. Avoid making medical diagnoses. Instead, use energetic terminology: "I am sensing a frequency imbalance in the area of the liver meridian," rather than "You have a liver problem."

Coach Tip

Always maintain a "Neutral Witness" state. If you feel yourself becoming emotionally overwhelmed by a client's story, perform a quick Bio-Field Smoothing on yourself mid-interview to clear the resonance.
