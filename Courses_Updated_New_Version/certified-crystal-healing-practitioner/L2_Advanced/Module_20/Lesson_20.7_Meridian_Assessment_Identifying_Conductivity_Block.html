Point Sensitivity Testing: The "Mu" Points

One of the fastest ways to assess meridian health is through Alarm Points (Front-Mu points). These are specific anatomical landmarks on the torso that "react" when their corresponding meridian is out of balance. In Lesson 2, we covered hand sensitivity; now we apply that tactile feedback to these specific points.

The Assessment Protocol:

  1. Light Palpation: Use two fingers to apply light, steady pressure to the Alarm Point.
  2. Client Feedback: Ask the client if the point feels "Tender," "Numb," or "Neutral."
  3. Tactile Feedback: Do you feel a "rebound" (healthy), a "hardness" (congestion), or a "sinkhole" (depletion)?

A 2021 study on bio-impedance at acupuncture points (n=120) demonstrated that areas of chronic pain correlate with significantly higher electrical resistance compared to healthy tissue. By identifying these points of resistance, you know exactly where to place your C (Crystal Selection) for maximum impact.
