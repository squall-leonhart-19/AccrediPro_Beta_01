Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Lepidolite chosen for Elena's Phase 1 intervention instead of a high-energy stone like Citrine?

Elena is in a "wired but tired" state with high cortisol. High-energy stones like Citrine can over-stimulate an already taxed HPA-axis. Lepidolite contains lithium, which provides a calming resonance to help downregulate the sympathetic nervous system.

2. Elena has a TSH of 3.8 and takes Levothyroxine. What is your primary responsibility regarding her medication?

Your responsibility is to remain within scope. You must never suggest changing the dose or stopping the medication. Instead, you support the "terrain" (e.g., reducing stress so the body can better convert T4 to T3) and encourage her to share your mineral protocol with her endocrinologist.

3. Which mineral property is specifically utilized when using Clear Quartz for joint support?

Piezoelectricity. Clear Quartz can convert mechanical stress into electrical energy (and vice versa), which research suggests may assist in cellular signaling within the collagen-dense connective tissues.

4. What "Red Flag" in Elena's profile would require an immediate pause in mineral therapy and a referral to an MD?

If Elena reported sudden unilateral (one-sided) leg swelling, heart palpitations, or unexplained rapid weight loss, these are clinical red flags that require medical diagnostic imaging or blood work beyond the scope of a mineral practitioner.
