The Four Pillars of Ethical Informed Consent

Informed consent is the process by which a practitioner provides a client with sufficient information to make an autonomous decision about their care. In the realm of crystal healing, where energy work can be subtle yet profound, the four pillars ensure both you and your client are energetically and legally aligned.

Pillar Definition Practitioner Application
Disclosure Providing all relevant facts about the session. Explaining that crystal healing is complementary, not medical.
Capacity The client‚Äôs ability to understand the information. Ensuring the client is not under the influence or in acute crisis.
Voluntariness Decision made without coercion or pressure. Allowing the client to choose their level of participation.
Comprehension The client actually understands what was disclosed. Asking "What questions do you have about the FACETS process?"

Touch Protocols & Physical Boundaries

The most sensitive aspects of the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ involve Energy Placement (E) and Stabilization (S). These phases often require either placing crystals on the body or "anchoring" the energy through light touch on the feet or shoulders.

Strict Protocols for Touch:

  • Pre-Session Verbal Consent: Always ask, "During the stabilization phase, I typically place my hands lightly on your feet to help anchor the energy. Is that acceptable to you?"
  • The 'Hover' Alternative: If a client is uncomfortable with touch, perform the placement or stabilization in the auric field (2-4 inches above the body).
  • Clothing: Crystal healing is always performed fully clothed. Ensure your consent form explicitly states this.
  • Sensitive Areas: Never place crystals on or near the breast or groin area without using a secondary cloth barrier and explicit, repeated verbal consent.

The 'Right to Withdraw' and Emotional Release

A 2022 survey of energy medicine practitioners found that 28% of clients experience some form of "cathartic emotional release" (crying, shaking, or sudden memory recall) during deep vibrational work. This is where client autonomy is most tested.

The Right to Withdraw means the client can stop the session at any time for any reason. As a practitioner, you must remain unattached to the "completion" of the protocol if the client needs to stop.

Managing Emotional Release:

  1. Pause the Protocol: If a client begins to cry or express distress, stop the crystal work immediately.
  2. Check In: Ask a simple, grounding question: "I see you're having a strong reaction. Would you like to continue, take a break, or stop for today?"
  3. Avoid "Processing": Unless you are also a licensed therapist, do not try to "fix" the emotion. Hold the space, offer water, and allow the client to lead the next step.

Documentation Requirements

Professionalism is reflected in your paperwork. Whether you use digital platforms like IntakeQ or physical paper files, your consent form should be a standalone document, separate from your intake history.

Essential Components of a Consent Form:

  • Description of Service: Clear language stating this is mineral-based energy work.
  • No-Guarantee Clause: Explicitly stating that results vary and are not guaranteed.
  • Touch Consent: A checkbox for physical touch versus non-touch sessions.
  • Cancellation & Termination: Policies for ending the session early.
  • Confidentiality: How their data and session details are protected.

Keep your consent forms for a minimum of 7 years. This is a standard professional practice that protects you and provides a historical record of the client's agreement to your protocols.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which pillar of consent is being addressed when you ask a client to repeat back their understanding of the session's limitations?

This addresses Comprehension. It ensures the client hasn't just heard the words, but has actually processed and understood the information provided.

2. During the "Energy Placement" (E) phase, you realize a crystal needs to be placed on the client's solar plexus, but you didn't mention this specific area in the intake. What should you do?

You must pause and provide verbal disclosure. Say, "I'd like to place a Citrine on your solar plexus area to support this shift. Is that okay with you?" Never assume prior consent covers new, specific placements.

3. True or False: If a client begins crying during a session, you should immediately ask them about their childhood to help them "clear" the energy.

False. Unless you are a licensed mental health professional, "processing" trauma is outside your scope of practice. Your role is to hold space, offer grounding, and respect their autonomy to continue or stop.

4. What is the "Hover" alternative in the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢?

The "Hover" alternative is performing energy placement or stabilization in the client's auric field (a few inches above the body) rather than making physical contact. This respects the autonomy of clients who prefer no physical touch.
