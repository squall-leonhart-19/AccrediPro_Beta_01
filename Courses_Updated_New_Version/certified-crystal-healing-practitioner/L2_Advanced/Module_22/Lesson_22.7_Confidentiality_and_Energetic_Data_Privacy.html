Digital Data Security for Practitioners

In our digital age, confidentiality isn't just about what you say; it's about where you store it. Many practitioners inadvertently leak data through unencrypted emails or casual text messages.

The "Digital Gold Standard" for Practitioners:

  • Email: Use a HIPAA-compliant email service (like G Suite with a BAA) or an encrypted provider like ProtonMail.
  • SMS: Avoid discussing specific health or emotional details over standard SMS. Use secure platforms like Signal or the messaging portal within your booking software.
  • Storage: If you use cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive), ensure two-factor authentication (2FA) is enabled.

Anonymizing Success Stories

You will want to share the amazing results of the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ to attract new clients. However, "energetic data privacy" means the client should not be recognizable even if you don't use their name.

The "Three-Point Alteration" Rule: To truly anonymize a case study for social media or your website, change at least three non-essential identifying factors:

  1. The Name: Use a pseudonym or just "Client A."
  2. The Occupation/Location: If they are a "Nurse in Seattle," refer to them as a "Healthcare Professional in the Pacific Northwest."
  3. The Specific Detail: If they have three children, say "a parent of multiple children."
Coach Tip: Verbal Consent

Even with anonymization, it is a "best practice" to ask the client: "Your progress with the Smokey Quartz placement was incredible. Would you mind if I shared a summary of this success anonymously to help others?" Most clients are honored to help.
