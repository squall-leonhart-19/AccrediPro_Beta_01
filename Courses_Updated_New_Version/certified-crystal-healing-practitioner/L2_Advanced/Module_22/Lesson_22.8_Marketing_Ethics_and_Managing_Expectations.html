Step 3: Ethical Boundary Check
Elena wants to "stop the Gabapentin." Ethical Action: You must state, "I cannot advise on medication changes. My focus is supporting your mineral foundations. As your mineral status improves, your doctor may choose to adjust your prescriptions based on your progress."

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena asks, "Should I take 50mg of Zinc to help my thyroid?" Given her low ferritin and liver status, what is the ethical response?

The ethical response is: "High-dose zinc can further deplete your copper levels, which are already stressed due to your liver status. Let's focus on whole-food sources and minerals that support your overall balance first. You should discuss specific high-dose supplements with your doctor." (Zinc-copper balance is a clinical priority).

2. If Elena tells you she has stopped her Levothyroxine because she "feels great," what must you do?

You must document this in your session notes and state: "I strongly advise you to contact your prescribing physician immediately regarding this change. My protocol is designed to work alongside your medication, not replace it." You may choose to pause further mineral therapy until she confirms her doctor is aware.

3. Why is Elena's ferritin of 18 a "Red Flag" in this case?

A ferritin below 30 is considered absolute iron deficiency in many clinical circles. Without iron, the enzyme Thyroid Peroxidase cannot function, making her thyroid medication much less effective. This is a "referral trigger" for her to ask her doctor for an iron panel.

4. What is the primary ethical danger of "Dual Relationships" in Elena's case?

Since Elena is a former executive, she might offer you business advice or "connections." Accepting this creates a dual relationship that clouds your clinical judgment and makes it harder to maintain boundaries when she needs firm clinical guidance.

Key Takeaways

  • Scope is Safety: Never advise on medication; focus on the mineral foundations that support the body's natural processes.
  • Referral is Professionalism: Referring a client to an MD for red flags (like ferritin < 20 or jaundice) increases your credibility.
  • Phased Approach: High-sensitivity clients like Elena require a "Low and Slow" mineral approach to avoid detox flares.
  • Documentation: Always record your ethical boundary statements in your client notes to protect your practice and the client.
