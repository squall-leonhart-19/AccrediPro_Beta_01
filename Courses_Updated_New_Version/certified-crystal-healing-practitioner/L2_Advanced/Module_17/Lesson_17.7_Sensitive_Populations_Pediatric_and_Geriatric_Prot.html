Mastering the Extremes

As a practitioner, you will encounter clients whose bio-energetic systems are either in a state of rapid development (pediatric) or gradual decline (geriatric). This lesson provides the clinical framework to adapt your mineral therapy safely and effectively for these sensitive clients, ensuring you provide legitimacy and results without overwhelming their delicate nervous systems.

The Bio-Field at the Extremes of Age

In mineral therapy, we must recognize that the bio-field is not a static construct. It evolves. A 2021 study on bio-field variability across the lifespan suggests that the coherence and amplitude of the human energy field peak in early adulthood and show distinct patterns in childhood and senescence.

Pediatric Field: Highly permeable, expansive, and rapidly fluctuating. Children are "energetic sponges," making them highly responsive but also easily overwhelmed by high-frequency stones like Moldavite or Phenacite.

Geriatric Field: Often contracted, less resilient, and may show signs of "energetic thinning." The goal here is often conservation and stabilization rather than radical transmutation.

Practitioner Insight

For many of you entering this field as a second career, your life experience as mothers or daughters caring for aging parents is your greatest asset. Use that intuitive "nurturer's sense" to gauge when a client needs less intensity. In these populations, less is almost always more.

F: Modifying Frequency Assessment for Children

When performing a Frequency Assessment (F) on a child, the standard hand-scanning technique must be faster and lighter. Children's fields move at a higher "refresh rate."

  • Distance: Scan 12-18 inches away from the body. Their auric field is often more expansive relative to their size.
  • Duration: Limit assessment to 3-5 minutes. Prolonged scanning can make a child restless or over-stimulated.
  • Tactile Feedback: Expect to feel "bubbly" or "electric" sensations. This is normal developmental energy, not necessarily an imbalance.

E: Energy Placement & Mobility Constraints

For geriatric clients, the Energy Placement (E) phase must prioritize comfort and safety. Many elderly clients suffer from arthritis, osteoporosis, or circulatory issues that make lying flat on a massage table difficult or impossible.

C: Soft-Vibration Mineral Selection

In the Crystal Selection (C) phase, we avoid "high-voltage" minerals. We look for stones with monoclinic or triclinic lattice structures, which tend to have a more "soothing" or "irregular" (and thus less demanding) frequency output.

The "Soft" List for Sensitive Fields:

  • Mangano Calcite: A gentler alternative to Rose Quartz for emotional support.
  • Blue Lace Agate: Excellent for cooling the "heat" of pediatric tantrums or geriatric inflammation.
  • Angelite: Provides a sense of safety without the intense "opening" effect of high-vibration crown chakra stones.
  • Lepidolite: High lithium content (naturally occurring) helps stabilize fluctuating moods in both age groups.
Safety Tip

Never use small, tumble-polished stones with children under 5 due to choking hazards. Always use large "palm stones" or secure smaller stones inside a sewn fabric pouch that the child cannot open.

The Proxy Protocol (A & S)

In many pediatric and geriatric cases, the client cannot communicate their needs or maintain the Stabilization (S) phase on their own. This is where the Proxy Protocol‚Äîworking through a caregiver‚Äîbecomes essential.

During Attunement (A), you must attune not only to the client but to the caregiver-client dyad. If a mother is anxious, the child will not stabilize. Your role involves:

  • Providing the caregiver with a "Grounding Stone" (e.g., Shungite) to hold during the session.
  • Teaching the caregiver how to "seal" the child's field using a Selenite wand after you leave.
  • Ensuring the home environment is energetically cleared to support the work done in session.

Safety, Ethics, and Fragile Clients

Working with these populations carries a higher ethical burden. You are often dealing with individuals who cannot give full legal consent (children) or who may have impaired judgment (some elderly clients).

1. Medical Primacy: Never suggest that crystals replace pediatric medication or geriatric life-support. We are complementary practitioners.

2. Physical Integrity: Always have a third party (parent or nurse) present when working with minors or vulnerable adults to protect both the client and your professional reputation.

3. Time Management: A 60-minute session is often too much. For children, 20 minutes is the "sweet spot." For the elderly, 30 minutes is usually sufficient.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is Mangano Calcite often preferred over Rose Quartz for a sensitive 6-year-old?

Mangano Calcite has a "softer" vibration that is less emotionally demanding than Rose Quartz, making it ideal for the rapidly fluctuating and permeable bio-field of a child.

2. What is the primary focus of the Stabilization (S) phase in geriatric clients?

Conservation and stabilization. The goal is to anchor the remaining energy and provide a sense of safety and grounding, rather than attempting radical energetic shifts.

3. What is the "Proxy Protocol"?

It is the practice of involving the caregiver in the healing process, ensuring they are grounded and attuned so they can support the client's energy field between sessions.

4. How should session duration be adjusted for a client with dementia?

Sessions should be shortened to 15-30 minutes to accommodate shorter attention spans and prevent energetic over-stimulation which can lead to increased agitation.
