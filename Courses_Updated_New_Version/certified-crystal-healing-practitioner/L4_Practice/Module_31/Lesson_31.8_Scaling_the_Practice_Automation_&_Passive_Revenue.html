Handling Common Objections

Objections are not "No's." They are requests for more information or a manifestation of the client's fear of change.

The Objection The "Sarah" Response (Confident & Kind)
"It's a lot of money." "I completely understand. It is an investment. But let me ask‚Äîwhat is the cost of not fixing this? How much are you spending on quick fixes that aren't working?"
"I need to talk to my husband." "I love that you support each other in big decisions. When you talk to him, what do you think his main concern will be? Is it the cost, or the belief that crystal healing can actually work for you?"
"I'm too busy right now." "I hear that. But usually, the reason we feel 'too busy' is because our energy is so fragmented. This program is designed to give you back your time by making you more efficient and grounded."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

To build rapport and identify the "tipping point"‚Äîthe specific reason the client is seeking help now rather than six months ago.

2. Why is package pricing superior to hourly billing in a crystal healing practice?

Package pricing focuses on the outcome and transformation rather than the time spent. It increases client commitment and ensures you are paid for your expertise, not just your clock-time.

3. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Acknowledge the importance of the decision, then gently probe to find the real hesitation (money, time, or doubt) by asking, "What specifically do you need to think through?"

4. According to Sarah, what is a practitioner's greatest marketing asset?

Life experience and emotional intelligence. Clients in your age bracket (40-55) value the grounded wisdom and empathy that comes with age.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Enrollment is Healing: If you don't close the sale, the client stays in pain. View the call as a service.
  • Listen More, Talk Less: Aim for a 70/30 split where the prospect does most of the talking.
  • State Prices with a Flat Tone: Don't "up-talk" or sound like you're asking for permission when stating your fee.
  • Focus on Root Causes: Just like with crystals, your business should focus on solving the source of the client's problem, not just the symptoms.
