Mastering the 'Discovery Call'

In the F.A.C.E.T.S.‚Ñ¢ Method, the healing process begins the moment a potential client interacts with you. We view the Discovery Call not as a "sales pitch," but as the initial Frequency Assessment. Your goal is to determine if the client‚Äôs current energetic state and physical needs are a match for your specific mineral therapy expertise.

A high-ticket discovery call (typically 20-30 minutes) should follow a specific energetic arc:

  1. Attunement (0-5 mins): Building rapport and establishing a safe container.
  2. Frequency Assessment (5-15 mins): Asking deep, probing questions about their history, current imbalances, and failed past interventions.
  3. Transmutation (15-20 mins): Reframing their struggle as a vibrational opportunity and presenting the solution.
  4. Commitment/Stabilization (20-30 mins): Inviting them into a container that ensures long-term results.

In a discovery call, the client should be speaking 80% of the time. Your job is to listen for "vibrational leaks"‚Äîareas where they feel powerless, exhausted, or skeptical. Reflect their words back to them to show you have fully assessed their frequency before offering a mineral solution.

The Psychology of the 'Close'

Closing a high-ticket sale is not about pressure; it is about leadership. Many practitioners feel "guilty" asking for thousands of dollars. This is often a symptom of Imposter Syndrome. To overcome this, focus on the cost of the client not taking action.

Use the Stabilization Promise: "If we only do one session, we are just stirring the energy. To anchor this change so it becomes your new normal, we must commit to the Stabilization phase. Are you ready to stop the cycle of temporary fixes and commit to a permanent shift?"

Coach Tip: Silence is Golden

After you state the price of your package, stop talking. The silence that follows is the "Transmutation" space where the client is deciding to invest in themselves. Do not fill the space with justifications. Let them feel the weight of the commitment.

Onboarding & Professional Boundaries

High-ticket clients expect a high-ticket experience. Your onboarding process sets the "Energetic Container." If your onboarding is messy, the client will subconsciously doubt your ability to handle their energy.

Professional Onboarding Protocol:

  1. The Welcome Kit: An immediate email with a "Practitioner-Client Agreement" (legal/ethical boundaries) and a detailed intake form.
  2. The Mineral Preparation: Instructions on how to prepare their field before the first session (e.g., hydration, salt baths).
  3. The Scheduling Anchor: Booking all sessions in the package upfront to ensure the "Stabilization" timeline is honored.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Stabilization" the key psychological hook for closing a high-ticket package?

Because most clients have experienced "temporary fixes" from other modalities. By emphasizing that one-off sessions only "stir the energy" and that long-term commitment is required to "anchor" the change, you position yourself as the solution to their cycle of regression.

2. How should a practitioner respond when a client says, "I can't afford $3,000 right now"?

Do not drop your price. Instead, perform a "Frequency Assessment" on the objection. Ask: "I understand. Is it the total cost that feels heavy, or are you not yet certain that this transformation is the priority for you right now?" This shifts the conversation from money to value and commitment.

3. What is the primary goal of the "Frequency Assessment" phase of a discovery call?

To listen for "vibrational leaks" and determine if the client is a match for your expertise. You are assessing their readiness for change and identifying the core imbalances that your mineral therapy will address.

4. Why is the "Piezoelectric Effect" a valuable tool in sales?

It provides a bridge of scientific legitimacy. By explaining that crystals (like quartz) generate a measurable electrical charge under pressure, you move the conversation from "magic" to "physics," which builds trust with high-ticket, analytical clients.
