2. The 30-Minute Discovery Script

A discovery call is not a therapy session. It is a diagnostic interview to see if you can help and if the client is ready to commit. Use this exact structure.

Phase 1: The Connection (0-5 Minutes)
YOU:
"Hi Deborah! Iâ€™ve been looking forward to our chat. Before we dive into the details, Iâ€™d love to hear a bit about what drew you to Mineral Therapy at this specific time in your life?"

Goal: Let them speak first. Listen for "pain points."

Phase 2: The Deep Dive (5-15 Minutes)
YOU:
"You mentioned the insomnia is affecting your work performance. How long has that been the case? And what have you tried so far that hasn't quite hit the mark?"
YOU:
"If we could fast-forward three months and you were sleeping through the night and feeling completely grounded, what would that change for you?"
Phase 3: The Prescription (15-25 Minutes)
YOU:
"Based on what you've shared, Deborah, you're a perfect candidate for my 8-week 'Resilient Spirit' program. We use specific mineral protocols to address that 'wired but tired' state. Here is how we would structure our sessions..."
Phase 4: The Invitation (25-30 Minutes)
YOU:
"Does that approach resonate with what you're looking for? ... Great. The investment for the 8-week transformation is $1,200. Would you like to start next Tuesday or Thursday?"
Sarah's Insight

The most important part of the script is the silence after you state the price. Do not keep talking. Wait for them to respond. If you talk, you signal that you are uncomfortable with your own value.

3. Handling Common Objections

Objections aren't "no's"â€”they are requests for more information. A 2023 survey of wellness practitioners found that 74% of clients who eventually signed up raised at least one objection during the initial call.

The Objection The "Service-First" Response
"It's too expensive." "I completely understand. It's an investment. Tell me, is it the total amount, or is it a matter of fitting it into your monthly budget? I do offer payment plans to make this accessible."
"I need to think about it." "Of course. This is an important commitment to yourself. What specifically do you need to sit with? Is it the time commitment, or are you wondering if this approach will work for your specific situation?"
"I need to ask my husband." "I respect that. How do you think he'll feel about you finally getting the relief you've been looking for? Would it help if I sent you a summary of our plan that you can share with him?"

As a career changer, you might feel the urge to "discount" your way into your first few clients. Resist this. Low prices often attract clients who are less committed to the work. Professional pricing signals professional results.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Deep Dive" phase of the discovery call?

The goal is to understand the client's "pain points," what they have tried before, and what their life would look like if the problem was solved. This builds the "bridge" between their current state and your solution.

2. How should you respond when a client says, "I need to ask my husband"?

Acknowledge and respect the boundary, then ask how the partner would feel about them getting relief. Offer to provide a written summary of the plan to facilitate a professional conversation at home.

3. Why is the "silence" after stating your price so critical?

It demonstrates professional confidence. Talking immediately after stating a price often leads to "over-explaining" or pre-emptive discounting, which lowers your perceived value.

4. According to the income scenarios, how many clients per month are typically needed to replace a standard professional salary?

Typically, 4 to 6 clients per month on a premium package model ($1,200 - $1,500 range) is enough to meet or exceed most mid-level professional salaries while working part-time hours.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery calls are a service, not a high-pressure sales tactic.
  • Focus on the transformation (the result), not just the crystals (the tool).
  • Professional pricing attracts committed clients and prevents practitioner burnout.
  • Handle objections with empathy and inquiry rather than defensiveness.
  • Consistency in your discovery call structure leads to predictable business growth.
