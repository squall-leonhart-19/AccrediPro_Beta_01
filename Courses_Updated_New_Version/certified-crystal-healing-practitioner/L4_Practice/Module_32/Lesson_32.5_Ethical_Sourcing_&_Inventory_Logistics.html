High-Value Inventory Systems for Professional Placement

A professional practitioner does not just have a "collection"; they have clinical inventory. High-value specimens used for Energy Placement (such as large Lemurian Seed Crystals or museum-grade Malachite) must be tracked as business assets.

Digital Tracking vs. Physical Tagging

For a practitioner seeing 10+ clients a week, memory is not a reliable system. You must implement a SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) system. Each stone in your professional kit should have a corresponding entry in your CMS (Client Management System) or a dedicated spreadsheet including:

  • Purchase Date & Cost: For tax depreciation and insurance purposes.
  • Mineralogical Data: Specific gravity, Mohs hardness, and chemical formula (e.g., SiO2 for Quartz).
  • Energetic History: When it was last used and what protocols it was involved in.

Just like a nurse rotates surgical tools, you should rotate your "active" healing stones. Use a "Rest Period" of 48 hours for any stone used in a heavy Transmutation session before it is returned to the general inventory.

Provenance Documentation & Client Transparency

One of the ways to overcome "imposter syndrome" and establish yourself as an expert is through Scientific Documentation. When you select a stone for a client, providing them with a "Mineral Provenance Card" elevates the experience from "mystical" to "professional mineral therapy."

A professional Provenance Card should include:

  • Origin: (e.g., Minas Gerais, Brazil).
  • Chemical Composition: (e.g., Copper Carbonate Hydroxide for Malachite).
  • Lattice System: (e.g., Monoclinic).
  • Ethical Seal: A brief statement on the sourcing standards of your practice.
