    Optimizing Scheduling for Energy Work

    One of the biggest mistakes new practitioners make is scheduling clients "back-to-back." In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, the Attunement (A) and Stabilization (S) phases require the practitioner to be in a high-vibrational, clear state. If you are rushing to clear the room from the previous client, your own frequency will be compromised.

    The "Buffer" Rule: Professional systems allow you to set "Buffer Times." For crystal healing, a minimum of 30 minutes is required between sessions. This time is used for:

    1. Clearing the mineral tools used in the previous session.
    2. Smudging or sound-clearing the physical space.
    3. Practitioner self-attunement (grounding and centering).
    4. Briefly reviewing the next client's digital Frequency Assessment.
    Coach Tip: Protecting Your Field

    Never let a client book a session without a 24-hour lead time. This allows you to energetically prepare for their specific frequency. Most CMS tools have a "Minimum Scheduling Notice" setting‚Äîuse it to protect your energetic boundaries!

    Automated Follow-ups: Monitoring Transmutation

    The Transmutation (T) phase of the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ continues long after the client leaves your table. Often, the most significant shifts happen 48-72 hours post-session as the bio-field integrates the new frequencies.

    • A link to book their follow-up "Integration Call" or next session.
