Formalizing Affiliate & Marketing Structures

Strategic alliances are built on reciprocity. A formal affiliate structure ensures that both parties are incentivized to promote the partnership. This goes beyond a simple "handshake" deal.

Professional Cross-Promotion includes:

  • The "Warm Handoff": The partner mentions your service during their consultation as a necessary next step.
  • Digital Co-Branding: Featuring the partner's logo on your "Partners" page and vice versa.
  • Exclusive Referral Credits: Providing the partner's clients with a 10% discount, while the partner receives a referral fee or "marketing credit" toward their own sessions.
Coach Tip

Always track your referrals using a simple CRM or spreadsheet. Data is your best leverage when renegotiating revenue splits in the future.

Legal Frameworks & Revenue-Sharing Models

To maintain your professional standing, your partnerships must be governed by clear legal agreements. This protects your brand and your income.

Key Components of a B2B Agreement:

  • Scope of Practice: Clearly stating that you are a Certified Crystal Healing Practitioner and not a medical doctor.
  • Revenue Split vs. Room Rental: Deciding between a percentage of sales (common for new partnerships) or a flat monthly fee (better for established practitioners).
  • Liability Insurance: Ensuring your professional liability insurance covers you while working at a third-party location.
  • Client Ownership: Defining who "owns" the client data if the partnership dissolves.
Coach Tip

Never start a B2B partnership without a signed "Memorandum of Understanding" (MOU). It keeps the relationship professional and prevents misunderstandings about money.
