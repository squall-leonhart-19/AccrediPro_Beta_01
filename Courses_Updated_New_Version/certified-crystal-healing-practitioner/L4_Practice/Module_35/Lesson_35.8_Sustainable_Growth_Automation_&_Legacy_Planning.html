Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between selling a "session" and selling a "program"?

A session sells time and a temporary feeling; a program sells a specific outcome or transformation. Scaling requires focusing on the end result for the client.

2. When Martha says, "I've tried crystals before and didn't feel much," how should you respond?

Acknowledge her experience, then pivot to your professional methodology. Explain that your work isn't about "feeling" a stone, but about a structured protocol and frequency entrainment over time.

3. According to the "Scaling Ladder," which model offers the highest revenue with the most strategic effort?

The Authority model, which focuses on group experiences (retreats) and digital assets (courses), decoupling income from hourly labor.

4. Why is "silence" a powerful tool after stating your program price?

It demonstrates confidence and gives the prospect space to process the investment. Breaking the silence often leads to "discounting" yourself out of nervous energy.
