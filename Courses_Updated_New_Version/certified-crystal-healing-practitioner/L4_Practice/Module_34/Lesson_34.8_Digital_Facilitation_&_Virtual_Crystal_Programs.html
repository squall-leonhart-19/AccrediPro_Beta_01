Linda loved 1:1 sessions but felt she was "trading hours for dollars" and hitting a ceiling of $3,000/month. She launched a 4-week "Mineral Mindfulness" group program for local teachers.

Linda's success came from Niche Marketing. She didn't just offer a "crystal class"; she offered a class specifically for stressed teachers. Always speak to a specific pain point!

Check Your Understanding

1. When pitching to a venue owner, what is the most effective way to establish professional legitimacy?

Focus on the science-based aspects of Mineral Therapy, such as piezoelectric properties and nervous system regulation, rather than abstract "healing" claims.

2. If a group program attendee asks if they will get enough personal attention, how should you respond?

Reassure them by mentioning your strict capacity limits (e.g., capping at 10-12 people) and explaining that the structure includes individual check-ins for stone placement and Q&A.

3. What is the primary financial benefit of a "split" (70/30) with a venue instead of a flat rental?

It lowers your "upfront" risk. If fewer people show up, you aren't stuck paying a high rental fee. It also incentivizes the venue owner to help you market the event.

4. Why did Linda's "Stressed Teacher" program succeed so well in the case study?

Because she chose a specific niche with a shared pain point. Niche marketing allows you to speak directly to the client's experience, making the "sale" feel like an invitation to a solution.

Key Takeaways

  • Leverage the 1:Many Model: Group programs allow you to increase your hourly rate significantly while serving more people.
  • Lead with Professionalism: Use the scientific terminology of Mineral Therapy to differentiate yourself from "hobbyist" crystal healers.
  • Niche is King: Workshops for "everyone" are hard to sell. Workshops for "new moms," "nurses," or "entrepreneurs" sell themselves.
  • Tier Your Pricing: Always include the cost of materials (mineral sets) in your ticket price to increase the perceived value.
  • Build the Ladder: Use low-cost workshops to introduce clients to your high-ticket 1:1 or group coaching programs.
