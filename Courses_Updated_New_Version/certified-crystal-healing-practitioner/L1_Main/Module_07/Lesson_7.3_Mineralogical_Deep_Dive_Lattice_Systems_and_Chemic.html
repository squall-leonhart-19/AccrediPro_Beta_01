Mohs Scale & Energetic Density Matching

Hardness in mineralogy is measured by the Mohs Scale (1 to 10). In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we match the hardness of the mineral to the "density" of the energetic blockage identified during the Frequency Assessment (F) phase.

  • Low Hardness (Mohs 1-3): Stones like Selenite or Talc. Used for subtle field smoothing and "ethereal" blockages. Their structure is easily disrupted, making them "soft" transmitters.
  • Medium Hardness (Mohs 4-6): Stones like Fluorite or Lapis Lazuli. Used for emotional integration and mental clarity.
  • High Hardness (Mohs 7-10): Stones like Quartz, Topaz, or Sapphire. Used for chronic, calcified blockages or deep physical anchoring. Their rigid lattice provides a "piercing" frequency that can break through stagnant patterns.
Coach Tip: Selection Strategy

If a client has a "stubborn" pattern (like a 20-year habit), don't start with Selenite. Use a high-Mohs stone like Clear Quartz or Garnet to provide the necessary "vibrational force" to initiate a shift.
