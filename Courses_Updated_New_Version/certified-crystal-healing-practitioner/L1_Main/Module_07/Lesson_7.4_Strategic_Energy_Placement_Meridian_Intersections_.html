Anatomical Considerations: Organs and Endocrine Glands

As a professional practitioner, you must understand the biological interface. Crystals placed on the body interact with the piezoelectric nature of bone and the electromagnetic field of the heart and brain.

  • Thyroid/Parathyroid: Use gentle, cooling stones (Blue Lace Agate) over the throat. Avoid heavy or sharp stones here.
  • Adrenals: Located on the mid-back (above kidneys). Placing grounding stones like Hematite here during the Stabilization (S) phase can help "calm" the HPA-axis (stress response).
  • Pancreas/Liver: Be mindful of the Solar Plexus placement. Use Citrine for the pancreas to support metabolic "sweetness" and Bloodstone over the liver for detoxification support.
Coach Tip: Weight and Pressure

Always ask: "Is the weight of this stone comfortable?" A heavy Jasper on the stomach may cause physical tension, which triggers a "fight or flight" response, negating the energetic work. When in doubt, use smaller stones or place them on the table adjacent to the body.

Directional Placement: In-Flow vs. Out-Flow

The geometry of the crystal itself dictates how energy moves. In advanced F.A.C.E.T.S. application, we use Terminated Points (crystals with a natural point) as directional tools.

1. Charging (In-Flow): Point the termination toward the body or the specific chakra. This "injects" the mineral's frequency into the client's field. Use this for depletion, fatigue, or when building up a weak energy center.

2. Extraction (Out-Flow): Point the termination away from the body. This "draws out" stagnant, heavy, or over-active energy. This is essential during the Transmutation (T) phase for clearing trauma or inflammation.

Coach Tip: Double Terminated (DT) Crystals

DT crystals (points at both ends) are the "holy grail" for Bridging. They allow energy to flow in both directions simultaneously, creating a state of homeostasis rather than just charging or clearing. They are excellent for the "S" (Stabilization) phase.
