Radiesthetic Diagnosis: The Pendulum

While hand scanning provides a broad overview, the pendulum serves as a diagnostic tool for precision. In a clinical setting, we use the pendulum not for "fortune telling," but as a bio-feedback mechanism that amplifies the subconscious micro-tremors of the practitioner‚Äôs hand in response to the client‚Äôs field.

The Diagnostic Protocol

Hold the pendulum 2-3 inches above each primary chakra. Observe the Direction, Diameter, and Velocity of the swing. A healthy, balanced chakra typically displays a consistent clockwise circle of moderate diameter (approx. 2-4 inches).

  • Clockwise Swing: Open, receptive, and processing energy effectively.
  • Counter-Clockwise Swing: Defensive, closed, or pushing energy away (often seen in trauma).
  • Back-and-Forth (Linear): Partial block; energy is moving but not circulating.
  • No Movement: Significant stagnation or "frozen" state.

Identifying "Leaks" vs. "Blocks"

Understanding the difference between a leak and a block is critical for the Transmutation (T) phase of the healing session. A 2021 study on bio-field variations suggested that "areas of low potential" (leaks) and "areas of high impedance" (blocks) require diametrically opposed mineral interventions.

1. Energetic Leaks

A leak occurs when the auric field is compromised, usually due to trauma, surgery, or extreme grief. It feels like a cool breeze or a vacuum pulling at your hand. Clients with leaks often feel "drained," "scattered," or "not fully here."

2. Energetic Blocks

A block is a localized area of stagnant frequency. It feels like resistance, thickness, or push-back against your hand. It is often the result of repressed emotions or chronic physical tension. Clients with blocks often feel "stuck," "heavy," or "frustrated."
