Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-Explaining: New practitioners often talk too much to compensate for imposter syndrome. Give the science, then allow for silence.
  • Being Vague: Avoid saying "this stone is good for vibes." Instead, say "this stone's iron content makes it an ideal tool for grounding sessions."
  • Ignoring Physical Comfort: Ensure Diane is warm and supported with bolsters before you begin the layout. Professionalism is found in the details.
  • Rushing the Intake: The first 15 minutes of the session (the intake) are where the healing actually begins through the power of being heard.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary scientific concept used to explain how a client's energy "syncs" with a crystal?

The concept is Entrainment. This is a principle of physics where two oscillating systems (like a crystal's lattice and a human biofield) naturally synchronize to the more stable frequency.

2. Why is it beneficial to mention a stone's mineral content (like iron in Hematite) to a client?

It provides clinical legitimacy. Connecting the physical properties of the stone to its energetic use helps bridge the gap for skeptical clients and demonstrates your expertise as a Certified Practitioner.

3. If Diane feels "scattered," why is the Grounding Grid placed primarily at the feet and lower body?

In mineral therapy, we use high-density, dark-colored stones at the lower extremities to "pull" excess energy downward, mimicking the earth's gravitational pull and helping the client feel physically rooted.

4. What is the most effective way to handle a client's skepticism during the intake?

Acknowledge it with warmth, then pivot to the physics-based explanation. Using analogies like the "tuning fork" helps demystify the process and builds trust.
