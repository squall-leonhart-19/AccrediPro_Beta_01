The Geometry of Vibration

Every crystal belongs to one of seven basic "systems" or families based on its internal geometric arrangement, known as a crystal lattice. This lattice isn't just a static structure; it is a three-dimensional blueprint that determines how the mineral interacts with light, heat, and bio-energetic frequencies.

A 2021 study on crystalline structures (n=1,200 samples) confirmed that the symmetry of a lattice directly impacts its refractive index and vibrational stability. In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we view the lattice as the "routing system" for the energy we are introducing to the client's field.

The Cubic System: The Great Stabilizer

The Cubic (or Isometric) system is the most symmetrical. Imagine a cube where all sides are equal and all angles are 90 degrees. This perfect symmetry creates a highly stable, grounding frequency.

Common Minerals: Pyrite, Fluorite, Garnet, Diamond.

  • Vibrational Signature: Stability, structure, and foundation.
  • Energy Direction: Equal in all directions (omnidirectional). It creates a "buffer zone" of protection.
  • Best Use: The Stabilization (S) phase. Use Cubic stones to anchor a shift after a deep transmutation.

Linear vs. Diffused Energy Flow

The internal lattice dictates whether the energy emitted by the crystal is Linear (moving in a straight line) or Diffused (spreading out like a mist).

Linear Flow (Hexagonal/Trigonal): Best for "unblocking" meridians or Nadis. If the Frequency Assessment shows a complete blockage (zero flow), a linear stone acts like a needle to pierce through the stagnation.

Diffused Flow (Orthorhombic/Monoclinic): Stones like Topaz (Orthorhombic) or Selenite (Monoclinic) provide a "wash" of energy. This is ideal for Bio-Field Smoothing during the Attunement phase, where you want to gently bathe the client's field rather than target a specific point.
