Common Pitfalls & Success Markers

4 Mistakes New Coaches Make with Mineralogy

  • Over-Explaining: Giving a 20-minute lecture on geology instead of connecting it to the client's problem.
  • Being Vague: Saying "It just has good energy" when a client like Elena wants to hear about "molecular symmetry."
  • Faking Knowledge: If you don't know the crystal system, don't guess! Say, "Let me double-check the mineralogical record for that specific specimen." (This actually increases your professional credibility!)
  • Ignoring the Physical: Forgetting to let the client feel the weight and texture of the stone while you explain its density.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the Cubic (Isometric) system particularly useful for a client like Elena who feels "unmoored"?

The Cubic system is the most symmetrical and stable of all crystal systems. It provides a geometric "anchor" or foundation, representing structure and organization‚Äîexactly what a client in transition needs.

2. What is the risk of using too much "woo-woo" language with a former science teacher?

It creates a "credibility gap." If the client feels the practitioner doesn't respect physical laws or science, they will struggle to trust the practitioner's advice on emotional or spiritual matters.

3. How does the Hexahedron relate to the Cubic crystal system?

The Hexahedron is the Platonic Solid (Sacred Geometry) associated with the Cube. It is the geometric archetype of the Cubic system, representing the element of Earth and grounding.

4. What should you do if a client asks a technical mineralogy question you can't answer?

Remain professional. State that you want to provide the most accurate data and will consult the mineralogical records/database and get back to them. This demonstrates academic integrity.
