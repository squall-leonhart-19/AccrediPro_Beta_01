The Physics of Pressure: Piezoelectricity Explained

The term "piezoelectricity" comes from the Greek word piezein, which means to squeeze or press. Discovered by the Curie brothers in 1880, this effect describes the ability of certain materials‚Äîmost notably Quartz‚Äîto generate an electric charge in response to applied mechanical stress.

In a Quartz crystal, the atoms are arranged in a highly symmetrical, yet non-centrosymmetric lattice. When you apply pressure (squeeze the crystal), the internal arrangement of positive and negative ions shifts. This displacement creates an electrical dipole, resulting in a measurable voltage across the crystal's surface.
