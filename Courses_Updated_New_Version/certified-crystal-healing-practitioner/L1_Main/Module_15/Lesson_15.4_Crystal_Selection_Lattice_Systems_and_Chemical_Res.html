Hardness and Amplitude: The Mohs Scale

In professional mineral therapy, the Mohs Scale of Hardness (1-10) is used to determine the "vibrational velocity" of a stone. Hardness is a result of the strength of the atomic bonds.

High Hardness (7-10): Stones like Quartz, Topaz, and Diamond have tightly packed atomic structures. Their energy is "high velocity," sharp, and focused. Use these when you need to "break through" stagnant patterns or provide a laser-like focus during the Transmutation phase.

Low Hardness (2-4): Stones like Selenite, Calcite, and Fluorite have looser atomic bonds. Their energy is "low velocity," diffuse, and gentle. These are ideal for the Attunement phase or for sensitive clients (children, elderly, or those with acute trauma) where a high-intensity frequency would be overwhelming.

Safety Protocol

Always check the Mohs scale before cleansing. Stones with a hardness of 5 or below (like Selenite or Malachite) are often water-soluble or easily damaged. Use "Dry Cleansing" methods (breath, sound, or smoke) for these minerals to preserve their physical and energetic integrity.
