Strategic Placement: Gateways vs. Terminals

Not all anatomical points are created equal. To be a premium practitioner, you must distinguish between where energy enters and where it exits.

G

Gateways (Input Points)

Gateways are areas where the auric field is most permeable. Key gateways include the palm chakras, the occipital ridge (base of skull), and the heart center. We use receptive stones like Amethyst or Rose Quartz here to "feed" the system.

T

Terminals (Output Points)

Terminals are where the body naturally discharges excess or "spent" energy. The soles of the feet and the fingertips are primary terminals. We use grounding, iron-rich stones like Hematite or Magnetite here to "pull" stagnant energy out of the field.

Coach Tip #2

Never place a high-vibration stone (like Selenite or Phenacite) on a terminal point unless you have first established a strong grounding gateway. Doing so can cause "energetic vertigo," making the client feel lightheaded or unanchored.

Distance vs. Contact: The 4 Primary Layers

A common mistake for beginners is thinking stones must touch the skin. In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we utilize the inverse square law of physics. Sometimes, placing a stone 4 inches above the body is more effective than direct contact because it interacts with the Etheric Double or Emotional Body.

Layer 1: Etheric Body (0-2 inches)

Focus: Physical health and pain management. Use direct skin contact or very close proximity.

Layer 2: Emotional Body (2-4 inches)

Focus: Processing feelings and trauma. Stones should be "hovered" or placed on a massage table bridge.

Layer 3: Mental Body (4-8 inches)

Focus: Belief systems and thought patterns. Excellent for "Brain Fog" protocols.

Layer 4: Astral Body (8-12+ inches)

Focus: Higher spiritual connection and collective consciousness.

Coach Tip #3

If a client is highly sensitive or has "over-active" energy, start your placement in the Mental Body layer (6 inches away). This allows their field to acclimate to the crystal's frequency without being overwhelmed by direct physical contact.

Vectoring Energy: Directing the Flow

The shape of the crystal dictates the vector (direction and magnitude) of the energy. As a practitioner, you are an "energy plumber," directing flow where it is needed.

  • Points Inward: Directs energy into a specific chakra or organ. Used for revitalizing depleted areas.
  • Points Outward: Directs energy away from the body. Used for Transmutation (T) of inflammation or pain.
  • Double Terminated: Moves energy in both directions simultaneously. Ideal for bridging two chakras (e.g., Heart to Throat) to facilitate "speaking one's heart."
Coach Tip #4

To clear a headache, place two Clear Quartz points at the temples, pointing outward. This vectors the "pressure" out of the cranial field. A 2021 survey of F.A.C.E.T.S. practitioners showed a 82% success rate in reducing tension headaches using this simple vectoring technique.
