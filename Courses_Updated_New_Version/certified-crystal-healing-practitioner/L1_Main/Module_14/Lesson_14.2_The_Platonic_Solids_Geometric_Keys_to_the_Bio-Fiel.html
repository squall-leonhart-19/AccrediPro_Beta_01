The Geometry of Creation: Why Platonic Solids Matter

Named after the Greek philosopher Plato, who theorized that these shapes formed the basis of the physical world, the Platonic Solids are the only five regular polyhedra possible in three-dimensional space. To qualify as a Platonic Solid, a shape must have:

  • Identical faces: Every face must be the same regular polygon.
  • Identical vertices: The same number of faces must meet at every corner.
  • Equal angles: All interior angles must be congruent.

In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we view these shapes as vibrational templates. Because they are perfectly symmetrical, they emit a highly coherent frequency. When a client's bio-field is "jagged" or incoherent due to stress or trauma, introducing these perfect geometric templates helps the field entrain back to a state of equilibrium.
