Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Watch out for these four habits that can break the "attunement loop" between you and your client:

  • The "Healer's Ego": Telling the client exactly what they are feeling instead of asking. (e.g., "You're angry," vs "I'm sensing some heat here, how does that area feel to you?")
  • Cold Hands: Always rub your palms together to activate your own minor palm chakras before starting the scan. Physical warmth translates to energetic receptivity.
  • Over-Explaining: Using too much "woo-woo" jargon with a beginner. Use scientific terms like biofield or electromagnetic frequency to provide legitimacy.
  • Holding Your Breath: If you stop breathing, your energy stops moving. Your breath is the pump for the entire attunement process.

Success Markers

How do you know the attunement went well? Look for these physical and energetic cues:

The Parasympathetic Shift

The client takes a deep, spontaneous "sigh" or their stomach growls (a sign of the nervous system moving into 'Rest and Digest').

The client reports that their arms or legs feel very heavy or like they are "sinking" into the table.

Pupil Dilation

When the client opens their eyes, their pupils are slightly dilated and their gaze is soft, indicating a shift in brainwave state (Alpha/Theta).

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks, "What if I don't feel anything during the attunement?" What is the most professional response?

Reassure them that "not feeling anything" is a common and valid experience. Explain that the crystals are working at a subtle frequency and that deep relaxation is often the first sign of successful attunement.

2. Why should you rub your hands together before starting a field scan?

This physically warms the hands and energetically "wakes up" the palm chakras, increasing your sensitivity to the client's biofield.

3. What is "Bridge Language" in the context of crystal healing?

Using scientific or grounded terminology (like biofield, frequency, or nervous system) to explain energetic concepts to clients who may be skeptical of spiritual jargon.

4. If you sense a "hot spot" in the client's field, what should you do?

Note the location, remain calm, and use a cooling or grounding crystal (like Selenite or Black Tourmaline) to help vent or stabilize that excess energy.
