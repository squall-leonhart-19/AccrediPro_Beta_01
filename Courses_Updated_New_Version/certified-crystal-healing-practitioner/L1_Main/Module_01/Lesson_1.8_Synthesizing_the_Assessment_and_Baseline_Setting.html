Common Mistakes & Success Markers

The Mistake Why It Fails The Professional Correction
Over-explaining the science Bores the client; loses the emotional connection. Use 1-2 simple analogies (like the tuning fork).
Using "Maybe" or "I think" Undermines your authority as a practitioner. Use confident phrases: "What we see here is..." or "The resonance indicates..."
Ignoring the client's physical symptoms Makes the work feel "un-grounded" or irrelevant. Always tie the frequency back to their real-life stress or fatigue.
Rushing the intake Client feels like a "number" rather than a person. Hold space for 5-10 minutes of pure listening before assessing.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use analogies like "radio stations" or "tuning forks" with clients like Elena?

Analogies bridge the gap between complex energetic concepts and the client's lived experience, making the "invisible" work of frequency feel tangible and logical.

2. What is "Reflective Listening" in a coaching context?

Reflective listening is the practice of repeating back the client's own words and feelings to ensure they feel understood and to confirm your assessment is accurate.

3. If a client asks "Is this just a placebo effect?", what is a professional response?

"While the mind is a powerful tool, mineral therapy is based on the principle of resonance‚Äîhow a stable frequency (the crystal) interacts with a variable one (your biofield). It's about physics as much as it is about intention."

4. What is a "Success Marker" in a Frequency Assessment session?

A success marker is when the client reports a shift in their "felt sense"‚Äîsuch as feeling lighter, more grounded, or having a visible reduction in physical tension.
