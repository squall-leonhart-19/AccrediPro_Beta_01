Geometric Flow: Hexagonal vs. Trigonal Systems

The internal lattice structure of a crystal acts as a waveguide for energy. In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we use geometry to dictate the direction and intensity of the energy we are moving during a session.

The Hexagonal System (The Expander)

Minerals like Apatite or Beryl (Emerald/Aquamarine) belong to the hexagonal system. These structures have a six-fold symmetry that allows energy to radiate outward in all directions simultaneously. This is ideal for Diffusion Strategies (Lesson 5.4), where we want to break up a large, stagnant pattern and spread it thin so the body can process it.

The Trigonal System (The Laser)

Minerals like Quartz, Tourmaline, and Calcite are trigonal. While they look similar to hexagonal stones, their energy flow is more focused and "spiraled." Trigonal structures are the workhorses of Absorption Techniques (Lesson 5.3). They act like a vacuum, pulling energy into a central axis and transmuting it through the crystal's internal spiral.

Mohs Hardness: Physical vs. Etheric Stabilization

The Mohs Scale of Mineral Hardness isn't just for jewelry safety; it's a proxy for vibrational density. In our practice, we use hardness to determine which "layer" of the bio-field we are targeting.

High Mohs (7-10): These stones (Quartz, Topaz, Sapphire) interact most strongly with the physical body and the etheric double. They are "hard" anchors. Use these for physical pain or structural stabilization.

Low Mohs (2-4): These stones (Selenite, Calcite, Fluorite) interact with the emotional and mental bodies. They are "soft" and fluid. Use these for transmuting feelings or racing thoughts.

Coach Tip

A frequent mistake is using a "soft" stone (like Selenite, Mohs 2) to ground a client who is physically dizzy. Selenite is too high-vibration and physically "soft" for this. Use a "hard" stone like Hematite (Mohs 5.5-6.5) or Smoky Quartz (Mohs 7) for physical grounding.
