Directional Flow: Vectors and Wands

Crystals are not passive; they are directional conductors. The termination (point) of a crystal determines the vector of energy flow. This is a critical component of the "E" in F.A.C.E.T.S.‚Ñ¢.

  • Inward Flow (Point toward the body): Used for supplementing a deficiency. If a client is depleted, point the crystal toward the chakra to "feed" the center.

Safety & Sensitivity Protocols

Professionalism requires clinical safety. As a practitioner, you are responsible for the physical comfort and integrity of your client's body.

Clinical Safety Checklist

Weight: Never place heavy clusters (over 1lb) directly on the abdomen or throat without a protective barrier or bolster. This can trigger the "fight or flight" response due to perceived physical threat.

Temperature: Stones like Hematite or Obsidian can retain cold. Ensure stones are at least room temperature (approx. 72¬∞F) before skin contact.

Skin Integrity: Avoid placing stones on broken skin, rashes, or surgical scars unless they are wrapped in sterile silk. Some minerals contain trace elements (like Copper in Malachite) that should not interact with open wounds.

Coach Tip: The Silk Barrier

Keep a supply of small, organic silk squares. Placing silk between the stone and the skin doesn't block the frequency, but it does provide a premium, hypoallergenic barrier that prevents "stone sliding" and protects clients with sensitive skin.
