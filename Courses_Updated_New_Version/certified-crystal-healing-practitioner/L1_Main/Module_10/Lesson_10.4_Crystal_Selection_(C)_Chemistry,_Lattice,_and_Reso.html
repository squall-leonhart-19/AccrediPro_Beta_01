The Chemistry of Vibration: Silicates vs. Carbonates

Every crystal is a chemical compound. The way these atoms bond determines how the crystal interacts with the human bio-field. In a 2022 study on mineral-dielectric constants, researchers noted that the chemical composition of a mineral directly affects its permittivity‚Äîits ability to store and transmit electrical energy.

Silicates: The Masters of Conduction

Over 90% of the Earth's crust is made of Silicates (minerals containing Silicon and Oxygen). From a practitioner's perspective, silicates like Quartz (SiO2) are the "hard drive" of the mineral world. Their high silicon content makes them exceptional conductors of the piezoelectric effect, meaning they can transform mechanical pressure (or bio-electric stress) into a clear frequency.

Carbonates: The Softeners

Carbonates, such as Calcite or Malachite, contain the (CO3) group. These minerals are generally softer (Mohs 3-4) and react chemically with acids. Energetically, they are "absorbers" rather than "projectors." They are ideal for the Transmutation (T) phase of the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢ because they help break down rigid energetic patterns.

Practitioner Insight

When working with clients experiencing high inflammation or "hot" energetic states, avoid high-silica stones initially. Start with Carbonates or Halides (like Fluorite) to soothe the field before introducing the high-intensity amplification of Quartz.
