Chemical Signatures: The Alchemy of Minerals

The chemical composition of a stone provides the "nutrition" for the bio-field. When we place a stone on the body, the trace elements and chemical bonds interact with our own biological chemistry via the piezoelectric effect and ion exchange.

1. Silicates (The Builders)

Comprising over 90% of the Earth's crust, silicates (like Quartz and Mica) are rich in Silicon and Oxygen. They are the "conductors" of the mineral world. In healing, they provide structural support and facilitate the flow of information across the nervous system.

2. Oxides (The Protectors)

Oxides are minerals where a metal is bonded with oxygen (e.g., Hematite, Magnetite). These are often heavy and iron-rich. They are essential for Stabilization (the 'S' in FACETS) because they ground the bio-field into the physical body‚Äôs circulatory system.

3. Carbonates (The Releasers)

Minerals like Calcite and Malachite contain the carbonate group (CO3). These minerals are chemically "soft" and often react with acids. Energetically, they are used to break down stagnant emotional patterns and "dissolve" blockages in the auric field.

Hardness & Vibrational Intensity: The Mohs Scale

In Level 1 practice, the Mohs Scale of Mineral Hardness (1-10) is a critical safety and selection tool. It doesn't just tell you if a stone will scratch; it tells you about the density of the atomic bonds.

  • Low Hardness (1-4): These stones (like Selenite or Fluorite) have "loose" atomic bonds. Their energy is gentle, fast-moving, and easily absorbed. Use these for sensitive clients or subtle emotional work.
  • Medium Hardness (5-6): Stones like Lapis Lazuli or Rhodonite. These provide a balance of stability and movement, ideal for general balancing sessions.
  • High Hardness (7-10): Stones like Quartz, Topaz, and Sapphire. These have incredibly tight atomic bonds. They are high-intensity "amplifiers." Use these when significant Transmutation is required, but be cautious with clients who are prone to over-stimulation.
