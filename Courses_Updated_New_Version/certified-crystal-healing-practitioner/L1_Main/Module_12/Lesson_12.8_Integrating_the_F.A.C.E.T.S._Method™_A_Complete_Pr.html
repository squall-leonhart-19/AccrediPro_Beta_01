The Power of Silence

After you explain a mineral protocol, stop talking. Count to three in your head. Give Diane space to absorb the information. New coaches often talk too much when they're nervous. Silence is where the client's "Aha!" moments happen.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using "I think" or "Maybe": These are confidence killers. Instead of "I think this might help," say "Based on your symptoms, I recommend this protocol."
  • Over-explaining the Chemistry: While we use science to ground the practice, don't give a 20-minute lecture on geology. Keep the focus on the client's feeling.
  • Ignoring the "Skeptical Eye": If a client looks doubtful, acknowledge it! "I can see you're a bit skeptical, and that's actually greatâ€”it means you're paying attention to what feels true for you."
  • Rushing the Intake: Don't jump to the crystals in the first 5 minutes. Spend at least 15 minutes listening to the client's story.
