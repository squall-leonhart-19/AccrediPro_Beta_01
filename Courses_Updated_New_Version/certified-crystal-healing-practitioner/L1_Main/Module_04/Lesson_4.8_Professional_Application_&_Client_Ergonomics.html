Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to narrate your movements during a placement session?

It prevents the startle response, maintains a sense of safety, and keeps the client informed so they can stay in a relaxed, receptive state.

2. What should you do if a client says a grounding stone feels "uncomfortably cold" or "heavy"?

Immediately remove or adjust the stone. The client's physical and energetic comfort always takes precedence over the "standard" layout.

3. What is the primary purpose of the "Three-Point Grounding" layout used in the scenario?

To anchor the client's energy, reduce feelings of being "scattered" or "floating," and help them feel more present in their physical body.

4. How should you respond if a client says they "don't feel anything" during the session?

Validate their experience by explaining that there is no "right" way to feel and that the stones are working on a subtle energetic level regardless of immediate physical sensation.
