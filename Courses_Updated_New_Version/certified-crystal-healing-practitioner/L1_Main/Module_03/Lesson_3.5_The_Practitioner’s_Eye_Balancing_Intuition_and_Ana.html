Removing Practitioner Bias: Data-First Selection

As practitioners, we are human. We have favorite colors, stones we find personally comforting, and minerals we are currently "working with" in our own lives. This is known as projection bias. If you are currently feeling stressed, you may subconsciously reach for Amethyst for every client, regardless of their specific bio-field needs.

To remove this bias, we use the data gathered in Module 1 (Frequency Assessment). Before touching a single crystal, you must review your assessment notes:

  • Auric Density: Is the field congested or depleted?
  • Chakra Velocity: Which centers are hypo-active (under-functioning)?
  • Tactile Heat: Where did you feel thermal spikes during the scan?
Coach Tip

Always perform your Frequency Assessment before you look at your crystal cabinet. If you select the crystals first, you will subconsciously "find" the imbalances that match those crystals during your scan. Let the client's body speak first.

The Kinesiology Test: Bio-Feedback Verification

Applied Kinesiology (muscle testing) is a method of using the body's nervous system as a binary (Yes/No) feedback loop. In the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢, we use this as a secondary verification tool after we have narrowed our selection to 2-3 candidates.

The O-Ring Method for Practitioners

  1. Form a strong circle with your thumb and index finger (non-dominant hand).
  2. Hold the candidate crystal in your dominant hand (or place it on the client's sternum).
  3. Attempt to pull the "O-Ring" apart with the thumb and index finger of your other hand.
  4. Resonance (Yes): The muscles remain strong; the ring stays closed.
  5. Dissonance (No): The muscles weaken; the ring breaks easily.
Coach Tip

Kinesiology requires a "neutral mind." If you are rooting for a specific crystal to work, you will manually hold the ring closed. Practice on stones you know are wrong for a specific purpose to calibrate your "No" response.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is "projection bias" in a clinical setting?

Projection bias occurs when a practitioner subconsciously selects crystals based on their own personal preferences, current moods, or needs, rather than the objective data gathered from the client's frequency assessment.

2. Why is the "Rule of Three" important in the Pendulum Protocol?

Limiting selection to three primary stones prevents "frequency noise." Too many different mineral signals can overwhelm the client's bio-field, making it difficult for the body to achieve resonance or entrainment with any specific frequency.

3. In Kinesiology testing, what does a "breaking" O-ring signify?

A breaking ring signifies "dissonance" or a "No" response. It indicates that the crystal being tested does not resonate positively with the client's current energetic state or the practitioner's focused intent.

4. How does the Selection Matrix increase professional legitimacy?

It provides a documented, repeatable framework that combines chemical, geometric, and frequency data. This allows the practitioner to explain their choices scientifically to clients, which builds trust and justifies professional service fees.
