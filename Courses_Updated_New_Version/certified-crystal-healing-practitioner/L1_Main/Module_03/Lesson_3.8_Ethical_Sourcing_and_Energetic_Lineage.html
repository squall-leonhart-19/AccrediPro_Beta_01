Common Mistakes to Avoid

In my first year, I made all of these! Avoiding them will set you apart as a Certified Practitioner rather than a hobbyist.

  • The "Everything" Mistake: Suggesting 10 different stones. This overwhelms the client's energetic field. Stick to 3-4 max.
  • Over-Promising: Saying "This stone will cure your insomnia." Instead, use: "This stone is mineralogically structured to support a parasympathetic nervous system response."
  • Ignoring the Client's Aesthetic: If a client viscerally dislikes a stone's color or texture, don't force it. The "biological aversion" is a real data point.
  • Using Vague Language: Avoid "This stone has good vibes." Use "This stone has a high-frequency crystalline lattice."

Success Markers

How do you know if your selection session was successful? Look for these physiological and verbal cues from your client:

  1. The "Sigh" Response: The client takes a deep, involuntary breath (indicating a shift from sympathetic to parasympathetic nervous system).
  2. Pupil Dilation: A sign of relaxation and "dropping in" to the session.
  3. Ownership: The client begins to describe the stone's effect in their own words (e.g., "It feels like a warm blanket").
  4. Clarity: The client can articulate a specific goal for their "at-home" crystal work.
