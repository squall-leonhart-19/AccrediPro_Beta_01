The Physics of Light: Wavelengths in Mineralogy

Every crystal owes its color to the way its internal lattice absorbs and reflects specific portions of the visible light spectrum. This spectrum ranges from approximately 380 to 750 nanometers (nm). When we see a "Red" Jasper, we are actually witnessing the mineral reflecting the longest, slowest wavelengths of the visible spectrum while absorbing the others.

In bio-energetic therapy, these wavelengths correspond directly to the rotational speeds of the human chakras. A 2022 study published in the Journal of Photomedicine indicates that specific light frequencies can influence cellular ATP production and mitochondrial signaling. When we place a crystal on the body, we are introducing a stable, coherent frequency of light into a potentially incoherent bio-field.

    Beyond the Rainbow: Black, White, and Metallic

    Not all therapeutic minerals fit neatly into the 7-chakra rainbow. These "outlier" colors are some of the most powerful tools in the F.A.C.E.T.S. Method‚Ñ¢.

    1. Black Minerals (Absorbing)

    Minerals like Black Tourmaline (Schorl) and Obsidian appear black because they absorb all visible light frequencies. Energetically, they act as "sponges" for chaotic or dense frequencies. They are essential when the Frequency Assessment reveals "Heavy" or "Dark" energy in the auric field.

    2. White & Clear Minerals (Clarifying)

    Clear Quartz and Selenite reflect or transmit the full spectrum of light. They are considered "Master Healers" because they can be "programmed" to any frequency. They act as amplifiers, increasing the amplitude of the other stones in the layout.

    3. Metallic Minerals (Conductive)

    Pyrite and Hematite possess a metallic luster. These stones are highly conductive. Use them when a client feels "disconnected" from their body or when you need to bridge the gap between two disparate energy centers.

    Complementary Color Selection: The Balance Principle

    When an energy center is overactive (manifesting as inflammation, anger, or anxiety), adding the same color can exacerbate the issue. Instead, we use the Law of Opposites.

    • Overactive Solar Plexus (Anger/Heat): Use Violet or Amethyst (the opposite of Yellow) to cool the "fire."
    • Overactive Heart (Grief/Heaviness): Use Soft Pinks or even light Oranges to provide warmth to the "cold" heart.
    • Overactive Third Eye (Insomnia/Racing Thoughts): Use Orange or Earth-toned stones (Carnelian/Jasper) to pull the energy down into the physical body.
