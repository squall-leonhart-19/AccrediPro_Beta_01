Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference between an "accommodation" and "Universal Design"?

An accommodation is a reactive change made for one specific person (e.g., giving one person a private office). Universal Design is a proactive change to the environment that benefits everyone (e.g., creating quiet zones for the entire company).

2. When a client like Karen feels "advocacy fatigue," what is the first step for the coach?

The first step is validation and emotional regulation. The coach must acknowledge the difficulty of the work before moving into strategic problem-solving.

3. How does framing changes as "Performance Optimization" help the business case?

It aligns the inclusion goals with the company's bottom line (productivity, retention, and profit), making it harder for leadership to dismiss as "optional."

4. What is the "Cost of Inaction"?

It is the financial and cultural loss a company incurs by NOT changingâ€”including high turnover, loss of talent, burnout, and reduced productivity.
