Common Mistakes & Success Markers

As you transition into this professional role, you might find yourself falling into old habits. Thatâ€™s perfectly normal! Here is a guide to help you stay on track.

The Mistake Why It Happens The Neuro-Affirming Shift
Setting "SMART" goals only We are trained to value "Measurable" and "Time-bound" above all else. Prioritize "Meaningful" and "Manageable" first.
Ignoring the sensory cost We forget that a "simple" task might have a high sensory price for the client. Ask: "What is the sensory energy required for this goal?"
Cheerleading instead of Coaching We want the client to feel good, so we over-praise. Use "Reflective Listening" to validate their internal experience.
Rushing to the "How" We want to solve the problem quickly to prove our value. Linger in the "Why" and the "Who" (Who is this goal for?).
Success Marker

You know the session went well if the client says something like, "I never thought I was allowed to do it that way." That sentence is the sound of a neuro-affirming breakthrough!

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "compliance trap" dangerous for neurodivergent clients?

It reinforces the idea that the client's value is tied to how well they can mimic neurotypical standards, leading to burnout and a loss of autonomy.

2. What is a "Sensory Price Tag" in goal setting?

The amount of neurological and emotional energy a task requires. A "simple" task like a phone call may have a high sensory price tag for an autistic client.

3. If a client like Leo feels "guilty" about their goals, what should the coach do?

Validate the feeling as a byproduct of past conditioning and help the client pivot to a goal that prioritizes their actual needs over external expectations.

4. How does co-creation differ from traditional goal setting?

In co-creation, the coach acts as a partner/facilitator rather than an expert, ensuring the goal originates from the client's own desires and neurobiology.

Key Takeaways

  • Growth-oriented goals must be autonomy-driven, not compliance-based.
  • Your tone and validation create the psychological safety necessary for clients to stop masking.
  • Always consider the sensory and energetic cost of a goal before finalizing it.
  • Success isn't just "hitting the target"; it's the client feeling empowered to set their own targets.
  • You are becoming a coach who changes lives by changing how goals are defined!
