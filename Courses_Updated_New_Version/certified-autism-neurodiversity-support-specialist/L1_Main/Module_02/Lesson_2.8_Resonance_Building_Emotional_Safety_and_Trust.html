Common Pitfalls to Avoid

When we are new and eager to help, we often fall into "Fix-It Mode." In Module 2, we must resist this at all costs.

The Pitfall Why It Fails The "Resonance" Shift
Giving advice too early The client's brain is in "survival mode" and cannot process logic. Wait until you see a visible "sigh" or shoulder drop.
Polishing the pain Saying "It's not that bad" or "Think positive" dismisses their reality. "I can see how incredibly heavy this is for you."
Matching dysregulation If the client gets frantic, the coach gets frantic/fast-talking. Slow your tempo. Be the "non-anxious presence" in the room.
Over-explaining science Too much "Amydgala talk" can feel cold and clinical. Use metaphors like "Internal Weather" or "The Overflowing Cup."
Success Marker

You know the session is working when the client's breathing slows down and they start using "I" statements about their feelings rather than "He" statements about their child's behavior.

Check Your Understanding

1. Lisa says, "I should be able to handle this." What is the most effective way to respond using the Resonate model?

Validate the feeling and challenge the "should." Example: "That 'should' is carrying a lot of judgment. What if we acknowledged that this is objectively hard, regardless of your professional background?"

2. What is a biological signal that your co-regulation is working during the session?

Visible signs include: a deep spontaneous sigh, softening of the jaw/shoulders, slower speech rate, and the ability to make eye contact again.

3. Why do we avoid "polishing the pain" (e.g., "At least he's healthy!")?

It triggers a "shame response" in the client, making them feel like their genuine suffering is "wrong" or "ungrateful," which further dysregulates the nervous system.

4. True or False: A coach should always have a solution ready by the end of the first 15 minutes.

False. In the "Regulate & Resonate" phase, the "solution" IS the regulation. Forcing a tactical solution too early often leads to client overwhelm and "Yes, but..." responses.
