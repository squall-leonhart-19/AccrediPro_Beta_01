Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

  • The "Small Talk" Trap: Spending 10 minutes on "How's the weather?" which can actually increase social anxiety for neurodivergent clients.
  • Over-Indexing on Eye Contact: Expecting the client to look at the camera. A neuro-affirming coach knows that looking away often helps a client concentrate better.
  • The "Fix-It" Reflex: Jumping to "You should use a planner" before validating the sensory or executive function barrier.
  • Using "Person-First" Language Exclusively: If a client calls themselves "Autistic," don't correct them to "Person with Autism." Honor their identity.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why do we ask about lighting and sensory comfort at the start of a session?

It demonstrates that you understand sensory biology and immediately establishes the coaching space as a "safe container" where their needs are prioritized over social norms.

2. What is the "Double Empathy Problem" in the context of this lab?

It's the theory that communication breakdowns between neurodivergent and neurotypical people are a two-way street, not a "deficit" in the neurodivergent person. As a coach, you bridge this by adapting to the client's style.

3. If Elena stops speaking for 10 seconds, what should you do?

Wait. Offer a "processing pause." Avoid the urge to interrupt or clarify. She is likely processing the previous statement or formulating her response.

4. How does neuro-affirming language differ from traditional coaching?

Traditional coaching often focuses on "overcoming weaknesses." Neuro-affirming coaching focuses on "environmental fit" and "identity validation," moving the "problem" out of the person's character and into the interaction.

Key Takeaways

  • Collaboration over Correction: Your job is to be a "thinking partner," not a teacher.
  • Sensory First: A dysregulated brain cannot learn or set goals. Always address the environment first.
  • Validation is an Intervention: Telling a client "That makes total sense" can be the most powerful part of the session.
  • Practice Makes Permanent: Speaking these scripts out loud builds the confidence needed to charge professional rates and deliver results.
