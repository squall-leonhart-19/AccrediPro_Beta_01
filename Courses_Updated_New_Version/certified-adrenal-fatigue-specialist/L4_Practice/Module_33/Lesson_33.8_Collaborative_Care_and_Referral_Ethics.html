Handling Objections with Confidence

An objection is rarely a "no." Usually, it's a request for more information or a fear of failure. A 2022 study in the Journal of Health Coaching found that practitioners who addressed financial concerns directly had a 40% higher retention rate.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the Legal Disclaimer essential in the first 5 minutes of a discovery call?

It establishes professional boundaries and protects you legally by clarifying that you are a lifestyle/functional specialist, not a medical doctor diagnosing disease.

2. What is the most effective way to respond when a client says, "I need to think about it"?

Ask a clarifying question to uncover the real concern (e.g., "Is it the time commitment or the financial investment?"). This allows you to address the specific fear rather than a vague delay.

3. In the revenue model provided, how many new clients per month are needed to generate $9,000 in revenue?

5 new clients per month at a package price of $1,800.

4. True or False: You should lower your price immediately if a client seems hesitant.

False. You should offer a payment plan or emphasize the value/ROI. Lowering your price immediately devalues your expertise and the program's perceived effectiveness.

Key Takeaways

  • Discovery is Consultation: Your goal is to identify the "gap" between their current fatigue and their desired energy.
  • Sell Packages, Not Hours: A 12-week program ensures client commitment and practitioner financial stability.
  • Compliance is Confidence: Clearly stating your scope of practice protects you and builds trust with the client.
  • Listen More, Talk Less: The client should do 70% of the talking in the first half of the call.
  • Handle Objections with Empathy: Treat financial concerns as a request for reassurance of value.
