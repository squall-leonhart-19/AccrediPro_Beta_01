Clinical Use of Adrenal Glandulars

Adrenal glandulars (organotherapy) involve the use of bovine or porcine adrenal tissue to provide the body with the specific nucleic acids, enzymes, and precursors found in healthy adrenal glands. Unlike synthetic hormones, glandulars are designed to support the gland's own function.

Sourcing and Safety Standards

In your practice, the quality of the glandular is non-negotiable. You must ensure products are:

  • Sourced from New Zealand or Argentina: These regions have the strictest standards against BSE (Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy) and utilize pasture-raised animals.
  • Hormone-Free vs. Whole Gland: Some glandulars are "concentrates" (hormones removed), while others are "whole gland." For severe exhaustion (Phase 3), whole gland support is often necessary for the first 90 days.
Coach Tip: The Feedback Loop Risk

Avoid long-term, high-dose glandular use. Prolonged exogenous support can signal the brain to further downregulate its own production (negative feedback). Use a "3 months on, 1 month off" cycle or pulse dosing to maintain the HPA axis's sensitivity.

Phosphatidylserine & Magnolia Bark

When a client presents with High Evening Cortisol (confirmed via 4-point saliva or Dutch testing), they often suffer from "Sleep Latency"‚Äîthe inability to fall asleep despite being exhausted. This is where Phosphatidylserine (PS) shines.

Mechanism of Action: PS has been shown in clinical trials to blunt the ACTH (Adrenocorticotropic Hormone) response to stress. By lowering ACTH, the signal to produce cortisol is dampened. A 2014 study found that 400mg of PS significantly reduced cortisol levels and improved mood in stressed subjects.

Magnolia Bark (Honokiol/Magnolol): This botanical acts as a mild GABA-A agonist. When combined with PS, it provides a "one-two punch": PS lowers the cortisol signal, while Magnolia calms the nervous system, allowing for restorative sleep architecture.

IV Nutrient Therapy vs. Liposomal Delivery

In advanced cases of Adrenal Fatigue, the gut is often "shut down" due to low vagal tone and chronic sympathetic dominance. This leads to malabsorption, where the client takes expensive supplements but sees no change in serum levels.

The Liposomal Advantage

Liposomal delivery encapsulates nutrients (like Vitamin C or B-Complex) in a phospholipid bilayer. This allows the nutrient to bypass the digestive enzymes and be absorbed directly into the lymphatic system and bloodstream.
Clinical Note: Liposomal Vitamin C can achieve blood levels 2-3 times higher than standard oral capsular forms.

When to Escalate to IV Therapy

The "Myers' Cocktail" (IV Magnesium, Calcium, B-Vitamins, and Vitamin C) is the gold standard for rapid cellular repletion. As a Specialist, you do not administer these, but you partner with functional MDs to recommend them for clients who:

  • Have a history of Crohn‚Äôs/Celiac or severe IBS.
  • Are in "Adrenal Crisis" (Phase 3 exhaustion).
  • Demonstrate zero improvement after 60 days of high-quality oral supplementation.
Coach Tip: Identifying Malabsorption

If a client reports seeing undigested supplement tablets in their stool, or if they take 1000mg of Magnesium Citrate without experiencing a "laxative effect," their absorption is likely compromised. Switch to liposomal or topical (transdermal) forms immediately.

Advanced Electrolyte Balancing & Aldosterone

The adrenal cortex produces Aldosterone, the hormone responsible for regulating the sodium-potassium balance. In late-stage Adrenal Fatigue, aldosterone levels drop, leading to "salt wasting."

The Symptoms of Low Aldosterone:

  • Orthostatic Hypotension (dizziness when standing up).
  • Cravings for salt.
  • Frequent urination (polyuria) because the body cannot hold onto water without sodium.

The Sodium-Potassium Ratio

Many "healthy" diets are too high in potassium and too low in sodium for an adrenal-fatigued client. While the general population is told to avoid salt, your clients likely need more high-quality sea salt (Celtic or Himalayan).

Protocol: Aim for a Sodium to Potassium (Na:K) ratio of approximately 10:1 in the diet during the recovery phase. This supports blood pressure and reduces the workload on the adrenal glands to produce aldosterone.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which adaptogen is most appropriate for a client with a "Flatline" (low all day) cortisol pattern?

Rhodiola Rosea. Because of its stimulating and ergogenic properties, it helps elevate the morning cortisol response and combat the lethargy associated with Phase 3 fatigue.

2. What is the primary mechanism by which Phosphatidylserine improves sleep in adrenal clients?

It blunts the ACTH signal from the pituitary gland. By reducing the message to the adrenals to produce cortisol, it helps lower pathological evening cortisol spikes that prevent sleep onset.

3. Why is New Zealand or Argentina sourcing critical for adrenal glandulars?

These countries are BSE-free and have higher standards for pasture-raising animals without the use of synthetic growth hormones or antibiotics, ensuring the purity of the organ tissue.

4. A client feels dizzy when standing up. Which hormone is likely low, and what is the immediate nutritional fix?

Aldosterone is likely low. The immediate fix is increasing high-quality sodium (sea salt) intake and ensuring proper electrolyte ratios to support blood pressure and fluid retention.
