Telomeres: The Cellular Clocks of Stress

Chronological age is simply the number of times you have traveled around the sun. Biological age is the actual state of your physiological systems. At the heart of this distinction are telomeres‚Äîthe protective caps at the ends of our chromosomes.

A landmark 2004 study by Dr. Elizabeth Blackburn (Nobel Laureate) found that women under high chronic stress had telomeres that were significantly shorter than their low-stress counterparts. The difference? Approximately 9 to 17 years of additional cellular aging.

Coach Tip

When explaining telomeres to clients, use the "shoelace" analogy. Telomeres are like the plastic tips on shoelaces. When the tips wear off, the shoelace (your DNA) starts to fray. Stress burns through those plastic tips faster than anything else.

Biomarkers of Vitality: DHEA-S & Cortisol

In the world of anti-aging, DHEA-S is often called the "youth hormone." It is the most abundant circulating steroid in the body and acts as a direct counter-balance to cortisol. While cortisol is catabolic (breaks things down), DHEA is anabolic (builds things up).

As Adrenal Fatigue progresses, we often see a "DHEA Crash." This is why clients in Stage 3 recovery look and feel significantly older; they have lost the anabolic protection that maintains muscle mass, skin elasticity, and cognitive function.
