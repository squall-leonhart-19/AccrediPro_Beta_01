Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. What is the primary goal of the "Resilience Transition" phase of coaching?

The goal is to move the client from a "fragile" recovery mindset to a "resilient" mastery mindset, where they trust their body to handle stress without relapsing.

2. Why is "regression fear" common in women aged 40-55 during this phase?

They have often spent years in a cycle of burnout and temporary "fixes." They fear the current success is just another temporary phase rather than a permanent physiological shift.

3. Which "Pitfall" involves a coach adding too many new protocols at the end of a program?

Over-Coaching. This often stems from the coach's imposter syndrome and can overwhelm the client, leading to a loss of focus on the core habits that actually work.

4. According to Sarah, how should you view a client's fear of the "old stress"?

View it as a sign of success. It indicates the client highly values their new state of vitality and is motivated to protect it.

Lab Summary & Mastery Points

  • Validation First: Always validate the fear of relapse before providing the solution.
  • Resilience Over Perfection: Shift the client's goal from "never being stressed" to "recovering quickly from stress."
  • Professional Authority: Your role is to remind the client of their physiological progress when their mind feels doubtful.
  • Sustainable Business: Graduation isn't "goodbye"; it's a transition to a long-term mastery partnership.
