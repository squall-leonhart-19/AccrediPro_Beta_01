4 Common Coaching Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Adding More Information: When a client is overwhelmed, do NOT explain the Krebs cycle. They need safety, not science.
  • The "Cheerleader" Trap: Saying "You can do it!" can feel dismissive. Instead, use "We will do this together."
  • Rigidity: If they can't quit coffee yet, don't force it. Move to a "half-caf" or "coffee after breakfast" strategy.
  • Ignoring the Win: If they did 1 out of 10 things, celebrate that 1 thing like they won the Olympics.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is "Decision Fatigue" common in Adrenal Fatigue Stage 2/3?

Chronic cortisol dysregulation impacts the prefrontal cortex, which is responsible for executive function and complex decision-making. When the body is in "survival mode," it prioritizes immediate safety over long-term planning.

2. What is the "Integration Bridge" technique?

It is the coaching move of validating the client's overwhelm, explaining the physiological reason for it, and then radically simplifying the protocol into 1-2 "micro-wins" to build self-efficacy.

3. If Linda refuses to give up coffee, what is the best "Level 1" coaching move?

Negotiate a "Harm Reduction" strategy. Suggest she has her coffee *after* a protein-rich breakfast to blunt the cortisol/glucose spike, rather than on an empty stomach.

4. What does Sarah mean by "selling transformation, not information"?

Information is easily found online. Transformation is the coaching process of helping a client actually *implement* changes, overcome mental blocks, and achieve a new state of health.
