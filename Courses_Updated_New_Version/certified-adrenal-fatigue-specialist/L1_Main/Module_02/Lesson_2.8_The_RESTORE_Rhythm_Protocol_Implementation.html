Common Coaching Pitfalls

The Pitfall Why It Fails The Specialist Fix
The "Science Dump" Overwhelming the client with talk of the Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN). Use metaphors like "The Internal Clock" or "The Hormonal Timer."
Rigid Scheduling Demanding they wake up at 6:00 AM immediately. Focus on the order of events, not the exact minute.
Ignoring Blue Light Focusing only on the morning and ignoring the midnight iPad use. Introduce blue-light blocking glasses as a "bridge" for night owls.
Missing the "Why" Telling them what to do without explaining the energy benefit. Always link the habit back to their specific goal (e.g., grandchildren).

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explain the "Cortisol Pulse" to a client like Diane?

It provides a biological "why" that moves the intervention from "lifestyle advice" to "clinical necessity," helping the client understand that their morning actions dictate their evening sleep quality.

2. What is a "Morning Anchor"?

A Morning Anchor is a consistent, repeatable habit performed shortly after waking (usually light exposure) that signals the HPA-axis to start the daily hormonal cycle.

3. How should a coach handle a client who refuses to give up their late-night screen time?

Instead of demanding they stop, offer a "harm reduction" strategy like blue-light blocking glasses or dimming the screen brightness, explaining how this protects their melatonin production.

4. Why is recording your coaching script out loud recommended?

It helps identify vocal patterns that may undermine your authority, such as "up-talking" or excessive filler words, allowing you to develop a more confident, professional presence.

Key Takeaways

  • Rhythm over Rigidity: Focus on anchoring the start and end of the day rather than policing every minute.
  • Metaphors Matter: Use relatable terms like "Internal Clock" to make complex circadian biology accessible.
  • Collaborative Coaching: Use "we" and "our" to build a partnership with the client, reducing resistance to change.
  • The 14-Day Window: Remind clients that rhythmic changes often take about two weeks to "reset" the internal clock.
