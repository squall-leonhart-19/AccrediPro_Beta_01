Practical Exercise: The "Say It Out Loud" Challenge

Mastering the tone of your voice is just as important as the words. For a woman like Diane, she needs to hear unshakeable confidence in your voice to help quiet her own internal anxiety.

Step 1: The Transition Statement

Say this out loud: "Diane, we've spent the last few months in 'Repair Mode.' Today, we officially move into 'Vitality Mode.' This means our focus shifts from fixing what was broken to fueling what is growing."

Step 2: The Boundary Inquiry

Say this out loud: "When you look at your new business schedule, where is the one place you feel most tempted to 'cheat' on your recovery habits? Let's look at that specific spot together."

Step 3: Record & Review

Use your phone to record yourself saying the Coach's lines from the script above. Listen back. Do you sound like a peer, or an expert? Aim for a voice that is warm but authoritative.

Common Coaching Pitfalls in Module 7

Even experienced coaches can stumble when a client starts feeling better. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

The Mistake Why It Happens The "Vitality" Fix
Ending Coaching Too Soon The client feels "fine" and wants to save money. Explain that the maintenance phase is where the permanent habits are sealed.
Becoming Too Relaxed The coach stops tracking data because the client is happy. Continue using the Vitality Dashboard to catch "micro-slips" in cortisol rhythm.
Ignoring the "Fear of Relapse" The coach assumes the client is as confident as they look. Proactively ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how much do you trust your body today?"
Over-Protocolizing The coach keeps the client on strict supplements for too long. Transition to "Intuitive Vitality"â€”helping the client lean more on food and lifestyle.

Success Markers: Knowing Theyâ€™re Ready

How do you know when your coaching has truly been successful in the Empower Vitality phase? Look for these specific shifts in your client:

  • From "Fear" to "Feedback": They no longer see a tired afternoon as a "relapse," but as feedback that they need an extra hour of sleep or more minerals.
  • Proactive Boundary Setting: They say "no" to a social or work commitment before they are exhausted.
  • Stable Energy Rhythms: Their energy remains consistent (e.g., a steady 7/10 or 8/10) even during a moderately stressful week.
  • Identity Shift: They stop identifying as "someone with adrenal fatigue" and start identifying as "a high-performance woman who prioritizes her health."
Sarah's Insight

When you hear a client say, "I realized I was getting tired, so I took a 20-minute nap instead of a third coffee," celebrate that! That is a massive win in the vitality phase. It shows they are finally listening to their body's "whispers" before they become "screams."

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological shift a coach must facilitate in Module 7?

The shift from a "Fragile/Repair" mindset (fear of relapse) to a "Resilient/Vitality" mindset (trusting the body's feedback).

2. Why is "ending coaching too soon" considered a pitfall in the vitality phase?

Because the vitality phase is where habits become permanent. Without guidance, clients often revert to old "push-through" behaviors the moment they feel a surge of energy, leading to a potential crash.

3. What is a "Vitality Dashboard" in the context of this lesson?

A set of early-warning signals (like sleep quality, mood shifts, or digestive changes) that the client learns to monitor to adjust their lifestyle in real-time before a full adrenal crash occurs.

4. How should a coach respond when a client expresses fear about starting a new, stressful project?

Acknowledge and validate the fear, then pivot to the tools and "upgraded wiring" the client now possesses, shifting the focus to how they will use their vitality tools to navigate the stress safely.

  • Celebrate Identity Shifts: The ultimate goal is for the client to see themselves as resilient and capable, not "broken."
