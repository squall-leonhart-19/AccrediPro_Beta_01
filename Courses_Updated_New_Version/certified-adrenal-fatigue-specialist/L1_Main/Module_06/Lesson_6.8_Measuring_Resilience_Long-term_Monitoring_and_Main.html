Handling Resistance

If a client says, "I don't have time for a blueprint," remind them: "We can spend 20 minutes planning now, or 2 weeks recovering later." This gentle reality check is part of your value as a coach.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why It Fails The Better Approach
Dismissing the Fear Telling them "You're fine!" ignores their internal physiological trauma. Validate the fear, then pivot to the evidence of their recovery.
Over-Supplementing Adding more adaptogens to "push through" the new business launch. Focus on lifestyle boundaries and "Recovery Sprints" instead.
Lack of Specificity Saying "Just listen to your body" is too vague for an ambitious person. Define 3 specific "Early Warning Signs" (e.g., eye twitch, craving salt).
The "Fixed" Fallacy Acting like they are "cured" and no longer need support. Frame resilience as an ongoing practice, not a destination.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary psychological hurdle for a client in the "Rebuild Resilience" phase?

The primary hurdle is "Relapse Anxiety"â€”the fear that any increase in activity or stress will trigger a return to Stage 1 exhaustion.

2. How does the coach redefine "Resilience" for the client?

Resilience is redefined not as the absence of stress, but as the increased capacity to bounce back from stress without a prolonged crash.

3. What are "Early Warning Signs" in the Resilience Blueprint?

These are subtle, individualized physiological signals (like salt cravings, specific muscle tension, or mild insomnia) that indicate the HPA axis is under strain before a full crash occurs.

4. Why is it important to validate Elena's fear rather than just telling her she's healthy?

Validation builds the coaching alliance and acknowledges the real physiological memory of burnout. It makes the client feel "seen," which lowers their immediate stress response.

Key Takeaways

  • Validate first, Pivot second: Always acknowledge the fear of relapse before moving into planning.
  • Resilience is a Muscle: Remind clients that they have built real physiological capacity through their recovery work.
  • The Blueprint is the Anchor: Giving clients a concrete plan for "Early Warning Signs" reduces the anxiety of the unknown.
  • Professional Authority: Your calm, steady presence as a coach is as healing as the protocols themselves.
  • You are a Coach: By practicing these scripts, you are moving from "student" to "expert practitioner."
