Common Coaching Pitfalls

In my early years, I made these mistakes often. Watch out for these as you start your practice:

  • The "Info-Dump": Explaining every single lab marker in the first 10 minutes. (Result: Client glazes over).
  • Fixing, Not Coaching: Telling the client what to do without asking how it fits their life. (Result: Low compliance).
  • Ignoring the "Wins": Diving straight into what's wrong without celebrating that the client actually showed up. (Result: Client feels discouraged).
  • Under-charging for the Deep Dive: This synthesis is high-level work. Specialists often charge $250-$350 for this specific session because of the expertise required.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to explain the "Why" behind stopping HIIT workouts for an L1 client like Diane?

Because Type A clients often view exercise as a "weight loss tool" only. Explaining that HIIT can trigger cortisol-driven weight gain during exhaustion helps them see restorative movement as a strategic choice rather than "quitting."

2. What is the "Minimum Effective Dose" strategy in coaching?

Finding the smallest possible change (e.g., 5 minutes of breathing) that a busy client can commit to with 100% success, building momentum and trust without causing further stress.

3. If Diane has a low Sodium/Potassium ratio, what does this typically indicate in the L1 phase?

It typically indicates the "Exhaustion Phase" of the HPA-axis dysfunction, where the body is struggling to maintain mineral balance and cellular energy, requiring immediate support with minerals and rest.

4. Which of the following is a "Success Marker" for a coaching session?

The client being able to clearly articulate the "one thing" they are changing this week. This shows clarity and manageable action steps.
