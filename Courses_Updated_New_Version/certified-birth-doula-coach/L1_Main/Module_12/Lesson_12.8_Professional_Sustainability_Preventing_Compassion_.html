Common Coaching Pitfalls

  • Fixing instead of Coaching: Trying to "save" the natural birth instead of helping the client process the change.
  • Over-identifying: Saying "I know exactly how you feel." (You don'tâ€”her IVF journey is hers alone). Instead, say "I can see how much this matters to you."
  • Medical Encroachment: Telling her the induction is "wrong." Your role is to facilitate her conversation with her medical team.
  • Ignoring the Partner: Forgetting to coach the partner on how to support her through this "pivot."
Emma's Insight

If you feel your own heart rate rising during a session, you've likely slipped into 'Fixer Mode.' Take a breath. You are the anchor, not the engine.

Success Markers

How do you know the session worked? Look for these shifts in Sarah-Jane:

  • Physical Softening: Her shoulders drop, and her breathing slows down.
  • Language Shift: She moves from "I have to" to "I choose to" or "I am curious about."
  • Proactive Planning: She begins asking how to bring her labor music and essential oils to the induction.
  • Connection: She mentions the baby's well-being as the primary driver, rather than her "plan."

Check Your Understanding

1. Sarah-Jane asks: "Is my OB just being lazy by wanting an induction?" How should a Birth Doula Coachâ„¢ respond?

Avoid taking sides. Respond with: "That's a question worth exploring with them. Let's write down a few ways you can ask about the medical necessity versus the hospital's standard protocol so you can get the clarity you need."

2. What is the primary goal of the "Pivot Technique"?

To shift the client's focus from a rigid outcome (which they can't control) to their internal state and decision-making power (which they can control).

3. True or False: A coach should tell the client that inductions always lead to C-sections to help them stay "natural."

False. This is fear-mongering and outside the scope of coaching. A coach provides evidence-based resources and helps the client navigate their own choices.

4. Which part of the C.R.A.D.L.E. Frameworkâ„¢ addresses the client's sense of failure?

The "E" - Embodying the Mother. This helps her realize that mothering starts with how she handles the unexpected and how she advocates for her child's and her own well-being.
