Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why it Hinders Growth The "Deep Dive" Correction
Giving Medical Advice Increases liability and disempowers the client's relationship with their provider. Provide questions for the client to ask their provider instead.
Validating Fear Saying "I'd be scared too" keeps the client in a sympathetic nervous system state. Acknowledge the feeling, then pivot to facts: "I hear that fear; let's look at your actual stats."
Talking Too Much The coach should only speak about 30% of the time in a Deep Dive session. Use powerful pauses. Let the client sit with the realization of their own strength.
Ignoring the "Why" Focusing only on the birth plan without addressing the underlying imposter syndrome. Address the "story" the client is telling themselves (The Reframe).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Clarify" stage in the C.R.A.D.L.E. Frameworkâ„¢ when dealing with a high-risk label?

The goal is to separate generalized population statistics (e.g., "women over 40") from the client's individual clinical data (e.g., her specific blood pressure, fetal movement, and health history) to provide a grounded perspective.

2. Why is the "B.R.A.I.N." acronym particularly useful for a client like Elena who feels "medically fragile"?

It provides a logical, non-confrontational structure (Benefits, Risks, Alternatives, Intuition, Nothing) that shifts the conversation from emotional reaction to informed, collaborative decision-making.

3. A coach says: "Don't let them induce you; it's just because of your age." Why is this a mistake?

This is medical advice and "rescuing." It oversteps the coaching scope and can create an adversarial relationship between the client and provider, potentially leading to a stressful birth environment.

4. How does addressing a client's "imposter syndrome" improve birth outcomes?

By reducing psychological stress and "cortisol flooding," the client remains in a more relaxed state, which supports the natural hormonal flow of oxytocin necessary for labor progression.

Key Takeaways

  • Coaching is Empowerment: Your role is to provide the "scaffolding" so the client can stand tall in their own advocacy.
  • Facts Over Fear: Always pivot the conversation back to the client's specific, healthy markers to counter generalized medical labels.
  • The Power of the Pause: Give your clients space to process the shift from "patient" to "protagonist."
