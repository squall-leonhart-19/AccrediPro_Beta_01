Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Gate Control Theory" useful for a client like Sarah?

It provides a logical, physiological explanation for how comfort measures work, which helps "Type A" or anxious clients feel more in control of the process.

2. What is the difference between "pain" and "suffering" in a coaching context?

Pain is a physical sensation; suffering is the emotional state of feeling overwhelmed or alone. Coaching focuses on preventing suffering even when pain is present.

3. How should you address a client's fear of "losing control"?

By validating the fear and then reframing "control" as "active participation" and "Dynamic Comfort" rather than "silent endurance."

4. True or False: A Birth Doula Coach should be the one performing all the physical comfort measures during labor.

False. A coach empowers the partner to provide physical comfort while guiding the overall strategy and emotional environment.
