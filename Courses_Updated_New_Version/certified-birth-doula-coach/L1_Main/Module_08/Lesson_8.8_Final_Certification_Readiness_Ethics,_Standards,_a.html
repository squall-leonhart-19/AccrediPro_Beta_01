Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of using the B.R.A.I.N. acronym in a coaching session?

The goal is to facilitate informed decision-making by helping the client evaluate the Benefits, Risks, Alternatives, Intuition, and what happens if they do Nothing. It shifts the power from the provider back to the client.

2. According to neurophysiology, why does fear increase labor pain?

Fear triggers the sympathetic nervous system, releasing catecholamines (adrenaline/cortisol). This diverts blood away from the uterus to the limbs, causing uterine muscles to work harder and increasing the activation of pain receptors (nociceptors).

3. Elena feels "locked up." Which C.R.A.D.L.E.â„¢ pillar addresses this directly?

Dynamic Comfort (D). This involves using physical and psychological techniques to shift the body from a state of tension to a state of relaxation, facilitating oxytocin release.

4. How should a coach respond if a client asks, "Should I refuse the induction?"

A professional coach avoids "shoulds." Instead, respond: "I can't make that medical decision for you, but I can help you explore the data and your own feelings so you can make the decision that feels right for your family."
Confidence Boost

Imposter syndrome is common when you first start these "high-stakes" conversations. Remember: You don't need to know more than the doctor; you just need to know how to support the human being in front of the doctor.

Key Takeaways

  • Rights are the Foundation: Always ground your coaching in the client's right to informed consent.
  • Science Calms Fear: Explaining the neurophysiology of pain (catecholamines vs. oxytocin) gives clients a logical way to understand their physical sensations.
  • Preparation is Advocacy: Your role is to give the client the tools (like B.R.A.I.N.) to advocate for themselves.
  • Maintain Scope: Stay in the coaching laneâ€”focus on values, education, and comfort, not medical directives.
