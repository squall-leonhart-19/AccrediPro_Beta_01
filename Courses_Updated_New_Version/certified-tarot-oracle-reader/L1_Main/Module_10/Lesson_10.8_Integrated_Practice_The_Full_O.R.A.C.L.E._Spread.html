3. Practice Exercise: Vocal Authority

One of the most common signs of imposter syndrome is "up-talking" (making statements sound like questions) or over-explaining your prices. Use the following table to audit your speech patterns.

The "Imposter" Phrasing The "Professional" Phrasing The Goal
"Does that make sense?" (Seeking validation) "How does that land with you?" (Inviting feedback) Authority
"I think the cards might be saying..." "The imagery here indicates..." Objectivity
"My price is $150, if that's okay?" "The investment for this consultation is $150." Confidence
Coach Tip

Record yourself saying the Professional Phrasings on your phone. Listen back. If you sound like you're asking for permission, try again with a deeper breath and a slower tempo.

4. Common Pitfalls in First Consultations

Even experienced coaches can stumble when they are first establishing their intuitive brand. Watch out for these four professional "red flags":

  • Over-Delivering to Prove Worth: Extending a 30-minute discovery call to 60 minutes. This signals that your time isn't valuable.
  • Apologizing for the "Woo": If you act embarrassed by tarot, your client will be too. Stand by your tools.
  • Solving Before Signing: Giving a full reading during a discovery call. The discovery call is to see if you are a fit, not to do the work for free.
  • The "Friend Zone": Becoming the client's "best friend" rather than their consultant. Maintain a professional distance to ensure your advice carries weight.
Sarah's Insight

In my practice, I've seen that clients who respect your boundaries are the ones who get the best results. A coach who can't say 'our time is up' usually can't hold the space for a client's transformation either.

5. Success Markers & Integration

How do you know if your practice session was successful? Look for these three internal shifts:

  1. The "Drop" in Breath: You stopped breathing from your chest and started breathing from your belly during the script.
  2. Zero Apologies: You made it through the dialogue without using the words "just," "sorry," or "I'm only."
  3. Active Listening: You felt more curious about Deborah than worried about what card you would pull next.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to use "Consultant Language" with professional clients like Deborah?

It creates a bridge between her logical, career-focused world and the intuitive tools you use, building instant credibility and reducing her skepticism.

2. What is "up-talking" and why should a professional intuitive avoid it?

Up-talking is making a statement sound like a question. It signals a lack of confidence and invites the client to doubt your expertise.

3. What is the primary purpose of a Discovery Call?

To determine if the coach and client are a good fit for a working relationship, not to provide a full, free service or "solve" the client's problem immediately.

4. Which phrase is more professional when discussing fees?

"The investment for this consultation is $150." This frames the cost as a value-based exchange rather than a request for permission.
