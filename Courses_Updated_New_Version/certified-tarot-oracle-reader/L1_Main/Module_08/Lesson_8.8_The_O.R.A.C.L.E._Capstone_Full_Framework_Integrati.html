Common Mistakes for New Coaches

When you are starting out, especially if you struggle with your own imposter syndrome, you might fall into these traps:

Over-Explaining the Symbols

Spending 20 minutes talking about the history of the Empress instead of how the Empress relates to the client's business launch.

Ignoring the Resistance

When Elena says "I don't feel like an expert," a mistake is to say "Oh, don't worry, you are!" Instead, use the cards to help *her* discover why she feels that way.

Ending Without Action

A beautiful reading that ends with "That was so insightful!" but no clear next step is a missed opportunity for transformation.

Taking On the Client's Stress

Feeling like *you* have to fix Elena. Remember: The cards and the client do the work; you are the facilitator.

Success Markers

How do you know if your session was successful? Look for these "shifts" in the client's energy:

  • Physiological Change: The client's shoulders drop, or they take a deep, audible breath (a sign of the nervous system regulating).
  • Language Shift: Moving from "I can't" or "I'm not sure" to "I see now" or "What if I tried..."
  • Clarity: The client can summarize the reading back to you in one sentence.
  • Commitment: They enthusiastically agree to a specific action step before the session ends.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary goal of the "Empowerment Synthesis"?

The goal is to move the client from passive insight to active, soul-aligned momentum by weaving card meanings into a cohesive action plan.

2. In the Elena scenario, which card represents her internal "imposter syndrome"?

The 8 of Swords, which traditionally represents mental entrapment and self-imposed restrictions.

3. Why is it important to ask "How does it feel to see these cards sitting next to each other?"

This question encourages the client to externalize their internal conflict, allowing them to view their fear (8 of Swords) as separate from their potential (The Empress).

4. What is the most common reason a "spiritual reading" fails to create change?

It lacks a concrete "Action Invitation" or "Soul-Aligned Step," leaving the client with information but no implementation.

Key Takeaways

  • Synthesis is the art of turning a "spread" of cards into a single, powerful narrative for the client.
  • Your role is to act as a bridge between the intuitive world (the cards) and the physical world (coaching action).
  • Empowerment happens when a client recognizes that their "mental swords" are self-imposed and they have the "Empress energy" to move forward.
  • Always conclude a session with a specific, manageable action step to anchor the intuitive work.
