The Hero‚Äôs Journey: Mapping the Major Arcana

The Major Arcana is not just a collection of 22 cards; it is a cyclical blueprint of human evolution. Drawing from the work of Joseph Campbell and Carl Jung, we view the Major Arcana as the "Hero's Journey." When a client draws multiple Major Arcana cards, it signals that they are in a "Macro Phase"‚Äîa period of significant life transition where universal forces are at play.

A professional reader identifies where on this journey the Seeker currently stands. Are they at the Call to Adventure (The Fool/Magician), the Ordeal (Death/The Devil), or the Return with the Elixir (The World/The Sun)?

Symbolic Shorthand: Colors, Numbers, and Geometry

To decode patterns rapidly, you must master symbolic shorthand. This is the "Secret Language" hidden in the artwork. For example, a 2022 study on visual semiotics suggests that 93% of communication is non-verbal, and this applies to the tarot deck as well.

  • Color Theory: Yellow backgrounds signify conscious awareness and clarity; Blue signifies the subconscious and passivity; Red signifies physical vitality and passion.
  • Geometry: Triangles (The Temperance/The Star) represent spiritual aspiration; Squares (The Emperor/Hierarchy) represent stability and restriction; Circles represent wholeness and the divine.
  • Numerology: Threes represent expansion (The Empress); Fours represent structure (The Emperor); Nines represent near-completion (The Hermit).

Court Cards: Internal Personas vs. External Influences

Court Cards (Pages, Knights, Queens, Kings) are often the most confusing part of analysis. In advanced practice, we view them through two lenses:

  1. Internal Personas: A specific part of the Seeker's personality that needs to be "activated." (e.g., The Queen of Pentacles as the Seeker's need to nurture their own finances).
  2. External Influences: A literal person in the Seeker's life who is affecting the situation.

The "Mirror Test": If the Seeker reacts strongly (positive or negative) to the description of a Court Card, it is likely an Internal Persona (projection). If they remain neutral but identify the traits in someone else, it is likely an External Influence.

4. What is the difference between a "Square" and a "Triangle" in symbolic geometry?

A Square represents stability, structure, and sometimes restriction (the material world), while a Triangle represents spiritual aspiration, direction, and the movement of energy.
