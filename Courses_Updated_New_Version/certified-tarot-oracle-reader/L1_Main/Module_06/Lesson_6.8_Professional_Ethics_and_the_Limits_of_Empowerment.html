Linguistic Shifts for Empowerment

Passive Reading (Predictive) Empowered Coaching (Action-Oriented)
"The cards say you will get a client soon." "What action can you take today to invite your first client in?"
"This reversal means you'll face delays." "Where is your energy being diverted, and how can we reclaim it?"
"You are meant to be a leader." "In what ways are you already leading, and how can we amplify that?"

Common Pitfalls for New Coaches

When you're starting out, it's tempting to want to "save" the client. Avoid these four common traps:

  • Over-Predicting: Giving a "Yes/No" answer robs the client of their agency. Always bridge back to their choice.
  • The "Fix-It" Trap: If you provide all the answers, the client becomes dependent on you. Ask more than you tell.
  • Ignoring the Professional Background: Diane is a VP. Don't talk to her like a child. Use her existing skills as metaphors.
  • Fear of Reversals: Don't apologize for "bad" cards. There are no bad cards, only opportunities for course correction.

Check Your Understanding

1. A client asks, "When will I find my soulmate?" How do you reframe this for empowerment?

Instead of giving a date, ask: "What qualities are you currently cultivating in yourself to be ready for that partnership?" or "What does the 'soulmate' version of you do differently than the 'single' version of you?"

2. What is the primary goal of the "E" (Empowerment) phase in the framework?

The goal is to move the client from a state of passive observation to a state of active co-creation, ensuring they leave the session with a clear sense of their own power and a specific action step.

3. True or False: A reversed card always indicates a negative outcome.

False. In an empowerment context, a reversal often indicates internal work, a need for alignment, or a redirection of energy. It is a diagnostic tool, not a doom-and-gloom prediction.

4. Why is Diane's professional background (Vice Principal) relevant to the reading?

By acknowledging her existing authority and skills, you help bridge the gap between her "old self" and "new self," reducing imposter syndrome and making the empowerment feel grounded and realistic.

Success Markers

How do you know if you've successfully empowered Diane? Look for these signs:

  • Shift in Body Language: She moves from leaning back (passive) to leaning in (engaged).
  • Language Change: She stops saying "I hope" and starts saying "I will."
  • Specific Questions: She asks "How can I handle X?" instead of "Will X happen?"
  • Immediate Action: She writes down a specific task to complete within the next 48 hours.

Key Takeaways

  • Empowerment is the transition from "victim of fate" to "architect of destiny."
  • Your voice and linguistic choices are your most powerful coaching tools.
  • Every reading should conclude with a "First Step" action plan.
  • Professionalism comes from the results you facilitate, not just the accuracy of your cards.
  • Imposter syndrome is common in career changers; use the cards to mirror their existing strengths back to them.
