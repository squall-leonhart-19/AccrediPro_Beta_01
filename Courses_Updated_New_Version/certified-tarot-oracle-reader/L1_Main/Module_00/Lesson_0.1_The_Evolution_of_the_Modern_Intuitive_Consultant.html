A Letter from Sarah: My Story and Your Potential

I remember exactly where I was when I decided to turn my "spiritual hobby" into a professional career. I was 43, sitting at my desk in a corporate office, feeling like a part of my soul was slowly dimming. I had a stable job, but I lacked meaning. I was a nurse for 15 years before that, and while I loved helping people, the system left me burnt out and disconnected.

When I first picked up a Tarot deck, I was terrified someone would find out. I felt like an "imposter." I thought, "Who am I to give people guidance? I'm just a middle-aged woman with a desk job." But as I began to read for others, I realized that my years of nursing‚Äîlistening to patients, understanding human suffering, and offering comfort‚Äîwere actually my greatest strengths as a reader.

I created the Certified Tarot & Oracle Reader‚Ñ¢ program because I wanted to provide the legitimacy and professional structure that I wish I had when I started. I want you to feel the same sense of pride when you tell someone your profession as you would if you were a therapist or a coach. I believe in you, and I am honored to be your mentor.

Coach Sarah's Tip

Silence the Inner Critic: That voice telling you it's "too late" or you're "not spiritual enough" is just fear trying to keep you safe. In this program, we don't just learn cards; we build the confidence to stand in our truth. Whenever that voice speaks up, remind yourself: I am exactly where I am supposed to be.

You Belong Here: The Superpower of Life Experience

If you are in your 40s, 50s, or beyond, you might be wondering if you can "keep up" with the younger generation of readers on social media. Let me tell you a secret: Life experience is your greatest professional asset.

Tarot and Oracle reading is, at its heart, about the human experience. Because you have lived through career changes, parenting, loss, health challenges, and personal triumphs, you possess a level of empathy and perspective that cannot be taught in a book. Your clients aren't looking for someone who can just memorize card meanings; they are looking for someone who understands life.

You Are Not Alone: Your Support System (PODs)

One of the biggest reasons people fail to complete certifications is isolation. That will not happen here. In this program, you will be placed into a Private Practice Group (POD).

Your POD consists of 4-6 fellow students who are at the same stage of the journey as you. Together, you will:

  • Practice Readings: Build confidence in a safe, non-judgmental environment.
  • Peer Feedback: Get constructive insights on your interpretation style.
  • Emotional Support: Have a group of women who "get it" when you're having a breakthrough or a moment of doubt.
  • Accountability: Keep each other on track toward your graduation date.
Coach Sarah's Tip

Lean on Your Peers: Don't wait until you're "perfect" to join your POD sessions. The messy, early practice sessions are where the real learning happens. We are all here to grow together.

First Steps to Success

Transformation doesn't happen all at once; it happens through consistent, small actions. To get the most out of this certification, I want you to commit to these three steps right now:

  1. Schedule Your Study Time: Block out at least 3-5 hours a week in your calendar. Treat this time as an unbreakable appointment with your future self.
  2. Set Up Your Sanctuary: Find a small corner of your home where you can keep your decks, your journal, and your course materials. This is your "Professional Office."
  3. Introduce Yourself: Head over to the community forum and share your "Why." Why are you here? What does this career change mean to you?
Coach Sarah's Tip

Embrace the "Beginner's Mind": It can be hard to be a student again when you've been an expert in another field for decades. Give yourself permission to not know everything right away. Curiosity is much more powerful than perfectionism.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is life experience considered a "superpower" in this profession?

Life experience provides deep empathy, perspective, and an understanding of the human condition that allows a reader to connect with clients on a level that goes beyond simple card memorization.

2. What is the primary purpose of the Private Practice Groups (PODs)?

To prevent isolation, provide a safe space for practice readings, offer peer feedback, and ensure accountability throughout the certification process.

3. According to the lesson, what should you do if you feel "imposter syndrome"?

Acknowledge it as fear trying to keep you safe, remind yourself that you are exactly where you are supposed to be, and focus on the value of your unique journey.

4. What are the three immediate steps recommended to start your journey?

1. Schedule study time in your calendar; 2. Set up a dedicated physical sanctuary/workspace; 3. Introduce yourself and your "Why" in the community forum.
