The Psyche as a Landscape: The Major Arcana Map

The Major Arcana is not merely a collection of 22 cards; it is a holistic map of the human psyche. Based on Carl Jung‚Äôs theory of the collective unconscious, these cards represent "primordial images" that exist within every human being, regardless of culture or era.

In the O.R.A.C.L.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, we view the Major Arcana as the Macro-Environment of the seeker. While the Minor Arcana describes the "weather" (daily events, emotions, thoughts), the Major Arcana describes the "climate" (the fundamental soul-contract and psychological stage of development).

Soul Lessons vs. Situational Events

One of the biggest mistakes amateur readers make is treating a Major Arcana card as a mundane event. If The Empress appears, it is rarely just about "having a baby" or "buying a plant." It is a Soul Lesson regarding the archetypal principle of Abundance and Creative Sovereignty.

Situational Events (Minor Arcana): These are transient. They are the "what" of the reading. (e.g., 3 of Swords: A painful conversation).

Soul Lessons (Major Arcana): These are structural. They are the "why" of the reading. (e.g., The Tower: The dismantling of an identity that no longer serves the soul).
