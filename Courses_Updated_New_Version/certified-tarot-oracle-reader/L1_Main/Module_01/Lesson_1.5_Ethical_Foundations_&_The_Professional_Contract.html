The AccrediPro Code of Ethics

Professionalism is the bridge between a "parlor trick" and a "transformational consultation." As a Certified Tarot & Oracle Reader‚Ñ¢, you adhere to a standard that prioritizes seeker autonomy and practitioner integrity.

The 7 Pillars of Professional Conduct

  1. Non-Prescriptive Guidance: We provide archetypal insight, not medical, legal, or financial prescriptions.
  2. Radical Confidentiality: The seeker's data, questions, and cards are sacred and never shared without explicit written consent.
  3. Seeker Autonomy: We never tell a seeker what they "must" do; we present options reflected in the archetypes.
  4. Non-Judgmental Presence: We hold space for all lifestyles, beliefs, and choices without personal bias.
  5. Informed Consent: Seekers must understand the nature of the reading and its limitations before the first card is drawn.
  6. Professional Development: A commitment to ongoing study of the O.R.A.C.L.E. Framework‚Ñ¢ and psychological archetypes.
  7. Referral Integrity: Recognizing when a seeker‚Äôs needs exceed our scope (e.g., active crisis, clinical depression) and referring to licensed professionals.

Scope of Practice: Guidance vs. Advice

The most common legal pitfall for intuitive practitioners is "practicing without a license." In the United States and many other regions, giving specific medical, legal, or financial advice is a regulated activity. To protect your business‚Äîwhich can comfortably earn $150-$300 per hour‚Äîyou must master the Scope of Practice.

Legal Discussing the Justice archetype and personal integrity. "You will win your lawsuit" or "Sign this contract today."

The "Contract of Truth": Fate vs. Free Will

A professional reader never claims to see a "fixed" future. Within the O.R.A.C.L.E. Framework‚Ñ¢, we view the cards as a map of current momentum, not a set-in-stone destiny.

We establish a Contract of Truth with the seeker: "The cards show where you are headed if nothing changes. Because you have free will, you have the power to change the outcome by changing your resonance."

The Ethics of the Absent: Third-Party Readings

One of the most frequent requests you will receive is: "What is my ex-boyfriend thinking?" or "Is my boss going to fire my colleague?"

Reading for a person who has not given consent is often considered "Energetic Trespassing." Ethically, we focus the lens back on the seeker. A professional practitioner shifts the question:

  • Seeker asks: "Is my ex dating someone else?"
  • Professional shift: "Let‚Äôs look at what is blocking your heart from moving forward and finding new love."

Professional Distance & Empathy

As a woman in the 40-55 age demographic, you likely have high natural empathy. While this is your superpower, it can also lead to "Compassion Fatigue."

Professional distance does not mean being cold; it means being a "Clear Mirror." If you take on the seeker's pain, you can no longer see the cards clearly. You must maintain the Architect perspective‚Äîobserving the patterns without falling into the emotional well with the client.

The Professional Contract: Implementing Informed Consent

Before any exchange of money or energy, your seeker should agree to an Informed Consent statement. This can be a checkbox on your booking form. It should include:

  • A statement that the reading is for "educational and archetypal insight purposes."
  • A clear "18+ only" policy (unless parental consent is provided).
  • Your cancellation and refund policy.
  • The explicit disclaimer: "This reading does not replace professional medical, legal, or psychiatric help."

Check Your Understanding

1. A seeker asks if they should sell their stocks because "The Tower" appeared in their career spread. What is the most ethical response?

You must state that you are not a financial advisor. Reframe the card to discuss the energy of sudden change and ask them how they can create a more stable foundation, then suggest they consult a licensed financial professional.

2. Why is "Radical Confidentiality" essential for a premium-priced practice?

High-value clients (executives, business owners) share sensitive information. If they don't feel 100% safe, they won't go deep. Confidentiality is the "product" just as much as the reading itself.

3. What is "Energetic Trespassing"?

Attempting to read the private thoughts or future of someone who has not given consent. Ethically, we always pull the focus back to the seeker who is actually in the room/on the call.

4. How does the "Contract of Truth" help with imposter syndrome?

It removes the pressure of "being right" about a fixed future. Instead, your job is to accurately interpret the current archetypal patterns, which is a skill you can master through the O.R.A.C.L.E. Framework‚Ñ¢.

Key Takeaways

  • Stay in Your Lane: Never cross the line into medical, legal, or financial advice.
  • Seeker Agency: Always prioritize the seeker's free will over "fated" predictions.
  • Focus on the Seeker: Avoid third-party readings; keep the lens on the person who sought your guidance.
  • The Mirror Metaphor: Be an empathetic but detached mirror to avoid burnout.
