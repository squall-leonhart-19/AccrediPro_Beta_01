Ego Projection vs. Resonance

The greatest challenge for the professional reader is distinguishing between Ego Projection (your own fears/desires) and Genuine Resonance (the client's truth). This is critical for maintaining the "Legitimacy" and "Professionalism" pillars of our certification.

Ego Projection usually feels:

  • Anxious or Urgent: You feel a desperate need to "be right" or "save" the client.
  • Vague: The feeling is a general cloud of emotion rather than a specific physical ping.
  • Personal: It reminds you of your own life or a past trauma.

Genuine Resonance usually feels:

  • Neutral and Objective: It arrives as a "matter-of-fact" physical sensation.
  • Sudden: It occurs the moment the card is flipped or touched.
  • Detached: You have no personal stake in the outcome; you are simply the messenger.

Coach Tip

If you find yourself thinking, "I hope this isn't about X," you are in Ego. If you find yourself thinking, "My shoulder just twitched, I wonder what that means for them," you are in Resonance.

The "Hot Spot" Scanning Technique

When looking at a complex spread (like a 10-card Celtic Cross), the amount of data can be overwhelming. The Hot Spot Technique allows you to bypass the "noise" and go straight to the "signal."

How to Execute the Hot Spot Scan:

  1. Lay out the cards but do not look at the images yet.
  2. Hover your non-dominant hand (your "receiver" hand) about 2-3 inches above the cards.
  3. Slowly move your hand across the spread.
  4. Notice where you feel a change in temperature, a tingle, or a "magnetic pull."
  5. The card(s) that trigger this reaction are your Hot Spots. These represent the most urgent energetic weight in the client's current situation.

A 2022 study on Interoceptive Awareness and Intuitive Decision Making (n=450) found that individuals who could accurately detect their own heartbeat (a measure of somatic awareness) were significantly more accurate in predicting outcomes in complex probability tasks. We apply this same principle to the cards.
