Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it critical to address the "Root Chakra" (finances/safety) before spiritual purpose in Elena's case?

Because the nervous system's "Safety First" mechanism (controlled by the amygdala) will block higher-order cognitive and spiritual insights if it perceives a threat to survival (financial ruin). Grounding ensures the client is "regulated" enough to integrate spiritual guidance.

2. Elena draws the 10 of Wands and the 6 of Pentacles Reversed. What does this combination likely indicate in a clinical context?

This is a classic "Caregiver Burnout" signature. The 10 of Wands shows the literal burden of her responsibilities, while the 6 of Pentacles reversed indicates a severe imbalance in giving vs. receiving‚Äîshe is pouring from an empty cup and likely feels unappreciated or resentful.

3. Which card would be a "Red Flag" for potential referral if accompanied by a flat affect and lack of hygiene?

The 4 of Cups or the 5 of Cups, especially if they appear repeatedly. In a clinical setting, this can indicate "Anhedonia" (the inability to feel pleasure), which is a key symptom of clinical depression requiring a medical referral.

4. What is the "Anchor" in Elena's case?

Her identity as a "Caregiver." This archetype is the common thread through her career as a nurse, her role as a mother, and her role as a daughter. Shifting this identity is the key to her long-term recovery.

Key Takeaways
