Standardized Reporting: The 'Spread Anatomy' Breakdown

Professional supervision begins with a clear, objective record of what occurred in a session. Without a standardized reporting format, case reviews become subjective and muddled. The Spread Anatomy Breakdown is our clinical standard for reporting difficult or complex readings.

A professional case report should include five critical data points:

  1. The Inquiry Baseline: The specific question asked, including the client's emotional state (Orientation).
  2. The Structural Layout: The specific cards pulled and their positions.
  3. The Primary Archetypal Hit: The initial interpretation delivered to the client.
  4. The Somatic Response: What the reader felt in their body during the pull (Resonance).
  5. The Pivot Point: Where the reading shifted from information to action (Empowerment).

Diagnosing 'Resonance' Failures

A "Resonance Failure" occurs when the reader's intuitive signal is either blocked, distorted, or misinterpreted. In a clinical review, we categorize these failures to ensure they don't become habitual patterns.

Common categories of resonance failure include:

  • Projection: The reader sees their own current life struggle in the client's cards.
  • Signal Interference: Physical fatigue or environmental noise disrupting the "Clairs."
  • Premature Synthesis: Jumping to a conclusion before the full spread is analyzed.

Refining the 'Clarity' Delivery

The Clarity (Phase 4) of our framework is where most reader-client disconnects happen. You may have the right "hit," but if the delivery is muddled, the client leaves confused. Clinical protocols for refining clarity involve reviewing session recordings or transcripts (with client consent) to analyze:

  • Jargon Usage: Are you using too much "Tarot-speak" (e.g., "Your 8th house is activated")?
  • The "Pivot" Speed: Did you spend too much time on the problem and not enough on the solution?
  • Empowerment Language: Are you using "The cards say you must..." (Disempowering) or "The cards suggest an opportunity to..." (Empowering)?

Long-Term Documentation & Record-Keeping

For long-term client supervision (6+ months), documentation is non-negotiable. While we do not fall under HIPAA in most jurisdictions, maintaining Professional Intuitive Records (PIR) builds massive credibility and helps you track client progress over time.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary purpose of the 'Spread Anatomy' breakdown in a professional setting?

To provide a standardized, objective record of a session so that it can be accurately reviewed by a supervisor or peer group without the distortion of subjective memory.

2. In Collaborative Archetypal Analysis (CAA), what is a "Void Analysis"?

An analysis of which elements (Earth, Air, Fire, Water) are *missing* from a spread, which often indicates where the client is lacking energy or focus.

3. How does "Projection" manifest as a resonance failure?

Projection occurs when a reader unknowingly interprets the cards through the lens of their own personal problems or current life situation, rather than focusing on the client's unique context.

4. Why is the "24-Hour Rule" recommended for session documentation?

Documentation should be completed within 24 hours of a session while the somatic resonance and nuances of the client's energy are still fresh in the reader's memory.
