Check Your Understanding

1. Why is validation the first step in the L1 Deep Dive script?

Validation reduces the client's cortisol and shame, opening their "prefrontal cortex" so they can actually process the recovery plan you are proposing.

2. What is the "L1 Foundational Gap"?

It is the period where a mother's physiological and emotional needs were unmet during the initial postpartum phase, leading to long-term depletion that persists years later.

3. How should a coach handle a client who says, "I've already tried vitamins and they didn't work"?

Acknowledge their effort, then explain that vitamins without a foundational recovery of the nervous system and gut (the L1 pillars) often cannot be properly absorbed or utilized by the body.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Pause" technique?

The pause allows the client to sit with the validation you've provided and prevents the coach from "over-talking" out of nervousness, which can undermine professional authority.

Key Takeaways

  • Recovery is a physiological process, not a chronological one; time alone does not heal depletion.
  • Your voice is your most powerful coaching tool; practice professional authority through consistent scripts.
  • Validation is the "bridge" that allows a client to move from shame into action.
  • Professionalism includes setting appropriate boundaries and pricing for your specialized expertise.
  • Success is measured by the client's shift from self-blame to physiological understanding.
