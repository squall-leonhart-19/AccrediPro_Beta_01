Common Pitfalls for New Somatic Coaches

The Pitfall Why it Happens The Somatic Correction
Rushing the Silence We feel awkward when the client is "just feeling." Wait 5 seconds longer than you think you should. Silence is where the body speaks.
Over-Explaining Imposter syndrome makes us want to prove we know the science. Save the science for the end. Keep the session in the "felt sense."
Ignoring Your Own Body We get "hooked" by the client's frantic energy. If the client is breathing fast, you must breathe slow. Use co-regulation.
Focusing Only on "Negative" We only look for where it hurts. Ask: "Where in your body feels even 5% neutral or comfortable right now?"

Success Markers: How to Know it Worked

You don't need a client to say "I'm cured" to know the session was a success. Look for these physiological shifts:

  • The Spontaneous Sigh: A deep, involuntary exhale is the #1 sign of a nervous system shift.
  • Softening of the Eyes: The "fixed" stare of high stress relaxes into a softer gaze.
  • Skin Color Changes: Blood returning to the face (a slight flush) indicates a move out of the "freeze" state.
  • Slower Speech: The client moves from "staccato" talking to a more rhythmic flow.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client is speaking very quickly and seems "stuck in their head," what is the most effective somatic first step?

Interrupt the "story" gently and ask them to notice a physical sensation (like the weight of their feet or the temperature of their hands) to ground them in the present moment.

2. What is "co-regulation" in a coaching context?

It is the process where the coachâ€™s calm, regulated nervous system helps the clientâ€™s dysregulated nervous system return to a state of safety through tone of voice, presence, and steady breathing.

3. Why do we ask "What do you notice?" instead of "How do you feel?"

"How do you feel?" often leads to an emotional label (e.g., "I feel sad"). "What do you notice?" directs the client to the raw physical sensation (e.g., "I notice a tightness in my throat"), which is the gateway to somatic processing.

4. Which physiological sign indicates a client has moved from the Sympathetic (Stress) state to the Ventral Vagal (Safety) state?

A spontaneous deep sigh, a softening of the jaw/shoulders, and a slower, more rhythmic heart rate and speech pattern.
