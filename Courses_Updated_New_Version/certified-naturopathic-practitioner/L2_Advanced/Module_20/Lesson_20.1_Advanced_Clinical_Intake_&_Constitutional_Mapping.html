The Four Temperaments in Modern Practice

Dating back to Hippocratic medicine but validated by modern psychology, the four temperaments provide a lens into the neuro-endocrine profile of your client.

  • Sanguine (Air): Enthusiastic, social, but often lacks follow-through. Protocol Strategy: Keep it simple and exciting; provide frequent check-ins.
  • Choleric (Fire): Goal-oriented, decisive, prone to "heat" (inflammation). Protocol Strategy: Focus on cooling foods and clear, measurable KPIs.
  • Melancholic (Earth): Analytical, detail-oriented, prone to stagnation. Protocol Strategy: Provide scientific evidence and focus on gut-brain axis support.
  • Phlegmatic (Water): Calm, steady, but resistant to change. Protocol Strategy: Slow, incremental habit shifts; focus on metabolic stimulation.
Coach Tip

When you identify a Choleric client, don't give them a vague plan. They want to know exactly what to do and how fast they will see results. If you don't match their intensity, they will lose confidence in your expertise.

Clinical Timeline Mapping: ATM

To truly Analyze Root Causes (the 'A' in N.A.T.U.R.E.), we must map the ATM. This is a visual timeline of the client's life that identifies three critical factors:

A

Antecedents

The "pre-existing" conditions. Genetics, birth method (C-section vs. vaginal), childhood environment, and early trauma.

T

Triggers

The "precipitating" events. An infection, a divorce, a period of extreme overwork, or a specific toxic exposure.

M

Mediators

The "perpetuating" factors. Poor sleep, ongoing stress, nutrient deficiencies, or hidden food sensitivities that keep the body in a state of dysfunction.

The 7-Day Holistic Audit

Data is only as good as its accuracy. While a client's memory may fail, a 7-Day Holistic Audit provides a real-time snapshot of their internal and external environment. This audit tracks:

  • The Food-Mood Connection: Tracking what they eat and how they feel 2 hours later.
  • Elimination Patterns: Frequency, consistency, and ease of bowel movements.
  • Sleep Architecture: Time to fall asleep, mid-night wakings, and morning energy levels.
  • The Stress Pulse: Identifying the specific times of day when perceived stress is highest.
Coach Tip

The Holistic Audit often reveals "hidden" stressors, like the 3 PM energy crash caused by a high-carb lunch, or the 2 AM waking caused by blood sugar drops. Use these "Aha!" moments to build your client's health literacy.

Advanced Interviewing: Uncovering Hidden Stressors

Motivational Interviewing (MI) is a collaborative, person-centered form of guiding to elicit and strengthen motivation for change. As a career changer, your life experience is your greatest asset here. You are not just a "technician"; you are a guide.

Key Technique: The OARS Model

  • Open-ended questions: "Tell me more about how your fatigue affects your relationship with your children."
  • Affirmations: "It sounds like you have been incredibly resilient despite these challenges."
  • Reflective listening: "It sounds like you're feeling frustrated because you've tried so many things without success."
  • Summarizing: "Let me see if I have this right... [summarize their story]."
Coach Tip

As a practitioner in your 40s or 50s, you have a unique "authority of empathy." Use it. When a client sees that you understand the pressures of perimenopause, caregiving, or career pivots, the therapeutic bond is forged instantly.
