1. Tongue Topography

The tongue provides a real-time snapshot of the GI tract and the Vital Force. A geographic tongue (migratory glossitis) is often a subclinical indicator of B-vitamin deficiencies or zinc depletion, occurring in approximately 2-3% of the population but appearing much more frequently in clients with autoimmune tendencies.

2. Nail Bed Analysis

Vertical ridges (onychorrhexis) are not just a sign of aging; they often indicate hypochlorhydria (low stomach acid) and poor protein assimilation. In a clinical review of 200 patients, 64% of those with significant vertical ridging also tested positive for sub-optimal pepsin levels.

3. Iris Markings (Subclinical Iridology)

While we do not use iridology for diagnosis, we use "iris mapping" to identify tissue density and inherited areas of low resistance. Mapping Temperament to Autonomic Tone

How a client reacts to the world tells you how their cells are reacting to internal stressors. We categorize these into the four traditional temperaments, mapped to Autonomic Nervous System (ANS) tone:

  • Choleric (The Leader): High sympathetic drive. Prone to "burnout" through sheer force of will. Biochemical markers: High catecholamines, low secretory IgA.
  • Sanguine (The Socialite): High volatility. Prone to histamine issues and "allergic" responses to life. Biochemical markers: Fluctuating blood sugar, high histamine.
  • Melancholic (The Thinker): High parasympathetic "freeze" response. Prone to stasis and toxicity. Biochemical markers: Low stomach acid, poor Phase II detoxification.
  • Phlegmatic (The Peacemaker): Low metabolic fire. Prone to lymphatic congestion and thyroid sluggishness. Biochemical markers: Elevated C-Reactive Protein (CRP), high triglycerides.
