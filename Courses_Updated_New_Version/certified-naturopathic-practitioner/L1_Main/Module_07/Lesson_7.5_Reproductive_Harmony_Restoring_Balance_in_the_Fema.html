The 'Estrogen Dominance' Pattern

Estrogen dominance is rarely about having "too much" estrogen in absolute terms; it is typically about a relative deficiency of progesterone or a failure to clear used estrogens through the liver and gut.

Root Causes: Why Estrogen Accumulates

  • Impaired Hepatic Phase II Detoxification: Specifically the methylation and glucuronidation pathways (refer to Module 4).
  • The 'Estrobolome': Certain gut bacteria produce beta-glucuronidase, an enzyme that "un-clips" estrogen destined for excretion, allowing it to be reabsorbed into the bloodstream.
  • Xenoestrogens: Environmental toxicants (BPA, phthalates) that mimic estrogen at the receptor site.

PCOS: The Metabolic-Endocrine Crossroad

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is primarily a disorder of insulin signaling. Elevated insulin stimulates the theca cells in the ovaries to produce excess androgens (testosterone), leading to acne, hair loss, and anovulation. Restoring balance requires the A: Analyze approach‚Äîchecking fasting insulin and HbA1c before reaching for hormone-blockers.

Navigating the Transition: Perimenopause

Perimenopause is the 2-10 year transition leading to menopause. It is characterized by hormonal chaos, not just hormonal decline. Progesterone is often the first to drop, leading to the estrogen dominance symptoms discussed earlier.

Bone Density Preservation

As estrogen fluctuates and eventually declines, bone resorption outpaces bone formation. Naturopathic support must include:

  • Resistance Training: Essential for osteoblastic stimulation.
  • Vitamin K2 (MK-7): To ensure calcium is directed to the bones, not the arteries.
  • Trace Minerals: Boron, Silica, and Manganese are the "forgotten" bone builders.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which enzyme, produced by gut bacteria, can cause the reabsorption of estrogen into the bloodstream?

Beta-glucuronidase. When elevated, it "un-clips" the glucuronic acid molecule from estrogen, allowing it to bypass excretion and re-enter circulation.

2. Why is insulin sensitivity the primary focus in PCOS management?

High levels of insulin signal the ovaries to produce excess androgens (like testosterone), which disrupts the follicle maturation process and prevents ovulation.

3. During which phase of the cycle should a practitioner recommend sesame and sunflower seeds in a seed cycling protocol?

The Luteal Phase (Days 15-28), to provide the selenium and Vitamin E necessary for healthy progesterone production.

4. What is the primary mechanism of Vitex agnus-castus?

It modulates the pituitary gland to lower prolactin and support the surge of LH, which encourages the corpus luteum to produce more progesterone.
