Common Mistakes to Avoid

When practitioners first learn the "Restore" phase, they often fall into these traps:

  • The "Expert" Overload: Giving the client a 10-page protocol on day one. (Result: Client gets overwhelmed and quits).
  • Ignoring the "Why": Telling a client to meditate without explaining how it lowers the cortisol that is keeping their belly fat on.
  • Being Too Rigid: Insisting on a 2-hour morning routine for a busy mom.
  • Apologizing for the Plan: Saying "I'm sorry, I know this is boring, but we need to sleep." (Confidence is contagiousâ€”own the plan!).

Success Markers: How You Know It Worked

A successful "Restore" coaching session doesn't end with the client losing 10lbs. It ends with these shifts:

  1. The "Sigh of Relief": The client visibly relaxes because they finally feel understood.
  2. The "Lightbulb Moment": They realize that their "laziness" was actually physiological exhaustion.
  3. Micro-Commitment: They agree to one small change (e.g., drinking more water or a 5-minute breathing exercise).
  4. Shift in Language: They stop saying "I should" and start saying "I need."

Check Your Practice Knowledge

1. Why is it often a mistake to start a burnt-out client on a rigorous detox or heavy exercise?

Because these are "stressors" (hormetic or otherwise). If the body is already in a depleted "Restore" state, adding more stressâ€”even "healthy" stressâ€”can lead to further adrenal exhaustion and metabolic shutdown.

2. What is the "Double-Bind" technique in habit setting?

It is giving the client two positive options to choose from (e.g., "Morning sun or evening screen-free time?"). This ensures a restorative action is chosen while giving the client autonomy.

3. If Diane says, "I don't have time to sleep 8 hours," what is the best coaching response?

Validate her stress first ("I hear how busy your schedule is"), then pivot to the benefit ("What if 7 hours of high-quality sleep made your 10 hours of work feel like 6 hours because your brain was sharper?").

4. What is a "Restoration Anchor"?

A small, non-negotiable daily habit that signals safety to the nervous system, such as 5 minutes of deep breathing or a consistent wake-up time.

Lab Summary: The Path to Restoration

  • Safety First: The body cannot heal in a state of perceived danger (chronic stress).
  • Language Matters: Use analogies (like the tired horse) to help clients grasp complex physiological states.
  • Pivot the Goal: Move the client from "weight loss at all costs" to "energy restoration for sustainable health."
  • Practice is Mastery: Speaking the dialogue out loud builds the confidence needed to handle high-achieving clients.
