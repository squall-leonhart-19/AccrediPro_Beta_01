Objective Bio-Feedback: HRV & BBT

In modern naturopathy, we leverage technology to validate intuition. Two of the most powerful objective markers are Heart Rate Variability (HRV) and Basal Body Temperature (BBT).

Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV measures the variation in time between each heartbeat. Contrary to popular belief, a "steady" heart rate like a metronome is actually a sign of stress. A high HRV indicates a resilient autonomic nervous system capable of switching between the sympathetic (fight/flight) and parasympathetic (rest/digest) branches.

  • High HRV: Indicates recovery, readiness, and parasympathetic dominance.
  • Low HRV: Indicates systemic stress, overtraining, or impending illness.

Basal Body Temperature (BBT)

BBT is the body's temperature at rest, measured immediately upon waking. In naturopathic philosophy, BBT is a direct window into thyroid function and metabolic rate. For women of reproductive age, it also tracks the ovulatory cycle (the "Fifth Vital Sign").
