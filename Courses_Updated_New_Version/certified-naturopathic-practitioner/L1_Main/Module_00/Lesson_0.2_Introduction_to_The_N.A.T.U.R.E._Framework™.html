Your Realistic Timeline to Income

While some programs promise "six figures in six weeks," we prefer the Sustainable Growth Model. Here is what a typical path looks like for a dedicated practitioner:

üìÖ
The Practitioner's Growth Roadmap

Months 1-3: The Foundation
Focus on completing your certification and working with "beta" clients (often at a discount) to gather case studies and testimonials. Income: $0 - $1,500/mo.

Months 4-6: The Launch
Official launch of your practice. You begin charging professional rates ($150-$250/hour). You secure your first 5-10 consistent clients. Income: $1,500 - $4,000/mo.

Months 7-12: The Momentum
Referrals start coming in. You might introduce a small group program or a digital guide. You are becoming a known "expert" in your niche. Income: $5,000 - $8,000/mo.

Year 2+: The Scale
You have a waitlist. You increase your rates or move toward a "one-to-many" model. Many practitioners hit the $100k+ mark during this phase. Income: $10,000 - $25,000+/mo.

The Math that Makes Sense

Let's demystify the numbers. To earn a high income, you don't need thousands of clients; you need a strategic offer.

  • Low Volume / High Value: 10 clients paying $1,000 for a 3-month "Hormone Reset" package = $10,000.
  • Moderate Volume / Group Model: 20 people in a $500 6-week "Gut Health Intensive" = $10,000.
  • The Hybrid Approach: 5 private clients ($3,000) + 1 corporate workshop ($2,000) + Supplement commissions ($500) = $5,500/month.
Sarah's Insight

Most career changers try to charge by the hour. Don't do this. Charge for the result. A client isn't paying for 60 minutes of your time; they are paying to finally get rid of the brain fog that has plagued them for a decade. Package your services into 3 or 6-month transformations.

Check Your Understanding

1. Which career model is generally considered the most "scalable" for high income without increasing working hours?

The "One-to-Many" or Group/Digital Course model. This allows you to serve 20, 50, or 100+ people in the same time it takes to see one private client.

2. According to the "Math of Practice," how many clients paying $1,000 for a package do you need to reach a $10,000 month?

You only need 10 clients. This illustrates why "High-Value Packaging" is more sustainable than "Hourly Billing."

3. What is the primary focus of the first 3 months (The Foundation phase) of the growth roadmap?

Completing certification, working with beta clients, and gathering testimonials/case studies to build professional social proof.

4. True or False: You must choose only one career path (e.g., only private practice) to be successful.

False. Most successful practitioners use a "Hybrid Model" that combines 1-on-1 work, group programs, and passive income streams like supplements or digital guides.
