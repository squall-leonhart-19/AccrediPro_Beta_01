Section 3: Presenting Your Prices with Authority

One of the biggest hurdles for new practitioners is the "price drop." Many practitioners lower their voice or keep talking after stating the price. The secret is the "Golden Silence."

When you state your price, stop talking. Let the client process the value. A 2022 study on consumer psychology indicated that "over-explaining" a price immediately after stating it triggers a "defense response" in the buyer, making them more likely to decline.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the "Golden Silence" in a discovery call?

The practice of remaining silent immediately after stating your program's price. This allows the prospect to process the value and prevents the practitioner from "talking them out of it" due to their own discomfort.

2. If a prospect says "I need to think about it," what is the most professional next step?

Acknowledge their need for time, then ask a clarifying question to uncover the underlying concern (e.g., "Is there a specific part of the program you have questions about, or is it the investment?").

3. Why is it better to sell a 12-week "Package" rather than single sessions?

Packages emphasize the transformation and results rather than trading time for money. They also provide the practitioner with stable, predictable income and ensure the client commits to the time needed for real energetic shifts.

4. How many new clients per month (at $2,400 each) does it take to exceed a $100k annual income?

Approximately 3.5 clients per month (42 clients per year) would generate $100,800 in gross revenue.
