4. The Feedback Dialogue

How you deliver this information is as important as the information itself. Use the Validation-Inquiry-Instruction model.

Step 1: Validation

"Elena, I want to acknowledge how thorough your notes are. Your technical application of Root Chakra protocols is exactly as we taught in Level 1. It‚Äôs clear you care deeply about Diane‚Äôs progress."

Step 2: Inquiry

"When you were scanning Diane's Solar Plexus, even though it wasn't the focus, what did you notice? And how did your own energy feel when she said she wasn't getting better?"

Step 3: Instruction

"In these cases, we often see a 'Power Drain.' The Root can't stabilize because the Solar Plexus is leaking energy through a need for control. Next time, I want you to spend the first 10 minutes on the Solar Plexus before even touching the Root. Let's see if that provides the 'anchor' the Root needs."
Luna's Leadership Rule

Always leave your mentee with one specific "Clinical Experiment" to try. It moves them from a state of "failure" into a state of "research."

5. Supervision Best Practices: Do's and Don'ts

To maintain your status as a Master Practitioner, you must adhere to the ASI Standards for Clinical Supervision. This ensures you are protected legally and professionally.

  • DO: Maintain a separate file for each mentee documenting their case presentations and your feedback.
  • DO: Set clear time boundaries. If a session is 50 minutes, end at 50 minutes.
  • DON'T: Become their therapist. If the mentee‚Äôs personal trauma is blocking their work, refer them to another practitioner for healing.
  • DON'T: Give the answer immediately. Use Socratic questioning to help them find the link themselves.

Check Your Understanding

1. Elena is panicked because her client isn't improving. What is the first step in the Validation-Inquiry-Instruction model?

The first step is Validation. You must acknowledge the practitioner's effort and technical proficiency before offering corrections to lower their defensive "imposter" response.

2. Why is it clinically relevant to ask Elena how HER energy felt during the session?

This addresses Counter-transference. If the practitioner feels anxious or like a "failure," that energy can disrupt the healing field, making it harder for the client's chakras to stabilize.

3. If a mentee starts crying about their own childhood during a case review, what should the supervisor do?

Maintain professional boundaries. Acknowledge the emotion, but gently steer back to the case or recommend they see a practitioner for their own healing. Supervision is not therapy.

4. What is the primary goal of the "Clinical Experiment" approach?

To shift the mentee's mindset from binary success/failure to observational clinical reasoning. It builds their confidence as a "detective" of energy.

  • Clinical supervision is a high-value professional service that adds legitimacy and income to your practice.
