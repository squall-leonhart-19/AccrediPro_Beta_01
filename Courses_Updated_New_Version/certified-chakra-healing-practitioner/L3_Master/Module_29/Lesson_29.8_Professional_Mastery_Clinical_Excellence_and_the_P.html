5. Supervision Best Practices

Effective supervision is a professional service. As a Master Practitioner, you can offer these sessions for $150‚Äì$250 per hour. This not only increases your income but also establishes you as a leader in the community.

  • Maintain the "Supervisory Space": This is not a therapy session for Sarah. Keep the focus on her clinical skills and her relationship with the client.
  • The 80/20 Rule: Let Sarah speak for 80% of the time. Ask open-ended questions like, "What was your intention with that specific crystal placement?"

Check Your Understanding

1. What was the primary energetic mistake Sarah made in Elena's case?

She over-activated the Solar Plexus (Manipura) without ensuring the Root Chakra (Muladhara) was sufficiently grounded to hold the increased energy flow.

2. Why is the "Sandwich Method" or "Validation-Education-Empowerment" loop critical when mentoring?

It prevents the mentee from becoming defensive or losing confidence. By validating their intent first, they are more open to the clinical correction (education) and more likely to apply the change (empowerment).

3. According to the 80/20 rule of supervision, what should the mentor be doing most of the time?

Listening and asking probing, open-ended questions that encourage the mentee to develop their own clinical reasoning skills.

4. What is a key indicator that a case review has moved outside the scope of energy healing?

If the client presents with severe physical symptoms (like intense burning gastric pain) or mental health crises that require medical or psychological intervention.
