Check Your Understanding

1. How does a "Muladhara Rupture" differ from a standard chakra blockage?

A blockage is characterized by stagnation and density (feeling "stuck"), whereas a rupture is characterized by a loss of field integrity, coldness, and energy leaking out (feeling "shattered" or "dissociated").

2. What is the primary goal of the "Safety Seal" technique?

The primary goal is containment. It repairs the porous or shattered boundaries of the energetic field to prevent further leakage of vital force and to provide a stable container for subsequent healing steps.

3. Why is it recommended to start the "Dissolving" (D) process at the feet in trauma cases?

This is called "titration." Starting at the periphery (feet/ankles) allows the nervous system to release small amounts of trapped energy without overwhelming the client or triggering a massive re-experiencing of the core trauma.

4. Which physical muscle is most associated with the "frozen flight impulse" in the Root Chakra?

The psoas muscle. It is the primary connector between the spine and the legs and is deeply involved in the body's instinctual fight-flight response.
