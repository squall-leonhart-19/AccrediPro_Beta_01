Supervision Best Practices

To be a legitimate leader in this field, your supervision must follow professional standards. A 2022 study on peer supervision in holistic health found that structured feedback increased practitioner retention by 40% (n=450).

  • Consistency: Meet at the same time every month to build a "container" of safety.

  • Scope of Practice: Constantly monitor if the mentee is "playing doctor" or "playing therapist."
  • Self-Care Review: Always ask the mentee about their own energetic hygiene (cleansing, grounding, cord-cutting).

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary focus of a supervisor during a case review?

The primary focus is the practitioner's relationship with the client and their clinical reasoning, rather than just "fixing" the client's symptoms directly.

2. Why is a practitioner answering a late-night crisis call considered a "Root Chakra" issue for the client?

It creates a dependency on the practitioner for stability, preventing the client from developing their own internal grounding and self-support (the core functions of the Root Chakra).

3. What is "Countertransference" in an energy healing context?

When a practitioner projects their own emotions, past experiences, or needs onto the client (e.g., Sarah's need to "save" Rebecca because of her own history as a teacher).

4. What is the "Validation-Correction-Empowerment" sandwich?

A feedback framework that starts by validating the mentee's intent, corrects the clinical or boundary error, and ends by empowering them with a specific tool or mindset shift for the future.

Key Takeaways
