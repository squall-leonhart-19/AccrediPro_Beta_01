The Resonant Assessment (R) of Relationship Fields

In the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢, Resonant Assessment usually focuses on the client's internal system. In relationship work, we assess the Relational Field. This field is a distinct energetic entity created by the interaction of two people's auric fields.

When assessing a relationship, look for Energetic Cords. According to a 2022 survey of holistic practitioners, approximately 85% of clients presenting with "chronic fatigue" or "unexplained resentment" show significant energetic attachments to former or current partners. These cords are most commonly found in three locations:

  • The Root (Ancestral/Survival): Cords here relate to financial dependence or shared domestic fears.

Dissolving (D) Hooks and Cords: A Professional Protocol

The term "cord cutting" is often used loosely in wellness circles, but in a professional RADIANT practice, it is a surgical energetic process. We do not "cut" love; we dissolve parasitic attachments.

The 3-Step Dissolving Protocol:

  1. Identification: Use hand scanning to locate the exact anchor point of the hook (usually the back of the chakra).
  2. Neutralization: Use frequency (Sound or Violet Light visualization) to "vibrate" the hook loose. A hook cannot stay anchored in a high-frequency field.
  3. Sealing: Once the cord is dissolved, the "wound" in the aura must be sealed with Anchoring Frequency (A) to prevent re-attachment.
Coach Tip

Always ask for the client's conscious permission before dissolving a cord. Even toxic cords can provide a false sense of security. The client must be ready to stand in their own power before you remove the "crutch" of an external energetic attachment.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the difference between a "Healthy Cord" and an "Energetic Hook"?

A healthy cord is a reciprocal, pulsating flow of energy (usually Heart-to-Heart) that supports both individuals. A hook is a one-way, parasitic attachment (usually in the Solar Plexus or Sacral) where one person's energy is being drained or controlled by another.

2. Why is the Root chakra assessed first in couples' work?

The Root chakra governs safety and survival. If a couple does not feel energetically "safe" with each other at a foundational level, higher-level work (like Heart or Sacral intimacy) will be unstable and temporary.

3. What does the "Sacral-Heart Axis" refer to?

It refers to the energetic bridge between physical/sexual intimacy (Sacral) and emotional vulnerability/love (Heart). Healing this axis ensures that physical connection is grounded in emotional safety.

4. In the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢, what is the purpose of the "Nourishing Ritual" after a cord-cutting session?

To establish energetic sovereignty and prevent re-attachment. It involves sealing the aura and practicing boundary-setting to ensure the client remains in their own power.
