Common Practitioner Pitfalls

When you're starting out, it's easy to fall into these traps. Recognize them so you can pivot quickly!

  • The "Prescription" Trap: Giving the client a list of 10 things to do. Correction: Let the client choose 1-2 rituals they actually enjoy.
  • Ignoring the "Why": Telling them to eat yellow foods without explaining the connection to the Solar Plexus. Correction: Always link the physical action to the energetic outcome.
  • The "Perfection" Standard: Making the ritual too long or complex. Correction: A 3-minute ritual done daily is 100x better than a 60-minute ritual done once.
  • Over-Apologizing: Feeling bad for asking the client to set boundaries. Correction: Remember that boundaries are the "container" for their healing.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to let Elena choose her "yellow food" (like ginger tea) rather than just telling her to drink it?

Autonomy is a key component of the Solar Plexus (Manipura). By allowing Elena to choose, you are already helping her exercise her "willpower" and "choice," which strengthens the very chakra you are trying to heal.

2. What is the primary difference between a "habit" and a "ritual" in this coaching context?

A habit is a mechanical action (brushing teeth). A ritual is a habit performed with conscious intention and energetic focus (brushing teeth while visualizing the clearing of the Throat Chakra).

3. If Elena says, "I don't have time for this," what is the best coaching response?

Acknowledge the feeling of overwhelm, then "shrink the change." Ask: "If we couldn't do 15 minutes, could we do 2 minutes? What is the smallest version of this ritual we could start with tomorrow?"

4. How does the "Power Breath" (Breath of Fire) support the Solar Plexus?

It physically stimulates the diaphragm and abdominal area (the seat of the Solar Plexus) and increases internal heat (Agni), which helps "burn off" energetic stagnation and lethargy.
