The Art of Titration: Pacing for Success

One of the most common mistakes made by novice practitioners is trying to "clear everything at once." This leads to Energetic Overwhelm, where the client's nervous system enters a state of high-arousal (sympathetic) or shutdown (dorsal vagal), causing them to abandon the healing process.

We use the principle of Titrationâ€”the process of breaking down a large release into small, manageable "sips."

  • Watch the Breath: If the client starts holding their breath or breathing rapidly, you are moving too fast. Stop the dissolving work and return to Anchoring (the 'A' in RADIANT).
  • Check the Eyes: "Glassy" eyes or a fixed stare indicate dissociation. Bring the client back to the room before continuing.
  • The 70% Rule: Aim to dissolve only 70% of a blockage in a single session. This leaves the client with enough energetic "bandwidth" to integrate the change without crashing.

Crisis vs. Mismatch: The Practitioner's Discernment

It is vital to distinguish between a Healing Crisis (which is productive) and an Energetic Mismatch (which is harmful). Use the following criteria for your clinical assessment:

Signs of a Productive Healing Crisis:

  • Symptoms usually appear 12-48 hours after a session.
  • The client feels a sense of "relief" or "lightness" underneath the discomfort.
  • Symptoms are temporary (lasting 1-3 days).
  • The client feels a sense of empowerment or "truth" about the release.

Signs of an Energetic Mismatch:

  • The client feels "violated" or "intruded upon" during the session.
  • Symptoms are sharp, stabbing, or cause a significant increase in chronic pain.
  • Anxiety levels skyrocket and do not settle with grounding.
  • The practitioner felt they were "forcing" the energy to move.

The Safe Container: Holding the Space

The "Safe Container" is the energetic and psychological boundary you create. For the 40+ woman transitioning into this field, your life experience is your greatest asset here. You are the Steady Presence.

To maintain the container during the Dissolving phase:

  1. Maintain Neutrality: If a client begins to sob, do not rush to "fix" it. Your discomfort with their pain will close their container. Breathe and stay present.
  2. Verbal Pacing: Use soft, slow tones. "You are safe. This is just energy leaving the body. You have plenty of room for this."
  3. Physical Boundaries: Always ask for permission before touching a client during a release. The nervous system is hyper-sensitive during the 'D' phase.
