Common Pitfalls for New Practitioners

I see these four mistakes most often when students are starting out. Watch for these in your own practice today:

  • Over-Explaining: You don't need to give a 20-minute lecture on physics. Give the "hook" and then move to the healing.
  • The "Healer's Whisper": Using a soft, airy voice can actually make clients feel less safe. Use your natural, confident speaking voice.
  • Apologizing for the Price: If you've done the deep dive into the science, your expertise is valuable. Don't say "It's only $X." Say "The investment for this session is $X."
  • Ignoring the Body: Always bring the conversation back to where the client feels the sensation physically.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is it important to mention Diane's Oura ring or HRV data in the consultation?

It creates "Cognitive Buy-In." By using data the client already trusts, you build a bridge of legitimacy between their logical mind and the energetic work you are about to perform.

2. What is the "Bridge Technique"?

It is the process of starting with a physical/scientific symptom or fact (like chest tension or HRV) and connecting it to an energetic concept (the Heart Chakra) to make the healing process feel grounded and professional.

3. If a client says "This sounds a bit woo-woo," how should a professional practitioner respond?

Welcome the skepticism. Explain that the "woo-woo" is often just ancient terminology for what we now call biofield science and nervous system regulation.

4. What does the "Healer's Whisper" pitfall refer to?

The tendency to use an overly soft, "spiritual" voice that can actually undermine your authority and make the client feel less grounded or safe in a professional setting.
