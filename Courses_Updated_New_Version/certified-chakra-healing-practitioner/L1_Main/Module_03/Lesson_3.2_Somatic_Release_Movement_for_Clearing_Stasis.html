Shaking Medicine: Utilizing Neurogenic Tremors

In the animal kingdom, animals instinctively shake after a life-threatening event to discharge the "fight-or-flight" energy. Humans have largely socialized this instinct away, leading to stagnant energy trapped in the nervous system and muscular fascia.

Neurogenic tremors are an innate regulatory mechanism. When we induce these tremors through specific postures, we target the Psoas muscle‚Äîthe primary muscle of the Root and Sacral chakras. This is often called "Shaking Medicine."

The Mechanism of Release

  • Root Center Clearing: Tremors ground the client by discharging excess charge from the lower extremities, resolving "survival mode" stasis.
  • Sacral Center Clearing: The Psoas muscle is deeply connected to the pelvic bowl. Shaking breaks up the "frozen" emotional energy often stored in the hips.
Practitioner Tip

When facilitating shaking, watch for the "freeze" response. If a client stops breathing or their eyes glaze over, they have moved out of the therapeutic window. Gently guide them back with a grounding touch or a vocal cue: "Stay with your breath, you are safe."

Manual Lymphatic Drainage & Energetic Detox

The lymphatic system is the physical counterpart to the energetic "waste removal" system. Unlike the circulatory system, the lymph has no pump; it requires movement. When energy is stagnant, the lymph often follows suit, leading to physical puffiness, brain fog, and fatigue.

Applying MLD Principles to Energy Work:

Safety Protocols: Managing Catharsis

Somatic release can be intense. As a Certified Chakra Healing Practitioner‚Ñ¢, your primary role during release is to hold a "Safe Container."

The 3 Pillars of Safety

  1. Titration: Do not try to clear 20 years of stasis in 20 minutes. Release energy in small, manageable "bites" to avoid overwhelming the nervous system.
  2. Pendulation: Move the client‚Äôs attention between the "stuck" area and a "resource" area (an area of the body that feels safe, strong, or neutral).
  3. Managing Involuntary Movement: If a client begins to shake, twitch, or vocalize, remain calm. This is the body working, not breaking. Ensure they remain on their mat and do not hit any furniture.
Client Communication

Always warn the client: "You may feel emotional or shaky. This is normal and a sign that your body is successfully processing energy. You are in control, and we can stop at any time."
