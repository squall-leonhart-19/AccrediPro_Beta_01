The Physiology of the Solvent: Why Breath Works

To the layperson, breathwork is "relaxing." To the AccrediPro Practitioner, breathwork is chemical modulation. When we talk about "purging" energetic blockages, we are referencing the removal of the physiological markers of stress: stored cortisol, muscular tension, and pH imbalances.

The primary mechanism behind energetic purging is the modulation of Carbon Dioxide (CO2). Through specific patterns of hyper-oxygenation or controlled retention, we alter the blood's pH level. This shift facilitates the Bohr Effect, where a decrease in CO2 (through rapid exhales) increases the blood's pH, making it more alkaline. This temporary shift creates a "window of neuroplasticity" where the brain's defense mechanisms (the Ego) soften, allowing the Prana to flow through previously blocked channels.

Practitioner Insight

When a client experiences "tingling" (paresthesia) during purging breathwork, explain it as the Prana dissolving the density of the blockage. This builds their confidence in the process and helps them stay with the discomfort rather than retreating.

The Vocalized Sigh: Clearing Throat & Heart

Blockages in the Heart (Anahata) and Throat (Vishuddha) often manifest as a "tightness" or a "lump in the throat." These are frequently caused by suppressed grief or unexpressed truth. The "Sigh of Relief" technique combines breath with vibrational purging.

When we add sound to an exhale, we stimulate the Vagus Nerve, which runs through the throat and heart. A 2019 study published in the Journal of Psychological Anthropology found that vocalized breathing significantly reduced sympathetic nervous system arousal. For the energy practitioner, this sound acts as a carrier wave that pulls the "density" out of the upper chakras and releases it into the environment.
