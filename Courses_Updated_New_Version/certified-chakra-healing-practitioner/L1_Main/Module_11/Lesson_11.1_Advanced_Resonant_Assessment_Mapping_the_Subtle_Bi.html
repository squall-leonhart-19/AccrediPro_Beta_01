Layered Scanning: The Practitioner's Technique

Advanced Resonant Assessment requires the practitioner to "calibrate" their hands to different depths. This is known as Frequency Depth Perception. When scanning, you are not just looking for a "yes/no" on energy; you are looking for the quality of the resistance.

Step 1: The Etheric Sweep. Move your hands slowly within two inches of the skin. You are looking for "thickenings" or "voids" that indicate physical stagnation or depletion.

Step 2: The Emotional Probe. Pull back to the 3-4 inch mark. Here, the energy often feels more "fluid" or "sticky." Heat in this layer frequently indicates unresolved anger or grief.

Step 3: The Mental Map. At the 6-inch mark, you are scanning for "static." This feels like a subtle vibration or "buzzing" in the palms, often indicating overactive thinking or anxiety.

Leaks vs. Congested Nodes: The RADIANT Framework

In the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢, we classify all energetic disturbances into two primary categories. Understanding this distinction is the difference between a "hobbyist" and a "professional."

1. Energetic Leaks (Hypo-activity)

A leak occurs when the chakra or biofield layer cannot hold a charge. This is often seen in women who have spent years in "caretaker" roles, giving more than they receive. In a scan, this feels like a cool breeze or a sudden drop-off in pressure.

  • Symptom: Chronic fatigue, inability to set boundaries, feeling "invisible."
  • Practitioner Goal: Infusion and Anchoring.

2. Congested Nodes (Hyper-activity)

A node is a localized "traffic jam" of energy. It is energy that has become stagnant, dense, and hot. In a scan, this feels like resistance, push-back, or intense heat.

  • Symptom: Inflammation, irritability, obsessive thoughts, physical tension.
  • Practitioner Goal: Dissolving and Clearing.
Coach Tip: Detecting the "Mask"

Many clients present with high-energy "congested" nodes in their upper chakras (throat/third eye) to mask significant "leaks" in their root. Always scan the lower chakras first to ensure the foundation is stable before clearing the top.

Rotational Velocity and Bio-Tensor Calibration

While hand scanning is foundational, advanced practitioners often use a Bio-Tensor or a professional-grade pendulum to measure Rotational Velocity. This provides a visual metric for the client and confirms your somatic findings.

A healthy chakra should rotate in a steady, clockwise direction. However, we must look at two specific variables:

1. Velocity (Speed): An extremely fast rotation often indicates a state of "fight or flight" or sympathetic nervous system dominance. A sluggish, slow rotation indicates metabolic or energetic stagnation.

2. Direction: Counter-clockwise rotation isn't necessarily "bad"‚Äîit often indicates the chakra is in a purging or releasing phase. However, if it remains counter-clockwise for weeks, it indicates a refusal to receive new life force (Prana).
