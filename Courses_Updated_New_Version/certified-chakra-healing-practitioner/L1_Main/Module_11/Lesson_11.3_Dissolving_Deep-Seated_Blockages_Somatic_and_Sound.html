Trauma-Informed Clearing: Safety First

The 'Dissolve' phase is the most likely to trigger a Healing Crisis. As blockages break up, the stored emotional energy (amygdala-based memories) can flood the system. As a professional practitioner, you must maintain a "holding environment."

  • Titration: Only dissolve a small amount of the blockage at a time. Do not try to clear 20 years of trauma in 20 minutes.
  • Pendulation: Move the client's focus between the "tight/stuck" area and a "safe/neutral" area in the body.
  • The "Vagus Reset": If a client begins to hyperventilate or shake, use a low, humming sound to stimulate the vagus nerve and return them to the window of tolerance.
Practitioner Tip

Always remind your client: "The feeling is the healing." If emotions arise, they are simply leaving the building. Your job isn't to stop the emotion, but to be the steady anchor while it passes through.
