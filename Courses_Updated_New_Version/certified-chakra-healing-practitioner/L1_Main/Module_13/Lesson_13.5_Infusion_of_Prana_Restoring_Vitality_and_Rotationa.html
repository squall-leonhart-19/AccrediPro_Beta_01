Restoring 'Spin': Correcting Direction and Velocity

The Law of Rotational Dynamics

In most traditions, the healthy direction for a chakra to receive energy is clockwise (from the practitioner's perspective looking at the client). If a chakra is spinning counter-clockwise during an infusion, it is effectively "leaking" or "repelling" the very Prana you are trying to provide.

  • Correcting Velocity: Use small, rhythmic circular motions with your dominant hand about 3-4 inches above the body. Start slow and gradually increase the speed of your hand to "entrain" the chakra to a healthy rhythm.
  • Correcting Direction: If the spin is reversed, use a "firm" energetic intention to halt the motion before slowly initiating a clockwise rotation.

Direct Infusion vs. Environmental Induction

How you deliver Prana depends on the client's energetic permeability. Not every client can handle a "Direct Infusion."

Direct Infusion

Practitioner beams energy directly from the palm chakras into the client's vortex. Best for deeply depleted clients (burnout, post-illness) who need an immediate "jumpstart."

Environmental Induction

Practitioner raises the frequency of the entire room or the client's outer auric field, allowing the client's chakras to "pull" what they need. Best for highly sensitive clients (HSPs) or those with trauma who may feel "invaded" by direct energy.

Professional Standards

Always ask: "How are you integrating this energy?" halfway through the infusion. If they feel "jittery," switch from Direct Infusion to Environmental Induction to slow down the absorption rate.
