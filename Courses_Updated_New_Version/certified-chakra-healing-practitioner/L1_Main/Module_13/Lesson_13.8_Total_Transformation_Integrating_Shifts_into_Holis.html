Common Practitioner Pitfalls

Even experienced coaches can stumble during integration. Watch out for these four common mistakes:

1

Over-Explaining the Science

When a client is emotional, they don't need a lecture on the physics of the biofield. They need to feel seen. Keep your explanations brief and focused on their felt experience.

2

"Fixing" Instead of "Holding"

If Elena feels like a fraud, don't immediately try to talk her out of it. Let her feel it, identify where it lives in her body, and then move the energy. Integration is a process, not a quick fix.

3

Neglecting the Grounding Phase

Always end an integration session with a concrete "Root Chakra" action. For Elena, this might be setting up her professional email or drafting one social media post. Energy must be grounded into action.

4

Ignoring Your Own Imposter Syndrome

If you feel like a fraud while coaching someone who feels like a fraud, the session will stall. Clear your own Solar Plexus before every client call.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is "Spiritual Vertigo" in the context of a career pivot?

It is the sensation where a client's energetic frequency has shifted (they feel more aligned/awakened), but their external identity and daily habits haven't caught up yet, leading to feelings of being a "fraud" or "unplugged."

2. Which chakra is primarily associated with the "Inner Critic" and professional confidence?

The Solar Plexus Chakra (Manipura). It governs will, personal power, and the "Right to Act."

3. Why is it important to ask the client "where the feeling lives in their body"?

It moves the coaching from a purely mental/logical exercise to a somatic/energetic one, allowing the practitioner to identify which chakra requires the most attention during the integration.

4. What is the best way to "ground" an integration session?

By assigning a small, manageable physical action (Root Chakra) that aligns with the new identity the client is stepping into.

Key Takeaways for Your Practice

  • Embody the Guide: Your tone should be warm but authoritative. You are the "anchor" for their transformation.
  • Identity Lag is Normal: Reassure clients that feeling like a "fraud" is often just a symptom of rapid growth.
  • Bridge the Chakras: Use the Solar Plexus for confidence and the Throat for authentic expression in every integration session.
  • Action is Grounding: Never leave a session without a physical "3D" step the client can take.
