Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake Why it Hurts Your Practice The Professional Correction
Over-Explaining Giving a 20-minute history of ancient India bores the client. Keep it focused on their symptoms and their goals.
Using "Maybe" "Maybe your chakra is blocked?" sounds like you aren't sure. "I'm seeing a pattern of congestion in your throat center."
Apologizing for Price "It's $150, but I can give a discount..." devalues your work. State the price, then stop talking. Let the value hang in the air.
Ignoring the Body Talking only about "vibes" makes you seem ungrounded. Always link the energy back to their physical sensations.
Confidence Booster

Remember: You don't need to know everything to be helpful. You just need to know more than the person you are helping. You are already an expert in Elena's eyes.

Success Markers

How do you know if your session went well? Look for these four indicators:

  • The "Exhale": The client physically sighs or relaxes their shoulders when you explain their block. This is a sign of energetic resonance.
  • Validation: The client says, "How did you know that?" or "That's exactly how I feel!"
  • Action: The client is eager to know the "homework" or next steps.
  • Professional Exchange: The client pays your full fee without hesitation because they see the value in the transformation.

Check Your Understanding

1. Why is the "Bridge Technique" important in a professional consultation?

It connects the client's physical symptoms (which they understand) to their energetic blocks (which you are teaching them), creating legitimacy and trust.

2. If a client says "I don't believe in chakras," how should a professional respond?

Pivot to the science of the biofield or the nervous system. "That's okay! We can look at this as a map of how your nervous system is responding to stress in different areas of your body."

3. What two chakras are most commonly involved when a client is struggling with a career transition?

The Solar Plexus (Confidence/Power) and the Throat (Self-Expression/Truth).

4. How should you handle a situation where you feel "imposter syndrome" during a session?

Take a deep breath to ground your own Root Chakra, focus entirely on the client's needs rather than your own performance, and use your prepared scripts.
