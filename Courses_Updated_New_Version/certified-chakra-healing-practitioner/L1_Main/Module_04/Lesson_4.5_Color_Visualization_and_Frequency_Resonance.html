Saturation vs. Tint: Calibrating the Infusion

A common mistake for novice practitioners is using the same "intensity" of color for every client. In the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢, we use the Saturation Scale to match the client's current energetic capacity.

The Tint Technique (For Acute Sensitivity)

If a client is highly stressed, anxious, or "raw" (often seen in those with high empathy or recent trauma), a high-saturation color infusion can be overwhelming. In these cases, we use a Tint‚Äîa pale, translucent version of the color mixed with white light. This provides a gentle "sip" of frequency rather than a "flood."

The Saturation Technique (For Chronic Depletion)

For clients experiencing chronic fatigue, "burnout," or long-term stagnation, we use Saturation. This is a deep, vivid, "neon" version of the color. We visualize the color as a thick liquid or a dense mist that fills every crevice of the chakra until it can hold no more.
