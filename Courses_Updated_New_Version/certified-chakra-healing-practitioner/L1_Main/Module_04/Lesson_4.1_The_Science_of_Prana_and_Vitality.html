The Anatomical Relationship: Nadis and the Nervous System

The Nadis are the subtle channels through which Prana flows. Traditional texts identify 72,000 nadis, but for clinical practice, we focus on the "Big Three" that correlate directly with our central and autonomic nervous systems:

  • Sushumna Nadi: Correlates with the Spinal Cord. It is the central channel of equilibrium.
  • Ida Nadi: Correlates with the Parasympathetic Nervous System (Rest & Digest). It terminates in the left nostril and represents cooling, lunar energy.
  • Pingala Nadi: Correlates with the Sympathetic Nervous System (Fight or Flight). It terminates in the right nostril and represents heating, solar energy.

When we "infuse" Prana, we are essentially stimulating the Sushumna to ensure that the "current" of vitality is balanced between the calming Ida and the activating Pingala. Energetic depletion (hypoactivity) often occurs when a client is stuck in a Pingala-dominant state (chronic stress), leading to "adrenal burnout" of the subtle body.
