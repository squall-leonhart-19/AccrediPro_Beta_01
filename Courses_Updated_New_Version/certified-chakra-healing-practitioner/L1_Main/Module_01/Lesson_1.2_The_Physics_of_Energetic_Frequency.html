The Law of Entrainment

In physics, entrainment is the process where two oscillating systems, which have different periods, fall into synchrony. This was first discovered by Christiaan Huygens in 1665 when he noticed that two pendulum clocks on the same wall eventually swung in perfect unison.

As a practitioner, you are the stronger pendulum. If you enter an assessment feeling anxious, scattered, or "low frequency," your field will entrain the client's field toward that chaos, leading to an inaccurate assessment. This is why the R.A.D.I.A.N.T. Method‚Ñ¢ emphasizes practitioner grounding before every session.

Identifying 'Frequency Noise'

Professional assessment requires a "clean" environment. Just as a scientist needs a sterile lab, an energy practitioner needs a field free of Frequency Noise. This noise comes from two main sources:

  • EMF Interference: Wi-Fi routers, smartwatches, and cell phones emit high-frequency electromagnetic fields that can "mask" the subtler bio-energetic signals of the chakras.
  • Geopathic Stress: Natural radiation from the earth or structural issues in the building that create "pockets" of stagnant energy.

Research suggests that chronic exposure to environmental frequency noise can lead to a 15-20% decrease in parasympathetic tone, making it harder for clients to enter the "receptive state" necessary for assessment.

Coach Tip

Always ask your clients to remove smartwatches and place phones in "Airplane Mode" at least 30 feet away. This isn't just for focus; it's to reduce the "electronic hum" in their biofield that can blur your scanning results.

The Mechanics of Rotational Flow

Chakras are often described as wheels, but physically, they are toroidal vortices. The direction of their spin provides critical data during the Resonant Assessment phase:

Clockwise (CW) Rotation

In most traditions, a clockwise spin is considered "open" and "distributing." It indicates that the center is actively processing and radiating energy outward into the client's life. It is the direction of manifestation.

Counter-Clockwise (CCW) Rotation

A counter-clockwise spin often indicates "drawing in" or "clearing." While sometimes labeled as "closed," in a healthy system, CCW movement can signify a period of internal processing or the purging of emotional waste. However, chronic CCW spin in a center like the Heart may indicate deep-seated protection or trauma.

Coach Tip

Don't label CCW as "bad." Instead, view it as the body's current "strategy." Ask: "What is this center trying to protect or clear right now?" This empowers the client rather than making them feel "broken."

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client's Heart Chakra is measured at 600 Hz instead of its resonant 639 Hz, what is the best physics-based term for this?

This is called Dissonance. The center is oscillating, but not in harmony with its optimal frequency, leading to "energetic static" or emotional friction.

2. Why is the practitioner's personal state considered a "diagnostic tool" in the Law of Entrainment?

Because the stronger, more coherent field (the grounded practitioner) will naturally pull the less coherent field (the stressed client) into its rhythm. If the practitioner is not grounded, they risk "picking up" the client's chaos or reflecting their own.

3. What sensation might you feel in your palms when scanning a hyper-resonant Solar Plexus?

You would likely feel heat, "prickly" static, or a distinct pressure pushing your hand away, indicating an excess of chaotic energy or congestion.

4. True or False: A counter-clockwise (CCW) spin always means a chakra is "broken."

False. A CCW spin is a movement strategy that may indicate the body is clearing, purging, or protecting. It is a state to be assessed, not a "fault" to be judged.
