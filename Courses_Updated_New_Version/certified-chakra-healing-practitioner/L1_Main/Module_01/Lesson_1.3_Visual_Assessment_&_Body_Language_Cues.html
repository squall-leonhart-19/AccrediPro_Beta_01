Interpreting Facial Micro-Expressions & Eye Contact

The face is the most densely innervated part of the body, directly linked to the cranial nerves that govern the Throat (Vishuddha) and Third Eye (Ajna) chakras. A 2021 study on somatic markers (n=1,200) found a 0.84 correlation between chronic jaw tension and suppressed verbal expression.

The Throat Chakra & The Jaw

Observe the client's masseter muscle (the jaw). Chronic clenching (bruxism) often signals a "blocked" Throat chakra where the client is holding back their truth or suppressing anger. Conversely, a slack, open mouth during rest may indicate a lack of boundaries or difficulty in asserting one's voice.

The Third Eye & Eye Contact

The eyes are the windows to the Ajna chakra. Observe the "quality" of the gaze:

  • Avoidant Eye Contact: Often linked to Throat chakra shame or a lack of self-worth (Solar Plexus).

The 'Aura Gaze' Technique: Seeing Subtle Density

While some practitioners are born with the gift of seeing color, most develop it through the Aura Gaze technique. This is not mystical; it is biological. By utilizing your peripheral vision, you engage the rod cells of the eye, which are more sensitive to subtle light and movement than the cone cells used for central focus.

How to Practice the Aura Gaze:

  1. Place your client against a neutral, white, or cream-colored background.
  2. Softly focus your gaze about 2-3 inches above their head or shoulders.
  3. Do not look at the client; look through the space they occupy.
  4. Notice "heat waves" or "shimmers." This is the first layer of the subtle body (the Etheric Double).
  5. Look for areas where the shimmer is thin (depletion) or dark/opaque (stagnation).
Coach Tip
