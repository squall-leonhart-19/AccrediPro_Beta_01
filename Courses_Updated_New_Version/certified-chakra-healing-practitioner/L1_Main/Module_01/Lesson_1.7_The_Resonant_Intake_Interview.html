The Art of the High-Frequency Question

Standard intake questions focus on facts (e.g., "When did the pain start?"). In a Resonant Intake, we focus on frequency. High-frequency questions are designed to elicit an emotional and energetic response, bypassing the client‚Äôs rehearsed narrative.

Instead of asking, "Do you feel stressed?", a Resonant Practitioner asks, "Where in your body does your responsibility feel the heaviest?" This forces the client to connect their mental state to their somatic experience, revealing exactly which chakra is bearing the burden.

Observing Tonal Shifts: The Voice as a Diagnostic Tool

The voice is the literal resonance of the Throat Chakra, powered by the Solar Plexus. As a practitioner, you aren't just listening to what they say, but how it sounds.

  • Fading at the end of sentences: Often indicates a lack of Solar Plexus power or a fear of taking up space.
  • High-pitched, "airy" tone: Can suggest a lack of grounding (Root) or a disconnection from the physical body.
  • Monotone delivery: Often reflects a suppressed Heart or Sacral chakra, where emotional "color" has been drained to maintain safety.
  • Sudden vocal "cracks": These usually occur when the client touches upon a "Resonant Truth"‚Äîa piece of information that is energetically charged.
