The Science of Cellular Memory

For decades, the concept of "cellular memory" was relegated to the fringes of alternative medicine. However, modern epigenetics and psychoneuroimmunology are providing a scientific framework for what energy practitioners have long observed: the body keeps the score.

Cellular memory refers to the body's ability to store emotional and physical trauma within the non-neuronal tissues. When a client experiences a significant emotional event, the biochemical correlates (neuropeptides and hormones) flood the system. If the emotion is not processed, these molecules can bind to cell receptors, effectively "locking" the memory into the physical and energetic structure of the body.
