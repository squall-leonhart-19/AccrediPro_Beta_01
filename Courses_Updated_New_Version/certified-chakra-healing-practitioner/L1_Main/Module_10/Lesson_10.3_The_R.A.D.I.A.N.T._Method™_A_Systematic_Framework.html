The Practitioner as a Neutral Facilitator

A core tenet of the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢ is that the practitioner is not the healer. The client's own system does the healing; the practitioner simply provides the resonant frequency and the space for that healing to occur.

In the physics of entrainment, a stronger, more stable frequency will naturally pull a weaker, chaotic frequency into alignment. As a practitioner, your job is to maintain a "Neutral Zero Point" state. If you try to "force" a healing, you create resistance. If you remain a neutral conduit, the Prana flows effortlessly.

Coach Tip: Energetic Hygiene

Being a "neutral facilitator" protects you from burnout. You aren't using your own energy; you are channeling universal life force. If you feel exhausted after a session, you are likely "pushing" rather than "facilitating."

Phase R: The Diagnostic Foundation

Everything in the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢ succeeds or fails based on the Resonant Assessment (Phase R). If you misdiagnose the blockage, your strategy (Phase A) will be flawed.

Phase R involves three primary layers of investigation:

  1. Somatic Resonance: Using hand scanning to feel for heat (inflammation/excess), cold (depletion/deficiency), or "push-back" (blockage).
  2. Visual Cues: Observing the client's posture, skin tone, and eye clarity, which correspond to specific chakra health.
  3. Psychological Mapping: Identifying the "story" the client tells. A client complaining of "not being heard" at work is providing a clear diagnostic marker for the Throat Chakra (Vishuddha).
Coach Tip: The Intake Form

Your professional intake form should be designed to screen for these three layers before the client even enters the room. This allows you to walk into the session with a preliminary Phase R already in mind.
