The 'Interval Test': Finding Dissonance

The Interval Test is a core diagnostic procedure in acoustic assessment. It relies on the principle of destructive interference. When two frequencies are played within the biofield, they should create a clean, harmonious interval (such as a Perfect Fifth). If the biofield is congested, the sound will "waver," create "beats," or produce a metallic "tinny" quality.

Practitioners typically use weighted tuning forks (like 128Hz and 136.1Hz) because the weights provide a stronger vibration that can be felt by both the practitioner and the client. By moving these forks slowly through the client‚Äôs field, you are essentially "sonar mapping" the energetic terrain.

Coach Tip

When performing the Interval Test, pay attention to the "beats"‚Äîthat wah-wah-wah sound. The faster the beats, the higher the level of dissonance or "static" in that specific chakra. This often correlates with acute stress or physical inflammation in that region.

Vocal Scanning: Sensing Absorption and Reverb

Your voice is the most sophisticated diagnostic tool you possess. Unlike a tuning fork, which has a fixed pitch, the human voice can modulate to find the exact frequency where a client's energy responds. Vocal Scanning involves toning a steady, neutral vowel sound (usually "Ah" or "Oh") while moving your hands or focus over the client's chakras.

You are looking for two primary acoustic phenomena:

  • Absorption (The "Dead" Spot): The sound feels like it is being "sucked in" by the body. The volume seems to drop, and the resonance in your own throat feels muffled. This indicates depletion or hypo-activity (the "I" in RADIANT - Infusion needed).
  • Reverb (The "Hard" Spot): The sound feels like it is bouncing off a wall. It becomes sharp or piercing. This indicates stagnation or hyper-activity (the "D" in RADIANT - Dissolving needed).

Measuring the 'Decay Rate' of Sound

The Decay Rate refers to how long a tuning fork continues to ring after being struck. In a healthy, permeable biofield, a high-quality tuning fork should ring for a standard duration (e.g., 20-30 seconds). However, the density of the client's energy field significantly alters this rate.

Decay Observation Energetic Meaning Clinical Presentation
Rapid Decay (Stops quickly) High Density / Blockage Physical pain, deep-seated trauma, or "thick" energetic armor.
Standard Decay Healthy Permeability Energy is flowing; the field is receptive and balanced.
Extended Decay Hyper-Sensitivity The field is "leaky" or ungrounded; client may feel overwhelmed by others' energy.
Wavering Decay Inconsistency Fluctuating moods or unstable physical health markers.

Identifying the 'Dominant Frequency'

Every client has a "Dominant Frequency"‚Äîthe primary vibrational "note" they are broadcasting at any given time. This is not static; it is the sum of their current physical health, emotional state, and mental focus. Assessing this allows you to determine where they are on the Resonant Scale.

A client in a state of fear or survival will often broadcast a low, "muddy" frequency that feels heavy. A client in a state of inspiration or love broadcasts a higher, "clearer" frequency. Using the "Match and Lead" technique, you first find their dominant frequency with your voice or a fork, and then slowly shift the frequency toward the desired state to see if their field "entrains" (follows) the new sound.

Solfeggio Frequencies as a Diagnostic Tool

While Solfeggio frequencies are famous for healing, they are equally powerful for Resonance Testing. By playing a specific Solfeggio frequency and observing the client's physical and energetic reaction, you can pinpoint exactly which "theme" of their life is currently out of alignment.

  • 396 Hz (Liberating Guilt/Fear): If the client experiences discomfort or "reverb" at this frequency, the blockage is likely rooted in the Root Chakra (Muladhara).
  • 528 Hz (Transformation/DNA Repair): A "flat" response here often indicates a lack of vitality or a Solar Plexus (Manipura) deficiency.
  • 741 Hz (Awakening Intuition): Dissonance here suggests a "noisy" mind or a Throat/Third Eye blockage.

Check Your Understanding

1. What does a "Rapid Decay Rate" (the sound stopping quickly) typically indicate in a chakra assessment?

It indicates high energetic density or a blockage. The energy is "thick" and does not allow the sound waves to pass through freely, causing the vibration to be absorbed and extinguished prematurely.

Reverb suggests a hyper-active or "hard" spot where energy is stagnant and pushing back against the field, rather than flowing. This usually requires "Dissolving" techniques.

3. Why are weighted tuning forks preferred over unweighted ones for biofield assessment?

Weighted forks produce a stronger physical vibration that can be felt in the practitioner's hand and by the client's body, making it easier to detect subtle changes in the "drag" or "static" of the energy field.

4. Which Solfeggio frequency is considered the "Universal Tuner" for checking general vitality?

528 Hz. It is used to test for transformation, DNA repair, and overall Solar Plexus vitality.
