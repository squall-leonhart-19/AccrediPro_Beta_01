The Dual-Aspect Scan: Feeling vs. Will

In advanced energy medicine, we recognize that the major chakras (excluding the Root and Crown) are actually double-headed cones that extend through the body, opening both to the front and the back. This dual nature is critical for a comprehensive assessment.

The Front Aspect of the chakra is generally associated with our emotional life, our receptivity, and our "feeling" state. The Back Aspect is associated with our "will," our ability to manifest, and our executive function.

Heart (Anahata) Self-love, empathy, openness Ego-will, power to act from love
Solar Plexus (Manipura) Emotional sensitivity, intuition Intentionality, "getting things done"
Sacral (Svadhisthana) Sensuality, emotional flow Creative output, sexual drive
Throat (Vishuddha) Listening, receiving truth Speaking truth, professional expression

Auric Structural Integrity: Leaks & Tears

While the chakras are the engines, the Auric Field is the protective casing. A 2021 study on bioelectromagnetic fields (n=450) suggested that "field density" correlates directly with psychological resilience and immune function.

Identifying Energetic Leaks

A "leak" feels like a localized drain. During a manual scan, your hand may feel a sudden drop in temperature or a sensation of "suction." Leaks often occur at the site of old physical injuries or emotional traumas where the energy "bleeds" out of the system, leading to chronic fatigue.

Identifying Energetic Tears

A "tear" is a more aggressive rupture in the peripheral field. These are often caused by sudden shocks, drug/alcohol abuse, or extreme grief. A tear feels like a "jagged" or "static" edge in the biofield. Clients with tears often report feeling "scattered," "unprotected," or like they are "absorbing everyone else's emotions."

The 21 Minor Chakras: Supporting Evidence

Think of the 7 major chakras as the "highways" and the 21 Minor Chakras as the "exit ramps." When you find a blockage in a major center, use the minor centers to validate your findings. This level of detail is what allows you to command premium rates of $200-$500 per session.

  • The Palm Chakras: If these feel closed, the client is likely struggling with "giving or receiving" (Heart chakra connection).
  • The Foot Chakras: Cold foot chakras indicate a failure to "anchor" energy, often seen in clients who are "all in their head" (Root/Crown imbalance).
  • The Knee Chakras: These relate to flexibility and moving forward in life. Stagnation here often mirrors a blocked Solar Plexus.

Sushumna Flow & Spinal Obstructions

The Sushumna Nadi is the central vertical channel through which Prana flows. In your assessment, you are looking for the "vertical alignment" of energy. If the chakras are not stacked correctly, the flow is compromised.

Visual Assessment Technique: Imagine a plumb line running from the Crown to the Perineum. Does the energy "zig-zag"? Use your hand to trace the spine. Any area where your hand "jumps" or feels a "bump" in the energy indicates a spinal obstruction that may be manifesting as physical back pain or neurological tension.

Check Your Understanding

1. If a client is highly empathetic but cannot seem to set boundaries or "say no," which aspect of the Heart chakra is likely blocked?

The Back Aspect (Will). While the front aspect is open (empathy), the back aspect (the power to act and set boundaries) is likely deficient or stagnant.

2. What is the primary difference between an energetic "leak" and a "tear"?

A leak is a localized drain (often at an injury site) that feels like suction or cold. A tear is a structural rupture in the outer field that feels jagged or static, usually caused by sudden shock or trauma.

3. Why should a practitioner scan the foot chakras during a Root chakra assessment?

The foot chakras act as the "exit ramps" for the Root. If they are closed, the client cannot ground excess energy or "anchor" the healing work, leading to temporary results.

4. True or False: Distance assessment requires the client to be in a meditative state during the scan.

False. While helpful, the client does not need to be meditating. Because energy is non-local, the practitioner can access the biofield through resonant intent regardless of the client's activity (with prior permission).
