The Science of Gas Discharge Visualization (GDV)

At the heart of modern technological assessment is Gas Discharge Visualization (GDV), a technique developed by Dr. Konstantin Korotkov. Based on the "Kirlian Effect," GDV uses a high-intensity electromagnetic field to stimulate a "glow" (gas discharge) from the fingertips.

This discharge is captured by a specialized camera and processed into digital data. The patterns of light emitted are not random; they reflect the electron-ion emission from the skin, which is influenced by the state of the autonomic nervous system and the body's bio-energetic field.

Coach Tip: Explaining GDV to Clients

Think of the Bio-Well like an EKG for your biofield. Just as an EKG measures the electrical activity of your heart, GDV measures the photonic activity of your fingertips, which are connected to your entire system through meridian endpoints.

The Limitations of Technology

While Bio-Well is a breakthrough, it is sensitive. As a professional, you must understand the variables that can "skew" a reading:

  • Environmental Humidity: High humidity can increase the size of the gas discharge, potentially showing "false" high energy.
  • Practitioner Presence: The biofield of the practitioner can influence the client's field during the scan.
  • Recent Stimulants: Caffeine or nicotine within 2 hours of a scan will artificially inflate the "Energy Reserve" data.
  • Emotional Volatility: A scan taken immediately after an argument will show "Chaos" (Entropy) that may not be the client's baseline state.
Coach Tip: Intuition First

If the machine says the client is "perfect" but your intuition and the client's somatic symptoms say otherwise, trust the human element. The machine measures a snapshot in time; you are witnessing a living soul.
