The Science of the Sacred Pulse: What is HRV?

Contrary to popular belief, a healthy heart does not beat with the regularity of a metronome. Instead, a healthy heart is dynamic and irregular. Heart Rate Variability (HRV) is the measurement of the variation in time between each consecutive heartbeat (the R-R interval).

In the context of the RADIANT Method‚Ñ¢, HRV serves as a physiological "mirror" of a client's energetic resilience. A high HRV indicates a nervous system that is flexible, resilient, and capable of "infusing" high-frequency Prana. A low HRV suggests energetic stagnation or "blockage," where the body is stuck in a defensive, low-vitality state.
