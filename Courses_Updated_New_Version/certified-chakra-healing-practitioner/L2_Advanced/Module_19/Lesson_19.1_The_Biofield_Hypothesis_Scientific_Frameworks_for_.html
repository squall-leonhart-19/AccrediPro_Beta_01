The NIH Biofield Classification

In 1992, the National Institutes of Health (NIH) convened a panel of scientists to investigate "unconventional" medical practices. It was during this landmark meeting that the term "Biofield" was officially coined. This was a pivotal moment for chakra practitioners, as it provided a standardized scientific vocabulary for what had previously been called "aura" or "subtle energy."

The NIH defines the biofield as a "massless field, not necessarily electromagnetic, that surrounds and permeates living bodies." However, modern research has expanded this to include the complex, organized, and multi-layered fields that regulate the biological functions of the organism.
