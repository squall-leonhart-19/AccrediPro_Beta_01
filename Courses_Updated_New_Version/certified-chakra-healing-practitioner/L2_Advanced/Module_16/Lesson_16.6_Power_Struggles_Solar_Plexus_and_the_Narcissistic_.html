The Solar Plexus, or Manipura, is the seat of our willpower, autonomy, and self-definition. When a client has been subjected to narcissistic abuse‚Äîwhether from a parent, partner, or toxic workplace‚Äîtheir sense of "I am" is replaced by "I am what you need me to be."

A major blockage in the Solar Plexus is the Fear of Authority. For survivors of narcissistic abuse, being "powerful" was often punished. Therefore, the ego-mind creates a "safety shield" of smallness.

The A (Anchoring) phase is where we lock in the new frequency. Without boundaries, the Solar Plexus will simply cave in again. Research in Neuro-Energetic Psychology suggests that physicalizing a boundary can rewire the brain's threat response.

The "caved-in" signature, characterized by a sunken chest, rounded shoulders, and a collapsed midsection, signifying the client is hiding their "Self" and protecting their "soft underbelly."
