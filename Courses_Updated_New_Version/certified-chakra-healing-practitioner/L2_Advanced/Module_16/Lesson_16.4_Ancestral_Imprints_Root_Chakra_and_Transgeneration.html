The Science of the Inherited Field

For decades, energy practitioners observed that certain clients carried "scarcity mindsets" or "fear of displacement" that bore no relation to their actual life experiences. Today, epigenetics provides the scientific framework for what we call the Inherited Field. Research indicates that trauma can leave chemical marks on genes, which are then passed down to subsequent generations.

A landmark 2015 study by Rachel Yehuda found that epigenetic markers on the FKBP5 gene were significantly different in Holocaust survivors and their offspring (n=32), suggesting transgenerational transmission of stress sensitivity. In the context of the Root Chakra (Muladhara), this manifests as a persistent, non-local sense of "un-safety" or "not belonging."
