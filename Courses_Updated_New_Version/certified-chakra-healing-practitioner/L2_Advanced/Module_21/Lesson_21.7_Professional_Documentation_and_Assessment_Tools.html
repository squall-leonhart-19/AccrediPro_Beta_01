Standardized Intake Protocols

Ethical Record-Keeping: The SOAP Method

One of the biggest challenges for energy practitioners is balancing intuitive insights with professional documentation. If you write "I saw a dark cloud over their liver," and that note is ever subpoenaed or shared with a doctor, it lacks professional credibility. Instead, we use the SOAP Note format adapted for energy work.

S

Subjective

What the client reports in their own words. "Client states they feel 'stuck' in their career and experience tension in the throat."

O

Objective

What you observe. "Observed shallow chest breathing; hand scanning revealed thermal coolness over the 5th Chakra (Throat)."

A

Assessment

Your professional synthesis. "Probable hypo-activity in Vishuddha related to suppressed communication; secondary Root instability."

P

Plan

What you did and what comes next. "Applied 528Hz tuning fork to Throat; assigned 'Truth Journaling' as a Nourishing Ritual."

Coach Tip: The "Why" of SOAP

Using SOAP notes protects you. If a client has a "Healing Crisis" (as discussed in Module 8), your notes show a logical, methodical progression of care rather than haphazard "energy playing."

The Progress Review Session

The Progress Review is a dedicated session (usually every 4-5 sessions) where no "active" healing occurs. Instead, you sit with the client and review the Chakra Maps and Frequency Charts.

The Structure of a Progress Review:

  • The "Win" Recap: Highlight 3 specific areas where quantitative scores improved.
  • The Obstacle Audit: Identify which chakra is still "lagging" (often the Heart or Solar Plexus).
  • The Strategy Pivot: Adjust the Alignment Strategy (Module 2) based on the data.
  • The Re-Commitment: Discuss the next phase of the RADIANT journey.
Coach Tip: Professional Income

Progress reviews are where you upsell the next package. By showing the data, you aren't "selling"; you are providing a professional recommendation for the next stage of their evolution. This is how you build a $10k/month practice with just 15-20 loyal clients.

Check Your Understanding

1. What does the "O" in a SOAP note represent for a Chakra Practitioner?

Objective. This includes what you observe through hand scanning (thermal changes, tingling), visual cues (body language, skin flushing), or breathing patterns. It does NOT include your intuitive "guesses" (which go in Assessment).

2. Why is a Progress Review session conducted without active energy work?

To allow the client to cognitively integrate the energetic shifts, review the data/maps, and move from a passive recipient to an active participant in their healing roadmap. It also provides a professional touchpoint for package renewal.

3. How do you document an intuitive "hit" professionally?

By placing it in the "A" (Assessment) section and phrasing it as a possibility: "Energetic presentation suggests possible stagnation related to [X], to be explored in next session."

4. Which RADIANT phase primarily uses the Chakra Resonance Map?

The R: Resonant Assessment phase. It is the baseline tool used to identify the hierarchy of intervention.
