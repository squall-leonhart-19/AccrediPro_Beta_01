The "Emptying the Vessel" Principle

In the RADIANT Methodâ„¢, the sequence of intervention is non-negotiable. We must always Dissolve (D) before we Infuse (I). Attempting to infuse high-frequency prana into a system that is still congested with stagnant energy is akin to pouring fresh spring water into a glass full of mud. The result is not a clean glass; it is simply more mud.

This principle, known as Emptying the Vessel, ensures that the client's biofield has the "bandwidth" to receive new information. If you skip the dissolving phase, the client may experience what we call a "healing crisis" or "energetic indigestion," where the new energy has nowhere to go and causes temporary agitation, anxiety, or physical discomfort.

Calibrating Intensity & Flow

One of the biggest mistakes new practitioners make is applying the same "volume" of energy to every client. You must adjust your delivery based on the client's Energetic Sensitivity Profile.

  • The Sensitive/HSP Client: Requires a "whisper" of energy. Use long, slow breathwork and soft, melodic sound. High intensity can cause them to shut down.
  • The Stagnant/Resistant Client: Requires a "shout" of energy. Use percussive sound, rapid breathwork, and strong, direct visualizations to penetrate the energetic armor.
Professional Standard

Always start at 20% intensity and check in with your client. Ask: "On a scale of 1-10, how intense does this frequency feel in your body right now?" This ensures they stay within their "Window of Tolerance" for healing.

Sensory Integration Techniques

  • Tactile Anchors: Placing a specific crystal or the client's own hand on the chakra being infused.
  • Auditory Anchors: A specific chime or bell that signals the completion of the clearing and the start of the charging.
