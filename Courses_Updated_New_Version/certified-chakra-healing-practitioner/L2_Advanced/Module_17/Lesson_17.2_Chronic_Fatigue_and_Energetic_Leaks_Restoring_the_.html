Blocked vs. Blown: The Manipura Distinction

This is the most common mistake new practitioners make: treating a depleted chakra as if it were blocked. If you use "aggressive clearing" techniques (like heavy breathwork or loud sound healing) on a "blown" Solar Plexus, you will further exhaust the client.

  • Blocked (Hyper-active/Stagnant): The chakra has too much energy that can't move. It feels hot, hard, and pressurized. Intervention: Dissolve (D Step).
  • Blown (Hypo-active/Depleted): The chakra has no "charge" left. It feels cold, hollow, and soft. Intervention: Infuse (I Step).

Nourishing Rituals & Energetic Budgeting

Transformation isn't just what happens on your table; it's what happens in the client's kitchen and bedroom. For chronic fatigue, the N (Nourishing Rituals) phase must focus on Metabolic Recovery.

The Energetic Budget

Clients with energetic leaks often suffer from the "Boom-Bust" cycle: they feel a tiny bit of energy, spend it all on chores, and then crash for three days. You must teach them to live on a "budget."

  • Income: Sleep, meditation, nourishing food, gentle sunlight.
  • Expenses: Email, difficult conversations, exercise, sensory overload.
  • The Rule: Never spend more than 70% of your daily "income" until the Solar Plexus is fully reconstructed.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary difference in "feel" between a blocked chakra and a leaking one during a scan?

A blocked chakra feels hot, hard, and pressurized (excess energy), while a leaking/depleted chakra feels cool, hollow, and "magnetic," as if it is pulling energy in to fill a void.

It is the tendency for depleted clients to over-exert themselves the moment they feel a slight increase in energy, which then causes a total collapse of their fragile energetic field.
