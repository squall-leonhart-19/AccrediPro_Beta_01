The Shadow of the Light: Defining Spiritual Bypassing

The term Spiritual Bypassing was first coined by psychologist John Welwood in the early 1980s. It refers to the tendency to use spiritual ideas and practices to sidestep, or avoid facing, unresolved emotional issues, psychological wounds, and unfinished developmental tasks.

In the context of chakra healing, this often manifests as a hyper-focus on the "Upper Chakras" (Third Eye and Crown) while neglecting the "Lower Chakras" (Root, Sacral, Solar Plexus) where somatic trauma is primarily stored. As a practitioner, you must be vigilant: are you helping a client heal, or are you helping them hide?

Coach Tip

If a client consistently uses phrases like "It's all for the best" or "I've already forgiven them" while their Root Chakra remains severely depleted, they may be bypassing. True forgiveness is a result of healing, not a prerequisite for it.

Trauma is not just an event that happened in the past; it is a physiological state currently residing in the nervous system. When we apply the D: Dissolving Blockages step of our method, we must do so with extreme care.

The Ethics of Toxic Positivity and the Heart Chakra

In many spiritual circles, there is an unwritten rule that one must "stay high vibe" or "think positive" to manifest health. This is often referred to as Toxic Positivity. Ethically, this can be incredibly damaging to the Heart Chakra (Anahata).

The Heart Chakra is the bridge between the physical and spiritual. When we force positivity, we create a "mask" over the heart. This prevents true Resonant Assessment because the client is presenting a false energetic signature. A 2022 meta-analysis published in the Journal of Psychological Research found that emotional suppression increases systemic inflammationâ€”the very thing many chakra practitioners are trying to reduce.

When Blockages Serve a Purpose

One of the most profound ethical shifts in professional chakra work is recognizing that not all blockages should be removed.

Consider the Solar Plexus (Manipura). If a client is currently in an abusive situation, a "blocked" or "armored" Solar Plexus might be the only thing keeping them from a complete psychological collapse. If you "open" that chakra without addressing the external reality, you leave the client vulnerable.

Coach Tip

Always ask the energy: "What is the function of this blockage?" If it feels like a shield, your job is to help the client build internal strength (Root) so they no longer need the external shield.

Anchoring Frequency Without Dismissing Reality

The final stage of the RADIANT Methodâ„¢, Anchoring Frequency, involves stabilizing the client's energy at a higher vibration. However, an ethical practitioner never uses this to dismiss the client's physical or socio-economic reality.

If a client is struggling with chronic illness or financial hardship, telling them to "just vibrate higher" is a form of spiritual gaslighting. Instead, anchoring should be about finding resilience within the reality. We anchor the frequency of peace amidst the storm, not the denial that the storm exists.

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the primary danger of "dissolving" a chakra blockage too quickly in a trauma survivor?

Retraumatization or "flooding." If the nervous system isn't prepared to process the stored energy, the client may experience a crisis rather than healing.

2. How does toxic positivity specifically affect the Heart Chakra?

It creates an energetic "armor" or mask, preventing genuine emotional processing and leading to a state of dissonance between the client's internal state and external expression.

3. True or False: Every energetic blockage should be removed during a healing session.

False. Some blockages serve as vital protective mechanisms for a client who is not yet stable enough to handle the underlying vulnerability.

4. Which chakras are most often neglected in spiritual bypassing?

The Lower Chakras (Root, Sacral, and Solar Plexus), as these are the primary sites for somatic trauma and physical reality storage.
