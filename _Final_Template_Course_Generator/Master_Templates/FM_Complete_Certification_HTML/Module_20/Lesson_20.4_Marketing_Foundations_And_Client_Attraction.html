The Marketing Mindset

Before diving into tactics and channels, we need to address the beliefs that block effective marketing. Many health coaches bring internal resistance to the idea of "marketing themselves." This resistance often stems from misunderstandings about what marketing truly is‚Äîand what it isn't.

Marketing is not manipulation. It's not about convincing people they need something they don't. It's about reaching the people who are already searching for solutions and showing them that you can help.

Marketing Myths
  • Marketing = Being pushy or salesy
  • Good work should speak for itself
  • Visibility = Self-promotion
  • I should wait until I'm "ready"
Marketing Realities
  • Marketing = Reaching people who need help
  • People can't hire you if they don't know you exist
  • Visibility = Service to those searching
  • You learn by doing, not waiting
1

Marketing = Service

You can't serve people who don't know you exist. Visibility enables impact. Every time you share valuable information, you're potentially changing someone's life.

2

Content = Public Coaching

Share freely what you would tell clients. Your best content builds trust and demonstrates your expertise. Those who can implement alone will; those who need support will hire you.

3

Promotion = Invitation

Inviting people to solutions isn't pushy‚Äîit's generous. When you've demonstrated value, making an offer is helping people take the next step.

4

Consistency = Reliability

Showing up regularly builds trust over time. People hire coaches they've watched, learned from, and come to trust through repeated exposure.

Understanding the Client Journey

People don't go from stranger to paying client instantly. Understanding the journey from first awareness to enrollment helps you create appropriate content for each stage. This is often called the marketing funnel‚Äîthough it's better understood as a relationship-building path.

The Five Stages of Client Attraction

üîç Stage 1: Awareness

Client's Mindset: "I have a problem but don't know solutions exist"

What They Need: Educational content that names their problem and hints at solutions. Blog posts, social media content, podcast interviews that meet them where they are.

üí° Stage 2: Interest

Client's Mindset: "I'm curious about this approach"

What They Need: Deeper content, free resources, proof of your expertise. Lead magnets, webinars, email sequences that build trust and demonstrate value.

ü§î Stage 3: Consideration

Client's Mindset: "Is this the right solution for me?"

What They Need: Case studies, testimonials, connection with you personally. Stories of transformation, behind-the-scenes content, Q&A sessions that let them "try you on."

‚úÖ Stage 4: Decision

Client's Mindset: "I'm ready to take action"

What They Need: Clear offer, consultation invitation, easy next step. Application process, discovery call opportunity, straightforward path to working together.

üéØ Stage 5: Action

Client's Mindset: "I'm enrolling in coaching"

What They Need: Simple enrollment process, welcome experience, confirmation they made the right choice. Seamless onboarding that reinforces their decision.

Coach Tip

Most coaches focus all their content on awareness and education, then wonder why people don't hire them. Create content for every stage. Share client success stories (consideration), make clear invitations to work together (decision), and make enrollment easy (action).

Choosing Your Marketing Channels

Here's a truth that will save you enormous frustration: You don't need to be everywhere. In fact, trying to maintain presence on every platform is a recipe for burnout and mediocrity.

Instead, choose 2-3 channels where your ideal clients naturally gather and where you can show up consistently with content that feels authentic to you.

üìß Email Marketing

Why it works: You own your list (unlike social media followers), direct communication, highest conversion rates, builds relationship over time, not dependent on algorithms.

Best for: All coaches‚Äîthis should be foundational regardless of other channels

Getting started: Choose an email platform (ConvertKit, MailerLite, MailChimp). Create a simple lead magnet. Add signup form to your website. Send weekly valuable emails.

Email Strategy

Offer valuable free resource to build list ‚Ä¢ Send regular newsletters with helpful content ‚Ä¢ Occasional invitations to work together ‚Ä¢ Mix of education, story, and invitation

üì± Instagram

Why it works: Visual platform, personal connection, large health-interested audience, reels and stories provide organic reach, ability to build community.

Best for: Coaches comfortable on camera, niches where visual content works (food, lifestyle, movement), younger demographic (25-45).

Getting started: Create professional but personal profile. Post 3-5 times per week mixing feed posts and reels. Use stories daily for behind-the-scenes connection. Engage authentically with your niche community.

Instagram Strategy

Educational carousel posts ‚Ä¢ Personal story reels ‚Ä¢ Client transformation highlights ‚Ä¢ Behind-the-scenes stories ‚Ä¢ Engage genuinely with followers' content

üë• Facebook

Why it works: Groups create engaged community, older demographic than Instagram (great for 40+ audience), excellent for local connections, live video capabilities.

Best for: Coaches targeting age 40+, local practice building, community-focused approaches, niches with existing active groups.

Getting started: Join 5-10 groups where your ideal clients gather. Contribute genuinely without promoting. Consider starting your own community group. Use personal profile strategically.

Facebook Strategy

Join and serve in relevant groups ‚Ä¢ Answer questions generously ‚Ä¢ Share valuable posts on personal profile ‚Ä¢ Consider creating your own community ‚Ä¢ Facebook Live for deeper teaching

üíº LinkedIn

Why it works: Professional audience, B2B opportunities, thought leadership platform, less saturated with health coaches, corporate wellness connections.

Best for: Corporate wellness coaches, executive health coaching, professional/career-focused niches, B2B service providers.

Getting started: Optimize professional profile. Post thought leadership content 2-3x per week. Engage with complementary professionals. Share longer-form articles.

LinkedIn Strategy

Thought leadership posts ‚Ä¢ Professional articles ‚Ä¢ Network with complementary providers ‚Ä¢ Comment meaningfully on others' content ‚Ä¢ Position as expert in your niche

ü§ù In-Person/Local Marketing

Why it works: Highest trust-building, personal connection, less competition locally, naturally referral-friendly, great for community impact.

Best for: Local practice focus, community-oriented coaches, those who thrive in-person, coaches uncomfortable with social media.

Getting started: Offer free workshop at local venue. Partner with gyms, yoga studios, functional medicine clinics. Join local business networking groups. Speak at community events.

Critical Decision Point

Choose channels based on: (1) Where your ideal clients naturally spend time, (2) Which formats you can sustain long-term, (3) What feels authentic to your personality. It's better to do two channels consistently well than five channels sporadically and poorly.

Content That Attracts

Effective content does three things simultaneously: educates (demonstrates your expertise), connects (builds relationship and trust), and positions you as the solution (makes clear how you help).

The coaches who struggle with client attraction often do one or two of these well but miss the third. They educate endlessly without personal connection, or they share lots of personal stories without demonstrating expertise, or they do both but never clearly invite people to work with them.

50-60%

Educational Content

Teach what you know. Tips, explanations, myth-busting, how-tos, research simplified. "Here's why you're not sleeping well and what to do about it."

20-30%

Connection/Story Content

Your journey, behind-the-scenes, personal experiences, values. "Why I became a health coach after my own health crisis."

10-15%

Social Proof Content

Client testimonials, transformations, results. "Sarah came to me exhausted and skeptical. Six months later, she's off three medications."

5-10%

Invitation Content

Direct invitations to work with you. "I have two spots opening in my spring program. Here's how to apply."

Coach Tip

Speak to one person. Write or record content as if addressing your ideal client directly. "You" is more powerful than "people." Compare: "Are you exhausted but your doctor says you're fine?" creates more connection than "Many people experience unexplained fatigue."

Content Creation Principles That Work

üìù Answer Real Questions

What do clients ask you? What do people in your niche search for? Create content that answers actual questions. Keep a running list of questions and concerns you hear‚Äîeach one is a content idea.

üéÅ Give Your Best Stuff Free

Don't hold back fearing people won't hire you. Share valuable information generously. People who can implement on their own will; those who need support will hire you. Your best content builds trust.

üìñ Tell Stories

Stories bypass skepticism and create emotional connection. Share client transformations (with permission), your own journey, case studies. People remember stories long after they forget facts.

üîÑ Repurpose Everything

One idea becomes: email, social post, video, podcast appearance, workshop topic. Create once, share in multiple formats. This isn't repetitive‚Äîit's strategic reinforcement.

Building Your Lead Magnet

A lead magnet is a free valuable resource offered in exchange for email signup. It moves strangers into your world, gives you permission to continue the conversation, and demonstrates your expertise upfront.

The best lead magnets solve one specific problem for your ideal client quickly. They should be valuable enough that people would pay for them, but you're giving them away to start the relationship.

üìã Guides and Checklists

Simple to create, high perceived value, actionable immediately.

üéØ Mini-Courses or Challenges

The Sustainable Marketing System

Sporadic marketing doesn't work. Posting intensely for three weeks, then disappearing for two months, then starting again doesn't build momentum or trust. Consistent, sustainable systems do.

Your marketing system should be something you can maintain long-term without burning out. This means creating realistic expectations and building in efficiency through batching and systems.

Simple Weekly Marketing System

üìß Weekly Email

One valuable email to your list every week. Educational content, personal stories, client wins, occasional invitations. (Time: 30-60 min to write)

üì± Social Posts

3-5 posts per week on your primary platform. Can batch-create these in advance. Mix of educational, personal, and invitation content. (Time: 2-3 hours to create week's content)

üí¨ Engagement

15 minutes per day genuinely engaging with others' content in your niche. Comment thoughtfully, answer questions, build relationships. (Time: 1.75 hours per week)

ü§ù Connection

2-3 personal outreach messages per week to referral sources, potential collaborators, or warm leads. Build relationships proactively. (Time: 30 min per week)

Total Weekly Time Investment: 5-7 hours

Coach Tip

Batch your content creation. Rather than creating content daily, dedicate one day per month to creating all your content in batches. Write 4 emails, create 15-20 social posts, plan any live content. Then schedule it out. This is far more efficient than daily content creation and reduces decision fatigue.

Common Pitfall

Consistency beats intensity. Better to post three times a week forever than daily for a month then nothing. Your audience needs to see you regularly to remember you, trust you, and eventually hire you. Build a rhythm you can sustain through busy seasons.

Marketing Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Inconsistency

Posting intensely for two weeks, then disappearing for a month, then starting again. This creates no momentum. People forget you between appearances. Solution: Create a sustainable weekly rhythm and stick to it.

Mistake #2: Being Everywhere

Trying to maintain presence on Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, Twitter, YouTube simultaneously. You'll burn out or do everything poorly. Solution: Choose 2-3 channels and do them well. You can always expand later.

Mistake #3: All Education, No Invitation

Giving valuable content endlessly without ever inviting people to work with you. People need to be told how to take the next step. Solution: Include regular clear invitations to book consultations or join programs.

Mistake #4: Comparing Yourself

Looking at coaches with 50,000 followers and feeling inadequate. You don't need followers‚Äîyou need clients. Solution: Focus on connection with your audience, not size. A coach with 500 engaged subscribers can have a thriving practice.

Mistake #5: Waiting for Perfect

Not starting until your website is perfect, your branding is dialed in, your content is polished. Solution: Start before you're ready. You'll learn by doing, and imperfect action beats perfect inaction every time.

Coach Tip

Track your marketing metrics simply: email list growth, social media engagement, consultation requests, client enrollment. What gets measured gets improved. Review monthly and adjust your approach based on what's working.
